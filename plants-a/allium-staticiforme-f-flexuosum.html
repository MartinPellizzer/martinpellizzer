Allium staticiforme f. flexuosum

Allium staticiforme f. flexuosum, commonly known as the flexible leek, is a botanical variant of the Allium genus.

It is characterized by its elongated, flexible leaves that form a rosette structure. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in a dense umbel. It is native to temperate regions of Europe and is often found in moist, grassy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
