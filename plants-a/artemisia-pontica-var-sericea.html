Artemisia pontica var. sericea

Artemisia pontica var. sericea, commonly known as silver wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its gray-green, finely hairy foliage that gives it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
