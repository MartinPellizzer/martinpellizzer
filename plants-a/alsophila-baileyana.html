Alsophila baileyana

Alsophila baileyana, commonly known as the Baileys' tree fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically evergreen and can grow quite long, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.

Alsophila baileyana is also noted for its slow growth rate and its role in tropical ecosystems as a habitat for various small animals and insects.
