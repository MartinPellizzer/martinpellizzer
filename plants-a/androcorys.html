Androcorys

Androcorys, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Androcorys is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a challenge for crop management.
