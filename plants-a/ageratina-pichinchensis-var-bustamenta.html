Ageratina pichinchensis var. bustamenta

Ageratina pichinchensis var. bustamenta, commonly known as the Pichincha blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments within cloud forests. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
