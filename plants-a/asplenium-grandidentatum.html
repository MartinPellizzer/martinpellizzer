Asplenium grandidentatum

Asplenium grandidentatum, commonly known as the broad-toothed spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a small, epiphytic or lithophytic fern that typically grows on rocks or tree trunks in shaded, humid environments. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated margins, and its fronds are usually dark green and glossy. It produces spores in clusters called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

This fern is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, including parts of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.
