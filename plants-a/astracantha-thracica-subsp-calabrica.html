Astracantha thracica subsp. calabrica

Astracantha thracica subsp.

calabrica, commonly known as the Greek bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of blue to violet flowers.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
