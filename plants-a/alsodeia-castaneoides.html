Alsodeia castaneoides

Alsodeia castaneoides, commonly known as the redstem alsike, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, inflated pods. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Alsodeia castaneoides is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
