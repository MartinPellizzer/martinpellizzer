Avena elatior f. subhirsuta

Avena elatior f. subhirsuta, commonly known as the hairy oat, is a variant of the common oat species characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in temperate climates, often found in meadows and along roadsides. The plant features slender, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, grass-like foliage.
