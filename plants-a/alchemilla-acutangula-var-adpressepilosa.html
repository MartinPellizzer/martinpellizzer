Alchemilla acutangula var. adpressepilosa

Alchemilla acutangula var. adpressepilosa, commonly known as the woolly lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features basal rosettes of soft, woolly, silver-gray foliage that is densely covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are borne on slender, upright stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive, leathery texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
