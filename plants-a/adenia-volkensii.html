Adenia volkensii

Adenia volkensii, commonly known as the African mountain cucumber, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is characterized by its thick, woody stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with five petals and a distinctive crown of filaments. Its fruit is a fleshy, green, cucumber-like structure that turns yellow when mature.

Adenia volkensii is often found in forested areas and is adapted to humid, shaded environments.
