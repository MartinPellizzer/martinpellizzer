Astragalus asterias subsp. radiatus

Astragalus asterias subsp.

radiatus, commonly known as the radiate milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations.
