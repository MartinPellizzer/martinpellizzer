Ammannia alata

Ammannia alata, commonly known as prairie bluemink, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist soils along stream banks and in wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a reddish or purplish hue.

Ammannia alata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
