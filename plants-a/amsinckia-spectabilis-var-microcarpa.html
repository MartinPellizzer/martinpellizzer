Amsinckia spectabilis var. microcarpa

Amsinckia spectabilis var. microcarpa, commonly known as the narrowleaf flossflower, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This annual plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and inland valleys.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and role in supporting local pollinators.
