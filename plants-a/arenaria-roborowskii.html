Arenaria roborowskii

Arenaria roborowskii, commonly known as the Roborowsky's sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the cold, arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces clusters of tiny white flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and flies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
