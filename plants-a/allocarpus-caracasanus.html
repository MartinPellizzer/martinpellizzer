Allocarpus caracasanus

Allocarpus caracasanus, commonly known as the Caracasanus tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and distinctive, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a smooth, waxy surface.

Allocarpus caracasanus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
