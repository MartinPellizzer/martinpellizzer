Arundinaria macrosperma var. arborescens

Arundinaria macrosperma var. arborescens, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a species of grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or golden in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
