Agropyron × duvalii

Agropyron × duvalii, commonly known as the tall wheatgrass, is a hybrid grass species that originated from the cross between Agropyron cristatum and Agropyron elongatum.

It is characterized by its tall, tufted growth habit and coarse, bluish-green foliage. The plant typically grows in a variety of habitats, including grasslands, disturbed sites, and along roadsides. It produces spike-like inflorescences with small, spikelet-based flowers.

Agropyron × duvalii is valued for its adaptability and is often used in forage and erosion control.
