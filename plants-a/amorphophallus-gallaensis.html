Amorphophallus gallaensis

Amorphophallus gallaensis, commonly known as the African giant arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, elephant-like tuberous roots. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe. Its tubers are edible and have been used as a food source in various African cultures.

The botanical features of A. gallaensis make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
