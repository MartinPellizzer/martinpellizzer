Aglaia janowskyi

Aglaia janowskyi, commonly known as the Java aglaia, is a tropical plant belonging to the Meliaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and Malaysia, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Aglaia janowskyi is also noted for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
