Aconitum anthora var. jacquinii

Aconitum anthora var. jacquinii, commonly known as the Jacob's aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers in late summer, which are borne in racemes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are compound, with deeply lobed divisions, and the plant is adapted to grow in well-drained, acidic soils.

It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist environments.
