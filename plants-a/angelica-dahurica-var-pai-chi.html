Angelica dahurica var. pai-chi

Angelica dahurica var. pai-chi, commonly known as the Chinese angelica or Bai Zhi, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features tall, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are the primary part used medicinally, valued for their aromatic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its therapeutic benefits.
