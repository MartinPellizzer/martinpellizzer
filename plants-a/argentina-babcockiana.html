Argentina babcockiana

Argentina babcockiana, commonly known as the Patagonian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of Argentina.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments and thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Argentina babcockiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
