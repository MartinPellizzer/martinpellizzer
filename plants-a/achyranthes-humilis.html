Achyranthes humilis

Achyranthes humilis, commonly known as the prostrate spurge, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically features slender, prostrate stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing tiny seeds. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Achyranthes humilis is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in environments with limited water availability.

Morphology

Achyranthes humilis has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a glossy dark green upper surface and a lighter underside, typically ranging from 2 to 5 centimeters in length.

The stem is slender, herbaceous, and slightly hairy, with a reddish-brown color and a spreading growth pattern. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences, featuring five sepals and petals, and bloom from late spring to early summer, primarily pollinated by wind. The fruit is a small, three-chambered capsule that disperses its seeds by wind after drying.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, anchoring the plant in moist, disturbed soils.
