Aesculus pavia var. humilis

Aesculus pavia var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf red horsechestnut, is a compact variant of the red horsechestnut native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, red-orange flowers that appear in late spring. The plant features palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its bark is smooth and grayish-brown. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its striking floral display.

The fruit is a spiny capsule containing glossy, dark red seeds.
