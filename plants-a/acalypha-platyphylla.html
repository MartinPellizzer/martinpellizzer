Acalypha platyphylla

Acalypha platyphylla, commonly known as the broadleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often bright green with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from red to purple, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Acalypha platyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and vibrant floral displays.
