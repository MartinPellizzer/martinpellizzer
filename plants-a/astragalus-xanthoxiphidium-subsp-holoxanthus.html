Astragalus xanthoxiphidium subsp. holoxanthus

Astragalus xanthoxiphidium subsp.

holoxanthus, commonly known as the yellow-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically visited by pollinators such as bees. Its roots are known to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at higher elevations.
