Alternanthera denticulata var. typica

Alternanthera denticulata var. typica, commonly known as the red-veined or red-leaf alternanthera, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vivid red veins contrasting against its dark green foliage. This plant thrives in freshwater environments and is often cultivated in ponds or as a floating plant in aquariums. It prefers moderate to high light conditions and can grow rapidly under optimal care.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for both gardeners and aquatic enthusiasts.
