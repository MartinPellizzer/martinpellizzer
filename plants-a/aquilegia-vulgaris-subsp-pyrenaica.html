Aquilegia vulgaris subsp. pyrenaica

Aquilegia vulgaris subsp.

pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean columbine, is a perennial herb native to the Pyrenees mountain range. It features striking blue to purple spurred petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with compound leaves divided into 5–9 leaflets. It thrives in woodland and meadow habitats, preferring well-drained, loamy soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in temperate regions.
