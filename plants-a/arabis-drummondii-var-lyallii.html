Arabis drummondii var. lyallii

Arabis drummondii var. lyallii, commonly known as Lyall's rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately divided leaves. This species typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or arid environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, rounded seed pods that split open to release its seeds.

The plant is generally hardy and adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient wildflower.
