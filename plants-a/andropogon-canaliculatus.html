Andropogon canaliculatus

Andropogon canaliculatus, commonly known as narrow-ribbed bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and prominent, narrow leaf veins. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Andropogon canaliculatus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
