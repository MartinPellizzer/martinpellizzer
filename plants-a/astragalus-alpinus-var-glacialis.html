Astragalus alpinus var. glacialis

Astragalus alpinus var. glacialis, commonly known as the alpine milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus alpinus var. glacialis is adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in mountainous regions.
