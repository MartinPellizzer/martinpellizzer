Atriplex monoica

Atriplex monoica, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent leaves, which are adapted to store water and reduce transpiration in dry environments. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in saline and poor soils. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Atriplex monoica plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
