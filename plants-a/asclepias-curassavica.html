Asclepias curassavica

Asclepias curassavica, commonly known as the milkweed, is a tropical milkweed native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of vibrant orange or red flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains toxic compounds, which deter herbivores. It is a crucial host plant for monarch butterfly larvae, providing them with nourishment and protection.

Asclepias curassavica is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and full sun conditions.
