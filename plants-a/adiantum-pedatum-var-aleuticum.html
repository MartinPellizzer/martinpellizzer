Adiantum pedatum var. aleuticum

Adiantum pedatum var. aleuticum, commonly known as the Aleut maidenhair fern, is a variant of the eastern maidenhair fern native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are typically dark green and have a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its rhizomes are slender and produce a dense network of roots that help anchor it to rocky or soil substrates.

This fern is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
