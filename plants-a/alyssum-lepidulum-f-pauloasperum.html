Alyssum lepidulum f. pauloasperum

Alyssum lepidulum f. pauloasperum, commonly known as the roughleaf alyssum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The leaves are typically hairy and have a rough texture, which gives the plant its common name. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.
