Allium dalmaticum

Allium dalmaticum, commonly known as the Dalmatian garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, rounded bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium dalmaticum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
