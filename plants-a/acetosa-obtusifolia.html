Acetosa obtusifolia

Acetosa obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has rounded, fleshy leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and along stream banks.

Acetosa obtusifolia is often found in nutrient-rich soils and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
