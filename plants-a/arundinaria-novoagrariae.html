Arundinaria novoagrariae

Arundinaria novoagrariae, commonly known as the New Guinea bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, and its leaves are long and narrow with a smooth texture. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
