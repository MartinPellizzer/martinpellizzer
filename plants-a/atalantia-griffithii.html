Atalantia griffithii

Atalantia griffithii, commonly known as the Indian frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange-yellow, fleshy fruits. Its latex, which is milky and aromatic, has been traditionally used in folk medicine.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its historical significance in traditional herbal practices.
