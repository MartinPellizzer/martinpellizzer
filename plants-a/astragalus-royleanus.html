Astragalus royleanus

Astragalus royleanus, commonly known as the Himalayan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to cold and arid environments, often growing in rocky or alpine habitats. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it plays a role in soil stabilization in its native range.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is also of interest in traditional herbal medicine.
