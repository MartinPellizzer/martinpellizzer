Amygdalus communis f. platycarpa

Amygdalus communis f. platycarpa, commonly known as the flat-seeded peach.

This variety is a botanical variant of the peach tree, characterized by its flattened, less rounded fruits compared to the typical peach. The plant is native to regions with temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil. It produces edible fruits that are often used in culinary applications.

The botanical features of this form include its distinctive fruit morphology and its genetic relationship to the common peach.
