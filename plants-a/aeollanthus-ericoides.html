Aeollanthus ericoides

Aeollanthus ericoides, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing shrub native to South America, particularly found in the Andean regions of Peru and Bolivia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically features small, leathery leaves and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
