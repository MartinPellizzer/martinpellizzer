Aristea majubensis

Aristea majubensis, commonly known as the Cape aristea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to arid environments. Its small, strap-like leaves are usually blue-gray in color, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant yellow flowers.

Aristea majubensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
