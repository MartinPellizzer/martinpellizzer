Aceras anthropophorum f. purpurata

Aceras anthropophorum f. purpurata, commonly known as the purple-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with a distinctive pinkish hue. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators.
