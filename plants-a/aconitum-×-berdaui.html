Aconitum × berdaui

Aconitum × berdaui, commonly known as the hybrid monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, helmet-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring palmately divided leaves and a robust root system. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Aconitum × berdaui is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its dramatic floral display.
