Anthemis tinctoria var. australis

Anthemis tinctoria var. australis, commonly known as the Australian tansy, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow-orange flower heads. The plant produces small, composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its historical use in dyeing textiles and as a medicinal herb highlights its botanical and cultural significance.
