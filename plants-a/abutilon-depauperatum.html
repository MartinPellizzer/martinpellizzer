Abutilon depauperatum

Abutilon depauperatum, commonly known as the poor man's abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. Its fibrous roots and woody stems are adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in arid conditions.
