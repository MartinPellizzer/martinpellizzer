Anguria pedisecta

Anguria pedisecta, commonly known as the splitfoot cucumber, is a unique species within the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that resemble the shape of a foot, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pale green fruits that are typically harvested when immature. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rare occurrence, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental and scientific value.
