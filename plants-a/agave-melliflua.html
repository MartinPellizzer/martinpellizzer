Agave melliflua

Agave melliflua, commonly known as the honey plant, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears numerous small, white flowers, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. Its scientific name, "melliflua," refers to its nectar-rich flowers, which contribute to its role as a valuable honey source.

Agave melliflua is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
