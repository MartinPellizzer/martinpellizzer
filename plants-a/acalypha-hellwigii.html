Acalypha hellwigii

Acalypha hellwigii, commonly known as the silverleaf acalypha, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features striking, silver-white to blue-green foliage that is often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or reddish in color, followed by spherical, fuzzy seed capsules. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Acalypha hellwigii is also valued for its ability to tolerate coastal conditions and air pollution, enhancing its adaptability in various environments.
