Amorphophallus abyssinicus

Amorphophallus abyssinicus, commonly known as the Abyssinian arrowhead, is a striking plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a distinctive, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant produces a fleshy, underground tuber that serves as its storage organ. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is known for its rapid growth and ability to regenerate from its tuber.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
