Aechmea brassicoides

Aechmea brassicoides, commonly known as the yellow pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features a rosette-shaped leaf structure with a central rosette that collects water, forming a small pool that supports aquatic life. The leaves are typically green with a waxy coating, and the plant produces vibrant orange or red flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Aechmea brassicoides is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea brassicoides has rosette-shaped leaves that are long, narrow, and sword-like, arranged in a tight spiral around the central stem, with a waxy blue-green color and a hairy texture.

The plant has a short, thick stem that is covered in a thick, fibrous, and sometimes reddish-brown bark, growing upright from the base. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix surrounded by a colorful bract, with small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the summer months and are pollinated by insects. The fruit is a berry that contains small, black seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.
