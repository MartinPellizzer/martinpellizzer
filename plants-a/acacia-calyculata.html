Acacia calyculata

Acacia calyculata, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in rounded clusters. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood and has been used traditionally in various applications.
