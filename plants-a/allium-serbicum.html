Allium serbicum

Allium serbicum, commonly known as the Serbian leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and clusters of grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers in umbel-like clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic-like flavor, making it a valuable addition to local cuisines.

Allium serbicum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and culinary uses.
