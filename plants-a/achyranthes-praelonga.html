Achyranthes praelonga

Achyranthes praelonga, commonly known as the long-stalked knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. Its roots are fibrous and can be used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is often found in moist, disturbed soils in open woodlands and along stream banks.
