Aframomum stipulatum

Aframomum stipulatum, commonly known as the African cardamom, is a tropical plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark red to black berries containing small, aromatic seeds. Its rhizomes and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its essential oils.
