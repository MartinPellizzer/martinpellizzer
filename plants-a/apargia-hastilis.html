Apargia hastilis

Apargia hastilis, commonly known as the slender false dragonhead, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in open woodlands. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink to purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Apargia hastilis is noted for its delicate appearance and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
