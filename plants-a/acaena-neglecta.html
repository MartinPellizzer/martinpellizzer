Acaena neglecta

Acaena neglecta, commonly known as the New Zealand burr bush, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its small, spiny fruits that contain seeds. The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a hairy texture.

Acaena neglecta is known for its ability to form dense thickets, which can be both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in non-native environments.
