Allophylus insignis

Allophylus insignis, commonly known as the African snowball, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white flowers that bloom in winter, giving it a striking appearance against the backdrop of the dry season. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a slight pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Allophylus insignis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscape design for its winter interest.
