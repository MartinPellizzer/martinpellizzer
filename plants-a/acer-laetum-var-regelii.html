Acer laetum var. regelii

Acer laetum var. regelii, commonly known as the Regel's maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The tree produces small, red samaras that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for cooler climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape tree.
