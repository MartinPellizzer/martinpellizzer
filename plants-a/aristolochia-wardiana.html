Aristolochia wardiana

Aristolochia wardiana, commonly known as the purple waxflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, and often fragrant flowers that range in color from purple to white. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex, winding floral structure that resembles a pipe or horn. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
