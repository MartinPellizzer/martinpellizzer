Astragalus saganlugensis

Astragalus saganlugensis, commonly known as the Saganluk milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus saganlugensis is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
