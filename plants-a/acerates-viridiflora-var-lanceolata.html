Acerates viridiflora var. lanceolata

Acerates viridiflora var. lanceolata, commonly known as the green-flowered maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lanceolate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, which are clustered in panicles. Its fruits are samaras, or winged seeds, that are dispersed by wind.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its foliage and seasonal interest.
