Astragalus missouriensis var. humistratus

Astragalus missouriensis var. humistratus, commonly known as the Missouri milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the central United States.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are usually hairy.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest to ecologists and botanists studying native plant species.
