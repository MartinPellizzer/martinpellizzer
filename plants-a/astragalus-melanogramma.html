Astragalus melanogramma

Astragalus melanogramma, commonly known as the black-veined milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and the seeds are enclosed in hairy pods.

Astragalus melanogramma plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native range.
