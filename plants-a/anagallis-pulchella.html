Anagallis pulchella

Anagallis pulchella, commonly known as the pretty willow or pink willow, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces delicate, spurred flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often prostrate or trailing.

Anagallis pulchella thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.
