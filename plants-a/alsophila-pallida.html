Alsophila pallida

Alsophila pallida, commonly known as the pale tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive crown of pinnate fronds that give it a lush, tropical appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Alsophila pallida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
