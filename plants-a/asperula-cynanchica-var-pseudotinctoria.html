Asperula cynanchica var. pseudotinctoria

Asperula cynanchica var. pseudotinctoria, commonly known as the purple-flowered asperula, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of delicate, violet to purple flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
