Aster hispidus var. hispidus

Aster hispidus var. hispidus, commonly known as the roughleaf aster, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. The plant features hairy, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers in the fall. Its flowering period occurs from late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
