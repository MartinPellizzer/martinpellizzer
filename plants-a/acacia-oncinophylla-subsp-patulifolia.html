Acacia oncinophylla subsp. patulifolia

Acacia oncinophylla subsp.

patulifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its yellow floral spikes and broad, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
