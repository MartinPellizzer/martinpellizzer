Aeschynanthus bracteatus

Aeschynanthus bracteatus, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or flame vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from orange to red, often resembling small tangerines. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and contain a single seed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms.

Aeschynanthus bracteatus prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and indoor gardens.
