Androsace carnea var. pubescens

Androsace carnea var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy pink rock rose, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features dense clusters of pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically broad, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of soft, hairy pubescence. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often used in alpine gardens for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
