Aldrovanda

Aldrovanda, commonly known as the waterwheel plant, is a carnivorous plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of tiny, snap-trap leaves that rapidly close around prey. The plant's stems are slender and often float just below the water's surface. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its unique trapping mechanism and delicate appearance make it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
