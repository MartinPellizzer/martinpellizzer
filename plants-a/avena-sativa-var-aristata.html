Avena sativa var. aristata

Avena sativa var. aristata, commonly known as the slender oat, is a variant of the common oat characterized by its elongated, slender leaves and reduced inflorescence.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of florets that are adapted for wind pollination. Its seeds are enclosed in achenes, which are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural distribution.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for soil stabilization and wildlife habitat.
