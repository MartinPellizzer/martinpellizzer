Acacia masliniana

Acacia masliniana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging branches and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are typically pollinated by birds and insects. Acacia masliniana thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
