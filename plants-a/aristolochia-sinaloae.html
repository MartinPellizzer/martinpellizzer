Aristolochia sinaloae

Aristolochia sinaloae, commonly known as the Sinaloa birthwort, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular, and often fragrant flowers that resemble a pipe or horn, which is a defining feature of the Aristolochiaceae family. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is typically found in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, and the plant contains various bioactive compounds.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
