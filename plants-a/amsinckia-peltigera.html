Amsinckia peltigera

Amsinckia peltigera, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender stems and small, pinnately compound leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow to orange in color, arranged in dense, branched clusters, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Amsinckia peltigera is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
