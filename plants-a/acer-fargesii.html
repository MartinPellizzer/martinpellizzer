Acer fargesii

Acer fargesii, commonly known as the Chinese maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its vibrant autumn foliage, which displays shades of red, orange, and yellow. The tree has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its bark is smooth and grayish-brown. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
