Agave attenuata var. paucibracteata

Agave attenuata var. paucibracteata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features long, narrow, blue-green leaves that are often lined with sharp spines. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
