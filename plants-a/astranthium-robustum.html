Astranthium robustum

Astranthium robustum, commonly known as the robust mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive heart-shaped base.

Astranthium robustum produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.
