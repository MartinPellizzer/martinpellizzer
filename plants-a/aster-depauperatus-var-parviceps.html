Aster depauperatus var. parviceps

Aster depauperatus var. parviceps, commonly known as the small-headed aster, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense, spherical heads, and bloom in late summer to early fall.

The species is noted for its low-growing habit and is often found in disturbed or poorly drained soils.
