Angelica minor

Angelica minor, commonly known as lesser angelica, is a biennial or perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided leaves and umbel-shaped flowers. The plant grows up to 1.5 meters tall, with a tall, hollow stem and aromatic, slightly toxic sap. Its roots are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent, while the seeds are often used in herbal remedies.

Angelica minor thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.
