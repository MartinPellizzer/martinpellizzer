Abutilon amoenum

Abutilon amoenum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Abutilon amoenum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
