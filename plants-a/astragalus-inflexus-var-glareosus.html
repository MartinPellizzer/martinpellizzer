Astragalus inflexus var. glareosus

Astragalus inflexus var. glareosus, commonly known as the golden milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to golden pea-like flowers. Its pods are slender and contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinators.
