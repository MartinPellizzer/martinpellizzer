Acacia verticillata var. riceana

Acacia verticillata var. riceana, commonly known as the Ricean wattle, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in poor soil.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
