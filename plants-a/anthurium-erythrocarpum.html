Anthurium erythrocarpum

Anthurium erythrocarpum, commonly known as the red-fruited anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-shaped inflorescence. The plant produces bright red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Anthurium erythrocarpum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage and unique floral structure.
