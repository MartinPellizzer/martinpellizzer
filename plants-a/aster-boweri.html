Aster boweri

Aster boweri, commonly known as the Bower's aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster boweri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
