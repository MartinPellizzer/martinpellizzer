Aster confinis

Aster confinis, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its narrow, linear leaves and showy purple to blue flowers make it a distinctive and ornamental species.

Aster confinis is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and wildlife.
