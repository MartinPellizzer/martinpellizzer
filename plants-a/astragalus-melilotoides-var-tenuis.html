Astragalus melilotoides var. tenuis

Astragalus melilotoides var. tenuis, commonly known as narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are compound, with narrow, trifoliate leaflets that are often hairy.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
