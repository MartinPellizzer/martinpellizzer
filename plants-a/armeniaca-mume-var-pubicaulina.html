Armeniaca mume var. pubicaulina

Armeniaca mume var. pubicaulina, commonly known as the Chinese date or jujube, is a deciduous fruit tree native to China.

It produces edible, sweet, and fleshy fruits that are rich in nutrients such as vitamin C and antioxidants. The plant has a deep root system and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, while its flowers are small and fragrant.

The fruit develops from the flower and typically ripens in late summer to early autumn.
