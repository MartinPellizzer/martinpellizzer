Acacia depressa

Acacia depressa, commonly known as the low acacia, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions.

Acacia depressa is often used in landscaping for its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
