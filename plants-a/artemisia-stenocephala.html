Artemisia stenocephala

Artemisia stenocephala, commonly known as narrow-headed wormwood, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in dense clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Artemisia stenocephala is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
