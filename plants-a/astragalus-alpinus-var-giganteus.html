Astragalus alpinus var. giganteus

Astragalus alpinus var. giganteus, commonly known as the giant alpine milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This species is adapted to harsh alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.
