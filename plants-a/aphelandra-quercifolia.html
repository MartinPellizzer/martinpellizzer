Aphelandra quercifolia

Aphelandra quercifolia, commonly known as the oak-leaf arum, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that resemble the shape of an oak leaf, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white, spadix-like flowers surrounded by a showy, greenish-white spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Aphelandra quercifolia is a member of the Araceae family, known for its unique floral structures and adaptability to various growing conditions.
