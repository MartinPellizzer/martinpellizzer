Acianthera laxa

Acianthera laxa, commonly known as the loose beard orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America.

It typically grows in grasslands and open areas, often in regions with moderate to high rainfall. The plant features a slender, hairy stem that supports a cluster of small, pale green to yellowish flowers. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the soil.

The species is notable for its delicate appearance and is often found in association with other orchid species in its habitat.
