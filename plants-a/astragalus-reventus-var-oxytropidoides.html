Astragalus reventus var. oxytropidoides

Astragalus reventus var. oxytropidoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish in color, and it produces small, hard pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and drought tolerance.
