Aphelexis macrantha var. rosea

Aphelexis macrantha var. rosea, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and the stems are erect and branching.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in drought conditions.
