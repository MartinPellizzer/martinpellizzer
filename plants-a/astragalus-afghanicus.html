Astragalus afghanicus

Astragalus afghanicus, commonly known as Afghan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, including Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with deep roots and drought tolerance. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are also a food source for various wildlife species.
