Astragalus isabellae

Astragalus isabellae, commonly known as the Isabella milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus isabellae is often found in arid regions and is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient soils.
