Asystasia macrocarpa

Asystasia macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited asystasia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, or purple, often arranged in spikes. Its fruits are capsules that contain numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Asystasia macrocarpa is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display.
