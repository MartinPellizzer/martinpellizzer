Aster nigromontanus

Aster nigromontanus, commonly known as the black mountain aster, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Appalachian Mountains.

It is characterized by its dark purple to blackish flower heads, which are composed of numerous small, tubular florets. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Aster nigromontanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
