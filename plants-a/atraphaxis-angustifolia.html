Atraphaxis angustifolia

Atraphaxis angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf desert bladder, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, narrow leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep roots that help it access water from underground sources. Its structure allows it to store moisture efficiently, making it resilient to drought conditions.

Atraphaxis angustifolia plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and providing habitat for small desert animals.
