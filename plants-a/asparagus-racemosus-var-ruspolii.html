Asparagus racemosus var. ruspolii

Asparagus racemosus var. ruspolii, commonly known as wild asparagus or Indian asparagus, is a climbing shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into fleshy, red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various soil types and climates.
