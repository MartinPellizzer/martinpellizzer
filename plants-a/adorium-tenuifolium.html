Adorium tenuifolium

Adorium tenuifolium, commonly known as the slender-leafed adorium, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the humid tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep magenta, typically blooming during the late spring and early summer. Its root system is fibrous and shallow, making it well adapted to moist, well-drained soils.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Adorium tenuifolium is now considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
