Astragalus monspessulanus var. atticus

Astragalus monspessulanus var. atticus, commonly known as the Attic milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
