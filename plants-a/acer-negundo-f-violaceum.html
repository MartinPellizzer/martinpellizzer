Acer negundo f. violaceum

Acer negundo f. violaceum, commonly known as the purple-leaf silver maple, is a variant of the silver maple tree characterized by its striking purple foliage.

It is native to North America and typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often displaying a vibrant purple hue in the fall. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This ornamental tree is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
