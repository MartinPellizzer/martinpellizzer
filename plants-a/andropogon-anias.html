Andropogon anias

Andropogon anias, commonly known as silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and tufted inflorescence, which consists of compact clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in full sun and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a common sight in open woodlands and prairies. Its growth habit is clump-forming, with upright stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Andropogon anias is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
