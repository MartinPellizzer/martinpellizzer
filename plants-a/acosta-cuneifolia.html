Acosta cuneifolia

Acosta cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acosta, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
