Artocarpus longifolius

Artocarpus longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaf breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The tree produces a fruit that resembles a breadfruit but is smaller and has a different texture and flavor. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female inflorescences, and it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

The plant is valued for its ornamental leaves and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
