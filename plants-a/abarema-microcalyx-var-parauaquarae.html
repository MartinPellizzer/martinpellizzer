Abarema microcalyx var. parauaquarae

Abarema microcalyx var. parauaquarae, commonly known as the Parauaqueira velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are divided into five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by bean-like pods that contain the seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
