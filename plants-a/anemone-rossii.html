Anemone rossii

Anemone rossii, commonly known as the Pacific anemone or Ross's anemone, is a species of flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive, hairy center, and its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in areas with rich, well-drained soil.

Anemone rossii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
