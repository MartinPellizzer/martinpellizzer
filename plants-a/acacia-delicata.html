Acacia delicata

Acacia delicata, commonly known as the delicate acacia, is a shrub native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched structure and small, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which attract various pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are enclosed in pod-like fruits that split open when mature.

Acacia delicata is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
