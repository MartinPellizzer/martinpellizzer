Amomum pyramidosphaera

Amomum pyramidosphaera, commonly known as the pyramid-shaped cardamom, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, known for its aromatic and medicinal properties.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces tall, slender stems with lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and the fruit is a spherical, three-lobed capsule containing aromatic seeds.

The plant is valued for its essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.
