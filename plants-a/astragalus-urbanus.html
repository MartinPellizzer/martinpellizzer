Astragalus urbanus

Astragalus urbanus, commonly known as the urban milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and urban areas, and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.

Astragalus urbanus is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which benefits soil fertility.
