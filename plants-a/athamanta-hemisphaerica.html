Athamanta hemisphaerica

Athamanta hemisphaerica, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in dry or semi-arid environments. The plant features a short, upright stem with clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually violet or blue in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Athamanta hemisphaerica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
