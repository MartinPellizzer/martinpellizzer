Actinidia kengiana

Actinidia kengiana, commonly known as the Keng's actinidia, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It belongs to the Actinidiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food for wildlife.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
