Acaena microphylla var. inermis

Acaena microphylla var. inermis, commonly known as the silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It features dense clusters of small, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by spiny, berry-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
