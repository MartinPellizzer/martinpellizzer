Astragalus hoantchy subsp. dshimensis

Astragalus hoantchy subsp.

dshimensis, commonly known as the Tibetan milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant is adapted to harsh, arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in alpine ecosystems.
