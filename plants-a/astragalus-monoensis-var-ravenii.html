Astragalus monoensis var. ravenii

Astragalus monoensis var. ravenii, commonly known as Raven's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces small, yellowish to reddish pea-like flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Astragalus monoensis var. ravenii is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.
