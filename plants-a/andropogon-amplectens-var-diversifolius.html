Andropogon amplectens var. diversifolius

Andropogon amplectens var. diversifolius, commonly known as the variegated bluestem, is a flowering grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, blade-like leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in dense, spike-like inflorescences during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy choice for native gardens.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a popular plant for wildlife habitats and landscape design.
