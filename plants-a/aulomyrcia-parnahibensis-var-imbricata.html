Aulomyrcia parnahibensis var. imbricata

Aulomyrcia parnahibensis var. imbricata, commonly known as the Parnaiba palm, is a species native to the Brazilian Atlantic Forest.

It is a small to medium-sized palm characterized by its pinnate leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within the understory of dense tropical forests. Its botanical features include a short trunk and compound leaves with a distinctive, overlapping arrangement of leaflets.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
