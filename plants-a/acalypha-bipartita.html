Acalypha bipartita

Acalypha bipartita, commonly known as the two-parted glory flower, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble petals, often in shades of red, yellow, or orange. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are smooth and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Acalypha bipartita is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
