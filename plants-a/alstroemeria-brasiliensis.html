Alstroemeria brasiliensis

Alstroemeria brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian lily, is a flowering plant native to the subtropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with strap-like, grassy leaves. Its flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their vibrant hues and long-lasting blooms.

Alstroemeria brasiliensis thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
