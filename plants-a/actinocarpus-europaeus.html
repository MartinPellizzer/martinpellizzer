Actinocarpus europaeus

Actinocarpus europaeus, commonly known as the European actinocarpus, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family, which includes roses and cherries, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its fruits are small, dry, and contain a single seed, typical of the Rosaceae family.

Actinocarpus europaeus is often found in woodland edges and rocky soils, thriving in temperate climates.
