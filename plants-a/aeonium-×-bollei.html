Aeonium × bollei

Aeonium × bollei, commonly known as the "Crimson Crown," is a hybrid succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically red or pink in color, especially when exposed to bright sunlight. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are capable of storing water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

Aeonium × bollei is popular as a ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
