Aloysia velutina

Aloysia velutina, commonly known as velvet honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety hair, giving it its common name. Its fragrant, tubular flowers are usually pink or red and bloom in the summer months.

Aloysia velutina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and showy blossoms.
