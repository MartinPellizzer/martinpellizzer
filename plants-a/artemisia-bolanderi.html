Artemisia bolanderi

Artemisia bolanderi, commonly known as Bolander's sage, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and aromatic, pungent scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flower clusters that bloom in the summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal sage scrub habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is a valuable component of its native ecosystem.
