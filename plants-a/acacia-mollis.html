Acacia mollis

Acacia mollis, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and feathery, finely divided leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in globular inflorescences, which are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silky hair, contributing to its common name.

Acacia mollis is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid regions.
