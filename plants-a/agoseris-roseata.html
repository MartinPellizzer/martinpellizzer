Agoseris roseata

Agoseris roseata, commonly known as the pink hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its basal leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stem is erect and branched.

Agoseris roseata is often found in dry, sunny environments and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
