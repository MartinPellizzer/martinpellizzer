Allardia glabra

Allardia glabra, commonly known as the smooth allardia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in open woodlands. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Allardia glabra is often used in native plant gardens due to its ecological value and ornamental appeal.
