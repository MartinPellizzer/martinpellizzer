Asplenium conchatum var. squamulosum

Asplenium conchatum var. squamulosum, commonly known as the scalloped maidenhair fern, is a fern species native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often display a scalloped or wavy edge. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rock crevices. Its rhizomes are short and often covered with small, scale-like structures.

The fronds are usually dark green and may have a slightly glossy appearance.
