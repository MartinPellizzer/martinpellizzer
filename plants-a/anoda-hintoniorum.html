Anoda hintoniorum

Anoda hintoniorum, commonly known as the Hinton's bluemouth, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, legume-like pods that contain its seeds.

Anoda hintoniorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
