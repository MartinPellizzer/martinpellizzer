Ascolepis elata

Ascolepis elata, commonly known as the slender asclepias, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release tiny seeds attached to silky filaments, aiding in wind dispersal. It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Ascolepis elata is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
