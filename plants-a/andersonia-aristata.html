Andersonia aristata

Andersonia aristata, commonly known as the mountain heath, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to withstand cold and dry conditions.

Andersonia aristata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to rock gardens and alpine settings.
