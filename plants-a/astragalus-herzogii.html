Astragalus herzogii

Astragalus herzogii, commonly known as Herzog's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
