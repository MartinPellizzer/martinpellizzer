Allium gemiciana

Allium gemiciana, commonly known as the purple garlic.

It is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, purple flowers in the summer. Its bulbs are similar to garlic but are less commonly cultivated for culinary use.

Allium gemiciana is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
