Actinodaphne molochina

Actinodaphne molochina, commonly known as the Moluccan laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its bark is smooth and often has a distinct aromatic scent, while its leaves are glossy and lance-shaped.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that contains a single seed, contributing to its ecological importance in tropical forests.
