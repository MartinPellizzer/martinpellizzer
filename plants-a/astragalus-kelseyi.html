Astragalus kelseyi

Astragalus kelseyi, commonly known as Kelse's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, leathery pods that contain several seeds, typical of many legumes. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Astragalus kelseyi is often found in steppe and desert habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
