Anemopaegma grandifolium

Anemopaegma grandifolium, commonly known as the large-leaved anemopaegma, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
