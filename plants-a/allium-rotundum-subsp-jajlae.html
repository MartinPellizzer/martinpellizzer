Allium rotundum subsp. jajlae

Allium rotundum subsp.

jajlae, commonly known as Jajla garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the highlands of central Asia. It produces small, rounded bulbs with a pungent, garlicky aroma.

The plant features grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its edible bulbs. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This subspecies is valued both for its culinary uses and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
