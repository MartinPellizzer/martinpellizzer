Archeria traversii var. traversii

Archeria traversii var. traversii, commonly known as the slender arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in humid environments. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, a common feature in the Araceae family.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in water gardens.
