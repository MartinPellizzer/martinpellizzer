Anthemis muricata

Anthemis muricata, commonly known as the rough anthemis, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow to orange ray flowers surrounded by a dense cluster of tubular florets. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a rough, hairy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Anthemis muricata is often found in rocky or dry soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
