Arundinaria fauriei

Arundinaria fauriei, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or Faurie's bamboo, is a species of grass native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces panicles of small, greenish-white flowers, which are typical of the grass family. Its rhizomes are fibrous and contribute to its structural strength.

Arundinaria fauriei is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
