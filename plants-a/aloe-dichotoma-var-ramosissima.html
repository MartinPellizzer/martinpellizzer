Aloe dichotoma var. ramosissima

Aloe dichotoma var. ramosissima, commonly known as the "Giant Barrel Cactus," is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, segmented stems that form a rounded, barrel-like shape. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom at the top of the stems during the rainy season. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it stores water in its stem for survival in arid conditions.

It is well adapted to hot, dry climates and is often used in traditional medicine.
