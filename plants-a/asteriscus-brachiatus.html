Asteriscus brachiatus

Asteriscus brachiatus, commonly known as the creeping aster or Brazilian aster, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant features opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Asteriscus brachiatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
