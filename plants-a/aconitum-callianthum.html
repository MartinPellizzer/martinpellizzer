Aconitum callianthum

Aconitum callianthum, commonly known as the Pacific woad, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, spikelike clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a rough, hairy texture.

Aconitum callianthum is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
