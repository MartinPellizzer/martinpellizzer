Astragalus khalifatensis

Astragalus khalifatensis, commonly known as the Khalifatensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has compound leaves with small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry, sandy environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
