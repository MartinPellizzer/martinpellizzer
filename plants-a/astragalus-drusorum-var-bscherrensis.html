Astragalus drusorum var. bscherrensis

Astragalus drusorum var. bscherrensis, commonly known as the Alpine milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in mountainous environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
