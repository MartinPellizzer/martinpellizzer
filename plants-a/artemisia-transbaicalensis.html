Artemisia transbaicalensis

Artemisia transbaicalensis, commonly known as the Transbaikal wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Mongolia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late summer. The plant contains essential oils and various bioactive compounds, making it valuable in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, arctic environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for use in herbal remedies.
