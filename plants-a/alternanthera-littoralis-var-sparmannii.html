Alternanthera littoralis var. sparmannii

Alternanthera littoralis var. sparmannii, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, pink to lavender flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically small, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs. This species thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and salt marshes.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
