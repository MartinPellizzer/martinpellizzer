Acanthophyllum recurvum

Acanthophyllum recurvum, commonly known as the sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that thrives in sandy and gravelly soils. The plant features dense clusters of small, spiny leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, appearing in late spring to early summer.

Acanthophyllum recurvum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
