Artemisia ferganensis

Artemisia ferganensis, commonly known as the Fergana wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant has aromatic, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, and its essential oils have been used in traditional medicine.

Artemisia ferganensis is valued for its adaptability to harsh environments and its potential medicinal properties.
