Achyranthes flabellifera

Achyranthes flabellifera, commonly known as the Indian knotweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in wetlands and marshy areas. The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually green with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are typically greenish or reddish in color.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and diuretic effects.
