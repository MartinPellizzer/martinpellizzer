Astragalus sinaicus

Astragalus sinaicus, commonly known as the Sinai milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East, particularly the Sinai Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and can thrive in harsh, semi-arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
