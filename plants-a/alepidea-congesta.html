Alepidea congesta

Alepidea congesta, commonly known as the Cape mountain aloes, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, spiny leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear orange-red flowers during the winter months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
