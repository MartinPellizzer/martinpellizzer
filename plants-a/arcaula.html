Arcaula

Arcaula, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, white, nocturnal blooms that open at night and close by dawn. The plant belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is known for its fragrant, sweet scent. Arcaula thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its unique blooming pattern makes it a popular subject in botanical studies and horticulture.
