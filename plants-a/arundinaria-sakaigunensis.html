Arundinaria sakaigunensis

Arundinaria sakaigunensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps, typical of many bamboo species. The plant is native to Japan and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Arundinaria sakaigunensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
