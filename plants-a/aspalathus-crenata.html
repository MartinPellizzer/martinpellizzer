Aspalathus crenata

Aspalathus crenata, commonly known as the small-leaf rooibos, is a small shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaves and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to grow in sandy, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant. It is closely related to other members of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes.

Aspalathus crenata is primarily cultivated for its leaves, which are used to produce a unique type of herbal tea.
