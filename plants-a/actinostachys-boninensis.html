Actinostachys boninensis

Actinostachys boninensis, commonly known as the Bonin grass or Bonin reed, is a flowering plant native to the Bonin Islands.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. Its botanical characteristics are adapted to thrive in humid, coastal conditions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
