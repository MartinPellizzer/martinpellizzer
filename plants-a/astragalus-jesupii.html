Astragalus jesupii

Astragalus jesupii, commonly known as Jesup's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as rocky slopes and dry grasslands. It has small, hairy stems and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus jesupii is often found in areas with well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
