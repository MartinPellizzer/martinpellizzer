Acantholimon compactum

Acantholimon compactum, commonly known as the dwarf flaxflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, compact growth with silver-gray foliage that helps it retain moisture in arid conditions. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers in early spring, adding a delicate touch to rocky or sandy soils. It is well adapted to coastal environments and thrives in full sun.

Acantholimon compactum is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
