Alsophila aquilina

Alsophila aquilina, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It features a tall, slender trunk with a crown of feathery, dark green fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant's fronds are dimorphic, with sterile and fertile fronds differing in size and shape. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Alsophila aquilina plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various forest organisms.
