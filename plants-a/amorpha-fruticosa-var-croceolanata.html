Amorpha fruticosa var. croceolanata

Amorpha fruticosa var. croceolanata, commonly known as the yellow false indigo, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in a grayish bloom. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late spring, which are highly attractive to pollinators. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a smooth, lance-shaped appearance.

The species is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
