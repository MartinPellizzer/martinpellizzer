Aphelandra limbatifolia

Aphelandra limbatifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed torch plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces striking, pendulous inflorescences with bright yellow bracts that resemble torches. It thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light.

Aphelandra limbatifolia is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and long-lasting blooms.
