Artemisia sacrorum subf. vestita

Artemisia sacrorum subf.

vestita, commonly known as the sacred wormwood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, aromatic stems and narrow, gray-green leaves with a strong, pungent odor.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, typically in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
