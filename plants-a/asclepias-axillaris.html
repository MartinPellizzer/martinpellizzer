Asclepias axillaris

Asclepias axillaris, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with prominent, silky seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It is a member of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is known for its role in supporting monarch butterfly populations.

Asclepias axillaris thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
