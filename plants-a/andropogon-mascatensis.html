Andropogon mascatensis

Andropogon mascatensis, commonly known as the Mascat grass, is a perennial grass native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating remarkable drought tolerance. Its flowering inflorescence consists of a panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers.

Andropogon mascatensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
