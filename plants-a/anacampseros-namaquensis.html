Anacampseros namaquensis

Anacampseros namaquensis, commonly known as the Namaqua groundsel, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
