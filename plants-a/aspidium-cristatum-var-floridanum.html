Aspidium cristatum var. floridanum

Aspidium cristatum var. floridanum, commonly known as the Florida water fern, is a terrestrial fern native to the southeastern United States.

It features a distinctive, dense frond structure with a serrated margin and a creeping rhizome that helps anchor it to moist soil. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in a unique, scaly arrangement. It thrives in shaded, wet environments such as swamps and wetlands, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of these habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for studying the adaptation of ferns to humid, low-light conditions.
