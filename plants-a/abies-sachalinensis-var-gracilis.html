Abies sachalinensis var. gracilis

Abies sachalinensis var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender firs, is a variant of the Sakhalin fir, native to the northern regions of Japan and the Russian Far East.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and slender, dark green needles. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate, making it a long-lived species with a dense, compact form. Its foliage is typically arranged in spirals along the shoots, contributing to its elegant appearance.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique morphology and adaptability to cooler climates.
