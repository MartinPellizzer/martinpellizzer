Alyssum granatense var. subminutulum

Alyssum granatense var. subminutulum, commonly known as the dwarf alpine alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features compact, mat-forming growth with small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically blooms in early summer, producing numerous flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely packed, giving it a tidy appearance.

Alyssum granatense var. subminutulum is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it suitable for alpine gardens and rockeries.
