Acer campestre var. deflexum

Acer campestre var. deflexum, commonly known as the creeping maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with multiple stems that often form dense thickets. The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a coarsely toothed margin and a bright green upper surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by samaras that disperse by wind.

This plant thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments and is often found in woodland edges or rocky soils.
