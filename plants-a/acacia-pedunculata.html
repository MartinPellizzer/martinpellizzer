Acacia pedunculata

Acacia pedunculata, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spikes. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its bark is fibrous and often exfoliates, revealing a smooth, pale inner layer.

Acacia pedunculata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
