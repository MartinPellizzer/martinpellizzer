Aesculus × rubicunda var. rosea

Aesculus × rubicunda var. rosea, commonly known as the pink horse chestnut, is a hybrid species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pinkish-white bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces large, spiny capsules containing bright red, ornamental seeds. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic value.
