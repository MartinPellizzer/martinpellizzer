Amorphophallus cidarioides

Amorphophallus cidarioides, commonly known as the giant arrowroot or elephant yam, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-shaped inflorescence. The plant produces a starchy, edible tuberous root that is used in various cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant's unique morphology and culinary value make it an important species in both horticulture and agriculture.
