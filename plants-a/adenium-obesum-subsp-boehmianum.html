Adenium obesum subsp. boehmianum

Adenium obesum subsp.

boehmianum, commonly known as the Desert Candle, is a striking succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa. It is characterized by its swollen, bottle-shaped trunk and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the wet season, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its root system is highly adapted for absorbing moisture from the soil, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is a popular specimen in botanical gardens and among plant enthusiasts.
