Alyssum algeriense var. montanum

Alyssum algeriense var. montanum, commonly known as Algerian alpine alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features dense clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
