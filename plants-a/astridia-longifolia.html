Astridia longifolia

Astridia longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf astridia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments, such as forest understories, and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy surface, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

Astridia longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
