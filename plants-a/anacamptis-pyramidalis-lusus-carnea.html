Anacamptis pyramidalis lusus carnea

Anacamptis pyramidalis lusus carnea, commonly known as the pink pyramidal orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its inflorescence is a tall, unbranched spike that bears numerous showy flowers with a distinctive lip.

The leaves are linear and grass-like, forming a basal rosette that helps conserve moisture.
