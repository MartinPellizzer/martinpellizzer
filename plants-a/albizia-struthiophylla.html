Albizia struthiophylla

Albizia struthiophylla, commonly known as the silky albizia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in open woodlands and along riverbanks. The plant produces fragrant, fluffy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Albizia struthiophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
