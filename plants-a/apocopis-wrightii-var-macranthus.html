Apocopis wrightii var. macranthus

Apocopis wrightii var. macranthus, commonly known as the Wright's apocopis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, cylindrical seed heads.

This variant is distinguished by its larger flower heads compared to other varieties within the species.
