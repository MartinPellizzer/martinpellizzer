Ardisia picardae

Ardisia picardae, commonly known as the Picard's beautyberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that give way to bright purple berries in the fall. These berries are an important food source for various bird species.

Ardisia picardae thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
