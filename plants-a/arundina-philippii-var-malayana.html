Arundina philippii var. malayana

Arundina philippii var. malayana, commonly known as the Malay bamboo orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its tall, bamboo-like stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in its native habitats.
