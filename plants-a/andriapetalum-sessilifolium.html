Andriapetalum sessilifolium

Andriapetalum sessilifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved andriapetalum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, sessile leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
