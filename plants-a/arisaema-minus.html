Arisaema minus

Arisaema minus, commonly known as the lesser arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves and produces clusters of greenish-white spathes that enclose spadices. The plant is characterized by its unique underground tubers, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Araceae family.
