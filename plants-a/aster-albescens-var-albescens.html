Aster albescens var. albescens

Aster albescens var. albescens, commonly known as the white aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its white to pale blue flowers bloom in the late summer to early fall, attracting various pollinators.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is often used in ornamental gardening.
