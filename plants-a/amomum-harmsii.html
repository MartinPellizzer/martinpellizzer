Amomum harmsii

Amomum harmsii, commonly known as the Vietnamese cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers followed by dark purple berries, which contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its essential oils, rich in compounds like cineole and limonene, contribute to its medicinal properties.

Amomum harmsii is often cultivated for its therapeutic uses in treating digestive ailments and respiratory conditions.
