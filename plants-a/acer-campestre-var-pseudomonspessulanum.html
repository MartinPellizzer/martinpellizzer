Acer campestre var. pseudomonspessulanum

Acer campestre var. pseudomonspessulanum, commonly known as the False Monspessulanum maple, is a variant of the field maple.

It is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree or shrub with distinctive lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The plant has smooth, greyish-brown bark that becomes fissured with age. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
