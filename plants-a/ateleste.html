Ateleste

Ateleste, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique aquatic plant that thrives in shallow, still waters.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves that resemble ghostly apparitions, giving it its ethereal common name. The plant lacks true roots and instead anchors itself to substrates with its rhizomes, which are often covered in a slimy, gelatinous coating. Ateleste is known for its ability to absorb and filter pollutants from water, making it valuable in ecological restoration projects.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and ability to outcompete native flora.
