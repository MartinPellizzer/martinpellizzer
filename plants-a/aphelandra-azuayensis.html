Aphelandra azuayensis

Aphelandra azuayensis, commonly known as the Azuay flame plant, is a tropical species native to the Andean region of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences. The plant features large, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with yellow and green patterns. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Aphelandra azuayensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
