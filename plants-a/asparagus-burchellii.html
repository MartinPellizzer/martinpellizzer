Asparagus burchellii

Asparagus burchellii, commonly known as Burchell's asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features slender, branched stems that bear small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the spring season. Its berries are red and edible, often consumed by birds and small mammals.

Asparagus burchellii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
