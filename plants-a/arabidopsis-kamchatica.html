Arabidopsis kamchatica

Arabidopsis kamchatica, commonly known as the Kamchatka Arabidopsis, is a small flowering plant native to the Far East of Russia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other well-known plants such as mustard and cabbage. This species is characterized by its delicate, upright growth habit and small white flowers. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cool, temperate climates.

The plant is often used in scientific research due to its genetic simplicity and short life cycle.
