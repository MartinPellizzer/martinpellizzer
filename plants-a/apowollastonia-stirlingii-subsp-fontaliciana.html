Apowollastonia stirlingii subsp. fontaliciana

Apowollastonia stirlingii subsp.

fontaliciana, commonly known as the Fontalica lilly pilly, is a small evergreen shrub native to the humid subtropical regions of eastern Australia. It features dense, glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture.

The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes.
