Aphelandra albinotata

Aphelandra albinotata, commonly known as the white-veined croton, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves with prominent white veins. The plant produces clusters of small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, which are followed by bright orange berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil and partial shade.

Aphelandra albinotata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage.
