Acalypha polynesiaca

Acalypha polynesiaca, commonly known as the Polynesian copperleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with smooth, often glossy leaves. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Acalypha polynesiaca is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and unique floral display.
