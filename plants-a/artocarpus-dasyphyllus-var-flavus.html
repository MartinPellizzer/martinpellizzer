Artocarpus dasyphyllus var. flavus

Artocarpus dasyphyllus var. flavus, commonly known as the golden fig or flavus fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces a distinctive fruit with a rough, green exterior that turns yellow or golden when ripe. The fruit contains a sweet, creamy pulp and is often consumed fresh or used in various culinary applications.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
