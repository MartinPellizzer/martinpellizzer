Agave perplexans

Agave perplexans, commonly known as the Mexican hat plant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of thick, fleshy, blue-green leaves. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer. Agave perplexans is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
