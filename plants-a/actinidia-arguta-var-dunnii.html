Actinidia arguta var. dunnii

Actinidia arguta var. dunnii, commonly known as the Hardy Kiwifruit or Chinese Actinidia, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, fuzzy fruits that ripen in late summer to early autumn, turning from green to orange-yellow. It thrives in temperate climates and requires a cold winter dormancy period for optimal fruit production.

This variety is prized for its hardiness and is often cultivated for both ornamental and edible purposes.
