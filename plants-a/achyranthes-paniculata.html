Achyranthes paniculata

Achyranthes paniculata, commonly known as the Indian knotgrass, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles, which are clustered inflorescences. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Achyranthes paniculata is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
