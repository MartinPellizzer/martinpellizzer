Athyrium niponicum

Athyrium niponicum, commonly known as Japanese painted fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often variegated with shades of white, gold, and green. The plant typically grows in shady, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Athyrium niponicum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in partial shade.
