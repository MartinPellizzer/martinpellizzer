Agelaea brevipaniculata

Agelaea brevipaniculata, commonly known as the short-bracted pandanus, is a tropical plant belonging to the Pandanaceae family.

It is characterized by its robust, fibrous roots and stiff, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and culinary applications. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove swamps or near the shoreline.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts in its native regions.
