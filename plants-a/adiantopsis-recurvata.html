Adiantopsis recurvata

Adiantopsis recurvata, commonly known as the zigzag maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that have a distinctive zigzag pattern along their edges. The plant features small, spoon-shaped pinnae that are closely spaced and give it a lush, dense appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is closely related to other maidenhair ferns.
