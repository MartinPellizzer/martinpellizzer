Astragalus cytisoides

Astragalus cytisoides, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow to orange pea-like flowers. Its seed pods are slender and contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Astragalus cytisoides is adapted to arid conditions and has a deep root system for water absorption.
