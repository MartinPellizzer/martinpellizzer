Aster patagonicus

Aster patagonicus, commonly known as the Patagonian aster, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing a hardy adaptation to dry conditions. Its vibrant purple or blue flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine layer of hair, helping to reduce water loss in its native habitat.
