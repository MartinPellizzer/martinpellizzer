Ardisia botryosa

Ardisia botryosa, commonly known as the clustered beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright red berries that appear in the fall, making it a visually striking plant. The leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy texture. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and gardens.

The plant is valued for its ornamental berries and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
