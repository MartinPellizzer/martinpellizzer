Anabasis aphylla

Anabasis aphylla, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia and Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub or perennial herb that thrives in sandy and rocky soils, often found in desert environments. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss, and it produces yellow or green flowers that bloom in summer. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep underground water sources.

Anabasis aphylla plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
