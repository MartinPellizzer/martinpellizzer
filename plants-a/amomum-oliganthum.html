Amomum oliganthum

Amomum oliganthum, commonly known as the Chinese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other spices like ginger and turmeric. The plant features a rhizomatous stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, typically greenish-white in color.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
