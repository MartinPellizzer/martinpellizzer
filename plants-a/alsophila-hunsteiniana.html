Alsophila hunsteiniana

Alsophila hunsteiniana, commonly known as the Hunstein's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, compound fronds. The plant has a distinctive trunk that is covered in a fibrous, spongy material, which helps it retain moisture. Its fronds are typically arching and can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Alsophila hunsteiniana is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
