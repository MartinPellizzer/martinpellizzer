Atriplex parvifolia var. palaestina

Atriplex parvifolia var. palaestina, commonly known as the Palestine saltbush, is a halophytic shrub adapted to saline and arid environments.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are pollinated by wind. It is native to regions of the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils in degraded areas.
