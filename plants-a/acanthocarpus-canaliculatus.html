Acanthocarpus canaliculatus

Acanthocarpus canaliculatus, commonly known as the narrow-ridged acanthocarp, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in open woodland and grassland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, yellow flowers are followed by narrow, ribbed pods that contain several seeds.

The species is often used in traditional medicine and for forage in livestock grazing.
