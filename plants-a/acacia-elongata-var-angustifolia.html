Acacia elongata var. angustifolia

Acacia elongata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features slender, angular stems and pinnate leaves composed of 7 to 15 elliptic, narrow leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in spring, which are clustered in spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
