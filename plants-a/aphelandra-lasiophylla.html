Aphelandra lasiophylla

Aphelandra lasiophylla, commonly known as the golden torch plant, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that resemble torches, with bright yellow bracts and small white flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Aphelandra lasiophylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant appearance and ease of care.
