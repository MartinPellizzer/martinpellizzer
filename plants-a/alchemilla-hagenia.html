Alchemilla hagenia

Alchemilla hagenia, commonly known as Hagen's lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their astringent properties.

Alchemilla hagenia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.
