Abietia douglasii var. fastigiata

Abietia douglasii var. fastigiata, commonly known as the Douglas fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow growth habit and dense foliage, with needle-like leaves that are blue-green in color. The tree produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against fire and pests.

This species is valued for its timber and is also an important component of forest ecosystems.
