Adiantum gomphophyllum

Adiantum gomphophyllum, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Adiantaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bright green and somewhat glossy. This plant is native to regions of Africa and is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in shaded, moist environments. Its rhizomes are short and its leaves are usually clustered at the base of the plant.

Adiantum gomphophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
