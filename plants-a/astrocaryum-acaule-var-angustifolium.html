Astrocaryum acaule var. angustifolium

Astrocaryum acaule var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buriti, is a palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched stem and long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a fan shape. The plant produces large, yellowish-brown fruits that are encased in a hard, spiny shell. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and for thatching.

The species is valued for its ecological role and its potential in sustainable forestry.
