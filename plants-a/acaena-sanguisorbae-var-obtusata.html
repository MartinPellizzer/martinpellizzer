Acaena sanguisorbae var. obtusata

Acaena sanguisorbae var. obtusata, commonly known as the broad-leaved acacia, is a variant of the species native to New Zealand.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its robust, woody stems and dense clusters of small, spiny fruits. The leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, with a serrated margin. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine environments.

This plant plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
