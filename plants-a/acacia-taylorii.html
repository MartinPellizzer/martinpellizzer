Acacia taylorii

Acacia taylorii, commonly known as the Taylor's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorned branches and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf-like structures that perform photosynthesis, reducing water loss in its harsh environment. It is adapted to survive in low-nutrient soils and can tolerate extreme temperatures.

Acacia taylorii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
