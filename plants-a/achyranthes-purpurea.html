Achyranthes purpurea

Achyranthes purpurea, commonly known as purpleweed, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and alternate leaves that are typically lanceolate or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with greenish-white flowers that are often surrounded by reddish bracts. Its roots are fibrous and can be used in traditional medicine for their purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Achyranthes purpurea is often found in disturbed soils and thrives in temperate climates.
