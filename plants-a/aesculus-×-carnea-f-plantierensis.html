Aesculus × carnea f. plantierensis

Aesculus × carnea f. plantierensis, commonly known as the red horse chestnut, is a hybrid plant that exhibits striking botanical features.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that appear in late spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant produces large, spiny capsules containing glossy, dark brown seeds. Its leaves are palmately compound, consisting of 5–7 ovate leaflets with serrated edges.

Native to the southeastern United States, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.
