Aspalathus tridentata

Aspalathus tridentata, commonly known as the African red bush, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets, and its clusters of small, red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is well adapted to the region's Mediterranean climate, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil. Its scientific name reflects its distinctive leaf structure, with "tridentata" referring to the three-toothed appearance of its foliage.

Aspalathus tridentata is also known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
