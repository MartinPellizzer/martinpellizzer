Alternanthera kurtzii subsp. sclerosperma

Alternanthera kurtzii subsp.

sclerosperma, commonly known as the hard-seeded alternanthera, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America. It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage that ranges from red to purple, often changing with environmental conditions.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, followed by hard, seed-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is popular in aquatic and bog gardens due to its ornamental value and adaptability.
