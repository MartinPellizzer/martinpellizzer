Aidia foveata

Aidia foveata, commonly known as the crested bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, rounded fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Aidia foveata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
