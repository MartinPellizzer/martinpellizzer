Atractylodes chinensis var. simplicifolia

Atractylodes chinensis var. simplicifolia, commonly known as Chinese Atractylodes, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features a stout rhizome with a distinctive aromatic odor and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which are borne on slender peduncles. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or mountainous regions.

This species is valued in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
