Achillea sibirica subsp. japonica

Achillea sibirica subsp.

japonica, commonly known as Japanese yarrow, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and long flowering period make it a valuable addition to ornamental gardens.
