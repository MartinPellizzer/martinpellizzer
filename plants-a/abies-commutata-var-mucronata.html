Abies commutata var. mucronata

Abies commutata var. mucronata, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada fir, is a variant of the white fir native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are shorter and more densely packed than those of the typical white fir. The cones are typically reddish-brown and mature to a dark brown, with a distinctive short, sharp bract at the base. This variety thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in subalpine or alpine zones with cool, moist conditions.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to the harsh climates of its native range.
