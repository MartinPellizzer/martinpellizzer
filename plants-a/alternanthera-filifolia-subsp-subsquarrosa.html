Alternanthera filifolia subsp. subsquarrosa

Alternanthera filifolia subsp.

subsquarrosa, commonly known as the velvet leaf plant, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptic in shape and may display a range of colors, including green, red, and purple, depending on light conditions.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
