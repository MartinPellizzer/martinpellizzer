Aspidosperma chiapense f. tenax

Aspidosperma chiapense f. tenax, commonly known as the Chiapas aspidosperma, is a shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional remedies.
