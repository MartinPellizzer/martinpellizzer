Acarpha laciniata

Acarpha laciniata, commonly known as the fringed grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often finely toothed or fringed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish to brownish flowers in spikelets, which are typically arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Acarpha laciniata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small wildlife.
