Amyema seemeniana subsp. seemeniana

Amyema seemeniana subsp.

seemeniana, commonly known as the grey box, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its dense, grey-green foliage and smooth, flaky bark that peels in thin layers.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions.

The species is part of the Myrtaceae family and is often found in heathlands and woodland communities.
