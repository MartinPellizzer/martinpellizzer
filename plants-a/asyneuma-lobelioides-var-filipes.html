Asyneuma lobelioides var. filipes

Asyneuma lobelioides var. filipes, commonly known as the threadleaf asyneuma, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with slender, thread-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
