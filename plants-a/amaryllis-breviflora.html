Amaryllis breviflora

Amaryllis breviflora, commonly known as the dwarf amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It produces clusters of vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow during the summer months. The plant features strap-like, dark green leaves that emerge from a basal rosette of bulbs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its compact size and showy blooms, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
