Acrostichum callifolium

Acrostichum callifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed brake, is a species of fern native to the wetlands and marshes of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant has a robust rhizome system that helps it thrive in waterlogged soils. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture.

Acrostichum callifolium plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
