Apocynum × leuconeuron

Apocynum × leuconeuron, commonly known as the white milkweed, is a hybrid plant in the Apocynaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a fuzzy texture and produces toxic, milky sap that contains cardiac glycosides. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
