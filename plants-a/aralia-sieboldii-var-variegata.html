Aralia sieboldii var. variegata

Aralia sieboldii var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated Japanese aralia, is a striking ornamental plant native to Japan.

It features compound, pinnately arranged leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbellate clusters, which are followed by spiny, berry-like fruits. It thrives in partial to full shade and is valued for its elegant foliage and moderate growth habit.

This variety is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
