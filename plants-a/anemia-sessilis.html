Anemia sessilis

Anemia sessilis, commonly known as the sessile anemone, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Pittosporaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, sessile (without a stalk) leaves and showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils. It has a short stem and produces small, berry-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Anemia sessilis is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
