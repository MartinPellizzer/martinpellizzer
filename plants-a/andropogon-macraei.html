Andropogon macraei

Andropogon macraei, commonly known as tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, reaching heights of up to six feet. The plant has narrow, blue-green leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that are often purple or brown in color. It is well adapted to fire-prone environments and plays a key role in maintaining ecosystem diversity.

Its deep root system helps prevent soil erosion and supports a variety of wildlife.
