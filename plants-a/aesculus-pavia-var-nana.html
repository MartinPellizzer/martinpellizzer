Aesculus pavia var. nana

Aesculus pavia var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf crimson horse chestnut, is a compact variety of the horse chestnut tree.

It is characterized by its striking red bracts that surround the small, yellow flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant produces rounded, spiny fruits that contain toxic seeds, making it important to handle with care. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.
