Achnatherum eminens

Achnatherum eminens, commonly known as the tall wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and grasslands.

Achnatherum eminens is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
