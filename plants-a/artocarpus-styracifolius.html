Artocarpus styracifolius

Artocarpus styracifolius, commonly known as the Indian breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces a unique fruit with a thick, fibrous outer layer. The fruit contains a soft, edible pulp and is often used in traditional cuisines. The plant thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is also valued for its ornamental features and has cultural significance in various regions.
