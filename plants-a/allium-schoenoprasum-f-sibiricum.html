Allium schoenoprasum f. sibiricum

Allium schoenoprasum f. sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian chives, is a botanical variant of the common chive.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. Native to the temperate regions of Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant is valued for its culinary uses, as its leaves and flowers are edible and have a mild onion-like flavor.

It is also appreciated in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
