Argyreia pinangiana

Argyreia pinangiana, commonly known as the pink morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, often used as a ornamental vine. Its flowers open in the morning and close in the late afternoon, hence its common name.

Argyreia pinangiana is cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
