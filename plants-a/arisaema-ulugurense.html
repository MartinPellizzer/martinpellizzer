Arisaema ulugurense

Arisaema ulugurense, commonly known as the Uluguru mountain arisaema, is a flowering plant native to the Uluguru Mountains in Tanzania.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories, thriving in humid and cool environments. Its leaves are broad and arrow-shaped, while its flowers are small and clustered around the central spadix.

Arisaema ulugurense is of interest to botanists due to its restricted habitat and potential medicinal properties.
