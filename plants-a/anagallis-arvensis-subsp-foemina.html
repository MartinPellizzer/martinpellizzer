Anagallis arvensis subsp. foemina

Anagallis arvensis subsp.

foemina, commonly known as the pink willow herb, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red, with five petals, and it blooms from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is often found in moist habitats and is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open areas.
