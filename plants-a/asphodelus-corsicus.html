Asphodelus corsicus

Asphodelus corsicus, commonly known as the Corsican asphodel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica and Sardinia.

It features tall, grass-like leaves and produces striking yellow or orange flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in coastal areas and open woodlands, where it contributes to the local flora.

Asphodelus corsicus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
