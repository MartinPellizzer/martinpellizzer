Acer commutatum

Acer commutatum, commonly known as the California maple, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, ovate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds called samaras. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Acer commutatum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
