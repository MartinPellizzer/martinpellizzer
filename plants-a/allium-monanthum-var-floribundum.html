Allium monanthum var. floribundum

Allium monanthum var. floribundum, commonly known as the pink onion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear dense clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant produces small, bulbous structures at the base, which are edible and resemble onions in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy blooms and pleasant fragrance.
