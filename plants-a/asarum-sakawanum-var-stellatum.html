Asarum sakawanum var. stellatum

Asarum sakawanum var. stellatum, commonly known as the star-shaped wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. Its rhizomatous root system is often used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
