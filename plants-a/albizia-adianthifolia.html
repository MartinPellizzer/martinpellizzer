Albizia adianthifolia

Albizia adianthifolia, commonly known as the lacewood or small-leaved albizia, is a species of legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that give it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow or white, arranged in clusters. Albizia adianthifolia thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it useful in soil enrichment and ecological restoration projects.
