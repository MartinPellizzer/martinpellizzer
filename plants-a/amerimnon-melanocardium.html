Amerimnon melanocardium

Amerimnon melanocardium, commonly known as the black-hearted amaryllis, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its dark, almost black, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that range from red to orange. Its blooming period typically occurs during the warmer months, and it thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
