Acacia pycnantha

Acacia pycnantha, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, globular crown and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that serve as the primary photosynthetic organs. Its flowers are clustered in spherical heads, and it produces long, slender pods that contain seeds.

Acacia pycnantha is a symbol of Australia and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
