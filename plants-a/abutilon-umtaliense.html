Abutilon umtaliense

Abutilon umtaliense, commonly known as the Natal abutilon, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its rough, hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are capsules that split open to release seeds, and it is often found in coastal and sandy soils.

Abutilon umtaliense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
