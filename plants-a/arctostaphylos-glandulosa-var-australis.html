Arctostaphylos glandulosa var. australis

Arctostaphylos glandulosa var. australis, commonly known as the Australian heath, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, leathery leaves that are often covered in glandular dots. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by red or purple berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, coastal or mountainous environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
