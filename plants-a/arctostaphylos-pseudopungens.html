Arctostaphylos pseudopungens

Arctostaphylos pseudopungens, commonly known as the Sierra manzanita, is a shrub native to California and parts of Nevada.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for some wildlife but not typically for humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, rocky habitats.

Sierra manzanita is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native range.
