Asplenium furcatisorum

Asplenium furcatisorum, commonly known as the forked spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its small, dark green, leathery fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant exhibits a unique reproductive structure with sporangia clustered in clusters called sori, which are usually located on the undersides of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found growing on rocks or tree trunks in temperate regions.

Asplenium furcatisorum is a cryptogamic plant that plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing microhabitats for other organisms.
