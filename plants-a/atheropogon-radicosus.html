Atheropogon radicosus

Atheropogon radicosus, commonly known as the red panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves with a distinctive reddish hue, particularly noticeable in the inflorescence. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it ecologically significant in its native habitat.
