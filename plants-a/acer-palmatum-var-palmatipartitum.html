Acer palmatum var. palmatipartitum

Acer palmatum var. palmatipartitum, commonly known as the Japanese maple with variegated leaves, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are partially variegated with white or cream margins. The foliage changes through distinct color phases in the growing season, from bright green to red and orange in autumn. It typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet and prefers partial shade and well-drained soil.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
