Aconitum funckianum

Aconitum funckianum, commonly known as the Funck's monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant has dark green, doubly pinnately compound leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Aconitum funckianum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is noted for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
