Arctostaphylos × media

Arctostaphylos × media, commonly known as the manzanita hybrid, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in winter or early spring. The plant produces small, leathery berries that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Arctostaphylos × media is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
