Astragalus canadensis var. longilobus

Astragalus canadensis var. longilobus, commonly known as the longlobed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow flowers in late spring. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

This variety is often found in the eastern and central United States.
