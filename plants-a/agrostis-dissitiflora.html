Agrostis dissitiflora

Agrostis dissitiflora, commonly known as the scattered-flower grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Agrostis dissitiflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping and erosion control.
