Andropogon curtipendulus

Andropogon curtipendulus, commonly known as inland needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Andropogon curtipendulus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
