Acetosella rosea

Acetosella rosea, commonly known as purple sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often tinged with purple or red hues. The plant produces small, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
