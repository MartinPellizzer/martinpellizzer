Alnus corylifolia

Alnus corylifolia, commonly known as the Chinese alder, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and serrated leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, followed by oval, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including forests and streambanks.

Alnus corylifolia is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
