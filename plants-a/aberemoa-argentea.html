Aberemoa argentea

Aberemoa argentea, commonly known as silverleaf sedge, is a perennial herb native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-tomentose leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowering stems produce small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
