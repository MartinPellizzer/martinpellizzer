Allium roseum var. corbariense

Allium roseum var. corbariense, commonly known as the pink garlic chive, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are bluish-green in color and produce small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The plant is valued for its mild garlic flavor and is often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental and edible qualities, it is a popular choice for both gardeners and chefs.
