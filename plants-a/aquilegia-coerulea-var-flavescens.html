Aquilegia coerulea var. flavescens

Aquilegia coerulea var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple, though this variety exhibits a distinctive yellow hue. The plant has compound, palmately arranged leaves with a serrated margin, and its stems are hairy and upright. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

The flowers bloom in early to mid-summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
