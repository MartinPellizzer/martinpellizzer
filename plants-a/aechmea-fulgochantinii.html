Aechmea fulgochantinii

Aechmea fulgochantinii, commonly known as the flamingo flower, is a bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescence that resembles a flamingo's beak, hence its common name. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect water, forming a central pool. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The flowers are typically bright red or orange, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.

Morphology

Aechmea fulgochantinii has rosette-shaped leaves that are sword-like, arranged in a tight spiral, with a dark green color and a waxy texture.

The plant has a short, thick stem covered in a fibrous, reddish-brown bark, growing from the base of the rosette. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix surrounded by showy, pinkish-red bracts, blooming during the summer months and pollinated by insects. The fruit is a capsule that produces small, black seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

The roots are fibrous and form a dense mat around the base of the plant, helping to anchor it and absorb moisture.
