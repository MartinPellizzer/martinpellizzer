Alchemilla lanuginosa

Alchemilla lanuginosa, commonly known as the fuzzy lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves that form a dense, mat-like structure. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla lanuginosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
