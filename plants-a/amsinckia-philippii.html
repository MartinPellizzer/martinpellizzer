Amsinckia philippii

Amsinckia philippii, commonly known as the Philippi's fiddlehead, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curling leaves that resemble the neck of a fiddle, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in the spring, which are clustered in dense, globular inflorescences. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Amsinckia philippii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
