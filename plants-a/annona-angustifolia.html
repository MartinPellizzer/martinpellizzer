Annona angustifolia

Annona angustifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pawpaw, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a smooth, yellow or orange berry that contains a single large seed.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
