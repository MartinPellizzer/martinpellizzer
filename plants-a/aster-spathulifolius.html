Aster spathulifolius

Aster spathulifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering heads consist of a central disk surrounded by ray florets, which are usually blue to purple in color.

Aster spathulifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
