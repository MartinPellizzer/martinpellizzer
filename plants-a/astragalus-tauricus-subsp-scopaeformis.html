Astragalus tauricus subsp. scopaeformis

Astragalus tauricus subsp.

scopaeformis, commonly known as the horned milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, inflated pods and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant has compound leaves with a hairy texture, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in steppe and desert habitats.

Due to its ecological importance and traditional uses, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
