Antennaria villifera

Antennaria villifera, commonly known as the mouse ear cress, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, hairy leaves that resemble mouse ears. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

Antennaria villifera is adapted to harsh climates and is known for its ability to survive in nutrient-poor conditions.
