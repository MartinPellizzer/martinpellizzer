Aphloia theiformis subsp. laciniata

Aphloia theiformis subsp.

laciniata, commonly known as the lacey milkweed, is a striking plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, white, five-petaled flowers.

The leaves are finely divided, giving the plant a delicate, lace-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
