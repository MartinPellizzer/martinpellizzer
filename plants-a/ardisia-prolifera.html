Ardisia prolifera

Ardisia prolifera, commonly known as the spreading evergreen ardisia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, glossy green leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its botanical characteristics include opposite leaf arrangement and the ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative means.

Ardisia prolifera is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.
