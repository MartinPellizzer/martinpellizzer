Aristolochia hassleriana var. guaranitica

Aristolochia hassleriana var. guaranitica, commonly known as the Guarani Dutchman's pipe, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, heart-shaped leaves and striking, tubular flowers that resemble a pipe, hence its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of tropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
