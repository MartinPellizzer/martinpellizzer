Acanthophyllum albidum

Acanthophyllum albidum, commonly known as the white thorned grass, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, gray-green leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is well-suited to harsh, dry environments.

Acanthophyllum albidum plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and providing habitat for small desert animals.
