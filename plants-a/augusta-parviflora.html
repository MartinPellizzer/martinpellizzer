Augusta parviflora

Augusta parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered Augusta, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Augusta parviflora prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for garden landscapes.
