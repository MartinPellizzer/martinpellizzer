Astragalus managettae

Astragalus managettae, commonly known as the Managetta milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
