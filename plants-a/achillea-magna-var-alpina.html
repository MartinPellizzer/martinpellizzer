Achillea magna var. alpina

Achillea magna var. alpina, commonly known as Alpine yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a rosette of silver-white, hairy leaves that are aromatic and slightly fuzzy to the touch. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-resistant plants.
