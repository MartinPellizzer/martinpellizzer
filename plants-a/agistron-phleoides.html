Agistron phleoides

Agistron phleoides, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Agistron phleoides is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant color and low maintenance requirements.
