Aster gebleri

Aster gebleri, commonly known as the New England aster, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows up to 2 to 4 feet tall, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Aster gebleri is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
