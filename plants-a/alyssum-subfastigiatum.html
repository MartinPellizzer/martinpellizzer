Alyssum subfastigiatum

Alyssum subfastigiatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved alyssum, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are densely packed along its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and long blooming period make it an attractive addition to ornamental landscapes.
