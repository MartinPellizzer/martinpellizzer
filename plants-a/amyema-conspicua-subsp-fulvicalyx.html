Amyema conspicua subsp. fulvicalyx

Amyema conspicua subsp.

fulvicalyx, commonly known as the yellow bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow floral spikes that resemble a bottlebrush, which are produced in summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
