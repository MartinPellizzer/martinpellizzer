Agave jaiboli

Agave jaiboli, commonly known as the Sierra Madre agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, with a bluish-green color and sharp spines. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears yellow flowers, which are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and well-draining soil.

Agave jaiboli is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and resilience.
