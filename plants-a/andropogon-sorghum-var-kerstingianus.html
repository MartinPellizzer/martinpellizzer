Andropogon sorghum var. kerstingianus

Andropogon sorghum var. kerstingianus, commonly known as Kersting's lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for erosion control in arid regions.

Kersting's lovegrass is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
