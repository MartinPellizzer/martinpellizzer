Ayenia ancistrodonta

Ayenia ancistrodonta, commonly known as the silverleaf ayenia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name, and it has narrow, lance-shaped foliage. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Ayenia ancistrodonta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought-resistant qualities.
