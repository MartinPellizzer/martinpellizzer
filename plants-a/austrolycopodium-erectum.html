Austrolycopodium erectum

Austrolycopodium erectum, commonly known as the upright clubmoss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive upright growth habit, with simple, linear leaves arranged in whorls around its central axis. It produces spore-bearing cones, or strobila, at the tips of its branches, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.

Austrolycopodium erectum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and small invertebrates.
