Asclepiodora viridis var. angustior

Asclepiodora viridis var. angustior, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, angular stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The leaves are lance-shaped, typically with a waxy coating, and are arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by follicles containing toxic, milky sap.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is known for its medicinal and ornamental value.
