Aceratium sinuatum

Aceratium sinuatum, commonly known as the sinuous aceratium, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive sinuous or wavy edge. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is native to parts of southern Europe and northern Africa.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
