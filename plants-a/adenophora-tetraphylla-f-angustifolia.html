Adenophora tetraphylla f. angustifolia

Adenophora tetraphylla f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features lanceolate, narrow leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
