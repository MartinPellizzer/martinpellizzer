Anathallis mediocarinata

Anathallis mediocarinata, commonly known as the medium-ribbed spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and its unique floral structure, which features a single, showy flower with a distinctive hood-like labellum. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flower is often adorned with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Anathallis mediocarinata is a rare and specialized orchid, showcasing the complex adaptations of epiphytic plants in the rainforest ecosystem.
