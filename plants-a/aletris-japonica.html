Aletris japonica

Aletris japonica, commonly known as Japanese False Spikenard, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Aletris japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
