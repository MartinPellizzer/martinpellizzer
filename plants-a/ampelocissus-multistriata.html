Ampelocissus multistriata

Ampelocissus multistriata, commonly known as the African moon vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves with a distinctive white-veined pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

Its vigorous growth habit makes it suitable for trellises and as a ground cover in shaded areas.
