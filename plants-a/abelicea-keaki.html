Abelicea keaki

Abelicea keaki, commonly known as the Hawaiian mountain apple tree, is a member of the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Hawaiian Islands and is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves and showy white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Abelicea keaki thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in montane rainforests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
