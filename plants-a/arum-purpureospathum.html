Arum purpureospathum

Arum purpureospathum, commonly known as the purple spathed arum, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a distinctive purple spathe that encloses the spadix, which bears small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense tropical forests. Its unique floral structure is adapted for pollination by specific insect species, such as flies and beetles.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and greenhouses.
