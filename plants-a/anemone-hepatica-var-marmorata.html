Anemone hepatica var. marmorata

Anemone hepatica var. marmorata, commonly known as the marbled hepatica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are often covered in a marbled pattern of green and white. The plant produces early spring flowers that are typically white or pale blue with five petals. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas with rich, loamy soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting early pollinators.
