Anthemis thracica f. puberula

Anthemis thracica f. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent Thracian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open grasslands.

The species is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
