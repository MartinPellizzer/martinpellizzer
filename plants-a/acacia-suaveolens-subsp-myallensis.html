Acacia suaveolens subsp. myallensis

Acacia suaveolens subsp.

myallensis, commonly known as the Myall acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia. It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched form and phyllodes that are typically straight or slightly curved.

The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, followed by flat, leathery pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand dry conditions.
