Acacia aulacocarpa

Acacia aulacocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, thread-like leaflets. The plant produces yellow floral spikes, which are typical of the Acacia genus and attract various pollinators. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through its deep root system.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in reforestation efforts.
