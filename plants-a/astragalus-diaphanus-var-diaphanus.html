Astragalus diaphanus var. diaphanus

Astragalus diaphanus var. diaphanus, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish to greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being narrow and pointed, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert scrub habitats.
