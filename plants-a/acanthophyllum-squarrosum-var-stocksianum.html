Acanthophyllum squarrosum var. stocksianum

Acanthophyllum squarrosum var. stocksianum, commonly known as the stock's spinyain, is a low-growing, perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in areas with limited rainfall.

This species plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
