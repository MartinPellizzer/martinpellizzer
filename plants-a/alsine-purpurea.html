Alsine purpurea

Alsine purpurea, commonly known as purple alsike, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are usually pink or purple.

Alsine purpurea is an annual plant that blooms from late spring to early summer.
