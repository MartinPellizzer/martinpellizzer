Adina polycephala

Adina polycephala, commonly known as the African snowball, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky outcrops.

Adina polycephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
