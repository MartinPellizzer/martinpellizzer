Alchemilla cinerea var. geranioides

Alchemilla cinerea var. geranioides, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are gray-green with a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
