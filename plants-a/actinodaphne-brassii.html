Actinodaphne brassii

Actinodaphne brassii, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green foliage and fragrant wood. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by elliptical, dark blue-black fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

Actinodaphne brassii is valued for its essential oils and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
