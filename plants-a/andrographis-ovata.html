Andrographis ovata

Andrographis ovata, commonly known as the oval leaf andrographis, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in whorled clusters, and its medicinal properties have been widely studied. It is commonly used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments.
