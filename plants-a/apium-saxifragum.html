Apium saxifragum

Apium saxifragum, commonly known as rock celery, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Its roots are edible and have a mild, celery-like flavor, making it a valuable forage plant.

Apium saxifragum thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
