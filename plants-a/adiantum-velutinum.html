Adiantum velutinum

Adiantum velutinum, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a delicate fern species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that are covered in a soft, velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are usually light green and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

Adiantum velutinum is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade.
