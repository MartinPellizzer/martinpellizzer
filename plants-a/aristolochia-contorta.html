Aristolochia contorta

Aristolochia contorta, commonly known as Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are often a deep purple or maroon color. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are typically large and glossy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

However, it is important to note that all parts of the plant are toxic and should not be ingested.
