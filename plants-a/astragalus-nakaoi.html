Astragalus nakaoi

Astragalus nakaoi, commonly known as Nakao's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate climates. It produces pod fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
