Acacia leiophylla

Acacia leiophylla, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems and small, feathery leaves that are adapted to coastal conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which attract a variety of pollinators. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant.

Acacia leiophylla plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
