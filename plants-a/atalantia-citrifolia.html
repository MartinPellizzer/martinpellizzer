Atalantia citrifolia

Atalantia citrifolia, commonly known as the yellow mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The tree produces small, round fruits with a thick, purple rind and a segmented, white, juicy pulp. The fruit is rich in antioxidants and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and potential pharmacological properties.
