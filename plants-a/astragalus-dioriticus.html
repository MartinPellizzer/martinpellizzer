Astragalus dioriticus

Astragalus dioriticus, commonly known as the diorite milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant thrives in rocky and arid environments, often found on limestone and diorite substrates. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
