Alchemilla kornasiana

Alchemilla kornasiana, commonly known as the Kornatsky lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of rounded, softly hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Alchemilla kornasiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
