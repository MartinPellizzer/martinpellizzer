Actinophyllum farinosum

Actinophyllum farinosum, commonly known as the farina leaf or silky leaf, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the warmer months. It is typically found in coastal regions and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils.

Actinophyllum farinosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
