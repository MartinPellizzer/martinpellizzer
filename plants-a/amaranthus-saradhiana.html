Amaranthus saradhiana

Amaranthus saradhiana, commonly known as red amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant red or purple pigmentation in its foliage and inflorescences. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or red, depending on the variety. Its leaves are edible and rich in nutrients, making it a valuable crop in some agricultural systems.

Amaranthus saradhiana is also known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in warm climates.
