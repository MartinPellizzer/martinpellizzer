Anoectochilus concinnus

Anoectochilus concinnus, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and produces small, dark green leaves with a velvety texture. The plant blooms with delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Anoectochilus concinnus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
