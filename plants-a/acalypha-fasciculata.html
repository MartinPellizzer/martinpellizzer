Acalypha fasciculata

Acalypha fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, often displaying vibrant colors such as red, yellow, or orange. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by distinctive, fleshy, berry-like capsules. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Acalypha fasciculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
