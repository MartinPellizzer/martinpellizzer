Auganthus geraniifolius

Auganthus geraniifolius, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features vibrant yellow ray florets surrounding a central cluster of tubular disk florets, giving it a distinctive crown-like appearance. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a splash of color to the landscape.
