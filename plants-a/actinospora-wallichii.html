Actinospora wallichii

Actinospora wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its root system is specialized for absorbing nutrients from rocky soils, and it plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species in botanical research.
