Adiantum lendigerum

Adiantum lendigerum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, humus-rich soil. Its rhizomes are short and creeping, allowing it to spread slowly through vegetative growth.

Adiantum lendigerum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in indoor settings with proper care.
