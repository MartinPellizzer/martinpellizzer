Andropogon citriodorus

Andropogon citriodorus, commonly known as lemon grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and a strong, citrusy aroma due to the presence of citral, a volatile oil. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces small, clustered inflorescences. Its rhizomes and leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Botanically, it belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is valued for its aromatic properties and versatility in various traditional uses.
