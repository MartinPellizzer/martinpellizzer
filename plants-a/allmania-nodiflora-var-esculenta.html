Allmania nodiflora var. esculenta

Allmania nodiflora var. esculenta, commonly known as the edible allmania, is a variant of the allmania plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines for their tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Due to its unique flavor and nutritional value, it has gained attention as a potential crop for sustainable agriculture.
