Aleuritia consocia

Aleuritia consocia, commonly known as the white-flowered honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in late summer.

Aleuritia consocia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
