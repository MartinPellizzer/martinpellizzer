Alchemilla irregularis

Alchemilla irregularis, commonly known as the irregular lady's mantle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in loose panicles. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla irregularis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and its ability to thrive in wet conditions.
