Acacia grandifolia

Acacia grandifolia, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact spikes, which are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants. Acacia grandifolia thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
