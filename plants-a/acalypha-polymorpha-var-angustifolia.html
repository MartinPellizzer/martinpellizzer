Acalypha polymorpha var. angustifolia

Acalypha polymorpha var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its ovate, dark green leaves with a distinctive velvety texture on the upper surface. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are often overlooked due to their modest appearance. Its growth habit is typically herbaceous, with upright stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.
