Athanasia vestita

Athanasia vestita, commonly known as the naked everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flower heads that resemble dandelion blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, and the plant is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Athanasia vestita is often used in garden landscapes for its ornamental value and resilience.
