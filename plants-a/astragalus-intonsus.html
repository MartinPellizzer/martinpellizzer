Astragalus intonsus

Astragalus intonsus, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its root system is deep and fibrous. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.

Astragalus intonsus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
