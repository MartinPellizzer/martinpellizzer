Agrostis quadriseta var. lobata

Agrostis quadriseta var. lobata, commonly known as the lobed bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats on well-drained soils. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and is sometimes used in horticultural settings for ornamental purposes.
