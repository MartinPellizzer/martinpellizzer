Astragalus lentiginosus var. chartaceus

Astragalus lentiginosus var. chartaceus, commonly known as the paperleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, papery leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
