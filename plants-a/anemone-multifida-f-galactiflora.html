Anemone multifida f. galactiflora

Anemone multifida f. galactiflora, commonly known as the Milky Anemone, is a variant of the multifida anemone characterized by its distinctive white to pale pink flowers with a milky or creamy hue.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States, typically found in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are alternate and often covered in a soft, downy texture. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a delicate, ornamental touch to gardens and natural landscapes.

The species is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
