Ambora amplifolia

Ambora amplifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf ambora, is a striking plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the rainy season. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Ambora amplifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.
