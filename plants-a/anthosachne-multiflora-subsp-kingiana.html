Anthosachne multiflora subsp. kingiana

Anthosachne multiflora subsp.

kingiana, commonly known as King's mullein, is a perennial herb native to certain regions of East Asia. It features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, clustered white to greenish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fibrous roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
