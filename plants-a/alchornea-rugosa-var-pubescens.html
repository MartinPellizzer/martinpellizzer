Alchornea rugosa var. pubescens

Alchornea rugosa var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy alchornea, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features lobed, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid lowland areas.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for tropical gardens and ecological restoration projects.
