Allium angulosum var. leucorhizum

Allium angulosum var. leucorhizum, commonly known as the white-rooted leek, is a botanical variant of the Allium genus.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical bulbs that are typically white or pale yellow in color. The plant produces tall, hollow stems with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, typically pink to purple in color.

This variety is often cultivated for its mild, onion-like flavor and ornamental value.
