Amorpha tenesseensis

Amorpha tenesseensis, commonly known as Tennessee false indigo, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with oval leaflets and produces showy, violet-blue to purple flowers in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

Tennessee false indigo is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
