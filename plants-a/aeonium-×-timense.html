Aeonium × timense

Aeonium × timense, commonly known as the "Crimson Beauty," is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically red or pink in color, especially when exposed to full sunlight. Aeonium × timense is a hybrid species, resulting from the cross between Aeonium tabuliforme and Aeonium haworthii.

It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
