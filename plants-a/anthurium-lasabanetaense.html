Anthurium lasabanetaense

Anthurium lasabanetaense, commonly known as the Lasabaneta Anthurium, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence composed of a spadix and spatula-shaped bracts. The plant typically features vibrant, colorful bracts that range from red to orange, often mistaken for flowers. Its foliage consists of large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are dark green in color.

This plant thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance.
