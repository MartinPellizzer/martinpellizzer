Acinodendron verticillatum

Acinodendron verticillatum, commonly known as the clustered acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets arranged in a whorl, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
