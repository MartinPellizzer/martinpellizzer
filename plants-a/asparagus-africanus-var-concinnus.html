Asparagus africanus var. concinnus

Asparagus africanus var. concinnus, commonly known as the African asparagus or African featherfoil, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, scale-like leaves that are typically green or blue-gray in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or green flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
