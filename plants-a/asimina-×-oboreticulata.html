Asimina × oboreticulata

Asimina × oboreticulata, commonly known as the Mexican yellow custard apple, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Asimina triloba and A. obovata.

It is a deciduous small tree or shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America. The plant produces showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a large, yellowish-green berry with a thick, leathery skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp.

The botanical characteristics of this plant make it a notable species in the Anacardiaceae family, valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
