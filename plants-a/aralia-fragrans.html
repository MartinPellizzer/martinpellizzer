Aralia fragrans

Aralia fragrans, commonly known as sweet spikenard, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often divided into narrow, feather-like segments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, giving it a delicate appearance. Its fragrant, aromatic foliage has made it a popular choice in ornamental gardening.

Aralia fragrans thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
