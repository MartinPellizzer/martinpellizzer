Acanthorhiza argentea

Acanthorhiza argentea, commonly known as silver root, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features a distinctive silver-gray foliage due to a dense covering of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its root system is notable for being tuberous and often used in traditional medicine.

Acanthorhiza argentea thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
