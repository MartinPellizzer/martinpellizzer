Atractocarpus pentagonioides

Atractocarpus pentagonioides, commonly known as the pentagonal sycamore, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pentagon-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. Its bark is thick and fissured, often exuding a milky sap when damaged.

The species is valued for its timber and has some traditional medicinal uses in local cultures.
