Agrostocrinum scabrum subsp. scabrum

Agrostocrinum scabrum subsp.

scabrum, commonly known as the rough agrostocrinum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in grassland habitats and is characterized by its upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to well-drained soils.

This species is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and resilience in various garden settings.
