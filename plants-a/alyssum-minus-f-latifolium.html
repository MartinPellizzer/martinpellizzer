Alyssum minus f. latifolium

Alyssum minus f. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf winter alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has dense, mat-forming foliage with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long flowering period and ability to attract pollinators.
