Areca latiloba

Areca latiloba, commonly known as the broad-leaf areca, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts, and the plant is valued for its ornamental beauty.

Areca latiloba is also noted for its ability to tolerate partial shade, making it a versatile choice for landscaping in warm regions.
