Anotites discurrens

Anotites discurrens, commonly known as the creeping marshwort, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, often forming dense mats. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in spikes and are usually white or pale green, with four petals.

Anotites discurrens plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
