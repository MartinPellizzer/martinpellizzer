Astragalus chakassiensis

Astragalus chakassiensis, commonly known as Chakassian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological significance is notable in its native habitats.
