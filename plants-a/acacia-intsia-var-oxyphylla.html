Acacia intsia var. oxyphylla

Acacia intsia var. oxyphylla, commonly known as the white acacia, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and long, flat, pale green phyllodes that resemble leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular inflorescences, which bloom during the dry season. Its bark is smooth and grayish, often exuding a milky sap when damaged.

This species is valued for its timber and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
