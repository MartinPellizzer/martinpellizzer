Arthrocereus bylesianus

Arthrocereus bylesianus, commonly known as the Bylesian night beauty, is a species of cactus native to southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its columnar stem and is typically found in rocky or limestone soils. The plant produces striking white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It has a slow growth rate and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
