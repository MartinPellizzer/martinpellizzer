Acer tenellum var. tenellum

Acer tenellum var. tenellum, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 meters, with a broad, spreading canopy. Its small, inconspicuous flowers appear in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The tree prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
