Acacia reclinata var. binervis

Acacia reclinata var. binervis, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, linear leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, which are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
