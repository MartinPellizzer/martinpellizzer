Atriplex argentea var. caput-medusae

Atriplex argentea var. caput-medusae, commonly known as the silverleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
