Abarema triplinervia

Abarema triplinervia, commonly known as the three-nerve legume, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with three leaflets, each having a distinct venation pattern. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as forest edges and clearings.
