Arisaema simplicifolium

Arisaema simplicifolium, commonly known as the simple-leaved arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces striking, arrow-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and it relies on insect pollination for reproduction.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
