Aconitum delphiniifolium var. americanum

Aconitum delphiniifolium var. americanum, commonly known as the American monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant features large, deeply lobed leaves that resemble the tail of a dolphin, giving it its common name. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Aconitum delphiniifolium var. americanum is noted for its toxicity, containing potent alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
