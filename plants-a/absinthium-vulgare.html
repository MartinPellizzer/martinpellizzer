Absinthium vulgare

Absinthium vulgare, commonly known as wormwood, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and western Asia, often found in dry, disturbed soils. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, branched stems and narrow, gray-green leaves. Its small yellow flowers are clustered in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Wormwood contains compounds like thujone, which contribute to its distinctive bitter taste and historical use in herbal medicine.
