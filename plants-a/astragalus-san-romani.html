Astragalus san-romani

Astragalus san-romani, commonly known as San Romani milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its root system is known for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Astragalus san-romani plays a role in soil stabilization and supports local biodiversity in its native range.
