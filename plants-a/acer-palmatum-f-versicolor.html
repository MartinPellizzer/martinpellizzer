Acer palmatum f. versicolor

Acer palmatum f. versicolor, commonly known as the Variegated Japanese maple, is a cultivar of the Japanese maple tree known for its striking foliage.

Its leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a distinctive pattern of green, white, and pink variegation. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

The leaves change color with the seasons, displaying shades of red, orange, and yellow in autumn.
