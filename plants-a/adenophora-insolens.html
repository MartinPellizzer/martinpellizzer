Adenophora insolens

Adenophora insolens, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Adenophora insolens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
