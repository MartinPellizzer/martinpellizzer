Altamiranoa ramulosa

Altamiranoa ramulosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow, typically blooming during the warmer months. Altamiranoa ramulosa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
