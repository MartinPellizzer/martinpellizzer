Amaranthus vulgatissimus

Amaranthus vulgatissimus, commonly known as redroot amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the variety. Its seeds are small and hard, typically reddish in color, and are often used in traditional cuisines.

Amaranthus vulgatissimus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
