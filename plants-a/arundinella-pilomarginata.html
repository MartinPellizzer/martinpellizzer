Arundinella pilomarginata

Arundinella pilomarginata, commonly known as the pilose bamboo, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family (Poaceae).

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender culms covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a slightly hairy texture along the edges.

Arundinella pilomarginata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
