Astragalus vogelii subsp. vogelii

Astragalus vogelii subsp.

vogelii, commonly known as Vogel's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of western North America. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry environments with low precipitation.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, and its leaves are usually compound with numerous leaflets. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed or open habitats, such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
