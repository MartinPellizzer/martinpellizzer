Aquilegia vicaria

Aquilegia vicaria, commonly known as the common columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features striking, spurred flowers that are typically blue, purple, or white, with five petals arranged in a distinctive helmet-like shape. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces seeds in a cylindrical follicle. Aquilegia vicaria thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and meadows.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for centuries in gardens.
