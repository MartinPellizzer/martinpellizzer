Arum oblongum

Arum oblongum, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It features a striking, arrow-shaped leaf that can grow up to 3 feet in length, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers surrounded by a spadix, a characteristic feature of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near water bodies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
