Alkanna orientalis subsp. integrifolia

Alkanna orientalis subsp.

integrifolia, commonly known as the red sandalwood or Persian gum, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It is characterized by its woody stems and palmate leaves that are typically divided into five to seven lobes.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive orange or red, corky fruits that contain several seeds. Its roots and stems are valued for their medicinal properties and have been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

The plant is well adapted to dry environments and can thrive in poor soils.
