Anemone pratensis f. atropurpurea

Anemone pratensis f. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple meadow anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers.

It features dark purple to nearly black petals that contrast with its green, palmate leaves. Native to Europe, this plant thrives in moist meadows and wetlands, preferring partial to full shade. Its basal leaves are typically heart-shaped, while the stem is slender and unbranched.

The plant blooms in early spring, adding a striking visual element to its natural habitat.
