Aframomum sceleratum

Aframomum sceleratum, commonly known as the black cardamom, is a tropical plant native to Africa, particularly found in countries like Ghana and Nigeria.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its tall, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces dark purple or black, aromatic pods that are used as a spice in various cuisines.

Its botanical features include lance-shaped leaves and a distinctive, spicy aroma that contributes to its culinary and medicinal uses.
