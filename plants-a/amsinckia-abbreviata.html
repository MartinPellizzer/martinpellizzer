Amsinckia abbreviata

Amsinckia abbreviata, commonly known as the dwarf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features hairy, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellow to brown flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and scrub habitats.

Amsinckia abbreviata is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
