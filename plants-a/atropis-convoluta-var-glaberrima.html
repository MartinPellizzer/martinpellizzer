Atropis convoluta var. glaberrima

Atropis convoluta var. glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed atropis, is a variant of the atropis plant characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems.

It is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly found in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The leaves are typically alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and possess a slightly hairy underside. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in loose inflorescences, often appearing in the axils of the leaves.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in traditional medicine in some African communities.
