Augusta condensata

Augusta condensata, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and clusters of delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Augusta condensata is also valued for its drought tolerance, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
