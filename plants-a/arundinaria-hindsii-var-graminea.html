Arundinaria hindsii var. graminea

Arundinaria hindsii var. graminea, commonly known as the bamboo grass or Hinds' bamboo, is a variant of the bamboo genus Arundinaria.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, grass-like foliage, which give it a unique appearance compared to typical bamboo species. This plant is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense clumps, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The leaves are narrow and grassy, with a soft texture and a slight sheen that adds visual interest to the landscape.
