Antirrhinum murale proles cirrhigerum

Antirrhinum murale proles cirrhigerum, commonly known as the trailing antirrhinum or snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, trailing stems and clusters of bright red or yellow tubular flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable addition to both wild and cultivated landscapes.
