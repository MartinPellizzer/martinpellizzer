Archidendron occultatum

Archidendron occultatum, commonly known as the ghost flame tree, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that appear in clusters, giving it a dramatic visual display. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The botanical name reflects its elusive or hidden nature, possibly due to its rare occurrence in certain habitats.
