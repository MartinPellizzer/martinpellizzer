Arikury

Arikury, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Erythroxylum novogranatense*, is a shrub or small tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Arikury is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
