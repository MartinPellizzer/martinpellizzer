Angraecum apiculatum

Angraecum apiculatum, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a unique epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It features striking white and purple floral structures with a long, slender spur that is characteristic of the Angraecum genus. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media to prevent root rot. Its flowers bloom during the dry season, often at night, attracting specific nocturnal pollinators like moths.

Angraecum apiculatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a subject of interest in botanical research due to its ecological adaptations.
