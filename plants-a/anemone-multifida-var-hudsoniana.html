Anemone multifida var. hudsoniana

Anemone multifida var. hudsoniana, commonly known as Hudson's anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features showy, white to pinkish flowers with multiple lobes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a soft, velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
