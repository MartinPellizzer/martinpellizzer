Anemone multifida f. polysepala

Anemone multifida f. polysepala, commonly known as the lacey anemone, is a distinctive cultivar of the Anemone genus.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and showy, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing clusters of small, star-shaped petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate foliage and prolonged flowering period.
