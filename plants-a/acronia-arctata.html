Acronia arctata

Acronia arctata, commonly known as the blackfoot daisy, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to arid environments. Its dark purple to black flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Acronia arctata is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
