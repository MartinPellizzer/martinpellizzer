Aizoon affine

Aizoon affine, commonly known as the narrowleaf aizoon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family, which includes many species with showy flowers and edible leaves. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are typically greenish-white or reddish, and it produces small, dry fruits called utricles.

Aizoon affine is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats.
