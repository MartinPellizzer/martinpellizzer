Aristida sabulicola

Aristida sabulicola, commonly known as sand dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seed heads that are characteristic of the grass family. It is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
