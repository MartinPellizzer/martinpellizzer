Asperula ghautensis

Asperula ghautensis, commonly known as the Ghaut evergreen, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Asperula ghautensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings.
