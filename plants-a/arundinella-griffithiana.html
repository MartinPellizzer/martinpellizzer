Arundinella griffithiana

Arundinella griffithiana, commonly known as the Indian bamboo or bamboo grass, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, forming dense clumps in moist, fertile soils.

Arundinella griffithiana is often used in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant due to its ornamental appearance.
