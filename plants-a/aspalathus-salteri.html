Aspalathus salteri

Aspalathus salteri, commonly known as Salter's bushveld bushveld, is a small shrub native to the Eastern Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Aspalathus salteri plays a role in local ecosystems and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
