Allium jaubertii

Allium jaubertii, commonly known as the Jaubert's garlic, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and leeks. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spherical bulbs that are often used in culinary applications. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in a dense umbel, and bloom in the summer months.

Allium jaubertii is valued for its aromatic foliage and is often cultivated in gardens for both its ornamental and edible qualities.
