Arabis lithophila

Arabis lithophila, commonly known as the rock Arabis, is a flowering plant native to rocky and alpine environments in Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in crevices and on rocky slopes, showcasing its adaptation to harsh, nutrient-poor conditions. Its small, white or pink flowers bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch to its rugged habitat.

Arabis lithophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environments.
