Acacia decipiens var. triangularis

Acacia decipiens var. triangularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, triangular-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are arranged in cylindrical spikes. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

The species is often used in reforestation and erosion control due to its hardy nature.
