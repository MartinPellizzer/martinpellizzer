Anatherum spathiflorum

Anatherum spathiflorum, commonly known as the swordgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are typically greenish or pale yellow in color. Anatherum spathiflorum thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

It plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
