Allium globosum var. albidum

Allium globosum var. albidum, commonly known as the white globe onion, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a bulbous base with a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of white, spherical flower heads, which are composed of numerous small, tubular florets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is also used in some traditional culinary applications.
