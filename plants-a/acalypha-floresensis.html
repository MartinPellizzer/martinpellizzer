Acalypha floresensis

Acalypha floresensis, commonly known as the Flores spurflower, is a flowering plant native to the island of Flores in Indonesia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, which bloom during the wet season. Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for studies on tropical flora and ecological adaptation.

The species is also noted for its potential ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitat.
