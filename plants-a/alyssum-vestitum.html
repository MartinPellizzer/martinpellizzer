Alyssum vestitum

Alyssum vestitum, commonly known as hairy alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum vestitum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
