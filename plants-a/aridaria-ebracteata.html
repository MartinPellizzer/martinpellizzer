Aridaria ebracteata

Aridaria ebracteata, commonly known as the naked-leaf aridaria, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems that store water, enabling it to survive in drought conditions. The plant typically lacks leaves, relying on its stems for photosynthesis. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of yellow or orange.

Aridaria ebracteata is well adapted to harsh environments, making it a resilient addition to xeriscaping and desert gardens.
