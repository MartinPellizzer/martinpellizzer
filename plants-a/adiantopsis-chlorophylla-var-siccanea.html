Adiantopsis chlorophylla var. siccanea

Adiantopsis chlorophylla var. siccanea, commonly known as the dryland maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern genus Adiantopsis.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically gray-green in color. The plant thrives in dry, shaded environments and is native to specific regions in southern Africa. Its fronds are pinnately divided, with a leathery texture that helps it retain moisture in arid conditions.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a houseplant.
