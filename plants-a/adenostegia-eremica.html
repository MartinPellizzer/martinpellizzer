Adenostegia eremica

Adenostegia eremica, commonly known as the desert honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, evergreen leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry, sandy soils. Its flowers are adapted to attract specific pollinators, such as bees and moths.

Adenostegia eremica is well-suited to harsh desert environments and is often found in chaparral and sagebrush habitats.
