Astragalus deickianus

Astragalus deickianus, commonly known as the Deickian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
