Amaranthus flexuosus

Amaranthus flexuosus, commonly known as the curlleaf amaranth, is an annual herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its prostrate to ascending growth habit and bright green, ovate to lanceolate leaves that often curl at the tips. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences, which are typically greenish to reddish in color. Its seeds are small and often used as a grain in traditional cuisines.

Amaranthus flexuosus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
