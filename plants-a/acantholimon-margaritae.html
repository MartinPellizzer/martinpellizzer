Acantholimon margaritae

Acantholimon margaritae, commonly known as the saltbush or sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in arid environments. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in sandy or saline soils and is often found in dune ecosystems.

Acantholimon margaritae is well adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds typical of coastal habitats.
