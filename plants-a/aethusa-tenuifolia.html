Aethusa tenuifolia

Aethusa tenuifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved cow parsley, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its finely divided leaves and umbellate flowers.

It is a biennial plant that typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields. The plant has a hollow stem that is often tinged with purple, and its leaves are narrow and feather-like. It produces small white or pale yellow flowers in umbrella-shaped clusters, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
