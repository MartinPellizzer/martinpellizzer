Agrostis clavata subsp. matsumurae

Agrostis clavata subsp.

matsumurae, commonly known as the Japanese slender bentgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that often form dense mats.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in temperate regions of Asia.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.
