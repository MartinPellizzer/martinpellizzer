Arundinella hirta f. variegata

Arundinella hirta f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated bamboo grass, is a variant of the native bamboo grass species found in tropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display a mix of green, white, and sometimes yellow hues. The plant typically grows as a clump-forming perennial, with slender, hollow stems that are often used in ornamental gardening. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The variegation in its leaves makes it a popular choice for landscape design in warm climates.
