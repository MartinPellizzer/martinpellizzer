Anthericum corymbosum

Anthericum corymbosum, commonly known as the clustered arum, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its spadix and spathe structure. The plant features a short, stout rhizome with dense clusters of small, white flowers. Its leaves are broad, arrow-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Anthericum corymbosum typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
