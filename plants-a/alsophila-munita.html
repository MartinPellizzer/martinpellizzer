Alsophila munita

Alsophila munita, commonly known as the mountain hakea, is a species of tree fern native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests and mountainous areas. Its rhizomes are robust, allowing it to anchor firmly in rocky or soil substrates.

Alsophila munita plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
