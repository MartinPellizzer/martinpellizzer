Abutilon hernandioides

Abutilon hernandioides, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, velvety appearance.

Abutilon hernandioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
