Anthriscus pilosa

Anthriscus pilosa, commonly known as the hedge garlic or cow parsley, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, white or greenish flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils. It has a mild garlic scent and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.

Its life cycle includes a rosette stage in the first year and flowering in the second year before dying.
