Astragalus gaudanensis

Astragalus gaudanensis, commonly known as the Gaudan milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, particularly found in Kazakhstan and southern Russia. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Astragalus gaudanensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems in its native habitat.
