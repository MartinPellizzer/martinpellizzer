Abarema mindanaensis

Abarema mindanaensis, commonly known as the Mindanao acacia, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often compound leaves. The plant typically features bipinnate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences. It is often found in open woodland and secondary forest habitats.

Its seeds are encased in elongated pods, which contribute to its ecological role in soil nitrogen fixation.
