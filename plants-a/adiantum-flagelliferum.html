Adiantum flagelliferum

Adiantum flagelliferum, commonly known as the flying spider fern, is a delicate and elegant fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features finely divided, feathery fronds that give it a lacy appearance, with each frond typically measuring up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, nutrient-rich soil. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Adiantum flagelliferum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
