Anthemis pauciloba var. brachysperma

Anthemis pauciloba var. brachysperma, commonly known as the narrow-fruit goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, narrow leaves with a hairy texture. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The variety brachysperma is distinguished by its short, narrow fruits, which contribute to its unique botanical identity.
