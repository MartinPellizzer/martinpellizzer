Alloplectus circinnatus

Alloplectus circinnatus, commonly known as the spiral rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant has hollow, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Alloplectus circinnatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
