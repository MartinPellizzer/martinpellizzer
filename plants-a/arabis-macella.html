Arabis macella

Arabis macella, commonly known as the common rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of small, white or yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments. It is an herbaceous perennial that is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
