Anemone helleboriflora

Anemone helleboriflora, commonly known as the Swiss anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Alps and parts of Central Europe.

It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hairs. The plant produces showy, white to pinkish flowers with five to seven petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and herbal remedies.
