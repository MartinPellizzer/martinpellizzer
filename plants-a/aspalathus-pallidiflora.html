Aspalathus pallidiflora

Aspalathus pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered bushveld clover, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, typically arranged in dense clusters. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Aspalathus pallidiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
