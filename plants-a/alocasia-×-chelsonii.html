Alocasia × chelsonii

Alocasia × chelsonii, commonly known as the Black Velvet Plant, is a hybrid cultivar of the Alocasia genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety foliage with deep green to almost black hues, often featuring intricate patterns of lighter green veins. The plant typically grows as an upright herb with broad, heart-shaped leaves that can reach several feet in length. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its striking appearance, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical plant collections.
