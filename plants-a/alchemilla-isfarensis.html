Alchemilla isfarensis

Alchemilla isfarensis, commonly known as the Isfahan lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
