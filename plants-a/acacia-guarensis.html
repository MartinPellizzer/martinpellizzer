Acacia guarensis

Acacia guarensis, commonly known as the Brazilian acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, sandy soils.

Acacia guarensis is also valued for its hard, durable wood and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
