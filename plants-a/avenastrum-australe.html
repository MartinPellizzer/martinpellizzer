Avenastrum australe

Avenastrum australe, commonly known as the Australian feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, silky foliage and produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often used in floral arrangements. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments. Its growth habit is low and spreading, forming dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
