Aster celtibericus

Aster celtibericus, commonly known as the Celtiberian aster, is a species of flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It features narrow, grayish-green leaves and produces purple or pink flower heads in the summer.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
