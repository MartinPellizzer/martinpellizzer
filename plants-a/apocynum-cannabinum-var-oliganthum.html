Apocynum cannabinum var. oliganthum

Apocynum cannabinum var. oliganthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dogbane, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in the summer, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

This variant is distinguished by its reduced floral display compared to the typical form of Apocynum cannabinum.
