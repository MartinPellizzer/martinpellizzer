Anthemis divergens

Anthemis divergens, commonly known as the yellow tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk, with finely divided, fern-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry meadows and rocky areas.

The essential oils extracted from its flowers are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
