Athanasia recurvifolia

Athanasia recurvifolia, commonly known as the recurved everlasting flower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, composite flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in a dense, rounded cluster. Athanasia recurvifolia is known for its long-lasting blooms, which can persist for several months under suitable conditions.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
