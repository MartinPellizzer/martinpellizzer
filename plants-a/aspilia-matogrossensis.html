Aspilia matogrossensis

Aspilia matogrossensis, commonly known as the Matogrossense torchflower, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow-orange flower heads that resemble torches, giving it its common name. It typically grows in grasslands and open habitats, preferring well-drained soils.

Aspilia matogrossensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
