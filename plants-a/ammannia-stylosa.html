Ammannia stylosa

Ammannia stylosa, commonly known as prairie ammannia, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and swamps. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pink to red in color and appear in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Ammannia stylosa plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
