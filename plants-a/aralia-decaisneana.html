Aralia decaisneana

Aralia decaisneana, commonly known as the giant aralia, is a striking plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter, showcasing a deep green color with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in an umbel, which are followed by bright red berries that attract various birds and mammals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understorey of dense forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
