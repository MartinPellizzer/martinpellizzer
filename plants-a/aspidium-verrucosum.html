Aspidium verrucosum

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds that are often covered with small, raised dots or warts, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a common reproductive feature in ferns.

Aspidium verrucosum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
