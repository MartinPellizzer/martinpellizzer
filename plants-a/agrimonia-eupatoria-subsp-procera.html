Agrimonia eupatoria subsp. procera

Agrimonia eupatoria subsp.

procera, commonly known as tall agrimony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its erect, branching stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are pinnately lobed and have a serrated margin, often covered in fine hairs.

The plant is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
