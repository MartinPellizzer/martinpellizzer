Alstroemeria distichophylla

Alstroemeria distichophylla, commonly known as the two-ranked alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial with strap-like leaves arranged in two rows along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
