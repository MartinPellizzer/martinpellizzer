Anemone aurea

Anemone aurea, commonly known as the golden anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in a fine, white down. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, often found in woodland habitats.

Anemone aurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
