Andropogon filipendulus var. pilosus

Andropogon filipendulus var. pilosus, commonly known as hairy switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks. Its flowering spikes are slender and often appear in late spring to early summer.

This grass is valued for its ecological benefits, including erosion control and wildlife habitat support.
