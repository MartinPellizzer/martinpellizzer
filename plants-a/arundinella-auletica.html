Arundinella auletica

Arundinella auletica, commonly known as the narrowleaf bamboo, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that grow in dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a tendency to curl at the edges.

Arundinella auletica is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive form and adaptability to various soil conditions.
