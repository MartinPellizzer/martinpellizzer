Acacia costaricensis

Acacia costaricensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and often nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant has feathery, bipinnate leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. It is typically found in dry to semi-deciduous forests and is adapted to tropical climates.

Acacia costaricensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
