Anthurium violaceum f. latifolium

Anthurium violaceum f. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf anthurium, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a purple tinge.

The plant produces vibrant, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a showy, waxy spathe that can range in color from white to deep red. It thrives in humid, warm environments with indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. The leaves are thick and waxy, which helps the plant retain moisture in its native habitat.

This cultivar is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive as a houseplant with proper care.
