Alsophila glabrescens

Alsophila glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered with a fibrous, scaly rind. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically in tropical and subtropical regions.

Alsophila glabrescens plays a significant role in forest ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various organisms.
