Acacia acinacea var. brevipedunculata

Acacia acinacea var. brevipedunculata, commonly known as the narrow-pedunculate wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous tiny leaflets, and it produces flat, leathery pods that contain seeds.

It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.
