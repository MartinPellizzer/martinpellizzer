Amyris commiphora

Amyris commiphora, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often fragrant. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, aromatic fruits containing dark brown seeds. It is valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumes and traditional medicine.

Amyris commiphora thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.
