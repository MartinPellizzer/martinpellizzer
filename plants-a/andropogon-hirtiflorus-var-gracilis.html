Andropogon hirtiflorus var. gracilis

Andropogon hirtiflorus var. gracilis, commonly known as slender hairy bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and compact, upright growth habit. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and disturbed sites, preferring well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, blue-green flowers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
