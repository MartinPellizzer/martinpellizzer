Abutilon yucatanum

Abutilon yucatanum, commonly known as the Yucatan velvetleaf, is a shrub native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its seeds are encased in a capsule that splits open to release the seeds.

Abutilon yucatanum is often found in moist, tropical environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
