Aldasorea percarnea

Aldasorea percarnea, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that give it its common name, and it produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the moist forest floor.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
