Artemisia norica

Artemisia norica, commonly known as mountain wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the Alps and other mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that bloom in late summer. Artemisia norica thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in herbal remedies.
