Anthemis rotata

Anthemis rotata, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish-green foliage and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Its essential oils contain compounds like chamazulene, which have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
