Aniba guatemalensis

Aniba guatemalensis, commonly known as Guatemalan birch, is a small evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its fragrant, aromatic foliage, which emits a strong, spicy scent. It typically grows in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Its wood is lightweight and has been used traditionally for various purposes.
