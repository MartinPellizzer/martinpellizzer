Acacia eremophila var. eremophila

Acacia eremophila var. eremophila, commonly known as the spinifex acacia, is a small shrub native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and narrow, linear leaves that reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It is highly drought-tolerant and adapted to sandy or saline soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
