Ammannia coccinea subsp. purpurea

Ammannia coccinea subsp.

purpurea, commonly known as red inkplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States. It features striking red and purple foliage, which becomes more vibrant as the plant matures.

The plant produces small, white flowers that appear in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and bog landscapes.
