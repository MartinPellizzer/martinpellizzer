Acacia nilotica subsp. vediana

Acacia nilotica subsp.

vediana, commonly known as the Sudanese acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval leaflets.

The plant produces yellow, globular inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its bark and wood are valued for their durability and have been used in traditional construction and crafts.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
