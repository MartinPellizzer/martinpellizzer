Anthericum excellens

Anthericum excellens, commonly known as the golden club, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It features a robust rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies. The plant produces tall, leafy stems that bear large, showy, yellowish-green inflorescences. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a distinctive waxy coating.

Anthericum excellens plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
