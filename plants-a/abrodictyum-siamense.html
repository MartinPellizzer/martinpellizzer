Abrodictyum siamense

Abrodictyum siamense, commonly known as the Siam orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which helps it store water and nutrients, adapting to its tropical and subtropical habitats. Its leaves are usually strap-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Abrodictyum siamense is often cultivated for its beauty and is a popular choice in horticultural gardens.
