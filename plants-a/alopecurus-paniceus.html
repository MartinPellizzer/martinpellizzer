Alopecurus paniceus

Alopecurus paniceus, commonly known as hairy oatgrass, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in moist soils, often found in meadows, stream banks, and wetlands. The plant has slender, tufted stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Alopecurus paniceus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
