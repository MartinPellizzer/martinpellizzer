Aorchis spathulata var. wilsonii

Aorchis spathulata var. wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's broad-leaved orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and produces clusters of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forests and rocky slopes. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate floral structure.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
