Aquilegia bauhinii subsp. bertolonii

Aquilegia bauhinii subsp.

bertolonii, commonly known as the Bertoloni's columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andes mountains of South America. It is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and vibrant flower colors, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

The plant produces compound leaves with palmate lobes and grows in rocky or alpine environments. It is adapted to high-altitude conditions with a preference for well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable species in the genus Aquilegia.
