Achillea × maxima

Achillea × maxima, commonly known as the yarrow hybrid, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its flat, composite flower heads that range from white to pink or red. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–90 cm, with finely divided, gray-green leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and disturbed habitats.

Achillea × maxima is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
