Arabis hirsuta var. eschscholtziana

Arabis hirsuta var. eschscholtziana, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous or coastal areas. The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, oval-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. Its flowering stems are usually upright and bear several flowers along their length.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh, well-drained environments.
