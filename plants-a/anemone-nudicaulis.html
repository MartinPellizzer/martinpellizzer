Anemone nudicaulis

Anemone nudicaulis, commonly known as the narrowleaf anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of delicate, white to pale pink flowers in early spring. Its flowers have five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family.

The plant is often found in moist soils and can thrive in partial to full shade.
