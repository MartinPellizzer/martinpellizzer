Anthurium brevipetiolatum

Anthurium brevipetiolatum, commonly known as the short-petioled anthurium, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant spathes.

Native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage is typically a deep green with a waxy texture, and the spathe can range from red to orange, often with a contrasting green spadix. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers within the spathe, which are followed by berries that mature to a bright red.

Anthurium brevipetiolatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive as a houseplant with proper care.
