Arisaema elephas

Arisaema elephas, commonly known as the elephant's foot arisaema, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and a distinctive, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant produces yellowish-green berries that contain toxic seeds, making it potentially harmful to animals and humans. Arisaema elephas thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Araceae family, known for its unique floral structures.
