Agalinis ramosissima

Agalinis ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy false nettle, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features opposite, hairy leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. It is pollinated primarily by insects, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

Agalinis ramosissima is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.
