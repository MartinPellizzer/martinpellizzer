Achillea millefolium var. gigantea

Achillea millefolium var. gigantea, commonly known as giant yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a robust root system and upright, branched stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall. The plant produces clusters of small, flat-topped flower heads in shades of white, pink, or red, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are finely divided, giving it a feathery appearance, and it thrives in full sun and well-drained soils.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
