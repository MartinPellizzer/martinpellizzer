Alseodaphne superba

Alseodaphne superba, commonly known as the giant ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood and glossy, elliptical leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest areas.

Due to its hardiness and ecological importance, it plays a significant role in tropical ecosystems.
