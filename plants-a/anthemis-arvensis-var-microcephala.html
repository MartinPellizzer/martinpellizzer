Anthemis arvensis var. microcephala

Anthemis arvensis var. microcephala, commonly known as the narrow-headed ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and thrives in temperate climates. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in the summer, and the plant is often found in open, sunny habitats.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
