Athyrium fluviale

Athyrium fluviale, commonly known as the river fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often display a bluish-green hue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

Athyrium fluviale is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
