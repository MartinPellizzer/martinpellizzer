Acanthodesmos gibarensis

Acanthodesmos gibarensis, commonly known as the giant rock cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a thick, ribbed stem that can grow up to several meters in height, adapted to store water in its fleshy tissues. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the rainy season, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and deterring herbivores.

The species is well-suited to rocky and sandy soils, thriving in full sun with minimal water requirements.
