Astragalus echinops var. ovalifoliolatus

Astragalus echinops var. ovalifoliolatus, commonly known as the woolly milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its spiny, bristly stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant has palmately divided leaves with oval-shaped leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy species in xerophytic environments.

This variety is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.
