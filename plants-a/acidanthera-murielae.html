Acidanthera murielae

Acidanthera murielae, commonly known as the Muriel's spider orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

Native to the Western Cape of South Africa, it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is typically found in fynbos vegetation. The plant produces delicate, spider-like flowers with a unique coloration of purple and white, which helps in attracting specific pollinators. It is a perennial herb that blooms during the summer months, showcasing its ornamental value.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
