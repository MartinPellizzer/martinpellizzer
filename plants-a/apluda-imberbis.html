Apluda imberbis

Apluda imberbis, commonly known as the hairless grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in ecological research.
