Anchusa obliqua

Anchusa obliqua, commonly known as the narrowleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and blue to purple flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. Anchusa obliqua thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
