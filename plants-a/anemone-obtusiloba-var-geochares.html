Anemone obtusiloba var. geochares

Anemone obtusiloba var. geochares, commonly known as the Pacific anemone or the coastal anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive, rounded center. Its leaves are palmately divided, with lobes that are broad and rounded.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to coastal conditions and its role in supporting local wildlife.
