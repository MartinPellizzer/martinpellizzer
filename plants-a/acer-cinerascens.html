Acer cinerascens

Acer cinerascens, commonly known as the smoke maple, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely divided, feathery leaves that turn a striking red or orange in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that disperse by wind. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full to partial sunlight.

The smoke maple is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its unique foliage.
