Acalypha papillosa

Acalypha papillosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silky hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often with a contrasting throat. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Acalypha papillosa is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
