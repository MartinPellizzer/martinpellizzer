Aster angustissimus

Aster angustissimus, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, clustered blue to purple flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.

Aster angustissimus plays a role in supporting pollinators and is often found in native plant gardens.
