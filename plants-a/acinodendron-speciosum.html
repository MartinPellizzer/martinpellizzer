Acinodendron speciosum

Acinodendron speciosum, commonly known as the beautiful acacia, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem and produces pods that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in dry forest or scrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
