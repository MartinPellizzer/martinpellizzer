Anthurium harrisii f. assimile

Anthurium harrisii f. assimile, commonly known as the red-tipped anthurium, is a cultivar of the Anthurium genus native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

This plant is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a red or purple vein running through the center. The inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, which can range from white to pink or red. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture in its soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse settings.
