Adenophora paniculata var. pilosa

Adenophora paniculata var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage, which gives it its varietal name "pilosa.". The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
