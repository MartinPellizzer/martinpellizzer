Arisaema submonoicum

Arisaema submonoicum, commonly known as the single-leaf arisaema, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its unique botanical features.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is notable for its striking, umbrella-shaped inflorescence, which consists of a spadix surrounded by a large, showy spathe. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Arisaema submonoicum is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in tropical gardens and as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive in humid conditions.
