Acalypha carpinifolia

Acalypha carpinifolia, commonly known as the maple-leaved spurflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, maple-like leaves that are palmately lobed and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or red, followed by distinctive, spiky seed capsules. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Acalypha carpinifolia is valued for its ornamental foliage and is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
