Aquilegia vulgaris var. haenkeana

Aquilegia vulgaris var. haenkeana, commonly known as the Haenke's columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It features striking, spurred flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with deeply lobed foliage, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral structure.
