Asparagus altilis subsp. polyphyllus

Asparagus altilis subsp.

polyphyllus, commonly known as the giant asparagus or the African asparagus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its robust, fibrous roots and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid conditions.

The plant produces long, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height, often branching into multiple shoots. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the spiny, edible spears are harvested for culinary use.

This subspecies is valued not only for its nutritional content but also for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
