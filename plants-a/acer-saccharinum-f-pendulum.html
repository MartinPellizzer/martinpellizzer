Acer saccharinum f. pendulum

Acer saccharinum f. pendulum, commonly known as the weeping silver maple, is a cultivar of the silver maple tree characterized by its distinctive cascading branches.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically pale green with a silvery underside. The plant is native to North America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring followed by winged seeds in the fall.

This ornamental tree is often planted for its aesthetic appeal and shade-providing capabilities.
