Aristolochia rimbachii

Aristolochia rimbachii, commonly known as the Rimbach's Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are often purple or greenish in color. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is typically found in moist, shaded habitats. It is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
