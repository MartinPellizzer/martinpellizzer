Astragalus lentiginosus var. mokiacensis

Astragalus lentiginosus var. mokiacensis, commonly known as the Mokiacense milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers arranged in umbel-like clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of arid ecosystems.
