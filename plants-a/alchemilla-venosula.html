Alchemilla venosula

Alchemilla venosula, commonly known as the veiny lady's mantle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with silver or white. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in branched clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Alchemilla venosula is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
