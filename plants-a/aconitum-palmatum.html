Aconitum palmatum

Aconitum palmatum, commonly known as the Japanese monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features striking, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tall spikes of blue to purple flowers, which are arranged in a spire-like cluster. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aconitum palmatum is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and is also noted for its toxic properties.
