Artemisia camphorata var. xerophila

Artemisia camphorata var. xerophila, commonly known as narrowleaf wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It contains volatile oils, including camphor, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

This variety is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
