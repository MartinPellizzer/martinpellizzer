Agave toneliana

Agave toneliana, commonly known as the Mexican hat agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It features a thick, rosette-shaped stem with sharp, spiky leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears yellowish-green flowers, which bloom once in its lifetime. It is well adapted to dry climates and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its striking appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
