Acacia ataxacantha var. australis

Acacia ataxacantha var. australis, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It typically grows along coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and salty sea breezes. The plant has feathery, bipinnate leaves and produces yellow globular flower heads during the spring and summer. It is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize dunes.

The species is also valued for its role in ecological restoration projects.

Introduced Distribution
