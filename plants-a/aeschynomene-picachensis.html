Aeschynomene picachensis

Aeschynomene picachensis, commonly known as the Picacho horsegram, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale green to yellow in color. Its trifoliate leaves are often hairy, and it is adapted to thrive in poor soils with minimal water.

Aeschynomene picachensis is also known for its potential use in forage and soil stabilization in dryland agriculture.
