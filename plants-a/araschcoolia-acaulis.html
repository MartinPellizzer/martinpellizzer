Araschcoolia acaulis

Araschcoolia acaulis, commonly known as the stemless bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its lack of a distinct stem, with flowers directly emerging from the base of the plant. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, often with a white throat. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, typically growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Araschcoolia acaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
