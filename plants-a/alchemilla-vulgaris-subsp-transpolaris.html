Alchemilla vulgaris subsp. transpolaris

Alchemilla vulgaris subsp.

transpolaris, commonly known as the polar lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Arctic and alpine regions. It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and distinctive silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in compact clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates. Alchemilla vulgaris subsp.

transpolaris is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardiness in challenging environments.
