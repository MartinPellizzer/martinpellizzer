Astracantha ambigua

Astracantha ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous astracantha, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in a dense, spherical cluster. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a slight hairy texture.

Astracantha ambigua is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions.
