Acacia pycnantha var. latissima

Acacia pycnantha var. latissima, commonly known as the broad-leaved golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is well adapted to dry conditions and thrives in full sun. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardiness.

The variety latissima is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to the typical form of A. pycnantha.
