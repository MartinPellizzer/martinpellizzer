Allium virens

Allium virens, commonly known as the green leek, is a perennial plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like basal leaves and a cluster of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and is native to the Mediterranean region. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens.

Allium virens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and mild garlic-like scent.
