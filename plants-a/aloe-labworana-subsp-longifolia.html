Aloe labworana subsp. longifolia

Aloe labworana subsp.

longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating to store water.

The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its primary botanical interest lies in its morphology and adaptability to harsh climates.
