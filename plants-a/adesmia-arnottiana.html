Adesmia arnottiana

Adesmia arnottiana, commonly known as the mountain burr, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina, Chile, and Peru.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
