Alopecurus pratensis subsp. songaricus

Alopecurus pratensis subsp.

songaricus, commonly known as the meadow foxtail, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, pastures, and wetlands, preferring well-drained to moderately wet soils.

The plant has slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces spikelets that are often tinged with reddish hues. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and the plant is valued for its role in maintaining biodiversity in grassland ecosystems.

It is also used in some traditional herbal remedies for its purported medicinal properties.
