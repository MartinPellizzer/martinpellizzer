Aglaia rudolfii

Aglaia rudolfii, commonly known as the yellow mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and dense canopy of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a rounded, segmented berry that turns from green to yellow when ripe, containing a sweet, aromatic pulp.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
