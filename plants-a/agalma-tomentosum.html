Agalma tomentosum

Agalma tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly agalma, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
