Astragalus asterias subsp. polyactinus

Astragalus asterias subsp.

polyactinus, commonly known as the many-rayed milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological restoration projects.

Its subspecies status highlights its distinct morphological and genetic traits compared to other Astragalus species.
