Aspalathus quinquefolia subsp. acocksii

Aspalathus quinquefolia subsp.

acocksii, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant produces small, edible fruits that contain seeds used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in local ecosystems and horticulture.
