Agave rosalesii

Agave rosalesii, commonly known as the Rosalesii agave, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that store water. The leaves are typically blue-green in color with a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces a tall flowering stalk that bears small, white flowers, which are attractive to pollinators.

This plant is well adapted to hot, dry climates and requires minimal water once established.
