Acanthus mollis

Acanthus mollis, commonly known as the woolly acanthus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, soft, and fuzzy foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces showy, spiny, and often fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Acanthus mollis is also valued for its historical use in classical decorative motifs, particularly in ancient Greek and Roman art.
