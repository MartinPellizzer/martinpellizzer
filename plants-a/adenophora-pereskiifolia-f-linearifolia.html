Adenophora pereskiifolia f. linearifolia

Adenophora pereskiifolia f. linearifolia, commonly known as the linear-leaved bellflower, is a variant of the Adenophora genus native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that give it its distinguishing name. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and floral display in gardens.
