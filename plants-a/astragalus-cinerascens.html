Astragalus cinerascens

Astragalus cinerascens, commonly known as the ash-colored milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are compound, with several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus cinerascens plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
