Aegiphila conturbata

Aegiphila conturbata, commonly known as the roughleaf aegiphila, is a perennial shrub native to the highland regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
