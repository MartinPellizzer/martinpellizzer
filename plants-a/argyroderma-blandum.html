Argyroderma blandum

Argyroderma blandum, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, woolly leaves that reflect light, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy seed heads. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Argyroderma blandum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
