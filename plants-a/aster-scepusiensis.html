Aster scepusiensis

Aster scepusiensis, commonly known as the Scepusian aster, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue to violet flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower structure.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
