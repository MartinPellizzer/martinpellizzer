Arabis linneana

Arabis linneana, commonly known as the Linnaean rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants, and is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic regions. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are short and often prostrate.

Arabis linneana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
