Aristida stipoides var. meridionalis

Aristida stipoides var. meridionalis, commonly known as the southern jointed grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as prairies and woodlands. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a cluster of spikelets arranged in a panicle, giving it a delicate, feathery appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife and ecological diversity.
