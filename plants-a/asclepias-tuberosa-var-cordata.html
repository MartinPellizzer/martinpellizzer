Asclepias tuberosa var. cordata

Asclepias tuberosa var. cordata, commonly known as the showy milkweed or heartleaf milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking orange to yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette at the base, with hairy stems that become more prominent as the plant matures. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

This species is also important as a host plant for monarch butterfly larvae.
