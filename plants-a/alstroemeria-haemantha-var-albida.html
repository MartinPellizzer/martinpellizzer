Alstroemeria haemantha var. albida

Alstroemeria haemantha var. albida, commonly known as the white-flowered alstroemeria, is a striking perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The leaves are narrow and strap-like, arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant. This variety is particularly valued for its elegant appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of growing conditions.
