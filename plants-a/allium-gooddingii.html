Allium gooddingii

Allium gooddingii, commonly known as Goodding's onion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its bulbous structure is similar to other allium species, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Allium gooddingii is often found in rocky or open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
