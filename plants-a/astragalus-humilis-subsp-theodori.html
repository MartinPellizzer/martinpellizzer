Astragalus humilis subsp. theodori

Astragalus humilis subsp.

theodori, commonly known as the Theodore's milkvetch, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife, though it is not commonly cultivated.
