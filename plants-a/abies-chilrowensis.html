Abies chilrowensis

Abies chilrowensis, commonly known as the Chiloe fir, is a species of evergreen conifer native to southern Chile.

It belongs to the genus Abies, which includes other firs such as the balsam fir and the silver fir. The tree is characterized by its slender, conical shape and its dark green, needle-like leaves. It produces cones that are typically cylindrical and contain edible seeds.

This species is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with moist, well-drained soils.
