Alsine mantica

Alsine mantica, commonly known as the Mediterranean sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb with hairy stems and small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry habitats. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

Alsine mantica plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
