Abies lowiana

Abies lowiana, commonly known as the lowland fir, is a species of conifer native to the mountainous regions of southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The cones of Abies lowiana are typically small and cylindrical, maturing to a reddish-brown color. This species thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in high-altitude forests.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various wildlife species and contributing to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
