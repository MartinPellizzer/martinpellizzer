Androsace lehmannii var. longipedicellata

Androsace lehmannii var. longipedicellata, commonly known as the long-stemmed rock sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant features a compact rosette of hairy, ovate leaves that are adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions. This variety is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in full sun.
