Arundo roxburghii

Arundo roxburghii, commonly known as the giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to Asia.

It is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant thrives in wet or moist environments, often growing along riverbanks and in marshy areas. It can reach heights of up to 6 meters and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands.

The plant is also valued for its use in basket-making and as a source of thatching.
