Andrachne virgatenuis

Andrachne virgatenuis, commonly known as the slender sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fibrous roots enable it to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils, making it well adapted to desert conditions.

Andrachne virgatenuis is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
