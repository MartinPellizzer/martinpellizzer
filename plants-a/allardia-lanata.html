Allardia lanata

Allardia lanata, commonly known as the woolly allardia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is distinguished by its dense, woolly foliage that covers the stems and leaves, providing a soft, fuzzy texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Allardia lanata typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
