Abronia turbinata var. marginata

Abronia turbinata var. marginata, commonly known as the Margined Sand Verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in sandy or gravelly habitats. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The varietal name 'marginata' refers to the distinctive white or pale margins on its leaves.
