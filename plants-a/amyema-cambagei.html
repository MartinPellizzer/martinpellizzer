Amyema cambagei

Amyema cambagei, commonly known as the narrow-leafed honey-myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry forest areas.

Amyema cambagei is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
