Astragalus crassicarpus var. paysonii

Astragalus crassicarpus var. paysonii, commonly known as Payson milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Payson milkvetch is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
