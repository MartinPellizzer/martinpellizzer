Anisochilus petraeus

Anisochilus petraeus, commonly known as the rock anise, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in spring. Its aromatic seeds are used in traditional medicine for their digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

Anisochilus petraeus thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
