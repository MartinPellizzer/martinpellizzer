Alstroemeria declinata

Alstroemeria declinata, commonly known as the leaning alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for borders and containers.

Alstroemeria declinata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
