Agave weingartii

Agave weingartii, commonly known as the Weingartii agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, spiny leaves that form a rosette shape, which helps it store water and reduce transpiration. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with numerous white to pale yellow flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in arid conditions.

Agave weingartii prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
