Astragalus geniorum

Astragalus geniorum, commonly known as the noble milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its deep taproot system, which allows it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. Astragalus geniorum is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are often used in herbal remedies due to their purported health benefits.
