Alstroemeria philippii subsp. philippii

Alstroemeria philippii subsp.

philippii, commonly known as the Chilean lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Chile. It features striking, colorful, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with a contrasting throat.

The plant has strap-like, lance-shaped leaves that are typically evergreen, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a member of the Alstroemeriaceae family, which is closely related to the lilies.
