Aquilegia vulgaris subsp. ballii

Aquilegia vulgaris subsp.

ballii, commonly known as the Colorado blue larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It features showy, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple, with five petals and prominent nectar-rich spurs.

The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are usually hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental plants with both beauty and ecological value.
