Aphelandra pectinata var. macra

Aphelandra pectinata var. macra, commonly known as the Macra flameleaf plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated, paddle-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green with a red or orange midrib. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Aphelandra pectinata var. macra is popular as a ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
