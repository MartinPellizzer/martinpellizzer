Acacia arcuata

Acacia arcuata, commonly known as the warty acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and small, compound leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by flat, leathery pods containing seeds. Acacia arcuata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and animals.
