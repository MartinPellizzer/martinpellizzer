Aria margaretae

Aria margaretae, commonly known as the Margaretae bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Aria margaretae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
