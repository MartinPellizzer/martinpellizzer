Arachnorchis Applanata Subsp. Erubescens

Arachnorchis applanata subsp. erubescens is a rare and striking orchid native to the wetlands of southeastern Australia. This subspecies is known for its vibrant red and pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near water bodies. Due to habitat loss, it faces significant conservation challenges. Its unique appearance and ecological importance make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
