Atractylis purpurata

Atractylis purpurata, commonly known as purple sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, purple or pink flowers in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which is also known as the mustard family.
