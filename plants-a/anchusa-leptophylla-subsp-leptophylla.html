Anchusa leptophylla subsp. leptophylla

Anchusa leptophylla subsp.

leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bugloss, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry garden settings.
