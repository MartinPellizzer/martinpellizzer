Asplenium breedlovei

Asplenium breedlovei, commonly known as the breedlove's spleenwort, is a rare and endangered fern species native to the mountainous regions of Jamaica.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate fronds that typically grow in dense, moss-like clusters. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found on rock surfaces or in crevices within tropical forests. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists studying rare plant adaptations.

Conservation efforts are crucial due to its vulnerability to habitat loss and environmental changes.
