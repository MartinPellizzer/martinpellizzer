Astragalus argyrothamnos

Astragalus argyrothamnos, commonly known as silverleaf milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soils.

Astragalus argyrothamnos plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
