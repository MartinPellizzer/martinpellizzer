Altamiranoa

Altamiranoa, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Altamiranoa is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
