Ammi trifoliatum

Ammi trifoliatum, commonly known as Indian licorice, is a flowering plant native to South Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, three-lobed leaves and has a branched root system. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
