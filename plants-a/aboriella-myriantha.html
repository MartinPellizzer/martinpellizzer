Aboriella myriantha

Aboriella myriantha, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
