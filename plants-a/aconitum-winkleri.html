Aconitum winkleri

Aconitum winkleri, commonly known as the Winkler aconite, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. This perennial herb features showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a distinctive veining pattern.

The plant contains potent alkaloids, making it toxic and requiring careful handling.
