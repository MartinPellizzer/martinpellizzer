Anemone japonica var. tomentosa

Anemone japonica var. tomentosa, commonly known as Japanese anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant has finely divided, fern-like foliage that is covered in a dense, woolly texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
