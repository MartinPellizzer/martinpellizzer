Adesmia pumahuasiana

Adesmia pumahuasiana, commonly known as the Pumahuasana burro's tail, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its unique, fleshy, succulent leaves that resemble those of a burro's tail. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
