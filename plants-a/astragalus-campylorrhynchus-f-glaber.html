Astragalus campylorrhynchus f. glaber

Astragalus campylorrhynchus f. glaber, commonly known as the smooth-horned milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and the flowers are usually pale yellow to cream in color.

The smooth-horned milkvetch is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
