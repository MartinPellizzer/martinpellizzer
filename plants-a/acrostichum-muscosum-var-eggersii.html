Acrostichum muscosum var. eggersii

Acrostichum muscosum var. eggersii, commonly known as the Eggers' brake, is a variant of the common brake fern.

It is a terrestrial fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, producing clusters of fiddlehead-like fronds. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with a dense arrangement of small, lance-shaped pinnae.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
