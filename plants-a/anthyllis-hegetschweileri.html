Anthyllis hegetschweileri

Anthyllis hegetschweileri, commonly known as the Alpine anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as deep root systems and reduced leaf surfaces. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees, contributing to its ecological significance in alpine ecosystems.

Anthyllis hegetschweileri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
