Artocarpus nanca

Artocarpus nanca, commonly known as the Nanca or Philippine native breadfruit, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries. The plant produces a unique fruit that is typically round or oval, with a rough, greenish-brown skin. The fruit contains a starchy, edible pulp that is used in local cuisines.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and potential medicinal properties.
