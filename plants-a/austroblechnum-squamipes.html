Austroblechnum squamipes

Austroblechnum squamipes, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Blechnaceae.

It is native to New Zealand and is characterized by its distinctive, silvery, scale-covered fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rock crevices. Its fronds are pinnately divided with a unique, almost metallic sheen that gives it its common name.

Austroblechnum squamipes plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to soil stability and providing shelter for various invertebrates.
