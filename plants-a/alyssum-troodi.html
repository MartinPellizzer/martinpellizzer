Alyssum troodi

Alyssum troodi, commonly known as the Sierra foothill alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Alyssum troodi is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
