Arabis flagellosa var. kawachiensis

Arabis flagellosa var. kawachiensis, commonly known as the Kawachiensis rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and segmented stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are white or pale yellow, appearing in clusters.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
