Astragalus sinkiangensis

Astragalus sinkiangensis, commonly known as the Xinjiang milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia, particularly in Xinjiang, China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments and possesses deep root systems that help it survive in nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, and it produces pods that contain small, hard seeds.

Astragalus sinkiangensis is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological resilience and potential medicinal properties.
