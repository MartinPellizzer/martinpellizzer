Arenaria ferganica

Arenaria ferganica, commonly known as Fergana sandflower, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Its ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
