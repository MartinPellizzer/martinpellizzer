Asparagus dubius

Asparagus dubius, commonly known as the common asparagus fern, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features feathery, arching foliage composed of slender, needle-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its adaptability and low maintenance requirements contribute to its widespread cultivation.
