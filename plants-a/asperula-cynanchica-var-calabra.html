Asperula cynanchica var. calabra

Asperula cynanchica var. calabra, commonly known as the Calabrian sweet pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant's leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
