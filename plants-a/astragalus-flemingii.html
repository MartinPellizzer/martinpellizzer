Astragalus flemingii

Astragalus flemingii, commonly known as Fleming's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its root system is well adapted to survive in dry conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Astragalus flemingii is also valued for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
