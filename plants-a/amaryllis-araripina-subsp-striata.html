Amaryllis araripina subsp. striata

Amaryllis araripina subsp.

striata, commonly known as the streaked amaryllis, is a striking tropical plant native to Brazil. It features large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with distinctive streaking patterns.

The plant produces strap-like leaves that are broad and pointed, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

This subspecies is prized for its vibrant blooms and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
