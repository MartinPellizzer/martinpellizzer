Asparagus gracilis

Asparagus gracilis, commonly known as slender asparagus, is a perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and produces small, white flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its stems are often used in culinary applications, adding a subtle, earthy flavor to dishes.

Asparagus gracilis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively low maintenance once established.
