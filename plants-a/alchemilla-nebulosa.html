Alchemilla nebulosa

Alchemilla nebulosa, commonly known as cloud alkanet, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are typically grayish-white and fuzzy, with a distinctive appearance that resembles a cloud, hence its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is noted for its ability to tolerate wet conditions.
