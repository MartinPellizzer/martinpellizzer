Acampe wightiana var. longepedunculata

Acampe wightiana var. longepedunculata, commonly known as the long pedunculate acampe, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated peduncles that bear clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and riverine areas. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
