Acantholimon androsaceum var. olympicum

Acantholimon androsaceum var. olympicum, commonly known as the Olympic sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the coastal areas of the Olympic Peninsula in Washington State.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky habitats. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.

This variety is considered rare and is of particular interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and limited distribution.
