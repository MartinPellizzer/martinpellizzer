Aralia chinensis var. canescens

Aralia chinensis var. canescens, commonly known as the hairy aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and a rough, hairy texture on the stems and underside of the leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbel-like clusters, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability in garden settings.
