Aster quiescens

Aster quiescens, commonly known as the quiet aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, pale blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Aster quiescens is known for its delicate appearance and its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.

It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of high-elevation ecosystems.
