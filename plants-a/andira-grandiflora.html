Andira grandiflora

Andira grandiflora, commonly known as the sweet shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
