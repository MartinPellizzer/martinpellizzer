Astragalus reshadianus

Astragalus reshadianus, commonly known as the Reshadian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential medicinal properties.

However, it is relatively rare and requires specific conditions for successful cultivation.
