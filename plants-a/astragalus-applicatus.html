Astragalus applicatus

Astragalus applicatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus applicatus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
