Alcantarea brasiliana

Alcantarea brasiliana, commonly known as the Brazilian crown of thorns, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of long, stiff, spiny leaves. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters at the top of the stem. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, which helps it conserve water in its natural habitat.

Alcantarea brasiliana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
