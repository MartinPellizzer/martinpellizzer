Alpinia rufa

Alpinia rufa, commonly known as the red ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a reddish tint on the underside. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, red or reddish-orange flowers, which are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants. Alpinia rufa thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

It is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers like turmeric and cardamom.
