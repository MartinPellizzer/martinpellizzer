Astragalus macrocarpus

Astragalus macrocarpus, commonly known as the large-seeded milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several large, smooth seeds.

Astragalus macrocarpus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
