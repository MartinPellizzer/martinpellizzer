Artemisia integrifolia f. sublata

Artemisia integrifolia f. sublata, commonly known as silver sagebrush, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, typically in August and September. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

This subspecies is notable for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
