Amsinckia angustata

Amsinckia angustata, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant has slender, angular stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing small yellow to orange composite flowers that attract pollinators.

Amsinckia angustata is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
