Aconitum vivantii

Aconitum vivantii, commonly known as the Alpine Monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers, which are hood-shaped and arranged in a raceme. The plant has dark green, doubly serrated leaves that are basal and often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Aconitum vivantii thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its toxic properties due to the presence of potent alkaloids.
