Astragalus setsureianus

Astragalus setsureianus, commonly known as the Turkish milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many nitrogen-fixing species. Its seeds are enclosed in a hairy pod and are often dispersed by wind.

Astragalus setsureianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
