Amelanchier subintegra

Amelanchier subintegra, commonly known as the smooth serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its oval, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, dark purple berries in late summer. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Amelanchier subintegra is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinators.
