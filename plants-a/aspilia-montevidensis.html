Aspilia montevidensis

Aspilia montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevidense torchflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow-orange flower heads that resemble torches, which are composed of numerous small florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Aspilia montevidensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
