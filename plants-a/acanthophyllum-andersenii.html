Acanthophyllum andersenii

Acanthophyllum andersenii, commonly known as the Anderson's jointfoot, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub with small, spiny leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is adapted to sandy and gravelly soils, often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
