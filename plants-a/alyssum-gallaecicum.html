Alyssum gallaecicum

Alyssum gallaecicum, commonly known as the Galician alyssum, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has compact, upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, and its leaves are linear to lanceolate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum gallaecicum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
