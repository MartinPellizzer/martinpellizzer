Acalypha spinescens

Acalypha spinescens, commonly known as the spiny hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiny leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to yellow. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are usually alternate, ovate, and covered with fine, stiff hairs.

Acalypha spinescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
