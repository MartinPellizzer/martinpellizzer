Aspidium phegopteris

Aspidium phegopteris, commonly known as the common holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are typically light green with a feathery appearance. The plant produces sporangia on the undersides of its fertile fronds, which are clustered in sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions.

Aspidium phegopteris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
