Abarema cochleata

Abarema cochleata, commonly known as the Brazilian bean or velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated pods. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in agricultural systems. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a protein source in some regions.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.
