Andinia panica

Andinia panica, commonly known as the panicle andinia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery fronds. The plant produces panicles of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are long and pinnately compound, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Andinia panica is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
