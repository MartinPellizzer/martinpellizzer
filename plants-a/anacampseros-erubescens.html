Anacampseros erubescens

Anacampseros erubescens, commonly known as the red-tipped or pink-flowered stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, upright stems covered in dense, overlapping leaves that are typically green with a reddish or pinkish hue. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, often blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is widely cultivated as a decorative plant.
