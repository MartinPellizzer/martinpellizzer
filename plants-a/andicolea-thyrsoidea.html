Andicolea thyrsoidea

Andicolea thyrsoidea, commonly known as the spiral orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Andicolea thyrsoidea is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it adapts to harsh climatic conditions.
