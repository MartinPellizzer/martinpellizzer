Alopecurus antarcticus var. bracteatus

Alopecurus antarcticus var. bracteatus, commonly known as the Antarctic hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southern hemisphere.

It is a perennial grass species characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in alpine and subantarctic regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the vegetation in its native habitats.
