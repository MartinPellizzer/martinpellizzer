Anomianthus auritus

Anomianthus auritus, commonly known as the ear-shaped bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale pink. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slight pubescence. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its unique ear-like appearance and delicate blooms make it a striking addition to botanical collections.
