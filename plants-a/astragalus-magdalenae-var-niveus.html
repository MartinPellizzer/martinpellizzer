Astragalus magdalenae var. niveus

Astragalus magdalenae var. niveus, commonly known as the White Milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical seed pods that release tiny seeds when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinators.
