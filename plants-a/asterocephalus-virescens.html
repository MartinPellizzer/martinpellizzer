Asterocephalus virescens

Asterocephalus virescens, commonly known as the green-headed aster, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

Asterocephalus virescens is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
