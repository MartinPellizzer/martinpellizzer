Astragalus dumetorum

Astragalus dumetorum, commonly known as the rock milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in desert or steppe environments.

Astragalus dumetorum is adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
