Acacia arabica var. adansoniana

Acacia arabica var. adansoniana, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow to golden flowers arranged in dense, globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are highly attractive to pollinators. Its small, woody pods contain several seeds, and the plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and fuelwood in its native habitats.
