Alopecurus pratensis f. alpestris

Alopecurus pratensis f. alpestris, commonly known as the alpine meadowgrass, is a variant of the meadow grass species.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude environments.
