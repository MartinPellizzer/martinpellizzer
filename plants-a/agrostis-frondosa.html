Agrostis frondosa

Agrostis frondosa, commonly known as tufted hairgrass, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form compact clumps. The leaves are narrow, flat, and covered with fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Agrostis frondosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
