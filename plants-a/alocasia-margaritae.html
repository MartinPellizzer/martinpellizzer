Alocasia margaritae

Alocasia margaritae, commonly known as the "Pearl and Jade" Alocasia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with a waxy, white or cream-colored pattern resembling pearls. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its foliage is not only ornamental but also contributes to air purification by absorbing toxins.

Alocasia margaritae is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
