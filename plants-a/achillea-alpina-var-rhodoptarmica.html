Achillea alpina var. rhodoptarmica

Achillea alpina var. rhodoptarmica, commonly known as the Alpine yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, dry climates. The plant features slender, upright stems and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. Its foliage is aromatic and composed of finely divided, gray-green leaves.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
