Armeria alliacea var. allioides

Armeria alliacea var. allioides, commonly known as the garlic rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its strong garlic-like odor, which is most noticeable when the foliage is crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
