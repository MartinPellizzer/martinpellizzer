Astragalus adanus

Astragalus adanus, commonly known as the Kashmir goat's bean, is a species of legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant has palmately divided leaves and is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many nitrogen-fixing plants.

Astragalus adanus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
