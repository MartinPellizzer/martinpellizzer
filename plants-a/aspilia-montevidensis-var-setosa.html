Aspilia montevidensis var. setosa

Aspilia montevidensis var. setosa, commonly known as the hairy aspilia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features bristly, hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale green to white ray florets.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.
