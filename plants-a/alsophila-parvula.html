Alsophila parvula

Alsophila parvula, commonly known as the dwarf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and feathery fronds that grow in a compact, bushy form. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces sporangia on the undersides of its fronds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Alsophila parvula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
