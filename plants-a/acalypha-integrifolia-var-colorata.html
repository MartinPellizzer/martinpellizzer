Acalypha integrifolia var. colorata

Acalypha integrifolia var. colorata, commonly known as the red-veined nettle or velvetleaf, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves with bright red veins contrasting against dark green foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, reddish-brown seed capsules. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its vibrant leaf coloration.

Due to its rapid growth and potential invasiveness, it requires careful management in gardens and natural habitats.
