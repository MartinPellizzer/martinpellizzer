Aster cordifolius var. incisus

Aster cordifolius var. incisus, commonly known as the heart-leaved aster, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves with deep, jagged teeth, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers in the late summer and early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
