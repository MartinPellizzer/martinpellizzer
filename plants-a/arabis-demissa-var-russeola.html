Arabis demissa var. russeola

Arabis demissa var. russeola, commonly known as the Russian rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal leaves that are oval to lance-shaped, with a bluish-green color and a hairy surface. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
