Aronia nigra pubescens

Aronia nigra pubescens, commonly known as the Canadian black chokeberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, dark red to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities, including its showy white flowers in spring and vibrant fall foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
