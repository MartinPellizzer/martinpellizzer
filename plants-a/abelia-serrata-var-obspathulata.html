Abelia serrata var. obspathulata

Abelia serrata var. obspathulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and have serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pinkish flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
