Acer palmatum var. nambuanum

Acer palmatum var. nambuanum, commonly known as the Nambu maple, is a cultivar of the Japanese maple characterized by its striking foliage.

It features delicate, palmate leaves that change color throughout the growing season, often displaying shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant is native to Japan and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for temperate climates.

Its compact growth habit and elegant appearance make it a favored specimen for bonsai and small garden spaces.
