Andinia peruviana

Andinia peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high elevations. Its leaves are lanceolate and hairy, adapted to the cold and arid conditions of its habitat.
