Aconitum luparia

Aconitum luparia, commonly known as the alpine aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense clusters at the top of the stem. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed leaflets that are covered in a fine, waxy coating.

Aconitum luparia thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.
