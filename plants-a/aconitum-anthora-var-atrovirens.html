Aconitum anthora var. atrovirens

Aconitum anthora var. atrovirens, commonly known as the dark green aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
