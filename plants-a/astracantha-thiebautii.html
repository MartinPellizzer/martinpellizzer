Astracantha thiebautii

Astracantha thiebautii, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom during the rainy season. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water absorption.

The plant contains milky sap, which is a distinguishing feature of the milkweed genus.
