Alopecurus granulatus

Alopecurus granulatus, commonly known as hairy mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive hairy stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing greenish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow, slightly hairy, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Alopecurus granulatus is typically found in moist, disturbed soils such as meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.
