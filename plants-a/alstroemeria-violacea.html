Alstroemeria violacea

Alstroemeria violacea, commonly known as the violet-flowered alstroemeria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to red. The plant features strap-like, grass-like leaves and produces showy blooms during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting flowers, which are often used in cut flower arrangements.
