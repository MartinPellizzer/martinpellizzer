Anemone hepatica f. nipponica

Anemone hepatica f. nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese liverleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features delicate, kidney-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a waxy texture. The plant produces showy, early spring blooms of white or pale yellow petals, often with a red or purple center. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical structure is characterized by a short rhizome and a basal rosette of leaves.
