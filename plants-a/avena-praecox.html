Avena praecox

Avena praecox, commonly known as early barley, is a species of grass native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. The plant produces edible grains that are often used in animal feed and traditional foods. Avena praecox thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in fields, meadows, and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth cycle makes it a valuable forage crop in agricultural settings.
