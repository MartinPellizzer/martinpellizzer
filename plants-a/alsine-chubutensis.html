Alsine chubutensis

Alsine chubutensis, commonly known as the Chubut sandweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern South America.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Alsine chubutensis plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat and is of interest for its ecological and potential medicinal properties.
