Acourtia dugesii var. pilulosa

Acourtia dugesii var. pilulosa, commonly known as the sticky acourtia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are covered in a sticky substance, which helps in seed dispersal. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral environments.

The sticky acourtia is adapted to arid conditions and has a low, sprawling growth habit.
