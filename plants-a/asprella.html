Asprella

Asprella, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rhamnaceae family and is characterized by its striking, yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Asprella thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in tropical regions.
