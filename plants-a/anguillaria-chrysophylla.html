Anguillaria chrysophylla

Anguillaria chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden-leaf anguillaria, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its striking, bright yellow foliage, which contrasts with its dark green stems. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Anguillaria chrysophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique and vibrant appearance.
