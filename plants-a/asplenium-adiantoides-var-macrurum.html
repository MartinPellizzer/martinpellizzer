Asplenium adiantoides var. macrurum

Asplenium adiantoides var. macrurum, commonly known as the long-stemmed maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant.
