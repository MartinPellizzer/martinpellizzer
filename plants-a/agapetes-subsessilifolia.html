Agapetes subsessilifolia

Agapetes subsessilifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved silver pine, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, often in cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a distinctive glaucous appearance.

Agapetes subsessilifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
