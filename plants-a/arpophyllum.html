Arpophyllum

Arpophyllum, commonly known as the fairy lantern, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, orange to yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Arpophyllum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant color and unique appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.
