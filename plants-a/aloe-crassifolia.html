Aloe crassifolia

Aloe crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, which attract pollinators like bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Aloe crassifolia is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
