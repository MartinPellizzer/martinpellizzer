Anthephora gracilis

Anthephora gracilis, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and swamps. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in a spike.

Anthephora gracilis is often found in association with other orchids and is an important component of wetland ecosystems.
