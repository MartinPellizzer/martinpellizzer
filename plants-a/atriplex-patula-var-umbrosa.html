Atriplex patula var. umbrosa

Atriplex patula var. umbrosa, commonly known as the shaded saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and succulent, bluish-gray leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Atriplex patula var. umbrosa is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
