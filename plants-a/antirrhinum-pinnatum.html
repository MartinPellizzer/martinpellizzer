Antirrhinum pinnatum

Antirrhinum pinnatum, commonly known as the fringed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which are typically tubular with fringed petals. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Antirrhinum pinnatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
