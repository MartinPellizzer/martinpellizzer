Asarum cardiophyllum

Asarum cardiophyllum, commonly known as heart-leaved asarum, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features a thick, rhizomatous root system and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

The leaves and roots of Asarum cardiophyllum have been used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.
