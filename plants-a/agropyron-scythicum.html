Agropyron scythicum

Agropyron scythicum, commonly known as the Siberian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate and cold regions of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. It is well adapted to harsh environments, including sandy soils and arid conditions.

Agropyron scythicum is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and is often used in restoration ecology.
