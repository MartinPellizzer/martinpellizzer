Anthurium promininerve

Anthurium promininerve, commonly known as the veined anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins that give it its common name. The plant produces striking spathes that are typically bright red or orange, enclosing a spadix that bears small, clustered flowers. Anthurium promininerve prefers warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its foliage and vibrant inflorescences make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
