Asperula brachyphylla

Asperula brachyphylla, commonly known as the dwarf sweet pepper, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces clusters of white, five-petaled flowers. The plant is known for its fragrant foliage and is often cultivated as an ornamental in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Asperula brachyphylla is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
