Aconitum brachypodum

Aconitum brachypodum, commonly known as the short-rooted monkshood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmate leaves with a hairy underside, and its roots are tuberous, which contribute to its common name. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
