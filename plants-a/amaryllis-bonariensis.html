Amaryllis bonariensis

Amaryllis bonariensis, commonly known as the Brazilian belladonna lily, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces a cluster of strap-like, grassy leaves that are often mistaken for grass due to their appearance. It is a bulbous plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Amaryllis bonariensis is known for its ability to bloom reliably in colder climates when provided with proper care.
