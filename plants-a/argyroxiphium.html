Argyroxiphium

Argyroxiphium, commonly known as the Haleakalā silversword, is a rare and unique plant native to the Hawaiian island of Maui.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its silvery, woolly leaves and striking, spiky flower heads. The plant thrives in the high-altitude, volcanic soil of the Haleakalā National Park, where it has adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Due to its limited habitat and historical overharvesting, it is listed as an endangered species.

Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect and restore its population in its native environment.
