Albuca littoralis

Albuca littoralis, commonly known as the coastal albuca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats.

Albuca littoralis is adapted to withstand salt spray and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
