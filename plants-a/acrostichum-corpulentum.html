Acrostichum corpulentum

Acrostichum corpulentum, commonly known as the broad-leafed fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, robust fronds that can reach lengths of up to two meters. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, contributing to its common name.

The fronds are pinnately compound, with a distinctive, broad shape that makes it easily recognizable.
