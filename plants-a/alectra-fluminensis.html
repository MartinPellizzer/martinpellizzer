Alectra fluminensis

Alectra fluminensis, commonly known as the Brazilian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and riverbanks. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces small, bead-like fruits.
