Acalypha psilostachyoides

Acalypha psilostachyoides, commonly known as silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, silky hairs that give them their distinctive silvery appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
