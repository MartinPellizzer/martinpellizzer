Athyrium naganumanum

Athyrium naganumanum, commonly known as the Naganuma Japanese shield fern, is a species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Dryopteridaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are wiry and form dense colonies, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The leaves are bipinnate, with finely divided segments that give it a feathery appearance.
