Aristida mallica

Aristida mallica, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades and tufted inflorescences that resemble plumes. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.

Aristida mallica is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small insects and birds.
