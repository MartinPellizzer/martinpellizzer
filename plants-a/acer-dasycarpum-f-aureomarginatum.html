Acer dasycarpum f. aureomarginatum

Acer dasycarpum f. aureomarginatum, commonly known as the golden-margin maple, is a variant of the hairy maple, characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow margins on the leaves.

This plant is a deciduous tree or shrub native to the Himalayas, thriving in temperate climates with well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a serrated margin and a vibrant green upper surface. The flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge, while the tree produces small, red samaras in the fall.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage, particularly the striking golden edges that enhance its visual appeal.
