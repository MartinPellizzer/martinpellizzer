Ancistrocactus uncinatus subsp. wrightii

Ancistrocactus uncinatus subsp.

wrightii, commonly known as Wright's hooked cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a small, branching cactus with cylindrical stems that are typically green and ribbed.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often followed by small, red fruits. Its spines are short and hooked, providing protection from herbivores.

This subspecies is well adapted to dry, rocky environments and requires minimal water to survive.
