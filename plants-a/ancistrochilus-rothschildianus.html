Ancistrochilus rothschildianus

Ancistrochilus rothschildianus, commonly known as Rothschild's orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of western South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, spurred petals and vibrant, often purple or red flower colors. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks in cloud forests. Its botanical features include a sympodial growth habit and specialized pollination mechanisms adapted to its unique habitat.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
