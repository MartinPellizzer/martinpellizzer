Aplectrum hyemale

Aplectrum hyemale, commonly known as the tawny false unicorn, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellowish-brown, tuberous roots. The plant produces a single, erect stem with lance-shaped leaves and a single, showy white or pale yellow flower. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in woodlands and wetlands.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is considered a valuable species for conservation efforts.
