Aristolochia gorgona

Aristolochia gorgona, commonly known as the Gorgon's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, serpentine-shaped flowers that are often purple or greenish in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex, winding floral structure. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds, some of which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.
