Asperula brevifolia

Asperula brevifolia, commonly known as the dwarf sweet pea, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters along the stems. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are densely packed, giving it a lush appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Asperula brevifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
