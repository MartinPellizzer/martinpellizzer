Amorphophallus hirsutus

Amorphophallus hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and a stout, underground tuber that serves as its storage organ. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes other well-known species like calla lilies and peace lilies. Its tuber is edible and has been used in traditional cuisines for its starchy texture.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
