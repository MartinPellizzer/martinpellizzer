Asyneuma lobelioides f. aspera

Asyneuma lobelioides f. aspera, commonly known as the rough asyneuma, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy foliage and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine, rough hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical or subtropical regions.
