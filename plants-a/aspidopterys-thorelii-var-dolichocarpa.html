Aspidopterys thorelii var. dolichocarpa

Aspidopterys thorelii var. dolichocarpa, commonly known as the long-fruit hibiscus, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in clusters. Its fruits are elongated capsules that contain numerous small, flat seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
