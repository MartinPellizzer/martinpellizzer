Amorpha flexuosa

Amorpha flexuosa, commonly known as yellow indigo, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often contorted branches and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Amorpha flexuosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional dyeing practices.
