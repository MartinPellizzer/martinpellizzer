Aster indicus var. stenolepis

Aster indicus var. stenolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aster, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface. This species thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for use in gardens and horticultural displays.
