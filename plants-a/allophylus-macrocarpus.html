Allophylus macrocarpus

Allophylus macrocarpus, commonly known as the large-fruited holly, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and produces small, white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy fruit. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is commonly found in lowland rainforests.

Allophylus macrocarpus is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
