Artemisia igniaria

Artemisia igniaria, commonly known as brittle bluestem, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and aromatic, silvery leaves that emit a strong, pungent scent when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are clustered in loose panicles, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie and steppe habitats.

Artemisia igniaria is well adapted to arid conditions and is resistant to drought and grazing.
