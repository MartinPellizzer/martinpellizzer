Acaena microcephala

Acaena microcephala, commonly known as New Zealand spinach or mountain spinach, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its small, spiny fruits and alternate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the development of its distinctive, spherical, bristly fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.

Acaena microcephala is valued for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients and have a mild, spinach-like flavor.
