Aster sedifolius var. viscosus

Aster sedifolius var. viscosus, commonly known as sticky leaf aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaves that are covered in a sticky, resinous substance. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers in the late summer and early fall. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its unique foliage and flowering period make it a distinctive addition to native plant gardens.
