Arrabidaea anguillulicarpa

Arrabidaea anguillulicarpa, commonly known as the snakefruit tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces distinctive fruits that resemble small snakes, hence its common name. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy green surface.

The tree is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
