Avena bromoides var. letourneuxii

Avena bromoides var. letourneuxii, commonly known as the Letourneux's oatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
