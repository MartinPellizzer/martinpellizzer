Aconitum leibergii

Aconitum leibergii, commonly known as Leiberg's monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, palmately lobed leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
