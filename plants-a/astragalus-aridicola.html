Astragalus aridicola

Astragalus aridicola, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers and compound leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub habitats. Its deep root system allows it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus aridicola plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native environment.
