Allamanda thevetifolia

Allamanda thevetifolia, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Allamanda thevetifolia is also valued for its ability to grow quickly and adapt to various soil types, making it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
