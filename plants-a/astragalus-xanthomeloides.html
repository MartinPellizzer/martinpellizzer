Astragalus xanthomeloides

Astragalus xanthomeloides, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor, sandy soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
