Aristida setifolia

Aristida setifolia, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades and tufted inflorescences that resemble plumes. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its fine, thread-like leaves are covered in a dense layer of hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

Aristida setifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
