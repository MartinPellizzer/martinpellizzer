Azalea rosea

Azalea rosea, commonly known as the pink azalea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rhododendron genus.

It is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, particularly China and Japan. The plant is characterized by its clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are typically elliptical and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant blooms.

Azalea rosea prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
