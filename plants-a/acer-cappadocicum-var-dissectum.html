Acer cappadocicum var. dissectum

Acer cappadocicum var. dissectum, commonly known as the dissected maple, is a variant of the Cappadocian maple.

It is characterized by its finely dissected leaves that resemble lace, giving it a delicate and ornamental appearance. The plant is native to Turkey and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras in the fall.

This variety is often cultivated for its unique foliage and is suitable for small gardens or as a specimen plant.
