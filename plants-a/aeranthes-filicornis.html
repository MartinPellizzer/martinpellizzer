Aeranthes filicornis

Aeranthes filicornis, commonly known as the feather-tongue orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, and the flowers have a distinctive, elongated shape with intricate veining.

Aeranthes filicornis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
