Araucaria cunninghamii subsp. tahitiensis

Araucaria cunninghamii subsp.

tahitiensis, commonly known as the Tahitian cedar, is a coniferous tree native to the Society Islands in French Polynesia. It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, pyramidal crown.

The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain edible seeds, which are an important food source for local wildlife. Its foliage consists of scale-like leaves that are dark green and closely packed.

This subspecies is adapted to humid, tropical climates and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.
