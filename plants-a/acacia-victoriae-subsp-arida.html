Acacia victoriae subsp. arida

Acacia victoriae subsp.

arida, commonly known as the arid acacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed structure and phyllodes that are typically linear to narrowly elliptic.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which bloom during the rainy season. It has adapted to harsh environments through its deep root system and ability to conserve water.

The species is often used in ecological restoration due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soils.
