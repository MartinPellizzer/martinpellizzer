Astragalus hegelmaieri

Astragalus hegelmaieri, commonly known as Hegelmaier's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or pink. The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
