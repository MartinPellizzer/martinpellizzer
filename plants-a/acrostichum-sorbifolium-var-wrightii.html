Acrostichum sorbifolium var. wrightii

Acrostichum sorbifolium var. wrightii, commonly known as Wright's broadleaf fern, is a variant of the broadleaf fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial evergreen fern characterized by its large, leathery, palmately compound leaves that can reach up to two feet in length. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its fronds are deeply divided, with a distinctive broad, serrated appearance that gives it its common name.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticultural settings where its lush foliage can thrive.
