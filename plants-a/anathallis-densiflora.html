Anathallis densiflora

Anathallis densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered anathallis, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, hairy stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in cloud forests. Its flowers are pollinated by specific species of bees, showcasing a mutualistic relationship.

Anathallis densiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
