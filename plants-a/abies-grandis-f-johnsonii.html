Abies grandis f. johnsonii

Abies grandis f. johnsonii, commonly known as the Johnson's white fir, is a rare variant of the white fir species native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

This subspecies is characterized by its dense, conical crown and pale blue-green needles that give it a striking appearance. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions, often at elevations between 1,500 and 2,500 meters. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.

Due to its limited natural range and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
