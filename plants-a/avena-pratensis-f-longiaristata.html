Avena pratensis f. longiaristata

Avena pratensis f. longiaristata, commonly known as the long-leaved oat, is a variant of the common oat characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves.

This plant is a grass species native to temperate regions and is often found in meadows and grasslands. It has a tufted growth habit with culms that are typically erect and somewhat hairy. The inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster, and the flowers are small and inconspicuous, typical of the Poaceae family.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for erosion control and forage.
