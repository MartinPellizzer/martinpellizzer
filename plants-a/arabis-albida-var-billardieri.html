Arabis albida var. billardieri

Arabis albida var. billardieri, commonly known as the white rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments. Its white flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
