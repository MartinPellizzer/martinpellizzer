Aster occidentalis var. fremontii

Aster occidentalis var. fremontii, commonly known as Fremont's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, purple to blue floral heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Fremont's aster blooms in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
