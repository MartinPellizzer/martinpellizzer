Aeschynomene rhodesiaca

Aeschynomene rhodesiaca, commonly known as Rhodesian goober, is a leguminous plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves, which are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by cylindrical pods containing small, edible seeds. It has a deep root system that helps in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.

This plant is also valued for its forage quality and is used in ecological restoration projects.
