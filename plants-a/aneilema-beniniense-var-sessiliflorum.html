Aneilema beniniense var. sessiliflorum

Aneilema beniniense var. sessiliflorum, commonly known as the Benin arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its upright, tuberous stems and broad, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are enclosed in a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. Its rhizomes are used as a food source, similar to taro, and it thrives in moist, tropical environments.

The species is valued both for its culinary use and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
