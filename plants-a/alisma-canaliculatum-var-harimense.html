Alisma canaliculatum var. harimense

Alisma canaliculatum var. harimense, commonly known as the Japanese water plantain, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that grow underwater or in wet soil. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in marshy areas and shallow water environments.

Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
