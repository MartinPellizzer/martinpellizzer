Asclepias plicata

Asclepias plicata, commonly known as the zigzag milkweed, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagged stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Asclepias plicata is a host plant for the monarch butterfly, providing essential nourishment for monarch caterpillars.

It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
