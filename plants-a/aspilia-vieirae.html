Aspilia vieirae

Aspilia vieirae, commonly known as the Vieira's torch flower, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small, bright yellow daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. Aspilia vieirae thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and coastal vegetation.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
