Acer palmifolium var. nigrum

Acer palmifolium var. nigrum, commonly known as the black maple, is a deciduous tree native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves, which are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras that disperse its seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with a distinctive, flaky texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
