Adenophora fusifolia f. albiflora

Adenophora fusifolia f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered lanceleaf bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is native to regions of East Asia, including China and Korea. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies albiflora is distinguished by its notably white flowers, setting it apart from the more commonly blue-flowered forms of the species.
