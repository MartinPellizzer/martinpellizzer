Adiantum fuscum

Adiantum fuscum, commonly known as the rusty maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green with a rusty or reddish hue on the underside. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with each leaflet (pinna) having a narrow, linear shape.

Adiantum fuscum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
