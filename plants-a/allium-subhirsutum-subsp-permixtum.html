Allium subhirsutum subsp. permixtum

Allium subhirsutum subsp.

permixtum, commonly known as the hybrid garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its clumping growth habit and produces dense, grass-like foliage with a mild garlic scent.

The plant typically features small, white or pale purple flowers that appear in umbels during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild culinary uses in various cuisines.
