Amphipogon strictus var. brevisetus

Amphipogon strictus var. brevisetus, commonly known as the short-stemmed sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or dry habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying xerophytic plants.
