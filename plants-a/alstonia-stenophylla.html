Alstonia stenophylla

Alstonia stenophylla, commonly known as the African leadwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and opposite, simple, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in racemes, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in riverine forests and savanna woodlands.

Alstonia stenophylla is also valued for its hard, heavy wood and has traditional uses in medicine and construction.
