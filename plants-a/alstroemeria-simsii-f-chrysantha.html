Alstroemeria simsii f. chrysantha

Alstroemeria simsii f. chrysantha, commonly known as the golden alstroemeria, is a hybrid cultivar prized for its vibrant, showy flowers.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is native to South America, specifically Chile. The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves and produces clusters of trumpet-shaped blooms in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It is typically grown as a perennial in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for garden displays and floral arrangements.
