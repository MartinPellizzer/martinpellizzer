Aster puniceus f. albiligulatus

Aster puniceus f. albiligulatus, commonly known as the White-veined Purple Aster, is a cultivar of the Asteraceae family characterized by its striking, lavender-pink flowers with white veins.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in narrow, gray-green leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, adding vibrant color to gardens.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to a variety of garden conditions.
