Amyris polygama

Amyris polygama, commonly known as the fragrant wood or sweet wood, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a leathery texture.

Amyris polygama is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
