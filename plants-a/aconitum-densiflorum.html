Aconitum densiflorum

Aconitum densiflorum, commonly known as the dense-flowered monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant features a thick, spindle-shaped root system and lanceolate, doubly serrated leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a dangerous plant to handle without proper protection.
