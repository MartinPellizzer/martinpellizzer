Andropogon compressus

Andropogon compressus, commonly known as narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed sites, reaching heights of 1 to 3 feet. The plant has narrow, bluish-green leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or pink. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in restoration projects.

Its botanical characteristics make it a key species in ecological studies and native plant landscaping.
