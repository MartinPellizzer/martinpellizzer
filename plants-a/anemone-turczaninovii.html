Anemone turczaninovii

Anemone turczaninovii, commonly known as the Turczaninov's anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often with a distinctive purple or red center. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough, hairy texture that helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

Anemone turczaninovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
