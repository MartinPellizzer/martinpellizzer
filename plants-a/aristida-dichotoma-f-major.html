Aristida dichotoma f. major

Aristida dichotoma f. major, commonly known as the giant wiregrass, is a variant of the wiregrass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, grass-like foliage that forms a mat-like growth. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical structure makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
