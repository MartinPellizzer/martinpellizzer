Absinthium candidum

Absinthium candidum, commonly known as common wormwood, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its grayish-green foliage and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant contains high concentrations of essential oils, including thujone, which contributes to its distinctive aroma and historical use in absinthe. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

Absinthium candidum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.
