Anthemis argyrophylla

Anthemis argyrophylla, commonly known as silverleaf chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and traditional herbal use.
