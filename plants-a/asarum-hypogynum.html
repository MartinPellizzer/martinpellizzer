Asarum hypogynum

Asarum hypogynum, commonly known as the lesser hogweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a thick, fleshy rhizome and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a spike. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a distinctive, hairy texture.

The plant is known for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
