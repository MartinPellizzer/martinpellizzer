Agave pax

Agave pax, commonly known as the Mexican blue agave, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces an inflorescence that can reach several meters in height, bearing white or pale purple flowers. Agave pax is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its leaves and stem.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local cultures.
