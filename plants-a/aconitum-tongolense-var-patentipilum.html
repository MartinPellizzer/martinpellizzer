Aconitum tongolense var. patentipilum

Aconitum tongolense var. patentipilum, commonly known as the Tibetan monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of Tibet.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately compound with serrated margins, and its stems are typically hairy. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
