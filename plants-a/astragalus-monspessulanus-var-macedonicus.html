Astragalus monspessulanus var. macedonicus

Astragalus monspessulanus var. macedonicus, commonly known as the Macedonian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to rocky and dry environments, showcasing a deep root system for water retention. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological and potential medicinal properties.
