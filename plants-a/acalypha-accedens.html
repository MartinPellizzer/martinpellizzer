Acalypha accedens

Acalypha accedens, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas due to its adaptability.
