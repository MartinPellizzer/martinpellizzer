Anthericum incohatum

Anthericum incohatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arum, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects, particularly beetles. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a prominent midrib.

Anthericum incohatum thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in swampy areas or along riverbanks.
