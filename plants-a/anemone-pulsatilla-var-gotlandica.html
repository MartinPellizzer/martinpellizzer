Anemone pulsatilla var. gotlandica

Anemone pulsatilla var. gotlandica, commonly known as the Gotland anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Baltic region.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive central tuft of stamens. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
