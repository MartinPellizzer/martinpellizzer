Artocarpus palembanicus

Artocarpus palembanicus, commonly known as the Palembang breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive fruit. The fruit, which is typically yellow when ripe, has a fibrous exterior and a starchy, edible pulp. It is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is used in various traditional dishes.

The tree is also valued for its timber and has cultural significance in the regions where it is found.
