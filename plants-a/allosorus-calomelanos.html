Allosorus calomelanos

Allosorus calomelanos, commonly known as the black-root fern, is a rare and striking member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black rhizomes and its delicate, elongated fronds that often have a bluish hue. Native to specific regions of Southeast Asia, this fern thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained, acidic soils. Its unique appearance and limited distribution make it a sought-after species among botanists and enthusiasts.

Due to habitat loss, it is also considered vulnerable, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
