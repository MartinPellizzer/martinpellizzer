Aster argophyllus var. oliganthemus

Aster argophyllus var. oliganthemus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aster, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers in the late summer and early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
