Astragalus bombycalyx var. subpatulus

Astragalus bombycalyx var. subpatulus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets, and it produces cylindrical seed pods that contain numerous tiny seeds. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
