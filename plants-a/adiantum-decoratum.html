Adiantum decoratum

Adiantum decoratum, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are bi-lobed and have a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant prefers shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping underground stems that produce new shoots.

Adiantum decoratum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
