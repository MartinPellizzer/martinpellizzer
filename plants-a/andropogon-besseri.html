Andropogon besseri

Andropogon besseri, commonly known as Bessera grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to wet soil conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Andropogon besseri plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
