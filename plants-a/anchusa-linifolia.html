Anchusa linifolia

Anchusa linifolia, commonly known as silverleaf anchusa, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Anchusa linifolia is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
