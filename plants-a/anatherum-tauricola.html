Anatherum tauricola

Anatherum tauricola, commonly known as the Tauric grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which are typical of the Poaceae family. Anatherum tauricola is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in ecological and taxonomic research.
