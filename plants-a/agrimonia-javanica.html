Agrimonia javanica

Agrimonia javanica, commonly known as the Java agrimony, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright stem and alternate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Agrimonia javanica is known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.
