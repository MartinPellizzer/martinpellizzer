Allium cannifolium

Allium cannifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf garlic, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its large, broad, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a strong garlic flavor, often used in traditional cuisine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
