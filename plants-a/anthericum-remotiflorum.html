Anthericum remotiflorum

Anthericum remotiflorum, commonly known as the distant-flowered arum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves and a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the arum family. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
