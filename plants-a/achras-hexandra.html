Achras hexandra

Achras hexandra, commonly known as the breadfruit tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces a fruit that is botanically classified as a syncarp, formed from the fusion of multiple berries. The plant belongs to the family Moraceae, which includes figs and mulberries. Its trunk is typically smooth and gray, with a tendency to exude a milky latex when wounded.

The breadfruit tree is notable for its ecological and nutritional importance, providing a staple food source in many Pacific island communities.
