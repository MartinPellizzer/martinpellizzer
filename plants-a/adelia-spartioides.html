Adelia spartioides

Adelia spartioides, commonly known as the coastal dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or arid regions. It has a low-growing habit and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to harsh environments.

Adelia spartioides plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects.
