Aconitum kurilense

Aconitum kurilense, commonly known as the Kuril monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the Russian Far East and the northern regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its striking, helmet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Aconitum kurilense contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires careful handling.
