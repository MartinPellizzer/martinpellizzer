Ajuga pachyrrhiza

Ajuga pachyrrhiza, commonly known as broadleaf bugleweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its spreading growth habit and dense mat of dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetlands.
