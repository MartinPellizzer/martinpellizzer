Adiantum asarifolium

Adiantum asarifolium, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Adiantaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble hair, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive rachis that is often grooved or ridged.

Adiantum asarifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
