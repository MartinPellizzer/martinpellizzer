Avenochloa pruinosa

Avenochloa pruinosa, commonly known as the silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in spikelets, which are typically arranged in loose clusters along the stem. Avenochloa pruinosa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
