Astragalus stocksii var. elongatus

Astragalus stocksii var. elongatus, commonly known as the long-stemmed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its stems are elongated and often branched, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Astragalus stocksii var. elongatus is also valued for its role in ecological systems, providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
