Attalea czerniaewii

Attalea czerniaewii, commonly known as the Paraguay palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

It is a medium-sized tree that typically grows to a height of 5 to 10 meters, characterized by its slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by clustered fruits containing edible seeds.

Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and for thatching, highlighting its cultural and practical significance in local communities.
