Anagallis meyeri-johannis

Anagallis meyeri-johannis, commonly known as the "pink evening primrose," is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its delicate, tubular pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its stems are upright and branching.

Anagallis meyeri-johannis is often found in open habitats such as grasslands, disturbed areas, and along roadsides.
