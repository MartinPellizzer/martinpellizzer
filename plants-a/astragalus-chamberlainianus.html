Astragalus chamberlainianus

Astragalus chamberlainianus, commonly known as the Chamberlain's milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a species of conservation concern in some regions.
