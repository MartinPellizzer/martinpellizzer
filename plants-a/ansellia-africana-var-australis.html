Ansellia africana var. australis

Ansellia africana var. australis, commonly known as the Australian arrowroot, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, strap-like leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
