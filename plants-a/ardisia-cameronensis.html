Ardisia cameronensis

Ardisia cameronensis, commonly known as the Cameron's beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by bright red berries that are edible for some bird species. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive berries and foliage.
