Abuta cuspidata var. ovalifolia

Abuta cuspidata var. ovalifolia, commonly known as the ovalleaf abuta, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by orange or red berries. Its leaves are typically oval or ovate, giving it its varietal name.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in shaded garden environments.
