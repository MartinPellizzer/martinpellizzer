Acacia douglasica

Acacia douglasica, commonly known as Douglas's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and compound leaves that are typically bipinnate. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular inflorescences, which attract a variety of pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are often dispersed by animals.

Acacia douglasica is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and deep root systems.
