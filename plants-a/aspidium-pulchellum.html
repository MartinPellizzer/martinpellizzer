Aspidium pulchellum

Aspidium pulchellum, commonly known as the slender shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its spores are borne on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Aspidium pulchellum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
