Anchusa milleri

Anchusa milleri, commonly known as the Mediterranean bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 1 meter tall, covered with soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers in racemes, which bloom from summer to early autumn. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Anchusa milleri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
