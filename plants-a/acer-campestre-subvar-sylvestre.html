Acer campestre subvar. sylvestre

Acer campestre subvar. sylvestre, commonly known as the European hedge maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its oval to ovate leaves with serrated edges and a distinctive red or orange coloration in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds called samaras. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense hedges.

This variety is more compact and upright compared to the typical Acer campestre.
