Acrostichum minutum

Acrostichum minutum, commonly known as the dwarf fern, is a small, evergreen fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features delicate, lace-like fronds that grow close to the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forests or along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and colonize areas effectively.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its small, spore-bearing structures called sori.
