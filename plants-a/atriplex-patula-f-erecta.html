Atriplex patula f. erecta

Atriplex patula f. erecta, commonly known as the upright saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, which distinguishes it from the more sprawling form of the species. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments. It produces greenish-yellow flowers in compact clusters, which are typically pollinated by wind.

This variety is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
