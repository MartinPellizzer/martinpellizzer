Abutilon terminale var. typica

Abutilon terminale var. typica, commonly known as velvetleaf or Chinese lantern plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically green with a slight pubescence. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped bracts that range in color from red to orange, often mistaken for flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
