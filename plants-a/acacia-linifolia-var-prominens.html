Acacia linifolia var. prominens

Acacia linifolia var. prominens, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub native to coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems and small, yellow floral spikes that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has bipinnate leaves, with each leaflet being narrow and linear, contributing to its overall delicate appearance. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in dunes or coastal heathlands.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is considered invasive in some areas.
