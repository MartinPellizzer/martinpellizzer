Astragalus fernaldii

Astragalus fernaldii, commonly known as the Fernald milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Astragalus fernaldii is also of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
