Anaphalis todaiensis

Anaphalis todaiensis, commonly known as the Japanese woolly aster, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its fluffy, white capitula that resemble small clouds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Anaphalis todaiensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique visual appeal.
