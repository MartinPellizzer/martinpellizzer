Argyroderma crateriforme

Argyroderma crateriforme, commonly known as the star-shaped rock cactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, star-like appearance with radial spines and a compact, globular shape. The plant features a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, emerging from the axils of the spines.

Argyroderma crateriforme is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun exposure, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
