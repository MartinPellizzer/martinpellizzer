Asperula aristata subf. sublongiflora

Asperula aristata subf.

sublongiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered evergreen azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan. It features lanceolate to elliptic leaves that are dark green and glossy on top with a lighter underside.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy flowers and evergreen foliage.
