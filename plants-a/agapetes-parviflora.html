Agapetes parviflora

Agapetes parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered silverleaf, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the Andes mountains in South America, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. It typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter.

Agapetes parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
