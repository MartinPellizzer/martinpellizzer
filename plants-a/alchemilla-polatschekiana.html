Alchemilla polatschekiana

Alchemilla polatschekiana, commonly known as the alpine lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of velvety, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.

Alchemilla polatschekiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
