Acanthocarpus serra

Acanthocarpus serra, commonly known as the serrated acanthocarpus, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that are often covered in stiff, bristly hairs, which aid in seed dispersal. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a common sight in savannas and rocky habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
