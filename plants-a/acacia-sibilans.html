Acacia sibilans

Acacia sibilans, commonly known as the weeping acacia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique visual characteristics.
