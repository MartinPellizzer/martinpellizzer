Aspidium pedicellatum

Aspidium pedicellatum, commonly known as the slender water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 30 cm in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in wetlands, stream banks, and along the edges of ponds. Its sporangia are clustered in sori along the margins of the fronds, a typical feature of many ferns.

Aspidium pedicellatum plays a role in maintaining aquatic and riparian ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to nutrient cycling.
