Anthericum auratum

Anthericum auratum, commonly known as the golden lily, is a striking plant native to the wetlands of Europe.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of bright yellow flowers, which are actually modified leaves called bracts. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged in water, aiding in its aquatic adaptation. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it thrives in moist or aquatic environments.

Anthericum auratum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
