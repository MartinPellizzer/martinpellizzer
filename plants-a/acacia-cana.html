Acacia cana

Acacia cana, commonly known as the white acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow, globular flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Acacia cana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
