Anthericum elongatum

Anthericum elongatum, commonly known as the longleaf lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that grow in dense tufts, and it produces tall, slender flowering stems topped with clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense ground covers. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or marshy areas.

Anthericum elongatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
