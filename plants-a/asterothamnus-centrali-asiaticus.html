Asterothamnus centrali-asiaticus

Asterothamnus centrali-asiaticus, commonly known as the Central Asian thistle, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny foliage. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with rough, serrated edges, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

Asterothamnus centrali-asiaticus is often found in steppe and desert environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
