Adiantum obliquum var. majus

Adiantum obliquum var. majus, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, arching fronds that are typically 15 to 30 centimeters long. The leaves are bipinnate, with finely divided, silky-textured segments that give the plant a lacy appearance. It prefers shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is popular in horticulture for its elegant foliage.
