Aotus subglauca var. filiformis

Aotus subglauca var. filiformis, commonly known as the slender bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to freshwater habitats in South America.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap aquatic prey. The plant typically grows in shallow, still waters and relies on these bladders to obtain nutrients from the surrounding environment. Its leaves are filamentous, allowing it to float and maximize exposure to potential prey.

The species is notable for its specialized adaptations to nutrient-poor aquatic environments.
