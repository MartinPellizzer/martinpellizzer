Asyneuma argutum

Asyneuma argutum, commonly known as the roughleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its yellow-orange composite flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Asyneuma argutum is typically found in open grasslands and rocky outcrops, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.
