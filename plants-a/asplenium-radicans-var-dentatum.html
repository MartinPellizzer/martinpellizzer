Asplenium radicans var. dentatum

Asplenium radicans var. dentatum, commonly known as the creeping shield fern, is a variant of the shield fern species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, producing dense, low-growing mats of foliage. The leaves are typically lanceolate, with serrated margins that give it the "dentatum" designation. The fronds are usually dark green and glossy, with a leathery texture.

This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist, rocky or shaded forest floors.
