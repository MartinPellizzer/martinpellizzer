Allium ampeloprasum subsp. pardoi

Allium ampeloprasum subsp.

pardoi, commonly known as the purple onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its robust, upright stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green.

The plant produces umbels of small, pale purple to pink flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its bulbs are often used in culinary applications, similar to other Allium species.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more compact growth habit and vibrant flower color compared to other members of the species.
