Anthyllis vulneraria var. hispida

Anthyllis vulneraria var. hispida, commonly known as the hispid bladder campion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
