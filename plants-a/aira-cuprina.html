Aira cuprina

Aira cuprina, commonly known as the purple lovegrass, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, purple to pinkish flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its rapid growth and ability to colonize open areas make it both a pioneer species and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
