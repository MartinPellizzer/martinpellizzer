Acaciella costaricensis

Acaciella costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican acacia, is a shrub native to Central America, particularly Costa Rica.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or orange flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry forests and disturbed areas.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
