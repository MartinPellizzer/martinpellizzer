Aster × blakei

Aster × blakei, commonly known as the New England aster, is a hybrid perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly rough texture.

The flowers consist of ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular florets, making it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower displays.
