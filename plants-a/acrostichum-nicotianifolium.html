Acrostichum nicotianifolium

Acrostichum nicotianifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf hakea, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves that resemble those of tobacco plants. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. It is often found in coastal regions and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

Its foliage is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
