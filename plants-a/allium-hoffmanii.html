Allium hoffmanii

Allium hoffmanii, commonly known as Hoffman's onion, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Nevada. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

Hoffman's onion is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
