Astragalus urbanianus

Astragalus urbanianus, commonly known as the Urbanian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus urbanianus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
