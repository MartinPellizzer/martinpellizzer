Acacia randelliana

Acacia randelliana, commonly known as the red acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in dense, globular clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has feathery, bipinnate leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland dunes.

Acacia randelliana is also valued for its hardy nature and is used in ecological restoration projects.
