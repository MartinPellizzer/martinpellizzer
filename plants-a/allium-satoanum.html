Allium satoanum

Allium satoanum, commonly known as the Japanese garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spherical flower clusters. The plant produces edible bulbs and leaves that are used in various culinary traditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and useful in both cooking and traditional medicine.
