Aster nardophyllum

Aster nardophyllum, commonly known as the sweet fern aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The genus Aster is known for its showy, often colorful flowers, while nardophyllum refers to its aromatic properties.
