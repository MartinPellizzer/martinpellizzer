Agastache lanceolata

Agastache lanceolata, commonly known as narrowleaf hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of violet or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its fragrant foliage and showy blossoms make it a valuable addition to pollinator gardens.
