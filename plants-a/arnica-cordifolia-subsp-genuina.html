Arnica cordifolia subsp. genuina

Arnica cordifolia subsp.

genuina, commonly known as the heart-leaved arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and stems that bear yellow-orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. It is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and wildflower displays.
