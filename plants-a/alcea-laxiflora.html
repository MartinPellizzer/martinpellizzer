Alcea laxiflora

Alcea laxiflora, commonly known as the slender hollyhock, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, rose-like flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, with a distinct central vein.

Alcea laxiflora is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in well-drained soil.
