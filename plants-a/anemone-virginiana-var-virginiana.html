Anemone virginiana var. virginiana

Anemone virginiana var. virginiana, commonly known as the common wood anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, loamy soils. The plant features trifoliate basal leaves and showy white to pinkish flowers with a distinctive yellow center. It produces small, dark brown berries that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by birds.

This species is valued for its early spring blooms and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
