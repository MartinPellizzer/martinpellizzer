Acilepis attenuata

Acilepis attenuata, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features slender, branching stems with small, compound leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in racemes, producing small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Acilepis attenuata is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
