Adelphia hiraea

Adelphia hiraea, commonly known as the small-leaf false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has a fibrous root system. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy pods that mature in the fall.

Adelphia hiraea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
