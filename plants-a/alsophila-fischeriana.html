Alsophila fischeriana

Alsophila fischeriana, commonly known as the Fischer's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a unique reproductive system, producing spores on the undersides of its fronds rather than flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
