Arundinaria brachyclada

Arundinaria brachyclada, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species of grass native to China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and slender, erect stems that rarely exceed 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically greenish or pale yellow in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Arundinaria brachyclada is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
