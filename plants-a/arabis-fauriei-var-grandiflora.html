Arabis fauriei var. grandiflora

Arabis fauriei var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
