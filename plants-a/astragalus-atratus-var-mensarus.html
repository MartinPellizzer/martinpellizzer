Astragalus atratus var. mensarus

Astragalus atratus var. mensarus, commonly known as the black locust milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive dark purple to black coloration. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and woodland habitats.

Astragalus atratus var. mensarus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
