Anomospermum excelsum

Anomospermum excelsum, commonly known as the giant African starling tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and broad, umbrella-shaped crown, which provides shade in its natural habitat. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are compound, with multiple leaflets arranged alternately along the stems.

Anomospermum excelsum is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions, showcasing resilience through deep root systems and water-storing tissues.
