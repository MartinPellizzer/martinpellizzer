Anil trita

Anil trita, commonly known as Indian mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Anil trita is also valued for its role in ecological systems, providing food for various wildlife species.
