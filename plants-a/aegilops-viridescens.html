Aegilops viridescens

Aegilops viridescens, commonly known as green wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its hardiness and adaptability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Morphology

Aegilops viridescens has leaves that are narrow, grass-like in shape, alternate in arrangement, and typically range from 5 to 15 cm in length, with a bluish-green color.

The stem is erect, cylindrical, and smooth, with a light green to reddish-brown color, growing in a clumping pattern. Its flowers are spike-like inflorescences with small, greenish-white florets, blooming in early summer, and are wind-pollinated. The fruit is a small, three-angled caryopsis, and the seeds are dispersed by wind due to their light weight.

The roots are fibrous and extensive, helping the plant to anchor in soil and absorb nutrients efficiently.
