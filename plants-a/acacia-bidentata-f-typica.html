Acacia bidentata f. typica

Acacia bidentata f. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, bipinnate leaves and yellow globular flowers that appear in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

Due to its hardy nature, it is also used in reforestation and erosion control efforts.
