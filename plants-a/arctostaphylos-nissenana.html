Arctostaphylos nissenana

Arctostaphylos nissenana, commonly known as the Nissen manzanita, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, showy flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland forest habitats.

Arctostaphylos nissenana is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native range.
