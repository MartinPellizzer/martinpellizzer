Acer bicolor var. serrulatum

Acer bicolor var. serrulatum, commonly known as the red maple variant with serrated leaves, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking red foliage in the fall, which contrasts with its green leaves in spring and summer. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with finely serrated margins. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds called samaras.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
