Amorpha georgiana

Amorpha georgiana, commonly known as the yellow false indigo, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with 8 to 16 pairs of oval, serrated leaflets. The plant produces showy, pea-like yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Amorpha georgiana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
