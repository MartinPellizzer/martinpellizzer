Acacia brassii

Acacia brassii, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that serve as the primary means of photosynthesis. Acacia brassii is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils.

It plays an important role in the ecosystem, providing nectar for native pollinators and habitat for various wildlife species.
