Arisaema serratum f. blumei

Arisaema serratum f. blumei, commonly known as the Blume's spathephyllum, is a rare and unique variant of the common arisaema species.

It is characterized by its striking, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a large, leaf-like spathe. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of China and is often found in shaded, moist environments. Its botanical features include narrow, serrated leaves and a distinctive floral structure that resembles a spike.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
