Astragalus polozhiae

Astragalus polozhiae, commonly known as the Polozhia milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Additionally, it has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
