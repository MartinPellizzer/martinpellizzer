Argentina habbemana

Argentina habbemana, commonly known as the Argentine sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the grasslands of Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that resemble those of the sunflower. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.

The seeds of Argentina habbemana are often used for bird food and can be pressed for oil.
