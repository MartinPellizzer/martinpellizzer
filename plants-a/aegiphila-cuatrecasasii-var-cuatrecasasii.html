Aegiphila cuatrecasasii var. cuatrecasasii

Aegiphila cuatrecasasii var. cuatrecasasii, commonly known as the Cuatrecasas' aegiphila, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are often gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple or pink.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
