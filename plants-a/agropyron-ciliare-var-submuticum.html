Agropyron ciliare var. submuticum

Agropyron ciliare var. submuticum, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a soft, silky texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands, meadows, and disturbed sites.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
