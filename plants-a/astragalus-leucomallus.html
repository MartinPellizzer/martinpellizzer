Astragalus leucomallus

Astragalus leucomallus, commonly known as the white-hairy milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus leucomallus plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.
