Artocarpus brevisericeus

Artocarpus brevisericeus, commonly known as the short-bristled breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its small, edible fruits that resemble those of breadfruit but are generally smaller. The plant has a broad, spreading canopy with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, short hairs. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female inflorescences, a common trait in the Moraceae family.

The tree is often cultivated for its fruit, which is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
