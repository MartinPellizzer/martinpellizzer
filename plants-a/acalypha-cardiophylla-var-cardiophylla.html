Acalypha cardiophylla var. cardiophylla

Acalypha cardiophylla var. cardiophylla, commonly known as the heart-leaf alypha, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from deep red to vibrant green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage.

The leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its primary value lies in its aesthetic appeal.
