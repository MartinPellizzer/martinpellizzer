Anthyllis multifolia

Anthyllis multifolia, commonly known as the many-leaved anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Anthyllis multifolia is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
