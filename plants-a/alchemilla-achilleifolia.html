Alchemilla achilleifolia

Alchemilla achilleifolia, commonly known as lady's mantle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, typically from May to June. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Alchemilla achilleifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
