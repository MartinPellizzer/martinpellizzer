Annona macrocalyx

Annona macrocalyx, commonly known as the giant custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its fruit is a large, spiny capsule that contains several yellow, fleshy segments with a sweet, custard-like pulp.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
