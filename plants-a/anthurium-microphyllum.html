Anthurium microphyllum

Anthurium microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces striking, spatulate-shaped spathes that can range in color from white to pink or red, depending on the variety. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium microphyllum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
