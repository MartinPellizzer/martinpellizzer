Ampelocissus tomentosa

Ampelocissus tomentosa, commonly known as the velvet leaf or climbing fig, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a woody vine characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage.

Ampelocissus tomentosa is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
