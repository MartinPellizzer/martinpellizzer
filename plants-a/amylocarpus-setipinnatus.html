Amylocarpus setipinnatus

Amylocarpus setipinnatus, commonly known as the hairy-pinioned arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy inflorescence. The plant produces edible tubers that are similar in texture to traditional arrowroot but have a different flavor profile. Its leaves are large, arrow-shaped, and often have a waxy surface.

The plant is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its potential culinary uses.
