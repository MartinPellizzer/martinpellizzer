Anerincleistus macranthus

Anerincleistus macranthus, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in diameter, floating on the water's surface. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom at night. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in the muddy bottom of its habitat while absorbing nutrients from the water.

Anerincleistus macranthus plays an important ecological role by providing shelter for aquatic life and contributing to water filtration.
