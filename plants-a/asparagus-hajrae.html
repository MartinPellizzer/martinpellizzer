Asparagus hajrae

Asparagus hajrae, commonly known as the Kashmiri asparagus, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Pakistan and India.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental, needle-like leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant is evergreen and thrives in cool, moist environments, typically in mountainous areas. It is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Asparagus hajrae is a hardy species that requires well-drained soil and partial shade to flourish.
