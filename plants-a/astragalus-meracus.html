Astragalus meracus

Astragalus meracus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions. Astragalus meracus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.

It is also valued in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
