Alocasia boa

Alocasia boa, commonly known as the black magic plant, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, arrowhead-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 feet in length, often with a dark green or almost black coloration. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it anchor and absorb nutrients from the soil. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistently moist soil to flourish.

Due to its dramatic appearance, it is popular as a decorative houseplant.
