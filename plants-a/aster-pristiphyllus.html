Aster pristiphyllus

Aster pristiphyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aster, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, woodland environments. The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of purple and white. It blooms in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
