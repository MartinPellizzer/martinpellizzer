Althaea sulphurea

Althaea sulphurea, commonly known as the sulfur althea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, and its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Althaea sulphurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
