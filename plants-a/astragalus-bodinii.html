Astragalus bodinii

Astragalus bodinii, commonly known as the Bodin's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments. It has compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Astragalus bodinii is often found in mountainous areas and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological and potential medicinal significance.
