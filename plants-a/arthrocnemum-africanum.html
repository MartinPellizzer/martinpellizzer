Arthrocnemum africanum

Arthrocnemum africanum, commonly known as the African jointed grass, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline and arid environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its jointed stems and succulent leaves. The plant thrives in coastal areas and saline soils, often found in dune ecosystems. Its structure allows it to store water efficiently, making it resilient to drought conditions.

Arthrocnemum africanum plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and preventing erosion in its native habitats.
