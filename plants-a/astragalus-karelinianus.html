Astragalus karelinianus

Astragalus karelinianus, commonly known as Karelin's milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It produces small, hard seeds that are often dispersed by wind.

Astragalus karelinianus is of interest to botanists due to its ecological significance and potential medicinal properties.
