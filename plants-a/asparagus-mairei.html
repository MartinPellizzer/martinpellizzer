Asparagus mairei

Asparagus mairei, commonly known as the Maire asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red berries. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil.

Asparagus mairei is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
