Aristolochia aurita

Aristolochia aurita, commonly known as the golden vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves and distinctive, intricate, pipe-shaped flowers that are often greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that turn from green to bright red as they mature. Its unique floral structure is adapted for pollination by specific insects, and it contains various chemical compounds with potential medicinal properties.

However, it is also known to be toxic to humans and animals if ingested.
