Allium angulosum var. montanum

Allium angulosum var. montanum, commonly known as mountain garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale purple flowers in umbels. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, angular bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and herbal gardens.
