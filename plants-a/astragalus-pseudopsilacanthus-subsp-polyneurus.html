Astragalus pseudopsilacanthus subsp. polyneurus

Astragalus pseudopsilacanthus subsp.

polyneurus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

The plant features slender, upright stems with small, pinnately compound leaves. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to cream-colored, arranged in dense clusters.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
