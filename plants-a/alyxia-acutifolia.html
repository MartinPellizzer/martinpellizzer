Alyxia acutifolia

Alyxia acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf alyxia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a pointed tip, giving it its common name. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and are usually white or pale green.

Alyxia acutifolia is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
