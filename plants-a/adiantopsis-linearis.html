Adiantopsis linearis

Adiantopsis linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically 15 to 30 centimeters long. The plant features bipinnate leaves with a distinctive, finely divided structure, giving it a feathery appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Adiantopsis linearis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in a humid, indoor setting.
