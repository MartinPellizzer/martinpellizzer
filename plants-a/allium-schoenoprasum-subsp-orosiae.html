Allium schoenoprasum subsp. orosiae

Allium schoenoprasum subsp.

orosiae, commonly known as mountain chives, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

The plant produces slender, grass-like leaves and small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in early summer. Its bulbs are often used in culinary applications, similar to other Allium species, and it is valued for its aromatic foliage.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and adaptability to high-altitude conditions.
