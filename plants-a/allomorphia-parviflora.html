Allomorphia parviflora

Allomorphia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered allomorph, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hairy texture.

Allomorphia parviflora is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.
