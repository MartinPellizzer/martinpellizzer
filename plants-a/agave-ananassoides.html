Agave ananassoides

Agave ananassoides, commonly known as the pineapple agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage composed of thick, waxy, blue-green leaves that are often adorned with sharp spines. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears small, yellowish-green flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and ability to store water in its leaves make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
