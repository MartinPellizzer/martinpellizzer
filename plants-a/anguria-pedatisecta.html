Anguria pedatisecta

Anguria pedatisecta, commonly known as the footed cucumber, is a unique species within the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, foot-like leaf lobes that give it its scientific name. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical fruits that are typically green and turn yellow when mature. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blooms on the same plant.

Anguria pedatisecta is native to tropical regions and is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.
