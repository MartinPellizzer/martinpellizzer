Aspidosperma marcgravianum

Aspidosperma marcgravianum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Cerbera odollam*, is a member of the Apocynaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical, dark green leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it produces small, red berries that contain toxic seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture and traditional medicine.
