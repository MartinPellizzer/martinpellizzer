Anthurium griseosessile

Anthurium griseosessile, commonly known as the gray-stalked anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, grayish pubescence. The plant produces spadices, which are modified leaves that house the actual flowers, typically in a vivid red or orange color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Anthurium griseosessile is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and air-purifying properties.
