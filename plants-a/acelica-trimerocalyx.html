Acelica trimerocalyx

Acelica trimerocalyx, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant color make it a popular choice for gardeners and horticulturists.
