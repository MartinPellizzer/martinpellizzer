Alocasia suhirmaniana

Alocasia suhirmaniana, commonly known as the Suhirman's arrow leaf, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically hidden within a spathe, a feature common to many aroid species. It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
