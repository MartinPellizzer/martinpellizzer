Angorkis armeniaca

Angorkis armeniaca, commonly known as Armenian bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus, particularly Armenia and Turkey. The plant features showy, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stems.

Angorkis armeniaca is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also used in traditional medicine.
