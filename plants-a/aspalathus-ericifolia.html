Aspalathus ericifolia

Aspalathus ericifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf bush, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or sandy environments. It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and drought tolerance.

Aspalathus ericifolia is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
