Allium rollii

Allium rollii, commonly known as the Roll's leek, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, particularly in the Pyrenees and Alps. The plant features a bulbous base with long, narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are clustered in a loose umbel.

Allium rollii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
