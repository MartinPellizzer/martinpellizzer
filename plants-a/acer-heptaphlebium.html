Acer heptaphlebium

Acer heptaphlebium, commonly known as the seven-veined maple, is a rare and ornamental species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves with seven prominent veins, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, red or yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in temperate forests.

Due to its limited natural range and ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
