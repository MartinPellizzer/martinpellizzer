Aniseia salicifolia

Aniseia salicifolia, commonly known as the silky willow, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like branches and small, oval leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves and stems are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their mild medicinal properties.

Aniseia salicifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands.
