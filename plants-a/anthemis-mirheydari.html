Anthemis mirheydari

Anthemis mirheydari, commonly known as Persian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers that are often mistaken for chamomile due to their similar appearance. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, containing compounds that contribute to its medicinal properties.

Anthemis mirheydari is valued for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
