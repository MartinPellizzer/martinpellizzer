Allium assadii

Allium assadii, commonly known as the Armenian garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Turkey.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes various species of garlic and onions, and is characterized by its bulbous structure and grass-like foliage. The plant typically produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. Its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices due to their pungent aroma and potential health benefits.

Allium assadii is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
