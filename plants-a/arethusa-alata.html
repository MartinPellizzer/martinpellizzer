Arethusa alata

Arethusa alata, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, showy pink or purple flowers. The plant has a short, erect stem with a single, pouch-like flower that resembles a slipper. Its leaves are typically oval and arranged in a rosette pattern at the base.

Arethusa alata thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.
