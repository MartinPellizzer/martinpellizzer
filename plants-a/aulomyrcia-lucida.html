Aulomyrcia lucida

Aulomyrcia lucida, commonly known as the shining leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest understories, where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its moist habitat.

The flowers, which are usually white or pale yellow, bloom in clusters and attract various pollinators like bees and moths.
