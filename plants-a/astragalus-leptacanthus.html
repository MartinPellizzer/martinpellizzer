Astragalus leptacanthus

Astragalus leptacanthus, commonly known as the slender thorned milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny pods and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, bean-like seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus leptacanthus plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying desert flora.
