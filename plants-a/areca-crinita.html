Areca crinita

Areca crinita, commonly known as the areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery, palmate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Areca crinita is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its reproductive structures include small, clustered flowers that develop into drupes, which are typically dispersed by birds.
