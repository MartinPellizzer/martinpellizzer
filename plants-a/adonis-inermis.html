Adonis inermis

Adonis inermis, commonly known as the naked adonis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, single flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features a short, upright stem with heart-shaped leaves that are usually hairy. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens.

Adonis inermis is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, moist environments.
