Arisaema brasilianum

Arisaema brasilianum, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berries containing toxic seeds. Its rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though caution is advised due to the plant's potentially harmful properties.

Arisaema brasilianum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
