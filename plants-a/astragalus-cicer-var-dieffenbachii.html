Astragalus cicer var. dieffenbachii

Astragalus cicer var. dieffenbachii, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
