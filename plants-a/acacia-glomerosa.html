Acacia glomerosa

Acacia glomerosa, commonly known as the globular acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, globular crown and small, rounded phyllodes that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are a distinctive feature of the species. Acacia glomerosa is well adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in poor soils.

It is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental appearance and resilience.
