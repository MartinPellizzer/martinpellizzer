Andira inermis subsp. grandiflora

Andira inermis subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its large, showy red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects.

The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
