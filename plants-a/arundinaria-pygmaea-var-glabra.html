Arundinaria pygmaea var. glabra

Arundinaria pygmaea var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth bamboo, is a variant of the dwarf bamboo species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that grow in dense clumps, often reaching heights of only a few inches. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and covered with a smooth, glabrous surface, giving it its varietal name. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
