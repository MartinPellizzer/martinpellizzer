Acacia leeuweniana

Acacia leeuweniana, commonly known as the Cape York acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

The species is well adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystem.
