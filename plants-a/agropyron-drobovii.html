Agropyron drobovii

Agropyron drobovii, commonly known as the Drobovii wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in steppes and dry meadows, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion.

Agropyron drobovii is also valued for its potential in forage and ecological restoration projects.
