Astragalus Tephrodes Var. Typicus

Desert milkvetch, scientifically known as Astragalus tephrodes var. typicus, is a resilient plant adapted to survive in arid and semi-arid environments. It belongs to the legume family and is commonly found in desert regions across North America. This species thrives in sandy soils and can withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in the summer, adding a splash of color to the otherwise barren landscape. Despite its hardy nature, it plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
