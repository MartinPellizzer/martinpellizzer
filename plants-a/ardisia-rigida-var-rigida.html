Ardisia rigida var. rigida

Ardisia rigida var. rigida, commonly known as the hardy evergreen shrub, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green elliptical leaves and produces small, red berries that are attractive to birds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
