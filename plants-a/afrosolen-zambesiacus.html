Afrosolen zambesiacus

Afrosolen zambesiacus, commonly known as the Zambesi lily, is a striking perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant's leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and wetlands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections across the region.
