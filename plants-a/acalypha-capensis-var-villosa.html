Acalypha capensis var. villosa

Acalypha capensis var. villosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or woolly spurflower, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
