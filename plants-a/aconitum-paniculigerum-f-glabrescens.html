Aconitum paniculigerum f. glabrescens

Aconitum paniculigerum f. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers arranged in a panicle. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves with a distinctive veining pattern. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The f. glabrescens form is notable for its reduced pubescence, giving it a smoother appearance compared to the typical species.
