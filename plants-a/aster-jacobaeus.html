Aster jacobaeus

Aster jacobaeus, commonly known as the common mullein, is a biennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, velvety leaves that are covered in soft, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of purple, pink, or white, which bloom in the summer months. Aster jacobaeus thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in roadsides, fields, and waste areas.

It has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties, particularly for respiratory ailments.
