Ampelopsis quinquefolia var. laciniata

Ampelopsis quinquefolia var. laciniata, commonly known as the dissected ivy-leaf grapevine, is a climbing vine native to North America.

It features compound leaves with finely cut lobes, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into blue-black berries. It thrives in woodland areas and is often found along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
