Anocheile

Anocheile, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a member of the Cactaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is known for its striking white flowers that open at night. The plant has a tall, spiny stem that can grow up to several meters in height. Its flowers are large, showy, and emit a sweet fragrance to attract pollinators.

Anocheile is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
