Ardisia caudata

Ardisia caudata, commonly known as the red-berried evergreen or Chinese beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by clusters of bright red berries, which are edible for humans and birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The berries are a key feature, providing ornamental value and supporting local wildlife.
