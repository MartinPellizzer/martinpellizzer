Attalea phalerata

Attalea phalerata, commonly known as the palm heart or pequi, is a small palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its short trunk and pinnate leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellow fruits that are edible and have a nutty flavor. Its leaves are also used for thatching and basket weaving in some regions.

The species is valued for both its ecological role and economic utility in local communities.
