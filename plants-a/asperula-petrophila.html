Asperula petrophila

Asperula petrophila, commonly known as the rock asperula, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is a member of the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Asperula petrophila thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found growing on rocky slopes or in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a decorative plant in shaded environments.
