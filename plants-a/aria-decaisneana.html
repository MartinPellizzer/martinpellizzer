Aria decaisneana

Aria decaisneana, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a dramatic appearance in the spring. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that turn red in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Aria decaisneana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
