Angelica crucifolia

Angelica crucifolia, commonly known as the garden angelica, is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, umbrella-shaped compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clustered umbels, which bloom in late summer. Angelica crucifolia is known for its aromatic, slightly sweet scent and its use in traditional herbal medicine.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
