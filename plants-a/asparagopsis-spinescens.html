Asparagopsis spinescens

Asparagopsis spinescens, commonly known as the spiny asparagus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in umbels, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Asparagopsis spinescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
