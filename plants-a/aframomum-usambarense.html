Aframomum usambarense

Aframomum usambarense, commonly known as African cardamom, is a tropical plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, tubular flowers and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and its leaves are broad and lance-shaped. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
