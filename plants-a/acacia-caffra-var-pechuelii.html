Acacia caffra var. pechuelii

Acacia caffra var. pechuelii, commonly known as the silver acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grey-green foliage and long, yellow flower spikes that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has small, oval leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
