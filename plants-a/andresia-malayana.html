Andresia malayana

Andresia malayana, commonly known as the Malayan bellflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
