Aristida chapadensis

Aristida chapadensis, commonly known as the Chapada grass, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of numerous small spikelets.

Aristida chapadensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
