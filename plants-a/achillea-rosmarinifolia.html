Achillea rosmarinifolia

Achillea rosmarinifolia, commonly known as sea holly, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, fern-like leaves and tall, spiky flower heads that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its aromatic foliage and long-lasting blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Additionally, it is known for its resilience to drought and salt spray, enhancing its adaptability to harsh environments.
