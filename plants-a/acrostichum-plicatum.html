Acrostichum plicatum

Acrostichum plicatum, commonly known as the fan-leaf fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, pinnate fronds that are often folded or pleated, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and swamps. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine.

The fan-leaf fern is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in humid ecosystems.
