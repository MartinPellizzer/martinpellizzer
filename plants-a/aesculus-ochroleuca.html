Aesculus ochroleuca

Aesculus ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow buckeye, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Saraceae family and is characterized by its palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The tree produces showy, yellowish-green flowers in upright clusters, followed by spiny, spherical fruits containing one to three seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often with shallow furrows, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in Native American cultures.
