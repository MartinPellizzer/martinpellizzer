Amomum yunnanense

Amomum yunnanense, commonly known as Yunnan cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces spindle-shaped, dark brown fruits that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and distinctive flavor.
