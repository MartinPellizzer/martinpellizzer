Ascolepis trigona

Ascolepis trigona, commonly known as the triangular orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, triangular-shaped pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Ascolepis trigona is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
