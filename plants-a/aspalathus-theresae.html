Aspalathus theresae

Aspalathus theresae, commonly known as the Red Bush willow, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often reddish stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Aspalathus theresae is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
