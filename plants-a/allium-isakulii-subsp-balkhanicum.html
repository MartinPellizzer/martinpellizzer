Allium isakulii subsp. balkhanicum

Allium isakulii subsp.

balkhanicum, commonly known as the Balkhan mountain garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers.

The plant produces rounded, fleshy bulbs that are adapted to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it tolerates cold temperatures and limited moisture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cuisines.
