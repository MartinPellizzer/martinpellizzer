Adenoropium divaricatum

Adenoropium divaricatum, commonly known as the variable orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are elongated and slightly hairy, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

Adenoropium divaricatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
