Astragalus ochotensis

Astragalus ochotensis, commonly known as the Ochotan milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Siberia and northern Mongolia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to cold, arid environments and often grows in rocky or sandy soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus ochotensis is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
