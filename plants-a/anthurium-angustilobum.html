Anthurium angustilobum

Anthurium angustilobum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed anthurium, is a tropical flowering plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its leaves are elongated, glossy, and often have a dark green to reddish hue, while its flowers are small and clustered.

Anthurium angustilobum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse environments.
