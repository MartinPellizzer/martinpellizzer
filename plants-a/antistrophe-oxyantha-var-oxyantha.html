Antistrophe oxyantha var. oxyantha

Antistrophe oxyantha var. oxyantha, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small blue or purple clouds. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine, silvery hairs, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often hairy. It produces showy, daisy-like blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
