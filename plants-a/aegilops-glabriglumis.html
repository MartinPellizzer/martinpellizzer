Aegilops glabriglumis

Aegilops glabriglumis, commonly known as the glabrous spikelet wild oat, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, glabrous (smooth) spikelets and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its close relation to cultivated wheat, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Morphology

Aegilops glabriglumis has narrow, grass-like leaves that are linear in shape, alternate in arrangement, and typically range from 5 to 15 cm in length, with a bluish-green color.

The stem is cylindrical, smooth, and slightly hairy, growing upright with a clumping growth pattern. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with greenish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, relying on wind pollination. The fruit is a small, three-angled spikelet containing three glumes, and the seeds are dispersed by wind due to their lightweight nature.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, anchoring the plant in loose, sandy soils.
