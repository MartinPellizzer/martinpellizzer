Anthyllis × currasii

Anthyllis × currasii, commonly known as the Portuguese clover, is a hybrid plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink or purple. The plant is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is often found in rocky or calcareous soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it is well adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Anthyllis × currasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
