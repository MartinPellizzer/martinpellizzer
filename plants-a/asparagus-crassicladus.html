Asparagus crassicladus

Asparagus crassicladus, commonly known as the thick-leaved asparagus, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its robust, dark green, leathery leaves that are broader and more substantial than those of other asparagus species. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, needle-like spears that emerge from the soil. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its dense foliage and ability to form compact, upright clumps.
