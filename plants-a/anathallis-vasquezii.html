Anathallis vasquezii

Anathallis vasquezii, commonly known as the Vasquez's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, translucent flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant has a unique structure with a slender, hairless stem and a single, elongated leaf. It is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees without deriving nutrients from them.

Due to its restricted habitat and fragility, Anathallis vasquezii is considered endangered and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
