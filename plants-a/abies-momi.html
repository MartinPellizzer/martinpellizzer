Abies momi

Abies momi, commonly known as the Japanese fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its pyramidal shape and dense, dark green foliage. The needles are flat, soft, and have a subtle blue-gray tint, while the cones are small and cylindrical. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and reforestation efforts.
