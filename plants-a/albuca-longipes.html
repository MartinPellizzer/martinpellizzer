Albuca longipes

Albuca longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed albuca, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and tall, erect flowering stems. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in dense clusters. Albuca longipes thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
