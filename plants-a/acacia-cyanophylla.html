Acacia cyanophylla

Acacia cyanophylla, commonly known as the blue-leaf acacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, alternate leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in spherical clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its wood is hard and durable, making it useful for construction and fuel.

Acacia cyanophylla is adapted to survive in harsh conditions with minimal water, showcasing its resilience in dry environments.
