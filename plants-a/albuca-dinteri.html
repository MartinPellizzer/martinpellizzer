Albuca dinteri

Albuca dinteri, commonly known as the Dinter's albuca, is a striking bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color, and it produces clusters of white or yellow flowers in the spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes. Its bulbs are perennial, allowing it to regrow each year after a period of dormancy.

Albuca dinteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
