Astragalus xanthoxiphidium subsp. rectus

Astragalus xanthoxiphidium subsp.

rectus, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia. It is characterized by its yellow flowers and stipitate leaves, which are typically pubescent.

The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep taproot system that allows it to survive in dry conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock.
