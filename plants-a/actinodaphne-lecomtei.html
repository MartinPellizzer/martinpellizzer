Actinodaphne lecomtei

Actinodaphne lecomtei, commonly known as the Lecomte's camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by spherical, black berries. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional medicine.
