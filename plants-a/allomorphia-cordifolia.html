Allomorphia cordifolia

Allomorphia cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf allomorph, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
