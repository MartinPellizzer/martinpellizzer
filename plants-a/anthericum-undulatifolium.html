Anthericum undulatifolium

Anthericum undulatifolium, commonly known as the wavy-leaved lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the wetlands and marshes of eastern North America.

It features rhizomatous growth and produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with six petals. The leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive wavy margin, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swampy areas along rivers and lakes.

The plant is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
