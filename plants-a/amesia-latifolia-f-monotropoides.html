Amesia latifolia f. monotropoides

Amesia latifolia f. monotropoides, commonly known as the creeping amerika, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has narrow, slightly hairy leaves that are arranged in a compact, cushion-like structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and drought-tolerant ornamental plant.
