Aconitum sherriffii

Aconitum sherriffii, commonly known as Sherriff's aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely leafed stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense clusters at the top of the stem. Its roots contain toxic alkaloids, making it a potentially dangerous plant to handle.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.
