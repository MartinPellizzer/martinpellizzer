Anoda periptera var. macrantha

Anoda periptera var. macrantha, commonly known as the Mexican blueweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, clustered blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Acanthaceae family.
