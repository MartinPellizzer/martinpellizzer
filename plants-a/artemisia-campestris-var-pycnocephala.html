Artemisia campestris var. pycnocephala

Artemisia campestris var. pycnocephala, commonly known as rough tarragon, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, gray-green foliage and aromatic, slightly bitter scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden environments.

This variety is often used in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb due to its distinctive flavor.
