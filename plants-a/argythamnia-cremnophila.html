Argythamnia cremnophila

Argythamnia cremnophila, commonly known as the rock thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, gray-green foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flowers.

Its unique morphology makes it a notable species in its native habitat.
