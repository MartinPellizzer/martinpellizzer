Asteromyrtus angustifolia

Asteromyrtus angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal environments.

The essential oils from its leaves are also valued for their fragrance and potential medicinal properties.
