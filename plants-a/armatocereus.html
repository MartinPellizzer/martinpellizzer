Armatocereus

Armatocereus, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a genus of cacti native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and clusters of vibrant, showy flowers that bloom at night. The plant typically grows in arid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive. Its flowers are often pale yellow or white, with some varieties displaying red or orange hues.

Armatocereus is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
