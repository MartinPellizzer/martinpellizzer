Astragalus ancistrocarpus var. brachycarpus

Astragalus ancistrocarpus var. brachycarpus, commonly known as the shortpod milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in late spring. Its small, elliptical pods are characteristic of the varietal form brachycarpus.

It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
