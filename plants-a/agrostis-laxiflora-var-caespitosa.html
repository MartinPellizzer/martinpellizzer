Agrostis laxiflora var. caespitosa

Agrostis laxiflora var. caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted bentgrass, is a perennial grass species characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit.

It typically features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. This variety is native to Europe and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in meadows and along stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in turfgrass and landscaping.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
