Arabis thaliana var. urbicola

Arabis thaliana var. urbicola, commonly known as the urban rockcress, is a variant of the Arabis thaliana species that thrives in urban environments.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous plant with basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It is adapted to tolerate pollution and compacted soils, making it a common sight in city settings.

This plant is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for small insects.
