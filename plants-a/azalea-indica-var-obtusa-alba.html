Azalea indica var. obtusa-alba

Azalea indica var. obtusa-alba, commonly known as the white-flowered azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, broad leaves and clusters of showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant floral display and compact growth habit.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
