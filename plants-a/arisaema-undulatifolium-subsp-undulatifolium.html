Arisaema undulatifolium subsp. undulatifolium

Arisaema undulatifolium subsp.

undulatifolium, commonly known as the wavy-leaf arisaema, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, undulating, heart-shaped leaves that give it its common name.

The plant produces striking spadices, which are typically green with a yellow or white spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
