Alchemilla venusta var. fulvescens

Alchemilla venusta var. fulvescens, commonly known as the golden alchemilla, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features upright, branching stems covered in soft, velvety leaves that range from green to reddish hues. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
