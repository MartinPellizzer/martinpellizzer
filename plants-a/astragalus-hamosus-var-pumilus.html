Astragalus hamosus var. pumilus

Astragalus hamosus var. pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf milkvetch, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically features slender, branched stems and small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its roots are often fibrous and adapted to drought conditions. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

This variety is notable for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
