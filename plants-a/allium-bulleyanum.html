Allium bulleyanum

Allium bulleyanum, commonly known as the Chinese leek or Chinese chives, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its long, hollow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple or white flowers in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and are often used in Chinese cuisine for their mild, onion-like flavor.

The plant is relatively easy to grow and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
