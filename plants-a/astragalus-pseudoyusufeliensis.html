Astragalus pseudoyusufeliensis

Astragalus pseudoyusufeliensis, commonly known as the Yushufel-like milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. Native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, this species thrives in sandy or rocky soils. The plant has small, compound leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
