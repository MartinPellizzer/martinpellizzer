Adamanthus longibracteatus var. luteorubrus

Adamanthus longibracteatus var. luteorubrus, commonly known as the red-bracted hibiscus, is a striking flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular red and orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a deep green color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens and native plant landscapes.
