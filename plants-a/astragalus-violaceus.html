Astragalus violaceus

Astragalus violaceus, commonly known as violet milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, violet or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe and open woodlands.

Astragalus violaceus plays a role in soil stabilization and is a food source for various pollinators and wildlife.
