Ardisia veraguasensis

Ardisia veraguasensis, commonly known as the Veragua beautyberry, is a shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by bright red berries. These berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Ardisia veraguasensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive foliage and colorful fruit.
