Acrotriche halmaturina

Acrotriche halmaturina, commonly known as the Halmaturina daisy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale blue daisy-like flowers. The plant has a low-growing, mat-forming structure with hairy stems and leaves adapted to withstand harsh, dry conditions. Its flowering period typically occurs in spring, and it is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is considered a species of conservation interest in certain areas.
