Aconitum lasiocarpum subsp. lasiocarpum

Aconitum lasiocarpum subsp.

lasiocarpum, commonly known as the woolly fringed monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers with distinctive hood-shaped petals.

The plant features a tall, unbranched stem covered in dense, woolly hairs, which gives it its common name. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments that are also hairy. Aconitum lasiocarpum subsp.

lasiocarpum is adapted to grow in rocky or alpine environments, often at high elevations where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
