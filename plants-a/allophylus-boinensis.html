Allophylus boinensis

Allophylus boinensis, commonly known as the African snowberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets, and its flowers are small, greenish-white, and arranged in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically white or pale yellow when mature. It is often found in rocky or coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Allophylus boinensis is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
