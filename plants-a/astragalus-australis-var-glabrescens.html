Astragalus australis var. glabrescens

Astragalus australis var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth astragalus, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It has small, hairy leaves that become smoother as the plant matures, giving it its varietal name.

This species is adapted to withstand drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
