Allocasuarina decussata

Allocasuarina decussata, commonly known as the mountain she-oak, is a shrub or small tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, needle-like foliage that forms dense, cylindrical clusters. The plant produces small, spherical flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland heathlands.

Allocasuarina decussata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
