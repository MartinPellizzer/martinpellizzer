Agapetes leiocarpa

Agapetes leiocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-leaved silverbush, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows at high elevations, often in cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Agapetes leiocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
