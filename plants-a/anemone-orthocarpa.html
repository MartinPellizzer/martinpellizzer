Anemone orthocarpa

Anemone orthocarpa, commonly known as the showy anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern United States and southern Canada.

It features showy, pink to purple flowers with five to nine petals, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a fibrous root system and basal leaves that are deeply lobed and hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a nectar source for pollinators and contributes to its ecological significance in native habitats.
