Aspidium adiantiforme

Aspidium adiantiforme, commonly known as the false maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble those of the true maidenhair fern, Adiantum. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky areas, where it thrives in humid conditions. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a common reproductive feature in ferns.

Aspidium adiantiforme is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
