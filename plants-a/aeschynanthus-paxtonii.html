Aeschynanthus paxtonii

Aeschynanthus paxtonii, commonly known as the Christmas ivy, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and dense, glossy dark green foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color, which bloom in clusters during the winter season. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture to maintain its lush appearance.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in hanging baskets.
