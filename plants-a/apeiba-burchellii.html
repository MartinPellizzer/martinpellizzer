Apeiba burchellii

Apeiba burchellii, commonly known as the African violet tree, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often arranged in clusters at the ends of branches.

Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
