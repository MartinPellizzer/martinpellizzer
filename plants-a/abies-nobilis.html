Abies nobilis

Abies nobilis, commonly known as the white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its pyramidal shape, dark green needles, and distinctive cone-like structures that hang from the branches. The tree typically grows in high elevation areas, preferring cool, moist climates with well-drained soils. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against environmental stressors.

The white fir plays an important role in forest ecosystems, offering habitat and food for various wildlife species.
