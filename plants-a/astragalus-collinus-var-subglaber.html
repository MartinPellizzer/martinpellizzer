Astragalus collinus var. subglaber

Astragalus collinus var. subglaber, commonly known as the smoothleaf milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant has small, pinnately compound leaves with rounded leaflets, and it produces narrow, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
