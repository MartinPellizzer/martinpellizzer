Astragalus glaux var. macrocephalus

Astragalus glaux var. macrocephalus, commonly known as the large-headed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, yellowish to brownish pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are typically dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or steppe environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
