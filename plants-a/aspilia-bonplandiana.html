Aspilia bonplandiana

Aspilia bonplandiana, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Aspilia bonplandiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
