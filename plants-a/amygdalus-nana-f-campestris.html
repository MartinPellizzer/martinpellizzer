Amygdalus nana f. campestris

Amygdalus nana f. campestris, commonly known as the dwarf peach or miniature peach, is a compact, ornamental variant of the peach tree.

It features small, rounded leaves and produces fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its short stature, making it ideal for small gardens or containers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring regular watering during the growing season.

This cultivar is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ability to bear edible fruit despite its diminutive size.
