Andropogon glaucopsis

Andropogon glaucopsis, commonly known as blue bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its bluish-gray foliage and tufted inflorescence, which consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle. The plant typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed sites, preferring well-drained soils. Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil, making it valuable for erosion control.

Andropogon glaucopsis is also an important forage plant for wildlife and livestock.
