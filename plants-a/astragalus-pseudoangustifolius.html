Astragalus pseudoangustifolius

Astragalus pseudoangustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with slender, linear leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow to reddish-brown flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in early summer. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in arid and rocky environments.

Astragalus pseudoangustifolius is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
