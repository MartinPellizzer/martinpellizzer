Areca triandra var. bancana

Areca triandra var. bancana, commonly known as the Borneo areca, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters at the base of the leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The Borneo areca is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been used in traditional crafts and construction.
