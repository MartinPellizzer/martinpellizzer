Astragalus xinjiangnensis

Astragalus xinjiangnensis, commonly known as Xinjiang milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Xinjiang, China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stem and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by lens-shaped pods. It is well adapted to dry and saline soils, making it a hardy species found in semi-desert and steppe environments.

Astragalus xinjiangnensis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
