Astragalus cartilagineus subsp. cartilagineus

Astragalus cartilagineus subsp.

cartilagineus, commonly known as the cartilaginous milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers in racemes. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity.
