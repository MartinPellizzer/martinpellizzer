Averrhoa dolichocarpa

Averrhoa dolichocarpa, commonly known as the Natal plum, is a tropical shrub native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are typically eaten fresh or used in jams and beverages. The plant has simple, alternate leaves and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is rich in vitamins and has been used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.
