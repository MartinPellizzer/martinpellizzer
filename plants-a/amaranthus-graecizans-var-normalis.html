Amaranthus graecizans var. normalis

Amaranthus graecizans var. normalis, commonly known as the Greek amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a slightly rough texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in some traditional cuisines.
