Anthyllis hermanniae subsp. hermanniae

Anthyllis hermanniae subsp.

hermanniae, commonly known as the Hermann's anthyllis, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple.

The plant features trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture, and its root system is adapted to thrive in dry, rocky soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.

This species is often found in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and coastal cliffs.
