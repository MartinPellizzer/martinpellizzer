Achillea aleppica subsp. zederbaueri

Achillea aleppica subsp.

zederbaueri, commonly known as the Aleppo thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features a robust root system and erect, branched stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, yellow to golden flower heads, which are surrounded by pale green, spiny bracts. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with serrated edges and a hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is well adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open, sunny areas.
