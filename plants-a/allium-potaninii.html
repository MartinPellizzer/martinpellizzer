Allium potaninii

Allium potaninii, commonly known as the Potanin's garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, ovoid bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
