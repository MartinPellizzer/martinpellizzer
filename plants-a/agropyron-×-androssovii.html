Agropyron × androssovii

Agropyron × androssovii, commonly known as the Androsowian wheatgrass, is a hybrid grass species that results from the cross between Agropyron cristatum and Agropyron elongatum.

It is native to the steppes of Central Asia and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, fibrous root system. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and produces spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the stems. It is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it a valuable forage grass in arid regions.

Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and a hardy nature that allows it to thrive in poor soil conditions.
