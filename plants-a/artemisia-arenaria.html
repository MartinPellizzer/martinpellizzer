Artemisia arenaria

Artemisia arenaria, commonly known as sand artemisia, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and small, narrow leaves that are adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in small heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Artemisia arenaria is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
