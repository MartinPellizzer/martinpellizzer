Aralia bipinnata f. inermis

Aralia bipinnata f. inermis, commonly known as the smooth-leaf aralia, is a variant of the bipinnate aralia characterized by its smooth, often waxy foliage.

It is a member of the Araliaceae family and is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Indonesia. The plant typically features compound leaves with multiple pairs of leaflets, giving it a feathery appearance. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbels, which are followed by bright red berries.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
