Astragalus aharicus

Astragalus aharicus, commonly known as the Armenian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of many legumes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or semi-arid environments.

Astragalus aharicus is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
