Aspalathus argyrella

Aspalathus argyrella, commonly known as silverleaf bush, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a leathery texture.

Aspalathus argyrella is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, Mediterranean climates.
