Aechmea magdalenae var. quadricolor

Aechmea magdalenae var. quadricolor, commonly known as the Rainbow Bromeliad, is a striking bromeliad native to Colombia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that display red, orange, yellow, and green hues, creating a visually stunning display. The plant has a rosette-shaped leaf structure that forms a central cup, which collects rainwater for hydration. Its foliage is typically broad and grass-like, with a waxy texture that helps retain moisture.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
