Androsace lanuginosa var. typica

Androsace lanuginosa var. typica, commonly known as the downy androsace, is a low-growing perennial herb native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with dense, hairy foliage that helps it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a soft, downy appearance.

This species is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude climates.
