Aster liburnicus

Aster liburnicus, commonly known as the Liburnian aster, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, displaying shades of purple and white.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
