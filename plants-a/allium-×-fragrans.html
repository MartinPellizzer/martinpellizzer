Allium × fragrans

Allium × fragrans, commonly known as the Chinese leek or Egyptian walking onions, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its bulbous base and tall, hollow stems that bear clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers in umbel-like clusters, which are followed by spherical, bulb-like structures that can be harvested for culinary use. Native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Its aromatic foliage and edible bulbs make it a valued addition to both gardens and kitchens.
