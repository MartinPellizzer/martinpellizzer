Aconitum sczukinii var. pauciflorum

Aconitum sczukinii var. pauciflorum, commonly known as the rare alpine aconite, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has a robust root system and lance-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
