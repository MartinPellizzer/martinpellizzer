Asparagus falcatus var. ternifolius

Asparagus falcatus var. ternifolius, commonly known as the three-leaved asparagus, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The leaves are pinnately compound, with three to five leaflets that are lanceolate and often have a bluish-green hue. This variety is prized for its unique foliage and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is typically grown in containers or as part of rock gardens due to its preference for sunny, dry conditions.
