Astragalus lasiosemius subsp. roschanicus

Astragalus lasiosemius subsp.

roschanicus, commonly known as the Roschan's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy stems and small, pod-like fruits. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to dry, temperate climates.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
