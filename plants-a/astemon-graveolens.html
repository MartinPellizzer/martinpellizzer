Astemon graveolens

Astemon graveolens, commonly known as the sweet sultan or African star, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its fragrance and ornamental value make it a sought-after ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
