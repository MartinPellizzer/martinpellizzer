Ascocentrum pumilum

Ascocentrum pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf arrowroot, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped, hence the common name, and are often used in traditional medicine.

Ascocentrum pumilum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some medicinal uses in its native regions.
