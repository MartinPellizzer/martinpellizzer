Arisaema lackneri

Arisaema lackneri, commonly known as the Lackner's arisaema, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence structure.

It is native to the mountainous regions of China, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments. The plant features a stout, underground rhizome with a single, leafy stem that bears a large, palmately lobed leaf. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe, which is often brightly colored.

Arisaema lackneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its distinctive foliage and floral display.
