Artocarpus reniformis

Artocarpus reniformis, commonly known as the breadfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a broad, buttressed trunk and large, lobed leaves. The plant produces a unique fruit with a thick, fibrous outer layer and a starchy, edible interior. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same tree.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and cultural significance.
