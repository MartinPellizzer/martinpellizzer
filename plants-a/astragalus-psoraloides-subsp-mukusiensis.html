Astragalus psoraloides subsp. mukusiensis

Astragalus psoraloides subsp.

mukusiensis, commonly known as Mukusi milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to eastern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow.

The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is of interest for its potential ecological roles and may have uses in traditional medicine or for soil stabilization.
