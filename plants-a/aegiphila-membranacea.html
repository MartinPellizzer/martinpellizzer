Aegiphila membranacea

Aegiphila membranacea, commonly known as the membranous aegiphila, is a shrub native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color.

Aegiphila membranacea is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.
