Arachnothryx inconstans

Arachnothryx inconstans, commonly known as the variable arachnothryx, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in forest understories where it utilizes its climbing structure to reach sunlight.

Botanically, it is noted for its variable morphology, which contributes to its common name.
