Aloe inyangensis var. kimberleyana

Aloe inyangensis var. kimberleyana, commonly known as Kimberley aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, adapted for water storage in arid environments. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellow to orange flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its resilience and ornamental value make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
