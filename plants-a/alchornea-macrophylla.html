Alchornea macrophylla

Alchornea macrophylla, commonly known as the broadleaf alchornea, is a flowering plant species native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Alchornea macrophylla thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests and along riverbanks.
