Amelanchier ovalis var. rhamnoides

Amelanchier ovalis var. rhamnoides, commonly known as the Canadian serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 8 meters, featuring a rounded crown and smooth, grayish bark. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, red or yellow berries in the summer. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges, and turn vibrant red or orange in the fall.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
