Astragalus schimperi var. aradensis

Astragalus schimperi var. aradensis, commonly known as the Arad Astragalus, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to brown flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically visited by pollinators such as bees. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance through deep root systems and reduced leaf surface area.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
