Agropyron panormitanum var. zahlense

Agropyron panormitanum var. zahlense, commonly known as the Sicilian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and gravelly fields.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
