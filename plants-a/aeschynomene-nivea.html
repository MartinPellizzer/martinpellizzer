Aeschynomene nivea

Aeschynomene nivea, commonly known as white horsegram, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its seeds are encased in pods and are often used as a protein-rich food source in some parts of the world.

Aeschynomene nivea is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
