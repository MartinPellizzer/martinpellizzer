Andropogon blumii

Andropogon blumii, commonly known as the Blumii switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for native plant gardens and restoration projects.

Andropogon blumii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife and soil health.
