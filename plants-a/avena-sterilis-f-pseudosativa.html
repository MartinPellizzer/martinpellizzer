Avena sterilis f. pseudosativa

Avena sterilis f. pseudosativa, commonly known as false oat, is a sterile variant of the wild oat species.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open areas and is often found in disturbed soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers and lacks the ability to produce viable seeds.

This plant is sometimes used as a forage crop or in ecological restoration projects.
