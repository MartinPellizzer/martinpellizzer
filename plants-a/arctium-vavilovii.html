Arctium vavilovii

Arctium vavilovii, commonly known as the Siberian burdock, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with rough textures and produces purple to brown flower heads that develop into spiky burrs. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in meadows.

Arctium vavilovii is also noted for its medicinal properties, with its roots traditionally used in herbal remedies.
