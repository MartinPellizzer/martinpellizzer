Aspidium sieboldii

Aspidium sieboldii, commonly known as Siebold's water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive, light green coloration. Native to East Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. The plant's spores are produced on the underside of its fronds, and it reproduces through sexual reproduction via spore dispersal.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
