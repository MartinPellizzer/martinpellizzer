Assonia lucida

Assonia lucida, commonly known as the shining sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. Its showy, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Assonia lucida is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils.
