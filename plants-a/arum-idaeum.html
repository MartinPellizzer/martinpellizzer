Arum idaeum

Arum idaeum, commonly known as the Idaeum arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features arrowhead-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and have a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, white spadix flowers surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Arum idaeum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and wildlife.
