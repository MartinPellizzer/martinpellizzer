Anagallis sessilis

Anagallis sessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water primrose, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or white in color. The plant has opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist environments, such as wetlands and stream banks, and is often found in shallow water.

Anagallis sessilis is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.
