Andropogon virginicus

Andropogon virginicus, commonly known as switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. Switchgrass thrives in a variety of soil types and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for prairie and wetland environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This plant is also valued for its ecological benefits, including erosion control and as a potential bioenergy crop.
