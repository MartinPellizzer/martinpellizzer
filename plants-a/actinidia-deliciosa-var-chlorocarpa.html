Actinidia deliciosa var. chlorocarpa

Actinidia deliciosa var. chlorocarpa, commonly known as the golden kiwi, is a variant of the Actinidia deliciosa species native to China.

It is characterized by its yellow fruit, which is typically smaller and less sweet compared to the standard kiwi. The plant is a vigorous vine that produces heart-shaped leaves and has a rapid growth rate. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique fruit.
