Acacia alaticaulis

Acacia alaticaulis, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and bipinnate leaves that are often reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in clusters, which are followed by narrow, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia alaticaulis plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
