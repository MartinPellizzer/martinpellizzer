Allium fibrillum

Allium fibrillum, commonly known as the threadleaf garlic, is a bulbous plant native to central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered white or purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its elongated, fibrous bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium fibrillum is valued for its aromatic properties and ornamental appeal in gardens.
