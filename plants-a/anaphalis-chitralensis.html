Anaphalis chitralensis

Anaphalis chitralensis, commonly known as the Chitral silverweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces fluffy, white capitula that resemble small clouds, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

Anaphalis chitralensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique visual appeal.
