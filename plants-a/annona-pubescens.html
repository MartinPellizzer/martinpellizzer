Annona pubescens

Annona pubescens, commonly known as the rough-skinned custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small, deciduous tree or shrub that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant has a rough, hairy texture on its leaves and stems, which gives it its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow with a reddish tinge and are followed by oval, fleshy fruits.

The fruit has a thick, yellowish rind and a sweet, creamy pulp with a distinctive aroma.
