Aponogeton abyssinicus

Aponogeton abyssinicus, commonly known as the Abyssinian arrowhead, is a unique aquatic plant native to Ethiopia.

It features slender, arrow-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, with a distinctive waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, white flowers that emerge above the water, adding a striking visual element to its habitat. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for aquatic gardens and artificial ponds.
