Acacia seyal var. kassonionga

Acacia seyal var. kassonionga, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, yellow flowers that bloom in globular clusters. The plant produces flat, bean-like pods containing seeds, and its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, feather-like leaflets. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that help it survive in harsh environments.

The wood of this acacia is hard and durable, making it valuable for local use in construction and fuel.
