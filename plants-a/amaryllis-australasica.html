Amaryllis australasica

Amaryllis australasica, commonly known as the New Zealand amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces a rosette of broad, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

Amaryllis australasica is also known for its ability to bloom multiple times throughout the growing season.
