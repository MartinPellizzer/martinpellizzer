Astripomoea malvacea var. parviflora

Astripomoea malvacea var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered mallows, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
