Aconitum sakuraii var. micranthum

Aconitum sakuraii var. micranthum, commonly known as the Japanese monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features striking blue to purple flowers arranged in tall, spires, which are characteristic of the Aconitum genus. The plant has palmate leaves with deeply cut lobes, giving it a distinctive and ornamental appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

However, it is highly toxic due to the presence of potent alkaloids, requiring caution in cultivation and handling.
