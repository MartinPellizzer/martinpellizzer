Anthurium johnsoniae

Anthurium johnsoniae, commonly known as the "Red Velvet" plant, is a tropical species native to Colombia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are deep red on the underside and green on the top. The plant produces small, spadix-shaped inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is typically white or yellow. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a houseplant.
