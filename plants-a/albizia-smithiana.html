Albizia smithiana

Albizia smithiana, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal regions and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for wildlife.

Albizia smithiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
