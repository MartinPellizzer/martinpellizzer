Amaranthus flavus var. benhardii

Amaranthus flavus var. benhardii, commonly known as the yellow amaranth, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and upright growth habit. The plant produces small, bead-like seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. Its leaves are edible and can be consumed both raw and cooked.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and nutritional content.
