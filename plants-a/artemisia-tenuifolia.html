Artemisia tenuifolia

Artemisia tenuifolia, commonly known as silver sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, and it is often found in dry, rocky habitats. Artemisia tenuifolia is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its aromatic properties.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife in arid environments.
