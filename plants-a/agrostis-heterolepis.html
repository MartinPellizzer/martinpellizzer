Agrostis heterolepis

Agrostis heterolepis, commonly known as rough bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and have a slightly rough texture, while the seed heads are slender and often reddish in color.

Agrostis heterolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
