Aniba terminalis

Aniba terminalis, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces black, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.
