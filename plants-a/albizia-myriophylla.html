Albizia myriophylla

Albizia myriophylla, commonly known as the flame acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are often feathery and bipinnate, giving it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, arranged in dense inflorescences. Albizia myriophylla thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
