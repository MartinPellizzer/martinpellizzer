Acer Platanoides F. Undulatum

Norway maple, scientifically known as Acer platanoides f. undulatum, is a striking ornamental tree prized for its vibrant autumn foliage and distinctive heart-shaped leaves. This cultivar, a variant of the Norway maple, exhibits a unique undulating leaf margin that sets it apart from its parent species. Native to Europe, it has been widely cultivated in temperate regions for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions. Its popularity in urban landscapes stems from its tolerance to pollution and ability to thrive in compact spaces. However, its invasive tendencies in some regions have sparked debates about its ecological impact.
