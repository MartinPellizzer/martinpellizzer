Archidendron racemosum

Archidendron racemosum, commonly known as the racemose legume, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are usually small and clustered in racemes, giving it its common name.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
