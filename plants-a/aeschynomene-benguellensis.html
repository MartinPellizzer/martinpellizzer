Aeschynomene benguellensis

Aeschynomene benguellensis, commonly known as the African burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, often branched stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered pods that contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous husk. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Aeschynomene benguellensis is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
