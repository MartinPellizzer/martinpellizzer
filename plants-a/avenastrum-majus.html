Avenastrum majus

Avenastrum majus, commonly known as the lesser tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and sandy soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters.

Avenastrum majus is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
