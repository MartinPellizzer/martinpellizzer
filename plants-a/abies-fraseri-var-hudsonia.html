Abies fraseri var. hudsonia

Abies fraseri var. hudsonia, commonly known as the Hudson's fir, is a variant of the Fraser fir native to the Appalachian Mountains.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves. The foliage is typically dark green with a silvery-white underside, providing a striking visual contrast. It produces cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-elevation forests.
