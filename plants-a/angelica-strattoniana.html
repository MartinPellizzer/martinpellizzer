Angelica strattoniana

Angelica strattoniana, commonly known as the Sierra Angelica, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, umbrella-like compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a complex inflorescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Angelica strattoniana is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
