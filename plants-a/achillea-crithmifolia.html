Achillea crithmifolia

Achillea crithmifolia, commonly known as sea holly, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in coastal areas and well-drained soils, making it well adapted to saline and rocky environments. Its aromatic leaves and showy blooms make it a popular choice for coastal gardens and rockeries.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
