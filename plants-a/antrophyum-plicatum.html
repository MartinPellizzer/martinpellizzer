Antrophyum plicatum

Antrophyum plicatum, commonly known as the pleated cycad, is a rare and ancient plant species belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pleated leaf sheaths that give it its common name. Native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant has a short trunk and produces large, feather-like leaves that can reach several meters in length.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is often found in conservation programs aimed at preserving biodiversity.
