Apocynum villosum

Apocynum villosum, commonly known as Indian hemp, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. Its fruit is a follicle that contains toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine.

Apocynum villosum is also known for its latex, which has been historically used in the production of rubber and as a natural insecticide.
