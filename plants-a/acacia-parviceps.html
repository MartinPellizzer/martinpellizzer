Acacia parviceps

Acacia parviceps, commonly known as the small-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
