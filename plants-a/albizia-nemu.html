Albizia nemu

Albizia nemu, commonly known as Indian siris, is a deciduous tree native to South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant has a deep root system that helps in soil stabilization and prevents erosion. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or white, arranged in racemes, and it produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Albizia nemu is valued for its timber, medicinal properties, and role in traditional agriculture.
