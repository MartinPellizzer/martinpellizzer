Adenophora leptosepala

Adenophora leptosepala, commonly known as the slender-sepaled bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often with a distinctive purple or pinkish hue at the base. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a hairy texture, while the stems are upright and branched.

Adenophora leptosepala is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate moisture conditions.
