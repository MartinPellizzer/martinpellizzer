Astragalus newberryi var. funereus

Astragalus newberryi var. funereus, commonly known as the funereal milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as slopes and gravelly soils. It has a deep taproot system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
