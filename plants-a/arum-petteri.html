Arum petteri

Arum petteri, commonly known as the Petter's arum, is a striking tropical plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, typically in shades of green, red, or purple. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and it produces small berries as fruit.

Arum petteri is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its dramatic and exotic appearance.
