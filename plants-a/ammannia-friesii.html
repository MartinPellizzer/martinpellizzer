Ammannia friesii

Ammannia friesii, commonly known as the redroot dayflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red stems and small, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Ammannia friesii is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant color and adaptability to various soil types.
