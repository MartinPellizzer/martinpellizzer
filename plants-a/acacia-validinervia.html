Acacia validinervia

Acacia validinervia, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical spikes, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Acacia validinervia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
