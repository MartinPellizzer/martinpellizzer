Auganthus palmatus

Auganthus palmatus, commonly known as the palmate lily, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately divided leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length, giving it a dramatic, tropical appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its unique foliage and vibrant floral display.
