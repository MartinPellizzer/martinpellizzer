Acacia spooneri

Acacia spooneri, commonly known as the spoonleaf acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, spoon-shaped leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its woody stems are often covered in a layer of fine, white hairs, which provide additional protection against intense sunlight and drought.

Acacia spooneri is well adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils and can thrive in conditions with minimal rainfall.
