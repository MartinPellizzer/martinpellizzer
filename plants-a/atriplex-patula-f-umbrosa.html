Atriplex patula f. umbrosa

Atriplex patula f. umbrosa, commonly known as the shade-forming saltbush, is a variant of the saltbush species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It exhibits darker, more compact foliage compared to the typical form, adapted to lower light conditions. The plant has small, succulent leaves that help reduce water loss in dry environments. It is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in areas with limited water availability.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
