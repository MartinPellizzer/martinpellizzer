Acorellus pannonicus

Acorellus pannonicus, commonly known as the Pannonian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowering period occurs in late summer.

Acorellus pannonicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
