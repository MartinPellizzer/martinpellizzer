Acacia multisiliqua

Acacia multisiliqua, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

Acacia multisiliqua is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
