Acinos exiguus

Acinos exiguus, commonly known as the small mountain aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its small, daisy-like flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, adding color to high-altitude environments.

Acinos exiguus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators in mountain ecosystems.
