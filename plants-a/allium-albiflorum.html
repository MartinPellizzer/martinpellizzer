Allium albiflorum

Allium albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered garlic.

It is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces a bulb composed of modified leaves called scales, which are used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Allium albiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild garlic scent.
