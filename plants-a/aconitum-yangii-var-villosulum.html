Aconitum yangii var. villosulum

Aconitum yangii var. villosulum, commonly known as the hairy Yang's aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, spire-like clusters. Its leaves are compound, with palmate divisions and a hairy texture, giving it its varietal name.

This species is noted for its toxicity, containing potent alkaloids that make it dangerous to handle or ingest.
