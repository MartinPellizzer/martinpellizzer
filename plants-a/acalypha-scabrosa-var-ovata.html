Acalypha scabrosa var. ovata

Acalypha scabrosa var. ovata, commonly known as velvetleaf or smoothleaf acalypha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often hairy leaves that are typically ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish-green flowers, which are not particularly showy. Its growth habit is usually shrub-like, with multiple stems and a tendency to branch profusely.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage.
