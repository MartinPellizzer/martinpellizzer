Aconitum caucasicum

Aconitum caucasicum, commonly known as Caucasian monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features striking dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive hooded flower that is typically purple or blue. The plant belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its toxic alkaloids, which make it highly poisonous to both humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and toxic properties, it is both cultivated and carefully handled in horticulture.
