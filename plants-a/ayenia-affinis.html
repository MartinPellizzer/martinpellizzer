Ayenia affinis

Ayenia affinis, commonly known as the clustered greenbriar, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a dry, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Ayenia affinis is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
