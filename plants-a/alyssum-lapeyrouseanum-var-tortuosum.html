Alyssum lapeyrouseanum var. tortuosum

Alyssum lapeyrouseanum var. tortuosum, commonly known as the twisted alyssum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
