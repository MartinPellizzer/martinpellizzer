Anacheilium grammatoglossum

Anacheilium grammatoglossum, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, with a distinctive pinkish-red color and a prominent white throat. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes. Its leaves are broad and floating, providing shade and habitat for various aquatic life.

The sacred lotus is also culturally significant in many Asian countries, symbolizing purity and spiritual enlightenment.
