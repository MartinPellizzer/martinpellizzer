Arabis lemmonii var. paddoensis

Arabis lemmonii var. paddoensis, commonly known as the Paddo rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many species with small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
