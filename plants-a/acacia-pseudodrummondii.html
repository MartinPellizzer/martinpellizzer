Acacia pseudodrummondii

Acacia pseudodrummondii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Acacia pseudodrummondii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh conditions.
