Aporum grande

Aporum grande, commonly known as the giant arum, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces a dramatic inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a showy spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with rich, well-draining soil.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
