Alstroemeria pygmaea

Alstroemeria pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf flame lily, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of brightly colored, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its compact size and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Alstroemeria pygmaea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
