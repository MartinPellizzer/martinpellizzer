Acacia pentadenia subsp. pentadenia

Acacia pentadenia subsp.

pentadenia, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures.

The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, globular clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods containing seeds. It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often tolerating saline conditions.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
