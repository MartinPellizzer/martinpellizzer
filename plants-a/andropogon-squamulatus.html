Andropogon squamulatus

Andropogon squamulatus, commonly known as the hairy bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairie habitats.

Andropogon squamulatus plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.
