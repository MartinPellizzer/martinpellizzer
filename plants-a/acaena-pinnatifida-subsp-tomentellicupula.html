Acaena pinnatifida subsp. tomentellicupula

Acaena pinnatifida subsp.

tomentellicupula, commonly known as the woolly burr plant, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces spherical, hairy fruits that contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by attaching to the fur of animals. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

This subspecies is often found in coastal and subalpine habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native range.
