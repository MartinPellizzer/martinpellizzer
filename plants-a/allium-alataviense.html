Allium alataviense

Allium alataviense, commonly known as the Altavina onion, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous root system and grass-like foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and cylindrical, growing from the base of the plant.

Allium alataviense is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
