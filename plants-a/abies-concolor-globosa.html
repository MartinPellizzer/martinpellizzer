Abies concolor globosa

Abies concolor globosa, commonly known as the Colorado blue spruce, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and bluish-gray foliage, which gives it a striking appearance. The leaves are needle-like, short, and arranged in spirals along the branches. Its cones are typically cylindrical and measure around 2 to 3 inches in length.

This plant is well adapted to cold climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.
