Arenaria huteri

Arenaria huteri, commonly known as the mountain sandwort, is a low-growing flowering plant native to the alpine regions of South America.

It typically thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in mountainous environments. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are usually succulent and adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

Arenaria huteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude habitats.
