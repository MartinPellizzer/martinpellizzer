Anil pratensis

Anil pratensis, commonly known as meadow vetchling, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. It is a perennial species that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

Anil pratensis plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility through its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
