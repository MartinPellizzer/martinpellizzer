Aconitum napellus f. rigidum

Aconitum napellus f. rigidum, commonly known as the rigid monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, erect stem and striking violet-blue flowers.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Europe. The plant features palmate leaves with a distinctive hooded flower structure, typical of the genus Aconitum. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
