Aeollanthus uliginosus

Aeollanthus uliginosus, commonly known as the swamp lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow in color. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Aeollanthus uliginosus is adapted to humid conditions and plays a role in supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
