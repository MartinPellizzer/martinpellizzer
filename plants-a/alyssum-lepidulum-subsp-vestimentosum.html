Alyssum lepidulum subsp. vestimentosum

Alyssum lepidulum subsp.

vestimentosum, commonly known as the hairy alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in spring and early summer.

The plant has hairy, gray-green foliage that is often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a valued addition to many garden settings.
