Allium pleianthum

Allium pleianthum, commonly known as the broadleaf garlic, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers during the spring and summer months. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making them useful in culinary applications.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other Allium species like garlic and onions.
