Acanthopsis hoffmannseggiana

Acanthopsis hoffmannseggiana, commonly known as Hoffmannsegg's acanthus, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, spires of showy, violet-blue bracts that resemble flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. The plant features lance-shaped, velvety leaves with prominent veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it can be challenging to grow outside its native habitat.
