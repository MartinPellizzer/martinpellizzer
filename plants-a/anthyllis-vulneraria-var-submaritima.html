Anthyllis vulneraria var. submaritima

Anthyllis vulneraria var. submaritima, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Atlantic coasts of Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its gray-green, woolly leaves and spiny, blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and rocky coastal habitats. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense spikes, and it produces small, leathery pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is adapted to withstand harsh maritime conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
