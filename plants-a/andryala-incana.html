Andryala incana

Andryala incana, commonly known as the hairy andryala, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Andryala incana is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
