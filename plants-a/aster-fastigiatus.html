Aster fastigiatus

Aster fastigiatus, commonly known as the upright aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow growth habit and produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers in the fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Aster fastigiatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
