Astragalus porphyrogrammus

Astragalus porphyrogrammus, commonly known as the purple-stemmed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus porphyrogrammus is often found in alpine and steppe habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
