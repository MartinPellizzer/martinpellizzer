Astranthium mexicanum

Astranthium mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican starflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, star-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, with a central disc surrounded by ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Astranthium mexicanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
