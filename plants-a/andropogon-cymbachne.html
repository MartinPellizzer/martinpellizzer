Andropogon cymbachne

Andropogon cymbachne, commonly known as flat-stemmed grass, is a perennial grass species native to the warm temperate and tropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed soils, exhibiting a clump-forming habit with sturdy, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow, smooth, and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Andropogon cymbachne is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage plant.
