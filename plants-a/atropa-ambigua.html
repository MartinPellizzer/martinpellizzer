Atropa ambigua

Atropa ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous belladonna, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and striking violet or purple flowers. The plant produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals, containing tropane alkaloids such as atropine and scopolamine. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a noxious weed in some areas.
