Atalantia monophylla

Atalantia monophylla, commonly known as the single-leaf atalantia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in axillary inflorescences, producing a fragrant aroma.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
