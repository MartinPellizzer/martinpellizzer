Anethum graveolens var. submarginatum

Anethum graveolens var. submarginatum, commonly known as the curly parsley or Persian cumin, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its seeds, which are often used as a spice, have a warm, earthy flavor.

This variety is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary and medicinal uses.
