Adenophora liliifolia var. pocsii

Adenophora liliifolia var. pocsii, commonly known as the Pocci's bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
