Anoectochilus siamensis

Anoectochilus siamensis, commonly known as the Siam orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant typically has elliptical or ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with colors ranging from white to pink and purple.

Anoectochilus siamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
