Acidocroton trichophyllus

Acidocroton trichophyllus, commonly known as the hairy-leaved croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its botanical properties are not extensively studied.
