Astragalus nevskii

Astragalus nevskii, commonly known as Nevskii milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions.

Astragalus nevskii is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
