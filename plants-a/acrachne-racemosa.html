Acrachne racemosa

Acrachne racemosa, commonly known as the clustered acanthus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and vibrant blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Acrachne racemosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
