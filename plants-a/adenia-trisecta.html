Adenia trisecta

Adenia trisecta, commonly known as the three-lobed adenium, is a striking tropical shrub native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, often woody stems and large, palmately lobed leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are highly fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its unique morphology and adaptability to arid conditions make it a notable species in botanical studies.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been the subject of various horticultural interest.
