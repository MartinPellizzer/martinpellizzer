Ainsliaea integrifolia

Ainsliaea integrifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf ainsliaea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb with upright stems and palmately divided leaves that are typically broad and entire. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
