Arisaema undulatifolium var. yoshinagae

Arisaema undulatifolium var. yoshinagae, commonly known as the Yoshinaga's wavy-leaf arisaema, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Japan.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, wavy-edged leaves that are typically a deep green with a silvery underside. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
