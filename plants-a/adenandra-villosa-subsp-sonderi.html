Adenandra villosa subsp. sonderi

Adenandra villosa subsp.

sonderi, commonly known as the silky adenandra, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and small, clustered white flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds dispersed by animals.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
