Agalinis Auriculata

Agalinis auriculata is a striking flowering plant native to the southeastern united states. it is commonly known as the ear-shaped or golden cupplant due to its distinctive leaf shape and vibrant yellow blooms. this species thrives in open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. it plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies. its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable plant in native plant gardens.
