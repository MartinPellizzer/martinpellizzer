Achyronia fornicata

Achyronia fornicata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and cylindrical, blue to purple flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its growth habit is typically low and sprawling, with stems that can reach up to 1 meter in length.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
