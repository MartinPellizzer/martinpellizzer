Arenaria congesta var. kingii

Arenaria congesta var. kingii, commonly known as King's sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a mat-forming habit with succulent, fleshy leaves that are adapted to withstand saline and arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscaping.
