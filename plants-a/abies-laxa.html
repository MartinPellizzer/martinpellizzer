Abies laxa

Abies laxa, commonly known as the narrowleaf fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and its distinctive, needle-like leaves that are shorter and more rigid compared to those of other fir species. The cones of Abies laxa are typically small and upright, maturing to release seeds that are dispersed by wind. This species thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in high-altitude forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the biodiversity in its native habitat.
