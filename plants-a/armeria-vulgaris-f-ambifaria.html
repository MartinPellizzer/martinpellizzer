Armeria vulgaris f. ambifaria

Armeria vulgaris f. ambifaria, commonly known as the double-flowered thrift, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant features dense clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
