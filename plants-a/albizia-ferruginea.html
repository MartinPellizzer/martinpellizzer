Albizia ferruginea

Albizia ferruginea, commonly known as the red sandalwood or ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a reddish hue. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and rich reddish color, making it a significant resource in traditional crafts and construction.

Albizia ferruginea also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
