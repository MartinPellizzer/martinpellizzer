Antidesma laciniatum var. genuinum

Antidesma laciniatum var. genuinum, commonly known as the African starfruit or red starfruit, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, elliptic leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that resemble star-shaped berries. The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often consumed fresh or used in local cuisines.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
