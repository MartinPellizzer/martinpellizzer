Adesmia aprica

Adesmia aprica, commonly known as the dwarf legume, is a small shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers. Its root system helps in soil enrichment by forming symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Adesmia aprica is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
