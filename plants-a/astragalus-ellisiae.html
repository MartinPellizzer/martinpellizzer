Astragalus ellisiae

Astragalus ellisiae, commonly known as the Sierra foothill milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus ellisiae plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native pollinators.
