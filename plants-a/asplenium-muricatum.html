Asplenium muricatum

Asplenium muricatum, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows on rocky surfaces or in shaded, moist environments, thriving in humid conditions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium muricatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
