Artemisia crithmifolia

Artemisia crithmifolia, commonly known as sea lavender or rock wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in maritime climates. It has a strong, distinctive scent due to the presence of volatile essential oils.

Artemisia crithmifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
