Acer laevigatum subsp. reticulatum

Acer laevigatum subsp.

reticulatum, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan. It is characterized by its ornamental foliage, which displays a range of colors from bright green to red and orange in the fall.

The leaves are typically palmately lobed with a finely toothed margin, and the tree has a slender, upright growth habit. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit.

This species is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
