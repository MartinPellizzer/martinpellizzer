Aniba krukovii

Aniba krukovii, commonly known as the Brazilian camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and elliptical, leathery fruits. Its leaves contain essential oils that have been used in traditional medicine.

Aniba krukovii plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
