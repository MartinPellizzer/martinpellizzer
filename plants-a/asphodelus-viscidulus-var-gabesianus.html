Asphodelus viscidulus var. gabesianus

Asphodelus viscidulus var. gabesianus, commonly known as the sticky asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features strap-like, linear leaves that are covered in a sticky, resinous substance, which is a characteristic feature of the Asphodelaceae family. The plant produces tall, slender flowering stems that bear clusters of small, white or greenish-white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
