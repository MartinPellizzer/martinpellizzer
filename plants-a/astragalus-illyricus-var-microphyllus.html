Astragalus illyricus var. microphyllus

Astragalus illyricus var. microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It plays a role in soil stabilization and supports local pollinators.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an important species in its native habitat.
