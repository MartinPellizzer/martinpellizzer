Aegilops lorentii var. velutina

Aegilops lorentii var. velutina, commonly known as the hairy wild rye, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous pale green to brownish florets. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and scrub habitats.

Aegilops lorentii var. velutina is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
