Ajuga chamaepitys

Ajuga chamaepitys, commonly known as common bugleweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Ajuga chamaepitys is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
