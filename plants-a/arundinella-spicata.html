Arundinella spicata

Arundinella spicata, commonly known as the spike rush, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often hollow and covered in fine, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It is commonly found in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments.

Arundinella spicata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
