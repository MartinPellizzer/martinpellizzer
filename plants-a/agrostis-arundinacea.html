Agrostis arundinacea

Agrostis arundinacea, commonly known as slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along streambanks, and in disturbed sites.

Agrostis arundinacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in erosion control and as a forage crop.
