Amomum platyandrum

Amomum platyandrum, commonly known as the flat-leaf cardamom, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are followed by dark red, spadix-like fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its rhizomes and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

Amomum platyandrum is often cultivated for its fragrant seeds, which are used in culinary and aromatic applications.
