Anemone minima

Anemone minima, commonly known as the dwarf anemone, is a small flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow center. Its leaves are palmately divided, creating a lacy appearance, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Anemone minima is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in native plant gardens.
