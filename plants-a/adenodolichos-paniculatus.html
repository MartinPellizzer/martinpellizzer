Adenodolichos paniculatus

Adenodolichos paniculatus, commonly known as the panicled cowpea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and racemose inflorescence, which bears small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its pods are typically slender and contain several seeds, which are used as a food source in some regions.

The plant is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.
