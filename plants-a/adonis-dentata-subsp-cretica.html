Adonis dentata subsp. cretica

Adonis dentata subsp.

cretica, commonly known as the Cretan bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in sunny, well-drained habitats.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it may be challenging to grow outside its native range.
