Acer × avilae

Acer × avilae, commonly known as the red maple, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage in the fall, which makes it a popular ornamental species. The tree typically grows to a height of 15–25 meters and has a rounded crown with opposite branching. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with three to five lobes, and are often red or orange in the autumn.

The bark is smooth and reddish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
