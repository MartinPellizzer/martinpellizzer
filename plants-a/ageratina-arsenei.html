Ageratina arsenei

Ageratina arsenei, commonly known as the black sage, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dark green, aromatic leaves and small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties. Ageratina arsenei thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrub and chaparral habitats.
