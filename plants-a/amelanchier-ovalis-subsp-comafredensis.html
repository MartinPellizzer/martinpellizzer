Amelanchier ovalis subsp. comafredensis

Amelanchier ovalis subsp.

comafredensis, commonly known as the Comafred's serviceberry, is a shrub or small tree native to the Andean regions of South America. It typically grows in humid, temperate climates and is characterized by its deciduous leaves and showy white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces edible berries that ripen in late summer, resembling small, dark red to black fruits. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf shape and growth habit compared to the typical form of Amelanchier ovalis.
