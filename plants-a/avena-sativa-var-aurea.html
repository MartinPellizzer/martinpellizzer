Avena sativa var. aurea

Avena sativa var. aurea, commonly known as golden oat, is a variant of the common oat plant characterized by its striking golden hue in its foliage and inflorescence.

This variety is native to temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. Its botanical features include slender, hollow stems and panicles of delicate, golden florets that bloom in late summer. The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

It is also valued in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
