Astragalus shearii

Astragalus shearii, commonly known as Shearii milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ecological role in stabilizing soil.

Astragalus shearii is also of interest in botanical research due to its potential medicinal properties.
