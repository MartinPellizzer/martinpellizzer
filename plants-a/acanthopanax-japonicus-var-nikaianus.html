Acanthopanax japonicus var. nikaianus

Acanthopanax japonicus var. nikaianus, commonly known as the Japanese five-leaf aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, typically consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in umbel-like clusters, followed by red berries that are edible for humans and birds. It thrives in shaded to partially sunny environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
