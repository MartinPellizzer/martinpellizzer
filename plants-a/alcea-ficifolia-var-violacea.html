Alcea ficifolia var. violacea

Alcea ficifolia var. violacea, commonly known as the purpleleaf hollyhock, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to deep purple, often with a distinctive veined pattern. The leaves are palmately lobed, giving the plant a lush, foliage-heavy appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is valued for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
