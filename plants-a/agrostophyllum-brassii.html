Agrostophyllum brassii

Agrostophyllum brassii, commonly known as the Australian sandalwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, woody scent. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, white powdery coating.

Agrostophyllum brassii is valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
