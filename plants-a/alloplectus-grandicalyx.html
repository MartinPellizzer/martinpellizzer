Alloplectus grandicalyx

Alloplectus grandicalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered false hibiscus, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow throat. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in native gardens.
