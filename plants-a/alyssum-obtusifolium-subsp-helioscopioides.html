Alyssum obtusifolium subsp. helioscopioides

Alyssum obtusifolium subsp.

helioscopioides, commonly known as sun-leaf alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring.

The plant has slender, branching stems and small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
