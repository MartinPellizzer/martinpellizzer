Amomum corynostachyum

Amomum corynostachyum, commonly known as the Yunnan cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric, and is characterized by its aromatic rhizomes. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often with a distinct spicy fragrance.

The rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, valued for their aromatic and medicinal properties.
