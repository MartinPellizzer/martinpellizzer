Aspidosperma dispermum

Aspidosperma dispermum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Ceratonia sylvestris*, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its deciduous nature, shedding its leaves during the dry season. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, woody, spiny fruits containing two seeds. It is often found in dry forest and savanna habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
