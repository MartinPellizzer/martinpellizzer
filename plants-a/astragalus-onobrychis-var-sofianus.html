Astragalus onobrychis var. sofianus

Astragalus onobrychis var. sofianus, commonly known as the Turkish milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to southeastern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by pea-like pods containing lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
