Aeschynomene siifolia

Aeschynomene siifolia, commonly known as slenderleaf aeschynomene, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically features slender, upright stems with small, trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are usually pale blue or purple, arranged in racemes, and it produces small, pod-like fruits.

Aeschynomene siifolia is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and its role in soil enrichment.
