Abies oaxacana

Abies oaxacana, commonly known as the Oaxacan fir, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged spirally along the branches. The tree produces small, cylindrical cones that typically measure between 5 to 10 centimeters in length. It thrives in cool, moist environments at high altitudes, often growing in cloud forests.

The Oaxacan fir plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
