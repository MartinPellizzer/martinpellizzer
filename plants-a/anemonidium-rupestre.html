Anemonidium rupestre

Anemonidium rupestre, commonly known as the rock anemone, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. The plant produces showy, pink to white flowers in early spring, which are clustered in a rounded inflorescence. Its foliage consists of basal, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom.

Anemonidium rupestre is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh, alpine conditions.
