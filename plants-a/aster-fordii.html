Aster fordii

Aster fordii, commonly known as the Ford's aster, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the sunflower family, Asteraceae, and is characterized by its showy, clustered flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped, hairy leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, producing blue to purple petals that attract pollinators.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.
