Acacia besleyi

Acacia besleyi, commonly known as the Besley's wattle, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It features slender, often spindly branches and produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters. The leaves are phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of true leaves. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions with a deep root system and waxy foliage to reduce water loss.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping in arid environments.
