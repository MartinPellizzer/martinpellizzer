Angelica sativa

Angelica sativa, commonly known as European angelica, is a tall biennial or perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features large, umbrella-shaped compound leaves and tall, clustered flowering heads that produce small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its strong, aromatic roots, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Angelica sativa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its growth cycle involves a vegetative stage in the first year followed by flowering and seed production in the second year.
