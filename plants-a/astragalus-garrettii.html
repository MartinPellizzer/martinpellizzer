Astragalus garrettii

Astragalus garrettii, commonly known as Garrett's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers clustered in racemes. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Astragalus garrettii is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to nutrient cycling in its ecosystem.
