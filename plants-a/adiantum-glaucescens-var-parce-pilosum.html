Adiantum glaucescens var. parce-pilosum

Adiantum glaucescens var. parce-pilosum, commonly known as the silky maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern family (Adiantaceae).

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a soft, almost velvety appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as woodland floors or rocky crevices, where it can thrive in partial to full shade. Its fronds are bi-lobed and arching, with a distinctive rachis that is covered in small, glandular dots.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens or as a houseplant due to its graceful appearance and ease of care.
