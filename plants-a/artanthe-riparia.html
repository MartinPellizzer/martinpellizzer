Artanthe riparia

Artanthe riparia, commonly known as the river artanthe, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats along riverbanks and stream sides. The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-gray hue. Its flowers are small, pinkish-white, and arranged in dense, terminal clusters.

Artanthe riparia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging, wet environments.
