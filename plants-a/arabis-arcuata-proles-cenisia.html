Arabis arcuata proles cenisia

Arabis arcuata proles cenisia, commonly known as the white sand arabis, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant produces small, white, four-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, oval-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
