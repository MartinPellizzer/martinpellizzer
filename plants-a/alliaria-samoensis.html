Alliaria samoensis

Alliaria samoensis, commonly known as the Samoan garlic or wild garlic, is a flowering plant native to the islands of Samoa and nearby regions in the Pacific.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its aromatic, garlic-like scent. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and small white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

Alliaria samoensis is valued for its culinary and medicinal properties in traditional Pacific Island cultures.
