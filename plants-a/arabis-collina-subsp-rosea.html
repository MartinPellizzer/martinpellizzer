Arabis collina subsp. rosea

Arabis collina subsp.

rosea, commonly known as the pink rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with characteristic cruciform flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are small, pink to rose-colored, and appear in clusters during the spring.

It is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
