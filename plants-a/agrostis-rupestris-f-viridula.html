Agrostis rupestris f. viridula

Agrostis rupestris f. viridula, commonly known as the green rock grass, is a variant of the rock grass species native to rocky and coastal environments.

This plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a vibrant green color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in maritime climates. The foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.

It is a low-maintenance plant that can tolerate salt spray and coastal winds.
