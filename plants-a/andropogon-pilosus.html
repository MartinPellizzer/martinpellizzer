Andropogon pilosus

Andropogon pilosus, commonly known as hairy bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and tufted seed heads that turn blue-gray in color. This plant typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed sites, often forming dense stands. Andropogon pilosus is adapted to a variety of soil types and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy component of native plant communities.

It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
