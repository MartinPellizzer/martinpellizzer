Andropogon thollonii

Andropogon thollonii, commonly known as the African grass or thollonii grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in open woodlands, grasslands, and disturbed soils, exhibiting a robust growth habit with tall, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are characteristic of the grass family Poaceae. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a bluish-green hue, and it is well adapted to warm, humid climates.

Andropogon thollonii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in erosion control due to its dense root system.
