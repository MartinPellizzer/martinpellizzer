Astragalus gracaninii

Astragalus gracaninii, commonly known as the Sierra foothill milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus gracaninii plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
