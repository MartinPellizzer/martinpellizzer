Atadinus pumilus subsp. hispanicus

Atadinus pumilus subsp.

hispanicus, commonly known as the Spanish dwarf bellflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe. It features small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in sunny locations. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare in the wild.
