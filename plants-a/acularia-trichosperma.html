Acularia trichosperma

Acularia trichosperma, commonly known as the white sand cactus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem covered in white, woolly hairs that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is often cultivated in xeriscaping and as an ornamental plant.
