Anthemis saxatilis var. macrocephala

Anthemis saxatilis var. macrocephala, commonly known as the large-headed mountain sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces showy, yellowish-white composite flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
