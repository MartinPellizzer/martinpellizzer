Ardisia villosa subsp. brevisepala

Ardisia villosa subsp.

brevisepala, commonly known as the hairy ardisia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It features glossy, ovate leaves with a dense covering of fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by bright red berries that are edible. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
