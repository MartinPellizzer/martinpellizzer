Abutilon minarum

Abutilon minarum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a velvety texture, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Abutilon minarum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
