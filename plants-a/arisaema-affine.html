Arisaema affine

Arisaema affine, commonly known as the narrow-leaf arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves and spadix inflorescence, which is enclosed by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Arisaema affine is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
