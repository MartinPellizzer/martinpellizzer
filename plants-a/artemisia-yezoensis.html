Artemisia yezoensis

Artemisia yezoensis, commonly known as Yezo wormwood, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its gray-green, aromatic foliage and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
