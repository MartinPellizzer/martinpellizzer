Atriplex joaquinana

Atriplex joaquinana, commonly known as Joaquin saltbush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, providing it with a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to arid and saline environments, making it a hardy species found in desert and coastal areas.

Its leaves are succulent and often used by desert animals as a food source.
