Agapetes similis

Agapetes similis, commonly known as the small tree heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Ecuador and Peru. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are small, leathery, and often have a bluish-green hue.

The flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and are typically white or pale pink, attracting various pollinators.
