Aira nitida

Aira nitida, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-blue foliage that gives it a striking appearance in gardens. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate, feathery seed heads that persist into winter.
