Aster pilosus f. pulchellus

Aster pilosus f. pulchellus, commonly known as the silky blue aster, is a distinctive cultivar of the aster plant characterized by its soft, fluffy blue-purple flower heads.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet, featuring narrow, gray-green leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces numerous small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a favored plant among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
