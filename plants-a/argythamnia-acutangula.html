Argythamnia acutangula

Argythamnia acutangula, commonly known as the sharp-leaved thornapple, is a shrub native to the deserts of North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that contain seeds dispersed by animals. It is adapted to arid environments with deep roots and a low water requirement.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
