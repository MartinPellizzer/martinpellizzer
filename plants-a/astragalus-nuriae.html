Astragalus nuriae

Astragalus nuriae, commonly known as the Turkestan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Astragalus nuriae is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine in some regions.
