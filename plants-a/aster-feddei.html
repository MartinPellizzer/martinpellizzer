Aster feddei

Aster feddei, commonly known as the Fedde's aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes with cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Aster feddei is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
