Abies dammara

Abies dammara, commonly known as the Dammara fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds surrounded by a papery covering. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in high-altitude forests.

The bark of Abies dammara is typically smooth and reddish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
