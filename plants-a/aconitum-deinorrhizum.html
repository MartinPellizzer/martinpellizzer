Aconitum deinorrhizum

Aconitum deinorrhizum, commonly known as the narrow-rooted monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive hooded flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant produces a tall, sparsely branched stem with palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a poisonous plant and should be handled with care.
