Anthemis macrantha

Anthemis macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered anthemis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy yellow ray florets surrounded by tubular disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats like grasslands and shrublands.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
