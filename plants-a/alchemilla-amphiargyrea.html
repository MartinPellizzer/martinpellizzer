Alchemilla amphiargyrea

Alchemilla amphiargyrea, commonly known as the silverweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported diuretic properties.

Alchemilla amphiargyrea thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.
