Allium prattii var. thanguense

Allium prattii var. thanguense, commonly known as the Tibetan leek, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, and it produces clusters of small, pale purple to white flowers in the summer. The plant is adapted to cold, arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a useful crop in certain regions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh climates.
