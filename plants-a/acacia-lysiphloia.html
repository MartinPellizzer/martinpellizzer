Acacia lysiphloia

Acacia lysiphloia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and bipinnate leaves that are composed of numerous small, narrow leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Acacia lysiphloia is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in nutrient-poor areas.
