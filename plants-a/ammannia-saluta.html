Ammannia saluta

Ammannia saluta, commonly known as prairie ammannia, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its root system helps stabilize soil and supports aquatic ecosystems.

Ammannia saluta is valued for its ecological role in wetland environments and its ornamental appeal in water gardens.
