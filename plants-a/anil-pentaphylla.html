Anil pentaphylla

Anil pentaphylla, commonly known as the five-leafed anil, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Anil pentaphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
