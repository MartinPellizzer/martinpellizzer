Artemisia borealis var. adamsii

Artemisia borealis var. adamsii, commonly known as the Alaska tarragon, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and aromatic, narrow leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, spherical heads. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in tundra and alpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for use in herbal remedies.
