Acetosella serpens

Acetosella serpens, commonly known as the snake's head or serpentine sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in early spring. Acetosella serpens thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

It is a hardy species that is relatively easy to cultivate in shaded garden settings.
