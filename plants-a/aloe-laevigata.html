Aloe laevigata

Aloe laevigata, commonly known as the silver aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in a whitish, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow-orange flowers, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves contain a gel-like substance that has been used for its medicinal properties.

Aloe laevigata is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
