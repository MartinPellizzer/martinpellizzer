Agathosma ciliaris

Agathosma ciliaris, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation. It has narrow, gray-green leaves with fine hairs, and its stems are usually covered in a fuzzy layer.

Agathosma ciliaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
