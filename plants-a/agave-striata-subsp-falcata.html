Agave striata subsp. falcata

Agave striata subsp.

falcata, commonly known as the narrowleaf agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often blue-green in color.

The plant produces striking white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring or early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
