Asclepias crocea

Asclepias crocea, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is known for its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds.

Asclepias crocea serves as a vital host plant for monarch butterfly larvae, making it ecologically significant.
