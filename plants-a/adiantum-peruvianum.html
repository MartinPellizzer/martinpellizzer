Adiantum peruvianum

Adiantum peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian maidenhair fern, is a member of the Adiantaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bright green and have a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to South America, particularly Peru. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, which are protected by a specialized structure called a sorus.

Adiantum peruvianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated for its elegant, lacy foliage.
