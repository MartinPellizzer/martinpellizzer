Ampelopsis heterophylla var. sinica

Ampelopsis heterophylla var. sinica, commonly known as Chinese ivy, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that vary in size and shape depending on light conditions. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by orange-red berries that are attractive to birds. Its stems are woody and can trail or climb, making it suitable for trellises or ground cover.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in ornamental landscaping.
