Aloe × paxii

Aloe × paxii, commonly known as the hybrid aloe or the hybrid aloe plant, is a succulent species resulting from the crossbreeding of Aloe vera and Aloe arborescens.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge, especially under stress or in full sun. The plant produces yellowish-orange flowers in clusters during the summer months, adding ornamental value. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Aloe × paxii is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and mild medicinal properties.
