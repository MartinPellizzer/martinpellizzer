Aquilegia rockii

Aquilegia rockii, commonly known as the Rock Corydalis, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and vibrant, often crimson or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Aquilegia rockii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
