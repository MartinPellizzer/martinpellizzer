Acacia ericifolia var. crassa

Acacia ericifolia var. crassa, commonly known as the thick-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are broader and more robust than those of the typical species. The plant produces yellow floral spikes, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, inland areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
