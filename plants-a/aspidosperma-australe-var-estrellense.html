Aspidosperma australe var. estrellense

Aspidosperma australe var. estrellense, commonly known as the Estrellense Quinine Tree, is a species native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Uruguay.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red berries. It contains alkaloids, including quinine, which have historically been used in traditional medicine.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
