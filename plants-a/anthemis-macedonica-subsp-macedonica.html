Anthemis macedonica subsp. macedonica

Anthemis macedonica subsp.

macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian chamomile, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region. It features aromatic, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming from late spring to early summer.

The plant has linear, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and sunny to partially shaded locations.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal uses and ornamental appeal in gardens.
