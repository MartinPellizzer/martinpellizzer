Alpinia pulchra

Alpinia pulchra, commonly known as the red ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its striking, vivid red flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a subtle fragrance. Its leaves are broad, glossy, and arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Alpinia pulchra thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a houseplant.
