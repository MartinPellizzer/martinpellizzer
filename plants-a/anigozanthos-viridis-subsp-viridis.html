Anigozanthos viridis subsp. viridis

Anigozanthos viridis subsp.

viridis, commonly known as the green kangaroo paw, is a species native to the southwestern region of Western Australia. It is a perennial herb with distinctive, bright green, paw-like flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences.

The plant features narrow, strap-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
