Astragalus daghestanicus

Astragalus daghestanicus, commonly known as the Daghestan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region, particularly in the mountainous areas of Daghestan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color. Astragalus daghestanicus is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh climatic conditions.

Its ecological significance includes nitrogen fixation, which enriches the soil in its native habitat.
