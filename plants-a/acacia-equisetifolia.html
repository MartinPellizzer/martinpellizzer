Acacia equisetifolia

Acacia equisetifolia, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It has a deep root system that helps it thrive in arid environments.

Acacia equisetifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
