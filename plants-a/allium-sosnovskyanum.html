Allium sosnovskyanum

Allium sosnovskyanum, commonly known as the Sosnovsky's leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and broad, grass-like leaves. The plant produces umbels of white to pale purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its bulbous underground stems are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
