Achyropappus woodhousei

Achyropappus woodhousei, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant has small, yellowish flower heads that are surrounded by bracts, and it produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It is well adapted to harsh, dry environments, with small, waxy leaves that reduce water loss.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
