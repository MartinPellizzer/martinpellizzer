Agave deserti var. simplex

Agave deserti var. simplex, commonly known as the Linearleaf Agave, is a species native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its tall, spire-like growth habit and long, narrow, blue-green leaves. The leaves are typically lined with sharp spines and are arranged in a rosette pattern. It produces a tall flowering stalk that bears white or pale yellow flowers, which appear after the plant has matured.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
