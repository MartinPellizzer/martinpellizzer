Aster coerulescens var. angustior

Aster coerulescens var. angustior, commonly known as the narrowleaf blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, sky-blue to lavender flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces numerous small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
