Adiantum edgeworthii var. spencerianum

Adiantum edgeworthii var. spencerianum, commonly known as the Spencer's maidenhair fern, is a delicate and graceful fern native to the southeastern United States.

It features finely divided, lace-like fronds that are typically dark green with a silvery underside. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are clustered on the undersides of the fronds.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in humid, indoor settings.
