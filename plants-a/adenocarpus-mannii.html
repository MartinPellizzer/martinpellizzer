Adenocarpus mannii

Adenocarpus mannii, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish or greenish flowers. The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain seeds with tufts of silky hair, aiding in wind dispersal. It is often found in dry savannas and woodland areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Adenocarpus mannii is also noted for its milky sap, which can cause skin irritation in some individuals.
