Alstroemeria philippii

Alstroemeria philippii, commonly known as the Peruvian lily, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, producing clusters of vibrant blooms during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Alstroemeria philippii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.
