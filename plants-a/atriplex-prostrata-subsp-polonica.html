Atriplex prostrata subsp. polonica

Atriplex prostrata subsp.

polonica, commonly known as Polish saltbush, is a low-growing shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are adapted to conserve water in harsh environments.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its salt-tolerant nature allows it to thrive in saline soils, making it an important species in arid ecosystems.

The subspecies is often used for erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
