Amorphophallus bulbifer var. tuberculiger

Amorphophallus bulbifer var. tuberculiger, commonly known as the giant waterlily, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, showy yellow flowers that bloom above the water surface, often reaching several feet in height. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly in still water environments.

The species is noted for its unique reproductive strategy, with flowers that emit a strong odor to attract pollinators.
