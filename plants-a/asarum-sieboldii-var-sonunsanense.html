Asarum sieboldii var. sonunsanense

Asarum sieboldii var. sonunsanense, commonly known as the Korean wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a rough texture. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers that are clustered in a spadix, enclosed by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its aromatic rhizomes and is used in traditional medicine.
