Ayenia ovatifolia

Ayenia ovatifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ayenia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Ayenia ovatifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
