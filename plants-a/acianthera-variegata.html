Acianthera variegata

Acianthera variegata, commonly known as the variegated acianthera, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and strikingly colored flowers, which often display a mix of white, yellow, and green hues. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves with a waxy texture, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments. It produces small, fragrant blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Acianthera variegata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
