Alphitonia erubescens

Alphitonia erubescens, commonly known as the red-flowered acacia, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a reddish tinge, giving it its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
