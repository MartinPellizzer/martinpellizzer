Aloe semimargaritifera var. multipapillosa

Aloe semimargaritifera var. multipapillosa, commonly known as the papillose aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in small, white papillae, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellowish-green flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and minimal water.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry conditions.
