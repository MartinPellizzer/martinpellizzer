Acrodiclidium chrysophyllum

Acrodiclidium chrysophyllum, commonly known as the golden feather cactus, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South America.

It features a short, cylindrical stem covered in dense, spiny bracts that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding vibrant color to its environment. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss and protect it from herbivores.

Acrodiclidium chrysophyllum is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.
