Asperula oppositifolia subsp. sikaramensis

Asperula oppositifolia subsp.

sikaramensis, commonly known as the Sikaram mountain azalea, is a flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white.

The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is typically found in shaded forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are borne in terminal panicles.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
