Adenodolichos salvifoliolatus

Adenodolichos salvifoliolatus, commonly known as the Indian senna, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing small, flat seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Botanically, it is valued for its medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
