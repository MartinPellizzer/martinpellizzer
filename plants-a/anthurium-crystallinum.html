Anthurium crystallinum

Anthurium crystallinum, commonly known as the crystalline anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white or silver hairs, giving it a shimmering appearance. The plant produces vibrant, spatulate-shaped spathes that can range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting midrib. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Its unique foliage and colorful inflorescences make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
