Aristolochia transtillifera

Aristolochia transtillifera, commonly known as the Brazilian pipevine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and emits a mild, somewhat unpleasant odor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

The plant is part of the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
