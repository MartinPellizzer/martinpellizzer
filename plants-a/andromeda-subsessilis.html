Andromeda subsessilis

Andromeda subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wintergreen, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy white flowers. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, and it produces berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streamside areas.
