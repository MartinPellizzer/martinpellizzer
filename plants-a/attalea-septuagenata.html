Attalea septuagenata

Attalea septuagenata, commonly known as the "palm of the Amazon," is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a tall, slender tree that can reach heights of up to 25 meters, with a straight trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces large, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark, fibrous fruits containing edible seeds. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and for thatching, while its trunk is valued for its strong, fibrous material.

The plant plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
