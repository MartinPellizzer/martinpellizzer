Ageratum heterolepis

Ageratum heterolepis, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fluffy blue or purple flowers that bloom throughout the summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Ageratum heterolepis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
