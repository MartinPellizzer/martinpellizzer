Anoectochilus glaber

Anoectochilus glaber, commonly known as the naked lip orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, grass-like leaves and a single, showy flower with a distinctive, non-furred lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forests and mountainous regions. Its roots are often clustered and can form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

Anoectochilus glaber is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
