Acmena claviflora

Acmena claviflora, commonly known as the broad-leaved satinash, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows up to 8 meters in height. The plant features glossy, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
