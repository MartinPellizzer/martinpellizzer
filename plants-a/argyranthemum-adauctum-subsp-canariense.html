Argyranthemum adauctum subsp. canariense

Argyranthemum adauctum subsp.

canariense, commonly known as Canary Islands flossflower, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its soft, silvery-white foliage and showy, daisy-like flowers.

The plant typically grows as a compact shrub, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
