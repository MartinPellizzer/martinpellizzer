Aloe bergeriana

Aloe bergeriana, commonly known as the desert aloe, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, covered in a layer of white powder called farina. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer. Aloe bergeriana is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its leaves and stems.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
