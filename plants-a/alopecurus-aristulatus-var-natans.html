Alopecurus aristulatus var. natans

Alopecurus aristulatus var. natans, commonly known as floating barleygrass, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its floating leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help it stay buoyant on the water's surface. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
