Aegiphila lehmannii

Aegiphila lehmannii, commonly known as Lehmann's aegiphila, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil and Argentina.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, narrow leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
