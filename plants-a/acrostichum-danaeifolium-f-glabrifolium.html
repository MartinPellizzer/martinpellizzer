Acrostichum danaeifolium f. glabrifolium

Acrostichum danaeifolium f. glabrifolium, commonly known as the smooth-leaf fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive fronds.

It features long, slender fronds that are typically dark green and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces spores on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, making it a common sight in tropical and subtropical regions.

This fern is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
