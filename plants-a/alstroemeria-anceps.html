Alstroemeria anceps

Alstroemeria anceps, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of vibrant, often red or orange, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Alstroemeria anceps is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its botanical features include strap-like leaves and a distinctive, elongated inflorescence.
