Acacia dysophylla

Acacia dysophylla, commonly known as the white wattle, is a small shrub or tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces woody pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Acacia dysophylla is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions with its deep root system and ability to fix nitrogen.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
