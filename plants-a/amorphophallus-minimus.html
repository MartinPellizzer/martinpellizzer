Amorphophallus minimus

Amorphophallus minimus, commonly known as the dwarf giant taro, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces a large, edible corm that is used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and cultural significance, it is also valued in horticulture and traditional medicine.
