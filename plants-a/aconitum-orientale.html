Aconitum orientale

Aconitum orientale, commonly known as the oriental monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has large, palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
