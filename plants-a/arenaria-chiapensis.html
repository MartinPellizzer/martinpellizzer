Arenaria chiapensis

Arenaria chiapensis, commonly known as the Chiapas sandwort, is a flowering plant native to Central America, particularly found in Mexico.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are typically linear and succulent, adapted to survive in arid or rocky environments.

Arenaria chiapensis is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
