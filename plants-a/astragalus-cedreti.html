Astragalus cedreti

Astragalus cedreti, commonly known as the Cedreti milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus cedreti is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
