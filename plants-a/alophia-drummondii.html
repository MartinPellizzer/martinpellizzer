Alophia drummondii

Alophia drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features strap-like, grass-like leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Alophia drummondii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to native plant gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and botanical gardens.
