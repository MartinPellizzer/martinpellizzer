Alopecurus vaginatus

Alopecurus vaginatus, commonly known as the common mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically forms dense mats of fine, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish or reddish spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the stems. It is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and is often found in moist, disturbed soils such as roadside verges and wet meadows.

Alopecurus vaginatus is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of habitats and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
