Acalypha macrostachya var. tristis

Acalypha macrostachya var. tristis, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silky ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage that ranges from green to reddish hues, often with a soft, fuzzy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, adding visual interest to its appearance. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
