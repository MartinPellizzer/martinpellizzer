Anisostachya elliptica

Anisostachya elliptica, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically arranged in a panicle-like structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Anisostachya elliptica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
