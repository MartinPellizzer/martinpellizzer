Atropis distans proles intricata

Atropis distans proles intricata, commonly known as the tangled broom, is a unique plant characterized by its intricate branching pattern and dense growth habit.

It features small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a subtle aesthetic to its foliage. The plant's leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, arranged in a spiral pattern along its stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its complex structure, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes in botanical gardens.
