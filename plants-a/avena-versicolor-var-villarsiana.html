Avena versicolor var. villarsiana

Avena versicolor var. villarsiana, commonly known as the variegated oat, is a variant of the oat species characterized by its strikingly colored foliage.

This plant belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to certain regions of Europe. Its leaves display a mix of green, white, and sometimes yellow or red, creating a visually appealing appearance. It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and produces small, fluffy seeds in clusters.

The plant is often cultivated as an ornamental grass due to its unique coloration and graceful growth habit.
