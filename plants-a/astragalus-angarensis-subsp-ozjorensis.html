Astragalus angarensis subsp. ozjorensis

Astragalus angarensis subsp.

ozjorensis, commonly known as the Karakoram milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its subspecies status highlights subtle morphological differences from the main species, Astragalus angarensis.

This plant plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to soil stabilization in arid regions.
