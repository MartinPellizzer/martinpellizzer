Aquilegia flabellata var. protypica

Aquilegia flabellata var. protypica, commonly known as the flannel leaf candleflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are soft and fuzzy to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
