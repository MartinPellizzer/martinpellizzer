Anemone fanninii var. parviflora

Anemone fanninii var. parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf fannin's anemone, is a compact perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive, narrow corona that resembles a crown. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for woodland gardens and native plant collections.
