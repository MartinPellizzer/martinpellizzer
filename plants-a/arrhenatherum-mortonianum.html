Arrhenatherum mortonianum

Arrhenatherum mortonianum, commonly known as the Mortonian feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, upright stems that bear narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
