Asparagus falcatus

Asparagus falcatus, commonly known as the sharp asparagus or hook asparagus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, and often curved leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal umbels. It is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its unique foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
