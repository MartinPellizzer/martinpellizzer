Astragalus nevadensis subsp. nevadensis

Astragalus nevadensis subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and mountain slopes.

The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pale pink to purple. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access water.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological role and limited distribution.
