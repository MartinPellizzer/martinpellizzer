Aberia verrucosa

Aberia verrucosa, commonly known as the rough-leaved alder, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its rough, bark-like leaves that are often covered in small, raised bumps. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins, while its fruits are three-winged samaras.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
