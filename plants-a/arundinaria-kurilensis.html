Arundinaria kurilensis

Arundinaria kurilensis, commonly known as the Kuril bamboo, is a perennial grass native to the Russian Far East and the northern part of Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and smooth on the upper surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Arundinaria kurilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
