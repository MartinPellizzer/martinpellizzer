Aira corsica

Aira corsica, commonly known as the Corsican aira, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in southern Europe and North Africa. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. It produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, pale blue to purple flowers.

Aira corsica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
