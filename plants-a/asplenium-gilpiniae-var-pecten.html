Asplenium gilpiniae var. pecten

Asplenium gilpiniae var. pecten, commonly known as the feather fern, is a variant of the genus Asplenium native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble the structure of a feather, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, which helps it retain moisture in humid conditions.

This fern is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable indoor or outdoor settings.
