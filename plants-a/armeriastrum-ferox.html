Armeriastrum ferox

Armeriastrum ferox, commonly known as the feral armeria, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across open habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in desert scrub and chaparral ecosystems.
