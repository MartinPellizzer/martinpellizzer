Aristida longiramea

Aristida longiramea, commonly known as the long-bracted grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically arranged in a panicle structure. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, it is adapted to survive in dry environments with poor soil conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
