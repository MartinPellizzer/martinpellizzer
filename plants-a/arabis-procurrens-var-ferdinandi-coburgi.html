Arabis procurrens var. ferdinandi-coburgi

Arabis procurrens var. ferdinandi-coburgi, commonly known as the Coburg rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, dark green leaves that are covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are borne on slender, branching stems. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and alpine settings.
