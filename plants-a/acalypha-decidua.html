Acalypha decidua

Acalypha decidua, commonly known as the desert mallow, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1-3 meters, characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple, which bloom during the rainy season. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater and survive prolonged droughts.

Acalypha decidua is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
