Amaranthus deflexus f. rufescens

Amaranthus deflexus f. rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed amaranth, is a variant of the amaranth species characterized by its distinct reddish stem coloration.

It is an annual herb native to tropical and subtropical regions, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in some traditional agricultural practices.

The species is also notable for its potential as a forage crop due to its rapid growth and nutritional content.
