Astragalus comosoides

Astragalus comosoides, commonly known as the hairy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features compound leaves with small, hairy leaflets and produces yellow to orange pea-like flowers. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

Astragalus comosoides is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
