Autonoe

Autonoe, commonly known as the "weeping willow," is a deciduous tree native to the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a graceful, weeping appearance. The leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, turning yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist soils near rivers and streams.

The plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and its ability to stabilize riverbanks.
