Antirrhinum quinquelobum

Antirrhinum quinquelobum, commonly known as the five-lobed snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bilaterally symmetrical, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color. The plant produces erect, branched stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Antirrhinum quinquelobum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
