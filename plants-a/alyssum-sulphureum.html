Alyssum sulphureum

Alyssum sulphureum, commonly known as sulfur yellow alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum sulphureum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
