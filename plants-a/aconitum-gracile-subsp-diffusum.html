Aconitum gracile subsp. diffusum

Aconitum gracile subsp.

diffusum, commonly known as the slender monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe. It features striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a helmet-like shape, typical of the genus Aconitum.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive basal rosette, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care.
