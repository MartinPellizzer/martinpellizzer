Alcanna

Alcanna, commonly known as the white lady or white sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It has slender, branching stems and small, narrow leaves.

Alcanna is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
