Aechmanthera wallichii

Aechmanthera wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant is known for its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
