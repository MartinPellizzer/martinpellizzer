Atriplex canescens var. macilenta

Atriplex canescens var. macilenta, commonly known as silverleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which reflects sunlight and reduces water loss. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces clusters of greenish-yellow flowers. It is well adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable species for stabilizing disturbed soils.

Its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in harsh environments make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
