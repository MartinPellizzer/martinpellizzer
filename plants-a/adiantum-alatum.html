Adiantum alatum

Adiantum alatum, commonly known as the Maidenhair Fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Adiantaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are bi-lobed and often have a distinctive, finely divided structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodland areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually bright green and can be either evergreen or deciduous depending on the climate.

Adiantum alatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
