Astragalus parnassi var. albanicus

Astragalus parnassi var. albanicus, commonly known as the Parnassian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its ecological role includes nitrogen fixation and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
