Arrabidaea platyphylla var. firmula

Arrabidaea platyphylla var. firmula, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a deciduous shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
