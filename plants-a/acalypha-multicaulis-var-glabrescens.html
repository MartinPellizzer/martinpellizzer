Acalypha multicaulis var. glabrescens

Acalypha multicaulis var. glabrescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red-leaf acalypha, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and striking, lobed leaves that often display a vibrant red or purple hue. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in warm climates.
