Actinidia championii

Actinidia championii, commonly known as the Chinese gooseberry, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces berries that mature to a bright red color, often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

Actinidia championii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
