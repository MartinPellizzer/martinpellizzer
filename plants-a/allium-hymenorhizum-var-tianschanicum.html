Allium hymenorhizum var. tianschanicum

Allium hymenorhizum var. tianschanicum, commonly known as the Tianshan garlic, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and leeks, and is characterized by its bulbous structure and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its culinary use and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
