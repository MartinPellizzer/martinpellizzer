Araucaria luxurians

Araucaria luxurians, commonly known as the Brazilian pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It features a distinctive pyramidal shape with long, needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in spirals. The tree produces cones that contain edible seeds, which are a food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in some areas.
