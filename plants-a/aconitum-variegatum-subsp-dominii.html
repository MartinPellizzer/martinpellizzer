Aconitum variegatum subsp. dominii

Aconitum variegatum subsp.

dominii, commonly known as the purple aconite, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant features a basal rosette of dark green, heart-shaped leaves and has a tall, unbranched stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
