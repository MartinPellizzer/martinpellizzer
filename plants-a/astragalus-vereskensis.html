Astragalus vereskensis

Astragalus vereskensis, commonly known as the Veresk milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and may have traditional uses in local medicine.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
