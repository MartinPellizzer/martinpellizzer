Adesmia filipes

Adesmia filipes, commonly known as the silky pod vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, delicate white or pale yellow flowers that are followed by slender, silky pods. Its root system is known to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
