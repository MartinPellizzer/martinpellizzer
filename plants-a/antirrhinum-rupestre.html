Antirrhinum rupestre

Antirrhinum rupestre, commonly known as the rock snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, with a rough texture.

Antirrhinum rupestre is often cultivated in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
