Acaena sanguisorbae var. conjungens

Acaena sanguisorbae var. conjungens, commonly known as the New Zealand bracken burr, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, red, spiny fruits that mature in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its unique morphology includes hooked bracts that aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animal fur.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
