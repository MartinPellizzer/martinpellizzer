Ageratum aquaticum

Ageratum aquaticum, commonly known as water ageratum, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist environments and is often used in water gardens or as a pond margin plant.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
