Amaryllis leopoldii f. whitakeri

Amaryllis leopoldii f. whitakeri, commonly known as the Whitaker's amaryllis, is a rare and striking hybrid within the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink, with a distinctive red or purple stripe down the center of each petal. The plant produces strap-like, evergreen leaves that are broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It is native to South Africa and thrives in well-drained soil with moderate sunlight.

Due to its rarity and horticultural value, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts and botanic gardens.
