Astragalus tribuloides var. mareoticus

Astragalus tribuloides var. mareoticus, commonly known as the Egyptian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Nile Valley and surrounding regions of Egypt.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces small, yellowish pods containing several elliptical seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
