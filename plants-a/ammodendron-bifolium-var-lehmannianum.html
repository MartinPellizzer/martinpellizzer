Ammodendron bifolium var. lehmannianum

Ammodendron bifolium var. lehmannianum, commonly known as the Lehmann's date palm, is a shrub-like plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with multiple stems that bear clusters of small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, date-like fruits that are typically consumed fresh or dried. It is well adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and resistance to high temperatures.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts within its native range.
