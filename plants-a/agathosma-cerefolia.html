Agathosma cerefolia

Agathosma cerefolia, commonly known as Cape geranium, is a perennial herb native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, pale blue or purple flowers. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
