Agapetes odontocera var. odontocera

Agapetes odontocera var. odontocera, commonly known as the toothed agapete, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in cool, shaded garden environments.
