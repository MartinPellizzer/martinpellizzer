Acer paxii var. ningpoense

Acer paxii var. ningpoense, commonly known as the Ningpo maple, is a variant of the maple species native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and parks.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
