Aster variabilis

Aster variabilis, commonly known as the New England aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features vibrant purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually hairy and sturdy.

Aster variabilis is often found in moist meadows, prairies, and along stream banks, where it thrives in full sun.
