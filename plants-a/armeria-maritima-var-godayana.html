Armeria maritima var. godayana

Armeria maritima var. godayana, commonly known as the Godyana thrift, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to salt spray and coastal conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
