Amomum cassumunar

Amomum cassumunar, commonly known as Greater Galangal, is a rhizomatous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, tubular roots that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant typically grows in wet, tropical environments and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a distinct, spicy aroma.

The rhizomes are harvested for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and digestive benefits.
