Agave geminiflora var. filamentosa

Agave geminiflora var. filamentosa, commonly known as the threadleaf agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a blue-gray waxy coating. The leaves are narrow and have a fibrous texture, giving the plant its common name. It produces an inflorescence of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

This species is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
