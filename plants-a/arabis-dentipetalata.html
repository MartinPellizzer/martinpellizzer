Arabis dentipetalata

Arabis dentipetalata, commonly known as the toothed rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed and often have toothed margins, giving it its scientific name. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in early spring.

Arabis dentipetalata is generally hardy and thrives in full sun to partial shade conditions.
