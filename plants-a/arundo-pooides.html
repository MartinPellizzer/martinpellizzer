Arundo pooides

Arundo pooides, commonly known as the narrowleaf reed, is a tall perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. The plant has long, slender leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is known for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland areas.

Arundo pooides is also sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance.
