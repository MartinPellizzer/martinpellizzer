Abelia davidii

Abelia davidii, commonly known as the Chinese abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Abelia davidii is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
