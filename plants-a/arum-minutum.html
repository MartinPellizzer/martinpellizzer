Arum minutum

Arum minutum, commonly known as the dwarf arum, is a species of flowering plant in the Araceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in the Appalachian region. The plant features a distinctive, arrow-shaped leaf that is typically dark green with a reddish underside. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, which is usually white or pale green.

Arum minutum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
