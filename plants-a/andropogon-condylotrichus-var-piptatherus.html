Andropogon condylotrichus var. piptatherus

Andropogon condylotrichus var. piptatherus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and tufted inflorescence, which consists of spikelets arranged in a loose panicle. The plant typically grows in full sun and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a common sight in open woodlands and prairies. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a distinctive flowering structure that aids in wind pollination.

This grass is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
