Artemisia occitanica

Artemisia occitanica, commonly known as the Occitanian wormwood, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that appear in late summer. It thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thujone, which have been used historically in traditional medicine.
