Acer campestre var. austriacum

Acer campestre var. austriacum, commonly known as the Austrian Field Maple, is a variant of the Field Maple native to Central Europe.

It is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with five to seven shallow lobes, and turn vibrant shades of yellow and red in autumn. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.
