Astragalus podperae var. major

Astragalus podperae var. major, commonly known as the Sierra foothill milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces elongated, narrow pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves, which are often hairy and grayish-green in appearance.
