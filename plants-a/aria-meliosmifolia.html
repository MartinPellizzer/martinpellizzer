Aria meliosmifolia

Aria meliosmifolia, commonly known as the fragrant flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has palmate leaves composed of five to seven leaflets, which are glossy and dark green. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Aria meliosmifolia is also valued for its fragrant flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
