Angraecum paniculatum

Angraecum paniculatum, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It features long, drooping inflorescences with numerous fragrant, white flowers that have a distinctive star-like shape. The plant produces aerial roots and thrives in bright, indirect light with high humidity. Its flowers bloom at night, attracting specific moths for pollination.

Angraecum paniculatum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
