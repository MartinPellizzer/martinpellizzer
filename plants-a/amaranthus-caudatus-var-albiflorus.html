Amaranthus caudatus var. albiflorus

Amaranthus caudatus var. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered amaranth, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients, making it valuable in agriculture and nutrition. Its leaves are also consumed as a leafy green vegetable in various cuisines.

The plant thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and edible purposes.
