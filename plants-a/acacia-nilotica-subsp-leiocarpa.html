Acacia nilotica subsp. leiocarpa

Acacia nilotica subsp.

leiocarpa, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Africa and the Arabian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval leaflets.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, spherical clusters, which are followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry climates.

The wood and gum of this species have been used traditionally for various purposes, including construction and medicinal applications.
