Aster obovatus

Aster obovatus, commonly known as the broad-leaf aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has lance-shaped to obovate leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, bogs, and stream banks.

Aster obovatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
