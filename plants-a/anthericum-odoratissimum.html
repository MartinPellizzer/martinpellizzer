Anthericum odoratissimum

Anthericum odoratissimum, commonly known as the fragrant arum, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves and its distinctive inflorescence, which consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered around the spadix, which is covered in a dense layer of tiny, pollen-producing structures called microsporangia. Anthericum odoratissimum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Its unique botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
