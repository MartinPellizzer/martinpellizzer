Alyssum preissmannii

Alyssum preissmannii, commonly known as the desert alyssum, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that thrives in dry, rocky soils and full sun. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in spring and summer. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Alyssum preissmannii is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in harsh environments.
