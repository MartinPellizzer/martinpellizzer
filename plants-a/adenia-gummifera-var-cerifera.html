Adenia gummifera var. cerifera

Adenia gummifera var. cerifera, commonly known as the wax tree, is a tropical shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy bark that exudes a sticky resin when damaged. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is often found in coastal and dry forest habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
