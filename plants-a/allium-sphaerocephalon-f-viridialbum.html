Allium sphaerocephalon f. viridialbum

Allium sphaerocephalon f. viridialbum, commonly known as the greenish globe onion, is a botanical variant of the globe onion species.

It is characterized by its compact, spherical bulbs and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in a spherical cluster, typical of the Allium genus. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild, sweet flavor, often used in culinary applications.
