Acanthopsis nitida

Acanthopsis nitida, commonly known as the silver leaf acanthopsis, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the cooler months. It is often found growing in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
