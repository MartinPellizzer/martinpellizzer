Allium porraceum

Allium porraceum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garlic.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, often mistaken for grasses or other herbaceous plants.

The plant is valued for its edible bulbs, which have a mild garlic flavor and are used in various culinary applications.
