Aechmea angustifolia

Aechmea angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pineapple plant, is a flowering bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and grass-like, forming a central cup that collects rainwater. The plant produces striking, spiky inflorescences that bloom in shades of red, orange, or yellow, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Aechmea angustifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in humid environments.

Morphology

Aechmea angustifolia has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, with a bluish-green color and a pointed tip.

The plant lacks a traditional stem, instead having a short, underground rhizome that supports its growth. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix surrounded by a colorful bract, usually in shades of red or orange, and bloom during the summer months, pollinated by insects. The fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that disperses seeds through wind and animal consumption.

The roots are fibrous and help anchor the plant while absorbing nutrients from the surrounding soil.
