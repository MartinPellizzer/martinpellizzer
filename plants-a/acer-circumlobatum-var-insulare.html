Acer circumlobatum var. insulare

Acer circumlobatum var. insulare, commonly known as the island maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its alternate, palmately lobed leaves that are typically green on top and fuzzy white underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by orange-red samaras, or winged seeds, in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and along stream banks.

This variety is distinguished by its smaller size and more compact growth habit compared to the typical form of Acer circumlobatum.
