Allium consimile

Allium consimile, commonly known as the bulbous garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and leeks, and is characterized by its aromatic, bulbous underground stems. The plant produces grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer. Its bulbs are often used in culinary applications for their mild garlic-like flavor.

Botanically, it is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in temperate climates.
