Allium fissile

Allium fissile, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garlic.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale purple flowers in clusters. Its bulbous stem is used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice.

Allium fissile is characterized by its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
