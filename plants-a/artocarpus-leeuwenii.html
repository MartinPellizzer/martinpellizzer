Artocarpus leeuwenii

Artocarpus leeuwenii, commonly known as the Samoan breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive fruit. The fruit, which is typically green when mature, develops a rough, hairy exterior and contains a starchy, edible pulp. The tree is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is an important part of the local diet and culture.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
