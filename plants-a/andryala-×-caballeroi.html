Andryala × caballeroi

Andryala × caballeroi, commonly known as the hybrid snowbell, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural collections.
