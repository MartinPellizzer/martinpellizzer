Acisanthera beccabunga

Acisanthera beccabunga, commonly known as the water buttercup, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It features small, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are floating or submerged, depending on the water depth. Its roots help stabilize soil and improve water quality by filtering nutrients.
