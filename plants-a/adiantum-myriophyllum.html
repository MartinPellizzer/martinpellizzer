Adiantum myriophyllum

Adiantum myriophyllum, commonly known as the royal fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are finely divided, giving it a feathery appearance. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

The sori, which are clusters of spore-producing structures, are located on the underside of the fronds.
