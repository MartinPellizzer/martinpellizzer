Astragalus simplicifolius var. spatulatus

Astragalus simplicifolius var. spatulatus, commonly known as the spatulate milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are simple and spatulate in shape, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands and sagebrush habitats.

This plant is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in arid environments.
