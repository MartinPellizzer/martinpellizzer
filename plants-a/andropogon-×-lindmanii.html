Andropogon × lindmanii

Andropogon × lindmanii, commonly known as the inland salt marsh grass, is a hybrid grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant typically thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond edges, where it forms dense stands. Its flowering stems produce panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Andropogon × lindmanii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
