Achyranthes bidentata var. longifolia

Achyranthes bidentata var. longifolia, commonly known as Chinese knotweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are often reddish in color and covered with stiff hairs. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in spikes, while the fruit is a three-lobed capsule containing numerous tiny seeds.

This plant is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.
