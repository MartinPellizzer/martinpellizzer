Aria dunnii

Aria dunnii, commonly known as the Dunns' arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and striking blue-violet flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value, while its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aria dunnii is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
