Angelica boissieuana

Angelica boissieuana, commonly known as the Boissieu angelica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like carrots and parsley. The plant features a tall, slender stem with finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a soft, hairy texture. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbrella-like inflorescences, typically blooming in late summer.

Angelica boissieuana is valued for its aromatic foliage and historical use in traditional medicine.
