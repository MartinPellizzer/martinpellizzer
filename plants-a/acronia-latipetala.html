Acronia latipetala

Acronia latipetala, commonly known as the broad-petioled acronia, is a species of flowering plant native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant has elongated, broad leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Acronia latipetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
