Andropogon linnaeanus

Andropogon linnaeanus, commonly known as tallgrass bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairie ecosystems.

Andropogon linnaeanus plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
