Amauropelta amambayensis

Amauropelta amambayensis, commonly known as the Amambay fern, is a rare species of tree fern native to the lowland rainforests of Paraguay.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant exhibits a unique growth habit, with its fronds emerging from the top of the trunk and cascading downward. Its spores are produced in sori located on the undersides of the fronds, typical of fern reproductive structures.

Due to habitat loss, Amauropelta amambayensis is considered endangered and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
