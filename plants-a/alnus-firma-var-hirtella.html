Alnus firma var. hirtella

Alnus firma var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy alder, is a variant of the alder tree native to parts of North America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian areas.

The bark is typically smooth and gray, with a distinctive, fragrant aroma.
