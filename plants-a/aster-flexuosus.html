Aster flexuosus

Aster flexuosus, commonly known as the drooping aster, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender, branched stems that often droop, giving it its common name. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, with ray florets that bloom in late summer to early fall.

Aster flexuosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
