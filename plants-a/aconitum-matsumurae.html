Aconitum matsumurae

Aconitum matsumurae, commonly known as the Japanese monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple spurred flowers. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. It is known for its toxic alkaloids, which make it a plant of both botanical interest and caution.

Aconitum matsumurae is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
