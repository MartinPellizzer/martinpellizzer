Allium × cornutum

Allium × cornutum, commonly known as the woolly leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grayish-white pubescence covering its leaves and stems. The plant produces umbels of pale purple to white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic-like flavor, making it a cultivated ornamental and culinary plant.

Allium × cornutum thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
