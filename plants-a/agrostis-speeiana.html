Agrostis speeiana

Agrostis speeiana, commonly known as Spee's bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growths and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas or along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually light to dark green in color. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are slightly hairy and produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Spee's bentgrass is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
