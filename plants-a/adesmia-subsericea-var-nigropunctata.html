Adesmia subsericea var. nigropunctata

Adesmia subsericea var. nigropunctata, commonly known as the black-punctured adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, elliptical leaflets and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white or pale pink. Its fruit is a legume pod that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its ecological role in soil enrichment.

This variant is distinguished by its dark, punctuated markings on the leaves, which give it its common name.
