Abutilon virgatum

Abutilon virgatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian mallow, is a deciduous shrub native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Abutilon virgatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
