Aquilegia bertolonii var. reuteri

Aquilegia bertolonii var. reuteri, commonly known as the Reuter's columbine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that come in shades of red, purple, and blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and hardy nature.
