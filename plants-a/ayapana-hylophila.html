Ayapana hylophila

Ayapana hylophila, commonly known as the Brazilian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking blue or violet flowers during the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

Ayapana hylophila is also valued for its traditional medicinal uses in Brazilian folk medicine.
