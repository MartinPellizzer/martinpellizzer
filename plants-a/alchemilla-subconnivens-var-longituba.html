Alchemilla subconnivens var. longituba

Alchemilla subconnivens var. longituba, commonly known as the long-stemmed lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are borne on slender, upright stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, often found in woodland or meadow environments.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and delicate inflorescences.
