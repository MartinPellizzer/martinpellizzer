Athamanta della-cellae

Athamanta della-cellae, commonly known as the lacecap orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, intricate floral structure. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically arranged in a dense, spiky inflorescence. Its leaves are elongated and narrow, often with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Athamanta della-cellae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
