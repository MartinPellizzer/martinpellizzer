Agrimonia eupatoria var. typica

Agrimonia eupatoria var. typica, commonly known as common agrimony, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are pinnately lobed with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
