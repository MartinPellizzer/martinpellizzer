Anemone thomsonii var. friesiorum

Anemone thomsonii var. friesiorum, commonly known as the Thomson's anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded woodland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
