Athroisma viscida

Athroisma viscida, commonly known as sticky leaf or sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glandular, sticky leaves that emit a sweet, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves contain a high concentration of aromatic oils, which contribute to its unique fragrance.

Athroisma viscida is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as swamps and riverbanks.
