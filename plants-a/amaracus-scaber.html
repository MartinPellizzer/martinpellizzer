Amaracus scaber

Amaracus scaber, commonly known as the rough amaranth, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that range from red to orange. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. Its seeds are rich in protein and have been used as a food source in some cultures.

Amaracus scaber thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
