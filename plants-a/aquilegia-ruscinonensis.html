Aquilegia ruscinonensis

Aquilegia ruscinonensis, commonly known as the Ruscinonensis Columbine, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is characterized by its striking, spurred petals that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are compound, with finely divided leaflets, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant flowers and ecological benefits.
