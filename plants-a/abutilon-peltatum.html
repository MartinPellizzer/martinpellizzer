Abutilon peltatum

Abutilon peltatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive tactile appearance. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often blooming in late summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Abutilon peltatum is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to attract pollinators.
