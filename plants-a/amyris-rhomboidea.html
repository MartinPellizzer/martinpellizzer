Amyris rhomboidea

Amyris rhomboidea, commonly known as the rhomboid sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional crafts and incense.

Amyris rhomboidea thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
