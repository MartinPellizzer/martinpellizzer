Allophylus grandifolius

Allophylus grandifolius, commonly known as the large-leaved alder, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and consist of several oval to lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellowish, followed by small, fleshy berries that serve as a food source for various birds and mammals. Allophylus grandifolius thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems it inhabits.
