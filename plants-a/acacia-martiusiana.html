Acacia martiusiana

Acacia martiusiana, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.

The golden wattle is also the national floral emblem of Australia, symbolizing the country's natural beauty and resilience.
