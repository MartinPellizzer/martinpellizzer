Aconitum firmum nothosubsp. paxii

Aconitum firmum nothosubsp.

paxii, commonly known as the Pacific aconite, is a hybrid subspecies native to the western United States. It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks. It has dark green, doubly serrated leaves and is known for its toxic alkaloids, which make it a plant to be handled with care.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens despite its poisonous nature.
