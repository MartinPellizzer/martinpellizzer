Arabis hirsuta f. grandiflora

Arabis hirsuta f. grandiflora, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as cabbages and mustard. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in racemes, typically blooming in early spring.

The species is often found growing in rocky soils and along coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
