Aegilops searsii

Aegilops searsii, commonly known as the Sears' goatgrass, is a wild grass species native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets with small, awned florets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. Aegilops searsii is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It has been studied for its potential in agricultural breeding programs due to its genetic diversity and adaptability.
