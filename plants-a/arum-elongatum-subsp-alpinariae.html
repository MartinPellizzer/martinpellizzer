Arum elongatum subsp. alpinariae

Arum elongatum subsp.

alpinariae, commonly known as the alpine arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and a distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and it relies on insect pollination.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
