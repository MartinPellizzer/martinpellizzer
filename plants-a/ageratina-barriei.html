Ageratina barriei

Ageratina barriei, commonly known as the Barrie's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of tiny flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy blue to purple flower heads appear in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ageratina barriei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
