Aquilegia flavescens var. rubicunda

Aquilegia flavescens var. rubicunda, commonly known as the red-flowered columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred petals that range from red to orange in color. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are deeply lobed.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
