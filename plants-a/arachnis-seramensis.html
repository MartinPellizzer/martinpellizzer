Arachnis seramensis

Arachnis seramensis, commonly known as the Seram orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Indonesian island of Seram.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-white or pale yellow flowers with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
