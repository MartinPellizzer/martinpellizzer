Asplenium wudangense

Asplenium wudangense, commonly known as the Wudang spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a small, terrestrial fern that typically grows in shaded, humid environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a rosette-shaped frond structure with closely spaced, linear leaflets. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium wudangense is native to China and is noted for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitat.
