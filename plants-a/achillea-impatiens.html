Achillea impatiens

Achillea impatiens, commonly known as common yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its flat, composite flower heads that range from white to pinkish in color. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces numerous small flowers arranged in a single cluster. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Achillea impatiens is also known for its medicinal properties and historical use in traditional remedies.
