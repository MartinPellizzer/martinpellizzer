Asperula aristata var. macrorrhiza

Asperula aristata var. macrorrhiza, commonly known as the large-leaf woodruff, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers. The leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a glossy green surface. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
