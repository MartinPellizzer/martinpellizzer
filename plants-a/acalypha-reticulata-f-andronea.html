Acalypha reticulata f. andronea

Acalypha reticulata f. andronea, commonly known as the variegated nettleleaf ironweed, is a hybrid form of the species Acalypha reticulata.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring combinations of green, white, and yellow on its leaves. The plant is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or reddish in color.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its attractive foliage rather than its flowers.
