Amphithalea muirii

Amphithalea muirii, commonly known as the Muir's orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family found in the wet tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a combination of white, yellow, and green hues. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on the trunks of trees or in dense undergrowth. The plant has a complex root system that helps it absorb nutrients from the nutrient-poor soil in its habitat.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for undisturbed rainforest ecosystems.
