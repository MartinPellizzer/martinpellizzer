Allium caesioides

Allium caesioides, commonly known as purple onion, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, edible bulbs similar to onions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
