Asplenium arboreum var. obtusum

Asplenium arboreum var. obtusum, commonly known as the broad-leaf rock fern, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive, broad, lance-shaped leaves.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Indonesia. The leaves are typically evergreen, with a leathery texture and a deep green color, and they often have a serrated or lobed margin. This fern thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in moist forest understories.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a notable species in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
