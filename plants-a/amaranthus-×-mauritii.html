Amaranthus × mauritii

Amaranthus × mauritii, commonly known as lovegrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to Africa and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the variety. It is often cultivated as a ornamental grass due to its hardiness and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.

Amaranthus × mauritii is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop in some regions.
