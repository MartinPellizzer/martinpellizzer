Agave aloina

Agave aloina, commonly known as the Mexican heartleaf agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears clusters of small, white flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Agave aloina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
