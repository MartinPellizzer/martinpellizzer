Agrostis rupestris var. viridula

Agrostis rupestris var. viridula, commonly known as the green rock grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often found in alpine or coastal environments. The plant has slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually green to bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
