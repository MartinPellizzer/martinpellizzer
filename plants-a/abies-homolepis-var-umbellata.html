Abies homolepis var. umbellata

Abies homolepis var. umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella fir, is a variant of the Japanese fir.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broadly conical shape and dense, soft needles. The tree produces small, cylindrical cones that hang in clusters, giving it its common name. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions of East Asia.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
