Aconitum jin-muratae

Aconitum jin-muratae, commonly known as the Japanese aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and western Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, spikelike inflorescences. Its leaves are palmately divided with a serrated margin, and the plant has a toxic nature due to the presence of alkaloids such as aconitine.

Aconitum jin-muratae thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.
