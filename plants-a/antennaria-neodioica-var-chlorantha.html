Antennaria neodioica var. chlorantha

Antennaria neodioica var. chlorantha, commonly known as the greenleaf mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of white or pale yellow.

The variety chlorantha is distinguished by its more greenish foliage compared to other varieties of Antennaria neodioica.
