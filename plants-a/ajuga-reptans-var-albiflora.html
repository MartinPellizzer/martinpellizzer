Ajuga reptans var. albiflora

Ajuga reptans var. albiflora, commonly known as white-flowered bugleweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ground cover in shaded areas.
