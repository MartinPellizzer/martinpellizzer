Aegilops mutica var. puluci

Aegilops mutica var. puluci, commonly known as the rough goatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and glabrous or slightly hairy leaves, depending on the variety. The plant produces spikelets that are typically borne in loose, open panicles, with each spikelet containing two to three florets. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and along roadsides.

The species is notable for its role in agricultural ecosystems and its potential as a forage crop.
