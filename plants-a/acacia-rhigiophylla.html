Acacia rhigiophylla

Acacia rhigiophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, phyllodes that are typically narrow and linear. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
