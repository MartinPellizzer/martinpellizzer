Arthraxon cuspidatus

Arthraxon cuspidatus, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, linear leaves that are typically hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread readily in moist, well-drained soils.

Arthraxon cuspidatus is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.
