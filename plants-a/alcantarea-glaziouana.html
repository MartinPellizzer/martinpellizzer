Alcantarea glaziouana

Alcantarea glaziouana, commonly known as the Glaziouana Cactus, is a striking member of the Cactaceae family native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to several meters in height, often adorned with spines and clusters of vibrant yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid or semi-arid environments. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
