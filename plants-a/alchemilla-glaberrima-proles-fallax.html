Alchemilla glaberrima proles fallax

Alchemilla glaberrima proles fallax, commonly known as the silverweed or lesser silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in umbels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or meadows.

Alchemilla glaberrima proles fallax is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
