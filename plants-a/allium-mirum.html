Allium mirum

Allium mirum, commonly known as the fairy garlic, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, bulbous structure and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
