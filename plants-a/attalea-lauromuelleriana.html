Attalea lauromuelleriana

Attalea lauromuelleriana, commonly known as the Paraguay palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina.

It is a tall, slender tree that can reach heights of up to 20 meters, with a straight trunk and a crown of pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, fibrous fruits containing edible seeds. Its leaves are long and narrow, often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in wetlands or along riverbanks.
