Aquilegia vulgaris var. typica

Aquilegia vulgaris var. typica, commonly known as the common columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It features striking, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple, with five sepals that are often colored in contrasting hues. The plant has palmately divided leaves, which are compound and have a distinctive, fern-like appearance. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, often found in woodland edges and meadows.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens and natural habitats.
