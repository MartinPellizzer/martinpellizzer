Austrodanthonia procera

Austrodanthonia procera, commonly known as the tall kangaroo grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands.

Austrodanthonia procera plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
