Acianthera neojordanensis

Acianthera neojordanensis, commonly known as the Jordanian orchid, is a rare and endangered species native to the Middle East.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture from the limited soil available in its habitat.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
