Allium saxatile var. rubriflorum

Allium saxatile var. rubriflorum, commonly known as the redflower garlic or rock garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink to red flowers. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pale purple bulbs that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its aromatic foliage and long-lasting blooms.
