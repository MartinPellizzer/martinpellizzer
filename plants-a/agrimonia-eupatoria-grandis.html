Agrimonia eupatoria grandis

Agrimonia eupatoria grandis, commonly known as greater agrimony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by red, velvety berries. Its leaves are pinnately divided with serrated edges, and the plant has a strong, aromatic scent.

Agrimony is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
