Acer floridanum var. villipes

Acer floridanum var. villipes, commonly known as the Florida maple, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves that turn bright red in the fall. The tree produces clusters of small, yellow-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by winged seeds. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in its native range.
