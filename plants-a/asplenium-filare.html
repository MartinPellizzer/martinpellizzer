Asplenium filare

Asplenium filare, commonly known as the narrow fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, slender fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered on the undersides of its leaves. Asplenium filare prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found growing in rocky or wooded areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for studying fern morphology and ecological adaptation.
