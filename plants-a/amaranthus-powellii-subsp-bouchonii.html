Amaranthus powellii subsp. bouchonii

Amaranthus powellii subsp.

bouchonii, commonly known as the Bouchon's amaranth, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its erect growth habit and bracteate inflorescences.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green to reddish in color. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal practices.
