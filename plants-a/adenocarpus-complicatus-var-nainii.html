Adenocarpus complicatus var. nainii

Adenocarpus complicatus var. nainii, commonly known as the Naini thornapple, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
