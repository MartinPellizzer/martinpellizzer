Amauriopsis biternata

Amauriopsis biternata, commonly known as the three-leaved amauriopsis, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, triangular leaves arranged in a three-leaf cluster. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its unique leaf morphology, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
