Allium schoenoprasum var. buhseanum

Allium schoenoprasum var. buhseanum, commonly known as the Buhse's chive, is a botanical variant of the common chive.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white flowers. This variety is native to Central Asia and is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in culinary applications. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, exhibiting a hardy nature suitable for temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to both ornamental and edible gardens.
