Antirrhinum variabile

Antirrhinum variabile, commonly known as the variable snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas, thriving in full sun. Its flowers are tubular at the base and flare out at the top, with two upper lips that form a distinctive "snapdragon" shape.

Antirrhinum variabile is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public landscapes.
