Aspidium albopunctatum

Aspidium albopunctatum, commonly known as the white-spotted shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds. The fronds are typically light green with distinctive white or pale spots, which give the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rock crevices.

The plant's rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, highlighting its ecological and cultural significance.
