Amomum bilabiatum

Amomum bilabiatum, commonly known as the two-lip cardamom, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark brown, three-lobed fruits. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is cultivated for both its medicinal value and aromatic qualities.
