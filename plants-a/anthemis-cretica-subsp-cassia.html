Anthemis cretica subsp. cassia

Anthemis cretica subsp.

cassia, commonly known as Cretan tansy, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early autumn.

The plant has finely divided, silvery-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its historical use in herbal medicine and as a natural insect repellent highlights its botanical and practical significance.
