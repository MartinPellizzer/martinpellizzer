Actinidia cinerascens var. longipetiolata

Actinidia cinerascens var. longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled actinidia, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and compound leaves with elongated petioles. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer, followed by edible, fuzzy berries that ripen in late summer. Its foliage turns vibrant yellow in autumn, adding ornamental value to gardens.

It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for shaded landscapes.
