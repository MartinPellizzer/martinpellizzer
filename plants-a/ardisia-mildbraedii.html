Ardisia mildbraedii

Ardisia mildbraedii, commonly known as the Hawaiian ardisia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for native birds and wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and lowland forests.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
