Abies polita

Abies polita, commonly known as the Japanese white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and silver-blue foliage that gives it a striking appearance. The needles are flat, soft, and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its native habitat. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of the Pinaceae family.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
