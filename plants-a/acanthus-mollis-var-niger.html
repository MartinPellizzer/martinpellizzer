Acanthus mollis var. niger

Acanthus mollis var. niger, commonly known as black acanthus, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black foliage, which provides a dramatic contrast to its soft, velvety leaves. The plant produces tall spikes of small, pale blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often used in landscaping for its visual impact and low maintenance requirements.
