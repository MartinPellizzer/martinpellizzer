Acrocomia karukerana

Acrocomia karukerana, commonly known as the African palm, is a tropical plant native to southern Africa.

It is a tall, slender palm with a single stem that can grow up to 20 meters in height. The plant produces large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used for thatching and roofing. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, and it produces edible fruits that are consumed by both humans and wildlife.

The plant is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
