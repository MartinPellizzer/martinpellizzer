Achyranthes hamata

Achyranthes hamata, commonly known as the spurred spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family, and are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.

Achyranthes hamata is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
