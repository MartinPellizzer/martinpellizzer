Acmadenia rosmarinifolia

Acmadenia rosmarinifolia, commonly known as silverleaf acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in coastal landscaping due to its salt tolerance.
