Arundinella hookeriana

Arundinella hookeriana, commonly known as the Hooker's bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru, where it thrives in high-altitude environments. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 6 meters, forming dense clumps. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in panicles during the dry season.

Arundinella hookeriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional ecological systems.
