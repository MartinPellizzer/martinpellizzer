Adinandra excelsa

Adinandra excelsa, commonly known as the tall ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood, which is highly valued for its durability and strength. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and dark green in color. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, woody fruits.

Adinandra excelsa plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
