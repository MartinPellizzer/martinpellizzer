Aspidosperma subincanum

Aspidosperma subincanum, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow center. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds covered in a fleshy, sticky coating.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
