Androsace × escheri

Androsace × escheri, commonly known as the snow bell, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pink flowers. The plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments, such as alpine meadows or rocky soils. Its foliage consists of rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Androsace × escheri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in shaded areas.
