Astragalus sinaicus var. pedunculatus

Astragalus sinaicus var. pedunculatus, commonly known as the Sinai milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East, particularly Egypt and Jordan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-shaped flowers and compound leaves. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments, with deep roots that help it survive in sandy soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
