Anthericum fimbriatum

Anthericum fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters at the top of the stems, which are typical of the Cyperaceae family. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a fibrous texture.

Anthericum fimbriatum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
