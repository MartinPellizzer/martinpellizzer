Azalea indica var. speciosa

Azalea indica var. speciosa, commonly known as the Chinese azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in acidic, well-drained soil. It produces elliptical, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine, fuzzy hairs.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural landscapes.
