Anchusa azurea var. macrocarpa

Anchusa azurea var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited bluebottle, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces large, showy fruits that are typically cylindrical or ovoid in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long flowering period and striking foliage.
