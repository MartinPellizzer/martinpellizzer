Andropogon elythrophyllus

Andropogon elythrophyllus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland areas and is characterized by its slender, blue-green leaves and tall, cylindrical flower spikes. The plant has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. It is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Andropogon elythrophyllus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various insects and birds.
