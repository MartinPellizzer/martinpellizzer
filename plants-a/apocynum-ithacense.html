Apocynum ithacense

Apocynum ithacense, commonly known as Indian hemp, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its milky sap contains toxic compounds, which have historically been used in traditional medicine.

Apocynum ithacense is also noted for its resilience in various environmental conditions.
