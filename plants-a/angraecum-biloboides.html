Angraecum biloboides

Angraecum biloboides, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a unique epiphytic plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom at night. The plant features long, wiry stems and broad, leathery leaves that help it conserve moisture in its arid habitat. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue with intricate patterns, attracting specific nocturnal pollinators like moths.

Angraecum biloboides is known for its slow growth and requires specific care to thrive in cultivation.
