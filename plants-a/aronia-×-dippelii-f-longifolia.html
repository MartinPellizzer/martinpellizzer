Aronia × dippelii f. longifolia

Aronia × dippelii f. longifolia, commonly known as the purple chokeberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are rich in antioxidants and anthocyanins. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in temperate climates. It has elliptical leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers in the spring.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and nutritional benefits.
