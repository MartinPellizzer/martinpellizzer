Asyneuma limonifolium subsp. limonifolium

Asyneuma limonifolium subsp.

limonifolium, commonly known as the lemon-leaf asyneuma, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive, fragrant aroma, while the flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
