Aquilegia vulgaris proles arbascensis

Aquilegia vulgaris proles arbascensis, commonly known as the Western Columbine, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in woodland and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, with finely divided foliage that adds to its ornamental appeal.

It is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
