Acacia visciflua

Acacia visciflua, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, slender, and silky-haired stipules, which give it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Acacia visciflua is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
