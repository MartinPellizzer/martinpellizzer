Artemisia albicaulis

Artemisia albicaulis, commonly known as silverleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which results from dense covering of fine white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flower clusters that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it a valuable plant in traditional medicine and horticulture.
