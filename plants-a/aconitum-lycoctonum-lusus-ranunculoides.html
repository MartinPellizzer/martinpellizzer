Aconitum lycoctonum lusus ranunculoides

Aconitum lycoctonum lusus ranunculoides, commonly known as the Larkspur, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, spurred flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has palmate or pinnate leaves that are typically deeply lobed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland areas.

However, it is highly toxic due to the presence of various alkaloids, making it dangerous to handle or ingest.
