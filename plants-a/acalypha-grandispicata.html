Acalypha grandispicata

Acalypha grandispicata, commonly known as the giant spiky hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy bracts that resemble petals, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered within the bracts. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Acalypha grandispicata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
