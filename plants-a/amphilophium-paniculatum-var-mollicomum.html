Amphilophium paniculatum var. mollicomum

Amphilophium paniculatum var. mollicomum, commonly known as the soft-leaved panicled amphilophium, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage and branched inflorescences that bear small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are opposite and elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
