Astragalus lippertii

Astragalus lippertii, commonly known as Lippert's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as rocky slopes and sandy soils. Astragalus lippertii is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root system.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important species in its native habitat.
