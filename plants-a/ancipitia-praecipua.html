Ancipitia praecipua

Ancipitia praecipua, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves covered in a dense silvery-white pubescence, which helps reflect intense sunlight. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pale blue and violet, typically blooming during the cooler months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, allowing it to thrive in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
