Astragalus dasysemius

Astragalus dasysemius, commonly known as the woolly milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its roots are known for their ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Astragalus dasysemius is often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
