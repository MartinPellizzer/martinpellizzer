Amaryllis dubia

Amaryllis dubia, commonly known as the ghost amaryllis, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, often in shades of pink, white, and red. The plant produces strap-like leaves that are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native arid environment. It blooms in the summer and can be grown both indoors and outdoors in suitable climates.

Amaryllis dubia is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a dramatic and exotic addition to their collection.
