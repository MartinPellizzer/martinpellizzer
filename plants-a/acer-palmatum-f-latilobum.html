Acer palmatum f. latilobum

Acer palmatum f. latilobum, commonly known as the Japanese maple with broad lobes, is a variant of the Japanese maple characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

Its leaves are palmately lobed, with broader and more pronounced lobes compared to the typical Acer palmatum. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The foliage displays vibrant colors during the growing season, adding aesthetic appeal to gardens and parks.
