Argemone platyceras subvar. rotundata

Argemone platyceras subvar. rotundata, commonly known as the round-seeded prickly poppy, is a species native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny seed pods that turn red as they mature. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are broad and lobed, often covered in fine hairs.

This subvariety is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
