Andropogon cryptopus

Andropogon cryptopus, commonly known as the Florida panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Due to its adaptability, it is often used in wetland restoration projects.
