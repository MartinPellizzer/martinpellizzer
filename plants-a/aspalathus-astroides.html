Aspalathus astroides

Aspalathus astroides, commonly known as the star bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its distinctive star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Aspalathus astroides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
