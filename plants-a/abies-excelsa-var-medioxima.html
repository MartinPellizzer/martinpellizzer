Abies excelsa var. medioxima

Abies excelsa var. medioxima, commonly known as the Mexican fir, is a variant of the white fir native to high-altitude regions of Mexico.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its conical shape and needle-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The cones of this species are cylindrical and maturing to a reddish-brown hue, containing winged seeds. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous forests.

The plant has limited distribution and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological and genetic traits.
