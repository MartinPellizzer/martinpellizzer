Aristida takeoi

Aristida takeoi, commonly known as Japanese needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, fluffy seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as meadows and forest edges.

Aristida takeoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its fine texture and drought tolerance.
