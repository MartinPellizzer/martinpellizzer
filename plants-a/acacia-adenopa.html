Acacia adenopa

Acacia adenopa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and compound leaves composed of several linear leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in globular clusters, which appear in spring and attract various pollinators. Acacia adenopa is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in reforestation efforts due to its hardy nature.
