Aglaia reticulata

Aglaia reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined aglaia, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family, which includes mahogany and other valuable hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

Aglaia reticulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
