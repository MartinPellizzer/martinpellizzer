Argemone mexicana var. spinosa

Argemone mexicana var. spinosa, commonly known as the Mexican prickly poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and striking yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain oil-rich seeds, which have been used traditionally for various purposes. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered an invasive species in some regions.
