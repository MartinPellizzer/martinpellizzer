Alopecurus grandiflorus

Alopecurus grandiflorus, commonly known as the meadow foxtail, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks. The plant features tall, slender stems that bear dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually greenish or pale blue, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

Alopecurus grandiflorus is often found in temperate climates and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
