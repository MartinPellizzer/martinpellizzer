Astracantha parnassi subsp. calabra

Astracantha parnassi subsp.

calabra, commonly known as the Calabrian Astracantha, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
