Acalypha brasiliensis var. obtusa

Acalypha brasiliensis var. obtusa, commonly known as the Brazilian nettle or velvetleaf, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are typically followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to spread rapidly, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
