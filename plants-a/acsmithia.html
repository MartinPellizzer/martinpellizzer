Acsmithia

Acsmithia, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow and sometimes pink. These plants are typically found in temperate regions and thrive in well-drained soils. Acsmithia species are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

They are perennial herbs that can grow up to 1 meter tall, with lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant blooms.
