Acilepis cirsiifolia

Acilepis cirsiifolia, commonly known as the woollyleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
