Astragalus geyeri var. geyeri

Astragalus geyeri var. geyeri, commonly known as Geyeri milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to cream-colored flowers in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in restoration projects due to its hardy nature.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
