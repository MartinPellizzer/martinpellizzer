Alliaria macrostachya

Alliaria macrostachya, commonly known as garlic mustard, is a biennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a strong garlic-like odor. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters at the end of its stems. Its fruit is a cylindrical silique containing several seeds.

It is known for its invasive tendencies in North American ecosystems.
