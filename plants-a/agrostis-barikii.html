Agrostis barikii

Agrostis barikii, commonly known as the Barik's bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia, particularly China and the Korean Peninsula.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender, wiry stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces compact spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Agrostis barikii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural applications.
