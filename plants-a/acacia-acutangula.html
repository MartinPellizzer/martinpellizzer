Acacia acutangula

Acacia acutangula, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, thread-like leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Acacia acutangula is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought resistance and thriving in well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
