Acacia bursaria

Acacia bursaria, commonly known as the burr acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and compound leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of whitish, powdery bloom. The plant produces yellow flowers in summer, followed by distinctive burr-like seed pods that persist into winter. Acacia bursaria is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important plant in its native habitat.
