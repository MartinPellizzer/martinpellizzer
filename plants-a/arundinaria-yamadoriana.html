Arundinaria yamadoriana

Arundinaria yamadoriana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Yamadori bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that form dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically greenish or pale yellow. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a slightly wavy margin, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Arundinaria yamadoriana is valued in horticulture for its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
