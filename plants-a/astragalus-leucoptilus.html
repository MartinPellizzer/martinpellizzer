Astragalus leucoptilus

Astragalus leucoptilus, commonly known as the white-winged milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus leucoptilus is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil.
