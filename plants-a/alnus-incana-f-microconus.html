Alnus incana f. microconus

Alnus incana f. microconus, commonly known as the dwarf alder, is a variant of the alder tree native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its small, compact growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by cylindrical cones that contain winged seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment in ecological restoration projects.
