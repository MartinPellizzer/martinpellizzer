Adenia bequaertii

Adenia bequaertii, commonly known as the African mountain cucumber, is a climbing shrub native to eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is characterized by its thick, woody stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive crown of bracts, followed by edible, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
