Aristida uruguayensis

Aristida uruguayensis, commonly known as the Uruguay grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Aristida uruguayensis is often used in landscaping and as a forage plant due to its hardy nature.
