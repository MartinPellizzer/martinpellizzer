Amaryllis × hybrida

Amaryllis × hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid amaryllis, is a popular ornamental plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors including red, pink, white, and yellow. The plant produces a tall, sturdy stem that bears multiple blooms, often in late winter to early spring. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous underground stem.

Amaryllis × hybrida is valued for its vibrant flowers and ease of cultivation, making it a favorite among gardeners and florists.
