Arisaema psittacus

Arisaema psittacus, commonly known as the parrot's beak arisaema, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant features a distinctive, elongated spadix surrounded by a hooded spathe, giving it its unique appearance. Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped and can grow quite large, often reaching several feet in length.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
