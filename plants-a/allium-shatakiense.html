Allium shatakiense

Allium shatakiense, commonly known as the Shikoku garlic, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes various species of onions and garlic. The plant produces small, clustered bulbs that are often used in traditional Japanese cuisine. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, growing up to 30 centimeters in height.

The flowers are typically white or pale purple, forming umbels at the top of the stem.
