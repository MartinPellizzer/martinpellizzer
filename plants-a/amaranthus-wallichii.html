Amaranthus wallichii

Amaranthus wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy inflorescences that range in color from red to orange. The plant produces small, triangular seeds that are often used as a food source in various traditional cuisines. Its leaves are edible and can be consumed both raw and cooked, adding nutritional value to the diet.

Amaranthus wallichii is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
