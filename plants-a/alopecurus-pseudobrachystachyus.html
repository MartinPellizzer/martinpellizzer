Alopecurus pseudobrachystachyus

Alopecurus pseudobrachystachyus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mouse-ear, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in moist, often calcareous soils, such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually pale green to blue in color, and it blooms during the summer months.

Alopecurus pseudobrachystachyus is often found in habitats with high humidity and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
