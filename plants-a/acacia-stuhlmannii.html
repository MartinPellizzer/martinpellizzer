Acacia stuhlmannii

Acacia stuhlmannii, commonly known as the Stuhlmann's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Acacia stuhlmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
