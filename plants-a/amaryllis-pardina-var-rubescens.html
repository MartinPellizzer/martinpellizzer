Amaryllis pardina var. rubescens

Amaryllis pardina var. rubescens, commonly known as the red-flowered amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from deep red to orange-red in color, often with a contrasting white or yellow base. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are typically a deep green, providing a lush backdrop to its showy blooms. It thrives in well-draining soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

This variety is known for its ease of care and ability to bloom reliably when provided with proper conditions.
