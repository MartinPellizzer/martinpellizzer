Anabasis gontscharowii

Anabasis gontscharowii, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low, sprawling growth habit adapted to harsh desert conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are usually succulent to store water. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in steppe and desert ecosystems.

Due to its resilience, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitat.
