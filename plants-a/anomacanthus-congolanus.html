Anomacanthus congolanus

Anomacanthus congolanus, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with a distinctive appearance, featuring thick, fleshy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
