Astragalus microphysopsis

Astragalus microphysopsis, commonly known as the small-fruited milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, harsh environments. It produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
