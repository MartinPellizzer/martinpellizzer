Allium fuscum subsp. fussii

Allium fuscum subsp.

fussii, commonly known as the purple bulbous onion, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features basal rosettes of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, pale purple to pink flowers.

The plant is characterized by its bulbous underground storage organs, which are used for propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
