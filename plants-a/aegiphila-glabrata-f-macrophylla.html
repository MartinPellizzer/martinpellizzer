Aegiphila glabrata f. macrophylla

Aegiphila glabrata f. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved aegiphila, is a variant of the species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its unique foliage and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
