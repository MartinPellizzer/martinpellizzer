Agrostis hugoniana var. aristata

Agrostis hugoniana var. aristata, commonly known as the aristate meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, wet meadows, and along streambanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are distinctive for their elongated, bristly awns. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
