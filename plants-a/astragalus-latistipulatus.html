Astragalus latistipulatus

Astragalus latistipulatus, commonly known as the broad-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Astragalus latistipulatus is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil.
