Anagallis serpens subsp. meyeri-johannis

Anagallis serpens subsp.

meyeri-johannis, commonly known as the Meyer's spreading willow herb, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features slender, trailing stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across rocky or gravelly soils.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the base. It thrives in sunny, well-drained environments and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and hardy member of the Primulaceae family.
