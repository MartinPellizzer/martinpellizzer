Allium stellerianum var. prostratum

Allium stellerianum var. prostratum, commonly known as the prostrate leek, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It features basal leaves that are narrow and grass-like, with a strong onion-like odor. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers in umbels, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental choice for rock gardens and borders.
