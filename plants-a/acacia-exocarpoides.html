Acacia exocarpoides

Acacia exocarpoides, commonly known as the African wattle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its bark is often used in traditional medicine, and the wood is valued for its durability.

The acacia's ability to thrive in arid conditions makes it an important component of dryland ecosystems.
