Archidendron arborescens

Archidendron arborescens, commonly known as the climbing tamarind, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, followed by pod fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its rapid growth makes it a popular choice for ornamental and ecological purposes.
