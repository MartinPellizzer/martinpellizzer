Aquilegia vulgaris var. thalictrifolia

Aquilegia vulgaris var. thalictrifolia, commonly known as the thalictrifolia columbine, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound leaves with a distinctive trifoliate arrangement, resembling the leaves of the genus Thalictrum. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
