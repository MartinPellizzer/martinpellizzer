Argyrolobium roseum var. album

Argyrolobium roseum var. album, commonly known as the white flaxflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features slender, branching stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
