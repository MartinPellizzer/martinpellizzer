Asplenium pinnatifidum var. gravesii

Asplenium pinnatifidum var. gravesii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, dark green fronds that are pinnately arranged. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive, almost lace-like appearance.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
