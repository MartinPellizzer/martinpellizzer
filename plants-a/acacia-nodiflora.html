Acacia nodiflora

Acacia nodiflora, commonly known as the climbing acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often climbing stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in compact clusters, which are followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. Acacia nodiflora thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

It plays a role in local ecosystems and has been used traditionally for various purposes.
