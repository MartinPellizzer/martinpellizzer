Acalypha tenuifolia

Acalypha tenuifolia, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain areas.
