Acronia suinii

Acronia suinii, commonly known as the pig's ear plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that resemble a pig's ear. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads. Acronia suinii thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and compact growth habit.
