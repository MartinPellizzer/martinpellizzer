Argyreia reticulata

Argyreia reticulata, commonly known as the "Cape jasmine" or "Bull's horn plant," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a climbing shrub that can grow up to several meters in height, featuring heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its rapid growth and ability to cover trellises or fences.
