Arnica lanceolata subsp. prima

Arnica lanceolata subsp.

prima, commonly known as the narrowleaf arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of linear, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers in the summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows. Its flowers are typically clustered in dense, showy inflorescences, and the plant is known for its medicinal properties. Arnica lanceolata subsp.

prima is valued in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
