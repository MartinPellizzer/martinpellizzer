Arisaema vituperatum

Arisaema vituperatum, commonly known as the Chinese cobra lily, is a herbaceous plant native to southern China.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features a short, rhizomatous stem with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and it produces berries that contain toxic seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional purposes.
