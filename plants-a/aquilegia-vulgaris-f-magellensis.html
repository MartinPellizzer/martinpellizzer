Aquilegia vulgaris f. magellensis

Aquilegia vulgaris f. magellensis, commonly known as the Magellanic columbine, is a horticultural form of the common columbine.

It is characterized by its striking blue and purple double flowers, which are more showy than those of the typical species. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. It is a perennial that thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
