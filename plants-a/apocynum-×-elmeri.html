Apocynum × elmeri

Apocynum × elmeri, commonly known as the hybrid dogbane, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruits are follicles containing numerous seeds, and the plant is often found in moist, disturbed soils.

Apocynum × elmeri is noted for its ornamental value and has some medicinal uses, though it contains toxic compounds.
