Amygdalopsis eburnea

Amygdalopsis eburnea, commonly known as the white acorn tree, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown. Its flowers are small and typically yellow or green, and it produces edible seeds that are encased in hard, woody pods.

The plant is valued for its timber and is also used in traditional medicine.
