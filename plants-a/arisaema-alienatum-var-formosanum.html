Arisaema alienatum var. formosanum

Arisaema alienatum var. formosanum, commonly known as the Formosan snake-root, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to Taiwan.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a broad, hooded spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are arrow-shaped and can reach up to 30 cm in length, with a dark green upper surface and lighter veins.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral structure.
