Astragalus argophyllus var. castaneiformis

Astragalus argophyllus var. castaneiformis, commonly known as the redstem milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, followed by small, bean-like pods that mature into brown, woody seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert habitats.

This species is valued for its role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
