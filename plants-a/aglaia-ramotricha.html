Aglaia ramotricha

Aglaia ramotricha, commonly known as the hairy aglaia, is a tropical plant belonging to the Meliaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, typically clustered in inflorescences, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

Aglaia ramotricha is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
