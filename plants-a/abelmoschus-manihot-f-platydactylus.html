Abelmoschus manihot f. platydactylus

Abelmoschus manihot f. platydactylus, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a variant of the okra plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and cylindrical, green pods that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its starchy roots, which are used as a food source. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a flowering structure that produces small yellow flowers.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and nutritional content, making it an important crop in certain agricultural systems.
