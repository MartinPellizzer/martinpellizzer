Aconitum × teppneri

Aconitum × teppneri, commonly known as the Teppner hybrid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that are typically arranged in racemose inflorescences. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed divisions, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Aconitum × teppneri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
