Amaranthus × ozanonii subsp. romanus

Amaranthus × ozanonii subsp.

romanus, commonly known as redroot amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive red stems, which become more pronounced as the plant matures.

The leaves are lanceolate, with a hairy texture, and the plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage and ability to thrive in arid conditions.

The species is also valued for its potential as a forage crop and for its use in traditional herbal medicine.
