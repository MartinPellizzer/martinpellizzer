Albizia elliptica

Albizia elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf silk tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features pinnately compound leaves with elliptical leaflets that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces fragrant, pale yellow to white flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in spring or early summer. Its fruit is a flat, dehiscent pod containing several elliptical seeds.

Albizia elliptica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
