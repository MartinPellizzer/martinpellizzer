Angraecum ornithorrhynchum

Angraecum ornithorrhynchum, commonly known as the bird-beak orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, slender, and often curved inflorescence, which bears numerous fragrant, white flowers with a distinctive bird-like shape. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high air humidity and good drainage for optimal growth. Its unique floral structure is adapted for pollination by specific nectar-feeding moths.

Angraecum ornithorrhynchum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
