Adesmia villosa

Adesmia villosa, commonly known as the hairy adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of white or pale yellow.

Adesmia villosa is also noted for its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name.
