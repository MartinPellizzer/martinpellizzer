Acacia mimula

Acacia mimula, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, woody stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by small, woody pods containing seeds. Acacia mimula is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, making it a hardy species in xerophytic ecosystems.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
