Adonis annua var. atrorubens

Adonis annua var. atrorubens, commonly known as the red-flowered autumn crocus, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, crimson or orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces distinctive, helmet-shaped seed pods. It is often found in dry meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

Botanically, it is characterized by its toxic alkaloids and is part of the Ranunculaceae family.
