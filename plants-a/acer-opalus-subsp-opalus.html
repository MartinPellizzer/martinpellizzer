Acer opalus subsp. opalus

Acer opalus subsp.

opalus, commonly known as the Caucasian maple, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are typically red or orange in the autumn.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged fruits called samaras. Its bark is smooth and light grey, becoming fissured with age.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its vibrant fall color.
