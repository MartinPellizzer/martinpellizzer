Agapanthus insignis

Agapanthus insignis, commonly known as the blue agapanthus, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features dense, spherical clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, surrounded by broad, strap-like leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes. Its foliage remains evergreen in mild climates, providing visual interest throughout the year.

Agapanthus insignis is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant due to its architectural form and vibrant floral display.
