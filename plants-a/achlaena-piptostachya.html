Achlaena piptostachya

Achlaena piptostachya, commonly known as the slender-leaved ironwood, is a woody shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is known for its hard, dense wood, which has been historically used for tool-making and construction. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Achlaena piptostachya is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining biodiversity in its native range.
