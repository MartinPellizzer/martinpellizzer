Abies harryana

Abies harryana, commonly known as the Harry's fir, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The cones of Abies harryana are typically small and cylindrical, maturing to a reddish-brown color. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found at high altitudes where it forms part of the alpine ecosystem.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable component of its native habitat.
