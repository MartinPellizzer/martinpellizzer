Acianthus caudatus

Acianthus caudatus, commonly known as the purple orchid or the cat's tail orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its unique, pendulous inflorescence that resembles a cat's tail. The plant features vibrant purple and pink flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a dramatic visual impact to its habitat. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to its arid environment.

Acianthus caudatus thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
