Anacyclus purpurascens

Anacyclus purpurascens, commonly known as the purple hawk's-bill, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, round, greenish-brown fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in harsh, dry environments.

Anacyclus purpurascens is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
