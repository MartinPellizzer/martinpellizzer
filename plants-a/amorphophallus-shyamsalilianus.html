Amorphophallus shyamsalilianus

Amorphophallus shyamsalilianus, commonly known as the Shyamsalilian arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of India.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a striking, spadix-like inflorescence that resembles a phallic shape, giving it its genus name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are pollinated by specific insect species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing along riverbanks or in dense undergrowth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
