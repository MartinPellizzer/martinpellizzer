Acacia lasiocalyx

Acacia lasiocalyx, commonly known as the roughbark acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its greyish-brown, fissured bark and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in cylindrical spikes, which appear during the spring and summer months. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are adapted for dispersal by animals.

Acacia lasiocalyx is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
