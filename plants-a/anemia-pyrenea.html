Anemia pyrenea

Anemia pyrenea, commonly known as the fireweed or chamaedorea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescence that consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually large, broad, and have a waxy texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to waterlogged soils.

Anemia pyrenea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
