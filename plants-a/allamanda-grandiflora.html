Allamanda grandiflora

Allamanda grandiflora, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, funnel-shaped, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that are opposite and ovate in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Allamanda grandiflora is also known for its fast growth rate and ability to climb or trail when supported.
