Aquilaria walla

Aquilaria walla, commonly known as the white agarwood, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Thymelaeaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant heartwood, which is highly valued in traditional medicine and perfumery. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests and requires a humid, warm climate with well-drained soil. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green.

The tree is also known for its slow growth and the unique scent that develops in its resinous wood over time.
