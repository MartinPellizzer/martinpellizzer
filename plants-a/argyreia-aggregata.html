Argyreia aggregata

Argyreia aggregata, commonly known as the "Cocaine plant" or "Crimson beauty," is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces small, round berries that turn red when mature, containing seeds enclosed in a fleshy pulp. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its association with the illicit drug cocaine, it is heavily regulated in many countries.
