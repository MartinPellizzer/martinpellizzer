Aponogeton dioecus

Aponogeton dioecus, commonly known as the African water clock, is a unique aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length, often floating on the water's surface. The plant is dioecious, meaning it has separate male and female plants, which is a rare trait among aquatic flora. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters near the water's surface.

Aponogeton dioecus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
