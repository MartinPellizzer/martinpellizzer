Andryala serotina

Andryala serotina, commonly known as late-flowering andryala, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear small, white to pale pink flowers clustered in terminal panicles. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy coating.

Andryala serotina is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
