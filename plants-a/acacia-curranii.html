Acacia curranii

Acacia curranii, commonly known as the Curran's wattle, is a shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves, which are typical features of the Acacia genus. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to the arid and semi-arid environments of its native habitat. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens in regions with a suitable climate.
