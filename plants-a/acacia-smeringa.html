Acacia smeringa

Acacia smeringa, commonly known as the white acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny branches and small, feathery leaves that are typically shed during dry periods. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Acacia smeringa thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates.

It is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
