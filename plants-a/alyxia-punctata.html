Alyxia punctata

Alyxia punctata, commonly known as the spotted alyxia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers with yellow stamens, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Alyxia punctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a habitat for pollinators.
