Asperula setulosa

Asperula setulosa, commonly known as the hairy woodruff, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Asperula setulosa is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
