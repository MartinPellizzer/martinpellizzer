Acacia podalyriifolia

Acacia podalyriifolia, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a small shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
