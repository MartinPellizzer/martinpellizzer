Adonis coerulea var. puberula

Adonis coerulea var. puberula, commonly known as the blue adonis or blue snowflake, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a pubescent appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early-season blooms.
