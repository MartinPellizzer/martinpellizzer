Acer cappadocicum subsp. cappadocicum

Acer cappadocicum subsp.

cappadocicum, commonly known as the Cappadocian maple, is a deciduous tree native to Turkey. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn.

The tree produces small, clustered green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is well adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
