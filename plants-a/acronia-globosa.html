Acronia globosa

Acronia globosa, commonly known as the globe acronia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in wetlands. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming in the spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Acronia globosa is often found in wetland habitats and is considered an indicator species for healthy, undisturbed wetland ecosystems.
