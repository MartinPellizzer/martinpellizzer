Acacia thailandica

Acacia thailandica, commonly known as Thai acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow floral spikes. The plant is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry or semi-arid regions. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, and its roots can fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local communities.
