Acosta paniculata

Acosta paniculata, commonly known as the yellow panicled orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in paniculate inflorescences. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid environments with well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Acosta paniculata is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
