Astragalus azureus

Astragalus azureus, commonly known as the blue milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Astragalus azureus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
