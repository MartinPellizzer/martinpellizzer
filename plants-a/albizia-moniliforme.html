Albizia moniliforme

Albizia moniliforme, commonly known as the bead tree, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces distinctive bead-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in dry forests and woodlands.

Albizia moniliforme is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
