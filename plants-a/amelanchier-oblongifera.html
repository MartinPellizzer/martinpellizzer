Amelanchier oblongifera

Amelanchier oblongifera, commonly known as the roundleaf serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that ripen in the summer, providing food for birds and wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and conservation efforts.
