Acacia hispidissima

Acacia hispidissima, commonly known as the rough acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its grey-green, feathery foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species.

Acacia hispidissima is often used in traditional medicine and for its ornamental value.
