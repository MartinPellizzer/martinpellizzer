Allium hierochuntinum

Allium hierochuntinum, commonly known as the Crimean leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Crimean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and umbel-shaped inflorescences. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a valuable crop in local cuisines.

Allium hierochuntinum is also appreciated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
