Aspidium forsteri

Aspidium forsteri, commonly known as the Forster's shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its intricate fronds with a distinctive, shield-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce numerous small, lance-shaped leaves.

Aspidium forsteri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
