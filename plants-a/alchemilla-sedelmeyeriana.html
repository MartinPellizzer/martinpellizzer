Alchemilla sedelmeyeriana

Alchemilla sedelmeyeriana, commonly known as the sedelmeyer's lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its common name reflects its historical use in folk medicine for its purported healing properties.
