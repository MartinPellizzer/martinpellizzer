Alkanna tinctoria subsp. tripolitana

Alkanna tinctoria subsp.

tripolitana, commonly known as the Tripoli alkanna, is a perennial shrub native to North Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its distinctive red-orange roots, which are rich in alkaloids and used in traditional medicine and dyeing.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in natural dye production.
