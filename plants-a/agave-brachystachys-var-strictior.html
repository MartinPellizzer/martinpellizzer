Agave brachystachys var. strictior

Agave brachystachys var. strictior, commonly known as the narrow-leaved agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are shorter and more narrow compared to other agave species. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence bearing small, white to pale yellow flowers, which appear after the plant has matured. It is adapted to survive in hot, dry climates with minimal water requirements.

Its dense foliage and ability to store water make it a hardy and drought-resistant plant.
