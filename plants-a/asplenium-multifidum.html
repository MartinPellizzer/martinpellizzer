Asplenium multifidum

Asplenium multifidum, commonly known as the common spleenwort, is a fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a small, terrestrial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has delicate, finely divided leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium multifidum is known for its adaptability and can be found in a variety of habitats across temperate regions.
