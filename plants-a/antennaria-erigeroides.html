Antennaria erigeroides

Antennaria erigeroides, commonly known as the hooked sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are often glandular, and it thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands, dunes, and disturbed areas.

Antennaria erigeroides is a member of the Asteraceae family, which is known for its composite flower heads.
