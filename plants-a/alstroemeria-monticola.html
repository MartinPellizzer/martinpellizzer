Alstroemeria monticola

Alstroemeria monticola, commonly known as the mountain alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces multiple stems that bear clusters of blooms.

Alstroemeria monticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant flowers and hardy nature.
