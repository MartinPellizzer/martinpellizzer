Austromyrtus prolixa

Austromyrtus prolixa, commonly known as the broad-leaved myrtle, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. It typically grows in coastal areas and is often found in sandy soils.

Austromyrtus prolixa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in indigenous cultures.
