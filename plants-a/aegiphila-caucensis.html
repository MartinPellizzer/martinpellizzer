Aegiphila caucensis

Aegiphila caucensis, commonly known as Caucasian aegiphila, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant typically grows as a small shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that are often glandular-dotted. Its flowers are clustered in whorls and are usually pink or purple, adding ornamental value.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
