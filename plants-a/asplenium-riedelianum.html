Asplenium riedelianum

Asplenium riedelianum, commonly known as the Riedel's spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant has finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It is often found on rock surfaces or in crevices, thriving in humid, temperate climates.

Asplenium riedelianum is relatively low maintenance and is popular in horticulture for its ornamental appeal.
