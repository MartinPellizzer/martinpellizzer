Anthericum zeyheri

Anthericum zeyheri, commonly known as Zeyher's ladies' tresses, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and long, slender inflorescences. The plant produces intricate, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in a spiral pattern. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
