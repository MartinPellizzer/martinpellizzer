Amethystanthus japonicus var. albiflorus

Amethystanthus japonicus var. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered Japanese amethyst anthus, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

This plant is native to Japan and is characterized by its unique, showy flowers that resemble small amethyst clusters. The flowers are predominantly white with a faint purple hue, adding a delicate contrast to the plant's foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

The plant's ornamental value lies in its elegant floral display and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
