Aldama dentata var. dentata

Aldama dentata var. dentata, commonly known as the toothed aldama, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
