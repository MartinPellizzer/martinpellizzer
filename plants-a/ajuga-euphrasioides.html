Ajuga euphrasioides

Ajuga euphrasioides, commonly known as carpet bugle or silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with heart-shaped, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in early to mid-summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream sides.

Ajuga euphrasioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
