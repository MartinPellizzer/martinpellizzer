Acer rubrum var. viride

Acer rubrum var. viride, commonly known as the green maple, is a variant of the red maple tree characterized by its distinctively green foliage throughout the growing season.

This variety exhibits broad, palmate leaves that are typically a vibrant green, setting it apart from the more common red or orange foliage of other Acer species. It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The tree produces small, red flowers in early spring followed by winged seeds in the fall.

Its unique coloration makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
