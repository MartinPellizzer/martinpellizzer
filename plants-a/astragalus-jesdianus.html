Astragalus jesdianus

Astragalus jesdianus, commonly known as the Jesdian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Astragalus jesdianus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
