Argyrolobium vaginiferum

Argyrolobium vaginiferum, commonly known as the silverlobe burr veldt, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grassland habitats, often in areas with well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately divided, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is valued for its resilience to drought and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
