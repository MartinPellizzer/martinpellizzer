Acalypha obovata

Acalypha obovata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces showy, papery bracts that often resemble flowers, though they are not true blossoms. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or near water sources.
