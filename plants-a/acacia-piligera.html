Acacia piligera

Acacia piligera, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and small, feathery leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that appear in globular clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are adapted to disperse in dry conditions.

Acacia piligera is well-suited to harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
