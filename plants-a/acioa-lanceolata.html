Acioa lanceolata

Acioa lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in racemes, which bloom during the warmer months. Acioa lanceolata is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
