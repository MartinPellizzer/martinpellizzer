Aloe horombensis subsp. ruffingiana

Aloe horombensis subsp.

ruffingiana, commonly known as the Ruffin's Aloe, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar. It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, adapted for water storage in arid environments.

The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with bright yellow flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in dry climates.
