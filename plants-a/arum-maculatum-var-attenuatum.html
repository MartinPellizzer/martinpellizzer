Arum maculatum var. attenuatum

Arum maculatum var. attenuatum, commonly known as the spotted arum or white arum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a distinctive, arrow-shaped leaf that is usually bright green with a lighter green or white midrib. The plant produces an umbel of small, white flowers that are often tinged with pink or purple, surrounded by a spadix. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
