Aegilops brachyathera

Aegilops brachyathera, commonly known as the short-awned goatgrass, is a wild grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with short awns, which are bristle-like structures at the base of the florets. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Aegilops brachyathera is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential genetic contributions to wheat improvement.
