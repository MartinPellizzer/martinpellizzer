Astragalus abbas-riazi

Astragalus abbas-riazi, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its deep taproot system, which allows it to thrive in arid and rocky environments. It is often found in high-altitude grasslands and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Astragalus abbas-riazi is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
