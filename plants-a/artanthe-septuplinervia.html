Artanthe septuplinervia

Artanthe septuplinervia, commonly known as the seven-veined artanthe, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a distinctive seven-veined structure, which gives the plant its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
