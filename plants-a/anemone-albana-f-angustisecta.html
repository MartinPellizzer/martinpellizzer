Anemone albana f. angustisecta

Anemone albana f. angustisecta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf white anemone, is a botanical variant characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and showy white flowers.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, particularly in the Alps. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch to woodland gardens.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its elegant appearance.
