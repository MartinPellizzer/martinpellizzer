Asparagus falcatus var. pubescens

Asparagus falcatus var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy asparagus, is a unique variant of the asparagus genus characterized by its dense pubescence.

This plant is native to South Africa and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that give way to red, berry-like fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh growing conditions.
