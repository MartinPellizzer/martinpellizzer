Astragalus missouriensis f. microphyllus

Astragalus missouriensis f. microphyllus, commonly known as the Missouri milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in prairie and woodland habitats. It has small, elliptical leaves and produces yellow or pale green flowers in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is notable for its compact growth habit and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
