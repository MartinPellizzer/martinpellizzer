Adesmia quadripinnata

Adesmia quadripinnata, commonly known as the four-pinnate burr legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with four pairs of pinnate divisions, giving it a finely divided appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by hairy, spiny pods. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Adesmia quadripinnata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
