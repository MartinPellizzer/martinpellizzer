Alseodaphnopsis rugosa

Alseodaphnopsis rugosa, commonly known as the rough-leaved daphne, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and bay trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.
