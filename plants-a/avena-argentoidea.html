Avena argentoidea

Avena argentoidea, commonly known as silver oat, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, white to pale blue flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
