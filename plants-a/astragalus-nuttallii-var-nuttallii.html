Astragalus nuttallii var. nuttallii

Astragalus nuttallii var. nuttallii, commonly known as Nuttall's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Nuttall's milkvetch plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
