Agelanthus igneus

Agelanthus igneus, commonly known as the fiery agelanthus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features striking, tubular leaves that are often tinged with red or orange hues, giving it its fiery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it drought-tolerant.

Agelanthus igneus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in low-maintenance garden settings.
