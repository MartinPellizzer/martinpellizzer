Allium caesium

Allium caesium, commonly known as purple onion, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features a bulbous base with long, narrow leaves and clusters of purple or pinkish flowers. The plant is characterized by its strong onion-like odor and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is related to other allium species such as garlic and leeks.

The flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
