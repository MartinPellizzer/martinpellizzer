Adelobotrys adscendens

Adelobotrys adscendens, commonly known as the climbing adenoglossum, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is a tropical vine characterized by its climbing habit and striking, showy inflorescences. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white flowers surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It is native to regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
