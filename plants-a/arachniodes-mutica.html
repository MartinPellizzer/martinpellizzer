Arachniodes mutica

Arachniodes mutica, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, leathery, and hairy fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its fronds are bi- or tri-pinnate, with a complex structure that enhances its ornamental value.

Arachniodes mutica is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
