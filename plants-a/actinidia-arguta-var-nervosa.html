Actinidia arguta var. nervosa

Actinidia arguta var. nervosa, commonly known as the hardy kiwi, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its finely veined leaves and small, edible berries that are often consumed fresh or used in jams. The plant thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained, fertile soil. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to produce fruit in cooler regions.

Its vigorous growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or espalier training.
