Asplenium × claphamii nothosubsp. lawalreei

Asplenium × claphamii nothosubsp.

lawalreei, commonly known as the Lawalree's shield fern, is a hybrid species within the Aspleniaceae family. It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage with leathery, lanceolate leaves that are often dark green and glossy.

The plant produces small, clustered spore cases on the underside of its fronds, typical of ferns. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid, showcasing genetic diversity within the genus Asplenium.
