Aeschynomene lagenaria

Aeschynomene lagenaria, commonly known as the bottle gourd, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its large, hollow, bottle-shaped fruit. The plant has compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in groups. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is widely used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its versatility and nutritional value.
