Actinodaphne chinensis

Actinodaphne chinensis, commonly known as Chinese camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and fragrant wood. The plant is known for its high camphor content, which gives it medicinal and industrial uses. It typically grows in humid, lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
