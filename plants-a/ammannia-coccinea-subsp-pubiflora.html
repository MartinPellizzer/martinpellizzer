Ammannia coccinea subsp. pubiflora

Ammannia coccinea subsp.

pubiflora, commonly known as the redroot ammannia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and reddish stems, which are particularly noticeable during the growing season.

The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface, depending on the subspecies.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and disturbed areas.
