Aegiphila vallensis

Aegiphila vallensis, commonly known as the valley aegiphila, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain seeds.

Aegiphila vallensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
