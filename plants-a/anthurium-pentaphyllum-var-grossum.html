Anthurium pentaphyllum var. grossum

Anthurium pentaphyllum var. grossum, commonly known as the giant anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, with a deep green color and a waxy texture. The plant produces vibrant red spadices, which are the showy inflorescences that house the small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium pentaphyllum var. grossum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
