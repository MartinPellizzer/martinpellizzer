Astragalus afghanopersicus

Astragalus afghanopersicus, commonly known as Afghan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has hairy stems and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus afghanopersicus is often found in rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
