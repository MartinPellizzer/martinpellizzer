Arabis parryoides

Arabis parryoides, commonly known as Parry's rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant features basal leaves that are rounded and slightly hairy, with white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments.

Arabis parryoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
