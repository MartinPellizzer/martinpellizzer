Alchemilla perspicua

Alchemilla perspicua, commonly known as the showy lady's mantle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Alchemilla perspicua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
