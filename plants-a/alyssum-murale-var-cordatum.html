Alyssum murale var. cordatum

Alyssum murale var. cordatum, commonly known as heartleaf alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are ovate to cordate in shape, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract beneficial insects.
