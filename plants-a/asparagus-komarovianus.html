Asparagus komarovianus

Asparagus komarovianus, commonly known as Komarov's asparagus, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features slender, fern-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its stems are often spiny, which serves as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
