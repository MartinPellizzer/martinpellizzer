Amyema latifolia

Amyema latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved amyema, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. Amyema latifolia produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months.

It is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
