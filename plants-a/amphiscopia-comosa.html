Amphiscopia comosa

Amphiscopia comosa, commonly known as the hairy sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically forms dense mats of succulent, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch to its rocky or sandy habitat. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to stabilize loose soils, making it a common pioneer species in disturbed areas.

Amphiscopia comosa is often found in dry streambeds, gravelly slopes, and other xeric environments where it thrives with minimal water.
