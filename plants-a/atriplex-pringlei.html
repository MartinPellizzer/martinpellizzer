Atriplex pringlei

Atriplex pringlei, commonly known as Pringle's saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its salt-tolerant nature allows it to thrive in soils with high salinity, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.

Atriplex pringlei plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
