Acalypha brasiliensis var. mollis

Acalypha brasiliensis var. mollis, commonly known as velvetleaf or silky hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that are often a deep green with a slight pubescence. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically red or purple, with five petals and a distinctive central column. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in disturbed or secondary forest areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
