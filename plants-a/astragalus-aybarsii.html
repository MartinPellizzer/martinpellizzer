Astragalus aybarsii

Astragalus aybarsii, commonly known as Aybars' milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid habitats, demonstrating a high degree of adaptability to harsh environments. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several small, hard seeds.

Astragalus aybarsii is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
