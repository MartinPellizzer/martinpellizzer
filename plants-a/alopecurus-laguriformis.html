Alopecurus laguriformis

Alopecurus laguriformis, commonly known as the creeping bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along streambanks.

Alopecurus laguriformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and turfgrass mixes.
