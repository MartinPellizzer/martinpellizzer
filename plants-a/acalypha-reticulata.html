Acalypha reticulata

Acalypha reticulata, commonly known as the nettleleaf ironplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves with a net-like venation pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers clustered in cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

Acalypha reticulata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
