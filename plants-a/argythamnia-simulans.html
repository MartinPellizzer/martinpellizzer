Argythamnia simulans

Argythamnia simulans, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a perennial herb with narrow, linear leaves that reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It has a deep root system that allows it to access groundwater, making it highly drought-tolerant.

Its fibrous stems and leaves have been historically used for textile production in traditional communities.
