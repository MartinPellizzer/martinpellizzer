Asyneuma multicaule

Asyneuma multicaule, commonly known as the many-stemmed false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous annual or perennial, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its root system is fibrous, and it often thrives in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
