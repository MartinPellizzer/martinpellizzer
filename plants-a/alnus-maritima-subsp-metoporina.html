Alnus maritima subsp. metoporina

Alnus maritima subsp.

metoporina, commonly known as the coastal alder, is a shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic coastal regions of North America. It is characterized by its small, simple leaves and catkin-like flowers, which are typical of the Betulaceae family.

The plant thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in dunes, salt marshes, and coastal woodlands. It plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
