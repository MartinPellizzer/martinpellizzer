Aristida orizabensis var. pseudospadicea

Aristida orizabensis var. pseudospadicea, commonly known as the Mexican dropseed, is a grass species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its fine, slender blades and compact growth habit, often forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, pale green to brownish inflorescences that are typically adorned with delicate, hair-like structures. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
