Ageratina enixa

Ageratina enixa, commonly known as the white mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, often giving it a mist-like appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ageratina enixa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
