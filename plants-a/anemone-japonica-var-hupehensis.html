Anemone japonica var. hupehensis

Anemone japonica var. hupehensis, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has a compact growth habit with basal leaves that are palmately lobed and hairy. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
