Ageratina fastigiata

Ageratina fastigiata, commonly known as the spiked ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with narrow, gray-green leaves and produces fluffy, white or blue-purple flower clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable to pollinators.
