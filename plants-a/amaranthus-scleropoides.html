Amaranthus scleropoides

Amaranthus scleropoides, commonly known as the rough amaranth, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. Its seeds are small and can be used as a grain or for ornamental purposes.

Amaranthus scleropoides is often cultivated for its resilience to poor soil conditions and its ability to thrive in dry environments.
