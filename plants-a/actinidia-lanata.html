Actinidia lanata

Actinidia lanata, commonly known as the downy actinidia, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, often used in culinary applications. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Actinidia lanata is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
