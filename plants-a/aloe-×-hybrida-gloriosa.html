Aloe × hybrida gloriosa

Aloe × hybrida gloriosa, commonly known as the Gloriosa Aloe, is a hybrid succulent plant that combines traits from various aloe species.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically fleshy and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces striking, spiky inflorescences with orange or red flowers, which bloom in the spring or early summer. It is native to southern Africa and thrives in well-draining soil with ample sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor settings.
