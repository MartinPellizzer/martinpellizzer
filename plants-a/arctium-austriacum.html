Arctium austriacum

Arctium austriacum, commonly known as the Austrian burdock, is a biennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces purple to pink flower heads that develop into spiny, bur-like fruits, which aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Arctium austriacum is valued for its medicinal properties, with its roots traditionally used in herbal remedies.
