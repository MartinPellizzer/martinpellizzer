Abutilon glechomifolium

Abutilon glechomifolium, commonly known as the heartleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring a woody stem and hairy foliage. The leaves are heart-shaped, with a velvety texture and a deep green color, often with a slight reddish tinge on the underside. It produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom in clusters during the summer months.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and vibrant floral display.
