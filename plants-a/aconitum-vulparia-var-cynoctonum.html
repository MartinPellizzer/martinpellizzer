Aconitum vulparia var. cynoctonum

Aconitum vulparia var. cynoctonum, commonly known as the European monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant has large, palmately divided leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Like other members of its genus, it contains potent alkaloids, making it highly toxic to both humans and animals.
