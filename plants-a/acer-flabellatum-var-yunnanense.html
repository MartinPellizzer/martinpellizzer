Acer flabellatum var. yunnanense

Acer flabellatum var. yunnanense, commonly known as the Yunnan maple, is a deciduous tree native to southwestern China.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and conservation efforts.
