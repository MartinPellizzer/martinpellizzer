Aspidium punctilobulum

Aspidium punctilobulum, commonly known as the spotted wood fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Aspidium punctilobulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
