Aeschynanthus dischidioides

Aeschynanthus dischidioides, commonly known as the coral vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences that attract pollinators such as birds and insects. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Aeschynanthus dischidioides thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
