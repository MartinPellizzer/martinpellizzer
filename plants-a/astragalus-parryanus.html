Astragalus parryanus

Astragalus parryanus, commonly known as the Parry's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.

Due to its limited range and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern in some regions.
