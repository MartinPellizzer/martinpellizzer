Artemisia mauiensis

Artemisia mauiensis, commonly known as Maui wormwood, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky areas and is adapted to the unique climate of Hawaii. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its restricted habitat, Artemisia mauiensis is considered a rare and endangered species.
