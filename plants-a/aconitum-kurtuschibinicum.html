Aconitum kurtuschibinicum

Aconitum kurtuschibinicum, commonly known as the Kurtushibin aconite, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply cut lobes, and are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Aconitum kurtuschibinicum is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Morphology

Aconitum kurtuschibinicum has large, compound leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside, arranged alternately along the stem.

The stem is upright, somewhat woody at the base, with a rough, bark that is dark brown to black in color. Its flowers are showy, blue to purple in color, with a spurred petal, and bloom in late summer, primarily pollinated by bees. The fruit is a follicle that splits open to release small, flat seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
