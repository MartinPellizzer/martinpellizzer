Aspalathus alopecurus

Aspalathus alopecurus, commonly known as the small-leaved tea plant, is a species native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is closely related to the commercially important rooibos and honeybush teas. Its leaves are used in the production of herbal infusions, valued for their mild flavor and potential health benefits.

Aspalathus alopecurus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
