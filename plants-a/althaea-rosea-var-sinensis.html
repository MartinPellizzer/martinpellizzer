Althaea rosea var. sinensis

Althaea rosea var. sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese marshmallow, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its hairy, broad leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other plants like cotton and hibiscus.

The fleshy, mucilaginous roots are used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.
