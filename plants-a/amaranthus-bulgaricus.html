Amaranthus bulgaricus

Amaranthus bulgaricus, commonly known as Bulgarian amaranth, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, feather-like leaves that range from green to red or purple. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, arranged in dense clusters. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional cuisine in some regions.

Amaranthus bulgaricus is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy choice for gardens.
