Adiantum serratifolium

Adiantum serratifolium, commonly known as the notched maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that have serrated edges, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce a dense carpet of foliage.

Adiantum serratifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
