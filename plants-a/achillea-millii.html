Achillea millii

Achillea millii, commonly known as yellow archangel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that are hairy and can grow up to 60 cm tall. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette and are lance-shaped with serrated edges.

Achillea millii is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
