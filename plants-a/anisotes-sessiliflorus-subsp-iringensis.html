Anisotes sessiliflorus subsp. iringensis

Anisotes sessiliflorus subsp.

iringensis, commonly known as the Iringa arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to eastern Africa. It is characterized by its broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and conservation efforts in its native range.
