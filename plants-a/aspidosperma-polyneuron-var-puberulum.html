Aspidosperma polyneuron var. puberulum

Aspidosperma polyneuron var. puberulum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Pitanga*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries containing a single seed. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a street tree.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional medicine.
