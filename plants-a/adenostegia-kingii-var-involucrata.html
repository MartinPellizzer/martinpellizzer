Adenostegia kingii var. involucrata

Adenostegia kingii var. involucrata, commonly known as the velvetleaf tamarisk, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant has small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along stream banks.

Its growth habit makes it an important component of local ecosystems, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
