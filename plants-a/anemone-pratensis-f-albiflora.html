Anemone pratensis f. albiflora

Anemone pratensis f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered meadow anemone, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, typically pink or white flowers. The plant features a basal rosette of finely divided, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white blooms in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized areas.
