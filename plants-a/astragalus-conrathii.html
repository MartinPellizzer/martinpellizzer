Astragalus conrathii

Astragalus conrathii, commonly known as Conrath's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems through nitrogen fixation.

Astragalus conrathii is also of interest in botanical research due to its unique adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
