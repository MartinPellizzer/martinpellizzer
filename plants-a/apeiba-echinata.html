Apeiba echinata

Apeiba echinata, commonly known as the golden apple tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy on the upper surface. The tree produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that give way to bright orange, fleshy fruits. These fruits are edible and contain numerous seeds enclosed in a juicy, aromatic pulp.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
