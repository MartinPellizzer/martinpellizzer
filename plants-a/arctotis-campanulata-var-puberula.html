Arctotis campanulata var. puberula

Arctotis campanulata var. puberula, commonly known as the Woolly Bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, hairy stems and produces showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers with dark centers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature contribute to its appeal in both garden and horticultural settings.
