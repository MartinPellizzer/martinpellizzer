Artemisia sacrorum subsp. iwayomogi

Artemisia sacrorum subsp.

iwayomogi, commonly known as Japanese wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to East Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage and small, yellowish flowers that appear in late summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves contain volatile oils, contributing to its strong medicinal and aromatic properties.

It is widely used in traditional herbal medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
