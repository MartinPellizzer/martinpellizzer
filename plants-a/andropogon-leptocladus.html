Andropogon leptocladus

Andropogon leptocladus, commonly known as slender bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant has narrow, bluish-green leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often pinkish or purplish in color. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Due to its ecological significance, it is often found in restoration projects aimed at wetland conservation.
