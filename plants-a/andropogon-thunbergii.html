Andropogon thunbergii

Andropogon thunbergii, commonly known as tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that turn reddish in autumn. This plant thrives in full sun and is often found in prairies, open woodlands, and disturbed sites. Its fibrous root system helps prevent soil erosion, making it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.

Andropogon thunbergii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
