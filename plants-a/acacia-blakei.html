Acacia blakei

Acacia blakei, commonly known as the Blake's wattle, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant produces yellow floral spikes during the warmer months, which are characteristic of the Acacia genus. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets, and it is well adapted to drought conditions.

Acacia blakei plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
