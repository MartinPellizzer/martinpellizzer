Andropogon trispicatus

Andropogon trispicatus, commonly known as the three-bristle grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to wet soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a tall, slender stem that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Andropogon trispicatus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
