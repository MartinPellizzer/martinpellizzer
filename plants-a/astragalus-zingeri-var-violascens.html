Astragalus zingeri var. violascens

Astragalus zingeri var. violascens, commonly known as the violet-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of violet to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has small, pinnately compound leaves and produces lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or steppe environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
