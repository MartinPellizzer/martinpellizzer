Acacia tolmerensis

Acacia tolmerensis, commonly known as the Tolmer's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly found in countries like Bolivia and Peru.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, which bloom during the dry season. Acacia tolmerensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its root system helps prevent soil erosion, contributing to its ecological importance in its native habitat.
