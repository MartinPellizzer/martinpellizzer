Abronia californica

Abronia californica, commonly known as the California sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of Southern California.

It is a low-growing annual herb that produces clusters of small, showy pink or purple flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild, wet winters and warm, dry summers. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, helping to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
