Astragalus papasianus

Astragalus papasianus, commonly known as the Armenian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small leaflets, and it produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus papasianus is often found in alpine meadows and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological and potential medicinal significance.
