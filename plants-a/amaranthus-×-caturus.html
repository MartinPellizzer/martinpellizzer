Amaranthus × caturus

Amaranthus × caturus, commonly known as lovegrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and produces small, greenish to reddish flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant is native to South America but has been naturalized in various parts of the world. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a range of environmental conditions.

Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
