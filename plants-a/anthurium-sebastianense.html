Anthurium sebastianense

Anthurium sebastianense, commonly known as the Sebastian's Anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, spadix-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It prefers warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and botanical collections.
