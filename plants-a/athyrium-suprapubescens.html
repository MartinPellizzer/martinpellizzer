Athyrium suprapubescens

Athyrium suprapubescens, commonly known as the hairy male fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, frond-like structures. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit, with fertile fronds that produce spores on the underside. Its leaves are typically divided into pinnae, which are covered in fine, hairy structures.

This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical forests.
