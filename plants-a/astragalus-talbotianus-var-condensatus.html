Astragalus talbotianus var. condensatus

Astragalus talbotianus var. condensatus, commonly known as the denseleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, compound leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitats.
