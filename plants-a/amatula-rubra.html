Amatula rubra

Amatula rubra, commonly known as the red amata, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red petals and robust, succulent leaves adapted to withstand dry conditions. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and produces showy inflorescences that bloom in the spring. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Amatula rubra is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging environments.
