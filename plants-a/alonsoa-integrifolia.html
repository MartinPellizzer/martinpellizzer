Alonsoa integrifolia

Alonsoa integrifolia, commonly known as the white star vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. The leaves are opposite, simple, and usually have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative vine.
