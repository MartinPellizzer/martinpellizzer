Alopecurus agrestis var. decumbens

Alopecurus agrestis var. decumbens, commonly known as rough meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and covered with short, stiff hairs.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.
