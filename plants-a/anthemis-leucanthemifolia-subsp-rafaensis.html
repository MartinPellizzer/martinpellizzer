Anthemis leucanthemifolia subsp. rafaensis

Anthemis leucanthemifolia subsp.

rafaensis, commonly known as the Rafta's fleur-de-lis, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow central disk.

The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showing a preference for well-drained soils. It produces small, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
