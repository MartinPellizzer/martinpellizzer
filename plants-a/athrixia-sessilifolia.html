Athrixia sessilifolia

Athrixia sessilifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, fleshy, green to reddish leaves that are sessile, meaning they are attached directly to the stem without a stalk. The plant produces tiny, white to pinkish flowers in dense clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover in arid landscapes.
