Ageria ovalis

Ageria ovalis, commonly known as the oval ageratum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white, purple, or pink, with narrow, oval-shaped leaves. Ageria ovalis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and long blooming period.
