Abies × insignis f. beissneriana

Abies × insignis f. beissneriana, commonly known as the Beissner's fir, is a hybrid coniferous tree belonging to the genus *Abies* in the family Pinaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are shorter and more densely packed than those of its parent species. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and release seeds in the autumn, contributing to its reproductive cycle. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant in suitable climates.
