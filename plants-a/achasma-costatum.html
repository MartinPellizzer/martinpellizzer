Achasma costatum

Achasma costatum, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, zigzag-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often clustered in spikes. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
