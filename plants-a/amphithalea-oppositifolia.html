Amphithalea oppositifolia

Amphithalea oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It is characterized by its opposite arrangement of small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its berries are usually red or orange, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Amphithalea oppositifolia is found in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in humid, shaded environments.
