Astragalus striatiflorus

Astragalus striatiflorus, commonly known as the strip-flowered milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.

Astragalus striatiflorus is also of interest in botanical research due to its unique floral structure and ecological significance.
