Attalea cryptanthera

Attalea cryptanthera, commonly known as the pink palm or pink-fruit palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 5 meters in length. The plant produces striking pink to red flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by fleshy, red fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in wetland and swampy environments, often forming dense stands in these habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated for both aesthetic and conservation purposes.
