Araucaria nemorosa

Araucaria nemorosa, commonly known as the silver pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, pyramidal crown. The tree has needle-like leaves that are typically a dull silver-green color, giving it its common name. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Araucaria nemorosa is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance.
