Aralia kentiifolia

Aralia kentiifolia, commonly known as the Chinese angelica tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often deeply lobed and arranged in a feather-like pattern. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers in umbel-like clusters, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aralia kentiifolia is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes used in traditional Chinese medicine.
