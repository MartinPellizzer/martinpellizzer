Acer pictum subsp. tenorei

Acer pictum subsp.

tenorei, commonly known as the Sicilian maple, is a deciduous tree native to southern Italy. It is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, which are typically green on top and silver-white underneath.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and parks.
