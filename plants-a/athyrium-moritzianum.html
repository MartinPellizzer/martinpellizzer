Athyrium moritzianum

Athyrium moritzianum, commonly known as the Moritz's lady fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often arching or drooping. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its rhizomes are creeping and can form dense colonies, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

The fronds are usually dark green, with a finely divided structure that enhances its visual appeal.
