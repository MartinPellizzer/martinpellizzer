Agapetes meiniana

Agapetes meiniana, commonly known as the silverleaf agapete, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that often displays a silvery or grayish hue due to a thick layer of fine, white hairs. The plant typically grows in cool, humid environments at high altitudes, often in cloud forests. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually white or pale pink, and are clustered in umbels.

Agapetes meiniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
