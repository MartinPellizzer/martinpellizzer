Adenophora verticillata f. dentata

Adenophora verticillata f. dentata, commonly known as the toothed bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it the "dentata" designation. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
