Alcantarea benzingii

Alcantarea benzingii, commonly known as the Benzing's waxplant, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, waxy leaves that form a rosette shape, often with a reddish or purple hue. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing numerous small, showy flowers in shades of pink or red. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Alcantarea benzingii is a popular ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
