Anodendron punctatum

Anodendron punctatum, commonly known as the spotted anodendron, is a shrub native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a distinctive spotted pattern on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid tropical environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
