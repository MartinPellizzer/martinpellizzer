Allium amethystinum

Allium amethystinum, commonly known as the purple onion, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple flowers and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant color. The plant produces small, bulbous structures that are similar to onions but are typically smaller and more delicate. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both gardens and botanical collections.
