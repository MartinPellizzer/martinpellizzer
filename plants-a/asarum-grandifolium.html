Asarum grandifolium

Asarum grandifolium, commonly known as the large-leaved wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and a strong, spicy aroma. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by dark purple berries. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Asarum grandifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
