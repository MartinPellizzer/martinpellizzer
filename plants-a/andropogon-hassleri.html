Andropogon hassleri

Andropogon hassleri, commonly known as bluestem grass, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and tufted inflorescences that produce small, spikelet-like flowers. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies, woodlands, and open fields. It plays an important role in ecological systems by providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil.

Andropogon hassleri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and restoration projects.
