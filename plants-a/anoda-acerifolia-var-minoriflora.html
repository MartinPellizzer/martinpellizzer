Anoda acerifolia var. minoriflora

Anoda acerifolia var. minoriflora, commonly known as the lesser-flowered velvetleaf, is a variant of the velvetleaf species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which resemble those of maple trees, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
