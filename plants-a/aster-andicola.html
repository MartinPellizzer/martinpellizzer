Aster andicola

Aster andicola, commonly known as the Andean aster, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its flowers are often blue or purple, and it produces small, seed-like fruits called achenes.

Aster andicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
