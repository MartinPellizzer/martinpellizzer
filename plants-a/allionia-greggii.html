Allionia greggii

Allionia greggii, commonly known as Gregg's allionia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and clusters of small, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Allionia greggii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
