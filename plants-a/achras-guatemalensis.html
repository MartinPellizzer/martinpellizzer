Achras guatemalensis

Achras guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan achiote, is a small, evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers followed by edible, red, fleshy fruits. Its fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
