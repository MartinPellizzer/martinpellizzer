Alchemilla vulgaris f. versipila

Alchemilla vulgaris f. versipila, commonly known as the silverleaf lady’s mantle, is a variant of the common lady’s mantle.

It is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features broad, shield-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in loose inflorescences.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited for rock gardens or as a ground cover.
