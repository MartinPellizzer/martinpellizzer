Anthemis semperflorens

Anthemis semperflorens, commonly known as the everlasting flower or everlastings, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its historical use in herbal medicine is also noted for its mild antiseptic and calming properties.
