Anemone quinquefolia

Anemone quinquefolia, commonly known as the Pennsylvania poppy, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features a basal rosette of palmately compound leaves with five leaflets, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in woodland habitats and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Anemone quinquefolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
