Aegilops cylindrica var. gobustanica

Aegilops cylindrica var. gobustanica, commonly known as the goblet wheat, is a wild grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, showing adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. Its grains are small and cylindrical, with a distinctive hollow stem that gives it its common name.

This species is of interest to researchers due to its potential genetic contributions to wheat improvement.
