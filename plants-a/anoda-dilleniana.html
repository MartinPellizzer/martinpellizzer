Anoda dilleniana

Anoda dilleniana, commonly known as the Mexican blueweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pea-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Anoda dilleniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
