Andropogon glaucophyllus

Andropogon glaucophyllus, commonly known as blueleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland areas and along the edges of ponds and marshes. The plant features narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered with a powdery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like clusters of flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
