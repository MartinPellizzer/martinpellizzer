Alchemilla alpina var. podophylla

Alchemilla alpina var. podophylla, commonly known as the alpine lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal, hairy, and often silver-gray leaves that are adapted to cold and harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and ornamental plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine displays.
