Allium chitralicum

Allium chitralicum, commonly known as the Chitral garlic, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of northern Pakistan and parts of Afghanistan. The plant produces large, bulbous structures that are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisine. Its leaves are long and grass-like, and it blooms with clusters of white or pale pink flowers.

The botanical features of Allium chitralicum make it an important species in alpine ecosystems.
