Acalypha stuhlmannii

Acalypha stuhlmannii, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silverleaf.

It is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, three-lobed seed capsules. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Acalypha stuhlmannii is also known for its adaptability and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
