Acalypha obovata var. cuneata

Acalypha obovata var. cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spurge-plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, particularly India and Southeast Asia. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
