Avena antarctica

Avena antarctica, commonly known as Antarctic oatgrass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the cold climates of the Southern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny flowers. The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, including Antarctica, South America, and southern Africa, where it thrives in rocky soils and extreme weather conditions. Its ability to survive in nutrient-poor soils and withstand freezing temperatures makes it an important component of polar and alpine ecosystems.

Avena antarctica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for microorganisms in its native regions.
