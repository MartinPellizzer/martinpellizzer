Amblyanthus melanostictus

Amblyanthus melanostictus, commonly known as the black-streaked or black-spotted hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with dark green veins and its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The flowers bloom in summer and are pollinated primarily by insects.
