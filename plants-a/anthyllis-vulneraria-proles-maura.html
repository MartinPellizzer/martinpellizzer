Anthyllis vulneraria proles maura

Anthyllis vulneraria proles maura, commonly known as the Maura broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and limestone habitats. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
