Abies concolor f. violacea

Abies concolor f. violacea, commonly known as the violet-silver fir, is a rare alpine plant characterized by its distinctive foliage.

Its leaves are a striking combination of silver-white and deep violet, creating a unique visual effect. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist conditions. It is a variant of the white fir, exhibiting genetic variations that result in its unique coloration.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
