Acoridium simulacrum

Acoridium simulacrum, commonly known as the false arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acoraceae and is characterized by its arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy areas and has a rhizomatous root system. Its flowers are small and greenish, often clustered in spadices, and it produces berries that contain the seeds.

Acoridium simulacrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
