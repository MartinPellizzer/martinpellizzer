Andropogon eremicus

Andropogon eremicus, commonly known as the desert grass or sand dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, narrow leaves that are adapted to conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces fluffy, silver-gray seed heads that are both ornamental and wind-dispersed, aiding in its propagation. Andropogon eremicus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or dry landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and xeriscaping.
