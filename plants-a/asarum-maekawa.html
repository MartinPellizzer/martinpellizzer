Asarum maekawa

Asarum maekawa, commonly known as Japanese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves and unique, hooded flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine, though the plant contains toxic compounds that require careful handling.

Asarum maekawa plays a role in local ecosystems and is of interest to botanists and herbalists alike.
