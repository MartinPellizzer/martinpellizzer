Ageratina alexanderi

Ageratina alexanderi, commonly known as Alexander's ageratum, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in dense, fluffy clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in coastal or lowland habitats.

Ageratina alexanderi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
