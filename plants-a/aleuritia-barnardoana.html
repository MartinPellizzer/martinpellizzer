Aleuritia barnardoana

Aleuritia barnardoana, commonly known as the mountain elder, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its leaves are oval-shaped, with a glossy surface and a slightly serrated edge.

Aleuritia barnardoana is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to cooler, humid climates.
