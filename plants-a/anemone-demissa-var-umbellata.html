Anemone demissa var. umbellata

Anemone demissa var. umbellata, commonly known as the white wood anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to woodland areas of eastern North America.

It features delicate, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has a short, rhizomatous root system and produces basal leaves that are heart-shaped with a hairy texture. Its flowers are typically arranged in an umbel, giving it its specific epithet "umbellata.".

It thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
