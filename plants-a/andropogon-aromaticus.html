Andropogon aromaticus

Andropogon aromaticus, commonly known as sweetgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green blades and a sweet, aromatic scent when crushed. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lipped flowers.

Sweetgrass is often used in traditional cultural practices and for its ornamental and aromatic properties.
