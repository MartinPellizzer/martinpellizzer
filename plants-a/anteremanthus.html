Anteremanthus

Anteremanthus, commonly known as the African star of Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in shrub form, with woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Anteremanthus blooms during the summer months, producing clusters of flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
