Arundinaria lima

Arundinaria lima, commonly known as the Lima bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically green and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Arundinaria lima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its use in basketry and construction.
