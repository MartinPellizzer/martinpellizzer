Acacia arabica var. tomentosa

Acacia arabica var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly acacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white pubescent foliage, which gives it a woolly appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular inflorescences, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. Its root system is deep and drought-resistant, allowing it to thrive in poor soils.

It is also valued for its hard, durable wood and has traditional uses in various cultures.
