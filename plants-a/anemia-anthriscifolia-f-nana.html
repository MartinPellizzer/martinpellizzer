Anemia anthriscifolia f. nana

Anemia anthriscifolia f. nana, commonly known as the dwarf parsley.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae). The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbels, which are characteristic of the Apiaceae family.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
