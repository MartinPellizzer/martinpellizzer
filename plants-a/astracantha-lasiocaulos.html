Astracantha lasiocaulos

Astracantha lasiocaulos, commonly known as the woolly aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features hairy, gray-green foliage that gives it a distinctive, woolly appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
