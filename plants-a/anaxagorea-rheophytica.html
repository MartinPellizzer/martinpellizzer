Anaxagorea rheophytica

Anaxagorea rheophytica, commonly known as the streamside evergreen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elongated and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in dense forest undergrowth.

Anaxagorea rheophytica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
