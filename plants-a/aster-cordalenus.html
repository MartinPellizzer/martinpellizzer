Aster cordalenus

Aster cordalenus, commonly known as the hooked aster, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands, and it has hairy, branching stems with opposite leaves. Its scientific name reflects its distinctive, hooked fruiting structures, which aid in seed dispersal.

Aster cordalenus is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a valuable plant for pollinators due to its nectar-rich flowers.
