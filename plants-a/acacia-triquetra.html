Acacia triquetra

Acacia triquetra, commonly known as the three-lobed wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a triangular shape. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. Acacia triquetra is drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or loamy soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
