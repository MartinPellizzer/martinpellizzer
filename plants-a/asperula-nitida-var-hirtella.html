Asperula nitida var. hirtella

Asperula nitida var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy woodbine, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, dark green, leathery leaves that are densely covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped white or pale pink flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Its compact size and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
