Aglaia multijuga

Aglaia multijuga, commonly known as the golden apple tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The tree produces yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, spiny fruits containing bright red, fleshy arils. Its seeds are encased in a hard, capsule-like structure, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Aglaia multijuga is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
