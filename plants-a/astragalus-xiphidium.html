Astragalus xiphidium

Astragalus xiphidium, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and supports local wildlife.
