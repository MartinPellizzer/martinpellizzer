Astragalus confertiflorus

Astragalus confertiflorus, commonly known as the denseflower milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of western North America.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. The plant features compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces clusters of yellow to orange flowers. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access moisture from lower soil layers.

Astragalus confertiflorus is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
