Artemisia indica var. orientalis

Artemisia indica var. orientalis, commonly known as mugwort, is a perennial herb native to Asia and parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thujone and camphor, which contribute to its distinctive aroma and various therapeutic uses.
