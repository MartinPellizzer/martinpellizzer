Asarum heterotropoides var. mandshuricum

Asarum heterotropoides var. mandshuricum, commonly known as Chinese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in dense, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers that are clustered in a spathaceous inflorescence. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.
