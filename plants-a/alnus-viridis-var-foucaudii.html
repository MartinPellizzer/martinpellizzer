Alnus viridis var. foucaudii

Alnus viridis var. foucaudii, commonly known as the Foucaud's alder, is a variant of the green alder native to arid regions of North America.

It is a small, deciduous tree or shrub characterized by its finely toothed leaves and catkin-like flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry streambeds or rocky areas. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

This species is adapted to harsh environments, making it an important component of desert and semi-desert ecosystems.
