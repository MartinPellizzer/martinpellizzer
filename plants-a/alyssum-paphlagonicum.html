Alyssum paphlagonicum

Alyssum paphlagonicum, commonly known as the Armenian candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers, which bloom in early summer. The plant has finely divided, silver-gray leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Alyssum paphlagonicum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
