Aconitum jucundum var. chloranthum

Aconitum jucundum var. chloranthum, commonly known as the yellow aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, contrasting with its deep green foliage. The plant has a tall, erect stem with sparsely arranged leaves that are typically palmately lobed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and kept away from children and pets.
