Anemone decapetala var. biflora

Anemone decapetala var. biflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in clusters of two, giving it its common name. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are basal and often covered in a dense layer of silky hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral morphology and its role in pollinator ecosystems.
