Acaena × dieckii

Acaena × dieckii, commonly known as the New Zealand spinach, is a hybrid plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its hairy, prickly stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces edible, nutrient-rich seeds that are often consumed by both humans and wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.
