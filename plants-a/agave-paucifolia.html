Agave paucifolia

Agave paucifolia, commonly known as the lace agave, is a species of flowering plant in the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of Mexico, where it thrives in rocky soils and full sun. The plant is characterized by its narrow, silver-blue leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a striking appearance. It produces a tall flower stalk that bears white or pale purple flowers, which bloom in the summer months.

Agave paucifolia is drought-tolerant and requires minimal care, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
