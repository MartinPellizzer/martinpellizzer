Angelica villosa

Angelica villosa, commonly known as the hairy angelica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features tall, hollow stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its large, compound leaves are deeply lobed and often have a silvery underside.

Angelica villosa produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in umbrella-like clusters, which bloom in late summer.
