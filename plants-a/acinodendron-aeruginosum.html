Acinodendron aeruginosum

Acinodendron aeruginosum, commonly known as the blue acacia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it ecologically significant in its native habitat.
