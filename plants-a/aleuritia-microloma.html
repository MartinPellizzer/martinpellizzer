Aleuritia microloma

Aleuritia microloma, commonly known as the small-leaved elder, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Aleuritia microloma is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
