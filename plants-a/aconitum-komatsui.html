Aconitum komatsui

Aconitum komatsui, commonly known as Japanese aconite, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, typically arranged in a spicate inflorescence. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed foliage that is often covered in fine hairs.

Aconitum komatsui is known for its toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires caution when handling.
