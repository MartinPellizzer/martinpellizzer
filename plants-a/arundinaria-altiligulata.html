Arundinaria altiligulata

Arundinaria altiligulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and robust rhizome system, which allows it to spread vegetatively. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its structure resembles bamboo, with hollow, segmented stalks that are often used in traditional crafts.

Arundinaria altiligulata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
