Abutilon peyritschii

Abutilon peyritschii, commonly known as the Peyritsch's velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Abutilon peyritschii is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
