Acacia xanthophloea

Acacia xanthophloea, commonly known as the yellow-wood tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

The plant also plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
