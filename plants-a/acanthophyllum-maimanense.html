Acanthophyllum maimanense

Acanthophyllum maimanense, commonly known as the desert honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is well adapted to dry environments, with deep root systems that help it survive in sandy soils. It plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife.

Due to its resilience, it is often used in xeriscaping and habitat restoration projects.
