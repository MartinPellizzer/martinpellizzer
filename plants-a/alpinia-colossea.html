Alpinia colossea

Alpinia colossea, commonly known as the pink ginger or fragrant ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and striking pinkish-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers, and is valued for its ornamental appeal. Alpinia colossea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens.

Its unique fragrance and vibrant floral display contribute to its use in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
