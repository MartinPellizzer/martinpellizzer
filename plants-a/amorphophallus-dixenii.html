Amorphophallus dixenii

Amorphophallus dixenii, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces a unique inflorescence that resembles a phallic shape, hence its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

Botanically, it belongs to the Araceae family and is known for its ability to store energy in its underground corm, which allows it to survive periods of drought.
