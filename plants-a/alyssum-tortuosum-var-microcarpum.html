Alyssum tortuosum var. microcarpum

Alyssum tortuosum var. microcarpum, commonly known as the dwarf alpine alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compact, mat-forming growth with small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves and produces tiny, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude conditions.
