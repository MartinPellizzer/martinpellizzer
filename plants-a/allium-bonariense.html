Allium bonariense

Allium bonariense, commonly known as the Buenos Aires garlic, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous structure and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild garlic scent.

Allium bonariense prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens and borders.
