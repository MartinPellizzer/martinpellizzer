Acacia mikanii

Acacia mikanii, commonly known as the Japanese acacia, is a flowering plant native to Japan and parts of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in compact clusters, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods. Acacia mikanii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing properties, which can improve soil fertility.
