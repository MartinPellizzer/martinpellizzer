Amaracarpus vaccinioides

Amaracarpus vaccinioides, commonly known as the red-flowered heath, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, showy red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its unique floral display.
