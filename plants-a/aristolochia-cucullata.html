Aristolochia cucullata

Aristolochia cucullata, commonly known as the hooded Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hood-shaped flowers that resemble a pipe, with a deep purple to brown coloration. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The genus Aristolochia is known for its complex chemical compounds, some of which have medicinal uses but can also be toxic.
