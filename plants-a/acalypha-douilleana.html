Acalypha douilleana

Acalypha douilleana, commonly known as the silky hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with a contrasting central disk. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Acalypha douilleana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
