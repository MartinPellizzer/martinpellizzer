Amygdalus browiczii

Amygdalus browiczii, commonly known as the Browicz almond, is a rare and lesser-known species within the Prunus genus.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in parts of Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by the development of almond-like fruits. Its botanical characteristics include compound leaves and a woody structure typical of many Prunus species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
