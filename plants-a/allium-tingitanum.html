Allium tingitanum

Allium tingitanum, commonly known as the Moroccan garlic or Tingitana onion, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear umbels of white to pale purple flowers. Its bulbs are small and have a mild garlic-like flavor, making them suitable for culinary use.

Allium tingitanum is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience in challenging conditions.
