Allium cyaneum var. macrostemon

Allium cyaneum var. macrostemon, commonly known as the large-stemmed purple onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, hollow stems that bear clusters of violet to purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its robust, swollen bulbs that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking floral display and aromatic foliage.
