Achyronia opaca

Achyronia opaca, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is notable for containing compounds used in traditional medicine.

Achyronia opaca thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.
