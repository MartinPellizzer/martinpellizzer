Arceuthobium yecorense

Arceuthobium yecorense, commonly known as the Yecora witchweed, is a parasitic flowering plant belonging to the Orobanchaceae family.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host plant for nutrients, typically attaching itself to the roots of grasses or other herbaceous plants. The plant is characterized by its small, inconspicuous flowers and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Due to its parasitic nature, it can cause significant damage to agricultural crops.

Its life cycle is closely tied to the growth and health of its host, making it a challenging weed to control in farmlands.
