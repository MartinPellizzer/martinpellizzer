Acacia leprosa var. graveolens

Acacia leprosa var. graveolens, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
