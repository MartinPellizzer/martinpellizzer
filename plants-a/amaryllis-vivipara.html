Amaryllis vivipara

Amaryllis vivipara, commonly known as the dwarf amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces clusters of bright red, pink, or white flowers during the summer months. The plant has strap-like leaves that are dark green and often have a reddish or purple tint along the edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Amaryllis vivipara is popular as a garden ornamental due to its compact size and showy blooms.
