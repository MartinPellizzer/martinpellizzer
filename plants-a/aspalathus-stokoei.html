Aspalathus stokoei

Aspalathus stokoei, commonly known as Stokoe's bushveld clover, is a small, low-growing plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
