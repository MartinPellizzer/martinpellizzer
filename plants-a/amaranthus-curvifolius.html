Amaranthus curvifolius

Amaranthus curvifolius, commonly known as curled leaf amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its distinctive, curling leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that eventually develop into clusters of dry, three-angled seeds. It is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Amaranthus curvifolius is also valued for its resilience to drought and poor soil, making it a hardy choice for gardeners.
