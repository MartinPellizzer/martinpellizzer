Arabis ovata

Arabis ovata, commonly known as the hoary whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features basal leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring.

Arabis ovata is often found in dry, open habitats such as roadsides and gravelly soils.
