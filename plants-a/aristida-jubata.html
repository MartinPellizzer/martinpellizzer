Aristida jubata

Aristida jubata, commonly known as the tufted dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are adorned with delicate, bristly flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a valuable addition to ecological and landscape designs.
