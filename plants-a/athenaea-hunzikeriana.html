Athenaea hunzikeriana

Athenaea hunzikeriana, commonly known as the Hunziker's athenaea, is a rare and endangered plant species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine regions. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help to reduce water loss in its harsh habitat. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white in color and bloom during the short growing season.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
