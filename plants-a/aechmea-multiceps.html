Aechmea multiceps

Aechmea multiceps, commonly known as the pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients from the environment. The plant's foliage is typically dark green with a metallic sheen, and it produces vibrant orange or red flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. Aechmea multiceps thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor ornamental gardening.

Morphology

Aechmea multiceps has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, stiff, and sword-like, arranged in a tight spiral around the central stem.

The leaves are typically dark green with a waxy surface and can grow up to 40 cm in length. The plant has a short, thick, and often reddish stem that is covered in a fibrous, spongy bark. Its flowers are borne in a terminal inflorescence, consisting of violet to purple bracts with small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom during the dry season and are pollinated by insects.

The fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by birds and animals.
