Amphiscopia beyrichii

Amphiscopia beyrichii, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in various regions with a suitable climate.
