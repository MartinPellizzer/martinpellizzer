Aspidosperma macrophyllum subsp. morii

Aspidosperma macrophyllum subsp.

morii, commonly known as the Morii's large-leaf aspidosperma, is a member of the Apocynaceae family. It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

The plant is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange. It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad crown.

The bark is thick and fissured, and the wood is valued for its durability and resistance to decay.
