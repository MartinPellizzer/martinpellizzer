Achyrocline polycephala

Achyrocline polycephala, commonly known as the clustered achyrocline, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical sunflower-like structure. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and saline environments. Its small, white to pale blue flowers bloom in clusters, and the plant is often found in dune habitats.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing coastal dunes.
