Albizia versicolor

Albizia versicolor, commonly known as the rainbow acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are often bright green with a slight reddish or purple tinge. The plant produces small, fragrant, yellow or white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Albizia versicolor is a deciduous shrub or small tree that thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
