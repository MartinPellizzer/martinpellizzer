Aegopogon rigidisetus

Aegopogon rigidisetus, commonly known as the rigidset foxtail, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family, Poaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescence composed of tightly arranged spikelets. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and rigid, with a hairy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
