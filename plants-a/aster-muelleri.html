Aster muelleri

Aster muelleri, commonly known as Mueller's aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its foliage is generally basal, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves, and it produces vibrant blue to purple flowers.

Aster muelleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
