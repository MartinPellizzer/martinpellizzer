Acosta lactiflora

Acosta lactiflora, commonly known as the milkflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a hairy texture.

Acosta lactiflora is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun exposure.
