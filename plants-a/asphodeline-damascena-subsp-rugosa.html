Asphodeline damascena subsp. rugosa

Asphodeline damascena subsp.

rugosa, commonly known as the rugose asphodel, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features strap-like, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

The plant produces tall spikes of white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its architectural form and long-lasting blooms.
