Anthurium pedatifidum

Anthurium pedatifidum, commonly known as the laceleaf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive, almost lace-like appearance. The plant produces striking spadices, which are modified leaves that house the small, inconspicuous flowers. Anthurium pedatifidum thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
