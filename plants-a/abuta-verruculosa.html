Abuta verruculosa

Abuta verruculosa, commonly known as the rough abuta, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and gourds, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Abuta verruculosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
