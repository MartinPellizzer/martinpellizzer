Aegilops longissima var. major

Aegilops longissima var. major, commonly known as the longawn goatgrass, is a wild grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its long, slender awns that can reach several centimeters in length, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged along the stem.

The species is of interest in agricultural research due to its potential genetic contributions to wheat improvement.
