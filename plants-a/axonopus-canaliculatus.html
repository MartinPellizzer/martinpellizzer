Axonopus canaliculatus

Axonopus canaliculatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf axonopus, is a low-growing, perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 2 cm wide, with a smooth, often bluish-green appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lawns, gardens, and disturbed areas.

Its rapid growth and dense foliage make it both a desirable ornamental plant and a potential weed in certain environments.
