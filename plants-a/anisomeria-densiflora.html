Anisomeria densiflora

Anisomeria densiflora, commonly known as desert honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, characterized by its dense, glandular leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.

The plant's dense foliage and flowering structure are key features of its botanical identity.
