Aristida funiculata

Aristida funiculata, commonly known as the slender dropseed, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, delicate spikelets that are arranged in panicles, giving it a delicate, airy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as prairies and rocky slopes.

Aristida funiculata is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
