Aleuritia helodoxa

Aleuritia helodoxa, commonly known as the red-flowered honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces fragrant, red flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. Its berries are small, dark red, and edible, often consumed by birds.

Aleuritia helodoxa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
