Atractylis carduus

Atractylis carduus, commonly known as common centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to reddish in color.

Atractylis carduus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
