Avena lachnantha

Avena lachnantha, commonly known as slender oat, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Botanically, it is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
