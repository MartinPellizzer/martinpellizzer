Acacia ataxiphylla

Acacia ataxiphylla, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a small, shrub-like tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, zigzagging branches and small, phyllodes that are typically green and narrow. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical spikes, which appear during the warmer months. Acacia ataxiphylla is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
