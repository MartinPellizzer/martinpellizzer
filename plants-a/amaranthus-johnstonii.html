Amaranthus johnstonii

Amaranthus johnstonii, commonly known as Johnston's amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the species. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Amaranthus johnstonii is an annual plant that is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
