Anthoshorea

Anthoshorea, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, velvety petals with distinctive red veins. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often displaying a combination of pink, white, and red hues.

Anthoshorea is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its beauty in botanical gardens and private collections.
