Acaena pinnatifida var. aculeata

Acaena pinnatifida var. aculeata, commonly known as the hooked acaena, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, spiny growth and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the development of hard, berry-like fruits. Its prickly structure helps in seed dispersal by attaching to the fur of animals.

This species is often found in coastal and forested areas, contributing to the local ecosystem's biodiversity.
