Aconitum arunii

Aconitum arunii, commonly known as the Himalayan monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple spurred flowers. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, and it typically grows in moist, shady environments. Aconitum arunii contains potent alkaloids, making it highly toxic and requiring careful handling.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
