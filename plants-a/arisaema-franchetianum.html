Arisaema franchetianum

Arisaema franchetianum, commonly known as the Chinese cobra plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to China.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, hooded spathe that resembles a cobra's head. The plant produces a spadix covered in small, yellowish flowers, which are surrounded by a leaf-like bract. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
