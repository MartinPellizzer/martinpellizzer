Agastache pallidiflora subsp. neomexicana

Agastache pallidiflora subsp.

neomexicana, commonly known as New Mexico blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States. It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and gray-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of pale blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
