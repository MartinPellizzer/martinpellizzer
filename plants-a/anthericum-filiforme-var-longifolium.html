Anthericum filiforme var. longifolium

Anthericum filiforme var. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that bear alternate, lanceolate leaves with a hairy underside. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences, which are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported soothing properties.
