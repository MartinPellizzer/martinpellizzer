Argyrorchis

Argyrorchis, commonly known as the silver orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are characterized by their striking, often metallic-sheened leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. Argyrorchis species are typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in shaded, moist environments. The flowers usually have a complex structure with three sepals and three petals, with the latter often modified into a lip.

Botanically, the genus is noted for its unique adaptations to specific ecological niches and its ornamental value in horticulture.
