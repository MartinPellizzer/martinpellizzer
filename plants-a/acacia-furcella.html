Acacia furcella

Acacia furcella, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Acacia furcella is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in poor soils.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
