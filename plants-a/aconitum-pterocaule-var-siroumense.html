Aconitum pterocaule var. siroumense

Aconitum pterocaule var. siroumense, commonly known as the Siroumense Monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant features palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and studied for its alkaloid content.
