Alpinia gigantifolia

Alpinia gigantifolia, commonly known as the giant ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences and are typically white or pale pink in color.

Alpinia gigantifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
