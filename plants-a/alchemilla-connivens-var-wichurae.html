Alchemilla connivens var. wichurae

Alchemilla connivens var. wichurae, commonly known as the rock alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and alpine regions.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, shield-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery-white down. Its flowers are inconspicuous, appearing as greenish-yellow clusters in early summer.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in rock gardens for its ornamental value and hardiness.
