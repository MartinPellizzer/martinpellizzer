Aster rengifoanus

Aster rengifoanus, commonly known as the Rengifo's aster, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its flowers bloom in the summer, producing vibrant purple or blue hues that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
