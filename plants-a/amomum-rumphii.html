Amomum rumphii

Amomum rumphii, commonly known as wild cardamom, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, finger-like rhizomes that are rich in essential oils. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark red berries containing aromatic seeds. Its rhizomes are widely used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.

Amomum rumphii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its economic and culinary value.
