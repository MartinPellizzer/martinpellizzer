Amyris novaehollandiae

Amyris novaehollandiae, commonly known as the Australian sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, aromatic, and fragrant leaves and bark, which emit a distinctive woody scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, cone-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Due to its unique fragrance and limited natural range, it is of interest in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
