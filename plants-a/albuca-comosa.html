Albuca comosa

Albuca comosa, commonly known as the woolly albuca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Albuca comosa is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
