Acacia × grayana

Acacia × grayana, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Acacia dealbata and Acacia longifolia.

It is native to southern Australia and is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and yellow globular flowerheads. The plant is a shrub or small tree, typically growing up to 5 meters in height. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for coastal and semi-arid environments.

The botanical features of Acacia × grayana make it an ornamental plant valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
