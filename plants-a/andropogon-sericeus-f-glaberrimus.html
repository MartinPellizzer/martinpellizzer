Andropogon sericeus f. glaberrimus

Andropogon sericeus f. glaberrimus, commonly known as the silky grass or silky bluestem, is a variant of the silky grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It is a perennial grass that produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in native plant landscapes.
