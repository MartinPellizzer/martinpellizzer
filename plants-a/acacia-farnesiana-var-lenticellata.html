Acacia farnesiana var. lenticellata

Acacia farnesiana var. lenticellata, commonly known as the weeping acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and small, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its bark is smooth and often has a flaky, peeling texture, and it is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique form.
