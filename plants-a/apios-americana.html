Apios americana

Apios americana, commonly known as the American groundnut, is a perennial legume native to North America.

It produces distinctive, tuberous roots that are edible and have a nutty flavor. The plant features trifoliate leaves and clusters of white to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Apios americana is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
