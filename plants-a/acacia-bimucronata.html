Acacia bimucronata

Acacia bimucronata, commonly known as the broad-leaved wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family Fabaceae and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance. It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its seeds are often used in revegetation projects due to their hardiness and rapid growth.
