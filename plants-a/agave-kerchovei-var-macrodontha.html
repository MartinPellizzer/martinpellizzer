Agave kerchovei var. macrodontha

Agave kerchovei var. macrodontha, commonly known as the Mexican giant agave, is a striking succulent native to Mexico.

It features large, thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color with white marginal teeth. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow-green flowers, which bloom once in its lifetime. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
