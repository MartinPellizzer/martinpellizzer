Anemone raddeana subsp. villosa

Anemone raddeana subsp.

villosa, commonly known as the hairy anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal leaves that are typically lobed or toothed.

The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for woodland gardens and naturalized landscapes.
