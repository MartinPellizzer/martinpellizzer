Adenophorus tamariscinus

Adenophorus tamariscinus, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments with deep roots that help it access groundwater. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine layer of hair to reduce water loss.

Adenophorus tamariscinus plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
