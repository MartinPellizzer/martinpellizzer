Atriplex cinerea subsp. isatidea

Atriplex cinerea subsp.

isatidea, commonly known as silverleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which reflects sunlight and reduces water loss.

The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. It produces small greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated.

This subspecies is adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable plant for stabilizing coastal and desert environments.
