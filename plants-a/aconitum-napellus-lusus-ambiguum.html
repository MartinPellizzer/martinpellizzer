Aconitum napellus lusus ambiguum

Aconitum napellus lusus ambiguum, commonly known as the ambiguous form of European aconite, is a variant of the highly toxic plant Aconitum napellus.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and palmate leaves, typical of the genus Aconitum. This plant is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its botanical features include a tall, unbranched stem and a cluster of showy flowers at the top.

Due to its toxicity, it is important to handle it with caution and avoid ingestion.
