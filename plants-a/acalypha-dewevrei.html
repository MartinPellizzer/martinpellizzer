Acalypha dewevrei

Acalypha dewevrei, commonly known as the African copperleaf, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, copper-colored leaves that turn reddish in the shade. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Acalypha dewevrei is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
