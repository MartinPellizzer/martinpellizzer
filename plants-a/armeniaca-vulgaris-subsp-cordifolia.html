Armeniaca vulgaris subsp. cordifolia

Armeniaca vulgaris subsp.

cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf cherry, is a subspecies of the Prunus genus native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, sweet-tart fruits that are typically harvested in late summer.

The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, often growing in rocky or dry soils. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it is valued for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

The subspecies is closely related to the more widely cultivated sweet cherry (Prunus avium).
