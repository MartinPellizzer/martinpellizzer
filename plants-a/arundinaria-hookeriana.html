Arundinaria hookeriana

Arundinaria hookeriana, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or hooker's bamboo, is a tall, clumping grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clusters, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, grass-like texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Arundinaria hookeriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its graceful appearance.
