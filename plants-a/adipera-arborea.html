Adipera arborea

Adipera arborea, commonly known as the tree fatroot, is a woody plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots that store large amounts of nutrients and water. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically greenish-white in color.

Adipera arborea is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
