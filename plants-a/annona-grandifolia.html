Annona grandifolia

Annona grandifolia, commonly known as the custard apple, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces large, fragrant, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in groups of three. Its fruit is a tropical berry with a thick, scaly rind and a soft, juicy interior.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough, leathery texture.
