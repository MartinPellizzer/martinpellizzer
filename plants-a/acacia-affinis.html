Acacia affinis

Acacia affinis, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and phyllodes that are typically pale green to white in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by flat, leathery pods. Acacia affinis thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry inland regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in providing habitat for native wildlife.
