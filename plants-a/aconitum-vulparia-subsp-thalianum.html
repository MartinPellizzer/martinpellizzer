Aconitum vulparia subsp. thalianum

Aconitum vulparia subsp.

thalianum, commonly known as the European monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple-blue, hood-shaped flowers.

The plant has palmate leaves with deeply lobed margins, and it typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It is known for its toxic alkaloids, which make it highly poisonous to both humans and animals.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens despite its dangerous properties.
