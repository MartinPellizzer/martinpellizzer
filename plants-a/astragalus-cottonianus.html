Astragalus cottonianus

Astragalus cottonianus, commonly known as the cottony milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus cottonianus is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
