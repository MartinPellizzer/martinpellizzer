Aristida zittelii

Aristida zittelii, commonly known as Zittel's dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in arid environments, making it a valuable native plant for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
