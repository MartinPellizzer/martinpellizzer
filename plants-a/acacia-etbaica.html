Acacia etbaica

Acacia etbaica, commonly known as the Eritrean acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Horn of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless branches and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow or pale green globular flower heads, which are arranged in compact clusters. Acacia etbaica is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry regions.

It plays a role in local ecosystems and has been used traditionally for various purposes.
