Alyssum alyssoides

Alyssum alyssoides, commonly known as the common alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in early summer. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Alyssum alyssoides is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
