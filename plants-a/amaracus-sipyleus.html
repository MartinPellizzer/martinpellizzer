Amaracus sipyleus

Amaracus sipyleus, commonly known as the Sipylean amaracus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice for gardeners in arid climates.
