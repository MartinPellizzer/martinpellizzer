Astragalus quisqualis subsp. longiscapus

Astragalus quisqualis subsp.

longiscapus, commonly known as the long-stemmed milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow to reddish-purple flowers.

The plant produces cylindrical, woody pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry environments, with deep roots that help it access groundwater.

This subspecies is often found in steppe and rocky habitats, contributing to soil stabilization in its native range.
