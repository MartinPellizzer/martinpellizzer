Astragalus hintonii var. confrensis

Astragalus hintonii var. confrensis, commonly known as the Hinton's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, yellow to cream-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and is of interest to botanists and conservationists due to its limited distribution.
