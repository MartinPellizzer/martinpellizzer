Agapetes acuminatissimum

Agapetes acuminatissimum, commonly known as the sharp-pointed silverleaf, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, moss-like foliage. The plant typically grows at high elevations, often in cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, with a silvery underside that gives it its common name.

Agapetes acuminatissimum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
