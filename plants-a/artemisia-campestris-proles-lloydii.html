Artemisia campestris proles lloydii

Artemisia campestris proles lloydii, commonly known as the mountain sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in compact clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it an important component of high-elevation ecosystems.
