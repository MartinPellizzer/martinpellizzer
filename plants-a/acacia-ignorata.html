Acacia ignorata

Acacia ignorata, commonly known as the white wattle, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Acacia ignorata is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and can endure periods of drought.

Its foliage is often used by native animals, and it plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils in its habitat.
