Achyropappus schkuhrioides

Achyropappus schkuhrioides, commonly known as sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows as a low, mat-forming shrub with hairy, silver-gray foliage adapted to withstand drought. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in the spring, and the plant produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

Sandmat is often found in sandy soils and is well-suited to desert environments due to its drought tolerance and shallow root system.
