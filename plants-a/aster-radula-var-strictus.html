Aster radula var. strictus

Aster radula var. strictus, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense heads, and range in color from white to pale blue.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
