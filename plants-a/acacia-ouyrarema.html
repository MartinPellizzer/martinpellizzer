Acacia ouyrarema

Acacia ouyrarema, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant is notable for its bright yellow flowers, which appear in clusters and attract a variety of pollinators. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, feather-like leaflets.

Acacia ouyrarema is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
