Amorphophallus fischeri

Amorphophallus fischeri, commonly known as the giant water chestnut, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and a striking, spadix-shaped inflorescence that resembles a phallus, hence its scientific name. The plant thrives in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, often growing in shallow water or muddy soils. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is sometimes cultivated for its edible tubers.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some regions where it outcompetes native vegetation.
