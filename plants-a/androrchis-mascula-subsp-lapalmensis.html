Androrchis mascula subsp. lapalmensis

Androrchis mascula subsp.

lapalmensis, commonly known as the Lapalmensis orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions in South America. It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring intricate sepals and petals that often display vibrant colors.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories, where it forms symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi. Its flowering period is brief, usually occurring in late spring or early summer.
