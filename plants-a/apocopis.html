Apocopis

Apocopis, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It consists of several species native to various regions of the Americas. The plants are characterized by their basal rosettes of leaves and yellow composite flowers that resemble dandelions. Apocopis species typically grow in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

They are often used in traditional medicine and have ecological importance as a food source for insects.
