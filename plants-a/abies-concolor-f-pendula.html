Abies concolor f. pendula

Abies concolor f. pendula, commonly known as the white fir pendula, is a unique variant of the white fir tree.

It is characterized by its drooping branches, which give it a distinctive, weeping appearance. The foliage consists of needle-like leaves that are typically a silvery-blue color, providing a striking visual contrast. This form is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and parks.

It thrives in cooler climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.
