Astragalus pycnocephalus var. segtunensis

Astragalus pycnocephalus var. segtunensis, commonly known as the Segtun milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variant is notable for its ecological significance in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
