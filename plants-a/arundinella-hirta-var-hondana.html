Arundinella hirta var. hondana

Arundinella hirta var. hondana, commonly known as the Honduras bamboo, is a perennial grass native to Central America.

It features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
