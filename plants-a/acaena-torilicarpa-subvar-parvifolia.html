Acaena torilicarpa subvar. parvifolia

Acaena torilicarpa subvar. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved New Zealand spinach, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It features small, ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically hairy and have a serrated margin. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, which are characteristic of the genus Acaena. Its fruits are small, dry achenes surrounded by a woolly bract, typical of the family Rosaceae.

This subvariety is often found in coastal and inland areas, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
