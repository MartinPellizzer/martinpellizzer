Andropogon macrostachyus

Andropogon macrostachyus, commonly known as giant panicgrass, is a tall, perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features long, narrow leaves with a rough texture and can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces large, open panicles of pale green to golden yellow flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Its robust growth makes it an important species in ecological studies and potential for use in erosion control.
