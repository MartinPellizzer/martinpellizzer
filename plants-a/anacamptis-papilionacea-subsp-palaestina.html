Anacamptis papilionacea subsp. palaestina

Anacamptis papilionacea subsp.

palaestina, commonly known as the Palestine orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region. It is a terrestrial herb with a short, stout rhizome and produces a single, erect stem bearing a cluster of showy, pale pink to white flowers.

The leaves are linear and grass-like, typically arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant. The flowers have a distinctive spurred lip and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
