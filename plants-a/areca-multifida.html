Areca multifida

Areca multifida, commonly known as the crested areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and fan-shaped leaves that are often divided into several segments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into dark blue-black berries. Areca multifida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping due to its adaptability to various soil types.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
