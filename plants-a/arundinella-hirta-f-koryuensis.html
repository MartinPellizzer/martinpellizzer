Arundinella hirta f. koryuensis

Arundinella hirta f. koryuensis, commonly known as the Koryuensis reed, is a variant of the perennial grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountainous regions and forest understories. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to brownish flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
