Adenandra marginata var. angusta

Adenandra marginata var. angusta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved adenandra, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the cooler months. These flowers are followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
