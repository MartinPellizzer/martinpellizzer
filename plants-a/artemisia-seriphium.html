Artemisia seriphium

Artemisia seriphium, commonly known as mountain wormwood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, exhibiting a compact growth habit with silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellowish flower heads appear in late summer, contributing to its ecological role as a nectar source for pollinators.

Artemisia seriphium is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
