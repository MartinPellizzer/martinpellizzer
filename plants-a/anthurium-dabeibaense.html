Anthurium dabeibaense

Anthurium dabeibaense, commonly known as the Andean flame, is a tropical flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive spathe and spadix inflorescence. The plant features large, brightly colored leaves that can vary in hue from deep red to green, depending on the variety. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium dabeibaense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
