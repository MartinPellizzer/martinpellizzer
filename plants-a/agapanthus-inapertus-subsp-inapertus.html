Agapanthus inapertus subsp. inapertus

Agapanthus inapertus subsp.

inapertus, commonly known as the white agapanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear large, showy clusters of white or pale blue flowers.

The plant produces strap-shaped leaves that are often blue-green in color, providing a striking contrast to the flowers. It is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
