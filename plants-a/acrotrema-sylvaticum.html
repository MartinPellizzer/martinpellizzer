Acrotrema sylvaticum

Acrotrema sylvaticum, commonly known as the forest acrotrema, is a small, perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and forest floors. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, which are often hidden among the foliage.

Acrotrema sylvaticum is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its ecological role in supporting insect populations.
