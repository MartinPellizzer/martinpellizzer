Abies engelmannii var. glauca

Abies engelmannii var. glauca, commonly known as the Engelmann spruce var. glauca, is a variant of the Engelmann spruce native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves, which are typically bluish-green in color, giving the plant its "glauca" designation. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, and it thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. It is often found in high-elevation forests and is adapted to withstand harsh alpine conditions.

This variant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
