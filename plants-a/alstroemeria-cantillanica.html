Alstroemeria cantillanica

Alstroemeria cantillanica, commonly known as the Cantillanica lily, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Alstroemeria cantillanica is valued in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and is often cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.
