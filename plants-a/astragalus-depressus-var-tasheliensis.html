Astragalus depressus var. tasheliensis

Astragalus depressus var. tasheliensis, commonly known as the Tasheli milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers in late spring. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in semi-desert environments.

This species is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
