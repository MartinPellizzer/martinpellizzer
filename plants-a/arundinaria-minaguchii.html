Arundinaria minaguchii

Arundinaria minaguchii, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Minaguchii bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, green shoots that grow in dense clumps, forming a compact and ornamental appearance. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often used in traditional Japanese gardens for their aesthetic value. It is well-suited for moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Arundinaria minaguchii is valued for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of garden settings.
