Achillea herba-rota

Achillea herba-rota, commonly known as yarrow, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its flat, composite flower heads and feathery, aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It produces small white or yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Yarrow is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
