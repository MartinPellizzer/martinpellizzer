Annona oligocarpa

Annona oligocarpa, commonly known as the Brazilian pawpaw, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves and is notable for its fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a large, green, spiny berry that ripens to a yellowish color and is edible when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
