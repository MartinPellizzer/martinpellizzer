Asplenium calophyllum

Asplenium calophyllum, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, arching fronds with a deep green color. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, lance-shaped pinnae that give it a lacy appearance.

Asplenium calophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
