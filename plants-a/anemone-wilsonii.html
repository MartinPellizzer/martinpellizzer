Anemone wilsonii

Anemone wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive red center, blooming in early spring. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves that are palmately arranged, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
