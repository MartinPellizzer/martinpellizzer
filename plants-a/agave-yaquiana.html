Agave yaquiana

Agave yaquiana, commonly known as the Yaquilla agave, is a species native to Mexico, particularly in the states of Jalisco and Nayarit.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears numerous white flowers, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Agave yaquiana is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.
