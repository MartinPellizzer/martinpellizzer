Ampelocissus africana

Ampelocissus africana, commonly known as the African leafy vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, yellowish fruits. It thrives in semi-shade to full sun and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its ability to climb and its attractive foliage make it a popular choice for trellises and green walls.
