Anil capitata

Anil capitata, commonly known as Indian mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often hairy. Anil capitata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
