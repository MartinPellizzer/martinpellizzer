Aconogonon baicalense

Aconogonon baicalense, commonly known as the Baikal bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
