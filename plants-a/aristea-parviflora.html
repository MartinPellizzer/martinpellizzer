Aristea parviflora

Aristea parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered aristea, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features dense clusters of tiny, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple, typically during the summer months. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a rosette at the base, and it produces small, bead-like seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and coastal gardens.
