Andropogon smithianus

Andropogon smithianus, commonly known as Smith's switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. This plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowering inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets that mature into fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

Andropogon smithianus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
