Acalypha major

Acalypha major, commonly known as the velvetleaf or redroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple hues. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are typically followed by three-winged seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Acalypha major is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage.
