Acridocarpus alternifolius

Acridocarpus alternifolius, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its alternate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are often hairy, giving it the common name, and it is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
