Acacia tessellata

Acacia tessellata, commonly known as the tessellated wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are composed of multiple small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bark is typically smooth and may have a tessellated or mosaic-like pattern, giving it its common name.

Acacia tessellata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
