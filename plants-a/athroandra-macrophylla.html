Athroandra macrophylla

Athroandra macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leafed sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, aromatic fruits. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The tree is valued for its hard, fragrant wood, which is used in the production of incense and furniture.
