Agave delamateri

Agave delamateri, commonly known as Delamater's agave, is a species of agave native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flower stalk with yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun.

Agave delamateri is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
