Aristida diffusa subsp. burkei

Aristida diffusa subsp.

burkei, commonly known as the burke's threeawn, is a grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often appear in dense clumps.

The plant produces spikelets that are typically three to five in number, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

This subspecies is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
