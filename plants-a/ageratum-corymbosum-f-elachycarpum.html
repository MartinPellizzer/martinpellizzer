Ageratum corymbosum f. elachycarpum

Ageratum corymbosum f. elachycarpum, commonly known as the dwarf ageratum, is a compact, flowering plant native to Mexico.

It features small, showy flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has finely divided, gray-green foliage that adds texture to garden displays. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for borders and containers.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
