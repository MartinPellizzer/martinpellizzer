Androsaemum × parviflorum

Androsaemum × parviflorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved angel's trumpets, is a hybrid plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant has opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
