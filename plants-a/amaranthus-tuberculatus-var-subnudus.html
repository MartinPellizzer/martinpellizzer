Amaranthus tuberculatus var. subnudus

Amaranthus tuberculatus var. subnudus, commonly known as buffalobur, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is native to the eastern and central United States and is characterized by its prickly, spiny leaves and stems. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that develop into spherical, spiky seed heads. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Buffalobur is known for its hardiness and ability to spread rapidly, making it both a weed and a plant of ecological interest.
