Aster scabricaulis

Aster scabricaulis, commonly known as the roughstem aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Aster scabricaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
