Abies concolor subsp. lowiana

Abies concolor subsp.

lowiana, commonly known as the lowland white fir, is a subspecies of the white fir native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are slightly curved.

The cones are typically short and cylindrical, maturing to a reddish-brown color. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies plays an important role in forest ecosystems and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
