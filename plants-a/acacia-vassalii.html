Acacia vassalii

Acacia vassalii, commonly known as the Vassal acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, yellowish-green phyllodes that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, globular flower heads that are typically yellow or pale brown, blooming during the warmer months. Acacia vassalii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.

It is often found in rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine by local communities.
