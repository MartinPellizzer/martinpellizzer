Aster paniculatus var. polychrous

Aster paniculatus var. polychrous, commonly known as the showy panicled aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, branched inflorescences that bear numerous small, violet to blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. It has lance-shaped to oval leaves that are usually hairy and arranged alternately along the stem.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
