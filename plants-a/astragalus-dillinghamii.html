Astragalus dillinghamii

Astragalus dillinghamii, commonly known as Dillingham's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as sagebrush steppe and desert environments. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

Astragalus dillinghamii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
