Achyranthes paraguayensis

Achyranthes paraguayensis, commonly known as Paraguay knotweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.
