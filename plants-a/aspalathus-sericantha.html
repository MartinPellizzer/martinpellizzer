Aspalathus sericantha

Aspalathus sericantha, commonly known as the sweet cycad, is a unique plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stem and large, pinnately compound leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Aspalathus sericantha is adapted to arid conditions, making it a hardy species suitable for dry environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
