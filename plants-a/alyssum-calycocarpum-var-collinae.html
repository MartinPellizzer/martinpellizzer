Alyssum calycocarpum var. collinae

Alyssum calycocarpum var. collinae, commonly known as the Sierra Alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its foliage consists of gray-green, fuzzy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
