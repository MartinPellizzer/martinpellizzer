Adiantum cristatum var. nigrescens

Adiantum cristatum var. nigrescens, commonly known as the black-stemmed maidenhair fern, is a striking fern species characterized by its dark green, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soil. Its unique appearance, with dark stems and elegant, lace-like leaves, makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It is also noted for its ability to tolerate low light conditions, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
