Asparagus officinalis var. campestris

Asparagus officinalis var. campestris, commonly known as the field asparagus, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asparagaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, cylindrical stems and feathery, compound leaves that emerge from the base. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that develop into spiky, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its edible shoots, which are harvested in early spring.
