Adenocarpus commutatus

Adenocarpus commutatus, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain seeds with tufted hairs for wind dispersal. It is often found in grassland and woodland habitats, and it plays a role in supporting local wildlife.

Its latex sap is milky and can cause skin irritation, which is a notable botanical feature.
