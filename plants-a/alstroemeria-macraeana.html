Alstroemeria macraeana

Alstroemeria macraeana, commonly known as the Macrae's alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a range of colors including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, producing clusters of showy blooms during the spring and summer months.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
