Astragalus lentiginosus var. sesquimetralis

Astragalus lentiginosus var. sesquimetralis, commonly known as the bicolored milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, producing flowers in its second year. The plant features compound leaves with stipules and small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is notable for its distinct leaf and flower morphology, which distinguish it from other Astragalus species.
