Atriplex flavida

Atriplex flavida, commonly known as silverleaf cholla, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and small, scale-like leaves that are covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in drought conditions. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, fleshy fruits.

Atriplex flavida plays an important role in desert ecosystems, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
