Angorkis recurva

Angorkis recurva, commonly known as the twisted angorkis, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiraling growth pattern and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green to yellow in color. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in dense undergrowth.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research.
