Aconitum chloranthum

Aconitum chloranthum, commonly known as the green-flowered monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers that are surrounded by two large, petal-like bracts. The plant has palmately divided, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. Aconitum chloranthum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous to handle.
