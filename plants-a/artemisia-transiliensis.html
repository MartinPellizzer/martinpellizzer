Artemisia transiliensis

Artemisia transiliensis, commonly known as the Transylvanian wormwood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in compact clusters, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and rocky habitats.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thujone, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
