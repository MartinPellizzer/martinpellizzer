Alchemilla tacikii

Alchemilla tacikii, commonly known as the Tacik's lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, hairy leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Alchemilla tacikii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
