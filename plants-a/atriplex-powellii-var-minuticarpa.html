Atriplex powellii var. minuticarpa

Atriplex powellii var. minuticarpa, commonly known as the Powell's saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, smooth, oval leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white salt crystals, a feature typical of the Chenopodiaceae family. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, round, edible berries. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments.

This variety is notable for its ability to tolerate extreme drought and poor soil conditions, contributing to its ecological significance in arid landscapes.
