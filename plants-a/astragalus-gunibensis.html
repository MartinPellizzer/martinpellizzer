Astragalus gunibensis

Astragalus gunibensis, commonly known as the Gunib milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces pods that contain several small, round seeds.

Astragalus gunibensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
