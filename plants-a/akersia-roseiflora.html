Akersia roseiflora

Akersia roseiflora, commonly known as the rose-flowered akersia, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple rose-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
