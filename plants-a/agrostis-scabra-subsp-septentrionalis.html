Agrostis scabra subsp. septentrionalis

Agrostis scabra subsp.

septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern rough bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of North America. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, meadows, and along stream banks.

The plant has slender, erect stems with rough, hairy leaves that are often compressed and folded. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-based flowers.

This subspecies is characterized by its adaptability to wet environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
