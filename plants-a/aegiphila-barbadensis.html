Aegiphila barbadensis

Aegiphila barbadensis, commonly known as the Caribbean senna, is a shrub native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is often found in coastal areas and dry forests, adapting to sandy and well-drained soils. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular plant in horticulture.
