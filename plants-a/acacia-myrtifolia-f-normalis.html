Acacia myrtifolia f. normalis

Acacia myrtifolia f. normalis, commonly known as the common acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless branches and bipinnate leaves composed of small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces yellow to golden-green globular inflorescences, which are rich in nectar and attract various pollinators. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soils and drought conditions.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and fuel.
