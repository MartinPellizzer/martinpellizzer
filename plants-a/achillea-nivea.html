Achillea nivea

Achillea nivea, commonly known as the snowy yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in flat, circular clusters, often resembling a snowball. The plant has finely divided, silvery-green leaves that are aromatic and emit a subtle, pleasant scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
