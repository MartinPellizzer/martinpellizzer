Aconitum napellus var. rubellum

Aconitum napellus var. rubellum, commonly known as the red-flowered monkshood, is a variant of the European aconite characterized by its striking red-purple flowers.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. The plant features large, spurred flowers that are typically arranged in a raceme, with deep violet to red petals. Its leaves are palmately compound, with a distinctive palmate shape and serrated edges.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
