Alpinia vitiensis

Alpinia vitiensis, commonly known as the Vitiensis ginger, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known gingers such as ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green in color. Its flowering stems produce clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to white flowers, which are followed by small, triangular berries.

Alpinia vitiensis is valued both for its ornamental appeal and for its potential medicinal properties.
