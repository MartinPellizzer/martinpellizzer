Albertinia saligna

Albertinia saligna, commonly known as the Cape ash, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 meters, featuring a straight trunk and a rounded crown. The leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its small, pale yellow flowers appear in spring, followed by small, round, brown fruits.

The tree is well adapted to the local climate and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.
