Acacia drummondii var. parviflora

Acacia drummondii var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered drummond acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has small, bipinnate leaves that reduce water loss, adapting it to its harsh environment. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is well-suited to dry climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in low-water conditions.
