Achyranthes bangii

Achyranthes bangii, commonly known as the Indian mallow or Bangi mallow, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows up to 1.5 meters in height, featuring erect, branched stems and hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and diuretic properties.

Achyranthes bangii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in wetlands and along riverbanks.
