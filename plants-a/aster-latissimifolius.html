Aster latissimifolius

Aster latissimifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaf aster, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or blue. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are broad and velvety to the touch. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Aster latissimifolius plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
