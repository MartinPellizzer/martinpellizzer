Arcytophyllum caracasanum

Arcytophyllum caracasanum, commonly known as the Carpathian sage, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Labiatae, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
