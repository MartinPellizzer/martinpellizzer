Acer miyabei subsp. miaotaiense

Acer miyabei subsp.

miaotaiense, commonly known as the Miaotai maple, is a deciduous tree native to China. It is characterized by its palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall.

The plant typically grows in mountainous regions with cool, humid climates. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
