Astragalus scorpiurus

Astragalus scorpiurus, commonly known as the scorpion milkweed, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain numerous seeds. It is adapted to harsh environments, often growing in rocky soils and dry habitats.

Astragalus scorpiurus plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
