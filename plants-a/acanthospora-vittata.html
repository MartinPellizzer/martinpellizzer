Acanthospora vittata

Acanthospora vittata, commonly known as the zigzag coralroot, is a small, perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its lack of chlorophyll, making it a myco-heterotroph that relies on fungal associations for nutrients. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in a spiral pattern, which is a distinctive botanical feature. Its roots are specialized for forming symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, enabling it to absorb nutrients from the soil indirectly.

Acanthospora vittata typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and is often found in association with other shade-tolerant plants.
