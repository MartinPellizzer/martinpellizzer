Aconitum gilvum

Aconitum gilvum, commonly known as the yellow aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmate leaves with a coarse texture and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. It contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous to handle or ingest.

Despite its toxicity, it is appreciated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
