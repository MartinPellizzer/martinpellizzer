Astragalus hypoleucus

Astragalus hypoleucus, commonly known as the pale milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus hypoleucus is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland ecosystems.
