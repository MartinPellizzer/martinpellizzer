Ariopsis peltata f. protanthera

Ariopsis peltata f. protanthera, commonly known as the pink-flowered sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a variant of the sea hibiscus, characterized by its distinctive pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are adapted to salt spray and sandy soils. Its flowers are typically 3 to 4 inches in diameter, with five petals and a prominent central stamen.

This plant is often found in dune ecosystems and plays a role in stabilizing coastal sands.
