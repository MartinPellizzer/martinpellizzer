Andropogon glaucescens

Andropogon glaucescens, commonly known as bluestem grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its bluish-gray foliage and tufted inflorescence, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Andropogon glaucescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
