Alchemilla crinita

Alchemilla crinita, commonly known as the hairy lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves that resemble a mantle. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are clustered in cup-like formations. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Alchemilla crinita is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
