Astraea subcomosa

Astraea subcomosa, commonly known as the slender star orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, star-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on tree trunks or branches in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, adapted to capture light in its natural habitat.

Astraea subcomosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
