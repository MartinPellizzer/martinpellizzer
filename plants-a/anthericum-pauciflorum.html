Anthericum pauciflorum

Anthericum pauciflorum, commonly known as the few-flowered arum, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-like inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a dense cluster on the spadix, which is typical of arum species. Its leaves are typically broad and arrow-shaped, with a glossy surface.

Anthericum pauciflorum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
