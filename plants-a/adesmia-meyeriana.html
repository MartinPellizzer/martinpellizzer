Adesmia meyeriana

Adesmia meyeriana, commonly known as the Meyer's adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain viable in the soil for several years.

Adesmia meyeriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
