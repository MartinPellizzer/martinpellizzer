Amberboa

Amberboa, commonly known as the goldenroot, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust root system that is the primary source of its medicinal properties. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its roots are harvested in the autumn and dried for use in herbal remedies.

The botanical name of amberboa is Commiphora myrrha, though it is sometimes confused with other resin-producing plants.
