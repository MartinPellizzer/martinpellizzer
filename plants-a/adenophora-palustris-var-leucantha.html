Adenophora palustris var. leucantha

Adenophora palustris var. leucantha, commonly known as the white milkvetch, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetlands and marshy areas of North America.

It features slender, hairy stems that grow up to 60 cm in height, often branching near the base. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers in racemes, which bloom from June to August. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and covered with fine hairs.

The species is adapted to moist soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
