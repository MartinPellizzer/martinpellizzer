Axanthopsis

Axanthopsis, commonly known as the golden leaf plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, golden-yellow leaves that stand out against its deep green foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically a mix of red and orange, adding to its ornamental appeal. Native to tropical regions, it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-draining soil.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor settings.
