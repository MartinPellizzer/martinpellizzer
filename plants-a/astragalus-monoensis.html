Astragalus monoensis

Astragalus monoensis, commonly known as the Mono milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States, particularly found in the Mono Lake area of California.

It is a perennial herb with a slender, branching stem and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Astragalus monoensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and open habitats.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain wildlife.
