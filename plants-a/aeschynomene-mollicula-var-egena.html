Aeschynomene mollicula var. egena

Aeschynomene mollicula var. egena, commonly known as the silky aeschynomene, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The flowers are typically pale green to yellowish and are arranged in clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

This plant is valued for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
