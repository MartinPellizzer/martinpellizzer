Alnus × nikolskensis

Alnus × nikolskensis, commonly known as the Siberian white poplar, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Alnus tatarica and Alnus alnobetula.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its white to grey bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant is native to the Russian Far East and is adapted to cold climates, making it a hardy species. It plays an important role in ecological succession, often colonizing disturbed sites.

Alnus × nikolskensis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility in its growing environment.
