Alnus glutinosa lusus californica

Alnus glutinosa lusus californica, commonly known as the California sticky alder, is a variant of the sticky alder native to California.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish flowers in catkins. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in local habitats.
