Ageratum koulianum

Ageratum koulianum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to blue and purple. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners. Its foliage is typically gray-green, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

Ageratum koulianum is often used in ornamental gardening for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
