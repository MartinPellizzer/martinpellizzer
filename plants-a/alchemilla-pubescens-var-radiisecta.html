Alchemilla pubescens var. radiisecta

Alchemilla pubescens var. radiisecta, commonly known as the silver-haired lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It features a basal rosette of hairy, silver-gray leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces upright flowering stems bearing clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves and flowers are often used in ornamental gardening for their attractive appearance and ability to thrive in moist, shady conditions.

It is also valued for its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
