Aster novi-belgii var. atlanticus

Aster novi-belgii var. atlanticus, commonly known as the Atlantic aster, is a perennial flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, featuring narrow, gray-green leaves and showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
