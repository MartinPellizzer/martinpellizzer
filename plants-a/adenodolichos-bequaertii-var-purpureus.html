Adenodolichos bequaertii var. purpureus

Adenodolichos bequaertii var. purpureus, commonly known as the purple-flowered cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking purple flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain edible seeds, similar to other members of its genus. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
