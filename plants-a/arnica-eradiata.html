Arnica eradiata

Arnica eradiata, commonly known as the radiate arnica, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers with a central disk and surrounding ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky and meadow habitats at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Arnica eradiata is valued for its medicinal properties, though it contains compounds that can be toxic if misused.
