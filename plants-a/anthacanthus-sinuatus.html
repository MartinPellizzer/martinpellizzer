Anthacanthus sinuatus

Anthacanthus sinuatus, commonly known as the sinuous anthacanthus, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a humid, temperate climate to flourish. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered vulnerable and is often found in protected natural reserves.
