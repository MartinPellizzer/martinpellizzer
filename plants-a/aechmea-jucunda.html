Aechmea jucunda

Aechmea jucunda, commonly known as the pink pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater to support its growth. The plant produces vibrant red or pink flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette, adding a dramatic visual element. Its foliage is typically dark green with a waxy texture, and it thrives in bright, indirect light.

Aechmea jucunda is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea jucunda has leaves that are typically grass-like, arranged in a rosette pattern, with a bluish-green color and a waxy surface.

The stem is short and often hidden beneath the leaves, with a smooth texture and a pale greenish-brown color. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, blooming during the summer months and pollinated by wind or insects. The fruit is a small, berry-like structure that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by animals or through water.

The roots are fibrous and help anchor the plant while absorbing nutrients from the soil.
