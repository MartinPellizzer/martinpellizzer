Adiantum melanoleucum var. nanum

Adiantum melanoleucum var. nanum, commonly known as the miniature maidenhair fern, is a compact, delicate fern native to the southeastern United States.

It features finely divided, bright green fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soil. Its small size makes it an excellent choice for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

It is characterized by its fern-like appearance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
