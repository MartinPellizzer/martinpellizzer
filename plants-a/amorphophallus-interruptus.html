Amorphophallus interruptus

Amorphophallus interruptus, commonly known as the giant swamp arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrowhead-shaped leaves and a stout, underground tuber that stores nutrients. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowering stems can reach several meters in height, producing large, white or violet-colored flowers.

The species is notable for its rapid growth and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
