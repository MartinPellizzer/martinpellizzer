Aloe × schimperi

Aloe × schimperi, commonly known as the hybrid aloe or the hybrid aloe vera, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Aloe vera and Aloe aristata.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in white tubercles. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in panicles, typically during the spring or early summer. It is native to South Africa and thrives in well-drained soil with plenty of sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor settings.
