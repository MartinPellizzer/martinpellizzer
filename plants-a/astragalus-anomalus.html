Astragalus anomalus

Astragalus anomalus, commonly known as the anomalous milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It produces pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Astragalus anomalus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
