Achyranthes benthamii

Achyranthes benthamii, commonly known as the red root or Chinese knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
