Asparagus zanzibaricus

Asparagus zanzibaricus, commonly known as Zanzibar asparagus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several narrow, lance-shaped leaflets.

The plant is adapted to sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal dunes and scrublands.
