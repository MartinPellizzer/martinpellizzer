Ataxia tenuis

Ataxia tenuis, commonly known as the slender ataxia, is a rare and delicate plant species characterized by its finely divided, grass-like leaves.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in dense forests. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white in color. Its reproductive structures are adapted for wind pollination, which is common in its native habitat.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, Ataxia tenuis is often found in isolated populations and is considered a species of conservation concern.
