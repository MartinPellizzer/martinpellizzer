Aira elegantissima subsp. hosteana

Aira elegantissima subsp.

hosteana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf foxtail, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has fine, linear leaves that are often covered in a short layer of hair.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
