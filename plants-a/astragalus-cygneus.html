Astragalus cygneus

Astragalus cygneus, commonly known as the swanhead milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Astragalus cygneus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root system.

Its ecological significance includes providing forage for wildlife and contributing to soil stabilization in its native habitats.
