Acacia buxifolia var. neglecta

Acacia buxifolia var. neglecta, commonly known as the dwarf acacia or coastal acacia, is a small, evergreen shrub native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, yellow, spherical flowers that appear in clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is highly tolerant of salt spray, making it well-suited for coastal environments. Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for landscaping in arid and semi-arid climates.
