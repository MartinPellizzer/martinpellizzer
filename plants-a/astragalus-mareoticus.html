Astragalus mareoticus

Astragalus mareoticus, commonly known as the Nile milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Egypt and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the banks of the Nile River. It produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus mareoticus is known for its ability to thrive in arid environments and has historical significance in traditional medicine.
