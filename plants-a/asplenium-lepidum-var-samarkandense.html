Asplenium lepidum var. samarkandense

Asplenium lepidum var. samarkandense, commonly known as the Samarkand maidenhair fern, is a variant of the common maidenhair fern native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, dark green fronds that are typically arranged in a graceful, feathery pattern. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
