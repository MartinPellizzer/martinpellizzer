Armeniaca mandshurica f. major

Armeniaca mandshurica f. major, commonly known as the Manchurian apricot.

This plant is a cultivar of the apricot species, characterized by its large, showy flowers and edible fruits. It is native to regions of East Asia, including parts of China, Russia, and Korea. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Its botanical features include smooth, dark green leaves and a fruit that is generally larger and sweeter than that of the typical apricot.
