Alternanthera lehmannii

Alternanthera lehmannii, commonly known as red flame plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and aquatic settings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its rapid growth and vibrant foliage make it a popular choice for both terrestrial and water garden displays.
