Anathallis micrantha

Anathallis micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered anathallid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact stature and distinctive greenish-white flowers with a short, narrow spur. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in cloud forests or rainforests. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette.

Anathallis micrantha is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators such as bees and moths.
