Acer palmatum f. rubellum

Acer palmatum f. rubellum, commonly known as the red-leaf maple, is a cultivar of the Japanese maple known for its striking crimson foliage.

It features delicate, palmate leaves that turn a vibrant red in the fall, making it a popular ornamental tree. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in USDA hardiness zones 5 through 8. It prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, and it is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.

The leaves are typically smaller and more vibrant than those of the species, adding a unique visual element to gardens.
