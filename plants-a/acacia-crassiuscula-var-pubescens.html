Acacia crassiuscula var. pubescens

Acacia crassiuscula var. pubescens, commonly known as the rough acacia or white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and small, pale green phyllodes that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads during the winter months, which are arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

The species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
