Abarema bigemina

Abarema bigemina, commonly known as the two-flowered legume, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy, elongated pods that mature and split open to release the seeds.

Abarema bigemina is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of soil types.
