Astragalus gezeldarensis

Astragalus gezeldarensis, commonly known as the Gezeldale milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Astragalus gezeldarensis plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
