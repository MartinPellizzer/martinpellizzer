Alpinia longiflora

Alpinia longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered ginger, is a tropical perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features a rhizomatous root system and lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers, which are followed by spindle-shaped berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Alpinia longiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
