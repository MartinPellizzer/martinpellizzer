Aulomyrcia pilantha var. parvifolia

Aulomyrcia pilantha var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved myrcia, is a species native to the rainforests of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves with a glossy, dark green surface. Its flowers are small, clustered, and usually white or pale yellow, while the fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small seeds.

This plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
