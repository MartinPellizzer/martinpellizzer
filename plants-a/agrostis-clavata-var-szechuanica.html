Agrostis clavata var. szechuanica

Agrostis clavata var. szechuanica, commonly known as Szechuan bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to China.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, hair-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
