Anil teysmannii

Anil teysmannii, commonly known as the Malabar nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 1.5 meters in height, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible but can be toxic if consumed in large quantities. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Anil teysmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
