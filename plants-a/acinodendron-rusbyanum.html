Acinodendron rusbyanum

Acinodendron rusbyanum, commonly known as the African orchid tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble orchids, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a glossy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its aesthetic value.
