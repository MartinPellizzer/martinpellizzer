Acer tetramerum f. longiracemosum

Acer tetramerum f. longiracemosum, commonly known as the long-branch maple, is a variant of the four-leaf maple.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated branches and small, palmately lobed leaves. The leaves typically turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall, making it a striking ornamental plant. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This form is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
