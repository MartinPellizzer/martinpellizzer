Amerimnon congestiflorum

Amerimnon congestiflorum, commonly known as the clustered-flowered amaryllis, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its vibrant blooms and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking to add color and interest to their landscapes.
