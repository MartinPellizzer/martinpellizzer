Apium laconicum

Apium laconicum, commonly known as Greek celery or sea celery, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like parsley and carrots. The plant is characterized by its hollow, aromatic stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in umbrella-like clusters. Its leaves are slender and often used in traditional Mediterranean cuisine.

Apium laconicum is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used historically for its purported health benefits.
