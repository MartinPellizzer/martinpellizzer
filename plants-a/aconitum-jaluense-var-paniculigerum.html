Aconitum jaluense var. paniculigerum

Aconitum jaluense var. paniculigerum, commonly known as the Jaluense Monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple, helmet-shaped flowers. The plant features large, palmately compound leaves with serrated edges, typical of the genus Aconitum. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, often found in forested areas.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care, as all parts of the plant contain potent alkaloids.
