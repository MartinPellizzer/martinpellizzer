Aquilegia nevadensis var. litardieri

Aquilegia nevadensis var. litardieri, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada columbine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple spurred flowers and basal leaves with deeply lobed margins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as meadows and forest understories. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in high-elevation ecosystems.
