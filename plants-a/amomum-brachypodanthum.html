Amomum brachypodanthum

Amomum brachypodanthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cardamom, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its aromatic qualities.
