Aira caryophyllea var. multiculmis

Aira caryophyllea var. multiculmis, commonly known as the purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and its distinctive purple to pinkish flower heads that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its visual appeal.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in various regions with a Mediterranean climate.
