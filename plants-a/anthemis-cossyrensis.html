Anthemis cossyrensis

Anthemis cossyrensis, commonly known as the Cossyrian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are surrounded by white, petal-like structures, giving it a chamomile-like appearance. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the plant is known for its aromatic properties.
