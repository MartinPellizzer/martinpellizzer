Achras bahamensis

Achras bahamensis, commonly known as the Bahamian achiote, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
