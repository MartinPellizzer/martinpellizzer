Aster mutisianus

Aster mutisianus, commonly known as the Mutisia aster, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in countries like Peru and Ecuador.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, thriving in cooler and more temperate climates. Its flowers are usually blue or violet, with a distinctive central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it produces small, cylindrical fruits called cypselae.

Aster mutisianus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
