Aconitum napellus f. leiopodum

Aconitum napellus f. leiopodum, commonly known as the smooth-horned monkshood, is a variant of the European monkshood.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features striking purple to blue flowers arranged in a raceme, with distinctive hooded petals. Its leaves are palmately compound, consisting of five to seven leaflets.

This subspecies is native to the Alps and is characterized by its smooth, less hairy stems compared to the typical form.
