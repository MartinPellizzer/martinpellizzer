Asarca calopogon

Asarca calopogon, commonly known as the grass pink, is a striking orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves and its distinctive, showy pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and bogs, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is pollinated by various insects, including bees and flies.

Asarca calopogon is a rare and endangered species, requiring conservation efforts to protect its habitat.
