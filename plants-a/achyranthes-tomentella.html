Achyranthes tomentella

Achyranthes tomentella, commonly known as hairy senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its fruits are capsules that contain several seeds, and the plant is often found in moist, disturbed soils.

Achyranthes tomentella is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
