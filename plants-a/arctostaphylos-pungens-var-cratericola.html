Arctostaphylos pungens var. cratericola

Arctostaphylos pungens var. cratericola, commonly known as the Sierra foothill manzanita, is a shrub native to California and southern Oregon.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of pinkish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ecological role and is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental qualities and drought tolerance.
