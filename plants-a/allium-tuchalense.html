Allium tuchalense

Allium tuchalense, commonly known as the Tuchalense leek, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe, particularly in the Carpathian Basin. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and is characterized by its clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium tuchalense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has mild culinary uses in some regions.
