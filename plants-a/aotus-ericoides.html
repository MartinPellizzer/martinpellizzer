Aotus ericoides

Aotus ericoides, commonly known as the Ericoid Aotus, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
