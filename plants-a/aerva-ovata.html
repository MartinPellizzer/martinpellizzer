Aerva ovata

Aerva ovata, commonly known as Indian nettle or Indian spinach, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and thrives in warm climates.

Aerva ovata is also valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.
