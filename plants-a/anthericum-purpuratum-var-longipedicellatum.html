Anthericum purpuratum var. longipedicellatum

Anthericum purpuratum var. longipedicellatum, commonly known as the purple nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple underside and a hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
