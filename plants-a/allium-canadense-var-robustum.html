Allium canadense var. robustum

Allium canadense var. robustum, commonly known as Canada garlic or wild leek, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of long, strap-like leaves. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem bearing clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a strong garlic-like flavor, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
