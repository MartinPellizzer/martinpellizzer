Aphelandra edmundoana var. monocephala

Aphelandra edmundoana var. monocephala, commonly known as the single-headed flame plant, is a tropical species native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated, sword-shaped leaves that are dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
