Artocarpus corneri

Artocarpus corneri, commonly known as the Kauri fruit or Kauri apple, is a tropical tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries. The plant produces large, spiky fruits that are typically harvested when they are fully ripe and fallen to the ground. The leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique fruit.
