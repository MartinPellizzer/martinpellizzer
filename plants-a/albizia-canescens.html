Albizia canescens

Albizia canescens, commonly known as the white acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate and covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pale yellow to white in color, arranged in racemes. Albizia canescens thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
