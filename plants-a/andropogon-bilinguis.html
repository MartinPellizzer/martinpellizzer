Andropogon bilinguis

Andropogon bilinguis, commonly known as the eastern gamagrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often tinged with silver on the underside. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Andropogon bilinguis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
