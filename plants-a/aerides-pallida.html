Aerides pallida

Aerides pallida, commonly known as the pale orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale green to white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

The flowers are relatively small but have a subtle fragrance, making it a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
