Antirrhinum virgatulum

Antirrhinum virgatulum, commonly known as the slender snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Antirrhinum virgatulum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
