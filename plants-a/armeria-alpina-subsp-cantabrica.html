Armeria alpina subsp. cantabrica

Armeria alpina subsp.

cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian thrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features dense clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
