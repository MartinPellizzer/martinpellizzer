Aster boykinii

Aster boykinii, commonly known as the Boykin's aster, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. It produces showy purple to pink ray flowers surrounded by yellow disk flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster boykinii is often found in sandy or loamy soils and is adapted to partial shade to full sun conditions.
