Amomum oligophyllum

Amomum oligophyllum, commonly known as the small-leaf cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces dark brown, three-lobed fruits that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its medicinal properties.

The essential oils extracted from its seeds are valued for their therapeutic and culinary applications.
