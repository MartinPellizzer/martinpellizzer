Alyssum minus var. mazandaranicum

Alyssum minus var. mazandaranicum, commonly known as the Persian alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has gray-green foliage with a fuzzy texture, and its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and borders. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

The variety is notable for its adaptability to harsh climates and its historical use in traditional medicine.
