Amasonia

Amasonia, commonly known as the goldenrods, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

These plants are native to North America and are characterized by their showy, yellow or golden inflorescences. Amasonia species typically thrive in sunny, open habitats such as meadows and prairies. They are often used in landscaping for their ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

The genus includes several species, such as Amasonia tenuifolia and Amasonia tabernaemontana, which vary in growth habit and floral structure.
