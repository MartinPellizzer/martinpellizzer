Aster × cordifolius

Aster × cordifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaved aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Aster novi-belgii and Aster cordifolius, exhibiting characteristics of both parents. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are heart-shaped, with a rough texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Aster × cordifolius is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
