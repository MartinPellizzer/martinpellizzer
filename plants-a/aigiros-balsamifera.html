Aigiros balsamifera

Aigiros balsamifera, commonly known as the balsam tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, aromatic bark that exudes a balsamic resin when cut. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with broad, glossy leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, fleshy fruits.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.
