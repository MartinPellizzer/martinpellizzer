Aponogeton echinatus

Aponogeton echinatus, commonly known as the spiny arrowhead, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features elongated, arrow-shaped leaves with a distinctive spiny margin, which gives it its common name. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments and is often cultivated in ponds and aquariums.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for water gardening.
