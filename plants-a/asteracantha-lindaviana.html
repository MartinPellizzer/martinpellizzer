Asteracantha lindaviana

Asteracantha lindaviana, commonly known as the Lindau's aster, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and typically includes daisies and sunflowers. The plant features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Asteracantha lindaviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
