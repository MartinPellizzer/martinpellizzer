Aechmea blanchetii

Aechmea blanchetii, commonly known as the white-tipped pineapple plant, is a species of bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The plant produces vibrant orange or red flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Aechmea blanchetii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and air-purifying properties.

Morphology

Aechmea blanchetii has rosette-shaped leaves that are narrow, strap-like, and arranged in a compact spiral, with a bluish-gray color and a waxy texture.

The plant lacks a traditional stem, with the base of the leaves forming a tight rosette that holds water, and the surface is smooth with a slightly fibrous texture. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix surrounded by a colorful bract, typically in shades of red or orange, and bloom during the dry season, pollinated primarily by insects. The fruit is a berry that turns red when mature, dispersing seeds through animals that eat the fruit.

The roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.
