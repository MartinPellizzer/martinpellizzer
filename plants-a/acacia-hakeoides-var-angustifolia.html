Acacia hakeoides var. angustifolia

Acacia hakeoides var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features feathery, bipinnate leaves that are narrow and often densely arranged along the stems. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that appear in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its rapid growth and hardy nature make it a popular choice for erosion control and ornamental planting.
