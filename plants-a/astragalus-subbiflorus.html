Astragalus subbiflorus

Astragalus subbiflorus, commonly known as the two-flowered milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of desert ecosystems.
