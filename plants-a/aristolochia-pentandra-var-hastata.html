Aristolochia pentandra var. hastata

Aristolochia pentandra var. hastata, commonly known as the broad-leaved Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, broad leaves and unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as moths and bees. Its stems are woody and often have a distinctive, hollow structure.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.
