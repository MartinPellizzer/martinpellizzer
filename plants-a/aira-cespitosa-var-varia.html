Aira cespitosa var. varia

Aira cespitosa var. varia, commonly known as rough bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green blades that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in panicles, with small, fluffy florets that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in ecological restoration projects.
