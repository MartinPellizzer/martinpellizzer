Allium urceolatum

Allium urceolatum, commonly known as the basket onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal leaves that are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive rosette shape. The plant produces a tall, hollow stem topped with a cluster of small, white to pale purple flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, often used in culinary applications.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, basket-like leaf arrangement.
