Astragalus pseudotataricus

Astragalus pseudotataricus, commonly known as the Tatar milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

Astragalus pseudotataricus is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in arid regions.
