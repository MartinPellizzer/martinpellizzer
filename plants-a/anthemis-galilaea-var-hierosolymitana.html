Anthemis galilaea var. hierosolymitana

Anthemis galilaea var. hierosolymitana, commonly known as the Jerusalem chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

It is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
