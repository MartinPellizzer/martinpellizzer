Abutilon americanum

Abutilon americanum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or American abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Abutilon americanum is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and fields, and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
