Apocynum × vestitum

Apocynum × vestitum, commonly known as the hybrid dogbane, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
