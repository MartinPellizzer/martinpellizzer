Arthrophyllum lancifolium

Arthrophyllum lancifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arthrophyllum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in late summer. Arthrophyllum lancifolium thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Its foliage and floral structure make it an interesting subject for botanical study and a potential candidate for horticultural cultivation.
