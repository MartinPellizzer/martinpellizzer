Adiantum venustum var. monochlamys

Adiantum venustum var. monochlamys, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a delicate and ornamental fern species native to the southeastern United States.

It features finely divided, arching fronds with a distinctive silvery-white underside, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor the plant in its natural habitat.

It is valued in horticulture for its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in humid, indoor conditions.
