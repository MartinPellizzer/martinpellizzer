Aconitum atrox

Aconitum atrox, commonly known as the giant death flower, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a tall, perennial herb that can grow up to 2 meters in height, characterized by its large, showy blue or purple flowers. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed divisions, and its stems are smooth and often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

Aconitum atrox contains potent alkaloids, making it highly toxic and dangerous to both humans and animals.
