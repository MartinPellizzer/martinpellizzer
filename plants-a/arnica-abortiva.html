Arnica abortiva

Arnica abortiva, commonly known as meadow snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its medicinal properties.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
