Andropogon tamba

Andropogon tamba, commonly known as wild switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. This plant thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It is often found in open woodlands, prairies, and along roadsides.

Andropogon tamba is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
