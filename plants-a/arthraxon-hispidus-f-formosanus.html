Arthraxon hispidus f. formosanus

Arthraxon hispidus f. formosanus, commonly known as the Formosan hairy grass, is a variant of the hairy grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, spikelet-like flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered with short, stiff hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and riverbanks.
