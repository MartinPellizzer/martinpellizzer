Antimima ventricosa

Antimima ventricosa, commonly known as the balloon vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its inflated, balloon-like seed pods that give it its common name. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique morphology makes it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses.
