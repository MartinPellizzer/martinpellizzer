Aglaia cooperae

Aglaia cooperae, commonly known as the Java aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family, which includes species known for their aromatic wood and medicinal properties. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds, and it is often found in lowland and hill forest areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
