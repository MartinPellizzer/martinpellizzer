Alloteropsis amphistemon

Alloteropsis amphistemon, commonly known as the coastal grass or sea grass, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial grass that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in dune environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny flowers. It is adapted to salt spray and can tolerate harsh coastal conditions.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various small organisms.
