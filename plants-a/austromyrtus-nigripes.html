Austromyrtus nigripes

Austromyrtus nigripes, commonly known as the black-leaved myrtle, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subtropical regions.

Austromyrtus nigripes is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally by Indigenous Australians for various purposes.
