Acanthophyllum yasamin-nassehiae

Acanthophyllum yasamin-nassehiae, commonly known as the Yasamin-Nassehia's spiny sage, is a low-growing shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers during the summer months, which are adapted for pollination by wind and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
