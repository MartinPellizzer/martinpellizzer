Alyxia calophylla

Alyxia calophylla, commonly known as the glossy alyxia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which features glossy, dark green leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Alyxia calophylla thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
