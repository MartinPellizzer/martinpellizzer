Andropogon schoenanthus var. caesius

Andropogon schoenanthus var. caesius, commonly known as purple bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking purple tinge on its leaves and inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in open woodlands and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or reddish hues.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
