Aegiphila integrifolia var. lopez-palacii

Aegiphila integrifolia var. lopez-palacii, commonly known as the Lopez-Palacii's hollyhock, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features lanceolate, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and often have a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to rocky or arid environments.
