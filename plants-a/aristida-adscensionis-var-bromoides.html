Aristida adscensionis var. bromoides

Aristida adscensionis var. bromoides, commonly known as the bromoides lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces fluffy, feathery seed heads that are often mistaken for those of other grasses in the same region. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is often used in restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
