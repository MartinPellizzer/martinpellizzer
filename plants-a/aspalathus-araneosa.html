Aspalathus araneosa

Aspalathus araneosa, commonly known as the spider plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its legume characteristics. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soil.

Aspalathus araneosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
