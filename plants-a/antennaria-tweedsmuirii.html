Antennaria tweedsmuirii

Antennaria tweedsmuirii, commonly known as the Tweedsmuir's mouse-eared cowwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Scottish Highlands.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its tiny, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in heathland and moorland habitats. It has a compact growth habit, with stems that often lie close to the ground, forming dense mats.
