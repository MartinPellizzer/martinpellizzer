Acacia spinii

Acacia spinii, commonly known as the spinous acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and bipinnate leaves, which are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. Acacia spinii is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and fodder.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various desert animals.
