Acmella filipes var. parvifolia

Acmella filipes var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved spurge, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of South America.

It features a succulent stem with small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
