Abelia serrata var. sanguinea

Abelia serrata var. sanguinea, commonly known as the redleaf abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its attractive, serrated leaves that turn a striking red or orange-red in the fall. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in the summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This ornamental plant is popular in gardens for its vibrant foliage and showy blooms.
