Astragalus dainellii

Astragalus dainellii, commonly known as Dainell's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It produces small, hard seeds that are often dispersed by wind.

Astragalus dainellii is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and ecological significance in its native environment.
