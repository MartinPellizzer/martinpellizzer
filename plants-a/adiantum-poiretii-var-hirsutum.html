Adiantum poiretii var. hirsutum

Adiantum poiretii var. hirsutum, commonly known as the Hirsute Maidenhair Fern, is a fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble feathers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually dark green with a silvery underside, and the fronds are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a hirsute appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
