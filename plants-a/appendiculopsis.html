Appendiculopsis

Appendiculopsis, commonly known as the "flowering orchid" or "butterfly orchid," is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are characterized by their intricate, often showy flowers with specialized structures that aid in pollination. Appendiculopsis species are typically epiphytic, meaning they grow on the surfaces of trees or other plants without being parasitic. They are native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

Their botanical significance lies in their unique floral morphology and ecological roles within their native habitats.
