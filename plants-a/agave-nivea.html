Agave nivea

Agave nivea, commonly known as the white agave, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that can reach several meters in height, bearing small, white flowers. Agave nivea is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.
