Apocynum androsaemifolium var. obtusifolium

Apocynum androsaemifolium var. obtusifolium, commonly known as the bluntleaf dogbane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with rounded or blunt tips. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by orange to red berries. Its stems are often covered in fine hairs, and the leaves are typically glandular-dotted.

This species is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.
