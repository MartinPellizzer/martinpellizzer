Anticlea mexicana

Anticlea mexicana, commonly known as Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its leaves are ovate and hairy, while its fruits are capsule-like and contain numerous seeds.

Anticlea mexicana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
