Aspalathus purpurascens

Aspalathus purpurascens, commonly known as the rooibos tea plant, is a small shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems, small trifoliate leaves, and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is particularly noted for its unique ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it well adapted to the local climate. Its leaves are harvested and processed to produce the popular herbal tea known as rooibos.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat and is also valued for its economic significance in the tea industry.
