Amomum pratisthana

Amomum pratisthana, commonly known as the Greater Galangal, is a tropical plant belonging to the Zingiberaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous root system and tall, grass-like leaves. The plant produces inflorescences with small, pale yellow flowers that are often hidden within leaf sheaths. Its rhizomes are widely used in traditional medicine and as a spice in various cuisines.

The botanical features of Amomum pratisthana make it an important species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
