Alciope

Alciope, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-colored petals and a central cone-like structure. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer and early fall.

Alciope is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
