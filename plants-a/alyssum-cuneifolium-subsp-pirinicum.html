Alyssum cuneifolium subsp. pirinicum

Alyssum cuneifolium subsp.

pirinicum, commonly known as the Pirin alpine alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers.

The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators in high-altitude habitats.
