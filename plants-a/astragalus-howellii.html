Astragalus howellii

Astragalus howellii, commonly known as Howell's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a species of conservation concern in some areas.
