Artemisia tridentata var. spiciformis

Artemisia tridentata var. spiciformis, commonly known as the spiky tansy or spiny sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-green foliage and distinctive, spiky inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems. Its leaves are aromatic and contain essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal and historical uses.

It plays a vital role in providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
