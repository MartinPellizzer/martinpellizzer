Allium mareoticum

Allium mareoticum, commonly known as the Egyptian leek, is a perennial herb native to the Nile Delta region of Egypt.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives, and is characterized by its hollow, cylindrical leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy environments and has a distinct garlic-like aroma. Its bulbs are edible and have been used in traditional cuisine and medicine for centuries.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in ancient Egyptian culture.
