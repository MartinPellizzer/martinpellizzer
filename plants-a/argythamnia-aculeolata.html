Argythamnia aculeolata

Argythamnia aculeolata, commonly known as the spiny leaf tree, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its gray-green, sparsely hairy leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots and a capacity to store water.

Argythamnia aculeolata is often used in traditional medicine and for its ornamental value.
