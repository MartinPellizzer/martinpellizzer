Achillea erba-rotta subsp. calcarea

Achillea erba-rotta subsp.

calcarea, commonly known as the limestone yarrow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy foliage and flat, pinkish-white flower heads that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils, often found on limestone-rich habitats. It is part of the Asteraceae family and is known for its medicinal and ornamental uses.

Its common name reflects its preference for calcareous environments.
