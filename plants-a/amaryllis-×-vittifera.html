Amaryllis × vittifera

Amaryllis × vittifera, commonly known as the hybrid amaryllis, is a striking ornamental plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is a hybrid between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis paradisicola, resulting in a plant with vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. This plant is native to South America and thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its showy blooms and is popular in gardens and as a potted plant.
