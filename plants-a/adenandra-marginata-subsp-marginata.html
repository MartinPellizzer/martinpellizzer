Adenandra marginata subsp. marginata

Adenandra marginata subsp.

marginata, commonly known as the Cape rosewood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant produces woody, capsule-like fruits that contain small, flat seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in the timber industry, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
