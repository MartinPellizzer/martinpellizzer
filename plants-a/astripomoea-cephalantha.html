Astripomoea cephalantha

Astripomoea cephalantha, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling bells. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in coastal areas.

Astripomoea cephalantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
