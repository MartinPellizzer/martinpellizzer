Artemisia sacrorum var. santolinifolia

Artemisia sacrorum var. santolinifolia, commonly known as the sacred wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage with finely divided leaves that emit a strong, bitter scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that appear in late summer, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its medicinal and historical significance, it has been used in traditional herbal remedies for centuries.
