Adiantum pseudocajennense

Adiantum pseudocajennense, commonly known as the Brazilian maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fan-shaped fronds with a distinctive rachis. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often near streams or in humid forests. Its leaves are finely divided, with a light green color and a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Adiantum pseudocajennense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
