Actaea spicata var. acuminata

Actaea spicata var. acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped bugbane, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that bear clusters of small, white, spiky flowers. The leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands. It produces red-orange berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
