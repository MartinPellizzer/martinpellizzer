Armeria vulgaris var. rugica

Armeria vulgaris var. rugica, commonly known as the roughleaf thrift, is a low-growing, flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are typically gray-green and covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This plant is also valued for its ability to tolerate coastal conditions and its long-lasting blooms.
