Athyrium ebeneum

Athyrium ebeneum, commonly known as the broad-leaf Japanese forest fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, bipinnate leaves that are often dark green and glossy. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine in some Asian countries.

The fronds are known for their ornamental value and are sometimes cultivated in gardens.
