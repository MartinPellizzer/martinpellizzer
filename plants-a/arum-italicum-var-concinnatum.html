Arum italicum var. concinnatum

Arum italicum var. concinnatum, commonly known as the Italian arum or white arum, is a striking ornamental plant native to southern Europe.

It features a tall, leafy stem topped with a large, showy white spathe that encloses a spadix of small, densely packed flowers. The plant is part of the Araceae family, known for its unique inflorescence structure. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its dramatic foliage and floral display.

However, it can be toxic if ingested, containing calcium oxalate crystals.
