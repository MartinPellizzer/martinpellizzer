Abies vulgaris var. nana

Abies vulgaris var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf fir, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its slow growth, dense foliage, and small, needle-like leaves that are typically a dark green color. The plant produces cones that are small and cylindrical, containing edible seeds that are a food source for various wildlife.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, attractive clumps.
