Angraecum coutrixii

Angraecum coutrixii, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a rare and exquisite orchid species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with intricate patterns resembling celestial constellations. The plant produces long, slender spikes that bear numerous blooms, which are highly fragrant, especially at night. Angraecum coutrixii thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and unique beauty, it is a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts and conservationists.
