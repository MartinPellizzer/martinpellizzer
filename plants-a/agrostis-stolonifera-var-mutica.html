Agrostis stolonifera var. mutica

Agrostis stolonifera var. mutica, commonly known as silky bentgrass, is a low-growing, perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted blades and a dense, mat-forming growth habit, making it well-suited for ground cover. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are covered with short, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, lawns, and along streambanks.
