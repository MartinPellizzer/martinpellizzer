Arctotis semipapposa var. scabra

Arctotis semipapposa var. scabra, commonly known as the rough arctotis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves with a rough, hairy texture, which gives it its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
