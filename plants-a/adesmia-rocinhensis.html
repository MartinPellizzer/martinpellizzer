Adesmia rocinhensis

Adesmia rocinhensis, commonly known as the Brazilian burro's tail, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the dry season. Its root system is well adapted to nutrient-poor soils, allowing it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Adesmia rocinhensis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
