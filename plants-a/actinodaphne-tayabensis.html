Actinodaphne tayabensis

Actinodaphne tayabensis, commonly known as the Philippine laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark-barked trunk and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible drupes that are often used in local cuisine.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance in its native habitat.
