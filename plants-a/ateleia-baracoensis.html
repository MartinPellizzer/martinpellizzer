Ateleia baracoensis

Ateleia baracoensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cacao tree, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is native to the Amazon rainforest and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain bitter seeds used in the production of cocoa. It thrives in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it is a key species in the biodiversity of the region.
