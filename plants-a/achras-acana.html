Achras acana

Achras acana, commonly known as the Natal plum or Natal date, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by fleshy, orange-red fruits resembling small dates. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a serrated margin.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.
