Aloe haworthii

Aloe haworthii, commonly known as the Haworth's aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, gray-green leaves that are covered in a white powdery substance called farina. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Aloe haworthii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
