Aceratium gracile

Aceratium gracile, commonly known as the slender acanthus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Aceratium gracile is a perennial species that produces seeds through its showy inflorescences.
