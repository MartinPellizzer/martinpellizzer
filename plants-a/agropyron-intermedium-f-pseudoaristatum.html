Agropyron intermedium f. pseudoaristatum

Agropyron intermedium f. pseudoaristatum, commonly known as the intermediate wheatgrass, is a grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces spikelets that are often clustered at the ends of the stems, with the lemma bearing a distinctive awn. It is a perennial species that thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Its botanical features make it an important forage crop and a valuable component of native plant communities.
