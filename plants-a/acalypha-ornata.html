Acalypha ornata

Acalypha ornata, commonly known as the Chinese nettle or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that range from red to purple, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with lobed edges, and it produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Acalypha ornata is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
