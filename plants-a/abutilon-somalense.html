Abutilon somalense

Abutilon somalense, commonly known as the Somali velvetleaf, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Somalia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in hot, dry climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
