Acalypha chamaedrifolia var. pendula

Acalypha chamaedrifolia var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping copperleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its pendulous growth habit, with long, drooping stems that bear clusters of small, copper-colored bracts. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by the showy bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value comes from its distinctive foliage and unique growth form.
