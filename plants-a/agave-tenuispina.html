Agave tenuispina

Agave tenuispina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette growth form and long, narrow, blue-green leaves with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces an impressive inflorescence of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

Agave tenuispina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
