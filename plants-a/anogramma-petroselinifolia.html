Anogramma petroselinifolia

Anogramma petroselinifolia, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of fern native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, delicate fronds with a serrated edge, and its spores are released from specialized structures called sori. It is often found in high-altitude environments where it contributes to the unique biodiversity of these ecosystems.

Anogramma petroselinifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in rocky habitats.
