Astragalus chrysanthus var. elbursensis

Astragalus chrysanthus var. elbursensis, commonly known as the Elburs mountain milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

This variety is of interest to botanists and conservationists due to its limited distribution and ecological significance.
