Audibertia humilis

Audibertia humilis, commonly known as the dwarf audibertia, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 15-30 centimeters and features delicate, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers with five petals, blooming from late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Audibertia humilis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
