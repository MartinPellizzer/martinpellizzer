Acetosa integrifolia

Acetosa integrifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf sorrel, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with smooth, undivided margins, which give it its specific epithet "integrifolia.". The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Acetosa integrifolia contains oxalic acid, contributing to its tart flavor and potential medicinal uses.
