Allium cepa var. luteum

Allium cepa var. luteum, commonly known as the yellow onion, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous structure.

The plant produces a tall, hollow stem with narrow, grass-like leaves, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in a spherical inflorescence. The bulb, which is the main edible part, is rich in sulfur compounds that contribute to its pungent flavor. It is widely cultivated for culinary use and is also valued for its medicinal properties.

This variety is known for its vibrant yellow color and is often used in traditional and modern cooking across various cultures.
