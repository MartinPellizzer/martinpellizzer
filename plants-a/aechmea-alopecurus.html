Aechmea alopecurus

Aechmea alopecurus, commonly known as the curly leaf bromeliad, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant features striking, spiky inflorescences that bloom in shades of red, orange, or yellow. Its leaves are thick and leathery, with a waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

Aechmea alopecurus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in indoor environments.
