Anguloa × ruckeri

Anguloa × ruckeri, commonly known as the hybrid anguloa, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Anguloa subulata and Anguloa lycioides, characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant pink or purple blooms that are highly attractive to pollinators. It is native to the Andean regions of South America, where it thrives in cool, humid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
