Asparagus pectinatus

Asparagus pectinatus, commonly known as the slender asparagus, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fine, feather-like leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that mature to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
