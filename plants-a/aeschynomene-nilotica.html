Aeschynomene nilotica

Aeschynomene nilotica, commonly known as slender sickle grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated pods containing small, kidney-shaped seeds. Aeschynomene nilotica is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It also has potential applications in soil remediation and as a forage crop.
