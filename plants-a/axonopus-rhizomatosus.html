Axonopus rhizomatosus

Axonopus rhizomatosus, commonly known as creeping spikerush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has a rhizomatous growth habit, forming dense mats of foliage that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny flowers. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread vegetatively make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
