Achyrophorus tenuifolius var. gayanus

Achyrophorus tenuifolius var. gayanus, commonly known as the Gayanus sandmat, is a low-growing, succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features fleshy, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette formation, adapted to store water and reduce transpiration. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
