Astragalus alienus

Astragalus alienus, commonly known as the alien milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces cylindrical seed pods that contain numerous tiny, hard seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry environments with deep root systems and minimal water requirements.

Astragalus alienus is often found in rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
