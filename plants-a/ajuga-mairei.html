Ajuga mairei

Ajuga mairei, commonly known as Maire's bugleweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Valerianaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and hairy, and it produces clusters of blue to purple flowers in the spring. Ajuga mairei is well adapted to rocky and shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking hardy, shade-tolerant plants.
