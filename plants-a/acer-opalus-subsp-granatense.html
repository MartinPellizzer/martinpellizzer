Acer opalus subsp. granatense

Acer opalus subsp.

granatense, commonly known as the Granate maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

Its compact size and striking seasonal color changes make it a popular choice for landscaping.
