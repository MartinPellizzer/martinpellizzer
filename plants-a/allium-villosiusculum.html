Allium villosiusculum

Allium villosiusculum, commonly known as the woolly garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous root system and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in the summer months. Its name derives from the fine, hairy texture of its leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.

Allium villosiusculum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has mild culinary uses in some regions.
