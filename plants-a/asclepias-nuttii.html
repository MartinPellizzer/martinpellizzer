Asclepias nuttii

Asclepias nuttii, commonly known as the Nuttall's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by seed pods containing silky-haired seeds. Asclepias nuttii is a host plant for the monarch butterfly and plays an important role in supporting pollinators.

It thrives in rocky, dry habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
