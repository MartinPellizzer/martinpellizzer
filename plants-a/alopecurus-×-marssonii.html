Alopecurus × marssonii

Alopecurus × marssonii, commonly known as creeping marsh grass, is a hybrid species belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many grasses. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, meadows, and along stream banks.

Alopecurus × marssonii is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects.
