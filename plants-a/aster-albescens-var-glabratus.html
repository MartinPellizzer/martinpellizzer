Aster albescens var. glabratus

Aster albescens var. glabratus, commonly known as the white aster, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and a compact growth habit, often forming dense clumps. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
