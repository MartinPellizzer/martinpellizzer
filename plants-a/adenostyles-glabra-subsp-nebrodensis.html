Adenostyles glabra subsp. nebrodensis

Adenostyles glabra subsp.

nebrodensis, commonly known as the Nebrodensis false oat, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky soils.

The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in summer. It is characterized by its glabrous (smooth) surface and compact growth habit.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats such as slopes and gravelly fields.
