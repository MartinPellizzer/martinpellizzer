Acaena krausei var. massonandra

Acaena krausei var. massonandra, commonly known as the New Zealand thistle, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its spiny, bristly stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces achene fruits that are often covered in hooked bristles, aiding in seed dispersal by attaching to animals. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including coastal areas and disturbed soils, and is often found in grasslands and shrublands.

Due to its invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain regions.
