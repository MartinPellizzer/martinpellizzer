Alstroemeria gayana

Alstroemeria gayana, commonly known as the Peruvian lily, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has strap-like leaves that are often used in horticultural settings. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

The flowers are also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
