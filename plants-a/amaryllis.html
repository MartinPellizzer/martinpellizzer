Amaryllis

Amaryllis, commonly known as the belladonna lily, is a genus of flowering plants in the Amaryllidaceae family.

These plants are native to South America and are characterized by their large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors such as red, white, and pink. The leaves are typically broad, strap-like, and arranged in a rosette pattern. Amaryllis bulbs are perennial and can be grown in both indoor and outdoor settings.

They thrive in well-draining soil and require bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
