Alchemilla amicorum

Alchemilla amicorum, commonly known as the friendship plant, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. Its flowers are tiny, greenish-yellow, and appear in clusters, typically blooming in early summer.

Alchemilla amicorum is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in moist, shady conditions.
