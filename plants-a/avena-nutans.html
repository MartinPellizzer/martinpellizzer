Avena nutans

Avena nutans, commonly known as the drooping oat, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces oval, three-angled fruits that contain single, dark brown seeds. Avena nutans is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a common species in agricultural and ecological studies.
