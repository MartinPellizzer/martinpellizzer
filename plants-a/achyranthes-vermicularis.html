Achyranthes vermicularis

Achyranthes vermicularis, commonly known as red root, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its roots are thick and fleshy, which contribute to its common name.

Achyranthes vermicularis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
