Akebia quinata f. diplochlamys

Akebia quinata f. diplochlamys, commonly known as the double-flowered akebia, is a unique cultivar of the Japanese vine.

It is characterized by its striking, double flowers that are larger and more showy than those of the typical species. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy underside and produces small, red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in landscape design.
