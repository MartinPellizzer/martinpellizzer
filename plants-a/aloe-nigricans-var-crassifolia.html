Aloe nigricans var. crassifolia

Aloe nigricans var. crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The leaves are typically adorned with white or yellowish spots and have a waxy texture. It produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, yellowish-green flowers.

This plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with plenty of sunlight.
