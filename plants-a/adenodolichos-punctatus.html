Adenodolichos punctatus

Adenodolichos punctatus, commonly known as the spotted cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Its root system has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Adenodolichos punctatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its distinctive leaf patterns.
