Aconitum delphiniifolium subsp. pavlovae

Aconitum delphiniifolium subsp.

pavlovae, commonly known as the Pavlov's delphiniifolium aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant features palmately compound leaves with deeply lobed segments, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
