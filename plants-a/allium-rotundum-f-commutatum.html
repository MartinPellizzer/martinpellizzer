Allium rotundum f. commutatum

Allium rotundum f. commutatum, commonly known as the Armenian garlic or curled leek, is a botanical variant of the common garlic species.

It is characterized by its bulbous, rounded cloves and a distinctive, curled appearance of its leaves. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to the Caucasus region. It is valued for its pungent flavor and is often used in culinary applications.

Its botanical features make it a unique and sought-after variety in both horticulture and gastronomy.
