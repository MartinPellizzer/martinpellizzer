Aira capillaris var. corsica

Aira capillaris var. corsica, commonly known as Corsican feathergrass, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, hair-like leaves that form dense, low-growing clumps. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in marginal areas.
