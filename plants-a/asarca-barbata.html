Asarca barbata

Asarca barbata, commonly known as the hairy asarum, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and woodland edges. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a unique, intricate structure that attracts pollinators like flies and beetles.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional herbal remedies for centuries.
