Agave megalacantha

Agave megalacantha, commonly known as the giant Mexican agave, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, spiky leaves that form a rosette shape, often reaching several meters in height. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that can bear numerous white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Agave megalacantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in traditional Mexican practices.
