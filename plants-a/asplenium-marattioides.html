Asplenium marattioides

Asplenium marattioides, commonly known as the Marattioid spleenwort, is a fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, lance-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed or divided. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests or rocky crevices. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in ferns.

Asplenium marattioides is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, showcasing its adaptability to humid climates.
