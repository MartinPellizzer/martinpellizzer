Alchemilla cashmeriana

Alchemilla cashmeriana, commonly known as the Kashmir stonecress, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with basal leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, and its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Alchemilla cashmeriana thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in mountain ecosystems.
