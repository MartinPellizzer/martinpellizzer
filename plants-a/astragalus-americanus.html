Astragalus americanus

Astragalus americanus, commonly known as the American milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. The plant has compound leaves and produces small, yellow or greenish flowers in late spring. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
