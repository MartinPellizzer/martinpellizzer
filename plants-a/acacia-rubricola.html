Acacia rubricola

Acacia rubricola, commonly known as the red acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are usually gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Acacia rubricola thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and fuelwood.
