Ascolepis protea subsp. rhizomatosa

Ascolepis protea subsp.

rhizomatosa, commonly known as the rhizomatous protea, is a perennial shrub native to the coastal regions of South Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, showy inflorescences composed of numerous tubular flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape.

The plant possesses a rhizomatous root system, which allows it to spread vegetatively and form dense clumps. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered with a waxy coating to reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
