Anthocephalus indicus var. glabrescens

Anthocephalus indicus var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth anthocephalus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and has lance-shaped leaves that are often glabrous or nearly hairless. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by three-lobed, orange to reddish fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
