Anticharis linearis

Anticharis linearis, commonly known as the slender anticharis, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and wetlands. Its linear, lance-shaped leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer.

Anticharis linearis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
