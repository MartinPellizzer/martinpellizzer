Astragalus lasioglottis var. subacaulis

Astragalus lasioglottis var. subacaulis, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and pinnately compound leaves with small, oval leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow to greenish-yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky habitats.

This variety is noted for its drought tolerance and is sometimes used in restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
