Allocarya mollis var. vestita

Allocarya mollis var. vestita, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically a deep green, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces showy, pale pink to lavender flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its fibrous roots and robust growth habit make it adaptable to a variety of soil types.

However, it is often considered invasive in some regions due to its rapid spread and competitive nature.
