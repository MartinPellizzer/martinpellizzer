Alonsoa honoraria

Alonsoa honoraria, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Alonsoa honoraria is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
