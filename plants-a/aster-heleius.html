Aster heleius

Aster heleius, commonly known as the evening aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing showy purple or blue flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Aster heleius thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking hardy, ornamental plants.

Its foliage is usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
