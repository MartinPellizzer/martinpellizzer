Actinidia rubricaulis var. coriacea

Actinidia rubricaulis var. coriacea, commonly known as the rough-stemmed actinidia, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown stems that become rough and fissured with age. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by edible, bright red berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin and a glossy upper surface.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in herbal medicine.
