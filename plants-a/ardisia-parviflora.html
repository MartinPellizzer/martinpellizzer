Ardisia parviflora

Ardisia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are a distinctive feature. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
