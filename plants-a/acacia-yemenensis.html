Acacia yemenensis

Acacia yemenensis, commonly known as the Yemen acacia, is a small, shrub-like tree native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched trunk and dense foliage of small, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in cylindrical spikes, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh, dry conditions.

Acacia yemenensis is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and forage.
