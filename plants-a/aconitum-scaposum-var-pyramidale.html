Aconitum scaposum var. pyramidale

Aconitum scaposum var. pyramidale, commonly known as the pyramidal aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features a tall, slender stem with alternate, palmately compound leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
