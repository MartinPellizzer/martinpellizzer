Allium oreoscordum

Allium oreoscordum, commonly known as the alpine leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives, and is characterized by its bulbous base and grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Allium oreoscordum is often used in traditional herbal medicine and is valued for its mild, garlic-like scent.
