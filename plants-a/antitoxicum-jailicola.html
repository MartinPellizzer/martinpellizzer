Antitoxicum jailicola

Antitoxicum jailicola, commonly known as the toxic shield plant, is a rare species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its roots are known to contain compounds with mild detoxifying properties, which have been studied for potential medicinal use.

However, due to its toxic nature, it is not recommended for cultivation or consumption without expert guidance.
