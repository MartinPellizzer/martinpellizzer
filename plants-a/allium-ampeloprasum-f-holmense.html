Allium ampeloprasum f. holmense

Allium ampeloprasum f. holmense, commonly known as the giant garlic or purple flowering onion, is a botanical variant of the leek family.

It is characterized by its large, bulbous stem and striking purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and hollow, resembling those of leeks, while its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
