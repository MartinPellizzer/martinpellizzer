Artemisia vulgaris subsp. flodmanii

Artemisia vulgaris subsp.

flodmanii, commonly known as the flodman's wormwood, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late summer.

The plant is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands, rocky soils, and disturbed areas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its strong, camphor-like scent.

This subspecies is typically smaller than the main species, with shorter stems and narrower leaves.
