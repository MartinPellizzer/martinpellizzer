Acanthospermum xanthioides var. obtusifolium

Acanthospermum xanthioides var. obtusifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is often found in disturbed soils. Its leaves are typically broad and have a slightly waxy texture, while the stems are erect and branched.
