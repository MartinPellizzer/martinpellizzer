Aldama nesomii

Aldama nesomii, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped blooms that emit a strong scent to attract nocturnal pollinators. It is a perennial shrub that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its unique blooming habit and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
