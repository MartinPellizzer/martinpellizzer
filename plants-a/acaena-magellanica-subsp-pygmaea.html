Acaena magellanica subsp. pygmaea

Acaena magellanica subsp.

pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf bull's-eyes, is a low-growing shrub native to South America. It features small, spiny stems and produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant's fruit is a small, hard achene enclosed in a spiny bract, typical of the genus Acaena. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in maritime climates.

Due to its compact size and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
