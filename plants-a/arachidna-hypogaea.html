Arachidna hypogaea

Arachidna hypogaea, commonly known as the peanut, is an annual flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its underground pods that contain the edible seeds. The plant has compound leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into the familiar peanut pods. Its roots have a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

The peanut is widely cultivated for its high oil and protein content, making it an important crop globally.
