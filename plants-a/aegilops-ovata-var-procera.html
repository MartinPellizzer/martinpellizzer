Aegilops ovata var. procera

Aegilops ovata var. procera, commonly known as the tall wild oat, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces spikelets with long, narrow lemmas and palea, which are typical features of the Poaceae family. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a rough texture and prominent veins.

Aegilops ovata var. procera is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
