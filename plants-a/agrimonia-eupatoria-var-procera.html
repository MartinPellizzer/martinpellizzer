Agrimonia eupatoria var. procera

Agrimonia eupatoria var. procera, commonly known as tall agrimony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from June to August, and its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and serrated. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

The plant is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
