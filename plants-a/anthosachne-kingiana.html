Anthosachne kingiana

Anthosachne kingiana, commonly known as the King's grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of grass species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
