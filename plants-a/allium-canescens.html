Allium canescens

Allium canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf garlic, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its gray-green, narrow leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures similar to garlic, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Allium canescens thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it an ornamental and useful plant in both horticulture and herbal applications.
