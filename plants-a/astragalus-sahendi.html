Astragalus sahendi

Astragalus sahendi, commonly known as Sahndi milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, lens-shaped seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to harsh climates.

Astragalus sahendi is valued for its medicinal properties and is also used as forage for livestock.
