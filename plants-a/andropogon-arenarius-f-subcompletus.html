Andropogon arenarius f. subcompletus

Andropogon arenarius f. subcompletus, commonly known as the sand bluestem grass, is a variant of the sand bluestem species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant typically thrives in sandy or well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites. Its inflorescence consists of a dense panicle, which produces small, spikelet-like flowers.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological adaptability and role in stabilizing sandy soils.
