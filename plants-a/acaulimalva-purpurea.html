Acaulimalva purpurea

Acaulimalva purpurea, commonly known as the purple mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a sprawling growth habit with hairy stems and heart-shaped, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy, pale purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics include a lack of a distinct stem base, hence the species name "acaulis.".
