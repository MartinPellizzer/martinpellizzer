Acanthopale cuneifolia

Acanthopale cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acanthopale, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
