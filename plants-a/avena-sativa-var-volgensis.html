Avena sativa var. volgensis

Avena sativa var. volgensis, commonly known as the slender oat, is a variant of the common oat species characterized by its narrow leaves and reduced grain size.

It is native to temperate regions of Europe and is typically found in grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers. Its botanical features make it suitable for certain ecological restoration projects.

However, it is less cultivated than other oat varieties due to its lower yield and agronomic challenges.
