Aristea uhehensis

Aristea uhehensis, commonly known as the mountain aristea, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in a compact cluster, often with a yellowish or brownish hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts.
