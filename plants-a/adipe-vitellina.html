Adipe vitellina

Adipe vitellina, commonly known as the golden eggplant, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive, egg-shaped fruits.

The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes and peppers, and is native to tropical regions of South America. Its fruits are typically yellow or orange in color, with a thick, waxy skin that protects the fleshy interior. The leaves are broad and lobed, often with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant can grow up to 2 meters in height.

Adipe vitellina is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its potential culinary uses in traditional dishes.
