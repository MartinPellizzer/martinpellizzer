Allium narcissiflorum var. minus

Allium narcissiflorum var. minus, commonly known as the small narcissus garlic.

It is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that resemble daffodils, hence its common name. Its foliage consists of strap-like, grassy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color.

The bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a valued plant in both ornamental and culinary contexts.
