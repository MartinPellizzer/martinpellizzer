Astragalus quisqualis f. arcuatus

Astragalus quisqualis f. arcuatus, commonly known as the wavy milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
