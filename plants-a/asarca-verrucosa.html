Asarca verrucosa

Asarca verrucosa, commonly known as the warty asarum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and unique, warty, reddish-brown flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, and the plant is known for its aromatic, often spicy scent.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
