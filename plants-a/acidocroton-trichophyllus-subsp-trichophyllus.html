Acidocroton trichophyllus subsp. trichophyllus

Acidocroton trichophyllus subsp.

trichophyllus, commonly known as the hairy-leaved croton, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage.
