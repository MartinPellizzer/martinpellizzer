Aconitum arcuatum subsp. axilliflorum

Aconitum arcuatum subsp.

axilliflorum, commonly known as the alpine aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its stems are usually unbranched and somewhat woody at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
