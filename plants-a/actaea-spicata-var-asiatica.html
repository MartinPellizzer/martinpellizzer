Actaea spicata var. asiatica

Actaea spicata var. asiatica, commonly known as the Asian whitebane, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its upright, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces clusters of white, spicate flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
