Adenandra rugosa

Adenandra rugosa, commonly known as the rough adenandra, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, bark-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Adenandra rugosa is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique foliage and winter flowering habit.
