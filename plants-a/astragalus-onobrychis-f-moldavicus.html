Astragalus onobrychis f. moldavicus

Astragalus onobrychis f. moldavicus, commonly known as the Moldavian yellow vetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility, and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
