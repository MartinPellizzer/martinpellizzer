Acacia pulviniformis

Acacia pulviniformis, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Its seeds are encased in a hard, pod-like structure that splits open when mature.

Acacia pulviniformis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.
