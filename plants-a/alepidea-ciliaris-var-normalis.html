Alepidea ciliaris var. normalis

Alepidea ciliaris var. normalis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed daisy bush, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, thriving in full sun.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and aesthetic value.
