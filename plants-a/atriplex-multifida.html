Atriplex multifida

Atriplex multifida, commonly known as the narrowleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in saline soils, making it an important species in ecological restoration projects.

Atriplex multifida plays a vital role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
