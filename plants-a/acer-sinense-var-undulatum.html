Acer sinense var. undulatum

Acer sinense var. undulatum, commonly known as the wavy-leaf maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, undulating leaf margins that give it a unique appearance. The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a bright green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red samaras in the fall.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic foliage.
