Astragalus eginensis

Astragalus eginensis, commonly known as the Eginian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has hairy stems and produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds.

Astragalus eginensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native range.
