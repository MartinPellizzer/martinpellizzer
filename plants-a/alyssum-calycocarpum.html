Alyssum calycocarpum

Alyssum calycocarpum, commonly known as the rock alpine alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand harsh alpine conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine habitats.
