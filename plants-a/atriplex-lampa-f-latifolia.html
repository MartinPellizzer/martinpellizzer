Atriplex lampa f. latifolia

Atriplex lampa f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf saltbush, is a variant of the saltbush species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its broad, fleshy leaves that are adapted to retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant has a low, spreading growth habit and is often found in saline soils. Its leaves are typically gray-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Atriplex lampa f. latifolia is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and is used in ecological restoration projects.
