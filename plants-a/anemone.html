Anemone

Anemone, commonly known as the windflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Ranunculaceae.

These plants are characterized by their showy, often pink or white, single or double flowers that resemble small, delicate blossoms. Anemone species are typically found in temperate regions and thrive in moist, well-drained soils. They have palmate or lobed leaves and produce seeds in a cluster of follicles.

The name "anemone" is derived from the Greek word for wind, reflecting the plant's sensitivity to air movement.
