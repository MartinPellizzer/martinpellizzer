Anoectochilus luzonensis

Anoectochilus luzonensis, commonly known as the Luzon toothed-orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
