Angraecum filicornu

Angraecum filicornu, commonly known as the long-horned orchid, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers with long, slender sepals and petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are pollinated by moths, which are attracted to its nectar and the shape of its floral structure.

Angraecum filicornu is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
