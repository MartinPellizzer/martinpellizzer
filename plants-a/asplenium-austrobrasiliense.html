Asplenium austrobrasiliense

Asplenium austrobrasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian spleenwort, is a member of the fern family (Polypodiaceae).

It is a small, epiphytic fern that typically grows on trees in humid tropical regions. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a dark green color and a waxy texture. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Asplenium austrobrasiliense is often found in shaded, moist environments throughout South America.
