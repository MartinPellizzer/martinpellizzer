Aster speciosus

Aster speciosus, commonly known as the showy aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands and meadows, reaching heights of up to 2 feet. Its showy purple to blue flowers bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.
