Ageratina rivalis

Ageratina rivalis, commonly known as the whiteface ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom atop slender, branched stems. The leaves are usually opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
