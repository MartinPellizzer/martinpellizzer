Acacia heteroneura var. jutsonii

Acacia heteroneura var. jutsonii, commonly known as the Jutson's wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
