Atriplex suberecta

Atriplex suberecta, commonly known as the silverleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which helps reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, enhancing its drought tolerance. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Atriplex suberecta is well adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.
