Allium valdesianum

Allium valdesianum, commonly known as the Valdesian garlic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, round bulbs that are typically used as a spice in traditional cuisines.

Botanically, it is noted for its aromatic sulfur compounds, which contribute to its distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.
