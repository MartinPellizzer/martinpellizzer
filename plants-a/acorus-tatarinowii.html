Acorus tatarinowii

Acorus tatarinowii, commonly known as Japanese sweetflag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features a rhizomatous root system and narrow, sword-shaped leaves that grow from a central rosette. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of streams.

Acorus tatarinowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
