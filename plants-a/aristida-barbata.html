Aristida barbata

Aristida barbata, commonly known as the barbed wire grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Aristida barbata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
