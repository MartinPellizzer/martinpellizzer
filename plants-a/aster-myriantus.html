Aster myriantus

Aster myriantus, commonly known as the many-flowered aster, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with a central yellow disk. The plant produces numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a dense, spherical cluster, which gives it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Aster myriantus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
