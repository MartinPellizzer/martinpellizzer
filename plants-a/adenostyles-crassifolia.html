Adenostyles crassifolia

Adenostyles crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved adenostyle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a slightly wavy margin.

Adenostyles crassifolia is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
