Aristida mauritiana var. glabripetala

Aristida mauritiana var. glabripetala, commonly known as the glabrous-petaled false grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically glabrous, or smooth to the touch. The inflorescence consists of a loose panicle bearing small, spikelet-like flowers, which are often pale green to brown in color. This plant thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in open grasslands or rocky areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
