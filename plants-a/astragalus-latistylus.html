Astragalus latistylus

Astragalus latistylus, commonly known as the broad-styled milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Astragalus latistylus is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
