Astragalus owerinii

Astragalus owerinii, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in dry and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are compound, consisting of several leaflets. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Astragalus owerinii is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh climates.
