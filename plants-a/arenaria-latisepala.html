Arenaria latisepala

Arenaria latisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepaled sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It typically features dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in tundra and mountainous habitats.

Its botanical structure is adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.
