Allium bucharicum

Allium bucharicum, commonly known as the Bukhara onion, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Allium bucharicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
