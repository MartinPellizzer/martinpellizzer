Amaranthus enervis

Amaranthus enervis, commonly known as the slender amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features upright stems that are often branched and covered with fine hairs. The leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and typically have a bluish-green hue when young. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or red, depending on the variety.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in some traditional cuisines.
