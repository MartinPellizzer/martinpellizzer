Alchemilla erythropoda

Alchemilla erythropoda, commonly known as the red-stemmed lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces upright flowering stems that bear clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Alchemilla erythropoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
