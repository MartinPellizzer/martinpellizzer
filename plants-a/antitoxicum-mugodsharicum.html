Antitoxicum mugodsharicum

Antitoxicum mugodsharicum, commonly known as the Mugodsharic poison ivy, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with three leaflets, often arranged oppositely along the stem. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the spurge family. Its sap contains toxic compounds that can cause skin irritation in humans.

It is native to specific regions in Central Asia and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
