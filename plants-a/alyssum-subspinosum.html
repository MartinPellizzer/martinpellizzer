Alyssum subspinosum

Alyssum subspinosum, commonly known as the spiny alyssum, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, and the stems are often spiny, giving it its common name.

Alyssum subspinosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
