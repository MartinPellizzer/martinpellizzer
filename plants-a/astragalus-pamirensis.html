Astragalus pamirensis

Astragalus pamirensis, commonly known as the Pamir milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including the Pamir and Tien Shan ranges.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions with its deep root system.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential medicinal properties.
