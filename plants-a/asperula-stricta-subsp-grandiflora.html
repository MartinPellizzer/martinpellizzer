Asperula stricta subsp. grandiflora

Asperula stricta subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered sweet woodruff, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features slender, arching stems that bear small, fragrant, white flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and rocky areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a shade-tolerant, flowering plant.
