Aspilia tomentosa

Aspilia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly aster, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, white, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a compact, spherical inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Aspilia tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
