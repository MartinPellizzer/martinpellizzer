Allium tribracteatum var. andersonii

Allium tribracteatum var. andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's onion, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, pale purple to white flowers in the summer. The plant is typically found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a specimen plant.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a delicate and somewhat challenging plant to grow in cultivation.
