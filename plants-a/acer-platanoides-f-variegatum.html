Acer platanoides f. variegatum

Acer platanoides f. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated Norway maple, is a cultivar of the Norway maple tree.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves, which display a mix of green, white, and cream colors. The plant is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a moderate height, making it suitable for smaller gardens. Its foliage provides ornamental value throughout the growing season.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, thriving in temperate climates.
