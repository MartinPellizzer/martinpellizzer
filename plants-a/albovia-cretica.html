Albovia cretica

Albovia cretica, commonly known as the Cretan white bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly rough texture.

Albovia cretica is typically found in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun.
