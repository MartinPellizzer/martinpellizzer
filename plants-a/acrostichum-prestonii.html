Acrostichum prestonii

Acrostichum prestonii, commonly known as the New Zealand tree fern, is a distinctive fern species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its spores are dispersed by wind, contributing to its propagation in suitable habitats.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native ecosystem, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
