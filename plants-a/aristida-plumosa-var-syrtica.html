Aristida plumosa var. syrtica

Aristida plumosa var. syrtica, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its fine, soft, and silky foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in low, dense tufts, often forming mats on rocky or sandy soils. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
