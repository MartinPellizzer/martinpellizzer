Acia amara

Acia amara, commonly known as bitter cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, shady environments. Its seeds are often dispersed by water, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Acia amara is known for its peppery taste, which is due to the presence of glucosinolates.
