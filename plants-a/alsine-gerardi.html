Alsine gerardi

Alsine gerardi, commonly known as Carolina germander, is a low-growing annual herb native to the eastern United States.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and a distinctive, branched root system. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along streambanks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaranthaceae family, which includes many plants with showy flowers and edible species.
