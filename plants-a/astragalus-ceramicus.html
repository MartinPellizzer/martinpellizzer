Astragalus ceramicus

Astragalus ceramicus, commonly known as the ceramic milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Astragalus ceramicus is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and efficient water use through its deep root system.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
