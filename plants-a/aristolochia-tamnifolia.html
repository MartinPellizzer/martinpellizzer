Aristolochia tamnifolia

Aristolochia tamnifolia, commonly known as the Indian birthwort, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, aromatic leaves and produces distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often purple or greenish in color. The plant is characterized by its complex, winding stems and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers attract insects, particularly flies, which play a role in its pollination.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds and has been used in traditional medicine.
