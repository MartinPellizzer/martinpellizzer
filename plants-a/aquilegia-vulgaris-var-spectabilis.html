Aquilegia vulgaris var. spectabilis

Aquilegia vulgaris var. spectabilis, commonly known as the showy lady's mantle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features striking, spurred flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in a fine layer of down. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a robust stem that supports its showy blooms.
