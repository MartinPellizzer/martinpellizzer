Anthistiria semiberbis

Anthistiria semiberbis, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that primarily grows on the branches of coniferous trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stems. The plant produces small white or greenish flowers, followed by berries that are often eaten by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It is native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can weaken its host trees if left unchecked.
