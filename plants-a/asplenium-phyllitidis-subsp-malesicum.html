Asplenium phyllitidis subsp. malesicum

Asplenium phyllitidis subsp.

malesicum, commonly known as the maleficent spleenwort, is a fern native to Southeast Asia. It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments.

The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a glossy surface and a distinctive, segmented appearance. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
