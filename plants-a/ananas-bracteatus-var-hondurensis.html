Ananas bracteatus var. hondurensis

Ananas bracteatus var. hondurensis, commonly known as the Honduras pineapple, is a bromeliad native to Central America.

It features a rosette of thick, waxy leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces a striking inflorescence with bright red flowers, which are followed by a spiky, fleshy fruit. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to adapt to indoor growing conditions.
