Athanasia minuta subsp. minuta

Athanasia minuta subsp.

minuta, commonly known as the dwarf immortelle, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and numerous tiny, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its delicate appearance and long-lasting flowers, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
