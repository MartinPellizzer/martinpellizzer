Arenaria chamissonis

Arenaria chamissonis, commonly known as sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the coastal regions of the northern hemisphere.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in dunes and rocky coastal areas. The plant produces small, white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize coastal soils.
