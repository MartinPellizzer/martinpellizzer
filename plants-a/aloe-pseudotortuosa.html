Aloe pseudotortuosa

Aloe pseudotortuosa, commonly known as the twisted aloe, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often curved or twisted, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with clusters of yellow or orange flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The gel inside its leaves is sometimes used for its soothing properties, though it is not as widely used as other aloe species.
