Aegiphila steyermarkii var. obtusifolia

Aegiphila steyermarkii var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved aegiphila, is a perennial shrub native to the highlands of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive grayish-green color. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches.

This species is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often growing in open, sunny habitats at higher elevations.
