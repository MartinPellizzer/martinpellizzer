Astracantha imbricata

Astracantha imbricata, commonly known as the fringed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has palmate leaves with a rough, hairy texture and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Astracantha imbricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
