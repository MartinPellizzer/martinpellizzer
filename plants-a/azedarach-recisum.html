Azedarach recisum

Azedarach recisum, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lilac-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement, and its bark is smooth and grayish-brown. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release its seeds.
