Adhatoda orbicularis

Adhatoda orbicularis, commonly known as the Indian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, orbicular-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinctive waxy coating. Its flowers are trumpet-shaped, with five lobes, and are typically blue or violet in color.

Adhatoda orbicularis is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.
