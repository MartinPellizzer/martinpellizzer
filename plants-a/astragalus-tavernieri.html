Astragalus tavernieri

Astragalus tavernieri, commonly known as the Turkish milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it thrives in dry, rocky habitats.

Astragalus tavernieri is often found in alpine and subalpine environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
