Anacolus

Anacolus, commonly known as the spiny spurge, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and leaves, which are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Anacolus species are typically found in arid or semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions.
