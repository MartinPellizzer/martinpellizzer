Aloe deltoideodonta subsp. fallax

Aloe deltoideodonta subsp.

fallax, commonly known as the Cape aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a distinctive white powdery coating called farina.

The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellowish-green flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and for their soothing properties.
