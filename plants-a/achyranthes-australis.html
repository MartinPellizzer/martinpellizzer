Achyranthes australis

Achyranthes australis, commonly known as the Indian knotgrass, is a perennial herb native to the warmer regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by distinctive, three-lobed seed pods. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
