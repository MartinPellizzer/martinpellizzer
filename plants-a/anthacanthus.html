Anthacanthus

Anthacanthus, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, slender stalks rising from water. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that provide shade over the water's surface. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The sacred lotus is also symbolically significant in various cultures and religions.
