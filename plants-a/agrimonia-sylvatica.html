Agrimonia sylvatica

Agrimonia sylvatica, commonly known as wood agrimony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and serrated leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are dry, achene-like structures that are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Agrimonia sylvatica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
