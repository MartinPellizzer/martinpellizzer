Achyronychia parryi

Achyronychia parryi, commonly known as the Parry's acanthus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its striking, spiny leaves and showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
