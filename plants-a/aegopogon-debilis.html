Aegopogon debilis

Aegopogon debilis, commonly known as the slender goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, leafless stems with whorled leaves that are hairy and lance-shaped. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a dense cluster at the top of the stem.

Aegopogon debilis typically grows in grassy areas and is adapted to dry, sandy soils.

Morphology

Aegopogon debilis has slender, hairy stems that are typically greenish or reddish in color, growing in a spreading or ascending pattern.

Its leaves are alternate, linear to lanceolate, and have a pale green color with a slightly hairy surface. The flowers are small, clustered in loose inflorescences, with white or pale blue petals and yellow stamens, blooming in early spring and pollinated by wind. The fruit is a small capsule that disperses seeds by wind.
