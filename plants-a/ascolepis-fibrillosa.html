Ascolepis fibrillosa

Ascolepis fibrillosa, commonly known as the fibrous pincushion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly inflorescence that resembles a pincushion. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers surrounded by soft, fibrous bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Ascolepis fibrillosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
