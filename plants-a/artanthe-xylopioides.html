Artanthe xylopioides

Artanthe xylopioides, commonly known as the mountain flax, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from white to pink.

Artanthe xylopioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
