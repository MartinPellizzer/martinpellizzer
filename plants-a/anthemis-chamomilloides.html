Anthemis chamomilloides

Anthemis chamomilloides, commonly known as wild chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, while its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
