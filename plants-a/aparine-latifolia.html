Aparine latifolia

Aparine latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf burrweed, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, serrated leaves and produces small, greenish flowers that develop into burrs with hooked spines. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is also known for its ability to spread rapidly through its seed-like burrs, which adhere to clothing and animal fur.
