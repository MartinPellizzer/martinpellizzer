Ageratina ocanensis

Ageratina ocanensis, commonly known as the Ocanensis blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy inflorescences that resemble clouds, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental value.

Ageratina ocanensis is valued in horticulture for its ability to attract pollinators and its aesthetic appeal in garden settings.
