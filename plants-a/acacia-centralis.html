Acacia centralis

Acacia centralis, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stem and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact spikes, which are followed by flat, leathery pods containing seeds. Acacia centralis thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is often used in traditional medicine and has ecological significance as a source of food for various animals.
