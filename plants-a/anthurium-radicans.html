Anthurium radicans

Anthurium radicans, commonly known as the climbing anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that can grow quite large. The plant produces vibrant spadices, which are the colorful, spadix-like structures that house the actual flowers. Anthurium radicans is a vining plant that can climb using its aerial roots, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets or trained shelves.

Its foliage and unique inflorescence make it a visually appealing addition to indoor gardens.
