Asplenium aethiopicum subsp. tripinnatum

Asplenium aethiopicum subsp.

tripinnatum, commonly known as the African maidenhair fern, is a fern species native to parts of Africa. It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that give it a lacy appearance.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or rocky areas. Its rhizomes are often covered with a layer of brown, fuzzy tissue.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
