Aconitum shensiense

Aconitum shensiense, commonly known as the Szechuan aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy flowers. The plant produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are compound with serrated edges. Aconitum shensiense contains potent alkaloids, making it highly toxic and requiring careful handling.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
