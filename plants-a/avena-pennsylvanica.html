Avena pennsylvanica

Avena pennsylvanica, commonly known as wild oat, is a annual grass native to North America.

It grows in a variety of habitats, including roadsides, fields, and disturbed soils. The plant produces slender, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. Its leaves are long, narrow, and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green color.

The inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster, and the plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.
