Actinoseris polyphylla

Actinoseris polyphylla, commonly known as the many-leaved sunflower, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with a distinctive center of bracts, which resemble a cluster of small petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Actinoseris polyphylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in xerophytic gardens.
