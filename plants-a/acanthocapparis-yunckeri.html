Acanthocapparis yunckeri

Acanthocapparis yunckeri, commonly known as the desert thornapple, is a shrub native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and spiny stems. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits containing several seeds.

It is well adapted to dry conditions, with deep roots and a slow growth rate.
