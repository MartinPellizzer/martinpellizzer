Arabis nordmanniana

Arabis nordmanniana, commonly known as the Caucasian rock cress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to mountainous environments. It is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it blooms in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
