Amerimnon benthamianum

Amerimnon benthamianum, commonly known as the Benthamic daisy, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers with white ray florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Amerimnon benthamianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
