Atropis preslii subsp. breviculmis

Atropis preslii subsp.

breviculmis, commonly known as the short-stemmed marsh gentian, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in Europe. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its shorter stems and compact growth habit compared to other members of the species. It typically blooms in late summer and relies on specific moisture conditions for successful growth.

The plant plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in peat bogs and marshy areas.
