Alibum

Alibum, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Alibum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
