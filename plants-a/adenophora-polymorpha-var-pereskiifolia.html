Adenophora polymorpha var. pereskiifolia

Adenophora polymorpha var. pereskiifolia, commonly known as the Korean bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland or rocky areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

This variety is a subspecies of Adenophora polymorpha, distinguished by its unique floral characteristics and growth habits.
