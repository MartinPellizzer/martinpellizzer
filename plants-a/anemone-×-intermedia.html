Anemone × intermedia

Anemone × intermedia, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a hybrid perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Anemone × intermedia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
