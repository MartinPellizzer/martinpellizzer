Achyranthes tenella

Achyranthes tenella, commonly known as smallleaf senna, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
