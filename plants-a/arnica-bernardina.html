Arnica bernardina

Arnica bernardina, commonly known as the alpine tansy, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are dark green and slightly hairy, while its flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

Arnica bernardina is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
