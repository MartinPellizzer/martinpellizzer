Acer saccharum var. viride

Acer saccharum var. viride, commonly known as the green sugar maple, is a variant of the sugar maple native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive green leaves, which remain vibrant throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its natural propagation. Its leaves turn a striking golden hue in the fall, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
