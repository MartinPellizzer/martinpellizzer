Amaranthus albus

Amaranthus albus, commonly known as white amaranth, is an annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that develop into spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. White amaranth is often cultivated for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients such as protein and iron.

It thrives in warm climates and is adaptable to various soil types.
