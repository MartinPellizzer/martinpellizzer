Acanthorrhinum ramosissimum

Acanthorrhinum ramosissimum, commonly known as the hairy goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is considered a hardy species adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
