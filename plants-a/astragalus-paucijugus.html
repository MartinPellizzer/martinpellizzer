Astragalus paucijugus

Astragalus paucijugus, commonly known as the few-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has adapted to arid environments through its deep root system.

Its small, pod-like fruits contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ecological role in stabilizing soils.
