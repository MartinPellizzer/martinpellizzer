Aniseia cernua f. parviflora

Aniseia cernua f. parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf anise plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features slender, ascending stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage has a mild anise-like fragrance, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and suitability in rock gardens or as a border plant.
