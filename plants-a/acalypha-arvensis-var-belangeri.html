Acalypha arvensis var. belangeri

Acalypha arvensis var. belangeri, commonly known as the velvetleaf or belanger's acalypha, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are typically inconspicuous. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread make it both a common weed and a potential ornamental plant.
