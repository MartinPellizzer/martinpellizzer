Actinoschoenus aphyllus

Actinoschoenus aphyllus, commonly known as the leafless star grass, is a unique flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves, with its photosynthetic function carried out by its cylindrical, grass-like stems. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or arid regions.

Due to its specialized morphology, it is of interest to botanists studying adaptations to harsh environments.
