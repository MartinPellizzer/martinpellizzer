Allium ledebourianum var. maximowiczii

Allium ledebourianum var. maximowiczii, commonly known as the Siberian leek, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a rosette of broad, grass-like leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces a tall, flowering stem topped with a dense cluster of white to pale purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
