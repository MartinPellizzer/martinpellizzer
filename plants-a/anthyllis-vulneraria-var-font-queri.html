Anthyllis vulneraria var. font-queri

Anthyllis vulneraria var. font-queri, commonly known as the Font-Queri's Scotch thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has palmate leaves with a hairy surface, and its root system is fibrous and adapted to rocky or dry soils. It thrives in sunny, well-drained habitats and is often found in coastal areas and limestone formations.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local insect populations.
