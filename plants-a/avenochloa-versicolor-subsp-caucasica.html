Avenochloa versicolor subsp. caucasica

Avenochloa versicolor subsp.

caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian variable grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually green or yellowish, and it is adapted to temperate climates.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in its native range.
