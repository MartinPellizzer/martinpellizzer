Angelica arenaria

Angelica arenaria, commonly known as the sand angelica, is a perennial herb native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in cold, nutrient-poor environments. The plant has tall, slender stems that bear compound leaves with finely divided, silvery-white undersides. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are arranged in a complex, branched inflorescence.

Angelica arenaria is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
