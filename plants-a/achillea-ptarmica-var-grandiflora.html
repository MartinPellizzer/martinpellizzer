Achillea ptarmica var. grandiflora

Achillea ptarmica var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Great Flowered Yarrow, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, silver-gray leaves and produces clusters of small, flat, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is well adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Its flowering period extends from late spring to early autumn, making it a valuable addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
