Alstroemeria pulchra f. liliacina

Alstroemeria pulchra f. liliacina, commonly known as the lavender-flowered alstroemeria, is a hybrid cultivar of the alstroemeria plant.

It is characterized by its striking lavender-pink flowers with a subtle white base, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to South America and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements. It features strap-like, narrow leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for container gardening.

The f. liliacina form is prized for its unique coloration and adaptability to various growing conditions.
