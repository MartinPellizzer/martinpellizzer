Anthurium clarinervium

Anthurium clarinervium, commonly known as the broad-veined anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent, broad veins that give it its common name. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-shaped inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in humid, warm environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Anthurium clarinervium is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
