Adenophora thunbergiana f. totoki

Adenophora thunbergiana f. totoki, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland areas and streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
