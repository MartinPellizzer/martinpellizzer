Amsinckia condensata

Amsinckia condensata, commonly known as the desert gourd, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy, ribbed stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by spiny, spherical fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it an important species in ecological studies of desert ecosystems.
