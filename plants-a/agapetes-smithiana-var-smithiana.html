Agapetes smithiana var. smithiana

Agapetes smithiana var. smithiana, commonly known as the Smith's silver leaf.

It is a species of evergreen shrub native to the high elevations of the Andes in South America. The plant is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that reflects light and gives it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

The flowers are small and white, appearing in clusters, and the plant is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
