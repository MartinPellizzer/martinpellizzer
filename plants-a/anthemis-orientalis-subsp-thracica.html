Anthemis orientalis subsp. thracica

Anthemis orientalis subsp.

thracica, commonly known as the Thrace hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
