Acacia rossei

Acacia rossei, commonly known as the Rosse's acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, fern-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in pod-like fruits that split open when mature, dispersing the seeds by wind.

Acacia rossei is valued for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments.
