Aquilegia viscosa subsp. montsicciana

Aquilegia viscosa subsp.

montsicciana, commonly known as the sticky corydalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, spurred flowers that typically range in color from pink to red.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in early to mid-summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and the plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
