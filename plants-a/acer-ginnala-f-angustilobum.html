Acer ginnala f. angustilobum

Acer ginnala f. angustilobum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves with narrow, elongated lobes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
