Arum mucronatum

Arum mucronatum, commonly known as the narrow-sheath arum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and a striking, spadix-bearing inflorescence. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique floral structures. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
