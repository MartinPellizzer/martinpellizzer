Achillea × girgioensis

Achillea × girgioensis, commonly known as the hybrid yarrow, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Achillea millefolium (common yarrow) and Achillea × triennis (tall yarrow). The plant is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and flat, composite flower heads that range in color from white to pink. It typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Achillea × girgioensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
