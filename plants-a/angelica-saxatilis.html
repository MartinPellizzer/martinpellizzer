Angelica saxatilis

Angelica saxatilis, commonly known as the rock angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool, moist conditions. The plant features tall, slender stems with large, compound leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in a fine, white down. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, producing a mild, aromatic scent.

Angelica saxatilis is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
