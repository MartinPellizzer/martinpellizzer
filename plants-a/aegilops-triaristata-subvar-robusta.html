Aegilops triaristata subvar. robusta

Aegilops triaristata subvar. robusta, commonly known as the robust wild rye, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its robust growth habit and distinctive three-branched spikelets. The plant produces small, awned florets that are typically arranged in dense clusters along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
