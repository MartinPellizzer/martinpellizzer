Azalea hirsuta

Azalea hirsuta, commonly known as the woolly azalea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white during the spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial shade to full sun.

Azalea hirsuta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
