Angraecum schimperianum

Angraecum schimperianum, commonly known as the Schimper's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a short, hairy rhizome and produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale blue. The plant is adapted to grow in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers have a unique structure with long, slender sepals and petals, and it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

Angraecum schimperianum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
