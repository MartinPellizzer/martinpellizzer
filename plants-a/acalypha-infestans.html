Acalypha infestans

Acalypha infestans, commonly known as the red-rooted spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking red stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Acalypha infestans is also known for its ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative propagation.
