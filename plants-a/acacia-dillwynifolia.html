Acacia dillwynifolia

Acacia dillwynifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular heads, which are arranged in clusters along the branches. Its leaves are narrow and feathery, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Acacia dillwynifolia is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
