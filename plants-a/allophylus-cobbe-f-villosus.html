Allophylus cobbe f. villosus

Allophylus cobbe f. villosus, commonly known as the hairy alderleaf, is a botanical variant of the Allophylus cobbe species.

It is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America, particularly in Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, typically arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round fruits.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
