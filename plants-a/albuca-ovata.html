Albuca ovata

Albuca ovata, commonly known as the eggplant lily, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features strap-like, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, often used in traditional cuisine.

Albuca ovata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
