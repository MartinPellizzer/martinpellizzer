Aganope macrophylla

Aganope macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf aganope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
