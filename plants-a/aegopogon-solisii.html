Aegopogon solisii

Aegopogon solisii, commonly known as the sun's grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to southern Africa, this plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The plant is adapted to full sun exposure and is relatively drought-tolerant.
