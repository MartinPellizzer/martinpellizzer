Aegopogon solisii

Aegopogon solisii, commonly known as the sun's grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to southern Africa, this plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The plant is adapted to full sun exposure and is relatively drought-tolerant.

Morphology

Aegopogon solisii has leaves that are narrow and lanceolate, arranged alternately along the stem, with a bright green color and a slightly hairy surface.

The stem is slender, hairy, and grows upright, with a light green bark that remains smooth throughout its life. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, featuring white petals and yellow stamens, blooming in late spring to early summer, and are pollinated by insects. The fruit is a dry capsule that releases small seeds through mechanical means when mature.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, helping the plant anchor in sandy or rocky soils.
