Aphyllanthes monspeliensis

Aphyllanthes monspeliensis, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry environments. It is often found in sandy or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is considered an important species for ecological studies in desert ecosystems.
