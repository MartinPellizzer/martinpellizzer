Astragalus macropodius

Astragalus macropodius, commonly known as the large-horned milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellowish to greenish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to dry environments and has a deep root system to access water.

Astragalus macropodius plays a role in stabilizing soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
