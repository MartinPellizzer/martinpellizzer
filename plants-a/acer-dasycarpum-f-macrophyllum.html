Acer dasycarpum f. macrophyllum

Acer dasycarpum f. macrophyllum, commonly known as the giant maple, is a variant of the hairy maple.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 15 centimeters in length. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of China and is typically found in temperate climates. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage.
