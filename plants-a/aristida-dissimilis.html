Aristida dissimilis

Aristida dissimilis, commonly known as the slender dropseed, is a perennial grass species native to the central and western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact, cylindrical seed heads that are often tinged with red or purple. This plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, rocky slopes, and sandy soils. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Aristida dissimilis is also valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
