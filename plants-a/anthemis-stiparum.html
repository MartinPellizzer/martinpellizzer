Anthemis stiparum

Anthemis stiparum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and produce a strong, often minty scent.

Anthemis stiparum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
