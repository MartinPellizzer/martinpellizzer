Astragalus valesiacus

Astragalus valesiacus, commonly known as the Valesian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus valesiacus is often found in alpine and steppe habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
