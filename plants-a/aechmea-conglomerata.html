Aechmea conglomerata

Aechmea conglomerata, commonly known as the pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, waxy leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-like flowers that are often surrounded by colorful bracts. Its foliage is typically green to bluish-green, with some varieties displaying red or purple hues.

Aechmea conglomerata is popular as a decorative plant due to its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in various indoor environments.

Morphology

Aechmea conglomerata has leaves that are sword-shaped, arranged in a rosette, with a waxy blue-green color and a leathery texture.

The plant lacks a traditional stem, but its basal rosette forms a compact, tight structure with a dark, almost bark-like surface. Its flowers are clustered in a dense inflorescence, with purple and pink petals, blooming during the dry season and pollinated by birds, particularly hummingbirds. The fruit is a small, berry-like structure that turns red when mature, dispersing seeds through both animal consumption and wind.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for absorbing moisture from the surrounding soil.
