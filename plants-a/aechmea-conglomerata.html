Aechmea conglomerata

Aechmea conglomerata, commonly known as the pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, waxy leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-like flowers that are often surrounded by colorful bracts. Its foliage is typically green to bluish-green, with some varieties displaying red or purple hues.

Aechmea conglomerata is popular as a decorative plant due to its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in various indoor environments.
