Amitostigma monanthum

Amitostigma monanthum, commonly known as the single-flower lily, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, showy flowers. The plant typically produces a single, large, bell-shaped flower that is often white or pale pink in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
