Alpinia manostachys

Alpinia manostachys, commonly known as the giant ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, rhizomatous growth habit and large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

Alpinia manostachys is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of aromatic rhizomes.
