Acalypha caturoides

Acalypha caturoides, commonly known as red hibiscus or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy red bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often surrounded by the vibrant bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

Acalypha caturoides is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds and vegetative propagation.
