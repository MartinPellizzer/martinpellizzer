Aristolochia pallida subsp. pallida

Aristolochia pallida subsp.

pallida, commonly known as the pale Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States. It features heart-shaped, aromatic leaves with a distinctive waxy texture and a deep green color.

The plant produces unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-white with a strong, musky scent. Its fruit is a dark blue, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and historical use in traditional medicine.
