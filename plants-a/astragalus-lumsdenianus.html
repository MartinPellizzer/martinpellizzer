Astragalus lumsdenianus

Astragalus lumsdenianus, commonly known as Lumsden's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, harsh environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
