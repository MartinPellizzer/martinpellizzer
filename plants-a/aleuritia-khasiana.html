Aleuritia khasiana

Aleuritia khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces clusters of small, dark purple berries that are edible and used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are elliptical with a smooth margin and a glossy surface.

Aleuritia khasiana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at elevations between 1,500 and 2,500 meters.
