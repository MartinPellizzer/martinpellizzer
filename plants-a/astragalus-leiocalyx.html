Astragalus leiocalyx

Astragalus leiocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous oval-shaped leaflets, and its root system is typically deep and fibrous, adapted to survive in dry conditions. It is often found in rocky soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its role in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
