Amyris abeggii

Amyris abeggii, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its fragrance and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by three-winged fruits.

Amyris abeggii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
