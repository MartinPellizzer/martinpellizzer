Artemisia maritima proles gallica

Artemisia maritima proles gallica, commonly known as sea wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to coastal regions of Europe and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in maritime climates. Its leaves contain compounds such as thujone and camphor, which contribute to its strong, medicinal scent.

Artemisia maritima proles gallica is valued for its historical use in herbal medicine and as a natural insect repellent.
