Acacia trichopetala

Acacia trichopetala, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and distinctive, flat, yellowish-green pods. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Acacia trichopetala is also valued for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes used in local crafts and construction.
