Anthurium gymnopus

Anthurium gymnopus, commonly known as the velvet leaf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are often deep green with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, spadix-shaped flowers surrounded by a spathe, which can range in color from white to reddish-brown. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium gymnopus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
