Annona jahnii

Annona jahnii, commonly known as the Mexican apple or sweet annona, is a tropical plant native to Central America and Mexico.

It is a small tree or shrub that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces large, green, fleshy fruits that turn yellow when ripe, containing a single large seed. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow with a strong, unpleasant odor, attracting pollinators like flies and beetles.

The fruit is edible, with a sweet and acidic flavor, and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.
