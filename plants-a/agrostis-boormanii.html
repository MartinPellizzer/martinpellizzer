Agrostis boormanii

Agrostis boormanii, commonly known as Boorman's bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has fine, tufted leaves that are usually less than 10 cm in length, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities in landscaping.
