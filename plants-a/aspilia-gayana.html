Aspilia gayana

Aspilia gayana, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flower heads surrounded by pale green bracts. The plant typically grows as a shrub or perennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and sometimes hairy, while its flowers bloom in clusters.

Aspilia gayana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
