Annona pachyantha

Annona pachyantha, commonly known as the custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces large, fragrant, yellow-green fruits with a rough, spiny exterior. Its leaves are broad, oval, and arranged alternately along the stems.

The flowers are white or greenish-white, with a strong scent, and are followed by the characteristic custard apple fruit.
