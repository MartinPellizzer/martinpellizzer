Andropogon hallii var. bispicatus

Andropogon hallii var. bispicatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, blue-green leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common component of prairie and woodland ecosystems.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
