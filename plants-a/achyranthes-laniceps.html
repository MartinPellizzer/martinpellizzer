Achyranthes laniceps

Achyranthes laniceps, commonly known as the hairy knotweed, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, hairy stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically borne in the upper leaf axils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and often covered with fine hairs.

Achyranthes laniceps is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Morphology

Achyranthes laniceps has leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape, arranged alternately along the stem, with a moderate size ranging from 5 to 10 cm in length, and a dark green color with a slightly hairy upper surface.

The stem is erect and cylindrical, with a smooth, greenish-brown bark that becomes rougher with age, and it exhibits a branching growth pattern. The flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, with greenish-white petals and stamen, blooming during the summer months, and are primarily pollinated by wind. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, kidney-shaped seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and extensive, helping to stabilize the plant and absorb nutrients from the soil.
