Anthriscus sylvestris proles alpina

Anthriscus sylvestris proles alpina, commonly known as Alpine cow parsley, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of theApiaceae family. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of high-altitude ecosystems.
