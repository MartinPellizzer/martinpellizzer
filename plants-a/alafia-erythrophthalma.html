Alafia erythrophthalma

Alafia erythrophthalma, commonly known as the red-eyed cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant features a stout, unbranched trunk with a thick, fibrous outer layer. Its leaves are large, pinnately compound, and have a striking reddish hue at the base.

The plant produces cycad-like cones, with the female cones being particularly showy and often adorned with a reddish coloration.
