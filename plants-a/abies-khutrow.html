Abies khutrow

Abies khutrow, commonly known as the Khutrow fir, is a coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged spirally along its branches. The tree produces cones that are typically cylindrical and contain seeds enclosed in woody scales. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Abies khutrow is adapted to high-altitude environments and plays a significant role in the ecosystems of its native habitat.
