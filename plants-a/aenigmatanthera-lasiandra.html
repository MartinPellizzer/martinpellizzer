Aenigmatanthera lasiandra

Aenigmatanthera lasiandra, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a dense, fuzzy mass. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives in the understory. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Due to habitat loss, this rare orchid is considered endangered in its native range.
