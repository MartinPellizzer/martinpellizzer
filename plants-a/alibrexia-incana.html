Alibrexia incana

Alibrexia incana, commonly known as the silver-haired alibrexia, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. Alibrexia incana thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
