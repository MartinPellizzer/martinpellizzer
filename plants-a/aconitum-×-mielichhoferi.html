Aconitum × mielichhoferi

Aconitum × mielichhoferi, commonly known as the Mielichhofer'saconite, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, conical clusters at the top of the stems. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance.

Aconitum × mielichhoferi is known for its ornamental value in gardens but is also toxic due to the presence of potent alkaloids.
