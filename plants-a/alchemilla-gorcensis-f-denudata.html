Alchemilla gorcensis f. denudata

Alchemilla gorcensis f. denudata, commonly known as the naked alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage is noted for its medicinal properties, containing compounds with potential anti-inflammatory and healing effects.
