Ambaiba leucocoma

Ambaiba leucocoma, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives on the forest floor or as an epiphyte on trees. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers display a unique white and green coloration with intricate veining.

Ambaiba leucocoma is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
