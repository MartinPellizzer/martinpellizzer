Acacia leiocalyx subsp. herveyensis

Acacia leiocalyx subsp.

herveyensis, commonly known as the Hervey's wattle, is a shrub native to northern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and produces small, yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring.

The leaves are phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that function as leaves, and they are typically narrow and linear. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

It is a hardy species that contributes to local biodiversity and is of interest in ecological studies.
