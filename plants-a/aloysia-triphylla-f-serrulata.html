Aloysia triphylla f. serrulata

Aloysia triphylla f. serrulata, commonly known as the serrated verbena, is a hybrid plant species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and pairs of opposite, serrated leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in subtropical and temperate climates.
