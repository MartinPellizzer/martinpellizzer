Arthroclianthus caudatus

Arthroclianthus caudatus, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
