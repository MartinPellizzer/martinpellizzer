Acacia hirsuta

Acacia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and pairs of small, oval leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. Acacia hirsuta is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species that thrives in dry conditions.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of browse for livestock.
