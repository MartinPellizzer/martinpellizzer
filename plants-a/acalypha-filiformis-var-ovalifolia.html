Acalypha filiformis var. ovalifolia

Acalypha filiformis var. ovalifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on short, hairy pedicels. Its growth habit is prostrate or spreading, making it suitable for ground cover in shaded or moist environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
