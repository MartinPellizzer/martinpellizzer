Andropogon tetra-aristatus

Andropogon tetra-aristatus, commonly known as the four-awned grass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with four awns, which are bristle-like structures that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a potential indicator of water quality.
