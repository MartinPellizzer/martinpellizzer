Aristida adscensionis var. normalis

Aristida adscensionis var. normalis, commonly known as the common wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 millimeters wide. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
