Aceratium brassii

Aceratium brassii, commonly known as the African snowbush, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink and purple during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, Aceratium brassii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
