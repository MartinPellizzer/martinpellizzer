Augustea anomala

Augustea anomala, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant typically grows as a shrub or herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Augustea anomala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
