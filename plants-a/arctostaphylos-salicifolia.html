Arctostaphylos salicifolia

Arctostaphylos salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, red or orange berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Arctostaphylos salicifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
