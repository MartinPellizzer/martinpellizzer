Adiantum venustum var. wuliangense

Adiantum venustum var. wuliangense, commonly known as the Wuliang maidenhair fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to China.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, bi-pinnate fronds that are often covered with a thin layer of white hairs. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments and is typically found in mountainous regions with rich, moist soil. Its leaves are usually a vibrant green, and it produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
