Appunia tenuiflora var. leiophylla

Appunia tenuiflora var. leiophylla, commonly known as the smooth-leaved appunia, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This plant is often found in rocky outcrops and sandy soils, making it a hardy component of local flora.
