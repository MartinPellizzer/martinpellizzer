Acalypha juruana

Acalypha juruana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Brazilian nettle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, slightly hairy leaves that are often a vibrant green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments.

Acalypha juruana thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.
