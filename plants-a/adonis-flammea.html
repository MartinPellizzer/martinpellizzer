Adonis flammea

Adonis flammea, commonly known as the flame bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear clusters of bright orange-red, bell-shaped flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Adonis flammea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
