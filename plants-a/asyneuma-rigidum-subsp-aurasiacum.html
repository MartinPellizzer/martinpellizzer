Asyneuma rigidum subsp. aurasiacum

Asyneuma rigidum subsp.

aurasiacum, commonly known as the rigid aster, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features upright, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, while the flowers are small, blue to purple, and arranged in composite heads.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
