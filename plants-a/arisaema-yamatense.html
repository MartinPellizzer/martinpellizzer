Arisaema yamatense

Arisaema yamatense, commonly known as the Yamato arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are arrowhead-shaped, which is a key identifying feature.

The flowers are usually greenish-yellow, and the plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
