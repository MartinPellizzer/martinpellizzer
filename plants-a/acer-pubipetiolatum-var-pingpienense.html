Acer pubipetiolatum var. pingpienense

Acer pubipetiolatum var. pingpienense, commonly known as the Pingshui maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant has smooth, gray bark and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
