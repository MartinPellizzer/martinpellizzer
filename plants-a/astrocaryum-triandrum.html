Astrocaryum triandrum

Astrocaryum triandrum, commonly known as the Brazilian buriti palm, is a tall, slender palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and produces yellowish-orange fruits that are clustered in bunches. The plant's trunk is typically unbranched and covered with a fibrous, spongy material. Its fruits are an important food source for various wildlife, including birds and mammals.

The palm is also valued for its hard, durable wood and has cultural significance among indigenous communities.
