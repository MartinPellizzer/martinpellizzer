Adiantum caudatum var. angustilobatum

Adiantum caudatum var. angustilobatum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species characterized by its finely divided, dark green fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and Georgia. The plant features small, finger-like pinnae with a distinctive, narrow shape that gives it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or wooded areas.

The fronds are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, contributing to its intricate and elegant appearance.
