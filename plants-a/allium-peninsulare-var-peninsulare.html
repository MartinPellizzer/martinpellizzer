Allium peninsulare var. peninsulare

Allium peninsulare var. peninsulare, commonly known as the Peninsular garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves and forms a dense, low-growing rosette. Its bulbs are typically small and have a mild garlic flavor, making them useful in culinary applications.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other allium species like garlic and onions.
