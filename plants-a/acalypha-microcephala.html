Acalypha microcephala

Acalypha microcephala, commonly known as velvetleaf or redleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep red to green, often with a distinctive purple underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellow, which bloom in the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

Acalypha microcephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
