Acer pseudoplatanus f. grandicorne

Acer pseudoplatanus f. grandicorne, commonly known as the giant-horned maple, is a distinctive variant of the common maple.

It is characterized by its large, elongated bracts that resemble horns, giving it its unique appearance. The plant is a deciduous tree or shrub with palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This form is a natural mutation of the parent species, showcasing the genetic diversity within the Acer genus.
