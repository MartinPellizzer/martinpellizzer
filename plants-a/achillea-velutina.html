Achillea velutina

Achillea velutina, commonly known as silky yarrow, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long flowering period and drought tolerance.
