Acrocomia horrida

Acrocomia horrida, commonly known as the spiny date palm, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clusters of edible, sweet fruits. Its spiny leaves and robust structure make it well adapted to dry, coastal environments.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential as a source of sustainable food and fiber.
