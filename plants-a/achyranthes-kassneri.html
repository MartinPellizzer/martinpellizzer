Achyranthes kassneri

Achyranthes kassneri, commonly known as the purple borage, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in dense clusters. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and erect.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
