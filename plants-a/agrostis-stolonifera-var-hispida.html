Agrostis stolonifera var. hispida

Agrostis stolonifera var. hispida, commonly known as silvergrass, is a variant of the tufted hairgrass species.

It is characterized by its silver-blue foliage and dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces slender, cylindrical spikelets that are typically arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
