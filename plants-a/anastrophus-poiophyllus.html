Anastrophus poiophyllus

Anastrophus poiophyllus, commonly known as the moonlight fern, is a rare and intriguing species native to the misty highlands of Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its delicate, bioluminescent fronds that emit a soft, blue-green glow during the night, a unique adaptation for attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are highly specialized, featuring a complex network of vascular tissues that allow for efficient water transport in its humid, shaded habitat. The plant's reproductive structures are concealed within protective sheaths, which only open during specific environmental conditions.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, Anastrophus poiophyllus is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
