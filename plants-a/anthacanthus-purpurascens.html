Anthacanthus purpurascens

Anthacanthus purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered anthacanthus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple blooms, which appear in dense clusters during the warmer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
