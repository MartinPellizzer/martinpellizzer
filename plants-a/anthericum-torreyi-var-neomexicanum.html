Anthericum torreyi var. neomexicanum

Anthericum torreyi var. neomexicanum, commonly known as the New Mexico arum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features a distinctive, arrowhead-shaped leaf with a waxy, light green surface and a prominent midrib. The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a spathe, a characteristic feature of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and woodland areas.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a shade-tolerant species with potential ecological and ornamental value.
