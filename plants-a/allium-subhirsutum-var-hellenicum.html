Allium subhirsutum var. hellenicum

Allium subhirsutum var. hellenicum, commonly known as the Greek leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its onion-like bulbs, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other allium species such as garlic and chives.
