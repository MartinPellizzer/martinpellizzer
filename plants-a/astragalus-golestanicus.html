Astragalus golestanicus

Astragalus golestanicus, commonly known as Golestan milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential medicinal properties.

Its seeds are often used in traditional herbal medicine.
