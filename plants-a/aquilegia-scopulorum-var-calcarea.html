Aquilegia scopulorum var. calcarea

Aquilegia scopulorum var. calcarea, commonly known as the limestone columbine, is a perennial plant native to rocky, calcareous habitats in Europe.

It features showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, palmately arranged leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in limestone-rich environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
