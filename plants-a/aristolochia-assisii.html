Aristolochia assisii

Aristolochia assisii, commonly known as the Brazilian pipevine, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and its unique, intricate flowers that resemble a pipe or a horn. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the early to mid-summer. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
