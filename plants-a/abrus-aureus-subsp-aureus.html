Abrus aureus subsp. aureus

Abrus aureus subsp.

aureus, commonly known as the golden robin, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The plant produces distinctive seed pods that are long and slender, containing seeds with a bright red aril. These seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental elements in gardens.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
