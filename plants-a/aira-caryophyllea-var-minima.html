Aira caryophyllea var. minima

Aira caryophyllea var. minima, commonly known as the dwarf purple grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green with a powdery coating, and it produces small, spike-like flower clusters in shades of purple or pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its compact size and drought tolerance make it an excellent choice for xeriscaping and sustainable landscaping.
