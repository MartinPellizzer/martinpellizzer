Anthurium corrugatum

Anthurium corrugatum, commonly known as the corrugated anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with a waxy, corrugated texture. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-shaped inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Anthurium corrugatum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
