Anthurium jaramilloi

Anthurium jaramilloi, commonly known as the Jaramillo's anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Colombia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant spadix. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage is often a deep green with a waxy texture, and it produces colorful, showy inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant, though it requires specific care to thrive.
