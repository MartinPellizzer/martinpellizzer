Asperula divaricata

Asperula divaricata, commonly known as the creeping azalea, is a flowering shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit. The plant features small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a glossy surface and a slightly waxy texture.

Asperula divaricata thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ground cover in woodland gardens.
