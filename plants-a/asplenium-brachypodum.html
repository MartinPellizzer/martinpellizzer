Asplenium brachypodum

Asplenium brachypodum, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually upright, with a distinctive, delicate appearance.

Asplenium brachypodum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
