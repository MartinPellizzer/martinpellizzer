Amelanchier racemosa

Amelanchier racemosa, commonly known as the common serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in early summer. The plant has alternate, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The berries are a valuable food source for birds and small mammals.
