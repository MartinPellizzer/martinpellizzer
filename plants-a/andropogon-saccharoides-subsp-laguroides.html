Andropogon saccharoides subsp. laguroides

Andropogon saccharoides subsp.

laguroides, commonly known as Florida panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in ecological and botanical research.
