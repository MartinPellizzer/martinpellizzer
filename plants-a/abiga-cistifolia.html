Abiga cistifolia

Abiga cistifolia, commonly known as the African sandthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Cistaceae and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and thorny stems. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are usually small and deciduous, and it produces small, red fruits that are edible but have a sour taste.

The plant has traditional medicinal uses in some African communities.
