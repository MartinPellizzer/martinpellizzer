Anacamptis coriophora

Anacamptis coriophora, commonly known as the narrow-leaf orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces a cluster of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers with a distinctive hooded lip. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in calcareous soils and open habitats.

It is often found in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
