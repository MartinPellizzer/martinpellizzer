Astragalus leporinus var. hirsutus

Astragalus leporinus var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. Its leaves are compound, with small, rounded leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

This species is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions.
