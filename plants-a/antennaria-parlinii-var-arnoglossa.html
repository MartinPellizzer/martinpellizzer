Antennaria parlinii var. arnoglossa

Antennaria parlinii var. arnoglossa, commonly known as the slender leaf mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy, often hairy foliage. The plant features small, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, producing yellowish to reddish blooms that attract pollinators.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
