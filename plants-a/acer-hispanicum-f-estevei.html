Acer hispanicum f. estevei

Acer hispanicum f. estevei, commonly known as the Spanish maple, is a rare and distinctive variety of maple native to the Iberian Peninsula.

This plant is characterized by its unique foliage, featuring palmately lobed leaves with a striking reddish-brown underside. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and streamside areas. The species is noted for its ornamental value, with showy red flowers appearing in early spring.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Acer hispanicum f. estevei is considered a rare and endangered plant.
