Aristolochia parviflora

Aristolochia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular flowers that are typically purple or greenish in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its aromatic, often foul-smelling blossoms.

The plant contains toxic compounds and is used in traditional medicine, though its use is limited due to potential health risks.
