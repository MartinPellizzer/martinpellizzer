Antennaria scariosa

Antennaria scariosa, commonly known as the scaly willow herb, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its mat-forming growth habit and hairy, often scaly stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually linear or lance-shaped and covered with fine, silky hairs.

Antennaria scariosa thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.
