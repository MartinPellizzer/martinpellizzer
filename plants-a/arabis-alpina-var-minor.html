Arabis alpina var. minor

Arabis alpina var. minor, commonly known as alpine rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 5 to 15 centimeters. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers clustered in tight, compact inflorescences. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.
