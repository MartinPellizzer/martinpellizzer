Asparagus stachyoides

Asparagus stachyoides, commonly known as the silver asparagus or mountain asparagus, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
