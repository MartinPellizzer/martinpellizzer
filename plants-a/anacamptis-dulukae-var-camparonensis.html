Anacamptis dulukae var. camparonensis

Anacamptis dulukae var. camparonensis, commonly known as the Camparon orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flower spikes with intricate markings. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy habitats, often in mountainous regions with well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom during the summer months, attracting specific pollinators such as bees and wasps.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered vulnerable to environmental changes and human encroachment.
