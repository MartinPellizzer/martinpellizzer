Arundarbor spinosa

Arundarbor spinosa, commonly known as the thorned willow, is a shrub native to temperate regions.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorny stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, which are followed by distinctive, woody capsules containing seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources.

Arundarbor spinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
