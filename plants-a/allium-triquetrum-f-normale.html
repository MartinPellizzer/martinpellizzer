Allium triquetrum f. normale

Allium triquetrum f. normale, commonly known as the common garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, bulbous structures that resemble garlic cloves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value and edible qualities make it a popular choice for gardens and traditional herbal use.
