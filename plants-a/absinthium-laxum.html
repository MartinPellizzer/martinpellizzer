Absinthium laxum

Absinthium laxum, commonly known as slender wormwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in clusters.

Absinthium laxum is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported digestive and antiseptic properties.
