Abies mexicana

Abies mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Pinaceae and is characterized by its pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves. The tree produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates.

The bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against environmental stress.
