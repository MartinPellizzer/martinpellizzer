Aconitum anthora var. confertiflorum

Aconitum anthora var. confertiflorum, commonly known as the dense-flowered monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with deeply lobed segments, and its stems are typically erect and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and toxicity, it is often cultivated in gardens but requires careful handling.
