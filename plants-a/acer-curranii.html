Acer curranii

Acer curranii, commonly known as Curran's maple, is a species of maple native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 10 to 20 meters, featuring a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. The leaves are palmately lobed, with five to seven pointed lobes, and are often bright green during the growing season. In autumn, the foliage turns vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow, making it a striking ornamental tree.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often found in subalpine and alpine environments.
