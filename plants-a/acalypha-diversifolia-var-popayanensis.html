Acalypha diversifolia var. popayanensis

Acalypha diversifolia var. popayanensis, commonly known as the Popayan spurflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Colombia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red or purple. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellow, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
