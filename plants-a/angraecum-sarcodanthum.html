Angraecum sarcodanthum

Angraecum sarcodanthum, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescence bearing numerous white, star-shaped flowers with prominent, spurred petals. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high air circulation and moderate temperatures. Its unique floral structure is adapted for pollination by the hawkmoth, which has a long proboscis.

Angraecum sarcodanthum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its rarity and elegance.
