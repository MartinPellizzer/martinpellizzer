Anthemis deserti-syriaci

Anthemis deserti-syriaci, commonly known as the Syrian sandthrift, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or pale green, and it produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its resilience, it is often used in xeriscaping and is of interest to botanists studying desert flora.
