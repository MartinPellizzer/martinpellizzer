Arivela bundeica

Arivela bundeica, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange and yellow petals that resemble flames, making it a visually captivating species. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its intricate, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Arivela bundeica thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish.

Its botanical features make it a prized specimen in both horticulture and ecological studies.
