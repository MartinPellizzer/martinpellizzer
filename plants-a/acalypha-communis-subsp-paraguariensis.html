Acalypha communis subsp. paraguariensis

Acalypha communis subsp.

paraguariensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan copperleaf, is a shrub native to South America. It is characterized by its striking copper-colored leaves, which are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
