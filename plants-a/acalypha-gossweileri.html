Acalypha gossweileri

Acalypha gossweileri, commonly known as the yellow velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety yellow bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Acalypha gossweileri is adapted to thrive in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
