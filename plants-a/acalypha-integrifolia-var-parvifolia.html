Acalypha integrifolia var. parvifolia

Acalypha integrifolia var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved gloryflower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are entire and often have a slight sheen. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that are borne on short peduncles. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
