Arundinella macauensis

Arundinella macauensis, commonly known as Macau bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight sheen.

Arundinella macauensis is often found in moist, shady environments such as forest understories.
