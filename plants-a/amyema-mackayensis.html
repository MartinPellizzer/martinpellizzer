Amyema mackayensis

Amyema mackayensis, commonly known as the Mackay's ghost gum, is a small evergreen tree native to the arid regions of northern Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes eucalyptus and other aromatic plants. The tree is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the cooler months.

Amyema mackayensis is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in sandy soils.
