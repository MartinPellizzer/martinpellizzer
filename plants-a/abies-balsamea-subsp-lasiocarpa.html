Abies balsamea subsp. lasiocarpa

Abies balsamea subsp.

lasiocarpa, commonly known as the white spruce, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to North America. It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in spirals.

The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions.

This subspecies is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in boreal and subarctic regions.
