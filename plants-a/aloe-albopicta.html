Aloe albopicta

Aloe albopicta, commonly known as the white-stripe aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color with distinctive white or pale yellow markings. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers, which bloom in the spring. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its leaves are often used for their medicinal properties, though it is important to note that not all aloe species are safe for human consumption.
