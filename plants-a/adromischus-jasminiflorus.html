Adromischus jasminiflorus

Adromischus jasminiflorus, commonly known as the jasmine-flowered stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, adapted for water storage. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers resembling jasmine, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
