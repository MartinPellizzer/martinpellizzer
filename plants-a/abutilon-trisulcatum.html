Abutilon trisulcatum

Abutilon trisulcatum, commonly known as the three-ridged abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and its flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in clusters. It produces small, three-lobed fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
