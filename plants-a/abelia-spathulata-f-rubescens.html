Abelia spathulata f. rubescens

Abelia spathulata f. rubescens, commonly known as the red-leaf abelia, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that turn a striking red or bronze color in the fall. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pink flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often used in gardens for its seasonal color changes.
