Adesmia vallsii

Adesmia vallsii, commonly known as the Valls's adesmia, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile. The plant features compound leaves with stipules and produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of white or pale pink. Its fruit is a legume that splits open to release seeds, a common reproductive strategy in the Fabaceae family.

Adesmia vallsii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.
