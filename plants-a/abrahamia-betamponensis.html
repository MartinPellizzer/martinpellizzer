Abrahamia betamponensis

Abrahamia betamponensis, commonly known as the Betampona starling's orchid, is a rare and endemic species found exclusively in Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure, which includes a spurred labellum and showy petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the rainforests of the eastern part of the island. Its small, delicate flowers are adapted for specific pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered critically endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
