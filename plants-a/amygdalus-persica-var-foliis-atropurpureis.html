Amygdalus persica var. foliis-atropurpureis

Amygdalus persica var. foliis-atropurpureis, commonly known as the purple-leaf peach, is a cultivar of the peach tree characterized by its striking purple foliage.

The leaves are typically a deep, rich purple, especially when exposed to full sun, while the stems and young shoots also exhibit a similar hue. This variety produces medium-sized, sweet fruits with a mild flavor, similar to other peach cultivars. It is often grown for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to maintain its vibrant leaf color.
