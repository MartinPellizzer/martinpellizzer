Althaea kusjariensis

Althaea kusjariensis, commonly known as the Kusjari marshmallow, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its hairy, cylindrical stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces showy, pale pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, containing a mucilaginous substance used in traditional medicine.

Althaea kusjariensis thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wet meadows and riverbanks.
