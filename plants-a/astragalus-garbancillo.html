Astragalus garbancillo

Astragalus garbancillo, commonly known as the Spanish milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus garbancillo is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
