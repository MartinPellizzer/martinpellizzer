Aster cusickii var. lyallii

Aster cusickii var. lyallii, commonly known as the Lyall's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. This perennial herb typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in shades of purple and white.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native gardens.
