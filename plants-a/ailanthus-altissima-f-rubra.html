Ailanthus altissima f. rubra

Ailanthus altissima f. rubra, commonly known as the red-leaf tree of heaven, is a variant of the tree of heaven, native to China.

It is characterized by its striking red foliage, which becomes more pronounced in autumn. The plant is a deciduous tree with compound leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring. It is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.

However, it is considered an invasive species in many regions due to its aggressive spread and ecological impact.
