Aglaia celebica

Aglaia celebica, commonly known as the Java aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces distinctive, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in lowland and hill forest areas, thriving in humid and warm climates.

Due to its ornamental value and traditional uses, it is cultivated in various horticultural settings.
