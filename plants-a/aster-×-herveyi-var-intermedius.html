Aster × herveyi var. intermedius

Aster × herveyi var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate aster, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that typically range in color from white to purple, depending on the specific cultivar. This variety is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It often grows in woodland edges and moist meadows, preferring partial to full sunlight.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
