Asparagus kuisibensis

Asparagus kuisibensis, commonly known as the Kuisibensis asparagus, is a flowering plant species native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
