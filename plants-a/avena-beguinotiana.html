Avena beguinotiana

Avena beguinotiana, commonly known as the beguinotian oat, is a rare and unique species of grass native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves and its small, spike-like inflorescence. The plant exhibits a peculiar growth habit, with its leaves curling tightly around the stem, forming a compact, spiral structure. It thrives in the arid and rocky environment of Socotra, adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is considered a botanical curiosity and a subject of scientific interest.
