Anthocephalus indicus var. macrophyllus

Anthocephalus indicus var. macrophyllus, commonly known as the large-leaved anthocephalus, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its foliage is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
