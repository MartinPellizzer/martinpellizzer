Aglossorrhyncha jabiensis

Aglossorrhyncha jabiensis, commonly known as the Jabiensis orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family native to specific regions in Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, featuring a short, narrow labellum and a reduced galea, which are key botanical traits. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it relies on mycorrhizal associations for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a color palette ranging from greenish-white to pale yellow, adapting to its specific ecological niche.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
