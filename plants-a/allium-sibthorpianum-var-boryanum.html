Allium sibthorpianum var. boryanum

Allium sibthorpianum var. boryanum, commonly known as the Boryanum garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant's bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a valuable addition to culinary and medicinal uses. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
