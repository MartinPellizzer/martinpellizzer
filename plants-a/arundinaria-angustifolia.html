Arundinaria angustifolia

Arundinaria angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf bamboo, is a species native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as along stream banks and in forest understories. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a grass-like appearance.

Arundinaria angustifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland areas.
