Arundina graminifolia

Arundina graminifolia, commonly known as the grass-like bamboo, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Arundina graminifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
