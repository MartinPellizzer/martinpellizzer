Astragalus subcinereus var. basalticus

Astragalus subcinereus var. basalticus, commonly known as the basaltic milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, yellowish to greenish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often on slopes or in crevices, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets that help reduce water loss.

The species is known for its ecological importance, providing forage for wildlife and contributing to soil stabilization in its native habitat.
