Aster trinervius var. adustus

Aster trinervius var. adustus, commonly known as the rusty aster, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features showy, pale purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough, hairy texture.

The variety adustus is distinguished by its distinctive rust-colored stems and leaves, which give it its common name.
