Astragalus katunicus

Astragalus katunicus, commonly known as the Karst milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, thriving in well-drained environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus katunicus is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to temperate climates.
