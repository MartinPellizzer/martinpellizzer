Aconitum uchiyamae

Aconitum uchiyamae, commonly known as Uchiyama's aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, typically arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed leaflets that are often covered in a fine pubescence.

Aconitum uchiyamae contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
