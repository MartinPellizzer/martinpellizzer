Andropogon sorghum subvar. japonicus

Andropogon sorghum subvar. japonicus, commonly known as Japanese sorghum, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or marshy areas.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
