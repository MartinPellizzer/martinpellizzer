Ageratina hederifolia

Ageratina hederifolia, commonly known as creeping ageratum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped, serrated, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to spread easily in moist, shady conditions.
