Astilbe glaberrima lusus saxatilis

Astilbe glaberrima lusus saxatilis, commonly known as the rock astilbe, is a perennial plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems that bear clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant typically grows in rocky, moist environments and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.

Astilbe glaberrima lusus saxatilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
