Artemisia nipponica

Artemisia nipponica, commonly known as Japanese wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant contains volatile oils and sesquiterpene lactones, which contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in woodland edges and rocky areas.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional herbal medicine.
