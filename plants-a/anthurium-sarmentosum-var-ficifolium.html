Anthurium sarmentosum var. ficifolium

Anthurium sarmentosum var. ficifolium, commonly known as the fig-leaf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped, glossy green leaves that resemble a fig leaf, hence its common name. The plant produces striking spadices, which are inflorescences that appear as bright red or orange structures. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

This variety is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive as a houseplant.
