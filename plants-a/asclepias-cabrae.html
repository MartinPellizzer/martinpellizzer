Asclepias cabrae

Asclepias cabrae, commonly known as the Cape Hare's-foot, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the milkweed family, Asclepiadaceae, and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately divided, giving it a distinctive, feathery appearance.

Asclepias cabrae is typically found in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
