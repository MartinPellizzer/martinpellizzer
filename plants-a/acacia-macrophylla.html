Acacia macrophylla

Acacia macrophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with bacteria. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Acacia macrophylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.

Native Distribution
