Antitoxicum amplexicaule

Antitoxicum amplexicaule, commonly known as the clasping antitoxic plant, is a herbaceous species characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that often wrap around the stem, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its roots are known for containing various bioactive compounds, contributing to its historical use in traditional medicine.

However, it is important to note that the plant is toxic and should not be ingested without proper guidance.
