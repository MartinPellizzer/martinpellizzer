Acacia legnota

Acacia legnota, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features slender, bipinnate leaves and produces yellow, pea-like flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Acacia legnota is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
