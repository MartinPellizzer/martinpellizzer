Abarema kerrii

Abarema kerrii, commonly known as the Kerri's bristlepod, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Abarema kerrii is not widely cultivated but is of interest to botanists due to its distinctive morphology and ecological role.
