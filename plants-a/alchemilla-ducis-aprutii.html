Alchemilla ducis-aprutii

Alchemilla ducis-aprutii, commonly known as the Apulian lady's mantle, is a member of the Rosaceae family and is native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of soft, silvery-white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Alchemilla ducis-aprutii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
