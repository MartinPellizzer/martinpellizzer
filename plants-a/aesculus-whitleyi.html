Aesculus whitleyi

Aesculus whitleyi, commonly known as the Whitley's horsechestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The tree produces large, spiny capsules containing shiny, black seeds. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are ovate to lanceolate in shape.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
