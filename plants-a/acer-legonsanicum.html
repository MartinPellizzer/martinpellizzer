Acer legonsanicum

Acer legonsanicum, commonly known as the Léonson maple, is a deciduous tree native to western Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds. It typically grows to a height of 10–15 meters and prefers well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
