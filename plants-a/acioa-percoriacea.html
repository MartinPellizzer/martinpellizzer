Acioa percoriacea

Acioa percoriacea, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink bract-like flowers that resemble coral, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

Acioa percoriacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
