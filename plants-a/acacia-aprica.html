Acacia aprica

Acacia aprica, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and pinnate leaves composed of small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow-orange inflorescences that consist of numerous small, globular flowers. Acacia aprica thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
