Arenaria ciliata var. moehringioides

Arenaria ciliata var. moehringioides, commonly known as the Moehring's sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and sandy habitats in Europe.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are covered in fine hairs, helping it retain moisture in arid environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky outcrops, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
