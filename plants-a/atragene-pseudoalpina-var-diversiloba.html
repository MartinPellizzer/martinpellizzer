Atragene pseudoalpina var. diversiloba

Atragene pseudoalpina var. diversiloba, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks at high elevations. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive blue or purple throat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and the stems are hairy.

The species is notable for its adaptability to alpine environments and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
