Arabis petraea var. kamchatica

Arabis petraea var. kamchatica, commonly known as the Kamchatka rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the rocky and alpine regions of the Russian Far East.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and ornamental plants. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in mountainous and coastal habitats.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
