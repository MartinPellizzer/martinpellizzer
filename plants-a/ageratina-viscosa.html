Ageratina viscosa

Ageratina viscosa, commonly known as sticky ageratum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that produce a sticky substance, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Ageratina viscosa is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
