Astragalus vesicarius var. albidus

Astragalus vesicarius var. albidus, commonly known as the white milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and disturbed areas.

Its ecological importance includes nitrogen fixation and providing food for various insects and wildlife.
