Amomum miriflorum

Amomum miriflorum, commonly known as the fragrant cardamom plant, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous root system. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in branched clusters. Its leaves are elongated, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine, waxy coating.

The plant is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its potential medicinal and aromatic properties.
