Acacia erythrophloea

Acacia erythrophloea, commonly known as the red-flowered acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow to orange globular inflorescences, which are highly attractive to bees and other pollinators. Its flowers are followed by flat, fibrous pods that contain several seeds.

Acacia erythrophloea is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
