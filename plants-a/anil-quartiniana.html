Anil quartiniana

Anil quartiniana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a history of use in traditional medicine.

However, it can become invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
