Aerides praemorsa

Aerides praemorsa, commonly known as the Orchid of the Night, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, dark purple to black flowers with a contrasting white lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers bloom at night, attracting specific pollinators such as moths.

The botanical features of Aerides praemorsa make it a unique and sought-after specimen in the world of orchid cultivation.
