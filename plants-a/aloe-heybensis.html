Aloe heybensis

Aloe heybensis, commonly known as the Heyne's aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, which helps it store water. The plant produces tall, branched stems with clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for arid environments.

Aloe heybensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
