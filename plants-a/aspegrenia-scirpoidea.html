Aspegrenia scirpoidea

Aspegrenia scirpoidea, commonly known as the swamp milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring slender, upright stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Aspegrenia scirpoidea plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
