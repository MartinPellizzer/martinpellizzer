Acaena argentea var. interruptepinnata

Acaena argentea var. interruptepinnata, commonly known as the silverweed with interrupted pinnate leaves, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It features silver-gray leaves that are deeply divided into a pinnate pattern, though the leaflets are interrupted, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fluffy, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by spherical, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
