Astragalus neocarpus

Astragalus neocarpus, commonly known as the Sierra foothill milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its root system is typically fibrous and shallow. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Astragalus neocarpus plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.
