Asphodelus chambeironii var. ovoideus

Asphodelus chambeironii var. ovoideus, commonly known as the oval asphodel, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and tall, slender flower stalks. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to greenish-yellow flowers, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight bluish or grayish hue.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
