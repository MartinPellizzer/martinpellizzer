Aster denudatus

Aster denudatus, commonly known as the naked aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in shades of white to pale blue.

Aster denudatus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
