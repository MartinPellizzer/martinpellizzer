Alliaria kunthiana

Alliaria kunthiana, commonly known as wild garlic or garlic mustard, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, white, four-petaled flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a garlic-like flavor, making it a useful plant in some culinary contexts.

However, it can be invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
