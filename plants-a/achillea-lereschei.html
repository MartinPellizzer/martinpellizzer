Achillea lereschei

Achillea lereschei, commonly known as the Leresche's yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are usually pale yellow to white, with a fluffy appearance.

Achillea lereschei is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
