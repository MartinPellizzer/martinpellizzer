Alnus fruticosa f. macrophylla

Alnus fruticosa f. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved alder, is a shrub belonging to the Betulaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green catkins that are typically male, while female flowers are found on the same plant. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones or wetlands.

This species is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a habitat for various wildlife species.
