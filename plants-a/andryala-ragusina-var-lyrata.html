Andryala ragusina var. lyrata

Andryala ragusina var. lyrata, commonly known as the Sicilian wild thyme, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in local cuisines for their aromatic properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone areas.
