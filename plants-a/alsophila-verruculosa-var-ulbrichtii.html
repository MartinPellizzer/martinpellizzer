Alsophila verruculosa var. ulbrichtii

Alsophila verruculosa var. ulbrichtii, commonly known as the Ulbricht's tree fern, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feather-like fronds that can grow quite large. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, fertile soil to flourish. Its reproductive structures, including strobila, are typically found at the tips of the fronds, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
