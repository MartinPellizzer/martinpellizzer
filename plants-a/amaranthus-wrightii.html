Amaranthus wrightii

Amaranthus wrightii, commonly known as Wright's amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring lance-shaped leaves and vibrant red or green bracts. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellow, surrounded by colorful foliage. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in disturbed areas such as fields and roadsides.

Wright's amaranth is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Native American practices.
