Anathallis racemosa

Anathallis racemosa, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance due to its elongated, slender inflorescence. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of spiders.

Anathallis racemosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or tropical gardens.
