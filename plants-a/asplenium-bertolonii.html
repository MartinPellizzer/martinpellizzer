Asplenium bertolonii

Asplenium bertolonii, commonly known as the Bertoloni's spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a small, evergreen fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rocky crevices or forest floors. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a serrated margin, and it produces spores in compact, clustered structures called sorangia. It is native to regions in southern Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East.

Asplenium bertolonii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.
