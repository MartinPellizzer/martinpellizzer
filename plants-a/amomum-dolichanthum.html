Amomum dolichanthum

Amomum dolichanthum, commonly known as the long-flowered cardamom, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, elongated spikes of pale yellow to green flowers. The plant produces small, dark brown berries that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its rhizomes are also utilized for their medicinal properties, similar to other members of the ginger family.

Amomum dolichanthum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its valuable aromatic compounds.
