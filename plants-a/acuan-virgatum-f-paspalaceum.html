Acuan virgatum f. paspalaceum

Acuan virgatum f. paspalaceum, commonly known as the silvergrass or paspalum grass, is a variant of the native grass species found in the Americas.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale flowers in clusters.

This ornamental grass is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in landscaping for its visual texture and drought tolerance.
