Aleuritia chrysochlora

Aleuritia chrysochlora, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, which bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that matures to a dark red or black color, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
