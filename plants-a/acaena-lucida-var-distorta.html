Acaena lucida var. distorta

Acaena lucida var. distorta, commonly known as the distorted sunflower, is a variant of the sunflower species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive, twisted and elongated flower heads, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, with a rough texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.
