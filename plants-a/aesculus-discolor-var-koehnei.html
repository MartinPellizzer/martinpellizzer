Aesculus discolor var. koehnei

Aesculus discolor var. koehnei, commonly known as the Koehne's horse chestnut, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its palmately compound leaves, which are typically 5-7 lobed and have a distinctive, slightly serrated margin. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers in upright panicles during the spring, followed by spiny, capsule-like fruits. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardens.

Due to its unique foliage and floral display, it is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
