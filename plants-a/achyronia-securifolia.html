Achyronia securifolia

Achyronia securifolia, commonly known as the swordleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments. Its botanical features include hairy stems and capsule fruits that release seeds when mature.

Achyronia securifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in xeriscaping.
