Amomum elettarioides

Amomum elettarioides, commonly known as the lesser cardamom, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a smooth texture and a strong, spicy scent.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
