Acrostichum lindenii

Acrostichum lindenii, commonly known as the giant reed or giant grass, is a tall, perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow, and often bluish-green leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist soil conditions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, forming dense stands that can dominate aquatic habitats.

Acrostichum lindenii is also notable for its ability to tolerate saline conditions, making it adaptable to coastal areas.
