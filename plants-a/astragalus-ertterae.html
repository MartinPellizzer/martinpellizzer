Astragalus ertterae

Astragalus ertterae, commonly known as the Ertter's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh environments, with deep root systems that help it access water from deeper soil layers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local ecosystems through nitrogen fixation.
