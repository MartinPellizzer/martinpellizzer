Albuca collina

Albuca collina, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically evergreen, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Albuca collina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
