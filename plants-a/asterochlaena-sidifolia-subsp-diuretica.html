Asterochlaena sidifolia subsp. diuretica

Asterochlaena sidifolia subsp.

diuretica, commonly known as the diuretic stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding ornamental value. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick leaves.

Due to its diuretic properties, it has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
