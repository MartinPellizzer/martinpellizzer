Acacia edulis

Acacia edulis, commonly known as the edible acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are followed by long, flat pods containing edible seeds. The seeds are rich in nutrients and have been traditionally used as a food source by various indigenous communities.
