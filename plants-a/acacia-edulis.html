Acacia edulis

Acacia edulis, commonly known as the edible acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, thorned stems and bipinnate leaves that are often reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are followed by long, flat pods containing edible seeds. The seeds are rich in nutrients and have been traditionally used as a food source by various indigenous communities.

Additionally, the plant has cultural and medicinal significance in the regions where it is found.
