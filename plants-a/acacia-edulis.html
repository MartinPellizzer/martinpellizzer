Acacia edulis

Acacia edulis, commonly known as the edible acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorny stems and bipinnate leaves composed of small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow or brown flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Acacia edulis is valued for its nutritious seeds, which are rich in protein and can be ground into flour or used in traditional dishes.

It also plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
