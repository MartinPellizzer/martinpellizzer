Artemisia scoparia subsp. villosa

Artemisia scoparia subsp.

villosa, commonly known as woolly wormwood, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in compact, spherical shapes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
