Atriplex nitrophiloides

Atriplex nitrophiloides, commonly known as the nitrophilous saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, gray-green leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable species for soil stabilization in degraded landscapes.

Atriplex nitrophiloides plays an important role in desert ecosystems by providing forage for wildlife and contributing to soil health.
