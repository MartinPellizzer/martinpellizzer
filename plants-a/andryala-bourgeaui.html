Andryala bourgeaui

Andryala bourgeaui, commonly known as the African snowball, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It features small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, creating a striking visual effect. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Andryala bourgeaui is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
