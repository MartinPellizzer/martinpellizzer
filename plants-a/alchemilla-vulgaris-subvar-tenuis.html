Alchemilla vulgaris subvar. tenuis

Alchemilla vulgaris subvar. tenuis, commonly known as narrow-leaved lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features silver-gray, hairy leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in rounded clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
