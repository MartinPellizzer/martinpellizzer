Acidocroton spinosus

Acidocroton spinosus, commonly known as the spiny croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny leaves and branched, woody stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange or red berries that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
