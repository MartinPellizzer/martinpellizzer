Amorphophallus tinekeae

Amorphophallus tinekeae, commonly known as the Tineke's giant arrowhead, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of western Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length, contributing to its dramatic appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species in botanical research and conservation efforts.
