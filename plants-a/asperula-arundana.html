Asperula arundana

Asperula arundana, commonly known as the spring snowflake, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a delicate floral display to gardens. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing year-round visual interest. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Asperula arundana is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its compact growth habit and ornamental value.
