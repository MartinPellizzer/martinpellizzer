Astragalus sewertzowii

Astragalus sewertzowii, commonly known as the Sewertzow's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid habitats, showcasing adaptations to harsh environmental conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus sewertzowii is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
