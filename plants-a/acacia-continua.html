Acacia continua

Acacia continua, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic if ingested. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in savannas and rocky areas.

Acacia continua plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
