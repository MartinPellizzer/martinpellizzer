Albizia guillainii

Albizia guillainii, commonly known as the African silk tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and produces small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowering display.

Albizia guillainii is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
