Arnica alpina var. vestita

Arnica alpina var. vestita, commonly known as the alpine tawnpuff, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers are yellow to orange in color, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This variety is distinguished by its distinctive white, woolly leaves and is often found in alpine meadows at elevations above 1,500 meters.
