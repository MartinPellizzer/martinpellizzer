Arabis divaricarpa var. typica

Arabis divaricarpa var. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rockcress, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments. The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and linear, often with a bluish-gray hue due to dense pubescence. Its flowers are small, white, and arranged in clusters, blooming in early spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
