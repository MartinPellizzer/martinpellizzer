Abutilon bakerianum

Abutilon bakerianum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves that give it a velvety appearance. The plant produces lantern-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
