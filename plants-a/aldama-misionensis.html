Aldama misionensis

Aldama misionensis, commonly known as the Misiones Aldama, is a flowering plant native to the subtropical regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with lance-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
