Actinocarya kansuensis

Actinocarya kansuensis, commonly known as the Kansu actinocarya, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by edible berries that are an important food source for wildlife. Due to its ecological significance, it is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities.
