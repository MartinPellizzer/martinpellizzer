Alyssum scabrum

Alyssum scabrum, commonly known as common alyssum, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb with small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves and produces numerous tiny seeds in elongated pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Alyssum scabrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
