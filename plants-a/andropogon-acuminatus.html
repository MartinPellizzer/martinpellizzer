Andropogon acuminatus

Andropogon acuminatus, commonly known as narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually blue-green in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
