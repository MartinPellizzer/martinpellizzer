Amoora ficiformis

Amoora ficiformis, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a striking tropical tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble flames, composed of numerous bright red or orange flowers. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets, and it produces woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in coastal areas and open woodlands.

Amoora ficiformis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its dramatic floral display.
