Ayenia flaccida

Ayenia flaccida, commonly known as the soft ayenia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Ayenia flaccida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
