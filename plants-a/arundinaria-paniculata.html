Arundinaria paniculata

Arundinaria paniculata, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or black bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces panicles of small, greenish-white flowers that appear in the summer months. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Arundinaria paniculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
