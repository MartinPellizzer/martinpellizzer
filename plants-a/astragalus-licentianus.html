Astragalus licentianus

Astragalus licentianus, commonly known as the Licentian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It produces pods that contain small, round seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus licentianus is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.
