Aloe recurva

Aloe recurva, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features long, narrow, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a pale blue or grayish tint. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, which are followed by red or orange berries. Aloe recurva is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
