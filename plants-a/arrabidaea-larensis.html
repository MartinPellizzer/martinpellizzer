Arrabidaea larensis

Arrabidaea larensis, commonly known as the Cape jasmine, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in the Western and Northern Cape provinces. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly rough texture.

The Cape jasmine is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual interest.
