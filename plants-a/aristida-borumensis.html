Aristida borumensis

Aristida borumensis, commonly known as the Borum grass, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, wiry leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental appeal and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
