Alchemilla gracilis

Alchemilla gracilis, commonly known as slender lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Alchemilla gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
