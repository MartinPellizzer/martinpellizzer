Athyrium roseum f. pubigerum

Athyrium roseum f. pubigerum, commonly known as the pubescent lady fern, is a fern species characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds.

It features a distinctive silvery-blue hue on its young leaves, which gradually matures to a more greenish tone. The plant has a creeping rhizome that produces multiple erect, branching fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
