Argyrolobium barikotense

Argyrolobium barikotense, commonly known as the Barikot silverlobed pea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Pakistan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its silvery-white foliage and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptation to harsh environments.

Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used for forage or soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
