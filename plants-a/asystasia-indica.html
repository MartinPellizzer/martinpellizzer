Asystasia indica

Asystasia indica, commonly known as the Indian asystasia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Asystasia indica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and flowering display.
