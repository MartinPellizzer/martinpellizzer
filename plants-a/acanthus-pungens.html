Acanthus pungens

Acanthus pungens, commonly known as the spiked acanthus, is a striking ornamental plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves with prominent, spiny margins that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, spire-like inflorescences topped with small, pale yellow flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its architectural form and long-lasting foliage, it is often used in horticultural designs for its visual impact.
