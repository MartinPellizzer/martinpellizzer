Amaryllis harrisonii

Amaryllis harrisonii, commonly known as the Harrison's amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem that bears multiple blooms, often with a strong, spicy fragrance. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, emerging from a bulbous base, and it thrives in well-drained soil with moderate sunlight.

Amaryllis harrisonii is a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its bold floral display and relatively easy care requirements.
