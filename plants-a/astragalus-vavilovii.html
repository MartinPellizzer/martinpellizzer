Astragalus vavilovii

Astragalus vavilovii, commonly known as Vavilov's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and compound leaves. The plant produces yellow or white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, bean-like pods. It is typically found in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils.

Astragalus vavilovii is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its potential medicinal properties.
