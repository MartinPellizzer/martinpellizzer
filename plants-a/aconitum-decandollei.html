Aconitum decandollei

Aconitum decandollei, commonly known as the Decandolle's monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple hooded flowers that resemble a medieval monk's habit. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Aconitum decandollei contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
