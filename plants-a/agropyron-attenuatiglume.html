Agropyron attenuatiglume

Agropyron attenuatiglume, commonly known as the slender-sheath wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated glumes and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides and fields. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet that is often loosely arranged.

Agropyron attenuatiglume is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
