Anacampseros ternata

Anacampseros ternata, commonly known as the Three-lobed Stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it an ideal plant for arid and semi-arid environments.
