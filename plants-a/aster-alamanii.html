Aster alamanii

Aster alamanii, commonly known as the Alamanii aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats. It has narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Aster alamanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
