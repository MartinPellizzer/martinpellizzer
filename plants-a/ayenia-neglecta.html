Ayenia neglecta

Ayenia neglecta, commonly known as the slender sand verbena, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has slender, branching stems with small, opposite leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pale blue to violet and appear in clusters, blooming from late spring to early summer.

Ayenia neglecta is often found in open habitats such as grasslands, scrublands, and along rocky outcrops.
