Atriplex pentandra

Atriplex pentandra, commonly known as saltbush, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in saline and drought-prone environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. Its root system is extensive, helping to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Atriplex pentandra is also valued for its nutritional content, often used as forage for livestock in desert regions.
