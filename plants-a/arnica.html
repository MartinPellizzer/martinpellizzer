Arnica

Arnica, commonly known as the mountain tobacco, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, rough leaves. The plant typically grows in alpine or meadow environments, thriving in well-drained soil and full sunlight. Arnica species, such as Arnica montana, are known for their medicinal properties, often used in topical treatments for bruises and inflammation.

Its chemical composition includes sesquiterpene lactones and flavonoids, contributing to its therapeutic effects.
