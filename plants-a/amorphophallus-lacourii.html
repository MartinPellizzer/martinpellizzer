Amorphophallus lacourii

Amorphophallus lacourii, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic life. The plant's flowers are typically white or pink, with a strong, unpleasant odor that attracts pollinators like flies. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and ponds.

Due to its impressive size and ecological importance, it is a notable species in wetland ecosystems.
