Astragalus agrestis var. polyspermus

Astragalus agrestis var. polyspermus, commonly known as the rough milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant has a deep taproot system, which helps it survive in arid and rocky environments. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands, sagebrush steppe, and disturbed soils.

The species is also valued for its role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
