Adiantum philippense subsp. teestae

Adiantum philippense subsp.

teestae, commonly known as the Philippine maidenhair fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the Philippines. It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, arching fronds that resemble a feather.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests. Its fronds are typically bioluminescent under certain conditions, though this is not a common trait.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
