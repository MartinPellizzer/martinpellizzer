Acianthera bahorucensis

Acianthera bahorucensis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and vibrant coloration. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually small to medium-sized, with intricate patterns and a distinct scent to attract pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
