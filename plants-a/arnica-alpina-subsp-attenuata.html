Arnica alpina subsp. attenuata

Arnica alpina subsp.

attenuata, commonly known as the alpine tawnpuff, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils at high altitudes.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
