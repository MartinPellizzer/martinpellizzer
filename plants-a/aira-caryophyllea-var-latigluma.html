Aira caryophyllea var. latigluma

Aira caryophyllea var. latigluma, commonly known as the purple false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. The plant produces dense clusters of small, pinkish to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and flowering display.
