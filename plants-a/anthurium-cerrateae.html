Anthurium cerrateae

Anthurium cerrateae, commonly known as the Cerrado anthurium, is a species native to Brazil, specifically the Cerrado region.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, which can range from red to orange. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil.

Anthurium cerrateae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
