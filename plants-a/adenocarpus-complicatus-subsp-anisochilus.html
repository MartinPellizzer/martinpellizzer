Adenocarpus complicatus subsp. anisochilus

Adenocarpus complicatus subsp.

anisochilus, commonly known as the African burr bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by distinctive burr-like fruits that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The subspecies is notable for its adaptability to arid conditions and its role in local ecosystems.
