Allium contortum

Allium contortum, commonly known as the twisted garlic or snake's garlic, is a bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It features a compact, clustered growth habit with small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, rounded bulbs that are edible and have a mild garlic flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Allium contortum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
