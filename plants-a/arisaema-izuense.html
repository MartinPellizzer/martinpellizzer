Arisaema izuense

Arisaema izuense, commonly known as the Izu mountain arisaema, is a herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, preferring rich, organic soil. Its leaves are arrow-shaped and often have a striking green color with a reddish tint on the underside.

The flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and the plant produces berries that are dispersed by animals.
