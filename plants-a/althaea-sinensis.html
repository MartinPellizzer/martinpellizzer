Althaea sinensis

Althaea sinensis, commonly known as Chinese marshmallow, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy pink or white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional Chinese medicine for their mucilage content, which has soothing properties.

Althaea sinensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
