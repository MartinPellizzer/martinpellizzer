Artanthe exserens

Artanthe exserens, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its deep root system and reduced leaf surface. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Artanthe exserens is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is considered a symbol of resilience in desert ecosystems.
