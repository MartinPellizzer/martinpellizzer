Alchemilla vulgaris var. tirolensis

Alchemilla vulgaris var. tirolensis, commonly known as the Alpine lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in a dense layer of silky white hairs, giving them a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and resilience.
