Aquilegia atrata var. salvatoriana

Aquilegia atrata var. salvatoriana, commonly known as the black cohosh, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged in a palmate pattern. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that are usually deep red or purple, with five petals and prominent sepals. Its roots are valued in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Botanically, it belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often toxic flowers.
