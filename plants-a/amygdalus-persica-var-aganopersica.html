Amygdalus persica var. aganopersica

Amygdalus persica var. aganopersica, commonly known as the Persian peach, is a variant of the peach tree characterized by its ornamental value and unique fruit characteristics.

It typically produces small, round fruits with a thin, fuzzy skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp. The plant is native to regions of Central Asia and has been cultivated for centuries for both its fruit and ornamental appeal. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sunlight for optimal growth.

The flowers are usually pink or white, adding to its aesthetic value in gardens.
