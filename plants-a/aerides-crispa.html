Aerides crispa

Aerides crispa, commonly known as the crisped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and the presence of long, narrow leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive crinkled appearance. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Aerides crispa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in both indoor and outdoor settings.
