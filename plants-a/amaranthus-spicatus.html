Amaranthus spicatus

Amaranthus spicatus, commonly known as the prince's feather, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in summer. The plant produces distinctive, spiky bracts that give it its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides.

Amaranthus spicatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
