Acer circumlobatum

Acer circumlobatum, commonly known as the Pacific madrone, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and clusters of white flowers that appear in early spring. The tree produces orange-red berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous regions.
