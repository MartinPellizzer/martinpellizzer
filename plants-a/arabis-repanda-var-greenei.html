Arabis repanda var. greenei

Arabis repanda var. greenei, commonly known as the Greene's rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features basal leaves that are spoon-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and arid environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh conditions.

The species is also noted for its role in stabilizing soils in ecological restoration projects.
