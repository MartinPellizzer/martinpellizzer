Acacia varians

Acacia varians, commonly known as the variable acacia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in spherical or cylindrical clusters, which are followed by flat, woody pods containing seeds. Acacia varians is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, where it adapts to dry conditions through its deep root system and water-storing tissues.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
