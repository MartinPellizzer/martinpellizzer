Aleuritia muscarioides

Aleuritia muscarioides, commonly known as the clustered honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its berries are red or orange, containing small seeds, and are often consumed by birds.

Aleuritia muscarioides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
