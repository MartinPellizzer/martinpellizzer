Acacia purpurea

Acacia purpurea, commonly known as the purple wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain seeds covered in a fluffy, white coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Acacia purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
