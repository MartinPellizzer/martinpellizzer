Alstroemeria zamioides

Alstroemeria zamioides, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors including red, orange, and yellow. The plant features strap-like leaves and produces clusters of showy blooms during the spring and summer seasons.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower due to its long-lasting blooms and vibrant appearance.
