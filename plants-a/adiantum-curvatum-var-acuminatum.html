Adiantum curvatum var. acuminatum

Adiantum curvatum var. acuminatum, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that give it a soft, velvety appearance.

It is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant's fronds are typically bright green and curl inward, giving it a unique, almost spiral-like shape. It produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds.

This fern is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited for humid indoor environments.
