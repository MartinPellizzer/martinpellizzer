Amaranthus gangeticus var. angustior

Amaranthus gangeticus var. angustior, commonly known as narrow-leaf amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces dense clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, spiky seed heads. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional cuisines for its edible leaves and seeds.
