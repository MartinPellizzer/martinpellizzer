Allium cithaeronis

Allium cithaeronis, commonly known as the Greek leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces edible bulbs that are similar in taste to garlic and onions, making it a valuable crop in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, often growing in rocky or dry environments.

Allium cithaeronis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual and culinary contributions.
