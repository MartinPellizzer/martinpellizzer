Arenaria tetraquetra subsp. armerina

Arenaria tetraquetra subsp.

armerina, commonly known as Armenian sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey. It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in early summer. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value.

This subspecies is notable for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
