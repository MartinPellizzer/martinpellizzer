Anemone cairnesiana

Anemone cairnesiana, commonly known as the purple anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, pentagonal flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has finely dissected, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant collections and ecological restoration projects.
