Agrostis sandwicensis

Agrostis sandwicensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray blades and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in Hawaii.
