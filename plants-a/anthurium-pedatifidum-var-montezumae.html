Anthurium pedatifidum var. montezumae

Anthurium pedatifidum var. montezumae, commonly known as the Montezuma anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance. The plant produces vibrant red spathes that enclose its yellow-orange spadix, which is typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This ornamental plant is popular in horticulture for its exotic foliage and dramatic flowering display.
