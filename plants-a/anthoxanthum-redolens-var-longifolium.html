Anthoxanthum redolens var. longifolium

Anthoxanthum redolens var. longifolium, commonly known as the sweet vernalgrass, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with a reddish hue. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spikelets, which are typically arranged in clusters along the stem. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands, and is often found in temperate regions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant communities and a subject of ecological study.
