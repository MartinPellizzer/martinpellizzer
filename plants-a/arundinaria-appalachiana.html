Arundinaria appalachiana

Arundinaria appalachiana, commonly known as Appalachian bamboo, is a grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can grow up to 10 feet in height. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers, which are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a subject of conservation interest.
