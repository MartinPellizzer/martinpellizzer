Anemone irinae

Anemone irinae, commonly known as the Irina anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are usually hairy and stout.

Anemone irinae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
