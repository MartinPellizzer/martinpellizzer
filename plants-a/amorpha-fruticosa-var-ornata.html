Amorpha fruticosa var. ornata

Amorpha fruticosa var. ornata, commonly known as the ornamental false indigo, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces striking violet to purple pea-shaped flowers in early summer. The plant is characterized by its bushy growth habit and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

Botanically, it is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities through root nodules.
