Aristida fasciculata

Aristida fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in summer, which are arranged in panicles. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Aristida fasciculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
