Argemone platyceras subvar. subintegra

Argemone platyceras subvar. subintegra, commonly known as the narrowleaf castorbean, is a variant of the castor oil plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces capsule fruits containing oily seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
