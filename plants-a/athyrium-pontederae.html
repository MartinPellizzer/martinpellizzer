Athyrium pontederae

Athyrium pontederae, commonly known as the Brazilian chain fern, is a member of the Dryopteridaceae family.

It is a terrestrial fern characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds that are typically bipinnate. Native to the humid forests of South America, particularly Brazil, this plant thrives in shaded, moist environments. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense mat of roots, which help anchor it to the forest floor.

The plant's delicate appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, though it requires specific conditions to flourish.
