Achyranthes peploides

Achyranthes peploides, commonly known as the silky knotweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along riverbanks and wetlands. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and are usually greenish-white in color.

Achyranthes peploides is known for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
