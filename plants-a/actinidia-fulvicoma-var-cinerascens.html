Actinidia fulvicoma var. cinerascens

Actinidia fulvicoma var. cinerascens, commonly known as the silver vine, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-gray stems and heart-shaped leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer, followed by edible, sweet, and aromatic berries. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for both landscape design and cultivation in temperate climates.
