Achyranthes aspera var. obtusifolia

Achyranthes aspera var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leaf cowherb, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a robust stem that is often branched and covered with stiff hairs. The leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a blunt or rounded apex, and are usually hairy on both surfaces. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in spikes, followed by three-lobed, winged fruits.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.
