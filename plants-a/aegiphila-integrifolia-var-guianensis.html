Aegiphila integrifolia var. guianensis

Aegiphila integrifolia var. guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan boxwood, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
