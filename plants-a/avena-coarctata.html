Avena coarctata

Avena coarctata, commonly known as the narrowleaf oat, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster that produces small, two-lobed flowers.

Avena coarctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
