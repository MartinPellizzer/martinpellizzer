Adiantum lancea var. gracile

Adiantum lancea var. gracile, commonly known as the slender maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It features finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are typically light green and have a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, humus-rich soil. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense, rusty-brown coating, a characteristic feature of the Adiantum genus.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
