Acanthocalycium leucanthum

Acanthocalycium leucanthum, commonly known as the White-flowered Prickly Pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rounded, ribbed stems that are covered in white to pale blue spines. The plant produces showy, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
