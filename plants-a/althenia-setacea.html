Althenia setacea

Althenia setacea, commonly known as the hairy althenia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly rough texture.

Althenia setacea is often found in grassy areas and along the edges of woodlands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
