Adhatoda furcata var. terminalis

Adhatoda furcata var. terminalis, commonly known as the African balsam, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with prominent veins, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant is known for its aromatic leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Adhatoda furcata var. terminalis is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
