Alsophila novogranadensis

Alsophila novogranadensis, commonly known as the Colombian tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a tall, tree-like fern that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests. Its fronds are evergreen and can grow up to 3 meters in length, contributing to its ornamental value.

Alsophila novogranadensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
