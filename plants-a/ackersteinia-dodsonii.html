Ackersteinia dodsonii

Ackersteinia dodsonii, commonly known as the dodson's starling vine, is a climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of white and green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow or white, which bloom in the spring. Its unique growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a sought-after plant for gardeners.

However, it is considered invasive in some regions due to its rapid spread and ability to outcompete native vegetation.
