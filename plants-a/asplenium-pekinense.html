Asplenium pekinense

Asplenium pekinense, commonly known as the Peking spleenwort, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rock crevices or forest floors. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a serrated margin.

Asplenium pekinense is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
