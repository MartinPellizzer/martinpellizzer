Anacamptis papilionacea subsp. papilionacea

Anacamptis papilionacea subsp.

papilionacea, commonly known as the butterfly orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, butterfly-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, and it thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer, and it is pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

The subspecies papilionacea is distinguished by its elongated, strap-like leaves and its ability to form clumps over time.
