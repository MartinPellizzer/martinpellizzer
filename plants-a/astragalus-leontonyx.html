Astragalus leontonyx

Astragalus leontonyx, commonly known as the lion's tooth milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny pods and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces a milky sap that is characteristic of many members of the genus Astragalus.

This species is often found in grasslands and shrublands, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
