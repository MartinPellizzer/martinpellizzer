Alstroemeria landimana

Alstroemeria landimana, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are often branched and upright.

Alstroemeria landimana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
