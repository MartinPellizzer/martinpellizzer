Alsinella subulata

Alsinella subulata, commonly known as the slender alsinella, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Alsinella subulata is often found in open areas such as deserts, grasslands, and along rocky outcrops.
