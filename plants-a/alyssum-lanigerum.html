Alyssum lanigerum

Alyssum lanigerum, commonly known as sweet alyssum, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, mat-forming growth with clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The leaves are narrow, gray-green, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
