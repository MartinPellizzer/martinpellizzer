Anchusa ochroleuca subsp. popovii

Anchusa ochroleuca subsp.

popovii, commonly known as the Popov's blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
