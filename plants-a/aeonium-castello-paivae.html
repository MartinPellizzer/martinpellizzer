Aeonium castello-paivae

Aeonium castello-paivae, commonly known as the Castello-Paiva's Aeonium, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern, with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with red or purple hues. The plant produces tall, branching inflorescences that bear yellow flowers, which appear in spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its striking appearance and hardy nature make it a desirable addition to both indoor and outdoor gardens.
