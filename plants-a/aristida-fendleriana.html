Aristida fendleriana

Aristida fendleriana, commonly known as desert threeawn, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, exhibiting a low, sprawling growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous bristly, awned lemmas, which are characteristic of the grass family Poaceae. Its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in harsh environments make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
