Astragalus sarypulensis

Astragalus sarypulensis, commonly known as the Sarypul milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with deep root systems.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
