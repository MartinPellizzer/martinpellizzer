Avena nuda subsp. wiestii

Avena nuda subsp.

wiestii, commonly known as the slender oat, is a grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its thin, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous minute flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Botanically, it is notable for its adaptations to arid environments and its role in ecological succession.
