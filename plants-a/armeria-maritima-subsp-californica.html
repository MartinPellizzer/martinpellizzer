Armeria maritima subsp. californica

Armeria maritima subsp.

californica, commonly known as the California thrift, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of California. It is a low-growing perennial that produces dense clusters of white, pink, or red flowers.

The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves and stems adapted to withstand salt spray and coastal winds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes and rocky habitats.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
