Aster incisus var. incisus

Aster incisus var. incisus, commonly known as the New England aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, composite flower heads with purple to pink petals and yellow centers, typical of the Asteraceae family. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often toothed or lobed. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural habitats.

Its blooming period occurs in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
