Actinodaphne akoensis var. sasakii

Actinodaphne akoensis var. sasakii, commonly known as the Sasakii camphor tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in white or pale yellow. Its fruits are drupes, typically containing a single seed, and the tree can grow up to 15 meters tall.

This species is valued for its essential oils and is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
