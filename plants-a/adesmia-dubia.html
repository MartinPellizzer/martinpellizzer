Adesmia dubia

Adesmia dubia, commonly known as the narrowleaf burrpear, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The fruit is a hairy pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
