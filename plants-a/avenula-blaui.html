Avenula blaui

Avenula blaui, commonly known as the blue oat grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and compact inflorescence that resembles a dense oat cluster. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Avenula blaui is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
