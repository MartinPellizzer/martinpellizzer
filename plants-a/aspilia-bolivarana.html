Aspilia bolivarana

Aspilia bolivarana, commonly known as Bolivar's torchflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces striking yellow-orange flower heads that resemble torches, with ray florets surrounding a central disk. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Aspilia bolivarana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
