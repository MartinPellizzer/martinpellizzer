Arundinaria gallatlyi

Arundinaria gallatlyi, commonly known as the gallant bamboo, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, green foliage, which give it a striking appearance. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its growth habit is clump-forming, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Arundinaria gallatlyi is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining soil stability.
