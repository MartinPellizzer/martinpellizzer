Acrocarpus polyphyllus

Acrocarpus polyphyllus, commonly known as the broad-leaved ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and distinctive, broad, leathery leaves. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing edible seeds. It is valued for its hard, heavy wood used in construction and furniture.

Acrocarpus polyphyllus plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
