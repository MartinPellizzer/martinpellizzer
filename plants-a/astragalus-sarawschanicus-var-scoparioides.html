Astragalus sarawschanicus var. scoparioides

Astragalus sarawschanicus var. scoparioides, commonly known as the Karakoram milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil in arid and alpine regions.
