Azorella ranunculus

Azorella ranunculus, commonly known as the Andean buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial plant native to the high Andes of South America. The plant features dense, woolly foliage that helps it withstand harsh alpine conditions. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, with five petals and numerous stamens.

Azorella ranunculus plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various alpine organisms.
