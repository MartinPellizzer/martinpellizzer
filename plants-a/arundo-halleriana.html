Arundo halleriana

Arundo halleriana, commonly known as the Haller's reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features long, narrow leaves that are often used for thatching and weaving in local communities. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist environments and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and lakes.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both useful and potentially invasive in certain ecosystems.
