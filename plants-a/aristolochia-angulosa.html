Aristolochia angulosa

Aristolochia angulosa, commonly known as the angular pipevine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and its unique, intricate flowers that resemble a pipe or a horn. The plant belongs to the family Aristolochiaceae and is known for its aromatic, often foul-smelling blossoms. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found along stream banks and in shaded undergrowth.

The plant is also notable for its role in supporting certain species of butterflies, particularly the pipevine swallowtail.
