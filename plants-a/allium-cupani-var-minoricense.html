Allium cupani var. minoricense

Allium cupani var. minoricense, commonly known as the Mexican garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and leeks, and is characterized by its bulbous structure and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its culinary use is limited, but it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
