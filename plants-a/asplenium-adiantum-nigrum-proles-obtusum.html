Asplenium adiantum-nigrum proles obtusum

Asplenium adiantum-nigrum proles obtusum, commonly known as the broad-leaved maidenhair fern, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that are dark green and often glossy. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce new growth from the base.

The leaves are usually leathery and have a distinctive, elegant appearance that makes it a popular ornamental plant.
