Aira koelerioides

Aira koelerioides, commonly known as the Koeler's air grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It features slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific ecological requirements, it is a valued plant in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
