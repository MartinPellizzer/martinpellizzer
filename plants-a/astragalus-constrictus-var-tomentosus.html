Astragalus constrictus var. tomentosus

Astragalus constrictus var. tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Astragalus constrictus var. tomentosus is a member of the legume family and has adapted to arid conditions through its deep root system.
