Abies concolor f. conica

Abies concolor f. conica, commonly known as the white fir, is a cultivar of the white fir tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its conical shape and dense, dark green foliage that remains throughout the year. The cones are typically reddish-brown and hang downward, adding visual interest to the tree. It thrives in cooler climates and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

This cultivar is valued for its hardiness and ability to provide year-round visual appeal in gardens and parks.
