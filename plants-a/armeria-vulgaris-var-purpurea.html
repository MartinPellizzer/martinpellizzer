Armeria vulgaris var. purpurea

Armeria vulgaris var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple thrift, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing evergreen perennial that produces dense clusters of pink to purple flowers in early spring. The plant has basal leaves that are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
