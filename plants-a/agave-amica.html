Agave amica

Agave amica, commonly known as the Mexican heartleaf agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are often blue-green in color. The leaves are lined with sharp spines and have a distinctive heart-shaped base. It produces an impressive flowering stalk that can reach several meters in height, bearing white or pale yellow flowers.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
