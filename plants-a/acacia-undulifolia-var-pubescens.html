Acacia undulifolia var. pubescens

Acacia undulifolia var. pubescens, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a pubescent appearance. It thrives in coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
