Allium sativum var. ophioscorodon

Allium sativum var. ophioscorodon, commonly known as the Persian leek or shallot, is a botanical variant of the common garlic plant.

It is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical bulbs that are typically smaller than those of regular garlic. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bulbous flowers that are often used in ornamental gardening. Its leaves are slender and grass-like, growing in dense, upright clumps.

This variety is valued for its mild flavor and is commonly used in culinary applications across various cuisines.
