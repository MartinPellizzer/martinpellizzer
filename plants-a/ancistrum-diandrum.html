Ancistrum diandrum

Ancistrum diandrum, commonly known as the Mexican flame plant, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Mexico and Central America. The plant features striking, brightly colored spathes that range from red to orange, often with contrasting white veins. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and evergreen.

Ancistrum diandrum is typically grown as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
