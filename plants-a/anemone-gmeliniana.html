Anemone gmeliniana

Anemone gmeliniana, commonly known as the alpine anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, often with five to seven petals. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette and are usually covered in fine hairs.

Anemone gmeliniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
