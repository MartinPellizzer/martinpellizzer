Agave tigrina

Agave tigrina, commonly known as the tiger agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often lined with white or yellowish teeth, giving it its common name. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that can reach several meters in height, bearing clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are typically blue-green in color and can grow up to 1 meter in length.

Agave tigrina is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.
