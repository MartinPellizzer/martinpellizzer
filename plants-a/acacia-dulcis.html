Acacia dulcis

Acacia dulcis, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces flat, compound leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white powder, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. Acacia dulcis is well adapted to dry climates and can thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Its seeds are encased in a hard pod and are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of gum arabic.
