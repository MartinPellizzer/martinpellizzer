Acronia cordata subsp. trachysepala

Acronia cordata subsp.

trachysepala, commonly known as the rough-leaved acronia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and thick, leathery leaves that are covered in a rough, textured surface.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
