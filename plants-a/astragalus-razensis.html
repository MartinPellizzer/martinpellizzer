Astragalus razensis

Astragalus razensis, commonly known as the Razensky milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Astragalus razensis is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional medicine practices.

Its root system plays a crucial role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its natural habitat.
