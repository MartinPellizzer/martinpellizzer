Arabis doumetii

Arabis doumetii, commonly known as the rock Arabis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
