Astragalus caspicus subsp. knappianus

Astragalus caspicus subsp.

knappianus, commonly known as Knapp's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists and ecologists.

Its subspecific classification highlights its distinct genetic and morphological traits compared to other Astragalus species.
