Aleuritia euchaites

Aleuritia euchaites, commonly known as the Chinese elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, white, bell-shaped flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Aleuritia euchaites thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.
