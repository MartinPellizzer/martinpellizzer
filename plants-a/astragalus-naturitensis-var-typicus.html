Astragalus naturitensis var. typicus

Astragalus naturitensis var. typicus, commonly known as the typical form of the naturalitensis astragalus, is a member of the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is notable for its limited distribution and is often found in specific regions of Central Asia.
