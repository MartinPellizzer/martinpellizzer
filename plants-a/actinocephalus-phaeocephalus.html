Actinocephalus phaeocephalus

Actinocephalus phaeocephalus, commonly known as the black-headed daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive dark purple to black central flower head. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow ray florets surrounding the central disc. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Actinocephalus phaeocephalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
