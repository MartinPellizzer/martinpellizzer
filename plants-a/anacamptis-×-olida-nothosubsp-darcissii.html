Anacamptis × olida nothosubsp. darcissii

Anacamptis × olida nothosubsp.

darcissii, commonly known as the Darcie's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and its flowers are often adorned with intricate patterns and a distinctive lip.

This nothosubspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and fragrance.
