Andropogon contortus subvar. roxburghii

Andropogon contortus subvar. roxburghii, commonly known as twisted bluestem, is a perennial grass native to parts of Asia, particularly India.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often rolled when young. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
