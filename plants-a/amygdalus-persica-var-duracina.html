Amygdalus persica var. duracina

Amygdalus persica var. duracina, commonly known as the Persian almond, is a variant of the peach tree characterized by its distinctive botanical features.

It typically produces small, round fruits with a thick, fuzzy skin and a sweet, juicy flesh. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in autumn, and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its flowers are pink to white, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, which is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
