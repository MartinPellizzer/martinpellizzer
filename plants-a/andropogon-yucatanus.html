Andropogon yucatanus

Andropogon yucatanus, commonly known as Yucatan grass, is a perennial grass native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as savannas and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to warm climates and is often found in areas with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
