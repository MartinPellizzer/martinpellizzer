Armeria welwitschii var. platyphylla

Armeria welwitschii var. platyphylla, commonly known as the flatleaf sea thrift, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The leaves are typically broad and fleshy, adapted to arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environments.
