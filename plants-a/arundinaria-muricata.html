Arundinaria muricata

Arundinaria muricata, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and distinctive golden-colored inflorescences. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its elegant appearance and rapid growth rate. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Arundinaria muricata is also valued for its use in traditional Chinese medicine and as a source of sustainable materials.
