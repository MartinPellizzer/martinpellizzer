Artemisia takeshimensis

Artemisia takeshimensis, commonly known as desert sagebrush, is a hardy shrub native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and aromatic, pungent scent, which is due to the presence of volatile oils. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability. Its small, yellow flowers appear in late spring and are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

Artemisia takeshimensis plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
