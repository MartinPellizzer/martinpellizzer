Acinos purpurascens

Acinos purpurascens, commonly known as the purple mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces showy, purple to pinkish flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.

Its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions makes it an important component of its native ecosystems.
