Ampelodesmos tenax var. microstachys

Ampelodesmos tenax var. microstachys, commonly known as the slender climbing fig, is a variant of the Ampelodesmos tenax species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a woody vine characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered fig-like fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
