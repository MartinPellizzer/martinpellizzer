Alectorolophus × digeneus

Alectorolophus × digeneus, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Alectorolophus falcatus and Alectorolophus digeneus.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure and vibrant coloration. The plant typically produces showy, fragrant flowers with intricate patterns on its petals and sepals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring high humidity and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
