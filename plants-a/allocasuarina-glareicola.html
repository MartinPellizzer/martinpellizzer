Allocasuarina glareicola

Allocasuarina glareicola, commonly known as the Sturt's Desert Pea, is a shrub native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, needle-like phyllodes that are typically bright red or orange in color. The plant produces striking violet or blue flowers, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. It is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often found in sandy soils.

Allocasuarina glareicola plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
