Acalypha virginica var. ovalifolia

Acalypha virginica var. ovalifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as woodland edges and wetlands. The plant features lance-shaped, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, clustered inflorescences produce small, greenish-white flowers that are surrounded by bracts.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
