Angelica carvifolia var. pratensis

Angelica carvifolia var. pratensis, commonly known as the meadow angelica, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, compound leaves with a distinctive serrated edge. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, typically white or pale green in color.
