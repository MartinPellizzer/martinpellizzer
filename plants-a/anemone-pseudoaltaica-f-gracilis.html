Anemone pseudoaltaica f. gracilis

Anemone pseudoaltaica f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmate leaves that are typically hairy and have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces showy, white or pale pink flowers with five to seven petals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in rock gardens or alpine settings.
