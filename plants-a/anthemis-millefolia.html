Anthemis millefolia

Anthemis millefolia, commonly known as common yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic, slightly bitter scent.

Anthemis millefolia has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
