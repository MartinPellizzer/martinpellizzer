Aloe neoqaharensis

Aloe neoqaharensis, commonly known as the Qaharensis Aloe, is a rare and endemic species native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers, which bloom during the cooler months. Its habitat is restricted to specific regions in the Eastern Cape, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to its limited distribution and threats from habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable.
