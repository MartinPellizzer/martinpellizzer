Adenocarpus cincinnatus

Adenocarpus cincinnatus, commonly known as the small globular thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, spherical shape and spiny leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grassland and shrubland habitats.

Adenocarpus cincinnatus is well adapted to arid environments and is relatively drought-tolerant.
