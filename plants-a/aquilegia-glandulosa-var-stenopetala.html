Aquilegia glandulosa var. stenopetala

Aquilegia glandulosa var. stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved columbine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features striking blue to purple spurred flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves with a hairy texture, and its stems are typically covered in glandular dots. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable addition to woodland gardens.

This variety is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to other forms of Aquilegia glandulosa.
