Annona maritima

Annona maritima, commonly known as the sea apple or custard apple, is a tropical plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is a small, evergreen tree or shrub that thrives in saline and brackish environments. The plant produces large, fragrant, yellowish-green fruits that are edible and have a creamy, custard-like texture. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and the plant has a shallow root system adapted to coastal conditions.

Annona maritima is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
