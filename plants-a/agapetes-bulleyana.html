Agapetes bulleyana

Agapetes bulleyana, commonly known as the Bulley's silverleaf, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers during the spring season. Its leaves are leathery and have a rough texture, which helps it withstand harsh mountainous climates.

Agapetes bulleyana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively low maintenance once established.
