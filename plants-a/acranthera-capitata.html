Acranthera capitata

Acranthera capitata, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pale blue flowers that resemble snowflakes. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun. It produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Acranthera capitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
