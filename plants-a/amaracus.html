Amaracus

Amaracus, commonly known as the sweet potato, is a tropical vine belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and hairy stems, which help in identifying the plant. The plant produces tuberous roots that are rich in carbohydrates and nutrients, making it a staple food in many cultures. Its flowers are typically white or purple, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Amaracus is cultivated for its edible roots and is also used in traditional medicine in various regions.
