Andrographis elongata

Andrographis elongata, commonly known as the longleaf andrographis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, arranged in spikes. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.

Andrographis elongata is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
