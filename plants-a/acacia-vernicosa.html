Acacia vernicosa

Acacia vernicosa, commonly known as the gum acacia or white wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its slender, often spiny branches. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by long, flat seed pods. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, feather-like segments.

Acacia vernicosa is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and adaptable species.
