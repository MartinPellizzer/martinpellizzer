Ardisia suboppositifolia

Ardisia suboppositifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ardisia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features elliptical, glossy dark green leaves that are arranged oppositely along its stems. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
