Armeria scabriuscula

Armeria scabriuscula, commonly known as the rough-leaved thrift, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with dense clusters of white or pink flowers. The leaves are gray-green, rough to the touch, and often have a fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
