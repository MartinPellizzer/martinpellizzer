Archidendron havilandii

Archidendron havilandii, commonly known as the Haviland's legume, is a woody climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are often dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
