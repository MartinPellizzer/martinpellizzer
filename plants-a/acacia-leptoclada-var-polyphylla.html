Acacia leptoclada var. polyphylla

Acacia leptoclada var. polyphylla, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage composed of small, narrow leaves that are often clustered at the ends of branches. The plant produces yellow flowers in spherical or cylindrical inflorescences, which bloom during the warmer months. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots that help it access groundwater.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
