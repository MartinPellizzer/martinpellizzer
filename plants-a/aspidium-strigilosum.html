Aspidium strigilosum

Aspidium strigilosum, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Aspidium strigilosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.
