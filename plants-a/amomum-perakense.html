Amomum perakense

Amomum perakense, commonly known as the Perak cardamom, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and rhizomes. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and thrives in well-drained, fertile soils. The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
