Arenaria aggregata var. pseudoquerioides

Arenaria aggregata var. pseudoquerioides, commonly known as the desert sandflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It typically forms dense mats of foliage, with small, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dunes or rocky habitats.

Its succulent characteristics make it well-suited for harsh, dry environments.
