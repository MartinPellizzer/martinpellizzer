Allium vineale subsp. cupuliferum

Allium vineale subsp.

cupuliferum, commonly known as the purple garlic or wild leek, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, white to purple flowers arranged in a dense umbel, which bloom in early spring. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in culinary applications.

This subspecies is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
