Aridaria globosa

Aridaria globosa, commonly known as the globe lily, is a succulent plant native to arid regions.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It grows in compact, rounded clusters, making it an attractive ornamental plant.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
