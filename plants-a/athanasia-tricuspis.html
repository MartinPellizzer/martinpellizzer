Athanasia tricuspis

Athanasia tricuspis, commonly known as the three-lobed everlasting flower, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are narrow, linear, and have a distinctive three-lobed shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The plant's resilience and attractive appearance make it a popular choice among gardeners in arid climates.
