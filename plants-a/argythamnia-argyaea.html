Argythamnia argyaea

Argythamnia argyaea, commonly known as silverleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, often in clusters at the ends of stems. Its leaves are typically gray-green and covered in fine, silvery hairs, which help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

Argythamnia argyaea is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in rocky or sandy habitats.
