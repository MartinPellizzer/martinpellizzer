Acantholimon bracteatum var. capitatum

Acantholimon bracteatum var. capitatum, commonly known as the clustered sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are adapted to tolerate saline and sandy soils. It is often found in dunes and rocky coastal habitats, where it thrives in full sun.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a distinctive component of coastal flora.
