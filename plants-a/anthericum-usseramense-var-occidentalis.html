Anthericum usseramense var. occidentalis

Anthericum usseramense var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western marsh bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland and marsh environments in western North America.

It features strap-like, grass-like leaves that are typically green and glossy, growing in dense clusters. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and swampy areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
