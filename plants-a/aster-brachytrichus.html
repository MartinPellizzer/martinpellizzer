Aster brachytrichus

Aster brachytrichus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and typically herbaceous growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of purple and blue. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Aster brachytrichus is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations.
