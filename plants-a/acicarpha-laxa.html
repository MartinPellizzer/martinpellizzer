Acicarpha laxa

Acicarpha laxa, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, feather-like leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Acicarpha laxa is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
