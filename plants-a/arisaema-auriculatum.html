Arisaema auriculatum

Arisaema auriculatum, commonly known as the auriculate arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a prominent spadix, and it produces berries that contain toxic seeds.

Arisaema auriculatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in some regions.
