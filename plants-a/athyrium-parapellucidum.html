Athyrium parapellucidum

Athyrium parapellucidum, commonly known as the translucent fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds with translucent veins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Athyrium parapellucidum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated in shaded gardens.
