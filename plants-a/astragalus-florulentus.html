Astragalus florulentus

Astragalus florulentus, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, bean-shaped pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus florulentus is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for various pollinators.
