Artemisia lindleyana var. coronopus

Artemisia lindleyana var. coronopus, commonly known as blue mist shrub, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage that emits a strong, pungent scent when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and wildlife habitats.
