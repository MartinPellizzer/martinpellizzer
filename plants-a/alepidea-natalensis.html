Alepidea natalensis

Alepidea natalensis, commonly known as Natal aloes, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted for water storage. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
