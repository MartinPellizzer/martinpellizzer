Amsinckia celsa

Amsinckia celsa, commonly known as the desert smokeplant, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelion-like structures. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Amsinckia celsa is often found in rocky areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
