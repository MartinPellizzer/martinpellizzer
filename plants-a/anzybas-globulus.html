Anzybas globulus

Anzybas globulus, commonly known as the globe anzybas, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spherical, fleshy fruit that resembles a small globe, which is a key feature in its common name. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
