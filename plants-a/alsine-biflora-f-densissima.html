Alsine biflora f. densissima

Alsine biflora f. densissima, commonly known as the dense-flowered alsike, is a variant of the alsike plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its dense clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinator populations.
