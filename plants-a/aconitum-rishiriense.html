Aconitum rishiriense

Aconitum rishiriense, commonly known as the Rishiri aconite, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is native to Japan.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces palmate leaves with deeply lobed segments, typical of many members of its family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
