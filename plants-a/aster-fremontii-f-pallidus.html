Aster fremontii f. pallidus

Aster fremontii f. pallidus, commonly known as the pale fremont's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its pale blue to white flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its low-growing habit and drought tolerance, making it well adapted to arid environments.
