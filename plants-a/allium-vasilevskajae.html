Allium vasilevskajae

Allium vasilevskajae, commonly known as the Vasilevskaya leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of long, narrow leaves. The plant produces tall, erect stems that bear umbels of white or pale purple flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic-like flavor, making it a potential crop for culinary use.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun, though it can tolerate partial shade.
