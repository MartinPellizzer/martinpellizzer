Ascolepis erythrocephala

Ascolepis erythrocephala, commonly known as the red-headed orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking red and orange inflorescence. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a distinctive coloration that helps attract specific pollinators.

Ascolepis erythrocephala is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
