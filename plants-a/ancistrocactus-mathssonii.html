Ancistrocactus mathssonii

Ancistrocactus mathssonii, commonly known as the Mexican star cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white, woolly hair. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth rate, it is a popular choice for collectors and succulent enthusiasts.
