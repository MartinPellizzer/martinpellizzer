Astragalus longipetalus

Astragalus longipetalus, commonly known as the long-petaled milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated petals. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems. It produces small, yellow to reddish-brown flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

Astragalus longipetalus is a perennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
