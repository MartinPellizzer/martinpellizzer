Alsophila gracilis

Alsophila gracilis, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery fronds. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to anchor firmly in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.

Alsophila gracilis thrives in humid climates and requires consistent moisture and indirect sunlight for optimal growth.
