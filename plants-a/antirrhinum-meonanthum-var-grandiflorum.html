Antirrhinum meonanthum var. grandiflorum

Antirrhinum meonanthum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the grandiflorum snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bright red or orange tubular flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This species thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, enhancing its ecological value.
