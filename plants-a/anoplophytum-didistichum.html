Anoplophytum didistichum

Anoplophytum didistichum, commonly known as the two-ranked grass, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-ranked arrangement of leaves, which gives it its scientific name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and covered in a fine layer of hairs. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, often at the ends of the stems.

Anoplophytum didistichum is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
