Agropyron cimmericum

Agropyron cimmericum, commonly known as the Siberian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to cold climates in Eurasia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is adapted to harsh environments, including permafrost and arid soils, and is often used for erosion control.

Its seeds are also utilized in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
