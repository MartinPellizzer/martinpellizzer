Aspilia patentipilis

Aspilia patentipilis, commonly known as the Cape umbrella plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-like inflorescence composed of numerous small white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and has a shrub-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. It is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
