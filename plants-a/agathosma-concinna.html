Agathosma concinna

Agathosma concinna, commonly known as silver leaf bush or Cape silver leaf, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, rounded fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Agathosma concinna is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
