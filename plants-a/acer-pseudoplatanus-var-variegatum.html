Acer pseudoplatanus var. variegatum

Acer pseudoplatanus var. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated maple, is a variant of the common maple, characterized by its distinctive foliage.

The leaves are typically lobed and have a striking variegation pattern of white, yellow, and green, which makes it a popular ornamental plant. It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, red samaras that mature in the fall, contributing to its seasonal appeal.

Its adaptability to various soil types and moderate sunlight makes it suitable for a range of garden settings.
