Aspicarpa argentea var. tenuifolia

Aspicarpa argentea var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf aspicarpa, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that give it the species name *tenuifolia*, meaning "narrow-leaved.". The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its silvery foliage, which reflects light and adds visual interest to gardens.
