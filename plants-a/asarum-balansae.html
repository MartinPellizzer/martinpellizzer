Asarum balansae

Asarum balansae, commonly known as Balansae wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, spathate flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-brown fruits that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
