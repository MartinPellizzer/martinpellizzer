Astragalus lasiosemius var. gracilior

Astragalus lasiosemius var. gracilior, commonly known as the slender hairy milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, pinnately compound leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
