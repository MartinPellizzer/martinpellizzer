Arenaria ciliata var. frigida

Arenaria ciliata var. frigida, commonly known as the alpine sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to cold, alpine environments.

It features compact, mat-forming growth with small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
