Acacia pachyphloia subsp. brevipinnula

Acacia pachyphloia subsp.

brevipinnula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are densely arranged along the stems.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, typically during the winter months. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with a deep root system and a waxy coating on its leaves to reduce water loss.

This subspecies is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
