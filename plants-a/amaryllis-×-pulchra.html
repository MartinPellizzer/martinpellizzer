Amaryllis × pulchra

Amaryllis × pulchra, commonly known as the pink amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from crosses between Amaryllis bellona and Amaryllis galilaea.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers and strap-like leaves. The plant is typically grown as a bulbous perennial, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. Its blooming period usually occurs in late winter to early spring, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

The flowers can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, adding a dramatic visual impact to any setting.
