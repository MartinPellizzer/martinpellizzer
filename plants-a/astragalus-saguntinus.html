Astragalus saguntinus

Astragalus saguntinus, commonly known as the Saguntine milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pea-like flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its small, yellow to orange flowers bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees.

The plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought conditions.
