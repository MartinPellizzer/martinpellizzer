Alternanthera collina

Alternanthera collina, commonly known as red-veined sorrel, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with bright red veins that contrast sharply with its dark green foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
