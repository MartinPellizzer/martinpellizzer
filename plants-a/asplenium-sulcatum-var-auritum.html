Asplenium sulcatum var. auritum

Asplenium sulcatum var. auritum, commonly known as the ear-shaped spleenwort, is a fern that belongs to the Aspleniaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, dark green, leathery leaves that are often adorned with distinctive, shallow grooves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rock crevices or on tree trunks. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the leaves.

This variety is notable for its unique leaf morphology and is often found in temperate regions.
