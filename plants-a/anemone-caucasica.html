Anemone caucasica

Anemone caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces erect, branched stems. Its flowers are surrounded by a cluster of petal-like sepals, giving it a delicate, star-like appearance.

Anemone caucasica thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
