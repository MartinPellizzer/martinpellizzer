Alstroemeria bracteata

Alstroemeria bracteata, commonly known as the Peruvian lily, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves and showy bracts that enhance its ornamental appeal. Alstroemeria bracteata is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and is used in floral arrangements.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
