Agropyron × nothum

Agropyron × nothum, commonly known as the intermediate wheatgrass, is a hybrid grass species that results from the cross between *Agropyron elongatum* and *Agropyron cristatum*.

It is characterized by its tall, robust growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Agropyron × nothum is often cultivated for its forage value and is also studied for its potential in sustainable agriculture and biofuel production.
