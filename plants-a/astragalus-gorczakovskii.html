Astragalus gorczakovskii

Astragalus gorczakovskii, commonly known as the Gorczakov's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many nitrogen-fixing species.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and for soil enrichment due to its ecological benefits.
