Anthyllis montana subsp. hispanica

Anthyllis montana subsp.

hispanica, commonly known as Spanish broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and northern Africa. It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats.

The plant produces yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soils.
