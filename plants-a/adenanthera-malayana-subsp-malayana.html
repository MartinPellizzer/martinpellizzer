Adenanthera malayana subsp. malayana

Adenanthera malayana subsp.

malayana, commonly known as the Malayan burr bean, is a leguminous shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain several rounded seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it useful in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
