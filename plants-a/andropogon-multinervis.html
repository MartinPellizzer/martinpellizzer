Andropogon multinervis

Andropogon multinervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, and its leaves are narrow with prominent parallel veins. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a panicle, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Andropogon multinervis plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
