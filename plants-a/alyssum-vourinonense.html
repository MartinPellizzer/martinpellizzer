Alyssum vourinonense

Alyssum vourinonense, commonly known as the Vourinon sand-alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial species characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically features gray-green foliage with a fuzzy texture, and its flowers range in color from white to pale yellow. Alyssum vourinonense thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
