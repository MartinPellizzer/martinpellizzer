Abutilon atropurpureum

Abutilon atropurpureum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The plant is part of the Malvaceae family, which includes other well-known species like cotton and hibiscus.
