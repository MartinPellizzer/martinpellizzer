Andropogon filipendulus var. calvescens

Andropogon filipendulus var. calvescens, commonly known as slender bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and slender, erect stems that often grow in clumps. The plant typically thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in open woodlands and prairies. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing small, spike-like inflorescences that are often pale blue or purple in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native plant landscapes.
