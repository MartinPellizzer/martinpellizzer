Achnatherum breviaristatum

Achnatherum breviaristatum, commonly known as the short-awned needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Achnatherum breviaristatum is adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Its ecological significance includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in degraded landscapes.
