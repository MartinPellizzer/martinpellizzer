Andromeda latifolia

Andromeda latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf wintergreen, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils along stream banks and in woodland areas. The plant features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, with a glossy green surface and a distinctive waxy coating.

Andromeda latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
