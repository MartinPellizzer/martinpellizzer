Aster elongatus var. crassifolius

Aster elongatus var. crassifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers in the late summer and early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Its foliage and floral structure make it an attractive addition to native gardens and ecological landscapes.
