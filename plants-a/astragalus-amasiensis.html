Astragalus amasiensis

Astragalus amasiensis, commonly known as the Amasiensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia, including parts of Mongolia and China. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, trifoliate leaves, and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Astragalus amasiensis is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and can thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
