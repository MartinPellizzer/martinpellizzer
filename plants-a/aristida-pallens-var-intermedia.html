Aristida pallens var. intermedia

Aristida pallens var. intermedia, commonly known as the slender dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands and moist meadows, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or gray. Its small, pale green flowers appear in late spring to early summer, forming delicate, panicle-like inflorescences.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
