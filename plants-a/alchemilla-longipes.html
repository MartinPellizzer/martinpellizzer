Alchemilla longipes

Alchemilla longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are covered in dense, silky white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in panicles during the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported soothing properties.

Alchemilla longipes thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
