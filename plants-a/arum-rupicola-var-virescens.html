Arum rupicola var. virescens

Arum rupicola var. virescens, commonly known as the green-veined arum, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features large, arrow-shaped leaves with prominent green veins contrasting against its dark green foliage. The plant produces striking, umbrella-like inflorescences with a spadix surrounded by a showy spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor plant collections.
