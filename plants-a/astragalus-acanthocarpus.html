Astragalus acanthocarpus

Astragalus acanthocarpus, commonly known as the spiny fruit milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive spiny fruit pods. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its flowers are usually yellow or pale green, and it produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus acanthocarpus is often found in steppe and desert steppe habitats.
