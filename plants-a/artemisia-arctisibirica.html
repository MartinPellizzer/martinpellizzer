Artemisia arctisibirica

Artemisia arctisibirica, commonly known as alpine tarragon, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky environments. Its essential oils contain compounds like thujone and camphor, which contribute to its strong scent and medicinal properties.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and digestive benefits.
