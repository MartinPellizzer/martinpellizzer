Asemeia miamiensis

Asemeia miamiensis, commonly known as Miami wild indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along waterways, thriving in moist, sandy soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Asemeia miamiensis plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
