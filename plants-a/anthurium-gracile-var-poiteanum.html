Anthurium gracile var. poiteanum

Anthurium gracile var. poiteanum, commonly known as the Poiteanum Anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, typically in shades of red or orange. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse settings.
