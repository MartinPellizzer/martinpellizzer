Aconitum wallichianum

Aconitum wallichianum, commonly known as the Himalayan monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers arranged in a helmet-like shape, typical of the genus Aconitum. The plant has palmate leaves with deeply lobed segments, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is toxic due to the presence of potent alkaloids, making it both dangerous and valuable in traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens, though caution is necessary due to its harmful properties.
