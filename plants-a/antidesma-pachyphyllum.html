Antidesma pachyphyllum

Antidesma pachyphyllum, commonly known as the thick-leaved antidesma, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries. Its bark is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
