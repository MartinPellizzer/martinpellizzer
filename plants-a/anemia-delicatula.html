Anemia delicatula

Anemia delicatula, commonly known as the delicate anemone, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade. The plant is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, where it contributes to the understory vegetation.

Its delicate appearance and early blooming habit make it a valued addition to native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
