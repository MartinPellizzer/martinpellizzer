Agapetes salicifolia var. glanduliflora

Agapetes salicifolia var. glanduliflora, commonly known as the glandular-flowered agapete, is a shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that are clustered in racemes, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to a dark red color.

This species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in cool, moist climates for its attractive foliage and floral display.
