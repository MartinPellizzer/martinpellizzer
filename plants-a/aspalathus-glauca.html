Aspalathus glauca

Aspalathus glauca, commonly known as the silver bush, is a small, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features narrow, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Aspalathus glauca is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
