Agrostis alba var. colorata

Agrostis alba var. colorata, commonly known as the pink wheatgrass, is a variant of the common wheatgrass species.

It is a low-growing perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and distinctive pinkish or reddish coloration in the inflorescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

This ornamental grass is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
