Acanthus spinosus

Acanthus spinosus, commonly known as the spiny acanthus, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves with prominent veins and its showy, spires of purple or pink flowers. The plant features robust, woody stems and produces distinctive, spiny bracts that protect its inflorescences. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often used in garden designs for its architectural form and visual impact.

Acanthus spinosus is also valued in horticulture for its historical association with classical art and architecture.
