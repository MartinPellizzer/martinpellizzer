Aeollanthus paludosus

Aeollanthus paludosus, commonly known as the swamp lily, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in South America.

It is characterized by its slender, herbaceous stems and showy, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in moist, often swampy soils and is adapted to periodic flooding. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Aeollanthus paludosus plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food and shelter for various insect and bird species.
