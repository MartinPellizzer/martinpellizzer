Aconitum iwatekense

Aconitum iwatekense, commonly known as the Iwate aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Aconitum iwatekense thrives in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or stream banks.

Its toxic properties, due to the presence of alkaloids, make it a plant that requires careful handling.
