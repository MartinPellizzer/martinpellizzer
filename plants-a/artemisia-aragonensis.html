Artemisia aragonensis

Artemisia aragonensis, commonly known as Aragon wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, arid environments and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other aromatic herbs and medicinal plants.

Artemisia aragonensis is valued for its historical use in traditional medicine and its role in ecological systems.
