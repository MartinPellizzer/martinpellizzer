Albizia garrettii

Albizia garrettii, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in a fine, silky texture that gives it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod fruits containing flat, edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

Albizia garrettii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
