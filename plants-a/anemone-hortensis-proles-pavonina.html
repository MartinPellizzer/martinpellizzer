Anemone hortensis proles pavonina

Anemone hortensis proles pavonina, commonly known as the peacock anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features showy, semi-double to fully double flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom in early to mid-summer. The plant has finely cut, fern-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
