Amaranthus aureus

Amaranthus aureus, commonly known as the golden amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy inflorescences that range in color from gold to red, depending on the variety. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are usually green or reddish in color. Its seeds are small and often used as a grain in traditional cuisines, while the plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal.

Amaranthus aureus thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
