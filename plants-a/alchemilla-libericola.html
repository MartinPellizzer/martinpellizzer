Alchemilla libericola

Alchemilla libericola, commonly known as the mountain lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Alchemilla libericola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
