Adhatoda hookeriana

Adhatoda hookeriana, commonly known as the Indian Trumpet or Hooker's Trumpet, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually blue or violet in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.

Adhatoda hookeriana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional medicine.
