Asplenium crenulatum

Asplenium crenulatum, commonly known as the notched maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that have a distinctive notched appearance along the margins. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. The fronds are typically arching and can grow up to 30 cm in length.

Asplenium crenulatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant and intricate foliage.
