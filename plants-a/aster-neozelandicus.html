Aster neozelandicus

Aster neozelandicus, commonly known as the New Zealand aster, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments, such as forest floors and rocky areas. Its flowers are usually purple or blue, with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Aster neozelandicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for native insects and birds.
