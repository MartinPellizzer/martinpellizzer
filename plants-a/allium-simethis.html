Allium simethis

Allium simethis, commonly known as the Armenian garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes various species of onions and garlic. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in local cuisines.

The leaves are grass-like and form a dense rosette near the ground.
