Astragalus beckwithii var. sulcatus

Astragalus beckwithii var. sulcatus, commonly known as Beckwith's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to low moisture conditions. Its leaves are compound, with numerous small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds.

This species is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role in stabilizing soils in harsh environments.
