Amelanchier pisidica

Amelanchier pisidica, commonly known as the Caucasian serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It produces edible berries that ripen in early summer, typically turning a deep red or purple color. The plant has compound leaves with 5 to 9 leaflets, and its flowers are white or pale pink, appearing in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or open woodlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
