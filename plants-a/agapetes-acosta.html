Agapetes acosta

Agapetes acosta, commonly known as the Acosta's silverleaf, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically covered in a fine silvery or whitish pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. Agapetes acosta is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil.
