Abies vulgaris

Abies vulgaris, commonly known as the white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and its dense, dark green foliage that is often covered in a layer of white, powdery wax. The tree produces small, cylindrical cones that hang from the branches, containing seeds surrounded by a papery wing. Its bark is typically gray to brown, becoming furrowed with age, and its roots are deep and extensive, helping to stabilize soil in its native habitat.

The white fir is valued for its timber and is also a popular ornamental plant in gardens and parks.
