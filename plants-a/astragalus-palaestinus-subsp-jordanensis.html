Astragalus palaestinus subsp. jordanensis

Astragalus palaestinus subsp.

jordanensis, commonly known as Jordanian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East, particularly Jordan and parts of Israel. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with deep root systems.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by contributing to soil stabilization and providing nectar for pollinators.
