Ajuga mesogitana

Ajuga mesogitana, commonly known as the Greek bugleweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Valerianaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit. The plant features small, opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, arranged in spike-like clusters, and bloom in early spring.

Ajuga mesogitana is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
