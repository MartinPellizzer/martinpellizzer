Arabis colorata

Arabis colorata, commonly known as the candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil.

Arabis colorata is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its ornamental value.
