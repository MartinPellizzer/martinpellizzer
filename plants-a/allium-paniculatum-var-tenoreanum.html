Allium paniculatum var. tenoreanum

Allium paniculatum var. tenoreanum, commonly known as the Armenian garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces dense, branched inflorescences of small, purple to pink flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic bulbs, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and hardiness in temperate climates.
