Arisaema tortuosum var. curvatum

Arisaema tortuosum var. curvatum, commonly known as the twisted arrow plant, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features a rhizomatous underground stem and produces striking, often brightly colored, leaf-like bracts. Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped, giving it its common name, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
