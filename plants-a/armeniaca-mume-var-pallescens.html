Armeniaca mume var. pallescens

Armeniaca mume var. pallescens, commonly known as the white apricot, is a variant of the apricot tree native to China.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in early spring, preceding the development of its fruit. The leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge. The fruit is typically smaller and less sweet compared to common apricots, with a delicate flavor.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its unique fruit.
