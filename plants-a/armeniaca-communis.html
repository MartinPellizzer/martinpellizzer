Armeniaca communis

Armeniaca communis, commonly known as the peach, is a deciduous tree native to central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, pink or white flowers in the spring, followed by fuzzy, orange-red fruits. Its fruit contains a single large seed surrounded by a juicy, aromatic flesh.

Armeniaca communis is widely cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
