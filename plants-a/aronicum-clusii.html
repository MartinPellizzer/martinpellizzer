Aronicum clusii

Aronicum clusii, commonly known as red mountain mint, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and showy, clustered purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage contains essential oils that contribute to its strong, minty scent.

Aronicum clusii is valued in native plant gardens for its ecological benefits and ornamental appeal.
