Aloysia gratissima var. sellowii

Aloysia gratissima var. sellowii, commonly known as Sellow's verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue. The plant typically grows as a shrub with woody stems and aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
