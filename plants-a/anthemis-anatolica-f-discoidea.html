Anthemis anatolica f. discoidea

Anthemis anatolica f. discoidea, commonly known as the Anatolian dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Turkey and parts of the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it produces small, dry fruits called achenes.

This species is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
