Aphanamixis pinatubensis

Aphanamixis pinatubensis, commonly known as the Philippine ebony, is a member of the Ebenaceae family and is native to the Philippines.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in lowland and montane forests. The plant is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy appearance.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
