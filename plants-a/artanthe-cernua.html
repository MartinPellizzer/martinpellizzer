Artanthe cernua

Artanthe cernua, commonly known as the bentleaf artanthe, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features hairy, basal leaves that are often curled or crinkled, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in a dense cluster at the end of each stem. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
