Aster sonoriensis

Aster sonoriensis, commonly known as the Sonoran aster, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, often blooming in late summer to early fall.

Its structure includes narrow, linear leaves and a compact growth habit, making it well-suited for its harsh environment.
