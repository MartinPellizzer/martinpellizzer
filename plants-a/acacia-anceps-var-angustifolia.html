Acacia anceps var. angustifolia

Acacia anceps var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It features slender, angular branches and is characterized by its narrow, linear phyllodes that are typically green with a slight whitish bloom. The plant produces yellow globular flowerheads in late winter to early spring, which are composed of numerous small flowers. Its leaves are reduced to flattened, leaf-like structures called phyllodes, which perform the function of photosynthesis.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in dry sclerophyllous forests and open woodlands.
