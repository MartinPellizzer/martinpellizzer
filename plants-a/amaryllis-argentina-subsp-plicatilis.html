Amaryllis argentina subsp. plicatilis

Amaryllis argentina subsp.

plicatilis, commonly known as the pleated amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, white, and pink.

The plant features strap-like leaves that are often pleated, giving it its common name. It is a bulbous plant that thrives in well-drained soil and requires a period of dormancy to bloom successfully.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
