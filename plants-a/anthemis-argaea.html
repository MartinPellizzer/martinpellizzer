Anthemis argaea

Anthemis argaea, commonly known as the silverweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and roadsides. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic leaves.

The plant has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
