Astragalus goniatus

Astragalus goniatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to reddish-brown. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus goniatus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
