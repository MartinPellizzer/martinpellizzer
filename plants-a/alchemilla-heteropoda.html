Alchemilla heteropoda

Alchemilla heteropoda, commonly known as the lesser lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla heteropoda is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and its ability to thrive in humid conditions.
