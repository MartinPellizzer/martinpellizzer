Angelica dahurica

Angelica dahurica, commonly known as Chinese angelica or big root, is a biennial or perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like carrots and parsley. The plant is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its roots are the primary part used medicinally, containing compounds such as ligustilide and butylphthalide.

Angelica dahurica is also valued for its aromatic properties and is used in both culinary and herbal applications.
