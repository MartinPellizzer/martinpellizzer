Acer kwangnanense

Acer kwangnanense, commonly known as the Guangnan maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The tree produces samaroid fruits that disperse its seeds, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
