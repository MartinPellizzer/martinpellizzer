Astragalus tribuloides var. kirghisicus

Astragalus tribuloides var. kirghisicus, commonly known as the Kirghiz milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and grasslands. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

This species is also valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
