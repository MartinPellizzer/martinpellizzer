Adesmia spinosissima

Adesmia spinosissima, commonly known as the spiny adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and spiny leaves, which provide a natural defense against herbivores. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Adesmia spinosissima is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
