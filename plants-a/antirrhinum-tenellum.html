Antirrhinum tenellum

Antirrhinum tenellum, commonly known as the dwarf snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically blooms from late spring to early autumn, producing clusters of flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its overall textured appearance.

Antirrhinum tenellum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
