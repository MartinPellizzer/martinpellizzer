Adelia myrtifolia

Adelia myrtifolia, commonly known as the pink myrtle, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a glossy appearance.

Adelia myrtifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
