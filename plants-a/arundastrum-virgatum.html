Arundastrum virgatum

Arundastrum virgatum, commonly known as the Virginia arrowhead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and striking white flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The plant plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
