Antennaria neodioica subsp. petaloidea

Antennaria neodioica subsp.

petaloidea, commonly known as the pink sandwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, clustered stems and small, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in coastal or inland dunes. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It plays a role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and is adapted to withstand harsh, arid conditions.
