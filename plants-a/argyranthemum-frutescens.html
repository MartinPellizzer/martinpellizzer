Argyranthemum frutescens

Argyranthemum frutescens, commonly known as the silverleaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, velvety foliage and clusters of small, daisy-like white or pink flowers. The plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in full sun. It prefers well-drained soil and is drought-tolerant once established.

Argyranthemum frutescens is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
