Azara serrata var. serrata

Azara serrata var. serrata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *azara*, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are serrated along the edges. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits resembling cherries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
