Acacia pachyacra

Acacia pachyacra, commonly known as the thick-shanked acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flat, shield-like phyllodes that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. Acacia pachyacra is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various wildlife.
