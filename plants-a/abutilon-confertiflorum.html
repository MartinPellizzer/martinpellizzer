Abutilon confertiflorum

Abutilon confertiflorum, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.

Its unique appearance and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
