Alternanthera erecta

Alternanthera erecta, commonly known as red flame or red arrow, is a tropical perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, pink, or orange foliage, which is particularly striking when grown in full sun. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, inconspicuous white flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often used in water gardens or as a bedding plant.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
