Amerimnon foliaceum

Amerimnon foliaceum, commonly known as the leafy amerimnon, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and yellow, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
