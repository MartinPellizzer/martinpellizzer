Argyreia kerrii

Argyreia kerrii, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Philippines, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in various indigenous communities. Its flowers bloom in clusters and emit a sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.
