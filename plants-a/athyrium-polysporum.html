Athyrium polysporum

Athyrium polysporum, commonly known as the Japanese painted fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often variegated with shades of green, silver, and cream. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, humus-rich soil. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores, typical of the fern life cycle.

Athyrium polysporum is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
