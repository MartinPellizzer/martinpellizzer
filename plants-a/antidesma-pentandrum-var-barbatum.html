Antidesma pentandrum var. barbatum

Antidesma pentandrum var. barbatum, commonly known as the hairy antidesma, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, fleshy berries. Its growth habit is typically erect or spreading, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
