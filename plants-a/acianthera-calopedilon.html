Acianthera calopedilon

Acianthera calopedilon, commonly known as the spotted leaching orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with distinctive red or purple spots. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often epiphytic on trees. Its roots are specialized for absorbing nutrients from the air and decaying organic matter.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
