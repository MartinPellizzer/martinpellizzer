Alectryon oleifolius

Alectryon oleifolius, commonly known as the African olive, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by fleshy, orange-red berries that are edible for humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Alectryon oleifolius is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
