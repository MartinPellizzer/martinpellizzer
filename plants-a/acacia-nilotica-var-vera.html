Acacia nilotica var. vera

Acacia nilotica var. vera, commonly known as the pale acacia, is a flowering plant native to Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval leaflets. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in cylindrical spikes, which are followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

The pale acacia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of gum arabic.
