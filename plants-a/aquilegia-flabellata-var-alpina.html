Aquilegia flabellata var. alpina

Aquilegia flabellata var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine garden bell, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a distinctive flared shape. The plant typically grows in rocky or meadow habitats at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are compound, with finely divided foliage that adds to its ornamental appeal.

The species is valued in horticulture for its beauty and ability to bloom in challenging environments.
