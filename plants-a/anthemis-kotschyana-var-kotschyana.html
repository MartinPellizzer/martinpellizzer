Anthemis kotschyana var. kotschyana

Anthemis kotschyana var. kotschyana, commonly known as the Kashmir goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, erect stems with finely divided, gray-green leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow composite flowers in late summer, consisting of a central disk surrounded by ray florets. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in alpine and subalpine ecosystems.
