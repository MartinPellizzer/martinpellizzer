Alsine michauxii var. erecta

Alsine michauxii var. erecta, commonly known as the erect alsike clover, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
