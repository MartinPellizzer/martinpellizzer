Arundo corea

Arundo corea, commonly known as the Korean reed, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and riverbanks, where it thrives in moist soil conditions. Its flowering spikes produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked.

Arundo corea is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
