Astragalus musaianus

Astragalus musaianus, commonly known as the Mongolian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, featuring deep root systems that help it survive in dry conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across open landscapes.
