Ailanthus pongelion

Ailanthus pongelion, commonly known as the tree of heaven, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a distinctive bipinnate structure. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by woody, winged seeds. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The species is often considered invasive in many regions outside its native habitat.
