Acacia robbertsei

Acacia robbertsei, commonly known as the Robberts' acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features straight, often thorned stems and bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, narrow seed pods.

Acacia robbertsei is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
