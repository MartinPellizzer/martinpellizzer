Anoda hastata

Anoda hastata, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with toothed edges. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and butterflies, making it an important species for pollinators.

Anoda hastata also contains milky sap, which is a distinguishing feature of the genus Anoda.
