Austrolycopodium aberdaricum

Austrolycopodium aberdaricum, commonly known as the Aberdare clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of Kenya, particularly in the Aberdare Mountains. This plant is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and a compact, cushion-like growth form. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

Austrolycopodium aberdaricum plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing microhabitat for various invertebrates.
