Acanthonychia

Acanthonychia, commonly known as the spiny leaf cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, paddle-shaped stems that store water, enabling it to survive in arid environments. The plant typically has small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of yellow or orange. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it produces berries that contain seeds.

Acanthonychia is native to regions of South America and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.
