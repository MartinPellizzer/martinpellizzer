Astragalus pishanxianensis

Astragalus pishanxianensis, commonly known as the Pishanxian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has compound leaves with small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
