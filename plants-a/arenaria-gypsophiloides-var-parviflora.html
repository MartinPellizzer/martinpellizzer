Arenaria gypsophiloides var. parviflora

Arenaria gypsophiloides var. parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf rock sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments. The plant features small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically blue-gray in color, providing a striking contrast to the white blooms.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in difficult garden conditions.
