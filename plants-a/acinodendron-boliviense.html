Acinodendron boliviense

Acinodendron boliviense, commonly known as the Bolivian acacia, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It is adapted to dry and rocky environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and ecosystem.
