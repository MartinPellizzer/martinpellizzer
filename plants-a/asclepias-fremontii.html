Asclepias fremontii

Asclepias fremontii, commonly known as the fremont's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to reddish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, hairy leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.

Asclepias fremontii is an important host plant for monarch butterflies, providing nourishment for their larvae.
