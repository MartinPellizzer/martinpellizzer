Abutilon notolophium

Abutilon notolophium, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
