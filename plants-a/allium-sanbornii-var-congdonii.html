Allium sanbornii var. congdonii

Allium sanbornii var. congdonii, commonly known as the Sanborn's garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers. The plant is characterized by its strong garlic-like aroma, which is most pronounced in the leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses, particularly in gourmet cooking.
