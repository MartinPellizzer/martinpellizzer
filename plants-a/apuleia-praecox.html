Apuleia praecox

Apuleia praecox, commonly known as the early apuleia, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its timber and medicinal properties.
