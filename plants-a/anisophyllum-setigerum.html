Anisophyllum setigerum

Anisophyllum setigerum, commonly known as the rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Anisophyllum setigerum produces small white or yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.
