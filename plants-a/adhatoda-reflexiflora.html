Adhatoda reflexiflora

Adhatoda reflexiflora, commonly known as the Indian borage, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinctive aromatic scent. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
