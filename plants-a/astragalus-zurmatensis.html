Astragalus zurmatensis

Astragalus zurmatensis, commonly known as the Zermat milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this species thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Astragalus zurmatensis is considered a rare and endangered species.
