Astragalus decumbens var. oblongifolius

Astragalus decumbens var. oblongifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features slender, prostrate stems that root at the nodes, and it produces small, yellow flowers clustered in racemes. Its leaves are oblong and have a distinctive pinnate structure with small, rounded leaflets.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
