Aulomyrcia stricta

Aulomyrcia stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white or pink flowers. It typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils.

Aulomyrcia stricta is often used in traditional Aboriginal medicine for its purported healing properties.
