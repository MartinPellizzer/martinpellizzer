Amaranthus caudatus f. alopecurus

Amaranthus caudatus f. alopecurus, commonly known as the curly top amaranth, is a variant of the amaranth species characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescence.

This plant is native to the Americas and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant, colorful bracts. The foliage is typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds to its visual appeal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its nutritional content, as its seeds are rich in protein and minerals.
