Alsodeia mutica

Alsodeia mutica, commonly known as the hairy alsike, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Alsodeia mutica is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
