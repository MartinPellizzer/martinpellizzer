Acacia pachypoda

Acacia pachypoda, commonly known as the umbrella thorn acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-shaped crown formed by its dense, drooping branches. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are often reduced to small, scale-like structures in hot, dry conditions. Its yellow flowers are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences and attract various pollinators.

Acacia pachypoda is adapted to survive in harsh environments with its deep root system and ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria.
