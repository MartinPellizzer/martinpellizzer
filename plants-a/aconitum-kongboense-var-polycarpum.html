Aconitum kongboense var. polycarpum

Aconitum kongboense var. polycarpum, commonly known as the Tibetan monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately compound leaves with a distinctive notched appearance, and its roots are thick and fleshy. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
