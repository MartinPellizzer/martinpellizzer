Acer cappadocicum subsp. trilobum

Acer cappadocicum subsp.

trilobum, commonly known as the Turkish maple, is a deciduous tree native to Turkey and parts of the Middle East. It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three lobes, giving it a unique appearance.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by samaras that aid in seed dispersal. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This species is often found in rocky or mountainous regions, adapting well to dry and poor soil conditions.
