Atropis millegrana

Atropis millegrana, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous yellow flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Atropis millegrana is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
