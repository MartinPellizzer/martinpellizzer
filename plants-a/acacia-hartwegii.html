Acacia hartwegii

Acacia hartwegii, commonly known as the Hartweg's wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Acacia hartwegii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and efficient water use.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
