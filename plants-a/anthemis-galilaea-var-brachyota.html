Anthemis galilaea var. brachyota

Anthemis galilaea var. brachyota, commonly known as the short-stalked tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are aromatic and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and emit a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
