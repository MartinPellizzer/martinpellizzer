Aralia dasyphylla var. strigosa

Aralia dasyphylla var. strigosa, commonly known as the hairy aralia, is a variant of the broad-leaf aralia native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its clusters of small, white flowers appear in the summer, followed by dark blue berries that are attractive to birds.

This variant is valued for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various garden conditions.
