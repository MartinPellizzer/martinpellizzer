Anaphalis sinica var. lanata

Anaphalis sinica var. lanata, commonly known as the woolly cudweed, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, white, woolly coating on its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, dandelion-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
