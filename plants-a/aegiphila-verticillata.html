Aegiphila verticillata

Aegiphila verticillata, commonly known as the clustered leaf mallow, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and arranged in whorls of three to five. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, rocky environments.

Aegiphila verticillata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in xeriscaping.
