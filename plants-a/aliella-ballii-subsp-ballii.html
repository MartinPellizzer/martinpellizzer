Aliella ballii subsp. ballii

Aliella ballii subsp.

ballii, commonly known as the Ball's rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and segmented fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

The species is noted for its resilience to cold climates and is often found in areas with limited soil nutrients.
