Achmandra odorata

Achmandra odorata, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has long, slender leaves and produces intricate, orchid-like blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Achmandra odorata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
