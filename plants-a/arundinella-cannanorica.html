Arundinella cannanorica

Arundinella cannanorica, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can grow up to 3 meters in height, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically green or reddish in color. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a valuable ornamental plant and a potential invasive species.

Arundinella cannanorica is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is used in landscaping and traditional crafts.
