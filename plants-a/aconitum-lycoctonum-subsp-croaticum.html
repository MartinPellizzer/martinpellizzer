Aconitum lycoctonum subsp. croaticum

Aconitum lycoctonum subsp.

croaticum, commonly known as Croatian monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It features striking purple-blue flowers arranged in a spire, typical of the Ranunculaceae family.

The plant has palmate leaves with deep lobes and is characterized by its toxic alkaloid content. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
