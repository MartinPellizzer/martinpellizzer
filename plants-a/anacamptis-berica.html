Anacamptis berica

Anacamptis berica, commonly known as the Balkan orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a single, erect flowering stem with several showy, tubular flowers. Anacamptis berica thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitats.
