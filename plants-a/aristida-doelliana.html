Aristida doelliana

Aristida doelliana, commonly known as the desert dropseed, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically tan to brown in color. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its fine texture and upright growth habit make it an attractive ornamental plant in dry landscapes.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small animals and insects in its native environment.
