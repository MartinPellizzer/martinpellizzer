Aesculus megaphylla

Aesculus megaphylla, commonly known as the giant horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The tree produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers in clustered panicles during the spring. Its fruit is a spiny capsule containing several chestnut-like seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and floral display.
