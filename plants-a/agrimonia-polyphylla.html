Agrimonia polyphylla

Agrimonia polyphylla, commonly known as the many-leaved agrimony, is a perennial herb native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces oval, hairy leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant is known for its medicinal properties, including use in traditional herbal remedies.

Agrimonia polyphylla is often found in dry meadows, woodland edges, and rocky soils.
