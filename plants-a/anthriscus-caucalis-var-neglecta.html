Anthriscus caucalis var. neglecta

Anthriscus caucalis var. neglecta, commonly known as lesser cow parsley, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial that grows up to 1 meter tall, featuring finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in umbels, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
