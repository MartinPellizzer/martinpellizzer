Aponogeton subconjugatus

Aponogeton subconjugatus, commonly known as the dwarf water arrowhead, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, arrow-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, with roots submerged in the substrate. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically short-lived and emerge from the water. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, preferring moderate to high light conditions.

Aponogeton subconjugatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in ponds and water gardens due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
