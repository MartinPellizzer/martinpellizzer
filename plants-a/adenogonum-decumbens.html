Adenogonum decumbens

Adenogonum decumbens, commonly known as the creeping adenogonum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It features prostrate stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread along the ground. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Adenogonum decumbens is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
