Aspidistra erecta

Aspidistra erecta, commonly known as the Japanese evergreen, is a perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers enclosed in a spadix, which is surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Aspidistra erecta is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its tolerance for a variety of indoor environments.
