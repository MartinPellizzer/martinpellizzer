Astragalus antalyensis

Astragalus antalyensis, commonly known as the Antalya milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and may have potential medicinal uses.

Its ecological significance is notable in its native habitat.
