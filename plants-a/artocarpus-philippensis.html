Artocarpus philippensis

Artocarpus philippensis, commonly known as the Philippine breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, spiky fruit. The fruit, which is typically green when unripe, turns yellow or orange when mature and is edible when cooked. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and is often found in lowland forests.

It plays an important role in both local ecosystems and traditional agriculture.
