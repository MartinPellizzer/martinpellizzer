Avenula pratensis subsp. amethystea

Avenula pratensis subsp.

amethystea, commonly known as the amethyst grass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale blue to purple florets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and along stream banks.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
