Athyrium heterophlebium

Athyrium heterophlebium, commonly known as the Japanese painted fern, is a deciduous fern native to East Asia.

It features elegant, bipinnate fronds that are often adorned with a striking, variegated pattern of light and dark green. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its role in ecological restoration projects.
