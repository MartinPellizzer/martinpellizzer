Arctotis squarrosa

Arctotis squarrosa, commonly known as the woolly arctotis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and bright, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in a compact, bushy form, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Arctotis squarrosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
