Achimenes misera

Achimenes misera, commonly known as the weeping achimenes, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often hairy to the touch. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and containers.

Its blooming period typically occurs during the warmer months, making it a popular choice for summer displays.
