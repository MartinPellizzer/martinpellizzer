Acaulimalva alismatifolia

Acaulimalva alismatifolia, commonly known as the water mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland environments in Asia.

It features a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and colonize moist soils effectively. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it produces showy pink or purple flowers with five petals. It thrives in shallow water or damp terrestrial habitats, making it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.
