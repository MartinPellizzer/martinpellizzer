Astragalus cruciatus var. pterolobus

Astragalus cruciatus var. pterolobus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with pinnate arrangement and produces pods that contain small, hard seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its range.
