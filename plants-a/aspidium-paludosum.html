Aspidium paludosum

Aspidium paludosum, commonly known as the swamp maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like fronds that are typically bioluminescent in certain conditions. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and help anchor it to damp soils, while its spore-producing structures are located on the underside of its fronds.

Aspidium paludosum plays an important ecological role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
