Agave yavapaiensis

Agave yavapaiensis, commonly known as the Yavapai agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color with a waxy coating. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Agave yavapaiensis is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
