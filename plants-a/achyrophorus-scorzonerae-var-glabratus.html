Achyrophorus scorzonerae var. glabratus

Achyrophorus scorzonerae var. glabratus, commonly known as the smoothleaf scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
