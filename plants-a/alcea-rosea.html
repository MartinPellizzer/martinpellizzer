Alcea rosea

Alcea rosea, commonly known as the common hollyhock, is a flowering plant in the Malvaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, velvety flowers that come in a range of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Alcea rosea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
