Aloe concinna

Aloe concinna, commonly known as the dwarf aloe or Cape aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers on a tall stem, which bloom during the summer months. Aloe concinna is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its leaves contain a gel-like substance that is sometimes used for its perceived medicinal properties.
