Acalypha divaricata

Acalypha divaricata, commonly known as the red-veined or variegated spurflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with distinctive red or yellow veins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are often clustered at the ends of branches. Its ornamental value comes from its colorful foliage, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Acalypha divaricata is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to partial shade.
