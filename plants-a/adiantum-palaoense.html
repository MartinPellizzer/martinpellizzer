Adiantum palaoense

Adiantum palaoense, commonly known as the Palaoense maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are typically bright green and finely divided. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forest understories. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce numerous upright, lanceolate leaves.

Adiantum palaoense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage.
