Anthurium cruxiphyllum

Anthurium cruxiphyllum, commonly known as the Cross-veined Anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns of veins. The plant produces striking spadices, which are modified leaves that enclose the actual flowers. It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Anthurium cruxiphyllum is popular as a decorative plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
