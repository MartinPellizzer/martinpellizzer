Acacia lachnocarpa

Acacia lachnocarpa, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and produces yellow flowers in clusters. The plant has small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate salt spray, making it a common species in coastal ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and habitat for various wildlife.
