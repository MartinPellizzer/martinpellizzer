Artemisia amorpha

Artemisia amorpha, commonly known as silverleaf sage, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Artemisia amorpha has been used traditionally for its aromatic properties and in herbal medicine.
