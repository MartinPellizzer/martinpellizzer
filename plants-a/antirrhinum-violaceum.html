Antirrhinum violaceum

Antirrhinum violaceum, commonly known as the purple snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dry seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Antirrhinum violaceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both garden use and horticultural research.
