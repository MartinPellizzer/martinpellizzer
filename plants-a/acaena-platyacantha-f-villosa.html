Acaena platyacantha f. villosa

Acaena platyacantha f. villosa, commonly known as the hairy spinney, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
