Artemisia saharae

Artemisia saharae, commonly known as the Saharan wormwood, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense heads, typical of the Compositae family. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.

Artemisia saharae is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
