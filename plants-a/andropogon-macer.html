Andropogon macer

Andropogon macer, commonly known as purple false panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, purple or pinkish flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in wet to moist soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
