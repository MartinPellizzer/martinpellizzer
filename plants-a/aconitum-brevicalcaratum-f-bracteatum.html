Aconitum brevicalcaratum f. bracteatum

Aconitum brevicalcaratum f. bracteatum, commonly known as the bracteate monkshood, is a variant of the monkshood plant characterized by its distinctive bracts.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features showy blue to purple flowers arranged in a spire, typical of the genus Aconitum. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a striking, almost poisonous appearance.

This variant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique foliage.
