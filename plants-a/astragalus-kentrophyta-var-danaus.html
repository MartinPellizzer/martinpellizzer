Astragalus kentrophyta var. danaus

Astragalus kentrophyta var. danaus, commonly known as the Danaus milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many nitrogen-fixing plants.

This variety is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats, contributing to soil stabilization and supporting local wildlife.
