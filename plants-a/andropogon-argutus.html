Andropogon argutus

Andropogon argutus, commonly known as eastern woodland grass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils in shaded or partially shaded environments, such as along stream banks and in forest understories. The plant has slender, cylindrical leaves and produces panicles of small, pale green to golden flowers. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.
