Amaranthus paniculatus var. strictus

Amaranthus paniculatus var. strictus, commonly known as the Mexican amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and feather-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces dense, conical inflorescences bearing small, clustered flowers that range in color from red to orange. Its seeds are encased in a spiky, three-lobed hull, which is used in traditional culinary and ceremonial practices.

It is valued for its nutritional content, including high levels of protein and minerals.
