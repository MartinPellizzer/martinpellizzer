Amblyopogon xanthocephalus

Amblyopogon xanthocephalus, commonly known as the yellow-headed grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often tinged with yellow at the base. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, commonly found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for erosion control and ornamental purposes.
