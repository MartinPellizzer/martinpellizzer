Althaea micrantha

Althaea micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered mallows, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pale pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, and its fibrous roots can be used in traditional herbal medicine.

Althaea micrantha is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
