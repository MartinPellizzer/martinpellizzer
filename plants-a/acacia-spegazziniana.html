Acacia spegazziniana

Acacia spegazziniana, commonly known as the Spegazzini acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually phyllodes, and its yellow, globular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It has small, woody pods that contain seeds, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various animals.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
