Aster laevis var. undulatifolius

Aster laevis var. undulatifolius, commonly known as the wavy-leaved aster, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and broad, undulating leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and wetlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
