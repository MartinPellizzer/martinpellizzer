Aeschynomene dissitiflora

Aeschynomene dissitiflora, commonly known as hairy leaf aeschynomene, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its slender stems and alternate, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in axillary clusters. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a common weed in agricultural fields.
