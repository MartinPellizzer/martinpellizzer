Allium ericetorum subsp. palentinum

Allium ericetorum subsp.

palentinum, commonly known as the Scottish garlic or mountain garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the British Isles. It features basal leaves that are narrow and grass-like, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in a dense, spherical cluster.

The plant produces small, bulbous structures that are used for propagation. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and woodland margins.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
