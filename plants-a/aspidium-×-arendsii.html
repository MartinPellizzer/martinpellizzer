Aspidium × arendsii

Aspidium × arendsii, commonly known as the Arends' shield fern, is a hybrid fern species resulting from the cross between Aspidium cuneifolium and Aspidium polyblepharum.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, giving it a lacy appearance. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate regions with high humidity. Its spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds in clusters, and it reproduces through spore dispersal rather than seeds.

Aspidium × arendsii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in controlled indoor or greenhouse settings.
