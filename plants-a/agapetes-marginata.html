Agapetes marginata

Agapetes marginata, commonly known as the silverleaf agapete, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive silvery margin, which gives it its common name.

Agapetes marginata produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, followed by edible berries that are a food source for various wildlife.
