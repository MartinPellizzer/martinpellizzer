Alcea talassica

Alcea talassica, commonly known as the coastal hollyhock, is a herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to salty, windy environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Alcea talassica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging coastal conditions.
