Acacia aurita

Acacia aurita, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, feathery leaves that are typically green with a yellowish or whitish underside. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, spherical clusters, which bloom in spring. Acacia aurita is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy soils.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in soil rehabilitation efforts.
