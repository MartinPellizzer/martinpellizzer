Abies firma var. brachyphylla

Abies firma var. brachyphylla, commonly known as the dwarf fir, is a variant of the fir tree native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, conical shape, making it a striking ornamental plant. The foliage consists of short, stiff needles that are typically dark green, often with a silvery underside. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to maintain its form even in harsh environmental conditions.
