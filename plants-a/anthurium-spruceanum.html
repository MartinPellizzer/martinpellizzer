Anthurium spruceanum

Anthurium spruceanum, commonly known as the Spruce Anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features dark green, leathery leaves that are often heart-shaped or lanceolate in form. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a showy, brightly colored spathe.

Anthurium spruceanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive in humid, warm environments.
