Acacia multipinnata

Acacia multipinnata, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry branches and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow globular flowers, which are typically arranged in dense clusters. Acacia multipinnata is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
