Avena clauda var. solida

Avena clauda var. solida, commonly known as the solid oat, is a variant of the oat species characterized by its dense, compact growth habit.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that becomes more pronounced as the plant matures.

This variety is often grown as an ornamental grass due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
