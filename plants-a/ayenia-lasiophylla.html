Ayenia lasiophylla

Ayenia lasiophylla, commonly known as the roughleaf ayenia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, where it thrives in dry, rocky habitats. The plant is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. It is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions, adapting well to harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
