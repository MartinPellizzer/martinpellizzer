Ammannia aurita

Ammannia aurita, commonly known as the redroot dayflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright red roots and clusters of small, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a reddish tinge along the veins.

Ammannia aurita is an important species in wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
