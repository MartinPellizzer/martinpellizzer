Aridaria fulva

Aridaria fulva, commonly known as the golden sandbloom, is a succulent plant adapted to arid environments.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the rainy season, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its root system is shallow but extensive, enabling it to quickly absorb moisture from the soil.

Aridaria fulva is often found in rocky soils and sandy plains across regions with low rainfall.
