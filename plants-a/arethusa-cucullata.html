Arethusa cucullata

Arethusa cucullata, commonly known as the hooded violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive violet-blue flowers with a hood-like structure over the reproductive organs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Arethusa cucullata is often found in association with other wetland plants, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
