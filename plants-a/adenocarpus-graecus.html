Adenocarpus graecus

Adenocarpus graecus, commonly known as the Greek milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds with tufted hairs, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Adenocarpus graecus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
