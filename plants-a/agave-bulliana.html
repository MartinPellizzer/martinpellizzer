Agave bulliana

Agave bulliana, commonly known as the blue agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with white to grayish variegation. The plant forms a rosette shape, and its leaves are lined with sharp spines. It produces a tall flowering stalk that bears small, greenish-white flowers.

Agave bulliana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in the production of tequila.
