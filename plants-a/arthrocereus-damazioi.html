Arthrocereus damazioi

Arthrocereus damazioi, commonly known as the Damazo's night bloomer, is a flowering cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and spiny, segmented structure, typical of the Cactaceae family. The plant produces large, showy white flowers at night, which are pollinated by moths. It thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habit, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
