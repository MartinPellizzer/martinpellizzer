Acalypha reticulata var. urophylloides

Acalypha reticulata var. urophylloides, commonly known as the red-veined nettle or red-veined copperleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, red-veined leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are typically borne in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
