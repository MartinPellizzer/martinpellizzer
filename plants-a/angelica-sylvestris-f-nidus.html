Angelica sylvestris f. nidus

Angelica sylvestris f. nidus, commonly known as the clustered angelica, is a botanical variant of the common angelica.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit with multiple stems arising from a single rootstock. The plant produces large, umbrella-shaped inflorescences composed of small, yellow flowers. Its leaves are large, pinnately compound, and have a strong, aromatic scent.

This variety is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its medicinal properties.
