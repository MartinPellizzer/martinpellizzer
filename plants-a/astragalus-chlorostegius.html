Astragalus chlorostegius

Astragalus chlorostegius, commonly known as the blue-stemmed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often blue-green stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus chlorostegius is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinators.
