Alchemilla tiryalensis

Alchemilla tiryalensis, commonly known as the Tiryal alkanet, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces achenes as its fruit, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
