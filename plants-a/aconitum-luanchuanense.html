Aconitum luanchuanense

Aconitum luanchuanense, commonly known as the Luanchuan aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy flowers. The plant produces violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments that are covered in a dense layer of hairs.

Aconitum luanchuanense contains potent alkaloids, making it toxic and requiring careful handling.
