Anacyclus × valentinus

Anacyclus × valentinus, commonly known as the Valentine's Day flower, is a hybrid plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white, often resembling heart-shaped petals. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. It is typically short-lived, reaching heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and romantic appearance.
