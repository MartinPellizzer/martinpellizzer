Aconitum noveboracense

Aconitum noveboracense, commonly known as New York aconite, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces large, egg-shaped seed pods that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care.
