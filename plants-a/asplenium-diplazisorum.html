Asplenium diplazisorum

Asplenium diplazisorum, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, finely divided fronds that resemble a fern's traditional appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in many fern species.

Asplenium diplazisorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
