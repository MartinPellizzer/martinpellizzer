Apium dulce

Apium dulce, commonly known as sweet celery, is a flowering plant in the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its hollow, ridged stalks and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in umbrella-shaped clusters called umbels. Its roots are often harvested for their aromatic, crisp texture and mild flavor.

Apium dulce is native to southern Europe and is cultivated for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
