Atriplex paludosa var. acuticordata

Atriplex paludosa var. acuticordata, commonly known as the sharp-leafed saltbush, is a perennial shrub native to wet or seasonally flooded areas of the western United States.

It is characterized by its succulent, alternate leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, typical of the Amaranthaceae family. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it an important species for stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it valuable for ecological restoration projects.
