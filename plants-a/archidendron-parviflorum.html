Archidendron parviflorum

Archidendron parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered legume, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in moist, tropical forests. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many leguminous plants known for nitrogen fixation.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
