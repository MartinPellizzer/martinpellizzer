Astragalus vasticola

Astragalus vasticola, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow to green pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds.

Astragalus vasticola plays a role in stabilizing soil in desert environments and is of interest in ecological restoration projects.
