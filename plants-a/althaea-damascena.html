Althaea damascena

Althaea damascena, commonly known as the rose of Sharon, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia and the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Althaea damascena is also known for its mucilage-rich roots, which have been used in traditional medicine.
