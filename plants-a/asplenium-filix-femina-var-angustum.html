Asplenium filix-femina var. angustum

Asplenium filix-femina var. angustum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
