Aldama excelsa

Aldama excelsa, commonly known as the Mexican cedar, is a tall evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Cupressaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and conical shape. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and timber quality, it is cultivated in various regions for landscaping and commercial purposes.
