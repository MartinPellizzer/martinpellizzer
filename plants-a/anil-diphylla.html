Anil diphylla

Anil diphylla, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. The plant has a climbing habit and produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are typically tubular with five petals and are pollinated by birds and insects.

Anil diphylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
