Andropogon neesii subvar. riedelianus

Andropogon neesii subvar. riedelianus, commonly known as the Riedel's switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
