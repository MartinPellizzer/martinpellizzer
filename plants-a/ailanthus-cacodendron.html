Ailanthus cacodendron

Ailanthus cacodendron, commonly known as the silky ash, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a silvery underside. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by winged fruits that aid in wind dispersal. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas due to its adaptability.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for landscaping purposes.
