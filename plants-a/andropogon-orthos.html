Andropogon orthos

Andropogon orthos, commonly known as common bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and blue-green foliage, which becomes more pronounced during the growing season. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Andropogon orthos is often found in prairies, open woodlands, and disturbed sites, contributing to ecological stability and soil conservation.
