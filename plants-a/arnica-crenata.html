Arnica crenata

Arnica crenata, commonly known as mountain arnica, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, thriving at high altitudes. Its flowers are yellow to orange in color, with a distinctive central disc surrounded by ray florets.

Arnica crenata is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
