Amphinomia decumbens

Amphinomia decumbens, commonly known as the creeping amphinomia, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It features slender, prostrate stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a slight purple tinge. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slightly succulent appearance.

Amphinomia decumbens is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.
