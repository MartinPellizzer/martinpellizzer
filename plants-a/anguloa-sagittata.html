Anguloa sagittata

Anguloa sagittata, commonly known as the arrowhead lily, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant features strap-like, evergreen leaves that form a rosette at the base. It produces a tall, slender stem that bears clusters of showy, bell-shaped blooms.

Anguloa sagittata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
