Anemone decapetala var. araucana

Anemone decapetala var. araucana, commonly known as the Araucana anemone, is a striking perennial plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive, petal-like structure that resembles a crown. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a soft, silvery down. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also valued for its historical and cultural significance in its native region.
