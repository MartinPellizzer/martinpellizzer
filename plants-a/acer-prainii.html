Acer prainii

Acer prainii, commonly known as the Prain's maple, is a deciduous tree native to the Himalayas.

It typically grows at high altitudes, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The tree is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, which turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in early spring before the leaves emerge.

Acer prainii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
