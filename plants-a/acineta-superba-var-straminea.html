Acineta superba var. straminea

Acineta superba var. straminea, commonly known as the yellow acineta, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the stems are upright and often branched.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
