Aster lemmonii

Aster lemmonii, commonly known as Lemmon's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster lemmonii is a perennial species that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.
