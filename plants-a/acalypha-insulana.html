Acalypha insulana

Acalypha insulana, commonly known as the island false spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a distinct reddish or purple hue on the underside. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, round berries that are edible.

Acalypha insulana is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils.
