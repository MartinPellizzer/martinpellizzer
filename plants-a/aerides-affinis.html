Aerides affinis

Aerides affinis, commonly known as the Orchid of the forest, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters of up to 12 blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are strap-shaped and dark green, providing a lush backdrop for its colorful blossoms.

Aerides affinis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
