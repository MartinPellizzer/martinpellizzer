Acacia deltoidea var. pungens

Acacia deltoidea var. pungens, commonly known as the umbrella thorn acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its flat, fan-shaped leaves and long, slender, thorned stems. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in savannas and along riverbanks.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
