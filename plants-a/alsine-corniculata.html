Alsine corniculata

Alsine corniculata, commonly known as the horned vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as fields and roadsides. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain viable in the soil for several years.

Alsine corniculata is often considered a weed but has ecological value as a food source for certain insects.
