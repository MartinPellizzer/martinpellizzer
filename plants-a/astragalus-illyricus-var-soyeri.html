Astragalus illyricus var. soyeri

Astragalus illyricus var. soyeri, commonly known as the Balkan milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and steppe habitats. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being narrow and lance-shaped.

The species is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its potential use in traditional medicine.
