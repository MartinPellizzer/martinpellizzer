Argylia tomentosa

Argylia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly argylia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
