Aconitum laeve var. curvipilosum

Aconitum laeve var. curvipilosum, commonly known as the curly-hair aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple spathaceous flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with lobes that are often hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant contains potent alkaloids, making it toxic and requiring careful handling.
