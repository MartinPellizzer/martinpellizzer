Acer semenovii

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves, which are typically lobed and have a striking red or orange hue during the autumn season. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.
