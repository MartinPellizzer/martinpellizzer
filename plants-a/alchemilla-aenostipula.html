Alchemilla aenostipula

Alchemilla aenostipula, commonly known as narrow-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves that are covered in a dense layer of silky white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in compact clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla aenostipula is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
