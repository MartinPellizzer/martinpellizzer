Anthyllis vulneraria var. maura

Anthyllis vulneraria var. maura, commonly known as the Maura Scotch broom, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a creeping growth habit with woody stems and produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
