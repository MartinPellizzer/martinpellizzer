Acianthera auriculata

Acianthera auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate acianthera, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant features a sympodial growth pattern, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette, while the flowers are borne on terminal racemes.

Acianthera auriculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
