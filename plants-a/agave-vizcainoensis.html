Agave vizcainoensis

Agave vizcainoensis, commonly known as the Vizcaino agave, is a species native to the Baja California peninsula in Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments. The leaves are typically blue-green in color with white or grayish markings, providing camouflage against the rocky terrain. It produces a tall flowering stalk that bears small, white flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and moths.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens due to its low water requirements.
