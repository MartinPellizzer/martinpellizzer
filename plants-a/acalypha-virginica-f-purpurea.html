Acalypha virginica f. purpurea

Acalypha virginica f. purpurea, commonly known as purple-leaf ironweed, is a strikingly colorful shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple foliage, which contrasts beautifully with its green stems and yellow flowers. The plant features palmate leaves that are often tinged with red or purple hues, especially during the growing season. Its inflorescences consist of clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

This ornamental plant is well-suited for garden use due to its eye-catching appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
