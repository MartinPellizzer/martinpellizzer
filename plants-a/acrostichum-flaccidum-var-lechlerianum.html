Acrostichum flaccidum var. lechlerianum

Acrostichum flaccidum var. lechlerianum, commonly known as the lechlerianum fern, is a variant of the flaccid fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a medium-sized fern characterized by its long, arching fronds that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The leaves are typically bipinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance, and they exhibit a range of green to brown hues depending on age and light exposure. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in dense tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
