Agrostis canina var. breviaristata

Agrostis canina var. breviaristata, commonly known as the short-awned bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is usually short and compact.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in lawn and garden settings.
