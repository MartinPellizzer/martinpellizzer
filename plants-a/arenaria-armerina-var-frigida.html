Arenaria armerina var. frigida

Arenaria armerina var. frigida, commonly known as the alpine sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine tundra and rocky slopes. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in harsh conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
