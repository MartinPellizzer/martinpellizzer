Astragalus libycus

Astragalus libycus, commonly known as the Libyan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow or white pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves and produces pods that contain small, round seeds. It is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or desert environments.

Astragalus libycus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its habitat.
