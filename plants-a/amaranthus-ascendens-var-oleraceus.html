Amaranthus ascendens var. oleraceus

Amaranthus ascendens var. oleraceus, commonly known as garden amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with branching stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, typically displaying vibrant red or purple hues.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also cultivated for its edible leaves and seeds.
