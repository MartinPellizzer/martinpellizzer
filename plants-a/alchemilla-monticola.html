Alchemilla monticola

Alchemilla monticola, commonly known as the alpine lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and resilience.
