Aster novi-belgii var. crenifolius

Aster novi-belgii var. crenifolius, commonly known as the curlyleaf aster, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curly, fern-like leaves and clusters of daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and prefers full sun to partial shade. It thrives in well-drained soil and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

The variety is noted for its compact growth habit and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
