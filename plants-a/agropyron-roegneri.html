Agropyron roegneri

Agropyron roegneri, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils. Agropyron roegneri is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
