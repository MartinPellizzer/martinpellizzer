Astragalus asterabadensis

Astragalus asterabadensis, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, particularly Iran and Afghanistan. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, compound leaves, and small, yellowish or greenish flowers that appear in clusters. It produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus asterabadensis is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
