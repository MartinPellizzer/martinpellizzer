Ambraria glabra var. tulbaghica

Ambraria glabra var. tulbaghica, commonly known as the tulbagh iceplant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, ribbed stems and leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its hardy nature and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its ability to prevent soil erosion in dry landscapes.
