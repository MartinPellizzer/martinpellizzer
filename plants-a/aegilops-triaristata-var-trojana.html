Aegilops triaristata var. trojana

Aegilops triaristata var. trojana, commonly known as the Trojan triticale, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between wheat and rye.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically three-branched, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its hardiness and adaptability.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in agricultural research and breeding programs.
