Astragalus schimperi

Astragalus schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.

Astragalus schimperi is also of interest for its potential medicinal properties.
