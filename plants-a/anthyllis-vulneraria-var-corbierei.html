Anthyllis vulneraria var. corbierei

Anthyllis vulneraria var. corbierei, commonly known as the Corsican broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in Corsica and Sardinia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local insect populations.
