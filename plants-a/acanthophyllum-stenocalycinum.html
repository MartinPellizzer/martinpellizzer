Acanthophyllum stenocalycinum

Acanthophyllum stenocalycinum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acanthophyllum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its spiny stems and small, succulent leaves that help reduce water loss. Native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is often found in desert environments where water is scarce.
