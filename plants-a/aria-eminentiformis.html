Aria eminentiformis

Aria eminentiformis, commonly known as the skyward lily, is a rare flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and striking white petals that resemble clouds. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern, allowing maximum sunlight absorption.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, Aria eminentiformis is considered a botanical curiosity and is often studied for its adaptation to extreme environments.
