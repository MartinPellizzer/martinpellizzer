Agoseris apargioides var. maritima

Agoseris apargioides var. maritima, commonly known as the coastal agoseris, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to maritime climates with salt spray and strong winds. It is often found in dunes and coastal grasslands, playing a role in stabilizing sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
