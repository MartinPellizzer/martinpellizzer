Aloe mwanzana

Aloe mwanzana, commonly known as the Zanzibar aloe, is a succulent plant native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating that helps it retain moisture. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with clusters of yellow flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Aloe mwanzana is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its leaves can be used for medicinal purposes, though it is less commonly cultivated for commercial use compared to other aloe species.
