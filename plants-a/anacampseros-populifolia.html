Anacampseros populifolia

Anacampseros populifolia, commonly known as the poplar-leaved stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it store water and reduce transpiration. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange, which bloom in summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular ornamental plant among succulent enthusiasts.
