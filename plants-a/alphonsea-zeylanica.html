Alphonsea zeylanica

Alphonsea zeylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon alphonse, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and compound leaves with elongated leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, round fruits with a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and the culinary use of its fruits.
