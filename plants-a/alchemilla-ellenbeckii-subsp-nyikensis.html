Alchemilla ellenbeckii subsp. nyikensis

Alchemilla ellenbeckii subsp.

nyikensis, commonly known as the Nyikeni lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to eastern Africa. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in early spring, which are clustered in cymes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is a subject of interest in botanical conservation efforts.
