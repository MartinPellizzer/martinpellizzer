Acacia glandulosa

Acacia glandulosa, commonly known as the hairy wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and feathery leaves, which are covered in small glands. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fibrous covering. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

Acacia glandulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
