Astragalus hoffmeisteri

Astragalus hoffmeisteri, commonly known as Hoffmeister's milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, legume-like pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is often of interest to botanists and conservationists.
