Amanoa marapiensis

Amanoa marapiensis, commonly known as the Marapi nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Indonesian island of Java.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
