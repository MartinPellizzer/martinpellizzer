Acacia euthycarpa

Acacia euthycarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are grouped into spherical heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, temperate climates.

Acacia euthycarpa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
