Aristida valida

Aristida valida, commonly known as the slender grass or narrowleaf dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the eastern and central United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed sites, reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are clustered at the ends of the stems. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.

Aristida valida is also important for wildlife, providing food and habitat for various insects and birds.
