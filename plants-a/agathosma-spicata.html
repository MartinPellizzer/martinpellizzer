Agathosma spicata

Agathosma spicata, commonly known as African geranium, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.

Agathosma spicata is also cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and resilience.
