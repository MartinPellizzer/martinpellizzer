Adenophora erysimoides

Adenophora erysimoides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Adenophora erysimoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
