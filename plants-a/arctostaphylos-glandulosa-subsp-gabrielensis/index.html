Arctostaphylos Glandulosa Subsp. Gabrielensis

Arctostaphylos glandulosa subsp. gabrielensis is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of California. This subspecies is part of the heath family, known for its hardy and drought-resistant characteristics. It thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in coastal and inland habitats. Its small, leathery leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers make it visually distinctive. Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
