Aristolochia laheyana

Aristolochia laheyana, commonly known as the Lahey's Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are often greenish or reddish in color. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and emits a mild, somewhat unpleasant odor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
