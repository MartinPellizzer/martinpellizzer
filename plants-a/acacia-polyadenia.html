Acacia polyadenia

Acacia polyadenia, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and is often found in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, globular clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, thread-like leaflets.

Acacia polyadenia is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important species in soil enrichment.
