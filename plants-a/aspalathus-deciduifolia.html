Aspalathus deciduifolia

Aspalathus deciduifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round fruits containing several seeds. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rocky or sandy environments.

Aspalathus deciduifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine.
