Agapetes costata

Agapetes costata, commonly known as the wrinkled silverleaf, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States and parts of western Canada.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its leaves, which are typically dark green with a silvery underside and a distinctive wavy or wrinkled margin. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Agapetes costata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
