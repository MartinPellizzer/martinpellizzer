Adenandra coriacea var. oblongifolia

Adenandra coriacea var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the rough-leaved adenandra, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in a rough, granular texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in arid conditions.
