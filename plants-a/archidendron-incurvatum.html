Archidendron incurvatum

Archidendron incurvatum, commonly known as the yellow shower tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its canopy. The plant has compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate structures, and its bark is often rough and fissured. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
