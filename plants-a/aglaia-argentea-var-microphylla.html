Aglaia argentea var. microphylla

Aglaia argentea var. microphylla, commonly known as silver aglaia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in its native habitats.
