Arctagrostis latifolia f. aristata

Arctagrostis latifolia f. aristata, commonly known as the broadleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its wide, flat blades and a bluish-green coloration, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-based flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
