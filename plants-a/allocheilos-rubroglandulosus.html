Allocheilos rubroglandulosus

Allocheilos rubroglandulosus, commonly known as the red glandular orchid, is a species of orchid native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red and purple floral structures, which are adorned with glandular hairs. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are specialized for pollination by specific insect species, showcasing a unique co-evolutionary relationship.

The botanical features of this orchid make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
