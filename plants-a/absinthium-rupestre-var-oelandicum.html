Absinthium rupestre var. oelandicum

Absinthium rupestre var. oelandicum, commonly known as Oeland wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves contain high concentrations of volatile oils, contributing to its distinctive bitter taste.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
