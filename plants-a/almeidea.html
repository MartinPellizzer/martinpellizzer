Almeidea

Almeidea, commonly known as the "white thorn" or "white acanthus," is a flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. Almeidea is native to parts of southern Africa and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
