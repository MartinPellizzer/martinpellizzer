Aspidium albicaule

Aspidium albicaule, commonly known as the white-stemmed water fern, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically light green to pale yellow in color. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, such as ponds and slow-moving streams, where it attaches itself to submerged surfaces. Its rhizomes are wiry and help anchor the plant while absorbing nutrients from the surrounding water.

Aspidium albicaule plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and contributing to water filtration.
