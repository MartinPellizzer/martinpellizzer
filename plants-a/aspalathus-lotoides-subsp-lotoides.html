Aspalathus lotoides subsp. lotoides

Aspalathus lotoides subsp.

lotoides, commonly known as rooibos, is a small shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive needle-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or green.

The plant is renowned for its unique antioxidant-rich leaves, which are used to produce the popular herbal tea. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires a warm, Mediterranean climate.

The subspecies lotoides is the primary source of commercial rooibos tea, known for its earthy and slightly sweet flavor.
