Alysicarpus glaber

Alysicarpus glaber, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is an annual legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a cylindrical pod containing several elliptical, smooth seeds.

Alysicarpus glaber is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines.
