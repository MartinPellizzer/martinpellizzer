Abies procera f. prostrata

Abies procera f. prostrata, commonly known as the dwarf subalpine fir, is a low-growing variant of the white fir.

It exhibits a prostrate growth habit, forming dense mats of needle-like foliage close to the ground. The plant has scale-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in spirals along the stems. It thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in subalpine and alpine regions.

This form is adapted to harsh mountain conditions, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
