Acer velutinum f. wolfii

Acer velutinum f. wolfii, commonly known as the velvet maple, is a unique variant of the velvet maple tree.

It is characterized by its finely hairy leaves that give it a soft, velvety texture. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia. Its foliage displays a striking color change in autumn, turning vibrant shades of red and orange.

This ornamental tree is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
