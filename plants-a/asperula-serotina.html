Asperula serotina

Asperula serotina, commonly known as late woodbine, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Asperula serotina is often found in woodlands, hedgerows, and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade and moist soil.
