Allium dolonkarense

Allium dolonkarense, commonly known as the Armenian garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and Turkey.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces a bulb that is often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines for its pungent aroma and flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.

Allium dolonkarense is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
