Allium leucocephalum

Allium leucocephalum, commonly known as the white garlic or meadow garlic, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and clusters of white, globe-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, and it is often found in moist meadows and grasslands.

Allium leucocephalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
