Acalypha bracteata

Acalypha bracteata, commonly known as the red-hot poker or fireplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red bracts that surround small, yellow flowers, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a rough texture and prominent veins.

Acalypha bracteata thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
