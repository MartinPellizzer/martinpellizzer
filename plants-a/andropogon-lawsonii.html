Andropogon lawsonii

Andropogon lawsonii, commonly known as tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the eastern and central United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to six feet. The plant has narrow, blue-green leaves and produces fluffy, pale blue or silver-green seed heads in late summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native landscapes.

Tall bluestem is also an important food source for various wildlife species.
