Adenanthera malayana subsp. andersonii

Adenanthera malayana subsp.

andersonii, commonly known as the Malayan burr bean, is a leguminous shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spiny pods that contain edible seeds.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to green, and it plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its association with Rhizobium bacteria.

The species is valued for its ornamental features and potential agricultural use.
