Acacia chundra

Acacia chundra, commonly known as the Indian acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorny stems and bipinnate leaves that are typically small and delicate. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Acacia chundra is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant in regions with low rainfall.

It is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
