Acacia aemula subsp. aemula

Acacia aemula subsp.

aemula, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and yellow globular flowers that appear in late winter to early spring.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal regions. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes many legumes known for their nitrogen-fixing abilities.
