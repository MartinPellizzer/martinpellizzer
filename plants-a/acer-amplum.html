Acer amplum

Acer amplum, commonly known as the mountain maple, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in cool, moist environments. The tree has a rounded crown and produces small, palmate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its flowers are inconspicuous, and it produces samaras, or winged seeds, for dispersal.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
