Acer stenolobum var. pubescens

Acer stenolobum var. pubescens, commonly known as the Florida maple, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, palmately lobed leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy areas and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
