Aristida dinteri

Aristida dinteri, commonly known as Dinter's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are characteristic of the grass family. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes cultivated as a forage plant for livestock in dry climates.
