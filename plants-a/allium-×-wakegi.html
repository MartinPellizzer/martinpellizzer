Allium × wakegi

Allium × wakegi, commonly known as the Japanese garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces umbels of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its edible leaves and bulbs, which have a mild garlic flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary gardens.

Allium × wakegi is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects and its low maintenance requirements.
