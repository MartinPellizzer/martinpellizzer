Actinodaphne blumei

Actinodaphne blumei, commonly known as the Blume's camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and smooth, greyish bark. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in catkins, followed by elliptical, dark blue-black berries. It is valued for its essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of camphor.

The species is often found in lowland rainforests and is adapted to humid, warm climates.
