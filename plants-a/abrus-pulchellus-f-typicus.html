Abrus pulchellus f. typicus

Abrus pulchellus f. typicus, commonly known as the Indian rattlepod, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, red-orange pods that resemble rattles, which contain flat, kidney-shaped seeds. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye due to their vibrant color.
