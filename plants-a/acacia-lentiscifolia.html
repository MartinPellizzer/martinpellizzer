Acacia lentiscifolia

Acacia lentiscifolia, commonly known as the honey bush, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny foliage and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a resin similar to that of the mastic tree, which has been used historically for various purposes. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic compounds.

The species is well adapted to dry, rocky environments and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
