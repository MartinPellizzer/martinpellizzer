Actinodaphne johorensis

Actinodaphne johorensis, commonly known as the Johore nut or Malayan laurustin, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces drupes that are typically reddish or yellowish when mature, and it is often found in lowland to hill forest areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the understory vegetation in its native habitat.
