Andrographis longipedunculata

Andrographis longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-stemmed bitter grass, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
