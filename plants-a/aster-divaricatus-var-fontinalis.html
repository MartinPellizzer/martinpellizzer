Aster divaricatus var. fontinalis

Aster divaricatus var. fontinalis, commonly known as the Fontinalis aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to lavender, blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for wetland restoration and native plant gardens.
