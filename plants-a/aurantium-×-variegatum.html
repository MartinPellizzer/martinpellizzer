Aurantium × variegatum

Aurantium × variegatum, commonly known as the variegated orange.

This hybrid citrus plant is a cross between Citrus aurantium and Citrus sinensis, resulting in a unique combination of traits. It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves, which display a mix of green, white, and yellow patterns. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers and round, orange-colored fruits that are typically less acidic than standard oranges.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and is also used in some culinary applications.
