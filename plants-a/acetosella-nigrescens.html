Acetosella nigrescens

Acetosella nigrescens, commonly known as the black sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or shady areas.
