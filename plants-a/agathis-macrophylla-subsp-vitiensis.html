Agathis macrophylla subsp. vitiensis

Agathis macrophylla subsp.

vitiensis, commonly known as the Vitiensis Agathis, is a tropical tree native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Vanuatu and Fiji. It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves that are often glaucous in appearance.

The plant produces distinctive, fragrant flowers and woody fruits containing oily seeds. It is valued for its durable timber and has ecological significance in its native habitats.

However, it is considered endangered due to habitat loss and overexploitation.
