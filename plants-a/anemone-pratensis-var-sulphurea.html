Anemone pratensis var. sulphurea

Anemone pratensis var. sulphurea, commonly known as the sulphur anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and often covered in a fine, hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical name reflects its association with the genus Anemone and its distinctive sulfur-yellow coloration.
