Aconitum koelleanum

Aconitum koelleanum, commonly known as the Koelreuter's aconite, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, somewhat hairy texture.

Aconitum koelleanum is known for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that make it dangerous to both humans and animals.
