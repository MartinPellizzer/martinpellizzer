Alchemilla flabellata

Alchemilla flabellata, commonly known as the fanleaf lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Alchemilla flabellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
