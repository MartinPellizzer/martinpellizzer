Afrodaphne zenkeri

Afrodaphne zenkeri, commonly known as the Zambian sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant wood that is highly valued for its unique scent and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy surface. It produces small, white flowers followed by dark blue berries, which are an important food source for local wildlife.

Due to its ornamental value and timber quality, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
