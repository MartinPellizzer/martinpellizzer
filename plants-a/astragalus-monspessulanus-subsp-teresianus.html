Astragalus monspessulanus subsp. teresianus

Astragalus monspessulanus subsp.

teresianus, commonly known as the Spanish milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, often found in scrublands and limestone soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is of interest in ecological and botanical studies due to its unique adaptations and limited distribution.
