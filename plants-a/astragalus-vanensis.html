Astragalus vanensis

Astragalus vanensis, commonly known as the Vanensis milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Astragalus vanensis is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
