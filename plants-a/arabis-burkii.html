Arabis burkii

Arabis burkii, commonly known as the Burk's rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, and its leaves are usually basal and lobed.
