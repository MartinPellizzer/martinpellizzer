Acacia reticulata

Acacia reticulata, commonly known as the Indian acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often bipinnate and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow, globular inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its bark is rough and fissured, often exuding a milky sap when damaged.

Acacia reticulata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in tropical and subtropical regions.
