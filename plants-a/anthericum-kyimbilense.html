Anthericum kyimbilense

Anthericum kyimbilense, commonly known as the Kyimbilense arum, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by insects, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible for some animals.

Anthericum kyimbilense is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems.
