Antholyza quadrangularis

Antholyza quadrangularis, commonly known as the four-angled antholyza, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its square, ridged stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
