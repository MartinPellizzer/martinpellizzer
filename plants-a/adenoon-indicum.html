Adenoon indicum

Adenoon indicum, commonly known as the Indian spider orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spider-like appearance with long, thin sepals and petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a distinctive, intricate pattern.

Adenoon indicum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
