Astragalus famelicus

Astragalus famelicus, commonly known as the poor man's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant is adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils. It is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

Astragalus famelicus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
