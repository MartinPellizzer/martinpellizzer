Aristolochia punctata

Aristolochia punctata, commonly known as the spotted Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and its unique, winding, pipe-like flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often adorned with purple or brown markings. It is typically found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and stream banks.

The genus Aristolochia is known for containing aristolochic acids, which are toxic to humans and animals.
