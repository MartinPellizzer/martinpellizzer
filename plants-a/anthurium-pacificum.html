Anthurium pacificum

Anthurium pacificum, commonly known as the Pacific anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant spadix flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage is often dark green with a waxy texture, and it produces showy, colorful inflorescences that attract pollinators.

Anthurium pacificum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid environments.
