Acacia acuaria

Acacia acuaria, commonly known as the sharp-leaved acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorned stems and bipinnate leaves that are narrow and linear. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, globular inflorescences, which are followed by elongated, flat pods containing seeds. Acacia acuaria is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and efficient water use.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and animals.
