Asparagus umbellatus var. flavescens

Asparagus umbellatus var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow asparagus, is a perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems that produce small, yellowish-green flowers in umbel-like clusters. The plant has feathery, grass-like foliage and is known for its aromatic, slightly sweet scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
