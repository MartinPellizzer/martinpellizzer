Aquilegia pyrenaica var. macrantha

Aquilegia pyrenaica var. macrantha, commonly known as the Pyrenean columbine, is a perennial herb native to the Pyrenees mountain range.

It features striking, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple, with five petals and two elongated nectar spurs. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are lobed and hairy, adapted to its alpine environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance as a pollinator plant.
