Acer atropurpureum

Acer atropurpureum, commonly known as the red maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage in the fall, which makes it a popular ornamental plant. The tree has palmate leaves with five lobes, and its bark is smooth and grayish-brown when young. It produces small, red to yellow flowers in the spring, followed by winged seeds.

This species thrives in a variety of soil types and is relatively hardy, making it adaptable to different climates.
