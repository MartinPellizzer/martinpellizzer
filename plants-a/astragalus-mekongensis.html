Astragalus mekongensis

Astragalus mekongensis, commonly known as the Mekong milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its deep root system, which helps it thrive in rocky and arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance makes it a subject of interest for conservation efforts in its native habitat.
