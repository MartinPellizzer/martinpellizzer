Allophylus ussheri

Allophylus ussheri, commonly known as the African snowberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and pinnate. The plant produces small, white to pale green flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its berries are usually red or yellow when mature and serve as a food source for various birds and mammals.

Allophylus ussheri is often found in coastal forests and sandy soils, thriving in humid, tropical climates.
