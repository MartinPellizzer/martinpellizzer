Anguina truncata

Anguina truncata, commonly known as the truncated angleworm, is a species of plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Anguina truncata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
