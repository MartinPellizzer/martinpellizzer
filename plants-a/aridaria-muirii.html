Aridaria muirii

Aridaria muirii, commonly known as the Muir's aridaria, is a rare succulent plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. Its leaves are covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss through evaporation.

Aridaria muirii is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
