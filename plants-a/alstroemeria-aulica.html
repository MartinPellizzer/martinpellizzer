Alstroemeria aulica

Alstroemeria aulica, commonly known as the golden alstroemeria, is a species native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the family Alstroemeriaceae, characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a range of colors, including yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with strap-like leaves arranged in a fan shape.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, although it is less commonly cultivated than other alstroemeria species.
