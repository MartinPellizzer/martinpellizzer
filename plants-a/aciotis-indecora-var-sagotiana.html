Aciotis indecora var. sagotiana

Aciotis indecora var. sagotiana, commonly known as the Sagot's aciotis, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white ray florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and is often found in grasslands and disturbed habitats. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it produces small, inconspicuous fruit capsules.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
