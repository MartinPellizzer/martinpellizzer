Achillea millefolium subsp. setacea

Achillea millefolium subsp.

setacea, commonly known as the Alpine yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a soft, silvery bloom.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, or pale yellow, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine and rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
