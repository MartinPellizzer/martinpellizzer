Astracantha macrolacis

Astracantha macrolacis, commonly known as the broad-leaved sandbur, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces spiny, spherical fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
