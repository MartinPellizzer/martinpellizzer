Alstonia moui

Alstonia moui, commonly known as the Malayan alstonia, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers followed by orange-red berries. Alstonia moui is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some regions.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
