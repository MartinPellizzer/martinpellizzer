Adiantum × variopinnatum

Adiantum × variopinnatum, commonly known as the variegated maidenhair fern, is a hybrid fern species that combines traits from various Adiantum species.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and gold. The plant is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It has a rhizomatous growth habit and produces spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
