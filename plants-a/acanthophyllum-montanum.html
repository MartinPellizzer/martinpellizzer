Acanthophyllum montanum

Acanthophyllum montanum, commonly known as mountain acanthophyllum, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. Its leaves are usually small, fleshy, and adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments.

Acanthophyllum montanum is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to survive in extreme climatic conditions.
