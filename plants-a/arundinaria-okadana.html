Arundinaria okadana

Arundinaria okadana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Okadama bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green and smooth on the upper surface. Arundinaria okadana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Japanese gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
