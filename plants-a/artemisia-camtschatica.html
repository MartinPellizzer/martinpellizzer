Artemisia camtschatica

Artemisia camtschatica, commonly known as alpine tarragon, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is known for its aromatic, slightly minty scent.

Artemisia camtschatica is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
