Acaena sericea subsp. subglabricupula

Acaena sericea subsp.

subglabricupula, commonly known as the silky burrplant, is a low-growing shrub native to South America. It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and produces small, spherical fruits that are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is typically found in grasslands and disturbed soils. Its unique morphology makes it an important component of local ecosystems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its reduced pubescence compared to other subspecies of Acaena sericea.
