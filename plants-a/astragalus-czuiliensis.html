Astragalus czuiliensis

Astragalus czuiliensis, commonly known as the Czuliensis milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its ecological significance lies in its ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
