Acalypha pubiflora

Acalypha pubiflora, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silky iron plant, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are not particularly showy but are followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in warm climates.
