Allium tourneuxii

Allium tourneuxii, commonly known as the Turk's cap leek, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, clustered white or pale purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its strong garlic-like odor and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.

Allium tourneuxii is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun.
