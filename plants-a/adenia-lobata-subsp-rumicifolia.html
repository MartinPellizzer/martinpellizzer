Adenia lobata subsp. rumicifolia

Adenia lobata subsp.

rumicifolia, commonly known as the rough-leaved adenia, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, fleshy, red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
