Aspidium dilatatum var. major

Aspidium dilatatum var. major, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds. The plant features bipinnate leaves with a distinctive, glossy appearance and a finely divided structure. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its reproductive structures include spore-producing sorus clusters on the undersides of the fronds.
