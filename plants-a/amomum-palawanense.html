Amomum palawanense

Amomum palawanense, commonly known as Palawan cardamom, is a tropical plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, elongated fruits that resemble small pods. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within lowland forests. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a strong, spicy fragrance.

The fruit contains aromatic seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in local cuisines.
