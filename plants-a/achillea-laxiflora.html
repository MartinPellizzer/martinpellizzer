Achillea laxiflora

Achillea laxiflora, commonly known as the looseflower yarrow, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features finely divided, silvery-green leaves that are aromatic and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Achillea laxiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
