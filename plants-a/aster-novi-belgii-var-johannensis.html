Aster novi-belgii var. johannensis

Aster novi-belgii var. johannensis, commonly known as the Johann's New Belgium Aster, is a cultivar of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of purple and blue. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, adding vibrant color to gardens. It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for perennial gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
