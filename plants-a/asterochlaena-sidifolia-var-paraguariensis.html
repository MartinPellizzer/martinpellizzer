Asterochlaena sidifolia var. paraguariensis

Asterochlaena sidifolia var. paraguariensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and delicate, feathery inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slight sheen, and it produces small, pale green to yellow flowers in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
