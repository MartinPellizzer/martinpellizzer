Absinthium frigidum var. fischerianum

Absinthium frigidum var. fischerianum, commonly known as the Siberian wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Siberia and northern Russia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments. It is often found growing in open tundra and rocky areas, where it withstands low temperatures and strong winds.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and tonic effects.
