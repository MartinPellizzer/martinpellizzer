Astragalus arnoldianus

Astragalus arnoldianus, commonly known as Arnold's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus arnoldianus is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert scrub ecosystems.
