Avena flavescens f. major

Avena flavescens f. major, commonly known as the tall oat, is a botanical variant of the common oat species.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and elongated, narrow leaves. The plant produces spikelets that bear small, yellowish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

This variant is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in agricultural or ornamental settings.
