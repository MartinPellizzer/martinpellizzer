Acrostichum calomelas

Acrostichum calomelas, commonly known as the black mangrove, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to the coastal regions of the Americas. The plant has a distinctive appearance with dark, leathery leaves that help reduce water loss in salty conditions. It plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.

Its unique morphology and ecological importance make it a key component of mangrove ecosystems.
