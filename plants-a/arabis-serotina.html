Arabis serotina

Arabis serotina, commonly known as late honesty, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. Its leaves are basal, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Arabis serotina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
