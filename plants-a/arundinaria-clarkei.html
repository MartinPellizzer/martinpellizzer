Arundinaria clarkei

Arundinaria clarkei, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or Clarke's bamboo, is a species of grass native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Arundinaria clarkei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
