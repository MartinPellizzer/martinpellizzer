Achyranthes splendens

Achyranthes splendens, commonly known as the red bengal spinach, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange-red stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines, while the plant also has medicinal properties in some cultures.

Achyranthes splendens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
