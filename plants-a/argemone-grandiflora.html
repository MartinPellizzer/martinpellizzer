Argemone grandiflora

Argemone grandiflora, commonly known as the Mexican poppy, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers with prominent yellow centers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain oily seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
