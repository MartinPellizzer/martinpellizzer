Agrostis vulgaris var. divaricata

Agrostis vulgaris var. divaricata, commonly known as the bentgrass or silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along streambanks. The plant has slender, tufted shoots with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often silver-haired, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are usually pink to reddish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.
