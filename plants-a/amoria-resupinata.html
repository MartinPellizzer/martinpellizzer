Amoria resupinata

Amoria resupinata, commonly known as the hanging lily, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that hang from long, slender stems. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow blooms that are often mistaken for orchids due to their intricate structure. Amoria resupinata thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture to flourish.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical and subtropical climates.
