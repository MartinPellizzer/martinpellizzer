Acalypha hartwegiana

Acalypha hartwegiana, commonly known as the Hartweg's spurflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are often more showy than the small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
