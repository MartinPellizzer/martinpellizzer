Alsophila emilei

Alsophila emilei, commonly known as the Emile's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are arranged in clusters called sori. Alsophila emilei thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its slow growth rate and unique morphology make it a valuable addition to botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
