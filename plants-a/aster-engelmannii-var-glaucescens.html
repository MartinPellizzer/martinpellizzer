Aster engelmannii var. glaucescens

Aster engelmannii var. glaucescens, commonly known as Engelmann's aster, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are blue to purple, with ray florets that bloom in the fall.

This variety is notable for its distinctive appearance and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
