Amorpha fruticosa var. glabra

Amorpha fruticosa var. glabra, commonly known as silky leadplant, is a deciduous shrub native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

Silky leadplant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
