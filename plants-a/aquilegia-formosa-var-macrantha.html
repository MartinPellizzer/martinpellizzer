Aquilegia formosa var. macrantha

Aquilegia formosa var. macrantha, commonly known as the showy lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spurred petals and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink and red, bloom in early spring.

The varietal name *macrantha* refers to its relatively large, showy flowers compared to other varieties of *Aquilegia formosa*.
