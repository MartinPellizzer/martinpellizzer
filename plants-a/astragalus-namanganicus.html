Astragalus namanganicus

Astragalus namanganicus, commonly known as Namangan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which helps improve soil fertility.

Due to its ecological importance and limited distribution, it is considered a species of conservation concern in some areas.
