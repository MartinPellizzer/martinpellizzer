Acaena × pallens

Acaena × pallens, commonly known as the New Zealand bramble, is a hybrid shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces bright red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

Acaena × pallens is often found in coastal and subalpine regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
