Arachnorchis brumalis

Arachnorchis brumalis, commonly known as the Christmas Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom during the late autumn to early winter months. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Arachnorchis brumalis is a short-lived perennial, with a life cycle that is closely tied to seasonal rainfall patterns.
