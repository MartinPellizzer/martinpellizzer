Astragalus inflexus var. leucolobus

Astragalus inflexus var. leucolobus, commonly known as the white globeflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive globular flower heads, which are composed of numerous small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
