Anisotome latifolia

Anisotome latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf anisotome, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features basal rosettes of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
