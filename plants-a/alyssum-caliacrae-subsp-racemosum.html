Alyssum caliacrae subsp. racemosum

Alyssum caliacrae subsp.

racemosum, commonly known as the desert sand alyssum, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa. It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring.

The plant has compact, succulent-like leaves that help it conserve water in dry environments. It produces elongated racemes of flowers, which are characteristic of the species. Alyssum caliacrae subsp.

racemosum is well adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in full sun.
