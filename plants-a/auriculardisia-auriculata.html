Auriculardisia auriculata

Auriculardisia auriculata, commonly known as the ear-shaped leaf orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive auricle-like leaves that resemble small ears, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking, orchid-like flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Auriculardisia auriculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
