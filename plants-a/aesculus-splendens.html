Aesculus splendens

Aesculus splendens, commonly known as the Chinese horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves and showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The tree produces spiny capsules containing glossy, chestnut-like seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with longitudinal ridges.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can grow up to 20 meters tall.
