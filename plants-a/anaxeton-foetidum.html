Anaxeton foetidum

Anaxeton foetidum, commonly known as the stink bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a rough or hairy texture.

The plant is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is most noticeable when the flowers are in bloom.
