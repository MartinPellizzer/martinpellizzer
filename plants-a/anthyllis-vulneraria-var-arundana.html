Anthyllis vulneraria var. arundana

Anthyllis vulneraria var. arundana, commonly known as the creeping yellow sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in dry, sandy, or rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

This variety is adapted to harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects.
