Acaena pinnatifida var. aconcaguensis

Acaena pinnatifida var. aconcaguensis, commonly known as the Aconcagua burr plant, is a species native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces spherical, woody fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals and wind. Its thick, hairy leaves are adapted to withstand harsh, dry conditions, making it a resilient plant in arid environments.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
