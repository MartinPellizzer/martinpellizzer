Anguillaria dioica

Anguillaria dioica, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has a unique reproductive system, with separate male and female flowers on different plants. Its leaves are broad and floating, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Anguillaria dioica is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has cultural significance in many regions.
