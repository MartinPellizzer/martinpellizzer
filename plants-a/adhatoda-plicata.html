Adhatoda plicata

Adhatoda plicata, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive plicate (folded) margin. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Adhatoda plicata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.
