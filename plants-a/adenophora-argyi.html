Adenophora argyi

Adenophora argyi, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and covered in fine glands. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often with a violet or purple hue. These flowers bloom in late summer and are clustered in racemes at the top of the stems.

Adenophora argyi is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant.
