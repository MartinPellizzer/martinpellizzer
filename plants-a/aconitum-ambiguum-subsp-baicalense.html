Aconitum ambiguum subsp. baicalense

Aconitum ambiguum subsp.

baicalense, commonly known as the Baikal aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height.

The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes along the stem. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance.

The species is noted for its toxic alkaloids, which make it a potentially dangerous plant to handle or ingest.
