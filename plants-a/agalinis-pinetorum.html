Agalinis pinetorum

Agalinis pinetorum, commonly known as the pine barren gentian, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sandy soils in pine flatwood and scrub habitats. It has opposite, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Agalinis pinetorum is often found in disturbed or fire-maintained ecosystems.
