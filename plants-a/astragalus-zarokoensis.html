Astragalus zarokoensis

Astragalus zarokoensis, commonly known as Zaroko's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

It is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
