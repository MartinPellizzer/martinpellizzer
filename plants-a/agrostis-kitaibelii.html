Agrostis kitaibelii

Agrostis kitaibelii, commonly known as slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It is well adapted to temperate climates and can tolerate a range of soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it a common indicator plant in ecological studies and a valuable component of diverse grassland ecosystems.
