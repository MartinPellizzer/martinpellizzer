Alternanthera puberula

Alternanthera puberula, commonly known as the velvet leaf or red velvet plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves that range in color from deep red to green, depending on the variety. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, which are followed by small, round seed capsules. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value lies primarily in its striking foliage, making it a popular choice for water gardens and containers.
