Amygdalus triloba f. plena

Amygdalus triloba f. plena, commonly known as the double-flowered peach tree, is a cultivar of the peach species characterized by its ornamental value.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that produces dense clusters of double flowers, which are more showy than those of the typical peach. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of Asia, particularly China. Its flowers are usually pink to white, and it bears edible fruits similar to common peaches.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
