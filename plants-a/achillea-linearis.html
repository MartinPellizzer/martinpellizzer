Achillea linearis

Achillea linearis, commonly known as narrowleaf yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, linear leaves that are gray-green in color and arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in flat-topped clusters called cymes, which bloom from early summer to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its aromatic foliage and extended blooming period make it a valuable plant for pollinators and ornamental use.
