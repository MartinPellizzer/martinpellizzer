Aceras lanceum

Aceras lanceum, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
