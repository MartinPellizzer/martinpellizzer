Acaena poterioides

Acaena poterioides, commonly known as the New Zealand bramble, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its prickly stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, dark reddish-brown berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
