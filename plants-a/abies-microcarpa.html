Abies microcarpa

Abies microcarpa, commonly known as the Mexican fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are shorter than those of many other fir species. The tree produces small, cylindrical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, and it is typically found in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates. Abies microcarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Pinaceae family.
