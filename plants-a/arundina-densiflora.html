Arundina densiflora

Arundina densiflora, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or evergreen bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and tall, slender stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that appear in summer, followed by clusters of dark blue berries. Its leaves are long, narrow, and slightly curved, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Arundina densiflora is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
