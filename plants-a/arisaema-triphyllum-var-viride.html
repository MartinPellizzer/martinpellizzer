Arisaema triphyllum var. viride

Arisaema triphyllum var. viride, commonly known as the green cobra plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Araceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and the Carolinas. The plant is characterized by its distinctive leaf structure, with three large, heart-shaped leaflets and a tall, spadix-like inflorescence. Its foliage is typically a vibrant green, and it produces a unique, poisonous fruit that contains toxic seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
