Andinia pendens

Andinia pendens, commonly known as the hanging bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture.
