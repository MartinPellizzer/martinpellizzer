Aster cornuti

Aster cornuti, commonly known as the hooked aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy purple to blue flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it often grows in moist, open habitats.

Aster cornuti is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
