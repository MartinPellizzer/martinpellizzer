Agropyron spicatum var. vaseyi

Agropyron spicatum var. vaseyi, commonly known as the vasey's wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in restoration ecology projects.
