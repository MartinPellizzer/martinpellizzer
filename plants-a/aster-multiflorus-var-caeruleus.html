Aster multiflorus var. caeruleus

Aster multiflorus var. caeruleus, commonly known as the blue aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and naturalized areas.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
