Arundinaria pubiflora

Arundinaria pubiflora, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, particularly India and Southeast Asia. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, feathery foliage. The leaves are typically narrow and have a distinctive pubescent (hairy) texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
