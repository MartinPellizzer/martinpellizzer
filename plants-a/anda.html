Anda

Anda, commonly known as the Indian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet or blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Anda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
