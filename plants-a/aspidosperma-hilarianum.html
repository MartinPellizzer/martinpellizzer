Aspidosperma hilarianum

Aspidosperma hilarianum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Cerbera odollam*, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a street tree due to its adaptability and aesthetic qualities.
