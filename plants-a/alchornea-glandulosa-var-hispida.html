Alchornea glandulosa var. hispida

Alchornea glandulosa var. hispida, commonly known as rough-seeded alchornea, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed habitats and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
