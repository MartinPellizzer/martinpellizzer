Aleuritia cortusoides

Aleuritia cortusoides, commonly known as the lesser elder, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Aleuritia cortusoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
