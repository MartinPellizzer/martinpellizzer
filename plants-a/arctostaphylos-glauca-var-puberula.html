Arctostaphylos glauca var. puberula

Arctostaphylos glauca var. puberula, commonly known as the white mountain manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-green foliage and reddish bark that peels in thin layers. The plant produces small, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. Its leaves are typically leathery and have a pubescent underside, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

This variety is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
