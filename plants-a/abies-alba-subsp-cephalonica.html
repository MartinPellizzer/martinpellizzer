Abies alba subsp. cephalonica

Abies alba subsp.

cephalonica, commonly known as the Greek fir, is a rare and endemic subspecies native to the mountainous regions of Greece. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves.

The cones are typically small and cylindrical, maturing to a reddish-brown color. This subspecies thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
