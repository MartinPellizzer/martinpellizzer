Amaranthus oleraceus var. maximus

Amaranthus oleraceus var. maximus, commonly known as red leaf amaranth, is a flowering plant in the amaranth family, Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, purple, and green leaves, which are often used in culinary and ornamental applications. The plant is native to the Americas but has been widely cultivated in Asia and Africa for its nutritional value and aesthetic appeal. It thrives in warm climates and is relatively drought-tolerant, making it a hardy choice for gardens and agriculture.

The leaves are rich in protein, iron, and antioxidants, contributing to its popularity as a healthful vegetable.
