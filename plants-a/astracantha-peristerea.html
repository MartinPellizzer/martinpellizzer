Astracantha peristerea

Astracantha peristerea, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. Its milky sap is toxic and contains cardiac glycosides, which can be harmful to humans and animals.

Astracantha peristerea is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun.
