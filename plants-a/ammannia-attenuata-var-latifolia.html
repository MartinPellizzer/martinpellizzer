Ammannia attenuata var. latifolia

Ammannia attenuata var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf ammannia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States.

It features upright, slender stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape with smooth margins. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences of pink to red flowers, which bloom from late spring to early fall. Its leaves and stems are typically covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, sunny conditions.

It is often found in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.
