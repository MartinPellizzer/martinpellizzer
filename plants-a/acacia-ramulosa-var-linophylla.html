Acacia ramulosa var. linophylla

Acacia ramulosa var. linophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging branches and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
