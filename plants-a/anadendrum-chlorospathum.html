Anadendrum chlorospathum

Anadendrum chlorospathum, commonly known as the green-veined anadendrum, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas. The plant features compound leaves with bright green veins and produces small, showy flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Anadendrum chlorospathum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
