Amaranthus edulis

Amaranthus edulis, commonly known as fall armyworm or pigweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into clusters of triangular seeds. The plant is often cultivated for its edible leaves, which are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in warm climates and is commonly found in disturbed soils.

Amaranthus edulis is also known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
