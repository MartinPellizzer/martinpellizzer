Achyronia laricifolia

Achyronia laricifolia, commonly known as the white cedar, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cupressaceae family and is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
