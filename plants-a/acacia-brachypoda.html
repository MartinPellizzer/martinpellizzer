Acacia brachypoda

Acacia brachypoda, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, fern-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils.

Acacia brachypoda is well adapted to drought conditions and is a hardy plant in native landscapes.
