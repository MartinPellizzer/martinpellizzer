Allium crameri

Allium crameri, commonly known as the Cramer's onion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers in clusters during the spring. Allium crameri is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
