Artocarpus albobrunneus

Artocarpus albobrunneus, commonly known as the white-barked breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces a unique fruit with a fibrous husk and a starchy, edible pulp inside. It is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is used in traditional cuisines.

The plant also has ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and distinctive fruit structure.
