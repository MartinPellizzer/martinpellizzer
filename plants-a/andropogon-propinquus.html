Andropogon propinquus

Andropogon propinquus, commonly known as the eastern gamagrass, is a tall perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often clustered at the top of the stem. It is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Andropogon propinquus plays an important role in ecological systems, providing habitat and forage for wildlife.
