Astragalus kuldshensis

Astragalus kuldshensis, commonly known as Kuldshen's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus kuldshensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential medicinal properties.
