Anchusa italica var. azurea

Anchusa italica var. azurea, commonly known as blue anchusa, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long bloom period and ability to attract pollinators.
