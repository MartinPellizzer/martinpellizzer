Artemisia caerulescens subsp. densiflora

Artemisia caerulescens subsp.

densiflora, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and dense clusters of small, pale blue to white flowers.

The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its aromatic, slightly minty scent. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
