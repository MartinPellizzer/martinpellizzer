Agathryon arcticum

Agathryon arcticum, commonly known as the Arctic agathryon, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is adapted to cold, nutrient-poor environments. The plant typically has small, dark green leaves and produces white or pale pink flowers in the summer. It plays an important role in Arctic ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in tundra and alpine habitats.
