Astragalus fissilis

Astragalus fissilis, commonly known as the fissured milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus fissilis is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
