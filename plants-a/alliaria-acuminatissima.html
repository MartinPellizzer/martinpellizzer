Alliaria acuminatissima

Alliaria acuminatissima, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garlic mustard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical fruits known as siliques, which contain numerous tiny seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential in some regions, it is sometimes considered a noxious weed.
