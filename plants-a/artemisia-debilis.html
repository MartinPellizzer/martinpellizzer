Artemisia debilis

Artemisia debilis, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and aromatic, often pungent, scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that appear in late summer, and it is often found in dry, rocky soils. Artemisia debilis is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

It has been used historically in traditional medicine and as a natural insect repellent.
