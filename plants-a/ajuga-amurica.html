Ajuga amurica

Ajuga amurica, commonly known as Siberian bugloss, is a perennial herb native to the forests of eastern Asia.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Boraginaceae, characterized by its hairy stems and showy flowers.
