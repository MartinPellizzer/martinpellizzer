Acacia oraria var. amblyphylla

Acacia oraria var. amblyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping stems and bipinnate leaves that are narrow and finely divided. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in loose spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
