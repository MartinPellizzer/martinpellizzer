Apeiba trombetensis

Apeiba trombetensis, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The tree produces long, curved pods that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.

Additionally, the plant has traditional medicinal uses among indigenous communities.
