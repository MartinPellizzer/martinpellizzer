Aegilops columnaris var. glabriuscula

Aegilops columnaris var. glabriuscula, commonly known as the glabrous wild wheat, is a wild grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed areas. Its seeds are small and have a distinctive, pale color.

This variety is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and adaptation to harsh environments.
