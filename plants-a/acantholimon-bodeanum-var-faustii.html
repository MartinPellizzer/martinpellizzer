Acantholimon bodeanum var. faustii

Acantholimon bodeanum var. faustii, commonly known as the Desert Sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, showy blue or purple flowers during the spring and early summer. The plant has silver-gray foliage covered in fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its root system is adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

It plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
