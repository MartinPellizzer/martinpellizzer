Aulomyrcia citrifolia

Aulomyrcia citrifolia, commonly known as the citron tree, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces a yellow-orange fruit with a thick, aromatic rind and a juicy, acidic pulp. It is often cultivated for its fruit, which is used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
