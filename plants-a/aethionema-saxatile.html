Aethionema saxatile

Aethionema saxatile, commonly known as the rock mustards, is a low-growing, perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers in the spring. The plant typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually fleshy and succulent, aiding in water retention.

Aethionema saxatile is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in rock gardens.
