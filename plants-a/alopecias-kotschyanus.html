Alopecias kotschyanus

Alopecias kotschyanus, commonly known as the Kotschyan's hairless plant, is a unique species native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its reduced foliage and specialized adaptations to survive in harsh, arid environments. The plant exhibits a compact growth form with thick, fleshy stems that store water, aiding in its survival during dry periods. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Alopecias kotschyanus is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
