Albuca grandis

Albuca grandis, commonly known as the giant albuca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow blooms that appear in late summer or early autumn. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette at the base.

Albuca grandis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
