Acyntha roxburghiana

Acyntha roxburghiana, commonly known as the Himalayan bluebell, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.
