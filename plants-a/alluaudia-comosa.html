Alluaudia comosa

Alluaudia comosa, commonly known as the woolly alluaudia, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
