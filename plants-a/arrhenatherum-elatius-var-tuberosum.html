Arrhenatherum elatius var. tuberosum

Arrhenatherum elatius var. tuberosum, commonly known as the tuberous false panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is notable for its tuberous roots, which are sometimes used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Its flowering spikes appear in late summer, adding ornamental value to the landscape.
