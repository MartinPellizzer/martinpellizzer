Antirrhinum meonanthum subsp. ambiguum

Antirrhinum meonanthum subsp.

ambiguum, commonly known as the slender toadflax, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas and rocky soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive two-lipped structure. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
