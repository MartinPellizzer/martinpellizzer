Astragalus gigantifoliolatus

Astragalus gigantifoliolatus, commonly known as the giant-leaf milkvetch, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, making it one of the most striking species in the genus. The plant produces yellowish to reddish flowers in spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of Mongolia and China.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is often of interest to botanists and conservationists.
