Acer macrophyllum subsp. kimballiae

Acer macrophyllum subsp.

kimballiae, commonly known as the Kimball's bigleaf maple, is a deciduous tree native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States. It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged fruits known as samaras. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and forested areas.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
