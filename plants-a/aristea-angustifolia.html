Aristea angustifolia

Aristea angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf aristea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
