Astragalus orotrephes

Astragalus orotrephes, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from yellow to pink. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.

Astragalus orotrephes plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
