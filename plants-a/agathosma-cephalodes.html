Agathosma cephalodes

Agathosma cephalodes, commonly known as African geranium, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features aromatic, narrow, needle-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is also cultivated for essential oils and herbal remedies.
