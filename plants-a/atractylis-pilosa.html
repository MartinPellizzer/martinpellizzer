Atractylis pilosa

Atractylis pilosa, commonly known as the hairy buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a rosette of hairy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in a single, terminal head, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands.

Atractylis pilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
