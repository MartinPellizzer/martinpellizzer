Amsinckia humilis

Amsinckia humilis, commonly known as the dwarf ironweed, is a low-growing annual plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has hairy leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Amsinckia humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
