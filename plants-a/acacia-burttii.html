Acacia burttii

Acacia burttii, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a shrub or small tree native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, feather-like segments. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact globular inflorescences, followed by woody pods containing flat, fibrous seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

The bark and sap of Acacia burttii have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
