Albizia malacophylla

Albizia malacophylla, commonly known as the silky albizia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and covered in a fine, silky white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming in the spring. Its fruit is a legume pod that splits open to release seeds.

Albizia malacophylla is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
