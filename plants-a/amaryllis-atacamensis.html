Amaryllis atacamensis

Amaryllis atacamensis, commonly known as the Atacama lily, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of northern Chile.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink blooms that are often fragrant. It is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and can go through periods of dormancy.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
