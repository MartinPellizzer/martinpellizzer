Adenopetalum oerstedii

Adenopetalum oerstedii, commonly known as the prairie smoke, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery plumes that resemble smoke. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and prairies, thriving in well-drained soils. Its showy inflorescences consist of small, pink to purple flowers surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.

Adenopetalum oerstedii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
