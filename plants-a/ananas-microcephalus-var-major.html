Ananas microcephalus var. major

Ananas microcephalus var. major, commonly known as the pineapple guava, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It produces a small, aromatic fruit with a textured skin and a sweet, juicy interior. The plant has a rosette-like growth habit with thick, fleshy leaves that are often used for ornamental purposes. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is typically harvested when it turns a deep red or orange color, indicating maturity.
