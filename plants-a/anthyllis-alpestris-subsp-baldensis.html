Anthyllis alpestris subsp. baldensis

Anthyllis alpestris subsp.

baldensis, commonly known as the Balkan anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, rocky, and dry habitats.

The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh alpine conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
