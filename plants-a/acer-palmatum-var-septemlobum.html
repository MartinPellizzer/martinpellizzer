Acer palmatum var. septemlobum

Acer palmatum var. septemlobum, commonly known as the seven-lobed maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its finely divided, palmate leaves that typically have seven lobes, giving it its common name. The leaves are typically bright green in summer and turn vibrant red, orange, or yellow in autumn. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
