Annona conifera

Annona conifera, commonly known as the coniferous annona, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces a fragrant, yellowish fruit that is edible and has a mildly sweet flavor. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
