Ancipitia crocodiliceps

Ancipitia crocodiliceps, commonly known as the crocodile-headed orchid, is a striking plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated floral structure that resembles a crocodile's head, with intricate patterns resembling scales. The plant features broad, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its humid, shaded habitat. Its flowers bloom in clusters, producing a mix of vibrant yellow and orange hues.

Ancipitia crocodiliceps is primarily pollinated by specific species of bees and wasps, which are attracted to its nectar-rich sepals.
