Artemisia × seileri

Artemisia × seileri, commonly known as the Seiler's wormwood, is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage and grayish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is closely related to other Artemisia species.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
