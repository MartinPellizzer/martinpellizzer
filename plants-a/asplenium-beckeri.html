Asplenium beckeri

Asplenium beckeri, commonly known as the Becker's spleenwort, is a small, terrestrial fern belonging to the Aspleniaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found growing on rocks or soil in temperate regions. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of its fronds, which are typically arranged in a rosette pattern.

Asplenium beckeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
