Aracium gmelinii, commonly known as the Mexican firecracker, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America, characterized by its striking red and yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, hairy seeds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.
