Aeschynomene montevidensis

Aeschynomene montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevideo aeschynomene, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in dry, open habitats.
