Asclepias palmeri

Asclepias palmeri, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers and its distinctive, leafy stems that often have a fuzzy texture. The plant belongs to the milkweed family, Apocynaceae, and is known for its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds. Asclepias palmeri plays a crucial role in supporting monarch butterfly populations by providing a source of nectar and a site for egg-laying.

Its ecological importance makes it a valuable component of desert and grassland ecosystems.
