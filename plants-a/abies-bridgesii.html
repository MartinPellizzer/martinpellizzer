Abies bridgesii

Abies bridgesii, commonly known as the Bridgess fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the high-altitude regions of western South America.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged spirally along the branches. The cones of Abies bridgesii are typically erect and contain small, winged seeds. This species is adapted to cold, moist environments and thrives in mountainous areas with well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the Andean forest ecosystems.
