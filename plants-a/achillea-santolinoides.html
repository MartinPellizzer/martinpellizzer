Achillea santolinoides

Achillea santolinoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been historically utilized for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
