Aristida pampeana

Aristida pampeana, commonly known as the Pampas grass, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Argentina, Uruguay, and southern Brazil.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces fluffy, white seed heads that are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.

Aristida pampeana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid regions.
