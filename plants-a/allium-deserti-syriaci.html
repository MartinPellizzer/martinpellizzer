Allium deserti-syriaci

Allium deserti-syriaci, commonly known as the Syrian desert onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale purple flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with a deep root system that helps it retain moisture. Its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb in local cuisines.

Allium deserti-syriaci plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its native habitats.
