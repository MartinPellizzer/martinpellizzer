Amomum mizoramense

Amomum mizoramense, commonly known as the Mizoram cardamom, is a herbaceous plant native to the northeastern region of India.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clustered, spadix-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by dark, aromatic berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its unique fragrance and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and herbalists alike.
