Aerangis falcifolia

Aerangis falcifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and elongated, narrow leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with a distinctive, spurred shape. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
