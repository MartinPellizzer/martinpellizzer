Acalypha rupestris

Acalypha rupestris, commonly known as the rock hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, often reddish stems. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically ovate to lance-shaped with a distinctive waxy coating.

Acalypha rupestris thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
