Andira frondosa

Andira frondosa, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are typically greenish-white in color.

The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are blue-black when mature, containing several seeds.
