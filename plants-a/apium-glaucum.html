Apium glaucum

Apium glaucum, commonly known as wild celery or sea holly, is a flowering plant in the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and fern-like, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the carrot family. Apium glaucum is native to coastal regions of Europe and is often found growing in sandy or gravelly soils.

It is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
