Adenophora liliifolia f. infundibuliformis

Adenophora liliifolia f. infundibuliformis, commonly known as the bell-shaped adenophora, is a variant of the adenophora liliifolia species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale blue flowers. It is a perennial herb native to regions of East Asia, including China and Korea.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its elegant floral display.
