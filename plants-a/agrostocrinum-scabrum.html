Agrostocrinum scabrum

Agrostocrinum scabrum, commonly known as rough agrimony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, grassy areas and has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their astringent properties.

The plant is characterized by its rough, textured foliage and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
