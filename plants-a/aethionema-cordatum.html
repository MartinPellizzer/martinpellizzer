Aethionema cordatum

Aethionema cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountains of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped, with a rough texture and a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are small, yellow, and borne in clusters, appearing in the summer months.

Aethionema cordatum thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.
