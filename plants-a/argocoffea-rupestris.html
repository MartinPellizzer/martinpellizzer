Argocoffea rupestris

Argocoffea rupestris, commonly known as the rock coffee plant, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, glossy leaves. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
