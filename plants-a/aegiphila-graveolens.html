Aegiphila graveolens

Aegiphila graveolens, commonly known as the sweet shrub, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in wet or moist habitats. The plant features small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are aromatic and have a strong, pleasant scent, which contributes to its common name.

Aegiphila graveolens is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
