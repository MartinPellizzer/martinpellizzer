Aconitum wardii var. hopeiense

Aconitum wardii var. hopeiense, commonly known as the Hope's Monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers that range from blue to purple. The plant features compound leaves with deeply lobed foliage, and its roots are tuberous, typical of the genus Aconitum. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes rather than medicinal use.
