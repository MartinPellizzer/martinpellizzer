Arabis collina

Arabis collina, commonly known as rock白菜 or rock mustard, is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features basal leaves that are elongated and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.

Arabis collina is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
