Arundinaria floribunda

Arundinaria floribunda, commonly known as the flowering bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, glossy surface.

Arundinaria floribunda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
