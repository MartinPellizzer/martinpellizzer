Acalypha agrestis

Acalypha agrestis, commonly known as the roughleaf ironweed, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with rough or hairy surfaces, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with greenish-yellow flowers, which are often surrounded by bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Acalypha agrestis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
