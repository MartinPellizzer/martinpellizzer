Amyris mexicana

Amyris mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish wood that emits a pleasant scent. The plant features simple, opposite leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Amyris mexicana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a resilient plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
