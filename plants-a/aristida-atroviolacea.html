Aristida atroviolacea

Aristida atroviolacea, commonly known as black violetgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and panicles of small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a desirable choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.

Aristida atroviolacea plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
