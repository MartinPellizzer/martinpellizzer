Acrolophia sphaerocarpa

Acrolophia sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe-headed orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, rounded flower spikes that resemble small globes. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
