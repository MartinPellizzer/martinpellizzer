Abies concolor var. lowiana

Abies concolor var. lowiana, commonly known as the lowland white fir, is a variant of the white fir native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark green, needle-like foliage. The plant has short, stout cones that typically mature to a reddish-brown color. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, thriving in cooler, moist environments.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden settings.

Native Distribution

Abies concolor var. lowiana is primarily native to the western United States, particularly in California and Oregon.

It is commonly found in high-elevation coniferous forests, often growing at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. This variant of the white fir is adapted to cool, moist environments with well-drained soils. It is often associated with other conifer species such as Douglas fir and lodgepole pine.

Its range extends into parts of Nevada and northern Baja California, where it thrives in mountainous regions.
