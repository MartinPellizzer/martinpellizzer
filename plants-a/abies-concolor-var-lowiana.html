Abies concolor var. lowiana

Abies concolor var. lowiana, commonly known as the lowland white fir, is a variant of the white fir native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark green, needle-like foliage. The plant has short, stout cones that typically mature to a reddish-brown color. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, thriving in cooler, moist environments.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden settings.
