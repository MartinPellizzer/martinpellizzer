Athenaea angustifolia

Athenaea angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved athenaea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
