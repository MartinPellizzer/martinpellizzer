Allium paniculatum var. longispathum

Allium paniculatum var. longispathum, commonly known as the long-spurred onion, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, grass-like foliage and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers on tall, branched stems. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, elongated flower buds that give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild, edible bulbs.
