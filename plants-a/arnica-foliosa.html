Arnica foliosa

Arnica foliosa, commonly known as leafy arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, dark green leaves. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves and flowers contain bioactive compounds, making it valued in traditional herbal medicine.

Arnica foliosa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.
