Alkanna baeotica

Alkanna baeotica, commonly known as the red sandalwood or alkanna, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots contain a resinous substance that has been historically used in dyeing and medicinal applications.

Alkanna baeotica thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
