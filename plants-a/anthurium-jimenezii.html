Anthurium jimenezii

Anthurium jimenezii, commonly known as the Jimenez's anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spatula-shaped leaf. The plant features vibrant, colorful spathe that can range from deep red to orange, often contrasting with its dark green foliage. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium jimenezii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
