Anethum dulce

Anethum dulce, commonly known as dill, is an annual herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its feathery, aromatic leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for temperate climates. Its seeds, often used in cooking, have a distinct licorice-like flavor.

Botanically, it belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and carrots.
