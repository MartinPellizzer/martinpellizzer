Amelanchier goldmanii

Amelanchier goldmanii, commonly known as the golden alleth, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically dark red to black in color, with a tart flavor. The plant has oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding visual interest to the landscape. It prefers well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a versatile addition to native gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy white flowers in spring and vibrant autumn foliage.
