Aparine tenera

Aparine tenera, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy leaves and stems, as well as its showy, violet or purple flowers. However, the plant is toxic and should not be consumed by humans or animals.
