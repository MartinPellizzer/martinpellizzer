Allium pallens var. pseudooleraceum

Allium pallens var. pseudooleraceum, commonly known as the purple garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green with a silvery sheen, and it produces clusters of small, pale purple to pink flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is closely related to other allium species and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild garlic-like aroma.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or borders.
