Acacia wickhamii subsp. wickhamii

Acacia wickhamii subsp.

wickhamii, commonly known as the Wicking's wickhamia, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces yellow globular flowers in spring, which are arranged in compact clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
