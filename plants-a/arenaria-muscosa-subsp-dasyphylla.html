Arenaria muscosa subsp. dasyphylla

Arenaria muscosa subsp.

dasyphylla, commonly known as the mossy sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions. It typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils, demonstrating a high tolerance for cold and nutrient-poor environments.

The plant has small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention and protection against harsh weather. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This subspecies is characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to extreme climatic conditions.
