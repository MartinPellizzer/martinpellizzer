Adenandra villosa subsp. imbricata

Adenandra villosa subsp.

imbricata, commonly known as the rough-leaved adenandra, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, often grayish leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry or semi-arid environments.
