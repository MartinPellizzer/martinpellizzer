Asplundia tetragonopus

Asplundia tetragonopus, commonly known as the four-angled arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, four-sided stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers enclosed in a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Asplundia tetragonopus is valued for its edible tubers, which are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
