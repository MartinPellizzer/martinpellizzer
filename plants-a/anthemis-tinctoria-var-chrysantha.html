Anthemis tinctoria var. chrysantha

Anthemis tinctoria var. chrysantha, commonly known as golden chamomile, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which resemble those of the common chamomile but are more densely packed. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

The flowers are often used in herbal teas and have been historically valued for their medicinal properties.
