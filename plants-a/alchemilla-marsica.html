Alchemilla marsica

Alchemilla marsica, commonly known as the alpine lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring, which are clustered in corymbs. Alchemilla marsica thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
