Astragalus feddei

Astragalus feddei, commonly known as the Fedde's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Astragalus feddei is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules.

Its ecological role includes providing forage for wildlife and contributing to soil stabilization in its native habitat.
