Alternanthera polycephala

Alternanthera polycephala, commonly known as the red veined sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red veins on its heart-shaped leaves, which are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its growth habit is typically herbaceous, with a short-lived perennial cycle in warmer climates.
