Aconitum corsicum

Aconitum corsicum, commonly known as Corsican aconite, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, spikelike clusters. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, waxy appearance.

Aconitum corsicum contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires caution when handling.
