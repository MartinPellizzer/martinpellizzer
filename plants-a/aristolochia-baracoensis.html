Aristolochia baracoensis

Aristolochia baracoensis, commonly known as the Brazilian pipevine, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and its unique, intricate flowers that resemble a pipe or a horn. The plant produces small, greenish-white fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, red aril. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
