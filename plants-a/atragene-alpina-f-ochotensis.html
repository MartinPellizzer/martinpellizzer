Atragene alpina f. ochotensis

Atragene alpina f. ochotensis, commonly known as the Ochotensis form of the Alpine Atragene, is a variant of the species native to mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to alpine environments.
