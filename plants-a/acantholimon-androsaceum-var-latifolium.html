Acantholimon androsaceum var. latifolium

Acantholimon androsaceum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved sandverbena, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically broad and rounded, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to rocky or sandy soils. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
