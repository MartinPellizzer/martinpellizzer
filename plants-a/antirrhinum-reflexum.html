Antirrhinum reflexum

Antirrhinum reflexum, commonly known as the reflexed snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
