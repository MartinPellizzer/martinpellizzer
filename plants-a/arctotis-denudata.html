Arctotis denudata

Arctotis denudata, commonly known as the naked arctotis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal regions and is characterized by its gray-green foliage and showy yellow or orange flowers. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
