Anerincleistus echinatus

Anerincleistus echinatus, commonly known as the spiny leaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in small, spiny protrusions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Anerincleistus echinatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
