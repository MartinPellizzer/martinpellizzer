Anemia tomentosa var. anthriscifolia

Anemia tomentosa var. anthriscifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved anemia, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are soft to the touch and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Apiaceae family.
