Acinos majoranifolius

Acinos majoranifolius, commonly known as the sweet everlasting or sweet everlasting thyme, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, aromatic leaves and clusters of pale yellow to white flowers. The plant produces small, seed-like fruits called caryopses, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden environments.

Its fragrant foliage and long-lasting blooms make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and herbal gardens.
