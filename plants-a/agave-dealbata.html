Agave dealbata

Agave dealbata, commonly known as the white agave or silver agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves covered in a white, powdery coating called bloom. The leaves are typically blue-green in color and have sharp, spiny tips. It produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellowish-green flowers that bloom once in its lifetime.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
