Artemisia sarawschanica

Artemisia sarawschanica, commonly known as the Karakoram wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or mountainous regions. Its leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its strong, bitter scent.

Artemisia sarawschanica is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
