Acrostichum wawrae

Acrostichum wawrae, commonly known as the Wawra's fern, is a rare and endangered species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Davalliaceae family and is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in dense rainforests. Its spores are dispersed by wind, and it reproduces through both sexual and asexual means.

Due to habitat destruction, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
