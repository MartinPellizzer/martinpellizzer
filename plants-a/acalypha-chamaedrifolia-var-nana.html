Acalypha chamaedrifolia var. nana

Acalypha chamaedrifolia var. nana, commonly known as dwarf buttonweed, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for use in ornamental gardening and as a ground cover.
