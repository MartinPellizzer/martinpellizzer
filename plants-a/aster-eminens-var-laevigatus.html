Aster eminens var. laevigatus

Aster eminens var. laevigatus, commonly known as the smoothleaf aster, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers are visited by a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The smooth, glabrous texture of its leaves gives it its varietal name, laevigatus.
