Allium tytthanthum

Allium tytthanthum, commonly known as the purple garlic or purple onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features strap-shaped, bluish-green leaves and produces clusters of purple or pinkish flowers in the summer. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value and mild garlic-like scent. Its bulbs are edible and can be used in cooking, similar to other allium species.

However, it is less commonly cultivated than other onions and garlic varieties.
