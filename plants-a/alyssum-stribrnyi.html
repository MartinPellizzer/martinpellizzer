Alyssum stribrnyi

Alyssum stribrnyi, commonly known as the striped alyssum, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale purple. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, elongated seed pods.

Alyssum stribrnyi is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.
