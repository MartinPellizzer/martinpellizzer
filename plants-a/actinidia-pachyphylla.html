Actinidia pachyphylla

Actinidia pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved actinidia, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It is characterized by its large, thick, leathery leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in early summer, followed by edible berries that ripen to a yellowish color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The species is also valued for its potential in horticulture and as a food source for wildlife.
