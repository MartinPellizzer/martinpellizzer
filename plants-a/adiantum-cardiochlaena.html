Adiantum cardiochlaena

Adiantum cardiochlaena, commonly known as the heart-shaped maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble lace. The plant features a creeping rhizome with clusters of small, spore-bearing leaves called sorus. Its leaves are typically bright green and have a distinctive, heart-shaped appearance.

Adiantum cardiochlaena thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
