Allantodia nigrosquamosa

Allantodia nigrosquamosa, commonly known as the black-scaled allantodia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its dark, scaly bark and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing year-round structure.

Allantodia nigrosquamosa is often found in coastal regions and is adapted to withstand salt spray and drought.
