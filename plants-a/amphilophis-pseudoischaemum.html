Amphilophis pseudoischaemum

Amphilophis pseudoischaemum, commonly known as the narrowleaf bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It possesses specialized bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms, which it then digests for nutrients. The plant typically grows in shallow waters and has finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that aid in photosynthesis. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often green or yellowish in color.

Amphilophis pseudoischaemum plays a role in its ecosystem by controlling populations of tiny aquatic invertebrates.
