Angelica sinensis var. wilsonii

Angelica sinensis var. wilsonii, commonly known as Chinese angelica or Dang Gui, is a biennial or perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which eventually develop into oval, dry fruits. Its roots are the primary part used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers temperate climates.
