Alsophila lepidotricha

Alsophila lepidotricha, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The fronds are typically dark green with a silvery underside, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
