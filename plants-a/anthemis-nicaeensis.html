Anthemis nicaeensis

Anthemis nicaeensis, commonly known as the Nicaean chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with white ray florets, resembling those of other chamomile species. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

The leaves are aromatic and have been used traditionally for their mild medicinal properties.
