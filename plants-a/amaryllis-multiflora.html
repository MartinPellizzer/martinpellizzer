Amaryllis multiflora

Amaryllis multiflora, commonly known as the clustered amaryllis, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It produces multiple flower stems that bear clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that are typically evergreen, providing a lush foliage backdrop. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Amaryllis multiflora is known for its ability to bloom reliably in warm climates, often displaying its vibrant flowers in late spring to early summer.
