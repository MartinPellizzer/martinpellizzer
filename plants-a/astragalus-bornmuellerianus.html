Astragalus bornmuellerianus

Astragalus bornmuellerianus, commonly known as the Bornmueller's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves with stipules. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many nitrogen-fixing plants.

Its medicinal properties have been explored in traditional medicine, though further research is needed to confirm its efficacy.
