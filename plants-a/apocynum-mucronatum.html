Apocynum mucronatum

Apocynum mucronatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dogbane, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes many milkweed species, and is characterized by its milky sap that contains toxic compounds. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a pointed tip. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and are usually pink to reddish in color.

Apocynum mucronatum is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
