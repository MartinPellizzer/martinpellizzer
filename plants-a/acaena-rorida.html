Acaena rorida

Acaena rorida, commonly known as the silver weed, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Acaena rorida is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and alpine regions.
