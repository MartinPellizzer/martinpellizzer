Atriplex velutinella subsp. tomentosa

Atriplex velutinella subsp.

tomentosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a soft, velvety appearance.

The plant has small, green to reddish leaves with a pubescent surface, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters. It is adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a hardy species for harsh environments.

The subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and provide habitat for wildlife.
