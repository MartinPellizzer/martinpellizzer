Aesculus × woerlitzensis var. ellwangeri

Aesculus × woerlitzensis var. ellwangeri, commonly known as the Ellwanger horse chestnut, is a hybrid species within the Aesculus genus, known for its ornamental value.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in late spring, often with a subtle pinkish tinge. The plant produces spiny, capsule-like fruits that contain several glossy, chestnut-like seeds. It typically grows as a deciduous tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

The leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are dark green and glossy on top.
