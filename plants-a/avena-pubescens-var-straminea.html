Avena pubescens var. straminea

Avena pubescens var. straminea, commonly known as the silky oat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and soft, silky hairs covering its foliage. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that appear in dense clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
