Aristolochia ringens

Aristolochia ringens, commonly known as the purple waxflower, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves and distinctive, intricate, purple-tinged flowers that resemble a pipe or a horn. The plant produces small, greenish-white berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its unique floral structure and historical use in traditional medicine make it a notable species in botanical studies.
