Allium paterfamilias

Allium paterfamilias, commonly known as the giant garlic, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, bulbous stem and broad, strap-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its size and striking appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
