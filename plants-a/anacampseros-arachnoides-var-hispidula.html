Anacampseros arachnoides var. hispidula

Anacampseros arachnoides var. hispidula, commonly known as the hairy anacampseros, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy, hairy leaves that store water, adapting to arid environments. The leaves are typically gray-green with a waxy coating, enhancing their ability to retain moisture. It produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding ornamental value.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
