Anthemis ammanthus subsp. ammanthus

Anthemis ammanthus subsp.

ammanthus, commonly known as the Ammanthus tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

Native to the Mediterranean region, this species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has a subtle, aromatic scent.

It is also known for its historical use in herbal medicine.
