Asarum hisauchii

Asarum hisauchii, commonly known as Japanese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves. The plant produces a unique, irregularly shaped flower that is often covered in fine hairs. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine, though the plant contains toxic compounds.

Asarum hisauchii thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
