Astragalus haematinus

Astragalus haematinus, commonly known as the red bupleurum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by lens-shaped pods containing seeds. Astragalus haematinus is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soils, making it a common sight in alpine environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
