Astragalus peruvianus

Astragalus peruvianus, commonly known as the Peruvian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Astragalus peruvianus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional South American herbal practices.
