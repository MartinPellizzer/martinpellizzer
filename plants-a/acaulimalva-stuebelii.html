Acaulimalva stuebelii

Acaulimalva stuebelii, commonly known as the Stuebel's mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, velvety leaves and produces showy, pink to purple flowers in late spring. The plant has a short, unbranched stem, which is why it is classified as acauline. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Acaulimalva stuebelii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
