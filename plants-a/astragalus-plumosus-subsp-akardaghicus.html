Astragalus plumosus subsp. akardaghicus

Astragalus plumosus subsp.

akardaghicus, commonly known as the Akardagh milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender stems and compound leaves with finely divided leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
