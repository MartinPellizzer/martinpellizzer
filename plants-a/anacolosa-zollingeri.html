Anacolosa zollingeri

Anacolosa zollingeri, commonly known as the Zollinger's acanthus, is a tropical plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in spikes along the stem. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in moist tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in suitable climates.
