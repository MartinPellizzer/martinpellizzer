Albizia buntingii

Albizia buntingii, commonly known as the yellow albizia, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a soft, feathery appearance. The tree produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it can grow up to 10 meters tall in suitable conditions.

Albizia buntingii is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
