Axonopus fissifolius

Axonopus fissifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf axonopus, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along waterways.

Axonopus fissifolius is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic environments.
