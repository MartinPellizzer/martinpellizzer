Aspidostemon apiculatus

Aspidostemon apiculatus, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring slender, hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers, usually in shades of blue or violet, which bloom in late summer. Its flowers are characterized by a narrow, pointed tip, giving the species its specific epithet "apiculatus.".

Aspidostemon apiculatus is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes.
