Athyrium cystopteroides

Athyrium cystopteroides, commonly known as the Chinese water fern, is a fern species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are divided into small, finger-like segments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in aquatic and riparian habitats.
