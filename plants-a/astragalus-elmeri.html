Astragalus elmeri

Astragalus elmeri, commonly known as Elmer's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus elmeri is part of the legume family (Fabaceae) and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.
