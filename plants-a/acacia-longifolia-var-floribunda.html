Acacia longifolia var. floribunda

Acacia longifolia var. floribunda, commonly known as the coastal wattles, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in dense, globular clusters. The plant is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. It plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion.

Its ornamental value has also made it a popular garden plant in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
