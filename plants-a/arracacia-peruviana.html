Arracacia peruviana

Arracacia peruviana, commonly known as Peruvian aloe, is a succulent plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are thick and fleshy. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.

It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
