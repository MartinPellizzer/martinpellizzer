Arabis alpina var. clusiana

Arabis alpina var. clusiana, commonly known as the Clusian alpine rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
