Allium rotundum var. erectum

Allium rotundum var. erectum, commonly known as the Armenian garlic or mountain garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It features a bulbous stem with clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in umbels. The plant has lance-shaped, grass-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its bulbs are rich in sulfur compounds, contributing to its pungent aroma and potential health benefits.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary versatility.
