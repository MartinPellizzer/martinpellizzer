Alchemilla kiwuensis subsp. rhodesica

Alchemilla kiwuensis subsp.

rhodesica, commonly known as the Rhodes alkanet, is a perennial herb native to eastern Africa. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in compact clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
