Abrotanella nivigena

Abrotanella nivigena, commonly known as the snowplant, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to the dry, sandy soils of its habitat. It thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant once established.

Abrotanella nivigena is also known for its ability to survive in harsh coastal conditions, making it a characteristic species of coastal scrub ecosystems.
