Astragalus triqueter

Astragalus triqueter, commonly known as three-lobed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats. Astragalus triqueter is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
