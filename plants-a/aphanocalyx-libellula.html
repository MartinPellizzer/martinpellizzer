Aphanocalyx libellula

Aphanocalyx libellula, commonly known as the small-flowered flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features delicate, pendulous flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often appearing in clusters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Aphanocalyx libellula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
