Aristida hassleri var. aculeolata

Aristida hassleri var. aculeolata, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, bract-like flowers, which are typically greenish to brown in color. This plant is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that help it survive in poor soil environments.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
