Aechmea isabellina

Aechmea isabellina, commonly known as the pink pineapple plant, is a species of bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety coating of trichomes, which help collect and retain moisture. The plant produces a striking pink or red flower spike that emerges from the center of the rosette. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Aechmea isabellina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in humid environments.

Morphology

Aechmea isabellina has rosette-shaped leaves that are narrow, grass-like, and arranged in a tight spiral, with a vibrant green color and a waxy texture.

The plant lacks a true stem, and its base is a thick, fibrous structure resembling a pseudostem, which is usually green and slightly hairy. Its inflorescence is a spadix surrounded by a spathe, with small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom during the summer months and are pollinated by insects. The fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for absorbing moisture from the surrounding soil.
