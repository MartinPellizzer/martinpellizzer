Anthericum liliago var. sphaerocarpum

Anthericum liliago var. sphaerocarpum, commonly known as the globe bellflower, is a variant of the common bellflower.

It is characterized by its distinctive spherical, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The flowers are often a striking shade of blue or purple, adding visual interest to garden landscapes.
