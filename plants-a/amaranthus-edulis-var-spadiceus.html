Amaranthus edulis var. spadiceus

Amaranthus edulis var. spadiceus, commonly known as black amaranth, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dark purple to black bracts that surround the flowering heads. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into seeds, which are edible and rich in protein and minerals. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

Its leaves and seeds are used in traditional dishes and have been valued for their nutritional benefits for centuries.
