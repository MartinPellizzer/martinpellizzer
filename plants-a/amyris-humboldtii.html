Amyris humboldtii

Amyris humboldtii, commonly known as the white sand verbena, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping. It produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
