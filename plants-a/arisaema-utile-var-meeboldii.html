Arisaema utile var. meeboldii

Arisaema utile var. meeboldii, commonly known as the Meebold's Indian shot, is a variant of the arrowhead plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves and its unique, spadix-like inflorescence that resembles a cluster of small, clustered berries. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers, which are enclosed within a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage and floral structure.
