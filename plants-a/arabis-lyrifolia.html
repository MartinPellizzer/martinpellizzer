Arabis lyrifolia

Arabis lyrifolia, commonly known as the lyrical rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a perennial species that is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

The flowers have four petals arranged in a cross shape, giving it the common name "rockcress.".
