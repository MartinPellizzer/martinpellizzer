Acanthophyllum coloratum var. eglandulosum

Acanthophyllum coloratum var. eglandulosum, commonly known as the glandular acanthophyllum, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and spiny stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, adapted to the harsh conditions of its environment. Its leaves are typically small and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This species is well-suited to sandy soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert habitats.
