Aster leptophyllus

Aster leptophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers bloom in shades of purple or blue.

Aster leptophyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
