Astragalus oleifolius var. kurdistanicus

Astragalus oleifolius var. kurdistanicus, commonly known as the Kurdish milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Kurdistan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh conditions.

This variety is of interest for its potential medicinal properties and ecological role in its native habitat.
