Acaena leptacantha subsp. connectens

Acaena leptacantha subsp.

connectens, commonly known as the slender bristled weed, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to New Zealand. It features small, spiny leaves and produces spherical, yellowish-brown fruits that are adapted for animal dispersal.

The plant thrives in coastal and inland sandy soils, often found in disturbed or open habitats. Its flowers are typically inconspicuous, with stamens that are often longer than the petals.

This subspecies is characterized by its distinctive branching pattern and robust, thorn-like structures.
