Aquilegia einseleana var. pseudothalictrifolia

Aquilegia einseleana var. pseudothalictrifolia, commonly known as the alpine columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, with five petals that are often adorned with contrasting veins. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are generally upright and branched. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
