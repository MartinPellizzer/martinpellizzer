Austroblechnum bullatum

Austroblechnum bullatum, commonly known as the Bullate Water Fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to New Zealand.

It thrives in freshwater environments, particularly in still or slow-moving streams and ponds. The plant has finely divided, needle-like leaves that are typically submerged, contributing to its delicate appearance. It plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for various aquatic organisms.

Its reproductive structures are borne on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.
