Anthemis tempskyana

Anthemis tempskyana, commonly known as the Tempsky's fleur-de-lis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble clusters of small flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with ray florets that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
