Acacia macrocentra

Acacia macrocentra, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant. Its flowers are visited by a variety of insects, including bees and birds, making it an important component of local ecosystems.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture.
