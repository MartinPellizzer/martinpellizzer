Asplenium acutum

Asplenium acutum, commonly known as the sharp-leaf spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a small, terrestrial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are often dark green and glossy. It produces spores in clusters called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium acutum is known for its adaptability and is found in various regions across the Northern Hemisphere.
