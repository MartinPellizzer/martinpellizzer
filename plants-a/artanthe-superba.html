Artanthe superba

Artanthe superba, commonly known as the mountain heath, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form with dense foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and arranged in a compact rosette pattern.

Artanthe superba is well adapted to cold, dry environments and is often found in alpine meadows.
