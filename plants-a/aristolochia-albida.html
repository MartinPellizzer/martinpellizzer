Aristolochia albida

Aristolochia albida, commonly known as the white Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular, white to pale green flowers that resemble a pipe, hence its common name. The plant produces heart-shaped, veined leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The genus Aristolochia includes many species with similar morphological features and ecological roles.
