Acacia picachensis

Acacia picachensis, commonly known as the Picacho acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry climates. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in compact, globular inflorescences, which are typically visited by native pollinators such as bees and moths. Acacia picachensis is well-suited to sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert scrub and creosote bush communities.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife species.
