Aster macrophyllus

Aster macrophyllus, commonly known as the broadleaf aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that can reach up to 10 inches in length. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers in the late summer and early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Aster macrophyllus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
