Artemisia seriphium var. marina

Artemisia seriphium var. marina, commonly known as sea wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It thrives in saline and sandy soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh maritime environments. The plant features aromatic, silver-gray foliage with small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in compact clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is valued in horticulture for its resilience and ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
