Allium bilgilii

Allium bilgilii, commonly known as the Armenian garlic or mountain garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a short, stout stem.

Allium bilgilii is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes.
