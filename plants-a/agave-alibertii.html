Agave alibertii

Agave alibertii, commonly known as the Mexican spineless agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The leaves are often covered in a layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. It produces a tall flowering stalk that bears yellow flowers, which are pollinated by insects.

This plant is adapted to thrive in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping in dry climates.
