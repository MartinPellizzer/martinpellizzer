Asplundia sparrei

Asplundia sparrei, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its unique, velvety pink petals and a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch that serves as a pollinator trap. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in cloud forests or high-altitude grasslands. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a delicate, fragrant aroma that attracts specific pollinators.
