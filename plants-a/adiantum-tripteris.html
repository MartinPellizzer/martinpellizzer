Adiantum tripteris

Adiantum tripteris, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant plant belonging to the family Adiantaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds that are typically bright green and have a distinctive, almost lace-like appearance. The plant is native to various regions in the Americas and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are wiry and often form dense, compact clusters.

Adiantum tripteris is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
