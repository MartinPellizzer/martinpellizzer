Aristolochia quercetorum

Aristolochia quercetorum, commonly known as the European birthwort, is a climbing vine native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often greenish or brownish in color. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including aristolochic acids, which have been studied for their medicinal properties. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
