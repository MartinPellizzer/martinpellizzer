Asterochlaena platyloba var. hirsutula

Asterochlaena platyloba var. hirsutula, commonly known as the hairy broadleaf asterochlaena, is a perennial herb native to southern China.

It features erect, hairy stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
