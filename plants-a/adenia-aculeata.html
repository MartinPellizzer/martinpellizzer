Adenia aculeata

Adenia aculeata, commonly known as the prickly adenia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its woody stems and thorny branches. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are often followed by distinctive, fleshy, red berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and typically have a rough, hairy texture.

Adenia aculeata is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forested areas or along stream banks.
