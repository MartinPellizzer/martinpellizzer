Aphelandra nobilis

Aphelandra nobilis, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves with a deep green color and prominent veins. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and moths. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a houseplant.

Aphelandra nobilis thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture to maintain its lush foliage.
