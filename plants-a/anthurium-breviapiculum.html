Anthurium breviapiculum

Anthurium breviapiculum, commonly known as the short-spurred anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon basin.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, spathaceous flower. The plant's inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, which varies in hue depending on the cultivar. It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium breviapiculum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is popular in indoor gardening.
