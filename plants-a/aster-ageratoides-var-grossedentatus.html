Aster ageratoides var. grossedentatus

Aster ageratoides var. grossedentatus, commonly known as the roughhead aster, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodland edges and wetlands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy composite flower heads.

The variety grossedentatus is distinguished by its prominently toothed leaves and robust growth habit.
