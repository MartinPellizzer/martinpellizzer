Anguria capitata var. pallida

Anguria capitata var. pallida, commonly known as the pale-flowered melon, is a unique variant of the anguria genus.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers, which contrast with its green foliage. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically less than 5 centimeters in diameter. It is native to tropical regions of South America and thrives in warm, humid climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
