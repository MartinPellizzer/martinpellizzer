Araracuara

Araracuara, commonly known as the "Amazonian orchid," is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, colorful blossoms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the dense canopy of the rainforest. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Araracuara plays a vital role in the ecological balance of its habitat, supporting various pollinators and contributing to biodiversity.
