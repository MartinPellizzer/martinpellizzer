Acilepis divergens

Acilepis divergens, commonly known as the narrowleaf false indigo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features slender, upright stems with trifoliate leaves composed of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets. Its small, blue to purple flowers are clustered in racemes and bloom in early summer.

Acilepis divergens is often found in dry, sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
