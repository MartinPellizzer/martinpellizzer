Aster watsonii

Aster watsonii, commonly known as the Watson's aster, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and streambanks. It has linear to lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Aster watsonii is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
