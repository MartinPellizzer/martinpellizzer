Anguillaria racemosa

Anguillaria racemosa, commonly known as the swamp hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in swampy or marshy areas, often reaching heights of up to six feet. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central tube. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and usually have a waxy surface to withstand moist conditions.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in saturated soils, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
