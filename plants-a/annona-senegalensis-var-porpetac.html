Annona senegalensis var. porpetac

Annona senegalensis var. porpetac, commonly known as the African breadfruit, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by spherical, greenish fruits that turn yellow when mature. The fruit's pulp is edible, with a mild, sweet flavor, and it is often consumed fresh or used in traditional dishes.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental value and has some medicinal uses in local communities.
