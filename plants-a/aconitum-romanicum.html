Aconitum romanicum

Aconitum romanicum, commonly known as the Romanish monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features palmately compound leaves with a distinctive hooded flower structure, typical of the genus Aconitum. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
