Alnus × pubescens

Alnus × pubescens, commonly known as the European mountain ash or European white ash, is a hybrid species within the genus Alnus.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green leaves that are typically oval in shape and have a slightly hairy underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spherical, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions across Europe.

Alnus × pubescens plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.
