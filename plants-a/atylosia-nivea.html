Atylosia nivea

Atylosia nivea, commonly known as the white coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and a tall, branching stem that supports its floral display. Atylosia nivea is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
