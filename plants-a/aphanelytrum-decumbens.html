Aphanelytrum decumbens

Aphanelytrum decumbens, commonly known as the decumbent grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically forms dense mats on the ground, with slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Aphanelytrum decumbens is adapted to poor soils and is often found in disturbed or dry habitats.
