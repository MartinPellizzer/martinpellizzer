Anisopappus orbicularis

Anisopappus orbicularis, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its broad, orbicular leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Its root system is deep and drought-tolerant, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry conditions.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
