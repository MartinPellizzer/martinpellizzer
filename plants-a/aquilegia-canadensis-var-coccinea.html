Aquilegia canadensis var. coccinea

Aquilegia canadensis var. coccinea, commonly known as the red cardinalflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features striking red to crimson flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
