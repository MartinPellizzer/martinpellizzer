Achillea millefolium subsp. chitralensis

Achillea millefolium subsp.

chitralensis, commonly known as the Chitral yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features a basal rosette of silver-gray, fern-like leaves that are finely divided and aromatic.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species within the genus Achillea.
