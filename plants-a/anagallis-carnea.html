Anagallis carnea

Anagallis carnea, commonly known as the pink evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and woodland edges. It has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Anagallis carnea is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
