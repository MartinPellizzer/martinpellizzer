Astragalus kentrophyta subsp. neomexicanus

Astragalus kentrophyta subsp.

neomexicanus, commonly known as the New Mexico milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats. It plays a role in soil stabilization and supports local pollinators.

Its subspecies status highlights its distinct ecological and morphological traits compared to other Astragalus species.
