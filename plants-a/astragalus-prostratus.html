Astragalus prostratus

Astragalus prostratus, commonly known as the prostrate milkvetch, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its sprawling habit and small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or dry habitats.

Astragalus prostratus plays a role in soil stabilization and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
