Aeonium × aguajilvense

Aeonium × aguajilvense, commonly known as the Mexican rose.

It is a hybrid succulent plant belonging to the Crassulaceae family, characterized by its rosette-forming structure and thick, fleshy leaves. Native to Mexico, it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance due to its succulent nature. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, or orange during the growing season.

Its compact size and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
