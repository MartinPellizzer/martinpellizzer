Avena myriantha

Avena myriantha, commonly known as the many-flowered oat, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous florets, which are typically greenish to brown in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, temperate climates.

Avena myriantha is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
