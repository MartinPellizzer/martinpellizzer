Astrocarpa sesamoides var. suffruticosa

Astrocarpa sesamoides var. suffruticosa, commonly known as the coastal dune morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are often blue or purple in color, blooming throughout the warmer months. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and succulent.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dune systems and providing habitat for various coastal organisms.
