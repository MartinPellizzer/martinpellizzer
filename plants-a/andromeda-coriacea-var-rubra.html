Andromeda coriacea var. rubra

Andromeda coriacea var. rubra, commonly known as the red alder, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, acidic soils along stream banks and in coastal areas. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by elliptical, dark green leaves. Its botanical features include a shallow root system and the ability to thrive in wet environments.

The species is also known for its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
