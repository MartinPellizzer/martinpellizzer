Astragalus narbonensis var. claryi

Astragalus narbonensis var. claryi, commonly known as Clary's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with small, hairy stipules. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
