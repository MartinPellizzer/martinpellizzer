Agrostis alba var. lutescens

Agrostis alba var. lutescens, commonly known as the golden meadowgrass, is a variant of the common meadowgrass species.

It is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in moist grasslands and meadows. The plant features slender, tufted stems and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that becomes more yellowish in the varietal form.

The golden meadowgrass is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
