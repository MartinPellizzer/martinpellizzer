Acacia corusca

Acacia corusca, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. Acacia corusca is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

It is a hardy species that plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
