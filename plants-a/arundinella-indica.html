Arundinella indica

Arundinella indica, commonly known as the Indian reed, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found near water bodies.

Its fibrous stalks have been historically used for thatching and basket weaving.
