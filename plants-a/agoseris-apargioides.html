Agoseris apargioides

Agoseris apargioides, commonly known as the Sierra goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.
