Acacia ibirocayensis

Acacia ibirocayensis, commonly known as the Ibirocayensis acacia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to specific regions in South America, particularly Brazil, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or golden in color, arranged in compact spherical or cylindrical inflorescences.

Acacia ibirocayensis is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its natural habitat.
