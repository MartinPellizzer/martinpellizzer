Amaranthus purgans

Amaranthus purgans, commonly known as the redroot amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that develop into three-angled, spiny seed capsules. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy species adaptable to various climates.

Amaranthus purgans is also noted for its historical use in traditional medicine.
