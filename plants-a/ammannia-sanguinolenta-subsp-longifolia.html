Ammannia sanguinolenta subsp. longifolia

Ammannia sanguinolenta subsp.

longifolia, commonly known as redroot ammannia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its striking red stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and streambanks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a reddish tinge, adding to its ornamental appeal.

The subspecies is distinguished by its longer leaves compared to other varieties of Ammannia.
