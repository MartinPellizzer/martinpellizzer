Angraecum leprechaunicum

Angraecum leprechaunicum, commonly known as the Leprechaun's Orchid, is a rare and unique species native to Madagascar.

It is an epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with intricate patterns resembling the clover associated with leprechauns. Its blooming period occurs during the rainy season, and it requires specific care to mimic its natural habitat.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological needs, it is often cultivated by experienced growers.
