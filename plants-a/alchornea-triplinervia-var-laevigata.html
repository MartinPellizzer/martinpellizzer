Alchornea triplinervia var. laevigata

Alchornea triplinervia var. laevigata, commonly known as the smoothleaf alchornea, is a species native to parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its smooth, often glabrous bark and elliptical leaves with three prominent veins. The leaves are typically arranged alternately along the stems and have a leathery texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by oval-shaped berries containing a single seed.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
