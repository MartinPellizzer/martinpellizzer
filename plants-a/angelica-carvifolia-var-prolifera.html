Angelica carvifolia var. prolifera

Angelica carvifolia var. prolifera, commonly known as the fragrant angelica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features tall, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, compound leaves that have a distinctive serrated edge. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are aromatic and have been historically used in traditional medicine.

This variety is notable for its vigorous growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
