Asperula exasperata subsp. tephrocarpa

Asperula exasperata subsp.

tephrocarpa, commonly known as the tephrocarpa azalea, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a low-growing shrub with evergreen foliage and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white.

The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a leathery texture and a glossy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
