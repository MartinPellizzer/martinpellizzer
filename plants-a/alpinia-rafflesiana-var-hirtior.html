Alpinia rafflesiana var. hirtior

Alpinia rafflesiana var. hirtior, commonly known as the hairy ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright rhizomes and large, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking orange-red inflorescences with fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and as a decorative plant in indoor settings.
