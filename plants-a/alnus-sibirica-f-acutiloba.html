Alnus sibirica f. acutiloba

Alnus sibirica f. acutiloba, commonly known as the Siberian alder, is a variant of the Siberian alder tree native to eastern Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its compound leaves and catkin flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with Frankia bacteria, enriching the soil. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and as a source of timber and forage.
