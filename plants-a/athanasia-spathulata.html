Athanasia spathulata

Athanasia spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed immortality, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Athanasia spathulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
