Abies nobilis var. glauca

Abies nobilis var. glauca, commonly known as the white fir, is a variant of the noble fir native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are needle-like, typically measuring between 1 and 2 inches in length, and are arranged spirally along the branches. This evergreen tree can grow to heights of up to 100 feet, forming a conical shape with a broad, spreading crown.

Its cones are small and reddish-brown, maturing within a single season.
