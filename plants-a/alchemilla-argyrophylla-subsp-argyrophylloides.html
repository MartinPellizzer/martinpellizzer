Alchemilla argyrophylla subsp. argyrophylloides

Alchemilla argyrophylla subsp.

argyrophylloides, commonly known as silverleaf lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and southern Europe. It features rosettes of silver-gray, woolly leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
