Aira involucrata

Aira involucrata, commonly known as the purple lovegrass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces fluffy, pale purple plumes that resemble small clouds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It blooms during the spring and summer months, attracting various pollinators with its showy inflorescences.

Aira involucrata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
