Arenga distincta

Arenga distincta, commonly known as the giant palm or wild areca, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that develop into elongated, fibrous fruits. Arenga distincta is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used traditionally for its fibers and as a source of starch.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for both landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
