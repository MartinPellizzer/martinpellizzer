Aristida funiculata var. paradoxa

Aristida funiculata var. paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox grass, is a perennial grass species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale brown to beige flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscaping.
