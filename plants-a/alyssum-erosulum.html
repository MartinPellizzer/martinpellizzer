Alyssum erosulum

Alyssum erosulum, commonly known as the creeping alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming throughout the summer months. The plant has hairy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum erosulum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
