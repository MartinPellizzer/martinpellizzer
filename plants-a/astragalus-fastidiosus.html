Astragalus fastidiosus

Astragalus fastidiosus, commonly known as the western honeyvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is a valuable forage plant for certain livestock.

Astragalus fastidiosus is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
