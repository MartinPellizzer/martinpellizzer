Anemone hirsutissima var. taraoi

Anemone hirsutissima var. taraoi, commonly known as the Taraoi anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often with a distinctive red or purple eye. The plant has hairy, lobed leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
