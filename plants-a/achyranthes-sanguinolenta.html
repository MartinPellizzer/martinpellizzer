Achyranthes sanguinolenta

Achyranthes sanguinolenta, commonly known as Chinese knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in cultivated fields and along roadsides.
