Antirrhinum sempervirens var. densiflorum

Antirrhinum sempervirens var. densiflorum, commonly known as the dense-flowered snapdragon, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, upright growth with clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and purple. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its prolonged flowering period and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
