Allium bidentatum var. andaense

Allium bidentatum var. andaense, commonly known as the Andean leek, is a botanical variant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a perennial bulbous plant characterized by its slender, hollow stems and clustered, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Allium genus. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a valuable crop in local cuisines.

The plant thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often cultivated for both its culinary and ornamental qualities.
