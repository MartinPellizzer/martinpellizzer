Anthurium cerrobaulense

Anthurium cerrobaulense, commonly known as the Cerrobaulense arrowhead, is a striking tropical plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive spathe and spadix inflorescence. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically deep green with a velvety texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium cerrobaulense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
