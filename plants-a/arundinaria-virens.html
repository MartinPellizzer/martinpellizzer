Arundinaria virens

Arundinaria virens, commonly known as the green bamboo, is a species of grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fine, green leaves. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its growth habit is clump-forming, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer.

Arundinaria virens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
