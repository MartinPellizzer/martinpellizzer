Adiantum aethiopicum

Adiantum aethiopicum, commonly known as the African maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble lace, often with a bluish-green hue. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or rocky crevices. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Adiantum aethiopicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
