Amaryllis lutea

Amaryllis lutea, commonly known as the yellow amaryllis, is a bulbous plant native to South America.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Amaryllis lutea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
