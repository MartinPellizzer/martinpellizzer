Azara integrifolia

Azara integrifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pink azara, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits resembling cherries. Azara integrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to various tropical and subtropical climates.
