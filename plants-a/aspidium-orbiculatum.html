Aspidium orbiculatum

Aspidium orbiculatum, commonly known as the round shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its orbicular, leathery fronds that are typically divided into lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or on rocky surfaces.

Aspidium orbiculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its unique foliage.
