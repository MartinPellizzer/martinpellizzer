Anemone narcissiflora var. zawratensis

Anemone narcissiflora var. zawratensis, commonly known as the Zawrat anemone, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a subtle pinkish tinge. The plant has basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, spring-blooming flowers.
