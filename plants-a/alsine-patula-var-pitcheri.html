Alsine patula var. pitcheri

Alsine patula var. pitcheri, commonly known as the Pitcher's alsike, is a flowering plant native to the central and northern United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in prairies and open woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.
