Ayenia implicabilis

Ayenia implicabilis, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features dense, soft, woolly hairs on its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ayenia implicabilis is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
