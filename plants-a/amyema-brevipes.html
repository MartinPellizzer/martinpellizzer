Amyema brevipes

Amyema brevipes, commonly known as the short-footed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees in Australia.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Amyema brevipes plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food for various wildlife species.
