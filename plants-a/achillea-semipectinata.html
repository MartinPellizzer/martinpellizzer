Achillea semipectinata

Achillea semipectinata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features finely divided, gray-green leaves that are narrow and linear, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing small white to pale yellow flowers.

Achillea semipectinata is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
