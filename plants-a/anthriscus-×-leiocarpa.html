Anthriscus × leiocarpa

Anthriscus × leiocarpa, commonly known as the giant hogweed, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, umbrella-shaped flowers and deeply lobed, hairy leaves. The plant can grow up to 5 meters tall, making it one of the tallest members of the Apiaceae family. Its stems are covered in white, silky hairs and have a reddish-brown color.

The fruit is a dry, two-lobed structure that contains the seeds.
