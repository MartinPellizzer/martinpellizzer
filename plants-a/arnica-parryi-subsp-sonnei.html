Arnica parryi subsp. sonnei

Arnica parryi subsp.

sonnei, commonly known as the Sonoran arnica, is a perennial herb native to the Sonoran Desert region. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid environments.

The plant features upright stems with hairy foliage and produces yellow to orange flowers in the summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences. Arnica parryi subsp.

sonnei is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.
