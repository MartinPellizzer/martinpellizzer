Alopecurus antarcticus var. triceps

Alopecurus antarcticus var. triceps, commonly known as the Antarctic three-leaf clover, is a flowering plant native to the southern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and delicate blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in alpine and subantarctic regions. Its growth habit is low and mat-forming, allowing it to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Alopecurus antarcticus var. triceps plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptations to extreme environments.
