Acacia oxycedrus var. microstachys

Acacia oxycedrus var. microstachys, commonly known as the Mediterranean acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to coastal and semi-arid environments. It is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.

The species is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
