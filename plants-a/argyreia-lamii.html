Argyreia lamii

Argyreia lamii, commonly known as the red devil's backbone, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, red stems that resemble a backbone. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers followed by spherical, orange-red berries. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Argyreia lamii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
