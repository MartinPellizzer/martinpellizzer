Agrostis pseudoarundinacea

Agrostis pseudoarundinacea, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray blades and tufted growth habit, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spikelet that is often tinged with brown or reddish hues.

Agrostis pseudoarundinacea is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
