Aristolochia grandiflora

Aristolochia grandiflora, commonly known as the giant Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, intricate, and often fragrant, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically purple or greenish in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure that resembles a pipe or a hood. Its leaves are heart-shaped and can grow quite large, often reaching several feet in diameter.

The plant is also notable for containing aristolochic acids, which are toxic compounds that have been historically used in traditional medicine.
