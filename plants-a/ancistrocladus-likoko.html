Ancistrocladus likoko

Ancistrocladus likoko, commonly known as the African poisonwood, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Ancistrocladaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small yellow flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing minute seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.

However, it is toxic to both humans and animals, containing various toxic compounds in its leaves and other parts.
