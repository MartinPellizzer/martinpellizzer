Ascolepis protea var. transiens

Ascolepis protea var. transiens, commonly known as the Transvaal protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape. The plant is native to the grassland regions of South Africa, particularly in the eastern and western parts of the country. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
