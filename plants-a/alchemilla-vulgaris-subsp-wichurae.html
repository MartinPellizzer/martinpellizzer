Alchemilla vulgaris subsp. wichurae

Alchemilla vulgaris subsp.

wichurae, commonly known as the common lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, hairy leaves that form a dense, cushion-like structure.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in branched clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

Its foliage is known for its ability to retain moisture, which contributes to its resilience in various environmental conditions.
