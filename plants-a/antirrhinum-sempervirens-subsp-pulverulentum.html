Antirrhinum sempervirens subsp. pulverulentum

Antirrhinum sempervirens subsp.

pulverulentum, commonly known as the dusty antirrhinum, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems covered in a fine, powdery coating that gives it its common name.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rocky landscapes.
