Acinos suaveolens subsp. thessalicus

Acinos suaveolens subsp.

thessalicus, commonly known as the Thessalian yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Greece. It features basal rosettes of silver-gray, woolly leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided foliage and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
