Acacia nossibiensis

Acacia nossibiensis, commonly known as the Nossibie acacia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, feathery leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are encased in hard pods, which open to release the seeds when mature.

Acacia nossibiensis is well adapted to survive in low-water conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
