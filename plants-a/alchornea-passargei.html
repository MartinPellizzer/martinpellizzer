Alchornea passargei

Alchornea passargei, commonly known as the African snowflake, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its bark contains compounds that have been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

Alchornea passargei is often found in moist, shaded areas of rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appearance.
