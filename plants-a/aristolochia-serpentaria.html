Aristolochia serpentaria

Aristolochia serpentaria, commonly known as the Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, wooded areas. It is a member of the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its distinctive morphology.

However, it contains toxic compounds and is not recommended for cultivation due to its harmful effects on humans and animals.
