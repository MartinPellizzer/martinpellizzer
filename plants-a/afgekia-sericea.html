Afgekia sericea

Afgekia sericea, commonly known as the silver afgekia, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive and striking appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Afgekia sericea is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique aesthetic and hardy nature.
