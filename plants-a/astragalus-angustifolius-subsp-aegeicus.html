Astragalus angustifolius subsp. aegeicus

Astragalus angustifolius subsp.

aegeicus, commonly known as the Aegean milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a perennial herb with slender, erect stems and small, compound leaves.

The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring to early summer, typically in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
