Anagallis arvensis var. monelli

Anagallis arvensis var. monelli, commonly known as the redstem mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its hairy stems and small, fleshy leaves. The flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and a distinctive spurred shape. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
