Aralia japonica var. variegata

Aralia japonica var. variegata, commonly known as Japanese Aralia, is a striking ornamental plant native to Japan.

It features large, palmately compound leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings, adding visual interest to gardens. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in late summer, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in partial to full shade and is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its upright growth habit and attractive foliage make it a versatile addition to both indoor and outdoor spaces.
