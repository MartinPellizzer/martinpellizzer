Allium syriacum

Allium syriacum, commonly known as the Syrian onion, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features strap-like, grassy leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant is characterized by its bulbous base, which is used for culinary and medicinal purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun.

Allium syriacum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional medicine.
