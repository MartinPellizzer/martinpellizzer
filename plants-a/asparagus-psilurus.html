Asparagus psilurus

Asparagus psilurus, commonly known as the narrowleaf asparagus, is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, typical of the genus Asparagus. Its root system consists of fibrous roots, which help in anchoring the plant and absorbing nutrients from the soil.

Asparagus psilurus is native to southern Africa and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.
