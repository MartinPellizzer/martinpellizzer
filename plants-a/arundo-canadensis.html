Arundo canadensis

Arundo canadensis, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its stiff, hollow stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that can grow up to 10 feet in height. This plant thrives in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist environments, often forming dense stands. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it an invasive species in many regions.

Despite its aggressive growth, it is sometimes used for erosion control and as a source of biomass.
