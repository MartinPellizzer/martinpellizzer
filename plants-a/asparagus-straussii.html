Asparagus straussii

Asparagus straussii, commonly known as the Desert Asparagus, is a shrub-like plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, spiny stems that often grow close to the ground, adapting to harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, white to greenish flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are reduced to scales, and its berries are typically red or purple, containing tiny seeds.

Asparagus straussii is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
