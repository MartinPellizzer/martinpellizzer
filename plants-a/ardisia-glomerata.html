Ardisia glomerata

Ardisia glomerata, commonly known as the clustered beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. These fruits are an important food source for various birds and mammals.

Ardisia glomerata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy fruits.
