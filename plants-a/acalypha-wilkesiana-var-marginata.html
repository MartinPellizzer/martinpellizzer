Acalypha wilkesiana var. marginata

Acalypha wilkesiana var. marginata, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a flowering shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, papery bracts that resemble small lanterns, which are typically red, orange, or yellow in color. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy green leaves with a waxy texture and a slightly serrated margin. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that emerge from the base of the bracts.

This species thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage.
