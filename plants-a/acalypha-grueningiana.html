Acalypha grueningiana

Acalypha grueningiana, commonly known as the Hawaiian copperleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or copper-colored underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red, which bloom during the summer months. Its foliage is often used in traditional Hawaiian crafts, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Acalypha grueningiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
