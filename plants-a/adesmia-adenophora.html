Adesmia adenophora

Adesmia adenophora, commonly known as the hairy legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features small, clustered leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Its hairy stems and leaves are adaptations to reduce water loss in dry conditions.
