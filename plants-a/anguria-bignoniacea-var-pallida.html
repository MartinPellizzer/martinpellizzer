Anguria bignoniacea var. pallida

Anguria bignoniacea var. pallida, commonly known as the pale bignonia, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, pale green leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces long, slender fruits that mature to a pale yellow or white color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
