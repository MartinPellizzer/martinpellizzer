Angraecum imbricatum

Angraecum imbricatum, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous inflorescences bearing numerous fragrant, star-shaped flowers with white petals and a deep violet or purple labellum. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is a lithophytic species, often growing on rocky surfaces or in crevices.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
