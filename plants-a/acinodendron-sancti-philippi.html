Acinodendron sancti-philippi

Acinodendron sancti-philippi, commonly known as the Saint Philip's acacia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of Socotra.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces yellow flowers in clusters. It is adapted to the arid conditions of its native habitat, with deep roots and waxy foliage to reduce water loss.

Due to its unique morphology and restricted distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
