Aceras longibracteatum var. gallica

Aceras longibracteatum var. gallica, commonly known as the Galician bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features upright, branching stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers during the late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
