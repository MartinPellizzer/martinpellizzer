Acer platanoides f. plicatum

Acer platanoides f. plicatum, commonly known as the European horse chestnut, is a cultivar of the horse chestnut tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy white flowers in spring, which are followed by spiny, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This ornamental tree is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in landscaping.
