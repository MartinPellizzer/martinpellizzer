Andryala pinnatifida var. tolpicifolia

Andryala pinnatifida var. tolpicifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved andryala, is a variant of the Andryala pinnatifida species, which belongs to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, pinnatifid leaves that give it a feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often in shaded or partially shaded areas. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in umbels, and it produces small, round fruits.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
