Astragalus sofianus

Astragalus sofianus, commonly known as the Sofia milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, yellow to reddish-pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a deep taproot system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus sofianus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
