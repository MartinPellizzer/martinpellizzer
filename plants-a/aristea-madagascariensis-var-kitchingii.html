Aristea madagascariensis var. kitchingii

Aristea madagascariensis var. kitchingii, commonly known as the Kitching's aristea, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, rosette-like foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in a single head, often in shades of yellow or orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or arid landscapes.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to dry conditions.
