Acer creticum var. sublobatum

Acer creticum var. sublobatum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves with five to seven narrow lobes, which are typically bright green in summer and turn vibrant red or orange in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant for gardens and landscapes.

Due to its unique foliage, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in horticulture.
