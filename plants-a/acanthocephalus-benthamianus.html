Acanthocephalus benthamianus

Acanthocephalus benthamianus, commonly known as the Bentham's spinyhead, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, while its stems are often hairy and branched.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
