Allium illyricum

Allium illyricum, commonly known as the Balkan garlic or wild garlic, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and leeks, and is characterized by its bulbous stem and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or purple flowers arranged in a spherical cluster, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its underground bulb is edible and has a mild garlic flavor, often used in traditional cuisines.

Allium illyricum is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
