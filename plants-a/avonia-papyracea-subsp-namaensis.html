Avonia papyracea subsp. namaensis

Avonia papyracea subsp.

namaensis, commonly known as the Namibian paperplant, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, aiding in water retention in arid environments.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
