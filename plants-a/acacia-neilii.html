Acacia neilii

Acacia neilii, commonly known as the Neil's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow or golden flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Acacia neilii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a habitat for various desert wildlife.
