Aconitum metajaponicum var. iwatekense

Aconitum metajaponicum var. iwatekense, commonly known as the Iwate aconite, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to Japan, specifically the Iwate Prefecture, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense racemes. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, complex structure typical of the genus Aconitum.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
