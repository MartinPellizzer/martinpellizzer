Amalia fulva

Amalia fulva, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It features showy, yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or wetlands.

Amalia fulva is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
