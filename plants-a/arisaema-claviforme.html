Arisaema claviforme

Arisaema claviforme, commonly known as the club-shaped arisaema, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its unique, club-like spadix surrounded by a hooded spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually broad and arrow-shaped, with a prominent midrib.

Arisaema claviforme is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
