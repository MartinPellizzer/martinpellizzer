Ananas ananassoides var. typicus

Ananas ananassoides var. typicus, commonly known as the pineapple plant, is a tropical perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of sword-shaped leaves that form a central cup. The plant produces a flowering spike that eventually bears a fruit resembling a pineapple. Its leaves are covered in small, scale-like structures called trichomes, which help in water retention.

The plant is widely cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
