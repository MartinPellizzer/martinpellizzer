Astragalus peregrinus subsp. peregrinus

Astragalus peregrinus subsp.

peregrinus, commonly known as the wandering milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western North America. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has compound leaves with multiple pairs of oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
