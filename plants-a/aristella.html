Aristella

Aristella, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Aristella is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
