Aster indicus

Aster indicus, commonly known as the Indian aster, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands and marshes. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color and bloom in the summer months.

Aster indicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
