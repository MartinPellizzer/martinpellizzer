Acalypha nigritiana

Acalypha nigritiana, commonly known as the black nightshade or blackleaf croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful foliage, which can range from deep green to reddish or purple hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its attractive leaves.
