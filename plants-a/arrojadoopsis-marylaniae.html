Arrojadoopsis marylaniae

Arrojadoopsis marylaniae, commonly known as the Marlyania's arrojadoopsis, is a rare and unique plant species native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, spirally arranged leaves that exhibit a vibrant mix of green and silver hues. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring, adding to its ornamental appeal. Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered endangered and is often found in protected reserves.
