Axinanthera macrophylla

Axinanthera macrophylla, commonly known as the giant sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, strap-like leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found in mangrove ecosystems.

Axinanthera macrophylla plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
