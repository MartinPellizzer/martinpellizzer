Ananas sativus var. hispanorum

Ananas sativus var. hispanorum, commonly known as the Spanish pineapple, is a variant of the pineapple plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, sparsely leafed rosette and a fruit that is typically smaller and more fibrous than the common pineapple. The plant produces a single, edible fruit that is often used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and texture, making it a popular choice in certain regions for local consumption and cultivation.
