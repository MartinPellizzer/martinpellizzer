Acalypha subvillosa

Acalypha subvillosa, commonly known as velvetleaf or silky mallow, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a velvety texture, and its clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its stems are often prostrate or erect, and it can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

Acalypha subvillosa is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage.
