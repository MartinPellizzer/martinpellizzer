Acacia ligulata var. minor

Acacia ligulata var. minor, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and yellow flowers that appear in winter. The plant produces seed pods that mature into a hard, woody structure. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
