Astragalus michauxii

Astragalus michauxii, commonly known as Michaux's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as sagebrush steppe and open woodlands. It has a deep taproot system, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus michauxii is also known for its role in supporting local pollinators and contributing to soil stabilization in its native range.
