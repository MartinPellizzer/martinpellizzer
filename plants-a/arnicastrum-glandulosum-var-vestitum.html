Arnicastrum glandulosum var. vestitum

Arnicastrum glandulosum var. vestitum, commonly known as the velvet arnica, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, velvety appearance. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
