Amaranthus aragonensis

Amaranthus aragonensis, commonly known as the Armenian amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features erect, branching stems that are typically hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are small and clustered in dense panicles. The plant produces numerous seeds that are small, dry, and often wind-dispersed.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
