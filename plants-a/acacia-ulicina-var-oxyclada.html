Acacia ulicina var. oxyclada

Acacia ulicina var. oxyclada, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and narrow, phyllodes that are typically green and hairless. The plant produces small yellow flowers in dense, globular inflorescences, which appear during the warmer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in degraded landscapes.
