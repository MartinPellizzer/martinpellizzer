Allionia expansa

Allionia expansa, commonly known as the broadleaf allionia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are broad and ovate, often with a waxy coating, and stems that are erect and branched. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a preference for moist, well-drained soils.

Allionia expansa is often found in woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
