Anamirta flavescens

Anamirta flavescens, commonly known as the yellow water spinach, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface and bright yellow veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near streams or in humid forests.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and adaptable species within its native habitat.
