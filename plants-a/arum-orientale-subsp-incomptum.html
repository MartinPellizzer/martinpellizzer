Arum orientale subsp. incomptum

Arum orientale subsp.

incomptum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia. It features arrow-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and acuminate, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a spathe, a characteristic feature of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for aquatic gardens and bog landscapes.
