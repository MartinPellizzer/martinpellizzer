Alocasia gibsonii

Alocasia gibsonii, commonly known as the Gibson's elephant ear, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in diameter, often with a deep green color and a velvety texture. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique inflorescence structure consisting of a spadix and spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Alocasia gibsonii is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
