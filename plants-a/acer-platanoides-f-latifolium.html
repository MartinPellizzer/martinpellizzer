Acer platanoides f. latifolium

Acer platanoides f. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf Norway maple, is a cultivar of the Norway maple tree.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically 10 to 15 centimeters in diameter. The leaves are usually dark green on the upper surface and lighter green underneath, with a smooth margin. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping.

It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras that disperse its seeds.
