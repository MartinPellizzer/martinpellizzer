Abutilon oxyphyllum

Abutilon oxyphyllum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Abutilon oxyphyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
