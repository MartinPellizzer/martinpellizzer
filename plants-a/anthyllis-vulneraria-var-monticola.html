Anthyllis vulneraria var. monticola

Anthyllis vulneraria var. monticola, commonly known as the alpine anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is adapted to harsh climatic conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude ecosystems.
