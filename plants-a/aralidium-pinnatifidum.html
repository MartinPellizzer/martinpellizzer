Aralidium pinnatifidum

Aralidium pinnatifidum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aralid, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence.

It features a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of many arum plants. The plant is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a slightly serrated edge.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and unique floral structure.
