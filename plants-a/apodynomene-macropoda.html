Apodynomene macropoda

Apodynomene macropoda, commonly known as the giant sago palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach several meters in diameter. The plant has a robust trunk that is covered in a fibrous, spongy material. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red berries.

Apodynomene macropoda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
