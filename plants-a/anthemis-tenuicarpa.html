Anthemis tenuicarpa

Anthemis tenuicarpa, commonly known as narrow-fruit everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
