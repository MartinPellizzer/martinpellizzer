Artemisia mongolorum

Artemisia mongolorum, commonly known as Mongolian wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the cold regions of northern China.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small yellow flowers that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments. It is often found in mountainous areas and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its essential oils contain compounds with potential antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.
