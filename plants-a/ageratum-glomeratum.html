Ageratum glomeratum

Ageratum glomeratum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Ageratum glomeratum thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant color and ability to attract pollinators.
