Argyrolobium involucratum

Argyrolobium involucratum, commonly known as the silver globular pea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spherical, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, flat, lens-shaped pods that contain one or two seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
