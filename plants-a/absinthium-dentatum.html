Absinthium dentatum

Absinthium dentatum, commonly known as common wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that are often hairy, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that have serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, which bloom in late summer. Its aromatic foliage contains volatile oils, including thujone, which contribute to its distinctive bitter taste.

Absinthium dentatum is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine and as a component in absinthe.
