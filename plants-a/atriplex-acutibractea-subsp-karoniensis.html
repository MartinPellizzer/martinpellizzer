Atriplex acutibractea subsp. karoniensis

Atriplex acutibractea subsp.

karoniensis, commonly known as the Karoi saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its salt-tolerant properties, allowing it to thrive in saline soils.

The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically greenish-yellow in color.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
