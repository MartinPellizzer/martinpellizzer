Anthurium hylophilum

Anthurium hylophilum, commonly known as the velvet leaf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a velvety texture. The plant produces striking spadices, which are the modified leaves that enclose the small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium hylophilum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
