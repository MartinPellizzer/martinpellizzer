Arisaema sugimotoi

Arisaema sugimotoi, commonly known as the Japanese Cuckoo Plant, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence structure.

It features a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe, which is typically green with a purple or red base. The plant is native to Japan and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant produces berries that are dispersed by animals.

Arisaema sugimotoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and floral display.
