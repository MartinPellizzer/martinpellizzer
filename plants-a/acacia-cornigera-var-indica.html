Acacia cornigera var. indica

Acacia cornigera var. indica, commonly known as the Indian acacia or the thorn acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces yellow, fragrant flowers that are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its seeds are encased in a pod and are sometimes used as a source of gum arabic.

Acacia cornigera var. indica thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, making it a common species in dry regions of India and neighboring countries.
