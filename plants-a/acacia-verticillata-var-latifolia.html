Acacia verticillata var. latifolia

Acacia verticillata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a whorl. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
