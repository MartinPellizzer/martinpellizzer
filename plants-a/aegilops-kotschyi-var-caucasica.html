Aegilops kotschyi var. caucasica

Aegilops kotschyi var. caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian wild barley, is a wild grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and spikelets that bear small, awned grains. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has been studied for its potential in agricultural improvement due to its genetic diversity and resilience.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of wild relatives of cultivated cereals.
