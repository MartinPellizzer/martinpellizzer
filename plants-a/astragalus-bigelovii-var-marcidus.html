Astragalus bigelovii var. marcidus

Astragalus bigelovii var. marcidus, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Its flowers are usually pale purple or pink, and it produces small, hard pods that contain seeds.
