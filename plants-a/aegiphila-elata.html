Aegiphila elata

Aegiphila elata, commonly known as the silky aegiphila, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region, particularly found in parts of Cuba and the Bahamas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are typically small and clustered in whorls, displaying shades of pink to purple.

Aegiphila elata is often found in moist, sandy soils near coastal areas and is adapted to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
