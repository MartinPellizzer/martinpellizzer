Acacia kekapur

Acacia kekapur, commonly known as the black wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and compound leaves with multiple pairs of leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by woody pods containing seeds. Acacia kekapur is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems and has been used by Indigenous Australians for various purposes.
