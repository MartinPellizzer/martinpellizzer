Arachis hypogaea f. typica

Arachis hypogaea f. typica, commonly known as the peanut.

This variety is a flowering plant native to South America, characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. It produces yellow flowers that develop into pods containing edible seeds, known as peanuts. The plant is notable for its unique trait of pegging, where the flower stem bends downward to form the pod underground.

It thrives in warm climates and is cultivated for its high nutritional value and oil content.
