Adenocarpus divaricatus var. graecus

Adenocarpus divaricatus var. graecus, commonly known as the Greek sicklepod, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its small, yellow flowers appear in clusters and are followed by elongated, ribbed seed pods.
