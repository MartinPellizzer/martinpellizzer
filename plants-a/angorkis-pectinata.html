Angorkis pectinata

Angorkis pectinata, commonly known as the silky antelope's foot, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a basal rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces tall, branched stems with clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range from white to pale pink. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
