Amyris cordata

Amyris cordata, commonly known as the fragrant cedar or sweet cedar, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, fragrant yellow flowers that appear in clusters. Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Amyris cordata is also valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional remedies.
