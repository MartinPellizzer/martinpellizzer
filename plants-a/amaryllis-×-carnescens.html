Amaryllis × carnescens

Amaryllis × carnescens, commonly known as the red amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis spp.

It is characterized by its striking, large, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to pink and white. The plant produces thick, fleshy leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a rosette pattern. It is native to South America and thrives in warm, sunny environments with well-draining soil.

This species is popular as a flowering houseplant due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
