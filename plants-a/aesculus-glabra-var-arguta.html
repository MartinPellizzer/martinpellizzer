Aesculus glabra var. arguta

Aesculus glabra var. arguta, commonly known as the Canadian buckeye, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets. The tree produces showy, yellowish-green flowers clustered in upright panicles, followed by spiny, greenish-brown fruits containing one to three seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks.

The plant is also known for its ornamental value and historical significance in indigenous cultures.
