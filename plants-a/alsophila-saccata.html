Alsophila saccata

Alsophila saccata, commonly known as the saddleback tree fern, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, light green fronds. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are clustered in distinctive, saddle-shaped structures. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Alsophila saccata plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various small organisms.
