Asperula spinosa

Asperula spinosa, commonly known as the prickly woodrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, spiny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Asperula spinosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
