Acacia atrox

Acacia atrox, commonly known as the black acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, thorny stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in cylindrical spikes, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are adapted for dispersal by animals.

Acacia atrox is notable for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.
