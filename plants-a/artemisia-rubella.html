Artemisia rubella

Artemisia rubella, commonly known as sticky wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silver-gray foliage with tiny, branched hairs that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Artemisia rubella is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
