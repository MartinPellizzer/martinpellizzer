Acinodendron theiforme

Acinodendron theiforme, commonly known as the threadleaf acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Acinodendron theiforme thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important component of local ecosystems.
