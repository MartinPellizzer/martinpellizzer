Allamanda verticillata

Allamanda verticillata, commonly known as the yellow allamanda, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Allamanda verticillata is also known for its rapid growth and ability to produce abundant blooms throughout the growing season.
