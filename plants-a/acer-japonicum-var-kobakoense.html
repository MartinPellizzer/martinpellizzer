Acer japonicum var. kobakoense

Acer japonicum var. kobakoense, commonly known as the Kobako maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange foliage that displays vivid colors in the fall. The leaves are typically palmately lobed with serrated edges, and they turn vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow during autumn. The tree produces small, clustered flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and parks.
