Aspidium xanthomelas

Aspidium xanthomelas, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed water fern, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are often covered in a fine layer of spongy tissue, which helps it absorb nutrients from the water. The plant typically grows in slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and streams, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. Its spores are released into the water, allowing for widespread propagation.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
