Astragalus fremontii

Astragalus fremontii, commonly known as the Fremont milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. It has a deep taproot system that helps it survive in arid environments.

Astragalus fremontii is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil.
