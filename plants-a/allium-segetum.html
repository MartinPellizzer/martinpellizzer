Allium segetum

Allium segetum, commonly known as field garlic, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pink flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Allium segetum is known for its strong garlic scent and potential medicinal properties.
