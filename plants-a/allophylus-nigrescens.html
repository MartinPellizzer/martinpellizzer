Allophylus nigrescens

Allophylus nigrescens, commonly known as black alder, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its small, clustered flowers are usually green or yellowish, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are dark when mature.

Allophylus nigrescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
