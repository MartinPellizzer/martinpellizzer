Arundinaria paniculata f. nebulosa

Arundinaria paniculata f. nebulosa, commonly known as the clouded bamboo, is a variant of the giant bamboo species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its striking, bluish-gray culms that are often covered in a fine, powdery bloom, giving it a misty appearance. The plant produces panicles of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are borne on long, drooping branches. Its foliage is dense and grass-like, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

This ornamental bamboo is prized for its unique visual appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
