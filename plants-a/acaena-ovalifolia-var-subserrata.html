Acaena ovalifolia var. subserrata

Acaena ovalifolia var. subserrata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burr bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically covered in fine, short hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by spiny, woody fruits known as achenes. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
