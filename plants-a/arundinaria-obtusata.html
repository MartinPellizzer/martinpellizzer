Arundinaria obtusata

Arundinaria obtusata, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a grass species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated.

Arundinaria obtusata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping and traditional Chinese medicine.
