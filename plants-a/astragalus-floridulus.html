Astragalus floridulus

Astragalus floridulus, commonly known as Florida milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or disturbed soils and is adapted to warm, humid climates. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native insects.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across open habitats.
