Aquilegia canadensis subsp. americana

Aquilegia canadensis subsp.

americana, commonly known as the American columbine, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America. It features showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has compound leaves with lobed divisions and produces follicles containing seeds with tufts of silky hair. It thrives in woodland habitats and prefers partial shade with moist, well-drained soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valued addition to native plant gardens and ecological landscapes.
