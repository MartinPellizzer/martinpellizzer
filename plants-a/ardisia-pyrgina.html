Ardisia pyrgina

Ardisia pyrgina, commonly known as the dwarf beautyberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species. Ardisia pyrgina thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes in tropical and subtropical regions.
