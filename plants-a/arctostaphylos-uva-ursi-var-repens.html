Arctostaphylos uva-ursi var. repens

Arctostaphylos uva-ursi var. repens, commonly known as creeping bearberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to North America.

It features small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces red or orange berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and is commonly used in native plant landscaping.
