Arceuthobium bicarinatum

Arceuthobium bicarinatum, commonly known as the Indian broomrape, is a holoparasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly impact the growth and health of its host plants.
