Acer heldreichii subsp. visianii

Acer heldreichii subsp.

visianii, commonly known as the Visianii maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, which turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers cooler, moist environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
