Acalypha coryloides

Acalypha coryloides, commonly known as the Chinese iron plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous true flowers surrounded by these showy bracts. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Acalypha coryloides prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
