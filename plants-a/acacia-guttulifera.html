Acacia guttulifera

Acacia guttulifera, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and compound leaves that are typically bipinnate. The plant produces yellow to golden-green flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which bloom during the dry season. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods, and the plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia guttulifera is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and fuelwood.
