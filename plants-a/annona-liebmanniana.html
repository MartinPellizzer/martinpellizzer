Annona liebmanniana

Annona liebmanniana, commonly known as the Brazilian custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces a fragrant, yellowish fruit with a rough, spiny exterior. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a smooth margin.

The flowers are actinomorphic, with white or pale green petals and numerous stamens.
