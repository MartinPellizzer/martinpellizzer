Aristolochia tuberosa

Aristolochia tuberosa, commonly known as the tuberous Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often greenish or brownish in color. The plant produces tuberous roots that are sometimes used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length.

The species is known for its aromatic foliage and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
