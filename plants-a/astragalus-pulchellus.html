Astragalus pulchellus

Astragalus pulchellus, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, yellowish to reddish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus pulchellus is a perennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
