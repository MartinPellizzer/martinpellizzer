Ageratum pohlii

Ageratum pohlii, commonly known as the Mexican blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of blue to purple flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. Ageratum pohlii thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

It is also valued in horticulture for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
