Arabis furcata var. olympica

Arabis furcata var. olympica, commonly known as the Olympic rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, clustered flowers and lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers, which are usually white or pale yellow, bloom in early spring.

The variety 'olympica' is distinguished by its compact growth habit and specific geographic distribution.
