Alternanthera articulata

Alternanthera articulata, commonly known as the red-veined sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red-veined leaves that contrast with its bright green foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pink flowers that appear in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Alternanthera articulata is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive leaves and easy care requirements.
