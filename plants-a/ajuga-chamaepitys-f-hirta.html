Ajuga chamaepitys f. hirta

Ajuga chamaepitys f. hirta, commonly known as hairy woodruff, is a flowering plant belonging to the plantain family (Plantaginaceae).

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a hairy texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
