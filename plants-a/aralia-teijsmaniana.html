Aralia teijsmaniana

Aralia teijsmaniana, commonly known as the Java aralia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring compound leaves with a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for humans and birds. Its roots are sometimes used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Aralia teijsmaniana thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.
