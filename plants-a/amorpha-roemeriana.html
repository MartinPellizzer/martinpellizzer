Amorpha roemeriana

Amorpha roemeriana, commonly known as the yellow false indigo, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or bean family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Amorpha roemeriana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
