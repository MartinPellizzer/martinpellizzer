Aegilops variabilis var. brachyathera

Aegilops variabilis var. brachyathera, commonly known as the short awn wild rye, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in dry, disturbed soils. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with short awns, which are specialized bristle-like structures. It is often found in open habitats such as roadsides, fields, and disturbed areas.

This species is of interest in agricultural research due to its potential for use in wheat improvement programs.
