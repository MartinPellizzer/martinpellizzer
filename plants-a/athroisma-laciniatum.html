Athroisma laciniatum

Athroisma laciniatum, commonly known as the laceleaf saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided leaves that give it a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its unique foliage, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
