Alophia caerulea

Alophia caerulea, commonly known as the blue alophia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is widely cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
