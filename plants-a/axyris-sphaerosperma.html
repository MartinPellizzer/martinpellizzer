Axyris sphaerosperma

Axyris sphaerosperma, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical-shaped seed heads that resemble a globe, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

Axyris sphaerosperma is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
