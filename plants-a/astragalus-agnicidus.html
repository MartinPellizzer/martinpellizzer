Astragalus agnicidus

Astragalus agnicidus, commonly known as the fire-killer milkvetch, is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, Mongolia, and Russia. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces small, lens-shaped seeds.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
