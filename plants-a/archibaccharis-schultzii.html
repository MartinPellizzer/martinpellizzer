Archibaccharis schultzii

Archibaccharis schultzii, commonly known as the Schultze's baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically producing yellow or white blooms that attract various pollinators.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as savannas and coastal areas.
