Ampelygonum

Ampelygonum, commonly known as the climbing pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. The plant has compound leaves with a glossy, dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its stems are woody and often have a reddish hue, which helps in identifying the species.

Ampelygonum is known for its vigorous climbing habit and is often used in ornamental landscaping.
