Aster medius

Aster medius, commonly known as the middle aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers. The plant features showy, daisy-like flowers with purple to pink petals and yellow centers, blooming in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand harsh alpine conditions.

Aster medius is often found in rocky slopes and meadows, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
