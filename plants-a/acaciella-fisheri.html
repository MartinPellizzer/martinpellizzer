Acaciella fisheri

Acaciella fisheri, commonly known as the Fisher's acaciella, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume-like flowers and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, often blooming during the rainy season. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, feather-like leaflets.

Acaciella fisheri is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep root systems that help it access groundwater.
