Acalypha purpurascens var. eglandulosa

Acalypha purpurascens var. eglandulosa, commonly known as the purple false heather, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features striking, velvety purple foliage that adds vibrant color to gardens. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscape design in warm climates.
