Antirrhinum meonanthum subsp. onubensis

Antirrhinum meonanthum subsp.

onubensis, commonly known as the Onubian snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to southern Spain. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tubular, brightly colored flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
