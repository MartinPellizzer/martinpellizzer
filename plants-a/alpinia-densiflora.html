Alpinia densiflora

Alpinia densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Alpinia densiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
