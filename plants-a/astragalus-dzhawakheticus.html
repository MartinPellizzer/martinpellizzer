Astragalus dzhawakheticus

Astragalus dzhawakheticus, commonly known as the Dzhawakhetic milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, typical of the genus Astragalus. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and rocky soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
