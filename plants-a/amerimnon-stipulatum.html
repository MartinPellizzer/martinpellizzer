Amerimnon stipulatum

Amerimnon stipulatum, commonly known as the slender leafed fern, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, thriving in the understory of dense forests. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, relying on wind pollination for reproduction.

The botanical features of Amerimnon stipulatum make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
