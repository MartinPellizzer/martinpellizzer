Arctostaphylos tomentosa subsp. daciticola

Arctostaphylos tomentosa subsp.

daciticola, commonly known as the dwarf manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white foliage and small, evergreen leaves.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in white or pink during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to rocky or coastal environments.

Its botanical features make it a hardy, drought-tolerant species suitable for xeriscaping.
