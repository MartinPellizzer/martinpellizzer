Asperula kovalevskiana

Asperula kovalevskiana, commonly known as the Kovalevskaya azalea, is a flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing a fragrant display that attracts pollinators. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Asperula kovalevskiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability in temperate climates.
