Aspidium melanochlamys

Aspidium melanochlamys, commonly known as the black-leaved water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy fronds that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its spores are produced on the underside of its fronds in clusters called sori.

Aspidium melanochlamys is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
