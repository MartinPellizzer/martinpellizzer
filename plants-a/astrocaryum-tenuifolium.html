Astrocaryum tenuifolium

Astrocaryum tenuifolium, commonly known as buriti, is a palm native to the Amazon rainforest of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces yellowish-orange fruits that contain a nutrient-rich pulp and seeds. The leaves are often used in traditional medicine and for making thatch and rope.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for wildlife and supporting local communities through sustainable harvesting.
