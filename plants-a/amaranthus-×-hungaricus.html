Amaranthus × hungaricus

Amaranthus × hungaricus, commonly known as Hungarian amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and production of dense, branched inflorescences that bear numerous small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has alternate, lanceolate leaves with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are usually greenish-white to reddish in color, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The seeds are small, hard, and often used as a grain in traditional cuisines.
