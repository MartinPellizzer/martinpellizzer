Acacia etilis

Acacia etilis, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow globular flower heads that appear in spring. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

The silver wattle is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
