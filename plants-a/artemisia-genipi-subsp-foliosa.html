Artemisia genipi subsp. foliosa

Artemisia genipi subsp.

foliosa, commonly known as the Mexican tarragon, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive scent and have been used in traditional medicine.

This subspecies is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and as a source of aromatic compounds.
