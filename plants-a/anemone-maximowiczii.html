Anemone maximowiczii

Anemone maximowiczii, commonly known as the Korean anemone, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Korea and eastern Asia.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often hairy and covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Anemone maximowiczii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
