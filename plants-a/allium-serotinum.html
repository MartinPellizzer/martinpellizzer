Allium serotinum

Allium serotinum, commonly known as the late garlic or winter garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its bulbs are typically harvested in the fall and are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

Allium serotinum thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
