Anemopaegma leucanthum

Anemopaegma leucanthum, commonly known as the white anemone, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Anemopaegma leucanthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
