Aster laetus

Aster laetus, commonly known as the showy aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its showy, violet to blue-purple flowers bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators like butterflies and bees.

Aster laetus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
