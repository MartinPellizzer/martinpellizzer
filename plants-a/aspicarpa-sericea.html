Aspicarpa sericea

Aspicarpa sericea, commonly known as the silky aspicarpa, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters. The plant has narrow, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Aspicarpa sericea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
