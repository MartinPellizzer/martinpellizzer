Ampelocalamus sinovietnamensis

Ampelocalamus sinovietnamensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam, and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization.

Ampelocalamus sinovietnamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in maintaining biodiversity in its native habitat.
