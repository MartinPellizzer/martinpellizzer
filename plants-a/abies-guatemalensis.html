Abies guatemalensis

Abies guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender form and needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in spirals. The tree produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind, aiding in its natural propagation. It thrives in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates, often found in cloud forests.

The species is valued for its timber and plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
