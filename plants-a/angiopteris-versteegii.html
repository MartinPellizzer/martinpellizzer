Angiopteris versteegii

Angiopteris versteegii, commonly known as the Versteeg's water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, intricate fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in dense forest undergrowth. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Angiopteris versteegii plays an important ecological role in its habitat by contributing to soil stability and providing shelter for various small organisms.
