Anguria pachyphylla

Anguria pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved cucumber, is a unique plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to its harsh environment. The plant produces small, edible fruits similar to cucumbers, which are often used in local cuisines. Its succulent structure allows it to thrive in low-water conditions, making it a resilient species.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, sharing traits with other gourd plants.
