Aster cunninghamii

Aster cunninghamii, commonly known as the Cunningham's aster, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. It produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring and summer.

Aster cunninghamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
