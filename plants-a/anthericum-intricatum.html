Anthericum intricatum

Anthericum intricatum, commonly known as the intricate lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing white or pale pink flowers that are often tinged with green. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

Anthericum intricatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
