Artocarpus parviflorus

Artocarpus parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered inflorescences and rough, fissured bark. The tree produces a fruit that is typically smaller and less fleshy compared to other breadfruit species. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a distinctive heart-shaped base.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
