Allophylus aporeticus

Allophylus aporeticus, commonly known as the African snowberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers and red, berry-like fruits that persist through the winter. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces a milky sap when damaged. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Allophylus aporeticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
