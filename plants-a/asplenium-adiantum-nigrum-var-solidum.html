Asplenium adiantum-nigrum var. solidum

Asplenium adiantum-nigrum var. solidum, commonly known as the black maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, dark green fronds that are often glossy and have a delicate, feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest floors or along stream banks. Its spores are produced in small, clustered structures called sorus, which are typically located on the underside of the fronds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in humid indoor environments.
