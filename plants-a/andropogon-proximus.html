Andropogon proximus

Andropogon proximus, commonly known as little bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted inflorescence, which becomes more visible in the fall. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. It plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.

Andropogon proximus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in native plant landscaping.
