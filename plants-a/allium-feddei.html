Allium feddei

Allium feddei, commonly known as the Fedde's onion, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its robust, fleshy bulbs and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces umbels of white or pale pink flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, garlic-like flavor, making it a potential candidate for culinary use.

Allium feddei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
