Acantholimon armenum var. balansae

Acantholimon armenum var. balansae, commonly known as Armenian thimbleweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and Turkey.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae, which includes flowering plants characterized by their showy flowers and often succulent leaves. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, typically pink or purple, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to alpine and subalpine environments.
