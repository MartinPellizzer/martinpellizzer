Arrhenatherum palaestinum subsp. macedonicum

Arrhenatherum palaestinum subsp.

macedonicum, commonly known as Macedonian feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Balkan region. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The plant has slender, upright culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
