Aristida beyrichiana

Aristida beyrichiana, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
