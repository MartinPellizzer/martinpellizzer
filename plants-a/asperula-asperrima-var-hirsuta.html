Asperula asperrima var. hirsuta

Asperula asperrima var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy woodruff, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Europe.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has hairy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and wildlife habitats.
