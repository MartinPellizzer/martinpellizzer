Argemone mexicana var. anacanthoidea

Argemone mexicana var. anacanthoidea, commonly known as the Mexican prickly poppy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and spiny, hairy stems. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
