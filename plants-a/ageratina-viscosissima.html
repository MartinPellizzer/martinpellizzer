Ageratina viscosissima

Ageratina viscosissima, commonly known as sticky ageratum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which often exude a sticky substance. The plant produces small, fluffy blue or purple flower clusters that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its unique appearance and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable addition to ecological and horticultural settings.
