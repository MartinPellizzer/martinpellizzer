Acacia dorsenna

Acacia dorsenna, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and small, phyllodes that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which appear during the warmer months. Acacia dorsenna is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and efficient water use.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various native animals and insects.
