Aletris capensis

Aletris capensis, commonly known as the Cape aleroot, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its tufted, grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a spike, typically blooming in late spring. Aletris capensis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
