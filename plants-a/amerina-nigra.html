Amerina nigra

Amerina nigra, commonly known as black American nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

Amerina nigra is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
