Acacia delibrata

Acacia delibrata, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow globular flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is well adapted to dry and arid environments, making it a hardy species. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are dispersed by animals and wind.

Acacia delibrata plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
