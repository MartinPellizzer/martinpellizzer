Arundo hexandra

Arundo hexandra, commonly known as the giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense, cylindrical clusters during the summer months. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found near rivers, lakes, and marshes.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands, it is both a valuable ecological resource and a potential invasive species in non-native regions.
