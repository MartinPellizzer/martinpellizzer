Acacia tortilis var. spirocarpa

Acacia tortilis var. spirocarpa, commonly known as the spiral acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of branches, which gives it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers and flat, bean-like pods that contain seeds. It has small, leathery phyllodes that help reduce water loss in dry environments.

Acacia tortilis var. spirocarpa is adapted to survive in harsh conditions with minimal water availability.
