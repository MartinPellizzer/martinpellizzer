Amaranthus cuspidifolius

Amaranthus cuspidifolius, commonly known as the spike amaranth, is an annual herb native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces dense clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that mature into spiny, triangular seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environments.

Due to its ornamental value and nutritional content, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and edible purposes.
