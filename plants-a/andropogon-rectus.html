Andropogon rectus

Andropogon rectus, commonly known as tallgrass bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common component of native prairie ecosystems.

Andropogon rectus plays a vital role in soil stabilization and provides habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
