Achyranthes argentea var. viridescens

Achyranthes argentea var. viridescens, commonly known as silverleaf achyranthes, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features erect, branched stems that are often covered with a silvery-white pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and they often have a bluish or silver-gray hue.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
