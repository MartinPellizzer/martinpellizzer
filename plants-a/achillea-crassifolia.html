Achillea crassifolia

Achillea crassifolia, commonly known as broadleaf yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal rosettes of large, broad, gray-green leaves that are rough to the touch. The plant produces flat-topped clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its historical use in medicine and as a symbol of love and devotion adds to its cultural significance.
