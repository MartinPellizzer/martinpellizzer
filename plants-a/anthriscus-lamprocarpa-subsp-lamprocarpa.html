Anthriscus lamprocarpa subsp. lamprocarpa

Anthriscus lamprocarpa subsp.

lamprocarpa, commonly known as the silverleaf cow parsley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are silver-haired beneath, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters, typical of the Apiaceae family. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream sides.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
