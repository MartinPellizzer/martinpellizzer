Allium botschantzevii

Allium botschantzevii, commonly known as the Botchanzhev's onion, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous root system and grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, spherical, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated by gardeners interested in rare and exotic plants.
