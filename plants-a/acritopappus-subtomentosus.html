Acritopappus subtomentosus

Acritopappus subtomentosus, commonly known as the silky pappus plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, inconspicuous flowers and a distinctive, silky pappus that aids in wind dispersal of its seeds. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually hairy and covered with fine, soft hairs, contributing to its overall silvery appearance.

It is often found in the western United States, particularly in California and Nevada.
