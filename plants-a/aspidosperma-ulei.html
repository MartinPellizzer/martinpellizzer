Aspidosperma ulei

Aspidosperma ulei, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Cerbera ulei*, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries containing toxic seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in lowland and mountainous areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in various parts of the world.
