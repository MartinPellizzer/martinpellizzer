Aspidistra fungilliformis subsp. formosa

Aspidistra fungilliformis subsp.

formosa, commonly known as the Formosa aspidistra, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dark green, elongated, and leathery leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are typically hidden beneath a leaf-like spathe. It thrives in shady, humid environments and prefers well-draining, rich soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate low light conditions.
