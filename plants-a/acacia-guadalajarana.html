Acacia guadalajarana

Acacia guadalajarana, commonly known as the Guadalajara acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Its bark and leaves have been used traditionally for various purposes, including medicinal applications.
