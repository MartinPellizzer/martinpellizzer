Aconitum lycoctonum lusus hirsutum

Aconitum lycoctonum lusus hirsutum, commonly known as the hirsute monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant features large, palmately compound leaves with a hairy texture, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
