Aspidosperma dasycarpon

Aspidosperma dasycarpon, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Ceratonia siliqua* in some regions, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lanceolate, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to red, fleshy berries containing several seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential pharmacological properties.
