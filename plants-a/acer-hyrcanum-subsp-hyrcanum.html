Acer hyrcanum subsp. hyrcanum

Acer hyrcanum subsp.

hyrcanum, commonly known as the Hyrcanian maple, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia. It typically grows in mountainous regions and is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves with five to seven lobes.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds called samaras. Its bark is smooth and greyish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
