Aframomum cuspidatum

Aframomum cuspidatum, commonly known as African grain of paradise, is a tropical plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. Its rhizomes are the primary part used, containing aromatic compounds that contribute to its distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.

It is often cultivated for its culinary and traditional medicinal uses.
