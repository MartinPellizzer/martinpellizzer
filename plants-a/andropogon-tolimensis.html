Andropogon tolimensis

Andropogon tolimensis, commonly known as the Tolima grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central America, particularly Colombia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
