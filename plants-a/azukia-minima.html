Azukia minima

Azukia minima, commonly known as the dwarf azuki bean, is a small annual legume native to East Asia.

It produces tiny, spherical seeds that are often used in traditional Asian cuisines. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few inches, making it a compact and ornamental species. Its leaves are trifoliate, with small, rounded leaflets, and it flowers with small, pale purple blooms.

Azukia minima is valued for its nutritional content and is often cultivated in home gardens for both culinary and decorative purposes.
