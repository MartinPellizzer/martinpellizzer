Anthericum praetermissum

Anthericum praetermissum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features a distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical structure is adapted for efficient pollination by specific insect species.
