Andropogon sorghum var. coimbatoricus

Andropogon sorghum var. coimbatoricus, commonly known as Coimbatoric Sorghum, is a variant of sorghum characterized by its unique botanical features.

It is a tall, perennial grass native to certain regions of South Asia, particularly India. The plant exhibits robust stalks with a distinctive reddish hue, and its leaves are broad and serrated. It produces panicles of small, two-lipped flowers that are typically pale green to golden in color.

This variety is valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its potential use in both ornamental and agricultural contexts.
