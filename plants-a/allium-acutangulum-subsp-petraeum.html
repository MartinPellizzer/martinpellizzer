Allium acutangulum subsp. petraeum

Allium acutangulum subsp.

petraeum, commonly known as the rock garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and arid environments in the Mediterranean region. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers.

The plant is characterized by its bulbous roots, which store nutrients and help it survive in harsh conditions. It is often found growing in crevices and on rocky outcrops, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical structure and adaptability make it a resilient species in its native habitat.
