Azalea laetitiae

Azalea laetitiae, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rhododendron genus.

It is native to East Asia, particularly Japan, and thrives in temperate climates with cool, moist conditions. The plant produces showy clusters of pink or white flowers in the spring, making it a popular ornamental species. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

Its botanical features include evergreen foliage and a compact, shrub-like growth habit.
