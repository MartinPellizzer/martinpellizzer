Amitostigma capitatum

Amitostigma capitatum, commonly known as the Sierra lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or open woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette.
