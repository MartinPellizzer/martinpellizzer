Alloplectus affinis

Alloplectus affinis, commonly known as the common marsh hibiscus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red. It blooms from late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Alloplectus affinis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
