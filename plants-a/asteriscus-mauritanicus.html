Asteriscus mauritanicus

Asteriscus mauritanicus, commonly known as the Moroccan aster, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late summer and autumn.

The leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, with a fuzzy texture that helps reduce water loss.
