Argyrolobium woodii

Argyrolobium woodii, commonly known as the white globular vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive spherical seed pods and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
