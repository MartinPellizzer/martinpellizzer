Acer amoenum

Acer amoenum, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its ornamental foliage, which displays vibrant colors of red, orange, and yellow during the autumn season. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 meters, with a broad, spreading canopy. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with finely serrated edges, and the tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring.

The bark is smooth and reddish-brown, becoming more fissured with age.
