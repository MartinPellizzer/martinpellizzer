Agave platyphylla

Agave platyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It features a short, cylindrical stem and produces rosettes of fleshy, narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in a white powdery coating. The plant is well adapted to dry climates, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves. It produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring or early summer.

Agave platyphylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
