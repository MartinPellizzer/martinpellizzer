Arabis brassica

Arabis brassica, commonly known as wallflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, four-petaled flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

Arabis brassica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
