Atropis scabrella

Atropis scabrella, commonly known as the roughleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, rough-textured leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near streams or in dense undergrowth.

Atropis scabrella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
