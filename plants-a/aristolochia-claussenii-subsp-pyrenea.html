Aristolochia claussenii subsp. pyrenea

Aristolochia claussenii subsp.

pyrenea, commonly known as the pyrenea Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often found in moist, shaded habitats. It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its aromatic foliage.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller, more compact growth compared to other members of the species.
