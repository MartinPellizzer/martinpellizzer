Alaternus phylica

Alaternus phylica, commonly known as the common thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, needle-like leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its high content of essential oils and phenolic compounds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy and adaptable species.

Alaternus phylica is widely used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its distinctive flavor and therapeutic benefits.
