Alkanna tuberculata

Alkanna tuberculata, commonly known as the Indian madder, is a flowering plant native to South Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a bright red to orange dye from its roots, which has been historically used in textiles and cosmetics. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Alkanna tuberculata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
