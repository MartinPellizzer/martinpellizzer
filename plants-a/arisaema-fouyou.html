Arisaema fouyou

Arisaema fouyou, commonly known as the Japanese arrowroot, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to East Asia, particularly Japan, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features a stout, rhizomatous rootstock with large, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated. Its striking inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

The plant is cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
