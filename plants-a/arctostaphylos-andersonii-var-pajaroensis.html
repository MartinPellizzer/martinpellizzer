Arctostaphylos andersonii var. pajaroensis

Arctostaphylos andersonii var. pajaroensis, commonly known as the Pajaro manzanita, is a shrub native to California.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, leathery berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of the region.

This variety is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.
