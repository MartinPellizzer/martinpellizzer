Arabis gracilis

Arabis gracilis, commonly known as slender rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other well-known flowering plants. This plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments. Arabis gracilis produces small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, contributing to its delicate appearance.
