Aconitum jaluense subsp. taigicola

Aconitum jaluense subsp.

taigicola, commonly known as the Siberian monkshood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy blue or violet flowers.

The plant features palmately divided leaves with a waxy surface, typical of the genus Aconitum. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions of eastern Siberia.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is handled with care and is not commonly cultivated.
