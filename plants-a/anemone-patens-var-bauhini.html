Anemone patens var. bauhini

Anemone patens var. bauhini, commonly known as the spreading anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and produces basal leaves that are heart-shaped with a hairy texture. Its flowers are typically arranged in a single cluster at the top of the stem.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
