Astragalus baibutensis

Astragalus baibutensis, commonly known as the Baibut Astragalus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
