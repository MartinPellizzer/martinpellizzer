Angelonia tomentosa

Angelonia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly angelonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a bedding plant.

Angelonia tomentosa prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
