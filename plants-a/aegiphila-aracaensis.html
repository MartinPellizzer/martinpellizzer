Aegiphila aracaensis

Aegiphila aracaensis, commonly known as the Aracaense shrub, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape and have a slightly hairy texture.

Aegiphila aracaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.

Morphology

Aegiphila aracaensis has leaves that are elliptical or ovate in shape, with a smooth texture, dark green on top, and lighter green underneath, arranged alternately along the stem.

The stem is slender, with a smooth, pale green to reddish-brown bark, and grows upright with a branching pattern. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, with white to pale pink petals, blooming during the dry season, and are pollinated by insects. The fruit is a small capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, adapting to the dry, rocky soils of its native habitat.
