Aegiphila aracaensis

Aegiphila aracaensis, commonly known as the Aracaense shrub, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape and have a slightly hairy texture.

Aegiphila aracaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
