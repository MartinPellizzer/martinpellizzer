Archangelica sativa

Archangelica sativa, commonly known as giant hogweed, is a tall biennial or short-lived perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It can grow up to 5 meters in height, with large, umbrella-like compound leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant contains phototoxic compounds in its sap, which can cause severe skin irritation when exposed to sunlight. It is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and other moist habitats.

Due to its invasive nature, it is considered a noxious weed in many regions.
