Alona baccata

Alona baccata, commonly known as the watercress, is a small aquatic plant that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its submerged or floating leaves, which are typically heart-shaped and arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often borne on long, slender stalks. Alona baccata is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize various water bodies, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.

It is also valued for its nutritional content, as it is rich in vitamins and minerals.
