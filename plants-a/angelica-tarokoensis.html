Angelica tarokoensis

Angelica tarokoensis, commonly known as Taroko Angelica, is a perennial herb native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes plants like carrots and parsley. The plant is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, compound leaves that are often feather-like in appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Angelica tarokoensis is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
