Alyssoides bulgarica f. glabra

Alyssoides bulgarica f. glabra, commonly known as the glabrous Bulgarian alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered with fine hairs, though the 'glabra' form is hairless.

The flowers are usually white or pale yellow, blooming in early summer.
