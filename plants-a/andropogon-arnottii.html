Andropogon arnottii

Andropogon arnottii, commonly known as Arnott's bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and blue-green foliage, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in prairies and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or pink.

Andropogon arnottii plays a vital role in maintaining wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
