Alsine erythrosepala

Alsine erythrosepala, commonly known as the red-sepal alsike, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and typically grows in moist, disturbed soils. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bright red flowers with prominent sepals. It is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
