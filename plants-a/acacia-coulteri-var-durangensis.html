Acacia coulteri var. durangensis

Acacia coulteri var. durangensis, commonly known as the Durango acacia, is a shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, showing drought tolerance and efficient water use. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
