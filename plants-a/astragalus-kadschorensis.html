Astragalus kadschorensis

Astragalus kadschorensis, commonly known as Kadschorensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
