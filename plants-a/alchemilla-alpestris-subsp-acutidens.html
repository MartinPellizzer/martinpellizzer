Alchemilla alpestris subsp. acutidens

Alchemilla alpestris subsp.

acutidens, commonly known as the alpine lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It features basal rosettes of lanceolate, silver-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in branched inflorescences during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental plant suitable for alpine gardens and rockeries.
