Alchemilla vulgaris subsp. comosa

Alchemilla vulgaris subsp.

comosa, commonly known as the velvet cinquefoil, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of soft, hairy leaves that give it a velvety appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in branched clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
