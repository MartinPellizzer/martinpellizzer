Alchornea castaneifolia var. salicifolia

Alchornea castaneifolia var. salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaved alchornea, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features lanceolate leaves that are smooth and often have a waxy coating, giving them a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by orange to red drupes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
