Arabis hirsuta subsp. hornungiana

Arabis hirsuta subsp.

hornungiana, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant features basal leaves that are hairy and lobed, with small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
