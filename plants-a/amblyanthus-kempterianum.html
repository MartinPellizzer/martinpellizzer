Amblyanthus kempterianum

Amblyanthus kempterianum, commonly known as the African tamarind tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Leguminosae (Fabaceae) family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain edible seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Amblyanthus kempterianum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand dry conditions.
