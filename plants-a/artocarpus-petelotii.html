Artocarpus petelotii

Artocarpus petelotii, commonly known as the Kauri fruit or Bornean breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries. The plant produces a large, round fruit with a thick, fibrous rind and a starchy, edible pulp inside. Its leaves are large, palmately lobed, and have a rough texture.

The tree is valued for its fruit, which is used in traditional cuisine and has potential for cultivation in tropical regions.
