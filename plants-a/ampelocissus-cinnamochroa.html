Ampelocissus cinnamochroa

Ampelocissus cinnamochroa, commonly known as the cinnamon-colored leaf fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with a reddish or cinnamon hue, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its vines can grow quite long, making it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover.
