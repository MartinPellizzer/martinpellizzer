Alchemilla anglica

Alchemilla anglica, commonly known as English lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of velvety, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in herbal medicine for their astringent properties.

Alchemilla anglica thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
