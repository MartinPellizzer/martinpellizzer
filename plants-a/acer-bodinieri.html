Acer bodinieri

Acer bodinieri, commonly known as the Bodinier maple, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by samaras with winged seeds. It typically grows to a height of 10-20 meters and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
