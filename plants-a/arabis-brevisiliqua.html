Arabis brevisiliqua

Arabis brevisiliqua, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rockcress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or mountainous regions. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of hairs.
