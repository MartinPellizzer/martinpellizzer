Artemisia ghazniensis

Artemisia ghazniensis, commonly known as the Ghazni wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia, particularly Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in panicles. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its essential oils contain compounds like thujone and camphor, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Artemisia ghazniensis is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of natural pesticides.
