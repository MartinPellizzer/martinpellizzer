Aristida adscensionis var. ehrenbergii

Aristida adscensionis var. ehrenbergii, commonly known as the Ehrenberg's threeawn, is a grass species native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
