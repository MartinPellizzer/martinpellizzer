Achyranthes rosea

Achyranthes rosea, commonly known as the red borage or red spurflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb with branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, often appearing in shades of red, pink, or purple.

Achyranthes rosea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
