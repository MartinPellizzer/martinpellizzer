Angraecum emarginatum

Angraecum emarginatum, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It features long, pendulous inflorescences with star-shaped, white to pale pink flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. The plant has fleshy, dark green leaves and can grow up to several feet in length, often cascading from trees or rocks. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to flower.

Due to its rarity and unique beauty, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
