Aira stricta

Aira stricta, commonly known as slender air grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in woodland edges.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
