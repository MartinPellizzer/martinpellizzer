Astragalus hartwegii

Astragalus hartwegii, commonly known as Hartweg's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and desert grasslands. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.

Astragalus hartwegii is often found in association with other native plants in arid and semi-arid environments.
