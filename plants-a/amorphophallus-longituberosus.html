Amorphophallus longituberosus

Amorphophallus longituberosus, commonly known as the long-tubed arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a distinctive, often spiny stem. The plant produces a single, spadix-like inflorescence that can reach several meters in height. Its tuberous roots are the primary storage organs, allowing it to survive periods of drought.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
