Allium sergii

Allium sergii, commonly known as the Siberian leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem that bears small, spherical purple or white flowers in late summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic-like flavor, making it a potential culinary and medicinal plant.

Allium sergii thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.
