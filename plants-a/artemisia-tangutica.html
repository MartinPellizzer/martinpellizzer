Artemisia tangutica

Artemisia tangutica, commonly known as the Tangut wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant contains essential oils and various bioactive compounds, making it valuable in traditional medicine. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Due to its resilience and medicinal properties, it is of interest to both botanists and herbalists.
