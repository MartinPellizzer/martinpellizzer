Acacia alata var. genuina

Acacia alata var. genuina, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular heads, which are typically visited by nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
