Ananas ananassoides var. nanus

Ananas ananassoides var. nanus, commonly known as the dwarf pineapple plant, is a compact, low-growing bromeliad native to Brazil.

It features rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, apple-shaped fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Due to its compact size, it is often used as a decorative plant in gardens and homes.
