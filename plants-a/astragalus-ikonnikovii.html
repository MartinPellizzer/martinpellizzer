Astragalus ikonnikovii

Astragalus ikonnikovii, commonly known as Ikonnikov's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its survival in harsh environments.

Astragalus ikonnikovii is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
