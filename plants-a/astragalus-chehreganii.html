Astragalus chehreganii

Astragalus chehreganii, commonly known as Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to regions of Central Asia, including parts of Iran and Afghanistan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to dry climates. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus chehreganii is also noted for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
