Abutilon tulla

Abutilon tulla, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its velvety, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a waxy texture, and it produces capsules that contain numerous seeds.

Abutilon tulla is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
