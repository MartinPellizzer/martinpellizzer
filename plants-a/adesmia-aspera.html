Adesmia aspera

Adesmia aspera, commonly known as the rough adesmia, is a flowering plant species native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, often woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, and it bears small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Adesmia aspera is adapted to dry, arid environments and is often found in shrubland or rocky habitats.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various herbivores.
