Asparagus officinalis var. sativus

Asparagus officinalis var. sativus, commonly known as garden asparagus, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asparagaceae family.

It is characterized by its fern-like, compound leaves and edible, needle-like stems that are harvested in the spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, typically growing in temperate climates. It produces small, white or greenish flowers that develop into red berries.

Asparagus is valued for its nutritional content and has been cultivated for thousands of years for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
