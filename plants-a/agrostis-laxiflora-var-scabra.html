Agrostis laxiflora var. scabra

Agrostis laxiflora var. scabra, commonly known as rough hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often bear a rough, scabrous texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous minute, pale green to brownish florets. Its leaves are narrow, usually hairy, and have a bluish-green hue when fresh.

This variety is often found in moist meadows, along stream banks, and in other damp, open habitats.
