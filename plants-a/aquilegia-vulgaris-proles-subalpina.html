Aquilegia vulgaris proles subalpina

Aquilegia vulgaris proles subalpina, commonly known as the subalpine columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive spurred flowers, which are typically blue or purple in color and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has palmately divided leaves with a deep green color and a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained, loamy soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This subspecies is adapted to cooler, high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
