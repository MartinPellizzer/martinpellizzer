Acacia retivenea

Acacia retivenea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets.

Acacia retivenea is well adapted to arid conditions and is often used in reforestation projects.
