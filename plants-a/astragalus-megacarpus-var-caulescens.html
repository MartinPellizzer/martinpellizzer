Astragalus megacarpus var. caulescens

Astragalus megacarpus var. caulescens, commonly known as the giant milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its robust, branching stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several small, hard seeds.

This species is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert habitats.
