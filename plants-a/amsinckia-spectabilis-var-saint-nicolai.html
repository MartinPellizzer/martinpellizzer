Amsinckia spectabilis var. saint-nicolai

Amsinckia spectabilis var. saint-nicolai, commonly known as the Saint-Nicolas fiddleneck, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a taproot system and grows from 1 to 3 feet tall, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in dry, open habitats.

The botanical name reflects its ornamental value and its resemblance to a fiddle, with its elongated flower clusters.
