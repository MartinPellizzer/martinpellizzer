Actephila excelsa var. brevifolia

Actephila excelsa var. brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved everlasting, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions. Its foliage is usually narrow and grass-like, with the leaves being shorter than the typical variety of Actephila excelsa.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its long-lasting blooms.
