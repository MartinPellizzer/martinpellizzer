Antennaria anaphaloides var. straminea

Antennaria anaphaloides var. straminea, commonly known as the yellow sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features a mat-forming habit with small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The flowers are typically pale yellow and appear in clusters, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This plant is well adapted to harsh conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
