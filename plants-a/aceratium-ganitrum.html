Aceratium ganitrum

Aceratium ganitrum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces a capsule fruit containing numerous seeds with feathery parachutes for wind dispersal. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky soils.

Aceratium ganitrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
