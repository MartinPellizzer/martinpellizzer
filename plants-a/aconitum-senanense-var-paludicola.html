Aconitum senanense var. paludicola

Aconitum senanense var. paludicola, commonly known as the swamp aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in racemes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply cut lobes that are typically green and glossy.

This variety is adapted to moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshy areas and along riverbanks.
