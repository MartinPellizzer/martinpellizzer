Aerangis platyphylla

Aerangis platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its broad, flat leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often near streams or in forest understories. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a unique structure that attracts specific pollinators.

Aerangis platyphylla is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
