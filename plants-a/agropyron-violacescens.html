Agropyron violacescens

Agropyron violacescens, commonly known as the violet wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with violet-tinted glumes, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Agropyron violacescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
