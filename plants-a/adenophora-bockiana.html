Adenophora bockiana

Adenophora bockiana, commonly known as the Bock's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Adenophora bockiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
