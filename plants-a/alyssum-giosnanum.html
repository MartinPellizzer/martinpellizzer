Alyssum giosnanum

Alyssum giosnanum, commonly known as the Armenian alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often arranged in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture.

Alyssum giosnanum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
