Archidendron vaillantii

Archidendron vaillantii, commonly known as the pink shower tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities in tropical gardens. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The tree is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
