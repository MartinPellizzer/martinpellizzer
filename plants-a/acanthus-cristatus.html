Acanthus cristatus

Acanthus cristatus, commonly known as the crown-of-thorns, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that resemble a crown, hence its common name. The plant features large, waxy, dark green leaves with a rough texture and prominent veins. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow and appear in summer, adding a unique visual element to its appearance.

Acanthus cristatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its dramatic and architectural form.
