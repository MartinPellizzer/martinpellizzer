Azarolus platyphylla

Azarolus platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf azarolus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, sprawling branches. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are broad and flat, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

The plant is characterized by its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions, making it well-suited for arid landscapes.
