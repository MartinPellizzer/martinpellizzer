Aegilops triuncialis var. muricata

Aegilops triuncialis var. muricata, commonly known as the rough spikegrass, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rough, and bristly foliage, which gives it its scientific name "muricata," meaning "rough.". The plant produces spike-like inflorescences with small, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is also valued for its potential use in forage and erosion control due to its hardy nature.
