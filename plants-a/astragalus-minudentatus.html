Astragalus minudentatus

Astragalus minudentatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinctive hairy texture, while its pods contain small, hard seeds.

Astragalus minudentatus plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local pollinator populations.
