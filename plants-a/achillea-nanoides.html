Achillea nanoides

Achillea nanoides, commonly known as small yarrow, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ecological and ornamental gardening.
