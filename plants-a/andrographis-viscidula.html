Andrographis viscidula

Andrographis viscidula, commonly known as sticky andrographis, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves covered in a sticky resin. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

Andrographis viscidula is often found in moist, shaded environments in tropical regions.
