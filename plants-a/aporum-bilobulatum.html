Aporum bilobulatum

Aporum bilobulatum, commonly known as the bilobed aporum, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter, with a distinctive bifurcated shape at the base. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white and yellow, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its unique leaf structure and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and private collections.

However, it requires high humidity and indirect light to thrive, making it a challenging plant for casual growers.
