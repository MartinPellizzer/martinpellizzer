Adiantum cooperi

Adiantum cooperi, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are dark green and deeply divided. The plant prefers moist, shady environments and thrives in well-draining, acidic soil. Adiantum cooperi is known for its ability to grow in hanging baskets or as a houseplant due to its compact size.

Its unique appearance and ease of care make it a popular choice for both gardeners and indoor plant enthusiasts.
