Amaranthus guadelupensis

Amaranthus guadelupensis, commonly known as the Guadeloupe amaranth, is a species of flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy inflorescences. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that develop into three-angled, bead-like seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it is sometimes grown as a garden plant.
