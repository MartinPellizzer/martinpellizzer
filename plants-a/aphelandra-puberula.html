Aphelandra puberula

Aphelandra puberula, commonly known as the velvetleaf torchflower, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that are covered in a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Aphelandra puberula thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture to flourish.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
