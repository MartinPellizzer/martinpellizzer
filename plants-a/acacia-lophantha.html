Acacia lophantha

Acacia lophantha, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, feathery leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun.

Acacia lophantha is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and ability to tolerate drought.
