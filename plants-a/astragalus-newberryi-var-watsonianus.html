Astragalus newberryi var. watsonianus

Astragalus newberryi var. watsonianus, commonly known as the Watson's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to cream-colored flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to survive in arid conditions.

It is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.
