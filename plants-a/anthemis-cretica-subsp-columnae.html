Anthemis cretica subsp. columnae

Anthemis cretica subsp.

columnae, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its upright, branching stems.

The plant produces small, yellow flower heads that are arranged in a compact, cylindrical shape, giving it its subspecies name "columnae.". Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered with fine hairs, and it has a strong, aromatic scent.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Anthemis genus, which are known for their medicinal and ornamental uses.
