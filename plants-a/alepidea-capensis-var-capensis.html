Alepidea capensis var. capensis

Alepidea capensis var. capensis, commonly known as the Cape alepidea, is a perennial herb native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a valued species in local botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
