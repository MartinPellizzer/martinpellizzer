Angraecum pseudopetiolatum

Angraecum pseudopetiolatum, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It features long, slender inflorescences with star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, often with intricate patterns. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its unique floral structure is adapted for pollination by specific moths, showcasing a fascinating co-evolutionary relationship.

Angraecum pseudopetiolatum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
