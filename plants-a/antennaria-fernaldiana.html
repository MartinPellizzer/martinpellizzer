Antennaria fernaldiana

Antennaria fernaldiana, commonly known as the Fernald's mouse-ear, is a small, mat-forming plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and grass-like leaves. The plant features tiny, hairy leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in length, arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are inconspicuous, consisting of numerous tiny florets that bloom in the spring.

Antennaria fernaldiana is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments.
