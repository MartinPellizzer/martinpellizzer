Abromeitiella brevifolia subsp. chlorantha

Abromeitiella brevifolia subsp.

chlorantha, commonly known as the greenleaf abromeitiella, is a flowering plant species native to the southeastern United States. It is a perennial herb with slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green.

The flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences and are usually pale blue to purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

This subspecies is notable for its compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
