Adelia parvifolia

Adelia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved adelia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, white to pale blue flowers. The leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hair, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in xerophytic gardens.

The plant produces clusters of tiny white flowers with four petals, blooming in early spring and relying on wind pollination. Its roots are fibrous and shallow, adapting to dry conditions by forming a dense root system near the soil surface.
