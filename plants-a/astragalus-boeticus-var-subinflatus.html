Astragalus boeticus var. subinflatus

Astragalus boeticus var. subinflatus, commonly known as the European milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or pale yellow flowers in late spring. Its fruit is a slender, hairy pod that contains several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

This species is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
