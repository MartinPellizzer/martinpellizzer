Aconitum ranunculoides subsp. umbrosum

Aconitum ranunculoides subsp.

umbrosum, commonly known as the shade aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, palmately lobed leaves and showy blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in woodland and shady habitats, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. It produces a tall flowering stem with clustered flowers that bloom in late summer.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care.
