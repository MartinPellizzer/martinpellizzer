Aloysia catamarcensis

Aloysia catamarcensis, commonly known as the Catamarcensis verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple and violet. The plant features slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.

Aloysia catamarcensis is also valued for its role in supporting local pollinators and its potential in phytochemical research.
