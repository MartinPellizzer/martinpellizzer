Arctostaphylos montaraensis

Arctostaphylos montaraensis, commonly known as Montara manzanita, is a shrub native to California's coastal regions.

It is characterized by its thick, gnarled stems and evergreen leaves that are leathery and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dark seeds.

Montara manzanita plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
