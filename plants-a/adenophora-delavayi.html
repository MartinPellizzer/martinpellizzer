Adenophora delavayi

Adenophora delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas at high altitudes. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with a fine pubescence, while its stems are often hairy.

Adenophora delavayi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
