Asplenium rhomboidale

Asplenium rhomboidale, commonly known as the rhomboid shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its compact, rhomboid-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually leathery and have a distinctive, shield-like appearance.

Asplenium rhomboidale is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
