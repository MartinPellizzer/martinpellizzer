Acacia catechu var. catechuoides

Acacia catechu var. catechuoides, commonly known as the Indian blackwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-green, globular inflorescences that bear small, fragrant flowers. Its most notable feature is the production of a dark brown to black resin, which has been traditionally used in dyeing and tanning.

The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and musical instruments.
