Aster linguiformis

Aster linguiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are elongated and narrow, giving it its common name, while its flowers are usually blue to purple in color.

Aster linguiformis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
