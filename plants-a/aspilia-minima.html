Aspilia minima

Aspilia minima, commonly known as the dwarf flame flower, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that are surrounded by narrow, leaf-like bracts, giving it a striking appearance. Aspilia minima thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
