Aristida prodigiosa var. calva

Aristida prodigiosa var. calva, commonly known as the hairy wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow spikelet cluster, which is often adorned with short, bristly bracts.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
