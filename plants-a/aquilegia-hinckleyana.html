Aquilegia hinckleyana

Aquilegia hinckleyana, commonly known as the Hinckley's columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It produces showy blue to purple flowers in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
