Achyranthes ovata

Achyranthes ovata, commonly known as the Indian mallow, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia, including India and Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Achyranthes ovata is often found in moist, fertile soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
