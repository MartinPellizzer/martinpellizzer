Alcea chrysantha

Alcea chrysantha, commonly known as the yellow hollyhock, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Alcea chrysantha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
