Astragalus pachypus

Astragalus pachypus, commonly known as the thick-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus pachypus is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
