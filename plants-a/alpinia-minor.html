Alpinia minor

Alpinia minor, commonly known as the lesser ginger, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and related plants. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Alpinia minor thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued both for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
