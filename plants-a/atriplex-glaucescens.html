Atriplex glaucescens

Atriplex glaucescens, commonly known as green saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often bluish-green in color, adapted to conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes, typical of the Amaranthaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland salt-affected areas.

Atriplex glaucescens is also valued for its ability to stabilize soils and provide habitat for native wildlife.
