Acalypha crenulata

Acalypha crenulata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are often covered in a fine, soft pubescence, giving the plant a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white or yellowish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Acalypha crenulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
