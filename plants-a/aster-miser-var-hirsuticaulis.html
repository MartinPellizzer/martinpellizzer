Aster miser var. hirsuticaulis

Aster miser var. hirsuticaulis, commonly known as the hairy stalk aster, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features narrow, hairy stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
