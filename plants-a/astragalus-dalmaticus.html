Astragalus dalmaticus

Astragalus dalmaticus, commonly known as Dalmatian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its root system is often deep, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus dalmaticus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
