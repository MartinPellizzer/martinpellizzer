Andropogon strictus

Andropogon strictus, commonly known as tallgrass bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, stiff stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that turn a striking golden hue in the fall. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its flowering spikes produce small, spikelet-like flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Andropogon strictus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
