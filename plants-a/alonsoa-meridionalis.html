Alonsoa meridionalis

Alonsoa meridionalis, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces striking blue to violet flowers, which open in the morning and close by midday. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include a slender stem and small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Alonsoa meridionalis is adapted to harsh environments and is often found in disturbed soils and rocky areas.
