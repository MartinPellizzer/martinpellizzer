Acalypha echinus

Acalypha echinus, commonly known as the spiked hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiny calyx that surrounds its showy, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture and often a reddish or purple underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
