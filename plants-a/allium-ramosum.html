Allium ramosum

Allium ramosum, commonly known as the slender onion or grass leek, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, bulbous stem that is often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Allium ramosum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
