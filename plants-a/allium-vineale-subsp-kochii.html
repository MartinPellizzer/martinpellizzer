Allium vineale subsp. kochii

Allium vineale subsp.

kochii, commonly known as Koch's garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Koch's garlic is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky soils.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.
