Acalypha wilderi

Acalypha wilderi, commonly known as the velvetleaf or wild spurflower, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with smooth, often hairy stems. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, contributing to its common name.

Acalypha wilderi produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often surrounded by the showy bracts.
