Allium ravenii

Allium ravenii, commonly known as the purple onion.

It is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its striking violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces clusters of small, rounded bulbs that are often used in culinary applications. Its foliage consists of long, grass-like leaves that emerge from the base of the plant.

Allium ravenii is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
