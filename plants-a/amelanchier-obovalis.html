Amelanchier obovalis

Amelanchier obovalis, commonly known as the oval serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that ripen in late summer, turning from red to dark purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

Amelanchier obovalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
