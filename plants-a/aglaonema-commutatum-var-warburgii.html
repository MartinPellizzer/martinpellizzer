Aglaonema commutatum var. warburgii

Aglaonema commutatum var. warburgii, commonly known as the Warburg's Chinese evergreen, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive white and silver markings that create a visually appealing contrast. The plant is a member of the Araceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers, though it is primarily valued for its foliage rather than its blooms.

The plant is also noted for its air-purifying qualities, contributing to its popularity in both residential and commercial settings.
