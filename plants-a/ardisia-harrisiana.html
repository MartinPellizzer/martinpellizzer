Ardisia harrisiana

Ardisia harrisiana, commonly known as the Harris's evergreen, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtle family (Myrtaceae).

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, white to pink flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Ardisia harrisiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
