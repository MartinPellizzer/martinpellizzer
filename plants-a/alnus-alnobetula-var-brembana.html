Alnus alnobetula var. brembana

Alnus alnobetula var. brembana, commonly known as the Bremban alder, is a variant of the alder tree native to the Alps.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves and yellowish flowers that appear in early spring. The plant is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Its ecological importance makes it a valuable species in forest ecosystems.
