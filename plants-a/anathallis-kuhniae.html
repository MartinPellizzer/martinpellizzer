Anathallis kuhniae

Anathallis kuhniae, commonly known as the Kuhniae spider orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with long, slender petals and a distinctive, elongated flower structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
