Aglaia odoratissima

Aglaia odoratissima, commonly known as the fragrant aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that emit a strong, sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators like bees and wasps. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and the tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.

The plant's botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
