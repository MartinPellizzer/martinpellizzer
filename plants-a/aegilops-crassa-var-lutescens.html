Aegilops crassa var. lutescens

Aegilops crassa var. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow rye grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its robust growth habit and yellowish-green foliage, with leaves that are typically rough and hairy. The plant produces spikelets that are clustered at the base of the stems, and its flowers are small and wind-pollinated. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

This variant is notable for its potential use in agricultural and ecological contexts due to its adaptability and resilience.
