Antholyza gracilis

Antholyza gracilis, commonly known as the slender antholyza, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks, preferring temperate climates. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, linear to lanceolate leaves. Its small, yellowish flowers are arranged in composite heads, which are often mistaken for single flowers.

Antholyza gracilis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
