Ardisia glandulosomarginata

Ardisia glandulosomarginata, commonly known as the glandular-margin ardisia, is a member of the Myricaceae family.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have prominent glandular margins. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

The plant's ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in tropical and subtropical regions.
