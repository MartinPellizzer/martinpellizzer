Arctotis crithmifolia

Arctotis crithmifolia, commonly known as the coastal daisy or sea daisy, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal gardens.

Arctotis crithmifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
