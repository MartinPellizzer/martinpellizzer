Acalypha macrostachya f. androgyna

Acalypha macrostachya f. androgyna, commonly known as the Androgyna Acalypha, is a variant of the species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often a vibrant green with a distinctive reddish or purple hue on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are borne on long, slender spikes. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
