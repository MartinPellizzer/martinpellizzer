Arenaria capitata subsp. mauritanica

Arenaria capitata subsp.

mauritanica, commonly known as the Mauritanian sandflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North Africa. It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink.

The plant thrives in sandy and rocky soils, often found in coastal and inland desert environments. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it well adapted to harsh, dry climates.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
