Acrostichum pectinatum

Acrostichum pectinatum, commonly known as the broadleaf hanger fern, is a tropical fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that are often used in traditional basketry and thatching. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests. Its fronds are typically bright green and can grow up to several meters in length.

Acrostichum pectinatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in humid tropical ecosystems.
