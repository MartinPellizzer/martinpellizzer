Acacia parvifolia

Acacia parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, feathery leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its seeds are encased in pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread across arid and semi-arid regions.
