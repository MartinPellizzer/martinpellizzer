Allium spicatum

Allium spicatum, commonly known as the slender leek, is a flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in grassy areas and woodland edges. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces umbels of white or pale purple flowers. Its bulbous stem is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The botanical characteristics of Allium spicatum make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
