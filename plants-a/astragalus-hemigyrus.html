Astragalus hemigyrus

Astragalus hemigyrus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and steppe environments.

Astragalus hemigyrus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local herbal practices.
