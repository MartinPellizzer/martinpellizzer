Aniserica gracilis

Aniserica gracilis, commonly known as the slender anise plant, is a herbaceous species belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, often found in moist, shaded environments. It produces small, aromatic seeds that are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Due to its delicate structure and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
