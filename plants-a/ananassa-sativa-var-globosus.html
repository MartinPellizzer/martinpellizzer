Ananassa sativa var. globosus

Ananassa sativa var. globosus, commonly known as the pineapple, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central stem. The plant produces a spiky, fleshy fruit that is typically yellow or golden in color. The fruit contains multiple segments, each with a central core, and is rich in bromelain, an enzyme with digestive properties.

It is widely cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
