Allium hemisphaericum

Allium hemisphaericum, commonly known as the globe onion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, globe-shaped bulb and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in a loose cluster at the top of the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Allium hemisphaericum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic and low-maintenance qualities.
