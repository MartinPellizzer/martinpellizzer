Astragalus nigritus var. vegetior

Astragalus nigritus var. vegetior, commonly known as the greater milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stems and pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is robust, allowing it to thrive in poor, sandy soils.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
