Arcytophyllum aristatum

Arcytophyllum aristatum, commonly known as the spiked sagebrush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, semi-desert environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies due to their medicinal properties.

Arcytophyllum aristatum plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
