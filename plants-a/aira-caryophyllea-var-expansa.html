Aira caryophyllea var. expansa

Aira caryophyllea var. expansa, commonly known as the broadleaf false oat, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves and panicles of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. It has a fibrous root system and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to arid environments.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
