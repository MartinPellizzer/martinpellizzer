Arundo isiaca

Arundo isiaca, commonly known as the giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 6 meters in height, with long, narrow leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and riverbanks, and is often used for erosion control due to its dense root system.

Arundo isiaca is also valued in traditional crafts and as a source of natural fiber.
