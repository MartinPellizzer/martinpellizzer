Ammannia subspicata

Ammannia subspicata, commonly known as the redroot ammannia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in wetlands and disturbed soils. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a reddish underside, and it produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers. Its root system is characterized by reddish pigmentation, which gives it its common name.

Ammannia subspicata is often found in moist environments and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-rich soils.
