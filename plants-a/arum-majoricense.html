Arum majoricense

Arum majoricense, commonly known as the Major Island arum, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean island of Major.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking, spatulate inflorescence that resembles a single flower. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique floral structures and often toxic sap. Arum majoricense typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as swamps and forest floors.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and botanical gardens.
