Astragalus glycyphyllos f. bosniacus

Astragalus glycyphyllos f. bosniacus, commonly known as the Bosnian sweet vetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats and is often found in mountainous areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
