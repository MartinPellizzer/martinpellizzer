Abies brunoniana

Abies brunoniana, commonly known as the Brunon's fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical trunk and dense, pyramidal crown. The needles are short, flat, and dark green, with two white stomatal bands on the underside. Its cones are typically pendulous, maturing to a reddish-brown color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
