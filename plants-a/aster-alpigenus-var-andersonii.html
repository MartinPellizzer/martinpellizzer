Aster alpigenus var. andersonii

Aster alpigenus var. andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's alpine aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, rocky habitats at high elevations. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white to purple. It is well adapted to cold climates and has a low-growing habit to withstand harsh alpine conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
