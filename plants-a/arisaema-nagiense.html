Arisaema nagiense

Arisaema nagiense, commonly known as the Nagi's arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow, and it produces berries that contain toxic seeds.

Arisaema nagiense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
