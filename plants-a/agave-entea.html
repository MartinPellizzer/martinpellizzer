Agave entea

Agave entea, commonly known as the blue agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped arrangement of thick, blue-gray leaves that are covered in a white powdery coating called farina. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that can reach up to 10 feet in height, bearing clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Agave entea is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its leaves are used in the production of tequila and other agave-based spirits.
