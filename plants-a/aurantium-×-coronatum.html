Aurantium × coronatum

Aurantium × coronatum, commonly known as the sweet orange, is a hybrid citrus plant originating from the cross between the common orange (Citrus sinensis) and the pomelo (Citrus maxima).

It is characterized by its thick, peely rind and juicy, segmented fruit filled with aromatic pulp. The plant typically grows as a small evergreen tree with glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is widely cultivated for culinary and commercial purposes, and it is also used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Aurantium × coronatum make it a valuable species in both agriculture and horticulture.
