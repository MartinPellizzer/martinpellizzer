Aralia hypoglauca

Aralia hypoglauca, commonly known as the blue-leaf aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are often tinged with a bluish hue, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Aralia hypoglauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
