Astragalus didymocarpus var. obispoensis

Astragalus didymocarpus var. obispoensis, commonly known as the Obispo's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb with slender, branched stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in clusters, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its seeds are enclosed in a distinctive, two-lobed pod, which splits open to release the seeds.
