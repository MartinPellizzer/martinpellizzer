Adenocarpus artemisiifolius

Adenocarpus artemisiifolius, commonly known as the golden weed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers arranged in composite heads. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
