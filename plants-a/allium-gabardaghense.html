Allium gabardaghense

Allium gabardaghense, commonly known as the Gabardagh onion, is a species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its bulbous stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in a dense umbel. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure.

This plant is valued for its culinary use and is also of interest in botanical studies due to its unique genetic makeup.
