Achyranthes aspera

Achyranthes aspera, commonly known as prickly chaffflower, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its branched stems, which are covered in stiff, bristly hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, commonly found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Achyranthes aspera is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
