Aconitum jaluense var. truncatum

Aconitum jaluense var. truncatum, commonly known as the Jaluense Monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, helmet-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant features large, deeply lobed leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
