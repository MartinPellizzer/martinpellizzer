Appendicula rivularis

Appendicula rivularis, commonly known as the water bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its tiny, bladder-like structures that trap small organisms, aiding in its nutrient acquisition. The plant typically grows in shallow, slow-moving water and has delicate, branched stems. Its leaves are reduced to scale-like structures, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Appendicula rivularis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to nutrient cycling.
