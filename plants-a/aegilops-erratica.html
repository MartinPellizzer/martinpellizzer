Aegilops erratica

Aegilops erratica, commonly known as the goat grass, is a wild grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are often covered in a distinctive, hairy coating. It is a perennial species that thrives in dry, rocky soils.

Aegilops erratica is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.

Morphology

Aegilops erratica has narrow, grass-like leaves that are linear in shape, alternate in arrangement, and typically range from 5 to 15 cm in length, with a bluish-green color and a slightly rough texture.

The stem is upright, cylindrical, and slightly hairy, with a light green color and a growth pattern that is usually clumping or spreading. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, pale blue to greenish flowers that bloom in the spring, and they are primarily pollinated by wind. The fruit is a small, three-angled spikelet that contains three seeds, which are dispersed by wind due to the lightweight nature of the seeds.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, helping the plant to anchor itself and absorb nutrients from the soil.
