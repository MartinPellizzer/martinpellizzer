Athyrium × tsurutanum

Athyrium × tsurutanum, commonly known as the Japanese painted fern, is a hybrid fern species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that display a striking combination of green, silver, and rust-colored hues. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, which are clustered in sori.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural displays due to its unique foliage.
