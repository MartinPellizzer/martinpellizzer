Acaena elongata var. robusta

Acaena elongata var. robusta, commonly known as the New Zealand bracken, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and has dark green, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by hard, spiny fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Acaena elongata var. robusta is also known for its ability to colonize disturbed areas and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
