Amyema vernicosa

Amyema vernicosa, commonly known as the glossy apple or satin apple, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the eastern regions of Australia, where it typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It produces edible, apple-like fruits that are often consumed by both humans and wildlife.

Amyema vernicosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
