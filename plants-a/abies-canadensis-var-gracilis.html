Abies canadensis var. gracilis

Abies canadensis var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender white fir, is a variant of the balsam fir native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and soft, silvery-blue needles that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces cones that are typically smaller and more clustered compared to the standard variety. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
