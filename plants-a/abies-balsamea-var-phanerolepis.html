Abies balsamea var. phanerolepis

Abies balsamea var. phanerolepis, commonly known as the eastern white fir, is a variant of the balsam fir native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in spirals. The plant produces small, rounded cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing winged seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mixed forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of boreal and temperate forest ecosystems.
