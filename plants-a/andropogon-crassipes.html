Andropogon crassipes

Andropogon crassipes, commonly known as inland needlegrass, is a tall, perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are often clustered in dense, upright clusters. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its botanical features make it a key component of wetland ecosystems.
