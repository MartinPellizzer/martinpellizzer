Appendicula isoglossa

Appendicula isoglossa, commonly known as the purple marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features a slender, erect stem with a single, showy purple or violet flower that blooms in late summer. The plant has a fibrous root system and possesses a distinctive, narrow leaf that is often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, particularly bees, and it thrives in moist, acidic soils.

Appendicula isoglossa is a rare and endangered species, requiring specific ecological conditions for successful growth.
