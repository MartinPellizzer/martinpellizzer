Alyssum montanum var. pradense

Alyssum montanum var. pradense, commonly known as alpine alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early summer. The plant has hairy, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
