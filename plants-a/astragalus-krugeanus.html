Astragalus krugeanus

Astragalus krugeanus, commonly known as Kruge's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus krugeanus is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil.
