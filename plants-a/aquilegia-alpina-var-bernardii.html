Aquilegia alpina var. bernardii

Aquilegia alpina var. bernardii, commonly known as the Alpine columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features showy, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, with five petals and two prominent nectar-rich spurs. The plant has palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, which are usually deeply lobed and hairy. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountain meadows and alpine tundra.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
