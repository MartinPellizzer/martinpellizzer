Astragalus dicksonii

Astragalus dicksonii, commonly known as Dickson's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical seed pods that contain numerous tiny seeds.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.
