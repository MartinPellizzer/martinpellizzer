Astragalus koikitaensis

Astragalus koikitaensis, commonly known as the Kikita's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its seed pods contain small, rounded seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its survival in its native habitat.

Astragalus koikitaensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its ecosystem.
