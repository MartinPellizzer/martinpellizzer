Artemisia centiflora

Artemisia centiflora, commonly known as blue mist shrub, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine.

Artemisia centiflora is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
