Assonia pseudopopulus

Assonia pseudopopulus, commonly known as the false poplar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy pulp.

Assonia pseudopopulus is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.
