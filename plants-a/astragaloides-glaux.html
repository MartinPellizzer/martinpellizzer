Astragaloides glaux

Astragaloides glaux, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in sagebrush habitats.

Astragaloides glaux plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in restoration ecology projects.
