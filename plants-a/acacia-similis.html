Acacia similis

Acacia similis, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, linear leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular inflorescences, which are a defining feature of the genus Acacia. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia similis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
