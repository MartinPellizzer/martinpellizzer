Anchusa latifolia

Anchusa latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf bugle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has a fibrous root system and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Anchusa latifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
