Aristida megapotamica var. longipes

Aristida megapotamica var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed threeawn, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental potential and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
