Aureolaria flava subsp. macrantha

Aureolaria flava subsp.

macrantha, commonly known as the yellow sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its bright yellow, showy flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems.

The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for xeriscaping and pollinator gardens.
