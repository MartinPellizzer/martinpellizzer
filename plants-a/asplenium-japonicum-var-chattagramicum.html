Asplenium japonicum var. chattagramicum

Asplenium japonicum var. chattagramicum, commonly known as the Indian shield fern, is a tropical fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen fronds that form a compact, shield-like structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its leaves are bi-lobed and have a leathery texture, with a dark green coloration.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens and indoor settings.
