Aneurolepidium petraeum

Aneurolepidium petraeum, commonly known as the rock grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous or alpine environments. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single, dense spikelet that is often clustered at the top of the stem.

Aneurolepidium petraeum is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
