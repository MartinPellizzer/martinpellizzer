Astragalus subglaber

Astragalus subglaber, commonly known as the nakedpod milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh environmental conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread across open landscapes.
