Anthericum flavoviride

Anthericum flavoviride, commonly known as the yellow-green arum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of southern Europe.

It features a distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a greenish-yellow spathe. The plant's leaves are arrow-shaped, with a glossy surface and a deep green color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects.

Anthericum flavoviride thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.
