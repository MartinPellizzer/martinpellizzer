Acrocarpidium rotundifolium

Acrocarpidium rotundifolium, commonly known as the round-leaved acrocarp, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and round, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered near the base of the leaves. Its leaves are succulent and can store water, which helps it survive in arid or semi-arid environments.

Acrocarpidium rotundifolium is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
