Aconitum heterophylloides

Aconitum heterophylloides, commonly known as the variable monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive hooded flowers, which are typically blue or purple in color, and its compound leaves with varying leaflet shapes. The plant produces toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aconitum heterophylloides is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological interest.
