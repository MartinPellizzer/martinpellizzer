Astragalus campylanthus var. subglobosus

Astragalus campylanthus var. subglobosus, commonly known as the globular milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.
