Alchemilla argyrophylla subsp. trifolioidea

Alchemilla argyrophylla subsp.

trifolioidea, commonly known as silverleaf lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features a rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural use in rocky gardens.
