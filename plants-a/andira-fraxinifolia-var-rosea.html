Andira fraxinifolia var. rosea

Andira fraxinifolia var. rosea, commonly known as the pink andira, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and often have a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its attractive foliage and floral display.
