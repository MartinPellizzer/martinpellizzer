Alsophila paleolata var. villosa

Alsophila paleolata var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a unique appearance with a dense covering of short, soft hairs on its trunk, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
