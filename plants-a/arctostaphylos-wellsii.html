Arctostaphylos wellsii

Arctostaphylos wellsii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre manzanita, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with leathery, elliptical leaves that are often reddish beneath. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pink, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and the plant is well adapted to dry, rocky soils.

Arctostaphylos wellsii plays an important role in its native ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
