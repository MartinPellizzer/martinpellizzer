Anthurium chamulense subsp. oaxacanum

Anthurium chamulense subsp.

oaxacanum, commonly known as the Oaxaca anthurium, is a species native to Mexico, particularly in the state of Oaxaca. It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant spatulate flowers.

The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within cloud forests, where it thrives in high humidity and moderate temperatures. Its foliage is usually dark green with a glossy texture, and the inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
