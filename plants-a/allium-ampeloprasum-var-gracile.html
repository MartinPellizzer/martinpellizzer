Allium ampeloprasum var. gracile

Allium ampeloprasum var. gracile, commonly known as the slender leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, hollow leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, white, bulbous flowers that are clustered in umbels, which bloom in the spring. It is often cultivated for its edible leaves and mild, onion-like flavor.

This variety is closely related to leeks and shallots, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
