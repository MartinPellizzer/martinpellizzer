Allium albopilosum

Allium albopilosum, commonly known as the white-hair leek, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, garlic-like flavor, making it a potential culinary and medicinal plant.

Allium albopilosum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
