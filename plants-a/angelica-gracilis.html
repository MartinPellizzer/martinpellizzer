Angelica gracilis

Angelica gracilis, commonly known as slender angelica, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes carrots, parsley, and celery, and is characterized by its finely divided leaves and umbel-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with a slender stem and a strong, aromatic scent. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine and are known for their digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The flowers, which bloom in late summer, attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
