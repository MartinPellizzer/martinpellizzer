Astragalus negevensis

Astragalus negevensis, commonly known as the Negev milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Israel and the Negev Desert.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Astragalus negevensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native environment.
