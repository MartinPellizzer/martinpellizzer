Acianthera ciliata

Acianthera ciliata, commonly known as the fringed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers with distinctive fringed petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests or along stream banks.

Acianthera ciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
