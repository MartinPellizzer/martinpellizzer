Aleuritia iljinskii

Aleuritia iljinskii, commonly known as the Siberian snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the cold climates of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant produces bright red berries that are a striking contrast to its dark green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Aleuritia iljinskii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its seasonal interest.
