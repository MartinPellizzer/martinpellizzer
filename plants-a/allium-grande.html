Allium grande

Allium grande, commonly known as the giant garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features strap-like, grassy leaves and produces large, showy purple or pink flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant belongs to the Allium genus, which includes many edible and ornamental species. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its striking appearance.

The bulbs of Allium grande are not typically eaten but are sometimes used in herbal remedies.
