Astragalus vexans

Astragalus vexans, commonly known as the vexed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, harsh environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor conditions.

Astragalus vexans plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
