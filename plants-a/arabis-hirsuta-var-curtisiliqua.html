Arabis hirsuta var. curtisiliqua

Arabis hirsuta var. curtisiliqua, commonly known as the curly Arabis, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curly leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The foliage and flowers contribute to its visual appeal throughout the growing season.
