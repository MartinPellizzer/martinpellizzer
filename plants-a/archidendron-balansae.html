Archidendron balansae

Archidendron balansae, commonly known as the Balansae legume, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, tropical climates. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, often in dense inflorescences.

The tree is sometimes used in agroforestry due to its nitrogen-fixing properties and shade-providing canopy.
