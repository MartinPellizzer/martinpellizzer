Asystasia riparia

Asystasia riparia, commonly known as the creeping asystasia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or pink. It is often found in wetlands, along stream banks, and in forest understories.

Asystasia riparia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for ground cover.
