Astragalus maharluensis

Astragalus maharluensis, commonly known as the Maharlu milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several oval, flat seeds, typical of the genus Astragalus. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
