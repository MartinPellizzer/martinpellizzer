Acer saccharum var. trilobum

Acer saccharum var. trilobum, commonly known as the red maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are lobed and turn vibrant shades of red in the fall. The tree produces small, red flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds called samaras. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
