Asplenium albersii

Asplenium albersii, commonly known as the Albers' spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a small, terrestrial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rock crevices or forest floors. The plant has a short, upright rhizome with dense clusters of lance-shaped, dark green leaves. Its fronds are usually less than 10 centimeters in length and have a serrated or toothed margin.

Asplenium albersii is notable for its ability to thrive in rocky or calcareous soils, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
