Allium margaritaceum var. battandieri

Allium margaritaceum var. battandieri, commonly known as the pearl onion garlic, is a botanical variant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, rounded bulbs that resemble tiny onions, with a mild garlic flavor. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-50 cm, featuring grass-like leaves and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. Its bulbs are often used in culinary applications, particularly in Mediterranean and Middle Eastern cuisines.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for both its ornamental and edible qualities.
