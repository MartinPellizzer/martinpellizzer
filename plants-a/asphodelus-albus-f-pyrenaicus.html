Asphodelus albus f. pyrenaicus

Asphodelus albus f. pyrenaicus, commonly known as the Pyrenean white asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Asphodelaceae and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and tall, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and they bloom in late spring to early summer. This plant thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and open woodlands.

It is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
