Astragalus angustifolius subsp. tymphresteus

Astragalus angustifolius subsp.

tymphresteus, commonly known as narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, compound leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, which are typically arranged in racemes. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in dry, rocky soils.

It is often found in sagebrush steppe and other arid or semi-arid habitats.
