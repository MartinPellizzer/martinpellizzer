Aulacocarpus quadrangularis

Aulacocarpus quadrangularis, commonly known as the African date palm, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that develop into edible, fleshy fruits. The fruit has a sweet, honey-like flavor and is often consumed fresh or processed into beverages.

It plays an important role in both local ecosystems and human cultures across its native range.
