Anil quinquefolia

Anil quinquefolia, commonly known as wild strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features trifoliate and quinquefoliate leaves, with five leaflets arranged in a distinctive palmate pattern. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible, fragrant berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along forest edges.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and have been used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
