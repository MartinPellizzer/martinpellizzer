Asperula hoskingii

Asperula hoskingii, commonly known as the Hosking's woodruff, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features small, dark green, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces tiny, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Asperula hoskingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
