Acanthocereus tetragonus var. micracanthus

Acanthocereus tetragonus var. micracanthus, commonly known as the small-spined night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered with short, pale spines. The plant produces white, fragrant flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habit, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and desert gardens.
