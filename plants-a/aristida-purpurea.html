Aristida purpurea

Aristida purpurea, commonly known as purple threeawn, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or pink hues. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces wind-dispersed seeds.

Aristida purpurea is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and thrive in challenging environments.
