Alstroemeria monantha

Alstroemeria monantha, commonly known as the Chilean lily, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with strap-like, grassy leaves. Its flowers are borne on long, slender stalks and are known for their fragrance and ornamental value in gardens.

Alstroemeria monantha is also valued for its ability to thrive in a range of soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
