Aconitum plicatum

Aconitum plicatum, commonly known as the pleated monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive pleated, hood-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has dark green, doubly serrated leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Aconitum plicatum grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and prefers well-drained, moist soils in full sun to partial shade.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous to handle or ingest.
