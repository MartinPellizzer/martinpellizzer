Astragalus raswendicus

Astragalus raswendicus, commonly known as the Raswen's milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus raswendicus is also noted for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its therapeutic uses.
