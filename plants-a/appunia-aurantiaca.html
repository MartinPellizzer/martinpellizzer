Appunia aurantiaca

Appunia aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bell-shaped blooms that hang in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Appunia aurantiaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
