Aquilegia plena

Aquilegia plena, commonly known as the full-flowered columbine, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and western Asia.

It features showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, purple, and white, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has palmately divided, lobed leaves that are typically green with a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, often growing to a height of 1 to 3 feet.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
