Aeranthes gladiator

Aeranthes gladiator, commonly known as the gladiator orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful floral structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually tubular or spurred, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

The genus Aeranthes is notable for its diversity in floral morphology and ecological adaptations.
