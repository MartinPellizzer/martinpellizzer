Agropyron schrenkianum

Agropyron schrenkianum, commonly known as the Schrenk's wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

Agropyron schrenkianum is valued for its drought tolerance and is used in ecological restoration projects.
