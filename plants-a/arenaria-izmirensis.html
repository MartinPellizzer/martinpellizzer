Arenaria izmirensis

Arenaria izmirensis, commonly known as the Izmir sandflower, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience.
