Arabis costae

Arabis costae, commonly known as rock cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or arctic environments. It is native to regions across Europe, Asia, and North America.

Arabis costae is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in harsh, cold climates.
