Astragalus leansanicus

Astragalus leansanicus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

The plant also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
