Astragalus brevipedunculatus

Astragalus brevipedunculatus, commonly known as the short-stemmed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus brevipedunculatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
