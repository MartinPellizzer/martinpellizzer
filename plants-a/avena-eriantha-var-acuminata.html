Avena eriantha var. acuminata

Avena eriantha var. acuminata, commonly known as the slender oat, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its seeds are encased in a hairy, bristly husk, which aids in seed dispersal.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
