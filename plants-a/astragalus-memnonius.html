Astragalus memnonius

Astragalus memnonius, commonly known as the golden milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus memnonius is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for various wildlife species.
