Asplenium sororium

Asplenium sororium, commonly known as the maidenhair spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically dark green and have a distinctive, narrow shape. The plant belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is native to various regions in the Northern Hemisphere. It has small, clustered spore-producing structures called sori located on the underside of its leaves.

Asplenium sororium is often found growing on rocks or trees in humid, temperate climates.
