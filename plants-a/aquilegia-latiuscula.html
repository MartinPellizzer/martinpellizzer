Aquilegia latiuscula

Aquilegia latiuscula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved columbine, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features striking blue or purple spurred flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has finely divided, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in a silvery-white down. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
