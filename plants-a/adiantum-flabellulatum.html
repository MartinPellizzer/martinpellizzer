Adiantum flabellulatum

Adiantum flabellulatum, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a delicate and graceful fern native to various regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its finely divided, arching fronds that resemble flowing ribbons, giving it a distinctive and elegant appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, moist soil. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, with a unique fan-like shape in mature plants.

Adiantum flabellulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
