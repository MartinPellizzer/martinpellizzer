Acianthera monophylla

Acianthera monophylla, commonly known as the single-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a single, broad leaf and a compact inflorescence. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
