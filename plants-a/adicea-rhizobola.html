Adicea rhizobola

Adicea rhizobola, commonly known as the purple root vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and distinctive violet-colored roots that are often harvested for medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elliptical, dark green leaves. Its roots contain compounds with potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Adicea rhizobola thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
