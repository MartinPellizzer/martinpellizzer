Arikuryroba capanemae

Arikuryroba capanemae, commonly known as the Capaneque palm, is a species of palm native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by drupes that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The Capaneque palm is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its ecological and potential medicinal properties.
