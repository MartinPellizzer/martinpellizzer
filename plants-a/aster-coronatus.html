Aster coronatus

Aster coronatus, commonly known as the crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers. The plant is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange-colored flower heads, which are surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. Aster coronatus typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens and a valuable species for ecological studies.
