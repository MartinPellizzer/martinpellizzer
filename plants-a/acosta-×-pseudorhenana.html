Acosta × pseudorhenana

Acosta × pseudorhenana, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring cross between Acosta and pseudorhenana species.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, featuring showy, often fragrant blossoms that attract pollinators. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, well-drained environments. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a vibrant green color.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental qualities and genetic diversity in hybrid breeding programs.
