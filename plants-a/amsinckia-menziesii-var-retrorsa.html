Amsinckia menziesii var. retrorsa

Amsinckia menziesii var. retrorsa, commonly known as the desert cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers and has narrow, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
