Achillea claudiopolina

Achillea claudiopolina, commonly known as the Alpine yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its medicinal properties have been historically recognized, though it is less commonly cultivated than other members of the Achillea genus.

The species is notable for its resilience in harsh environments and its ornamental value in garden settings.
