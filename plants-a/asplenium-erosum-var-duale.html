Asplenium erosum var. duale

Asplenium erosum var. duale, commonly known as the dual-leaf spleenwort, is a fern species belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive fronds that often exhibit a dichotomous branching pattern. The plant typically features small, lance-shaped leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rock crevices or forest floors.

The varietal name 'duale' refers to its unique leaf structure, which is divided into two distinct parts.
