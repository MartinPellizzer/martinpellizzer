Aerides migueldavidii

Aerides migueldavidii, commonly known as the David's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and the presence of pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Aerides migueldavidii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is popular among orchid enthusiasts.
