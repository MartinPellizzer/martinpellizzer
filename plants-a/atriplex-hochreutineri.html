Atriplex hochreutineri

Atriplex hochreutineri, commonly known as the Hochreuter's saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are adapted to conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. Its salt-tolerant nature allows it to thrive in saline soils, making it a valuable species for reclaiming degraded lands.

Atriplex hochreutineri plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
