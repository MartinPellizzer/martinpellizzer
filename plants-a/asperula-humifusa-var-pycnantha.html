Asperula humifusa var. pycnantha

Asperula humifusa var. pycnantha, commonly known as the creeping azalea, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It features dense, dark green foliage that remains throughout the year, with small, clustered flowers in shades of pink and white during late spring. The plant has a compact growth habit, making it ideal for ground cover or rock gardens. Its leaves are glossy and leathery, providing year-round visual interest.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.
