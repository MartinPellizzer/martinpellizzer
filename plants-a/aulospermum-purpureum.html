Aulospermum purpureum

Aulospermum purpureum, commonly known as the purple aulospermum, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Aulospermum purpureum is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
