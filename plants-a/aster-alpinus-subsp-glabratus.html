Aster alpinus subsp. glabratus

Aster alpinus subsp.

glabratus, commonly known as the alpine aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
