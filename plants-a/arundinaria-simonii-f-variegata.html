Arundinaria simonii f. variegata

Arundinaria simonii f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated bamboo, is a unique cultivar of the bamboo species Arundinaria simonii.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring white and green stripes on its leaves. This plant is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile choice for various climates.

Its growth habit is clump-forming, with tall, slender culms that add a graceful touch to any setting.
