Artemisia tilesii subsp. unalaschcensis

Artemisia tilesii subsp.

unalaschcensis, commonly known as the Alaska tarragon, is a perennial herb native to the subarctic regions of North America. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open, rocky areas. It is often found in alpine and arctic tundra environments.

Its essential oils contribute to its distinctive scent and have been used in traditional herbal medicine.
