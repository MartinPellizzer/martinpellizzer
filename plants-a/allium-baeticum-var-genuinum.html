Allium baeticum var. genuinum

Allium baeticum var. genuinum, commonly known as the Spanish garlic or garum plant, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It produces small, white, spherical bulbs that are often used as a spice in traditional Spanish cuisine. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is characterized by its strong garlic-like aroma. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

This variety is valued both for its culinary uses and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
