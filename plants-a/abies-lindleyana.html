Abies lindleyana

Abies lindleyana, commonly known as the Lindley fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, pyramidal crown. The needles are flat, dark green, and have a subtle resinous scent, while the cones are typically short and cylindrical. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in high-altitude forests.

The plant has ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
