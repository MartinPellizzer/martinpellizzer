Acacia prismatica

Acacia prismatica, commonly known as the diamond acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, triangular-shaped phyllodes that are often covered in a fine, silky pubescence. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with deep roots that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Acacia prismatica is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in landscaping.
