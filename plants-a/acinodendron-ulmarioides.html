Acinodendron ulmarioides

Acinodendron ulmarioides, commonly known as the African elm, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and a rounded crown. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a distinctive pinnate venation. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits that are edible.

It is adapted to various soil types and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
