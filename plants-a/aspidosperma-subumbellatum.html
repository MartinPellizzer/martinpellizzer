Aspidosperma subumbellatum

Aspidosperma subumbellatum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Ceratonia siliqua*, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen canopy and smooth, gray bark. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by orange-red fruits that split open to release seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood.
