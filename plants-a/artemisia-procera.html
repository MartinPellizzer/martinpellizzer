Artemisia procera

Artemisia procera, commonly known as big sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant has small, yellowish-green flower clusters that appear in the spring. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems that help it survive drought conditions.

Artemisia procera plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
