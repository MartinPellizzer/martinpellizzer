Andropogon macrothrix

Andropogon macrothrix, commonly known as tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and blue-green foliage, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed sites, thriving in full sun. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spikelet that matures into a fluffy, white to pale blue mass.

Andropogon macrothrix plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
