Alpinia longipetiola

Alpinia longipetiola, commonly known as the long petiole ginger, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in inflorescences, which bloom during the warmer months. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and subtle fragrance.

Alpinia longipetiola is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
