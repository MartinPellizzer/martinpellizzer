Asparagus virgatus

Asparagus virgatus, commonly known as the Virgin's asparagus, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It features slender, spiny stems that grow close to the ground, often forming a dense mat. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring, which are followed by feathery, pale blue to white bracts. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Asparagus virgatus is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
