Aruncus sylvester f. incisus

Aruncus sylvester f. incisus, commonly known as the cutleaf goat's beard, is a perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its feathery, plume-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features large, deeply lobed, heart-shaped leaves that are often dissected, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
