Asperula tyraica

Asperula tyraica, commonly known as the Turkish bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes. Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Asperula tyraica is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
