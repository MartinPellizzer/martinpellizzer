Anthoxanthum laxiflorum

Anthoxanthum laxiflorum, commonly known as the slender-flowered goatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach up to 60 cm in height, with narrow, linear leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle bearing small, yellowish flowers.

Anthoxanthum laxiflorum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for invertebrates.
