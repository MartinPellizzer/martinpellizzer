Arabis yokoscensis

Arabis yokoscensis, commonly known as the Japanese rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Arabis yokoscensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
