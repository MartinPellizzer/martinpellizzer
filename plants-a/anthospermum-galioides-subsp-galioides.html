Anthospermum galioides subsp. galioides

Anthospermum galioides subsp.

galioides, commonly known as the slender swamp mallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in Southeast Asia. It typically grows in marshy or aquatic environments, showcasing its adaptation to waterlogged soils.

The plant features opposite, linear leaves and produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in racemes. Its root system is fibrous, contributing to its ability to stabilize soil in swampy areas.

It is valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
