Asperula staliana subsp. issaea

Asperula staliana subsp.

issaea, commonly known as the Italian azalea, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in spring.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its colorful blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long flowering period.
