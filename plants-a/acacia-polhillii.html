Acacia polhillii

Acacia polhillii, commonly known as the Polhill's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces long, slender pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Acacia polhillii is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
