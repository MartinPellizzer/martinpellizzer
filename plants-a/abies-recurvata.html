Abies recurvata

Abies recurvata, commonly known as the curl-leaf fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the high elevations of the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and its distinctive, upward-curving needle-like leaves. The tree produces cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its foliage is dense and dark green, with a slightly bluish tinge, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Abies recurvata plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
