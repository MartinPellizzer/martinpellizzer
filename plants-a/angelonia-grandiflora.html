Angelonia grandiflora

Angelonia grandiflora, commonly known as the summer snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pink to red and purple. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Angelonia grandiflora is often used in gardens for its long blooming season and ornamental value.
