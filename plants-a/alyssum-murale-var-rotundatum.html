Alyssum murale var. rotundatum

Alyssum murale var. rotundatum, commonly known as roundleaf alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has rounded, succulent leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a valuable addition to both wild and cultivated landscapes.
