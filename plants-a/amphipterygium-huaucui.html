Amphipterygium huaucui

Amphipterygium huaucui, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a striking flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in native habitats.
