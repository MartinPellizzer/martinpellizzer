Alstonia deplanchei var. deplanchei

Alstonia deplanchei var. deplanchei, commonly known as the Madagascar flame tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces showy, orange-red flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom in the dry season. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its striking floral display.
