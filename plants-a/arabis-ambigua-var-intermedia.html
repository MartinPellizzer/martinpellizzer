Arabis ambigua var. intermedia

Arabis ambigua var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are basal, toothed, and often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
