Argythamnia serrata

Argythamnia serrata, commonly known as the serrated argythamnia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive serrated leaf margins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Argythamnia serrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
