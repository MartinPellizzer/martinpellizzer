Asplenium inaequilaterale var. falciforme

Asplenium inaequilaterale var. falciforme, commonly known as the falcate spleenwort, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive, lance-shaped fronds.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as rock crevices or forest floors. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with closely spaced, often overlapping leaves that give it a dense appearance. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are typically located on the underside of the fronds, arranged in a unique pattern.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in humid, temperate climates.
