Amaranthus gangeticus var. angustifolius

Amaranthus gangeticus var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaf amaranth, is an annual herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 1-2 meters in height, covered with fine, soft hairs. The leaves are elongated and narrow, typically ranging from 5 to 15 centimeters in length, with a bluish-green hue. Its small, greenish flowers are arranged in dense spikes, and the plant produces numerous seeds that are small and triangular in shape.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional cuisine and medicinal practices.
