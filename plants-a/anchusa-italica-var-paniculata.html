Anchusa italica var. paniculata

Anchusa italica var. paniculata, commonly known as Italian bugloss, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its hardiness and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
