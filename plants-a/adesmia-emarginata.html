Adesmia emarginata

Adesmia emarginata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burrobrush, is a perennial shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties, allowing the plant to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant features small, compound leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind and animals.

Adesmia emarginata is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems and a capacity to survive prolonged droughts.
