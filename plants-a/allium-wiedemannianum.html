Allium wiedemannianum

Allium wiedemannianum, commonly known as the Chinese leek or broad-leaved garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features long, narrow, grass-like leaves and produces umbels of white or pale purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its strong garlic scent and is often grown for ornamental purposes. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

Allium wiedemannianum is also valued in some traditional cuisines for its edible leaves and bulbs.
