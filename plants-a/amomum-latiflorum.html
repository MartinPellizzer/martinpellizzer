Amomum latiflorum

Amomum latiflorum, commonly known as the broadflower cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its inflorescence consists of numerous small, pale yellow to white flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom in the summer months.

The rhizomes of Amomum latiflorum are also utilized in culinary and medicinal applications due to their spicy, aromatic properties.
