Amaracarpus lanceolatus

Amaracarpus lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed amara, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
