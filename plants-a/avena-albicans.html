Avena albicans

Avena albicans, commonly known as white oat, is a species of grass native to Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray leaves and compact, whitish inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. Its seeds are small and oval, with a smooth surface, and are used in traditional herbal medicine.

Avena albicans is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
