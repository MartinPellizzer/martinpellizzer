Artanthe lapathifolium

Artanthe lapathifolium, commonly known as the common artanthe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense terminal clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in native plant gardens for their ornamental value and ecological benefits.
