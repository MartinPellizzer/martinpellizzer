Acaena millefolia

Acaena millefolia, commonly known as New Zealand spinach, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces bracts that are often mistaken for leaves, and its fruits are dry, achene-like structures. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Despite its common name, it is not related to true spinach and is primarily grown for its ornamental value.
