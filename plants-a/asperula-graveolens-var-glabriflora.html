Asperula graveolens var. glabriflora

Asperula graveolens var. glabriflora, commonly known as the smooth-flowered sweet pepper plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are often hairy but become smoother with age. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

The variety is noted for its aromatic foliage and compact growth habit, ideal for small garden spaces.
