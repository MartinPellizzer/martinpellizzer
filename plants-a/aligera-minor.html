Aligera minor

Aligera minor, commonly known as the lesser aligera, is a small herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 10-15 centimeters, featuring slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white and green, which bloom in early spring. Aligera minor prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for woodland gardens.

Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its mild sedative properties.
