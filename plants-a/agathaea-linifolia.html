Agathaea linifolia

Agathaea linifolia, commonly known as the silky milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that allow it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils. Its seed pods contain small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize disturbed areas.

Agathaea linifolia is also valued for its role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
