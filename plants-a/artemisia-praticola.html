Artemisia praticola

Artemisia praticola, commonly known as roughleaf sage, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, aromatic foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its strong, minty scent.

Artemisia praticola is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
