Acrotriche

Acrotriche, commonly known as the sandmat, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

These plants are typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils. They are characterized by their erect or sprawling growth habit and often have small, clustered flowers. The leaves are usually succulent, adapted to conserve water in harsh environments.

Acrotriche species are often used in xeriscaping due to their low water requirements and ornamental value.
