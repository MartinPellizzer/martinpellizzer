Adlumia cirrhosa var. elatior

Adlumia cirrhosa var. elatior, commonly known as the greater false Solomon's seal, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with elongated, arching leaflets that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clustered umbels, followed by dark purple berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its foliage and unique flowering habit, which adds visual interest throughout the growing season.
