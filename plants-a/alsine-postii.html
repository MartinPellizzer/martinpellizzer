Alsine postii

Alsine postii, commonly known as the desert sandflower, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has hairy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in its harsh environment. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize disturbed soils and sandy areas.

Alsine postii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and animals.
