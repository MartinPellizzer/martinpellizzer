Antrophyum boryanum

Antrophyum boryanum, commonly known as the Boryanum cycad, is a species of cycad native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feathery leaves. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
