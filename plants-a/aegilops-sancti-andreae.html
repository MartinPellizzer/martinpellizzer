Aegilops sancti-andreae

Aegilops sancti-andreae, commonly known as the Andean goatgrass, is a wild wheat species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow leaves and small, spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils. It produces small, pale yellow flowers that are wind-pollinated, and its seeds are enclosed in tough, dry capsules.

Due to its genetic similarity to cultivated wheat, it is of interest in agricultural research for potential crop improvement.
