Allium hyemale

Allium hyemale, commonly known as the autumn garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces tall, slender stems that bear clusters of small, pale purple to white flowers. The plant's bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn.

Allium hyemale is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
