Arthrostemma ladanoides

Arthrostemma ladanoides, commonly known as the fragrant arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. Its flowers are typically greenish-white with a strong, sweet scent, attracting pollinators such as beetles and flies.

Arthrostemma ladanoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
