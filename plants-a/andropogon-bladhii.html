Andropogon bladhii

Andropogon bladhii, commonly known as bladh's switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy and adaptable species.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for wildlife, as well as its potential use in bioenergy production.
