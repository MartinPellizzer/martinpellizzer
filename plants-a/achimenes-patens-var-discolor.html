Achimenes patens var. discolor

Achimenes patens var. discolor, commonly known as the variegated flame violet, is a hybrid species of the Achimenes genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its strikingly colorful, tubular flowers that range from red to orange and yellow, often with contrasting patterns. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

The variegation in its foliage adds to its visual appeal, making it a popular choice among plant enthusiasts.
