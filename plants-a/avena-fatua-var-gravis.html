Avena fatua var. gravis

Avena fatua var. gravis, commonly known as wild oat, is a grass species native to Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue or greenish flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and competitive nature, it is considered a weed in many cultivated areas.
