Acronia cordifolia

Acronia cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf acronia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Acronia cordifolia is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
