Alpinia purpurata var. albobracteata

Alpinia purpurata var. albobracteata, commonly known as the white bract ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring large, showy inflorescences with bright red flowers surrounded by white bracts. The plant has strap-shaped, dark green leaves that are glossy and lanceolate in shape. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-draining soil.

Alpinia purpurata var. albobracteata is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a decorative plant.
