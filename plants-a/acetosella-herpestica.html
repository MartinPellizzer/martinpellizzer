Acetosella herpestica

Acetosella herpestica, commonly known as lesser wood sorrel, is a small perennial herb belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of delicate, yellow flowers. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves contain oxalic acid, which gives them a slightly tart taste.

The plant is often found in acidic soils and is known for its ability to form dense mats in suitable habitats.
