Allium amblyophyllum

Allium amblyophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces edible bulbs that are similar in taste to garlic but are less commonly cultivated. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary potential, it is occasionally grown in gardens and herb collections.
