Amomum renealmia

Amomum renealmia, commonly known as black cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, dark reddish-brown berries that are harvested when fully mature. These berries are widely used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their aromatic and warming properties.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both culinary and medicinal applications.
