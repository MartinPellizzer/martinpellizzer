Aeschynomene pratensis var. pratensis

Aeschynomene pratensis var. pratensis, commonly known as white clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. This species is native to Europe, Asia, and Africa but has been introduced to many other regions. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils.

The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important component of agricultural ecosystems.
