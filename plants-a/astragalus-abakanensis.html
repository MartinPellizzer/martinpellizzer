Astragalus abakanensis

Astragalus abakanensis, commonly known as the Abakan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically followed by cylindrical seed pods. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

Astragalus abakanensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
