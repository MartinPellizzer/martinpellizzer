Acacia dissona

Acacia dissona, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, feathery leaves and produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities, which enriches the soil. Acacia dissona is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resistance to drought and poor soil conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
