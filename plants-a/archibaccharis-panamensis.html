Archibaccharis panamensis

Archibaccharis panamensis, commonly known as the Panama baccharis, is a shrub native to Central America, particularly Panama.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowering period occurs during the dry season.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
