Acanthotheca integrifolia

Acanthotheca integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf acanthotheca, is a species of flowering plant in the family Acanthaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its simple, smooth leaves and showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercialized.
