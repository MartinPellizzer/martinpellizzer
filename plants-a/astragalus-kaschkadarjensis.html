Astragalus kaschkadarjensis

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in parts of Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus kaschkadarjensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.
