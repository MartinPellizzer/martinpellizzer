Aloysia scorodonioides var. mathewsii

Aloysia scorodonioides var. mathewsii, commonly known as the Mathews' verbena, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It features slender, aromatic leaves with a mint-like scent and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple and blue. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its hardiness and drought tolerance.
