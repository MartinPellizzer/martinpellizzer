Acacia magnibracteosa

Acacia magnibracteosa, commonly known as the large-bract acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are divided into numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are followed by long, flat pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Acacia magnibracteosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in degraded landscapes.
