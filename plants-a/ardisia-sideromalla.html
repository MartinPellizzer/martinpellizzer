Ardisia sideromalla

Ardisia sideromalla, commonly known as the ironwood ardisia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by bright red berries. The plant has a dense, compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal. Botanically, it belongs to the family Ericaceae and is valued for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Its wood is dense and strong, contributing to its common name "ironwood.".
