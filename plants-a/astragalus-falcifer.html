Astragalus falcifer

Astragalus falcifer, commonly known as the sicklepod milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in dry environments. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
