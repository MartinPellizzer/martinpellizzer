Andropogon melanocarpus

Andropogon melanocarpus, commonly known as black grama, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its dark-colored, hardy seeds and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie and steppe ecosystems. Black grama plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its drought tolerance makes it an important component of native grassland vegetation.
