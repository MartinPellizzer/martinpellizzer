Aronia prisca

Aronia prisca, commonly known as the ancient chokeberry, is a species of shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves and small, red berries that ripen in the fall. The plant is known for its hardy nature, thriving in a variety of soil types and climates. Its berries are rich in antioxidants and have been used historically for both culinary and medicinal purposes.

Aronia prisca is also valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
