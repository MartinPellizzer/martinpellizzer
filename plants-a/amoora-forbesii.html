Amoora forbesii

Amoora forbesii, commonly known as the white-veined arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with prominent white veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often hidden within a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. Its rhizomes are used as a starchy food source, similar to taro.

Amoora forbesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in various cultures.
