Acoridium auritum

Acoridium auritum, commonly known as the golden ear orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that resemble ears, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, often with a waxy surface.

Acoridium auritum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
