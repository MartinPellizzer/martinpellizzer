Astragalus glycyphyllos subsp. glycyphylloides

Astragalus glycyphyllos subsp.

glycyphylloides, commonly known as the sweet carline, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and the pods are slender, containing several small, rounded seeds.
