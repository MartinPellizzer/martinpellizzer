Aeschynomene rubroviolacea

Aeschynomene rubroviolacea, commonly known as the red-violet sesban, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, purple to red flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.
