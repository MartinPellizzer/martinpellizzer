Amaranthus glaucus

Amaranthus glaucus, commonly known as blue amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bluish-white coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the stage of bloom. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible leaves.

Amaranthus glaucus is also known for its drought tolerance and ability to grow in poor soil conditions.
