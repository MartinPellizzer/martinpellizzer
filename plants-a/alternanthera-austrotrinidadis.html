Alternanthera austrotrinidadis

Alternanthera austrotrinidadis, commonly known as the Trinidadian Alternanthera, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Trinidad and Venezuela.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its strikingly colored foliage, often displaying vibrant red, pink, or purple hues. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters, with slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

Its flowers are inconspicuous, clustered in dense inflorescences, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in aquatic and terrestrial gardens.
