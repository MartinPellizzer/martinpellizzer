Arabidopsis thaliana var. apetala

Arabidopsis thaliana var. apetala, commonly known as the flowerless thale cress, is a variant of the widely studied model plant Arabidopsis thaliana.

This variant is notable for its lack of functional flowers, which results from mutations in genes responsible for floral development. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with small, elongated leaves that are typically green and serrated. It retains the genetic simplicity and short life cycle of its parent species, making it valuable for genetic research.

Despite its flowerless appearance, it shares many biological features with other Arabidopsis species, contributing to its utility in plant science.
