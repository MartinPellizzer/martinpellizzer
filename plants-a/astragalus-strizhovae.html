Astragalus strizhovae

Astragalus strizhovae, commonly known as Strizhovae milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

It is adapted to dry, rocky environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
