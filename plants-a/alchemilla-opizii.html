Alchemilla opizii

Alchemilla opizii, commonly known as the Opiz's lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of rounded, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Alchemilla opizii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
