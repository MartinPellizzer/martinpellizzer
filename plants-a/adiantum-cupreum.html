Adiantum cupreum

Adiantum cupreum, commonly known as the copper maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to the eastern United States.

It features finely divided, bright green, feather-like fronds that are often tinged with a reddish or copper hue, especially in the younger leaves. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its fronds are typically about 15 to 30 centimeters long and have a graceful, arching appearance.

Adiantum cupreum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
