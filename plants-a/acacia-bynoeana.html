Acacia bynoeana

Acacia bynoeana, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers, which are clustered in spherical heads, and its bipinnate leaves that are compound and feathery. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils. It plays an important role in the ecosystem, providing food and habitat for various native animals.

The golden wattle is also the national floral emblem of Australia, symbolizing the country's natural heritage.
