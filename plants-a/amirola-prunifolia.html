Amirola prunifolia

Amirola prunifolia, commonly known as the cherryleaf plant, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2-5 meters. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often oval or elliptic in shape, with a smooth, waxy surface. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and appear in clusters, followed by small, edible berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
