Apteranthes gussoneana

Apteranthes gussoneana, commonly known as the Gussone's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture.

Apteranthes gussoneana is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, where it thrives in full sun.
