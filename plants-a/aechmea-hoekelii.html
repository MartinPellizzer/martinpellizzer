Aechmea hoekelii

Aechmea hoekelii, commonly known as the Hoekel's bromeliad, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces striking inflorescences with brightly colored flowers, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. Its foliage is typically broad and waxy, with a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Aechmea hoekelii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
