Allmania

Allmania, commonly known as the common allmania, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Amaryllidaceae.

It is characterized by its bulbous root system and strap-shaped leaves that emerge from the base. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Allmania is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.
