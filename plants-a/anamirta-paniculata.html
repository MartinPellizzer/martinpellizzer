Anamirta paniculata

Anamirta paniculata, commonly known as the water spinach or kangkong, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender, succulent stems that are often used in culinary applications. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, and its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and slightly hairy. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by small, round fruits containing seeds.

Anamirta paniculata is valued not only for its nutritional content but also for its role in traditional medicine and as a water purifier.
