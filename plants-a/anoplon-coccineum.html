Anoplon coccineum

Anoplon coccineum, commonly known as the red-flowered anoplon, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a splash of color to gardens and natural habitats. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Anoplon coccineum is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
