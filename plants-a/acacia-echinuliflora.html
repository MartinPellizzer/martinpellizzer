Acacia echinuliflora

Acacia echinuliflora, commonly known as the spiny-flowered acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny branches and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval leaflets. The plant produces yellow, globular inflorescences that are densely packed with tiny, fluffy flowers. Acacia echinuliflora thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable for soil enrichment in degraded landscapes.
