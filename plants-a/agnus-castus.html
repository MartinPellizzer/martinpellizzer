Agnus-castus

Agnus-castus, commonly known as the chastetree, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiny leaves and clusters of small, reddish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces dark, woody berries that contain seeds used in traditional herbal remedies. Agnus-castus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history in herbal medicine.

Its botanical name, *Vitex agnus-castus*, reflects its historical and cultural significance.
