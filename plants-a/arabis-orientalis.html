Arabis orientalis

Arabis orientalis, commonly known as the oriental rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in clusters. Arabis orientalis thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
