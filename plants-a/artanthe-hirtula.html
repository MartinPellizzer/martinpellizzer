Artanthe hirtula

Artanthe hirtula, commonly known as the hairy artanthe, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Artanthe hirtula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
