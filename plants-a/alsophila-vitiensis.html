Alsophila vitiensis

Alsophila vitiensis, commonly known as the Vitiensis tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Fiji.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizomes are fibrous and contribute to its structural support, while its fronds are often used in traditional practices.

Alsophila vitiensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
