Acer pseudoplatanus f. cupreum

Acer pseudoplatanus f. cupreum, commonly known as the copperleaf maple, is a rare and striking variant of the common maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves that display a unique coppery or bronze coloration during the growing season. This form is a natural variant or cultivar. often found in specific geographic regions where the climate supports its growth. The leaves are typically lobed, with a serrated margin, and the tree produces small, clustered flowers in early spring.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for botanical gardens and specialized collections.
