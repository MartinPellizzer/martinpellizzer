Aria decipiens

Aria decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often mistaken for other similar species, contributing to its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Aria decipiens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
