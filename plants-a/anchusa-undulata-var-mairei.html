Anchusa undulata var. mairei

Anchusa undulata var. mairei, commonly known as the Maire's heartsease, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring, often in clusters at the ends of the stems. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
