Arkezostis cordifolia

Arkezostis cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf arkezostis, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Arkezostis cordifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
