Astragalus arrectus

Astragalus arrectus, commonly known as the erect milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to greenish flowers that are clustered in racemes. Astragalus arrectus is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
