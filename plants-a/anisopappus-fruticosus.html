Anisopappus fruticosus

Anisopappus fruticosus, commonly known as the desert aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky slopes. The plant has erect, branching stems covered with fine hairs, and its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green in color. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense heads, and often bloom in shades of yellow to orange.

The plant is adapted to survive in harsh conditions with minimal water, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
