Agave galvaniae

Agave galvaniae, commonly known as the Galvania agave, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears yellowish-green flowers, which bloom during the spring or early summer. It is adapted to thrive in hot, dry climates with well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in desert gardens.
