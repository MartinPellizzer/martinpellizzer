Aconitum amurense

Aconitum amurense, commonly known as Chinese aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant produces toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its foliage consists of palmately compound leaves with serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
