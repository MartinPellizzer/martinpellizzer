Alternanthera bettzickiana

Alternanthera bettzickiana, commonly known as the red flame plant, is a strikingly colorful aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, pink, or orange foliage, which becomes more intense under strong lighting conditions. The plant typically grows in dense clusters, with slender stems that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. It thrives in moderate to high light and requires regular fertilization to maintain its vivid coloration.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular in aquariums and water gardens.
