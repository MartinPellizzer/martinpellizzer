Aristolochia krausei

Aristolochia krausei, commonly known as the Krause's Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are often greenish or reddish in color. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and has a strong, often unpleasant odor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The genus Aristolochia is known for its complex chemical compounds, some of which have medicinal properties.
