Astragalus subkahiricus

Astragalus subkahiricus, commonly known as Kahir's milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow to greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan.

Astragalus subkahiricus is adapted to drought conditions and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
