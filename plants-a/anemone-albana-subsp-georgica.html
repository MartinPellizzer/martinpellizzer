Anemone albana subsp. georgica

Anemone albana subsp.

georgica, commonly known as the Georgian white anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It features showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May.

The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
