Acacia ramosissima

Acacia ramosissima, commonly known as the screwpod acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and phyllodes that are often twisted or coiled, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, followed by distinctive pod-like fruits that are spiral-shaped. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Acacia ramosissima is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which enriches the soil and supports other plant life.
