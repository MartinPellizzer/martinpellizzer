Acalypha benensis

Acalypha benensis, commonly known as the Beninese spurge, is a shrub native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are often surrounded by bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Acalypha benensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local communities.
