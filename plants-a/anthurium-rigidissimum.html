Anthurium rigidissimum

Anthurium rigidissimum, commonly known as the stiff anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, spadix-shaped inflorescence surrounded by a brightly colored spathe. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Anthurium rigidissimum prefers warm, humid conditions and is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value.
