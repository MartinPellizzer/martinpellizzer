Arisaema angustifoliolatum

Arisaema angustifoliolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features a distinctive, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a hooded spathe, typical of the Araceae family. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a prominent midrib, and its underground tuber is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant's botanical structure reflects its adaptation to humid, tropical climates.
