Acacia minutissima

Acacia minutissima, commonly known as the dwarf acacia or small-leaved acacia, is a small shrub native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Acacia minutissima plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert animals.
