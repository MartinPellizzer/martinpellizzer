Adesmia propinqua

Adesmia propinqua, commonly known as the small-flowered legume, is a small shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Its botanical structure supports its role in ecological systems, providing habitat and forage for local wildlife.
