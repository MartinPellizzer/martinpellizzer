Atriplex pharaonis

Atriplex pharaonis, commonly known as the desert saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its succulent leaves, which are adapted to retain water in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its salt-tolerant nature allows it to thrive in soils with high salinity.

Atriplex pharaonis plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing forage for wildlife.
