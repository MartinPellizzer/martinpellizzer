Abies taxifolia

Abies taxifolia, commonly known as the Japanese fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged spirally along the branches. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, and it is often found in mountainous regions with cool, moist climates. Its foliage provides habitat for various wildlife species, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

The Japanese fir is also known for its ability to thrive in acidic soils and its resistance to certain pests and diseases.
