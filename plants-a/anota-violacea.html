Anota violacea

Anota violacea, commonly known as the violet bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to blue. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Anota violacea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
