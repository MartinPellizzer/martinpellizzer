Astragalus ancistrocarpus subsp. gudrunensis

Astragalus ancistrocarpus subsp.

gudrunensis, commonly known as Gudrun's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has small, elliptical pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
