Asphodelus bakeri

Asphodelus bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's asphodel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asphodelaceae and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and erect, spike-like flower clusters. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Asphodelus bakeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
