Aspidosperma discolor

Aspidosperma discolor, commonly known as the yellow aspidosperma, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries containing seeds. Its leaves and bark contain various alkaloids, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

The tree is often found in dry forests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
