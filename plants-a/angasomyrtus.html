Angasomyrtus

Angasomyrtus, commonly known as the Australian bottlebrush, is a genus of flowering plants native to Australia.

It belongs to the myrtle family (Myrtaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, brush-like inflorescences. The plants typically have aromatic leaves and produce small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to red. Angasomyrtus species are often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in horticulture for their unique floral displays.
