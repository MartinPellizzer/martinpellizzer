Anthemis suffruticosa

Anthemis suffruticosa, commonly known as rough anthemis, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features wiry, branched stems that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish to golden flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in a dense layer of fine, white to greyish hairs.

Anthemis suffruticosa is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or arid landscapes.
