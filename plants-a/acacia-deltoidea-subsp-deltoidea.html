Acacia deltoidea subsp. deltoidea

Acacia deltoidea subsp.

deltoidea, commonly known as the weeping boerkea, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, drooping, feathery foliage that gives it a unique appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

The leaves are compound, consisting of small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged in pairs.
