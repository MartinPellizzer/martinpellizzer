Alchemilla micans f. pratensis

Alchemilla micans f. pratensis, commonly known as the silverweed or lesser alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive, textured appearance.
