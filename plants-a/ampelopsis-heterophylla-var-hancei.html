Ampelopsis heterophylla var. hancei

Ampelopsis heterophylla var. hancei, commonly known as the Hance's broadleaf grape, is a climbing vine native to China.

It features compound leaves that vary in shape, with the upper leaves typically broader than the lower ones. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by blue-black berries that are edible when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and berries, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
