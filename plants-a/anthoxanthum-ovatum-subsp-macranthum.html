Anthoxanthum ovatum subsp. macranthum

Anthoxanthum ovatum subsp.

macranthum, commonly known as the broad-leaved oatgrass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing yellow to brown florets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

Its botanical structure makes it a valuable species for ecological studies and habitat restoration projects.
