Andropogon impressus

Andropogon impressus, commonly known as the flatleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along creek banks. The plant has narrow, flat leaves with a distinctive blue-green color, and its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, white to bluish flowers. It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Andropogon impressus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ecological value.
