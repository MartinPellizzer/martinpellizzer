Anthurium upalaense

Anthurium upalaense, commonly known as the Upala arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive spatulate leaves and unique inflorescence. The plant produces striking, colorful spathes that can range from red to orange, often with a contrasting midrib. Its foliage is typically broad and arrow-shaped, contributing to its ornamental value.

Anthurium upalaense thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in indoor settings.
