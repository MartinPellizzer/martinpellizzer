Avena × vilis

Avena × vilis, commonly known as intermediate oat, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Avena sativa (common oat) and Avena barbata (barbed oat).

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that bear small, two-rowed flowers, which develop into oval, dark brown seeds. Avena × vilis is often found in disturbed soils and is cultivated for its forage value.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in agricultural and ecological contexts.
