Amphiblestra

Amphiblestra, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, blue or purple ray florets and spiny, bract-like leaves. Native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean, it thrives in sandy or rocky soils. The plant's unique structure allows it to withstand salt spray and strong winds.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for coastal gardens and rockeries.
