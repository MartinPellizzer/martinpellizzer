Acalypha insulana var. pubescens

Acalypha insulana var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy ironwood, is a shrub native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered with fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the variety. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping and for erosion control in tropical regions.
