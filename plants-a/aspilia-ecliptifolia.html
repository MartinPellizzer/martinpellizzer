Aspilia ecliptifolia

Aspilia ecliptifolia, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features bright yellow flowers that are arranged in a single head, surrounded by bracts that resemble leaves. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and have a distinctive basal rosette arrangement.

Aspilia ecliptifolia is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
