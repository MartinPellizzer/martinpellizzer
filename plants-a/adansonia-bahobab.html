Adansonia bahobab

Adansonia bahobab, commonly known as the baobab tree, is a member of the Malvaceae family and is native to Madagascar.

It is a large, deciduous tree characterized by its thick, bottle-shaped trunk and extensive root system. The tree can grow up to 25 meters tall and has thick, waxy leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or white, and it produces large, woody fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The bark is fibrous and has been used by local communities for various purposes, including rope-making and medicinal uses.
