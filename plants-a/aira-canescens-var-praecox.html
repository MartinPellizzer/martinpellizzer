Aira canescens var. praecox

Aira canescens var. praecox, commonly known as early bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and tufted inflorescence that appears early in the growing season. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and prairies. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
