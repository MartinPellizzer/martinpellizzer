Acer platanoides f. albovariegatum

Acer platanoides f. albovariegatum, commonly known as the white-variegated Norway maple, is a cultivar of the Norway maple tree.

It is characterized by its striking white and green variegated leaves, which provide a unique ornamental appearance. The plant is a deciduous tree with a broad, spreading canopy and heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This cultivar is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
