Anthoxanthum madagascariense

Anthoxanthum madagascariense, commonly known as the Madagascan oxalis, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish inflorescences that are often clustered at the base of the stems. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a delicate appearance.

Anthoxanthum madagascariense is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as savannas and rocky outcrops.
