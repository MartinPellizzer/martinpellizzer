Arisaema penicillatum

Arisaema penicillatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf arrowroot, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. The plant features a stout rhizome with a distinctive, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Its leaves are narrow and arrow-shaped, typically ranging from 10 to 30 centimeters in length.

Arisaema penicillatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
