Aegilops triuncialis

Aegilops triuncialis, commonly known as the wild wheat or goat grass, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to reddish florets. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Aegilops triuncialis has historical significance in the domestication of wheat and is considered a close relative of modern wheat species.

Morphology

Aegilops triuncialis has alternate, narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually bluish-green in color, with a linear shape and a rough texture.

The stem is cylindrical, with a smooth, reddish-brown bark, and grows upright with a branching pattern. Its flowers are spike-like, composed of small, greenish-white florets, and bloom in early summer; they are primarily wind-pollinated. The fruit is a caryopsis, and the seeds are dispersed by wind due to their light weight and small size.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, allowing the plant to thrive in disturbed soils.
