Arrhenatherum album var. montanum

Arrhenatherum album var. montanum, commonly known as mountain false oat, is a perennial grass species native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, leafless stems that bear panicle-like inflorescences with white to pale green florets. Its foliage is characterized by narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in mountainous habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
