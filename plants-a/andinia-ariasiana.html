Andinia ariasiana

Andinia ariasiana, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet or blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and has a compact, herbaceous growth habit. Its leaves are often hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Andinia ariasiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
