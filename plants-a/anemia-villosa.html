Anemia villosa

Anemia villosa, commonly known as the hairy anemone, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of North America.

It features a short, thick rhizome and produces basal leaves that are typically hairy and lobed. The plant blooms with showy, pink to red flowers that have five to seven petals and numerous stamens. Its flowers are typically borne on long, slender stems that rise above the foliage.

Anemia villosa thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
