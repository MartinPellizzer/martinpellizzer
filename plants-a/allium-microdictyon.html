Allium microdictyon

Allium microdictyon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures similar to garlic, which are used for culinary and medicinal purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Allium microdictyon is valued for its aromatic properties and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine.
