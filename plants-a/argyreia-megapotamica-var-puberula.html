Argyreia megapotamica var. puberula

Argyreia megapotamica var. puberula, commonly known as the velvet flower or Brazilian nightshade, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to almost black flowers, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has velvety, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

However, it contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
