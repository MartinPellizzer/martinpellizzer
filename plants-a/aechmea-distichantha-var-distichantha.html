Aechmea distichantha var. distichantha

Aechmea distichantha var. distichantha, commonly known as the silver pine bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The leaves are typically long, narrow, and covered with a silvery-gray sheen, giving the plant its common name. It produces tall, branched flower spikes that bear white or pale pink flowers, which bloom for several weeks.

This plant thrives in bright, indirect light and is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
