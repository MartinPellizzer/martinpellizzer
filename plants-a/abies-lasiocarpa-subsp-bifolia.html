Abies lasiocarpa subsp. bifolia

Abies lasiocarpa subsp.

bifolia, commonly known as the white fir with bifid leaves, is a subspecies of the white fir native to the western United States. It is characterized by its distinctive, sharply toothed, bifid (two-lobed) leaves that give it a unique appearance.

The plant typically grows in high elevation forests, often in association with other coniferous species. Its cones are cylindrical and maturing in the second year, releasing seeds in the fall.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in mountainous ecosystems.
