Alsobia antirrhina

Alsobia antirrhina, commonly known as the pink fairy lantern, is a flowering plant native to the Cape Province of South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble small lanterns. The plant produces clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers that are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Alsobia antirrhina is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and unique appearance.
