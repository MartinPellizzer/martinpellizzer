Aloe sabaea

Aloe sabaea, commonly known as the Socotra aloe, is a succulent plant native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often ribbed and covered in a white, powdery substance. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Aloe sabaea is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its leaves to survive in dry conditions.

It is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
