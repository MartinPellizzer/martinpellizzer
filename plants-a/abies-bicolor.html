Abies bicolor

Abies bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive bicolored foliage, with upper foliage appearing silver-white and lower foliage a dark green. The tree typically grows to a height of 20 to 40 meters, forming a conical shape with a dense crown. Its needles are flat, soft, and arranged in spirals along the branches, while its cones are cylindrical and typically measure 5 to 8 centimeters in length.

The species is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments and is often found in subalpine and alpine forests.
