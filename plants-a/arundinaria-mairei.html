Arundinaria mairei

Arundinaria mairei, commonly known as the Maire bamboo, is a species of grass native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces panicles of small, greenish-white flowers, which are typically borne on long, drooping branches. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Arundinaria mairei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
