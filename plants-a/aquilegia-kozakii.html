Aquilegia kozakii

Aquilegia kozakii, commonly known as the Kozak's columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features distinctive spurred petals that are typically violet or blue in color, arranged in a showy, bell-shaped flower cluster. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces bright red or orange seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Aquilegia kozakii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
