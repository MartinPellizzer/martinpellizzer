Aretia longiflora

Aretia longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered aretia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, delicate flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in a fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
