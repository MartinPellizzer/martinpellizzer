Astragalus burkeanus var. randii

Astragalus burkeanus var. randii, commonly known as the Burke's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as gravelly soils and open woodlands. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This species is part of the legume family (Fabaceae) and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.
