Albizia sessilis

Albizia sessilis, commonly known as the sessile acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow or white flowers arranged in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it often grows in coastal areas or sandy soils.

Albizia sessilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
