Aster occidentalis var. yosemitanus

Aster occidentalis var. yosemitanus, commonly known as the Yosemite aster, is a flowering plant native to the Sierra Nevada mountain range in California.

It is characterized by its showy blue to purple flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. This perennial herb typically grows in moist meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It plays an important ecological role in its native environment by supporting local wildlife and contributing to the biodiversity of alpine ecosystems.
