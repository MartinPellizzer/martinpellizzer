Alsophila × elliottii

Alsophila × elliottii, commonly known as the Elliott's tree fern, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Alsophila costaricana and Alsophila spinulosa.

It is a tall, evergreen tree fern characterized by its large, feathery fronds and a sturdy, fibrous trunk. Native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia, it thrives in warm, moist environments with partial shade. The plant's fronds are typically bioluminescent and can reach lengths of up to 3 meters.

Alsophila × elliottii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
