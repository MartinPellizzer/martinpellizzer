Astragalus alpinus subsp. alpinus

Astragalus alpinus subsp.

alpinus, commonly known as the alpine milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems and small, yellowish to reddish flowers.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its root system is typically deep and fibrous. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. Astragalus alpinus subsp.

alpinus is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at high elevations.
