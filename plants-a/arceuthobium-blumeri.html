Arceuthobium blumeri

Arceuthobium blumeri, commonly known as the dwarf mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to the stems of various coniferous trees.

It lacks true roots and instead absorbs nutrients and water through its haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and berries that are dispersed by birds. It is often found in forested areas where its host trees thrive.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly impact the growth and health of its host plants.
