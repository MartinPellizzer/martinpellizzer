Asclepias incarnata subsp. pulchra

Asclepias incarnata subsp.

pulchra, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a striking perennial plant native to the southeastern United States. It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of pink to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant is characterized by its opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is a valuable host plant for monarch butterfly larvae.

Its showy flowers also attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
