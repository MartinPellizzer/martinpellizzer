Allium dshungaricum

Allium dshungaricum, commonly known as the Siberian leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and Central Asia.

It features a bulbous stem that is typically white or pale yellow, with a strong garlic-like odor. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, growing from the base of the plant.

Allium dshungaricum is valued for its culinary uses and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
