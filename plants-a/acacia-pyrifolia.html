Acacia pyrifolia

Acacia pyrifolia, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has feathery, bipinnate leaves that are often shed during dry periods. Its bark is smooth and greyish, with a flaky texture that peels away in strips.

Acacia pyrifolia is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient and ornamental species in native gardens.
