Astragalus miser var. miser

Astragalus miser var. miser, commonly known as the hoary vetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, yellowish to brownish pea-like flowers. The plant produces cylindrical, brown pods that contain several kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Astragalus miser var. miser is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with limited water availability.
