Allowissadula sessei

Allowissadula sessei, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant produces violet-colored berries that contain the seeds. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

Allowissadula sessei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooming habit.
