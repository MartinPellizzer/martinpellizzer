Armoracia lapathifolia

Armoracia lapathifolia, commonly known as the lesser hogweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as carrots and parsley. The plant features a robust, hollow stem that is often reddish in color and covered with fine hairs. Its leaves are large, deeply lobed, and have a distinctive, somewhat hairy texture.

The small white flowers are arranged in umbrella-like clusters, typical of the Apiaceae family.
