Acalypha brevipes

Acalypha brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed beautyberry, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, clustered purple berries that appear in late summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Acalypha brevipes is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
