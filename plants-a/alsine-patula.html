Alsine patula

Alsine patula, commonly known as narrowleaf stormgrass, is a low-growing annual plant native to the western United States.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and has slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in dry, open areas such as roadsides and fields.

Alsine patula is known for its adaptability to arid environments and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
