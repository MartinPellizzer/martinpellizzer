Acer sinense var. pubinerve

Acer sinense var. pubinerve, commonly known as the Chinese maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The plant has smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by samaras with feathery wings.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal color changes.
