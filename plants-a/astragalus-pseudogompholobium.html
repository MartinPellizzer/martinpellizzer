Astragalus pseudogompholobium

Astragalus pseudogompholobium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically borne on the upper parts of the stems. Its pods are narrow and oblong, containing several seeds.

Native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia, it is adapted to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.
