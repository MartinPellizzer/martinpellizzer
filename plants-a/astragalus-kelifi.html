Astragalus kelifi

Astragalus kelifi, commonly known as Kelifi milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Astragalus kelifi is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ecological role includes nitrogen fixation, which enriches the soil in its native habitats.
