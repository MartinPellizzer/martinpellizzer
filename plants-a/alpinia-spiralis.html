Alpinia spiralis

Alpinia spiralis, commonly known as the spiral ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spiraling inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. Its flowers are small and fragrant, typically ranging in color from white to pink.

Alpinia spiralis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil and partial shade.
