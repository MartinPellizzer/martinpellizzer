Amyema rigidiflora

Amyema rigidiflora, commonly known as the rigid-flowered amyema, is a member of the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of northern Australia, where it thrives in wetland and swampy environments. The plant is characterized by its stiff, often woody stems and small, clustered leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive, fragrant aroma.

Amyema rigidiflora plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various native wildlife species.
