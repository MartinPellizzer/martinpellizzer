Anthurium parvispathum

Anthurium parvispathum, commonly known as the dwarf arrowhead plant, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are often dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces striking spadices, which are the inflorescences consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Anthurium parvispathum is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
