Apterigia valerianoides

Apterigia valerianoides, commonly known as the blue-eyed weed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early autumn. The plant has slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks.

Apterigia valerianoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
