Astragalus campylanthus f. leucanthus

Astragalus campylanthus f. leucanthus, commonly known as the white-flowered milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are typically pea-shaped and arranged in racemes. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of western North America, including parts of California and Nevada.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in poor soils, making it a hardy addition to native plant gardens.
