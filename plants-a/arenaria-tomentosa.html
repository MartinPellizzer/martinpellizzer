Arenaria tomentosa

Arenaria tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has hairy, silver-green foliage that helps it retain moisture in harsh, cold environments. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in tundra and rocky habitats.

Arenaria tomentosa plays a role in stabilizing soil in extreme environments and is adapted to withstand strong winds and freezing temperatures.
