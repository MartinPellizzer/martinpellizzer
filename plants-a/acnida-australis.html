Acnida australis

Acnida australis, commonly known as the Australian acacia, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for native pollinators.

Its growth habit and ecological significance make it an important component of its native habitat.
