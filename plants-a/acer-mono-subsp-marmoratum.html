Acer mono subsp. marmoratum

Acer mono subsp.

marmoratum, commonly known as the marble maple, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Himalayas. It is characterized by its distinctive mottled leaves that display a mix of green, white, and red hues, especially during the growing season.

The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and have a serrated margin. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
