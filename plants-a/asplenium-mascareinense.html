Asplenium mascareinense

Asplenium mascareinense, commonly known as the Mascarene spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern native to the Mascarene Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It typically grows on tree trunks or rocky surfaces, thriving in humid, shaded environments. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its reproductive structures, including spore-bearing sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium mascareinense is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitats.
