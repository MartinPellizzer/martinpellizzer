Aconitum illinitum

Aconitum illinitum, commonly known as the Illinois aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features palmately divided leaves and produces showy, spikelike inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

However, it is highly toxic, containing potent alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
