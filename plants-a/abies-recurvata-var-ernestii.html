Abies recurvata var. ernestii

Abies recurvata var. ernestii, commonly known as the Ernestii fir, is a rare and unique variant of the recurvata fir.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often twisted and pendulous branches, which give it an unusual appearance. The foliage is typically dense and needle-like, with a bluish-green hue that becomes more pronounced in sunlight. This variety is native to specific regions in the western United States, where it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
