Achillea baldaccii

Achillea baldaccii, commonly known as the Balkan yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, while its flowers are white to pale pink, blooming in summer.

Achillea baldaccii is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
