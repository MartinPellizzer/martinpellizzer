Acanthopanax delavayi

Acanthopanax delavayi, commonly known as the Yunnan five-leaf ginseng, is a climbing shrub native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which typically consist of five to seven leaflets, and its distinctive yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, red berries that are a source of food for various wildlife species. It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits, including its use as an adaptogen.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, Acanthopanax delavayi is considered an endangered species.
