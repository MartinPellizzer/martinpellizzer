Austrocylindropuntia rossiana

Austrocylindropuntia rossiana, commonly known as the Ross's cylinder cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Argentina.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping in dry climates.
