Atriplex repanda

Atriplex repanda, commonly known as the broadleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. The plant has a branched stem system that allows it to spread and colonize disturbed soils effectively. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, smooth seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Atriplex repanda is well adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
