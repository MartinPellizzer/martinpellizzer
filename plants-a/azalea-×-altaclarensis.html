Azalea × altaclarensis

Azalea × altaclarensis, commonly known as the Altamira azalea, is a hybrid shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, funnel-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and thrives in acidic, well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Azalea × altaclarensis blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of pink to red flowers that attract pollinators.
