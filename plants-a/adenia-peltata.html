Adenia peltata

Adenia peltata, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by berries that are typically orange or red when mature. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Adenia peltata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and unique fruit.
