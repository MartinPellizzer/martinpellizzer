Anisophyllum glaucophyllum

Anisophyllum glaucophyllum, commonly known as the blue-green anisophyllum, is a member of the Polygonaceae family.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant is characterized by its glaucous, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, clustered in dense, greenish-white inflorescences.

Anisophyllum glaucophyllum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant found in regions with limited moisture.
