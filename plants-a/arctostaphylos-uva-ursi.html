Arctostaphylos uva-ursi

Arctostaphylos uva-ursi, commonly known as bearberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to North America.

It features small, leathery leaves that are typically evergreen and emit a faint, aromatic scent. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for wildlife, including bears. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and heathland environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and adaptability to harsh conditions.
