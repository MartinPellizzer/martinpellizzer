Achillea sylvatica

Achillea sylvatica, commonly known as mountain yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It features pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are narrow, gray-green, and covered in fine hairs.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and has a long history of cultivation for ornamental purposes.
