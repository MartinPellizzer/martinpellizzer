Alyssum schemachense

Alyssum schemachense, commonly known as the desert alyssum, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

Alyssum schemachense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging soils.
