Amaranthus blitoides

Amaranthus blitoides, commonly known as the blitter amaranth, is an annual herb native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its branched, hairless stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped with a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that develop into three-angled, dry fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Amaranthus blitoides is known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
