Astripomoea malvacea var. involuta

Astripomoea malvacea var. involuta, commonly known as the curled mallows, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, spirally curled leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
