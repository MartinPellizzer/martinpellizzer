Atriplex verna

Atriplex verna, commonly known as spring saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in sandy or saline soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Atriplex verna is adapted to withstand drought and high salinity, making it an important species in xerophytic ecosystems.
