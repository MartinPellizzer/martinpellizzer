Actinomeris squarrosa

Actinomeris squarrosa, commonly known as the hairy bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant belonging to the family Lentibulariaceae.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures called bladders, which it uses to trap and digest aquatic invertebrates. The plant is adapted to nutrient-poor waters, relying on its prey for essential nutrients.
