Aegopodium angelicifolium

Aegopodium angelicifolium, commonly known as angelica leaf groundsel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in woodland edges and moist meadows. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional herbal medicine. Its small yellow flowers bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.

Morphology

Aegopodium angelicifolium has compound leaves that are pinnately arranged, with serrated, ovate leaflets that are usually green and slightly hairy.

The stem is erect, cylindrical, and covered with fine hairs, with a light green to pale yellowish bark. Its small white flowers are clustered in umbels, bloom in early summer, and are pollinated by insects such as bees. The fruit is a dry, three-lobed schizocarp that splits into three one-seeded parts, and the seeds are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and form a dense network near the soil surface.
