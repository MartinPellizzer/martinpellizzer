Acalypha volkensii

Acalypha volkensii, commonly known as the velvetleaf or African nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that range from red to purple, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with lobed edges, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
