Aequatorium caucanum var. abbreviatum

Aequatorium caucanum var. abbreviatum, commonly known as the short-stemmed bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. Its flowers are usually pale blue or white, with a tubular shape that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded habitats.
