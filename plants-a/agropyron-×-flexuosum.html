Agropyron × flexuosum

Agropyron × flexuosum, commonly known as the creeping wheatgrass, is a hybrid perennial grass native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces dense, tufted clusters of spikelets. The plant has narrow, blue-green leaves that are often rolled in the bud, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas.

Agropyron × flexuosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone environments.
