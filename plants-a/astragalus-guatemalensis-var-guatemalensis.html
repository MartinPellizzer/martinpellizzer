Astragalus guatemalensis var. guatemalensis

Astragalus guatemalensis var. guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it access water from deeper soil layers.

This species is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its unique adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
