Amatlania elliptica

Amatlania elliptica, commonly known as the oval leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Amatlania elliptica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or tropical gardens.
