Argyrocoma dichotoma

Argyrocoma dichotoma, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a creeping rhizomatous root system, allowing it to spread and form dense mats. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and shrubland habitats.

Its common name reflects the silvery appearance of its foliage, which is covered in fine, white hairs.
