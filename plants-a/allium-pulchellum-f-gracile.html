Allium pulchellum f. gracile

Allium pulchellum f. gracile, commonly known as the slender garlic chive, is a cultivated form of the garlic chive species.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale purple flowers. This variety is often grown for its mild garlic flavor and ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.

The plant is a perennial, often used in landscaping and culinary applications.
