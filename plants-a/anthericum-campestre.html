Anthericum campestre

Anthericum campestre, commonly known as the meadow anthericum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist meadows and woodland edges. The plant features a tall, slender stem that bears a single, showy inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette at the base.

The flowers are primarily pollinated by insects, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
