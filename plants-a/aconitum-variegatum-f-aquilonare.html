Aconitum variegatum f. aquilonare

Aconitum variegatum f. aquilonare, commonly known as the variegated aconite, is a distinctive cultivar of the aconite genus.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring white and green patterns on the leaves. The plant produces tall, spire-like clusters of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. It is a perennial herb native to Europe and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care and planted in areas where children and pets cannot access it.
