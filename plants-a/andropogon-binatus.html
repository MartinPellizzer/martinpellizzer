Andropogon binatus

Andropogon binatus, commonly known as switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and is often found in prairies and disturbed areas. Switchgrass is a valuable resource for biofuel production due to its high biomass yield and low input requirements.

It also plays an important role in soil conservation and wildlife habitat.
