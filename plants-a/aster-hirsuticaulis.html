Aster hirsuticaulis

Aster hirsuticaulis, commonly known as the hairy stalk aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Aster hirsuticaulis is often found in the northeastern and midwestern United States.
