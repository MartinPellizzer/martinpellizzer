Adriana acerifolia var. heterophylla

Adriana acerifolia var. heterophylla, commonly known as the variable mapleleaf plant, is a unique variant of the Adriana acerifolia species.

It is characterized by its striking variation in leaf morphology, with some leaves resembling maple leaves and others displaying different shapes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions. Its foliage changes color with the seasons, displaying shades of green, yellow, and red.

This botanical variation makes it a subject of interest for horticulturists and botanists alike.
