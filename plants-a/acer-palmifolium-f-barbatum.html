Acer palmifolium f. barbatum

Acer palmifolium f. barbatum, commonly known as the Barbatum maple, is a rare and ornamental variant of the palmate maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that resemble a hand, with a broad, palm-like shape. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its foliage displays vibrant colors during the autumn season, making it a sought-after specimen for botanical gardens.

Due to its limited natural range and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and challenging plant to cultivate.
