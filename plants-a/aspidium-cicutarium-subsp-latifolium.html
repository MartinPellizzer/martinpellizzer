Aspidium cicutarium subsp. latifolium

Aspidium cicutarium subsp.

latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf water fern, is a fern species native to various regions in Asia and Africa. It is characterized by its large, broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically divided into multiple segments.

The fronds are usually evergreen and can grow up to several feet in length, making it a striking addition to moist, shaded environments. It thrives in humid conditions and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and forest floors.

The plant reproduces through spores and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in maintaining wetland ecosystems.
