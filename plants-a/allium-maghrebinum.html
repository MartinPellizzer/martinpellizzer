Allium maghrebinum

Allium maghrebinum, commonly known as the North African leek, is a hardy perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its hollow, cylindrical leaves and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces edible bulbs that are similar in texture to leeks but have a milder flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Allium maghrebinum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
