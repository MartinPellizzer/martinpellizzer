Aloe ghibensis

Aloe ghibensis, commonly known as the Ghibli Aloe, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its harsh environment. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. Its leaves contain a gel-like substance that is sometimes used for its soothing properties.

Aloe ghibensis is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
