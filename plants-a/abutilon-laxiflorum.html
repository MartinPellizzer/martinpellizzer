Abutilon laxiflorum

Abutilon laxiflorum, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Abutilon laxiflorum is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
