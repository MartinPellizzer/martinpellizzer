Ampacus ruticarpa

Ampacus ruticarpa, commonly known as the roughleaf acacia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes. Its woody stems are covered in rough, ridged bark, giving it its common name.

Ampacus ruticarpa is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
