Archidendron vaillantii var. pentzkeanum

Archidendron vaillantii var. pentzkeanum, commonly known as the pentzkeanum varietal of the ironwood tree, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features compound leaves with alternate, pinnate arrangements, and its flowers are typically small, clustered in racemes, often with a distinctive yellow or greenish hue. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and its root system is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber in its native habitat.
