Amphibromus fluitans

Amphibromus fluitans, commonly known as the floating bladdergrass, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its specialized adaptations for life in water.

It possesses floating leaves that are covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss and facilitates gas exchange. The plant's roots are typically short and non-photosynthetic, anchoring it to submerged surfaces. Its reproductive structures, including small, greenish flowers, emerge from the water surface, allowing for wind pollination.

Amphibromus fluitans is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and marshes.
