Amphibromus sinuatus

Amphibromus sinuatus, commonly known as the zigzag sedge, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color, arranged in spikelets. It thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found in freshwater habitats.

Amphibromus sinuatus is noted for its ability to tolerate waterlogged soils, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
