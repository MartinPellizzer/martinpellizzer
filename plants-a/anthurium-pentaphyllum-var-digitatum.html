Anthurium pentaphyllum var. digitatum

Anthurium pentaphyllum var. digitatum, commonly known as the digitate anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with striking patterns and a deep green color. The plant produces clusters of spadices, which are typically green with a red or orange spathe, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, warm environments with indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular in horticulture for its aesthetic value and relatively easy care requirements.
