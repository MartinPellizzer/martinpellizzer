Acacia gunnii var. hirsutior

Acacia gunnii var. hirsutior, commonly known as the hairy acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, which are often followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in degraded areas.
