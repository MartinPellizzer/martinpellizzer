Agoseris dens-leonis

Agoseris dens-leonis, commonly known as the dense-leaved agoseris, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are densely packed, often with a silvery or grayish underside, providing a distinctive appearance.

It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
