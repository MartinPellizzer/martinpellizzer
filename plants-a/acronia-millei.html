Acronia millei

Acronia millei, commonly known as the golden trumpet plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly waxy appearance.
