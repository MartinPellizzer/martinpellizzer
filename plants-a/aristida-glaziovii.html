Aristida glaziovii

Aristida glaziovii, commonly known as Glaziov's threeawn, is a perennial grass species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences composed of bristly spikelets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, and the plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Aristida glaziovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
