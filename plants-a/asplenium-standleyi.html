Asplenium standleyi

Asplenium standleyi, commonly known as the standley's spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It typically grows on tree trunks or rocks, relying on atmospheric moisture rather than soil for nutrients. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive, segmented appearance. It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit.

Asplenium standleyi is often found in shaded, humid environments, making it a common sight in tropical rainforests.
