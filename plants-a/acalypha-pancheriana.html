Acalypha pancheriana

Acalypha pancheriana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has palmate leaves with lobed margins, and its stems are often hairy, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Acalypha pancheriana is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its traditional uses in herbal medicine.
