Actinidia arguta f. rufinervis

Actinidia arguta f. rufinervis, commonly known as the red-veined kiwifruit, is a variant of the actinidia genus native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red veins on the undersides of its leaves, which set it apart from other actinidia species. The plant produces small, fuzzy fruits that are typically eaten raw or used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This cultivar is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its edible fruit.
