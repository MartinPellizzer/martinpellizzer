Antennaria rosea var. nitida

Antennaria rosea var. nitida, commonly known as the silver cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northwestern United States and parts of Canada.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with hairy, silver-gray foliage that reflects light, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a slightly waxy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.
