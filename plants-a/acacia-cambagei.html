Acacia cambagei

Acacia cambagei, commonly known as the broad-leafed wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, phyllodes that are typically broad and flat. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. Acacia cambagei is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resistance to drought and salinity.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
