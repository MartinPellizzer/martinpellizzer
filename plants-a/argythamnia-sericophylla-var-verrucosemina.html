Argythamnia sericophylla var. verrucosemina

Argythamnia sericophylla var. verrucosemina, commonly known as the velvetleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its soft, woolly leaves that give it a distinctive, almost velvety appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its unique foliage and resilience make it an interesting subject for botanical study and a potential candidate for xeriscaping in dry climates.
