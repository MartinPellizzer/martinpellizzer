Acer monspessulanum subsp. turcomanicum

Acer monspessulanum subsp.

turcomanicum, commonly known as the Turkestan maple, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, which turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and semi-arid regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
