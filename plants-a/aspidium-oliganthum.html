Aspidium oliganthum

Aspidium oliganthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Aspidium oliganthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
