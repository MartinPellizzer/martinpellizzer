Aconitum × engadinense

Aconitum × engadinense, commonly known as the Engadine monkshood, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the Alps.

It features striking blue to purple spurred flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with lobed segments and is characterized by its tall, erect stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical gardens.
