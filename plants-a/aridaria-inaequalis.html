Aridaria inaequalis

Aridaria inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a small, perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, showcasing its adaptation to harsh environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, helping to conserve water in drought conditions.

Aridaria inaequalis is often found in coastal and inland desert areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
