Alchemilla vulgaris var. heterophylla

Alchemilla vulgaris var. heterophylla, commonly known as the variable lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and traditional herbal medicine.
