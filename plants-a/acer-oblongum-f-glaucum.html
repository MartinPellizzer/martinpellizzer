Acer oblongum f. glaucum

Acer oblongum f. glaucum, commonly known as the glaucous maple, is a variant of the winged maple native to the eastern United States.

This deciduous tree is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that are often tinged with a bluish-white hue on the upper surface. The leaves are typically lobed and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It produces small, red flowers in early spring followed by winged samaras that disperse its seeds.

The tree is valued for its unique foliage and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
