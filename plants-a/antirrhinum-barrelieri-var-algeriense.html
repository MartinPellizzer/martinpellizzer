Antirrhinum barrelieri var. algeriense

Antirrhinum barrelieri var. algeriense, commonly known as the Algerian snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
