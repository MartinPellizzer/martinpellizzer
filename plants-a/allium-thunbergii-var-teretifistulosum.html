Allium thunbergii var. teretifistulosum

Allium thunbergii var. teretifistulosum, commonly known as the slender leek or Japanese leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features long, narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers atop a tall, slender stem. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, garlic-like flavor.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and delicate blossoms.
