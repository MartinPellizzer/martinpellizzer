Aristida pilgeri

Aristida pilgeri, commonly known as the Pilger's threeawn, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are narrow and usually have a bluish-green hue, and the plant produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Aristida pilgeri is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
