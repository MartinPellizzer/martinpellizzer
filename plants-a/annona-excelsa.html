Annona excelsa

Annona excelsa, commonly known as the giant soursop or custard apple, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The fruit is typically large, with a spiky exterior and a sweet, creamy white pulp that is edible. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
