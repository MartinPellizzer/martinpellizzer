Acer saccharum f. subvestitum

Acer saccharum f. subvestitum, commonly known as the white ash, is a distinctive variant of the sugar maple.

It is characterized by its unique bark that is smoother and lighter in color compared to the typical sugar maple. The leaves are typically smaller and more ovate, with a serrated margin. This form is often found in specific geographic regions where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
