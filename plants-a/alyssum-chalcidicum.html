Alyssum chalcidicum

Alyssum chalcidicum, commonly known as the Greek alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, branched stems and gray-green, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum chalcidicum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
