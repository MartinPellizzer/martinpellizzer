Astragalus accidens var. accidens

Astragalus accidens var. accidens, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers in racemes. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
