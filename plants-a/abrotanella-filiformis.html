Abrotanella filiformis

Abrotanella filiformis, commonly known as slender sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically thrives in dry, sandy, or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink, appearing in clusters. It reproduces through seeds and has a compact growth habit that helps it conserve water.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
