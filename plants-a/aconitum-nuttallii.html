Aconitum nuttallii

Aconitum nuttallii, commonly known as Western Monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive hood-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple. The plant has large, palmately lobed leaves and produces showy, spurs on its flowers, which are adapted for pollination by insects. Aconitum nuttallii contains potent alkaloids, making it toxic and requiring caution during handling or cultivation.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
