Aechmea itapoana

Aechmea itapoana, commonly known as the Itapoan bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant features vibrant, long-lasting bracts that display a range of colors, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its foliage is typically dark green with a waxy texture, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Aechmea itapoana is appreciated for its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
