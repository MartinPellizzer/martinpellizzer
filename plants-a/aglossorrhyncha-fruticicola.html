Aglossorrhyncha fruticicola

Aglossorrhyncha fruticicola, commonly known as the small-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees in tropical and subtropical regions.

It lacks true leaves and instead has scale-like structures that reduce water loss. The plant relies on its host for water and some nutrients, while producing its own food through photosynthesis. Its flowers are small and white or greenish, often clustered in dense inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains seeds covered in a sticky substance to aid in attachment to potential host plants.
