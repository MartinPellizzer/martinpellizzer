Aconitum tapeinocranum

Aconitum tapeinocranum, commonly known as the lesser aconite, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are arranged in dense, spicate inflorescences. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed leaflets that are often hairy.

Aconitum tapeinocranum contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires caution when handling.
