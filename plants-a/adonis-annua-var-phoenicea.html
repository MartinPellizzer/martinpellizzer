Adonis annua var. phoenicea

Adonis annua var. phoenicea, commonly known as the scarlet globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright red, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet and has finely divided, fern-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and relatively easy-to-cultivate plant for gardeners.
