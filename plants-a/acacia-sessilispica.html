Acacia sessilispica

Acacia sessilispica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and small, bipinnate leaves that are typically green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical spikes, which are a key feature for identification. Its seeds are encased in hard, woody pods that split open when mature.

Acacia sessilispica is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of Australian flora.
