Adiantum chevalieri

Adiantum chevalieri, commonly known as the golden maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble lace. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually bright green, with a distinctive veining pattern that enhances its ornamental appeal.

Adiantum chevalieri is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic beauty and ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.
