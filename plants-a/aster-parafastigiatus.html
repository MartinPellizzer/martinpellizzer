Aster parafastigiatus

Aster parafastigiatus, commonly known as the slender aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
