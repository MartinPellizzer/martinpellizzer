Artemisia sinensis

Artemisia sinensis, commonly known as sweet wormwood, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is native to regions of Asia, including China, where it has been traditionally used in herbal medicine. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage and small yellow flowers. It contains the compound artemisinin, which is used in the treatment of malaria.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is cultivated both for pharmaceutical purposes and as a traditional remedy.
