Aloe humilis var. acuminata

Aloe humilis var. acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features short, narrow, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, covered in a powdery white coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers on upright stems during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with full sun exposure.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact growth habit and aesthetic appeal.
