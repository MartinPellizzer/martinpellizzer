Amomum tenellum

Amomum tenellum, commonly known as small cardamom or lesser cardamom, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of India, China, and Indonesia. The plant produces small, aromatic seeds enclosed in a dark, three-lobed fruit. These seeds are widely used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their strong, spicy aroma.

The botanical features of Amomum tenellum include its rhizomatous growth habit and its ability to thrive in tropical climates.
