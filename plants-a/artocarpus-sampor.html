Artocarpus sampor

Artocarpus sampor, commonly known as the sampor or the wild breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces a fruit that is typically green when unripe and turns yellow or orange when mature. The fruit has a fibrous, starchy pulp that is edible and often used in local cuisines.

It also has a woody, inedible core that contains seeds.
