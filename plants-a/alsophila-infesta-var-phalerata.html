Alsophila infesta var. phalerata

Alsophila infesta var. phalerata, commonly known as the Phalerata tree fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

This fern is often found in tropical rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role.
