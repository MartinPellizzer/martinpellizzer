Anisopappus davyi

Anisopappus davyi, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, woolly leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, oblong seeds with pappus for wind dispersal. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and desert scrub habitats.

Botanically, it is classified in the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties used by indigenous peoples.
