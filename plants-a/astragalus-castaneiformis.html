Astragalus castaneiformis

Astragalus castaneiformis, commonly known as the chestnut-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-pink flowers. The plant is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its survival in harsh environments.

Astragalus castaneiformis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
