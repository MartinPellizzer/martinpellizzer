Abies magnifica subsp. shastensis

Abies magnifica subsp.

shastensis, commonly known as the Shasta fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches.

The cones are typically cylindrical and hang downward, containing winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. This subspecies is adapted to high-altitude environments, often growing in subalpine or alpine regions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important component of mountainous forests.
