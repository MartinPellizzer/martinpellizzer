Adicea depressa

Adicea depressa, commonly known as the narrowleaf false flax, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in spikes and bloom in the summer months.

Adicea depressa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
