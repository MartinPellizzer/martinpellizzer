Aster lacunarum

Aster lacunarum, commonly known as the lacunar aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

The lacunar aster is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
