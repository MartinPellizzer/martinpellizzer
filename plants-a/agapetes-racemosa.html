Agapetes racemosa

Agapetes racemosa, commonly known as the clustered silverleaf, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant typically features small, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy, with a silvery underside. It produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped white or pale pink flowers in the spring, followed by small, dark blue berries.

Agapetes racemosa thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests at high elevations.
