Adenia cordifolia

Adenia cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-shaped adenium, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, succulent stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding visual interest to its appearance. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stems to survive drought conditions.

Its unique morphology and ability to thrive in harsh climates make it a notable species in botanical studies.
