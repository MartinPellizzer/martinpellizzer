Achillea millefolium subsp. millefolium

Achillea millefolium subsp.

millefolium, commonly known as yarrow, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America. It features a robust root system and upright, branched stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces flat, composite flower heads composed of numerous small, white or pinkish florets, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are finely divided into numerous thread-like segments, giving it a feathery appearance.

Yarrow is adapted to a wide range of soil types and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.
