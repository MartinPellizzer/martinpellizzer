Aconitum semigaleatum

Aconitum semigaleatum, commonly known as the semi-galeate monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant features palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care.
