Acanthobotrya decumbens

Acanthobotrya decumbens, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically forms dense mats on the ground. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often accompanied by heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Its botanical name reflects its thorny stems and cup-like flower structure.
