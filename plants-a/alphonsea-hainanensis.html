Alphonsea hainanensis

Alphonsea hainanensis, commonly known as the Hainan alphonsea, is a flowering plant native to southern China, particularly Hainan Island.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible fruits resembling small, round drupes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
