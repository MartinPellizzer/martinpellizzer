Acritopappus confertus

Acritopappus confertus, commonly known as the clustered acornweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellowish.

It is an annual or biennial herb that produces small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by wind.
