Alnus incana f. glabrescens

Alnus incana f. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth alder, is a shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

It is a nitrogen-fixing species, making it valuable for soil enrichment in ecological restoration projects.
