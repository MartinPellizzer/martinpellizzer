Acer pseudosieboldianum f. macrocarpum

Acer pseudosieboldianum f. macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-seeded maple, is a rare and distinctive variant of the Japanese maple.

It is characterized by its large, elongated samaras, which are its winged seeds, making it unique among maple species. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, with a rounded crown and delicate, palmate leaves that turn vibrant red or orange in the fall. It prefers well-drained, acidic soils and partial to full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
