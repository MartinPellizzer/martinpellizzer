Atractylis carduus var. latifolia

Atractylis carduus var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf wormwood, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features a robust, upright stem with gray-green, hairy leaves that are broad and lobed. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been used historically in herbal medicine and as a source of essential oils.
