Achillea salicifolia

Achillea salicifolia, commonly known as silverleaf yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silver-gray, fern-like foliage and produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and stems, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its historical use dates back to ancient times, where it was believed to have healing properties.
