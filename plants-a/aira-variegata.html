Aira variegata

Aira variegata, commonly known as the variegated air grass, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its ornamental, variegated foliage with white and green stripes. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that add visual interest during the flowering season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
