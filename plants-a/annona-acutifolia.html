Annona acutifolia

Annona acutifolia, commonly known as the sour sop or graviola, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces a distinctive fruit with a thick, spiky rind. The fruit contains a soft, white, segmented pulp that is typically sour and aromatic. The plant is part of the Annonaceae family and is valued for its medicinal properties as well as its culinary use.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its unique fruit.
