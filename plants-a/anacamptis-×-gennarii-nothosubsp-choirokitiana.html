Anacamptis × gennarii nothosubsp.

choirokitiana, commonly known as the Chirokitian orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing violet to purple-colored blossoms. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This nothosubspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
