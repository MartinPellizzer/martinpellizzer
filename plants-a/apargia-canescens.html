Apargia canescens

Apargia canescens, commonly known as the grayleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and showy purple to pink composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its propagation in wetland environments.

Apargia canescens is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its aesthetic appeal in native plant gardens.
