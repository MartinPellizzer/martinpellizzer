Anthyllis vulneraria subsp. cantabrica

Anthyllis vulneraria subsp.

cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian viper's bugloss, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and coastal areas.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
