Acer palmatum f. sanguineum

Acer palmatum f. sanguineum, commonly known as the Bloodtwig Dogwood, is a variant of the Japanese maple characterized by its distinctive red twigs and foliage.

This plant is native to Japan and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. The leaves are typically lobed and turn vibrant shades of red in the autumn, adding seasonal interest. It prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.

Its unique coloration and elegant form make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
