Anserina ficifolia

Anserina ficifolia, commonly known as the golden star gooseberry, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that mature in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a glossy appearance.

The fruit is edible and has a tart flavor, making it a valuable plant in both ecological and culinary contexts.
