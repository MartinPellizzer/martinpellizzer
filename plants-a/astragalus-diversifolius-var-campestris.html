Astragalus diversifolius var. campestris

Astragalus diversifolius var. campestris, commonly known as the field milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate arrangements. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and open shrublands.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
