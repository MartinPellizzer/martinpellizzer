Anredera aspera

Anredera aspera, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its trailing growth habit and dense foliage. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with a fine, silvery pubescence that gives the plant its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds.

This plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
