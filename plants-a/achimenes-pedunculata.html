Achimenes pedunculata

Achimenes pedunculata, commonly known as the flamingo flower, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features upright, branching stems and heart-shaped, dark green leaves. Its flowers are borne on long, slender peduncles, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
