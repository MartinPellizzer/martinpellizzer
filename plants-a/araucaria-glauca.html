Araucaria glauca

Araucaria glauca, commonly known as the silver pine, is a coniferous tree native to the southern Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, pyramidal crown. The tree has needle-like leaves that are blue-green in color, giving it its common name. Its cones are typically small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Araucaria glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
