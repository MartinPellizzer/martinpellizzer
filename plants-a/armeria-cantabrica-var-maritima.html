Armeria cantabrica var. maritima

Armeria cantabrica var. maritima, commonly known as the Cantabrian thrift, is a hardy, low-growing plant native to the coastal regions of northern Spain.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh maritime conditions, including salt spray and strong winds. The plant features dense clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer, adding ornamental value to rocky coastal landscapes. It is often used in garden settings to mimic its natural habitat due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal.

Its foliage is linear and evergreen, providing visual interest throughout the year.
