Astragalus zionis

Astragalus zionis, commonly known as Zion milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States, particularly in Utah and Nevada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often on rocky soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.

Astragalus zionis is also of interest in botanical studies due to its adaptations to harsh environmental conditions.
