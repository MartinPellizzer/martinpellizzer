Abarema racemosa

Abarema racemosa, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that resemble peacock feathers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant flowers and unique appearance.

Abarema racemosa thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.
