Anemone narcissiflora var. major

Anemone narcissiflora var. major, commonly known as the giant white anemone, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features large, showy white flowers with a prominent central yellow eye, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has broad, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a dramatic and elegant addition to their landscape.
