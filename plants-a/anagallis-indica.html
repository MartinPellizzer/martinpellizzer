Anagallis indica

Anagallis indica, commonly known as the common mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically hairy and heart-shaped, and the plant often thrives in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It is well adapted to a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in many temperate regions.

The plant's rapid growth and prolific seed production contribute to its widespread distribution.
