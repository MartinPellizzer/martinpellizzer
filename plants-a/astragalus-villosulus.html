Astragalus villosulus

Astragalus villosulus, commonly known as the hairy milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, and its seed pods are slender and elongated.

Astragalus villosulus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in grasslands and sagebrush habitats.
