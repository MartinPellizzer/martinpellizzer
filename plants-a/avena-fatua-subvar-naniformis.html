Avena fatua subvar. naniformis

Avena fatua subvar. naniformis, commonly known as the slender oat, is a grass species characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and compact inflorescence.

It is an annual plant that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as fields and roadsides, and is often considered a weed. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of florets, which are wind-pollinated. Its seeds are enclosed in a protective lemma and palea, typical of the Poaceae family.

This subspecies is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions, contributing to its invasive potential in agricultural settings.
