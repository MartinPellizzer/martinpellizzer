Alstroemeria hookeriana

Alstroemeria hookeriana, commonly known as the Hooker's lily, is a perennial plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower due to its long-lasting blooms.
