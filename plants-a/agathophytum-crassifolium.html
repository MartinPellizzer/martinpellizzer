Agathophytum crassifolium

Agathophytum crassifolium, commonly known as the thick-leaved agathophytum, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick, and often spiny leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the rainy season, typically in spring or early summer. Its growth habit is typically low-growing and sprawling, forming dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.

Agathophytum crassifolium is well adapted to high temperatures and low precipitation, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
