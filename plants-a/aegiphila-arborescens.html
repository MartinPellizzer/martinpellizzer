Aegiphila arborescens

Aegiphila arborescens, commonly known as the tree-like aegiphila, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its branching structure, lance-shaped leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal or disturbed soils.

Aegiphila arborescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
