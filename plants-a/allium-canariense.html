Allium canariense

Allium canariense, commonly known as the Canary Islands garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clustered, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, rounded bulbs that are often used in local cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
