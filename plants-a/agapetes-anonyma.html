Agapetes anonyma

Agapetes anonyma, commonly known as the white-flowered silver leaf plant, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white wax, giving it a silvery appearance. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental value.

Agapetes anonyma thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude regions with high humidity.
