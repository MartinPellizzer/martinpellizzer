Alsophila excelsior

Alsophila excelsior, commonly known as the cloud fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in rainforests and along stream banks. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its fibrous roots, which help stabilize the soil.

Alsophila excelsior plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.
