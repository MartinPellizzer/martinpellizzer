Andropogon provincialis var. paucipilus

Andropogon provincialis var. paucipilus, commonly known as the slender bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, blue-green leaves and slender, erect culms. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and disturbed sites, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets.

This variety is often noted for its ecological value, providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
