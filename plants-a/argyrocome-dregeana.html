Argyrocome dregeana

Argyrocome dregeana, commonly known as the silver-leafed candelabra, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It features a branching structure with thick, fleshy stems that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, pale green leaves that are covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. During the growing season, it may bloom with yellow flowers that resemble those of a dandelion.

Its distinctive appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
