Allium phanerantherum subsp. deciduum

Allium phanerantherum subsp.

deciduum, commonly known as the deciduous leek, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features a bulbous stem that is often edible, with a mild garlic-like flavor.

The plant produces clusters of white or pale purple flowers, which are typically borne on slender stems. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is characterized by its seasonal leaf drop, distinguishing it from other Allium species.
