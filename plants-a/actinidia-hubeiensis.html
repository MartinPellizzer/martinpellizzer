Actinidia hubeiensis

Actinidia hubeiensis, commonly known as Hubei kiwifruit, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It belongs to the Actinidiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces small, hairy fruits that are typically eaten raw or used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is cultivated in various regions for both horticultural and agricultural purposes.
