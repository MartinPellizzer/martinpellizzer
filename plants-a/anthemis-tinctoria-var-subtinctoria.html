Anthemis tinctoria var. subtinctoria

Anthemis tinctoria var. subtinctoria, commonly known as lesser tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage, which contains compounds responsible for its distinctive scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its historical use in dyeing and as a medicinal herb, it holds cultural and botanical significance.
