Abies sachalinensis var. sachalinensis

Abies sachalinensis var. sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Russian Far East and northeastern China.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like foliage. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain edible seeds. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
