Alyssum lepidulum subsp. genuinum

Alyssum lepidulum subsp.

genuinum, commonly known as the narrowleaf alyssum, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
