Asplenium × bavaricum nothosubsp.

bavaricum, commonly known as the hybrid maidenhair fern, is a sterile hybrid resulting from the cross between Asplenium trichomanes and Asplenium bulbiferum. It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble those of its parent species.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are clustered on the undersides of the fronds.

Due to its hybrid nature, it does not produce viable seeds and relies on vegetative propagation for survival.
