Asphodelus albus subsp. albus

Asphodelus albus subsp.

albus, commonly known as the white asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features tall, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, tubular flowers.

The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as coastal areas and limestone formations. Its flowering period typically occurs in spring, and it is known for its resilience to drought conditions.

The species has historical uses in traditional medicine and horticulture.
