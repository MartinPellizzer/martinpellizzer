Aralia malabarica

Aralia malabarica, commonly known as the Malabar aralia, is a tropical plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several narrow, lance-shaped leaflets arranged in an alternate pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbel-like clusters, followed by bright red berries that are edible for humans and birds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in dense forests or along stream banks.
