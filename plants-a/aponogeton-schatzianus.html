Aponogeton schatzianus

Aponogeton schatzianus, commonly known as the Schatz's arrowhead, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its arrowhead-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, with long, slender stems that often extend into the water. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom above the water, adding ornamental value to ponds and water gardens. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and prefers moderate to high light conditions.

Aponogeton schatzianus is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care in aquatic settings.
