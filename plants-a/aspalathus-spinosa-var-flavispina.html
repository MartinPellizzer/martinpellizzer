Aspalathus spinosa var. flavispina

Aspalathus spinosa var. flavispina, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed bushveld clover, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers clustered in dense heads, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

This variety is distinguished by its distinctive yellow stems, which set it apart from other Aspalathus species.
