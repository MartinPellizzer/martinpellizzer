Anthericum alooides

Anthericum alooides, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a unique plant native to South Africa.

It features a rosette of fleshy, sword-shaped leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a spike, which are followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant's structure and growth habits reflect its evolutionary adaptations to survive in harsh, dry climates.
