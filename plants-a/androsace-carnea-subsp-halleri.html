Androsace carnea subsp. halleri

Androsace carnea subsp.

halleri, commonly known as the Haller's pink, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features dense, mat-forming foliage with small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white in color.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a rosette structure, which helps it retain moisture in harsh conditions.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils.
