Acacia puccioniana

Acacia puccioniana, commonly known as the red acacia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky soils. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, which splits open to release the seeds when mature.

Acacia puccioniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in coastal and arid conditions.
