Aulomyrcia pruinosa

Aulomyrcia pruinosa, commonly known as the silverleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces showy, pink to lavender-colored flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Aulomyrcia pruinosa is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
