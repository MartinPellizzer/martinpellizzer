Asplenium tenerum var. retusum

Asplenium tenerum var. retusum, commonly known as the blunt-lobed maidenhair fern, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often have a bluish-green hue. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth. Its rhizomes are wiry and help anchor it to rocky or soil surfaces.

The fronds are typically leathery and have a distinctive, arching growth habit.
