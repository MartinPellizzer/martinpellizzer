Aegopogon strictus

Aegopogon strictus, commonly known as the narrowleaf goatherb, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Aegopogon strictus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.

Morphology

Aegopogon strictus has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, with a bright green color and a slightly hairy surface.

The stem is cylindrical, smooth, and slightly woody, with a light green to brownish color, and it grows upright with a branching pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, with a tubular shape and a single stamen, blooming in early summer and relying on wind for pollination. The fruit is a dry capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Its roots are fibrous and shallow, anchoring the plant in sandy or loamy soils.
