Agastache rugosa

Agastache rugosa, commonly known as autumn sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its aromatic leaves and flowers attract pollinators such as butterflies and hummingbirds.

Agastache rugosa is also valued for its use in traditional herbal medicine.
