Argythamnia berteroana var. psiladenia

Argythamnia berteroana var. psiladenia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf argythamnia, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention in its harsh environment. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is known for its composite flower heads.
