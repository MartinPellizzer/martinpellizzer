Aristida scabrivalvis

Aristida scabrivalvis, commonly known as the rough-ventured grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves with a rough, sandpapery texture on the upper surface. The inflorescence consists of a compact, open panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
