Anemone narcissiflora var. aconitifolia

Anemone narcissiflora var. aconitifolia, commonly known as the blue anemone, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, resembling the petals of a traditional anemone. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in water retention. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
