Andropogon mohrii var. pungensis

Andropogon mohrii var. pungensis, commonly known as the Mohri grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
