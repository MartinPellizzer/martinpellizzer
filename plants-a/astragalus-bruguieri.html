Astragalus bruguieri

Astragalus bruguieri, commonly known as the Mediterranean milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, including parts of Turkey and the Mediterranean basin. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to reddish pea-like flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is often found in steppe and scrub habitats.

Astragalus bruguieri is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native environments.
