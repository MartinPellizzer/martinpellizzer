Avenula marginata

Avenula marginata, commonly known as the marginsedge, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Avenula marginata is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
