Asphodelus albus var. occidentalis

Asphodelus albus var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western white asphodel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant produces tall, narrow leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers during the spring season. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a short, rhizomatous stem.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
