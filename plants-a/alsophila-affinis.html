Alsophila affinis

Alsophila affinis, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting its tall growth form.

The fronds are bioluminescent, which is a unique feature that aids in attracting certain nocturnal pollinators.
