Anemone thalictroides

Anemone thalictroides, commonly known as the showy anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow center. The plant has palmately divided, hairy leaves that are typically lobed and resemble those of a maple tree. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Anemone thalictroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
