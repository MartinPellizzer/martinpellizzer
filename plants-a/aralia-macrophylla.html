Aralia macrophylla

Aralia macrophylla, commonly known as the giant aralia, is a striking plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 inches in diameter, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces white, fragrant flowers in compound umbels, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in ornamental gardening for its visual impact.

Aralia macrophylla is also known for its ability to tolerate a variety of soil types and moisture conditions.
