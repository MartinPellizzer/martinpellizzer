Argyrolobium ramosissimum

Argyrolobium ramosissimum, commonly known as the silverleaf burr medic, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in compact inflorescences, followed by pods that contain edible seeds. It is often used in agriculture as a forage crop and for soil stabilization due to its hardy nature.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils and withstand drought conditions.
