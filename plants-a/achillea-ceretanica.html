Achillea ceretanica

Achillea ceretanica, commonly known as the Alpine yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and silvery-green foliage. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Achillea ceretanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
