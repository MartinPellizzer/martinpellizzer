Asperula acerosa

Asperula acerosa, commonly known as the prickly woodruff, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Asperula acerosa is often found in rocky or wooded areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
