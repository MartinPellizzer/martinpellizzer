Alyssum thessalum

Alyssum thessalum, commonly known as the Greek wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial species that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant has hairy, branching stems and linear, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum thessalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
