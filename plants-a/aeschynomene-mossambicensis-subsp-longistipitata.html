Aeschynomene mossambicensis subsp. longistipitata

Aeschynomene mossambicensis subsp.

longistipitata, commonly known as the long-stipitate aeschynomene, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically glabrous.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers arranged in racemes, which are often pink to purple in color. It is native to parts of southern Africa and thrives in moist, sandy soils.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its use in certain agricultural contexts.
