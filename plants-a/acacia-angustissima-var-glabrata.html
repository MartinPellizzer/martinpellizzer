Acacia angustissima var. glabrata

Acacia angustissima var. glabrata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often glabrous (hairless) phyllodes, which are typically dark green and narrow. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late summer, which are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
