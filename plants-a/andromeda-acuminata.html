Andromeda acuminata

Andromeda acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wintergreen, is a perennial evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, with a distinctive red or orange coloration on the undersides. Its small, fragrant flowers bloom in late winter or early spring, adding visual interest during the colder months.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
