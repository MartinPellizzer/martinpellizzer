Amorpha floridana

Amorpha floridana, commonly known as Florida honeyflower, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with 10 to 20 pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
