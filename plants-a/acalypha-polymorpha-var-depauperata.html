Acalypha polymorpha var. depauperata

Acalypha polymorpha var. depauperata, commonly known as the staghorn fern, is a tropical plant characterized by its unique, tree-like appearance with a thick, fibrous trunk.

It features broad, waxy leaves that form a rosette pattern, often with a distinctive reddish or brownish coloration. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often epiphytic, growing on the surfaces of trees or rocks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spore-bearing structures called sporangia.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
