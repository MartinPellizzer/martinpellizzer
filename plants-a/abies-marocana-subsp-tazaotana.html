Abies marocana subsp. tazaotana

Abies marocana subsp.

tazaotana, commonly known as the Tazaotan fir, is a rare and endemic conifer native to the Atlas Mountains of Morocco. It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and conical shape, typical of the fir genus.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments at high altitudes, often in mountainous regions with well-drained soils. Its cones are small and cylindrical, containing winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species facing threats from climate change and habitat loss.
