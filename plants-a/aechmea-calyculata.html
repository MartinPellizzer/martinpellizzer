Aechmea calyculata

Aechmea calyculata, commonly known as the velvet bromeliad, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its foliage is often covered in a dense, velvety coating, giving it its common name.

Aechmea calyculata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea calyculata has leaves that are strap-shaped, arranged in a rosette, with a dark green to blue-gray coloration, and can grow up to 40 cm in length.

The plant has a short, thick stem with a rough, fibrous bark that is usually greenish-brown in color. Its flowers are clustered in a panicle inflorescence, featuring bright red or orange bracts, and bloom during the dry season, primarily pollinated by wind. The fruit is a fleshy, berry-like structure that contains small, hard seeds, which are dispersed by birds and mammals.
