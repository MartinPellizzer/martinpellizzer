Argemone mexicana var. lutea

Argemone mexicana var. lutea, commonly known as the Mexican poppy or yellow argemone, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in arid regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some parts of the world.
