Aspleniopsis decipiens

Aspleniopsis decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive asplenium, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered spore clusters on the underside of its fronds, which are essential for its reproductive cycle. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique leaf morphology and growth habits make it a distinctive subject in botanical studies.
