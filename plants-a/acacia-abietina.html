Acacia abietina

Acacia abietina, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces long, flat seed pods that contain small, hard seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Acacia abietina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
