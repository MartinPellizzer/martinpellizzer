Anomospermum chloranthum subsp. isthmicola

Anomospermum chloranthum subsp.

isthmicola, commonly known as the Isthmian acacia, is a shrub native to Central America. It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, tropical environments and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in local ecosystems and traditional uses.
