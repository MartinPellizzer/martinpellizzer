Actinidia callosa var. sabiifolia

Actinidia callosa var. sabiifolia, commonly known as the Sabiifolia Actinidia, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically three to five lobes and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries that mature to a dark purple color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and edible landscaping.

This variety is also valued for its potential in fruit cultivation and its role in ecological restoration projects.
