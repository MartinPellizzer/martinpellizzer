Asplenium lineare

Asplenium lineare, commonly known as the linear hartsia, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant has a compact, upright growth habit and often forms dense clusters. Its fronds are pinnate, with small, closely spaced pinnae that give it a delicate appearance.

Asplenium lineare is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
