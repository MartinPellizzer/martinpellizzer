Atriplex isatidea

Atriplex isatidea, commonly known as Russian spinach or leaf spinach, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines for their mild, spinach-like flavor.

Atriplex isatidea is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions, making it a hardy ornamental and forage plant.
