Astragalus vicia

Astragalus vicia, commonly known as the common vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in a variety of soil types. Astragalus vicia is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and fields, due to its adaptability.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
