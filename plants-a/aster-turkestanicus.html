Aster turkestanicus

Aster turkestanicus, commonly known as the Turkestan aster, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while its flowers bloom in shades of purple or blue.

Aster turkestanicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
