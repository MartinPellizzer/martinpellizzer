Andromachia excelsa

Andromachia excelsa, commonly known as the lofty andromachia, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. This plant is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
