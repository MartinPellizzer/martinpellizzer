Arenaria capillaris var. americana

Arenaria capillaris var. americana, commonly known as American sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It typically thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as dunes, grasslands, and disturbed areas. The plant features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are slender and grass-like, and the stems are usually prostrate or ascending.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in coastal and arid environments.
