Amasonia erecta var. punicea

Amasonia erecta var. punicea, commonly known as the redflower false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, featuring upright, branching stems. The plant produces showy, violet to purple flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth margin and a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is well-suited for gardens and natural landscapes.
