Acalypha fruticosa

Acalypha fruticosa, commonly known as the red torch or red flame plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its vibrant red bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that are surrounded by striking red, papery bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a velvety texture, often with a reddish or purple tinge on the underside.

Acalypha fruticosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
