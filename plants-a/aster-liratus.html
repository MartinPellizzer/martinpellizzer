Aster liratus

Aster liratus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its foliage is typically gray-green with a velvety texture, and it often grows in moist, open habitats.

Aster liratus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
