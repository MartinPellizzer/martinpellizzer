Aframomum giganteum

Aframomum giganteum, commonly known as giant cardamom, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elliptical, dark blue-black berries. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is adapted to humid tropical climates.
