Aspilia andersonii

Aspilia andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's torch flower, is a flowering plant native to the island of Socotra.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, yellow-orange flower heads that resemble torches. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into bristly, spiky seed heads. It thrives in arid environments and is adapted to the unique climate of Socotra.

Aspilia andersonii is notable for its distinctive appearance and is considered a rare and endemic species.
