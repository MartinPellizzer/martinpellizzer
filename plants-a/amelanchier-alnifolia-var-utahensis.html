Amelanchier alnifolia var. utahensis

Amelanchier alnifolia var. utahensis, commonly known as the Utah serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. Its leaves are oval with a smooth margin and turn vibrant yellow or red in the fall.

The Utah serviceberry is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife and supporting local biodiversity.
