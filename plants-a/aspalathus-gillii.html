Aspalathus gillii

Aspalathus gillii, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments.

Aspalathus gillii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
