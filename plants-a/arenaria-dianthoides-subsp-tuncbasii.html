Arenaria dianthoides subsp. tuncbasii

Arenaria dianthoides subsp.

tuncbasii, commonly known as Turkish sandpink, is a flowering plant native to the rocky and sandy soils of Turkey. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit.

The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are typically linear and covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand arid conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy environments.
