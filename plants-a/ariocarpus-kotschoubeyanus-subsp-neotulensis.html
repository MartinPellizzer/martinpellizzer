Ariocarpus kotschoubeyanus subsp. neotulensis

Ariocarpus kotschoubeyanus subsp.

neotulensis, commonly known as the "Mexican windowleaf," is a succulent cactus native to the Chihuahuan Desert of Mexico. It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are often covered in a white, waxy coating.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the spring or early summer. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable specimen in horticulture.
