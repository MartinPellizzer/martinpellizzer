Aster anticostensis

Aster anticostensis, commonly known as the Anticosti aster, is a rare flowering plant native to the island of Anticosti in the Gulf of Saint Lawrence.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy purple or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in open woodlands or along streambanks. It is listed as endangered due to habitat loss and limited distribution.

Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect this unique and ecologically important species.
