Aframomum amaniense

Aframomum amaniense, commonly known as African cardamom, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and tall, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries containing numerous seeds. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their distinct flavor and potential medicinal properties.

Aframomum amaniense thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories across its native range.
