Aster paniculatus var. acutidens

Aster paniculatus var. acutidens, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy along the edges. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
