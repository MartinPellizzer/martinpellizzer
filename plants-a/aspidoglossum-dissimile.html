Aspidoglossum dissimile

Aspidoglossum dissimile, commonly known as the silver sword or silver grass, is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is a perennial herb native to South Africa, characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance with narrow, silver-blue leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers in summer, which are arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value lies in its striking foliage and drought tolerance.
