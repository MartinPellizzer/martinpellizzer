Amauropelta hydrophila

Amauropelta hydrophila, commonly known as the water shield, is a aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant has floating leaves that are broad and rounded, with a waxy surface that helps repel water. Its roots are anchored in the substrate, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged.

Amauropelta hydrophila plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
