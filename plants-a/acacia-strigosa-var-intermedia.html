Acacia strigosa var. intermedia

Acacia strigosa var. intermedia, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. The leaves are linear and often have a hairy texture, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

This plant plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
