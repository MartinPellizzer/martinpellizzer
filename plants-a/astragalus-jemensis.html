Astragalus jemensis

Astragalus jemensis, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in alpine and subalpine regions. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh, high-altitude environments.

Astragalus jemensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
