Alopecurus cassius

Alopecurus cassius, commonly known as red top, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist soils and is often found in meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, and its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pale green to reddish-brown.

Alopecurus cassius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
