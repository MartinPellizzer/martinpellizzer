Alunia

Alunia, commonly known as the wild indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant has a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun. Alunia is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
