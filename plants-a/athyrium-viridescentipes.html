Athyrium viridescentipes

Athyrium viridescentipes, commonly known as the emerald fern, is a species of fern belonging to the Dryopteridaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that display a vibrant green hue, especially under optimal light conditions. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in groups known as sorangia. Native to moist, shaded environments in tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in high humidity and well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and shaded landscapes.
