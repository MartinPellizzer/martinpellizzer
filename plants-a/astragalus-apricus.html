Astragalus apricus

Astragalus apricus, commonly known as the pink milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. Astragalus apricus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
