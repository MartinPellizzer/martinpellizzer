Astragalus baionensis

Astragalus baionensis, commonly known as the Baionensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus baionensis is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
