Acacia irrorata subsp. velutinella

Acacia irrorata subsp.

velutinella, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its pale yellow flowers and slender, often glabrous or softly hairy stems.

The leaves are bipinnate, with small, thread-like leaflets that give the plant a delicate appearance. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

This subspecies is often used in revegetation projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soils in harsh environments.
