Angiopteris lauterbachii

Angiopteris lauterbachii, commonly known as the Lauterbach's water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its large, delicate fronds that are typically bipinnate. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in dense forest undergrowth. Its rhizomes are extensively branched and help in the plant's spread and survival.

The fronds are used in traditional medicine in some regions, highlighting its cultural and ecological significance.
