Agrostistachys indica var. subintegra

Agrostistachys indica var. subintegra, commonly known as the Indian grass variant subintegra, is a perennial grass native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands, thriving in moist to wet soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a tufted growth habit.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
