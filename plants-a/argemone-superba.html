Argemone superba

Argemone superba, commonly known as the Indian safflower, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, ribbed stems. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain oily seeds. These seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.

Argemone superba is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
