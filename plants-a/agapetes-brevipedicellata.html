Agapetes brevipedicellata

Agapetes brevipedicellata, commonly known as the short-stemmed silverleaf, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a silvery or pale waxy coating, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

Agapetes brevipedicellata thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests at high elevations.
