Aphelandra laxa

Aphelandra laxa, commonly known as the hanging croton, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, drooping stems and vibrant, colorful leaves that often display shades of yellow, red, and green. The plant belongs to the Araceae family and is known for its striking foliage rather than its flowers, which are small and inconspicuous. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Aphelandra laxa is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ability to add a tropical feel to indoor spaces.
