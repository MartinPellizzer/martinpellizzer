Agrostophyllum grubbianum subsp. grubbianum

Agrostophyllum grubbianum subsp.

grubbianum, commonly known as the Grubbian grass-leaf plant, is a small perennial herb native to specific regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in late summer.

The plant grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and locally endemic species.
