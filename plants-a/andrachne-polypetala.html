Andrachne polypetala

Andrachne polypetala, commonly known as the many-petaled sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in dry environments.

Andrachne polypetala is often found in sandy soils and is well-suited to xerophytic conditions.
