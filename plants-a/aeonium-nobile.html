Aeonium nobile

Aeonium nobile, commonly known as the Noble Aeonium, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features rosettes of fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery waxy coating. The plant produces striking clusters of yellow flowers during the spring or early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact size and ornamental appearance make it a desirable addition to both indoor and outdoor landscapes.
