Aconitum longtouense

Aconitum longtouense, commonly known as the long-tongued aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments, and are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Aconitum longtouense contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant with both ornamental and cautionary significance in botanical studies.
