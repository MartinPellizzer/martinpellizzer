Anisonema glaucinum

Anisonema glaucinum, commonly known as the blue-green mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various trees and shrubs.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a waxy, bluish-green coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered together, followed by small, round berries that are edible for some bird species. It is native to parts of Africa and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its parasitic nature allows it to draw nutrients and water from its host, while still performing photosynthesis to some extent.
