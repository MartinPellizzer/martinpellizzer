Amsinckia angustifolia

Amsinckia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and chaparral.

Amsinckia angustifolia is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native gardens.
