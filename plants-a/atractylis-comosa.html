Atractylis comosa

Atractylis comosa, commonly known as the hairy thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features hairy, rhizomatous stems and has palmately divided, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in dense, spherical heads, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Atractylis comosa is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and wastelands.
