Agrimonia pilosa var. daurica

Agrimonia pilosa var. daurica, commonly known as Daurian agrimony, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It features upright stems covered with soft, glandular hairs and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that split open to release seeds.

Agrimonia pilosa var. daurica is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along streambanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
