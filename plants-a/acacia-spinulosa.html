Acacia spinulosa

Acacia spinulosa, commonly known as the spiny wattle, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces oval, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia spinulosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
