Acaciopsis sesquijuga

Acaciopsis sesquijuga, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often divided into narrow, linear segments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
