Acer barbatum var. nigrum

Acer barbatum var. nigrum, commonly known as the black-haired maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It prefers well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate foliage and seasonal color changes.
