Artemisia portae

Artemisia portae, commonly known as the narrowleaf wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are clustered in dense, spherical shapes. Artemisia portae thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
