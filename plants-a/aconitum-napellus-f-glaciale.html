Aconitum napellus f. glaciale

Aconitum napellus f. glaciale, commonly known as the glacier aconite, is a variant of the European aconite characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. The plant features showy, spathaceous flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of large, deeply lobed leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
