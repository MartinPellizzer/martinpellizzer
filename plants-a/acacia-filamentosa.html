Acacia filamentosa

Acacia filamentosa, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like phyllodes that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge. The plant produces small, yellow globular flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

Acacia filamentosa is a hardy species that is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
