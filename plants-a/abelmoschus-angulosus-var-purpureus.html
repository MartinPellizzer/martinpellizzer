Abelmoschus angulosus var. purpureus

Abelmoschus angulosus var. purpureus, commonly known as purple okra, is a variant of the okra plant characterized by its distinctive purple foliage and fruit.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain edible seeds, similar to other okra varieties. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value, combined with its culinary uses, makes it a versatile plant in both gardens and agriculture.
