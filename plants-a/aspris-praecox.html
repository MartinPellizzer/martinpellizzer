Aspris praecox

Aspris praecox, commonly known as the early asparagus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale blue flowers in early spring, which are clustered in umbels. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies in woodland or shaded areas.

Aspris praecox is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
