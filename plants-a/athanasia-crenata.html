Athanasia crenata

Athanasia crenata, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and slightly serrated, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Athanasia crenata is often cultivated for its beauty and is adapted to dry, well-drained soils.
