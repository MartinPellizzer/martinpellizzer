Arctium pulchrum

Arctium pulchrum, commonly known as the showy burdock, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a tall, hairy stem with large, heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed. Its most distinctive feature is the spiny, bristly fruit, which contains seeds enclosed in a fibrous, woody casing.

Arctium pulchrum is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.
