Arundo arenaria

Arundo arenaria, commonly known as the desert reed, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Europe and North Africa.

It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is drought-tolerant and can survive in harsh environmental conditions with minimal water. It often forms dense clumps and is sometimes used for erosion control due to its extensive root system.

Arundo arenaria is also valued for its ornamental appeal in xeriscaping and garden design.
