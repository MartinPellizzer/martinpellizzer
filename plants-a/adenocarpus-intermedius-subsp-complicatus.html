Adenocarpus intermedius subsp. complicatus

Adenocarpus intermedius subsp.

complicatus, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to southern Africa. It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring compound leaves with a pinnate structure.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by distinctive, inflated pods that contain the seeds. Its root system is known for producing a milky sap, which is characteristic of many species in the milkweed genus.

The subspecies complicatus is distinguished by its complex leaf venation and the intricate patterns on its seed pods.
