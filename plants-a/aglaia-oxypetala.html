Aglaia oxypetala

Aglaia oxypetala, commonly known as the white aglaia, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, spherical fruits containing toxic seeds. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
