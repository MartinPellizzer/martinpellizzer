Antirrhinum comintanum

Antirrhinum comintanum, commonly known as the common snapdragon, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dry seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in meadows and along roadsides.

Antirrhinum comintanum is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
