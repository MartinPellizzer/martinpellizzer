Astragalus campylosema var. cappadocicus

Astragalus campylosema var. cappadocicus, commonly known as the Cappadocian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Turkey.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in racemes. The plant has palmately divided leaves with 5-7 leaflets, and its root system is fibrous and shallow. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and steppe environments.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
