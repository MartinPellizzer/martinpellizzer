Anthyllis vulneraria var. rubra

Anthyllis vulneraria var. rubra, commonly known as the red bladder campion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds.

The species is known for its medicinal properties, historically used for its vulnerary (wound-healing) qualities.
