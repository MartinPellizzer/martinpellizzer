Asclepias kotolo

Asclepias kotolo, commonly known as Kotolo milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It belongs to the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is characterized by its showy, orange and yellow flowers. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release silky-haired seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats and plays a vital role in supporting local pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
