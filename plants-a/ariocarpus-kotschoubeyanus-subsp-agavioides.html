Ariocarpus kotschoubeyanus subsp. agavioides

Ariocarpus kotschoubeyanus subsp.

agavioides, commonly known as the Agave-like Ariocarpus, is a succulent perennial plant native to the Chihuahuan Desert in Mexico. It features a short, thick, fleshy stem covered with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Its leaves are blue-gray, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted for arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers on a tall, branching inflorescence during the spring.

It is highly valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.
