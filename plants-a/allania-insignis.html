Allania insignis

Allania insignis, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

Allania insignis is often found in open woods, clearings, and along stream banks.
