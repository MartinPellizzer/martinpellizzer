Alsophila hirta

Alsophila hirta, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in a fibrous, hairy layer that gives it its common name. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. Alsophila hirta thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
