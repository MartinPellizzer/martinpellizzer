Arthraxon quartinianus var. monostachyus

Arthraxon quartinianus var. monostachyus, commonly known as the slender spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and wetlands.

This variant is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
