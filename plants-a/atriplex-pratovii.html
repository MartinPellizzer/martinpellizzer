Atriplex pratovii

Atriplex pratovii, commonly known as desert saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent leaves, which are often covered in a layer of salt-tolerant glands. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought resistance and efficient water conservation mechanisms. It produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences.

Atriplex pratovii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
