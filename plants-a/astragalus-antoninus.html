Astragalus antoninus

Astragalus antoninus, commonly known as Antonine milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Siberia. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine or steppe environments.

Astragalus antoninus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility in its native ecosystems.
