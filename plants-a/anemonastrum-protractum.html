Anemonastrum protractum

Anemonastrum protractum, commonly known as the long-stemmed sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its flowers are usually pink or red, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain seeds.
