Aquilegia vulgaris var. pseudoatrata

Aquilegia vulgaris var. pseudoatrata, commonly known as the black cohosh, is a variant of the common columbine.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black flowers that contrast with its green foliage. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, including North America. It typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade.

Its botanical features include compound leaves with five to seven leaflets and spurred petals that are distinctive in shape and color.
