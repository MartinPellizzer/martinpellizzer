Alocasia tonkinensis

Alocasia tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin arrowleaf, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, featuring a deep green color with a glossy texture. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, known for its unique inflorescence structure consisting of a spadix and spathe. It thrives in moist, shady environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Alocasia tonkinensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its dramatic foliage and air-purifying qualities.
