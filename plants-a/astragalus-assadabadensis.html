Astragalus assadabadensis

Astragalus assadabadensis, commonly known as the Assadabad milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by pods containing lens-shaped seeds. Astragalus assadabadensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and may have potential uses in traditional medicine.
