Anemone laevigata

Anemone laevigata, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow center. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Anemone laevigata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
