Ammannia erecta

Ammannia erecta, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its erect growth habit and red-tinged stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Ammannia erecta is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
