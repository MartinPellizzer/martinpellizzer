Aethionema grandiflorum subsp. coridifolium

Aethionema grandiflorum subsp.

coridifolium, commonly known as the Armenian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red.

The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its drought-resistant nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
