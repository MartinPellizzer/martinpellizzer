Aster furcatus f. erythractis

Aster furcatus f. erythractis, commonly known as the red-veined aster, is a variant of the common aster characterized by its striking red veins on the foliage.

This plant belongs to the family Asteraceae and is native to eastern North America. It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and produces showy purple to pink flowers in the fall. The foliage is often variegated with white and red markings, making it a distinctive ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking unique and colorful plant specimens.
