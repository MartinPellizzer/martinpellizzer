Acer mono subsp. incurvatum

Acer mono subsp.

incurvatum, commonly known as the Pacific silver fir, is a coniferous tree native to the western regions of North America. It is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in a distinctive spiral pattern.

The tree produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural range expansion. It thrives in moist, cool climates and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
