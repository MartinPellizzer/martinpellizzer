Asplenium partitum

Asplenium partitum, commonly known as the divided maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble a feather, giving it a graceful appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce small, dark green leaves that are often divided into narrow segments.

Asplenium partitum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
