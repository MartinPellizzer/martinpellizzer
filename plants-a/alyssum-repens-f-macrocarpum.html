Alyssum repens f. macrocarpum

Alyssum repens f. macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-seeded creeping alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its dense mat-forming growth habit.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant's leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
