Anacamptis champagneuxii var. grandis

Anacamptis champagneuxii var. grandis, commonly known as the Champagne orchid, is a species of terrestrial orchid native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color. The plant produces a single spike of flowers, each with a distinctive, hooded lip that is often darker than the rest of the flower. It thrives in sunny, well-drained habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
