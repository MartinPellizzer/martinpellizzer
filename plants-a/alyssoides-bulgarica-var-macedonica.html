Alyssoides bulgarica var. macedonica

Alyssoides bulgarica var. macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring rocky soils and sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant features a compact rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale blue. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often found in mountainous areas with cool, moist conditions.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitat.
