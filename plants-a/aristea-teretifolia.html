Aristea teretifolia

Aristea teretifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aristea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid regions.
