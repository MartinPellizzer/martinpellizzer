Acer dasycarpum weiri-laciniatum

Acer dasycarpum weiri-laciniatum, commonly known as the Weiri-laciniatum maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, clustered samaras with elongated wings, typical of the Acer genus. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in forested areas at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,500 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
