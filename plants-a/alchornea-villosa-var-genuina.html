Alchornea villosa var. genuina

Alchornea villosa var. genuina, commonly known as the silky alchornea, is a shrub native to parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

Alchornea villosa var. genuina is often found in moist, shaded environments such as tropical forests and riverbanks.
