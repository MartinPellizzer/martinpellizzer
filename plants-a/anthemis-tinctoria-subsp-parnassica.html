Anthemis tinctoria subsp. parnassica

Anthemis tinctoria subsp.

parnassica, commonly known as the Parnassian tansy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, aromatic leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It is known for its medicinal properties, including antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more robust growth and slightly larger flowers compared to the typical *Anthemis tinctoria*.
