Ayenia fulva

Ayenia fulva, commonly known as the golden aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in the fall, typically from September to November. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Ayenia fulva thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators during its brief flowering season.
