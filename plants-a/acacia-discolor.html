Acacia discolor

Acacia discolor, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are grouped in dense, cylindrical spikes. Acacia discolor is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for native pollinators and habitat for various wildlife.
