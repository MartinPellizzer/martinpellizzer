Abutilon fruticosum var. hepperi

Abutilon fruticosum var. hepperi, commonly known as the Hepper's velvetleaf, is a variant of the velvetleaf plant native to tropical regions.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
