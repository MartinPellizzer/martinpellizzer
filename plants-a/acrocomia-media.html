Acrocomia media

Acrocomia media, commonly known as the Brazilian palm, is a tall, slender palm native to South America.

It features a single, unbranched stem that can grow up to 20 meters in height. The plant produces large, feather-like leaves that are arranged in a fan shape at the top of the stem. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, and it produces edible fruits that are consumed by both humans and wildlife.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to thrive in a variety of tropical and subtropical conditions.
