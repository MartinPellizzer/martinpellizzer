Andropogon alectoridia

Andropogon alectoridia, commonly known as the slender bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant features narrow, blue-green leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it ecologically significant in its habitat.

Andropogon alectoridia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
