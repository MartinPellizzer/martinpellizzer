Acer laxiflorum var. longilobum

Acer laxiflorum var. longilobum, commonly known as the long-lobed maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves with elongated lobes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring before the leaves emerge. Its fruits are samaras, which are winged seeds that disperse easily by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
