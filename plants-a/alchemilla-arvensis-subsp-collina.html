Alchemilla arvensis subsp. collina

Alchemilla arvensis subsp.

collina, commonly known as field lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Rosaceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in white or silver hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
