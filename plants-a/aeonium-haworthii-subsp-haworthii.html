Aeonium haworthii subsp. haworthii

Aeonium haworthii subsp.

haworthii, commonly known as the Haworth's aeonium, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands. It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a powdery white coating, known as farina.

The plant produces tall, branched flowering stems that bear yellow flowers in the spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and striking appearance make it a desirable ornamental plant.
