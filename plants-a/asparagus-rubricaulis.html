Asparagus rubricaulis

Asparagus rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed asparagus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, wiry stems that are typically reddish in color, which distinguish it from other asparagus species. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that develop into red berries, which are a key part of its reproductive cycle. Its foliage consists of feathery, compound leaves that are adapted for efficient photosynthesis in its native habitat.

Asparagus rubricaulis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
