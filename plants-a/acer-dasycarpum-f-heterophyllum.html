Acer dasycarpum f. heterophyllum

Acer dasycarpum f. heterophyllum, commonly known as the Japanese maple with variegated leaves, is a unique cultivar of the maple tree.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, which features a mix of green, yellow, and white variegation. The plant is native to Japan and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 meters.

Its leaves are palmately lobed and turn vibrant colors in the fall, making it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
