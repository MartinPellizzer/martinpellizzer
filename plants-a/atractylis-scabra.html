Atractylis scabra

Atractylis scabra, commonly known as roughhead wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a tall, branched stem covered with rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, rough to the touch, and have a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Atractylis scabra is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
