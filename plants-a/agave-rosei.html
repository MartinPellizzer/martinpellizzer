Agave rosei

Agave rosei, commonly known as the roseate agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are often bluish-gray in color. The plant produces striking white to pink flowers on tall, branched inflorescences during the warmer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Agave rosei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional crafts.
