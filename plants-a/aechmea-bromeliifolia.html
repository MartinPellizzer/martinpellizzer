Aechmea bromeliifolia

Aechmea bromeliifolia, commonly known as the silver pine bromeliad, is a striking evergreen plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky rosette of dark green, sword-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in the center of the rosette. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Aechmea bromeliifolia is often used as a decorative plant in both indoor and outdoor settings due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
