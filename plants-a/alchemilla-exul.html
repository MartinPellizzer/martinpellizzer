Alchemilla exul

Alchemilla exul, commonly known as the lesser lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their astringent properties.

Alchemilla exul thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
