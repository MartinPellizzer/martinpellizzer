Angraecum platycornu

Angraecum platycornu, commonly known as the narrow-lobed angraecum, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers with long, slender petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are pollinated by moths, a relationship that highlights its adaptation to nocturnal pollinators.

Angraecum platycornu is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
