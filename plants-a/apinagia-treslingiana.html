Apinagia treslingiana

Apinagia treslingiana, commonly known as the Three-lobed saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Saxifragaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in meadows and slopes.

Apinagia treslingiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
