Aristida stipitata var. spicata

Aristida stipitata var. spicata, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, exhibiting a compact growth habit with slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are usually pale green to brown in color. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray hue, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
