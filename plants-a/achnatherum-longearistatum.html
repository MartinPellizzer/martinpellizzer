Achnatherum longearistatum

Achnatherum longearistatum, commonly known as the western wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous slender, awned flowers. It is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Western wheatgrass is valued for its drought tolerance and is used in ecological restoration projects.
