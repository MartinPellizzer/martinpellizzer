Acer cinerascens f. subglabrum

Acer cinerascens f. subglabrum, commonly known as the smooth-leaf maple, is a variant of the smoke maple characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves.

It is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America, typically found in moist, well-drained soils. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a finely toothed margin and a pale green underside. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras that disperse its seeds.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
