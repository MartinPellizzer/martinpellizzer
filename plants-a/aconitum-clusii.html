Aconitum clusii

Aconitum clusii, commonly known as the Italian monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, helmet-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils in partial to full shade. Aconitum clusii contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous to humans and animals if ingested.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to pollinators.
