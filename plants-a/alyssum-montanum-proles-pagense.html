Alyssum montanum proles pagense

Alyssum montanum proles pagense, commonly known as the alpine alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Alyssum montanum proles pagense is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
