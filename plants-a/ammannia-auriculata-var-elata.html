Ammannia auriculata var. elata

Ammannia auriculata var. elata, commonly known as the tall ammannia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
