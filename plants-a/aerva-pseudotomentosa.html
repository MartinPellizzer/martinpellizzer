Aerva pseudotomentosa

Aerva pseudotomentosa, commonly known as the hairy aerva, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are often followed by small, dry fruits. Aerva pseudotomentosa thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a common species in disturbed or arid environments.

Its adaptability and resilience contribute to its presence in various ecological settings.
