Acacia etbaica subsp. uncinata

Acacia etbaica subsp.

uncinata, commonly known as the Ethiopian acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Ethiopia. It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and phyllodes that are narrow and often curved.

The plant produces yellow flowers in compact spikes, which are followed by flat, leathery pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

The species is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in traditional practices.
