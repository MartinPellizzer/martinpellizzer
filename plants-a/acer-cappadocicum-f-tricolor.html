Acer cappadocicum f. tricolor

Acer cappadocicum f. tricolor, commonly known as the tricolor maple, is a unique variant of the Cappadocian maple.

It is characterized by its striking trifoliate leaves that display a combination of green, white, and red hues. This ornamental tree is native to Turkey and thrives in temperate climates. The foliage changes color throughout the seasons, adding visual interest to gardens.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic value rather than for fruit production.
