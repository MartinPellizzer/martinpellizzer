Anachyris rosea

Anachyris rosea, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.
