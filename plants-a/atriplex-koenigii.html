Atriplex koenigii

Atriplex koenigii, commonly known as shikakai, is a shrub native to the arid regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, green, edible leaves that are often used in traditional Indian cuisine. The plant is well adapted to dry environments, with succulent leaves that help store water. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The leaves are rich in nutrients and are also used in the preparation of natural dyes and as a mild cleanser for hair.
