Avonia lavbleckiana subsp. major

Avonia lavbleckiana subsp.

major, commonly known as the large-flowered avonia, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers.

The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
