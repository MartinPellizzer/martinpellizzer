Arum italicum var. wettsteinii

Arum italicum var. wettsteinii, commonly known as the Wettstein's arrowhead, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features arrow-shaped, glossy dark green leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. The plant produces a distinctive, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a colorful spathe, typically in shades of red or purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shady garden areas.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and botanical collections.
