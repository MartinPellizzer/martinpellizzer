Aristida leichhardtiana

Aristida leichhardtiana, commonly known as Leichhardt's threeawn, is a grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
