Anisopetalon careyanum

Anisopetalon careyanum, commonly known as the Malabar sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant wood and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, and its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its valuable timber and ecological importance, it is also considered a threatened species in some areas.
