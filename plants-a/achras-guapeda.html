Achras guapeda

Achras guapeda, commonly known as the Guapeda aloe, is a succulent plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted for water storage. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
