Astragalus mercklinii subsp. farsicus

Astragalus mercklinii subsp.

farsicus, commonly known as Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of western Asia, particularly Iran and Afghanistan.

The plant is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, compound leaves. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to reddish in color, arranged in dense, globular clusters.

It is adapted to dry environments and plays a role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
