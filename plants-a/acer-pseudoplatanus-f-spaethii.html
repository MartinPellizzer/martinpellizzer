Acer pseudoplatanus f. spaethii

Acer pseudoplatanus f. spaethii, commonly known as the Spaethii maple, is a rare and unique variant of the woolly maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely toothed leaves that are typically a deep green with a metallic sheen. The plant is a cultivar that exhibits a compact growth habit, making it suitable for smaller garden spaces. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras in late summer.

This form is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in horticultural settings.
