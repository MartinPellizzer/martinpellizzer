Aristea affinis

Aristea affinis, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers in dense spikes, typically during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value lies in its distinctive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
