Arundinaria variabilis var. akebono

Arundinaria variabilis var. akebono, commonly known as the Akebono bamboo, is a clump-forming grass native to Japan.

It features slender, green culms that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts and landscaping. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and cultural significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
