Allium clusianum

Allium clusianum, commonly known as the Persian onion or mountain garlic, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia, often growing in rocky or arid environments. The plant features bulbous stems and lance-shaped leaves, with clusters of white or pale purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its bulbs are used in traditional medicine and culinary practices, valued for their pungent aroma and potential health benefits.

Allium clusianum is also noted for its adaptability to poor soil conditions and drought resistance.
