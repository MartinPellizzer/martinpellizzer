Acanthus tetragonus

Acanthus tetragonus, commonly known as the four-angled acanthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features rosettes of lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with prominent veins and a distinctive, waxy texture. The plant produces spikes of small, pale blue to violet flowers in the summer months. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is well-suited to dry, rocky soils.

Acanthus tetragonus is also valued in horticulture for its ability to thrive in coastal and Mediterranean climates.
