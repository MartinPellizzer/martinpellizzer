Aegilops kotschyi var. hirta

Aegilops kotschyi var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy wild rye, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It features erect, slender stems with hairy foliage, which contributes to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
