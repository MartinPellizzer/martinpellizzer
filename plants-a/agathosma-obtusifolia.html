Agathosma obtusifolia

Agathosma obtusifolia, commonly known as Cape chamomile, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it suitable for rocky or sandy soils.

Agathosma obtusifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
