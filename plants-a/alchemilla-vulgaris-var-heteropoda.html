Alchemilla vulgaris var. heteropoda

Alchemilla vulgaris var. heteropoda, commonly known as the lesser lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of soft, velvety leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months, which are typically borne on slender, hairy stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported healing properties.
