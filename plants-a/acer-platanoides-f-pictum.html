Acer platanoides f. pictum

Acer platanoides f. pictum, commonly known as the painted maple, is a cultivar of the Norway maple.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display a mix of white, yellow, and green hues. The plant is a deciduous tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of 4 to 8 meters. Its foliage changes color throughout the seasons, offering ornamental value in both summer and autumn.

The leaves are palmately lobed, with a smooth margin and a slightly serrated edge.
