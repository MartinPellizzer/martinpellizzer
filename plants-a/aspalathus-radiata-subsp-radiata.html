Aspalathus radiata subsp. radiata

Aspalathus radiata subsp.

radiata, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it is often found in coastal and sandy environments. It is well adapted to arid conditions and can thrive in full sun.

The subspecies is notable for its ecological importance and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
