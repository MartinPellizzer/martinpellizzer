Aristolochia clusii

Aristolochia clusii, commonly known as the Brazilian pipevine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal spikes. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Aristolochia clusii is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
