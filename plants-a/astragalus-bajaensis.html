Astragalus bajaensis

Astragalus bajaensis, commonly known as the Mojave milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the deserts of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in harsh, dry conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is often found in dunes and rocky areas.

Astragalus bajaensis is also of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
