Adesmia morenonis

Adesmia morenonis, commonly known as the narrowleaf burrobrush, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features small, compound leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Adesmia morenonis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in dry landscapes.
