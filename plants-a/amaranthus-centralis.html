Amaranthus centralis

Amaranthus centralis, commonly known as the red amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and vibrant, feather-like leaves that range from green to red or purple. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Amaranthus centralis is known for its adaptability to various soil types and climates, making it a hardy species.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and as a source of edible seeds.
