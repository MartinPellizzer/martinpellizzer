Anadelphia

Anadelphia, commonly known as the "false flowers," refers to a botanical term describing a type of floral structure where the stamens are fused together but not connected to the petals.

This arrangement is distinct from a syngenesious condition, where petals and stamens are fused. Anadelphia is often observed in certain plant families, such as the Asteraceae, where it contributes to the overall symmetry and function of the flower. The fusion of stamens in anadelphia can enhance the efficiency of pollen dispersal.

This characteristic is an important feature in the reproductive strategy of many flowering plants.
