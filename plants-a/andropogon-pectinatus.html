Andropogon pectinatus

Andropogon pectinatus, commonly known as the silky grass or silky bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and a dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spikelet that is often adorned with silky, hairy bracts.

Andropogon pectinatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in ecological restoration projects.
