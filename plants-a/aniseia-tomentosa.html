Aniseia tomentosa

Aniseia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly anise, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its aromatic properties make it useful in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and digestive benefits.

Aniseia tomentosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its unique foliage and delicate blossoms.
