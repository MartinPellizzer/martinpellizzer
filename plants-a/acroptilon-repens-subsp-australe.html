Acroptilon repens subsp. australe

Acroptilon repens subsp.

australe, commonly known as the Australian spurrey, is a low-growing, perennial herb native to Australia. It features slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to form dense mats on the ground.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on elongated peduncles. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and resilience to poor soil conditions.
