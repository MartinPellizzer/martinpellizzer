Atractylis macrophylla var. incana

Atractylis macrophylla var. incana, commonly known as the woolly cow parsley, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and its clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive, soft, woolly texture.

The plant is known for its aromatic scent and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
