Allamanda angustifolia

Allamanda angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf allamanda, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has glossy, narrow leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Allamanda angustifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display.
