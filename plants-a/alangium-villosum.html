Alangium villosum

Alangium villosum, commonly known as the hairy alangium, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces red, fleshy berries that contain seeds.

Alangium villosum is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
