Amaryllis × expansa

Amaryllis × expansa, commonly known as the giant amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis papilio.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors including red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that can grow up to several feet in length.

It is a popular ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to bloom reliably indoors during the holiday season.
