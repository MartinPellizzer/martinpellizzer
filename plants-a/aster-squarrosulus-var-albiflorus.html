Aster squarrosulus var. albiflorus

Aster squarrosulus var. albiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower sand aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features narrow, gray-green leaves with a woolly texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and dunes.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
