Allium pisidicum

Allium pisidicum, commonly known as the Crimean leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus and parts of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of long, narrow leaves. The plant produces umbel-like clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers in the summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic-like flavor, making it a potential culinary and medicinal plant.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
