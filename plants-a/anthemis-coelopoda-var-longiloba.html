Anthemis coelopoda var. longiloba

Anthemis coelopoda var. longiloba, commonly known as the long-stemmed ragged robin, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color, with long, slender petals that give it a delicate appearance.

The variety longiloba is distinguished by its elongated stems and more robust foliage compared to the typical form.
