Acacia idiomorpha

Acacia idiomorpha, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are feathery in appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, which are typically followed by elongated, flat pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia idiomorpha is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment.
