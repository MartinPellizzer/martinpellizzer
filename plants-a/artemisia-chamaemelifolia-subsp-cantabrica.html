Artemisia chamaemelifolia subsp. cantabrica

Artemisia chamaemelifolia subsp.

cantabrica, commonly known as Spanish tarragon, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats. Its aromatic properties make it a valuable species in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

It is also noted for its resilience and ability to colonize disturbed areas.
