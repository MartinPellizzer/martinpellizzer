Acaena adenocarpa var. basiinermis

Acaena adenocarpa var. basiinermis, commonly known as the smooth burrplant, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces spherical, woody fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in non-native regions.
