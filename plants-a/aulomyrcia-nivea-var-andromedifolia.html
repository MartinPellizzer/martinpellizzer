Aulomyrcia nivea var. andromedifolia

Aulomyrcia nivea var. andromedifolia, commonly known as the white-flowered alderleaf, is a variant of the alderleaf plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring season. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones or near streams.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
