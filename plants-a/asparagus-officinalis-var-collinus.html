Asparagus officinalis var. collinus

Asparagus officinalis var. collinus, commonly known as the colline asparagus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, green stems and fern-like, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its edible young shoots are often harvested in the spring for culinary use.

This variety is well-suited to rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun.
