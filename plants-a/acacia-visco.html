Acacia visco

Acacia visco, commonly known as the sticky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in spikes. The plant produces flat, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple small leaflets, and the plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia visco is also known for its sticky resin, which has been used historically for various purposes, including as a natural adhesive.
