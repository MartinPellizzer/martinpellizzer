Adenodolichos caeruleus

Adenodolichos caeruleus, commonly known as the blue bean plant, is a flowering legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers, which are arranged in dense clusters. The plant has trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets. Its pods are elongated and contain edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines.

Adenodolichos caeruleus thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
