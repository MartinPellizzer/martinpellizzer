Adenodolichos punctatus subsp. decumbens

Adenodolichos punctatus subsp.

decumbens, commonly known as the creeping cowpea, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and Asia. It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded lobes.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. Its fruit is a slender, elongated pod containing several seeds.

This species is often grown as a forage crop due to its nutritional value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
