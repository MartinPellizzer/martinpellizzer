Amsinckia obliqua

Amsinckia obliqua, commonly known as the desert tobacco or Indian tobacco, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along streambanks.

Amsinckia obliqua is adapted to withstand drought and heat, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
