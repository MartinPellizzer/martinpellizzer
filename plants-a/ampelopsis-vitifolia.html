Ampelopsis vitifolia

Ampelopsis vitifolia, commonly known as the grape ivy, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are usually dark green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by blue-black berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. Its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in partial shade make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not properly managed.
