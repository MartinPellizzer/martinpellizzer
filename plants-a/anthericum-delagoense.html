Anthericum delagoense

Anthericum delagoense, commonly known as the Delagoense arum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a dense cluster on the spadix. Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped and have a waxy texture.

Anthericum delagoense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
