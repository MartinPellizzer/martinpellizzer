Aconogonon paniculatum

Aconogonon paniculatum, commonly known as the clustered spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in paniculate inflorescences, which are borne on elongated stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along roadsides.

Aconogonon paniculatum is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense colonies, making it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential weed.
