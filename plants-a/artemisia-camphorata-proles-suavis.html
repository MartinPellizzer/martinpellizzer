Artemisia camphorata proles suavis

Artemisia camphorata proles suavis, commonly known as sweet wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features aromatic, silvery-gray foliage with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, which bloom in late summer. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine.

The essential oil derived from its leaves contains compounds like camphor and thujone, contributing to its distinct aroma and therapeutic uses.
