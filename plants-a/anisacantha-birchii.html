Anisacantha birchii

Anisacantha birchii, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a hairy texture, while the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release its seeds.

Anisacantha birchii is known for its ability to thrive in arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
