Argemone mexicana var. ochroleuca

Argemone mexicana var. ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow poinsettia, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the fall, contrasting with its green, spiny bracts. The plant has alternate, simple, and hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gardens.

The yellow poinsettia is also noted for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.
