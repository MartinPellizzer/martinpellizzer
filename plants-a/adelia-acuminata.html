Adelia acuminata

Adelia acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to arid conditions.

Adelia acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
