Araucaria bernieri subsp. buchholzii

Araucaria bernieri subsp.

buchholzii, commonly known as the Buchholz's Araucaria, is a rare and ancient conifer native to the cloud forests of Ecuador. It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, pyramidal crown, with long, slender leaves that are slightly curved.

The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil, and is adapted to the cool, misty conditions of its mountainous habitat.

Due to its restricted range and habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species.
