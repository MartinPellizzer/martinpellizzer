Arisaema limbatum f. plagiostomum

Arisaema limbatum f. plagiostomum, commonly known as the broad-leaf arrowhead, is a variant of the arrowhead plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and heart-shaped cotyledons.

The flowers are typically yellow or green, with a unique structure that aids in pollination by insects.
