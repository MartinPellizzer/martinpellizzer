Aconitum jacquinii

Aconitum jacquinii, commonly known as the Jacquin's monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant features palmately lobed leaves that are typically deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. Aconitum jacquinii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in temperate climates.

It is noted for its ornamental value in gardens but contains toxic alkaloids, requiring careful handling.
