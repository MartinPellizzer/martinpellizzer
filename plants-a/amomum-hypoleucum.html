Amomum hypoleucum

Amomum hypoleucum, commonly known as the white cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces slender, erect stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a distinct, fragrant aroma. Its flowering stems are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The fruit, which is a small, three-lobed capsule, contains aromatic seeds used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
