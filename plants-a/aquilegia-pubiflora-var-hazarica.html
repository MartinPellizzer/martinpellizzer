Aquilegia pubiflora var. hazarica

Aquilegia pubiflora var. hazarica, commonly known as the Hazara monkshood, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and a hairy texture, which gives it its species name "pubiflora.". It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
