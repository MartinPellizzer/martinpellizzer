Astragalus levieri var. juzepczukii

Astragalus levieri var. juzepczukii, commonly known as the Juzepczuk's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to drought conditions. Its flowers are usually yellow or pale purple, with a distinctive spurred structure.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
