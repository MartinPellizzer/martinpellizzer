Astragalus marandicus

Astragalus marandicus, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its seeds are often used as a source of protein in some cultures.
