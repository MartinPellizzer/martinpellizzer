Aerides zollingeri

Aerides zollingeri, commonly known as the Zollinger's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a pseudobulb with a single, lance-shaped leaf and produces racemes of fragrant, showy flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale pink with a dark red lip, and they bloom in the summer. It prefers shady, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated for its unique floral display.
