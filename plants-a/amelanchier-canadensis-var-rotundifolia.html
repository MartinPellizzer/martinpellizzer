Amelanchier canadensis var. rotundifolia

Amelanchier canadensis var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the roundleaf serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It features alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often rounded at the base, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of white to pink flowers in the spring, followed by edible, sweet berries in the summer. It typically grows in moist to wet soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and wildlife.
