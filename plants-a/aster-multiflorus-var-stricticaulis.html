Aster multiflorus var. stricticaulis

Aster multiflorus var. stricticaulis, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed aster, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It features upright, slender stems that are often branched and covered with fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers in the late summer and early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and prominent veins.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
