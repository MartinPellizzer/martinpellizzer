Acaena multifida subsp. abavia

Acaena multifida subsp.

abavia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burr bush, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile. It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
