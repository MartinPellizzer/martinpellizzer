Aglaia chartacea

Aglaia chartacea, commonly known as the white aglaia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Meliaceae family, which includes mahogany and other hardwood trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant yellow flowers. It produces woody capsules that contain aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine.
