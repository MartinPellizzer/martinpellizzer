Anemone bucharica subsp. baissunensis

Anemone bucharica subsp.

baissunensis, commonly known as the Baissun anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its basal leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are often hairy.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in dry climates.
