Anisostachya ungemachii

Anisostachya ungemachii, commonly known as the East African grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces inflorescences that are typically spike-like, with bract-like structures that distinguish it from other grasses. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils.

Anisostachya ungemachii is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential ecological roles in its native environment.
