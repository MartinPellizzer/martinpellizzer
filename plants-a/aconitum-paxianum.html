Aconitum paxianum

Aconitum paxianum, commonly known as the Paxian aconite, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and striking blue to purple flowers. The plant has palmately divided leaves and produces follicular fruits containing elliptical seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a dangerous plant for both humans and animals.
