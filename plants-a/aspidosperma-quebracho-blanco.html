Aspidosperma quebracho-blanco

Aspidosperma quebracho-blanco, commonly known as white quebracho, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It is characterized by its thick, fissured bark and large, compound leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It is valued for its hard, heavy wood used in construction and furniture-making.

Additionally, it has medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its alkaloid content.
