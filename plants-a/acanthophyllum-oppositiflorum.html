Acanthophyllum oppositiflorum

Acanthophyllum oppositiflorum, commonly known as the spiked flax, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its spiny stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
