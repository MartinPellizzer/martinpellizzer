Astragalus curvidens

Astragalus curvidens, commonly known as the bent-veined milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

Astragalus curvidens plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.
