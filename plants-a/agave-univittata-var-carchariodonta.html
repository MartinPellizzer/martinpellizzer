Agave univittata var. carchariodonta

Agave univittata var. carchariodonta, commonly known as the Mexican spineless agave, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically spineless, though some varieties may have short spines. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and for making fibers, while the plant itself is adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments.

Agave univittata var. carchariodonta is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its striking appearance.
