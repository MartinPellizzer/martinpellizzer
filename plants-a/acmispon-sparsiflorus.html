Acmispon sparsiflorus

Acmispon sparsiflorus, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its ecological importance is significant in desert ecosystems due to its ability to fix nitrogen and support biodiversity.
