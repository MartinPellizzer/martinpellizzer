Antennaria latisquamea

Antennaria latisquamea, commonly known as the broad-scale mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically forms a mat-like growth pattern, with leaves that are broad and covered in fine, white to grayish hairs. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish in color and are arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems.

Antennaria latisquamea is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
