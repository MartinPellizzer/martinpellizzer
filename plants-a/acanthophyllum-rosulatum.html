Acanthophyllum rosulatum

Acanthophyllum rosulatum, commonly known as the Rosulate Acanthophyllum, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth form. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with thick, fleshy leaves that store water and reduce transpiration.

This species is often found in sandy soils and is an important component of desert ecosystems.
