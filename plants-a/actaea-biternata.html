Actaea biternata

Actaea biternata, commonly known as the white cohosh, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with three pairs of leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in early summer, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

Actaea biternata is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
