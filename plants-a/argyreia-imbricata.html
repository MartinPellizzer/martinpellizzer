Argyreia imbricata

Argyreia imbricata, commonly known as the velvet tangle or blue curl, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety blue flowers that bloom in dense clusters. The plant has heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Argyreia imbricata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique and vibrant floral display.
