Actaea arizonica

Actaea arizonica, commonly known as Arizona black cohosh, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that bear large, palmately compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its berries are dark red and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by birds.

Actaea arizonica typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in woodlands and canyons.
