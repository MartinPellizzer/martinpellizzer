Apium thorei

Apium thorei, commonly known as the European sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of Europe.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in saline or sandy soils and is often found along the seashore. Its hollow, cylindrical stems and finely divided leaves are adapted to withstand coastal conditions.

Apium thorei is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
