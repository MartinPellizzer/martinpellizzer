Allium lutescens

Allium lutescens, commonly known as the yellow onion, is a bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its fibrous, yellowish bulbs that store nutrients. The plant produces grass-like leaves and clusters of white or pale purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often used as a culinary herb and vegetable.

Its bulbs are rich in sulfur compounds, contributing to its pungent aroma and flavor.
