Allium roseum var. carneum

Allium roseum var. carneum, commonly known as the pink onion or rose garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in a dense cluster at the top of the stem. The plant is characterized by its mild, sweet aroma and edible bulbs, which are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate flowers and foliage.
