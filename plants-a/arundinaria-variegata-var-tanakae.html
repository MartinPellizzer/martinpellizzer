Arundinaria variegata var. tanakae

Arundinaria variegata var. tanakae, commonly known as the Tanaka's bamboo, is a variant of the variegated bamboo species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display a combination of green, white, and sometimes pink or red hues. This plant typically grows as a clump-forming grass, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.

The plant is also valued for its resilience and ability to tolerate partial shade.
