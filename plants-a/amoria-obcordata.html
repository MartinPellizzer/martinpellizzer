Amoria obcordata

Amoria obcordata, commonly known as the heartleaf nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Amoria obcordata is known for its toxic properties, containing compounds such as solanine and scopolamine.
