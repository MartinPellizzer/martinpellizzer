Asplenium pellucidum subsp. pseudohorridum

Asplenium pellucidum subsp.

pseudohorridum, commonly known as the silver spleenwort, is a fern species native to parts of Asia and Africa. It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery coating.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as rock crevices or along stream banks. Its fronds are usually narrow and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
