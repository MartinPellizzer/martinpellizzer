Acacia semitriptera

Acacia semitriptera, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and narrow, feather-like leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Acacia semitriptera is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
