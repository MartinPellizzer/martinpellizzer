Agave lophantha var. angustifolia

Agave lophantha var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often blue-gray in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, white flowers, which bloom once in its lifetime. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
