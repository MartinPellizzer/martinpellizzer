Allocarya scalpta

Allocarya scalpta, commonly known as the narrowleaf mountain-mahogany, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and mountainous regions. Its bark is typically rough and fissured, providing habitat for various insects and small animals.

Allocarya scalpta plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
