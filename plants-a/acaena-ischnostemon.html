Acaena ischnostemon

Acaena ischnostemon, commonly known as the small-stemmed wood rose, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its prickly, thorn-like stems and clusters of small, white to pink flowers. The plant produces bright red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for native birds. Its foliage consists of alternate, simple leaves with a rough, hairy texture.

Acaena ischnostemon is well adapted to the temperate climate of New Zealand and thrives in well-drained soils.
