Acacia tortilis var. pubescens

Acacia tortilis var. pubescens, commonly known as the umbrella thorn acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-shaped crown formed by its branching pattern. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of leaves. It produces yellow flowers in summer, followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds.

This species is well adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and nitrogen fixation capabilities.
