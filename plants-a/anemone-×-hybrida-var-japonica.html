Anemone × hybrida var. japonica

Anemone × hybrida var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a hybrid perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, single or double flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
