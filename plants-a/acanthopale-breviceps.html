Acanthopale breviceps

Acanthopale breviceps, commonly known as the short-headed acanthopale, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink and purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
