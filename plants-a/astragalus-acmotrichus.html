Astragalus acmotrichus

Astragalus acmotrichus, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soils and during drought periods.

Astragalus acmotrichus is also known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
