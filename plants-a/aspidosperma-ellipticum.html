Aspidosperma ellipticum

Aspidosperma ellipticum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Pitanga*, is a species of flowering plant in the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its elliptical leaves and showy, red or orange flowers. The plant produces small, round, red fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in furniture-making.

The plant also contains alkaloids, which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
