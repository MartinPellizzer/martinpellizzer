Aechmea azurea

Aechmea azurea, commonly known as the blue-tongued bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue and silver rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant's foliage is often adorned with intricate patterns and a waxy texture that enhances its aesthetic appeal. Aechmea azurea produces striking inflorescences with colorful flowers that emerge from the leaf rosette.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Morphology

Aechmea azurea has broad, strap-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, with a dark green color and a waxy texture.

The plant has a short, stout stem with a smooth, grayish-brown bark that grows upward from the base. Its flowers are clustered in a dense inflorescence, featuring bright blue petals and yellow stamens, blooming during the summer months and pollinated by insects. The fruit is a capsule that contains small, black seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and help anchor the plant in the soil while absorbing moisture and nutrients.
