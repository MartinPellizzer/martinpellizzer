Aristida chaseae

Aristida chaseae, commonly known as Chase's threeawn, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spikelet-like inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture that helps reduce water loss.

Aristida chaseae is adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
