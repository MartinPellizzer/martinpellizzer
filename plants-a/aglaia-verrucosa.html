Aglaia verrucosa

Aglaia verrucosa, commonly known as the warty aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its rough, bark-like texture on its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, spiny fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a smooth or slightly wavy margin.
