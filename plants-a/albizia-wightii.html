Albizia wightii

Albizia wightii, commonly known as the Indian siris, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in clusters. Its seeds are encased in elongated, woody pods that split open when mature.

Albizia wightii is often used in traditional medicine and for soil stabilization due to its deep root system.
