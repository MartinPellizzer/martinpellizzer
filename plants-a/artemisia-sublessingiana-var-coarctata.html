Artemisia sublessingiana var. coarctata

Artemisia sublessingiana var. coarctata, commonly known as narrowleaf wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves with a strong, camphor-like scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are typically clustered in dense, rounded formations. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
