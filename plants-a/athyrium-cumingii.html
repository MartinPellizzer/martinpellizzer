Athyrium cumingii

Athyrium cumingii, commonly known as the Cuming's lady fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically divided into numerous narrow segments. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clusters of bright green, lance-shaped leaves.

Athyrium cumingii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in humid, cool climates.
