Anacamptis sancta

Anacamptis sancta, commonly known as the Holy Orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowers have a unique structure with three sepals and three petals, one of which is prominently hooded.

Anacamptis sancta is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
