Allium rubicundum

Allium rubicundum, commonly known as the red onion, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its purple to reddish outer skin and white to pink inner layers. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer. Its bulbs are used as a culinary ingredient and are rich in sulfur compounds that contribute to its pungent aroma.

Allium rubicundum is relatively hardy and can thrive in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
