Allium canadense var. ovoideum

Allium canadense var. ovoideum, commonly known as the oval-leaf garlic.

It is a perennial herb native to North America, characterized by its bulbous base and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in culinary applications.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
