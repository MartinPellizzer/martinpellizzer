Acantholimon caryophyllaceum var. brachystachium

Acantholimon caryophyllaceum var. brachystachium, commonly known as the short-stalked flaxvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This variety is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil.
