Acacia sciophanes

Acacia sciophanes, commonly known as the shade acacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny foliage and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has adapted to low-water environments through its deep root system and waxy leaf surfaces that reduce water loss. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the seed pods produce hard, angular seeds.

Acacia sciophanes is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of firewood in its native habitats.
