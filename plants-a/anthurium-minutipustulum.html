Anthurium minutipustulum

Anthurium minutipustulum, commonly known as the small-pustule anthurium, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features dark green, leathery leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive pattern of veins. Its flowers are small and clustered, often surrounded by a showy, brightly colored spathe.

This plant is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive in warm, humid environments.
