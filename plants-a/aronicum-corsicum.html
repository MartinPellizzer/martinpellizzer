Aronicum corsicum

Aronicum corsicum, commonly known as Corsican arnica, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

Aronicum corsicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
