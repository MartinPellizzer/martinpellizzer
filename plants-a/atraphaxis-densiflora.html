Atraphaxis densiflora

Atraphaxis densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower burrgrass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by woody, spiny fruits. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss.

Atraphaxis densiflora is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
