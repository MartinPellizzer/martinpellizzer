Aconitum pekinense

Aconitum pekinense, commonly known as the Peking monkshood, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of northern China.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple hooded flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant grows up to 1.5 meters tall, with large, palmately compound leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aconitum pekinense is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that should be handled with care.
