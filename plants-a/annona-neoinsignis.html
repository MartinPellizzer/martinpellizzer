Annona neoinsignis

Annona neoinsignis, commonly known as the Mexican custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces large, fragrant, yellowish-green fruits with a rough, spiny exterior. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a waxy surface.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit.
