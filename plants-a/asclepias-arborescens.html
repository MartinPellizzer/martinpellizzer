Asclepias arborescens

Asclepias arborescens, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems that can reach up to six feet in height. The plant produces clusters of pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Asclepias arborescens is a vital host plant for monarch butterfly larvae and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
