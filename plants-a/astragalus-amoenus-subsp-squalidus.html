Astragalus amoenus subsp. squalidus

Astragalus amoenus subsp.

squalidus, commonly known as the dirty milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It is a perennial herb that produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

This subspecies is often found in steppe and desert environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
