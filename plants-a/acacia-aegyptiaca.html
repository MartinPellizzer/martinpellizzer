Acacia aegyptiaca

Acacia aegyptiaca, commonly known as the Egyptian acacia or sacred thorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the hot, arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and bipinnate leaves that are often reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, followed by woody pods containing flat, fibrous seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in poor soil and extreme temperatures.

Acacia aegyptiaca has been used traditionally for its timber, gum, and medicinal properties.
