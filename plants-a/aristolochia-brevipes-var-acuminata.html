Aristolochia brevipes var. acuminata

Aristolochia brevipes var. acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular, maroon-colored flowers that resemble a pipe, with a distinctive hooded shape. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

The species is part of the Aristolochiaceae family, known for its complex chemical compounds and ecological significance.
