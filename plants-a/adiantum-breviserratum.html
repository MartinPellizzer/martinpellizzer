Adiantum breviserratum

Adiantum breviserratum, commonly known as the narrow-serrated maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically bright green. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves with short, blunt teeth along the edges, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Adiantum breviserratum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
