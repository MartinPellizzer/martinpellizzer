Astragalus austrinus

Astragalus austrinus, commonly known as the Australian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus austrinus is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along coastal areas.
