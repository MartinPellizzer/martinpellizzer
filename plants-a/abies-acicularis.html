Abies acicularis

Abies acicularis, commonly known as the white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color and have a distinctive white bloom on the underside. The tree produces cones that are cylindrical and often hang downward, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.

The white fir plays an important ecological role, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
