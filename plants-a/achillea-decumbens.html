Achillea decumbens

Achillea decumbens, commonly known as creeping yarrow, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silver-gray, fuzzy foliage and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system and spreads by stolons, making it a hardy ground cover. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry environments.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers have been used historically for their medicinal properties.
