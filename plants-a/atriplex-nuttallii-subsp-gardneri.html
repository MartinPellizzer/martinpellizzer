Atriplex nuttallii subsp. gardneri

Atriplex nuttallii subsp.

gardneri, commonly known as silverleaf shadscale, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, glandular hairs.

The leaves are typically alternate and have a waxy texture, aiding in water retention in arid environments. It produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, bead-like fruits.

This plant is adapted to rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid regions.
