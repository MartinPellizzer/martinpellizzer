Astragalus lilacinus

Astragalus lilacinus, commonly known as the blue milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus lilacinus is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
