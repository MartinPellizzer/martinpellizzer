Agapetes glandulosissima

Agapetes glandulosissima, commonly known as the glandular agapete, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the Andes mountains in South America, particularly in regions of Peru and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves that are covered in glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in the spring.

Agapetes glandulosissima is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as cloud forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
