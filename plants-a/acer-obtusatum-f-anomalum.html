Acer obtusatum f. anomalum

Acer obtusatum f. anomalum, commonly known as the Japanese maple with variegated leaves, is a unique cultivar of the Japanese maple species.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage that displays shades of white, yellow, and green, creating a dramatic visual effect. The plant is typically deciduous, shedding its leaves in the autumn season. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

This ornamental tree is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
