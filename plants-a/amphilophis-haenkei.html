Amphilophis haenkei

Amphilophis haenkei, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Amphilophis haenkei is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
