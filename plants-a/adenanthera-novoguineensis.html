Adenanthera novoguineensis

Adenanthera novoguineensis, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or bean family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy that provides shade. Its bark is smooth and grayish, while its flowers are small and clustered in racemes, often with a yellowish or greenish hue.

The plant is adapted to dry climates and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.
