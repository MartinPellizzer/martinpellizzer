Acianthera quadricristata

Acianthera quadricristata, commonly known as the four-ribbed acianthera, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and roots that are often covered in a dense layer of velvety pubescence. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

The orchid blooms during the late summer and early autumn, making it a notable species in tropical and subtropical regions.
