Acacia melanoxylon f. frutescens

Acacia melanoxylon f. frutescens, commonly known as the red bloodwood, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, gnarled trunk and dark reddish-brown bark that peels in thin, flaky layers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spherical clusters, which are followed by flat, leathery pods. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This form is a compact variant of the species, adapted to harsh, dry environments.
