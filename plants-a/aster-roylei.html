Aster roylei

Aster roylei, commonly known as the Afghan aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, with narrow, linear leaves that help reduce water loss.

Aster roylei is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
