Agathaea strigosa

Agathaea strigosa, commonly known as the rough agathis, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing oily seeds. It is valued for its durable wood and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Agathaea strigosa plays a significant role in the ecological systems of its native habitats.
