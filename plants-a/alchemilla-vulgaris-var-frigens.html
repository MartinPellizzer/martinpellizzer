Alchemilla vulgaris var. frigens

Alchemilla vulgaris var. frigens, commonly known as silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features basal rosettes of silver-gray, hairy leaves that are covered in fine, soft trichomes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in loose clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and adaptable species suitable for various ecological and ornamental purposes.
