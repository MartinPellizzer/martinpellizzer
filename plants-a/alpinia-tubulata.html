Alpinia tubulata

Alpinia tubulata, commonly known as the tubular ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Alpinia tubulata thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.
