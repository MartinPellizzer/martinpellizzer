Acinopetala livingstoneana

Acinopetala livingstoneana, commonly known as the Livingstone's acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The bark is often used in traditional medicine, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal.
