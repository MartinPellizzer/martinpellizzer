Aspilia thouarsii

Aspilia thouarsii, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in a spherical shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Aspilia thouarsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
