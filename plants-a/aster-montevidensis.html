Aster montevidensis

Aster montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevidense aster, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Uruguay and southern Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its showy purple or blue flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster montevidensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic contribution.
