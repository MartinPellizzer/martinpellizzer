Astrantia candida

Astrantia candida, commonly known as white wood betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It features showy, white flower heads that resemble small daisies, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Astrantia candida is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
