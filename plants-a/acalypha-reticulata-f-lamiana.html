Acalypha reticulata f. lamiana

Acalypha reticulata f. lamiana, commonly known as the silverleaf kochia, is a variant of the species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage due to dense pubescence. The leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape and often have a reticulate venation pattern. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
