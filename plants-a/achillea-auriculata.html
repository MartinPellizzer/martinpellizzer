Achillea auriculata

Achillea auriculata, commonly known as the golden yarrow, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. Its yellow flowers, which bloom in summer, are composed of both ray and disk florets, and the leaves are narrow and linear with a distinctive silvery underside.

Achillea auriculata is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant in gardens.
