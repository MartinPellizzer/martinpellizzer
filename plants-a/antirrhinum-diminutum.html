Antirrhinum diminutum

Antirrhinum diminutum, commonly known as the dwarf snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a compact, herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of only a few inches. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Antirrhinum diminutum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
