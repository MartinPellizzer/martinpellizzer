Acacia colleana

Acacia colleana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are typically narrow and arranged in a feathery pattern. The plant produces yellow, spherical flower heads that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Acacia colleana is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought conditions.
