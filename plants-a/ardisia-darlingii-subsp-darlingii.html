Ardisia darlingii subsp. darlingii

Ardisia darlingii subsp.

darlingii, commonly known as the Darling's ardisia, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub that typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments.

The plant features glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, white flowers followed by bright red berries. Its botanical characteristics include a compact growth habit and a preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
