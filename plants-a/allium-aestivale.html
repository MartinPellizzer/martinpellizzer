Allium aestivale

Allium aestivale, commonly known as summer garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a strong garlic scent and is often used in culinary applications. Its bulbs are smaller than those of common garlic and have a milder flavor.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other allium species.
