Arnica subplumosa var. macillenta

Arnica subplumosa var. macillenta, commonly known as the alpine arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers with prominent yellow centers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used for their anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
