Allium porrum var. breve

Allium porrum var. breve, commonly known as the globe artichoke, is a cultivated variant of the artichoke plant.

It is characterized by its compact, globe-shaped inflorescence, which is typically harvested before the flowers open. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a cool climate for optimal growth.

Its tender stems and leaves are edible, making it a popular vegetable in Mediterranean cuisine.
