Agrostis canina f. arida

Agrostis canina f. arida, commonly known as the desert bentgrass, is a variant of the bentgrass species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant is well adapted to dry environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and efficient water use. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or marginal habitats.
