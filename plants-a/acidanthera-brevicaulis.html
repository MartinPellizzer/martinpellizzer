Acidanthera brevicaulis

Acidanthera brevicaulis, commonly known as the dwarf lily, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to rock gardens or container plantings.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice among gardeners seeking low-maintenance, visually appealing plants.
