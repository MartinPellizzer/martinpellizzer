Anthyllis polycephala var. podocephala

Anthyllis polycephala var. podocephala, commonly known as the woolly broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Its dense, bushy growth habit makes it a useful plant for erosion control and habitat provision.
