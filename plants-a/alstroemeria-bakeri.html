Alstroemeria bakeri

Alstroemeria bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's Flame, is a striking perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, resembling flames. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves and produces clusters of blooms that are highly ornamental. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Alstroemeria bakeri is also valued for its long-lasting cut flowers and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
