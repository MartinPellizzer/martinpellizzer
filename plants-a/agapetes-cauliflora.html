Agapetes cauliflora

Agapetes cauliflora, commonly known as the calypso flower, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its unique cauliflorous flowering habit, where flowers and sometimes fruits develop directly on the trunk and branches. The plant typically grows in cloud forests at high altitudes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually white or pink, and it produces edible berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

Agapetes cauliflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
