Aster chapmanii

Aster chapmanii, commonly known as Chapman's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. This perennial herb typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, producing vibrant purple to white petals.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
