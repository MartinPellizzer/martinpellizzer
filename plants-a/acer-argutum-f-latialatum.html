Acer argutum f. latialatum

Acer argutum f. latialatum, commonly known as the broad-leaf maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves, which are typically larger and more broadly shaped compared to the species. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
