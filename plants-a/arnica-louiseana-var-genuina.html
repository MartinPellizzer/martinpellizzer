Arnica louiseana var. genuina

Arnica louiseana var. genuina, commonly known as the Louisiana mountain arnica, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers in the summer. It is characterized by its hairy foliage and showy inflorescence.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is valued for its medicinal properties.
