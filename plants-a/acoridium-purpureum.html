Acoridium purpureum

Acoridium purpureum, commonly known as the purple acoridium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acoraceae and is characterized by its distinctive, strap-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-white with a purple tinge. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Acoridium purpureum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
