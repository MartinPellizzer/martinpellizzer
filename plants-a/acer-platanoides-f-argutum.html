Acer platanoides f. argutum

Acer platanoides f. argutum, commonly known as the Norway maple variegated form, is a cultivar of the Norway maple, characterized by its distinctive variegated foliage.

It features leaves with a mix of green, white, and yellow colors, creating an ornamental appearance. The plant is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

This form is known for its moderate growth rate and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
