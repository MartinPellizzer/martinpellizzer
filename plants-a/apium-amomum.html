Apium amomum

Apium amomum, commonly known as Chinese celery or wild celery, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables. The plant features slender, hollow stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Apium amomum is often cultivated for its edible leaves and roots, which have a distinct, slightly spicy flavor.
