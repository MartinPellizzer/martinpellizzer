Aceratium sericeum

Aceratium sericeum, commonly known as the silky acanthus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are covered in a dense layer of silky, white hairs. The plant produces showy, spire-like clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in horticulture, it has been cultivated for centuries.
