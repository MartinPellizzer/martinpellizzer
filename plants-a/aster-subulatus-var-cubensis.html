Aster subulatus var. cubensis

Aster subulatus var. cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban aster, is a flowering plant native to Cuba.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and clusters of tiny, star-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that produces copious nectar, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
