Aegiphila lewisiana

Aegiphila lewisiana, commonly known as Lewis's aegiphila, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color.

Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.
