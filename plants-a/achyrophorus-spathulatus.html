Achyrophorus spathulatus

Achyrophorus spathulatus, commonly known as the broad-leaved achyrophorus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, spatulate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive basal rosette structure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptations to humid climates.
