Asperula taurina

Asperula taurina, commonly known as the Turkish speedwell, is a low-growing evergreen perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, bright blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has oval, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy to the touch. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Asperula taurina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
