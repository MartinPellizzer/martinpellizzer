Acostaea trilobata

Acostaea trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed water clover, is a aquatic plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It has a distinctive appearance with its small, floating leaves that are divided into three lobes, giving it a clover-like appearance. The plant thrives in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater environments. It plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for aquatic organisms.

Acostaea trilobata is often used in pond ecosystems for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
