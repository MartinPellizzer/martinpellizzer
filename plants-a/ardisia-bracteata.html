Ardisia bracteata

Ardisia bracteata, commonly known as the Christmas berry, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is a small evergreen shrub that produces clusters of bright red berries, which are often consumed by birds and small mammals. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and produces small white flowers with a pleasant fragrance. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

The berries are also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
