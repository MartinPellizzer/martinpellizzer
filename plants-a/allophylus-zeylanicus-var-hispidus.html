Allophylus zeylanicus var. hispidus

Allophylus zeylanicus var. hispidus, commonly known as the hairy Indian alder, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional medicine.
