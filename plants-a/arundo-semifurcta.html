Arundo semifurcta

Arundo semifurcta, commonly known as the narrowleaf reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. This plant is often found growing in wetlands, along riverbanks, and in disturbed soils. It has a robust root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

Arundo semifurcta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
