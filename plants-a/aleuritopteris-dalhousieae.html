Aleuritopteris dalhousieae

Aleuritopteris dalhousieae, commonly known as the Dalhousie fern, is a species of water fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, feathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1.5 meters. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are typically located on the underside of the fronds.

Aleuritopteris dalhousieae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
