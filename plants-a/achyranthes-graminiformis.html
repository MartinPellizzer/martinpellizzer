Achyranthes graminiformis

Achyranthes graminiformis, commonly known as the slender knotweed, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in areas with high humidity. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its adaptability and is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental plant.

Morphology

Achyranthes graminiformis has narrow, grass-like leaves that are linear in shape, alternate in arrangement, and typically range from 5 to 15 centimeters in length, with a dark green color.

The stem is erect, cylindrical, and has a smooth texture, with a light green to reddish-brown color, and grows in a clumping pattern. The flowers are small, clustered in dense panicles, with greenish-white petals, blooming from late spring to early summer, and are primarily pollinated by wind. The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that disperses seeds by wind, and the seeds are small, dry, and winged for easy transport.

The roots are fibrous and extensive, helping the plant anchor itself and absorb nutrients from the soil.
