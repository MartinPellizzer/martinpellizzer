Antirrhinum mohavea

Antirrhinum mohavea, commonly known as Mohave monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Antirrhinum mohavea is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
