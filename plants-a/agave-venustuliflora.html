Agave venustuliflora

Agave venustuliflora, commonly known as the beautiful-flowered agave, is a succulent plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with white to pale yellow flowers, which bloom once in its lifetime. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its striking appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
