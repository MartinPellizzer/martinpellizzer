Aristolochia kankauensis

Aristolochia kankauensis, commonly known as the Kankau pipevine, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, pipe-like flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that contain the seeds. It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its aromatic foliage.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
