Acacia latzii

Acacia latzii, commonly known as the Lutz acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in compact clusters. The plant produces flat, bipinnate leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Acacia latzii is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in full sun, making it well-suited for xerophytic conditions.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various desert wildlife species.
