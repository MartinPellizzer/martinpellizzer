Andropogon finitimus

Andropogon finitimus, commonly known as the Florida panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. This plant thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in swampy areas and along the edges of water bodies. Its flowering inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, white to pale green flowers.

Andropogon finitimus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
