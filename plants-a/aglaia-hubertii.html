Aglaia hubertii

Aglaia hubertii, commonly known as the golden apple tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with potential pharmacological properties.

Aglaia hubertii is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
