Anthericum liliago var. transmontanum

Anthericum liliago var. transmontanum, commonly known as the Transmontane Lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped white or pale pink flowers. The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-gray hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
