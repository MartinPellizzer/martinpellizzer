Astragalus hamrinensis

Astragalus hamrinensis, commonly known as the Hamrin milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iraq and Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to brown flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in arid and rocky environments.

Astragalus hamrinensis is often found in steppe and shrubland habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native range.
