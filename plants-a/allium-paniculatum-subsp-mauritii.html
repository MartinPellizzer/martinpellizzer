Allium paniculatum subsp. mauritii

Allium paniculatum subsp.

mauritii, commonly known as the Mauritius onion, is a perennial herb native to the island of Mauritius. It features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces umbels of small, white to pale purple flowers.

The plant is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats. It has a strong onion scent and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

This subspecies is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
