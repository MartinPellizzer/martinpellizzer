Agropyron repens f. heberhachis

Agropyron repens f. heberhachis, commonly known as the hairy wheatgrass, is a variant of the common wheatgrass species.

It is characterized by its hairy leaves and stems, which distinguish it from the typical form. This plant is a perennial grass that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open, disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

The hairy wheatgrass is valued for its ornamental use and as a forage plant for livestock.
