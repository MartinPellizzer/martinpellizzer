Arctostaphylos pungens subsp. nevadensis

Arctostaphylos pungens subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra foothill manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant produces red or orange berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of chaparral and woodland ecosystems.
