Aristida curtifolia

Aristida curtifolia, commonly known as slender hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny grains of grass seed. The plant is adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in sandy or rocky soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its ecological significance is enhanced by its ability to thrive in harsh environments with minimal water.
