Aira tenorei var. mixta

Aira tenorei var. mixta, commonly known as the mixed aira, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or as a decorative ground cover.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
