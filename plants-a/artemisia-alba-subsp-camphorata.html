Artemisia alba subsp. camphorata

Artemisia alba subsp.

camphorata, commonly known as white sage, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and aromatic, aromatic leaves that emit a strong camphor-like scent.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its medicinal and cultural significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional practices.
