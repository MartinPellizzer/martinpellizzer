Achillea umbellata subsp. vulgaris

Achillea umbellata subsp.

vulgaris, commonly known as common yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia. It features a basal rosette of gray-green, fern-like leaves and produces flat, composite flower heads in shades of white to pink.

The plant is characterized by its finely divided foliage and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a long flowering period, typically from late spring to early autumn, and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its historical use in traditional medicine.
