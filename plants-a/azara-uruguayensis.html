Azara uruguayensis

Azara uruguayensis, commonly known as the Uruguay azara, is a small evergreen tree native to South America, particularly found in Uruguay and parts of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Azara uruguayensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and fragrance.
