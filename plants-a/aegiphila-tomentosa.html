Aegiphila tomentosa

Aegiphila tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly aegiphila, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its densely hairy foliage, which helps reduce water loss in its dry environment. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is of interest to botanists studying xerophytic plants.
