Anthemis dubia

Anthemis dubia, commonly known as the Alpine cudweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features silver-gray foliage with finely toothed leaves, and its flowers are typically yellow with a central disc. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, Anthemis dubia is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
