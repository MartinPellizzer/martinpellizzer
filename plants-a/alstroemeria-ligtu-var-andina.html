Alstroemeria ligtu var. andina

Alstroemeria ligtu var. andina, commonly known as the Andean lily, is a striking South American plant characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is native to the Andes Mountains, where it thrives in high-altitude environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, clustered blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements due to its long-lasting flowers and elegant appearance.
