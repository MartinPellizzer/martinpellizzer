Anthurium chequitavense

Anthurium chequitavense, commonly known as the Chequitavense arrowhead, is a striking tropical plant native to Colombia.

It features vibrant, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a glossy texture. The plant produces spadices, which are unique inflorescences that resemble a cluster of small flowers, often in shades of red or yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium chequitavense is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
