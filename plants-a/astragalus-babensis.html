Astragalus babensis

Astragalus babensis, commonly known as the Babens milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or pale yellow flowers in late spring. Its seed pods are slender and contain small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Astragalus babensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife.
