Albizia acradena

Albizia acradena, commonly known as the African siris, is a leguminous shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in agricultural settings. Albizia acradena is also valued for its timber and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its adaptability to various soil types contributes to its widespread distribution across sub-Saharan Africa.
