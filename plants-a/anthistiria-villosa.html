Anthistiria villosa

Anthistiria villosa, commonly known as the hairy anthistiria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Anthistiria villosa is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and dry landscapes.
