Aspalathus campestris

Aspalathus campestris, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is well adapted to dry conditions and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Aspalathus campestris is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine.
