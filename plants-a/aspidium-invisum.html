Aspidium invisum

Aspidium invisum, commonly known as the invisible fern, is a unique member of the Dryopteridaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that give it a ghostly appearance, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest undergrowth or rocky crevices. Its spores are released in the form of small, brown clusters, and it reproduces through both sexual and asexual means.

Aspidium invisum is notable for its subtle beauty and ecological significance in maintaining forest biodiversity.
