Acer saccharum var. acuminatum

Acer saccharum var. acuminatum, commonly known as the pointed sugar maple, is a variant of the sugar maple native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, elongated leaves that often turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The tree typically grows to heights of 20-30 meters and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming more rugged with age.

This variety is often used in landscaping due to its striking foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
