Acacia spinosa

Acacia spinosa, commonly known as the screwpod wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Acacia spinosa thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to poor soil conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various native animals.
