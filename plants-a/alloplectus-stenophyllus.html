Alloplectus stenophyllus

Alloplectus stenophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed alpine plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it adapts to harsh climatic conditions such as cold temperatures and strong winds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss and protect against UV radiation. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually green or pale yellow, and it reproduces through seeds dispersed by wind.

Alloplectus stenophyllus plays a role in stabilizing soil in its fragile habitat and supports local biodiversity.
