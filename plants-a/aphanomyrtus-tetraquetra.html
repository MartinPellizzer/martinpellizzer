Aphanomyrtus tetraquetra

Aphanomyrtus tetraquetra, commonly known as the mountain gum, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalypts and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, typically in spring, followed by woody, egg-shaped capsules.

Aphanomyrtus tetraquetra is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and forest margins.
