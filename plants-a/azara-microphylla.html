Azara microphylla

Azara microphylla, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *azara*, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 5 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Azara microphylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

Its ecological importance and unique botanical features make it a significant species in its native habitat.
