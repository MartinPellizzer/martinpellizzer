Aquilegia chaplinei

Aquilegia chaplinei, commonly known as the chapman's columbine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred petals that come in shades of red, purple, and yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
