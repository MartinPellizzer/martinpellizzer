Acacia scorpioides var. nilotica

Acacia scorpioides var. nilotica, commonly known as the African thorn acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow, spherical flower heads. The plant produces long, slender, straight thorns, which are often used for fencing or as a source of fiber. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry climates.

The wood of this acacia is hard and durable, historically used for construction and tool-making.
