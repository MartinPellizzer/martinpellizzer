Alcea microchiton

Alcea microchiton, commonly known as the dwarf hollyhock, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that are covered in soft, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Alcea microchiton thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
