Acidocroton gentryi

Acidocroton gentryi, commonly known as Gentry's acidocroton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a small, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. Its flowers are inconspicuous, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
