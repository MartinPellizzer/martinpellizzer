Astragalus mixtus

Astragalus mixtus, commonly known as the mixed milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pale purple. Native to regions of Central Asia, including parts of China and the Russian Federation, it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus mixtus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
