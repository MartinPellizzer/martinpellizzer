Alloplectus tetragonus

Alloplectus tetragonus, commonly known as the four-angled thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, serrated margin.

Alloplectus tetragonus is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in sunny, open habitats.
