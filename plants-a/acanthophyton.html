Acanthophyton

Acanthophyton, commonly known as the spiny leaf plant, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiny or toothed leaves, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Acanthophyton species produce small, tubular flowers that are often brightly colored, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

These plants are valued in horticulture for their ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in diverse soil conditions.
