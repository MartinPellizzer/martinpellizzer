Acacia longispinea

Acacia longispinea, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and its distinctive yellow flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant produces small, flat pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Acacia longispinea thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

It is a hardy species that contributes to local biodiversity and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
