Aspidosperma pohlianum

Aspidosperma pohlianum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Pohlianum*, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy, fragrant pink or white flowers. The tree produces small, red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a yellowish aril. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant also has historical use in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
