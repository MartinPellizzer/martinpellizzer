Acacia scorpioides var. adstringens

Acacia scorpioides var. adstringens, commonly known as the African silver acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that are often clustered at the ends of branches. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in winter, adding ornamental value. Its bark and leaves contain tannins, which have been traditionally used for their astringent properties.

It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
