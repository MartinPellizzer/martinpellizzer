Anemone obtusiloba var. chrysantha

Anemone obtusiloba var. chrysantha, commonly known as the golden anemone, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are rounded and often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
