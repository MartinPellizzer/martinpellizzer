Athamanta macrosperma

Athamanta macrosperma, commonly known as the broad-seeded sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.

Athamanta macrosperma is often found in sandy or rocky soils, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments.
