Arnica lessingii subsp. genuina

Arnica lessingii subsp.

genuina, commonly known as the Colorado gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high elevations of the Rocky Mountains. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and is adapted to cold, alpine conditions. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

It plays an important role in pollination by attracting various insects, including bees and butterflies.
