Andropogon schinzii

Andropogon schinzii, commonly known as Schinz's switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. This species thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions. Its flowering inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers, which contribute to its ornamental value.

Andropogon schinzii is also valued for its ecological benefits, including soil stabilization and wildlife habitat support.
