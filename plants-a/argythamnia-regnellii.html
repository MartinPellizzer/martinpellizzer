Argythamnia regnellii

Argythamnia regnellii, commonly known as the desert gumplant, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.

Argythamnia regnellii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
