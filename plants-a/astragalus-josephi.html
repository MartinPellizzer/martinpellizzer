Astragalus josephi

Astragalus josephi, commonly known as the Joseph's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in dry, sunny environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus josephi is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
