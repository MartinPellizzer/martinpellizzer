Astragalus podosphaerus

Astragalus podosphaerus, commonly known as the broad-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain lens-shaped seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

Astragalus podosphaerus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
