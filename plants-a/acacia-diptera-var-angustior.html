Acacia diptera var. angustior

Acacia diptera var. angustior, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and bipinnate leaves that are narrow and often hairy. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late summer, which are arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for dryland environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.
