Anthericum nematodes

Anthericum nematodes, commonly known as the lily of the valley nematode, are microscopic worms that primarily infest the roots of plants in the genus Anthericum.

These nematodes are plant-parasitic and feed on the root cells, causing stunted growth and reduced vigor in affected plants. They are particularly prevalent in moist, temperate climates and can be a significant threat to ornamental and agricultural plants. The damage they cause is often difficult to detect until the plant shows signs of decline.

Effective management involves soil treatments and the use of resistant plant varieties.
