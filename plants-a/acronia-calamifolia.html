Acronia calamifolia

Acronia calamifolia, commonly known as the flameleaf maple, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red to orange foliage that turns vibrant in the fall. The tree has smooth, light gray bark and exudes a strong, aromatic scent when cut. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a distinctive flame-like appearance during the growing season.

Acronia calamifolia prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian habitats.
