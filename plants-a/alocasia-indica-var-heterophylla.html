Alocasia indica var. heterophylla

Alocasia indica var. heterophylla, commonly known as the African mask plant, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its dramatic, often asymmetrical foliage.

Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and deeply veined, with a glossy green surface that can vary in color depending on light exposure. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, warm environments with high levels of indirect light. It is a member of the Araceae family, which includes other ornamental plants like calla lilies and peace lilies.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant or in shaded outdoor gardens.
