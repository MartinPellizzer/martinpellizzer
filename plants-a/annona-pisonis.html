Annona pisonis

Annona pisonis, commonly known as the soursop or custard apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Americas.

It produces a large, spiky, green fruit with a sweet, creamy flesh that is rich in nutrients. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish flowers. Its fruit is widely used in cuisines and traditional medicine across its native range.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
