Artemisia × burnatii

Artemisia × burnatii, commonly known as the silverleaf sage, is a hybrid shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which results from dense covering of fine, hair-like trichomes. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale yellow to greenish in color. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Artemisia × burnatii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
