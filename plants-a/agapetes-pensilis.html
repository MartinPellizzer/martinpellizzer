Agapetes pensilis

Agapetes pensilis, commonly known as the hanging heather, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white. Its leaves are evergreen, glossy, and arranged in a compact, dense manner.

Agapetes pensilis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
