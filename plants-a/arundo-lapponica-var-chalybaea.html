Arundo lapponica var. chalybaea

Arundo lapponica var. chalybaea, commonly known as the Lapland reed, is a perennial grass native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater bodies. Its fibrous stalks are used in traditional basket-making and as a source of fiber for various crafts.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wetland wildlife.
