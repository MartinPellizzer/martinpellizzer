Arctostaphylos diversifolia

Arctostaphylos diversifolia, commonly known as the Sierra buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with leathery leaves that are typically dark green and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by red or purple berries in the fall. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it an important species in its native ecosystem.
