Astragalus quindecimjugus

Astragalus quindecimjugus, commonly known as the fifteen-horned milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its hairy stems and the presence of small, inflated pods that contain its seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ecological significance and medicinal potential, it is of interest to botanists and herbalists alike.
