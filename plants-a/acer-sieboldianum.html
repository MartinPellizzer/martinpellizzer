Acer sieboldianum

Acer sieboldianum, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its ornamental foliage, which displays vibrant colors of red, orange, and yellow during the autumn season. The tree has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its bark is smooth and reddish-brown when young. It typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 meters and prefers well-drained soil and partial shade.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
