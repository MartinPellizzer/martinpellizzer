Acacia oreophila

Acacia oreophila, commonly known as the mountain acacia, is a species of flowering plant native to the highland regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and compound leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow, spherical flower heads that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds. Acacia oreophila thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the cooler, more humid conditions of its mountainous habitat.

Its ecological importance includes providing shelter and food for local wildlife and contributing to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
