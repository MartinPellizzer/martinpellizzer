Alchemilla kemlensis

Alchemilla kemlensis, commonly known as the Kemlens alkanet, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Alchemilla kemlensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in partial shade.
