Andropogon sorghum var. caudatus

Andropogon sorghum var. caudatus, commonly known as switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stalks and feathery, silver-green plumes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and is drought-resistant, making it suitable for marginal lands. Its deep root system helps prevent soil erosion and improves soil structure.

Switchgrass is also valued for its potential as a bioenergy crop due to its high biomass yield and low input requirements.
