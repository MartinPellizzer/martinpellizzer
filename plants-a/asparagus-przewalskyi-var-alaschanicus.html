Asparagus przewalskyi var. alaschanicus

Asparagus przewalskyi var. alaschanicus, commonly known as the Alaschan asparagus, is a rare and distinctive plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and small, scale-like leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
