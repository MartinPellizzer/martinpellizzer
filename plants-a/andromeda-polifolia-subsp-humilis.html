Andromeda polifolia subsp. humilis

Andromeda polifolia subsp.

humilis, commonly known as the lowland wintergreen, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Europe and western Asia. It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters, featuring dense clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant has leathery, evergreen leaves that are dark green and glossy, often arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and heathlands.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to bloom during late winter and early spring.
