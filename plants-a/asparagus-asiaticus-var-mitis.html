Asparagus asiaticus var. mitis

Asparagus asiaticus var. mitis, commonly known as the Chinese asparagus or silver asparagus, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in early spring, followed by spiny, cone-like fruits that mature to a reddish color. Its foliage consists of narrow, feather-like leaves that are arranged in a dense, grass-like manner.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and resilience.
