Abutilon newberryi

Abutilon newberryi, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Abutilon newberryi is drought-tolerant and often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
