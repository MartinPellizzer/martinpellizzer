Aregelia microps

Aregelia microps, commonly known as the dwarf aregelia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, featuring delicate, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming during the warmer months. Aregelia microps thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders.
