Armeria maritima subsp. miscella

Armeria maritima subsp.

miscella, commonly known as sea thrift, is a low-growing, evergreen flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia. It features dense clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has narrow, silver-gray leaves that are adapted to tolerate salt spray and sandy soils. It is often used in coastal landscaping for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Cariophyllaceae and is characterized by its succulent stems and compact growth habit.
