Astragalus lipskyanus

Astragalus lipskyanus, commonly known as the Liskyn's milkvetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stem, trifoliate leaves, and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus lipskyanus is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
