Andropogon ternarius var. glaucescens

Andropogon ternarius var. glaucescens, commonly known as blue panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green blades that are often coated with a waxy cuticle. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically greenish or purplish in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Its fibrous root system makes it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
