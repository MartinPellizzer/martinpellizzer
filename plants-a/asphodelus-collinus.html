Asphodelus collinus

Asphodelus collinus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and tall, erect flowering stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It is often found in open woodlands, grasslands, and along coastal areas.

Asphodelus collinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
