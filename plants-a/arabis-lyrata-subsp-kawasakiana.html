Arabis lyrata subsp. kawasakiana

Arabis lyrata subsp.

kawasakiana, commonly known as the Kawasakiana rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are basal, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in clusters.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
