Asparagus gypsaceus

Asparagus gypsaceus, commonly known as the desert asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in dense, branched inflorescences. Its root system is extensive and deep, enabling it to access moisture from lower soil layers.

Asparagus gypsaceus is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in xeriscaping.
