Astragalus kurdicus var. muschianus

Astragalus kurdicus var. muschianus, commonly known as the Kurdish milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and northern Iraq.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It possesses deep root systems that help it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

This variety is also noted for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to fully understand its applications.
