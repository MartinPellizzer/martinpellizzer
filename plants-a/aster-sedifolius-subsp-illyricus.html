Aster sedifolius subsp. illyricus

Aster sedifolius subsp.

illyricus, commonly known as the Illyrian aster, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, serrated leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers in the summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It has hairy stems and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology compared to other Aster sedifolius varieties.
