Aster tweedyi

Aster tweedyi, commonly known as Tweedy's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces violet to blue-purple ray florets. It is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in full sun.

Tweedy's aster is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
