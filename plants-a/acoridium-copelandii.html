Acoridium copelandii

Acoridium copelandii, commonly known as the golden club, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acoraceae family and is characterized by its broad, strap-like leaves and yellowish-green flower spikes. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in humid, moist environments. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Acoridium copelandii is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
