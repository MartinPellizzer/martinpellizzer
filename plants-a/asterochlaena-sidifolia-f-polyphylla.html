Asterochlaena sidifolia f. polyphylla

Asterochlaena sidifolia f. polyphylla, commonly known as the velvetleaf or golden aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, pubescent leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces showy yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its foliage is characterized by a dense, velvety texture, which gives it its common name.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical regions.
