Adesmia echinus

Adesmia echinus, commonly known as the spiny burr legume, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow or white flowers followed by elongated, spiny pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with low rainfall.

Adesmia echinus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small desert animals.
