Adiantopsis radiata f. bifurcata

Adiantopsis radiata f. bifurcata, commonly known as the bifurcated maidenhair fern, is a rare and delicate fern species characterized by its finely divided, bi-lobed fronds.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is native to specific regions in South America, particularly in Brazil. The plant exhibits a unique frond structure with a central rachis that splits into two distinct branches, giving it its common name. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires high humidity and well-draining soil to flourish.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
