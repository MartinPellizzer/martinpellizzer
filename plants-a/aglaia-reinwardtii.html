Aglaia reinwardtii

Aglaia reinwardtii, commonly known as the yellow mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by fruits that are initially green and turn yellow when ripe. The fruit has a thick, leathery rind and contains several segments with a sweet, aromatic pulp.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in medicine and cuisine.
