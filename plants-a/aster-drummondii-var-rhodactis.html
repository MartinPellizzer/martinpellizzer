Aster drummondii var. rhodactis

Aster drummondii var. rhodactis, commonly known as the showy aster, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, prairies, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The variety rhodactis is notable for its more intense flower color and slightly larger blooms compared to the typical form of Aster drummondii.
