Aster moranensis

Aster moranensis, commonly known as the Moran's aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and produces small, violet-blue flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and somewhat woody.

Aster moranensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
