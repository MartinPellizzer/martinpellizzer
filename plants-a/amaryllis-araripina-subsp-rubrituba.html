Amaryllis araripina subsp. rubrituba

Amaryllis araripina subsp.

rubrituba, commonly known as the red-tube amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to Brazil. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height.

The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a distinctive red tube at the base of the petals. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge.

This species is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
