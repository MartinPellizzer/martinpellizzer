Allium lilacinum

Allium lilacinum, commonly known as the purple garlic or lilac garlic, is a bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, pale lilac-colored flowers. The plant is closely related to other allium species, such as garlic and onions, and is valued for its ornamental appeal. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, often used in culinary applications.

Allium lilacinum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
