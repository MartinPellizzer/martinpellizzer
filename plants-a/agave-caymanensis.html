Agave caymanensis

Agave caymanensis, commonly known as the Cayman Islands agave, is a succulent plant native to the Cayman Islands.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color with a waxy coating. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that bears white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in the spring. It is well adapted to arid and coastal environments, with deep roots that help it survive in poor soil conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native region.
