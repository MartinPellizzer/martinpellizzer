Asplenium × breynii

Asplenium × breynii, commonly known as the hybrid maidenhair fern, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Asplenium trichomanes and Asplenium bulbiferum.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green with a glossy appearance. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in sori, a feature common among ferns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Asplenium × breynii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
