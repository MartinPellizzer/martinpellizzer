Apium graveolens convar. dulce

Apium graveolens convar. dulce, commonly known as celery, is a biennial or perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hollow, ridged stems and finely divided, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in umbels, which are typical of the Apiaceae family. Its roots and stalks are widely cultivated for their distinct flavor and nutritional value.

Celery is often used in cooking and is also valued for its medicinal properties.
