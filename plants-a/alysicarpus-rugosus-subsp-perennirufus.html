Alysicarpus rugosus subsp. perennirufus

Alysicarpus rugosus subsp.

perennirufus, commonly known as the hairy senna, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically covered in a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
