Aspalathus rubescens

Aspalathus rubescens, commonly known as red bush tea, is a small shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant is well adapted to the local climate, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils. Its leaves are harvested and processed to make a popular herbal tea.

Aspalathus rubescens is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
