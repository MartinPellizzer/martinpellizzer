Artanthe adunca f. angustifolia

Artanthe adunca f. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf artanthe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its slender, angular stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments and suitable for xeriscaping.
