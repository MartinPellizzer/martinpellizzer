Aloe soccotorina

Aloe soccotorina, commonly known as the Cape aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, spiny stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height, and its leaves are thick, fleshy, and covered in a white powdery substance. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators like bees and birds. Aloe soccotorina is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid environments.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties, with its gel used in traditional remedies.
