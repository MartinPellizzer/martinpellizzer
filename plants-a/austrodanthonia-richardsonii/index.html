Austrodanthonia Richardsonii

Richardson's wallaby grass, scientifically known as Austrodanthonia richardsonii, is a resilient grass species native to the south-western region of Western Australia. This plant thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and coastal dunes. Its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions makes it an important component of local ecosystems. The grass is characterized by its fine, tufted growth and distinctive silver-green foliage. Despite its hardy nature, it remains a rare and ecologically significant plant that requires careful conservation efforts.
