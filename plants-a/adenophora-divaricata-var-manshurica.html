Adenophora divaricata var. manshurica

Adenophora divaricata var. manshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to violet in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in temperate regions.
