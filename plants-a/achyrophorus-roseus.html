Achyrophorus roseus

Achyrophorus roseus, commonly known as the rosebay willowherb, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its tall, reddish stems and showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in wetlands. It is a member of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
