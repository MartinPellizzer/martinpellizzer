Anacampseros paradoxa

Anacampseros paradoxa, commonly known as the paradoxical stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a striking addition to arid landscapes.
