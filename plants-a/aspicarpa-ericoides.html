Aspicarpa ericoides

Aspicarpa ericoides, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically green or reddish in color, and it produces tiny, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Aspicarpa ericoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
