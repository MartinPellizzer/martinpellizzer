Acacia senegal subsp. trispinosa

Acacia senegal subsp.

trispinosa, commonly known as the gum arabic acacia, is a small, thorny shrub native to the Sahel region of Africa. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, rounded leaflets.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by pods containing a sticky, resinous substance called gum arabic, which is harvested for commercial use. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and its economic importance as a source of natural gum.
