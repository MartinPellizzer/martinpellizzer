Athyrium delavayi

Athyrium delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's shield fern, is a species of flowering plant in the family Athyriaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China, particularly in Yunnan Province. The plant is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often adorned with small, spore-bearing structures called sori. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
