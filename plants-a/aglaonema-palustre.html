Aglaonema palustre

Aglaonema palustre, commonly known as the swamp aglaonema, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display shades of green, white, and pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as swamps and wetlands. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its aesthetic appeal.

Aglaonema palustre is also valued for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
