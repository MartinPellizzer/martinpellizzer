Alstroemeria lutea

Alstroemeria lutea, commonly known as the yellow alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers with six tepals. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil and full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Alstroemeria lutea is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of climates, making it a popular choice for horticultural use.
