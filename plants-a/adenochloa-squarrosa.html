Adenochloa squarrosa

Adenochloa squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrosa grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies.

Adenochloa squarrosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
