Anthemis discoidea

Anthemis discoidea, commonly known as the meadow sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in early summer, and the plant has a compact, low-growing habit.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
