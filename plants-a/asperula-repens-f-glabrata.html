Asperula repens f. glabrata

Asperula repens f. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth trailing azalea, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to Japan.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous, giving it its specific epithet "glabrata.". The plant produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white, blooming in early spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
