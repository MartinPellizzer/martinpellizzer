Artemisia indica var. kumaonensis

Artemisia indica var. kumaonensis, commonly known as Kumaon wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in panicles. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, typically at elevations between 2,000 and 4,000 meters. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and digestive properties.

The species is also valued for its role in local ecosystems and as a source of essential oils.
