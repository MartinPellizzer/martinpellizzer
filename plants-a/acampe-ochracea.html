Acampe ochracea

Acampe ochracea, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Acampe ochracea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
