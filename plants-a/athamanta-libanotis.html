Athamanta libanotis

Athamanta libanotis, commonly known as the Lebanon bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountains of the Eastern Mediterranean.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of blue to violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Athamanta libanotis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
