Amyris caranifera

Amyris caranifera, commonly known as the Cuban sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its fragrance and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, waxy surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by clustered, woody fruits containing seeds.

Amyris caranifera thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forest habitats.
