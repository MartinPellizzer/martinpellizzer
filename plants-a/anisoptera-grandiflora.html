Anisoptera grandiflora

Anisoptera grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is known for its elongated, woody pods that contain edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local cultures.
