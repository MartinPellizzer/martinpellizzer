Anthurium lividispica

Anthurium lividispica, commonly known as the silver-spined anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a silvery or white midrib, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, spadix-like flowers surrounded by a colorful spathe, often in shades of red or yellow. It thrives in humid, warm environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Anthurium lividispica is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and air-purifying qualities.
