Adinandra tomentosa

Adinandra tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly adinandra, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are usually elliptical or ovate, with a leathery texture and a silvery-white underside. It produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by dark blue to black berries.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
