Aquilegia vulgaris subsp. paui

Aquilegia vulgaris subsp.

paui, commonly known as the Pyrenean columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region. It features showy, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple, with five petals and a distinctive hooded shape.

The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and it thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils. It is often found in rocky or limestone habitats at higher elevations.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
