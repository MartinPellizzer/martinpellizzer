Atractylis erosodentata

Atractylis erosodentata, commonly known as the serrated marsh foxtail, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its clumping growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows and marshy areas.

Atractylis erosodentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
