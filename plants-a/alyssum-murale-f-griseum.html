Alyssum murale f. griseum

Alyssum murale f. griseum, commonly known as the silverleaf alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its silvery-gray foliage.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.
