Armeniaca vulgaris var. rushanica

Armeniaca vulgaris var. rushanica, commonly known as the Turk's Turban, is a variant of the peach tree characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often ribbed fruit.

The plant is native to the Caucasus region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in a fine pubescence, while the flowers are white with a pinkish tinge. The fruit, which can reach up to 10 cm in length, is typically harvested in late summer and is prized for its sweet, aromatic flavor.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value as well as its culinary and medicinal uses.
