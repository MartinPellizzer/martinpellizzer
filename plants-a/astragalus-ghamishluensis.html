Astragalus ghamishluensis

Astragalus ghamishluensis, commonly known as the Ghamishlu milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant is adapted to survive in dry, rocky environments, with deep root systems that help it access water.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
