Azalea multiflora

Azalea multiflora, commonly known as the Southern azalea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of funnel-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its blooming season occurs in late spring to early summer, producing vibrant pink to red flowers.

Azalea multiflora is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form thickets.
