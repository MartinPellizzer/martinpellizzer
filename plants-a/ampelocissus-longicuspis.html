Ampelocissus longicuspis

Ampelocissus longicuspis, commonly known as the long-spined fig-leaf gooseberry, is a woody vine native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, edible berry that turns red when mature.

Ampelocissus longicuspis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
