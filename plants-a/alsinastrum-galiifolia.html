Alsinastrum galiifolia

Alsinastrum galiifolia, commonly known as the curly dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as along stream banks and in marshy areas.

Its roots are known for their medicinal properties and have been used in traditional herbal remedies.
