Alchemilla vulgaris subvar. semisecta

Alchemilla vulgaris subvar. semisecta, commonly known as the lesser lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features basal rosettes of broadly ovate, softly hairy leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicles during late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its leaves are known for their astringent properties and have been used in traditional herbal remedies.
