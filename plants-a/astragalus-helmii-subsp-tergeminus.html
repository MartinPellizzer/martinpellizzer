Astragalus helmii subsp. tergeminus

Astragalus helmii subsp.

tergeminus, commonly known as the three-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands.

The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and its flowers are usually pink to purple in color. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
