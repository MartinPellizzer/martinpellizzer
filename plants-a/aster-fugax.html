Aster fugax

Aster fugax, commonly known as the annual aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and showy composite flower heads that typically bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant produces small, tubular florets surrounded by ray florets that range in color from white to purple. Aster fugax thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open habitats.

It is an annual species, completing its life cycle within a single growing season.
