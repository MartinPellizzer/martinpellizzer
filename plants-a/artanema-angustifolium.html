Artanema angustifolium

Artanema angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf artanema, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its botanical features include a basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that rises above the foliage.

Artanema angustifolium is often found in the Andes and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
