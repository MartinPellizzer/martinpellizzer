Aspidium lobulatum

Aspidium lobulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and indoor plant collections.
