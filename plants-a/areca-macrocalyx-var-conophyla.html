Areca macrocalyx var. conophyla

Areca macrocalyx var. conophyla, commonly known as the giant areca palm, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach several meters in diameter. The plant produces inflorescences with small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The variety conophyla is distinguished by its unique leaf structure and robust growth habit.
