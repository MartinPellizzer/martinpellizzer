Agropyron barbulatum

Agropyron barbulatum, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves that are often covered with short, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, open woodlands, and disturbed sites.

Agropyron barbulatum is valued for its erosion control capabilities and is sometimes used in restoration ecology.
