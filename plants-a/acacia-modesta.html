Acacia modesta

Acacia modesta, commonly known as the white wattle, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces yellow flowers in cylindrical spikes, which bloom in spring and attract various pollinators. Acacia modesta is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its wood is hard and durable, historically used for construction and tool-making by indigenous communities.
