Anisopappus longipes

Anisopappus longipes, commonly known as the longpod dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by elongated, bristly seed pods. Its botanical features include a taproot system and hairy surfaces on its stems and leaves.

Anisopappus longipes is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
