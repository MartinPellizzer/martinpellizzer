Acacia mimica var. angusta

Acacia mimica var. angusta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features slender, angular stems and narrow, feather-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
