Anthericum rautanenii

Anthericum rautanenii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arum, is a herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and a spadix surrounded by a showy spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and foliage structure.
