Andropogon condensatus var. typicus

Andropogon condensatus var. typicus, commonly known as the tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blue-green leaves that turn golden in the fall. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of small, spike-like flowers.

Andropogon condensatus var. typicus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
