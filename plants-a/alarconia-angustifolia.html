Alarconia angustifolia

Alarconia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf alarconia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in terminal panicles, and they bloom in shades of white or pale pink.

Alarconia angustifolia is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
