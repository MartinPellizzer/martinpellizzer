Aloe humilis subvar. semiguttata

Aloe humilis subvar. semiguttata, commonly known as the guttate aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features short, upright stems and narrow, fleshy leaves that are typically bluish-green with a waxy coating. The leaves are adorned with small, translucent glands that give the plant its "guttate" appearance. It produces yellow flowers in terminal clusters during the warmer months.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
