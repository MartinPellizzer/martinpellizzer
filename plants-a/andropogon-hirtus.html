Andropogon hirtus

Andropogon hirtus, commonly known as hairy bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. Its flowering spikes are usually tall and cylindrical, producing small, spikelet-based flowers.

Andropogon hirtus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
