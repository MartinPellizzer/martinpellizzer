Alopecurus geniculatus var. strictus

Alopecurus geniculatus var. strictus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mouse-ear, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-angled, hairy seeds.

This variety is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in supporting insect and bird populations.
