Achillea herba-rotta

Achillea herba-rotta, commonly known as lesser yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and flat, composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The species is known for its medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
