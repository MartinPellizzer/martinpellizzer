Avenula schelliana

Avenula schelliana, commonly known as the Schell's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has slender, triangular stems and produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a waxy coating.

Avenula schelliana is valued for its ecological role in supporting insect populations and maintaining soil stability.
