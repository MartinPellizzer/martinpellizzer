Atriplex suckleyi

Atriplex suckleyi, commonly known as Suckley's saltbush, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a layer of fine, salt-excreting crystals. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in saline soils and along coastal areas. It produces greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are pollinated by insects.

Atriplex suckleyi is adapted to withstand drought and high salinity, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.
