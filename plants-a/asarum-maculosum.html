Asarum maculosum

Asarum maculosum, commonly known as the spotted asarum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and unique, fragrant flowers that resemble a pipe. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a rhizomatous root system. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive, intricate pattern of veins and spots.

Asarum maculosum is valued in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
