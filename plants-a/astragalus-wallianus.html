Astragalus wallianus

Astragalus wallianus, commonly known as the Wallian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus wallianus is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
