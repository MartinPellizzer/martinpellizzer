Argyreia calcicola

Argyreia calcicola, commonly known as the rock morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry environments. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine, silky hair.

Argyreia calcicola is often found growing on rocky outcrops and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
