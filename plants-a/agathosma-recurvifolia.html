Agathosma recurvifolia

Agathosma recurvifolia, commonly known as the Cape garlic or wild garlic, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fleshy leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Agathosma recurvifolia is also valued for its unique scent and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
