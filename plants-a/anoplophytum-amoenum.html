Anoplophytum amoenum

Anoplophytum amoenum, commonly known as the velvetleaf spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, succulent stems and broad, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid environments and can thrive in well-drained soils.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
