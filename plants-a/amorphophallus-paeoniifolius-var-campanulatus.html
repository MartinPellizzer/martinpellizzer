Amorphophallus paeoniifolius var. campanulatus

Amorphophallus paeoniifolius var. campanulatus, commonly known as the elephant's foot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves and a thick, tuberous root system that can grow to significant sizes. The plant produces striking, hooded flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tuber is also used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
