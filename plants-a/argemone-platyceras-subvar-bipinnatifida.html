Argemone platyceras subvar. bipinnatifida

Argemone platyceras subvar. bipinnatifida, commonly known as the two-feathered argemone, is a variant of the argemone plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnatifid leaves, which are finely divided and give the plant a feathery appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid conditions.

The seeds of this plant are encased in a hard, spiky capsule and are used in traditional medicine in some regions.
