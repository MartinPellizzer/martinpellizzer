Albizia floribunda

Albizia floribunda, commonly known as the African velvetwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in racemose inflorescences. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinctive, flaky texture.

Albizia floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
