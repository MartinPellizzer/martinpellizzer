Amelanchier purpusii

Amelanchier purpusii, commonly known as the Pacific serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that ripen in late summer, turning from red to dark blue. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value.

Amelanchier purpusii is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
