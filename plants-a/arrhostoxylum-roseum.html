Arrhostoxylum roseum

Arrhostoxylum roseum, commonly known as the roseate cholla, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, segmented stems that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it stores water in its fleshy stems, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

The plant is also known for its ability to form dense stands, contributing to the ecological structure of desert habitats.
