Antimima nobilis

Antimima nobilis, commonly known as the noble antimima, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its botanical classification places it in the family Apiaceae, which includes parsley and carrots.

Antimima nobilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
