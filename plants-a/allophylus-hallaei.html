Allophylus hallaei

Allophylus hallaei, commonly known as the mountain alder, is a small evergreen shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and leathery, elliptical leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spherical, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife. Allophylus hallaei thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests and alpine meadows.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to the challenging conditions of its native habitat.
