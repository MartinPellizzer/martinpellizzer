Acinodendron caudigerum

Acinodendron caudigerum, commonly known as the long-tailed acacia, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often glandular and emit a strong, unpleasant odor. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Acinodendron caudigerum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
