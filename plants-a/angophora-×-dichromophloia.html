Angophora × dichromophloia

Angophora × dichromophloia, commonly known as the Sydney red gum, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Angophora costata and Angophora robusta.

It is a medium to large tree characterized by its reddish-barked trunk and lance-shaped leaves. The plant is native to eastern Australia, particularly in the Sydney region, where it thrives in a variety of soil types. It produces small, red flowers followed by woody capsules containing seeds, contributing to its ecological significance in local ecosystems.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in timber production.
