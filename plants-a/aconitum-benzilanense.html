Aconitum benzilanense

Aconitum benzilanense, commonly known as the Beni Aconite, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and a tall, unbranched stem that supports its showy inflorescence. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and toxicity, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens but requires careful handling.
