Appendicula uncata

Appendicula uncata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue or violet. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in dense rosettes, and its stems are short and sturdy. Appendicula uncata thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
