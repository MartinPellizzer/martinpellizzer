Artemisia schischkinii

Artemisia schischkinii, commonly known as Schischkin's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are clustered in panicles, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Artemisia schischkinii is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
