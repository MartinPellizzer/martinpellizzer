Astragalus cuatrecasasii

Astragalus cuatrecasasii, commonly known as the Cuatrecasas' milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique adaptations.
