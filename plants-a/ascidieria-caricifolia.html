Ascidieria caricifolia

Ascidieria caricifolia, commonly known as the water spinach or kangkong, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glossy, with a slightly succulent texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white in color. Its roots are edible and often used in various cuisines, particularly in Asian countries.

Ascidieria caricifolia thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often cultivated in wetland areas or along water bodies.
