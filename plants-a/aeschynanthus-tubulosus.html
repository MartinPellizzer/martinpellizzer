Aeschynanthus tubulosus

Aeschynanthus tubulosus, commonly known as the red-tipped pelargonium, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red bracts that surround small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually dark green and glossy, with a velvety texture.

Aeschynanthus tubulosus is popular in horticulture for its vibrant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
