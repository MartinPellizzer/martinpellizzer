Achyranthes aspera f. subgrandifolia

Achyranthes aspera f. subgrandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered prickly chaffflower, is a variant of the common cowhair plant.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its upright, branched stems and hairy surfaces. The leaves are typically lanceolate, with serrated edges and a rough texture. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and are usually greenish-white in color.

This subspecies is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
