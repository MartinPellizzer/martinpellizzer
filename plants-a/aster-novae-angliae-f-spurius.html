Aster novae-angliae f. spurius

Aster novae-angliae f. spurius, commonly known as the New England aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy purple to pink composite flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties.
