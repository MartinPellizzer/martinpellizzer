Aspidium aculeatum var. rufibarbatum

Aspidium aculeatum var. rufibarbatum, commonly known as the red-tailed shield fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant features wiry, sparsely hairy rhizomes and produces small, clustered sporangia on the underside of its leaves. Its fronds are typically lance-shaped and may have a slightly curled appearance.

This variety is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices.
