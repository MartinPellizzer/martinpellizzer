Asphodelus dunensis

Asphodelus dunensis, commonly known as the Mediterranean asphodel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry grasslands. The plant produces tall, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of pale yellow or greenish-white flowers. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and a short rhizome, which help it survive in arid conditions.

Asphodelus dunensis is adapted to withstand drought and salt spray, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
