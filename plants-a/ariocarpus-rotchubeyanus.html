Ariocarpus rotchubeyanus

Ariocarpus rotchubeyanus, commonly known as the "Horned Cactus," is a rare and unique cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular stems that often develop a horn-like appearance as it matures. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged structure. It is well adapted to extreme desert conditions, with thick, fleshy stems that store water efficiently.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is highly valued in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
