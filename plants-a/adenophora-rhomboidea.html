Adenophora rhomboidea

Adenophora rhomboidea, commonly known as the rhombic bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
