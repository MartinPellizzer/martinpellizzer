Androsace sublanata

Androsace sublanata, commonly known as the hairy rock jasmine, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, often hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
