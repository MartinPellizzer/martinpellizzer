Azalea pentaphylla

Azalea pentaphylla, commonly known as the five-leaf azalea, is a species of flowering plant in the Rhododendron genus.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern China and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of pink or red flowers in the spring, which are borne on short, hairy stems. Azalea pentaphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.
