Amelanchier alnifolia var. venulosa

Amelanchier alnifolia var. venulosa, commonly known as the Pacific serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to western North America.

It features oval, dark green leaves that turn yellow or red in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white to pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen to a bright red or yellow color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, often found in forest edges and mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and wildlife habitat benefits.
