Anemone sukaczewii

Anemone sukaczewii, commonly known as the Siberian anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a short, thick rhizome and produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with five to seven petal-like sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are broadly ovate with a hairy surface.

Anemone sukaczewii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate spring blooms.
