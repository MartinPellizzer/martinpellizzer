Alectra arachidis

Alectra arachidis, commonly known as the broadleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Alectra arachidis is valued for its adaptability and role in sustainable agriculture.
