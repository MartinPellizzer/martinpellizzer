Aregelia sarmentosa

Aregelia sarmentosa, commonly known as the creeping aregelia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features trailing stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread along the ground. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically opposite and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

Aregelia sarmentosa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
