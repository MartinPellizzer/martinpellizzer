Alpinia parviflora

Alpinia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic and have a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are small, pale yellow, and clustered in spikes, which bloom during the warmer months.

Alpinia parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
