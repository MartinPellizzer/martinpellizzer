Acacia armatoides

Acacia armatoides, commonly known as the rough wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and phyllodes, which are modified leaves that serve as photosynthetic organs. The plant has a woody stem with a thick, ridged bark that provides protection against fire and drought. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in open woodlands and rocky soils.

Acacia armatoides is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil in degraded landscapes.
