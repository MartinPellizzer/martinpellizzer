Agapetes neriifolia var. maxima

Agapetes neriifolia var. maxima, commonly known as the white-flowered agapete, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically elliptical, glossy, and have a leathery texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests at high elevations.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
