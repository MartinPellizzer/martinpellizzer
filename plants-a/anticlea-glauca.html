Anticlea glauca

Anticlea glauca, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are typically covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Anticlea glauca is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and disturbed areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects.
