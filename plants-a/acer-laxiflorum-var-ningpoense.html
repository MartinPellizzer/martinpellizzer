Acer laxiflorum var. ningpoense

Acer laxiflorum var. ningpoense, commonly known as the Ningpo maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant red in the autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring before the leaves emerge. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
