Actaea obtusiloba

Actaea obtusiloba, commonly known as the blunt-leaf bugbane, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, palmately compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of white, spicate flowers in late summer, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shady woodland habitats and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
