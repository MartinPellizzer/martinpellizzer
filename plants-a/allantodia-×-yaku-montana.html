Allantodia × yaku-montana

Allantodia × yaku-montana, commonly known as the hybrid alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, which bloom in early summer. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural alpine environment.
