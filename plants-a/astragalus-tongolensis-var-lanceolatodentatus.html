Astragalus tongolensis var. lanceolatodentatus

Astragalus tongolensis var. lanceolatodentatus, commonly known as the lanceolate-toothed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clustered inflorescences, typical of the genus Astragalus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The species is noted for its medicinal potential, with various parts of the plant used in traditional herbal remedies.
