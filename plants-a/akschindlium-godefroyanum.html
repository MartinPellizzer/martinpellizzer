Akschindlium godefroyanum

Akschindlium godefroyanum, commonly known as the Godefroy's ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale pink or white flowers with intricate patterns that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant has a slender, sympodial growth habit and produces small, clustered pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapting to the cool, moist conditions of its mountainous habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a critically endangered species.
