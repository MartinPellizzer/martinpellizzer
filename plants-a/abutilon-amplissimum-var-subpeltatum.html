Abutilon amplissimum var. subpeltatum

Abutilon amplissimum var. subpeltatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and thrives in warm, humid climates.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and greenhouses.
