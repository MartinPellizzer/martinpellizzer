Abolboda acaulis

Abolboda acaulis, commonly known as the naked-stemmed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, unbranched stem that is often leafless above the base. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
