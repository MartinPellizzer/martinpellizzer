Acanthus montanus

Acanthus montanus, commonly known as the mountain acanthus, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with serrated edges and its tall, spire-like inflorescences that bear small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice in horticulture, though it is often cultivated in controlled environments due to its specific habitat requirements.

Acanthus montanus is also noted for its historical use in classical art and architecture, where its foliage was frequently depicted in decorative motifs.
