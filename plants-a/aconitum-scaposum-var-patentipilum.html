Aconitum scaposum var. patentipilum

Aconitum scaposum var. patentipilum, commonly known as the Showy Monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, doubly serrated leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aconitum scaposum var. patentipilum is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and is also noted for its toxic properties.
