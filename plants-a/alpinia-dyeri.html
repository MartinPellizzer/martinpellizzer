Alpinia dyeri

Alpinia dyeri, commonly known as the Chinese ginger or mountain ginger, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia, including China, India, and Indonesia. The plant features tall, slender stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow.

Alpinia dyeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
