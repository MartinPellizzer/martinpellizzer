Arrowsmithia Abyssinica

Abyssinian arrowhead, scientifically known as Arrowsmithia abyssinica, is a rare and unique aquatic plant native to the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia. This plant is characterized by its striking arrowhead-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, giving it its common name. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in mountain lakes and ponds. Due to its limited distribution and fragile ecosystem, it is considered a species of conservation concern. Its beauty and ecological significance make it a fascinating subject for both botanists and plant enthusiasts.
