Artanthe phthinotricha

Artanthe phthinotricha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved artanthe, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Artanthe phthinotricha is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
