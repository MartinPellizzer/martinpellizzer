Achyronia nudiflora

Achyronia nudiflora, commonly known as the naked-flowered achyronia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered near the base of the plant. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
