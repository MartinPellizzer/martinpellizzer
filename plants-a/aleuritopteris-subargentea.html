Aleuritopteris subargentea

Aleuritopteris subargentea, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a shimmering appearance. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as tropical rainforests. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce dense clusters of foliage.

Aleuritopteris subargentea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
