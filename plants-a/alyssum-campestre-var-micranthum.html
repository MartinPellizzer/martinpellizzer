Alyssum campestre var. micranthum

Alyssum campestre var. micranthum, commonly known as the small-flowered winter alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in winter and early spring. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
