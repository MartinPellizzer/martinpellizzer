Anemia proxima

Anemia proxima, commonly known as the near-leaf plant, is a species characterized by its unique foliage.

It features narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem. The plant exhibits a moderate growth rate and thrives in well-drained soil with indirect sunlight. Its botanical name suggests a close resemblance to other leafy plants in its genus.

Anemia proxima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and air-purifying properties.
