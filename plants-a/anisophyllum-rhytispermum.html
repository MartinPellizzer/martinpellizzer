Anisophyllum rhytispermum

Anisophyllum rhytispermum, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its low, spreading growth habit. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a hairy surface, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. Its reproductive strategy involves wind pollination, and it is adapted to survive in harsh, drought-prone environments.

Anisophyllum rhytispermum is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in local herbal remedies.
