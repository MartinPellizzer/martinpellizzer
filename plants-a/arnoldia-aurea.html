Arnoldia aurea

Arnoldia aurea, commonly known as the golden arnoldia, is a strikingly beautiful flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals that contrast beautifully with its deep green foliage. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers, which bloom in clusters, are highly fragrant and attract a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, Arnoldia aurea is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
