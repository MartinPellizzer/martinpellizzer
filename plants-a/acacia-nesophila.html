Acacia nesophila

Acacia nesophila, commonly known as the white acacia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, particularly in Namibia and Angola. The plant is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, thread-like leaflets.

Acacia nesophila is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its role in stabilizing sandy soils.
