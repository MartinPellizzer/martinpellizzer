Arundinella anomala var. glauca

Arundinella anomala var. glauca, commonly known as blue bamboo, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, blue-green stems that resemble bamboo. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly waxy texture.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique coloration and graceful appearance.
