Aristotelia racemosa

Aristotelia racemosa, commonly known as the kōwhai kākā, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in spring. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing seeds enclosed in a gelatinous pulp.

Aristotelia racemosa is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.
