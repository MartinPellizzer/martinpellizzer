Acalypha laxiflora

Acalypha laxiflora, commonly known as the silky mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, silky hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Acalypha laxiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
