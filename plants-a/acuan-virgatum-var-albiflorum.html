Acuan virgatum var. albiflorum

Acuan virgatum var. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered acuan, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, upright stems and delicate, lance-shaped leaves.

It produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant, airy look.
