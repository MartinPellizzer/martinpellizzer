Alternanthera triandra var. nodiflora

Alternanthera triandra var. nodiflora, commonly known as the velvet plant, is a striking aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that ranges in color from deep red to purple, depending on light conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, which appear in clusters near the water surface. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires moderate to high light for optimal coloration.

This variety is popular in aquariums and water gardens for its ornamental value and ease of maintenance.
