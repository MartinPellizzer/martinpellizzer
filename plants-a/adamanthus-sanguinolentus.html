Adamanthus sanguinolentus

Adamanthus sanguinolentus, commonly known as the Crimson Blood Tree, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers, which bloom in dense clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has a thick, woody stem with a distinctive reddish-brown bark that exudes a faintly blood-like hue when cut. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, with a glossy surface that reflects sunlight.

Adamanthus sanguinolentus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
