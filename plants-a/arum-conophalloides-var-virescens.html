Arum conophalloides var. virescens

Arum conophalloides var. virescens, commonly known as the green-spored arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces a distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a showy spathe. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female parts separated, a characteristic common in the Araceae family.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its bold foliage and unique floral structure.
