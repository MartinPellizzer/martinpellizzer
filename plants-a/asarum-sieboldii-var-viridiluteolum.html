Asarum sieboldii var. viridiluteolum

Asarum sieboldii var. viridiluteolum, commonly known as the green-veined wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with prominent veins that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers in early spring, which are clustered in umbels. It thrives in shaded, moist woodland environments and is valued for its aromatic rhizomes.

The variety is noted for its unique leaf coloration and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
