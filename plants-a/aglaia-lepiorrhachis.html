Aglaia lepiorrhachis

Aglaia lepiorrhachis, commonly known as the yellow mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits with a thick, segmented rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp. Its botanical features make it valuable for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.

The plant is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in humid climates.
