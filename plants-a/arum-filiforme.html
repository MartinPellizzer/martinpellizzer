Arum filiforme

Arum filiforme, commonly known as the slender arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves that are often described as filament-like, giving it its scientific name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color.

Arum filiforme is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
