Afzelia glandulosa

Afzelia glandulosa, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood that is highly valued in the furniture and musical instrument industries. The plant has compound leaves with small, glandular structures that give it its scientific name. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by legume-like pods containing seeds.

Afzelia glandulosa is also noted for its ecological importance in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
