Alstonia costata

Alstonia costata, commonly known as the fragrant alstonia, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. Alstonia costata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
