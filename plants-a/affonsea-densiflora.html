Affonsea densiflora

Affonsea densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered affonsea, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
