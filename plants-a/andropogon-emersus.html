Andropogon emersus

Andropogon emersus, commonly known as tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies, woodlands, and disturbed areas. Its flowering inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Andropogon emersus plays an important role in ecological systems by providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in grassland habitats.
