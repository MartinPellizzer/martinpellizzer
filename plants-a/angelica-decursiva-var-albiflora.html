Angelica decursiva var. albiflora

Angelica decursiva var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered angelica, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant features large, compound leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in dense, terminal clusters called umbels.

This variety is valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
