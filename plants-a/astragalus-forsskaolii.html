Astragalus forsskaolii

Astragalus forsskaolii, commonly known as the Somali milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Horn of Africa, particularly Somalia and Ethiopia. The plant is characterized by its upright stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, yellow or pale purple pea-like flowers. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Astragalus forsskaolii plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest for its potential medicinal properties.
