Arundinella bengalensis

Arundinella bengalensis, commonly known as the Bengal bamboo, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant features narrow, linear leaves arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. It is native to the Indian subcontinent and thrives in moist, tropical environments.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its fast growth and structural strength.
