Alloplectus ichthyoderma

Alloplectus ichthyoderma, commonly known as the fish-scale plant, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, scale-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Alloplectus ichthyoderma is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental foliage.
