Alyssum obtusifolium var. cordatocarpum

Alyssum obtusifolium var. cordatocarpum, commonly known as the heart-shaped fruit alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, and the variety name refers to the heart-shaped fruits that develop after flowering. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
