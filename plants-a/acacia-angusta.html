Acacia angusta

Acacia angusta, commonly known as the narrowleaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through its deep root system. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for various pollinators.

Its ecological significance makes it an important species in native landscaping and restoration projects.
