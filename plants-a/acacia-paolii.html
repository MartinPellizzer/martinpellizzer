Acacia paolii

Acacia paolii, commonly known as the Paul's acacia, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikes, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods. Acacia paolii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
