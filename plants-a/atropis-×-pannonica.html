Atropis × pannonica

Atropis × pannonica, commonly known as the hybrid bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Atropis × pannonica is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
