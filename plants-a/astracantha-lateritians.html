Astracantha lateritians

Astracantha lateritians, commonly known as the rock thistle, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in drought conditions. The plant produces spiny, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual element to its habitat. Its leaves are typically covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Astracantha lateritians is often found growing on rocky outcrops and in dry soils, making it a resilient and hardy species.
