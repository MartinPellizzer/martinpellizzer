Astragalus kuhikakaschanicus

Astragalus kuhikakaschanicus, commonly known as Kuhikakaschanic milkvetch, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central Asia, particularly in the Tian Shan and Pamir Mountains. This perennial herb is characterized by its slender stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.
