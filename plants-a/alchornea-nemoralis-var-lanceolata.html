Alchornea nemoralis var. lanceolata

Alchornea nemoralis var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf alchornea, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in open woodlands and scrublands, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features lanceolate leaves that are often pubescent, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in greenish-white or pale yellow, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
