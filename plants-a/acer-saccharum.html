Acer saccharum

Acer saccharum, commonly known as sugar maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant autumn foliage, which displays shades of red, orange, and yellow. The tree produces samaras, which are winged seeds that aid in wind dispersal. Its sap, tapped in early spring, is used to make maple syrup.

The leaves are opposite, palmately lobed, and typically have five pointed lobes.
