Anisostachya delphinensis

Anisostachya delphinensis, commonly known as the Delphin's reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers. The plant is characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to various soil types. It thrives in full sun and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Its botanical structure makes it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
