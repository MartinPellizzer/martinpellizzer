Acrostichum deorsum

Acrostichum deorsum, commonly known as the upland fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. The plant has large, pinnately compound leaves that are dark green and glossy on top. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Acrostichum deorsum plays an important role in maintaining ecological balance by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
