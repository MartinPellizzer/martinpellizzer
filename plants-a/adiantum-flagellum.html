Adiantum flagellum

Adiantum flagellum, commonly known as the mottled maidenhair fern, is a delicate, evergreen fern native to the southeastern United States.

It features finely divided, bi-lobed fronds that are typically a mix of green and silver hues, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with each leaflet being narrow and tapering.

Adiantum flagellum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
