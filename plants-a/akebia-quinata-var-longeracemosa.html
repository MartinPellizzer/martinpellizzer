Akebia quinata var. longeracemosa

Akebia quinata var. longeracemosa, commonly known as the long-flowered Chinese vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purplish underside. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a slender, hairy pod that splits open to release small, red seeds.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering display.
