Arundinaria communis

Arundinaria communis, commonly known as the bamboo grass, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically green and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. Arundinaria communis is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a ornamental plant in gardens.
