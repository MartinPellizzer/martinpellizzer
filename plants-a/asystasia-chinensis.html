Asystasia chinensis

Asystasia chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese nettle or broadleaf asystasia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Asystasia chinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally used in traditional medicine.
