Acerates gomphocarpoides

Acerates gomphocarpoides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed gomphocarp, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to certain regions of southern Africa and thrives in dry, sandy soils.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
