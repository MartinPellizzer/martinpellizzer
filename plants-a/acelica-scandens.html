Acelica scandens

Acelica scandens, commonly known as the climbing cypress, is a shrub-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems that allow it to climb or trail along surfaces. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. Its flowers are inconspicuous, and it is often grown for its ornamental foliage rather than its blooms.

Acelica scandens thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.
