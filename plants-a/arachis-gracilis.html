Arachis gracilis

Arachis gracilis, commonly known as the slender peanut, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its trailing stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces underground pods containing edible seeds, similar to other peanut species. It thrives in warm, moist climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Arachis gracilis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
