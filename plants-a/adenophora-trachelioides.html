Adenophora trachelioides

Adenophora trachelioides, commonly known as the blue flax or blue bellflower, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Adenophora trachelioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
