Anemone albana f. coerulea

Anemone albana f. coerulea, commonly known as the blue anemone, is a variant of the white anemone species.

It is characterized by its striking blue flowers, which contrast with its white-pinkish foliage. This plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in cool, moist environments. It typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of delicate, petal-like bracts.

The botanical features of this variety make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine collections.
