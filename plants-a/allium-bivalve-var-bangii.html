Allium bivalve var. bangii

Allium bivalve var. bangii, commonly known as the Bangi garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces bulbs that are often used in cooking for their mild garlic-like flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued both for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
