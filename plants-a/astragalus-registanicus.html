Astragalus registanicus

Astragalus registanicus, commonly known as the Registan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, particularly found in arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitat.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value or ecological benefits.
