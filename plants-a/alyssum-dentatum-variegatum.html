Alyssum dentatum variegatum

Alyssum dentatum variegatum, commonly known as the variegated stormy allyssum, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. The plant has silvery-gray, ovate leaves with a distinctive toothed margin, adding visual interest to gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value comes from its compact growth habit and attractive foliage.
