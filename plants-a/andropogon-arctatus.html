Andropogon arctatus

Andropogon arctatus, commonly known as stiff grass or prairie grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Andropogon arctatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
