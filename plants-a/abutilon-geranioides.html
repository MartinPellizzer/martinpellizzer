Abutilon geranioides

Abutilon geranioides, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Abutilon geranioides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
