Agrostophyllum stipulatum subsp. bicuspidatum

Agrostophyllum stipulatum subsp.

bicuspidatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed feather grass, is a grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental appeal and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
