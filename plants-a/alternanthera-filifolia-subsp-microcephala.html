Alternanthera filifolia subsp. microcephala

Alternanthera filifolia subsp.

microcephala, commonly known as dwarf threadleaf Alternanthera, is a compact, low-growing herbaceous plant native to South America. It features small, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is popular in aquatic and bog gardens due to its ornamental foliage and adaptability to wet environments.
