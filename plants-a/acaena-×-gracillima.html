Acaena × gracillima

Acaena × gracillima, commonly known as the slender bullion bush, is a hybrid shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces spherical, woolly fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and hardy nature.
