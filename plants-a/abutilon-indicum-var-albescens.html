Abutilon indicum var. albescens

Abutilon indicum var. albescens, commonly known as the White Indian mallow, is a deciduous shrub native to India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated as an ornamental for its attractive foliage and floral display. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens in warm climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both landscape design and botanical study.
