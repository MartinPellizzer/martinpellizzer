Asperula arcadiensis

Asperula arcadiensis, commonly known as the Florida beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, violet-blue berries that appear in the fall, providing a striking visual display. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval or elliptical in shape. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in moist, wooded areas.

Asperula arcadiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting birds and other wildlife.
