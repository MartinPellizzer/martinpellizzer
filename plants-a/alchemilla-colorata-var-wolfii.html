Alchemilla colorata var. wolfii

Alchemilla colorata var. wolfii, commonly known as the silver leaf alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its striking silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
