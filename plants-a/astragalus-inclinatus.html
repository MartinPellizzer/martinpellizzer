Astragalus inclinatus

Astragalus inclinatus, commonly known as the leaning milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its roots can form nodules with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, contributing to soil fertility.

Astragalus inclinatus is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
