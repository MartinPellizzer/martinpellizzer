Acrostichum danaeifolium f. lobatum

Acrostichum danaeifolium f. lobatum, commonly known as the lobed lace fern, is a variant of the lace fern species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed fronds that give it a unique, feathery appearance. The plant features long, arching fronds with a pale green to yellowish hue, often adorned with small, clustered sori on the underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
