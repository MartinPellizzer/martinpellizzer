Andira fraxinifolia var. latifoliolata

Andira fraxinifolia var. latifoliolata, commonly known as the broad-leaved andira, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically broad and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody capsules containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
