Aristotelia macqui

Aristotelia macqui, commonly known as the Macquira, is a woody shrub or small tree native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. It is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
