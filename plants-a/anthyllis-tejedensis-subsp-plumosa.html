Anthyllis tejedensis subsp. plumosa

Anthyllis tejedensis subsp.

plumosa, commonly known as the plumose anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to southern Spain. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
