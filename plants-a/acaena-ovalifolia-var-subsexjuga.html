Acaena ovalifolia var. subsexjuga

Acaena ovalifolia var. subsexjuga, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burr plant, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with small, oval-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces spherical, prickly burrs that contain seeds, a characteristic feature of the genus Acaena. Its flowers are small and yellow, typically appearing in clusters, and it thrives in dry, open habitats.

This variety is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to other Acaena species.
