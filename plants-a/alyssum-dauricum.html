Alyssum dauricum

Alyssum dauricum, commonly known as Daurian alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves and a compact, mat-forming growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum dauricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
