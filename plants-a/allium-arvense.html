Allium arvense

Allium arvense, commonly known as field garlic, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces bulb-like structures called bulbs, which are used for propagation and are edible when young. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found growing in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Allium arvense is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
