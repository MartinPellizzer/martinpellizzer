Acalypha lanceolata var. glandulosa

Acalypha lanceolata var. glandulosa, commonly known as the sticky dock, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, sticky hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its unique botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
