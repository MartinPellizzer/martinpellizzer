Asplenium stipitatum

Asplenium stipitatum, commonly known as the rock fern, is a small, evergreen fern native to regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It typically grows in rocky or shaded environments, often on cliffs or in crevices. The plant has a compact rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. Its fronds are usually dark green and may have a bluish tint, with a distinctive, narrow shape.

Asplenium stipitatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a houseplant.
