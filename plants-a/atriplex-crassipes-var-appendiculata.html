Atriplex crassipes var. appendiculata

Atriplex crassipes var. appendiculata, commonly known as saltbush or shadscale, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves and small, green or yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is highly adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a common component of desert ecosystems. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its resilience to drought and high salinity makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
