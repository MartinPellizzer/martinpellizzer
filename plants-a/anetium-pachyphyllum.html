Anetium pachyphyllum

Anetium pachyphyllum, commonly known as the thick-leaved anetium, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, adding a subtle aesthetic to its appearance. Its growth habit is usually low and spreading, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Anetium pachyphyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in dry climates.
