Asplenium × hungaricum

Asplenium × hungaricum, commonly known as the Hungarian spleenwort, is a hybrid fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate fronds that typically measure between 10 to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces spores in clusters called sori, which are usually located on the underside of the fronds. Asplenium × hungaricum thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky crevices or along stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid species, showcasing the genetic diversity within the Asplenium genus.
