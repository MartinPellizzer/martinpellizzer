Andropogon thorelii

Andropogon thorelii, commonly known as tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. This plant is well adapted to prairie and open woodland habitats, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils. It plays an important role in ecological systems by providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil.

Andropogon thorelii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in restoration projects.
