Agropyron cristatum var. submuticum

Agropyron cristatum var. submuticum, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and distinctive silky, awned florets that give it a unique appearance. The plant has narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically covered with short, soft hairs, contributing to its silvery-green coloration. It is well adapted to harsh environments, thriving in poor soils and withstanding drought conditions.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
