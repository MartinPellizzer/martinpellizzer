Abelia biflora var. insularis

Abelia biflora var. insularis, commonly known as the island abelia, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the southern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It prefers partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens in USDA hardiness zones 6-9.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and showy blooms, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
