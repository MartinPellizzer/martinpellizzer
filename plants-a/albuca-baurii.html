Albuca baurii

Albuca baurii, commonly known as the Zulu lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features strap-like, linear leaves that are often blue-green in color, and it produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments and prefers well-drained soil. It is typically grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.

Albuca baurii is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
