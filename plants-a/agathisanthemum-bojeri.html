Agathisanthemum bojeri

Agathisanthemum bojeri, commonly known as the Bojer's agathisanthemum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a dense, spherical cluster. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely known outside its native region.
