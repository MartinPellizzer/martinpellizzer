Albuca exigua

Albuca exigua, commonly known as the slender albuca, is a small, bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil to thrive. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and unique floral display.

Albuca exigua is typically grown in containers or as part of a rock garden due to its compact size and drought tolerance.
