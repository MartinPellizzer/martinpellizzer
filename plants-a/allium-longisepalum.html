Allium longisepalum

Allium longisepalum, commonly known as the long-sepaled garlic.

It is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features a bulb composed of several fleshy, white or pale yellow cloves. Its leaves are grass-like, and it produces clusters of small, purple or pinkish flowers.

Allium longisepalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
