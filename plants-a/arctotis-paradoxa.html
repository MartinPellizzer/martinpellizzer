Arctotis paradoxa

Arctotis paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil. It prefers full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The leaves are hairy and gray-green, adding texture to its appearance.
