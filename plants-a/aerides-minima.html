Aerides minima

Aerides minima, commonly known as the miniature orchid or miniature phalaenopsis, is a small epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are typically aerial, anchoring it to trees or other surfaces in its natural habitat.

Aerides minima is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
