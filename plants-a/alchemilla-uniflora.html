Alchemilla uniflora

Alchemilla uniflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers on slender stems, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla uniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
