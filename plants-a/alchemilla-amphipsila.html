Alchemilla amphipsila

Alchemilla amphipsila, commonly known as the narrowleaf lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are covered in a fine layer of silky hairs, which give them a distinctive appearance.

Alchemilla amphipsila is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodland edges and stream banks.
