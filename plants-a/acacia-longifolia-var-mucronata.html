Acacia longifolia var. mucronata

Acacia longifolia var. mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and feathery, yellow flowers that appear in spring. The leaves are narrow and linear, with a greyish-green color and a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
