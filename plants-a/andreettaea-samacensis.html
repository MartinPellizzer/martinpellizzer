Andreettaea samacensis

Andreettaea samacensis, commonly known as the Samacense Cactaceae, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cactaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its columnar stem and spiny, ribbed structure. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
