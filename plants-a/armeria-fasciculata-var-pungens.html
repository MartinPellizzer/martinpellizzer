Armeria fasciculata var. pungens

Armeria fasciculata var. pungens, commonly known as the spiked thrift, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are linear and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and low-maintenance plant for ornamental gardening.
