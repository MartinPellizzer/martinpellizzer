Asclepias galioides

Asclepias galioides, commonly known as showy milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers with distinctive, hood-like structures. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and slightly hairy.

Asclepias galioides is a valuable host plant for monarch butterfly larvae and plays an important role in supporting local pollinators.
