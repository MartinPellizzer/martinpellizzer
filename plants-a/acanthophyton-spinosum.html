Acanthophyton spinosum

Acanthophyton spinosum, commonly known as the spiny acanthophyton, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves, which are adapted to store water and reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the rainy season, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. Its spiny structure serves as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

Acanthophyton spinosum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
