Aploleia diffusa

Aploleia diffusa, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aploleia diffusa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
