Astragalus fedorovii

Astragalus fedorovii, commonly known as Fedorov's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found at high altitudes.

Astragalus fedorovii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
