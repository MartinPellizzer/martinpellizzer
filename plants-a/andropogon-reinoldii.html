Andropogon reinoldii

Andropogon reinoldii, commonly known as inland needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features tall, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Andropogon reinoldii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
