Alchemilla argyrophylla subsp. euargyrophylla

Alchemilla argyrophylla subsp.

euargyrophylla, commonly known as silverleaf lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features rosettes of silver-white, fuzzy leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and low maintenance requirements.
