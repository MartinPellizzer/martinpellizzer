Astragalus heterodontus f. ramosus

Astragalus heterodontus f. ramosus, commonly known as the branched milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is notable for its branched stems and its ability to thrive in poor soils, making it an important species in ecological restoration.

It also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
