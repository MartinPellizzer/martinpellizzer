Aegiphila stricta

Aegiphila stricta, commonly known as the rigid aegiphila, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

Aegiphila stricta is often found in moist, woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
