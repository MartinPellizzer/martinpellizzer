Astragalus stragulus

Astragalus stragulus, commonly known as milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus stragulus is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its role in ecological restoration projects.
