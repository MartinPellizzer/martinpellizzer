Aiouea salicifolia

Aiouea salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features simple, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange with a prominent red throat. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources.

Aiouea salicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
