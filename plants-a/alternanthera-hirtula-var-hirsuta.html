Alternanthera hirtula var. hirsuta

Alternanthera hirtula var. hirsuta, commonly known as the velvet plant, is a tropical perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a soft, velvety texture. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape and display a range of colors, from deep red to purple, depending on light exposure. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is often grown as an ornamental in gardens and indoor spaces due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
