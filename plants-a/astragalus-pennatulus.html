Astragalus pennatulus

Astragalus pennatulus, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and compound leaves with small, oval leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Astragalus pennatulus is often found in arid environments and is adapted to tolerate drought conditions.
