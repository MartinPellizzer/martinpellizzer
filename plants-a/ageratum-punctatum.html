Ageratum punctatum

Ageratum punctatum, commonly known as the dotted ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its fluffy, conical flower heads that are typically pink, purple, or white. The plant features narrow, slightly hairy leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Ageratum punctatum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
