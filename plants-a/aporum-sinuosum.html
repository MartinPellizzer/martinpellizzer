Aporum sinuosum

Aporum sinuosum, commonly known as the sinuous aporus, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence called a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf known as a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often green with a bluish tinge. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture for optimal growth.

Aporum sinuosum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouses.
