Asperula cynanchica var. lactea

Asperula cynanchica var. lactea, commonly known as the white-flowered woodruff, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, fragrant flowers in early spring, which are borne on long, drooping pedicels. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and rocky slopes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
