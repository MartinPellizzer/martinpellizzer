Anguina thwaitesii

Anguina thwaitesii, commonly known as the thwaitesii nematode, is a microscopic, thread-like worm belonging to the family Anguinidae.

It is primarily a parasitic organism, feeding on the roots of certain plants, particularly grasses and sedges. The plant's botanical relevance lies in its role as a host for this nematode, which can impact the health and growth of the host plant. While not a plant itself, its presence in plant ecosystems is significant for understanding soil biology and plant-parasite interactions.

This nematode is often studied in agricultural contexts due to its potential effects on crop plants.
