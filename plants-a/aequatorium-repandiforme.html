Aequatorium repandiforme

Aequatorium repandiforme, commonly known as the broad-form velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous small, round seeds.

Aequatorium repandiforme is often found in disturbed habitats and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.
