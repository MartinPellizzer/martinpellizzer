Amomum sumatrense

Amomum sumatrense, commonly known as Java cardamom, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, finger-like rhizomes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elliptical, dark blue berries. Its leaves are elongated, lance-shaped, and have a fragrant, slightly spicy aroma.

The plant is cultivated for its rhizomes, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
