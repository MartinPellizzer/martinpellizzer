Astragalus luristanicus

Astragalus luristanicus, commonly known as the Luristan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is known for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Astragalus luristanicus plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
