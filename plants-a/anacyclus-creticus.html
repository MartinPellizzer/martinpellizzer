Anacyclus creticus

Anacyclus creticus, commonly known as the Cretan dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny conditions. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring, followed by fluffy seed heads that disperse easily.

Anacyclus creticus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
