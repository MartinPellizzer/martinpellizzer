Acacia verniciflua var. pendula

Acacia verniciflua var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its drooping, feathery foliage, which gives it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, oval leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is well adapted to drought and poor soil conditions, making it a hardy plant suitable for arid landscapes.
