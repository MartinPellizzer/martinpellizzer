Arabis magna

Arabis magna, commonly known as the large rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. It produces small white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are usually basal, lobed, and somewhat succulent.

Arabis magna is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and delicate appearance.
