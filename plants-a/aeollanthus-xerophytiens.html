Aeollanthus xerophytiens

Aeollanthus xerophytiens, commonly known as the desert lily, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces tall, spire-like inflorescences adorned with large, showy white or pale blue flowers. The plant is well adapted to drought conditions, featuring thick, fleshy leaves and a deep root system for water storage. Its flowers are typically nocturnal, opening at night to attract specific pollinators.

The plant's botanical features make it a unique and resilient member of its ecosystem.
