Anthurium carlablackiae

Anthurium carlablackiae, commonly known as the Black Beauty Anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to the understory of humid forests in Central and South America.

It features vibrant, heart-shaped leaves with a deep green color and a unique, glossy texture. The plant produces striking spadices, which are inflorescences that resemble bright red or orange spikes, often surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent humidity and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among collectors and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
