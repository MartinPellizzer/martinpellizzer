Aconitum tuoliense

Aconitum tuoliense, commonly known as the Tibetan aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Tibet and adjacent areas in China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply cut lobes, and it thrives in cool, moist, and well-drained soils.

Aconitum tuoliense contains potent alkaloids, making it toxic and requiring careful handling.
