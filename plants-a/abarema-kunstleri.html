Abarema kunstleri

Abarema kunstleri, commonly known as the Brazilian velvet bean, is a tropical leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant is often cultivated for its high-protein seeds, which are used in animal feed and human consumption. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
