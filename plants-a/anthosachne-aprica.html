Anthosachne aprica

Anthosachne aprica, commonly known as the goldenleaf grass, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear yellowish to brownish flowers. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
