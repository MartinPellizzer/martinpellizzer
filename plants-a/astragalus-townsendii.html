Astragalus townsendii

Astragalus townsendii, commonly known as Townsend's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and desert environments. It has a deep taproot system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus townsendii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
