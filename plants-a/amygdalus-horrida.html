Amygdalus horrida

Amygdalus horrida, commonly known as the bitter almond tree, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small deciduous tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by green, oval fruits that mature into dark brown nuts. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

The plant is characterized by its bitter-tasting seeds, which contain toxic compounds like hydrogen cyanide.
