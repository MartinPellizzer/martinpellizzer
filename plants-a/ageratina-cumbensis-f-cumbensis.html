Ageratina cumbensis f. cumbensis

Ageratina cumbensis f. cumbensis, commonly known as the Cumbrian blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cooler, temperate climates. Its blue to violet flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
