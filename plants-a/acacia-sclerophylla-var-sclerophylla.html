Acacia sclerophylla var. sclerophylla

Acacia sclerophylla var. sclerophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and yellow flowers that appear in globular clusters. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, featuring small, tough leaves to reduce water loss. It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.

The species is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
