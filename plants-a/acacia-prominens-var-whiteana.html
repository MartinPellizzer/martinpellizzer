Acacia prominens var. whiteana

Acacia prominens var. whiteana, commonly known as the White's Acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed structure and its distinctive, feathery foliage composed of small, needle-like phyllodes. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are arranged in dense, spherical clusters. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and resistance to fire.

The species is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in providing habitat and food for native fauna.
