Aquilegia coerulea var. alpina

Aquilegia coerulea var. alpina, commonly known as the Alpine columbine, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features showy, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple, with five sepals that are often colored differently from the petals. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves, and its stems are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or mountainous habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
