Achillea × leptophylloides

Achillea × leptophylloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yarrow, is a hybrid perennial plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and flat, clusters of small white or pale yellow flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has a long history of medicinal use.

Its name honors the mythological figure Achilles, who was said to have used its leaves to treat wounds.
