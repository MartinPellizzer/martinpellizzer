Aeonium lindleyi var. viscatum

Aeonium lindleyi var. viscatum, commonly known as the silver aeonium, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features rosettes of fleshy, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tall, branching flower stalks that bear yellow flowers, which appear in spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a valuable addition to both indoor and outdoor landscapes.
