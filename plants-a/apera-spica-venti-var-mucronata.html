Apera spica-venti var. mucronata

Apera spica-venti var. mucronata, commonly known as windgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that are often carried on long, slender stalks. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is well adapted to windy conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive bluish-green hue.

The variety mucronata is distinguished by its short, blunt tip on the spike, giving it a unique appearance compared to other varieties of Apera spica-venti.
