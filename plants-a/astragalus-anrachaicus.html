Astragalus anrachaicus

Astragalus anrachaicus, commonly known as the Armenian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to grow in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native habitat.
