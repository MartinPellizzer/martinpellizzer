Aleuritopteris squamosa

Aleuritopteris squamosa, commonly known as the Japanese cliff fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, scale-like fronds that are often divided into narrow segments. The plant typically grows on rocky outcrops or in shaded, moist environments. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Aleuritopteris squamosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
