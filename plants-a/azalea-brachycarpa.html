Azalea brachycarpa

Azalea brachycarpa, commonly known as the dwarf azalea, is a species of flowering plant in the Rhododendron genus.

It is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, particularly China and Korea. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white during the spring. Its leaves are typically narrow and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental value.

Azalea brachycarpa prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
