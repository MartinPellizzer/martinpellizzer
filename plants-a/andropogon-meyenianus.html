Andropogon meyenianus

Andropogon meyenianus, commonly known as inland needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.

Andropogon meyenianus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
