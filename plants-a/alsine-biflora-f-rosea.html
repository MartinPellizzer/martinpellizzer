Alsine biflora f. rosea

Alsine biflora f. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered alsine, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its small, pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in dense clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has alternate, ovate leaves with a soft, hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
