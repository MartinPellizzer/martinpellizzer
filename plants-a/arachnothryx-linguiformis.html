Arachnothryx linguiformis

Arachnothryx linguiformis, commonly known as the tongue-shaped arachnothryx, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, also known as the legume or pea family. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with leaflets arranged in a distinctive, tongue-like shape. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or pale green.

The species is notable for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
