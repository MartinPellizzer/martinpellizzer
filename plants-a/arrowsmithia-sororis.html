Arrowsmithia sororis

Arrowsmithia sororis, commonly known as the twin sister lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It features a dark green to blackish upper surface with a distinctive, almost velvety texture. The thallus is composed of a complex interwoven network of fungal and algal tissues. It is typically found on tree trunks and rocks in temperate forests.

This lichen plays a vital ecological role in nutrient cycling and serves as an indicator of environmental health.
