Arundo coleotricha var. barbigera

Arundo coleotricha var. barbigera, commonly known as the hairy reed, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, hairy foliage that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetland or riparian habitats, thriving in moist, sandy soils. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens or used in ecological restoration projects.
