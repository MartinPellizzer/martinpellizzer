Allium monspessulanum

Allium monspessulanum, commonly known as the purple garlic or wild garlic, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its strong garlic scent and is often found in rocky or dry soils. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is related to other allium species such as onions and leeks.

Allium monspessulanum is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses in traditional Mediterranean cooking.
