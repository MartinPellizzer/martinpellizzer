Acacia sclerosperma subsp. sclerosperma

Acacia sclerosperma subsp.

sclerosperma, commonly known as the hard-seeded acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and phyllodes that are typically straight or slightly curved.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are followed by hard, woody pods containing the seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The subspecies is notable for its resilience and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of firewood.
