Acacia nitida

Acacia nitida, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny branches and compound leaves that are feather-like in appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact spherical clusters, which are followed by slender, woody pods containing seeds. Acacia nitida thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry climates.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and fuel.
