Andrographis neesiana var. rotundifolia

Andrographis neesiana var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf andrographis, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to round leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves and stems are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
