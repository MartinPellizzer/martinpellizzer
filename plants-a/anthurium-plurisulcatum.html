Anthurium plurisulcatum

Anthurium plurisulcatum, commonly known as the multi-grooved anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces striking spadices, which are modified leaves that enclose the true flowers. Its foliage is often a deep green with a glossy texture, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Anthurium plurisulcatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant.
