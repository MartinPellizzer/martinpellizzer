Aquilegia vulgaris subsp. viscosa

Aquilegia vulgaris subsp.

viscosa, commonly known as the sticky celandine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its clustered, spurred flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has palmately compound leaves with a sticky surface, which is a distinguishing feature of this subspecies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
