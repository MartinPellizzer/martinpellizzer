Aspalathus recurvispina

Aspalathus recurvispina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bushveld clover, is a small, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features slender, upright stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers, which bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Aspalathus recurvispina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
