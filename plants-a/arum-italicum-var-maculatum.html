Arum italicum var. maculatum

Arum italicum var. maculatum, commonly known as the spotted arum, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white and green variegation pattern. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a showy, purple spathe. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its dramatic foliage.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
