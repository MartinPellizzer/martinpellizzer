Adenoropium mollissimum

Adenoropium mollissimum, commonly known as the silky adenoropium, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found growing in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and somewhat obscure species within its native habitat.
