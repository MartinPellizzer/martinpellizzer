Asclepias nuttalliana

Asclepias nuttalliana, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 2 feet tall, adorned with palmate leaves that are deeply lobed. The plant produces striking clusters of pink to reddish-orange flowers with a distinctive crown of hooked appendages. It is a critical host plant for monarch butterfly larvae and supports a variety of pollinators.

Asclepias nuttalliana is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
