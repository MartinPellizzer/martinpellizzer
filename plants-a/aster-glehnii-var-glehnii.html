Aster glehnii var. glehnii

Aster glehnii var. glehnii, commonly known as the Glehn's aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features showy, blue to purple ray florets surrounding yellow disk flowers. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
