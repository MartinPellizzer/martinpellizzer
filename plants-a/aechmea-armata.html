Aechmea armata

Aechmea armata, commonly known as the pineapple plant, is a bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The plant produces a spadix of small white flowers surrounded by colorful bracts. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light.

Its unique structure and ability to store water make it well adapted to its natural habitat.

Morphology

Aechmea armata has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, sword-like, and arranged in a tight spiral around the central stem.

The leaves are typically dark green with a waxy coating and can grow up to 60 cm in length. The stem is short and sturdy, with a smooth, pale green bark that grows upward from the base. The plant produces striking pinkish-white flowers in a spadix surrounded by bright red bracts, blooming during the summer months and pollinated by insects.

Its fruit is a capsule that releases seeds through wind dispersal, and its roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in rocky or dry soils.
