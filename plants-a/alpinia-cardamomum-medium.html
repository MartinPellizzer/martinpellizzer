Alpinia cardamomum-medium

Alpinia cardamomum-medium, commonly known as the lesser cardamom, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, dark brown berries that contain aromatic seeds used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial use.
