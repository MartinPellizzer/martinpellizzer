Alstroemeria psittacina var. errembaultii

Alstroemeria psittacina var. errembaultii, commonly known as the golden alstroemeria, is a striking flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forest edges, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The variety errembaultii is distinguished by its particularly intense and showy blooms.
