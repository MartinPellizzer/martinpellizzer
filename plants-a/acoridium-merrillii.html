Acoridium merrillii

Acoridium merrillii, commonly known as the Philippine arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Acoraceae family and is characterized by its sword-shaped leaves and distinctive, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant is typically found in moist, tropical environments, often near water bodies. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a starch source.

Acoridium merrillii plays a significant role in the local biodiversity and cultural practices of its native region.
