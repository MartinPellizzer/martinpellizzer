Astragalus tuus

Astragalus tuus, commonly known as the Russian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its deep root system, which allows it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. It produces pods that contain several small, edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Astragalus tuus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
