Achillea cancrini

Achillea cancrini, commonly known as the Cancer root, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its gray-green foliage and flat-topped clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has a long history of use in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
