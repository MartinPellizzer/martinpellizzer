Achneria capillaris

Achneria capillaris, commonly known as the silky willow, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like stems and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are narrow, elongated, and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is often found along stream banks and wetlands.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wet conditions.
