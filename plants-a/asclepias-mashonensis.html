Asclepias mashonensis

Asclepias mashonensis, commonly known as the Mashonaland milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Zimbabwe.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its milky sap, which is a common feature among milkweeds. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. Its leaves are typically opposite and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Asclepias mashonensis plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing food and habitat for various insects.
