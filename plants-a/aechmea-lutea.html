Aechmea lutea

Aechmea lutea, commonly known as the yellow bromeliad, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically broad and covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Aechmea lutea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant colors and unique appearance.

Morphology

Aechmea lutea has leaves that are broad, sword-shaped, and arranged in a rosette pattern, with a vibrant green color and a waxy surface.

The plant has a short, thick stem with a rough, fibrous texture and a reddish-brown color, growing upright from the base of the rosette. Its flowers are clustered in a spike, with bright yellow petals and a spadix, blooming during the summer months and pollinated by insects. The fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains small, black seeds, which are dispersed by birds and water.
