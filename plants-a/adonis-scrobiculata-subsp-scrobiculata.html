Adonis scrobiculata subsp. scrobiculata

Adonis scrobiculata subsp.

scrobiculata, commonly known as the Alpine globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments.

The plant features a basal rosette of silver-gray, woolly leaves and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late spring. Its flowers are tubular and arranged in a globular cluster, giving it its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
