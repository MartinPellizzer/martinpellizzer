Alyssum campestre var. collinum

Alyssum campestre var. collinum, commonly known as the mountain alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
