Anthonotha acuminata

Anthonotha acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically linear or lance-shaped. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Anthonotha acuminata is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native environment.
