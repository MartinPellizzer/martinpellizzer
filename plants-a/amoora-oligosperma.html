Amoora oligosperma

Amoora oligosperma, commonly known as the silky fig, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and small, clustered flowers. The fruit is a small, round fig-like structure that is typically green when mature. It produces a milky sap that can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.

This plant is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
