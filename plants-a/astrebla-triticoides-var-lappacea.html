Astrebla triticoides var. lappacea

Astrebla triticoides var. lappacea, commonly known as the lappaceastrebla, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and compact growth habit, typically reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its drought tolerance and nutritional value, it is an important forage species for livestock in arid regions.
