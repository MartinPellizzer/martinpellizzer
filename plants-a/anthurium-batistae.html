Anthurium batistae

Anthurium batistae, commonly known as the Batista's arrowhead, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy dark green leaves that are often adorned with subtle veining. Its flowers, while not showy, play a crucial role in attracting pollinators such as beetles and flies.

Anthurium batistae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
