Acanthocereus atropurpureus

Acanthocereus atropurpureus, commonly known as the Purple Night Blooming Cereus, is a flowering cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are often ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white, pink, or purple. These flowers emit a strong fragrance to attract pollinators like bats and moths.

It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
