Anthyllis vulneraria subsp. macedonica

Anthyllis vulneraria subsp.

macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region. It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage.

The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

It thrives in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
