Arnica angustifolia subsp. attenuata

Arnica angustifolia subsp.

attenuata, commonly known as narrowleaf arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and often reddish in color.

The plant produces yellow-orange flowers in the summer, which are clustered in dense, flat-topped heads. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic compounds.
