Aloe macrocarpa subsp. wollastonii

Aloe macrocarpa subsp.

wollastonii, commonly known as the giant aloe, is a striking succulent native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, fleshy leaves that are typically green with red or purple margins.

The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
