Aster novi-belgii var. thyrsiflorus

Aster novi-belgii var. thyrsiflorus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with purple petals surrounding a prominent, cone-shaped center. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are dark green in color.

The botanical name reflects its origin and the unique floral structure characteristic of the species.
