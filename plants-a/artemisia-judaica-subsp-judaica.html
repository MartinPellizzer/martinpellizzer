Artemisia judaica subsp. judaica

Artemisia judaica subsp.

judaica, commonly known as Jerusalem artemisia, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, silver-gray leaves with a strong, camphor-like scent.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its chemical composition includes essential oils, making it valuable in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.
