Anil cytisoides

Anil cytisoides, commonly known as the golden wisteria, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is often found growing in moist, well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical regions. It has a climbing habit and can reach lengths of several meters.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
