Agnirictus agninum

Agnirictus agninum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture that helps retain moisture.

Agnirictus agninum is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in traditional medicine.
