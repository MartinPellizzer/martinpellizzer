Alsophila microchlamys

Alsophila microchlamys, commonly known as the small-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and fronds that are often deeply divided. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 6 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to rocky or forested substrates.

The fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value in landscaping.
