Alchemilla gemmia f. glabrior

Alchemilla gemmia f. glabrior, commonly known as the smooth-leaved ladies' mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
