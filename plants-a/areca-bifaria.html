Areca bifaria

Areca bifaria, commonly known as the areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive, palm-like appearance. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, which are typically pollinated by wind. Areca bifaria is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habits make it well-suited for indoor and outdoor environments in warm climates.
