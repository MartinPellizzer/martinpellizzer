Avena pratensis f. hirsuta

Avena pratensis f. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy oat, is a variant of the common oat species characterized by its dense, hairy foliage.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in temperate climates, often found in meadows and grasslands. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, blade-like leaves covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, each bearing two to three small, two-lobed flowers.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
