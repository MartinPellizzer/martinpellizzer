Aclisia condensata

Aclisia condensata, commonly known as the clustered acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant has small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, contributing to its common name. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Aclisia condensata is often used in local horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
