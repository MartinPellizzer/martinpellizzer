Arum elongatum subsp. engleri

Arum elongatum subsp.

engleri, commonly known as the Engler's arrow arum, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia. It features a distinctive spathe that is typically green to reddish in color, with a spadix that bears small, clustered flowers.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are elongated and arrow-shaped, contributing to its common name.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
