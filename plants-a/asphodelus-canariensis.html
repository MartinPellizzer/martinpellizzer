Asphodelus canariensis

Asphodelus canariensis, commonly known as Canary asphodel, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the family Asphodelaceae and is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves and spikes of yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation.

The plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance and resilience.
