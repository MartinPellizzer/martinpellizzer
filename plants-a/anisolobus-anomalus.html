Anisolobus anomalus

Anisolobus anomalus, commonly known as the strangeleaf plant, is a unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unusual leaf morphology, with lobed leaves that deviate from typical plant structures. The plant exhibits a climbing habit, often using its tendrils to ascend trees or other supports. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-white in color, and it produces small, fleshy fruits.

Due to its rarity and distinct features, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
