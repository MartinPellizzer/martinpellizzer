Actinidia chinensis f. longipila

Actinidia chinensis f. longipila, commonly known as the longipedicellate kiwifruit, is a cultivar of the Actinidia genus native to China.

It is characterized by its long, slender pedicels, which distinguish it from other kiwifruit varieties. The plant is a deciduous vine that produces small, edible fruits with a fuzzy, green exterior. Its botanical features include compound leaves with serrated margins and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer.

This variety is valued for its unique fruit morphology and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
