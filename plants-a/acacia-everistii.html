Acacia everistii

Acacia everistii, commonly known as the everistii acacia, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its slender, branching structure. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, spherical clusters, which are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, feather-like leaflets, and it has a woody, fibrous trunk.

Acacia everistii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in regions with low rainfall.
