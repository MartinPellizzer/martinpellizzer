Ammannia icosandra

Ammannia icosandra, commonly known as the redroot ammannia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its reddish stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Ammannia icosandra is sometimes used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and ecological value for pollinators.
