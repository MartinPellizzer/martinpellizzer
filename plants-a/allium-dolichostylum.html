Allium dolichostylum

Allium dolichostylum, commonly known as the long-stemmed leek, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its hollow, cylindrical leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a valuable crop in some regions.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional agriculture.
