Aristida setifolia var. arenaria

Aristida setifolia var. arenaria, commonly known as sand dropseed, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, hair-like leaves and produces fluffy, silver-gray seed heads that are highly visible in the landscape. This plant thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and dunes. It is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and birds.
