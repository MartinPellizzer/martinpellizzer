Adenophora hunanensis subsp. huadungensis

Adenophora hunanensis subsp.

huadungensis, commonly known as the Huadong adenophora, is a perennial herb native to eastern China. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lanceolate and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and somewhat woody at maturity.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
