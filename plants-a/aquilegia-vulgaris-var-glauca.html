Aquilegia vulgaris var. glauca

Aquilegia vulgaris var. glauca, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features striking blue to purple flowers with five petals and numerous sepals, creating a distinctive spurred appearance. The plant has basal leaves that are trifoliate, with heart-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, often found in alpine and woodland habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
