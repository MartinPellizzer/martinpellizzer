Amesia somaliensis

Amesia somaliensis, commonly known as the Somali fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Somalia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a cluster, often with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and limited water availability.

Amesia somaliensis is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional remedies.
