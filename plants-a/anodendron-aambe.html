Anodendron aambe

Anodendron aambe, commonly known as the African snowbell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture, which helps it conserve water.

Anodendron aambe is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
