Abies nobilis var. compacta

Abies nobilis var. compacta, commonly known as the compact form of the white fir, is a cultivar of the white fir tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and shorter, more compact needles compared to the standard species. The plant features a conical shape with a thick layer of foliage, making it an attractive ornamental plant. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, and it is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
