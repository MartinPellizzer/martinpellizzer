Aristida chapmaniana

Aristida chapmaniana, commonly known as Chapman's threeawn, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences that produce small, pale green to brownish flowers. This plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils, showing a preference for well-drained environments. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, which can help prevent soil erosion.

The species is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid climates.
