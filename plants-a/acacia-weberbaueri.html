Acacia weberbaueri

Acacia weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and its distinctive, feathery compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Acacia weberbaueri is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
