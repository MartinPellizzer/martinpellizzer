Aleuritia erythra

Aleuritia erythra, commonly known as the red-flowered aleurita, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often appearing in clusters. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a glossy texture, while its bark is smooth and reddish-brown.

Aleuritia erythra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
