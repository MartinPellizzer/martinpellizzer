Andropogon mannii

Andropogon mannii, commonly known as African switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and savannas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Andropogon mannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
