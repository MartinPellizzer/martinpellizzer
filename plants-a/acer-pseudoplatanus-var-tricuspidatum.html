Acer pseudoplatanus var. tricuspidatum

Acer pseudoplatanus var. tricuspidatum, commonly known as the three-lobed maple, is a variant of the common maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are deeply lobed and resemble the shape of a three-leaf clover. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

Its foliage turns vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and parks.
