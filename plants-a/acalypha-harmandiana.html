Acalypha harmandiana

Acalypha harmandiana, commonly known as the red hot poker or fireplant, is a striking tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vivid red bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. Its foliage is often a deep green, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Acalypha harmandiana is popular in ornamental horticulture for its bold, colorful appearance.
