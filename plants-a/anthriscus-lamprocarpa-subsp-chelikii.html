Anthriscus lamprocarpa subsp. chelikii

Anthriscus lamprocarpa subsp.

chelikii, commonly known as the Caucasian cow parsley, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region. It features slender, hairy stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the carrot family (Apiaceae).
