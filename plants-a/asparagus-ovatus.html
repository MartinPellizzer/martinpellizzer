Asparagus ovatus

Asparagus ovatus, commonly known as the oval asparagus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, egg-shaped, fleshy roots that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, followed by edible, needle-like shoots that are harvested in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Asparagus ovatus is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its edible parts, making it a versatile plant in gardens and agriculture.
