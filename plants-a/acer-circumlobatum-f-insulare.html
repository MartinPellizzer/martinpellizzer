Acer circumlobatum f. insulare

Acer circumlobatum f. insulare, commonly known as the island maple, is a unique variant of the maple tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

This plant is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring palmately lobed leaves that are often smaller and more rounded compared to the typical species. The leaves are typically bright green in summer and turn vibrant red or orange in autumn, adding ornamental value to the landscape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or island environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
