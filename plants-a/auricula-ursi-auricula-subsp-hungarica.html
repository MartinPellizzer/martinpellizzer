Auricula-ursi auricula subsp. hungarica

Auricula-ursi auricula subsp.

hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian bear's ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It features small, kidney-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in early summer. It thrives in rocky, acidic soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
