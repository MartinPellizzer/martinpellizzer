Andropogon intumescens

Andropogon intumescens, commonly known as purple bluestem, is a tall grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its striking purple tinge on the inflorescence. The plant has narrow, blade-like leaves and produces spikelets that are often clustered in panicles. It thrives in full sun and is commonly found in prairies, open woodlands, and disturbed soils.

Andropogon intumescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
