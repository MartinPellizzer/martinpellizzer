Alstroemeria formosa

Alstroemeria formosa, commonly known as the Peruvian lily, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features strap-like, grassy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Alstroemeria formosa is also noted for its long-lasting blooms and adaptability to various climate conditions.
