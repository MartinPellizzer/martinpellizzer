Aconitum napellus lusus paradoxum

Aconitum napellus lusus paradoxum, commonly known as the paradoxical monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking blue to purple hooded flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

However, it is highly toxic due to the presence of potent alkaloids like aconitine, making it dangerous to handle or ingest.
