Achillea alpina

Achillea alpina, commonly known as alpine yarrow, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a compact, bushy growth habit with silver-gray, hairy leaves and flat, composite flower heads that range from white to pale pink. The plant is adapted to cold, alpine environments and thrives in well-drained soils. Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Achillea alpina is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat wounds and digestive ailments.
