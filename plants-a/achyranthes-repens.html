Achyranthes repens

Achyranthes repens, commonly known as spreading knotweed, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has a sprawling growth habit with numerous stolons that root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its root system is fibrous and can become invasive in certain environments.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
