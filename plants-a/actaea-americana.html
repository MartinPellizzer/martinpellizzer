Actaea americana

Actaea americana, commonly known as American bugbane, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in late summer, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in moist, shady environments.

Actaea americana is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
