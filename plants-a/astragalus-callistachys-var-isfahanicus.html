Astragalus callistachys var. isfahanicus

Astragalus callistachys var. isfahanicus, commonly known as the Isfahan milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Iranian plateau.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in racemes. The plant has compound leaves with pinnate arrangement, and its root system is extensive, adapted to arid environments. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is well-suited to dry climates.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
