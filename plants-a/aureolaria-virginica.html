Aureolaria virginica

Aureolaria virginica, commonly known as the Virginia sandwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along streambanks and woodland edges. The plant features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, linear, and often covered in fine hairs.

Aureolaria virginica is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in moist, well-drained habitats.
