Acamptopappus

Acamptopappus, commonly known as the hooked pappus plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their unique floral structures, including the presence of a hooked pappus, which aids in wind dispersal of their seeds. Acamptopappus species are typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in sandy or rocky soils. They produce small, daisy-like flowers that are often yellow or orange in color.

The genus includes several species, many of which are adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.
