Aconitum pendulicarpum var. circinatum

Aconitum pendulicarpum var. circinatum, commonly known as the curled fruit aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed leaflets that are often curled or twisted, giving the plant its distinctive appearance.

The variety circinatum is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
