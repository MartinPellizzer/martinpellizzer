Amauropelta semilunata

Amauropelta semilunata, commonly known as the semi-lunar shield fern, is a unique species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are often divided into lobes, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many ferns.

Amauropelta semilunata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
