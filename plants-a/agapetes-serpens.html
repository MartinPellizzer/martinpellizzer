Agapetes serpens

Agapetes serpens, commonly known as the snake's head or serpentine evergreen, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes, particularly in countries like Peru and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
