Andropogon arrhenobasis

Andropogon arrhenobasis, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically blue-green in color. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Andropogon arrhenobasis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
