Anacolosa cauliflora

Anacolosa cauliflora, commonly known as the climbing hibiscus, is a tropical plant characterized by its woody stems and climbing habit.

It produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or red, often with a yellow center. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, warm climates. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Anacolosa cauliflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
