Anacardium officinarum

Anacardium officinarum, commonly known as the cashew tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It produces a unique fruit consisting of an outer husk and an inner shell containing the edible cashew nut. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The tree's bark and leaves contain toxic compounds, including urushiol, which can cause skin irritation.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is widely cultivated for its economic value.
