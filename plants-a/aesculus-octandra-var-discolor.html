Aesculus octandra var. discolor

Aesculus octandra var. discolor, commonly known as the pink horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces showy, white or pale pink, clustered flowers in spring, which are followed by spiny, spherical fruits. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
