Adiantum incisum subsp. indicum

Adiantum incisum subsp.

indicum, commonly known as the Indian maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to regions of Asia, including India and parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are often arched or curved, giving it a graceful appearance.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or along stream banks, where it thrives in high humidity. Its fronds are bi-lobed and have a distinctive, feathery texture, with a pale underside that contrasts with the darker upper surface.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its elegant foliage.
