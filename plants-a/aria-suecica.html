Aria suecica

Aria suecica, commonly known as the Swedish bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland margins or wet meadows. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

Aria suecica plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
