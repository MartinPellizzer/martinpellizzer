Artemisia pachystachya

Artemisia pachystachya, commonly known as the woolly mugwort, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy foliage and slender, branched stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic leaves contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
