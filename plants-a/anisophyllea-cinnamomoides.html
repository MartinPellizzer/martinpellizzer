Anisophyllea cinnamomoides

Anisophyllea cinnamomoides, commonly known as the cinnamon leaf tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that emit a distinct cinnamon-like scent when crushed. The plant belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as laurel and cinnamon. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The plant's botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into oval, woody fruits.
