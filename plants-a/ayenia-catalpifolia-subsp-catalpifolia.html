Ayenia catalpifolia subsp. catalpifolia

Ayenia catalpifolia subsp.

catalpifolia, commonly known as the Catalpa leaf ayenia, is a species native to the southeastern United States. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and typically appear in late spring to early summer.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun conditions.
