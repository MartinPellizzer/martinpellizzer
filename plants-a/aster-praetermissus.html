Aster praetermissus

Aster praetermissus, commonly known as the slender aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Aster praetermissus is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
