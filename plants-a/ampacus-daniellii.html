Ampacus daniellii

Ampacus daniellii, commonly known as the Danville's flax, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid tropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
