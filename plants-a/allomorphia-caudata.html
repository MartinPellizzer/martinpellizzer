Allomorphia caudata

Allomorphia caudata, commonly known as the spurred orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, spurred petals that resemble a tail, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its flowers are usually brightly colored, ranging from yellow to orange, and are pollinated by specific insects such as bees and wasps.

Allomorphia caudata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
