Astragalus americanus f. glabrescens

Astragalus americanus f. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth milkvetch, is a variant of the American milkvetch.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and eastern United States. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound and usually have a smooth texture, distinguishing it from other forms of Astragalus.

The flowers are typically pink to purple, and the plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.
