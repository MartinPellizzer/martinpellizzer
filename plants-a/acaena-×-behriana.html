Acaena × behriana

Acaena × behriana, commonly known as the New Zealand spinach or silverweed, is a hybrid plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional Māori cuisine. Its leaves are edible and have a slightly tangy flavor, making it a valuable forage plant.

Acaena × behriana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.
