Astragalus stevenianus var. kochianus

Astragalus stevenianus var. kochianus, commonly known as the Steven's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow to reddish-pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh, arid environments.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native range.
