Aconitum nemorum

Aconitum nemorum, commonly known as the European monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple hooded flowers, which are arranged in clusters on tall, leafy stems. The plant has palmately compound leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. Aconitum nemorum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is also known for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that make it both dangerous and historically significant.
