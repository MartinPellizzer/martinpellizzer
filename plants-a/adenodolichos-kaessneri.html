Adenodolichos kaessneri

Adenodolichos kaessneri, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and foliage, which contribute to its common name. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities.

Adenodolichos kaessneri is often cultivated for its edible seeds and ornamental value.
