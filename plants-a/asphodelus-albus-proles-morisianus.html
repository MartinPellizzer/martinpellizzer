Asphodelus albus proles morisianus

Asphodelus albus proles morisianus, commonly known as the white asphodel of Malta, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, including parts of southern Europe and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asphodelaceae and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and tall, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Asphodelus albus proles morisianus is also noted for its historical and cultural significance in the region.
