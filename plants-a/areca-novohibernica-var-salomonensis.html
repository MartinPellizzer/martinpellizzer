Areca novohibernica var. salomonensis

Areca novohibernica var. salomonensis, commonly known as the Solomon Islands areca palm, is a tropical plant native to the Solomon Islands.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is well adapted to humid, warm climates. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and for thatching.

The fruit, which is edible, contains a starchy kernel used in local cuisines.
