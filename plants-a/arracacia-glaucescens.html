Arracacia glaucescens

Arracacia glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-veined arracacha, is a herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic, compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Its roots are used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable in some cuisines.
