Acacia fauntleroyi

Acacia fauntleroyi, commonly known as the desert acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its woody, thorny stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are often clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Acacia fauntleroyi plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
