Anthemis scariosa

Anthemis scariosa, commonly known as the scarious fleabane, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are usually covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

The flowers, which bloom in the summer, are yellow and attract various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
