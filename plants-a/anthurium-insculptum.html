Anthurium insculptum

Anthurium insculptum, commonly known as the sculptured anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and a deep, velvety green color. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-shaped flowers surrounded by a colorful spathe, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in humid, warm environments with indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium insculptum is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
