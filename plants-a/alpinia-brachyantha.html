Alpinia brachyantha

Alpinia brachyantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and are clustered in inflorescences, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Alpinia brachyantha is valued both for its aesthetic appeal and its use in traditional medicine.
