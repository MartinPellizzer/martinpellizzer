Artemisia calophylla

Artemisia calophylla, commonly known as silverleaf sage, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Artemisia calophylla is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
