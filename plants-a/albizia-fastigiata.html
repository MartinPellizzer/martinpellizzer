Albizia fastigiata

Albizia fastigiata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf silk tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pinnately compound leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Albizia fastigiata is relatively low-maintenance and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun to partial shade.
