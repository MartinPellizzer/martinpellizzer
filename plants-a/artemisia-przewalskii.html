Artemisia przewalskii

Artemisia przewalskii, commonly known as the Chinese wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the high altitudes of central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish flowers that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils. Its leaves contain essential oils and have been used traditionally in herbal medicine.

Artemisia przewalskii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh climates.
