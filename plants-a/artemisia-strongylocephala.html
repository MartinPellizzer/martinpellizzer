Artemisia strongylocephala

Artemisia strongylocephala, commonly known as blue mist shrub, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is often grown as an ornamental due to its drought tolerance and attractive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its essential oils contain compounds with potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed.
