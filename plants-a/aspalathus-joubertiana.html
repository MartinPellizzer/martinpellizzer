Aspalathus joubertiana

Aspalathus joubertiana, commonly known as the rooibos plant, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is well adapted to the region's Mediterranean climate, thriving in sandy, well-drained soils. It is most famous for its leaves, which are used to make the popular herbal tea known as rooibos.

Due to its unique properties and ecological significance, it plays an important role in the local flora and biodiversity.
