Allosorus marginatus var. brevilobus

Allosorus marginatus var. brevilobus, commonly known as the narrow-lobed allosorus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with distinctive, narrow lobes that give it its botanical name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, which are clustered in loose inflorescences.

The species is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich, well-drained soils.
