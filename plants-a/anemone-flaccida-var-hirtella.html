Anemone flaccida var. hirtella

Anemone flaccida var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features basal leaves that are hairy and oval in shape, with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and early-season blooms.
