Asperugalium occidentale

Asperugalium occidentale, commonly known as the Western thornbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, pale green leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Asperugalium occidentale plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
