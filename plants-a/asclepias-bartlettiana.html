Asclepias bartlettiana

Asclepias bartlettiana, commonly known as the Bartlett's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and pairs of opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by distinctive, horn-like seed pods. It is a member of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is known for its role in supporting monarch butterfly populations.

Asclepias bartlettiana is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and desert landscapes.
