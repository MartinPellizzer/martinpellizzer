Arthraxon comorensis

Arthraxon comorensis, commonly known as the Comoros grass, is a perennial herb native to the Comoros Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Arthraxon comorensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
