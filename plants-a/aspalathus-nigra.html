Aspalathus nigra

Aspalathus nigra, commonly known as the black tea plant, is a species native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is a small shrub characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, black, spherical fruits that contain seeds. It is closely related to the commercially important rooibos plant, Aspalathus linearis.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, Aspalathus nigra is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
