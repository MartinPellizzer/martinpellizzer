Aulomyrcia piauhiensis

Aulomyrcia piauhiensis, commonly known as the Piauhi palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red fruits.

Due to habitat loss, Aulomyrcia piauhiensis is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation areas.
