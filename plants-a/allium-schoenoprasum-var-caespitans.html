Allium schoenoprasum var. caespitans

Allium schoenoprasum var. caespitans, commonly known as woodland garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Botanically, it is closely related to other members of the Allium genus, sharing similar chemical compositions and growth patterns.
