Arundinaria purpurascens

Arundinaria purpurascens, commonly known as the purple bamboo, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can grow up to 10 feet in height, with a distinctive purple tinge on the nodes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, forming dense clumps in moist, well-drained soils.

Arundinaria purpurascens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional basket-making.
