Albertinia incana

Albertinia incana, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems covered in fine, silver-white hairs, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in grassland habitats. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spike-like clusters, and it produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

Albertinia incana is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
