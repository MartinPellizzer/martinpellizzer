Aspilia eylesii

Aspilia eylesii, commonly known as the Eyles' flame bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange inflorescences that resemble flames, which are borne on long, slender stalks. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Aspilia eylesii thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy and attractive ornamental plant.
