Azalea roylei

Azalea roylei, commonly known as the Kashmiri azalea, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of Pakistan and India.

It is a flowering shrub that belongs to the Rhododendron genus, characterized by its dense foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing clusters of pink to red blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Azalea roylei prefers well-drained, acidic soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value and hardiness make it a sought-after plant in horticultural collections.
