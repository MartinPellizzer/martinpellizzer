Anthericum ruwense

Anthericum ruwense, commonly known as the European arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, which is often brightly colored to attract pollinators. Native to Europe and parts of western Asia, it thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and wetlands.

Anthericum ruwense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
