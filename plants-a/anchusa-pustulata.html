Anchusa pustulata

Anchusa pustulata, commonly known as the spurred speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of small, blue or violet flowers with distinctive spurred petals. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular and emit a mild, spicy scent. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Anchusa pustulata is known for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
