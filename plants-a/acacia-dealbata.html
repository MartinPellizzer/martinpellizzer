Acacia dealbata

Acacia dealbata, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves, which are covered in a fine, silvery-white powder, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that appear in late autumn to early spring. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Acacia dealbata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
