Amaranthus albus var. parviflorus

Amaranthus albus var. parviflorus, commonly known as white amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It features erect stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, covered with fine hairs. The leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and have a bluish-green color with a slight pubescence. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense panicles, typically white or pale green in color.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional cuisines for its edible leaves and seeds.
