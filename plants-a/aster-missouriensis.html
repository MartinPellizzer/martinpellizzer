Aster missouriensis

Aster missouriensis, commonly known as Missouri aster, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows in prairies, open woods, and disturbed sites, preferring well-drained soils. It has narrow, linear leaves and can reach heights of up to 3 feet.

Missouri aster is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
