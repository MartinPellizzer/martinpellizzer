Achyranthes hispida

Achyranthes hispida, commonly known as the hairy spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems covered in fine, stiff hairs. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
