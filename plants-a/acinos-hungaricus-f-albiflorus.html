Acinos hungaricus f. albiflorus

Acinos hungaricus f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and the stems are upright with few branches.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
