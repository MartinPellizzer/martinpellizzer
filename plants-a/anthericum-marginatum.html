Anthericum marginatum

Anthericum marginatum, commonly known as the white adder's mouth, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a rosette of lance-shaped, silver-gray leaves with a distinctive white margin. The plant produces tall, slender flowering stems that bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
