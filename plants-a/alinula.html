Alinula

Alinula, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and unique plant belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture.

Alinula is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unusual appearance.
