Arabidopsis monachorum

Arabidopsis monachorum, commonly known as the monastery Arabidopsis, is a small flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its compact stature, with leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, and it is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its unique ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation to harsh environments.
