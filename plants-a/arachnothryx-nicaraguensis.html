Arachnothryx nicaraguensis

Arachnothryx nicaraguensis, commonly known as the Nicaraguan spider tree, is a tropical tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spiny thorns. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, lowland forests and is often found in secondary growth areas.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
