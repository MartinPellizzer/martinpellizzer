Amaranthus albiflorus

Amaranthus albiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, three-angled, smooth seeds that are often used as a grain. It thrives in warm climates and is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types.

Amaranthus albiflorus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional agriculture.
