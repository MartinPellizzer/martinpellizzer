Anemia ulei

Anemia ulei, commonly known as oilseed flax, is an annual plant belonging to the Linaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical seed pods. Its seeds are rich in essential fatty acids and are widely used in food and industrial applications.

Botanically, it is notable for its fibrous stalks and its ability to thrive in temperate climates.
