Allium pseudophanerantherum

Allium pseudophanerantherum, commonly known as the false onion, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in a dense umbel. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the bulbs are often used in traditional medicine.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
