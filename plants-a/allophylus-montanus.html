Allophylus montanus

Allophylus montanus, commonly known as the mountain alder, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical forests. The plant has simple, opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top, with a lighter underside. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, woody fruits containing a single seed.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
