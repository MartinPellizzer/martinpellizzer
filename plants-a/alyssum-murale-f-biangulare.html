Alyssum murale f. biangulare

Alyssum murale f. biangulare, commonly known as the two-lobed alyssum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture. This plant is native to southern Europe and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
