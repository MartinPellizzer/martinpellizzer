Asparagus gaudichaudianus

Asparagus gaudichaudianus, commonly known as the Cook Islands asparagus, is a flowering plant native to the South Pacific.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and sandy soils.

Asparagus gaudichaudianus is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its potential culinary uses.
