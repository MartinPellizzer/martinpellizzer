Allium incomptum

Allium incomptum, commonly known as the slender leek, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Tajikistan. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces umbels of white or pale purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and cooler climates, often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Allium incomptum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
