Astragalus samuelssonii

Astragalus samuelssonii, commonly known as Samuelson's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its seeds are enclosed in pods, a common feature among legumes, and it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
