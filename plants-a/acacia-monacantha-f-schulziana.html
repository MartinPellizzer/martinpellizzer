Acacia monacantha f. schulziana

Acacia monacantha f. schulziana, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, feathery inflorescences that bloom throughout the warmer months. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened, modified leaf stems that perform the function of leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and inland regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and public landscapes.
