Anisopappus petitianus

Anisopappus petitianus, commonly known as the desert aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.

Anisopappus petitianus plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is an indicator species for dry, disturbed environments.
