Achyranthes japonica var. katsuudakemontana

Achyranthes japonica var. katsuudakemontana, commonly known as Japanese knotweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features rhizomatous growth, allowing it to spread aggressively and form dense stands. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often reddish on the underside, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its stems are typically reddish and become woody with age.

It is known for its rapid growth and can be invasive in non-native habitats.
