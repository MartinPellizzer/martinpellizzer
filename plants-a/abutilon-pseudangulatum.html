Abutilon pseudangulatum

Abutilon pseudangulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has alternate, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five petals and a distinctive, broad, funnel-shaped corolla.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
