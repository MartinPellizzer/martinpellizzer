Alchemilla hybrida f. guarensis

Alchemilla hybrida f. guarensis, commonly known as the Guarensis lady's mantle, is a variant of the lady's mantle genus characterized by its ornamental foliage.

It is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, particularly in Spain and Portugal. The plant features broad, velvety leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the spring and early summer.

This cultivar is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained, moist soils in partial shade.
