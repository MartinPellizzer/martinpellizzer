Ageratina bellidifolia

Ageratina bellidifolia, commonly known as the pinkhead ageratum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its showy inflorescences consist of white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Ageratina bellidifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant floral display.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
