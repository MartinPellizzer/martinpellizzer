Amomum costatum

Amomum costatum, commonly known as the fragrant ginger or Chinese ginger, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features tall, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, typically ranging in color from white to pale yellow.

The rhizomes of Amomum costatum are valued for their aromatic properties and are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
