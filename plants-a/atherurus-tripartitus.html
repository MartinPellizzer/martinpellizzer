Atherurus tripartitus

Atherurus tripartitus, commonly known as the three-parted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue or purple. The plant has a low-growing habit with slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Atherurus tripartitus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
