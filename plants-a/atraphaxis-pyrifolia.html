Atraphaxis pyrifolia

Atraphaxis pyrifolia, commonly known as the flame tree, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its ability to withstand drought conditions.
