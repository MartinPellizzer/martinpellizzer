Angelica decurrens

Angelica decurrens, commonly known as the common angelica, is a tall biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbrella-shaped inflorescences and aromatic foliage. The plant features large, deeply lobed leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are small and white to greenish in color, producing tiny, oily seeds.

Angelica decurrens is also valued for its culinary uses, particularly in herbal teas and as a flavoring agent.
