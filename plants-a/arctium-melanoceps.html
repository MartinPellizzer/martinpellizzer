Arctium melanoceps

Arctium melanoceps, commonly known as the black burdock, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces prickly, bur-like fruits that aid in seed dispersal. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine.

The species is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in supporting local wildlife.
