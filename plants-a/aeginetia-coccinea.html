Aeginetia coccinea

Aeginetia coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 30 cm tall, characterized by its small, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and short life cycle.
