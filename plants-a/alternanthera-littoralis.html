Alternanthera littoralis

Alternanthera littoralis, commonly known as the saltwater beauty, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetland or coastal areas, thriving in saline or brackish environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often red or purple, which can vary in color depending on light exposure and water conditions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellow, and it is often used in aquatic gardens or as a decorative plant in water features.

Its adaptability to moist and saline conditions makes it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
