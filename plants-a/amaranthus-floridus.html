Amaranthus floridus

Amaranthus floridus, commonly known as the Florida amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Amaranthus floridus is well adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in disturbed habitats.
