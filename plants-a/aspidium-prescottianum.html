Aspidium prescottianum

Aspidium prescottianum, commonly known as the ancient fern, is a prehistoric plant that thrived during the Paleozoic era.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its large, intricate fronds. The plant's structure includes a rhizome with sporangia that produce spores for reproduction. Aspidium prescottianum was widespread in swampy environments and played a significant role in ancient ecosystems.
