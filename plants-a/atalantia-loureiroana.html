Atalantia loureiroana

Atalantia loureiroana, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit or Natal tree, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and pinnate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright orange, fleshy fruits. The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
