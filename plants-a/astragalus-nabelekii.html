Astragalus nabelekii

Astragalus nabelekii, commonly known as the Nabelek's milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.

Astragalus nabelekii is also of interest in botanical research due to its potential medicinal properties.
