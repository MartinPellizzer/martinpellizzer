Astragalus creticus subsp. rumelicus

Astragalus creticus subsp.

rumelicus, commonly known as the Cretan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Greece. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to reddish flowers in late spring. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
