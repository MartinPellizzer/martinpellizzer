Avena sativa var. fusca

Avena sativa var. fusca, commonly known as the dark oat, is a variant of the common oat species.

It is characterized by its dark-colored grain, which is typically used in the production of animal feed and specialty foods. This variety is known for its high nutritional value, containing significant amounts of protein, fiber, and minerals. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features include slender stems, narrow leaves, and spikes of florets that mature into the dark, hard grains.
