Alpinia rheedei

Alpinia rheedei, commonly known as the red ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red inflorescences that contrast sharply with its dark green, strap-like leaves. The plant belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. Alpinia rheedei typically grows in shaded, moist environments and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and floral arrangements.
