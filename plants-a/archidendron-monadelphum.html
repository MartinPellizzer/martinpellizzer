Archidendron monadelphum

Archidendron monadelphum, commonly known as the yellow shower tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in dense clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its vibrant floral display.

Additionally, it has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, contributing to soil fertility.
