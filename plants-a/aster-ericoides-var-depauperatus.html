Aster ericoides var. depauperatus

Aster ericoides var. depauperatus, commonly known as the slender heath aster, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically features small, dark blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are densely packed, giving it a compact appearance. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or heathlands.

This variety is noted for its resilience and ability to flourish in harsh, nutrient-poor environments.
