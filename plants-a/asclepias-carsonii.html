Asclepias carsonii

Asclepias carsonii, commonly known as Carson's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to orange-red flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains toxic compounds, which deter herbivores. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, often with a fuzzy texture.

Carson's milkweed is an important host plant for the endangered monarch butterfly, providing essential habitat for their larvae.
