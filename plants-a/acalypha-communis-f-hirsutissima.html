Acalypha communis f. hirsutissima

Acalypha communis f. hirsutissima, commonly known as the velvetleaf or woolly hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive texture. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, red, or orange, often with a prominent central disk. It thrives in humid environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
