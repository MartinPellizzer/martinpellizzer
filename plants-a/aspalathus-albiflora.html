Aspalathus albiflora

Aspalathus albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered bushveld clover, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the cooler months. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Aspalathus albiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
