Astragalus puniceus

Astragalus puniceus, commonly known as the crimson milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its upright, spiny stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun and low moisture conditions.

Astragalus puniceus plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
