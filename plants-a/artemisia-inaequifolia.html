Artemisia inaequifolia

Artemisia inaequifolia, commonly known as the unequal-leaved wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which consists of narrow, linear leaves with a distinctive asymmetrical shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are clustered in loose panicles, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Artemisia inaequifolia is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
