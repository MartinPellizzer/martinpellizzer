Arabis resediflora

Arabis resediflora, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small, white or pink, and arranged in clusters. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Arabis resediflora is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is known for its hardy nature.
