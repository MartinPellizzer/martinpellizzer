Astragalus chlorotaenius

Astragalus chlorotaenius, commonly known as the green-stem milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its roots are capable of fixing nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Astragalus chlorotaenius is often found in steppe and desert-steppe environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
