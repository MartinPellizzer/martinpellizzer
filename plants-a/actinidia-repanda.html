Actinidia repanda

Actinidia repanda, commonly known as the Chinese mayapple, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Actinidiaceae family and is characterized by its broad, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by edible, bright red berries. Its fruit is often consumed by wildlife and has a mildly sweet, aromatic flavor.

Actinidia repanda is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for both horticultural and medicinal purposes.
