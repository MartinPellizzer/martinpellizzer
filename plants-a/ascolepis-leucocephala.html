Ascolepis leucocephala

Ascolepis leucocephala, commonly known as the white-headed pincushion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, spherical inflorescence that resembles a pincushion. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Ascolepis leucocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
