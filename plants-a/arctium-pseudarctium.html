Arctium pseudarctium

Arctium pseudarctium, commonly known as the false burdock, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a biennial plant characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces spiny, prickly seed heads that are adapted for wind dispersal. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it easily distinguishable from other members of the genus Arctium.
