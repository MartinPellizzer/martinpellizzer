Alliaria malabarica

Alliaria malabarica, commonly known as Indian garlic or Malabar garlic, is a herbaceous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like broccoli and mustard. The plant produces small, white, four-petaled flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a strong, pungent odor similar to garlic.

Alliaria malabarica is often found in moist, shaded areas and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
