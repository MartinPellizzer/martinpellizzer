Aralia undulata var. cirrhifolia

Aralia undulata var. cirrhifolia, commonly known as the curly-stemmed aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive undulating or wavy edge, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbels, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in moist, woodland habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native plant species.
