Aspasia fragrans

Aspasia fragrans, commonly known as the sweet sarsaparilla, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and often medicinal properties. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
