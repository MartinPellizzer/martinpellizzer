Abies concolor wattezii

Abies concolor wattezii, commonly known as the white fir, is a coniferous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against environmental stressors.

This species is adapted to high-altitude environments and plays an important role in forest ecosystems.
