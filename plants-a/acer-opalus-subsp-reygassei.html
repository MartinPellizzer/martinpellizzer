Acer opalus subsp. reygassei

Acer opalus subsp.

reygassei, commonly known as the Reygassei maple, is a rare and unique subspecies native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Europe. It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features palmately lobed leaves with a striking combination of green and red hues.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that aid in its dispersal. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
