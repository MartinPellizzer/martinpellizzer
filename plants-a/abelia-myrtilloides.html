Abelia myrtilloides

Abelia myrtilloides, commonly known as sweetbee or bluebees, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, fragrant, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Abelia myrtilloides is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
