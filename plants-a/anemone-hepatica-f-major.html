Anemone hepatica f. major

Anemone hepatica f. major, commonly known as the greater hepatica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features distinctive, palmately divided leaves that are typically bright green and often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, early spring blooms in shades of white, pink, or yellow, with five to seven petals arranged in a star-like pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

The species is noted for its ability to tolerate shade and is a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
