Azorella ameghinoi

Azorella ameghinoi, commonly known as the Andean stonecress, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-like growth habit and dense, mat-forming structure. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in its harsh, arid environment.

Azorella ameghinoi is also known for its ability to survive extreme cold and is often found in alpine and paramo ecosystems.
