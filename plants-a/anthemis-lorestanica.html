Anthemis lorestanica

Anthemis lorestanica, commonly known as Persian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellow-orange flower heads. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with ray florets that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, contributing to its use in herbal remedies.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or semi-arid habitats.
