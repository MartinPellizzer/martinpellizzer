Achillea lycaonica

Achillea lycaonica, commonly known as the Anatolian yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in full sun.

Its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine contribute to its significance in both horticulture and herbal practices.
