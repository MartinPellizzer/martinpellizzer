Astragalus aznabjurticus

Astragalus aznabjurticus, commonly known as the Kyzylkum milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with deep root systems. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in its native ecosystems.
