Acosta pseudocineraria

Acosta pseudocineraria, commonly known as the false cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy blue or violet flowers that attract pollinators. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions.

Acosta pseudocineraria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
