Atriplex acutibractea

Atriplex acutibractea, commonly known as the sharpbract saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its salt-tolerant nature allows it to thrive in saline soils, making it an important component of coastal and inland saltbush habitats.

Atriplex acutibractea plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native range.
