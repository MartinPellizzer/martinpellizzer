Acosta spachii

Acosta spachii, commonly known as the mountain lily, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

Acosta spachii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
