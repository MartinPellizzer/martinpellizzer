Argyranthemum canariense

Argyranthemum canariense, commonly known as Canary Island flossflower, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its fluffy, white to pale pink flower heads. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with silvery-gray foliage. It produces showy, daisy-like blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Argyranthemum canariense is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
