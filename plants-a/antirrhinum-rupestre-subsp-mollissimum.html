Antirrhinum rupestre subsp. mollissimum

Antirrhinum rupestre subsp.

mollissimum, commonly known as the velvet snapdragon, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
