Atropa punctata

Atropa punctata, commonly known as the spotted nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other toxic plants like deadly nightshade.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is characterized by its distinctive purple or violet flowers with yellow stamens. The plant produces small, round berries that are bright red when ripe, though they are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are typically ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Due to its toxicity, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
