Adiantum colpodes

Adiantum colpodes, commonly known as the water fern, is a species of fern native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bipinnate, with small, closely spaced pinnae. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments, often found near streams or in humid forests. Its rhizomes are creeping and can produce new plants through vegetative propagation.

Adiantum colpodes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with high humidity.
