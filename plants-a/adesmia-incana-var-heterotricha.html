Adesmia incana var. heterotricha

Adesmia incana var. heterotricha, commonly known as the hairy leaf tree, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds. It is adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots and a slow growth rate.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a notable species in its native habitat.
