Aconitum refracticarpum

Aconitum refracticarpum, commonly known as the refractive fruit aconite, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, intricate structure.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it highly poisonous to both humans and animals.
