Achras xerocarpa

Achras xerocarpa, commonly known as the breadfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is a large, evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its thick, fibrous trunk and broad, palm-like leaves. The plant produces large, green, spiny fruits that mature to a yellowish color, containing a starchy, edible pulp and a central core of seeds. Its botanical features include a robust root system and the ability to thrive in coastal and tropical climates.

The tree is valued for its fruit, which is a staple food in many Pacific Island cultures.
