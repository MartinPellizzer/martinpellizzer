Aleurites remyi

Aleurites remyi, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple, and ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by orange to red fruits containing three to four seeds. Aleurites remyi is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common ornamental plant.

However, it is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its aggressive spread and ecological impact.
