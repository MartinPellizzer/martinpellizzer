Acanthus leucostachyus

Acanthus leucostachyus, commonly known as the white spurge or white acanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features striking, palmate leaves with a rough, hairy texture and produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Acanthus leucostachyus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
