Arum variolatum

Arum variolatum, commonly known as the spotted arum, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands and marshes of Southeast Asia.

It features a distinctive purple-tinged, arrow-shaped leaf that often has a spotted or mottled appearance. The plant produces a striking inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a showy, petal-like spathe. Its flowers are typically greenish-white with a subtle fragrance, and it blooms during the warmer months.

Arum variolatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded garden settings.
