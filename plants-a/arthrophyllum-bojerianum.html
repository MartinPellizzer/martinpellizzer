Arthrophyllum bojerianum

Arthrophyllum bojerianum, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and heat. The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are small and often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Arthrophyllum bojerianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
