Ajania furcata

Ajania furcata, commonly known as the mountain ajania, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, dark green leaves and produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler regions.
