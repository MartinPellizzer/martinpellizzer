Aspalathus robusta

Aspalathus robusta, commonly known as the African red bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant is notable for its dense, bushy growth habit and its ability to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils. Its small, clustered flowers are typically red or purple, and it produces small, round pods that contain seeds.

Aspalathus robusta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
