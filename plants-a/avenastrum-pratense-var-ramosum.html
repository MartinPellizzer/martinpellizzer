Avenastrum pratense var. ramosum

Avenastrum pratense var. ramosum, commonly known as the ramose meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe.

It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. The plant features slender, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-bearing branches, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family.

Avenastrum pratense var. ramosum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
