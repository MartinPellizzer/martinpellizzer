Agrestis viridis

Agrestis viridis, commonly known as the green field grass, is a species of flowering plant in the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically a vibrant green color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are a common feature among grasses. Agrestis viridis is often found in open, sunny areas such as meadows and disturbed soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals.
