Aster junceus var. major

Aster junceus var. major, commonly known as the tall blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, covered with fine, grayish hairs. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in a narrow, spike-like cluster, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and often have a bluish-green hue with a fuzzy texture.

This variety is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands and sagebrush steppe.
