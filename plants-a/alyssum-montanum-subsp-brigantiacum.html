Alyssum montanum subsp. brigantiacum

Alyssum montanum subsp.

brigantiacum, commonly known as the Sierra foothill alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a low-growing annual herb with small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow.

The plant typically thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
