Andreadoxa

Andreadoxa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Andreadoxa is known for its rapid growth and is often considered an invasive species in some areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable example of plant adaptability in diverse environments.
