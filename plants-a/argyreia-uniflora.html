Argyreia uniflora

Argyreia uniflora, commonly known as the holy leaf or sacred lotus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, large, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and spiritual practices. The plant produces a single, showy white or pink flower at the top of a long, slender stem. Its leaves are typically covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its native tropical environment.

Argyreia uniflora is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
