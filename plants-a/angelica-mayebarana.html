Angelica mayebarana

Angelica mayebarana, commonly known as the Japanese angelica, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow-green flowers in umbrella-shaped clusters, known as umbels, during the summer months. Its roots are often harvested for their aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses.

Angelica mayebarana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
