Aristida pittieri

Aristida pittieri, commonly known as the Pittier's threeawn, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and wiry, with a bluish-green coloration.

Aristida pittieri is adapted to arid conditions and is often used in restoration projects due to its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil.
