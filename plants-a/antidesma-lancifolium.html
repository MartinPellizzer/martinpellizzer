Antidesma lancifolium

Antidesma lancifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved antidesma, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually lance-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in clusters and are followed by small, fleshy berries that are often red or yellow when mature.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
