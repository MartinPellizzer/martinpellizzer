Arnica louiseana subsp. genuina

Arnica louiseana subsp.

genuina, commonly known as the Louisiana mountain arnica, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils in forested areas and along stream banks.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers in the summer. It is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and foliage.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
