Acacia armata var. ornithophora

Acacia armata var. ornithophora, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, flat, grey-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
