Antirrhinum murale subsp. barrelieri

Antirrhinum murale subsp.

barrelieri, commonly known as the Moroccan snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa. It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
