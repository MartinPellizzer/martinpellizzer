Achyranthes velutina

Achyranthes velutina, commonly known as velvet leaf, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

Achyranthes velutina is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
