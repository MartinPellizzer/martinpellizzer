Acaena anserinifolia var. minor

Acaena anserinifolia var. minor, commonly known as the small silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It features dense clusters of small, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, which later develop into spiny, berry-like structures. Its growth habit is mat-forming, with creeping stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across rocky or sandy soils.

This species is well adapted to harsh environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.
