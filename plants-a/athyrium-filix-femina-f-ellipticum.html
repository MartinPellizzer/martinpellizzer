Athyrium filix-femina f. ellipticum

Athyrium filix-femina f. ellipticum, commonly known as the Japanese maidenhair fern, is a variant of the common maidenhair fern characterized by its elliptical, leathery fronds.

It is a perennial evergreen fern native to eastern Asia, including Japan, Korea, and China. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are dark green and glossy, with a delicate, feathery appearance. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and indoor settings.

This fern is particularly valued for its ability to thrive in humid conditions and its elegant, refined foliage.
