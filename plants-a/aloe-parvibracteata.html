Aloe parvibracteata

Aloe parvibracteata, commonly known as the narrow-bracted aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features rosettes of fleshy, upright leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tint. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellowish-green flowers during the cooler months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves.

Aloe parvibracteata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry conditions.
