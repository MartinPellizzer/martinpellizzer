Astragalus asperulus

Astragalus asperulus, commonly known as rough milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a deep taproot system, which allows it to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Astragalus asperulus is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
