Acalypha racemosa

Acalypha racemosa, commonly known as the red-hot poker or fireplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, red bracts that surround small yellow flowers, creating a vibrant display. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Acalypha racemosa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
