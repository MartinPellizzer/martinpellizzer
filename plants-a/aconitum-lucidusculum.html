Aconitum lucidusculum

Aconitum lucidusculum, commonly known as the glossy aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple spathaceous flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has palmately divided, hairy leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
