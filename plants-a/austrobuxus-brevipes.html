Austrobuxus brevipes

Austrobuxus brevipes, commonly known as the short-footed boxwood, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage, which consists of small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for certain wildlife species. Austrobuxus brevipes thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a hardy plant suitable for various garden settings.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for landscaping and horticultural use.
