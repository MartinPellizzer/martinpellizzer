Aconitum jinyangense

Aconitum jinyangense, commonly known as the Jinyang monkshood, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy purple or blue flowers. The plant produces distinctive, helmet-shaped flowers with five sepals that are often darker in color. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed leaflets, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Aconitum jinyangense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
