Agrostis merxmuelleri

Agrostis merxmuelleri, commonly known as the Merxmueller's bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Agrostis merxmuelleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in high-altitude ecosystems.
