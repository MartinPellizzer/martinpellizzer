Alyssum compactum

Alyssum compactum, commonly known as the dwarf sweet Alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, blooming throughout the summer months. The plant has compact, mat-forming foliage with silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum compactum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects such as bees and butterflies.
