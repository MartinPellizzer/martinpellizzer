Archirhodomyrtus turbinata

Archirhodomyrtus turbinata, commonly known as the turbinated rhodomyrtus, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly rough texture.

The plant is typically found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions.
