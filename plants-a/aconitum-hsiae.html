Aconitum hsiae

Aconitum hsiae, commonly known as the Yunnan monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely leafed stems and showy, blue to purple hooded flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. Its roots contain toxic alkaloids, which have been historically used in traditional medicine.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
