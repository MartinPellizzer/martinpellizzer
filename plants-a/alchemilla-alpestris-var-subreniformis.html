Alchemilla alpestris var. subreniformis

Alchemilla alpestris var. subreniformis, commonly known as the Alpine lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features basal leaves that are kidney-shaped with a hairy underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is cultivated in gardens with dry, sunny conditions.
