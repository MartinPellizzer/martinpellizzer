Acacia difficilis

Acacia difficilis, commonly known as the black wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are followed by woody pods containing flattened seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry forests.

Acacia difficilis is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in soil enrichment.
