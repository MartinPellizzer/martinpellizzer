Amaryllis scillifolia

Amaryllis scillifolia, commonly known as the sea squill, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a bulbous plant that produces clusters of white or pale blue flowers with a slight pink tinge. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often emerging after the flowers have bloomed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

The plant is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
