Allium nuristanicum

Allium nuristanicum, commonly known as the Nuristan garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of northern Pakistan and Afghanistan.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that are similar to garlic and onions, containing sulfur compounds that give it a pungent aroma. It thrives in high-altitude environments with well-drained soils and cool climates.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
