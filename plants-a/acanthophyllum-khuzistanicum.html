Acanthophyllum khuzistanicum

Acanthophyllum khuzistanicum, commonly known as the Khuzistan spiny herb, is a flowering plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing features typical of xerophytes. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often green or reddish in color.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
