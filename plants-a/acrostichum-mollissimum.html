Acrostichum mollissimum

Acrostichum mollissimum, commonly known as the silky fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, soft, and velvety fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores for propagation.

The silky fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
