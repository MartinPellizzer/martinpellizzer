Acacia proxima

Acacia proxima, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, which are often visited by native bees and birds. Acacia proxima is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features such as deep roots and reduced leaf surface area.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
