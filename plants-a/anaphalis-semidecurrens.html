Anaphalis semidecurrens

Anaphalis semidecurrens, commonly known as the silverleaf frostflower, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and often have a woolly texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Anaphalis semidecurrens is a perennial herb that blooms from late spring to early summer.
