Acaena valdiviana

Acaena valdiviana, commonly known as the Chilean burr bush, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its woody shrub growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the development of prickly, spherical fruits. Its fruiting bodies, known as burrs, contain seeds enclosed in a tough, spiny covering.

Acaena valdiviana is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
