Artocarpus brasiliensis

Artocarpus brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive fruit. The fruit, which is typically yellow when ripe, has a fibrous outer layer and a starchy, edible pulp inside. It is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is used in traditional cuisines.

The tree is also valued for its hard wood and is sometimes planted for ornamental purposes.
