Achillea × barthiana

Achillea × barthiana, commonly known as the yarrow hybrid, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its flat, composite flower heads that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features finely divided, gray-green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its historical use in herbal medicine is attributed to its ability to promote wound healing and its antiseptic properties.
