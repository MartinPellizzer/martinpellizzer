Agave sobria subsp. sobria

Agave sobria subsp.

sobria, commonly known as the narrow-leaf agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico. It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.
