Alchemilla chatiparensis

Alchemilla chatiparensis, commonly known as the Chatiparensis lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a cluster at the end of each stem, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Alchemilla chatiparensis is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
