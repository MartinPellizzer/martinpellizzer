Astragalus franciscanus var. longulus

Astragalus franciscanus var. longulus, commonly known as the longpod milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, slender pods that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and sensitive species.
