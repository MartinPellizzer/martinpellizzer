Aspalathus juniperina subsp. monticola

Aspalathus juniperina subsp.

monticola, commonly known as the mountain aspalathus, is a shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa. It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It is adapted to a Mediterranean climate with wet winters and dry summers.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
