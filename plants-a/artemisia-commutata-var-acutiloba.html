Artemisia commutata var. acutiloba

Artemisia commutata var. acutiloba, commonly known as rough tarragon, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and aromatic, feathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

Due to its medicinal properties and distinctive appearance, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental or herbal purposes.
