Acalypha diminuta

Acalypha diminuta, commonly known as the dwarf croton, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, colorful foliage that often displays shades of red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are typically ovate or heart-shaped and have a velvety texture. It produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are followed by three-lobed seed capsules.

This plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its vibrant leaves and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
