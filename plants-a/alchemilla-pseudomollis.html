Alchemilla pseudomollis

Alchemilla pseudomollis, commonly known as the lesser lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Alchemilla pseudomollis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in partial shade.
