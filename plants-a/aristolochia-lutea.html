Aristolochia lutea

Aristolochia lutea, commonly known as the yellow Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, maroon-colored flowers that resemble a pipe, with a yellow throat. The plant grows as a vine, often climbing or trailing along the ground, and has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

The species is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
