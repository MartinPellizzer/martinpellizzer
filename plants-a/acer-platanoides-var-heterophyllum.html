Acer platanoides var. heterophyllum

Acer platanoides var. heterophyllum, commonly known as the Norway maple variant, is a deciduous tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically five-lobed and have a distinctive, smooth texture. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras that disperse its seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is commonly planted in gardens and urban landscapes.
