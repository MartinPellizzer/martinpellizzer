Aconitum lamarckii subsp. pantocsekianum

Aconitum lamarckii subsp.

pantocsekianum, commonly known as the Hungarian aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height.

The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed divisions, and the plant contains toxic alkaloids throughout its tissues.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
