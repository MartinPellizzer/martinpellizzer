Arundo valdiviana

Arundo valdiviana, commonly known as the Valdivian reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in moist environments, such as riverbanks and wetlands, and is well adapted to humid climates. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly and form dense stands.

The plant is also valued for its use in traditional basketry and as a source of material for construction in some regions.
