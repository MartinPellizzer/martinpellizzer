Annona coriacea var. cuneata

Annona coriacea var. cuneata, commonly known as the Brazilian custard apple, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that produces large, green, fleshy fruits with a sweet, creamy pulp. The leaves are alternate, simple, and usually have a rough, hairy texture. The plant belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive floral structure with a fleshy, yellowish perianth.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit.
