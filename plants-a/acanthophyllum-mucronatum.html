Acanthophyllum mucronatum

Acanthophyllum mucronatum, commonly known as the spikeless jointfoot, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, shrub-like growth form. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It has adapted to survive in harsh environments with minimal water availability.

Its structure and physiology make it well-suited for desert and steppe ecosystems.
