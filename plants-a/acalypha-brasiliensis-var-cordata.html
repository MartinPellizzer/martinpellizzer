Acalypha brasiliensis var. cordata

Acalypha brasiliensis var. cordata, commonly known as the Brazilian nettle or cordate acalypha, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are often surrounded by bracts. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in warmer climates.
