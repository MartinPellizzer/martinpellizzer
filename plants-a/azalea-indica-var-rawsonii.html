Azalea indica var. rawsonii

Azalea indica var. rawsonii, commonly known as the Rawson's azalea, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a contrasting throat. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and elliptical in shape, providing a striking contrast to the flowers.

Azalea indica var. rawsonii thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
