Acinodendron symplocoideum

Acinodendron symplocoideum, commonly known as the African tamarind, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its leguminous properties and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes. Its fruit is a pod containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological contributions in its native habitat.
