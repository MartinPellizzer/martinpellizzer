Astragalus kurdaicus

Astragalus kurdaicus, commonly known as Kurdish milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Turkey, Iran, and Iraq, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus kurdaicus is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity.
