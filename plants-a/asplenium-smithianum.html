Asplenium smithianum

Asplenium smithianum, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of fern native to various regions in the Americas.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and is often found growing on rocky substrates or in shaded, moist environments. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with dense, overlapping fronds that form a rosette-like structure. Its leaves are typically lanceolate or ovate, with a smooth or serrated margin depending on the variety.

Asplenium smithianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
