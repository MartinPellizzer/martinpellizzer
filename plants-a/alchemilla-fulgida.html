Alchemilla fulgida

Alchemilla fulgida, commonly known as the golden tutsan, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant features basal leaves that are softly hairy and have a distinctive silver-green color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or shady gardens.

Alchemilla fulgida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
