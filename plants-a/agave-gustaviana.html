Agave gustaviana

Agave gustaviana, commonly known as the Gustaviana agave, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flower stalk bearing yellow flowers, which bloom in the spring. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Agave gustaviana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil.
