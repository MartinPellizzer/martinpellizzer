Agathosma hortensis

Agathosma hortensis, commonly known as the African germander, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rocky or coastal gardens.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is also cultivated for the production of essential oils.
