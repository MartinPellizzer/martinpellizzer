Asclepias syriaca var. kansana

Asclepias syriaca var. kansana, commonly known as showy milkweed, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of vibrant orange to red flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains cardiac glycosides, which are toxic to many animals. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

This variety is often found in prairies, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in full sun.
