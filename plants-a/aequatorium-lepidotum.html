Aequatorium lepidotum

Aequatorium lepidotum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Aequatorium lepidotum is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
