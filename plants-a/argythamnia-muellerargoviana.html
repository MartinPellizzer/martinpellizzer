Argythamnia muellerargoviana

Argythamnia muellerargoviana, commonly known as the Miller's thorn.

It is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia, characterized by its small, pale green leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in poor soil conditions and withstand drought. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
