Athyrium yui

Athyrium yui, commonly known as the Yui Japanese shield fern, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Dryopteridaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its rhizomes are covered in a dense network of roots, which help anchor it to rocky or soil substrates.

Athyrium yui is valued for its ornamental foliage and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
