Anathallis carnosifolia

Anathallis carnosifolia, commonly known as the meaty-leaved anathallis, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its unique, fleshy, and often hairy leaves that store water, adapting it to its humid, tropical environment. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are typically clustered in a spadix, typical of the Bromeliaceae family. It thrives in shaded, moist conditions and is often found in forest understories, where it plays a role in the ecosystem by providing habitat for various small organisms.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
