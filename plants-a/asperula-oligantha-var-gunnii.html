Asperula oligantha var. gunnii

Asperula oligantha var. gunnii, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, arching stems. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a serrated edge, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
