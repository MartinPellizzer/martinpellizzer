Acinos majoranifolius f. hirsutior

Acinos majoranifolius f. hirsutior, commonly known as the hairy yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant features hairy, gray-green foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its hirsute (hairy) appearance is a distinguishing trait of this particular form.
