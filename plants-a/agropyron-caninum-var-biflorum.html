Agropyron caninum var. biflorum

Agropyron caninum var. biflorum, commonly known as the two-flowered wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces spikelets with two florets, giving it its specific epithet "biflorum.". Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a hairy texture.

It is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.
