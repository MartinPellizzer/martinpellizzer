Acacia dacrydioides

Acacia dacrydioides, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and feathery leaves. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and open grasslands.

Acacia dacrydioides plays an important ecological role and is also a symbol of Australia's natural heritage.
