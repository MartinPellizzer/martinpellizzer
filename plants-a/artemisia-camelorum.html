Artemisia camelorum

Artemisia camelorum, commonly known as the Armenian cedar or mountain wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage with finely divided leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are typically clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. Artemisia camelorum is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
