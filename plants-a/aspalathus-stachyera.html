Aspalathus stachyera

Aspalathus stachyera, commonly known as the silver leaf bush, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-resistant. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions.

The silver leaf bush is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging climates.
