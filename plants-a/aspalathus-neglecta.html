Aspalathus neglecta

Aspalathus neglecta, commonly known as the small bract protea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its unique, cone-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in coastal and mountainous regions. Its small, pale yellow flowers are clustered around a central stem, and it is often found in the Cape Floristic Region.

Aspalathus neglecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
