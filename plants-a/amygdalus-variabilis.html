Amygdalus variabilis

Amygdalus variabilis, commonly known as the variable almond, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its deciduous nature and ornamental value. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by the development of almond-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a serrated margin, and the bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Amygdalus variabilis is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
