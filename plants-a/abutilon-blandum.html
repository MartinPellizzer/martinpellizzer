Abutilon blandum

Abutilon blandum, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments. Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.

Abutilon blandum is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
