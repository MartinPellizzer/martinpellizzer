Agave laticincta

Agave laticincta, commonly known as the broad-belt agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color with white or grayish margins. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence bearing yellow flowers, which appear in late spring or early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sun exposure.

Agave laticincta is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.
