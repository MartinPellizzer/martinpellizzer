Aloe richaudii

Aloe richaudii, commonly known as the Cape aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy. The leaves are typically green with a reddish or purplish tinge, especially when exposed to full sunlight. It produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellowish-green flowers during the summer months.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
