Adesmia nana

Adesmia nana, commonly known as the dwarf adesmia, is a small shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows to a height of less than 30 centimeters, with compact, rounded foliage. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Adesmia nana thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
