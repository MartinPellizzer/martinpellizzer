Acianthus halleanus

Acianthus halleanus, commonly known as the Hall's acianthus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native arid environments.

Acianthus halleanus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
