Andropogon brevifolius var. paradoxus

Andropogon brevifolius var. paradoxus, commonly known as the paradox grass, is a variant of the switchgrass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, twisted leaf blades that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that becomes more pronounced as the plant matures.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
