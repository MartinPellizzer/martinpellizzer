Achillea buglossis

Achillea buglossis, commonly known as narrowleaf yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, aromatic leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its historical use in herbal medicine is attributed to its purported ability to stop bleeding, hence its common name.
