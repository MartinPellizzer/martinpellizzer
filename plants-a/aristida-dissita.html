Aristida dissita

Aristida dissita, commonly known as the scattered hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and panicles that are loosely arranged and often appear scattered. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides, fields, and sandy soils. Its small, spikelet-based flowers are usually inconspicuous and produce light seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Aristida dissita is adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
