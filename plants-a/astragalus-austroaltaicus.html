Astragalus austroaltaicus

Astragalus austroaltaicus, commonly known as the southern Alps milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typically hairy and serrated. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Astragalus austroaltaicus plays a role in nitrogen fixation and is of interest for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
