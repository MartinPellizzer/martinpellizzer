Aneurolepidium akmolinense

Aneurolepidium akmolinense, commonly known as the Akmolinsk grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and semi-deserts. The plant has slender, erect culms with narrow, flat leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet at the tip of each branch, which is characteristic of the Poaceae family.

Aneurolepidium akmolinense is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
