Artemisia gyitangensis

Artemisia gyitangensis, commonly known as the Gyitang wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
