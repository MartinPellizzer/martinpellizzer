Adansonia gregorii f. globosa

Adansonia gregorii f. globosa, commonly known as the Christmas tree baobab, is a unique variant of the baobab tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, globular trunk that resembles a Christmas tree, giving it its common name. This form of the species is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, where it has adapted to thrive in harsh, dry conditions. The plant's thick, spongy bark helps it store water, and its extensive root system allows it to survive long periods without rainfall.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
