Ammoides leiocarpa

Ammoides leiocarpa, commonly known as the white sand verbena, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suited to arid environments.

Ammoides leiocarpa is often found in sandy or rocky habitats, contributing to the biodiversity of desert ecosystems.
