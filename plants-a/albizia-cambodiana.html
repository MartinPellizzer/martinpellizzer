Albizia cambodiana

Albizia cambodiana, commonly known as Cambodian silk tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant has a deep root system that helps in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of soil types.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
