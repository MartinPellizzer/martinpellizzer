Aria tomentosa

Aria tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, white to pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

Aria tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
