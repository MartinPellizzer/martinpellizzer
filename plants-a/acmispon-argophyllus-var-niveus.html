Acmispon argophyllus var. niveus

Acmispon argophyllus var. niveus, commonly known as the silverleaf burrobrush, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
