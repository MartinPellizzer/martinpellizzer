Andropogon exilis var. plurispicatus

Andropogon exilis var. plurispicatus, commonly known as the slender grass or tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and have a bluish-green hue, while the inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common component of prairie and savanna ecosystems.

It plays a vital role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects and small animals.
