Achillea sprengeliana

Achillea sprengeliana, commonly known as the Sprengel's yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves and flat, composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers bloom in shades of white to pale yellow, often appearing in late summer to early autumn.

Achillea sprengeliana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
