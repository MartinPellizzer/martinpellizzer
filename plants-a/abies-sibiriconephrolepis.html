Abies sibiriconephrolepis

Abies sibiriconephrolepis, commonly known as the Siberian fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its conical shape, dark green needles, and distinctive cone structure. The tree typically grows in cold, high-altitude environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against harsh weather conditions.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various wildlife species and contributing to forest biodiversity.
