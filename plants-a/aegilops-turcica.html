Aegilops turcica

Aegilops turcica, commonly known as the Turkish goatgrass, is a wild annual grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown bracts. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural areas.

Aegilops turcica is of interest in botanical studies due to its potential genetic contributions to wheat improvement.
