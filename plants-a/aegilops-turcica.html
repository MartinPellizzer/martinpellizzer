Aegilops turcica

Aegilops turcica, commonly known as the Turkish goatgrass, is a wild annual grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown bracts. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural areas.

Aegilops turcica is of interest in botanical studies due to its potential genetic contributions to wheat improvement.

Morphology

Aegilops turcica has alternate, narrow, linear leaves that are typically bluish-green in color and range from 5 to 15 cm in length, with a grass-like appearance.

The stem is erect, cylindrical, and smooth, with a light green to reddish-brown color, growing in dense tufts. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences with small, pale blue to greenish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, and are wind-pollinated. The fruit is a small, three-lobed caryopsis, and seeds are dispersed by wind due to their light weight.

The root system is fibrous and shallow, adapting to arid environments.
