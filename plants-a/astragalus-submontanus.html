Astragalus submontanus

Astragalus submontanus, commonly known as the mountain milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils at high elevations, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus submontanus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
