Astragalus teresianus

Astragalus teresianus, commonly known as the Spanish broom or goat's head, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and small, pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
