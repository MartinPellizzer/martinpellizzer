Agropyron koryoense

Agropyron koryoense, commonly known as the Korean wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces spikelets that are typically pale green to reddish in color. The plant has narrow, blade-like leaves and is adapted to grow in temperate climates with well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Agropyron koryoense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
