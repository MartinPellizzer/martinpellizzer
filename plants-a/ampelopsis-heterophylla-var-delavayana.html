Ampelopsis heterophylla var. delavayana

Ampelopsis heterophylla var. delavayana, commonly known as the Delavay's grape ivy, is a climbing vine native to China.

It features compound leaves that vary in shape, with the upper leaves typically being trifoliate and the lower ones usually single. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for vertical gardening.

Its ornamental value comes from its foliage and berries, which attract birds and add visual interest to gardens.
