Actinidia fulvicoma

Actinidia fulvicoma, commonly known as the red-edged mountain ash, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Actinidiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and a distinctive reddish hue along the edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that turn red when mature. Actinidia fulvicoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also cultivated for its fruit, which is used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a versatile plant for landscaping and ecological restoration.
