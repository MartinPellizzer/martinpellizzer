Abildgaardia microelegans

Abildgaardia microelegans, commonly known as the dwarf tamarisk, is a small shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 meter and features narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
