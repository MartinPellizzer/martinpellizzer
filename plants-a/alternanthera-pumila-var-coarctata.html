Alternanthera pumila var. coarctata

Alternanthera pumila var. coarctata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Alternanthera, is a compact, herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically red or pink on the underside, creating a striking contrast with the green upper surface. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is often cultivated as a decorative plant in aquatic or bog gardens due to its ornamental foliage.
