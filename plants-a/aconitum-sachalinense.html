Aconitum sachalinense

Aconitum sachalinense, commonly known as the Sakhalin aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Far East of Russia and parts of Korea.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense racemes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed divisions, and are covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

Aconitum sachalinense is known for its toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires careful handling.
