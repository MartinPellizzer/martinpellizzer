Aspalathus pinguis subsp. occidentalis

Aspalathus pinguis subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the Western fat-leaf bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. It is a hardy species that can withstand periods of drought.

Its unique morphology makes it an interesting subject for botanical study and ecological research.
