Acarna macrocephala

Acarna macrocephala, commonly known as the large-headed acarna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which is characterized by its unique inflorescence structure consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with a central spadix surrounded by a colorful, petal-like spathe. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are typically broad and arrow-shaped, contributing to its aesthetic appeal in both natural and garden settings.
