Achyranthes villosa

Achyranthes villosa, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can become invasive in agricultural areas. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread.

Velvetleaf is often found in moist environments and can thrive in partial shade.
