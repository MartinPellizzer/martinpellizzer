Allium pallens subsp. paniculatum

Allium pallens subsp.

paniculatum, commonly known as the purple garlic chive, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant produces bulbils along its flowering stems, which can be used for propagation. Native to temperate regions of Asia, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild garlic-like flavor.
