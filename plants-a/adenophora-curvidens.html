Adenophora curvidens

Adenophora curvidens, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Adenophora curvidens is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
