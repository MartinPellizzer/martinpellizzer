Asplenium callipteris

Asplenium callipteris, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and have a distinctive, feathery appearance. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. The fronds are usually leathery and have a unique, intricate structure that aids in water retention.

Asplenium callipteris is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated for its ability to thrive in humid indoor conditions.
