Armeniaca mandshurica var. glabra

Armeniaca mandshurica var. glabra, commonly known as the Manchurian apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and pinnately compound leaves that are alternate and ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange to reddish-yellow drupes. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.

The species is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
