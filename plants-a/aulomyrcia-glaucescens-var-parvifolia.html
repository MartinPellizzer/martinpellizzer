Aulomyrcia glaucescens var. parvifolia

Aulomyrcia glaucescens var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved glaucous aulomyrcia, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bluish-gray coating. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its growth habit is typically upright, with multiple stems forming a compact, bushy form.

Aulomyrcia glaucescens var. parvifolia is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
