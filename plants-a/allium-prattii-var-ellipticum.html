Allium prattii var. ellipticum

Allium prattii var. ellipticum, commonly known as the elliptic garlic.

It is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish-purple flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in culinary applications.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
