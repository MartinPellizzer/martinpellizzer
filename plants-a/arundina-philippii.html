Arundina philippii

Arundina philippii, commonly known as the Philippi's bamboo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, which are often overlooked due to their modest appearance. Arundina philippii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.

Its growth habit and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical climates.
