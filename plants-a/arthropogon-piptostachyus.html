Arthropogon piptostachyus

Arthropogon piptostachyus, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences that are typically arranged in a panicle structure. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and the plant is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Arthropogon piptostachyus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
