Angelica koreana

Angelica koreana, commonly known as Korean angelica, is a herbaceous plant native to Korea and parts of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the carrot family. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Angelica koreana thrives in moist, shady environments and is valued both for its ornamental qualities and medicinal properties.
