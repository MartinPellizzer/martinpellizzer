Acacia oerfota var. brevifolia

Acacia oerfota var. brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, typical of the Acacia genus. The leaves are bipinnate, with numerous tiny leaflets that give the plant a feathery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand arid conditions.
