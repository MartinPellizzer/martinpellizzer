Alchemilla hirsutiflora

Alchemilla hirsutiflora, commonly known as the hairy-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla hirsutiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
