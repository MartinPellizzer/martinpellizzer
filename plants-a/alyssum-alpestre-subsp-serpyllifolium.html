Alyssum alpestre subsp. serpyllifolium

Alyssum alpestre subsp.

serpyllifolium, commonly known as alpine mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically has hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. Alyssum alpestre subsp.

serpyllifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh climatic conditions.
