Acacia epedunculata

Acacia epedunculata, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in compact, globular heads. The plant produces flat, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well-adapted to drought conditions.

Acacia epedunculata is also known for its role in providing habitat and food for various desert animals.
