Alchemilla strigosula subsp. pilosicaulis

Alchemilla strigosula subsp.

pilosicaulis, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of fuzzy, gray-green leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive appearance, making it a notable species in its native environment.
