Arytera leichhardtii var. hebepetala

Arytera leichhardtii var. hebepetala, commonly known as the broad-leafed lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or mauve, with a distinctive downward-curving petal. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

This variety is valued for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
