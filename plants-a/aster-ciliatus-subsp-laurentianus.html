Aster ciliatus subsp. laurentianus

Aster ciliatus subsp.

laurentianus, commonly known as the Laurentian aster, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States and Canada. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, clustered blue to purple flowers in the fall. It is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and has a taproot system that helps it survive in various soil conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
