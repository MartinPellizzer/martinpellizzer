Aiouea impressa

Aiouea impressa, commonly known as the Hawaiian mahogany, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, orange-red fruits containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland to montane rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
