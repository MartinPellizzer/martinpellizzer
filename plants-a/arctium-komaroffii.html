Arctium komaroffii

Arctium komaroffii, commonly known as the Komaroff's burdock, is a herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous roots. The plant produces large, spiny burs that aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animal fur or human clothing. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often hairy.

Arctium komaroffii is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
