Archibaccharis tuxtlensis

Archibaccharis tuxtlensis, commonly known as the Tuxtlas baccharis, is a shrub native to the humid forests of southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, often aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, contributing to its ecological importance as a nectar source for local pollinators.
