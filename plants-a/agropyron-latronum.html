Agropyron latronum

Agropyron latronum, commonly known as the prairie grass or western wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and stiff, ascending or spreading blades. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, prairies, and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Agropyron latronum is valued for its adaptability and is often used in restoration projects and as forage for livestock.
