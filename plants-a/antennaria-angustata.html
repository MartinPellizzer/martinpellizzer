Antennaria angustata

Antennaria angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf mouseear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northwestern United States and southwestern Canada.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces delicate yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Antennaria angustata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
