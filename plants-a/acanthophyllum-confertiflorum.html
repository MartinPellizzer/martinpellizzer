Acanthophyllum confertiflorum

Acanthophyllum confertiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower thrift, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant has small, spiny leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to extreme temperature fluctuations.

Acanthophyllum confertiflorum plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
