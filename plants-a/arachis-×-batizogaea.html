Arachis × batizogaea

Arachis × batizogaea, commonly known as the peanut hybrid, is a botanical species resulting from the cross between two peanut species, Arachis hypogaea and Arachis stenosperma.

This plant is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets. It produces flowers that develop into underground pods containing edible seeds, a trait shared with its parent species. The plant thrives in warm climates and well-drained soils, making it suitable for cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its genetic diversity contributes to the development of improved peanut varieties with enhanced yield and disease resistance.
