Anthyllis vulneraria var. catalonica

Anthyllis vulneraria var. catalonica, commonly known as the Catalan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in supporting local insect populations and its historical use in traditional medicine.
