Araucaria imbricata var. aurea

Araucaria imbricata var. aurea, commonly known as the golden Norfolk pine, is a variant of the Norfolk pine native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slow growth and distinctive, feathery foliage that turns a striking golden hue in sunlight. The plant features a tall, slender trunk with a conical shape, and its leaves are needle-like, arranged in whorls along the branches. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

This variety is particularly valued for its unique coloration and elegant appearance.
