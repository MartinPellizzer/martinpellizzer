Aponogeton undulatus

Aponogeton undulatus, commonly known as the wavy-leaf water arum, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that undulate in the water, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in ponds and aquariums.

Aponogeton undulatus is also notable for its ability to produce large, fleshy tubers that can survive in water for extended periods.
