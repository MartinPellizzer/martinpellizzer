Adiantum collisii

Adiantum collisii, commonly known as the mountain maidenhair fern, is a delicate and graceful fern species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, arching fronds that give it a lacy appearance. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are typically bright green and turn a reddish-brown in the autumn, adding seasonal interest.

Adiantum collisii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in shaded, humid conditions.
