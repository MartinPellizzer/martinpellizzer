Aberia zeyheri var. velutina

Aberia zeyheri var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet abelia, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its appeal in horticultural settings.
