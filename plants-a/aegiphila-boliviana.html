Aegiphila boliviana

Aegiphila boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in high-altitude environments. It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is known for its adaptability to harsh, arid conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
