Azalea macrantha var. albiflora-souchetiana

Azalea macrantha var. albiflora-souchetiana, commonly known as the Souchet's white azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that provide a striking contrast to its delicate blooms. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
