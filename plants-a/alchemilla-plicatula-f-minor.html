Alchemilla plicatula f. minor

Alchemilla plicatula f. minor, commonly known as the lesser lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features basal rosettes of rounded, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for wetland restoration and native garden landscapes.
