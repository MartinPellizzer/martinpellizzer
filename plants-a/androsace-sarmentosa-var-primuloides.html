Androsace sarmentosa var. primuloides

Androsace sarmentosa var. primuloides, commonly known as the creeping mouse-eared chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to cold climates and harsh mountain conditions.
