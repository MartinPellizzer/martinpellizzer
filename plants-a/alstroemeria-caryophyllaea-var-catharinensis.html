Alstroemeria caryophyllaea var. catharinensis

Alstroemeria caryophyllaea var. catharinensis, commonly known as the Catharinensis Alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, with lance-shaped leaves and a compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms, which can persist for several weeks during the growing season.
