Aconitum novoaxillare

Aconitum novoaxillare, commonly known as the New Zealand aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces showy, spicate inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
