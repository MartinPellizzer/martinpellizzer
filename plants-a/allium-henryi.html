Allium henryi

Allium henryi, commonly known as the Chinese leek or Henry's onion, is a perennial herb native to China.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, aromatic leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its bulbous stem and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses.

Allium henryi is also appreciated in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardiness.
