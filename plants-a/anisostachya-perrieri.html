Anisostachya perrieri

Anisostachya perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant is typically found in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It has a clumping growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening for its architectural form.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes many grasses and cereal crops.
