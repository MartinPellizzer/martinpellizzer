Angraecum eichlerianum var. curvicalcaratum

Angraecum eichlerianum var. curvicalcaratum, commonly known as the hooked calyx angraecum, is a rare and striking orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its elongated, twisted floral spurs that can extend several centimeters, a feature adapted for specialized pollination by moths. The plant produces large, showy flowers with intricate patterns that attract specific nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and unique morphology, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
