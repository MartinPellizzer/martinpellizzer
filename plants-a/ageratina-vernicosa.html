Ageratina vernicosa

Ageratina vernicosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf ageratum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant is recognized for its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive velvety texture. It produces small white to pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Ageratina vernicosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
