Astragalus rhizocephalus subsp. rhizocephalus

Astragalus rhizocephalus subsp.

rhizocephalus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments. Astragalus rhizocephalus subsp.

rhizocephalus plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
