Aeranthes setiformis

Aeranthes setiformis, commonly known as the feather-tongue orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the understory of rainforests. The plant features a slender, erect stem with numerous lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its most distinctive feature is its elongated, spurred flowers that resemble feathers, giving it its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of tropical flora.
