Allium schoenoprasum f. foliosum

Allium schoenoprasum f. foliosum, commonly known as garden chives, is a cultivated form of the common chive.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are aromatic and used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for herb gardens.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible leaves.
