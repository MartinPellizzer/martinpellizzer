Alopecurus pratensis

Alopecurus pratensis, commonly known as meadow foxtail, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist meadows, pastures, and wetlands, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences with small, greenish flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various insects and birds.
