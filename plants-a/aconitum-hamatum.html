Aconitum hamatum

Aconitum hamatum, commonly known as the European spotted aconite, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has large, palmately divided leaves that are typically green with a hairy texture. Aconitum hamatum contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and meadows.
