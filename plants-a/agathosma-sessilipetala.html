Agathosma sessilipetala

Agathosma sessilipetala, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features compact, aromatic foliage with small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and fragrant properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
