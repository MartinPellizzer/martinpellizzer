Angelica rivulorum

Angelica rivulorum, commonly known as the stream angelica, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland areas. The plant features large, compound leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length, with a distinctive celery-like aroma. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbrella-like inflorescences, producing a yellowish-green color.

Angelica rivulorum is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
