Acacia subporosa var. linearis

Acacia subporosa var. linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are typically green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
