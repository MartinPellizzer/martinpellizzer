Astragalus lentiginosus var. lentiginosus

Astragalus lentiginosus var. lentiginosus, commonly known as the spotted milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow flowers in late spring. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
