Adromischus marianiae f. alveolatus

Adromischus marianiae f. alveolatus, commonly known as the "Honeycomb Adromischus," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that form a rosette shape, often with a waxy coating. The leaves are typically green with a bluish or purplish tint, and they have a unique honeycomb-like texture on the surface. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months.

This plant is well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant due to its compact size and low water requirements.
