Avena radula

Avena radula, commonly known as the slender oat, is a species of grass native to the northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds that are often used in traditional herbal remedies. Avena radula thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wet meadows and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
