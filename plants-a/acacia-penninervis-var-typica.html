Acacia penninervis var. typica

Acacia penninervis var. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, linear leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense spikes. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in drought conditions.

The wood is hard and durable, making it useful for various practical applications.
