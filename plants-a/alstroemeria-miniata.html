Alstroemeria miniata

Alstroemeria miniata, commonly known as the dwarf alstroemeria, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with contrasting dark veins. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are dark green and slightly wavy in texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its vibrant blooms and long-lasting flowers.
