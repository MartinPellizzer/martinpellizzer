Aspalathus jacobaea

Aspalathus jacobaea, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-white flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure. It is often found in rocky or sandy environments.

Aspalathus jacobaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
