Ampelopsis rubricaulis

Ampelopsis rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed grapevine, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems that turn reddish in color as the plant matures. The leaves are simple, alternate, and typically have a lobed or heart-shaped outline. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by clusters of small, dark blue berries.

This plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and unique stem color.
