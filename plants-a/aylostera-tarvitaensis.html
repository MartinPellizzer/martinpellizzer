Aylostera tarvitaensis

Aylostera tarvitaensis, commonly known as the Tarvita aster, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are usually blue or violet, with ray florets surrounding a central disc of tubular flowers.

Aylostera tarvitaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
