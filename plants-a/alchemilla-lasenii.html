Alchemilla lasenii

Alchemilla lasenii, commonly known as the mountain lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported healing properties.

Alchemilla lasenii thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.
