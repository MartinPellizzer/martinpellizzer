Acacia riceana var. axillaris

Acacia riceana var. axillaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. It is adapted to thrive in poor soils and can tolerate drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

The wood of Acacia riceana var. axillaris is sometimes used locally for fuel or minor construction purposes.
