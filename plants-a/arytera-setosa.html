Arytera setosa

Arytera setosa, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in high-altitude regions. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Arytera setosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
