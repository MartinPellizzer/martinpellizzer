Astragalus huninensis

Astragalus huninensis, commonly known as the Hunin's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation across rugged terrains.

Astragalus huninensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
