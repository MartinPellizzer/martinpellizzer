Aconitum anthora var. gilvum

Aconitum anthora var. gilvum, commonly known as the golden alpine aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely leafed stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The flowers are blue to purple in color, with a distinctive spurred petal that aids in pollination by insects. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous environments.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
