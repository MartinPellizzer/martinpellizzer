Ampelopsis pinnata

Ampelopsis pinnata, commonly known as the compound leaf grapevine, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged with elongated leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, grape-like berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Ampelopsis pinnata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden settings.
