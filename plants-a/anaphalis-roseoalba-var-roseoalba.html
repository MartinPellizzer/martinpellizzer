Anaphalis roseoalba var. roseoalba

Anaphalis roseoalba var. roseoalba, commonly known as the rose-white cudweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forested areas or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a cluster of small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
