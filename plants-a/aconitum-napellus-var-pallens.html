Aconitum napellus var. pallens

Aconitum napellus var. pallens, commonly known as the pale monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant features palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland edges and meadows, and it is known for its toxic properties due to the presence of alkaloids.

Despite its toxicity, it is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty.
