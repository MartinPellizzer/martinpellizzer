Adiantum kohautianum

Adiantum kohautianum, commonly known as the Kohaut's maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble lace. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories or rocky crevices. Its leaves are bi-lobed and have a distinctive, feathery appearance, with a pale, often silvery underside.

Adiantum kohautianum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.
