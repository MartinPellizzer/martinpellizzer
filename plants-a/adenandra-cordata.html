Adenandra cordata

Adenandra cordata, commonly known as the red-stemmed adenandra, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features simple, elliptical leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense inflorescences, which are often borne on the stems.

The plant is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
