Acer fabri var. rubrocarpum

Acer fabri var. rubrocarpum, commonly known as the red-veined maple, is a deciduous tree characterized by its striking foliage.

Its leaves are typically palmately lobed with a distinctive red or orange vein pattern that becomes more pronounced in autumn. The tree produces small, red samaras that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant seasonal color changes.
