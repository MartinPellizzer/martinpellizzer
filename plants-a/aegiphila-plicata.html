Aegiphila plicata

Aegiphila plicata, commonly known as the zigzag mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive zigzagging stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Aegiphila plicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
