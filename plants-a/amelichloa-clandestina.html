Amelichloa clandestina

Amelichloa clandestina, commonly known as the ghost grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and a unique ability to thrive in wet, swampy environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often described as appearing almost ethereal due to their color and texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

Amelichloa clandestina is often found in wetlands and is an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
