Acacia chalkeri

Acacia chalkeri, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom throughout the dry season. The plant produces flat, bean-like pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Acacia chalkeri is adapted to survive in harsh, low-nutrient soils and can tolerate high temperatures and drought conditions.

Its leaves are typically small and feathery, reducing water loss through transpiration.
