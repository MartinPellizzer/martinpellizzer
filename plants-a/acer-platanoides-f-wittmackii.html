Acer platanoides f. wittmackii

Acer platanoides f. wittmackii, commonly known as the Wittmackii maple, is a rare and cultivar form of the Norway maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that are typically lighter green in color compared to the parent species. The plant is deciduous, shedding its foliage in the autumn, and it produces small, red to orange samaras in the spring. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage.

This cultivar is valued for its unique leaf shape and adaptability to various soil conditions.
