Andrographis glandulosa

Andrographis glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular andrographis, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Its leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
