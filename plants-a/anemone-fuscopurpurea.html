Anemone fuscopurpurea

Anemone fuscopurpurea, commonly known as the purple-fuscous anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in early spring, often with a dark purple center. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and basal leaves that are palmately lobed with hairy surfaces. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to rock gardens and woodland settings.
