Aira lendigera var. mutica

Aira lendigera var. mutica, commonly known as the hairy air grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to greenish flowers. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its fibrous root system and drought tolerance make it adaptable to various soil types.
