Arundinaria hidaensis

Arundinaria hidaensis, commonly known as the Hida bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, typical of the grass family. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Arundinaria hidaensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and hardiness.
