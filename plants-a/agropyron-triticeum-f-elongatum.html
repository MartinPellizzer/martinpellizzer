Agropyron triticeum f. elongatum

Agropyron triticeum f. elongatum, commonly known as the elongated wheatgrass, is a variant of the wheatgrass species characterized by its elongated leaves and robust growth habit.

It is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of Asia and North America, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

This form is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
