Allium monadelphum var. atrosanguineum

Allium monadelphum var. atrosanguineum, commonly known as the purple garlic.

This plant is a bulbous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are long, grass-like, and often bluish-green in color.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in well-drained soil.
