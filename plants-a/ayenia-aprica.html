Ayenia aprica

Ayenia aprica, commonly known as the yellow-flowered ayenia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, growing in dense tufts.

Ayenia aprica is often found in moist, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
