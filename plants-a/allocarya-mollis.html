Allocarya mollis

Allocarya mollis, commonly known as the softleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features palmate leaves with lobed segments and produces showy, pink to purple flowers in the spring. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and stems that are often covered in fine hairs.

Allocarya mollis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and bloom.
