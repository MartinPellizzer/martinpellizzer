Arisaema sazensoo f. viride

Arisaema sazensoo f. viride, commonly known as the green-flowered arrowhead, is a variant of the Arisaema genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves and a spadix surrounded by a pale green spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or mountainous regions. Its botanical features include a short rhizome and a showy inflorescence that attracts pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it requires specific conditions to thrive.
