Aglaia longifolia

Aglaia longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered aglaia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Magnoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and it is often found in lowland rainforests.

Aglaia longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
