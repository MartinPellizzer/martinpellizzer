Astragalus crassicarpus var. crassicarpus

Astragalus crassicarpus var. crassicarpus, commonly known as the broad-fruit milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically grows in dry, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod that becomes thick and fleshy when mature, containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinators.
