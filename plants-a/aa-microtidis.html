Aa microtidis

Aa microtidis, commonly known as the Hawaiian ironwood, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant is known for its hard, durable wood, which has been historically used by Native Hawaiians for tools and construction. Aa microtidis thrives in coastal and lowland areas, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
