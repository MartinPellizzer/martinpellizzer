Aconitum napellus subsp. compactum

Aconitum napellus subsp.

compactum, commonly known as the European garden aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August.

The plant has a tall, slender stem with palmately compound leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
