Amaracarpus trichocalyx

Amaracarpus trichocalyx, commonly known as the silky flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery leaves and showy, flame-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various soil types.
