Aster puniceus

Aster puniceus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking purple or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with narrow, gray-green leaves. Its conical flower heads are composed of many small, tubular florets surrounded by bracts.

Aster puniceus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural habitats.
