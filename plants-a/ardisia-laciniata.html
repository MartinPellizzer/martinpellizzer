Ardisia laciniata

Ardisia laciniata, commonly known as the serrated ardisia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant features opposite, elliptic leaves with serrated edges, giving it its common name. Its small, white flowers are clustered in umbels and are followed by bright red, fleshy berries.

The berries are an important food source for various birds and small mammals.
