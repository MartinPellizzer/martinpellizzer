Anisotes macrophyllus

Anisotes macrophyllus, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a tropical aquatic plant characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length.

It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and marshes. The plant has long, slender stems that float on the water surface, with roots submerged in the substrate. Its leaves are typically a deep green color and have a waxy texture, which helps reduce water loss.

Anisotes macrophyllus is also known for its ability to provide habitat and shelter for various aquatic organisms.
