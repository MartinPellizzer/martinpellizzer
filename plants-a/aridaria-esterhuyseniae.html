Aridaria esterhuyseniae

Aridaria esterhuyseniae, commonly known as the Esterhuysenia grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
