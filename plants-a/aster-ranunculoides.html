Aster ranunculoides

Aster ranunculoides, commonly known as the meadow aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in open meadows, prairies, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from white to purple.

Aster ranunculoides is an important nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
