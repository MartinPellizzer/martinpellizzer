Amphicarpaea africana

Amphicarpaea africana, commonly known as the African amphicarp, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its unique ability to produce two types of fruits: one above ground and one below ground. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers.

Its underground fruits, called cleistogamous fruits, are self-pollinated and contribute to its reproductive success in various soil conditions.
