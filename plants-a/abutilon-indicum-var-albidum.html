Abutilon indicum var. albidum

Abutilon indicum var. albidum, commonly known as the white velvet bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and clusters of small, white, lantern-shaped flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a visually striking yet low-maintenance plant.
