Alysicarpus violaceus var. pilosus

Alysicarpus violaceus var. pilosus, commonly known as purple hairy sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and also hairy, with a distinctive purple tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, hairy pods that contain several seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
