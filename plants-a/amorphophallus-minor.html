Amorphophallus minor

Amorphophallus minor, commonly known as the little giant arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which includes plants like calla lilies and peace lilies. The plant produces a large, showy inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. Its underground corm is used as a starchy food source in some regions.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appearance and its edible tuberous roots.
