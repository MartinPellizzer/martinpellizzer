Aristida rosei

Aristida rosei, commonly known as the rose hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its delicate, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, adding a subtle ornamental value. Its fine, silver-green foliage makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Aristida rosei is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
