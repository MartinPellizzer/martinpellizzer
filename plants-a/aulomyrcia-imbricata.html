Aulomyrcia imbricata

Aulomyrcia imbricata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It has simple, opposite, elliptic leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruit is a small, red, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and salt spray.
