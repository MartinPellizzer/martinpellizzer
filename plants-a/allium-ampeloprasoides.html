Allium ampeloprasoides

Allium ampeloprasoides, commonly known as the mountain garlic or Armenian garlic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, pale purple to white flowers. The plant's bulbs are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice due to their pungent aroma. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium ampeloprasoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in herbal practices.
