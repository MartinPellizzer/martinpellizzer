Astragalus rumelicus

Astragalus rumelicus, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has small, bean-shaped seeds that are often dispersed by wind.

Astragalus rumelicus is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its potential medicinal properties.
