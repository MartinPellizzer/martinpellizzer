Anthurium acaule var. brevipes

Anthurium acaule var. brevipes, commonly known as the dwarf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces spadices, which are spathes that enclose the true flowers, though these are not showy and are often overlooked. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
