Alchemilla versipiloides

Alchemilla versipiloides, commonly known as the velvet cinquefoil, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
