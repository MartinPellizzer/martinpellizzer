Asplenium ruta-muraria proles multicaule

Asplenium ruta-muraria proles multicaule, commonly known as the common rock spleenwort, is a fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a small, perennial plant that typically grows in rocky or shaded environments, often on walls or stones. The plant has a compact, upright growth habit with dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are usually divided into several segments. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

This species is widely distributed across temperate regions and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
