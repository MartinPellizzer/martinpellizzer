Adenophora polyantha var. glabricalyx

Adenophora polyantha var. glabricalyx, commonly known as the glabrous bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in summer. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, though the variety 'glabricalyx' is distinguished by its smooth, hairless stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
