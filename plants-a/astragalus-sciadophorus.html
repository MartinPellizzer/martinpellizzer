Astragalus sciadophorus

Astragalus sciadophorus, commonly known as the shade-plant milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
