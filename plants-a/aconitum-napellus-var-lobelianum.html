Aconitum napellus var. lobelianum

Aconitum napellus var. lobelianum, commonly known as the Alpine monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking, hooded blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a distinctive notched appearance, and it thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its toxic properties due to the presence of various alkaloids.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and alpine settings.
