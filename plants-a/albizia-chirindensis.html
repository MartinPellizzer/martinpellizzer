Albizia chirindensis

Albizia chirindensis, commonly known as the African silktree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to central and eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a silvery underside. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Albizia chirindensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.

It plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
