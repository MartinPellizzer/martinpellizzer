Artemisia officinalis

Artemisia officinalis, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage and small yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant contains volatile oils, including thujone, which contribute to its strong, bitter scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its medicinal and historical uses, it has been cultivated for centuries in traditional herbal practices.
