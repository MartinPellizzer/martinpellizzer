Aspalathus pinguis subsp. pinguis

Aspalathus pinguis subsp.

pinguis, commonly known as the yellow bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, yellow-green foliage and small, pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it contributes to local biodiversity.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
