Aconitum raddeanum

Aconitum raddeanum, commonly known as the Siberian monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces distinctive, helmet-shaped seed pods. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
