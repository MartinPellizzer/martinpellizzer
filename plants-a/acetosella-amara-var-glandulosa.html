Acetosella amara var. glandulosa

Acetosella amara var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy coating and small, yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is characterized by its glandular structures on the leaves and stems, which secrete a sticky substance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

The species is often found in nutrient-rich soils and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
