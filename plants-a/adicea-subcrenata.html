Adicea subcrenata

Adicea subcrenata, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, violet-blue flowers that open in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with scalloped edges and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. It is often found growing along dunes and in coastal scrub habitats.

Adicea subcrenata is adapted to salt-laden winds and is an important part of the local flora in its native range.
