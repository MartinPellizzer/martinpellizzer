Astragalus rockii

Astragalus rockii, commonly known as the Rocky Mountain milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is well adapted to harsh environments, often growing in rocky soils and dry landscapes.

Astragalus rockii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
