Anacamptis pyramidalis

Anacamptis pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers are spurred at the base, a distinctive feature of the Orchidaceae family.

Anacamptis pyramidalis is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
