Acalypha accedens var. viridis

Acalypha accedens var. viridis, commonly known as green velvet plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage with a velvety texture. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and often have a prominent midrib. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

This plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor conditions.
