Azalea periclymenoides var. carnea

Azalea periclymenoides var. carnea, commonly known as the pink azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range from pink to red in color, typically blooming in the spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of garden settings.
