Artemisia genipi subsp. nivalis

Artemisia genipi subsp.

nivalis, commonly known as the alpine wormwood, is a perennial herb native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and mountainous environments. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to cold climates with short growing seasons.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
