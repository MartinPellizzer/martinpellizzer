Agrostis rigidula var. kinabaluensis

Agrostis rigidula var. kinabaluensis, commonly known as the Kinabalu grass, is a species of grass native to the high-altitude regions of Borneo, particularly Mount Kinabalu.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in montane forests and alpine meadows. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

This variety is notable for its ecological significance in stabilizing soil in its mountainous habitat.
