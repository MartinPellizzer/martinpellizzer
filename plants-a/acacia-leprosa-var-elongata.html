Acacia leprosa var. elongata

Acacia leprosa var. elongata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically yellow-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late autumn to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
