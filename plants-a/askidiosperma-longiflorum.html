Askidiosperma longiflorum

Askidiosperma longiflorum, commonly known as the long-flowered sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and incense.

The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.
