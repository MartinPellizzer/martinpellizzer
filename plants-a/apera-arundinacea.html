Apera arundinacea

Apera arundinacea, commonly known as common couch grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit, with rhizomes that allow it to spread aggressively across various habitats. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny white or greenish flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in agricultural fields.

Apera arundinacea is known for its ability to thrive in a wide range of soil types and environmental conditions.
