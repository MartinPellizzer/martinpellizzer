Acalypha neomexicana var. jaliscana

Acalypha neomexicana var. jaliscana, commonly known as the Jalisco copperleaf, is a shrub native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It features small, copper-colored leaves that give it its common name, and it typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, papery bracts. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in desert scrub habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
