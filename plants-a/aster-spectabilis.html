Aster spectabilis

Aster spectabilis, commonly known as the New England aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, violet-blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its flowers are composed of ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural habitats.
