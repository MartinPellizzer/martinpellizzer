Alsinanthus serpyllifolius

Alsinanthus serpyllifolius, commonly known as common yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in flat-topped umbels. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Alsinanthus serpyllifolius is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
