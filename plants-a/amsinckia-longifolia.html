Amsinckia longifolia

Amsinckia longifolia, commonly known as the slender pinnate filaree, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Amsinckia longifolia is an important plant for pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
