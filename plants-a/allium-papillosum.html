Allium papillosum

Allium papillosum, commonly known as the bulbous garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its onion-like bulbs and grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are often used as a culinary herb, similar to chives or garlic.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
