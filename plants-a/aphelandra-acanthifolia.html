Aphelandra acanthifolia

Aphelandra acanthifolia, commonly known as the red-veined begonia, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, dark green leaves with bright red veins, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Aphelandra acanthifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique foliage and aesthetic appeal.
