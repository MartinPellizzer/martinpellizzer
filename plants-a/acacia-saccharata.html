Acacia saccharata

Acacia saccharata, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a small shrub native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically feathery and arranged in pairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to harsh desert conditions.

The plant is also valued for its role in local ecosystems and traditional uses.
