Amaryllis cuzcoensis

Amaryllis cuzcoensis, commonly known as the Cuzco amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features strap-like leaves that are typically broad and waxy, and it produces a tall stem with multiple blooms during the growing season.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
