Anemone scherfelii

Anemone scherfelii, commonly known as the Scherfel's anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often colorful flowers. The plant features a basal rosette of lobed, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
