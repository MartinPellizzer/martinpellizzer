Amomum validum

Amomum validum, commonly known as Chinese cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by dark blue or black berries. Its rhizomes and fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Amomum validum is valued for its aromatic properties and is often cultivated for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
