Astragalus minor var. curtiflorus

Astragalus minor var. curtiflorus, commonly known as the narrow-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators.
