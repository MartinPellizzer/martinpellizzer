Aster praealtus var. angustior

Aster praealtus var. angustior, commonly known as the narrowleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its drought tolerance.

This variety is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the typical form of Aster praealtus.
