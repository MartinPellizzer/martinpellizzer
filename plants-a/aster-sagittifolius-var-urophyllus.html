Aster sagittifolius var. urophyllus

Aster sagittifolius var. urophyllus, commonly known as the showy aster, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring narrow, arrow-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its showy purple to blue flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The variety urophyllus is distinguished by its larger, more showy flower heads compared to the typical form.
