Amaranthus albus var. monosepalus

Amaranthus albus var. monosepalus, commonly known as the white amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in dense clusters. The plant produces small, triangular seeds that are often used as a grain or for ornamental purposes. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable candidate for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
