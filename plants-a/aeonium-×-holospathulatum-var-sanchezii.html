Aeonium × holospathulatum var. sanchezii

Aeonium × holospathulatum var. sanchezii, commonly known as the Sanche's Aeonium, is a hybrid succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped growth form with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with red or purple hues at the edges. The plant produces striking clusters of yellow flowers during the spring or early summer. Its leaves are densely packed, providing a dense, ornamental appearance that makes it popular in rock gardens and as a container plant.

It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
