Acalypha insulana var. insulana

Acalypha insulana var. insulana, commonly known as the insular acalypha, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy bracts that are often red or purple in color, resembling flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with smooth, dark green leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some tropical gardens.
