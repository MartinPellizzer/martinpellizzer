Aspalathus usnoides

Aspalathus usnoides, commonly known as the sweet thorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets, and it produces small, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Aspalathus usnoides is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
