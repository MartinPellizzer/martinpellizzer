Adesmia hedysaroides

Adesmia hedysaroides, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring upright stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to thrive in poor, sandy soils with minimal water.

Adesmia hedysaroides plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
