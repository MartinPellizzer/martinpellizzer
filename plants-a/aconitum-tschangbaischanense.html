Aconitum tschangbaischanense

Aconitum tschangbaischanense, commonly known as the Tsangbaishan aconite, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments, and are covered in a dense layer of hairs.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it a dangerous species to handle without proper precautions.
