Arcyphyllum caribaeum

Arcyphyllum caribaeum, commonly known as the Caribbean sage, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically purple or violet, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom during the dry season.

Arcyphyllum caribaeum is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a resilient plant in tropical and subtropical environments.
