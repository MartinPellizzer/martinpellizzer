Antennaria chilensis var. magellanica

Antennaria chilensis var. magellanica, commonly known as the Magellanic sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, adapted to withstand the harsh, windy conditions of its habitat.

This variety is often found in sandy or rocky soils in Patagonia and surrounding areas.
