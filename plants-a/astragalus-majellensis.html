Astragalus majellensis

Astragalus majellensis, commonly known as the Jordan Valley milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has small, usually yellow or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

Astragalus majellensis is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
