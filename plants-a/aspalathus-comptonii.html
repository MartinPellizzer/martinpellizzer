Aspalathus comptonii

Aspalathus comptonii, commonly known as Compton's bushveld pea, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded foliage and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, leathery pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
