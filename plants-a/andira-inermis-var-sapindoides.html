Andira inermis var. sapindoides

Andira inermis var. sapindoides, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and pinnately arranged, and its showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, yellow fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
