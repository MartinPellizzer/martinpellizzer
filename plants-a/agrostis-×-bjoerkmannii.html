Agrostis × bjoerkmannii

Agrostis × bjoerkmannii, commonly known as the Bjoerkmann's bentgrass, is a hybrid grass species that results from the cross between Agrostis capillaris and Agrostis stolonifera.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically forms dense, low-growing turf, making it suitable for specialized horticultural applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in temperate regions.

Its botanical features include compact inflorescences and small, spike-like flowers.
