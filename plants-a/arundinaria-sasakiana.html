Arundinaria sasakiana

Arundinaria sasakiana, commonly known as the Sasak bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Arundinaria sasakiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional crafts and construction.
