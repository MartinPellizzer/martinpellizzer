Astragalus persicus

Astragalus persicus, commonly known as Persian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant has deep taproots that allow it to survive in dry conditions, and it plays a role in soil stabilization.

Astragalus persicus is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies for its purported health benefits.
