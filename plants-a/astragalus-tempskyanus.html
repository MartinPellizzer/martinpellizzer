Astragalus tempskyanus

Astragalus tempskyanus, commonly known as the Tempskian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its seed pods contain small, hard seeds that are often dispersed by wind.

Astragalus tempskyanus is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native range.
