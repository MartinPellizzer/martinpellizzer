Acacia guaiaguilensis

Acacia guaiaguilensis, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly found in Argentina, Chile, and Peru.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are typically yellow-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Acacia guaiaguilensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.
