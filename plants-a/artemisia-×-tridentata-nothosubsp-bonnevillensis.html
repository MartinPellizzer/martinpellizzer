Artemisia × tridentata nothosubsp. bonnevillensis

Artemisia × tridentata nothosubsp.

bonnevillensis, commonly known as the sagebrush, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-green foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often on rocky soils. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh conditions.

Its leaves contain volatile oils, which contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties.
