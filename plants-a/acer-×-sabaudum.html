Acer × sabaudum

Acer × sabaudum, commonly known as the Sabaudian maple, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Acer saccharum (sugar maple) and Acer pseudoplatanus (common maple).

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters, featuring a broad, rounded crown. The leaves are palmately lobed, with three to five lobes, and turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
