Aclisia

Aclisia, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It includes several species, most of which are native to Africa, particularly South Africa. The plants are typically shrubs or subshrubs, characterized by their silver-green foliage and showy, often violet or blue flowers. Aclisia species are known for their toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.

They are often found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
