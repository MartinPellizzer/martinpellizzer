Asperula wimmeriana

Asperula wimmeriana, commonly known as Wimmer's woodruff, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red berries in autumn. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that provide year-round foliage. It is adapted to cool, moist climates and thrives in partial shade.

Asperula wimmeriana is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
