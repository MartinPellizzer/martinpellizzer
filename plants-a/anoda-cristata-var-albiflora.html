Anoda cristata var. albiflora

Anoda cristata var. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery foliage. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
