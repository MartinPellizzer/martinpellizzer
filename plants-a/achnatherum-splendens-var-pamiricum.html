Achnatherum splendens var. pamiricum

Achnatherum splendens var. pamiricum, commonly known as the Pamir feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and delicate, feathery inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in mountainous regions.
