Arabis hirsuta subsp. gerardi

Arabis hirsuta subsp.

gerardi, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, linear leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered in racemes.

Native to rocky and limestone habitats in Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant is perennial, often found in alpine or subalpine regions, and is known for its frost-resistant nature.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and hardy member of the mustard family.
