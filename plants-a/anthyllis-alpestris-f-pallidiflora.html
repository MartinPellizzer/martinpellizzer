Anthyllis alpestris f. pallidiflora

Anthyllis alpestris f. pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered broom, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptation to harsh, cold climates and its role in stabilizing soil in mountainous regions.
