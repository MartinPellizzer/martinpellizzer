Abronia fendleri

Abronia fendleri, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to violet, typically blooming in spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. It has a compact, low-growing habit with narrow, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

Abronia fendleri is adapted to survive in harsh desert conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
