Alpinia adana

Alpinia adana, commonly known as the Adana ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant features narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to white flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Alpinia adana thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued both for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
