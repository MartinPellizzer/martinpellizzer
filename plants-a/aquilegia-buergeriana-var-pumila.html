Aquilegia buergeriana var. pumila

Aquilegia buergeriana var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf Chinese columbine, is a compact perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features delicate, spurred petals that range in color from blue to violet, typical of the genus Aquilegia. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with finely toothed margins, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
