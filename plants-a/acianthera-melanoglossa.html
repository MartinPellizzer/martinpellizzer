Acianthera melanoglossa

Acianthera melanoglossa, commonly known as the black-lipped orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its dark, velvety petals and a distinctive, elongated lip that resembles a tongue. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient absorption. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a mix of deep purples and blacks, which contribute to its dramatic appearance.

Due to habitat destruction and limited distribution, it is considered an endangered species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
