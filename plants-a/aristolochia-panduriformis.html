Aristolochia panduriformis

Aristolochia panduriformis, commonly known as the Indian birthwort, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and its unique, tubular flowers that resemble a horn or a pouch. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex chemical composition, including various alkaloids. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along riverbanks.

However, due to its toxic properties, it is not recommended for cultivation or consumption.
