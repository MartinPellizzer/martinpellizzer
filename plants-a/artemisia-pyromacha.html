Artemisia pyromacha

Artemisia pyromacha, commonly known as the flame sage, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and aromatic, often fuzzy leaves that emit a strong, medicinal scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Artemisia pyromacha is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

It has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
