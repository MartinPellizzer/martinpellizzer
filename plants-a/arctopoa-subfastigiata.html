Arctopoa subfastigiata

Arctopoa subfastigiata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some areas.
