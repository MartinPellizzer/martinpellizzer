Arundinella pilaxilis

Arundinella pilaxilis, commonly known as the silky bamboo, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery inflorescences. The plant produces small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, silky texture that gives it its common name.

Arundinella pilaxilis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to various climates.
