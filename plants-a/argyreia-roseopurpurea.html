Argyreia roseopurpurea

Argyreia roseopurpurea, commonly known as the purple passion vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple and pink flowers, which are often adorned with a white or pale pink corona. The plant produces small, round, red berries that contain seeds, and it has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its ability to climb using tendrils, making it a popular choice for trellises and vertical gardening.
