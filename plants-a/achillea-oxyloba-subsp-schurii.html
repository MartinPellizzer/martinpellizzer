Achillea oxyloba subsp. schurii

Achillea oxyloba subsp.

schurii, commonly known as Schur's yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow composite flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, with a silvery-gray underside.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
