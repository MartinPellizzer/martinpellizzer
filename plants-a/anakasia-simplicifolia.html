Anakasia simplicifolia

Anakasia simplicifolia, commonly known as the simple-leaved anakasia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
