Astragalus interpositus

Astragalus interpositus, commonly known as the interposed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to pale green flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Astragalus interpositus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and open habitats.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local pollinator populations.
