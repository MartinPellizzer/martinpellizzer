Armeniaca nana

Armeniaca nana, commonly known as the dwarf Chinese date, is a small shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, date-like fruits in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

Armeniaca nana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
