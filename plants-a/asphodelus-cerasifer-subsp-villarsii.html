Asphodelus cerasifer subsp. villarsii

Asphodelus cerasifer subsp.

villarsii, commonly known as the violet-flowered asphodel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the family Asphodelaceae and is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves and showy, violet or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand dry conditions.
