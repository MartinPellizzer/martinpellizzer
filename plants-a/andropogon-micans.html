Andropogon micans

Andropogon micans, commonly known as the silver bluestem, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and slender, erect growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, three-lobed flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Andropogon micans is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
