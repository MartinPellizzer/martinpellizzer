Agrostis × kamtschatica

Agrostis × kamtschatica, commonly known as the Kamchatka bentgrass, is a hybrid grass species native to the northern regions of the Pacific coast of Russia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, tufted habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Agrostis × kamtschatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its fine texture and ability to form dense, attractive turf.
