Adenandra tetragona

Adenandra tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled adenandra, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems with four distinct angular ridges, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a rough texture.

Adenandra tetragona is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
