Adonis dentata subsp. persica

Adonis dentata subsp.

persica, commonly known as Persian saffron crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus and parts of Turkey. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces striking red or orange flowers with prominent stamens.

The plant is characterized by its corm-like underground storage structures, which help it survive in its native environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
