Alyssum nuttallii

Alyssum nuttallii, commonly known as Nuttall's alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants.

Alyssum nuttallii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
