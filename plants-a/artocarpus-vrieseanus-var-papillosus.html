Artocarpus vrieseanus var. papillosus

Artocarpus vrieseanus var. papillosus, commonly known as the hairy fig or Vriesea fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The species is part of the Moraceae family, which includes other well-known plants like the common fig and breadfruit.
