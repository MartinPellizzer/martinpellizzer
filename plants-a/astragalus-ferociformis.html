Astragalus ferociformis

Astragalus ferociformis, commonly known as the devil's needle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Astragalus ferociformis is adapted to arid and rocky environments, often found in sagebrush habitats.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native range.
