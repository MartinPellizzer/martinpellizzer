Astragalus labradoricus var. occidentalis

Astragalus labradoricus var. occidentalis, commonly known as Labrador milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as tundra, barrens, and dry meadows. The plant features slender stems with alternate, pinnately compound leaves that have small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are yellowish-green to pale purple, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is adapted to cold climates and is often found in arctic and subarctic environments.
