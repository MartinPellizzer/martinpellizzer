Amaryllis × grandiflora

Amaryllis × grandiflora, commonly known as the Easter lily, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis paradisiaca.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with a slight pinkish tinge, which bloom in the spring. The plant is native to South Africa and is cultivated for its ornamental value. It produces a tall, erect stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height.

The leaves are strap-like, broad, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.
