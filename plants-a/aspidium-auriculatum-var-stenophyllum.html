Aspidium auriculatum var. stenophyllum

Aspidium auriculatum var. stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wood fern, is a variant of the common wood fern, characterized by its narrow, elongated fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping stems that produce both roots and new shoots. Its fronds are usually bipinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance that contributes to its ornamental value.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
