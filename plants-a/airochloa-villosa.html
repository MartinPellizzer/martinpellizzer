Airochloa villosa

Airochloa villosa, commonly known as the velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and silky, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive velvety appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering spikes are slender and often appear in late spring to early summer.

Airochloa villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for erosion control and ground cover.
