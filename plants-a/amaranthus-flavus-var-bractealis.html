Amaranthus flavus var. bractealis

Amaranthus flavus var. bractealis, commonly known as the golden amaranth, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and upright, branching stems that often bear bracts resembling leaves. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-angled, smooth-seeded capsules. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This species is valued in horticulture for its striking color and adaptability to various growing conditions.
