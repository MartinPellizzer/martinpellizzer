Agastache scrophulariifolia var. mollis

Agastache scrophulariifolia var. mollis, commonly known as the softleaf hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces spikes of pale lavender to violet flowers, which bloom in mid to late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic leaves and showy flowers make it a valuable addition to pollinator gardens.
