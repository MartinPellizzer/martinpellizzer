Anthemis mysorensis

Anthemis mysorensis, commonly known as the Mysore chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in regions such as Karnataka and Tamil Nadu. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

The plant's botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and medicinal research.
