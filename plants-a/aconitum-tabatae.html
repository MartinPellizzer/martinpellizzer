Aconitum tabatae

Aconitum tabatae, commonly known as Tabata's monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive helmet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has palmately compound leaves with a deeply lobed structure, and its roots are tuberous, which help it survive in harsh alpine environments.

Aconitum tabatae contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested, though it is primarily valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
