Androcymbium gramineum var. palaestinum

Androcymbium gramineum var. palaestinum, commonly known as the Palestine grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It is often found in open habitats such as fields, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Its adaptability to arid conditions makes it an important component of local vegetation in its native range.
