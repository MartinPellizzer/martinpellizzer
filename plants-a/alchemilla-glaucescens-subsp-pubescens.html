Alchemilla glaucescens subsp. pubescens

Alchemilla glaucescens subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It features basal rosettes of lanceolate, gray-green leaves that are softly hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces upright flowering stems bearing small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Its foliage and flowers are used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported astringent and healing properties.
