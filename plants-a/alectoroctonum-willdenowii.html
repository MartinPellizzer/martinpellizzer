Alectoroctonum willdenowii

Alectoroctonum willdenowii, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
