Acacia nigricans var. subracemosa

Acacia nigricans var. subracemosa, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemose inflorescences, which are followed by flat, leathery pods containing seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
