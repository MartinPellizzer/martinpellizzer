Achillea oxyloba subsp. barrelieri

Achillea oxyloba subsp.

barrelieri, commonly known as the Barrelier's yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and northern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens. Its aromatic foliage and long blooming period make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology and flowering habits compared to the main species.
