Antennaria russellii

Antennaria russellii, commonly known as Russell's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments. Its leaves are spoon-shaped and clustered at the base, while its flowers are usually pink or white.

Antennaria russellii is valued for its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil in harsh conditions.
