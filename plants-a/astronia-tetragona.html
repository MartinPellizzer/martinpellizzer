Astronia tetragona

Astronia tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled aster, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features opposite, four-angled leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom in the spring.

Astronia tetragona is often found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges.
