Agalmyla angustifolia

Agalmyla angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf agalmyla, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Agalmyla angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to Mediterranean climates.
