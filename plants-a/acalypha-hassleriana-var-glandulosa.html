Acalypha hassleriana var. glandulosa

Acalypha hassleriana var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular acalypha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage with deep green, almost metallic sheen. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are often hairy and have a glandular texture, which contributes to its unique appearance.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
