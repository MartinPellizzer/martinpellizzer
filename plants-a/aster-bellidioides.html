Aster bellidioides

Aster bellidioides, commonly known as the sand aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, producing showy purple to white flowers.

Aster bellidioides is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, showcasing its resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
