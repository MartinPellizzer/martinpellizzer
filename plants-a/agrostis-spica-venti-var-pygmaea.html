Agrostis spica-venti var. pygmaea

Agrostis spica-venti var. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf windgrass, is a low-growing grass species native to temperate regions.

It features fine, narrow leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are wind-dispersed. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and pastures. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for ornamental use in gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its resilient nature and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
