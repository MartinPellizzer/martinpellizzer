Asclepias verticillata var. leptophylla

Asclepias verticillata var. leptophylla, commonly known as the slenderleaf milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow in clusters, and its leaves are narrow and arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers with distinctive pink to reddish-orange hues, typical of the milkweed genus. It is a host plant for the monarch butterfly and other milkweed specialists, supporting vital pollinators and larvae.

Its fibrous roots and adaptability make it a resilient plant in various soil types and light conditions.
