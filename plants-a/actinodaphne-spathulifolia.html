Actinodaphne spathulifolia

Actinodaphne spathulifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark green, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by oval-shaped, woody fruits. Its bark is thick and aromatic, often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
