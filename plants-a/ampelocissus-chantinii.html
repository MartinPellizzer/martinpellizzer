Ampelocissus chantinii

Ampelocissus chantinii, commonly known as the African climbing fig, is a woody vine native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its climbing habit makes it suitable for use as a living fence or ornamental plant in gardens.
