Aglaia ridleyi

Aglaia ridleyi, commonly known as the white aglaia, is a tropical plant belonging to the Meliaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its ornamental value due to its fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
