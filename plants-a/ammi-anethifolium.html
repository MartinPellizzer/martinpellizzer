Ammi anethifolium

Ammi anethifolium, commonly known as bishop's weed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in umbrella-like clusters. The plant produces small, oval seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ammi anethifolium is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in herbal remedies for various ailments.
