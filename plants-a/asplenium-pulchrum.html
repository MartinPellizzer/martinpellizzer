Asplenium pulchrum

Asplenium pulchrum, commonly known as the pretty spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are often adorned with a distinctive silvery or pale green hue. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as rocky crevices or forest floors, where it can thrive in humid conditions. Its reproductive structures, including spore-bearing sorus, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

Asplenium pulchrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
