Anthurium gallardoae

Anthurium gallardoae, commonly known as the Gallo's anthurium, is a species of flowering plant native to the rainforests of Central America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces striking, colorful spathe that can range from red to orange, often with contrasting veins. Its leaves are typically large, glossy, and heart-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Anthurium gallardoae thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its aesthetic qualities in horticulture.
