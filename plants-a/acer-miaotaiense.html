Acer miaotaiense

Acer miaotaiense, commonly known as the Miaotai maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves with five lobes, which turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape tree.
