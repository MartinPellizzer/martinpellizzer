Astragalus arbelicus

Astragalus arbelicus, commonly known as the Arbelus milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus arbelicus is often found in rocky soils and is considered a valuable species for ecological studies due to its resilience and role in local ecosystems.
