Ardisia moonii var. serratifolia

Ardisia moonii var. serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated-leaved ardisia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves with serrated edges, which give it its common name. Its small, fragrant white flowers are followed by bright red berries that are edible but often used as ornamental features.

The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
