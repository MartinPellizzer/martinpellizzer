Annona volubilis

Annona volubilis, commonly known as the Mexican custard apple, is a tropical vine native to Central America and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces a fruit that is typically green when unripe and turns yellow or red when mature. The fruit has a creamy, custard-like texture and is often used in culinary and traditional medicinal applications.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
