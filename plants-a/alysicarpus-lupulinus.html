Alysicarpus lupulinus

Alysicarpus lupulinus, commonly known as the golden shower vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and clusters of yellow, pendulous flowers that resemble showers of golden rain. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds, and its leaves are typically trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
