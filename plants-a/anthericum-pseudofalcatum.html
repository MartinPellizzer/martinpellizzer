Anthericum pseudofalcatum

Anthericum pseudofalcatum, commonly known as the false lily of the valley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features grass-like leaves that are narrow and slightly curved, with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during late spring to early summer. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies in moist, shaded environments.

Anthericum pseudofalcatum is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
