Anisacantha anisacanthoides

Anisacantha anisacanthoides, commonly known as the silverleaf globemallow, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact growth habit with branching stems and small, ovate leaves covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a drought-tolerant species suitable for xeriscaping.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Malvaceae and is known for its ornamental value in arid landscapes.
