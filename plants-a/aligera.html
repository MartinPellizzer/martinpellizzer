Aligera

Aligera, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Aligera thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
