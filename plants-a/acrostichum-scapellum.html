Acrostichum scapellum

Acrostichum scapellum, commonly known as the dwarf hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for shaded or partially sunny environments.
