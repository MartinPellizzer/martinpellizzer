Alpinia diracodes

Alpinia diracodes, commonly known as the black ginger or velvet leaf, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender rhizome and striking dark purple to black leaves that are velvety to the touch. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by spadices that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Alpinia diracodes is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in various cultural and culinary practices.
