Acacia abyssinica subsp. calophylla

Acacia abyssinica subsp.

calophylla, commonly known as the Abyssinian acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Ethiopia and parts of Eritrea. It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets.

The plant produces yellow, spherical flower heads that are arranged in dense clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its hardy nature allows it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important component of local ecosystems.

The wood of this species is used for fuel and minor construction, while its thorny structure provides habitat for small animals.
