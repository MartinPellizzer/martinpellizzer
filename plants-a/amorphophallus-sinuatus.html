Amorphophallus sinuatus

Amorphophallus sinuatus, commonly known as the giant swamp arrowhead, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrowhead-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces a unique inflorescence that resembles a phallic structure, which is why it is also called the "corpse flower" in some regions. Its botanical features include a tuberous root system that allows it to survive in wetland environments.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in marshy areas along riverbanks.
