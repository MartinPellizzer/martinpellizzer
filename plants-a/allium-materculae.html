Allium materculae

Allium materculae, commonly known as the bulbous garlic chive, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-like, grass-like leaves and small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures similar to garlic, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its mild garlic scent and edible qualities.
