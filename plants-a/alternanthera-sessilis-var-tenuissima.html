Alternanthera sessilis var. tenuissima

Alternanthera sessilis var. tenuissima, commonly known as the narrow-leaf velvet plant, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant typically grows in wetland environments and prefers full sun to partial shade. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellow in color.

This variety is popular in aquatic gardens and as a foreground plant in water features due to its attractive foliage.
