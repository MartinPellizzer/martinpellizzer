Athyrium decurrenti-alatum f. platyphyllum

Athyrium decurrenti-alatum f. platyphyllum, commonly known as the broad-leafed lady fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive frond structure.

It features bipinnate leaves that are broad and flat, with a soft, feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its fronds are adorned with small, rounded pinnae that are arranged in a dense, elegant pattern.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
