Aristea bakeri

Aristea bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's aristea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing form. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and pink, often arranged in a dense cluster. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

Aristea bakeri is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
