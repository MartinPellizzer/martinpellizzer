Artemisia chamissoniana var. kuragiuskiana

Artemisia chamissoniana var. kuragiuskiana, commonly known as the Kuragiuskian wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, aromatic foliage with small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments.

This variety is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its distinctive scent and resilience.
