Adesmia mucronata

Adesmia mucronata, commonly known as the mucronate adesmia, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly found in countries like Argentina and Chile. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale purple. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, and it is adapted to grow in arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, which makes it valuable in ecological and agricultural contexts.
