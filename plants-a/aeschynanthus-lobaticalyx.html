Aeschynanthus lobaticalyx

Aeschynanthus lobaticalyx, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or physalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange berries enclosed in a papery calyx, which resembles a lantern. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The species is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants.
