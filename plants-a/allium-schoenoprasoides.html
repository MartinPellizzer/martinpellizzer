Allium schoenoprasoides

Allium schoenoprasoides, commonly known as the purple garlic or purple onion, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of lavender to purple flowers in the summer. The plant's bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor compared to other Allium species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flower color and aromatic foliage.
