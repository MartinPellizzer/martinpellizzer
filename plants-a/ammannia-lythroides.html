Ammannia lythroides

Ammannia lythroides, commonly known as redroot dayflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking red stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Ammannia lythroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
