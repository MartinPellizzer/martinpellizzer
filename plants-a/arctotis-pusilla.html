Arctotis pusilla

Arctotis pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf arctotis, is a small, flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 10 inches and features bright yellow to orange flowers with white ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its compact size and vibrant blooms, it is often used in ornamental horticulture.
