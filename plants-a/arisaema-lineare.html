Arisaema lineare

Arisaema lineare, commonly known as the straight-leaf arisaema, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence known as a spadix, enclosed by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and marshy areas. Its leaves are elongated and linear, giving it its common name, while its flowers are small and clustered around the spadix.

Arisaema lineare is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in some regions.
