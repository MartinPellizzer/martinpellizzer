Asarum heterotropoides var. seoulense

Asarum heterotropoides var. seoulense, commonly known as Seoul asarum, is a perennial herb native to the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, maroon-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

However, the plant contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
