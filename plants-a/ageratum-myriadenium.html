Ageratum myriadenium

Ageratum myriadenium, commonly known as the many-leaved ageratum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.

Ageratum myriadenium prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
