Acer hookeri

Acer hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 15 meters and has smooth, gray bark. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters before the leaves emerge in spring.

Acer hookeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its colorful foliage.
