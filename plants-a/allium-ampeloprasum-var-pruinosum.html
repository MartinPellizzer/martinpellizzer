Allium ampeloprasum var. pruinosum

Allium ampeloprasum var. pruinosum, commonly known as the purple onion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a powdery, waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by spherical, pale purple to pink bulbs. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, making them suitable for culinary use.

The plant is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
