Athamanta pisana

Athamanta pisana, commonly known as the Canary Island pine, is a species of conifer native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, woody cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the island's unique climate.

Athamanta pisana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
