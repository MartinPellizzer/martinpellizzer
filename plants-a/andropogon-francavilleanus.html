Andropogon francavilleanus

Andropogon francavilleanus, commonly known as the Frenchman's grass, is a tall perennial grass native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.
