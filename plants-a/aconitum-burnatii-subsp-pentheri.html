Aconitum burnatii subsp. pentheri

Aconitum burnatii subsp.

pentheri, commonly known as the Balkan aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height.

The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense racemes. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments, and are covered in a dense layer of silky hairs. Aconitum burnatii subsp.

pentheri contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant to be handled with care.
