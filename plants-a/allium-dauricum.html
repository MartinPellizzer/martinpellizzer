Allium dauricum

Allium dauricum, commonly known as Siberian leek, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Asia.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its bulbous base, which is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.

Allium dauricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
