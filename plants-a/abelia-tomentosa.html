Abelia tomentosa

Abelia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has dense, grayish-white pubescent foliage that gives it a soft, woolly appearance. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Abelia tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
