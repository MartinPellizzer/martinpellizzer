Acer mairei

Acer mairei, commonly known as the Maire maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by samaroid fruits that disperse its seeds. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for temperate climates.

Due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits, Acer mairei is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
