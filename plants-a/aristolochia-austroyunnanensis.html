Aristolochia austroyunnanensis

Aristolochia austroyunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan pipevine, is a climbing perennial plant native to China.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and has a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor. It is known for its toxic properties, containing aristolochic acids that can be harmful to humans and animals.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments in mountainous regions.
