Astragalus camptodontoides

Astragalus camptodontoides, commonly known as the bent-tooth milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus camptodontoides plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
