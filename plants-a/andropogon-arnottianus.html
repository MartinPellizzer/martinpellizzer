Andropogon arnottianus

Andropogon arnottianus, commonly known as Arnott's bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers in late summer. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its botanical structure is characteristic of the Poaceae family, with rhizomes and a fibrous root system.
