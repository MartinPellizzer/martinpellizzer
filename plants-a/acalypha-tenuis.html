Acalypha tenuis

Acalypha tenuis, commonly known as slenderleaf croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or yellowish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Acalypha tenuis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
