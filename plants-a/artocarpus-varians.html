Artocarpus varians

Artocarpus varians, commonly known as the variable breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive fruit. The fruit, which is typically green when mature, turns yellow or orange as it ripens and develops a rough, bumpy surface. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and is often found in lowland rainforests.

It is valued for its edible fruit and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
