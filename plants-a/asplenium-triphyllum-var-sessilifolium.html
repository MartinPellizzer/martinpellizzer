Asplenium triphyllum var. sessilifolium

Asplenium triphyllum var. sessilifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a variant of the triphyllum maidenhair fern.

It is a terrestrial fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern. The leaves are usually narrow and have a distinctive, feathery appearance, with a pale green coloration. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This variant is notable for its unique leaf morphology and is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities.
