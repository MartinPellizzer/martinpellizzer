Andryala pinnatifida subsp. antonii

Andryala pinnatifida subsp.

antonii, commonly known as the Florida pellitory, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are pinnately divided, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, white to pale green flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swamps, often growing along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
