Alloplectus hirtellus f. umbrophilus

Alloplectus hirtellus f. umbrophilus, commonly known as the hairy alpine bellflower, is a variant of the alpine bellflower species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. This subspecies typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its flowers are bell-shaped and often display shades of blue or purple.

The plant is adapted to high-altitude regions and is found in the mountainous areas of the western United States.
