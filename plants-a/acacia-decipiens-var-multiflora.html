Acacia decipiens var. multiflora

Acacia decipiens var. multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered wattle, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and its clusters of small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant produces flattened, leathery pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
