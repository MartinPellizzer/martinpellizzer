Astragalus naftabensis

Astragalus naftabensis, commonly known as the Naftab's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain seeds.

Astragalus naftabensis is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
