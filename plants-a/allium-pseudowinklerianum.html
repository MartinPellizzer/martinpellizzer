Allium pseudowinklerianum

Allium pseudowinklerianum, commonly known as the Winkler's leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.

Allium pseudowinklerianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
