Ambrosia variabilis

Ambrosia variabilis, commonly known as the variable ambrosia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Ambrosiaceae family and is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, elongated fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Ambrosia variabilis is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
