Astragalus iranicus

Astragalus iranicus, commonly known as Persian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume structure and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant produces small, yellow to green flowers arranged in racemes, which are often clustered at the top of the stems. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus iranicus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing a deep root system for water absorption.
