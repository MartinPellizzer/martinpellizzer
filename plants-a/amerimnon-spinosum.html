Amerimnon spinosum

Amerimnon spinosum, commonly known as the spiny amaranth, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and spiny stems, which provide a natural defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from red to purple, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Amerimnon spinosum is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
