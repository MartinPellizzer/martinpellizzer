Agrostis spica-venti var. purpurea

Agrostis spica-venti var. purpurea, commonly known as purple windgrass, is a variant of the windgrass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing, perennial grass that typically thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and the flowers are usually pale green to purple in color.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to colonize areas with minimal care.
