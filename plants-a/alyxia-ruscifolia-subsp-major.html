Alyxia ruscifolia subsp. major

Alyxia ruscifolia subsp.

major, commonly known as the showy alyxia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is a shrub characterized by its opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
