Antirrhinum valentinum f. huteri

Antirrhinum valentinum f. huteri, commonly known as the Huter's Snapdragon, is a botanical variant of the snapdragon genus characterized by its distinctive floral morphology.

It features vibrant, tubular flowers that typically display a range of colors, including red, orange, and yellow, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly grown in gardens for its showy blooms.
