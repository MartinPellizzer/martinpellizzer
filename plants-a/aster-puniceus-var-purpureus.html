Aster puniceus var. purpureus

Aster puniceus var. purpureus, commonly known as the Purple Coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant features a tall, upright stem covered in narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. Its flowers consist of a purple-pink central cone surrounded by white to lavender petals.

This species is well adapted to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens and pollinator habitats.
