Agropyron cristatum subsp. mongolicum

Agropyron cristatum subsp.

mongolicum, commonly known as the Mongolian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often sparsely hairy leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in dry, sandy or loamy soils and is commonly found in steppe and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its role in stabilizing soils and as forage for livestock.
