Aconitum balfourii

Aconitum balfourii, commonly known as the Balfour's monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It features striking, hood-shaped blue-violet flowers that are typically borne on tall, sparsely branched stems. The plant has palmately lobed, dark green leaves with a distinctive veining pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Like other members of the genus Aconitum, it contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
