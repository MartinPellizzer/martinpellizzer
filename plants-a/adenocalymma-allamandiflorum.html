Adenocalymma allamandiflorum

Adenocalymma allamandiflorum, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
