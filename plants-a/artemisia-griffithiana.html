Artemisia griffithiana

Artemisia griffithiana, commonly known as Griffith's wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Artemisia griffithiana is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
