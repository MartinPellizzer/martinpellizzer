Andropogon nardus

Andropogon nardus, commonly known as sweet grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and distinctive, aromatic scent. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its flowering spikes are slender and produce small, inconspicuous flowers.

Sweet grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional crafts and ceremonies.
