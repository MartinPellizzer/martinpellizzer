Aloe barbara-jeppeae

Aloe barbara-jeppeae, commonly known as the Cape aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and fleshy, sword-shaped leaves that are covered in a layer of white wax. The plant produces striking orange to red flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
