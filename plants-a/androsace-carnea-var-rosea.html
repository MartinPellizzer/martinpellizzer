Androsace carnea var. rosea

Androsace carnea var. rosea, commonly known as the pink rock jasmine, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has a mat-forming habit with hairy, fleshy leaves adapted to cold and dry environments. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in rock gardens and alpine habitats.
