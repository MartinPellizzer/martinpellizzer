Acer saccharum f. glaucum

Acer saccharum f. glaucum, commonly known as the white maple, is a variant of the sugar maple characterized by its distinctive white or pale green undersides on its leaves.

This form exhibits a unique leaf morphology, with a broader, more rounded shape compared to the typical sugar maple. The bark is smooth and grayish, often developing shallow fissures as the tree matures. It is native to eastern North America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic foliage.
