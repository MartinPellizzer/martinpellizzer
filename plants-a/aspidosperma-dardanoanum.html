Aspidosperma dardanoanum

Aspidosperma dardanoanum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Cerbera odollam*, is a shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy pulp.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
