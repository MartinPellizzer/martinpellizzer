Astragalus pseudozagrosicus

Astragalus pseudozagrosicus, commonly known as the Zagros milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of western Asia, particularly in Iran and Turkey. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, compound leaves, and small, yellowish-pink flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

Astragalus pseudozagrosicus plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential medicinal properties.
