Arundinaria multifloscula

Arundinaria multifloscula, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or sweet bamboo, is a species of grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.

Arundinaria multifloscula is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
