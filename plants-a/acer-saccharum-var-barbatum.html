Acer saccharum var. barbatum

Acer saccharum var. barbatum, commonly known as the Canadian sugar maple, is a variant of the sugar maple native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds called samaras. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in forest ecosystems.
