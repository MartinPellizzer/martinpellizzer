Anthemis frutescens

Anthemis frutescens, commonly known as common tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are aromatic and have a strong, bitter scent due to the presence of compounds like thymol.

It is often found in dry, sunny grasslands and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
