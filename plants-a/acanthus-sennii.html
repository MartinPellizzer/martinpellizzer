Acanthus sennii

Acanthus sennii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acanthus, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in a silvery-blue waxy coating. The plant produces showy, spires of purple or violet flowers in late spring to early summer. Its foliage and floral display make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Acanthus sennii is also valued for its historical use in classical architecture and decorative arts.
