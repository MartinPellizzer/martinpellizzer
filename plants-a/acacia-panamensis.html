Acacia panamensis

Acacia panamensis, commonly known as the Panama acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often thorny stems and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in dry, tropical climates and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Acacia panamensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and animals.
