Actinella chrysanthemoides

Actinella chrysanthemoides, commonly known as the Chinese chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are usually alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are composed of ray florets and disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
