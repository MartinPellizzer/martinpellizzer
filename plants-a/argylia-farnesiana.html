Argylia farnesiana

Argylia farnesiana, commonly known as the sweet shrub, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Argylia farnesiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant scent.
