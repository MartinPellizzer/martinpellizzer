Artocarpus involucratus

Artocarpus involucratus, commonly known as the sand apple or Java apple, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, rough, spiky fruits that resemble a pineapple. The plant produces a fragrant, yellowish-green fruit with a tough outer husk and a sweet, aromatic pulp inside. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly rough texture.

The tree is often grown for its edible fruit and is also valued for its ornamental features.
