Aster brevilingulatus

Aster brevilingulatus, commonly known as the short-ligule aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces small, clustered flowers with yellow disk florets surrounded by white to pale blue ray florets.

Aster brevilingulatus is often found in wetland environments and is a valuable component of native plant ecosystems.
