Aegiphila hirsuta var. hirsuta

Aegiphila hirsuta var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy aegiphila, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Lamiaceae family.
