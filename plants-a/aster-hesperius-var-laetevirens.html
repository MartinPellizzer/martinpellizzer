Aster hesperius var. laetevirens

Aster hesperius var. laetevirens, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern and central United States.

It features showy, lavender-blue flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in moist to wet habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

The variety laetevirens is distinguished by its more intense blue coloration compared to the typical form.
