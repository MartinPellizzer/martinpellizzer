Aira cespitosa f. tenera

Aira cespitosa f. tenera, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a variant of the common bluegrass species.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, silky blades. The plant is native to North America and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and grassy areas. Its foliage is typically a vibrant blue-green color, which distinguishes it from other grasses.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.
