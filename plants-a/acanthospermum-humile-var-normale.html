Acanthospermum humile var. normale

Acanthospermum humile var. normale, commonly known as the small burrweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animal fur or clothing. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in roadside areas and dry grasslands.
