Amorpha fragrans

Amorpha fragrans, commonly known as sweetcaraway, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, round seeds within its pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites.

Its aromatic properties make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
