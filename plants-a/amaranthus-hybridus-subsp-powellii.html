Amaranthus hybridus subsp. powellii

Amaranthus hybridus subsp.

powellii, commonly known as Powell's amaranth, is an annual herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant red to purple bracts that surround the flowering spikes.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Powell's amaranth is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking foliage and seed heads.
