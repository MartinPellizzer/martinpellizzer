Anacyclus alexandrinus

Anacyclus alexandrinus, commonly known as the Alexandrian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Anacyclus alexandrinus is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
