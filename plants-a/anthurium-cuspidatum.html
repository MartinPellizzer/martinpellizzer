Anthurium cuspidatum

Anthurium cuspidatum, commonly known as the spined anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often dark green with a metallic sheen. The plant produces bright red spathes that enclose its small, yellowish spadices, which are the actual flowers. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Anthurium cuspidatum is popular as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and unique floral structure.
