Anthurium smithii

Anthurium smithii, commonly known as the red anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage is often a deep green, while the spathe can range from red to orange, depending on the variety.

Anthurium smithii prefers bright, indirect light and moderate humidity, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
