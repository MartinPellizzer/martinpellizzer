Alloplectus glabrescens

Alloplectus glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth marsh hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Alloplectus glabrescens plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
