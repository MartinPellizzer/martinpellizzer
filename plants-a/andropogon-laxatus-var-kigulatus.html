Andropogon laxatus var. kigulatus

Andropogon laxatus var. kigulatus, commonly known as the Kigulat grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed sites, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a prominent midrib and produces spikelets that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its flowering period occurs during the dry season, and it is adapted to tolerate drought conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
