Actinella richardsonii var. floribunda

Actinella richardsonii var. floribunda, commonly known as the richardson's actinella, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features small, showy flowers arranged in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
