Aconitum × mariae nothosubsp. paxii

Aconitum × mariae nothosubsp.

paxii, commonly known as the Alpine monkshood, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple hood-shaped flowers and deeply lobed, palmately arranged leaves.

Native to the alpine regions of Central Europe, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 50–100 cm.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
