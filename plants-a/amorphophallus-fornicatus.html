Amorphophallus fornicatus

Amorphophallus fornicatus, commonly known as the giant swamp arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks. Its botanical features include a tuberous root system that stores nutrients, allowing it to survive periods of drought.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in some regions.
