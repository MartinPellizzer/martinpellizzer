Aristolochia chamissonis subsp. paulistana

Aristolochia chamissonis subsp.

paulistana, commonly known as the Brazilian pipevine, is a climbing vine native to South America. It features heart-shaped, aromatic leaves and produces intricate, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a strong, spicy odor.

The plant is characterized by its unique floral morphology, which resembles a pipe or a horn, hence its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is notable for its chemical compounds, some of which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.
