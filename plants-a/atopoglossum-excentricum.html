Atopoglossum excentricum

Atopoglossum excentricum, commonly known as the eccentric tonguegrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family (Poaceae).

It is native to parts of Europe and is characterized by its distinctive, tongue-shaped inflorescence. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, spikelet-based flowers.

The species is often found in low nutrient soils and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.
