Achyrophorus barbatus

Achyrophorus barbatus, commonly known as the bearded bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a hairy appearance on the petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged, resilient appearance.

Achyrophorus barbatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
