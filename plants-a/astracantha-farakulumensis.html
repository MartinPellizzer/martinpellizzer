Astracantha farakulumensis

Astracantha farakulumensis, commonly known as the Farakulum thornveld, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has adapted to harsh environmental conditions through its succulent stems and deep root system. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
