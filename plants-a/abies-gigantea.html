Abies gigantea

Abies gigantea, commonly known as the giant fir, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, pyramidal crown, which can reach heights of up to 50 meters. The needles are dark green, flat, and arranged in spirals along the branches, while the cones are typically pendulous and contain edible seeds. This plant thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in high-altitude forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
