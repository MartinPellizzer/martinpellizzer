Atheropogon acuminatus

Atheropogon acuminatus, commonly known as the slender pappus grass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in open habitats such as fields, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Atheropogon acuminatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
