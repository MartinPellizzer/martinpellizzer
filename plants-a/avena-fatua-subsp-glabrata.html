Avena fatua subsp. glabrata

Avena fatua subsp.

glabrata, commonly known as slender oat, is a wild grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and agricultural fields, and is considered a weed in some regions.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common component of early successional plant communities.
