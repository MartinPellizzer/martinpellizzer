Acronia coriacardia

Acronia coriacardia, commonly known as the heartleaf acronia, is a rare flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Acronia coriacardia thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is often found in conservation programs aimed at preserving its population.
