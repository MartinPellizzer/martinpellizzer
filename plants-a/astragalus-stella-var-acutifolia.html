Astragalus stella var. acutifolia

Astragalus stella var. acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with sharp, pointed leaflets, giving it its common name. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.

Astragalus stella var. acutifolia is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
