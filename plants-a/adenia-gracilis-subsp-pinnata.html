Adenia gracilis subsp. pinnata

Adenia gracilis subsp.

pinnata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved adenia, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It features opposite, pinnately compound leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, narrow shape.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. Its fruits are follicles that split open to release seeds, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Adenia gracilis subsp.

pinnata is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
