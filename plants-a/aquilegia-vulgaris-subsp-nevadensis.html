Aquilegia vulgaris subsp. nevadensis

Aquilegia vulgaris subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada columbine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features showy, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple, with five petals and two elongated nectar spurs.

The plant has palmately divided leaves with five to seven leaflets, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows and along streams in mountainous regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
