Albuca erlangeriana

Albuca erlangeriana, commonly known as the Erlandinger's albuca, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bulbous stem and strap-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid regions.
