Astragalus akhundzadahensis

Astragalus akhundzadahensis, commonly known as the Akhundzadeh milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale purple flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features such as deep root systems.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and may have potential ecological or medicinal significance.
