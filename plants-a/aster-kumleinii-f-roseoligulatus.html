Aster kumleinii f. roseoligulatus

Aster kumleinii f. roseoligulatus, commonly known as the rose-leaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive ray florets. The plant features showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its foliage is typically hairy and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows.
