Acaena argentea var. contracta

Acaena argentea var. contracta, commonly known as silver burr or silver weed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spiny burrs that are adapted for seed dispersal by attaching to animal fur. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it both an ornamental plant and a potential weed in certain regions.
