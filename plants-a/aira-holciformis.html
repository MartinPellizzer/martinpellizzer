Aira holciformis

Aira holciformis, commonly known as the tufted air grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Aira holciformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
