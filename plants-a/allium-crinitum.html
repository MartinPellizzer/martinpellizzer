Allium crinitum

Allium crinitum, commonly known as the curly vial or curling onion, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant's bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, often used in Mediterranean cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Allium crinitum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
