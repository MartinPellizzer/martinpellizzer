Ancipitia driessenii

Ancipitia driessenii, commonly known as the Drissen's ancient fern, is a rare and ancient fern species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate fronds that exhibit a unique dichotomous branching pattern, which is a distinctive botanical feature. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in dense rainforests. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, a common trait among ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Ancipitia driessenii is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
