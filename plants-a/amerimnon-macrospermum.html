Amerimnon macrospermum

Amerimnon macrospermum, commonly known as the large-seeded amaranth, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant red or purple flowers that bloom in dense clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are encased in a hard outer shell, which aids in seed dispersal and survival in dry conditions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
