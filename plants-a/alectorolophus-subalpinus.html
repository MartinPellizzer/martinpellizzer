Alectorolophus subalpinus

Alectorolophus subalpinus, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Alps and adjacent mountain ranges.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often appearing in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Alectorolophus subalpinus plays an important ecological role in alpine ecosystems, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a habitat for small invertebrates.
