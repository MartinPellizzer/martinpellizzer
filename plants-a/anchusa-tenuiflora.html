Anchusa tenuiflora

Anchusa tenuiflora, commonly known as narrow-leaved anchusa, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, cylindrical stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking blue or violet flowers in spikes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Anchusa tenuiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
