Acacia moirii

Acacia moirii, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and small, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Acacia moirii thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
