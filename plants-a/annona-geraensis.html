Annona geraensis

Annona geraensis, commonly known as the Brazilian pawpaw, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish-green flowers, followed by fruit that resembles a pawpaw but has a distinct flavor. Its fruit is edible, though it is less commonly cultivated than other annona species.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses.
