Acantholimon capitatum

Acantholimon capitatum, commonly known as the clustered sandthrift, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with dense, compact growth habit, featuring gray-green foliage that is covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in dense clusters at the ends of its stems, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an important species for stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local pollinators.
