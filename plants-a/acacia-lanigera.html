Acacia lanigera

Acacia lanigera, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and dense clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces flattened, leathery pods that contain several seeds. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Acacia lanigera thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and savanna habitats.
