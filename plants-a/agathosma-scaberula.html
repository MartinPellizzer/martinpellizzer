Agathosma scaberula

Agathosma scaberula, commonly known as the rough agathosma, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. It is valued for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

Agathosma scaberula is also an important plant for pollinators and contributes to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
