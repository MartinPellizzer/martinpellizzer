Arabis williamsii var. saximontana

Arabis williamsii var. saximontana, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Sierra Nevada mountain range in California.

It typically forms dense mats of foliage with bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

It is a valuable plant for rock gardens and is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
