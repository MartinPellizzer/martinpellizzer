Acnistus confertiflorus

Acnistus confertiflorus, commonly known as the clustered milkweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a common feature in the milkweed family.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.
