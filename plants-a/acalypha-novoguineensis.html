Acalypha novoguineensis

Acalypha novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea copperleaf, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of New Guinea.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its striking copper-colored leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, round, red or orange seed capsules. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Acalypha novoguineensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other tropical regions for horticultural purposes.
