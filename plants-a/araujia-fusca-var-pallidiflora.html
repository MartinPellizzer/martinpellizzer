Araujia fusca var. pallidiflora

Araujia fusca var. pallidiflora, commonly known as the paleflower moon vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its fragrant blossoms and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release its seeds.
