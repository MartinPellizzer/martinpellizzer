Acalypha weinlandii

Acalypha weinlandii, commonly known as the Mexican flamebush, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking red bracts that resemble flames, which are actually modified leaves, not flowers. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Acalypha weinlandii is also valued for its ability to attract birds and butterflies to the garden.
