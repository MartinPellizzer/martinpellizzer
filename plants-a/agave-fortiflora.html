Agave fortiflora

Agave fortiflora, commonly known as the rigid agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color and covered in a whitish bloom. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear yellow flowers, which bloom in the spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Agave fortiflora is often used as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
