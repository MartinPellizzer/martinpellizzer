Acacia miersii

Acacia miersii, commonly known as the Miers' acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in winter. Acacia miersii is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various insects and small animals.
