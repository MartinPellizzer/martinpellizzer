Arabis alpina var. purpurea

Arabis alpina var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple rockcress, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range from white to purple. The plant typically blooms in early spring and is adapted to cold, rocky environments. Its leaves are basal, linear, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
