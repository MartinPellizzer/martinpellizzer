Alchemilla humilicaulis

Alchemilla humilicaulis, commonly known as the lesser lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish down. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Alchemilla humilicaulis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
