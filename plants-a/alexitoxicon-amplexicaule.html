Alexitoxicon amplexicaule

Alexitoxicon amplexicaule, commonly known as the embrace-leaved poisonplant, is a rare and toxic species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring broad, heart-shaped leaves that wrap around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves and stems contain potent alkaloids that can cause severe irritation or poisoning upon contact.

Due to its toxicity and rarity, it is often found in secluded, humid environments, making it both a botanical curiosity and a potential hazard.
