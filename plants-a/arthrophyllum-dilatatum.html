Arthrophyllum dilatatum

Arthrophyllum dilatatum, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Araceae, which includes plants such as philodendrons and calla lilies. The plant is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
