Azara chiloensis

Azara chiloensis, commonly known as the Chilean azara, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Chile.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Azara chiloensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
