Astragalus gossypinus var. filagineus

Astragalus gossypinus var. filagineus, commonly known as the silky milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its erect stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
