Anatherum macrourum

Anatherum macrourum, commonly known as the long-beak grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and long, narrow leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland ecosystems.

Anatherum macrourum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its elegant appearance.
