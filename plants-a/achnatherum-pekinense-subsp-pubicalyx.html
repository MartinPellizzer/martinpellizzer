Achnatherum pekinense subsp. pubicalyx

Achnatherum pekinense subsp.

pubicalyx, commonly known as the Peking needlegrass, is a grass species native to China. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, typically in late summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
