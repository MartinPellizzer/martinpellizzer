Astragalus neochorgosicus

Astragalus neochorgosicus, commonly known as the Mongolian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus neochorgosicus is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
