Arctostaphylos pungens subsp. chaloneorum

Arctostaphylos pungens subsp.

chaloneorum, commonly known as the Chaloneum manzanita, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of small, leathery leaves.

The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild, wet winters.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
