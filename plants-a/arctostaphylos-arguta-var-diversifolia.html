Arctostaphylos arguta var. diversifolia

Arctostaphylos arguta var. diversifolia, commonly known as the Sierra Madre manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and red-barked stems, which provide habitat for various wildlife species. The plant produces small, showy white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Its unique leaf shape and variability in foliage color make it a distinctive and ecologically significant plant in its native range.
