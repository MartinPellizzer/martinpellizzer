Altamiranoa parviflora

Altamiranoa parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered altamiranoa, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Brazil. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
