Anemia coriacea subsp. coriacea

Anemia coriacea subsp.

coriacea, commonly known as the smooth anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features basal leaves that are smooth and waxy.

The plant produces showy white to pink flowers in early spring, which are clustered in umbels. Its botanical characteristics include a short, stout rhizome and a fibrous root system.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smooth, coriaceous (leathery) leaves and is often found in shaded, well-drained soils.
