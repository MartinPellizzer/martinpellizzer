Agave striata

Agave striata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of narrow, blue-green leaves with white marginal teeth. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellowish-green flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil.

Agave striata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in arid environments.
