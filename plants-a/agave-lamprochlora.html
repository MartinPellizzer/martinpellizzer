Agave lamprochlora

Agave lamprochlora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved agave, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves. The leaves are typically blue-green in color with a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. It produces tall flower stalks that bear yellow flowers, which are followed by small, greenish fruits.

This plant is adapted to thrive in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping in dry climates.
