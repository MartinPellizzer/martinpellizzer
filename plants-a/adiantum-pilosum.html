Adiantum pilosum

Adiantum pilosum, commonly known as the southern maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bioluminescent in the dark. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its fronds are pinnately divided, with small, toothed segments that give it a distinctive appearance.

Adiantum pilosum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
