Acacia robusta subsp. usambarensis

Acacia robusta subsp.

usambarensis, commonly known as the Usambarensis acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, flat, bipinnate leaves and yellow to orange globular flower heads.

The plant produces long, slender pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including savannas and rocky areas, and is adapted to seasonal rainfall patterns.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
