Aristida kunthiana

Aristida kunthiana, commonly known as the desert threeawn, is a grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry blades that often have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically borne on long, slender stalks. Its fibrous roots help it survive in drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.

Aristida kunthiana is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.
