Asarum virginicum var. grandiflorum

Asarum virginicum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, reddish-brown veined leaves that are aromatic when crushed. The plant produces clusters of small, showy greenish-white flowers with a distinctive, spicy scent. It thrives in moist, shaded woodland habitats and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
