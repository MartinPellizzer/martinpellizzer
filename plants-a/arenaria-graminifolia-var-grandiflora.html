Arenaria graminifolia var. grandiflora

Arenaria graminifolia var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and it produces showy clusters of small, white or pink flowers in early summer. The plant is adapted to rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

It is native to regions of Europe and western Asia and is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
