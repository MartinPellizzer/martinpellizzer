Achyronia polycephala

Achyronia polycephala, commonly known as the clustered orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant has a slender, upright stem with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Achyronia polycephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
