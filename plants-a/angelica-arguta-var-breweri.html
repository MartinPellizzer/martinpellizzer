Angelica arguta var. breweri

Angelica arguta var. breweri, commonly known as the Brewer's angelica, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant features large, compound leaves with a distinctive silvery underside. Its flowering stems bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early summer.

The roots of this species are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.
