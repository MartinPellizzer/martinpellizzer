Amaryllis jagus

Amaryllis jagus, commonly known as the Jamaican lily, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped blooms. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with a contrasting white or yellow throat. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are broad and pointed.

Amaryllis jagus thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
