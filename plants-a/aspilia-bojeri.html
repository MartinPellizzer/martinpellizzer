Aspilia bojeri

Aspilia bojeri, commonly known as the Bojer's torchflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features striking yellow-orange flower heads that resemble torches, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Aspilia bojeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
