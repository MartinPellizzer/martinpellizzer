Amomum diphyllum

Amomum diphyllum, commonly known as the two-leaved cardamom, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish itself in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture, while its inflorescence consists of small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its essential oils and medicinal properties.
