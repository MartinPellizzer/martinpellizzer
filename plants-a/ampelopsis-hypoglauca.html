Ampelopsis hypoglauca

Ampelopsis hypoglauca, commonly known as the blue mist vine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive bluish-green hue, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by blue-black berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full sun and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage.

Its vigorous growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in shaded areas.
