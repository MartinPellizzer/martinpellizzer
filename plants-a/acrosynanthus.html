Acrosynanthus

Acrosynanthus, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

These plants are characterized by their showy, velvety flowers that come in a wide range of colors. The leaves are typically fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps the plant conserve moisture. Acrosynanthus species are popular houseplants due to their ability to thrive in low-light conditions and their attractive appearance.

They are also valued for their ornamental use in gardens and indoor settings.
