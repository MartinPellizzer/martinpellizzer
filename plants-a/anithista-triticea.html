Anithista triticea

Anithista triticea, commonly known as the three-leaf grass, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered along the stem. Anithista triticea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of grassland ecosystems.
