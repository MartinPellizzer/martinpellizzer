Aster glaucus var. wasatchensis

Aster glaucus var. wasatchensis, commonly known as the Wasatch bluebell, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and meadows. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of white to grayish hairs. Its flowers are blue to purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
