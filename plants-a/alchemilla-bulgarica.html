Alchemilla bulgarica

Alchemilla bulgarica, commonly known as Bulgarian lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of shiny, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla bulgarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
