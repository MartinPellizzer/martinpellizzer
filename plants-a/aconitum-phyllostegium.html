Aconitum phyllostegium

Aconitum phyllostegium, commonly known as the Chinese monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, helmet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple. The plant has large, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Aconitum phyllostegium is also noted for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
