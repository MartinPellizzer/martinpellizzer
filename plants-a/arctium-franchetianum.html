Arctium franchetianum

Arctium franchetianum, commonly known as the French burdock, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features a thick, swollen root that is often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head.

The plant is known for its prickly burrs that cling to clothing and animal fur, aiding in seed dispersal.
