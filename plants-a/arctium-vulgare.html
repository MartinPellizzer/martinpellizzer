Arctium vulgare

Arctium vulgare, commonly known as common burdock, is a biennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by the development of spiny, bur-like fruits. Its root is thick, fleshy, and often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is commonly found along roadsides and in waste areas.
