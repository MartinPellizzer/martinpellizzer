Anthurium albispatha

Anthurium albispatha, commonly known as the White-veined Anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are predominantly green with prominent white veins, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces spadices, which are specialized inflorescences that are typically green with a red or purple spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium albispatha is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
