Aerides recurvipes

Aerides recurvipes, commonly known as the recurved orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often forming a rosette at the base.

Aerides recurvipes is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
