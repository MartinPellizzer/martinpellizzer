Acacia chordophylla

Acacia chordophylla, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs, and its yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is often found along coastal areas and inland dunes. It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Acacia chordophylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
