Arctostaphylos australis var. sericea

Arctostaphylos australis var. sericea, commonly known as the silky manna gum, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage with a fine, silky texture due to the presence of glandular hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are evergreen, and the plant is adapted to dry, well-drained soils.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
