Arthromeris amplexifolia

Arthromeris amplexifolia, commonly known as the clinging leaf orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, where the leaves are often clasping the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
