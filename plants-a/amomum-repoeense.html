Amomum repoeense

Amomum repoeense, commonly known as the Vietnamese cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric. The plant features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its rhizomes are the primary part used, containing essential oils that contribute to its distinctive fragrance.

Amomum repoeense is valued in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
