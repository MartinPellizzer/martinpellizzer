Aristea corymbosa

Aristea corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered aristea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features dense clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Aristea corymbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
