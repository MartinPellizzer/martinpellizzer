Acer mono f. albomaculatum

Acer mono f. albomaculatum, commonly known as the white-spotted maple, is a unique variant of the singleleaf maple.

It is characterized by its striking white or cream-colored spots on the leaves, which contrast with its green background. The plant is a deciduous tree or shrub native to eastern North America. Its foliage changes color in the fall, displaying shades of red and orange.

The white-spotted maple is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
