Alchemilla parijae

Alchemilla parijae, commonly known as the Armenian lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, hairy leaves that resemble a shield. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Alchemilla parijae is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in its native region.
