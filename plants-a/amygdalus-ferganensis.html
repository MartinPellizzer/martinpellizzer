Amygdalus ferganensis

Amygdalus ferganensis, commonly known as the Fergana almond, is a species of shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and almond-like fruits. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to dry, temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The fruit, which contains a single seed, is used for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
