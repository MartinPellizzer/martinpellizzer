Artemisia afghanica

Artemisia afghanica, commonly known as Afghan wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Artemisia afghanica is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
