Aconitum × paniculatum var. matthioli

Aconitum × paniculatum var. matthioli, commonly known as the purple-flowered aconite, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep purple to violet. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring dark green, compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained, fertile soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from late summer into early autumn.
