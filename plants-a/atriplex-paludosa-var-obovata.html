Atriplex paludosa var. obovata

Atriplex paludosa var. obovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf saltbush, is a perennial shrub native to wetland and saline environments in North America.

It is characterized by its obovate, succulent leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of salt-excreting glands. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in poorly drained soils and is adapted to tolerate high salinity and periodic flooding.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
