Astragalus lanocarpus

Astragalus lanocarpus, commonly known as the wooly milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seed pods are typically hairy and contain several small, round seeds.

Astragalus lanocarpus is adapted to harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
