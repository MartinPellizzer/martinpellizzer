Astragalus malheurensis

Astragalus malheurensis, commonly known as the unhappy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its seeds are enclosed in pods, and it plays a role in soil stabilization due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

However, it is considered rare and faces threats from habitat loss and overgrazing.
