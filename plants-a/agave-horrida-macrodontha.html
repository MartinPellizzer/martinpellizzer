Agave horrida macrodontha

Agave horrida macrodontha, commonly known as the giant desert agave, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, spiny leaves that can grow up to several feet in length, forming a rosette structure. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with small, white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is a succulent, adapted to store water in its thick, fleshy leaves and stems.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
