Andropogon domingensis

Andropogon domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicle inflorescences. It is often found in open woodlands, along stream banks, and in disturbed areas.

Botanically, it is classified within the Poaceae family, which includes many economically important grasses.
