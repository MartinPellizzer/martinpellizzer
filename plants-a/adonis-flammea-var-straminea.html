Adonis flammea var. straminea

Adonis flammea var. straminea, commonly known as the yellow belladonna, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, yellow-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with a distinctive red or orange throat, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically arranged in a single terminal umbel, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in alpine gardens.

However, it is toxic and should be handled with care.
