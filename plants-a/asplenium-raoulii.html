Asplenium raoulii

Asplenium raoulii, commonly known as the Raouli's shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit and dark green, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are short and thick, helping it anchor in soil or substrates.

Asplenium raoulii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
