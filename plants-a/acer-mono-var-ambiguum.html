Acer mono var. ambiguum

Acer mono var. ambiguum, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. It blooms with small, inconspicuous yellow-green flowers in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The variety 'ambiguum' is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
