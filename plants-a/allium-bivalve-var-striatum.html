Allium bivalve var. striatum

Allium bivalve var. striatum, commonly known as the striped garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are often striped with white or pale green markings. The plant produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers in the summer, which are followed by spherical, pale blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for both garden beds and container planting.

This variety is valued for its mild garlic flavor and ornamental appeal, often used in culinary and decorative gardens.
