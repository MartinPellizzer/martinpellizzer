Azalea indica coccinea

Azalea indica coccinea, commonly known as the red azalea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to pink. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

Azalea indica coccinea thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
