Arenaria fendleri var. subcongesta

Arenaria fendleri var. subcongesta, commonly known as desert sandflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in spring. Its leaves are typically blue-gray, covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss.

It is well adapted to sandy soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
