Arabis hirsuta subsp. allionii

Arabis hirsuta subsp.

allionii, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features small, white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically has hairy, lobed leaves and thrives in rocky or gravelly soils. It is native to regions in southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

This subspecies is often found in alpine or subalpine environments, where it adapts to harsh climatic conditions.
