Anthemis incisa

Anthemis incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinctive serrated edge, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Anthemis incisa is also known for its medicinal properties, having been used traditionally for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
