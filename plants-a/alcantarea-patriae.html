Alcantarea patriae

Alcantarea patriae, commonly known as the Brazilian crown of thorns, is a striking bromeliad native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that can reach up to 3 meters in height, crowned with vibrant orange or red flowers. The plant features thick, waxy leaves that form a rosette shape, capable of storing water, which helps it survive in humid, shaded environments. Its striking appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant, though it requires specific conditions to thrive.

Alcantarea patriae plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
