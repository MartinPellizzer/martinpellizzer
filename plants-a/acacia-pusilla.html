Acacia pusilla

Acacia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf wattle, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage. The plant produces small yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Acacia pusilla is typically found in sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small native animals.
