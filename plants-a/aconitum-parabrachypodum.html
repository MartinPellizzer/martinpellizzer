Aconitum parabrachypodum

Aconitum parabrachypodum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are deeply divided into narrow, elongated lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Aconitum parabrachypodum is known for containing potent alkaloids, making it toxic and requiring careful handling.
