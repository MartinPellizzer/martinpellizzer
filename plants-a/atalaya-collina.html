Atalaya collina

Atalaya collina, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in high-altitude areas with cool, moist climates. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Atalaya collina is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
