Amomum macrocheilos

Amomum macrocheilos, commonly known as the large-lobed cardamom, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Vietnam and Cambodia. The plant produces fragrant, pale yellow flowers that bloom in terminal inflorescences. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.
