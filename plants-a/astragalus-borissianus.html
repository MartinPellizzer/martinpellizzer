Astragalus borissianus

Astragalus borissianus, commonly known as the Borissian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in late spring. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus borissianus is part of the legume family and has potential medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.
