Acinos majoranifolius f. rosiflorus

Acinos majoranifolius f. rosiflorus, commonly known as the rose-flowered mountain savory, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, low-growing rosettes of gray-green, aromatic leaves with a strong, pungent scent. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its antiseptic and carminative properties.

The species is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
