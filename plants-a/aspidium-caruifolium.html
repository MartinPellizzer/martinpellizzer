Aspidium caruifolium

Aspidium caruifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaf fern, is a species of fern native to various regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of the fronds, which are arranged in a spiral pattern.

Aspidium caruifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
