Amphiglossa foliosa

Amphiglossa foliosa, commonly known as the broad-leafed amphiglossa, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. Its small, clustered flowers are typically greenish-white in color and bloom during the wet season.

Amphiglossa foliosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
