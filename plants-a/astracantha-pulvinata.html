Astracantha pulvinata

Astracantha pulvinata, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and distinctive, spiny leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

The plant has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating ailments related to the respiratory and digestive systems.
