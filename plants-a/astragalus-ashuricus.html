Astragalus ashuricus

Astragalus ashuricus, commonly known as the Assyrian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish-pink flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Astragalus ashuricus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
