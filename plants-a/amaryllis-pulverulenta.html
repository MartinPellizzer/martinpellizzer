Amaryllis pulverulenta

Amaryllis pulverulenta, commonly known as the dusty amaryllis, is a rare and striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its unique, dusty-pink flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts and botanists alike.
