Aegilops squarrosa var. truncata

Aegilops squarrosa var. truncata, commonly known as the truncated wild oat, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that bear bristle-like awns, which are distinctive features of the genus Aegilops. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

This variety is of interest in agricultural research due to its potential for genetic contributions to wheat improvement.
