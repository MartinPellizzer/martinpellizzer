Acer × parvifolium

Acer × parvifolium, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 meters, with a rounded or conical shape. Its small, inconspicuous flowers appear in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The bark is smooth and reddish-brown, becoming fissured with age, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
