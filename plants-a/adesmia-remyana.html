Adesmia remyana

Adesmia remyana, commonly known as the Remy's burro's tail, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to purple flowers during the warm season. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
