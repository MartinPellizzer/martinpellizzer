Acca sellowiana var. rugosa

Acca sellowiana var. rugosa, commonly known as the rough-hairy guava, is a tropical shrub native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and rough, textured bark. The plant produces small, white flowers that give way to round, yellow or orange fruits with a distinctive, aromatic flavor. The fruit is typically harvested when fully mature, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruits.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
