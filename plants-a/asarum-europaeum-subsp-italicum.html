Asarum europaeum subsp. italicum

Asarum europaeum subsp.

italicum, commonly known as Italian wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It features heart-shaped, glossy green leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy texture.

The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers that are clustered in a spathaceous sheath, typical of the Aristolochiaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its aromatic rhizomes and leaves have been historically used in traditional medicine.
