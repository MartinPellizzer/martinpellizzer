Akebia quinata

Akebia quinata, commonly known as the Japanese hop plant, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Akebia quinata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or fences.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.
