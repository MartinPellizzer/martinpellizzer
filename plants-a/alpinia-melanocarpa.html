Alpinia melanocarpa

Alpinia melanocarpa, commonly known as black sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces fragrant, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark red, berry-like fruits. Its roots are valued in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties.

Alpinia melanocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its essential oils used in perfumery and aromatherapy.
