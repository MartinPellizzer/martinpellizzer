Acosmium inornatum

Acosmium inornatum, commonly known as the smooth acosmium, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbel-like inflorescences.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features compound leaves with finely divided leaflets, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and herbal practices.
