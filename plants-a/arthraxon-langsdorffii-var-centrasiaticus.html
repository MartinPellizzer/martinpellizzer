Arthraxon langsdorffii var. centrasiaticus

Arthraxon langsdorffii var. centrasiaticus, commonly known as the Central Asian arthraxon, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and grasslands. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to arid conditions and its role in local ecosystems.
