Amyema cercidioides

Amyema cercidioides, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The tree is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
