Acer trinerve

Acer trinerve, commonly known as the three-veined maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which have three leaflets with a central vein. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds known as samaras. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with shallow fissures.

Acer trinerve is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
