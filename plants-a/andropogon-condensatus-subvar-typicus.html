Andropogon condensatus subvar. typicus

Andropogon condensatus subvar. typicus, commonly known as the tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves. This plant typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed areas, thriving in full sun. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

The species is valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and as forage for wildlife.
