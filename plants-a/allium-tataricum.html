Allium tataricum

Allium tataricum, commonly known as wild leek, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that are often used in culinary applications, similar to garlic and onions. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and meadows.

Allium tataricum is also valued for its medicinal properties, including potential antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects.
