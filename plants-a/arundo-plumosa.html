Arundo plumosa

Arundo plumosa, commonly known as the feathery reed, is a tall, slender grass-like plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features long, narrow leaves that are soft and feathery to the touch, giving it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer, which are typically clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and damp meadows.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to spread vegetatively, it can become invasive in certain regions.
