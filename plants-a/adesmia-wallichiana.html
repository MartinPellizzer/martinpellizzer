Adesmia wallichiana

Adesmia wallichiana, commonly known as the Himalayan legume, is a perennial shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia in its root nodules. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of white or pale purple. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Adesmia wallichiana is adapted to cold, arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing mountainous soils.
