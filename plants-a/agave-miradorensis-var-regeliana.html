Agave miradorensis var. regeliana

Agave miradorensis var. regeliana, commonly known as the Mexican blue agave, is a species native to the Sierra Madre mountain range in Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and robust, sword-shaped leaves that are thick and fibrous. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears clusters of white to pale green flowers, which are typically pollinated by bats and insects. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
