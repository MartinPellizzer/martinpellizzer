Allium leucosphaerum

Allium leucosphaerum, commonly known as the white onion or white globe garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its spherical, white to pale yellow bulbs. The plant produces grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. It is valued for its culinary uses and has a mild, sweet flavor compared to other allium species.

Due to its ornamental appeal and edible qualities, it is cultivated in gardens and for local food traditions.
