Astracantha pterocephala

Astracantha pterocephala, commonly known as the winged thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique appearance.
