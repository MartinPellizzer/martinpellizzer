Amomum parviflorum var. bifidum

Amomum parviflorum var. bifidum, commonly known as the bifid cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features aromatic, elliptic leaves with a serrated margin and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles. The plant is characterized by its distinctive bifid (split) petals in its floral structure. Its rhizomes and seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The botanical features of this plant make it a valuable species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
