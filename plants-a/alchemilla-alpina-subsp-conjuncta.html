Alchemilla alpina subsp. conjuncta

Alchemilla alpina subsp.

conjuncta, commonly known as the alpine lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, velvety leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in terminal clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with dry, sunny conditions.
