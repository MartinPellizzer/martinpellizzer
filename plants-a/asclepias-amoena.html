Asclepias amoena

Asclepias amoena, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the milkweed family, Apocynaceae, and is characterized by its striking, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its stems are typically hairy and greenish in color. Asclepias amoena is a host plant for the monarch butterfly, providing essential nourishment for both the larvae and adult insects.

Its flowers also attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
