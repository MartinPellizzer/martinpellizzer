Arisaema sazensoo f. serratum

Arisaema sazensoo f. serratum, commonly known as the serrated arisaema, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves and a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and it produces berries that contain toxic seeds.

The plant is native to parts of East Asia and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
