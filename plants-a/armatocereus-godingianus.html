Armatocereus godingianus

Armatocereus godingianus, commonly known as the Goding's night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines and its ability to produce large, fragrant white flowers at night. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. Its flowers open in response to cool temperatures and darkness, making it a popular choice for nocturnal gardeners.

The species is also valued for its ornamental beauty and resilience in harsh conditions.
