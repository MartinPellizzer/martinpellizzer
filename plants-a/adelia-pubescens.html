Adelia pubescens

Adelia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy adelia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy inflorescences. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves covered in fine, silky hairs, which give it its common name. It produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

Adelia pubescens is adapted to grow in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
