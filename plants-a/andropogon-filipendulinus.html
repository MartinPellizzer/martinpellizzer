Andropogon filipendulinus

Andropogon filipendulinus, commonly known as the silky grass or silky bluestem, is a tall, warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and fine, silvery-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common component of native grasslands and prairies.

Andropogon filipendulinus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
