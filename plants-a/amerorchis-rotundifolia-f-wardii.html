Amerorchis rotundifolia f. wardii

Amerorchis rotundifolia f. wardii, commonly known as the Ward's round-leaf orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to specific regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, rounded leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The flowers are typically small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink, depending on the specific cultivar. This orchid thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable and sometimes endangered species in its native range.
