Actinodaphne cinerea

Actinodaphne cinerea, commonly known as the grey-leafed camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its dark, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, making it valuable in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
