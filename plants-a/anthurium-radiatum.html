Anthurium radiatum

Anthurium radiatum, commonly known as the flamingo flower, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful spathes that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow spadices inside the spathe, which contain the actual flowers. Anthurium radiatum thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental purposes in both indoor and outdoor settings.
