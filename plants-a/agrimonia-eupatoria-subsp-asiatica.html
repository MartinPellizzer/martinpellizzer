Agrimonia eupatoria subsp. asiatica

Agrimonia eupatoria subsp.

asiatica, commonly known as Asiatic agrimony, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, pinnately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional medicine for digestive and anti-inflammatory purposes.
