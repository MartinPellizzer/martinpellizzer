Acnistus achalensis

Acnistus achalensis, commonly known as the Indian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-yellow in color, which bloom during the summer months. Acnistus achalensis is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for small animals.
