Acinodendron hyemale

Acinodendron hyemale, commonly known as the white acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are compound, with several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid environments.
