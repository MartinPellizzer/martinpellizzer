Arpophyllum alpinum

Arpophyllum alpinum, commonly known as the alpine arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in alpine meadows and grasslands.

Due to its habitat, it is adapted to cold climates and can withstand harsh environmental conditions.
