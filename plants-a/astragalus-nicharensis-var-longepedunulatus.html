Astragalus nicharensis var. longepedunulatus

Astragalus nicharensis var. longepedunulatus, commonly known as the long-pedunculate milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The variety is distinguished by its elongated peduncles, which bear clusters of small, yellow to reddish-purple blooms. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open grasslands.

This plant plays a role in soil stabilization and serves as a food source for various pollinators and herbivores.
