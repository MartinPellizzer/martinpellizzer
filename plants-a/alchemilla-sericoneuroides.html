Alchemilla sericoneuroides

Alchemilla sericoneuroides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicle clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Alchemilla sericoneuroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
