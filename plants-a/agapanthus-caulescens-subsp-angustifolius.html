Agapanthus caulescens subsp. angustifolius

Agapanthus caulescens subsp.

angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved agapanthus, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa. It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color.

The plant produces tall, upright flower stalks that bear clusters of large, showy blue or purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom reliably in the summer months.
