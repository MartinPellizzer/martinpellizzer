Aconitum stapfianum var. pubipes

Aconitum stapfianum var. pubipes, commonly known as the pubescent aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, spikelike inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, with deeply lobed foliage that is often covered in fine, soft hairs.

This variant is distinguished by its pubescent (hairy) stems and is typically found in rocky, alpine environments.
