Anemone virginiana f. rhodantha

Anemone virginiana f. rhodantha, commonly known as the showy anemone, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb with a basal rosette of palmately lobed, hairy leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically 5-lobed and have a distinctive, fuzzy center.

The species is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
