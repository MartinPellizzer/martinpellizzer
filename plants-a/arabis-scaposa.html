Arabis scaposa

Arabis scaposa, commonly known as the scapose rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its flowering stems are simple and unbranched, with small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes.

The leaves are basal and linear, often with a rough or hairy texture, and the plant is known for its ability to survive in harsh, cold environments.
