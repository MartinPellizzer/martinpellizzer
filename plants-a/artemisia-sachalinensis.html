Artemisia sachalinensis

Artemisia sachalinensis, commonly known as Sakhalin wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Russian Far East and parts of northern Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested or wetland habitats. Its small, yellowish flowers appear in composite inflorescences, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

Due to its ecological and medicinal significance, it is also of interest in conservation efforts.
