Agave ellemeetiana subsp. subdentata

Agave ellemeetiana subsp.

subdentata, commonly known as the narrow-toothed agave, is a species native to Mexico. It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves with a distinctive narrow, tooth-like margin.

The plant produces tall flower stalks that bear clusters of yellowish-green flowers, which are typical of the Agavaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in xeriscaping and desert gardens.
