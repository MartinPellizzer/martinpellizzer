Astragalus nepalensis

Astragalus nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India, typically growing in alpine and subalpine environments. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Astragalus nepalensis is also valued for its ecological importance in high-altitude ecosystems.
