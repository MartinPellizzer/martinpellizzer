Aristolochia paecilantha

Aristolochia paecilantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe, typically green or brown in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its aromatic, tubular blossoms that attract specific pollinators. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and twining.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
