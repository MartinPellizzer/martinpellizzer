Astragalus keratensis

Astragalus keratensis, commonly known as the Keratensian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often dispersed by wind.

Astragalus keratensis is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
