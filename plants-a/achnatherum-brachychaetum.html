Achnatherum brachychaetum

Achnatherum brachychaetum, commonly known as the short-hairy needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, harsh environments. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-ranked spikelets.

Achnatherum brachychaetum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitats.
