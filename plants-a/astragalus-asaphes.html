Astragalus asaphes

Astragalus asaphes, commonly known as the asaphes milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Astragalus asaphes is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules.

Its ecological significance includes providing forage for wildlife and contributing to soil stabilization in its native habitats.
