Avenula praeusta subsp. adsurgens

Avenula praeusta subsp.

adsurgens, commonly known as the tall meadow grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and meadows.

The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators.
