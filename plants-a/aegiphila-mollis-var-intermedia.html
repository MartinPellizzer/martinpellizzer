Aegiphila mollis var. intermedia

Aegiphila mollis var. intermedia, commonly known as the Mexican mint or wild mint, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in desert or semi-arid environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used by indigenous communities for various therapeutic purposes.
