Agave americana var. theometel

Agave americana var. theometel, commonly known as the Mexican century plant, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often blue-green in color and lined with sharp spines. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that can reach several meters in height, bearing numerous white flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments.

The plant's ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in harsh, dry conditions.
