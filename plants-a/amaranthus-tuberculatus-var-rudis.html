Amaranthus tuberculatus var. rudis

Amaranthus tuberculatus var. rudis, commonly known as rough pigweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered greenish to reddish flowers that develop into three-angled, smooth seed capsules. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is considered a common weed in many regions.
