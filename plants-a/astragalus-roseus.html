Astragalus roseus

Astragalus roseus, commonly known as the pink milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Astragalus roseus is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in native landscapes.
