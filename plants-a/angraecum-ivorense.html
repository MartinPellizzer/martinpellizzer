Angraecum ivorense

Angraecum ivorense, commonly known as the Ivory Coast orchid, is a species of orchid native to western Africa.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a slender rhizome and produces striking, fragrant flowers with long, slender petals. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, with a distinctive spurred lip.

Angraecum ivorense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
