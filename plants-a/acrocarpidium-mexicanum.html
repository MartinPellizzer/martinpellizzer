Acrocarpidium mexicanum

Acrocarpidium mexicanum, commonly known as the Mexican acrocarp, is a rare and unique species of liverwort found primarily in the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Acrocarpaceae and is characterized by its small, leaf-like structures called thallus, which are typically green and photosynthetic. The plant exhibits a complex morphology with distinct reproductive structures, including male and female gametophytes, which are essential for its reproductive cycle. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found on rocks or in humid forest floors.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological specificity, Acrocarpidium mexicanum is considered a significant subject in the study of cryptogamic flora.
