Angelica nelsonii

Angelica nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's angelica, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces large, compound leaves with a distinctive pinnate structure and yellowish-green flowers arranged in clustered umbels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its aromatic foliage and historical use in traditional medicine.
