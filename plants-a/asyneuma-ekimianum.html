Asyneuma ekimianum

Asyneuma ekimianum, commonly known as the Ethiopian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its bright yellow flower heads bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is adapted to the arid and semi-arid climates of its native region, showcasing resilience to drought conditions.
