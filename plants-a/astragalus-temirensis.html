Astragalus temirensis

Astragalus temirensis, commonly known as the Temirensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
