Aspidosperma chiapense

Aspidosperma chiapense, commonly known as Chiapan redwood, is a deciduous tree native to the humid lowlands of southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and elongated, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal color changes.
