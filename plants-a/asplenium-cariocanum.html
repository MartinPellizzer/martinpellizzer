Asplenium cariocanum

Asplenium cariocanum, commonly known as the Brazilian maidenhair fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble those of other maidenhair ferns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through spore dispersal rather than seeds.

Asplenium cariocanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in humid conditions.
