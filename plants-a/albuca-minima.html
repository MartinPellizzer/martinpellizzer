Albuca minima

Albuca minima, commonly known as the dwarf albuca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and attractive, grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers on slender stems during the warmer months. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice among gardeners.
