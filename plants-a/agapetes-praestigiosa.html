Agapetes praestigiosa

Agapetes praestigiosa, commonly known as the silverleaf agapete, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a silvery or whitish bloom, giving it its common name. It produces bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in early spring.

Agapetes praestigiosa is adapted to humid, shaded environments and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.
