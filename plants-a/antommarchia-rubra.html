Antommarchia rubra

Antommarchia rubra, commonly known as the red-tipped ant-mimic plant, is a striking member of the Apocynaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant red tips on its lance-shaped leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It is native to regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

Antommarchia rubra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
