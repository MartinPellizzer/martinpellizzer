Aster foliaceus var. hesperius

Aster foliaceus var. hesperius, commonly known as the Hesperus leaf aster, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, such as deserts and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
