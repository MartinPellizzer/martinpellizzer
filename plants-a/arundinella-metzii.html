Arundinella metzii

Arundinella metzii, commonly known as the Mexican bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Arundinella metzii is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or disturbed soils.
