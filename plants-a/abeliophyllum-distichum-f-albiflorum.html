Abeliophyllum distichum f. albiflorum

Abeliophyllum distichum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflowered abelia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a delicate floral display. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its blossoms. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its elegant appearance.
