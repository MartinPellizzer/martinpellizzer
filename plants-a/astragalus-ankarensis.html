Astragalus ankarensis

Astragalus ankarensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus ankarensis plays a role in local ecosystems by contributing to soil stabilization and providing forage for wildlife.
