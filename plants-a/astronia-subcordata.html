Astronia subcordata

Astronia subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed astronia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in shrubland and coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers are small and yellow, forming dense clusters.

Astronia subcordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
