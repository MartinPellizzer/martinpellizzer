Aira cristata

Aira cristata, commonly known as the tufted grass or cock's-comb grass, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces showy, feathery inflorescences that resemble a comb or a plume. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Aira cristata is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ease of cultivation.
