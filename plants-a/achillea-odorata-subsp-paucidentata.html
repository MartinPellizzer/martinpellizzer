Achillea odorata subsp. paucidentata

Achillea odorata subsp.

paucidentata, commonly known as the fragrant yarrow, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used historically in herbal medicine.

Its subspecies paucidentata distinguishes itself through fewer leaf teeth compared to other varieties within the species.
