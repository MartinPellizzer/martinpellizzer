Ampelocissus brunneorubra

Ampelocissus brunneorubra, commonly known as the red-bronze fig-leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often reddish-brown in color, especially when mature. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage.

The species is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its potential medicinal properties.
