Astragalus cretaceus var. globosa

Astragalus cretaceus var. globosa, commonly known as the globular milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, yellowish-green stems and small, globular, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has palmately divided leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its root system is typically deep and fibrous. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is often found in grasslands, shrublands, and along canyon walls.
