Asparagus ritschardii

Asparagus ritschardii, commonly known as the desert asparagus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and desert scrublands. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has fleshy, succulent stems that store water, aiding in its survival in harsh conditions.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small desert animals.
