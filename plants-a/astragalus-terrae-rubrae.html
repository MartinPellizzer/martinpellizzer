Astragalus terrae-rubrae

Astragalus terrae-rubrae, commonly known as the red soil milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, typical of many Astragalus species. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, this plant is well adapted to dry environments with deep root systems. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

The species is also of interest in botanical research due to its unique adaptations to harsh climates.
