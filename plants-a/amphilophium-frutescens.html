Amphilophium frutescens

Amphilophium frutescens, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring thick, woody stems and large, showy flowers. The leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and the flowers are typically pink to red in color, with five petals. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
