Acalypha argomuelleri

Acalypha argomuelleri, commonly known as the red-veined nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red veins on its heart-shaped leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
