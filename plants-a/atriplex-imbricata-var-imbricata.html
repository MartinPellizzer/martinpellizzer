Atriplex imbricata var. imbricata

Atriplex imbricata var. imbricata, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in saline and alkaline soils. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences.

Atriplex imbricata var. imbricata is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and provide habitat for wildlife.
