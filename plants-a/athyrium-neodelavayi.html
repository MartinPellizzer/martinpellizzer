Athyrium neodelavayi

Athyrium neodelavayi, commonly known as the Chinese male fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often arching or drooping. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Athyrium neodelavayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant foliage.
