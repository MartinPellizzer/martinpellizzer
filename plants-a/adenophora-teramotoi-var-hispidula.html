Adenophora teramotoi var. hispidula

Adenophora teramotoi var. hispidula, commonly known as the Hispid Honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that release tiny seeds.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along streambanks.
