Arrhostoxylum hartwegianum

Arrhostoxylum hartwegianum, commonly known as the Hartweg's cholla, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water efficiently. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its structure is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal rainfall.

The plant's root system is shallow but extensive, allowing it to quickly absorb surface moisture.
