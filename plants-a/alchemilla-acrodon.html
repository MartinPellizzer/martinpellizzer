Alchemilla acrodon

Alchemilla acrodon, commonly known as the mountain lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, broad leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Alchemilla acrodon is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
