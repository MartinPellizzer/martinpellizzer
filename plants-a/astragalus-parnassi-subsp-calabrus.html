Astragalus parnassi subsp. calabrus

Astragalus parnassi subsp.

calabrus, commonly known as the Parnassian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its subspecific name, *calabrus*, refers to its origin in the Calabria region of southern Italy.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in mountainous areas.
