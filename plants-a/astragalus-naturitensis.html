Astragalus naturitensis

Astragalus naturitensis, commonly known as the natural mountain milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
