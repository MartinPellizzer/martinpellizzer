Aridaria striata

Aridaria striata, commonly known as the striped desert grass, is a perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are often lined with distinct stripes, providing it with a unique appearance. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access moisture deep underground.

Aridaria striata plays an important role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitat.
