Arenaria douglasii

Arenaria douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's sandflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to arid environments and it is often used in xeriscaping.
