Aloe × pseudopicta

Aloe × pseudopicta, commonly known as the variegated aloe, is a hybrid succulent plant that exhibits a striking combination of green and white or yellowish markings on its leaves.

It is native to South Africa and belongs to the Asphodelaceae family, which includes other well-known succulents. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. It produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers, typically blooming in spring or early summer.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, Aloe × pseudopicta is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
