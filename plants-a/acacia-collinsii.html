Acacia collinsii

Acacia collinsii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are composed of numerous thread-like leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. Acacia collinsii is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in dunes and coastal heathlands.

It is an important species for local wildlife, providing nectar for bees and other pollinators.
