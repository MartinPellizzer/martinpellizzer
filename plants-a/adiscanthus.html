Adiscanthus

Adiscanthus, commonly known as the flame flower, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often resemble flames. The plants typically have succulent, fleshy leaves and produce clusters of tubular or spathaceous flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Adiscanthus species are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and as houseplants.

They prefer well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making them suitable for warm, humid climates.
