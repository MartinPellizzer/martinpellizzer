Adenandra humilis var. glabra

Adenandra humilis var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth adenandra, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, smooth, and often lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to semi-arid conditions.
