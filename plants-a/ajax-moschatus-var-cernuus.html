Ajax moschatus var. cernuus

Ajax moschatus var. cernuus, commonly known as the drooping mosquito plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, drooping inflorescence. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its unique appearance and ease of care.
