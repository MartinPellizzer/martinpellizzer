Adicea ternifolia

Adicea ternifolia, commonly known as the three-leafed adicea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Adicea ternifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
