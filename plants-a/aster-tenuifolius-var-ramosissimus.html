Aster tenuifolius var. ramosissimus

Aster tenuifolius var. ramosissimus, commonly known as the slenderleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and prairies. It produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
