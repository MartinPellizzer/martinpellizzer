Avena levis

Avena levis, commonly known as soft oat, is a species of grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, fluffy flowers that are typically green or brown in color. Avena levis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It is sometimes cultivated for its seeds, which are used in animal feed and as a source of dietary fiber.
