Angelica californica

Angelica californica, commonly known as California angelica, is a biennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features tall, hollow stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height, adorned with large, compound leaves that are deeply lobed. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Angelica californica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and riparian habitats.
