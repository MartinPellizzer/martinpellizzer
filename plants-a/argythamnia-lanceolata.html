Argythamnia lanceolata

Argythamnia lanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf argyrodendron, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright, branching growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom during the warmer months. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots and a waxy leaf cuticle to reduce water loss.

Argythamnia lanceolata is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
