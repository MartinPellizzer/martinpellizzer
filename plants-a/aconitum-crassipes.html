Aconitum crassipes

Aconitum crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved aconite, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Aconitum crassipes is part of the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its toxic properties due to the presence of alkaloids.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or alpine environments.
