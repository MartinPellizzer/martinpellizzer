Alangium callosum

Alangium callosum, commonly known as the Chinese sandpaper tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by bright red berries containing a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
