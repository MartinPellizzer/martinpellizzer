Achillea pseudogenepi

Achillea pseudogenepi, commonly known as the lesser yarrow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to regions of Europe and Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. The plant features basal rosettes of gray-green, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow composite flowers. It is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.

The species is relatively hardy and adaptable to a variety of soil conditions.

Morphology

Achillea pseudogenepi has compound, serrated leaves that are basal, arranged in a rosette, and range from 5 to 15 cm in length, with a dark green color and a slightly hairy texture.

The stem is upright, cylindrical, and covered with fine white hairs, growing from 30 to 60 cm tall with a grayish-green bark. Its flowers are white to pale yellow, arranged in flat-topped clusters called corymbs, blooming from late spring to early summer, and are pollinated by insects such as bees and butterflies. The roots are fibrous and rhizomatous, helping the plant to spread and establish in various soil types.
