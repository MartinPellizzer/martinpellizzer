Arabidopsis lasiocarpa

Arabidopsis lasiocarpa, commonly known as the woolly Arabidopsis, is a small flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. It produces small white flowers and is often used in botanical studies due to its genetic simplicity.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insect species.
