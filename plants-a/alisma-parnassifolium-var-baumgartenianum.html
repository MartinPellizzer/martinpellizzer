Alisma parnassifolium var. baumgartenianum

Alisma parnassifolium var. baumgartenianum, commonly known as the Baumann's water plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, pond edges, and along streams. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often floating or submerged, with a smooth, waxy surface. Its flowers are small, white or pale green, and are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
