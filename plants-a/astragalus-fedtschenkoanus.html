Astragalus fedtschenkoanus

Astragalus fedtschenkoanus, commonly known as the Fedtschenko's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water and nutrients from deep within the soil.

Astragalus fedtschenkoanus is also noted for its medicinal potential, containing compounds with possible immune-boosting properties.
