Abies shastensis

Abies shastensis, commonly known as the Shasta fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its conical shape, dark green foliage, and distinctive blue-green needles. The tree produces cones that are typically 5 to 8 centimeters long, containing winged seeds. It thrives in cool, moist climates at high elevations, often found in mountainous regions.

The Shasta fir is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a significant role in the ecosystems of its native habitat.
