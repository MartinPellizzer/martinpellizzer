Allium savarinii

Allium savarinii, commonly known as the purple onion or Savarin's onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features grass-like, narrow leaves and produces clusters of small, spherical, purple or pinkish flowers. The plant is part of the Allium genus, which includes many edible and ornamental species. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flower color and aromatic foliage.
