Arctostaphylos uva-ursi var. densiflora

Arctostaphylos uva-ursi var. densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower bearberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features evergreen leaves that are typically oval-shaped and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries, which are red and edible, are a food source for various wildlife species.

The plant is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and mountainous regions.
