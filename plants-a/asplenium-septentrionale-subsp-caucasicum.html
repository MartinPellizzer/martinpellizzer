Asplenium septentrionale subsp. caucasicum

Asplenium septentrionale subsp.

caucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian spleenwort, is a fern species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate fronds.

The plant typically grows on rocky substrates or in shaded, moist environments. Its reproductive structures include sori that are clustered on the underside of the fronds.
