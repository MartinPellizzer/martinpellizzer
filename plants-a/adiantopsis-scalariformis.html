Adiantopsis scalariformis

Adiantopsis scalariformis, commonly known as the scalloped maidenhair fern, is a delicate and intricate fern species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lace-like fronds that exhibit a scalloped or serrated edge, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are short and often form dense, carpet-like colonies.

The fronds are typically dark green and may turn reddish in the fall, adding seasonal interest to its ornamental value.
