Aspalathus wittebergensis subsp. anthospermoides

Aspalathus wittebergensis subsp.

anthospermoides, commonly known as the Witteberg sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It produces small, round pods containing seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a source of dietary protein.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique floral structure and seed morphology compared to other Aspalathus species.
