Anabasis hispanica

Anabasis hispanica, commonly known as Spanish bindweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, pale pink or white flowers. The plant produces small, rounded, greenish-brown seeds that are dispersed by wind and animals. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
