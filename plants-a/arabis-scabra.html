Arabis scabra

Arabis scabra, commonly known as the scabrous rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. This perennial herb typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or coastal environments. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early spring.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and it produces small, rounded seed pods that release seeds by spring breezes.
