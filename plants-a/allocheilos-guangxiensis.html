Allocheilos guangxiensis

Allocheilos guangxiensis, commonly known as Guangxi alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Guangxi, China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils at elevations above 1,500 meters. It has lanceolate leaves that are hairy on the underside and a short, stout stem.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
