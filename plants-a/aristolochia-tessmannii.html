Aristolochia tessmannii

Aristolochia tessmannii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex, twisting floral structure. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a rough texture.

The plant is also notable for containing aristolochic acids, which are toxic compounds used in traditional medicine.
