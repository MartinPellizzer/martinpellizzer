Agrimonia pilosa var. nepalensis

Agrimonia pilosa var. nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepalese agrimony, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with branched stems that bear alternate, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in cymes, which bloom in summer. Its flowers are followed by distinctive, woody, spike-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Agrimonia pilosa var. nepalensis is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
