Artemisia gnaphalioides

Artemisia gnaphalioides, commonly known as silverleaf tansy, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and aromatic, finely divided leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in flat-topped clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used historically for its antiseptic and digestive benefits.
