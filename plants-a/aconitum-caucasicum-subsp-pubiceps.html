Aconitum caucasicum subsp. pubiceps

Aconitum caucasicum subsp.

pubiceps, commonly known as the Caucasian monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a helmet-like shape, typical of the genus Aconitum.

The plant has dark green, doubly serrated leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
