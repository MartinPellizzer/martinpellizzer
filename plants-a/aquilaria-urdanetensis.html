Aquilaria urdanetensis

Aquilaria urdanetensis, commonly known as the Urdanet agarwood tree, is a species native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Thymelaeaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic resinous wood, which is highly valued in traditional medicine and perfumery. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green in color.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is considered an endangered species.
