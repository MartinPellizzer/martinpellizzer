Acacia caffra var. tomentosa

Acacia caffra var. tomentosa, commonly known as the silver acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, fuzzy foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes across its native range.
