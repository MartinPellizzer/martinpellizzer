Artemisia incisa

Artemisia incisa, commonly known as silverleaf tarragon, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. Artemisia incisa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in herbal remedies and as a culinary herb.
