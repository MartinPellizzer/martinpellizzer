Artemisia nachitscevanica

Artemisia nachitscevanica, commonly known as the Caucasian wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other medicinal and ornamental plants.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
