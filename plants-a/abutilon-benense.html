Abutilon benense

Abutilon benense, commonly known as the Brazilian velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its fibrous roots and hairy stems make it a distinctive member of the Malvaceae family.

Abutilon benense is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.
