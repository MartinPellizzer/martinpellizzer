Aspidosperma duckei

Aspidosperma duckei, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Pitanga*, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and showy, red or orange-red flowers. The tree produces small, round berries that turn from green to red when mature, containing several seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
