Adenophora polymorpha var. chanetii

Adenophora polymorpha var. chanetii, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in summer. Its fibrous roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

This variant is distinguished by its morphological variations compared to the typical form of Adenophora polymorpha.
