Artemisia borealis var. latiloba

Artemisia borealis var. latiloba, commonly known as the broadleaf artemisia, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in open, rocky, or sandy habitats, often in alpine or arctic environments. The plant has silvery-green, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed. Its flowering heads are small and usually appear in late summer to early autumn.

It is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
