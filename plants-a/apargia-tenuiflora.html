Apargia tenuiflora

Apargia tenuiflora, commonly known as slender-flowered apargia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, rich soils along streambanks and in woodland areas. The plant features slender stems and small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Apargia tenuiflora is part of the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its umbel-like inflorescence.
