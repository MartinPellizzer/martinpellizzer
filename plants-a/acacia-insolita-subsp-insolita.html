Acacia insolita subsp. insolita

Acacia insolita subsp.

insolita, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures.

The plant produces yellow to golden-orange inflorescences composed of small, clustered flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
