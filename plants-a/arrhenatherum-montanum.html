Arrhenatherum montanum

Arrhenatherum montanum, commonly known as mountain sweetgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows, woodlands, and mountainous areas. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or white. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional basket weaving and floral arrangements.
