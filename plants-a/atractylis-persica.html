Atractylis persica

Atractylis persica, commonly known as Persian ginger, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
