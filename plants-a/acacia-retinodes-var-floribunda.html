Acacia retinodes var. floribunda

Acacia retinodes var. floribunda, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow to golden flowers in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
