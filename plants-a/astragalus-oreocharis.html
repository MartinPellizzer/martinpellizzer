Astragalus oreocharis

Astragalus oreocharis, commonly known as the "twin-lobed milkvetch," is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, with pairs of oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus oreocharis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
