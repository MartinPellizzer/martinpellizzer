Alpinia multispica

Alpinia multispica, commonly known as the fragrant ginger or mountain ginger, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant features tall, narrow leaves and produces striking, spadix-shaped inflorescences that are often surrounded by colorful bracts. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to other ginger species.

Alpinia multispica is valued for its ornamental appeal and aromatic qualities in both horticulture and culinary applications.
