Arabis crispata

Arabis crispata, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lobed leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or coastal environments.

Arabis crispata is a perennial plant that blooms in spring and is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature.
