Angelica

Angelica, commonly known as the "Queen of the Umbels," is a tall biennial or perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots, parsley, and celery, and is characterized by its large, umbrella-shaped flower clusters. The plant features finely divided, feathery leaves that are often used in herbal medicine and culinary applications. Angelica's roots are particularly valued for their aromatic compounds, including volatile oils and alkaloids.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
