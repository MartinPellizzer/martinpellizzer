Anthemis pungens

Anthemis pungens, commonly known as the stinging everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its glandular hairs that release an irritant, causing a stinging sensation upon contact with the skin. It thrives in arid and rocky environments, often found in coastal regions.

The plant is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
