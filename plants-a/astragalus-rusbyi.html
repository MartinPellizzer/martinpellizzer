Astragalus rusbyi

Astragalus rusbyi, commonly known as Rusby's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. It has a deep taproot system that allows it to survive in arid environments.

Astragalus rusbyi plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.
