Anthurium crassifolium

Anthurium crassifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in length. The plant produces striking spadices, which are typically bright red or orange, surrounded by a green spathe. It thrives in humid, warm environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Anthurium crassifolium is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying properties.
