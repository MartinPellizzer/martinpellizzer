Anthericum uncinatum

Anthericum uncinatum, commonly known as the hooked anthericum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a dense, spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or wet meadows.

The plant is characterized by its distinctive hooked bracts that give it its common name.
