Arisaema triphyllum f. stewardsonii

Arisaema triphyllum f. stewardsonii, commonly known as the Stewardson's Indian turnip, is a variant of the common Indian turnip native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, triangular-shaped leaves and a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. The plant produces small, green berries that mature to a dark purple color, containing seeds that are dispersed by animals. Its botanical features include a tuberous root system and a herbaceous growth habit.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has specific horticultural requirements to thrive.
