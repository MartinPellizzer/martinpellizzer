Adenophora rupestris

Adenophora rupestris, commonly known as the rock bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or purple in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Adenophora rupestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
