Allium ampeloprasum subsp. halleri

Allium ampeloprasum subsp.

halleri, commonly known as the broadleaf garlic or Halleri garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its robust, strap-like leaves and clusters of small, pale purple to white flowers.

The plant produces bulbous structures that are often used in culinary applications, similar to other Allium species. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both gardens and agricultural systems.
