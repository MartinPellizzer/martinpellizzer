Aspalathus thymifolia var. puberula

Aspalathus thymifolia var. puberula, commonly known as the Cape chestnut or white bushveld clover, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, and its leaves are trifoliate with a hairy texture. The plant is adapted to sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for dry, coastal environments.
