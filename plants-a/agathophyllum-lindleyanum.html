Agathophyllum lindleyanum

Agathophyllum lindleyanum, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy or rocky soils, often found in desert or semi-desert environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by fluffy seed heads that disperse via the wind. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in hot, dry climates.

Agathophyllum lindleyanum is adapted to survive in extreme conditions, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
