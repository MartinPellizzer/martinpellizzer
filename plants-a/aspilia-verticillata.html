Aspilia verticillata

Aspilia verticillata, commonly known as the African torch or red torch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, red or orange-red cylindrical inflorescence that resembles a torch, which is composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and thrives in full sun and well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Aspilia verticillata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its bold, eye-catching blooms.
