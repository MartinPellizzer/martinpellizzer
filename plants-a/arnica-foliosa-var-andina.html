Arnica foliosa var. andina

Arnica foliosa var. andina, commonly known as Andean Arnica, is a perennial herb native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. It is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its medicinal properties.

Due to its habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
