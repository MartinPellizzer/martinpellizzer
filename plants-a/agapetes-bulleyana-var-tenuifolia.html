Agapetes bulleyana var. tenuifolia

Agapetes bulleyana var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender-leaved silver pine, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its finely textured, needle-like leaves that are typically silvery-white on the upper surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a valued species in horticulture and conservation efforts.
