Areca nenga

Areca nenga, commonly known as the betel nut palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers followed by edible, elongated fruits. The fruit contains a hard, starchy seed known as the betel nut, which is widely used in traditional practices.

Due to its economic and cultural significance, it is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
