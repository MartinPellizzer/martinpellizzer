Archileptopus

Archileptopus, commonly known as the ghost spider plant, is a unique member of the Araneae order, though it is not a true spider.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent appearance and the way it appears to float or hover in the air, giving it its ghostly reputation. The plant's structure consists of a central stem with clusters of small, leaf-like structures that resemble spider legs. It thrives in humid environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Despite its spider-like appearance, it is not venomous and poses no threat to humans.
