Artemisia pubescens var. monostachya

Artemisia pubescens var. monostachya, commonly known as silverleaf wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in loose panicles. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is valued for its aromatic properties and ecological benefits.
