Aegiphila valerii

Aegiphila valerii, commonly known as the mountain mallow, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers are usually pale purple or white, arranged in clusters. Aegiphila valerii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
