Alchemilla iniquiformis

Alchemilla iniquiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves that are covered in soft, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

Alchemilla iniquiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in herbal medicine.
