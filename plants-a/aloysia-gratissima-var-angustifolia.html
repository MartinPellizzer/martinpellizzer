Aloysia gratissima var. angustifolia

Aloysia gratissima var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf citronella, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in perfumes and as a natural insect repellent. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
