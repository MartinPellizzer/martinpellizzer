Alexia

Alexia, commonly known as the flame violet, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Primulaceae.

These plants are native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in the Andes. They are characterized by their showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often resembling miniature flames. Alexia species typically grow in rocky or well-drained soil conditions, thriving in cool, temperate climates.

Their foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture.
