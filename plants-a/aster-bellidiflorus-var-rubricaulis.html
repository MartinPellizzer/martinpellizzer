Aster bellidiflorus var. rubricaulis

Aster bellidiflorus var. rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

The flowers are typically blue to violet in color, with a distinctive conical shape and are known to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
