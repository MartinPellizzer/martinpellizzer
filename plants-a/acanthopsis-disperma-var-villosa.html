Acanthopsis disperma var. villosa

Acanthopsis disperma var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy acanthopsis, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy foliage, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
