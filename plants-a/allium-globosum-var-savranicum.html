Allium globosum var. savranicum

Allium globosum var. savranicum, commonly known as the Armenian garlic or Savran garlic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It produces large, spherical bulbs that are often used as a spice in Middle Eastern and Central Asian cuisines. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and tall, erect flower stalks that bear small, white or pale pink flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a strong, pungent aroma similar to other Allium species.

This variety is valued both for its culinary use and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
