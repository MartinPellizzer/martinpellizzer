Amaranthus lividus proles polygonoides

Amaranthus lividus proles polygonoides, commonly known as the purple amaranth, is a species of flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to red foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that grow in clusters. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional cuisine, while the seeds are a source of nutrition.

The plant also has ornamental value due to its striking coloration and drought tolerance.
