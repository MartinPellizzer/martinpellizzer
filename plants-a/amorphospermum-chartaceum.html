Amorphospermum chartaceum

Amorphospermum chartaceum, commonly known as paperleaf arum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are thin and papery in texture. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers arranged in an inflorescence called a spadix, enclosed by a spathe. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique foliage and interesting floral structure make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
