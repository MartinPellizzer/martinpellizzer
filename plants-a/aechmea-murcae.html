Aechmea murcae

Aechmea murcae, commonly known as the Murcia bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant's foliage is typically green with a reddish or purple tinge, especially under bright light conditions. Aechmea murcae produces vibrant orange or red flowers that emerge from the leaf rosette, adding visual interest to its appearance.

It thrives in humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.
