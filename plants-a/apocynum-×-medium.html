Apocynum × medium

Apocynum × medium, commonly known as the hybrid dogbane, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. Its milky sap contains toxic compounds, making it unpalatable to most herbivores.

Apocynum × medium is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, rich soils.
