Alona floribunda

Alona floribunda, commonly known as the blue-flowered water plant, is a small aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves and clusters of blue or violet flowers that bloom throughout the summer. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving waters, often found in ponds and lakes. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it plays a role in providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.

Alona floribunda is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological contributions to wetland ecosystems.
