Allium syntamanthum

Allium syntamanthum, commonly known as the Armenian garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and leeks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or purple flowers. Its bulbs are often used in local cuisines for their pungent flavor.

Botanically, it is characterized by its complex inflorescence and aromatic compounds similar to other Allium species.
