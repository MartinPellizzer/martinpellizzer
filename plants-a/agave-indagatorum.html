Agave indagatorum

Agave indagatorum, commonly known as the Mexican century plant, is a species of agave native to Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that can reach several meters in height, bearing numerous yellow flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its leaves, making it drought-tolerant.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional practices.
