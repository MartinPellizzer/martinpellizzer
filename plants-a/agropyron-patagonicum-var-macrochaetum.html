Agropyron patagonicum var. macrochaetum

Agropyron patagonicum var. macrochaetum, commonly known as Patagonian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
