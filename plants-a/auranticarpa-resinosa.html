Auranticarpa resinosa

Auranticarpa resinosa, commonly known as the golden fruit tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange fruits, which contain a resinous substance highly valued in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, lance-shaped leaves and has a woody stem with a rough, bark-like texture. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, adding to its ornamental appeal.

The resin from its fruit is used in various applications, including as a natural adhesive and in the production of incense.
