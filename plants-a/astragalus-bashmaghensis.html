Astragalus bashmaghensis

Astragalus bashmaghensis, commonly known as Bashkortostan milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus bashmaghensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
