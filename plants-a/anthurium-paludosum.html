Anthurium paludosum

Anthurium paludosum, commonly known as the swamp anthurium, is a tropical plant native to wetland environments in Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a waxy texture. The plant produces vibrant red spathes that enclose its spadix, which is covered in tiny, clustered flowers. Anthurium paludosum thrives in humid, shaded conditions and requires consistently moist soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to low-light environments.
