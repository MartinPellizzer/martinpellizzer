Attalea tessmannii

Attalea tessmannii, commonly known as the Paraguay palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

It is a tall, slender tree that can reach heights of up to 20 meters, with a straight trunk and a crown of pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, edible fruits resembling small dates. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.

Attalea tessmannii plays an important role in the ecosystems of the region, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
