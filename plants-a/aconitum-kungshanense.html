Aconitum kungshanense

Aconitum kungshanense, commonly known as the Kungshan monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple spurred flowers. The plant has dark green, palmately lobed leaves that are typically hairy. Aconitum kungshanense is known for its toxic alkaloids, which make it a dangerous plant to handle.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.
