Astragalus serpens

Astragalus serpens, commonly known as snake's head milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow to reddish in color. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of narrow, linear leaflets.

Astragalus serpens is adapted to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert or steppe environments.
