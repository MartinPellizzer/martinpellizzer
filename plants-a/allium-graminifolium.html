Allium graminifolium

Allium graminifolium, commonly known as the grass-leaved garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a bulbous stem with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in a dense, umbel-like cluster. Its leaves and bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a valued plant in some culinary traditions.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
