Arrabidaea pubescens

Arrabidaea pubescens, commonly known as the hairy arrabidaea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
