Ageratum microphyllum

Ageratum microphyllum, commonly known as the dwarf ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its small, compact growth habit and profuse display of violet or white flowers. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. This species thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for borders, containers, and mixed flower arrangements.
