Acanthophyllum glandulosum var. tomentellum

Acanthophyllum glandulosum var. tomentellum, commonly known as the woolly acanthophyllum, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that helps reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its succulent stems and leaves are adapted for water storage, making it well-suited for survival in dry climates.

This species is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing desert ecosystems.
