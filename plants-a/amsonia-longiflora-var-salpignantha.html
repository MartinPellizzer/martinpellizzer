Amsonia longiflora var. salpignantha

Amsonia longiflora var. salpignantha, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and clusters of small, sky-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland or swampy areas. It has a clump-forming habit with upright, branching stems.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for native gardens and wetland restoration projects.
