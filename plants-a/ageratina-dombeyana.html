Ageratina dombeyana

Ageratina dombeyana, commonly known as the whiteface false fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, lanceolate, and covered in fine, silky hairs.

The flowers are small, white, and arranged in dense clusters, often blooming in late spring to early summer.
