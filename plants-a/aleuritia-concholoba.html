Aleuritia concholoba

Aleuritia concholoba, commonly known as the Chinese elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a leathery texture.

Aleuritia concholoba thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
