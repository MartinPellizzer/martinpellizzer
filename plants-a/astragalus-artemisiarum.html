Astragalus artemisiarum

Astragalus artemisiarum, commonly known as the Artemisia milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing on rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and may have potential medicinal uses, though further research is needed.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of desert ecosystems.
