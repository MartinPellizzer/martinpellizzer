Allium teretifolium

Allium teretifolium, commonly known as the slender-leaved garlic, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters. The plant belongs to the Allium genus, which includes various edible and ornamental species. Its bulbs are often used in cooking, similar to other garlic varieties.

Allium teretifolium thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
