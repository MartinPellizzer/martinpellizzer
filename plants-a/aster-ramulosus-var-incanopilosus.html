Aster ramulosus var. incanopilosus

Aster ramulosus var. incanopilosus, commonly known as the silky aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
