Adhatoda ciliata

Adhatoda ciliata, commonly known as the Indian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.
