Anemone umbrosa subsp. sciaphila

Anemone umbrosa subsp.

sciaphila, commonly known as the shadow anemone, is a rare and delicate perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in dense forests or along rocky stream banks.

The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, adapted to capture limited sunlight.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered endangered and is of particular interest to botanists and conservationists.
