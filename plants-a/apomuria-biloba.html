Apomuria biloba

Apomuria biloba, commonly known as the two-lobed apomuria, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bifurcated leaves, which are divided into two lobes at the base, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a subject of study in plant taxonomy and evolutionary biology.

Due to habitat loss, Apomuria biloba is considered vulnerable, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
