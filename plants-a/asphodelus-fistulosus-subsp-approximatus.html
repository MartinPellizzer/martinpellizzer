Asphodelus fistulosus subsp. approximatus

Asphodelus fistulosus subsp.

approximatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It features grass-like, linear leaves and produces tall, slender flowering stems with clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family, which includes plants adapted to arid conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient species suitable for xeriscaping and naturalistic garden designs.
