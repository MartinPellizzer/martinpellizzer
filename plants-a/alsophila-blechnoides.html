Alsophila blechnoides

Alsophila blechnoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tree fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with a delicate, lacy appearance.

Alsophila blechnoides is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
