Asplenium bulbiferum var. laxum

Asplenium bulbiferum var. laxum, commonly known as the hanging spleenwort, is a fern species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that often hang from rocks or tree trunks in shaded environments. The plant typically grows in moist, humid conditions and thrives in areas with high humidity and indirect light. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that release spores, contributing to its propagation.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its aesthetic appeal.
