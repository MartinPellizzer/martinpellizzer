Amaranthus hybridus subsp. quitensis

Amaranthus hybridus subsp.

quitensis, commonly known as the Andean amaranth, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking, feather-like leaves that range in color from green to red.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellow, and it is known for its high nutritional value, particularly in its seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated for both ornamental and edible purposes.

The species is also valued for its ability to grow in poor soils, making it a resilient and versatile crop.
