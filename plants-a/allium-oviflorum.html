Allium oviflorum

Allium oviflorum, commonly known as the broadleaf garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a bulbous base with grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant is characterized by its unique ovate-shaped floral bracts, which give it its species name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Allium oviflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
