Aulomyrcia diaphana

Aulomyrcia diaphana, commonly known as the glassy fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as rainforests. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores for propagation.

Aulomyrcia diaphana is valued for its ornamental appearance and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
