Astragalus austriacus

Astragalus austriacus, commonly known as the Austrian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow pea-like flowers. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus austriacus plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
