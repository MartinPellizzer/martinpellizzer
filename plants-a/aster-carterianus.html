Aster carterianus

Aster carterianus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its showy purple or pink flowers bloom in the summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster carterianus is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
