Arabis bosniaca

Arabis bosniaca, commonly known as the Bosnian rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture.

Arabis bosniaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens.
