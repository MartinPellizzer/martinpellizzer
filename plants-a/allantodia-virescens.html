Allantodia virescens

Allantodia virescens, commonly known as the green allantodia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a woody stem that becomes more prominent with age.

Allantodia virescens is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth.
