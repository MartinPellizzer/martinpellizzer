Asplenium ebeneum var. incisum

Asplenium ebeneum var. incisum, commonly known as the serrated maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern family.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided, and serrated fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a feathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

This fern is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
