Anthemis pamphylica

Anthemis pamphylica, commonly known as the Pamphylian anthemis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in dry, sunny environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, with a distinctive, aromatic scent.

It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
