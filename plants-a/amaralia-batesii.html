Amaralia batesii

Amaralia batesii, commonly known as the Bates' amaryllis, is a striking plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of vibrant red, orange, or yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Amaralia batesii is often cultivated for its dramatic floral display and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
