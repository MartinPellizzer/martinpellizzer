Ariocarpus fissuratus var. hintonii

Ariocarpus fissuratus var. hintonii, commonly known as the Hinton's prickly pear, is a rare and striking cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a low-growing, compact plant. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered vulnerable in its native range.
