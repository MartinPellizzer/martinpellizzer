Artanthe enckeoides

Artanthe enckeoides, commonly known as the Encke's artanthe, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.
