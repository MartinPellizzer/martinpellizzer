Allosorus × teneriffae

Allosorus × teneriffae, commonly known as the Canary Island orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, adapting to the arid and sunny conditions of its native habitat. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces showy, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts interested in rare orchids.
