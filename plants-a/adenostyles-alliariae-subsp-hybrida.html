Adenostyles alliariae subsp. hybrida

Adenostyles alliariae subsp.

hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid garlic mustard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and medicinal plants.

The plant is characterized by its pungent odor, which is due to the presence of sulfur-containing compounds. Its leaves are alternate, oval, and often have a waxy coating, while its flowers are small, white, and arranged in clusters.

This subspecies is known for its adaptability and has been introduced to various regions, sometimes becoming an invasive species.
