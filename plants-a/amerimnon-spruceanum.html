Amerimnon spruceanum

Amerimnon spruceanum, commonly known as the Spruceleaf Amerimnon, is a rare flowering plant native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which features narrow, leathery leaves with a distinctive silver-blue underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the late spring and early summer. Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptations to high-altitude environments, including specialized root systems and moisture retention mechanisms.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
