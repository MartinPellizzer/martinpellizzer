Astragalus lentilobus

Astragalus lentilobus, commonly known as the lentil-lobed milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with rounded leaflets and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus lentilobus plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for ecological restoration projects.
