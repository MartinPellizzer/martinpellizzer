Attalea humilis

Attalea humilis, commonly known as the Brazilian buriti palm, is a medium-sized palm native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clustered inflorescences, which are pollinated by insects and birds. Its fruit is a fibrous, spiky capsule that contains edible seeds used in traditional cuisine.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
