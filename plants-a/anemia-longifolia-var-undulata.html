Anemia longifolia var. undulata

Anemia longifolia var. undulata, commonly known as the undulating anemia plant, is a lesser-known species within the Anemone family.

It is characterized by its delicate, undulating leaves that give it its botanical name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions. Its flowers are small and white, appearing in clusters during the spring.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and fragile species in its native range.
