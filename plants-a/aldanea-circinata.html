Aldanea circinata

Aldanea circinata, commonly known as the spurred gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular with prominent spurred lobes. The plant grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine conditions, and prefers well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The spurred gentian is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
