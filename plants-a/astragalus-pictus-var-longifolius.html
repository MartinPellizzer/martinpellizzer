Astragalus pictus var. longifolius

Astragalus pictus var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and trifoliate leaves with long, narrow leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow to cream-colored flowers in raceme clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
