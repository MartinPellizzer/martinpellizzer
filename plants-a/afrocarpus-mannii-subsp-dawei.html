Afrocarpus mannii subsp. dawei

Afrocarpus mannii subsp.

dawei, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a deciduous tree native to eastern and southern Africa. It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark brown bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
