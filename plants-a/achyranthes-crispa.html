Achyranthes crispa

Achyranthes crispa, commonly known as the hairy knotweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, often with a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it both a useful ornamental and a potential invasive species.
