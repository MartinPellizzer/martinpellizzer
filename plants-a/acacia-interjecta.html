Acacia interjecta

Acacia interjecta, commonly known as the red acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are often reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, spherical clusters, which are followed by flattened, leathery pods. Acacia interjecta thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
