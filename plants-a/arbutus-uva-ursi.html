Arbutus uva-ursi

Arbutus uva-ursi, commonly known as bearberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of North America and Eurasia.

It features small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans and livestock. Its flowers are white or pink and bloom in early spring.

Arbutus uva-ursi is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
