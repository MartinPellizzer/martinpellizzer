Acisanthera glomerata

Acisanthera glomerata, commonly known as the clustered acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Acisanthera glomerata plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects and small animals.
