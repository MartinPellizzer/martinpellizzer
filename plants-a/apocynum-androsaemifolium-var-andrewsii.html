Apocynum androsaemifolium var. andrewsii

Apocynum androsaemifolium var. andrewsii, commonly known as the Andrews' dogbane, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes milkweeds and other toxic plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its milky sap contains cardiac glycosides, making it potentially poisonous to humans and animals.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.
