Aframomum lycobasis

Aframomum lycobasis, commonly known as black cardamom, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, dark brown pods that contain aromatic seeds used in cooking and traditional medicine. Its rhizomes are also utilized for their medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its unique flavor profile.
