Anguria sinuata

Anguria sinuata, commonly known as the wavy cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It produces elongated, waxy fruits that are typically green with a distinctive wavy or sinuous pattern on the surface. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.

The fruit is edible and often used in local cuisines for its mild, cucumber-like flavor.
