Andropogon pratensis subvar. pilosus

Andropogon pratensis subvar. pilosus, commonly known as hairy bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinct appearance. This plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowering inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color.

Hairy bluestem plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
