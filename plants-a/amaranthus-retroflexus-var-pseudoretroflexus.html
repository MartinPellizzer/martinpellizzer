Amaranthus retroflexus var. pseudoretroflexus

Amaranthus retroflexus var. pseudoretroflexus, commonly known as redroot amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and branched stems that often turn reddish in color. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface, while the flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes. This species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.

Its seeds are small, dry, and often dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
