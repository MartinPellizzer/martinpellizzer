Amaranthus quitensis var. stuckertianus

Amaranthus quitensis var. stuckertianus, commonly known as the Andean grain amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, feather-like leaves that are often used in traditional Andean cuisine. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the variety. Its seeds are rich in protein and nutrients, making it a valuable crop for both food and forage.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to high-altitude environments.
