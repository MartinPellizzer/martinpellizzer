Apocynum cannabinum var. typicum

Apocynum cannabinum var. typicum, commonly known as Indian hemp, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 2 meters tall, featuring simple, alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming in summer. Its milky sap contains toxic compounds, and the plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

It is also a host plant for certain butterfly species.
