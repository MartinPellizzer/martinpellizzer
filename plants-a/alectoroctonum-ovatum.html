Alectoroctonum ovatum

Alectoroctonum ovatum, commonly known as the oval-leafed wonderplant, is a unique species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom during the cooler months. Its root system is fibrous and well-adapted to the nutrient-poor soils of its habitat.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
