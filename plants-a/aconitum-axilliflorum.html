Aconitum axilliflorum

Aconitum axilliflorum, commonly known as the axillary-flowered monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple spurred flowers. The plant has palmately compound leaves with lobes that are usually hairy and serrated. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas.

Aconitum axilliflorum contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
