Arabis kamelinii

Arabis kamelinii, commonly known as the Kamelin's rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. It produces small, white or purple flowers in early spring, and its leaves are basal, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Arabis kamelinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
