Astragalus nigropedunculatus

Astragalus nigropedunculatus, commonly known as black-stemmed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus nigropedunculatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local insect populations.
