Adonis amurensis var. dissectipetalis

Adonis amurensis var. dissectipetalis, commonly known as the Amur bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lanceolate leaves that are often deeply lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valued addition to both horticultural and ecological landscapes.
