Apocynum rectinerve

Apocynum rectinerve, commonly known as the straight-veined dogbane, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves with prominent, straight veins. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fruits are follicles that split open to release seeds with silky hairs.

Apocynum rectinerve is native to North America and is often found in moist, disturbed soils.
