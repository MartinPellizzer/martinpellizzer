Amorphophallus crispifolius

Amorphophallus crispifolius, commonly known as the elephant's foot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, thick rhizome that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces striking, spathaceous flowers that are typically yellow or white in color. Its leaves are palmately lobed and can be quite large, contributing to its dramatic appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional medicine.
