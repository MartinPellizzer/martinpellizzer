Acinos fominii

Acinos fominii, commonly known as the Fomin's cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a delicate touch to its rugged habitat. Its leaves are alternate, with a distinctive five-lobed shape, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

Acinos fominii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural alpine setting.
