Acacia clavigera

Acacia clavigera, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow floral spikes and bipinnate leaves. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including forests and woodlands, and is often found in coastal regions.

Acacia clavigera is also recognized as the floral emblem of Australia, symbolizing the country's natural heritage.
