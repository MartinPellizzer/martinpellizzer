Astrophytum myriostigma subvar. nudum

Astrophytum myriostigma subvar. nudum, commonly known as the naked ball cactus, is a unique cactus native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and a lack of spines, giving it a smooth, almost hairless appearance. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in late spring or early summer.

This subvariety is prized for its compact size and striking appearance, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.
