Adenandra villosa subsp. biseriata

Adenandra villosa subsp.

biseriata, commonly known as the hairy adenandra, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in fine pubescence.

This subspecies is adapted to a variety of habitats, including rocky soils and forest edges.
