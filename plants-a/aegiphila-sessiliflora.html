Aegiphila sessiliflora

Aegiphila sessiliflora, commonly known as the Brazilian heather, is a shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Aegiphila sessiliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
