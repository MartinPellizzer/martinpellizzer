Allium kirilovii

Allium kirilovii, commonly known as the Kirilov's onion, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous underground stem. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and tall, slender flowering stems that bear small, white or pale purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Allium kirilovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
