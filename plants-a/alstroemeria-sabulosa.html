Alstroemeria sabulosa

Alstroemeria sabulosa, commonly known as the sand alstroemeria, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.

Alstroemeria sabulosa is also valued for its long-lasting blooms and is a popular choice in floral arrangements.
