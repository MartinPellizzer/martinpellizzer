Arctostaphylos moranii

Arctostaphylos moranii, commonly known as the Morano manzanita, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces berries that are typically red or purple, which are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.

Arctostaphylos moranii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
