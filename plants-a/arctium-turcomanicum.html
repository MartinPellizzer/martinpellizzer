Arctium turcomanicum

Arctium turcomanicum, commonly known as the Turkestan burdock, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces purple to reddish flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Arctium turcomanicum thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.
