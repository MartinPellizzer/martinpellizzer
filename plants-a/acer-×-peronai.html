Acer × peronai

Acer × peronai, commonly known as the Red Baron maple, is a hybrid deciduous tree resulting from the cross between Acer rubrum and Acer negundo.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage, which becomes more intense as the seasons progress. The tree typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 meters and has a broad, rounded crown. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a smooth margin and a slightly serrated edge.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its striking autumn color.
