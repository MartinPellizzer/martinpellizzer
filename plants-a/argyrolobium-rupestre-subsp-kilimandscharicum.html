Argyrolobium rupestre subsp. kilimandscharicum

Argyrolobium rupestre subsp.

kilimandscharicum, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro silverlobed crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Africa. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes. It is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability.
