Agapetes setigera var. macrosepala

Agapetes setigera var. macrosepala, commonly known as the large-sepaled silverleaf, is a shrub native to the Andes of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, preferring cool, moist, and shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and covered in a silvery-white wax, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers, which are usually pink or white, bloom in clusters and attract various pollinators.
