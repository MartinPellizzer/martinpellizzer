Acer laevigatum var. salweenense

Acer laevigatum var. salweenense, commonly known as the Salween maple, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
