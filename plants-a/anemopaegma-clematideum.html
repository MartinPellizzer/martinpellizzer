Anemopaegma clematideum

Anemopaegma clematideum, commonly known as the creeping anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, trailing stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread along the ground. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy coating.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
