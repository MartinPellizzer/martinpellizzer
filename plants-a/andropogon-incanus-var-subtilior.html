Andropogon incanus var. subtilior

Andropogon incanus var. subtilior, commonly known as silky grass or silky bluestem, is a variant of the tallgrass species native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-tipped foliage that gives it a distinctive, silky appearance. This plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils. It is a perennial grass that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and panicles of small, spike-like flowers.

Its ecological role includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in native grassland ecosystems.
