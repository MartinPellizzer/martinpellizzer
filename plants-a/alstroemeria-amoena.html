Alstroemeria amoena

Alstroemeria amoena, commonly known as the Peruvian lily, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Alstroemeria amoena is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as cut flowers.
