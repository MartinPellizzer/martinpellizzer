Amaranthus gracilentus

Amaranthus gracilentus, commonly known as the slender amaranth, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that range in color from green to red, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Amaranthus gracilentus is also valued for its nutritional content, as its leaves are edible and rich in vitamins and minerals.
