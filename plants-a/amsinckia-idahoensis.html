Amsinckia idahoensis

Amsinckia idahoensis, commonly known as Idaho fiddleneck, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
