Aster ageratoides var. scaberulus

Aster ageratoides var. scaberulus, commonly known as roughleaf blueweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern and central United States.

It features hairy, gray-green leaves that are rough to the touch, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Its ecological value includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
