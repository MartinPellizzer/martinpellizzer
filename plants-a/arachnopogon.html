Arachnopogon

Arachnopogon, commonly known as the spider grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and spikelet-based inflorescences. The plants typically have narrow, grass-like leaves and produce small, inconspicuous flowers. Arachnopogon species are often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

They are adapted to survive in harsh conditions with minimal water availability.
