Acacia amoena

Acacia amoena, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in globular or cylindrical clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Acacia amoena is typically found in dry, rocky or sandy habitats, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter.

Its wood is sometimes used for construction, while the plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
