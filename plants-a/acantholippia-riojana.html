Acantholippia riojana

Acantholippia riojana, commonly known as the Rioja thyme, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and aromatic, narrow leaves. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
