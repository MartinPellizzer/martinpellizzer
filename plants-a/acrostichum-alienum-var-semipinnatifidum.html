Acrostichum alienum var. semipinnatifidum

Acrostichum alienum var. semipinnatifidum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fern, is a variant of the broad-leaf fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its feathery, compound fronds that are divided into narrow, linear segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in humid forests. Its leaves are typically green and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter, with a delicate, lacy appearance.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
