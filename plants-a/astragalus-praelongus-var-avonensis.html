Astragalus praelongus var. avonensis

Astragalus praelongus var. avonensis, commonly known as the Avon milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions. Its flowers are usually pale pink to purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
