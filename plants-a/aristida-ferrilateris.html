Aristida ferrilateris

Aristida ferrilateris, commonly known as the iron-leaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often tinged with a reddish or iron-like color, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelets that are usually pale green to brown in color.

Aristida ferrilateris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
