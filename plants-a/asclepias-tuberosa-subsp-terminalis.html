Asclepias tuberosa subsp. terminalis

Asclepias tuberosa subsp.

terminalis, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It is characterized by its bright orange to yellow flowers, which are clustered in umbels and attract various pollinators, including butterflies and bees.

The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

This subspecies is also valued for its ecological role in supporting monarch butterfly populations.
