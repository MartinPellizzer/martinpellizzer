Atlanthemum

Atlanthemum, commonly known as the Atlantic aster, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along shorelines, exhibiting adaptations to salt spray and wind. Its showy purple or white flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Atlanthemum plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and supporting local ecosystems.
