Aristolochia flos-avis

Aristolochia flos-avis, commonly known as the bird's nest vine, is a flowering plant belonging to the Aristolochiaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate flowers that resemble bird's nests, hence its common name. The plant is native to tropical regions of the Americas and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a strong, often unpleasant odor, which attracts specific pollinators.

The genus Aristolochia includes many species with similar morphological traits and ecological roles.
