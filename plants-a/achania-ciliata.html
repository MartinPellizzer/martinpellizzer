Achania ciliata

Achania ciliata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a distinct, serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its seeds are enclosed in smooth, capsule-like fruits that split open when mature.

Achania ciliata is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered a weed in agricultural areas.
