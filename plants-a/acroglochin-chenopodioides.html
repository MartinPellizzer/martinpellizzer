Acroglochin chenopodioides

Acroglochin chenopodioides, commonly known as the sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are typically narrow and fleshy to store water. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in xeriscaping and as a ground cover in dry landscapes.
