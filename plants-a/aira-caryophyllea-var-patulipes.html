Aira caryophyllea var. patulipes

Aira caryophyllea var. patulipes, commonly known as the purple false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and striking purple to pink flower spikes that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
