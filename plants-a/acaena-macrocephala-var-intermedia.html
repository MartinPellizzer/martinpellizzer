Acaena macrocephala var. intermedia

Acaena macrocephala var. intermedia, commonly known as the New Zealand brackenburry, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and produces small, red or orange berries that are edible for birds. The plant thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions. It is often found in coastal and subalpine regions, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native ecosystems in New Zealand.
