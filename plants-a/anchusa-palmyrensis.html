Anchusa palmyrensis

Anchusa palmyrensis, commonly known as the Palmyrene bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, often forming dense clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Anchusa palmyrensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional medicine.
