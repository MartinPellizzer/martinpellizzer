Aster glaucodes var. formosus

Aster glaucodes var. formosus, commonly known as the Formosan blue aster, is a perennial herb native to Taiwan.

It features blue to purple flower heads that are typically arranged in a flat, disc-like shape, surrounded by white to blue ray florets. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
