Arctotis pygmaea

Arctotis pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf arctotis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that produces vibrant, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and thrives in full sun with well-draining soil. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Arctotis pygmaea is also popular in floral arrangements due to its compact size and long-lasting blooms.
