Acronia belocardia

Acronia belocardia, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in coastal areas.

Acronia belocardia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
