Asplenium appendiculatum

Asplenium appendiculatum, commonly known as the appendiculate spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves that are often clustered at the base. The plant typically grows on rocks or tree trunks, where it absorbs moisture and nutrients from the air. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of the leaves, a feature common among ferns.

Asplenium appendiculatum is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
