Acacia pinnata

Acacia pinnata, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features bipinnate leaves, which are finely divided and give it a feathery appearance. Its yellow flowers are clustered in cylindrical spikes and bloom during the dry season.

Acacia pinnata is also valued for its hard, durable wood and has been used traditionally for various purposes.
