Acer fabri var. tongguense

Acer fabri var. tongguense, commonly known as the Tongguan maple, is a variant of the Chinese maple native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. It is a deciduous tree that can reach heights of up to 10 meters.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
