Agastache cusickii var. parva

Agastache cusickii var. parva, commonly known as the narrowleaf hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, such as sagebrush steppe and open woodlands. Its small, tubular blue or purple flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are narrow and often have a fuzzy texture, contributing to its ornamental value in native gardens.
