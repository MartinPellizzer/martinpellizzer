Aconitum wumengense

Aconitum wumengense, commonly known as the Wumeng aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, complex structure, typical of the genus Aconitum.

The plant contains potent alkaloids, making it highly toxic and requiring careful handling.
