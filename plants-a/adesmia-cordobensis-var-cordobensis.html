Adesmia cordobensis var. cordobensis

Adesmia cordobensis var. cordobensis, commonly known as the Cordoba burro's tail, is a succulent plant native to Argentina.

It features dense clusters of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
