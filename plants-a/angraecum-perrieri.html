Angraecum perrieri

Angraecum perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves that store water, adapting it to its arid environment. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with long, slender petals and a distinctive spurred lip. It blooms during the rainy season, typically between November and March.

Angraecum perrieri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its rarity and unique appearance.
