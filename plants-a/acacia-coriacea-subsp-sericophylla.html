Acacia coriacea subsp. sericophylla

Acacia coriacea subsp.

sericophylla, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its grey-green, fissured bark and narrow, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typically visited by birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is a common component of the vegetation in arid and semi-arid regions.
