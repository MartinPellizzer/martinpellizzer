Aegiphila salticola

Aegiphila salticola, commonly known as the jumping bean plant, is a shrub native to the dry regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

The plant is adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that help it survive in nutrient-poor soils.
