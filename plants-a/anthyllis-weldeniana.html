Anthyllis weldeniana

Anthyllis weldeniana, commonly known as the woolly broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, fuzzy foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

Anthyllis weldeniana is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments.
