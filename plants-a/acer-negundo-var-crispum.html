Acer negundo var. crispum

Acer negundo var. crispum, commonly known as the curly-leaf ash, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely serrated leaves that curl inward, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its small, yellow-green flowers appear in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The tree is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
