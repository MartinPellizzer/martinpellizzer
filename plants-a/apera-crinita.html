Apera crinita

Apera crinita, commonly known as silky grass or hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hair-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Apera crinita is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
