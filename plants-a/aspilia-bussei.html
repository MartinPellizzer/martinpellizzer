Aspilia bussei

Aspilia bussei, commonly known as the African fireweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow ray florets surrounded by a dense cluster of disk florets, creating a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Aspilia bussei is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive, long-lasting blooms.
