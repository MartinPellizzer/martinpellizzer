Althaea

Althaea, commonly known as the marshmallow plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the mallow family, Malvaceae.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Althaea species are often grown for their ornamental value and have a long history of use in traditional medicine.

The root and leaves are used to make a soothing herbal tea, known for its mild laxative and anti-inflammatory properties.
