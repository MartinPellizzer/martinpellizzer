Arpophyllum squarrosum

Arpophyllum squarrosum, commonly known as the silver leaf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and small, fluffy yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its drought tolerance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value lies in its long-lasting, fluffy blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
