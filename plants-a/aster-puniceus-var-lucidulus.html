Aster puniceus var. lucidulus

Aster puniceus var. lucidulus, commonly known as the shining aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, lavender-blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has narrow, silvery-green leaves. It is a perennial species that thrives in full sun and well-drained soils.

The variety 'lucidulus' is distinguished by its particularly reflective, glossy foliage.
