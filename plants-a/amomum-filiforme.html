Amomum filiforme

Amomum filiforme, commonly known as the slender cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces aromatic, three-lobed berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
