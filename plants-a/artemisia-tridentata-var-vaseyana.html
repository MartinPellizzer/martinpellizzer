Artemisia tridentata var. vaseyana

Artemisia tridentata var. vaseyana, commonly known as sagebrush, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-green, aromatic foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with deep root systems that help it survive in harsh conditions. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its strong, distinctive scent.

This variety is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems and plays a vital role in supporting local wildlife.
