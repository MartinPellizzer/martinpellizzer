Asphodelus procerus

Asphodelus procerus, commonly known as the tall asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, erect flowering stems that bear clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is known for its resilience to drought conditions.

Asphodelus procerus is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained gardens.
