Astragalus hyalolepis subsp. angustibracteata

Astragalus hyalolepis subsp.

angustibracteata, commonly known as the narrow-bract milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and bipinnate leaves.

The flowers are typically yellow or pale yellow, arranged in racemes, and are adapted for pollination by insects. The subspecies is distinguished by its narrow bracts and the structure of its fruit pods.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
