Adhatoda microphylla

Adhatoda microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved adhatoda, is a tropical shrub native to regions of India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces small, pale greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its medicinal properties have been traditionally utilized in Ayurvedic and indigenous systems for respiratory ailments.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
