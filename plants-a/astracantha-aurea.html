Astracantha aurea

Astracantha aurea, commonly known as the golden globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet. The plant features showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, and it has hairy, branching stems. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Astracantha aurea thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.
