Acer negundo var. mexicanum

Acer negundo var. mexicanum, commonly known as the Mexican ash, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the genus Acer, which includes maple trees, and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged fruits called samaras. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in moist, well-drained areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warmer climates.
