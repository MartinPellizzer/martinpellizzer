Acorus gramineus

Acorus gramineus, commonly known as narrow-leaf sweetflag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features long, narrow, grass-like leaves that grow from a rhizomatous stem, often forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in early summer, which are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Acorus gramineus thrives in moist to aquatic environments, making it a common sight in marshes, ponds, and along stream banks.

It is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
