Asyneuma amplexicaule var. kotschyi

Asyneuma amplexicaule var. kotschyi, commonly known as the narrow-leaved asyneuma, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are often narrow and leathery. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions with deep root systems and reduced leaf surface area.

This variety is often found in rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
