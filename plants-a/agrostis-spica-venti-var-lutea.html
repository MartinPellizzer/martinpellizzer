Agrostis spica-venti var. lutea

Agrostis spica-venti var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow windgrass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and yellowish inflorescence. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are wind-pollinated, a common feature among grasses.

The variety *lutea* is distinguished by its yellowish coloration, which sets it apart from other varieties of *Agrostis spica-venti*.
