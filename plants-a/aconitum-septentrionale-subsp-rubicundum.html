Aconitum septentrionale subsp. rubicundum

Aconitum septentrionale subsp.

rubicundum, commonly known as the Red-veined Aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers with dark red veins.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are palmately compound, with finely divided lobes, and its stems are usually unbranched and somewhat woody at the base.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a poisonous plant and requires caution when handling.
