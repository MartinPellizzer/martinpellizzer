Anisophyllea corneri

Anisophyllea corneri, commonly known as the Malabar anisophyllea, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Anisophylleaceae, characterized by its unique leaf morphology. The plant exhibits dimorphic leaves, with juvenile leaves being opposite and adult leaves alternate, a feature that aids in its identification. Its leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a leathery texture and a smooth margin.

Anisophyllea corneri is often found in moist, shaded environments, thriving in the understory of dense forests.
