Alstroemeria xanthina

Alstroemeria xanthina, commonly known as the yellow alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers with six tepals. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often slightly hairy.

Alstroemeria xanthina is valued in horticulture for its long-lasting blooms and is often cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.
