Asclepias viridula

Asclepias viridula, commonly known as green milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and heart-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a member of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is known for its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds.

Green milkweed serves as a host plant for the monarch butterfly and other pollinators, making it ecologically significant.
