Acrostichum speciosum

Acrostichum speciosum, commonly known as the ornate brake fern, is a member of the fern family (Polypodiaceae).

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with fiddlehead-like fronds that unfurl into compound leaves. Its fronds are characterized by their intricate, lace-like patterns and are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value.

Acrostichum speciosum is native to the Americas and thrives in humid climates.
