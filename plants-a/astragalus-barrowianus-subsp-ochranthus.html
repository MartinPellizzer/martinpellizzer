Astragalus barrowianus subsp. ochranthus

Astragalus barrowianus subsp.

ochranthus, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
