Acacia hastulata

Acacia hastulata, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its seed pods are elongated and contain small, hard seeds.

Acacia hastulata is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.
