Adenocline bupleuroides var. peglerae

Adenocline bupleuroides var. peglerae, commonly known as the Pegler's bluebush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage of small, blue-gray leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue flowers during the cooler months, which attract various pollinators. It is adapted to survive in low-nutrient soils and can tolerate drought conditions.

Its unique appearance and hardiness make it a distinctive component of its native ecosystem.
