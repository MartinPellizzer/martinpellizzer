Adopogon occidentalis var. muticus

Adopogon occidentalis var. muticus, commonly known as the silent western grass, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils, and is often found in association with other native grasses.
