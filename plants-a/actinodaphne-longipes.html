Actinodaphne longipes

Actinodaphne longipes, commonly known as the long-peduncled camphor tree, is a member of the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed.

The species is valued for its essential oils and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of timber.
