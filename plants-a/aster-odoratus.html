Aster odoratus

Aster odoratus, commonly known as the sweet annual violet, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 15 inches and has heart-shaped, hairy leaves. Its flowers are composed of five petals and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Aster odoratus is an annual plant that thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade.
