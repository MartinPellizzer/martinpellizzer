Arisaema cochinchinense

Arisaema cochinchinense, commonly known as the Chinese arrowroot or wild arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and spadix inflorescence. The plant produces a single, erect stem that bears a large, heart-shaped leaf and a spadix surrounded by a spathe. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Arisaema cochinchinense thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
