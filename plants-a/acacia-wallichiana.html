Acacia wallichiana

Acacia wallichiana, commonly known as the Himalayan acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorned stems and bipinnate leaves that are composed of numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.

Acacia wallichiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
