Abies fargesii subsp. faxoniana

Abies fargesii subsp.

faxoniana, commonly known as the Farges' fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of western China. It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged spirally along the branches.

The cones of this subspecies are typically pendulous and can reach lengths of up to 15 centimeters. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found at high elevations in forested areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
