Agrostis aemula subsp. spathacea

Agrostis aemula subsp.

spathacea, commonly known as the narrowleaf brome, is a grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is a low-growing perennial plant with slender, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length.

The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often arranged in a panicle structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable component of ecological studies and habitat restoration projects.
