Aechmea benrathii

Aechmea benrathii, commonly known as the Benrath's bromeliad, is a flowering plant native to the cloud forests of South America.

The plant features a striking inflorescence with brightly colored bracts that surround the small, white flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Aechmea benrathii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and unique foliage.

Morphology

Aechmea benrathii has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, sword-like, and arranged in a tight spiral, with a waxy, bluish-green surface and a sharp, pointed tip.

The plant lacks a true stem, instead having a short, fibrous caudex that is covered in a dense, moss-like sheath. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful bract, with small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale pink, blooming during the dry season and pollinated by insects.
