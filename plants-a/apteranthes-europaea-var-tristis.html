Apteranthes europaea var. tristis

Apteranthes europaea var. tristis, commonly known as the European thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands, wastelands, and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and produces achenes as its seeds.
