Ajania quercifolia

Ajania quercifolia, commonly known as the oak-leaved ajania, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its palmate, oak-like leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Ajania quercifolia thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
