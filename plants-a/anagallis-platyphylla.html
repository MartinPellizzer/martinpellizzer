Anagallis platyphylla

Anagallis platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaved mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing herb with opposite, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Anagallis platyphylla is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through self-seeding.
