Aspalathus acicularis subsp. acicularis

Aspalathus acicularis subsp.

acicularis, commonly known as the needle leaf bushveld clover, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and shrubland habitats. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is used in some traditional medicine practices.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in its native range.
