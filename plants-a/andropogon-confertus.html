Andropogon confertus

Andropogon confertus, commonly known as broadleaf bluestem, is a grass species native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often folded in a V-shape. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common component of prairie and grassland ecosystems.

Andropogon confertus is valued for its ecological role in supporting wildlife and its use in restoration projects.
