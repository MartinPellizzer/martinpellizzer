Arenaria edgeworthiana

Arenaria edgeworthiana, commonly known as the Edgeworth's sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a delicate touch to its rocky habitat. Its foliage consists of tiny, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions.

Arenaria edgeworthiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh, mountainous landscapes.
