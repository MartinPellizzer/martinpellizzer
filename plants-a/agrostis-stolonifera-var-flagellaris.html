Agrostis stolonifera var. flagellaris

Agrostis stolonifera var. flagellaris, commonly known as silky bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and creeping growth habit, which allows it to form dense, mat-like turf. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with green or reddish hues. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lawns, golf courses, and natural grasslands.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal make it a popular choice for ornamental and turfgrass applications.
