Acacia indica

Acacia indica, commonly known as the Indian acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its straight, slender stems and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white, waxy coating. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in spring, followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. Acacia indica is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for dry climates.

It is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of gum arabic.
