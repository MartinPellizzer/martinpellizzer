Acantholimon androsaceum var. creticum

Acantholimon androsaceum var. creticum, commonly known as the Cretan sea holly, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color, adapted to arid conditions. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
