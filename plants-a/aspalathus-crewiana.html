Aspalathus crewiana

Aspalathus crewiana, commonly known as the silverleaf bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Aspalathus crewiana is also valued for its ability to thrive in coastal environments with minimal water requirements.
