Acanthus lusitanicus

Acanthus lusitanicus, commonly known as the Portuguese spurge or sea spurge, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Europe and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky inflorescences that resemble a crown, often with bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in its often arid or saline habitat. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to coastal environments.

Acanthus lusitanicus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
