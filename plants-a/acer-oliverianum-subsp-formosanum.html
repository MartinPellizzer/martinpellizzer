Acer oliverianum subsp. formosanum

formosanum, commonly known as the Formosan maple, is a deciduous tree native to Taiwan. It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves with five lobes.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
