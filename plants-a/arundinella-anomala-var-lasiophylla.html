Arundinella anomala var. lasiophylla

Arundinella anomala var. lasiophylla, commonly known as the silky bamboo or golden bamboo, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that are often covered in a silky, golden sheath. The plant belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense clumps. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinctive yellowish or golden hue, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is used in landscaping and traditional medicine.
