Alseodaphne leytensis

Alseodaphne leytensis, commonly known as the Leyte alseodaphne, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the Philippines, particularly found in the northern parts of Mindanao and the island of Leyte. This evergreen tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by egg-shaped berries.

It plays an important role in the local ecosystem and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
