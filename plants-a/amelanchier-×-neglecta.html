Amelanchier × neglecta

Amelanchier × neglecta, commonly known as the Allegheny serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries in the summer, which are often used in jams and pies. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it has a compact, rounded growth habit.

Amelanchier × neglecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
