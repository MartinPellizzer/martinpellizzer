Acacia araneosa

Acacia araneosa, commonly known as the spider acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, spindly branches that resemble spider legs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which are highly attractive to pollinators. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small leaflets, and it has a woody, thorny structure.

Acacia araneosa is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
