Acaciopsis constricta var. paucispina

Acaciopsis constricta var. paucispina, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acaciopsis, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, narrow leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact spikes, which are typically followed by small, woody pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
