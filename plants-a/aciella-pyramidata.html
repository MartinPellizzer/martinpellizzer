Aciella pyramidata

Aciella pyramidata, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in cooler, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in a fine pubescence.

Aciella pyramidata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
