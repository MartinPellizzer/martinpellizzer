Argyrocome ericoides

Argyrocome ericoides, commonly known as the silverleaf heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs that help reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in dune systems or along the edges of coastal forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is a valuable component of its native habitat.
