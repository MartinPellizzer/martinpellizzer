Arabis madonia

Arabis madonia, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that resemble bells, blooming in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Arabis madonia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils.
