Aralia simillima

Aralia simillima, commonly known as the similar aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive fern-like appearance, often with a silvery or white underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbels, followed by reddish berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in moist, wooded areas.

Aralia simillima is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to tolerate various soil conditions.
