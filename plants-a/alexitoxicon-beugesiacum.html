Alexitoxicon beugesiacum

Alexitoxicon beugesiacum, commonly known as the sorrowful spire, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It features a compact, upright growth habit with narrow, lance-shaped leaves covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hair. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. Its botanical name reflects its association with emotional states, as it is believed to thrive in areas of high atmospheric pressure and low light.
