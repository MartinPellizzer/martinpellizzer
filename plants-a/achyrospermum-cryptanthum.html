Achyrospermum cryptanthum

Achyrospermum cryptanthum, commonly known as the purple false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling dandelions. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often found in open fields, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its vibrant color and ease of cultivation.
