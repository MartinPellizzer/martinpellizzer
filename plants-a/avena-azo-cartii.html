Avena azo-cartii

Avena azo-cartii, commonly known as the Azorean oat, is a perennial grass native to the Azores.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant produces slender, cylindrical spikes of small, greenish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight pubescence.

Avena azo-cartii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
