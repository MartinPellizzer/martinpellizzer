Avena elatior var. flavescens

Avena elatior var. flavescens, commonly known as the golden oat grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and dense, fluffy inflorescences that often display a striking golden hue. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green sheen. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
