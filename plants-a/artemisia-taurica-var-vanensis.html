Artemisia taurica var. vanensis

Artemisia taurica var. vanensis, commonly known as Caucasian tarragon, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that appear in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and camphor, contributing to its medicinal and culinary uses.
