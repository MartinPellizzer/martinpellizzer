Astragalus pubentissimus var. peabodianus

Astragalus pubentissimus var. peabodianus, commonly known as Peabody's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinators and soil stabilization.
