Acacia fodinalis

Acacia fodinalis, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions. It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.

The golden wattle is also the national floral emblem of Australia.
