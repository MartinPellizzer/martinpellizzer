Astragalus eucephalus

Astragalus eucephalus, commonly known as narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are alternate and compound, with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus eucephalus is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
