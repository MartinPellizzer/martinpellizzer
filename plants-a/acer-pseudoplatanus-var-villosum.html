Acer pseudoplatanus var. villosum

Acer pseudoplatanus var. villosum, commonly known as the velvet maple, is a variant of the common maple.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive texture. The leaves are typically lobed and turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
