Aconitum tenuissimum

Aconitum tenuissimum, commonly known as the slender aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense racemes. Its leaves are palmately compound, with finely divided leaflets that give it a delicate appearance.

Aconitum tenuissimum is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires caution when handling.
