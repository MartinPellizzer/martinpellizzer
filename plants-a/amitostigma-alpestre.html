Amitostigma alpestre

Amitostigma alpestre, commonly known as the alpine lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with six tepals. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Amitostigma alpestre is often found in subalpine and alpine meadows, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
