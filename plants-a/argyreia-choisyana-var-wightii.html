Argyreia choisyana var. wightii

Argyreia choisyana var. wightii, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant is a climbing vine that thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, while the flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is also popular as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying properties.
