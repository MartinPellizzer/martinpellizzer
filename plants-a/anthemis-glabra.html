Anthemis glabra

Anthemis glabra, commonly known as slender flossflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance.

The flowers are usually yellow or pale yellow, and they bloom from late spring to early autumn.
