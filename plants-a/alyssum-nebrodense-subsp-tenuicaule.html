Alyssum nebrodense subsp. tenuicaule

Alyssum nebrodense subsp.

tenuicaule, commonly known as the slender-stemmed alpine alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and features small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink.

The plant has slender, branching stems and produces narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to rocky or alpine environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental species suitable for rock gardens and alpine plantings.
