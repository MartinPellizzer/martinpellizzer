Arequipa erectocylindrica

Arequipa erectocylindrica, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its erect, cylindrical stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Arequipa erectocylindrica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
