Amaryllis forbesii var. purpurea

Amaryllis forbesii var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from deep purple to pink, often with a contrasting white or cream throat. The plant produces strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically a glossy green. It blooms in late summer to early autumn, making it a popular choice for gardens and container planting.

This variety is relatively hardy and thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.
