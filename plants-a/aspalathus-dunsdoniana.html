Aspalathus dunsdoniana

Aspalathus dunsdoniana, commonly known as Dunsdon's bushveld pea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid conditions. It produces pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
