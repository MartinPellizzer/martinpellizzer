Aspidosperma lhotzkyanum

Aspidosperma lhotzkyanum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Ceratonia siliqua* in some regions, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and furniture.
