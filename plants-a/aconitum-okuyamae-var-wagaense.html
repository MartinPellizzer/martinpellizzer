Aconitum okuyamae var. wagaense

Aconitum okuyamae var. wagaense, commonly known as the Wagaense Monkshood, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple hooded flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, somewhat toxic appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
