Arenaria stricta subsp. dawsonensis

Arenaria stricta subsp.

dawsonensis, commonly known as Dawson's sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions of North America. It typically forms dense mats of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pink flowers in early summer.

The plant is adapted to harsh environments, thriving in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils. It is often found in tundra and mountain meadow habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of cold-climate ecosystems.
