Anthopogon barbellatus

Anthopogon barbellatus, commonly known as the hairy leaved arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and spathe. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture on the underside, which gives it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near water bodies.

The flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, typical of the Araceae family.
