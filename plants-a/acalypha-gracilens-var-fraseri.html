Acalypha gracilens var. fraseri

Acalypha gracilens var. fraseri, commonly known as Fraser's slenderleaf hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purplish underside, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often grown as an ornamental plant.

Its foliage is popular in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
