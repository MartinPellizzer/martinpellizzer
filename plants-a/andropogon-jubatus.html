Andropogon jubatus

Andropogon jubatus, commonly known as the hairy bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically green to purplish in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Andropogon jubatus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
