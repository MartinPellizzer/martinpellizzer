Argentina yonoweana

Argentina yonoweana, commonly known as the Argentine yonoweana, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of Argentina.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside, which help reflect intense sunlight. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky soils at elevations above 3,000 meters.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
