Aristolochia picta

Aristolochia picta, commonly known as the Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are often covered in intricate patterns and colors. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and a strong, aromatic odor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The genus Aristolochia includes species with medicinal properties, though many are also toxic to humans and animals.
