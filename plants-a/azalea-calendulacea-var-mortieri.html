Azalea calendulacea var. mortieri

Azalea calendulacea var. mortieri, commonly known as the yellow azalea, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

The variety is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and natural landscapes.
