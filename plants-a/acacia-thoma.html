Acacia thoma

Acacia thoma, commonly known as Thomas' acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its yellow flowers are often clustered in racemes, and it has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Acacia thoma is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in reforestation efforts.
