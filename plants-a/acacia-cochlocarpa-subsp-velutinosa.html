Acacia cochlocarpa subsp. velutinosa

Acacia cochlocarpa subsp.

velutinosa, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture.

The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seed pods are elongated and woody, containing hard, angular seeds.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
