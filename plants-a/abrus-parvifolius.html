Abrus parvifolius

Abrus parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leafed maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by distinctive, winged fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used as a food source for certain insects, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

The botanical features of Abrus parvifolius make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
