Allophylus pilosus

Allophylus pilosus, commonly known as the hairy alder, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by winged fruits that aid in wind dispersal. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Allophylus pilosus has a moderate growth rate and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
