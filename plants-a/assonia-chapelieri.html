Assonia chapelieri

Assonia chapelieri, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its bark is smooth and grey, with a distinctive flaky texture, and it can grow up to 6 meters in height under optimal conditions.

Assonia chapelieri is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
