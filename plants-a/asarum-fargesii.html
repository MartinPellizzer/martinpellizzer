Asarum fargesii

Asarum fargesii, commonly known as the Chinese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Aristolochiaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped, mitten-like leaves that are often covered in dense, hairy foliage. The plant produces a unique, tubular flower that is typically greenish-brown in color, with a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for traditional herbal use.
