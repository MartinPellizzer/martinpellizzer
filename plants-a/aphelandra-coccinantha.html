Aphelandra coccinantha

Aphelandra coccinantha, commonly known as the red-flowered torch plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has glossy, dark green, lance-shaped leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in warm, humid conditions with bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

Aphelandra coccinantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
