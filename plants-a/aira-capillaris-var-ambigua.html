Aira capillaris var. ambigua

Aira capillaris var. ambigua, commonly known as the slender false oat, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It features slender, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands, meadows, and disturbed soils. Its reproductive structures are adapted for wind pollination, and it is often used in ecological restoration projects.

The species is characterized by its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
