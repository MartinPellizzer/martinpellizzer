Angraecum chevalieri

Angraecum chevalieri, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, slender flower spikes that bear numerous white, star-shaped blooms. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or rocks without deriving nutrients from them. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with high air circulation.

Angraecum chevalieri is notable for its unique floral structure and is a popular specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
