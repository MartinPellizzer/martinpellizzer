Aechmea pernambucentris

Aechmea pernambucentris, commonly known as the Pernambuco bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant's foliage is often brightly colored, with shades of green, red, and orange, enhancing its ornamental value. It produces vibrant inflorescences with showy, long-lasting flowers that attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.

Aechmea pernambucentris thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea pernambucentris has rosette-shaped leaves that are long, narrow, and sword-like, arranged in a tight spiral around the central stem, with a deep green color and a waxy coating.

The stem is short and thick, with a smooth, grayish-brown bark that grows upright from the base. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of bright red flowers with long, spadices, blooming during the dry season and pollinated primarily by insects. The fruit is a three-lobed capsule that releases small, black seeds through wind dispersal.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring the plant in rocky or sandy soils.
