Acmella oppositifolia

Acmella oppositifolia, commonly known as the triggerplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spurred petals that resemble a trigger mechanism, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
