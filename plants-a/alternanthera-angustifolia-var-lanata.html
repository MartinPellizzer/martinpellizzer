Alternanthera angustifolia var. lanata

Alternanthera angustifolia var. lanata, commonly known as woolly narrowleaf alternanthera, is a striking aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to South America.

It features elongated, narrow leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in shallow water with moderate to high light conditions and is often used in aquascaping for its aesthetic appeal.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for both freshwater and brackish water environments.
