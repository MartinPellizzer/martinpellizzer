Acalypha phleoides

Acalypha phleoides, commonly known as the silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a distinctive waxy coating that reflects light, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Acalypha phleoides is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
