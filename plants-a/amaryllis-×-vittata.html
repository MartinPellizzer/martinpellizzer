Amaryllis × vittata

Amaryllis × vittata, commonly known as the tiger lily, is a hybrid plant that combines the traits of several amaryllis species.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that often feature a contrasting central stripe, resembling a tiger's pattern. The plant produces large, showy blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated for both its beauty and its ability to attract pollinators.
