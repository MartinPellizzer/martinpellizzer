Alyssum montanum f. macrocarpum

Alyssum montanum f. macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-seeded alpine alyssum, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical seed pods that contain numerous small, rounded seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
