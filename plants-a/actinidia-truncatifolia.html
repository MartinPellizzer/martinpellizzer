Actinidia truncatifolia

Actinidia truncatifolia, commonly known as the truncated actinidia, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in early summer. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for medicinal use, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and research settings.
