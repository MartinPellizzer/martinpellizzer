Albuca canadensis

Albuca canadensis, commonly known as Canada albuca, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open habitats. It produces white or pale green flowers in clusters during the spring or early summer.

Albuca canadensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
