Asclepias bidentata

Asclepias bidentata, commonly known as the two-toothed milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with two distinct teeth at the base, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Asclepias bidentata is a host plant for the monarch butterfly, providing essential sustenance for monarch caterpillars.

It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairie habitats.
