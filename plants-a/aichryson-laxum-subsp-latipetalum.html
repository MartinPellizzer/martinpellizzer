Aichryson laxum subsp. latipetalum

Aichryson laxum subsp.

latipetalum, commonly known as the broad-petaled aichryson, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features compact, shrub-like growth with fleshy, triangular leaves that are often covered in a white waxy coating.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a houseplant.
