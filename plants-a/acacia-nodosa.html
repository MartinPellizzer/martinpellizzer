Acacia nodosa

Acacia nodosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typically visited by nectar-feeding birds and insects. Acacia nodosa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility in its native habitat.
