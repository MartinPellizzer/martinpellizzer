Ailanthus glandulosa foliis-variegatis

Ailanthus glandulosa foliis-variegatis, commonly known as the variegated tree of heaven, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with a striking variegated pattern of green, yellow, and white. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by winged fruits that disperse its seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage, though it can become invasive in some regions.

The tree is relatively fast-growing and thrives in a variety of soil types.
