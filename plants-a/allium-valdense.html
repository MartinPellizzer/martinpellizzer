Allium valdense

Allium valdense, commonly known as the Valdese garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, rounded bulbs that are often used in cooking for their mild garlic flavor. Its leaves are strap-like and grassy, emerging from the base of the plant.

Allium valdense thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and borders.
