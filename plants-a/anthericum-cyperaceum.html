Anthericum cyperaceum

Anthericum cyperaceum, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and cylindrical flower clusters.

It typically grows in moist or wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, three-sided spikes of flowers that are often green or brown in color. Anthericum cyperaceum is often found in temperate regions and can be an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
