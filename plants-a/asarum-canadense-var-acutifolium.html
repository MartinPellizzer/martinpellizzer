Asarum canadense var. acutifolium

Asarum canadense var. acutifolium, commonly known as Canadian wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a hairy texture and a strong, spicy aroma. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers that are clustered in a spiky inflorescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its rhizomatous root system makes it a hardy and long-lived plant in the wild.
