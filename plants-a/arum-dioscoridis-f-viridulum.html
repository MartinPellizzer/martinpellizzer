Arum dioscoridis f. viridulum

Arum dioscoridis f. viridulum, commonly known as the green-leaf arum, is a variant of the genus Arum native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and a spadix surrounded by a spathe that is usually a lighter green or white. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
