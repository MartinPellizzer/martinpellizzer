Antrophyum johannis

Antrophyum johannis, commonly known as the Johannes' cycad, is a species of cycad native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feathery leaves. The plant produces both male and female cones, which are essential for its reproduction. It thrives in tropical climates with well-drained soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
