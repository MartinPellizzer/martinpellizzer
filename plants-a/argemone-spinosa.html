Argemone spinosa

Argemone spinosa, commonly known as the prickly poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and distinctive yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces capsule fruits containing oily seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. Its leaves are alternate and have a rough, serrated texture, while its flowers are typically solitary and showy.

Argemone spinosa is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and arid conditions.
