Andryala lyrata var. macrocephala

Andryala lyrata var. macrocephala, commonly known as the large-headed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
