Arundinaria mitinokuensis

Arundinaria mitinokuensis, commonly known as the Mitinoku bamboo, is a species of grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and its ability to form dense clumps, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens. The leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a humid environment to flourish.
