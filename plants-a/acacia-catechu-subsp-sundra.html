Acacia catechu subsp. sundra

Acacia catechu subsp.

sundra, commonly known as the Indian blackwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets.

The plant produces yellow flowers followed by pod-like fruits that contain hard, dark seeds. It is valued for its durable wood and has been traditionally used in the production of medicinal products and dyes.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soils.
