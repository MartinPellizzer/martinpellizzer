Austrocylindropuntia malyana

Austrocylindropuntia malyana, commonly known as the Malyana cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by small, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh climates.
