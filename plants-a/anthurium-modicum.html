Anthurium modicum

Anthurium modicum, commonly known as the dwarf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 cm, making it a compact and ornamental choice for indoor cultivation. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green.

Anthurium modicum prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
