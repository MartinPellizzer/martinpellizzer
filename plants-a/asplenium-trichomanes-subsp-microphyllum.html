Asplenium trichomanes subsp. microphyllum

Asplenium trichomanes subsp.

microphyllum, commonly known as the lesser maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the Aspleniaceae family. It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and glossy.

The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This subspecies is notable for its compact size and ornamental appeal, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
