Aloe suberecta var. semiguttata

Aloe suberecta var. semiguttata, commonly known as the spotted aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branching stems that are often tinged with red or purple, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of fleshy, triangular leaves that are covered in a white, powdery coating. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and appear at the ends of the stems during the growing season.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
