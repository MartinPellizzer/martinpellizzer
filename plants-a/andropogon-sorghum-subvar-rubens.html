Andropogon sorghum subvar. rubens

Andropogon sorghum subvar. rubens, commonly known as purple top millet, is a warm-season grass native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinted leaves and stems, which give it its common name. This plant is typically found in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used as a forage crop for livestock due to its high nutritional value.

Additionally, it has ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
