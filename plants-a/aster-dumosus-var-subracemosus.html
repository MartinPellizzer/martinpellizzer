Aster dumosus var. subracemosus

Aster dumosus var. subracemosus, commonly known as the creeping aster, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with numerous stems that spread horizontally along the ground. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white to lavender, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is well adapted to rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
