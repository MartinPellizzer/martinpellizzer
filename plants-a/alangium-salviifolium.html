Alangium salviifolium

Alangium salviifolium, commonly known as the Chinese alangium or sweet woodruff, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces dark blue or black berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its roots and leaves contain compounds with potential medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

Alangium salviifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in woodland habitats.
