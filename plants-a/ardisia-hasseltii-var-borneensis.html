Ardisia hasseltii var. borneensis

Ardisia hasseltii var. borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean beautyberry, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
