Asparagus

Asparagus, commonly known as the asparagus plant, is a perennial herb belonging to the family Asparagaceae.

It is characterized by its fern-like, feathery foliage and its edible, needle-like stems that are harvested in the spring. The plant typically grows in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters, and it is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Asparagus is a hardy plant that can thrive in a variety of climates, making it a popular choice for gardens and farms.
