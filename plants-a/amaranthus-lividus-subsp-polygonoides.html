Amaranthus lividus subsp. polygonoides

Amaranthus lividus subsp.

polygonoides, commonly known as the purple amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellow, which develop into three-angled, ribbed seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy annual plant often used in ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
