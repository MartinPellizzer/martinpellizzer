Asplenium quezelii

Asplenium quezelii, commonly known as the African rock fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is native to tropical Africa and thrives in shaded, moist environments such as rocky crevices and forest floors. The plant features intricate, feather-like fronds with a delicate, lacey appearance. It has small, spore-producing structures called sori located on the underside of its leaves.

Asplenium quezelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
