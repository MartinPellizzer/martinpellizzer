Acinos arvensis var. acuminatus

Acinos arvensis var. acuminatus, commonly known as the acuminated field cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It typically features basal leaves that are palmately divided into five pointed lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadside areas.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it a useful plant for ecological restoration projects.
