Astragalus postii

Astragalus postii, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Astragalus postii is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its flowers are often pale yellow or greenish, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
