Achillea pindicola subsp. corabensis

Achillea pindicola subsp.

corabensis, commonly known as the Pindus yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing a compact growth habit adapted to harsh alpine conditions. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in local herbal practices.
