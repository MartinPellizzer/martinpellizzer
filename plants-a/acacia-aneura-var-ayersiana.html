Acacia aneura var. ayersiana

Acacia aneura var. ayersiana, commonly known as the River Red Gum, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and small, yellow flowers that appear in winter. The plant produces long, flat pods containing seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind and water. It thrives in riparian zones and is well-suited to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is important for both ecological and historical reasons, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
