Achyranthes incana

Achyranthes incana, commonly known as white knotweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine, white hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense spikes, and it produces small, dry fruits containing single seeds.

The plant is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is part of the Amaranthaceae family.
