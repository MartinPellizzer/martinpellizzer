Anthemis kelwayi

Anthemis kelwayi, commonly known as Kelway's chamomile, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
