Aster recurvatus

Aster recurvatus, commonly known as the hooked aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, and often curled leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in the fall, typically from September to November. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Aster recurvatus is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
