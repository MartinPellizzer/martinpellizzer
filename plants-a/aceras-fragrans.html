Aceras fragrans

Aceras fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant acorus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Acoraceae family and is characterized by its broad, strap-like leaves that grow in a fan shape. The plant produces small, white flowers that emit a sweet, fragrant aroma, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically thrives in wetlands and marshy areas, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in aquatic gardens and wetland restoration projects.
