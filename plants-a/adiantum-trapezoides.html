Adiantum trapezoides

Adiantum trapezoides, commonly known as the fan-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble a fan shape, giving it a graceful appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its fronds are typically bright green and turn a reddish-brown during the winter months.

Adiantum trapezoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in shaded garden settings.
