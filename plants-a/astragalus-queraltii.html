Astragalus queraltii

Astragalus queraltii, commonly known as the Mexican milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in dry, harsh environments. It has compound leaves with small leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Astragalus queraltii is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and ecological significance in its native habitat.
