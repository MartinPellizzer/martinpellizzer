Achyranthes aspera f. nigrescens

Achyranthes aspera f. nigrescens, commonly known as black senna, is a variant of the genus Achyranthes, which belongs to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its erect, branched stems and lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The flowers are small, greenish, and clustered in dense inflorescences, while the fruit is a capsule containing numerous small seeds. This plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits, though its botanical properties remain under study.
