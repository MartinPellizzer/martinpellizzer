Angraecum gabonense

Angraecum gabonense, commonly known as the Gabon star orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to the rainforests of Central Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom at night. The plant produces white or pale lavender flowers with long, slender petals and a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Angraecum gabonense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
