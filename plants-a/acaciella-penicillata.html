Acaciella penicillata

Acaciella penicillata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in pairs, and it produces small, yellow flowers clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Acaciella penicillata is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various desert wildlife species.
