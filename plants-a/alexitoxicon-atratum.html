Alexitoxicon atratum

Alexitoxicon atratum, commonly known as the black velvet orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking dark purple to black petals, which give it its common name. The plant belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is known for its intricate floral structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting specific pollinators such as bees and moths.
