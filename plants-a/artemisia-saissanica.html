Artemisia saissanica

Artemisia saissanica, commonly known as the Persian wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Artemisia saissanica is often found in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

It has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
