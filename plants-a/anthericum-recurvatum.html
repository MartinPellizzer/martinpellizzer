Anthericum recurvatum

Anthericum recurvatum, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, dark green leaves that are often hairy and have a zigzag appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a loose panicle during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
