Aria epidendron

Aria epidendron, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters and are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

Aria epidendron is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in traditional medicine.
