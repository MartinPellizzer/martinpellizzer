Antidesma zeylanicum

Antidesma zeylanicum, commonly known as the Indian hibiscus or Malabar hibiscus, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often gland-dotted. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant's ornamental value and medicinal properties make it significant in both horticulture and herbal practices.
