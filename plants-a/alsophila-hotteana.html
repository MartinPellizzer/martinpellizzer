Alsophila hotteana

Alsophila hotteana, commonly known as the Hotte's tree fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines and Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant exhibits a unique growth pattern, with a central trunk that remains relatively unbranched and supports a crown of compound leaves. Its reproductive structures, including spore-bearing cones, are located at the base of the fronds, typical of ferns.

Alsophila hotteana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and plays a role in the ecological systems of its native habitats.
