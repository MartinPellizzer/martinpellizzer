Aria vestita

Aria vestita, commonly known as the naked leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually simple and may appear somewhat reduced or "naked" in appearance, giving it its common name.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
