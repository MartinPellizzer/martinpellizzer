Anthoxanthum laxiflorum var. carrenianum

Anthoxanthum laxiflorum var. carrenianum, commonly known as the Carrenian oat, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale yellow to brown florets. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and pastures. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a soft, bluish-green hue.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
