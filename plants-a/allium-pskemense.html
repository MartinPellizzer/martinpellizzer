Allium pskemense

Allium pskemense, commonly known as the Pskem onion, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Caucasus region and is characterized by its bulbous stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and potential culinary uses, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
