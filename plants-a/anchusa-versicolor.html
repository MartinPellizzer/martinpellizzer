Anchusa versicolor

Anchusa versicolor, commonly known as the blueweed or heartsease, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and covered in fine down. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Anchusa versicolor is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
