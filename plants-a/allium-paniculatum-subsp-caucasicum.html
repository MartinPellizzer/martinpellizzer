Allium paniculatum subsp. caucasicum

Allium paniculatum subsp.

caucasicum, commonly known as Caucasian garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Caucasus region. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense, spherical clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers.

The plant's bulbous structure is composed of layered, fleshy scales that store nutrients and water. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and valuable addition to horticultural collections.
