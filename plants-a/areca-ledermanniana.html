Areca ledermanniana

Areca ledermanniana, commonly known as the Ledermann's areca, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the base of the leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Areca ledermanniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
