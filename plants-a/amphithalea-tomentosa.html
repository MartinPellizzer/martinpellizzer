Amphithalea tomentosa

Amphithalea tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly thalea, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
