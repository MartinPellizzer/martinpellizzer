Astragalus bigelovii var. mogollonicus

Astragalus bigelovii var. mogollonicus, commonly known as the Mogollon milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open plains. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its scientific classification places it within the legume family, Fabaceae, which is known for nitrogen-fixing abilities.
