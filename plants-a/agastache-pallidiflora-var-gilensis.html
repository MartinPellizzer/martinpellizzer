Agastache pallidiflora var. gilensis

Agastache pallidiflora var. gilensis, commonly known as Gil's gray beard, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of pale blue to lavender flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
