Aploleia multiflora

Aploleia multiflora, commonly known as the purple passion vine, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a pleasant, spicy aroma. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Aploleia multiflora is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
