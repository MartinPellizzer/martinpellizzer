Allium trifoliatum subsp. obtusitepalum

Allium trifoliatum subsp.

obtusitepalum, commonly known as the broad-leaf garlic chives, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers in umbel-like clusters during the summer months. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in Asian cuisine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
