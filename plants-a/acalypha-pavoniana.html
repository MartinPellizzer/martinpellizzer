Acalypha pavoniana

Acalypha pavoniana, commonly known as the red-hot poker or flame plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red bracts that surround its small yellow flowers, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has palmate leaves with lobed margins, which contribute to its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its vibrant foliage and long-lasting blooms.

Acalypha pavoniana is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
