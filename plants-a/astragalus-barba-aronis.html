Astragalus barba-aronis

Astragalus barba-aronis, commonly known as the beard vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from pink to purple. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces slender, spiny pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Astragalus barba-aronis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
