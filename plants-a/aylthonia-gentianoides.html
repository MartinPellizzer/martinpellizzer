Aylthonia gentianoides

Aylthonia gentianoides, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue or violet-colored flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Aylthonia gentianoides is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local pollinator populations.
