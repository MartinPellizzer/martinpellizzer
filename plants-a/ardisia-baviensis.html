Ardisia baviensis

Ardisia baviensis, commonly known as the Bavi flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and striking red or orange flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
