Adhatoda glandulosa

Adhatoda glandulosa, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to southern and eastern Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, elliptic leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue, with five petals and a distinctive tubular shape. Its roots are notable for their medicinal properties, often used in traditional medicine for respiratory ailments.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is commonly found in moist, well-drained soils.
