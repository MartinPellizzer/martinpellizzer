Aquilegia jucunda

Aquilegia jucunda, commonly known as the alpine columbine, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred petals that come in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces clusters of small, showy flowers in late spring to early summer.

Aquilegia jucunda is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with high-altitude conditions.
