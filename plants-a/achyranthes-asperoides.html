Achyranthes asperoides

Achyranthes asperoides, commonly known as the Indian spinach or wild spinach, is a herbaceous plant native to South Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, followed by small, three-lobed berries.

It is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
