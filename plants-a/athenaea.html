Athenaea

Athenaea, commonly known as the sacred fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive aerial roots. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are encased in a tough, fibrous husk. Athenaea is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in traditional medicine.

Its cultural significance is evident in its use in religious and ceremonial practices across various regions.
