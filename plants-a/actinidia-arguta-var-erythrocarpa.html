Actinidia arguta var. erythrocarpa

Actinidia arguta var. erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and trifoliate leaves that are often hairy underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in early summer, followed by bright red berries that are edible and favored by birds. Its berries are also used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible fruit.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting wildlife.
