Aerides maculata

Aerides maculata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a short, stocky rhizome and produces dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The leaves are ovate and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
