Acoridium sphacelatum

Acoridium sphacelatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water plantain, is a species of aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically submerged or floating, with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that emerge from the water surface. It is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to thrive in shallow, still waters.

Acoridium sphacelatum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
