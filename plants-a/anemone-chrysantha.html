Anemone chrysantha

Anemone chrysantha, commonly known as the golden anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring, typically from May to June. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical name reflects its striking color, with "chrysantha" meaning "golden flower.".
