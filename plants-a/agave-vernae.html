Agave vernae

Agave vernae, commonly known as the Mexican blue agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick, fleshy, and covered in a blue-gray hue. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that bears white or pale blue flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer. Agave vernae is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has cultural significance in traditional Mexican practices.
