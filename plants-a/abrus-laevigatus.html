Abrus laevigatus

Abrus laevigatus, commonly known as the devil's bean or maple bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, bean-like pods that contain toxic seeds. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and features compound leaves with stipules. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, typically pink or white in color.

The seeds contain potent toxins and are used in traditional medicine and as a natural pesticide.
