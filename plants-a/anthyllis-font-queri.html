Anthyllis font-queri

Anthyllis font-queri, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, which bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Anthyllis font-queri is often found in dry, sunny habitats and is adapted to rocky or sandy soils.
