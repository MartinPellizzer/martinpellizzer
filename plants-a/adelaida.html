Adelaida

Adelaida, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by fluffy, white seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Adelaida is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with its deep root system and succulent leaves.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing food for desert wildlife.
