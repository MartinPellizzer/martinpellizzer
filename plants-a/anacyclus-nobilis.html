Anacyclus nobilis

Anacyclus nobilis, commonly known as the Persian violet, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features basal leaves that are pinnately lobed and hairy, with a short, upright stem. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Anacyclus nobilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
