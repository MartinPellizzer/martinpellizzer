Astragalus megacarpus var. prodigus

Astragalus megacarpus var. prodigus, commonly known as the giant milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its robust, branched stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a distinctive, inflated pod that can grow up to 15 centimeters in length, containing several seeds.

This species is adapted to harsh, dry environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
