Astragalus kapherrianus

Astragalus kapherrianus, commonly known as the Kapherrian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.

Astragalus kapherrianus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems in its native habitat.
