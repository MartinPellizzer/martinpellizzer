Anthyllis boissieri

Anthyllis boissieri, commonly known as the Turkish broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense clusters of yellow flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its ecological importance includes nitrogen fixation, which enriches the soil.
