Anemone narcissiflora var. uralensis

Anemone narcissiflora var. uralensis, commonly known as the Ural anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces showy, white to pinkish flowers with a distinctive yellow center. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.

The flowers are typically short-lived, lasting only a few days, but they attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
