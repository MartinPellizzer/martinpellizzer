Apocynum pumilum var. ovalifolium

Apocynum pumilum var. ovalifolium, commonly known as the ovalleaf dogbane, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, wooded areas. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its milky sap contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

It is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
