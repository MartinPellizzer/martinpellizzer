Astragalus nephtonensis

Astragalus nephtonensis, commonly known as the Nephton's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in rocky soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its ecosystem.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
