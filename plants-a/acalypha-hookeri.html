Acalypha hookeri

Acalypha hookeri, commonly known as the silver leaf or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that often resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture.

Acalypha hookeri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
