Allium transtagana

Allium transtagana, commonly known as the Trinidadian leek, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures similar to onions, which are used in local cuisines for their mild, garlic-like flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable ornamental and edible plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
