Agave applanata

Agave applanata, commonly known as the flatleaf agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length, often with a bluish-gray coloration. The plant produces an inflorescence that can reach several meters in height, bearing yellowish-green flowers. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves.

Agave applanata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and resilience.
