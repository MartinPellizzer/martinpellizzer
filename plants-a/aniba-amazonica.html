Aniba amazonica

Aniba amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and fragrant, resinous bark. The plant is notable for its high content of essential oils, particularly camphor, which has been used in traditional medicine. Its leaves and bark are often harvested for their medicinal and aromatic properties.

Aniba amazonica plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat, supporting a diverse range of flora and fauna.
