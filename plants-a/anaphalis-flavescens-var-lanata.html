Anaphalis flavescens var. lanata

Anaphalis flavescens var. lanata, commonly known as the woolly frostweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and stems, which give it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and wetlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
