Aralia cissifolia

Aralia cissifolia, commonly known as the ivy-leaf aralia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that resemble those of ivy, with elongated, lobed foliage. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, which are typically followed by bright red berries. It thrives in shaded to partially sunny environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Aralia cissifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and berries.
