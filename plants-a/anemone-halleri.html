Anemone halleri

Anemone halleri, commonly known as the Haller's anemone, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a hairy texture.

Anemone halleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
