Azalea nudiflora var. carnea

Azalea nudiflora var. carnea, commonly known as the naked-stem azalea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, showy clusters of pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
