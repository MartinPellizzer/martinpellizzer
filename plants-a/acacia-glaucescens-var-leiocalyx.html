Acacia glaucescens var. leiocalyx

Acacia glaucescens var. leiocalyx, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage composed of small, glandular, and often silky-haired leaves. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The wood of this species is hard and durable, historically used for construction and tool-making.
