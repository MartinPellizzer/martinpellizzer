Allium govanianum

Allium govanianum, commonly known as the Kashmiri chive, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of pale purple to white flowers. The plant is characterized by its bulbous stem and is often cultivated for its edible leaves and flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and cooler climates, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Allium govanianum is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses in regional cuisines.
