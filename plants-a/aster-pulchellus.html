Aster pulchellus

Aster pulchellus, commonly known as the showy aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodlands, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its showy, violet-blue flowers bloom in the late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster pulchellus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
