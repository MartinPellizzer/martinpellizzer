Adenostephanus incanus

Adenostephanus incanus, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is typically found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a low-growing habit with a rosette of leaves and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Adenostephanus incanus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
