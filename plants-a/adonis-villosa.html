Adonis villosa

Adonis villosa, commonly known as the hairy adonis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name, and it often grows in rocky or alpine habitats. Adonis villosa produces small, yellowish-orange flowers with numerous stamens, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
