Aethusa meum

Aethusa meum, commonly known as the common cow-wheat, is a biennial plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along woodland edges.

Aethusa meum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
