Achyronia erythrodes

Achyronia erythrodes, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with deep red veins contrasting against its green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
