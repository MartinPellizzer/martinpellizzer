Amomum acuminatum var. indutum

Amomum acuminatum var. indutum, commonly known as the hairy cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, finger-like rhizomes that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant produces clusters of small, pale green to white flowers followed by dark purple berries. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and covered with a fine, hairy texture, which gives it its common name.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often cultivated for its essential oils and flavoring compounds.
