Anemone ranunculoides var. subintegris

Anemone ranunculoides var. subintegris, commonly known as the broad-leaf anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white to pinkish flowers with five to nine petals, typically blooming in early spring. The leaves are palmately divided, with a broad, rounded shape and a hairy texture. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, often found in woodland settings.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
