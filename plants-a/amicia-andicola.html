Amicia andicola

Amicia andicola, commonly known as the Andean amicia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amicaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy foliage. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a distinct aromatic scent.

Amicia andicola plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
