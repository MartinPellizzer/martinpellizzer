Acer saccharum var. palmeri

Acer saccharum var. palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer sugar maple, is a variant of the sugar maple native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which displays vibrant autumn colors, and its heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, winged seeds known as samaras, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
