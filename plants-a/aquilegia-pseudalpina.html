Aquilegia pseudalpina

Aquilegia pseudalpina, commonly known as the Alpine columbine, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features showy, spurred flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are often hairy and adapted to cold, alpine environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or meadow habitats at high elevations.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine collections.
