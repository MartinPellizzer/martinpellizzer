Achnatherum arnowiae

Achnatherum arnowiae, commonly known as the Arnow's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the grasslands of North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often tinged with reddish hues, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-based flowers.

Achnatherum arnowiae plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
