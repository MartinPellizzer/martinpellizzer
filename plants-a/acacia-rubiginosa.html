Acacia rubiginosa

Acacia rubiginosa, commonly known as the red acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging branches and small, yellow flowers that appear in globular clusters. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that function as leaves, and it produces long, thin pods that contain seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
