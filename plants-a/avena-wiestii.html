Avena wiestii

Avena wiestii, commonly known as the Wiest's oat, is a species of grass native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. Avena wiestii is typically found in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

It plays a minor role in local ecosystems but is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
