Aster falcifolius

Aster falcifolius, commonly known as the sickle-leaved aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, sickle-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
