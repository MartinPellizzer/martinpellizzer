Andropogon nardus var. zeyheri

Andropogon nardus var. zeyheri, commonly known as Zeyher's sweetgrass, is a grass species native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which are typical of the grass family Poaceae. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slight sheen, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

Zeyher's sweetgrass is often used in traditional crafts and as a source of aromatic material.
