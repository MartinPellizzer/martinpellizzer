Andropogon lodicularis

Andropogon lodicularis, commonly known as inland needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Andropogon lodicularis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
