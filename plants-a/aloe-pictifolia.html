Aloe pictifolia

Aloe pictifolia, commonly known as the painted aloe, is a striking succulent native to South Africa.

It features tall, branching stems covered in dense, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple hues. The leaves are typically narrow and pointed, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss. During the growing season, it produces vibrant yellow-orange flowers in clusters at the tips of the stems.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
