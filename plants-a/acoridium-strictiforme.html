Acoridium strictiforme

Acoridium strictiforme, commonly known as the slender-leaf waterplantain, is a species of aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged, with a waxy surface to reduce water loss. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in a spike-like inflorescence.

Acoridium strictiforme plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
