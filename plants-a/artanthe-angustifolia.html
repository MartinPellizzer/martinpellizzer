Artanthe angustifolia

Artanthe angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf artanthe, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant collections and ecological restoration projects.
