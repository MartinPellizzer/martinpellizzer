Astragalus cordatus

Astragalus cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. The plant has compound leaves with heart-shaped leaflets, giving it its common name. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus cordatus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
