Aristolochia goliathiana

Aristolochia goliathiana, commonly known as the giant Dutchman's pipe, is a striking tropical vine native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, intricate, and often fragrant flowers that resemble a pipe, with a unique spiral structure. The plant's leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and can grow quite large, contributing to its dramatic appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found climbing trees or shrubs in its natural habitat.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
