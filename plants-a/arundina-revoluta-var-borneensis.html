Arundina revoluta var. borneensis

Arundina revoluta var. borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean bamboo orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Borneo.

It is characterized by its slender, bamboo-like stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
