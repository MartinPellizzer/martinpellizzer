Astragalus cephalanthus var. schirasicus

Astragalus cephalanthus var. schirasicus, commonly known as the Sierra milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is often found in mountainous regions and is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role and limited distribution.
