Aconitum puchonroenicum

Aconitum puchonroenicum, commonly known as the Puchonroenicum monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions in Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple spurred flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with a deeply lobed structure, and its stems are usually upright and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care.
