Anthericum annuum

Anthericum annuum, commonly known as the common asparagus fern, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, needle-like leaves that form a feathery appearance. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its foliage is often used in floral arrangements due to its delicate and decorative qualities.
