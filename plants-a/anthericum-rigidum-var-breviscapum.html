Anthericum rigidum var. breviscapum

Anthericum rigidum var. breviscapum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features a striking, arrow-shaped leaf that is typically narrow and elongated, with a smooth, dark green upper surface. The plant produces a spadix, a central spike of tiny flowers, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe, which is usually a deep purple or reddish color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and wetlands.

The species is characterized by its unique floral structure and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
