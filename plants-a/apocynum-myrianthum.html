Apocynum myrianthum

Apocynum myrianthum, commonly known as rose milkweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a woody stem and produces capsule fruits that contain toxic, milky sap. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Apocynum myrianthum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
