Acalypha eugeniifolia

Acalypha eugeniifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silky fanflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Acalypha eugeniifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
