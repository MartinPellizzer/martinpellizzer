Alopecurus armenus

Alopecurus armenus, commonly known as mouse ear chickweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing herb with fine, hairy leaves and small white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are characterized by their rounded, ear-like shape, which gives it its common name.

Alopecurus armenus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
