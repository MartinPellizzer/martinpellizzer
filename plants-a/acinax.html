Acinax

Acinax, commonly known as the common acornweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fluffy, seed-filled capsules. Acinax thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in open fields.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds make it a common weed in many regions.
