Alnus spachii

Alnus spachii, commonly known as the Spach's alder, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its finely toothed leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in catkins. The plant produces small, nut-like fruits that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Alnus spachii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
