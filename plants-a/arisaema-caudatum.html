Arisaema caudatum

Arisaema caudatum, commonly known as the tailflower arisaema, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are arrow-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while the spadix produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Arisaema caudatum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking foliage and unusual floral structure.
