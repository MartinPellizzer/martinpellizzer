Aspalathus agardhiana

Aspalathus agardhiana, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Aspalathus agardhiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in local habitats.
