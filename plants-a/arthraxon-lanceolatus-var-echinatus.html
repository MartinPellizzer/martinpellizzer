Arthraxon lanceolatus var. echinatus

Arthraxon lanceolatus var. echinatus, commonly known as the spiny flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and distinctive spiny structures on its flowering spikes. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters that bear small, three-lobed flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
