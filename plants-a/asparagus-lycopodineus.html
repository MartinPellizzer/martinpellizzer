Asparagus lycopodineus

Asparagus lycopodineus, commonly known as the lycopod asparagus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asparagaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems that often root at the nodes, a feature typical of many asparagus species. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, which are then followed by small, red berries. Asparagus lycopodineus is native to southern Africa, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
