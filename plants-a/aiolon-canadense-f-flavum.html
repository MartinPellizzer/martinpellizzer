Aiolon canadense f. flavum

Aiolon canadense f. flavum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered false nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes stinging nettles, and is characterized by its opposite leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has a fibrous root system. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, and the flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright floral display.
