Agrostis capillaris subsp. olivetorum

Agrostis capillaris subsp.

olivetorum, commonly known as silky bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its fine, soft blades and tufted growth habit, often forming dense mats on dry, sandy soils.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to well-drained environments and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its ecological role includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
