Adenocaulon bicolor

Adenocaulon bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves and produces small, white or pinkish flowers in spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to drought conditions and rocky habitats.
