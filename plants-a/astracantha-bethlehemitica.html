Astracantha bethlehemitica

Astracantha bethlehemitica, commonly known as the Bethlehemitic thornvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by legume pods containing several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and dry habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
