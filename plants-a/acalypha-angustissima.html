Acalypha angustissima

Acalypha angustissima, commonly known as narrowleaf croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 meters. The plant features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Acalypha angustissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage.
