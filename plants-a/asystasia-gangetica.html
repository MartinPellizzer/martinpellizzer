Asystasia gangetica

Asystasia gangetica, commonly known as the Indian violet or blue violets, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Asystasia gangetica is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its traditional medicinal uses.
