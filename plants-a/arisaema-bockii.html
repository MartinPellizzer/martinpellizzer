Arisaema bockii

Arisaema bockii, commonly known as the Bock's arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence known as a spadix, enclosed by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered around the spadix, which is often surrounded by a colorful, leaf-like structure. Arisaema bockii typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests and rocky areas.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
