Amelanchier magnifica

Amelanchier magnifica, commonly known as the showy serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its attractive white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

The showy serviceberry is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
