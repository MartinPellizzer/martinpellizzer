Acrothamnus suaveolens

Acrothamnus suaveolens, commonly known as sweet wattle, is a perennial shrub native to southern Australia.

It features slender, wiry stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, spike-like clusters during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
