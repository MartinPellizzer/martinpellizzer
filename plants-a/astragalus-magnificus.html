Astragalus magnificus

Astragalus magnificus, commonly known as the magnificent milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is known for its deep taproot system, which allows it to thrive in arid and rocky environments.

Astragalus magnificus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
