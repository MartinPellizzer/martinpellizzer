Aparinanthus verticillatus

Aparinanthus verticillatus, commonly known as the spiral rush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spirally arranged inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy areas and is adapted to waterlogged soils. It produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters.

Its unique morphology makes it a subject of interest in botanical studies and ecological research.
