Amelanchier acuminulata

Amelanchier acuminulata, commonly known as the sharp-lobed serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It features oval, serrated leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, dark purple berries in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Amelanchier acuminulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
