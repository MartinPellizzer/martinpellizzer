Apium ternatum subvar. angustisectum

Apium ternatum subvar. angustisectum, commonly known as narrow-leaved parsley, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats.
