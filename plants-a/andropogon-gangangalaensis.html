Andropogon gangangalaensis

Andropogon gangangalaensis, commonly known as the African switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated for landscaping purposes.
