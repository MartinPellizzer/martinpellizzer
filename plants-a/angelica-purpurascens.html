Angelica purpurascens

Angelica purpurascens, commonly known as the purple angelica, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and distinctive floral structures.

It is native to parts of Asia and is recognized for its tall, slender stem and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces a unique, pungent odor due to the presence of volatile oils such as phellandrene and limonene. Its leaves are compound, with deeply lobed blades that often have a silvery underside.

Angelica purpurascens is typically found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
