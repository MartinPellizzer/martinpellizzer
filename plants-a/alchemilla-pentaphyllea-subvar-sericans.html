Alchemilla pentaphyllea subvar. sericans

Alchemilla pentaphyllea subvar. sericans, commonly known as the silverleaf lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, velvety leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicle-like clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden settings for its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements.
