Acacia fagonioides

Acacia fagonioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging branches and narrow, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

Acacia fagonioides is often used in revegetation projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for native wildlife.
