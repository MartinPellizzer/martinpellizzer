Arthrophytum articulatum

Arthrophytum articulatum, commonly known as the artichoke cactus, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months, followed by small, edible fruits. Its leaves are typically spineless and arranged in a rosette pattern, helping to reduce water loss.

Arthrophytum articulatum is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
