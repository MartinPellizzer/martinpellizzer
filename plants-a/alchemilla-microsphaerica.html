Alchemilla microsphaerica

Alchemilla microsphaerica, commonly known as the lesser tutsan, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features silver-gray foliage with a fuzzy texture, and its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats. It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Alchemilla microsphaerica is also known for its historical use in herbal medicine.
