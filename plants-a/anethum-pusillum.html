Anethum pusillum

Anethum pusillum, commonly known as dwarf cumin, is a small annual herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces tiny, spherical seed pods that contain aromatic, spice-like seeds used in various culinary applications. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, making it a low-growing ground cover. Its leaves are finely divided and emit a distinct, peppery fragrance when crushed.

The botanical features of Anethum pusillum make it well-suited for rock gardens and dry, well-drained soils.
