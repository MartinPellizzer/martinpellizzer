Acalypha diversifolia var. claoneura

Acalypha diversifolia var. claoneura, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red-leaf acalypha, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, velvety foliage that ranges from deep red to purplish tones, especially in cooler weather. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and often have a slightly serrated margin. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its unique coloration and compact growth habit.

It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it adaptable to a variety of indoor and outdoor environments.
