Azedarach excelsa

Azedarach excelsa, commonly known as the Persian silk tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a silvery underside. The tree produces fragrant, showy flowers in clusters, typically in spring, which range in color from white to pink. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Azedarach excelsa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
