Amomum flavidulum

Amomum flavidulum, commonly known as the yellow cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by oval, yellowish-brown fruits. The rhizomes and fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their distinctive fragrance and flavor.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal properties.
