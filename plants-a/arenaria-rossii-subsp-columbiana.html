Arenaria rossii subsp. columbiana

Arenaria rossii subsp.

columbiana, commonly known as the Columbia sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States. It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in alpine and arctic environments.

The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually fleshy and succulent, adapted to conserve moisture in harsh conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing rocky and sandy habitats.
