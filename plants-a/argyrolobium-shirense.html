Argyrolobium shirense

Argyrolobium shirense, commonly known as the Shiren's silver globular pea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive globular, silver-green seed pods. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval-shaped and slightly hairy.

Argyrolobium shirense is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
