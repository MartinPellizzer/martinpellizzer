Aridarum orientale

Aridarum orientale, commonly known as the Eastern Desert Rose, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red and pink. The plant thrives in hot, dry climates and is well adapted to low water conditions. Its roots are deep and extensive, allowing it to access moisture from underground sources.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
