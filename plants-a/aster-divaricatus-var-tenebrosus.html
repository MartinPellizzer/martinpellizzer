Aster divaricatus var. tenebrosus

Aster divaricatus var. tenebrosus, commonly known as the dark-leaved aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive addition to native plant gardens and ecological landscapes.
