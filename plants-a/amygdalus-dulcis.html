Amygdalus dulcis

Amygdalus dulcis, commonly known as the sweet almond tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces oval, green leaves and fragrant white or pink flowers in the spring. The tree's fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed surrounded by a hard shell. Sweet almonds are widely cultivated for their edible seeds, which are processed into oil and used in food and cosmetics.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
