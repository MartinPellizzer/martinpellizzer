Acacia lanata

Acacia lanata, commonly known as the downy acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fluffy appearance. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in flat, pod-like fruits that split open to release the seeds.

Acacia lanata is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
