Annona laevis

Annona laevis, commonly known as the smooth custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small, evergreen tree or shrub that produces a distinctive fruit with a smooth, waxy skin. The plant belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and fragrant flowers. The fruit, which is typically harvested when fully ripe, has a creamy, custard-like pulp and is rich in nutrients.

It is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its potential medicinal properties.
