Argyroderma schuldtii

Argyroderma schuldtii, commonly known as the silverleaf cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, spiny stems that are covered in a distinctive silvery coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its adaptations to arid environments include succulent leaves and the ability to store water in its stems.

Argyroderma schuldtii is often found in rocky, dry habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
