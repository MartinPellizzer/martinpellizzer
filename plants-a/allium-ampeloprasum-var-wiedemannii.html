Allium ampeloprasum var. wiedemannii

Allium ampeloprasum var. wiedemannii, commonly known as the purple flowering onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces large, elongated bulbs with a mild, sweet flavor, often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, growing from the base of the plant.

This variety is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its edible qualities.
