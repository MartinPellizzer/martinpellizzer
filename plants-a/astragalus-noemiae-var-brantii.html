Astragalus noemiae var. brantii

Astragalus noemiae var. brantii, commonly known as Brant's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to low moisture conditions.

This species is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
