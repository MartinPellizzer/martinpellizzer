Aporosa nigropunctata var. antennifera

Aporosa nigropunctata var. antennifera, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed aporosa, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are woody capsules that split open to release seeds.

It is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
