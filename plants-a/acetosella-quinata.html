Acetosella quinata

Acetosella quinata, commonly known as sorrel or wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet having a heart-shaped base. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by kidney-shaped seeds. Acetosella quinata thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
