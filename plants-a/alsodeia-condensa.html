Alsodeia condensa

Alsodeia condensa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved alsike, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Alsodeia condensa is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it ecologically significant in nutrient-poor environments.
