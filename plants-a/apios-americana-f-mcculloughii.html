Apios americana f. mcculloughii

Apios americana f. mcculloughii, commonly known as the McCullough's wild groundnut, is a rare and unique variant of the American groundnut.

It is characterized by its distinctive, compound leaves and its ability to produce edible, tuberous roots. This plant is native to specific regions of the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Its botanical significance lies in its genetic diversity and ecological role in native habitats.
