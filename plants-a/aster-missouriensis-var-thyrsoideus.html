Aster missouriensis var. thyrsoideus

Aster missouriensis var. thyrsoideus, commonly known as the Missouri aster, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, lavender to pinkish-purple flowers in the late summer and early fall. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators in native habitats.
