Allium ampeloprasum var. lussinense

Allium ampeloprasum var. lussinense, commonly known as the Tuscan leek, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its long, cylindrical leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild, garlic-like flavor, which can be used in culinary applications.
