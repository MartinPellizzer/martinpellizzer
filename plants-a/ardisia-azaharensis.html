Ardisia azaharensis

Ardisia azaharensis, commonly known as the golden flame ardisia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and emit a slight fragrance. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Following flowering, it develops bright red, berry-like fruits that are edible but have a tart flavor.

Ardisia azaharensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a variety of soil conditions.
