Amomum longibractea

Amomum longibractea, commonly known as the long-bract cardamom, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, spindle-shaped rhizomes. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pale yellow flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive, waxy texture.

Amomum longibractea is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
