Astus

Astus, commonly known as the American beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of vibrant purple berries that appear in the fall, providing a striking visual display. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and turn red in the autumn. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various birds and small mammals.
