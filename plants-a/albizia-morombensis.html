Albizia morombensis

Albizia morombensis, commonly known as the Madagascar Albizia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are often hairy, and its flowers are typically pale yellow or white, arranged in racemose inflorescences. Albizia morombensis is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional uses.
