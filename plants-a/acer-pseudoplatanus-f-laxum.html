Acer pseudoplatanus f. laxum

Acer pseudoplatanus f. laxum, commonly known as the looseleaf maple, is a variant of the common maple tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that are often more loosely arranged than those of the typical species. The plant exhibits a broadleaf deciduous habit, with leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This form is particularly noted for its unique foliage structure, making it a popular choice in botanical gardens and landscapes.
