Arabis hirshbergiae

Arabis hirshbergiae, commonly known as the Hirsberg's rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many species of mustard and cress. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are often lobed or divided, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
