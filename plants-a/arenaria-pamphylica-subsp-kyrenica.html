Arenaria pamphylica subsp. kyrenica

Arenaria pamphylica subsp.

kyrenica, commonly known as the Pamphylian sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky and sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant produces small, white or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapted to conserve water.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
