Aristolochia odoratissima f. brasiliensis

Aristolochia odoratissima f. brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian birthwort, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and showy, hooded flowers. The plant produces a strong, aromatic odor that attracts pollinators such as flies and beetles. Its tuberous roots contain various alkaloids, which have been used in traditional medicine.

However, it is important to note that the plant is toxic and should not be ingested.
