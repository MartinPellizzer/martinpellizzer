Astragalus fontanesii subsp. numidicus

Astragalus fontanesii subsp.

numidicus, commonly known as the Numidian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North Africa and parts of the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, lens-shaped seeds that are often dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
