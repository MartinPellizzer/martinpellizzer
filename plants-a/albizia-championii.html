Albizia championii

Albizia championii, commonly known as the African siris, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Albizia championii is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and fodder.
