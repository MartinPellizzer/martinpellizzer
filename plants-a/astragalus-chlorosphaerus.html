Astragalus chlorosphaerus

Astragalus chlorosphaerus, commonly known as the green globe milkvetch, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, preferring well-drained soils. Its seeds are encased in a hard, spherical pod, which contributes to its common name.

Astragalus chlorosphaerus is often found in grasslands and rocky habitats across the western United States.
