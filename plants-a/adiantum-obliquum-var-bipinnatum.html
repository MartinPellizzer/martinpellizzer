Adiantum obliquum var. bipinnatum

Adiantum obliquum var. bipinnatum, commonly known as the slant fern or the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a delicate and ornamental fern native to the southeastern United States.

It features finely divided, bi-pinnate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a slender, upright growth habit. The fronds are characterized by their narrow, elongated pinnae that curve slightly, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

This variety is prized for its elegant appearance and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
