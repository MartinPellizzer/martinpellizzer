Asyneuma strictum

Asyneuma strictum, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, exhibiting a low, sprawling growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss.

Asyneuma strictum is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
