Aspalathus horrida

Aspalathus horrida, commonly known as the African red bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, bright red flowers. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough appearance.

Aspalathus horrida is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
