Acrocephalus buettneri

Acrocephalus buettneri, commonly known as the African marsh grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa.

It typically grows in wetland and marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the grass family.

Acrocephalus buettneri plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various small organisms.
