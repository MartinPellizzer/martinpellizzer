Amyema queenslandica

Amyema queenslandica, commonly known as the Queensland emu bush, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Amyema queenslandica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract native pollinators.
