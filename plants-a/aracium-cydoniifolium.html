Aracium cydoniifolium

Aracium cydoniifolium, commonly known as the pineapple apple or false pineapple, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, spiky leaves that form a central cup. The plant produces an edible fruit that resembles a pineapple in appearance and has a sweet, tropical flavor. The plant's unique structure and adaptability make it a popular choice for tropical gardens.
