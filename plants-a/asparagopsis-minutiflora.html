Asparagopsis minutiflora

Asparagopsis minutiflora, commonly known as the dwarf asparagus plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense tufts at the base of the plant.

Asparagopsis minutiflora thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
