Acer caudatum subsp. georgei

Acer caudatum subsp.

georgei, commonly known as the George's weeping maple, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its distinctive, cascading branches that create a weeping appearance, with leaves that are typically oval and have a serrated edge.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red-orange samaras that contribute to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its unique form and seasonal color changes make it a striking addition to botanical collections.
