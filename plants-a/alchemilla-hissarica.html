Alchemilla hissarica

Alchemilla hissarica, commonly known as the Hissar lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Alchemilla hissarica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
