Ardisia acuminata var. dentata

Ardisia acuminata var. dentata, commonly known as the serrated flame tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves with serrated edges, which give it its common name. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by bright red berries.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and vibrant fruit.
