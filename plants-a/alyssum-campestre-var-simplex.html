Alyssum campestre var. simplex

Alyssum campestre var. simplex, commonly known as sweet Alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features clusters of small, fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring to summer. The plant has gray-green, hairy leaves and a compact, spreading growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
