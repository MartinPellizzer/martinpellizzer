Ailanthus Altissima Var. Tanakae

Ailanthus altissima var. tanakae is a unique variant of the tree of heaven known for its striking foliage and adaptability. This plant thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in urban environments where it can outcompete native species. Its rapid growth and ability to tolerate pollution make it a common sight in cities and disturbed landscapes. Despite its ecological impact, it has gained attention for its ornamental value and resilience. This article explores the characteristics, history, and significance of this intriguing plant.
