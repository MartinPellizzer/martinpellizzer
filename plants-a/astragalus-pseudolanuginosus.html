Astragalus pseudolanuginosus

Astragalus pseudolanuginosus, commonly known as the woolly milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, harsh environments. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus pseudolanuginosus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
