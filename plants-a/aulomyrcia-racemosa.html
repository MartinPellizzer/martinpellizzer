Aulomyrcia racemosa

Aulomyrcia racemosa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, white, fragrant flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some bird species. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of soil types.

Due to its invasive nature in some areas, it has been the subject of ecological studies and management efforts.
