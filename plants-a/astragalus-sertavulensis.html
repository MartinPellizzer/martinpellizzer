Astragalus sertavulensis

Astragalus sertavulensis, commonly known as the Sertavul milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of arid ecosystems.
