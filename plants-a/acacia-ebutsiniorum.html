Acacia ebutsiniorum

Acacia ebutsiniorum, commonly known as the East African acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are often shed during the dry season. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical heads, which are followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in savannas and along riverbanks.

The plant is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
