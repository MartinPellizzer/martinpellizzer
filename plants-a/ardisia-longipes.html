Ardisia longipes

Ardisia longipes, commonly known as the longiped ardisia, is a member of the Primulaceae family.

It is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant features glossy, ovate leaves that are arranged alternately along its stems. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, followed by dark purple berries that are edible.

Ardisia longipes is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive foliage and seasonal fruit display.
