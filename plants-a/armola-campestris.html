Armola campestris

Armola campestris, commonly known as the field armola, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its daisy-like composite flowers, which consist of a central disk surrounded by ray florets. The plant typically grows in open fields and grassy areas, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Armola campestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
