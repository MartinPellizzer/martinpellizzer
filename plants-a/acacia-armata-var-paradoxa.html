Acacia armata var. paradoxa

Acacia armata var. paradoxa, commonly known as the Paradoxical Acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, bean-like pods that contain seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to harsh, semi-arid environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
