Asplenium simplicifolium

Asplenium simplicifolium, commonly known as the simple-leaved spleenwort, is a species of moss belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a single row along the stem. This fern is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks in moist, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are clustered on the underside of the leaves.

Asplenium simplicifolium plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms in its ecosystem.
