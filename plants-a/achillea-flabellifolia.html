Achillea flabellifolia

Achillea flabellifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal rosettes of finely dissected, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, slightly bitter scent and is often found in rocky or dry soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Achillea flabellifolia is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
