Acicarpha lanata

Acicarpha lanata, commonly known as the woolly acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-green foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a woody stem with a fibrous, hairy texture, which contributes to its common name. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple small leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits.

Acicarpha lanata is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
