Aspalathus lactea

Aspalathus lactea, commonly known as the milk bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, woody stems and small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds covered in a fine, white, milky substance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Aspalathus lactea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
