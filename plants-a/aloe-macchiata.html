Aloe macchiata

Aloe macchiata, commonly known as the spotted aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, gray-green leaves with distinctive white or pale blue spots along the edges. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with plenty of sunlight.

Aloe macchiata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
