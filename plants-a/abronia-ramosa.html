Abronia ramosa

Abronia ramosa, commonly known as the branched sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms a dense mat of foliage, with small, pale green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to arid environments.

Abronia ramosa is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and animals.
