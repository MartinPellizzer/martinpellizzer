Aconitum plicatum subsp. sudeticum

Aconitum plicatum subsp.

sudeticum, commonly known as the Sudetic monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers.

The plant has palmate leaves with deeply lobed segments, and its stems are typically covered in a dense layer of hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is known for its toxic alkaloids, which make it a plant of both ecological and medicinal interest.
