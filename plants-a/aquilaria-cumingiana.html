Aquilaria cumingiana

Aquilaria cumingiana, commonly known as the white aloes or white agarwood, is a member of the Thymelaeaceae family.

It is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia, Indonesia, and Thailand. The plant is notable for producing a fragrant resin, known as agarwood, which is highly valued in traditional medicine and perfumery. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and lanceolate, while its flowers are small and clustered in axillary inflorescences.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
