Abelia triflora subsp. parvifolia

Abelia triflora subsp.

parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved abelia, is a compact evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a glossy green, with a fine, silvery underside.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate foliage and showy blooms, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
