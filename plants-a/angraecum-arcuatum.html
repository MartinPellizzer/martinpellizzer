Angraecum arcuatum

Angraecum arcuatum, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It features long, slender stems and produces pendulous, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue with a deep violet throat. The plant is adapted to grow in humid, shaded environments, often on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are pollinated by specific moths, highlighting its ecological niche and evolutionary adaptations.

Angraecum arcuatum is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts due to its beauty and rarity.
