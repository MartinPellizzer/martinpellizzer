Avena sativa subsp. brevis

Avena sativa subsp.

brevis, commonly known as slender oat, is a cultivated variety of the oat plant characterized by its shorter stature and compact growth habit. It is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and is often grown for its grains, which are used in food and animal feed.

The plant produces spikelets that bear small, oval-shaped seeds, which are enclosed in a protective husk. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires moderate moisture for optimal growth.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in agricultural systems for its adaptability and nutritional value.
