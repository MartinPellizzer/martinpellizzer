Artocarpus nigrifolius

Artocarpus nigrifolius, commonly known as the black-leaf breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mulberry family, Moraceae, and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves. The tree produces a large, inedible fruit that turns from green to black as it ripens. Its bark is rough and fissured, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine.
