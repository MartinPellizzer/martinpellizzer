Allionia campanulata

Allionia campanulata, commonly known as the bellflower allionia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Campanulaceae and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Allionia campanulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
