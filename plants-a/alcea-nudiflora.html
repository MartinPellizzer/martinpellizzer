Alcea nudiflora

Alcea nudiflora, commonly known as the naked-flower hollyhock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Alcea nudiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to temperate climates.
