Astragalus falciformis

Astragalus falciformis, commonly known as the sicklepod milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive sickle-shaped pods. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers in late spring.

Astragalus falciformis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
