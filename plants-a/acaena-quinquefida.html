Acaena quinquefida

Acaena quinquefida, commonly known as the five-lobed burrplant, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense clusters of small, spiny fruits that form persistent burrs. The plant has compound leaves with five leaflets, which are typically hairy and lance-shaped. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine environments.

Acaena quinquefida plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
