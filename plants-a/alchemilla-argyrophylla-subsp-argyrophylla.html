Alchemilla argyrophylla subsp. argyrophylla

Alchemilla argyrophylla subsp.

argyrophylla, commonly known as silverleaf lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
