Agrostis chamaecalamus

Agrostis chamaecalamus, commonly known as bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy, with a dense growth habit that forms a mat-like structure. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lawns, meadows, and along stream banks.

Due to its fine texture and durability, it is also cultivated for ornamental and turfgrass purposes.
