Aridaria notha

Aridaria notha, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in low-water conditions. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and purple, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are small, hairy, and adapted to reduce water loss through transpiration.

Aridaria notha is often used in xeriscaping due to its resilience and ornamental value in dry climates.
