Aulomyrcia leptoclada

Aulomyrcia leptoclada, commonly known as the slender-leaved myrcia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests.

Aulomyrcia leptoclada is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
