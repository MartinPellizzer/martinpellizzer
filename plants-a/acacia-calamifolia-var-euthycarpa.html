Acacia calamifolia var. euthycarpa

Acacia calamifolia var. euthycarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are typically green with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers.

The wood of this acacia is hard and durable, making it valuable for local use in construction and fencing.
