Alsine pulvinaris

Alsine pulvinaris, commonly known as the pulvinate alsike, is a low-growing annual herb native to Eurasia.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Alsine pulvinaris is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.
