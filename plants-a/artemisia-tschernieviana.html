Artemisia tschernieviana

Artemisia tschernieviana, commonly known as the Tschernieff's wormwood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other aromatic plants.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thujone, which have been studied for their medicinal properties.
