Arenaria tetraquetra var. brevifolia

Arenaria tetraquetra var. brevifolia, commonly known as the dwarf sand-sedge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale pink flowers in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates. It is often found in tundra and mountain meadow habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of high-altitude and polar ecosystems.
