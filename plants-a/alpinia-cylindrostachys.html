Alpinia cylindrostachys

Alpinia cylindrostachys, commonly known as the slender ginger, is a tropical perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and lance-shaped, glossy green leaves. The plant produces inflorescences of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are followed by cylindrical, finger-like fruits. Alpinia cylindrostachys thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
