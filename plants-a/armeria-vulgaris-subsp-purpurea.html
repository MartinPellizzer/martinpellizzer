Armeria vulgaris subsp. purpurea

Armeria vulgaris subsp.

purpurea, commonly known as the purple thrift, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial that produces dense clusters of small, pink to purple flowers.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, gray-green leaves and upright flowering stems. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and full sun.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
