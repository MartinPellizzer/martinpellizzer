Acrachne verticillata

Acrachne verticillata, commonly known as the clustered acanthus, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking, spiny bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

Acrachne verticillata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
