Albizia poissonii

Albizia poissonii, commonly known as the golden wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that turn a striking golden-yellow color in the fall. The plant produces fragrant, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white to pink. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.

Albizia poissonii prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden settings.
