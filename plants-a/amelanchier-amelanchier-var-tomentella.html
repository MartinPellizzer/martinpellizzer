Amelanchier amelanchier var. tomentella

Amelanchier amelanchier var. tomentella, commonly known as the smooth serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth texture. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
