Ageratina remyana

Ageratina remyana, commonly known as the Remy's blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of flowers. The plant produces small, blue to purple florets surrounded by white, fluffy bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Ageratina remyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
