Aconitum laxum

Aconitum laxum, commonly known as the slender aconite, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Aconitum laxum contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals if ingested.
