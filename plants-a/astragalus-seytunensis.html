Astragalus seytunensis

Astragalus seytunensis, commonly known as the Seyedun milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
