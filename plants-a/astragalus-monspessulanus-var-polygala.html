Astragalus monspessulanus var. polygala

Astragalus monspessulanus var. polygala, commonly known as the Mediterranean milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from pink to purple. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, rocky soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

This variant is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.
