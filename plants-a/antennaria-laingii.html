Antennaria laingii

Antennaria laingii, commonly known as the Laing's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and woolly foliage. The plant features small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in spring, often nestled among dense, silver-white leaves. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Antennaria laingii is adapted to harsh alpine environments, making it a resilient and hardy species.
