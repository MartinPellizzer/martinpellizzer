Astragalus crassicarpus var. cavus

Astragalus crassicarpus var. cavus, commonly known as the broad-fruited milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces distinctive, inflated pods that are thick-walled and often ribbed, which help in seed dispersal. It is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and deep root systems to access water.

This species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
