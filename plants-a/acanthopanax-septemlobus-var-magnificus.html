Acanthopanax septemlobus var. magnificus

Acanthopanax septemlobus var. magnificus, commonly known as the magnificent seven-lobed dogwood, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 20 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and seasonal color changes.
