Anthurium gehrigeri

Anthurium gehrigeri, commonly known as the silver star anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green with a silvery sheen. The plant produces bright red spadices, which are the spiky, club-shaped inflorescences typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, warm environments with indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Anthurium gehrigeri is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and unique floral structures.
