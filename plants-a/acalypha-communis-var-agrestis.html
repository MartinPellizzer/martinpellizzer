Acalypha communis var. agrestis

Acalypha communis var. agrestis, commonly known as the roughleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features palmate leaves with serrated margins and a distinctive, showy inflorescence of small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its botanical characteristics include fibrous roots and a woody stem that becomes more prominent with age.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
