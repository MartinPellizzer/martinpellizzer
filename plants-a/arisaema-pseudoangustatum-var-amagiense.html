Arisaema pseudoangustatum var. amagiense

Arisaema pseudoangustatum var. amagiense, commonly known as the Amami arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to the Amami Islands of Japan.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves and a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers, which are followed by edible, starchy tubers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
