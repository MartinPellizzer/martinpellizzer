Astragalus pamphylicus var. argolicus

Astragalus pamphylicus var. argolicus, commonly known as the Argolic milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe, particularly Greece.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It plays a role in soil stabilization and supports local pollinator populations.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
