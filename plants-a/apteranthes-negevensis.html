Apteranthes negevensis

Apteranthes negevensis, commonly known as the Negev dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Israel and the surrounding regions.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and other composite flowers. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a single, elongated flower head that produces seeds with fluffy pappus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to the climate of the Negev Desert.

Due to its hardiness and ecological significance, it plays a role in local biodiversity and soil stabilization.
