Aphelandra davidsonii

Aphelandra davidsonii, commonly known as the Davidson's torch ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, torch-like inflorescences that are typically orange or red in color. The plant features large, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Aphelandra davidsonii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display.
