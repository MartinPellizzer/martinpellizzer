Allium sandrasicum

Allium sandrasicum, commonly known as the sand leek, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary potential, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
