Arundinaria auriculata

Arundinaria auriculata, commonly known as the golden bamboo or golden cane, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that are often golden-yellow in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, grass-like texture.

Arundinaria auriculata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and ease of growth.
