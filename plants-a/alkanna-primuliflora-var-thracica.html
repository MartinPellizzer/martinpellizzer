Alkanna primuliflora var. thracica

Alkanna primuliflora var. thracica, commonly known as the Balkan alkanna, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features slender, branched stems that grow up to 30 cm in height, adorned with alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish to reddish flowers in late spring, which are typically arranged in cymes. Its roots are rich in alkannin, a red-orange pigment used in traditional medicine and dyeing.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
