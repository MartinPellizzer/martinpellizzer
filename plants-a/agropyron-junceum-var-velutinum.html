Agropyron junceum var. velutinum

Agropyron junceum var. velutinum, commonly known as silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, soft blades that give it a silvery appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
