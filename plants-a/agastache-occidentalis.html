Agastache occidentalis

Agastache occidentalis, commonly known as western hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and spikes of lavender to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments. Its aromatic leaves and showy flowers make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and pollinator gardens.

Agastache occidentalis is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
