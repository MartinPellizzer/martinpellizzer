Alyssum incanum var. viride

Alyssum incanum var. viride, commonly known as green hoary alyssum, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. The leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving the plant a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
