Apurimacia libertatis

Apurimacia libertatis, commonly known as the "Liberty Plant," is a rare alpine species native to the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and silver-green foliage, which reflects its adaptation to high-altitude conditions. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Apurimacia libertatis is considered endangered and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation to extreme climates.
