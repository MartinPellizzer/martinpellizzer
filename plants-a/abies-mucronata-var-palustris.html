Abies mucronata var. palustris

Abies mucronata var. palustris, commonly known as the bog fir, is a variant of the white fir native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are short and thick. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs or swampy areas. Its cones are small and cylindrical, typically maturing in the second year.

This variant is adapted to cold, wet environments and plays a significant role in the ecological structure of its habitat.
