Aster rugulosus

Aster rugulosus, commonly known as the rough aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster rugulosus is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
