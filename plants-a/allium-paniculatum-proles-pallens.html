Allium paniculatum proles pallens

Allium paniculatum proles pallens, commonly known as the silverleaf garlic.

This plant is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage. It produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant is native to southern Europe and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has a mild garlic scent when crushed.
