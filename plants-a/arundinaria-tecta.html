Arundinaria tecta

Arundinaria tecta, commonly known as the bamboo palm, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and the Caribbean. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery, grass-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Arundinaria tecta is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
