Alstonia villosa f. calvescens

Alstonia villosa f. calvescens, commonly known as the hairy alstonia, is a variant of the alstonia species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its rough, hairy bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by elongated, woody fruits containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
