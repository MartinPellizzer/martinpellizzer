Aspilia welwitschii

Aspilia welwitschii, commonly known as the African torch or red torch, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and dense clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that resemble torches. The plant produces feathery, bristly leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Aspilia welwitschii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
