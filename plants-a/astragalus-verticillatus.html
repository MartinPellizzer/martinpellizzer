Astragalus verticillatus

Astragalus verticillatus, commonly known as the clustered milkvetch, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as prairies and open woodlands. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets that are usually hairy and narrow.

Astragalus verticillatus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
