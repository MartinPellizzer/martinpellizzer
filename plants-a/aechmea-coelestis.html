Aechmea coelestis

Aechmea coelestis, commonly known as the sky flower or blue star bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients from the environment. The plant produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that range in color from white to blue, often resembling a star. Its leaves are typically broad, waxy, and have a bluish-green hue with a slightly serrated edge.

Aechmea coelestis thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Morphology

Aechmea coelestis has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, leathery, and dark green with a metallic sheen, arranged in a compact spiral at the base.

The plant lacks a typical stem, with the leaves forming a dense, compact rosette that protects the central bud. Its inflorescence is a striking violet or purple spadix surrounded by colorful bracts, blooming during the summer months and pollinated by insects. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the air and soil, typical of epiphytic growth.
