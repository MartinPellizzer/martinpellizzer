Angraecum parcum

Angraecum parcum, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with a distinctive star-shaped pattern on the petals. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in the humid and shaded environments of its native habitat. Its flowers emit a strong scent at night, attracting specific pollinators such as moths.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, Angraecum parcum is highly valued in botanical collections and conservation efforts.
