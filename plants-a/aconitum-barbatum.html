Aconitum barbatum

Aconitum barbatum, commonly known as the beardroot monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has large, palmately lobed leaves and produces toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or meadows.

Aconitum barbatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
