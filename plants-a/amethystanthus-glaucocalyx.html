Amethystanthus glaucocalyx

Amethystanthus glaucocalyx, commonly known as the blue-veined orchid, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with contrasting white veins. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a powdery blue-gray coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
