Acacia aucheri

Acacia aucheri, commonly known as the Persian acacia, is a shrub native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates. Its ornamental value comes from its delicate foliage and fragrant blooms.

However, it is not typically used for timber or medicinal purposes.
