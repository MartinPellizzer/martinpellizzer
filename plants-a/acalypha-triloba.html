Acalypha triloba

Acalypha triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed spurflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, three-lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Acalypha triloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
