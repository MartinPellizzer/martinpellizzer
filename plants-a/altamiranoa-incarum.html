Altamiranoa incarum

Altamiranoa incarum, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking red petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom during the wet season, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Altamiranoa incarum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
