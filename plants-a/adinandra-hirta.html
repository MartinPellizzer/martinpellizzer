Adinandra hirta

Adinandra hirta, commonly known as the hairy adinandra, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, elliptical leaves that are often arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
