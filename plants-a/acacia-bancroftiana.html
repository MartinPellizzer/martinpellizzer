Acacia bancroftiana

Acacia bancroftiana, commonly known as the African bristled acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorny stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia bancroftiana is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its role in providing habitat for wildlife.
