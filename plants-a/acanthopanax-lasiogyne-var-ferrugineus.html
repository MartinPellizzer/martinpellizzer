Acanthopanax lasiogyne var. ferrugineus

Acanthopanax lasiogyne var. ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty-leaf dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The varietal name 'ferrugineus' refers to the rusty-colored hairs present on its stems and leaves.
