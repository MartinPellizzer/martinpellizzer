Arisaema guixiense

Arisaema guixiense, commonly known as the Guizhou arrowhead, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and a spadix inflorescence surrounded by a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories and thrives in humid, temperate climates. Its ornamental value is appreciated in horticulture due to its unique foliage and dramatic floral structure.

However, it is important to note that some species within the genus Arisaema contain calcium oxalate crystals, which can be toxic if ingested.
