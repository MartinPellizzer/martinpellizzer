Ardisia dubia

Ardisia dubia, commonly known as the dwarf beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features dark green, glossy leaves and produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. Following flowering, it develops bright red, berry-like fruits that are attractive to birds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
