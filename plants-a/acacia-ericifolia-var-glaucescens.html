Acacia ericifolia var. glaucescens

Acacia ericifolia var. glaucescens, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features narrow, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes across southern Australia.
