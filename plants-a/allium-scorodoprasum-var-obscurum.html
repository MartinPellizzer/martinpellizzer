Allium scorodoprasum var. obscurum

Allium scorodoprasum var. obscurum, commonly known as black garlic, is a botanical variant of the garlic species characterized by its dark purple to black bulb.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces small, clustered bulbs that are typically smaller than common garlic. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it flowers with umbels of white or pale purple blossoms.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is often used in culinary applications.
