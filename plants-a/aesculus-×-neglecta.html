Aesculus × neglecta

Aesculus × neglecta, commonly known as the Chinese horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its palmately compound leaves that are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, elegant appearance. The tree produces showy, white or pale yellow flowers in panicles during early summer. Its fruit is a spiny capsule containing 1–3 large, smooth seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
