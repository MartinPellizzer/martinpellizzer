Astragalus shuturunkuhensis

Astragalus shuturunkuhensis, commonly known as the Xinjiang milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Xinjiang, China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in racemous inflorescences. It is adapted to dry and saline soils, making it a hardy species found in semi-desert environments.

Astragalus shuturunkuhensis is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
