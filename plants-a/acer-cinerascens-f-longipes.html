Acer cinerascens f. longipes

Acer cinerascens f. longipes, commonly known as the long-peduncled smoke tree, is a variant of the smoke tree species native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery inflorescences that give it a smoky appearance, especially when in bloom. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a finely serrated margin and a pale green underside. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal, particularly in gardens where its unique foliage and floral display stand out.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile choice for landscape design.
