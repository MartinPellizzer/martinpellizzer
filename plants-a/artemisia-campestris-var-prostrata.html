Artemisia campestris var. prostrata

Artemisia campestris var. prostrata, commonly known as creeping wormwood, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features silver-gray foliage with small, narrow leaves that are densely arranged along the stems. The plant produces tiny, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry meadows and rocky areas.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is sometimes used in herbal medicine and as a decorative plant in gardens.
