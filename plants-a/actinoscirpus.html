Actinoscirpus

Actinoscirpus, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, spiny bracts that surround the small, inconspicuous flowers. These plants are typically found in coastal areas and saline environments, showcasing their adaptability to harsh conditions. Actinoscirpus species are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological roles in stabilizing coastal soils.
