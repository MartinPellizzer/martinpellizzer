Abutilon ranadei

Abutilon ranadei, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with multiple stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Abutilon ranadei is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
