Aristida plumosa var. eichwaldiana

Aristida plumosa var. eichwaldiana, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like foliage that gives it a soft, feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant landscaping.
