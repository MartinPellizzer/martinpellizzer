Arnebia fimbriata var. bungei

Arnebia fimbriata var. bungei, commonly known as the Bunge's Armenian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers with five petals, often adorned with a distinctive white or pink border. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its ornamental appeal in horticultural landscapes.
