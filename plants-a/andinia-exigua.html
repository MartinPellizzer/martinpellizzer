Andinia exigua

Andinia exigua, commonly known as the dwarf lily of the valley, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and form a dense rosette near the ground.

Andinia exigua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
