Astragalus australis var. rubra

Astragalus australis var. rubra, commonly known as the red milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pink to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility, and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
