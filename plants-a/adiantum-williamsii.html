Adiantum williamsii

Adiantum williamsii, commonly known as the silky maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to the southeastern United States.

It features finely divided, arching fronds that are covered in soft, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and thrives in well-draining, humus-rich soil. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Adiantum williamsii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
