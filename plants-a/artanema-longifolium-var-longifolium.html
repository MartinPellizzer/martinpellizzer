Artanema longifolium var. longifolium

Artanema longifolium var. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved artanema, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The leaves are elongated, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
