Agathosma spinescens

Agathosma spinescens, commonly known as the Cape aster, is a South African endemic shrub belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid conditions, featuring hairy leaves that reduce water loss. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a valued plant in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
