Acmena lanceolata

Acmena lanceolata, commonly known as the glossy buckthorn, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by bright red or orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or dry woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
