Acinodendron amblyandrum

Acinodendron amblyandrum, commonly known as the African velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its rough, often hairy bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
