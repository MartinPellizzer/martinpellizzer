Acer pseudosieboldianum var. ishidoyanum

Acer pseudosieboldianum var. ishidoyanum, commonly known as the Ishidoyama maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within mountainous regions. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
