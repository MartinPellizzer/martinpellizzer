Ampelodesmos tenax

Ampelodesmos tenax, commonly known as the rock fig, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, evergreen foliage that thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are an important food source for various animals. Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Ampelodesmos tenax is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
