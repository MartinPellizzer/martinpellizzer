Aigiros nigra

Aigiros nigra, commonly known as black mangrove, is a species of mangrove tree native to the coastal regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and its ability to thrive in saline and tidal environments. Aigiros nigra plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
