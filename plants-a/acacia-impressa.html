Acacia impressa

Acacia impressa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Acacia impressa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

Its root system helps prevent soil erosion, contributing to its ecological importance in native landscapes.
