Acacia oblonga

Acacia oblonga, commonly known as the narrowleaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and narrow, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. Acacia oblonga is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and role in ecological restoration.
