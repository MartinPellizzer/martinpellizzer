Acacia nilotica var. typica

Acacia nilotica var. typica, commonly known as the Egyptian acacia or the gum arabic tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are typically reduced to spines in mature plants. The plant produces yellow to golden-orange flowers arranged in cylindrical inflorescences, followed by flat, elongated pods containing seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a common species in savanna and desert regions.

The tree is also valued for its gum arabic, which is harvested from its bark and used in food, pharmaceutical, and industrial applications.

Native Distribution

Acacia nilotica var. typica is primarily native to regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of southern Asia.

It is commonly found in countries such as Sudan, Ethiopia, Eritrea, and Somalia. This plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in savannas and along riverbanks. It is well adapted to hot and dry climates with poor soil conditions.

Its natural range extends into parts of India and Pakistan as well.

Introduced Distribution
