Armeria filicaulis subsp. valentina

Armeria filicaulis subsp.

valentina, commonly known as the Valentine's thrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features dense clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and long flowering period make it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
