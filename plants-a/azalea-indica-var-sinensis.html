Azalea indica var. sinensis

Azalea indica var. sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in spring, typically in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
