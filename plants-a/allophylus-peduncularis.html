Allophylus peduncularis

Allophylus peduncularis, commonly known as the clustered holly or small-leaved holly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, with small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, red drupe that contains a single seed, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

Allophylus peduncularis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
