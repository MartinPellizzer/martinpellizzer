Aristolochia kaempferi

Aristolochia kaempferi, commonly known as the Japanese birthwort, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, intricate, and often twisted leaves that are heart-shaped with a deep green color. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically purple or greenish in color, resembling a pipe or horn, which is a common feature in the Aristolochiaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal uses in traditional medicine, though some parts may contain toxic compounds.
