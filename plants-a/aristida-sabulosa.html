Aristida sabulosa

Aristida sabulosa, commonly known as sand bluestem, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often white or gray in color. It plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing forage for wildlife.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and resilient species in challenging environments.
