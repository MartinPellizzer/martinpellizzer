Aira complanata

Aira complanata, commonly known as flat air grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant has narrow, flat blades and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are often characterized by a distinct midrib and a smooth, grass-like texture.

Aira complanata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
