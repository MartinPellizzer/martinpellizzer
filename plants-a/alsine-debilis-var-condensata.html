Alsine debilis var. condensata

Alsine debilis var. condensata, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It features small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in dense clusters along the stems. The plant produces tiny, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or gravelly habitats.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it an adaptable species in harsh environments.
