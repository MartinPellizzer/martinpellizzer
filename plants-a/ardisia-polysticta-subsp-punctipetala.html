Ardisia polysticta subsp. punctipetala

Ardisia polysticta subsp.

punctipetala, commonly known as the spotted ardisia, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia. It features glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive white or pale spots along the midribs.

The plant produces small, urn-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Its foliage and berries make it a popular choice for woodland gardens and as a houseplant.
