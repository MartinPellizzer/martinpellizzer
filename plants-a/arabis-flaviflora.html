Arabis flaviflora

Arabis flaviflora, commonly known as the yellow-flowered rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. This perennial herb typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by round, capsule-like fruits.

Its leaves are basal, rosette-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.
