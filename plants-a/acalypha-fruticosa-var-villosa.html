Acalypha fruticosa var. villosa

Acalypha fruticosa var. villosa, commonly known as velvetleaf acalypha, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a velvety texture, hence its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape and often have a slightly waxy appearance.

It is often found in moist, shady environments and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
