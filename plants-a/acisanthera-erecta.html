Acisanthera erecta

Acisanthera erecta, commonly known as the erect acanthus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy bracts. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences and are often brightly colored, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Acisanthera erecta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
