Anthericum physodes

Anthericum physodes, commonly known as the broad-leaved lily of the wall, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in damp, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Anthericum physodes is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local insect populations.
