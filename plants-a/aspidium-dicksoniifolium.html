Aspidium dicksoniifolium

Aspidium dicksoniifolium, commonly known as the Dickson's water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are usually bipinnate, with small, lance-shaped pinnae that give it a lacy appearance.

Aspidium dicksoniifolium is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in both indoor and outdoor settings.
