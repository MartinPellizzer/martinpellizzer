Agave auricantha

Agave auricantha, commonly known as the golden crown agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color with a waxy coating. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears yellow-green flowers, which are attractive to pollinators. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and well-drained soil.

Agave auricantha is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
