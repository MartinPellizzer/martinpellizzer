Alsine setacea subsp. phrygia

Alsine setacea subsp.

phrygia, commonly known as the Phrygian sandgrass, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or arid environments.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or pale in color. It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion in its habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
