Anomalanthus curviflorus

Anomalanthus curviflorus, commonly known as the curlflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally twisted inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, colorful flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of red, yellow, and orange. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy surface.

Anomalanthus curviflorus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique and striking appearance.
