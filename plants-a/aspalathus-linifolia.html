Aspalathus linifolia

Aspalathus linifolia, commonly known as the African red bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and small, needle-like leaves that are dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of bright red flowers during the summer months, which are highly attractive to pollinators. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun exposure.

Aspalathus linifolia is also known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional African medicine.
