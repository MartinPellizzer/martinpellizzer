Amygdalus zangezura

Amygdalus zangezura, commonly known as the Caucasian hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, similar to other members of the Prunus genus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for nut production, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and agricultural purposes.
