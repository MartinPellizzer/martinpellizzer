Abies obliqua

Abies obliqua, commonly known as the zigzag fir, is a species of conifer native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in a distinctive zigzag pattern along the branches. The cones of Abies obliqua are typically pendulous and contain edible seeds that are an important food source for various wildlife species. This tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found at higher elevations.

Its ecological significance lies in its role as a habitat provider and its contribution to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
