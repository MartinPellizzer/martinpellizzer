Achnodonton bulbosum

Achnodonton bulbosum, commonly known as the bulbous acorn plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, bulb-like underground stems that store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and succulent, adapted to reduce water loss.

Achnodonton bulbosum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
