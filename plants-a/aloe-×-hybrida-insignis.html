Aloe × hybrida insignis

Aloe × hybrida insignis, commonly known as the noble aloe, is a hybrid plant that results from the cross between Aloe aristata and Aloe polyphylla.

It is characterized by its striking, spirally arranged rosettes of fleshy, pointed leaves that are often adorned with distinctive markings. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing clusters of yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer. Native to South Africa, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a desirable ornamental plant.
