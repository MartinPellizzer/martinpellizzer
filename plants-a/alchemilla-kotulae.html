Alchemilla kotulae

Alchemilla kotulae, commonly known as the Kotula's lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Alchemilla kotulae is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
