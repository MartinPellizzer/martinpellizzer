Alternanthera ciliata

Alternanthera ciliata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red-stemmed alternanthera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
