Anagyris neapolitana

Anagyris neapolitana, commonly known as the Naples burr or Neapolitan burr, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the development of spiny, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
