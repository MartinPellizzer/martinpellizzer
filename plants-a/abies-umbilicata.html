Abies umbilicata

Abies umbilicata, commonly known as the white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its pyramidal shape, needle-like leaves that are typically light green, and its distinctive cones that hang downward. The tree thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in subalpine and alpine zones.

Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age, and it produces a fragrant resin that is used in various traditional and commercial applications.
