Alchemilla hoppeana var. longinodis

Alchemilla hoppeana var. longinodis, commonly known as the long-awned lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features rosettes of basal leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape, with a distinctive silver-gray underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. Its leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils.
