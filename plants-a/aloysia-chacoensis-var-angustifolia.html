Aloysia chacoensis var. angustifolia

Aloysia chacoensis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and grassland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability.

The species is also valued for its potential in traditional medicine and ecological restoration projects.
