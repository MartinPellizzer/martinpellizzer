Acacia stipellata

Acacia stipellata, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its grey-green, feathery foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The plant produces long, flat pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions and dry inland areas.

Acacia stipellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand drought conditions.
