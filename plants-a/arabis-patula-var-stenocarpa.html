Arabis patula var. stenocarpa

Arabis patula var. stenocarpa, commonly known as narrow-seeded rockcress, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to rocky and alpine regions of Europe.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has a compact growth habit with fleshy, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate flowers and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
