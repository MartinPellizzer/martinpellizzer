Aristida funiculata var. stocksii

Aristida funiculata var. stocksii, commonly known as the narrowleaf dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like foliage with a distinctive silver-blue hue, and its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, fluffy flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy ornamental grass.

Its delicate appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
