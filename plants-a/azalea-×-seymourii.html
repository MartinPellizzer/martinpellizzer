Azalea × seymourii

Azalea × seymourii, commonly known as the Shirley azalea, is a hybrid shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and showy, funnel-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer with vibrant shades of red, pink, and white. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Azalea × seymourii is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
