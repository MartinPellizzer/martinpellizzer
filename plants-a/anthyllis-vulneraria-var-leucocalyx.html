Anthyllis vulneraria var. leucocalyx

Anthyllis vulneraria var. leucocalyx, commonly known as the white-flowered bitter vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow or white pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
