Aconitum willdenowii

Aconitum willdenowii, commonly known as Willdenow's aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant produces follicles containing elliptical, brown seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
