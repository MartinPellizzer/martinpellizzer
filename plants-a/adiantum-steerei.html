Adiantum steerei

Adiantum steerei, commonly known as the Steere's maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble lace. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive black midrib that contrasts with the light green fronds.

Adiantum steerei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
