Alternanthera flava

Alternanthera flava, commonly known as the golden Alternanthera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow foliage, which is particularly vibrant in full sun. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in aquatic gardens or as a decorative plant in water features.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
