Aralia vitiensis

Aralia vitiensis, commonly known as the Vitiensis aralia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the South Pacific, particularly Vanuatu.

It is a member of the Araliaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like the devil's ivy and poison ivy. The plant features compound leaves with a feathery appearance, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels. Aralia vitiensis is often found growing in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
