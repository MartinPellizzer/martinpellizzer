Ayenia geminifolia

Ayenia geminifolia, commonly known as the twinleaf ayenia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom in the spring.

Ayenia geminifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
