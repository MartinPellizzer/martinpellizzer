Anabasis articulata

Anabasis articulata, commonly known as the jointed goatweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its branching stems and small, greenish flowers. The plant has succulent leaves and stems, which help it retain water in dry environments. It often grows in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.

Anabasis articulata is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
