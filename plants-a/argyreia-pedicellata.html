Argyreia pedicellata

Argyreia pedicellata, commonly known as the red veined morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with prominent red veins and large, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to violet. The plant produces showy, spiral-shaped seed pods that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it requires careful management in garden settings.
