Antennaria neglecta var. petaloidea

Antennaria neglecta var. petaloidea, commonly known as the showy mouse-eared cress, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with dense, woolly foliage that is often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink to white, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

This species is adapted to rocky and dry environments, often found in open, sunny habitats.
