Asclepias euphorbiifolia

Asclepias euphorbiifolia, commonly known as the euphorbiifolia milkweed, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a common feature among the milkweed genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Asclepias euphorbiifolia serves as a host plant for the monarch butterfly, supporting its life cycle.
