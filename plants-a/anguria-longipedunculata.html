Anguria longipedunculata

Anguria longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-stemmed cucumber, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and large, lobed leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its fruit is a long, cylindrical cucumber with a smooth skin and a mild, sweet flavor.

This species is often cultivated for its edible fruit and is adapted to grow in warm, tropical climates.
