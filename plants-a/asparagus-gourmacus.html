Asparagus gourmacus

Asparagus gourmacus, commonly known as the purple asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and striking clusters of purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that turn from yellow to red as they mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique coloration and hardy nature make it a valuable addition to drought-tolerant landscapes.
