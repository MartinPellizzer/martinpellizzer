Arum cuspidatum

Arum cuspidatum, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces a spadix, which is surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of arum species. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and bog landscapes.
