Astragalus habaheensis

Astragalus habaheensis, commonly known as the Habahe milkvetch, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, particularly found in the Xinjiang Uyghur Autonomous Region of China. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, yellow to brown flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Astragalus habaheensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
