Abacosa canescens var. argentea

Abacosa canescens var. argentea, commonly known as silver abacosa, is a variant of the abacosa plant characterized by its silvery-gray foliage.

It is a shrub native to certain regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Uruguay. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers during the spring and summer months.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
