Astragalus echanensis

Astragalus echanensis, commonly known as the Echanensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus echanensis is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility in arid environments.
