Alocasia ramosii

Alocasia ramosii, commonly known as the "Black Velvet" Alocasia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns of lighter green. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes other well-known genera like Zantedeschia and Calla. Alocasia ramosii thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a statement plant in tropical landscapes.
