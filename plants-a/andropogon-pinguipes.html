Andropogon pinguipes

Andropogon pinguipes, commonly known as the hairy bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant features tall, slender stalks with narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, producing small, pale blue to purple flowers.

Andropogon pinguipes plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
