Arabis albida variegata

Arabis albida variegata, commonly known as the variegated whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is often grown in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate flowers.
