Aronicum carpathicum

Aronicum carpathicum, commonly known as Carpathian aronia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible and often used in jams and culinary applications. It thrives in woodland and shrubland habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

Aronicum carpathicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and wildlife.
