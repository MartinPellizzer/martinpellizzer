Astrocaryum paramaca var. javarense

Astrocaryum paramaca var. javarense, commonly known as the Java Wax Palm, is a species of palm native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clustered inflorescences with small, yellowish flowers, followed by woody, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
