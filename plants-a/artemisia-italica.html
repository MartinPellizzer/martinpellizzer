Artemisia italica

Artemisia italica, commonly known as sweet wormwood, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-green, aromatic foliage and small yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant contains high levels of essential oils, including thujone, which contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties. Artemisia italica is often used in traditional medicine for its antiparasitic and digestive benefits.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it adaptable to various garden environments.
