Allium huber-morathii

Allium huber-morathii, commonly known as the Morath's onion, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the high altitudes of the Eastern Alps, particularly in Austria and Italy. The plant features a bulbous stem with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pinkish flowers in umbel-like clusters. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
