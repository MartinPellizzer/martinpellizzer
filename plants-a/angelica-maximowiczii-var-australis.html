Angelica maximowiczii var. australis

Angelica maximowiczii var. australis, commonly known as the Australian angelica, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Australia.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, often branching near the top. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a strong, aromatic scent.

The roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
