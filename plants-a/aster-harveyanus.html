Aster harveyanus

Aster harveyanus, commonly known as Harvey's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its showy purple to blue flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Harvey's aster is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
