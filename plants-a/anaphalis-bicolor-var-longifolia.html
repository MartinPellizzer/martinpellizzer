Anaphalis bicolor var. longifolia

Anaphalis bicolor var. longifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf frostflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, woolly leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant produces a unique, frost-like appearance when its flowers are covered in ice during winter. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in horticultural settings.
