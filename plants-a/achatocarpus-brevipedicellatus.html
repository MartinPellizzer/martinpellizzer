Achatocarpus brevipedicellatus

Achatocarpus brevipedicellatus, commonly known as the short-stemmed acacia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features small, rounded leaves with a dense arrangement along the stems. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, arranged in small clusters, and it produces small, flat pods that contain seeds.

It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
