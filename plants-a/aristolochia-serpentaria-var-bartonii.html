Aristolochia serpentaria var. bartonii

Aristolochia serpentaria var. bartonii, commonly known as the narrowleaf Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, heart-shaped leaves and a distinctive, winding, pipe-like flower that is often purple or reddish in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex, tubular flowers that attract specific pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The species is also notable for containing aristolochic acids, which have been linked to human health concerns.
