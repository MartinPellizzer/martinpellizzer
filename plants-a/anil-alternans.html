Anil alternans

Anil alternans, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, alternating leaves that create a zigzag pattern along the stem. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Anil alternans is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
