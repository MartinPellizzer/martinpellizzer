Andropogon laxiflorus

Andropogon laxiflorus, commonly known as the looseflower bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as swamps and marshes, and is often found in wetland ecosystems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

Andropogon laxiflorus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
