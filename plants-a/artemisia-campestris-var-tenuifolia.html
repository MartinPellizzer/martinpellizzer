Artemisia campestris var. tenuifolia

Artemisia campestris var. tenuifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features silver-gray foliage with finely divided, linear leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of tiny, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in panicles, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been used in traditional medicine and as a herbal remedy for various ailments.
