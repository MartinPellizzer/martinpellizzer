Arum sintenisii

Arum sintenisii, commonly known as the Sintenisi arum, is a species of flowering plant native to the island of Sinten in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a colorful, leaf-like structure called a spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
