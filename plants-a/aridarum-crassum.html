Aridarum crassum

Aridarum crassum, commonly known as the thick-leaved desert shrub, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in drought conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warm season, typically in spring or early summer. Its root system is deep and extensive, enabling it to access moisture from underground water sources.

Aridarum crassum plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
