Aulomyrcia chilensis

Aulomyrcia chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Chile.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and glossy, dark green leaves. It thrives in temperate climates with mild winters and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Aulomyrcia chilensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited use in traditional medicine.
