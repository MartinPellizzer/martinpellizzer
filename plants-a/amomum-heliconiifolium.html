Amomum heliconiifolium

Amomum heliconiifolium, commonly known as the spiral-leaf cardamom, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental, spirally arranged leaves that give it a unique and striking appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by red or yellow berries, which contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in both horticultural and agricultural settings.
