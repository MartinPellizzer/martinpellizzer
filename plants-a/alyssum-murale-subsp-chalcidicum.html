Alyssum murale subsp. chalcidicum

Alyssum murale subsp.

chalcidicum, commonly known as the rock Alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has silvery-gray foliage with hairy leaves, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for landscaping and horticultural use.
