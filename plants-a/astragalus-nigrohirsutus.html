Astragalus nigrohirsutus

Astragalus nigrohirsutus, commonly known as black-hirsute milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, harsh environments. The plant features compound leaves with small, hairy leaflets and produces yellow or pale yellow flowers in late spring. Its pods are elongated and contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Astragalus nigrohirsutus is part of the Fabaceae family and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid landscapes.
