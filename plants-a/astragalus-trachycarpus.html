Astragalus trachycarpus

Astragalus trachycarpus, commonly known as the roughcarp Astragalus, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces unique, inflated fruit called a schizocarp, which splits into separate parts when mature. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or steppe environments.

Astragalus trachycarpus is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
