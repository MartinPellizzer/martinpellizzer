Alpinia longipetiolata

Alpinia longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled ginger, is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces fragrant, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to its appearance. Its rhizomes are similar to those of other ginger species, though less commonly used in culinary applications.

Alpinia longipetiolata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
