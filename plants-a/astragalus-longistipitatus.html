Astragalus longistipitatus

Astragalus longistipitatus, commonly known as the long-stemmed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to dry conditions, making it a hardy species in harsh environments. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain viable in the soil for several years.

Astragalus longistipitatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
