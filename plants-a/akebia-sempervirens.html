Akebia sempervirens

Akebia sempervirens, commonly known as the Japanese arrowroot or climbing hempweed, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers in early spring. The plant is notable for its edible tuberous roots, which are rich in starch and used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Akebia sempervirens is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
