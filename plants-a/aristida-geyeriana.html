Aristida geyeriana

Aristida geyeriana, commonly known as silvergrass, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. The plant has narrow, silver-gray leaves that help reduce water loss, and its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale flowers. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.

The species is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.
