Acer pseudosieboldianum f. ishidoyanum

Acer pseudosieboldianum f. ishidoyanum, commonly known as the Ishidoyanum maple, is a rare and ornamental variant of the Japanese maple.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely cut leaves that display a range of colors from bronze to deep green, often with a red or purplish tinge during the growing season. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
