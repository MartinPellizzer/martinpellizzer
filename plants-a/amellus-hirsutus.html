Amellus hirsutus

Amellus hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy amellus, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or stream banks. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its small, greenish-white flowers bloom in clusters during the summer months.

Amellus hirsutus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
