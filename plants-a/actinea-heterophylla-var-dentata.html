Actinea heterophylla var. dentata

Actinea heterophylla var. dentata, commonly known as the toothed actinia, is a species of sea anemone found in temperate coastal waters.

It is characterized by its distinctive, serrated oral disc and long, slender tentacles. The polyp body is typically pale pink to reddish in color, with a soft, gelatinous texture. This variant is often found attached to rocky substrates in shallow marine environments.

Its botanical classification places it within the order Actiniaria, reflecting its close relationship to other sea anemones.
