Acacia galioides f. denudata

Acacia galioides f. denudata, commonly known as the naked acacia, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often leafless stems. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typical of the acacia genus. Its leaves are usually reduced to small, scale-like structures, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
