Acetosella maritima

Acetosella maritima, commonly known as sea sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North America.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a reddish or purple tinge, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring. It is adapted to salt spray and can tolerate harsh coastal conditions.

Acetosella maritima is also known for its edible leaves, which have a tangy, sour flavor.
