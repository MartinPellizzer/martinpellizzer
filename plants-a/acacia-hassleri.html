Acacia hassleri

Acacia hassleri, commonly known as the silver acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, pale green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its succulent stems.

Acacia hassleri plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various desert animals.
