Athyrium thysanocarpa

Athyrium thysanocarpa, commonly known as the golden shield fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are covered in fine, golden hairs, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Athyrium thysanocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
