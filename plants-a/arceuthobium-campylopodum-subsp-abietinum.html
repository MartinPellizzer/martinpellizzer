Arceuthobium campylopodum subsp. abietinum

Arceuthobium campylopodum subsp.

abietinum, commonly known as the dwarf mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that primarily infests coniferous trees such as firs and spruces. It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients, forming dense clusters on the branches.

The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds, aiding in its spread. Its life cycle involves a complex interaction with its host, including the germination of seeds through a process called autochory.

This subspecies is notable for its unique morphology and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
