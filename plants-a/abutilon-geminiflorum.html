Abutilon geminiflorum

Abutilon geminiflorum, commonly known as the double-flowered abutilon, is a shrub native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and showy, lantern-shaped inflorescences that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant produces small, green flowers that develop into woody, spherical fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a standout in the genus Abutilon.
