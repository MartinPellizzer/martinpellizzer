Argyreia curtisii

Argyreia curtisii, commonly known as the red sanders or red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stem and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is often cultivated for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
