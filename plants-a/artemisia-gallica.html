Artemisia gallica

Artemisia gallica, commonly known as mugwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-green, aromatic leaves that are covered in fine, hairy glands. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clustered inflorescences. Mugwort is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is known for its strong, earthy scent.

It has been used historically in traditional medicine and as a component in herbal remedies.
