Alchemilla vulgaris var. trichocalycina

Alchemilla vulgaris var. trichocalycina, commonly known as the velvetleaf lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in compact clusters, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
