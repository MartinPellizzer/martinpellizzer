Alstonia spectabilis

Alstonia spectabilis, commonly known as the candlewood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, opposite, ovate leaves that are glossy green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces showy, fragrant yellow flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by woody, cylindrical fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is commonly found in coastal areas and lowland forests.
