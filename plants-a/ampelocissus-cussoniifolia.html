Ampelocissus cussoniifolia

Ampelocissus cussoniifolia, commonly known as the climbing fig, is a vine native to tropical Africa.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used as a ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it valuable for both ecological and medicinal purposes.
