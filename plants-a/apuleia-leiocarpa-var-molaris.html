Apuleia leiocarpa var. molaris

Apuleia leiocarpa var. molaris, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or rosewood tree, is a species native to Brazil, particularly the Atlantic Forest region.

It is a medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its dense, dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its tonal qualities in musical instruments. The leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. The flowers are small, fragrant, and white or pale yellow, arranged in panicles, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

This plant is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
