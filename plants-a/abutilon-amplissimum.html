Abutilon amplissimum

Abutilon amplissimum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Mexican abutilon, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common weed in agricultural areas.
