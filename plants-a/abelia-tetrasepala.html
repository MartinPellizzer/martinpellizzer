Abelia tetrasepala

Abelia tetrasepala, commonly known as the four-parted abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and slightly pubescent. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Abelia tetrasepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
