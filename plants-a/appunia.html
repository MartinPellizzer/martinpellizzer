Appunia

Appunia, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces a milky sap that contains latex, which can be toxic if ingested. Appunia thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
