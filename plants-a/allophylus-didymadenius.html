Allophylus didymadenius

Allophylus didymadenius, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, reddish-brown wood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are followed by small, spherical fruits containing a single seed.

The tree is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan.
