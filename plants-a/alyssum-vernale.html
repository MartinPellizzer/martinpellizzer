Alyssum vernale

Alyssum vernale, commonly known as spring alyssum, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial plant that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. It blooms from late spring to early summer and is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance.

Alyssum vernale is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
