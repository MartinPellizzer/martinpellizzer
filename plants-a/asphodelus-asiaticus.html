Asphodelus asiaticus

Asphodelus asiaticus, commonly known as the Asian asphodel, is a flowering plant native to regions of Central and South Asia.

It is a member of the Asparagaceae family and typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils. The plant produces tall, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and bears clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along riverbanks.

Asphodelus asiaticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
