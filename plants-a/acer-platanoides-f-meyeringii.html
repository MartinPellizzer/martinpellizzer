Acer platanoides f. meyeringii

Acer platanoides f. meyeringii, commonly known as the Meier's Norway maple, is a cultivar of the Norway maple (Acer platanoides).

It is characterized by its distinctive, large, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow margins. The plant is a deciduous tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit.

The leaves are usually serrated and can turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the autumn.
