Anacampseros subnuda subsp. subnuda

Anacampseros subnuda subsp.

subnuda, commonly known as the naked anacampseros, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a thin layer of white, powdery wax.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscape designs.
