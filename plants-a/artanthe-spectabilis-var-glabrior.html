Artanthe spectabilis var. glabrior

Artanthe spectabilis var. glabrior, commonly known as the showy artanthe, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features striking, showy flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, with five petals arranged in a radial pattern. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, though the varietal name "glabrior" suggests a smoother texture compared to the typical form. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or open habitats.

Its botanical interest lies in its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
