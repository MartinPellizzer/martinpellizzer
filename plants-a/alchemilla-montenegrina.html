Alchemilla montenegrina

Alchemilla montenegrina, commonly known as the Montenegro lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Alchemilla montenegrina is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
