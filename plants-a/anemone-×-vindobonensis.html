Anemone × vindobonensis

Anemone × vindobonensis, commonly known as the Vienna anemone, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Anemone coronaria and Anemone nemorosa.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white, often with a contrasting central blotch. The plant typically grows to a height of 15–30 cm, featuring upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lobed and hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This hybrid is known for its hardiness and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
