Acalypha subcinerea

Acalypha subcinerea, commonly known as silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its grayish-white, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Acalypha subcinerea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
