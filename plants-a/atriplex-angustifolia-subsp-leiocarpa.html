Atriplex angustifolia subsp. leiocarpa

Atriplex angustifolia subsp.

leiocarpa, commonly known as the narrowleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its slender, often hairless leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are often subtended by specialized bracts. It is well adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a common component of desert and coastal ecosystems.

This species plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
