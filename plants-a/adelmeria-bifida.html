Adelmeria bifida

Adelmeria bifida, commonly known as the twinleaf manzanita, is a shrub native to California and Oregon.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive two-lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that split open to release seeds.

Adelmeria bifida is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal and chaparral environments.
