Aquilegia vulgaris var. albiflora

Aquilegia vulgaris var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered columbine, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and compound leaves. The flowers are primarily white or pale pink, contrasting with the green foliage, and bloom in late spring to early summer. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and wildflower habitats.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it adaptable to a range of garden conditions.
