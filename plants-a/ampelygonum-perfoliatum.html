Ampelygonum perfoliatum

Ampelygonum perfoliatum, commonly known as the perforate speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, blue or purple flowers in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive perforation in the middle, which gives it its common name. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Ampelygonum perfoliatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
