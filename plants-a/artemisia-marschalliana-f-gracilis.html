Artemisia marschalliana f. gracilis

Artemisia marschalliana f. gracilis, commonly known as slender wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features silver-gray foliage with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and dry habitats, thriving in full sun.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and traditional herbal use.
