Aesculus maxima

Aesculus maxima, commonly known as the giant horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves and showy, white or pinkish flowers that appear in spring. The tree produces spiny capsules containing shiny, dark brown seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Aesculus maxima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in parks and gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
