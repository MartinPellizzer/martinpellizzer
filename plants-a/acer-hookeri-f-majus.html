Acer hookeri f. majus

Acer hookeri f. majus, commonly known as the large-leaf maple, is a variant of the hookeri maple species.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 15 centimeters in length. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters during the spring.

The fruit is a samara, a winged seed that aids in wind dispersal.
