Alyxia spicata

Alyxia spicata, commonly known as the spiny alyxia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has woody stems with thorn-like projections, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical in shape.

Alyxia spicata is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.
