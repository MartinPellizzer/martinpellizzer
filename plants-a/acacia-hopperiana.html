Acacia hopperiana

Acacia hopperiana, commonly known as the Hopper's wattle, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has bipinnate leaves, which are finely divided and give it a feathery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salt exposure.

Acacia hopperiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscaping.
