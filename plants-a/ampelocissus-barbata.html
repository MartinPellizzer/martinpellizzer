Ampelocissus barbata

Ampelocissus barbata, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing habit and distinctive, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are a key feature in its ecological role. The berries are also used in traditional medicine, highlighting its cultural and practical significance.
