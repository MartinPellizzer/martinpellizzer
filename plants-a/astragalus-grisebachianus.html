Astragalus grisebachianus

Astragalus grisebachianus, commonly known as the gray-barked milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus grisebachianus is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
