Acrostichum longifolium

Acrostichum longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaf fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its long, slender, and often arching fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests or near water bodies. Its fronds are usually compound, with a feathery appearance, and the leaves are often arranged in a spiral pattern.

Acrostichum longifolium plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
