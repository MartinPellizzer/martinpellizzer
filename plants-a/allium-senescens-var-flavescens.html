Allium senescens var. flavescens

Allium senescens var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow onion.

This plant is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its robust, bulbous stem and long, narrow leaves. The bulb, which is the primary edible part, develops underground and is known for its pungent flavor due to sulfur compounds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, typically growing in temperate climates.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities, with its striking purple flowers appearing in the summer.
