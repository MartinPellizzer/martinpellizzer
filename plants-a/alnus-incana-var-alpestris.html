Alnus incana var. alpestris

Alnus incana var. alpestris, commonly known as the alpine gray alder, is a shrub or small tree native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its grayish bark and small, simple leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spherical fruits called nutlets, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Alnus incana var. alpestris plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
