Abutilon senile

Abutilon senile, commonly known as the fuzzy abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. It is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 6 feet tall, with a sprawling habit that often requires pruning.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to landscapes seeking both aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
