Aspalathus trigona

Aspalathus trigona, commonly known as the African geranium, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright red or orange flowers, which bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Aspalathus trigona is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
