Achillea fruticulosa

Achillea fruticulosa, commonly known as yellow archangel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
