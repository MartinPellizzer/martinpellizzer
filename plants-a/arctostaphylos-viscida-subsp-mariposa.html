Arctostaphylos viscida subsp. mariposa

Arctostaphylos viscida subsp.

mariposa, commonly known as the Mariposa manzanita, is a shrub native to California and southern Oregon. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of small, leathery leaves.

The plant produces clusters of showy, pinkish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
