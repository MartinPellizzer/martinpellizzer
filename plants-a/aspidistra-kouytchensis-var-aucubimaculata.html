Aspidistra kouytchensis var. aucubimaculata

Aspidistra kouytchensis var. aucubimaculata, commonly known as the spotted aspidistra, is a rare and striking evergreen plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves that are often marked with distinctive white or pale spots, giving it a unique visual appeal. The plant is relatively low-maintenance, thriving in shady, humid environments with well-draining soil. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, followed by dark blue berries.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often sought after by collectors and enthusiasts of rare foliage plants.
