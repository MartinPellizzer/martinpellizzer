Azalea indica var. vittata

Azalea indica var. vittata, commonly known as the spotted azalea, is a flowering shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range from pink to red, often with a white or pale edge. The plant features narrow, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
