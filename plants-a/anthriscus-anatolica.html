Anthriscus anatolica

Anthriscus anatolica, commonly known as Turkish cow parsley, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish-white flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters called umbels. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Anthriscus anatolica is also valued as a ornamental plant for its delicate appearance and historical use in traditional medicine.
