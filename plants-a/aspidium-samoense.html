Aspidium samoense

Aspidium samoense, commonly known as the Samoan water fern, is a species of fern native to the Pacific islands.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks and in forest understories. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense network of roots, which help anchor it to substrates.

Aspidium samoense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
