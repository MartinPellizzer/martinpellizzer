Acanthophyllum ejtehadii

Acanthophyllum ejtehadii, commonly known as the Persian sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, shrub-like structure with fleshy, spine-tipped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. It is well adapted to harsh, dry environments, with deep roots that help it survive in sandy soils.

Acanthophyllum ejtehadii plays a role in stabilizing dunes and is of interest for its potential ecological and medicinal uses.
