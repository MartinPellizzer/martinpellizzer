Anthemis marschalliana subsp. sosnowskyana

Anthemis marschalliana subsp.

sosnowskyana, commonly known as the Sosnowsky's anthemis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray foliage and yellow-orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.

This subspecies is relatively rare and requires specific ecological conditions to thrive.
