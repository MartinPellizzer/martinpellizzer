Azalea × mortieriana

Azalea × mortieriana, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a hybrid evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and showy, funnel-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of vibrant pink, red, or white blooms in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its foliage remains lush and green throughout the growing season, providing year-round visual interest.
