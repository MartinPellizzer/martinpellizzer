Adenoglossa decurrens

Adenoglossa decurrens, commonly known as the slender leaf orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its flowers are small and typically white or pale green, arranged in a spike-like inflorescence.

Adenoglossa decurrens thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
