Allium ursinum var. ucrainicum

Allium ursinum var. ucrainicum, commonly known as the Ukrainian garlic chive, is a botanical variant of the common garlic chive.

It is a perennial herb native to Eastern Europe, particularly Ukraine, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, white or pink flowers. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its pungent, garlic-like aroma. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
