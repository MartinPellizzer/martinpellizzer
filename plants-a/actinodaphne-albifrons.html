Actinodaphne albifrons

Actinodaphne albifrons, commonly known as the white-fruited camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces yellowish-orange fruits that contain a single seed, and its bark contains essential oils used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
