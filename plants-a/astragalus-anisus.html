Astragalus anisus

Astragalus anisus, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Astragalus anisus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in ecological and medicinal contexts.
