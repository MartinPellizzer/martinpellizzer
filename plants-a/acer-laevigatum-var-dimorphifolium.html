Acer laevigatum var. dimorphifolium

Acer laevigatum var. dimorphifolium, commonly known as the Japanese maple with two-leaf form, is a variant of the Japanese maple characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features leaves that are typically alternate and palmately lobed, with a striking coloration that ranges from green to red or orange during the growing season. The plant is a deciduous tree or shrub, native to Japan, and thrives in temperate climates. Its botanical features include smooth, gray bark and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in spring.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
