Aporum rivesii

Aporum rivesii, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that supports its massive leaves and aids in buoyancy. Its flowers are typically white or pink, blooming at night and emitting a sweet fragrance to attract pollinators.

Aporum rivesii plays an important role in its ecosystem by contributing to water filtration and providing shelter for aquatic life.
