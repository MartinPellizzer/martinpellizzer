Aspidium chrysolobum

Aspidium chrysolobum, commonly known as the golden fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds that are typically bright green with a golden hue on the underside. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental appearance.

The plant's spores are dispersed by wind, contributing to its reproductive strategy in natural habitats.
