Aspilia burchellii

Aspilia burchellii, commonly known as the African flame flower, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Aspilia burchellii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
