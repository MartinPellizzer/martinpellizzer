Achyranthes totonaca

Achyranthes totonaca, commonly known as Mexican knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants such as buckwheat and sorrel. The plant features erect stems that are often reddish in color and covered with small, sticky hairs. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a distinctive reddish or purple hue on the underside.

The flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in clusters, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits.
