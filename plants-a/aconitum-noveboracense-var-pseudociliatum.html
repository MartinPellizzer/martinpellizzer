Aconitum noveboracense var. pseudociliatum

Aconitum noveboracense var. pseudociliatum, commonly known as the New York aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are compound, with palmate lobes, and the plant has a distinctive, poisonous alkaloid-rich root system.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
