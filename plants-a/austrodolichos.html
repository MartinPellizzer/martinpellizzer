Austrodolichos

Austrodolichos, commonly known as the Australian cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typically green with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Austrodolichos is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.

It thrives in warm climates and is often grown as a forage crop or for its edible seeds.
