Annona conica

Annona conica, commonly known as the sour sop or horned custard apple, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is a small, evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 5 meters tall, featuring thick, waxy leaves and distinctive, horn-like buds. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that develop into large, spiny fruits. Its fruit is characterized by a rough, green exterior with a creamy, aromatic pulp containing numerous black seeds.

Annona conica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its edible fruit, which is used in various culinary applications.
