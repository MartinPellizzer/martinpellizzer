Amaranthus retroflexus var. rubricaulis

Amaranthus retroflexus var. rubricaulis, commonly known as red-stemmed amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and hairy stems that turn reddish in color as the plant matures. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture and a bluish-green hue. It produces small, clustered greenish flowers that are followed by three-angled, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an invasive weed in some regions.
