Agave filifera

Agave filifera, commonly known as the threadleaf agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hair. The leaves are narrow and linear, with a bluish-green color and a distinctive thread-like appearance. It produces tall flower stalks that bear clusters of yellow-green flowers, which bloom after several years.

This plant is well adapted to hot, dry climates and requires minimal water once established.
