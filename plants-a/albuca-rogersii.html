Albuca rogersii

Albuca rogersii, commonly known as the Rogers' albuca, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, spherical bulbs that are often used for propagation. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Albuca rogersii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
