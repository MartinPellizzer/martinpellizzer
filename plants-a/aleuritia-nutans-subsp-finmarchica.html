Aleuritia nutans subsp. finmarchica

Aleuritia nutans subsp.

finmarchica, commonly known as the northern elderberry, is a shrub native to northern Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest edges and wetlands.

The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by dark purple berries that are edible. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have serrated edges.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to cold climates and its role in supporting local wildlife.
