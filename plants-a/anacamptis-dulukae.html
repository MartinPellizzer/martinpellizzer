Anacamptis dulukae

Anacamptis dulukae, commonly known as the Dukas' orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species found primarily in specific regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers with a distinct coloration pattern. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its morphology includes erect stems and lanceolate leaves, with flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
