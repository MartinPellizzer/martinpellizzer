Aponogeton longiplumulosus

Aponogeton longiplumulosus, commonly known as the long-plumed water arrowhead, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, arrow-shaped leaves that are often floating or partially submerged. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically borne on slender stems above the water surface. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the substrate in aquatic environments.

Aponogeton longiplumulosus is often cultivated in ponds and water gardens for its ornamental value.
