Amaranthus × ralletii subsp. soproniensis

Amaranthus × ralletii subsp.

soproniensis, commonly known as the Sopronian amaranth, is a herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
