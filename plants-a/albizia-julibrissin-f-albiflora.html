Albizia julibrissin f. albiflora

Albizia julibrissin f. albiflora, commonly known as the white silk tree, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves and showy, fluffy white flowers. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. Its flowers, which bloom in summer, are arranged in long, pendulous clusters and are highly ornamental.

The plant also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
