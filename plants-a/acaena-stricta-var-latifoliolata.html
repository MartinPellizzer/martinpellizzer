Acaena stricta var. latifoliolata

Acaena stricta var. latifoliolata, commonly known as the broad-leaved bullock's wheat, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by spiny, berry-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of New Zealand's native flora.
