Acacia pyrifolia var. morrisonii

Acacia pyrifolia var. morrisonii, commonly known as the Morrison's flame tree, is a variant of the flame tree species native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that appear in long, cylindrical clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are feathery and divided into small, narrow leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental tree in gardens and urban landscapes.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to both natural and cultivated environments.
