Arenaria fendleri var. brevifolia

Arenaria fendleri var. brevifolia, commonly known as desert sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically small, fleshy, and adapted to reduce water loss.

It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and provides habitat for small insects and animals.
