Astragalus dichroanthus

Astragalus dichroanthus, commonly known as the two-colored milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus dichroanthus is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.
