Anthericum gracillimum

Anthericum gracillimum, commonly known as the slender tawny sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale yellow to brownish flowers in dense, spike-like clusters. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands.

Anthericum gracillimum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various invertebrates.
