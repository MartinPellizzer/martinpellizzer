Anthericum leucocomum

Anthericum leucocomum, commonly known as the white nettle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive white, net-like bracts that surround the spadix. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered around the central spadix, a common feature in the Araceae family. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Anthericum leucocomum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
