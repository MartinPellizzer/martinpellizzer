Albuca nathoana

Albuca nathoana, commonly known as the Natal grass or Natal albuca, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features strap-like, grassy leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Albuca nathoana prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
