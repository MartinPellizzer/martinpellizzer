Allophylus calophyllus

Allophylus calophyllus, commonly known as the glossy buckthorn, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, glossy leaves that provide a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark purple berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Allophylus calophyllus is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
