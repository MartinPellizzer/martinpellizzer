Amaranthus vulgatissimus var. sublanceolatus

Amaranthus vulgatissimus var. sublanceolatus, commonly known as red root amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southern United States.

It features slender, reddish stems that often root at the nodes, giving it its common name. The leaves are lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and the plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas, along roadsides, and in fields.

This species is valued for its ornamental potential and has been used traditionally for its edible leaves and seeds.
