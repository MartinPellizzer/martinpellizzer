Allium schoenoprasum var. pumilum

Allium schoenoprasum var. pumilum, commonly known as dwarf chives, is a compact variety of the chive plant.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically shorter than those of the standard chive. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers in clusters, which appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This variety is prized for its mild garlic flavor and is often used in culinary applications.
