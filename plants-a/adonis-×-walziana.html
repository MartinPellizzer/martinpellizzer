Adonis × walziana

Adonis × walziana, commonly known as the hybrid foetid adder's-tongue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often fragrant, yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers are borne on tall, slender stalks.

Adonis × walziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
