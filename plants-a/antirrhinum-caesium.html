Antirrhinum caesium

Antirrhinum caesium, commonly known as the spiked snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Antirrhinum caesium thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value and ease of care contribute to its widespread use in horticulture.
