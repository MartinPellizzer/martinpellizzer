Aspalathus bracteata

Aspalathus bracteata, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its woody shrub form and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Aspalathus bracteata is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
