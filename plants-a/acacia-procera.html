Acacia procera

Acacia procera, commonly known as the umbrella thorn acacia, is a tall, woody shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its long, straight thorns and compound leaves that are often reduced to small, needle-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Acacia procera is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that allow it to access groundwater.

It plays an important role in providing shade and habitat in its native ecosystems.
