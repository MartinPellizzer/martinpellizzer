Alsine trivialis

Alsine trivialis, commonly known as common sandspurry, is a low-growing annual herb that thrives in disturbed soils and sandy environments.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by oval, fleshy fruits called胞果. It is often found in open areas such as roadsides, fields, and coastal dunes.
