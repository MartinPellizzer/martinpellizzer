Acacia samoryana

Acacia samoryana, commonly known as the Samoryana acacia, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, narrow leaflets.

Acacia samoryana is also valued for its hard, durable wood and has been used in traditional practices in some regions.
