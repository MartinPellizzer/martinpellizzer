Alchemilla oxyodonta

Alchemilla oxyodonta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Alchemilla oxyodonta is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
