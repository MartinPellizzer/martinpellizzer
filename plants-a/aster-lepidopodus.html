Aster lepidopodus

Aster lepidopodus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. The plant features showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture.

Aster lepidopodus is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
