Argentina litoralis

Argentina litoralis, commonly known as the coastal argentine plant, is a species native to the coastal regions of Argentina.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in early spring. It is well adapted to salt spray and wind, making it a resilient species in coastal environments.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
