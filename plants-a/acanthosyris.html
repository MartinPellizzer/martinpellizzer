Acanthosyris

Acanthosyris, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers. The plants typically grow in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and nutrient-poor soils. Acanthosyris species often have a short stature and are well-suited for xerophytic conditions.

Their flowers are usually arranged in a dense, globular head, typical of the Asteraceae family.
