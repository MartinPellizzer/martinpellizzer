Alchemilla × algida

Alchemilla × algida, commonly known as the silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, streambanks, and woodland edges.

Alchemilla × algida is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
