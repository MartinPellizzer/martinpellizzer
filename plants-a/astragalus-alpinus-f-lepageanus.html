Astragalus alpinus f. lepageanus

Astragalus alpinus f. lepageanus, commonly known as the alpine milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in cold, arid environments. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from nutrient-poor soils.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil in mountainous regions.
