Alyxia bracteolosa var. angustifolia

Alyxia bracteolosa var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that thrives on host trees in arid and semi-arid regions.

It has small, elliptical leaves that are typically narrow and pointed, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are usually red or yellow, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is adapted to harsh environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife.
