Abutilon carterae

Abutilon carterae, commonly known as the Carter's Abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

The plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
