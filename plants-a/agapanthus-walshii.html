Agapanthus walshii

Agapanthus walshii, commonly known as the white agapanthus, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features dense, spherical clusters of white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has strap-like, evergreen leaves that are blue-green in color and remain throughout the year. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
