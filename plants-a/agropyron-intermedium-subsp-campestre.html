Agropyron intermedium subsp. campestre

Agropyron intermedium subsp.

campestre, commonly known as the field wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, greenish-blue blades with a rough texture.

The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense, compact clusters along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields, meadows, and disturbed sites. Agropyron intermedium subsp.

campestre is valued for its forage quality and its ability to stabilize soil in agricultural and ecological settings.
