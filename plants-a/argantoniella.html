Argantoniella

Argantoniella, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, silver-gray leaves and clusters of pale blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It is native to regions of southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
