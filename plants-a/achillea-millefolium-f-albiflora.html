Achillea millefolium f. albiflora

Achillea millefolium f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered yarrow, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The white-flowered form distinguishes it from the more common varieties of yarrow.
