Apocynum × medium var. lividum

Apocynum × medium var. lividum, commonly known as the purple-leaf dogbane, is a hybrid shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking purple foliage, which contrasts with its green stems and leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink, blooming in late summer. It is a member of the Apocynaceae family, known for its milky sap and ornamental value.

This variety is often cultivated for its unique coloration and adaptability to various soil types.
