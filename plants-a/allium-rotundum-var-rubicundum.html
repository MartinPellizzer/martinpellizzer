Allium rotundum var. rubicundum

Allium rotundum var. rubicundum, commonly known as the purple onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features rounded, fleshy bulbs that are typically purple in color, which gives the plant its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are broad, flat, and grass-like, growing from the base of the plant.

The purple onion is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses, particularly in Mediterranean cuisines.
