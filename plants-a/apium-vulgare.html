Apium vulgare

Apium vulgare, commonly known as cow parsley, is a flowering plant in the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, umbrella-like clusters of small white or yellowish flowers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

Apium vulgare is known for its mild, celery-like flavor and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
