Andropogon koretrostachys

Andropogon koretrostachys, commonly known as the tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blue-green blades that turn coppery in the fall. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in prairie and woodland habitats. Its flowering spikes appear in late summer, producing small, pale blue to purple flowers.

Andropogon koretrostachys is valued for its ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
