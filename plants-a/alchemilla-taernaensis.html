Alchemilla taernaensis

Alchemilla taernaensis, commonly known as the alpine lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Alchemilla taernaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude environments.
