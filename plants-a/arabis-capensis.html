Arabis capensis

Arabis capensis, commonly known as candytuft, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has basal leaves that are lobed and hairy, and its stems are often hairy as well. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Arabis capensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
