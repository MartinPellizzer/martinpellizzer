Arabis rigidula

Arabis rigidula, commonly known as the rigid rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, and the plant is known for its hardy, drought-resistant nature.

Arabis rigidula is often used in rock gardens due to its ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
