Acinodendron secundiflorum

Acinodendron secundiflorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in savannas and rocky areas.

The species is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
