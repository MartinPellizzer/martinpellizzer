Allium schoenoprasum var. duriminum

Allium schoenoprasum var. duriminum, commonly known as the Siberian chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern regions of Russia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color, and it produces small, white or pale purple flowers in umbels. The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in cooking and traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in harsh, temperate climates.
