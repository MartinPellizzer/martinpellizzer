Anacyclus latealatus

Anacyclus latealatus, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces yellow flowers with a distinctive, flattened shape. The plant has a deep taproot system, allowing it to survive in dry conditions by accessing groundwater. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Anacyclus latealatus is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
