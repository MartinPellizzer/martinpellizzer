Actaea spicata f. arguta

Actaea spicata f. arguta, commonly known as the woolly green actaea, is a variant of the green actaea species characterized by its densely hairy, or pubescent, stems and leaves.

This plant is native to eastern North America and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage consists of compound leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in upright clusters. The plant's botanical features include its unique pubescence, which helps reduce water loss and protects against herbivory.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in woodland gardens.
