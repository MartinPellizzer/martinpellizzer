Acacia flagellaris

Acacia flagellaris, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, flagelliform branches that give it its common name, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the warmer months. The plant has phyllodes rather than true leaves, which are narrow and strap-like, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Acacia flagellaris is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules.

Its seeds are often dispersed by animals, contributing to its ecological role in arid ecosystems.
