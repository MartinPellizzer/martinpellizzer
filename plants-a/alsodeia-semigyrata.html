Alsodeia semigyrata

Alsodeia semigyrata, commonly known as the spiral sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils along stream banks and in wetlands. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing small, greenish-white flowers.

Alsodeia semigyrata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
