Aira subspicata

Aira subspicata, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Aira subspicata is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
