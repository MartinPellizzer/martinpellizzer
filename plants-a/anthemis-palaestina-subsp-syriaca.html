Anthemis palaestina subsp. syriaca

Anthemis palaestina subsp.

syriaca, commonly known as the Syrian tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It produces small yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different morphology compared to the nominate form, including variations in leaf shape and flower size.
