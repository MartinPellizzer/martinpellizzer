Adenophora ornata

Adenophora ornata, commonly known as the showy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to moist, well-drained soils.

Adenophora ornata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
