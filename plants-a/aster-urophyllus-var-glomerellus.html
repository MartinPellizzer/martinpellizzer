Aster urophyllus var. glomerellus

Aster urophyllus var. glomerellus, commonly known as the clustered blue aster, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features a compact rosette of basal leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers in the summer. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, spherical heads, giving it its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
