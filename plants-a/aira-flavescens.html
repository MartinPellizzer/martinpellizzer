Aira flavescens

Aira flavescens, commonly known as yellow air grass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with yellow or golden hues, especially at the base. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, pale yellow to brownish flowers.

Aira flavescens plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
