Acostaea colombiana

Acostaea colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian acostaea, is a species of aquatic plant native to the Andean region of Colombia.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are typically oval or elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and marshes.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptations to the high-altitude aquatic ecosystems of its native habitat.
