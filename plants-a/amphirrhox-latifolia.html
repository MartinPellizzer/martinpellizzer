Amphirrhox latifolia

Amphirrhox latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed amphirrhox, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its root system is fibrous and helps it thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Amphirrhox latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
