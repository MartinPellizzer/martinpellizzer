Atalanthus angustifolius

Atalanthus angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved atalanta, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to ecological restoration projects in arid environments.
