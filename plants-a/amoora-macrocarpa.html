Amoora macrocarpa

Amoora macrocarpa, commonly known as the broad-leafed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release its seeds. Amoora macrocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in gardens and natural habitats.
