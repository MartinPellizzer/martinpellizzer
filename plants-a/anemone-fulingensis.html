Anemone fulingensis

Anemone fulingensis, commonly known as the Fuling anemone, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive hairy texture, and it produces small, aggregate fruits that disperse its seeds.

Anemone fulingensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
