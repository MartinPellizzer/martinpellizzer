Arachis palustris

Arachis palustris, commonly known as the marsh peanut, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows in wet or marshy environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has underground pods that contain edible seeds. Its leaves are alternate and palmately lobed, and it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Arachis palustris is often found in wetlands and along the edges of water bodies.
