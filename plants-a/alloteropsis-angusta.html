Alloteropsis angusta

Alloteropsis angusta, commonly known as narrowleaf star-grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 2 millimeters wide. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

Alloteropsis angusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
