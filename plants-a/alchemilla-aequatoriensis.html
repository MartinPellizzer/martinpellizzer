Alchemilla aequatoriensis

Alchemilla aequatoriensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian lady's mantle, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of fuzzy, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Alchemilla aequatoriensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
