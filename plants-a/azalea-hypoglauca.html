Azalea hypoglauca

Azalea hypoglauca, commonly known as the pale-green azalea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, particularly China and Korea.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant's leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth surface and a waxy texture. Azalea hypoglauca thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
