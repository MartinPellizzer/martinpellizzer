Acalypha integrifolia var. crateriana

Acalypha integrifolia var. crateriana, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are typically bright green and deeply lobed. The plant produces showy, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
