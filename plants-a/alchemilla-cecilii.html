Alchemilla cecilii

Alchemilla cecilii, commonly known as Cecil's lady's mantle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, velvety leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
