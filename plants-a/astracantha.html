Astracantha

Astracantha, commonly known as the star thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, globe-shaped heads and is native to the Mediterranean region. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and wastelands. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing purple or white petals that attract pollinators.

Astracantha is considered an invasive species in many areas outside its native range.
