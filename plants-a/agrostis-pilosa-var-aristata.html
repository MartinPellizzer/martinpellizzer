Agrostis pilosa var. aristata

Agrostis pilosa var. aristata, commonly known as the hairy bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted leaves and dense, low-growing habit, making it suitable for lawns and turfgrass applications. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are typically covered with short, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.
