Asplenium cuneifolium subsp. dacicum

Asplenium cuneifolium subsp.

dacicum, commonly known as the Dacian spleenwort, is a fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae. It is a small, epiphytic or lithophytic plant, typically growing on rocks or tree trunks in moist, shaded environments.

The plant has lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are usually dark green and densely packed. It produces spore-bearing structures called sorus, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

This subspecies is native to parts of Europe and is often found in temperate forest habitats.
