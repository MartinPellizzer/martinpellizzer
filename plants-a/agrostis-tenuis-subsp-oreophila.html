Agrostis tenuis subsp. oreophila

Agrostis tenuis subsp.

oreophila, commonly known as the alpine bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to tolerate cold temperatures and high altitudes.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
