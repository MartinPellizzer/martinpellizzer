Acer cappadocicum f. rubrum

Acer cappadocicum f. rubrum, commonly known as the Crimson Maple, is a rare and striking variant of the Cappadocian maple.

This plant is characterized by its vibrant red foliage, which intensifies during the autumn season. It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in temperate climates. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a serrated margin, adding to its ornamental value.

Due to its limited natural range and unique coloration, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens and botanical collections.
