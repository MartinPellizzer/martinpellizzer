Arenaria bisulca

Arenaria bisulca, commonly known as the two-ribed sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates. Its leaves are often ribbed, giving it the species name *bisulca*, which means "two-ribbed" in Latin.

Arenaria bisulca plays a role in stabilizing soil in its harsh environments and is often found in tundra and mountainous regions.
