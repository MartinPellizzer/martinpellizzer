Aydendron aciphyllum

Aydendron aciphyllum, commonly known as the spiny leaf tree, is a perennial shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Aydendron aciphyllum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
