Abronia villosa var. villosa

Abronia villosa var. villosa, commonly known as the woolly bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique texture and color, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate drought conditions once established.
