Aneurolepidium dasystachys

Aneurolepidium dasystachys, commonly known as the broad-stemmed grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, upright growth habit and dense, tufted foliage. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields, meadows, and along roadsides.

Aneurolepidium dasystachys is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in various ecological settings.
