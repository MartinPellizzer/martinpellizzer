Aquarius decumbens

Aquarius decumbens, commonly known as the creeping aquarius, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It typically features prostrate stems that spread along the ground, often rooting at the nodes. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand arid conditions.

Aquarius decumbens is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
