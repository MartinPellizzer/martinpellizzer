Amaryllis variabilis

Amaryllis variabilis, commonly known as the variable amaryllis, is a bulbous plant native to South America.

It produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, white, and yellow, often with a contrasting central stripe. The plant features strap-like, dark green leaves that emerge from a basal rosette of fleshy, white or purple bulbs. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Amaryllis variabilis is popular as a decorative plant, especially during the winter and early spring seasons.
