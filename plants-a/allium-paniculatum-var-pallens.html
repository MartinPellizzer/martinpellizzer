Allium paniculatum var. pallens

Allium paniculatum var. pallens, commonly known as the pale onion or silver leaf garlic.

It is a perennial herb native to Central Asia, characterized by its bulbous base and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silvery-white coating, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental qualities and mild garlic-like scent.
