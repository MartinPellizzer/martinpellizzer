Allium severtzovioides

Allium severtzovioides, commonly known as the Siberian leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers in late summer, which are typically borne on tall, slender stems. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, garlic-like flavor, making it a potential candidate for cultivation in temperate gardens.

Allium severtzovioides is adapted to cold climates and can thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.
