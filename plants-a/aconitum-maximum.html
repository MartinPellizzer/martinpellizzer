Aconitum maximum

Aconitum maximum, commonly known as the giant monkshood, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces large, showy blue or purple flowers arranged in a helmet-like shape, which is typical of the genus Aconitum. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply cut lobes that give it a distinctive appearance.

Aconitum maximum is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and contains toxic alkaloids, making it both a beautiful and dangerous plant.
