Aphyllon corymbosum

Aphyllon corymbosum, commonly known as the common tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that are often reduced to mere structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its root system is extensive and can tolerate saline and arid conditions, making it adaptable to various environments.

Aphyllon corymbosum is often used in landscaping due to its drought resistance and ornamental value.
