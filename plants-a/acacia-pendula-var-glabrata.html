Acacia pendula var. glabrata

Acacia pendula var. glabrata, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and small, feathery leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are highly attractive to bees and other pollinators. Its wood is lightweight and has been used traditionally for making tools and crafts.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
