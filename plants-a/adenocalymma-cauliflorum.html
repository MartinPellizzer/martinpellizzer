Adenocalymma cauliflorum

Adenocalymma cauliflorum, commonly known as the Cape fuchsia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique cauliflorous growth habit, where flowers and fruits develop directly on the trunk and branches. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to purple, often attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. Its leaves are typically thick and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in its arid native environment.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
