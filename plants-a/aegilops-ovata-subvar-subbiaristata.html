Aegilops ovata subvar. subbiaristata

Aegilops ovata subvar. subbiaristata, commonly known as the slender wild oat, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with distinctive, awned glumes. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

This subspecies is of interest for its potential in forage and as a wild relative of cultivated wheat.
