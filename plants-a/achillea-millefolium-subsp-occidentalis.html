Achillea millefolium subsp. occidentalis

Achillea millefolium subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as western yarrow, is a flowering plant native to western North America. It is a perennial herb with finely divided, fern-like leaves and flat, composite flower heads that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. It has a widespread distribution and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The subspecies is closely related to the more widely known common yarrow, but it exhibits distinct morphological and ecological characteristics.
