Aria porrigentiformis

Aria porrigentiformis, commonly known as the spiral-leaf fern, is a unique fern species characterized by its distinctive, curling fronds that resemble spirals.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that unfurl in a helical pattern, creating an intricate visual display. Its rhizome is creeping and produces numerous aerial roots, which aid in its attachment to trees and rocks.

Aria porrigentiformis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and adaptability to shaded environments.
