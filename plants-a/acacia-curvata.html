Acacia curvata

Acacia curvata, commonly known as the warty acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, warty thorns and compound leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Acacia curvata thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

It is well adapted to drought conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
