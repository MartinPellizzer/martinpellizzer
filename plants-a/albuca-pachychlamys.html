Albuca pachychlamys

Albuca pachychlamys, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, or orange. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. It is known for its long blooming period, typically from late winter to early spring.

Albuca pachychlamys is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
