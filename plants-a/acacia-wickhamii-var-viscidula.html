Acacia wickhamii var. viscidula

Acacia wickhamii var. viscidula, commonly known as the sticky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular-pubescent foliage, which gives it a sticky texture. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy coating.

It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in dry, rocky soils.
