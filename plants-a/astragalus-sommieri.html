Astragalus sommieri

Astragalus sommieri, commonly known as the summer milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus sommieri plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.
