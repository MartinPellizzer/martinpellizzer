Aristolochia krisagathra

Aristolochia krisagathra, commonly known as the Thai horse chestnut, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, and often fragrant flowers that resemble a horse's hoof, giving it its common name. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, colorful aril. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a velvety texture and are often used in traditional medicine.

However, the plant is toxic and should not be ingested.
