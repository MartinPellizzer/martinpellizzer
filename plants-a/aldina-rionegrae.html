Aldina rionegrae

Aldina rionegrae, commonly known as the Rio Negro Aldina, is a rare flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments along riverbanks and forest understories. Its botanical features include a short stem and clustered inflorescences.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
