Acacia camptoclada

Acacia camptoclada, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, yellow flowers and bipinnate leaves. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Acacia camptoclada thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various native animals.
