Amorpha fruticosa var. lewisii

Amorpha fruticosa var. lewisii, commonly known as Lewis's false indigo, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces striking blue to purple pea-shaped flowers in late spring. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient plant suitable for native landscaping.
