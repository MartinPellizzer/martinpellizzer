Andinia dentata

Andinia dentata, commonly known as the toothed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.
