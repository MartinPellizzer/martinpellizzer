Arctostaphylos glabrata

Arctostaphylos glabrata, commonly known as the smooth manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and clusters of small, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers.

Arctostaphylos glabrata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
