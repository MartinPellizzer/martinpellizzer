Achillea monticola

Achillea monticola, commonly known as mountain yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its flat, composite flower heads that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for alpine gardens. Its feathery leaves and delicate flowers make it an attractive addition to wildflower displays.

It is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
