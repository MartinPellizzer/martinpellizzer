Alyssum glomeratum

Alyssum glomeratum, commonly known as clustered alyssum, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming from spring to summer. The plant has small, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum glomeratum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
