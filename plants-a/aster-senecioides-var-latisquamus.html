Aster senecioides var. latisquamus

Aster senecioides var. latisquamus, commonly known as the broad-leaved aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its coarse, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a valuable resource for pollinators.
