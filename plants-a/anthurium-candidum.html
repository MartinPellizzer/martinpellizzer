Anthurium candidum

Anthurium candidum, commonly known as the white spathiphyllum, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. The plant produces striking white spathes that enclose clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Anthurium candidum is popular as a decorative plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
