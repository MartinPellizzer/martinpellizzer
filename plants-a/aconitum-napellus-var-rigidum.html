Aconitum napellus var. rigidum

Aconitum napellus var. rigidum, commonly known as the rigid monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a strong, poisonous aroma. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic nature, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
