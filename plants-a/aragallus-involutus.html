Aragallus involutus

Aragallus involutus, commonly known as the curled lily, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and often rolled at the edges, giving it its common name.

Aragallus involutus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
