Astragalus gaillardotii

Astragalus gaillardotii, commonly known as the Gaillardot's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with compound leaves and produces small, yellow to greenish flowers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
