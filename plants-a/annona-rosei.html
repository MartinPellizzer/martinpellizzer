Annona rosei

Annona rosei, commonly known as the rose apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with a dense canopy and smooth, greyish bark. The leaves are large, elliptical, and dark green, while the flowers are fragrant and white with a yellow center. The fruit is a round, greenish-yellow berry that turns red when ripe, containing a single large seed.

This plant is valued for its edible fruit and is often cultivated in warm climates for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
