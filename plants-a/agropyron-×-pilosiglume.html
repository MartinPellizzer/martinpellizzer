Agropyron × pilosiglume

Agropyron × pilosiglume, commonly known as the intermediate wheatgrass, is a hybrid grass species native to the Great Plains of North America.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to various soil conditions. The plant produces spikelets that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it its scientific name. It is valued for its high forage quality and potential as a bioenergy crop.

Its botanical features make it an important species in agricultural and ecological contexts.
