Alstroemeria ligtu

Alstroemeria ligtu, commonly known as the pink alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with long, narrow leaves arranged in a rosette pattern.

Its flowers are known for their longevity and are often used in floral arrangements due to their vibrant colors and durability.
