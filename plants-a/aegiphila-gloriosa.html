Aegiphila gloriosa

Aegiphila gloriosa, commonly known as the gloryflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, tropical environments, often near streams or in shaded forest understories. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. Its leaves are usually opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Aegiphila gloriosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
