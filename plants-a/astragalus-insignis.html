Astragalus insignis

Astragalus insignis, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions.

Astragalus insignis is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native range.
