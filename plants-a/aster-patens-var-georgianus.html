Aster patens var. georgianus

Aster patens var. georgianus, commonly known as the Georgia aster, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
