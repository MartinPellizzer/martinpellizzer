Asperula trifida

Asperula trifida, commonly known as the clustered wood rose, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of about 30 centimeters. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three leaflets, and are often hairy to the touch.

Asperula trifida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
