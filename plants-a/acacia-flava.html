Acacia flava

Acacia flava, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typically followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Acacia flava thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

It is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
