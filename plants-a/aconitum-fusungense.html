Aconitum fusungense

Aconitum fusungense, commonly known as the Fusung monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple-blue flowers arranged in a helmet-like shape. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. Aconitum fusungense is known for its toxic alkaloids, which make it a plant to be handled with care.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
