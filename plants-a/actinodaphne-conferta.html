Actinodaphne conferta

Actinodaphne conferta, commonly known as the Malabar camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces dark, glossy berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and resistance to pests.
