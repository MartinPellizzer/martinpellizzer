Acacia andamanica

Acacia andamanica, commonly known as the Andaman acacia, is a flowering plant native to the Andaman Islands.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in coastal and rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in globular clusters, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Acacia andamanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
