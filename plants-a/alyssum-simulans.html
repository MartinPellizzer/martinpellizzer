Alyssum simulans

Alyssum simulans, commonly known as the rock allyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has succulent, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Alyssum simulans is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like butterflies and bees.
