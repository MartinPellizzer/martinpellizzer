Alchemilla anisiaca

Alchemilla anisiaca, commonly known as sweet Alison, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its silver-green foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces oval, herbaceous leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

Alchemilla anisiaca is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
