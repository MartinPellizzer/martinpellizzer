Arum immaculatum

Arum immaculatum, commonly known as the white arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features a large, showy spathe that is usually white or pale green, surrounding a spadix that bears tiny, clustered flowers. The plant is characterized by its arrow-shaped, heart-veined leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers emit a mild, pleasant fragrance, especially in the evening.
