Arisaema stenophyllum

Arisaema stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered around the spadix. Its leaves are narrow and arrow-shaped, giving it its common name, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Arisaema stenophyllum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
