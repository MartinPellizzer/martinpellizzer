Arthraxon junghuhnii

Arthraxon junghuhnii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grass or Junghuhn's grass, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Arthraxon junghuhnii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
