Alstroemeria chapadensis

Alstroemeria chapadensis, commonly known as the Andean lily, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while its stems are upright and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Alstroemeria chapadensis is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in some indigenous communities.
