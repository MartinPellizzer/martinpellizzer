Astragalus holmgreniorum

Astragalus holmgreniorum, commonly known as the Holmgren's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus holmgreniorum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinator populations.
