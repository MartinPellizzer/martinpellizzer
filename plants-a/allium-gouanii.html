Allium gouanii

Allium gouanii, commonly known as the Gouan's garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features strap-like, grassy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a useful addition to culinary dishes.

Allium gouanii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, preferring temperate climates.
