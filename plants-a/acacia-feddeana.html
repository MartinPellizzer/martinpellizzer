Acacia feddeana

Acacia feddeana, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and produces yellow to golden globular flower heads. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-resistant. Its bark and leaves have been used traditionally for medicinal purposes.

Acacia feddeana plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
