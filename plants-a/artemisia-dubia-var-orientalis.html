Artemisia dubia var. orientalis

Artemisia dubia var. orientalis, commonly known as oriental mugwort, is a perennial herb native to regions of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, pale green to yellowish-gray leaves. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that are typically inconspicuous and clustered in branched inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thujone and camphor, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
