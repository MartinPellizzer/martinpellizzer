Antirrhinum nuttallianum var. effusum

Antirrhinum nuttallianum var. effusum, commonly known as the Nuttall's snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
