Anthurium tamaense

Anthurium tamaense, commonly known as the Tamaense arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Ecuador and Colombia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces spadices, which are the modified inflorescences that house the actual flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium tamaense is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
