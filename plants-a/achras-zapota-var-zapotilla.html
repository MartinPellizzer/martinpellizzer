Achras zapota var. zapotilla

Achras zapota var. zapotilla, commonly known as the cherimoya or custard apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and northern South America.

It produces large, showy, white or pink flowers that are typically pollinated by bats and insects. The fruit is a tropical berry with a thick, leathery rind and a creamy, aromatic flesh containing numerous large, inedible seeds. The plant has a shallow root system and thrives in well-drained, fertile soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit, which is rich in vitamins and minerals.
