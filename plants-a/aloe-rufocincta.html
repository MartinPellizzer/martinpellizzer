Aloe rufocincta

Aloe rufocincta, commonly known as the red-banded aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically green with red or purple markings along the edges. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellow flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Aloe rufocincta is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
