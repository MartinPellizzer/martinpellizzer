Agathophytum acuminatum

Agathophytum acuminatum, commonly known as the sharp-pointed agathophytum, is a unique succulent native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a cylindrical stem with a distinctive, spiny appearance, which helps it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, pale green leaves that are adapted to minimize water loss. Its flowers, which are typically yellow or greenish, bloom in the early morning and are pollinated by insects.

Agathophytum acuminatum is prized by botanists and collectors for its striking morphology and ecological adaptations.
