Acacia stellata

Acacia stellata, commonly known as the star acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically bipinnate and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular inflorescences, which are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. Acacia stellata is adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in dry conditions.

It is also valued for its hard, durable wood and has been used in traditional medicine.
