Astragalus tephrosioides

Astragalus tephrosioides, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus tephrosioides is often found in open grasslands and rocky slopes, contributing to soil stabilization and supporting local biodiversity.
