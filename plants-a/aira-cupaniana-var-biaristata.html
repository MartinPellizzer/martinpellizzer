Aira cupaniana var. biaristata

Aira cupaniana var. biaristata, commonly known as the purple air plant, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically bluish-green with a waxy coating. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear small, purple or pink flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
