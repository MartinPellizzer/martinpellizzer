Augusta rivalis

Augusta rivalis, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from purple to pink, with a distinctive central cone. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Augusta rivalis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
