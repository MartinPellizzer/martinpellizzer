Allium suaveolens var. appendiculatum

Allium suaveolens var. appendiculatum, commonly known as the fragrant garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Himalayas.

It features long, narrow leaves that are typically bluish-green in color and emit a mild garlic scent when crushed. The plant produces small, spherical umbels of white or pale purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a popular ingredient in Asian cuisine.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary versatility in gardens and kitchens.
