Anthodiscus

Anthodiscus, commonly known as the starflower, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped flowers, which typically consist of five to seven petals. The plants are native to various regions in Africa, South America, and parts of Asia. Anthodiscus species often thrive in moist, well-drained soils and are commonly found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their vibrant floral displays.
