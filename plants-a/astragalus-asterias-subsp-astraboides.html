Astragalus asterias subsp. astraboides

Astragalus asterias subsp.

astraboides, commonly known as the Astragalus astraboides, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance.
