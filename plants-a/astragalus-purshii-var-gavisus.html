Astragalus purshii var. gavisus

Astragalus purshii var. gavisus, commonly known as Gavies' milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It has a deep taproot system, which allows it to survive in arid conditions.

This species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators.
