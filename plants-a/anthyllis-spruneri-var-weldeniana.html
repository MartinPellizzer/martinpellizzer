Anthyllis spruneri var. weldeniana

Anthyllis spruneri var. weldeniana, commonly known as the Welden's anthyllis, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to arid conditions. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is often found in open habitats such as coastal cliffs and dry grasslands.
