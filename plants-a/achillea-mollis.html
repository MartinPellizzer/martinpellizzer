Achillea mollis

Achillea mollis, commonly known as velvetleaf yarrow, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-green foliage that gives it a distinctive, velvety appearance. The plant produces flat, composite flower heads composed of numerous small white to pale yellow florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Achillea mollis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
