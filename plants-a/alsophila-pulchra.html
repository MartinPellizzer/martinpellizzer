Alsophila pulchra

Alsophila pulchra, commonly known as the elegant tree fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia and parts of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, compound leaves that give it a graceful appearance. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is typically found in humid, tropical environments. Its fronds are often used in traditional crafts and as ornamental plants in gardens.

Alsophila pulchra plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and shelter for various small animals and insects.
