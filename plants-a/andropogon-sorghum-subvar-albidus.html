Andropogon sorghum subvar. albidus

Andropogon sorghum subvar. albidus, commonly known as white sorghum, is a variant of sorghum characterized by its pale or white inflorescence.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is native to Africa, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is known for its tall, robust stalks and is often cultivated for its grain, which is used in food and animal feed. Its botanical features include narrow leaves and a fibrous stem, making it adaptable to various soil conditions.

This subvariety is also valued for its drought tolerance and potential in biofuel production.
