Adonis aestivalis subsp. marginata

Adonis aestivalis subsp.

marginata, commonly known as the marginated globemallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features basal rosettes of lanceolate, dark green leaves with distinctive white margins.

The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in racemes. Its flowers are characterized by five narrow, spreading petals and a prominent yellow throat.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
