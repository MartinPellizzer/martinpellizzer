Amaranthus melancholicus var. obovatus

Amaranthus melancholicus var. obovatus, commonly known as the weedy amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish flowers that transition to red or purple as they mature. Native to the Americas, this plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. It is an annual herb that grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters.

The plant is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
