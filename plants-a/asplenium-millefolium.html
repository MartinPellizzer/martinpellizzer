Asplenium millefolium

Asplenium millefolium, commonly known as the maidenhair spleenwort, is a fern that belongs to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided leaves that give it a lacy appearance. This plant is typically found growing in moist, shaded environments such as rock crevices or on tree trunks. It has a rhizomatous growth habit and can spread by producing spores.

Asplenium millefolium is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for.
