Astragalus gudrathii

Astragalus gudrathii, commonly known as Gudrathii milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in dry, harsh environments. Astragalus gudrathii is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various health benefits.

Its root system is deep, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers, which contributes to its resilience in arid climates.
