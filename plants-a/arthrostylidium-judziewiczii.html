Arthrostylidium judziewiczii

Arthrostylidium judziewiczii, commonly known as the glassy mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairless stems and small, clustered white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often along stream banks or in undergrowth. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Arthrostylidium judziewiczii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and indirect light.
