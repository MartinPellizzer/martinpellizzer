Alyssum sativum

Alyssum sativum, commonly known as sweet alyssum, is an annual flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom throughout the summer. The plant has compact, low-growing foliage with a mat-forming habit, making it ideal for rock gardens and borders. Its flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their delicate appearance and pleasant scent.

Alyssum sativum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
