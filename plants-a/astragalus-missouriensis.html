Astragalus missouriensis

Astragalus missouriensis, commonly known as Missouri milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It typically grows in prairies, open woods, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow to greenish flowers in late spring. Its root system is extensive, contributing to its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

Astragalus missouriensis is also valued for its role in ecological restoration and as a forage plant for wildlife.
