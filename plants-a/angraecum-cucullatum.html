Angraecum cucullatum

Angraecum cucullatum, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its unique, hood-like flower structure, which gives it its common name. The plant produces striking white flowers with a deep violet throat, often fragrant, which attract specific pollinators like moths. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Angraecum cucullatum is a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively manageable care requirements.
