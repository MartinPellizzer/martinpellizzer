Acrostephanus syringifolius

Acrostephanus syringifolius, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that are lanceolate in shape. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are characterized by their dense covering of fine white hairs, which give the plant its common name.

The plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
