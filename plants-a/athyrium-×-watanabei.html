Athyrium × watanabei

Athyrium × watanabei, commonly known as the Japanese maidenhair fern, is a hybrid fern species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Athyriaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bioluminescent in the dark. The plant produces spores on the underside of its leaves, which are released during the late spring and early summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses worldwide.
