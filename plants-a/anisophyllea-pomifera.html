Anisophyllea pomifera

Anisophyllea pomifera, commonly known as the fig-leaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that resemble the shape of a fig leaf, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer.

Anisophyllea pomifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
