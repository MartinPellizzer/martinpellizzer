Actinocephalus geniculatus

Actinocephalus geniculatus, commonly known as the zigzagged mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging stems and palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
