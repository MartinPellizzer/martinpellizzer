Allium haussmannii

Allium haussmannii, commonly known as the Parisian onion, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, typically growing in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant features strap-like, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in late spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its mild, onion-like scent.

Allium haussmannii is relatively easy to grow and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
