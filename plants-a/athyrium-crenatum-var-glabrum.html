Athyrium crenatum var. glabrum

Athyrium crenatum var. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth fern, is a species native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is a fern characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs on the underside. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a distinctive, graceful appearance.

The varietal name "glabrum" refers to its smooth, hairless texture compared to other varieties of Athyrium crenatum.
