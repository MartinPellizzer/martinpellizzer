Amaryllis brasiliensis

Amaryllis brasiliensis, commonly known as the red belladonna lily, is a striking tropical plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of red, white, or pink. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that are dark green and often have a waxy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Amaryllis brasiliensis is also known for its ability to produce a strong, sweet fragrance, especially when in bloom.
