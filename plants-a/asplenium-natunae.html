Asplenium natunae

Asplenium natunae, commonly known as the New Zealand maidenhair fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble lace. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often on rocks or in forest undergrowth. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of its leaves.

Asplenium natunae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
