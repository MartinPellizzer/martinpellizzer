Aconitum chiachaense var. glandulosum

Aconitum chiachaense var. glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments that are often covered in glandular hairs.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
