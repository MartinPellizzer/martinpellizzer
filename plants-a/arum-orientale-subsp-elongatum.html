Arum orientale subsp. elongatum

Arum orientale subsp.

elongatum, commonly known as the elongated arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves that are often dark green with a glossy surface.

The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

However, it contains calcium oxalate crystals, making it potentially toxic if ingested.
