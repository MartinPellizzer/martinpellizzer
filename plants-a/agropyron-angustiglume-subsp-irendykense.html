Agropyron angustiglume subsp. irendykense

Agropyron angustiglume subsp.

irendykense, commonly known as the Irendyken needlegrass, is a grass species native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, spike-like inflorescence and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions. It has small, lanceolate leaves with a rough texture, and its flowering period occurs in late summer.

This subspecies is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing soil in dry regions.
