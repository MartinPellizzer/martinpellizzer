Agropyron bourgaei

Agropyron bourgaei, commonly known as the Mongolian grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters, and its leaves are narrow and often hairy. Agropyron bourgaei is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species for dry climates.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
