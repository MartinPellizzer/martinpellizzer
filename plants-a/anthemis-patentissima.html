Anthemis patentissima

Anthemis patentissima, commonly known as the Mexican tansy, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk, typical of the family, and has a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
