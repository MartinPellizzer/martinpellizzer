Astragalus bezudensis

Astragalus bezudensis, commonly known as the Bezudensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Astragalus bezudensis is known for its small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
