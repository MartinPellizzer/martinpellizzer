Apium carvi

Apium carvi, commonly known as caraway, is a biennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and distinctive umbellate inflorescences. The plant features slender, hollow stems and feathery, finely divided leaves. Its fruit, known as a "carpophore," is a dry, two-lobed structure that contains the aromatic seeds.

Caraway is cultivated for its seeds, which are used in cooking and traditional medicine.
