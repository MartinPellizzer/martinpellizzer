Acacia tomentella var. chrysocoma

Acacia tomentella var. chrysocoma, commonly known as the golden acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, greyish-white pubescent foliage and small, yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
