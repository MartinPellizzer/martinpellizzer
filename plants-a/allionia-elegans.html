Allionia elegans

Allionia elegans, commonly known as the silverleaf lily, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, silvery coating that reflects light.

Allionia elegans is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
