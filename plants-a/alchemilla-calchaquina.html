Alchemilla calchaquina

Alchemilla calchaquina, commonly known as the Andean lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of rounded, hairy leaves that resemble a shield. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Alchemilla calchaquina is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
