Argyrolobium pumilum

Argyrolobium pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf silver globular pea, is a low-growing, mat-forming annual plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, rounded, silver-green leaves and produces delicate, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in poor, well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is known for its ornamental value in garden settings.

The species is also valued for its role in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
