Astragalus cornutus

Astragalus cornutus, commonly known as the horned milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, curved seed pods. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, compound leaves that help reduce water loss. Its yellow flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Astragalus cornutus is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
