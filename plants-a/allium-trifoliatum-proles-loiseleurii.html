Allium trifoliatum proles loiseleurii

Allium trifoliatum proles loiseleurii, commonly known as the Loiseleur's garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green and emit a mild garlic scent when crushed. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild culinary uses.
