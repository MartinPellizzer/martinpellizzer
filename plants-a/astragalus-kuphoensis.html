Astragalus kuphoensis

Astragalus kuphoensis, commonly known as the Kuphoen's milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Astragalus kuphoensis is adapted to harsh, dry environments and has a deep root system to access water.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and plays a role in stabilizing desert ecosystems.
