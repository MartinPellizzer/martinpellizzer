Allium carinatum var. stramineum

Allium carinatum var. stramineum, commonly known as the Striped Leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically striped with white and green, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tall, slender stems topped with small, clustered white or pale purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its foliage and delicate floral display.
