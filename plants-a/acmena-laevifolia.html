Acmena laevifolia

Acmena laevifolia, commonly known as the satin leaf tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
