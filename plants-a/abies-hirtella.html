Abies hirtella

Abies hirtella, commonly known as the rough bark fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that are short and covered in a fine layer of white wax. The tree's bark is thick and furrowed, giving it its common name, and it produces small, rounded cones that mature to release seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests.

The rough bark fir plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
