Astragalus arpilobus subsp. arpilobus

Astragalus arpilobus subsp.

arpilobus, commonly known as the bearded milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant has a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in sagebrush steppe and rocky slopes, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for pollinators.

Its subspecies is distinguished by its hairy stems and distinctive floral structure.
