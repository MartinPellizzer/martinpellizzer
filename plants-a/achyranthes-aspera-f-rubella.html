Achyranthes aspera f. rubella

Achyranthes aspera f. rubella, commonly known as red bignay, is a variant of the horse radish plant native to tropical regions.

It is a herbaceous perennial with a stout, hairy stem that branches near the base. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in spikes, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and laxative effects.
