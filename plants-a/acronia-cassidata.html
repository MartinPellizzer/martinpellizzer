Acronia cassidata

Acronia cassidata, commonly known as the purple tamarind, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple flowers, which are clustered in inflorescences and attract various pollinators. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

Acronia cassidata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
