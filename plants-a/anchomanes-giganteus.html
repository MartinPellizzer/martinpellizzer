Anchomanes giganteus

Anchomanes giganteus, commonly known as giant marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, often forming dense stands in shallow water or moist soil. The plant produces small, white to pale green flowers arranged in loose spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Anchomanes giganteus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
