Ambrosia simplicifolia

Ambrosia simplicifolia, commonly known as the common ragweed, is an annual flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is known for its allergenic pollen, which can cause hay fever in sensitive individuals. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in open fields.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, also known as the sunflower family.
