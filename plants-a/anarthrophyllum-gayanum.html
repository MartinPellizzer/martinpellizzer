Anarthrophyllum gayanum

Anarthrophyllum gayanum, commonly known as the Sri Lankan sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the island of Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with a central disk. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
