Aglaonema costatum var. maculatum

Aglaonema costatum var. maculatum, commonly known as the leopard plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, mottled foliage with shades of green, white, and pink, creating a distinctive leopard-like pattern. The plant thrives in low-light conditions and prefers indirect sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. Its leaves are typically oval to lance-shaped, with a waxy texture and a slightly serrated edge.

Aglaonema costatum var. maculatum is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and ability to thrive in humid environments.
