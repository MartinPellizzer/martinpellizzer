Acsmithia pedunculata

Acsmithia pedunculata, commonly known as the slender-stemmed acsmithia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Acsmithia pedunculata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.
