Adelopetalum howense

Adelopetalum howense, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to China.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Morphology

Adelopetalum howense has alternate, simple, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter green underneath, with a slightly hairy surface.

The stem is slender, with a smooth, light brown bark that becomes rougher with age, and it grows upright with a spreading habit. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, with white petals and yellow stamens, blooming in late spring and pollinated by insects. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, adapting to rocky soils in its native habitat.
