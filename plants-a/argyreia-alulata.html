Argyreia alulata

Argyreia alulata, commonly known as the blue passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue and purple flowers that resemble a passionfruit, with intricate patterns and a strong, sweet scent. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces showy, five-petaled blooms that attract pollinators like butterflies and hummingbirds. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in warm climates.

However, it requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive.
