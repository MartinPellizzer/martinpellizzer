Arabis × wilczekii

Arabis × wilczekii, commonly known as the hybrid rockcress, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Arabis caucasica and Arabis alpina.

It is a low-growing perennial plant that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces basal rosettes of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Arabis × wilczekii is often found in alpine and rocky habitats across Europe.
