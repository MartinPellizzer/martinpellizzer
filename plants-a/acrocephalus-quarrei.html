Acrocephalus quarrei

Acrocephalus quarrei, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant has tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowering spikes are usually greenish or reddish, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Acrocephalus quarrei plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
