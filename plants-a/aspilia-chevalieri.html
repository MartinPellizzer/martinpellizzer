Aspilia chevalieri

Aspilia chevalieri, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flower heads. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and somewhat woody.

Aspilia chevalieri is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
