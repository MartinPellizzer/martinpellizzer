Asplenium emarginatum

Asplenium emarginatum, commonly known as the notched spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a small, terrestrial fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with lance-shaped, notched leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of trichomes. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium emarginatum is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
