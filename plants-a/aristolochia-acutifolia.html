Aristolochia acutifolia

Aristolochia acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe, typically green or purple in color. The plant has heart-shaped, cordate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

The genus Aristolochia is known for containing aristolochic acids, which are toxic to humans and animals.
