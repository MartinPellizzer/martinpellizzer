Azalea × splendidissima

Azalea × splendidissima, commonly known as the Southern Beauty azalea, is a hybrid shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 feet and produces dense, leathery foliage that provides year-round interest. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom repeatedly throughout the growing season.
