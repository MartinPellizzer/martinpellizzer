Acanthus macer

Acanthus macer, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Acanthaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its striking, spiny leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to semi-shaded environments.

Acanthus macer is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and floral display.
