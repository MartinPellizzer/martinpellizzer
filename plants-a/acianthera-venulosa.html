Acianthera venulosa

Acianthera venulosa, commonly known as the veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern around the base of the plant.

Acianthera venulosa prefers shaded, humid environments and is often found growing in tropical rainforests.
