Agrostis aequata

Agrostis aequata, commonly known as the slender bentgrass, is a species of grass native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial grass that typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in open, disturbed areas and is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions.

Botanically, it is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
