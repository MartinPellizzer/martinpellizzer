Agrostis canina f. inclusa

Agrostis canina f. inclusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved meadowgrass, is a variant of the common meadowgrass.

It is a perennial grass species characterized by its slender, narrow leaves and compact growth habit. This plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along the edges of wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, two-rowed spikelets.

Agrostis canina f. inclusa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
