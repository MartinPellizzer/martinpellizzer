Alchemilla vulgaris var. glaucescens

Alchemilla vulgaris var. glaucescens, commonly known as the silver-haired lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in rounded inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its leaves are used in traditional herbal medicine and are also popular in ornamental gardening for their aesthetic appeal.
