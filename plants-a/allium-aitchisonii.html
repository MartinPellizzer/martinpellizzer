Allium aitchisonii

Allium aitchisonii, commonly known as the Aitchison's garlic, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to the arid regions of Pakistan.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rosette of broad, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant is known for its strong garlic-like odor and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Allium aitchisonii is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
