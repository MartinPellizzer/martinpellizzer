Astrophytum senile

Astrophytum senile, commonly known as the old man cactus, is a unique species native to the desert regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are covered in short, white spines and clusters of small, pink or red flowers. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its wrinkled, almost puffy texture, which helps it store water and withstand arid conditions. It produces a single, tall flowering stalk each year, typically in late spring or early summer.

Astrophytum senile is a slow-growing plant that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for desert gardens.
