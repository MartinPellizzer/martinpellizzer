Acanthopanax senticosus f. subinermis

Acanthopanax senticosus f. subinermis, commonly known as the Siberian ginseng or Chinese goat's rue, is a variant of the Acanthopanax senticosus species.

It is a woody shrub native to the mountainous regions of East Asia, including China, Korea, and Russia. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and have serrated margins. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red berries that are edible for birds and mammals.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Chinese medicine, where it is used to enhance physical endurance and support immune function.
