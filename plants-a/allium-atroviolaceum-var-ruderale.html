Allium atroviolaceum var. ruderale

Allium atroviolaceum var. ruderale, commonly known as the rough leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant is characterized by its robust, fibrous roots and its ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it withstands cold temperatures and high altitudes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
