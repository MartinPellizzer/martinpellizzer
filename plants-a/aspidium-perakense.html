Aspidium perakense

Aspidium perakense, commonly known as the Perak water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats. The plant has a rhizomatous structure that helps it anchor in muddy or sandy substrates. Its spore-producing structures, called sorus, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.

Aspidium perakense plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to water quality through nutrient cycling.
