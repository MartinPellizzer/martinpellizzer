Amaryllis angustifolia

Amaryllis angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features tall, sturdy stems that bear clusters of funnel-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, emerging from the base of the plant. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

This species is prized for its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
