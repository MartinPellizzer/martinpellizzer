Astragalus watsonii

Astragalus watsonii, commonly known as Watson's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus watsonii is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland ecosystems.
