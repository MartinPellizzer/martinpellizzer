Aristida annamensis

Aristida annamensis, commonly known as the Annam grass, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands.

Aristida annamensis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
