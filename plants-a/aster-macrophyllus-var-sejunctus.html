Aster macrophyllus var. sejunctus

Aster macrophyllus var. sejunctus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with purple petals and a prominent yellow central disk. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
