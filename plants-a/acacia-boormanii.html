Acacia boormanii

Acacia boormanii, commonly known as the flame acacia, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a small shrub or tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, thread-like segments.

The plant is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its striking appearance.
