Aeollanthus candelabrum

Aeollanthus candelabrum, commonly known as the candlestick tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive branching pattern, which resembles a candlestick, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.

Aeollanthus candelabrum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates.
