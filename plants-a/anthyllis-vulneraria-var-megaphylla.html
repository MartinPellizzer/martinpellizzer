Anthyllis vulneraria var. megaphylla

Anthyllis vulneraria var. megaphylla, commonly known as the large-leaved woundwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with the central leaflet being significantly larger than the lateral ones. This variant is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the legume family, Fabaceae.
