Aulacostigma inerme

Aulacostigma inerme, commonly known as the common tamarind tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pod-like fruits containing edible seeds.

It is valued for its timber and the seeds, which are used in cooking and traditional medicine.
