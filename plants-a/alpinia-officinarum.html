Alpinia officinarum

Alpinia officinarum, commonly known as the fragrant ginger or wild ginger, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, rhizomatous growth and large, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a strong, spicy fragrance.
