Aster puniceus var. firmus

Aster puniceus var. firmus, commonly known as the Fireweed, is a striking perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom from late summer to early fall. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its common name reflects its fiery appearance, which is particularly noticeable during its blooming season.
