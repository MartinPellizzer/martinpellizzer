Antimima hexamera

Antimima hexamera, commonly known as the six-angled antimony plant, is a rare and unique species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive hexagonal leaf structure, which gives it its scientific name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research.
