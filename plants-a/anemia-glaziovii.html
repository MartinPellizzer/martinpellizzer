Anemia glaziovii

Anemia glaziovii, commonly known as Glaziov's anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the semi-arid regions of Brazil.

It features a basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale pink during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Anemia glaziovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
