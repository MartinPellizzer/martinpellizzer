Aconitum bullatifolium

Aconitum bullatifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense spires. Its leaves are large, palmately divided, and often have a waxy texture.

Aconitum bullatifolium is part of the Ranunculaceae family and contains potent alkaloids, making it toxic to both animals and humans.
