Acer syriacum f. cyprium

Acer syriacum f. cyprium, commonly known as the Cyprian maple, is a rare and ornamental variant of the Syriac maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely serrated leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. This plant is native to the mountainous regions of Cyprus and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. It prefers full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its striking autumn foliage.

Due to its limited natural range, it is considered a valuable species for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
