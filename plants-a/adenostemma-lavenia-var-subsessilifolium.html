Adenostemma lavenia var. subsessilifolium

Adenostemma lavenia var. subsessilifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved adenostemma, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and typically grows as a perennial shrub or subshrub. The plant features lanceolate leaves that are often sessile or nearly sessile, giving it its specific variety name. Its flowers are usually clustered in whorls and are characterized by their two-lipped structure, typical of the Acanthaceae family.

It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
