Astragalus ptilocephalus subsp. splendidissimus

Astragalus ptilocephalus subsp.

splendidissimus, commonly known as the splendid milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish to greenish pea-like flowers.

The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, stiff, hairy leaflets, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces small, legume-like pods that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and its potential medicinal properties.
