Allium spirale

Allium spirale, commonly known as the spiral garlic or spiral onion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, purple or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in culinary applications.

Allium spirale is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in various climates.
