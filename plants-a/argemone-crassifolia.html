Argemone crassifolia

Argemone crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf prickly poppy, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain oily seeds, which are sometimes used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to drought conditions.

Its striking appearance makes it a notable species in xerophytic ecosystems.
