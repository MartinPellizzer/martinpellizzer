Apocynum armenum

Apocynum armenum, commonly known as the pink and white milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes many milkweed species known for their toxic sap. The plant features opposite, oval-shaped leaves and produces showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its milky sap contains cardiac glycosides, making it toxic to mammals and insects.

Apocynum armenum plays a role in supporting pollinators but is avoided by many herbivores due to its defensive chemicals.
