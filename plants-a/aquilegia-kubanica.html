Aquilegia kubanica

Aquilegia kubanica, commonly known as the Kuban columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and vibrant flower colors. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately compound, and it produces seed pods that disperse its seeds by wind.

Aquilegia kubanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
