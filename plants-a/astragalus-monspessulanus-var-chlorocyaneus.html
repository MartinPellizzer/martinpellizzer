Astragalus monspessulanus var. chlorocyaneus

Astragalus monspessulanus var. chlorocyaneus, commonly known as the green-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive green hue. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and its pods are typically hairy and oblong. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and scrublands.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
