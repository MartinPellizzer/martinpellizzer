Ampelocissus cirrhata

Ampelocissus cirrhata, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Its ability to climb and trail makes it suitable for trellises and hanging baskets.
