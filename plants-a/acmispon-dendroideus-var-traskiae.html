Acmispon dendroideus var. traskiae

Acmispon dendroideus var. traskiae, commonly known as the Trask's burrobrush, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to withstand drought and grazing pressures.
