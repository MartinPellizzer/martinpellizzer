Alstroemeria butantanensis

Alstroemeria butantanensis, commonly known as the Butantan lily, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, often colorful flowers with six tepals. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, strap-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

Alstroemeria butantanensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.
