Avena pubescens subsp. hugeninii

Avena pubescens subsp.

hugeninii, commonly known as the Huguenin's oat, is a perennial grass species native to South America. It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in glumes, typical of the Poaceae family.

It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.
