Aporum capitellatoides

Aporum capitellatoides, commonly known as the dwarf umbrella plant, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which is characterized by its unique inflorescence structure known as a spathe and spadix. The plant features a short, thick rhizome with dense clusters of small, white flowers arranged in a compact, umbrella-like pattern. Aporum capitellatoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
