Aconitum senanense var. glabratum

Aconitum senanense var. glabratum, commonly known as the smooth aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple spathaceous flowers that bloom in summer, typically from July to September. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments, and are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Aconitum senanense var. glabratum is known for its toxic alkaloids, making it a plant of both botanical interest and caution.
