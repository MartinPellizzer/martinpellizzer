Aloysia fiebrigii

Aloysia fiebrigii, commonly known as the Fiebrig's verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Aloysia fiebrigii is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
