Aster rockianus

Aster rockianus, commonly known as the rock aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Aster rockianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
