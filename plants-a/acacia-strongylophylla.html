Acacia strongylophylla

Acacia strongylophylla, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and small, feathery leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or silver hair. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. Acacia strongylophylla is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world.
