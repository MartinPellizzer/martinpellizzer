Acer tetramerum

Acer tetramerum, commonly known as the Pacific madrone, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and clusters of white flowers that appear in early spring. The tree produces small, red berries that are an important food source for wildlife. Its leaves are broad, elliptical, and turn vibrant shades of orange and red in the fall.

Acer tetramerum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
