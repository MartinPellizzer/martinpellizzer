Arundo collina subsp. hellenica

Arundo collina subsp.

hellenica, commonly known as the Greek reed, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and riverbanks. It produces small, clustered flower heads that are often pale green or brown in color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
