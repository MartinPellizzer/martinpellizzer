Astragalus eucosmus f. caespitosus

Astragalus eucosmus f. caespitosus, commonly known as the tufted milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
