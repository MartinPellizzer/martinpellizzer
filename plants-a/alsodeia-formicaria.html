Alsodeia formicaria

Alsodeia formicaria, commonly known as the common alsike clover, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks. It is an important forage plant for livestock and also supports various pollinators.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
