Aeranthes tropophila

Aeranthes tropophila, commonly known as the pink orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink and white flowers, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

Aeranthes tropophila is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
