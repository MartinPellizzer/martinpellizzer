Acacia purpurascens

Acacia purpurascens, commonly known as the purple wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Acacia purpurascens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens across the country.
