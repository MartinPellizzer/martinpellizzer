Aloysia virgata var. platyphylla

Aloysia virgata var. platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaved verbena, is a shrub native to South America.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in summer. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

The variety platyphylla is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to the typical form.
