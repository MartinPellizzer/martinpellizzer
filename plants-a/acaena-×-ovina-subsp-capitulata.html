Acaena × ovina subsp. capitulata

Acaena × ovina subsp.

capitulata, commonly known as the coastal kōwhai, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant produces bright red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for native birds. It thrives in coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
