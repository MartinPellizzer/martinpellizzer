Allium paniculatum subsp. marginatum

Allium paniculatum subsp.

marginatum, commonly known as the purple garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, pale purple to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant produces bulbils along the stems, which can be used for propagation. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant foliage and delicate floral display.
