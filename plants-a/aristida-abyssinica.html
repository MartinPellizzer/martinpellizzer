Aristida abyssinica

Aristida abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian threeawn, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is a grass species that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid conditions. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences composed of multiple bracts, which bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its drought tolerance.

This species is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing soils in semi-arid environments.
