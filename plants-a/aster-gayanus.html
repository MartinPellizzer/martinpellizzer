Aster gayanus

Aster gayanus, commonly known as the mountain aster, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Aster gayanus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
