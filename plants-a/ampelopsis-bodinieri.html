Ampelopsis bodinieri

Ampelopsis bodinieri, commonly known as silver vine, is a vigorous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that turn red in the fall, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by bright red berries that attract birds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used as a ground cover or trained on trellises.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
