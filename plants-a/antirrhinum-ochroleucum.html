Antirrhinum ochroleucum

Antirrhinum ochroleucum, commonly known as the white toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. It typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing delicate, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and borders.
