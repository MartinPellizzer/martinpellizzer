Alsine roylei

Alsine roylei, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are typically succulent to conserve water. Alsine roylei is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal soil and water.

It is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
