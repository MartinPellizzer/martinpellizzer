Aconitum breiterianum

Aconitum breiterianum, commonly known as the Himalayan aconite, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves and produces showy, sparsely hairy flower clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its toxic nature, it should be handled with care.

Morphology

Aconitum breiterianum has large, compound leaves that are palmately arranged, with lance-shaped leaflets that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath.

The stem is upright and cylindrical, with a rough, bark that is typically grayish-brown and has a slightly fibrous texture. Its flowers are showy, blue to purple in color, with spurred petals and are arranged in racemes; they bloom in late summer and are pollinated primarily by bees. The fruit is a follicle that contains numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by wind due to their lightweight and hairy coating.

The roots are thick, fleshy, and tuberous, adapted for storing nutrients and water.
