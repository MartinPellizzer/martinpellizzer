Acacia pellacantha

Acacia pellacantha, commonly known as the broad-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and bipinnate leaves that are often broad and leathery. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical heads, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The plant is also valued for its hard wood and has been used traditionally for various purposes.
