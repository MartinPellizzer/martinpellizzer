Agarista revoluta var. revoluta

Agarista revoluta var. revoluta, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades and tomatoes. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its fruits are berries that turn red when mature, containing seeds within a fleshy pulp.
