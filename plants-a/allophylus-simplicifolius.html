Allophylus simplicifolius

Allophylus simplicifolius, commonly known as the African holly, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features simple, ovate leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
