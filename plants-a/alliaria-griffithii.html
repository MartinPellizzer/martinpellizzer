Alliaria griffithii

Alliaria griffithii, commonly known as the Indian garlic or garlic mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a biennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes, typical of the mustard family.

The plant is known for its pungent odor and is native to regions of Asia, including India and Nepal.
