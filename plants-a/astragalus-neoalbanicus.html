Astragalus neoalbanicus

Astragalus neoalbanicus, commonly known as the Albanian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Balkans, particularly Albania.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience in challenging conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological significance highlights its importance in local biodiversity.
