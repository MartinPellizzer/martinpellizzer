Actinophloeus capitis-yorki

Actinophloeus capitis-yorki, commonly known as the York Island pandanus, is a unique flowering plant native to the Torres Strait and surrounding islands.

It belongs to the Pandanus genus, characterized by its pinnate leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant exhibits a distinctive growth habit with a short, woody stem and long, fibrous leaves that droop downward. Its flowers are small and clustered, often producing a fragrant scent to attract pollinators.

This species plays an important ecological role in its coastal habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife.
