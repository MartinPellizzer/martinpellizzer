Allium flavum subsp. pulchellum

Allium flavum subsp.

pulchellum, commonly known as the purple garlic or purple leek, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It is characterized by its striking purple to violet flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July.

The plant produces small, bulbous structures that resemble garlic cloves, which are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained, fertile soils.

This subspecies is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its historical use in culinary and medicinal applications.
