Athyrium goeringianum

Athyrium goeringianum, commonly known as the silver lace fern, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features delicate, bipinnate fronds that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.

The plant's reproductive structures include sporangia that release spores for propagation.
