Agrostis divaricatissima

Agrostis divaricatissima, commonly known as silver bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and compact growth habit, making it well-suited for low-maintenance landscaping. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to yellowish flowers.

Agrostis divaricatissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
