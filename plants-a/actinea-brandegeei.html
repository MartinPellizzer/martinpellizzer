Actinea brandegeei

Actinea brandegeei, commonly known as the Brandegee's actinia, is a species of sea anemone found in the Pacific Ocean.

It belongs to the family Actinidiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, translucent polyps that extend from a central pedal disk. The organism is typically found in shallow coastal waters, where it attaches itself to rocks or other substrates. Its reproductive system involves both sexual and asexual methods, contributing to its ability to thrive in various marine environments.

The plant plays a role in the ecosystem by providing habitat and food for small marine organisms.
