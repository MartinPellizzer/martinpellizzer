Alchornea oblongifolia

Alchornea oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf alchornea, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features opposite, elliptic to ovate leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
