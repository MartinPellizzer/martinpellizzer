Andropogon columbiensis

Andropogon columbiensis, commonly known as the Columbia grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences that bloom in late summer. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
