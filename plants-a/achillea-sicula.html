Achillea sicula

Achillea sicula, commonly known as Sicilian yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

Sicilian yarrow is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
