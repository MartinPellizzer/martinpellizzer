Acrocarpidium cordifolium

Acrocarpidium cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaved acrocarp, is a unique species within the Acrocarpidae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically broad and glossy, providing a striking visual appearance. The plant is native to specific regions in South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures are distinctive, featuring small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
