Adiantum palmatum

Adiantum palmatum, commonly known as the Japanese maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble lace, giving it a graceful appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are bioluminescent in the dark, a unique trait that aids in nocturnal pollination.

Adiantum palmatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and indoor settings.
