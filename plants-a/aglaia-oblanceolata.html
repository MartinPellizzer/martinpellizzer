Aglaia oblanceolata

Aglaia oblanceolata, commonly known as the broad-leaf aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often a deep green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by orange or red berries containing toxic seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
