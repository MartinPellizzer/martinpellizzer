Aeschynomene goetzei

Aeschynomene goetzei, commonly known as Goetz's horsegram, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its high-protein seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enhancing soil fertility.
