Astragalus haarbachii var. bulgaricus

Astragalus haarbachii var. bulgaricus, commonly known as the Bulgarian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. It has small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native range.
