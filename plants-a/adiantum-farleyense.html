Adiantum farleyense

Adiantum farleyense, commonly known as the Farley's maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, bi-lobed fronds that resemble the shape of a maidenhair. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a finely divided structure, with a distinctive rachis that is slightly hairy.

Adiantum farleyense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
