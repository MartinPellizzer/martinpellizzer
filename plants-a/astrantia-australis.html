Astrantia australis

Astrantia australis, commonly known as the Southern Aster, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy composite flowers. The plant typically features a basal rosette of leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are often pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky habitats.

Astrantia australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
