Aspalathus recurva

Aspalathus recurva, commonly known as the silverleaf bush, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, narrow leaves that are usually covered in a fine silvery or grayish pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Aspalathus recurva is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
