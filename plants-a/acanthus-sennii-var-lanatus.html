Acanthus sennii var. lanatus

Acanthus sennii var. lanatus, commonly known as the woolly acanthus, is a striking perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are densely covered with soft, silky hairs, giving it a unique, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces tall, spires of showy, violet-blue flowers in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value lies in both its foliage and its dramatic floral display.
