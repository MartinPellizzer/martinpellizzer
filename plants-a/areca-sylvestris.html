Areca sylvestris

Areca sylvestris, commonly known as the wild areca palm, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, three-angled fruits. Areca sylvestris is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.
