Adonis turkestanica

Adonis turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan lily, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, single flowers with yellow stamens and a white or pinkish pistil, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but is also threatened by habitat loss and over-collection.
