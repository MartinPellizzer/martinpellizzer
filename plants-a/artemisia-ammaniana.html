Artemisia ammaniana

Artemisia ammaniana, commonly known as the Amman wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are typically clustered in dense, spherical shapes. Artemisia ammaniana thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Its essential oils contain compounds like camphor and thujone, which have been used traditionally for their medicinal and aromatic properties.
