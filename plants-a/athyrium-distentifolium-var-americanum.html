Athyrium distentifolium var. americanum

Athyrium distentifolium var. americanum, commonly known as the American lady fern, is a deciduous fern native to eastern North America.

It features delicate, bipinnate fronds that are typically arching and can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and produces spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
