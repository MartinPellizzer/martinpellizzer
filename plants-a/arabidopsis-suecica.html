Arabidopsis suecica

Arabidopsis suecica, commonly known as the Swedish Arabidopsis, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Arabidopsis thaliana and Arabidopsis lyrata.

It is a small, annual plant with simple, alternate leaves and small, white flowers. Native to northern Europe, it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant is widely used in genetic research due to its short life cycle and ease of cultivation.

Its genome has been extensively studied, making it a model organism in plant biology.
