Arabis clusiana

Arabis clusiana, commonly known as the clustered rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other well-known flowering plants. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Arabis clusiana thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
