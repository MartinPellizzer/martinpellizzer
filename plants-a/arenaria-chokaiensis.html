Arenaria chokaiensis

Arenaria chokaiensis, commonly known as Chokai sandflower, is a flowering plant native to the Chokai region of India.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in sandy and rocky soils, typically found in coastal and mountainous areas. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to withstand arid conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and animals in its native environment.
