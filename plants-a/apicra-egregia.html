Apicra egregia

Apicra egregia, commonly known as the golden spire plant, is a striking member of the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, almost ethereal appearance. Native to the mountainous regions of South America, it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Due to its unique appearance and medicinal properties, it is a sought-after plant in botanical gardens and herbal medicine.
