Aeonium paivae

Aeonium paivae, commonly known as the "Snowy Aeonium," is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are covered in a fine, white powdery coating called farina. The plant produces striking pink to red flowers on tall, branching stems during the spring and summer months. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Aeonium paivae is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its unique appearance and ease of care.
