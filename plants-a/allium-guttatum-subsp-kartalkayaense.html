Allium guttatum subsp. kartalkayaense

Allium guttatum subsp.

kartalkayaense, commonly known as the Karakaya leek, is a bulbous plant native to Central Asia. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in umbels.

The plant is characterized by its swollen, garlic-scented bulbs, which are often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued both for its culinary uses and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
