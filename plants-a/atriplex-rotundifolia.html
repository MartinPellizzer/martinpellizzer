Atriplex rotundifolia

Atriplex rotundifolia, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are adapted to retain water in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in saline soils and is often found in coastal areas, salt marshes, and disturbed sites.

Atriplex rotundifolia plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
