Absinthium divaricatum

Absinthium divaricatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in compound umbels, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Absinthium divaricatum is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported digestive and antiseptic properties.
