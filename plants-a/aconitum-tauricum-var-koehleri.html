Aconitum tauricum var. koehleri

Aconitum tauricum var. koehleri, commonly known as the Tauric monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a spire-like cluster, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic visual impact.
