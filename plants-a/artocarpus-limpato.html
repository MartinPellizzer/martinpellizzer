Artocarpus limpato

Artocarpus limpato, commonly known as the red-fruit breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes other fruit-bearing trees like the breadfruit and fig. The plant produces small, round fruits that mature to a deep red color, often with a smooth, leathery skin. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The plant is valued not only for its edible fruit but also for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in local cultures.
