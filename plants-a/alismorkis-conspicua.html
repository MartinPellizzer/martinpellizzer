Alismorkis conspicua

Alismorkis conspicua, commonly known as the showy waterplantain, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to wetlands and shallow waters in Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that rise from rhizomes, supporting broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers with prominent, showy petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense inflorescences at the top of the stems, and it thrives in nutrient-rich, still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Alismorkis conspicua plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
