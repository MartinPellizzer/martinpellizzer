Aspilia belo-horizontinae

Aspilia belo-horizontinae, commonly known as the Belo Horizonte torchflower, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces vibrant yellow to orange flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking floral display and drought tolerance.
