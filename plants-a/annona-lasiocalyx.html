Annona lasiocalyx

Annona lasiocalyx, commonly known as the rough-leaf custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small tree or shrub that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces a fleshy, yellow or green fruit with a distinctive star-shaped pattern on its surface. The fruit is edible, with a sweet and creamy pulp that contains numerous small, inedible seeds.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
