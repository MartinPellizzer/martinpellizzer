Acaena anserinifolia var. sericeinitens

Acaena anserinifolia var. sericeinitens, commonly known as the silky burr bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny, and woody growth habit, with small, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by distinctive, spherical, bristled fruits. Its leaves and stems are covered in a silky, silver-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and inland shrublands.
