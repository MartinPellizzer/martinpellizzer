Albizia sinaloensis

Albizia sinaloensis, commonly known as the Mexican siris, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

Its seeds are encased in a pod and can be dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural distribution across its native range.
