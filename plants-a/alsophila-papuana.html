Alsophila papuana

Alsophila papuana, commonly known as the Papuan tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Guinea and nearby islands.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its fronds can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to the dense canopy of the forest.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in the regions where it is found.
