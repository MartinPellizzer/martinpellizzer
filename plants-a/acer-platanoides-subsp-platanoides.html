Acer platanoides subsp. platanoides

Acer platanoides subsp.

platanoides, commonly known as the European horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy, white or pinkish flowers that appear in spring.

The tree produces spiny, capsule-like fruits that contain one or two seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can reach heights of up to 25 meters.

The bark is smooth and greyish when young, becoming fissured with age.
