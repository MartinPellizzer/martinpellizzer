Agrimonia eupatoria subsp. major

Agrimonia eupatoria subsp.

major, commonly known as greater agrimony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fruits are dry, schizocarpic capsules containing tiny seeds.

It is often found in dry grasslands, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.
