Asperula aristata f. subglabra

Asperula aristata f. subglabra, commonly known as the smooth asperula, is a variant of the asperula species characterized by its smooth, hairless leaves.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers during the spring season. Its foliage is typically dark green and compact, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
