Allium microcephalum

Allium microcephalum, commonly known as the bulbous leek, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It features a compact, bulbous stem covered in narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale purple flowers that bloom in the summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, garlic-like flavor.

Allium microcephalum is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
