Allium tricoccum

Allium tricoccum, commonly known as ramps, is a perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, broad leaves and white or pinkish flowers. The plant thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests. Its bulbs, which are rich in sulfur compounds, are edible and highly valued in culinary traditions.

The plant plays an important role in both ecological systems and local food cultures.
