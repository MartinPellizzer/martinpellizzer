Aster furcatus f. elaciniatus

Aster furcatus f. elaciniatus, commonly known as the velvetleaf aster, is a variant of the aster species characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered with fine, silky hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with ray florets that are shorter than the central disk.

This form is often found in the eastern United States and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
