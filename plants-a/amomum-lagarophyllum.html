Amomum lagarophyllum

Amomum lagarophyllum, commonly known as the long-leaf cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark blue berries containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.

Its essential oils are valued for their culinary and medicinal properties.
