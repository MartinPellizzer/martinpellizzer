Atriplex chenopodioides

Atriplex chenopodioides, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, often gray-green leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense, terminal spikes. Its salt-tolerant nature makes it a valuable plant for stabilizing soils in saline areas.

Atriplex chenopodioides is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its use in traditional medicine and as forage for wildlife.
