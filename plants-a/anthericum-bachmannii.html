Anthericum bachmannii

Anthericum bachmannii, commonly known as Bachmann's lily, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer, featuring a distinctive spatulate shape and vibrant colors. The plant has lanceolate, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
