Arenaria rhodantha

Arenaria rhodantha, commonly known as the pink sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas or arid environments. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that range in color from pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to conserve moisture in dry conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in harsh landscapes.
