Ajuga nipponensis

Ajuga nipponensis, commonly known as Japanese bugleweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue to purple in color and bloom in early spring.

Ajuga nipponensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
