Arctotis nodosa

Arctotis nodosa, commonly known as the nodding arctotis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features striking violet-blue to purple flowers with a yellow center, and it has narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Arctotis nodosa is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
