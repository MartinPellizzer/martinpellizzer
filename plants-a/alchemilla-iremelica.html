Alchemilla iremelica

Alchemilla iremelica, commonly known as the Irish pennywort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and it produces achenes as its fruit.

Alchemilla iremelica is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in damp conditions.
