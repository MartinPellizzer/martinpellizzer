Astragalus adpressus

Astragalus adpressus, commonly known as the flat milkvetch, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Astragalus adpressus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
