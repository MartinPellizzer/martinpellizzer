Astragalus pseudorobustus

Astragalus pseudorobustus, commonly known as the purple milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and can often be found in rocky or gravelly habitats. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
