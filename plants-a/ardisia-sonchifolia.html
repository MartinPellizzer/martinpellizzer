Ardisia sonchifolia

Ardisia sonchifolia, commonly known as the yellow ardisia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green in color. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in umbels, which bloom in the spring.

The fruit is a small, red berry that matures in the fall and is an important food source for various birds and small mammals.
