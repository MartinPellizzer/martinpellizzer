Artorima

Artorima, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Andes, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It features large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant has broad, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid environment. Artorima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and holds cultural significance in Andean communities.

Its botanical name, Artorima, reflects its revered status in local traditions.
