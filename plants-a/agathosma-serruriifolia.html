Agathosma serruriifolia

Agathosma serruriifolia, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a small shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
