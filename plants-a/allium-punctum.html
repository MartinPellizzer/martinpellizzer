Allium punctum

Allium punctum, commonly known as the purple onion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a bulbous stem with a distinctive purple hue, and its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like. The plant produces small, white to purple flowers arranged in a loose umbel, which bloom in the summer months. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, often used in culinary applications.

Allium punctum is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary versatility in Mediterranean cuisine.
