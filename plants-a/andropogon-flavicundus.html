Andropogon flavicundus

Andropogon flavicundus, commonly known as the yellow bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and produces small, pale yellow flowers in late summer. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairie habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It is a perennial grass that forms dense clumps and is often used in restoration projects due to its ecological benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities.
