Andropogon elliottii var. projectus

Andropogon elliottii var. projectus, commonly known as the tallgrass bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The plant has narrow, bluish-green leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often pale green to brownish in color. It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.

Its adaptability makes it a key species in restoration projects and native plant gardens.
