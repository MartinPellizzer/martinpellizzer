Abutilon attenuatum

Abutilon attenuatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, contributing to its common name.

Abutilon attenuatum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
