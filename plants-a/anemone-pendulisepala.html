Anemone pendulisepala

Anemone pendulisepala, commonly known as the hanging anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features showy, white to pink flowers that hang in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
