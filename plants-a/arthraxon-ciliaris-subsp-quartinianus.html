Arthraxon ciliaris subsp. quartinianus

Arthraxon ciliaris subsp.

quartinianus, commonly known as the Queensland grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves with a prominent midrib.

The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and woodland areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
