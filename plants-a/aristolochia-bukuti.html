Aristolochia bukuti

Aristolochia bukuti, commonly known as the Bukuti Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, heart-shaped leaves and striking, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a strong, unpleasant odor. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex floral structure that attracts specific pollinators. Its flowers are often adorned with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of female insects, a strategy to enhance pollination.

However, due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
