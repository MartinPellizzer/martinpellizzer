Amorphophallus gratus

Amorphophallus gratus, commonly known as the beauty leaf or elephant foot plant, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes many ornamental and edible species. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in humid environments.

The plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
