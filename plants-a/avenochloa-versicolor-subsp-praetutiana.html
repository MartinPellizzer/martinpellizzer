Avenochloa versicolor subsp. praetutiana

Avenochloa versicolor subsp.

praetutiana, commonly known as the variable wild rice, is a grass species native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its variable leaf coloration, which can range from green to reddish hues, depending on environmental conditions.

The plant typically grows in wetland or aquatic habitats, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often loosely arranged, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its morphological variability and potential ecological significance in its native range.
