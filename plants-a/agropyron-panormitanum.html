Agropyron panormitanum

Agropyron panormitanum, commonly known as the Sicilian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Agropyron panormitanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in marginal environments.
