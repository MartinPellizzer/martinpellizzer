Achillea griseovirens

Achillea griseovirens, commonly known as the grey-green yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features finely divided, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped umbels. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its medicinal properties have been historically recognized, though it is primarily valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
