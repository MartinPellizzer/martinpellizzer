Aristolochia burchellii

Aristolochia burchellii, commonly known as the African birthwort, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and its complex, pipe-shaped flowers that often have a strong, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, red aril. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
