Astragalus nevadensis var. catalaunicus

Astragalus nevadensis var. catalaunicus, commonly known as the Catalaunian milkvetch, is a perennial forb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces cylindrical seed pods that contain numerous small seeds.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
