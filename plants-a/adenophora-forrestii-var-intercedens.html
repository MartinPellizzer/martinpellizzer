Adenophora forrestii var. intercedens

Adenophora forrestii var. intercedens, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to China.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
