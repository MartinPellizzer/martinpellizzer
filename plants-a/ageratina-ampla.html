Ageratina ampla

Ageratina ampla, commonly known as whiteface tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and fluffy, white to pale blue flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is often found in moist, shaded areas. Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, due to their nectar-rich structure.

Ageratina ampla is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can thrive in partial shade to full sun conditions.
