Abies chensiensis

Abies chensiensis, commonly known as the Sichuan fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It belongs to the family Pinaceae and is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The tree produces cones that are typically pendulous and contain seeds surrounded by a papery wing. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found at high elevations in temperate forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
