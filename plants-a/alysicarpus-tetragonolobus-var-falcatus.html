Alysicarpus tetragonolobus var. falcatus

Alysicarpus tetragonolobus var. falcatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sida, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, often hairy stems and small, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. The flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, with five petals that are usually pale pink to white in color. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, and it is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

It is often used in traditional medicine and has potential for forage in certain agricultural systems.
