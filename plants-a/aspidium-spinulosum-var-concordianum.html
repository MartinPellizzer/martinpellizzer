Aspidium spinulosum var. concordianum

Aspidium spinulosum var. concordianum, commonly known as the Concordian water fern, is a variant of the water fern family, characterized by its delicate, floating fronds.

It typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and wetlands, where it thrives in nutrient-rich environments. The plant's fronds are usually lance-shaped and have a finely divided structure, aiding in its ability to absorb nutrients from the water. It is known for its ability to spread rapidly through vegetative reproduction, forming dense mats on the water surface.

This species plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water filtration.
