Amaracarpus calcicola

Amaracarpus calcicola, commonly known as the limestone amaranth, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is adapted to grow in calcium-rich soils, often found on rocky or limestone outcrops. Its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments makes it a resilient species in its native habitat.

The plant is often used in local horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
