Aster molliusculus var. minor

Aster molliusculus var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf blue aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, blue to purple flowers, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
