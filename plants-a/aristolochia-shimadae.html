Aristolochia shimadae

Aristolochia shimadae, commonly known as Japanese birthwort, is a flowering plant native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its unique, heart-shaped leaves and its distinctive, pipe-like flowers that are often greenish or brownish in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex chemical composition, including various aristolochic acids. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not recommended for cultivation or consumption.
