Alchemilla pedata var. gracilipes

Alchemilla pedata var. gracilipes, commonly known as slender alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in spring, clustered in upright cymes. Its leaves are typically basal, with a soft, velvety texture and a silvery underside.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
