Astelma crassifolium

Astelma crassifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf astelma, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red and orange, which attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds. Its succulent nature makes it drought-tolerant and suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Overall, Astelma crassifolium is a unique and visually appealing addition to botanical collections.
