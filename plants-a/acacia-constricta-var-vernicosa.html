Acacia constricta var. vernicosa

Acacia constricta var. vernicosa, commonly known as the smooth wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spindly stems and small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
