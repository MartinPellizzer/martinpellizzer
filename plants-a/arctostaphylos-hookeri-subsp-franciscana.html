Arctostaphylos hookeri subsp. franciscana

Arctostaphylos hookeri subsp.

franciscana, commonly known as Franciscan manzanita, is a shrub native to California, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, reddish-brown berries. It typically grows in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

The plant has smooth, reddish bark that peels in thin layers, and its leaves are oval to elliptical with a leathery texture. It is adapted to mild, Mediterranean climates and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

The species is also valued for its ecological role, providing food and shelter for local wildlife.
