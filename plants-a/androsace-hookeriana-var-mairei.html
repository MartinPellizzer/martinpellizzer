Androsace hookeriana var. mairei

Androsace hookeriana var. mairei, commonly known as the Maire's rock moss, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of southern Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in early summer. The plant has a compact, cushion-like growth habit, with hairy stems and leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
