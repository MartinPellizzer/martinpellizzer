Acaena chubutensis

Acaena chubutensis, commonly known as the Chubut burr bush, is a shrub native to southern South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spherical, bristly fruits that contain seeds covered in hooked hairs, aiding in seed dispersal by attaching to animals. It thrives in dry, rocky environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Acaena chubutensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
