Aspalathus caerulescens

Aspalathus caerulescens, commonly known as the bluebush, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Aspalathus caerulescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
