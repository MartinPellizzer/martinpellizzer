Aster ageratoides subsp. yoshinaganus

Aster ageratoides subsp.

yoshinaganus, commonly known as Yoshinaganus aster, is a flowering plant native to Japan. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountain forests and streamside areas. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in a spherical or conical shape.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
