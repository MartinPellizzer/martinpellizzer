Allophylus zeylanicus

Allophylus zeylanicus, commonly known as the Indian myrtle, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Allophylus zeylanicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
