Anthyllis asturiae

Anthyllis asturiae, commonly known as the Asturian broom, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers clustered in racemes. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Anthyllis asturiae is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its presence in local biodiversity.
