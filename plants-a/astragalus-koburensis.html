Astragalus koburensis

Astragalus koburensis, commonly known as the Korean milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate climates. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which makes it valuable in ecological restoration projects.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
