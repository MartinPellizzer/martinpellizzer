Agropyron smithii var. riparium

Agropyron smithii var. riparium, commonly known as riparian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green blades that are often found in moist environments. This plant typically grows along riverbanks and wet meadows, where it thrives in well-drained to slightly wet soils. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Riparian wheatgrass is valued for its ecological benefits and is often used in restoration projects.
