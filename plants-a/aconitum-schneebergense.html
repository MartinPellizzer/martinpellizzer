Aconitum schneebergense

Aconitum schneebergense, commonly known as Schneeberg Aconite, is a herbaceous perennial native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately compound leaves with lobed segments, and its roots are tuberous. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxicity, it is important to handle the plant with care.

Morphology

Aconitum schneebergense has compound, palmately lobed leaves that are dark green on top and silver-white underneath, arranged alternately along the stem.

The stem is smooth and cylindrical, with a reddish-brown bark that becomes darker with age, growing upright and reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its flowers are blue to purple, arranged in dense spires, consisting of showy petals and sepals, blooming in late summer to early autumn and pollinated by insects. The fruit is a follicle that splits open to release small, dark seeds dispersed by wind.

The roots are thick, tuberous, and fibrous, serving as storage organs and anchoring the plant in the soil.
