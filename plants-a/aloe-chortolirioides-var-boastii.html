Aloe chortolirioides var. boastii

Aloe chortolirioides var. boastii, commonly known as the Cape Peninsula aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, upright leaves that are typically blue-green in color, covered in a layer of white powder. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and habitat specificity, it is often cultivated in arid gardens and conservation efforts.
