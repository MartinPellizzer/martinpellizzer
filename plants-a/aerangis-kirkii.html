Aerangis kirkii

Aerangis kirkii, commonly known as the Kirk's orchid, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Africa.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulbous stem and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and green, often with a yellow or greenish tinge. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil.

Aerangis kirkii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
