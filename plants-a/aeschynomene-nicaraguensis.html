Aeschynomene nicaraguensis

Aeschynomene nicaraguensis, commonly known as Nicaraguan goatherd's grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

It has potential agricultural uses due to its nutritional value for livestock and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
