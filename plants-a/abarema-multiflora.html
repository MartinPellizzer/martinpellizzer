Abarema multiflora

Abarema multiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its fruits are legumes that contain several seeds, and the plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Abarema multiflora is valued for its hard, dense wood, which has been traditionally used in woodworking and musical instrument making.
