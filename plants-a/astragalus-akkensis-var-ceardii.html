Astragalus akkensis var. ceardii

Astragalus akkensis var. ceardii, commonly known as the Ceard's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance is significant in its native habitat, supporting local wildlife and contributing to biodiversity.
