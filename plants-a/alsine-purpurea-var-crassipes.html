Alsine purpurea var. crassipes

Alsine purpurea var. crassipes, commonly known as purple sandparsley, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its rosette growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
