Aristida meccana

Aristida meccana, commonly known as the meccan grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It features slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
