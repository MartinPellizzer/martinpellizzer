Apium ternatum

Apium ternatum, commonly known as wild celery, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and small yellowish-green flowers that appear in umbels. It is often found in moist, disturbed soils along roadsides and in meadows.

Its edible leaves and seeds have been used historically in cooking and traditional medicine.
