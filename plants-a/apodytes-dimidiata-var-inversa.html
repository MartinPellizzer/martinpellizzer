Apodytes dimidiata var. inversa

Apodytes dimidiata var. inversa, commonly known as the inverted apodytes, is a unique variant of the apodytes plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive inverted leaf structure, where the upper surface is typically pale and the lower surface is dark green, creating a striking visual contrast. The plant is evergreen, with small, glossy leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, adding to its ornamental value.

Due to its unusual appearance, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
