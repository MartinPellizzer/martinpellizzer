Ambrosia johnstoniorum

Ambrosia johnstoniorum, commonly known as Johnston's ragweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small yellow flowers that appear in the fall. The plant is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and open areas. It is known for producing large amounts of allergenic pollen, contributing to seasonal allergies.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a common nuisance in certain regions.
