Acanthocalycium formosum

Acanthocalycium formosum, commonly known as the Mexican night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and blooming habits, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
