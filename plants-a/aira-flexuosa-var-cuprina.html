Aira flexuosa var. cuprina

Aira flexuosa var. cuprina, commonly known as the cuprum grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It typically grows in moist or wetland habitats, often found in marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often bent or flexed, giving it its species name "flexuosa.". Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a rough texture.
