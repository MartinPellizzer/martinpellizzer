Alocasia neoguineensis

Alocasia neoguineensis, commonly known as the Neoguinean arrow leaf, is a tropical plant native to Indonesia and Papua New Guinea.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 60 cm in length. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and thrives in humid, shady environments. Its foliage is often variegated with white and green patterns, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Alocasia neoguineensis requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture to flourish.
