Alchemilla zeyheri

Alchemilla zeyheri, commonly known as Zeyher's lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of soft, hairy, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Alchemilla zeyheri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
