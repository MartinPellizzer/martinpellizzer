Agropyron attenuatum var. platense

Agropyron attenuatum var. platense, commonly known as the plains wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. This plant typically thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with spikelets that are often reddish in color.

Agropyron attenuatum var. platense is valued for its erosion control capabilities and is often used in restoration ecology.
