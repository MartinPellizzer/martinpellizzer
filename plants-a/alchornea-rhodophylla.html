Alchornea rhodophylla

Alchornea rhodophylla, commonly known as red-leaf alchornea, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or purple foliage, which becomes more pronounced in cooler temperatures. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Alchornea rhodophylla is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and streamside areas.
