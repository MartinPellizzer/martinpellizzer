Acer dasycarpum f. lacteum

Acer dasycarpum f. lacteum, commonly known as the milky maple, is a rare and distinctive variant of the species *Acer dasycarpum*.

It is characterized by its unique milky-white or pale yellow sap, which sets it apart from other maple varieties. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in areas of Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. Its leaves are typically palmate with five to seven lobes, and it produces small, clustered flowers in early spring.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological specificity, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
