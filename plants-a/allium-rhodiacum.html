Allium rhodiacum

Allium rhodiacum, commonly known as the purple bulbous onion, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a bulbous stem with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to purple flowers in clusters. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild, onion-like scent.

Allium rhodiacum prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
