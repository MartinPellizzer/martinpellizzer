Arenaria verna var. caespitosa

Arenaria verna var. caespitosa, commonly known as the spring sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It forms dense, mat-like growths with small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its foliage is typically blue-green, with narrow, grass-like leaves.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
