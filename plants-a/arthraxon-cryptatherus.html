Arthraxon cryptatherus

Arthraxon cryptatherus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to Australia, characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its fibrous roots make it a valuable forage plant for livestock in arid and semi-arid regions.

Arthraxon cryptatherus is adapted to a wide range of soil types and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
