Arundinaria brevipes

Arundinaria brevipes, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and slender, reed-like culms. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as swampy woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring.

Arundinaria brevipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
