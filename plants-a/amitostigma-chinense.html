Amitostigma chinense

Amitostigma chinense, commonly known as the Chinese winter gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It has small, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
