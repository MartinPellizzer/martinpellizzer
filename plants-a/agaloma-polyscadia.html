Agaloma polyscadia

Agaloma polyscadia, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature five petals and a distinctive, intricate pattern of lobes. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough, textured surface, and it has a strong, aromatic scent.

Agaloma polyscadia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice in tropical gardens.
