Acer italum f. opalus

Acer italum f. opalus, commonly known as the Opalus maple, is a rare and ornamental variant of the Italian maple.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Due to its limited distribution and unique foliage, it is highly valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
