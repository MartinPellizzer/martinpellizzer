Alpinia brevilabris

Alpinia brevilabris, commonly known as the short-lipped ginger, is a flowering plant belonging to the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Alpinia brevilabris thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued both for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
