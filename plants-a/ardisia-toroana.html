Ardisia toroana

Ardisia toroana, commonly known as the Torrean evergreen, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for landscaping purposes.
