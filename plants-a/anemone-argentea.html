Anemone argentea

Anemone argentea, commonly known as the silver anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive, saucer-like shape that blooms in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a silvery or grayish bloom, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Anemone argentea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
