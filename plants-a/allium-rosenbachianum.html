Allium rosenbachianum

Allium rosenbachianum, commonly known as the Rosenbach onion, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, round bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Allium rosenbachianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
