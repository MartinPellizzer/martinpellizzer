Acacia asplenioides

Acacia asplenioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and small, feathery leaves that are arranged in pairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact spikes, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Acacia asplenioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh conditions.
