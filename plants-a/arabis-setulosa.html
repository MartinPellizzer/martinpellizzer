Arabis setulosa

Arabis setulosa, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other well-known flowering plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters and bloom in early spring.

The leaves are hairy and lobed, giving the plant its common name.
