Astragalus sukaczevii

Astragalus sukaczevii, commonly known as Sukaczev's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus sukaczevii is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
