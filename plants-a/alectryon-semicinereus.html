Alectryon semicinereus

Alectryon semicinereus, commonly known as the grey-leafed glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant is characterized by its greyish, papery leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Alectryon semicinereus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils in full sun.
