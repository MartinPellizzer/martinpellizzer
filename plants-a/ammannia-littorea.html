Ammannia littorea

Ammannia littorea, commonly known as water spinach or river spinach, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with succulent, ribbed stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, followed by small, round berries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Ammannia littorea thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, along riverbanks, and in flooded soils.
