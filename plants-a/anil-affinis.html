Anil affinis

Anil affinis, commonly known as the Indian violet, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and gardens. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Anil affinis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in horticulture.
