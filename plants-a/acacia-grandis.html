Acacia grandis

Acacia grandis, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.

Acacia grandis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
