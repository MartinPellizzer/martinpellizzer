Astragalus genuflexus

Astragalus genuflexus, commonly known as the bowing milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Astragalus genuflexus is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

Its seeds are often used in ecological restoration projects due to their hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
