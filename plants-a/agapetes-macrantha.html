Agapetes macrantha

Agapetes macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered silverleaf, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are often covered in a silvery or whitish pubescence, giving it its common name.

Agapetes macrantha is typically found in cool, moist habitats at high elevations.
