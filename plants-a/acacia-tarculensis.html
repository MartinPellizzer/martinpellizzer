Acacia tarculensis

Acacia tarculensis, commonly known as the Tarcal acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact spikes, which are followed by elongated, flat seed pods. It is well adapted to dry environments, with deep roots that enable it to access groundwater.

Acacia tarculensis plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
