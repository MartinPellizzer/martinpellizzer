Abutilon esquirolii

Abutilon esquirolii, commonly known as the Mexican velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

The leaves are palmately lobed, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
