Acacia minyura

Acacia minyura, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and can thrive in arid environments. Its flowers are a key feature, often used in floral arrangements and as a symbol of Australian identity.

Acacia minyura plays an important role in providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
