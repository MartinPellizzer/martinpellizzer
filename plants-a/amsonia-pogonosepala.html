Amsonia pogonosepala

Amsonia pogonosepala, commonly known as the eastern bluecurls, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces distinctive, curling bracts that give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for native gardens.

Amsonia pogonosepala is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
