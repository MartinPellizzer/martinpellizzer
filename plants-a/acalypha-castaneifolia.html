Acalypha castaneifolia

Acalypha castaneifolia, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red bracts that resemble flames, which appear in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
