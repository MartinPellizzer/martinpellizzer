Alyssum heterophyllum

Alyssum heterophyllum, commonly known as the woolly alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant features fuzzy, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and border plantings.

Alyssum heterophyllum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects such as bees and butterflies.
