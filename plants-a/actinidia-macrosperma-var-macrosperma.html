Actinidia macrosperma var. macrosperma

Actinidia macrosperma var. macrosperma, commonly known as the Chinese gooseberry, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with 5 to 9 leaflets, and its small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in early summer. The plant produces berries that mature to a bright red color, typically in late summer or early autumn.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also cultivated for its edible fruits, which are rich in vitamin C. The species is adaptable to various soil types and thrives in partial to full sun.
