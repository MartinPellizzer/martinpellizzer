Aloe compressa var. schistophila

Aloe compressa var. schistophila, commonly known as the rock aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color and covered in a white powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on branched inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for low-maintenance gardening.
