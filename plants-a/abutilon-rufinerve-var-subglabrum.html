Abutilon rufinerve var. subglabrum

Abutilon rufinerve var. subglabrum, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a deciduous shrub native to the southern United States.

It features hairy, ovate leaves that are typically green with a slightly rough texture. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in the summer months. Its stems are woody and can be branched, with a tendency to exude a milky sap when broken.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
