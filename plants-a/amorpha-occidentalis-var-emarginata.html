Amorpha occidentalis var. emarginata

Amorpha occidentalis var. emarginata, commonly known as the western wild indigo, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with a distinctive notched base, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from violet to yellow, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

As a member of the Fabaceae family, it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil with nutrients.
