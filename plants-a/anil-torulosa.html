Anil torulosa

Anil torulosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Anil torulosa is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.

It is also noted for its rapid growth rate, making it both a useful forage crop and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
