Aria cucullifera

Aria cucullifera, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, hood-like structure that encloses its flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and white or pale green, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Aria cucullifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservatories.
