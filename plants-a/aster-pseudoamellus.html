Aster pseudoamellus

Aster pseudoamellus, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads made up of many small florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It features slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Its showy purple to blue flowers bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators like butterflies and bees.
