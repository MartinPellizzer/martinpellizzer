Antiaris usambarensis

Antiaris usambarensis, commonly known as the Natal tree or elephant bark tree, is a large, evergreen tree native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark that exudes a milky sap when cut. The plant has large, palmately lobed leaves and produces small, clustered flowers followed by orange-yellow berries.

Its latex has been traditionally used for medicinal and industrial purposes, and it plays a significant role in the ecosystems of its native habitats.
