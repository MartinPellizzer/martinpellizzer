Amaranthus quitensis

Amaranthus quitensis, commonly known as Inca wheat, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and vibrant, showy flowers that range from red to orange. The plant is known for its high nutritional value, containing significant amounts of protein, fiber, and minerals. Its seeds are often used in traditional Andean cuisine and have been cultivated for thousands of years.

Amaranthus quitensis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
