Aristolochia croatica

Aristolochia croatica, commonly known as the Croatian birthwort, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern and produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-brown with a purple or violet tint. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its unique, pipe-like floral structure. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
