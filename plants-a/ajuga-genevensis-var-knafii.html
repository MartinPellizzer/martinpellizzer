Ajuga genevensis var. knafii

Ajuga genevensis var. knafii, commonly known as knaf's bugleweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, often violet or blue flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ground cover in shady areas.
