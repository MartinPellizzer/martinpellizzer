Anoda waltheriifolia

Anoda waltheriifolia, commonly known as the purpletop wildflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
