Acanthocephala haselbergii

Acanthocephala haselbergii, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to spread, it is considered an invasive species in some areas.
