Aspidopterys albomarginata

Aspidopterys albomarginata, commonly known as the white-margin tree fern, is a species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The fronds are typically bright green with a distinctive white or pale margin, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in native forests.

The plant plays an important role in the ecological structure of its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
