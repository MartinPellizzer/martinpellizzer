Austrocylindropuntia clavarioides

Austrocylindropuntia clavarioides, commonly known as the Easter Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the island of Easter Island.

It is characterized by its branching, cylindrical stems that are covered in small, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, typically blooming during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and limited natural habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
