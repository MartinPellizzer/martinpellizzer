Acacia praecox f. armata

Acacia praecox f. armata, commonly known as the hairy wattle, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical spikes, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
