Arctostaphylos uva-ursi var. suborbiculata

Arctostaphylos uva-ursi var. suborbiculata, commonly known as bearberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage with elliptical to oval-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
