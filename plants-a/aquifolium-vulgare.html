Aquifolium vulgare

Aquifolium vulgare, commonly known as the common holly.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant is characterized by its spiny, ovate leaves that are glossy on top and lighter in color on the underside. Its small, red berries are an important food source for birds during the winter months.

The plant is often used in landscaping for its evergreen appearance and ornamental value.
