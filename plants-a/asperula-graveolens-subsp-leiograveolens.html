Asperula graveolens subsp. leiograveolens

Asperula graveolens subsp.

leiograveolens, commonly known as sweet woodruff, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its fragrant, heart-shaped leaves that emit a mild, vanilla-like scent when crushed.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its aromatic foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and traditional herbal remedies.
