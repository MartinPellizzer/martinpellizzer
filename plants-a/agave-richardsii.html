Agave richardsii

Agave richardsii, commonly known as the Richards' agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky leaves that form a rosette shape, often reaching heights of up to 10 feet. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with yellowish-green flowers, which bloom once in its lifetime. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves.

Agave richardsii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a landscape plant.
