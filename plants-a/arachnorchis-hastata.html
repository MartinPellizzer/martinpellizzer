Arachnorchis hastata

Arachnorchis hastata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. It blooms from late summer to early autumn, producing clusters of delicate, orchid-like flowers.

Arachnorchis hastata is a protected species, highlighting its ecological significance and the need for conservation efforts.
