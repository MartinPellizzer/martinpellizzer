Aristida humidicola

Aristida humidicola, commonly known as the wetland dropseed, is a perennial grass species native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 60 cm, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, fluffy panicles, which are often white or pale green.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
