Arundinaria longiramea

Arundinaria longiramea, commonly known as the long-rammed bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy on the underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Arundinaria longiramea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
