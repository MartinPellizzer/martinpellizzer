Arabis platysperma var. typica

Arabis platysperma var. typica, commonly known as the broad-seeded rockcress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant features basal rosettes of linear, silver-gray leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring. Its flowers are arranged in racemes and are characterized by four sepals and four petals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and early blooming habit.
