Acacia burbidgeae

Acacia burbidgeae, commonly known as the Burridge acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and small, leathery leaves that are often clustered at the ends of the stems. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which bloom during the summer months. Acacia burbidgeae is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for rocky or sandy soils.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
