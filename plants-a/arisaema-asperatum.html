Arisaema asperatum

Arisaema asperatum, commonly known as the rough arum, is a herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features a distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant has rough, textured leaves that are typically heart-shaped or arrowhead-like in form. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Arisaema asperatum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
