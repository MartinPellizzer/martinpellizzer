Achillea × borzae

Achillea × borzae, commonly known as the hybrid yarrow, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a hybrid between Achillea millefolium (common yarrow) and Achillea ptarmica (white yarrow), resulting in a plant with distinctive floral and foliage characteristics. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, flat-topped flower heads in shades of white, pink, or red. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and for its historical use in traditional medicine.

Achillea × borzae thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a versatile addition to many landscapes.
