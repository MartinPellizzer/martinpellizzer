Agrostis gracilifolia

Agrostis gracilifolia, commonly known as slenderleaf bentgrass, is a species of grass native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, wiry stems. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color, and the plant produces small, inconspicuous spikelets that are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

Agrostis gracilifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its delicate appearance.
