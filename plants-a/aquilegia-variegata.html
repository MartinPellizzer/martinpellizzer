Aquilegia variegata

Aquilegia variegata, commonly known as the variegated columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, spurred flowers that range in color from blue to purple, with yellow or orange markings. The plant has basal leaves that are palmately compound, with three to five leaflets, and it produces seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and unique foliage patterns.
