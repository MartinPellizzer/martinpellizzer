Acacia psilostachya

Acacia psilostachya, commonly known as the silver acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact spikes, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
