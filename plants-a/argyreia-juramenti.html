Argyreia juramenti

Argyreia juramenti, commonly known as the sacred lotus of India, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb various supports, making it a popular ornamental species. Its flowers open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant has cultural and medicinal significance in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
