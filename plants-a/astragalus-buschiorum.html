Astragalus buschiorum

Astragalus buschiorum, commonly known as the Buschiorum milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water deep underground.

Astragalus buschiorum plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems in its native habitat.
