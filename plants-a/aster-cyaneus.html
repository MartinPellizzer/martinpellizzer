Aster cyaneus

Aster cyaneus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant features a robust root system and upright, hairy stems that support its delicate foliage. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Aster cyaneus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.
