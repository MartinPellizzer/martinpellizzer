Athyrium subtriangulare

Athyrium subtriangulare, commonly known as the triangular lady fern, is a fern species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often triangular in shape, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are wiry and covered in a dense layer of brownish scales.

Athyrium subtriangulare is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
