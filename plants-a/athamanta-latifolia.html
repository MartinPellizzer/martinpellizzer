Athamanta latifolia

Athamanta latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed athamanta, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in terminal panicles, usually displaying shades of white or pale yellow.

Athamanta latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
