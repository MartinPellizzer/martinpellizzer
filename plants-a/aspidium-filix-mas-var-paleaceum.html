Aspidium filix-mas var. paleaceum

Aspidium filix-mas var. paleaceum, commonly known as the pale male fern, is a variant of the common male fern, characterized by its distinctive frond structure.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan. The plant features fertile fronds with sporangia clustered in sori, which are typically located on the underside of the leaves. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The paleaceum variety is noted for its delicate, pale-green fronds that contrast with the darker veins, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant.
