Astragalus ambigens

Astragalus ambigens, commonly known as the ambiguous milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus ambigens is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
