Armeniaca × dasycarpa var. patakii

Armeniaca × dasycarpa var. patakii, commonly known as the Patakii apricot, is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its medium-sized, fuzzy fruits that typically ripen in the summer months. The plant is native to Central Asia and is cultivated for its edible, sweet-tart fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Its botanical features include alternate, ovate leaves and a moderate growth habit.
