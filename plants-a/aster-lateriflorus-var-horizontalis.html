Aster lateriflorus var. horizontalis

Aster lateriflorus var. horizontalis, commonly known as the horizontal aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has slender, branching stems that grow horizontally along the ground, giving it a unique, spreading appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued for its ability to thrive in moist, shady environments and its role in supporting pollinators.
