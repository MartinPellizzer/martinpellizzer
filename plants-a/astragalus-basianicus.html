Astragalus basianicus

Astragalus basianicus, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh environments, with deep taproots that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers.

It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
