Antirrhinum amethysteum

Antirrhinum amethysteum, commonly known as the amethyst snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of striking violet to purple flowers that resemble small, elongated snapdragons. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
