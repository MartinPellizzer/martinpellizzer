Aspalathus desertorum

Aspalathus desertorum, commonly known as the desert bushveld pea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow, pea-like flowers and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant typically grows in shrubland and rocky areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually small and hairy, helping to reduce water loss.

Aspalathus desertorum plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
