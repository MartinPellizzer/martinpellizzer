Aloe juddii

Aloe juddii, commonly known as the Judd's aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy. The leaves are typically green with a distinctive white or yellowish margin, and they produce a gel-like substance that is often used for its medicinal properties. Aloe juddii thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

It produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to the plant.
