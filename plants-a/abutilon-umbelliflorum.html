Abutilon umbelliflorum

Abutilon umbelliflorum, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Abutilon umbelliflorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
