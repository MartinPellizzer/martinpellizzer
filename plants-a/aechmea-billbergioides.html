Aechmea billbergioides

Aechmea billbergioides, commonly known as the silver pine bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like rosette of narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces a showy inflorescence with bright orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.

Aechmea billbergioides thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
