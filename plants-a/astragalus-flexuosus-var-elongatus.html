Astragalus flexuosus var. elongatus

Astragalus flexuosus var. elongatus, commonly known as the elongated milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant features slender, branching stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, with small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
