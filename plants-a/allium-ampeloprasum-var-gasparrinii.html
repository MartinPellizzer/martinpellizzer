Allium ampeloprasum var. gasparrinii

Allium ampeloprasum var. gasparrinii, commonly known as the Gasparrini leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features long, narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often cultivated for its edible leaves, which have a mild, onion-like flavor.

This variety is valued in both culinary and horticultural contexts for its ornamental appeal and versatility.
