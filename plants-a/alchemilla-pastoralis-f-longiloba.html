Alchemilla pastoralis f. longiloba

Alchemilla pastoralis f. longiloba, commonly known as the long-stemmed lady's mantle, is a variant of the lady's mantle plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in loose panicles. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental foliage and its ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
