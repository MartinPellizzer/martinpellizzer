Abutilon hirtum

Abutilon hirtum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its fuzzy, hairy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive velvety texture.

Abutilon hirtum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
