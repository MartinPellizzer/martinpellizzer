Aegiphila meridensis

Aegiphila meridensis, commonly known as the Desert Sage, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry environments.

Aegiphila meridensis is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
