Artemisia imponens

Artemisia imponens, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage and small yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant contains volatile oils and is often used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and digestive properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various arid environments.

Its historical use dates back to ancient times, where it was valued for both medicinal and culinary purposes.
