Acacia siamensis

Acacia siamensis, commonly known as the Siam acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are dispersed by wind or animals.

Acacia siamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
