Abrodictyum parviflorum

Abrodictyum parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered sword fern, is a species of fern native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the biodiversity in its native habitat.
