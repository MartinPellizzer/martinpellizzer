Amphicarpaea ferruginea

Amphicarpaea ferruginea, commonly known as rusty false flax, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to produce two types of fruits: one above ground and one below ground, a trait known as amphicarpy. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas.

Its rusty-colored stems and leaves give it its common name, and it plays a role in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.
