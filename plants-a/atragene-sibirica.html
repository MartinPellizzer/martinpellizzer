Atragene sibirica

Atragene sibirica, commonly known as Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Atragene sibirica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
