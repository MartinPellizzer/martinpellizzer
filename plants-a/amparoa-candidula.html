Amparoa candidula

Amparoa candidula, commonly known as the white-flowered Amparoa, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often giving it a delicate appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Amparoa candidula is often found in rocky or sandy soils in coastal regions.
