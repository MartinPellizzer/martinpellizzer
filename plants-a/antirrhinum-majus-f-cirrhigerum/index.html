Antirrhinum Majus F. Cirrhigerum

Antirrhinum majus f. cirrhigerum is a unique variant of the common snapdragon known for its distinctive, elongated floral structures. This plant belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements. Its flowers display a range of colors, from vibrant reds to soft pinks, making it a popular choice among horticulturists. The species is also noted for its adaptability to various growing conditions, thriving in both full sun and partial shade. Despite its beauty, it remains a less commonly cultivated form compared to its more widely recognized relatives.
