Acacia pterygocarpa

Acacia pterygocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, feathery foliage and produces yellow flowers in clusters during the spring. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that function as leaves, and it is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Acacia pterygocarpa is often found in sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh conditions.

It also plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
