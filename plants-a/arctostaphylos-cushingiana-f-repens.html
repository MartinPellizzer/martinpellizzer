Arctostaphylos cushingiana f. repens

Arctostaphylos cushingiana f. repens, commonly known as the creeping manzanita, is a low-growing shrub native to the coastal regions of California.

It features dense, prostrate stems that form a mat-like structure, often carpeting the ground with a thick layer of foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, providing year-round coverage and contributing to its drought tolerance.

This subspecies is well adapted to sandy or rocky soils and thrives in coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.
