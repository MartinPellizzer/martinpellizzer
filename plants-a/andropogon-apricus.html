Andropogon apricus

Andropogon apricus, commonly known as the yellow bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands and grassy areas, reaching heights of up to three feet. The plant has narrow, bluish-green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in late summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native plant landscapes.

Andropogon apricus is also an important food source for various wildlife species.
