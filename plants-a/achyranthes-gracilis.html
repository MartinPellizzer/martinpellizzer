Achyranthes gracilis

Achyranthes gracilis, commonly known as slender cowtail, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features slender, branching stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in spikes, and it produces small, three-lobed fruits.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
