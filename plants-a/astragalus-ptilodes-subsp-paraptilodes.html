Astragalus ptilodes subsp. paraptilodes

Astragalus ptilodes subsp.

paraptilodes, commonly known as the Sierra foothill milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy stems and smaller, more compact flowers compared to the typical form. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

This plant plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.
