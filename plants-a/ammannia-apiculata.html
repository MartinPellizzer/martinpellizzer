Ammannia apiculata

Ammannia apiculata, commonly known as the prickly hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and emits a mild, aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a common ornamental plant in warm climates.

Ammannia apiculata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
