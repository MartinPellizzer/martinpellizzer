Asperula cappadocica

Asperula cappadocica, commonly known as the Cappadocian speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Cappadocia region of Turkey.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, sky-blue or white flowers. The plant has oval, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy and arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Asperula cappadocica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
