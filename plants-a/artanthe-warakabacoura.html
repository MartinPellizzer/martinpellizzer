Artanthe warakabacoura

Artanthe warakabacoura, commonly known as the Warakabacoura sunflower, is a unique member of the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in dense, showy clusters. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, showcasing its adaptability to dry environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
