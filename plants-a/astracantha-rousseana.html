Astracantha rousseana

Astracantha rousseana, commonly known as the Rousseau's thornveld, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features spiny stems and leaves, with small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
