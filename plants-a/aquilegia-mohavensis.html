Aquilegia mohavensis

Aquilegia mohavensis, commonly known as the Mohave monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and vibrant, often red or orange, flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, aiding in water retention.

Aquilegia mohavensis plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
