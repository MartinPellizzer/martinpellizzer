Annona senegalensis var. chrysophylla

Annona senegalensis var. chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden-leaf custard apple, is a tropical shrub native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves that turn a striking yellowish-green color as they mature. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible fruits with a thick, creamy pulp. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, aromatic flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.
