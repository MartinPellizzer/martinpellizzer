Acacia angustissima subsp. lemmonii

Acacia angustissima subsp.

lemmonii, commonly known as the narrowleaf wattle, is a shrub native to arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, narrow leaflets.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It is well adapted to drought and poor soils, making it a hardy species in harsh environments.

The subspecies lemmonii is distinguished by its narrower leaves and more compact growth form compared to other acacia species.
