Ajuga lupulina f. breviflora

Ajuga lupulina f. breviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bugleweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Valerianaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Ajuga lupulina f. breviflora is often used in gardens for its ability to thrive in shaded areas and its attractive, low-maintenance growth form.
