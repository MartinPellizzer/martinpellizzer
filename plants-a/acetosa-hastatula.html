Acetosa hastatula

Acetosa hastatula, commonly known as the hairy bittercress, is a small annual herb belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The flowers are typically white or pink, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, a common feature in the mustard family. It produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
