Astragalus vesicarius subsp. pastellianus

Astragalus vesicarius subsp.

pastellianus, commonly known as the Pastellian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and the presence of specialized structures on its fruit pods. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems in its native habitat.
