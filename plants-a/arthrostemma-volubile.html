Arthrostemma volubile

Arthrostemma volubile, commonly known as the creeping arthrostemma, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers, which bloom in shades of red, pink, and white, are often arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Arthrostemma volubile is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.
