Aechmanthera tomentosa var. wallichii

Aechmanthera tomentosa var. wallichii, commonly known as the Woolly Bristleroot, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often covered in a dense, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers, which are typically borne in terminal or axillary inflorescences. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist climates with well-drained soils.
