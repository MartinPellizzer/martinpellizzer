Allium trachyscordum

Allium trachyscordum, commonly known as the purple globemallow, is a herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette of gray-green, spoon-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a short, sturdy stem that supports its showy inflorescence.

Allium trachyscordum is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
