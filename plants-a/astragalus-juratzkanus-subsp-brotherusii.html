Astragalus juratzkanus subsp. brotherusii

Astragalus juratzkanus subsp.

brotherusii, commonly known as the Juratzkanus milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as rocky slopes and sandy soils. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions.

It is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
