Aloinopsis luckhoffii

Aloinopsis luckhoffii, commonly known as the White Lion's Tail, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil with plenty of sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
