Arisaema laminatum var. inclusum

Arisaema laminatum var. inclusum, commonly known as the Chinese cobra lily, is a flowering plant native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, hooded spathaceous flower that resembles a cobra's head, with a central spathe and a spadix of tiny, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
