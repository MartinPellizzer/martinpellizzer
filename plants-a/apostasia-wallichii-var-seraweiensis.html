Apostasia wallichii var. seraweiensis

Apostasia wallichii var. seraweiensis, commonly known as the Seraweiensian snake's mouth orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, snake-like appearance of its flower, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinct, elongated shape.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its rarity and aesthetic qualities.
