Aster multiflorus var. incanopilosus

Aster multiflorus var. incanopilosus, commonly known as the gray-haired aster, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its grayish-blue foliage, which is covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white to lavender, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open meadows and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
