Aeschynanthus podocarpus

Aeschynanthus podocarpus, commonly known as the velvet tangerine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright orange, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and horticultural collections.
