Astragalus baytopianus

Astragalus baytopianus, commonly known as Baytopian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for nitrogen-fixing capabilities through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climates.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native habitat.
