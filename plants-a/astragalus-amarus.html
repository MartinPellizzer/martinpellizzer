Astragalus amarus

Astragalus amarus, commonly known as bitter milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats. Astragalus amarus is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Chinese medicine, where it is used to support immune function.

Its roots are the primary part used in herbal preparations.
