Angelica longipedicellata

Angelica longipedicellata, commonly known as the long-fruited angelica, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and floral structure.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features large, compound leaves with a pinnate arrangement and small, white flowers clustered in umbels. Its fruit is distinctive, with elongated, ribbed structures that aid in seed dispersal.

Angelica longipedicellata is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
