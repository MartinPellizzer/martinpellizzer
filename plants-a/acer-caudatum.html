Acer caudatum

Acer caudatum, commonly known as the weeping maple, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive cascading branches that create a weeping appearance, giving it a unique and ornamental look. The tree produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Its leaves are palmately lobed, turning vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall.

Acer caudatum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and along stream banks.
