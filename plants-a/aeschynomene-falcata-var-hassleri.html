Aeschynomene falcata var. hassleri

Aeschynomene falcata var. hassleri, commonly known as the hairy sickleleaf aeschynomene, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale purple, followed by narrow, linear seed pods. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as fields and along roadsides.

This species is valued for its potential use in forage and soil stabilization in arid and semi-arid environments.
