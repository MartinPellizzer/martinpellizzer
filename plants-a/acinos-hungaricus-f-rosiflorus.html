Acinos hungaricus f. rosiflorus

Acinos hungaricus f. rosiflorus, commonly known as the Hungarian mountain cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges, typical of many members of its family. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe and is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
