Asplenium vittaeforme

Asplenium vittaeforme, commonly known as the veined maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive midrib. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are often divided into smaller, serrated segments, giving it a feathery appearance.

Asplenium vittaeforme is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
