Acacia mucronata var. dependens

Acacia mucronata var. dependens, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The variety 'dependens' is distinguished by its more upright growth habit compared to other acacia species.
