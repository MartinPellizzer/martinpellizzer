Arachnanthe moschifera

Arachnanthe moschifera, commonly known as the musk orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish-green flowers that emit a musky scent, attracting specific pollinators. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and forest margins, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs during the cooler months, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Arachnanthe moschifera is valued for its unique fragrance and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental and ecological significance.
