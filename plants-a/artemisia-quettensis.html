Artemisia quettensis

Artemisia quettensis, commonly known as the Quett's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau and adjacent regions.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and slender, branched stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, contributing to its ecological role in alpine ecosystems. Artemisia quettensis is adapted to cold, arid conditions and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
