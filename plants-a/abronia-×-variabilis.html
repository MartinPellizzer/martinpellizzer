Abronia × variabilis

Abronia × variabilis, commonly known as the variable sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family, characterized by its succulent, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
