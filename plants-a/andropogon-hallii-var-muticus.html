Andropogon hallii var. muticus

Andropogon hallii var. muticus, commonly known as the muticous bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant features narrow, bluish-green leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its foliage is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting appearance.

Botanically, it is characterized by its rhizomatous growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
