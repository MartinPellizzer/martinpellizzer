Aerobion pectinatum

Aerobion pectinatum, commonly known as the golden pectin plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow leaves covered in a thick layer of pectin.

This pectin coating helps the plant retain moisture and provides some resistance to herbivores. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant's structure includes short, erect stems with alternate, ovate leaves.

Its botanical significance lies in its ability to produce and maintain pectin on its surface, making it a subject of interest in both horticulture and scientific research.
