Azalea barbata

Azalea barbata, commonly known as the barbed azalea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink and red, bloom in the spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Azalea barbata is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
