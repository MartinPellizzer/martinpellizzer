Amylotheca formicarum

Amylotheca formicarum, commonly known as the ant-like lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It forms a symbiotic relationship with fungi and algae, contributing to its unique structure and nutrient acquisition. The plant exhibits a dark green to blackish-green thallus with a leathery texture. It is typically found on tree bark and rock surfaces in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ecological role includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for microorganisms.
