Anthyllis webbiana

Anthyllis webbiana, commonly known as the Webb's anthyllis, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils, often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Anthyllis webbiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
