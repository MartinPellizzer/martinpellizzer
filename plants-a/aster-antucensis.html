Aster antucensis

Aster antucensis, commonly known as the Antuco aster, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers display a mix of purple and white petals.

Aster antucensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
