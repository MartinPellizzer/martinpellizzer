Apostasia wallichii subsp. nipponica

Apostasia wallichii subsp.

nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese bird's-nest orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid native to Japan. It is characterized by its unique, nest-like inflorescence that resembles a bird's nest, hence its common name.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually greenish-white with a faintly colored lip, and it produces small, elongated pseudobulbs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
