Argolasia tomentosa

Argolasia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly argolasia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage, which gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
