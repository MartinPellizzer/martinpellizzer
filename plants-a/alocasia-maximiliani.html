Alocasia maximiliani

Alocasia maximiliani, commonly known as the giant elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 feet in diameter. The plant features a stout, rhizomatous stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in humid, shady environments. Its foliage is typically a deep green with a waxy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous white flowers.

Alocasia maximiliani is popular as a decorative plant due to its striking appearance and air-purifying qualities.
