Asplenium succulentum

Asplenium succulentum, commonly known as the succulent spleenwort, is a member of the fern family (Polypodiaceae).

It is a small, epiphytic or lithophytic plant that typically grows on rocks or tree trunks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often clustered at the base, and it produces small, spore-bearing structures called soruses on the underside of its fronds. Asplenium succulentum is adapted to retain moisture, which is essential for its survival in its often arid or semi-arid habitats.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
