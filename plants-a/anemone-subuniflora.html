Anemone subuniflora

Anemone subuniflora, commonly known as the single-flower anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features a short, thick rhizome and produces a single, showy white or pink flower with five to seven petals. Its leaves are basal, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Anemone subuniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
