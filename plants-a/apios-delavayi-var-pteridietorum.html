Apios delavayi var. pteridietorum

Apios delavayi var. pteridietorum, commonly known as the fern-leaved wild bean, is a perennial legume native to China.

It features compound leaves with a fern-like appearance, giving it its common name. The plant produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
