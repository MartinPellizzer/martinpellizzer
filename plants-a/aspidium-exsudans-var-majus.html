Aspidium exsudans var. majus

Aspidium exsudans var. majus, commonly known as the giant wall fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
