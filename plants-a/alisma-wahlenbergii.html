Alisma wahlenbergii

Alisma wahlenbergii, commonly known as the narrowleaf arrowhead, is a perennial aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, arrow-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette at the water's surface. Its small, white flowers bloom in early summer, adding ornamental value to its habitat.

Alisma wahlenbergii plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for insects and serving as a food source for various wildlife.
