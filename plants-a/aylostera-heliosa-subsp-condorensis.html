Aylostera heliosa subsp. condorensis

Aylostera heliosa subsp.

condorensis, commonly known as the Condor sunflower, is a perennial herb native to the Andean region of South America. It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that resemble those of the common sunflower.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes, thriving in temperate to cold climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapting to the harsh environmental conditions of its habitat.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological roles in its native ecosystems.
