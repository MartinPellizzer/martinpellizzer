Anchusa acaulis

Anchusa acaulis, commonly known as the upright bugloss, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Anchusa acaulis is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
