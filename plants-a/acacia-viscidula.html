Acacia viscidula

Acacia viscidula, commonly known as the sticky acacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are feathery and often shed during dry periods, allowing the plant to conserve water. Its bark is sticky and emits a strong, unpleasant odor when broken.

This plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.
