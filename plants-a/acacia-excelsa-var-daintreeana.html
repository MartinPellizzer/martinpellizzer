Acacia excelsa var. daintreeana

Acacia excelsa var. daintreeana, commonly known as the Daintree acacia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of northeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow to golden-pink spherical flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is well adapted to the humid, warm climate of its native habitat and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. It plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various native fauna.

The timber of this species is valued for its strength and durability, though it is rarely harvested due to its limited distribution and conservation status.
