Adicea betulifolia

Adicea betulifolia, commonly known as the birchleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that resemble those of birch trees, hence its common name. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streams.
