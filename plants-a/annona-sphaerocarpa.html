Annona sphaerocarpa

Annona sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the golden cherimoya, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, oval-shaped fruit with a thick, leathery rind. The fruit contains a creamy, aromatic pulp that is rich in nutrients and has a unique flavor combining sweet and tart notes. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental leaves and is often cultivated for both its fruit and aesthetic appeal.
