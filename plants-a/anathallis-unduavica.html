Anathallis unduavica

Anathallis unduavica, commonly known as the Unduava spider orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

This terrestrial orchid typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often near the forest floor. It produces small, delicate flowers with a unique spider-like appearance, which is a characteristic feature of many Anathallis species. The plant has a short, erect stem with a few leaves and blooms during the late summer months.

Its specific conservation status is threatened due to habitat loss, making it a rare and ecologically significant species.
