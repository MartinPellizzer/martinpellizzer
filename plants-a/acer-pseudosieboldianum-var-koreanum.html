Acer pseudosieboldianum var. koreanum

Acer pseudosieboldianum var. koreanum, commonly known as the Korean maple, is a deciduous tree native to Korea.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its growth habit includes a rounded crown and can reach heights of up to 15 meters in its natural habitat.
