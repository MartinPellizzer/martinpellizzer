Asperula suffruticosa

Asperula suffruticosa, commonly known as sweet woodruff, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters along the stems. The plant has a creeping growth habit with woody stems that can become semi-woody over time. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three heart-shaped leaflets, and are often used in herbal teas.

Asperula suffruticosa is valued for its aromatic properties and is often grown in gardens for its ornamental and medicinal uses.
