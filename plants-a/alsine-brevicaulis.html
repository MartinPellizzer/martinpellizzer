Alsine brevicaulis

Alsine brevicaulis, commonly known as narrowleaf sandgrass, is a low-growing annual plant native to the western United States.

It typically reaches heights of 5 to 15 centimeters and has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Alsine brevicaulis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
