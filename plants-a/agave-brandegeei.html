Agave brandegeei

Agave brandegeei, commonly known as the Desert Agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears white or pale green flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and moths. Its leaves are typically blue-green in color with white teeth along the edges, and it is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.

Agave brandegeei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically by indigenous peoples for various purposes.
