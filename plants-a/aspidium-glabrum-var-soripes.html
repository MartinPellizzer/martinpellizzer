Aspidium glabrum var. soripes

Aspidium glabrum var. soripes, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a variant of the common shield fern.

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with long, creeping stems that produce fronds. Its fronds are bipinnate, with a distinctive appearance that features a broad, shield-shaped base.

The undersides of the fronds are covered in fine, hairy structures, which give the variety its common name.
