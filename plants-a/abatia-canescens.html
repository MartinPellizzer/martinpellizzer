Abatia canescens

Abatia canescens, commonly known as the gray abatia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in wetlands. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, grayish-white hairs. Its showy, yellowish-white flowers bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Abatia canescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wetland environments.
