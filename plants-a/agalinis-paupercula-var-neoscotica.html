Agalinis paupercula var. neoscotica

Agalinis paupercula var. neoscotica, commonly known as the slender stemless false foxglove, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is characterized by its delicate stems and rapid growth, making it a notable component of its native ecosystems.
