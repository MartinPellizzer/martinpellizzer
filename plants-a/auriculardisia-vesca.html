Auriculardisia vesca

Auriculardisia vesca, commonly known as the golden cup-moss, is a small, perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, auriculate (ear-shaped) leaves that give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
