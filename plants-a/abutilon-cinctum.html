Abutilon cinctum

Abutilon cinctum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive texture. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often with a prominent central tube. Its capsules contain numerous small, flat, kidney-shaped seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Abutilon cinctum is adapted to thrive in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.
