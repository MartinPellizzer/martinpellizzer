Alopecurus vaginatus var. tiflisiensis

Alopecurus vaginatus var. tiflisiensis, commonly known as the Tiflis grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in supporting local biodiversity.
