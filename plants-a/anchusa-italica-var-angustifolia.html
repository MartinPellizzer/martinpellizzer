Anchusa italica var. angustifolia

Anchusa italica var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and sunny environments. The plant features slender, angular stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy blue or purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
