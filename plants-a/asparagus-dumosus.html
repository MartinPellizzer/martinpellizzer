Asparagus dumosus

Asparagus dumosus, commonly known as the dwarf asparagus or the shrub asparagus, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are fibrous and capable of storing moisture, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

Asparagus dumosus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
