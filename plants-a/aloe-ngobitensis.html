Aloe ngobitensis

Aloe ngobitensis, commonly known as the Ngobitensis Aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy. The leaves are typically gray-green with a textured surface, and they contain a gel-like substance that is often used for its medicinal properties. This plant is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves to survive in dry conditions.

It produces yellowish-green flowers on tall stems, adding ornamental value to its appearance.
