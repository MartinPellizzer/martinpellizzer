Acanthopanax gracilistylus var. pubescens

Acanthopanax gracilistylus var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy horse chestnut, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
