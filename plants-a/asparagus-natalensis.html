Asparagus natalensis

Asparagus natalensis, commonly known as Natal asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky areas. Its berries are small and red, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Natal asparagus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
