Astragalus atenicus

Astragalus atenicus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Astragalus atenicus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
