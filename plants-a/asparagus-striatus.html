Asparagus striatus

Asparagus striatus, commonly known as the striped asparagus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features delicate, fern-like foliage with a bluish-gray hue, and its stems are often adorned with distinct stripes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by tiny, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
