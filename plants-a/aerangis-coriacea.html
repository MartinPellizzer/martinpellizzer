Aerangis coriacea

Aerangis coriacea, commonly known as the rough-skinned orchid, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that give it a distinctive, rugged appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
