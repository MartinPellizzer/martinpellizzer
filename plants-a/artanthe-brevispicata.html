Artanthe brevispicata

Artanthe brevispicata, commonly known as the short-spurred artanthe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Artanthe brevispicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
