Actaea alba

Actaea alba, commonly known as white crowfoot, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features a stout, unbranched stem that is often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

Actaea alba is typically found in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soils.
