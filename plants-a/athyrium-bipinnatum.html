Athyrium bipinnatum

Athyrium bipinnatum, commonly known as the Japanese painted fern, is a fern species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, bipinnate fronds that create a lacy, delicate appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its fronds are often adorned with a subtle, variegated pattern that resembles painted brush strokes.

Athyrium bipinnatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
