Acacia genistifolia subsp. attenuata

Acacia genistifolia subsp.

attenuata, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and small, yellow, globular flowers that appear in clusters.

The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous tiny, thread-like leaflets. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
