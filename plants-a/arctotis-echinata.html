Arctotis echinata

Arctotis echinata, commonly known as the spiked arctotis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces vibrant, showy blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Arctotis echinata is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
