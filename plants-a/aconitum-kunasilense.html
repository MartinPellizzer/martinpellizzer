Aconitum kunasilense

Aconitum kunasilense, commonly known as the Kunasil Valley aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant produces distinctive, helmet-shaped seed pods that contain numerous small, dark brown seeds. Aconitum kunasilense is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a hazardous plant and should be handled with care.
