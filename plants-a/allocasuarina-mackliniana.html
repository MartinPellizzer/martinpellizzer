Allocasuarina mackliniana

Allocasuarina mackliniana, commonly known as the Macklin's she-oak, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its unique feathery foliage, which consists of modified leaves called phyllodes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize disturbed areas.

Allocasuarina mackliniana is adapted to dry and sandy soils, making it a resilient species in arid environments.
