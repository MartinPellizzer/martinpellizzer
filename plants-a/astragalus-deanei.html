Astragalus deanei

Astragalus deanei, commonly known as the Deane's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Astragalus deanei is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to nutrient cycling in its ecosystem.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
