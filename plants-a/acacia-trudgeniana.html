Acacia trudgeniana

Acacia trudgeniana, commonly known as the Trudgen's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its yellow flowers and is often found in coastal and sandy soils. It has adapted to arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
