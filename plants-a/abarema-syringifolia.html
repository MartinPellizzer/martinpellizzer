Abarema syringifolia

Abarema syringifolia, commonly known as the pink-flowered acacia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and dense foliage of small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, allowing it to thrive in dry, tropical environments.

The plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
