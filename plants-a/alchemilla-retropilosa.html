Alchemilla retropilosa

Alchemilla retropilosa, commonly known as the purple alchemilla, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in a fine, silvery down. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

Alchemilla retropilosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, shady conditions.
