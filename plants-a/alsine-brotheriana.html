Alsine brotheriana

Alsine brotheriana, commonly known as the coastal alsike, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-gray hue.

Alsine brotheriana is adapted to salt spray and is an important component of coastal dune ecosystems.
