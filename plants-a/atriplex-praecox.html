Atriplex praecox

Atriplex praecox, commonly known as early saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent leaves, which are typically gray-green and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Atriplex praecox is adapted to tolerate saline and alkaline soils, making it a common pioneer species in disturbed or degraded lands.

Its ability to thrive in harsh environments makes it an important plant for stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
