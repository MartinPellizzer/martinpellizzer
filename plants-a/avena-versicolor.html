Avena versicolor

Avena versicolor, commonly known as the rainbow oat, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, multicolored inflorescence that displays shades of red, orange, yellow, and green. The plant belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, delicate flowers in clusters.

Avena versicolor thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
