Artemisia altopyrenaica

Artemisia altopyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flower heads. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

Its essential oils contain compounds with potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
