Amblostoma armeniacum

Amblostoma armeniacum, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
