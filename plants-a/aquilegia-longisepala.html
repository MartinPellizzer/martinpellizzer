Aquilegia longisepala

Aquilegia longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepal poppy, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features striking, spurred petals that are typically red or orange in color, with elongated sepals that give it its common name. The foliage consists of deeply lobed leaves, which are typically green and hairy.
