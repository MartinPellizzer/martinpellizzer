Angiopteris subfurfuracea

Angiopteris subfurfuracea, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound fronds that are covered in a fine, hairy texture. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

The hairy shield fern is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
