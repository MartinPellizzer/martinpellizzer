Agrostis canina f. varians

Agrostis canina f. varians, commonly known as the variable meadowgrass, is a variant form of the common meadowgrass.

It is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Botanically, it is characterized by its variability in growth form and habitat preference, making it an adaptable species within its range.
