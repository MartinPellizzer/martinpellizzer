Anomalopterys chloroptera

Anomalopterys chloroptera, commonly known as the golden-winged fern, is a unique plant species characterized by its striking leaf morphology.

It features large, bipinnate leaves that resemble the wings of a bird, giving it its common name. The plant exhibits a high degree of chlorophyll concentration, contributing to its vibrant green coloration. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system and specialized vascular tissues that support its growth in diverse habitats.
