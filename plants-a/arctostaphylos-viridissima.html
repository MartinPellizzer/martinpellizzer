Arctostaphylos viridissima

Arctostaphylos viridissima, commonly known as the greenleaf manzanita, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of leathery, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its fruit is a clustered drupe that turns red when mature, providing food for various wildlife species.

Arctostaphylos viridissima is adapted to coastal climates and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
