Adenia staudtii

Adenia staudtii, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a woody shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped fruits that resemble small, green berries. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces showy, fragrant flowers with a unique structure. Its fruits are edible when ripe, though they are typically sour and used in local cuisines.

Adenia staudtii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
