Aulomyrcia subverticillaris

Aulomyrcia subverticillaris, commonly known as the spiral-leaf cycad, is a unique cycad species characterized by its distinctive, spirally arranged leaves.

It is native to the humid lowland forests of Southeast Asia, particularly in regions of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant exhibits a short, stout trunk with a crown of feather-like leaves that form a dense, umbrella-like structure. Its reproductive structures include a large, cone-like strobilus that produces both male and female spores.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its striking foliage and historical significance in traditional horticulture.
