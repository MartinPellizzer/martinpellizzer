Anthurium peregrinense

Anthurium peregrinense, commonly known as the red anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces vibrant red spathes that enclose its small, spadix-shaped inflorescence, making it a popular ornamental species. It thrives in humid, warm environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Anthurium peregrinense is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
