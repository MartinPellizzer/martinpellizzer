Avena nuda subsp. brevis

Avena nuda subsp.

brevis, commonly known as slender oat, is a grass species characterized by its compact growth habit and short, stiff stems. It originates from the Mediterranean region and is often found in rocky or disturbed soils.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with slender, glabrous lemma and palea. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered with short hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
