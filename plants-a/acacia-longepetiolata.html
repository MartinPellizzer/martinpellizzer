Acacia longepetiolata

Acacia longepetiolata, commonly known as the long-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spindly growth habit and its distinctive, compound leaves that are elongated and have long petioles. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical spikes, which appear in late winter to early spring. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are adapted for dispersal by animals.

Acacia longepetiolata is well-suited to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant in suitable conditions.
