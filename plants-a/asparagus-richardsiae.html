Asparagus richardsiae

Asparagus richardsiae, commonly known as the Richards' asparagus, is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is a flowering plant native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant features slender, erect stems that produce small, greenish-white flowers. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine.

Asparagus richardsiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
