Avena sativa var. brevis

Avena sativa var. brevis, commonly known as short oat, is a cultivated variant of the oat plant characterized by its shorter stature and compact growth habit.

This variety is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils with moderate moisture levels. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous florets. The seeds, which are the primary product, are rich in nutrients such as protein, fiber, and minerals.

Due to its resilience and adaptability, it is often used in agricultural settings for forage or grain production.
