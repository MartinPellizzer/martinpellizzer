Aubrieta kotschyi var. kurdica

Aubrieta kotschyi var. kurdica, commonly known as the Kurdish aubrieta, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and northern Iraq.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces vibrant clusters of yellow or orange flowers in early spring. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves and is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it prefers cooler climates.
