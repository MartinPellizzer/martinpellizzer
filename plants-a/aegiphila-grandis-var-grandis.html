Aegiphila grandis var. grandis

Aegiphila grandis var. grandis, commonly known as the large-leaved aegiphila, is a shrub native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or limestone areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
