Aira versicolor

Aira versicolor, commonly known as the variable air grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or white. The plant produces small, clustered flower spikes that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically pale green to pinkish in color, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Aira versicolor is often found in open woodlands, meadows, and along roadsides, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
