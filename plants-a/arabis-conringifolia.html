Arabis conringifolia

Arabis conringifolia, commonly known as Conring's rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of linear, glaucous leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is adapted to cold, rocky environments. It typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,500 meters.

Arabis conringifolia is often found in alpine meadows and is a hardy perennial that thrives in well-drained soils.
