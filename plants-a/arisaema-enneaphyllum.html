Arisaema enneaphyllum

Arisaema enneaphyllum, commonly known as the nine-lobed arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and a unique inflorescence that resembles a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into berries containing bright red seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Arisaema enneaphyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
