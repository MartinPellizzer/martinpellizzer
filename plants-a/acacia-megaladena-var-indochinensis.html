Acacia megaladena var. indochinensis

Acacia megaladena var. indochinensis, commonly known as the Indochina acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family Fabaceae and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its pod-like fruit contains seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread in the region.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
