Aniseia costata

Aniseia costata, commonly known as the coastal anise, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Aniseia costata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
