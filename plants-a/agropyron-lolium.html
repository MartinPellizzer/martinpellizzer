Agropyron lolium

Agropyron lolium, commonly known as goatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in some agricultural settings.

Botanically, it is classified within the Poaceae family and is notable for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
