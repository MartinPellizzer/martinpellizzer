Abelmoschus coccineus

Abelmoschus coccineus, commonly known as red okra, is a flowering plant native to Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces distinctive pods that are typically red or orange when mature, though they can also be green. These pods are edible and are commonly used in various cuisines for their mucilaginous texture and nutritional value.

The plant thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
