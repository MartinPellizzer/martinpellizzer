Astragalus longistylus

Astragalus longistylus, commonly known as the long-styled milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus longistylus is also noted for its medicinal properties, though it is not widely used in modern herbal medicine.
