Arctium leptophyllum

Arctium leptophyllum, commonly known as the lesser burdock, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny fruit structures. The plant features tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are typically purple or pink, arranged in a cluster at the top of the stem.

The plant is known for its prickly burrs, which can attach to clothing or animal fur, aiding in seed dispersal.
