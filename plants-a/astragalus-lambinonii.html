Astragalus lambinonii

Astragalus lambinonii, commonly known as the Lambinon milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several oval, smooth seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. Astragalus lambinonii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
