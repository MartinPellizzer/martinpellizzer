Acinodendron globuliferum

Acinodendron globuliferum, commonly known as the globular acacia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its distinctive globular shape, which is formed by dense clusters of small, woody fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are bipinnate, and the flowers are small and yellow, arranged in compact inflorescences.

The species is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
