Alyssum microcarpum var. sphaerocarpum

Alyssum microcarpum var. sphaerocarpum, commonly known as the globular alyssum, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters and has a mat-forming habit. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the flowers are usually pink or white.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover.
