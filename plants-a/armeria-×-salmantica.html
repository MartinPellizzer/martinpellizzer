Armeria × salmantica

Armeria × salmantica, commonly known as the hybrid sea thrift, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces dense clusters of white or pinkish flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal dunes and cliffs. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically evergreen.

Armeria × salmantica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
