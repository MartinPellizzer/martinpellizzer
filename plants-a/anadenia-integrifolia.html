Anadenia integrifolia

Anadenia integrifolia, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that resemble spiders, which bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white berries that are edible but not commonly consumed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
