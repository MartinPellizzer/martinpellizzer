Albizia tanganyicensis subsp. adamsoniorum

Albizia tanganyicensis subsp.

adamsoniorum, commonly known as the Adamson's legume, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Africa. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged with small, oval leaflets.

The plant produces fluffy, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its pods are elongated and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is often found in dry, sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental and potential ecological benefits.
