Acer yangbiense

Acer yangbiense, commonly known as the Yangbi maple, is a species of maple native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the autumn. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions, preferring cool, moist environments. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in early spring before the leaves.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
