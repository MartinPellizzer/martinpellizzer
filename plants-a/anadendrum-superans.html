Anadendrum superans

Anadendrum superans, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking red and orange floral display during the spring and summer months. The tree has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
