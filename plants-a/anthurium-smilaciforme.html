Anthurium smilaciforme

Anthurium smilaciforme, commonly known as the climbing anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often deep green with a reddish or purple tint on the underside. The plant produces spadices, which are its inflorescences, typically surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Anthurium smilaciforme is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and unique flowering structure.
