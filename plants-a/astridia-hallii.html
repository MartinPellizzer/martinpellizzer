Astridia hallii

Astridia hallii, commonly known as the Hall's aster, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in shades of white to pale purple.

Astridia hallii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
