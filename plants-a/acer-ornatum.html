Acer ornatum

Acer ornatum, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The tree typically grows to a height of 8 to 15 meters, with a spreading, rounded crown. Its flowers are small, inconspicuous, and appear in clusters before the leaves in early spring.

The bark is smooth and reddish-brown, becoming more fissured with age.
