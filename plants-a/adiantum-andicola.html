Adiantum andicola

Adiantum andicola, commonly known as the Andean maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments, often growing in rocky or shaded areas. Its fronds are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive rachis that is often grooved.

Adiantum andicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
