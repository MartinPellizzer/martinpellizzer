Adenophora lamarkii

Adenophora lamarkii, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Adenophora lamarkii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.

Morphology

Adenophora lamarkii has simple, opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically 2-5 cm long, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter green underside.

The stem is erect, cylindrical, and hairy, with a reddish-brown bark that becomes rough with age. Its flowers are clustered in inflorescences, featuring five white to pale blue petals, and bloom in summer, relying on insect pollination. The fruit is a dry capsule that releases small, winged seeds through wind dispersal.

The roots are fibrous and rhizomatous, aiding in the plant's ability to spread and establish in various habitats.
