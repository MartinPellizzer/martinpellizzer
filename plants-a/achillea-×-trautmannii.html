Achillea × trautmannii

Achillea × trautmannii, commonly known as the trautmann's yarrow, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and versatile choice for rock gardens and borders.
