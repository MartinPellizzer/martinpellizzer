Arum italicum subsp. byzantinum

Arum italicum subsp.

byzantinum, commonly known as the Byzantine arum, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features striking, arrow-shaped leaves with a deep green hue and a unique, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a spathe.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its dramatic foliage and distinctive appearance.
