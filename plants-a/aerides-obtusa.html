Aerides obtusa

Aerides obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves and produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. It thrives in warm, moist conditions with indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
