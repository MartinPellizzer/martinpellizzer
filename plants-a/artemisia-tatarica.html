Artemisia tatarica

Artemisia tatarica, commonly known as tatarian aster, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, aromatic foliage and small yellow flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and prefers well-drained soil in full sun. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thujone, which contribute to its strong scent and medicinal properties.
