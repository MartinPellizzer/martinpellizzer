Arabis pectinata

Arabis pectinata, commonly known as the white rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine environments. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.
