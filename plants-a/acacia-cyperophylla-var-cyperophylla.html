Acacia cyperophylla var. cyperophylla

Acacia cyperophylla var. cyperophylla, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often growing in dunes or on rocky outcrops. It has a woody stem and is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.

This species is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
