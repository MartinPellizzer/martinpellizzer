Alstonia smithii

Alstonia smithii, commonly known as the white ghost tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers followed by woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
