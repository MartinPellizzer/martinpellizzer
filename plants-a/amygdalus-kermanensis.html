Amygdalus kermanensis

Amygdalus kermanensis, commonly known as Kerman almond, is a species of shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, elongated leaves and pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, hard almonds that are typically not suitable for human consumption but are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
