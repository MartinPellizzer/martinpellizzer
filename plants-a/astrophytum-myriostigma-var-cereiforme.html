Astrophytum myriostigma var. cereiforme

Astrophytum myriostigma var. cereiforme, commonly known as the "cotton candle" or "cotton tree," is a unique cacti species native to the Chihuahuan Desert in Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that can grow up to 10 feet in height, covered in dense, white, woolly spines that give it a fluffy appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise desolate form. It is a slow-growing, columnar cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for arid garden environments.

Its distinctive appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a standout in the world of succulents.
