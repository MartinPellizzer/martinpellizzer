Aspalathus chenopoda

Aspalathus chenopoda, commonly known as the silverleaf bush, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. It is drought-tolerant and can withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical research.
