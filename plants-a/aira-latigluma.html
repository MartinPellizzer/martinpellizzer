Aira latigluma

Aira latigluma, commonly known as the narrow-leaved air grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
