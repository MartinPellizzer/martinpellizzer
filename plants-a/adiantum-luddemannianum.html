Adiantum luddemannianum

Adiantum luddemannianum, commonly known as the Luddemann's maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Adiantaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soil. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
