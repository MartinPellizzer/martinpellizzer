Alpinia grandiceps

Alpinia grandiceps, commonly known as the giant ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect rhizomes and broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Alpinia grandiceps is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
