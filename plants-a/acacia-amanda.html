Acacia amanda

Acacia amanda, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features slender, often twisted branches and small, bipinnate leaves. Its yellow flowers are arranged in cylindrical spikes, typical of many Acacia species.

Acacia amanda is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of native Australian flora.
