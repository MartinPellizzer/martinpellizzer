Anplectrum cyanocarpum

Anplectrum cyanocarpum, commonly known as the blue-fruited grass.

It is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces striking blue to purple berries, which are a key feature in its botanical description. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Anplectrum cyanocarpum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and fruit.
