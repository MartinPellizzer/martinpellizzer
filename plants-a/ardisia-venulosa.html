Ardisia venulosa

Ardisia venulosa, commonly known as the glossy laurel, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which features dark green, glossy leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. Ardisia venulosa thrives in shaded to partially sunny environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
