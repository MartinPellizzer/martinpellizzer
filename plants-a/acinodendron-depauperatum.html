Acinodendron depauperatum

Acinodendron depauperatum, commonly known as the poorleaf acinodendron, is a small shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically small and elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and understudied species in botanical research.
