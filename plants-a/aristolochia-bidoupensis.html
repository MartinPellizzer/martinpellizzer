Aristolochia bidoupensis

Aristolochia bidoupensis, commonly known as the Bidoup Narrowleaf Dutchman's Pipe, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex floral structure that attracts specific pollinators. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and often climbing.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional medicine practices.
