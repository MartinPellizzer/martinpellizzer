Alvaradoa amorphoides subsp. typica

Alvaradoa amorphoides subsp.

typica, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its succulent leaves and deep root system. It has small, wind-dispersed seeds that allow it to colonize disturbed soils.

The species is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
