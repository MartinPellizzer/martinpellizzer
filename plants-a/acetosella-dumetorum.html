Acetosella dumetorum

Acetosella dumetorum, commonly known as mouse ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the family Montiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their mild diuretic properties.

Acetosella dumetorum is also noted for its ability to spread through runners, forming dense mats in suitable habitats.
