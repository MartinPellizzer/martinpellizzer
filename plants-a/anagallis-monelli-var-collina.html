Anagallis monelli var. collina

Anagallis monelli var. collina, commonly known as the mountain mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are hairy and oval-shaped, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
