Anemone slavica var. rhodopaea

Anemone slavica var. rhodopaea, commonly known as the Rhodopean anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow center. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that are typically bluish-green in hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early spring blooms.
