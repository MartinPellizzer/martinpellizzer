Ataccia cristata

Ataccia cristata, commonly known as the crowned bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant features a compact, bushy growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Ataccia cristata is also noted for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
