Agrostis sibirica

Agrostis sibirica, commonly known as Siberian bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to cold climates of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its low-growing, tufted habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and tundra regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is used in ecological restoration projects and as a ornamental plant in cooler climates.
