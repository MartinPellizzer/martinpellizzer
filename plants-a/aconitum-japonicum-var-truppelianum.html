Aconitum japonicum var. truppelianum

Aconitum japonicum var. truppelianum, commonly known as Japanese monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features striking blue to purple spurred flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, doubly serrated leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its toxic alkaloids make it a plant that should be handled with care.
