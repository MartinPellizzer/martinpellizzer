Alchemilla xerampelina

Alchemilla xerampelina, commonly known as the silver-leaved alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features succulent, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping.
