Armeria maritima subsp. hornburgensis

Armeria maritima subsp.

hornburgensis, commonly known as hornburg's thrift, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa. It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in maritime environments.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, white or pink flowers during the summer months. Its foliage consists of narrow, fleshy leaves that are adapted to tolerate salt spray and strong winds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
