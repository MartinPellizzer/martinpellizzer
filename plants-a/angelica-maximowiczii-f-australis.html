Angelica maximowiczii f. australis

Angelica maximowiczii f. australis, commonly known as the Australian angelica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Australia.

It features large, compound leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a distinctive silvery underside. The plant produces tall, branched stems topped with clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along waterways.

The species is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
