Allium sphaerocephalon subsp. curtum

Allium sphaerocephalon subsp.

curtum, commonly known as the purple globeflower, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features a compact, spherical bulb and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from the base of the bulb.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
