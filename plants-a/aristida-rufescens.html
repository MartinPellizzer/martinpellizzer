Aristida rufescens

Aristida rufescens, commonly known as redstem dropseed, is a grass species native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, a common adaptation in grasses. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open prairies and disturbed areas.

Aristida rufescens is a drought-tolerant plant that plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects.
