Absinthium arborescens

Absinthium arborescens, commonly known as the tree absinth, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a robust, branching stem that can grow up to two meters in height, with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicle clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its root system is fibrous and deep, allowing it to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

Absinthium arborescens is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and antispasmodic effects.
