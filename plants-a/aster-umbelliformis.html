Aster umbelliformis

Aster umbelliformis, commonly known as the umbrella aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flat-topped inflorescence composed of numerous small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with hairy surfaces.

Aster umbelliformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
