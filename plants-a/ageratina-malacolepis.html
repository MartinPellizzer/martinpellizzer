Ageratina malacolepis

Ageratina malacolepis, commonly known as the Malacolepis ageratum, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically features slender stems and small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Ageratina malacolepis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.
