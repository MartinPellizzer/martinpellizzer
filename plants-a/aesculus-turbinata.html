Aesculus turbinata

Aesculus turbinata, commonly known as the Japanese horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves that are often adorned with a distinctive red or purple coloration on the underside. The tree produces showy, white or pink flowers in upright clusters during the spring, followed by spiny, capsule-like fruits. Its bark is smooth and grey, and it can grow up to 15 meters tall.

Aesculus turbinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
