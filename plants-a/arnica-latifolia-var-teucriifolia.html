Arnica latifolia var. teucriifolia

Arnica latifolia var. teucriifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf arnica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with reddish or purple hues. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers with prominent yellow centers, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
