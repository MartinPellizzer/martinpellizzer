Allophylus eustachys

Allophylus eustachys, commonly known as the narrow-leaved alpine holly, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and is often found in rocky or forested habitats. It is adapted to cool, humid climates and can tolerate partial shade.

Allophylus eustachys is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
