Anthosachne falcis

Anthosachne falcis, commonly known as the narrowleaf arrowhead, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers.

Anthosachne falcis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
