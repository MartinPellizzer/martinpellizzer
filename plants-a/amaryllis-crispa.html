Amaryllis crispa

Amaryllis crispa, commonly known as the crisped amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The leaves are broad, strap-like, and often have a waxy texture. This species thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom reliably in warm climates.
