Abutilon densiflorum

Abutilon densiflorum, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance and adaptability.
