Adiantum claussenii

Adiantum claussenii, commonly known as the California maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern native to the western United States.

It features finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green with a silvery underside. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Adiantum claussenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
