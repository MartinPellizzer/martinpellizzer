Adiantum novae-caledoniae

Adiantum novae-caledoniae, commonly known as the New Caledonia maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the island of New Caledonia.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble lace. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are bipinnate, with a distinctive rachis that is grooved and often covered in fine hairs.

Adiantum novae-caledoniae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
