Artemisia santolinifolia var. scheludjakoviae

Artemisia santolinifolia var. scheludjakoviae, commonly known as the Scheludjakov's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, contributing to its ornamental value. It is adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its aromatic properties make it a subject of interest in both traditional medicine and horticulture.
