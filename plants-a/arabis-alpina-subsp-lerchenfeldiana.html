Arabis alpina subsp. lerchenfeldiana

Arabis alpina subsp.

lerchenfeldiana, commonly known as the alpine rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, often in well-drained soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring. It is adapted to cold climates and can withstand harsh conditions such as strong winds and low temperatures.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
