Adriana glabrata var. acerifolia

Adriana glabrata var. acerifolia, commonly known as the maple-leaved adriana, is a unique hybrid plant characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It exhibits broad, palmately lobed leaves that resemble those of maple trees, giving it its common name. The plant is native to specific regions in southern Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in spring, while its ornamental value lies primarily in its foliage.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is a sought-after plant among horticultural enthusiasts.
