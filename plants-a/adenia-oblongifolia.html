Adenia oblongifolia

Adenia oblongifolia, commonly known as the African butter tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by edible, fleshy fruits. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil types.
