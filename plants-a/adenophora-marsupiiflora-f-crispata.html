Adenophora marsupiiflora f. crispata

Adenophora marsupiiflora f. crispata, commonly known as the crinkled bellflower, is a variant of the Adenophora genus native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crinkled leaves and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage is often hairy, and the flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden settings for its unique appearance.
