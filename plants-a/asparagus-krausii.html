Asparagus krausii

Asparagus krausii, commonly known as the Cape asparagus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate foliage and unique growth habit.
