Aphelandra montis-scalaris

Aphelandra montis-scalaris, commonly known as the mountain scallop plant, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is native to the high-altitude regions of Central and South America, particularly in the Andes. The plant is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, yellow, and white. It produces inflorescences consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the Aroid family.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability to shaded environments, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
