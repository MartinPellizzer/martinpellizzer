Anthurium palacioanum

Anthurium palacioanum, commonly known as the Palacio's arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, which can range from green to red. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium palacioanum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying properties.
