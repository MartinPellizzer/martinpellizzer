Artocarpus scortechinii

Artocarpus scortechinii, commonly known as the Java breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces a unique fruit with a fibrous outer husk and a starchy, edible interior. The fruit is typically harvested when it is fully mature and has a sweet, nutty flavor.

It is often used in traditional cooking and has cultural significance in the regions where it grows.
