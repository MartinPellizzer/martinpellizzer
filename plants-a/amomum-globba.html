Amomum globba

Amomum globba, commonly known as the Greater Galangal, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric. The plant features tall, cylindrical rhizomes that are the primary part used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a distinct aroma.

Amomum globba is valued for its spicy, fragrant properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory benefits.
