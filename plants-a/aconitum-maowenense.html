Aconitum maowenense

Aconitum maowenense, commonly known as Maowen aconite, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is native to China.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its tall, sparsely leafy stems and striking blue to purple flowers. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are typically palmately divided. Aconitum maowenense contains potent alkaloids, making it toxic and requiring careful handling.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments in mountainous regions.
