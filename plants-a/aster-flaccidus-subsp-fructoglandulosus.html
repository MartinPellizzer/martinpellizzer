Aster flaccidus subsp. fructoglandulosus

Aster flaccidus subsp.

fructoglandulosus, commonly known as the glandular-fruited aster, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruit is distinctive, featuring glandular structures that give it its subspecies name.

It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.
