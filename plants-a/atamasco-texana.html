Atamasco texana

Atamasco texana, commonly known as Texas atamasco, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, edible berries that are popular among wildlife.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits in native gardens.
