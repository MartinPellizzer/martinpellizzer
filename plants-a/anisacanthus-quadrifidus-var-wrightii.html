Anisacanthus quadrifidus var. wrightii

Anisacanthus quadrifidus var. wrightii, commonly known as the desert lantana, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-green hue. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of orange, red, and yellow, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native gardens.
