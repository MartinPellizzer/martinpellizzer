Aeschynanthus cordifolius

Aeschynanthus cordifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaved ivy or red-veined ivy, is a tropical evergreen vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green with prominent red veins. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually orange or red in color, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Aeschynanthus cordifolius is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and unique flowering display.
