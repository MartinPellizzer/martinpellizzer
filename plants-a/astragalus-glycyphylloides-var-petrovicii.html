Astragalus glycyphylloides var. petrovicii

Astragalus glycyphylloides var. petrovicii, commonly known as the sweet carline, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, typical of the genus Astragalus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
