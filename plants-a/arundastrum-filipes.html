Arundastrum filipes

Arundastrum filipes, commonly known as the slender rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often densely packed, forming a dense mat or tussock. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and may produce small, inconspicuous flowers.

Arundastrum filipes plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
