Aspidosperma nitidum

Aspidosperma nitidum, commonly known as the white cedar, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds with tufts of hair for wind dispersal.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
