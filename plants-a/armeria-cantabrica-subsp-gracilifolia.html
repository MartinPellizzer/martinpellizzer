Armeria cantabrica subsp. gracilifolia

Armeria cantabrica subsp.

gracilifolia, commonly known as the slender-leaved thrift, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of northern Spain. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers.

The leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to maritime climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh coastal environments.
