Acerates incarnata

Acerates incarnata, commonly known as the Swamp Rose or Swamp Beauty, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetlands and moist woodlands in the eastern United States.

It features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purple beneath, adding visual interest. It thrives in shallow water or saturated soil, making it a valuable plant for wetland restoration.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a popular choice for water garden and naturalized landscapes.
