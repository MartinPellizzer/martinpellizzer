Aquilegia canadensis var. incarnata

Aquilegia canadensis var. incarnata, commonly known as the Canadian columbine, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features striking, spurred flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, typically arranged in a palmate pattern. It thrives in partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soils, often found in woodland areas.

Its showy blooms appear in early spring, adding vibrant color to natural landscapes.
