Alopecurus aristatus

Alopecurus aristatus, commonly known as the tufted bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and dense, tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue-green spikelets that are often clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

Alopecurus aristatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in ecological restoration projects.
