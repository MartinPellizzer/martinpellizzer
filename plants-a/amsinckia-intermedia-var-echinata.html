Amsinckia intermedia var. echinata

Amsinckia intermedia var. echinata, commonly known as the spiny milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species that thrives in well-drained soils.

The plant is also known for its milky sap, which is a distinguishing botanical feature.
