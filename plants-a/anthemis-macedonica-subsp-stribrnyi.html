Anthemis macedonica subsp. stribrnyi

Anthemis macedonica subsp.

stribrnyi, commonly known as the Macedonian ragwort, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish to orange flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, such as slopes and cliffs. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
