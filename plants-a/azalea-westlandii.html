Azalea westlandii

Azalea westlandii, commonly known as the Westland azalea, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the West Indies.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring with vibrant pink to red hues, making it a popular ornamental plant. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Azalea westlandii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
