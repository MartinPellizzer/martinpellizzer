Atriplex patula var. obtusa

Atriplex patula var. obtusa, commonly known as the broadleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in dry environments. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in saline and alkaline soils. Its small greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it is often used for erosion control and wildlife habitat.

This variety is distinguished by its broader, more rounded leaves compared to the typical form of Atriplex patula.
