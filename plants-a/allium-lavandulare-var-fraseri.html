Allium lavandulare var. fraseri

Allium lavandulare var. fraseri, commonly known as Fraser's garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are aromatic and often tinged with a pale blue or purple hue. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic or onion flavor, making it a useful plant in culinary applications.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, often found in moist, shady woodlands.
