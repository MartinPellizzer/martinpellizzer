Anacamptis × murgiana

Anacamptis × murgiana, commonly known as the Murgian orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Anacamptis × murgiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
