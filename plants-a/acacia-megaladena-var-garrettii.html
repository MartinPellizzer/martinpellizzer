Acacia megaladena var. garrettii

Acacia megaladena var. garrettii, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, yellow-green foliage and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping and restoration projects.
