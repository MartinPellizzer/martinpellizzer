Acacia lanuginosa

Acacia lanuginosa, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems covered in soft, white, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Acacia lanuginosa is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Native Distribution

It is well adapted to high salinity and wind exposure, making it a characteristic species of the Arabian coastal environment.

Acacia lanuginosa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.
