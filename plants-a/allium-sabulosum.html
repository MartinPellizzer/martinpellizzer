Allium sabulosum

Allium sabulosum, commonly known as sand garlic, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale purple flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its bulbous underground stem, which stores nutrients and water. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Allium sabulosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has mild culinary uses in some traditional cuisines.
