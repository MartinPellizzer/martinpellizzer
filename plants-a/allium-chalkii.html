Allium chalkii

Allium chalkii, commonly known as the Armenian garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, pale purple to white flowers. The plant's bulbs are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun.

Allium chalkii is known for its strong garlic-like aroma and is relatively easy to cultivate in temperate climates.
