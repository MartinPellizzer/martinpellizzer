Aspidium evenulosum

Aspidium evenulosum, commonly known as the common water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically bipinnate, with small, rounded pinnae. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in damp woodlands. Its spores are produced in sori that are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Aspidium evenulosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
