Aster sellowii

Aster sellowii, commonly known as the Sellow's aster, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, thriving in temperate climates. Its showy purple or blue flowers bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster sellowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
