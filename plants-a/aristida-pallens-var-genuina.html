Aristida pallens var. genuina

Aristida pallens var. genuina, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a grass species native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and fine, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, fluffy inflorescences that are often mistaken for wheat or other grasses. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as grasslands and disturbed sites.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
