Arenaria sivasica

Arenaria sivasica, commonly known as the Sivas sandflower, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapted to thrive in dry, sandy environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid regions and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
