Arnica amplexicaulis var. piperi

Arnica amplexicaulis var. piperi, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are densely hairy, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers in the summer, arranged in a single terminal inflorescence. Its leaves and flowers contain bioactive compounds, making it useful in traditional medicine.

However, it is important to note that the plant is toxic if ingested and should be handled with care.
