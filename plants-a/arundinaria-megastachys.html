Arundinaria megastachys

Arundinaria megastachys, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces large, feather-like leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a useful and sometimes invasive species.
