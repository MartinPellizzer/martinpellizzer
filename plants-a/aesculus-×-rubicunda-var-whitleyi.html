Aesculus × rubicunda var. whitleyi

Aesculus × rubicunda var. whitleyi, commonly known as the Red Horsechestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, red-purple bracts that resemble flowers, which appear in late spring. The tree has palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its bark is smooth and grayish-brown. It produces large, spiny capsules that contain glossy, dark brown seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its vibrant fall foliage.
