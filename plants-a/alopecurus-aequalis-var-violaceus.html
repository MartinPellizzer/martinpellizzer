Alopecurus aequalis var. violaceus

Alopecurus aequalis var. violaceus, commonly known as violet false oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and small, violet-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. Its slender, creeping rhizomes allow it to spread and form dense mats, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds ornamental value to gardens.
