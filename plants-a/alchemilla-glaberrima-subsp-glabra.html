Alchemilla glaberrima subsp. glabra

Alchemilla glaberrima subsp.

glabra, commonly known as the smooth lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that become smooth in maturity.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense, cup-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its foliage is used in herbal medicine for its purported soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.
