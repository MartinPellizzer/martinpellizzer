Anemone eunrenia

Anemone eunrenia, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are alternate and often covered in a soft, hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Anemone eunrenia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
