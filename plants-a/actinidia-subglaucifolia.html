Actinidia subglaucifolia

Actinidia subglaucifolia, commonly known as the silver vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in early summer, followed by edible berries that are popular among both humans and animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value and ability to climb make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
