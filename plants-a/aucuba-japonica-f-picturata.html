Aucuba japonica f. picturata

Aucuba japonica f. picturata, commonly known as the variegated Japanese aucuba, is a cultivar prized for its striking foliage.

Its leaves are typically variegated with combinations of yellow, white, and green, creating a vibrant visual effect. The plant is evergreen, with a compact, rounded growth habit that makes it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation. It thrives in partial to full shade and requires moderate watering, making it a versatile choice for shaded garden spaces.

The botanical features of this plant include glossy, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in the spring.
