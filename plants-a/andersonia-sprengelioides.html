Andersonia sprengelioides

Andersonia sprengelioides, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and strikingly colored flowers. The plant typically produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with a distinctive lip petal. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
