Aphelandra squarrosa var. angustifolia

Aphelandra squarrosa var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf torch ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-like inflorescences that range in color from yellow to orange-red. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves that are glossy and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Aphelandra squarrosa var. angustifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant flowers and foliage.
