Astragalus altiusculus

Astragalus altiusculus, commonly known as the tall milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and steppe environments.

Astragalus altiusculus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
