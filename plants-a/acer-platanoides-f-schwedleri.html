Acer platanoides f. schwedleri

Acer platanoides f. schwedleri, commonly known as the Swedish maple, is a cultivar of the Norway maple.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are typically lobed and have a distinctive white or cream-colored margin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras that disperse its seeds. It is a deciduous tree or shrub that thrives in temperate climates and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This cultivar is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
