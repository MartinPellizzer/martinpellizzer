Agropyron lavrenkoanum var. pectinatum

Agropyron lavrenkoanum var. pectinatum, commonly known as the Pectinate wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
