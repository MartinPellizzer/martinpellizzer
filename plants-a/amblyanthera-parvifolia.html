Amblyanthera parvifolia

Amblyanthera parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved blushing flower, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant white flowers that turn pink as they mature. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Amblyanthera parvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
