Aspidium zerophyllum

Aspidium zerophyllum, commonly known as the barren fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its sterile fronds, which lack spore-producing structures, making it unique among ferns. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a delicate, feathery appearance.

Despite its name, it plays an important ecological role in its habitat by contributing to soil stability and providing shelter for small organisms.
