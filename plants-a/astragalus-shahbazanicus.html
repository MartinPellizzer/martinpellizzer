Astragalus shahbazanicus

Astragalus shahbazanicus, commonly known as the Shahbazan hairy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in racemes, and the leaves are compound with small, hairy leaflets.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
