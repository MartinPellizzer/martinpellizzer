Atriplex erecta

Atriplex erecta, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its salt-tolerant nature allows it to thrive in soils with high salinity.

Atriplex erecta plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
