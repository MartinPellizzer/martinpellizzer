Astragalus bryogenes

Astragalus bryogenes, commonly known as the alpine milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus bryogenes is often found in high-altitude environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
