Astragalus peregrinus subsp. warionis

Astragalus peregrinus subsp.

warionis, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in steppes and deserts.

The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow or pale yellow pea-like flowers. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with limited water availability.

The subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife.
