Astragalus laxus

Astragalus laxus, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Astragalus laxus thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
