Atriplex truncata var. stricta

Atriplex truncata var. stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to saline soils and can thrive in areas with low rainfall.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
