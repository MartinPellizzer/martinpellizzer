Arctostaphylos campbellae

Arctostaphylos campbellae, commonly known as Campbell's manzanita, is a shrub native to California and Oregon.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
