Anisopappus anemonifolius subsp. abercornensis

Anisopappus anemonifolius subsp.

abercornensis, commonly known as the Abercorn aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are deeply lobed and resemble those of an anemone, giving it its common name.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
