Aspalathus exigua

Aspalathus exigua, commonly known as the small African pea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It produces small, yellowish-green pods that contain several seeds.

Aspalathus exigua is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
