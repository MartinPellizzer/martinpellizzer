Acanthus dioscoridis var. dioscoridis

Acanthus dioscoridis var. dioscoridis, commonly known as the Greek Acanthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking, palmate leaves with a silvery underside and tall spikes of showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant is characterized by its robust root system and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, exhibiting a hardy nature that allows it to survive in temperate climates.

Its historical significance in ancient art and architecture further highlights its botanical and cultural importance.
