Amorpha coerulea

Amorpha coerulea, commonly known as blue false indigo, is a perennial legume native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated seed pods that turn yellow as they mature. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Amorpha coerulea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration projects.
