Aster amellus subsp. scepusiensis

Aster amellus subsp.

scepusiensis, commonly known as the Scepusian aster, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains in Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center.

The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, often growing in meadows and woodland edges.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
