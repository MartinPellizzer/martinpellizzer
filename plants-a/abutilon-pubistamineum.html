Abutilon pubistamineum

Abutilon pubistamineum, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a velvety texture, and it has a fibrous root system that helps it thrive in well-drained soils.

Abutilon pubistamineum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in warm climates.
