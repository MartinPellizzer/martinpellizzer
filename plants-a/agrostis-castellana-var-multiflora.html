Agrostis castellana var. multiflora

Agrostis castellana var. multiflora, commonly known as the mountain bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine environments. The plant has slender, tufted shoots with narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for invertebrates in high-altitude ecosystems.
