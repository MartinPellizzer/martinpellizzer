Allium ovalifolium var. ovalifolium

Allium ovalifolium var. ovalifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf garlic.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped, with a bluish-gray hue. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and typically bloom in shades of pink to purple.

The bulb is edible and has a mild garlic flavor, making it a useful plant in culinary and medicinal applications.
