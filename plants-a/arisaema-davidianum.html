Arisaema davidianum

Arisaema davidianum, commonly known as the David's arisaema, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and it produces berries that contain the seeds.

Arisaema davidianum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
