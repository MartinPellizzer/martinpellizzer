Azarolus crataegoides var. piriformis

Azarolus crataegoides var. piriformis, commonly known as the pear-shaped azarolus, is a variant of the azarolus plant native to specific regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive pear-shaped fruits, which are typically yellow when ripe and have a slightly tart flavor. The plant produces small, leathery leaves that are arranged alternately along its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique fruit characteristics.
