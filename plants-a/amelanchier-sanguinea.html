Amelanchier sanguinea

Amelanchier sanguinea, commonly known as the red elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that ripen to a deep red color in late summer. These berries are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Amelanchier sanguinea is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits to wildlife.
