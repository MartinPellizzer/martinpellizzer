Athyrium esculentum

Athyrium esculentum, commonly known as the edible water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, frond-like leaves that are often used in traditional cuisine. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is commonly found in wetlands and along stream banks. Its edible parts include the young fronds, which are harvested and cooked.

The botanical features of Athyrium esculentum make it a valuable plant in both ecological and culinary contexts.
