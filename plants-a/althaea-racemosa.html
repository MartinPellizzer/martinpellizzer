Althaea racemosa

Althaea racemosa, commonly known as the marshmallow plant, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features erect, hairy stems that can grow up to 2 meters tall, often with a reddish-brown color. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

The roots and leaves are traditionally used in herbal medicine for their mucilage content, which has soothing properties.
