Atriplex patula subsp. verreauxii

Atriplex patula subsp.

verreauxii, commonly known as silverleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its gray-green foliage, which is covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, providing protection against intense sunlight and water loss.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it an important component of stabilizing dunes and salt-affected landscapes.

Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
