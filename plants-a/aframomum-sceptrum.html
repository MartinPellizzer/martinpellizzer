Aframomum sceptrum

Aframomum sceptrum, commonly known as the sacred ginger, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces tall, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The botanical features of Aframomum sceptrum make it a valuable species in both horticulture and ethnobotanical research.
