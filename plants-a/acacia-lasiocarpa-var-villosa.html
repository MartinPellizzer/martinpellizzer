Acacia lasiocarpa var. villosa

Acacia lasiocarpa var. villosa, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and its distinctive foliage of small, dense, grey-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its woody, fibrous stems are often used in traditional Aboriginal practices for basket-making and other crafts.

The species is well adapted to drought and poor soils, making it a hardy component of dryland ecosystems.
