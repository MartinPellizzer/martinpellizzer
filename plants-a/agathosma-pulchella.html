Agathosma pulchella

Agathosma pulchella, commonly known as the sweet cycad, is a unique plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Cycadaceae and is characterized by its palm-like appearance with a stout trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant is adapted to arid environments, showcasing a slow growth rate and drought tolerance. Its reproductive structures include large, cone-like inflorescences that produce both male and female flowers.

Agathosma pulchella is also noted for its aromatic foliage, which has been used traditionally in various cultural practices.
