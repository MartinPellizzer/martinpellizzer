Altingia gracilipes

Altingia gracilipes, commonly known as the slender-stemmed altingia, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, shrub-like plant characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Altingia gracilipes is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
