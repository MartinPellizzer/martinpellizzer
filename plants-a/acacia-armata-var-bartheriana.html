Acacia armata var. bartheriana

Acacia armata var. bartheriana, commonly known as the Barther's wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters. The leaves are phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that function as the primary photosynthetic organs. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought conditions.
