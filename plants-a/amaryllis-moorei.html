Amaryllis moorei

Amaryllis moorei, commonly known as the Moore's amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces a tall, sturdy stem with broad, strap-like leaves that are typically a deep green color. It is a bulbous plant that thrives in well-drained soil and requires a period of dormancy to bloom successfully.

Amaryllis moorei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care in both indoor and outdoor settings.
