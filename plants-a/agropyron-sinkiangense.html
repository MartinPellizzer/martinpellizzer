Agropyron sinkiangense

Agropyron sinkiangense, commonly known as the Xinjiang grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often used for soil stabilization and as forage for livestock.

Its ecological importance lies in its role in preventing desertification in its native habitat.
