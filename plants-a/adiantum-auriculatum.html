Adiantum auriculatum

Adiantum auriculatum, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is native to regions of North America, particularly the eastern United States. The plant is distinguished by its bright green, lance-shaped leaves that are often adorned with small, ear-like lobes at the base of the fronds. Adiantum auriculatum thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found growing along stream banks or in woodland areas.

Its intricate leaf structure and graceful appearance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
