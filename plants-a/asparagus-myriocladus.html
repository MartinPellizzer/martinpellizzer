Asparagus myriocladus

Asparagus myriocladus, commonly known as the desert asparagus, is a shrub-like plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It features spiny, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in length, adapted to store water in its succulent tissues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, followed by small, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

Asparagus myriocladus is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its resilience in harsh climates.
