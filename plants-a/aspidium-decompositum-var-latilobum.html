Aspidium decompositum var. latilobum

Aspidium decompositum var. latilobum, commonly known as the broad-lobed water fern, is a variant of the water fern genus Aspidium.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds that are typically borne on long, flexible stipes. The plant thrives in wet, shaded environments such as marshes and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually pale green and have a delicate, translucent appearance.

This variety is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental qualities in aquatic gardens.
