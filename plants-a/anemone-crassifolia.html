Anemone crassifolia

Anemone crassifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf anemone, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained soils. The plant features heart-shaped, rough-textured leaves and produces showy, white to pink flowers in early spring. Its botanical characteristics include a short, stout stem and compound leaves with a waxy surface.

Anemone crassifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
