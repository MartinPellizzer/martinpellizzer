Allium sardoum var. gherardii

Allium sardoum var. gherardii, commonly known as the Gherardii garlic, is a botanical variant of the species Allium sardoum.

It is characterized by its robust, bulbous stem and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a rosette near the ground.

This variety is valued for its culinary uses and ornamental appeal in gardens.
