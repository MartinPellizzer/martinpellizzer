Acanthogonum rigidum

Acanthogonum rigidum, commonly known as the rigid acanthogonum, is a member of the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright, branching stems that are typically covered in stiff, spiny hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink to red in color, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Acanthogonum rigidum is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.
