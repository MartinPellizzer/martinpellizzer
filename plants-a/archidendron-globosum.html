Archidendron globosum

Archidendron globosum, commonly known as the globular legume, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, spherical flowers that give it its common name. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and conservation efforts.
