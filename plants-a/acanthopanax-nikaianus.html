Acanthopanax nikaianus

Acanthopanax nikaianus, commonly known as the Nikaians' spurge, is a woody shrub native to the Korean Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
