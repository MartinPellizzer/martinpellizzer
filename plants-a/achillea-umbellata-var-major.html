Achillea umbellata var. major

Achillea umbellata var. major, commonly known as the giant yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped umbels. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, with a strong, slightly bitter scent.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
