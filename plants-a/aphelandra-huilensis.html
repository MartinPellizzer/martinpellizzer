Aphelandra huilensis

Aphelandra huilensis, commonly known as the Huila flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to the Andean region of Colombia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, commonly known as the palm family, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow-orange inflorescences that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and private collections.

However, due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
