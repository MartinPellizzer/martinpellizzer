Actinidia henryi var. glabricaulis

Actinidia henryi var. glabricaulis, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed kiwifruit vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, smooth stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by fuzzy, oval fruits that ripen in late summer. The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often used in juices and desserts.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.
