Astragalus arakansis

Astragalus arakansis, commonly known as the Arakan milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the eastern Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia, including Bangladesh and Myanmar. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

Astragalus arakansis is typically found in moist, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.
