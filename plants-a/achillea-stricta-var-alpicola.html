Achillea stricta var. alpicola

Achillea stricta var. alpicola, commonly known as alpine yarrow, is a perennial flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and finely divided, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for alpine gardens and rockeries.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in high-altitude environments.
