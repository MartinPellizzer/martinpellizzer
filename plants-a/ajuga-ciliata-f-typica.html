Ajuga ciliata f. typica

Ajuga ciliata f. typica, commonly known as the typical form of the carpet bugle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ground cover in gardens and natural landscapes.
