Amygdalus stocksiana

Amygdalus stocksiana, commonly known as the Stocks' almond, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and the Tian Shan. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a leathery texture and a bluish-green hue.

The fruit is a drupe, containing a single almond-like seed enclosed in a hard shell.
