Amphinomia lupinifolia

Amphinomia lupinifolia, commonly known as the silver lupin, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with a silvery underside, giving it its common name. It produces small, clustered white or pale purple flowers that bloom in spring.

Amphinomia lupinifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained, sandy soils.
