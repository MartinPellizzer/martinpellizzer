Aristolochia chrysops

Aristolochia chrysops, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its unique, winding stems that often climb or trail along the ground. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the flowers emit a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor to attract specific pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
