Amaranthus gangeticus var. linearis

Amaranthus gangeticus var. linearis, commonly known as Indian spinach or Chinese spinach, is an annual herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are edible and rich in nutrients such as iron and vitamins. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that develop into spiky, dry capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for both culinary and horticultural purposes.
