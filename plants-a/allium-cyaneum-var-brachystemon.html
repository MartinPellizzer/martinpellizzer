Allium cyaneum var. brachystemon

Allium cyaneum var. brachystemon, commonly known as the blue onion or blue garlic, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, spherical clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, bulbous structure, which is used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ease of cultivation.
