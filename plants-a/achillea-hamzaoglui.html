Achillea hamzaoglui

Achillea hamzaoglui, commonly known as Hamzaoglu's yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its flat, composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, while its flowers range in color from white to pale yellow.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and healing properties.
