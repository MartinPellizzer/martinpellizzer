Aglaia argentea var. curtisii

Aglaia argentea var. curtisii, commonly known as the silver aglaia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by oval, woody fruits containing smooth, black seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings.
