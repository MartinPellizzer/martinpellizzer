Abutilon holstii

Abutilon holstii, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves. The plant produces showy, pendulous flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a soft, fuzzy texture, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Abutilon holstii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
