Astragalus lurorum f. chamchidensis

Astragalus lurorum f. chamchidensis, commonly known as the Chamchidian milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, typical of the genus Astragalus. It is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus, particularly in Georgia.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
