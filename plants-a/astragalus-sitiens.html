Astragalus sitiens

Astragalus sitiens, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in dry conditions.

Astragalus sitiens plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing food for various wildlife species.
