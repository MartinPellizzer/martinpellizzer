Attalea tiasse

Attalea tiasse, commonly known as the Brazilian palm, is a tall, slender palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It has a slender trunk that can grow up to 20 meters in height, with a smooth, grayish-brown bark. The plant produces large, feather-like leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces edible fruits that are consumed by both wildlife and humans.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various species.
