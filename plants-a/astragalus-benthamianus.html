Astragalus benthamianus

Astragalus benthamianus, commonly known as Bentham's milkvetch, is a flowering plant species belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, Russia, and Mongolia. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. It produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus benthamianus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
