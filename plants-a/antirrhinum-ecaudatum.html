Antirrhinum ecaudatum

Antirrhinum ecaudatum, commonly known as the slender snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Antirrhinum ecaudatum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
