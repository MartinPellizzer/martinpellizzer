Asterothamnus centrali-asiaticus var. centrali-asiaticus

Asterothamnus centrali-asiaticus var. centrali-asiaticus, commonly known as the Central Asian thistle, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and a basal rosette of grayish-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
