Acacia disparrima subsp. disparrima

Acacia disparrima subsp.

disparrima, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are typically narrow and linear.

The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

This species is often used in reclamation projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil in degraded areas.
