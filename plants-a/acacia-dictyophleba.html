Acacia dictyophleba

Acacia dictyophleba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spindly growth habit and small, feathery leaves that are typically arranged in pairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. Acacia dictyophleba is adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
