Adnaria fasciculata

Adnaria fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often arranged in clusters, while its flowers are small and yellow, forming dense inflorescences.

Adnaria fasciculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in coastal gardens.
