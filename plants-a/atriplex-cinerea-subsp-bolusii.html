Atriplex cinerea subsp. bolusii

Atriplex cinerea subsp.

bolusii, commonly known as silverleaf shadscale, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico. It is characterized by its gray-green, succulent leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.

Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it an important component of xerophytic ecosystems.
