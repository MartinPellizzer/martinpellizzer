Apluda mutica subsp. aristata

Apluda mutica subsp.

aristata, commonly known as the narrowleaf grassvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

The subspecies aristata is distinguished by its narrower leaves and more compact growth habit compared to other subspecies of Apluda mutica.
