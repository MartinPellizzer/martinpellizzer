Astragalus coltonii

Astragalus coltonii, commonly known as the Colton's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It has small, compound leaves and produces pods that contain seeds.

Astragalus coltonii is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.
