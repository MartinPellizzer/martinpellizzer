Arum ptychiurum

Arum ptychiurum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf arum, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are usually narrow and pointed, giving it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color.

Arum ptychiurum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in moist, shaded environments.
