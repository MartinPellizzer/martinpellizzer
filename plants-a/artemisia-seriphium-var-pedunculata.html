Artemisia seriphium var. pedunculata

Artemisia seriphium var. pedunculata, commonly known as the slender tarragon, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features gray-green, aromatic foliage with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or alpine gardens.

Its essential oils contribute to its use in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
