Anthyllis sericea

Anthyllis sericea, commonly known as silky anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats like grasslands and scrublands.

Anthyllis sericea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
