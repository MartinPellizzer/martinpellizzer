Aretia helvetica var. multiflora

Aretia helvetica var. multiflora, commonly known as the Swiss bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm, featuring upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of blue or purple flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
