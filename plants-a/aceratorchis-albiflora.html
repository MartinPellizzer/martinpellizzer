Aceratorchis albiflora

Aceratorchis albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The leaves are lanceolate and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude meadows.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
