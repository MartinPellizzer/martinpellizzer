Astragalus spinosus f. palaestinus

Astragalus spinosus f. palaestinus, commonly known as the Palestine thornvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is adapted to withstand drought and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
