Amomum sumatranum

Amomum sumatranum, commonly known as Java cardamom, is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, finger-like rhizomes. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries containing aromatic seeds. These seeds are widely used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its economic and therapeutic value.
