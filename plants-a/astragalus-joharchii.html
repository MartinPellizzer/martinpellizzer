Astragalus joharchii

Astragalus joharchii, commonly known as the Afghan milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia, including Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils and withstand drought.

Astragalus joharchii is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
