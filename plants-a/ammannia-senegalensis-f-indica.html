Ammannia senegalensis f. indica

Ammannia senegalensis f. indica, commonly known as the Indian bladdernut, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, inflated seed pods that resemble bladders, which are a key feature in its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
