Astragalus visunicus

Astragalus visunicus, commonly known as the Visunica milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, with multiple pairs of leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus visunicus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
