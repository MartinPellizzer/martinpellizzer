Astragalus ventorum

Astragalus ventorum, commonly known as the Sierra foothill milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.
