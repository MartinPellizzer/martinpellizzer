Amomum tridentatum

Amomum tridentatum, commonly known as the three-lobed cardamom, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, finger-like rhizomes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are elongated, lance-shaped, and have a distinct, spicy fragrance.

The rhizomes are harvested for their essential oils and are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.
