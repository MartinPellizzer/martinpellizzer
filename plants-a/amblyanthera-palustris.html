Amblyanthera palustris

Amblyanthera palustris, commonly known as the marsh anther plant, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in South America.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often found near water bodies. The plant has slender stems and heart-shaped leaves that are adapted to thrive in moist or aquatic environments. Its reproductive structures are specialized for pollination by insects, contributing to its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.

Amblyanthera palustris is also valued for its ornamental appeal in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
