Albuca cremnophila

Albuca cremnophila, commonly known as the rock albuca, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, strap-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white to yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it withstands dry conditions.

Albuca cremnophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and blooms.
