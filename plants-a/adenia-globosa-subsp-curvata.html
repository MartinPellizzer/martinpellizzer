Adenia globosa subsp. curvata

Adenia globosa subsp.

curvata, commonly known as the zigzagged adenium, is a strikingly ornamental shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its thick, woody stems that often exhibit a zigzag or twisted growth pattern, giving it a unique appearance.

The plant produces clusters of small, white, tubular flowers that are typically followed by bright orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, dry climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and resilience.

Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough, hairy texture.
