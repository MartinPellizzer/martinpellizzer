Aalius macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered aalius, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in summer. Its foliage consists of long, strap-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

Aalius macrantha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate dry conditions.
