Arabis vesula

Arabis vesula, commonly known as the common rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or coastal areas. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Arabis vesula produces yellow or white flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
