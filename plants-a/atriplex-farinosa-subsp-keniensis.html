Atriplex farinosa subsp. keniensis

Atriplex farinosa subsp.

keniensis, commonly known as Kenyan saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of East Africa. It is characterized by its succulent, gray-green leaves that are adapted to conserve water in dry environments.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is often found growing on rocky soils and in saline areas, demonstrating its tolerance to harsh conditions.

This species plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native habitat.
