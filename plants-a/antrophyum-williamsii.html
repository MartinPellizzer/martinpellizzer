Antrophyum williamsii

Antrophyum williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' antrophyum, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a tall, slender palm characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and smooth, dark green foliage. The plant produces large, spadix-like inflorescences that are often surrounded by brightly colored bracts. Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.

However, it requires well-drained soil and partial shade to thrive in cultivation.
