Acinodendron mucronatum

Acinodendron mucronatum, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by green, fleshy capsules containing toxic seeds. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its milky sap, it is also referred to as the milk tree.
