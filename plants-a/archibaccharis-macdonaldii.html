Archibaccharis macdonaldii

Archibaccharis macdonaldii, commonly known as the Macdonald's holly, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy green surface.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to provide dense cover for wildlife.
