Astragalus amunateguianus

Astragalus amunateguianus, commonly known as the Amunateguian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to dry environments, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
