Acacia mildbraedii

Acacia mildbraedii, commonly known as the Karoo acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

Its wood is hard and has been historically used for making tools and fencing.
