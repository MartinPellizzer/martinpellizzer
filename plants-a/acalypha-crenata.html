Acalypha crenata

Acalypha crenata, commonly known as the serrated glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of clusters of small, pink or red flowers surrounded by bracts. The leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
