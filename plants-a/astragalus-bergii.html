Astragalus bergii

Astragalus bergii, commonly known as the Berg's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale pink flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, where it thrives in harsh, arid conditions. Astragalus bergii is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.

Its ecological importance makes it a valuable species in alpine ecosystems.
