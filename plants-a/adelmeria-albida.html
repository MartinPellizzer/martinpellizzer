Adelmeria albida

Adelmeria albida, commonly known as the white-flowered manzanita, is a shrub native to California and southern Oregon.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of white, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Adelmeria albida is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
