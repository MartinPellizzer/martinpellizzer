Aconitum tsukubense

Aconitum tsukubense, commonly known as Japanese monkshood, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple hooded flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with deep lobes and is often found in moist, shaded woodland areas. It contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

Despite its toxicity, it is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal.
