Astragalus rattanii

Astragalus rattanii, commonly known as the rattan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb with slender stems and small, compound leaves. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish, arranged in racemes, and it produces distinctive pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus rattanii is adapted to harsh environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
