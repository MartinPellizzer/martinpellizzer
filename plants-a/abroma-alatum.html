Abroma alatum

Abroma alatum, commonly known as the winged bean or angel's trumpets, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins that give it a wing-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. Its fruit is a long, pod-like structure that contains edible seeds and a starchy, tuberous root.

Abroma alatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in various cultures for food and medicine.
