Alternanthera ficoidea var. radicans

Alternanthera ficoidea var. radicans, commonly known as the red-veined beggar's lice, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that often display vibrant red veins against a deep green or purple background. This variety is known for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soil. It prefers partial to full sunlight and is commonly cultivated in water gardens or as a bog plant.

The plant's unique foliage makes it a popular choice for aquatic and semi-aquatic displays.
