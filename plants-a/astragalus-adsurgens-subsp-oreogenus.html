Astragalus adsurgens subsp. oreogenus

Astragalus adsurgens subsp.

oreogenus, commonly known as the mountain milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its erect stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils. Astragalus adsurgens subsp.

oreogenus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
