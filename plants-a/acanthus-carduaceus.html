Acanthus carduaceus

Acanthus carduaceus, commonly known as the thistle acanthus, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features spiny leaves with a silvery-blue underside, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its architectural foliage and long-lasting blooms.
