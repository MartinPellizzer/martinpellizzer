Anthocercis viscosa var. baueriana

Anthocercis viscosa var. baueriana, commonly known as the sticky anthocercis, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its viscid, sticky leaves that produce a thick, resinous substance, which serves as a natural defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical or subtropical forests. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from green to reddish hues.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a botanical curiosity and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
