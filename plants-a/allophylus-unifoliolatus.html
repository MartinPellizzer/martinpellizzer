Allophylus unifoliolatus

Allophylus unifoliolatus, commonly known as the single leaf alder, is a small evergreen shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, consisting of a single leaf-like structure at the end of each branch. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Allophylus unifoliolatus is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
