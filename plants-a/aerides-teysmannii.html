Aerides teysmannii

Aerides teysmannii, commonly known as the Teymann's orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its upright, hairy pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Aerides teysmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
