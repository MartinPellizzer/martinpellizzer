Ambora fruticosa

Ambora fruticosa, commonly known as the silverleaf shrub, is a deciduous plant native to the temperate regions of the northeastern United States.

It features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, which are clustered in terminal panicles. Its root system is fibrous and moderately deep, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Ambora fruticosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
