Adenopus pynaertii

Adenopus pynaertii, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, which are characteristic of the daisy family. Its leaves are usually opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Adenopus pynaertii is known for its milky sap, which is often used in traditional medicine.
