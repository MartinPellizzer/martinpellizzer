Anogra albicaulis

Anogra albicaulis, commonly known as the white-stemmed anogra, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, pale green stems that are covered in a fine, waxy layer. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to thrive in arid environments.

Anogra albicaulis is of interest to botanists due to its distinct morphology and limited geographic distribution.
