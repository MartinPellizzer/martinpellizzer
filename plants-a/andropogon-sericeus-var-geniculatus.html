Andropogon sericeus var. geniculatus

Andropogon sericeus var. geniculatus, commonly known as the silky grass or silver bluestem, is a variant of the tallgrass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray leaves and compact, cylindrical inflorescences that produce small, fluffy seeds. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grassy areas, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
