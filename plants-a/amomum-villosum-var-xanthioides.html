Amomum villosum var. xanthioides

Amomum villosum var. xanthioides, commonly known as the golden spike ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and distinctive yellowish flowers. The plant belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and related species. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The varietal name "xanthioides" refers to its resemblance to the yellow-flowered species of the genus Amomum.
