Acalypha tubuaiensis

Acalypha tubuaiensis, commonly known as the Island Caper, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and disturbed soils. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive yellowish or reddish coloration. Its small, clustered flowers are usually greenish-white and lack showy petals.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
