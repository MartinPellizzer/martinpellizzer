Acacia galpinii

Acacia galpinii, commonly known as the Galpin's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched structure and phyllodes that are narrow and linear. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Acacia galpinii is valued for its ecological role and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
