Amelanchier amelanchier var. dalmatica

Amelanchier amelanchier var. dalmatica, commonly known as the Dalmatian serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its oval to elliptic leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen to a dark purple or black color in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable choice for ornamental and native plant gardens.

The Dalmatian serviceberry is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
