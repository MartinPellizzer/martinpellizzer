Anemanthele lessoniana

Anemanthele lessoniana, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface.

Anemanthele lessoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
