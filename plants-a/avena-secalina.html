Avena secalina

Avena secalina, commonly known as barley grass, is a member of the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit.

It is a perennial plant that thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny grains. Its leaves are rich in chlorophyll, making it a popular choice for dietary supplements and health foods.

Avena secalina is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
