Agathis philippensis

Agathis philippensis, commonly known as the Philippine agarwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Araucariaceae and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and broad, feather-like leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers and has a unique ability to produce aromatic resin under certain conditions. Its wood is highly valued for its fragrant properties and is used in traditional medicine and incense production.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, supporting diverse flora and fauna.
