Anonis minutissima

Anonis minutissima, commonly known as the minute anona, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Anonaceae, which includes plants known for their aromatic and often medicinal properties. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically white or greenish-white, and are accompanied by a distinctive, fleshy calyx.

The fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to yellow or red when mature, and it contains several small, hard seeds.
