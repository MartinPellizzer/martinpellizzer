Astragalus angelinus

Astragalus angelinus, commonly known as angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus angelinus is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
