Alstroemeria gardneri

Alstroemeria gardneri, commonly known as the Chilean lily, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern.

Alstroemeria gardneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and long-lasting flowers.
