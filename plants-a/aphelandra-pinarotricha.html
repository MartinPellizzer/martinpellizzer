Aphelandra pinarotricha

Aphelandra pinarotricha, commonly known as the Pinarotricha torch ginger, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its striking inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are large, ovate, and glossy, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The flowers, which bloom in summer, are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as beetles and flies.
