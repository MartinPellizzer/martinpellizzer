Aristea

Aristea, commonly known as the star grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and small, star-shaped flowers. The plants typically have narrow, grass-like leaves and produce showy inflorescences that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, or red. Aristea species are often used in rock gardens and as ornamental plants due to their compact size and vibrant floral display.

They are drought-tolerant and thrive in well-drained soils, making them suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.
