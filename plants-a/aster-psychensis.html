Aster psychensis

Aster psychensis, commonly known as the sacred aster, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant is known for its striking violet-blue petals and is often associated with spiritual and ceremonial practices by indigenous communities. Its botanical features include hairy stems, lance-shaped leaves, and a short stature adapted to its harsh mountainous environment.

Aster psychensis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional healing practices.
