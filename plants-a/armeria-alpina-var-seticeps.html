Armeria alpina var. seticeps

Armeria alpina var. seticeps, commonly known as the Alpine thrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners interested in hardy, drought-tolerant plants.
