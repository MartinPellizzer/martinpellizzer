Amaracarpus acuminatus

Amaracarpus acuminatus, commonly known as the slender-leafed amaracarpus, is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features trifoliate leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in slender, hairy pods, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

Amaracarpus acuminatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
