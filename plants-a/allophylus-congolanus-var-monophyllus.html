Allophylus congolanus var. monophyllus

Allophylus congolanus var. monophyllus, commonly known as the single-leaf alderleaf, is a species native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds.

This plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in moist, shaded areas within forest understories.
