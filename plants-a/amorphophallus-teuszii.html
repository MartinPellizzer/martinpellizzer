Amorphophallus teuszii

Amorphophallus teuszii, commonly known as the giant water chestnut, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and a distinctive, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant is adapted to wetland environments and thrives in marshy or aquatic habitats. Its underground tubers are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.

The species is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands in its native range.
