Amorpha canescens f. glabrata

Amorpha canescens f. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth false indigo, is a variant of the false indigo plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pea-like, violet-blue flowers in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen make it a useful plant in ecological restoration projects.
