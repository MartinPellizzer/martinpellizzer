Attalea maripa

Attalea maripa, commonly known as the palmate palm or maripa palm, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to the Amazon Basin and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that develop into clustered, fibrous fruits. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts, and the palm is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.

The plant is adapted to wetland environments and plays an important part in the biodiversity of its habitat.
