Alchemilla palmatifida var. minor

Alchemilla palmatifida var. minor, commonly known as the lesser lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves that form a dense mat. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
