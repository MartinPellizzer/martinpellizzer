Achillea setacea subsp. dolopica

Achillea setacea subsp.

dolopica, commonly known as the Dolopica yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves and flat, composite flower heads that range from white to pale pink.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is relatively low maintenance and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
