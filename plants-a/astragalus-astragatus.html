Astragalus astragatus

Astragalus astragatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus astragatus is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
