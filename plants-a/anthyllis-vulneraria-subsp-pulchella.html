Anthyllis vulneraria subsp. pulchella

Anthyllis vulneraria subsp.

pulchella, commonly known as the pretty everlasting pea, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. It is characterized by its distinctive, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as coastal dunes or dry grasslands. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This subspecies is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments.
