Arabis crucisetosa

Arabis crucisetosa, commonly known as the crosswort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbages and mustards. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 cm and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Arabis crucisetosa is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
