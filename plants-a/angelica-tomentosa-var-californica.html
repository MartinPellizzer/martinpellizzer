Angelica tomentosa var. californica

Angelica tomentosa var. californica, commonly known as the California angelica, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features large, compound leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its root system is thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is an important part of the local ecosystem.
