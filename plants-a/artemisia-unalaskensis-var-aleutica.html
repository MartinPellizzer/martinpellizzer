Artemisia unalaskensis var. aleutica

Artemisia unalaskensis var. aleutica, commonly known as the Aleutian wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Aleutian Islands and surrounding regions of Alaska.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and slender, branched stems. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and thrives in coastal and alpine environments. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in late summer, and it is often used in traditional herbal practices.

This variety is closely related to other Artemisia species and is adapted to harsh, northern climates.
