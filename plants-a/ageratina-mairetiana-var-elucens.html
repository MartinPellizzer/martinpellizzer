Ageratina mairetiana var. elucens

Ageratina mairetiana var. elucens, commonly known as the white feverfew, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in damp, shaded areas and is often found in forest understories. It has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

The species is also noted for its potential medicinal properties.
