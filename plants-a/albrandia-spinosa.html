Albrandia spinosa

Albrandia spinosa, commonly known as the spiny albrandia, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems covered in sharp, spiny protrusions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
