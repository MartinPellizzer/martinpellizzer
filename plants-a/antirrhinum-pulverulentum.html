Antirrhinum pulverulentum

Antirrhinum pulverulentum, commonly known as the dust-laden snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, dust-like seeds, which are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Antirrhinum pulverulentum is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
