Aronia japonica

Aronia japonica, commonly known as Japanese chokeberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, dark red berries. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The flowers are white and appear in early spring, followed by the development of its distinctive berries.
