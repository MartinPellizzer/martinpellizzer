Abutilon listeri

Abutilon listeri, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, lantern-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its hairy stems and foliage give it a distinctive, velvety texture.

Abutilon listeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
