Astragalus kentrophyta subsp. jessiae

Astragalus kentrophyta subsp.

jessiae, commonly known as Jessiae milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of western North America. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, harsh environments. It is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor conditions.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
