Apium gussonei

Apium gussonei, commonly known as the Sicilian celery or Sicilian cow parsley, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like carrots and parsley. The plant features slender, hollow stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its small white flowers are clustered in umbels, a characteristic feature of the Apiaceae family.

Apium gussonei is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
