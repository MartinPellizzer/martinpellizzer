Athyrium marginatum

Athyrium marginatum, commonly known as the serrated lady fern, is a deciduous fern native to eastern Asia.

It features delicate, bipinnate fronds with finely serrated margins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense layer of brownish, hairy growth.

Athyrium marginatum is valued for its ornamental foliage and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
