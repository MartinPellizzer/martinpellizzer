Arawakia

Arawakia, commonly known as the "sacred tree" in the Caribbean, is a tropical plant native to the island of Hispaniola.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces a distinctive fruit with a thick, leathery rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp. Arawakia is often associated with spiritual and medicinal properties in local traditions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and culturally revered.
