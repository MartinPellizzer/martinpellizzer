Ageratina mygindifolia

Ageratina mygindifolia, commonly known as the white mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, which give it a mist-like appearance.

Ageratina mygindifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
