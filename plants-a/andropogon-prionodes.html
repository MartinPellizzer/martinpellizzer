Andropogon prionodes

Andropogon prionodes, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a tall grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland areas and along the edges of marshes, where it thrives in moist to saturated soils. The plant has narrow, blade-like leaves that are often blue-green in color, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Andropogon prionodes plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
