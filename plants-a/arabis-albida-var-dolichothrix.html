Arabis albida var. dolichothrix

Arabis albida var. dolichothrix, commonly known as the long-stemmed white rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many species of mustard and cress. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This variety is particularly adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant in alpine environments.
