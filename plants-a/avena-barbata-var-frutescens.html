Avena barbata var. frutescens

Avena barbata var. frutescens, commonly known as the red oat grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces awned lemmas, which are bristle-like structures that aid in seed dispersal. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and it is adapted to thrive in arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils in dry landscapes.
