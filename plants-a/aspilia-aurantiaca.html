Aspilia aurantiaca

Aspilia aurantiaca, commonly known as the red torch lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red, torch-like inflorescence composed of numerous small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Aspilia aurantiaca is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
