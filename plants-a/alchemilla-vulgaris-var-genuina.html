Alchemilla vulgaris var. genuina

Alchemilla vulgaris var. genuina, commonly known as the common lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and ability to attract pollinators.
