Astragalus serenoi var. sordescens

Astragalus serenoi var. sordescens, commonly known as the darkleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is distinguished by its darker, often purplish or brownish foliage, which gives it its common name.
