Aira capillaris subsp. ambigua

Aira capillaris subsp.

ambigua, commonly known as the narrowleaf goatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States and parts of Canada. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, disturbed soils, and rocky areas.

The plant has slender, hairless stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

This subspecies is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
