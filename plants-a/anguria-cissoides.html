Anguria cissoides

Anguria cissoides, commonly known as the creeping cucumber, is a vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like squash and melons. The plant produces small, green, cucumber-like fruits that are typically harvested when immature. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are trailing and often climb using tendrils.

Anguria cissoides is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
