Arthrostylidium maculatum

Arthrostylidium maculatum, commonly known as the spotted marsh grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments. The plant features slender, erect stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences and are usually pale green or white in color.

Arthrostylidium maculatum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
