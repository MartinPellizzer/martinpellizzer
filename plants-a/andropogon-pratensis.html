Andropogon pratensis

Andropogon pratensis, commonly known as tallgrass bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. It plays an important role in maintaining soil stability and supporting biodiversity in native grassland ecosystems.

Andropogon pratensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
