Andropogon leucostachyus subvar. subvillosus

Andropogon leucostachyus subvar. subvillosus, commonly known as silky grass or silky bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green blades and tufted inflorescence that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and disturbed sites, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are covered in a fine layer of hair, contributing to its silvery sheen.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
