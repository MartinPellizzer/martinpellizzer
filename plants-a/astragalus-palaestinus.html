Astragalus palaestinus

Astragalus palaestinus, commonly known as the Palestine milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating a high tolerance for drought conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus palaestinus is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to survive in regions with limited water availability.
