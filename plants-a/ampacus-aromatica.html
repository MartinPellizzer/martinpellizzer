Ampacus aromatica

Ampacus aromatica, commonly known as the aromatic ampac, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, small white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet, spicy scent. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture, and it has a compact growth habit.

Ampacus aromatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique fragrance and visual interest.
