Agathophyllum aromaticum

Agathophyllum aromaticum, commonly known as the sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic properties. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and silvery-green, often covered in fine hairs that contribute to its aromatic scent.

Agathophyllum aromaticum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal remedies.
