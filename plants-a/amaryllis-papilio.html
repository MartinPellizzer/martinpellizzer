Amaryllis papilio

Amaryllis papilio, commonly known as the pink amaryllis, is a striking flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces broad, strap-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Amaryllis papilio is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is popular in gardens and as a houseplant.
