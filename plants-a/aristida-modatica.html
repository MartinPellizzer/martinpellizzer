Aristida modatica

Aristida modatica, commonly known as slender threeawn, is a grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in disturbed or open habitats. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

Aristida modatica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native range.
