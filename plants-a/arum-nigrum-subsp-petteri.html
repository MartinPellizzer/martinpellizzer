Arum nigrum subsp. petteri

Arum nigrum subsp.

petteri, commonly known as the black arum, is a species of flowering plant in the Araceae family. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia.

The plant is characterized by its large, dark green, heart-shaped leaves and a distinctive, spadix-shaped inflorescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral structure.
