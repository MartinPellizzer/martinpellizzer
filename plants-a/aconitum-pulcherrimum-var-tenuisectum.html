Aconitum pulcherrimum var. tenuisectum

Aconitum pulcherrimum var. tenuisectum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a spires, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are finely divided, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
