Acetosella setosa

Acetosella setosa, commonly known as mouse ear chickweed, is a small, low-growing plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of tiny, spoon-shaped leaves that resemble mouse ears, hence its common name. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas.

Acetosella setosa is often found in temperate regions and is known for its hardy, resilient nature.
