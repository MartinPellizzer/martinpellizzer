Amaryllis josephinae

Amaryllis josephinae, commonly known as the Josephina amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 inches in diameter, typically in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces strap-like leaves that are broad and waxy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a period of dormancy to bloom successfully.

Amaryllis josephinae is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and is a popular choice for gardens and indoor settings.
