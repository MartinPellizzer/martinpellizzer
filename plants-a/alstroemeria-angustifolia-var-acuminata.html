Alstroemeria angustifolia var. acuminata

Alstroemeria angustifolia var. acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved alstroemeria, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and clusters of vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.

The variety acuminata is distinguished by its pointed leaf tips and compact growth habit.
