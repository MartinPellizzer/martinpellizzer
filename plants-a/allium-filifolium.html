Allium filifolium

Allium filifolium, commonly known as the slender leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, grass-like leaves that are narrow and linear, often with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces umbel-like clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers in the spring. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, garlic-like flavor, making it a useful culinary plant.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to onions and garlic.
