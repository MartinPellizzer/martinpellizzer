Astragalus baharensis

Astragalus baharensis, commonly known as the Bahrein milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and heat. Its leaves are compound, with small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus baharensis plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is of interest for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
