Arum rapiforme

Arum rapiforme, commonly known as the arrowleaf arum, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a showy, colorful spathe, which is typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
