Areca costata

Areca costata, commonly known as the zigzag areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed trunk and long, pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that eventually develop into edible, red or black fruits. Areca costata thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
