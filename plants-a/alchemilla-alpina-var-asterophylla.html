Alchemilla alpina var. asterophylla

Alchemilla alpina var. asterophylla, commonly known as the alpine lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that provides insulation against cold temperatures. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
