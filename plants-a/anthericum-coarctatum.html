Anthericum coarctatum

Anthericum coarctatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ladies' tresses, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the orchid family (Orchidaceae).

It is characterized by its dense, dark green, narrow leaves and its long, cylindrical inflorescence that bears numerous small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Anthericum coarctatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
