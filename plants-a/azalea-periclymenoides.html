Azalea periclymenoides

Azalea periclymenoides, commonly known as the sweet azalea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with leathery, elliptical leaves. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Azalea periclymenoides blooms in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to woodland gardens.
