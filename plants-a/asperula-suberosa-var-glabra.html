Asperula suberosa var. glabra

Asperula suberosa var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth evergreen azalea, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery, dark green foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is evergreen, retaining its leaves throughout the year, and thrives in acidic, well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Asperula suberosa var. glabra is relatively low maintenance and can tolerate partial shade to full sun.
