Antidesma venosum subsp. membranaceum

Antidesma venosum subsp.

membranaceum, commonly known as the membranous antidesma, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often membranous in texture, giving it its subspecies name.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
