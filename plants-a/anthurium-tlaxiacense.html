Anthurium tlaxiacense

Anthurium tlaxiacense, commonly known as the Tlaxiaca arrowhead, is a rare and striking plant native to the tropical regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spatula-shaped leaf called a spathe. The plant features broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green with a waxy texture. Its flowers are typically green or yellow, and it produces small, edible berries that are sometimes consumed by local communities.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
