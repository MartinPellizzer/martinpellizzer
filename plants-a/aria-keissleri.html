Aria keissleri

Aria keissleri, commonly known as the Keissler's aster, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically has narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of purple and pink. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with a deep root system that helps it access water from deeper soil layers.

Aria keissleri is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in xeriscaping.
