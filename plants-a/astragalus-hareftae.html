Astragalus hareftae

Astragalus hareftae, commonly known as the Iranian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to survive in harsh, semi-arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its biological features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native range.
