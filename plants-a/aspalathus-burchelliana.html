Aspalathus burchelliana

Aspalathus burchelliana, commonly known as the Cape Flats dandelion, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant is adapted to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. It produces small, round seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its spread across the landscape.

Aspalathus burchelliana is also notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional African medicine.
