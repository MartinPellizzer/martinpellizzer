Albizia altissima

Albizia altissima, commonly known as the giant silk tree, is a deciduous legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often trifoliate or pinnate, and its showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces elongated pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Albizia altissima has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.
