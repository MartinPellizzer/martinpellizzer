Arundinaria hosidaikitiana

Arundinaria hosidaikitiana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

This species is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
