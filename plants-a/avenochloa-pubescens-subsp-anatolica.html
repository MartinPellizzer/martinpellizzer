Avenochloa pubescens subsp. anatolica

Avenochloa pubescens subsp.

anatolica, commonly known as Anatolian pubescent grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and disturbed areas.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a dense covering of short, stiff hairs, giving it a pubescent appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is often clustered at the base of the stem.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and adaptability to arid conditions.
