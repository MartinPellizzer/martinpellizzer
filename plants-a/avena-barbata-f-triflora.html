Avena barbata f. triflora

Avena barbata f. triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered oat, is a variant of the common oat species.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features slender, erect stems that bear small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

The flowers are typically greenish-yellow and produce three to four spikelets, giving it its distinguishing name.
