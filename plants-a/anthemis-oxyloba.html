Anthemis oxyloba

Anthemis oxyloba, commonly known as the rock everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features linear, gray-green leaves with a hairy texture, adapted to thrive in arid and rocky environments. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it withstands harsh conditions.

The flowers are known to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
