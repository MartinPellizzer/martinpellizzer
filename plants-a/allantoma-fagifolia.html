Allantoma fagifolia

Allantoma fagifolia, commonly known as the European allantoid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in loose inflorescences, usually in shades of white or pale green.

Allantoma fagifolia is characterized by its unique, inflated fruit capsules that split open to release its seeds.
