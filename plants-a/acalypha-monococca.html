Acalypha monococca

Acalypha monococca, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silverleaf acalypha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage that ranges from silver to deep green, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal.

Acalypha monococca is also known for its ability to tolerate a variety of soil types and light conditions, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
