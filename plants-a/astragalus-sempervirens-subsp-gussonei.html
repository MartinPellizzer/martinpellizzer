Astragalus sempervirens subsp. gussonei

Astragalus sempervirens subsp.

gussonei, commonly known as the evergreen milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with leathery, lance-shaped leaves that remain green throughout the year.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to contribute to soil stabilization in arid environments.
