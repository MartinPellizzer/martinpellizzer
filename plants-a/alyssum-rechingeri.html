Alyssum rechingeri

Alyssum rechingeri, commonly known as the Kashmir alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Alyssum rechingeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
