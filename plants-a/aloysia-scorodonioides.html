Aloysia scorodonioides

Aloysia scorodonioides, commonly known as Cape vermouth, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the verbena family (Verbenaceae) and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers are usually pale blue or violet in color.

Aloysia scorodonioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
