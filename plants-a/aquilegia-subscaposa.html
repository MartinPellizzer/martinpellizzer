Aquilegia subscaposa

Aquilegia subscaposa, commonly known as the scapose columbine, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a hairy texture. Its stems are upright and often have a scapose (leafless) appearance, giving it its common name.

Aquilegia subscaposa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
