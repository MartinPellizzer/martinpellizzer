Ambrosia bipinnatifida

Ambrosia bipinnatifida, commonly known as the Southern Ambrosia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragweed, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and feathery leaves. The plant is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides and in open fields.

Its pollen is a significant allergen, contributing to seasonal allergies in some individuals.
