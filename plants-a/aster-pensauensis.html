Aster pensauensis

Aster pensauensis, commonly known as the Peninsular Aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from white to pale purple.

Aster pensauensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
