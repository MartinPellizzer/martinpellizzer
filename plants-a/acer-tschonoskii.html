Acer tschonoskii

Acer tschonoskii, commonly known as the Tschonoskii maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters and is characterized by its vibrant autumn foliage, which ranges from yellow to orange-red. The tree has palmately lobed leaves that are usually 5–10 centimeters in length, with a serrated margin. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The bark is smooth and greyish-brown, becoming fissured with age, and the tree is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
