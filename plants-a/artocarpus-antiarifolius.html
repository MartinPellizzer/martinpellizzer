Artocarpus antiarifolius

Artocarpus antiarifolius, commonly known as the golden fig or the sweet fig, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The tree produces small, clustered flowers followed by fig-like fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. The fruit is edible, with a sweet, honey-like flavor, and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for shade and beauty in gardens.
