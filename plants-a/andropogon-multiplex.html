Andropogon multiplex

Andropogon multiplex, commonly known as inland sea oats, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery, silver-green foliage that resembles the plumes of a sea horse. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Andropogon multiplex is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
