Aster fendleri

Aster fendleri, commonly known as the desert aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and desert scrublands. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and has a deep root system for water absorption.

Aster fendleri plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is considered an important species in its native ecosystem.
