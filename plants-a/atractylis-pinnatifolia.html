Atractylis pinnatifolia

Atractylis pinnatifolia, commonly known as the slender cow-wheat, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are pinnately divided, giving it a distinctive appearance, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer.

Atractylis pinnatifolia is often found in grasslands and along coastal areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
