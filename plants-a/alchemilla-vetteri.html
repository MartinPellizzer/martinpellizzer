Alchemilla vetteri

Alchemilla vetteri, commonly known as the Vetter's lady's mantle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer, typically arranged in clusters atop the stems. Native to Europe, it thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage is often used in herbal medicine for its supposed soothing and healing properties.
