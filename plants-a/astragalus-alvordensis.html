Astragalus alvordensis

Astragalus alvordensis, commonly known as Alvord milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States, particularly found in arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to low moisture conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
