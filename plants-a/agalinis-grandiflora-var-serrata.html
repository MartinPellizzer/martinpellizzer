Agalinis grandiflora var. serrata

Agalinis grandiflora var. serrata, commonly known as the serrated blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and have serrated edges, giving the plant its specific variety name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

The plant is an important nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
