Ajax lorifolius

Ajax lorifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ajax, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and striking, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its flowers, which are usually red or orange, bloom in clusters and attract various pollinators such as bees and birds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
