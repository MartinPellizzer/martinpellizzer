Amyris multijuga

Amyris multijuga, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and can grow up to 6 feet tall, making it a striking addition to gardens. Its aromatic properties make it popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and pleasant scent.

Amyris multijuga is also valued for its potential medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
