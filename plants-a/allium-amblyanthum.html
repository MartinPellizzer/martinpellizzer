Allium amblyanthum

Allium amblyanthum, commonly known as the Persian leek or garlic chives, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives, and is known for its pungent aroma and flavor. Its bulbs are edible and often used in traditional cuisines.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
