Adenophora pereskiifolia var. hirsuta

Adenophora pereskiifolia var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy adenophora, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features slender, hairy stems that often grow in clusters, with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its flowers are borne on long pedicels and are visited by various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
