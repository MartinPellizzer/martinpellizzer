Adonis sibirica

Adonis sibirica, commonly known as Siberian glory-of-the-snow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and Siberia.

It features a basal rosette of dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that bear clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Adonis sibirica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
