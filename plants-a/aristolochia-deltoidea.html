Aristolochia deltoidea

Aristolochia deltoidea, commonly known as the Delta arrowwood, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and unique, intricate flowers that resemble a bird's nest. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
