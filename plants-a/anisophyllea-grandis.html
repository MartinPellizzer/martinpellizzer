Anisophyllea grandis

Anisophyllea grandis, commonly known as the giant anisophyllea, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant exhibits a unique leaf morphology with two distinct types of leaves on the same plant, a feature known as anisophylly. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Anisophyllea grandis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
