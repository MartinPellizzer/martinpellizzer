Asclepias purpurea

Asclepias purpurea, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers. The plant has velvety green leaves that are opposite and ovate in shape. Its stems are hairy and often tinged with red or purple, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Asclepias purpurea is also an important host plant for monarch butterflies, supporting their life cycle.
