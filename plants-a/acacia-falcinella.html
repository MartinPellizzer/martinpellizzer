Acacia falcinella

Acacia falcinella, commonly known as the Socotra acacia, is a flowering plant native to the island of Socotra in the Arabian Sea.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery foliage and long, thin pods that contain seeds. The plant has a unique appearance with its thorned stems and palm-like leaves, which are adapted to the arid environment. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Acacia falcinella plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
