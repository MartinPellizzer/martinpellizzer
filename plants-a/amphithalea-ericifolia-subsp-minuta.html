Amphithalea ericifolia subsp. minuta

Amphithalea ericifolia subsp.

minuta, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, dense foliage and is typically found in sandy or rocky soils.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late winter to early spring. It is well adapted to coastal conditions, tolerating salt spray and strong winds.

This subspecies is often used in horticulture for its hardy nature and ornamental value.
