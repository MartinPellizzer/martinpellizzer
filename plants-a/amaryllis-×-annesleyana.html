Amaryllis × annesleyana

Amaryllis × annesleyana, commonly known as the Annesley's amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis paradisicola.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors including red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as a bulbous perennial, producing vibrant blooms during the summer months. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are broad and grass-like, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

This hybrid is prized for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements, making it a popular choice for horticultural enthusiasts.
