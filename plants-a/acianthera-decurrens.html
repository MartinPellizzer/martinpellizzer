Acianthera decurrens

Acianthera decurrens, commonly known as the ascending acianthera, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in a loose raceme. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and private collections.
