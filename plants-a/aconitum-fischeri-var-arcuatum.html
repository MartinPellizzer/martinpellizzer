Aconitum fischeri var. arcuatum

Aconitum fischeri var. arcuatum, commonly known as the Alpineaconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely leafy stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers in late summer, arranged in dense clusters at the top of the stem. Its leaves are compound, with palmate divisions, and the plant thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil.

Due to its toxicity, it is important to handle the plant with care.
