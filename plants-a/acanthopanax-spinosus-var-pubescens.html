Acanthopanax spinosus var. pubescens

Acanthopanax spinosus var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy devil's ivy, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and hairy leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small greenish-white flowers followed by red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and rocky areas.

Its adaptability makes it a common ornamental plant in horticulture.
