Anthyllis vulneraria proles hispidissima

Anthyllis vulneraria proles hispidissima, commonly known as the hispid mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves that give it its common name. This species is typically found in rocky or sandy soils in arid and semi-arid regions. It has a low-growing habit and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

The plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.
