Astragalus mediterraneus

Astragalus mediterraneus, commonly known as the Mediterranean milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry soils, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets that help reduce water loss.

Astragalus mediterraneus is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
