Anacamptis morio subsp. tlemcenensis

Anacamptis morio subsp.

tlemcenensis, commonly known as the Tlemcen orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species found primarily in the Maghreb region of North Africa. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pale yellow to greenish flowers with distinct markings.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is adapted to semi-arid or Mediterranean climates. Its morphology includes a short rhizome and a single, erect stem bearing several lance-shaped leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
