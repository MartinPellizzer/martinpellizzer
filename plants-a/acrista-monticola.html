Acrista monticola

Acrista monticola, commonly known as the mountain acrista, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh mountain climates. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of hair to reduce water loss.

Acrista monticola produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer.
