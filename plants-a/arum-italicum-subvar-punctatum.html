Arum italicum subvar. punctatum

Arum italicum subvar. punctatum, commonly known as the spotted arum, is a striking tropical plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple and green mottled pattern. The plant produces an ornamental, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a showy, white to pale pink spathe. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
