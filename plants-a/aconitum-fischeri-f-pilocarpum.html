Aconitum fischeri f. pilocarpum

Aconitum fischeri f. pilocarpum, commonly known as the Pilocarpum aconite, is a botanical variant of the aconite genus.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features showy, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically compound, with deeply lobed foliage that contributes to its ornamental value.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its striking appearance and hardy nature in cooler climates.
