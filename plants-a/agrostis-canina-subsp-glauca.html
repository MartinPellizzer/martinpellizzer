Agrostis canina subsp. glauca

Agrostis canina subsp.

glauca, commonly known as blue hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike that bears small, spikelet-like flowers. Agrostis canina subsp.

glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
