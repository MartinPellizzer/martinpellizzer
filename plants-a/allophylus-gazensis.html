Allophylus gazensis

Allophylus gazensis, commonly known as the African holly, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 5 meters and has simple, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red or orange berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
