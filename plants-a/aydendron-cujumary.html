Aydendron cujumary

Aydendron cujumary, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems that can climb and twine around supports. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically yellow or orange in color, which bloom throughout the warmer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Aydendron cujumary is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
