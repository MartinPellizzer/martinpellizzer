Acanthodium angustum

Acanthodium angustum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acanthodium, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
