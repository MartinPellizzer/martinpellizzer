Amelanchier vapincis

Amelanchier vapincis, commonly known as the Canadian serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that ripen in late summer, turning from green to red or yellow depending on the variety. Amelanchier vapincis is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and open forests, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and conservation efforts.
