Allium paniculatum var. macilentum

Allium paniculatum var. macilentum, commonly known as the slender garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers in summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its bulbous stem is typically shorter than the leaves, and it prefers well-drained soil in full sun.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and edible qualities.
