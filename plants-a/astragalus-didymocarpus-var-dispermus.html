Astragalus didymocarpus var. dispermus

Astragalus didymocarpus var. dispermus, commonly known as the two-seeded milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain two seeds, a feature that distinguishes this variety from others in the species. It is adapted to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its ecological importance and medicinal potential, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
