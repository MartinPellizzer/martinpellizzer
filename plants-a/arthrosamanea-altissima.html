Arthrosamanea altissima

Arthrosamanea altissima, commonly known as the giant palm, is a towering tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its massive, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant can grow up to 30 meters in height, making it one of the tallest palms in the world. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as thatching material.

The species is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
