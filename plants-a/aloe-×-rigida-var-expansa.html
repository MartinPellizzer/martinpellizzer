Aloe × rigida var. expansa

Aloe × rigida var. expansa, commonly known as the broad-leaf aloe, is a hybrid succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy, and often ribbed leaves that can grow quite wide, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with clusters of yellow or orange flowers, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine and for their soothing gel, which has various healing properties.
