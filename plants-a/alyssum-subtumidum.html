Alyssum subtumidum

Alyssum subtumidum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved alyssum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers.

Alyssum subtumidum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
