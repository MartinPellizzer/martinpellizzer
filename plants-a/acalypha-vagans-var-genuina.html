Acalypha vagans var. genuina

Acalypha vagans var. genuina, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage and small, clustered flowers that range from green to red or purple. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in disturbed soils. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
