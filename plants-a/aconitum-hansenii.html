Aconitum hansenii

Aconitum hansenii, commonly known as Hansen's monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple hooded flowers, which resemble a medieval monk's habit. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and grows to a height of approximately 1 to 2 meters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
