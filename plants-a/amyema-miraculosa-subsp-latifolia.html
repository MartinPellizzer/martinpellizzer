Amyema miraculosa subsp. latifolia

Amyema miraculosa subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to host trees through its haustoria. It is native to Australia and is typically found in eucalyptus forests.

The plant has broad, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It produces small, white or pale flowers followed by fleshy, red berries.

Its botanical features make it an important part of the ecosystem, supporting various bird species that consume its berries.
