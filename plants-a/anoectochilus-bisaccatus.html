Anoectochilus bisaccatus

Anoectochilus bisaccatus, commonly known as the two-lobed orchid, is a rare and highly sought-after epiphytic orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high air humidity and indirect light for optimal growth. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a popular subject for horticultural study and conservation efforts.

Anoectochilus bisaccatus is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in specialized orchid collections.
