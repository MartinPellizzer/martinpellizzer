Achyranthes acuminata aurea

Achyranthes acuminata aurea, commonly known as the golden knotweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with yellow or orange, giving it its distinctive appearance. Its small, clustered flowers are typically greenish-white in color and appear in summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
