Achillea chamaecyparissus

Achillea chamaecyparissus, commonly known as the yarrow fern, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its feathery, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a creeping rhizome system that allows it to spread and form dense mats.

Achillea chamaecyparissus is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
