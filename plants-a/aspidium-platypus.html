Aspidium platypus

Aspidium platypus, commonly known as the broad-leafed water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that are typically divided into lance-shaped segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in dense forests. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Aspidium platypus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in maintaining ecological balance in wetland habitats.
