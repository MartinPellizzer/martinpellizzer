Arceuthobium pini var. sichuanense

Arceuthobium pini var. sichuanense, commonly known as the Sichuan dwarf mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches to the stems and branches of pine trees.

It lacks true roots and instead absorbs nutrients and water through its haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and berries that are dispersed by wind or animals. It is native to regions of China, particularly in the Sichuan province, where it thrives in temperate forest ecosystems.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly impact the growth and health of its host trees.
