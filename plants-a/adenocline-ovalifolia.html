Adenocline ovalifolia

Adenocline ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf adenocline, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its growth habit is typically compact, with multiple stems emerging from the base.

Adenocline ovalifolia is drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid environments.
