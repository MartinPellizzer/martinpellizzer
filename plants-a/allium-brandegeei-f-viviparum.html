Allium brandegeei f. viviparum

Allium brandegeei f. viviparum, commonly known as the viviparous garlic, is a rare botanical variant of the allium genus.

It is characterized by its bulbous structure and grass-like leaves, typical of many allium species. This plant is notable for its unique reproductive method, producing bulbils that can grow into new plants without the need for seeds. Native to specific regions of the southwestern United States, it thrives in arid and rocky environments.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptation to harsh conditions and its role in the study of plant reproduction.
