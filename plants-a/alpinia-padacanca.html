Alpinia padacanca

Alpinia padacanca, commonly known as the Philippine ginger, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric. The plant features tall, slender stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in spike-like inflorescences.

Alpinia padacanca is valued for its aromatic rhizomes, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
