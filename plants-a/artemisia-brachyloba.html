Artemisia brachyloba

Artemisia brachyloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Artemisia brachyloba is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
