Acacia atomiphylla

Acacia atomiphylla, commonly known as the desert wattle, is a shrub native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellow flower spikes that bloom in spring. The plant has small, compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, helping to reduce water loss in dry conditions. It produces seed pods that are woody and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Acacia atomiphylla is well adapted to survive in harsh, low-nutrient soils and extreme temperatures.
