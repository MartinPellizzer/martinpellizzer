Allium chinense

Allium chinense, commonly known as Chinese leek, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its hollow, cylindrical leaves and clusters of small, white or purple flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and valuable in traditional Asian cuisine.
