Ailanthus vietnamensis

Ailanthus vietnamensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese tree of heaven, is a member of the Simaroubaceae family.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, reddish-brown fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, sticky coating. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and urban environments.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both ecologically significant and sometimes invasive.
