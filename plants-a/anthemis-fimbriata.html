Anthemis fimbriata

Anthemis fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in summer, and the leaves are covered in fine, hair-like structures.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ease of cultivation.
