Aster albescens var. pilosus

Aster albescens var. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy white aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its white to pale purple flowers and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
