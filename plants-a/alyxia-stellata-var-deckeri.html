Alyxia stellata var. deckeri

Alyxia stellata var. deckeri, commonly known as the Desert Starvine, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a trailing or climbing vine characterized by its small, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has small, elliptical leaves and is adapted to thrive in hot, dry conditions with minimal water requirements. Its botanical features include woody stems and a tendency to form dense mats, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
