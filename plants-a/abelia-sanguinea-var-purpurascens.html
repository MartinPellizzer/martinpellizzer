Abelia sanguinea var. purpurascens

Abelia sanguinea var. purpurascens, commonly known as the roseum abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features attractive, fragrant, tubular flowers that range from white to pink, often with a hint of red, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and turn reddish in the fall. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from both its foliage and its showy, long-lasting blooms.
