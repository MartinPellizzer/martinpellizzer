Atriplex polycarpa

Atriplex polycarpa, commonly known as the silverleaf chenopod, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which helps reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, green to reddish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine by indigenous peoples for their purported healing properties.

Atriplex polycarpa is well adapted to saline soils and can thrive in harsh, desert-like conditions.
