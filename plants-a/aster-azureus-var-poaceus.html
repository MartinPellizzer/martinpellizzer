Aster azureus var. poaceus

Aster azureus var. poaceus, commonly known as the blue aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet, with narrow, grass-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits in native gardens.
