Astragalus brauntonii var. lativexillum

Astragalus brauntonii var. lativexillum, commonly known as the Braunton's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, demonstrating a high degree of drought tolerance. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and limited distribution.
