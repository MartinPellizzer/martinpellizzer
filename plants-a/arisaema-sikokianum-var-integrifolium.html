Arisaema sikokianum var. integrifolium

Arisaema sikokianum var. integrifolium, commonly known as the Japanese crown imperial flower, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its striking, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a broad, waxy spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a smooth, undivided margin.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and unique floral structure.
