Aloe glauca var. humilior

Aloe glauca var. humilior, commonly known as the dwarf aloe or blue aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are typically shorter and more compact compared to other aloe species. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves for survival.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
