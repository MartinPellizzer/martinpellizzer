Astragalus shortianus var. cyaneus

Astragalus shortianus var. cyaneus, commonly known as the blue milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range from blue to purple in color. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and the pods are slender and hairy.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in native plant gardens.
