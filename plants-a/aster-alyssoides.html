Aster alyssoides

Aster alyssoides, commonly known as the alpine aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its small, daisy-like flowers are usually blue to purple in color, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Aster alyssoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to bloom in challenging mountainous habitats.
