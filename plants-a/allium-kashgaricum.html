Allium kashgaricum

Allium kashgaricum, commonly known as the Kashgar garlic or the Chinese leek, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of China, Kazakhstan, and Afghanistan. The plant features a bulbous stem covered in a fibrous sheath, with narrow, grass-like leaves that can grow up to 50 cm in length. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in a loose umbel, and bloom in the summer months.

This plant is valued for its culinary uses and has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
