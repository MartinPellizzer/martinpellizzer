Asteriscium fimbriatum

Asteriscium fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like inflorescence. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and purple. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Asteriscium fimbriatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
