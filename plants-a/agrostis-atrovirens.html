Agrostis atrovirens

Agrostis atrovirens, commonly known as blackgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

Agrostis atrovirens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
