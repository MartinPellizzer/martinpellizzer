Acacia decurrens f. pauciglandulosa

Acacia decurrens f. pauciglandulosa, commonly known as the sparse-glandular wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with a sparse arrangement of small glands. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It is adapted to dry environments and is often found in coastal regions.

The foliage and flowers make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental use.
