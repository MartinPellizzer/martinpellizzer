Alocasia indica var. typica

Alocasia indica var. typica, commonly known as the giant elephant's ear, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, featuring a deep green color with prominent veins. The plant thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining, rich soil to flourish. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Alocasia indica var. typica is also valued for its ability to purify indoor air, making it a popular choice for interior landscaping.
