Armeniaca armeniaca

Armeniaca armeniaca, commonly known as the peach tree, is a deciduous fruit tree native to Asia.

It produces sweet, fuzzy fruits that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. The plant has a taproot system and develops a broad, spreading canopy with alternate, ovate leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, appearing before the leaves in spring.

The fruit's development is influenced by climate, with optimal growth in temperate regions.
