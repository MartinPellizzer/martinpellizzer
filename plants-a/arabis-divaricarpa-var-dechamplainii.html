Arabis divaricarpa var. dechamplainii

Arabis divaricarpa var. dechamplainii, commonly known as the Dechamplain's rockcress, is a perennial herb native to the rocky and alpine regions of eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. Its flowers are usually arranged in racemes and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky crevices and along stream banks.
