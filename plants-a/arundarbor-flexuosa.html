Arundarbor flexuosa

Arundarbor flexuosa, commonly known as the wavyleaf willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by spherical, woody fruits that disperse seeds by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found along stream banks and in woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological stability and habitat diversity.
