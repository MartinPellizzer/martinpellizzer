Astragalus gombaultii

Astragalus gombaultii, commonly known as the Gombault's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus gombaultii plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
