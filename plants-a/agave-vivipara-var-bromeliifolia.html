Agave vivipara var. bromeliifolia

Agave vivipara var. bromeliifolia, commonly known as the bromeliad agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick, fleshy, and covered in a layer of white wax. The plant produces striking, sword-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 feet in length. It is a flowering plant that produces a tall flowering stalk with clusters of yellow-green flowers.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
