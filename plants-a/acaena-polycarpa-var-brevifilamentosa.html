Acaena polycarpa var. brevifilamentosa

Acaena polycarpa var. brevifilamentosa, commonly known as the short-filament bramble, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, persistent leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by distinctive burrs containing seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
