Andropogon provincialis subvar. pycnanthus

Andropogon provincialis subvar. pycnanthus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wet to mesic habitats such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often pale blue or purple in color. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist environments.
