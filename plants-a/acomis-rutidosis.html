Acomis rutidosis

Acomis rutidosis, commonly known as the roughleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are rough and hairy, giving it its common name, and it produces small, clustered flowers in the fall.

Acomis rutidosis is an important nectar source for pollinators and contributes to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
