Arabis media var. intermedia

Arabis media var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and have a distinctive wavy or serrated edge.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
