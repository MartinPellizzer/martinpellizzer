Asperula cynanchica subsp. oreophila

Asperula cynanchica subsp.

oreophila, commonly known as the alpine asperula, is a perennial evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

The plant features small, dark green leaves that are often densely packed along its stems. Its flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in clusters during the late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
