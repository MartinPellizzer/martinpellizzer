Artemisia kaschgarica

Artemisia kaschgarica, commonly known as the Karakoram wormwood, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid climates.

Due to its resilience, it is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant in harsh environments.
