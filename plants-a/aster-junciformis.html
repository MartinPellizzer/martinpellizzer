Aster junciformis

Aster junciformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered blue to purple flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Aster junciformis plays a role in supporting pollinators and is often found in native plant gardens.
