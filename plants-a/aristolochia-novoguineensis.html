Aristolochia novoguineensis

Aristolochia novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea arrow vine, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and its unique, intricate flowers that resemble a pipe or a horn. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings.
