Abutilon indicum var. populifolium

Abutilon indicum var. populifolium, commonly known as the Indian mallow or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

Its fibrous bark and seeds have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
