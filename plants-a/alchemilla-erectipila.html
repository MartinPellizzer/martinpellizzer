Alchemilla erectipila

Alchemilla erectipila, commonly known as upright lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features basal leaves that are round to heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.

Its common name reflects its historical association with alchemy and the belief in its healing properties.
