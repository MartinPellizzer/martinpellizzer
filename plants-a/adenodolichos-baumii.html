Adenodolichos baumii

Adenodolichos baumii, commonly known as the Baum's climbing bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its twining stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its pods are elongated and contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Adenodolichos baumii is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.
