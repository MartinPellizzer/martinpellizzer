Actaea spicata subsp. nigra

Actaea spicata subsp.

nigra, commonly known as black cohosh, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America. It features tall, unbranched stems that bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers.

The plant produces dark, glossy berries that are an important food source for birds. Its leaves are compound, with pinnately arranged leaflets that are often described as having a fern-like appearance.

It thrives in woodland habitats and is valued for its medicinal properties.
