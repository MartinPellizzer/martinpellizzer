Arctostaphylos subcordata var. confertiflora

Arctostaphylos subcordata var. confertiflora, commonly known as the thick-flowered manzanita, is a shrub native to California.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of California's native plant communities.
