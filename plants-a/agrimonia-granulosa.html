Agrimonia granulosa

Agrimonia granulosa, commonly known as rough agrimony, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant has serrated leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Agrimonia granulosa is often found in dry meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported astringent and anti-inflammatory properties.
