Astragalus michauxianus subsp. haussknechtianus

Astragalus michauxianus subsp.

haussknechtianus, commonly known as the Haussknecht's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western North America. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish to greenish pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
