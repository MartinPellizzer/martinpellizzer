Ardisia creaghii

Ardisia creaghii, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright purple berries in the fall, which are a striking feature and attract various wildlife. Ardisia creaghii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive foliage and berries.

It prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.
