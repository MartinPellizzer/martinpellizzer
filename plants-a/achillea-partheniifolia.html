Achillea partheniifolia

Achillea partheniifolia, commonly known as the fern-leaved yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as rocky soils and grasslands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic foliage.

Achillea partheniifolia is often used in herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
