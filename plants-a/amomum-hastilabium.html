Amomum hastilabium

Amomum hastilabium, commonly known as the Chinese ginger or wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features erect, branched stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in spike-like inflorescences.

The rhizomes of Amomum hastilabium are used in traditional medicine and are valued for their aromatic and medicinal properties.
