Astracantha acicularis

Astracantha acicularis, commonly known as the needlestem thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, bristly stems and leaves, which are covered in fine, needle-like hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands.

Astracantha acicularis is a perennial species that is well adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
