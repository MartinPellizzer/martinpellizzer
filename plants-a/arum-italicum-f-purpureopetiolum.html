Arum italicum f. purpureopetiolum

Arum italicum f. purpureopetiolum, commonly known as the purple-veined arum, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with deep purple veins.

The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a showy, spatulate inflorescence that is typically white or pale pink. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its dramatic foliage and unique floral structure. The leaves are glossy and can reach up to 30 cm in length, adding a bold presence to gardens or indoor spaces.

This cultivar is a popular choice for those seeking a dramatic and exotic plant with both aesthetic and botanical interest.
