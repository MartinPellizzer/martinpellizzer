Actinidia rubrafilmenta

Actinidia rubrafilmenta, commonly known as the red-veined actinidia, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn red in the fall, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer, which are followed by edible, sweet berries. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
