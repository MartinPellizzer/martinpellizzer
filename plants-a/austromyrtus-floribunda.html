Austromyrtus floribunda

Austromyrtus floribunda, commonly known as the coastal bottlebrush, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cylindrical flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in dense clusters. The plant has smooth, aromatic leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powder. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

Austromyrtus floribunda is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
