Amethystanthus excisinflexus

Amethystanthus excisinflexus, commonly known as the Cape rose or the orchid of the veld, is a striking flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling orchids in appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy addition to arid gardens. Its unique floral structure includes a prominent, showy lip that aids in attracting pollinators.

Despite its beauty, it is relatively rare in cultivation due to its specific environmental requirements.
