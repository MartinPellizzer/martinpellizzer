Asparagus africanus var. puberulus

Asparagus africanus var. puberulus, commonly known as the silky asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
