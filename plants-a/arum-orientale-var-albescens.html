Arum orientale var. albescens

Arum orientale var. albescens, commonly known as the white arum, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a distinctive white spathe that surrounds a spadix, creating an elegant and exotic appearance. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and prefers partial to full shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.

The flowers, while not showy, play a role in attracting specific pollinators.
