Aegiphila hassleri

Aegiphila hassleri, commonly known as the hairy leaf aegiphila, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy foliage and small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a woody stem with thick, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities for their purported healing properties.

Aegiphila hassleri plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
