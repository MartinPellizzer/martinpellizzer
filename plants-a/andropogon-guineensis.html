Andropogon guineensis

Andropogon guineensis, commonly known as Guinea grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, savannas, and along riverbanks.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it plays an important role in both ecological and agricultural systems.
