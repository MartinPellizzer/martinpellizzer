Antirrhinum orchidiflorum

Antirrhinum orchidiflorum, commonly known as the orchid-flowered snapdragon, is a unique hybrid plant that combines features of both the snapdragon genus and orchid-like flowers.

It is characterized by its elongated, tubular flowers that resemble orchids in structure, though they are actually part of the Plantaginaceae family. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil with partial sunlight. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including pink, purple, and white, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Due to its hybrid nature, it is often cultivated for its distinctive appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
