Avonia gariepensis

Avonia gariepensis, commonly known as the Gariep sandthorn, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by birds and small mammals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Avonia gariepensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
