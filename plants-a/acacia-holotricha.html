Acacia holotricha

Acacia holotricha, commonly known as the hairy wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces small, flat pods that contain seeds covered in fine, hairy structures. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Acacia holotricha is valued for its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
