Anemia cornea

Anemia cornea, commonly known as the "eye of the anemic," is a rare and unique plant species characterized by its translucent, glass-like leaves that resemble the cornea of the human eye.

The plant exhibits a delicate structure with thin, veined leaves that range in color from pale green to nearly transparent. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests. Its botanical features include a short, unbranched stem and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale yellow.

Due to its rarity and striking appearance, it is highly valued in botanical research and conservation efforts.
