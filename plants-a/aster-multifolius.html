Aster multifolius

Aster multifolius, commonly known as the New England aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flower heads that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its scientific name reflects its multileaf structure, with "multifolius" meaning "many-leaved" in Latin.

Aster multifolius is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in gardens and floral arrangements.
