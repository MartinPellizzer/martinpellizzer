Aeonium strepsicladum

Aeonium strepsicladum, commonly known as the "Coral Cactus," is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features rosettes of fleshy, triangular leaves that range in color from green to red, depending on light exposure. The plant produces vibrant orange to yellow flowers on tall, branching stems during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with full sun.

Aeonium strepsicladum is popular in rock gardens and as a decorative houseplant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
