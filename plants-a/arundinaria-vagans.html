Arundinaria vagans

Arundinaria vagans, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a striking golden-green color. This plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense clumps, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping. Arundinaria vagans thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically beneficial in certain environments.
