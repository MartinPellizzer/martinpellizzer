Astragalus scaberrimus

Astragalus scaberrimus, commonly known as the rough milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by pods containing several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in prairies and steppe environments.

Astragalus scaberrimus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
