Arum cylindraceum subsp. cylindraceum

Arum cylindraceum subsp.

cylindraceum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf arum, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It features a short, stocky rhizome and produces striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a metallic sheen.

The plant's inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, which is usually pale green or white. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
