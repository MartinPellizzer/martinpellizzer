Amsonia hirtella var. pogonosepala

Amsonia hirtella var. pogonosepala, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and showy, spherical clusters of blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodlands or along streambanks. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partially shaded environments.
