Acinodendron rubiginosum

Acinodendron rubiginosum, commonly known as the red-stained acacia, is a shrub native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant is known for its distinctive reddish-brown bark and its ability to thrive in arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various small animals.

Its flowers are typically yellow or pale green, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
