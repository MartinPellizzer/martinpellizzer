Aspalathus floribunda

Aspalathus floribunda, commonly known as the Cape tea or African red bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, red or orange-red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is well adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region, with drought-resistant properties.

Aspalathus floribunda is also known for its medicinal and culinary uses, particularly in traditional African medicine.
