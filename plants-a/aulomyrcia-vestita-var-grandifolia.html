Aulomyrcia vestita var. grandifolia

Aulomyrcia vestita var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its growth habit is herbaceous, with a sprawling or upright stem depending on environmental conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
