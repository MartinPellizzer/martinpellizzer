Adenophora pereskiifolia f. puberula

Adenophora pereskiifolia f. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent adenophora, is a variant of the adenophora species characterized by its hairy or pubescent leaves.

It is a flowering plant native to East Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in woodland and mountainous regions. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

This cultivar is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various growing conditions.
