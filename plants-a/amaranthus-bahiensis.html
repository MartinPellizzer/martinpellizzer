Amaranthus bahiensis

Amaranthus bahiensis, commonly known as the Brazilian amaranth, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from green to red, depending on the variety. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible seeds.

Amaranthus bahiensis thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warmer climates.
