Airochloa alopecurus

Airochloa alopecurus, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and wetlands. The plant has slender, erect culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, two-lobed flowers.

Airochloa alopecurus plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects and small animals.
