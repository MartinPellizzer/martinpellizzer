Atropis nevadensis

Atropis nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada lily, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing white or pale pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Atropis nevadensis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
