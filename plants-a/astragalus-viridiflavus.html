Astragalus viridiflavus

Astragalus viridiflavus, commonly known as the green-yellow milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces pods that contain small, round seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Astragalus viridiflavus plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.
