Adenostoma fasciculatum var. fasciculatum

Adenostoma fasciculatum var. fasciculatum, commonly known as the chamise, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, needle-like leaves that are densely packed along the branches. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Its aromatic foliage and resilience to drought make it a notable species in its ecological community.
