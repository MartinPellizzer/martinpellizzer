Anchusa leptophylla subsp. tomentosa

Anchusa leptophylla subsp.

tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of blue or violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to dry, coastal climates.

The subspecies tomentosa is distinguished by its dense, woolly foliage, which helps protect it from harsh environmental conditions.
