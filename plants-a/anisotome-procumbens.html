Anisotome procumbens

Anisotome procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate anisotome, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its sprawling, rhizomatous growth habit and small, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces delicate, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Anisotome procumbens is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
