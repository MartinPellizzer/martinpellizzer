Arabis × raciborskii

Arabis × raciborskii, commonly known as the hybrid rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in rocky or sandy soils. It is often found in alpine or coastal environments due to its tolerance for harsh conditions.

Arabis × raciborskii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
