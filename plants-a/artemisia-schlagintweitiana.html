Artemisia schlagintweitiana

Artemisia schlagintweitiana, commonly known as the Kashmir silverfoil, is a perennial shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, typically in August or September. Artemisia schlagintweitiana is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

It is often used in traditional medicine and has aromatic properties that contribute to its ecological and cultural significance.
