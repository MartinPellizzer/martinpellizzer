Agrostistachys gaudichaudii

Agrostistachys gaudichaudii, commonly known as the mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountainous regions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are borne at the ends of its stems.

Agrostistachys gaudichaudii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its graceful appearance.
