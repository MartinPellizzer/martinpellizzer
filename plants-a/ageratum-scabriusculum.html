Ageratum scabriusculum

Ageratum scabriusculum, commonly known as roughleaf ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 30 inches and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for borders and containers due to its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
