Armeria chilensis var. curvifolia

Armeria chilensis var. curvifolia, commonly known as the curl-leaved thrift, is a flowering plant native to Chile.

It belongs to the family Amaryllidaceae and is characterized by its dense, low-growing clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant features linear, grass-like leaves that are often curled at the tips, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
