Acer confertifolium var. serrulatum

Acer confertifolium var. serrulatum, commonly known as the serrulate maple, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscape planting in temperate regions.
