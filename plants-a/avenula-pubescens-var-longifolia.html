Avenula pubescens var. longifolia

Avenula pubescens var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved mouse-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, silver-gray leaves that are densely covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Avenula pubescens var. longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
