Agalinis paupercula var. borealis

Agalinis paupercula var. borealis, commonly known as the northern povertyweed, is a flowering plant native to the northern United States and southern Canada.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, tubular flowers that are typically pink or reddish in color. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
