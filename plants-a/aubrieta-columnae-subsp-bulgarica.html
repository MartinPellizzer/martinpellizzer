Aubrieta columnae subsp. bulgarica

Aubrieta columnae subsp.

bulgarica, commonly known as the Balkan aubrieta, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It features compact, upright stems that bear clusters of bright yellow flowers, typically blooming in spring.

The plant has small, rounded leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental value and hardiness in temperate climates.
