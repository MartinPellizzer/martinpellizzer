Allium ochraceum

Allium ochraceum, commonly known as the yellow nutgrass, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous roots and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.

However, it can be invasive in some regions, posing a challenge to local ecosystems.
