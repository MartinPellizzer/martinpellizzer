Actinidia glaucophylla var. rotunda

Actinidia glaucophylla var. rotunda, commonly known as the round-leaf actinidia, is a variant of the actinidia genus native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive round or ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy bloom. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, hairy fruits that ripen in late summer. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and hardiness.

The species is also valued for its potential in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability to various soil types.
