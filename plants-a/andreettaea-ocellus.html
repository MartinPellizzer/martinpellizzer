Andreettaea ocellus

Andreettaea ocellus, commonly known as the spotted bromeliad, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are typically green with white or silver markings, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Andreettaea ocellus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
