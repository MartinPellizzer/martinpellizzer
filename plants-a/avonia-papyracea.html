Avonia papyracea

Avonia papyracea, commonly known as the paperleaf mallow, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are often described as papery and delicate in texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in the spring. Avonia papyracea thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
