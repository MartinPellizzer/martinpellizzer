Aegiphila mediterranea var. brevilobata

Aegiphila mediterranea var. brevilobata, commonly known as the Mediterranean aegiphila, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair, which helps reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, appearing in clusters during the spring and early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
