Aconitum × paniculatum

Aconitum × paniculatum, commonly known as the European monkshood, is a hybrid perennial plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple or blue hood-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring palmate leaves with a distinctive veining pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aconitum × paniculatum is known for its ornamental value in gardens and its historical use in traditional medicine, though it contains toxic alkaloids.
