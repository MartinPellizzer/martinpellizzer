Achillea nana var. conferta

Achillea nana var. conferta, commonly known as dwarf yarrow, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has feathery, finely divided leaves that are aromatic when crushed, contributing to its medicinal historical use. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact size and long flowering period make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, low-maintenance plant.
