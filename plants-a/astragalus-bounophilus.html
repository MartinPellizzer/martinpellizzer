Astragalus bounophilus

Astragalus bounophilus, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species adapted to poor soils. It produces small, round seeds that are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native range.

Astragalus bounophilus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
