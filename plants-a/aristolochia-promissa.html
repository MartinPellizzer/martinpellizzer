Aristolochia promissa

Aristolochia promissa, commonly known as the Brazilian pipevine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a prominent, hooded structure. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex floral morphology. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its vines can grow quite long.

The species is also notable for containing aristolochic acids, which have been linked to toxic effects in some animals and humans.
