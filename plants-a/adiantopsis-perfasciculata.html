Adiantopsis perfasciculata

Adiantopsis perfasciculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit, with arching stems that bear small, lance-shaped leaves. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a distinctive, feathery appearance.

Adiantopsis perfasciculata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.
