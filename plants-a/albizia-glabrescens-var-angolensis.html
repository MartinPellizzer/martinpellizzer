Albizia glabrescens var. angolensis

Albizia glabrescens var. angolensis, commonly known as the Angola siris, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments. It also has a shallow root system, which allows it to colonize disturbed areas quickly.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil stabilization and can be used in agroforestry practices.
