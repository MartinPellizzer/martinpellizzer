Asplenium obovatum subsp. lanceolatum

Asplenium obovatum subsp.

lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceleaf spleenwort, is a fern species characterized by its lanceolate, glossy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette. It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to regions in Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocks or soil in woodland areas. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with spore-producing structures called sori located on the underside.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
