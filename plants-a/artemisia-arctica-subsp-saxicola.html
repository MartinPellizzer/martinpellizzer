Artemisia arctica subsp. saxicola

Artemisia arctica subsp.

saxicola, commonly known as the alpine tarragon, is a perennial herb native to arctic and alpine regions. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers.

This subspecies thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates. It is often found in tundra and mountainous areas where temperatures are low.

The plant has a strong aromatic scent and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
