Astragalus bisulcatus var. nevadensis

Astragalus bisulcatus var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra Cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish to greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is distinguished by its narrow, two-lobed leaves and is often found in mountainous regions of Nevada and adjacent states.
