Ampelodesmos festucoides

Ampelodesmos festucoides, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to South Africa.

It features fine, silver-white leaves that give it a distinctive appearance, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is characterized by its clumping growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its soft, feathery foliage and delicate flowers.
