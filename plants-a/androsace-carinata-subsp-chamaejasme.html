Androsace carinata subsp. chamaejasme

Androsace carinata subsp.

chamaejasme, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its dense rosettes of hairy, ovate leaves. It produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, typically on slender stems.

Native to the alpine regions of Europe and western Asia, this species thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. Its botanical features include a compact growth habit and a preference for cool, moist environments.

The plant is often found in mountain meadows and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
