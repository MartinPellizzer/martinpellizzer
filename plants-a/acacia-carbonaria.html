Acacia carbonaria

Acacia carbonaria, commonly known as the black wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that function as leaves.

Acacia carbonaria is often found in coastal and sandy soils, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.
