Achillea sylvestris

Achillea sylvestris, commonly known as field fleabane, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features upright, branched stems with narrow, gray-green leaves that are softly hairy. Its flowers are small, yellowish-white composite blooms that appear in late summer to early autumn.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.
