Arundo vulgaris

Arundo vulgaris, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. This plant is highly adaptable and can thrive in a variety of soil types, including saline and alkaline conditions. It is often found growing along waterways, wetlands, and disturbed areas due to its ability to tolerate wet environments.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in many regions because of its rapid growth and ability to outcompete native vegetation.
