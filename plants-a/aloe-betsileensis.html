Aloe betsileensis

Aloe betsileensis, commonly known as the Betilean Aloe, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to its harsh environment. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is well-suited to low rainfall conditions. Its leaves are often tinged with a reddish hue, especially when exposed to direct sunlight.

Aloe betsileensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has potential medicinal uses, though it remains relatively understudied.
