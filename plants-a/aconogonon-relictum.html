Aconogonon relictum

Aconogonon relictum, commonly known as the relic aconogonon, is a rare and endangered plant species native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, often branched stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are usually pale purple or white, arranged in dense inflorescences. Aconogonon relictum thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
