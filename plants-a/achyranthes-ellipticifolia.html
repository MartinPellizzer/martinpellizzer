Achyranthes ellipticifolia

Achyranthes ellipticifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf smartweed, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often found in disturbed soils along waterways. The plant has alternate, elliptic to ovate leaves that are usually smooth and have a slightly waxy texture. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or red, and it produces small, three-lobed fruits.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and diuretic properties.
