Austrocactus spiniflorus

Austrocactus spiniflorus, commonly known as the spiny-flowered cactus, is a flowering plant native to southern South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in sharp spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, which are typically short-lived but attract pollinators like bees and birds. Austrocactus spiniflorus is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
