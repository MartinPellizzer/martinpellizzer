Anthemis parnassica var. pallida

Anthemis parnassica var. pallida, commonly known as the pale mountain fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the high alpine regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow to pale yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations above 2,000 meters. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
