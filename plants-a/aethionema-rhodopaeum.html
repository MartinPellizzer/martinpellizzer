Aethionema rhodopaeum

Aethionema rhodopaeum, commonly known as the Cyprus carnation, is a flowering plant native to the island of Cyprus.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. This perennial herb is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.
