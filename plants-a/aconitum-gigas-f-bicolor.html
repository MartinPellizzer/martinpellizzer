Aconitum gigas f. bicolor

Aconitum gigas f. bicolor, commonly known as the Twin-Colored Giant Aconite, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, with a distinctive spurred petal. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a rough, hairy texture.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
