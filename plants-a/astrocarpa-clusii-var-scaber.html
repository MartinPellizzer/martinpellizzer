Astrocarpa clusii var. scaber

Astrocarpa clusii var. scaber, commonly known as the rough-leafed astrocarpa, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features rough, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped, and it produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
