Allium dumanii

Allium dumanii, commonly known as the desert leek, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale purple flowers in umbels. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with deep root systems that help it access water. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, garlic-like flavor, making it a useful plant in certain culinary traditions.

Allium dumanii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
