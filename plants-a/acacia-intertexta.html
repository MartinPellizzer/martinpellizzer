Acacia intertexta

Acacia intertexta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, bipinnate leaves that are typically narrow and dark green. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. Acacia intertexta is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resistance to drought and poor soil conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
