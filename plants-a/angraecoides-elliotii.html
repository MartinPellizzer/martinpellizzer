Angraecoides elliotii

Angraecoides elliotii, commonly known as the Elliot's angraecoid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stem and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces white or pale yellow blooms with intricate patterns, which are adapted for pollination by specific moths. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Angraecoides elliotii is a rare and endangered species, making it a subject of interest for conservation efforts.
