Apinagia digitata

Apinagia digitata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree with smooth, reddish bark and elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange to red berries that are edible for some bird species. It is often found in disturbed habitats and can become invasive in certain regions.

The plant has historical uses in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
