Artemisia pontica

Artemisia pontica, commonly known as Caucasian wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features slender, aromatic stems and gray-green, feathery leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Artemisia pontica is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its medicinal properties.

Its essential oils contain compounds with antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects.
