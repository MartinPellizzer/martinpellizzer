Acacia palustris

Acacia palustris, commonly known as the swamp acacia, is a shrub native to wetland and marshy areas of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that function as leaves, reducing water loss in its moist habitat. Acacia palustris produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
