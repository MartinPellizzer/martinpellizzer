Allium paniculatum subsp. stearnii

Allium paniculatum subsp.

stearnii, commonly known as the purple garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, lavender-pink flowers arranged in a panicle inflorescence.

The plant is characterized by its fragrant, bulbous structures that resemble garlic cloves, which are used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant floral display and aromatic foliage.
