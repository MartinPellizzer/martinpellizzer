Allium tulipifolium var. subscabrum

Allium tulipifolium var. subscabrum, commonly known as the scabrous garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are slightly hairy, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers that resemble those of tulips, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild garlic scent, making it a popular choice in gardens.
