Arctostaphylos candidissima

Arctostaphylos candidissima, commonly known as white mountain manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its smooth, white bark and evergreen foliage, which remains dense throughout the year. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its fruit is a small, leathery capsule that contains several seeds.

Arctostaphylos candidissima is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
