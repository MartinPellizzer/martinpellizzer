Albizia granulosa

Albizia granulosa, commonly known as the granulate acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a rough texture. The plant produces yellow or greenish-yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the spring or early summer. Its pods are elongated, woody, and contain several seeds.

Albizia granulosa is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
