Aloe ahmarensis

Aloe ahmarensis, commonly known as the Ahmar aloe, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted for water storage. The leaves are typically green with a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional remedies.
