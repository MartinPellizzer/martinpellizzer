Acacia hostilis

Acacia hostilis, commonly known as the hostile acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, feather-like segments.

Acacia hostilis is adapted to arid environments and has deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions.
