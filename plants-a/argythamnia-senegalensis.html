Argythamnia senegalensis

Argythamnia senegalensis, commonly known as the Senegalese thornbush, is a shrub native to the Sahel region of Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by distinctive, three-lobed seed pods. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

Argythamnia senegalensis plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing forage for livestock in certain regions.
