Anthurium curvilaminum

Anthurium curvilaminum, commonly known as the twisted-leaf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that often exhibit a vibrant green color with red or purple veins. The plant produces an inflorescence known as a spadix, which is surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Anthurium curvilaminum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
