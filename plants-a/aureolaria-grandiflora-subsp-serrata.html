Aureolaria grandiflora subsp. serrata

Aureolaria grandiflora subsp.

serrata, commonly known as the serrated goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It features showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native landscaping.
