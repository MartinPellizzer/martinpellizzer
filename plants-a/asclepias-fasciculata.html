Asclepias fasciculata

Asclepias fasciculata, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and is a member of the milkweed family, Apocynaceae. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and its stems are covered in fine, white hairs. It produces a distinctive seed pod that splits open to release numerous tiny seeds with silky filaments.

Asclepias fasciculata is an important host plant for monarch butterflies and supports a variety of pollinators.
