Argyrolobium linnaeanum var. saharae

Argyrolobium linnaeanum var. saharae, commonly known as the Saharan silver globular pea, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It is characterized by its spherical, silver-gray foliage and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it an interesting subject for botanical study and potential use in xeriscaping.
