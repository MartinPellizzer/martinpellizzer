Acidoton lanceolatus

Acidoton lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acidoton, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
