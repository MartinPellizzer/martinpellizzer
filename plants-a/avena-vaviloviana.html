Avena vaviloviana

Avena vaviloviana, commonly known as the Vavilov's oat, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that bear small, two-lobed florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Avena vaviloviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
