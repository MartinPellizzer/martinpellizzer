Alyssum spinosum

Alyssum spinosum, commonly known as prickly alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial species that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in early spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often spiny, giving it its common name.

Alyssum spinosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
