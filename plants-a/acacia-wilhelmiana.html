Acacia wilhelmiana

Acacia wilhelmiana, commonly known as the white wattle, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, feathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, spherical clusters, which bloom during the dry season. Acacia wilhelmiana is adapted to harsh, dry environments and is drought-tolerant.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various herbivores.
