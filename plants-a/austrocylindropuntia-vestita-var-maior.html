Austrocylindropuntia vestita var. maior

Austrocylindropuntia vestita var. maior, commonly known as the giant night-blooming cactus, is a species native to northern Chile.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white and yellow. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a deep root system adapted to arid conditions.

This cactus is well-suited to rocky soils and full sun exposure.
