Amylotheca biangulata

Amylotheca biangulata, commonly known as the two-angled amaryllis, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its distinctive two-angled stems. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, often with a subtle fragrance. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Amylotheca biangulata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils with adequate sunlight.
