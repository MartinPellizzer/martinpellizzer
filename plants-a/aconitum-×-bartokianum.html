Aconitum × bartokianum

Aconitum × bartokianum, commonly known as the Bartok hybrid, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, showy flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white. The plant produces intricate, spurred petals and is known for its toxic alkaloids, which make it both dangerous and valuable in medicinal research. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental appeal and botanical interest, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
