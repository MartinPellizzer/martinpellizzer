Astragalus poljakovii

Astragalus poljakovii, commonly known as the Poljakov's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky or sandy soils. It is known for its deep root system, which allows it to survive in harsh, dry conditions.

Astragalus poljakovii is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
