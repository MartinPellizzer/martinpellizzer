Athanasia scariosa

Athanasia scariosa, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a succulent, hairy stem and is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
