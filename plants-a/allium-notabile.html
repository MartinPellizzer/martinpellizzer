Allium notabile

Allium notabile, commonly known as the showy garlic or the purple garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, purple or pink flowers. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow bulbs that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium notabile is valued for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in herbal remedies.
