Actinidia arguta var. rufa

Actinidia arguta var. rufa, commonly known as the red-leaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in early summer. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves turn a striking red or orange color in the fall, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.
