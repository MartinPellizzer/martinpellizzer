Alchemilla obconiciflora

Alchemilla obconiciflora, commonly known as the broad-leaved lady's mantle, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal rosettes of broad, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers on slender stalks, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported soothing properties.

Alchemilla obconiciflora thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
