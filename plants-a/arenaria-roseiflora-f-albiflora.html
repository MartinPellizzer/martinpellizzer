Arenaria roseiflora f. albiflora

Arenaria roseiflora f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered sandwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers, which are typically borne on slender stems. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or coastal environments.

This variant is distinguished by its notably white flowers, contrasting with the pink or rose-colored blooms of the typical form.
