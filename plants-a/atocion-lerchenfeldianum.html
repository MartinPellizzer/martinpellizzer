Atocion lerchenfeldianum

Atocion lerchenfeldianum, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flower heads that resemble a crown. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along riverbanks.

Atocion lerchenfeldianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
