Aconceveibum

Aconceveibum, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the wetlands of Florida and Cuba.

It is characterized by its unique, waxy, white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of tree trunks or branches without deriving nutrients from its host. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air, soil, and rain.

Due to habitat destruction and over-collection, Aconceveibum is listed as an endangered species, making it a symbol of conservation efforts in tropical ecosystems.
