Allium pevtzovii

Allium pevtzovii, commonly known as the Pevtsov's onion, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant produces spherical bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Allium pevtzovii is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to arid climates.
