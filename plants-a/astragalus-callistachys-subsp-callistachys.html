Astragalus callistachys subsp. callistachys

Astragalus callistachys subsp.

callistachys, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features slender, often hairy stems and small, compound leaves with a distinctive narrow shape. Its flowers are typically pale blue to purple, arranged in clusters, and are adapted for pollination by bees and other insects.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar sources for pollinators.
