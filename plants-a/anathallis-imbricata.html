Anathallis imbricata

Anathallis imbricata, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive star-shaped pattern on the petals. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are pollinated by specific species of bees, showcasing a unique mutualistic relationship.

The botanical features of Anathallis imbricata make it a notable subject in both horticulture and ecological studies.
