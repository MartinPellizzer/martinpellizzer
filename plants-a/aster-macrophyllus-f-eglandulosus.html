Aster macrophyllus f. eglandulosus

Aster macrophyllus f. eglandulosus, commonly known as the glandless aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
