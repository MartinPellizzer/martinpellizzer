Agave filifera subsp. schidigera

Agave filifera subsp.

schidigera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico. It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and long, narrow, blue-green leaves that are often lined with white teeth.

The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears clusters of yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its slow growth rate and striking appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and desert landscapes.
