Asclepias sinaica

Asclepias sinaica, commonly known as the Sinai milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the deserts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers with distinctive spurred petals. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access water.

Asclepias sinaica is also notable for its role as a host plant for the monarch butterfly.
