Aleuritia gambeliana

Aleuritia gambeliana, commonly known as Gambel's elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces dark purple berries in the summer, which are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn red in the fall.

Aleuritia gambeliana thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
