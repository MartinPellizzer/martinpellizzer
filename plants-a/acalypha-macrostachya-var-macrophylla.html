Acalypha macrostachya var. macrophylla

Acalypha macrostachya var. macrophylla, commonly known as the giant curlleaf mallow, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often curled or wavy in appearance. The plant produces showy, spiky inflorescences with vibrant red or orange bracts, which are often mistaken for flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
