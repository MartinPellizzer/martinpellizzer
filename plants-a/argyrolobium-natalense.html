Argyrolobium natalense

Argyrolobium natalense, commonly known as Natal silverball, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by pods containing small, round seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
