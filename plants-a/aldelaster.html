Aldelaster

Aldelaster, commonly known as the star of Bethlehem, is a flowering plant belonging to the genus Origanum in the family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling a star. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental value.

Aldelaster is often used in herbal medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
