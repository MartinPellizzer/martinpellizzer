Alchemilla subtatrica

Alchemilla subtatrica, commonly known as the Caucasian lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe and Western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.

Alchemilla subtatrica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
