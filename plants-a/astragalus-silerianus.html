Astragalus silerianus

Astragalus silerianus, commonly known as the Siler's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus silerianus is known for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and maintaining soil stability in arid environments.
