Anagallis phoenicea var. verticillata

Anagallis phoenicea var. verticillata, commonly known as the spiral willow herb, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers. The leaves are typically opposite and lance-shaped, while the stems are slender and often hairy. This variety is distinguished by its whorled arrangement of flowers, giving it a unique spiral appearance.

It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and riverbanks.
