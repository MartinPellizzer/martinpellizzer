Alchemilla fusoidea

Alchemilla fusoidea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Alchemilla fusoidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
