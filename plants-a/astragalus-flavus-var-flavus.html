Astragalus flavus var. flavus

Astragalus flavus var. flavus, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It has small, stipulate leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds.

This species is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
