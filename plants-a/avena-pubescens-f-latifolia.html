Avena pubescens f. latifolia

Avena pubescens f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf oat, is a variant of the common oat species characterized by its broader leaves compared to the standard form.

This plant is a perennial grass that typically grows in temperate climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage is dense and can reach heights of up to 1 meter, making it suitable for erosion control and ornamental use. The inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
