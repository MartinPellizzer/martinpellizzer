Aconitum × paniculatum f. prutense

Aconitum × paniculatum f. prutense, commonly known as the Prut Valley aconite, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces compound, pinnate leaves with serrated edges, typical of the genus Aconitum. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions of Eastern Europe.

Due to its ornamental value and toxicity, it is cultivated in gardens but requires caution due to its potent alkaloids.
