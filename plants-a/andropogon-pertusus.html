Andropogon pertusus

Andropogon pertusus, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces dense, purple-tinted inflorescences that are visually striking. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Andropogon pertusus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens and restoration projects.
