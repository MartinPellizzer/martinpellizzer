Agrimonia dahurica var. glabrata

Agrimonia dahurica var. glabrata, commonly known as smooth agrimony, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features upright stems with opposite, pinnately compound leaves that are smooth and lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late summer, which are followed by red, sticky seed capsules. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in dry, sandy soils.

Smooth agrimony is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for digestive and anti-inflammatory purposes.
