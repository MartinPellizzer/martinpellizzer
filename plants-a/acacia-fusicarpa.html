Acacia fusicarpa

Acacia fusicarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, feathery leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are arranged in cylindrical spikes. Acacia fusicarpa is drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or loamy soils.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
