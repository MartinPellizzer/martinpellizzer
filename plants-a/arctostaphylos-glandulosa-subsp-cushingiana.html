Arctostaphylos glandulosa subsp. cushingiana

Arctostaphylos glandulosa subsp.

cushingiana, commonly known as Cushing's manzanita, is a shrub native to California and southern Oregon. It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and red-brown bark that peels in thin layers.

The plant produces small, urn-shaped flowers in clusters, typically blooming in white or pink hues. Its fruit is a capsule that releases small, shiny seeds when mature.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.
