Atropa dentata

Atropa dentata, commonly known as the Eastern black nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges, and produces small, clustered flowers that transition from white to purple as they mature. The plant bears dark purple to black berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and botanical gardens.
