Aquilegia vulgaris var. subnuda

Aquilegia vulgaris var. subnuda, commonly known as the nakedleaf columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features striking, spurred flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are usually less hairy compared to other varieties of Aquilegia. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its unique foliage and showy flowers contribute to its ornamental value in horticulture.
