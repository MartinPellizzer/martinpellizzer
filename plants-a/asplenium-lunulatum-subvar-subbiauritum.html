Asplenium lunulatum subvar. subbiauritum

Asplenium lunulatum subvar. subbiauritum, commonly known as the auriculate spleenwort, is a fern native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive auriculate (ear-shaped) base. The plant typically grows on rocky surfaces or in shaded, moist environments. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a finely toothed margin.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
