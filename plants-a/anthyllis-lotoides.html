Anthyllis lotoides

Anthyllis lotoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several narrow, linear leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Anthyllis lotoides is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
