Acantholimon echinus var. puberulum

Acantholimon echinus var. puberulum, commonly known as the rough hairgrass, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in short, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil in arid environments.
