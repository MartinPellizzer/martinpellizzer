Amelanchier macrocarpa

Amelanchier macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It produces edible berries that ripen in late summer, typically turning from white to red or purple. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

The flowers are white and appear in clusters, followed by the showy fruit that attracts birds and other wildlife.
