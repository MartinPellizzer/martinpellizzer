Aronicum scorpioides

Aronicum scorpioides, commonly known as swamp cowbane, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It produces striking, spiky yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in dense clusters near water. It is adapted to moist soils and can thrive in marshy or swampy environments.

Its name reflects its historical use by indigenous peoples for medicinal purposes.
