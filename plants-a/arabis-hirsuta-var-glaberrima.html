Arabis hirsuta var. glaberrima

Arabis hirsuta var. glaberrima, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and alpine regions of Europe.

It features basal leaves that are hairy and lobed, with small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a compact growth habit, often forming dense mats on rocky soils. It is adapted to harsh environments, tolerating poor soil and full sun.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and hardy member of the Brassicaceae family.
