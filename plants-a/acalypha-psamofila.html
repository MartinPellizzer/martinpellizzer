Acalypha psamofila

Acalypha psamofila, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with a deep root system that helps it access water from deeper soil layers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Acalypha psamofila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
