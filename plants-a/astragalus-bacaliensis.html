Astragalus bacaliensis

Astragalus bacaliensis, commonly known as the Balkan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate climates. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native habitat.
