Astragalus tournefortii

Astragalus tournefortii, commonly known as the French milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus tournefortii is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
