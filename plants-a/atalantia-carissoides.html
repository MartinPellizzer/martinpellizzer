Atalantia carissoides

Atalantia carissoides, commonly known as the Chinese lantern tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, orange-red, lantern-shaped fruits that persist on the tree long after the leaves have fallen. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
