Ammannia fruticosa

Ammannia fruticosa, commonly known as prairie bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a reddish tinge, while its seed capsules are distinctive for their three-winged structure.

Ammannia fruticosa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
