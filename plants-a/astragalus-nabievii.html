Astragalus nabievii

Astragalus nabievii, commonly known as the Nabiev's milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus nabievii is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
