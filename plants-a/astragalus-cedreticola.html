Astragalus cedreticola

Astragalus cedreticola, commonly known as the cedar-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with thread-like leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and mountainous regions.

Astragalus cedreticola plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
