Astracantha qatmensis

Astracantha qatmensis, commonly known as the Qatmensis thornveld, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and compact growth habit, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a delicate contrast to its rugged appearance. Its thick, fleshy leaves are adapted to store water, making it well-suited for dry climates.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
