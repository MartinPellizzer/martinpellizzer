Amygdalus fructicosa

Amygdalus fructicosa, commonly known as the wild almond or bitter almond tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, oval fruits that contain bitter seeds, which are toxic if ingested. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional landscapes.
