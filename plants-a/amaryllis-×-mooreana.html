Amaryllis × mooreana

Amaryllis × mooreana, commonly known as the hybrid amaryllis, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis paradisicola.

It is a bulbous plant characterized by its sturdy, erect stems and large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant is native to South America and thrives in warm, sunny conditions with well-draining soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for gardeners and indoor plant enthusiasts.

The flowers typically bloom in late spring or early summer, adding a dramatic touch to gardens or floral arrangements.
