Aletes macdougalii

Aletes macdougalii, commonly known as the Macdougalii orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowering season occurs during the warmer months, producing fragrant blooms in shades of white and pale pink.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
