Amalia

Amalia, commonly known as the Amalian orchid, is a rare and exotic plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture that helps retain moisture.

Amalia is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and unique blooming patterns.
