Artemisia dracunculus var. qinghaiensis

Artemisia dracunculus var. qinghaiensis, commonly known as the Qinghai wormwood, is a variant of the common tarragon plant native to the Qinghai region of China.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, typically in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
