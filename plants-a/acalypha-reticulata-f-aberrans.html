Acalypha reticulata f. aberrans

Acalypha reticulata f. aberrans, commonly known as the variegated nettleleaf, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a variant of the species Acalypha reticulata, characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves with a striking combination of green, yellow, and white variegation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its unique foliage, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a houseplant.
