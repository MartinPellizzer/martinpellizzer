Aristida subinterrupta

Aristida subinterrupta, commonly known as the interrupted grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a cluster of spikelets arranged in a loose panicle, which is characteristic of the grass family.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
