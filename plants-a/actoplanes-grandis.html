Actoplanes grandis

Actoplanes grandis, commonly known as the grand magnolia, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, typically in shades of white, pink, and yellow. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stem. Actoplanes grandis thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
