Argythamnia dressleriana

Argythamnia dressleriana, commonly known as the Dressler's tamarisk, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, inconspicuous flowers and succulent leaves. The plant is typically shrub-like, with a compact growth habit and can thrive in dry, arid environments. Its botanical features include glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, which help reduce water loss.

It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.
