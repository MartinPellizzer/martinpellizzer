Arundarbor marginata

Arundarbor marginata, commonly known as the silver-edged fern, is a striking plant characterized by its distinctive foliage.

Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and exhibit a silvery margin that contrasts with the darker green center. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by spore-bearing structures called sori.

Arundarbor marginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to contribute to ecological diversity in natural habitats.
