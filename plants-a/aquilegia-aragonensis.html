Aquilegia aragonensis

Aquilegia aragonensis, commonly known as the Aragon bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and vibrant, usually red or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are deeply lobed.

Aquilegia aragonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
