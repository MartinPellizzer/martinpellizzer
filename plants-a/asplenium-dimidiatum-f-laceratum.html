Asplenium dimidiatum f. laceratum

Asplenium dimidiatum f. laceratum, commonly known as the shredded maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern characterized by its distinctive, frayed leaf margins.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a delicate, lace-like appearance that gives it its common name.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and indirect light for optimal growth.
