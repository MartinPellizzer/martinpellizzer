Alopecurus anatolicus

Alopecurus anatolicus, commonly known as Anatolian hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Alopecurus anatolicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
