Abrus lusorius

Abrus lusorius, commonly known as the devil's blood bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa.

It produces striking red pods with black seeds that contain a potent toxin, abrin, which is highly poisonous to humans and animals. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and small white or pink flowers. Its rapid growth and ornamental appearance make it popular in gardens, though its toxicity requires caution.

The species is also studied for its potential in biotechnology due to the unique properties of its seeds.
