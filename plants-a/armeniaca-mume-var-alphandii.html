Armeniaca mume var. alphandii

Armeniaca mume var. alphandii, commonly known as the Chinese date or Japanese medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that ripen in late summer, turning from green to a reddish-brown color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and historical significance.
