Aceras densiflorum

Aceras densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and clusters of blue to violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant collections.
