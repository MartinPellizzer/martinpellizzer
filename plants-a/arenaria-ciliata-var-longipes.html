Arenaria ciliata var. longipes

Arenaria ciliata var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with woolly, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
