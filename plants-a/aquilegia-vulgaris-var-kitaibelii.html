Aquilegia vulgaris var. kitaibelii

Aquilegia vulgaris var. kitaibelii, commonly known as the Japanese columbine, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features striking, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple, with five petals and two elongated nectar spurs. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can be found in various horticultural collections.

The variety is noted for its compact growth habit and prolonged blooming period.
