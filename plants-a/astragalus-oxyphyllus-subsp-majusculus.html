Astragalus oxyphyllus subsp. majusculus

Astragalus oxyphyllus subsp.

majusculus, commonly known as the alpine milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky, alpine environments and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets, and its root system is often extensive, adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude areas and is also of interest in traditional medicine.
