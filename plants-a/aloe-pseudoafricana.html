Aloe pseudoafricana

Aloe pseudoafricana, commonly known as the giant aloe or African aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stem and large, fleshy, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in summer. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Aloe pseudoafricana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential healing properties.
