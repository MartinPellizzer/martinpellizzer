Aldama nudibasillaris

Aldama nudibasillaris, commonly known as the naked basal alga, is a unique plant species characterized by its lack of traditional stems and leaves.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant's structure consists of a central body with branching filaments that resemble algae, giving it a distinct appearance. It reproduces through spores, which are released into the air and settle in suitable habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studying adaptation in aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
