Aconitum yangii var. yangii

Aconitum yangii var. yangii, commonly known as the Yang's monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple spurred flowers. The plant features large, palmately divided leaves that are typically hairy and have a waxy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
