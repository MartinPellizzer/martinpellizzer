Alectorolophus major var. alpestris

Alectorolophus major var. alpestris, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a silvery appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
