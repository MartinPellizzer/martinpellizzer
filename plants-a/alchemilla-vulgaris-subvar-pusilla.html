Alchemilla vulgaris subvar. pusilla

Alchemilla vulgaris subvar. pusilla, commonly known as lesser lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features dense, basal rosettes of narrow, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
