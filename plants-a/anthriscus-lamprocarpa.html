Anthriscus lamprocarpa

Anthriscus lamprocarpa, commonly known as the silverleaf cow parsley, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-veined leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its fragrant foliage and delicate appearance make it a notable species in alpine and subalpine ecosystems.

Anthriscus lamprocarpa is often found in meadows, slopes, and along forest edges, where it contributes to biodiversity and supports pollinators.
