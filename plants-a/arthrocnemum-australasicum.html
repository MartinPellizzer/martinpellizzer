Arthrocnemum australasicum

Arthrocnemum australasicum, commonly known as the Australian sandburs, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, spiny fruits that are adapted for dispersal by animals. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor, sandy soils. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Arthrocnemum australasicum plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes and providing habitat for small fauna.
