Arundo richardii

Arundo richardii, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and long, narrow leaves that can grow up to 6 feet in height. This plant thrives in wet or moist environments, often found near rivers, lakes, and wetlands. It has a robust root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

However, it can become invasive in non-native regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
