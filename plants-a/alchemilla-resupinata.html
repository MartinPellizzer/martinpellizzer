Alchemilla resupinata

Alchemilla resupinata, commonly known as the prostrate lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in a fine layer of silky white hairs. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and appear in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

Alchemilla resupinata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
