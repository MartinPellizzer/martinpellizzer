Amorphophallus lanceolatus

Amorphophallus lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceolate arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant produces a tuberous root that is used as a starchy food source in some regions. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its foliage and unique floral structure.
