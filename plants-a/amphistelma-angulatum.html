Amphistelma angulatum

Amphistelma angulatum, commonly known as the angular flatworm, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive angular leaf shape and flat, ribbon-like structure.

It is native to specific regions in southern Africa, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant's leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern, giving it a striking visual appearance. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and ecological studies.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
