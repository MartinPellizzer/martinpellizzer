Allium huntiae

Allium huntiae, commonly known as the Armenian garlic or mountain garlic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant's bulbs are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice. Allium huntiae thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or alpine gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both horticultural and ecological landscapes.
