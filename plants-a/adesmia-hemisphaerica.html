Adesmia hemisphaerica

Adesmia hemisphaerica, commonly known as the globular burr legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive globular, spiny fruits that enclose the seeds. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Its unique morphology makes it a subject of interest in botanical studies and ecological research.
