Asparagus popovii

Asparagus popovii, commonly known as the Popov's asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features slender, spiny stems that grow close to the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Asparagus popovii is adapted to arid environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
