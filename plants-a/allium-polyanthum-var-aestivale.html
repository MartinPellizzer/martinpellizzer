Allium polyanthum var. aestivale

Allium polyanthum var. aestivale, commonly known as the summer garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in the summer months. The plant is characterized by its strong garlic scent and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical name reflects its origin and the seasonal timing of its bloom.
