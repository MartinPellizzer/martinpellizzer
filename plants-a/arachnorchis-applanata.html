Arachnorchis applanata

Arachnorchis applanata, commonly known as the flat-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, greenish-brown rhizomes and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces delicate, pale green to yellowish flowers arranged in a loose spike, typically blooming from late autumn to early spring. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as under dense forest canopies.

Arachnorchis applanata is a rare species, often found in specific microhabitats with well-drained, humus-rich soils.
