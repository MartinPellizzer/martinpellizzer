Amphianthus pusillus

Amphianthus pusillus, commonly known as the small amphianthus, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wet, sandy soils along the edges of swamps and marshes. The plant has small, heart-shaped leaves and produces tiny, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. It is often found in association with other wetland plants, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
