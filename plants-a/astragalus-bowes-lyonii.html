Astragalus bowes-lyonii

Astragalus bowes-lyonii, commonly known as Bowes-Lyon's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native insects.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
