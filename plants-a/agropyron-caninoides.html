Agropyron caninoides

Agropyron caninoides, commonly known as the prairie wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie ecosystems.

Agropyron caninoides plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
