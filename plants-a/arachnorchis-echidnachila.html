Arachnorchis echidnachila

Arachnorchis echidnachila, commonly known as the echidna orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the wet tropical rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with long, slender petals and a strikingly intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, showcasing a specialized ecological relationship.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, it is considered critically endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
