Achras parvifolia

Achras parvifolia, commonly known as the African star apple, is a small, slow-growing tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features a dense, rounded canopy and produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is notable for its edible, star-shaped fruits that have a sweet, juicy pulp. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Achras parvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit in home gardens.
