Aster korshinskyi

Aster korshinskyi, commonly known as the Korshinsky's aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, dry climates. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Aster korshinskyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators in its native environment.
