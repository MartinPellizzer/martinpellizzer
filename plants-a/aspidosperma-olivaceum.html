Aspidosperma olivaceum

Aspidosperma olivaceum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or olivaceum tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by orange-red berries containing toxic seeds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its alkaloid-rich properties.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
