Astragalus alopecuroides f. gallicus

Astragalus alopecuroides f. gallicus, commonly known as the French milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers and trifoliate leaves. This subspecies is native to Europe, particularly France, and thrives in dry, calcareous soils. The plant is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, where it contributes to nitrogen fixation through its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
