Althaea pontica

Althaea pontica, commonly known as the Caucasian marshmallow, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its hairy, cylindrical stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces showy, pale pink to white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, containing a mucilage that is used in traditional medicine.

Althaea pontica thrives in moist, shady environments such as marshes and riverbanks.
