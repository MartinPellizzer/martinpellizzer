Astragalus subalabugensis

Astragalus subalabugensis, commonly known as the Subalabugian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain seeds.

Astragalus subalabugensis is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and potential ecological significance in its native range.
