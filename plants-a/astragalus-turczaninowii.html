Astragalus turczaninowii

Astragalus turczaninowii, commonly known as the Turczaninow's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its compound leaves are pinnately arranged, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus turczaninowii is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.
