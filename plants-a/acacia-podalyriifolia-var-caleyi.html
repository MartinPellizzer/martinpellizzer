Acacia podalyriifolia var. caleyi

Acacia podalyriifolia var. caleyi, commonly known as the Caleyan wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, narrow leaves that are arranged in a compact, rounded form. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense, globular inflorescences. Its leaves are typically covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soils.
