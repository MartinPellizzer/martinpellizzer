Amaranthus divaricatus

Amaranthus divaricatus, commonly known as the slender amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by spiky, triangular seed capsules. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in disturbed areas such as fields and roadsides.

Amaranthus divaricatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by various indigenous cultures.
