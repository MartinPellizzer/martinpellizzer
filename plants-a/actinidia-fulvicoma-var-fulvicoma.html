Actinidia fulvicoma var. fulvicoma

Actinidia fulvicoma var. fulvicoma, commonly known as the Chinese Actinidia, is a deciduous vine native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 5 to 9 lobed, and its small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in early summer. The plant produces small, edible berries that turn red when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.

This species is also valued for its use in traditional Chinese medicine.
