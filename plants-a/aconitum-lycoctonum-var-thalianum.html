Aconitum lycoctonum var. thalianum

Aconitum lycoctonum var. thalianum, commonly known as the European monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple, helmet-shaped flowers. The plant has dark green, doubly serrated leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
