Astragalus baldaccii

Astragalus baldaccii, commonly known as the Baldaccii milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in dry, harsh environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus baldaccii plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation to extreme conditions.
