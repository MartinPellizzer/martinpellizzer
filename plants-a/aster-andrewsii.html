Aster andrewsii

Aster andrewsii, commonly known as the Andrews' aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It features showy, purple to blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall.

Aster andrewsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
