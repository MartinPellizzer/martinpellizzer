Apostasia curvata

Apostasia curvata, commonly known as the curling spathiphyllum, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which is characterized by its unique inflorescence structure consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its leaves are usually broad and arrow-shaped, with a smooth texture and a dark green hue.

Apostasia curvata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
