Amaranthus emarginatus

Amaranthus emarginatus, commonly known as the margined amaranth, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from red to orange, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Amaranthus emarginatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
