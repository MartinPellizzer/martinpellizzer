Atriplex macrotheca

Atriplex macrotheca, commonly known as the desert saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its salt-tolerant nature allows it to thrive in soils with high salinity.

Atriplex macrotheca plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
