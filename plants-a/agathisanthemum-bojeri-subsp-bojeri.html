Agathisanthemum bojeri subsp. bojeri

Agathisanthemum bojeri subsp.

bojeri, commonly known as the Bojer's agathisanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy yellow flowers.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, daisy-like blooms that attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
