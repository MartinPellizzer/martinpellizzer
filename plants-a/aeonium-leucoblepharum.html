Aeonium leucoblepharum

Aeonium leucoblepharum, commonly known as the White-Eyed Aeonium, is a striking succulent native to the Canary Islands.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves with a distinctive white or pale yellow central stripe. The plant produces upright flower stalks that bear yellow flowers, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and rockeries.
