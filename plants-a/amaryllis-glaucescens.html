Amaryllis glaucescens

Amaryllis glaucescens, commonly known as the blue amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, erect stems that bear large, showy flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The leaves are broad, strap-like, and often have a bluish tinge, adding to the plant's ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

The plant is known for its ability to bloom reliably when grown under proper conditions.
