Atriplex hortensis var. lutea

Atriplex hortensis var. lutea, commonly known as golden orache, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically yellowish-green in color. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by triangular, beak-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
