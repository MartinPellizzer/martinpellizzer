Atractylis ovata

Atractylis ovata, commonly known as the common centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate and often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, and it blooms from late spring to early autumn.

Atractylis ovata is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
