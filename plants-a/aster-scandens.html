Aster scandens

Aster scandens, commonly known as the climbing aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial vine that produces showy blue to purple flowers in the fall. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, wooded areas. It thrives in partial to full sun and is known for its ability to climb using its tendrils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
