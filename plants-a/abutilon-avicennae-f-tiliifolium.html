Abutilon avicennae f. tiliifolium

Abutilon avicennae f. tiliifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf abutilon, is a variant of the abutilon species native to tropical regions.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is known for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The species is also valued for its ability to withstand coastal conditions and is sometimes used in horticultural landscaping.
