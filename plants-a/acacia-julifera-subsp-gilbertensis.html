Acacia julifera subsp. gilbertensis

Acacia julifera subsp.

gilbertensis, commonly known as the Gilbert's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Arabian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and compound leaves that are typically reduced to spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers in cylindrical spikes, which are followed by woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing resistance to drought and heat.

This subspecies is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
