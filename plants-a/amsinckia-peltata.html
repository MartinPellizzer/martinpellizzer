Amsinckia peltata

Amsinckia peltata, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, characterized by its succulent leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions by storing water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps reduce water loss through transpiration.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
