Adenorima gymnonota

Adenorima gymnonota, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its botanical characteristics include woody stems and a preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

Adenorima gymnonota is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
