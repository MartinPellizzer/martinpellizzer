Alocasia lowii var. grandis

Alocasia lowii var. grandis, commonly known as the Giant Elephant Ear, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, with a deep green color and prominent veins. The plant thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light and well-draining soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its dramatic foliage.

Alocasia lowii var. grandis requires consistent moisture and is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
