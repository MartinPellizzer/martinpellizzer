Acantholimon bromifolium var. bromifolium

Acantholimon bromifolium var. bromifolium, commonly known as the coastal buckthorn, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It features dense, silver-gray foliage with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery bloom. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by small, bladder-like fruits. It is well adapted to salty and windy conditions, making it a characteristic species of coastal dunes and rocky shores.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing dune soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
