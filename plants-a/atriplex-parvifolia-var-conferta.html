Atriplex parvifolia var. conferta

Atriplex parvifolia var. conferta, commonly known as the denseleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss through transpiration. The plant thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow and appear in dense clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

Atriplex parvifolia var. conferta is often used in rehabilitation of degraded lands due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
