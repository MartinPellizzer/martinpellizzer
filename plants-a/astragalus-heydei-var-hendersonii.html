Astragalus heydei var. hendersonii

Astragalus heydei var. hendersonii, commonly known as Henderson's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions.

This variety is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
