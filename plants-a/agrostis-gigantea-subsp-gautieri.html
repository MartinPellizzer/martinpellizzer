Agrostis gigantea subsp. gautieri

Agrostis gigantea subsp.

gautieri, commonly known as the Gautier's giant grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, two-lobed flowers.

The plant is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in regions with well-drained soils.
