Asperula cynanchica f. glabra

Asperula cynanchica f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth asperula, is a variant of the asperula species characterized by its smooth, hairless foliage.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, which are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
