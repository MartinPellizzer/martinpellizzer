Angraecopsis malawiensis

Angraecopsis malawiensis, commonly known as the Malawi orchid, is a flowering plant native to the eastern African region, particularly Malawi.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, pendulous inflorescences bearing numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, thriving in well-drained soils and partial shade. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators like moths.

Angraecopsis malawiensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
