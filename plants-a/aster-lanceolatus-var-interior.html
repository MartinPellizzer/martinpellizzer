Aster lanceolatus var. interior

Aster lanceolatus var. interior, commonly known as the Interior lanceleaf aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers in the late summer and early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
