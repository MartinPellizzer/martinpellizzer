Astrophytum castaneum

Astrophytum castaneum, commonly known as the "copper plant," is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, ribbed stems that are typically covered in a thick, waxy coating. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its stems are capable of storing water, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

Astrophytum castaneum is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
