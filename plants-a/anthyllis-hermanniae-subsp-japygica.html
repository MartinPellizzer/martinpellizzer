Anthyllis hermanniae subsp. japygica

Anthyllis hermanniae subsp.

japygica, commonly known as the Hermann's vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial subshrub characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of Mediterranean flora and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
