Athrixia ericifolia

Athrixia ericifolia, commonly known as the woolly heath, is a shrub native to the Cape Province of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, mat-like appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of the region. Its small, pinkish-white flowers appear in summer, adding a subtle ornamental value.

Athrixia ericifolia is often used in rock gardens and is valued for its drought tolerance and unique texture.
