Alchemilla diplophylla

Alchemilla diplophylla, commonly known as the lesser lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its leaves are used in traditional herbal remedies for their purported soothing properties.
