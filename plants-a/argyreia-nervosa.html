Argyreia nervosa

Argyreia nervosa, commonly known as the sacred lotus or holy vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Argyreia nervosa is also valued in cultural and spiritual practices across various Southeast Asian countries.
