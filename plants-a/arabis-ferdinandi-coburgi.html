Arabis ferdinandi-coburgi

Arabis ferdinandi-coburgi, commonly known as Coburg's rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent scent. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and its stems are often hairy.

The flowers, which bloom in early spring, are usually white or pale purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
