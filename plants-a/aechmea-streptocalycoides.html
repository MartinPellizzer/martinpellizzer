Aechmea streptocalycoides

Aechmea streptocalycoides, commonly known as the red-tipped pineapple plant, is a bromeliad native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater for the plant's use. The plant produces a striking inflorescence with red bracts and yellow flowers, adding visual interest. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Aechmea streptocalycoides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
