Artemisia richardsoniana

Artemisia richardsoniana, commonly known as silver sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.

Artemisia richardsoniana is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally by Indigenous peoples for medicinal and ceremonial purposes.
