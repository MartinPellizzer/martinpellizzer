Acacia capillosa

Acacia capillosa, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are clustered in dense, branched inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal regions.

Acacia capillosa is also valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
