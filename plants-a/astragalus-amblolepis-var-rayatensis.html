Astragalus amblolepis var. rayatensis

Astragalus amblolepis var. rayatensis, commonly known as Rayat's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in dry and semi-arid environments. The plant features slender stems and small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in racemes. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
