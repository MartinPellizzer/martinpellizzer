Aconitum elliotii

Aconitum elliotii, commonly known as Elliot's aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely leafy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in racemes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are palmately compound, with a deep green color and a slightly hairy surface.

Aconitum elliotii contains potent alkaloids, making it toxic and requiring careful handling.
