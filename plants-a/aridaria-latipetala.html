Aridaria latipetala

Aridaria latipetala, commonly known as the broad-petioled desert plant, is a succulent shrub adapted to arid environments.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, and its stems are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the rainy season, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is native to regions with extreme drought conditions, including parts of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

Its resilience makes it a valuable species for ecological restoration in desert ecosystems.
