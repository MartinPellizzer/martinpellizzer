Atriplex serenana var. davidsonii

Atriplex serenana var. davidsonii, commonly known as the Davidson's saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small green or yellowish flowers, which are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it an important species for stabilizing degraded lands.

Its ability to tolerate extreme conditions makes it valuable in ecological restoration projects.
