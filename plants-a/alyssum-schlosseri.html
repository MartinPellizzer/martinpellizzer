Alyssum schlosseri

Alyssum schlosseri, commonly known as the rock Alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has compact, mat-forming growth habits, making it well-suited for rock gardens and borders. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Alyssum schlosseri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soils.
