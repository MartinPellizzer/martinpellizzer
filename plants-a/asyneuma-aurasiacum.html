Asyneuma aurasiacum

Asyneuma aurasiacum, commonly known as the Asian goldenweed, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in composite inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
