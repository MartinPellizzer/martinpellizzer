Astrocasia

Astrocasia, commonly known as the moonflower, is a striking plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is known for its rapid growth and unique blooming pattern. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a velvety texture.

Astrocasia thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
