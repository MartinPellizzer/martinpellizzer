Allium baicalense

Allium baicalense, commonly known as the Baikal garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous structure and strap-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers during the summer months. Its bulbs are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The botanical features of Allium baicalense make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and pharmacological study.
