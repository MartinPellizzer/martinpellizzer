Astrochlaena

Astrochlaena, commonly known as the star cholla, is a type of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, branching stems that are often covered in small, triangular spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual element to its desert habitat. Astrochlaena is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Its unique structure and resilience make it an important part of the local ecosystem.
