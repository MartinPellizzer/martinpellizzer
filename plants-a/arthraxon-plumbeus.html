Arthraxon plumbeus

Arthraxon plumbeus, commonly known as the blue-green grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to Australia, characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and distinctive blue-green foliage. The plant has narrow, hairless leaves that are often rolled at the edges, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Arthraxon plumbeus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
