Agrostis rigidula subsp. arisan-montana

Agrostis rigidula subsp.

arisan-montana, commonly known as the Arisan mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia. It typically grows in high-altitude regions, often found in mountainous areas with cool and moist climates.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often forming dense mats in its natural habitat.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential horticultural use.
