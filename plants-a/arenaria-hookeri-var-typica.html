Arenaria hookeri var. typica

Arenaria hookeri var. typica, commonly known as the sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically forms dense mats of small, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of white or pink flowers in the spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its foliage is often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

It is a hardy species that is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in harsh conditions.
