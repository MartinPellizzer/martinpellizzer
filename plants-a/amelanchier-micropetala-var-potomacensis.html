Amelanchier micropetala var. potomacensis

Amelanchier micropetala var. potomacensis, commonly known as the Potomac serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It features oval, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall, and it produces clusters of white flowers in early spring. The plant bears edible, dark red berries in late summer, which are prized for their flavor and nutritional value. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits contribute to its significance in local ecosystems.
