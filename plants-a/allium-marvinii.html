Allium marvinii

Allium marvinii, commonly known as Marvin's onion, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves and a bulb that is often used in culinary applications. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or alpine beds.

Allium marvinii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
