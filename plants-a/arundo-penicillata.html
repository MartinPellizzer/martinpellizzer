Arundo penicillata

Arundo penicillata, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces panicles of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist environments and is often found near water bodies.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands make it both a useful and invasive species in various ecosystems.
