Arundinaria gammieana

Arundinaria gammieana, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a grass species native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, glossy surface.

Arundinaria gammieana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
