Achillea fraasii

Achillea fraasii, commonly known as the Fraas' yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.

Its structure and growth habits make it a resilient species in challenging ecological conditions.
