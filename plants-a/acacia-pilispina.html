Acacia pilispina

Acacia pilispina, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and small, feathery leaves that are typically green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in spherical or cylindrical heads. Acacia pilispina is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
