Acacia jurema

Acacia jurema, commonly known as the sacred acacia or raiz de palma, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its dense, thorny branches and compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in tropical and subtropical regions.

Acacia jurema is also known for its cultural and medicinal significance in various indigenous communities.
