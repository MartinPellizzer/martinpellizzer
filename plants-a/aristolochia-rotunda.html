Aristolochia rotunda

Aristolochia rotunda, commonly known as the broadleaf Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically purple or greenish-white in color. Its fruits are smooth, green berries that mature to a dark purple, containing seeds enclosed in a fleshy aril.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
