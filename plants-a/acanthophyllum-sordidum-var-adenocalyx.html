Acanthophyllum sordidum var. adenocalyx

Acanthophyllum sordidum var. adenocalyx, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability. Its leaves are often covered in a thick layer of wax to reduce water loss.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
