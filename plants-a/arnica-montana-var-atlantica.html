Arnica montana var. atlantica

Arnica montana var. atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic mountain arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its bright yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in alpine or subalpine habitats. It has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces a milky sap that contains various bioactive compounds.

This variety is often used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
