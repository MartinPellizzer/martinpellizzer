Andropogon triticiformis

Andropogon triticiformis, commonly known as false wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland restoration efforts.
