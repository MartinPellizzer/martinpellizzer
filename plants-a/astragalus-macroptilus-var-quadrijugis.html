Astragalus macroptilus var. quadrijugis

Astragalus macroptilus var. quadrijugis, commonly known as the four-winged milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with four pairs of leaflets and distinctive, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Astragalus macroptilus var. quadrijugis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
