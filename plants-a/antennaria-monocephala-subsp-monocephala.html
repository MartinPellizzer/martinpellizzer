Antennaria monocephala subsp. monocephala

Antennaria monocephala subsp.

monocephala, commonly known as the single-headed mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many plants with small, cross-shaped flowers.

The plant features dense, woolly foliage and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. It is typically found in rocky, dry habitats such as talus slopes and open woodlands.
