Ammannia pusilla

Ammannia pusilla, commonly known as dwarf ammannia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wet, marshy environments and is characterized by its compact, sprawling growth habit. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color, and it produces tiny, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red. Ammannia pusilla is known for its ability to thrive in moist soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important plant in its native habitats.
