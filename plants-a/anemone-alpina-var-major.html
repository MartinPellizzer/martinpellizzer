Anemone alpina var. major

Anemone alpina var. major, commonly known as the alpine anemone, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette and are deeply lobed with a hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
