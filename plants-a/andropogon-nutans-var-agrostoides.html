Andropogon nutans var. agrostoides

Andropogon nutans var. agrostoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in full sun and is often found in prairies, open woodlands, and disturbed soils.

Its growth habit makes it a valuable component of native plant communities and a potential candidate for ecological restoration projects.
