Aniba venezuelana

Aniba venezuelana, commonly known as the Venezuelan aniba, is a shrub native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which contain essential oils and are often used in traditional medicine. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, dark berries.

Aniba venezuelana is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
