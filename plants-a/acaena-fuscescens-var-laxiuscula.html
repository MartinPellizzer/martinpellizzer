Acaena fuscescens var. laxiuscula

Acaena fuscescens var. laxiuscula, commonly known as the New Zealand bramble, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its prickly, thorned stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round, berry-like fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is notable for its ability to form dense thickets, which can impact local ecosystems.
