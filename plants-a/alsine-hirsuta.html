Alsine hirsuta

Alsine hirsuta, commonly known as hairy alsike, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as meadows and roadsides. The plant features small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is an annual or biennial herb that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Due to its adaptability, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
