Artemisia turanica

Artemisia turanica, commonly known as Turanian wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert regions.

Artemisia turanica is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
