Achyranthes lanuginosa

Achyranthes lanuginosa, commonly known as the hairy cowhead, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
