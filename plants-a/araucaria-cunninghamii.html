Araucaria cunninghamii

Araucaria cunninghamii, commonly known as the mountain araucaria, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of dense, horizontal branches. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. Its foliage consists of needle-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem.

This species is often found in cool, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
