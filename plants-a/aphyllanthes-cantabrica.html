Aphyllanthes cantabrica

Aphyllanthes cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in rocky and coastal environments. The plant is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
