Aniba ovalifolia

Aniba ovalifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are often aromatic and have a leathery texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans.

Aniba ovalifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and riverbanks.
