Arundinaria humbertii

Arundinaria humbertii, commonly known as the Humbert bamboo, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, clumping growth habit. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
