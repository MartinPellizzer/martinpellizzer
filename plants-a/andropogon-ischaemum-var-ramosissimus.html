Andropogon ischaemum var. ramosissimus

Andropogon ischaemum var. ramosissimus, commonly known as woodland bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils in forested areas and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, blue to purple flower spikes in late summer. It is a valuable component of native plant communities due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for wetland restoration and ecological landscaping.
