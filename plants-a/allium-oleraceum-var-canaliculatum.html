Allium oleraceum var. canaliculatum

Allium oleraceum var. canaliculatum, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces bulbils along its stems, which are used for propagation and can be harvested as a culinary herb. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and useful in traditional cuisines.
