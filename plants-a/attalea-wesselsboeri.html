Attalea wesselsboeri

Attalea wesselsboeri, commonly known as the Wesselsboer palm, is a species native to the wetlands of northern South America.

It is a tall, slender palm with pinnate leaves that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant produces large, yellowish-white inflorescences that emerge from the leaf axils. Its fruits are small, round, and clustered in panicles, containing a single seed each.

The species is adapted to waterlogged soils and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its wetland habitat.
