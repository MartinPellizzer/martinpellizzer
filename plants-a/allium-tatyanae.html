Allium tatyanae

Allium tatyanae, commonly known as Tatyana's onion, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes various species of garlic and onions, and is characterized by its bulbous structure and striking purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are long and grass-like, while its flowers form a dense, spherical cluster at the top of the stem.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, Allium tatyanae is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
