Astragalus dieteri

Astragalus dieteri, commonly known as the Dieter's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh, dry climates. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind, aiding in its survival in challenging habitats.

Astragalus dieteri is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
