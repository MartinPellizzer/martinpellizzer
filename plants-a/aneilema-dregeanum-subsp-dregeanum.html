Aneilema dregeanum subsp. dregeanum

Aneilema dregeanum subsp.

dregeanum, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or rocky areas. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy surface.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
