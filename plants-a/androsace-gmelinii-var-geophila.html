Androsace gmelinii var. geophila

Androsace gmelinii var. geophila, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in alpine gardens.
