Astragalus alatavicus

Astragalus alatavicus, commonly known as the Central Asian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces cylindrical, dehiscent pods containing several elliptical seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and rocky soils.

Astragalus alatavicus is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a forage plant for livestock.
