Achillea ambigua

Achillea ambigua, commonly known as the lesser yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its flat, composite flower heads that range from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, while its roots are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Achillea ambigua is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
