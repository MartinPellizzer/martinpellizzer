Aira caryophyllea var. reverchonii

Aira caryophyllea var. reverchonii, commonly known as the Reverchon's false oat, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, pinkish to reddish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in coastal regions.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
