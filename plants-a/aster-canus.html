Aster canus

Aster canus, commonly known as the white wood aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features showy, white to pale purple ray flowers. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a fuzzy texture.

Aster canus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
