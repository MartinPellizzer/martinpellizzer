Ascocentrum ampullaceum

Ascocentrum ampullaceum, commonly known as the hanging basket plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Aroid family, Araceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including the Philippines and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that surround the small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Ascocentrum ampullaceum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to grow in hanging baskets or containers.
