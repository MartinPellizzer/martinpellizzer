Anemone raddeana var. glabrata

Anemone raddeana var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has smooth, glabrous stems and leaves that are alternate and pinnately divided. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
