Anthyllis vulneraria f. tenuicaulis

Anthyllis vulneraria f. tenuicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is adapted to dry conditions and often grows in rocky or sandy soils, making it a hardy species suited to harsh environments.
