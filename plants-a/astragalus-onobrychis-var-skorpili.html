Astragalus onobrychis var. skorpili

Astragalus onobrychis var. skorpili, commonly known as the Greek milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to southeastern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, trifoliate leaves, and small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces smooth, elongated pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
