Astragalus eremospartoides

Astragalus eremospartoides, commonly known as desert milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and its ability to thrive in harsh, drought-prone environments. The plant produces small, yellow to brown flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss.

Astragalus eremospartoides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
