Achnatherum coronatum

Achnatherum coronatum, commonly known as the blue grama, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with a crown of bracts, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is commonly found in grasslands and shrubsteppe ecosystems.

Achnatherum coronatum is an important forage plant for livestock and plays a key role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
