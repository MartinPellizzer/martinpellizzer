Adenophora triphylla var. glabra

Adenophora triphylla var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth bluebell, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features lance-shaped, smooth, bluish-green leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its smooth foliage and striking floral display make it a notable species in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
