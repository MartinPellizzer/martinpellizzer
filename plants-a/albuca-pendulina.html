Albuca pendulina

Albuca pendulina, commonly known as the hanging albuca, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, pendulous stems that bear clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale yellow, often with a greenish tinge. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Albuca pendulina is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
