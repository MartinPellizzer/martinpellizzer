Aconitum nasutum

Aconitum nasutum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are typically narrow and deeply divided. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
