Ampelocissus thyrsiflora

Ampelocissus thyrsiflora, commonly known as the forest grape or climbing fig, is a woody vine native to tropical Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb using its tendrils, making it suitable for living fences or ornamental purposes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The fruits are an important food source for various wildlife species.
