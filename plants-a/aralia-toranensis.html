Aralia toranensis

Aralia toranensis, commonly known as the Japanese aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features compound leaves that are typically pinnately arranged, with a serrated edge. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbellate clusters during the spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in the fall.

Aralia toranensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its foliage and seasonal interest.
