Acer campestre var. variegatum

Acer campestre var. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated field maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring white to cream-colored veins and margins on its heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
