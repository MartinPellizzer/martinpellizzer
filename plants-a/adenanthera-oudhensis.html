Adenanthera oudhensis

Adenanthera oudhensis, commonly known as the Indian velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, hairy pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.

Adenanthera oudhensis plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
