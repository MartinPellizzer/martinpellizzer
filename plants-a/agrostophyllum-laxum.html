Agrostophyllum laxum

Agrostophyllum laxum, commonly known as the slender plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their essential oils. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color.

Agrostophyllum laxum is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.
