Aster lilacinus

Aster lilacinus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features showy, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Aster lilacinus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators like butterflies and bees.
