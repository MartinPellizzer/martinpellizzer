Aquilegia formosa var. saxicola

Aquilegia formosa var. saxicola, commonly known as the Sierra formosa columbine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features compound, palmately arranged leaves and produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in native plant communities.
