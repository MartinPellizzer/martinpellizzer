Aristida grisebachiana

Aristida grisebachiana, commonly known as the gray beard grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky habitats, often in regions with a Mediterranean climate. The plant features slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that are often grayish in appearance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-gray hue that helps it reflect sunlight.

Aristida grisebachiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
