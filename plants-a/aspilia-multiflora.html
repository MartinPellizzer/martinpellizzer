Aspilia multiflora

Aspilia multiflora, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, daisy-like inflorescences composed of numerous small white to pale yellow ray florets surrounding a central disc of tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a basal rosette.

Aspilia multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
