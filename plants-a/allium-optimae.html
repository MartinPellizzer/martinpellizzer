Allium optimae

Allium optimae, commonly known as the Armenian garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous structure and grass-like foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers during the spring and summer seasons. Its bulbs are used in traditional medicine and cuisine, valued for their pungent aroma and potential health benefits.

Allium optimae thrives in well-drained soil and requires a cool climate for optimal growth.
