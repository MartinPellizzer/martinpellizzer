Acaena adscendens var. utrinqueglabrescens

Acaena adscendens var. utrinqueglabrescens, commonly known as the smooth thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a low-growing perennial with a mat-forming habit, characterized by its dense clusters of small, spiny fruits. The plant features alternate, hairy leaves that are often covered with fine, soft hairs, especially on the underside. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, appearing in early spring, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

This variety is distinguished by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves, setting it apart from other varieties of Acaena.
