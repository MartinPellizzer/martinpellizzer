Acalypha integrifolia subsp. marginata

Acalypha integrifolia subsp.

marginata, commonly known as the silverleaf ironweed, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive silver-white variegated leaves, which have a waxy texture and prominent veins.

The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or wetland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and hardy nature.
