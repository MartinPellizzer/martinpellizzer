Asparagopsis alba var. pastoriana

Asparagopsis alba var. pastoriana, commonly known as the white asparagus variety, is a cultivar of the asparagus plant characterized by its pale green to white stems.

It is botanically classified within the family Asparagaceae, and it is closely related to other asparagus species. The plant is typically grown in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its stems are harvested before they fully develop into the typical green asparagus, resulting in a unique flavor and texture.

This variety is prized for its culinary use and is often cultivated for its tender, mild-tasting shoots.
