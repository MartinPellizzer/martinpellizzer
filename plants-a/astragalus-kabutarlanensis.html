Astragalus kabutarlanensis

Astragalus kabutarlanensis, commonly known as the Kabutarlan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access moisture from lower soil layers.

Astragalus kabutarlanensis is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
