Acrodon Bellidiflorus Var. Striatus

Acrodon bellidiflorus var. striatus is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico. Its unique appearance features a tall, erect stem covered in striking, waxy leaves with a distinctive striped pattern. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically a vibrant red or orange, adding to its visual appeal. Due to its limited habitat and susceptibility to environmental changes, it is considered endangered. This species is of great interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
