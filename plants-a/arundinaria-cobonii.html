Arundinaria cobonii

Arundinaria cobonii, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height, forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically green or reddish in color. Its leaves are long, narrow, and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Arundinaria cobonii is native to China and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and resilience in various soil conditions.
