Anthemis macroglossa

Anthemis macroglossa, commonly known as the large-tongued anthemis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers with a distinctive, elongated central disk. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
