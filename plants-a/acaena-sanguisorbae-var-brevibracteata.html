Acaena sanguisorbae var. brevibracteata

Acaena sanguisorbae var. brevibracteata, commonly known as the short-bracted burrplant, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It features dense clusters of small, spiny, globe-shaped fruits that are characteristic of the genus Acaena. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in coastal and subalpine regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
