Anisostachya capuronii

Anisostachya capuronii, commonly known as the Capuron's reed, is a perennial grass native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, inflorescences that are often described as panicle-like, with delicate, feathery flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
