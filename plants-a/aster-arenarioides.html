Aster arenarioides

Aster arenarioides, commonly known as the sand aster, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
