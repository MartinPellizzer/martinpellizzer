Astragalus quisqualis f. nudicalycinus

Astragalus quisqualis f. nudicalycinus, commonly known as the naked-calyx milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers that appear in racemes. The plant has reduced calyces, giving it the "nudicalycinus" designation, which refers to its nearly leafless appearance. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphological features and potential ecological significance.
