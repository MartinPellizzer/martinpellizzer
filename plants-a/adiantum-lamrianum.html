Adiantum lamrianum

Adiantum lamrianum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and have a feathery appearance. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Adiantum lamrianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
