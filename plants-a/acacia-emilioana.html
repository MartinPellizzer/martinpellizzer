Acacia emilioana

Acacia emilioana, commonly known as the Emilio's acacia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features slender, spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. Its yellow flowers are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences, attracting various pollinators.

Acacia emilioana is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to harsh, dry environments.
