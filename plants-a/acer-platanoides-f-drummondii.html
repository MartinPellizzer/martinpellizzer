Acer platanoides f. drummondii

Acer platanoides f. drummondii, commonly known as the Drummond's Norway maple, is a variant of the Norway maple native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a serrated edge and a distinctive white to pale green underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged fruits that disperse its seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
