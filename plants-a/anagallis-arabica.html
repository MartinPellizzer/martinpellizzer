Anagallis arabica

Anagallis arabica, commonly known as the Arabic willow or Persian waterweed, is a flowering plant native to regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, trailing stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous areas. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that release seeds through mechanical means.

Anagallis arabica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
