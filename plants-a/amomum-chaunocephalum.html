Amomum chaunocephalum

Amomum chaunocephalum, commonly known as the black-headed cardamom, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in India, Nepal, and Bhutan. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark reddish-brown fruits that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often used in local cuisines for their distinctive flavor.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for both its medicinal properties and aromatic qualities.
