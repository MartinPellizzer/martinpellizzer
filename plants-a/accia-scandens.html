Accia scandens

Accia scandens, commonly known as the climbing violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing stems that can climb or trail along the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Accia scandens thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
