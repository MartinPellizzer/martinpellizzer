Achyranthes bidentata

Achyranthes bidentata, commonly known as Chinese knotweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in spikes, followed by clusters of three-winged seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
