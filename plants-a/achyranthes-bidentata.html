Achyranthes bidentata

Achyranthes bidentata, commonly known as Chinese knotweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in spikes, followed by clusters of three-winged seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.

Morphology

Achyranthes bidentata has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically 5-10 cm long and 2-4 cm wide, with a bluish-green upper surface and a lighter green underside.

The stem is erect, cylindrical, and smooth, with a light brown bark that becomes rougher with age. The flowers are small, greenish-white, and arranged in clusters, blooming from July to September, and are pollinated by wind. The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that splits open to release three elliptical seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are thick, fleshy, and often form a dense, fibrous network near the surface.

It is commonly found in a wide range of habitats, including forests, grasslands, and wastelands. The plant is well-adapted to tropical and subtropical climates. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its broad native distribution reflects its ecological versatility and adaptability across diverse environments.
