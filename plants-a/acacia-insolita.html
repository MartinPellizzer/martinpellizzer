Acacia insolita

Acacia insolita, commonly known as the rare desert acacia, is a shrub native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its survival in sparse habitats.

Acacia insolita plays a vital role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
