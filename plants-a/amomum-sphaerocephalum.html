Amomum sphaerocephalum

Amomum sphaerocephalum, commonly known as the spherical-headed amomum, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic rhizomes and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in inflorescences, which are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued in some cuisines for its spicy, aromatic flavor.
