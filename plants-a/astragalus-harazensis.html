Astragalus harazensis

Astragalus harazensis, commonly known as the Harazan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deep underground.

Astragalus harazensis is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
