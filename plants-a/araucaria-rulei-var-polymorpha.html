Araucaria rulei var. polymorpha

Araucaria rulei var. polymorpha, commonly known as the Brazilian pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, pyramidal crown composed of needle-like leaves. The plant produces cones that contain both male and female reproductive structures, typical of gymnosperms. Its foliage is often dark green and glossy, with a distinctive appearance that makes it a popular ornamental plant.

The species is known for its slow growth and longevity, with some trees living over a century.
