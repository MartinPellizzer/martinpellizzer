Anthemis tenuisecta

Anthemis tenuisecta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy surface, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

It is also known for its medicinal properties, historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
