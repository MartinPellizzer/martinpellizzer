Aechmea strobilina

Aechmea strobilina, commonly known as the pineapple bromeliad, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces striking, spire-like inflorescences that can reach several feet in height. Its leaves are typically broad, leathery, and have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Aechmea strobilina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage and unique floral structure.

Morphology

Aechmea strobilina has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, strap-like, and often have a waxy coating, arranged in a tight spiral around the central stem.

The leaves are typically dark green with a metallic sheen and can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant has a short, thick stem with a smooth, grayish-brown bark that grows upward from the base of the leaf rosette. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by colorful bracts, with small white to pink flowers that bloom during the summer months and are pollinated by insects.
