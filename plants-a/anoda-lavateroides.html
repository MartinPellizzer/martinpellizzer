Anoda lavateroides

Anoda lavateroides, commonly known as the purpletop wildflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Anoda lavateroides is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes used in restoration projects due to its ecological benefits.
