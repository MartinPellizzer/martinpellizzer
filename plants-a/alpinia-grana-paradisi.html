Alpinia grana-paradisi

Alpinia grana-paradisi, commonly known as the paradisiacal ginger, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known spices like ginger and turmeric. The plant features upright, rhizomatous stems and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to white, with a distinct, aromatic scent.
