Acer negundo var. californicum

Acer negundo var. californicum, commonly known as the California box elder, is a variant of the box elder tree native to California.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically three to five leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in riparian areas and along stream banks.

The California box elder is known for its adaptability and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its fast growth rate.
