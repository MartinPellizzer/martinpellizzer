Allium tsoongii

Allium tsoongii, commonly known as the Chinese leek or Chinese onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical bulbs and grass-like leaves. The plant produces umbel-like clusters of white or pale purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and often used in Chinese cuisine for their mild, onion-like flavor.

Allium tsoongii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental and edible plant in gardens.
