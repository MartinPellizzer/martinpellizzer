Anomalosicyos andreanus

Anomalosicyos andreanus, commonly known as the Andrean's horned cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems that are often branched and covered in a thick layer of white wax. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise desolate appearance. Its adaptations to extreme drought conditions include a deep root system and the ability to store water in its fleshy stems.

Despite its hardy nature, it is a rare and endangered species, requiring specific environmental conditions to thrive.
