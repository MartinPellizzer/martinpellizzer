Anthericum vittatum variegatum

Anthericum vittatum variegatum, commonly known as the variegated snake's head fritillary, is a striking perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings, adding to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, with a distinctive white or yellow throat. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This species is valued in gardens for its unique foliage and ephemeral floral display.
