Aristida vulgaris var. coerulescens

Aristida vulgaris var. coerulescens, commonly known as bluegrass lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and slender, tufted inflorescences that give it a delicate appearance. The plant typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a desirable choice for native landscaping and erosion control.

The species is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining soil stability in natural ecosystems.
