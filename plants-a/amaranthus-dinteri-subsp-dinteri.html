Amaranthus dinteri subsp. dinteri

Amaranthus dinteri subsp.

dinteri, commonly known as the Dinter's amaranth, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to harsh climatic conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and potential horticultural applications.
