Amaryllis equestris var. major

Amaryllis equestris var. major, commonly known as the giant amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of red, white, or pink. The plant produces strap-shaped, evergreen leaves that can grow up to three feet in length. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and container planting.

Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a dramatic floral display to the landscape.
