Albuca reflexa

Albuca reflexa, commonly known as the curly-tongue lily, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers with reflexed petals. The plant is adapted to arid conditions and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.

Albuca reflexa is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes other bulbous plants such as amaryllis and daffodils.
