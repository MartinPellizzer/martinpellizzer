Acrostichum costatum var. undulatum

Acrostichum costatum var. undulatum, commonly known as the undulating sword fern, is a variant of the sword fern species native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its long, arching fronds that have a distinctive undulating or wavy appearance along their edges. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and along stream banks. Its leaves are compound, with a serrated or toothed margin, and it produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
