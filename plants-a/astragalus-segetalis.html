Astragalus segetalis

Astragalus segetalis, commonly known as field milkvetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to Eurasia and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as fields and grasslands.

Astragalus segetalis is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant in certain environments.
