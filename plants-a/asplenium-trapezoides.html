Asplenium trapezoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spleenwort, is a member of the fern family (Polypodiaceae).

It is a small, epiphytic fern that typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in moist, shaded environments. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. Its fronds are usually dark green and possess a serrated or toothed margin.

Asplenium trapezoides is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid, shaded conditions.
