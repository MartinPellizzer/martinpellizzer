Abies inverta

Abies inverta, commonly known as the white fir, is a coniferous tree native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and its distinctive white bark, which becomes more prominent with age. The tree has needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color and are arranged spirally along the branches. Its cones are small and cylindrical, maturing to a reddish-brown color.

Abies inverta is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments and plays an important role in the ecosystems of the Andean mountain range.
