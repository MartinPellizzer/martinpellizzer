Anthoxanthum puelii var. lloydii

Anthoxanthum puelii var. lloydii, commonly known as the Lloyd's meadow grass, is a variant of the meadow grass species native to specific regions of South America.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender blades. The plant typically thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are clustered in panicles, and it produces small, pale yellow to golden florets.

Botanically, it is notable for its adaptability to wet conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
