Atriplex arabica

Atriplex arabica, commonly known as desert spinach, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are rich in nutrients, making it a valuable food source for both wildlife and humans. The plant thrives in saline and alkaline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its small, green flowers are clustered in spherical inflorescences, and it reproduces through wind pollination.

Atriplex arabica is also known for its ability to prevent soil erosion due to its extensive root system.
