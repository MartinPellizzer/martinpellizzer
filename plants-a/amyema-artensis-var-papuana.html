Amyema artensis var. papuana

Amyema artensis var. papuana, commonly known as the Papuan bottlebrush, is a rare and distinctive plant native to the rainforests of Papua, Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its unique, cylindrical flower spikes that resemble a bottlebrush. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that are clustered along the central axis, giving it a striking visual appearance. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and arranged in a dense, upright manner, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
