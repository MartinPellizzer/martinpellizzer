Atriplex varia

Atriplex varia, commonly known as the variable saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its silver-green foliage, which helps reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to minimize transpiration. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects.

Atriplex varia is often used in rehabilitation of degraded soils due to its tolerance to saline and drought conditions.
