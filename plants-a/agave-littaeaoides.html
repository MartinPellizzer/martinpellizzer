Agave littaeaoides

Agave littaeaoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The leaves are narrow and have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers that bloom once in its lifetime.

This plant is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
