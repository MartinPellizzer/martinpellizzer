Artanthe dimidiata

Artanthe dimidiata, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are arranged in dense, terminal clusters.

Artanthe dimidiata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
