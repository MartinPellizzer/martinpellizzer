Anthericum ciliatum

Anthericum ciliatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf arum, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a striking, umbrella-shaped inflorescence with a spathe that is typically greenish-white in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers within the spadix, which are pollinated by insects. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Anthericum ciliatum is often found in marshy areas and is adapted to thrive in humid, aquatic environments.
