Asperula aristata subsp. bosnensis

Asperula aristata subsp.

bosnensis, commonly known as Bosnian woodruff, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of white and pale pink.

The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its aromatic properties make it a valued plant in traditional herbal medicine and ornamental horticulture.
