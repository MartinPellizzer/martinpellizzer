Agrostis alba var. elata

Agrostis alba var. elata, commonly known as silver hairgrass, is a grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray blades that are often covered in a dense layer of hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. This perennial plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in stabilizing soil in wet environments.
