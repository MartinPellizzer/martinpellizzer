Anthobolus leptomeroides

Anthobolus leptomeroides, commonly known as the slender spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are surrounded by specialized leaf-like structures called bracts. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Anthobolus leptomeroides is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in dune environments.
