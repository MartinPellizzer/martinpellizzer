Aspidium excultum

Aspidium excultum, commonly known as the broad buckler fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds that are typically divided into two lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including countries such as India, China, and Japan. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Aspidium excultum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocks or in forest understories.
