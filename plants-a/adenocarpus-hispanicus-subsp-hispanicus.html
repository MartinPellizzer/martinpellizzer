Adenocarpus hispanicus subsp. hispanicus

Adenocarpus hispanicus subsp.

hispanicus, commonly known as the Spanish milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and sunny environments.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by inflated, seed-containing pods. Its leaves are pinnately divided, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinator populations.
