Acalypha communis subsp. apicalis

Acalypha communis subsp.

apicalis, commonly known as the red-veined nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive red veins on its heart-shaped leaves, which give it its common name.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.
