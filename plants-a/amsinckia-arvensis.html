Amsinckia arvensis

Amsinckia arvensis, commonly known as the field poppy, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features striking, bright yellow flowers with a distinctive saucer-shaped structure, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Amsinckia arvensis is known for its rapid growth and ability to produce a large number of seeds, contributing to its widespread distribution.
