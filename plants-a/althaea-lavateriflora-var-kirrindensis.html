Althaea lavateriflora var. kirrindensis

Althaea lavateriflora var. kirrindensis, commonly known as the Kirrindens althaea, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, soft pubescence. The plant produces showy, pale pink to lilac flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
