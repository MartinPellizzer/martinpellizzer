Antennaria neodioica var. attenuata

Antennaria neodioica var. attenuata, commonly known as the slender mouse-eared cow-wheat, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with hairy, linear leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. Its flowers are borne on slender, ascending pedicels, and the plant is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its dioecious nature, with separate male and female plants.
