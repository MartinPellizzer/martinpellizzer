Acalypha contermina

Acalypha contermina, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous true flowers surrounded by these vibrant, showy structures. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Acalypha contermina is a shrub that can grow up to several meters tall, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy.
