Athyrium filix-femina var. curtum-cristatum

Athyrium filix-femina var. curtum-cristatum, commonly known as the crested lady fern, is a variant of the lady fern characterized by its distinctive, crisped fronds.

It is a fern native to eastern Asia, including regions of China, Japan, and Korea. The plant features a delicate, upright rhizome with bipinnate leaves that are often curled or crisped at the edges. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
