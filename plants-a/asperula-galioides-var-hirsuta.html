Asperula galioides var. hirsuta

Asperula galioides var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy woodruff, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and rocky habitats.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, shade-tolerant plant.
