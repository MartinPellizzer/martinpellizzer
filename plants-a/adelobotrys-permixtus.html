Adelobotrys permixtus

Adelobotrys permixtus, commonly known as the mixed-flowered adelobotrys, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, elliptic leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, blooming in the spring and early summer. Its flowers are characterized by their unique arrangement, with both stamens and petals present in each floret, contributing to its ornamental value.

The plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
