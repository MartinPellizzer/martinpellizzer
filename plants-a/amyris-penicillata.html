Amyris penicillata

Amyris penicillata, commonly known as the pink torch or torch ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces striking clusters of pink to red flowers, which are borne on long, slender inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Amyris penicillata is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
