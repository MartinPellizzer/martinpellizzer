Alstroemeria pseudospathulata

Alstroemeria pseudospathulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, featuring strap-like leaves and showy blooms that attract pollinators. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value has led to its widespread use in floral arrangements and ornamental horticulture.
