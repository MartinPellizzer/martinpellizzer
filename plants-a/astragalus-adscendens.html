Astragalus adscendens

Astragalus adscendens, commonly known as the ascending milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to reddish-purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically visited by pollinators such as bees. Astragalus adscendens is known for its ability to thrive in arid and rocky environments, making it an important species in ecological restoration projects.

Its seeds are adapted for dispersal by wind, contributing to its distribution across rugged terrains.
