Acacia curvifolia

Acacia curvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has slender, twisted branches and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Acacia curvifolia is adapted to dry environments and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

It plays an important role in providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
