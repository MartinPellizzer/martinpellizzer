Astragalus krascheninnikovii

Astragalus krascheninnikovii, commonly known as the Krascheninnikov's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in late summer, typically arranged in racemes. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and rocky soils.

Astragalus krascheninnikovii is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in harsh, dry conditions.
