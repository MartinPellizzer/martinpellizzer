Astragalus comosus

Astragalus comosus, commonly known as the goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery seed heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink.

Astragalus comosus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
