Achillea decipiens

Achillea decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are basal, linear, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow to pale yellow in color.

It is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
