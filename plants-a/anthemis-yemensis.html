Anthemis yemensis

Anthemis yemensis, commonly known as Yemen chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Yemen.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in clusters.

The essential oils extracted from its flowers are used in traditional medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory and calming properties.
