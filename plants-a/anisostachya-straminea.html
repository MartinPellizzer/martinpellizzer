Anisostachya straminea

Anisostachya straminea, commonly known as the yellow panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves with a pale yellowish-green color. The plant produces fluffy, panicle-like inflorescences that are often bright yellow, giving it its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Anisostachya straminea is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
