Acaciella angustissima var. chisosiana

Acaciella angustissima var. chisosiana, commonly known as the Chisos acacia, is a shrub native to the Chisos Mountains of Texas.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing angular seeds. It is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

This variety is of interest to botanists and conservationists due to its limited distribution and ecological significance in the region.
