Azalea indica var. phoenicea

Azalea indica var. phoenicea, commonly known as the Chinese azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to China and Korea.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a striking contrast between the petals and the foliage. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its seasonal floral displays.
