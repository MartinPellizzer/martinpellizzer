Adesmia spinosa

Adesmia spinosa, commonly known as the spiny adesmia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bean-like pods containing seeds. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is adapted to arid conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
