Adiantum vogelii

Adiantum vogelii, commonly known as the Vogel's maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil to flourish. Its spores are produced on the underside of its fronds, and it reproduces through spore dispersal rather than seeds.

Adiantum vogelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
