Abutilon sonneratianum

Abutilon sonneratianum, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a flowering shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, ovate leaves and showy, lantern-shaped bracts that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by the distinctive papery bracts, which are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Abutilon sonneratianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
