Astragalus valdeviolaceus

Astragalus valdeviolaceus, commonly known as the violet-flowered milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, legume-like pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus valdeviolaceus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
