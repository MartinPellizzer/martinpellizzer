Amarenus speciosus

Amarenus speciosus, commonly known as the showy amaranth, is a striking annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, feather-like leaves that range in color from green to red and purple, depending on the variety. The plant produces clusters of small, bisexual flowers that are typically pink to red in color, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.

Amarenus speciosus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
