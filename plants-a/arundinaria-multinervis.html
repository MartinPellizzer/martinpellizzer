Arundinaria multinervis

Arundinaria multinervis, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery leaves that are often a striking golden color. This plant is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can grow in a variety of climates with adequate water.

Arundinaria multinervis is also valued for its use in traditional Chinese medicine and as a source of sustainable materials.
