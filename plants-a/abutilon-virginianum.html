Abutilon virginianum

Abutilon virginianum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Virginia abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features palmately lobed leaves with a soft, velvety texture, and it produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
