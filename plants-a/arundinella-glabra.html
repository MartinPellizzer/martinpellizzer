Arundinella glabra

Arundinella glabra, commonly known as the smooth bamboo or slender bamboo, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that are often smooth and glabrous, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in wetland or riparian habitats, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a grass-like appearance, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Arundinella glabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
