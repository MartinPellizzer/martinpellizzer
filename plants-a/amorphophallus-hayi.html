Amorphophallus hayi

Amorphophallus hayi, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant's flowers are typically pale yellow or white, with a strong, unpleasant odor that attracts insect pollinators. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its rhizome is sometimes used in traditional medicine, though it is not widely cultivated for commercial purposes.
