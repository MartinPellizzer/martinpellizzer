Astragalus microcephalus var. argaeus

Astragalus microcephalus var. argaeus, commonly known as the dwarf milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, sparsely hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia.

This variant is often found in mountainous regions and is of interest for its ecological and potential medicinal properties.
