Anthanotis viridis

Anthanotis viridis, commonly known as the green anthanot, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its foliage is characterized by narrow, grass-like leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings.

Anthanotis viridis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
