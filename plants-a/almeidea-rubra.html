Almeidea rubra

Almeidea rubra, commonly known as the red almeid, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red petals and compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Almeidea rubra is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
