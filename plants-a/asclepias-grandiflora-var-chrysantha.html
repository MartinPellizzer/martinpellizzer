Asclepias grandiflora var. chrysantha

Asclepias grandiflora var. chrysantha, commonly known as the yellow milkweed, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces a milky sap that contains latex, a feature common among milkweeds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a fuzzy texture.

This variety is often found in sandy or well-drained soils and is a host plant for monarch butterfly larvae.
