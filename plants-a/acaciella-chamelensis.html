Acaciella chamelensis

Acaciella chamelensis, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of northern South America.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, feathery leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in dry conditions. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, which bloom during the rainy season. Its seeds are enclosed in pod-like fruits that split open when mature, dispersing the seeds by wind.

Acaciella chamelensis is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is well-suited to harsh, semi-arid environments.
