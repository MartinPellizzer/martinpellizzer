Ariocarpus × nelissae

Ariocarpus × nelissae, commonly known as the hybrid moon cactus, is a rare and unique hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Ariocarpus fissuratus and Ariocarpus retusus.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored, upper portion known as the pseudostem, which is actually a grafted part of a different species. The lower, root-like structure is typically a green, succulent base that provides the plant with essential nutrients. This plant is native to the arid regions of northern Mexico and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Due to its rarity and horticultural appeal, Ariocarpus × nelissae is highly sought after by plant enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
