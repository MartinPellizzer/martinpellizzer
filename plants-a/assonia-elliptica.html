Assonia elliptica

Assonia elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandmat, is a perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. Assonia elliptica is adapted to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant.

It plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
