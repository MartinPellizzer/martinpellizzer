Andropogon tonkinensis

Andropogon tonkinensis, commonly known as Tonkin bluestem, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Andropogon tonkinensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
