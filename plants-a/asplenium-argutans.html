Asplenium argutans

Asplenium argutans, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green in color. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help it spread through vegetative propagation.

Asplenium argutans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and indoor spaces.
