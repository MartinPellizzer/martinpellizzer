Amauropelta mucosa

Amauropelta mucosa, commonly known as the sticky shield fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are covered in a fine, sticky resin. The plant's leaves are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, which helps it retain moisture in its humid environment. It thrives in shaded, moist areas and is often found in forest understories.

The resinous coating on its fronds may serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores and pathogens.
