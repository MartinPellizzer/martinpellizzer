Adenanthera bonplandiana

Adenanthera bonplandiana, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a leguminous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree's pods contain seeds that are encased in a fleshy, reddish pulp, which is edible and has a tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry forest ecosystems.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some regions for food and medicine.
