Annona haitiensis

Annona haitiensis, commonly known as the Haitian custard apple, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Haiti.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves and is notable for its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit, which is edible, has a smooth, yellowish skin and a creamy, sweet pulp with a mild aroma.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses in local cultures.
