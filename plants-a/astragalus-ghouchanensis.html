Astragalus ghouchanensis

Astragalus ghouchanensis, commonly known as Ghouchan milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is known for its deep penetration, which helps it access water from deeper soil layers.

This species is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in soil stabilization and potential medicinal properties.
