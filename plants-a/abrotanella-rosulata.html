Abrotanella rosulata

Abrotanella rosulata, commonly known as the clustered rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub with silver-gray foliage and is characterized by its dense, rosette-like growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer. It is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
