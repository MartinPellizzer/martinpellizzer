Alopecurus fulvus f. violaceus

Alopecurus fulvus f. violaceus, commonly known as the violet false oat, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, violet-colored inflorescences that distinguish it from other members of the species. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the flowers are arranged in spikelets that are borne on long, slender stalks.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value due to its distinctive flower color and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
