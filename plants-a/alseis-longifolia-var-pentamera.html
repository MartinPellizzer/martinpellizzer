Alseis longifolia var. pentamera

Alseis longifolia var. pentamera, commonly known as the Pacific silver fir, is a variant of the Pacific silver fir, native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree that typically grows in moist, cool climates at high elevations. The plant has needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged spirally along the branches. Its cones are small and often clustered, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to mountainous environments and its role in forest ecosystems.
