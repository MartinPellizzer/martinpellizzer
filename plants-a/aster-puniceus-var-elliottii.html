Aster puniceus var. elliottii

Aster puniceus var. elliottii, commonly known as the Elliott's purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Its conical inflorescence is composed of numerous small, tubular florets surrounded by showy bracts.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
