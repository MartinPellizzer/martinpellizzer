Argyreia obtusifolia

Argyreia obtusifolia, commonly known as the holy leaf or sacred leaf, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by spherical, red berries that are edible. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is also cultivated for its potential health benefits.
