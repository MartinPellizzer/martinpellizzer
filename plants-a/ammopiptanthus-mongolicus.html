Ammopiptanthus mongolicus

Ammopiptanthus mongolicus, commonly known as the Mongolian broom.

It is a small, deciduous shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of northern China. The plant is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are compound, with numerous small leaflets, and it produces pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Ammopiptanthus mongolicus is well adapted to harsh environments, making it an important species for soil stabilization and ecological restoration.
