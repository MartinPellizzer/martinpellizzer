Aphelandra sneidernii

Aphelandra sneidernii, commonly known as the sacred flame plant, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that bear bright yellow bracts, which resemble flames, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often have a waxy texture with a glossy appearance.

Aphelandra sneidernii thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to flourish.
