Acacia undulifolia

Acacia undulifolia, commonly known as the wavy-leaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that have a wavy or undulating appearance, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting various pollinators. Acacia undulifolia is well adapted to drought and can thrive in poor soils, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

It is also valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in landscaping in suitable climates.
