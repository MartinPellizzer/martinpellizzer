Adicea modesta

Adicea modesta, commonly known as the modest lily, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with strap-like leaves that are broad and lance-shaped. Its blooming period occurs during the summer months, and it thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.

Adicea modesta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
