Andropogon crispifolius

Andropogon crispifolius, commonly known as the curly leaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, blue-green leaves that are often curled at the edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Andropogon crispifolius plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
