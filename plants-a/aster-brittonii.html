Aster brittonii

Aster brittonii, commonly known as the Britton's aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features showy blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Aster brittonii is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
