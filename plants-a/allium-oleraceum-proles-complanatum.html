Allium oleraceum proles complanatum

Allium oleraceum proles complanatum, commonly known as the flat-leaved garlic.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. The plant features broad, flat, grass-like leaves and produces small, bulbous flowers in shades of pink to purple. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a bulbous base that stores nutrients.

It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild garlic-like scent.
