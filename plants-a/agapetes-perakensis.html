Agapetes perakensis

Agapetes perakensis, commonly known as the Perak heather, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Peninsular Malaysia, particularly in the states of Perak and Kedah. This evergreen shrub typically grows at elevations between 1,000 and 2,000 meters above sea level. Its foliage consists of small, leathery leaves that are densely packed, giving it a compact appearance.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in the cooler months.
