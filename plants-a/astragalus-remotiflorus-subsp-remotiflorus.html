Astragalus remotiflorus subsp. remotiflorus

Astragalus remotiflorus subsp.

remotiflorus, commonly known as the remote-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its root system is typically fibrous and deep. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
