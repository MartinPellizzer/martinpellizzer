Andropogon tenuiculus

Andropogon tenuiculus, commonly known as narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant has slender, blade-like leaves that are usually less than an inch wide, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers that are often tinged with blue or purple.

Andropogon tenuiculus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
