Argemone munita subsp. munita

Argemone munita subsp.

munita, commonly known as the Mexican prickly poppy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers with prominent red-orange markings.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
