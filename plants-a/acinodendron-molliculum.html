Acinodendron molliculum

Acinodendron molliculum, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds covered in a fine, silky coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
