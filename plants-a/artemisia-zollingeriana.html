Artemisia zollingeriana

Artemisia zollingeriana, commonly known as Zollinger's wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flower heads. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, have been used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and digestive properties.

Artemisia zollingeriana is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
