Acer changhuaense var. glabrescens

Acer changhuaense var. glabrescens, commonly known as the Changhua glabrous maple, is a deciduous tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and vibrant autumn foliage. The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a serrated margin and a glossy green surface. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and parks for its seasonal color changes.
