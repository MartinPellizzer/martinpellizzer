Abildgaardia polycephala

Abildgaardia polycephala, commonly known as the red-tipped photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn red in the autumn. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

Its adaptability and seasonal color changes make it a popular choice for gardeners.
