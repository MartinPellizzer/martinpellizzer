Argemone albiflora subsp. texana

Argemone albiflora subsp.

texana, commonly known as Texas pricklypoppy, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, lobed leaves.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain oily seeds, which are a key feature of the genus Argemone. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments.

This species is adapted to arid conditions and is drought-tolerant.
