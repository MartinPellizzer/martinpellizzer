Achnatherum parishii subsp. depauperatum

Achnatherum parishii subsp.

depauperatum, commonly known as the sparseleaf needlegrass, is a grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of western North America. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is adapted to drought conditions and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats such as grasslands, dunes, and disturbed areas.
