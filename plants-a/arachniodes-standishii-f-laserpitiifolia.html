Arachniodes standishii f. laserpitiifolia

Arachniodes standishii f. laserpitiifolia, commonly known as the laserpitiifolia fern, is a rare and striking fern species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its large, intricate fronds that can reach up to 1.5 meters in length, with a distinctive, lace-like appearance due to its finely divided pinnae. The plant features a deep green, leathery rachis and fertile spore-bearing structures that emerge from the fronds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after by horticulturists and conservationists alike.
