Anacyclus divaricatus

Anacyclus divaricatus, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with a taproot system that allows it to survive in dry conditions. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow flowers in early spring. Its flowers are followed by spherical, spiky seed heads that disperse seeds via the wind.

Anacyclus divaricatus is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
