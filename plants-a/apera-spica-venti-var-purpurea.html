Apera spica-venti var. purpurea

Apera spica-venti var. purpurea, commonly known as purple foxtail, is a variant of the common foxtail grass.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescence that turns a striking purplish hue as it matures. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its slender leaves are typically a bright green, and it can grow up to 60 cm in height.

This variety is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive coloration.
