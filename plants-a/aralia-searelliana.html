Aralia searelliana

Aralia searelliana, commonly known as the silver aralia, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with a silvery sheen, which are typically divided into numerous narrow, pointed segments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in an umbel, followed by dark blue berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in woodland gardens for its ornamental value.

Its foliage provides year-round interest, making it a popular choice for shade gardens.
