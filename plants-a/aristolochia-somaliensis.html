Aristolochia somaliensis

Aristolochia somaliensis, commonly known as the Somali Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the Horn of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, and often fragrant flowers that resemble a pipe, which is a common trait among the Aristolochiaceae family. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and has a climbing or shrub growth habit. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in woodland or shrubland environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
