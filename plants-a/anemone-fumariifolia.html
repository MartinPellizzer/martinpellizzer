Anemone fumariifolia

Anemone fumariifolia, commonly known as the smoke anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, white, bell-shaped flowers that resemble smoke, hence its common name. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Anemone fumariifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
