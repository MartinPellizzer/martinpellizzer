Apinagia fimbrifolia

Apinagia fimbrifolia, commonly known as the fringeleaf buckthorn, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a small shrub characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that often have a fringed or lobed appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and other arid environments.

Apinagia fimbrifolia plays a role in providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
