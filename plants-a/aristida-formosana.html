Aristida formosana

Aristida formosana, commonly known as the Formosan threeawn, is a species of grass native to Taiwan.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, with a rough texture.

Aristida formosana is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
