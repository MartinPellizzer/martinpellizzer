Aeschynomene mimosoides

Aeschynomene mimosoides, commonly known as the mimosa tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched, a response known as thigmonastic movement. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, followed by pods that contain several seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of soil types, making it an adaptable species in different environments.
