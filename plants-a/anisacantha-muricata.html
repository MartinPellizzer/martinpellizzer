Anisacantha muricata

Anisacantha muricata, commonly known as the spiny devil's backbone, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its spiny stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, red fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Its unique appearance and adaptations make it well-suited to its native habitat.
