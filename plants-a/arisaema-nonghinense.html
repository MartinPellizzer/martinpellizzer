Arisaema nonghinense

Arisaema nonghinense, commonly known as the Nonghin arrowleaf, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are arrow-shaped, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in traditional medicine.
