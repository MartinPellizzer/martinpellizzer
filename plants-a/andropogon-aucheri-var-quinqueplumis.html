Andropogon aucheri var. quinqueplumis

Andropogon aucheri var. quinqueplumis, commonly known as the five-branched panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and along stream banks. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

The variety name "quinqueplumis" refers to the five-branched structure of its flowering spikes, a distinguishing botanical feature.
