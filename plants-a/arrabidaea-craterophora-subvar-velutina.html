Arrabidaea craterophora subvar. velutina

Arrabidaea craterophora subvar. velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf arrabidaea, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming during the dry season. Its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature, contributing to its dispersal.

This species is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
