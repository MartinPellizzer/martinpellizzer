Aretia alpina

Aretia alpina, commonly known as the alpine aretia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, dry conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in a head.

Aretia alpina is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
