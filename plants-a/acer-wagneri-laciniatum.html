Acer wagneri laciniatum

Acer wagneri laciniatum, commonly known as the Wagner's maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a serrated edge. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds known as samaras. Its foliage turns vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
