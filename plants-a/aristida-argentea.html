Aristida argentea

Aristida argentea, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that reflects light, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and prairies. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

Aristida argentea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
