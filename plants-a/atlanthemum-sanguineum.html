Atlanthemum sanguineum

Atlanthemum sanguineum, commonly known as the bloodflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals that resemble drops of blood, giving it its common name. The plant features a compact, bushy growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Amaryllidaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.
