Anaphalis sordida

Anaphalis sordida, commonly known as the gray gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of China and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers arranged in a clustered inflorescence, typical of the aster family. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Anaphalis sordida is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
