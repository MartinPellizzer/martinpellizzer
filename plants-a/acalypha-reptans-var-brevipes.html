Acalypha reptans var. brevipes

Acalypha reptans var. brevipes, commonly known as the creeping spurflower, is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features slender, trailing stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground or climb supports. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are often overlooked but contribute to its ornamental value. Its leaves are elliptical, slightly hairy, and have a velvety texture, adding a tactile interest to gardens.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ability to cover ground and prevent erosion.
