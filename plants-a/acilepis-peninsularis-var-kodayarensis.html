Acilepis peninsularis var. kodayarensis

Acilepis peninsularis var. kodayarensis, commonly known as the Kodayar acilepis, is a rare flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats within dense forest understories. Its floral structure is adapted for pollination by insects, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
