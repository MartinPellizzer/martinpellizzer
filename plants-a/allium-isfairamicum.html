Allium isfairamicum

Allium isfairamicum, commonly known as the Isfahan garlic, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Iran, particularly around the city of Isfahan. The plant produces bulbous structures similar to garlic, which are used both as a spice and for medicinal purposes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it blooms with small, white or pale purple flowers in the spring.

The plant is valued for its strong aroma and potential health benefits.
