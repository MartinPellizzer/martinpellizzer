Anthemis aizoides

Anthemis aizoides, commonly known as the lesser tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are aromatic and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and bloom in summer.

Anthemis aizoides has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
