Anthemis tubicina

Anthemis tubicina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been historically used in herbal medicine.
