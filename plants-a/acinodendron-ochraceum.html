Acinodendron ochraceum

Acinodendron ochraceum, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in local medicine for their purported healing properties.

Acinodendron ochraceum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
