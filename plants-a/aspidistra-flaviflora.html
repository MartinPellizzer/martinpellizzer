Aspidistra flaviflora

Aspidistra flaviflora, commonly known as the golden-flowered aspidistra, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which is characterized by its unique inflorescence structure known as a spadix, surrounded by a spathe. The plant features broad, dark green, strap-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged in a rosette pattern. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered around the spadix, giving it its common name.

Aspidistra flaviflora is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant.
