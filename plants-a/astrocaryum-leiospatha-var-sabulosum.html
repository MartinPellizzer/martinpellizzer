Astrocaryum leiospatha var. sabulosum

Astrocaryum leiospatha var. sabulosum, commonly known as the Brazilian wax palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, coconut-like fruits that are important for both wildlife and local communities. It thrives in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil and is often found in swampy or wetland areas.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
