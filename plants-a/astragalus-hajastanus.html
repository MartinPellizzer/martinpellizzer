Astragalus hajastanus

Astragalus hajastanus, commonly known as the Armenian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, typical of the genus Astragalus. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine and steppe environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
