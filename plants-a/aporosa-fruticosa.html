Aporosa fruticosa

Aporosa fruticosa, commonly known as the Chinese hibiscus or wild hibiscus, is a shrub native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pink or red, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.
