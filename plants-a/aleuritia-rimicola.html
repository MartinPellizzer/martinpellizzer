Aleuritia rimicola

Aleuritia rimicola, commonly known as the mountain elderberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible berries that ripen in the summer. Aleuritia rimicola thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native range.
