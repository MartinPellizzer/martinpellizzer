Aureolaria pedicularia subsp. carolinensis

Aureolaria pedicularia subsp.

carolinensis, commonly known as Carolina catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and woodland edges.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution across its native range.
