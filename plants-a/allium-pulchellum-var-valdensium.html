Allium pulchellum var. valdensium

Allium pulchellum var. valdensium, commonly known as the Valdensian garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces bulbs that are often used in culinary applications, similar to other allium species. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable ornamental and edible plant for gardens.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and valuable for its aromatic properties.
