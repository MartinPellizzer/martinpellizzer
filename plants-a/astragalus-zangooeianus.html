Astragalus zangooeianus

Astragalus zangooeianus, commonly known as Zangoeian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access moisture from lower soil layers.

Astragalus zangooeianus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
