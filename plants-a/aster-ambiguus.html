Aster ambiguus

Aster ambiguus, commonly known as the gray-leaf aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, hairy stems and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of purple and white. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, grayish hairs. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas with well-drained soil.

Aster ambiguus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
