Abies forrestii subsp. chengii

Abies forrestii subsp.

chengii, commonly known as the Cheng's fir, is a rare and endemic coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China. It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are densely packed along its branches.

The cones of this subspecies are typically small and often hang downward, a feature that distinguishes it from other fir species. It thrives in cool, moist climates at high altitudes, often growing in mixed forests with other conifers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
