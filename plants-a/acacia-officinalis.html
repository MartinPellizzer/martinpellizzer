Acacia officinalis

Acacia officinalis, commonly known as the medicinal acacia, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow, globular flower heads. The plant produces a resinous gum that has been used in traditional medicine for its healing properties. Its botanical features include a woody stem, small leaves, and clusters of yellow flowers.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
