Acorus calamus subsp. vulgaris

Acorus calamus subsp.

vulgaris, commonly known as sweet flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas. It features narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are usually green on top and lighter in color underneath.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a spadix, which is enclosed by a leaf-like bract. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found near water bodies.

Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.
