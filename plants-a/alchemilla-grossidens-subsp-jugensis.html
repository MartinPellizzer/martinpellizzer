Alchemilla grossidens subsp. jugensis

Alchemilla grossidens subsp.

jugensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It features basal leaves that are narrow and elongated, often with a distinctive waxy coating.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, arranged in loose clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to tolerate wet conditions.
