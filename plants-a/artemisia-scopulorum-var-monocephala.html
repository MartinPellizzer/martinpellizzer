Artemisia scopulorum var. monocephala

Artemisia scopulorum var. monocephala, commonly known as the big sagebrush, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and is often found in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a strong, pungent scent.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
