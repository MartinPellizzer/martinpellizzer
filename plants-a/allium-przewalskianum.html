Allium przewalskianum

Allium przewalskianum, commonly known as the Przewalski's garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic bulbs, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium przewalskianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
