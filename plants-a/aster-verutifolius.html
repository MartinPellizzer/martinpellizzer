Aster verutifolius

Aster verutifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aster, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
