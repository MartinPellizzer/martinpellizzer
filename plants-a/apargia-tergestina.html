Apargia tergestina

Apargia tergestina, commonly known as the Sicilian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe, particularly Sicily.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Apargia tergestina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
