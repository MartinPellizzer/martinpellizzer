Aster hyssopifolius var. canus

Aster hyssopifolius var. canus, commonly known as the white aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage and produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly aromatic scent when crushed.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
