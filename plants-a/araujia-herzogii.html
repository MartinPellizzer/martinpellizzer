Araujia herzogii

Araujia herzogii, commonly known as the moonflower vine, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night and emit a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces fruit that splits open to reveal bright red seeds. It is a climbing vine that can grow up to 10 meters in length.

Due to its nocturnal blooming habit, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates.
