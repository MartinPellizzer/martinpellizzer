Areca nigra

Areca nigra, commonly known as the black areca, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark purple to black fruits. Areca nigra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable species in tropical horticulture.
