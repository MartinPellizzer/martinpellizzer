Aconitum nemorum subsp. saposhnikovii

Aconitum nemorum subsp.

saposhnikovii, commonly known as the Siberian monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple hooded flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant produces showy, spurred flowers in late summer.

It is noted for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
