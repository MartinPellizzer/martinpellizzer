Achnatherum calamagrostis subsp. mesatlasicum

Achnatherum calamagrostis subsp.

mesatlasicum, commonly known as the Mesatlas steppe grass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in open, dry habitats such as steppes and grasslands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct midrib, and it is often found in low vegetation communities.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small fauna.
