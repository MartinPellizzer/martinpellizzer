Aponogeton tofus

Aponogeton tofus, commonly known as the bladder spikerush, is a aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, narrow leaves that are often submerged, with a distinctive swollen base that resembles a bladder, hence its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically borne on short, erect spikes above the water surface. It is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and marshes.

Aponogeton tofus is valued in aquariums for its ornamental appearance and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
