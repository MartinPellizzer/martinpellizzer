Aloe trothae

Aloe trothae, commonly known as the Troth's aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, spiny leaves that store water, adapting to arid environments. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

Aloe trothae thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
