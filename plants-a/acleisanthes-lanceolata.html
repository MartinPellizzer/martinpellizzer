Acleisanthes lanceolata

Acleisanthes lanceolata, commonly known as the slender honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and opposite leaves that are lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
