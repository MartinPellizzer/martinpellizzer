Arundinaria parvifolia

Arundinaria parvifolia, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, narrow leaves that give it a delicate appearance. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps.

Arundinaria parvifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
