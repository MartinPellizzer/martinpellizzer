Acacia kosiensis

Acacia kosiensis, commonly known as the Kosi acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-green globular flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, woody pods that split open to release the seeds.

Acacia kosiensis is adapted to dry environments and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.
