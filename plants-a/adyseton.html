Adyseton

Adyseton, commonly known as the golden lily, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, waxy texture.

Adyseton is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
