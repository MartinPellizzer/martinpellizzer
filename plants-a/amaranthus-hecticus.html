Amaranthus hecticus

Amaranthus hecticus, commonly known as wild amaranth, is an annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces dense clusters of small, green or red flowers that develop into fluffy, three-lobed seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as fields and roadsides.

Due to its adaptability and nutritional value, it is also cultivated as a food crop in some regions.
