Aster nevinii

Aster nevinii, commonly known as the Nevin's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, often with a yellow center.

Aster nevinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
