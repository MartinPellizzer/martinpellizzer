Aronia ovalis

Aronia ovalis, commonly known as the oval chokeberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that transition from green to red as they ripen, typically in late summer. Aronia ovalis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a hardy and resilient species.
