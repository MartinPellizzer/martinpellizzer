Anthemis schischkiniana

Anthemis schischkiniana, commonly known as the Schischkin's tansy, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features finely divided, feathery leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often grown as an ornamental plant for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Due to its low maintenance and drought tolerance, it is suitable for rock gardens and borders.
