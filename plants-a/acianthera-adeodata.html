Acianthera adeodata

Acianthera adeodata, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its roots are epiphytic, meaning they attach to tree trunks without parasitizing them.

The flowers are fragrant and attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
