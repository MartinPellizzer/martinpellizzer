Ateleia glabrata

Ateleia glabrata, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood and aromatic properties. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers. Its fruit is a legume pod containing flat, winged seeds.

Due to its unique appearance and valuable timber, it is often cultivated for ornamental and commercial purposes.
