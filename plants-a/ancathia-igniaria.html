Ancathia igniaria

Ancathia igniaria, commonly known as the flameleaf plant, is a striking species native to the Mediterranean region.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a vibrant red or orange hue, which intensify in sunlight. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its botanical structure includes a woody stem with smooth, greyish bark.

Ancathia igniaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
