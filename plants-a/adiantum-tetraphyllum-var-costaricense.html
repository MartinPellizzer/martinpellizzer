Adiantum tetraphyllum var. costaricense

Adiantum tetraphyllum var. costaricense, commonly known as the Costarican maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to Central America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, bi- or tri-pinnate fronds that are typically bright green and glossy. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its fronds are often adorned with small, sporangia clusters along the margins of the pinnae.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in shaded gardens or as a houseplant.
