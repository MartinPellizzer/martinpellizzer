Anthyllis vulneraria subsp. rubriflora

Anthyllis vulneraria subsp.

rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to rocky and coastal areas of Europe. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bright red flowers.

The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to harsh environments, often growing in well-drained, calcareous soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
