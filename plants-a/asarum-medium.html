Asarum medium

Asarum medium, commonly known as Indian snakeroot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features a thick, fleshy rhizome and heart-shaped, reddish-purple leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a spike, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Asarum medium is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
