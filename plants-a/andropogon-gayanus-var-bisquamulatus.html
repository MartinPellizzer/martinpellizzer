Andropogon gayanus var. bisquamulatus

Andropogon gayanus var. bisquamulatus, commonly known as the African grass or bisquamulatus grass, is a variant of the tallgrass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, elongated leaves with a distinctive two-toned appearance on the leaf sheaths. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as savannas and grasslands, where it thrives in full sun and well-drained soils. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it valuable for erosion control.

This variant is also used in traditional practices and as a forage crop for livestock in certain regions.
