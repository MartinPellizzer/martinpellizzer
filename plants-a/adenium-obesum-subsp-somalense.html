Adenium obesum subsp. somalense

Adenium obesum subsp.

somalense, commonly known as the "Somalense Desert Trumpet," is a succulent shrub native to the arid regions of Somalia and southern Arabia. It is characterized by its swollen, bottle-shaped trunk and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments.

The plant produces striking red or pink flowers that bloom during the rainy season, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant is also cultivated as an ornamental species.

Due to its unique morphology and adaptability, it is a subject of interest in both botanical research and horticulture.
