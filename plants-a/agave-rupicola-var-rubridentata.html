Agave rupicola var. rubridentata

Agave rupicola var. rubridentata, commonly known as the red-toothed agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color with red or purple margins. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears small, white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
