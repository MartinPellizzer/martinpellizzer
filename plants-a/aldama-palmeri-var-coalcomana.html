Aldama palmeri var. coalcomana

Aldama palmeri var. coalcomana, commonly known as the Coalcoman Aldama, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
