Anacamptis pyramidalis var. purpuralis

Anacamptis pyramidalis var. purpuralis, commonly known as the Purple Pyramidal Orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of spurred flowers, which are pollinated by various insects.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its striking color and elegant form.
