Airosperma trichotomum

Airosperma trichotomum, commonly known as the three-branched air spore plant, is a unique member of the Lepidopygidae family.

It is characterized by its specialized structure that allows for the dispersal of its minute, airborne spores. The plant lacks traditional roots, stems, and leaves, instead relying on a network of filaments for nutrient absorption. Its reproductive strategy involves the production of trichotomous spores, which are divided into three distinct branches.

This adaptation enables it to thrive in nutrient-poor environments by maximizing spore dispersal efficiency.
