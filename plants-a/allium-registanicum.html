Allium registanicum

Allium registanicum, commonly known as the Registan garlic, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its robust, fleshy bulbs and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand harsh, dry conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
