Aster canadensis

Aster canadensis, commonly known as New England aster, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its showy purple to pink flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and thrives in full sun, making it a common sight in meadows and woodland edges.
