Alsine graminifolia var. rosanoi

Alsine graminifolia var. rosanoi, commonly known as Rosano's sand-verbena, is a low-growing annual plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. It is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and open fields.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
