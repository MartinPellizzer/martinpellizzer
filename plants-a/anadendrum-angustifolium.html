Anadendrum angustifolium

Anadendrum angustifolium, commonly known as the narrowleaf coralbean, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant features small, narrow leaves and produces vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Anadendrum angustifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
