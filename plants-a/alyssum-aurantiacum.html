Alyssum aurantiacum

Alyssum aurantiacum, commonly known as the orange alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum aurantiacum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects such as bees and butterflies.
