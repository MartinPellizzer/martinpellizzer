Armeria chilensis var. bella

Armeria chilensis var. bella, commonly known as the Chilean thrift, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Chile.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in early spring. The plant has linear, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in maritime climates.
