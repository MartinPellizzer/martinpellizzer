Argentipallium obtusifolium

Argentipallium obtusifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf mallow, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, woolly foliage and showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a distinctive soft, fuzzy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in xeriscaping.
