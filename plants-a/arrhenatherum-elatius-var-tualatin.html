Arrhenatherum elatius var. tualatin

Arrhenatherum elatius var. tualatin, commonly known as Tualatin reedgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes, stream banks, and other moist soils.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
