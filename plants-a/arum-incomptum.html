Arum incomptum

Arum incomptum, commonly known as the slender arum, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as marshes and swamps. Its leaves are arrow-shaped and often have a reddish or purple tint along the veins.

The flowers are primarily pollinated by insects, and the plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
