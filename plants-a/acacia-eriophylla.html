Acacia eriophylla

Acacia eriophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, feathery leaves and yellow flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its reduced leaf surface area. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.

The wood of Acacia eriophylla is lightweight and has been used traditionally by Indigenous Australians for various purposes.
