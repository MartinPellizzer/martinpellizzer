Allium tschimganicum

Allium tschimganicum, commonly known as the Crimean garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pinkish flowers in umbels. The plant is characterized by its strong garlic scent and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its hardiness, it is also valued as a medicinal plant in traditional herbal remedies.
