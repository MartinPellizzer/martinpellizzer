Achillea millefolium subsp. lanata

Achillea millefolium subsp.

lanata, commonly known as cloudless tansy, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves and flat, yellow flower heads that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in meadows and dry grasslands. Its common name is derived from its fluffy, cloud-like appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
