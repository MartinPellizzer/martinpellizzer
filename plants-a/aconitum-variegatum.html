Aconitum variegatum

Aconitum variegatum, commonly known as the variegated monkshood, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear large, hood-shaped flowers in shades of purple, blue, or white. The plant's leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, almost lace-like appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
