Alstroemeria orchidioides

Alstroemeria orchidioides, commonly known as the orchid lily, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces multiple blooms during the growing season.

Alstroemeria orchidioides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a cut flower.
