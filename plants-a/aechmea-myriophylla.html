Aechmea myriophylla

Aechmea myriophylla, commonly known as the "Rainbow Bromeliad," is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant's foliage is often brightly colored, ranging from red to orange, with a striking green base. It produces showy, long-lasting inflorescences that can last for several months.

Aechmea myriophylla thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil and regular watering.

Morphology

Aechmea myriophylla has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, sword-like, and arranged in a tight spiral, with a waxy blue-green color and a sharp, pointed tip.

The stem is short and often hidden beneath the leaves, with a smooth, pale green surface. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with small, violet flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn, and are pollinated by insects. The fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds through wind dispersal.

The roots are fibrous and form a dense network near the soil surface, aiding in water absorption and anchoring the plant.
