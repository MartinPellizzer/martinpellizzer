Asclepias curassavica f. flaviflora

Asclepias curassavica f. flaviflora, commonly known as the yellow milkweed, is a variant of the common milkweed species.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which contrast with its green, heart-shaped leaves. This plant is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm climates. It produces a milky sap that contains latex, typical of the Asclepias genus.

The yellow milkweed is often cultivated as an ornamental plant and also serves as a host for monarch butterfly larvae.
