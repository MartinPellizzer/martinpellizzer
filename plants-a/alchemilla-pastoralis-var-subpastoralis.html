Alchemilla pastoralis var. subpastoralis

Alchemilla pastoralis var. subpastoralis, commonly known as the lesser lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
