Acalypha vermifera

Acalypha vermifera, commonly known as the threadleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Acalypha vermifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
