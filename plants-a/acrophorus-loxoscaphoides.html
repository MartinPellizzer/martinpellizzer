Acrophorus loxoscaphoides

Acrophorus loxoscaphoides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed acanthus, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and spikes of showy, two-lipped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant floral displays, which range from purple to pink hues.

Acrophorus loxoscaphoides is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
