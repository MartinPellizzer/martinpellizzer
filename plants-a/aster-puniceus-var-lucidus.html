Aster puniceus var. lucidus

Aster puniceus var. lucidus, commonly known as the shining aster, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with purple-blue petals and a yellow center, blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is prized for its vibrant color and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
