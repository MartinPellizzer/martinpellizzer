Adesmia curvifolia

Adesmia curvifolia, commonly known as the hooked leaf tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curved, and often hooked leaflets, which give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. Its flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in racemes, and it produces pods that contain seeds.

Adesmia curvifolia is valued for its ornamental leaves and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
