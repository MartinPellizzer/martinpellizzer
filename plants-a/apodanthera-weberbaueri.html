Apodanthera weberbaueri

Apodanthera weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's apodanthra, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apodanthaceae.

It is a hemiparasitic plant, meaning it partially depends on other plants for water and nutrients. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and a unique structure where its flowers are borne on the stems of its host plant. It is native to regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile.

Due to its specialized growth habit and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
