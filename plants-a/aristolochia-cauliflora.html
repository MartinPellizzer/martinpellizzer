Aristolochia cauliflora

Aristolochia cauliflora, commonly known as the tropical Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are often purple or greenish in color. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters at the base of the stems, a feature known as cauliflorous growth.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
