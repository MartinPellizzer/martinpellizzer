Arnica tussilaginea

Arnica tussilaginea, commonly known as the meadow arnica, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are basal, hairy, and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
