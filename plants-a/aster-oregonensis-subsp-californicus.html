Aster oregonensis subsp. californicus

Aster oregonensis subsp.

californicus, commonly known as the California aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its showy, purple to blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This subspecies is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and ecological importance to pollinators.
