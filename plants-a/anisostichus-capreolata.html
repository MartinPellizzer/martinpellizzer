Anisostichus capreolata

Anisostichus capreolata, commonly known as the mountain aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Anisostichus capreolata plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens.
