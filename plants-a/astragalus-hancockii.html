Astragalus hancockii

Astragalus hancockii, commonly known as Hancock's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as sagebrush steppe and rocky soils. Astragalus hancockii is also valued for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various wildlife species.
