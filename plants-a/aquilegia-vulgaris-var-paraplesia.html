Aquilegia vulgaris var. paraplesia

Aquilegia vulgaris var. paraplesia, commonly known as the paraplesia columbine, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features distinctive spurred petals that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, creating a vibrant floral display. The plant produces compound leaves with a finely toothed margin, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
