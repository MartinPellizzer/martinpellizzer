Anathallis montipelladensis

Anathallis montipelladensis, commonly known as the Montipellade ghost orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to the cloud forests of southern Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, white, fragrant flowers that resemble those of the ghost orchid genus. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its unique morphology and restricted distribution make it a rare and ecologically significant species.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
