Aster kentuckiensis

Aster kentuckiensis, commonly known as the Kentucky aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy purple to blue flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in woodland edges, swamps, and stream banks.

The Kentucky aster is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
