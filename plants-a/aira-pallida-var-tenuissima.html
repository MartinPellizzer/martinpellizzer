Aira pallida var. tenuissima

Aira pallida var. tenuissima, commonly known as slender false oat, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, pale blue to lavender flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open meadows and rocky habitats.

Its delicate appearance and ecological value make it a notable species in native plant gardens.
