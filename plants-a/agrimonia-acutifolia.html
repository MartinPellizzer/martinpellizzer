Agrimonia acutifolia

Agrimonia acutifolia, commonly known as sharpleaf agrimony, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and serrated leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by reddish-brown fruits. Agrimonia acutifolia is often found in dry grasslands, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

It has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
