Arthrosolen inconspicuus

Arthrosolen inconspicuus, commonly known as the inconspicuous rock moss, is a small, epiphytic liverwort found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows on rocks or tree trunks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. The plant has a thalloid structure with a leafy, cushion-like growth form. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and arranged in a dense rosette.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides microhabitat for other organisms.
