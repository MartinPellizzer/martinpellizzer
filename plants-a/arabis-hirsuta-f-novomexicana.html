Arabis hirsuta f. novomexicana

Arabis hirsuta f. novomexicana, commonly known as the New Mexico hairy rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically features hairy, glandular leaves and stems, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert regions.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to withstand harsh, dry conditions.
