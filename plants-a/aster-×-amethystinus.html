Aster × amethystinus

Aster × amethystinus, commonly known as the Purple Coneflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to lavender flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant features a sturdy, upright stem with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are composed of both ray florets and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
