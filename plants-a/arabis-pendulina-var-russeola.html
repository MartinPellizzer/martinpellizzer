Arabis pendulina var. russeola

Arabis pendulina var. russeola, commonly known as the Russian pendulous rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically features basal rosettes of narrow, dark green leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its pendulous inflorescence adds a delicate, cascading appearance to its floral display.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
