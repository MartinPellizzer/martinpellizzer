Acinodendron cymosa

Acinodendron cymosa, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pentagonal-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
