Abies krylovii

Abies krylovii, commonly known as the Krylov fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are slightly curved. The cones of this species are typically pendulous and can reach lengths of up to 15 centimeters. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found at high altitudes.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited commercial use due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements.
