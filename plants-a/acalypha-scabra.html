Acalypha scabra

Acalypha scabra, commonly known as roughleaf mallow, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features hairy, rough-textured leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or reddish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Acalypha scabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
