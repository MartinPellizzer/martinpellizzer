Ajax maximus

Ajax maximus, commonly known as the giant waterwheel plant, is a unique aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, spiraling leaves that resemble the structure of a waterwheel, which helps it float on the surface of water. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its leaves are typically green and can grow up to several feet in length.

Ajax maximus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
