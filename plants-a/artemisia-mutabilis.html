Artemisia mutabilis

Artemisia mutabilis, commonly known as the golden thread, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to golden flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Artemisia mutabilis is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
