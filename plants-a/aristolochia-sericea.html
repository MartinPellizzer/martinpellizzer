Aristolochia sericea

Aristolochia sericea, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-white with a strong, spicy odor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and fragrance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
