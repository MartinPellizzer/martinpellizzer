Acer glabrum f. monophyllum

Acer glabrum f. monophyllum, commonly known as the single-leaf maple, is a unique variant of the silver maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive single leaf that emerges from the tip of a branch, making it visually striking. The leaves are typically bright green with a silvery underside, and they turn yellow in the fall. This plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various garden conditions.
