Alstroemeria macrocarpa

Alstroemeria macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant alstroemeria, is a striking perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of colorful, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, yellow, and pink. Its leaves are long, narrow, and slightly serrated, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Alstroemeria macrocarpa is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and is well-suited to garden environments with well-drained soil.
