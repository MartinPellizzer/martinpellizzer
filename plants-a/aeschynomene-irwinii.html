Aeschynomene irwinii

Aeschynomene irwinii, commonly known as the slender sesban, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, followed by legume-like pods containing seeds.

Aeschynomene irwinii is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop due to its nutritional value.
