Althaea transcaucasica

Althaea transcaucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian marshmallow, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pale pink to violet flowers during the summer months. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their mucilage content, which has soothing properties.

Althaea transcaucasica thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and riverbanks.
