Actaea rubifolia

Actaea rubifolia, commonly known as red-stemmed black snakeroot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red stems and dark green, palmately compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are follicles that mature into bright red, berry-like structures.

Actaea rubifolia is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
