Aristida setifolia var. parviflora

Aristida setifolia var. parviflora, commonly known as slender false lovegrass, is a grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, thread-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, rocky slopes, and disturbed areas. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy species adapted to harsh environments.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of native plant communities in its range.
