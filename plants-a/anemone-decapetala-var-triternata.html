Anemone decapetala var. triternata

Anemone decapetala var. triternata, commonly known as the three-lobed anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northeastern United States.

It features delicate, white to pale pink flowers with five to seven petals, arranged in a showy cluster at the top of slender stems. The plant has trifoliate leaves, consisting of three leaflets, which are deeply lobed and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
