Antennaria alpina var. intermedia

Antennaria alpina var. intermedia, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small bells. The plant features a mat-forming growth habit with hairy, silver-green leaves and produces small, white to pale pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

This variety is often found in open habitats such as meadows, cliffs, and scree slopes at high altitudes.
