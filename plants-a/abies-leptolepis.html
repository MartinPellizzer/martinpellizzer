Abies leptolepis

Abies leptolepis, commonly known as the Japanese white pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and its long, needle-like leaves that are bluish-green in color. The tree produces cones that are typically 5 to 8 centimeters in length, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

The bark of Abies leptolepis is thick and scaly, providing protection against environmental stress.
