Araujia variegata

Araujia variegata, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant white flowers that open at night and remain open for only a few hours. The plant has a thick, spiny stem and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are usually green with a waxy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Araujia variegata is also known for its ability to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
