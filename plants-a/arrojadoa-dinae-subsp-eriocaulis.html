Arrojadoa dinae subsp. eriocaulis

Arrojadoa dinae subsp.

eriocaulis, commonly known as the velvetleaf arrowhead, is a perennial herb native to the wetlands of South America. It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs.

The plant produces small, white to pale green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshy or swampy habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is an important species in wetland ecosystems.
