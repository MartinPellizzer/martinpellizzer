Amaryllis advena var. coccinea

Amaryllis advena var. coccinea, commonly known as the red-flowered amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red and pink. The foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are often dark green in color. This variety thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its blooming period typically occurs in the spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to the landscape.
