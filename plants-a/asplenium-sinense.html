Asplenium sinense

Asplenium sinense, commonly known as the Chinese maidenhair fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. The plant prefers shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce numerous small, dark green leaves.

Asplenium sinense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and indoor spaces.
