Astragalus burqinensis

Astragalus burqinensis, commonly known as the Burqin milkvetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are small and contain compounds that have been studied for potential pharmaceutical applications.
