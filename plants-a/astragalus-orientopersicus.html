Astragalus orientopersicus

Astragalus orientopersicus, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to reddish-brown. The plant produces cylindrical, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and rocky soils.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
