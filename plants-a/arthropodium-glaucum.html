Arthropodium glaucum

Arthropodium glaucum, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces tall, slender flowering stems that bear small, white to pale blue flowers. The leaves are linear, grass-like, and covered with a fine, silvery bloom, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
