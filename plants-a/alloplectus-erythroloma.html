Alloplectus erythroloma

Alloplectus erythroloma, commonly known as the red-lobed spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves and stems contain milky sap, which can be toxic and is a defense mechanism against herbivores.

Alloplectus erythroloma is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.
