Acaena × anserinacea

Acaena × anserinacea, commonly known as the New Zealand spinach or silverweed, is a hybrid plant native to New Zealand.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant features small, silver-green leaves with a fuzzy texture and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers. Its fruit is a round, hairy achene that is adapted for wind dispersal.

Acaena × anserinacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
