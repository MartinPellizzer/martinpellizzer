Atriplex pallida

Atriplex pallida, commonly known as pale saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are adapted to conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep water sources and stabilize sandy soils.

Atriplex pallida is often found in coastal areas, salt marshes, and inland deserts, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
