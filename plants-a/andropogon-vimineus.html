Andropogon vimineus

Andropogon vimineus, commonly known as switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, which are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. Switchgrass is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in prairies and disturbed areas.

It is also a valuable species for erosion control and has potential as a bioenergy crop.
