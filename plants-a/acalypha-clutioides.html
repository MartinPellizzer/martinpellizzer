Acalypha clutioides

Acalypha clutioides, commonly known as the velvetleaf or flameleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety, palmate leaves that often display a vibrant red or purple coloration. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, three-lobed seed capsules. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks.

Acalypha clutioides is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
