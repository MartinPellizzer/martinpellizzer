Adenophyllum cavanillesii

Adenophyllum cavanillesii, commonly known as the Cavanilles' adenophyllum, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in coastal areas. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
