Allamanda cathartica var. schottii

Allamanda cathartica var. schottii, commonly known as the Schott's yellow allamanda, is a variant of the yellow allamanda native to South America.

It is a fast-growing shrub or small tree with glossy, dark green leaves and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant produces orange-yellow berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for tropical gardens and landscapes.
