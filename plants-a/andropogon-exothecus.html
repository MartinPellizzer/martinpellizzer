Andropogon exothecus

Andropogon exothecus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green leaves and compact, cylindrical inflorescence. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, thriving in well-drained soils. Its reproductive structures include spikelets with two bracts, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Andropogon exothecus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
