Arctostaphylos glandulosa f. glaucoides

Arctostaphylos glandulosa f. glaucoides, commonly known as the glaucous manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland chaparral habitats.
