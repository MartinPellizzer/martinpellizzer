Acer negundo f. rectangulatum

Acer negundo f. rectangulatum, commonly known as the box elder maple, is a variant of the box elder tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, and often rectangular-shaped leaves. The plant is native to North America and thrives in a variety of soil types. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras.

This cultivar is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability.
