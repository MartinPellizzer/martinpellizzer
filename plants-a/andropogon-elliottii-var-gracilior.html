Andropogon elliottii var. gracilior

Andropogon elliottii var. gracilior, commonly known as slender bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flower spikes that appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairie habitats.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in native plant communities.
