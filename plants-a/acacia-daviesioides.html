Acacia daviesioides

Acacia daviesioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are typically narrow and yellow-green in color. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are clustered in spherical or oblong inflorescences, typically appearing in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia daviesioides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in revegetation projects.
