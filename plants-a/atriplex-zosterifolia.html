Atriplex zosterifolia

Atriplex zosterifolia, commonly known as saltbush or shadscale, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Atriplex zosterifolia is a perennial shrub that often forms dense thickets, contributing to soil stabilization in desert ecosystems.

Its ability to thrive in saline and alkaline soils makes it an important component of desert flora.
