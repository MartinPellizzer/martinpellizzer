Afrachneria aurea

Afrachneria aurea, commonly known as the golden sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, tropical environments.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
