Aeranthes aemula

Aeranthes aemula, commonly known as the dwarf spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features a compact rhizome with a few leaves and produces delicate, spider-like flowers in shades of white and pink. Its flowers are characterized by their narrow, elongated petals and a distinctive, spurred labellum.

Aeranthes aemula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouse settings due to its specific care requirements.
