Ammophila × baltica var. intermedia

Ammophila × baltica var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate beachgrass, is a hybrid plant that thrives in coastal environments.

It is characterized by its tall, tufted growth and extensive root system that helps stabilize sandy soils. This variety is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds typical of beach habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It plays a crucial role in dune formation and coastal erosion control.
