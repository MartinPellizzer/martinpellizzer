Aquilegia sulphurea

Aquilegia sulphurea, commonly known as the sulphur columbine, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of China and the Tibetan Plateau. This perennial herb features showy, bi- or tri-petaled flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which are typically borne on tall, slender stems. The plant has palmately divided, trifoliate leaves that are hairy and serrated.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
