Adiantum ceciliae

Adiantum ceciliae, commonly known as the Cecilia maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble lace. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Adiantum ceciliae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
