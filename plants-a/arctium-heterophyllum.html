Arctium heterophyllum

Arctium heterophyllum, commonly known as the lesser burdock, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces burrs with hooked bracts that aid in seed dispersal. Its flowers are typically purple or pink, arranged in a cluster at the top of the stem.

Arctium heterophyllum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and meadows.
