Apaturia smithiana

Apaturia smithiana, commonly known as the Smith's orchid, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments. The plant features delicate, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive, lip-like structure that aids in pollination. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the forest floor.

Apaturia smithiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
