Astragalus nelidae

Astragalus nelidae, commonly known as the Nelidae milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its erect stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the genus Astragalus. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Mongolia.

Astragalus nelidae is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
