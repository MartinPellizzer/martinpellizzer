Aristolochia tubiflora

Aristolochia tubiflora, commonly known as the tubular Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a strong, often unpleasant odor. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

The species is part of the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination mechanism involving flies.
