Aridaria blanda

Aridaria blanda, commonly known as the softleaf desert plant, is a succulent native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features fleshy, bluish-gray leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in low-rainfall environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. Its root system is shallow but extensive, enabling it to absorb moisture from a wide area.

Aridaria blanda is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to high temperatures and intense sunlight.
