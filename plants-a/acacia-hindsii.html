Acacia hindsii

Acacia hindsii, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that turn a striking silver-white color during the dry season. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its bark is smooth and greyish, often peeling in thin strips, and it has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

Acacia hindsii is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient and ornamental plant in native landscapes.
