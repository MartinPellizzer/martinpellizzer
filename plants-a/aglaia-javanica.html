Aglaia javanica

Aglaia javanica, commonly known as the Java aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. Aglaia javanica is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Its wood is also used in traditional crafts and furniture making.
