Antirrhinum sempervirens subsp. densiflorum

Antirrhinum sempervirens subsp.

densiflorum, commonly known as the dense-flowered snapdragon, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of tubular, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a soft texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
