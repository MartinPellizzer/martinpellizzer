Alsine tenuifolia subsp. hybrida

Alsine tenuifolia subsp.

hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid sand-verbena, is a low-growing annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It typically features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or purple.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or arid environments. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common species in xerophytic plant communities.
