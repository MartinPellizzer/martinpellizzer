Asarum sieboldii var. cornutum

Asarum sieboldii var. cornutum, commonly known as the horned wild ginger, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves with a strong, spicy aroma. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a spadix, typical of the Aristolochiaceae family. Its rhizomatous root system is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
