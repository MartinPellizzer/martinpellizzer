Azalea × daviesii

Azalea × daviesii, commonly known as the Carolina azalea, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron viscosum and Rhododendron boreale.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and produces small, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
