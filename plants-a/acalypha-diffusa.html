Acalypha diffusa

Acalypha diffusa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that are typically green with a soft texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in spikes, followed by distinctive, three-lobed seed pods. Acalypha diffusa thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

It is valued for its ornamental foliage and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
