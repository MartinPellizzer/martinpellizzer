Aztekium mexicanum

Aztekium mexicanum, commonly known as the Mexican aztekium, is a small, cactus-like plant native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a dense covering of white to pale green spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Aztekium mexicanum is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil and minimal water.

It is also known for its slow growth and is often cultivated as a rare and ornamental cactus.
