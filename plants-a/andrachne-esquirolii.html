Andrachne esquirolii

Andrachne esquirolii, commonly known as the Esquiroli's sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern California.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is often found in coastal scrub and coastal dune habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
