Aesculus memmingeri

Aesculus memmingeri, commonly known as the Memminger's horsechestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the soapberry family, Sapindaceae, and is characterized by its showy, white to pink flowers that appear in late spring. The tree produces large, spiny fruits that contain one or two seeds each, which are typically dispersed by animals. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and it has a rounded, upright growth habit.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
