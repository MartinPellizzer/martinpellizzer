Abies cephalonica subsp. apollinis

Abies cephalonica subsp.

apollinis, commonly known as the Apollo fir, is a rare and endemic subspecies native to the mountainous regions of northern Greece. It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like foliage.

The cones of this subspecies are typically small and have a distinctive reddish-brown color when mature. It thrives in cool, moist environments at high altitudes, often growing in association with other endemic species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
