Amygdalus nana var. microflora

Amygdalus nana var. microflora, commonly known as the dwarf almond or miniature almond, is a compact shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, rounded leaves and produces fragrant, white or pink flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its small, edible fruits that resemble almonds but are typically less than 1 cm in size. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Due to its unique size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated for decorative purposes in gardens.
