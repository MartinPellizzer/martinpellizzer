Agrostis spica-venti f. flavida

Agrostis spica-venti f. flavida, commonly known as the golden windgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny florets. The inflorescence is typically pale green to golden in color, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes, often in temperate regions.

This variant is notable for its unique coloration and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
