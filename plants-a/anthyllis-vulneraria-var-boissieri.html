Anthyllis vulneraria var. boissieri

Anthyllis vulneraria var. boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its pods are slender and linear. Native to regions of the Mediterranean, it thrives in rocky and arid environments.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soils in its native habitat.
