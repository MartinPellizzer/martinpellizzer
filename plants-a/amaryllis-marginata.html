Amaryllis marginata

Amaryllis marginata, commonly known as the white amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking white flowers with red or pink margins. The plant features strap-shaped, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette. It produces tall, erect stems that bear clusters of large, showy blooms.

Amaryllis marginata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
