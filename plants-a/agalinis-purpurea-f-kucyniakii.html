Agalinis purpurea f. kucyniakii

Agalinis purpurea f. kucyniakii, commonly known as the purplestem false foxglove, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink to purple in color, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It has slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

This subspecies is characterized by its distinct floral morphology and is often found in wetland ecosystems.
