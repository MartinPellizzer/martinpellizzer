Astilbe indica

Astilbe indica, commonly known as the Japanese featherflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features delicate, plume-like inflorescences that bloom in shades of pink, white, or red, depending on the variety. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and provides attractive texture throughout the growing season.

Astilbe indica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to add color to shaded garden spaces.
