Azalea glauca var. rosea

Azalea glauca var. rosea, commonly known as the Rosea Azalea, is a compact evergreen shrub native to China.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink to rose red during the spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in garden landscapes for its vibrant blooms.
