Arundinella hordeiformis

Arundinella hordeiformis, commonly known as the false barley, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that often bear small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Arundinella hordeiformis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in dry climates.
