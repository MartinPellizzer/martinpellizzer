Allosorus caespitosus

Allosorus caespitosus, commonly known as the hairy false oat, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the eastern United States.

It forms dense, mat-like growths with slender, wiry stems covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Allosorus caespitosus is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in shaded or partially shaded environments.
