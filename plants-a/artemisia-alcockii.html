Artemisia alcockii

Artemisia alcockii, commonly known as Alcock's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its strong, often pungent scent.

Artemisia alcockii is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its potential medicinal properties.
