Astragalus cicer var. pseudocicer

Astragalus cicer var. pseudocicer, commonly known as the Greek horsegram, is a leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes, followed by hairy pods containing small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid climates.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is used in traditional agriculture and as a forage crop.
