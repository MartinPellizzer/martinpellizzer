Astragalus austiniae

Astragalus austiniae, commonly known as Austin's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, rounded pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus austiniae is also valued for its role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife.
