Agave fourcroydes var. espiculata

Agave fourcroydes var. espiculata, commonly known as the Mexican hat agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color with white teeth along the edges. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that bears yellow-green flowers, which bloom in the spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and xeriscaping.
