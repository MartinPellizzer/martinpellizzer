Acacia caven var. microcarpa

Acacia caven var. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf acacia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are often clustered at the ends of the branches. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, spherical clusters, which are followed by small, woody pods containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with suitable climates.
