Adenia ballyi

Adenia ballyi, commonly known as the Bally's Adenia, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with a distinctive crown-like structure, which are followed by orange or red berries. Adenia ballyi thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
