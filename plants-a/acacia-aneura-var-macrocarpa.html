Acacia aneura var. macrocarpa

Acacia aneura var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the river red gum, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its distinctive red-barked trunk and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, which are often followed by woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and is commonly found near water sources.

The river red gum plays an important ecological role in Australian woodlands and is also valued for its timber and ornamental qualities.
