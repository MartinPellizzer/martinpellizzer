Arabidella eremigena

Arabidella eremigena, commonly known as the desert bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure and small, green to blue-gray leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by inflated, bladder-like seed pods. Arabidella eremigena is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
