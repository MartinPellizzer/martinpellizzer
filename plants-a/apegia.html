Apegia

Apegia, commonly known as the heartleaf vine, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple or white. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, Apegia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be grown as a climbing vine or trailing plant.

Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for ground cover or as a decorative element in gardens.
