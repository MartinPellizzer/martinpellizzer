Amorphophallus gliruroides

Amorphophallus gliruroides, commonly known as the mouse ear elephant's foot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, tuberous root system that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces a single, large, umbellate inflorescence with showy, white to purple flowers. Its leaves are typically large and palmately lobed, contributing to its striking appearance.

The species is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its potential medicinal uses.
