Ananassa semiserrata

Ananassa semiserrata, commonly known as the serrated ananas, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, pointed leaves. The plant produces striking, red-orange flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are covered in fine, silvery hairs, which help reduce water loss in its humid habitat.

Ananassa semiserrata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
