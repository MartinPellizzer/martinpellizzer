Arabidopsis minutiflora

Arabidopsis minutiflora, commonly known as the dwarf Arabidopsis, is a small flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its tiny, clustered flowers and short stature, typically growing no taller than 10 centimeters. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring.

Due to its compact size and rapid life cycle, it is often used in botanical studies and as a model organism in plant genetics research.
