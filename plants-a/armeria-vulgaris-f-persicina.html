Armeria vulgaris f. persicina

Armeria vulgaris f. persicina, commonly known as the Persian sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils. It is often found in alpine and arid environments, thriving in full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
