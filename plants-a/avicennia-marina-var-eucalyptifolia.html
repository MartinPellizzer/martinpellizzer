Avicennia marina var. eucalyptifolia

Avicennia marina var. eucalyptifolia, commonly known as the mangrove with eucalyptus-like leaves, is a variant of the gray mangrove.

It is a halophyte, meaning it thrives in saline environments such as tidal mudflats and estuaries. Its leaves are narrow, elongated, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant has specialized root systems, including pneumatophores, which allow it to absorb oxygen from the air.

It plays a crucial role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
