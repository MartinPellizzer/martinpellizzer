Arenaria graminifolia var. parviflora

Arenaria graminifolia var. parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf sandflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It typically forms dense mats of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pink flowers in clusters. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, thriving in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils. It is often found in tundra and mountainous areas where it contributes to soil stabilization.

Its botanical features make it an important component of cold-climate ecosystems.
