Alnus × balatonialis

Alnus × balatonialis, commonly known as the Hungarian alder, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Alnus glutinosa and Alnus incana.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in riparian zones and wetlands. The plant features compound leaves with serrated margins and produces small, spherical fruits called samaras. It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil restoration and ecological landscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
