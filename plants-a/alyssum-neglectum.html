Alyssum neglectum

Alyssum neglectum, commonly known as common alyssum, is an annual flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white, pink, or purple, arranged in dense clusters. The plant has hairy, slender stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum neglectum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
