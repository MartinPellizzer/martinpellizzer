Acacia sericea

Acacia sericea, commonly known as silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, grey-green foliage and yellow globular flowers that appear in spring. The plant has a deep root system, allowing it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are covered in a fine, silvery bloom, which gives it its common name.

Acacia sericea is often used in reforestation projects due to its hardiness and ability to prevent soil erosion.
