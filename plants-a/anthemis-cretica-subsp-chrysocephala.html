Anthemis cretica subsp. chrysocephala

Anthemis cretica subsp.

chrysocephala, commonly known as the goldenhead cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
