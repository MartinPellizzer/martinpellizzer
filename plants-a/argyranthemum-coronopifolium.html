Argyranthemum coronopifolium

Argyranthemum coronopifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming from spring to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a versatile addition to both ornamental and perennial gardens.
