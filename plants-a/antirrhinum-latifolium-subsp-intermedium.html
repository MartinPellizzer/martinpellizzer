Antirrhinum latifolium subsp. intermedium

Antirrhinum latifolium subsp.

intermedium, commonly known as the broad-leaved snapdragon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pairs of showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that range in color from red to yellow.

The plant typically features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in meadows and rocky areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
