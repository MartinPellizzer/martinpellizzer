Asclepias harveyana

Asclepias harveyana, commonly known as Harvey's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright orange to yellow flowers. The plant features a milky sap, which is a common trait among milkweed species. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, often with a hairy texture.

Harvey's milkweed serves as a critical host plant for the monarch butterfly, supporting the life cycle of this iconic species.
