Astragalus stevenianus subsp. glochideus

Astragalus stevenianus subsp.

glochideus, commonly known as Steven's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with small, rounded leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is often found in sagebrush steppe and other xeric habitats.
