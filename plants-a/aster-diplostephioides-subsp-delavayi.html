Aster diplostephioides subsp. delavayi

Aster diplostephioides subsp.

delavayi, commonly known as Delavay's aster, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China. It is characterized by its erect stem and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture. This subspecies is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows at elevations above 2,500 meters.
