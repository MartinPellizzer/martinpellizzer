Adenorachis melanocarpa

Adenorachis melanocarpa, commonly known as the black-fruited bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Adenorachis melanocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
