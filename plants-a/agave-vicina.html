Agave vicina

Agave vicina, commonly known as the narrowleaf agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears small, white flowers, which appear after several years of growth. Agave vicina is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems to survive in dry conditions.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
