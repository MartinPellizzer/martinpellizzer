Andromeda plumata

Andromeda plumata, commonly known as the silver-leafed andromeda, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils, often found in woodland areas. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in upright inflorescences, blooming in early spring.

Andromeda plumata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
