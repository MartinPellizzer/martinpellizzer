Acer amplum subsp. bodinieri

Acer amplum subsp.

bodinieri, commonly known as the Bohemian maple, is a deciduous tree native to Central Europe. It is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, which are typically green on the upper surface and lighter in color on the underside.

The tree produces small, yellow-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones or along stream banks.

The Bohemian maple is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
