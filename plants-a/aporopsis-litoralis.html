Aporopsis litoralis

Aporopsis litoralis, commonly known as the sea daisies, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Indo-Pacific.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and is often found in sandy or rocky shorelines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It has adapted to salt spray and strong winds, making it a hardy species in maritime environments.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
