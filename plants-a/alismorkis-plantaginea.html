Alismorkis plantaginea

Alismorkis plantaginea, commonly known as the marsh plantain, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland and marsh environments.

It features heart-shaped, smooth, and often waxy leaves that are typically submerged or floating. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in shallow water and is often found in ponds, ditches, and along the edges of streams.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for aquatic ecosystems and a useful indicator of water quality.
