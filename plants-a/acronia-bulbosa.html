Acronia bulbosa

Acronia bulbosa, commonly known as the bulbous acronia, is a perennial plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its underground bulb, which serves as a storage organ for nutrients and water. The plant produces tall, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, often in shades of white or pale purple.

Due to its unique structure and habitat, it is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptations in arid environments.
