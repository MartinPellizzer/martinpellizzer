Anthyllis vulneraria var. procerior

Anthyllis vulneraria var. procerior, commonly known as the creeping sweet clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and is native to the Mediterranean region. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
