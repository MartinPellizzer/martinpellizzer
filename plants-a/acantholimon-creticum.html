Acantholimon creticum

Acantholimon creticum, commonly known as Cretan thornweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in Greece and Turkey.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, spiny leaves that help reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually blue or violet, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom in the spring.

Acantholimon creticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
