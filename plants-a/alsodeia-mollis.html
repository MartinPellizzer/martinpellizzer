Alsodeia mollis

Alsodeia mollis, commonly known as the soft alsike, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, lance-shaped leaves with a soft, hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. Alsodeia mollis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
