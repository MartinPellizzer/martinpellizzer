Astragalus batesii

Astragalus batesii, commonly known as the Bates' milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and often compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, with a spurred structure typical of the genus Astragalus.

The species is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in supporting local pollinators and its potential medicinal properties.
