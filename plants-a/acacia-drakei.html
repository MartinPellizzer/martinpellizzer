Acacia drakei

Acacia drakei, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny branches and phyllodes that are narrow and linear. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which appear during the warmer months. Acacia drakei is adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

It plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.
