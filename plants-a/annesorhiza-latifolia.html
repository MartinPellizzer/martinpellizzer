Annesorhiza latifolia

Annesorhiza latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf annesorhiza, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Annesorhiza latifolia is a perennial herb that plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of its wetland habitat.
