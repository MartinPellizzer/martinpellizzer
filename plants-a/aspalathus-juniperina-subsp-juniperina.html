Aspalathus juniperina subsp. juniperina

Aspalathus juniperina subsp.

juniperina, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a shrub native to South Africa. It features small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental plant often used in gardens and landscapes.
