Amaranthus hybridus subvar. multispiculatus

Amaranthus hybridus subvar. multispiculatus, commonly known as the redroot amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and the presence of red-tinged stems and leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, greenish to red flowers, which are borne on branched inflorescences. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a smooth texture and can vary in color from green to reddish-purple.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in some traditional cuisines.
