Anchusa leucojifolia

Anchusa leucojifolia, commonly known as white bugloss, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and sunny environments. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Anchusa leucojifolia is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
