Armeniaca zhengheensis

Armeniaca zhengheensis, commonly known as the Zhenghe cherry plum, is a species of Prunus native to China.

It is characterized by its small, sweet fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine. The plant produces white or pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by the development of its edible drupes. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge.

The Zhenghe cherry plum is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its culinary and medicinal uses.
