Allium sulphureum

Allium sulphureum, commonly known as the sulfur garlic or wild garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and leeks, and is characterized by its bulbous structure and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are broad, grass-like, and emit a strong garlic scent when crushed.

Allium sulphureum is often found in moist, grassy areas and is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses.
