Astragalus zarreanus

Astragalus zarreanus, commonly known as Zarrean milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to pink. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments.

Astragalus zarreanus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
