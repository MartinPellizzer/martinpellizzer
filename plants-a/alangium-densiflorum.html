Alangium densiflorum

Alangium densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower alangium, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and often have a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and it is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Alangium densiflorum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
