Actinidia fairchildii

Actinidia fairchildii, commonly known as the Chinese mayapple, is a deciduous vine native to eastern China.

It belongs to the Actinidiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, which are edible but should be consumed in moderation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable in native habitats.

Morphology

Actinidia fairchildii has simple, alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically 5-10 cm long and 3-6 cm wide, with a smooth margin and a dark green upper surface and a lighter, sometimes hairy underside.

The stem is slender, woody, and has a smooth, light brown bark that becomes fissured with age, growing as an upright shrub or small tree. The flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in terminal panicles, blooming in early spring, and are pollinated by insects such as bees. The fruit is a yellow to orange berry that matures in late summer, containing multiple small seeds, and is dispersed primarily by birds and animals.

The roots are fibrous and rhizomatous, helping the plant to spread vegetatively.
