Aspidoglossum angustissimum subsp. brevilobum

Aspidoglossum angustissimum subsp.

brevilobum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aspidoglossum, is a perennial herb native to South America. It features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and blooms.
