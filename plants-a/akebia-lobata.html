Akebia lobata

Akebia lobata, commonly known as the Japanese ivy or five-leaf akebia, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive five-lobed leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that ripen in late summer, which are edible but often used in traditional medicine. Akebia lobata thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and adaptability have led to its introduction in various parts of the world, though it can become invasive in some regions.
