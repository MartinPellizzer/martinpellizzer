Acacia polymorpha var. pendula

Acacia polymorpha var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive drooping branches that give it a cascading appearance. The plant produces feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow, globular flower heads that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique form and drought tolerance make it an adaptable and visually striking plant.
