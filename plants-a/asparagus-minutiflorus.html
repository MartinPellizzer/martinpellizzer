Asparagus minutiflorus

Asparagus minutiflorus, commonly known as the dwarf asparagus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, delicate leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces tiny, yellowish flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its compact size and is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Asparagus minutiflorus is relatively low maintenance and is well-suited for rock gardens or container planting.
