Acacia runciformis

Acacia runciformis, commonly known as the umbrella acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flat, umbrella-like crown formed by its dense, spreading branches. The plant produces yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, feathery leaflets.

Acacia runciformis is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine.
