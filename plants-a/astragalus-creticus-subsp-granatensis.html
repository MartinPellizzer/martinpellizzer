Astragalus creticus subsp. granatensis

Astragalus creticus subsp.

granatensis, commonly known as the Granatene Astragalus, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates. It is often found in scrublands and open woodlands, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and supports local pollinators.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in Mediterranean ecosystems.
