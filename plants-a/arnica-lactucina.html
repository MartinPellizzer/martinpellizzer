Arnica lactucina

Arnica lactucina, commonly known as lesser arnica, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in clusters.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
