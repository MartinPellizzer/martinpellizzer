Agylla

Agylla, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its silver-gray leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are also valued for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.

Agylla thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important plant in regions with limited water resources.
