Alhagi maurorum subsp. kirghisorum

Alhagi maurorum subsp.

kirghisorum, commonly known as the Kirghiz alfalfa, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in racemes.

This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and sandy soils. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor conditions.

The species is valued for its forage qualities and is used in ecological restoration projects.
