Androrchis × colemanii

Androrchis × colemanii, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a natural cross between Androrchis alpina and Androrchis filiformis.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.

The hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
