Archidendron aruense

Archidendron aruense, commonly known as the Aru legume, is a tropical tree native to the Indo-Pacific region, particularly found in parts of Indonesia and Papua New Guinea.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate and composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale yellow to greenish in color, followed by elongated, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its timber and has ecological significance in its native habitat.
