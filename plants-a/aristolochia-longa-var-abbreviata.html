Aristolochia longa var. abbreviata

Aristolochia longa var. abbreviata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Dutchman's pipe, is a perennial climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves and distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-brown in color. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that contain seeds enclosed in a gelatinous coating. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
