Aquilegia darwazii

Aquilegia darwazii, commonly known as the Darwaza columbine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that come in shades of red, purple, and blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Aquilegia darwazii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
