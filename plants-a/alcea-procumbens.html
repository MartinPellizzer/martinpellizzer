Alcea procumbens

Alcea procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate hollyhock, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom from summer to autumn. Its fibrous roots and stems are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Alcea procumbens thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
