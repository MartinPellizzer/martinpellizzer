Allium reticulatum var. nuttallii

Allium reticulatum var. nuttallii, commonly known as the Nuttall's leek, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is known for its strong garlic-like odor. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
