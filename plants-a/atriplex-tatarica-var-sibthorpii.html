Atriplex tatarica var. sibthorpii

Atriplex tatarica var. sibthorpii, commonly known as Siberian saltbush, is a shrub native to the steppes and semi-desert regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in dense clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable species for soil stabilization.

Its robust growth habit and drought tolerance make it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
