Alstroemeria viridiflora

Alstroemeria viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered alstroemeria, is a species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, showy flowers in shades of green, yellow, and red. The plant has strap-like, lance-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

The flowers are typically arranged in clusters and are known for their long-lasting display, making it a popular choice in floral arrangements.
