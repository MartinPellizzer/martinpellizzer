Aspalathus perfoliata

Aspalathus perfoliata, commonly known as the African geranium, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and its flowers are typically pink or red, appearing in clusters. The plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates. It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine.

Aspalathus perfoliata plays an important role in local ecosystems and is a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
