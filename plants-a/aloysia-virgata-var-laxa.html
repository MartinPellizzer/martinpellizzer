Aloysia virgata var. laxa

Aloysia virgata var. laxa, commonly known as silverleaf verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and spikes of blue-violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is a shrub with woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its aromatic leaves and showy flowers make it a popular ornamental plant.
