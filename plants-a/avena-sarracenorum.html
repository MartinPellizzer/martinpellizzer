Avena sarracenorum

Avena sarracenorum, commonly known as the Persian oat or wild oat, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. Avena sarracenorum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
