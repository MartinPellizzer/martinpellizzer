Ageratum pedatum

Ageratum pedatum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fluffy, blue or purple flower clusters that resemble mist, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves and is often grown as an ornamental in gardens for its aesthetic appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Ageratum pedatum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
