Arabis media var. glabra

Arabis media var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or arid environments. The plant features basal leaves that are smooth and lance-shaped, with a rough texture. Its flowers are white or pale pink, arranged in clusters and blooming in early spring.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
