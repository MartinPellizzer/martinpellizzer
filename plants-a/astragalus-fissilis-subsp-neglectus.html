Astragalus fissilis subsp. neglectus

Astragalus fissilis subsp.

neglectus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. Astragalus fissilis subsp.

neglectus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
