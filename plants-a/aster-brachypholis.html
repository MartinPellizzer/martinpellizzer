Aster brachypholis

Aster brachypholis, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blossoms. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, producing purple to blue-purple ray florets.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows.
