Agrostis vinosa

Agrostis vinosa, commonly known as redtop grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including moist and wet environments. The plant produces dense, upright culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are typically reddish in color, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Agrostis vinosa is often used in landscaping and erosion control due to its hardiness and aesthetic value.
