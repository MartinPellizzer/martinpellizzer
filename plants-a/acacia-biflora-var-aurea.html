Acacia biflora var. aurea

Acacia biflora var. aurea, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, feather-like segments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

The golden wattle is a symbol of Australia and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value.
