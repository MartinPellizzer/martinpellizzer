Acer villosum f. sterculiaceum

Acer villosum f. sterculiaceum, commonly known as the silky maple or sterculiaceum maple, is a variant of the silky maple, characterized by its fine, soft hairs on the stems and leaves.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States, typically found in moist, acidic soils. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a serrated margin and a pale underside. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds known as samaras.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in woodland habitats.
