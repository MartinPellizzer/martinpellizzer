Astragalus syriacus

Astragalus syriacus, commonly known as Syrian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, dry legumes as fruits, which contain the seeds.

Astragalus syriacus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
