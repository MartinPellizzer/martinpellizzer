Andropogon scoparius f. calvescens

Andropogon scoparius f. calvescens, commonly known as the tall bluestem grass, is a variant of the tall bluestem species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often bluish-gray in color. This grass is typically found in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and disturbed soils. It plays an important role in ecological systems by providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for restoration and conservation efforts.
