Auriculardisia glomerata

Auriculardisia glomerata, commonly known as the clustered auriculate daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant features basal leaves that are auriculate, meaning they have ear-like lobes at the base. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically arranged in dense, spherical clusters.

Auriculardisia glomerata is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.
