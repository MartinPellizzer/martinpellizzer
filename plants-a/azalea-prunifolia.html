Azalea prunifolia

Azalea prunifolia, commonly known as the Chinese azalea, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring broad, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white during the spring season. Azalea prunifolia is part of the Rhododendron genus and is closely related to other azalea species, sharing similar botanical characteristics.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
