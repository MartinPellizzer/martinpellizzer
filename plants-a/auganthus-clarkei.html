Auganthus clarkei

Auganthus clarkei, commonly known as Clarke's auganthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Auganthus clarkei is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
