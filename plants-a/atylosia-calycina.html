Atylosia calycina

Atylosia calycina, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features a basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its flowers are followed by inflated, bladder-like fruits that contain seeds with tufts of white hair, aiding in wind dispersal.

Atylosia calycina is adapted to withstand drought and has a deep root system to access water from deeper soil layers.
