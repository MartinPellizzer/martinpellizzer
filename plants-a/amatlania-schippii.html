Amatlania schippii

Amatlania schippii, commonly known as the Schippi's amaranth, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically display red, orange, or yellow hues. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking visual display.

Amatlania schippii is also noted for its adaptability to poor soil conditions and its ability to thrive in full sun.
