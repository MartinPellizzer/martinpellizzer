Astragalus crotalariae var. virgatus

Astragalus crotalariae var. virgatus, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Astragalus crotalariae var. virgatus is a member of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen through its root nodules.
