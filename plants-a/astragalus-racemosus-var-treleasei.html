Astragalus racemosus var. treleasei

Astragalus racemosus var. treleasei, commonly known as the Trelease's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility through its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.

This variant is distinguished by its unique floral structure and is of interest to botanists and ecologists.
