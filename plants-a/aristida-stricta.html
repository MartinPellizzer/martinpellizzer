Aristida stricta

Aristida stricta, commonly known as slender threeawn, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, disturbed soils, and sandy areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are often bristly and difficult to remove. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and is often found in prairie ecosystems.

The botanical features of Aristida stricta make it an important component of native plant communities and a valuable forage species.
