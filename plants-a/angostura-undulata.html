Angostura undulata

Angostura undulata, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, undulating leaf patterns and striking, orchid-like flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
