Armeria beirana subsp. altimontana

Armeria beirana subsp.

altimontana, commonly known as the alpine thrift, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Pyrenees and southern Europe. It features compact, low-growing foliage with dense, silver-gray leaves that are adapted to cold and arid conditions.

The plant produces clusters of pink to red flowers in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
