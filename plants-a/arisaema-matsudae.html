Arisaema matsudae

Arisaema matsudae, commonly known as the Japanese mountain arum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are arrow-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts.

Arisaema matsudae is valued for its ornamental appearance and has cultural significance in traditional medicine.
