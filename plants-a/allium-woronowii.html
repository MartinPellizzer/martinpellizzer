Allium woronowii

Allium woronowii, commonly known as the Korean garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or purple flowers in umbels, which are followed by spherical, bulb-like structures. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible leaves and bulbs.

Allium woronowii is valued in traditional medicine and culinary practices for its pungent aroma and nutritional benefits.
