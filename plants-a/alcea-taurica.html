Alcea taurica

Alcea taurica, commonly known as the Crimean hollyhock, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features large, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and often branched.

Alcea taurica is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
