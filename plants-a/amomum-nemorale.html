Amomum nemorale

Amomum nemorale, commonly known as the wild cardamom, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries like India, Indonesia, and Malaysia. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces aromatic, spindle-shaped fruits that contain numerous aromatic seeds. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and covered with a waxy cuticle, which helps in reducing water loss.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
