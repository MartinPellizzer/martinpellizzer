Atriplex glabriuscula var. oblanceolata

Atriplex glabriuscula var. oblanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its glabrous (smooth) leaves that are typically narrow and oblanceolate in shape. The plant is adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable species for land rehabilitation. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense, terminal spikes, and it is often used in ecological restoration projects.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Atriplex genus, which are known for their drought tolerance and ability to thrive in harsh environments.
