Amelancus ovalis

Amelancus ovalis, commonly known as the oval leaf amelancus, is a lesser-known species within the Amelancaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, which are usually borne near the base of the plant. Its botanical description includes a fibrous root system and a branching structure that allows it to thrive in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its limited geographic distribution, it is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation and biodiversity.
