Archidendron nielsenianum

Archidendron nielsenianum, commonly known as the Nielsens' legume, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with stipules and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, leathery pods that split open upon maturity.

Archidendron nielsenianum is often found in humid, lowland areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
