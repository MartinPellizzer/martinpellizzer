Absinthium viride

Absinthium viride, commonly known as green wormwood, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and feathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in herbal remedies due to their bitter properties.

Absinthium viride is also noted for its historical use in the production of absinthe, a spirit with a distinctive green color.
