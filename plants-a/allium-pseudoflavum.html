Allium pseudoflavum

Allium pseudoflavum, commonly known as the pale garlic.

It is a bulbous perennial plant native to Central Asia, characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, pale purple to white flowers. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other allium species such as garlic and onions. Its bulbs are typically small and have a mild, garlic-like flavor.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.
