Aechmea moonenii

Aechmea moonenii, commonly known as the Moonen's bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaf structure, which forms a central cup to collect water and nutrients. The leaves are typically dark green with a waxy coating, and they often display intricate patterns of red, purple, and orange. The plant produces showy inflorescences with bright orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

Aechmea moonenii thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Morphology

Aechmea moonenii has rosette-shaped leaves that are long, narrow, and sword-like, arranged in a tight spiral around the central stem, with a waxy, dark green surface that often has a silvery or blueish tint.

The plant lacks an obvious stem, with the base of the leaves forming a dense, compact rosette that resembles a cushion. It produces striking inflorescences with clustered, brightly colored flowers, typically pink or red, that bloom in late summer to early autumn and are pollinated by insects such as bees and flies. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds, which are dispersed by wind or water.
