Alyxia affinis

Alyxia affinis, commonly known as the Florida beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the spring. These flowers are succeeded by bright red berries that attract birds and other wildlife.

Alyxia affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
