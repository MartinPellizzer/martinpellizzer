Agapetes variegata var. macrantha

Agapetes variegata var. macrantha, commonly known as the variegated agapete, is a shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking variegated foliage, which features white and green mottling on the leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color, blooming in the spring. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
