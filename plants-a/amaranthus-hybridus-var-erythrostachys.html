Amaranthus hybridus var. erythrostachys

Amaranthus hybridus var. erythrostachys, commonly known as red amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and vibrant red bracts that surround the small, green flowers. The plant produces small, clustered seeds that are often used as a grain or for ornamental purposes. It thrives in warm climates and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for cultivation in various environments.

Its foliage is also edible and rich in nutrients, contributing to its value in both culinary and agricultural contexts.
