Apera intermedia

Apera intermedia, commonly known as common brome, is a grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-rowed spikelets.

Apera intermedia is known for its adaptability and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
