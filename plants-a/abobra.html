Abobra

Abobra, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple. Its berries, which are toxic to humans, are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Abobra thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.
