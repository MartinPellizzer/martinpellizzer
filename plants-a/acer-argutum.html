Acer argutum

Acer argutum, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 meters, with a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters before the leaves emerge in spring.

Acer argutum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
