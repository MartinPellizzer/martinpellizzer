Asplenium falcatum var. bipinnatum

Asplenium falcatum var. bipinnatum, commonly known as the bipinnate maidenhair fern, is a variant of the common maidenhair fern.

It is characterized by its finely divided, bipinnate fronds that give it a delicate, lacy appearance. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a soft, leathery texture.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
