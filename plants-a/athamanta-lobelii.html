Athamanta lobelii

Athamanta lobelii, commonly known as the Lobel's orchid, is a striking flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, Athamanta lobelii is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
