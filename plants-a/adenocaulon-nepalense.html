Adenocaulon nepalense

Adenocaulon nepalense, commonly known as the Nepal aster, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, often on rocky slopes and meadows. Its leaves are basal, alternate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Adenocaulon nepalense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
