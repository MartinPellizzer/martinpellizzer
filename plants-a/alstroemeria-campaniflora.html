Alstroemeria campaniflora

Alstroemeria campaniflora, commonly known as the bell-shaped alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Alstroemeria campaniflora is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
