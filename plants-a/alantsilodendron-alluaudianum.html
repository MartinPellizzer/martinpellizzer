Alantsilodendron alluaudianum

Alantsilodendron alluaudianum, commonly known as the Madagascar star tree, is a unique evergreen tree native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a dramatic visual element to its appearance. The plant has a distinctive bark that is smooth and reddish-brown, often with a peeling texture. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and leathery, providing a lush green canopy.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
