Actartife

Actartife, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves and delicate, bioluminescent flowers that glow faintly in the dark. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and requires specific soil conditions rich in organic matter. Its unique appearance and elusive nature have made it a subject of fascination among botanists and plant enthusiasts.

Due to its endangered status, conservation efforts are underway to protect this rare botanical wonder.
