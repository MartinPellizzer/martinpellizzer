Acer dasycarpum var. aureovariegatum

Acer dasycarpum var. aureovariegatum, commonly known as the golden-leaved maple, is a deciduous tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, which displays a vibrant yellow variegation during the growing season. The leaves are typically palmately lobed and turn brilliant red or orange in the fall. It produces small, red flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This ornamental tree is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
