Avena laevigata var. insubrica

Avena laevigata var. insubrica, commonly known as the smooth oat grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically smooth to the touch. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with bracts that are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

The botanical features of this variety make it an important component of native ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
