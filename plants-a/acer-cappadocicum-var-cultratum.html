Acer cappadocicum var. cultratum

Acer cappadocicum var. cultratum, commonly known as the Cappadocian maple, is a deciduous tree native to Turkey.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that are often lobed and have a serrated edge. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. It typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils.

The varietal form cultratum is distinguished by its more elongated leaf lobes compared to the typical species.
