Artocarpus melinoxylus subsp. brevipedunculatus

Artocarpus melinoxylus subsp.

brevipedunculatus, commonly known as the short-stalked breadfruit, is a tropical plant belonging to the Moraceae family. It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and produces a distinctive fruit with a rough, fibrous outer layer.

The fruit contains a large, starchy pulp that is edible and used in various culinary applications. It typically grows in lowland rainforests and thrives in warm, humid climates.

This subspecies is valued for both its ecological role and potential economic importance in local communities.
