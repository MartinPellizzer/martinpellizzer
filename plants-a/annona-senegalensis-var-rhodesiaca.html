Annona senegalensis var. rhodesiaca

Annona senegalensis var. rhodesiaca, commonly known as African breadfruit, is a tropical plant native to Central and West Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree with smooth, grey bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that develop into round, yellowish fruits resembling small melons. The fruit is edible, with a sweet, creamy flesh that is rich in nutrients and has a mild, aromatic flavor.

It is often cultivated for both its fruit and its ornamental value in tropical regions.
