Astragalus chubsugulicus

Astragalus chubsugulicus, commonly known as the Chubsugulic milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus chubsugulicus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
