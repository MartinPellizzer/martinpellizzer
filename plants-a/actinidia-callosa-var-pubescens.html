Actinidia callosa var. pubescens

Actinidia callosa var. pubescens, commonly known as the downy kiwi, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent stems and heart-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, followed by fuzzy, edible fruits that are typically harvested in the fall. Its berries are rich in vitamin C and have a sweet-tart flavor, making them popular in culinary and medicinal uses.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires support for its climbing growth habit.
