Atriplex patula var. subspicata

Atriplex patula var. subspicata, commonly known as saltbush or shadscale, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that are typically covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are often borne on short, erect stalks. It is highly adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a common component of desert ecosystems.

This species is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide forage for wildlife.
