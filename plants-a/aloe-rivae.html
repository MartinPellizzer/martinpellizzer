Aloe rivae

Aloe rivae, commonly known as the river aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces striking yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in racemes. Aloe rivae is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

Its leaves contain a gel that is sometimes used for its soothing properties.
