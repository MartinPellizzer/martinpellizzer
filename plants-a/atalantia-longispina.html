Atalantia longispina

Atalantia longispina, commonly known as the long-spined huito, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow. Its fruit is a spherical, woody drupe that contains a single seed.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
