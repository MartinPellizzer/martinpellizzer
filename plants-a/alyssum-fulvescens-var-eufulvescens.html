Alyssum fulvescens var. eufulvescens

Alyssum fulvescens var. eufulvescens, commonly known as the golden alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features gray-green, fuzzy foliage that is often aromatic when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact size and long blooming period make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
