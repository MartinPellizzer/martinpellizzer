Amberboa ramosissima

Amberboa ramosissima, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which consists of narrow, leathery leaves arranged in a dense, compact manner. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round fruits that are edible for some wildlife. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in dry climates.
