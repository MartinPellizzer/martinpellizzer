Aspidium beccarianum

Aspidium beccarianum, commonly known as the Beccari's water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, giving it a finely divided appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its sporangia are clustered in sori along the margins of the fronds, a common reproductive feature in ferns.

Aspidium beccarianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitats.
