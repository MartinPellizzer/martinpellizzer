Anchusa procera var. alboprocera

Anchusa procera var. alboprocera, commonly known as the white-hairy verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and profuse clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant features soft, hairy leaves that give it a fuzzy appearance, and it blooms from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is notable for its showy, long-lasting flowers and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
