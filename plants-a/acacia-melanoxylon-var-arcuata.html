Acacia melanoxylon var. arcuata

Acacia melanoxylon var. arcuata, commonly known as the mountain acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and phyllodes that are usually curved or arcuate, giving it its specific epithet. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical spikes, which appear in spring. It is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in coastal and tableland regions.

The wood of this species is valued for its durability and is used in various traditional and commercial applications.
