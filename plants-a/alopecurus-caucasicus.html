Alopecurus caucasicus

Alopecurus caucasicus, commonly known as Caucasian mouse-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe and Western Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its fine, tufted leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, greenish flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Alopecurus caucasicus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
