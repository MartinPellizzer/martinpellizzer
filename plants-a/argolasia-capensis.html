Argolasia capensis

Argolasia capensis, commonly known as the Cape argolasia, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, which are adaptations to its arid and semi-arid habitats. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to its appearance. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine layer of hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

Argolasia capensis is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
