Andropogon benthamianus

Andropogon benthamianus, commonly known as the Bentham's bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and compact growth habit, often forming dense clumps. The plant typically grows in wetland or moist soil environments, making it a common sight in marshes and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spike that bears small, spikelet-like flowers.

Andropogon benthamianus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
