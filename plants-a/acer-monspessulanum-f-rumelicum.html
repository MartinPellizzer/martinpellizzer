Acer monspessulanum f. rumelicum

Acer monspessulanum f. rumelicum, commonly known as the Rumelic horse chestnut, is a rare and ornamental variant of the horse chestnut tree.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves that turn a striking yellow in autumn. The plant produces showy, white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, which are clustered in panicles. Its fruits are spiny, woody capsules containing one or two seeds.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental foliage and floral display.
