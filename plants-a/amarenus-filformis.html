Amarenus filformis

Amarenus filformis, commonly known as the slender amaranth, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its thin, thread-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or red. The plant thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its seeds are small and can be dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread.

Amarenus filformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
