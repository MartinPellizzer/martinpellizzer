Amelanchier huronensis

Amelanchier huronensis, commonly known as Huron serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 feet, featuring a rounded, multi-stemmed form. The plant produces clusters of white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries in the summer. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, turning yellow in the autumn.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
