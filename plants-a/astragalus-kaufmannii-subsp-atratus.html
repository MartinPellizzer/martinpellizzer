Astragalus kaufmannii subsp. atratus

Astragalus kaufmannii subsp.

atratus, commonly known as the blackleaf milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dark, almost black foliage, which gives it its common name. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and open woodlands.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
