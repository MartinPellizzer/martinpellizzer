Antidesma cochinchinense

Antidesma cochinchinense, commonly known as the Vietnamese hibiscus or red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, red or pink flowers with five petals, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
