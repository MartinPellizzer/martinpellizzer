Acer dasycarpum f. curvatum

Acer dasycarpum f. curvatum, commonly known as the curly-leaf maple, is a unique variant of the Chinese maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy, and curled leaf margins, which set it apart from typical maple species. The plant is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for those seeking unique and visually appealing tree specimens.
