Angraecum trichoplectron

Angraecum trichoplectron, commonly known as the Trichoplectron orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It features long, slender pseudobulbs and produces pendulous, fragrant flowers with white petals and a distinctive violet-tinged lip. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media to prevent root rot. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.

Angraecum trichoplectron is also notable for its potential role in ecological studies due to its specialized pollination mechanisms.
