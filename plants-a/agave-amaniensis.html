Agave amaniensis

Agave amaniensis, commonly known as the Amani agave, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Africa.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that store water. The leaves are typically blue-green in color with sharp, spiny margins. It produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear white to pale yellow flowers.

This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.
