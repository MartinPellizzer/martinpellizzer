Agastache verticillata

Agastache verticillata, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads that appear in late summer. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Agastache verticillata is also valued for its aromatic foliage and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
