Aspidium filix-mas var. abbreviatum

Aspidium filix-mas var. abbreviatum, commonly known as the dwarf male fern, is a variant of the male fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, evergreen fern characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and fronds that are typically shorter than those of the standard variety. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, typical of ferns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and role in tropical ecosystems.
