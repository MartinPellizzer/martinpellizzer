Adiantum lucidum var. poeppigianum

Adiantum lucidum var. poeppigianum, commonly known as the Poeppig's maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It features finely divided, bright green fronds that are often adorned with a waxy coating, giving them a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
