Aragallus multiceps var. minor

Aragallus multiceps var. minor, commonly known as the lesser multiceps gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving at high elevations. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
