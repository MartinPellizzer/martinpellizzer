Astragalus convallarius var. finitimus

Astragalus convallarius var. finitimus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and grasslands. The plant features slender stems with alternate, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellowish flowers are arranged in spikes and bloom in early summer.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinator populations.
