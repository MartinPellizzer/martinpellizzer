Allium bivalve var. flavescens

Allium bivalve var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow onion, is a bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its fibrous, layered bulbs that store nutrients. The plant produces a tall, leafy stem topped with a spherical inflorescence of white or pale purple flowers. Its leaves are long, grass-like, and often used as a culinary herb.

The yellow onion is widely cultivated for its pungent flavor and is a staple in many cuisines worldwide.
