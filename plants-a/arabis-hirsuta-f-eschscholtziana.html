Arabis hirsuta f. eschscholtziana

Arabis hirsuta f. eschscholtziana, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with small, cross-shaped flowers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it produces clusters of yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and hardiness in cooler climates.
