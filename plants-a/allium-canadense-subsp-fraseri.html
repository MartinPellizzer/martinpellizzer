Allium canadense subsp. fraseri

Allium canadense subsp.

fraseri, commonly known as the Fraser's garlic, is a perennial herb native to North America. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous structure and grass-like leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
