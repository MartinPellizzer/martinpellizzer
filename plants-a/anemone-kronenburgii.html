Anemone kronenburgii

Anemone kronenburgii, commonly known as the Alpine anemone, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It features showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are hairy and covered in a dense layer of fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Anemone kronenburgii is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
