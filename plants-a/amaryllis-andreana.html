Amaryllis andreana

Amaryllis andreana, commonly known as the Andreana amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features tall, strap-like leaves and produces clusters of vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight to flourish. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

The blooming period typically occurs in the spring or early summer, depending on the climate.
