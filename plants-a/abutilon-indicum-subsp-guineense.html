Abutilon indicum subsp. guineense

Abutilon indicum subsp.

guineense, commonly known as the African velvetleaf, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are often lobed and have a velvety texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain areas, outcompeting native vegetation.
