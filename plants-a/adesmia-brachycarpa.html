Adesmia brachycarpa

Adesmia brachycarpa, commonly known as the short-fruited burr legume, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and dense clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are often covered in hooked bracts, which aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animal fur. It is adapted to survive in harsh conditions with minimal water, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in ecological and agricultural studies.
