Aster altaicus var. canescens

Aster altaicus var. canescens, commonly known as the white mountain aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.

Its blooms appear in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
