Anisolobus distinctus

Anisolobus distinctus, commonly known as the distinct spirea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges. Native to parts of Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun conditions.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
