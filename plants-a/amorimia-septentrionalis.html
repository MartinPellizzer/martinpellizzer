Amorimia septentrionalis

Amorimia septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern amorimia, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bright red flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its botanical features make it an important subject for ecological and conservation studies.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires protection to ensure its survival.
