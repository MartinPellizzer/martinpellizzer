Andrographis alata

Andrographis alata, commonly known as winged andrographis, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with prominent lateral veins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellowish-green in color. Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

Andrographis alata is often cultivated for traditional herbal use and ornamental purposes.
