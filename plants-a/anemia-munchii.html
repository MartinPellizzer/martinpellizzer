Anemia munchii

Anemia munchii, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. It produces small, toxic berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not suitable for consumption and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
