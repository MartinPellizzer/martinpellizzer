Anthericum rudatisii

Anthericum rudatisii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arum, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are dispersed by animals.

Anthericum rudatisii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
