Athyrium gaudichaudii

Athyrium gaudichaudii, commonly known as the Gaudichaud's lady fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its rhizomes are fibrous, and it produces spores in small, clustered clusters on the underside of its leaves.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic foliage.
