Alyssum maritimum var. densiflorum

Alyssum maritimum var. densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant features silver-gray foliage with densely packed leaves, giving it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy coastal environments.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
