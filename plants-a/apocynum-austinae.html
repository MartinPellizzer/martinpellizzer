Apocynum austinae

Apocynum austinae, commonly known as the Austin's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the United States, particularly found in Texas.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes many species with milky sap. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release its seeds.

Apocynum austinae is also noted for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally by Native American tribes.
