Aconitum sinomontanum

Aconitum sinomontanum, commonly known as the Chinese monk's hood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers with long, spurred petals that resemble a hood, giving it its common name. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed divisions, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
