Acaena insularis

Acaena insularis, commonly known as the Chatham Islands burr, is a flowering plant native to the Chatham Islands in New Zealand.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its fruit is a spiky, woody bur that contains seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

Acaena insularis thrives in coastal and rocky habitats, and it plays an important ecological role in its native environment.
