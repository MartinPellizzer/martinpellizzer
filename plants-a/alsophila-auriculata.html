Alsophila auriculata

Alsophila auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The fronds are typically dark green with a leathery texture and have a distinctive auriculate (ear-shaped) base. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests and along stream banks.

Its slow growth rate makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and conservation efforts.
