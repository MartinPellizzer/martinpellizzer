Arnica louiseana var. griscomii

Arnica louiseana var. griscomii, commonly known as the Gray's arnica, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces achenes as its fruit.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
