Agropyron fuegianum var. patagonicum

Agropyron fuegianum var. patagonicum, commonly known as Patagonian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and open areas with well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity.
