Astragalus turrillianus

Astragalus turrillianus, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of California and Nevada.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and dry, open habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus turrillianus plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and supporting biodiversity in its native range.
