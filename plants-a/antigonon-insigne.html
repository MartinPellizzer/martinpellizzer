Antigonon insigne

Antigonon insigne, commonly known as the flame vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is a trailing vine with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.

Antigonon insigne is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and butterflies.
