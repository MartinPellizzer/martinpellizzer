Alchemilla czamsinensis

Alchemilla czamsinensis, commonly known as the Czech alchemilla, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of soft, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clustered inflorescences during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Alchemilla czamsinensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to attract pollinators.
