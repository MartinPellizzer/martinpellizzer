Andropogon monatherus

Andropogon monatherus, commonly known as purple bluestem, is a tall grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that turn reddish-purple in the fall. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically purple or reddish in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common component of native grasslands and prairies.

Andropogon monatherus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
