Arundinaria trianae

Arundinaria trianae, commonly known as the Triana bamboo, is a species of grass native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Arundinaria trianae is adapted to moist environments and is often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks.

Its growth habit and morphology make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
