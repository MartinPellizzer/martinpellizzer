Allium stevenii

Allium stevenii, commonly known as Steven's garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers during the summer months. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a potential culinary and medicinal plant.

Allium stevenii thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
