Aconitum sachalinense f. tenuisectum

Aconitum sachalinense f. tenuisectum, commonly known as the slender-leaved monkshood, is a variant of the monkshood plant native to the Korean Peninsula.

It is characterized by its distinctive hood-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The leaves are finely divided, giving the plant a delicate, fern-like appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
