Anemia tomentosa var. mexicana

Anemia tomentosa var. mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican velvetleaf, is a tropical plant characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name.

It is native to Central America and parts of Mexico, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive silver-gray underside, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can become invasive in certain regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticulture.
