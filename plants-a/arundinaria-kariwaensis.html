Arundinaria kariwaensis

Arundinaria kariwaensis, commonly known as the Kariwa bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a smooth texture and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
