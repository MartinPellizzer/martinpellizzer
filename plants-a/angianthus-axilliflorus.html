Angianthus axilliflorus

Angianthus axilliflorus, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, green leaves. The plant produces yellow dandelion-like flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Angianthus axilliflorus is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
