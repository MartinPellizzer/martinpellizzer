Alternanthera tomentella

Alternanthera tomentella, commonly known as the velvetleaf or woolly Alternanthera, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value comes from its dense, textured leaves, making it a popular choice for aquatic and bog gardens.
