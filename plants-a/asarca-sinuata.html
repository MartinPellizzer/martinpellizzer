Asarca sinuata

Asarca sinuata, commonly known as the zigzag fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, undulating fronds that give it its common name. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, which helps it retain moisture in humid conditions.

Asarca sinuata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
