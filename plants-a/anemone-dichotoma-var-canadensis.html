Anemone dichotoma var. canadensis

Anemone dichotoma var. canadensis, commonly known as the Canadian anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features a basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces showy, white to pinkish flowers with five to seven petals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
