Alkanna graeca subsp. slavjankae

Alkanna graeca subsp.

slavjankae, commonly known as the Slavjanka alkanna, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and oblong, slightly hairy leaves.

The plant produces vibrant orange-red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its roots are rich in alkannin, a red pigment used in traditional medicine and dyeing.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and dry landscapes.
