Acalypha williamsii

Acalypha williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that often resemble flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are usually ovate or elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Acalypha williamsii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and gardens.
