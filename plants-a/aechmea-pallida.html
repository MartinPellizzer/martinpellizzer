Aechmea pallida

Aechmea pallida, commonly known as the silver vulture, is a species of bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, powdery bloom, giving it its common name. The plant produces a tall, flower-bearing stem that can reach up to 3 meters in height, with bright orange or red flowers that bloom in the summer. Aechmea pallida thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in the understory of tropical forests.

Its unique appearance and ability to thrive in specific ecological niches make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservatories.

Morphology

Aechmea pallida has leaves that are broad, strap-like, and arranged in a rosette, with a waxy, silver-green color and a textured, slightly hairy surface.

The plant lacks a traditional stem, instead forming a compact rosette with a central inflorescence. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix, surrounded by a colorful bract, and are typically pink or red, blooming during the summer months and pollinated by insects. The fruit is a capsule that produces small, black seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, anchoring the plant in the soil and absorbing moisture from the environment.
