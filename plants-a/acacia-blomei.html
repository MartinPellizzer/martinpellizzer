Acacia blomei

Acacia blomei, commonly known as the Blome's acacia, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular inflorescences. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small leaflets that give it a feathery appearance.

Acacia blomei is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
