Aristolochia rufomarginata

Aristolochia rufomarginata, commonly known as the red-marginated Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe, with a distinctive red margin on the petals. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, sticky coating. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, often with a strong, aromatic scent.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
