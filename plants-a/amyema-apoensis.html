Amyema apoensis

Amyema apoensis, commonly known as the mountain mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or heathland environments.

Amyema apoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
