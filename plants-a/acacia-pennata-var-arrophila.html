Acacia pennata var. arrophila

Acacia pennata var. arrophila, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, bipinnate leaves that are typically narrow and linear. The plant produces yellow or pale yellow inflorescences, which are clustered and attract various pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

Acacia pennata var. arrophila is well-suited to harsh environments and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
