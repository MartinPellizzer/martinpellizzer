Agrostis alba var. limosa

Agrostis alba var. limosa, commonly known as silvergrass or meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted inflorescence, which consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks, thriving in well-drained to moderately wet soils. It has a clumping growth habit and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its attractive appearance.

Agrostis alba var. limosa is also valued for its role in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
