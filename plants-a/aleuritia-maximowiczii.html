Aleuritia maximowiczii

Aleuritia maximowiczii, commonly known as the Chinese snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces round, red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Aleuritia maximowiczii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.
