Aster latifolius

Aster latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. It features lance-shaped leaves and produces showy blue to violet flowers in the fall.

Aster latifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
