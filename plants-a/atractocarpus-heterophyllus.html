Atractocarpus heterophyllus

Atractocarpus heterophyllus, commonly known as the Java laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy, dark green leaves that are typically alternate and elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The tree is valued for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
