Amomum spathilabium

Amomum spathilabium, commonly known as the Chinese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric. The plant features a rhizomatous stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, typically with white or pale pink petals.

The plant is valued for its aromatic rhizomes, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
