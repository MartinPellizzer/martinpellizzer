Amaranthus spinosus var. circumscissus

Amaranthus spinosus var. circumscissus, commonly known as the prickly amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and leaves, which provide a natural defense against herbivores. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that develop into three-winged seeds, typical of the genus Amaranthus. It is native to the Americas and thrives in warm, dry climates.

This variety is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
