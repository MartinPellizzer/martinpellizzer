Austrocylindropuntia vestita var. shaferi

Austrocylindropuntia vestita var. shaferi, commonly known as the Shafer's Pencil Cactus, is a compact cactus native to the arid regions of northern Argentina.

It features cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to bluish-green in color, with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to hot, dry climates and requires minimal water once established.

This variety is prized for its ornamental appearance and ease of cultivation in suitable environments.
