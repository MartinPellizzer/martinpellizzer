Aristolochia clematitis

Aristolochia clematitis, commonly known as birthwort, is a flowering plant in the family Aristolochiaceae.

It is characterized by its unique, heart-shaped leaves and intricate, pipe-like flowers that resemble a small horn. The plant is native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa, often growing in woodland areas. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a strong, musky odor, which attracts specific insect pollinators.

However, the plant is toxic due to the presence of aristolochic acids, making it harmful to humans and animals.
