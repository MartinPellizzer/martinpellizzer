Ampherephis indica

Ampherephis indica, commonly known as the Indian spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, often with yellow or greenish petals.

Ampherephis indica is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
