Astragalus podolobus

Astragalus podolobus, commonly known as the Sierra foothill milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces slender, spiny pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Astragalus podolobus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
