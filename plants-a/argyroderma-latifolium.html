Argyroderma latifolium

Argyroderma latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf silverleaf, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its broad, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its unique foliage and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
