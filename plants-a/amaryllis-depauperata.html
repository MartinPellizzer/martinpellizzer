Amaryllis depauperata

Amaryllis depauperata, commonly known as the slender amaryllis, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a bulbous plant characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and clusters of striking, funnel-shaped flowers. The flowers typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, adding vibrant color to gardens. This plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for both outdoor gardens and container planting.

Its ornamental value and relatively easy care make it a popular choice among gardeners.
