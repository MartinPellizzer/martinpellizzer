Anisotes pubinervius

Anisotes pubinervius, commonly known as the hairy-veined water plant, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant has delicate, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy structures along the veins. Its reproductive structures include small, spore-bearing sporangia that develop on the underside of the leaves.

Anisotes pubinervius plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
