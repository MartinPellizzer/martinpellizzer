Allium pekinense

Allium pekinense, commonly known as the Peking garlic or Chinese leek, is a bulbous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical bulbs and grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. It is often cultivated for its edible bulbs, which have a mild garlic flavor.

Allium pekinense prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.
