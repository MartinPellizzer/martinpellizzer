Amphidasya colombiana

Amphidasya colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in cloud forests. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, and it produces small, clustered blooms in various shades of white and pink.

Amphidasya colombiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
