Anemone collina

Anemone collina, commonly known as the rock anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in open woodlands or along streambanks. The plant features showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, with petal-like sepals that resemble true petals. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette and are typically covered in fine hairs.

Anemone collina is often found in moist, well-drained habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
