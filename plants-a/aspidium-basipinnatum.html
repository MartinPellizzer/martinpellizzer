Aspidium basipinnatum

Aspidium basipinnatum, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that are typically green and glossy. The plant produces spores in sporangia located on the underside of its fertile fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate regions.

Aspidium basipinnatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
