Amelanchier neumanniana

Amelanchier neumanniana, commonly known as the Neumann's serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its attractive white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries in the summer, which are dark blue to black in color and have a sweet, tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or open areas.

Amelanchier neumanniana is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
