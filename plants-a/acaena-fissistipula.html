Acaena fissistipula

Acaena fissistipula, commonly known as the New Zealand thistle, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces achene fruits that are often covered in hooked bristles, aiding in seed dispersal by attaching to animals. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including coastal areas and dry forests, and is adapted to the temperate climate of New Zealand.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered an invasive species in some regions outside its native range.
