Aspidium mercurii

Aspidium mercurii, commonly known as the mercury fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically dark green with a glossy appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Aspidium mercurii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in controlled indoor conditions.
