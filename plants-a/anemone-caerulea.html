Anemone caerulea

Anemone caerulea, commonly known as the blue anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. Its stems are upright and support the flowers, which have five to seven petals.

Anemone caerulea prefers well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in alpine environments.
