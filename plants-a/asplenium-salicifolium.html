Asplenium salicifolium

Asplenium salicifolium, commonly known as the willow fern, is a species of fern native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, willow-like leaves that are typically lance-shaped and slightly hairy. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests or along stream banks. Its fronds are usually evergreen, with a dark green color and a fine, serrated edge.

Asplenium salicifolium is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in shady garden settings.
