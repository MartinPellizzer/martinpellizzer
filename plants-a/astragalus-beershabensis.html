Astragalus beershabensis

Astragalus beershabensis, commonly known as the Beersheba milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitats.
