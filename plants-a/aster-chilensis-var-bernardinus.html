Aster chilensis var. bernardinus

Aster chilensis var. bernardinus, commonly known as the Chilean aster, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in rocky or alpine environments, and thrives in cooler climates. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
