Adenophora communis var. latifolia

Adenophora communis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are broader than the typical variety, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
