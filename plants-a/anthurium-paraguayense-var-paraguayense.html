Anthurium paraguayense var. paraguayense

Anthurium paraguayense var. paraguayense, commonly known as the Paraguay anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped leaves and striking spadix inflorescence. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, thriving in humid, shaded environments with high air circulation. Its foliage is often a deep green with a glossy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a colorful spathe.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
