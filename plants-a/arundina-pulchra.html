Arundina pulchra

Arundina pulchra, commonly known as the purple bamboo, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 6 meters in height, forming dense clumps. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, violet-blue flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Arundina pulchra is well-suited for tropical and subtropical climates, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscape design.
