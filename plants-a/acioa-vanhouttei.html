Acioa vanhouttei

Acioa vanhouttei, commonly known as the Vanhoutte's acacia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in clusters. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
