Astragalus matthewsiae

Astragalus matthewsiae, commonly known as Matthews' milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant has compound leaves with small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
