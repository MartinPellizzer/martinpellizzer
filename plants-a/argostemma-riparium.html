Argostemma riparium

Argostemma riparium, commonly known as water arum, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are usually broad and arrow-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are small and clustered, often with a strong odor that attracts pollinators like flies and beetles.
