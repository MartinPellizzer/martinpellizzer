Allium amansii

Allium amansii, commonly known as the Chinese leek, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its long, cylindrical leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that are often used in culinary applications, particularly in Chinese and Korean cuisines. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a robust, aromatic foliage.

Allium amansii thrives in temperate climates and is valued both for its edible parts and ornamental qualities.
