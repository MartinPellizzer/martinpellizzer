Amana

Amana, commonly known as the "African violet," is a popular ornamental plant native to Tanzania.

It belongs to the genus Saintpaulia and is characterized by its velvety, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Amana flowers come in a variety of colors, including pink, purple, and white, making it a favorite among gardeners.

It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ability to bloom year-round under proper care.
