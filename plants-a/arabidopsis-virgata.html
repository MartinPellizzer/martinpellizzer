Arabidopsis virgata

Arabidopsis virgata, commonly known as the mountain Arabidopsis, is a species of flowering plant in the mustard family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America. This plant is characterized by its small, delicate flowers and hairy stems. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes.

Arabidopsis virgata is of interest to botanists due to its genetic diversity and adaptability to harsh environments.
