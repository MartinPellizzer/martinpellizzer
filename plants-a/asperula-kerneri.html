Asperula kerneri

Asperula kerneri, commonly known as the Alpine azalea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and evergreen foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during the spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist environments.

Asperula kerneri is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardiness.
