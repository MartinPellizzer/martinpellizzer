Aglaia soepadmoi

Aglaia soepadmoi, commonly known as the Java Aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Magnoliaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest regions.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
