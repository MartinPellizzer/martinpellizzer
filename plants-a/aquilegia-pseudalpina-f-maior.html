Aquilegia pseudalpina f. maior

Aquilegia pseudalpina f. maior, commonly known as the greater alpine columbine, is a botanical variant of the alpine columbine.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant is native to the alpine regions of Europe and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and it produces follicles that contain the seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in alpine ecosystems.
