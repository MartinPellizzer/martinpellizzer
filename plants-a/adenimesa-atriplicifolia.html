Adenimesa atriplicifolia

Adenimesa atriplicifolia, commonly known as the Mexican rue, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically trifoliate or pinnate, resembling those of the rue plant. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, leathery fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
