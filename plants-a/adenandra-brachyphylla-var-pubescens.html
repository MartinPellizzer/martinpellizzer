Adenandra brachyphylla var. pubescens

Adenandra brachyphylla var. pubescens, commonly known as the rough-leaved adenandra, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its densely hairy, narrow leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in fynbos vegetation.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
