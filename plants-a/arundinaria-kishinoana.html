Arundinaria kishinoana

Arundinaria kishinoana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Kishino bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its culms are hollow and jointed, a typical feature of the grass family.

Arundinaria kishinoana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Japanese crafts.
