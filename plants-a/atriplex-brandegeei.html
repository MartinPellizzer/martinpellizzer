Atriplex brandegeei

Atriplex brandegeei, commonly known as the desert saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, gray-green leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It is adapted to grow in saline and alkaline soils, making it a common species in desert ecosystems.

Its deep root system allows it to access water from deeper soil layers, enhancing its survival in harsh conditions.
