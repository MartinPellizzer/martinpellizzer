Astragalus pityusarum

Astragalus pityusarum, commonly known as the Canary Island milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus pityusarum is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and limited distribution.
