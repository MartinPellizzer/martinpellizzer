Acacia rohriana

Acacia rohriana, commonly known as the white acacia, is a small shrub native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which bloom during the rainy season. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are adapted to disperse in dry conditions.

Acacia rohriana is well-suited to harsh environments and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fodder.
