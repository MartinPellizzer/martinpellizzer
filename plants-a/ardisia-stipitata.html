Ardisia stipitata

Ardisia stipitata, commonly known as the clustered beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are a key feature of its botanical appearance. These berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various bird species.

Ardisia stipitata is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and ecological benefits.
