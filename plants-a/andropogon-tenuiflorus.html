Andropogon tenuiflorus

Andropogon tenuiflorus, commonly known as narrow-flower grass, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

Andropogon tenuiflorus is often found in open habitats such as savannas, grasslands, and disturbed soils.
