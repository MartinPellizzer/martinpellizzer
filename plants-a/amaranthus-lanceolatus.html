Amaranthus lanceolatus

Amaranthus lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceleaf amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and tall, branched inflorescences that produce numerous small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in a variety of habitats, including disturbed soils and open fields. Its foliage is often used in traditional cuisines, and it is also valued for its ornamental appeal.

Amaranthus lanceolatus is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
