Arabis deflexa

Arabis deflexa, commonly known as the drooping rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or arid environments. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed or divided, and its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Arabis deflexa is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with characteristic cruciform flowers.

It is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
