Asplenium megaphyllum

Asplenium megaphyllum, commonly known as the giant shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, broad, leathery fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium megaphyllum is a member of the Aspleniaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
