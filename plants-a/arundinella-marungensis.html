Arundinella marungensis

Arundinella marungensis, commonly known as the Marung mountain bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are usually arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Arundinella marungensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
