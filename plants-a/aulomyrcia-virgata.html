Aulomyrcia virgata

Aulomyrcia virgata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia uniflora*, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy evergreen leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
