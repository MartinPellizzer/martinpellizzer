Aechmea mcvaughii

Aechmea mcvaughii, commonly known as the McVaugh's Bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Mexico.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant, long-lasting flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture.

Aechmea mcvaughii is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
