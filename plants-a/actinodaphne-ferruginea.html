Actinodaphne ferruginea

Actinodaphne ferruginea, commonly known as the red sandalwood or ironwood tree, is a member of the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood and glossy, elliptical leaves. It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been historically used in construction and traditional crafts.
