Araucaria laubenfelsii

Araucaria laubenfelsii, commonly known as the Laubenfels' Araucaria, is a rare and ancient conifer native to the high-altitude forests of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of dense, feather-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of conifers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its limited natural range and habitat threats, it is considered a vulnerable species.
