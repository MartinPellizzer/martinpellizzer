Amblyopogon integrifolius

Amblyopogon integrifolius, commonly known as the smooth-leafed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with smooth margins. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

Amblyopogon integrifolius is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
