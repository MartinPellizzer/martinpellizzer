Acer ginnala var. aidzuense

Acer ginnala var. aidzuense, commonly known as the Aidzu maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by samaroid fruits that disperse its seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its seasonal foliage.
