Asphodelus fistulosus subsp. fistulosus

Asphodelus fistulosus subsp.

fistulosus, commonly known as the common asphodel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall spikes of pale yellow to greenish-yellow flowers.

The plant is characterized by its tubular flowers, which are typically arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is noted for its resilience and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
