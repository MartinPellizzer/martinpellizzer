Asphodelus microcarpus var. nervosus

Asphodelus microcarpus var. nervosus, commonly known as the narrow-veined asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features strap-like, grass-like leaves with prominent, parallel veins, and it produces tall, branched inflorescences of yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
