Adiantum trapeziforme

Adiantum trapeziforme, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically bright green. The plant has a creeping rhizome that produces upright, lance-shaped pinnae with a distinctive trapezoidal shape. It prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Adiantum trapeziforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
