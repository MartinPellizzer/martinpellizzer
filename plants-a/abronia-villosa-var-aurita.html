Abronia villosa var. aurita

Abronia villosa var. aurita, commonly known as the woolly sand verbena, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. Its growth habit is low and spreading, often forming dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
