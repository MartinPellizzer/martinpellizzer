Acacia orthocarpa

Acacia orthocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and yellow floral spikes that appear in spring. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Acacia orthocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
