Ajuga turkestanica

Ajuga turkestanica, commonly known as Persian bugleweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are often hairy, and it produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and have a serrated margin. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
