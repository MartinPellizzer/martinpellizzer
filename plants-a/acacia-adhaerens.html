Acacia adhaerens

Acacia adhaerens, commonly known as the Brazilian wattle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless branches and compound leaves with small, oval leaflets. The plant produces yellow floral spikes that bloom in the dry season, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are enclosed in pod-like fruits that split open to release the seeds.

Acacia adhaerens is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
