Alstroemeria pavoniana

Alstroemeria pavoniana, commonly known as the rainbow lily, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that resemble lilies. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a rosette pattern, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Alstroemeria pavoniana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a cut flower.
