Aeonium × riosjordanae

Aeonium × riosjordanae, commonly known as the "Coral Beauty," is a hybrid succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features dense, fleshy rosettes of blue-green leaves that resemble coral in appearance, giving it its common name. The plant produces tall, branching inflorescences with yellow flowers during the spring or early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact size and striking color make it a visually appealing addition to both indoor and outdoor landscapes.
