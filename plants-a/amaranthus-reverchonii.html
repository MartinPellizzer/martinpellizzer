Amaranthus reverchonii

Amaranthus reverchonii, commonly known as the velvetleaf amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that range in color from red to purple, adding visual interest to its appearance. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in fields.

Amaranthus reverchonii is an annual plant that is adapted to full sun and well-drained soil conditions.
