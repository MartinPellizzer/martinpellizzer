Agave cordillerensis

Agave cordillerensis, commonly known as the Mexican blue agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that can reach several meters in height, bearing small, white flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soil.

Agave cordillerensis is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance.
