Astragalus trichopodus var. lonchus

Astragalus trichopodus var. lonchus, commonly known as the long-beak milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish to greenish flowers arranged in racemes. The plant has compound leaves with pinnate arrangement, and its root system is extensive, adapted to nutrient-poor soils. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many nitrogen-fixing species.

This variant is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
