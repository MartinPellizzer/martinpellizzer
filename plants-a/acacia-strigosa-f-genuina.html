Acacia strigosa f. genuina

Acacia strigosa f. genuina, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features small, yellow, globular flowers that bloom in winter, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system for water uptake.

Its ecological significance includes providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
