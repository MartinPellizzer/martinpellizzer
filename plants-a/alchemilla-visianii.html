Alchemilla visianii

Alchemilla visianii, commonly known as the Visianii lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of velvety, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Alchemilla visianii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and modest blooms.
