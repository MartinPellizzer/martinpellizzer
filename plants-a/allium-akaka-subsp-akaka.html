Allium akaka subsp. akaka

Allium akaka subsp.

akaka, commonly known as the Hawaiian leek, is a herbaceous plant native to the Hawaiian Islands. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, strap-like leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
