Aromadendron bintuluense

Aromadendron bintuluense, commonly known as the Bintulu aromatic tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and fragrant, small flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, which is often used in traditional medicine. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves and bark, are valued for their aromatic properties and potential medicinal uses.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
