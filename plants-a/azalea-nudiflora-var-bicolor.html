Azalea nudiflora var. bicolor

Azalea nudiflora var. bicolor, commonly known as the pink and white azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring, displaying a striking bicolor pattern of pink and white. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a lush backdrop for its floral display. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to produce abundant blooms year after year.
