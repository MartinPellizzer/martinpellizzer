Avoira vulgaris

Avoira vulgaris, commonly known as the common avoira, is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions.

It typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Avoira vulgaris thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens and natural habitats.
