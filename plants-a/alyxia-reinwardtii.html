Alyxia reinwardtii

Alyxia reinwardtii, commonly known as the red-flowered alyxia, is a woody vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Alyxia reinwardtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
