Aster tomentosus

Aster tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and clusters of small, blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster tomentosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
