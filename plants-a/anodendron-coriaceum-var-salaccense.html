Anodendron coriaceum var. salaccense

Anodendron coriaceum var. salaccense, commonly known as the Salacca palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible fruits resembling small, round berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine and basket weaving in some regions.
