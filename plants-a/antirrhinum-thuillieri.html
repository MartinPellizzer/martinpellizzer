Antirrhinum thuillieri

Antirrhinum thuillieri, commonly known as the Moroccan snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces bright, showy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

Antirrhinum thuillieri is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
