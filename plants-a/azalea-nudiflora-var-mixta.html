Azalea nudiflora var. mixta

Azalea nudiflora var. mixta, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped blossoms in shades of pink, red, and white, which are often fragrant. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant flowers.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
