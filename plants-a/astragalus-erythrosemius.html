Astragalus erythrosemius

Astragalus erythrosemius, commonly known as the red-seed milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, Mongolia, and Russia. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, compound leaves, and small, yellowish to reddish flowers. Its pods contain small, rounded seeds that are typically reddish in color, giving the species its common name.

Astragalus erythrosemius is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
