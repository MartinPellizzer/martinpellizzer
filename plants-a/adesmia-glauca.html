Adesmia glauca

Adesmia glauca, commonly known as the bluebush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub with woody stems and small, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, pale yellow or greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Adesmia glauca plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
