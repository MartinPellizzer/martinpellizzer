Anticharis imbricata

Anticharis imbricata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Anticharis imbricata is an annual herb that thrives in disturbed soils and can be invasive in some areas.

It is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.
