Aeonium × junionae

Aeonium × junionae, commonly known as the "Tree Aeonium," is a hybrid succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically fleshy and covered in a white powdery coating called farina. The plant produces tall, branched flower stalks that bear clusters of yellow flowers, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
