Arenga micrantha

Arenga micrantha, commonly known as the small areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed trunk and pinnate leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, typical of the Arecaceae family. Its fruit is a small, fibrous capsule that contains edible seeds.

Arenga micrantha is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has traditional uses in some regions.
