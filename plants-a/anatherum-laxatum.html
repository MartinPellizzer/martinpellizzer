Anatherum laxatum

Anatherum laxatum, commonly known as the looseleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often arching stems. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with lemma-based flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a rough texture and prominent veins.

Anatherum laxatum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
