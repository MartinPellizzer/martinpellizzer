Antennaria germanica

Antennaria germanica, commonly known as the German mouse-eared chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with small, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Caryophyllaceae family.
