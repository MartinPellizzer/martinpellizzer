Aeschynomene pygmaea var. pygmaea

Aeschynomene pygmaea var. pygmaea, commonly known as dwarf poinciana, is a small shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of bright red or pink flowers. The plant is typically found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
