Asplenium obtusum

Asplenium obtusum, commonly known as the blunt fern, is a species of evergreen fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its leathery, dark green fronds that are typically broad and rounded at the tips. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in temperate regions.

Asplenium obtusum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
