Arthrosamanea polyantha

Arthrosamanea polyantha, commonly known as the clustered sago palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit with multiple stems. The plant produces large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length, contributing to its ornamental value. Its fruit is a small, fibrous drupe that contains a single seed.

Arthrosamanea polyantha is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is used in landscaping in warm climates.
