Atriplex elegans var. fasciculata

Atriplex elegans var. fasciculata, commonly known as silverleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs that help reduce water loss. The plant has small, green, egg-shaped leaves that are often clustered in fascicles, giving it its specific variety name. It produces small yellow flowers in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer.

This species is well adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable plant for stabilizing desert landscapes.
