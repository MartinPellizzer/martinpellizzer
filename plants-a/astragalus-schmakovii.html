Astragalus schmakovii

Astragalus schmakovii, commonly known as Schmakov's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky or sandy soils. It produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.
