Ambaiba pachystachya

Ambaiba pachystachya, commonly known as the hairy panic grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Ambaiba pachystachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
