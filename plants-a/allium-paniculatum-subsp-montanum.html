Allium paniculatum subsp. montanum

Allium paniculatum subsp.

montanum, commonly known as mountain garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant is characterized by its strong garlic scent and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. It blooms in late spring to early summer and is adapted to cool, moist environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
