Acacia scorpioides var. pubescens

Acacia scorpioides var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spherical or cylindrical clusters. Its leaves are bipinnately compound, with numerous tiny leaflets that give it a feathery appearance.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
