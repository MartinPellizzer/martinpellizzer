Amaranthus blitoides var. flexuosus

Amaranthus blitoides var. flexuosus, commonly known as the curlleaf amaranth, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its prostrate growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers that develop into three-angled, ribbed capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

The curlleaf amaranth is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
