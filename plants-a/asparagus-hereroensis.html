Asparagus hereroensis

Asparagus hereroensis, commonly known as the Herero asparagus, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in terminal panicles. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Asparagus hereroensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
