Aegilops squarrosa var. comosa

Aegilops squarrosa var. comosa, commonly known as the hairy wild rye, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces spikelets that are typically 3 to 5 centimeters long, with lemmas that are often hairy and awned. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils and full sun exposure.

Aegilops squarrosa var. comosa is often found in disturbed sites, along roadsides, and in open fields.
