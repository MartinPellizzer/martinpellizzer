Aristida kerstingii

Aristida kerstingii, commonly known as Kersting's dropseed, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Aristida kerstingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils in dry environments.
