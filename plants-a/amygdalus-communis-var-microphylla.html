Amygdalus communis var. microphylla

Amygdalus communis var. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf almond.

This variety is a compact shrub native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its small, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. It produces edible nuts similar to those of the common almond, though they are typically smaller and less oily. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its winter interest.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to many landscapes.
