Aesculus × carnea var. briotii

Aesculus × carnea var. briotii, commonly known as the Briot's horse chestnut, is a hybrid plant originating from the cross between Aesculus × carnea and Aesculus hippocastanum.

It is characterized by its striking, double-flowered blooms that range from pink to red, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant produces large, spiny seed pods that contain glossy, chestnut-like seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental planting.

The leaves are palmately compound, typically with 5–7 leaflets, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
