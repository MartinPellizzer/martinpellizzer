Arenaria colorata

Arenaria colorata, commonly known as the colored sandwort, is a flowering plant in the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

Arenaria colorata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover in suitable climates.
