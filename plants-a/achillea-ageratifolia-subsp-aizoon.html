Achillea ageratifolia subsp. aizoon

Achillea ageratifolia subsp.

aizoon, commonly known as the Armenian yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer.
