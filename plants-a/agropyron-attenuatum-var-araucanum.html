Agropyron attenuatum var. araucanum

Agropyron attenuatum var. araucanum, commonly known as the Araucanian needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native ecosystems in its range.
