Aloysia ovatifolia

Aloysia ovatifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, ovate leaves and spikes of showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or well-drained soils. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aloysia ovatifolia is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
