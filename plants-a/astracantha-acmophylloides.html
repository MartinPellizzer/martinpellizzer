Astracantha acmophylloides

Astracantha acmophylloides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sandburs, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, bur-like fruits that cling to animal fur, aiding in seed dispersal. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
