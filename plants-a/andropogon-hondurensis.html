Andropogon hondurensis

Andropogon hondurensis, commonly known as Honduras bluestem, is a perennial grass native to Central America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green foliage. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Andropogon hondurensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in native landscapes.
