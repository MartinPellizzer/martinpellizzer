Acacia lamprocarpa

Acacia lamprocarpa, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow, globular flower heads that appear in late summer to early autumn. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of true leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Acacia lamprocarpa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
