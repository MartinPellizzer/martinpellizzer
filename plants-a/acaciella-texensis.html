Acaciella texensis

Acaciella texensis, commonly known as the Texas acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Texas and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often reduced to small, needle-like structures to minimize water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates.

Acaciella texensis plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
