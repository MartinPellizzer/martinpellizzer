Arabidopsis bursifolia var. beringensis

Arabidopsis bursifolia var. beringensis, commonly known as the Bering Sea Arabidopsis, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. This variety is characterized by its small, compact growth habit and hairy stems, which help it survive in cold climates. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, and it produces small, pod-like fruits.

The plant is often studied for its genetic simplicity and its role in understanding plant adaptation to extreme environments.
