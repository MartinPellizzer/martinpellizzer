Argentina stenophylla var. stenophylla

Argentina stenophylla var. stenophylla, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are upright and branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
