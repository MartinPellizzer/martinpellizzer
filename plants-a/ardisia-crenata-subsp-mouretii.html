Ardisia crenata subsp. mouretii

Ardisia crenata subsp.

mouretii, commonly known as the Mouret's beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant produces bright purple berries that are edible for humans but toxic to livestock. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
