Acacia errabunda

Acacia errabunda, commonly known as the wandering acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched structure and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that function as leaves, and it is adapted to arid environments. Acacia errabunda is often found in sandy soils and is used in traditional medicine by local communities.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
