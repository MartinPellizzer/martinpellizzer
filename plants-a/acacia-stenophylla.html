Acacia stenophylla

Acacia stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and narrow, phyllodes that are typically yellow-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Acacia stenophylla is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through its reduced leaf structure.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
