Astragalus novoascanicus

Astragalus novoascanicus, commonly known as the Novascanic milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation across its native habitat.
