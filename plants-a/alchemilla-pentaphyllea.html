Alchemilla pentaphyllea

Alchemilla pentaphyllea, commonly known as the five-leafed lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. Its foliage is often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

Alchemilla pentaphyllea is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, shady conditions.
