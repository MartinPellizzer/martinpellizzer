Aglaia ferruginea

Aglaia ferruginea, commonly known as the red ironwood tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Meliaceae family, which includes mahogany and satinwood species. The tree is characterized by its dense, dark reddish-brown wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing seeds.
