Antiaris saccidora

Antiaris saccidora, commonly known as the African arrowroot or false arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its tropical, herbaceous growth habit. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface, and it produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. It is often cultivated for its starchy underground tubers, which are used as a food source.

However, the plant contains toxic compounds, requiring careful preparation before consumption.
