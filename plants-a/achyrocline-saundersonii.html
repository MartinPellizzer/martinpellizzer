Achyrocline saundersonii

Achyrocline saundersonii, commonly known as the sandalwood aster, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica.

It is a herbaceous perennial that grows in coastal areas and rocky soils, often in humid environments. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
