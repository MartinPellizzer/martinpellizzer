Avenastrum elongatum var. friesiorum

Avenastrum elongatum var. friesiorum, commonly known as the Fries' tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellowish-green flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It produces small, composite flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
