Amauropelta consanguinea

Amauropelta consanguinea, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish wood and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are small and white or pink, often appearing in clusters, and it produces small, woody fruits.

Due to its valuable timber, it is considered a threatened species in its native habitat.
