Acer sempervirens

Acer sempervirens, commonly known as the evergreen maple, is a species of maple native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage that remains throughout the year, giving it its evergreen name. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its bark is smooth and reddish-brown. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring followed by winged seeds.

This ornamental tree is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and adaptability to various climates.
