Acrocomia armentalis

Acrocomia armentalis, commonly known as the African palm or the white palm, is a tall, slender palm native to tropical Africa.

It features a straight, unbranched trunk covered with a fibrous, leafless sheath. The plant produces large, feather-like leaves that can reach several meters in length. Its inflorescence is a branched structure bearing small, yellowish flowers, which are followed by elongated, three-angled fruits.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used for its edible seeds and fiber.
