Andropogon floridanus

Andropogon floridanus, commonly known as Florida bluestem, is a native grass species of the United States, particularly found in the southeastern region.

It is a warm-season perennial grass that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant has narrow, blue-green leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale blue or purplish in color. It is often used in native plant landscaping due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.

Florida bluestem plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
