Alchemilla glabrata

Alchemilla glabrata, commonly known as smooth lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Alchemilla glabrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
