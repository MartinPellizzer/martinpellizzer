Ammannia prieuriana

Ammannia prieuriana, commonly known as the redroot dayflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a reddish or purple tinge.

Ammannia prieuriana is a perennial herb that plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
