Acacia pennata subsp. hainanensis

Acacia pennata subsp.

hainanensis, commonly known as Hainan acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern China. It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and phyllodes that are typically straight or slightly curved.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are modified into phyllodes, a common feature in the genus Acacia, reducing water loss in arid environments.

This subspecies is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in open forests or along coastal areas.
