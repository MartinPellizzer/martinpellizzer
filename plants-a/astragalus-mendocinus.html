Astragalus mendocinus

Astragalus mendocinus, commonly known as the Mendocino milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is known for its deep taproot system, which allows it to survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus mendocinus is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and soil stabilization.
