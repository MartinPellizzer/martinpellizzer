Arrhenatherum elatius var. album

Arrhenatherum elatius var. album, commonly known as the white meadow grass, is a variant of the common meadow grass species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in moist meadows and grasslands. The plant features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its distinguishing feature is the white to pale pink inflorescence, which consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small flowers.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its habitat.
