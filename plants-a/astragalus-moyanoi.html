Astragalus moyanoi

Astragalus moyanoi, commonly known as the Mongolian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its deep root system, which allows it to thrive in dry, sandy soils. Astragalus moyanoi is also valued for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
