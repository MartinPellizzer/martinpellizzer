Abutilon thurberi

Abutilon thurberi, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, maple-like leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Abutilon thurberi is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
