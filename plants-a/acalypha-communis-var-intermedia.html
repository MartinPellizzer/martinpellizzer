Acalypha communis var. intermedia

Acalypha communis var. intermedia, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a striking ornamental species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, orange, or yellow bracts that resemble small lanterns, which are modified leaves that enclose the tiny true flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of 1 to 3 feet, and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often green with a metallic sheen, adding visual interest to gardens.

It is well-suited for container gardening and is popular in tropical and subtropical climates for its ornamental value.
