Amblytropis multiflora

Amblytropis multiflora, commonly known as the multiflora amblytropis, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clustered growth habit and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, aiding in its dispersal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
