Anaxeton eximium

Anaxeton eximium, commonly known as the broad-leaved anaxeton, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Anaxeton eximium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to arid environments and resilient to drought conditions.
