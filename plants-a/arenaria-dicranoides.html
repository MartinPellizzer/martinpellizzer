Arenaria dicranoides

Arenaria dicranoides, commonly known as the sand sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It thrives in harsh, rocky, and sandy environments, showcasing its adaptability to extreme climates. The plant has small, needle-like leaves that help reduce water loss and withstand strong winds. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Arenaria dicranoides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
