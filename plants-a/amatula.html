Amatula

Amatula, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. Amatula thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its flowers bloom in the early morning, giving it its common name.
