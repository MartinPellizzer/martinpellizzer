Anemone henryi

Anemone henryi, commonly known as Henry's anemone, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It features a basal rosette of velvety, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
