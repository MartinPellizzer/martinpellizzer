Acanthophyllum gracile

Acanthophyllum gracile, commonly known as the slender spiny shrub, is a low-growing, perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems and reduced leaf surfaces to minimize water loss. It often forms part of the vegetation in sandy soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.

Its resilience makes it an important species for stabilizing desert landscapes.
