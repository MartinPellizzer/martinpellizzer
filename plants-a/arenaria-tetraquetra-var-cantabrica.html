Arenaria tetraquetra var. cantabrica

Arenaria tetraquetra var. cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the coastal regions of northern Spain.

It thrives in sandy and rocky soils, often found in dune environments and coastal cliffs. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. Its leaves are linear and succulent, adapted to retain moisture in arid conditions.

This variety is notable for its resilience and is often used in ecological restoration projects along shorelines.
