Achillea millefolium var. tanacetifolia

Achillea millefolium var. tanacetifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically narrow and linear, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow composite flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its aromatic foliage and medicinal properties have made it historically significant in herbal medicine.
