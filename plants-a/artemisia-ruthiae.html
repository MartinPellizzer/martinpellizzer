Artemisia ruthiae

Artemisia ruthiae, commonly known as Ruth's sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open plains. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its strong, minty scent.

Artemisia ruthiae plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
