Astragalus ancocarpus

Astragalus ancocarpus, commonly known as the redroot milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces cylindrical, woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Astragalus ancocarpus plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
