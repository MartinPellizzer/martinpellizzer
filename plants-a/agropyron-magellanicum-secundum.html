Agropyron magellanicum secundum

Agropyron magellanicum secundum, commonly known as the Patagonian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly found in the Patagonian region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in harsh, arid environments and is adapted to withstand cold temperatures and poor soil conditions.

This grass plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for grazing animals in its native habitat.
