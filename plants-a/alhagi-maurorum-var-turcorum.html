Alhagi maurorum var. turcorum

Alhagi maurorum var. turcorum, commonly known as the Turkish milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in drought conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are edible and can be processed into flour for human consumption.
