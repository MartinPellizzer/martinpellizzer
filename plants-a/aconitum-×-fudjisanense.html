Aconitum × fudjisanense

Aconitum × fudjisanense, commonly known as the Japanese monkshood, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple spathaceous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its foliage consists of deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

Aconitum × fudjisanense is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
