Aquilegia vulgaris var. corniculata

Aquilegia vulgaris var. corniculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved columbine, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features striking, spurred flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with finely divided lobes, and its stems are often reddish or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
