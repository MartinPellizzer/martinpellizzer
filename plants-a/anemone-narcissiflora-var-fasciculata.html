Anemone narcissiflora var. fasciculata

Anemone narcissiflora var. fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive yellow center, blooming in early to mid-summer. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its stems are hairy as well. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant collections and ecological restoration projects.
