Arachis diogoi f. sericeovillosa

Arachis diogoi f. sericeovillosa, commonly known as the silky hairy groundnut, is a botanical variant of the groundnut genus Arachis.

It is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. This plant is native to South America and is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. It produces small, yellow flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds.

The silky hairy groundnut is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its unique morphology and potential for cultivation.
