Aydendron sericeum

Aydendron sericeum, commonly known as silky aydendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in garden settings.
