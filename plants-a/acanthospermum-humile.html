Acanthospermum humile

Acanthospermum humile, commonly known as the small pricklyseed, is a low-growing annual herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, narrow leaves and produces tiny, spiny fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Its botanical structure supports its adaptation to arid and semi-arid environments.
