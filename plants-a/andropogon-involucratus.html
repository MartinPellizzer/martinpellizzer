Andropogon involucratus

Andropogon involucratus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and panicles of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Andropogon involucratus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
