Aristida vaseyi

Aristida vaseyi, commonly known as the yellow lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale yellow flowers, which are often wind-pollinated. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in poor soil conditions.

Aristida vaseyi is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
