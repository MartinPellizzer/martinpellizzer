Allagopappus canariensis

Allagopappus canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showing a preference for well-drained soils. It has a low, spreading growth habit with narrow, silver-gray leaves.

The flowers, which bloom in the spring, are yellow and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
