Aechmea distichantha

Aechmea distichantha, commonly known as the silver vase bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater to support its growth. The leaves are typically covered in a dense layer of white or silver hairs, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It produces showy inflorescences with red or pink flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.

Morphology

Aechmea distichantha has strap-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, with each leaf measuring up to 30 cm in length and 3 cm in width.

The plant has a short, unbranched stem that is covered in a thick, fibrous, grayish-brown bark. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, with bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom during the summer months and are pollinated by insects. The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, black seeds, which are dispersed by wind and water.

The roots are fibrous and form a dense network near the soil surface, aiding in water absorption and anchoring the plant.
