Agapanthus umbellatus var. excelsus

Agapanthus umbellatus var. excelsus, commonly known as the blue agapanthus, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features showy, umbellate inflorescences composed of numerous blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has strap-like, evergreen leaves that form a dense rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
