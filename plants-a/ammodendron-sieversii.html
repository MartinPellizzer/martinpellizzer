Ammodendron sieversii

Ammodendron sieversii, commonly known as the desert date tree, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, date-like fruits. It has a deep root system that allows it to access groundwater, making it highly drought-tolerant.

Ammodendron sieversii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
