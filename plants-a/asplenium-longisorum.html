Asplenium longisorum

Asplenium longisorum, commonly known as the long-veined spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a small, epiphytic or lithophytic plant, typically found growing on rocks or tree trunks in moist, shaded environments. The plant has slender, creeping rhizomes and produces pinnately compound leaves with long, prominent veins. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a delicate, feathery appearance.

Asplenium longisorum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in humid conditions.
