Alnus maritima var. metoporina

Alnus maritima var. metoporina, commonly known as the salt marsh alder, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to coastal areas of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are often hairy and have a serrated edge. The plant produces small, yellowish catkins that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in brackish or saline soils, making it well adapted to coastal environments.

Its root system helps stabilize shorelines and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
