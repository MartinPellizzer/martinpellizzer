Amphibolia maritima

Amphibolia maritima, commonly known as sea onion, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its strap-like, blue-green leaves that are often used in floral arrangements. The plant produces small, white or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in saline soils and is often found in rocky or sandy coastal areas.

Amphibolia maritima is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh maritime conditions.
