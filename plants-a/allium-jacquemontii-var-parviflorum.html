Allium jacquemontii var. parviflorum

Allium jacquemontii var. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered squill, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance.

The bulbs of this plant can be used in traditional medicine in some regions.
