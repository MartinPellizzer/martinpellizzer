Anthurium scherzerianum var. minutipunctatum

Anthurium scherzerianum var. minutipunctatum, commonly known as the dwarf punctured anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with a unique pattern of small, raised dots, giving it its common name. The plant produces vibrant, spatulate-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which are borne on a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
