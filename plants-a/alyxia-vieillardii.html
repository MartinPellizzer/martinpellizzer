Alyxia vieillardii

Alyxia vieillardii, commonly known as the red-flowered alyxia, is a shrub native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its small, opposite leaves and showy, tubular red flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically orange or red when mature, containing seeds surrounded by a fleshy pulp. Alyxia vieillardii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
