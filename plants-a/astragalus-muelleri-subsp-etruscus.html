Astragalus muelleri subsp. etruscus

Astragalus muelleri subsp.

etruscus, commonly known as the Etruscan milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep taproots that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
