Artemisia alba subsp. chitachensis

Artemisia alba subsp.

chitachensis, commonly known as the Chita wormwood, is a perennial herb native to China. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and aromatic scent.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
