Alnus sibirica f. hirsutoides

Alnus sibirica f. hirsutoides, commonly known as the Siberian alder, is a variant of the Siberian alder, characterized by its dense, hairy foliage.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the cold regions of Asia, including Siberia and parts of China. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or along riverbanks.

This form is notable for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
