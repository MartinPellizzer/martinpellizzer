Adiantum dolabriforme

Adiantum dolabriforme, commonly known as the sickle fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Adiantaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically arching or pendulous. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called soruses on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Adiantum dolabriforme is valued for its ornamental appearance and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
