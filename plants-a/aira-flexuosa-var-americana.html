Aira flexuosa var. americana

Aira flexuosa var. americana, commonly known as American feathergrass, is a native grass species found in the United States.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and feathery plumes that give it a delicate appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that are clustered in panicles, contributing to its ornamental value in landscaping.
