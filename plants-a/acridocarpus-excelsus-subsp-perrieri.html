Acridocarpus excelsus subsp. perrieri

Acridocarpus excelsus subsp.

perrieri, commonly known as the Madagascar native tree, is a member of the Anacardiaceae family. It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and can grow up to 20 meters in height.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange to red berries. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

It is native to the tropical regions of Madagascar and is adapted to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
