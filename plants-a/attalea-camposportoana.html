Attalea camposportoana

Attalea camposportoana, commonly known as the "palm of the Amazon," is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible fruits, which are consumed by various wildlife species. It plays a vital role in the ecological balance of the rainforest by providing food and shelter.

Due to habitat loss, it is also considered a vulnerable species.
