Arytera pseudofoveolata

Arytera pseudofoveolata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in crevices or on slopes, and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in racemes, blooming in early spring.

Arytera pseudofoveolata plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying xerophytic adaptations.
