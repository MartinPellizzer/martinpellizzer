Alchemilla pratensis

Alchemilla pratensis, commonly known as the lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal rosettes of dark green, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces upright flowering stems bearing clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Alchemilla pratensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
