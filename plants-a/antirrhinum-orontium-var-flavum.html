Antirrhinum orontium var. flavum

Antirrhinum orontium var. flavum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered antirrhinum, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its overall delicate appearance. This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils in open habitats.

It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
