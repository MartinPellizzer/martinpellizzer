Acronychia baueri f. majoriflora

Acronychia baueri f. majoriflora, commonly known as the large-flowered acronychia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and elliptical to ovate leaves that are usually glabrous. The plant produces large, showy yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
