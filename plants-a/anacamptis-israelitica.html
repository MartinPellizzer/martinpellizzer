Anacamptis israelitica

Anacamptis israelitica, commonly known as the Israeli lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to southern Europe and the Middle East.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a dense spike of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which consists of three rows of flowers that spiral around a central axis. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Anacamptis israelitica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a member of the Orchidaceae family.
