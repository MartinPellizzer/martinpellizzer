Astragalus purshii var. longilobus

Astragalus purshii var. longilobus, commonly known as the long-lobed milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus purshii var. longilobus is also known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
