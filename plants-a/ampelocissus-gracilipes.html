Ampelocissus gracilipes

Ampelocissus gracilipes, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a climbing plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. The plant is known for its vigorous growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments. Its aerial roots allow it to cling to surfaces, and it is often used in ornamental gardening.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not properly managed.
