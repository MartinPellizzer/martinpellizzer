Acacia angustissima var. hirta

Acacia angustissima var. hirta, commonly known as the narrowleaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, feathery leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in harsh conditions.
