Agalinis pinetorum var. delicatula

Agalinis pinetorum var. delicatula, commonly known as the slender false nettle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as pine woods and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink to reddish in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to various soil conditions.
