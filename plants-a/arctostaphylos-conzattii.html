Arctostaphylos conzattii

Arctostaphylos conzattii, commonly known as Conzatt's manzanita, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the United States, specifically found in California.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat.

Conzatt's manzanita is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its ecosystem.
