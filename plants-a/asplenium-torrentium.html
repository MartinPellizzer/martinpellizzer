Asplenium torrentium

Asplenium torrentium, commonly known as the water shield, is a species of fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant typically grows in wetlands, stream banks, and other waterlogged habitats. Its rhizomes are often submerged or partially submerged, aiding in its ability to absorb nutrients from the water.

Asplenium torrentium plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
