Arabis violacea

Arabis violacea, commonly known as purple rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. The plant produces small, showy purple or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs.

Arabis violacea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
