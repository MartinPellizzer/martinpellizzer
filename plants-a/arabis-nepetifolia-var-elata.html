Arabis nepetifolia var. elata

Arabis nepetifolia var. elata, commonly known as the tall rockcress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It features basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate and can reach heights of up to 30 cm.

Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, blooming in early spring.
