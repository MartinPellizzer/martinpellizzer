Ammodendron caspium

Ammodendron caspium, commonly known as the Sarcocornia, is a halophytic shrub native to arid and coastal regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a key component of desert and coastal ecosystems. Its ability to thrive in harsh conditions makes it valuable for stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion.

Ammodendron caspium also plays a role in traditional medicine and has been used for its purported health benefits.
