Aspilia buphthalmiflora var. angustifolia

Aspilia buphthalmiflora var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of yellow, daisy-like flowers with white centers. The leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This variant is closely related to other species in the Asteraceae family, sharing similar floral structures and growth patterns.
