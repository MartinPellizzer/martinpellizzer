Alsine rupicola var. rupicoloides

Alsine rupicola var. rupicoloides, commonly known as the rock alsike, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant features small, clustered leaves and produces delicate, blue to purple flowers. It is often found in mountainous or coastal areas where it thrives in well-drained substrates.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies in xerophytic environments.
