Anacyclodon pungens

Anacyclodon pungens, commonly known as the spiny candelabra, is a distinctive plant with a unique candelabra-like growth form.

It is characterized by its branching stems that rise vertically, often with spines along the nodes. The leaves are typically small, narrow, and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stems. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a subtle ornamental appeal.

This plant is native to arid and semi-arid regions, adapting well to dry environments with minimal water requirements.
