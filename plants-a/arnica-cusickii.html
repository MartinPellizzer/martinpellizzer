Arnica cusickii

Arnica cusickii, commonly known as Cusick's Arnica, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open, rocky habitats such as meadows and slopes. The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers with prominent central disks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Arnica cusickii is valued for its medicinal properties, though it is often confused with other arnica species.
