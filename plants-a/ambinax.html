Ambinax

Ambinax, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Ambinax thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be used to cover trellises or walls in gardens.
