Azalea oleifolia

Azalea oleifolia, commonly known as the evergreen azalea, is a species of flowering plant in the Rhododendron genus.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in Florida and Georgia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Azalea oleifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
