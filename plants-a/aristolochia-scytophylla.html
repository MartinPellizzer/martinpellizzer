Aristolochia scytophylla

Aristolochia scytophylla, commonly known as the hooded Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular, hood-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy coating. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The genus Aristolochia is known for its complex chemical compounds, some of which have medicinal uses.
