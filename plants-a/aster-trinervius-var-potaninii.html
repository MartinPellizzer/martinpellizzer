Aster trinervius var. potaninii

Aster trinervius var. potaninii, commonly known as the Potanin's aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with a distinctive central disk surrounded by ray florets.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
