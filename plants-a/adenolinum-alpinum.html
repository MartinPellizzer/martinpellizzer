Adenolinum alpinum

Adenolinum alpinum, commonly known as the alpine adenolinum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy, grayish-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Adenolinum alpinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to high-altitude environments.
