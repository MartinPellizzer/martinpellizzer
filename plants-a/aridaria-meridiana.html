Aridaria meridiana

Aridaria meridiana, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant adapted to arid environments.

It is characterized by its succulent leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is found in regions with low annual rainfall. Its ability to store water in its thick stems and leaves allows it to survive prolonged droughts.

Aridaria meridiana plays an important role in stabilizing desert ecosystems and providing nectar for pollinators.
