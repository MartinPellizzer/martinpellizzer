Acacia esculenta

Acacia esculenta, commonly known as the sour acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species in dryland ecosystems.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
