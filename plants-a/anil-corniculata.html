Anil corniculata

Anil corniculata, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-shaped seed pods that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Anil corniculata is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical use in traditional medicine.
