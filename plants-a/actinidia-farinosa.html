Actinidia farinosa

Actinidia farinosa, commonly known as the fuzzy actinidia, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in early summer. The plant produces edible berries that are typically harvested in late summer or early autumn. These berries are often used in jams and wines due to their tart flavor.

Actinidia farinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
