Alchemilla alba

Alchemilla alba, commonly known as white lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla alba is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
