Aspidixia minima

Aspidixia minima, commonly known as the dwarf aspidixia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features delicate, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers. Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for undisturbed ecosystems.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and sensitive plant.
