Amelanchier lamarckii var. scharnkeana

Amelanchier lamarckii var. scharnkeana, commonly known as the dwarf Juneberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries in late summer, which are dark blue to black in color and have a tart flavor. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
