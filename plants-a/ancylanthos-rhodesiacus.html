Ancylanthos rhodesiacus

Ancylanthos rhodesiacus, commonly known as the Rhodesian sandthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions.
