Astragalus pseudopurpureus

Astragalus pseudopurpureus, commonly known as the purpleleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus pseudopurpureus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
