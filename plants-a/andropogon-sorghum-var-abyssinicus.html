Andropogon sorghum var. abyssinicus

Andropogon sorghum var. abyssinicus, commonly known as Ethiopian sorghum, is a grass species native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, two-lipped flowers, typical of the grass family. It thrives in warm, arid climates and is adapted to poor soil conditions.

This variety is valued for its resilience and has been used traditionally for forage and grain in its native region.
