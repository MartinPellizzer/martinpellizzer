Atalanthus canariensis

Atalanthus canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island caryopsis, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, dry fruits called caryopses, which contain the seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the arid climate of its native region.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often of interest to botanists and conservationists.
