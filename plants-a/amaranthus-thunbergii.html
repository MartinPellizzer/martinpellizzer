Amaranthus thunbergii

Amaranthus thunbergii, commonly known as Japanese amaranth, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, triangular leaves that are often bright green or red in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the variety. It is commonly cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Amaranthus thunbergii is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.
