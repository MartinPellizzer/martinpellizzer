Abrus bottae

Abrus bottae, commonly known as the purple bean vine, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple to reddish-brown seed pods that contain flat, kidney-shaped seeds. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its seeds contain toxic lectins, making them harmful if ingested.

Abrus bottae is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking pod color and vine-like growth habit.
