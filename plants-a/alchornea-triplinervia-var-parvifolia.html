Alchornea triplinervia var. parvifolia

Alchornea triplinervia var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved three-veined alchornea, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with three prominent veins, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
