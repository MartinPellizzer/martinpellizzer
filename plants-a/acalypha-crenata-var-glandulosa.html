Acalypha crenata var. glandulosa

Acalypha crenata var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular acalypha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves with serrated margins and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of red or pink. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, glandular substance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
