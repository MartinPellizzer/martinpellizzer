Astragalus mucronifolius var. ovoideus

Astragalus mucronifolius var. ovoideus, commonly known as the egg-shaped milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are compound, with oval-shaped leaflets that give the plant its common name. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
