Atractylodes carlinoides

Atractylodes carlinoides, commonly known as the Mexican ginger or carlin root, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its aromatic rhizomes and tall, branched stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite inflorescences, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

The plant has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.
