Aquilaria hirta

Aquilaria hirta, commonly known as the hairy aquilaria, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Thymelaeaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark brown wood, which is highly valued in traditional medicine and incense production. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered with fine hairs, giving it its common name.

The flowers are small and yellowish, appearing in clusters, and the plant is known for its slow growth and long lifespan.
