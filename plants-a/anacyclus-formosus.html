Anacyclus formosus

Anacyclus formosus, commonly known as the Mexican dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features a hairy, cylindrical stem and produces yellow flowers that develop into spherical, fluffy seed heads. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Anacyclus formosus is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antifungal benefits.
