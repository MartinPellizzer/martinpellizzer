Angelphytum oppositifolium

Angelphytum oppositifolium, commonly known as the opposite-leaf angelphytum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive silvery-blue hue. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is appreciated by botanists and gardeners alike.
