Astragalus cryptanthus

Astragalus cryptanthus, commonly known as the cryptanthus milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant has a deep taproot system, allowing it to survive in dry, sandy soils. Its seeds are typically dispersed by wind, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.

Astragalus cryptanthus is also valued for its ornamental appeal in drought-tolerant gardens.
