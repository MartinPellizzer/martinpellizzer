Adenocarpus foliolosus var. adenocalyx

Adenocarpus foliolosus var. adenocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaf burr medic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, branched stems with opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, followed by spherical, woody pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for dryland conditions.

The plant is used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
