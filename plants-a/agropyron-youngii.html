Agropyron youngii

Agropyron youngii, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky, or alpine environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Agropyron youngii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
