Aegilops lorentii var. archipelagica

Aegilops lorentii var. archipelagica, commonly known as the island wild rye, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as rocky slopes and coastal areas.

Due to its resilience and adaptability, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
