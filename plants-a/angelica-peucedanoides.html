Angelica peucedanoides

Angelica peucedanoides, commonly known as greater angelica, is a tall biennial or short-lived perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbel-shaped flowers and aromatic leaves. The plant can grow up to 2 meters in height, with large, deeply lobed leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are typically white or pale green, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

The roots and leaves contain volatile oils, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
