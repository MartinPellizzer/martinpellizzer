Alysicarpus sedgwickii

Alysicarpus sedgwickii, commonly known as the African burr grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous, bur-like structure. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
