Acacia distachya

Acacia distachya, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact spikes, which are followed by long, straight pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to drought conditions.

Acacia distachya is often used in traditional medicine and for its ornamental value in landscaping.
