Acianthera rodriguesii

Acianthera rodriguesii, commonly known as Rodrigues's orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the island of Rodrigues in the Indian Ocean.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically display a mix of white, purple, and pink hues. The plant is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, featuring pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by specific insects, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil.
