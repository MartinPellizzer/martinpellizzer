Atalaya australiana

Atalaya australiana, commonly known as the Australian rain tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded canopy and compound leaves that are typically alternate and pinnate. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by woody pods containing flat, edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes across Australia.
