Acacia praetervisa

Acacia praetervisa, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in cylindrical spikes, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Acacia praetervisa is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive form and drought tolerance.
