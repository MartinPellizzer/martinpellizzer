Aspidosperma sanguineum

Aspidosperma sanguineum, commonly known as the Brazilian redwood, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its reddish-brown bark and dense, aromatic wood. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and red berries that contain seeds. It is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its durable timber.

The species also has medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
