Alnus alnobetula var. parvifolia

Alnus alnobetula var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaf alder, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are typically smaller than those of other alder species. The plant produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones or along stream banks.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
