Athyrium pitcairnense

Athyrium pitcairnense, commonly known as the Pitcairn fern, is a species of fern native to the Pitcairn Islands in the South Pacific.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lacy fronds that are typically green with a slight bluish tint. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing along rocky outcrops or in dense undergrowth. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Due to its limited geographic range, it is considered a rare and endemic species, highlighting its ecological significance in the region.
