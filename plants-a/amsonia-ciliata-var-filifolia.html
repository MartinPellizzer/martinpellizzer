Amsonia ciliata var. filifolia

Amsonia ciliata var. filifolia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a North American native plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and fluffy, blue-pink flower heads that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland or swampy areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy margins, and the varietal name "filifolia" refers to the thread-like appearance of its foliage.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators.
