Adolia buxifolia

Adolia buxifolia, commonly known as the waxleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that remains green throughout the year, giving it a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its compact growth habit and year-round interest make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, helping the plant anchor itself in moist, well-drained soil.
