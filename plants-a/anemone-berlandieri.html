Anemone berlandieri

Anemone berlandieri, commonly known as the Mexican anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing a compact growth habit with hairy stems. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with five petals, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided, with serrated edges, and it is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Anemone berlandieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is adapted to drought conditions.
