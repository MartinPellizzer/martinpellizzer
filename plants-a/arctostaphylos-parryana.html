Arctostaphylos parryana

Arctostaphylos parryana, commonly known as Parry manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, hard berries that are a food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Arctostaphylos parryana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native range.
