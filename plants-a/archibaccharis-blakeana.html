Archibaccharis blakeana

Archibaccharis blakeana, commonly known as Blake's holly.

It is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy appearance.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas.
