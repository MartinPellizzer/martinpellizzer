Abutilon asperifolium

Abutilon asperifolium, commonly known as the roughleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its adaptability and attractive blooms make it a valuable addition to both cultivated and natural landscapes.
