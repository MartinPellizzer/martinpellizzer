Adonis caudata

Adonis caudata, commonly known as the tufted glory-of-the-snow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and its striking yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Adonis caudata is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
