Anthriscus sylvestris subsp. fumarioides

Anthriscus sylvestris subsp.

fumarioides, commonly known as the hedge parsley, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its aromatic foliage and delicate blooms make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
