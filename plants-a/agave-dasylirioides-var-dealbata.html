Agave dasylirioides var. dealbata

Agave dasylirioides var. dealbata, commonly known as the white agave or silver agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a white powdery coating called bloom. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears small, white flowers, which are followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscape design.
