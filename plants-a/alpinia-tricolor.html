Alpinia tricolor

Alpinia tricolor, commonly known as the three-colored ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its strikingly colorful leaves, which display shades of green, red, and yellow. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Alpinia tricolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
