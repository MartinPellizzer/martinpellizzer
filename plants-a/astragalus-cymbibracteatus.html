Astragalus cymbibracteatus

Astragalus cymbibracteatus, commonly known as the Sierra foothill milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow to green flowers clustered in dense spikes. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators.
