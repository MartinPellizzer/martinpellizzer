Arundinella setosa var. nilagiriana

Arundinella setosa var. nilagiriana, commonly known as the Nilagiri bamboo, is a variant of the slender bamboo species native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a perennial grass-like plant characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in clusters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually smooth and slightly hairy on the underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
