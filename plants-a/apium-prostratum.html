Apium prostratum

Apium prostratum, commonly known as swamp cowparsley, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescences and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist or aquatic habitats, such as marshes and stream banks. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a mild, parsley-like scent.

The flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters that attract various pollinators.
