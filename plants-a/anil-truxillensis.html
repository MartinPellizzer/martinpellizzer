Anil truxillensis

Anil truxillensis, commonly known as the Andean mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters during the cooler months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
