Acacia longifolia f. floribunda

Acacia longifolia f. floribunda, commonly known as the coastal acacia or golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. It plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in coastal regions.
