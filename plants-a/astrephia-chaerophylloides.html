Astrephia chaerophylloides

Astrephia chaerophylloides, commonly known as the purple star orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant purple and white flowers that resemble stars. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters.

Astrephia chaerophylloides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral structure.
