Astilbe rubra var. divaricata

Astilbe rubra var. divaricata, commonly known as the red velvet astilbe, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features dense, upright clusters of soft, fluffy panicles that range in color from pink to deep red, depending on the variety and growing conditions. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet and prefers partial to full shade, making it ideal for woodland gardens. Its foliage is usually dark green and glossy, adding visual interest even when the flowers are not in bloom.

Astilbe rubra var. divaricata is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soil.
