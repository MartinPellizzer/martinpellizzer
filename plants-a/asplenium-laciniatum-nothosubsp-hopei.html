Asplenium laciniatum nothosubsp. hopei

Asplenium laciniatum nothosubsp.

hopei, commonly known as the Hope's maidenhair fern, is a rare and hybrid variant of the common maidenhair fern. It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that are often dark green with a glossy texture.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as woodland floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are delicate and can be prone to damage from environmental stressors.

This nothosubspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique genetic makeup and limited distribution.
