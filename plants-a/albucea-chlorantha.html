Albucea chlorantha

Albucea chlorantha, commonly known as the green morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically open in the morning and close in the evening. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow or white flowers with a distinct, sweet fragrance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Albucea chlorantha is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb using its tendrils.
