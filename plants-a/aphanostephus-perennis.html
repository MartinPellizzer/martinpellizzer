Aphanostephus perennis

Aphanostephus perennis, commonly known as the slender star grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It is characterized by its fibrous root system and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Aphanostephus perennis is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in dry landscapes.
