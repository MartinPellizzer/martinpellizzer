Arnebia lutea

Arnebia lutea, commonly known as the yellow orache, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and fleshy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the species. It is often found growing in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Arnebia lutea is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
