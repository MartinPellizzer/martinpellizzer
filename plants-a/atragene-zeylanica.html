Atragene zeylanica

Atragene zeylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its fragrant, aromatic wood, which is highly valued in traditional medicine and perfumery. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by dark purple berries.

The species is also noted for its slow growth and is considered a rare and protected plant in its native habitat.
