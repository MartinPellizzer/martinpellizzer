Angelonia pilosella

Angelonia pilosella, commonly known as the hairy angelonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Angelonia pilosella is known for its long blooming season, often lasting from spring through early autumn.
