Aegiphila setiformis

Aegiphila setiformis, commonly known as the hairy leaf germander, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs.

Aegiphila setiformis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Morphology

Aegiphila setiformis has leaves that are narrow, linear, and often arranged in a basal rosette, with a bluish-gray color due to a dense covering of trichomes.

The stem is upright, cylindrical, and slightly hairy, with a light green to grayish color, and it exhibits a branching growth pattern. The flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, with white or pale pink petals, blooming from late spring to early summer, and are pollinated primarily by insects. The fruit is a dry capsule that releases small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the arid environment.
