Alstroemeria modesta

Alstroemeria modesta, commonly known as the modest alstroemeria, is a species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with strap-like leaves that are often variegated.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, though it requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive.
