Adiantum globatum

Adiantum globatum, commonly known as the ball fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded fronds that resemble a ball, giving it its common name. The plant has a short, rhizomatous stem with closely spaced, clustered fronds. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture.

Adiantum globatum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
