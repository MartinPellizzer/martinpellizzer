Acer palmatum var. dissectum

Acer palmatum var. dissectum, commonly known as the Japanese maple with dissected leaves, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its finely cut, lace-like foliage that turns vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow in autumn. The leaves are palmately lobed and deeply divided, giving the plant a delicate, ornamental appearance. It prefers partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This cultivar is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in ornamental horticulture.
