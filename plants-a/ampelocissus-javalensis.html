Ampelocissus javalensis

Ampelocissus javalensis, commonly known as the rock fig, is a woody vine native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, greenish-white flowers. The plant bears edible fig-like fruits that are typically consumed raw or cooked. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are often used in traditional medicine.

Ampelocissus javalensis thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.
