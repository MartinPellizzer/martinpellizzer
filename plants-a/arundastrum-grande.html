Arundastrum grande

Arundastrum grande, commonly known as the giant arundo, is a tall, perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its robust, hollow stems and large, blade-like leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. This plant thrives in wetland environments and is often found along riverbanks and in marshy areas. Its flowering spikes produce small, greenish-white flowers that are adapted for wind pollination.

Arundastrum grande plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
