Alsophila hirsutissima

Alsophila hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately divided fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive silvery-hairy appearance on its young fronds, which gives it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Alsophila hirsutissima plays a significant ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various forest organisms.
