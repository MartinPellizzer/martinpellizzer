Amauropelta pilosissima

Amauropelta pilosissima, commonly known as the silky shield fern, is a unique member of the Pteris genus.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are covered in a dense layer of silky, white hairs. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizome is short and produces a single, upright, spore-bearing sorus at the base of each leaf.

The plant's ornamental value comes from its soft, feathery appearance and its ability to thrive in humid conditions.
