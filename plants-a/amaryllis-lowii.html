Amaryllis lowii

Amaryllis lowii, commonly known as the Cape Lily, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem that bears multiple blooms, often in late summer or early autumn. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base.

Amaryllis lowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
