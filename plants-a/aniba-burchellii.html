Aniba burchellii

Aniba burchellii, commonly known as the African sandthorn, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers. It produces small, round fruits that are edible but have a strong, aromatic flavor.

Aniba burchellii is often found in coastal and sandy habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion.
