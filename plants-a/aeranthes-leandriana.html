Aeranthes leandriana

Aeranthes leandriana, commonly known as the Leander's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often near streams or in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Aeranthes leandriana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
