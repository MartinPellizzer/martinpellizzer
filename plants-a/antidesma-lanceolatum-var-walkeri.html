Antidesma lanceolatum var. walkeri

Antidesma lanceolatum var. walkeri, commonly known as the African starfruit or yellow starfruit, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, lanceolate leaves that are usually yellowish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by distinctive star-shaped fruits that are yellow when ripe. The fruit is edible and has a sweet, acidic flavor, making it a popular fruit in some regions.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food.
