Arctostaphylos patula

Arctostaphylos patula, commonly known as the dwarf manzanita, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, ovate leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white to pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
