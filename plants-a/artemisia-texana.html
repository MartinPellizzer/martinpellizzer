Artemisia texana

Artemisia texana, commonly known as Texas wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and aromatic, feathery leaves that emit a strong, bitter scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Artemisia texana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
