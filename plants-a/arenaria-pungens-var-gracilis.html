Arenaria pungens var. gracilis

Arenaria pungens var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and sandy soils in temperate regions.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy addition to various landscapes.
