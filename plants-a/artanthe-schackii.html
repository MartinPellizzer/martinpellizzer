Artanthe schackii

Artanthe schackii, commonly known as Schack's artanthe, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture.

Artanthe schackii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
