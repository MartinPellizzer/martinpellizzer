Albizia croizatiana

Albizia croizatiana, commonly known as the velvetwood tree, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous leguminous tree characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Albizia croizatiana also plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for wildlife.
