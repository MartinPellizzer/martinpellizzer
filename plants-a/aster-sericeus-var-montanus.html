Aster sericeus var. montanus

Aster sericeus var. montanus, commonly known as the mountain aster, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, thriving at higher elevations. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
