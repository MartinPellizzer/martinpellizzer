Aloe saponaria var. latifolia

Aloe saponaria var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed soap aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features rosettes of broad, fleshy, gray-green leaves that are covered in a powdery white coating. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with full sun exposure.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to store water in its leaves.
