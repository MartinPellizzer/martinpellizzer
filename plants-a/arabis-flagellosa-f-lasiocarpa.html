Arabis flagellosa f. lasiocarpa

Arabis flagellosa f. lasiocarpa, commonly known as the hairy-leaved rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, typical of the family. It is native to rocky and alpine habitats in Europe and is adapted to harsh, well-drained soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
