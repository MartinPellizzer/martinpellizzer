Ardisia glauciflora

Ardisia glauciflora, commonly known as the blue-flowered ardisia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in the spring. Its fruit is a bright red berry that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by birds.

Ardisia glauciflora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
