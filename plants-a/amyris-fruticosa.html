Amyris fruticosa

Amyris fruticosa, commonly known as the Brazilian sand arnica, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Amyris fruticosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
