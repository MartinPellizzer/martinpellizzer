Amaranthus berlandieri

Amaranthus berlandieri, commonly known as desert amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant red or purple foliage, which becomes more pronounced as the plant matures. The species produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellow, and it is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional landscaping and as a food source in some regions.
