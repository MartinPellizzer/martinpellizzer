Astragalus balkaricus

Astragalus balkaricus, commonly known as Balkan milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to temperate climates. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus balkaricus is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
