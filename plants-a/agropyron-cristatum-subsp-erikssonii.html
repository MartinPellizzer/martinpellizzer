Agropyron cristatum subsp. erikssonii

Agropyron cristatum subsp.

erikssonii, commonly known as the Eriksson's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, spike-like inflorescence and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential use in restoration projects.
