Aniba ferruginea

Aniba ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty aniba, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

The tree is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
