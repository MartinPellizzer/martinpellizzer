Allium chilense

Allium chilense, commonly known as the Chilean garlic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces edible bulbs that are similar in taste to garlic but have a milder, more delicate flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental and culinary plant in temperate climates.

Allium chilense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant.
