Alternanthera straminea

Alternanthera straminea, commonly known as the golden hearts plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often bright red or orange, especially under full sun. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, which are followed by small, round seed pods. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This plant is popular in aquatic gardens and as a decorative foliage plant in water features.
