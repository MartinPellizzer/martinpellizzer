Acourtia reticulata var. maculata

Acourtia reticulata var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted acourtia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves with white spots. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
