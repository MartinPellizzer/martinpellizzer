Astragalus salavatabadensis

Astragalus salavatabadensis, commonly known as the Salavatabad milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus salavatabadensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
