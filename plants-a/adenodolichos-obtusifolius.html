Adenodolichos obtusifolius

Adenodolichos obtusifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved horsegram, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with rounded lobes and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often grown for its edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
