Astracantha gossypina

Astracantha gossypina, commonly known as the cotton thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and large, showy purple to pink flowers. The plant produces achenes, which are small, dry fruits that contain a single seed, typical of the sunflower family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Astracantha gossypina is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance.
