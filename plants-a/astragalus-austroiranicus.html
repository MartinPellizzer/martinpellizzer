Astragalus austroiranicus

Astragalus austroiranicus, commonly known as the southern Iranian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Iran and Afghanistan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky environments and has a deep root system to access water. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
