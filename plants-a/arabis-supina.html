Arabis supina

Arabis supina, commonly known as the prostrate rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or coastal environments. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, oblanceolate, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Arabis supina is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
