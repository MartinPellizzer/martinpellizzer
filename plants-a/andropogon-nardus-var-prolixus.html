Andropogon nardus var. prolixus

Andropogon nardus var. prolixus, commonly known as sweetgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, fragrant leaves that emit a sweet, honey-like scent when crushed. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and pine woods. Its flowering stems are often used in traditional crafts and ceremonies.

It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
