Aster frutetorum

Aster frutetorum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to blue, depending on soil pH. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Aster frutetorum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
