Aulacocalyx caudata

Aulacocalyx caudata, commonly known as the long-tailed flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous, orange-red inflorescences that resemble flames, which are composed of numerous small, clustered flowers. The plant has elliptical, glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
