Astragalus pseudojohannis

Astragalus pseudojohannis, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations and limited water availability. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus pseudojohannis plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
