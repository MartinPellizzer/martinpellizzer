Acacia viridiflora

Acacia viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are composed of numerous small leaflets arranged in a feather-like pattern. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical or cylindrical heads, which are highly attractive to pollinators. Acacia viridiflora is often found in open woodlands and savannas, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its hard, durable wood and has been used in traditional crafts and construction.
