Agropyron amurense

Agropyron amurense, commonly known as Siberian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in cold climates and is often found in alpine and subarctic regions.

Agropyron amurense is valued for its hardiness and is used in ecological restoration projects.
