Anemone coronaria var. purpurea

Anemone coronaria var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple garden anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with a yellow center. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches, with basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to the landscape.
