Abelia biflora f. minor

Abelia biflora f. minor, commonly known as the dwarf abelia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color, providing a dense, foliage-filled appearance. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for gardens and landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often used in mixed borders or as a foundation plant.
