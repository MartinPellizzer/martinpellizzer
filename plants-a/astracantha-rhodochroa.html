Astracantha rhodochroa

Astracantha rhodochroa, commonly known as the red-flowered burweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, spiny bracts that enclose its yellow flowers, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in grasslands and rocky soils.

Astracantha rhodochroa is known for its resilience to drought and its role in supporting local pollinators.
