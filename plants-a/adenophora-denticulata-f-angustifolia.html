Adenophora denticulata f. angustifolia

Adenophora denticulata f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a botanical variant of the species Adenophora denticulata.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pale blue. This plant is native to regions of East Asia, including China, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland or rocky habitats, preferring partial shade to full sun.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
