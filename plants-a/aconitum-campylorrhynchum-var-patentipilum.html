Aconitum campylorrhynchum var. patentipilum

Aconitum campylorrhynchum var. patentipilum, commonly known as the broad-leaf aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and a distinctive, spurred petal structure. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
