Artemisia arctica var. saxatilis

Artemisia arctica var. saxatilis, commonly known as the rock wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, gray-green leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, contributing to its role in pollinator habitats. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates with low nutrient availability.

This variant is often found in tundra and mountainous environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
