Acer crassum

Acer crassum, commonly known as the broad-leaf maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant colors in the fall. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Acer crassum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitats.
