Acioa icondere

Acioa icondere, commonly known as the red-stemmed acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its woody stems are often covered in a reddish-brown bark, giving it its common name.

Acioa icondere is well adapted to dry environments and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
