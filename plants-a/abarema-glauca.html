Abarema glauca

Abarema glauca, commonly known as the blue glaucous legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, white to pale blue flowers. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
