Abies sachalinensis f. corticosa

Abies sachalinensis f. corticosa, commonly known as the Sakhalin fir, is a rare and unique variant of the Sakhalin fir native to the Russian Far East.

This form exhibits distinctive botanical features, including a bark that is thicker and more flaky compared to the typical species. It has shorter, more densely packed needles that are often a darker green in color. The cones are typically smaller and more clustered, contributing to its unique appearance.

This form is of particular interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and distinct morphological characteristics.
