Allium rydbergii

Allium rydbergii, commonly known as the Rydberg onion, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous base and grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in native plant gardens.
