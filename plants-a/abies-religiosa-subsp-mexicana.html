Abies religiosa subsp. mexicana

Abies religiosa subsp.

mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Mexico. It belongs to the genus Abies in the family Pinaceae, characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure.

The tree typically grows in mountainous regions, preferring cool, moist climates with well-drained soil. Its foliage is dense and dark green, often retaining its color year-round.

The Mexican fir plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
