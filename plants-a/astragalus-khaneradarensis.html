Astragalus khaneradarensis

Astragalus khaneradarensis, commonly known as the Khaneradarensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus khaneradarensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
