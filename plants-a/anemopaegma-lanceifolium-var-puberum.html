Anemopaegma lanceifolium var. puberum

Anemopaegma lanceifolium var. puberum, commonly known as the hairy lanceleaf anemopaegma, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
