Avena alba subvar. aristulata

Avena alba subvar. aristulata, commonly known as the slender oat, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in spikes, which are often borne on elongated stalks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
