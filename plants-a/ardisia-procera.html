Ardisia procera

Ardisia procera, commonly known as the evergreen boxwood, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged in whorls along its stems. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments with moist, well-drained soil.

Ardisia procera is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its dense foliage and attractive appearance.
