Arctostaphylos × intermedia

Arctostaphylos × intermedia, commonly known as the Sierra manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, woody stems and evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink, typically blooming in late spring. Its berries, which are edible for some wildlife, mature to a dark red or black color.

The Sierra manzanita is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
