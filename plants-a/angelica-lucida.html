Angelica lucida

Angelica lucida, commonly known as sweet angelica, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes plants like carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic, compound leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The foliage and seeds are also used in culinary applications, adding a subtle licorice-like flavor to dishes.
