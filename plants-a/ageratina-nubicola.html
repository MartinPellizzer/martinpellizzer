Ageratina nubicola

Ageratina nubicola, commonly known as the cloud mountain blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, blue to purple florets arranged in dense, spherical clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Ageratina nubicola is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some Andean cultures.
