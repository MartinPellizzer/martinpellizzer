Abutilon integerrimum

Abutilon integerrimum, commonly known as velvetleaf or Indian mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that give it its common name, and it produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant is typically shrub-like, with a fibrous root system and can grow up to several meters in height. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its hairy stems and broad leaves make it easily identifiable in the botanical landscape.
