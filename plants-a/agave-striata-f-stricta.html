Agave striata f. stricta

Agave striata f. stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright, cylindrical stems and narrow, gray-green leaves with white margins. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear showy, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid landscapes.
