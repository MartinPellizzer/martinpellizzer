Ancipitia duplex

Ancipitia duplex, commonly known as the twin-lobed orchid, is a striking plant native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bifurcated leaves that give it its scientific name. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a unique two-lobed structure, which is a defining botanical feature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
