Amaranthus parodii

Amaranthus parodii, commonly known as the red amaranth, is a striking annual plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with upright stems and alternate leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Amaranthus parodii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its bold, eye-catching appearance.
