Arctostaphylos crassifolia

Arctostaphylos crassifolia, commonly known as the thickleaf manzanita, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color and arranged in clusters. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late winter or early spring. Its fruit is a capsule that releases small, smooth seeds when mature.

Arctostaphylos crassifolia is well adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub ecosystems.
