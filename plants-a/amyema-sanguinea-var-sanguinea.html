Amyema sanguinea var. sanguinea

Amyema sanguinea var. sanguinea, commonly known as the red amyema, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant typically grows in shrubland and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, white pubescence, adding to its ornamental appeal.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
