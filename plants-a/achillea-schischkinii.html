Achillea schischkinii

Achillea schischkinii, commonly known as Schischkin's yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features finely divided, silver-gray leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are typically white to pale yellow, arranged in flat-topped clusters.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
