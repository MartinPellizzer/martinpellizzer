Argyreia elliptica

Argyreia elliptica, commonly known as the velvetleaf or blue morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often a deep green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in the morning. Its rapid growth habit makes it an effective cover plant but also a potential weed in agricultural settings.

Argyreia elliptica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
