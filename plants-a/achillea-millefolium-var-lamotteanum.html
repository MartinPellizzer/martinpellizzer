Achillea millefolium var. lamotteanum

Achillea millefolium var. lamotteanum, commonly known as the Lamotte yarrow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves that are aromatic and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces flat-topped clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
