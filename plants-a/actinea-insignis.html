Actinea insignis

Actinea insignis, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant produces a unique, aromatic resin that has been historically valued for its use in incense and traditional medicine. Actinea insignis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
