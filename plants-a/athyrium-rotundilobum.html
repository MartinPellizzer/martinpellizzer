Athyrium rotundilobum

Athyrium rotundilobum, commonly known as the round-lobed Japanese paintbrush, is a fern native to East Asia.

It features a rhizomatous growth habit and produces delicate, compound fronds with a distinctive rounded lobes. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Athyrium rotundilobum is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated in shady gardens.
