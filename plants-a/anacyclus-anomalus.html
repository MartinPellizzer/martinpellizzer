Anacyclus anomalus

Anacyclus anomalus, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

Anacyclus anomalus is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
