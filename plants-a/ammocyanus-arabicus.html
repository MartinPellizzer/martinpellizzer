Ammocyanus arabicus

Ammocyanus arabicus, commonly known as the Arabian bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the deserts of the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, adapting to the harsh arid environment. The plant produces striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, helping to conserve water in its dry habitat.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and ecological significance in desert ecosystems.
