Agrostis kudoi var. inermis

Agrostis kudoi var. inermis, commonly known as the smooth meadowgrass, is a variant of the grass species native to East Asia.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, erect culms and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, spikelets.

The variety inermis is distinguished by its lack of awns, giving it a smoother appearance compared to other variants of Agrostis kudoi.
