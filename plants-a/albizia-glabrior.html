Albizia glabrior

Albizia glabrior, commonly known as the silky albizia, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features compound leaves that are pinnately arranged and often have a soft, silky texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically ranging in color from white to pale yellow. Its pods are elongated and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Albizia glabrior is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
