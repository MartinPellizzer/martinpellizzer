Acianthus uvarius

Acianthus uvarius, commonly known as the violet-flowered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a terrestrial herb with a rhizomatous root system and produces compact, low-growing foliage. The plant features showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in spring, typically in clusters at the base of the leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its distinctive floral display.
