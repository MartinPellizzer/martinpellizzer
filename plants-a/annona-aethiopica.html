Annona aethiopica

Annona aethiopica, commonly known as the African horned rush, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, leaf-like structures that resemble horns. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by edible, yellowish fruits with a sweet, creamy pulp. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy areas or along riverbanks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
