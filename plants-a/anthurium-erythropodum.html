Anthurium erythropodum

Anthurium erythropodum, commonly known as the red-veined anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with deep red veins. The plant produces spadices, which are inflorescences consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Anthurium erythropodum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
