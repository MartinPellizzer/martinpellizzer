Astragalus kangalicus

Astragalus kangalicus, commonly known as the Kangal milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is part of the diverse flora of its native region.

Astragalus kangalicus is also of interest for its potential medicinal properties.
