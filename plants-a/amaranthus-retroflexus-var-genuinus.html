Amaranthus retroflexus var. genuinus

Amaranthus retroflexus var. genuinus, commonly known as the common amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and is native to North America. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from red to green. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.

The seeds are often used in traditional cuisines and can also be cultivated as a nutritious food source.
