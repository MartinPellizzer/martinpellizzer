Alsophila parvifolia

Alsophila parvifolia, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive crown of large, pinnate fronds that give it a graceful appearance. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in rainforests.

Alsophila parvifolia plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various forest organisms.
