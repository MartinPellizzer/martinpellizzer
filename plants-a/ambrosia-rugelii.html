Ambrosia rugelii

Ambrosia rugelii, commonly known as the citrus rust mite, is a microscopic acarid that poses a significant threat to citrus crops.

It feeds on the sap of citrus leaves, causing a characteristic rust-colored discoloration and curling of the foliage. The plant's botanical features include its small, elongated body and the ability to reproduce rapidly under favorable conditions. It thrives in warm, humid environments, often leading to widespread infestations in citrus orchards.

Management of this pest typically involves integrated pest management strategies, including biological control and chemical treatments.
