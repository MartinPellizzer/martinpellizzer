Astragalus rudehendicus

Astragalus rudehendicus, commonly known as the rough milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open areas with well-drained soil.

Astragalus rudehendicus is a perennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
