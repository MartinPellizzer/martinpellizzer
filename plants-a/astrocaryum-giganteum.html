Astrocaryum giganteum

Astrocaryum giganteum, commonly known as the giant palm or Brazilian buriti, is a tall, slow-growing palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It can reach heights of up to 25 meters and is characterized by its large, pinnate leaves and a thick, fibrous trunk. The plant produces large, yellowish-pink flowers that develop into clustered, edible fruits. Its fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a source of food, while the trunk is valued for its strong, durable fibers.

Astrocaryum giganteum plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
