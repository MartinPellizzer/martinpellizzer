Asplenium linearifolium

Asplenium linearifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spleenwort, is a species of moss-like fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a compact rosette formation. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found growing on rock surfaces or in crevices. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium linearifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in humid indoor settings.
