Aristea angolensis subsp. angolensis

Aristea angolensis subsp.

angolensis, commonly known as the Angolan aristea, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or purple in color. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and thrives in full sun.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
