Astragalus argophyllus var. stocksii

Astragalus argophyllus var. stocksii, commonly known as the Sierra needlepod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, spindly stems and small, gray-green leaves. The plant produces delicate, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
