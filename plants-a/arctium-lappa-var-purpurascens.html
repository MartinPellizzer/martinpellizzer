Arctium lappa var. purpurascens

Arctium lappa var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple burdock, is a variant of the common burdock native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a biennial plant that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring large, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces purple-brown, spiky flowers that develop into the characteristic burrs used in traditional medicine. Its roots are often harvested for their medicinal properties, containing compounds like inulin and mucilage.

The species is valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in ecological and herbal contexts.
