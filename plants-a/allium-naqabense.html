Allium naqabense

Allium naqabense, commonly known as the Naqab onion, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces edible bulbs that are often used in local cuisines for their mild, onion-like flavor. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and high temperatures.

Allium naqabense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
