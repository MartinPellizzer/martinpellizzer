Astragalus pseudoshebarensis

Astragalus pseudoshebarensis, commonly known as the pseudoshebarensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant species belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. This perennial plant is native to specific regions in Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppe and rocky habitats.

The plant plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies due to its unique adaptations to harsh climates.
