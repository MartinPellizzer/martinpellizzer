Aetanthus mutisii

Aetanthus mutisii, commonly known as the Mexican broom, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It has been used traditionally in local medicine for its purported healing properties.

Aetanthus mutisii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
