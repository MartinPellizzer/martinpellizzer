Anthurium subsignatum

Anthurium subsignatum, commonly known as the spotted anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often speckled with red or purple spots. The plant produces vibrant spathes that can range in color from deep red to orange, resembling a flower but actually being a modified leaf. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Anthurium subsignatum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
