Anthericum floribundum

Anthericum floribundum, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces upright flowering stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
