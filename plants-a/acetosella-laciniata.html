Acetosella laciniata

Acetosella laciniata, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the family Montiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
