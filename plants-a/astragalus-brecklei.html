Astragalus brecklei

Astragalus brecklei, commonly known as the Breckley milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sensitive species.
