Arenga nana

Arenga nana, commonly known as the sugar palm, is a tall, slender palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and can grow up to 20 meters in height. The plant produces a fibrous stem that is used for making ropes and mats. Its inflorescence, which appears at the top of the plant, consists of small, yellowish flowers.

The plant is also valued for its sap, which can be tapped to produce a type of syrup.
