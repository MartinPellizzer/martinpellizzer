Arctium chabertii subsp. balearicum

Arctium chabertii subsp.

balearicum, commonly known as the Balearic burdock, is a perennial herb native to the Balearic Islands. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces purple flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. Its fruit is a spiny bur, which aids in seed dispersal by attaching to animal fur or clothing.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and coastal environments, showing a preference for well-drained soils.
