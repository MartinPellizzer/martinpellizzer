Athyrium blumei

Athyrium blumei, commonly known as the Japanese Painted Fern, is a fern species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bipinnate fronds that are often adorned with contrasting colors of green, silver, and rust. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it in the soil, while its spore-producing structures are located on the underside of the fronds.

Athyrium blumei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in partial shade.
