Anemia phyllitidis var. obliqua

Anemia phyllitidis var. obliqua, commonly known as the narrow-leaf iron plant, is a member of the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and relatively rare species within its native range.
