Achillea millefolium subsp. atrotegula

Achillea millefolium subsp.

atrotegula, commonly known as the dark-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped umbels. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
