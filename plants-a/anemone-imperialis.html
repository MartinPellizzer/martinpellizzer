Anemone imperialis

Anemone imperialis, commonly known as the imperial anemone, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, white to pink flowers with a distinctive central cone-shaped structure, which is characteristic of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has palmate, deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a subject of interest in both botanical research and horticulture.
