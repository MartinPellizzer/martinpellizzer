Abies ernestii

Abies ernestii, commonly known as the Ernestii fir, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in spirals along the branches. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing seeds enclosed in a thin, winged aril. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found at high elevations where it contributes to the biodiversity of temperate forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, the Ernestii fir is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
