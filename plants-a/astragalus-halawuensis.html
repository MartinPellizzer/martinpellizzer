Astragalus halawuensis

Astragalus halawuensis, commonly known as the Halawu milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant is adapted to harsh, arid environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential medicinal properties.

Its seeds are typically dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution in rugged landscapes.
