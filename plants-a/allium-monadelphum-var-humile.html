Allium monadelphum var. humile

Allium monadelphum var. humile, commonly known as the dwarf garlic.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. The plant features a bulbous base with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale purple flowers in umbel-like clusters. It is characterized by its aromatic bulb, which contains sulfur compounds responsible for its distinctive odor.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses in local cuisines.
