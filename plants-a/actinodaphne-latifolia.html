Actinodaphne latifolia

Actinodaphne latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by egg-shaped, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. It is valued for its essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of camphor.

The species is also appreciated in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
