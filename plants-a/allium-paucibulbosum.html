Allium paucibulbosum

Allium paucibulbosum, commonly known as the few-bulb onion, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered bulbs. The plant produces basal leaves that are grass-like in appearance, and its flowers are typically white or pale purple, arranged in umbels. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Allium paucibulbosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in temperate climates.
