Amsinckia microphylla

Amsinckia microphylla, commonly known as the lesser burro's tail, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and its distinctive, silver-gray foliage with small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Amsinckia microphylla is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
