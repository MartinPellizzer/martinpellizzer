Argorips caerulea

Argorips caerulea, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for wetland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
