Apargia autumnalis var. gouanii

Apargia autumnalis var. gouanii, commonly known as the Gouan's goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow to golden-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
