Armeria macropoda

Armeria macropoda, commonly known as sea thrift, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime climates. Its salt tolerance makes it a common species in coastal dunes and rocky shores.

Armeria macropoda is also cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
