Asteriscium tripartitum

Asteriscium tripartitum, commonly known as the three-part asterisk, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, clustered inflorescences that resemble tiny stars, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Southeast Asia. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are usually pale yellow to white, with a distinctive central disc surrounded by ray florets.
