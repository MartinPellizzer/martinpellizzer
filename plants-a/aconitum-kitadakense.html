Aconitum kitadakense

Aconitum kitadakense, commonly known as Japanese aconite, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy flowers. The plant produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed foliage that adds ornamental value to gardens.

Aconitum kitadakense contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
