Allium vineale

Allium vineale, commonly known as garlic chives, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant produces bulbils along its stems, which are used for propagation. Its leaves and bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in culinary applications.

Allium vineale thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
