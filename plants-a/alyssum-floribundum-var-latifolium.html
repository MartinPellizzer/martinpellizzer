Alyssum floribundum var. latifolium

Alyssum floribundum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb with dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and extended blooming period make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
