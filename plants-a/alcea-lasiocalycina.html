Alcea lasiocalycina

Alcea lasiocalycina, commonly known as the rough-hair hollyhock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant has hairy, rough-textured leaves that are typically ovate or lanceolate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often growing in rocky or dry environments.

Alcea lasiocalycina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
