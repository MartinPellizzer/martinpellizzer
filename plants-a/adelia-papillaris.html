Adelia papillaris

Adelia papillaris, commonly known as the hairy adelia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive hairy leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers, which come in shades of red and purple, bloom during the summer months.

Adelia papillaris is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
