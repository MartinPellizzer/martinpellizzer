Acanthodium capense var. leucographus

Acanthodium capense var. leucographus, commonly known as the white-stippled acanthodium, is a unique variant of the acanthodium plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive white stippling on the leaves, which sets it apart from other varieties of the species. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly succulent texture, and it produces small, pale yellow flowers during the warmer months.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
