Acacia pervillei

Acacia pervillei, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and its distinctive, feathery, yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The plant produces small, flat seed pods that contain hard, angular seeds. Acacia pervillei is well adapted to dry environments, with small, thick leaves that reduce water loss.

It plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
