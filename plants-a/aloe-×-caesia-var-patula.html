Aloe × caesia var. patula

Aloe × caesia var. patula, commonly known as the hybrid aloe or the yellow aloe, is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Aloe vera and Aloe aristata, which gives it unique traits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
