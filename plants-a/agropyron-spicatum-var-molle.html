Agropyron spicatum var. molle

Agropyron spicatum var. molle, commonly known as slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, slightly hairy leaves. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences with small, two-lobed florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in various ecosystems.
