Astragalus rumelicus var. euboicus

Astragalus rumelicus var. euboicus, commonly known as the Euboic milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Europe.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers in late spring. It is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through its root nodules.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and maintaining soil stability.
