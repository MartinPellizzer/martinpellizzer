Alocasia balgooyi

Alocasia balgooyi, commonly known as the Bornean arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, often with a striking dark green to almost black coloration. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are typically hidden among the foliage. Alocasia balgooyi is an ornamental plant valued for its dramatic foliage, though it is not commonly cultivated for its flowers.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
