Actaea arguta var. pauciflora

Actaea arguta var. pauciflora, commonly known as the slender bugbane, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant has compound, pinnately arranged leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
