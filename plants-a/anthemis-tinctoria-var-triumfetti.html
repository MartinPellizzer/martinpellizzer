Anthemis tinctoria var. triumfetti

Anthemis tinctoria var. triumfetti, commonly known as golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and dark brown central disc, forming daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening for its cheerful appearance.

The species is also valued for its historical use in dye production and traditional herbal medicine.
