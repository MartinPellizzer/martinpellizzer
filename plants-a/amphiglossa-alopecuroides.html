Amphiglossa alopecuroides

Amphiglossa alopecuroides, commonly known as the slender-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, often growing in forest understories. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
