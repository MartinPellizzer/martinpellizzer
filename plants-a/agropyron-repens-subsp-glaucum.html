Agropyron repens subsp. glaucum

Agropyron repens subsp.

glaucum, commonly known as the bluegrass or silvergrass, is a perennial grass species characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage. It is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils.

The plant has tufted growth habit with slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered with a waxy bloom, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
