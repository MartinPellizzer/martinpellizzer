Anthericum brevicaule

Anthericum brevicaule, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arum, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence, which consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments, such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly wavy margin.

Anthericum brevicaule is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral structure.
