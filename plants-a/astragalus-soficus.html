Astragalus soficus

Astragalus soficus, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus soficus is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
