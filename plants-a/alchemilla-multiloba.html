Alchemilla multiloba

Alchemilla multiloba, commonly known as the lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces upright flowering stems that bear small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade.

Alchemilla multiloba is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional herbal medicine.
