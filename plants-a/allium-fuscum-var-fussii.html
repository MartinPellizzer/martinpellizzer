Allium fuscum var. fussii

Allium fuscum var. fussii, commonly known as the purple garlic or wild leek, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal leaves that are broad and grass-like, often with a garlic scent when crushed. The plant produces small, clustered purple or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a useful culinary plant.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
