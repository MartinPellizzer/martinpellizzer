Arundinella setosa var. lanifera

Arundinella setosa var. lanifera, commonly known as the hairy bamboo or woolly bamboo, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems that are often covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
