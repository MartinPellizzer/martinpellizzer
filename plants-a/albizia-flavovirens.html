Albizia flavovirens

Albizia flavovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green mimosa, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spherical clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Albizia flavovirens is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
