Adiantum lathomi

Adiantum lathomi, commonly known as the Lathom's maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble lace. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are bi-lobed and have a distinctive, feathery appearance.

Adiantum lathomi is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
