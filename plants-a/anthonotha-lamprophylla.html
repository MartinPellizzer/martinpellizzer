Anthonotha lamprophylla

Anthonotha lamprophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has a sprawling growth habit and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Anthonotha lamprophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
