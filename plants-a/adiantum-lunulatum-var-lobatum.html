Adiantum lunulatum var. lobatum

Adiantum lunulatum var. lobatum, commonly known as the moonwort, is a fern that belongs to the family Adiantaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often have a silvery or pale underside. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices. Its spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds in clusters called sori.

This variety is distinguished by its lobed leaflets, which give it a unique and intricate appearance.
