Anthericum variegatum

Anthericum variegatum, commonly known as the variegated lily of the valley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, white flowers with a greenish tint. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves with distinctive white or yellow variegation. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.
