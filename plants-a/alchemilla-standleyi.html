Alchemilla standleyi

Alchemilla standleyi, commonly known as Standley's lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are clustered in a cup-like structure. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
