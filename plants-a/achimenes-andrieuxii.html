Achimenes andrieuxii

Achimenes andrieuxii, commonly known as the Andrieux's flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting throat. The plant produces showy bracts that enhance its ornamental appeal, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Achimenes andrieuxii is typically propagated through stem cuttings, which root easily and allow for quick propagation of new plants.
