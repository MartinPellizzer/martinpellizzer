Anthoxanthum occidentale

Anthoxanthum occidentale, commonly known as western false brome, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, meadows, and stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with reddish or purplish hues. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet at the tip of each stem, which produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Anthoxanthum occidentale plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
