Acacia inceana

Acacia inceana, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. Acacia inceana is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
