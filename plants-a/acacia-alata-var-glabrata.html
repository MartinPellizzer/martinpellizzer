Acacia alata var. glabrata

Acacia alata var. glabrata, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is distinguished by its smooth, glabrous stems, which set it apart from other acacia species.
