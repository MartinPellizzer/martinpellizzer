Atropis battandieri

Atropis battandieri, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow and green floral structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are small but numerous, forming dense clusters that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
