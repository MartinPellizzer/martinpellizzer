Asperula oppositifolia subsp. baltistanica

Asperula oppositifolia subsp.

baltistanica, commonly known as the Baltistan hollyhock, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in mountainous ecosystems.
