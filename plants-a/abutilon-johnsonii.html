Abutilon johnsonii

Abutilon johnsonii, commonly known as Johnson's abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red. It has a fibrous root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.

The plant is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
