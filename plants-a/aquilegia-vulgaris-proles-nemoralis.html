Aquilegia vulgaris proles nemoralis

Aquilegia vulgaris proles nemoralis, commonly known as the wood anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of pink, white, or purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas, thriving in loamy soils. Its flowers bloom in early spring, often before the canopy of trees fully develops.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
