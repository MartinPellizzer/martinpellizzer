Adamanthus lankesteri

Adamanthus lankesteri, commonly known as the Brazilian palm, is a striking plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a tall, slender palm with a single trunk and feathery, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody fruits containing dark brown seeds. Adamanthus lankesteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for landscape design.
