Atriplex serrata

Atriplex serrata, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its succulent, often lobed leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. It is well adapted to saline soils and can thrive in areas with low rainfall.

Atriplex serrata plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
