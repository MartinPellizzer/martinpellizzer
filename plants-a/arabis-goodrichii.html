Arabis goodrichii

Arabis goodrichii, commonly known as Goodrich's rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in mountainous regions. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, and its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Arabis goodrichii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
