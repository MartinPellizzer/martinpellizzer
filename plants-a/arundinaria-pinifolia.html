Arundinaria pinifolia

Arundinaria pinifolia, commonly known as the needleleaf bamboo, is a species of grass native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage with needle-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions. Its culms are hollow and jointed, typical of the grass family, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Arundinaria pinifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
