Andropogon subtilis

Andropogon subtilis, commonly known as slender bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, blue-green leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are usually greenish or reddish in color. It is often found in wetland ecosystems and plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.

Andropogon subtilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
