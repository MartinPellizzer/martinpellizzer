Asterocephalus atropurpureus

Asterocephalus atropurpureus, commonly known as the purple-headed aster, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, violet-purple flower heads that are typically surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Asterocephalus atropurpureus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
