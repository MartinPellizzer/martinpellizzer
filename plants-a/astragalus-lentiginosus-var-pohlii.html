Astragalus lentiginosus var. pohlii

Astragalus lentiginosus var. pohlii, commonly known as the Pohli's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, compound leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in dense, globular inflorescences. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This variety is often found in desert scrub and steppe habitats, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
