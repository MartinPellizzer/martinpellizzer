Argyreia breviscapa

Argyreia breviscapa, commonly known as the short-stemmed silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

However, it can be invasive in certain regions if not properly managed.
