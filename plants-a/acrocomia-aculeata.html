Acrocomia aculeata

Acrocomia aculeata, commonly known as the palm tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clusters of edible fruits, known as "pears," which are rich in nutrients. Acrocomia aculeata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of oil from its seeds.
