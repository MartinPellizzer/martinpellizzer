Acacia sclerophylla var. teretiuscula

Acacia sclerophylla var. teretiuscula, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear phyllodes that are typically green and slightly hairy. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
