Asclepias variegata var. major

Asclepias variegata var. major, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It features upright, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of striking, orange-tipped, pod-like fruits that contain seeds with silky parachutes. Its flowers are typically pink to reddish-purple, attracting a variety of pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

This species is well adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
