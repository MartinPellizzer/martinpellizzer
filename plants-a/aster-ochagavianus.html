Aster ochagavianus

Aster ochagavianus, commonly known as the Ochagavia aster, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, violet-blue to purple flower heads that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable subject for study in alpine botany and ecological research.
