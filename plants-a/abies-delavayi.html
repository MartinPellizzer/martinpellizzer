Abies delavayi

Abies delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the genus Abies, which includes various species of fir trees characterized by their conical shape and needle-like leaves. The tree typically grows at high altitudes, often in temperate and subalpine climates, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its cones are cylindrical and contain winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The bark of Abies delavayi is usually smooth and grayish, with a texture that becomes more rugged as the tree matures.
