Arthrostylidium pubescens

Arthrostylidium pubescens, commonly known as the hairy stick orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as rainforests or under dense vegetation. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its overall appearance.

The flowers are usually greenish or pale yellow, with a subtle fragrance, and bloom during the warmer months.
