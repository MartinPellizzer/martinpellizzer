Atitara caespitosa

Atitara caespitosa, commonly known as the coastal dune grass, is a perennial grass species native to the coastal regions of South America.

It forms dense, low-growing mats that help stabilize sandy soils and prevent erosion. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring. It thrives in saline and well-drained environments, making it an important component of coastal ecosystems.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively and establish quickly in disturbed areas.
