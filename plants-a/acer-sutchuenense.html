Acer sutchuenense

Acer sutchuenense, commonly known as the Sichuan maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, red flowers in early spring, followed by samaroid fruits that disperse its seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in gardens and parks.
