Acalypha phyllonomifolia

Acalypha phyllonomifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or golden leaf plant, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from green to reddish-brown. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Acalypha phyllonomifolia is also valued as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage.
