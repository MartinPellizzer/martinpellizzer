Anthericum kapiriense

Anthericum kapiriense, commonly known as the Kapiriense arum, is a herbaceous plant native to the rainforests of Central Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant features broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, aiding in moisture retention in its humid habitat. Its flowers are typically greenish-white with a prominent spadix, and it produces berries that are dispersed by birds.

Anthericum kapiriense plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
