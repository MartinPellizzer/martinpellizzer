Albuca ausensis

Albuca ausensis, commonly known as the Cape gold plant, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of yellow, bell-shaped flowers during the winter months. The plant is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to bloom reliably in suitable conditions.

Albuca ausensis is a member of the Asparagaceae family, which includes many other succulent plants.
