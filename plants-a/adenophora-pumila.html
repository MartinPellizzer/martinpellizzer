Adenophora pumila

Adenophora pumila, commonly known as the small milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy and bear small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale pink to white flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. Its milky sap is a distinguishing characteristic, and it is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Adenophora pumila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
