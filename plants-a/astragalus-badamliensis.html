Astragalus badamliensis

Astragalus badamliensis, commonly known as the Badamli milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Turkey.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is fibrous and can penetrate deep into the soil, allowing it to access limited water resources.

Astragalus badamliensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
