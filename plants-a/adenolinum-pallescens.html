Adenolinum pallescens

Adenolinum pallescens, commonly known as the pale adenolinum, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of South America. The plant is characterized by its rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. Its flowers, which are typically pink or red, bloom in a spike and are showy but short-lived.

Adenolinum pallescens thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.
