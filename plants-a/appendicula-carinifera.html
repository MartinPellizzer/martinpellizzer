Appendicula carinifera

Appendicula carinifera, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its erect stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are often clustered in dense inflorescences.

Appendicula carinifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
