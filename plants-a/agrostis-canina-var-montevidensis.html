Agrostis canina var. montevidensis

Agrostis canina var. montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevideo bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in temperate regions and is characterized by its fine, tufted blades and dense, mat-like growth habit. This variety is often found in moist, well-drained soils, particularly in grasslands and along riverbanks. Its botanical features include slender stems, small spikelets, and a high tolerance for cool climates.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in turfgrass applications.
