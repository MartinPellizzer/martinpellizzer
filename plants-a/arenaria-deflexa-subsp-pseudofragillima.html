Arenaria deflexa subsp. pseudofragillima

Arenaria deflexa subsp.

pseudofragillima, commonly known as the bent sand-arene, is a low-growing perennial plant native to sandy and gravelly habitats in the Mediterranean region. It typically features compact, mat-forming growth with small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This subspecies is notable for its resilience to harsh conditions, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
