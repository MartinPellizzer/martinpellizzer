Aesculus wilsonii

Aesculus wilsonii, commonly known as the Chinese horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The tree produces large, spiny capsules containing glossy, dark brown seeds. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are ovate to elliptical in shape.

Aesculus wilsonii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
