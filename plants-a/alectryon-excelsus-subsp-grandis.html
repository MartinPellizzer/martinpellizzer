Alectryon excelsus subsp. grandis

Alectryon excelsus subsp.

grandis, commonly known as the large-fruited alectryon, is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is native to eastern Australia. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils.

The plant is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and produces small, urn-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white. Its fruit is a distinctive, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
