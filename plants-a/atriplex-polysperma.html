Atriplex polysperma

Atriplex polysperma, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its succulent, salt-tolerant leaves that help it thrive in saline soils. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deep underground.

Atriplex polysperma plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for wildlife.
