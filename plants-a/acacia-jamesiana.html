Acacia jamesiana

Acacia jamesiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
