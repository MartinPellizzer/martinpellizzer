Anaphalis mucronata

Anaphalis mucronata, commonly known as the silverweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often appearing in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites.

Anaphalis mucronata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
