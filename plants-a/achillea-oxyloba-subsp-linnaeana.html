Achillea oxyloba subsp. linnaeana

Achillea oxyloba subsp.

linnaeana, commonly known as the Linnaean yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its finely divided, silvery-green leaves and flat, composite flower heads that range from white to pale pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic foliage.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
