Artemisia thuscula

Artemisia thuscula, commonly known as Great Sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-green, aromatic foliage and small, yellowish-gray flowers. The plant thrives in dry, arid environments and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its strong, minty scent.

Artemisia thuscula plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
