Astragalus paphlagonicus

Astragalus paphlagonicus, commonly known as the Paphlagonian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Caucasus.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Astragalus paphlagonicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
