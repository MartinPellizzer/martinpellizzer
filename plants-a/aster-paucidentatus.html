Aster paucidentatus

Aster paucidentatus, commonly known as the few-toothed aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped with few to no teeth, giving it its scientific name.

The showy purple or blue flowers bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
