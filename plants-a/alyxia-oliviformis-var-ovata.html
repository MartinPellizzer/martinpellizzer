Alyxia oliviformis var. ovata

Alyxia oliviformis var. ovata, commonly known as the oval-leaf alyxia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its ovate, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant communities in its habitat.
