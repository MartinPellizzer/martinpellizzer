Aspalathus cordata

Aspalathus cordata, commonly known as the Rooibos plant, is a shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and distinctive red-brown flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is well adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions. It is particularly noted for its unique chemical composition, which includes high levels of antioxidants and flavonoids.

Aspalathus cordata is primarily cultivated for its leaves, which are used to produce the popular herbal tea known as Rooibos.
