Acaciopsis constricta

Acaciopsis constricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are often arranged in pairs along the stems. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that help it survive in poor soil.

The plant is also valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
