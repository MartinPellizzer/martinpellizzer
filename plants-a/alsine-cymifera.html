Alsine cymifera

Alsine cymifera, commonly known as the common sandspurry, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellow or white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by capsules containing numerous small, rounded seeds. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in sandy or disturbed soils.

Alsine cymifera is often found in coastal areas and can be invasive in some regions.
