Aristida implexa

Aristida implexa, commonly known as the clustered dropseed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and compact growth habit, forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often mistaken for cotton or down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Aristida implexa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in dry, disturbed areas.
