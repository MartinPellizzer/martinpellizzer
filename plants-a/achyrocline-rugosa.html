Achyrocline rugosa

Achyrocline rugosa, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture.

Achyrocline rugosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
