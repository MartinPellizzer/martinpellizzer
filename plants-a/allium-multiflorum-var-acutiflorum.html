Allium multiflorum var. acutiflorum

Allium multiflorum var. acutiflorum, commonly known as the narrowflower garlic chives, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features upright, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, bulbous stem and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is closely related to other Allium species and is valued for its hardiness and ease of cultivation.
