Asparagus coddii

Asparagus coddii, commonly known as the Codd's asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Asparagus coddii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
