Andropogon echinatus

Andropogon echinatus, commonly known as the spiny grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, compact inflorescences that bear small, spiny bracts. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a rough, hairy texture.

Andropogon echinatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
