Ardisia rapaneifolia

Ardisia rapaneifolia, commonly known as the red-berried evergreen, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Ericaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are attractive to birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its compact growth habit and evergreen foliage make it suitable for use in hedges or as a specimen plant.
