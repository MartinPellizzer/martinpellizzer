Astragalus pseudomurinus

Astragalus pseudomurinus, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.
