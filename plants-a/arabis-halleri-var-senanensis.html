Arabis halleri var. senanensis

Arabis halleri var. senanensis, commonly known as the Korean rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Korean Peninsula.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The species is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
