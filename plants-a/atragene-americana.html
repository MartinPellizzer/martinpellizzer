Atragene americana

Atragene americana, commonly known as the American moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close during the day. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Its leaves are broad and ovate, often with a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are not only aesthetically pleasing but also attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
