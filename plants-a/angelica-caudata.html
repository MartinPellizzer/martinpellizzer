Angelica caudata

Angelica caudata, commonly known as the Indian cowplant, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots, parsley, and celery, and is characterized by its distinct aromatic compounds. The plant features a thick, fleshy root and tall, hollow stems with large, compound leaves that have a fern-like appearance. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are arranged in umbrella-like clusters called umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Angelica caudata is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
