Arundo phragmites var. gracilis

Arundo phragmites var. gracilis, commonly known as narrowleaf reed, is a variant of the common reed that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a tall, perennial grass that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of lakes. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often slightly narrower than those of the typical phragmites. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a smooth, grass-like texture.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
