Aster filiformis

Aster filiformis, commonly known as the threadleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, thread-like leaves and produces small, clustered blue to purple flowers in the fall. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.

Aster filiformis plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native range.
