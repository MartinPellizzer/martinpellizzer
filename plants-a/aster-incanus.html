Aster incanus

Aster incanus, commonly known as the hoary aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its grayish-white hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white to lavender, typically blooming in late summer and early fall. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and prairies, and is often found in disturbed soils.

Aster incanus is a valuable plant for pollinators, providing nectar and pollen to bees and butterflies.
