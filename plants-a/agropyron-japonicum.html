Agropyron japonicum

Agropyron japonicum, commonly known as Japanese wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as fields and roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a common species in ecological restoration projects.
