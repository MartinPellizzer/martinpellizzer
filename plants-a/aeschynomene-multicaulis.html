Aeschynomene multicaulis

Aeschynomene multicaulis, commonly known as smallleaf aeschynomene, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which are often accompanied by bracts. It is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Aeschynomene multicaulis is also known for its potential use in forage and as a nitrogen-fixing plant in agroecosystems.
