Acacia constablei

Acacia constablei, commonly known as the Constant's wattle, is a small shrub native to Western Australia.

It features feathery, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in winter, which are arranged in compact clusters. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

Acacia constablei is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
