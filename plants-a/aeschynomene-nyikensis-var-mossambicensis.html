Aeschynomene nyikensis var. mossambicensis

Aeschynomene nyikensis var. mossambicensis, commonly known as the African bushveld morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and savannas. The plant features slender, branching stems and produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and purple. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
