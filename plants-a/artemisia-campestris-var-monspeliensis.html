Artemisia campestris var. monspeliensis

Artemisia campestris var. monspeliensis, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. It is known for its strong, camphor-like scent and has been used historically in herbal medicine.

This variety is closely related to other artemisia species and is valued for its ecological and medicinal properties.
