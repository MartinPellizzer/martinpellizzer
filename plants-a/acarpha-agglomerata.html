Acarpha agglomerata

Acarpha agglomerata, commonly known as the clustered grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical structure makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
