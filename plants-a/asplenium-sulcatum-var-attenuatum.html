Asplenium sulcatum var. attenuatum

Asplenium sulcatum var. attenuatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a variant of the common maidenhair fern characterized by its slender, elongated fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan. The plant features delicate, finely divided leaves that are often arching or drooping, giving it a graceful appearance. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are typically found on the underside of the fronds, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
