Apium nodiflorum var. intermedium

Apium nodiflorum var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate celery.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. The plant features hollow, ridged stems and aromatic, fern-like leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

It is often grown as a herb for its edible leaves and seeds, which have a mild celery-like flavor.
