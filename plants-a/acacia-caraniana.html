Acacia caraniana

Acacia caraniana, commonly known as the silky-oak, is a large evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in a fine, silky down that gives the plant its common name. The tree produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which bloom in spring and attract various pollinators. Its wood is durable and has been used historically for construction and furniture.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
