Amaryllis × valletii

Amaryllis × valletii, commonly known as the hybrid amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis papilio.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as a bulbous perennial, with strap-like leaves that emerge from the base of the bulb. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Amaryllis × valletii is popular as a decorative plant, often cultivated for its vibrant blooms during the winter and early spring seasons.
