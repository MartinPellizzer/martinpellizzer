Aster subsalignus

Aster subsalignus, commonly known as the slender aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and showy, purple to blue flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster subsalignus is often found in sandy or loamy soils and is a valuable component of native plant ecosystems.
