Astragalus tscharikarensis

Astragalus tscharikarensis, commonly known as the Tscharikar Valley milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the genus Astragalus. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
