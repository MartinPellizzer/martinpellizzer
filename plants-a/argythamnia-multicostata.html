Argythamnia multicostata

Argythamnia multicostata, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring and are followed by small, three-lobed seed capsules. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that allow it to survive in sandy soils.

Argythamnia multicostata plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
