Angelica archangelica subsp. archangelica

Angelica archangelica subsp.

archangelica, commonly known as greater angelica, is a biennial or perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its large, compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

The plant produces tall, branched stems with small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, containing volatile oils that contribute to its aromatic properties.

This species is also valued in herbal remedies for its purported digestive and respiratory benefits.
