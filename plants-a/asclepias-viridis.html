Asclepias viridis

Asclepias viridis, commonly known as green milkweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, bean-like seed pods. It is a critical host plant for monarch butterfly larvae and plays a vital role in supporting pollinators.

Asclepias viridis thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed areas.
