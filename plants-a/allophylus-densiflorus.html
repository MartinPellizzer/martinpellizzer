Allophylus densiflorus

Allophylus densiflorus, commonly known as the denseflower alder, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and small mammals. It typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often forming dense thickets in coastal forests.

Allophylus densiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
