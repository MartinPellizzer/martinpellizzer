Alpinia galanga

Alpinia galanga, commonly known as wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features rhizomatous stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow and are clustered in spikes.

The rhizomes of Alpinia galanga are used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their pungent, aromatic qualities.
