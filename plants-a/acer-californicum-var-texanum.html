Acer californicum var. texanum

Acer californicum var. texanum, commonly known as the Texas maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, typically turning vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various garden settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its seasonal color changes and compact growth habit.
