Adesmia cordobensis

Adesmia cordobensis, commonly known as the Cordoba burr legume, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds, which are often dispersed by animals. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in ecological and agricultural research.
