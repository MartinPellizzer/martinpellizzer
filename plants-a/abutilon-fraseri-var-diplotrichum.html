Abutilon fraseri var. diplotrichum

Abutilon fraseri var. diplotrichum, commonly known as the Fraser's abutilon, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in suitable conditions.
