Allium rotundum subsp. preslianum

Allium rotundum subsp.

preslianum, commonly known as the Persian leek or garlic chives, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean and Middle Eastern regions. It is characterized by its rosette of broad, fleshy, garlic-scented leaves that are often used in culinary applications.

The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in the spring. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a valuable addition to various dishes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Alliaceae family and is closely related to other members of the allium genus.
