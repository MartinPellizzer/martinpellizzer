Aloe tweedieae

Aloe tweedieae, commonly known as the Tweedie's aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Aloe tweedieae is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves to survive dry periods.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
