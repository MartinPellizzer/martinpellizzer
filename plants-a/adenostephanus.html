Adenostephanus

Adenostephanus, commonly known as the desert star, is a genus of flowering plants native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

These plants are characterized by their low, sprawling growth habit and their ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The leaves are typically small, succulent, and often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss. Adenostephanus species produce small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple, blooming in spring.
