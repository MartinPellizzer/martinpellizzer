Anogra nuttalliana

Anogra nuttalliana, commonly known as the Nuttall's anogra, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its growth habit is typically low and sprawling, making it well adapted to arid and rocky environments.

Anogra nuttalliana is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its potential medicinal properties.
