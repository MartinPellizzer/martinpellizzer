Aesculus × carnea f. pendula

Aesculus × carnea f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping horse chestnut, is a striking ornamental tree characterized by its pendulous, weeping habit.

It features large, palmately compound leaves that are deeply lobed and have a velvety texture. The tree produces showy, white to pinkish flowers in upright clusters during late spring. Its fruit is a spiny capsule that contains shiny, amber-brown seeds.

This cultivar is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often planted in gardens and parks for its ornamental value.
