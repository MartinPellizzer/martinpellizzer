Adhatoda origanoides

Adhatoda origanoides, commonly known as the Indian banyan or the Indian adhatoda, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to India and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in Ayurvedic practices.

The plant thrives in humid climates and is adaptable to a range of soil types.
