Amyema vieillardii

Amyema vieillardii, commonly known as the yellow box, is a small to medium-sized tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, often grey or yellowish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, typically in spring, followed by woody pods containing seeds.

Amyema vieillardii is often found in dry sclerophyll forests and is valued for its ecological and ornamental significance.
