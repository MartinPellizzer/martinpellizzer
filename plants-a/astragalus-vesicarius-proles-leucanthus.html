Astragalus vesicarius proles leucanthus

Astragalus vesicarius proles leucanthus, commonly known as the white-flowered milkvetch, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in regions of the western United States and parts of Canada.
