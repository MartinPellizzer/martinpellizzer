Agrostis vulgaris var. pallida

Agrostis vulgaris var. pallida, commonly known as pale bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growths and is often found in moist, calcareous soils. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and have a bluish-green color, while the inflorescence consists of a single spikelet. It is characterized by its pale coloration, which gives it its common name.

This plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.
