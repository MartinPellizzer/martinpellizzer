Anthemis ximenezii

Anthemis ximenezii, commonly known as the Ximenez's chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers with a central disk, and it is often cultivated for its aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance and ecological role.
