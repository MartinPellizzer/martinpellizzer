Acacia barbinervis

Acacia barbinervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with narrow, parallel veins. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and can thrive in arid environments.

Acacia barbinervis is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its ornamental appeal.
