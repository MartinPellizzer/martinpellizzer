Arenaria ciliata var. subacaulis

Arenaria ciliata var. subacaulis, commonly known as the woolly mouse-eared cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has succulent, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in rocky areas or along coastal regions.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
