Acianthera aculeata

Acianthera aculeata, commonly known as the spiny acianthera, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its erect stem and showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive spiny structure on the petals. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are adapted for epiphytic growth, allowing it to thrive on tree trunks or branches.

Acianthera aculeata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
