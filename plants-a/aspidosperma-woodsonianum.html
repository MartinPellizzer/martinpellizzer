Aspidosperma woodsonianum

Aspidosperma woodsonianum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Ceratonia siliqua* var. *woodsonii*, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and elliptical, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and has a slow growth rate, making it a long-lived species.
