Amorpha canescens var. glabrata

Amorpha canescens var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth false indigo, is a perennial legume native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces striking blue to purple pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it a valuable plant for soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
