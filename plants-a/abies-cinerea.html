Abies cinerea

Abies cinerea, commonly known as the white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and its silver-blue foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The needles are short, stiff, and have a whitish coating, contributing to its common name. It produces cones that hang downward, containing seeds surrounded by a papery wing.

This species is often found in high-elevation forests and is adapted to cool, moist climates.
