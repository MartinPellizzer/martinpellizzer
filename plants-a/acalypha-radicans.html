Acalypha radicans

Acalypha radicans, commonly known as the wandering jew, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a trailing or climbing plant with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread easily, making it a popular ornamental plant.

However, it can become invasive in some environments if not controlled.
