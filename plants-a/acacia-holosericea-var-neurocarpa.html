Acacia holosericea var. neurocarpa

Acacia holosericea var. neurocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical spikes, which bloom in spring. It is adapted to dry and sandy soils, making it a hardy species suitable for arid environments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in reforestation projects.
