Atriplex patula var. tatarica

Atriplex patula var. tatarica, commonly known as tatarian saltbush, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are adapted to conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its salt-tolerant nature makes it a valuable plant for reclaiming saline soils.

It is often used in xeriscaping and as a forage plant for livestock in dry regions.
