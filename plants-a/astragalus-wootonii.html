Astragalus wootonii

Astragalus wootonii, commonly known as Wooton's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple pairs of small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain seeds.

Astragalus wootonii plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local pollinators.
