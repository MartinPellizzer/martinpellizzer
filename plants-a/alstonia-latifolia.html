Alstonia latifolia

Alstonia latifolia, commonly known as the African angel's trumpet, is a tropical tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its large, showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has smooth, grayish bark and produces clusters of woody, spindle-shaped fruits. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Alstonia latifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
