Acacia arceuthos

Acacia arceuthos, commonly known as the stinkwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The tree produces a distinctive, sticky resin that has been historically used for various purposes, including as a chewing gum. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing it with a rugged appearance.

Acacia arceuthos is also known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it well-adapted to its native environment.
