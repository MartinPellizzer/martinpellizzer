Ampelocissus elegans

Ampelocissus elegans, commonly known as the velvet leaf creeper, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used as a ornamental ground cover or climbing plant.

Its rapid growth and ability to cover large areas make it popular in horticulture.
