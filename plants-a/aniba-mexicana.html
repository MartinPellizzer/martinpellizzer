Aniba mexicana

Aniba mexicana, commonly known as Mexican wild laurier, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel trees and spices like cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine.

Aniba mexicana thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.
