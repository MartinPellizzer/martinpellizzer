Antirrhinum origanifolium

Antirrhinum origanifolium, commonly known as the lemon scented snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that emit a subtle lemony fragrance. The plant produces tall spikes of bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its aromatic foliage, which adds both visual and sensory appeal.
