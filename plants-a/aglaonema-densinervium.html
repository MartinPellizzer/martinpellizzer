Aglaonema densinervium

Aglaonema densinervium, commonly known as the dense-veined arrowroot, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent, dense veins that give it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows as an understory species in humid, shaded forest environments. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Aglaonema densinervium is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
