Argythamnia foliosa

Argythamnia foliosa, commonly known as the leafy argythamnia, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its gray-green, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in harsh climates. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, typically during the spring and early summer. Its root system is deep and drought-tolerant, allowing it to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Argythamnia foliosa is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
