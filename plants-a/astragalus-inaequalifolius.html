Astragalus inaequalifolius

Astragalus inaequalifolius, commonly known as the unequal-leaved milkvetch, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia, including parts of Mongolia and China. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically unequal in size. It produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in clusters, and its pods contain several small, flat seeds.

This plant is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.
