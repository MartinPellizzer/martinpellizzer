Aster phlogifolius

Aster phlogifolius, commonly known as the flameleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flower heads that bloom in the fall. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or orange, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Aster phlogifolius is a valuable addition to native gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
