Adenophora taiwaniana

Adenophora taiwaniana, commonly known as the Taiwan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are opposite, lanceolate, and often hairy, while its stems are erect and branched.

Adenophora taiwaniana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
