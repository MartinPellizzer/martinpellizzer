Aster choralis

Aster choralis, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its conical center is composed of many small, tubular flowers surrounded by petal-like bracts.

Aster choralis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
