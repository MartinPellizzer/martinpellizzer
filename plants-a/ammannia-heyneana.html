Ammannia heyneana

Ammannia heyneana, commonly known as redroot ammannia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. The plant is characterized by its reddish stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense spikes, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible for some animals.

Ammannia heyneana plays a role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
