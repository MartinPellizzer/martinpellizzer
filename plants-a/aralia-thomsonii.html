Aralia thomsonii

Aralia thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's aralia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically divided into several narrow, elongated segments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in an umbel, which are clustered together in a distinctive, flat-topped inflorescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
