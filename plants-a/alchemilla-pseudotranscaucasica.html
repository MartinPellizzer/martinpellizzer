Alchemilla pseudotranscaucasica

Alchemilla pseudotranscaucasica, commonly known as Caucasian lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Alchemilla pseudotranscaucasica is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
