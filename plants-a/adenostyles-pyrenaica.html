Adenostyles pyrenaica

Adenostyles pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Pyrenees mountain range.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky and alpine environments. The plant features erect, branched stems and produces clusters of bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

Adenostyles pyrenaica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in mountain ecosystems.
