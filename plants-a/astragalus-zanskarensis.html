Astragalus zanskarensis

Astragalus zanskarensis, commonly known as the Zanskar milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, particularly in the Zanskar region of India.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pale purple. The plant grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to extreme conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus zanskarensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its harsh habitat and is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
