Azalea recentissima

Azalea recentissima, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features lance-shaped, dark green leaves. Its flowers come in shades of pink, red, and white, often with a distinct scent.

Azalea recentissima prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
