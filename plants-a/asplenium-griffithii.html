Asplenium griffithii

Asplenium griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern native to the mountainous regions of South Asia.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its compact, clustered growth habit. The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found on rock surfaces or in rocky crevices.

Asplenium griffithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
