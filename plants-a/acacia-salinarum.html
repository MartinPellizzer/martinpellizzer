Acacia salinarum

Acacia salinarum, commonly known as the saltbush acacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and feathery, salt-tolerant foliage. The plant thrives in saline soils and is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions. Its root system helps stabilize sandy or degraded soils, making it useful for land rehabilitation.

Acacia salinarum plays a vital role in supporting local wildlife, including insects and birds.
