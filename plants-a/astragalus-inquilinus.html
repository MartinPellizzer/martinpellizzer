Astragalus inquilinus

Astragalus inquilinus, commonly known as the Sierra foothill milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Astragalus inquilinus is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
