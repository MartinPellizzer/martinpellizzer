Abolboda vaginata

Abolboda vaginata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety hair. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes. Its seeds are enclosed in a capsule that splits open when mature.

Abolboda vaginata is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
