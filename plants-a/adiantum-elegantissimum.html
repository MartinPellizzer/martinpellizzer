Adiantum elegantissimum

Adiantum elegantissimum, commonly known as the elegant maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often arched or sickle-shaped. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually bright green and have a distinctive, feathery appearance.

Adiantum elegantissimum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
