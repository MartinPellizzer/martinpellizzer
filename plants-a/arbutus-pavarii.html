Arbutus pavarii

Arbutus pavarii, commonly known as the Pavari stonecrop, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, leathery leaves that are typically grayish-green in color, adapted to withstand drought and rocky soil conditions. The plant produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers during the spring, which are followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It is often found growing on rocky outcrops and slopes, showcasing its resilience in harsh environments.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
