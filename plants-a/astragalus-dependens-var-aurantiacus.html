Astragalus dependens var. aurantiacus

Astragalus dependens var. aurantiacus, commonly known as the golden milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access moisture from lower soil layers.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators.
