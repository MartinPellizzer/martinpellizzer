Amaryllis × rubicunda

Amaryllis × rubicunda, commonly known as the red amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis papillosa.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant produces strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically a deep green. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight to bloom.

Amaryllis × rubicunda is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
