Acetosella corniculata var. villosa

Acetosella corniculata var. villosa, commonly known as hairy wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, giving it its common name, and small yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces capsule fruits containing several seeds.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable species in its native habitats.
