Acaciella angustissima

Acaciella angustissima, commonly known as the narrowleaf acaciella, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which bloom during the dry season. It is adapted to survive in harsh, low-nutrient soils and can tolerate high temperatures and drought.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
