Allocarya acanthocarpa var. oligochaeta

Allocarya acanthocarpa var. oligochaeta, commonly known as the spiny fruit Allocarya, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, spiny, yellowish flowers and small, elliptical leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces woody, spiny capsules that contain small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
