Arabis × digenea

Arabis × digenea, commonly known as the hybrid rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is relatively cold-hardy and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

Arabis × digenea is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
