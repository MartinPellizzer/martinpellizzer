Athyrium nigripes

Athyrium nigripes, commonly known as the black-stemmed lady fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy fronds that are typically divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are clustered on the undersides of the fronds.

Athyrium nigripes is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
