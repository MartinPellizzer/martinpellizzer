Aster diplopappus

Aster diplopappus, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of many small florets. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and its flowers are usually purple to blue in color.

Aster diplopappus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
