Alchemilla velutina

Alchemilla velutina, commonly known as velvet lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of velvety, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
