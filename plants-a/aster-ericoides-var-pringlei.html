Aster ericoides var. pringlei

Aster ericoides var. pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's heath aster, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It features dense clusters of small, showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or open woodland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
