Appendicula pendula

Appendicula pendula, commonly known as the hanging appendicula, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Gesneriaceae.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil, this species thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant's leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a glossy surface.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its compact size and attractive floral display.
