Astracantha denudata

Astracantha denudata, commonly known as the naked thorned cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its spineless, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months, typically from late spring to early summer. It is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
