Aquilegia ottonis var. grata

Aquilegia ottonis var. grata, commonly known as the golden crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, spurred flowers that are typically a vibrant yellow or orange, with five petals and two elongated nectar-rich spurs. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed, and it thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats at high elevations.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
