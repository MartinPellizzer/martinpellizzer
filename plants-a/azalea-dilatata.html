Azalea dilatata

Azalea dilatata, commonly known as the broadleaf azalea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 feet, with leathery, elliptical leaves. Its flowers range in color from white to deep pink, and it prefers acidic, well-drained soil.

Azalea dilatata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
