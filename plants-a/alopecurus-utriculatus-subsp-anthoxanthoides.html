Alopecurus utriculatus subsp. anthoxanthoides

Alopecurus utriculatus subsp.

anthoxanthoides, commonly known as the yellow basket grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces dense clusters of small, yellowish to brownish spikelets.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and the inflorescence consists of two rows of spikelets.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
