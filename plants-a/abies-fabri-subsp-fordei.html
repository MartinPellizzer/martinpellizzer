Abies fabri subsp. fordei

Abies fabri subsp.

fordei, commonly known as the Forde's fir, is a rare and endemic subspecies native to the mountainous regions of northern China. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure typical of the fir genus.

The foliage is typically dark green and densely packed, providing a dense canopy. It thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil, often found in high-altitude forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
