Anthyllis rubriflora

Anthyllis rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Anthyllis rubriflora plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
