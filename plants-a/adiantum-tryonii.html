Adiantum tryonii

Adiantum tryonii, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern species native to the eastern United States.

It features finely divided, lacy fronds that are typically dark green with a slightly curled appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soil. Adiantum tryonii is characterized by its unique, arching fronds that resemble the flowing hair of a maiden, giving it its common name.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
