Axonopus pubivaginatus var. limae

Axonopus pubivaginatus var. limae, commonly known as the coastal flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and coastal areas, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered spikelets that are arranged in a loose panicle.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to saline conditions, making it an important species in coastal ecosystems.
