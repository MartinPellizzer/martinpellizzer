Allophylus brachycalyx

Allophylus brachycalyx, commonly known as the short-calyx alpine holly, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clustered, bright red berries that are a striking feature of the plant. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests and alpine meadows. Its botanical structure includes woody stems and persistent foliage, which help it survive in harsh climates.

Allophylus brachycalyx is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and ecological role in its native habitat.
