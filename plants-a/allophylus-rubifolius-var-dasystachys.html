Allophylus rubifolius var. dasystachys

Allophylus rubifolius var. dasystachys, commonly known as the red-leafed alpine holly, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with dark green leaves that often turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by red berries that are a source of food for birds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested highlands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
