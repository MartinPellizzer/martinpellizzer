Anthyllis cytisoides var. garrafensis

Anthyllis cytisoides var. garrafensis, commonly known as the Garraf broom, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume pods and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in rocky and calcareous soils, often found in Mediterranean climates.

This variety is distinguished by its unique morphology and ecological adaptations to its native environment.
