Avena sativa var. ludoviciana

Avena sativa var. ludoviciana, commonly known as the Louisiana wild oat, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and wetlands. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, blade-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
