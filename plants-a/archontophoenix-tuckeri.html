Archontophoenix tuckeri

Archontophoenix tuckeri, commonly known as the Tucker's palms, is a species of palm native to the rainforests of northeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach lengths of up to 6 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by edible, orange-colored berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understorey of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
