Acalypha integrifolia var. saltuum

Acalypha integrifolia var. saltuum, commonly known as the Saltuum ironweed, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salinity and strong winds.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
