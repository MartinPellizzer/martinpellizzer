Allium rotundifolium

Allium rotundifolium, commonly known as the round-leaf garlic or mountain garlic, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of western North America, including parts of California and Oregon. The plant features a bulbous stem with narrow, rounded leaves and produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in cooler, high-altitude environments.

Allium rotundifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
