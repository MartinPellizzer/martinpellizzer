Andropogon schoenanthus subsp. proximus

Andropogon schoenanthus subsp.

proximus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its slender, blue-green foliage and compact, tufted growth habit.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
