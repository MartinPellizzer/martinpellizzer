Anogeissus phillyreifolia

Anogeissus phillyreifolia, commonly known as the Indian sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its aromatic, pale yellow wood that is highly valued in traditional medicine and incense. The plant has simple, opposite, and elliptic leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and pale underneath. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by small, round, woody fruits containing a single seed.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in dry and semi-arid conditions, making it an important component of dry forest ecosystems.
