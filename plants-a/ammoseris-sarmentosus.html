Ammoseris sarmentosus

Ammoseris sarmentosus, commonly known as the creeping ammoseris, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features a sprawling growth habit with numerous stolons that root at the nodes, forming dense mats. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in loose clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh environments.
