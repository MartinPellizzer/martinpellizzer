Amorpha croceolanata

Amorpha croceolanata, commonly known as the yellow indigo bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for xeriscaping.

Botanically, it belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
