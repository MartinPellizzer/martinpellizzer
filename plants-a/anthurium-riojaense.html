Anthurium riojaense

Anthurium riojaense, commonly known as the Riojaense Philodendron, is a tropical plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces a spadix inflorescence surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is typical of the genus Anthurium. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
