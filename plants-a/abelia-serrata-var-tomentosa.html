Abelia serrata var. tomentosa

Abelia serrata var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, grayish-green foliage and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and has a rounded, compact form. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges and a dense, velvety texture.

It prefers partial shade to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to gardens.
