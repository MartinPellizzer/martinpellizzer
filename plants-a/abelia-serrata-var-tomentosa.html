Abelia serrata var. tomentosa

Abelia serrata var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, grayish-green foliage and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers partial shade to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to gardens.
