Acaena argentea var. pluribracteata

Acaena argentea var. pluribracteata, commonly known as the silverweed or silver cushion, is a low-growing perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It features dense clusters of small, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny, yellow flowers that appear in early spring, followed by spiny, berry-like fruits that are adapted for animal dispersal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest floors or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of New Zealand's native flora and a subject of ecological study.
