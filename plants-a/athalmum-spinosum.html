Athalmum spinosum

Athalmum spinosum, commonly known as the spiny alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, or violet. The plant features spiny or hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Athalmum spinosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
