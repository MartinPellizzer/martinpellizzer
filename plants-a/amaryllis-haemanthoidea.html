Amaryllis haemanthoidea

Amaryllis haemanthoidea, commonly known as the red amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has strap-like, evergreen leaves that form a rosette at the base. It produces tall, erect stems that bear clusters of showy, tubular flowers.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
