Amorpha laevigata

Amorpha laevigata, commonly known as the silky rosinweed, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and bright yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in sunny, dry habitats such as prairies and open woodlands, and is known for its ecological importance as a food source for native insects.
