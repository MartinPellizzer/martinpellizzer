Astragalus depressus var. medius

Astragalus depressus var. medius, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically features slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
