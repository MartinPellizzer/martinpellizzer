Artemisia paucicephala

Artemisia paucicephala, commonly known as the narrow-headed wormwood, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish flower heads that appear in late summer. It typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter.

Artemisia paucicephala is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities in dry gardens.
