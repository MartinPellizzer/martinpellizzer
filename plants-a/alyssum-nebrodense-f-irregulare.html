Alyssum nebrodense f. irregulare

Alyssum nebrodense f. irregulare, commonly known as the irregular alpine alyssum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, particularly the Alps and the Iberian Peninsula. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and limestone habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
