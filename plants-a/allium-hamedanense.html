Allium hamedanense

Allium hamedanense, commonly known as the Hamedan garlic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fleshy bulbs and strap-like leaves. The plant produces umbels of white or pale purple flowers, which bloom in the spring. Its bulbs are used in traditional medicine and cuisine, valued for their pungent flavor and potential health benefits.

Allium hamedanense thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens in temperate climates.
