Arthraxon rudis

Arthraxon rudis, commonly known as the rigid arrowhead, is a perennial aquatic plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It typically grows in shallow water and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread across the water's surface or along the bottom. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually green or brown, and appear in clusters.

Arthraxon rudis plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems by reducing water currents and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
