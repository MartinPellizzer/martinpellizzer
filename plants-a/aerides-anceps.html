Aerides anceps

Aerides anceps, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and the production of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic nature. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

Aerides anceps is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
