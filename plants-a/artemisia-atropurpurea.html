Artemisia atropurpurea

Artemisia atropurpurea, commonly known as purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and purple to pink flower heads that resemble coneflowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its aromatic leaves and showy blooms contribute to its value in both ecological and ornamental contexts.
