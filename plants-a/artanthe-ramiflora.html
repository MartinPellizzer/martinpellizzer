Artanthe ramiflora

Artanthe ramiflora, commonly known as the branched artanthe, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Artanthe ramiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
