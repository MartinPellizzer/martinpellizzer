Astragalus bethlehemiticus var. grandiflorus

Astragalus bethlehemiticus var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered biblical balm, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb native to the Middle East and parts of the Mediterranean region. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of pink to purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
