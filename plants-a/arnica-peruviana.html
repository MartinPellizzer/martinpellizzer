Arnica peruviana

Arnica peruviana, commonly known as Peruvian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its roots and leaves contain compounds with potential anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Due to its medicinal value, it is sometimes harvested for use in herbal remedies.
