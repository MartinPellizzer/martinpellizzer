Alstroemeria andina var. venustula

Alstroemeria andina var. venustula, commonly known as the Andean lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors including red, orange, and yellow. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Alstroemeria andina var. venustula is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
