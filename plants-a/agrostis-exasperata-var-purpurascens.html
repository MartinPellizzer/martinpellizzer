Agrostis exasperata var. purpurascens

Agrostis exasperata var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its distinctive purple-tinged inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wet meadows, bogs, and heathlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological habitats.
