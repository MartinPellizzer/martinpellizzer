Adenandra macradenia

Adenandra macradenia, commonly known as the Natal pear, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of about 5 meters. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth texture with a glossy appearance.

Adenandra macradenia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
