Acacia bucerophora

Acacia bucerophora, commonly known as the giant acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact globular heads, which appear during the rainy season. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Acacia bucerophora is also valued for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
