Abutilon striatum f. palmatifidum

Abutilon striatum f. palmatifidum, commonly known as the palmate abutilon, is a cultivar of the velvetleaf plant.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its broad, palmately lobed leaves that give it a distinctive, maple-like appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.
