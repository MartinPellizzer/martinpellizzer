Allium fallax

Allium fallax, commonly known as the autumn garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features strap-like, grassy leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in the fall. The plant's bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making them useful in culinary applications. Allium fallax thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
