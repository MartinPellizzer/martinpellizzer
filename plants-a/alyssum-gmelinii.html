Alyssum gmelinii

Alyssum gmelinii, commonly known as the alpine alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Alyssum gmelinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and alpine settings.

It is also notable for its ability to tolerate cold temperatures and high altitudes.
