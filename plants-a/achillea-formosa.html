Achillea formosa

Achillea formosa, commonly known as the Showy Yarrow, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features finely divided, silver-green leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry meadows and open woodlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower landscapes.
