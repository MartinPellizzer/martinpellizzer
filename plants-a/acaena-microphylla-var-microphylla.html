Acaena microphylla var. microphylla

Acaena microphylla var. microphylla, commonly known as New Zealand spinach, is a herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that develop into spiny, spherical fruits. The plant is adapted to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its edible leaves, which have a spinach-like flavor.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the rose family (Rosaceae).
