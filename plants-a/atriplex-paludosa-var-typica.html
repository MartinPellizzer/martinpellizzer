Atriplex paludosa var. typica

Atriplex paludosa var. typica, commonly known as saltbush, is a perennial shrub native to wet or seasonally flooded areas of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, gray-green leaves that are adapted to tolerate saline and waterlogged soils. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland environments.

Atriplex paludosa var. typica is often found in marshes, pond edges, and other moist habitats.
