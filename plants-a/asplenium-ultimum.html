Asplenium ultimum

Asplenium ultimum, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often deeply divided. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with spore-producing structures called sori located on the underside.

Asplenium ultimum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
