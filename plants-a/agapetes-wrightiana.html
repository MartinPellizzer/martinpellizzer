Agapetes wrightiana

Agapetes wrightiana, commonly known as the Wright's silverleaf, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the cloud forests of western South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a silvery-gray pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale pink, which bloom in late spring or early summer.

Agapetes wrightiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
