Anthemis rumelica var. pallida

Anthemis rumelica var. pallida, commonly known as the pale anthemis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and often have a faintly aromatic scent.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
