Aberia tristis

Aberia tristis, commonly known as the bitter ash, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Oleaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Its bark is thick and fissured, often exuding a bitter sap that has been used traditionally in some cultures.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
