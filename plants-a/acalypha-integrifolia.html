Acalypha integrifolia

Acalypha integrifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, bract-based inflorescences with bright yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Acalypha integrifolia is also known for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and floral display.
