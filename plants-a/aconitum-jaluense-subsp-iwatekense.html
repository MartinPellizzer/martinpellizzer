Aconitum jaluense subsp. iwatekense

Aconitum jaluense subsp.

iwatekense, commonly known as the Iwate aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, terminal clusters. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments, and are covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

This subspecies is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions of northern Japan.
