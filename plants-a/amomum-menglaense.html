Amomum menglaense

Amomum menglaense, commonly known as Mengla cardamom, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is native to the Yunnan province in China.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, reddish-brown fruits that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique fragrance and medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and herbalists alike.
