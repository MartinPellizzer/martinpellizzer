Anthurium attenuatifolium

Anthurium attenuatifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence known as a spadix, which is surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant produces strikingly colorful spathes that can range from deep red to orange, often with intricate patterns. Its foliage consists of long, narrow, and pointed leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture.

Anthurium attenuatifolium is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
