Arum marmoratum

Arum marmoratum, commonly known as the marbled arrowhead, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves with a distinctive marbled pattern of green and white, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking, spatulate-shaped flowers that are typically green with a red or purple spathe. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often grown as an ornamental plant.

Its foliage and unique floral structure make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and indoor collections.
