Avena fedtschenkoi

Avena fedtschenkoi, commonly known as the Russian oat or Fedtschenko's oat, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with both male and female flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Avena fedtschenkoi is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
