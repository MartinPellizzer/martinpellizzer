Aesculus turbinata var. pubescens

Aesculus turbinata var. pubescens, commonly known as the Chinese horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces large, showy white or pink flowers in clusters during the spring. Its fruit is a spiny capsule containing several chestnut-like seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
