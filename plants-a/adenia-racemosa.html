Adenia racemosa

Adenia racemosa, commonly known as the mountain devil or rock fig, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats. The plant features simple, elliptical leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive reddish or purple underside. Its showy, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in racemes and bloom during the warmer months.

Adenia racemosa is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native environment.
