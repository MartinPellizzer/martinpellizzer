Agropyron panormitanum var. heterophyllum

Agropyron panormitanum var. heterophyllum, commonly known as the Sicilian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity.
