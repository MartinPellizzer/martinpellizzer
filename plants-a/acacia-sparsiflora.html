Acacia sparsiflora

Acacia sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparse-flower acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically bipinnate and covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are often visited by pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

The wood of Acacia sparsiflora is hard and durable, historically used by indigenous communities for various purposes.
