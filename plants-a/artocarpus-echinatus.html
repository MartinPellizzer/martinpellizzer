Artocarpus echinatus

Artocarpus echinatus, commonly known as the rough-fig or wild breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, spiny fruit. The fruit, which is typically green when mature, turns yellow or orange and has a rough, bumpy surface. The plant is valued for its edible fruit, which is used in traditional cuisines, and for its potential medicinal properties.

It thrives in humid climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.
