Anaphalis scopulosa

Anaphalis scopulosa, commonly known as the rock heath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble single blooms. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in alpine and subalpine environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, white to pale yellow flowers.

Anaphalis scopulosa is often found in open habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
