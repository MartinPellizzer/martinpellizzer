Arundinella berteroniana

Arundinella berteroniana, commonly known as the Mexican bamboo, is a perennial grass native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a prominent midrib and a rough, hairy texture.

Arundinella berteroniana is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is sometimes used in traditional landscaping for its ornamental value.
