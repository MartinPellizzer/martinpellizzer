Acacia auripila

Acacia auripila, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in globular clusters. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions. Its flowers are an important food source for native pollinators, including bees and birds.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is a symbol of Australian national identity.
