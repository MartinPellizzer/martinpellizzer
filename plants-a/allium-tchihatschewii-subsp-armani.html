Allium tchihatschewii subsp. armani

Allium tchihatschewii subsp.

armani, commonly known as Armenian garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and adjacent areas. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pinkish flowers.

The plant produces small, ovoid bulbs that are often used in traditional cuisine and herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and culinary plant collections.
