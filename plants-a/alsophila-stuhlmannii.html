Alsophila stuhlmannii

Alsophila stuhlmannii, commonly known as the Stuhlmann's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its root system helps stabilize soil, making it valuable in ecological restoration projects.

The fronds are often used in traditional medicine and basket weaving in local communities.
