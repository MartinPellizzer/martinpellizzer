Acacia trigona

Acacia trigona, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. Acacia trigona is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor soils.

It is also notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
