Artemisia camphorata var. saxatilis

Artemisia camphorata var. saxatilis, commonly known as mountain wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flower heads that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine habitats. Its essential oils, containing compounds like camphor and thujone, contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and digestive benefits.
