Artemisia campestris var. maritima

Artemisia campestris var. maritima, commonly known as sea wormwood, is a hardy perennial plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flower heads. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to salt-laden environments. It is often found in dunes and other coastal habitats where it helps stabilize the soil.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional remedies.
