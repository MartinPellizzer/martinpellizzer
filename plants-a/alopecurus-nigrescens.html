Alopecurus nigrescens

Alopecurus nigrescens, commonly known as black bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, dark green leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spikelets that mature into dark, shiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is often found in meadows, pastures, and along streambanks, thriving in temperate climates.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
