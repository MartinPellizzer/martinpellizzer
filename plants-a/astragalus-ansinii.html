Astragalus ansinii

Astragalus ansinii, commonly known as the Ansinii milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces pods that contain seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

Astragalus ansinii is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
