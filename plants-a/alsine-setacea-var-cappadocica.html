Alsine setacea var. cappadocica

Alsine setacea var. cappadocica, commonly known as the Cappadocian sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to regions of Turkey and the Middle East.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branched stems and small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is of interest to botanists and ecologists.
