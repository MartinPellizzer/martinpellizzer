Acacia nuceiana

Acacia nuceiana, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which bloom in spring. Its seeds are enclosed in flat, woody pods that split open when mature.

Acacia nuceiana is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
