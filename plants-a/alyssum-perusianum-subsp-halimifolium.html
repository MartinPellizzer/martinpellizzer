Alyssum perusianum subsp. halimifolium

Alyssum perusianum subsp.

halimifolium, commonly known as the Mediterranean alyssum, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has slender, hairy stems and compact, evergreen foliage that remains dense throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice in Mediterranean-style landscaping.
