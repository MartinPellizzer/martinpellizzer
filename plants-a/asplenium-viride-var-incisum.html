Asplenium viride var. incisum

Asplenium viride var. incisum, commonly known as the green spleenwort, is a fern species native to various regions of the world.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage with a dark green hue and a serrated or incised leaf margin, which gives it its botanical name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rock crevices or on tree trunks. Its fronds are usually leathery and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings for its hardiness and adaptability.
