Aegochloa intertexta

Aegochloa intertexta, commonly known as the silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage, which results from dense pubescence covering the leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and swamps, where it forms dense stands. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in panicles, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Aegochloa intertexta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.

Morphology

The flowers are small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to white florets, blooming during the dry season, and are wind-pollinated.
