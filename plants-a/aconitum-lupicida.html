Aconitum lupicida

Aconitum lupicida, commonly known as the wolf poison or wolf'sbane, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, palmately divided leaves. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are arranged in a raceme. It contains potent alkaloids, making it highly toxic to both humans and animals.

Native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.
