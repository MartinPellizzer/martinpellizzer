Ammannia pubescens

Ammannia pubescens, commonly known as the redroot dayflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. Its flowers are borne in clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Ammannia pubescens is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and along stream banks.
