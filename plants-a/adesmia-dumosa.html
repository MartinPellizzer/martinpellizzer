Adesmia dumosa

Adesmia dumosa, commonly known as the hairy adesmia, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its hairy stems and leaves help reduce water loss, making it resilient in harsh environments.

Adesmia dumosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
