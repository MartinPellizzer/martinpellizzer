Aconitum wulingense

Aconitum wulingense, commonly known as the Wuling monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments, and its roots are tuberous.

Aconitum wulingense contains toxic alkaloids, making it a potentially dangerous plant to handle.
