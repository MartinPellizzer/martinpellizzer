Acanthopanax gracilistylus var. villosulus

Acanthopanax gracilistylus var. villosulus, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed Chinese angel's trumpet, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
