Acalypha hederacea var. genuina

Acalypha hederacea var. genuina, commonly known as velvetleaf or Mexican nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically green with a distinctive reddish or purple underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which are often hidden among the foliage. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its rapid growth and invasive potential make it a common weed in agricultural and disturbed areas.
