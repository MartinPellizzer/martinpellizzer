Astragalus pterocephalus

Astragalus pterocephalus, commonly known as the winged milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and compound leaves with small, oval leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are tuberous and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Astragalus pterocephalus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
