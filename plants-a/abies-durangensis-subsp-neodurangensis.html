Abies durangensis subsp. neodurangensis

Abies durangensis subsp.

neodurangensis, commonly known as the Durango fir, is a rare and endemic conifer native to the high-altitude regions of Mexico. It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and conical shape, typical of the fir genus.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soils, often found in cloud forests. Its cones are small and erect, containing winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, this subspecies is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
