Arctostaphylos bolensis

Arctostaphylos bolensis, commonly known as Bolens' manzanita, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, branched structure and leathery, oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers and mild, wet winters.

Arctostaphylos bolensis is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.
