Astragalus magnibracteus

Astragalus magnibracteus, commonly known as the large bract milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
