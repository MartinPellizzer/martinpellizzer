Astragalus rasmontii

Astragalus rasmontii, commonly known as Rasmont's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus rasmontii is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
