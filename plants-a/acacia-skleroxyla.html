Acacia skleroxyla

Acacia skleroxyla, commonly known as the African wattle, is a deciduous tree native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow floral spikes. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its deep root system. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

The tree is also valued for its hard wood and historical use in traditional medicine.
