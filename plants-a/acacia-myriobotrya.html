Acacia myriobotrya

Acacia myriobotrya, commonly known as the red acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant features feathery, bipinnate leaves and produces yellow or orange flowers arranged in spherical clusters. Its bark is thick and fissured, often exuding a milky sap when damaged.

Acacia myriobotrya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
