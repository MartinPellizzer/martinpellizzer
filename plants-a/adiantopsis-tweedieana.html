Adiantopsis tweedieana

Adiantopsis tweedieana, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a small, delicate fern native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that give it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

Adiantopsis tweedieana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
