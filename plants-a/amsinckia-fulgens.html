Amsinckia fulgens

Amsinckia fulgens, commonly known as the golden globe globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, ribbed stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Amsinckia fulgens is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
