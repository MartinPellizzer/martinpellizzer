Arundarbor brandisii

Arundarbor brandisii, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery inflorescences that produce small, golden-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Arundarbor brandisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
