Artanthe cornifolia

Artanthe cornifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf artanthe, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Artanthe cornifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
