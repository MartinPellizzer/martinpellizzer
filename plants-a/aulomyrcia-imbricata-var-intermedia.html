Aulomyrcia imbricata var. intermedia

Aulomyrcia imbricata var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate aulomyrcia, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces oblong, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
