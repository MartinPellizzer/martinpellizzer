Acacia nernstii

Acacia nernstii, commonly known as the Natal acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and its compound leaves, which are typically feathery and arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which bloom during the rainy season. Acacia nernstii is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been used traditionally for various purposes.
