Anagallis parviflora var. micrantha

Anagallis parviflora var. micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered pink willow, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces delicate, pink to purple flowers with five petals. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are oval-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often trailing.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and flies.
