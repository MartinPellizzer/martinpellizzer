Andropogon lacunosus

Andropogon lacunosus, commonly known as giant bluestem, is a tall perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems and long, narrow leaves with a bluish-green hue. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairie and woodland habitats. Its flowering inflorescence consists of a loose panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Andropogon lacunosus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
