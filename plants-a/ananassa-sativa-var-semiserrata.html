Ananassa sativa var. semiserrata

Ananassa sativa var. semiserrata, commonly known as the serrated pineapple, is a cultivar of the pineapple plant characterized by its unique leaf morphology.

It features serrated leaf margins that distinguish it from other pineapple varieties. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and ample sunlight. It produces a spiky, yellowish fruit with a sweet and tangy flavor.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruit.
