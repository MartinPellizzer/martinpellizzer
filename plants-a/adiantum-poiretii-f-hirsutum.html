Adiantum poiretii f. hirsutum

Adiantum poiretii f. hirsutum, commonly known as the Hirsute Maidenhair Fern, is a fern species characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan. The plant features a creeping rhizome with upright, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its fronds are typically bright green and turn bronze in the fall, adding ornamental value.

This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and greenhouses.
