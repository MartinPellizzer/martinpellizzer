Aquilegia elata

Aquilegia elata, commonly known as the Western columbine, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features striking, spurred flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has compound, pinnately arranged leaves with toothed margins, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.
