Astragalus neomobayenii

Astragalus neomobayenii, commonly known as the Neomobayenii milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in dry, harsh environments. Astragalus neomobayenii is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its ecological role includes nitrogen fixation, which enriches the soil in its natural habitat.
