Acinodendron seriale

Acinodendron seriale, commonly known as the silverleaf acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in arid gardens.
