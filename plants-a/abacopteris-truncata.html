Abacopteris truncata

Abacopteris truncata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed marsh fern, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh habitats in eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and trifoliate leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds. It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in swampy or seasonally flooded areas.

Abacopteris truncata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
