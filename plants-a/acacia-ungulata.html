Acacia ungulata

Acacia ungulata, commonly known as the clawed acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in globular inflorescences, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, feather-like segments.

Acacia ungulata is also valued for its hard, durable wood and has been used traditionally for various purposes.
