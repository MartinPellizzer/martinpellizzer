Abies lowiana var. pendula

Abies lowiana var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping fir, is a variant of the lowiana fir native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

This evergreen conifer is characterized by its pendulous branches that droop gracefully, giving it a distinctive, cascading appearance. Its needles are dark green and densely packed, providing a lush, textured foliage. The tree typically grows to a moderate height, with a broad, rounded crown.

It thrives in well-drained soils and cooler climates, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
