Apocynum indicum

Apocynum indicum, commonly known as Indian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes many milkweeds and related species. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. It produces a milky sap that contains cardiac glycosides, which can be toxic to animals and humans.

The plant is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
