Agave jimenoi

Agave jimenoi, commonly known as the Blue Agave, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that can reach several meters in height, bearing small, white flowers. Agave jimenoi is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

It is also noted for its use in the production of tequila, although it is not the primary species used for that purpose.
