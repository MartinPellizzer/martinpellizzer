Astephanus cordatus

Astephanus cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette growth habit and showy yellow flowers surrounded by white ray florets. The leaves are broadly ovate with a heart-shaped base, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
