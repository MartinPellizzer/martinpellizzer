Avena sterilis var. algeriensis

Avena sterilis var. algeriensis, commonly known as Algerian wild oat, is a grass species native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and disturbed areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a hairy texture.

It is a perennial species that is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.
