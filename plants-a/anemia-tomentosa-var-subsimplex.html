Anemia tomentosa var. subsimplex

Anemia tomentosa var. subsimplex, commonly known as the silky anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a basal rosette of soft, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its stems are upright and often branched, supporting the flowering heads.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
