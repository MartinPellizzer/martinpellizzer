Aster eradiatus

Aster eradiatus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy purple to pink petals surrounding a central yellow disk, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds. Aster eradiatus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
