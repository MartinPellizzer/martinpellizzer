Aristida sericea

Aristida sericea, commonly known as silky grass or silky needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fine, silky hairs that cover its leaves and inflorescence. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with red or purple hues.

Aristida sericea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native plant landscapes.
