Astragalus daryouchianus

Astragalus daryouchianus, commonly known as Daryush's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is well-developed, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus daryouchianus is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
