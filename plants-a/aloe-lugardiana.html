Aloe lugardiana

Aloe lugardiana, commonly known as the Knysna aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a red or purple tinge on the edges. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Aloe lugardiana is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves to survive drought conditions.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
