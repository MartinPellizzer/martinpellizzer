Azalea × todmanii

Azalea × todmanii, commonly known as the Todman azalea, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron indicum and Rhododendron simsii.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is typically evergreen, featuring leathery, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in temperate regions for both its beauty and hardiness.
