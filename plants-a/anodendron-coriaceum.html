Anodendron coriaceum

Anodendron coriaceum, commonly known as the roughleaf acanthus, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces showy, spiny flower clusters that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in gardens across its native range.
