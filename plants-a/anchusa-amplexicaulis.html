Anchusa amplexicaulis

Anchusa amplexicaulis, commonly known as the purple anchusa, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that clasp the stem, giving it its species name *amplexicaulis*. The plant produces tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of bright blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Anchusa amplexicaulis is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and drought tolerance.
