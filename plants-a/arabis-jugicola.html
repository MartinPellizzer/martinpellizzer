Arabis jugicola

Arabis jugicola, commonly known as the rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in clusters, often resembling those of other members of its family.

Arabis jugicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
