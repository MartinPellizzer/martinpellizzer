Achimenes longiflora var. rosea

Achimenes longiflora var. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered achimenes, is a tropical perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a white or yellow throat. The plant produces tubular, spurred flowers that are typically borne in clusters on erect stems. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are dark green in color.

Achimenes longiflora var. rosea thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms.
