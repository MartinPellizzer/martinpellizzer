Acaena trifida var. mollissima

Acaena trifida var. mollissima, commonly known as the soft burr or velvet bramble, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces distinctive, spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are typically alternate, with a soft, velvety texture, and the stems are covered in fine, hooked hairs.

This species is often found in coastal and subalpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
