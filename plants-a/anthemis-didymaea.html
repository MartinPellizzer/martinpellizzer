Anthemis didymaea

Anthemis didymaea, commonly known as the double everlasting or double daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellow flowers with white ray florets, creating a striking visual effect. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its long-lasting blooms.
