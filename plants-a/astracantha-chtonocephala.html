Astracantha chtonocephala

Astracantha chtonocephala, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a striking, spiky appearance with elongated, tubular leaves that resemble ghostly fingers. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a delicate contrast to its rugged structure. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions with minimal water requirements.

Its botanical characteristics make it a fascinating subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
