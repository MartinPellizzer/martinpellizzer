Aeollanthus buchnerianus

Aeollanthus buchnerianus, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments.

Aeollanthus buchnerianus is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
