Asperula glabra

Asperula glabra, commonly known as the smooth asperula, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, dark green, leathery leaves that are smooth to the touch, as indicated by its species name "glabra.". The plant produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped white or pale pink flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Asperula glabra is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate moist conditions.
