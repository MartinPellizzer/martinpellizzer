Artocarpus anisophyllus

Artocarpus anisophyllus, commonly known as the Java or Malabar breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves that are alternately arranged and have a distinctive two-ranked venation pattern. The plant produces a unique fruit with a rough, woody exterior and a soft, edible pulp containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental foliage and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
