Aslia libanotica

Aslia libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanese asphodel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and tall, spiky flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers, which are usually yellow or orange, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Aslia libanotica is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
