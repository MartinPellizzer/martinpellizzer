Alchemilla acuminatidens

Alchemilla acuminatidens, commonly known as the sharp-pointed lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Alchemilla acuminatidens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
