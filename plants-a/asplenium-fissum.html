Asplenium fissum

Asplenium fissum, commonly known as the crested water fern, is a species of fern native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, serrated fronds that often have a wavy or zigzag appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically near streams or in humid forests. Its rhizomes are creeping and help it spread along the ground.

Asplenium fissum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
