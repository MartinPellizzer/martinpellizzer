Aquilegia japonica f. konoi

Aquilegia japonica f. konoi, commonly known as the Konoi Japanese columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It features showy, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple, with five petals and two elongated nectar spurs. The plant has basal leaves that are palmately divided into five to seven lobes, often with a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental value in gardens and its historical significance in traditional Japanese horticulture.
