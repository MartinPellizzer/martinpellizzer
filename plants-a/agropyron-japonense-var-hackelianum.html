Agropyron japonense var. hackelianum

Agropyron japonense var. hackelianum, commonly known as Japanese needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
