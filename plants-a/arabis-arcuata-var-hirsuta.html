Arabis arcuata var. hirsuta

Arabis arcuata var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cool, moist environments. The plant features basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in clusters, blooming in early spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
