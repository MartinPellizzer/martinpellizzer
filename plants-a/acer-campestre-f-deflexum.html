Acer campestre f. deflexum

Acer campestre f. deflexum, commonly known as the creeping maple, is a variant of the field maple characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit.

It features small, ovate leaves with a doubly serrated margin, typically turning yellow in the autumn. The plant produces samaras, or winged seeds, which aid in wind dispersal. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to gardens and ecological landscapes.

Native Distribution

Acer campestre f. deflexum is primarily native to southern and central Europe, including countries such as France, Spain, Italy, and parts of the Balkans.

This subspecies thrives in temperate climates and is commonly found in woodlands, hedgerows, and along stream banks. It prefers well-drained soils and is often part of the undergrowth in mixed deciduous forests. The plant is known for its distinctive, often twisted growth habit and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

Its distribution is closely tied to the natural habitats of the regions where it has historically grown.
