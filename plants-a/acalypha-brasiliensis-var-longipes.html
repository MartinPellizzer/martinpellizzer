Acalypha brasiliensis var. longipes

Acalypha brasiliensis var. longipes, commonly known as the Brazilian spurflower, is a shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spade-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences called cyathia. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
