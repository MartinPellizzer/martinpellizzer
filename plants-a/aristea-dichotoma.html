Aristea dichotoma

Aristea dichotoma, commonly known as the two-branched aristea, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped growth habit and its ability to store water in its fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or arid landscapes.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping.
