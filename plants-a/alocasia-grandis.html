Alocasia grandis

Alocasia grandis, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1.5 meters in length, featuring a deep green color with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by spiny, red berries. Alocasia grandis thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, fertile soil.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its dramatic foliage.
