Asperula aspera var. latifolia

Asperula aspera var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved woodruff, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters at the ends of stems. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside. This variety is known for its dense growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.
