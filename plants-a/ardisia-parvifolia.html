Ardisia parvifolia

Ardisia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are a striking feature of the species. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a leathery texture.

Ardisia parvifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to tropical and subtropical climates.
