Adelinia grandis

Adelinia grandis, commonly known as the giant adelinia, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, trifoliate leaves and showy, pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Adelinia grandis is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop due to its nutritional value.
