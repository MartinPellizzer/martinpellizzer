Aspidium rhizophyllum var. plaschnickianum

Aspidium rhizophyllum var. plaschnickianum, commonly known as the Plaschnick's water fern, is a variant of the common water fern found in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically green and often have a bluish tinge. The plant thrives in shallow, still water and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many ferns.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to aquatic conditions and its role in maintaining wetland ecosystems.
