Anemia tomentosa

Anemia tomentosa, commonly known as the silky anemone, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its botanical features include a short, hairy stem and palmate leaves with a velvety texture.

Anemia tomentosa is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
