Acacia sphacelata var. retrorsa

Acacia sphacelata var. retrorsa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are narrow and opposite. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system for water uptake.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in ecological restoration projects.
