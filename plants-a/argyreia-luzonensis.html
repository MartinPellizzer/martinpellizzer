Argyreia luzonensis

Argyreia luzonensis, commonly known as the Philippine morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The botanical name reflects its geographical origin and its association with the genus Argyreia, which includes other species of morning glories.
