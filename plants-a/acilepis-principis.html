Acilepis principis

Acilepis principis, commonly known as the royal acilepis, is a rare and unique plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help to reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, adding a delicate beauty to its rugged habitat. Its growth is slow and it thrives in cool, shaded conditions, often found in rocky crevices.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Acilepis principis is considered a species of conservation concern.
