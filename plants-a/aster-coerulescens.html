Aster coerulescens

Aster coerulescens, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, sky-blue to white flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Aster coerulescens is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators.
