Allophylus amazonicus var. angustifolia

Allophylus amazonicus var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sycamore, is a species native to the rainforests of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree with dark green, elliptical leaves that have a distinctive serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Its leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
