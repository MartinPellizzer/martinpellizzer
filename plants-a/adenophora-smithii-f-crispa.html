Adenophora smithii f. crispa

Adenophora smithii f. crispa, commonly known as the crinkled bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale blue to lavender flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often at high elevations. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to well-drained soils.
