Adenophora trachelioides var. cordatifolia

Adenophora trachelioides var. cordatifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often with a distinctive purple throat. The plant has opposite, cordate (heart-shaped) leaves that are typically hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
