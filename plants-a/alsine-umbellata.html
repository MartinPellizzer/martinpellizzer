Alsine umbellata

Alsine umbellata, commonly known as the common sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically pale blue or violet, with five petals and a distinctive central stamen. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
