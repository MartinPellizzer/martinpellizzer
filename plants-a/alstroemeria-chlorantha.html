Alstroemeria chlorantha

Alstroemeria chlorantha, commonly known as the green alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or gravelly soils. Its flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Alstroemeria chlorantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
