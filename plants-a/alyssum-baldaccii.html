Alyssum baldaccii

Alyssum baldaccii, commonly known as the baldaccii alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbages. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Alyssum baldaccii typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
