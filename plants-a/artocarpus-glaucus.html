Artocarpus glaucus

Artocarpus glaucus, commonly known as the white breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, smooth, pale green to white fruit, which is covered in a thick, fibrous husk. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has compound leaves with serrated edges. Its fruit is typically harvested when fully mature, and it is valued for its starchy, edible pulp.

The tree is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its shade and aesthetic appeal.
