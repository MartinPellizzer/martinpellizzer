Alopecurus navicularis

Alopecurus navicularis, commonly known as narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, blue-green flowers appear in early summer and are clustered in panicles.

Alopecurus navicularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
