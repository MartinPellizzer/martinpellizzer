Acetosella fasciculata

Acetosella fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb belonging to the Oxalidaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves with a reddish hue and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves contain oxalic acid, giving them a tangy, acidic taste.

The plant's structure and chemical composition make it a notable species in botanical studies and traditional herbal use.
