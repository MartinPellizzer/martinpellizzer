Acacia stereophylla var. stereophylla

Acacia stereophylla var. stereophylla, commonly known as the white wattle, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit and its distinctive white to pale yellow flowers that appear in dense, cylindrical clusters. The leaves are compound, consisting of multiple linear leaflets, and the plant produces small, woody pods that contain flat, edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
