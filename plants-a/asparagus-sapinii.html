Asparagus sapinii

Asparagus sapinii, commonly known as the mountain asparagus, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by edible, needle-like berries. Asparagus sapinii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it a unique and hardy species adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
