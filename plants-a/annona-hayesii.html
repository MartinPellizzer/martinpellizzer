Annona hayesii

Annona hayesii, commonly known as the Mexican custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that produces a distinctive, fragrant fruit with a smooth, waxy skin. The fruit is typically yellow or green when ripe and has a creamy, custard-like pulp that is rich in nutrients. The plant belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, white or pale yellow flowers.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
