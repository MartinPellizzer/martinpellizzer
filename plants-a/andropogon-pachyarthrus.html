Andropogon pachyarthrus

Andropogon pachyarthrus, commonly known as the thick-armed grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its robust, upright growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tint. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-bearing flowers, which are usually greenish or pale yellow in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in open woodlands and prairies.

Its dense growth habit makes it useful for erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
