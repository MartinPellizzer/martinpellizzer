Anthemis biebersteiniana f. rudolphiana

Anthemis biebersteiniana f. rudolphiana, commonly known as the Rudolph's tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. The plant is native to Central Asia and thrives in dry, rocky habitats. It produces a distinctive, aromatic essential oil from its foliage, which has been used in traditional medicine.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical pharmacological significance.
