Askidiosperma paniculatum

Askidiosperma paniculatum, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or *pau-rosa*, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and is often found in dense rainforests. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in panicles. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
