Anemone obtusiloba var. potentilloides

Anemone obtusiloba var. potentilloides, commonly known as the western anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers with five to nine petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a short, stout stem that grows from a rhizomatous root system, and its leaves are palmately divided with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
