Aconitum apetalum

Aconitum apetalum, commonly known as the naked lady's tresses, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its lack of petals, giving it its common name. The plant produces dense clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically basal, forming a rosette and are deeply lobed.

Aconitum apetalum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
