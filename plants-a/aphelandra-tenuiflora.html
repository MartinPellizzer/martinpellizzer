Aphelandra tenuiflora

Aphelandra tenuiflora, commonly known as the narrow-flowered torch ginger, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and striking, elongated inflorescences that bear clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pendulous, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which are often accompanied by showy bracts. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Aphelandra tenuiflora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its ornate foliage and floral display.
