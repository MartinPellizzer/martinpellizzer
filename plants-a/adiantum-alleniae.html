Adiantum alleniae

Adiantum alleniae, commonly known as the Allen's maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that resemble those of other maidenhair ferns. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually bright green and have a distinctive, elegant appearance.

Adiantum alleniae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
