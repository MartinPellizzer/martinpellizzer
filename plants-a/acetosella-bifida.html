Acetosella bifida

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are deeply lobed and often have a reddish or purple hue. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Acetosella bifida is also noted for its edible leaves, which have a tangy, sour flavor similar to that of sorrel.
