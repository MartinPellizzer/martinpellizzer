Acacia subflexuosa subsp. capillata

Acacia subflexuosa subsp.

capillata, commonly known as the silky wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems and delicate, feathery leaves that give it a soft, silky appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, which bloom from late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
