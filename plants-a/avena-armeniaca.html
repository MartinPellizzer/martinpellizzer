Avena armeniaca

Avena armeniaca, commonly known as Armenian oatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Avena armeniaca is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in difficult landscapes.
