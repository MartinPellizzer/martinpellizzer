Anthericum validum

Anthericum validum, commonly known as the common buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a rosette of lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming from April to June. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Anthericum validum is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
