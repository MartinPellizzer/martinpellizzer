Avena sempervirens subsp. parlatorei

Avena sempervirens subsp.

parlatorei, commonly known as the Parlatore's oat, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact inflorescence.

The plant produces small, spikelet-like flowers that are usually green or brown in color. It is well adapted to dry and sunny environments, making it a hardy species in arid climates.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
