Anguria insolita

Anguria insolita, commonly known as the rare watermelon, is a unique member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its unusual, elongated fruit that can grow up to two meters in length. The plant thrives in warm, arid climates and requires a long growing season with ample sunlight. Its flowers are typically yellow and unisexual, with separate male and female blooms.

The fruit has a thick rind and a sweet, juicy interior, making it a prized crop in certain regions.
