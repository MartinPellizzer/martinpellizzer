Ailanthus peekelii

Ailanthus peekelii, commonly known as the Chinese tree of heaven, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged with 10 to 30 small leaflets. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by winged fruits that resemble samaras. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is adaptable to different climates.

Its rapid growth rate and ability to tolerate urban environments make it a common sight in cities and along roadsides.
