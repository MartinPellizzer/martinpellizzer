Adesmia serrana

Adesmia serrana, commonly known as the serrated adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically features small, compound leaves with serrated edges, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of white or pale purple.

Adesmia serrana is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
