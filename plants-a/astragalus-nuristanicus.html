Astragalus nuristanicus

Astragalus nuristanicus, commonly known as the Nuristan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant is typically found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Astragalus nuristanicus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it ecologically significant in nutrient-poor environments.
