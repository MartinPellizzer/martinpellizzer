Argentina microphylla

Argentina microphylla, commonly known as the Argentine rosewood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a slow growth rate and is known for its hard, aromatic wood, which is highly valued in traditional craftsmanship. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution and slow regeneration, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native habitat.
