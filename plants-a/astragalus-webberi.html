Astragalus webberi

Astragalus webberi, commonly known as Webber's milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces pods that contain small, round seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus webberi is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert scrub ecosystems.
