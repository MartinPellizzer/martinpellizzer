Ardisia curtipes

Ardisia curtipes, commonly known as the dwarf beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. These fruits are an important food source for various bird species.

Ardisia curtipes is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and seasonal fruit display.
