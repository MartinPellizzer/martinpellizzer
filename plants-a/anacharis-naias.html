Anacharis naias

Anacharis naias, commonly known as waterweed, is a species of aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, and it thrives in freshwater environments. Anacharis naias is often found in slow-moving or still waters such as ponds and lakes.

Its rapid growth rate makes it both a popular aquarium plant and a potential invasive species in non-native habitats.
