Agapetes neriifolia

Agapetes neriifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf agapete, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage with a distinctive silvery underside on the leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which appear in clusters during the spring. Agapetes neriifolia thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found at high altitudes.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable plant in its native habitats.
