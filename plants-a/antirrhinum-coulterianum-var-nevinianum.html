Antirrhinum coulterianum var. nevinianum

Antirrhinum coulterianum var. nevinianum, commonly known as the Nevin's snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, two-lipped flowers that typically range in color from red to orange. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces seed capsules that split open to release its seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks.
