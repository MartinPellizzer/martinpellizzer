Arctostaphylos uva-ursi var. coactilis

Arctostaphylos uva-ursi var. coactilis, commonly known as bearberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to North America.

It features small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of red berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
