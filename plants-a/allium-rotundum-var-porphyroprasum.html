Allium rotundum var. porphyroprasum

Allium rotundum var. porphyroprasum, commonly known as the purple leek, is a botanical variant of the common leek.

It is characterized by its bulbous base and elongated, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible leaves and bulbs.

This variety is notable for its vibrant purple coloration in its foliage and stems.
