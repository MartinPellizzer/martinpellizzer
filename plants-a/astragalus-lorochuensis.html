Astragalus lorochuensis

Astragalus lorochuensis, commonly known as the Tibetan milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant is adapted to harsh, arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
