Albuca concordiana

Albuca concordiana, commonly known as the Zulu lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. The plant is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its ornamental value comes from its elegant foliage and striking blooms.

Albuca concordiana is also known for its ability to thrive in dry conditions, making it a low-maintenance choice for gardeners.
