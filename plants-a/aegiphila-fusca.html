Aegiphila fusca

Aegiphila fusca, commonly known as the hairy leaf shrub, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky areas.

Aegiphila fusca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
