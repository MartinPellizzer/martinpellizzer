Adenophyllum squamosum

Adenophyllum squamosum, commonly known as the scaly adenophyllum, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in coastal or mountainous regions. Its leaves are often covered in a dense layer of fine, scale-like hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Adenophyllum squamosum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers.
