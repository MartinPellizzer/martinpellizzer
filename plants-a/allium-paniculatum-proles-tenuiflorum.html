Allium paniculatum proles tenuiflorum

Allium paniculatum proles tenuiflorum, commonly known as the slender-flowered garlic chive, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in panicles. The plant is characterized by its aromatic bulbs, which are similar to garlic in both appearance and scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses, though it is less commonly cultivated than other Allium species.
