Aster intybaceus

Aster intybaceus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine, silky hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy surface. Its most distinctive feature is its clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.

Aster intybaceus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
