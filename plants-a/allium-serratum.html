Allium serratum

Allium serratum, commonly known as the serrated leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives. The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and serrated, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically purple or pink, forming a dense umbel at the top of the stem.

Allium serratum is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary potential.
