Avenastrum fedtschenkoi

Avenastrum fedtschenkoi, commonly known as the Siberian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the cold climates of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Avenastrum fedtschenkoi is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop in its native regions.
