Ayenia obliqua

Ayenia obliqua, commonly known as the red sandalwood or *Pterocarpus obliquus*, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces hard, dark wood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. Its fruit is a pod containing flat, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Ayenia obliqua plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
