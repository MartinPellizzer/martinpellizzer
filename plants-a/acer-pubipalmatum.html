Acer pubipalmatum

Acer pubipalmatum, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens. Its small, inconspicuous flowers appear in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The tree typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 feet, with a rounded, compact form.
