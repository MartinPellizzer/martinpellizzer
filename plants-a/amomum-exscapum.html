Amomum exscapum

Amomum exscapum, commonly known as the Chinese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and cardamom. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces fragrant, tubular flowers in clusters. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a distinct, spicy aroma.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.
