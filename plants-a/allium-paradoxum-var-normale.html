Allium paradoxum var. normale

Allium paradoxum var. normale, commonly known as the common garlic.

It is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. The plant features a bulb composed of several cloves, with a strong garlic scent. Its leaves are grass-like and often used in culinary applications.

The flowers are typically pale pink to white, forming a spherical cluster at the top of the stem.
