Astrochlaena malvacea

Astrochlaena malvacea, commonly known as the African marshmallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton plants. The plant is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. It produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

Astrochlaena malvacea is often found in wetlands and marshy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soil.
