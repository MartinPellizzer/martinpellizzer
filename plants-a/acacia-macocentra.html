Acacia macocentra

Acacia macocentra, commonly known as the red acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stem and bipinnate leaves that are feathery in appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are followed by flat, linear pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
