Astragalus remanens

Astragalus remanens, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in drought conditions. It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.

Astragalus remanens is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
