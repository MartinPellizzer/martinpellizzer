Acacia glaucocarpa

Acacia glaucocarpa, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grey-green phyllodes and yellow flower spikes that appear in spring. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system for water uptake. It produces small, woody pods that contain flattened seeds.

The species is often used in reforestation projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soils.
