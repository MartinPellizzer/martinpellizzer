Acacia pedicellata

Acacia pedicellata, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod-bearing fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular inflorescences, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, narrow leaflets.

Acacia pedicellata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry regions.
