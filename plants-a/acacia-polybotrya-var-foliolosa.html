Acacia polybotrya var. foliolosa

Acacia polybotrya var. foliolosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, narrow leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which appear in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
