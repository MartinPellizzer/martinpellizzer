Alyssum alpestre var. saxicola

Alyssum alpestre var. saxicola, commonly known as alpine alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage, often found in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

Alyssum alpestre var. saxicola is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
