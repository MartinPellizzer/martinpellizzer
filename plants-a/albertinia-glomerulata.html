Albertinia glomerulata

Albertinia glomerulata, commonly known as the clustered flax, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, lance-shaped leaves arranged in whorls. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
