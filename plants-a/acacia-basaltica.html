Acacia basaltica

Acacia basaltica, commonly known as the basalt acacia, is a unique shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume pods and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant is adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils, often found in rocky or coastal areas. Its leaves are small and feathery, and it produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators.

The wood of Acacia basaltica is dense and durable, making it valuable for local crafts and construction.
