Aspilia scabrida

Aspilia scabrida, commonly known as the rough aspilia, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flower heads. The plant features narrow, hairy leaves that are often rough to the touch, giving it its common name. It typically grows in grasslands and open areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Aspilia scabrida is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive blooms.
