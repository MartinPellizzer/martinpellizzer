Aconitum okuyamae

Aconitum okuyamae, commonly known as Okuyama's aconite, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are palmately compound, with finely divided lobes, and its stems are usually unbranched.

Aconitum okuyamae contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
