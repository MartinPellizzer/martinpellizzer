Amyema melaleucae

Amyema melaleucae, commonly known as the honeywood, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalypts and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and small, aromatic leaves. It produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

The honeywood is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
