Aureolaria pectinata var. ozarkensis

Aureolaria pectinata var. ozarkensis, commonly known as the Ozark sand verbena, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.

This variety is considered rare and is often found in specific habitats such as open woodlands and rocky outcrops.
