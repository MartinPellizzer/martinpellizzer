Artanthe brongniartii

Artanthe brongniartii, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, silver-gray leaves and produces showy yellow-orange flowers with a central disk. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Artanthe brongniartii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
