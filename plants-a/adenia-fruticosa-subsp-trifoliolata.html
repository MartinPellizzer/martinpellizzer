Adenia fruticosa subsp. trifoliolata

Adenia fruticosa subsp.

trifoliolata, commonly known as the three-leaf adenia, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a fan-like pattern.

The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. Its stems are often woody and can have a somewhat prickly texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
