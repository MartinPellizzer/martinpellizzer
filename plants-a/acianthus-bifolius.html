Acianthus bifolius

Acianthus bifolius, commonly known as the two-leaf orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and two broad, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and green, often arranged in a dense spike. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat preferences, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
