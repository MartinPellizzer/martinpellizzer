Aster adfinis

Aster adfinis, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in shades of purple and white.

Aster adfinis plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
