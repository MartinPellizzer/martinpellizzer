Amorphophallus selebicus

Amorphophallus selebicus, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, showy white flowers that bloom at night, attracting pollinators such as moths. The plant has broad, floating leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing shade and habitat for aquatic life. Its rhizome system allows it to spread rapidly in still water, making it a common invasive species in some regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence structure.
