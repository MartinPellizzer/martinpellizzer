Artemisia campestris subsp. pycnocephala

Artemisia campestris subsp.

pycnocephala, commonly known as woolly tarragon, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, gray-green foliage and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed areas. It has aromatic leaves that emit a strong, camphor-like scent.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
