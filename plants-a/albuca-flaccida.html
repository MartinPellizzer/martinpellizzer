Albuca flaccida

Albuca flaccida, commonly known as the Flaccid Albuca, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features linear, fleshy leaves that are often bluish-green in color, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Albuca flaccida is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
