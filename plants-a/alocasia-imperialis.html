Alocasia imperialis

Alocasia imperialis, commonly known as the giant elephant ear, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length, with a deep green color and a waxy texture. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes other ornamental plants like peace lilies and calla lilies. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Alocasia imperialis is often cultivated for its dramatic foliage and is a popular choice in tropical gardens and indoor spaces.
