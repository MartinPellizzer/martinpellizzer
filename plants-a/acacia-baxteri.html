Acacia baxteri

Acacia baxteri, commonly known as the Baxter's wattle, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are arranged in dense spikes. Acacia baxteri is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions with its deep root system and ability to fix nitrogen.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
