Asystasia tanzaniensis

Asystasia tanzaniensis, commonly known as Tanzanian asystasia, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or forested areas.

Asystasia tanzaniensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
