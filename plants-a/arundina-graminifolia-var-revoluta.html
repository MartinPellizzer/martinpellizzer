Arundina graminifolia var. revoluta

Arundina graminifolia var. revoluta, commonly known as the revolute bamboo, is a variant of the bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, green culms that grow in dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rolled or revolute margin, giving the plant its specific epithet. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for landscaping purposes.
