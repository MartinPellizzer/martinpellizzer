Anemia rosulata

Anemia rosulata, commonly known as the rose of Sharon, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, rose-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the genus Hibiscus and is part of the Malvaceae family.
