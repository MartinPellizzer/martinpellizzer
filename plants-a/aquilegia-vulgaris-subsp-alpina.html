Aquilegia vulgaris subsp. alpina

Aquilegia vulgaris subsp.

alpina, commonly known as the Alpine columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features showy, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple, with five petals and distinctive nectar-rich spurs.

The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often covered in a fine layer of down. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
