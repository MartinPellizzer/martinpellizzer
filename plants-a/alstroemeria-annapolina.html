Alstroemeria annapolina

Alstroemeria annapolina, commonly known as the "Peru lily," is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Peru and Chile.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has strap-like, grassy foliage.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for floral arrangements and garden use.
