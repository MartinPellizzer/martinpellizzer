Actinidia arguta var. cordifolia

Actinidia arguta var. cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf arrowwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically 5 to 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries that turn red in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
