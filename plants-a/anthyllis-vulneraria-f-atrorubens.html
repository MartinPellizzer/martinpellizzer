Anthyllis vulneraria f. atrorubens

Anthyllis vulneraria f. atrorubens, commonly known as the dark red broom, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers clustered in spike-like inflorescences. The plant is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a deep root system that helps it thrive in poor soil conditions.

This variety is distinguished by its dark red or purplish stems and leaves, giving it a striking appearance in its native habitat.
