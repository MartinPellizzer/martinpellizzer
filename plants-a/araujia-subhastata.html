Araujia subhastata

Araujia subhastata, commonly known as the moonflower vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a strong fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces fruits that are covered in hooked spines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

However, its seeds can be toxic to livestock and humans if ingested.
