Avena panicea var. neglecta

Avena panicea var. neglecta, commonly known as goat grass or wild oat, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural areas, where it can become a weed. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the seeds are enclosed in small, spikelet-like structures.

This variety is closely related to other wild oat species and is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
