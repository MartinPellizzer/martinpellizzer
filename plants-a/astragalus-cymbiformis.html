Astragalus cymbiformis

Astragalus cymbiformis, commonly known as the curlleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, yellow to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Astragalus cymbiformis is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential medicinal properties.
