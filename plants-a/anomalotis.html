Anomalotis

Anomalotis, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

These plants are characterized by their velvety, waxy leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. Anomalotis species typically grow as compact, bushy plants with a rosette formation of foliage. They thrive in well-drained soil and require bright, indirect light to flourish.

The genus is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and ease of care.
