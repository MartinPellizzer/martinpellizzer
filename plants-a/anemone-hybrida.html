Anemone hybrida

Anemone hybrida, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, fluffy flowers that range in color from white to pink and are typically 3 to 5 inches in diameter. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are alternate and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Anemone hybrida is known for its long blooming period, often lasting from late summer through early autumn.
