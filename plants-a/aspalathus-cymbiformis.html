Aspalathus cymbiformis

Aspalathus cymbiformis, commonly known as the sweet cycad, is a unique plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palm-like foliage and a thick, unbranched stem. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
