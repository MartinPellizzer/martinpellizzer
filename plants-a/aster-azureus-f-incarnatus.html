Aster azureus f. incarnatus

Aster azureus f. incarnatus, commonly known as the pink aster, is a flowering plant characterized by its striking pink to lavender-colored petals.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is native to the eastern United States. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland environments and features a compact, bushy habit with hairy stems.

Its flowers bloom in the late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
