Alyssum spruneri

Alyssum spruneri, commonly known as the spruneri alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has silvery-gray foliage with fine, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum spruneri is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
