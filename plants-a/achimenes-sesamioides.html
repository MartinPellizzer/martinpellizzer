Achimenes sesamioides

Achimenes sesamioides, commonly known as the sesameleaf achimenes, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and orange, typically blooming in summer. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often grown as a bedding plant or in containers.

Its rapid growth and showy flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking colorful, low-maintenance ornamental plants.
