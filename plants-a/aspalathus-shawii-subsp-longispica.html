Aspalathus shawii subsp. longispica

Aspalathus shawii subsp.

longispica, commonly known as the Natal sweet pea, is a South African plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or red in color.

The subspecies is distinguished by its long, spreading stipules and the presence of glandular hairs on its stems and leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions of KwaZulu-Natal.

This plant is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
