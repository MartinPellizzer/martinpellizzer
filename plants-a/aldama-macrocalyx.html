Aldama macrocalyx

Aldama macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered aldama, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forested areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are ovate and slightly hairy, while the stems are woody and branched.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, hard seeds.
