Alchemilla trichosantha

Alchemilla trichosantha, commonly known as the hairy alchemilla, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Alchemilla trichosantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
