Anathallis helmutii

Anathallis helmutii, commonly known as the Helmut's spider orchid, is a rare and delicate epiphytic orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with long, slender inflorescences and intricate floral structures. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments, relying on its aerial roots for nutrient absorption. Its flowers, which are often pale green or white, are adapted for specific pollinators, such as certain species of bees and wasps.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Anathallis helmutii is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
