Aeschynomene filipes

Aeschynomene filipes, commonly known as slender sicklepod, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in poor, sandy environments.

Aeschynomene filipes is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
