Agropyron truncatum subsp. banaticum

Agropyron truncatum subsp.

banaticum, commonly known as the Banat needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to southeastern Europe. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are characteristic of the Poaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and adapted to semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
