Alsophila subaculeata

Alsophila subaculeata, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery fronds that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome system supports its growth, and it produces sporangia on the undersides of its fronds for reproduction.

Alsophila subaculeata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can be found in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
