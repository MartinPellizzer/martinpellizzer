Acacia speciosa

Acacia speciosa, commonly known as the silky oaks, is a deciduous tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are finely divided and give the plant a feathery appearance. The tree produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and furniture.

Acacia speciosa is also used in traditional medicine and is a common ornamental plant in gardens.
