Aristolochia schippii

Aristolochia schippii, commonly known as the Schippi's Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its intricate floral structure that attracts specific pollinators. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

The plant contains aristolochic acids, which are toxic compounds that have been studied for their medicinal and harmful properties.
