Astrocaryum dasychaetum

Astrocaryum dasychaetum, commonly known as the Brazilian wax palm, is a species of palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by dark, waxy fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in moist lowland forests.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
