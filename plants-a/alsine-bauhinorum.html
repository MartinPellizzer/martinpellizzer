Alsine bauhinorum

Alsine bauhinorum, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a low-growing annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that range from pink to purple. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
