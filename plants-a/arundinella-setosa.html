Arundinella setosa

Arundinella setosa, commonly known as the slender bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that are often slightly hairy. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a soft texture.

Arundinella setosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
