Ateleia tomentosa

Ateleia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly soursop, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small evergreen tree characterized by its hairy, grayish leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces a large, spiky fruit that is often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include a woody stem, alternate leaves, and an aggregate fruit type.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
