Astragalus vesiculosus

Astragalus vesiculosus, commonly known as the bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and inflated seed pods. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus vesiculosus is also known for its ecological importance, providing food for various pollinators and wildlife.
