Anthericum deflexum

Anthericum deflexum, commonly known as the flexible adder's mouth, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, hooded spathe that encloses a spadix of small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are arrow-shaped and often have a reddish tinge, while its inflorescence is notable for its unique, curling structure.

Anthericum deflexum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and unusual floral display.
