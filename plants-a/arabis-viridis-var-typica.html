Arabis viridis var. typica

Arabis viridis var. typica, commonly known as the green rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to rocky or gravelly soils in alpine and arctic regions, this plant is adapted to cold and harsh environments. It is often found in mountainous areas of Europe and western Asia.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its resilience to extreme weather conditions.
