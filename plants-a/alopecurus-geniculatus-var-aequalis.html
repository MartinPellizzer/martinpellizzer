Alopecurus geniculatus var. aequalis

Alopecurus geniculatus var. aequalis, commonly known as the equal bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender, often curved stems that form a dense mat. The leaves are narrow and slightly folded, and the inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in tufts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
