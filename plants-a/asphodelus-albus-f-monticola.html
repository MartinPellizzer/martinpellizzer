Asphodelus albus f. monticola

Asphodelus albus f. monticola, commonly known as the white asphodel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asparagaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and tall, erect flowering stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often found in rocky or coastal habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.
