Artocarpus masticatus

Artocarpus masticatus, commonly known as the Indian breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive fruit with a rough, fibrous exterior. The fruit, which is typically yellow when ripe, contains a starchy, edible pulp surrounded by a hard, inedible shell. The plant is valued for its fruit, which is used in traditional cuisines and for its potential medicinal properties.

It thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
