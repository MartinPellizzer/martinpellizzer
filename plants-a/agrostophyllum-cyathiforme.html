Agrostophyllum cyathiforme

Agrostophyllum cyathiforme, commonly known as the cup-shaped sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cup-like inflorescence that resembles a small bowl, which is composed of tightly packed, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found growing in maritime climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its common name, while its flowers attract various pollinators.

Agrostophyllum cyathiforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in coastal gardens.
