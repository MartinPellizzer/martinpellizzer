Apocynum imbricatum

Apocynum imbricatum, commonly known as the Indian Tobacco, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink to red in color, blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and often have a waxy texture, while its stems are covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids and has been used historically for various medicinal purposes.
