Anthurium yurimaguense

Anthurium yurimaguense, commonly known as the Yurimaguense flamingo flower, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon basin, particularly in Ecuador.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, brightly colored spathe that resembles a flamingo's beak. The plant features a thick, fleshy rhizome and produces small, inconspicuous flowers within a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a glossy texture.

Anthurium yurimaguense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouses.
