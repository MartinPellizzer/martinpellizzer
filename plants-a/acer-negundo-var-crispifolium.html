Acer negundo var. crispifolium

Acer negundo var. crispifolium, commonly known as the curly-leaf maple, is a variant of the silver maple tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely toothed, and often curling leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring followed by winged seeds in the fall.

This variety is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
