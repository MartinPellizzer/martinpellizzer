Achillea jankae

Achillea jankae, commonly known as Janka's yarrow, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its foliage is aromatic and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually white or pale yellow.

Janka's yarrow is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
