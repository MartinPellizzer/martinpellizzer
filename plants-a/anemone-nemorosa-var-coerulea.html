Anemone nemorosa var. coerulea

Anemone nemorosa var. coerulea, commonly known as the blue wood anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features a basal rosette of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, five-petaled blue or violet flowers in early spring, typically from April to May. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

The flowers are pollinated by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological importance in woodland ecosystems.
