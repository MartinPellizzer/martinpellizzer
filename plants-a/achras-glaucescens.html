Achras glaucescens

Achras glaucescens, commonly known as the African silver tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, deciduous tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy leaves and yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry climates.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
