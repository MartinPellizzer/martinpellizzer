Aristida schiedeana var. orcuttiana

Aristida schiedeana var. orcuttiana, commonly known as Orcutt's threeawn, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, bristly spikelets that are typically borne in clusters. The plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas. Its flowering period occurs in the summer, producing delicate, pale green to brown inflorescences.

This variant is notable for its ecological adaptability and role in stabilizing arid environments.
