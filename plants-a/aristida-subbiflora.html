Aristida subbiflora

Aristida subbiflora, commonly known as the narrowleaf threeawn, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, which helps reduce water loss.

This species is often found in grasslands and is used in some regions for forage or erosion control.
