Apargia strigosa

Apargia strigosa, commonly known as the roughleaf twayblade, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the orchid family (Orchidaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant is native to North America, particularly in the eastern United States, and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Apargia strigosa has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the understory vegetation in its native habitats.
