Abutilon commutatum

Abutilon commutatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its fibrous roots and hairy foliage make it adaptable to various ecological conditions.
