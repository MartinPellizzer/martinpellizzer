Anthurium angelopolisense

Anthurium angelopolisense, commonly known as the Angelopolis anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage is often a deep green, with some varieties displaying variegation or reddish veins.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in indoor settings due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
