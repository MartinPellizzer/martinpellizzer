Amellus officinalis

Amellus officinalis, commonly known as the medicinal spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia. The plant produces a milky sap that contains various chemical compounds, some of which have medicinal properties. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is found in temperate regions.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
