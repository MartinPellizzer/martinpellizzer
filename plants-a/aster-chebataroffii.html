Aster chebataroffii

Aster chebataroffii, commonly known as the Chebataroff's aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high elevations with cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Aster chebataroffii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
