Adhatoda argyrostachya

Adhatoda argyrostachya, commonly known as the silver stachys or Indian banyan, is a flowering plant native to Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-blue leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or white flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Adhatoda argyrostachya is a perennial shrub that can grow up to several feet tall, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
