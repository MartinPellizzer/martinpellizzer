Acer dasycarpum f. arbusculum

Acer dasycarpum f. arbusculum, commonly known as the dwarf maple, is a unique variant of the genus Acer.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, rounded leaves that are typically a vibrant green during the growing season. The plant produces small, red-orange samaras, which are its distinctive seeds enclosed in a winged structure. This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun exposure.
