Austrocylindropuntia chichensis

Austrocylindropuntia chichensis, commonly known as the Chichén Itzá cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems and spiny areoles. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in limestone soils. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces small, edible fruits.

This cactus is notable for its role in traditional Mexican culture and its adaptation to harsh desert conditions.
