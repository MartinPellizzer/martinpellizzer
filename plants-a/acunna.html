Acunna

Acunna, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.

Its striking appearance makes it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
