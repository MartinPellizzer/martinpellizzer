Allium cyaneum

Allium cyaneum, commonly known as the blue onion, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces small, globe-shaped bulbs that are often used as ornamental plants in gardens. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of pale blue or violet flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.

Allium cyaneum is valued for its aesthetic appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
