Anemone patens lusus albolutea

Anemone patens lusus albolutea, commonly known as the white-flowered anemone, is a variant of the common anemone species characterized by its pale, almost white petals.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is native to certain regions of Europe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in spring, producing a cluster of delicate, showy blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
