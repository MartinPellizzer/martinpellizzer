Aster greatae

Aster greatae, commonly known as the Great Aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and prairies, where it thrives in full sunlight. Its showy purple or white flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
