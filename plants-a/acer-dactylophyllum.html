Acer dactylophyllum

Acer dactylophyllum, commonly known as the New Zealand maple, is a small deciduous tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves, which are typically five-lobed and have a bright green upper surface. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by red or orange samaras that disperse its seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
