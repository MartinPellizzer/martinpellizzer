Astragalus rubrifolius

Astragalus rubrifolius, commonly known as the red-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions.

Astragalus rubrifolius is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
