Annona cuneata

Annona cuneata, commonly known as the sour sop or custard apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The fruit is a rough, greenish-yellow berry that turns yellow when ripe, with a creamy, aromatic pulp. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit.

It also has medicinal uses in traditional medicine due to its bioactive compounds.
