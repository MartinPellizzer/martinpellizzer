Acronia nitida

Acronia nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf acronia, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing adaptations to arid environments. The plant features narrow, silvery-gray leaves that help reduce water loss through transpiration. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters and bloom during the cooler months.

Acronia nitida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil.
