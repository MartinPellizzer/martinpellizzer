Artemisia campestris subsp. glutinosa

Artemisia campestris subsp.

glutinosa, commonly known as sticky wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Eurasia. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage with glandular dots that produce a sticky resin.

The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and are wind-pollinated.

The subspecies is noted for its strong medicinal properties and historical use in traditional remedies.
