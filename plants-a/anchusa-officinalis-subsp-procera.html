Anchusa officinalis subsp. procera

Anchusa officinalis subsp.

procera, commonly known as tall bugloss, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows up to 1.5 meters tall, characterized by its blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, while the upper leaves are smaller and sessile. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas. Anchusa officinalis subsp.

procera is known for its ornamental value and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
