Armeriastrum assyriacum

Armeriastrum assyriacum, commonly known as the Assyrian sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in its dry habitat. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
