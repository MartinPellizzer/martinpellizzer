Albizia xanthoxylon

Albizia xanthoxylon, commonly known as the yellow albizia, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy and compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are typically yellow or cream-colored, and it produces flat, woody pods that contain several seeds.

The wood of Albizia xanthoxylon is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.
