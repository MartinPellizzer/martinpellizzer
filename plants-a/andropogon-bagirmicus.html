Andropogon bagirmicus

Andropogon bagirmicus, commonly known as the bagirmic grass, is a species of grass native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are wind-pollinated.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
