Astragalus abolinii

Astragalus abolinii, commonly known as the Abolini milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus abolinii is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
