Aconitum beckianum

Aconitum beckianum, commonly known as Beck's aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, terminal clusters. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance.

Aconitum beckianum contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires careful handling.
