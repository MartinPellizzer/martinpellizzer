Antennaria ambigens

Antennaria ambigens, commonly known as the slender mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features hairy, linear leaves and produces small yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in rocky, dry habitats and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.

Its botanical structure supports its adaptation to harsh environmental conditions.
