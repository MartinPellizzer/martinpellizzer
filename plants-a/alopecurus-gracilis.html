Alopecurus gracilis

Alopecurus gracilis, commonly known as slender veldtgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Alopecurus gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
