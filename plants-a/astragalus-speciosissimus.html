Astragalus speciosissimus

Astragalus speciosissimus, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmate leaves with 5 to 7 leaflets, and its root system is deep and fibrous. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open grasslands and sagebrush habitats.

Astragalus speciosissimus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
