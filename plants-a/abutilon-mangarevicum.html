Abutilon mangarevicum

Abutilon mangarevicum, commonly known as the mangareva fanflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, particularly the islands of Mangareva and Pitcairn.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and salt spray.

Abutilon mangarevicum is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in subtropical gardens.
