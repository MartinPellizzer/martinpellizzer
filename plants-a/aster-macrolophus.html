Aster macrolophus

Aster macrolophus, commonly known as the showy aster, is a flowering plant belonging to the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers consist of a central disk surrounded by ray florets, which are usually purple or pink in color.

Aster macrolophus is often found in moist meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full sunlight.
