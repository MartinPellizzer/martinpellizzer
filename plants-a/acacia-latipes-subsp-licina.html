Acacia latipes subsp. licina

Acacia latipes subsp.

licina, commonly known as the Licina acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched structure and its distinctive yellow flowers that appear in globular or cylindrical clusters.

The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, linear leaflets, and the plant produces long, slender pods that contain seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
