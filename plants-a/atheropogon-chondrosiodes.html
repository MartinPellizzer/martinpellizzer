Atheropogon chondrosiodes

Atheropogon chondrosiodes, commonly known as the sand sedge, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, clump-forming habit with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures. Its rhizomatous growth pattern allows it to spread and form dense vegetation, which helps stabilize soil in disturbed areas.

The sand sedge plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
