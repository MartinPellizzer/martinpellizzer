Amblyglottis abbreviata

Amblyglottis abbreviata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved amblyglottis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow.

It is primarily pollinated by insects and produces small, dry fruits known as achenes.
