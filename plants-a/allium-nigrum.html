Allium nigrum

Allium nigrum, commonly known as black garlic, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia. The plant produces small, dark purple to black bulbs that are prized for their intense flavor and aroma. The bulbs are harvested when fully mature, typically in the fall, and are used in various culinary applications.

Botanically, Allium nigrum is characterized by its fibrous roots, strap-like leaves, and clustered inflorescences.
