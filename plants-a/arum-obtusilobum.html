Arum obtusilobum

Arum obtusilobum, commonly known as the broad-leaved arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, broad leaves that are typically green with a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

The flowers are not showy but play a role in attracting specific pollinators.
