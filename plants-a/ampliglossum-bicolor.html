Ampliglossum bicolor

Ampliglossum bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-toned flowers, which typically display a combination of purple and white hues. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ampliglossum bicolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
