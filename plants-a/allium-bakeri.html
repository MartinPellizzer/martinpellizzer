Allium bakeri

Allium bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's onion, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Tajikistan. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor.

Allium bakeri is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and culinary potential.
