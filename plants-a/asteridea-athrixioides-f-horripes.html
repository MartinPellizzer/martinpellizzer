Asteridea athrixioides f. horripes

Asteridea athrixioides f. horripes, commonly known as the hairy aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage and showy floral displays.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is native to specific regions in South America. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped with dense, fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance, while its flowers are typically blue to purple in color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
