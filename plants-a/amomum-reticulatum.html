Amomum reticulatum

Amomum reticulatum, commonly known as the fragrant cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, finger-like rhizomes that are rich in essential oils. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

The flowers are small and greenish-white, typically clustered in inflorescences at the base of the plant.
