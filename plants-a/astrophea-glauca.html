Astrophea glauca

Astrophea glauca, commonly known as the blue glaucous bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, giving them a bluish sheen. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Astrophea glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
