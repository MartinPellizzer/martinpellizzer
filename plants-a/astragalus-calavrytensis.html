Astragalus calavrytensis

Astragalus calavrytensis, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in mountainous regions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
