Anthemis tenuiflora

Anthemis tenuiflora, commonly known as narrowleaf tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small yellow buttons. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been used traditionally in herbal remedies and as a natural insect repellent.
