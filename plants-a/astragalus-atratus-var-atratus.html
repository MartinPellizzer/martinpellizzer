Astragalus atratus var. atratus

Astragalus atratus var. atratus, commonly known as the black milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces cylindrical, woody pods that contain several seeds, and its leaves are typically trifoliate with hairy surfaces. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
