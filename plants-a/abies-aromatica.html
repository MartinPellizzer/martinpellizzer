Abies aromatica

Abies aromatica, commonly known as the white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and its distinctive silvery-blue foliage, which gives it a striking appearance in its natural habitat. The tree produces cones that are typically cylindrical and can reach lengths of up to 10 centimeters. Its needles are flat, soft, and have a fragrant resinous scent, which contributes to its common name.

Abies aromatica plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
