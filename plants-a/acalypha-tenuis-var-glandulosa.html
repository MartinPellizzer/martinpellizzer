Acalypha tenuis var. glandulosa

Acalypha tenuis var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular acalypha, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. The leaves typically have a bluish-green hue and may exhibit a waxy texture. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
