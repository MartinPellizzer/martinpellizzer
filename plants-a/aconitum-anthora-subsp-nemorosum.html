Aconitum anthora subsp. nemorosum

Aconitum anthora subsp.

nemorosum, commonly known as the woodland aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely leafed stems that bear large, showy blue or violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy texture. Its roots contain toxic alkaloids, making it potentially hazardous to both humans and animals.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
