Adiantum trapeziforme var. catharinae

Adiantum trapeziforme var. catharinae, commonly known as the Catharina maidenhair fern, is a delicate and ornamental fern native to southern China.

It features finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are typically bright green and have a graceful, feathery appearance. The plant thrives in shady, humid environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. It is known for its slow growth and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in indoor or garden settings.

The varietal name 'catharinae' honors a person, though the exact origin of the name is not widely documented.
