Acer palmatum f. rubrifolium

Acer palmatum f. rubrifolium, commonly known as the red-leaf maple, is a cultivar of the Japanese maple known for its striking foliage.

It features finely dissected leaves that turn a vibrant red in the fall, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet and has a rounded, upright growth habit. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, preferring cooler climates.

Its botanical characteristics include palmate leaf shapes and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in early spring.
