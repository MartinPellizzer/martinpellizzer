Actinidia chinensis var. rufopulpa

Actinidia chinensis var. rufopulpa, commonly known as the red-haired kiwifruit, is a variant of the Chinese actinidia species.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, fuzzy fruits. The fruit is typically hairy, hence the name "rufopulpa," which refers to its reddish, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in temperate climates and requires a chilling period for optimal fruit production.

The plant is valued for its nutritional content and is cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes.
