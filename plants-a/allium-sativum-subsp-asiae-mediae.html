Allium sativum subsp. asiae-mediae

Allium sativum subsp.

asiae-mediae, commonly known as the Chinese leek or yu xiang cai, is a botanical variant of the common garlic. It is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical bulbs that are typically white to pale yellow in color.

The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, spherical white flowers. It is native to regions of Asia, including China and parts of the Himalayas.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its mild flavor and is used in various traditional cuisines.
