Adesmia araujoi

Adesmia araujoi, commonly known as the Brazilian burr legume, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces burrs that encase its seeds, which are often dispersed by animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils.

Adesmia araujoi plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its natural habitat.
