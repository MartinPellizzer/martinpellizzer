Aquilegia baluchistanica

Aquilegia baluchistanica, commonly known as the Baluchistan columbine, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Pakistan and Afghanistan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred petals that come in shades of red, purple, and blue. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are palmately compound, with serrated edges, and it produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers.

Aquilegia baluchistanica is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native environment.
