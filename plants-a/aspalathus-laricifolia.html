Aspalathus laricifolia

Aspalathus laricifolia, commonly known as the African red bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and small, needle-like leaves that resemble those of a fir tree. The plant produces clusters of tiny, red flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Aspalathus laricifolia is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of red bush tea.
