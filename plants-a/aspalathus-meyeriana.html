Aspalathus meyeriana

Aspalathus meyeriana, commonly known as the Meyer's bushveld pea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in shrubland and grassland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It is often found in the Western and Eastern Cape provinces of South Africa.

Aspalathus meyeriana is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its hardy nature and ability to withstand dry conditions.
