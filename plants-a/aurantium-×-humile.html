Aurantium × humile

Aurantium × humile, commonly known as the bitter orange, is a hybrid citrus plant originating from the cross between *Citrus aurantium* and *Citrus sinensis*.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange fruits that are typically used for their medicinal and aromatic properties rather than for consumption. The plant produces fragrant white flowers and has dark green, glossy leaves. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its essential oils and ornamental value.

The fruit contains a high concentration of flavonoids and has been used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
