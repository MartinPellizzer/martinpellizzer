Astragalus hemsleyi

Astragalus hemsleyi, commonly known as Hemsley's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
