Arrhenatherum erianthum var. montanum

Arrhenatherum erianthum var. montanum, commonly known as mountain feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-green leaves and panicle-like inflorescences that produce small, wind-dispersed seeds. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and forest edges. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens and restoration projects.
