Austrosteenisia mollitricha

Austrosteenisia mollitricha, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of specific host trees, primarily in southern Africa.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered together, followed by small, fleshy fruits that are dispersed by birds. Its parasitic nature means it derives some of its nutrients from the host plant, while still performing photosynthesis.

Austrosteenisia mollitricha plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various bird species that feed on its fruits.
