Arundinaria horiyoshitakana

Arundinaria horiyoshitakana, commonly known as the Horiyoshi bamboo, is a species of grass in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fine, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. It is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in gardens.

The Horiyoshi bamboo is also noted for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
