Althaea borealis

Althaea borealis, commonly known as the northern marshmallow, is a flowering plant native to northern regions of North America and Eurasia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are thick and succulent.

Althaea borealis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in traditional medicine.
