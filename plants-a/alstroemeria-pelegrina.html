Alstroemeria pelegrina

Alstroemeria pelegrina, commonly known as the Peruvian lily, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with strap-like leaves arranged in a rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Alstroemeria pelegrina is also valued for its long-lasting blooms and adaptability to different climate conditions.
