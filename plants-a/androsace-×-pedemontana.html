Androsace × pedemontana

Androsace × pedemontana, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is native to the alpine regions of southern Europe. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically rosette-shaped, covered in a dense layer of fine, fuzzy hairs.

Androsace × pedemontana thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountain meadows and scree slopes.
