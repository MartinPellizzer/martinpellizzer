Astragalus hausknechtii

Astragalus hausknechtii, commonly known as the Sierra foothill milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils, often found in mountainous regions. The plant produces small, yellow to green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus hausknechtii is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.
