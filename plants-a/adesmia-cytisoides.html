Adesmia cytisoides

Adesmia cytisoides, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate and have a yellowish-green color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in cylindrical racemes, often blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

Adesmia cytisoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
