Alyxia torquata

Alyxia torquata, commonly known as the scarlet trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that contain seeds dispersed by animals. Alyxia torquata is a woody vine that thrives in sunny, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
