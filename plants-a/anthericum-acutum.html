Anthericum acutum

Anthericum acutum, commonly known as the sharp-tailed grass, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or greenish flowers in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Anthericum acutum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
