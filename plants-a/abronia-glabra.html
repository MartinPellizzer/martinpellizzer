Abronia glabra

Abronia glabra, commonly known as the smooth sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Abronia glabra is adapted to sandy and rocky soils, making it a characteristic species of desert and coastal environments.
