Alsine drummondii

Alsine drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's sandmat, is a low-growing annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate habit and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, helping it conserve water in dry conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small insects and animals.
