Actinidia purpurea

Actinidia purpurea, commonly known as the purple actinidia, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in early summer, followed by edible berries that ripen to a dark purple color. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in jams and wines.

It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental and edible landscaping.
