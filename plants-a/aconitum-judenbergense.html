Aconitum judenbergense

Aconitum judenbergense, commonly known as Judenberg's aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, palmately compound leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a hazardous plant and should be handled with care.
