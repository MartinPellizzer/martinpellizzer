Atalantia simplicifolia

Atalantia simplicifolia, commonly known as the sour orange, is a small evergreen tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its simple, oval-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically sour and seedy, though they are sometimes used in culinary applications. Its botanical features include a smooth, greyish bark and a compact growth habit.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in citrus cultivation.
