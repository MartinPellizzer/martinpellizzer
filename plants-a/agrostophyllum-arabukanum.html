Agrostophyllum arabukanum

Agrostophyllum arabukanum, commonly known as the Arabian sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in harsh environments.

Agrostophyllum arabukanum is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is well-suited to drought conditions.
