Astragalus nigrivestitus

Astragalus nigrivestitus, commonly known as the black-clothed milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, which are followed by narrow, inflated pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Astragalus nigrivestitus plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological restoration projects.
