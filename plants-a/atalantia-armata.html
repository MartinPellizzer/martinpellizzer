Atalantia armata

Atalantia armata, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and pinnate, with small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces yellowish flowers followed by orange to red berries that contain three to five seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy foliage and fruit.

However, it is considered invasive in many regions outside its native range.
