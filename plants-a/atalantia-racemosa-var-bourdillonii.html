Atalantia racemosa var. bourdillonii

Atalantia racemosa var. bourdillonii, commonly known as the California buckthorn, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in dense racemes, followed by red, fleshy berries. The plant thrives in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly waxy texture.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
