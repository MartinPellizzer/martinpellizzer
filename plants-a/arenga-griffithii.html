Arenga griffithii

Arenga griffithii, commonly known as the giant palm or wild areca, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, typical of many palms. Its trunk is robust and often covered with persistent leaf bases, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Arenga griffithii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in various regions.
