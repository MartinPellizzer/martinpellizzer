Acacia xerophila var. xerophila

Acacia xerophila var. xerophila, commonly known as the desert wattle, is a shrub native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, phyllode-like leaves that reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with deep root systems and a slow growth rate.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils in harsh environments.
