Alpinia macrocrista

Alpinia macrocrista, commonly known as the giant ginger or mountain ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, rhizomatous growth habit and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often adorned with a waxy bloom. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and are borne in clusters, adding ornamental value.

Alpinia macrocrista thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and aromatic properties.
