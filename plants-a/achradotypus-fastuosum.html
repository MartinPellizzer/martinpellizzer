Achradotypus fastuosum

Achradotypus fastuosum, commonly known as the showy bristly grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, bristly foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly observed in the wild.
