Anvillea

Anvillea, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Mexico and Central America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Anvillea species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are often cultivated in gardens for their vibrant blooms.
