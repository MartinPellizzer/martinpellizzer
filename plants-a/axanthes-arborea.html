Axanthes arborea

Axanthes arborea, commonly known as the white-flowered lily.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa, characterized by its slender, upright stems and showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Axanthes arborea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
