Allomorphia eupteroton

Allomorphia eupteroton, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for pollinators and wetland ecosystems.
