Arabis lyrata var. typica

Arabis lyrata var. typica, commonly known as the lyrata rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of linear, silver-green leaves and produces clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers. The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments. It is adapted to full sun and has a low water requirement, making it drought-tolerant.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
