Agave sobria subsp. roseana

Agave sobria subsp.

roseana, commonly known as the roseate agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico. It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically pale green with a reddish tinge.

The leaves are covered in a layer of white powder, known as bloom, which helps reduce water loss. It produces an erect flowering stalk that bears small, white flowers, which are often followed by blue-gray berries.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
