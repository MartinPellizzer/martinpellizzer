Abutilon terminale var. islae-malae

Abutilon terminale var. islae-malae, commonly known as the Isla Malae Abutilon, is a variant of the common velvetleaf.

It is a shrub or small tree native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are often lobed and have a velvety texture. It produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
