Acacia simsii var. typica

Acacia simsii var. typica, commonly known as the Natal acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and produces yellow flowers in clusters. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of true leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Acacia simsii var. typica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
