Alloplectus cristatus var. crenatus

Alloplectus cristatus var. crenatus, commonly known as the hairy crinkled bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal rosettes of ovate, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and alpine plant collections.
