Ampelocissus major

Ampelocissus major, commonly known as the giant wood vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and can grow to significant lengths, often reaching several meters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, round berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its vigorous growth and attractive foliage.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the genus Ampelocissus.
