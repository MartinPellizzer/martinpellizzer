Avenula bromoides subsp. crassifolia

Avenula bromoides subsp.

crassifolia, commonly known as broadleaf false oat, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

The plant features basal rosettes of broad, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, greenish flowers. Its leaves are characterized by their thick, fleshy texture and distinctive crassifolia subspecies traits.

It is often found in the eastern and central United States, where it plays a role in wetland ecosystems.
