Acalypha ceraceopunctata

Acalypha ceraceopunctata, commonly known as the silver-leaf acalypha, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, silver-gray foliage with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
