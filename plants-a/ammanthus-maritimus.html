Ammanthus maritimus

Ammanthus maritimus, commonly known as the sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Caribbean and the Gulf of Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows along sandy shores and in saltwater environments, showcasing its adaptability to saline conditions. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Ammanthus maritimus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
