Aldama filifolia

Aldama filifolia, commonly known as the silky leaf tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
