Abutilon abutiloides

Abutilon abutiloides, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, soft pubescence, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a spiny capsule that contains several seeds.

Abutilon abutiloides is often found in disturbed habitats and can be invasive in some areas.
