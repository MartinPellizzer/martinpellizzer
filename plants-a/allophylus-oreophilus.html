Allophylus oreophilus

Allophylus oreophilus, commonly known as the mountain alder, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by small, woody fruits.

Allophylus oreophilus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
