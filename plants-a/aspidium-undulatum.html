Aspidium undulatum

Aspidium undulatum, commonly known as the wavy water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically green and have a wavy appearance. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Aspidium undulatum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
