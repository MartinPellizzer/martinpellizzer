Alternanthera

Alternanthera, commonly known as the red-leaf plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its strikingly colored leaves, which can range from red to purple and even green. Many species are popular in aquariums and as ornamental plants due to their vibrant foliage. The plants typically have small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overshadowed by their colorful leaves.

Alternanthera thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
