Albuca trichophylla

Albuca trichophylla, commonly known as the woolly albuca, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking, strap-like leaves that are covered in a dense, white to grayish pubescence, giving it a distinctive, woolly appearance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
