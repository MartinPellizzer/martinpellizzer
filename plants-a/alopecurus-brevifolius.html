Alopecurus brevifolius

Alopecurus brevifolius, commonly known as meadow foxtail, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has slender, tufted stems with small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny flowers. Alopecurus brevifolius is often considered a weed in agricultural settings but can also serve as a food source for various insects and small mammals.
