Antholoma haplopoda

Antholoma haplopoda, commonly known as the woolly antholoma, is a species of fungus within the family Hyphomycetes.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly appearance, which gives it a distinctive visual texture. This fungus typically grows on decaying wood, particularly in moist, temperate environments. Its spore-producing structures, called conidiophores, are often covered in a layer of fine, hairy cells.

Antholoma haplopoda is of interest to mycologists due to its unique morphology and ecological role in forest decomposition processes.
