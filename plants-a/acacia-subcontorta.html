Acacia subcontorta

Acacia subcontorta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are narrow and often contorted, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or semi-arid regions.

Acacia subcontorta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for erosion control.
