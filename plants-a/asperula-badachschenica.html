Asperula badachschenica

Asperula badachschenica, commonly known as the Badakhshan azalea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are elliptical and evergreen, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

Asperula badachschenica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with acidic soil conditions.
