Astragalus vardziae

Astragalus vardziae, commonly known as the Vardzia milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor conditions.

Astragalus vardziae plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
