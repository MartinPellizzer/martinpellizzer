Arisaema linearifolium

Arisaema linearifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive spathe and spadix inflorescence. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. Its underground corm is used as a starch source in some regions.

The species is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
