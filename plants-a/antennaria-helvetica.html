Antennaria helvetica

Antennaria helvetica, commonly known as the Alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and cylindrical stems. The plant features small, hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or grayish down. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Antennaria helvetica thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
