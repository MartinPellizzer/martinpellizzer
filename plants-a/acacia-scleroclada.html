Acacia scleroclada

Acacia scleroclada, commonly known as the hard-shelled acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid regions of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often reduced to spines. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular inflorescences, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Acacia scleroclada is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots and a slow growth rate.

It plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
