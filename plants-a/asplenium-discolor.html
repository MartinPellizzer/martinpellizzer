Asplenium discolor

Asplenium discolor, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery or grayish coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with small, toothed pinnae that contribute to its ornamental value.

Asplenium discolor is valued in horticulture for its elegant foliage and ability to thrive in humid, indoor conditions.
