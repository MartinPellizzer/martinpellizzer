Allium fritschii

Allium fritschii, commonly known as the Fritsch's onion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous structure and clusters of white or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate to cold climates. Its leaves are grass-like and form a rosette near the ground, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Allium fritschii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
