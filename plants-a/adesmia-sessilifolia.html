Adesmia sessilifolia

Adesmia sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaved adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, characterized by its pod fruits and often nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, and it produces leguminous pods that contain several seeds.

Adesmia sessilifolia is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in such habitats.
