Athyriopsis grammitoides

Athyriopsis grammitoides, commonly known as the slender maidenhair fern, is a member of the family Athyriaceae.

It is a small, evergreen fern characterized by its delicate, arching fronds that resemble those of the maidenhair fern. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are wiry and creeping, allowing it to spread slowly over time.

The leaves are usually bi- or tri-pinnate, with a glossy, dark green appearance that adds a refined aesthetic to its natural habitat.
