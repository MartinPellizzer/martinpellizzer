Albizia sahafariensis

Albizia sahafariensis, commonly known as the Saharan Albizia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, featuring deep roots and a thick bark that helps it withstand drought.

It plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides habitat and forage for wildlife in its native range.
