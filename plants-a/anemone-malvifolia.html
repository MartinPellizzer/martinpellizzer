Anemone malvifolia

Anemone malvifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf anemone, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are typically hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
