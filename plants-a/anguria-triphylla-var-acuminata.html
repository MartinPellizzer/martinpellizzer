Anguria triphylla var. acuminata

Anguria triphylla var. acuminata, commonly known as the three-leaf cucumber, is a unique variant of the anguria plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves.

This plant is native to tropical regions of South America and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its fruits are elongated, typically measuring up to 20 centimeters, and have a mild, slightly sweet flavor. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal as well as its edible fruit.

It requires well-drained soil and full sun to grow optimally.
