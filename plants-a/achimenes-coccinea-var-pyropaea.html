Achimenes coccinea var. pyropaea

Achimenes coccinea var. pyropaea, commonly known as the red flame achimenes, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a contrasting yellow throat. The plant produces showy bracts that enhance its ornamental appeal, making it a popular choice for gardeners. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is known for its vigorous growth and ability to produce multiple blooms throughout the growing season.
