Acer ginnala var. euginnala

Acer ginnala var. euginnala, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 meters, with a rounded canopy and smooth, grey bark. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental tree in gardens.

Its botanical features include small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves.
