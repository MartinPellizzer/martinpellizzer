Atractylis diffusa

Atractylis diffusa, commonly known as giant fennel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, feather-like leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like carrots and parsley.
