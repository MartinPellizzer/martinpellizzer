Astragalus onobrychis var. banaticus

Astragalus onobrychis var. banaticus, commonly known as the Banat sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to southeastern Europe.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is adapted to dry, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas. It plays a role in soil fixation due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
