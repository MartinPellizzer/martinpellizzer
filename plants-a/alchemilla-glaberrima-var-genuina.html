Alchemilla glaberrima var. genuina

Alchemilla glaberrima var. genuina, commonly known as the smooth alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. Its leaves are basal, ovate to elliptic in shape, and covered with a fine, waxy coating.

Alchemilla glaberrima var. genuina thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.
