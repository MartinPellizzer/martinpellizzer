Arum trapezuntinum

Arum trapezuntinum, commonly known as the Trapezuntine arum, is a species of flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are arrow-shaped and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters, while its flowers are small and clustered on the spadix.

Arum trapezuntinum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and unusual floral structure.
