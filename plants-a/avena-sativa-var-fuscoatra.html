Avena sativa var. fuscoatra

Avena sativa var. fuscoatra, commonly known as black oat, is a variant of the oat species characterized by its dark-colored grains.

This variety is native to temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils with adequate sunlight. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, each bearing tiny, two-lobed flowers.

The seeds, which are rich in nutrients, are used for both animal feed and human consumption.
