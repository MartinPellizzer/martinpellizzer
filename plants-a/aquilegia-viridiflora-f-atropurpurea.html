Aquilegia viridiflora f. atropurpurea

Aquilegia viridiflora f. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-leaved columbine, is a variant of the green-flowered columbine with distinctive purplish foliage.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. The plant features showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided and often have a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

This cultivar is valued in gardens for its unique coloration and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
