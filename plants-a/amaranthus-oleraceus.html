Amaranthus oleraceus

Amaranthus oleraceus, commonly known as spinach amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that develop into spiny, three-angled seed capsules. It thrives in warm climates and is cultivated for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients such as iron and protein.

Amaranthus oleraceus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience to poor soil conditions.
