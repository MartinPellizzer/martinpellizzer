Austrodanthonia bipartita

Austrodanthonia bipartita, commonly known as the two-lobed grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often in heathland or shrubland environments. Austrodanthonia bipartita is well adapted to harsh conditions, including salt spray and drought.

It plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and supporting local biodiversity.
