Airopsis agrostidea var. natans

Airopsis agrostidea var. natans, commonly known as the floating sea lettuce, is a marine green alga that thrives in coastal waters.

It is characterized by its ribbon-like, flattened thallus that floats on the surface of the water. The plant lacks true roots, stems, and leaves, instead consisting of a single layer of cells. It reproduces through spores and is often found in brackish or saltwater environments.

Its presence is an indicator of nutrient-rich, stable aquatic ecosystems.
