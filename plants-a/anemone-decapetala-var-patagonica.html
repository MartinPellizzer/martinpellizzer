Anemone decapetala var. patagonica

Anemone decapetala var. patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or alpine habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species in its native environment.
