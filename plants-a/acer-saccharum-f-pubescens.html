Acer saccharum f. pubescens

Acer saccharum f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy sugar maple, is a botanical variant of the sugar maple.

It is characterized by its pubescent, hairy leaves that distinguish it from the typical smooth leaves of the species. This form is typically found in cooler, more shaded environments, often in the understory of mixed hardwood forests. The hairy texture of its foliage is an adaptation that may help reduce water loss and protect against herbivory.

It shares the same ecological role as the standard sugar maple, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
