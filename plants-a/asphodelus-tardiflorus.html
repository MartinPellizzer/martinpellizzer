Asphodelus tardiflorus

Asphodelus tardiflorus, commonly known as the late-flowering asphodel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its strap-like, grass-like leaves. The plant produces tall, slender flowering stems that bear clusters of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. It blooms late in the growing season, usually from late summer to early autumn.

Asphodelus tardiflorus is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.
