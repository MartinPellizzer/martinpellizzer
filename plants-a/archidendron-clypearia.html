Archidendron clypearia

Archidendron clypearia, commonly known as the white senna, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
