Anthyllis vulneraria subsp. valesiaca

Anthyllis vulneraria subsp.

valesiaca, commonly known as the Valesiaca Scotch broom, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
