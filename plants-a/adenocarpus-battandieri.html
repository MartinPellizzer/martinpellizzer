Adenocarpus battandieri

Adenocarpus battandieri, commonly known as the purple globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in the spring. Adenocarpus battandieri is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
