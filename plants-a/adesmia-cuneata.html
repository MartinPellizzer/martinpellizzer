Adesmia cuneata

Adesmia cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf burr legume, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its compound leaves and fruit pods. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a cuneate (wedge-shaped) base, and its flowers are typically small and clustered in racemes. Adesmia cuneata produces leguminous pods that contain several seeds, contributing to its role in soil nitrogen fixation.

It is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
