Acianthera teres

Acianthera teres, commonly known as the compact lady's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, compact growth habit and its distinctive, pouch-like flower structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a unique shape that resembles a lady's slipper.

Acianthera teres is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
