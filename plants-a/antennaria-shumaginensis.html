Antennaria shumaginensis

Antennaria shumaginensis, commonly known as Shumagin mouse-eared cowwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Aleutian Islands of Alaska.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaf structure. The plant features small, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, sandy or rocky soils and is typically found in coastal and alpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
