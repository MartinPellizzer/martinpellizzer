Achillea roseoalba

Achillea roseoalba, commonly known as the white garden chrysanthemum, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features finely divided, silver-gray leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its prolonged blooming period and drought tolerance.

Morphology

Achillea roseoalba has compound, serrated leaves that are basal and arranged in a rosette, with a bluish-green color and a rough texture.

The stem is upright, hairy, and has a grayish-green bark, growing in a clumping pattern. Its flowers are composite, with pale pink to white petals and yellow centers, blooming in summer, and are pollinated by insects. The fruit is a small, dry achene, and seeds are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and rhizomatous, helping the plant spread and survive in various conditions.
