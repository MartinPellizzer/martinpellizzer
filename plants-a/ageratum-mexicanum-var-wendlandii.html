Ageratum mexicanum var. wendlandii

Ageratum mexicanum var. wendlandii, commonly known as Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flower clusters. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes for its colorful blooms.
