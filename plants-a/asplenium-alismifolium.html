Asplenium alismifolium

Asplenium alismifolium, commonly known as the water fern, is a species of fern native to wetland and marshy environments.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble those of water plants. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, thriving in humid and shaded conditions. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, which are clustered in sori.

Asplenium alismifolium plays an important ecological role in maintaining the biodiversity of wetland ecosystems.
