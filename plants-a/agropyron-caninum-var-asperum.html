Agropyron caninum var. asperum

Agropyron caninum var. asperum, commonly known as the rough wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its robust, tufted growth habit and narrow, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

This species is valued for its hardiness and is used in erosion control and as a forage crop.
