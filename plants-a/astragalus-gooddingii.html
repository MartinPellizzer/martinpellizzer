Astragalus gooddingii

Astragalus gooddingii, commonly known as Goodding's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its ecosystem.

Due to its limited range and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern in some areas.
