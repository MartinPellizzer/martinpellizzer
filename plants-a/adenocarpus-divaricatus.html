Adenocarpus divaricatus

Adenocarpus divaricatus, commonly known as the variable burr mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant produces distinctive, hairy, spiny capsules that contain its seeds. Its flowers are small and usually pale pink to white, arranged in clusters.

The species is characterized by its variable growth form and adaptability to different environmental conditions.
