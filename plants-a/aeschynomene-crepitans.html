Aeschynomene crepitans

Aeschynomene crepitans, commonly known as the crackling aeschynomene, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, alternate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are clustered in spikes and are usually pale green or yellowish in color.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
