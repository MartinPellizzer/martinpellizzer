Aeschynomene fluminensis var. fluminensis

Aeschynomene fluminensis var. fluminensis, commonly known as river hyacinth, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, white to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, including marshes and along riverbanks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy coating.

It is known for its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and is sometimes used in phytoremediation efforts.
