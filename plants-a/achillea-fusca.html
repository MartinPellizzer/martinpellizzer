Achillea fusca

Achillea fusca, commonly known as the lesser yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are white to pale yellow.

Achillea fusca is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory qualities.
