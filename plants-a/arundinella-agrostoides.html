Arundinella agrostoides

Arundinella agrostoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf reed, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often forming dense stands that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often used in traditional basket-making and thatching. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture that contributes to its utility in various crafts.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family Poaceae.
