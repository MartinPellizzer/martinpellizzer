Albuca gageoides

Albuca gageoides, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with a distinctive, almost tulip-like shape. The plant is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil to thrive. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ease of cultivation.

Albuca gageoides is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is known for its drought tolerance.
