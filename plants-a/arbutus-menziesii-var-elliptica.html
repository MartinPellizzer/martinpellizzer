Arbutus menziesii var. elliptica

Arbutus menziesii var. elliptica, commonly known as the Pacific madrone, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and elliptical leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries that are dark red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal forests and chaparral habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is a notable species in its native range.
