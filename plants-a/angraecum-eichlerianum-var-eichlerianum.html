Angraecum eichlerianum var. eichlerianum

Angraecum eichlerianum var. eichlerianum, commonly known as the Eichler's angraecum, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This orchid is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
