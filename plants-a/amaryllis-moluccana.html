Amaryllis moluccana

Amaryllis moluccana, commonly known as the Moluccan amaryllis, is a bulbous plant native to the Moluccas in Indonesia.

It features striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that can be pink, red, or white, often with a contrasting throat. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem that bears multiple blooms during the growing season. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are dark green and glossy.

Amaryllis moluccana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
