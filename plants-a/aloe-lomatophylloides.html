Aloe lomatophylloides

Aloe lomatophylloides, commonly known as the rock aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Aloe lomatophylloides is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
