Assonia triumfettifolia

Assonia triumfettifolia, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It features palm-like leaves that are compound and often have a striking golden or yellow hue. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late winter to early spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Assonia triumfettifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
