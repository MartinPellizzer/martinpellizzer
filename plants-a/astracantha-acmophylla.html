Astracantha acmophylla

Astracantha acmophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved star-thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellowish-white.

Astracantha acmophylla is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native environment.
