Alchemilla looseri

Alchemilla looseri, commonly known as the Looser's lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in branched inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Alchemilla looseri is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
