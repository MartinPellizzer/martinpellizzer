Angraecum didieri

Angraecum didieri, commonly known as the Didier's orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It features long, slender stems and produces large, pendulous flowers with intricate, spurred petals. The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and is adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Angraecum didieri is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
