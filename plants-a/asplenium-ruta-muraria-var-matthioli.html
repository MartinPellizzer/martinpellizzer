Asplenium ruta-muraria var. matthioli

Asplenium ruta-muraria var. matthioli, commonly known as the yellow harts-tongue fern, is a variant of the harts-tongue fern native to Europe.

It is a small, evergreen fern characterized by its narrow, leathery, dark green leaves that are often tinged with yellow on the underside. The fronds are typically short and clustered, forming a dense, compact rosette. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or calcareous soils.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
