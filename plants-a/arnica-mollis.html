Arnica mollis

Arnica mollis, commonly known as leopard's bane, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, rough leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Arnica mollis is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory properties.
