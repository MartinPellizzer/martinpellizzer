Astragalus inexpectatus

Astragalus inexpectatus, commonly known as the unexpected milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus inexpectatus is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
