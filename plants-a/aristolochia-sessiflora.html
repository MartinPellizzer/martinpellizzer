Aristolochia sessiflora

Aristolochia sessiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often greenish or brownish in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The genus Aristolochia includes many species with similar morphological features and some containing toxic compounds.
