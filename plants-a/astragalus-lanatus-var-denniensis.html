Astragalus lanatus var. denniensis

Astragalus lanatus var. denniensis, commonly known as the Dennie's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, drought-prone environments.

Due to its ecological significance and medicinal potential, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
