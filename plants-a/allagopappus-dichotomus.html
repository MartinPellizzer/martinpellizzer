Allagopappus dichotomus

Allagopappus dichotomus, commonly known as the forked sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small sunflowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually opposite and linear, while its stems are often branched and covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
