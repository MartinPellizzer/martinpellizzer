Acer pictum f. novemlobum

Acer pictum f. novemlobum, commonly known as the nine-lobed sugar maple, is a cultivar of the sugar maple tree characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features leaves with nine prominent lobes, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant is native to North America and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds in the fall.

This cultivar is often planted for its aesthetic value in gardens and parks due to its vibrant fall color.
