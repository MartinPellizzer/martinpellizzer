Acacia zygioides

Acacia zygioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, feathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

Acacia zygioides is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility in degraded landscapes.
