Arundo alopecurus

Arundo alopecurus, commonly known as cocklebur, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear distinctive, spiky bracts. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into spherical, spiky fruits, which are adapted for dispersal by attaching to animals or clothing. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near streams, ditches, and wet meadows.

Botanically, it is noted for its fibrous root system and its ability to tolerate a variety of environmental conditions.
