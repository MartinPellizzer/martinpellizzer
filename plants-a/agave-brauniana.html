Agave brauniana

Agave brauniana, commonly known as the Mexican hat agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick, fleshy, and covered in a blue-gray waxy coating. The leaves are typically blue-green in color with white to yellowish margins, and they have sharp, spiny tips. It produces a tall flowering stalk that bears yellow-green flowers, which bloom in the spring.

This plant is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
