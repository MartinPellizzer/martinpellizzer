Araiostegia yaklaensis

Araiostegia yaklaensis, commonly known as the Yaklaensis rock fern, is a rare and specialized plant species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and features intricate, delicate fronds that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found on rocky outcrops or in dense forest undergrowth. Its reproductive structures, including spore-bearing sorus, are positioned along the margins of its leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
