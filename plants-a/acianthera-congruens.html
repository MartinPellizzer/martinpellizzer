Acianthera congruens

Acianthera congruens, commonly known as the clustered lady's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that resemble small slippers, typically in shades of pink and white. The plant produces a dense cluster of flowers on a single stem, which is a notable feature in its morphology. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The orchid's roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil, making it well-suited for its natural habitat.
