Ammannia rotundifolia

Ammannia rotundifolia, commonly known as prairie ammannia, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features smooth, waxy leaves that are rounded and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from June to September. It thrives in moist soils along stream banks and wetlands, preferring full sun to partial shade.

Ammannia rotundifolia is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
