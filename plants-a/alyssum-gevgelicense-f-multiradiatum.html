Alyssum gevgelicense f. multiradiatum

Alyssum gevgelicense f. multiradiatum, commonly known as the multibract alpine alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and cruciform petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
