Atalantia roxburghiana var. kerrii

Atalantia roxburghiana var. kerrii, commonly known as the Kerri's atalantia, is a variant of the Indian atalantia, native to the forests of southern India.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree with elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that are edible. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
