Acinodendron croceum

Acinodendron croceum, commonly known as the golden acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
