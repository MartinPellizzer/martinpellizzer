Actinodaphne javanica

Actinodaphne javanica, commonly known as Java cinnamon or wild cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces dark reddish-brown berries, which contain a single seed. Its bark and leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree is valued for its essential oils, which are extracted from its leaves and used in perfumery and aromatherapy.
