Astragalus qoturensis

Astragalus qoturensis, commonly known as the Qotur legume, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal in dry environments. Astragalus qoturensis is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and withstand extreme temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and ecosystem.
