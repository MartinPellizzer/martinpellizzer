Athyrium × simlense

Athyrium × simlense, commonly known as the hybrid Japanese painted fern, is a shade-loving plant native to East Asia.

It is a hybrid between Athyrium niponicum and Athyrium filix-femina, resulting in a unique combination of characteristics from both parents. The plant features delicate, lace-like fronds with a distinctive silvery-white margin, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its elegant foliage, which adds texture and visual interest to shaded landscapes.
