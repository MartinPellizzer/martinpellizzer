Arundinaria glabra

Arundinaria glabra, commonly known as bamboo, is a grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Arundinaria glabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in basket-making and construction.
