Amorphophallus brevispathus

Amorphophallus brevispathus, commonly known as the short-pathed elephant foot, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, underground tuberous structure. The plant produces a striking, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is typical of many arum species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
