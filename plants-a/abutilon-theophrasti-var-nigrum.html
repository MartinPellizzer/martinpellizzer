Abutilon theophrasti var. nigrum

Abutilon theophrasti var. nigrum, commonly known as black Indian hemp, is a variant of the common velvetleaf plant.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, inconspicuous green flowers. Its seeds are encased in a fibrous, woody capsule, which is characteristic of the Abutilon genus.

This variety is known for its potential use in fiber production and as a source of natural materials.
