Arachnorchis robinsonii

Arachnorchis robinsonii, commonly known as the Robinson's spider orchid, is a rare and striking terrestrial orchid native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with long, thin, hair-like filaments that resemble the legs of a spider. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and produces small, greenish-white flowers during the cooler months. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
