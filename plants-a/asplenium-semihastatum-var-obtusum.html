Asplenium semihastatum var. obtusum

Asplenium semihastatum var. obtusum, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern family.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded, humid areas such as woodlands or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually bipinnate, with small, overlapping pinnae that give it a lacy appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
