Anthyllis taurica

Anthyllis taurica, commonly known as the Tauric anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and scrubland habitats. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Anthyllis taurica is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in nutrient-poor environments.
