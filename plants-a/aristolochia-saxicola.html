Aristolochia saxicola

Aristolochia saxicola, commonly known as the rock pipevine, is a perennial climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and its unique, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its flowers are typically purple or greenish in color and produce a strong, musky scent.

Aristolochia saxicola is part of the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its toxic properties, containing aristolochic acids that are harmful to humans and animals.
