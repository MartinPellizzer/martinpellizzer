Anthemis sallei

Anthemis sallei, commonly known as the golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers surrounded by white ray florets, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
