Agathis longifolia

Agathis longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araucariaceae and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces large, cone-like structures that contain edible seeds, which are an important food source for various wildlife. Its hard, durable wood has been historically used for construction and furniture.

Agathis longifolia is also valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
