Aquilegia vulgaris subsp. bertolonii

Aquilegia vulgaris subsp.

bertolonii, commonly known as the Bertoloni's columbine, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals, which are typically violet or blue in color, and its compound leaves with a serrated edge.

The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, often growing in rocky or alpine habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinator populations.
