Acer negundo f. parallelum

Acer negundo f. parallelum, commonly known as the Chinese box elder, is a variant of the box elder tree native to China.

It is characterized by its alternate, palmately compound leaves that are typically three to five lobed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, which appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. Its fruits are samaras, which are winged seeds that disperse by wind.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
