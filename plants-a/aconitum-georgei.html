Aconitum georgei

Aconitum georgei, commonly known as George's monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple hood-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed divisions, and its stems are covered in soft, hairy tissue.

Aconitum georgei contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires caution when handling.
