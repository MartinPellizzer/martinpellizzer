Anemone pulsatilla subsp. rubra

Anemone pulsatilla subsp.

rubra, commonly known as the red pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to deep red, often with a distinctive central tuft of stamens.

The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette and are usually hairy and lobed.

The species is known for its early spring blooming, often appearing before the leaves of many other plants have fully emerged.
