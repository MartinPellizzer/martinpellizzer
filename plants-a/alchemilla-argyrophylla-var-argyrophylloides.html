Alchemilla argyrophylla var. argyrophylloides

Alchemilla argyrophylla var. argyrophylloides, commonly known as silver-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a dense rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that are rounded and slightly lobed. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
