It is characterized by its large, robust fronds that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping rhizomes that help it spread across moist environments. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Aspidium balbisii thrives in humid, shaded areas, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
