Annona friesii

Annona friesii, commonly known as the African sugar apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The fruit is a rough, green, spiky berry that turns yellow when ripe, containing a creamy, sweet pulp with a single large seed. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
