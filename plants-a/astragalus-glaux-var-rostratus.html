Astragalus glaux var. rostratus

Astragalus glaux var. rostratus, commonly known as the rostrated milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid to semi-arid environments. Its unique floral structure includes a spurred petal that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its ecological adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
