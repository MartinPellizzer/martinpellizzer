Apocynum cannabinum f. pennsilvanicum

Apocynum cannabinum f. pennsilvanicum, commonly known as the Pennsylvania milkweed, is a variant of the common milkweed.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States. The plant features opposite, elliptic leaves with a hairy texture and produces clusters of pink to reddish flowers. Its flowers are pollinated by various insects, including bees and butterflies.

The plant contains cardiac glycosides, which are toxic to some animals but serve as a defense mechanism.
