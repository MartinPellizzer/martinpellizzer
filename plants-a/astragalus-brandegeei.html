Astragalus brandegeei

Astragalus brandegeei, commonly known as the Brandegge milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow to cream-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and has a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces slender, erect stems.

Astragalus brandegeei is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
