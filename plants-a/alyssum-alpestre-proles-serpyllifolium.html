Alyssum alpestre proles serpyllifolium

Alyssum alpestre proles serpyllifolium, commonly known as alpine candytuft, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has compact, mat-forming growth habit with hairy, linear leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Alyssum alpestre proles serpyllifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
