Aragallus

Aragallus, commonly known as the desert bell, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Polygonaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and Southwestern Asia. The plants typically have succulent, fleshy stems and leaves adapted to conserve water. Aragallus species produce showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red.

They are often used in traditional medicine and have ornamental value due to their striking appearance.
