Angelica tenuisecta var. furcijuga

Angelica tenuisecta var. furcijuga, commonly known as the narrow-leaved angelica, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, compound leaves that have a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clustered umbels, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
