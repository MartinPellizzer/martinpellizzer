Atriplex parvifolia var. mauritanica

Atriplex parvifolia var. mauritanica, commonly known as the Mauritanian saltbush, is a halophytic shrub adapted to arid and saline environments.

It features small, glaucous leaves that reduce water loss through transpiration. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typical of the Chenopodiaceae family. It thrives in coastal areas and saline soils, often found in Mediterranean and North African regions.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for wildlife.
