Anthospermum thymoides subsp. thymoides

Anthospermum thymoides subsp.

thymoides, commonly known as sweetroot or thyme-leaved germander, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a perennial herb with aromatic, small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
