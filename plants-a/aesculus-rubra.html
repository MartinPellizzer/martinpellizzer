Aesculus rubra

Aesculus rubra, commonly known as the red buckeye, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, palmately compound leaves and clusters of orange-red, spurred flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces distinctive, spiny capsules that contain glossy, chestnut-like seeds. Aesculus rubra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
