Allophylus mollis

Allophylus mollis, commonly known as the silky alder, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage that is often covered in a fine, silvery bloom, giving it a silky appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Allophylus mollis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
