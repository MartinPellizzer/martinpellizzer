Amyris pentaphylla

Amyris pentaphylla, commonly known as the West Indian sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant wood, which is highly valued for its scent and durability. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy, and it produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits. Amyris pentaphylla is often found in moist, well-drained soils in lowland areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
