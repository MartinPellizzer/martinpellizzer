Acacia acuifera

Acacia acuifera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typically visited by nectar-feeding birds and insects. Acacia acuifera is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions.

It also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
