Astephanus furlanii

Astephanus furlanii, commonly known as the Furlanii flax, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are arranged in a circular pattern, typical of the family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Astephanus furlanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
