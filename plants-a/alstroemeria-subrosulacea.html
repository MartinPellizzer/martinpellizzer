Alstroemeria subrosulacea

Alstroemeria subrosulacea, commonly known as the Chilean lily, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with strap-like leaves that are often blue-green in color.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
