Abronia cheradophila

Abronia cheradophila, commonly known as the desert bluemouth, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and produces small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has adapted to arid environments with deep roots and thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and sensitive species.
