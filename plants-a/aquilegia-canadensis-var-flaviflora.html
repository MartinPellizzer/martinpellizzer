Aquilegia canadensis var. flaviflora

Aquilegia canadensis var. flaviflora, commonly known as the Canadian columbine, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom in early to mid-summer. The plant has palmate, trifoliate leaves that are finely divided and often covered in a soft, downy texture. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
