Argemone intermedia var. typica

Argemone intermedia var. typica, commonly known as the Mexican buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its yellow, cup-shaped flowers and hairy, alternate leaves. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not recommended for cultivation or consumption.
