Aesculus wangii var. rupicola

Aesculus wangii var. rupicola, commonly known as the rocky horse chestnut, is a rare and unique variant of the horse chestnut genus.

It is native to China and is characterized by its distinctive foliage and floral structure. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, showcasing its adaptability to challenging environments. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and it produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers in spring.

The fruit is a spiny capsule that contains several chestnut-like seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
