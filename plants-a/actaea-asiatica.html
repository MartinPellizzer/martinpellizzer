Actaea asiatica

Actaea asiatica, commonly known as the Asian black cohosh, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and large, palmately compound leaves. The plant produces showy, white to pinkish flowers in late summer, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in woodland habitats and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Actaea asiatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
