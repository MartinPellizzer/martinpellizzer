Athanasia tomentella

Athanasia tomentella, commonly known as the woolly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, daisy-like blooms that are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. Athanasia tomentella is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
