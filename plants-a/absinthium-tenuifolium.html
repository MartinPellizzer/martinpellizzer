Absinthium tenuifolium

Absinthium tenuifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, aromatic stems and narrow, gray-green leaves with a strong, bitter scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky soils.

Its historical use in herbal medicine and as a flavoring agent is well documented.
