Alyssum tortuosum subsp. heterophyllum

Alyssum tortuosum subsp.

heterophyllum, commonly known as the twisted alyssum, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fragrant yellow or white flowers.

The subspecies heterophyllum is distinguished by its variable leaf morphology, including lanceolate or ovate leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
