Acacia engleri

Acacia engleri, commonly known as the Kauri acacia, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a small tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal and lowland areas. The plant is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, feathery leaves. It produces yellow flowers in winter, which are followed by distinctive, flat, pod-like fruits.

Acacia engleri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
