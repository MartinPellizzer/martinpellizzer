Acacia browniana var. browniana

Acacia browniana var. browniana, commonly known as the Brown's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. The plant is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
