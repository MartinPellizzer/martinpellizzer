Arundo phragmites var. nigricans

Arundo phragmites var. nigricans, commonly known as black reed, is a variant of the common reed that exhibits distinctive dark pigmentation in its stems and leaves.

It is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant has hollow, jointed stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. It produces small, clustered spikes of brownish or grayish flowers during the summer months.

This variant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
