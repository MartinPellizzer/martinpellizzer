Armeria maritima f. legionensis

Armeria maritima f. legionensis, commonly known as the coastal thrift or sea thrift, is a flowering plant belonging to the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae).

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that thrives in coastal environments, characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers in early spring. It is well adapted to salty air and sandy soils, making it a hardy species often used in coastal landscaping.

Its ornamental value and resilience to harsh conditions make it a popular choice for gardeners in maritime regions.
