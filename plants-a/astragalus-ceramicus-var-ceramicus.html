Astragalus ceramicus var. ceramicus

Astragalus ceramicus var. ceramicus, commonly known as the ceramic milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellowish to greenish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
