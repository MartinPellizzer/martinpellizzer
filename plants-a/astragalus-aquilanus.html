Astragalus aquilanus

Astragalus aquilanus, commonly known as the mountain milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus aquilanus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
