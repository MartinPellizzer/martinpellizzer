Anthyllis oreigenes

Anthyllis oreigenes, commonly known as the Greek clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

Anthyllis oreigenes plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
