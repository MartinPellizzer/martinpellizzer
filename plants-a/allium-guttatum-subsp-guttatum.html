Allium guttatum subsp. guttatum

Allium guttatum subsp.

guttatum, commonly known as the red bulbous onion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features bulbous stems that are often reddish-purple in color, with narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. It is characterized by its ability to form dense colonies and is often found in dry, rocky soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
