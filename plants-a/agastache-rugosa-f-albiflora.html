Agastache rugosa f. albiflora

Agastache rugosa f. albiflora, commonly known as whiteflower hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale lavender flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens and pollinator habitats.
