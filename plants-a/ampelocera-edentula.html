Ampelocera edentula

Ampelocera edentula, commonly known as the toothless marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in southern Africa.

It features a slender, erect stem that can grow up to 60 cm in height, adorned with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a dense spike, which bloom during the rainy season. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Ampelocera edentula plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various insect species.
