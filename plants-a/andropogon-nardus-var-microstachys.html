Andropogon nardus var. microstachys

Andropogon nardus var. microstachys, commonly known as dwarf switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, clustered flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, including erosion control and wildlife habitat.
