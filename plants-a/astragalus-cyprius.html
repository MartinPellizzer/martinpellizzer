Astragalus cyprius

Astragalus cyprius, commonly known as Cyprus milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Cyprus.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by pods containing seeds. Astragalus cyprius is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological and horticultural studies.
