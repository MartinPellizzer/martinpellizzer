Arethusa bulbosa

Arethusa bulbosa, commonly known as the pink root, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, sandy soils, often found in wetlands and along streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Arethusa bulbosa is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
