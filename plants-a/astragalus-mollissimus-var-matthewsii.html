Astragalus mollissimus var. matthewsii

Astragalus mollissimus var. matthewsii, commonly known as the velvet milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinators.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
