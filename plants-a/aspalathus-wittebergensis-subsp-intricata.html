Aspalathus wittebergensis subsp. intricata

Aspalathus wittebergensis subsp.

intricata, commonly known as the intricately branched bushveld pea, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has a compact growth habit with dense foliage and is often found in the fynbos vegetation type.

The subspecies is notable for its intricate branching pattern, which contributes to its ornamental value.
