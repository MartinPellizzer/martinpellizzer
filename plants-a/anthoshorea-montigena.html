Anthoshorea montigena

Anthoshorea montigena, commonly known as the mountain geranium, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky soils and cool, moist environments, making it well-adapted to mountainous climates. Its leaves are usually palmately lobed, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Anthoshorea montigena is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
