Alstroemeria apertiflora

Alstroemeria apertiflora, commonly known as the "Peruvian lily," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences. The plant typically features strap-like leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of hues. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Alstroemeria apertiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
