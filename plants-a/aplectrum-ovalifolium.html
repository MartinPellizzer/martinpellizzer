Aplectrum ovalifolium

Aplectrum ovalifolium, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, pinkish-purple pouch-shaped flower that resembles a slipper, which is a distinctive botanical feature. The plant has a single, upright stem with a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in pine or hardwood forests.

Aplectrum ovalifolium is a rare and protected species, highlighting its ecological and botanical significance.
