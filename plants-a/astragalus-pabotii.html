Astragalus pabotii

Astragalus pabotii, commonly known as the Pabotii milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to dry conditions. It produces pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind, contributing to its survival in harsh climates.

Astragalus pabotii is often found in rocky or sandy soils, reflecting its ecological niche in regions with limited water availability.
