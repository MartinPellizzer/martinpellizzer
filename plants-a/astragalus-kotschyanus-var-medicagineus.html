Astragalus kotschyanus var. medicagineus

Astragalus kotschyanus var. medicagineus, commonly known as the medicinal milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces small, round pods containing several seeds, and its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert regions.

This species is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
