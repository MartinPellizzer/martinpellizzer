Aristida boisii

Aristida boisii, commonly known as the narrowleaf threeawn, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and fine hairs along the edges.

Aristida boisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
