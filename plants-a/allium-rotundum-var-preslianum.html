Allium rotundum var. preslianum

Allium rotundum var. preslianum, commonly known as the Persian leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features bulbous roots and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often used in traditional cooking. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other allium species such as garlic and onions.
