Alhagi maurorum var. assyriacum

Alhagi maurorum var. assyriacum, commonly known as Assyrian goat's rue, is a perennial herb native to parts of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to dry conditions. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in clusters, and it has been used traditionally for medicinal purposes.

The species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, contributing to soil fertility.
