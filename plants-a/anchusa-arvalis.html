Anchusa arvalis

Anchusa arvalis, commonly known as the field anchusa, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils. The plant produces clusters of violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it has a deep taproot system.

Anchusa arvalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
