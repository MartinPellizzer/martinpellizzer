Anemone patula

Anemone patula, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features showy, single or double flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are basal and often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in native habitats.
