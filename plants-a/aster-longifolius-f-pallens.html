Aster longifolius f. pallens

Aster longifolius f. pallens, commonly known as the paleleaf aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features slender, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a pale green coloration. The flowers are typically blue to purple in color, forming clusters at the ends of the stems.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open fields.
