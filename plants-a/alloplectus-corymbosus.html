Alloplectus corymbosus

Alloplectus corymbosus, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Alloplectus corymbosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
