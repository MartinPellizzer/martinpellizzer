Abies pinsapo subsp. euhispanica

Abies pinsapo subsp.

euhispanica, commonly known as the Spanish fir, is a rare and endemic coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of southern Spain. It is characterized by its conical shape, dark green needle-like leaves, and distinctive cones that hang downward.

The species thrives in cool, moist climates at high altitudes, often found in forested areas with well-drained soils. Its botanical features include a rugged trunk and a dense canopy, which contribute to its ecological significance in the Iberian Peninsula.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
