Acacia fischeri

Acacia fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Acacia fischeri is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various desert animals.
