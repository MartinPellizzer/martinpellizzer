Agnus-castus alba

Agnus-castus alba, commonly known as the white chastetree, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate. The plant produces small, woody fruits called drupes, which contain a single seed. Agnus-castus alba is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
