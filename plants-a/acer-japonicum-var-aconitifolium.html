Acer japonicum var. aconitifolium

Acer japonicum var. aconitifolium, commonly known as the Japanese maple withaconitifolium foliage, is a deciduous ornamental tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 meters, with a spreading, rounded canopy. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow catkins that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The tree prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
