Arundinaria variegata f. tsuboii

Arundinaria variegata f. tsuboii, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Tsubo bamboo, is a unique variant of the silver bamboo species.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring a mix of green, white, and yellow hues on its leaves. This plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It grows as a clump-forming bamboo, with slender, upright stems that add a graceful appearance.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to various garden environments.
