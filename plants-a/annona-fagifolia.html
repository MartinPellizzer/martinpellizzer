Annona fagifolia

Annona fagifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pawpaw, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces a yellow, fleshy fruit that is edible and has a taste similar to a papaya. Its botanical features include a woody stem, palmate leaves, and a unique, star-shaped flower structure.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and has medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
