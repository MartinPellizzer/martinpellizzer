Anthopogon filiformis

Anthopogon filiformis, commonly known as the slender feather grass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It features delicate, thread-like leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter in width, giving it a fine, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in narrow spikelets, which are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ecological value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
