Avena montevidensis

Avena montevidensis, commonly known as the Uruguay oat, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Uruguay and Argentina.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Avena montevidensis is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

It has been studied for its potential use in forage and as a source of plant-based compounds.
