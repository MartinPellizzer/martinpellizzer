Aquilegia buriatica

Aquilegia buriatica, commonly known as the Japanese columbine, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and vibrant flower colors. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers bloom in early to mid-summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aquilegia buriatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
