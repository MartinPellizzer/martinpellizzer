Artemisia heptapotamica

Artemisia heptapotamica, commonly known as the Potamica wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open areas such as hillsides and grasslands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties.

Artemisia heptapotamica has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its antiseptic and digestive benefits.
