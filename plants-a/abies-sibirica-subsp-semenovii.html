Abies sibirica subsp. semenovii

Abies sibirica subsp.

semenovii, commonly known as the Siberian fir, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe and Central Asia. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that are dark green and often slightly twisted.

The cones are typically pendulous, with scales that open to release seeds, facilitating wind dispersal. This subspecies thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in high-altitude forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of boreal and subalpine ecosystems.
