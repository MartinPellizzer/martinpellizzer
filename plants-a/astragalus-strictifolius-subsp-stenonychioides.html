Astragalus strictifolius subsp. stenonychioides

Astragalus strictifolius subsp.

stenonychioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of western North America. It typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

The plant has slender, branched stems and small, compound leaves with a distinctive narrow shape. Its flowers are yellow or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, pod-like fruits.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
