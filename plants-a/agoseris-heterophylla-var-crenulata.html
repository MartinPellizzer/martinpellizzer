Agoseris heterophylla var. crenulata

Agoseris heterophylla var. crenulata, commonly known as the roughleaf goldenweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a rough, hairy texture. Its yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
