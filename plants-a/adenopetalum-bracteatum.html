Adenopetalum bracteatum

Adenopetalum bracteatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf mallow, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and can spread through both seeds and vegetative means.

Adenopetalum bracteatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
