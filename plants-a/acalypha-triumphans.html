Acalypha triumphans

Acalypha triumphans, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, papery bracts that resemble small lanterns, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers surrounded by the showy bracts. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Acalypha triumphans is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
