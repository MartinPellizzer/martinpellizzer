Astragalus eerqisiensis

Astragalus eerqisiensis, commonly known as the Eerqisi milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Xinjiang, China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions. It plays a role in local ecosystems by contributing to soil stabilization and supporting pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
