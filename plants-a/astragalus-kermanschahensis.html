Astragalus kermanschahensis

Astragalus kermanschahensis, commonly known as the Kermanshah milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of western Iran, specifically the Kermanshah province. This species is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves, typical of many Astragalus species. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered plant.
