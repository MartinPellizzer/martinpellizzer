Adenophora nikoensis f. globiflora

Adenophora nikoensis f. globiflora, commonly known as the globeflower adenophora, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded flower clusters that resemble small globes, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and is native to parts of East Asia, including Korea and China. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
