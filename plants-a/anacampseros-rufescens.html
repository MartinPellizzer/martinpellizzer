Anacampseros rufescens

Anacampseros rufescens, commonly known as the red sand verbena, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features fleshy, ribbed stems and produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers during the warmer months. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments by storing water in its thick leaves and stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and desert landscapes.
