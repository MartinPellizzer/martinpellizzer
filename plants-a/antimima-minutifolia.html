Antimima minutifolia

Antimima minutifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved antimima, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tiny, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is adapted to survive in low-nutrient soils and can endure prolonged periods of drought.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
