Acacia verticillata var. cephalantha

Acacia verticillata var. cephalantha, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and bright yellow flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has slender, wiry stems and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. Its flowers are clustered in spherical heads, and it is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
