Aconitum venustum

Aconitum venustum, commonly known as the violet aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces palmately divided leaves with a distinctive, waxy texture. Aconitum venustum is known for its toxic alkaloids, which make it a hazardous plant to handle.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
