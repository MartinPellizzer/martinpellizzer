Astragalus migpo

Astragalus migpo, commonly known as the Tibetan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain small, round seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Astragalus migpo is adapted to harsh, arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
