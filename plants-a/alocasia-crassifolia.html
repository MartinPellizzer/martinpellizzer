Alocasia crassifolia

Alocasia crassifolia, commonly known as the giant arrow leaf, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length, with a deep green color and a waxy texture. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture for its dramatic appearance.

Alocasia crassifolia is also valued for its air-purifying properties and ability to thrive in indoor settings with proper care.
