Alopecurus pratensis var. lobatus

Alopecurus pratensis var. lobatus, commonly known as the meadow foxtail, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in meadows, pastures, and wetlands.

Alopecurus pratensis var. lobatus is valued for its role in ecological systems and as a forage plant for livestock.
