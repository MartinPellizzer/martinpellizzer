Astragalus bigelovii

Astragalus bigelovii, commonly known as the bigelov's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert scrub habitats. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to cream-colored flowers in late spring. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Astragalus bigelovii is part of the legume family (Fabaceae) and has the ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
