Astragalus touranicus

Astragalus touranicus, commonly known as the Turanian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in dry climates.

Astragalus touranicus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
