Aureolaria intermedia

Aureolaria intermedia, commonly known as the pinkroot, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a deep taproot system, which allows it to thrive in dry, rocky soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Aureolaria intermedia is also known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal medicine.
