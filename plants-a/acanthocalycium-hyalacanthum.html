Acanthocalycium hyalacanthum

Acanthocalycium hyalacanthum, commonly known as the translucent cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, pale flowers. The plant is notable for its translucent, waxy coating on its stems, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It typically grows in rocky soils and thrives in full sun, requiring minimal water due to its succulent nature.

This species is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
