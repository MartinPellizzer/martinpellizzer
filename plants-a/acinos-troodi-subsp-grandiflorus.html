Acinos troodi subsp. grandiflorus

Acinos troodi subsp.

grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. Its leaves are basal, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

This species is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.
