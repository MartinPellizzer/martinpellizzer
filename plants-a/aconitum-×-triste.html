Aconitum × triste

Aconitum × triste, commonly known as the melancholy monk's hood, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep blue to purple, with a distinctive hood-like structure over the reproductive parts. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Native to the mountainous regions of Europe, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, though it is toxic and should be handled with care.
