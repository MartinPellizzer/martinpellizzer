Acrocephalus sericeus var. teucrioides

Acrocephalus sericeus var. teucrioides, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, wiry stems that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish-white flowers. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to its wetland environment, with a fibrous root system and rapid growth habits.
