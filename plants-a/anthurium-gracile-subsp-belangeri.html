Anthurium gracile subsp. belangeri

Anthurium gracile subsp.

belangeri, commonly known as the Belanger's slipper, is a rare and striking member of the Araceae family. It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The plant is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and distinctive, spadix-like inflorescence. Its flowers are typically small and greenish-white, with a spathe that can vary in color from white to reddish.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly valued in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
