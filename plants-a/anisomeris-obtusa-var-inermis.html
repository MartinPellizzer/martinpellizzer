Anisomeris obtusa var. inermis

Anisomeris obtusa var. inermis, commonly known as the Mexican dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in low-nutrient conditions.
