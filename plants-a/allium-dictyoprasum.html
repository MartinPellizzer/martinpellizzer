Allium dictyoprasum

Allium dictyoprasum, commonly known as the purple garlic or clustered garlic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces clusters of small, rounded bulbs that are often used as a culinary herb, similar to garlic and onions. The plant features grass-like leaves and small, pale purple flowers that bloom in the summer. It is valued for its aromatic properties and is often cultivated in gardens for both ornamental and edible purposes.

Allium dictyoprasum is a hardy plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
