Araucaria columnaris

Araucaria columnaris, commonly known as the Norfolk Island pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Norfolk Island in the South Pacific.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar shape and its distinctive, tiered arrangement of spirally arranged leaves. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 20 meters in its natural habitat. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The species is widely cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor environments.
