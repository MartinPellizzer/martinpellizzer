Achudemia subpubera

Achudemia subpubera, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are typically a deep green with a pubescent texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and vibrant blooms.
