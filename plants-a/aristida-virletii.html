Aristida virletii

Aristida virletii, commonly known as the slender dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are often tinged with reddish or purplish hues. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and restoration projects.
