Aciotis discolor var. martinicensis

Aciotis discolor var. martinicensis, commonly known as the Martinique dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its yellow flowers bloom in the spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
