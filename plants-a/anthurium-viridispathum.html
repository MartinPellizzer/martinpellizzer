Anthurium viridispathum

Anthurium viridispathum, commonly known as the green-veined anthurium, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with prominent, contrasting veins. The plant produces striking spadices, which are often red or orange, surrounded by a leaf-like bract. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Anthurium viridispathum is popular as a decorative plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
