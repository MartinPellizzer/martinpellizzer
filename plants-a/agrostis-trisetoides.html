Agrostis trisetoides

Agrostis trisetoides, commonly known as the slender bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along streambanks. The plant has slender, tufted stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Agrostis trisetoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
