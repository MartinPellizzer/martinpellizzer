Acacia hemignosta

Acacia hemignosta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, linear leaflets. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia hemignosta is also valued for its hardy nature and is often used in landscaping and for erosion control.
