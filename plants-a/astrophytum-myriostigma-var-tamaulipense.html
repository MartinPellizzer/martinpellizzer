Astrophytum myriostigma var. tamaulipense

Astrophytum myriostigma var. tamaulipense, commonly known as the Tamaulipan cactus, is a compact, spineless cactus native to the Tamaulipan region of Mexico.

It features a globular stem covered in white, radial spines and a crown of yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a shallow root system and is adapted to arid environments with minimal rainfall. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in rocky soils and full sun conditions.
