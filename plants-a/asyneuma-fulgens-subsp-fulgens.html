Asyneuma fulgens subsp. fulgens

Asyneuma fulgens subsp.

fulgens, commonly known as the golden dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking appearance.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and somewhat hairy.

It is an annual species, completing its life cycle within a single growing season.
