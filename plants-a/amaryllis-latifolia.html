Amaryllis latifolia

Amaryllis latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features broad, strap-like leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length, and its flowers are typically red or orange with a yellow throat. The plant produces clustered, showy blooms that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Amaryllis latifolia is known for its long flowering period, often blooming from late winter through spring.
