Alyssum virgatum var. mutabile

Alyssum virgatum var. mutabile, commonly known as the variable alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its ornamental value.

Its variability in flower color and growth habit makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy and adaptable plant.
