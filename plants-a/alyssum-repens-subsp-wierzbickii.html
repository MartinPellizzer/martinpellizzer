Alyssum repens subsp. wierzbickii

Alyssum repens subsp.

wierzbickii, commonly known as the Wierzbickii sand verbena, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring and early summer.

The plant has compact, mat-forming foliage with narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
