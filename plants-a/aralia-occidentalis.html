Aralia occidentalis

Aralia occidentalis, commonly known as western aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves that are typically divided into several narrow, lance-shaped segments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbel-like clusters, which are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The plant has a relatively slow growth rate and is valued for its ornamental foliage and ecological benefits.
