Aethionema grandiflorum var. grandiflorum

Aethionema grandiflorum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the Armenian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of bright pink or red flowers. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
