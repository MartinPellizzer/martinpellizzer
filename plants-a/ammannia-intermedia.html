Ammannia intermedia

Ammannia intermedia, commonly known as prairie ammannia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, branching stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered white or pink flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Ammannia intermedia plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments and provides habitat for various insects and wildlife.
