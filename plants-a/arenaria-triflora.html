Arenaria triflora

Arenaria triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters of three, giving it its common name. Its leaves are typically linear and covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand cold and dry conditions.

Arenaria triflora thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
