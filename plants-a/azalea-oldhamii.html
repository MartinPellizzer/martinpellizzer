Azalea oldhamii

Azalea oldhamii, commonly known as the Chinese azalea, is a species native to China and belongs to the Rhododendron genus.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, broad leaves that are usually dark green. The plant produces showy clusters of flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Azalea oldhamii thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
