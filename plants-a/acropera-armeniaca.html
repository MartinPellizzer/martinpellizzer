Acropera armeniaca

Acropera armeniaca, commonly known as Armenian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Acropera armeniaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
