Acacia cyclocarpa

Acacia cyclocarpa, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces woody pods that contain seeds, and it is well adapted to dry, sandy soils. Acacia cyclocarpa is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in soil enrichment.

Its ecological and cultural significance makes it a notable species in Australian flora.
