Acaciella ciliata

Acaciella ciliata, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Acaciella ciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and restoration projects.
