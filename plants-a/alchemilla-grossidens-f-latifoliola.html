Alchemilla grossidens f. latifoliola

Alchemilla grossidens f. latifoliola, commonly known as the broad-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, waxy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces upright flowering stems topped with small, clustered yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its foliage is used in ornamental gardening for its attractive, long-lasting appearance.
