Achillea

Achillea, commonly known as yarrow, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It consists of about 100 species, most of which are native to Europe, Asia, and North America. The plants typically have feathery, aromatic leaves and flat, composite flower heads that range in color from white to pink and red. They are often found in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Achillea species are valued for their ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
