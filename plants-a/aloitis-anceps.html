Aloitis anceps

Aloitis anceps, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves and showy flowers. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are typically blue-gray in color, covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss.

Aloitis anceps is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
