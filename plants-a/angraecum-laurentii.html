Angraecum laurentii

Angraecum laurentii, commonly known as the star orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue. The plant produces its showy blooms at night, attracting specific nocturnal pollinators like the hawkmoth. Its flowers have a unique structure with long nectar-filled spurs that match the feeding habits of its pollinators.

Angraecum laurentii is also noted for its ability to survive in dry conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
