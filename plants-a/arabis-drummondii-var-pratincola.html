Arabis drummondii var. pratincola

Arabis drummondii var. pratincola, commonly known as Drummond's rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features basal leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape, often with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
