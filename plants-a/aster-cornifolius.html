Aster cornifolius

Aster cornifolius, commonly known as the horned aster, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, sharply lobed leaves that resemble horns, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, violet-blue to purple flower heads in the fall, which attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded to partially shaded areas.

Aster cornifolius is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower structures and medicinal properties in some species.
