Anaphalis gracilis

Anaphalis gracilis, commonly known as the slender woollyflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, woolly foliage and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its flowers are often clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Anaphalis gracilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
