Amomum echinocarpum

Amomum echinocarpum, commonly known as the spiky cardamom or wild cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that develop into spiky, red or brown fruits. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a fragrant, spicy scent.

The rhizomes and fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to cardamom.
