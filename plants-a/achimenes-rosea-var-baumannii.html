Achimenes rosea var. baumannii

Achimenes rosea var. baumannii, commonly known as the Baumann's velvetleaf, is a hybrid species within the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. Native to tropical regions of South America, this plant thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage and prolonged blooming period.

The variety is noted for its compact growth habit and adaptability to container gardening.
