Alstonia godeffroyi

Alstonia godeffroyi, commonly known as the African coral tree, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, coral-like inflorescences that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant display to its canopy. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets, and its bark is often smooth and light-colored. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Alstonia godeffroyi is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
