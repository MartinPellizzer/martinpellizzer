Actinodaphne amabilis

Actinodaphne amabilis, commonly known as the fragrant laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by oval, reddish-brown berries. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
