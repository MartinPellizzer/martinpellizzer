Acacia subflexuosa

Acacia subflexuosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging branches and narrow, dark green leaves. The plant produces yellow flower spikes during the autumn and winter seasons, which are highly attractive to pollinators. Acacia subflexuosa is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
