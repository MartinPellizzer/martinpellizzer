Amaranthus rugulosus

Amaranthus rugulosus, commonly known as rough amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish to reddish flowers arranged in dense clusters, followed by triangular, three-angled seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its ornamental value and nutritional content, it is cultivated in some regions for both decorative and edible purposes.
