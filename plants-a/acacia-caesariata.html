Acacia caesariata

Acacia caesariata, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow floral spikes that appear in spring, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

Acacia caesariata plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
