Avena mutata

Avena mutata, commonly known as the variable oat, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a rough texture.

Avena mutata is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
