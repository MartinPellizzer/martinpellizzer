Allophylus africanus var. africanus

Allophylus africanus var. africanus, commonly known as the African alder, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, simple leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, spherical fruits containing one or two seeds. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including forest edges, rocky soils, and disturbed areas.

The species is valued for its hardy nature and potential use in reforestation projects.
