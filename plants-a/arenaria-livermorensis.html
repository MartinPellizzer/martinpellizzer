Arenaria livermorensis

Arenaria livermorensis, commonly known as the Livermore sandflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and coastal dunes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and ecological adaptations to arid environments.
