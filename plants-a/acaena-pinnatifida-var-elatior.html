Acaena pinnatifida var. elatior

Acaena pinnatifida var. elatior, commonly known as the tall bentweed, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly, bristly stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright orange to red berries. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and have serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forest margins and along stream banks.
