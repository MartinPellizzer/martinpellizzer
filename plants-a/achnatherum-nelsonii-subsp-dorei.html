Achnatherum nelsonii subsp. dorei

Achnatherum nelsonii subsp.

dorei, commonly known as the Dore's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and meadows.

The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in arid conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and restoration projects.
