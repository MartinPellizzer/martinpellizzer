Anemopaegma album var. quinquedentata

Anemopaegma album var. quinquedentata, commonly known as the white anemopaegma, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, erect stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shady garden environments.
