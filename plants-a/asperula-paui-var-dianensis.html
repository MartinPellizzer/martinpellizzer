Asperula paui var. dianensis

Asperula paui var. dianensis, commonly known as the Dianense Azalea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white during the spring season. Its leaves are typically elliptical and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the blooms.

Asperula paui var. dianensis thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
