Aleuritia valentiniana

Aleuritia valentiniana, commonly known as the Valentine's Day shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aleuritia valentiniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in garden settings.
