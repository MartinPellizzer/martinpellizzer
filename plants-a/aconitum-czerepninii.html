Aconitum czerepninii

Aconitum czerepninii, commonly known as the Czerepnin'saconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments, and are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Aconitum czerepninii is adapted to grow in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
