Asarca magellanica

Asarca magellanica, commonly known as the Magellanic asarum, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the family Aristolochiaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, aromatic, and often reddish-brown flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its tuberous roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
