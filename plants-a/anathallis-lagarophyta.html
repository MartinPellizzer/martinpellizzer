Anathallis lagarophyta

Anathallis lagarophyta, commonly known as the orchid with the peculiar appearance of a small, greenish-white flower, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has a short, erect stem with a single flower that blooms in a spiral arrangement. Its botanical features include a sympodial growth pattern and a specialized root system adapted for nutrient absorption.

The species is noted for its unique floral structure and is often found in association with specific mycorrhizal fungi.
