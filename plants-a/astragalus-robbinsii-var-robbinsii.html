Astragalus robbinsii var. robbinsii

Astragalus robbinsii var. robbinsii, commonly known as Robbin's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, hairy stems and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and supports local wildlife.
