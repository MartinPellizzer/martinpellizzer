Agropyron aemulans

Agropyron aemulans, commonly known as the western wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie ecosystems.

Agropyron aemulans is valued for its ecological importance and is used in restoration projects due to its drought tolerance and soil stabilization capabilities.
