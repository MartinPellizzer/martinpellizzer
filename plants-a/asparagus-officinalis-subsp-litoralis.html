Asparagus officinalis subsp. litoralis

Asparagus officinalis subsp.

litoralis, commonly known as coastal asparagus, is a subspecies native to the coastal regions of Europe and North Africa. It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, fern-like foliage and edible, needle-like stems.

This plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to saline and coastal environments. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red berries.

Coastal asparagus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional cuisine.
