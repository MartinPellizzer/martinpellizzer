Arundinaria longifolia

Arundinaria longifolia, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or longleaf bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and long, narrow leaves that can reach several meters in length. This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Arundinaria longifolia is also valued for its role in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
