Aster fontinalis

Aster fontinalis, commonly known as the spring beauty, is a small flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of white and pink, usually blooming in early spring. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system.

Aster fontinalis is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its aesthetic appeal in natural landscapes.
