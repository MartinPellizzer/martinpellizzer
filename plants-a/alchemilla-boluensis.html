Alchemilla boluensis

Alchemilla boluensis, commonly known as the Bolu lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Alchemilla boluensis thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.
