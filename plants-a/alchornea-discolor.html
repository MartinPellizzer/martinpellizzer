Alchornea discolor

Alchornea discolor, commonly known as the whiteleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often pubescent and have a distinct white or pale underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Alchornea discolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
