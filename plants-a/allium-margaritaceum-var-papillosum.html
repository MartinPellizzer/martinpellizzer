Allium margaritaceum var. papillosum

Allium margaritaceum var. papillosum, commonly known as the pearl onion leek, is a variety of the Allium genus native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and broad, flat leaves that resemble those of leeks. The plant produces small, round bulbs that are often used as ornamental or edible components in various cuisines. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, forming loose clusters at the top of the stem.

The botanical features of this plant make it both visually appealing and useful in culinary applications.
