Amyema tenuisepala

Amyema tenuisepala, commonly known as the slender-winged mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that primarily grows on eucalyptus trees in southern Australia.

It has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence, and its flowers are typically white or pale green, arranged in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are dispersed by birds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Its unique botanical features make it an important component of the ecological systems it inhabits.
