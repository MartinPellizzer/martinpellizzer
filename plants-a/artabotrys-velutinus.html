Artabotrys velutinus

Artabotrys velutinus, commonly known as the velvet flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant, orange-red flowers that bloom in panicles, often appearing in late winter to early spring. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a velvety texture that gives the plant its common name. The tree produces small, round, yellowish fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types in suitable climates.
