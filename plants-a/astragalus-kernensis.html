Astragalus kernensis

Astragalus kernensis, commonly known as the Kern's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Nevada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Astragalus kernensis is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria, contributing to soil fertility.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various herbivores.
