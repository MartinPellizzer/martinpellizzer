Armeria bupleuroides

Armeria bupleuroides, commonly known as the sea thrift, is a low-growing evergreen plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has succulent, needle-like leaves that help it retain moisture in its saline, coastal environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscaping.

Its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh weather conditions contribute to its ecological and horticultural significance.
