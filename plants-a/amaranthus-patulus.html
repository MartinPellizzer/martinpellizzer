Amaranthus patulus

Amaranthus patulus, commonly known as the slender amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or red in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for cultivation in arid environments.

Amaranthus patulus is often used as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and resilience.
