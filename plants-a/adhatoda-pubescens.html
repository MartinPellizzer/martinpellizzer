Adhatoda pubescens

Adhatoda pubescens, commonly known as the Indian Trumpet or Malabar Nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually purple or blue in color, blooming in clusters. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a woody stem with a rough texture.

Adhatoda pubescens is often found in moist, shaded areas and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
