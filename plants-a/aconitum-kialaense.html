Aconitum kialaense

Aconitum kialaense, commonly known as the Kiala monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Eastern African region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
