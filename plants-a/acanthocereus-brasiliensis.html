Acanthocereus brasiliensis

Acanthocereus brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian night-blooming cactus, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a columnar cactus with ribbed stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators like bats and moths. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Its adaptations to arid environments include thick, water-storing stems and spines for protection.
