Aristida alopecuroides

Aristida alopecuroides, commonly known as bluegrass or slender grass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to bluish in color. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-gray appearance, which helps it retain moisture in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native landscapes.
