Anthericum monophyllum

Anthericum monophyllum, commonly known as the single-leaf lily, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its single, broad, lance-shaped leaf that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces a tall, slender stem topped with a single, showy white or pale pink flower that resembles a lily. It blooms during the summer months and is adapted to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
