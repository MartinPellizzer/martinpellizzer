Arctium minus f. tomentosum

Arctium minus f. tomentosum, commonly known as the hairy burdock, is a variant of the common burdock plant.

It is a biennial herbaceous plant that belongs to the Asteraceae family. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and spiny, woody fruit heads that contain seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

The hairy texture of its stems and leaves is a distinguishing feature of this subspecies.
