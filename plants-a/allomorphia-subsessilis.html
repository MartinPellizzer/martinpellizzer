Allomorphia subsessilis

Allomorphia subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved allomorph, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow, sessile, and often arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom during the rainy season. It has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through deep root systems and reduced water loss mechanisms.

Allomorphia subsessilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes.
