Agrostemma pyrenaicum

Agrostemma pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean flax, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula and southern France.

It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its seeds are enclosed in small, capsule-like fruits.

Agrostemma pyrenaicum is often found in open habitats such as scrublands and grassy slopes.
