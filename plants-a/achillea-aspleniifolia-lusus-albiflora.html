Achillea aspleniifolia lusus albiflora

Achillea aspleniifolia lusus albiflora, commonly known as the white clover yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
