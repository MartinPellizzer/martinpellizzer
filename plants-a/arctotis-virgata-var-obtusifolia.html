Arctotis virgata var. obtusifolia

Arctotis virgata var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved arctotis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The leaves are typically broad and rounded, with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
