Acacia obovata

Acacia obovata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, obovate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of whitish hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Acacia obovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
