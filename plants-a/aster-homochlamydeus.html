Aster homochlamydeus

Aster homochlamydeus, commonly known as the smooth disk aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, producing a showy display of purple to pink hues.

The species is notable for its simple, smooth appearance and is often found in shaded to partially sunny environments.
