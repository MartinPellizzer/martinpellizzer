Alchemilla microphylla

Alchemilla microphylla, commonly known as lesser lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features heart-shaped, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in loose inflorescences.

Alchemilla microphylla is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
