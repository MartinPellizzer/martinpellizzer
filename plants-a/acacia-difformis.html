Acacia difformis

Acacia difformis, commonly known as the weeping acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its drooping, feathery foliage and yellow flowers that appear in spring. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that function as leaves, and it produces pod-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Acacia difformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
