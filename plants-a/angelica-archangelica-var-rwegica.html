Angelica archangelica var. rwegica

Angelica archangelica var. rwegica, commonly known as the Siberian angelica, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Russia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height, covered with coarse hairs. The plant features large, compound leaves with a strong, aromatic scent, and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in umbels. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

This variety is adapted to harsh environments and is often found in moist, shaded areas of boreal forests.
