Arkezostis pilosa

Arkezostis pilosa, commonly known as the hairy rockrose, is a flowering plant native to arid and rocky environments.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense covering of fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with thick leaves and deep root systems.

Arkezostis pilosa is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
