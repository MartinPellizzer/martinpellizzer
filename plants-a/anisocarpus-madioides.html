Anisocarpus madioides

Anisocarpus madioides, commonly known as the desert grass or madioides grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, wiry leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family Poaceae. Its seeds are small and often dispersed by wind, aiding in its survival in harsh environments.

Anisocarpus madioides is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in dryland ecosystems.
