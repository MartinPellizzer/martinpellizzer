Azalea rollissonii

Azalea rollissonii, commonly known as the Red Hills azalea, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils in forested areas. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, blooming in late spring. Its leaves are elliptical and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Azalea rollissonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
