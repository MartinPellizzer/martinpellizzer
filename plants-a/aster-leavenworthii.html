Aster leavenworthii

Aster leavenworthii, commonly known as the Leavenworth's aster, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster leavenworthii is often found in prairie and wetland ecosystems, where it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
