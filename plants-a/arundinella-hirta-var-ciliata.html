Arundinella hirta var. ciliata

Arundinella hirta var. ciliata, commonly known as the hairy bamboo or velvet grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy surface that contributes to its distinctive appearance.

This species is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
