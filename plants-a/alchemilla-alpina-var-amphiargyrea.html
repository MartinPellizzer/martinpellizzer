Alchemilla alpina var. amphiargyrea

Alchemilla alpina var. amphiargyrea, commonly known as the Alpine Lady's Mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, velvety, shield-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh alpine conditions.
