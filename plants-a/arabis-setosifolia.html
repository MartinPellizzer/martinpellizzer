Arabis setosifolia

Arabis setosifolia, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbages and mustards. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Arabis setosifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
