Artocarpus riedelii

Artocarpus riedelii, commonly known as the Riedel's breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive fruit. The fruit, which is typically green when mature, turns yellow or orange when ripe and is edible. The plant is valued for its nutritious fruit and is often grown in agroforestry systems.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
