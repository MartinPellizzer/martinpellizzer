Avena alba subsp. abyssinica

Avena alba subsp.

abyssinica, commonly known as Ethiopian oat, is a perennial grass native to Ethiopia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in panicle inflorescences, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

The subspecies is valued for its potential in forage and traditional uses in local communities.
