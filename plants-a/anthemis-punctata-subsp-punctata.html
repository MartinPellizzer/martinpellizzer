Anthemis punctata subsp. punctata

Anthemis punctata subsp.

punctata, commonly known as the spotted tansy, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. Its leaves are finely toothed and covered in a dense layer of white hairs, while its yellow flowers are arranged in flat-topped clusters.

The subspecies is distinguished by its distinctive yellow-green spots on the leaves, which give it its common name.
