Arthromeris wallichiana var. biserialis

Arthromeris wallichiana var. biserialis, commonly known as the Himalayan spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
