Aster × hashimotoi

Aster × hashimotoi, commonly known as the Hashimoto's aster, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. Its foliage consists of alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color.

The flowers are usually blue to purple in hue, with a yellow center, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
