Agathosma ambigua var. major

Agathosma ambigua var. major, commonly known as Cape spearmint, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and perfumery. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a ground cover in Mediterranean climates.
