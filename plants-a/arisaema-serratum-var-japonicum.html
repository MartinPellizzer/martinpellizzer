Arisaema serratum var. japonicum

Arisaema serratum var. japonicum, commonly known as Japanese snake root, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
