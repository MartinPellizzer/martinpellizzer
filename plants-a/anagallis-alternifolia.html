Anagallis alternifolia

Anagallis alternifolia, commonly known as the mouse-ear or mouse-ear chickweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb with opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers with five petals, typically blooming from spring to summer. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, along paths, and in woodland edges.

Anagallis alternifolia is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
