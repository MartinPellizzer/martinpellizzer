Acacia spondylophylla

Acacia spondylophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, yellow flowers and feathery leaves that are often mistaken for those of other acacia species. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in degraded landscapes.
