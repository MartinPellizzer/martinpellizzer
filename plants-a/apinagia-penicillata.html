Apinagia penicillata

Apinagia penicillata, commonly known as the slender pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or alpine habitats. It produces small, achene-like fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Apinagia penicillata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
