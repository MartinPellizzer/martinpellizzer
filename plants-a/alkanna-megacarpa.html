Alkanna megacarpa

Alkanna megacarpa, commonly known as the Indian madder, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in the spring. Its roots contain a red dye called alkanet, which has been historically used for coloring textiles and food.

Alkanna megacarpa thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to its native environment.
