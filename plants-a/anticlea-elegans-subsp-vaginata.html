Anticlea elegans subsp. vaginata

Anticlea elegans subsp.

vaginata, commonly known as the velvetleaf antirrhinum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America. It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence.

The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, with a distinctive spurred structure. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
