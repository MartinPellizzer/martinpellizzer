Aspalathus staurantha

Aspalathus staurantha, commonly known as the silver leaf bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its small, pale yellow flowers. The plant is often found in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid conditions. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.

Aspalathus staurantha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
