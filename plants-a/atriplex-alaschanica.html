Atriplex alaschanica

Atriplex alaschanica, commonly known as the desert saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, salt-tolerant leaves that help it survive in saline soils. The plant produces small greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It has a deep root system that enables it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Atriplex alaschanica plays an important role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitats.
