Aegilops triaristata subsp. contracta

Aegilops triaristata subsp.

contracta, commonly known as the narrow-leafed goatee, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with three to five bracts. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry, rocky habitats.

Botanically, it is notable for its genetic relationships to wheat and its potential use in breeding programs for improved cereal crops.
