Actaea arguta

Actaea arguta, commonly known as white crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The berries it produces are red and edible, though they are often consumed by birds and other wildlife.
