Andropogon condensatus subsp. corymbosus

Andropogon condensatus subsp.

corymbosus, commonly known as the tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the eastern and central United States. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species typically grows in moist to wet soils, such as those found in swamps, marshes, and along riverbanks. It produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers during the summer months. Andropogon condensatus subsp.

corymbosus plays an important ecological role in wetland habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
