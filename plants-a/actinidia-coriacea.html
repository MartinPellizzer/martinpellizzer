Actinidia coriacea

Actinidia coriacea, commonly known as the rough actinidia, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by edible, fuzzy berries that ripen in late summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food for wildlife.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its adaptability to various soil types and climates.
