Avena flavescens var. villosa

Avena flavescens var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy wild oat, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-bearing flowers, which are typically greenish or pale yellow in color. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in other open habitats.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become a weed in agricultural settings.
