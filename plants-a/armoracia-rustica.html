Armoracia rustica

Armoracia rustica, commonly known as horseradish, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its deep taproot, which is the primary source of its pungent flavor. The plant has dark green, lobed leaves that are alternate and can grow up to 30 cm in length. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are arranged in umbels and bloom in the summer.

Horseradish is often cultivated for its root, which is used in culinary applications and traditional medicine.
