Anthoxanthum equisetum

Anthoxanthum equisetum, commonly known as the common bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

Anthoxanthum equisetum is known for its adaptability and is sometimes used in horticulture for erosion control.
