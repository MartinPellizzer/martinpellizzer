Argentina assimilis

Argentina assimilis, commonly known as the Argentine daisy, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and has a compact, bushy growth habit. It produces small, inconspicuous fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Argentina assimilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
