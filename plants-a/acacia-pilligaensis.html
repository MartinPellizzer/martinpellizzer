Acacia pilligaensis

Acacia pilligaensis, commonly known as the Pilliga acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Pilliga Forest in New South Wales, Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow, globular flowers that appear in late winter to early spring, followed by flat, woody pods containing seeds. It is adapted to dry, open habitats and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Acacia pilligaensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
