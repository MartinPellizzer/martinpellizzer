Astragalus trichopodus var. capillipes

Astragalus trichopodus var. capillipes, commonly known as the silky milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with fine, hairy textures, which give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
