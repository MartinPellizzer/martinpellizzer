Asplenium × clermontiae nothosubsp. clermontiae

Asplenium × clermontiae nothosubsp.

clermontiae, commonly known as the hybrid maidenhair fern, is a sterile hybrid resulting from the cross between *Asplenium trichomanes* and *Asplenium bulbiferum*. It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble those of its parent species, with a distinctive blackish-brown coloration on the underside of the leaves.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. It lacks true spores and reproduces vegetatively through rhizome growth, making it a non-flowering, sporeless fern.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
