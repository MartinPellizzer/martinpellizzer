Acrophorus blumei

Acrophorus blumei, commonly known as the white-flowered acanthus, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers with prominent bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in horticulture.
