Arachis batizocoi

Arachis batizocoi, commonly known as the Batizocoi groundnut, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds, similar to other groundnut species. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its genetic uniqueness, it is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers.
