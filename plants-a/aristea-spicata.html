Aristea spicata

Aristea spicata, commonly known as the spike aristea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant produces dense clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, arid environments.

Aristea spicata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
