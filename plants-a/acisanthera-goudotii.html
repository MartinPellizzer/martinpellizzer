Acisanthera goudotii

Acisanthera goudotii, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Acisanthera goudotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
