Agapetes kanjilalii

Agapetes kanjilalii, commonly known as the Kanjilal's silverleaf, is a species of evergreen shrub belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in India and Myanmar. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous forests.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife.
