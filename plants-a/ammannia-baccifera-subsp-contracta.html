Ammannia baccifera subsp. contracta

Ammannia baccifera subsp.

contracta, commonly known as the narrowleaf bladderwort, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as marshes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

The plant features slender, branching stems with small, opposite leaves that are usually less than 1 cm in length. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, with distinctive pink to red petals and yellow stamens.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
