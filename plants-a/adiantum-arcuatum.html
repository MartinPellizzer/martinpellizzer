Adiantum arcuatum

Adiantum arcuatum, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that resemble the hair of a maiden, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its leaves are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive black midrib that contrasts with the pale green fronds.

Adiantum arcuatum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
