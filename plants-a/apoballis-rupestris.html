Apoballis rupestris

Apoballis rupestris, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, hairy leaves and produces a capsule-like fruit that contains numerous seeds.

Apoballis rupestris is often found in fynbos vegetation and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
