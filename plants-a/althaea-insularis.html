Althaea insularis

Althaea insularis, commonly known as the island marshmallow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other plants like hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its showy, pink or white flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Althaea insularis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along streambanks.
