Arabis vochinensis

Arabis vochinensis, commonly known as the alpine rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.
