Artanthe xestophylla

Artanthe xestophylla, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the cooler months. Its unique bark, which has a reddish hue, is highly valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Artanthe xestophylla thrives in well-drained soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.
