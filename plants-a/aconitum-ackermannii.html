Aconitum ackermannii

Aconitum ackermannii, commonly known as Ackermann's aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, spire-like clusters. Its leaves are deeply divided into lance-shaped lobes, and the plant is known for its highly toxic alkaloids, which make it a dangerous species to handle.

Aconitum ackermannii thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
