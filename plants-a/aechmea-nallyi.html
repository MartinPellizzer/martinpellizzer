Aechmea nallyi

Aechmea nallyi, commonly known as the Nally's bromeliad, is a species native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It is a striking plant with a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces vibrant orange-red inflorescences that bloom in late summer. Its foliage is typically covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention and protection from intense sunlight.

Aechmea nallyi is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea nallyi has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, sword-like, and often have a waxy surface, arranged in a compact cluster at the base of the plant.

The leaves are typically green with a reddish or bronze hue on the underside, and they can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant has a short, fibrous stem that is often hidden beneath the leaves, with a rough, textured bark that is dark brown in color. Its inflorescence consists of a tall, spiky flower stalk bearing white or pale purple flowers that bloom during the dry season, primarily pollinated by insects.

The plant produces berries that contain seeds dispersed by wind and animals.
