Artemisia lobelii var. canescens

Artemisia lobelii var. canescens, commonly known as the grayleaf sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, woolly hairs, providing it with a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open plains.

Its aromatic properties and drought tolerance make it an important component of native plant communities.
