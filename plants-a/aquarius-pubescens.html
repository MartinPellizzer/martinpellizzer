Aquarius pubescens

Aquarius pubescens, commonly known as the hairy aquarius, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is native to specific regions in South America. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its hairy leaves provide a tactile texture and help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Aquarius pubescens is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
