Adonis flammea f. straminea

Adonis flammea f. straminea, commonly known as the yellow adonis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that produces striking yellow flowers in early spring. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
