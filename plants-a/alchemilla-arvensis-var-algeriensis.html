Alchemilla arvensis var. algeriensis

Alchemilla arvensis var. algeriensis, commonly known as Algerian lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in rocky or limestone soils.

Its foliage is known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
