Alchornea hirtella f. comoensis

Alchornea hirtella f. comoensis, commonly known as the Comoensis variant of the hairy alchornea, is a shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves that are often glandular-dotted. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
