Allium koelzii

Allium koelzii, commonly known as the Koelz's garlic, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, growing from a bulbous base.

Allium koelzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
