Aconitum pseudogeniculatum var. pubipes

Aconitum pseudogeniculatum var. pubipes, commonly known as the pubescent monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has large, palmately compound leaves with a distinctive wavy margin. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
