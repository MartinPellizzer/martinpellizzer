Alyssum strigosum subsp. cedrorum

Alyssum strigosum subsp.

cedrorum, commonly known as the Sierra allyssum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a low-growing perennial with dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Alyssum strigosum subsp.

cedrorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
