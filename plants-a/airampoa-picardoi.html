Airampoa picardoi

Airampoa picardoi, commonly known as the Madagascar grass, is a perennial grass species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories. It has small, spike-like inflorescences that produce wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
