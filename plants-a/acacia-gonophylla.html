Acacia gonophylla

Acacia gonophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a small, multi-stemmed shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and compound leaves composed of multiple small, linear leaflets. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that appear in late summer, adding a striking visual contrast to its grey-green foliage. Acacia gonophylla is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions, with deep root systems that help it access groundwater.

It is also an important plant for local wildlife, providing nectar for insects and birds.
