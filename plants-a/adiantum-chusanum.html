Adiantum chusanum

Adiantum chusanum, commonly known as the Chinese maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern species native to East Asia.

It features finely divided, bi-lobed fronds that are typically light green and have a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds, which are covered in small, specialized structures called sporangia.

Adiantum chusanum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and indoor spaces.
