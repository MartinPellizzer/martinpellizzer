Acacia multiflora

Acacia multiflora, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are grey-green in color, providing a striking visual contrast. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which bloom in spring and attract a variety of pollinators. Acacia multiflora is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and reforestation projects.
