Astragalus monspessulanus f. chaubardii

Astragalus monspessulanus f. chaubardii, commonly known as the Chaubard's milkvetch, is a rare and distinctive plant species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in sunny, arid environments. Its leaves are pinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.
