Artemisia dolichocephala

Artemisia dolichocephala, commonly known as narrow-headed wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in autumn. The plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its leaves contain volatile oils, which contribute to its strong, medicinal scent.

Artemisia dolichocephala is used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
