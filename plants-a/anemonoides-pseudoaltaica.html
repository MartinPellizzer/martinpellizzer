Anemonoides pseudoaltaica

Anemonoides pseudoaltaica, commonly known as the Altai anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of elongated, hairy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with numerous petal-like sepals, arranged in a single layer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to cold climates.

Anemonoides pseudoaltaica is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
