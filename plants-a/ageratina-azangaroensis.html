Ageratina azangaroensis

Ageratina azangaroensis, commonly known as the Azangaro blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and shrublands, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its small, blue to purple flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, making it visually striking.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
