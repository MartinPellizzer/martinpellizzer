Amaranthus blitum subsp. oleraceus

Amaranthus blitum subsp.

oleraceus, commonly known as spinach amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features alternate, broadly ovate leaves that are typically smooth or slightly hairy.

The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers, followed by three-winged seeds. It is valued for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients such as iron and protein.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common weed in agricultural fields and gardens.
