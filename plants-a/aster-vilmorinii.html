Aster vilmorinii

Aster vilmorinii, commonly known as the Vilmorin's aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features showy blue to purple ray florets surrounding yellow disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
