Actinotus leucocephalus var. nanella

Actinotus leucocephalus var. nanella, commonly known as the silver scurfpea, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Australia.

It features small, white, fluffy flowers that resemble a crown, giving it its common name. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered in a silvery, waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
