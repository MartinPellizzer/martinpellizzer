Azarolus crataegoides var. florifera

Azarolus crataegoides var. florifera, commonly known as the flowering azarolus, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and inland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
