Arophyton buchetii

Arophyton buchetii, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Arophyton buchetii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in full sun.

It prefers well-drained soil and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
