Arundinaria bitchuensis

Arundinaria bitchuensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Bitchu bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional Japanese crafts and as a food source.

Arundinaria bitchuensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and its aesthetic appeal in ornamental gardening.
