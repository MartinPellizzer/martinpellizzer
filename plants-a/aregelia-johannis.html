Aregelia johannis

Aregelia johannis, commonly known as the Johannes' arum, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by a showy, petal-like structure.

Aregelia johannis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
