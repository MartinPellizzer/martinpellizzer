Archanthemis marschalliana subsp. sosnovskyana

Archanthemis marschalliana subsp.

sosnovskyana, commonly known as the Sosnovsky's tansy, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, tubular flowers arranged in clusters.

This subspecies is often found in steppe regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
