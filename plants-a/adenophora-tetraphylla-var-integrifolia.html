Adenophora tetraphylla var. integrifolia

Adenophora tetraphylla var. integrifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually entire or slightly toothed. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue or pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
