Acacia intsia var. caesia

Acacia intsia var. caesia, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

The golden wattle is an important species in Australian flora and is often used in reforestation efforts.
