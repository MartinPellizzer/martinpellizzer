Acrolophia

Acrolophia, commonly known as the star orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, where it thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plants are characterized by their compact growth habit and showy, star-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors. Acrolophia species typically have thick, fleshy leaves adapted to store water, and they produce small, capsule-like fruits.

These orchids are popular among horticulturists for their unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
