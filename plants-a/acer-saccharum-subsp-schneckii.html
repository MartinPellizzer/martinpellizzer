Acer saccharum subsp. schneckii

Acer saccharum subsp.

schneckii, commonly known as the red maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its vibrant autumn foliage, which ranges from bright orange to deep red.

The tree has opposite, palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and it produces small, red to yellowish flowers in early spring. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
