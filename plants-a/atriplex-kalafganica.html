Atriplex kalafganica

Atriplex kalafganica, commonly known as Kalafgan saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. The plant is adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable species for stabilizing degraded landscapes. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as forage for livestock.

Atriplex kalafganica plays an important ecological role in desert ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
