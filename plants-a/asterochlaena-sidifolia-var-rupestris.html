Asterochlaena sidifolia var. rupestris

Asterochlaena sidifolia var. rupestris, commonly known as the rock aster, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a compact, branching structure with thick, fleshy stems that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
