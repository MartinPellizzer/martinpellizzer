Acacia nilotica var. kirkii

Acacia nilotica var. kirkii, commonly known as the Sudanese acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless branches and bipinnate leaves that are composed of numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, flat pods containing hard, woody seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid regions, often found in savannas and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its timber, which is used in construction and furniture-making, as well as for its traditional medicinal properties.
