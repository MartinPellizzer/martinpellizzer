Astragalus coahuilae

Astragalus coahuilae, commonly known as Coahuila milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
