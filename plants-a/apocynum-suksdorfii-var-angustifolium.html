Apocynum suksdorfii var. angustifolium

Apocynum suksdorfii var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf dogbane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive grayish-green color. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds covered in fine, silky hairs.

This species is often found in moist to wet soils in woodland or wetland habitats.
