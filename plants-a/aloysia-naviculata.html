Aloysia naviculata

Aloysia naviculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf verbena, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Aloysia naviculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils in full sun.
