Avena sativa var. pugnax

Avena sativa var. pugnax, commonly known as the aggressive oat, is a variant of the common oat characterized by its robust growth habit and high adaptability to various soil conditions.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in temperate climates and is known for its dense foliage and strong root system. The plant produces spikelets with awned lemmas, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family. It is often found in disturbed soils and can become invasive in certain environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in erosion control and as a forage crop.
