Alsodeia petiolaris

Alsodeia petiolaris, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Alsodeia petiolaris plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
