Arnicratea grahamii

Arnicratea grahamii, commonly known as the Graham's arnica, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes with cool, moist conditions. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
