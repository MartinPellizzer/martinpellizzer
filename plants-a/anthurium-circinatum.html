Anthurium circinatum

Anthurium circinatum, commonly known as the curly-leaved anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that curl inward, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, spadix-like flowers surrounded by a colorful spathe, typically in shades of red or yellow. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Anthurium circinatum is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
