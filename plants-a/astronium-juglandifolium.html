Astronium juglandifolium

Astronium juglandifolium, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing edible seeds known as Brazil nuts. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for several hundred years.

Its ecological importance is significant, as it provides habitat and food for various wildlife species.
