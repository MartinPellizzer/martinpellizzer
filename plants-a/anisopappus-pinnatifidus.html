Anisopappus pinnatifidus

Anisopappus pinnatifidus, commonly known as the desert sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to harsh desert conditions.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a drought-tolerant species with ecological significance in its native habitat.
