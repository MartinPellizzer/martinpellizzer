Anthriscus nemorosa var. hirtifructus

Anthriscus nemorosa var. hirtifructus, commonly known as hairy-fruited woodruff, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are hairy beneath, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in early spring. Its fruits are spherical and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
