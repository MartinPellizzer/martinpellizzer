Alsine octandra var. herniarioides

Alsine octandra var. herniarioides, commonly known as narrowleaf alsike clover, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, oval leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
