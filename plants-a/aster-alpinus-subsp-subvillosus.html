Aster alpinus subsp. subvillosus

Aster alpinus subsp.

subvillosus, commonly known as the Alpine aster, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of white to pale blue.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in harsh, high-altitude environments. It typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
