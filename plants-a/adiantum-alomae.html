Adiantum alomae

Adiantum alomae, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern species native to the humid forests of Central America.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are often tinged with a silvery hue. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil to flourish. Its fronds are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive, graceful appearance that makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Adiantum alomae is known for its ability to tolerate low light conditions, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
