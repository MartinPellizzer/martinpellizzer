Aeschynomene kapiriensis

Aeschynomene kapiriensis, commonly known as Kapiriensis sicklegrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by pods containing lens-shaped seeds.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and grasslands, and it plays a role in soil stabilization due to its extensive root system.
