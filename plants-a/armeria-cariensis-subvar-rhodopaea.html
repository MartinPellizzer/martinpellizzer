Armeria cariensis subvar. rhodopaea

Armeria cariensis subvar. rhodopaea, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shorelines, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
