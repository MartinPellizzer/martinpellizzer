Agrimonia pilosa

Agrimonia pilosa, commonly known as stiff agrimony, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and serrated leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by red, velvety berries. Agrimonia pilosa is often found in dry meadows, woodland edges, and rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
