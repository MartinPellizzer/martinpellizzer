Anthriscus sylvestris var. glabricaulis

Anthriscus sylvestris var. glabricaulis, commonly known as slender cow parsley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are glabrous, or hairless, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the carrot family (Apiaceae). It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and botanical gardens.
