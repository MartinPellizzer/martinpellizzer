Astragalus filamentosus

Astragalus filamentosus, commonly known as the filamentous milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, yellow or greenish flowers that bloom in early summer.

Astragalus filamentosus is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
