Amaranthus lanatus

Amaranthus lanatus, commonly known as the velvetleaf amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Amaranthus lanatus is also known for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
