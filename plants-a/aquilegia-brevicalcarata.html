Aquilegia brevicalcarata

Aquilegia brevicalcarata, commonly known as the short-horned columbine, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and stream banks. Its flowers, which bloom in spring, come in shades of red, purple, and yellow, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
