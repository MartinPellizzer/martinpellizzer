Argyrolobium zanonii subsp. stipulaceum

Argyrolobium zanonii subsp.

stipulaceum, commonly known as the Stipulate Silverlobe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical bean-like flowers and nitrogen-fixing ability.

The plant features slender, branching stems with small, stipulate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to white, arranged in racemes, and are adapted for insect pollination.

This subspecies is often found in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.
