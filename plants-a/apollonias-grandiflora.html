Apollonias grandiflora

Apollonias grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered apollonias, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to white, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Apollonias grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
