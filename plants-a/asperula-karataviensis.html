Asperula karataviensis

Asperula karataviensis, commonly known as the Karatavinskya azalea, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

The plant belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
