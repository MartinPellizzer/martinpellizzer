Airella capillaris

Airella capillaris, commonly known as slender marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 10 centimeters in length. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, often blooming in late spring to early summer.

Airella capillaris plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
