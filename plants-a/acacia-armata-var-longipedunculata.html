Acacia armata var. longipedunculata

Acacia armata var. longipedunculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, needle-like leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that are arranged in dense, elongated clusters, giving it its varietal name "longipedunculata.". It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
