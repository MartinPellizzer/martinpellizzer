Aquilegia scopulorum var. goodrichii

Aquilegia scopulorum var. goodrichii, commonly known as the Goodrich's columbine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spurred, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are deeply lobed.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
