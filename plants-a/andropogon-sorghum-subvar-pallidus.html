Andropogon sorghum subvar. pallidus

Andropogon sorghum subvar. pallidus, commonly known as pale sorghum, is a variant of the sorghum species native to warm climates.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, pale green to golden flowers, which are typical of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its drought tolerance.

This subvariety is valued for its use in forage, biofuel production, and as a source of grain.
