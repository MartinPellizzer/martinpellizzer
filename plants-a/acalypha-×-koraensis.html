Acalypha × koraensis

Acalypha × koraensis, commonly known as the Kora hibiscus, is a hybrid shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters and features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, often surrounded by colorful, leaf-like structures called bracts.

Acalypha × koraensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual impact.
