Allomorphia albiflora

Allomorphia albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered allomorph, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, white petals and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Allomorphia albiflora is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
