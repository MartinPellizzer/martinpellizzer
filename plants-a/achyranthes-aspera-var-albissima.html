Achyranthes aspera var. albissima

Achyranthes aspera var. albissima, commonly known as white leaf smartweed, is a variant of the smartweed species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is an annual herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and alternate leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by three-winged, dry fruits. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

The leaves and roots of this plant have been used in traditional medicine for their purported medicinal properties.
