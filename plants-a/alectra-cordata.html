Alectra cordata

Alectra cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow throat. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Alectra cordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
