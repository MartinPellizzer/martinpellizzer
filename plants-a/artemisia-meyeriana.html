Artemisia meyeriana

Artemisia meyeriana, commonly known as Meyer's wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant contains volatile oils and is often used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and digestive properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or dry landscapes.

Its strong scent also makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
