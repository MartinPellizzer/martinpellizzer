Agropyron spicatum var. pringlei

Agropyron spicatum var. pringlei, commonly known as Pringle's wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas.

This variant is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
