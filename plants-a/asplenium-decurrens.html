Asplenium decurrens

Asplenium decurrens, commonly known as the running spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that often form dense, carpet-like growths. The plant typically grows on rocks, walls, or soil, thriving in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a slightly glossy appearance.

Asplenium decurrens is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a common sight in humid, temperate climates.
