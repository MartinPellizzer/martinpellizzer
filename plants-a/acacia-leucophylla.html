Acacia leucophylla

Acacia leucophylla, commonly known as the white-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, pale green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

Acacia leucophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
