Arthraxon glabrescens

Arthraxon glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth arrowhead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Australia.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Its roots are fibrous and can form dense colonies, making it a common component of aquatic or semi-aquatic vegetation.

Arthraxon glabrescens is known for its adaptability to waterlogged conditions and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
