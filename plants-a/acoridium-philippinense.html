Acoridium philippinense

Acoridium philippinense, commonly known as the Philippine arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Acoraceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spadices, typical of the Acoraceae family. Its leaves are long, narrow, and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

The rhizomes of Acoridium philippinense are utilized in traditional medicine and as a starch source.
