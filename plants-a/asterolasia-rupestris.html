Asterolasia rupestris

Asterolasia rupestris, commonly known as the rock aster, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features wiry stems and small, clustered leaves that help reduce water loss. Its flowers, which range from white to purple, bloom in late summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Asterolasia rupestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscapes due to its low water requirements.
