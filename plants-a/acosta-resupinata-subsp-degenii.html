Acosta resupinata subsp. degenii

Acosta resupinata subsp.

degenii, commonly known as the Dune Morning Glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of South America. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped, pale blue to purple flowers.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh coastal conditions and its role in stabilizing dune ecosystems.
