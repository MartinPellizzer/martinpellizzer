Allophylus subfalcatus var. distachyus

Allophylus subfalcatus var. distachyus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf alder, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in coastal and moist forest habitats. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, a characteristic known as dioecy.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
