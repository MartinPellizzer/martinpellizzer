Acacia cundinamarcae

Acacia cundinamarcae, commonly known as the Colombian acacia, is a deciduous shrub native to the Andes mountains of Colombia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky soils. It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
