Arrabidaea truncata

Arrabidaea truncata, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, white flowers that are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and the demand for its wood, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
