Alyssum tortuosum f. ramosissimum

Alyssum tortuosum f. ramosissimum, commonly known as the branched alyssum, is a flowering plant characterized by its dense, branched growth habit.

It features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of white or pale pink. The plant is native to southern Europe and is often found in rocky or limestone soils. It has narrow, linear leaves and is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Alyssum tortuosum f. ramosissimum is generally drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun.
