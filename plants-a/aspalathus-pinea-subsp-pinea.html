Aspalathus pinea subsp. pinea

Aspalathus pinea subsp.

pinea, commonly known as the African green pea, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant produces small, oval-shaped pods containing edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its nutritional value, it is cultivated for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
