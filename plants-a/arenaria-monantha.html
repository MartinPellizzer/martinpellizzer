Arenaria monantha

Arenaria monantha, commonly known as the single-flowered sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh, cold environments. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in tundra and mountainous habitats.

Arenaria monantha is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in horticultural settings for its ornamental appeal.
