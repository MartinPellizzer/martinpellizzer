Astragalus Baba-Alliar Subsp. Nudicarpus

Astragalus baba-alliar subsp. nudicarpus is a rare and intriguing member of the legume family found in specific alpine regions. This subspecies is characterized by its unique morphology and ecological adaptations to harsh mountain environments. It thrives in rocky soils and high elevations where few plants can survive. Despite its limited distribution, it plays a vital role in local ecosystems. Studying this plant offers insights into plant resilience and biodiversity in extreme habitats.
