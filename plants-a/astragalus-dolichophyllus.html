Astragalus dolichophyllus

Astragalus dolichophyllus, commonly known as the long-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its root system is fibrous and deep-reaching. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.

Astragalus dolichophyllus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
