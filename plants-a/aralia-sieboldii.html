Aralia sieboldii

Aralia sieboldii, commonly known as Japanese angelica tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan and Korea.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbels, followed by orange-red berries that are edible for humans and birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its elegant foliage and seasonal color changes.
