Abutilon otocarpum var. broomense

Abutilon otocarpum var. broomense, commonly known as broom hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed. The plant produces showy, hibiscus-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its fibrous bark has also been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
