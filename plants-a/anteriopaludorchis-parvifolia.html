Anteriopaludorchis parvifolia

Anteriopaludorchis parvifolia, commonly known as the slender marsh orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific wetland habitats in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and pale greenish-white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant thrives in nutrient-poor, waterlogged soils and relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its reproductive strategy involves the production of tiny, ephemeral seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
