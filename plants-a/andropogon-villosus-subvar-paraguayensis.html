Andropogon villosus subvar. paraguayensis

Andropogon villosus subvar. paraguayensis, commonly known as Paraguay bluestem, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
