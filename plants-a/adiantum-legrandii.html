Adiantum legrandii

Adiantum legrandii, commonly known as the golden maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bright green with a golden hue on the underside. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its fronds are bi-lobed and arching, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Adiantum legrandii is valued in horticulture for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in humid, indoor conditions.
