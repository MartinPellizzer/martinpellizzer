Aulomyrcia prunifolia var. brevipes

Aulomyrcia prunifolia var. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed myrcia, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that ripens to a red or orange color.

The species is noted for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in local traditional medicine.
