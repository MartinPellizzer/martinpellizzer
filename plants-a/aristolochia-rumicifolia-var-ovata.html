Aristolochia rumicifolia var. ovata

Aristolochia rumicifolia var. ovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed Dutchman's pipe, is a perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, ovate leaves that are usually smooth and dark green. The plant produces unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are often purple or greenish in color, with a distinctive, aromatic scent. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is known for its medicinal properties, though it contains toxic compounds that require careful handling.
