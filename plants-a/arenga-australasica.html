Arenga australasica

Arenga australasica, commonly known as the giant palm, is a tall, slender palm native to the Pacific islands.

It is characterized by its large, pinnate leaves that can reach lengths of up to 5 meters. The plant produces large, yellowish-white inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers. Its fruit is a fibrous, three-lobed structure that contains a single seed.

Arenga australasica is often used for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in its native regions.
