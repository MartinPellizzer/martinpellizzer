Akebia trifoliata var. clematifolia

Akebia trifoliata var. clematifolia, commonly known as the Japanese kudzu or clematis akebia, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three leaflets, often with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that transition to red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Its ornamental value comes from its foliage, flowers, and unique fruit, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
