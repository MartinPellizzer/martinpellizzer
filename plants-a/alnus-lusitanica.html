Alnus lusitanica

Alnus lusitanica, commonly known as the Iberian alder, is a deciduous tree native to southwestern Europe.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and catkin-like flowers. The tree thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and along stream banks. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.
