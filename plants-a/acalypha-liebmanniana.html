Acalypha liebmanniana

Acalypha liebmanniana, commonly known as the silverleaf acalypha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray leaves with a velvety texture, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Acalypha liebmanniana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
