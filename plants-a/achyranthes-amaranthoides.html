Achyranthes amaranthoides

Achyranthes amaranthoides, commonly known as redroot amaranth, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. Its roots are often reddish in hue, which gives it its common name.

Achyranthes amaranthoides is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
