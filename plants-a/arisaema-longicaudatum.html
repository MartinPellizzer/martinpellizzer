Arisaema longicaudatum

Arisaema longicaudatum, commonly known as the long-tailed arisaema, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves and a unique, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a large, hooded spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streamside areas. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by insects, and the plant produces berries that are dispersed by animals.

Arisaema longicaudatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in some regions.
