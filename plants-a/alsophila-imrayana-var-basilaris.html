Alsophila imrayana var. basilaris

Alsophila imrayana var. basilaris, commonly known as the Imray's tree fern, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant exhibits a unique growth habit, with a distinctive base that forms a thick, woody rootstock. Its fronds are typically dimorphic, with sterile and fertile fronds differing in shape and structure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.
