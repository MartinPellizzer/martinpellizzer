Adenia crassa

Adenia crassa, commonly known as the broadleaf adenia, is a climbing shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Adenia crassa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in indigenous Australian medicine.
