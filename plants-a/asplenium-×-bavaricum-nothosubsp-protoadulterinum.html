Asplenium × bavaricum nothosubsp.

protoadulterinum, commonly known as the hybrid maidenhair fern, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Asplenium bavaricum and another related species. It exhibits characteristics of both parent species, featuring delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive dark green color and a glossy texture.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its fronds are bipinnate, with small, closely spaced pinnae that give it a feathery appearance.

This nothosubspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
