Angianthus cyathifer

Angianthus cyathifer, commonly known as the golden cup-mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from August to October. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often hairy and branched. Angianthus cyathifer thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in arid regions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
