Ampelopsis heterophylla var. integra

Ampelopsis heterophylla var. integra, commonly known as the smoothleaf arrowwood, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves that are alternate and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in moist woodland areas.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes.
