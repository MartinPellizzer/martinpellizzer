Alstroemeria roseoviridis

Alstroemeria roseoviridis, commonly known as the pink and green alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to green. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, with strap-like leaves that are often variegated. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its unique coloration and long-lasting blooms contribute to its ornamental value.
