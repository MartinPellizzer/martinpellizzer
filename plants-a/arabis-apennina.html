Arabis apennina

Arabis apennina, commonly known as the Italian rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are spoon-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering stems bear clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.

Arabis apennina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and delicate blooms.
