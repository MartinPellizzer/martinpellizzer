Anemone triloba

Anemone triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white to pinkish flowers with a distinctive, divided center, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has basal leaves that are palmately lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Anemone triloba is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native gardens.
