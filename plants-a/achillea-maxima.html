Achillea maxima

Achillea maxima, commonly known as the giant yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, stiff stems and large, flat-topped clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Achillea maxima is also valued for its medicinal properties, historically used to treat wounds and inflammation.
