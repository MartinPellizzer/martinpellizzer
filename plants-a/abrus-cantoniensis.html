Abrus cantoniensis

Abrus cantoniensis, commonly known as the Chinese honey bean, is a flowering plant native to China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking red and white flowers that resemble beans. The plant produces elongated pods containing flat, bean-like seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. The seeds contain toxic compounds, making them potentially harmful if ingested.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
