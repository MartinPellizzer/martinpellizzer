Aloe perfoliata var. glauca

Aloe perfoliata var. glauca, commonly known as the blue aloe or glaucous aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branching stems that bear fleshy, dark green leaves with a distinctive blue-gray hue due to a waxy coating. The leaves are often adorned with white or pale blue spots, giving the plant its characteristic appearance. It produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months.

This variety is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
