Astracantha pachyacantha

Astracantha pachyacantha, commonly known as the thick-stemmed aster, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and clustered, showy purple or blue flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent leaves and deep root system. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its striking appearance and resilience.
