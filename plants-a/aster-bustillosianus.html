Aster bustillosianus

Aster bustillosianus, commonly known as the Mexican aster, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces vibrant purple to pink flower heads that bloom in the fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Aster bustillosianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its seasonal beauty.
