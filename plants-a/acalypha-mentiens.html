Acalypha mentiens

Acalypha mentiens, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red-rooted iron plant, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or red in color. Its roots are thick and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Acalypha mentiens is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and resilience.
