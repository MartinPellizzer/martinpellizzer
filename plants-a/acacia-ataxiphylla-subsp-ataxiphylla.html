Acacia ataxiphylla subsp. ataxiphylla

Acacia ataxiphylla subsp.

ataxiphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to western Australia. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, narrow, phyllodes that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge.

The plant produces yellow globular flowerheads that appear in late summer to early autumn. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, with a deep root system to access water.

The species is often found in sandy soils and is part of the broader Acacia genus known for its ecological and ornamental value.
