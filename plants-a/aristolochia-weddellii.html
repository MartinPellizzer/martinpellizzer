Aristolochia weddellii

Aristolochia weddellii, commonly known as the Weddell's Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, and often twisted leaves, which are typically heart-shaped. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The genus Aristolochia is known for its complex chemical compounds, some of which have medicinal uses.
