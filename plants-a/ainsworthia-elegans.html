Ainsworthia elegans

Ainsworthia elegans, commonly known as the flame violet, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range from red to orange in color. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 12 inches, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Due to its showy blooms and relatively low maintenance, it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
