Acaena × brunnescens

Acaena × brunnescens, commonly known as the New Zealand brake, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and dense, spiny growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by spherical, reddish-brown fruits known as achenes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Acaena × brunnescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use by Māori for basket weaving.
