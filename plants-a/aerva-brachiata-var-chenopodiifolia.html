Aerva brachiata var. chenopodiifolia

Aerva brachiata var. chenopodiifolia, commonly known as the Chenopodiifolia spurges, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive texture. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

This plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
