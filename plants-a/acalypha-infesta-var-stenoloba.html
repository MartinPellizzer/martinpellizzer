Acalypha infesta var. stenoloba

Acalypha infesta var. stenoloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flame bush, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to orange bracts that resemble flames, which are actually modified leaves. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by three-winged seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun.
