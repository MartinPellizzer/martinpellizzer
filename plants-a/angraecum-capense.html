Angraecum capense

Angraecum capense, commonly known as the Cape orchid, is a species of orchid native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of fragrant, white or pale violet flowers. The plant typically blooms during the winter months, producing showy, spurred flowers that attract nocturnal pollinators like moths. Angraecum capense is a terrestrial orchid, often found growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations make it a notable species in the orchid family.
