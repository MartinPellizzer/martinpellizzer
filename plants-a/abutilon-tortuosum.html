Abutilon tortuosum

Abutilon tortuosum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Chinese lantern plant, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, lantern-like flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The fruit is a dry capsule that splits open to release small, wind-dispersed seeds.
