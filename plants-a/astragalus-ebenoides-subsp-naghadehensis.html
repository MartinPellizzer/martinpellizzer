Astragalus ebenoides subsp. naghadehensis

Astragalus ebenoides subsp.

naghadehensis, commonly known as the Naghadeh milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from pale yellow to pink.

The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds. It is native to the mountainous regions of Iran, where it thrives in rocky and arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
