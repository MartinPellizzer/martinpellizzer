Asphodelus inderiensis

Asphodelus inderiensis, commonly known as the Inderien asphodel, is a flowering plant native to the island of Socotra.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its striking, sword-shaped leaves and tall, branched inflorescences. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
