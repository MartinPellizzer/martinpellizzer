Ascyrum hypericoides var. typicum

Ascyrum hypericoides var. typicum, commonly known as the common tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and woody stems that often branch out in a bushy manner. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or rocky habitats.

Ascyrum hypericoides var. typicum is known for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
