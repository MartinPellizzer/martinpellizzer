Asteriscus smithii

Asteriscus smithii, commonly known as the Smith's asteriscus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces small, clustered yellow or white flowers. It has slender, branching stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Asteriscus smithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in arid gardens for its drought tolerance and unique appearance.
