Arrhenatherum ilievii

Arrhenatherum ilievii, commonly known as the Iliev's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and panicle-like inflorescence that bears small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a tendency to form dense colonies.

It is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
