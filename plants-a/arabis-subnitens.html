Arabis subnitens

Arabis subnitens, commonly known as the glabrous rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, glabrous (hairless) leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with four petals. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to survive in harsh climates.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
