Anthyllis vulneraria subproles linnaei

Anthyllis vulneraria subproles linnaei, commonly known as the Linnaean anthyllis, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in dense, globular inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This subspecies is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, where it thrives in sunny, arid environments.
