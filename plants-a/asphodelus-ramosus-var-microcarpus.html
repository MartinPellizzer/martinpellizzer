Asphodelus ramosus var. microcarpus

Asphodelus ramosus var. microcarpus, commonly known as the dwarf asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers during the summer months. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its botanical characteristics include a rhizomatous root system and a compact growth habit.

It is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
