Acalypha senegalensis

Acalypha senegalensis, commonly known as the Senegalese mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its palmate leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often surrounded by bracts, which can be red or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and savannas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
