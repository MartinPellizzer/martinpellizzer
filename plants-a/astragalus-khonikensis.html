Astragalus khonikensis

Astragalus khonikensis, commonly known as the Khonikian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor conditions.

Astragalus khonikensis is also noted for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its therapeutic uses.
