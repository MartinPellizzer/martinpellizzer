Amelanchier alnifolia var. alnifolia

Amelanchier alnifolia var. alnifolia, commonly known as the Saskatoon berry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its oval, dark green leaves and clusters of white flowers in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that ripen in late summer, turning from red to dark purple. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are used in various culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairie and woodland habitats.
