Acacia nilotica subsp. kraussiana

Acacia nilotica subsp.

kraussiana, commonly known as the white thorn acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, thorned stems and bipinnate leaves that are often reduced to small, scale-like structures.

The plant produces yellow or pale yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which are followed by flat, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

The species is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and charcoal.
