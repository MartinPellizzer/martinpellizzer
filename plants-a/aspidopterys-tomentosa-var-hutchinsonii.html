Aspidopterys tomentosa var. hutchinsonii

Aspidopterys tomentosa var. hutchinsonii, commonly known as the Hutchison's tree fern, is a variant of the tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, compound leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has a slow growth rate and thrives in moist, shaded environments with well-drained soil. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a soft, hairy texture on the underside.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
