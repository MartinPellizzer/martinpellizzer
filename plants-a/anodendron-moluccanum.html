Anodendron moluccanum

Anodendron moluccanum, commonly known as the Moluccan anodendron, is a flowering plant native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often with a distinctive yellowish or greenish hue.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release the seeds, which are small and winged, aiding in wind dispersal.
