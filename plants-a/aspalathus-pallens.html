Aspalathus pallens

Aspalathus pallens, commonly known as the white bushveld pea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and grassland habitats, often in sandy or well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Aspalathus pallens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
