Amygdalus mensarum

Amygdalus mensarum, commonly known as the mountain almond.

It is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family, native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, almond-like fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

The tree is typically deciduous, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, and is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils.
