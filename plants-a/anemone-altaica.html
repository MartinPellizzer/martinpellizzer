Anemone altaica

Anemone altaica, commonly known as the Siberian anemone, is a perennial plant native to the cold climates of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, hairy stem that supports its palmate leaves, which are deeply divided and resemble a hand. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky environments.

Anemone altaica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
