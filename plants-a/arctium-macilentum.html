Arctium macilentum

Arctium macilentum, commonly known as the lesser burdock, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features tall, hollow stems covered in bracts that develop into spiny burs, which aid in seed dispersal. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small yellow flowers in clusters.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.
