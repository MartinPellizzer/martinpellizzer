Aristotelia trilocularis

Aristotelia trilocularis, commonly known as the kōwhai kākāriki, is a small evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for native birds.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in New Zealand's flora.
