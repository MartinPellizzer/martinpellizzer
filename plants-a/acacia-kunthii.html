Acacia kunthii

Acacia kunthii, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and produces yellow globular flowers in spring. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod and are often used in traditional Aboriginal practices.

Acacia kunthii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
