Anemopsis

Anemopsis, commonly known as water plantain, is a genus of aquatic and semi-aquatic plants belonging to the family Araceae.

It is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating on the surface of water. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically enclosed in a spathe, a modified leaf-like structure. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and establish dense colonies in wetland environments.
