Acacia barrettiorum

Acacia barrettiorum, commonly known as the Barrett's wattle, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by long, straight pods. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets, and it is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to its native environment.

Acacia barrettiorum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various insects and animals.
