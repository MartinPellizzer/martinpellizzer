Astragalus nigritus

Astragalus nigritus, commonly known as black locust or black vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Astragalus nigritus has been used historically in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
