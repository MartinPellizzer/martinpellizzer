Acer coptophyllum

Acer coptophyllum, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental foliage, which displays vibrant colors during the growing season and in autumn. The plant has opposite, palmately compound leaves that are typically red or green, depending on the variety. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by samaroid fruits.

Acer coptophyllum thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
