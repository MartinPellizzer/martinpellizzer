Abrus gracilis

Abrus gracilis, commonly known as the slender maple bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. The seeds are encased in a hard, red aril that contains a toxic compound called abrin.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and research purposes.
