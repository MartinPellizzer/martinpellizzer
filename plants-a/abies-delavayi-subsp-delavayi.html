Abies delavayi subsp. delavayi

Abies delavayi subsp.

delavayi, commonly known as Delavay's fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of western China. It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged spirally along the shoots.

The cones are typically cylindrical, with short, thick scales, and mature to a reddish-brown color. This subspecies thrives in cool, moist climates at high altitudes, often found in temperate forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of alpine flora and ecological succession in mountain ecosystems.
