Aneilema aguense

Aneilema aguense, commonly known as the Brazilian flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Aneilema aguense is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant due to its eye-catching appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
