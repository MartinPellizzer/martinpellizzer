Abutilon pseudogiganteum

Abutilon pseudogiganteum, commonly known as the giant velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as along riverbanks.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
