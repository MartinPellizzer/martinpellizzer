Andropogon semitectus

Andropogon semitectus, commonly known as the tallgrass bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed sites, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue, and its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-based flowers. It plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.

Andropogon semitectus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience.
