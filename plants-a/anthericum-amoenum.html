Anthericum amoenum

Anthericum amoenum, commonly known as the lesser lily of the valley, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in early summer. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, nodding blossoms that resemble those of the more famous Convallaria majalis. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Anthericum amoenum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
