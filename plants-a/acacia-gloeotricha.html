Acacia gloeotricha

Acacia gloeotricha, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, yellowish-green foliage and produces small, fluffy seed pods. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions. It has small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators.

The bark is typically smooth and greyish, with a tendency to flake off in strips.
