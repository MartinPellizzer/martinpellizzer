Alpinia caudata

Alpinia caudata, commonly known as the black root ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright rhizomes and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pale yellow flowers arranged in branched inflorescences. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Alpinia caudata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
