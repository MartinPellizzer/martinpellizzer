Andromachia solidaginea

Andromachia solidaginea, commonly known as the red-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage that helps retain moisture in arid conditions. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to deep red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowering stems are often branched, and the leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges.

Andromachia solidaginea is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
