Alsophila excelsa

Alsophila excelsa, commonly known as the giant chain fern, is a towering tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its massive, trunk-like stem and large, pinnate fronds that can reach lengths of up to 6 meters. The plant has a robust root system that helps stabilize the soil and supports its growth in tropical environments. Its fronds are typically dark green and glossy, with a distinctive, elongated shape.

Alsophila excelsa plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and contributing to biodiversity.
