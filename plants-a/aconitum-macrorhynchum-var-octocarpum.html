Aconitum macrorhynchum var. octocarpum

Aconitum macrorhynchum var. octocarpum, commonly known as the purple aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant features large, showy flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, with five sepals and numerous stamens. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed divisions, and it produces follicles that contain the toxic alkaloids responsible for its potent medicinal and poisonous properties.

This variant is distinguished by its unique floral structure and is often cultivated for its ornamental value despite its dangerous nature.
