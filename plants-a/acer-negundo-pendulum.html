Acer negundo pendulum

Acer negundo pendulum, commonly known as the weeping ash, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that create a distinctive weeping appearance. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a finely toothed margin. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow catkins that appear in early spring, followed by winged samaras in the fall.

The weeping ash is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
