Acacia peregrina

Acacia peregrina, commonly known as the sacred acacia or the Arabian thorn tree, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a small, spiny shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers and has small, bean-like pods that contain seeds. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of many tiny leaflets, and it is well adapted to survive in hot, dry climates.

Acacia peregrina is also known for its cultural and historical significance in the region.
