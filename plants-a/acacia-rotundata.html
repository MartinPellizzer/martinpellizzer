Acacia rotundata

Acacia rotundata, commonly known as the knobthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its twisted, gnarled branches and small, round leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, followed by flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Acacia rotundata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in regions with limited rainfall.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
