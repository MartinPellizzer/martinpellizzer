Azalea villosa

Azalea villosa, commonly known as the hairy azalea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its dense, moss-like foliage and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink and red, bloom in the spring and are highly ornamental.

Azalea villosa is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic value and ability to thrive in woodland environments.
