Astragalus adylovii

Astragalus adylovii, commonly known as the Adylov's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. It has small, rounded seeds that are often dispersed by wind, aiding in its survival in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
