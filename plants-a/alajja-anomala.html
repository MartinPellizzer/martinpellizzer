Alajja anomala

Alajja anomala, commonly known as the Malayan arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces a spadix inflorescence surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Its rhizomes are used as a starchy food source and have medicinal properties.

Alajja anomala thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands and forest understories.
