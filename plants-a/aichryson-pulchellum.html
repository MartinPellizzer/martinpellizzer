Aichryson pulchellum

Aichryson pulchellum, commonly known as the silver velvet cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering of white or silver hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its textured foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor settings.
