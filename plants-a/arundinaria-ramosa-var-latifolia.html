Arundinaria ramosa var. latifolia

Arundinaria ramosa var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf bamboo, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated, and its leaves are long, narrow, and slightly leathery. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and structural form.
