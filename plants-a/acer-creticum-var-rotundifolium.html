Acer creticum var. rotundifolium

Acer creticum var. rotundifolium, commonly known as the round-leaf maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves, which are typically round or heart-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by samaras with winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercially available.
