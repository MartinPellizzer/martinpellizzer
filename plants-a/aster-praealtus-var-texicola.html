Aster praealtus var. texicola

Aster praealtus var. texicola, commonly known as the Texas aster, is a perennial flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Its foliage is generally gray-green, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy to the touch.
