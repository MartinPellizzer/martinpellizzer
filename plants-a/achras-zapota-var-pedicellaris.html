Achras zapota var. pedicellaris

Achras zapota var. pedicellaris, commonly known as the Mexican chicle tree, is a tropical plant native to Central America and southern Mexico.

It is a member of the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and smooth, pale-green bark. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by round, fleshy fruits that are typically green when mature. Its latex, known as chicle, has historically been used in the production of chewing gum and other products.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitats.
