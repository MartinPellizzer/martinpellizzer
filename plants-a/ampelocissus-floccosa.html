Ampelocissus floccosa

Ampelocissus floccosa, commonly known as the silky fig or woolly fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine, white pubescence. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
