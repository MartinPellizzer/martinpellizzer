Alchemilla elongata var. platyloba

Alchemilla elongata var. platyloba, commonly known as the broad-lobed lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are broad, lobed, and covered in a dense layer of silky white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its foliage is commonly used in ornamental gardening for its attractive, textured appearance.
