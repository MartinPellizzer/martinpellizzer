Auliza ciliaris

Auliza ciliaris, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

Auliza ciliaris is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
