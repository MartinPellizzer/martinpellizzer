Acacia platensis

Acacia platensis, commonly known as the quebracho blanco, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly Argentina and Paraguay.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces hard, dense wood that is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in dry forests and scrublands.

Acacia platensis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
