Apodiscus chevalieri

Apodiscus chevalieri, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pods that contain edible seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional medicine and local agriculture.
