Anacolosa lutea

Anacolosa lutea, commonly known as the yellow acanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Anacolosa lutea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
