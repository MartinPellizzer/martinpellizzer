Arctostaphylos glandulosa subsp. howellii

Arctostaphylos glandulosa subsp.

howellii, commonly known as Howell's manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and woody, branched stems.

The plant produces small, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in chaparral and coastal sage scrub ecosystems.
