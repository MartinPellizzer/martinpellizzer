Agalinis spiciflora

Agalinis spiciflora, commonly known as narrowleaf false foxglove, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are tubular and usually pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes at the top of the stem.

Agalinis spiciflora is adapted to wet environments and plays a role in supporting pollinators like bees and moths.
