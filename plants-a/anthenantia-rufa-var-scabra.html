Anthenantia rufa var. scabra

Anthenantia rufa var. scabra, commonly known as the rough-hairy anthenantia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems covered in dense, rough hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The leaves are lance-shaped, also hairy, and often have a grayish-green hue. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
