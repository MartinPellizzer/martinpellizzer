Aspidium × bicknellii

Aspidium × bicknellii, commonly known as the Bicknell's water fern, is a hybrid fern species that exhibits characteristics of its parent species, Aspidium and possibly others.

It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant has a distinctive frond structure with a creeping rhizome and pinnate leaves that are often divided into smaller segments. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of the fern family.

This hybrid is of interest to botanists due to its unique genetic makeup and ecological adaptability.
