Aniba granatensis

Aniba granatensis, commonly known as the Brazilian birch or granadilla tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by small, round, yellow fruits containing a single seed. Its leaves are typically elliptical, glossy, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Aniba granatensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
