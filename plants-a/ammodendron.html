Ammodendron

Ammodendron, commonly known as the desert date or goat's beard, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Chenopodiaceae.

These plants are native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, Asia, and the Middle East, where they thrive in harsh, dry environments. They have deep root systems that allow them to access groundwater, making them well adapted to drought conditions.
