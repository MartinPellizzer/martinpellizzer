Anthurium marginellum

Anthurium marginellum, commonly known as the red-veined anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with deep red veins. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-like inflorescences that are often surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in humid, warm environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Anthurium marginellum is popular as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
