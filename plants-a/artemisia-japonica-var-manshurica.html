Artemisia japonica var. manshurica

Artemisia japonica var. manshurica, commonly known as Manchurian wormwood, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features finely divided, gray-green leaves with a distinctive aromatic scent. Its flowering stems bear small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
