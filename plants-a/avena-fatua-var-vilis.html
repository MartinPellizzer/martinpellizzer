Avena fatua var. vilis

Avena fatua var. vilis, commonly known as slender wild oat, is a variant of the wild oat species native to temperate regions.

It is a grassy plant characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous pale green to brown flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and agricultural fields, where it can become a weed.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common invasive species in many ecosystems.
