Arundo nemorosa

Arundo nemorosa, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can grow up to 5 meters in height, and its long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in panicles during the summer months, which are followed by fluffy, seed-filled spikes. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and wetlands.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is often considered a weed in non-native regions.
