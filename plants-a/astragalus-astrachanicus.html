Astragalus astrachanicus

Astragalus astrachanicus, commonly known as the Astrachan milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seed pods are hairy and contain several small, rounded seeds.
