Astragalus globiceps f. jakkabagi

Astragalus globiceps f. jakkabagi, commonly known as the Jakkabagi milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.
