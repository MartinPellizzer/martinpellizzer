Achatocarpus bicornutus

Achatocarpus bicornutus, commonly known as the two-horned acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant is adapted to arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features such as deep root systems and reduced leaf surface area. Its flowers are typically small and yellow or green, contributing to its ecological role in nitrogen fixation.

The two-horned acacia is often found in savannas and rocky habitats, where it plays a significant part in local ecosystems.
