Alsophila ridleyi

Alsophila ridleyi, commonly known as the ridley's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of pinnate fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizomes are robust, allowing it to anchor firmly in moist, shaded environments.

The fronds are typically dark green and provide a lush, tropical appearance, making it a popular ornamental plant.
