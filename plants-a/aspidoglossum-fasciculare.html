Aspidoglossum fasciculare

Aspidoglossum fasciculare, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its foliage is evergreen, with narrow, grass-like leaves that add texture to the landscape.
