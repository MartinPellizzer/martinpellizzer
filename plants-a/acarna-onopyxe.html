Acarna onopyxe

Acarna onopyxe, commonly known as the waxplant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Acarna onopyxe is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
