Angraecum eburneum subsp. eburneum

Angraecum eburneum subsp.

eburneum, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a climbing epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar. It features long, slender stems with clustered, fragrant white or pale green flowers that open at night.

The plant has leathery, narrow leaves and produces pendulous, star-shaped blooms. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

This species is prized for its unique appearance and nocturnal flowering habit.
