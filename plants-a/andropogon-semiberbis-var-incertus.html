Andropogon semiberbis var. incertus

Andropogon semiberbis var. incertus, commonly known as the uncertain false bluestem, is a variant of the false bluestem grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
