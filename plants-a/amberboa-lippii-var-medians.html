Amberboa lippii var. medians

Amberboa lippii var. medians, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a red throat. The plant produces small, oblong berries that are typically orange or red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from spring to early summer.
