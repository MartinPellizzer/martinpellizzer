Abolboda inermis

Abolboda inermis, commonly known as the smooth balsa tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its light, porous wood, which is highly buoyant and historically used for model building and insulation. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, followed by small, round fruits containing seeds.

The species is adapted to grow in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.
