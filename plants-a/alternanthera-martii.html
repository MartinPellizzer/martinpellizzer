Alternanthera martii

Alternanthera martii, commonly known as the red-leaf Alternanthera, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its striking red or purple foliage. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape and often display vibrant colors due to the presence of anthocyanins.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellow, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in aquatic and terrestrial gardens.
