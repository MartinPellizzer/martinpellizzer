Anabasis foliata

Anabasis foliata, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that thrives in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are narrow and adapted to reduce water loss, and its stems are often covered in a layer of fine, white hairs.

Anabasis foliata is known for its resilience and is often used in traditional medicine.
