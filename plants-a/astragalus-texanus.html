Astragalus texanus

Astragalus texanus, commonly known as the Texas milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Astragalus texanus is often found in grasslands and shrublands, where it thrives in full sun.
