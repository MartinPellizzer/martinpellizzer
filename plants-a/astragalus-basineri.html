Astragalus basineri

Astragalus basineri, commonly known as the Basiner milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the genus Astragalus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Astragalus basineri is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native environments.
