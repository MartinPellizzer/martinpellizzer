Acer pictum f. mono

Acer pictum f. mono, commonly known as the single-flowered maple, is a unique variant of the red maple species.

It is characterized by its striking, single-flowered inflorescences that stand out against its finely toothed, palmately lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant spring foliage and distinctive floral display.

This cultivar is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
