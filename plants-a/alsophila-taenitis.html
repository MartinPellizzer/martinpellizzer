Alsophila taenitis

Alsophila taenitis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth rate and longevity. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a distinctive, narrow shape, contributing to its ornamental value.

Alsophila taenitis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
