Andropogon diversifolius

Andropogon diversifolius, commonly known as the Florida panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Andropogon diversifolius plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
