Aesculus octandra var. purpurascens

Aesculus octandra var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered horse chestnut, is a variant of the horse chestnut tree native to parts of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pale pink to purple flowers that bloom in panicles during the spring. The plant has palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its fruit is a spiny capsule containing bright red, shiny seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.

The species is adapted to temperate climates and prefers well-drained, fertile soils.
