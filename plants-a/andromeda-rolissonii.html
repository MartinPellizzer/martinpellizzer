Andromeda rolissonii

Andromeda rolissonii, commonly known as the dwarf alder, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its ornamental value is due to its compact size and seasonal blooms, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
