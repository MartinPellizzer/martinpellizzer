Adiantum amabile

Adiantum amabile, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a delicate, evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It features finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are typically silver-white on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soil. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.

Adiantum amabile reproduces via spores and is known for its graceful, flowing foliage.
