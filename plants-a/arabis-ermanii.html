Arabis ermanii

Arabis ermanii, commonly known as the Japanese rockcress, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal rosettes of silver-green, woolly leaves and produces clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and alpine settings.
