Arabis olympica

Arabis olympica, commonly known as the Olympic rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent aroma. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees.

Arabis olympica is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh conditions.
