Achras serrata

Achras serrata, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with a dense, rounded crown. The leaves are alternate, simple, and serrated, giving the plant its scientific name. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically arranged in clusters, while the fruit is a yellowish, star-shaped drupe with a sweet, aromatic pulp.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and edible fruit, which is often consumed fresh or used in local cuisines.
