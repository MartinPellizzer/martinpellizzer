Astragalus creticus var. samius

Astragalus creticus var. samius, commonly known as the Cretan milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its stems are often branched. It is adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrub.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
