Astragalus albovillosus var. nigrescens

Astragalus albovillosus var. nigrescens, commonly known as the white-haired milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, yellow flowers clustered in racemes, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with slender, hairy leaflets that give it a distinctive appearance.

The variety nigrescens is characterized by its darkening stems and leaves as they mature, contributing to its unique botanical identity.
