Apium graveolens var. variegatum

Apium graveolens var. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated celery.

This variant is a cultivar of the celery plant characterized by its distinctive variegated leaf patterns, featuring shades of white, yellow, and green. It is a biennial plant that thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil. The leaves are often used in culinary applications, though they are less commonly consumed than the stalks of regular celery.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both aesthetic and edible plants.
