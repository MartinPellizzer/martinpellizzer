Aerva madagassica

Aerva madagassica, commonly known as the Madagascar grass or African grass, is a perennial herb native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically borne on long, slender stalks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

Aerva madagassica is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy and adaptable species.
