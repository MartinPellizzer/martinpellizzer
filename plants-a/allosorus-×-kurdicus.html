Allosorus × kurdicus

Allosorus × kurdicus, commonly known as the Kurdish alpine bellflower, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It has hairy leaves and stems, which help it retain moisture in its harsh habitat.

Allosorus × kurdicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
