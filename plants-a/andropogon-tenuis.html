Andropogon tenuis

Andropogon tenuis, commonly known as slender bluestem, is a grass species native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, blue-green leaves and tufted inflorescence that resembles a bottlebrush. This plant typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed sites, preferring well-drained soils. Andropogon tenuis is an important component of native grassland ecosystems, providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience.
