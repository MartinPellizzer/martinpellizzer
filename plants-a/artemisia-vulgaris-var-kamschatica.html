Artemisia vulgaris var. kamschatica

Artemisia vulgaris var. kamschatica, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features aromatic, silver-gray foliage with silvery-white, feathery leaves that are densely covered in tiny, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in compact, spherical clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry meadows and rocky areas.

This variety is closely related to other wormwood species and is valued for its medicinal and aromatic properties.
