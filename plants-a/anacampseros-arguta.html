Anacampseros arguta

Anacampseros arguta, commonly known as the sharp-tipped ice plant, is a succulent native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
