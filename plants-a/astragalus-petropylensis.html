Astragalus petropylensis

Astragalus petropylensis, commonly known as the rock milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky soils and dry landscapes. The plant produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are usually compound with small, bean-like leaflets. Astragalus petropylensis is adapted to survive in harsh environmental conditions, showcasing resilience through deep root systems and drought tolerance.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems in its native habitat.
