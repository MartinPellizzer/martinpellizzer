Andromeda calyculata var. nana

Andromeda calyculata var. nana, commonly known as dwarf snowbell, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring, often accompanied by dark evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 30 cm, making it ideal for rock gardens or as a container plant. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, thriving in cool, moist environments.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate flowers and dense, foliage, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
