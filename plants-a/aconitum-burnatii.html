Aconitum burnatii

Aconitum burnatii, commonly known as the Burnatii monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and is known for its toxic alkaloids, which make it potentially dangerous to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and protected areas.
