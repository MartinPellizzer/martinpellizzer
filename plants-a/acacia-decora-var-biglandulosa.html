Acacia decora var. biglandulosa

Acacia decora var. biglandulosa, commonly known as the biglandular acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, linear leaflets. The plant produces yellow, spherical flower heads that are clustered in dense, globular inflorescences. Its bark is typically smooth and greyish, with a tendency to flake off in small, irregular pieces.

The species is well adapted to drought and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and resilience.
