Agrostis alba var. patula

Agrostis alba var. patula, commonly known as silvergrass or white grass, is a variant of the common silvergrass species.

It is a low-growing, perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plant is characterized by its silvery-white, fluffy inflorescence that gives it a distinctive appearance. It typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and restoration projects.
