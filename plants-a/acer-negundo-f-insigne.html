Acer negundo f. insigne

Acer negundo f. insigne, commonly known as the white ash or white fringe tree, is a cultivar of the ash tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has a slender, weeping habit with long, cascading branches that give it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a serrated margin, turning yellow in the fall.

This ornamental tree is often planted for its aesthetic value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
