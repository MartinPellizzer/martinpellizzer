Asarum chengkouense

Asarum chengkouense, commonly known as Chengkou wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the family Aristolochiaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, showy flowers. The plant produces a complex, aromatic compound known as asarone, which is used in traditional medicine. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
