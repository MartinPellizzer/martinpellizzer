Anemone apiifolia

Anemone apiifolia, commonly known as the Cow Parsley Anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed, resembling those of cow parsley. It thrives in moist, shaded to partially shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized areas.
