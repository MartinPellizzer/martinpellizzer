Astragalus jejunus var. jejunus

Astragalus jejunus var. jejunus, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, compound leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are usually pale blue to purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom in early summer.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
