Acranthera hallieri

Acranthera hallieri, commonly known as the mountain daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in mountainous regions. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces white or pale yellow flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
