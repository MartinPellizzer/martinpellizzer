Auganthus vaginatus

Auganthus vaginatus, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its stems are often hairy and can grow up to several meters in height.

Auganthus vaginatus is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
