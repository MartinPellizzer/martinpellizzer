Anemia schimperiana subsp. schimperiana

Anemia schimperiana subsp.

schimperiana, commonly known as the red-veined begonia, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent red veins, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, adding to its visual appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This species is popular in horticulture for its vibrant foliage and ease of cultivation indoors.
