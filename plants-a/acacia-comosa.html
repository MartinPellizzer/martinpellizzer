Acacia comosa

Acacia comosa, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, slender, feathery leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia comosa is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appearance.
