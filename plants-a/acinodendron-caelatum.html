Acinodendron caelatum

Acinodendron caelatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are often enclosed in specialized structures called involucres. Acinodendron caelatum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
