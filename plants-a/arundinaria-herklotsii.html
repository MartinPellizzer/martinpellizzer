Arundinaria herklotsii

Arundinaria herklotsii, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or Herklots' bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, typical of the grass family. Its rhizomes are extensively branched and help it spread vegetatively.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
