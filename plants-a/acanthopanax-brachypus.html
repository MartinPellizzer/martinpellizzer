Acanthopanax brachypus

Acanthopanax brachypus, commonly known as the short-stemmed Chinese angel's trumpet, is a climbing shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
