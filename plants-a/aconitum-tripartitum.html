Aconitum tripartitum

Aconitum tripartitum, commonly known as the three-part aconite, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. The plant features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in a spire-like cluster. Its leaves are palmately divided into three parts, giving it its common name.

Aconitum tripartitum contains potent alkaloids and is toxic to both humans and animals.
