Alsophila pubescens

Alsophila pubescens, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery fronds. The plant has a distinctive hairy covering on its trunk, which gives it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Alsophila pubescens is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
