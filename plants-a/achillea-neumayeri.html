Achillea neumayeri

Achillea neumayeri, commonly known as the Alpine yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and silvery-gray foliage. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flower heads arranged in flat-topped clusters. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Achillea neumayeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in alpine gardens.
