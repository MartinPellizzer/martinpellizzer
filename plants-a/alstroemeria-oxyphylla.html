Alstroemeria oxyphylla

Alstroemeria oxyphylla, commonly known as the Peruvian lily, is a striking flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features lance-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. Its roots are fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.

Alstroemeria oxyphylla is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its long-lasting blooms and elegant appearance.
