Avena alba var. barbata

Avena alba var. barbata, commonly known as the hairy oat, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with fine hairs along the edges. The inflorescence consists of a spikelet-bearing panicle, which produces small, white to pale green flowers. This variety is notable for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and its use in traditional herbal medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and agricultural research.
