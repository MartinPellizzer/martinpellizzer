Asclepias jaliscana

Asclepias jaliscana, commonly known as the Mexican milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, butterfly-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. Asclepias jaliscana plays an important role in supporting monarch butterfly populations by providing a host plant for their larvae.
