Alangium villosum var. salaccense

Alangium villosum var. salaccense, commonly known as the hairy alangium, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant produces red berries that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
