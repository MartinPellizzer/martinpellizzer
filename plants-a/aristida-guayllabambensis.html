Aristida guayllabambensis

Aristida guayllabambensis, commonly known as the Guayllabamba grass, is a perennial grass species native to the high Andean regions of South America.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are characteristic of the Aristida genus. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a compact growth habit.

This species is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role in Andean ecosystems.
