Anemone vesicatoria subsp. grossa

Anemone vesicatoria subsp.

grossa, commonly known as the Great Anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

The plant features showy, white to pale pink flowers with numerous petals and a central cluster of stamens, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
