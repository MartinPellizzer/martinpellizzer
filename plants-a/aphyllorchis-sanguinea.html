Aphyllorchis sanguinea

Aphyllorchis sanguinea, commonly known as the blood orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, crimson-colored flowers that resemble blood droplets, hence its common name. The plant has a short, erect stem and produces few, showy blooms during the late spring or early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing in a basal rosette.

Aphyllorchis sanguinea is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal.
