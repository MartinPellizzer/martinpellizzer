Alternanthera meridensis

Alternanthera meridensis, commonly known as the red flame plant, is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink foliage, which becomes more vibrant in full sun. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and water features.
