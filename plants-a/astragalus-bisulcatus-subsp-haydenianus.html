Astragalus bisulcatus subsp. haydenianus

Astragalus bisulcatus subsp.

haydenianus, commonly known as the Hayden's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as sagebrush steppe and mountain slopes. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
