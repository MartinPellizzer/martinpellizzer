Acanthus balcanicus

Acanthus balcanicus, commonly known as the Balkan acanthus, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces showy, spire-like inflorescences with violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves and flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their striking appearance.

Acanthus balcanicus is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
