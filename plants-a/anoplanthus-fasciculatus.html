Anoplanthus fasciculatus

Anoplanthus fasciculatus, commonly known as the clustered reed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has grass-like leaves that are arranged in dense clusters, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of white or pale blue flowers.

Anoplanthus fasciculatus plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and provides habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
