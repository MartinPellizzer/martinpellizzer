Astragalus kahiricus

Astragalus kahiricus, commonly known as the Kahiric milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Astragalus kahiricus is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native range.
