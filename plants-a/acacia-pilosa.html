Acacia pilosa

Acacia pilosa, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, feathery leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow, spherical flower heads that are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods that split open when mature.

Acacia pilosa is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
