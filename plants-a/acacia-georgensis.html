Acacia georgensis

Acacia georgensis, commonly known as the Georgian acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in mountainous areas. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Acacia georgensis is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in local horticulture.
