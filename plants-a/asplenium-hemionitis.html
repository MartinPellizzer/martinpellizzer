Asplenium hemionitis

Asplenium hemionitis, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, glossy, dark green fronds that are often adorned with silver or pale veins. Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are wiry and produce numerous small, lance-shaped leaves.

Asplenium hemionitis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
