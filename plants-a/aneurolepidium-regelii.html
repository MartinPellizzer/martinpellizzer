Aneurolepidium regelii

Aneurolepidium regelii, commonly known as the Siberian grass or Regel's grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in harsh, arid conditions.

This plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and adaptability.
