Anthurium pentaphyllum

Anthurium pentaphyllum, commonly known as the five-leaf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and deep green, often with a reddish or purple tint on the underside. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-shaped inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Anthurium pentaphyllum is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and unique floral structures.
