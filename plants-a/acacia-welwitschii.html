Acacia welwitschii

Acacia welwitschii, commonly known as the giant devil's claw, is a tall, spiny shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, straight, woody stems and compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical clusters, which are followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought resistance and deep root systems.

Acacia welwitschii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
