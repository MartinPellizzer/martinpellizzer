Anthoxanthum horsfieldii var. angustum

Anthoxanthum horsfieldii var. angustum, commonly known as narrowleaf field grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and Australia.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a clump-forming growth habit.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
