Aira caryophyllea subsp. cupaniana

Aira caryophyllea subsp.

cupaniana, commonly known as the purple airplant, is a grass-like plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its striking purple to pink flower spikes that bloom in summer.

The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers arranged in a panicle inflorescence. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green hue.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
