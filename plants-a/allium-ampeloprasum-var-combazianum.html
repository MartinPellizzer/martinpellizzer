Allium ampeloprasum var. combazianum

Allium ampeloprasum var. combazianum, commonly known as the Giant Purple Garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, upright stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with lance-shaped, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of large, violet-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are characterized by their elongated shape and mild garlic flavor, making them suitable for culinary use.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and edible qualities in gardens and farms.
