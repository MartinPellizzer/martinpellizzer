Aristida reverchonii

Aristida reverchonii, commonly known as the narrowleaf threeawn, is a grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically gray-green in color. The plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions and thrives in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture that helps reduce water loss.

Aristida reverchonii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and animals in its native environment.
