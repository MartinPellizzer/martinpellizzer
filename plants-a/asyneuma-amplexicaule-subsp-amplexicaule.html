Asyneuma amplexicaule subsp. amplexicaule

Asyneuma amplexicaule subsp.

amplexicaule, commonly known as the clasping asyneuma, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stem and opposite, ovate leaves that often wrap around the stem, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for botanists and horticultural enthusiasts alike.
