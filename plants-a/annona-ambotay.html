Annona ambotay

Annona ambotay, commonly known as the Amazonian custard apple, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces a large, yellowish-green fruit with a rough, spiny exterior. The fruit contains a creamy, sweet pulp surrounding inedible seeds.

It is valued for its unique flavor and is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
