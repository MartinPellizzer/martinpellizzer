Agave dussiana

Agave dussiana, commonly known as the "Blackfoot Agave," is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The leaves are adorned with sharp spines along their edges and have a waxy coating to reduce water loss. It produces a tall flower stalk that bears yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer.

This plant is well adapted to harsh desert environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
