Athyrium appendiculiferum

Athyrium appendiculiferum, commonly known as the Japanese painted fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, bipinnate fronds that are often adorned with small, tooth-like structures along the margins. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests and rocky slopes. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce new shoots, allowing it to spread slowly over time.

The fronds are usually a vibrant green, with a delicate, lace-like appearance that makes it a popular ornamental plant.
