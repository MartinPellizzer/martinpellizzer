Acosta savranica

Acosta savranica, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland edges.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flowers and compact growth habit.
