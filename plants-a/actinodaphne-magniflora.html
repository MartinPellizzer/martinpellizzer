Actinodaphne magniflora

Actinodaphne magniflora, commonly known as the magnificent laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants such as cinnamon and bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into edible berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
