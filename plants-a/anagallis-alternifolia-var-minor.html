Anagallis alternifolia var. minor

Anagallis alternifolia var. minor, commonly known as the lesser threadleaf willowherb, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, often hairy stems that spread along the ground. The leaves are alternate, narrow, and linear, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in meadows.
