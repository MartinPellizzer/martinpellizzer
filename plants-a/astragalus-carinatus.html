Astragalus carinatus

Astragalus carinatus, commonly known as the ridge milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Astragalus carinatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
