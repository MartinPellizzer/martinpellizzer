Acanthocalycium andreaeanum

Acanthocalycium andreaeanum, commonly known as the Andean prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to high-altitude environments with cool temperatures. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss in arid conditions.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
