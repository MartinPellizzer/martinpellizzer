Aconitum koreanum

Aconitum koreanum, commonly known as the Korean monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Korea.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stem is typically unbranched and hairy. Aconitum koreanum prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or rocky areas.

It is important to note that all parts of this plant are toxic and should be handled with care.
