Anthericum hookeri

Anthericum hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features grass-like leaves that are narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, with a basal sheath that clasps the stem. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white to pale blue flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its flowers have three petals and three sepals, and they are typically arranged in a dense, umbel-like cluster.

Anthericum hookeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.
