Arctostaphylos montana subsp. ravenii

Arctostaphylos montana subsp.

ravenii, commonly known as the black mountain manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and smooth, dark bark that becomes fissured with age.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, typically in late spring, which are followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to a variety of elevations.

The species is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for wildlife in its native range.
