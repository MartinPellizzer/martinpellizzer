Artemisia caudata var. richardsoniana

Artemisia caudata var. richardsoniana, commonly known as Richardson's sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, aromatic foliage and narrow, silvery leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters in the summer, which are borne on slender, branching stalks. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert habitats.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for wildlife.
