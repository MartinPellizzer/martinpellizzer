Arctium tabrisianum

Arctium tabrisianum, commonly known as the common burdock, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces spiny, bur-like fruits that aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animals or clothing. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
