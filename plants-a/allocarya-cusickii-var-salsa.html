Allocarya cusickii var. salsa

Allocarya cusickii var. salsa, commonly known as the saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It is adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable plant for stabilizing disturbed soils in harsh environments. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss through transpiration, a key adaptation to its dry habitat. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, and it is often found in salt marshes and coastal dunes.

Its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions makes it an important component of desert and coastal ecosystems.
