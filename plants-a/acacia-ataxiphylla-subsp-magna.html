Acacia ataxiphylla subsp. magna

Acacia ataxiphylla subsp.

magna, commonly known as the large-flowered acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive, flat, bipinnate leaves and yellow or pale yellow flowers that appear in dense, globular clusters.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. It is well adapted to withstand drought conditions due to its deep root system and waxy leaf surfaces.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique floral display.
