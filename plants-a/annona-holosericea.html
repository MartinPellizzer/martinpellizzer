Annona holosericea

Annona holosericea, commonly known as the custard apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit, which is typically harvested when fully ripe, has a thick, tough rind with a sweet, creamy pulp. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
