Artemisia glauca

Artemisia glauca, commonly known as blue wormwood, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and aromatic, finely divided leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flower heads that are clustered in dense, spherical shapes. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodlands.

Artemisia glauca is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
