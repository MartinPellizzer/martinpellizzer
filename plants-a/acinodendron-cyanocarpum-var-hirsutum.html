Acinodendron cyanocarpum var. hirsutum

Acinodendron cyanocarpum var. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy acinodendron, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rhamnaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "hirsutum.". The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are typically harvested for their medicinal properties. Native to tropical regions of Africa, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both traditional medicine and ecological studies.
