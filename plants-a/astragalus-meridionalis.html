Astragalus meridionalis

Astragalus meridionalis, commonly known as the southern milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.

Astragalus meridionalis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
