Astracantha rubens

Astracantha rubens, commonly known as the red globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking red, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Astracantha rubens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
