Andropogon sorghum var. jucundus

Andropogon sorghum var. jucundus, commonly known as sweet grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and produces tall, slender flowering stalks. The plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are frequently used in traditional crafts and rituals.

Sweet grass is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.
