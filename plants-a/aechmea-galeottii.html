Aechmea galeottii

Aechmea galeottii, commonly known as the Galeotti's bromeliad, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Bromeliaceae family, characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vivid red flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its foliage is often covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention and provide some protection against herbivores.

Aechmea galeottii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
