Antirrhinum linkianum var. medium

Antirrhinum linkianum var. medium, commonly known as the medium-flowered snapdragon, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers in shades of red, pink, and yellow. The flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with the upper lip divided into two lobes and the lower lip broader and spurred at the base. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dry seeds.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
