Acacia erinacea var. microphylla

Acacia erinacea var. microphylla, commonly known as the silky wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage composed of small, glandular, silky-haired phyllodes. The plant produces yellow flowers in cylindrical spikes, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique appearance.
