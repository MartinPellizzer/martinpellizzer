Aspidosperma quebracho-blanco var. pendula

Aspidosperma quebracho-blanco var. pendula, commonly known as the hanging quebracho, is a deciduous tree native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its long, pendulous branches that give it its common name. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody, elongated pods containing flat, dark seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture.

The plant also contains alkaloids, making it both ecologically significant and potentially toxic.
