Alstroemeria peregrina var. albescens

Alstroemeria peregrina var. albescens, commonly known as the white alstroemeria, is a striking plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of showy, white flowers with a subtle pinkish tinge. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly wavy edge. This variety is prized for its elegant appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for many garden settings.
