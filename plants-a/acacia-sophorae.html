Acacia sophorae

Acacia sophorae, commonly known as the Chinese acacia, is a flowering plant native to China and other parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and yellow floral spikes. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Acacia sophorae is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warmer climates.
