Aphanocalyx richardsiae

Aphanocalyx richardsiae, commonly known as the Richards' flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has pinnate leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
