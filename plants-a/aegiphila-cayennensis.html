Aegiphila cayennensis

Aegiphila cayennensis, commonly known as the Cayenne Aegiphila, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
