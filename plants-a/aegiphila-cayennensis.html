Aegiphila cayennensis

Aegiphila cayennensis, commonly known as the Cayenne Aegiphila, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.

Morphology

Aegiphila cayennensis has leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a glossy dark green upper surface and a lighter underside, arranged alternately along the stem.

The stem is slender and upright, covered with a smooth, pale brown bark that becomes slightly fissured with age. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, featuring tubular corollas with a distinctive purple to reddish color, blooming during the dry season and pollinated primarily by insects. The fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, adapted to absorb moisture from the sandy soils of its native habitat.
