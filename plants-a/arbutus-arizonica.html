Arbutus arizonica

Arbutus arizonica, commonly known as the Arizona curlleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that curl at the edges, giving it its common name. The plant produces red or orange berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in desert and chaparral habitats.

Its woody stems and dense growth habit make it a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
