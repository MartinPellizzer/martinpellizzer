Astragalus cicer

Astragalus cicer, commonly known as Greek honey-vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, and its compound leaves are typically pinnate. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Astragalus cicer is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
