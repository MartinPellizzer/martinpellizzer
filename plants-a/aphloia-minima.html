Aphloia minima

Aphloia minima, commonly known as the small-flowered aphloia, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 meter and has narrow, leathery leaves that are dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Aphloia minima is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact size and seasonal flowering.
