Actinidia globosa

Actinidia globosa, commonly known as the Chinese gooseberry, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in vitamin C and are widely cultivated for both culinary and commercial purposes. Its growth habit involves climbing or trailing along supports, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural landscapes.
