Arachis porphyrocalyx

Arachis porphyrocalyx, commonly known as the purple calyx groundnut, is a species of legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple calyx that surrounds its small, yellow flowers. The plant produces edible seeds that are similar to peanuts but have a unique flavor and texture. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential agricultural uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
