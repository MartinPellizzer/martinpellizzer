Aquilegia huteri

Aquilegia huteri, commonly known as the Alpine columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking flowers with five spurred petals, typically in shades of blue, purple, or red, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are palmately lobed, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Aquilegia huteri is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
