Acanthopanax trifoliatus

Acanthopanax trifoliatus, commonly known as the three-leaved arrowhead, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, typically consisting of three leaflets, and its clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It thrives in forested areas and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Acanthopanax trifoliatus is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
