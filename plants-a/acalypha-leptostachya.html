Acalypha leptostachya

Acalypha leptostachya, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed beautyleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage that ranges from red to orange and yellow, often with contrasting leaf veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant leaves.

Acalypha leptostachya is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
