Artemisia salsoloides

Artemisia salsoloides, commonly known as sand artemisia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, white hairs that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in terminal clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Artemisia salsoloides is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystem.
