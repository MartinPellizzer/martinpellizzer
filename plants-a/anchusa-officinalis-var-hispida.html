Anchusa officinalis var. hispida

Anchusa officinalis var. hispida, commonly known as hispid blueweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces striking clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in gardens and natural habitats.
