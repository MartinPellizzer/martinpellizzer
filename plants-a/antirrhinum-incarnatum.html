Antirrhinum incarnatum

Antirrhinum incarnatum, commonly known as the crimson snapdragon, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Plantaginaceae.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, tubular flowers that range from pink to deep red. The flowers are typically bilaterally symmetrical, with two lips, and are designed to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
