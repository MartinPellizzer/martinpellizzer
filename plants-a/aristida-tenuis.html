Aristida tenuis

Aristida tenuis, commonly known as slender threeawn, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and small, spikelet-based flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It is a perennial species that forms dense tussocks, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
