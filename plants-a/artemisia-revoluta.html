Artemisia revoluta

Artemisia revoluta, commonly known as rough tarragon, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features silver-gray foliage with finely textured leaves that are slightly hairy and rolled under. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
