Asclepias mucronata

Asclepias mucronata, commonly known as the Mexican milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking clusters of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers, which are highly attractive to pollinators such as butterflies and bees. Asclepias mucronata is a member of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is known for its latex sap, which is milky and sticky.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
