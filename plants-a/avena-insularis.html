Avena insularis

Avena insularis, commonly known as the island oat, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

Avena insularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
