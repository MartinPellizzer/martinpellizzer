Anthyllis vulneraria subsp. font-queri

Anthyllis vulneraria subsp.

font-queri, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its dense, spiny clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its medicinal uses, historically valued for its supposed healing properties.
