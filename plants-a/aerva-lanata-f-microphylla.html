Aerva lanata f. microphylla

Aerva lanata f. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved horsebean, is a flowering plant species native to regions of Asia, including India and China.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous growth habit. The plant features small, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, kidney-shaped seeds.

This species is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
