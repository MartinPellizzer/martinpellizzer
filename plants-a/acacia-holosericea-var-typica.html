Acacia holosericea var. typica

Acacia holosericea var. typica, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and phyllodes that are linear to narrowly elliptic, typically covered in a fine, silky white pubescence. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that appear in late winter to early spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas with a Mediterranean climate.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in reforestation projects.
