Apium bipinnatum

Apium bipinnatum, commonly known as the fern parsley or giant parsley, is a flowering plant in the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often used as a culinary herb. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the carrot family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Apium bipinnatum is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
