Asplenium barclayanum

Asplenium barclayanum, commonly known as the Barclay's spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocks or tree trunks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a delicate, fern-like appearance.

Asplenium barclayanum is notable for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its role in forest ecosystems as a pioneer species.
