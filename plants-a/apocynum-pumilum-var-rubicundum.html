Apocynum pumilum var. rubicundum

Apocynum pumilum var. rubicundum, commonly known as the redstem dogbane, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems that are typically reddish in color, especially when young. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by follicles containing toxic seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant contains cardiac glycosides, making it potentially poisonous to both humans and animals.
