Alsine glomerata

Alsine glomerata, commonly known as clustered alsike, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, pink or white flowers that bloom in dense clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered with fine hairs.

Alsine glomerata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
