Avenastrum longum var. grande

Avenastrum longum var. grande, commonly known as the giant tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
