Aspidium microchlaena

Aspidium microchlaena, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the family Water Ferns.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically submerged in water. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams. Its spores are produced in small, clustered sori on the underside of its fronds.

Aspidium microchlaena plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
