Abrus canescens

Abrus canescens, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers and bean-like pods that contain toxic seeds. The plant has feathery leaves and is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein. It thrives in warm climates and is adapted to poor soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
