Aster amellus

Aster amellus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy purple to pink petals surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Aster amellus typically grows in prairies, meadows, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soil.

It is a perennial plant that blooms in late summer to early fall, adding vibrant color to the landscape.
