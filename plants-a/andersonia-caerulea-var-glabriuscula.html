Andersonia caerulea var. glabriuscula

Andersonia caerulea var. glabriuscula, commonly known as the smooth-leaved bluebell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers. The leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and typically smooth, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
