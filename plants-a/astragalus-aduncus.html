Astragalus aduncus

Astragalus aduncus, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

Astragalus aduncus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
