Achillea clavennae

Achillea clavennae, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and small, pale yellow to white composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and wildflower meadows.

Its historical use in medicine and as a traditional remedy for wounds and ailments adds to its cultural significance.
