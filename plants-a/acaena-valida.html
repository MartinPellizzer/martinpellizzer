Acaena valida

Acaena valida, commonly known as New Zealand thistle, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, bristly leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant produces small, achene fruits that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread in natural habitats. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Acaena valida is often found in coastal and subalpine regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife.
