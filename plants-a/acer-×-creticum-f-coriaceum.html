Acer × creticum f. coriaceum

Acer × creticum f. coriaceum, commonly known as the Japanese maple with coriaceous leaves, is a unique hybrid species within the genus Acer.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery foliage, which gives it a distinctive texture compared to typical maple leaves. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant seasonal color changes. It typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 8 meters.

Its foliage displays a range of colors, from deep green in summer to brilliant red, orange, and yellow in autumn.
