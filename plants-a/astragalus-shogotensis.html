Astragalus shogotensis

Astragalus shogotensis, commonly known as the Shogoten milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought.

Astragalus shogotensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native range.
