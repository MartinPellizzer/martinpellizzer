Abronia umbellata f. villosa

Abronia umbellata f. villosa, commonly known as the hairy abronia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft pubescence. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and dune environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and coastal landscapes.
