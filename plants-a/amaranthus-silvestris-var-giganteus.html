Amaranthus silvestris var. giganteus

Amaranthus silvestris var. giganteus, commonly known as giant amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in some traditional cuisines.
