Anchusa italica var. kurdica

Anchusa italica var. kurdica, commonly known as Kurdish blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Kurdistan.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Anchusa italica var. kurdica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
