Acacia bullockii

Acacia bullockii, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, feather-like segments. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is often found in dune environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

Acacia bullockii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
