Acanthopanax asperatus

Acanthopanax asperatus, commonly known as the rough-leafed dogwood, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically serrated and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by red berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
