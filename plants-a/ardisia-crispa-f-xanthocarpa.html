Ardisia crispa f. xanthocarpa

Ardisia crispa f. xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow-fruited ardisia, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green ovate leaves that are densely packed along its stems. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by bright yellow berries, which are an attractive feature in ornamental gardening. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for container planting or as a ground cover.

The foliage and fruit coloration contribute to its popularity in tropical and subtropical landscapes.
