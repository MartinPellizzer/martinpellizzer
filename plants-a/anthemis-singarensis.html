Anthemis singarensis

Anthemis singarensis, commonly known as the Singarensis chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often surrounded by white ray florets, giving it a daisy-like appearance. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of its native region.

The species is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
