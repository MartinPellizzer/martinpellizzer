Allium isauricum

Allium isauricum, commonly known as the Isaurian leek, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a tall, slender flower stalk that bears small, white to pale purple flowers. It is often found in rocky or dry soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Allium isauricum is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
