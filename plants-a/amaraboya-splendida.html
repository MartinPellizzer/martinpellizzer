Amaraboya splendida

Amaraboya splendida, commonly known as the splendour tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant produces striking yellow and orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
