Aster sonomensis

Aster sonomensis, commonly known as the Sonoma aster, is a perennial herb native to California.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and chaparral. It produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall.

Due to its limited range and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
