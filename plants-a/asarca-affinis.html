Asarca affinis

Asarca affinis, commonly known as the dwarf arum, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 10 inches, with heart-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, but the plant is notable for its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.

Asarca affinis is often found in marshy areas and is an important component of wetland ecosystems.
