Apodocephala urschiana

Apodocephala urschiana, commonly known as the American lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and bear the floral clusters.

Apodocephala urschiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
