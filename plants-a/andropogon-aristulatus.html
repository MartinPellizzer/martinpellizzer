Andropogon aristulatus

Andropogon aristulatus, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers in the summer months.

Andropogon aristulatus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
