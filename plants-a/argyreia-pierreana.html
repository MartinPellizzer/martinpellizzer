Argyreia pierreana

Argyreia pierreana, commonly known as the purple passion vine, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically deep green with a purplish tinge. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by distinctive purple and black berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

The berries are edible when ripe but can be toxic if consumed in large quantities.
