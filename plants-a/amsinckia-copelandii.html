Amsinckia copelandii

Amsinckia copelandii, commonly known as the desert sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Amsinckia copelandii is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
