Arnica cordifolia var. humilis

Arnica cordifolia var. humilis, commonly known as the heart-leaved arnica, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features basal rosettes of heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically bright green with a waxy coating. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers with prominent central disks, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cool, high-altitude environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
