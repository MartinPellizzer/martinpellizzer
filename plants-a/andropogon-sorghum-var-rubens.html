Andropogon sorghum var. rubens

Andropogon sorghum var. rubens, commonly known as red sorghum, is a variant of sorghum characterized by its striking reddish pigmentation in its stems and leaves.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. This plant is known for its robust growth habit and adaptability to various soil types, making it suitable for both ornamental and agricultural purposes. Its foliage and inflorescence display a range of colors, from deep red to bronze, enhancing its visual appeal.

Red sorghum is also valued for its potential in biofuel production and as a forage crop for livestock.
