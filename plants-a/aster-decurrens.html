Aster decurrens

Aster decurrens, commonly known as the spreading aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features slender, upright stems that often spread horizontally, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its showy, blue to purple flower heads bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its identification in the field.
