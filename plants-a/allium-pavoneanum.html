Allium pavoneanum

Allium pavoneanum, commonly known as the Pavone garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves and umbel-like clusters of white to pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, rounded bulbs that are often used as ornamental plants in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to many landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance, it is popular among gardeners seeking both aesthetic and functional plantings.
