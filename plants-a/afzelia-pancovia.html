Afzelia pancovia

Afzelia pancovia, commonly known as African teak, is a tropical hardwood tree native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its straight grain and high density, making it valuable for construction and furniture. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall with a broad canopy, and its leaves are pinnate with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Afzelia pancovia flowers are small and yellowish, often appearing in clusters, and the fruit is a legume pod containing flat, winged seeds.

Due to overharvesting, it is also considered a threatened species in some regions.
