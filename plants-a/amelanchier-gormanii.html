Amelanchier gormanii

Amelanchier gormanii, commonly known as the Gorman serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its oval to elliptic leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth or slightly hairy upper surface. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. Its fruit is a small, sweet, and slightly tart drupe, often used in culinary applications.

Amelanchier gormanii is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
