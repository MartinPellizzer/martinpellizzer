Astragalus toeniolepis

Astragalus toeniolepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soils.

Astragalus toeniolepis is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native range.
