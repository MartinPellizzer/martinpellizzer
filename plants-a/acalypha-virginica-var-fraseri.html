Acalypha virginica var. fraseri

Acalypha virginica var. fraseri, commonly known as Fraser's beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, violet-blue berries that appear in late summer and persist into winter. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a reddish underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Fraser's beautyberry is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
