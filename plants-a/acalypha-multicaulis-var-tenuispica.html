Acalypha multicaulis var. tenuispica

Acalypha multicaulis var. tenuispica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nettleleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on bracted inflorescences. Its growth habit is upright, with multiple stems emerging from a single base, contributing to its bushy appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
