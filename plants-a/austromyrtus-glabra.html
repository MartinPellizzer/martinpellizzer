Austromyrtus glabra

Austromyrtus glabra, commonly known as the smooth myrtle, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically glabrous or nearly so. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, aromatic berries. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Austromyrtus glabra is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and has some historical uses in traditional medicine.
