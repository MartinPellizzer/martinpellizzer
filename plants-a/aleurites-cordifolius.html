Aleurites cordifolius

Aleurites cordifolius, commonly known as the breadnut tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into spherical fruits. The fruit contains a hard shell enclosing a single seed, which is used for oil extraction. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its economic value.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both traditional and modern horticulture.
