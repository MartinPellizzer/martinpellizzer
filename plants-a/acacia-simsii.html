Acacia simsii

Acacia simsii, commonly known as the silver acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, feathery leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Acacia simsii is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
