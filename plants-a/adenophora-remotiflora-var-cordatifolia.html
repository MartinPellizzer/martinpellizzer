Adenophora remotiflora var. cordatifolia

Adenophora remotiflora var. cordatifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its lanceolate to heart-shaped leaves, which are often hairy and have a distinct texture. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or mountainous habitats.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
