Andromeda axillaris var. longifolia

Andromeda axillaris var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved sweetshrub, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features dense, dark green foliage with lance-shaped leaves that are typically 2 to 4 inches long. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
