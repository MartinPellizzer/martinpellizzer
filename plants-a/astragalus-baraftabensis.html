Astragalus baraftabensis

Astragalus baraftabensis, commonly known as the Baraftab milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing a deep root system for water absorption.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
