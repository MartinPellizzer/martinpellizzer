Aira flexuosa var. umbrosa

Aira flexuosa var. umbrosa, commonly known as the shade-loving grass or umbrosa grass, is a perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green and slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
