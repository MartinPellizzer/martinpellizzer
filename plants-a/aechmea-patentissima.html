Aechmea patentissima

Aechmea patentissima, commonly known as the pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater and nutrients. The leaves are typically dark green with white or silver veins, and they are covered in a dense layer of trichomes that help in water retention. The plant produces a tall flower spike that bears vibrant red bracts, which are often mistaken for flowers.

Aechmea patentissima is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea patentissima has rosette-shaped leaves that are narrow, sword-like, and arranged in a spiral around the central stem, with a bright green color and a waxy surface.

The stem is short and thick, with a smooth, dark green bark that grows upward from the base. Its inflorescence consists of a tall, erect spike of violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer and are pollinated by insects. The fruit is a bright red, fleshy capsule that disperses seeds through wind and animal consumption.

The roots are fibrous and anchor the plant firmly in the soil, helping it to absorb water and nutrients.
