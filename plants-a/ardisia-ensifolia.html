Ardisia ensifolia

Ardisia ensifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf ardisia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers followed by bright red berries that are edible for humans and birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it suitable for garden borders and container planting.
