Acer colchicum

Acer colchicum, commonly known as the Turkish maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically five-lobed and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens or as a decorative element in landscapes.

Acer colchicum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant autumn foliage.

Native Distribution
