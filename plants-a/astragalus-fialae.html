Astragalus fialae

Astragalus fialae, commonly known as the Fiala milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus fialae is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
