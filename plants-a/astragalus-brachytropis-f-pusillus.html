Astragalus brachytropis f. pusillus

Astragalus brachytropis f. pusillus, commonly known as the dwarf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments, featuring deep taproots that allow it to access groundwater.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and is sometimes used in restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
