Anoectochilus pulcher

Anoectochilus pulcher, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and broad, ovate leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
