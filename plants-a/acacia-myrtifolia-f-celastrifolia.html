Acacia myrtifolia f. celastrifolia

Acacia myrtifolia f. celastrifolia, commonly known as the golden wattle variant, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It features slender, often multi-branched stems with a rough, bark-like texture. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in globular clusters, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and arranged in pairs along the stems.

This form is distinguished by its unique floral structure, resembling that of the genus Celastrus, hence its specific epithet.
