Amaryllis hyacinthina

Amaryllis hyacinthina, commonly known as the naked lady, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant's foliage consists of narrow, strap-like leaves that emerge after the flowers have bloomed. It is typically cultivated for its ornamental value and is often grown in containers or as a garden plant.

Amaryllis hyacinthina prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
