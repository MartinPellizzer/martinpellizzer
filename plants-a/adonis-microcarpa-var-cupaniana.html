Adonis microcarpa var. cupaniana

Adonis microcarpa var. cupaniana, commonly known as the cupaniana adonis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with numerous stamens, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
