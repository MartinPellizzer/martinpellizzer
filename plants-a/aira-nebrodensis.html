Aira nebrodensis

Aira nebrodensis, commonly known as the Nebroden air grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
