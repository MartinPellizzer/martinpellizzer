Alchemilla helenae

Alchemilla helenae, commonly known as the Helen's lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of shield-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring, which are clustered in branched inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Alchemilla helenae is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
