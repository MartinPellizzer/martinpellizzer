Astragalus baldshuanicus

Astragalus baldshuanicus, commonly known as the Baldshuan milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions.

Astragalus baldshuanicus plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptations to extreme environments.
