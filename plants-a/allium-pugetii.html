Allium pugetii

Allium pugetii, commonly known as the Puget Sound onion, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor.

Allium pugetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
