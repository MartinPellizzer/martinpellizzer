Antirrhinum subcordatum

Antirrhinum subcordatum, commonly known as the broadleaf toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers with a distinctive two-lipped structure. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Antirrhinum subcordatum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
