Amelanchier leptodendron

Amelanchier leptodendron, commonly known as the slender serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and features alternate, simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white to pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, red to purple berries in the summer. Its fruit is a valuable food source for birds and wildlife, and it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to partial shade.
