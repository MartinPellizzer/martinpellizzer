Acer Monspessulanum Subsp. Cinerascens

Acer monspessulanum subsp. cinerascens is a rare and unique maple species native to southern Europe. It is known for its distinctive bark that develops a grayish, ashen appearance with age. This subspecies thrives in rocky and limestone-rich soils, often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are typically oval with a slightly serrated edge and turn vibrant red in the fall. Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
