Alsophila mossambicensis

Alsophila mossambicensis, commonly known as the Mossambique tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a tall, upright fern that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a robust trunk that becomes woody with age, and its roots form a dense network that helps stabilize the soil. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural gardens and landscaping.
