Asarum canadense var. acuminatum

Asarum canadense var. acuminatum, commonly known as Canadian wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, reddish-brown leaves and its distinctive, aromatic underground rhizomes. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a spathaceous sheath, typical of the Aristolochiaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
