Achyranthes aspera f. robustiformis

Achyranthes aspera f. robustiformis, commonly known as the robust Indian spinach, is a variant of the common cowhage plant.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its erect growth habit and hairy stems. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with serrated margins, and the plant produces small yellow flowers in clusters. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often found in disturbed soils.

This plant is valued for its edible leaves, which are used in traditional cuisines and have medicinal properties.
