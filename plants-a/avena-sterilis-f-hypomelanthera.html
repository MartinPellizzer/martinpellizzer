Avena sterilis f. hypomelanthera

Avena sterilis f. hypomelanthera, commonly known as the pale oat, is a botanical variant of the oat species characterized by its reduced pigmentation.

It is a sterile form of the oat plant, meaning it does not produce viable seeds, which makes it useful in agricultural practices for soil cover. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and has slender, grass-like leaves. Its pale coloration is due to a genetic mutation affecting chlorophyll production.

This variant is often studied for its role in ecological systems and as a model for understanding plant genetics.
