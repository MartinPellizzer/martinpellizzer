Ariocarpus fissuratus subsp. bravoanus

Ariocarpus fissuratus subsp.

bravoanus, commonly known as the Bravo's prickly pear, is a succulent cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in spiny areoles, which bear clusters of white flowers.

The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and used in local cuisine. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has adapted to survive in extreme desert conditions.

This subspecies is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in succulent gardens.
