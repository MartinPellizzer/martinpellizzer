Aspidosperma neblinae

Aspidosperma neblinae, commonly known as the misty aspidosperma, is a species of flowering plant native to the cloud forests of northern South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale green, and its leaves are elliptical with a smooth surface. Its bark is thick and deeply fissured, providing a distinctive appearance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also of interest for its potential medicinal properties.
