Aster sikuensis

Aster sikuensis, commonly known as the Siku aster, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet and Nepal.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from white to pale purple.

Aster sikuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
