Alyssum linifolium var. cupreum

Alyssum linifolium var. cupreum, commonly known as copperleaf alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are linear and gray-green, giving the plant a distinctive silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
