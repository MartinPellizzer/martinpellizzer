Agave bernhardii

Agave bernhardii, commonly known as the Bernhard's agave, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears white or pale yellow flowers. It is well adapted to arid environments and can store water in its leaves.

Agave bernhardii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
