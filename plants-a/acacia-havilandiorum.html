Acacia havilandiorum

Acacia havilandiorum, commonly known as the Haviland's wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf-like structures that reduce water loss in its arid environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to coastal and sandy habitats.

Acacia havilandiorum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and role in stabilizing sandy soils.
