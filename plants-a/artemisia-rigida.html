Artemisia rigida

Artemisia rigida, commonly known as rigid wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which consists of narrow, aromatic leaves with a strong, pungent scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are clustered in dense, spherical clusters. It thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in prairies and rocky areas.

Artemisia rigida is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
