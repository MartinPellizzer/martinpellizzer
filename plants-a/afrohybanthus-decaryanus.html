Afrohybanthus decaryanus

Afrohybanthus decaryanus, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forest margins, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Afrohybanthus decaryanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
