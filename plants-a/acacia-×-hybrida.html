Acacia × hybrida

Acacia × hybrida, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a hybrid plant resulting from the crossbreeding of Acacia dealbata and Acacia longifolia.

It is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia, characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow globular flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in reforestation projects due to its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability.

The species is notable for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife and its role in stabilizing soil in degraded areas.
