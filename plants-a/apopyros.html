Apopyros

Apopyros, commonly known as the wild carrot or Queen Anne's lace, is a biennial plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and an umbel of small, white flowers that often have a single red floret in the center. The plant is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in a variety of soil types. Apopyros is known for its aromatic seeds, which are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

Its growth habit and appearance make it a common weed in agricultural fields and along roadsides.
