Acacia ewartiana

Acacia ewartiana, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Acacia ewartiana plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
