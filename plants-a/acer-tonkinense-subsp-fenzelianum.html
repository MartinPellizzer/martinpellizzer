Acer tonkinense subsp. fenzelianum

Acer tonkinense subsp.

fenzelianum, commonly known as the Fenzelianum maple, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves, which are typically five-lobed and have a vibrant green color during the growing season.

The plant produces small, red-orange samaras that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
