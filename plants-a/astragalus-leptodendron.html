Astragalus leptodendron

Astragalus leptodendron, commonly known as the slenderleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces small, brown pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and steppe environments.

Astragalus leptodendron is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
