Astragalus ammophilus var. baluchistanicus

Astragalus ammophilus var. baluchistanicus, commonly known as the Baluchistan milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of Pakistan and Afghanistan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, thriving in dry, temperate climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
