Alpinia denticulata

Alpinia denticulata, commonly known as the serrated ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric. The plant features tall, rhizomatous stems with lance-shaped leaves that have serrated edges. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in panicles, and bloom during the summer months.
