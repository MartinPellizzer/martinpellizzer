Aconitum nasutum subsp. anfalovii

Aconitum nasutum subsp.

anfalovii, commonly known as the Albanian monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkans. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a spire-like cluster.

The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
