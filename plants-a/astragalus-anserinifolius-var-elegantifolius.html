Astragalus anserinifolius var. elegantifolius

Astragalus anserinifolius var. elegantifolius, commonly known as the elegant leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with pinnate arrangement. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in dryland gardens.
