Asclepias sphacelata

Asclepias sphacelata, commonly known as showy milkweed, is a perennial plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range from pink to orange. The plant produces a milky sap, which is typical of the genus Asclepias. Its leaves are opposite and have a distinctive, heart-shaped appearance.

Asclepias sphacelata serves as a host plant for the monarch butterfly, making it ecologically significant.
