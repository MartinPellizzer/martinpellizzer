Achillea corabensis var. integrifolia

Achillea corabensis var. integrifolia, commonly known as the Balkan yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features basal rosettes of gray-green, lance-shaped leaves with entire margins. The plant produces upright, branched stems that bear small, white to pale yellow composite flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Its aromatic foliage and long blooming period make it a valuable plant for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
