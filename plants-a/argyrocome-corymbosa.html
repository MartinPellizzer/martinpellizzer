Argyrocome corymbosa

Argyrocome corymbosa, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a silvery underside, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
