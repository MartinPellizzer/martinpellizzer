Allium callimischon subsp. haemostictum

Allium callimischon subsp.

haemostictum, commonly known as the purple-streaked garlic. It is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers. Its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The subspecies is distinguished by its distinctive purple markings on the leaves and bulb scales.
