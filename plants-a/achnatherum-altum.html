Achnatherum altum

Achnatherum altum, commonly known as the tall wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands, disturbed sites, and rocky areas. The plant has slender, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, with narrow, blade-like leaves. Its inflorescence is a panicle, bearing small, spikelet-like flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Achnatherum altum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
