Arenaria serpyllifolia subsp. glutinosa

Arenaria serpyllifolia subsp.

glutinosa, commonly known as sticky sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically forms dense mats of foliage with small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a sticky, glandular coating.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Its unique botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
