Alchemilla vulgaris var. flavicoma

Alchemilla vulgaris var. flavicoma, commonly known as golden alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered in a silvery-white down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
