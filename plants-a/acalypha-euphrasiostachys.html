Acalypha euphrasiostachys

Acalypha euphrasiostachys, commonly known as the red-veined nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with prominent red veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming during the wet season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other tropical regions for horticultural purposes.
