Astragalus centroalpinus

Astragalus centroalpinus, commonly known as the Central Alps milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptation to harsh, high-altitude conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for certain wildlife species.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
