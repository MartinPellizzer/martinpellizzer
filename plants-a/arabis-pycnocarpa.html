Arabis pycnocarpa

Arabis pycnocarpa, commonly known as the dense-fruited rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, while the fruit is a short, thick capsule that contains several seeds.

Arabis pycnocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
