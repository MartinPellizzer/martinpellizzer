Arctostaphylos oblongifolia

Arctostaphylos oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically narrow and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
