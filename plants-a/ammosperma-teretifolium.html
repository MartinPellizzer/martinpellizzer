Ammosperma teretifolium

Ammosperma teretifolium, commonly known as the slender-leafed sandplant, is a unique member of the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions, particularly in southern Africa, where it thrives in sandy and rocky soils. The plant is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in early spring. It has a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers, making it well-adapted to drought conditions.

Due to its ecological role in stabilizing sand dunes, it is often considered an important species in desert ecosystems.
