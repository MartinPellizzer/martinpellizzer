Alnus pumila

Alnus pumila, commonly known as the dwarf alder, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its small, simple leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant is typically found in wet, acidic soils, often in riparian zones or bogs. Alnus pumila plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, enriching the surrounding soil.
