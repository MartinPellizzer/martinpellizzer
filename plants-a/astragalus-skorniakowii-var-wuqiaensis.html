Astragalus skorniakowii var. wuqiaensis

Astragalus skorniakowii var. wuqiaensis, commonly known as the Wuqia milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

This variant is of particular interest due to its limited distribution and potential medicinal properties.
