Aristolochia leptosticta

Aristolochia leptosticta, commonly known as the narrow-streaked Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are often greenish-yellow in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex, twisted floral structure. It produces small, dark berries that contain the seeds, and the plant is often grown for its ornamental value.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
