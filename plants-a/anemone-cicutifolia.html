Anemone cicutifolia

Anemone cicutifolia, commonly known as the leafy anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features delicate, white to pinkish flowers with five to seven petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often more prominent than the flowers. It prefers moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas.

Its botanical structure includes a short, stout rhizome and is part of the Ranunculaceae family.
