Austrocephalocereus purpureus

Austrocephalocereus purpureus, commonly known as the Purple Crowned Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and distinctive purple spines that give it its common name. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pink, blooming at night. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it well-suited for dry climates.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
