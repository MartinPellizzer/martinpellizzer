Areca purpurea

Areca purpurea, commonly known as the purple areca, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by edible fruits containing a single black seed. Areca purpurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.
