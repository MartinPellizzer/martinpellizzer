Abies hispanica

Abies hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in a flat, two-ranked pattern. The tree produces cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of the Pinaceae family. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions.

Abies hispanica is often found in high-altitude forests, where it plays a significant role in the local ecosystem.
