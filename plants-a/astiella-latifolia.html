Astiella latifolia

Astiella latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved astiella, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil, this plant thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forest understories or along stream banks.

The plant's botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
