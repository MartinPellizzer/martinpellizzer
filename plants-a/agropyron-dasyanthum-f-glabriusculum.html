Agropyron dasyanthum f. glabriusculum

Agropyron dasyanthum f. glabriusculum, commonly known as the smooth wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in degraded landscapes.
