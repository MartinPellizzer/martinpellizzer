Amaryllis cybister var. brevis

Amaryllis cybister var. brevis, commonly known as the short-stemmed amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact stature and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces clusters of blooms on short, sturdy stems, typically reaching heights of 10 to 20 inches. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are evergreen in warmer climates.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation in gardens and containers.
