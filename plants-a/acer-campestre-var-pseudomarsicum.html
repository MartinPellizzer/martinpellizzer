Acer campestre var. pseudomarsicum

Acer campestre var. pseudomarsicum, commonly known as the dwarf maple or creeping maple, is a low-growing variant of the common maple.

It features dense, compact growth with small, oval leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring. It is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
