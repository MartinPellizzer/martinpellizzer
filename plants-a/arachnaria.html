Arachnaria

Arachnaria, commonly known as the spider lily, is a striking plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy white or pink flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant features narrow, strap-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Arachnaria thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is native to regions in the southern United States.
