Andropogon sorghum var. albofuscus

Andropogon sorghum var. albofuscus, commonly known as white-tinged sorghum, is a variant of the sorghum genus characterized by its unique coloration and growth habits.

It is a grass species native to Africa, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant exhibits a tall, sturdy stalk with narrow leaves, and its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale flowers. Botanically, it is notable for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its resistance to drought.

This variant is often studied for its potential in sustainable agriculture and as a source of bioenergy.
