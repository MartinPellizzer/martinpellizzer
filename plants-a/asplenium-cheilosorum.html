Asplenium cheilosorum

Asplenium cheilosorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen rosette of lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in sori surrounded by indusia. It is a lithophytic species, often growing on rocks or in crevices, and prefers moist, shaded environments.

Asplenium cheilosorum is native to various regions in Asia, including China, Japan, and Southeast Asia.
