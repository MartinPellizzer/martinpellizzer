Allium ascalonicum var. majus

Allium ascalonicum var. majus, commonly known as the giant purple globemallow, is a striking plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, showy purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical name reflects its historical association with ancient Egyptian and Greek cultures.
