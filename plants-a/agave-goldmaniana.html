Agave goldmaniana

Agave goldmaniana, commonly known as the golden agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color with a waxy coating. The leaves are lined with sharp spines and can grow up to 3 feet in length. It produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears yellowish-green flowers.

This plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments.
