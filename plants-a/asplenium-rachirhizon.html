Asplenium rachirhizon

Asplenium rachirhizon, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically green with a slightly glossy surface. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori, which are clustered on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Asplenium rachirhizon is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
