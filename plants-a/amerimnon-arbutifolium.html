Amerimnon arbutifolium

Amerimnon arbutifolium, commonly known as the Florida beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright purple berries that appear in the fall, attracting various bird species. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant flowers and striking fruit display.
