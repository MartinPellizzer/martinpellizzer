Alyxia oliviformis f. rotundata

Alyxia oliviformis f. rotundata, commonly known as the broad-leaf alyxia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features elliptical, dark green leaves that are broader than those of the typical species, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.

The species is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
