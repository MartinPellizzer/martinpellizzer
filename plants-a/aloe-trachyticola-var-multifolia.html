Aloe trachyticola var. multifolia

Aloe trachyticola var. multifolia, commonly known as the African rock aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating called farina. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its varietal form, multifolia, distinguishes it by having multiple leaflets along its foliage.
