Acacia meridionalis

Acacia meridionalis, commonly known as the southern acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are compound and consist of multiple pairs of small leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to dry conditions.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
