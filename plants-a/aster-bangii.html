Aster bangii

Aster bangii, commonly known as the Bangi aster, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers are small and clustered in a flat, composite head.

Aster bangii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
