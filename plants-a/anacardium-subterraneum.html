Anacardium subterraneum

Anacardium subterraneum, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into woody capsules containing edible seeds. The tree's most notable feature is its large, woody fruit, which contains the highly nutritious Brazil nuts. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various wildlife species.
