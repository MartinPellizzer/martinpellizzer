Arum detruncatum var. virescens

Arum detruncatum var. virescens, commonly known as the green-veined arum, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves with prominent green veins. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and water features.
