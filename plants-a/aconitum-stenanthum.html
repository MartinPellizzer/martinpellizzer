Aconitum stenanthum

Aconitum stenanthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes at the top of the stems. Its leaves are deeply divided into narrow, lance-shaped lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Aconitum stenanthum contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires careful handling.
