Acacia doratoxylon var. laxiflora

Acacia doratoxylon var. laxiflora, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spindly branches and its distinctive, feathery foliage composed of small, delicate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or clustered inflorescences, which are highly attractive to nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

The wood of this species is lightweight and has been used traditionally for various purposes, including crafting and fuel.
