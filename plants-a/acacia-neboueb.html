Acacia neboueb

Acacia neboueb, commonly known as the African bristled acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorned branches and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in cylindrical clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Acacia neboueb is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, often found in savannas and woodland areas.

It plays a role in local ecosystems and has been used traditionally for various purposes, including medicine and crafts.
