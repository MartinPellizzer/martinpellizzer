Aberemoa marcgraviana

Aberemoa marcgraviana, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are berries that change color from green to yellow as they mature.

The plant contains alkaloids and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
