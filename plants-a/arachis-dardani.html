Arachis dardani

Arachis dardani, commonly known as the Dardani groundnut, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces pods containing one or two round, edible seeds. It is adapted to grow in dry and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and less-studied member of the Arachis genus.
