Agrostis hackeliana

Agrostis hackeliana, commonly known as the Hackel's bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and compact growth habit, making it well-suited for shady, moist environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats in woodland or grassland habitats.

Agrostis hackeliana is often found in association with other moisture-loving plants in shaded, forested areas.
