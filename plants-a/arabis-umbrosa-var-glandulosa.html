Arabis umbrosa var. glandulosa

Arabis umbrosa var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular rockcress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features basal leaves that are rosette-like and covered with glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, white or pale purple, and arranged in clusters at the top of the stems.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
