Ageratina porriginosa

Ageratina porriginosa, commonly known as the white mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its fluffy, white to pale blue flower heads that resemble clouds, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Ageratina porriginosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
