Anthemis calabrica

Anthemis calabrica, commonly known as the narrowleaf tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, aromatic leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used historically for its antiseptic and digestive benefits.
