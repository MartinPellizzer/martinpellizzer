Acer pseudoplatanus var. handjeryi

Acer pseudoplatanus var. handjeryi, commonly known as the Handjeryi maple, is a rare and distinctive variant of the Norway maple.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, which features deep green leaves with a subtle reddish tinge during the growing season. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit, making it suitable for smaller garden spaces. Its leaves are typically palmately lobed, with five to seven pointed lobes that contribute to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued for its unique coloration and is often cultivated for its aesthetic qualities in horticultural settings.
