Acacia boormanii subsp. gibba

Acacia boormanii subsp.

gibba, commonly known as the knob thorn, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive knobby, thickened stems that give it its common name.

The plant produces yellow, fluffy flowers in late winter to early spring, which are followed by pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of many small, thread-like leaflets.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought and heat.
