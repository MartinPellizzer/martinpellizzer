Aspalathus poliotes

Aspalathus poliotes, commonly known as the silver leaf bush, is a small, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, hard pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for gardens in arid regions.
