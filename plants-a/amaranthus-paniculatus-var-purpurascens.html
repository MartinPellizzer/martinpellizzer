Amaranthus paniculatus var. purpurascens

Amaranthus paniculatus var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, feather-like leaves that range from green to purple in color. The plant produces dense, branched inflorescences that bear small, clustered flowers, which are typically red or purple. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional cuisines for its nutritious seeds.

This variety is well-suited for growing in full sun and well-drained soil.
