Ascaricida anthelminthica

Ascaricida anthelminthica, commonly known as the medicinal wormwood, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic, branched stems and small, yellowish-green composite flowers. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including sesquiterpene lactones, which contribute to its medicinal properties. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in traditional and modern herbal medicine.
