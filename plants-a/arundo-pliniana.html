Arundo pliniana

Arundo pliniana, commonly known as the narrowleaf reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. The plant has long, slender leaves that are often narrower than those of its close relatives, and it produces small, greenish flowers in summer. It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its elegant appearance and ability to tolerate waterlogged soil.

Arundo pliniana plays a role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
