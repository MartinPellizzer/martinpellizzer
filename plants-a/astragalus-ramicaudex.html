Astragalus ramicaudex

Astragalus ramicaudex, commonly known as the narrow-caudex milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are typically arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low rainfall and poor soil conditions.

Astragalus ramicaudex is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
