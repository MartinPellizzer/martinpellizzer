Argentina baliemensis

Argentina baliemensis, commonly known as the Baliensis orchid, is a rare and unique species native to the high-altitude regions of Argentina.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and requires a cool, humid environment to thrive. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Due to its limited habitat and threatened status, Argentina baliemensis is a subject of conservation interest.
