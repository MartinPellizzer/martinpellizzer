Astragalus intarrensis

Astragalus intarrensis, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to survive in drought conditions. It has compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus intarrensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
