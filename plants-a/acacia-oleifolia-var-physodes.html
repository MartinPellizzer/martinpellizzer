Acacia oleifolia var. physodes

Acacia oleifolia var. physodes, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and small, feathery leaves that are typically green with a slight blue or grey tinge. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that appear in late winter to early spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

The plant's unique foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
