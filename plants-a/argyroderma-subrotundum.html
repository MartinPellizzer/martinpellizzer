Argyroderma subrotundum

Argyroderma subrotundum, commonly known as the silver-leafed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves that reflect sunlight, a feature that helps it conserve moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a clustered inflorescence, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
