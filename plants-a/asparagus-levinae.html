Asparagus levinae

Asparagus levinae, commonly known as Levina's asparagus, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, spiny stems and fern-like foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, needle-like spears. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Asparagus levinae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
