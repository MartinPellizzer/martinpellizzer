Allium cyclospathum

Allium cyclospathum, commonly known as the cyclospathum onion, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous base and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, making it a cultivated vegetable in some regions.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
