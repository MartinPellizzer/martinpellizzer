Alyssum cyclodonteum

Alyssum cyclodonteum, commonly known as the rock allyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in rocky or sandy soils, often found in coastal areas. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming from spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing a dense ground cover.

Alyssum cyclodonteum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in marginal environments.
