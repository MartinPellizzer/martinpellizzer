Avena strigosa var. abbreviata

Avena strigosa var. abbreviata, commonly known as the short-stippled oat, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with short, stiff hairs along the edges. The inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of spikelets, each bearing a single floret. This variety is notable for its compact growth and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and potential use in restoration projects.
