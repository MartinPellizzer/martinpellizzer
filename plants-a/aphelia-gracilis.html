Aphelia gracilis

Aphelia gracilis, commonly known as the slender water plant, is a aquatic plant native to freshwater environments.

It typically grows in shallow waters and has long, thin stems that are often floating or submerged. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish or white in color. Its leaves are narrow and ribbon-like, which helps it to float and absorb sunlight efficiently.

Aphelia gracilis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
