Astragalus krugeanus var. hareftae

Astragalus krugeanus var. hareftae, commonly known as the Krugean milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
