Acacia spectrum

Acacia spectrum, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Acacia spectrum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

It is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
