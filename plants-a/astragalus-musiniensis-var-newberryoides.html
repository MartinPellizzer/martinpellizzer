Astragalus musiniensis var. newberryoides

Astragalus musiniensis var. newberryoides, commonly known as the Newberry's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to nutrient cycling in its ecosystem.
