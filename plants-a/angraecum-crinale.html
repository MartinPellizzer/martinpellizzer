Angraecum crinale

Angraecum crinale, commonly known as the "Crimson Orchid," is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that bear numerous showy, white to pink flowers with a distinctive crimson lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by moths, featuring a long nectar spur that matches the proboscis of its specific pollinators.

Angraecum crinale is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
