Amygdalus communis var. sphaerocarpa

Amygdalus communis var. sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the Persian walnut, is a variety of the common walnut tree.

It is characterized by its large, round, and thick-shelled nuts that are highly valued for their size and quality. The plant produces compound leaves with elongated leaflets and is typically found in temperate climates. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, and it relies on wind pollination.

The fruit matures over several months, reaching full size and hardness before harvest.
