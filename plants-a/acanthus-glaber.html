Acanthus glaber

Acanthus glaber, commonly known as the smooth acanthus, is a striking ornamental plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are smooth and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces spires of pale blue to violet flowers in late summer, which are borne on tall, sturdy stalks. Acanthus glaber is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Its architectural form and ornate foliage make it a popular choice for garden design and historical architecture.
