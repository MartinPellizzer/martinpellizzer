Alepidea longifolia

Alepidea longifolia, commonly known as the Cape York lily, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Alepidea longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
