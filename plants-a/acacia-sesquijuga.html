Acacia sesquijuga

Acacia sesquijuga, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing capabilities through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobium bacteria. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects and small animals.
