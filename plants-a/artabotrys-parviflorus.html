Artabotrys parviflorus

Artabotrys parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered beautyberry, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are edible for humans and birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
