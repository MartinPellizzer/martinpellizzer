Ampelygonum umbellatum

Ampelygonum umbellatum, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters at the top of slender stems. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
