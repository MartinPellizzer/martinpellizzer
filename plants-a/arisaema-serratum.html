Arisaema serratum

Arisaema serratum, commonly known as the serrated arum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves and a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered on the spadix, which is enclosed by a colorful, leaf-like structure called a spathe. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Arisaema serratum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
