Aspalathus nivea

Aspalathus nivea, commonly known as the white bushveld pea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Aspalathus nivea is often found in open woodlands and along coastal areas, where it thrives in full sun.
