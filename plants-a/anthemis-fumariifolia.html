Anthemis fumariifolia

Anthemis fumariifolia, commonly known as the smokeplant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in dry conditions, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
