Aspidistra nutans

Aspidistra nutans, commonly known as the drooping aspidistra, is a species of flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves that often droop downward. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers enclosed within a spathe, which is typical of the Araceae family. Aspidistra nutans is known for its adaptability to low-light conditions and is often cultivated as an indoor plant.

Its ornamental value lies in its elegant foliage rather than its flowers.
