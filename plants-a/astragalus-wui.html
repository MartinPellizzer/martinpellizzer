Astragalus wui

Astragalus wui, commonly known as the Wui milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Astragalus wui is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

It is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
