Agave salmiana subsp. salmiana

Agave salmiana subsp.

salmiana, commonly known as the sacred blue agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, often with white or grayish markings.

The plant produces a tall, branched flowering stem that bears small, white flowers. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

This species is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in traditional Mexican practices.
