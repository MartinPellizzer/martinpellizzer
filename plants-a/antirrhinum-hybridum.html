Antirrhinum hybridum

Antirrhinum hybridum, commonly known as the common snapdragon, is a flowering plant in the family Plantaginaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that resemble a dragon's mouth, hence its common name. The flowers are typically red, orange, or yellow, with two lips that are often marked with dark veins. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dry seeds.

It is native to southern Europe and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
