Amaranthus dinteri subsp. brevipetiolatus

Amaranthus dinteri subsp.

brevipetiolatus, commonly known as the narrow-petioled amaranth, is a species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves with short petioles.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, greenish flowers that are borne on branched inflorescences. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in arid environments.
