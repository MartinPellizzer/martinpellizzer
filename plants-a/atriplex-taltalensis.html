Atriplex taltalensis

Atriplex taltalensis, commonly known as Taltal saltbush, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of northern Chile.

It is adapted to arid and saline environments, with succulent leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to access groundwater in dry conditions. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, hard seeds.

Atriplex taltalensis plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and is part of the broader saltbush family, Atriplex.
