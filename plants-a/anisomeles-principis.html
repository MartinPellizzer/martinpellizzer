Anisomeles principis

Anisomeles principis, commonly known as the Philippine dandelion, is a herbaceous plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its propagation in its native habitat.

Anisomeles principis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for potential medicinal uses.
