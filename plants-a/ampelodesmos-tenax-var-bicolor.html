Ampelodesmos tenax var. bicolor

Ampelodesmos tenax var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored climbing plant, is a variant of the Ampelodesmos tenax species native to Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its distinctive bicoloration, with alternating light and dark green leaves along its stems. It is a vine that typically grows along forest floors or in shaded areas, using its tendrils to climb and spread. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture, while the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in moist, humid environments.
