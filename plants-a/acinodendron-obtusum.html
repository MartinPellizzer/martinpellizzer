Acinodendron obtusum

Acinodendron obtusum, commonly known as the obtuse acinodendron, is a species of flowering plant in the family Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its fruits are legumes that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Acinodendron obtusum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
