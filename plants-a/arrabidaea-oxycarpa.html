Arrabidaea oxycarpa

Arrabidaea oxycarpa, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in spring. The tree has a spreading canopy and can grow up to 15 meters tall, with pinnate leaves and woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and visually striking in landscapes.
