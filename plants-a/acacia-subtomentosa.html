Acacia subtomentosa

Acacia subtomentosa, commonly known as the silky wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems covered in a dense layer of silky, grayish-white hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, spherical spikes, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, oblong leaflets.

Acacia subtomentosa is often found in coastal regions and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
