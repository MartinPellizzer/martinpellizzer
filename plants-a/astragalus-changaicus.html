Astragalus changaicus

Astragalus changaicus, commonly known as the Changai milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are small and adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.
