Acer cappadocicum var. brevialatum

Acer cappadocicum var. brevialatum, commonly known as the short-leaved maple, is a unique variant of the Cappadocian maple.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and shorter, broader leaves compared to the typical species. The leaves are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath, with a serrated edge. This variety is native to the Cappadocia region of Turkey and thrives in temperate climates.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
