Anthemis marschalliana subsp. biebersteiniana

Anthemis marschalliana subsp.

biebersteiniana, commonly known as the Alpine tansy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, yellow-orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
