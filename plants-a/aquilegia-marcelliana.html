Aquilegia marcelliana

Aquilegia marcelliana, commonly known as the Marcell's columbine, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes other well-known genera such as Ranunculus and Anemone. The plant features showy, pentagonal flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, with five spurred petals that extend backward. Its leaves are palmately compound, consisting of three to five leaflets that are deeply lobed.

Aquilegia marcelliana thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a common sight in forested areas.
