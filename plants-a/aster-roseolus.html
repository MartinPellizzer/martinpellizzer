Aster roseolus

Aster roseolus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy purple or pink flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soil in full sun.

Aster roseolus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
