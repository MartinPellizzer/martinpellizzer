Athyrium × boreo-occidentali-indobharaticola-birianum

Athyrium × boreo-occidentali-indobharaticola-birianum, commonly known as the hybrid fern, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the crossbreeding of several Athyrium species.

It typically exhibits a combination of traits from its parent species, including fronds that may vary in shape and color. This fern is native to regions where its parent species overlap geographically, often in temperate forest environments. It prefers moist, shaded areas with well-drained soil, making it a suitable plant for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate, feather-like fronds that add a lush, green texture to the landscape.
