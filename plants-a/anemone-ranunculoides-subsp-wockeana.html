Anemone ranunculoides subsp. wockeana

Anemone ranunculoides subsp.

wockeana, commonly known as the showy anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and eastern United States. It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and hairy, with a distinctive white to pale pink flower cluster that resembles a crown.

The flowers consist of numerous small petal-like sepals and are typically arranged in a single layer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
