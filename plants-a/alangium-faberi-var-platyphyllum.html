Alangium faberi var. platyphyllum

Alangium faberi var. platyphyllum, commonly known as the flat-leaf alangium, is a variant of the alangium genus native to eastern Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in clusters, and it produces red, fleshy berries that contain seeds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
