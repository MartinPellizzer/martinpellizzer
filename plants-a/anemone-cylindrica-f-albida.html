Anemone cylindrica f. albida

Anemone cylindrica f. albida, commonly known as the white-topped anemone, is a variant of the common anemone characterized by its pale white to nearly white flowers.

This plant is native to Europe and is often found in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. Its flowers are typically arranged in a cylindrical shape, with numerous small, petal-like bracts. The foliage consists of basal, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

It blooms in early spring, adding a delicate, ethereal appearance to its natural environment.
