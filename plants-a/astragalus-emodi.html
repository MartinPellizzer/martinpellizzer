Astragalus emodi

Astragalus emodi, commonly known as the Himalayan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Astragalus emodi is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ecological role includes nitrogen fixation, which enriches the soil in its native habitat.
