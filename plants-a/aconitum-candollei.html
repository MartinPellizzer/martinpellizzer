Aconitum candollei

Aconitum candollei, commonly known as the Himalayan monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer, arranged in a tall spire. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountain meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a poisonous plant and requires caution when handling.
