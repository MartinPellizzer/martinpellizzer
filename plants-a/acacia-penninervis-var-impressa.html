Acacia penninervis var. impressa

Acacia penninervis var. impressa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly curved and have a distinctive impressed midrib. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
