Arabis rotundifolia

Arabis rotundifolia, commonly known as roundleaf rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or coastal environments. The plant features basal leaves that are round or kidney-shaped, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. It is often found in open habitats such as cliffs, walls, and dry meadows.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
