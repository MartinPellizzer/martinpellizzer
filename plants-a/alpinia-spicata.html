Alpinia spicata

Alpinia spicata, commonly known as the spike ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Botanically, it belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other ginger species.
