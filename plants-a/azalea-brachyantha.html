Azalea brachyantha

Azalea brachyantha, commonly known as the dwarf azalea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring with clusters of pink or red flowers, adding vibrant color to gardens. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Azalea brachyantha is also valued for its adaptability and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
