Amaranthus diacanthus

Amaranthus diacanthus, commonly known as the red amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and vibrant, showy flowers that range from red to purple. The plant produces small, triangular seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Amaranthus diacanthus is also known for its nutritional content, including high levels of protein and minerals.
