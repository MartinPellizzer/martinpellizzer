Anemone patens subsp. flavescens

Anemone patens subsp.

flavescens, commonly known as the yellow anemone, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are basal, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more vibrant yellow petals compared to other anemone species.
