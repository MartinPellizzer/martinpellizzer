Athyrium hahajimense

Athyrium hahajimense, commonly known as the Hahajima black fern, is a species of fern native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its dark, almost black fronds that are densely covered with fine hairs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are relatively short, and the fronds are pinnately compound with a distinctive, narrow shape.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shady garden settings.
