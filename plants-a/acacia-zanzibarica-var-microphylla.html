Acacia zanzibarica var. microphylla

Acacia zanzibarica var. microphylla, commonly known as the Zanzibar umbrella tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-shaped crown formed by its dense, spreading branches. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, which are followed by elongated, flat seed pods. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several small, oval leaflets.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to coastal and sandy soils.
