Asystasia pusilla

Asystasia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf asystasia, is a small herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular, violet to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
