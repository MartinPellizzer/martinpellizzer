Aquilegia vulgaris proles ruscinonensis

Aquilegia vulgaris proles ruscinonensis, commonly known as the Ruscinonensis Columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and clusters of bright red or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately compound, with finely divided leaflets, and it produces attractive, biennial growth patterns.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
