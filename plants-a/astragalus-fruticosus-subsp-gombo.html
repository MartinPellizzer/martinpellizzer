Astragalus fruticosus subsp. gombo

Astragalus fruticosus subsp.

gombo, commonly known as the Gombo milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit and produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring.

The plant has small, compound leaves and is adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils. It is often found in steppe and desert environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.

The subspecies is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
