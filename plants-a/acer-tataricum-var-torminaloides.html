Acer tataricum var. torminaloides

Acer tataricum var. torminaloides, commonly known as the Tatarian maple or Torminaloid maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features distinctive palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often with serrated edges, and displays striking autumn coloration ranging from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras for seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in temperate climates.

Its compact size and seasonal beauty make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
