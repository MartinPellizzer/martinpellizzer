Atropis argentinensis f. mendozina

Atropis argentinensis f. mendozina, commonly known as the Mendoza form of the Argentine atropis, is a rare variant of the species native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and reduced floral structures compared to the typical form. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. Its flowers are usually reduced to small, clustered inflorescences, which may lack the full complement of petals.

This form is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological traits and potential for studying adaptive variation in the genus.
