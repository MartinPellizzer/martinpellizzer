Alopecurus villosus

Alopecurus villosus, commonly known as hairy bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and slender, tufted inflorescences that bear small, blue-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, which can help stabilize soil.

Alopecurus villosus is often found in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
