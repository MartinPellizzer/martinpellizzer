Acacia walkeri

Acacia walkeri, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, gray-green stems and bipinnate leaves that give it a feathery appearance. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in winter, which are followed by long, slender pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Acacia walkeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
