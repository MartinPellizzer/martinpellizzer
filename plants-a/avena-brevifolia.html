Avena brevifolia

Avena brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved oat, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with short, stiff leaves and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces minute, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into hard, bristly seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, Avena brevifolia is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
