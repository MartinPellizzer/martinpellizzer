Astragalus atropurpureus

Astragalus atropurpureus, commonly known as the purple milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and sagebrush habitats. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across arid landscapes.
