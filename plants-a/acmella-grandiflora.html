Acmella grandiflora

Acmella grandiflora, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers, which are typically tubular and arranged in clusters. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Acmella grandiflora is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
