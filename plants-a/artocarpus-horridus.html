Artocarpus horridus

Artocarpus horridus, commonly known as the rough fig or breadfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a large, evergreen tree that can grow up to 25 meters tall, with rough, bark-like leaves. The plant produces a unique fruit with a thick, spiky exterior and a soft, edible interior. Its leaves are palmately lobed and can be used for various purposes, including as a wrapping material.

The plant thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.
