Asperula hirsuta f. persquarrosa

Asperula hirsuta f. persquarrosa, commonly known as the hairy woodruff, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has hairy, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
