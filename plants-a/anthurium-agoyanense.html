Anthurium agoyanense

Anthurium agoyanense, commonly known as the Agoyanense Arrowhead, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy dark green leaves that are often adorned with intricate vein patterns. Its flowers, while not showy, play a crucial role in attracting pollinators such as beetles and flies.

Anthurium agoyanense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
