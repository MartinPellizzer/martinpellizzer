Atriplex sukhorukovii

Atriplex sukhorukovii, commonly known as the Siberian saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is adapted to saline and alkaline soils. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in panicles, and it produces small, hard seeds.

Atriplex sukhorukovii plays a role in stabilizing soil and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
