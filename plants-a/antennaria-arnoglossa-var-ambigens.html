Antennaria arnoglossa var. ambigens

Antennaria arnoglossa var. ambigens, commonly known as the ambiguous sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically features a mat-forming habit with slender, hairy stems and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant has basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate and are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

Its botanical structure is adapted to withstand harsh, cold climates and limited nutrient availability.
