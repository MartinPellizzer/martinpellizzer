Astragalus spirorrhynchus

Astragalus spirorrhynchus, commonly known as the spiralroot milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system is notable for its spiral pattern, which is unique among Astragalus species.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
