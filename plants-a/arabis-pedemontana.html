Arabis pedemontana

Arabis pedemontana, commonly known as the Alpine rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in alpine meadows. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and it produces round, capsule-like fruits.

Arabis pedemontana is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in areas with limited soil nutrients.
