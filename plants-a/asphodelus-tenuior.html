Asphodelus tenuior

Asphodelus tenuior, commonly known as the slender asphodel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asphodelaceae and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and tall, slender flower spikes. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Asphodelus tenuior is a hardy perennial that is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
