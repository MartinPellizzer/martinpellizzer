Astragalus purpureus

Astragalus purpureus, commonly known as purple milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus purpureus is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for wildlife.
