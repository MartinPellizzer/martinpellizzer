Allium nanodes

Allium nanodes, commonly known as the dwarf onion, is a small bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its delicate appearance.

Allium nanodes is relatively low maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
