Alchemilla vallesiaca

Alchemilla vallesiaca, commonly known as the Valdese lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Alchemilla vallesiaca is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
