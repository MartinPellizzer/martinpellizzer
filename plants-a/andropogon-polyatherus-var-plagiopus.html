Andropogon polyatherus var. plagiopus

Andropogon polyatherus var. plagiopus, commonly known as the broadleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces spikelets that are often clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in prairie and woodland habitats. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a robust stem that can reach heights of up to two meters.

It plays a vital role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.
