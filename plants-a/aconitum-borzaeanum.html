Aconitum borzaeanum

Aconitum borzaeanum, commonly known as the Borzaean monkshood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe, particularly the Carpathian Basin. The plant is characterized by its striking purple to blue flowers, which are arranged in a helmet-like structure typical of the genus Aconitum. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance.

Aconitum borzaeanum contains potent alkaloids, making it toxic and requiring caution during handling or cultivation.
