Amarenus badius

Amarenus badius, commonly known as the black amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Amarenus badius is known for its resilience to drought and its ability to grow in poor soil conditions.
