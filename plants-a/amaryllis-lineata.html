Amaryllis lineata

Amaryllis lineata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing clusters of bright red, pink, or white flowers. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Amaryllis lineata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
