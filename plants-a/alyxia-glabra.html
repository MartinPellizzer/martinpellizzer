Alyxia glabra

Alyxia glabra, commonly known as the smooth alyxia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a trailing vine characterized by its slender stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, clustered in axillary inflorescences. Alyxia glabra thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb or trail along structures.
