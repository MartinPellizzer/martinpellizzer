Alyssum montanum var. molliusculum

Alyssum montanum var. molliusculum, commonly known as the sticky alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring and summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The variety is distinguished by its sticky secretions on the stems and leaves, which give it its common name.
