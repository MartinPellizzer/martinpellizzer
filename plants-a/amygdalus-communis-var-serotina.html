Amygdalus communis var. serotina

Amygdalus communis var. serotina, commonly known as the late-blooming almond.

This variety is a subspecies of the domesticated almond tree, characterized by its delayed flowering period compared to other almond cultivars. It produces small, oval-shaped fruits that mature later in the growing season. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features include compound leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring.
