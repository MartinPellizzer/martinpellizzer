Adonis maculata var. miniata

Adonis maculata var. miniata, commonly known as the spotted glory flower, is a striking perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with dark spots. The plant has basal rosettes of narrow, blue-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
