Acer pseudoplatanus var. laciniatum

Acer pseudoplatanus var. laciniatum, commonly known as the serrated maple, is a variant of the common maple characterized by its distinctive lobed leaves.

The foliage is typically a vibrant green during the growing season, turning brilliant red or orange in the autumn. It is a deciduous tree that thrives in temperate climates, often reaching heights of 10 to 20 meters. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras for seed dispersal.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and urban landscapes.
