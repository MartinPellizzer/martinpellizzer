Acridocarpus glaucescens var. ferrugineus

Acridocarpus glaucescens var. ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red flowers that appear in clusters, giving it a fiery appearance. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glaucous or waxy coating. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and urban landscapes due to its vibrant floral display.
