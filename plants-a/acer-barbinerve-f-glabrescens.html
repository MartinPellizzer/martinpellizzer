Acer barbinerve f. glabrescens

Acer barbinerve f. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-veined maple, is a variant of the barbinerve maple, characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaf surfaces.

It belongs to the genus Acer, which includes various maple species known for their ornamental value. The leaves are typically palmately lobed with fine, parallel veins, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.

This form is less common than the typical barbinerve maple, making it a sought-after specimen among horticulturists.
