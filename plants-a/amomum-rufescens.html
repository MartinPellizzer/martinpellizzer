Amomum rufescens

Amomum rufescens, commonly known as wild cardamom, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves and clustered, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces dark brown, aromatic seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its rhizomes contain essential oils and compounds with potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Amomum rufescens thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
