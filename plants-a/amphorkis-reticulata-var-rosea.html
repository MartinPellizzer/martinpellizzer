Amphorkis reticulata var. rosea

Amphorkis reticulata var. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, hairy stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or rose-colored, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is noted for its stinging hairs, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.
