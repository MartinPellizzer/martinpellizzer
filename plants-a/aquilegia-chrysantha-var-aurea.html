Aquilegia chrysantha var. aurea

Aquilegia chrysantha var. aurea, commonly known as the golden columbine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It features striking, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, with a distinctive shape that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture that helps retain moisture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
