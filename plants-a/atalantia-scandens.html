Atalantia scandens

Atalantia scandens, commonly known as the climbing citrus or Chinese tangerine, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend trees or structures. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by citrus-like fruits that are typically orange in color. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated edge.
