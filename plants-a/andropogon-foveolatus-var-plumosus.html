Andropogon foveolatus var. plumosus

Andropogon foveolatus var. plumosus, commonly known as the silky grass or plumosus bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silky-textured foliage and tufted inflorescences that give it a feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. It has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, spike-like flowers in the summer.

This species is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
