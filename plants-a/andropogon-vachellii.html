Andropogon vachellii

Andropogon vachellii, commonly known as pineland dropseed, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pale green to blue-gray leaves. The plant produces fluffy, seed-bearing spikelets that are typically found at the tips of its flowering stalks. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Andropogon vachellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
