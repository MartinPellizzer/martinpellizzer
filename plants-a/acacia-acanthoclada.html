Acacia acanthoclada

Acacia acanthoclada, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow or golden flowers in dense, globular inflorescences, which are a key feature for identification. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salt exposure.

Acacia acanthoclada is also valued for its role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.
