Acer angustilobum var. sichourense

Acer angustilobum var. sichourense, commonly known as the Sichuan maple, is a variant of the narrow-leaf maple native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, which are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red to orange-red samaras in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
