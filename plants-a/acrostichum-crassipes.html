Acrostichum crassipes

Acrostichum crassipes, commonly known as the broadleaf paper plant, is a species of fern native to the wetlands of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, compound leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant thrives in brackish or freshwater environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomes form dense colonies, contributing to the stabilization of wetland soils.

The fronds are used in traditional crafts and have cultural significance in some regions.
