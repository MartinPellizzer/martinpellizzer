Annona haitiensis subsp. appendiculata

Annona haitiensis subsp.

appendiculata, commonly known as the Haitian custard apple, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces a fragrant, yellowish fruit with a smooth, white pulp.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid, lowland areas. It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is valued for its edible fruit and ornamental qualities.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique fruit structure and growth habits compared to other Annona species.
