Acacia complanata var. fasciculata

Acacia complanata var. fasciculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and small, phyllodes that are typically narrow and linear. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in spherical heads. It thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in coastal landscaping.
