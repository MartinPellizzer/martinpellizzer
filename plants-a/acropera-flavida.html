Acropera flavida

Acropera flavida, commonly known as the yellow spider orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, spider-like appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months, typically from September to November. Acropera flavida thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rainforests or near streams.

Its unique morphology and striking color make it a notable species in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
