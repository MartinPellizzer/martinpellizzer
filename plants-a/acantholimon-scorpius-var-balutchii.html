Acantholimon scorpius var. balutchii

Acantholimon scorpius var. balutchii, commonly known as the Baluch sandthorn, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It features compact, cushion-like growth with dense clusters of small, spiny leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, which are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to harsh, saline soils and extreme temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important component of its local flora.
