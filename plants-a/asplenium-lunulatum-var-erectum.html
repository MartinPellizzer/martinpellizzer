Asplenium lunulatum var. erectum

Asplenium lunulatum var. erectum, commonly known as the upright maidenhair fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, arching fronds that grow in a compact, upright habit. The plant produces small, clustered spore sacs on the underside of its leaves, typical of fern reproductive structures. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories where humidity is high.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
