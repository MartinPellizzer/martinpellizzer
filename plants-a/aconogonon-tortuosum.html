Aconogonon tortuosum

Aconogonon tortuosum, commonly known as the spiral gourd, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral-shaped fruits, which are typically yellow or orange when mature. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The spiral gourd is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which have a mild, nutty flavor.
