Amomum scarlatinum

Amomum scarlatinum, commonly known as the Sichuan pepper plant, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, spiny fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, while the flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit, which is the primary part used, contains numerous tiny seeds and is widely used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a spice.
