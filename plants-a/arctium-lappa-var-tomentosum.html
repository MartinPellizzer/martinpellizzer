Arctium lappa var. tomentosum

Arctium lappa var. tomentosum, commonly known as the hairy burdock, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves covered in a dense layer of soft, white hairs. The plant produces distinctive brown, spiky seed heads that contain numerous small, hooked seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in fields.

Botanically, it is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
