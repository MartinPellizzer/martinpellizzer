Acalypha entumenica

Acalypha entumenica, commonly known as the flame bush, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flames, which are actually modified leaves rather than flowers. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
