Aeollanthus subacaulis var. ericoides

Aeollanthus subacaulis var. ericoides, commonly known as the Ericoides variant of the subacaulis aeollanthus, is a shrub native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, and it is adapted to thrive in well-drained, acidic soils.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in ecological and horticultural studies due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences.
