Acer glabrum var. greenei

Acer glabrum var. greenei, commonly known as the Greene's paper birch, is a rare variant of the paper birch tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, white bark that peels in thin, papery layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree typically grows in high-elevation forests and is adapted to cool, moist climates. Its leaves are heart-shaped with doubly serrated margins and turn yellow in the fall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance in its native habitat.
