Allium carinatum subsp. violaceum

Allium carinatum subsp.

violaceum, commonly known as the violet-leaved garlic, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family. It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its striking violet or purple foliage.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in culinary applications.

The plant is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its potential medicinal properties.
