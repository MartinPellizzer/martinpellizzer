Arenaria modesta var. assoana

Arenaria modesta var. assoana, commonly known as the coastal sand-verbena, is a low-growing perennial plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and succulent, aiding in water retention.

It is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
