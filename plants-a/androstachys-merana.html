Androstachys merana

Androstachys merana, commonly known as the mountain thyme, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cold and moist conditions. Its small, pink or purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
