Arthrophytum leptocladum

Arthrophytum leptocladum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and fleshy, narrow leaves that store water. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments.

Due to its resilience and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
