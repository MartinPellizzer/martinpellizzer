Anthoxanthum ecklonii

Anthoxanthum ecklonii, commonly known as the Cape grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are typically shorter than the flowering stems. The plant produces small, yellowish spikelets that are arranged in dense clusters along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Anthoxanthum ecklonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
