Acacia scabra

Acacia scabra, commonly known as the rough acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow or orange globular flowers, which are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. Acacia scabra is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its wood and bark have been traditionally used for various purposes, including fuel and medicinal applications.
