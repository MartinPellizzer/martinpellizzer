Alpinia orthostachys

Alpinia orthostachys, commonly known as the black ginger or mountain ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences that are often violet or purple in hue. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Alpinia orthostachys is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
