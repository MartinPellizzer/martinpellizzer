Aster paniculatus var. cinerascens

Aster paniculatus var. cinerascens, commonly known as the cinerascens aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, clustered inflorescences of blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft, velvety texture. This variety thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
