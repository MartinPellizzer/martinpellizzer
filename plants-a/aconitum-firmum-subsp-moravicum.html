Aconitum firmum subsp. moravicum

Aconitum firmum subsp.

moravicum, commonly known as the Moravian monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

The plant features striking blue-violet flowers arranged in a spire-like inflorescence, characteristic of the Ranunculaceae family. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive appearance, often with a waxy coating.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
