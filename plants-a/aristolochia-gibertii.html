Aristolochia gibertii

Aristolochia gibertii, commonly known as the giant Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves and distinctive, pipe-like flowers that are often greenish or brownish in color. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that turn red when mature, containing seeds enclosed in a fleshy aril. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
