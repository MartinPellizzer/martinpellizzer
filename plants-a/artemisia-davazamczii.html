Artemisia davazamczii

Artemisia davazamczii, commonly known as the Persian wormwood, is a shrub native to Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are clustered in dense, spherical shapes. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thujone and camphor, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
