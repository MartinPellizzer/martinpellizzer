Angraecum poppendickianum

Angraecum poppendickianum, commonly known as the Poppendick's orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It features long, arching inflorescences adorned with fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting specific nocturnal pollinators. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich substrates.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
