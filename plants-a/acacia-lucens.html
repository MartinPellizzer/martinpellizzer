Acacia lucens

Acacia lucens, commonly known as the shining acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. Acacia lucens thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
