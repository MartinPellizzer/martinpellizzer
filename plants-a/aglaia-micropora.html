Aglaia micropora

Aglaia micropora, commonly known as the small-fruited aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family, which includes mahogany and other valuable hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by capsule fruits that release tiny, winged seeds.

Aglaia micropora is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its timber and traditional uses.
