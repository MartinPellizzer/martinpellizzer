Arachniodes gradata

Arachniodes gradata, commonly known as the veined evergreen fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, and deeply lobed leaves that often exhibit a distinctive veining pattern. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its fronds are usually evergreen, providing a lush appearance throughout the year.

Arachniodes gradata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
