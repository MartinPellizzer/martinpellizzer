Aerangis laurentii

Aerangis laurentii, commonly known as the Laurentii orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often with a waxy texture, and it prefers well-draining soil and bright, indirect light.

Aerangis laurentii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
