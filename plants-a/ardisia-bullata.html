Ardisia bullata

Ardisia bullata, commonly known as the dwarf beautyberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, dark purple berries that appear in clusters, often attracting birds. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and produces fragrant white flowers in the spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Ardisia bullata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate a variety of growing conditions.
