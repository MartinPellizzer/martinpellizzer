Aster indicus var. collinus

Aster indicus var. collinus, commonly known as the mountain aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in the summer months and is adapted to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
