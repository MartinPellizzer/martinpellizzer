Alsophila aramaganensis

Alsophila aramaganensis, commonly known as the Aramagan hakea, is a species of tree fern native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of arching fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Alsophila aramaganensis plays an important ecological role in its native ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various species.
