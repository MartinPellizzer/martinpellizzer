Athyrium filix-femina f. plano-rhoeticum

Athyrium filix-femina f. plano-rhoeticum, commonly known as the flat-leaf maidenhair fern, is a variant of the common maidenhair fern characterized by its broad, flat fronds.

It is a perennial plant native to eastern Asia, including regions of China and Japan. The plant features delicate, bright green, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a fan-like pattern. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
