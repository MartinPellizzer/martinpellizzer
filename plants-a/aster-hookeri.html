Aster hookeri

Aster hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Aster hookeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
