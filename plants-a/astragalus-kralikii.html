Astragalus kralikii

Astragalus kralikii, commonly known as Kralik's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Astragalus kralikii is known for its deep root system, which helps it access water from deeper soil layers.

It is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
