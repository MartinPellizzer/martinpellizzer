Amyris ambrosiacea

Amyris ambrosiacea, commonly known as the sweet myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces fragrant yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a slight aromatic scent.

Amyris ambrosiacea is often found in moist, well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical regions.
