Ajax lobularis var. amplicorona

Ajax lobularis var. amplicorona, commonly known as the broad-crown ajax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely packed along its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
