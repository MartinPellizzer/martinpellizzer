Aspicarpa boliviensis

Aspicarpa boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian apple or wild apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of Bolivia and is characterized by its small, edible fruits that resemble apples in appearance. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and shrublands, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a serrated margin, and it produces white or pinkish flowers in the spring.

The fruit, which is often consumed by local communities, has a tart flavor and is used in traditional remedies and culinary practices.
