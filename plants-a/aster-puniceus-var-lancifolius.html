Aster puniceus var. lancifolius

Aster puniceus var. lancifolius, commonly known as the Lance-leaved Purple Coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking purple to pink conical flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

This species is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
