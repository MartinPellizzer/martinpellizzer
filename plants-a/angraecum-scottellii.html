Angraecum scottellii

Angraecum scottellii, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, slender inflorescence bearing numerous white, star-shaped flowers with a distinctive purple lip. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth. Its blooming period typically occurs during the wet season, and it is particularly noted for its fragrant, nectar-rich flowers.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
