Aloe capitata var. angavoana

Aloe capitata var. angavoana, commonly known as the Angavoana Aloe, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color with a powdery coating. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
