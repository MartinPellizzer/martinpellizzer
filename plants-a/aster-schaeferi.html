Aster schaeferi

Aster schaeferi, commonly known as Schaefer's aster, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in shades of purple and white.

Aster schaeferi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
