Adicea diffusa

Adicea diffusa, commonly known as the spreading adicea, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a fibrous root system and erect, branched stems that are often hairy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a rough texture.

Adicea diffusa is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.
