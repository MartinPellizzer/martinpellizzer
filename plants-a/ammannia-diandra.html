Ammannia diandra

Ammannia diandra, commonly known as the redroot ammannia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a reddish tinge, especially along the veins.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in saturated soils and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
