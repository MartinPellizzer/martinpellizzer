Adenophora ornata var. alba

Adenophora ornata var. alba, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features showy, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
