Achillea compacta

Achillea compacta, commonly known as yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features dense clusters of small, flat-topped flower heads that bloom in shades of white, pink, or yellow. Its leaves are aromatic, finely divided, and covered in a white powdery coating called farina.

Achillea compacta is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has a long history of medicinal use.
