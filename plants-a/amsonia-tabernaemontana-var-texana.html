Amsonia tabernaemontana var. texana

Amsonia tabernaemontana var. texana, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and showy, mist-like blue flower clusters that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.

The varietal form 'texana' is distinguished by its slightly more compact growth and slightly paler flower color compared to the typical variety.
