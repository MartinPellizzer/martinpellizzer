Acuston perenne subsp. obovatum

Acuston perenne subsp.

obovatum, commonly known as the broad-ovate acuston, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and compact growth habit. It typically features clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is native to specific regions in Europe and prefers well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often described as having a slightly hairy texture, and it is known for its ability to thrive in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is relatively low-maintenance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
