Amaranthus hypochondriacus

Amaranthus hypochondriacus, commonly known as the Jerusalem artichoke, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers followed by spiky, triangular seeds. It is known for its edible tubers, which are rich in inulin and have a nutty flavor.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in various soil conditions.
