Adiantum schmidtchenii

Adiantum schmidtchenii, commonly known as the Schmidtchen's maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually bright green and have a distinctive, feathery appearance.

Adiantum schmidtchenii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
