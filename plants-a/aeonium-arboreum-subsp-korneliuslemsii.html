Aeonium arboreum subsp. korneliuslemsii

Aeonium arboreum subsp.

korneliuslemsii, commonly known as the Kornelius' tree aeonium, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands. It features a rosette of thick, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often tinged with red at the edges.

The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a striking addition to both indoor and outdoor landscapes.
