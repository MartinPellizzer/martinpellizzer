Aquilegia vulgaris subsp. moorcroftiana

Aquilegia vulgaris subsp.

moorcroftiana, commonly known as the Scottish heather bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Scottish Highlands. It is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and clusters of bright red or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, growing at elevations above 600 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
