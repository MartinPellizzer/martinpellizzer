Aglaia ignea

Aglaia ignea, commonly known as the flame tree or fire tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in dense clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glossy surface. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain toxic seeds, making it important to handle with care.

It thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
