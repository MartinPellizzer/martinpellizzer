Atropa solanacea

Atropa solanacea, commonly known as the deadly nightshade, is a toxic plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark purple berries and bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant contains tropane alkaloids, such as atropine and scopolamine, which are highly poisonous to humans and animals. Native to Europe, it thrives in woodland and hedgerow environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it has a long history of both medicinal and harmful use.
