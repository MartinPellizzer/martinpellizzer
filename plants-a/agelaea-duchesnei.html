Agelaea duchesnei

Agelaea duchesnei, commonly known as the Mexican agelaea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Agelaea duchesnei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in warm climates.
