Astragalus pseudomacrostachys

Astragalus pseudomacrostachys, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring. Its seed pods are slender and twisted, containing small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Astragalus pseudomacrostachys plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
