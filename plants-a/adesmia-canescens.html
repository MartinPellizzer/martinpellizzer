Adesmia canescens

Adesmia canescens, commonly known as the hairy adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, arid environments and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils. Its seed pods are woody and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Adesmia canescens is often found in grasslands and shrublands, contributing to local biodiversity.
