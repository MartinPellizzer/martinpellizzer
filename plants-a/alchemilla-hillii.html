Alchemilla hillii

Alchemilla hillii, commonly known as the mountain lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense, branched inflorescences. Alchemilla hillii thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine collections.
