Acacia aurea

Acacia aurea, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through its root nodules. Its flowers are often used in floral arrangements and are a symbol of Australian identity.

Acacia aurea thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun.
