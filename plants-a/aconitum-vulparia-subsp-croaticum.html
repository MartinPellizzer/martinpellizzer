Aconitum vulparia subsp. croaticum

Aconitum vulparia subsp.

croaticum, commonly known as the Croatian monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet, helmet-shaped flowers.

The plant features palmately lobed leaves and produces toxic alkaloids, making it highly poisonous. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
