Arivela uncifera

Arivela uncifera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arivela, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming during the rainy season. Arivela uncifera is adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in shrublands and grasslands.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
