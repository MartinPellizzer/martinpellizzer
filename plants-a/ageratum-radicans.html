Ageratum radicans

Ageratum radicans, commonly known as trailing ageratum, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and profuse displays of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of purple, white, and pink. The plant has opposite, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, soft pubescence. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and hanging baskets.

Ageratum radicans is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like ladybugs and lacewings.
