Astragalus falconeri

Astragalus falconeri, commonly known as Falconer's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia, particularly Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, yellow to cream-colored flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a deep taproot system that helps it survive in drought-prone areas.

Astragalus falconeri is part of the legume family and has been used traditionally in some regions for medicinal purposes.
