Aster gravesii

Aster gravesii, commonly known as the Sierra aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, purple-blue composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has narrow, linear leaves and a compact growth habit.

Aster gravesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
