Alyssum saxatile variegatum

Alyssum saxatile variegatum, commonly known as the variegated rock alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in early spring. The foliage is characterized by its variegated leaves, with white and green patterns that add visual interest. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
