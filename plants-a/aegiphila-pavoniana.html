Aegiphila pavoniana

Aegiphila pavoniana, commonly known as the velvetleaf aegiphila, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are often aromatic and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Aegiphila pavoniana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
