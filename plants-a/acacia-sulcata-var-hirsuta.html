Acacia sulcata var. hirsuta

Acacia sulcata var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems covered in short, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the dry season. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous tiny leaflets, and it has a deep root system adapted to drought conditions.

Acacia sulcata var. hirsuta is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment and land rehabilitation.

Native Distribution

It thrives in sandy soils and is commonly found in savannas and shrublands. This variant is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall, making it a resilient species in its native habitat. It plays an important ecological role by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
