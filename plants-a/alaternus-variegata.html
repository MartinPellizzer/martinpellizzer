Alaternus variegata

Alaternus variegata, commonly known as the variegated alaternus, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display a mix of green, white, and yellow hues. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring. Alaternus variegata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
