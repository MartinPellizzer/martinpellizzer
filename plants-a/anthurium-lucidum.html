Anthurium lucidum

Anthurium lucidum, commonly known as the shining anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features glossy, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a striking red or purple underside. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects, with the spathe serving as a colorful attractant.

Anthurium lucidum is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
