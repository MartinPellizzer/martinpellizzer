Azalea speciosa var. aurantia

Azalea speciosa var. aurantia, commonly known as the golden azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range from yellow to orange in color. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The blooming period usually occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual display to the landscape.
