Acalypha brasiliensis f. obtusa

Acalypha brasiliensis f. obtusa, commonly known as the Brazilian nettle or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous annual or perennial, often found in moist, tropical environments. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptical in shape, with a distinctive waxy texture.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
