Arundo formosana var. robusta

Arundo formosana var. robusta, commonly known as the robust reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its sturdy, hollow stems and narrow, blade-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, riverbanks, and marshes, making it a common sight in humid climates. Its rapid growth and extensive root system contribute to its role in soil stabilization and erosion control.

Additionally, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and resilience.
