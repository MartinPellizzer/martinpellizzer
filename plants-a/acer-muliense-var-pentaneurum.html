Acer muliense var. pentaneurum

Acer muliense var. pentaneurum, commonly known as the Chinese maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves that turn vibrant red in the autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
