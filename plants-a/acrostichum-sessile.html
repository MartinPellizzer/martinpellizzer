Acrostichum sessile

Acrostichum sessile, commonly known as the broadleaf hala.

It is a member of the fern family and is native to tropical regions of the Americas. This plant is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. The fronds are typically divided into numerous pinnae, giving it a distinctive, lush appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
