Amaranthus spinosus var. rubricaulis

Amaranthus spinosus var. rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed amaranth, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and vibrant red stems, which contribute to its ornamental value. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sunlight. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by fluffy, triangular seed heads.

This variety is often cultivated for its unique appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
