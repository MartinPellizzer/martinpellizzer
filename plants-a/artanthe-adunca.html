Artanthe adunca

Artanthe adunca, commonly known as the narrowleaf artanthe, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or red, which bloom in late summer.

Artanthe adunca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
