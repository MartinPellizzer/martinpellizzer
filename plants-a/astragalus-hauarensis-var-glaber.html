Astragalus hauarensis var. glaber

Astragalus hauarensis var. glaber, commonly known as the smooth hauarensis milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces pods that contain small, hard seeds. It is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and shrubland habitats.
