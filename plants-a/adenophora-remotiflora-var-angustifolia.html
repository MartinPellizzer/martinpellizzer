Adenophora remotiflora var. angustifolia

Adenophora remotiflora var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf adenophora, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pale blue or lavender in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
