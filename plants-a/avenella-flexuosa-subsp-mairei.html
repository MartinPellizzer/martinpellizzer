Avenella flexuosa subsp. mairei

Avenella flexuosa subsp.

mairei, commonly known as Maire's bentweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern China. It typically forms dense mats of prostrate stems covered with soft, hairy foliage.

The leaves are elongated, linear, and often have a slightly wavy margin. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring.

This species is well adapted to rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
