Allium senescens f. subalatum

Allium senescens f. subalatum, commonly known as the silver leaved garlic chives, is a botanical variant of the garlic chive species.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-blue foliage, which sets it apart from the typical green leaves of the standard variety. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

This cultivar is valued for both its ornamental appeal and mild garlic-like flavor, making it useful in culinary applications as well.
