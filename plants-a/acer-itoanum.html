Acer itoanum

Acer itoanum, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that change color with the seasons, often displaying vibrant red, orange, and yellow hues in autumn. The plant belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens and landscapes. Its small, yellow flowers appear in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
