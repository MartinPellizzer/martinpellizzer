Aldama densifolia

Aldama densifolia, commonly known as the denseleaf aldama, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom in the spring. Its root system is extensive and adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Aldama densifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
