Amaranthus cruentus

Amaranthus cruentus, commonly known as blood amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces vibrant red or purple flowers that bloom in dense, spike-like clusters, making it visually striking. Its seeds are small, oblong, and often used in traditional cuisines for their nutritional value.

Amaranthus cruentus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in various environmental conditions.
