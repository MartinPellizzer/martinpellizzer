Agrostis koelerioides var. pampeana

Agrostis koelerioides var. pampeana, commonly known as the Argentine meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, leafy blades that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous minute florets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing forage for livestock and wildlife.
