Alchemilla acrostegia

Alchemilla acrostegia, commonly known as the creeping lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in a fine, silvery down. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in cymes.

Alchemilla acrostegia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
