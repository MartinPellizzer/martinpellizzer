Aster indamellus

Aster indamellus, commonly known as the Indian aster, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads made up of many small florets. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, thriving in cool and moist environments. Its flowers are usually blue to violet in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disc of tubular flowers.

Aster indamellus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
