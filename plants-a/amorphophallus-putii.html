Amorphophallus putii

Amorphophallus putii, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom at night, attracting specific pollinators such as beetles. The plant has broad, floating leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter, providing shade over aquatic environments. Its underground stem, or tuber, is used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.

The botanical features of Amorphophallus putii make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
