Althaea officinalis var. obtusifolia

Althaea officinalis var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf marshmallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are typically broad and rounded. The plant produces clusters of pale pink or white flowers during the summer months, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their mucilage content, which has soothing properties.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes as well as for its medicinal value.
