Achyranthes

Achyranthes, commonly known as Chinese knotweed, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae.

These plants are typically annual or perennial herbs with alternate, often lance-shaped leaves. They produce small, clustered flowers that are usually green or reddish in color. Achyranthes species are native to various regions in Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

The genus includes several species, some of which are used in traditional medicine and horticulture.
