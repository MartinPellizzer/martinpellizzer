Alsophila smithiana

Alsophila smithiana, commonly known as the Smith's tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive, fibrous trunk that is covered in a thick layer of dead fronds, which provide insulation and protection. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Alsophila smithiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in many tropical and subtropical gardens.
