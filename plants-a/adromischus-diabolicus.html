Adromischus diabolicus

Adromischus diabolicus, commonly known as the Devil's Backbone, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the summer months. Its leaves are typically green with a waxy coating, and it has a compact, bushy growth habit.

Adromischus diabolicus is well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant due to its low maintenance requirements.
