Aegiphila vitelliniflora var. egleri

Aegiphila vitelliniflora var. egleri, commonly known as the Egler's yellow-flowered aegiphila, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant in horticulture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
