Alchemilla lovenii

Alchemilla lovenii, commonly known as the Alpine lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Alchemilla lovenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
