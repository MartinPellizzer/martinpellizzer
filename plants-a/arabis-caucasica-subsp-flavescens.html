Arabis caucasica subsp. flavescens

Arabis caucasica subsp.

flavescens, commonly known as the Caucasian rockcress, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in mountainous areas.

The plant features basal leaves that are spoon-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It produces small, round pods that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
