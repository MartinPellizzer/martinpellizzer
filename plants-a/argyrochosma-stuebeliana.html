Argyrochosma stuebeliana

Argyrochosma stuebeliana, commonly known as the silverleaf saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs that reflect light. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
