Aristida simpliciflora

Aristida simpliciflora, commonly known as plains dropseed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale green to reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairie and open woodland habitats.

Aristida simpliciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
