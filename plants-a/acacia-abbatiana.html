Acacia abbatiana

Acacia abbatiana, commonly known as the Ethiopian acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features. Its bark and pods have been traditionally used for medicinal and practical purposes by local communities.

Acacia abbatiana plays a significant role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
