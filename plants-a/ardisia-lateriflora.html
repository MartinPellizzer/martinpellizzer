Ardisia lateriflora

Ardisia lateriflora, commonly known as the side-flowered evergreen, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming during the wet season. Its berries are red or orange when mature and contain several seeds, making it an attractive ornamental plant.

Ardisia lateriflora is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
