Abutilon frutescens

Abutilon frutescens, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its fruits are capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Abutilon frutescens is often found in disturbed areas and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
