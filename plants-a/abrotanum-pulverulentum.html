Abrotanum pulverulentum

Abrotanum pulverulentum, commonly known as the dusty abrotanum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
