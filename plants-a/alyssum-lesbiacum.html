Alyssum lesbiacum

Alyssum lesbiacum, commonly known as the Lesbia's alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial species characterized by its compact, mat-forming habit. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, covered in fine hairs.

Alyssum lesbiacum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.
