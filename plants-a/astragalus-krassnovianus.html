Astragalus krassnovianus

Astragalus krassnovianus, commonly known as the Krassnovian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert organisms.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
