Acacia curryana

Acacia curryana, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion. Acacia curryana is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
