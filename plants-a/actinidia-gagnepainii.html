Actinidia gagnepainii

Actinidia gagnepainii, commonly known as the Gagnepain kiwi, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It belongs to the Actinidiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach lengths of up to 10 meters. Its fruit, similar to the common kiwi, is rich in nutrients and has a distinct flavor.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
