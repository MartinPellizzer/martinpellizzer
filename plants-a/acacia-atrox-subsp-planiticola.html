Acacia atrox subsp. planiticola

Acacia atrox subsp.

planiticola, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces yellow floral spikes that are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
