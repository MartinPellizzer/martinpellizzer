Acacia tenuispica

Acacia tenuispica, commonly known as the slender-leaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in clusters at the base of the stems. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Its wood is hard and durable, making it useful for local crafts and construction.

Acacia tenuispica is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for wildlife.
