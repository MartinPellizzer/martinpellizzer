Aceras calcaratum

Aceras calcaratum, commonly known as the rock bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. Its flowers are blue to violet in color, with five petals and a distinctive central spur.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
