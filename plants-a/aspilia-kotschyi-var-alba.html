Aspilia kotschyi var. alba

Aspilia kotschyi var. alba, commonly known as the white torchflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking white, spiky flower heads that resemble torches, which bloom in summer. The plant is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy, composite flower structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and rocky areas.
