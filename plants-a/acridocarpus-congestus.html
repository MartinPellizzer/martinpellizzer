Acridocarpus congestus

Acridocarpus congestus, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a tropical plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds surrounded by a fibrous pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both ornamental purposes and traditional uses in local communities.
