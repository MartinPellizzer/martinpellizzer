Andropogon capillaceus

Andropogon capillaceus, commonly known as narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed areas, reaching heights of 1 to 3 meters. The plant has slender, leafy stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Andropogon capillaceus plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
