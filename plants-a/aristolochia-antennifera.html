Aristolochia antennifera

Aristolochia antennifera, commonly known as the Indian birthwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and its unique, intricate, pipe-shaped flowers that resemble a horn or a pipe. The plant belongs to the family Aristolochiaceae and is known for its aromatic, often fragrant blossoms. Its vines can grow quite long, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking foliage and floral display.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
