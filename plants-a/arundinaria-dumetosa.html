Arundinaria dumetosa

Arundinaria dumetosa, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species of grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with culms that are often reddish in color. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Arundinaria dumetosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
