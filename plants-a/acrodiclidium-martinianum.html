Acrodiclidium martinianum

Acrodiclidium martinianum, commonly known as the Martin's spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it drought-tolerant.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
