Arabis drummondii var. typica

Arabis drummondii var. typica, commonly known as Drummond's rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, often found in alpine or arid environments. Its leaves are basal, lobed, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

The species is noted for its ability to tolerate harsh conditions, making it a hardy and resilient plant in its native habitat.
