Arundinella miliacea

Arundinella miliacea, commonly known as the milletgrass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Arundinella miliacea is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
