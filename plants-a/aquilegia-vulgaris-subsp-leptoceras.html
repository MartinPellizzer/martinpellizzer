Aquilegia vulgaris subsp. leptoceras

Aquilegia vulgaris subsp.

leptoceras, commonly known as the slender-flowered columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features striking, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple, with five petals and two elongated nectar-rich spurs.

The plant has compound leaves with palmate lobes, and it produces seeds in follicles that disperse via wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, often found in woodland edges and meadows.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural habitats.
