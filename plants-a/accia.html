Accia

Accia, commonly known as the common yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and flat, composite flower heads that typically range from white to pinkish in color. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America and thrives in well-drained soils. Accia is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas, and it is known for its medicinal properties.

Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
