Ardisia illicioides

Ardisia illicioides, commonly known as the Indian star anise, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in horticulture and pharmacology.
