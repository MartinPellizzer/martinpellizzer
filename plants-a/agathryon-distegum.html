Agathryon distegum

Agathryon distegum, commonly known as the red-rooted ironwood, is a hardy shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and woody stems that are resistant to drought and fire. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round, red berries. Its deep root system allows it to thrive in poor soils and extreme temperatures.

Agathryon distegum is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of durable wood.
