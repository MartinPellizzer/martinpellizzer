Aster hololachnus

Aster hololachnus, commonly known as the woolly aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy purple or blue flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is typically found in moist, wooded areas. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

Aster hololachnus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
