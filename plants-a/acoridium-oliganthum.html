Acoridium oliganthum

Acoridium oliganthum, commonly known as the slender swamp lily, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Acoraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. It has a short rhizomatous stem and produces a single spike of flowers that bloom in early spring.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
