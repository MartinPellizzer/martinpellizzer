Allium yatei

Allium yatei, commonly known as Yate's garlic, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Chile and Argentina. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. Its bulbous stem is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Allium yatei is characterized by its aromatic leaves and is closely related to other allium species like garlic and onions.
