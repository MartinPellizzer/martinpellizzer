Akebia lobata var. chaffanjonii

Akebia lobata var. chaffanjonii, commonly known as the Chaffanjonii vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its distinctive tubular flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a decorative vine.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
