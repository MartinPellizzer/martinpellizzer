Arisaema pusillum

Arisaema pusillum, commonly known as the dwarf arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-15 cm, with heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that are usually bright green. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries, which are an important food source for various wildlife species.
