Anarrhinum longipedicellatum

Anarrhinum longipedicellatum, commonly known as the long-pedicellate grass, is a species of flowering plant in the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that are typically green or pale in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides.
