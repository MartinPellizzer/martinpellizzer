Ammi majus subsp. glaucifolium

Ammi majus subsp.

glaucifolium, commonly known as the blueleaf angel's trumpets, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and showy, pale yellow to greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The leaves are broadly ovate with a distinctive blue-gray underside, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
