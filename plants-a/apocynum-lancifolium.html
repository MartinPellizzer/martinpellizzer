Apocynum lancifolium

Apocynum lancifolium, commonly known as Indian tobacco, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pinkish flowers. The plant contains toxic cardiac glycosides, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and meadows.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
