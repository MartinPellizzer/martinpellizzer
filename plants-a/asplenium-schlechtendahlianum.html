Asplenium schlechtendahlianum

Asplenium schlechtendahlianum, commonly known as the Schlechtendahlian's spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in moist, shaded environments, often in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and covered in a dense layer of tiny, scale-like structures. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in humid, high-altitude habitats.
