Azalea brookeana

Azalea brookeana, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features broad, ovate leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Azalea brookeana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
