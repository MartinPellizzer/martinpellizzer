Agropyron subulatiforme f. viride

Agropyron subulatiforme f. viride, commonly known as the green form of the needlegrass, is a variant of the species Agropyron subulatiforme.

It is a grass species characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are typically a vibrant green color. This form is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions due to its deep root system and low water requirements.

Its foliage contributes to soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
