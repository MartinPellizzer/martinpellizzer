Amyema spathulata

Amyema spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees in Australia.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain a single seed.

Amyema spathulata plays an important ecological role by providing food for native birds and insects.
