Aristida mauritiana

Aristida mauritiana, commonly known as silvergrass, is a flowering plant native to Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is a grass species that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils. The plant has narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
