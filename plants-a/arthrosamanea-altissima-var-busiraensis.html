Arthrosamanea altissima var. busiraensis

Arthrosamanea altissima var. busiraensis, commonly known as the Busira palm, is a species of palm native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish-white flowers, which are followed by elongated, three-angled fruits. Its growth habit makes it well-suited for tropical rainforest environments, where it thrives in humid, shaded conditions.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
