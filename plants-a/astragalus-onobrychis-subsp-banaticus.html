Astragalus onobrychis subsp. banaticus

Astragalus onobrychis subsp.

banaticus, commonly known as the Banat milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to southeastern Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a forage plant for livestock.
