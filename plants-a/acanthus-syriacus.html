Acanthus syriacus

Acanthus syriacus, commonly known as the Syrian acanthus, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with deep lobes and its spires of showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage and flowers are often used in ornamental gardening and historical architecture.

The species is closely related to other acanthus species and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
