Adenothamnus validus

Adenothamnus validus, commonly known as the slender saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt crystals, an adaptation to its saline environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which appear during the warmer months. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Adenothamnus validus plays a role in stabilizing soils in harsh environments and is sometimes used in rehabilitation projects.
