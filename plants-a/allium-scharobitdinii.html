Allium scharobitdinii

Allium scharobitdinii, commonly known as the Turkestan leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces edible bulbs that are similar in taste to garlic and onions, though they are less commonly cultivated. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
