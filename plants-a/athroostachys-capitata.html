Athroostachys capitata

Athroostachys capitata, commonly known as the clustered featherflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, clustered inflorescences. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and they bloom in spring and summer.

Athroostachys capitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
