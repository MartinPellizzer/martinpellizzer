Arundinaria graminea

Arundinaria graminea, commonly known as the bamboo grass, is a species of grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a grass-like appearance.

Arundinaria graminea is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its graceful appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
