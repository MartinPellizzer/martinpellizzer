Arabis transsilvanica

Arabis transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. This species typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and it has basal leaves that are lobed or toothed.

Arabis transsilvanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
