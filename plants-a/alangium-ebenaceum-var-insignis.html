Alangium ebenaceum var. insignis

Alangium ebenaceum var. insignis, commonly known as the golden alangium, is a rare and ornamental plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features glossy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem with smooth bark and a preference for well-drained, acidic soil.
