Asclepias lepida

Asclepias lepida, commonly known as the green milkweed, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Asclepias lepida is a host plant for the monarch butterfly and provides nectar for various pollinators.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
