Arabis bodiensis

Arabis bodiensis, commonly known as the Bodien's rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring.

Its leaves are basal, lobed, and often covered in a fine layer of wax, helping to reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.
