Alpinia samoensis

Alpinia samoensis, commonly known as the Samoan ginger, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Samoa and Tonga.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and related plants. The plant features strap-shaped, dark green leaves and produces fragrant, pale yellow to orange flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible rhizomes.

Alpinia samoensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.
