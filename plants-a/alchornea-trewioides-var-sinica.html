Alchornea trewioides var. sinica

Alchornea trewioides var. sinica, commonly known as Chinese alchornea, is a deciduous shrub native to southern China.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elliptical, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for garden cultivation in subtropical regions.
