Aconitum sajanense subsp. sajanense

Aconitum sajanense subsp.

sajanense, commonly known as the Sajanski aconite, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely leafed stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height.

The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense racemes. Its leaves are typically compound, with deeply lobed or divided foliage.

It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
