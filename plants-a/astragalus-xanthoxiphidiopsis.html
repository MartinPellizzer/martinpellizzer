Astragalus xanthoxiphidiopsis

Astragalus xanthoxiphidiopsis, commonly known as the yellow-veined milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain small, rounded seeds.

It is adapted to grow in poor soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.
