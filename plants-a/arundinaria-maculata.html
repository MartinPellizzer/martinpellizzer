Arundinaria maculata

Arundinaria maculata, commonly known as the bamboo orchid, is a striking ornamental plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that are often tinged with purple or pink, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its elegant, bamboo-like look.

Arundinaria maculata is well-suited for moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
