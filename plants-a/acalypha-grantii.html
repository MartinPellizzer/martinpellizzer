Acalypha grantii

Acalypha grantii, commonly known as the red-flowered croton, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking red bracts that surround small yellow flowers, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This plant is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
