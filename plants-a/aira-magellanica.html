Aira magellanica

Aira magellanica, commonly known as the Magellanic airgrass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its foliage is typically green and narrow, with a grass-like appearance.
