Ardisia pseudopedunculosa var. montana

Ardisia pseudopedunculosa var. montana, commonly known as the mountain ardisia, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. Its small, white flowers are followed by bright red berries that are edible but often contain toxic seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
