Acer cappadocicum var. tomentulosum

Acer cappadocicum var. tomentulosum, commonly known as the tomentulous maple, is a deciduous tree native to Turkey.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The tree produces small, red-orange samaras that mature in the fall, adding visual interest to the landscape. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming more fissured with age.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
