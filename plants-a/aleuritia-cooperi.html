Aleuritia cooperi

Aleuritia cooperi, commonly known as the hardy begonia, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Aleuritia cooperi is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
