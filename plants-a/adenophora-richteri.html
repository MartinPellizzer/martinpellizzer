Adenophora richteri

Adenophora richteri, commonly known as the Korean bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Adenophora richteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
