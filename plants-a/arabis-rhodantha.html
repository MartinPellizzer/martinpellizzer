Arabis rhodantha

Arabis rhodantha, commonly known as the pink rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. Arabis rhodantha thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
