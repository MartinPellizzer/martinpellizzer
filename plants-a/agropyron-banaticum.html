Agropyron banaticum

Agropyron banaticum, commonly known as the Banat grass, is a perennial grass species native to southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, leafy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in dense, cylindrical formations.

Agropyron banaticum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock.
