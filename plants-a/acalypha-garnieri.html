Acalypha garnieri

Acalypha garnieri, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silky fanflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to reddish-brown. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Acalypha garnieri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
