Arenaria curvifolia

Arenaria curvifolia, commonly known as the bent-leaved sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It typically forms dense mats of foliage with small, fleshy leaves that are often curved or twisted. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pink flowers during the summer months. It is adapted to harsh environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its botanical features make it a hardy species often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
