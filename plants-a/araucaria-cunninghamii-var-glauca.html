Araucaria cunninghamii var. glauca

Araucaria cunninghamii var. glauca, commonly known as the Blue Araucaria, is a variant of the Sydney Blue Gum.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree native to southeastern Australia, characterized by its distinctive blue-gray foliage. The leaves are needle-like, arranged in spirals along the branches, and they give the tree a striking appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
