Adenia multiflora

Adenia multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered adenia, is a striking shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often deeply lobed and have a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. These flowers are followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals.

Adenia multiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
