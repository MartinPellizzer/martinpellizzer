Abacopteris elegans

Abacopteris elegans, commonly known as the slender swamp fern, is a species of fern native to the wetlands and marshes of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistently moist soil to flourish. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it in the wetland substrate.

Abacopteris elegans plays a vital role in maintaining the ecological balance of its wetland habitat by providing shelter and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
