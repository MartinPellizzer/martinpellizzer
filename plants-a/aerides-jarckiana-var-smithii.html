Aerides jarckiana var. smithii

Aerides jarckiana var. smithii, commonly known as the Smith's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
