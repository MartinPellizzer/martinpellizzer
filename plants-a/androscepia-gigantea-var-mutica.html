Androscepia gigantea var. mutica

Androscepia gigantea var. mutica, commonly known as the giant velvet tamarind, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that eventually develop into elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often found in protected natural reserves.
