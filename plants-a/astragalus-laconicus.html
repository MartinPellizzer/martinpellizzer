Astragalus laconicus

Astragalus laconicus, commonly known as the Laconic milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow or greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are usually compound with small, oval leaflets. Astragalus laconicus is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
