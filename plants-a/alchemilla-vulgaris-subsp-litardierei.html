Alchemilla vulgaris subsp. litardierei

Alchemilla vulgaris subsp.

litardierei, commonly known as the Algerian lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to North Africa. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of softly hairy, spoon-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are clustered in umbels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and delicate blooms.
