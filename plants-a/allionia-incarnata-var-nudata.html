Allionia incarnata var. nudata

Allionia incarnata var. nudata, commonly known as the naked allionia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, showy, pink to red bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of water bodies, preferring moist to wet soils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a reddish hue, adding to its ornamental value.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in swampy environments and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
