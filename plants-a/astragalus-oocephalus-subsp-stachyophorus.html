Astragalus oocephalus subsp. stachyophorus

Astragalus oocephalus subsp.

stachyophorus, commonly known as the narrow-headed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western North America. It typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments.

The plant has small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, and its leaves are usually narrow and hairy. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with root nodules.

This subspecies is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert scrub habitats.
