Alsophila australis var. glauca

Alsophila australis var. glauca, commonly known as the glaucous tree fern, is a variant of the tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and fronds that are typically light green to bluish-green in color. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and forested areas. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with a distinctive silvery-blue hue on the underside.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
