Adicea urticella

Adicea urticella, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which can cause an irritating rash upon contact. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and disturbed areas.

Adicea urticella is also known for its edible leaves, which can be cooked and used in various culinary applications.
