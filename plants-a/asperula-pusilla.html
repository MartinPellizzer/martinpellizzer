Asperula pusilla

Asperula pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf sweet pea, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features delicate, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in spring. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Asperula pusilla is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
