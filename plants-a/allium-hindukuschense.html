Allium hindukuschense

Allium hindukuschense, commonly known as the Hindukush leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence that bears numerous small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often used in local cuisines. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, garlic-like flavor, making it a valuable crop in certain regions.

Allium hindukuschense thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to cold climates.
