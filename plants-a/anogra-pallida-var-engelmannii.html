Anogra pallida var. engelmannii

Anogra pallida var. engelmannii, commonly known as Engelmann's pale anogra, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pale green to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in fine hairs that help reduce water loss.

This variant is distinguished by its subtle coloration and compact growth habit compared to other anogra species.
