Amomum eriocarpum

Amomum eriocarpum, commonly known as the hairy cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark brown, hairy capsules that contain aromatic seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses.

The plant's rhizomes and seeds are valued for their essential oils and traditional applications in herbal medicine.
