Anisotome rosifolia

Anisotome rosifolia, commonly known as the roseleaf grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture.

Anisotome rosifolia is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
