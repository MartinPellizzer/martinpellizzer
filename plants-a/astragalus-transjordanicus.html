Astragalus transjordanicus

Astragalus transjordanicus, commonly known as Transjordan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus transjordanicus is often used in traditional medicine and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological and potential medicinal significance.
