Acer pseudoplatanus var. macrocarpum

Acer pseudoplatanus var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the giant maple, is a variant of the common maple tree.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 20 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras in the autumn. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This variety is notable for its robust growth and striking foliage, making it a popular choice in gardens and parks.
