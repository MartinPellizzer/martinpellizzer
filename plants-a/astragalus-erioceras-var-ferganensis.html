Astragalus erioceras var. ferganensis

Astragalus erioceras var. ferganensis, commonly known as the Fergana milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological and agricultural studies.
