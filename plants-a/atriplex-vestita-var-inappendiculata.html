Atriplex vestita var. inappendiculata

Atriplex vestita var. inappendiculata, commonly known as the desert saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its salt-tolerant nature makes it well adapted to saline soils and coastal areas.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
