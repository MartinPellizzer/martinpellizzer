Aechmea odora

Aechmea odora, commonly known as the fragrant pineapple bromeliad, is a striking plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The plant produces striking inflorescences with colorful bracts that range from red to orange, often resembling a pineapple in appearance. Aechmea odora is known for its pleasant, sweet fragrance, especially when the flowers open.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Morphology

Aechmea odora has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, leathery, and arranged in a tight spiral, with a dark green to bluish-green color and a waxy surface.

The stem is short and thick, with a smooth, grayish-brown bark that grows from the base of the plant. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix surrounded by colorful bracts, typically red or orange, and bloom in late spring to early summer; they are pollinated primarily by insects. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that turn red when mature, and the seeds are dispersed by wind and animals.

Its roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in the soil, with some rhizomes that help in vegetative propagation.
