Anemone obtusiloba subsp. saxicola

Anemone obtusiloba subsp.

saxicola, commonly known as the Sierra anemone, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in rocky and alpine habitats, showcasing a preference for well-drained soils.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape, with a woolly texture. Its flowers are showy, with white petals and yellow centers, blooming in early to mid-summer.

It is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
