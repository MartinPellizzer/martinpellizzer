Amphiscopia venosa

Amphiscopia venosa, commonly known as the veined orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, typically displaying a combination of white, green, and purple hues. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its delicate structure and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
