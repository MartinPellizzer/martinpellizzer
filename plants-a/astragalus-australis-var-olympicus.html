Astragalus australis var. olympicus

Astragalus australis var. olympicus, commonly known as the Olympic milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.
