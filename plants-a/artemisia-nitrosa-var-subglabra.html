Artemisia nitrosa var. subglabra

Artemisia nitrosa var. subglabra, commonly known as silverleaf tansy, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in compact clusters called capitula. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

Its aromatic leaves contain volatile oils, making it a plant of interest in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
