Achyranthes pedicellata

Achyranthes pedicellata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spurge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers surrounded by bracts, which are typical of the spurge family. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Achyranthes pedicellata is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
