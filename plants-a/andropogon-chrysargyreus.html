Andropogon chrysargyreus

Andropogon chrysargyreus, commonly known as the golden bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blue-green leaves that turn golden in the fall. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear yellowish to golden flowers, giving it its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Andropogon chrysargyreus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for wildlife.
