Astragalus neomozaffarianii

Astragalus neomozaffarianii, commonly known as the Neomozaffarian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
