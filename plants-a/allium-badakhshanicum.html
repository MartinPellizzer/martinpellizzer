Allium badakhshanicum

Allium badakhshanicum, commonly known as the Badakhshan garlic, is a bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces edible bulbs that are used in local cuisines for their pungent flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and somewhat endangered species.
