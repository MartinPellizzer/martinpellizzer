Amphiscopia martiana

Amphiscopia martiana, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil and decaying organic matter.

Amphiscopia martiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
