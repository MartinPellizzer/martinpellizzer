Acacia leucoclada

Acacia leucoclada, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Acacia leucoclada is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
