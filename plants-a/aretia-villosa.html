Aretia villosa

Aretia villosa, commonly known as the hairy aretia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Aretia villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and blooms.
