Aquilegia saximontana

Aquilegia saximontana, commonly known as the Sierra foothill columbine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. The plant features striking blue to purple spurred petals and bright yellow anthers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of palmately divided leaves with a coarse texture.

Aquilegia saximontana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native pollinator species.
