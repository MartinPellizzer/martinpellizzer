Artemisia suksdorfii

Artemisia suksdorfii, commonly known as the Saksdorf's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flower heads that appear in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its strong, camphor-like scent. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
