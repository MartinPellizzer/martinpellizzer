Aira tenorei proles intermedia

Aira tenorei proles intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate tenorei grass, is a perennial grass species native to the island of Sicily.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and coastal areas. It produces small, pale green flowers in dense, spike-like clusters, which are arranged in a distinct panicle inflorescence.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
