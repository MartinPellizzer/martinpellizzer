Acanthinophyllum strepitans

Acanthinophyllum strepitans, commonly known as the whistling thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, woolly leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in compact clusters. Native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its common name is derived from the sound it produces when the leaves are brushed, resembling a whistling noise.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
