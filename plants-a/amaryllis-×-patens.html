Amaryllis × patens

Amaryllis × patens, commonly known as the belladonna lily, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis paradisicola.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, producing showy blooms in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are broad, strap-like, and often persist through the winter in mild climates.

Amaryllis × patens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
