Aloe pembana

Aloe pembana, commonly known as the "miracle plant" or "life-giving aloe," is a succulent plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, spiky leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to its appearance. Aloe pembana is known for its ability to purify the air and has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties.

Its resilience and adaptability make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
