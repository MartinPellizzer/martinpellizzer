Acer mono f. latialatum

Acer mono f. latialatum, commonly known as the broad-leaf maple, is a unique variant of the maple tree characterized by its wide, ovate leaves.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value, featuring a striking combination of yellow-green foliage in spring and vibrant red leaves in autumn. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight. The plant is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its distinctive leaf shape and seasonal color changes contribute to its appeal in horticultural settings.
