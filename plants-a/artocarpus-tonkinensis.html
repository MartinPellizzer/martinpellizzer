Artocarpus tonkinensis

Artocarpus tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive fruit. The fruit, which is typically green when mature, develops a rough, bark-like exterior and contains a soft, edible pulp with seeds. The plant is often cultivated for its fruit, which is used in traditional cuisines and has a sweet, nutty flavor.

It thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
