Anthemis oppositifolia

Anthemis oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, yellowish-orange composite flowers. The plant has opposite leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape, often with hairy surfaces. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Anthemis oppositifolia is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
