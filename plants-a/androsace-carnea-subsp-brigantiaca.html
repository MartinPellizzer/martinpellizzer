Androsace carnea subsp. brigantiaca

Androsace carnea subsp.

brigantiaca, commonly known as the Alpine pink, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It features dense, mat-forming foliage with small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its flowers typically bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine displays.
