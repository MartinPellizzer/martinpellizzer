Andropogon latiflorus

Andropogon latiflorus, commonly known as broadflower bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy blue to purple flower heads that appear in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in moist to wet soils in prairies and wetlands. It plays an important role in ecological systems by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.

Broadflower bluestem is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping.
