Alsine fontinalis

Alsine fontinalis, commonly known as the spring beauty, is a small flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, silky hair.

Alsine fontinalis is known for its short stature and early blooming habit, making it a notable indicator plant for early spring conditions.
