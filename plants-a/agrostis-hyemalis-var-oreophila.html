Agrostis hyemalis var. oreophila

Agrostis hyemalis var. oreophila, commonly known as the alpine sweetgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growths and is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Its inflorescence consists of slender, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to brownish flowers.

Agrostis hyemalis var. oreophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude ecosystems.
