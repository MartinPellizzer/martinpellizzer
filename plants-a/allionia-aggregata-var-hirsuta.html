Allionia aggregata var. hirsuta

Allionia aggregata var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy allionia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive purple hue at the base. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
