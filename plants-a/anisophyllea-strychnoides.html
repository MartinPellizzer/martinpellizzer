Anisophyllea strychnoides

Anisophyllea strychnoides, commonly known as the Indian bell pepper, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with opposite, ovate leaflets that have a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by berries that mature to a bright red color. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
