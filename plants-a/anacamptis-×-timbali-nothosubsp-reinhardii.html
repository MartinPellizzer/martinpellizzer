Anacamptis × timbali nothosubsp. reinhardii

Anacamptis × timbali nothosubsp.

reinhardii, commonly known as the Reinhard’s hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Anacamptis morio and Anacamptis pyramidalis. It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing showy, spurred orchid flowers.

This nothosubspecies is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts due to its unique genetic makeup and ornamental value.
