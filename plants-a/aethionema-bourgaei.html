Aethionema bourgaei

Aethionema bourgaei, commonly known as the Syrian rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that typically thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch to its habitat. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in dry conditions.

Aethionema bourgaei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
