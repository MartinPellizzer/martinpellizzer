Amelanchier cretica var. chelmea

Amelanchier cretica var. chelmea, commonly known as the Chelmea serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its bark is smooth and reddish-brown. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and wildlife benefits.
