Astragalus erythrostachys

Astragalus erythrostachys, commonly known as red-stemmed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often reddish stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or steppe environments.

Astragalus erythrostachys is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
