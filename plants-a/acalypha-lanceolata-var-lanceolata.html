Acalypha lanceolata var. lanceolata

Acalypha lanceolata var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often bright green with a reddish tint on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, low-maintenance foliage.
