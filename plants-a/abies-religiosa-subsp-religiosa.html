Abies religiosa subsp. religiosa

Abies religiosa subsp.

religiosa, commonly known as the sacred fir, is a species of fir tree native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico. It is characterized by its dense, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in a single layer.

The tree produces small, spherical cones that release seeds, contributing to its reproductive cycle. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests at high elevations.

The plant has cultural significance in indigenous communities and is valued for its ornamental and ecological roles.
