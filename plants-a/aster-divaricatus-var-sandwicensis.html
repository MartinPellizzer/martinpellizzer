Aster divaricatus var. sandwicensis

Aster divaricatus var. sandwicensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian bluebell, is a perennial herb native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and sprawling.

This variety is considered rare and is of significant ecological and cultural importance to Hawaii.
