Alchemilla purohitii

Alchemilla purohitii, commonly known as the Himalayan lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine for its anti-inflammatory and astringent effects.

Alchemilla purohitii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
