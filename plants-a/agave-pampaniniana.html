Agave pampaniniana

Agave pampaniniana, commonly known as the Pampas agave, is a species native to Argentina.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears yellowish-green flowers, which are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to poor soil conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes in suitable climates.
