Astragalus rubromarginatus var. oeroilanicus

Astragalus rubromarginatus var. oeroilanicus, commonly known as the red-margin milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
