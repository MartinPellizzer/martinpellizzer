Amaryllis exigua

Amaryllis exigua, commonly known as the dwarf amaryllis, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a bulbous plant that produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, white, and pink. The plant typically grows to a height of only 10 to 15 centimeters, making it a compact and ornamental choice for gardens. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from the base of the bulb.

Amaryllis exigua is well-suited for container gardening and can be grown indoors or outdoors in suitable climates.
