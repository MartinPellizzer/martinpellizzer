Acoridium curranii

Acoridium curranii, commonly known as the Curran's arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acoraceae family, which includes aquatic and semi-aquatic plants. The plant features broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a distinctive waxy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically greenish-yellow in color, and it produces a unique, spirally arranged fruit.

Acoridium curranii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
