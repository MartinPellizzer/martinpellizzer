Amphinomia strigillosa

Amphinomia strigillosa, commonly known as the roughleaf spurge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving them a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Euphorbiaceae family.
