Arundinaria chino var. laydekeri

Arundinaria chino var. laydekeri, commonly known as Chinese bamboo or bamboo grass, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that form dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a bluish-green color and a smooth texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and traditional Chinese medicine.
