Alsodeia horneri

Alsodeia horneri, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces yellow, dandelion-like flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant has a hairy stem and leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
