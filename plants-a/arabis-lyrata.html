Arabis lyrata

Arabis lyrata, commonly known as the musical rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, often blooming in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and have a distinctive lobed shape.

Arabis lyrata thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine environments.
