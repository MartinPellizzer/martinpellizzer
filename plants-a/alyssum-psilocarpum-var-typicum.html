Alyssum psilocarpum var. typicum

Alyssum psilocarpum var. typicum, commonly known as the ghostflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its unique appearance and historical significance, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
