Amaranthus angustifolius

Amaranthus angustifolius, commonly known as pigweed, is an annual herb native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its narrow, feather-like leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of climates. Its seeds are small and wind-dispersed, allowing for rapid propagation.

Amaranthus angustifolius is also known for its nutritional value, containing high levels of protein and minerals.
