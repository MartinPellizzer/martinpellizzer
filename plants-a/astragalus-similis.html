Astragalus similis

Astragalus similis, commonly known as the similar milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Astragalus similis has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native range.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
