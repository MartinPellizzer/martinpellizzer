Abutilon inflatum

Abutilon inflatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian mallow, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in summer. Its capsules bear numerous small, hairy seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Abutilon inflatum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.
