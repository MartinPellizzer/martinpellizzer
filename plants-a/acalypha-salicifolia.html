Acalypha salicifolia

Acalypha salicifolia, commonly known as the silky ironwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It is often found growing in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
