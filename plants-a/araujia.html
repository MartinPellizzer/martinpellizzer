Araujia

Araujia, commonly known as the moonflower or nightshade vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night and emit a strong fragrance. The plant is a climbing vine with woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically trumpet-shaped and have a distinctive, sweet scent.

Araujia is often cultivated for its ornamental value, though some species contain toxic compounds.
