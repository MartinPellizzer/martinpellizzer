Amygdalus kuramica

Amygdalus kuramica, commonly known as the Kuramica almond, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Turkey and northern Iran. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single almond-like seed enclosed in a hard shell.

The botanical characteristics of Amygdalus kuramica make it a notable species within the Prunus genus, though it is less commonly cultivated than other almond varieties.
