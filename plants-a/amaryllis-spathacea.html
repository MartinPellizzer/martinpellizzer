Amaryllis spathacea

Amaryllis spathacea, commonly known as the yellow amaryllis, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can range in color from white to yellow and pink. The plant produces strap-like leaves that are often bluish-green in hue. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Amaryllis spathacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
