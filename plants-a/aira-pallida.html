Aira pallida

Aira pallida, commonly known as the pale aira, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, pale blue to white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Aira pallida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
