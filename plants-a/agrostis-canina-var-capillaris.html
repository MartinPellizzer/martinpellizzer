Agrostis canina var. capillaris

Agrostis canina var. capillaris, commonly known as silky bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves that give it a soft, silky appearance. The plant typically forms dense, mat-like growths and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
