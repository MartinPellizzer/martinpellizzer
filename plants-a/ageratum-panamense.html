Ageratum panamense

Ageratum panamense, commonly known as Panama ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant produces blue to purple flower heads with white ray florets, which are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Ageratum panamense is valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
