Adenostyles alpina subsp. alpina

Adenostyles alpina subsp.

alpina, commonly known as the alpine adenostyle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, often on rocky slopes and meadows.

The plant features a slender, erect stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually pink or purple in color.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude environments.
