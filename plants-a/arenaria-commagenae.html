Arenaria commagenae

Arenaria commagenae, commonly known as the Commagene sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically linear and covered in fine hairs, adapted to the harsh, arid conditions of its habitat.

Arenaria commagenae is often found in rocky soils and alpine meadows, showcasing its resilience to extreme environmental conditions.
