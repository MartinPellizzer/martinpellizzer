Aphelexis macrantha

Aphelexis macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered tamarisk, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are small, linear, and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Aphelexis macrantha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
