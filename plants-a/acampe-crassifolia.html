Acampe crassifolia

Acampe crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved acampe, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fleshy leaves that are adapted to conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its stems are woody and often branched, providing structural support in its harsh habitat.

Acampe crassifolia is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
