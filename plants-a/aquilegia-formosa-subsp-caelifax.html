Aquilegia formosa subsp. caelifax

Aquilegia formosa subsp.

caelifax, commonly known as the Sierra Colorado columbine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features distinctive spurred petals that are typically blue or purple, with a deep red or orange base.

The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces clusters of showy flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
