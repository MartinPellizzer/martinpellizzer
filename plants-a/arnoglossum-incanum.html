Arnoglossum incanum

Arnoglossum incanum, commonly known as the hoary pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its grayish-white, fuzzy leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. Its flowers are tubular and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Arnoglossum incanum is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun and well-drained soils.
