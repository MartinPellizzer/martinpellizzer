Astragalus glycyphyllos

Astragalus glycyphyllos, commonly known as sweet caraway, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain numerous tiny, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its aromatic foliage and historical use in traditional medicine, it holds cultural and ecological significance in its native range.
