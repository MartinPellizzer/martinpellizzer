Allium esfandiarii

Allium esfandiarii, commonly known as the Esfandiari onion, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Iran and is characterized by its bulbous stem and clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It is a hardy perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable addition to botanical collections.
