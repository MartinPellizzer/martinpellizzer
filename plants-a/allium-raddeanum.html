Allium raddeanum

Allium raddeanum, commonly known as the Siberian garlic, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Eastern Europe and Asia.

It features a bulbous stem that produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in a dense umbel, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.

Botanically, it belongs to the Allium genus, which includes many edible and medicinal plants.
