Aspidium ramosum

Aspidium ramosum, commonly known as the branched water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically divided into smaller pinnae. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in humid forests. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Aspidium ramosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
