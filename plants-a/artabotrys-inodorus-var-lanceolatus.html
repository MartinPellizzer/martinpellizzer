Artabotrys inodorus var. lanceolatus

Artabotrys inodorus var. lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceolate beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-purple bracts that surround its small, white flowers. The plant produces dark blue, oval berries that are edible but often have a slightly bitter taste. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring partial shade and well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
