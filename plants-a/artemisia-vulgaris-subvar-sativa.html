Artemisia vulgaris subvar. sativa

Artemisia vulgaris subvar. sativa, commonly known as mugwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage and small yellow flowers that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Mugwort has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for centuries.
