Acacia isoneura subsp. nimia

Acacia isoneura subsp.

nimia, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures.

The plant produces yellow to golden yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are visited by various pollinators. It is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh environments. The wood of Acacia isoneura subsp.

nimia is hard and durable, historically used by local communities for construction and tool-making.
