Astragalina alpestris

Astragalina alpestris, commonly known as the alpine milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalina alpestris plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
