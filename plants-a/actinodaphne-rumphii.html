Actinodaphne rumphii

Actinodaphne rumphii, commonly known as the Philippine laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark-barked trunk and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and it is often found in lowland to montane forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
