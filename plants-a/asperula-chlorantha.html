Asperula chlorantha

Asperula chlorantha, commonly known as the silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in early summer, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. Asperula chlorantha thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded locations.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
