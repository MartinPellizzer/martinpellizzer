Artocarpus tephrophyllus

Artocarpus tephrophyllus, commonly known as the giant breadfruit, is a large evergreen tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and produces a large, spiky fruit that is botanically classified as a syncarp. The plant belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries. Its fruit contains a starchy pulp that is edible when ripe, and the tree is often cultivated for its nutritional value.
