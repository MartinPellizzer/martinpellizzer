Agrostis magellanica var. cognata

Agrostis magellanica var. cognata, commonly known as the Magellanic bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to southern South America.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal areas and open grasslands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, including strong winds and salt spray.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and habitat conservation.
