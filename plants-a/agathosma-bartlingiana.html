Agathosma bartlingiana

Agathosma bartlingiana, commonly known as Cape spearmint, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is known for its strong, mint-like scent, which is due to the presence of essential oils in its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Agathosma bartlingiana is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils for aromatherapy.
