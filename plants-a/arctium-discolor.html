Arctium discolor

Arctium discolor, commonly known as the purple burr or lesser burdock, is a biennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant produces distinctive purple or brown bracts that form spiny, seed-bearing structures called burrs. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

Arctium discolor is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.
