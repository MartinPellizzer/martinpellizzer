Adonis marschalliana

Adonis marschalliana, commonly known as the marshallian adonis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers are composed of numerous stamens and a single pistil, and it is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Adonis marschalliana is also valued for its medicinal properties, though it contains toxic compounds that require careful handling.
