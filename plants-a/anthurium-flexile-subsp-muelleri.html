Anthurium flexile subsp. muelleri

Anthurium flexile subsp.

muelleri, commonly known as the Mueller's slipper anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its elongated, waxy leaves that can grow up to several feet in length.

The plant produces striking, spadix-shaped inflorescences surrounded by a broad, colorful spathe. It thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
