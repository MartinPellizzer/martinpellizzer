Astracantha echidna

Astracantha echidna, commonly known as the echidna thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of grey-green, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine spines. The plant produces tall, spiny stems that bear purple to pinkish flowers with five petals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
