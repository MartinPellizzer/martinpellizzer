Aleuritia apoclita

Aleuritia apoclita, commonly known as the silverleaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine silvery down, giving it its common name. Its fruit is a small, red drupe that ripens in late summer.

Aleuritia apoclita is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
