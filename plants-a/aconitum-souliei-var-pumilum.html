Aconitum souliei var. pumilum

Aconitum souliei var. pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf Chinese monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmate leaves with deeply lobed margins, and its roots contain toxic alkaloids, making it potentially hazardous to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and toxicity, it is cultivated in gardens but requires careful handling.
