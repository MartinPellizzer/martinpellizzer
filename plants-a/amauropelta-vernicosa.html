Amauropelta vernicosa

Amauropelta vernicosa, commonly known as the glossy shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, overlapping fronds that form a protective shield-like structure. The leaves are typically dark green and glossy, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss in humid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spore cases on the undersides of its fronds, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.

It thrives in shaded, moist habitats and is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks in forest understories.
