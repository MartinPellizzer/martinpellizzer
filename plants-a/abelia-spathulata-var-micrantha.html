Abelia spathulata var. micrantha

Abelia spathulata var. micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf abelia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to eastern North America.

It features narrow, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to a range of garden conditions.
