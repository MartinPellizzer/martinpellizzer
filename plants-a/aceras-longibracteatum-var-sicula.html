Aceras longibracteatum var. sicula

Aceras longibracteatum var. sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in mild climates.
