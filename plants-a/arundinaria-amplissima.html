Arundinaria amplissima

Arundinaria amplissima, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, perennial grass native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Arundinaria amplissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
