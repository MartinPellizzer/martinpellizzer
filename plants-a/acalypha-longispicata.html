Acalypha longispicata

Acalypha longispicata, commonly known as the long-spined spurflower, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny bracts that surround its small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Acalypha longispicata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and floral structure.
