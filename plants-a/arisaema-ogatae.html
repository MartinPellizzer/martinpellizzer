Arisaema ogatae

Arisaema ogatae, commonly known as the Japanese mountain arisaema, is a unique member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, hooded spathe that encloses a spadix, a typical feature of arum plants. The plant produces both male and female flowers on the spadix, which are often separated by a sterile zone. Native to Japan, it thrives in cool, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical structure and growth habits make it a distinctive and fascinating species within the genus Arisaema.
