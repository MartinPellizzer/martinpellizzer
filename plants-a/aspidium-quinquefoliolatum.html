Aspidium quinquefoliolatum

Aspidium quinquefoliolatum, commonly known as the five-foliolate water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are divided into multiple pinnae, with each pinna further subdivided into small, pointed segments called pinnules. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along streams or in forest understories, where it thrives in high humidity. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori, which are protected by a thin, membranous covering.

Aspidium quinquefoliolatum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
