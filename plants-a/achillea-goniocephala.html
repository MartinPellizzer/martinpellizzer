Achillea goniocephala

Achillea goniocephala, commonly known as the mountain yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators in its native habitats.
