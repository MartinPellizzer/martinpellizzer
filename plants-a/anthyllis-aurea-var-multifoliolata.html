Anthyllis aurea var. multifoliolata

Anthyllis aurea var. multifoliolata, commonly known as the golden broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in arid environments.
