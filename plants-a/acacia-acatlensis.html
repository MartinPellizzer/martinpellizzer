Acacia acatlensis

Acacia acatlensis, commonly known as the Mexican acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant is distinguished by its slender, often thorned stems and bipinnate leaves that are typically small and feathery. Its yellow flowers are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences, and it produces small, woody pods containing seeds.

Acacia acatlensis is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
