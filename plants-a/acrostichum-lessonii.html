Acrostichum lessonii

Acrostichum lessonii, commonly known as the New Zealand tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family.

It is a tall, tree-like fern that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with a trunk that is fibrous and covered in a layer of dead fronds. Its fronds are pinnate, with a long, narrow rachis and numerous pinnae that are often deeply lobed. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to New Zealand.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various species.
