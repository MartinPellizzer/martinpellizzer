Axonopus aureus var. pilosus

Axonopus aureus var. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy golden carpet grass, is a low-growing, perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with narrow, bright green leaves that have a distinctive hairy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by delicate, feathery seed heads. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ground-covering ability.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various environmental conditions, contributing to its popularity in horticulture.
