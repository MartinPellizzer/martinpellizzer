Alpinia africana

Alpinia africana, commonly known as African ginger, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring broad, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces fragrant, pale yellow to white flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its rhizomes are aromatic and have been utilized for their culinary and medicinal properties.

Alpinia africana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
