Anamelis

Anamelis, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces small, round, orange fruits that contain seeds. Anamelis thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be grown as a decorative vine in gardens or as a houseplant.
