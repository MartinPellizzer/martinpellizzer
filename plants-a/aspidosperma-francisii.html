Aspidosperma francisii

Aspidosperma francisii, commonly known as the African tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lanceolate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing flat, brown seeds. It is often found in savanna and woodland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in various herbal remedies.
