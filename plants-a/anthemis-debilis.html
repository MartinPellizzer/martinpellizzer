Anthemis debilis

Anthemis debilis, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer, followed by fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

The leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapting to arid conditions.
