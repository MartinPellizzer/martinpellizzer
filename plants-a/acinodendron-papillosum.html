Acinodendron papillosum

Acinodendron papillosum, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, papillate (bumpy) fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit, which is a legume, contains seeds that are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appearance and is occasionally cultivated for its aesthetic qualities.
