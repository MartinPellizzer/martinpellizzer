Acaulimalva acaulis

Acaulimalva acaulis, commonly known as the cushion mallows, is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form, with many small, tightly packed stems that form a compact rosette. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short pedicels above the foliage. Its leaves are typically woolly or hairy, providing a distinctive texture and aiding in water retention.

Acaulimalva acaulis is well adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
