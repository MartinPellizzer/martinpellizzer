Arundinella riparia

Arundinella riparia, commonly known as the river reed, is a perennial grass species native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly and form extensive colonies.

Arundinella riparia is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.
