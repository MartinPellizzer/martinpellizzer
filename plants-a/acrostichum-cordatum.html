Acrostichum cordatum

Acrostichum cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often near streams or in dense forests. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with a soft, feathery appearance.

Acrostichum cordatum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
