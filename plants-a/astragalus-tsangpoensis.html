Astragalus tsangpoensis

Astragalus tsangpoensis, commonly known as the Tsangpo milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by distinctive pod fruits containing several seeds.

Astragalus tsangpoensis is adapted to cold and arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
