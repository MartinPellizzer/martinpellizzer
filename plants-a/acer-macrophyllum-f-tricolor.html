Acer macrophyllum f. tricolor

Acer macrophyllum f. tricolor, commonly known as the variegated maple, is a cultivar of the Pacific maple.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, which features a combination of green, white, and gold colors. The plant is native to the western United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 15 feet.

The leaves are palmately lobed and turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall, making it a popular ornamental plant.
