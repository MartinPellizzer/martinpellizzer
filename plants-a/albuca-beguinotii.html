Albuca beguinotii

Albuca beguinotii, commonly known as the Cape daffodil, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Albuca beguinotii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
