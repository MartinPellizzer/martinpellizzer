Aldina aurea

Aldina aurea, commonly known as golden alder, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or stream banks. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in clusters, and are typically yellow or greenish in color.

Aldina aurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage.
