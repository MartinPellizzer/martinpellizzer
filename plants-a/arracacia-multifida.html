Arracacia multifida

Arracacia multifida, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a distinctive, lobed appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
