Aporum porphyrophyllum

Aporum porphyrophyllum, commonly known as the purple-leafed aporum, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, purple-tinged leaves that are often veined with a deep red hue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a subtle contrast to its vibrant foliage. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
