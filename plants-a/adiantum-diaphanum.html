Adiantum diaphanum

Adiantum diaphanum, commonly known as the translucent maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching fronds that are covered with fine, translucent veins, giving it a unique and ethereal appearance. The plant prefers cool, moist environments and thrives in partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden settings. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of the Adiantum genus.

Due to its ornamental value and delicate nature, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in specialized botanical gardens.
