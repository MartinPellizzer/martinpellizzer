Acinos graveolens

Acinos graveolens, commonly known as the sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form with dense clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces numerous tiny, white, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Its leaves are gray-green and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Acinos graveolens is known for its aromatic properties and is often used in herbal remedies.
