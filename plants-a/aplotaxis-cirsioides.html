Aplotaxis cirsioides

Aplotaxis cirsioides, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its resilience to drought makes it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.
