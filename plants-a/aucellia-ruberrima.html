Aucellia ruberrima

Aucellia ruberrima, commonly known as the crimson bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking red petals and bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Aucellia ruberrima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
