Alnus glutinosa f. maculata

Alnus glutinosa f. maculata, commonly known as the spotted alder, is a variant of the European alder characterized by its distinctive bark and foliage.

This plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a yellowish or reddish blotch on the underside. The flowers are catkins, typical of the Betulaceae family, and it produces small, winged seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones or near water bodies.
