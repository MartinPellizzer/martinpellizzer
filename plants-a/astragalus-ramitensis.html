Astragalus ramitensis

Astragalus ramitensis, commonly known as the ramose milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale green flowers. The plant is adapted to dry environments, with deep root systems that help it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
