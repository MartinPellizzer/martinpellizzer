Asplenium × gracile

Asplenium × gracile, commonly known as the slender maidenhair fern, is a hybrid species within the Aspleniaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically 20–40 cm in length. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in temperate regions.

Asplenium × gracile is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
