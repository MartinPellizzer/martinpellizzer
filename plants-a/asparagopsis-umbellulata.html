Asparagopsis umbellulata

Asparagopsis umbellulata, commonly known as the sea asparagus, is a marine flowering plant belonging to the Asparagaceae family.

It is characterized by its branching, fleshy stems and small, white or greenish flowers that bloom in umbel-like clusters. The plant thrives in shallow coastal waters, often found in intertidal zones where it can tolerate both saltwater and freshwater conditions. Its leaves are typically reduced to scale-like structures, while its stems are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

Asparagopsis umbellulata plays an important ecological role in coastal ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various marine organisms.
