Agelaea paradoxa var. paradoxa

Agelaea paradoxa var. paradoxa, commonly known as the paradoxical agelaea, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, spiraling inflorescence and striking, often iridescent flowers that change color depending on light conditions. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Its leaves are typically broad and have a waxy texture, adapted to retain moisture in its native habitat.

Due to its rarity and distinctive features, it is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists alike.
