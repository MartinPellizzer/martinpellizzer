Aspalathus salicifolia

Aspalathus salicifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves that are silvery-white underneath, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, rounded fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
