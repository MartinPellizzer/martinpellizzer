Asparagus buruensis

Asparagus buruensis, commonly known as the Buru asparagus, is a flowering plant native to the island of Buru in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its root system is fibrous, and it is adapted to grow in well-drained, sandy soils.

Asparagus buruensis is primarily of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and restricted geographic distribution.
