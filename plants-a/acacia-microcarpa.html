Acacia microcarpa

Acacia microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Acacia microcarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand drought conditions.
