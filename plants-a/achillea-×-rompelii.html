Achillea × rompelii

Achillea × rompelii, commonly known as the hybrid yarrow, is a perennial plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is a cross between Achillea millefolium (common yarrow) and Achillea × armandii, resulting in a plant with distinctive, feathery foliage. The species is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

This plant is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
