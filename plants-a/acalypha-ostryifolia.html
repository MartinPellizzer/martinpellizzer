Acalypha ostryifolia

Acalypha ostryifolia, commonly known as the white-leaf ironwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive, clustered inflorescences that often display a striking white or pinkish hue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by three-winged fruits, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is cultivated in some tropical gardens.
