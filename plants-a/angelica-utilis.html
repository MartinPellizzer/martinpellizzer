Angelica utilis

Angelica utilis, commonly known as common angelica, is a biennial or perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes other aromatic plants like carrots and parsley. The plant is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, umbrella-shaped compound leaves. Its flowers are small, white or greenish-yellow, and it produces dark, berry-like fruits.

Angelica utilis is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.
