Azalea trichoclada

Azalea trichoclada, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a flowering shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. Azalea trichoclada prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in a variety of climatic conditions.
