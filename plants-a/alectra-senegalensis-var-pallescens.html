Alectra senegalensis var. pallescens

Alectra senegalensis var. pallescens, commonly known as the Senegalese sandbur, is a flowering plant native to the Sahelian regions of Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows in sandy or dry soils. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
