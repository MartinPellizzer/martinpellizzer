Aira flammosa

Aira flammosa, commonly known as the flame grass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and clusters of small, bright orange to red flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its scientific name reflects its fiery appearance, with "flammosa" meaning "fiery" in Latin.

Aira flammosa is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
