Adenostyles albida

Adenostyles albida, commonly known as the white adenostyle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. Its small, white or pale pink flowers are arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems.

Adenostyles albida is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, airy foliage and blooms.
