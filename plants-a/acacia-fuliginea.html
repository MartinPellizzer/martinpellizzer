Acacia fuliginea

Acacia fuliginea, commonly known as the black wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, woody bark and compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which are followed by flat, ribbed pods. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy or rocky soils.

Acacia fuliginea is also known for its historical use in the tanning industry due to its high tannin content.
