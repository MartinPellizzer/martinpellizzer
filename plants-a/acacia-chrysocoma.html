Acacia chrysocoma

Acacia chrysocoma, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are yellowish-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact spikes, which are followed by flat, brown pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to semi-arid and arid environments.

Acacia chrysocoma is often used in traditional medicine and for soil stabilization in degraded landscapes.
