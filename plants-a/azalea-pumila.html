Azalea pumila

Azalea pumila, commonly known as the dwarf azalea, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and provide dense foliage. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Azalea pumila is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests and diseases, contributing to its popularity among gardeners.
