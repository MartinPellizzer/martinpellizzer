Abrus precatorius f. luteoseminalis

Abrus precatorius f. luteoseminalis, commonly known as the golden seed maple or yellow-seeded Abrus, is a variant of the common monkey bean.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow seeds, which contrast with the typical red or black seeds of the species. The plant is a climbing vine with compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded, moist environments.

Its ornamental value is largely due to its striking seed color, making it a popular choice for botanical gardens and collections.
