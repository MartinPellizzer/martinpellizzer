Acacia holcocarpa

Acacia holcocarpa, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched trunk and compound leaves that are bipinnate, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by distinctive, woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally for its seeds, which are a source of food and oil.
