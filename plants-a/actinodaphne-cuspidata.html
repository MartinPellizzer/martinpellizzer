Actinodaphne cuspidata

Actinodaphne cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been traditionally used in medicine and as a source of camphor.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
