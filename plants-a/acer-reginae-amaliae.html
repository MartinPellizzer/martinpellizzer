Acer reginae-amaliae

Acer reginae-amaliae, commonly known as the Queen Amalia maple, is a rare and ornamental species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often displaying vibrant colors during the autumn season. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in the spring, followed by samaras that aid in its seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is highly valued in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
