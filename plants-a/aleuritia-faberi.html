Aleuritia faberi

Aleuritia faberi, commonly known as the Chinese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aleuritia faberi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
