Acacia decora

Acacia decora, commonly known as the weeping acacia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern.

Acacia decora is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique form and seasonal blooms.
