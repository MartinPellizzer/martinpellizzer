Aster pyrenaeus

Aster pyrenaeus, commonly known as the Pyrenean aster, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It typically grows in rocky and open habitats, often found in alpine and subalpine zones. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces violet to blue flowers in the summer months. It is characterized by its sturdy stems and is often found in dry, sunny environments.

Aster pyrenaeus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
