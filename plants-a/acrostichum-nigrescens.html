Acrostichum nigrescens

Acrostichum nigrescens, commonly known as the black fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests or along stream banks. Its spores are produced on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in sori.

Acrostichum nigrescens plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
