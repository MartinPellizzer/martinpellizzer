Agrostis alba var. compacta

Agrostis alba var. compacta, commonly known as silvergrass or whitegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and silvery-blue foliage due to the fine, soft hairs covering its leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Agrostis alba var. compacta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
