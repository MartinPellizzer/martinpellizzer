Aster longifolius var. laevigatus

Aster longifolius var. laevigatus, commonly known as the smoothleaf aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers in the late summer and early fall. Its flowering stems are usually branched and bear numerous flower heads, which are composed of tiny tubular florets.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and bogs.
