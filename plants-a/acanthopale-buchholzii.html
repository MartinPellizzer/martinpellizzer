Acanthopale buchholzii

Acanthopale buchholzii, commonly known as the Buchholz's acanthopale, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
