Aeschynomene katangensis subsp. katangensis

Aeschynomene katangensis subsp.

katangensis, commonly known as the Katanga burr, is a flowering plant native to Central Africa. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads. It is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions.

Its seeds are encased in hard pods, which contribute to its role in soil stabilization and ecological diversity.
