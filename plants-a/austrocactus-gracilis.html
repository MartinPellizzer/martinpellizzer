Austrocactus gracilis

Austrocactus gracilis, commonly known as the slender desert cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically borne at the top of the stem. Austrocactus gracilis is adapted to extreme drought conditions, storing water in its succulent stems.

Its unique morphology makes it a striking addition to desert landscapes and a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
