Adonis dentata var. pseudoflammea

Adonis dentata var. pseudoflammea, commonly known as the false flame belladonna, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are palmately divided with serrated edges, and it produces capsules containing numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
