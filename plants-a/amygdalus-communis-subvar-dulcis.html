Amygdalus communis subvar. dulcis

Amygdalus communis subvar. dulcis, commonly known as the sweet almond tree, is a member of the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its small, ovate leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The tree produces oval, woody pods containing seeds, which are the source of edible almonds. The fruit is typically harvested when the pods split open, revealing the kernel inside.

This variety is cultivated for its nutritional value and is widely used in food, cosmetics, and medicine.
