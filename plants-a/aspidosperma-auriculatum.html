Aspidosperma auriculatum

Aspidosperma auriculatum, commonly known as the golden cup tree, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow flowers that resemble small cups. The tree has a slender, branching trunk and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a smooth texture and a dark green upper surface.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
