Alsophila ramisora

Alsophila ramisora, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its rhizome system supports the growth of the trunk and produces new shoots, contributing to its slow but steady growth rate.

The fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, making it a striking ornamental plant in botanical gardens.
