Amburana claudii

Amburana claudii, commonly known as the Claudii's amburana, is a member of the Bombacaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elongated, elliptical leaves. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.

However, it is relatively rare and faces threats from habitat loss and overharvesting.
