Achillea serrata

Achillea serrata, commonly known as the serrated yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely serrated leaves and flat, composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, and it produces small, achene-like seeds.

Achillea serrata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
