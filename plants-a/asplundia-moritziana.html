Asplundia moritziana

Asplundia moritziana, commonly known as the Moritz's arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which includes plants like philodendrons and peace lilies. The plant produces striking, spatulate-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Asplundia moritziana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
