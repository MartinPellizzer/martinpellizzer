Adiantum poiretii f. bottinoi

Adiantum poiretii f. bottinoi, commonly known as the Bottino's maidenhair fern, is a varietal form of the maidenhair fern genus Adiantum.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically arching and gracefully drooping. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori, which are clustered on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
