Alsophila javanica

Alsophila javanica, commonly known as the Javan holly fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Davalliaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The fronds are typically dark green and can grow up to several meters in length, giving the plant a lush, tropical appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
