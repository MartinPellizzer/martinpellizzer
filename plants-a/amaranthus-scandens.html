Amaranthus scandens

Amaranthus scandens, commonly known as the spreading amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically green to red in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from green to red, depending on the variety. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Amaranthus scandens is also known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions once established.
