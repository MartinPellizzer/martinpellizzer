Arachnites fuciflorus

Arachnites fuciflorus, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and silver-blue leaves, which help reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often growing in rocky or sandy coastal habitats. Its flowers are typically tubular and arranged in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
