Acaciella ferrisiae

Acaciella ferrisiae, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of northern Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in dry, sandy soils. Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.

Acaciella ferrisiae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environments.
