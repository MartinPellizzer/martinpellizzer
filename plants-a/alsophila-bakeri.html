Alsophila bakeri

Alsophila bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's tree fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in ideal conditions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays an important role in forest ecosystems.

The fronds are typically evergreen, providing both aesthetic value and habitat for various small organisms.
