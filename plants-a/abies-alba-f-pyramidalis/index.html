Abies Alba F. Pyramidalis

European white fir, scientifically known as Abies alba f. pyramidalis, is a striking coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe. It is characterized by its tall, conical shape and dense, dark green foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance in the forest. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests and alpine areas. Its slow growth and longevity make it a valuable component of the ecosystem. Despite its hardiness, it is sensitive to environmental changes and requires careful conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
