Aloe muricata

Aloe muricata, commonly known as the spiny aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in sharp, spiny margins, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with bright yellow flowers, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves store water, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Aloe muricata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some medicinal uses in traditional practices.
