Astragalus gandeensis

Astragalus gandeensis, commonly known as the Gande milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance lies in its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
