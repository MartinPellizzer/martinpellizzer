Alyssum Sativum

Alyssum sativum, commonly known as sweet alyssum, is a small flowering plant prized for its fragrant blooms and ornamental value. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners worldwide. Its delicate clusters of flowers come in shades of white, pink, and purple, adding a soft touch to landscapes and floral arrangements. Easy to grow from seeds, it is often used in borders, containers, and as a filler plant in mixed gardens. Despite its modest size, Alyssum sativum holds a special place in horticulture for its beauty and versatility.
