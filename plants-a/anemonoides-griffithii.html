Anemonoides griffithii

Anemonoides griffithii, commonly known as the Himalayan anemone, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas.

It features a short, upright stem that bears several showy, white to pinkish flowers with a distinctive central tuft of stamens. The plant has palmately divided, lobed leaves that are hairy and adapted to the cold, high-altitude environment. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Anemonoides griffithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early spring blooms.
