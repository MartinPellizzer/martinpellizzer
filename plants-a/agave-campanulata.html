Agave campanulata

Agave campanulata, commonly known as the bell agave, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette of thick, blue-green leaves that are often lined with white teeth. The plant produces a tall, branching inflorescence that bears numerous bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of yellow or green. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Agave campanulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
