Apodanthera undulata

Apodanthera undulata, commonly known as the undulating sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink and purple, which bloom during the warmer months. Apodanthera undulata plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and provides nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
