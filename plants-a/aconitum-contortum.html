Aconitum contortum

Aconitum contortum, commonly known as the twisted aconite, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. The plant features showy, blue to purple spire-like flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a waxy texture.

Aconitum contortum contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires caution when handling.
