Arisaema takeoi

Arisaema takeoi, commonly known as the Japanese mountain arum, is a herbaceous plant native to Japan and Korea.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features a short rhizome with heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers located on separate parts of the spadix.

Arisaema takeoi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
