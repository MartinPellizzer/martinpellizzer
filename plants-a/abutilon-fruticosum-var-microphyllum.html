Abutilon fruticosum var. microphyllum

Abutilon fruticosum var. microphyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
