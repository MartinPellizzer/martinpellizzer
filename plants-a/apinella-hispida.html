Apinella hispida

Apinella hispida, commonly known as the rough apinella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and also covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Apinella hispida is often found in rocky or dry soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
