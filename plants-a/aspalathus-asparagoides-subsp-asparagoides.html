Aspalathus asparagoides subsp. asparagoides

Aspalathus asparagoides subsp.

asparagoides, commonly known as African green oat, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological and horticultural applications.
