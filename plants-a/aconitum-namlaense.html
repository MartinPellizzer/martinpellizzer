Aconitum namlaense

Aconitum namlaense, commonly known as the Namla monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves that are typically deeply divided, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Aconitum namlaense thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested or alpine habitats.

Like other members of its genus, it contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous to handle or ingest.
