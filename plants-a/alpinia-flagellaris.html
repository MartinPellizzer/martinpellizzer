Alpinia flagellaris

Alpinia flagellaris, commonly known as the flagellate ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant is characterized by its slender, sword-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Botanically, it is notable for its unique floral structure and aromatic compounds.
