Acacia dissona var. dissona

Acacia dissona var. dissona, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It is well adapted to dry and arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

The wood of Acacia dissona var. dissona is strong and durable, historically used by Indigenous Australians for tool-making.
