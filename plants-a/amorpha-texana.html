Amorpha texana

Amorpha texana, commonly known as Texas false indigo, is a deciduous shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged with oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

Amorpha texana is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
