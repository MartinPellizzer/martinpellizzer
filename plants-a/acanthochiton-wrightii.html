Acanthochiton wrightii

Acanthochiton wrightii, commonly known as the Wright's acanthochiton, is a species of sea apple found in the Indo-Pacific region.

It belongs to the family Acanthochitonidae and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny shell. The plant's shell is typically reddish-brown with a smooth, glossy surface. It thrives in shallow, tropical waters and is often found in coral reefs.

Due to its unique appearance, it is a popular subject for marine aquarists and researchers.
