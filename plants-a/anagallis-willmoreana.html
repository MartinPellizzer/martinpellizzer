Anagallis willmoreana

Anagallis willmoreana, commonly known as the willmore's pink, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, branching stems and small, delicate flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Anagallis willmoreana is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
