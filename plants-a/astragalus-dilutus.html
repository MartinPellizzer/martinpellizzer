Astragalus dilutus

Astragalus dilutus, commonly known as the pale milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus dilutus is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
