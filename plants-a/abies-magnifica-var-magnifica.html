Abies magnifica var. magnifica

Abies magnifica var. magnifica, commonly known as the grand fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender form and dense, dark green foliage that is often covered in a layer of white wax. The needles are flattened and have a slight sheen, while the cones are typically slender and woody. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

The tree's botanical features make it well adapted to its native habitat, contributing to its ecological significance.
