Acacia ehrenbergiana

Acacia ehrenbergiana, commonly known as the white acacia, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark is often fissured and greyish in color, while its branches are usually thornless.

Acacia ehrenbergiana is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
