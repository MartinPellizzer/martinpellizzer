Artemisia sichuanensis var. tomentosa

Artemisia sichuanensis var. tomentosa, commonly known as the Sichuan mugwort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It is characterized by its grayish-white, woolly foliage, which is covered in dense, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in panicles, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins and rocky habitats.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a source of essential oils.
