Andropogon × sudanensis

Andropogon × sudanensis, commonly known as Sudan grass, is a hybrid grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, coarse stems and wide, blade-like leaves that are typically green and robust. This plant is well-adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils. It is widely cultivated for forage and soil conservation due to its rapid growth and drought tolerance.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in agricultural and ecological contexts.
