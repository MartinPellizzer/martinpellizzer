Acacia polycephala

Acacia polycephala, commonly known as the many-headed acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorned stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The plant produces flat, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. Acacia polycephala thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
