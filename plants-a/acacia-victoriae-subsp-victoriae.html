Acacia victoriae subsp. victoriae

Acacia victoriae subsp.

victoriae, commonly known as the Victoria acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are divided into numerous small leaflets.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
