Alchemilla firmiana

Alchemilla firmiana, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Alchemilla firmiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
