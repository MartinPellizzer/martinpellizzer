Axonopus barbigerus

Axonopus barbigerus, commonly known as the hairy axonopus, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Axonopus barbigerus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense ground cover.
