Alsophila tanzaniana

Alsophila tanzaniana, commonly known as the Tanzanian tree fern, is a species native to the Eastern African region, particularly Tanzania.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant exhibits a unique growth pattern, with a short rootstock and a tall, unbranched stem that supports a crown of compound leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests.

The fronds are typically dark green and can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, contributing to its striking appearance.
