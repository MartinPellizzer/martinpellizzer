Aristolochia moschata

Aristolochia moschata, commonly known as the Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular, heart-shaped leaves and its striking, fragrant, purple-brown flowers. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex, winding flower structure that resembles a pipe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
