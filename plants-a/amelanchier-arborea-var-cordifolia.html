Amelanchier arborea var. cordifolia

Amelanchier arborea var. cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 feet and features oval, heart-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen to a dark red or purple color in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is valued for its ornamental appeal and wildlife benefits.

The species is also known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to tolerate urban environments.
