Athyrocarpus persicariifolius f. tetraspermus

Athyrocarpus persicariifolius f. tetraspermus, commonly known as the four-seeded persicariifolius, is a rare botanical variant of the genus Athyrocarpus.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which resembles the leaves of Persicaria species, hence its common name. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with small, ovate leaves arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences, producing small, yellowish-green blooms.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique genetic traits and limited distribution in specific tropical regions.
