Abutilon stipulare

Abutilon stipulare, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its fruits are capsules that split open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Abutilon stipulare is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
