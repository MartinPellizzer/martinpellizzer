Allomorphia pauciflora

Allomorphia pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered allomorph, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories.
