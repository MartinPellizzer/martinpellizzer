Achillea umbellata subsp. pauciloba

Achillea umbellata subsp.

pauciloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate, with a rough, hairy texture.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped umbels, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties have made it a subject of interest in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
