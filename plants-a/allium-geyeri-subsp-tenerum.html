Allium geyeri subsp. tenerum

Allium geyeri subsp.

tenerum, commonly known as the woolly garlic, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features basal rosettes of narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine, woolly hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers on slender stems, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild garlic-like fragrance.
