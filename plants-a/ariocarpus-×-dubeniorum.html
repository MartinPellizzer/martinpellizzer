Ariocarpus × dubeniorum

Ariocarpus × dubeniorum, commonly known as the hybrid moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to the Chihuahuan Desert in North America.

It is a hybrid between Ariocarpus fissuratus and Ariocarpus retusus, resulting in a unique combination of characteristics from both parent species. The plant is notable for its swollen, often brightly colored stem that resembles a cactus fruit, while its true cactus body is usually smaller and less conspicuous. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its striking appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
