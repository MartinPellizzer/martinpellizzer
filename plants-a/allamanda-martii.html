Allamanda martii

Allamanda martii, commonly known as the yellow allamanda, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically 5 to 8 centimeters in length. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Allamanda martii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
