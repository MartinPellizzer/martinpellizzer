Angelica japonica var. hirsutiflora

Angelica japonica var. hirsutiflora, commonly known as the hairy-flowered angelica, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and often fern-like leaves. The plant features tall, hollow stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and are typically greenish-white with a subtle fragrance.

The roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
