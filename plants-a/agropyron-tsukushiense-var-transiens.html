Agropyron tsukushiense var. transiens

Agropyron tsukushiense var. transiens, commonly known as the Japanese wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated for forage or ornamental purposes.
