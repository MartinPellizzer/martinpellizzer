Aniba guianensis

Aniba guianensis, commonly known as Guianese aniba, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which emit a strong, spicy scent when crushed. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically white or pale green, and it produces dark, glossy berries.

Aniba guianensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
