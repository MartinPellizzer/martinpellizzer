Androscepia anathera var. glabrescens

Androscepia anathera var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smoothleaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically features succulent, fleshy leaves that are smooth and glabrous, adapted to conserve moisture in dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical structure reflects its adaptation to harsh, arid conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
