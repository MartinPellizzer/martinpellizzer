Aster concolor f. lasiocaulis

Aster concolor f. lasiocaulis, commonly known as the woolly blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant is distinguished by its grayish-blue foliage, which is covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. Its showy purple-blue flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

This cultivar is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
