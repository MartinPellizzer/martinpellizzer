Angraecopsis macrophylla

Angraecopsis macrophylla, commonly known as the giant star orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter, featuring a distinctive star-shaped pattern. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs in late winter to early spring, making it a rare and sought-after species in botanical gardens.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
