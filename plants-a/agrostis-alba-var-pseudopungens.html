Agrostis alba var. pseudopungens

Agrostis alba var. pseudopungens, commonly known as silver hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
