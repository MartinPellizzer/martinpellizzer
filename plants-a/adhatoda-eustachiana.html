Adhatoda eustachiana

Adhatoda eustachiana, commonly known as the Indian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its attractive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Adhatoda eustachiana is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional systems of medicine.
