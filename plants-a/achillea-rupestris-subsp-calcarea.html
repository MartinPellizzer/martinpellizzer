Achillea rupestris subsp. calcarea

Achillea rupestris subsp.

calcarea, commonly known as the limestone yarrow, is a perennial herb native to rocky and limestone soils in Europe. It features upright, branched stems that bear narrow, gray-green leaves with a silvery underside.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
