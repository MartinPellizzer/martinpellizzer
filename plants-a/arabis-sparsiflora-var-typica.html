Arabis sparsiflora var. typica

Arabis sparsiflora var. typica, commonly known as the slender rockcress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal leaves that are typically lobed or divided, and it produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
