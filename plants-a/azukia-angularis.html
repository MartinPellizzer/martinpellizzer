Azukia angularis

Azukia angularis, commonly known as the angular azuki, is a species of legume native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, angular seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet having a serrated edge.

Azukia angularis is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
