Aspalathus acicularis subsp. planifolia

Aspalathus acicularis subsp.

planifolia, commonly known as the flatleaf rooibos, is a shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa. It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

The plant is adapted to grow in sandy, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Its botanical features make it unique among the Aspalathus species, particularly in its leaf morphology and growth habits.
