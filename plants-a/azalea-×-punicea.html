Azalea × punicea

Azalea × punicea, commonly known as the Confederate Jack.

It is a hybrid shrub native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range from red to pink and white. The plant belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is known for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils. It produces bell-shaped flowers in clusters, often with a distinctive red or pink hue, and is popular in gardens for its ornamental value.

Azalea × punicea is also valued for its fragrance and is often used in landscaping and floral arrangements.
