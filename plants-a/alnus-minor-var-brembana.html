Alnus minor var. brembana

Alnus minor var. brembana, commonly known as the Bremban alder, is a shrub or small tree native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its finely toothed leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils. The Bremban alder plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
