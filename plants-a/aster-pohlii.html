Aster pohlii

Aster pohlii, commonly known as the Pohlii aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its showy, violet-blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer. It has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Aster pohlii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
