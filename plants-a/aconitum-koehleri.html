Aconitum koehleri

Aconitum koehleri, commonly known as the Himalayan monkshood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas. The plant features striking purple to blue flowers that are clustered in a helmet-like shape, characteristic of the genus Aconitum. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments, and the plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters.

Aconitum koehleri contains potent alkaloids, making it highly toxic and requiring careful handling.
