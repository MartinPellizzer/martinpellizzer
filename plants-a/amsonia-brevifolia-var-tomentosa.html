Amsonia brevifolia var. tomentosa

Amsonia brevifolia var. tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly bluemink, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in a dense, woolly white pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to sky-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky habitats.
