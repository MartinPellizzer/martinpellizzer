Atocion armeria var. lituanicum

Atocion armeria var. lituanicum, commonly known as the Lithuanian sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the coastal regions of the Baltic Sea.

It features dense clusters of tiny, white flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in its sandy, salty habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and coastal grasslands.

Its botanical structure is adapted to withstand harsh maritime conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
