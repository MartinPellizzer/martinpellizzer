Allium javorjense

Allium javorjense, commonly known as the Siberian leek, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives, and is characterized by its bulbous root system and grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a potential culinary and medicinal plant.

Allium javorjense thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
