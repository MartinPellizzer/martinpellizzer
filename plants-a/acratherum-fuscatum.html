Acratherum fuscatum

Acratherum fuscatum, commonly known as the blackleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by elongated, sparsely hairy seed capsules. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
