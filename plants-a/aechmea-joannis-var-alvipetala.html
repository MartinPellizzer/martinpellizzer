Aechmea joannis var. alvipetala

Aechmea joannis var. alvipetala, commonly known as the White-veined Bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater to support its growth. The leaves are typically broad, with a waxy surface and a distinctive white or pale green veining. It produces vibrant flower spikes that can range from red to orange, adding visual interest to its appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
