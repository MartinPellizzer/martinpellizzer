Antirrhinum latifolium

Antirrhinum latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bright red or orange tubular flowers. The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
