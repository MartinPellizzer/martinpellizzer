Acacia durabilis

Acacia durabilis, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions. It plays an important role in the ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various native animals.

The golden wattle is also a symbol of Australia and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value.
