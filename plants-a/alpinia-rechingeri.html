Alpinia rechingeri

Alpinia rechingeri, commonly known as the mountain ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric. The plant features tall, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in inflorescences at the top of the stems.

Alpinia rechingeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
