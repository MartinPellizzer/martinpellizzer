Arrhenatherum elatius var. biaristatum

Arrhenatherum elatius var. biaristatum, commonly known as the tall meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late summer.
