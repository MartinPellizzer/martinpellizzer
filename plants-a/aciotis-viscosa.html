Aciotis viscosa

Aciotis viscosa, commonly known as roughleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other daisy-like plants.
