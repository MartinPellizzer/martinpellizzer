Astragalus tijuanensis

Astragalus tijuanensis, commonly known as Tijuana milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Baja California, Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers and compound leaves. The plant is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky habitats. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
