Achillea magnifolia

Achillea magnifolia, commonly known as the purple yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features a robust root system and upright, branching stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, flat-topped flower heads in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are aromatic, finely divided, and covered in a soft, silvery down.

Achillea magnifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.
