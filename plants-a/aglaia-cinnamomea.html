Aglaia cinnamomea

Aglaia cinnamomea, commonly known as the cinnamon wood or yellow wood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown heartwood that is highly valued for its durability and scent. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits containing smooth, black seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and wood quality, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
