Agrostis fusca

Agrostis fusca, commonly known as black bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has slender, tufted stems and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green color.

Agrostis fusca is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
