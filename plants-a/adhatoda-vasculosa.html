Adhatoda vasculosa

Adhatoda vasculosa, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale green to yellowish in color, often with a reddish tint. Its roots are thick and fleshy, which contribute to its medicinal uses in traditional systems of medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage.
