Aerangis fastuosa

Aerangis fastuosa, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant with a short, upright stem and produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The flowers are borne on racemes and have a unique structure with three sepals and three petals, one of which is modified into a lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
