Aster pedunculosus

Aster pedunculosus, commonly known as the slender aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features upright, branching stems that bear small, daisy-like flowers with purple to pink petals. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Aster pedunculosus plays a role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
