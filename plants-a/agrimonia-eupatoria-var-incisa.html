Agrimonia eupatoria var. incisa

Agrimonia eupatoria var. incisa, commonly known as narrow-leaved agrimony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its medicinal properties have been historically used for digestive and anti-inflammatory purposes.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and woodland edges.
