Aegiphila cordifolia

Aegiphila cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaved aegiphila, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Aegiphila cordifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in some regions for its aromatic properties.

Morphology

Aegiphila cordifolia has heart-shaped leaves that are glossy green, arranged alternately along the stem, and typically measure between 2 to 5 centimeters in length.

The stem is upright and woody, with a smooth, greyish-brown bark that becomes rougher with age. Its flowers are small, clustered in panicles, with pinkish-white petals that bloom in the spring, and are pollinated by insects such as bees. The fruit is a small capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, allowing the plant to thrive in well-drained soils.
