Aegiphila cordifolia

Aegiphila cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaved aegiphila, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Aegiphila cordifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in some regions for its aromatic properties.
