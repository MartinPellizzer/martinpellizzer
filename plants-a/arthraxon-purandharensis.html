Arthraxon purandharensis

Arthraxon purandharensis, commonly known as the Purandhar grass, is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a distinct sheath at the base, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Arthraxon purandharensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
