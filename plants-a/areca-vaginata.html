Areca vaginata

Areca vaginata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elongated, woody pods containing several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a velvety texture, contributing to its common name.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
