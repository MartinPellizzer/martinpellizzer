Allium hirtovaginatum subsp. hirtovaginatum

Allium hirtovaginatum subsp.

hirtovaginatum, commonly known as the hairy bulb onion, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its bulbous base, which is often covered in a fibrous, hairy sheath.

The plant produces upright, slender stems that bear small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in umbels. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a slight pubescence.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
