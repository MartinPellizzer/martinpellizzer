Adenocarpus intermedius var. viciosorum

Adenocarpus intermedius var. viciosorum, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its hard, heavy wood has been traditionally used for tools and construction.
