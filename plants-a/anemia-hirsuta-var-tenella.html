Anemia hirsuta var. tenella

Anemia hirsuta var. tenella, commonly known as the hairy anemia or tenella anemia, is a variant of the species Anemia hirsuta, which belongs to the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are typically finely pubescent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, a common feature among members of its family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines for their mild, aromatic flavor.
