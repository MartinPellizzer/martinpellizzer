Anemia fulva

Anemia fulva, commonly known as the golden anemone, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its botanical name reflects its historical use in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Anemia fulva is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and resilience.
