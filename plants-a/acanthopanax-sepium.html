Acanthopanax sepium

Acanthopanax sepium, commonly known as Chinese angel's trumpets, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and is often found in forested areas. It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
