Ageratina espinosarum var. subintegrifolia

Ageratina espinosarum var. subintegrifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. It produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

This variant is distinguished by its more integrated leaf margins compared to other varieties of Ageratina espinosarum.
