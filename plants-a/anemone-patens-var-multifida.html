Anemone patens var. multifida

Anemone patens var. multifida, commonly known as the forked anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces showy, white to pinkish flowers with five to seven petals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for woodland gardens and naturalized areas.
