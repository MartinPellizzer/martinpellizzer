Adenostemma lavenia var. madurense

Adenostemma lavenia var. madurense, commonly known as the Madura grass, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil make it a useful plant in ecological restoration projects.
