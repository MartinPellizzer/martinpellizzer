Acacia longifolia subsp. sophorae

Acacia longifolia subsp.

sophorae, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves that are grey-green in color.

The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that appear in spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in dunes and heathlands.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens and for erosion control.
