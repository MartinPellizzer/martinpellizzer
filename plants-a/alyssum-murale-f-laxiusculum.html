Alyssum murale f. laxiusculum

Alyssum murale f. laxiusculum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved alyssum, is a compact, mat-forming perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in spring and early summer. The leaves are narrow and linear, with a grayish-blue hue due to a dense covering of fine hairs. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its hardy nature and low maintenance requirements.
