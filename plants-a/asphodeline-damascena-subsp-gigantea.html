Asphodeline damascena subsp. gigantea

Asphodeline damascena subsp.

gigantea, commonly known as the giant asphodel, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Europe and the Mediterranean region. It features tall, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-gray in color, and it produces showy, fragrant white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes. Its botanical characteristics include a robust root system and a clump-forming growth habit.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
