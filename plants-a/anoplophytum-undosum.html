Anoplophytum undosum

Anoplophytum undosum, commonly known as the wavy sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves that help it retain water in dry environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in sandy soils and withstand periods of drought.

Anoplophytum undosum is often used in xeriscaping due to its ability to survive with minimal water and maintenance.
