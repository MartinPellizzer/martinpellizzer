Arisaema wattii

Arisaema wattii, commonly known as the Chinese cobra lily, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, hooded spathe that resembles a cobra's head. The plant produces small, green berries that mature to a deep red, containing seeds enclosed in a fleshy aril. Arisaema wattii thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
