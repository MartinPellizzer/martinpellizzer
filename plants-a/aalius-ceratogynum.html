Aalius ceratogynum

Aalius ceratogynum, commonly known as the horned gynandromorph, is a rare and fascinating plant species characterized by its unique gynandromorphic traits, where both male and female floral structures coexist on the same plant.

It belongs to the family Aaliaceae and is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia. The plant exhibits a striking dichotomy in its floral morphology, with one side displaying stamens and the other showing pistils. Its botanical significance lies in its rare genetic and morphological characteristics, making it a subject of interest in botanical research.
