Amomophyllum patinii

Amomophyllum patinii, commonly known as the Patin's cardamom, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by capsule fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

Amomophyllum patinii is valued both for its culinary uses and its role in local ecosystems.
