Astragalus bethlehemiticus var. capitatus

Astragalus bethlehemiticus var. capitatus, commonly known as the Jerusalem balm, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of southern Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
