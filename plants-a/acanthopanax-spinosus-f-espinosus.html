Acanthopanax spinosus f. espinosus

Acanthopanax spinosus f. espinosus, commonly known as the spiny Chinese dogwood, is a variant of the Chinese dogwood characterized by its dense, thorny growth.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of East Asia, particularly China. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, clustered yellow flowers that appear in the spring. Its berries are red and edible, though often used in traditional medicine.

This form is notable for its robust, spiny structure and adaptability to various soil types.
