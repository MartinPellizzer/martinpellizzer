Aphananthe lucida

Aphananthe lucida, commonly known as the silky mistflower, is a small shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, almost ethereal appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
