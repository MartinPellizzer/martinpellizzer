Acroptilon picris var. subdentatum

Acroptilon picris var. subdentatum, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with pinnate arrangements and possesses a deep root system that helps it thrive in arid conditions. It is often found in dry grasslands and sagebrush steppe, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.

Its flowers are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
