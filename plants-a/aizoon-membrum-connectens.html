Aizoon membrum-connectens

Aizoon membrum-connectens, commonly known as the winged balsam, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often adorned with prominent veins and wing-like structures on the petioles. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
