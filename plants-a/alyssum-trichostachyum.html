Alyssum trichostachyum

Alyssum trichostachyum, commonly known as the mountain alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in early spring. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that are typically opposite or whorled along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Alyssum trichostachyum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
