Acacia formicarum

Acacia formicarum, commonly known as the weevil acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny foliage and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Acacia formicarum plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing shelter and food for various insects and animals.
