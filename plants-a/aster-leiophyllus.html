Aster leiophyllus

Aster leiophyllus, commonly known as the smoothleaf aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northeastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall, typically in shades of purple and blue. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Aster leiophyllus is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks, contributing to ecological diversity in its habitat.
