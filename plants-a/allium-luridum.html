Allium luridum

Allium luridum, commonly known as the yellow garlic or wild leek, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives, and is characterized by its bulbous structure and grass-like foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Allium luridum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native environment.
