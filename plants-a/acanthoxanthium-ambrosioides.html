Acanthoxanthium ambrosioides

Acanthoxanthium ambrosioides, commonly known as the ambrosia grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces wind-pollinated flowers, which are typically green or pale in color. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in open areas such as fields and roadsides.

Due to its ability to spread rapidly, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
