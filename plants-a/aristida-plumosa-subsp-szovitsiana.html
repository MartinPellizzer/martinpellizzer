Aristida plumosa subsp. szovitsiana

Aristida plumosa subsp.

szovitsiana, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, feathery foliage.
