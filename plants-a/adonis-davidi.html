Adonis davidi

Adonis davidi, commonly known as the David's glory of the snow, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, early spring blooms. The plant produces showy, white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, recurved petal structure. Its leaves are palmately divided, typically with three to five lobes, and are covered in a fine layer of soft hairs.

Adonis davidi thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
