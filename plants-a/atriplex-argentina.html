Atriplex argentina

Atriplex argentina, commonly known as the Argentine saltbush, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it well adapted to harsh, coastal or desert conditions. Its small, greenish flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences.

Atriplex argentina plays a role in stabilizing soil and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
