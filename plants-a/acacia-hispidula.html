Acacia hispidula

Acacia hispidula, commonly known as the rough wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves with small, stiff, hairy leaflets. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance. It produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Acacia hispidula is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
