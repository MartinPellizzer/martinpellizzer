Aster divaricatus var. graminifolius

Aster divaricatus var. graminifolius, commonly known as the slenderleaf blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and ecological restoration projects.
