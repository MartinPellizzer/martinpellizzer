Artemisia wellbyi

Artemisia wellbyi, commonly known as the wellby's sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and aromatic, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. Artemisia wellbyi is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sagebrush steppe and rocky soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
