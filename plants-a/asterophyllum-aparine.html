Asterophyllum aparine

Asterophyllum aparine, commonly known as sand mat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant has a mat-forming habit with small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to white, appearing in clusters and blooming in the spring.

Asterophyllum aparine is well adapted to harsh environments, with mechanisms to conserve water and withstand extreme temperatures.
