Astragalus laxmannii var. robustior

Astragalus laxmannii var. robustior, commonly known as the robust milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, erect stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinator populations.
