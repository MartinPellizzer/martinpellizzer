Acer ornatum var. latialatum

Acer ornatum var. latialatum, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental foliage, which displays a range of colors from bright green to deep red during the growing season. The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a smooth margin and a pointed tip. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds called samaras.

This variety is prized for its compact size and adaptability to various soil types, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
