Anthemis americana

Anthemis americana, commonly known as American pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils. Its flowers are yellow with a central disk, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and has been used historically for its insect-repelling properties.
