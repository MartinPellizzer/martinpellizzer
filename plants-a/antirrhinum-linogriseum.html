Antirrhinum linogriseum

Antirrhinum linogriseum, commonly known as the gray-leaved snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of bright yellow to orange-red tubular flowers. The leaves are typically gray-green in color, with a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
