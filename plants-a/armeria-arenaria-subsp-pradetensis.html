Armeria arenaria subsp. pradetensis

Armeria arenaria subsp.

pradetensis, commonly known as the sand armeria, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features compact, silver-gray foliage and produces clusters of small, white flowers in early summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant. It is often found in coastal dunes and rocky habitats, where it withstands harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical structure includes a basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that rise from the center.
