Acetosella amazonica

Acetosella amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian sorrel, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and marshy areas. Its leaves are edible and have a tangy, sour flavor due to the presence of oxalic acid.

Acetosella amazonica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
