Amaracarpus apoensis

Amaracarpus apoensis, commonly known as the Philippine sandarac tree, is a species native to the Philippines.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in lowland and mountainous areas. The plant has simple, opposite, and elliptical leaves that are usually dark green in color. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces woody capsules as fruit.

The tree is valued for its aromatic resin, which has been traditionally used in various applications.
