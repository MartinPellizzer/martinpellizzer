Almyra

Almyra, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features striking blue or purple flowers surrounded by bracts that resemble leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. Almyra thrives in coastal environments and is often found growing along rocky shores and sandy beaches.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for coastal gardens.
