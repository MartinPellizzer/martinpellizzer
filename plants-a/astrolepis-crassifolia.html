Astrolepis crassifolia

Astrolepis crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leafed fern, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, elliptical leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, humid environments.

Astrolepis crassifolia is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
