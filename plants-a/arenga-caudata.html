Arenga caudata

Arenga caudata, commonly known as the long-leaved areca, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences that bear small, yellowish flowers. Arenga caudata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its fibrous leaves can be processed into materials used in basket weaving and other crafts.
