Astragalus vexilliflexus var. vexilliflexus

Astragalus vexilliflexus var. vexilliflexus, commonly known as the western milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
