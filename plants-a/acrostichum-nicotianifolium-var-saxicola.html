Acrostichum nicotianifolium var. saxicola

Acrostichum nicotianifolium var. saxicola, commonly known as the rock hibiscus, is a variant of the rock hibiscus species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of tobacco plants. The plant produces strikingly large, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found growing on rocky slopes or in coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable ornamental plant in horticulture.
