Aster canescens var. tephrodes

Aster canescens var. tephrodes, commonly known as the tephrodes aster, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to harsh, dry conditions. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine, grayish-white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and arranged in clusters, blooming in late summer to early fall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
