Achras zapotilla var. parvifolia

Achras zapotilla var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved achiote, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and small, ovate leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers that develop into woody, three-lobed fruits. Its leaves and seeds contain compounds used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.
