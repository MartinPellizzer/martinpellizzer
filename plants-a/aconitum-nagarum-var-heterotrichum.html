Aconitum nagarum var. heterotrichum

Aconitum nagarum var. heterotrichum, commonly known as the Himalayan aconite, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
