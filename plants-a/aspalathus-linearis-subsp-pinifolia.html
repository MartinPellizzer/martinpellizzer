Aspalathus linearis subsp. pinifolia

Aspalathus linearis subsp.

pinifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf bush, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that give it a resemblance to pine needles, hence its subspecies name.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its unique morphology and adaptability contribute to its ecological and horticultural significance.
