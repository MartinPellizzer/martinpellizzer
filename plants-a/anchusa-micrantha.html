Anchusa micrantha

Anchusa micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as rocky soils and grasslands. The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Anchusa micrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
