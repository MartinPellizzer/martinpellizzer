Aglaia acuminatissima

Aglaia acuminatissima, commonly known as the narrow-pointed aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family, which includes mahogany and other valuable timber species. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, clustered in pendulous inflorescences, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and is often found in lowland rainforests.
