Astragalus donianus

Astragalus donianus, commonly known as Donian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus donianus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
