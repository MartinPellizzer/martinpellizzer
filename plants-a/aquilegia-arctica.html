Aquilegia arctica

Aquilegia arctica, commonly known as the Arctic bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It features showy, spurred flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound, pinnate leaves with finely toothed edges, adapted to withstand cold climates. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and alpine environments.

Aquilegia arctica is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
