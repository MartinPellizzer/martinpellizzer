Ammannia bequaertii

Ammannia bequaertii, commonly known as the red-stemmed ammannia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive red stems and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as wetlands and marshes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Ammannia bequaertii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
