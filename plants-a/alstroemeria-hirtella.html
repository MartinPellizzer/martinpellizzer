Alstroemeria hirtella

Alstroemeria hirtella, commonly known as the woolly alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a range of colors including red, orange, and yellow. The plant features narrow, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, woolly texture, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Alstroemeria hirtella is also valued for its long-lasting blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
