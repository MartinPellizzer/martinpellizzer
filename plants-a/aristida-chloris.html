Aristida chloris

Aristida chloris, commonly known as green lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, erect flowering stems. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Aristida chloris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
