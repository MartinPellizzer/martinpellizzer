Aristolochia ciliosa

Aristolochia ciliosa, commonly known as the spiral vine or hooded gentian, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often purple or greenish in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and is known for its climbing habit, often twining around supports. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.

The genus Aristolochia includes many species with similar morphological features and some containing toxic compounds.
