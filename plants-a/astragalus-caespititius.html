Astragalus caespititius

Astragalus caespititius, commonly known as the tufted milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its clustered growth habit and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus caespititius is often found in open grasslands and is a component of native plant communities in its range.
