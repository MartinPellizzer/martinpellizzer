Agrostis serotina

Agrostis serotina, commonly known as late blackgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist, fertile soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 60 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, spikelets that are often tinged with purple or brown.

Agrostis serotina is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
