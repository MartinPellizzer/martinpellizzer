Arundinaria longiaurita

Arundinaria longiaurita, commonly known as the long-tailed bamboo, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery inflorescences. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Arundinaria longiaurita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
