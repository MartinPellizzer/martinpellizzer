Alisma plantago-aquatica var. americanum

Alisma plantago-aquatica var. americanum, commonly known as American water plantain, is a herbaceous perennial aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow water, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are usually submerged or floating, and it produces small, white or greenish flowers during the summer months. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is adapted to tolerate fluctuating water levels and is an important component of wetland ecosystems.
