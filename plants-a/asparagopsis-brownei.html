Asparagopsis brownei

Asparagopsis brownei, commonly known as the Brown's asparagus, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Asparagaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in loose panicles, which are often borne at the base of the stems. It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it grows in rocky and sandy soils.

Asparagopsis brownei is often found in coastal and inland regions, thriving in well-drained environments.
