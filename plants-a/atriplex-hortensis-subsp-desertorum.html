Atriplex hortensis subsp. desertorum

Atriplex hortensis subsp.

desertorum, commonly known as desert spinach, is a halophytic plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to harsh desert environments.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Amaranthaceae family. Its leaves are edible and rich in nutrients, making it a valuable crop in drought-prone areas.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils, contributing to soil stabilization and erosion control.
