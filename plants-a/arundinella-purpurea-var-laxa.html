Arundinella purpurea var. laxa

Arundinella purpurea var. laxa, commonly known as purple bamboo or purple reed grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with purple or pink flowers, which are typical of the grass family. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slight sheen.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
