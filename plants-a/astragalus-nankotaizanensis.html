Astragalus nankotaizanensis

Astragalus nankotaizanensis, commonly known as the Nankotaizan milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to Japan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological niche.
