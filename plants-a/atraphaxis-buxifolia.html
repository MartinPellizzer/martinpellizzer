Atraphaxis buxifolia

Atraphaxis buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf desert shrub, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage, with leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes and rock gardens.
