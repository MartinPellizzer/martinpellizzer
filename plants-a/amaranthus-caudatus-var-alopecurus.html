Amaranthus caudatus var. alopecurus

Amaranthus caudatus var. alopecurus, commonly known as the curly top amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally twisted inflorescence that resembles a plume or a feather. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that give way to triangular, bead-like seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its ornamental value is primarily due to its unique, curly seed heads, which are used in dried floral arrangements.
