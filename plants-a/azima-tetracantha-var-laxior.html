Azima tetracantha var. laxior

Azima tetracantha var. laxior, commonly known as the slender azima, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of South Asia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots that help it access water from deeper soil layers. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
