Acacia hystrix

Acacia hystrix, commonly known as the needle acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny branches and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange flower clusters that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. Its seeds are encased in hard, woody pods that split open when mature.

Acacia hystrix is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
