Arundinaria tsurumatiana

Arundinaria tsurumatiana, commonly known as the Tsurumatiana bamboo, is a species native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in clusters. The plant typically reaches a height of about 2 to 3 meters and has narrow, linear leaves that are often glabrous. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
