Acinodendron cuspidatum

Acinodendron cuspidatum, commonly known as the spiky acacia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry, open habitats.

Its unique spiky appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in its native ecosystems.
