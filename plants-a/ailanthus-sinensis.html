Ailanthus sinensis

Ailanthus sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese empress tree, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Hippocastanaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with 12 to 24 leaflets. The tree produces yellow flowers in panicles, followed by spiny capsules containing seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world.
