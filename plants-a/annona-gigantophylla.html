Annona gigantophylla

Annona gigantophylla, commonly known as the giant star apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a large tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with broad, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is large, typically weighing up to 10 kilograms, and has a rough, green exterior with a sweet, creamy flesh. It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
