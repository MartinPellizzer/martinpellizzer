Asplundia labela

Asplundia labela, commonly known as the banana orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant with fleshy, banana-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a spike. Asplundia labela thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique appearance and medicinal properties make it a valuable species in both horticulture and traditional practices.
