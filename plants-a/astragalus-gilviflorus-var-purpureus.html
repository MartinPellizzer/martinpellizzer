Astragalus gilviflorus var. purpureus

Astragalus gilviflorus var. purpureus, commonly known as the purple-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from yellow to purple, depending on the variety. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert scrub habitats.

Astragalus gilviflorus var. purpureus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
