Abarema asplenifolia

Abarema asplenifolia, commonly known as the fern-like acacia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves that resemble those of ferns, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Abarema asplenifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
