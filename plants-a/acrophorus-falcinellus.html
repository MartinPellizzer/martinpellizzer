Acrophorus falcinellus

Acrophorus falcinellus, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its pinnate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
