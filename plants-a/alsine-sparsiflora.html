Alsine sparsiflora

Alsine sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparse-flower sandwort, is a low-growing annual herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Montiaceae and is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and clusters of tiny, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
