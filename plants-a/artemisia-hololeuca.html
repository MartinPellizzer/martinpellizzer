Artemisia hololeuca

Artemisia hololeuca, commonly known as big sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which consists of aromatic, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Artemisia hololeuca plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
