Adiantum trapeziforme var. oblongatum

Adiantum trapeziforme var. oblongatum, commonly known as the oblongatum maidenhair fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including China, India, and the Philippines. The plant features a creeping rhizome with upright, lanceolate pinnae that are often slightly curled at the tips. Its leaves are typically dark green and glossy, with a distinctive, almost feather-like appearance.

This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and indoor spaces.
