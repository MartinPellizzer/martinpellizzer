Aster trinervius

Aster trinervius, commonly known as the three-veined aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with three distinct veins, giving it its scientific name. Its flowers are typically blue to purple in color, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Aster trinervius is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands.
