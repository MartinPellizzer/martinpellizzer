Acacia westonii

Acacia westonii, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, feathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia westonii is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of firewood.
