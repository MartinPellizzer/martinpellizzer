Astragalus trojanus var. chius

Astragalus trojanus var. chius, commonly known as the closed-headed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to semi-arid to arid environments.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in such environments.
