Aira triflora

Aira triflora, commonly known as false wheat, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear three-flowered spikelets, giving it its scientific name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it often grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Aira triflora is typically found in moist soils and can thrive in a variety of climates.
