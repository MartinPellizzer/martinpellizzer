Acacia willdenowiana

Acacia willdenowiana, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its slender, often branched stem and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical clusters, which are typically followed by flat, bean-like pods. Acacia willdenowiana is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy soils.

It has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and forage.
