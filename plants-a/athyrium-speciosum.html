Athyrium speciosum

Athyrium speciosum, commonly known as the Japanese Painted Fern, is a fern species native to East Asia.

It features finely divided, lace-like fronds that are typically bi-colored, with a green base and a silvery-white to rust-colored margin. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and has a clump-forming growth habit. Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It is often used in gardens for its aesthetic value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
