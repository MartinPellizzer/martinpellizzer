Asplenium bosseri

Asplenium bosseri, commonly known as the Bosser's spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often clustered closely together. The plant typically grows on rocks or tree bark in shaded, moist environments. Its spore-producing structures, called sorocarps, are borne on the underside of the leaves.

Asplenium bosseri is native to regions of Africa and is often found in tropical and subtropical climates.
