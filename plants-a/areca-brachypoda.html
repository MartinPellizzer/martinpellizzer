Areca brachypoda

Areca brachypoda, commonly known as the dwarf areca palm, is a small, ornamental palm native to Southeast Asia.

It features a slender, unbranched trunk that is typically less than 2 meters tall. The plant produces feather-like, compound leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern at the top of the stem. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces edible fruits that are used in traditional medicine.

Areca brachypoda is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
