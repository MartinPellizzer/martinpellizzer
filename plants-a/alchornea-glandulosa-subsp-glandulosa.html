Alchornea glandulosa subsp. glandulosa

Alchornea glandulosa subsp.

glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular alchornea, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that often emit a mild, aromatic scent.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
