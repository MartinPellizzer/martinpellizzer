Aquilegia formosa f. truncata

Aquilegia formosa f. truncata, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada leopard's bane, is a horticultural form of the native Colorado lupine.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of spurred, violet-blue flowers. The leaves are compound, consisting of several oval to lance-shaped leaflets, and the plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
