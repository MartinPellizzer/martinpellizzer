Albizia rivularis

Albizia rivularis, commonly known as the river albizia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows near water sources, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments. The plant produces small, fragrant pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white or pale pink. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Albizia rivularis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
