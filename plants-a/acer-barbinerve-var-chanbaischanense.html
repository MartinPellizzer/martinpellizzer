Acer barbinerve var. chanbaischanense

Acer barbinerve var. chanbaischanense, commonly known as the Chanbaischan maple, is a variant of the barbinerve maple native to specific regions of China.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, featuring finely serrated edges and a unique vein pattern. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall, making it a notable ornamental species.

The tree has moderate growth rates and is valued for its ecological and aesthetic contributions in horticulture.
