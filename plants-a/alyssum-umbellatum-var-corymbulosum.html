Alyssum umbellatum var. corymbulosum

Alyssum umbellatum var. corymbulosum, commonly known as clustered alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically arranged in clustered umbels. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its foliage is silvery-gray, adding visual interest even when not in bloom.
