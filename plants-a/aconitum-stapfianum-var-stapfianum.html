Aconitum stapfianum var. stapfianum

Aconitum stapfianum var. stapfianum, commonly known as the Stapf's monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, spurred flowers and toxic alkaloids. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 4,500 meters. Its foliage consists of deeply lobed leaves, and it produces blue to purple flowers in late summer.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
