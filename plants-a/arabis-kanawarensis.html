Arabis kanawarensis

Arabis kanawarensis, commonly known as the Japanese rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with characteristic four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Arabis kanawarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
