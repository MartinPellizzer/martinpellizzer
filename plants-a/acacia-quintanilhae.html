Acacia quintanilhae

Acacia quintanilhae, commonly known as the Quintanilha acacia, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features bipinnate leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
