Alstroemeria variegata

Alstroemeria variegata, commonly known as the variegated lily of the Incas, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, colorful, and often variegated leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, orange, and pink. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Alstroemeria variegata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its long-lasting blooms.
