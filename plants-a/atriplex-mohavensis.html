Atriplex mohavensis

Atriplex mohavensis, commonly known as the Mohave saltbush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it a common species in desert regions.

Atriplex mohavensis plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
