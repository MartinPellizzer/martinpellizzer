Adenoderris glandulosa

Adenoderris glandulosa, commonly known as the hairy senna, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has small, glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Adenoderris glandulosa is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
