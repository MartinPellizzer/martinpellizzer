Adiantum cuneatum f. typicum

Adiantum cuneatum f. typicum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a fern species characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is native to regions of North America, particularly the eastern United States. The plant features small, bright green, lance-shaped leaves that are often held in a graceful, arching pattern. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and indoor settings.

The fronds are typically panicle-like, with multiple pinnae that give it a lacy appearance.
