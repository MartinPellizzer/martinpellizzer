Anthyllis praepropera

Anthyllis praepropera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. The plant features slender, branching stems that are often covered in a dense layer of short, stiff hairs. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in dense spikes and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are narrow and linear, typically growing in pairs along the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
