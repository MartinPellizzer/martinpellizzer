Andropogon exaltatus var. ambiguus

Andropogon exaltatus var. ambiguus, commonly known as the tall bluestem grass, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth and blue-green foliage, which becomes more pronounced during the growing season. The plant typically thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common species in prairie and savanna ecosystems. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets that are often tinged with purple or red, adding visual interest in the fall.

This grass is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
