Asperula nitida subsp. subcapitellata

Asperula nitida subsp.

subcapitellata, commonly known as the velvetleaf azalea, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to China. It features dense, dark green foliage that is slightly hairy, giving it a velvety texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive form and seasonal flowering.
