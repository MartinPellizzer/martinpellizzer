Asarum bashanense

Asarum bashanense, commonly known as the Bashan wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, tubular flowers. The plant produces a fragrant, spadix-like inflorescence that is often covered in dense, yellowish-white floral structures. Its rhizomes are used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported medicinal properties.

Asarum bashanense thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
