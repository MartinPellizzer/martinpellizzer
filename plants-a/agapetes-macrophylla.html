Agapetes macrophylla

Agapetes macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved silverleaf, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are leathery, glossy, and often have a silvery underside, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
