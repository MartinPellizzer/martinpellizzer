Arachis prostrata f. hagenbeckii

Arachis prostrata f. hagenbeckii, commonly known as the prostrate peanut, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features hairy, spreading stems that lie close to the ground, often forming a dense mat. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pod-like structures containing edible seeds. Its botanical characteristics include compound leaves with oval leaflets and a fibrous root system.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its use in ornamental gardening.
