Ardisia hortorum var. brachysepala

Ardisia hortorum var. brachysepala, commonly known as the short-fruit ardisia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late summer. The fruit is a bright red berry, which is edible and often used in traditional medicine. This species prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid climates.
