Asplenium pappei

Asplenium pappei, commonly known as the pappei spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in small groups called sori. Asplenium pappei thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rock surfaces or in humid forest understories.

Its delicate appearance and unique growth habits make it a notable species within its ecological niche.
