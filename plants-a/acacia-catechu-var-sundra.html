Acacia catechu var. sundra

Acacia catechu var. sundra, commonly known as the Indian blackwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves that are trifoliate at the base. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for tanning leather due to their high tannin content.

The species is also valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in various crafts and construction.
