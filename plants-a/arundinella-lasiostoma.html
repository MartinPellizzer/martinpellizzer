Arundinella lasiostoma

Arundinella lasiostoma, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that are often covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a slightly rough texture.

Arundinella lasiostoma is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of sustainable materials.
