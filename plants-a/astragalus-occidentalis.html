Astragalus occidentalis

Astragalus occidentalis, commonly known as western milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow to reddish-purple flowers in late spring. Its root system is extensive and fibrous, often used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
