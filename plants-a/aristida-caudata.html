Aristida caudata

Aristida caudata, commonly known as the silky grass or curlytail grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted, hairy inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slight curl, contributing to its ornamental value.

Aristida caudata is also valued for its role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small wildlife.
