Artemisia uralensis

Artemisia uralensis, commonly known as Russian wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the colder regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish flowers that appear in late summer. The plant contains volatile oils and sesquiterpene lactones, which contribute to its strong medicinal and aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its adaptability and chemical composition, it has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
