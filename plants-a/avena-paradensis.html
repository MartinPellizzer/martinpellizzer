Avena paradensis

Avena paradensis, commonly known as the slender oat, is a species of grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It is often found in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and stream banks.

Avena paradensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
