Anacamptis × dafnii nothosubsp.

camparonensis, commonly known as the Camparone orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers.

The plant typically produces a single spike with several pale pink to white flowers, each adorned with intricate patterns. It thrives in open, sunny habitats with well-drained soils, often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

This nothosubspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
