Anacamptis champagneuxii

Anacamptis champagneuxii, commonly known as the Champagne orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowering stems rise above the foliage.

Anacamptis champagneuxii is a protected species in several countries due to its ecological significance and declining populations.
