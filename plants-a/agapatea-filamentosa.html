Agapatea filamentosa

Agapatea filamentosa, commonly known as the silky agapatea, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, almost ethereal appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its unique foliage and delicate blossoms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Agapatea filamentosa thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
