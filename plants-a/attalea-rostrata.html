Attalea rostrata

Attalea rostrata, commonly known as the buriti palm, is a tall, slender palm native to the wetlands of northeastern South America.

It is characterized by its long, arching fronds and produces large, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and rich in nutrients. The plant thrives in swampy and seasonally flooded areas, making it an important component of the region's wetland ecosystems. Its fruits are also used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.

The buriti palm plays a vital role in supporting local biodiversity and is culturally significant to indigenous communities.
