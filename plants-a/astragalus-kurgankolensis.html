Astragalus kurgankolensis

Astragalus kurgankolensis, commonly known as the Kurgankol milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

Astragalus kurgankolensis is valued for its role in ecological systems and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
