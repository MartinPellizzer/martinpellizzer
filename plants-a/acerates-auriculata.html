Acerates auriculata

Acerates auriculata, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that appear in clusters during the spring. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a smooth margin. The tree produces small, round, dark brown fruits that contain one or two seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
