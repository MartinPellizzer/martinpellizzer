Adesmia pumila var. aurantiaca

Adesmia pumila var. aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange-flowered adesmia, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its striking orange-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it remains relatively rare in horticultural trade.
