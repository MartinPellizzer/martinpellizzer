Anemagrostis tenella

Anemagrostis tenella, commonly known as slender wild oat, is a low-growing grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically forms dense, tufted clumps with slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are usually green or purplish in color. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and disturbed soils.

Anemagrostis tenella is often found in association with other grasses and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for insects.
