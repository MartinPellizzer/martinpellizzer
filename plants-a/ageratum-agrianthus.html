Ageratum agrianthus

Ageratum agrianthus, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom throughout the summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Ageratum agrianthus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
