Asarca brachychila

Asarca brachychila, commonly known as the narrow-leaved asarum, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Aristolochiaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length, with a velvety texture and dark green coloration. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, often emerging from the base of the leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, preferring rich, loamy soils.

Asarca brachychila is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
