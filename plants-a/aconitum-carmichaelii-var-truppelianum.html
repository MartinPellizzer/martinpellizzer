Aconitum carmichaelii var. truppelianum

Aconitum carmichaelii var. truppelianum, commonly known as the Chinese aconite or the blue aconite, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has dark green, palmately compound leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care and avoid ingestion.
