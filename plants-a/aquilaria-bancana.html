Aquilaria bancana

Aquilaria bancana, commonly known as the Philippine agarwood tree, is a member of the Thymelaeaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, with smooth, grayish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant is notable for its ability to produce a fragrant resin, known as agarwood, when infected by specific fungi. This resin is highly valued in traditional medicine and perfumery for its unique woody and aromatic properties.

Aquilaria bancana plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to biodiversity and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
