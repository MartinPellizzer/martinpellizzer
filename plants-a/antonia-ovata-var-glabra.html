Antonia ovata var. glabra

Antonia ovata var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth antonia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. Its flowers bloom during the warmer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
