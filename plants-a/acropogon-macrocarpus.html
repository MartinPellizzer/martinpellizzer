Acropogon macrocarpus

Acropogon macrocarpus, commonly known as the large-fruited acanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bract-based inflorescences. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and prominent veins. Its flowers are typically clustered in terminal spikes, producing small, tubular blooms in shades of red, pink, or purple.

Acropogon macrocarpus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can thrive in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.
