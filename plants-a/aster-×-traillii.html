Aster × traillii

Aster × traillii, commonly known as the Traill's aster, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Aster laevis and Aster cordifolius.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States, particularly found in regions with moist, well-drained soils. The plant features showy, daisy-like flowers with blue to purple petals and yellow centers, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it often grows in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
