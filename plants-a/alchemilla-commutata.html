Alchemilla commutata

Alchemilla commutata, commonly known as lesser lady's mantle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Alchemilla commutata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and its ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
