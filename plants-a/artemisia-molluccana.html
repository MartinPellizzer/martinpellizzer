Artemisia molluccana

Artemisia molluccana, commonly known as the Moluccan wormwood, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring aromatic, gray-green leaves with a distinctive silvery underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported antispasmodic and digestive properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along coastal areas.
