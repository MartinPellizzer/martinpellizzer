Aster chilensis

Aster chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean aster, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Aster chilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
