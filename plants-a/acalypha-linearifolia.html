Acalypha linearifolia

Acalypha linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short, erect inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Acalypha linearifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique foliage.
