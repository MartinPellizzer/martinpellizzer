Andropogon sorghum var. propinquus

Andropogon sorghum var. propinquus, commonly known as dwarf sorghum, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, two-lipped flowers, typical of the Andropogoneae tribe. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Due to its resilience, it is also cultivated for forage and as a biofuel crop in some regions.
