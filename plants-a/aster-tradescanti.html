Aster tradescanti

Aster tradescanti, commonly known as the purple heart plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its unique, swollen stems and clusters of small, daisy-like flowers.

The plant is native to South America and is known for its striking, deep purple to red hues that intensify in low light conditions. Its foliage is often variegated with white or cream markings, adding visual interest to its appearance. Aster tradescanti thrives in well-draining soil and prefers indirect sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

It is also noted for its ability to purify the air, contributing to its appeal as a decorative and beneficial houseplant.
