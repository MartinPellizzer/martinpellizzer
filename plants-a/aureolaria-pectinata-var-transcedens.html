Aureolaria pectinata var. transcedens

Aureolaria pectinata var. transcedens, commonly known as the Transcendent Heath, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in woodland or swampy habitats. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and often have a hairy texture.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
