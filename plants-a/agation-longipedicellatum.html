Agation longipedicellatum

Agation longipedicellatum, commonly known as the long-stalked agation, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It features finely divided, feather-like leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in compound umbels, which attract various pollinators. Its roots are typically harvested for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.

Agation longipedicellatum thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
