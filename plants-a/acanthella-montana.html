Acanthella montana

Acanthella montana, commonly known as the mountain acanthella, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Acanthella montana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
