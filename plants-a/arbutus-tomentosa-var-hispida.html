Arbutus tomentosa var. hispida

Arbutus tomentosa var. hispida, commonly known as the hairy bearberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically oval with a leathery texture and are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible but have a tart flavor.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.
