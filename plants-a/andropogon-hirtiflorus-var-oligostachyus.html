Andropogon hirtiflorus var. oligostachyus

Andropogon hirtiflorus var. oligostachyus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale blue to purple flowers.

This variant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
