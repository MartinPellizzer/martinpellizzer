Alpinia ventricosa

Alpinia ventricosa, commonly known as the mountain ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, rhizomatous growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Alpinia ventricosa is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its potential medicinal properties.
