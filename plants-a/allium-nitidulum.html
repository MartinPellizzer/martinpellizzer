Allium nitidulum

Allium nitidulum, commonly known as the slender leek, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or purple flowers in umbels. The plant is characterized by its mild garlic-like scent and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium nitidulum is a perennial herb that is relatively low maintenance and suitable for rock gardens or borders.
