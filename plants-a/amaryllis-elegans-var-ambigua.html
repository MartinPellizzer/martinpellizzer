Amaryllis elegans var. ambigua

Amaryllis elegans var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive purple or red throat. The plant typically blooms in late spring or early summer, producing clusters of flowers on tall, slender stems. Its foliage consists of strap-like, dark green leaves that emerge from a basal rosette.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements in suitable growing conditions.
