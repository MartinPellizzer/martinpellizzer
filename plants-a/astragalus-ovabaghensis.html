Astragalus ovabaghensis

Astragalus ovabaghensis, commonly known as the Ovabagh milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus ovabaghensis is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local biodiversity.
