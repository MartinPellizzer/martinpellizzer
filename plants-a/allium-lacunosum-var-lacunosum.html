Allium lacunosum var. lacunosum

Allium lacunosum var. lacunosum, commonly known as the Sierra onion, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features basal leaves that are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in a spherical cluster, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a useful plant in some culinary traditions.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
