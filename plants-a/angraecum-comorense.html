Angraecum comorense

Angraecum comorense, commonly known as the Comorean orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to the Comoros Islands.

It features long, drooping inflorescences with white to pale pink flowers that have a distinctive, intricate structure. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth. Its nocturnal pollination mechanism is adapted for specific moths, showcasing a fascinating example of co-evolution.

Angraecum comorense is prized for its beauty and rarity in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
