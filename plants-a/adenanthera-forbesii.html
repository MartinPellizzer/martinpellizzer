Adenanthera forbesii

Adenanthera forbesii, commonly known as the African burr or wild cumin, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and produces distinctive, spiny pods that enclose its seeds. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species adapted to dry conditions.

The seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
