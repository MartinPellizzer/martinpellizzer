Astragalus skythropos var. acaulis

Astragalus skythropos var. acaulis, commonly known as the barrenstem milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often leafless stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or pale pink. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access moisture from deep within the soil.

This variety is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
