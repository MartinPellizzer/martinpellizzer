Agapetes merrilliana

Agapetes merrilliana, commonly known as the mountain heather, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other heathers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, small evergreen leaves, and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments with acidic soil, often found in cloud forests.

Agapetes merrilliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
