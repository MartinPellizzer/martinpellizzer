Aiouea amplexicaulis

Aiouea amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping evergreen, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that often wrap around the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by fleshy, red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for landscaping purposes.
