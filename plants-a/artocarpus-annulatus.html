Artocarpus annulatus

Artocarpus annulatus, commonly known as the ring-fig, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces unique fruits with a thick, fibrous outer layer and a sweet, edible interior. Its bark is smooth and grayish, often marked with distinct ring-like patterns.

Artocarpus annulatus is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its edible fruit.
