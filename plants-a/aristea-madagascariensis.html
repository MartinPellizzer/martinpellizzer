Aristea madagascariensis

Aristea madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascan aristea, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptation to arid environments. Its foliage is often silver or gray-green, with narrow, linear leaves that reduce water loss.

The showy inflorescence consists of yellow ray florets surrounding yellow disk florets, making it a striking ornamental plant.
