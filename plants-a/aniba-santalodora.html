Aniba santalodora

Aniba santalodora, commonly known as the fragrant wood or Brazilian sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant wood that emits a pleasant scent. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy pulp.

The tree is valued for its wood, which is used in traditional crafts and for its essential oils.
