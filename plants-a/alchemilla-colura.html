Alchemilla colura

Alchemilla colura, commonly known as yellow alkanet, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, silky hair, giving them a distinctive appearance.

Alchemilla colura is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to attract pollinators.
