Amorpha texana var. glabrescens

Amorpha texana var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous honeyvetch, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant has smooth, glabrous leaves and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with Rhizobium bacteria.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in arid landscapes.
