Anthericum lunatum

Anthericum lunatum, commonly known as the moonflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white flowers that open at night, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
