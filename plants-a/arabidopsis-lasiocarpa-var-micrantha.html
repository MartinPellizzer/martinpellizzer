Arabidopsis lasiocarpa var. micrantha

Arabidopsis lasiocarpa var. micrantha, commonly known as the woolly mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

It is a short-lived perennial that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
