Alchemilla vulgaris proles heteropoda

Alchemilla vulgaris proles heteropoda, commonly known as the lesser lady's mantle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white, waxy secretions. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in compact clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
