Acoridium exile

Acoridium exile, commonly known as the slender swamp lily, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Acoraceae and is characterized by its simple, strap-like leaves and small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Acoridium exile is often used in ornamental gardening due to its elegant appearance and ability to adapt to various aquatic environments.
