Armeniaca holosericea

Armeniaca holosericea, commonly known as the Armenian cherry or the pink-flowered almond, is a species of flowering plant in the Prunus genus.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey. The plant is characterized by its small, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the development of edible drupes. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while its bark is smooth and reddish-brown.

Armeniaca holosericea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical significance in traditional medicine.
