Anthericum capillare

Anthericum capillare, commonly known as the slender adder's mouth, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grassy areas and along riverbanks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a spike. Its scientific name reflects its resemblance to the adder's mouth, a type of orchid.

Anthericum capillare is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.
