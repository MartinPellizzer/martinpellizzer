Argythamnia linearifolia

Argythamnia linearifolia, commonly known as the linearleaf sandthrift, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Mediterranean Basin.

It is a low-growing shrub with narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Argythamnia linearifolia is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
