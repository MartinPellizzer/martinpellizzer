Acer aidzuense var. yezoense

Acer aidzuense var. yezoense, commonly known as the Yezo maple, is a variant of the aidzu maple native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions. Its bark is smooth and light gray, becoming more rugged with age.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its seasonal color changes.
