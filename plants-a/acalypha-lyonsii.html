Acalypha lyonsii

Acalypha lyonsii, commonly known as Lyon's spurge, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem that becomes more pronounced with age, and it is often found in wetland habitats.

Lyon's spurge is notable for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations and its adaptability to humid climates.
