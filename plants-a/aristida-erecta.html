Aristida erecta

Aristida erecta, commonly known as the upright wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and sandy soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, bract-like structures that bear the flowers.

Aristida erecta is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
