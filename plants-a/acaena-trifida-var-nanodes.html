Acaena trifida var. nanodes

Acaena trifida var. nanodes, commonly known as the small-flowered New Zealand burr, is a low-growing perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in spring. The plant produces numerous spiny, spherical fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Its foliage consists of alternate, trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

This species is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
