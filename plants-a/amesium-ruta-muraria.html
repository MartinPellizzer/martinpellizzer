Amesium ruta-muraria

Amesium ruta-muraria, commonly known as common rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, pinnately compound leaves and small yellowish-green flowers. The plant contains essential oils, which contribute to its strong, bitter scent and medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or woodland areas.

Its historical use in herbal medicine and as a spice dates back to ancient times.
