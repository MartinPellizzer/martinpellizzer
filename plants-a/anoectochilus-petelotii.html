Anoectochilus petelotii

Anoectochilus petelotii, commonly known as the Petelot's slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and a distinctive, pouch-like flower that resembles a slipper. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination.

Anoectochilus petelotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
