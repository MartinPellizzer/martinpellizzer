Astragalus andabilensis

Astragalus andabilensis, commonly known as the Andes burro's bean, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its seed pods contain small, kidney-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus andabilensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying high-altitude flora.
