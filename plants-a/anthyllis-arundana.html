Anthyllis arundana

Anthyllis arundana, commonly known as the woolly broom.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. The plant is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Anthyllis arundana is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
