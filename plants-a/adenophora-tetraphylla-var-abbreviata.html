Adenophora tetraphylla var. abbreviata

Adenophora tetraphylla var. abbreviata, commonly known as the short-stemmed bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
