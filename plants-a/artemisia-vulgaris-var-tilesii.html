Artemisia vulgaris var. tilesii

Artemisia vulgaris var. tilesii, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves and a strong, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in compact spherical shapes. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Artemisia genus and is known for its medicinal and aromatic properties.
