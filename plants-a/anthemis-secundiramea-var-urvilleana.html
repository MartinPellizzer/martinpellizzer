Anthemis secundiramea var. urvilleana

Anthemis secundiramea var. urvilleana, commonly known as the Urville's anthemis, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, often covered with fine hairs, and the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate appearance.
