Asarum mexicanum

Asarum mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican wild ginger, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the forests of Mexico.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, hooded flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic underground tuber that is used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered in some regions.
