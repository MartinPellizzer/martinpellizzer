Austromyrtus gyrosepala

Austromyrtus gyrosepala, commonly known as the Australian tea tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically consumed by native animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Austromyrtus gyrosepala is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally by Indigenous Australians.
