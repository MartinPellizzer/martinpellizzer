Acacia denticulosa

Acacia denticulosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments. Its seed pods are elongated and contain small, hard seeds.

Acacia denticulosa plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
