Arenaria guilielmi-waldemarii

Arenaria guilielmi-waldemarii, commonly known as the Waldemar's sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and coastal environments. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically succulent, adapted to retain moisture in harsh climates.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
