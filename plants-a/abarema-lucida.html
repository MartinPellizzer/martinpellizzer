Abarema lucida

Abarema lucida, commonly known as the shining abarema, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Abarema lucida is often found in open woodlands and savannas, where it plays a role in soil enrichment through nitrogen fixation.
