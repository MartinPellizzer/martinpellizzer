Astragalus aldaulgensis

Astragalus aldaulgensis, commonly known as the Almondleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological significance lies in its ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting surrounding plant life.
