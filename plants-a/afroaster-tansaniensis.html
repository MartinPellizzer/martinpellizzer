Afroaster tansaniensis

Afroaster tansaniensis, commonly known as the East African aster, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the coastline, thriving in coastal climates with high salinity. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Afroaster tansaniensis plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and provides habitat for various small invertebrates.
