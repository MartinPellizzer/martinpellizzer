Acacia cooleyi

Acacia cooleyi, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and feathery, bipinnate leaves that are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, globular spikes, which bloom in spring and attract a variety of pollinators. Acacia cooleyi is well adapted to coastal conditions, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
