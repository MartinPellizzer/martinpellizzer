Agapetes discolor

Agapetes discolor, commonly known as the white-flowered shrub, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, which bloom in late summer. Agapetes discolor thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and shady borders in horticulture.
