Avena trichopodia

Avena trichopodia, commonly known as the hairy oat, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense, hairy foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. Avena trichopodia is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a useful plant for erosion control and as a forage crop.
