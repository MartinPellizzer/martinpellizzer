Alsophila rubella

Alsophila rubella, commonly known as the red-stemmed tree fern, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk that is typically reddish in color, giving it its common name. The plant has a crowning frond that is usually bipinnate, with long, narrow pinnae that are often green or bluish-green. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Alsophila rubella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
