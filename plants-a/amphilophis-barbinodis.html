Amphilophis barbinodis

Amphilophis barbinodis, commonly known as the narrow-nosed water snake, is a species of aquatic snake found in the wetlands and marshes of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender body and distinctive narrow snout, which helps it navigate through dense vegetation and shallow water. The plant-like appearance of its scales gives it a unique visual resemblance to certain aquatic plants. It is primarily a nocturnal hunter, feeding on small fish and amphibians.

Due to habitat loss and environmental degradation, this species is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
