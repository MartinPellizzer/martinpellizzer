Artemisia lagopus

Artemisia lagopus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive purple to pink flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open fields.

Artemisia lagopus is valued for its ornamental beauty and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
