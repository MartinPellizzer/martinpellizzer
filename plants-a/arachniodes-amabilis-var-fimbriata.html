Arachniodes amabilis var. fimbriata

Arachniodes amabilis var. fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed evergreen fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, and often deeply lobed leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The fronds are typically dark green with a distinctive fringed margin, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
