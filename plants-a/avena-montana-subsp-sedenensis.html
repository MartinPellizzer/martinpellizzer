Avena montana subsp. sedenensis

Avena montana subsp.

sedenensis, commonly known as the Sierra foothill oat, is a perennial grass native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and is adapted to dry, rocky habitats. It flowers in late spring to early summer, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
