Acacia melanoxylon var. obtusifolia

Acacia melanoxylon var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved black wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and narrow, leathery leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces yellow, spherical flower heads that appear in late winter to early spring. It is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The species is valued for its hard, dense wood and has been used in traditional and contemporary woodworking.
