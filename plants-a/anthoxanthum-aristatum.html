Anthoxanthum aristatum

Anthoxanthum aristatum, commonly known as the slender oatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale yellow to brown florets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

Anthoxanthum aristatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting various insect species.
