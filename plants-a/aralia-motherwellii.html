Aralia motherwellii

Aralia motherwellii, commonly known as the Hawaiian aralia, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its foliage is often used in traditional Hawaiian basket-making due to its fibrous texture.

Aralia motherwellii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
