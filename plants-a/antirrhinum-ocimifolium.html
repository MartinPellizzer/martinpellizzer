Antirrhinum ocimifolium

Antirrhinum ocimifolium, commonly known as the creeping scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
