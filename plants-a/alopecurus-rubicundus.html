Alopecurus rubicundus

Alopecurus rubicundus, commonly known as red hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown coloration, which is most prominent in its inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, dunes, and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dense, fibrous root system that aids in soil stabilization.

Alopecurus rubicundus is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
