Adesmia negeri

Adesmia negeri, commonly known as the African bush bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural practices.

Adesmia negeri typically grows in open, sunny habitats and is often found in savanna and woodland areas.
