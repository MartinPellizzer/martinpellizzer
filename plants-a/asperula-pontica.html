Asperula pontica

Asperula pontica, commonly known as the Pontic woodruff, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features small, dark green leaves that are typically opposite or whorled in arrangement. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers in clusters during the spring season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Asperula pontica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
