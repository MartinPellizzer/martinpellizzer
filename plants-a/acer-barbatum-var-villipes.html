Acer barbatum var. villipes

Acer barbatum var. villipes, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters, with a broad, spreading crown. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters before the leaves emerge in spring.

The bark is smooth and reddish-brown, becoming more fissured with age.
