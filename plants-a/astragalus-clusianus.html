Astragalus clusianus

Astragalus clusianus, commonly known as the Clusian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions. Astragalus clusianus is also known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
