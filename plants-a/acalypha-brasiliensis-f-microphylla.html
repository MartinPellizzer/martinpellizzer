Acalypha brasiliensis f. microphylla

Acalypha brasiliensis f. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant has palmate leaves with a velvety texture and a deep green color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate a range of soil types and its resilience to urban environments.
