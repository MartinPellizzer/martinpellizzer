Anthurium macrophyllum

Anthurium macrophyllum, commonly known as the giant anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, which are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces striking spadices, which are inflorescences that consist of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. Anthurium macrophyllum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its dramatic foliage and unique floral structures.
