Aquilegia kitaibelii var. minor

Aquilegia kitaibelii var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf garden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of spurred, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are typically hairy and upright. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
