Acacia farnesiana var. heterocarpa

Acacia farnesiana var. heterocarpa, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compound leaves that are often feathery and gray-green in color, with small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to dry climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions make it a popular choice for landscaping.
