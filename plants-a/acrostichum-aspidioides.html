Acrostichum aspidioides

Acrostichum aspidioides, commonly known as the broadleaf tree fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is characterized by its large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a stout, unbranched trunk that is covered in a fibrous, spongy material. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appearance.
