Azalea × viscosepala

Azalea × viscosepala, commonly known as the broad-leaf azalea, is a hybrid shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes. Its compact growth habit and long blooming period contribute to its ornamental value.

Azalea × viscosepala is also valued for its resistance to certain pests and diseases, enhancing its suitability for cultivation in various climates.
