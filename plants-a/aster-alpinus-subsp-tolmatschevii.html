Aster alpinus subsp. tolmatschevii

Aster alpinus subsp.

tolmatschevii, commonly known as the Tolmatschev's alpine aster, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at high elevations.

The plant features slender stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are blue to purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
