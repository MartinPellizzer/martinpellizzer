Alepidea longifolia subsp. coarctata

Alepidea longifolia subsp.

coarctata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf alepidea, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
