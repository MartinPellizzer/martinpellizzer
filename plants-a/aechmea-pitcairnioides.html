Aechmea pitcairnioides

Aechmea pitcairnioides, commonly known as the Pineapple Bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The plant's leaves are typically dark green with a waxy coating, and they have sharp, pointed tips. It produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

Aechmea pitcairnioides is popular as a ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
