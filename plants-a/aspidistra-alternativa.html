Aspidistra alternativa

Aspidistra alternativa, commonly known as the Japanese evergreen, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its dark green, leathery, elliptical leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, followed by dark blue berries. It is a shade-tolerant plant that thrives in moist, well-drained soil.

Aspidistra alternativa is valued for its ornamental foliage and its ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a popular houseplant.
