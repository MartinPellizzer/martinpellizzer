Aristida patula

Aristida patula, commonly known as slender threeawn, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Aristida patula is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
