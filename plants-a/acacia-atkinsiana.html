Acacia atkinsiana

Acacia atkinsiana, commonly known as the Atkins' acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and bipinnate leaves, which are divided into numerous small leaflets. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain seeds, and it is adapted to survive in dry, hot climates. Acacia atkinsiana is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various desert animals.
