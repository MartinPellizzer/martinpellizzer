Astragalus yamamotoi

Astragalus yamamotoi, commonly known as Yamamoto's milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid to semi-arid environments. It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers in late spring.

Astragalus yamamotoi is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
