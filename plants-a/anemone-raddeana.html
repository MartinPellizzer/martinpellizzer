Anemone raddeana

Anemone raddeana, commonly known as the Siberian anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, white to pinkish flowers with a distinctive central tuft of stamens, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a short, stout rhizome and possesses basal leaves that are palmately lobed and hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to alpine gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders in colder climates.
