Artemisia vulgaris var. stolonifera

Artemisia vulgaris var. stolonifera, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features wiry, aromatic stems covered in fine, silvery-gray hairs, and lance-shaped leaves with a strong, pungent odor. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, which are typically borne in late summer. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its medicinal and historical uses have made it a notable species in traditional herbal medicine.
