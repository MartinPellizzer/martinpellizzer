Acacia rehmanniana

Acacia rehmanniana, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in spring, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments.

Acacia rehmanniana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping.
