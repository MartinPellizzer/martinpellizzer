Adiantum cordifolium

Adiantum cordifolium, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern species native to various regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and India.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that resemble the flowing hair of a maiden, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocks or in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Adiantum cordifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
