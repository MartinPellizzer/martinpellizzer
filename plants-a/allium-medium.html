Allium medium

Allium medium, commonly known as the elephant garlic, is a bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It produces large, white, spherical bulbs that are often used in culinary applications for their mild, garlic-like flavor. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that grow from a central stem. Its flowers are typically pink or purple and form in clusters at the top of the stem.

Allium medium is a hardy perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
