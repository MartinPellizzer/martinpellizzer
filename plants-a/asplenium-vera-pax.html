Asplenium vera-pax

Asplenium vera-pax, commonly known as the common spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, creeping growth habit and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rock crevices and woodland areas. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green fronds with a serrated margin, and its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the leaves. Asplenium vera-pax is a hardy species that can thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common sight in temperate regions.

Its simple structure and adaptability contribute to its widespread distribution across the globe.
