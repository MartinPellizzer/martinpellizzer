Adenophora polyantha subsp. scabricalyx

Adenophora polyantha subsp.

scabricalyx, commonly known as the rough-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China. It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its coarser foliage compared to other members of the Adenophora genus.
