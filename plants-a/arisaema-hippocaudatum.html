Arisaema hippocaudatum

Arisaema hippocaudatum, commonly known as the horse-tail arisaema, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features a distinctive, horse-tail-like inflorescence that gives it its common name, with a spadix surrounded by a broad, leaf-like spathe. The plant is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its unique appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is relatively hardy in tropical and subtropical climates.

Botanically, it belongs to the Araceae family, which includes many other ornamental and economically important plants.
