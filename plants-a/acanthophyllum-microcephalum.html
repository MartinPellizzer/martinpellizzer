Acanthophyllum microcephalum

Acanthophyllum microcephalum, commonly known as the dwarf spiny saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with succulent stems that store water and reduce transpiration. Its small size and hardy nature make it well-suited for desert and rocky habitats.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
