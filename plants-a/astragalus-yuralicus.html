Astragalus yuralicus

Astragalus yuralicus, commonly known as Yuralic milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, kidney-shaped pods that contain several seeds, typical of the genus Astragalus. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often growing at high elevations.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
