Anemia andersonii

Anemia andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's anemone, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and open woodland areas, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette and are often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer.

Anemia andersonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
