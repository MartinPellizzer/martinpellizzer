Anguillaria tenella

Anguillaria tenella, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Anguillaria tenella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
