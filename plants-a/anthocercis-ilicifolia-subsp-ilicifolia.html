Anthocercis ilicifolia subsp. ilicifolia

Anthocercis ilicifolia subsp.

ilicifolia, commonly known as the prickly pear cactus, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas. It is characterized by its spiny, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

The plant produces vibrant flowers, often in shades of yellow, orange, or red, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit, known as a "tuna," is a large, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance, providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife.
