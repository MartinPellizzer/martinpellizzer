Achyranthes splendens var. splendens

Achyranthes splendens var. splendens, commonly known as the redroot pigweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and reddish stems, which become more pronounced as the plant matures. The leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often have a hairy texture. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are borne on slender pedicels.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
