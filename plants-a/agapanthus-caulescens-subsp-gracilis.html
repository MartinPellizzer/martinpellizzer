Agapanthus caulescens subsp. gracilis

Agapanthus caulescens subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as the slender-flowered agapanthus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue or purple bell-shaped flowers in summer.

The plant is characterized by its slender stems and compact growth habit, making it suitable for small garden spaces. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in Mediterranean climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
