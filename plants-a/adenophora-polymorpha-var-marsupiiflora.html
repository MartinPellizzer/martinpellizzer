Adenophora polymorpha var. marsupiiflora

Adenophora polymorpha var. marsupiiflora, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to China.

It features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and horticultural collections.
