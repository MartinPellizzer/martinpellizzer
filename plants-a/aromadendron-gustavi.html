Aromadendron gustavi

Aromadendron gustavi, commonly known as the Gustav's aromatic tree, is a species native to the subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense canopy of dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, emitting a sweet, spicy scent. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves and flowers, are valued for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

The tree is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and in traditional herbal medicine.
