Asparagus erinaceus

Asparagus erinaceus, commonly known as the hairy asparagus, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its stout, spiny stems and dense, fern-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible, needle-like berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Asparagus erinaceus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
