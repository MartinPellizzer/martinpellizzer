Amerimnon afzelii

Amerimnon afzelii, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
