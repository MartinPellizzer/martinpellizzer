Anthericum altissimum

Anthericum altissimum, commonly known as the tall lily of the valley, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers with a faintly fragrant scent. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense colonies. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Anthericum altissimum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
