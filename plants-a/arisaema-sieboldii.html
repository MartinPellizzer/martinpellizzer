Arisaema sieboldii

Arisaema sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese cobra lily, is a herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features dark green, heart-shaped leaves and produces striking, hooded flowers that are often brightly colored. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Arisaema sieboldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in traditional medicine.
