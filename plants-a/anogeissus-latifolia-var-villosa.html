Anogeissus latifolia var. villosa

Anogeissus latifolia var. villosa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its rough, bark-like leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves and bark contain various bioactive compounds, making it valuable in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry deciduous forests.
