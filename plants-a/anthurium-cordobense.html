Anthurium cordobense

Anthurium cordobense, commonly known as the Cordoba anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and spathe. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. Its flowers are usually small and clustered around the spadix, often with a vibrant color contrast to the spathe.

Anthurium cordobense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in humid environments.
