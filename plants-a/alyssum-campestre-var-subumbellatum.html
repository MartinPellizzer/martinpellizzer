Alyssum campestre var. subumbellatum

Alyssum campestre var. subumbellatum, commonly known as the clustered alyssum, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has slender, branched stems covered in fine, soft hairs, and its leaves are linear to lance-shaped with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
