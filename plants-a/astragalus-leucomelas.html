Astragalus leucomelas

Astragalus leucomelas, commonly known as the white milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces yellow or white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

Astragalus leucomelas is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland ecosystems.
