Actaea grandis

Actaea grandis, commonly known as the tall bugbane, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in late summer, which are followed by bright red berries. Its leaves are large, palmately compound, and have a distinctive silvery underside.

Actaea grandis is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
