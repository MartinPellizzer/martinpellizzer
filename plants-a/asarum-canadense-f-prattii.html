Asarum canadense f. prattii

Asarum canadense f. prattii, commonly known as Canadian wild ginger, is a low-growing perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and a strong, spicy aroma. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers in late spring, which are clustered in a spathaceous bract. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
