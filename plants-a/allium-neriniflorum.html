Allium neriniflorum

Allium neriniflorum, commonly known as the slender leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal leaves that are narrow and grass-like, with a strong garlic-like odor. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in a dense umbel, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary potential, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
