Amblyanthopsis membranacea

Amblyanthopsis membranacea, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a small, annual herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant features slender stems and narrow, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Amblyanthopsis membranacea plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and provides nectar for pollinators in its ecosystem.
