Acetosa agrestis

Acetosa agrestis, commonly known as field sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and is often found along roadsides or in meadows. It produces edible leaves that have a sour taste due to the presence of oxalic acid.

Acetosa agrestis is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
