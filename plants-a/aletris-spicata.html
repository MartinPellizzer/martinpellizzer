Aletris spicata

Aletris spicata, commonly known as the pink root or red root, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Liliaceae and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces a red or orange tuberous root, which is the source of its common name. Aletris spicata typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in shaded, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
