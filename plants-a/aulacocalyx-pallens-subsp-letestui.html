Aulacocalyx pallens subsp. letestui

Aulacocalyx pallens subsp.

letestui, commonly known as the white-flowered flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, showy, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape and are often covered in fine hairs. It produces woody pods containing flat, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and urban landscapes.
