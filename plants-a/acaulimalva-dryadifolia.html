Acaulimalva dryadifolia

Acaulimalva dryadifolia, commonly known as the dryad's leaf mallow, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of pale pink to white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are typically prostrate or ascending, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Acaulimalva dryadifolia is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, where it forms dense mats.
