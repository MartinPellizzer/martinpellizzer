Astragalus expetitus

Astragalus expetitus, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to dry environments, with deep roots that allow it to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in restoration projects due to its hardy nature.

However, it is not commonly cultivated for ornamental or medicinal purposes.
