Arnica falconaria

Arnica falconaria, commonly known as the Falcon's arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Arnica falconaria is valued for its medicinal properties, with its roots and leaves used in traditional remedies for pain and inflammation.
