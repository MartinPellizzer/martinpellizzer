Artemisia furcata subsp. insulana

Artemisia furcata subsp.

insulana, commonly known as the island wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its strong, camphor-like scent.

This subspecies is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported digestive and antiseptic properties.
