Asclepias grandiflora

Asclepias grandiflora, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pink to reddish-orange flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains latex, which is toxic to some animals. Its flowers are pollinated by butterflies, particularly the monarch butterfly, which relies on it as a host plant for its larvae.

Asclepias grandiflora plays an important role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
