Astragalus hashtrudicus

Astragalus hashtrudicus, commonly known as the Hashtrud milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus hashtrudicus plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
