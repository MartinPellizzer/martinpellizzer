Allium baeticum subsp. genuinum

Allium baeticum subsp.

genuinum, commonly known as the Spanish garlic or wild garlic, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features a bulbous stem with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pinkish flowers in umbels.

The plant is characterized by its strong garlic scent and is often found in rocky or dry soils. It is closely related to other allium species and is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses.

Its botanical structure supports its role in both traditional and modern herbal practices.
