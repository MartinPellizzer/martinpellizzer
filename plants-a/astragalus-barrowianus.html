Astragalus barrowianus

Astragalus barrowianus, commonly known as the Barrow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as sagebrush steppe and mountain slopes. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.

Astragalus barrowianus is also valued for its ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators and wildlife.
