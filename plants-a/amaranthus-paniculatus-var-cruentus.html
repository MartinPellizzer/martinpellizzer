Amaranthus paniculatus var. cruentus

Amaranthus paniculatus var. cruentus, commonly known as the blood amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and vibrant red to purple inflorescences. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that develop into three-angled, ribbed seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance.

The leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines for their nutritional value.
