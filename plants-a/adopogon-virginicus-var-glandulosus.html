Adopogon virginicus var. glandulosus

Adopogon virginicus var. glandulosus, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, fluffy inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along coastal areas and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, contributing to its common name.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
