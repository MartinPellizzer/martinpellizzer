Andropogon exaratus

Andropogon exaratus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. This plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Andropogon exaratus is often found in wetland ecosystems where it contributes to the ecological balance and biodiversity.
