Asterocephalus prolifer

Asterocephalus prolifer, commonly known as the star-headed fig, is a unique plant belonging to the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped inflorescences, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that develop into edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
