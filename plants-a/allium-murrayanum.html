Allium murrayanum

Allium murrayanum, commonly known as Murray's garlic, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, rounded bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
