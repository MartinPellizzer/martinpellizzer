Agropyron vaginans

Agropyron vaginans, commonly known as the prairie grass or wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Agropyron vaginans is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in prairies, disturbed sites, and along roadsides.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
