Artemisia vulgaris subvar. sylvestris

Artemisia vulgaris subvar. sylvestris, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant contains volatile oils, particularly thujone, which contribute to its medicinal and historical uses. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
