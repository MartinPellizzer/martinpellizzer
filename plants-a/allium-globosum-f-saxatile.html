Allium globosum f. saxatile

Allium globosum f. saxatile, commonly known as the alpine garlic, is a botanical variant of the globe garlic species.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Caucasus, where it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. This plant is characterized by its compact, spherical bulbs and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces clusters of white or pale purple flowers in late summer, adding ornamental value to its habitat.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
