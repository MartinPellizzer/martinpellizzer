Anathallis sororcula

Anathallis sororcula, commonly known as the slender spider orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that grows on trees in tropical rainforests, relying on atmospheric moisture rather than soil. The plant produces delicate, spider-like flowers with a unique structure that facilitates pollination by specific insects. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to minimize water loss in its humid but competitive environment.

Anathallis sororcula is notable for its specialized morphology and ecological niche within its habitat.
