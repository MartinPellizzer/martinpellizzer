Acnistus punctatus

Acnistus punctatus, commonly known as the spotted spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with thin, succulent stems covered in small, white, translucent glands. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Acnistus punctatus is adapted to moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.
