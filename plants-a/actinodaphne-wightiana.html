Actinodaphne wightiana

Actinodaphne wightiana, commonly known as Wight's camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, rough-textured bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. It is valued for its essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of camphor.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
