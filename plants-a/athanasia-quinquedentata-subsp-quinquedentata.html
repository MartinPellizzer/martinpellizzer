Athanasia quinquedentata subsp. quinquedentata

Athanasia quinquedentata subsp.

quinquedentata, commonly known as the five-toothed everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form.

The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming in spring and summer. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and long-lasting blooms.
