Anchusa cespitosa

Anchusa cespitosa, commonly known as common blueweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and can spread to form dense mats.

Anchusa cespitosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
