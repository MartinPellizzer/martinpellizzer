Abutilon indicum var. hirtum

Abutilon indicum var. hirtum, commonly known as velvetleaf abutilon, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive tactile appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its fibrous roots and hairy stems are adaptations to arid environments.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an invasive species in some areas.
