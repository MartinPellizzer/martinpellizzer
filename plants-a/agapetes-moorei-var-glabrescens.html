Agapetes moorei var. glabrescens

Agapetes moorei var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-leaved silverton, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the cloud forests of South America.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. Its foliage remains evergreen throughout the year, contributing to its ornamental value in shaded garden settings.

Agapetes moorei var. glabrescens is well-suited to humid, cool climates and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.
