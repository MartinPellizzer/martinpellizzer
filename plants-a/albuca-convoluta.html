Albuca convoluta

Albuca convoluta, commonly known as the spiral lily, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that spiral around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers with six petals, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Albuca convoluta is relatively low maintenance and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
