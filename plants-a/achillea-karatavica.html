Achillea karatavica

Achillea karatavica, commonly known as Karatavsky yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces tall, branched flowering stems topped with small, white to pale yellow composite flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its medicinal properties have been historically recognized in traditional herbal practices.
