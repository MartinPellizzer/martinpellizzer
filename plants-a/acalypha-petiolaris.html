Acalypha petiolaris

Acalypha petiolaris, commonly known as the silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silver-white foliage, which contrasts beautifully with its deep green veins. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its compact growth habit and low maintenance requirements make it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
