Acsmithia parvifolia

Acsmithia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved acsmithia, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a sought-after species for specialized botanical gardens.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
