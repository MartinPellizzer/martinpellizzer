Astragalus eugeniae

Astragalus eugeniae, commonly known as the Eugenia's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus eugeniae is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
