Aphelandra blandii

Aphelandra blandii, commonly known as the silverleaf torchplant, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant, spathaceous flowers that are typically yellow or orange, with a red or purple spathe. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Aphelandra blandii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
