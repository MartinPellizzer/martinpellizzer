Amaranthus polygonoides

Amaranthus polygonoides, commonly known as the prostrate amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its prostrate or decumbent growth habit, with slender, branching stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs.

Amaranthus polygonoides is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
