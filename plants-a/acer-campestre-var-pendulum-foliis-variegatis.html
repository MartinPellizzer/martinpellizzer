Acer campestre var. pendulum-foliis-variegatis

Acer campestre var. pendulum-foliis-variegatis, commonly known as the variegated weeping maple, is a cultivar of the common maple tree.

It features distinctive, elongated, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, making it a striking ornamental plant. The leaves are heart-shaped, with a variegated pattern of white and green, adding visual interest throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is often used in landscaping for its unique appearance and seasonal color changes.
