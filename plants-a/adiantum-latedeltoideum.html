Adiantum latedeltoideum

Adiantum latedeltoideum, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern species native to the southeastern United States.

It features finely divided, bright green fronds that are often described as resembling the feathers of a bird. The plant's leaves are characterized by their wide, deltoid-shaped lobes and a distinctive, arching growth habit. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained, humus-rich soil.

Adiantum latedeltoideum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
