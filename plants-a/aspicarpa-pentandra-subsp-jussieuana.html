Aspicarpa pentandra subsp. jussieuana

Aspicarpa pentandra subsp.

jussieuana, commonly known as the Jussieu's aspicarpa, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often hairy on the underside.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom during the dry season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
