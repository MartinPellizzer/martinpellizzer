Acacia chlorantha

Acacia chlorantha, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and bipinnate leaves that are often yellow-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to semi-arid climates.

Acacia chlorantha is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and fodder.
