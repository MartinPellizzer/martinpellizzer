Antirrhinum sempervirens subsp. pertegasii

Antirrhinum sempervirens subsp.

pertegasii, commonly known as the Pertegasii Snapdragon, is a perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and profuse display of tubular, two-lipped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
