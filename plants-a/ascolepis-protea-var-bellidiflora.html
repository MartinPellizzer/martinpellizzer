Ascolepis protea var. bellidiflora

Ascolepis protea var. bellidiflora, commonly known as the bellflower protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble bells, composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of the region. Its floral structure consists of a central cone-like axis surrounded by a cluster of petal-like bracts.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
