Aristida paradisea

Aristida paradisea, commonly known as the paradise grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray appearance due to a dense covering of fine hairs.

Aristida paradisea is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
