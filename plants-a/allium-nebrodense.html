Allium nebrodense

Allium nebrodense, commonly known as the Nebrodes onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, rounded bulbs that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
