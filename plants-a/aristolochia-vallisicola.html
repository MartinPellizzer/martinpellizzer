Aristolochia vallisicola

Aristolochia vallisicola, commonly known as the canyon pipevine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, heart-shaped leaves and its fragrant, vine-like growth habit. The plant produces showy, purple to greenish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in canyons and arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
