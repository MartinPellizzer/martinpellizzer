Aristolochia brachyura

Aristolochia brachyura, commonly known as the narrowleaf Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are often pale green or yellowish in color. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy texture and a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The genus Aristolochia is known for containing aristolochic acids, which are toxic to humans and animals.
