Anacamptis × sarcidani

Anacamptis × sarcidani, commonly known as the Sarcidani Orchid, is a hybrid species native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing pinkish-purple to white flowers with a distinctive spur. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountain meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many areas.
