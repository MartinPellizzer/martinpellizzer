Alysicarpus tetragonolobus var. micranthus

Alysicarpus tetragonolobus var. micranthus, commonly known as the small-flowered burr legume, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pod fruit and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant has small, alternate leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. Its fruit is a hairy, inflated pod that contains several seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some traditional medicinal practices.
