Andropogon rhynchophorus

Andropogon rhynchophorus, commonly known as the tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, woodlands, and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are arranged in a panicle-like structure.

Andropogon rhynchophorus plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects and small animals.
