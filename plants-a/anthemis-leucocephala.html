Anthemis leucocephala

Anthemis leucocephala, commonly known as white cudweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, often hairy appearance. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as fields and roadsides. Its leaves are usually linear or lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic properties.
