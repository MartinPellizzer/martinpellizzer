Alchemilla chionophila

Alchemilla chionophila, commonly known as snowdrop alchemilla, is a perennial herb native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and silvery-white foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are clustered in loose panicles. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Alchemilla chionophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in garden landscaping for its delicate appearance.
