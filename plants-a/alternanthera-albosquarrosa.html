Alternanthera albosquarrosa

Alternanthera albosquarrosa, commonly known as white-splotched Alternanthera, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring white and pink splotches on its leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive leaf patterns.

Due to its unique appearance, it is popular among plant enthusiasts and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
