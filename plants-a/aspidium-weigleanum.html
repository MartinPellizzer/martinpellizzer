Aspidium weigleanum

Aspidium weigleanum, commonly known as the Weigle's water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, frond-like structure with a distinctive, segmented appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through spore dispersal rather than seeds.

Aspidium weigleanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
