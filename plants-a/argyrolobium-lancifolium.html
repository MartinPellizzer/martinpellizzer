Argyrolobium lancifolium

Argyrolobium lancifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gold-of-pleasure, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, giving it a distinctive appearance.
