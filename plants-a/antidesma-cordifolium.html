Antidesma cordifolium

Antidesma cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaved antidesma, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, often with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, clustered white or yellowish flowers, followed by berries that are usually red or black when mature. It is often found in coastal areas, disturbed soils, and near water bodies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
