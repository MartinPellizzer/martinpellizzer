Acicarpha runcinata

Acicarpha runcinata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grass or African lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its adaptability, it is both an important forage plant and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
