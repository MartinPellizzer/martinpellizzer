Anthoxanthum odoratum var. villosum

Anthoxanthum odoratum var. villosum, commonly known as sweet vernalgrass, is a perennial grass species characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and distinctive yellow-green foliage.

It features slender, spike-like inflorescences that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are covered with fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators in ecological restoration projects.
