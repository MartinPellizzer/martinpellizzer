Acacia flavescens var. typica

Acacia flavescens var. typica, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in globular or cylindrical heads, which bloom during the warm season. Its seed pods are elongated, woody, and contain several seeds.

Acacia flavescens var. typica is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in dry regions.
