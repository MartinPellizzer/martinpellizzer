Alsodeia parvifolia

Alsodeia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved alsodeia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial with small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its compact size and ornamental flowers, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
