Anemia × grossilobata

Anemia × grossilobata, commonly known as the hybrid hibiscus, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between *Anemia* and *Grossilobata* species.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, with a distinctive, broad petal shape. The plant is a perennial shrub that thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its foliage is usually dark green and leathery, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blossoms.

Anemia × grossilobata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
