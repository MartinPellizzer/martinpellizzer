Artemisia tridentata var. parishii

Artemisia tridentata var. parishii, commonly known as the Parry sagebrush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and aromatic, pungent scent. The plant has finely divided, needle-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flower heads. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

This variety is adapted to withstand drought and is an important component of its native habitat.
