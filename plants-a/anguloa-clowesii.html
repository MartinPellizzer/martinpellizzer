Anguloa clowesii

Anguloa clowesii, commonly known as the Clowes' anguloa, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant features a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth in high-altitude, cool environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple with a distinctive, spurred lip, and it prefers well-drained, humus-rich soil.

Anguloa clowesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
