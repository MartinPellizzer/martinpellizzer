Aeschynomene tambacoundensis

Aeschynomene tambacoundensis, commonly known as African grass or tambacounde grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences, which are typically borne on long, slender stalks. Aeschynomene tambacoundensis is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
