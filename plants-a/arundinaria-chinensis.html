Arundinaria chinensis

Arundinaria chinensis, commonly known as Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a type of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in panicles.

Arundinaria chinensis is also valued for its use in traditional Chinese medicine and as a source of sustainable materials.
