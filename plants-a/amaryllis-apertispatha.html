Amaryllis apertispatha

Amaryllis apertispatha, commonly known as the broad-leafed amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features large, lance-shaped leaves that are often broader than they are long, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the summer months. Its bulbs are adapted to store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

The species is popular among gardeners for its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
