Aira laevigata

Aira laevigata, commonly known as smooth air grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically smooth to the touch. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Aira laevigata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
