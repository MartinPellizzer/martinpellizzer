Avetra sempervirens

Avetra sempervirens, commonly known as the evergreen tree, is a species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense canopy of needle-like leaves. The plant exhibits slow growth and can live for several decades, making it a long-lived species. Its foliage remains green throughout the year, contributing to its evergreen nature.

Avetra sempervirens is often valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
