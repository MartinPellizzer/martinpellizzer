Allium tristylum

Allium tristylum, commonly known as the Sierra onion, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers during the spring and summer months. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a valuable plant for foraging and culinary use.

Allium tristylum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
