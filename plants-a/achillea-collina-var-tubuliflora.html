Achillea collina var. tubuliflora

Achillea collina var. tubuliflora, commonly known as the Alpine yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features a compact rosette of silvery-green, aromatic leaves and produces small, fluffy white or pale yellow flowers in elongated clusters. Its flowers are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
