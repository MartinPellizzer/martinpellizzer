Adansonia stanburyana

Adansonia stanburyana, commonly known as the Stanbury's baobab, is a unique species of the Adansonia genus native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, swollen trunk that stores water, a feature typical of baobab trees adapted to arid environments. The tree produces large, showy white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its fruit is a fibrous capsule that contains seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

The plant's botanical structure reflects its evolutionary adaptations to the harsh climate of its native habitat.
