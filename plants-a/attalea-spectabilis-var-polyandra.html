Attalea spectabilis var. polyandra

Attalea spectabilis var. polyandra, commonly known as the pink palm, is a species native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in swampy or wetland areas.

The plant has cultural and potential economic significance due to its unique appearance and uses in traditional practices.
