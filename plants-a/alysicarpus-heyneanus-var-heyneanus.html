Alysicarpus heyneanus var. heyneanus

Alysicarpus heyneanus var. heyneanus, commonly known as the African senna, is a perennial legume native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, spherical pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in warm, humid climates.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment and as a forage plant.
