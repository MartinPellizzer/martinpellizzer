Alyxia ruscifolia subsp. tropica

Alyxia ruscifolia subsp.

tropica, commonly known as the tropical alyxia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas. It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments.

The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers arranged in clusters, which are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a leathery texture, while its fruits are small berries that contain seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
