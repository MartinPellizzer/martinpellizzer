Adenophora tetraphylla var. angustifolia

Adenophora tetraphylla var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue to purple in color, which bloom in summer. Its flowers are tubular at the base and open into a bell shape, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
