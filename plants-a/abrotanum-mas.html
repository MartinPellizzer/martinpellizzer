Abrotanum mas

Abrotanum mas, commonly known as false hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include hairy foliage and a preference for well-drained soils.

It is often found in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in full sun.
