Astragalus grammocalyx

Astragalus grammocalyx, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, bean-like pods that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus grammocalyx is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
