Acer kansuense

Acer kansuense, commonly known as the Kansu maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value due to its vibrant autumn foliage. The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a smooth margin and a bright green upper surface. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that aid in its dispersal.
