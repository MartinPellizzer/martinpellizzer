Aster occidentalis var. scabriusculus

Aster occidentalis var. scabriusculus, commonly known as the roughleaf western aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs.

The flowers are usually blue to purple in color and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
