Acanthocereus princeps

Acanthocereus princeps, commonly known as the Mexican torch cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often sparsely branched and covered with white, woolly hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.

Due to its striking appearance and resilience, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and collections.
