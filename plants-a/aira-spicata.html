Aira spicata

Aira spicata, commonly known as slender grass or spike grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, upright stems that are typically hairless and produce dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color, and it blooms with small, wind-pollinated flowers. Aira spicata is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

It is also used in some traditional herbal practices for its potential medicinal properties.
