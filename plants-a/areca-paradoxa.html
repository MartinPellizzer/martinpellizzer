Areca paradoxa

Areca paradoxa, commonly known as the sacred areca palm, is a striking tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that give it a dramatic appearance. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its unique growth pattern and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.

Areca paradoxa is also notable for its cultural significance in various indigenous traditions.
