Anthyllis colorata

Anthyllis colorata, commonly known as the pink sweet clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, pinkish-purple flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces elongated pods that contain several seeds. Anthyllis colorata is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
