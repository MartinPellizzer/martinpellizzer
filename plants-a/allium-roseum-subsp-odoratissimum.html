Allium roseum subsp. odoratissimum

Allium roseum subsp.

odoratissimum, commonly known as the sweet garlic or rose garlic, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, strap-shaped leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers.

The plant produces bulbous structures that are often used in culinary applications due to their mild garlic flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant foliage and delicate blooms.
