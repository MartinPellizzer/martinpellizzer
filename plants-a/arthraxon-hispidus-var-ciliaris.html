Arthraxon hispidus var. ciliaris

Arthraxon hispidus var. ciliaris, commonly known as the hairy or silky grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and distinctive hairy leaves that are often covered with short, stiff hairs. The plant typically grows in a variety of habitats, including grasslands, wetlands, and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spikelet with bracts that are often hairy at the edges.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a component in native plant gardens.
