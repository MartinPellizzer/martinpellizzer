Armeria vulgaris subsp. serpentini

Armeria vulgaris subsp.

serpentini, commonly known as the serpent's head, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant produces slender, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
