Alternanthera repens

Alternanthera repens, commonly known as the wandering begonia, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, pink, or purple leaves, which are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often used in water gardens or as a ground cover.

Its rapid growth and colorful foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
