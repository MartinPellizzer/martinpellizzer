Armeniaca mera

Armeniaca mera, commonly known as the Persian peach, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its ornamental white flowers and sweet, juicy fruits. The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy green surface.

The fruit, which ripens in late summer, is prized for its flavor and used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
