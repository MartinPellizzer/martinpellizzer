Allomorphia macrophylla

Allomorphia macrophylla, commonly known as the giant leathery fern, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad, leathery leaves that can reach lengths of up to three meters. The plant's fronds are typically bipinnate, with a dense arrangement of small, finger-like leaflets. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest undergrowth.

The botanical features of Allomorphia macrophylla make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
