Anadendrum badium

Anadendrum badium, commonly known as the Texas mountain laurel, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

The bark is smooth and grayish, becoming fissured with age.
