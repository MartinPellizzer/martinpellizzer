Aspalathus holosericea

Aspalathus holosericea, commonly known as the silver needle bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is often found in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
