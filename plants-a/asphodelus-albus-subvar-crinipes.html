Asphodelus albus subvar. crinipes

Asphodelus albus subvar. crinipes, commonly known as the white asphodel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves and clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas. It is part of the Asphodelaceae family, which includes other ornamental and medicinal plants.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
