Astragalus asterias subsp. aristidis

Astragalus asterias subsp.

aristidis, commonly known as the aristoid milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and small, clustered flowers.

The plant produces yellow or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are typically trifoliate or pinnate. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
