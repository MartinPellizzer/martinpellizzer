Arundinaria arvensis

Arundinaria arvensis, commonly known as the field bamboo, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread readily in moist, fertile soils.

Arundinaria arvensis is often found in wetlands, stream sides, and other damp environments.
