Alectra pusilla

Alectra pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf fairy cup, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tiny, cup-shaped flowers that resemble miniature cups. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and wetlands, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are usually a pale yellow or greenish-white color.

Alectra pusilla is often found in clusters and is known for its delicate, ephemeral blooms that appear in the spring.
