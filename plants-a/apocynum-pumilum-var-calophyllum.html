Apocynum pumilum var. calophyllum

Apocynum pumilum var. calophyllum, commonly known as the small milkweed or dwarf milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow, opposite, and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds with silky filaments, aiding in wind dispersal.

This species is part of the Apocynaceae family, known for its milky sap and medicinal properties.
