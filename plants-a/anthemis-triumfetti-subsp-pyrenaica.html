Anthemis triumfetti subsp. pyrenaica

Anthemis triumfetti subsp.

pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Pyrenees region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow central disks and white to pale blue ray florets.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, herbaceous stems and has finely divided, aromatic leaves.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
