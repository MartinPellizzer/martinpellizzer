Astragalus calvescens

Astragalus calvescens, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Astragalus calvescens plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
