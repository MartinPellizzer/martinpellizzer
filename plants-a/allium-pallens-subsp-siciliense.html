Allium pallens subsp. siciliense

Allium pallens subsp.

siciliense, commonly known as the Sicilian leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical bulbs that are often used in local cuisine. Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other allium species such as garlic and onions.

Its ornamental value and culinary uses make it a notable plant in both horticulture and gastronomy.
