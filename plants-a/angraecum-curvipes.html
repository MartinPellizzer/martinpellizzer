Angraecum curvipes

Angraecum curvipes, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous inflorescences and dark, velvety petals that often feature intricate patterns. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrate to prevent root rot. Angraecum curvipes is notable for its unique floral structure, which includes a specialized labellum that aids in pollination by specific moths.

Due to its rarity and horticultural value, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
