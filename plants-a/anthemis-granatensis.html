Anthemis granatensis

Anthemis granatensis, commonly known as the granadina daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers. The plant features showy yellow-orange flower heads with white ray florets, typical of the Anthemis genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
