Abutilon hasslerianum

Abutilon hasslerianum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its fibrous roots and rapid growth make it both a useful plant for erosion control and a potential invasive species in certain environments.
