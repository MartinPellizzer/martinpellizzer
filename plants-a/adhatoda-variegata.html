Adhatoda variegata

Adhatoda variegata, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to India and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters in height, characterized by its dark green, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, blooming during the summer months. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported respiratory and digestive benefits.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.
