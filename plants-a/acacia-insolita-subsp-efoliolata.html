Acacia insolita subsp. efoliolata

Acacia insolita subsp.

efoliolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and small, narrow phyllodes that are typically green with a slight whitish bloom.

The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures. Acacia insolita subsp.

efoliolata is often used in rehabilitation projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
