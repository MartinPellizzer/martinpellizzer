Arenaria leptoclados subsp. peloponnesiaca

Arenaria leptoclados subsp.

peloponnesiaca, commonly known as the Peloponnesian sandwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in early summer. Its leaves are generally linear and succulent, helping it conserve water in arid conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
