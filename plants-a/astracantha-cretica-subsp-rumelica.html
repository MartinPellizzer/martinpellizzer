Astracantha cretica subsp. rumelica

Astracantha cretica subsp.

rumelica, commonly known as the Cretan thornvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and limestone formations. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
