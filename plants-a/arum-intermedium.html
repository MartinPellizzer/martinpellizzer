Arum intermedium

Arum intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate arum, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and a striking, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes many ornamental and economically important species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor plant collections.
