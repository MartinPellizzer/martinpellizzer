Agapetes variegata var. elegans

Agapetes variegata var. elegans, commonly known as the variegated silverleaf heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features dense, compact foliage with elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and variegated in color, often displaying shades of green, white, and silver. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, appearing in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
