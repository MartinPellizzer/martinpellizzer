Acacia cockertoniana

Acacia cockertoniana, commonly known as Cockerton's wattle, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are arranged in cylindrical spikes. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into smaller leaflets, and it has a deep root system.

Acacia cockertoniana is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.
