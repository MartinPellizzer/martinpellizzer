Artemisia nanschanica

Artemisia nanschanica, commonly known as Chinese wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found at high altitudes. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its medicinal properties.

Artemisia nanschanica is used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
