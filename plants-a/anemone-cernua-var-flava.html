Anemone cernua var. flava

Anemone cernua var. flava, commonly known as the yellow wood anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has alternate, palmately lobed leaves that are usually heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

The species is notable for its early blooming habit and its role in early season pollination.
