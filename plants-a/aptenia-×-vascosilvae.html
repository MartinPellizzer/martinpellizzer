Aptenia × vascosilvae

Aptenia × vascosilvae, commonly known as the coastal aptenia, is a hybrid succulent plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It features small, rounded, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is relatively low maintenance and resistant to drought and salt spray.
