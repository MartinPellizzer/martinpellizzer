Achnatherum pubicalyx

Achnatherum pubicalyx, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades and compact, cylindrical seed heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Achnatherum pubicalyx is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
