Aster × handelii

Aster × handelii, commonly known as the New England aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, vibrant purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves and sturdy, branching stems. Its flowers are composed of ray florets surrounding a central disk, which is typical of the Asteraceae family.

Aster × handelii thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural habitats.
