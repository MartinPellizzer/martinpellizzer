Acer caesium subsp. caesium

Acer caesium subsp.

caesium, commonly known as the Siberian maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often weeping form and distinctive, palmate leaves that turn vibrant red in autumn.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras for seed dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and parks.
