Ardisia zollingeri

Ardisia zollingeri, commonly known as the Zollinger's beautyberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are typically oval in shape and have a smooth texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer months. Following the flowering period, it bears clusters of bright purple berries that are highly ornamental and attract birds.

Ardisia zollingeri is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
