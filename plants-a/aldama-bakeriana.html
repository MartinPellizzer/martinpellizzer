Aldama bakeriana

Aldama bakeriana, commonly known as the Baker's aldama, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with prominent central disks. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
