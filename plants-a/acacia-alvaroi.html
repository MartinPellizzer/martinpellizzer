Acacia alvaroi

Acacia alvaroi, commonly known as the desert wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, yellowish-green phyllodes that resemble leaves. The plant produces small, globular flower heads, typically in shades of yellow or brown, which bloom during the warmer months. Acacia alvaroi is well adapted to harsh, dry environments, with deep root systems that help it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various desert species.
