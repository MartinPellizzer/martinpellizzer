Anthemis austriaca

Anthemis austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian tansy, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are aromatic and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

The plant is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported digestive and antiseptic properties.
