Achillea lanulosa var. eradiata

Achillea lanulosa var. eradiata, commonly known as the eradiate yarrow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. Native to the Mediterranean region, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, demonstrating its adaptability to arid conditions.

The plant's ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
