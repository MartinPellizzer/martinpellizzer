Anemone tetrasepala

Anemone tetrasepala, commonly known as the four-lobed anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers with four sepals that give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a hairy texture.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch to the forest floor.
