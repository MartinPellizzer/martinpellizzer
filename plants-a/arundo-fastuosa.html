Arundo fastuosa

Arundo fastuosa, commonly known as the narrowleaf reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles during the summer months. This plant is adapted to grow in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil.

However, it can become invasive in some areas if not managed properly.
