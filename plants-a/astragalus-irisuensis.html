Astragalus irisuensis

Astragalus irisuensis, commonly known as the Irkutsk milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and is adapted to grow in dry, rocky environments. Astragalus irisuensis is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid and semi-arid regions.
