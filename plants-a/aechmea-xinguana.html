Aechmea xinguana

Aechmea xinguana, commonly known as the Xingu bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater and supports a miniature ecosystem. The leaves are typically broad, stiff, and covered in a dense layer of trichomes, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces tall flower spikes with vibrant, showy bracts that range from red to orange in color.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in tropical gardens.
