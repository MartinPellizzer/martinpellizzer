Acer hyrcanum subsp. reginae-amaliae

Acer hyrcanum subsp.

reginae-amaliae, commonly known as the Persian maple, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, which turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras for seed dispersal. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
