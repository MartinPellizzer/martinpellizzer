Alloplectus schlimii

Alloplectus schlimii, commonly known as the Schlim's bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms, aiding in its nutrient acquisition. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are thin and ribbon-like, often floating on the water surface.

Alloplectus schlimii is a carnivorous plant that relies on both photosynthesis and the digestion of prey for survival.
