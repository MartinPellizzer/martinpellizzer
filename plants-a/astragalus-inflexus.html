Astragalus inflexus

Astragalus inflexus, commonly known as the bent-stem milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus inflexus is adapted to arid and rocky habitats, often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.
