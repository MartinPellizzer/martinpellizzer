Ariocarpus trigonus

Ariocarpus trigonus, commonly known as the "hedgehog cactus," is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in spines, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It has adapted to survive in harsh desert conditions with its thick, fleshy stems that store water.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for collectors and cactus enthusiasts.
