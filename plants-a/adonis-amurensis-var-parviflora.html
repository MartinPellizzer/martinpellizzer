Adonis amurensis var. parviflora

Adonis amurensis var. parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf white-flowered adonis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

However, it can be toxic to livestock and should be handled with care.
