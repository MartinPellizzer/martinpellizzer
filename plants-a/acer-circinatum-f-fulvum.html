Acer circinatum f. fulvum

Acer circinatum f. fulvum, commonly known as the golden flame maple, is a cultivar of the Pacific silver fir maple.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red foliage that turns brilliant yellow in the fall. The leaves are palmately lobed, with a serrated margin and a hairy underside. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

It is often used in landscaping for its striking seasonal color changes.
