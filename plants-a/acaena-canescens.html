Acaena canescens

Acaena canescens, commonly known as the New Zealand spinach or silver weed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage due to dense pubescence. The plant produces small, red-orange flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Its fruit is a dry, three-lobed achene that contains a single seed.

Acaena canescens is often found in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils.
