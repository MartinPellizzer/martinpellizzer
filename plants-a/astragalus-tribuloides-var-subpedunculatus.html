Astragalus tribuloides var. subpedunculatus

Astragalus tribuloides var. subpedunculatus, commonly known as the narrow pedunculate milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. The plant features slender stems with small, trifoliate leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring. Its fruit is a legume that splits open to release small, kidney-shaped seeds.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
