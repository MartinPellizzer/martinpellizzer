Agropyron gmelinii var. tenuisetum

Agropyron gmelinii var. tenuisetum, commonly known as the slender-stemmed wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats on dry, sandy soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to reddish bracts. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and resilience to grazing.

This variety is valued for its use in restoration ecology and as a forage plant for livestock.
