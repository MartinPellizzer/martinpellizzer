Asemanthia griffithii

Asemanthia griffithii, commonly known as the Kashmir rose, is a shrub native to the Himalayas, particularly found in regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Asemanthia griffithii is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
