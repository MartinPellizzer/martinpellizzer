Aporum truncatum

Aporum truncatum, commonly known as the dwarf pomegranate, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are oval in shape and have a slightly wavy margin. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries resembling pomegranates. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its compact size and ornamental berries make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
