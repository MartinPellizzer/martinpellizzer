Asplenium changputungense

Asplenium changputungense, commonly known as the Changpu tung spleenwort, is a rare fern species native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its small, leathery, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often on rocky or forested substrates. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
