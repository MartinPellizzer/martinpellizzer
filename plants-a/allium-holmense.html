Allium holmense

Allium holmense, commonly known as the Holm onion or wild garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale purple flowers in umbels. The plant is characterized by its strong garlic scent and is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its bulbs are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family, closely related to other Allium species like garlic and leeks.
