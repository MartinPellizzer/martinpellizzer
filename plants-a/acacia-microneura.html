Acacia microneura

Acacia microneura, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Acacia microneura is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy shrub.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various native animals.
