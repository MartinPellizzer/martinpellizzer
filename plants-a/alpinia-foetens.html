Alpinia foetens

Alpinia foetens, commonly known as the stinking mountain ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other ginger species. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous root system and narrow, linear leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, clustered in inflorescences.

Alpinia foetens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
