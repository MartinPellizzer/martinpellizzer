Afrocarpus falcatus subsp. gaussenii

Afrocarpus falcatus subsp.

gaussenii, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a member of the Podocarpaceae family and is native to southern Africa. It is a medium to large evergreen tree characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and straight, cylindrical trunk.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged spirally along the branches. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by cone-like structures that contain its seeds.

This species is valued for its durable wood and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
