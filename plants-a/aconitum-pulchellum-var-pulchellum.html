Aconitum pulchellum var. pulchellum

Aconitum pulchellum var. pulchellum, commonly known as the alpine monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking blue to purple hooded flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, and its stems are typically erect and covered in short, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
