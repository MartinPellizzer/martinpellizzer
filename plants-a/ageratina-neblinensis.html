Ageratina neblinensis

Ageratina neblinensis, commonly known as the misty ageratum, is a flowering plant native to the humid cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, small white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a fluffy cloud. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its botanical name reflects its association with misty, high-altitude habitats.

Ageratina neblinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
