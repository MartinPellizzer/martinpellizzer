Attalea eichleri

Attalea eichleri, commonly known as the Eichleri palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a medium-sized tree that typically grows to a height of 5 to 10 meters. The plant features a slender trunk with smooth, grayish bark and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. Its inflorescences are clustered and produce small, yellowish flowers, which are followed by edible fruits.

The plant is adapted to a variety of habitats, including wetlands and disturbed areas, and is often used in local ecosystems for its ecological value.
