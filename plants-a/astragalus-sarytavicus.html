Astragalus sarytavicus

Astragalus sarytavicus, commonly known as the Sarytav Pass milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus sarytavicus is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
