Aganosma dichotoma

Aganosma dichotoma, commonly known as the dichotomous aganosma, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elliptical, woody fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
