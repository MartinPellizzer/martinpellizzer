Astragalus andinus

Astragalus andinus, commonly known as the Andean milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to cold, arid environments and thrives in rocky or gravelly soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological significance is notable in its native habitat, where it contributes to biodiversity and ecosystem resilience.
