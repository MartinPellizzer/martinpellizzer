Astragalus trapezunticus

Astragalus trapezunticus, commonly known as the Trapezuntian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, often growing in alpine or steppe habitats. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus trapezunticus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
