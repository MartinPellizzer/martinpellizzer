Alaticaulia barbozae

Alaticaulia barbozae, commonly known as the Barboza's alate caudex plant, is a rare succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated caudex that resembles a club or a spindle, covered in a thick layer of bark. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
