Achillea damascena

Achillea damascena, commonly known as the sweet William, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers. The flowers typically range in color from white to pink and are arranged in flat-topped inflorescences. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
