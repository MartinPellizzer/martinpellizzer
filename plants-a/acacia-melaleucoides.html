Acacia melaleucoides

Acacia melaleucoides, commonly known as the white wattle, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, feathery leaves that are usually grey-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant.

Acacia melaleucoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
