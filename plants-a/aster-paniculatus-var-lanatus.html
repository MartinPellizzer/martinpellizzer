Aster paniculatus var. lanatus

Aster paniculatus var. lanatus, commonly known as the downy aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has a bushy growth habit with hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. Its flowers are typically arranged in panicles, giving it a distinctive, branching appearance.

This species is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
