Anthericum contortum

Anthericum contortum, commonly known as twisted thread-plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces a spadix, a dense cluster of tiny flowers surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Anthericum contortum is notable for its unique growth habit and its role in supporting local insect populations.
