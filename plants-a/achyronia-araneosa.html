Achyronia araneosa

Achyronia araneosa, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with long, thin sepals and petals that resemble webbing. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in areas with good drainage. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
