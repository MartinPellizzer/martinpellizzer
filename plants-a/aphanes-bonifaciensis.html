Aphanes bonifaciensis

Aphanes bonifaciensis, commonly known as the Bonifacius sandspurry, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its prostrate habit and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Aphanes bonifaciensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact growth and colorful blooms.
