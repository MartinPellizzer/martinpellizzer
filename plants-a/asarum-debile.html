Asarum debile

Asarum debile, commonly known as wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features a dense, low-growing mat of heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety hair. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by dark purple berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Asarum debile is valued for its aromatic leaves and its role in native ecosystems.
