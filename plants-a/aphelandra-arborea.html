Aphelandra arborea

Aphelandra arborea, commonly known as the tree torch plant, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated, pendant inflorescences that resemble torches, which are usually yellow or orange in color. The plant features large, glossy, dark green leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along its stems. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant for its vibrant floral display.

Aphelandra arborea is also valued for its ability to purify the air and its use in traditional medicine.
