Adiantum levingei

Adiantum levingei, commonly known as the Chinese maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern native to China.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that give it a light, airy appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in rocky crevices or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive silvery underside that shimmers in the light.

Adiantum levingei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
