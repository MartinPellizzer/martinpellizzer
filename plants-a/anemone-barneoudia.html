Anemone barneoudia

Anemone barneoudia, commonly known as the Barneoud's anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces showy, white to pinkish flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Anemone barneoudia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
