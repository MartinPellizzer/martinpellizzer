Alopecurus nigricans

Alopecurus nigricans, commonly known as blackgrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and dense, tufted inflorescences that produce small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as meadows, ditches, and fields. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in areas with high soil fertility.

Blackgrass is considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and competitive nature.
