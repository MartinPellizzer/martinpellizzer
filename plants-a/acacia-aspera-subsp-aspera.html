Acacia aspera subsp. aspera

Acacia aspera subsp.

aspera, commonly known as the rough acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its stiff, ridged, and often hairy leaves, which are typically arranged in pairs.

The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are visited by various pollinators. Its wood is hard and durable, making it useful for construction and fuel.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought and heat.
