Alchemilla ceylanica

Alchemilla ceylanica, commonly known as Ceylon lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, typically arranged in loose clusters. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Alchemilla ceylanica thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
