Achillea sibirica subsp. mongolica

Achillea sibirica subsp.

mongolica, commonly known as Mongolian yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features a rosette of gray-green, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, demonstrating drought tolerance and cold resistance. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Its historical use in traditional medicine is noted for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
