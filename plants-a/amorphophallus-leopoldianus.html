Amorphophallus leopoldianus

Amorphophallus leopoldianus, commonly known as the giant swamp taro, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and a striking, spadix-like inflorescence that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant thrives in wetland environments and is adapted to waterlogged soils. Its underground tuber is edible when mature and is used in traditional cuisine in some regions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitats.
