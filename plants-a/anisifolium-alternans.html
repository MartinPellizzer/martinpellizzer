Anisifolium alternans

Anisifolium alternans, commonly known as the alternate-leaf anisifolium, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

The plant features alternate, pinnately compound leaves with distinctive, narrow leaflets that give it its scientific name. It typically grows in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. The flowers are small and white, arranged in umbrella-like clusters, and the plant is known for its subtle, spicy scent.

Anisifolium alternans is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
