Asplenium × papyraceum

Asplenium × papyraceum, commonly known as the paper fern, is a hybrid fern species that combines traits from its parent species, Asplenium bulbiferum and Asplenium trichomanes.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often used in horticultural arrangements due to their aesthetic appeal. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as woodland floors or rocky crevices, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its spores are produced on the undersides of its fronds, contributing to its reproductive cycle.

As a hybrid, it exhibits genetic variability, making it a subject of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
