Arctium olivieri

Arctium olivieri, commonly known as the greater burdock, is a biennial plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, covered with coarse, hairy foliage. The plant produces large, spiny flower heads that are surrounded by purple or brown bracts, which give it a distinctive appearance. Its root system consists of a thick, fleshy taproot that is often harvested for medicinal and culinary purposes.

The species is known for its adaptability to a variety of soil types and its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats.
