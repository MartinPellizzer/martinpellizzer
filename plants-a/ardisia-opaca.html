Ardisia opaca

Ardisia opaca, commonly known as the Florida beautyberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are typically oval in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by bright red berries that are highly visible in the autumn. These berries are an important food source for various birds and small mammals.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
