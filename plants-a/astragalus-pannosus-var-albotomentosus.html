Astragalus pannosus var. albotomentosus

Astragalus pannosus var. albotomentosus, commonly known as the white-hairy milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to withstand drought and harsh environmental conditions.
