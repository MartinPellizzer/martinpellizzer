Allosorus crispus

Allosorus crispus, commonly known as sea rocket, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in salt marshes and dunes. Its leaves are typically curled or crinkled, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Sea rocket is an important part of coastal ecosystems, providing food for various insects and birds.
