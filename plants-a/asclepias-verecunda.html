Asclepias verecunda

Asclepias verecunda, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers, which are arranged in umbels. Asclepias verecunda is a member of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is known for its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds.

It serves as a host plant for the monarch butterfly and other milkweed-dependent species.
