Aesculus × mississippiensis

Aesculus × mississippiensis, commonly known as the Southern catalpa, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces long, bean-like pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can grow up to 20 meters tall in optimal conditions.
