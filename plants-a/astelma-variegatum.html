Astelma variegatum

Astelma variegatum, commonly known as the variegated astelma, is a striking plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a distinctive variegated pattern of green and white. This species thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, low-maintenance ornamental plants.

Its unique appearance and adaptability contribute to its popularity in both cultivated gardens and natural habitats.
