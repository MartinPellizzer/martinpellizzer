Anthemis chia f. inornata

Anthemis chia f. inornata, commonly known as the lesser tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in summer, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.

The subspecies inornata is distinguished by its less showy appearance compared to other forms of Anthemis chia.
