Abutilon arenarium

Abutilon arenarium, commonly known as sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, often forming dense mats in sandy or rocky soils. Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil, making it useful for erosion control in degraded landscapes.

Abutilon arenarium is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, thriving in environments with minimal water availability.
