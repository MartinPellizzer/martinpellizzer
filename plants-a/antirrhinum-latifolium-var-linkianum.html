Antirrhinum latifolium var. linkianum

Antirrhinum latifolium var. linkianum, commonly known as the Linkian snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and tall, branching stems that bear clusters of tubular, brightly colored flowers. The flowers typically range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant blooms and ease of care.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
