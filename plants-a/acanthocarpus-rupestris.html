Acanthocarpus rupestris

Acanthocarpus rupestris, commonly known as the rock thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its spiny stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
