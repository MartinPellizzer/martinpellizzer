Acuston perenne

Acuston perenne, commonly known as the everlasting grass, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions.

It features slender, upright stems that produce small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It is often cultivated for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its resilience to drought.

Acuston perenne is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a ground cover in landscaping.
