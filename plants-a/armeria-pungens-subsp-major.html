Armeria pungens subsp. major

Armeria pungens subsp.

major, commonly known as the sea thrift, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and northern Africa. It is a low-growing perennial that forms dense clusters of linear, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in early spring, which are clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky habitats.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in coastal landscaping.
