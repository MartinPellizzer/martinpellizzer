Amyris enneandra

Amyris enneandra, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its fragrance and durability. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy green surface.

Amyris enneandra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
