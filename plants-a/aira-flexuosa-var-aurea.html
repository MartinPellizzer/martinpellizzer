Aira flexuosa var. aurea

Aira flexuosa var. aurea, commonly known as golden wood oat, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and dense clusters of golden-yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This ornamental grass is valued for its attractive foliage and seasonal color, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
