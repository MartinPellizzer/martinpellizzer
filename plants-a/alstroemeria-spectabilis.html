Alstroemeria spectabilis

Alstroemeria spectabilis, commonly known as the Peruvian lily, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, producing showy blooms during the spring and summer seasons. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sun exposure.

Alstroemeria spectabilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
