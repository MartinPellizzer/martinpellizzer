Astragalus tingriensis

Astragalus tingriensis, commonly known as Tingri milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cold climates and well-drained soils. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Astragalus tingriensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its arid habitat and supports local biodiversity.
