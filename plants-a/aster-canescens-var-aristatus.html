Aster canescens var. aristatus

Aster canescens var. aristatus, commonly known as the gray hairgrass, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy foliage and produces showy, blue to purple ray flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its slender, erect stems are often covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
