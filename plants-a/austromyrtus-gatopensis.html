Austromyrtus gatopensis

Austromyrtus gatopensis, commonly known as the Tasmanian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the cool temperate regions of Tasmania.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas, often in association with other native flora. Austromyrtus gatopensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.

However, it is considered rare and is threatened by habitat loss and environmental changes.
