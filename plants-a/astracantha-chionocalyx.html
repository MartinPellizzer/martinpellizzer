Astracantha chionocalyx

Astracantha chionocalyx, commonly known as the white-flowered starburry, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a low-growing, mat-forming habit with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its most distinctive feature is the showy, white to pale yellow flower heads that bloom in spring.

The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
