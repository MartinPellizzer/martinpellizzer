Asperula rubioides

Asperula rubioides, commonly known as the European sweetbox, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Asperula rubioides is often used in hedges or as a ground cover due to its dense growth habit and ornamental appeal.
