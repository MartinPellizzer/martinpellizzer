Astragalus gummifer subsp. tournefortii

Astragalus gummifer subsp.

tournefortii, commonly known as the gumweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, yellowish to greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters.

The plant produces a distinctive, sticky resinous gum, which is one of its most notable botanical features. Its leaves are usually pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.
