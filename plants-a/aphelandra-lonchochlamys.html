Aphelandra lonchochlamys

Aphelandra lonchochlamys, commonly known as the narrow-lipped flame tree, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that bear vibrant orange or red flowers with long, narrow petals. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elongated, and often have a waxy texture.

Aphelandra lonchochlamys thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
