Adesmia villosa var. acutifolia

Adesmia villosa var. acutifolia, commonly known as the hairy legume, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots that help it access groundwater.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
