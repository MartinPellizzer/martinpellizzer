Andrographis tenuiflora var. recedens

Andrographis tenuiflora var. recedens, commonly known as the narrow-leavedandrographis, is a variant of the Andrographis genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves contain various bioactive compounds, including andrographolide, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

This variant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
