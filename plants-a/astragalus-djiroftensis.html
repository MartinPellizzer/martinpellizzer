Astragalus djiroftensis

Astragalus djiroftensis, commonly known as the Djiroft milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially vulnerable species.
