Asperula × jordanii

Asperula × jordanii, commonly known as the Japanese evergreen azalea, is a hybrid shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and prefers acidic, well-drained soil. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing pink to red flowers that attract pollinators.

Asperula × jordanii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded garden environments.
