Aloe africana var. latifolia

Aloe africana var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy, and often broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a white powdery coating. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with clusters of yellow to orange flowers. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and requiring minimal irrigation.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both horticultural and medicinal purposes.
