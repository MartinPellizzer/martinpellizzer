Acacia crassa

Acacia crassa, commonly known as the rough wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, yellow flowers that appear in globular clusters. The leaves are phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that function as leaves, a common adaptation in arid environments. The plant produces seed pods that are woody and contain numerous small, hard seeds.

It is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in sandy soils across the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.
