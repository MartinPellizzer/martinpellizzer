Acalypha schiedeana var. macrodonta

Acalypha schiedeana var. macrodonta, commonly known as the broad-leafed ironweed, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically hidden within the leaf axils. Its growth habit is upright, with multiple branches forming a dense, bushy structure.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
