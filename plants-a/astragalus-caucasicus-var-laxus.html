Astragalus caucasicus var. laxus

Astragalus caucasicus var. laxus, commonly known as the Caucasian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in rocky or arid environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
