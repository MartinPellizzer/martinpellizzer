Acer caudatum var. erosum

Acer caudatum var. erosum, commonly known as the red maple or scarlet maple, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red to orange-red foliage in the fall, which makes it a popular ornamental species. The tree has opposite, palmately lobed leaves that are typically 5-9 cm in length. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, appearing in clusters before the leaves in early spring.

The bark is smooth and reddish-brown, becoming fissured with age, and the tree can reach heights of up to 20 meters.
