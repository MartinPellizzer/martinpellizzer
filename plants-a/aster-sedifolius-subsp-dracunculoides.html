Aster sedifolius subsp. dracunculoides

Aster sedifolius subsp.

dracunculoides, commonly known as the woolly aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers in the late summer and early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
