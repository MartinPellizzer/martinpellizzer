Aster novae-angliae f. rosarius

Aster novae-angliae f. rosarius, commonly known as the New England aster with a rose-like appearance, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant features showy, violet-blue to pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental value.

This cultivar is valued for its vibrant color and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
