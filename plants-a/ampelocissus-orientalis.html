Ampelocissus orientalis

Ampelocissus orientalis, commonly known as the Chinese devil's ivy, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its vigorous growth habit and adaptability make it both useful and sometimes invasive in certain regions.
