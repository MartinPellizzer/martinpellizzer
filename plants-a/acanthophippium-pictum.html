Acanthophippium pictum

Acanthophippium pictum, commonly known as the painted acanthophippium, is a species of flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils and sunny conditions. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also notable for its adaptability to arid environments, showcasing its resilience in challenging climates.
