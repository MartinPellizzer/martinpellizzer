Anthurium trinerve

Anthurium trinerve, commonly known as the three-veined anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves with three prominent veins. The plant produces striking spadices, which are the modified leaves that enclose the small, inconspicuous flowers. Anthurium trinerve thrives in humid, warm environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental purposes in both indoor and outdoor settings.
