Alocasia lauterbachiana

Alocasia lauterbachiana, commonly known as the Lauterbach's elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique inflorescence structure called a spadix. Alocasia lauterbachiana thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Its striking foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
