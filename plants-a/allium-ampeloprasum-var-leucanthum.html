Allium ampeloprasum var. leucanthum

Allium ampeloprasum var. leucanthum, commonly known as the globe artichoke, is a perennial plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, rosette-like leaves that form a dense cluster around the base. The plant produces a large, spherical flower head that resembles an artichoke, with tightly packed bracts. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible flower heads.
