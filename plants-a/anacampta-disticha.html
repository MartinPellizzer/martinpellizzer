Anacampta disticha

Anacampta disticha, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive zigzagging stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Anacampta disticha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
