Aridaria oubergensis

Aridaria oubergensis, commonly known as the desert orchid, is a rare and unique plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white, adapted to attract pollinators in harsh environments. The plant has evolved deep root systems to access underground water sources, allowing it to survive in extreme drought conditions. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

Despite its beauty, Aridaria oubergensis is highly sensitive to environmental changes and is considered endangered due to habitat destruction and climate change.
