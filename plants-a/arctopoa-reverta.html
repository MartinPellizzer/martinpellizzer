Arctopoa reverta

Arctopoa reverta, commonly known as the reverse arctopoa, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, upward-facing flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of purple and pink. The plant has a compact growth habit and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Arctopoa reverta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialty gardens.
