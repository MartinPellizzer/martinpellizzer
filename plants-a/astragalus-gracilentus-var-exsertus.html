Astragalus gracilentus var. exsertus

Astragalus gracilentus var. exsertus, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces small, oblong pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and shrubland habitats.

Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval leaflets that are often hairy.
