Abronia umbellata subsp. insularis

Abronia umbellata subsp.

insularis, commonly known as the Insular Abronia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands. It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in umbels, which are typically borne on slender stems. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered with a fine layer of white hair, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and coastal environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
