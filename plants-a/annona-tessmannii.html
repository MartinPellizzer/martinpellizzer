Annona tessmannii

Annona tessmannii, commonly known as the tropical custard apple, is a small evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellow to orange fruits with a creamy, custard-like pulp.

The fruit is typically edible, though its texture and flavor can vary, and it is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
