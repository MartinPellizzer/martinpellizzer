Alchemilla andringitrensis

Alchemilla andringitrensis, commonly known as the Andringa lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Alchemilla andringitrensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
