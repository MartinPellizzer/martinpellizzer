Acanthocereus canoensis

Acanthocereus canoensis, commonly known as the Sonoran prickly pear, is a cactus native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its ribbed stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces edible fruits, known as tunas, which are used in local cuisines. It has spiny areoles and is adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements.

Its structure and adaptations make it a resilient and ecologically significant species in its habitat.
