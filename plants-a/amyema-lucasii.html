Amyema lucasii

Amyema lucasii, commonly known as the Lucas's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees in Australia.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients but can photosynthesize to some extent. Its berries are small and red, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Amyema lucasii plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat, influencing both the host tree and the local wildlife.
