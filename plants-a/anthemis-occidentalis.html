Anthemis occidentalis

Anthemis occidentalis, commonly known as western flossflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in a spherical shape, giving it a fluffy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Anthemis occidentalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
