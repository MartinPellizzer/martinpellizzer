Angelica taiwaniana

Angelica taiwaniana, commonly known as the Taiwan angelica, is a perennial herb native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes plants like carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant features tall, hollow stems and large, compound leaves that can reach several feet in height. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family, and it produces dark, berry-like fruits.

Angelica taiwaniana is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
