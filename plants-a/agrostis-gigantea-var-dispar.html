Agrostis gigantea var. dispar

Agrostis gigantea var. dispar, commonly known as giant bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender growth habit and dark green, often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
