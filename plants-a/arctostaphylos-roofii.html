Arctostaphylos roofii

Arctostaphylos roofii, commonly known as the Sierra foothill manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, rocky habitats.

Arctostaphylos roofii is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting biodiversity in its native range.
