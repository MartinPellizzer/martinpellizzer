Allium suworowii

Allium suworowii, commonly known as the Siberian leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, pale purple to white flowers. The plant produces edible bulbs that are similar in appearance to onions but have a milder flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Allium suworowii is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its potential culinary uses.
