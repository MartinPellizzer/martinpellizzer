Astragalus membranaceus var. purpurinus

Astragalus membranaceus var. purpurinus, commonly known as Chinese milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its erect stems, trifoliate leaves, and showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is known for its deep taproot system, which allows it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.

The variety purpurinus is distinguished by its vibrant flower color and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
