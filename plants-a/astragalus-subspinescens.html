Astragalus subspinescens

Astragalus subspinescens, commonly known as the spiny milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its seed pods are often hairy and contain several small, rounded seeds.

Astragalus subspinescens plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
