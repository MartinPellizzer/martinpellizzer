Alliaria nitida

Alliaria nitida, commonly known as winter cress, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alliaria nitida is also known for its peppery taste and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
