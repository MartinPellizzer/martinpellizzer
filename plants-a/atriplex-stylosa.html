Atriplex stylosa

Atriplex stylosa, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its succulent leaves that are adapted to store water, aiding in survival in dry environments. The plant has small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically appearing in spring. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep water sources and stabilize sandy soils.

Atriplex stylosa is also known for its ability to tolerate high salinity, making it a valuable plant in reclaiming saline soils.
