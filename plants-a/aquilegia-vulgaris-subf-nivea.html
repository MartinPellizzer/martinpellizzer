Aquilegia vulgaris subf. nivea

Aquilegia vulgaris subf.

nivea, commonly known as the white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking white, spurred flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are typically covered in soft, hairy foliage. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it has been historically cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
