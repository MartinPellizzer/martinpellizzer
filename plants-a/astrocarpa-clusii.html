Astrocarpa clusii

Astrocarpa clusii, commonly known as the Clusii's astrocarp, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow-orange flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
