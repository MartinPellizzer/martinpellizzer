Astragalus thomsonii

Astragalus thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and can be found in sagebrush steppe and grassland ecosystems.

Astragalus thomsonii is also valued for its ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators and wildlife.
