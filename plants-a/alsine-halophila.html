Alsine halophila

Alsine halophila, commonly known as saltgrass, is a flowering plant native to coastal and saline environments.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, grass-like leaves that help it retain water in arid conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in saline soils and is often found in salt marshes and coastal dunes.

Alsine halophila plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
