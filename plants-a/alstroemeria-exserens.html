Alstroemeria exserens

Alstroemeria exserens, commonly known as the "Peruvian lily," is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features long, strap-like leaves that are often mistaken for grasses, and its stems are typically erect and branching. Alstroemeria exserens thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
