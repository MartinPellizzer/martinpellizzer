Achillea schauloi

Achillea schauloi, commonly known as Schaul's yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features finely divided, feathery leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice among gardeners.
