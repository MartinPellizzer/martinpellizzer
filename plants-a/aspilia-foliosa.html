Aspilia foliosa

Aspilia foliosa, commonly known as the leafy aster, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy yellow-orange flower heads that attract pollinators. It is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Aspilia foliosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some African cultures.
