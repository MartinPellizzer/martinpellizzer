Amparoa majalis

Amparoa majalis, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Amparoa majalis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
