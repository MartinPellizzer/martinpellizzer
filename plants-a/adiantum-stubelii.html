Adiantum stubelii

Adiantum stubelii, commonly known as the African maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bipinnate, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through spore dispersal rather than seeds.

Adiantum stubelii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and indoor spaces.

Morphology

Adiantum stubelii has fern-like leaves that are lanceolate in shape, arranged in a bipinnate pattern, with a dark green color and a finely serrated margin.

The stems are slender, creeping, and covered with a smooth, pale green bark that grows along the ground. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are greenish-yellow in color, blooming in late spring, and are pollinated by wind.

The fruit is a dry, spore-bearing capsule that disperses spores by wind, and the roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in moist, shaded environments.
