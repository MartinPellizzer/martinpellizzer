Antirrhinum laevigatum

Antirrhinum laevigatum, commonly known as the smooth larkspur, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of blue, purple, or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Antirrhinum laevigatum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
