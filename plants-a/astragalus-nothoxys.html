Astragalus nothoxys

Astragalus nothoxys, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system is often deep, allowing it to access moisture from lower soil layers.

Astragalus nothoxys plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native range.
