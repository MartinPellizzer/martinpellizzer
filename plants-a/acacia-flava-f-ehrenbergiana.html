Acacia flava f. ehrenbergiana

Acacia flava f. ehrenbergiana, commonly known as the yellow acacia or golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, yellow-green stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and floral display.
