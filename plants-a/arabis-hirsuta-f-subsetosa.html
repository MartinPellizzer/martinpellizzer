Arabis hirsuta f. subsetosa

Arabis hirsuta f. subsetosa, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves that are typically hairy and linear to oblanceolate in shape. The flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes, often blooming in early spring. This subspecies is native to rocky or gravelly soils in mountainous regions of Europe.

It is often found in open habitats such as alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
