Arthrochilium thunbergii

Arthrochilium thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, often moss-like growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a dense spike. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found on rocky substrates or in forest undergrowth.

Arthrochilium thunbergii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
