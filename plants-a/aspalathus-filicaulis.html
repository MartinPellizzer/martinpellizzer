Aspalathus filicaulis

Aspalathus filicaulis, commonly known as the silver leaf bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, followed by small, bean-like pods containing seeds. It is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Aspalathus filicaulis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
