Ammannia robinsoniana

Ammannia robinsoniana, commonly known as the redroot ammannia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features erect, branched stems that are often tinged with red, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting species for wetland restoration and ecological studies.
