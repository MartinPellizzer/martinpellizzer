Amaracus cordifolius

Amaracus cordifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaved amaracus, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically large and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. Its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Amaracus cordifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in various indigenous communities.
