Acacia longispicata

Acacia longispicata, commonly known as the long-spined acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its long, slender, spiny stipules that are often mistaken for leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in summer, which are arranged in cylindrical or spicate inflorescences. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, feather-like leaflets.

Acacia longispicata is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystem.
