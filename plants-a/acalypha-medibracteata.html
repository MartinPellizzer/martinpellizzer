Acalypha medibracteata

Acalypha medibracteata, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, cylindrical inflorescences that are often brightly colored, typically in shades of red or orange. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, forming dense spikes that are visually prominent.

Acalypha medibracteata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
