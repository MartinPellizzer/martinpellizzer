Ageratina trapezoidea

Ageratina trapezoidea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features small, blue to purple florets arranged in dense, spherical clusters, giving it a mist-like appearance. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Ageratina trapezoidea is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological value.
