Agropyron cristatiforme

Agropyron cristatiforme, commonly known as the crested wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the steppes of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branched inflorescence that gives it its scientific name. The plant has a robust root system, allowing it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. It is widely used for erosion control and as a forage crop in dryland agriculture.

Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it an important component of ecological restoration projects.
