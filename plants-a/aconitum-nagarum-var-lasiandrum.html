Aconitum nagarum var. lasiandrum

Aconitum nagarum var. lasiandrum, commonly known as the Himalayan aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense racemes. Its leaves are palmately compound, with finely divided lobes, and are often covered in a dense layer of silky hairs.

Aconitum nagarum var. lasiandrum contains potent alkaloids, making it highly toxic and requiring careful handling.
