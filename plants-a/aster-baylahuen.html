Aster baylahuen

Aster baylahuen, commonly known as the Chilean aster, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its flowers are usually blue or violet, with a central yellow disk, and it blooms in the spring and summer.

Aster baylahuen is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
