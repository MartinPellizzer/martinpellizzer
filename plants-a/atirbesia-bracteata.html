Atirbesia bracteata

Atirbesia bracteata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as roadside ditches and agricultural fields.

Atirbesia bracteata is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
