Anthurium hypoleucum

Anthurium hypoleucum, commonly known as the white anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces vibrant, spathiflorous flowers that are usually bright red or orange, with a spadix that is often white or pale yellow. It thrives in humid, warm environments with indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium hypoleucum is popular as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and long-lasting blooms.
