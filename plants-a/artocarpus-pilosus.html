Artocarpus pilosus

Artocarpus pilosus, commonly known as the hairy breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its rough, hairy bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces a fruit that is typically round or oval, with a thick, fibrous outer layer. The fruit contains a starchy, edible pulp and seeds, which are used in various culinary and traditional applications.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and ornamental features.
