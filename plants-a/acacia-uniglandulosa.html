Acacia uniglandulosa

Acacia uniglandulosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, glandular-stemmed structure and bipinnate leaves that are narrow and often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces yellow floral spikes that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia uniglandulosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
