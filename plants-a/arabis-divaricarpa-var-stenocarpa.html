Arabis divaricarpa var. stenocarpa

Arabis divaricarpa var. stenocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruited rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh conditions.

The narrow, elongated fruits of this variety distinguish it from other members of the species.
