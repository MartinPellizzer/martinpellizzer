Asphodelus fistulosus subsp. madeirensis

Asphodelus fistulosus subsp.

madeirensis, commonly known as the Madeiran asphodel, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madeira. It is a perennial herb with linear, grass-like leaves and produces tall, slender flowering stems.

The plant blooms with clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers, which are typically tubular in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is appreciated in horticulture for its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
