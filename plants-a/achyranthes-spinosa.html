Achyranthes spinosa

Achyranthes spinosa, commonly known as the prickly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a dry capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Achyranthes spinosa is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
