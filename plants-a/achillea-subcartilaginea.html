Achillea subcartilaginea

Achillea subcartilaginea, commonly known as the brittle yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features slender, wiry stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. Its flowers are small, white to pale yellow, and arranged in flat-topped clusters, typical of the genus Achillea.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
