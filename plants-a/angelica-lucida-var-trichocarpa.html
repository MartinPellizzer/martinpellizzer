Angelica lucida var. trichocarpa

Angelica lucida var. trichocarpa, commonly known as the western angelica, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, compound leaves that are deeply lobed. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in umbrella-like clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are aromatic and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forested areas.
