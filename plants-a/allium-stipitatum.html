Allium stipitatum

Allium stipitatum, commonly known as the clustered leek, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces tall, slender flowering stems topped with clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant is part of the Allium genus, which includes many edible and ornamental species. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing at high elevations.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and visually appealing plant in alpine environments.
