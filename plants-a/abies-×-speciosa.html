Abies × speciosa

Abies × speciosa, commonly known as the white fir, is a hybrid coniferous tree resulting from the cross between Abies alba and Abies cephalonica.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean, including parts of southern Europe and North Africa. This species is characterized by its dense, pyramidal crown and needle-like leaves that are typically white or bluish-white underneath. The cones are small and often hang in clusters, and the tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and timber.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is relatively cold-hardy, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.
