Amaranthus lividus var. ascendens

Amaranthus lividus var. ascendens, commonly known as the ascending amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
