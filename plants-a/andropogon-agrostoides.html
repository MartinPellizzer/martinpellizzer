Andropogon agrostoides

Andropogon agrostoides, commonly known as tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in prairie ecosystems and disturbed areas. It plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.

Andropogon agrostoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
