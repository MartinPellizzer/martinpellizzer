Avicennia officinalis var. nitida

Avicennia officinalis var. nitida, commonly known as the slender mangrove, is a halophytic shrub that thrives in coastal and brackish water environments.

It is characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems and small, opposite leaves that are covered in a dense layer of salt-excreting glands. The plant produces tiny, white flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, and its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that release buoyant seeds for dispersal. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.

This variant is particularly adapted to fluctuating salinity levels and tidal conditions, making it an important species in mangrove ecosystems.
