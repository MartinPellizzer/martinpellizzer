Anemonastrum villosissimum

Anemonastrum villosissimum, commonly known as the silky sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with grayish-green, woolly leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Anemonastrum villosissimum is well adapted to sandy and rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
