Acrostichum affine

Acrostichum affine, commonly known as the common brake, is a species of fern native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories. Its fronds are dimorphic, with sterile and fertile leaves differing in structure.

Acrostichum affine plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various organisms.
