Abies grandis pendula

Abies grandis pendula, commonly known as the weeping white fir, is a variant of the white fir species native to North America.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that give it a distinctive, cascading appearance. The tree has needle-like leaves that are typically light green and covered in a waxy coating. It produces cones that hang downward, contributing to its ornamental value.

This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
