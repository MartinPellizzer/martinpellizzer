Alchemilla calvifolia

Alchemilla calvifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their astringent properties.

Alchemilla calvifolia thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland habitats.
