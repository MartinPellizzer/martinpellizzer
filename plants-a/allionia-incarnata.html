Allionia incarnata

Allionia incarnata, commonly known as the pinkroot, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy pink to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a distinctive reddish-brown root system, which is often harvested for its medicinal properties. It thrives in moist, wooded habitats and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Allionia incarnata is a perennial herb that grows up to 2 feet tall, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves.
