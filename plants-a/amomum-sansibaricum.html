Amomum sansibaricum

Amomum sansibaricum, commonly known as East African cardamom, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, linear leaves and clustered, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by oval, three-lobed fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its essential oils, extracted from the dried fruit, are valued for their distinctive spice and medicinal properties.

Amomum sansibaricum is often cultivated for its culinary and traditional medicinal uses.
