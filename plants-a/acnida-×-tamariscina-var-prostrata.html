Acnida × tamariscina var. prostrata

Acnida × tamariscina var. prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate tamarisk, is a hybrid shrub that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Acnida and Tamarix.

It is a low-growing plant with dense, often sprawling foliage, adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The leaves are typically small, scale-like, and arranged in clusters, reducing water loss through transpiration. Its flowers are small and usually pink or red, appearing in clusters along the stems, and it produces small, woody pods that contain seeds.

This plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
