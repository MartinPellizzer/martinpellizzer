Allium senescens var. glaucum

Allium senescens var. glaucum, commonly known as the purple globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spherical seed pods that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to arid gardens.
