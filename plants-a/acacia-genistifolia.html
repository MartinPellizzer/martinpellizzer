Acacia genistifolia

Acacia genistifolia, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, feathery leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a silky texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, narrow pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
