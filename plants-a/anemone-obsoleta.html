Anemone obsoleta

Anemone obsoleta, commonly known as the slender anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features delicate, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive, narrow corona. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed segments, and the stems are usually slender and hairy.

Anemone obsoleta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
