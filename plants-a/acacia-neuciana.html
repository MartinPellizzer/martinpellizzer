Acacia neuciana

Acacia neuciana, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny branches and small, bipinnate leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. The plant produces yellow to pale yellow flowers arranged in compact, globular inflorescences, which bloom during the dry season. Acacia neuciana is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its ecosystem.
