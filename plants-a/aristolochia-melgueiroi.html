Aristolochia melgueiroi

Aristolochia melgueiroi, commonly known as the Melgueiro's Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular flowers that are typically purple or brown in color, with a distinctive shape resembling a pipe. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
