Amorphophallus malkmus-husseinii

Amorphophallus malkmus-husseinii, commonly known as the Sudanese arrowhead, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its unique floral structure.

This plant is native to the savannas of Sudan and is known for its large, striking inflorescence that resembles a phallic shape, hence its common name. The plant produces a single, showy spathe that encloses a spadix, which is covered in tiny, densely packed flowers. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is a subject of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
