Allium nikolaii

Allium nikolaii, commonly known as the Nikolai's onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces rounded, globe-shaped bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid and high-altitude environments.
