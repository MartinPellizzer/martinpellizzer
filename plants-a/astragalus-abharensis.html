Astragalus abharensis

Astragalus abharensis, commonly known as the Abhar milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus abharensis is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
