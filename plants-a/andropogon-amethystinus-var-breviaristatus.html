Andropogon amethystinus var. breviaristatus

Andropogon amethystinus var. breviaristatus, commonly known as the purple bluestem grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for native plant gardens and restoration projects.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a desirable choice for sustainable landscaping.
