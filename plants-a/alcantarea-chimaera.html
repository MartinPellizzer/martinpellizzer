Alcantarea chimaera

Alcantarea chimaera, commonly known as the Ghost Plant, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, and often brightly colored leaves that can range from red to orange and yellow. The plant is known for its ability to store water in its thick, waxy leaves, making it drought-tolerant. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear at the base of the rosette.

Alcantarea chimaera is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
