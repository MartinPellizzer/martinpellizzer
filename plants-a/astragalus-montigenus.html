Astragalus montigenus

Astragalus montigenus, commonly known as the mountain milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access moisture from lower soil layers.

Astragalus montigenus is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
