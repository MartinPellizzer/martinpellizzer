Asparagus racemosus var. tetragonus

Asparagus racemosus var. tetragonus, commonly known as the tetragonal asparagus, is a variant of the asparagus plant native to India and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its branched, cylindrical stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellow or greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, and its berries are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine for its purported health benefits.
