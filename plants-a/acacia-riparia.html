Acacia riparia

Acacia riparia, commonly known as the river acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, flat seed pods. Acacia riparia is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing near water sources.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems and has been used traditionally for its timber and medicinal properties.

Native Distribution

Acacia riparia is primarily native to the African continent, including countries such as Sudan, South Sudan, Ethiopia, Kenya, and Tanzania.

It is also found in parts of the Arabian Peninsula and the Indian subcontinent. This plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing along riverbanks and in sandy soils. It is commonly associated with savanna and grassland ecosystems.

Acacia riparia plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.

Acacia riparia was mainly introduced to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in arid and semi-arid regions.
