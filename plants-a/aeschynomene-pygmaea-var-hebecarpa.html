Aeschynomene pygmaea var. hebecarpa

Aeschynomene pygmaea var. hebecarpa, commonly known as dwarf sicklepod, is a low-growing annual herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically reaches a height of only a few centimeters, with slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale green to yellow flowers that are followed by slender, spirally twisted pods containing several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environments, contributing to its widespread distribution.
