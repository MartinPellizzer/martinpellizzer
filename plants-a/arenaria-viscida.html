Arenaria viscida

Arenaria viscida, commonly known as sticky sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and hairy, often sticky leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Arenaria viscida plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptations to extreme climates.
