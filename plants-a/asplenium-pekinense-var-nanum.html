Asplenium pekinense var. nanum

Asplenium pekinense var. nanum, commonly known as the dwarf maidenhair fern, is a compact, evergreen fern native to East Asia.

It features delicate, finely divided, glossy green fronds that are typically less than 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, upright sporangia clusters on the underside of its leaves, typical of the Asplenium genus. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and rock gardens.
