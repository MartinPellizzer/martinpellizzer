Adiantum matogrossense

Adiantum matogrossense, commonly known as the Matogrossense maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Adiantum genus, which is part of the family Pteridaceae. This fern is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble the feathery structure of traditional maidenhair ferns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

The plant's botanical features include its rhizomatous growth habit and small, spore-producing sorus structures on the underside of its leaves.

Morphology

Adiantum matogrossense has fern-like leaves that are pinnately compound, with a delicate, lace-like appearance, and they are arranged alternately along the stem.

The leaves are typically small, ranging from 10 to 20 centimeters in length, with a bright green color that may turn bronze in the autumn. The stem is slender and creeping, with a smooth, light green bark that grows horizontally along the ground. This species does not produce flowers, as it is a spore-bearing plant, and instead reproduces through spores released from the undersides of the fronds.

Its roots are fibrous and rhizomatous, helping to anchor the plant and absorb moisture from the soil.
