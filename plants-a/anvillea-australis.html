Anvillea australis

Anvillea australis, commonly known as the Australian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils. It produces small, oval-shaped fruits called cypsela, which are dispersed by wind.

Anvillea australis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and coastal landscapes.
