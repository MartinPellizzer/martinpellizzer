Alternanthera macrorhiza

Alternanthera macrorhiza, commonly known as the red-hot poker or flame plant, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its foliage is often variegated, with shades of green, red, and yellow, adding to its ornamental value.

It thrives in full sun and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for water gardens and wetland habitats.
