Antimima piscodora

Antimima piscodora, commonly known as the water lily of the genus Antimima, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The botanical structure of Antimima piscodora includes a rhizomatous stem, which helps it anchor and spread in aquatic habitats.
