Astragalus eriocephalus

Astragalus eriocephalus, commonly known as the hairy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features compound leaves with small, hairy leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers in early summer. Its seed pods are cylindrical and contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Astragalus eriocephalus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinators.
