Anemone flava

Anemone flava, commonly known as the yellow anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and erect. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas.

Anemone flava is valued for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinators.
