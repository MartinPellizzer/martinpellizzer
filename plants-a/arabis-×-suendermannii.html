Arabis × suendermannii

Arabis × suendermannii, commonly known as the hybrid rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its low-growing habit and rosette-forming leaves. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions.

Arabis × suendermannii is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings due to its ornamental value and hardiness.
