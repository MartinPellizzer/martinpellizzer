Astragalus strictus

Astragalus strictus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright, slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.

Astragalus strictus is also valued for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
