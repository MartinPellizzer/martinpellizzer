Astragalus spinosus var. hamrinensis

Astragalus spinosus var. hamrinensis, commonly known as the Hamrin thorned milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Middle East.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and steppe habitats.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
