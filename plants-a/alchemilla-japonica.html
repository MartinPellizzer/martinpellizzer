Alchemilla japonica

Alchemilla japonica, commonly known as Japanese lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features rosettes of silvery-white, hairy leaves that are covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
