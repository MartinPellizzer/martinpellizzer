Alsophila pilosula

Alsophila pilosula, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant has a distinctive trunk covered in a fibrous, brownish layer that resembles bark. Its fronds are typically bright green and can grow up to several meters in length.

Alsophila pilosula is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in shaded, moist environments.
