Aloe sessiliflora

Aloe sessiliflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features rosettes of fleshy, narrow, gray-green leaves that are coated with a powdery substance, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Aloe sessiliflora is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
