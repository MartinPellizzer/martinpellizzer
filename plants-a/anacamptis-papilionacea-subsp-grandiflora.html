Anacamptis papilionacea subsp. grandiflora

Anacamptis papilionacea subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, butterfly-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color, with a distinctive lip that is often more intensely colored.

The subspecies is noted for its relatively large, showy blooms compared to other members of the Anacamptis genus.
