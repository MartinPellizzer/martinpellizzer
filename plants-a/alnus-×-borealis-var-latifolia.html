Alnus × borealis var. latifolia

Alnus × borealis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf alder, is a hybrid species native to North America.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often near streams or in riparian zones. The plant is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It produces small, yellowish catkins in the spring, which are important for pollination.

This species plays a vital role in soil stabilization and provides habitat and food for various wildlife species.
