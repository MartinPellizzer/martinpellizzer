Allium jaegeri

Allium jaegeri, commonly known as the Jaeger's onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, onion-like bulbs and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
