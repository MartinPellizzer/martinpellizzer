Anil mysorensis

Anil mysorensis, commonly known as the Mysore anil, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Anil mysorensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual beauty.
