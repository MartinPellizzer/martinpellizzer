Amygdalus persica camelliiflora

Amygdalus persica camelliiflora, commonly known as the Persian almond or camellia-flowered almond, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from the almond (Amygdalus persica) and the camellia.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring large, showy flowers that resemble those of camellias. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with lance-shaped leaves and a moderate growth rate. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

While not widely cultivated for fruit production, it is appreciated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
