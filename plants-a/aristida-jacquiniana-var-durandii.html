Aristida jacquiniana var. durandii

Aristida jacquiniana var. durandii, commonly known as Durand's threeawn, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features slender, tufted culms with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spiky inflorescences composed of dense clusters of spikelets. Its flowering period usually occurs during the warmer months, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
