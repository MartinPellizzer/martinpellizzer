Anthurium huacamayoense

Anthurium huacamayoense, commonly known as the Huacamayo anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green with a metallic sheen. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, which can range from red to orange in hue. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical and subtropical gardens.
