Asystasia chelonoides var. arabica

Asystasia chelonoides var. arabica, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, pale blue or white flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a silvery-gray underside, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used as a ground cover or in hanging baskets.

The plant is also known for its ability to attract beneficial insects, making it a valuable addition to ecological gardens.
