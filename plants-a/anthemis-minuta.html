Anthemis minuta

Anthemis minuta, commonly known as dwarf tansy, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features slender, branched stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets surrounding a central disk, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

Anthemis minuta is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
