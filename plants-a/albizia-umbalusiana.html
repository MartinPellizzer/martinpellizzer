Albizia umbalusiana

Albizia umbalusiana, commonly known as the African silk tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its fragrant, fluffy white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Albizia umbalusiana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.
