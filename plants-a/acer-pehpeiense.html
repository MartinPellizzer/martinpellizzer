Acer pehpeiense

Acer pehpeiense, commonly known as the Pehpei maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its striking red and yellow autumn foliage, which makes it a popular ornamental plant. The tree has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its bark is smooth with a reddish-brown color. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Acer pehpeiense is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and urban landscapes.
