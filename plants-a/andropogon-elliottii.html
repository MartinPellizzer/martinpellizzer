Andropogon elliottii

Andropogon elliottii, commonly known as bluestem grass, is a tall perennial grass native to the eastern and central United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant has narrow, bluish-green leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often reddish-brown in color. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Andropogon elliottii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and restoration projects.
