Actaea rubra subsp. arguta

Actaea rubra subsp.

arguta, commonly known as red baneberry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red, spore-bearing berries that contrast with its compound leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade. Its showy, white to pale pink flowers appear in early summer, followed by the bright red berries in late summer.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful if ingested.
