Acacia daviesii

Acacia daviesii, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate, lacy appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in spherical clusters, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Acacia daviesii is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
