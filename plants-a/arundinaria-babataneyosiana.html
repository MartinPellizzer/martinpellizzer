Arundinaria babataneyosiana

Arundinaria babataneyosiana, commonly known as the Babataneyosian bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
