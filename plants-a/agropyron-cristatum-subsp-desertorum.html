Agropyron cristatum subsp. desertorum

Agropyron cristatum subsp.

desertorum, commonly known as the desert wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and spikelets that are typically covered with soft hairs.

The plant has narrow, bluish-green leaves and produces small, pale blue to green flowers in summer. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

This species is often used for erosion control and as a forage crop in dryland environments.
