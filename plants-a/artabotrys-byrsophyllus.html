Artabotrys byrsophyllus

Artabotrys byrsophyllus, commonly known as the horned flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in clusters, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as birds and insects. Its bark is smooth and greyish, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a street tree in tropical regions.
