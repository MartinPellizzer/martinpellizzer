Aloe galpinii

Aloe galpinii, commonly known as the Galpin's aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers, which bloom in the spring. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Aloe galpinii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
