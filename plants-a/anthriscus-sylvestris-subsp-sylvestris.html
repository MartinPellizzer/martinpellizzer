Anthriscus sylvestris subsp. sylvestris

Anthriscus sylvestris subsp.

sylvestris, commonly known as wild chervil, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features finely divided, feathery leaves that resemble those of parsley, and it produces small, white, umbellate flowers.

The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. It is often used in culinary applications for its delicate, anise-like flavor.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ecological and gastronomic purposes.
