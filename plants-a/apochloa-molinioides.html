Apochloa molinioides

Apochloa molinioides, commonly known as the spurge nettle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features simple, alternate leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

Apochloa molinioides is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
