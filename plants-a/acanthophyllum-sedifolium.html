Acanthophyllum sedifolium

Acanthophyllum sedifolium, commonly known as the sand mat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its low-growing, mat-like structure and succulent leaves that help it conserve water. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its adaptations to harsh environments include thick, waxy leaves and deep root systems that enable it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Acanthophyllum sedifolium plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
