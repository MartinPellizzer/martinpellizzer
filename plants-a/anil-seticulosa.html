Anil seticulosa

Anil seticulosa, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and can grow up to 20 meters in height. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree's bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.
