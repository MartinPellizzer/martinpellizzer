Achras zapota var. typica

Achras zapota var. typica, commonly known as the chicozapote or zapote blanco, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its thick, aromatic, and edible fruit, which has a creamy white pulp and a sweet, nutty flavor. The tree produces large, palm-like leaves and has a sturdy, trunk-like stem that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. Its fruit is often used in culinary applications, including desserts and beverages, and it also has traditional medicinal uses.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
