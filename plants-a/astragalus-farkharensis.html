Astragalus farkharensis

Astragalus farkharensis, commonly known as the Farkhar milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep water sources, and it plays a role in soil stabilization.

Astragalus farkharensis is also of interest for its potential medicinal properties.
