Arctostaphylos alpina subsp. rubra

Arctostaphylos alpina subsp.

rubra, commonly known as red mountain manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Mexico. It is characterized by its reddish bark and dense, evergreen foliage.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink to red in color, which bloom in late spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dark seeds.

This subspecies is well adapted to rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
