Achillea pectinata

Achillea pectinata, commonly known as the fuzzy yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and flat, pinkish-white flower heads that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and wildflower meadows.

Its historical use in herbal medicine is attributed to its purported healing properties.
