Alchemilla laevipes

Alchemilla laevipes, commonly known as the smooth lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of rounded, silver-green leaves that are softly hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in upright clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Alchemilla laevipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
