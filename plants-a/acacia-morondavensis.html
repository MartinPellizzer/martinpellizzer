Acacia morondavensis

Acacia morondavensis, commonly known as the Madagascar wattle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that function as leaves, a common adaptation in arid environments. It produces yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, and its seeds are enclosed in woody pods.

Acacia morondavensis is adapted to dry conditions and plays a role in local ecosystems.
