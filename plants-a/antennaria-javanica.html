Antennaria javanica

Antennaria javanica, commonly known as the Javan mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Compositae (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands. Its flowers are usually clustered in dense, spherical heads, and it produces small, dry fruits called achenes.

Antennaria javanica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
