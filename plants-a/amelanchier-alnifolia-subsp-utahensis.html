Amelanchier alnifolia subsp. utahensis

Amelanchier alnifolia subsp.

utahensis, commonly known as the Utah serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States. It typically grows in open woodlands, rocky slopes, and mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and oval-shaped with a smooth margin.

The Utah serviceberry is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance, providing food for wildlife and supporting local biodiversity.
