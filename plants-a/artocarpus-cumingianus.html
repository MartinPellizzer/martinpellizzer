Artocarpus cumingianus

Artocarpus cumingianus, commonly known as the Cuming's breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive fruit with a rough, spiky exterior. The fruit, which resembles a breadfruit, contains a starchy, edible pulp that is used in various culinary applications. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit, which can be cooked or fermented.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
