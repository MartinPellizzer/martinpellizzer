Atylosia subrhombea

Atylosia subrhombea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with a hairy stem that is often reddish in color. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in spring, producing small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Atylosia subrhombea is typically found in grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
