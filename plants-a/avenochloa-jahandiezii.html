Avenochloa jahandiezii

Avenochloa jahandiezii, commonly known as the Persian wild oat, is a grass species native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a rough texture, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its potential for use in ecological restoration and its genetic diversity.
