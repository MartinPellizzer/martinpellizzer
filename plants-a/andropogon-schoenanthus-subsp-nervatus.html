Andropogon schoenanthus subsp. nervatus

Andropogon schoenanthus subsp.

nervatus, commonly known as the nervy bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves with distinct nervures.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in supporting native pollinators and maintaining grassland biodiversity.
