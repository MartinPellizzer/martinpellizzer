Aster eylesii

Aster eylesii, commonly known as the Eyles' aster, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Aster eylesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
