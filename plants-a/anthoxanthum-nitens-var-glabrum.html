Anthoxanthum nitens var. glabrum

Anthoxanthum nitens var. glabrum, commonly known as the glabrous bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact growth habit, making it well-suited for lawns and turfgrass applications. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish spikelets that are typically clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, exhibiting moderate drought tolerance.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological and horticultural studies.
