Anemone obtusiloba var. violacea

Anemone obtusiloba var. violacea, commonly known as the violet-flowered anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and meadows. The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate with a wavy margin and a hairy surface. Its flowers are showy, with violet to purple petals that bloom in early spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
