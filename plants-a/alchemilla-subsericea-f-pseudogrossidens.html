Alchemilla subsericea f. pseudogrossidens

Alchemilla subsericea f. pseudogrossidens, commonly known as the silverleaf alchemilla, is a perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is native to regions of Central Asia. The plant features narrow, silvery-gray leaves covered in dense, fine hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, white to pale yellow, and appear in clusters during the summer months.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
