Arctostaphylos glandulosa subsp. glandulosa

Arctostaphylos glandulosa subsp.

glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its woody stems, dense foliage, and small, elliptical leaves with glandular dots.

The plant produces clusters of showy, pinkish-white flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
