Arum ehrenbergii

Arum ehrenbergii, commonly known as the Schomburgk's arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces edible underground tubers that are rich in starch and used in local cuisines. Its leaves are large, arrow-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Arum ehrenbergii thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
