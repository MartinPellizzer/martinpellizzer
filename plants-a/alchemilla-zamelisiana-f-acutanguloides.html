Alchemilla zamelisiana f. acutanguloides

Alchemilla zamelisiana f. acutanguloides, commonly known as the sharp-leaved alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its foliage is commonly used in herbal remedies for its purported soothing properties.
