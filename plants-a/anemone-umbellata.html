Anemone umbellata

Anemone umbellata, commonly known as the common anemone, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pinkish flowers that resemble a crown, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are lobed and often covered in fine hairs. Native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form colonies in suitable habitats.
