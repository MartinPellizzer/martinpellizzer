Alsine lanceolata var. laxa

Alsine lanceolata var. laxa, commonly known as the looseleaf alsike, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
