Albuca angolensis

Albuca angolensis, commonly known as the Angola onion, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible bulbs that are similar in appearance to onions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Albuca angolensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
