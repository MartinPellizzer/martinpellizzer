Aconitum caeruleum

Aconitum caeruleum, commonly known as the blue monkshood, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of striking blue-purple, helmet-shaped flowers. The plant belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its toxic alkaloids, which make it highly poisonous to both humans and animals. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance.

Aconitum caeruleum thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.
