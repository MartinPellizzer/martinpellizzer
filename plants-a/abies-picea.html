Abies picea

Abies picea, commonly known as the white spruce, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in spirals along the branches. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions.

Abies picea is often found in boreal forests and is valued for its timber and ecological role in supporting various wildlife species.
