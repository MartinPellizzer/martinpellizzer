Artanthe javitensis

Artanthe javitensis, commonly known as the Javitean artanthe, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae, which includes many species known for their vibrant foliage and showy blooms. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, often colorful flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Artanthe javitensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
