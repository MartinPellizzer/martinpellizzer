Anemia cuneata

Anemia cuneata, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Anemia cuneata is often found in moist meadows, prairies, and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
