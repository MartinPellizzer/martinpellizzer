Ariocarpus × drabi

Ariocarpus × drabi, commonly known as the hybrid dragon's mouth cactus, is a rare and intriguing plant native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is a hybrid between Ariocarpus retusus and Ariocarpus agavoides, resulting in a unique combination of traits from both parent species. The plant is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and thick, ribbed structure, which help it store water in its native desert environment. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive, hooded shape that adds to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Ariocarpus × drabi is often cultivated by enthusiasts who appreciate its botanical uniqueness.
