Alectorolophus heldreichii

Alectorolophus heldreichii, commonly known as the Heldreich's lichen, is a species of lichen that belongs to the family Parmeliaceae.

It is characterized by its crustose growth form, forming a tightly adherent, foliose or crust-like structure on rock surfaces. The thallus is typically pale yellow to orange in color, with a distinctive, irregularly lobed appearance. It is often found in Mediterranean regions, particularly on sunny, calcareous substrates.

This lichen is known for its chemical composition, containing various secondary metabolites that contribute to its ecological and biochemical significance.
