Allium sphaerocephalon var. descendens

Allium sphaerocephalon var. descendens, commonly known as the dwarf globe onion, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, rounded bulbs and produces clusters of white, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and mild, onion-like flavor.

This variety is a hardy perennial that thrives in full sun and is relatively low maintenance.
