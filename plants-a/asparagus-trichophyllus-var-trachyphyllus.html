Asparagus trichophyllus var. trachyphyllus

Asparagus trichophyllus var. trachyphyllus, commonly known as the roughleaf asparagus, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its distinctive, rough-textured leaves.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is native to parts of southern Africa. The plant features slender, branching stems that produce small, inconspicuous white flowers. Its leaves are elongated, often with a waxy coating, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is typically grown as an ornamental plant for its unique foliage and hardy nature.
