Acca

Acca, commonly known as the feijoadeira or starfruit, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

It includes several species, the most well-known being Acca sellowiana, which is native to South America. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often cultivated for their tangy flavor. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a fragrant aroma, and the flowers are typically white or pink.

The genus is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its fruit-bearing potential.
