Anacardium orientale

Anacardium orientale, commonly known as the cashew tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It produces a unique fruit that contains both a nut and a toxic, sticky resin called urushiol. The plant belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like poison ivy and mango. The cashew tree is valued for its edible seeds and its ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.

Its botanical features include compound leaves and a distinctive, spiny fruit structure.
