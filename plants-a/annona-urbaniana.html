Annona urbaniana

Annona urbaniana, commonly known as the Brazilian custard apple, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a small tree or shrub that produces large, green, spiny fruits. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually heart-shaped or ovate. Its flowers are white or pale green, with a distinctive fragrance.

The fruit is typically harvested when it turns yellow or red, and it has a creamy, aromatic pulp.
