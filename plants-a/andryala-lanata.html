Andryala lanata

Andryala lanata, commonly known as the woolly andryala, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves and stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Andryala lanata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
