Arisaema murrayi

Arisaema murrayi, commonly known as the Murray's arisaema, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered around the spadix, which is often covered in a dense mass of tiny, inconspicuous flowers. Arisaema murrayi typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
