Amaranthus emarginatus var. pseudogracilis

Amaranthus emarginatus var. pseudogracilis, commonly known as the slender amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features erect, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a bluish-green hue and serrated edges. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes, typically producing a reddish or purple color.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in some traditional cuisines.
