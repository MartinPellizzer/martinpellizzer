Alternanthera sessilis var. neglecta

Alternanthera sessilis var. neglecta, commonly known as the velvet leaf plant, is a tropical aquatic plant characterized by its striking, velvety foliage.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep red or purple hue, which intensify under strong light conditions. The plant is native to South America and thrives in warm, humid environments with good water circulation. It is often cultivated in freshwater aquariums for its ornamental value and ease of care.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for foreground or midground planting in planted tanks.
