Aspalathus priorii

Aspalathus priorii, commonly known as the priorii bush, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small leaflets, and it produces small, pale yellow flowers.

Aspalathus priorii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
