Aster sedifolius subsp. acer

Aster sedifolius subsp.

acer, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It typically grows in open, dry habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes.

The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, linear to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in dense, flat-topped heads, producing blue to purple petals that bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
