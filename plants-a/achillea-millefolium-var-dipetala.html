Achillea millefolium var. dipetala

Achillea millefolium var. dipetala, commonly known as the two-petaled yarrow, is a variant of the common yarrow.

It is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves. The flowers are typically small and white or pale yellow, arranged in flat-topped clusters. This variety is noted for its shorter petals compared to the typical form, giving it a distinct appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grassy areas.
