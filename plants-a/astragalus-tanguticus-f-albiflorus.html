Astragalus tanguticus f. albiflorus

Astragalus tanguticus f. albiflorus, commonly known as the Tangut milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The subspecies albiflorus is distinguished by its white or pale yellow floral coloration, which sets it apart from other forms of Astragalus tanguticus. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to cold, arid conditions.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and is of interest for its potential medicinal properties.
