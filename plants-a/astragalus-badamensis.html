Astragalus badamensis

Astragalus badamensis, commonly known as Persian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus badamensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
