Anthurium moronense

Anthurium moronense, commonly known as the Moronense Philodendron, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, often with a deep green color and prominent veins. The plant produces bright red spadices surrounded by a showy white spathe, which is typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium moronense is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
