Andropogon minutiflorus

Andropogon minutiflorus, commonly known as littleflower bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, blue-gray flowers that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that becomes more pronounced in the dry season.

Andropogon minutiflorus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
