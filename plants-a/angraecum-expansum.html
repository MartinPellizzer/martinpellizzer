Angraecum expansum

Angraecum expansum, commonly known as the giant angraecum, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence that can reach up to 3 meters in height, making it one of the tallest orchids in the world. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting specific pollinators like hawkmoths. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its arid native habitat.

Angraecum expansum is a rare and endangered species, with conservation efforts focused on protecting its natural habitat.
