Atriplex macropoda

Atriplex macropoda, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its succulent leaves, which are adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant has small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. Its salt-tolerant nature makes it well-suited for coastal and saline soils.

Atriplex macropoda plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
