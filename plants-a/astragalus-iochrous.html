Astragalus iochrous

Astragalus iochrous, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant has compound leaves and produces pods that contain small, hard seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with deep root systems and reduced leaf surface area.

Astragalus iochrous plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
