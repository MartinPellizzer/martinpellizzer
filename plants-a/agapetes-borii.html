Agapetes borii

Agapetes borii, commonly known as the alpine silverleaf, is a small evergreen shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It typically grows in moist, cool environments at elevations above 3,000 meters. The plant features dense, leathery foliage that is often covered in a silvery or pale pubescence. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are clustered in umbels.

Agapetes borii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging alpine conditions.
