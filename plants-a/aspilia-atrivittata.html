Aspilia atrivittata

Aspilia atrivittata, commonly known as the black-vined aspilia, is a striking herbaceous plant native to eastern Africa.

It features tall, upright stems that are often covered in a dark, almost black, waxy coating. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical heads, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are lance-shaped and usually have a distinctive dark green color with a slightly hairy texture.

Aspilia atrivittata is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
