Acer saccharum var. sinuosum

Acer saccharum var. sinuosum, commonly known as the sinuous sugar maple, is a variant of the sugar maple native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that often display a striking yellow or orange color in the fall. The tree produces samaroid fruits, which are winged seeds dispersed by wind. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
