Artemisia vulgaris var. littoralis

Artemisia vulgaris var. littoralis, commonly known as coastal wormwood, is a variant of the common tarragon plant native to coastal regions.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shores. The plant features silver-gray foliage with finely divided leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in late summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to coastal environments.

This variant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
