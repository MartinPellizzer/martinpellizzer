Arisaema mirabile

Arisaema mirabile, commonly known as the beautiful arisaema, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant produces strikingly colorful flowers, often with a yellow or green spathe and a spadix that may bear both male and female flowers. Arisaema mirabile is typically found in shaded, moist forest environments, where it thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and specialty plant collections.
