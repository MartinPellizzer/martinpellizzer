Ancylostemon gamosepalus

Ancylostemon gamosepalus, commonly known as the Malabar aloe, is a succulent plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are thick and fleshy. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
