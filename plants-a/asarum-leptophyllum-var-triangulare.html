Asarum leptophyllum var. triangulare

Asarum leptophyllum var. triangulare, commonly known as the triangular wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, reddish-brown leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, maroon-colored flowers that are clustered in a spathaceous sheath, typical of the Aristolochiaceae family. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly through underground stems.
