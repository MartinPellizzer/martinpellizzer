Aspalathus fornicata

Aspalathus fornicata, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Aspalathus fornicata is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
