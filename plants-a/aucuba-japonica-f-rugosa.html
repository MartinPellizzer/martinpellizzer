Aucuba japonica f. rugosa

Aucuba japonica f. rugosa, commonly known as the Japanese aucuba variegated form, is a compact, evergreen shrub with striking, waxy, dark green leaves that have prominent, wavy margins.

The plant features showy, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters during late spring to early summer. It is native to Japan and thrives in partial to full shade, making it an excellent choice for woodland gardens. The leaves remain lush throughout the year, providing year-round interest in the landscape.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
