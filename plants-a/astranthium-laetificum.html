Astranthium laetificum

Astranthium laetificum, commonly known as the joyous thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive inflorescence structure. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Astranthium laetificum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
