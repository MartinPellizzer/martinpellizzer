Atriplex patula var. oblanceolata

Atriplex patula var. oblanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, bluish-gray leaves that are oblong in shape, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable species for stabilizing desert soils.

Its drought tolerance and ability to fix nitrogen contribute to its ecological importance in degraded landscapes.
