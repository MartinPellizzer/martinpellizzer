Androcymbium palaestinum

Androcymbium palaestinum, commonly known as the Palestine orchid, is a rare and endangered species native to Israel and Palestine.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, hairy stem and produces a single, erect flower with three petals and three sepals. It thrives in rocky, limestone soils and is often found in shaded, dry habitats.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is critically endangered and protected in several regions.
