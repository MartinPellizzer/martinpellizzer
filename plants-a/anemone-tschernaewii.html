Anemone tschernaewii

Anemone tschernaewii, commonly known as the Tschernaeff's anemone, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture that helps protect it from harsh environmental conditions.

Anemone tschernaewii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
