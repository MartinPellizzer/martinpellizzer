Adhatoda protracta

Adhatoda protracta, commonly known as the African bignonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, which bloom during the rainy season. Its bark is smooth and light brown, and it has a distinctive scent when crushed.

Adhatoda protracta is often found in moist woodland and riverine habitats.
