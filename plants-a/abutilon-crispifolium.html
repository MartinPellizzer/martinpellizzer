Abutilon crispifolium

Abutilon crispifolium, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, woolly leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Abutilon crispifolium is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
