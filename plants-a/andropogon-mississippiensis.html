Andropogon mississippiensis

Andropogon mississippiensis, commonly known as Mississippi panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant has tall, slender stalks that can reach up to 2 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
