Anchusa pauciflora

Anchusa pauciflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue or violet bell-shaped flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Anchusa pauciflora is adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
