Aristea juncifolia

Aristea juncifolia, commonly known as the grass-like flowering plant, is a species native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically in shades of white or yellow. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
