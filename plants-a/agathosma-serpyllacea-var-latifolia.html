Agathosma serpyllacea var. latifolia

Agathosma serpyllacea var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf agathosma, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping.
