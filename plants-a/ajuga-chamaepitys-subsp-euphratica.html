Ajuga chamaepitys subsp. euphratica

Ajuga chamaepitys subsp.

euphratica, commonly known as Persian bugleweed, is a perennial herb native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are small, tubular, and usually violet or blue in color. It belongs to the Valerianaceae family, known for its aromatic properties.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
