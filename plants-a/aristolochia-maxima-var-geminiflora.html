Aristolochia maxima var. geminiflora

Aristolochia maxima var. geminiflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often green or reddish in color. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. Its flowers are typically borne in pairs, giving the species its common name.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
