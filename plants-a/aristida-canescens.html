Aristida canescens

Aristida canescens, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with reddish or purplish hues. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Aristida canescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
