Anemone narcissiflora subsp. sachalinensis

Anemone narcissiflora subsp.

sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sachalin anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Korean Peninsula and parts of eastern Russia. It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
