Azalea nudiflora var. crispa

Azalea nudiflora var. crispa, commonly known as the crinkled azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crinkled leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pink, typically blooming in early spring. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its foliage and floral display.
