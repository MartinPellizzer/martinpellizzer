Adiantum pedatum var. tripartitum

Adiantum pedatum var. tripartitum, commonly known as the tripartite maidenhair fern, is a variant of the delicate maidenhair fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, trifoliate leaves that give it a lacy appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its fronds are typically dark green with a silvery underside, and it produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves.

This fern is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and indirect light.
