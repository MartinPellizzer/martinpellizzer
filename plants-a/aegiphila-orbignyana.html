Aegiphila orbignyana

Aegiphila orbignyana, commonly known as the Brazilian balm or false cinnamon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in folk remedies for various ailments.
