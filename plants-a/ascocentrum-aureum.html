Ascocentrum aureum

Ascocentrum aureum, commonly known as the golden caladium, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that display vibrant shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant belongs to the Araceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens and indoor spaces. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for decorative purposes, though it is not typically cultivated for its flowers.
