Asyneuma prenanthoides

Asyneuma prenanthoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a composite head.

Asyneuma prenanthoides is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in regions with well-drained soils.
