Anthyllis variegata var. caucasica

Anthyllis variegata var. caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian variable broom, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which makes it valuable in soil enrichment.

Its ornamental value has also led to its cultivation in gardens and landscapes.
