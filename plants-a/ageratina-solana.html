Ageratina solana

Ageratina solana, commonly known as whitecup bush, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense clusters, giving it a fluffy appearance. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Ageratina solana is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
