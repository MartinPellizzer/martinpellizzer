Acmella marajoensis

Acmella marajoensis, commonly known as the Marajo beauty, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland and marshy areas, thriving in humid, tropical climates. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface.

Acmella marajoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant display.
