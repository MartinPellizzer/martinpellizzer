Ardisia racemigera

Ardisia racemigera, commonly known as the clustered beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. These berries are a key feature, attracting birds and providing ornamental value.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy appearance, contributing to its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
