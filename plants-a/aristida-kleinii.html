Aristida kleinii

Aristida kleinii, commonly known as Klein's threeawn, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences composed of three to five bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and covered with fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Aristida kleinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
