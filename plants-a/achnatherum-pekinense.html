Achnatherum pekinense

Achnatherum pekinense, commonly known as the Peking needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to northern China.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, cylindrical leaves and produces feathery, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its fine, wiry stems and small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Achnatherum pekinense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
