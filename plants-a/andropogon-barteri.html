Andropogon barteri

Andropogon barteri, commonly known as Barter's switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that bloom in late summer, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for native plant gardens and restoration projects.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
