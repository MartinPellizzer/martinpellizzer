Amaryllis fulgida

Amaryllis fulgida, commonly known as the fiery amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow, often with a fiery orange center. The plant produces tall, erect stems that can reach up to 3 feet in height, adorned with broad, strap-like leaves. Its blooming period typically occurs in the spring or early summer, depending on the climate.

Amaryllis fulgida is prized for its vibrant colors and ease of cultivation, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
