Acalypha grandis var. genuina

Acalypha grandis var. genuina, commonly known as the velvetleaf or redroot, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by distinctive, three-lobed seed capsules. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain areas.
