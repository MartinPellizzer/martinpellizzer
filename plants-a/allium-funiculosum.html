Allium funiculosum

Allium funiculosum, commonly known as the threadleaf garlic, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures similar to garlic, though they are typically smaller and more delicate. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium funiculosum is often used in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
