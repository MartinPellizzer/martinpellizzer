Annona muricata f. mirabilis

Annona muricata f. mirabilis, commonly known as the strawberry apple or custard apple, is a tropical plant belonging to the Annonaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is distinctive for its rough, spiny exterior and sweet, creamy flesh with a unique flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
