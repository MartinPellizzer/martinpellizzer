Artemisia densifolia

Artemisia densifolia, commonly known as silverleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in dense, fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Botanically, it belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in traditional medicine.
