Allium rhodopeum subsp. turcicum

Allium rhodopeum subsp.

turcicum, commonly known as the Turkish leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features long, slender leaves that are typically bluish-green in color, and it produces umbels of white or pale purple flowers.

The plant is characterized by its bulbous stem, which is often used in traditional cuisines for its mild, onion-like flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a member of the Allium genus, which includes many economically important species.
