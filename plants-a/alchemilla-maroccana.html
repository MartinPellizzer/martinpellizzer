Alchemilla maroccana

Alchemilla maroccana, commonly known as Moroccan lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, velvety leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Alchemilla maroccana is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
