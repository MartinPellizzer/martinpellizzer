Agave lempana

Agave lempana, commonly known as the Mexican rope cactus, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are used to make ropes and textiles, which is why it is also called the Mexican rope cactus.

Agave lempana is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for arid environments.
