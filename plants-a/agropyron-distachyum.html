Agropyron distachyum

Agropyron distachyum, commonly known as the needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is often found in open grasslands, disturbed soils, and along roadsides. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Agropyron distachyum is valued for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
