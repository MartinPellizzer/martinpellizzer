Aridarum alatense

Aridarum alatense, commonly known as the desert wing plant, is a rare succulent native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the rainy season, adding a delicate touch to its harsh environment. Its root system is shallow but extensive, enabling it to absorb moisture from the surface after infrequent rains.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered vulnerable to environmental changes and human encroachment.
