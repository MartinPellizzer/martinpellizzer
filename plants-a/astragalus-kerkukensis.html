Astragalus kerkukensis

Astragalus kerkukensis, commonly known as Kerkuk milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological significance is notable in its native habitats due to its ability to thrive in harsh conditions.
