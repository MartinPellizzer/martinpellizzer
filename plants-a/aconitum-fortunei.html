Aconitum fortunei

Aconitum fortunei, commonly known as Chinese aconite, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely leafed stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, typically from July to September. Its roots contain potent alkaloids, making it highly toxic and requiring careful handling.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.
