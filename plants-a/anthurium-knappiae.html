Anthurium knappiae

Anthurium knappiae, commonly known as the Knapp's anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the humid lowland forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spatula-shaped bract. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a glossy, dark green surface and a prominent midrib. Its flowers are typically small and clustered around the spadix, with a unique coloration that varies by species.

Anthurium knappiae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
