Anacamptis morio subsp. morio

Anacamptis morio subsp.

morio, commonly known as the early purple orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of violet-blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while the inflorescence consists of a spike of showy, spurred flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
