Agrostis hyemalis var. laxiflora

Agrostis hyemalis var. laxiflora, commonly known as the winter bentgrass, is a cool-season grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that are typically arranged in a loose, open panicle. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, ditches, and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
