Abrus madagascariensis

Abrus madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar bean or velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to Madagascar.

It produces striking red and black seeds encased in a silky, fibrous pod. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

The seeds contain toxic compounds, making them hazardous if ingested.
