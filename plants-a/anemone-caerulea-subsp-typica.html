Anemone caerulea subsp. typica

Anemone caerulea subsp.

typica, commonly known as the blue anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features basal rosettes of deeply lobed, blue to violet-colored leaves and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of blue and purple.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
