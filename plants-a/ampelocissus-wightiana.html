Ampelocissus wightiana

Ampelocissus wightiana, commonly known as the climbing fig, is a woody vine native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing habit and can grow up to 10 meters in length. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture and a distinct scent. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and climbing ability.
