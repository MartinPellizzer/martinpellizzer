Achyrospermum tisserantii

Achyrospermum tisserantii, commonly known as the Madagascar daisy, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and dry forests, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually opposite and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from white to pink.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
