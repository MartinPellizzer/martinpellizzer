Aristolochia ceresensis var. acuminata

Aristolochia ceresensis var. acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and its unique, pipe-like flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.
