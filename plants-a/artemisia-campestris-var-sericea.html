Artemisia campestris var. sericea

Artemisia campestris var. sericea, commonly known as silver wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which results from dense covering of fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in compact, spherical clusters called heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and traditional herbal uses.
