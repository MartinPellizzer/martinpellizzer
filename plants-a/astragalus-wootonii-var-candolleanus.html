Astragalus wootonii var. candolleanus

Astragalus wootonii var. candolleanus, commonly known as the Wooton's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and open plains. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
