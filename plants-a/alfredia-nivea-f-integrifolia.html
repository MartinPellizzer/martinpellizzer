Alfredia nivea f. integrifolia

Alfredia nivea f. integrifolia, commonly known as the white alfredia, is a rare and striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, white, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its foliage is notable for its smooth, undivided margins, which distinguish it from other varieties in the genus.

Due to its unique appearance and limited natural range, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
