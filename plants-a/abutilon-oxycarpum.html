Abutilon oxycarpum

Abutilon oxycarpum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often appearing in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Abutilon oxycarpum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
