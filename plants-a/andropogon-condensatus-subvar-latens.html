Andropogon condensatus subvar. latens

Andropogon condensatus subvar. latens, commonly known as the tall bluestem grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. This grass thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in open woodlands and prairies. Its flowering heads are small and spike-like, typically appearing in late summer to early fall.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native landscapes.
