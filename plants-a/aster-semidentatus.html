Aster semidentatus

Aster semidentatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and produces showy purple or blue flowers in the fall. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinctive serration pattern, giving it its scientific name.

Aster semidentatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
