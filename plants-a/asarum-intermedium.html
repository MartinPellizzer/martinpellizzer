Asarum intermedium

Asarum intermedium, commonly known as Indian snakeroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features a creeping rhizome with heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers that are clustered in a dense umbel, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is part of the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its aromatic, sometimes spicy scent.
