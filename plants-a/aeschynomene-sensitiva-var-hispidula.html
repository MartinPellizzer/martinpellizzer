Aeschynomene sensitiva var. hispidula

Aeschynomene sensitiva var. hispidula, commonly known as the hairy sensitive aeschynomene, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its leguminous properties and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, trifoliate leaves that are sensitive to touch. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its potential healing properties.
