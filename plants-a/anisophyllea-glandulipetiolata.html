Anisophyllea glandulipetiolata

Anisophyllea glandulipetiolata, commonly known as the glandular-petioled anisophyllea, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are often borne on short peduncles. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in tropical botany and ethnobotany.
