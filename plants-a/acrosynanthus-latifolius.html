Acrosynanthus latifolius

Acrosynanthus latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf acrosynanthus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
