Acalypha abingdonii

Acalypha abingdonii, commonly known as the Abingdon hollyhock, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, featuring upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are ovate to lance-shaped, often with a velvety texture and a slight waxy coating.

Acalypha abingdonii is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial to full sun.
