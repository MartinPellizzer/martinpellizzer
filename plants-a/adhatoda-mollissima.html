Adhatoda mollissima

Adhatoda mollissima, commonly known as velvetleaf or Indian bellflower, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Adhatoda mollissima is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
