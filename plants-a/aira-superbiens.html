Aira superbiens

Aira superbiens, commonly known as the tufted air grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Aira superbiens plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
