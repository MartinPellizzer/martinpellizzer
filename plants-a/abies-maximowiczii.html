Abies maximowiczii

Abies maximowiczii, commonly known as the Korean fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The cones of this species are typically short and cylindrical, maturing to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

The plant has moderate to slow growth rates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
