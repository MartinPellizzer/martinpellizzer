Adina nobilis

Adina nobilis, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of small, round fruits. Adina nobilis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various tropical and subtropical environments.
