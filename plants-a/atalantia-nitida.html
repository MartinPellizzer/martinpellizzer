Atalantia nitida

Atalantia nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf ash, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, silvery-gray bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red fruits containing three seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland forest regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and parks for its attractive appearance and adaptability.
