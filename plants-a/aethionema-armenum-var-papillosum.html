Aethionema armenum var. papillosum

Aethionema armenum var. papillosum, commonly known as Armenian candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in early spring. The plant has basal leaves that are linear to oblanceolate in shape, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
