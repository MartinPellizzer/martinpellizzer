Amaranthus tucsonensis

Amaranthus tucsonensis, commonly known as the Tucson amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and branched stems, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or red in color, depending on the variety. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

Amaranthus tucsonensis is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and disturbed areas.
