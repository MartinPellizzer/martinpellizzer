Ammannia auriculata var. subsessilis

Ammannia auriculata var. subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ammannia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, erect stems that often branch near the base, and its leaves are lanceolate with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers in clusters, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Amaranthaceae family.
