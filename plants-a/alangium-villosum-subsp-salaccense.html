Alangium villosum subsp. salaccense

Alangium villosum subsp.

salaccense, commonly known as the hairy alangium, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the family Berberidaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with a hairy texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it of interest in horticulture and traditional medicine.
