Alchemilla alpina subsp. pallens

Alchemilla alpina subsp.

pallens, commonly known as the alpine lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. Alchemilla alpina subsp.

pallens is often used in rock gardens and alpine landscapes due to its hardy nature and ornamental foliage.
