Alchemilla fissimima

Alchemilla fissimima, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense, spherical heads. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Alchemilla fissimima is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and its ability to attract pollinators.
