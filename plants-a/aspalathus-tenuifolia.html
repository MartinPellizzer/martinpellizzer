Aspalathus tenuifolia

Aspalathus tenuifolia, commonly known as the African green tea or rooibos, is a small, shrub-like plant native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires a warm, Mediterranean climate. Its leaves are rich in antioxidants and are used to produce a popular herbal tea.

The plant's unique botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and commercial contexts.
