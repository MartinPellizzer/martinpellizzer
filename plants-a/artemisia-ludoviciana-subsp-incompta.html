Artemisia ludoviciana subsp. incompta

Artemisia ludoviciana subsp.

incompta, commonly known as gray mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the eastern and central United States. It features aromatic, gray-green foliage with silvery-white undersides, and its leaves are covered in fine, glandular hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clustered inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodlands or along streambanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
