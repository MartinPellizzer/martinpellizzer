Acer rubrum f. palmatum

Acer rubrum f. palmatum, commonly known as the red maple with palmate leaves, is a variant of the red maple tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaf shape, which resembles a hand with five fingers, and its vibrant red foliage in the fall. The plant typically grows as a deciduous tree, reaching heights of up to 20 meters, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens and parks due to its striking seasonal color changes.

The species is also notable for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it a popular choice for urban landscapes.
