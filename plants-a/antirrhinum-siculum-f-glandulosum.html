Antirrhinum siculum f. glandulosum

Antirrhinum siculum f. glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular antirrhinum, is a variant of the common snapdragon.

It is characterized by its glandular-pubescent stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The flowers are typically pink or red, with two lips that resemble a dragon's mouth, a feature common to the genus Antirrhinum. This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.
