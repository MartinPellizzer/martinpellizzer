Astragalus achundovii

Astragalus achundovii, commonly known as the Achundov's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing a high degree of adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus achundovii is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
