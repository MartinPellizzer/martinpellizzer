Ascyrum microsepalum

Ascyrum microsepalum, commonly known as the small-sepal orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, terrestrial growth habit. The plant features small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.

Ascyrum microsepalum is a rare and endangered species, requiring conservation efforts to protect its habitat.
