Anthemis jordanovii

Anthemis jordanovii, commonly known as Jordan's chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Eastern Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often surrounded by white or pale blue ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Its aromatic properties make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
