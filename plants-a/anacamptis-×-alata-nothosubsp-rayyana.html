Anacamptis × alata nothosubsp. rayyana

Anacamptis × alata nothosubsp.

rayyana, commonly known as the hybrid lady's tresses, is a hybrid orchid species originating from crosses between Anacamptis morio and Anacamptis pyramidalis. It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescence with numerous small, fragrant flowers arranged in a spiral pattern.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are grass-like, linear, and arranged in a rosette at the base.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, showy blooms.
