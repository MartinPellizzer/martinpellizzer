Anthyllis cytisoides var. sallustiani

Anthyllis cytisoides var. sallustiani, commonly known as the Sallustian broom, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound, trifoliate leaves and yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant is often found in dry, calcareous soils and is adapted to thrive in sunny, well-drained environments. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its association with rhizobia bacteria, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
