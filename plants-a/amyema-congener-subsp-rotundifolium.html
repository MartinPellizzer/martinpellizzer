Amyema congener subsp. rotundifolium

Amyema congener subsp.

rotundifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed amyema, is a unique species within the Myrtaceae family. It is characterized by its elliptical, often rounded leaves that are thick and leathery in texture.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, clustered in compact inflorescences. It is native to specific regions of Australia, where it thrives in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its limited distribution and distinctive morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
