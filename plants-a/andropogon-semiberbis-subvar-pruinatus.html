Andropogon semiberbis subvar. pruinatus

Andropogon semiberbis subvar. pruinatus, commonly known as the pruinose bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and a compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinctive powdery coating, giving them a silvery appearance.

This subshrub is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
