Agropyron caninum var. ustulatum

Agropyron caninum var. ustulatum, commonly known as the prairie grass or western wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
