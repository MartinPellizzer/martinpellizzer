Asarum albivenium

Asarum albivenium, commonly known as the white-veined wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, clustered in a spathe-like structure. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Asarum albivenium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
