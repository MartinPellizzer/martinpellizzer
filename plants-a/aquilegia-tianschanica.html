Aquilegia tianschanica

Aquilegia tianschanica, commonly known as the Tian Shan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with finely divided lobes, and it produces follicular fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Aquilegia tianschanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
