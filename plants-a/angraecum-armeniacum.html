Angraecum armeniacum

Angraecum armeniacum, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, branched inflorescence and distinctive star-shaped flowers with a prominent white or yellow center. The plant produces showy, fragrant blooms that are typically white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. Angraecum armeniacum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is a popular species among orchid enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
