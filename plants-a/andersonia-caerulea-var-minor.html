Andersonia caerulea var. minor

Andersonia caerulea var. minor, commonly known as the blue bell heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the wet tropical regions of Australia.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, which bloom during the warmer months. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in a dense, compact form, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained acidic soils and requires high levels of sunlight to flourish.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display and hardy nature.
