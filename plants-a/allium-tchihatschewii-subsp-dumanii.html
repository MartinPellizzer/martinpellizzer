Allium tchihatschewii subsp. dumanii

Allium tchihatschewii subsp.

dumanii, commonly known as the Armenian mountain garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, pale purple to white flowers.

The plant is characterized by its robust bulbs, which are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical structure makes it well adapted to cold and arid environments.
