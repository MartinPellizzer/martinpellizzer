Allium vaginatum

Allium vaginatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved garlic or the garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a basal rosette of long, narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces upright flowering stems that bear small, clustered white or pale purple flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a useful culinary plant.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive leaf sheaths that enclose the stem.
