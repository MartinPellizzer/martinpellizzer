Aristida venesuelae

Aristida venesuelae, commonly known as the Venezuelan threeawn, is a grass species native to South America, particularly Venezuela.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and covered with fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Aristida venesuelae is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
