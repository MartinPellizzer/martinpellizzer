Acer dasycarpum f. pendulum

Acer dasycarpum f. pendulum, commonly known as the weeping maple, is a unique variant of the hairy maple characterized by its cascading branches that create a striking, flowing appearance.

This cultivar exhibits the typical features of the species, including palmate leaves with five lobes and small, clustered flowers in early spring. The foliage turns vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn, adding ornamental value to gardens and landscapes. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for temperate climates.

Due to its distinctive form, it is often used as a specimen tree or in ornamental horticulture.
