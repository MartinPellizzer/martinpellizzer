Anabasis oppositifolia

Anabasis oppositifolia, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a low-growing shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, opposite leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep soil moisture.

Anabasis oppositifolia plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
