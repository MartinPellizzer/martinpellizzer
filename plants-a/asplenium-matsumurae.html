Asplenium matsumurae

Asplenium matsumurae, commonly known as the Matsuura's spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines and Indonesia, where it grows on tree trunks and rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has a distinctive morphology, featuring a short, creeping rhizome and lanceolate, serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of trichomes. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the leaves, typical of many fern species.

Asplenium matsumurae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
