Aster franklinianus

Aster franklinianus, commonly known as Franklin's aster, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. It produces showy purple to blue flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species in some regions.
