Anemone hirsutissima

Anemone hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white to pinkish flowers with a distinctive hairy texture on its petals and stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its common name.

Anemone hirsutissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
