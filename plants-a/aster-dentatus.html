Aster dentatus

Aster dentatus, commonly known as the toothed aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, toothed leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas.

Aster dentatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
