Anethum minus

Anethum minus, commonly known as cow parsley or wild carrot, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small, white or greenish-yellow flowers that form umbrella-like clusters. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. The plant has a mild carrot-like scent and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.

Its seeds are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.
