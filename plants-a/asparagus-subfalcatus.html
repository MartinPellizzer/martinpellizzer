Asparagus subfalcatus

Asparagus subfalcatus, commonly known as the sword asparagus, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its sword-shaped, stiff, and often spiny leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
