Acinodendron melinonis

Acinodendron melinonis, commonly known as the Melinon's acacia, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in spherical inflorescences. Its seeds are enclosed in hard, woody pods, a typical feature of the legume family.

Due to its drought tolerance and ecological significance, it plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils in its native habitat.
