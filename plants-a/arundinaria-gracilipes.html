Arundinaria gracilipes

Arundinaria gracilipes, commonly known as the slender bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically green and smooth to the touch. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Arundinaria gracilipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities.
