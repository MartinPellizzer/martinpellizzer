Aristolochia campaniformis

Aristolochia campaniformis, commonly known as the bell-shaped Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular, and often fragrant flowers that resemble a bell, hence its common name. The plant produces dark green, heart-shaped leaves and has a distinctive, aromatic odor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The genus Aristolochia includes many species with similar morphological features and ecological roles.
