Asplenium bidentatum

Asplenium bidentatum, commonly known as the two-tooth fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features a short, creeping rhizome with erect, lance-shaped fronds that are divided into pairs of serrated pinnae. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, which helps it retain moisture in humid conditions.

Asplenium bidentatum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded garden settings.
