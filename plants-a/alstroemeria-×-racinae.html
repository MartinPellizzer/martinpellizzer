Alstroemeria × racinae

Alstroemeria × racinae, commonly known as the hybrid alstroemeria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors including red, orange, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with strap-like leaves that are lanceolate in shape.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
