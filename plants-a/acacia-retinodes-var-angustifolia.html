Acacia retinodes var. angustifolia

Acacia retinodes var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Australia.

It features slender, angular stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, linear leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that bloom in late winter to early spring. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with a deep root system and waxy leaf coatings to reduce water loss.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in native gardens.
