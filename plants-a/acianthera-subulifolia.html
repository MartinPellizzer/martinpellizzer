Acianthera subulifolia

Acianthera subulifolia, commonly known as the needleleaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale pink. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without being parasitic. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its unique appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
