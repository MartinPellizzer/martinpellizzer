Artemisia allionii

Artemisia allionii, commonly known as Allion's wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has silvery-gray leaves with a strong, camphor-like scent. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, producing small yellow flowers that attract pollinators.

Artemisia allionii is often used in traditional herbal medicine and is valued for its medicinal and aromatic properties.
