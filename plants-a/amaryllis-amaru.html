Amaryllis amaru

Amaryllis amaru, commonly known as the Andean amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces a tall, erect stem with broad, strap-like leaves that are often dark green in color. Amaryllis amaru is typically grown as a decorative plant, prized for its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.
