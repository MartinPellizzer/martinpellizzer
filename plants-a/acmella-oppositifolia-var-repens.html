Acmella oppositifolia var. repens

Acmella oppositifolia var. repens, commonly known as the creeping shotcup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features a sprawling growth habit with prostrate stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers with a distinctive orange center, which bloom in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This species is often used in gardens for its ability to form dense mats and its vibrant floral display.
