Astragalus chubutensis

Astragalus chubutensis, commonly known as the Chubut milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often found in steppes and shrublands.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is part of the local flora in its native habitat.
