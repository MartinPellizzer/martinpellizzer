Ammannia attenuata f. perattenuata

Ammannia attenuata f. perattenuata, commonly known as the narrowleaf ammannia, is a variant of the Ammannia genus native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its foliage is often a vibrant red or purple, adding ornamental value to wetland landscapes.

The species is notable for its adaptability to waterlogged conditions and its role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.
