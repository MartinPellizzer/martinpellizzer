Alopecurus aequalis f. natans

Alopecurus aequalis f. natans, commonly known as floating foxtail, is a variant of the annual grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its unique growth habit, where the stems and leaves float on the surface of water, often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is an aquatic or semi-aquatic plant that thrives in moist environments such as ponds, ditches, and wet meadows.

Due to its adaptability, it can sometimes become invasive in non-native regions.
