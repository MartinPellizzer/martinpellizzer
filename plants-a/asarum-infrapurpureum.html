Asarum infrapurpureum

Asarum infrapurpureum, commonly known as purple-veined wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with prominent purple veins, and it produces small, reddish-brown flowers in early spring. The plant is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, organic soil. Its aromatic rhizomes and leaves have been used in traditional herbal medicine.

Asarum infrapurpureum is a valuable component of forest ecosystems and is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
