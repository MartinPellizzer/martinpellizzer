Acaciella houghii

Acaciella houghii, commonly known as the Hough's acaciella, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing abilities through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant has small, bipinnate leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Acaciella houghii is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
