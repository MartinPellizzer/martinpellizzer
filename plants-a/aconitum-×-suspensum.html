Aconitum × suspensum

Aconitum × suspensum, commonly known as the hanging monkshood, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple spurred flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features a tall, erect stem with palmately compound leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aconitum × suspensum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is often used in shaded borders or woodland gardens.
