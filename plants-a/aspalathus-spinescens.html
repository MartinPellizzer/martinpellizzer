Aspalathus spinescens

Aspalathus spinescens, commonly known as the African green tea or rooibos, is a shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. Its most notable feature is the unique, caffeine-free leaves used to make a popular herbal tea.

Aspalathus spinescens thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.
