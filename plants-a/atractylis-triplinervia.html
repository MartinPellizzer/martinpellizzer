Atractylis triplinervia

Atractylis triplinervia, commonly known as the three-veined gingergrass, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust rhizomatous root system and produces erect, hairy stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves with three prominent veins, giving it its scientific name. Its flowering heads are clustered in panicles and consist of small, yellowish-green florets.

Atractylis triplinervia is often found in dry, sunny habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
