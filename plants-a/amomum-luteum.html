Amomum luteum

Amomum luteum, commonly known as the yellow cardamom, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, three-lobed fruits that are rich in essential oils, contributing to its medicinal and culinary uses. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Amomum luteum is often cultivated for its aromatic rhizomes, which are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.
