Antirrhinum genistifolium

Antirrhinum genistifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to gardens while also supporting local pollinators.
