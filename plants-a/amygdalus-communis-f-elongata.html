Amygdalus communis f. elongata

Amygdalus communis f. elongata, commonly known as the elongated almond.

This botanical variant is a cultivar of the almond tree, characterized by its elongated fruiting structures. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with dense foliage, making it suitable for ornamental purposes. Its flowers are typically white to pale pink, appearing in early spring.

The fruit, while not as widely cultivated for nut production as the standard almond, is valued for its unique shape and aesthetic appeal.
