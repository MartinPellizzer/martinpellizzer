Athyrium sessilipinnum

Athyrium sessilipinnum, commonly known as the sessile pinna fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce numerous small, clustered spore-bearing structures.

The botanical features of Athyrium sessilipinnum make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and conservation efforts.
