Alopecurus latifolius

Alopecurus latifolius, commonly known as broadleaf mouse-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist soils, such as meadows, grasslands, and stream banks, and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes. Its root system is fibrous, and it is often found in disturbed or nutrient-rich soils.

Alopecurus latifolius is also known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.
