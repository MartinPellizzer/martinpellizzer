Aegilops triuncialis subsp. typica

Aegilops triuncialis subsp.

typica, commonly known as the slender wild wheat, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and spikelets that bear small, awned grains.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as fields and disturbed soils. It is a diploid species with a genome size of 2n = 14.

This subspecies is of interest in plant genetics due to its potential for use in wheat improvement programs.
