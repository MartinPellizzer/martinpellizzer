Alchemilla vulgaris subsp. obtusata

Alchemilla vulgaris subsp.

obtusata, commonly known as broad-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, velvety, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers on slender stems during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its foliage is known for its ability to retain moisture, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
