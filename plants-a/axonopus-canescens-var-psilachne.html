Axonopus canescens var. psilachne

Axonopus canescens var. psilachne, commonly known as the narrow-leaf axonopus, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikelets, which are typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense ground cover in suitable conditions.
