Acacia bidentata

Acacia bidentata, commonly known as the two-toothed acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and bipinnate leaves composed of small, oval leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Acacia bidentata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry regions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in some indigenous communities.
