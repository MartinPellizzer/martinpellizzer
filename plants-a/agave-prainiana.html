Agave prainiana

Agave prainiana, commonly known as the Prain's agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color with white or grayish markings. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that bears clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil.

Agave prainiana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and resilience.
