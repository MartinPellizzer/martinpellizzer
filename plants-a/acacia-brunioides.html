Acacia brunioides

Acacia brunioides, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and phyllodes that are typically pale green to yellowish in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. Acacia brunioides is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
