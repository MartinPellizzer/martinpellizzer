Astrocarpa clusii var. spathulifolia

Astrocarpa clusii var. spathulifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed astrocarpa, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in a cluster that resembles a crown, giving it its common name.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
