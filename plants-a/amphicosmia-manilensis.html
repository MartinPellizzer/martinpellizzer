Amphicosmia manilensis

Amphicosmia manilensis, commonly known as the Manila orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which features a distinctive, elongated structure with small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

The orchid plays an important role in the local ecosystem, supporting various pollinators and contributing to biodiversity.
