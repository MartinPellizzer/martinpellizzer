Alpinia cornu-cervi

Alpinia cornu-cervi, commonly known as the horned ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stem and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in an inflorescence that resembles a spike.

Alpinia cornu-cervi is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
