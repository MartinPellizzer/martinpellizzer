Astragalus breviscapus

Astragalus breviscapus, commonly known as the short-horned milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in dry conditions.
