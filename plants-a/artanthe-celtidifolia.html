Artanthe celtidifolia

Artanthe celtidifolia, commonly known as the mountain flax, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rocky or woodland gardens.
