Amaryllis × sexmaculata

Amaryllis × sexmaculata, commonly known as the spotted amaryllis, is a hybrid plant that results from the cross between Amaryllis bellodona and Amaryllis sanderiana.

It is characterized by its striking, spotted leaves that display a mix of green and white markings. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with a contrasting throat. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

Amaryllis × sexmaculata thrives in well-draining soil and prefers bright, indirect light.
