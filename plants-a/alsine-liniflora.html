Alsine liniflora

Alsine liniflora, commonly known as slender alsike, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and fields. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, alternate leaves that are usually hairy. Its flowers are pale blue to pink and are arranged in loose clusters, blooming from late spring to early summer.

Alsine liniflora is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
