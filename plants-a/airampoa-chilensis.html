Airampoa chilensis

Airampoa chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean grass or *Poa chilensis*, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and along coastal areas.

Airampoa chilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in marginal environments.
