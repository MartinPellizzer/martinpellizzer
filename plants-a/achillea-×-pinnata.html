Achillea × pinnata

Achillea × pinnata, commonly known as the yarrow hybrid, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its feathery, finely divided leaves and clusters of small, bright yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. It is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value and historical medicinal uses.

The species is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
