Anacamptis pyramidalis subsp. serotina

Anacamptis pyramidalis subsp.

serotina, commonly known as the late-flowering pyramidal orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of violet or purple flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its later blooming period compared to other members of the species, typically flowering from late summer to early autumn. It thrives in sunny, well-drained habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, spurred orchid flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
