Aframomum cereum

Aframomum cereum, commonly known as black cardamom, is a tropical plant native to the forests of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale green flowers that develop into dark, aromatic fruits. The seeds inside the fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, known for their intense, smoky flavor.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal properties.
