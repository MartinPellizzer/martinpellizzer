Acalypha protracta

Acalypha protracta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf iron plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are typically borne in terminal or axillary inflorescences. Its growth habit is usually shrub-like, with multiple stems arising from the base.

Acalypha protracta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
