Arctium carduelis

Arctium carduelis, commonly known as the lesser burdock, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small umbels. The plant features tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, and it produces burrs with hooked bracts that aid in seed dispersal.

Arctium carduelis is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.
