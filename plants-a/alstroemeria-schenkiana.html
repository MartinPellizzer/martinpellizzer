Alstroemeria schenkiana

Alstroemeria schenkiana, commonly known as the Schenk's lily, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a range of colors including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, producing clusters of blooms along its stems. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Alstroemeria schenkiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant and long-lasting flowers.
