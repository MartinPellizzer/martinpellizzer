Apium chilense

Apium chilense, commonly known as the Chilean celery, is a herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like parsley and carrots. The plant features hollow, ridged stems and finely divided, feather-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, typical of the family, and it produces small, dry fruits.

Apium chilense is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in folk remedies for various health conditions.
