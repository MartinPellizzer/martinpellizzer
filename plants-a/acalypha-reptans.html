Acalypha reptans

Acalypha reptans, commonly known as creeping touch-me-not, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to tropical regions.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are often surrounded by bracts that can be brightly colored. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Acalypha reptans is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to spread rapidly through stolons.
