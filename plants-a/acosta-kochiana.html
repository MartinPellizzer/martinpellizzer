Acosta kochiana

Acosta kochiana, commonly known as the Mexican rock cactus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stem and spiny appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers, which are usually yellow or orange, bloom in the spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Acosta kochiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil.
