Agropyron tenerum subsp. trichocoleum

Agropyron tenerum subsp.

trichocoleum, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its fine, soft, and silky-haired leaves, which give it its common name.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold and arid environments. Its flowering stems are usually short and often produce small, spike-like inflorescences.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
