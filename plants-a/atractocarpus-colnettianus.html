Atractocarpus colnettianus

Atractocarpus colnettianus, commonly known as the Colnett's tallowwood, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of northern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey to brown bark and can grow up to 30 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces woody fruits containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its durable timber, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
