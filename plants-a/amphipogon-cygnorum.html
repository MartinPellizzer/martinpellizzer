Amphipogon cygnorum

Amphipogon cygnorum, commonly known as the swan's bill, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

Amphipogon cygnorum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.
