Anthericum graminifolium

Anthericum graminifolium, commonly known as the grass-leaved arum, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. The plant has long, narrow, grass-like leaves that give it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects.

Anthericum graminifolium thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
