Asarca secunda

Asarca secunda, commonly known as the second asarum, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Aristolochiaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, intricate flowers that resemble a pipe or a horn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually greenish or reddish in color and produce a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor to attract pollinators.

Asarca secunda is known for its medicinal properties, though it contains toxic compounds that require careful handling.
