Angelica sylvestris var. nidus

Angelica sylvestris var. nidus, commonly known as the clustered angelica, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescences and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and white or greenish, arranged in compound umbels, and it produces dark purple or black berries.

The roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
