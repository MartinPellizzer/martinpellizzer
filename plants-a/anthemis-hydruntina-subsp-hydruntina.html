Anthemis hydruntina subsp. hydruntina

Anthemis hydruntina subsp.

hydruntina, commonly known as the Hydruntine Anthemis, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. Its flowers are yellow with a central brownish disc, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
