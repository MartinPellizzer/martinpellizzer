Aquilegia caucasica

Aquilegia caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features striking, spurred flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with contrasting yellow or orange markings. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed, contributing to its ornamental value. It typically grows in woodland or rocky habitats and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Aquilegia caucasica is valued in horticulture for its beauty and adaptability to various garden conditions.
