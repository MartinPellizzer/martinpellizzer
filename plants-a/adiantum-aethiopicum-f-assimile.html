Adiantum aethiopicum f. assimile

Adiantum aethiopicum f. assimile, commonly known as the African Maidenhair Fern, is a fern species native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble the shape of a feather. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires high humidity to flourish. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense, silky coating that helps retain moisture.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
