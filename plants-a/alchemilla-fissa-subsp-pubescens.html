Alchemilla fissa subsp. pubescens

Alchemilla fissa subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the hairy lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in loose clusters atop slender stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
