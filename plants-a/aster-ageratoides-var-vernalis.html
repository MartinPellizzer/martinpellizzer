Aster ageratoides var. vernalis

Aster ageratoides var. vernalis, commonly known as spring blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky areas. It blooms in the spring and is often found in the central and eastern United States.

Its small, composite flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
