Alchemilla jaquetiana

Alchemilla jaquetiana, commonly known as the alpine lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Alchemilla jaquetiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and alpine displays.
