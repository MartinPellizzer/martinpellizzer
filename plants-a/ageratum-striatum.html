Ageratum striatum

Ageratum striatum, commonly known as the striped ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to purple and pink. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. Ageratum striatum thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

It is also known for its ability to attract butterflies and other pollinators.
