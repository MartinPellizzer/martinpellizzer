Azalea macrosepala

Azalea macrosepala, commonly known as the large-sepal azalea, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a distinctive red or yellow throat. The plant produces showy, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Azalea macrosepala is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral displays.
