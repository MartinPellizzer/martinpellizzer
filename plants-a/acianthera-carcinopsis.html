Acianthera carcinopsis

Acianthera carcinopsis, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit, often found on trees or rocks in humid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and purple, typically blooming during the warmer months. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, and it thrives in shaded, moist conditions with good air circulation.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
