Anchusa undulata proles granatensis

Anchusa undulata proles granatensis, commonly known as the Granada waveblue, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
