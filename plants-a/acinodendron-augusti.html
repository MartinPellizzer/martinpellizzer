Acinodendron augusti

Acinodendron augusti, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Acinodendron augusti is often found in woodland and savanna habitats, where it plays a role in soil enrichment through nitrogen fixation.

Its seeds are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines in some African communities.
