Alchemilla hyrcana

Alchemilla hyrcana, commonly known as the Hyrcanian lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a dense cluster, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Alchemilla hyrcana is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
