Aster monactis

Aster monactis, commonly known as the single-rayed aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a single central ray floret surrounded by a cluster of disk florets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Aster monactis blooms in late summer to early fall, producing vibrant purple or white flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
