Aristolochia siamensis

Aristolochia siamensis, commonly known as the Siam Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular, and often fragrant flowers that resemble a pipe, with a deep purple to brown coloration. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly velvety texture. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The genus Aristolochia is known for its complex chemical compounds, some of which have been studied for potential medicinal uses.
