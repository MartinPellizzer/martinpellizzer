Arundinella hirta f. shotokuensis

Arundinella hirta f. shotokuensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Shotoku bamboo, is a variant of the hardhack genus Arundinella.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia, particularly Japan. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and as ornamental plants. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
