Acalypha echinata

Acalypha echinata, commonly known as the spiked iron plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, spiny bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are usually ovate or elliptical with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Acalypha echinata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant, long-lasting bracts.
