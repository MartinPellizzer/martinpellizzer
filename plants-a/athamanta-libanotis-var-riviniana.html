Athamanta libanotis var. riviniana

Athamanta libanotis var. riviniana, commonly known as the Lebanon bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
