Aster leucanthemus

Aster leucanthemus, commonly known as white wood aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features a basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale violet flowers in the late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is often found in open woods, meadows, and along roadsides.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
