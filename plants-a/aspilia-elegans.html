Aspilia elegans

Aspilia elegans, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale yellow flowers that resemble daisies, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in a basal rosette.

Aspilia elegans thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
