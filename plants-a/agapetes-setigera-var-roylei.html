Agapetes setigera var. roylei

Agapetes setigera var. roylei, commonly known as the Royle's silver fir, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are covered in a silvery-white wax. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by cone-like structures containing edible seeds. It thrives in cool, moist climates at high altitudes, often found in mountainous regions of India and Nepal.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
