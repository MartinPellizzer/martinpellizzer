Astragalus franchetianus

Astragalus franchetianus, commonly known as the Franchet's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine meadows.

Astragalus franchetianus plays a role in soil stabilization and is part of the larger Astragalus genus, which includes many species with medicinal and ecological significance.
