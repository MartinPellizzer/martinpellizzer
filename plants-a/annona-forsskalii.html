Annona forsskalii

Annona forsskalii, commonly known as the African horned apple, is a tropical shrub native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a large, fleshy fruit with a thick, spiky rind and a sweet, acidic pulp. The fruit is edible, though the seeds are typically inedible and contain toxic compounds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food in local communities.
