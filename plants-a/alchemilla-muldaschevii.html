Alchemilla muldaschevii

Alchemilla muldaschevii, commonly known as the Moldavian lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Alchemilla muldaschevii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
