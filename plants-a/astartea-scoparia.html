Astartea scoparia

Astartea scoparia, commonly known as the narrowleaf astera, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads made up of many small florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers that are usually white or pale purple.

Astartea scoparia is often found in wet or damp environments and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
