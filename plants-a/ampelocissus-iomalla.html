Ampelocissus iomalla

Ampelocissus iomalla, commonly known as the Iomalla cat's claw, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is a woody vine that produces small, yellowish-white flowers and red berries. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and the presence of tendrils that help it attach to supports. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

The berries are edible and often used in local cuisines.
