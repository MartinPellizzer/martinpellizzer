Albizia attopeuensis var. laui

Albizia attopeuensis var. laui, commonly known as the Laui Albizia, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in Laos and Vietnam.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Its root system has the ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable for soil enrichment in agroforestry systems.
