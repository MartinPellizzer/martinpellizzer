Adenostemma rufescens

Adenostemma rufescens, commonly known as redstem adenostemma, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its erect, often hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, with two-lipped corollas typical of the Acanthaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Adenostemma rufescens is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
