Alchemilla iratsyensis

Alchemilla iratsyensis, commonly known as the Madagascar lady's mantle, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, typically clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
