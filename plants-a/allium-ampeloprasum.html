Allium ampeloprasum

Allium ampeloprasum, commonly known as the purple onion or wild leek, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and broad, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet or purple flowers that bloom in the summer. Its bulb is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor, often used in culinary applications.

Allium ampeloprasum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
