Antirrhinum majus f. cirrhigerum

Antirrhinum majus f. cirrhigerum, commonly known as the curly-stemmed snapdragon, is a cultivar of the common snapdragon.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often curled flower stems that add a unique ornamental feature. The plant typically produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

This variety is valued for its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms throughout the growing season.
