Arenaria retusa subsp. retusa

Arenaria retusa subsp.

retusa, commonly known as the smooth sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions. It typically forms dense mats of succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its botanical features make it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
