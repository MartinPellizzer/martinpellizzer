Aerangis coursiana

Aerangis coursiana, commonly known as the Coursian's orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with a distinctive lip that attracts pollinators.

Aerangis coursiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
