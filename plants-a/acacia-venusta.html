Acacia venusta

Acacia venusta, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and bipinnate leaves. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. Acacia venusta thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of Australian national identity.
