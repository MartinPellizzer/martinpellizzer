Averrhoa sinica

Averrhoa sinica, commonly known as the Java apple or red carambola, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, with simple, ovate leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits with five pointed lobes, which are rich in vitamin C and antioxidants. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a woody stem, making it well adapted to humid, tropical climates.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal fruit production.
