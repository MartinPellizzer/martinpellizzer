Acinos arvensis var. perennans

Acinos arvensis var. perennans, commonly known as the perennial field everlasting, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fluffy yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a dense, mat-forming growth habit with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it a useful plant for erosion control.
