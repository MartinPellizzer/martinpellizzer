Achillea aspleniifolia var. hungaricum

Achillea aspleniifolia var. hungaricum, commonly known as the Hungarian yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical medicinal uses.

Its foliage and flowers contribute to its role in traditional herbal medicine and ecological garden settings.
