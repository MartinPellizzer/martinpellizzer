Athrixia multiceps

Athrixia multiceps, commonly known as the mountain bluet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, blue-green leaves and produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers. It is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to cool, moist environments.

Athrixia multiceps is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
