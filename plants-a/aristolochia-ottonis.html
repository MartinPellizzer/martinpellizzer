Aristolochia ottonis

Aristolochia ottonis, commonly known as the Mexican Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its unique, heart-shaped leaves and striking, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe, hence its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a strong, musky fragrance. Its fruits are elongated, woody capsules that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and distinctive floral structure.
