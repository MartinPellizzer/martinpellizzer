Acaena incisa

Acaena incisa, commonly known as the New Zealand burr bush, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, bramble-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, woody, spiky fruits known as achenes. Acaena incisa thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in New Zealand's native flora.
