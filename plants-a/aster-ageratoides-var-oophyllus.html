Aster ageratoides var. oophyllus

Aster ageratoides var. oophyllus, commonly known as the white mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and woodlands, and it thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its showy, white to pale blue flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
