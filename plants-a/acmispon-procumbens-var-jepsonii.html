Acmispon procumbens var. jepsonii

Acmispon procumbens var. jepsonii, commonly known as the Jepson's burroweed, is a low-growing annual plant native to California.

It features small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland habitats. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a species of concern in some areas.
