Aster iinumae

Aster iinumae, commonly known as the Japanese aster, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features showy purple or blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value has led to its popularity in horticulture, though it is less common in the wild.
