Acacia williamsonii

Acacia williamsonii, commonly known as the mountain acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are typically reduced to spines in mature plants. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are followed by elongated, flat pods containing seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Acacia williamsonii plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
