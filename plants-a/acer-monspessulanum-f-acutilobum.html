Acer monspessulanum f. acutilobum

Acer monspessulanum f. acutilobum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed maple, is a variant of the Spanish maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely serrated, lance-shaped leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden settings.

Its ornamental value is primarily due to its striking foliage, which makes it a popular choice for landscaping.
