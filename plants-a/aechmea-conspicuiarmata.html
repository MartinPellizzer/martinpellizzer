Aechmea conspicuiarmata

Aechmea conspicuiarmata, commonly known as the showy pineapple plant, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater for the plant's use. The leaves are typically bright green with red or purple margins, adding to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces an inflorescence with vibrant orange or red flowers that bloom in the center of the rosette.

Aechmea conspicuiarmata thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Morphology

Aechmea conspicuiarmata has rosette-shaped leaves that are sword-like, stiff, and covered with a dense layer of white trichomes, giving them a silvery appearance.

The leaves are arranged in a tight spiral around the central stem and can grow up to 30 cm in length. The stem is short and often hidden beneath the foliage, with a fibrous, reddish-brown bark that becomes woody with age. The plant produces tall, spike-like inflorescences bearing bright red or orange flowers with prominent, showy bracts, which bloom during the dry season and are pollinated by birds, particularly hummingbirds.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds, which are dispersed by wind and water.
