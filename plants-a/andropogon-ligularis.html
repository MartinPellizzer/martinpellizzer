Andropogon ligularis

Andropogon ligularis, commonly known as inland needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. It produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers.

Andropogon ligularis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
