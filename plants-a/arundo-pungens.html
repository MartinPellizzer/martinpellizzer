Arundo pungens

Arundo pungens, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to southern Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its stiff, gray-green leaves and tall, slender flowering stalks that produce small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist environments due to its preference for waterlogged soils. It has a robust root system that can spread aggressively, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its rapid growth and adaptability contribute to its ecological impact and ornamental use in landscaping.
