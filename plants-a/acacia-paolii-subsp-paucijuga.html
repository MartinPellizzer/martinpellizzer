Acacia paolii subsp. paucijuga

Acacia paolii subsp.

paucijuga, commonly known as the Enderby Island acacia, is a shrub native to the Enderby Islands in the southern Indian Ocean. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, compound leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in coastal environments and is adapted to saline and well-drained soils.

Due to its restricted distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
