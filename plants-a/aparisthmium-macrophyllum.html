Aparisthmium macrophyllum

Aparisthmium macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaved water plantain, is a perennial aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often floating or submerged, depending on water levels. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the soil in aquatic environments.

Aparisthmium macrophyllum plays an important role in water filtration and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
