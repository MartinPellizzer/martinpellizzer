Avicennia schaueriana f. glabrescens

Avicennia schaueriana f. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth mangrove, is a variant of the mangrove species Avicennia schaueriana.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless leaves, which distinguish it from other forms of the species. This plant thrives in brackish water environments, typically found in coastal areas where freshwater and saltwater meet. Its root system includes pneumatophores, which help in oxygen absorption in waterlogged soils.

The smooth mangrove plays a vital role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
