Astragalus micranthellus

Astragalus micranthellus, commonly known as the small-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus micranthellus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
