Alyxia concatenata

Alyxia concatenata, commonly known as the clustered alyxia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a woody vine that typically grows along forest edges and in moist, shaded areas. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Alyxia concatenata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
