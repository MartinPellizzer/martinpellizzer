Arctostaphylos uva-ursi var. crassifolia

Arctostaphylos uva-ursi var. crassifolia, commonly known as bearberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to North America.

It features small, leathery leaves that are typically evergreen and have a distinctive red or orange coloration in the fall. The plant produces tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pink, which develop into red berries in the summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or alpine environments.

This species is known for its aromatic foliage and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
