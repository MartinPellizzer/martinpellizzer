Anthemis cretica subsp. anatolica

Anthemis cretica subsp.

anatolica, commonly known as Anatolian tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, including parts of Turkey and Greece. It is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish-green leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
