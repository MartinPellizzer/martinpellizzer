Astragalus kessleri

Astragalus kessleri, commonly known as Kessler's milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus kessleri is also known for its medicinal properties, with some species in the genus used in traditional herbal remedies.
