Astragalus pomonensis

Astragalus pomonensis, commonly known as the appleleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soils.

Astragalus pomonensis is also noted for its ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators and wildlife.
