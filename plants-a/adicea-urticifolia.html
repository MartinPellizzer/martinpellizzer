Adicea urticifolia

Adicea urticifolia, commonly known as the stinging nettle plant, is a flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is characterized by its hollow, hairless stems and serrated leaves that often have stinging hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It is typically found in moist, disturbed habitats such as fields and along stream banks.

Adicea urticifolia is known for its defensive mechanisms, including the release of irritant chemicals from its leaves.
