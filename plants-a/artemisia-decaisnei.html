Artemisia decaisnei

Artemisia decaisnei, commonly known as the Decaisne's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats. Its leaves contain volatile oils, giving it a strong, bitter scent and making it a valued species in traditional medicine.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental qualities, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
