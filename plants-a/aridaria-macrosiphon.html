Aridaria macrosiphon

Aridaria macrosiphon, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

Aridaria macrosiphon is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
