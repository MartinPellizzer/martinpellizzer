Agapetes smithiana var. major

Agapetes smithiana var. major, commonly known as the giant silver fir, is a rare and endemic species native to the high-altitude forests of the Andes in South America.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of the conifer family. Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptation to high-altitude environments and its role in the local ecosystem.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
