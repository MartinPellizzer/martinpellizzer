Anemone coronaria proles albiflora

Anemone coronaria proles albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped white petals that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces a cluster of small, dark brown seeds in a fluffy, hairy capsule. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
