Allium paniculatum var. salinum

Allium paniculatum var. salinum, commonly known as the salt marsh onion, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of the United States.

It thrives in saline and brackish wetlands, showcasing its adaptability to harsh, coastal environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in late summer. Its bulbous stem is often covered in a layer of salt-tolerant skin, aiding in its survival in salty soils.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
