Atriplex tampicensis

Atriplex tampicensis, commonly known as desert saltbush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.

Atriplex tampicensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
