Asplenium borbonicum

Asplenium borbonicum, commonly known as the Bourbon spleenwort, is a species of fern native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its small, dark green, strap-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often on rocky substrates or tree trunks. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium borbonicum is notable for its unique morphology and ecological adaptation to its native habitat.
