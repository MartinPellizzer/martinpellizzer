Albizia austrobrasilica

Albizia austrobrasilica, commonly known as the Brazilian siris, is a leguminous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils.

Albizia austrobrasilica is also valued for its wood and ornamental qualities.
