Actinodaphne malabarica

Actinodaphne malabarica, commonly known as Malabar camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed. It is valued for its essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of camphor.

The tree thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest understories.
