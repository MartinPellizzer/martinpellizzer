Astragalus iodanthus var. vipereus

Astragalus iodanthus var. vipereus, commonly known as viper's bugloss, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Viper's bugloss is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
