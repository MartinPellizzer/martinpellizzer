Artemisia capillaris f. kohatica

Artemisia capillaris f. kohatica, commonly known as Korean Artemisia, is a variant of the common wormwood plant.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. This subspecies is native to East Asia, particularly Korea, and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in traditional medicine and horticulture for its ornamental value.

The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with fine, feathery leaves that release a strong, camphor-like scent when crushed.
