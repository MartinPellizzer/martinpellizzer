Astragalus sareptanus

Astragalus sareptanus, commonly known as the Sareptan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pink pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
