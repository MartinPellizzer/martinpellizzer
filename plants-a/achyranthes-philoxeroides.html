Achyranthes philoxeroides

Achyranthes philoxeroides, commonly known as the giant knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features a robust root system and erect, branched stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered an invasive species in some regions.
