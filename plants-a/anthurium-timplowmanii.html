Anthurium timplowmanii

Anthurium timplowmanii, commonly known as the Timplowman's arrowhead, is a rare and striking member of the Araceae family.

Native to the humid lowland forests of Ecuador, it thrives in shaded, moist environments. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a vibrant green color and deep veins. Its spadix, surrounded by a colorful spathe, is a defining feature of its floral structure.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is highly sought after by plant enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
