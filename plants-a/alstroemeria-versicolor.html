Alstroemeria versicolor

Alstroemeria versicolor, commonly known as the Peruvian lily, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its colorful, tubular flowers that come in a variety of hues. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Alstroemeria versicolor is also valued for its ability to thrive in shady conditions, making it a popular choice for garden landscaping.
