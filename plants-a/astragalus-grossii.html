Astragalus grossii

Astragalus grossii, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds.

Astragalus grossii is adapted to harsh, dry environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in desert ecosystems.
