Aristida teretifolia

Aristida teretifolia, commonly known as slender wiregrass, is a grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well adapted to harsh environments. Its root system helps prevent soil erosion, and it plays a role in stabilizing desert landscapes.

The species is often used in restoration projects due to its hardiness and ecological benefits.
