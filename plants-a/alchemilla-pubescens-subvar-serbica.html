Alchemilla pubescens subvar. serbica

Alchemilla pubescens subvar. serbica, commonly known as the Serbian lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features a rosette of softly hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne on slender, upright stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
