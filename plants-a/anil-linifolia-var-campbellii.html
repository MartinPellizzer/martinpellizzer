Anil linifolia var. campbellii

Anil linifolia var. campbellii, commonly known as the Campbell's anil, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often found in coastal regions. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are linear and somewhat succulent, adapted to withstand arid conditions.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
