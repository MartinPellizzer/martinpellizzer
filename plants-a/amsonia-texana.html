Amsonia texana

Amsonia texana, commonly known as bluestem panicum, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy blue to lavender flower clusters. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its foliage turns a striking coppery color in the fall, adding seasonal interest.

Amsonia texana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
