Astragalus longiramosus

Astragalus longiramosus, commonly known as the long-stemmed milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, compound leaves that are adapted to arid conditions. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus longiramosus is also known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
