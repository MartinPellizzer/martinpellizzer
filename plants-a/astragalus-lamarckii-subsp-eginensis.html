Astragalus lamarckii subsp. eginensis

Astragalus lamarckii subsp.

eginensis, commonly known as the Eginian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It produces pods that contain small, hard seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

This subspecies is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in soil stabilization and potential medicinal properties.
