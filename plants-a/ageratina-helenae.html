Ageratina helenae

Ageratina helenae, commonly known as Helen's blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, fluffy blue or white flowers that resemble a mist, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Ageratina helenae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
