Argythamnia polygama

Argythamnia polygama, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces yellow or white ray flowers surrounded by tubular disc flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It has adapted to harsh environments with its deep root system and water-storing tissues.

Argythamnia polygama is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
