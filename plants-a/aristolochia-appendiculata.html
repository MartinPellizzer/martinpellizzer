Aristolochia appendiculata

Aristolochia appendiculata, commonly known as the Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in moist, wooded areas. It belongs to the family Aristolochiaceae and is known for its complex floral structure that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its medicinal properties and toxicity, it is also of interest in botanical and pharmacological studies.
