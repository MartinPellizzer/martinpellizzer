Anthistiria scandens

Anthistiria scandens, commonly known as the climbing thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in open woodlands and along roadsides. The plant features a woody stem that climbs by means of hooked prickles, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces prickly, spiny fruits that contain seeds.

Anthistiria scandens is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
