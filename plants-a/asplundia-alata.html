Asplundia alata

Asplundia alata, commonly known as the winged arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in an inflorescence called a spadix, which is enclosed by a spathe. Asplundia alata is often grown for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
