Acacallis rosariana

Acacallis rosariana, commonly known as the Rosary Flower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family (Amaryllidaceae) and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its beauty and resilience.
