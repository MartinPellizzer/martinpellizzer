Aster listriformis

Aster listriformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, clustered blue to purple flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy, composite flower heads.

Aster listriformis plays a role in supporting pollinators and is often used in native plant gardens.
