Acacia paniculata var. martii

Acacia paniculata var. martii, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that appear in dense, branched clusters. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Acacia paniculata var. martii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional medicine.
