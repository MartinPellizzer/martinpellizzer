Anemone tagawae

Anemone tagawae, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Anemone tagawae is also noted for its ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense colonies in suitable habitats.
