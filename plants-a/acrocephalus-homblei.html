Acrocephalus homblei

Acrocephalus homblei, commonly known as the Hombli's sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers during the summer months. Its reproductive structures are adapted for wind pollination, a common trait among sedges.

Acrocephalus homblei plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various small organisms.
