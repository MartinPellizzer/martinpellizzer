Ambrosia pitcheri

Ambrosia pitcheri, commonly known as the plains ambrosia, is a flowering plant native to the central and northern Great Plains of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small yellow daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Ambrosia pitcheri is also known for its allergenic pollen, which can contribute to seasonal allergies in humans.
