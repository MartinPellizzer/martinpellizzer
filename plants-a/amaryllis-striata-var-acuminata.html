Amaryllis striata var. acuminata

Amaryllis striata var. acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This variety is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
