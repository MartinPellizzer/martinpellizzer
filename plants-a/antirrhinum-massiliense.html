Antirrhinum massiliense

Antirrhinum massiliense, commonly known as the Massilian snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Antirrhinum massiliense is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
