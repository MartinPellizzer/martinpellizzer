Andromeda tetragona

Andromeda tetragona, commonly known as the four-cornered andromeda, is a flowering plant native to parts of eastern Asia, including China and Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant features small, white, four-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Andromeda tetragona is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
