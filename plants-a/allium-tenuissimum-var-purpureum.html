Allium tenuissimum var. purpureum

Allium tenuissimum var. purpureum, commonly known as purple garlic or purple chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green with a slight purple tinge. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in a loose umbel, which bloom in early summer. Its bulbs are small and often used as a culinary herb, similar to regular garlic or chives.

This variety is valued for its aromatic foliage and ornamental appeal in gardens.
