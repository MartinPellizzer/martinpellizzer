Astragalus bicolor subsp. owerinii

Astragalus bicolor subsp.

owerinii, commonly known as the pink milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pink to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments. Astragalus bicolor subsp.

owerinii is adapted to arid conditions and is considered a rare and endemic species in its range.
