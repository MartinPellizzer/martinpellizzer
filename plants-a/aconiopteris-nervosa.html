Aconiopteris nervosa

Aconiopteris nervosa, commonly known as the veined fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that are divided into numerous pinnae. The plant has a unique vascular system that allows it to grow tall while maintaining structural integrity. Its fronds are often used in traditional medicine and horticulture due to their ornamental value.

Aconiopteris nervosa thrives in humid, shaded environments and plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native habitats.
