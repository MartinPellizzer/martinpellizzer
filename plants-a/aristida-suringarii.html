Aristida suringarii

Aristida suringarii, commonly known as the Suringar grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, bract-like structures.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its fine texture and drought tolerance.
