Arabis hirsuta var. nordlandica

Arabis hirsuta var. nordlandica, commonly known as the northern hairy rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments. The plant features small, white, cruciform flowers that bloom in early spring, and its hairy leaves are adapted to withstand cold and harsh weather conditions. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other members such as cabbage and mustard.

This variety is notable for its resilience and is often used in ecological restoration projects in its native habitats.
