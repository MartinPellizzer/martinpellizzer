Agrostis hoffmannii

Agrostis hoffmannii, commonly known as Hoffmann's bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based flowers during the summer months. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Agrostis hoffmannii is also known for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in natural habitats.
