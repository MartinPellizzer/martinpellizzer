Acacia hookeri

Acacia hookeri, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact globular heads, which are followed by elongated, woody pods. Acacia hookeri is adapted to coastal environments and can tolerate salt spray and sandy soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
