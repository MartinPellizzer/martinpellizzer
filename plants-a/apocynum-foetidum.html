Apocynum foetidum

Apocynum foetidum, commonly known as stinkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub-like perennial that typically grows in wetland areas and along stream banks. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are clustered in umbels, producing a distinctive, unpleasant odor. Its milky sap contains toxic compounds, which can cause skin irritation.

The plant is also known for its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that attract various pollinators.
