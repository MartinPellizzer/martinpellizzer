Atriplex malvana

Atriplex malvana, commonly known as Malvan saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, succulent leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to thrive in saline and nutrient-poor soils, making it a hardy species in challenging conditions.

Atriplex malvana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various insects and animals.
