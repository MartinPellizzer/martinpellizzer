Andryala tenuifolia var. ficalhoana

Andryala tenuifolia var. ficalhoana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved andryala, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline, where it is adapted to salt spray and strong winds. Its root system is fibrous and helps stabilize dunes, making it an important species in coastal ecosystems.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
