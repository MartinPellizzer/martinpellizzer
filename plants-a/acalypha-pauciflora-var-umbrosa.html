Acalypha pauciflora var. umbrosa

Acalypha pauciflora var. umbrosa, commonly known as the shadow acalypha, is a shade-tolerant shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features palmately lobed leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink, adding visual interest to shaded gardens. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by ornamental, winged bracts that persist for weeks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it ideal for woodland or container gardens.

Its unique foliage and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping in tropical and subtropical climates.
