Aegokeras caespitosa

Aegokeras caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted grass or false brome, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in grassy meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, forming dense, clump-like growths. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.

It is generally hardy and adaptable, thriving in a range of environmental conditions.

Morphology

Aegokeras caespitosa has leaves that are lance-shaped, arranged in a basal rosette, with a bluish-gray color and a waxy coating.

The stem is short and stout, with a rough, fibrous bark that is typically brown in color. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, featuring purple petals and yellow stamens, blooming in early spring and pollinated by wind.
