Aspilia leucoglossa

Aspilia leucoglossa, commonly known as the white-lipped aster, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Aspilia leucoglossa is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
