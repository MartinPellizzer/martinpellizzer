Artemisia kasakorum

Artemisia kasakorum, commonly known as the Karakorum wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Artemisia kasakorum is adapted to arid and cold climates, often thriving in rocky or well-drained soils.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
