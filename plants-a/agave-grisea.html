Agave grisea

Agave grisea, commonly known as the gray agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears small, white flowers, which are followed by the development of its characteristic blue-green berries. Agave grisea is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional practices by indigenous communities.
