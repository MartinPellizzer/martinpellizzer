Apactis japonica

Apactis japonica, commonly known as the Japanese featherflower, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, pink to purple flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Apactis japonica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its delicate, feathery appearance.
