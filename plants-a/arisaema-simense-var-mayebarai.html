Arisaema simense var. mayebarai

Arisaema simense var. mayebarai, commonly known as the Mayebarai arrowhead, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and palmate leaves with a waxy surface.

The flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, typical of the Araceae family.
