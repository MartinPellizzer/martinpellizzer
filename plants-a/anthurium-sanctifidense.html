Anthurium sanctifidense

Anthurium sanctifidense, commonly known as the sacred anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces spadices, which are inflorescences that consist of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Anthurium sanctifidense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
