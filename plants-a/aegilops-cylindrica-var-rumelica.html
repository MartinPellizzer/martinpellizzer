Aegilops cylindrica var. rumelica

Aegilops cylindrica var. rumelica, commonly known as the rye grass, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets and fine, blue-green leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as roadsides and fields. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and is often used as a forage crop.

Due to its hardy nature, it is adaptable to various soil types and climate conditions.
