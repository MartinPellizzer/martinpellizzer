Aconitum geniculatum var. longicalcaratum

Aconitum geniculatum var. longicalcaratum, commonly known as the long-beaked monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a spires, with distinctive hooded sepals that resemble a bishop's mitre. The plant has palmate, deeply lobed leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in woodland environments.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is not recommended for cultivation in areas accessible to children or pets.
