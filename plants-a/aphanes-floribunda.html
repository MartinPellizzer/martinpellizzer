Aphanes floribunda

Aphanes floribunda, commonly known as the common sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems that bear small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, round, fleshy fruits that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with a dense layer of fine pubescence.

Aphanes floribunda thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
