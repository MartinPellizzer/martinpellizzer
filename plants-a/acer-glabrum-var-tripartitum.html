Acer glabrum var. tripartitum

Acer glabrum var. tripartitum, commonly known as the tripartite maple, is a rare and distinctive variant of the sugar maple.

It is characterized by its uniquely divided leaves, which are typically trifoliate, consisting of three distinct leaflets. The plant is native to the northeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage displays vibrant autumn colors, making it a notable ornamental species.

Due to its rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
