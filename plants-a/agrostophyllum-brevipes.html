Agrostophyllum brevipes

Agrostophyllum brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and covered with fine, hair-like structures.

Agrostophyllum brevipes is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
