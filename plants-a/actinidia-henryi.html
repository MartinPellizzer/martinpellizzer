Actinidia henryi

Actinidia henryi, commonly known as Chinese mayapple, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Actinidiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Actinidia henryi is also a parent species in the development of the popular kiwifruit.
