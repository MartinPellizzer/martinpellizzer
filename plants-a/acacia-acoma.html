Acacia acoma

Acacia acoma, commonly known as the desert acacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and small, bipinnate leaves that reduce water loss. The plant produces yellowish or pale green flowers, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Its seeds are enclosed in pod-like fruits that split open when mature.

Acacia acoma is well adapted to harsh environments, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
