Agave impressa

Agave impressa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved agave, is a striking succulent native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features tall, spiky flower stems that can reach up to 10 feet in height, topped with clusters of yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, blue-green leaves with a waxy coating that helps it retain moisture in arid environments. It is well adapted to full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a standout in desert gardens.
