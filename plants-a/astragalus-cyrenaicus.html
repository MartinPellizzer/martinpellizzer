Astragalus cyrenaicus

Astragalus cyrenaicus, commonly known as the Cyrenaic milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Astragalus cyrenaicus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
