Amerimnon bariense

Amerimnon bariense, commonly known as the Bariense Cactus, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a cylindrical stem with prominent ridges and spines, adapted for water storage and protection from herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the dry season, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Amerimnon bariense thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping in drought-prone areas.
