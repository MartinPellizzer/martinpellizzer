Ammannia parviflora

Ammannia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered ammannia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils. The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red in hue. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Ammannia parviflora is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
