Alstroemeria flos-martini

Alstroemeria flos-martini, commonly known as the "Peruvian lily," is a member of the Alstroemeriaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Chile and Peru, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, yellow, and pink, often with contrasting dark veins. Its foliage consists of long, narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen.

Alstroemeria flos-martini is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
