Anthurium nigrescens

Anthurium nigrescens, commonly known as the black anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety foliage and long, spadix-like inflorescence that often appears in shades of red or purple. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique floral structures. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium nigrescens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying properties.
