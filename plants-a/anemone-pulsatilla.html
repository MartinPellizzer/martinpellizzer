Anemone pulsatilla

Anemone pulsatilla, commonly known as the pasque flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and produces a single, erect stem with basal leaves that are palmately divided. Its flowers bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge, and are pollinated by insects such as bees and butterflies.

Anemone pulsatilla is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
