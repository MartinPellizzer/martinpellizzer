Alchemilla walasii f. glabrior

Alchemilla walasii f. glabrior, commonly known as the smooth-leaved alchemilla, is a variant of the alchemilla genus native to Central Asia.

This plant is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in rocky or dry soils. Its leaves are typically smooth and glossy, with a silver-green hue, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer. The plant is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential healing properties.
