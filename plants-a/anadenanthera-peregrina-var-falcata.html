Anadenanthera peregrina var. falcata

Anadenanthera peregrina var. falcata, commonly known as the quebracho falcatus, is a leguminous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and distinctive, woody pods that contain seeds. The plant is known for its hard, durable wood, which is highly valued for its strength and resistance to decay. It thrives in dry, tropical climates and is often found in savannas and dry forests.

The tree also plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
