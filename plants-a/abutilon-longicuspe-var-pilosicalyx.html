Abutilon longicuspe var. pilosicalyx

Abutilon longicuspe var. pilosicalyx, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has a hairy stem and leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated primarily by insects.
