Aconitum grossedentatum var. sikokianum

Aconitum grossedentatum var. sikokianum, commonly known as the Sikokian monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Asia.

It features striking, hood-shaped blue to violet flowers that are typically borne on tall, sparsely branched stems. The plant has palmately compound leaves with deeply cut lobes, giving it a distinctive and elegant appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Like other members of the Ranunculaceae family, it contains toxic alkaloids, requiring caution during handling.
