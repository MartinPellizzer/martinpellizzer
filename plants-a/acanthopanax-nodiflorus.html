Acanthopanax nodiflorus

Acanthopanax nodiflorus, commonly known as the clustered five-lobed arrowhead, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with five lobes and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
