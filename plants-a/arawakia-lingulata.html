Arawakia lingulata

Arawakia lingulata, commonly known as the Caribbean starfruit, is a tropical plant native to the Greater Antilles.

It is characterized by its palm-like appearance, with a tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into the edible, star-shaped fruit. The fruit has a sweet, tangy flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

Due to its unique appearance and taste, it holds cultural significance in the regions where it grows.
