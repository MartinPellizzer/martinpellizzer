Amelanchier canadensis var. micropetala

Amelanchier canadensis var. micropetala, commonly known as the Canadian serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It features oval, dark green leaves that turn yellow or red in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, dark purple berries in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a versatile ornamental and wildlife plant.

Its compact size and seasonal interest make it a valuable addition to native gardens.
