Adesmia triphylla

Adesmia triphylla, commonly known as the three-leaf burr, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny pods. Adesmia triphylla is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

It is used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appearance.
