Achillea macedonica

Achillea macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.

Achillea macedonica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
