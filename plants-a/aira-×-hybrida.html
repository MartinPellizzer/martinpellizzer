Aira × hybrida

Aira × hybrida, commonly known as the tufted aira, is a hybrid grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with dense, grass-like foliage that forms compact clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
