Acalypha brachystachya

Acalypha brachystachya, commonly known as the narrow-stalked glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble small flowers, often displaying vibrant red, orange, or yellow hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous true flowers surrounded by these colorful leaf-like structures. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or near water bodies.

Acalypha brachystachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
