Asteropeia matrambody

Asteropeia matrambody, commonly known as the African starflower, is a rare and delicate plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small stars. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
