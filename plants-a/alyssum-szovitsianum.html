Alyssum szovitsianum

Alyssum szovitsianum, commonly known as the Szovits' alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Eastern Mediterranean.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pale pink, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Alyssum szovitsianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
