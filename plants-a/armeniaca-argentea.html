Armeniaca argentea

Armeniaca argentea, commonly known as the silver apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its silvery-white bark and showy pink to white flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically not edible for humans but are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its efficacy.
