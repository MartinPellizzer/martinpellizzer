Alcea annua

Alcea annua, commonly known as common hollyhock, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other plants like cotton and okra. The plant is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.
