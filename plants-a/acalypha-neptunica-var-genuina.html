Acalypha neptunica var. genuina

Acalypha neptunica var. genuina, commonly known as the Neptune's necklace, is a striking ornamental plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its unique, cascading clusters of bright red bracts that resemble tiny flames or beads, giving it its common name. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its vibrant appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a potted plant for its ornamental value.
