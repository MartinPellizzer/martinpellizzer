Annona humilis

Annona humilis, commonly known as the Little Muskmelon, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small, shrub-like tree that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant produces large, green, heart-shaped leaves and is known for its fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. Its fruit, which resembles a small melon, is edible and has a sweet, musky flavor.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
