Armeria glauca

Armeria glauca, commonly known as sea thrift, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces dense clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant has silver-gray foliage that is covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime climates.

Armeria glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
