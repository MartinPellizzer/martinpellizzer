Albizia parvifolia

Albizia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved silk tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often pinnately arranged and have small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, fluffy flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in dense clusters. Albizia parvifolia thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
