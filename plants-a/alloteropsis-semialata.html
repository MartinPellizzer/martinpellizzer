Alloteropsis semialata

Alloteropsis semialata, commonly known as the coastal panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a perennial grass that thrives in sandy or saline soils, often found in dune environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of coastal ecosystems and a resilient species for ecological restoration.
