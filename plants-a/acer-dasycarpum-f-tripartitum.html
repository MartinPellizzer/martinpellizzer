Acer dasycarpum f. tripartitum

Acer dasycarpum f. tripartitum, commonly known as the three-lobed maple, is a distinctive variant of the hairy maple.

It is characterized by its unique trifoliate leaves, which consist of three deeply lobed leaflets. The plant is native to the Himalayas and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant autumn foliage, which displays shades of red and orange.

This cultivar is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
