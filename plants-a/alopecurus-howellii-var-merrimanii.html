Alopecurus howellii var. merrimanii

Alopecurus howellii var. merrimanii, commonly known as Howell's hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in prairies, meadows, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of flowers that produce tiny grains.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for insects and small animals.
