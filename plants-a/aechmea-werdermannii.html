Aechmea werdermannii

Aechmea werdermannii, commonly known as the Werdermann's bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant's leaves are typically broad, stiff, and covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention and protection from intense sunlight. It produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that can range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Aechmea werdermannii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability to indoor environments.

Morphology

Aechmea werdermannii has rosette-shaped leaves that are narrow, sword-like, and arranged in a tight spiral, with a vibrant green color and a waxy surface.
