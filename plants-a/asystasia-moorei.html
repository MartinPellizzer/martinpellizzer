Asystasia moorei

Asystasia moorei, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silverleaf asystasia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in spikes at the tips of the stems. Asystasia moorei thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
