Attalea monosperma

Attalea monosperma, commonly known as the palm tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a tall, slender palm that can grow up to 20 meters in height, with a straight trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces large, yellowish flowers that develop into elongated fruits containing a single seed. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a distinctive, smooth, grayish trunk.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional practices and for its edible fruits.
