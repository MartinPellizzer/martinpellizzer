Achillea gossypina

Achillea gossypina, commonly known as the woolly yarrow, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic properties.

Achillea gossypina is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic uses.
