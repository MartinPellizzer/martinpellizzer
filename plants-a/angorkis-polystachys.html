Angorkis polystachys

Angorkis polystachys, commonly known as the clustered angorkis, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of purple and pink. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
