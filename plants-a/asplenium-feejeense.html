Asplenium feejeense

Asplenium feejeense, commonly known as the Feejeense spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a small, terrestrial fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or rocky crevices. The plant has a compact rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its sporangia are clustered on the undersides of the leaves, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

Asplenium feejeense is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia and is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
