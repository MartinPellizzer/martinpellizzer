Adiantum trilobum

Adiantum trilobum, commonly known as the southern maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, lacy fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. Its spores are produced on the undersides of its fronds in specialized structures called sori.

Adiantum trilobum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
