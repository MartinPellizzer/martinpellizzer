Atractylis mutica

Atractylis mutica, commonly known as the hairy thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and stream banks. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive thimble-like shape, giving it its common name.

Atractylis mutica is often found in the eastern and central United States, where it thrives in wetland environments.
