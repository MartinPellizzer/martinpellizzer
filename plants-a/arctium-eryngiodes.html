Arctium eryngiodes

Arctium eryngiodes, commonly known as common burdock, is a biennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by the distinctive burrs that aid in seed dispersal. Its root is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and thistles.
