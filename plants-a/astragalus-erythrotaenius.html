Astragalus erythrotaenius

Astragalus erythrotaenius, commonly known as the red-stemmed milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in dry conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

The seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread across open landscapes.
