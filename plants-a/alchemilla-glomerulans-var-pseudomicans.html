Alchemilla glomerulans var. pseudomicans

Alchemilla glomerulans var. pseudomicans, commonly known as the clustered lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features basal rosettes of heart-shaped, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and wetland restoration projects.
