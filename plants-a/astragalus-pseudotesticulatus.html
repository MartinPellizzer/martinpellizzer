Astragalus pseudotesticulatus

Astragalus pseudotesticulatus, commonly known as the false testicle milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to drought conditions, with deep taproots that allow it to survive in sandy or rocky soils. Its seeds are typically dispersed by wind, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species.
