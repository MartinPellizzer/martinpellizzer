Agrostis alba var. coarctata

Agrostis alba var. coarctata, commonly known as narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a fine texture and a distinctive blue-gray hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny florets, which are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed sites.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable component of native plant communities and a subject of study in ecological and horticultural contexts.
