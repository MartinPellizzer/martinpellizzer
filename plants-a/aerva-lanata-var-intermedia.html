Aerva lanata var. intermedia

Aerva lanata var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate marshmallow plant, is a herbaceous species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Aerva lanata var. intermedia is often found in wet or moist habitats, such as marshes and stream banks.
