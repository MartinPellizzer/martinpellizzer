Acanthocereus horribarbis

Acanthocereus horribarbis, commonly known as the devil's claw cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, columnar stems and produces vibrant yellow flowers during the night. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bats and moths, which are active during the evening.

The plant's unique morphology and nocturnal blooming habit make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
