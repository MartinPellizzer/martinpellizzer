Amyxa

Amyxa, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves that appear to shimmer in the sunlight, giving it its ethereal appearance. The plant produces small, pale pink flowers that bloom only at night, releasing a faint, sweet fragrance. Amyxa thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its elusive nature and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
