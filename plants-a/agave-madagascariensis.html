Agave madagascariensis

Agave madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar agave, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears yellow flowers, which are clustered in inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
