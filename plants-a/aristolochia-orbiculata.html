Aristolochia orbiculata

Aristolochia orbiculata, commonly known as the broadleaf Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves and distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically purple or greenish in color. The plant belongs to the family Aristolochiaceae and is known for its unique floral structure that attracts specific pollinators. Its vines can grow up to 10 feet long, making it a striking addition to gardens or natural landscapes.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
