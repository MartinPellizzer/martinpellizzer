Andinia pensilis

Andinia pensilis, commonly known as the hanging bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Andinia pensilis is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
