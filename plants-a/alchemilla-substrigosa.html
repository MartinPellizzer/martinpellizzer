Alchemilla substrigosa

Alchemilla substrigosa, commonly known as the hairy lady's mantle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are borne on slender stems. Alchemilla substrigosa thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its leaves are used in traditional herbal remedies and are known for their astringent properties.
