Alpinojuncus trachyphyllus

Alpinojuncus trachyphyllus, commonly known as the roughleaf bulrush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in South America.

It typically grows in marshes, lakes, and along the edges of water bodies, preferring moist to aquatic conditions. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a rough or scaly texture, giving it its scientific name. Its flowering spikes are usually tall and cylindrical, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Alpinojuncus trachyphyllus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
