Althaea atropurpurea

Althaea atropurpurea, commonly known as purple flowering alum root, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Althaea atropurpurea is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
