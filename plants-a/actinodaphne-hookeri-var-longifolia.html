Actinodaphne hookeri var. longifolia

Actinodaphne hookeri var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved actinodaphne, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, characterized by its aromatic leaves and often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is known for its lance-shaped, leathery leaves that can reach several centimeters in length. Its small, white flowers are clustered in umbels and are followed by small, round berries.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
