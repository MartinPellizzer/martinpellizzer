Astragalus turkmenorum

Astragalus turkmenorum, commonly known as Turkmen milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are small and typically dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native range.
