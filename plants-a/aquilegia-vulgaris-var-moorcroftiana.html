Aquilegia vulgaris var. moorcroftiana

Aquilegia vulgaris var. moorcroftiana, commonly known as the Moorcroft's columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to purple spurred flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound, pinnately arranged leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are typically hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
