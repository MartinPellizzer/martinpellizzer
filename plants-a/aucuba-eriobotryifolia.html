Aucuba eriobotryifolia

Aucuba eriobotryifolia, commonly known as the Japanese aucuba, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy, dark green leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow margins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in shaded environments and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Aucuba eriobotryifolia is relatively low maintenance and can tolerate a variety of soil conditions.
