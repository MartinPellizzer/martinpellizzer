Anthemis bellidiastrum

Anthemis bellidiastrum, commonly known as the goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, often with a faint aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
