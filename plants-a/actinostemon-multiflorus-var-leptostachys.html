Actinostemon multiflorus var. leptostachys

Actinostemon multiflorus var. leptostachys, commonly known as the slender-stalked actinostemon, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
