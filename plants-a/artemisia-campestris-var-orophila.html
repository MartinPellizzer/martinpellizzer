Artemisia campestris var. orophila

Artemisia campestris var. orophila, commonly known as mountain wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, aromatic foliage and slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in spherical shapes, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open meadows and rocky slopes.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thujone and camphor, which contribute to its strong medicinal and aromatic properties.
