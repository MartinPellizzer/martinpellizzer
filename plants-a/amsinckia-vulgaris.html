Amsinckia vulgaris

Amsinckia vulgaris, commonly known as the common ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces yellow to orange flower heads that are surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Amsinckia vulgaris is known for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
