Andropogon ringoetii

Andropogon ringoetii, commonly known as the Florida panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Due to its ecological significance, it is often found in conservation areas and wetland restoration projects.
