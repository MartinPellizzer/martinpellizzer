Alamania punicea subsp. punicea

Alamania punicea subsp.

punicea, commonly known as the red alamania, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely distributed outside its native range.
