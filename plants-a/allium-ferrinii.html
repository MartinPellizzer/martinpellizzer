Allium ferrinii

Allium ferrinii, commonly known as the Ferrini's onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-like, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces spherical bulbs that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
