Areca catechu var. silvatica

Areca catechu var. silvatica, commonly known as the wild areca palm, is a variant of the areca palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and pinnate leaves that are typically shorter and more compact compared to the standard areca palm. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, which are essential for its reproductive cycle. Its leaves are often used in traditional practices and for thatching due to their durability.

This variant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various tropical environments.
