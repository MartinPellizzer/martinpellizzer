Aloysia krapovickasii

Aloysia krapovickasii, commonly known as the Argentine verbena, is a species of flowering plant in the verbena family.

It is native to South America, particularly Argentina, and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in shades of purple and violet, producing a striking visual effect in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping and coastal gardens.

Aloysia krapovickasii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
