Aster mesopotamicus

Aster mesopotamicus, commonly known as the Mesopotamian aster, is a flowering plant native to the region of Mesopotamia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features showy purple or blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Due to its historical and ornamental significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
