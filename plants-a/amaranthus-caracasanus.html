Amaranthus caracasanus

Amaranthus caracasanus, commonly known as the red amaranth, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and vibrant, feather-like leaves that range in color from green to red. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or red, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant or for its edible leaves.

Amaranthus caracasanus is also valued for its hardiness and ability to grow in a variety of soil conditions.
