Asteriscus flosculosus

Asteriscus flosculosus, commonly known as the smallflower asteriscus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing a compact, shrub-like form with gray-green foliage. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually pale blue or white, and it produces small, dry fruits known as achenes.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
