Absinthium campestre

Absinthium campestre, commonly known as field wormwood, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are finely divided and have a strong, bitter aroma due to the presence of sesquiterpene lactones.

Absinthium campestre is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported digestive and antiseptic properties.
