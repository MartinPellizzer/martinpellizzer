Anchusa paniculata

Anchusa paniculata, commonly known as the spiked speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, cylindrical flower spikes. Anchusa paniculata is often found in rocky soils and dry habitats, making it a hardy species adapted to Mediterranean climates.

It is also valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
