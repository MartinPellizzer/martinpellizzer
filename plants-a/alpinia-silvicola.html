Alpinia silvicola

Alpinia silvicola, commonly known as the forest ginger, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in panicles, and bloom during the rainy season.

Alpinia silvicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
