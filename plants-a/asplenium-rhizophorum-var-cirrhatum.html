Asplenium rhizophorum var. cirrhatum

Asplenium rhizophorum var. cirrhatum, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a variant of the common shield fern.

It is a small, evergreen fern native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia. The plant features dark green, leathery fronds with a distinctive hairy texture on the underside. Its rhizomes are thick and creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

The fronds are typically divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance.
