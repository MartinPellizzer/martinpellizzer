Arundo laxa

Arundo laxa, commonly known as the soft reed or narrow reed, is a perennial grass native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. This plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as riverbanks and floodplains, where it forms dense stands. Its flowering spikes are small and clustered, producing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Arundo laxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
