Acer nikoense

Acer nikoense, commonly known as the Nikko maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its vibrant autumn foliage, which displays shades of red, orange, and yellow. The tree has palmately lobed leaves that are typically 5-9 cm in length. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
