Amoora lawii

Amoora lawii, commonly known as the white-veined arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its arrowhead-shaped leaves and striking white veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically surrounded by a spathe. Its rhizomes are the primary part used, containing starch that is harvested for culinary purposes.

Amoora lawii thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
