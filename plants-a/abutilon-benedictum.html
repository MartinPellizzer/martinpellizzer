Abutilon benedictum

Abutilon benedictum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive texture. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Abutilon benedictum is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique foliage and seasonal blooms.
