Ardisia pseudojambosa

Ardisia pseudojambosa, commonly known as the red-berried evergreen, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by bright red berries, which are an attractive feature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ardisia pseudojambosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense undergrowth in woodland settings.
