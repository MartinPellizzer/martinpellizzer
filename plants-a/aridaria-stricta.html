Aridaria stricta

Aridaria stricta, commonly known as the rigid arid grass, is a perennial plant adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is characterized by its upright, slender growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered at the ends of its stems. It is well-suited for drought conditions due to its deep root system and reduced leaf surface area.

Aridaria stricta is often found in sandy soils and is used in ecological restoration projects in dry regions.
