Acronia gargantua

Acronia gargantua, commonly known as the giant acronia, is a rare and striking member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its towering stature and unique floral structure.

It features large, umbrella-like umbels of white to pale yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant's leaves are deeply lobed and can grow up to several feet in length, contributing to its dramatic appearance. Native to remote mountainous regions, it thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is often protected in its natural habitat.
