Allium paniculatum var. floribundum

Allium paniculatum var. floribundum, commonly known as the showy onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features strap-like, grassy leaves and produces clusters of small, spherical, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its tall, upright stem and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical name reflects its classification within the Allium genus, which includes many species of onions and garlic.
