Acacia gelasina

Acacia gelasina, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

Acacia gelasina is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, enriching the soil and supporting other plant life.
