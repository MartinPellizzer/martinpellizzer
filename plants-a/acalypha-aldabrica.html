Acalypha aldabrica

Acalypha aldabrica, commonly known as the Aldabra spurge, is a shrub native to the Comoros Islands.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often growing in dune habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
