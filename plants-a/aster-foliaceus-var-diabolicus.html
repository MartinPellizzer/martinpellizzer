Aster foliaceus var. diabolicus

Aster foliaceus var. diabolicus, commonly known as the Devil's Aster, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers and finely dissected, fern-like foliage that gives it a delicate, almost otherworldly appearance. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its unique appearance and hardy nature have made it a favorite among botanists and gardeners alike.

The variety name "diabolicus" likely refers to its eerie, almost demonic look, which has inspired both admiration and superstition.
