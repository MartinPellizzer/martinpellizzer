Amaryllis cybister var. subsexuncialis

Amaryllis cybister var. subsexuncialis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically a deep green color, and its flowers are large, showy blooms with six to eight petals. The flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a prominent central greenish throat. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens or as a houseplant.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to many landscapes.
