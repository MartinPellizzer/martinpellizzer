Acronychia laevis var. dictyophleba

Acronychia laevis var. dictyophleba, commonly known as the rough-leaved acronychia, is a species native to northern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub characterized by its rough, fissured bark and elliptical leaves. The leaves are typically leathery and have a distinctive midrib that is prominent on the underside. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits.

This plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas of the Northern Territory and Queensland.
