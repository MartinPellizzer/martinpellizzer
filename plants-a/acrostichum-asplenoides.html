Acrostichum asplenoides

Acrostichum asplenoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It thrives in wetland environments, often found in ponds, ditches, and marshes. The plant has slender, pinnately compound leaves that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Acrostichum asplenoides plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water filtration.
