Astragalus drusorum f. maroniticus

Astragalus drusorum f. maroniticus, commonly known as the Maronitic horsegram, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, temperate climates. It is known for its hardy nature and is often found in open habitats such as hillsides and grasslands.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly larger seeds and more robust growth compared to the typical form of *Astragalus drusorum*.
