Acer campestre var. rubens

Acer campestre var. rubens, commonly known as the red maple or red horse chestnut, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange-red foliage, which becomes more vibrant in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds known as samaras. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with coarsely toothed edges.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
