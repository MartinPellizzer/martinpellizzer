Aquilegia sibirica var. bicolor

Aquilegia sibirica var. bicolor, commonly known as the Siberian garden bell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with pinnate divisions, and its stems are hairy and upright. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
