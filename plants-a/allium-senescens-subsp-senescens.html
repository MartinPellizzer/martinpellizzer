Allium senescens subsp. senescens

Allium senescens subsp.

senescens, commonly known as the common garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia. It features long, grass-like leaves that are often used in culinary applications for their mild garlic flavor.

The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in umbels, which are followed by spherical, dry fruits containing several seeds. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and edible plant collections.
