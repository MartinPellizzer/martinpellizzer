Acalypha zeyheri var. pubescens

Acalypha zeyheri var. pubescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silverleaf, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, velvety foliage with a soft, pubescent texture. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and exhibit a striking silver or gray-green hue due to dense trichomes. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun.
