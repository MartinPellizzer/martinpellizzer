Acacia brachyphylla var. recurvata

Acacia brachyphylla var. recurvata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and small, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are arranged in compact spherical clusters. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping.
