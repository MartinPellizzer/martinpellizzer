Artocarpus brevipedunculatus

Artocarpus brevipedunculatus, commonly known as the short-stemmed breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by round, green fruits that turn yellow when mature. The fruit is edible, with a sweet, starchy pulp similar to breadfruit, and is often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in humid climates and is typically found in lowland forests.
