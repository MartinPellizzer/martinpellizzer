Anacardium braziliense

Anacardium braziliense, commonly known as the Brazilian cashew or sweet chestnut, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like the cashew and poison ivy. The tree produces small, red, fleshy fruits that enclose a single hard seed. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
