Amaranthus tamaulipensis

Amaranthus tamaulipensis, commonly known as the Tamaulipan amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is native to the southern United States and northern Mexico, particularly in the Tamaulipan region. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy bracts that often display shades of red, orange, or yellow. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Amaranthus tamaulipensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking foliage and floral display.
