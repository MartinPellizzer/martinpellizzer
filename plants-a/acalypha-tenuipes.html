Acalypha tenuipes

Acalypha tenuipes, commonly known as the slender-leaved glory flower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, narrow leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous annual or perennial, depending on the climate. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Acalypha tenuipes is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
