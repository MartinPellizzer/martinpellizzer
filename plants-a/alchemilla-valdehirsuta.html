Alchemilla valdehirsuta

Alchemilla valdehirsuta, commonly known as the hairy alchemilla, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of soft, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla valdehirsuta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in partial shade.
