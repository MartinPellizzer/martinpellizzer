Astragalus halleri

Astragalus halleri, commonly known as the Haller's milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in rocky or arid environments. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus halleri is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
