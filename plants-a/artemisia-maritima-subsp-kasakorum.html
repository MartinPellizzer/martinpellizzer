Artemisia maritima subsp. kasakorum

Artemisia maritima subsp.

kasakorum, commonly known as the Caspian wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the steppes of Central Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flower heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils. It has a fibrous root system and produces numerous slender, branching stems.

Its essential oils contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties, making it valuable in traditional herbal medicine.
