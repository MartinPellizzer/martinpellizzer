Aleuritia glomerata

Aleuritia glomerata, commonly known as the cluster-flowered honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in dense clusters, typically in late spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aleuritia glomerata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
