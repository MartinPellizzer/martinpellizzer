Astragalus angustifolius subsp. balcanicus

Astragalus angustifolius subsp.

balcanicus, commonly known as the Balkan narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in rocky or dry grassland habitats, exhibiting a low, spreading growth habit.

The plant has slender, hairy stems and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and pinnately divided, with a distinctive, feathery appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also of interest in ecological and botanical studies due to its specific habitat requirements.
