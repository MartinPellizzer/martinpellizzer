Armeria allioides var. longifolia

Armeria allioides var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf sea thrift, is a perennial flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal rosettes of narrow, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes or rocky outcrops.

Its hardy nature and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for coastal gardens and landscaping.
