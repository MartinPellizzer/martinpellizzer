Agapetes leucocarpa

Agapetes leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-fruited silverleaf, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by white or pale yellow berries. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found at high elevations.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
