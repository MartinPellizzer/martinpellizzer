Aristida mollissima subsp. argentea

Aristida mollissima subsp.

argentea, commonly known as silver hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that reflects sunlight, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
