Aspalathus tulbaghensis

Aspalathus tulbaghensis, commonly known as rooibos, is a small shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires a warm, Mediterranean climate. Its most notable feature is the unique red-colored leaves, which are used to produce the popular herbal tea.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
