Adenophora gmelinii subsp. nystroemii

Adenophora gmelinii subsp.

nystroemii, commonly known as the Nystroem's bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or open woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, showy flowers.
