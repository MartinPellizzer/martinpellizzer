Aster elegans var. engelmannii

Aster elegans var. engelmannii, commonly known as Engelmann's slender aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early fall, producing blue to purple flower heads that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats like meadows and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
