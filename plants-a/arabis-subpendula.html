Arabis subpendula

Arabis subpendula, commonly known as the hanging rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and arranged in clusters, often with a subtle fragrance.
