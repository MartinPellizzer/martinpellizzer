Amaranthus torreyi f. prostratus

Amaranthus torreyi f. prostratus, commonly known as the prostrate amaranth, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant characterized by its sprawling habit and fleshy, lance-shaped leaves.

It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences, which are typically borne on slender stalks. The plant is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional cuisines, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to poor soils.

Amaranthus torreyi f. prostratus is also noted for its ability to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
