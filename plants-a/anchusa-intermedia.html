Anchusa intermedia

Anchusa intermedia, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soils, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Anchusa intermedia is also known for its medicinal properties, historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
