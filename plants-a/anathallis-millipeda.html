Anathallis millipeda

Anathallis millipeda, commonly known as the millipede orchid, is a species of orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated floral structure that resembles the segmented body of a millipede, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the Atlantic Forest biome. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive, spurred lip that aids in pollination by specific insects.

Anathallis millipeda is a rare and specialized orchid, showcasing the intricate adaptations found in tropical plant species.
