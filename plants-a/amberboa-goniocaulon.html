Amberboa goniocaulon

Amberboa goniocaulon, commonly known as the golden waxy plant, is a succulent species native to arid regions of Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the rainy season, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its unique morphology includes a swollen, bottle-shaped stem that helps reduce water loss.

Amberboa goniocaulon is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and resilience.
