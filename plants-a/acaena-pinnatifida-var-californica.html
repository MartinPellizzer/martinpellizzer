Acaena pinnatifida var. californica

Acaena pinnatifida var. californica, commonly known as the California thimbleweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, palmately lobed leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in heads. Its fruits are small, spiny achenes that are often referred to as "thimbleweeds" due to their shape.

This species is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal and inland grasslands.
