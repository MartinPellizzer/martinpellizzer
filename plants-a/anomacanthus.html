Anomacanthus

Anomacanthus, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, spiny bracts that surround the small, inconspicuous flowers. These bracts are often brightly colored, ranging from blue to purple, and serve as a key identifying feature. The plant is native to coastal regions of southern Africa and is adapted to saline and sandy soils.

Anomacanthus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and coastal landscapes.
