Aegiphila nuxia

Aegiphila nuxia, commonly known as the Brazilian thyme, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and clusters of purple or pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Aegiphila nuxia is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
