Acanthus gaed

Acanthus gaed, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acanthus, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically purple or red in color, arranged in spikes at the top of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental foliage.

Due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a border plant.
