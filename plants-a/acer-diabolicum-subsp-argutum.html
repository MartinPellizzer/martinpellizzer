Acer diabolicum subsp. argutum

Acer diabolicum subsp.

argutum, commonly known as the devil's maple, is a rare and striking member of the maple family native to the mountainous regions of central Asia. It is characterized by its deeply lobed, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
