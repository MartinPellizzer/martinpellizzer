Abelmoschus moschatus

Abelmoschus moschatus, commonly known as the musk thorn, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and heart-shaped leaves, with yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's name derives from the musky fragrance emitted by its flowers and fruits.
