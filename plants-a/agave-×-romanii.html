Agave × romanii

Agave × romanii, commonly known as the hybrid agave, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves, which are thick, fleshy, and often covered in a blue-gray or greenish hue. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that can reach several meters in height, bearing numerous white or pale yellow flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it drought-tolerant.

Agave × romanii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and resilience.
