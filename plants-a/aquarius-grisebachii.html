Aquarius grisebachii

Aquarius grisebachii, commonly known as the African violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of velvety, dark green leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple, blue, and white. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers indirect sunlight, making it a popular houseplant. Its botanical features include a short, thick stem and a fibrous root system.

Aquarius grisebachii is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
