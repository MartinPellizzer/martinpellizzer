Anagallis robertii

Anagallis robertii, commonly known as the Robert's pink, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to white. The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant prefers well-drained soil in sunny or partially shaded areas. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, and its delicate appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in various horticultural settings.
