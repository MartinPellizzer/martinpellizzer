Anthemis hondensis

Anthemis hondensis, commonly known as the Honduran ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
