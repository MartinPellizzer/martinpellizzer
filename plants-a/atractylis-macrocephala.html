Atractylis macrocephala

Atractylis macrocephala, commonly known as giant centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its robust, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Atractylis macrocephala is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual impact.
