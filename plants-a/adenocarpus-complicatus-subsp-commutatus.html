Adenocarpus complicatus subsp. commutatus

Adenocarpus complicatus subsp.

commutatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burr legume, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal applications.
