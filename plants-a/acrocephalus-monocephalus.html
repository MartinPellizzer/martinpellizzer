Acrocephalus monocephalus

Acrocephalus monocephalus, commonly known as the common reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence that appears at the top of the plant. The plant has long, narrow leaves and produces small, spike-like flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Acrocephalus monocephalus is widely distributed across temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
