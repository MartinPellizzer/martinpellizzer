Anil brachystachya

Anil brachystachya, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in dry, sandy soils. It is native to regions of southern Africa and is adapted to arid environments.

Anil brachystachya is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
