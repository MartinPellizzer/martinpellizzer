Alyssum nebrodense f. latoellipticum

Alyssum nebrodense f. latoellipticum, commonly known as the broad-elliptic alpine alyssum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The leaves are usually narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture. This plant is native to the mountainous regions of Southern Europe, particularly in the Alps and surrounding areas.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine plantings.
