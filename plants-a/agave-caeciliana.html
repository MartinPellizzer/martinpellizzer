Agave caeciliana

Agave caeciliana, commonly known as the Mexican rose agave, is a striking succulent native to Mexico.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are adorned with white marginal teeth. The plant produces an inflorescence of red, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and collections.
