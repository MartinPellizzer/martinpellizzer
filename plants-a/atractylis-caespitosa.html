Atractylis caespitosa

Atractylis caespitosa, commonly known as field eryngo, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as roadsides and meadows. The plant has a stout, upright stem with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its yellow flowers are clustered in dense, spherical heads, which are borne on long, slender stalks.

Atractylis caespitosa is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
