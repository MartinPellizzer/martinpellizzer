Aptosimum gossweileri

Aptosimum gossweileri, commonly known as the Gossweiler's aptosimum, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. This perennial grass is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute flowers, typical of many grass species.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
