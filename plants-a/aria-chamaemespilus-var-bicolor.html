Aria chamaemespilus var. bicolor

Aria chamaemespilus var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored woodbine, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and white or pale underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are usually white or pale yellow, followed by small, round berries that turn dark blue when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
