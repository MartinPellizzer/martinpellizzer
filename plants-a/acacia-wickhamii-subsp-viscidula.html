Acacia wickhamii subsp. viscidula

Acacia wickhamii subsp.

viscidula, commonly known as the sticky acacia, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and small, yellow globular flowers that appear in spring.

The leaves are phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of true leaves. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is often found in coastal and inland regions with sandy or gravelly soils.
