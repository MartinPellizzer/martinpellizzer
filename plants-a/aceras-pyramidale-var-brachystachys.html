Aceras pyramidale var. brachystachys

Aceras pyramidale var. brachystachys, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, slender stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, powdery bloom. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its compact size and ornamental flowers, it is often used in landscaping for its visual appeal.
