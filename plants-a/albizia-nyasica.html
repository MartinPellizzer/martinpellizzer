Albizia nyasica

Albizia nyasica, commonly known as the African silk tree, is a deciduous legume native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often trifoliate or pinnate, and its showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces long, flat pods that contain several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Albizia nyasica is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment and agroforestry systems.
