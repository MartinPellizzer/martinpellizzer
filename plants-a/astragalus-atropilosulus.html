Astragalus atropilosulus

Astragalus atropilosulus, commonly known as the dark-hairy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, harsh environments. Its seed pods are slender and contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Astragalus atropilosulus plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
