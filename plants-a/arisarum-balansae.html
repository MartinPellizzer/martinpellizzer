Arisarum balansae

Arisarum balansae, commonly known as the Balansae arum, is a striking tropical plant native to the wetlands of Central America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green markings. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by insects, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
