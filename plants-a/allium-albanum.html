Allium albanum

Allium albanum, commonly known as the Albanian leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, garlic-like flavor.

Allium albanum is often cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
