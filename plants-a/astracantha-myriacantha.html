Astracantha myriacantha

Astracantha myriacantha, commonly known as the many-bracted aster, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves and produces numerous bracts that give it its common name. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

The flowers are typically pink to purple in color and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
