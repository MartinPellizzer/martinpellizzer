Aconitum dolichostachyum

Aconitum dolichostachyum, commonly known as the long-stalked monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant has large, palmate leaves that are deeply lobed, and it produces a tall, sparsely branched stem. Aconitum dolichostachyum is known for its toxic alkaloids, which make it highly poisonous to both humans and animals.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
