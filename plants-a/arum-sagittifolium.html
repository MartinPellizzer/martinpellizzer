Arum sagittifolium

Arum sagittifolium, commonly known as the arrowleaf arum, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features a distinctive arrow-shaped leaf that is often deeply lobed, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces a spadix, which is surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
