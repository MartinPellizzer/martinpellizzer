Artemisia campestris subsp. variabilis

Artemisia campestris subsp.

variabilis, commonly known as variable wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage with feathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
