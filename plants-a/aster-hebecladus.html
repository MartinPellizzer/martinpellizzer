Aster hebecladus

Aster hebecladus, commonly known as the rock aster, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough or hairy texture.

Aster hebecladus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
