Acalypha reticulata var. urophylla

Acalypha reticulata var. urophylla, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red-veined nettle, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking, velvety, heart-shaped leaves with deep red veins that contrast beautifully with its bright green foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typically hidden among the leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden areas.

Its unique leaf coloration and texture make it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical landscapes.
