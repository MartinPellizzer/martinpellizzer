Atriplex sagittifolia

Atriplex sagittifolia, commonly known as arrowleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its alternate, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of salt-excreting glands. The plant is well adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a common component of desert ecosystems. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

Atriplex sagittifolia plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
