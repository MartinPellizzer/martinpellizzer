Athyrium rhaeticum

Athyrium rhaeticum, commonly known as the Alpine lady fern, is a species of fern native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically gray-green in color. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas or along stream sides. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of its fertile fronds.

Athyrium rhaeticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
