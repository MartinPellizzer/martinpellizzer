Angelica pubescens f. biserrata

Angelica pubescens f. biserrata, commonly known as the hairy angelica, is a variant of the angelica plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and large, compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters called umbels. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

The roots of this plant are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
