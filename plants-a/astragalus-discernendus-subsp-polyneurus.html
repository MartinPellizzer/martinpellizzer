Astragalus discernendus subsp. polyneurus

Astragalus discernendus subsp.

polyneurus, commonly known as the Sierra foothill milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and open woodlands.

The plant has slender, branched stems and small, clustered leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are pink to purple, appearing in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and habitat preferences.
