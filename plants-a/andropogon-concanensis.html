Andropogon concanensis

Andropogon concanensis, commonly known as the Indian switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Andropogon concanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
