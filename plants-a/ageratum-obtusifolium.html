Ageratum obtusifolium

Ageratum obtusifolium, commonly known as Mexican fairy fan, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple. The leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a blunt or rounded tip, giving the plant its species name "obtusifolium.". It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This plant is often used in floral arrangements due to its delicate appearance and long-lasting blooms.
