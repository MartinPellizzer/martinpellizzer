Adiantum venustum subsp. tibeticum

Adiantum venustum subsp.

tibeticum, commonly known as the Tibetan maidenhair fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It is characterized by its finely divided, glossy green fronds that are often adorned with a distinctive rusty-brown margin.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in rocky crevices or shaded forest floors at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its fronds are bi-directionally arranged, with the fertile and sterile fronds often occurring on separate branches.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
