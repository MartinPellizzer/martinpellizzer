Abies cephalonica f. pseudocilicica

Abies cephalonica f. pseudocilicica, commonly known as the Cilician fir, is a rare and distinctive subspecies of the Caucasian fir.

It is native to the eastern Mediterranean region, particularly in parts of Turkey and northern Cyprus. This plant is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and needle-like leaves that are typically dark green with a silvery underside. The cones are small and often hang in clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
