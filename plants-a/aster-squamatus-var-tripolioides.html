Aster squamatus var. tripolioides

Aster squamatus var. tripolioides, commonly known as the Tripoli aster, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy blue to purple flowers in the autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is well adapted to arid conditions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

The variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking floral display.
