Allium speculae

Allium speculae, commonly known as the spectre onion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bulbous structures that store nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
