Aframomum biauriculatum

Aframomum biauriculatum, commonly known as the two-lobed cardamom, is a tropical plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are followed by three-lobed fruits.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and potential medicinal uses.
