Astragalus jarmolenkoi

Astragalus jarmolenkoi, commonly known as the Jarmolenko milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its pods contain small, rounded seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation across its native habitat.

Astragalus jarmolenkoi is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its region.
