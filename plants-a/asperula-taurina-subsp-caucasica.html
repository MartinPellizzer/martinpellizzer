Asperula taurina subsp. caucasica

Asperula taurina subsp.

caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian asperula, is a perennial shrub native to the Caucasus region. It features small, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale pink to white flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
