Achyronia galeata

Achyronia galeata, commonly known as the rock lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Achyronia galeata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and dry landscapes.
