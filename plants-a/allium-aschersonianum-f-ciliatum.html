Allium aschersonianum f. ciliatum

Allium aschersonianum f. ciliatum, commonly known as the curled leek or curly leek, is a botanical variant of the Allium aschersonianum species.

It is characterized by its dense, curling foliage that forms a compact rosette, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white, spherical flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is a hardy perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in rock gardens and as a border plant.
