Apios apios var. boulderensis

Apios apios var. boulderensis, commonly known as the Boulder salsify, is a variant of the common salsify native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its erect, branching stems and palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by cylindrical, edible tuberous roots. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

This variant is notable for its adaptability and historical use by indigenous peoples for food and medicinal purposes.
