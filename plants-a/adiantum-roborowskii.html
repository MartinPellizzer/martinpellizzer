Adiantum roborowskii

Adiantum roborowskii, commonly known as the Roborowsky's maidenhair fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lace-like fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, giving it a delicate and ethereal appearance. The plant prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded, rocky crevices or along streams. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in the Adiantum genus.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its natural habitat.
