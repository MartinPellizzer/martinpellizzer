Astragalus helmii var. permiensis

Astragalus helmii var. permiensis, commonly known as the Permiense milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showing a high degree of adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small leaflets arranged in pairs, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

This species is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
