Aquilegia ottonis var. unguisepala

Aquilegia ottonis var. unguisepala, commonly known as the Mexican columbine, is a perennial plant native to central Mexico.

It features striking, spurred petals in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typical of the Aquilegia genus. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
