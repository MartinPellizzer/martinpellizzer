Anacamptis laxiflora subsp. laxiflora

Anacamptis laxiflora subsp.

laxiflora, commonly known as the loose-flowered orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features a basal rosette of linear leaves and produces tall, erect flowering stems. Its flowers are arranged in a raceme and are characterized by their pale yellow to greenish color with a distinctive, narrow lip.

This orchid is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.
