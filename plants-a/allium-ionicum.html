Allium ionicum

Allium ionicum, commonly known as mountain garlic, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and leeks. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers arranged in umbels, and its leaves are long, grass-like, and aromatic. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

The bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a useful plant in both culinary and medicinal applications.
