Astragalus neostipitatus

Astragalus neostipitatus, commonly known as the Neostipitate Milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine, though its botanical significance lies in its role within its native ecosystems.
