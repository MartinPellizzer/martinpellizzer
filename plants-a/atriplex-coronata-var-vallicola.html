Atriplex coronata var. vallicola

Atriplex coronata var. vallicola, commonly known as silverleaf shadscale, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense covering of fine, reflective hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

This species is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
