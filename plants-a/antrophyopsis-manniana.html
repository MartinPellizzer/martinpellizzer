Antrophyopsis manniana

Antrophyopsis manniana, commonly known as the African rock orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or inland areas with well-drained conditions. The plant features a slender, unbranched stem that bears a single, showy flower with a distinctive, spurred lip. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and the flower is usually pale green or yellowish in color.

Antrophyopsis manniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
