Astragalus neobarnebyanus

Astragalus neobarnebyanus, commonly known as the Barneby's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect stems and compound leaves with small, oval leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in spherical heads. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Astragalus neobarnebyanus is a member of the legume family and has a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh conditions.
