Andropogon gyrans var. stenophyllus

Andropogon gyrans var. stenophyllus, commonly known as narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for wildlife.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive species within the grass family, Poaceae.
