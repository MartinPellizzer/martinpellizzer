Anredera densiflora

Anredera densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or white. Its stems are often hairy and can spread via rhizomes, allowing it to form dense mats.

Anredera densiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
