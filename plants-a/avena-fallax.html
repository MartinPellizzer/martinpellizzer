Avena fallax

Avena fallax, commonly known as the false oat, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny flowers. Avena fallax is often found in rocky or disturbed soils and is adapted to dry conditions.

It has been used historically in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
