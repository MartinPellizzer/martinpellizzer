Anthodiscus glaucescens

Anthodiscus glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green anthodiscus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features a slender, upright stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a bluish-gray waxy coating. Its small, white to pale blue flowers are clustered in terminal umbels, giving it a delicate appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
