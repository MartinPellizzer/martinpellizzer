Arabis pamirica

Arabis pamirica, commonly known as the Pamir rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Pamir Mountains.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cold and dry conditions. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

Its leaves are basal and rosette-like, adapted to conserve moisture in its harsh habitat.
