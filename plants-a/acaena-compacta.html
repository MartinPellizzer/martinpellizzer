Acaena compacta

Acaena compacta, commonly known as the New Zealand bramble, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that contain hard, dark seeds. It thrives in coastal and subalpine environments, often forming dense mats on the ground.

Acaena compacta plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
