Abronia umbellata var. platyphylla

Abronia umbellata var. platyphylla, commonly known as the broadleaf sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with fleshy, succulent leaves that are typically broad and oval in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters at the ends of its stems, often blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Its succulent leaves help it store water, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.
