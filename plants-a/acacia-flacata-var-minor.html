Acacia flacata var. minor

Acacia flacata var. minor, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, yellow flowers that appear in winter. The plant has phyllodes, which are modified leaf stalks that function as leaves, and these are typically narrow and linear. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

The slender wattle is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
