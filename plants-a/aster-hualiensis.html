Aster hualiensis

Aster hualiensis, commonly known as the Hualien aster, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia, particularly found in China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers bloom in shades of blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
