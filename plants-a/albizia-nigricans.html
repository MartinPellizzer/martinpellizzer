Albizia nigricans

Albizia nigricans, commonly known as black albizia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, racemose inflorescences. Its root system is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Albizia nigricans is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and forage.
