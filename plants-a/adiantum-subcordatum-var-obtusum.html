Adiantum subcordatum var. obtusum

Adiantum subcordatum var. obtusum, commonly known as the broad-leaved maidenhair fern, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, dark green fronds that are typically broad and slightly curled at the tips. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its fronds are often described as having a delicate, lace-like appearance, which contributes to its ornamental value.

This variety is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
