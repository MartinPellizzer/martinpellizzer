Acacia carneorum

Acacia carneorum, commonly known as the red acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange flower spikes that appear during the warmer months. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Acacia carneorum produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

It is an important plant in its ecosystem, providing nectar for various pollinators and habitat for small animals.
