Azalea

Azalea, commonly known as a flowering shrub, is part of the Rhododendron genus and is native to East Asia, temperate regions of the Americas, and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including red, pink, white, and yellow. Azaleas have opposite, simple leaves that are usually glossy and elliptical in shape. They thrive in acidic, well-drained soil and prefer partial to full shade.

The plant is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping and floral arrangements.
