Actinidia × fairchildii

Actinidia × fairchildii, commonly known as the fuzzy kiwi, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Actinidia deliciosa and Actinidia chinensis.

It is a deciduous vine that produces small, fuzzy, edible berries, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires a cold winter period for proper fruit development. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture, while its stems are woody and can climb or trail.

This species is widely cultivated for its fruit and is also valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
