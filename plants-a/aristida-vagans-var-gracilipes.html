Aristida vagans var. gracilipes

Aristida vagans var. gracilipes, commonly known as slender hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with delicate, thread-like flowers. Its foliage is characterized by narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered with fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid environments with minimal water requirements.
