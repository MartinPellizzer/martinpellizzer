Arundinaria gigantea subsp. tecta

Arundinaria gigantea subsp.

tecta, commonly known as the bamboo palm, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a dense, fan-like cluster at the top of the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to form dense clumps.
