Aspalathus albanensis

Aspalathus albanensis, commonly known as the Albanian bushveld pea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shrubland and grassland habitats, often in areas with well-drained soils. It produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind, and it plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with soil bacteria.

Aspalathus albanensis is not widely cultivated but is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological and potential medicinal properties.
