Arenaria armerina subsp. armerina

Arenaria armerina subsp.

armerina, commonly known as the coastal sandspoon, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa. It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that thrives in sandy and gravelly soils.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments.

It is often found in dunes and other coastal habitats where it plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes.
