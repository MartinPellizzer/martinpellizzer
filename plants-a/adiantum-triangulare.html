Adiantum triangulare

Adiantum triangulare, commonly known as the triangular maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bright green and have a triangular shape. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and prefers well-draining soil. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine in some cultures.

Adiantum triangulare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
