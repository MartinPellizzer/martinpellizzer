Aletris punicea

Aletris punicea, commonly known as the fire pink or redroot.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States, typically found in moist, acidic soils of woodlands and wetlands. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs. Its flowering stems rise above the foliage, bearing clusters of small, pink to red flowers.

Aletris punicea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
