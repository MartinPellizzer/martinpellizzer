Abies communis var. pendula

Abies communis var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping fir, is a variant of the noble fir characterized by its distinctive drooping branches.

This evergreen conifer is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. Its foliage consists of needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in a dense, cascading pattern. The tree typically grows to a height of 10 to 20 meters, with a narrow, conical shape.

It thrives in well-drained soils and cooler climates, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
