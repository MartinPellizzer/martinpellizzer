Avena gaudiana

Avena gaudiana, commonly known as the golden oat or Mexican oat, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense clusters of golden-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Avena gaudiana is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
