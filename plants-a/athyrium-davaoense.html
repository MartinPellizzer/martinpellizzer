Athyrium davaoense

Athyrium davaoense, commonly known as the Davao holly fern, is a species of fern native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Dryopteridaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, which are clustered in sori.

Athyrium davaoense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
