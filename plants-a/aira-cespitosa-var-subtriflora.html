Aira cespitosa var. subtriflora

Aira cespitosa var. subtriflora, commonly known as the slender false grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The leaves are narrow, flat, and generally hairless, with a bluish-green coloration. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny white to pale blue flowers.

This plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.
