Acacia rupicola

Acacia rupicola, commonly known as the rock acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The plant produces flat, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds, which are a food source for various animals. Acacia rupicola thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat in its native ecosystems.
