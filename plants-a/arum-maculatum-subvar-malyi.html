Arum maculatum subvar. malyi

Arum maculatum subvar. malyi, commonly known as the Malyi Calla, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a distinctive, arrow-shaped leaf that is often adorned with intricate veining and a strikingly white, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by specific insect species, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.

The plant's ornamental value has led to its cultivation in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant in suitable climates.
