Aneilema taylorii

Aneilema taylorii, commonly known as the Taylor's arum, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of West Africa.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and a distinctive inflorescence that resembles a spadix surrounded by a showy spathe. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique floral structures and often dramatic appearance. Aneilema taylorii thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for collectors and botanical gardens.
