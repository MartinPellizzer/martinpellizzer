Agrostis littoralis

Agrostis littoralis, commonly known as saltgrass, is a perennial grass species native to coastal regions.

It thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found along shorelines and tidal flats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion.

Its adaptability to harsh coastal conditions makes it a resilient component of coastal ecosystems.
