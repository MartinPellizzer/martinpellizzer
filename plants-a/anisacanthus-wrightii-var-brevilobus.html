Anisacanthus wrightii var. brevilobus

Anisacanthus wrightii var. brevilobus, commonly known as the narrow-lobed flame flower, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, red-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
