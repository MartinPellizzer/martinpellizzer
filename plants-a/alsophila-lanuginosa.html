Alsophila lanuginosa

Alsophila lanuginosa, commonly known as the silky tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, light-green fronds that give it a delicate, ornamental appearance. The plant has a distinctive root system that forms a dense, mat-like structure at the base, which helps anchor it in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are bioluminescent in the dark, a unique feature that aids in attracting nocturnal pollinators.

Alsophila lanuginosa thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories where it plays a role in soil stabilization and biodiversity support.
