Armeriastrum tenuiflorum

Armeriastrum tenuiflorum, commonly known as narrow-flowered armeria, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
