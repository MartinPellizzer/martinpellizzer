Adiantum krameri

Adiantum krameri, commonly known as the Mexican maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lacy fronds that resemble those of other maidenhair ferns. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rock crevices. Its leaves are bipinnate, with fine, hair-like segments that give it a distinctive appearance.

Adiantum krameri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and thrives in humid conditions with well-drained soil.
