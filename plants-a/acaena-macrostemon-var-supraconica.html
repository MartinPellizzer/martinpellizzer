Acaena macrostemon var. supraconica

Acaena macrostemon var. supraconica, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, bright yellow flowers that appear in late spring. The plant produces spherical, berry-like fruits that are red when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
