Allium compactum

Allium compactum, commonly known as the globe onion or Egyptian onion, is a hardy perennial plant native to southern Europe and the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, rounded clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Allium compactum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
