Aspalathus longifolia

Aspalathus longifolia, commonly known as rooibos, is a shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and is typically found growing in sandy, well-drained soils. The plant is renowned for its unique ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it well-adapted to its native environment. Its flowers, which are usually pale yellow, appear in clusters and are followed by small, oval pods containing the seeds.

Aspalathus longifolia is most famous for its leaves, which are harvested and processed to produce the popular herbal tea known as rooibos.
