Aglaia elaeagnoidea var. bourdillonii

Aglaia elaeagnoidea var. bourdillonii, commonly known as the Bourdillon's aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural collections.
