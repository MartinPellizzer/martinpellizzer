Astragalus aphanassjievii

Astragalus aphanassjievii, commonly known as the Aphanassievii milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soils and contribute to soil stabilization.

Astragalus aphanassjievii is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
