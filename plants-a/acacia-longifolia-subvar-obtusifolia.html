Acacia longifolia subvar. obtusifolia

Acacia longifolia subvar. obtusifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and phyllodes that are narrow and oblong with a pointed tip. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical spikes, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and revegetation projects.

Native Distribution

Acacia longifolia subvar. obtusifolia is primarily native to southern Australia, including the states of South Australia, Victoria, and Tasmania.

It is commonly found in coastal areas, sandy soils, and open woodlands. This subspecies thrives in Mediterranean climates with dry summers and wet winters. It is often associated with coastal dunes and heathlands.

Its distribution is also observed in some parts of New Zealand, where it has been introduced.

Introduced Distribution

Acacia longifolia subvar. obtusifolia was mainly introduced to New Zealand.

It was brought there for its ornamental value and potential for soil stabilization. This subvariety is commonly found in coastal and urban areas due to its adaptability. It has since become naturalized and is now widespread across various habitats in the country.
