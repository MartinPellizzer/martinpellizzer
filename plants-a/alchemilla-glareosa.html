Alchemilla glareosa

Alchemilla glareosa, commonly known as the shining lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of silver-green, velvety leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla glareosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to attract pollinators.
