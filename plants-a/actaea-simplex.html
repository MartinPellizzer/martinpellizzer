Actaea simplex

Actaea simplex, commonly known as white cohosh, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Actaea simplex is valued for its ornamental appeal and has a long history of traditional medicinal use.
