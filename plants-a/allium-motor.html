Allium motor

Allium motor, commonly known as the common garlic.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plant produces a bulb composed of modified leaves called cloves, which are rich in sulfur compounds responsible for its pungent aroma. Allium motor thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in many climates.

Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and umbel-like clusters of white or pink flowers.
