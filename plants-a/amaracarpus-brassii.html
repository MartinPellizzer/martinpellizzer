Amaracarpus brassii

Amaracarpus brassii, commonly known as the golden tamarind, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible oil.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
