Amorphophallus eburneus

Amorphophallus eburneus, commonly known as the ivory root or elephant's tongue, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, umbrella-shaped leaves and a distinctive, often spiny stem. The plant produces a showy, white to pinkish inflorescence that resembles a giant flower. Its underground corm is thick and starchy, making it a valuable food source in some regions.

The plant is also known for its rapid growth and ability to regenerate from its corm after being cut or damaged.
