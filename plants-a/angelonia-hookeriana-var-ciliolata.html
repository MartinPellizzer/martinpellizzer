Angelonia hookeriana var. ciliolata

Angelonia hookeriana var. ciliolata, commonly known as the pink angelonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. This variety is prized for its long blooming season and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.

It is commonly used in garden landscapes and as a bedding plant due to its ornamental value and adaptability.
