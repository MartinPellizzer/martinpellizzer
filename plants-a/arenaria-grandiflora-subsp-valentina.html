Arenaria grandiflora subsp. valentina

Arenaria grandiflora subsp.

valentina, commonly known as Valentina's sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically features dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are adapted to arid environments, allowing it to store water efficiently. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after species for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
