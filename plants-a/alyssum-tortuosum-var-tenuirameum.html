Alyssum tortuosum var. tenuirameum

Alyssum tortuosum var. tenuirameum, commonly known as narrow-leaf alpine alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compact, cushion-like growth habit with dense clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to flourish in harsh environmental conditions.
