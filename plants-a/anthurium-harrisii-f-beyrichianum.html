Anthurium harrisii f. beyrichianum

Anthurium harrisii f. beyrichianum, commonly known as the red-veined anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with deep red veins contrasting against its dark green foliage. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-like inflorescences that are typically bright red or orange, surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in humid, warm environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse settings.
