Actinodaphne hirsuta

Actinodaphne hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy actinodaphne, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 15 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The species is valued for its aromatic wood and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
