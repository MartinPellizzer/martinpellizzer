Amaryllis entreriana

Amaryllis entreriana, commonly known as the Argentine amaryllis, is a bulbous plant native to South America.

It features striking, funnel-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem that bears multiple blooms during the growing season. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often emerging after the flowers have faded.

Amaryllis entreriana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
