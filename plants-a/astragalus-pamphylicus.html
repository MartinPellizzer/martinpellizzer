Astragalus pamphylicus

Astragalus pamphylicus, commonly known as the Pamphylian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native habitat.
