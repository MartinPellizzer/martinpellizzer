Anthyllis hamosa

Anthyllis hamosa, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountains of the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant has small, trifoliate leaves and produces legume pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to rocky and arid environments, often growing in open habitats with well-drained soils.

Anthyllis hamosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
