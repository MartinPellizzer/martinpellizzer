Aetheolaena senecioides

Aetheolaena senecioides, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and often have a bluish-green hue.

Aetheolaena senecioides plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
