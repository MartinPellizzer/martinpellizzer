Amelanchier laevis var. cordifolia

Amelanchier laevis var. cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which give it its botanical name, and its showy white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible berries in the summer, which are a food source for wildlife and humans. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and open fields.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and conservation efforts.
