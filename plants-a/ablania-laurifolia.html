Ablania laurifolia

Ablania laurifolia, commonly known as the laurel leaf ablania, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

Ablania laurifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
