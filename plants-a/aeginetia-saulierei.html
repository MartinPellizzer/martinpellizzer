Aeginetia saulierei

Aeginetia saulierei, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and showy, red bracts that surround its small white flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Aeginetia saulierei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
