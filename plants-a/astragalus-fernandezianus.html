Astragalus fernandezianus

Astragalus fernandezianus, commonly known as the Fernandez's milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. Native to specific regions in South America, this species thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility through its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
