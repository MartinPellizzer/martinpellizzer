Argillochloa

Argillochloa, commonly known as the clay grass, is a genus of grasses native to Australia.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in sandy or clay soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions. The plants typically have narrow, grass-like leaves and produce small, spike-like inflorescences. They are often found in coastal areas and are tolerant of salt spray.

Argillochloa species are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in stabilizing soils.
