Astrotricha cordata

Astrotricha cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf aster.

It is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States, typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, violet-blue flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Astrotricha cordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
