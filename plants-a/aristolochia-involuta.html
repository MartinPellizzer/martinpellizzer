Aristolochia involuta

Aristolochia involuta, commonly known as the Chinese horseshoe vine, is a climbing perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy texture and a strong, aromatic odor. The plant produces intricate, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a purple or brownish hue. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though some species are considered invasive in certain regions.
