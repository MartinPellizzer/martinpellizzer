Aporum uncatum

Aporum uncatum, commonly known as the cat's tail plant, is a unique species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cat-like tail-shaped inflorescence, which consists of numerous small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along riverbanks or in dense forests. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy texture, which helps it retain moisture in humid conditions.

Aporum uncatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unusual appearance.
