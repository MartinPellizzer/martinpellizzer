Amomum bifidum

Amomum bifidum, commonly known as the split amomum or wild cardamom, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by elongated, three-lobed fruits. The fruits are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their strong, aromatic flavor.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and culinary applications.
