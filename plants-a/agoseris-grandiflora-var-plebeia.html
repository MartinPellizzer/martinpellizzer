Agoseris grandiflora var. plebeia

Agoseris grandiflora var. plebeia, commonly known as the plebeian agoseris, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and grayish-green in color.

Its showy yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
