Abelmoschus strictus

Abelmoschus strictus, commonly known as the okra or lady's fingers, is a tropical flowering plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, ridged green pods that develop from yellow flowers. The plant has a bushy growth habit with hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Abelmoschus strictus is widely cultivated for its edible pods, which are used in various cuisines around the world.
