Annona spinescens var. nutans

Annona spinescens var. nutans, commonly known as the hanging custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that produces distinctive, fragrant flowers with five white petals. The fruit is a rounded, yellowish-green berry that ripens to a bright red or orange color. The plant has a smooth, greyish bark and is characterized by its spiny stems and leaves.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
