Amaryllis moreliana

Amaryllis moreliana, commonly known as the Brazilian amaryllis, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It features large, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with contrasting dark veins. The plant produces broad, strap-like leaves that are dark green and can grow up to 2 feet in length. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Amaryllis moreliana is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and ornamental value in gardens and indoor settings.
