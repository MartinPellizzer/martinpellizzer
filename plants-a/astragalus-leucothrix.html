Astragalus leucothrix

Astragalus leucothrix, commonly known as white milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to thrive in dry, harsh environments.

Astragalus leucothrix plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local insect populations.
