Arundinaria disticha

Arundinaria disticha, commonly known as the bamboo palm, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps.

Arundinaria disticha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
