Aerobion citratum

Aerobion citratum, commonly known as the citrate aerobion, is a unique plant species characterized by its specialized adaptation to nutrient-poor environments.

It possesses a distinctive root system that facilitates the uptake of citrate, allowing it to thrive in acidic soils where other plants struggle. The plant's leaves are narrow and slightly serrated, with a waxy surface that reduces water loss. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in shades of yellow and green, and it produces fruit that is both edible and aromatic.

This species is of particular interest to botanists due to its unique biochemical properties and ecological niche.
