Acanthophyllum laxiflorum

Acanthophyllum laxiflorum, commonly known as the spiny flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments, with thick leaves that store water and reduce transpiration. Its flowers are typically greenish or pale yellow, and it produces small, dry fruits that disperse seeds efficiently.

Acanthophyllum laxiflorum plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert fauna.
