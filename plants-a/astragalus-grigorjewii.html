Astragalus grigorjewii

Astragalus grigorjewii, commonly known as the Georgian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to temperate climates. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its scientific study contributes to the understanding of plant biodiversity in the region.
