Adenocarpus foliolosus

Adenocarpus foliolosus, commonly known as the leafy burr grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads. The plant produces distinctive burr-like fruits that aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animal fur. It typically grows in grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in dry to semi-arid conditions.
