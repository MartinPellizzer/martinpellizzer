Angolluma tubiformis

Angolluma tubiformis, commonly known as the tubular angolluma, is a rare and unique plant species native to the arid regions of central Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive tubular, succulent stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, pale green flowers during the cooler months, which are clustered at the tips of the stems. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

Angolluma tubiformis plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and nectar for native pollinators.
