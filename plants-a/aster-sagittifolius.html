Aster sagittifolius

Aster sagittifolius, commonly known as the arrowleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy, composite flower heads.

Aster sagittifolius is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
