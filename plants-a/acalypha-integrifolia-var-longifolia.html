Acalypha integrifolia var. longifolia

Acalypha integrifolia var. longifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silky fanflower, is a perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often hairy stems and palmately lobed leaves that are typically bright green with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are often surrounded by bracts, giving it a showy appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
