Aspidium goldieanum var. celsum

Aspidium goldieanum var. celsum, commonly known as the golden holly fern, is a fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its elegant, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are pinnate, with a distinctive golden hue on the underside, giving it its common name.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
