Achillea ptarmica

Achillea ptarmica, commonly known as lesser yarrow, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Achillea ptarmica is often used in herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
