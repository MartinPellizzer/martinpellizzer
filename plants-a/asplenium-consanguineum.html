Asplenium consanguineum

Asplenium consanguineum, commonly known as the red hakea, is a fern native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen habit and dark green, leathery fronds. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called soruses on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Asplenium consanguineum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
