Artemisia nitrosa subsp. obtusata

Artemisia nitrosa subsp.

obtusata, commonly known as the bluntleaf wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-gray foliage and narrow, linear leaves that are typically blunt at the tips.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
