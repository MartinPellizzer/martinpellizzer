Acalypha commersoniana var. obtusifolia

Acalypha commersoniana var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf ironweed, is a shrub native to South America.

It features palmate leaves with three to five lobes, often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are typically hidden within bracts. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
