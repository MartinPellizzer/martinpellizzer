Alyssum serpyllifolium var. metajnae

Alyssum serpyllifolium var. metajnae, commonly known as the Balkan wallflower, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, blooming from late spring to early autumn. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in appearance, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils. It is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as cliffs, slopes, and disturbed areas.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
