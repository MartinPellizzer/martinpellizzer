Achillea ptarmica var. godroniana

Achillea ptarmica var. godroniana, commonly known as Godron's yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is often found in dry, grassy areas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

This variant is noted for its compact growth habit and is cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
