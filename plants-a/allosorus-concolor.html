Allosorus concolor

Allosorus concolor, commonly known as the common tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes during the spring, which are followed by small, woody fruits. Allosorus concolor is often found in rocky soils and coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained conditions.

Its adaptability makes it a resilient plant in arid and semi-arid environments.
