Annona prestoei

Annona prestoei, commonly known as the Mexican custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is a small, evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 5 meters tall, with smooth, grayish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellowish fruits with a creamy, custard-like texture.

Its fruit is rich in nutrients and has a sweet, aromatic flavor, making it a valuable food source in its native habitat.
