Aconitum firmum nothosubsp. zapalowiczii

Aconitum firmum nothosubsp.

zapalowiczii, commonly known as the Zapalowiczii aconite, is a hybrid subspecies native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height.

The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a waxy texture, typical of the genus Aconitum.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
