Azalea nudiflora var. glandifera

Azalea nudiflora var. glandifera, commonly known as the glandular azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves that are often glandular-dotted, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
