Astragalus elazigensis

Astragalus elazigensis, commonly known as the Elazig milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Astragalus elazigensis is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its conservation status is of concern due to habitat loss and overharvesting.
