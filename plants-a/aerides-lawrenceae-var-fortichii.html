Aerides lawrenceae var. fortichii

Aerides lawrenceae var. fortichii, commonly known as the Fortichii Vanda, is a hybrid orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic growth habit. Its leaves are broad, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps in water retention.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
