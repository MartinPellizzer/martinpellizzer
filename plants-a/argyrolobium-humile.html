Argyrolobium humile

Argyrolobium humile, commonly known as the silver globular pea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, globular, silver-gray seed pods that contain several seeds. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
