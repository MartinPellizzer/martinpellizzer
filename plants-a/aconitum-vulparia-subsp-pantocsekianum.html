Aconitum vulparia subsp. pantocsekianum

Aconitum vulparia subsp.

pantocsekianum, commonly known as the Hungarian monkshood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is native to the Carpathian Mountains and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. It has palmately lobed leaves and produces showy, hood-shaped blooms.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
