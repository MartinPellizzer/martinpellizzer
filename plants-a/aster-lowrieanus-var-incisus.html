Aster lowrieanus var. incisus

Aster lowrieanus var. incisus, commonly known as the New Zealand aster, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically blue to purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins and streamside habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
