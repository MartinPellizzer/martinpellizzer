Acaena adscendens var. pilosiscapa

Acaena adscendens var. pilosiscapa, commonly known as the hairy burr bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth habit and produces small, spherical fruits that are adapted for seed dispersal by animals. The plant has compound leaves with multiple pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters.

The species is well adapted to coastal and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
