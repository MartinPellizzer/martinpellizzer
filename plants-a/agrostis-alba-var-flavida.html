Agrostis alba var. flavida

Agrostis alba var. flavida, commonly known as the yellow hairgrass, is a variant of the common hairgrass species.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant is characterized by its fine, silver-green leaves and distinctive yellowish inflorescence. It has small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are often tinged with yellow or golden hues.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
