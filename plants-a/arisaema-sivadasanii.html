Arisaema sivadasanii

Arisaema sivadasanii, commonly known as the Sivadasanii arisaema, is a unique member of the Araceae family native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its striking, hooded spathe and a spadix that bears both male and female flowers, typical of the genus Arisaema. The plant produces a single, large, leafy structure that supports its inflorescence, which is often covered in a dense cluster of tiny flowers. Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural study and conservation efforts.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
