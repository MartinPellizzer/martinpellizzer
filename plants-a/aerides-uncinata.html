Aerides uncinata

Aerides uncinata, commonly known as the hooked orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
