Acacia furcifera

Acacia furcifera, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically narrow and feathery, with a greyish-green color. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which bloom in spring and attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. Acacia furcifera is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

It is also valued for its hardy nature and is often used in revegetation projects.
