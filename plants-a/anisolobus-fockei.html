Anisolobus fockei

Anisolobus fockei, commonly known as the Fockei's cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white or pale blue spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its spiny exterior. It is well adapted to dry environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for succulent gardens and rockeries.
