Allium margaritaceum var. tenorei

Allium margaritaceum var. tenorei, commonly known as the Tenorei garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Italy.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color, and it produces small, white to pale purple flowers in umbels. The plant is closely related to other Allium species and is valued for its aromatic foliage and edible qualities. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value and culinary potential make it a popular choice among gardeners and foragers alike.
