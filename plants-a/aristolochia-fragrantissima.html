Aristolochia fragrantissima

Aristolochia fragrantissima, commonly known as the fragrant Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular, vine-like structure with heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, hooded flowers that are often purple or greenish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it contains toxic compounds that require caution.
