Amomum rugosum

Amomum rugosum, commonly known as the rough-shelled cardamom, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric, and is characterized by its aromatic rhizomes. The plant features tall, slender stems with lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a rough, textured surface. Its flowers are clustered in inflorescences and are typically greenish-white in color.

The rhizomes of Amomum rugosum are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to other members of its family.
