Achyranthes aspera var. viridescens

Achyranthes aspera var. viridescens, commonly known as greenleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in moist soils along stream banks, wetlands, and disturbed areas. The plant features upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a waxy coating. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or red, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
