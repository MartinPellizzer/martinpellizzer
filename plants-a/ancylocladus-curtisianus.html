Ancylocladus curtisianus

Ancylocladus curtisianus, commonly known as the Chinese cliff-rose, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces distinctive, woody fruits that contain two seeds. It thrives in rocky or coastal habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable in some regions.
