Argyreia populifolia var. africana

Argyreia populifolia var. africana, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is typically cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The species is also known for its climbing nature, often used as a decorative vine in gardens and landscapes.
