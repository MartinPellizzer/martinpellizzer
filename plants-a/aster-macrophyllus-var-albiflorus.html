Aster macrophyllus var. albiflorus

Aster macrophyllus var. albiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its large, showy white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture.

It blooms from late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
