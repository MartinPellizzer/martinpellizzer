Aster heteromallus

Aster heteromallus, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, purple to blue floral heads. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodlands, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are linear and often have a distinct serrated edge.

Aster heteromallus is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
