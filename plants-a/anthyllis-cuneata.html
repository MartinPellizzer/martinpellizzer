Anthyllis cuneata

Anthyllis cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Anthyllis cuneata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
