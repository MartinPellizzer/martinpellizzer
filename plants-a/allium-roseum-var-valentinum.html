Allium roseum var. valentinum

Allium roseum var. valentinum, commonly known as the Valentine's garlic or red garlic, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking purple to pink flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to gardens. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, rounded bulbs that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to perennial gardens.

This variety is particularly valued for its ornamental appeal and mild garlic-like aroma.
