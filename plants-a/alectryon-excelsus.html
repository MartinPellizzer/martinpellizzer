Alectryon excelsus

Alectryon excelsus, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. It produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed.

Alectryon excelsus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and urban landscapes.
