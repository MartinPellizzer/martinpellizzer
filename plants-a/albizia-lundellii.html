Albizia lundellii

Albizia lundellii, commonly known as the African siris, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its pinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to dry climates and is often found in savannas and woodland areas. It has a shallow root system and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Albizia lundellii also plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
