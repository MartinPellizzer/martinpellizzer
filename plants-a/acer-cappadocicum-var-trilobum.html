Acer cappadocicum var. trilobum

Acer cappadocicum var. trilobum, commonly known as the Cappadocian maple, is a deciduous tree native to the Cappadocia region of Turkey.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are deeply lobed and give it a unique appearance. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming more fissured with age.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
