Austromuellera valida

Austromuellera valida, commonly known as the coastal hakea, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and produces vibrant red or yellow flowers during the spring and summer seasons. The plant is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in dune ecosystems, showcasing its resilience to coastal conditions. Its flowering pattern attracts various pollinators, including birds and insects, contributing to local biodiversity.

Austromuellera valida plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and preventing erosion.
