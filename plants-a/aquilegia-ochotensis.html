Aquilegia ochotensis

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes other well-known genera such as Ranunculus and Delphinium. The plant is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with deeply lobed segments, and it thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil.
