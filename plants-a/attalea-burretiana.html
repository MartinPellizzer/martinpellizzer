Attalea burretiana

Attalea burretiana, commonly known as the Burret palm, is a species of palm native to the Amazon Basin.

It is a tall, slender palm with a straight trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 5 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elongated, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in swampy or wetland areas.

The Burret palm is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential ecological and economic uses.
