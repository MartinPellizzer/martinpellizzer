Amsinckia palustris

Amsinckia palustris, commonly known as the marsh milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America, particularly found in wetland and marsh habitats.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 60 cm tall, featuring slender, branching stems and heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from June to August. Its root system is adapted to waterlogged soils, allowing it to thrive in moist environments.

Amsinckia palustris plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
