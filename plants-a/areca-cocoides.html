Areca cocoides

Areca cocoides, commonly known as the areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery, compound leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that eventually develop into three-seeded berries. Areca cocoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its adaptability to warm, humid climates makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
