Allium leucanthum var. tridentatum

Allium leucanthum var. tridentatum, commonly known as the Tridentate Garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, rounded bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, with a strong garlic scent.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and aromatic properties.
