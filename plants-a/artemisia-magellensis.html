Artemisia magellensis

Artemisia magellensis, commonly known as Magellanic wormwood, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has aromatic, silver-gray foliage with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer.

Artemisia magellensis is often used in traditional medicine and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
