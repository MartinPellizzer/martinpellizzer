Aspidium simplex

Aspidium simplex, commonly known as the common water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or near water sources. Its rhizomes are creeping and help in the propagation of the plant.

Aspidium simplex is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture.
