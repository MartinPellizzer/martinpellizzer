Anthurium citrifolium

Anthurium citrifolium, commonly known as the lemon-leaf anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are a vibrant green with a waxy texture and a yellowish underside. The plant produces striking spathes that can range in color from white to red, often with a contrasting spadix. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect light to flourish.

Anthurium citrifolium is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and unique floral structures.
