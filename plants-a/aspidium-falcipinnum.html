Aspidium falcipinnum

Aspidium falcipinnum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the family Water Ferns.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lanceolate fronds that are typically submerged in water. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Aspidium falcipinnum plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen through photosynthesis.
