Avenastrum pratense var. hirtifolium

Avenastrum pratense var. hirtifolium, commonly known as hairy leaf field grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in open, grassy habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, erect stems with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, bearing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The variety hirtifolium is distinguished by its dense pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance and aiding in its identification.
