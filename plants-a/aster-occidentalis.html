Aster occidentalis

Aster occidentalis, commonly known as the western aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its showy, violet to blue flowers bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster occidentalis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
