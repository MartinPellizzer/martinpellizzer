Artocarpus lamellosus

Artocarpus lamellosus, commonly known as the banyan fig, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Moraceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive fig-like fruits. The tree can grow quite large, often developing aerial roots that form a dense network around its trunk. Its fruits are typically eaten by various animals, contributing to seed dispersal.
