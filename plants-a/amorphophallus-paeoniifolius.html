Amorphophallus paeoniifolius

Amorphophallus paeoniifolius, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, which float on the surface of water. The plant produces striking, purple flowers that bloom in the evening and emit a strong odor resembling rotting meat. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats and is often found in marshes and ponds.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is a notable species in wetland ecosystems.
