Acacia sublanata

Acacia sublanata, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact globular heads, which are followed by flat, leathery pods containing seeds. Acacia sublanata thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
