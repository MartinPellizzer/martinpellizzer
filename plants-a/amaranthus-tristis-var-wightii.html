Amaranthus tristis var. wightii

Amaranthus tristis var. wightii, commonly known as the slender amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender stems that are often branched. Its foliage is usually green to reddish in color, and it produces seeds in small, capsule-like structures.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value or as a forage crop.
