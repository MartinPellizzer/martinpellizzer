Aristida browniana

Aristida browniana, commonly known as the Brown's threeawn, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in restoration projects for stabilizing disturbed soils.
