Althaea apterocarpa var. lilacina

Althaea apterocarpa var. lilacina, commonly known as the lilac-flowered marshmallow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, pale lilac or pink flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has thick, fleshy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture. Its roots are rich in mucilage, which has been traditionally used in herbal remedies.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in moist, shady environments.
