Achyrophorus quitensis var. sonchoides

Achyrophorus quitensis var. sonchoides, commonly known as the Andean groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its leaves are hairy and arranged in a basal rosette, while its yellow flowers bloom in the summer months.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
