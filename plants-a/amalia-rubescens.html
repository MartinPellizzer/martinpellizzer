Amalia rubescens

Amalia rubescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed amalia, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Amalia rubescens prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value lies in its distinctive coloration and delicate floral display.
