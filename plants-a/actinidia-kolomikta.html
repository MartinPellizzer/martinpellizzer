Actinidia kolomikta

Actinidia kolomikta, commonly known as the fragrant honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated leaves that display white, pink, and green patterns during the growing season. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that mature to a red or orange color. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and attractive fruit.

Actinidia kolomikta is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
