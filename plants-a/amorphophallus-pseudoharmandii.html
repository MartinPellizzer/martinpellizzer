Amorphophallus pseudoharmandii

Amorphophallus pseudoharmandii, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a striking member of the Araceae family native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in diameter, making it a dramatic addition to any garden. Its flowering structure consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the Araceae family. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly valued in both horticultural and conservation contexts.
