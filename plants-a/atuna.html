Atuna

Atuna, commonly known as the "bitter apple" or "sour apple," is a small, deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus *Pyracantha* within the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, orange-yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red, berry-like fruits that are typically tart and used in culinary applications. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Atuna is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
