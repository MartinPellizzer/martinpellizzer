Acalypha boehmerioides var. glandulosa

Acalypha boehmerioides var. glandulosa, commonly known as the stickyweed or glandular acalypha, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, which give it a sticky texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptic in shape and may have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats or along stream banks.
