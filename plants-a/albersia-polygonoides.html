Albersia polygonoides

Albersia polygonoides, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
