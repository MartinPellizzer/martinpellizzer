Acacia consanguinea

Acacia consanguinea, commonly known as the red acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and its compound leaves, which are typically bipinnate. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular heads, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Acacia consanguinea thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

It is also known for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of browse for livestock.
