Anacis

Anacis, commonly known as the marsh woundwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in umbels. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Anacis has finely divided, feathery leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
