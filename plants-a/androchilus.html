Androchilus

Androchilus, commonly known as the orchid genus, is a group of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are characterized by their intricate, often showy flowers with specialized structures such as labellum and column. They are predominantly found in tropical and subtropical regions, with a diverse range of species exhibiting various morphological adaptations. Androchilus species typically have pseudobulbs and are epiphytic or lithophytic, growing on trees or rocks.

Their botanical significance lies in their ornamental value and ecological roles in their native habitats.
