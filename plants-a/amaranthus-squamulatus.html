Amaranthus squamulatus

Amaranthus squamulatus, commonly known as the velvetleaf amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Amaranthus squamulatus is known for its adaptability and can thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
