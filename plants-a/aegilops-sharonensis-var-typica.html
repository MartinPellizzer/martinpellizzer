Aegilops sharonensis var. typica

Aegilops sharonensis var. typica, commonly known as Sharon grass, is a wild wheat relative native to the Levant region.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow leaves and spikelets that bear small, pale florets. This species is notable for its genetic diversity and potential agricultural value, particularly in breeding programs for improved wheat varieties. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

The plant plays a role in ecological systems and has been studied for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
