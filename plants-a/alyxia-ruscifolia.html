Alyxia ruscifolia

Alyxia ruscifolia, commonly known as the waxleaf alyxia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers during the spring and summer months. Alyxia ruscifolia thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and showy blooms, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
