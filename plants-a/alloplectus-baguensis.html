Alloplectus baguensis

Alloplectus baguensis, commonly known as the Guianan arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Alloplectaceae and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves, which are typically alternate and lanceolate. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are often borne on slender pedicels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and lesser-known species within its native range.
