Amphithalea villosa var. brevifolia

Amphithalea villosa var. brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved hairy amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features a tall, slender stem that bears clusters of vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are broad, fleshy, and covered in a fine, hairy texture, giving it its "hairy" common name. This variety is known for its rapid growth and ability to produce multiple blooms in a single season.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking bold, colorful displays.
