Arctotis spathulata

Arctotis spathulata, commonly known as the broadleaf arctotis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Arctotis spathulata is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
