Acacia leucocephala

Acacia leucocephala, commonly known as the white-headed acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are divided into numerous small leaflets, and its distinctive yellow flowers that appear in dense, globular clusters. The plant produces flat, elongated pods that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Acacia leucocephala is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a source of fodder for livestock.
