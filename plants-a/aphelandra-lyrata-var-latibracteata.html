Aphelandra lyrata var. latibracteata

Aphelandra lyrata var. latibracteata, commonly known as the broad-bract candleplant, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces striking, pendulous inflorescences with vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a decorative plant.
