Acrophorus stipellatus

Acrophorus stipellatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in clusters. Its fruits are pod-like structures that contain several seeds.
