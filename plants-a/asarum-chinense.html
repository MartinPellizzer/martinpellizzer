Asarum chinense

Asarum chinense, commonly known as Chinese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves. The plant produces distinctive, hooded flowers that are typically greenish-brown in color. Its rhizomes are used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported medicinal properties.

Asarum chinense thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
