Aster hispidus

Aster hispidus, commonly known as the hairy aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces daisy-like flowers with purple or pink petals, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. Aster hispidus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies, meadows, and open woodlands.

It is a valuable plant for pollinators, providing nectar and pollen to bees and butterflies.
