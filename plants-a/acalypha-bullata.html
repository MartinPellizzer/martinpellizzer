Acalypha bullata

Acalypha bullata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or woolly cupplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Acalypha bullata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its unique foliage.
