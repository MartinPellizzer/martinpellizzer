Aralia dasyphylla var. latifolia

Aralia dasyphylla var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf aralia, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 feet tall, featuring compound leaves with large, palmately arranged leaflets. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in umbel-like clusters, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds.

Its ornamental value comes from its lush foliage and seasonal color changes, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
