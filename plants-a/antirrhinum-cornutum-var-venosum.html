Antirrhinum cornutum var. venosum

Antirrhinum cornutum var. venosum, commonly known as the veiny toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinct venation pattern, which gives it its varietal name.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
