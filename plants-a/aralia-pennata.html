Aralia pennata

Aralia pennata, commonly known as the white aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves that are pinnately arranged, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbel-like clusters during the summer months. Its berries are red and edible, though typically not consumed by humans.

Aralia pennata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
