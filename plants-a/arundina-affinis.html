Arundina affinis

Arundina affinis, commonly known as the bamboo orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are arranged in inflorescences. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette at the base.

Arundina affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
