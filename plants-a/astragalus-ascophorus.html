Astragalus ascophorus

Astragalus ascophorus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the western United States and parts of Canada, typically found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant has slender, upright stems with alternate, narrow, pinnately compound leaves. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually pink to purple in color and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Astragalus ascophorus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
