Arnica lonchophylla subsp. genuina

Arnica lonchophylla subsp.

genuina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant produces yellow-orange flowers with tubular corollas, which bloom from June to August. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic compounds.
