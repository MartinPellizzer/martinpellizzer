Anthyllis tirolensis

Anthyllis tirolensis, commonly known as the Tirolensis Anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically produces pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Anthyllis tirolensis plays a role in stabilizing alpine ecosystems and provides nectar for pollinators.
