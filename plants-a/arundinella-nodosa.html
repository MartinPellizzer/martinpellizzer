Arundinella nodosa

Arundinella nodosa, commonly known as the nodding reed, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, nodding flower heads. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its rhizomes are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

Arundinella nodosa plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
