Acinodendron gilvum

Acinodendron gilvum, commonly known as the golden acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

The bark and leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
