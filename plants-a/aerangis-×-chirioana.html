Aerangis × chirioana

Aerangis × chirioana, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a cross between Aerangis and Chirita, resulting in a unique blend of characteristics from both parent species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions, typically found in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns on the petals. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in well-draining soil with high humidity.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its beauty and fragrance.
