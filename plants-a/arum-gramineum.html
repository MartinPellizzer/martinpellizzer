Arum gramineum

Arum gramineum, commonly known as the arrow-leaved arum, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features arrow-shaped leaves that are typically green with a prominent midrib. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in moist, shaded environments.
