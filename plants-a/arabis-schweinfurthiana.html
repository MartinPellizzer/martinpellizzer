Arabis schweinfurthiana

Arabis schweinfurthiana, commonly known as the Swiss rock cress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of elongated, toothed leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh weather conditions.

Arabis schweinfurthiana is also noted for its role in supporting local pollinators.
