Acer mono f. pulvigerum

Acer mono f. pulvigerum, commonly known as the red maple variant with pulvigerous leaves, is a unique form of the red maple tree.

It is characterized by its striking red foliage, which becomes more vibrant as the seasons change. The leaves are typically smaller and more delicate compared to the standard red maple, with a distinctive red or reddish-brown coloration on the underside. This cultivar is often prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of growing conditions.
