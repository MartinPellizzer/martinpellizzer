Aristolochia nardiana

Aristolochia nardiana, commonly known as the narrowleaf Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular, maroon-colored flowers that resemble a pipe, which are typically borne on slender stems. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors and stream banks.

Due to its striking appearance and ecological importance, it is often found in conservation efforts aimed at preserving native plant diversity.
