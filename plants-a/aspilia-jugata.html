Aspilia jugata

Aspilia jugata, commonly known as the white candelabra, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, umbrella-shaped inflorescence composed of numerous white, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Aspilia jugata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
