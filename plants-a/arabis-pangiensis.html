Arabis pangiensis

Arabis pangiensis, commonly known as the Pangi's rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it forms low, mat-like growths. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and often appear in clusters.

Arabis pangiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
