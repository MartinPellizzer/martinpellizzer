Arenaria davurica

Arenaria davurica, commonly known as the alpine sandwort, is a low-growing flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It thrives in rocky and sandy soils, often found in alpine and subalpine environments. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually succulent and adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

Arenaria davurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
