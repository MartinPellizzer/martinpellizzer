Aechmea brachycaulis

Aechmea brachycaulis, commonly known as the pineapple plant or the orchid pineapple, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater for the plant's use. The plant's inflorescence consists of a tall, spiky flower stem that bears showy, colorful bracts, often in red, orange, or yellow. Aechmea brachycaulis thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is popular as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea brachycaulis has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, fleshy, and arranged in a tight spiral, with a dark green color and a waxy texture.

The plant lacks a true stem, instead having a short, thick, and often underground stem with a rough, fibrous bark. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful bract, with small, white to pink flowers that bloom during the rainy season and are pollinated by insects. The fruit is a berry that contains numerous small, hard seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring the plant in the soil, with some roots also contributing to water storage.
