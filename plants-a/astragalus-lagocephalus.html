Astragalus lagocephalus

Astragalus lagocephalus, commonly known as the mountain milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils. It plays a role in preventing soil erosion and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its root system is deep, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.
