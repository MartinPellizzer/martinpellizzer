Alyssum reverchonii

Alyssum reverchonii, commonly known as the Sierra foothill alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant features hairy, narrow leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It thrives in full sun and is adapted to dry, Mediterranean climates.

Alyssum reverchonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
