Astragalus carmanicus

Astragalus carmanicus, commonly known as Carmine milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus carmanicus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native range.
