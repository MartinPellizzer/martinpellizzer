Alloteropsis quintasii

Alloteropsis quintasii, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with a rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. Its succulent leaves and deep root system allow it to thrive in harsh, dry environments.

Alloteropsis quintasii is well adapted to desert conditions, making it a resilient component of its ecosystem.
