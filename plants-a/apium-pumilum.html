Apium pumilum

Apium pumilum, commonly known as the small celery or dwarf celery, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like carrots and parsley. The plant has slender, hollow stems and small, fern-like leaves that are often aromatic. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Apium pumilum is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.
