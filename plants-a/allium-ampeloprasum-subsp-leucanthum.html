Allium ampeloprasum subsp. leucanthum

Allium ampeloprasum subsp.

leucanthum, commonly known as the giant garlic or leucanthum garlic, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, robust stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height.

The plant produces large, bulbous structures composed of layered, fleshy scales, similar to other members of the Allium genus. Its leaves are broad, flat, and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in umbels, and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
