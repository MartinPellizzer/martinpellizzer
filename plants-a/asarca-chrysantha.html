Asarca chrysantha

Asarca chrysantha, commonly known as golden sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in fine hairs.

Asarca chrysantha is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
