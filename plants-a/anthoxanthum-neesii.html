Anthoxanthum neesii

Anthoxanthum neesii, commonly known as the narrowleaf brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, bearing small, two-lobed flowers.

Anthoxanthum neesii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for livestock.
