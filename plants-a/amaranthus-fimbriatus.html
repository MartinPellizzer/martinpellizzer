Amaranthus fimbriatus

Amaranthus fimbriatus, commonly known as the fringed amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely fringed leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in disturbed areas such as fields and roadsides.

The seeds of Amaranthus fimbriatus are small and can be dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
