Aster forsteri

Aster forsteri, commonly known as the Forster's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It produces showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster forsteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
