Artemisia alba subsp. lobelii

Artemisia alba subsp.

lobelii, commonly known as white sage, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which consists of aromatic, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flower clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been used traditionally in various cultural and medicinal practices.
