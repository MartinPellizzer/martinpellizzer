Astragalus graecus

Astragalus graecus, commonly known as Greek milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds, typical of the genus Astragalus. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Astragalus graecus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
