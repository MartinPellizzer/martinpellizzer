Acacia obscura var. moiriana

Acacia obscura var. moiriana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and compound leaves composed of numerous small, linear leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are arranged in cylindrical spikes. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and can tolerate drought conditions.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid environments.
