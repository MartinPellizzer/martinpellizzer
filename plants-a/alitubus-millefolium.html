Alitubus millefolium

Alitubus millefolium, commonly known as the thousand-leaf plant, is a herbaceous species characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the family. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Alitubus millefolium thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
