Anthyllis vulneraria subsp. lapponica

Anthyllis vulneraria subsp.

lapponica, commonly known as the Lapland bugloss, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and sandy soils.

The plant features small, blue to violet flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.
