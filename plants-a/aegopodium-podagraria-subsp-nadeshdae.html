Aegopodium podagraria subsp. nadeshdae

Aegopodium podagraria subsp.

nadeshdae, commonly known as the Persian cow parsley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
