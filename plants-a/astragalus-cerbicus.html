Astragalus cerbicus

Astragalus cerbicus, commonly known as the mountain milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus cerbicus is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
