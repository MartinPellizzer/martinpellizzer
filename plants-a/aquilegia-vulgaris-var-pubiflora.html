Aquilegia vulgaris var. pubiflora

Aquilegia vulgaris var. pubiflora, commonly known as the pubescent columbine, is a variant of the common columbine characterized by its hairy, pubescent petals.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features compound, palmately divided leaves and produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and butterflies, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

It is also noted for its ability to thrive in partial shade and well-drained soil.
