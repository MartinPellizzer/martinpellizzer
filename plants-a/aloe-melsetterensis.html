Aloe melsetterensis

Aloe melsetterensis, commonly known as the Melsetter aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy. The leaves are typically blue-green in color with a waxy coating, which helps the plant retain moisture in arid conditions. It produces clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
