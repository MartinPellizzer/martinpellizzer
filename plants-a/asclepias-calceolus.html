Asclepias calceolus

Asclepias calceolus, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct scent. It belongs to the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is known for its milky sap, which contains latex.

Asclepias calceolus plays an important role in supporting monarch butterfly populations by providing nectar and serving as a host plant for their larvae.
