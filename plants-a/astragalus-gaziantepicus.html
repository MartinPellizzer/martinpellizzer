Astragalus gaziantepicus

Astragalus gaziantepicus, commonly known as Gaziantep milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.

Its root system plays a crucial role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
