Adina rubescens

Adina rubescens, commonly known as red adina, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and reddish underneath, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals but not typically consumed by humans. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is cultivated in tropical gardens and botanical collections.
