Argemone albiflora

Argemone albiflora, commonly known as white-flowered argemone, is a flowering plant native to regions of North America.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, ribbed stems. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain oily seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
