Alyssum spathulifolium

Alyssum spathulifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb with compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are broad, oval, and slightly hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Alyssum spathulifolium is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
