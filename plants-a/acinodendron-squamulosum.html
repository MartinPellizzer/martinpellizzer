Acinodendron squamulosum

Acinodendron squamulosum, commonly known as the scaly acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves that are often covered in a fine, scaly texture, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand prolonged drought conditions.

The seeds are enclosed in pod-like fruits, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
