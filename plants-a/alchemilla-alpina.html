Alchemilla alpina

Alchemilla alpina, commonly known as mountain lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, velvety leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Alchemilla alpina is often used in rock gardens and is valued for its ornamental foliage and hardiness.
