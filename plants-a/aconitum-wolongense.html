Aconitum wolongense

Aconitum wolongense, commonly known as Wolong aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and showy, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces distinctive, helmet-shaped seed pods that contain numerous small, black seeds. Aconitum wolongense is known for its toxic alkaloids, which make it highly poisonous to both humans and animals.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows at high elevations.
