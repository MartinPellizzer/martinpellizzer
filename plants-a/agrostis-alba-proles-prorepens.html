Agrostis alba proles prorepens

Agrostis alba proles prorepens, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing plant with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in moist, temperate environments, often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its botanical features include fine, silvery-green foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland areas.
