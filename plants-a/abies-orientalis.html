Abies orientalis

Abies orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern Hemlock, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and its distinctive, flat, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its slow growth and long lifespan, it plays an important role in forest ecosystems.
