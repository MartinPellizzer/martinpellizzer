Achillea lucida

Achillea lucida, commonly known as white yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its flat, white or pale yellow composite flower heads. The plant features finely divided, silver-gray foliage that is aromatic and often used in herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its historical use dates back to ancient times, where it was valued for its medicinal properties.
