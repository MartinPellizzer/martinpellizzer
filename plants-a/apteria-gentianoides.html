Apteria gentianoides

Apteria gentianoides, commonly known as the slender gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in alpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a prominent central vein.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
