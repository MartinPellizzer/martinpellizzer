Aspidium pilosum

Aspidium pilosum, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its reproductive structures include sori, which are clusters of sporangia found on the underside of the fronds.

Aspidium pilosum is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
