Aquilegia vulgaris subf. magellensis

Aquilegia vulgaris subf.

magellensis, commonly known as the Magellan's columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America. It features showy, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple, with five sepals that are often brightly colored.

The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
