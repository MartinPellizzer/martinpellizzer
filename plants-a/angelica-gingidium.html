Angelica gingidium

Angelica gingidium, commonly known as Indian angelica, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas and parts of South Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, which are typical of the family. Its roots are often harvested for their aromatic properties and are used in traditional medicine.

Angelica gingidium is also valued in culinary applications for its distinctive, licorice-like flavor.
