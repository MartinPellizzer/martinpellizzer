Aristea kitchingii

Aristea kitchingii, commonly known as the Kitching's arid grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale blue to purple flowers, which bloom during the cooler seasons. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its hardy nature, Aristea kitchingii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
