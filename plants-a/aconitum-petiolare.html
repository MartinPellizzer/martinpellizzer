Aconitum petiolare

Aconitum petiolare, commonly known as the petioled monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has palmately divided, compound leaves that are deeply lobed and have a distinctive petiole. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aconitum petiolare contains potent alkaloids, making it toxic and requiring caution when handling.
