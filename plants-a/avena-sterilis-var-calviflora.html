Avena sterilis var. calviflora

Avena sterilis var. calviflora, commonly known as the slender oat, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear reduced, often sterile flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces a distinctive, cylindrical seed head.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
