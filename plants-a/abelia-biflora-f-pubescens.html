Abelia biflora f. pubescens

Abelia biflora f. pubescens, commonly known as the downy abelia, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has hairy or pubescent leaves and stems, giving it a soft, textured appearance. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This ornamental plant is valued for its attractive foliage and long blooming period.
