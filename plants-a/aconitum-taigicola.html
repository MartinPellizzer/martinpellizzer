Aconitum taigicola

Aconitum taigicola, commonly known as the Taiga Aconite, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely leafed stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, conical inflorescences. Its roots contain potent alkaloids, making it toxic and requiring caution during handling.

Aconitum taigicola thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas and along stream banks.
