Acianthera alphonsii

Acianthera alphonsii, commonly known as the Alphonsus orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces pale green to yellowish flowers with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
