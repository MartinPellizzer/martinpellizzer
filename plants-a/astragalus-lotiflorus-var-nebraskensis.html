Astragalus lotiflorus var. nebraskensis

Astragalus lotiflorus var. nebraskensis, commonly known as the Nebraska milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie habitats. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its role in ecological restoration and soil stabilization.
