Acaulimalva betonicifolia

Acaulimalva betonicifolia, commonly known as the betony-leaved acaulimalva, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a short, often woody stem with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive reddish or purplish hue. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, usually in shades of pink or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and herbal gardens.
