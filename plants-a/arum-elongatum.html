Arum elongatum

Arum elongatum, commonly known as the elongated arum, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and showy, spadix-bearing inflorescence.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which includes many ornamental and economically important plants. The plant's unique structure consists of a spadix, a fleshy spike of tiny flowers, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Arum elongatum is native to the wetlands and marshes of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for tropical gardens and greenhouse cultivation.
