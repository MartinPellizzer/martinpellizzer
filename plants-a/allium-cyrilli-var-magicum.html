Allium cyrilli var. magicum

Allium cyrilli var. magicum, commonly known as the Magic Onion, is a striking ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, globe-shaped bulbs that produce tall, slender stems topped with clusters of lavender-pink or purple flowers. The plant is known for its vibrant, showy blooms that appear in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of long, strap-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil and full sun.
