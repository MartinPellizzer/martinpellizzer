Amaracus rotundifolius

Amaracus rotundifolius, commonly known as the round-leaf amaracus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in shaded forest understories. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, used traditionally in herbal remedies.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
