Abutilon ochsenii

Abutilon ochsenii, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, grayish-green leaves and showy, lantern-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid environments. Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Abutilon ochsenii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
