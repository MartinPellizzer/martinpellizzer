Anabasis turgaica

Anabasis turgaica, commonly known as the Turkish sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are often covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss. It is adapted to thrive in sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to harsh environments.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
