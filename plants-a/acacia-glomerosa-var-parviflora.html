Acacia glomerosa var. parviflora

Acacia glomerosa var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellow, globular flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous tiny, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
