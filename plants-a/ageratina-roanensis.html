Ageratina roanensis

Ageratina roanensis, commonly known as Roan Mountain blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Ageratina roanensis is often found in the Appalachian region, particularly in Tennessee and North Carolina.
