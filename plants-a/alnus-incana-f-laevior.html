Alnus incana f. laevior

Alnus incana f. laevior, commonly known as the smooth alder, is a shrub or small tree native to northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish catkins that are typically male, while female flowers are found on the same plant. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and forest edges.

This variety is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
