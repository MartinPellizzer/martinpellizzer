Alstroemeria magnifica subsp. magnifica

Alstroemeria magnifica subsp.

magnifica, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile. It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors, including red, orange, and yellow.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features long, narrow leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

The botanical features of this subspecies include its showy inflorescences and its ability to produce numerous blooms throughout the growing season.
