Aristolochia wankeana

Aristolochia wankeana, commonly known as the Wanke's Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically dark purple or brown in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its aromatic, often sweet-smelling blooms. Its leaves are heart-shaped and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length.

The plant is also notable for containing aristolochic acids, which are toxic to humans and animals.
