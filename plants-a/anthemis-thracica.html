Anthemis thracica

Anthemis thracica, commonly known as the Thrace goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its yellow flowers, which bloom in the summer, are composed of both ray and disk florets, typical of the family.

Anthemis thracica is also noted for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
