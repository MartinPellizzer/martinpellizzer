Astrochlaena nogalensis

Astrochlaena nogalensis, commonly known as the Nogalensis Astrophytum, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, thick stem that is often covered in a white, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. Its leaves are modified into spines, which provide protection from herbivores and help in reducing transpiration.

Astrochlaena nogalensis is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for desert gardens.
