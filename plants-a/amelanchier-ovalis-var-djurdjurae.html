Amelanchier ovalis var. djurdjurae

Amelanchier ovalis var. djurdjurae, commonly known as the Djurdjura serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 8 meters, featuring a rounded or irregular crown with dark green, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of white to pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, dark blue to black berries in late summer. Its leaves turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn, making it a notable ornamental plant.

The species is adapted to well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
