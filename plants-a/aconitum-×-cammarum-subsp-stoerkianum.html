Aconitum × cammarum subsp. stoerkianum

Aconitum × cammarum subsp.

stoerkianum, commonly known as the Stoerk's monkshood, is a hybrid species within the genus Aconitum. It is characterized by its striking blue or purple spathaceous flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins and a tall, erect stem.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate soil and moisture conditions.
