Annesorhiza teretifolia

Annesorhiza teretifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf arrowhead, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and elongated. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
