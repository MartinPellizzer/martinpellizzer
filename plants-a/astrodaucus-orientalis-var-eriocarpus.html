Astrodaucus orientalis var. eriocarpus

Astrodaucus orientalis var. eriocarpus, commonly known as the wooly fruit astrodaucus, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in compound umbels. Its fruits are distinctive, covered in a dense layer of fine, woolly hairs, giving it the common name.

This species is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes.
