Arundinaria nikkomontana

Arundinaria nikkomontana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a perennial grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Arundinaria nikkomontana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Japanese gardens.
