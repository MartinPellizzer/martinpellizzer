Andropogon imerinensis

Andropogon imerinensis, commonly known as the Florida panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and swampy areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
