Aster kirghisorum

Aster kirghisorum, commonly known as the Kirghiz aster, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, violet or blue flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Aster kirghisorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
