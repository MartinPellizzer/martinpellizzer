Aponogeton lucens

Aponogeton lucens, commonly known as the golden duckweed, is a small aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tiny, floating leaves that are typically golden or yellow-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged or just at the water's surface. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in ponds and marshes.

Aponogeton lucens is popular in aquascaping due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of cultivation.
