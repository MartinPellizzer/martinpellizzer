Astragalus angustifolius subsp. postianus

Astragalus angustifolius subsp.

postianus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands.

The plant features slender, branched stems and small, compound leaves with a narrow, linear shape. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to cream-colored, arranged in dense spikes, and are adapted for pollination by insects.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
