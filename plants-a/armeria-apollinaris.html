Armeria apollinaris

Armeria apollinaris, commonly known as the Holy Thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Amaryllidaceae and is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Armeria apollinaris is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
