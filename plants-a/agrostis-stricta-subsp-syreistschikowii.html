Agrostis stricta subsp. syreistschikowii

Agrostis stricta subsp.

syreistschikowii, commonly known as the Siberian bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to cold climates in Asia. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subarctic regions.

The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
