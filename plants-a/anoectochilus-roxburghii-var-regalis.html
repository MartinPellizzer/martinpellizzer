Anoectochilus roxburghii var. regalis

Anoectochilus roxburghii var. regalis, commonly known as the royal toothed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern Himalayas and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, toothed leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. It has a short rhizome and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Due to its ornamental value and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
