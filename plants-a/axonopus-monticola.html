Axonopus monticola

Axonopus monticola, commonly known as the mountain axonopus, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant has slender, tufted shoots with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flower clusters.

Axonopus monticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland ecosystems.
