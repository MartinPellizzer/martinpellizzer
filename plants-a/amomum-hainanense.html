Amomum hainanense

Amomum hainanense, commonly known as Hainan cardamom, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Hainan Island in China.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clustered, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by three-lobed, dark brown fruits containing aromatic seeds. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found in lowland forests.
