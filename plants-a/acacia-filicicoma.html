Acacia filicicoma

Acacia filicicoma, commonly known as the fern-leaf acacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are often mistaken for ferns due to their delicate, feather-like appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, globular clusters, followed by flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems and waxy leaves that reduce water loss.

Acacia filicicoma is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fodder for livestock.
