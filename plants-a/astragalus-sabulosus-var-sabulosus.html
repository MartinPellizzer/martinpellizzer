Astragalus sabulosus var. sabulosus

Astragalus sabulosus var. sabulosus, commonly known as the sand astragalus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow to pale purple flowers in late spring. It is often found in disturbed sites such as roadsides and dry fields.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
