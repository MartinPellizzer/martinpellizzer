Adiantum culantrillo

Adiantum culantrillo, commonly known as the Mexican maidenhair fern, is a species native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that resemble the shape of a feather. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with each segment bearing small, tooth-like projections.

Adiantum culantrillo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
