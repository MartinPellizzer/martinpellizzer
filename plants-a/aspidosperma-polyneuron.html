Aspidosperma polyneuron

Aspidosperma polyneuron, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or copaiba, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release winged seeds.

The tree is valued for its aromatic resin, which has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
