Aeschynomene praticola

Aeschynomene praticola, commonly known as smallleaf aeschynomene, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and roadsides. It is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules.

Aeschynomene praticola is also valued for its potential use in forage and soil improvement due to its hardy nature and rapid growth.
