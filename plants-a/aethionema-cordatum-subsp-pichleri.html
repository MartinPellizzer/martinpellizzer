Aethionema cordatum subsp. pichleri

Aethionema cordatum subsp.

pichleri, commonly known as the Pichler's rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is a low-growing perennial species characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and small, fleshy leaves.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. Native to the rocky and arid regions of the Eastern Mediterranean, it is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
