Aloe × hexapetala

Aloe × hexapetala, commonly known as the hybrid aloe or the six-petaled aloe, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Aloe vera and Aloe arborescens.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences with six to eight bright yellow flowers arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces fleshy, succulent leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge at the edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its beauty rather than for its medicinal properties.
