Arisarum vulgare var. veslingii

Arisarum vulgare var. veslingii, commonly known as the yellow-flowered arum, is a variant of the arum plant characterized by its distinctive yellow spathe.

It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a short, thick rhizome and produces a striking inflorescence with a spadix surrounded by a yellowish spathe. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purple tinge on the underside.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
