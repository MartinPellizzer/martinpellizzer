Asclepias galeottii

Asclepias galeottii, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its stems are erect and often branched. Asclepias galeottii is a member of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is known for its milky sap, which contains latex.

It plays an important role in supporting monarch butterfly populations by providing nectar and serving as a host plant for their larvae.
