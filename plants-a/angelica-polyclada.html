Angelica polyclada

Angelica polyclada, commonly known as Chinese angelica or Dahurian angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with large, compound leaves and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. Its roots are often harvested for medicinal use, containing compounds like ligustilide and angelica acid.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested or meadow environments.
