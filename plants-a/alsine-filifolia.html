Alsine filifolia

Alsine filifolia, commonly known as slender alsike, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its small, green, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Alsine filifolia is an annual plant that is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
