Albuca oligophylla

Albuca oligophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed albuca, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its strap-like, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or xeriscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid regions.
