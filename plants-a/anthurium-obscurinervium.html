Anthurium obscurinervium

Anthurium obscurinervium, commonly known as the dark-veined anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with dark green veins that stand out against the lighter foliage. The plant produces spadices, which are elongated, spadix-like inflorescences, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium obscurinervium is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying properties.
