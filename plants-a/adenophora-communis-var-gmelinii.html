Adenophora communis var. gmelinii

Adenophora communis var. gmelinii, commonly known as the Gmelin's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pale blue. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
