Angelica megaphylla

Angelica megaphylla, commonly known as the giant angelica, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its large, deeply lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clustered inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Angelica megaphylla is valued for its aromatic foliage and historical use in traditional medicine.
