Alophia lahue subsp. caerulea

Alophia lahue subsp.

caerulea, commonly known as the blue-flowered alophia, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to South Africa. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of vivid blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Due to its striking appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
