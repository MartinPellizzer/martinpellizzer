Arundinaria chikatsuafumiana

Arundinaria chikatsuafumiana, commonly known as the Chikatsuafumiana bamboo, is a rare and ornamental grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, upright culms that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces panicle-like inflorescences with small, pale green to brownish flowers. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.

Due to its limited distribution and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
