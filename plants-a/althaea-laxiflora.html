Althaea laxiflora

Althaea laxiflora, commonly known as the woolly marshmallow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks.

Althaea laxiflora is valued for its medicinal properties, with its roots and leaves historically used in traditional remedies.
