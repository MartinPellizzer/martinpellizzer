Astragalus kermanschahanenicus

Astragalus kermanschahanenicus, commonly known as the Kermanshah astragalus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or pink. The plant produces pod fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions due to its deep root system and small, waxy leaves.
