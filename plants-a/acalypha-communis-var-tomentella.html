Acalypha communis var. tomentella

Acalypha communis var. tomentella, commonly known as velvetleaf or woolly hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage that gives it a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and yellow, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine, white to grayish hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or disturbed areas.
