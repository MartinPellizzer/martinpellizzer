Aquilegia atrata var. minor

Aquilegia atrata var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf black cohosh, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, downward-facing flowers in shades of deep red to black, which are typically borne on tall, slender stems. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade with moist, well-drained soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and valuable addition to native plant gardens.
