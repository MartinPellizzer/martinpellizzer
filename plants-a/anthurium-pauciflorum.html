Anthurium pauciflorum

Anthurium pauciflorum, commonly known as the rare red anthurium, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and small, red spadix. The plant produces clusters of tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are surrounded by a spathe, which is often brightly colored. Anthurium pauciflorum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is often cultivated as a prized ornamental plant.
