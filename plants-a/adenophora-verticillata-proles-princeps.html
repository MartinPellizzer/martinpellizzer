Adenophora verticillata proles princeps

Adenophora verticillata proles princeps, commonly known as the prince's prince bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, blooming in late summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
