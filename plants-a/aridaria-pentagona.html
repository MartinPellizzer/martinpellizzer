Aridaria pentagona

Aridaria pentagona, commonly known as the pentagon leaf cactus, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive pentagonal-shaped leaves, which are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant stores water in its thick, fleshy stems, which are often green and covered in a waxy cuticle. It produces small, white or yellow flowers during the spring and summer months.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is a valuable species for studying desert plant resilience.
