Acacia gilesiana

Acacia gilesiana, commonly known as the Giles' acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance. Its seeds are enclosed in pod-like fruits and are often dispersed by animals.

Acacia gilesiana plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
