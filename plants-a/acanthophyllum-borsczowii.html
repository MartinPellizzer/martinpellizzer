Acanthophyllum borsczowii

Acanthophyllum borsczowii, commonly known as the Borsczow's spiny cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

Acanthophyllum borsczowii plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing shelter and food for various desert animals.
