Anthyllis hermanniae var. aspalathi

Anthyllis hermanniae var. aspalathi, commonly known as Hermann's anthyllis, is a flowering plant species belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in Mediterranean climates, and is adapted to dry conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
