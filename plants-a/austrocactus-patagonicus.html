Austrocactus patagonicus

Austrocactus patagonicus, commonly known as the Patagonian cactus, is a species native to the dry, cold regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the top of the stems. Its adaptations include thick, water-storing stems and a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Austrocactus patagonicus is well-suited to harsh, arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
