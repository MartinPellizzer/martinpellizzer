Aniba parviflora

Aniba parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered aniba, is a shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, white flowers that appear in clusters. It produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not widely cultivated.

Aniba parviflora is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
