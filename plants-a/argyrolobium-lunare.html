Argyrolobium lunare

Argyrolobium lunare, commonly known as the moonflower or silver globular pea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, round pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Argyrolobium lunare is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique nocturnal blooming habit.
