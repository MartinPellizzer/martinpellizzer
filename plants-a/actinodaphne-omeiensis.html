Actinodaphne omeiensis

Actinodaphne omeiensis, commonly known as the Omei laurel, is a small evergreen tree native to China.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by dark blue berries. It is often found in subtropical forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.

The tree's growth habit and foliage make it a notable species in botanical collections and conservation efforts.
