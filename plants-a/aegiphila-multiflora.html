Aegiphila multiflora

Aegiphila multiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Aegiphila multiflora is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Morphology

Aegiphila multiflora has small, ovate, opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color and covered with fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

The stems are slender, branching, and have a smooth, reddish-brown bark that becomes fissured with age. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, with white petals and yellow stamens, blooming in spring and being pollinated by insects. The fruit is a dry capsule that releases small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, adapting to rocky or sandy soils.
