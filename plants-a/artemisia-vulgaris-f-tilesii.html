Artemisia vulgaris f. tilesii

Artemisia vulgaris f. tilesii, commonly known as the rough tarragon, is a variant of the common tarragon.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its silver-gray foliage and aromatic, slightly bitter scent. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and useful plant in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
