Allium wallichii

Allium wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan garlic, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its botanical features include fibrous roots, narrow linear leaves, and a short flowering stem.

Allium wallichii is adapted to cold climates and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
