Anemia glareosa

Anemia glareosa, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and striking plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that appear almost ethereal in sunlight. The plant produces small, pale pink flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and limited habitat, Anemia glareosa is considered a delicate and endangered species.
