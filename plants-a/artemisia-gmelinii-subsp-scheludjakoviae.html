Artemisia gmelinii subsp. scheludjakoviae

Artemisia gmelinii subsp.

scheludjakoviae, commonly known as the Siberian wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Central Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and mountainous regions. It has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties, particularly for treating digestive issues.

Its resilience to harsh environmental conditions makes it an important component of its native ecosystems.
