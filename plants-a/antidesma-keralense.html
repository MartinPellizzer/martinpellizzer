Antidesma keralense

Antidesma keralense, commonly known as Keralan antidesma, is a shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
