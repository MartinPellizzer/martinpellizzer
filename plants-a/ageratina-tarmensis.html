Ageratina tarmensis

Ageratina tarmensis, commonly known as Tarmian blueweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that are arranged in clusters, giving it a delicate appearance. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Ageratina tarmensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
