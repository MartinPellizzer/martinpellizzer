Areca oliviformis

Areca oliviformis, commonly known as the olive areca, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green, arranged in panicles. Its leaves are pinnate, with long, narrow leaflets that give it a graceful appearance.

Areca oliviformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
