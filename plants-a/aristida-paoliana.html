Aristida paoliana

Aristida paoliana, commonly known as the Pauliana grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental appeal and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
