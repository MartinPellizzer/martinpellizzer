Astragalus abyssinicus

Astragalus abyssinicus, commonly known as Ethiopian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia and parts of the Horn of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its seed pods contain small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its survival in challenging landscapes.

Astragalus abyssinicus is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
