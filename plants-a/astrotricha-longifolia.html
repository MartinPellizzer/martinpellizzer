Astrotricha longifolia

Astrotricha longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of many small florets. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are typically blue to purple in color.

Astrotricha longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
