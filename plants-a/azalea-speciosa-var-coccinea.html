Azalea speciosa var. coccinea

Azalea speciosa var. coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Japan.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range from deep red to orange-red in color, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
