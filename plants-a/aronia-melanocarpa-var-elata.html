Aronia melanocarpa var. elata

Aronia melanocarpa var. elata, commonly known as the tall black chokeberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dark green, oval-shaped leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are rich in antioxidants and other beneficial compounds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
