Asplenium kamchatkanum

Asplenium kamchatkanum, commonly known as the Kamchatka spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern native to the northern regions of the Pacific Rim.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of moss-like tissue. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Asplenium kamchatkanum is well adapted to cool, humid climates and is often found in alpine or subarctic regions.
