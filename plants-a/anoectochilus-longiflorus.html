Anoectochilus longiflorus

Anoectochilus longiflorus, commonly known as the long-flowered orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, long-lasting inflorescences that bear numerous fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth surface and a prominent midrib.

Anoectochilus longiflorus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
