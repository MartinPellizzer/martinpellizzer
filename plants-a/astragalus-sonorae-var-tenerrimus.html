Astragalus sonorae var. tenerrimus

Astragalus sonorae var. tenerrimus, commonly known as the Sonoran milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This species is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing desert soils.
