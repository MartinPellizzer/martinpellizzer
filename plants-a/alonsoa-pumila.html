Alonsoa pumila

Alonsoa pumila, commonly known as the dwarf alonsoa, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and clusters of pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, storing water in its succulent stems and leaves. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or desert areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
