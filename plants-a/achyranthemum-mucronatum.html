Achyranthemum mucronatum

Achyranthemum mucronatum, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 feet and features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies adds ecological value to the environment.
