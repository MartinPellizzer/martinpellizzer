Alternanthera sessilis var. parviflora

Alternanthera sessilis var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered Alternanthera, is a tropical aquatic plant native to South America.

It features compact, upright growth with lance-shaped leaves that are typically red or pink in color, though they can vary depending on light conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale pink flowers, which are usually borne in clusters near the water surface. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often used in aquascaping for its vibrant foliage.

This variety is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for freshwater aquariums.
