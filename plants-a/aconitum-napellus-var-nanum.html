Aconitum napellus var. nanum

Aconitum napellus var. nanum, commonly known as the dwarf monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features striking violet-blue flowers arranged in a raceme, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
