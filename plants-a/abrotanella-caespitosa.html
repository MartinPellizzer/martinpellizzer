Abrotanella caespitosa

Abrotanella caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that forms dense mats of foliage, typically in arid or rocky habitats. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water conservation.

Abrotanella caespitosa is adapted to harsh environments and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.
