Amphicarpaea monoica var. comosa

Amphicarpaea monoica var. comosa, commonly known as the hairy false flax, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Leguminosae (Fabaceae) family and is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and it is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobium bacteria.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and soil enrichment.
