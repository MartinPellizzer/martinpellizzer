Aquilegia vulgaris var. praecox

Aquilegia vulgaris var. praecox, commonly known as early columbine, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features showy, spurred flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
