Astragalus xylobasis var. angustissimus

Astragalus xylobasis var. angustissimus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with pinnate arrangement, and its flowers are typically pink to purple in color. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments, making it a hardy member of the Astragalus genus.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting native pollinators.
