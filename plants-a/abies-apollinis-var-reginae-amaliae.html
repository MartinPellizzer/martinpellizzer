Abies apollinis var. reginae-amaliae

Abies apollinis var. reginae-amaliae, commonly known as the Queen Amalia fir, is a rare and ornamental conifer native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and its distinctive, dark green needles that are slightly twisted. The cones are typically small and cylindrical, maturing to a reddish-brown color. This variety is prized for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.

Due to its limited natural range and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
