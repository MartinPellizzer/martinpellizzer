Achillea ochroleuca var. parviflora

Achillea ochroleuca var. parviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features basal rosettes of gray-green, narrow, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped umbels. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry meadows and rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental plant in garden settings.
