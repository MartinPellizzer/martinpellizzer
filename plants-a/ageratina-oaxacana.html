Ageratina oaxacana

Ageratina oaxacana, commonly known as Oaxacan blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has hairy leaves and stems, which help it retain moisture in its native arid environments. It is often found in dry, rocky areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Ageratina oaxacana is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
