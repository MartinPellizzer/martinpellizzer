Arracacia trifida

Arracacia trifida, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit or star apple, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped fruit. The plant produces a large, fleshy, yellow or orange fruit with a star-like appearance when mature. The leaves are large, lobed, and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental features.
