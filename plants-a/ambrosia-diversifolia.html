Ambrosia diversifolia

Ambrosia diversifolia, commonly known as the diverseleaf ambrosia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged in a pinnate pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.

Ambrosia diversifolia is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
