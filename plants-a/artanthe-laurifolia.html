Artanthe laurifolia

Artanthe laurifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved artanthe, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped, often hairy leaves and clusters of small, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Artanthe laurifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.

It is also used in traditional medicine in some regions for its purported health benefits.
