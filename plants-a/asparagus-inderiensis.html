Asparagus inderiensis

Asparagus inderiensis, commonly known as the Congo asparagus, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and compound leaves that are often feather-like in appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by cylindrical, spiny berries containing the seeds. Asparagus inderiensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
