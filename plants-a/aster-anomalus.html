Aster anomalus

Aster anomalus, commonly known as the strange aster, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to withstand arid conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Aster anomalus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
