Amaryllis linearis

Amaryllis linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its linear, grass-like leaves and striking bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing clusters of white or pink flowers that are often fragrant. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Amaryllis linearis is relatively easy to care for and can be grown both in gardens and as a potted plant.
