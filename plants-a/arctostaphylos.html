Arctostaphylos

Arctostaphylos, commonly known as manzanita, is a genus of evergreen shrubs native to western North America.

These plants are characterized by their smooth, reddish bark and dense, leathery leaves that are often evergreen. Arctostaphylos species produce small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by berries that are typically red or black. The genus includes over 60 species, many of which are adapted to dry, rocky habitats.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
