Acacia sulcaticaulis

Acacia sulcaticaulis, commonly known as the serrated acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, fern-like leaves that are typically clustered at the base of the stems. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are followed by distinctive pod-like fruits. Acacia sulcaticaulis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
