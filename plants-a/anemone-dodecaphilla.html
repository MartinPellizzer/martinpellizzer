Anemone dodecaphilla

Anemone dodecaphilla, commonly known as the twelve-lobed anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often colorful flowers. The plant features a short, stout stem with palmately divided leaves that have 12 lobes, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with numerous stamens and a prominent central pistil.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.
