Anagallis lusitanica

Anagallis lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese pink, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant produces small, delicate pink or purple flowers with five petals, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Anagallis lusitanica is often found in disturbed areas and is considered a characteristic species of Mediterranean vegetation.
