Aster oviformis

Aster oviformis, commonly known as the ovalleaf aster, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, producing clusters of small, purple to blue flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
