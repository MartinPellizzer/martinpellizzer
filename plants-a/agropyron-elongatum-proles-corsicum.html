Agropyron elongatum proles corsicum

Agropyron elongatum proles corsicum, commonly known as Corsican needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity.
