Arisaema fimbriatum

Arisaema fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed arum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its unique, arrow-shaped leaves and striking inflorescence. The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe, which is often brightly colored. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Arisaema fimbriatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
