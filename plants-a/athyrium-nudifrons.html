Athyrium nudifrons

Athyrium nudifrons, commonly known as the naked-fruited shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the leaves, a common feature in ferns.

Athyrium nudifrons is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
