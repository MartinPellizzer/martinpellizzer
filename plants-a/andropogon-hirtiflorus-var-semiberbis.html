Andropogon hirtiflorus var. semiberbis

Andropogon hirtiflorus var. semiberbis, commonly known as the slender hairy bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and hairy leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, thriving in well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, blue-green spikelets that bloom in late summer.

This variant is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
