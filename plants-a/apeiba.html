Apeiba

Apeiba, commonly known as the yellow myrtle, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. Apeiba is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in folk medicine.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
