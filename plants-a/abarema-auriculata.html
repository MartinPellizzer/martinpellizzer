Abarema auriculata

Abarema auriculata, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that resemble small bells, hence its common name. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces small, elongated fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Abarema auriculata is a climbing plant that can be grown as a vine or shrub, depending on the support it receives.
