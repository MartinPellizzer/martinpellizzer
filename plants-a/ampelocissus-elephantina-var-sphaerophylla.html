Ampelocissus elephantina var. sphaerophylla

Ampelocissus elephantina var. sphaerophylla, commonly known as the elephant's foot or sphaerophylla, is a unique climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, red berries that are edible for humans and birds. Its succulent leaves are adapted to store water, making it drought-tolerant.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and ability to thrive in various conditions.
