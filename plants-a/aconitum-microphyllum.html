Aconitum microphyllum

Aconitum microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers arranged in a helmet-like shape, typical of the genus Aconitum. The plant has palmately compound leaves with a distinctive spurred petal that resembles a hood, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
