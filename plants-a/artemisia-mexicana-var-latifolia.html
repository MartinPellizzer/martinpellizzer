Artemisia mexicana var. latifolia

Artemisia mexicana var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, aromatic foliage and silvery-gray, feathery flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its strong, medicinal scent.

It is used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and visual appeal.
