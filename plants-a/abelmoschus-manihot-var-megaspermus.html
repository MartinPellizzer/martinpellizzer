Abelmoschus manihot var. megaspermus

Abelmoschus manihot var. megaspermus, commonly known as the giant African nightshade, is a variant of the okra plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces thick, fleshy pods that contain numerous seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm climates and is cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.

Its botanical features make it distinct from other Abelmoschus species, particularly in its size and flowering habits.
