Aconitum popovii

Aconitum popovii, commonly known as the Siberian monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a helmet-like shape, typical of the genus Aconitum. The plant has palmately divided, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is not recommended for cultivation in gardens where children or pets are present.
