Aconitum variegatum var. paniculatum

Aconitum variegatum var. paniculatum, commonly known as the variegated aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, paniculate inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. The plant has deeply lobed, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
