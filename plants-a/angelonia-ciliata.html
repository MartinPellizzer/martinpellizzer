Angelonia ciliata

Angelonia ciliata, commonly known as the summer snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant produces showy, clustered blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Angelonia ciliata is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.
