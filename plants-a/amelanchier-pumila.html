Amelanchier pumila

Amelanchier pumila, commonly known as the dwarf serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features alternate, simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by edible, red-purple berries in the summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Amelanchier pumila is often found in open woodlands and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
