Allium sphaerocephalon var. sardoum

Allium sphaerocephalon var. sardoum, commonly known as the Sardinian leek, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a rosette of broad, grass-like leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces a spherical, purple bulb that is often used in local cuisines for its mild, onion-like flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and culinary versatility.
