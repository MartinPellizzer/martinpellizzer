Amyris verrucosa

Amyris verrucosa, commonly known as the roughleaf amyris, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rough, textured leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. Amyris verrucosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
