Acanthodium macrum

Acanthodium macrum, commonly known as the rough acanthodium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny bracts that enclose its small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Acanthodium macrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
