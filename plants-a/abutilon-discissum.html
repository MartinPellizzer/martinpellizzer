Abutilon discissum

Abutilon discissum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are often hairy and have a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Abutilon discissum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
