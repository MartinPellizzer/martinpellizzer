Archidendron ledermannii

Archidendron ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's legume, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with three leaflets, and its flowers are typically small and clustered. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories.

The seeds are enclosed in elongated pods, which contribute to its ecological role in seed dispersal.
