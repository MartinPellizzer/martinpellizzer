Agropyron glaucissimum

Agropyron glaucissimum, commonly known as the blue wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in cold, dry environments and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil in harsh climates.

Agropyron glaucissimum plays a role in preventing soil erosion and supports local wildlife in its native habitats.
