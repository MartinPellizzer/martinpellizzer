Anthoxanthum pluriflorum var. pluriflorum

Anthoxanthum pluriflorum var. pluriflorum, commonly known as the common oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks, thriving in temperate climates. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in panicles, bearing small, pale yellow to brown flowers.

The botanical characteristics of this species make it an important component of ecological and agricultural systems.
