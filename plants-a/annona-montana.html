Annona montana

Annona montana, commonly known as mountain custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant produces large, showy flowers with a white or pale green color, followed by a fruit that is fleshy and sweet when ripe. The fruit has a rough, spiny exterior and a creamy, custard-like pulp.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruit and has some traditional medicinal uses.
