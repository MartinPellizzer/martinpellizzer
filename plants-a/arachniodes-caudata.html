Arachniodes caudata

Arachniodes caudata, commonly known as the long-tailed arachniodes, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that can reach lengths of up to 2 meters. The plant features a distinctive, creeping rhizome with dense, fibrous roots that help it thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically divided into numerous pinnae, giving it a feathery appearance.

Arachniodes caudata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical and subtropical gardens.
