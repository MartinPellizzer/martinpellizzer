Arundinella chevalieri

Arundinella chevalieri, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, perennial grass native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, and its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a smooth texture. Arundinella chevalieri is valued for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in horticulture or as a source of biomass.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for various insects and birds in its native environments.
