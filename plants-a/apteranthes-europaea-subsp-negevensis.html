Apteranthes europaea subsp. negevensis

Apteranthes europaea subsp.

negevensis, commonly known as the Negev European aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, pale blue to violet flowers in the summer months. It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.

This subspecies is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
