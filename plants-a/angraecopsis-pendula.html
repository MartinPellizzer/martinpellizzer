Angraecopsis pendula

Angraecopsis pendula, commonly known as the hanging angraecum, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its pendulous inflorescence bearing numerous small, white flowers. The plant produces intricate, fragrant blooms that attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Angraecopsis pendula is valued for its ornamental appeal and unique floral structure.
