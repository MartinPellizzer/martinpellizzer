Asarum kurokawanum

Asarum kurokawanum, commonly known as the Japanese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, aromatic underground tubers. The plant produces showy, hooded flowers that are typically purple or greenish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
