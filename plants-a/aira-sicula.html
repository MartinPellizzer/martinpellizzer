Aira sicula

Aira sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian aira, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, white to pale green florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.

Aira sicula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
