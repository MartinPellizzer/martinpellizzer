Aglaia grata

Aglaia grata, commonly known as the golden apple tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its botanical features include a straight trunk, opposite leaf arrangement, and a distinctive fragrance.
