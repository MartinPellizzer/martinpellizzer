Acourtia patens

Acourtia patens, commonly known as the coast mallow, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of California and Baja California.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Acourtia patens plays an important role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
