Arum italicum var. neglectum

Arum italicum var. neglectum, commonly known as the Italian arum or the neglected arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are dark green with a prominent veins, and it produces spadices, which are inflorescences that resemble a cluster of small white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its dramatic foliage and unique flowering structure.

However, it can be invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
