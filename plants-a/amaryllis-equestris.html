Amaryllis equestris

Amaryllis equestris, commonly known as the horseman's lily, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with a dark throat. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a central bulb and are arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Amaryllis equestris is prized for its vibrant blooms and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
