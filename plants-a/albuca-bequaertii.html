Albuca bequaertii

Albuca bequaertii, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tulip-like flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Albuca bequaertii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in warm climates.
