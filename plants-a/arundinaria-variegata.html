Arundinaria variegata

Arundinaria variegata, commonly known as the variegated bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often striped with contrasting colors. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Arundinaria variegata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for landscaping purposes.
