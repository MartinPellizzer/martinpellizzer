Alchemilla lainzii

Alchemilla lainzii, commonly known as the Lainz's lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in compact inflorescences. Alchemilla lainzii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is often used in garden settings for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in woodland environments.
