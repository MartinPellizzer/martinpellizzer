Arenaria trinervia subsp. pentandra

Arenaria trinervia subsp.

pentandra, commonly known as the five-veined sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions. It typically features small, rounded leaves and produces clusters of tiny, white or pink flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates. Its subspecies pentandra is distinguished by its five distinct leaf veins.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
