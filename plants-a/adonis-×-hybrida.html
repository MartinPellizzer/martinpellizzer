Adonis × hybrida

Adonis × hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid foxtail lily, is a perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that resemble small, clustered bells. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are usually heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Adonis × hybrida is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in sunny, dry conditions.
