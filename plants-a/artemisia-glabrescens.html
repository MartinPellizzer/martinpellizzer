Artemisia glabrescens

Artemisia glabrescens, commonly known as silver thread, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in dense, fine hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and rocky habitats.

Artemisia glabrescens is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
