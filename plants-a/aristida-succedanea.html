Aristida succedanea

Aristida succedanea, commonly known as the common dropseed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It features slender, tufted stems that grow up to 60 cm tall, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies, meadows, and along roadsides.

Aristida succedanea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in dry environments.
