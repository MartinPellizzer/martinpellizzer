Alsophila setosa

Alsophila setosa, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of the world.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery fronds. The plant has a robust root system that helps it thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically long and narrow, with a distinctive pale green to silver underside.

Alsophila setosa is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to add a tropical feel to landscapes.
