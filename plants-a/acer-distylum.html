Acer distylum

Acer distylum, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its ornamental foliage, which displays vibrant colors ranging from green to red and orange during the growing season and autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 meters, with a rounded canopy and delicate, branching structure. Its leaves are palmately lobed, often with finely serrated edges, and it produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers in early spring.

Acer distylum prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
