Alyssum tenuiramea

Alyssum tenuiramea, commonly known as slender-stemmed alyssum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its low-growing habit and profuse clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant features linear, hairy leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Alyssum tenuiramea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
