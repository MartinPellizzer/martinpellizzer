Acacia sassa

Acacia sassa, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, slender leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in globular heads, which are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. Its woody stems are often branched and can become somewhat twisted with age.

Acacia sassa is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to arid and semi-arid environments.
