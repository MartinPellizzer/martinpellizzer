Angraecum bicallosum

Angraecum bicallosum, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It features long, slender, white or pale pink flowers that bloom at night, attracting specific nocturnal pollinators like hawkmoths. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to its humid, shaded forest habitat.

Angraecum bicallosum is prized for its beauty and rarity in horticulture.
