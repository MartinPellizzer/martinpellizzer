Alsophila bulligera

Alsophila bulligera, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive crown of compound leaves that give it a lush, tropical appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Alsophila bulligera plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various forest organisms.
