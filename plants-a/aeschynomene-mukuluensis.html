Aeschynomene mukuluensis

Aeschynomene mukuluensis, commonly known as Mukulu grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pods containing several seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
