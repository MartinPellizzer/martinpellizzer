Acacia capensis

Acacia capensis, commonly known as the silver acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Acacia capensis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in arid environments.
