Acacia julifera subsp. curvinervia

Acacia julifera subsp.

curvinervia, commonly known as the Socotra acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the island of Socotra. It is characterized by its distinctive, flat, thorn-like leaves and yellow, globular flower heads.

The plant has a unique morphology, with its leaves often appearing as modified stems. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to its harsh coastal habitat.

The species is of ecological and botanical interest due to its endemic status and unique evolutionary traits.
