Azalea haematodes

Azalea haematodes, commonly known as the blood azalea, is a species of flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Azalea haematodes thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
