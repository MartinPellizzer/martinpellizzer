Achillea spinosa

Achillea spinosa, commonly known as the thorny yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a compact, bushy growth habit with silver-gray, finely divided leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flower heads that are arranged in flat-topped clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens.

Its common name reflects its historical use in traditional medicine and its resemblance to other members of the Asteraceae family.
