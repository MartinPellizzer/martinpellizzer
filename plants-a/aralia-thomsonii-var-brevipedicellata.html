Aralia thomsonii var. brevipedicellata

Aralia thomsonii var. brevipedicellata, commonly known as the dwarf aralia, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive, feathery appearance, and its foliage is often a deep green with a slightly glossy texture. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by dark blue berries in the fall. It is well-suited for shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and attractive addition to woodland landscapes.
