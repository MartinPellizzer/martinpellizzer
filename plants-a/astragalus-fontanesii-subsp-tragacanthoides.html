Astragalus fontanesii subsp. tragacanthoides

Astragalus fontanesii subsp.

tragacanthoides, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its erect stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated margins.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the summer months. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

The subspecies is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
