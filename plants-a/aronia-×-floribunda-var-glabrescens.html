Aronia × floribunda var. glabrescens

Aronia × floribunda var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-fruited chokeberry, is a hybrid shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are rich in antioxidants and anthocyanins. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is usually green with a slightly glossy texture, and it produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
