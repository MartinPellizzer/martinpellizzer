Acacia mollissima var. angulata

Acacia mollissima var. angulata, commonly known as the angular acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive angular, often spiny branches and its dense, feathery foliage. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular heads, which are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. Its leaves are usually bipinnate, with small, rounded leaflets that contribute to its ornamental appeal.

The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.
