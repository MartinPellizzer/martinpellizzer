Anaxagorea floribunda

Anaxagorea floribunda, commonly known as the fragrant evergreen tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The tree thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
