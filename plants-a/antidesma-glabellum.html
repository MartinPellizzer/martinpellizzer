Antidesma glabellum

Antidesma glabellum, commonly known as the African starfruit or Natal starfruit, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkin-like inflorescences. Its fruit is a fleshy, star-shaped berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

Antidesma glabellum is often found in moist woodlands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in partial shade.
