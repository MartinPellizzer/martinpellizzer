Amorphophallus sagittarius

Amorphophallus sagittarius, commonly known as the arrowhead elephant's tusk, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and a distinctive underground tuber that resembles an elephant's tusk. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes plants like calla lilies and peace lilies. Its tuber is used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
