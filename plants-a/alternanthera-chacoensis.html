Alternanthera chacoensis

Alternanthera chacoensis, commonly known as the Chaco Alternanthera, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves that change color depending on light exposure, often displaying shades of red, pink, and purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and aquatic environments.
