Alsophila fulgens

Alsophila fulgens, commonly known as the shining tree fern, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds, which are arranged in distinct, overlapping layers.

Alsophila fulgens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its role in tropical ecosystems.
