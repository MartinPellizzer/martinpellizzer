Agrostistachys longifolia

Agrostistachys longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved grass.

It is a perennial herb native to Africa, characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, pale green to yellow flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a slight hairy texture.

Agrostistachys longifolia is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and savannas.
