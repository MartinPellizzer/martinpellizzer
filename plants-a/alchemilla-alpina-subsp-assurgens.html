Alchemilla alpina subsp. assurgens

Alchemilla alpina subsp.

assurgens, commonly known as the alpine lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, silver-green leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and hardy nature.
