Astragalus parnassi

Astragalus parnassi, commonly known as Parnassian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. Astragalus parnassi is adapted to harsh alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

It is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing mountainous terrain.
