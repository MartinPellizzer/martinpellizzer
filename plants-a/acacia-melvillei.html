Acacia melvillei

Acacia melvillei, commonly known as the Melville acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to poor soil conditions and drought.

Acacia melvillei is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
