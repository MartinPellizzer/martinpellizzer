Athamanta cretensis

Athamanta cretensis, commonly known as the Cretan thyme, is a perennial herb native to the island of Crete.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their essential oils. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually pink or purple, and it is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Athamanta cretensis is valued for its aromatic properties and has a long history of use in Mediterranean cultures.
