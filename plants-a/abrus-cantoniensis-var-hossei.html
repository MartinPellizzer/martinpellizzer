Abrus cantoniensis var. hossei

Abrus cantoniensis var. hossei, commonly known as the Chinese blood bean, is a climbing legume native to southern China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red, kidney-shaped seeds encased in a long, narrow pod. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets and produces showy, yellow flowers that attract pollinators. Its seeds contain toxic compounds, making it potentially hazardous if ingested.

This variant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance.
