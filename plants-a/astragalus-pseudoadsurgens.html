Astragalus pseudoadsurgens

Astragalus pseudoadsurgens, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus pseudoadsurgens is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
