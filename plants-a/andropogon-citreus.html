Andropogon citreus

Andropogon citreus, commonly known as lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves that are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often harvested for their essential oils. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale flowers.

Andropogon citreus thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its fragrance and utility in traditional medicine.
