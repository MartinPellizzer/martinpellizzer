Argemone pleiacantha var. ambigua

Argemone pleiacantha var. ambigua, commonly known as the Mexican prickly poppy, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
