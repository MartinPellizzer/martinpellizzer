Aster radula

Aster radula, commonly known as the rock aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

Aster radula is known for its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in alpine ecosystems.
