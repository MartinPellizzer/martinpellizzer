Arum maculatum subsp. angustatum

Arum maculatum subsp.

angustatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arum, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically narrower than those of the typical subspecies.

The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a spathe, which is often brightly colored to attract pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
