Agave flavescens var. macroacantha

Agave flavescens var. macroacantha, commonly known as the giant red agave, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy leaves that are typically reddish in color, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear yellow-green flowers, which are pollinated by insects and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for arid landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is often cultivated in xeriscapes and rock gardens.
