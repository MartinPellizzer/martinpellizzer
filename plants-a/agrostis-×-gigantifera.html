Agrostis × gigantifera

Agrostis × gigantifera, commonly known as the giant meadowgrass, is a hybrid grass species native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, blade-like leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, spikelet flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a key role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
