Aristolochia pearcei

Aristolochia pearcei, commonly known as the pearce's Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, pipe-like flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
