Aconitum gracile

Aconitum gracile, commonly known as the slender monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive hood-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has palmate leaves with deeply cut lobes, and it produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators. Aconitum gracile thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is also known for its toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires careful handling.
