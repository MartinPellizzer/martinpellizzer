Arachis monticola

Arachis monticola, commonly known as the mountain groundnut, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It is often found in rocky or mountainous regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for agricultural use, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and research settings.
