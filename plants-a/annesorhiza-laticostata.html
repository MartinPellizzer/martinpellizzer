Annesorhiza laticostata

Annesorhiza laticostata, commonly known as the broad-costate grass, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its robust, clump-forming growth habit. The plant features long, narrow leaves with prominent parallel veins, and its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Annesorhiza laticostata is adapted to arid environments and is relatively drought-tolerant.
