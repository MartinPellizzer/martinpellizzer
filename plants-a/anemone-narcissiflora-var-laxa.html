Anemone narcissiflora var. laxa

Anemone narcissiflora var. laxa, commonly known as the laxa anemone, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a slight pinkish tinge. The plant has basal leaves that are palmately lobed and hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable habitats.
