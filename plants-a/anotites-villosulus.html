Anotites villosulus

Anotites villosulus, commonly known as the hairy water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to the Americas.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has long, slender fronds that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its reproductive structures are borne on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

Anotites villosulus plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygenation for aquatic life.
