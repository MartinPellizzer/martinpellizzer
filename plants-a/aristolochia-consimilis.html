Aristolochia consimilis

Aristolochia consimilis, commonly known as the Indian Birthwort, is a member of the Aristolochiaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, often fragrant, flowers that resemble a pipe or a horn. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along streambanks.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
