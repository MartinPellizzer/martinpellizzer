Arundinaria tecta var. pumila

Arundinaria tecta var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a compact, ornamental grass native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, dark green culms that grow in dense clumps, often reaching a height of only a few inches. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by cylindrical seed heads. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its slow growth and ability to form dense mats make it an excellent choice for erosion control and ground cover.
