Armoracia valdiviana

Armoracia valdiviana, commonly known as the Valdivian horseradish, is a perennial herb native to the temperate rainforests of southern Chile.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its thick, aromatic root and pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

The plant is adapted to moist, cool climates and is often found in shaded, forested areas.
