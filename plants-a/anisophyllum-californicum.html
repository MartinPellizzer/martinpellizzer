Anisophyllum californicum

Anisophyllum californicum, commonly known as the California buckwheat, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Anisophyllum californicum plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
