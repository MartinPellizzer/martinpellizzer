Aegiphila monticola

Aegiphila monticola, commonly known as the mountain aegiphila, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
