Aconitum neosachalinense subsp. miyabei

Aconitum neosachalinense subsp.

miyabei, commonly known as the Miyabe aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height.

The plant produces showy, blue to purple spathaceous flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments, and are covered in a dense layer of soft, silky hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
