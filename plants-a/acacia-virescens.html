Acacia virescens

Acacia virescens, commonly known as the green acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are highly attractive to pollinators. Acacia virescens is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
