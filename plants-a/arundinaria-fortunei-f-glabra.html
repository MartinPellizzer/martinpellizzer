Arundinaria fortunei f. glabra

Arundinaria fortunei f. glabra, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a variant of the bamboo species native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems that lack the typical hairy texture found in other bamboo varieties. The plant grows as a clump-forming perennial, reaching heights of up to 6 meters with slender, hollow stalks. Its leaves are long, narrow, and dark green, providing a striking visual contrast to the silvery sheen of the stems.

This ornamental bamboo is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is well-suited to humid, temperate climates.
