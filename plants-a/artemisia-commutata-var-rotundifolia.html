Artemisia commutata var. rotundifolia

Artemisia commutata var. rotundifolia, commonly known as roundleaf wormwood, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-gray foliage with rounded, bluntly lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are typically clustered in dense, rounded clusters. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
