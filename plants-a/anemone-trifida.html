Anemone trifida

Anemone trifida, commonly known as the three-lobed anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, white to pink flowers with a distinctive, divided center that resembles a crown. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has palmate leaves divided into three lobes. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Anemone trifida is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
