Aster odontophyllus

Aster odontophyllus, commonly known as the toothleaf aster, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features narrow, toothed leaves that give it its common name, and it produces showy blue to purple flowers in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Aster odontophyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
