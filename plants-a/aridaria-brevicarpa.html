Aridaria brevicarpa

Aridaria brevicarpa, commonly known as the short-fruited desert shrub, is a hardy plant adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It typically grows in rocky soils and is found in regions with low precipitation. The plant features small, woody stems and compact, oval-shaped leaves that reduce water loss. Its small, dry fruits are dispersed by wind, aiding in its survival in harsh conditions.

Aridaria brevicarpa plays a vital role in stabilizing desert ecosystems and providing habitat for small fauna.
