Asarum yeonbyeonense

Asarum yeonbyeonense, commonly known as Korean wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, aromatic underground rhizomes. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often hidden beneath its foliage. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Korean medicine.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
