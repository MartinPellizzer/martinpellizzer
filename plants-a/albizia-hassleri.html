Albizia hassleri

Albizia hassleri, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Albizia hassleri is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
