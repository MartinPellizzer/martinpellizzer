Amelina

Amelina, commonly known as the bluebell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Amelina has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, fragrant blossoms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
