Aspilia aggregata

Aspilia aggregata, commonly known as the African flame flower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy yellow-orange flower heads that resemble flames. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower structure. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Aspilia aggregata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
