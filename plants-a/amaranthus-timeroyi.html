Amaranthus timeroyi

Amaranthus timeroyi, commonly known as the Timor amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the island of Timor.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy inflorescences. The plant typically produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Amaranthus timeroyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
