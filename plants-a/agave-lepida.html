Agave lepida

Agave lepida, commonly known as the lace agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in white, fuzzy hairs. The leaves are typically blue-green in color and have a serrated edge. It produces an inflorescence of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

This plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil.
