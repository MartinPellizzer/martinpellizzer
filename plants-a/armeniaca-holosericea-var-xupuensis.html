Armeniaca holosericea var. xupuensis

Armeniaca holosericea var. xupuensis, commonly known as the Yunnan apricot, is a variant of the apricot species native to China.

It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
