Adhatoda schimperiana

Adhatoda schimperiana, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its aromatic, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antispasmodic and expectorant properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
