Alocasia veitchii

Alocasia veitchii, commonly known as the elephant ear, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped, arrowhead-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique inflorescence structure called a spathe and spadix. Alocasia veitchii thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
