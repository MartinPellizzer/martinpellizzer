Anthericum elatum

Anthericum elatum, commonly known as the giant arum, is a striking plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces a distinctive spathe, which is a showy, petal-like structure that encloses the spadix, a dense cluster of tiny flowers. Anthericum elatum is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical and subtropical regions.
