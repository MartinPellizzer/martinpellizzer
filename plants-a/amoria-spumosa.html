Amoria spumosa

Amoria spumosa, commonly known as the foamy amoria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fluffy white to pale yellow inflorescence that resembles a foam-like structure. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to parts of southern Africa. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.

Amoria spumosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
