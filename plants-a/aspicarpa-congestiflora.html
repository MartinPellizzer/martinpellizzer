Aspicarpa congestiflora

Aspicarpa congestiflora, commonly known as the clustered fruit tree, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, grayish-brown bark and elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are typically red or orange when ripe, often used in local cuisines. Its flowers are white or pale yellow, arranged in dense clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

Aspicarpa congestiflora is valued for its edible fruits and is sometimes cultivated for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
