Aconitum prutense

Aconitum prutense, commonly known as the Purple Aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has large, palmately divided leaves and produces showy, spiky inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

However, it is highly toxic due to the presence of potent alkaloids, making it dangerous to handle or ingest.
