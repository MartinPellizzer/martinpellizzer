Achillea boissieriana

Achillea boissieriana, commonly known as the Boissier's yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to full sun conditions. It is often found in grasslands and along rocky outcrops, showcasing its resilience to arid environments.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
