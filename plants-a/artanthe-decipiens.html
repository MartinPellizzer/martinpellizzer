Artanthe decipiens

Artanthe decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive artanthe, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, and the flowers come in shades of pink to red.

Artanthe decipiens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
