Acacia brachybotrya var. argyrophylla

Acacia brachybotrya var. argyrophylla, commonly known as the silver-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and can tolerate drought conditions.

The species is often used in revegetation projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
