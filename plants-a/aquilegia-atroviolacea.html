Aquilegia atroviolacea

Aquilegia atroviolacea, commonly known as the black violet.

It is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in the Andes. The plant features showy, bi-colored flowers with purple petals and a deep violet center, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, serrated leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Aquilegia atroviolacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, unique blooms.
