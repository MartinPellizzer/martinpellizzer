Asarca glandulifera

Asarca glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular asarum, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Aristolochiaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, dark purple flowers. The plant has a distinctive, aromatic scent and contains various bioactive compounds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
