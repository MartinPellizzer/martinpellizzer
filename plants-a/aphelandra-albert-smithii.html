Aphelandra albert-smithii

Aphelandra albert-smithii, commonly known as the Albert-Smith's flame tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated, and often brightly colored bracts that resemble flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Aphelandra albert-smithii is often cultivated for its vibrant foliage and is a popular choice in tropical gardens.
