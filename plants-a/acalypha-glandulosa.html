Acalypha glandulosa

Acalypha glandulosa, commonly known as the sticky dock or glandular acalypha, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that often exude a sticky substance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Acalypha glandulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
