Asparagus racemosus var. subacerosus

Asparagus racemosus var. subacerosus, commonly known as Indian asparagus or wild asparagus, is a climbing shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by small, red or orange berries. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates and is often found in rocky or dry soils.

The plant has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine for its purported health benefits.
