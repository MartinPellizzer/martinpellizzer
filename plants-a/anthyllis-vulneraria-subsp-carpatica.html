Anthyllis vulneraria subsp. carpatica

Anthyllis vulneraria subsp.

carpatica, commonly known as Carpathian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It is a perennial herb with upright, branching stems and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters.

The plant has palmately divided leaves with three to five leaflets, and its root system is fibrous and adapted to rocky soils. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and alpine slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
