Anagallis tenella var. ascendens

Anagallis tenella var. ascendens, commonly known as the ascending purple-eyed violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, delicate flowers with a distinctive purple eye. The plant typically blooms in spring and early summer, with violet-blue petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical features include hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, which contribute to its ornamental value.
