Abelia biflora

Abelia biflora, commonly known as the twinflower abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, arching stems with opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne in pairs on short pedicels. Abelia biflora thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
