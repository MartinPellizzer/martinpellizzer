Apoballis longicaulis

Apoballis longicaulis, commonly known as the long-stemmed apoballis, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features woody stems that are elongated and often branched, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine layer of white wax, helping to reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Apoballis longicaulis is adapted to survive in low-water conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
