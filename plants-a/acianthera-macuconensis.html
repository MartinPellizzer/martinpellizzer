Acianthera macuconensis

Acianthera macuconensis, commonly known as the Macucon orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, specifically found in regions of Ecuador and Peru.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, often found in humid tropical forests. Its flowers are arranged in a spike and are known for their intricate structure and ornamental value.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
