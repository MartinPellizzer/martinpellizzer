Aspilia monocephala

Aspilia monocephala, commonly known as the single-headed flame flower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features a solitary, showy yellow flower at the top of a tall, sparsely branched stem. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Aspilia monocephala is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
