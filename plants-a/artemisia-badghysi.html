Artemisia badghysi

Artemisia badghysi, commonly known as Afghan wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are often used in traditional medicine. Artemisia badghysi contains essential oils and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.
