Amyris linanoe

Amyris linanoe, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

Amyris linanoe is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
