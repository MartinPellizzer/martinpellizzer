Alsophila pauciflora

Alsophila pauciflora, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, compound fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are arranged in clusters known as sori. Alsophila pauciflora thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its slow growth rate and unique morphology make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and conservation efforts.
