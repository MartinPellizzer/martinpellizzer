Aster strigosus

Aster strigosus, commonly known as the hoary aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. This perennial herb typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
