Adelia monoica

Adelia monoica, commonly known as the solitary adelia, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, single flower that blooms on a slender stem, often in shades of yellow or orange. The plant has small, elongated leaves that are arranged spirally along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Adelia monoica is considered a vulnerable species.
