Anthericum colubrinum

Anthericum colubrinum, commonly known as the bluebell of the woods, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained soil. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to the ecological balance of its habitat.

Anthericum colubrinum is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens.
