Agrostis atroviolacea

Agrostis atroviolacea, commonly known as black violet grass, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with purple to violet-colored florets, which give it its common name. It typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and wetlands.

Agrostis atroviolacea is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
