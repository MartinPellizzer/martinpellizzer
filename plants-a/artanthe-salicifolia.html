Artanthe salicifolia

Artanthe salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaved artanthe, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that resemble willow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Artanthe salicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
