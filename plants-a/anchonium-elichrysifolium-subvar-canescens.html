Anchonium elichrysifolium subvar. canescens

Anchonium elichrysifolium subvar. canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf anchonium, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense pubescence covering the leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming during the warmer months. Its root system is adapted to survive in dry conditions, allowing it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

The subvariety canescens is distinguished by its more extensive silvery covering compared to other varieties of Anchonium elichrysifolium.
