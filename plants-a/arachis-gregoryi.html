Arachis gregoryi

Arachis gregoryi, commonly known as the wild peanut, is a species native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds. It is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species.

Its genetic diversity is of interest to researchers studying peanut domestication and crop improvement.
