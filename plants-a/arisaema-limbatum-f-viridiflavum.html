Arisaema limbatum f. viridiflavum

Arisaema limbatum f. viridiflavum, commonly known as the green-yellow arum, is a variant of the common arum plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant produces a yellow-green spathe that surrounds the spadix, giving it its unique appearance. It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
