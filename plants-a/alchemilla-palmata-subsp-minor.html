Alchemilla palmata subsp. minor

Alchemilla palmata subsp.

minor, commonly known as lesser common lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features a rosette of basal leaves that are soft, hairy, and often covered in a waxy coating.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its foliage is used in traditional herbal medicine and as a decorative plant in gardens.
