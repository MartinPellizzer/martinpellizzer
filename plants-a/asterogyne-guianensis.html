Asterogyne guianensis

Asterogyne guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan asterogyne, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Asterogyne guianensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
