Anagallis arvensis var. latifolia

Anagallis arvensis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb with hairy stems and opposite leaves that are broad and rounded. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers with five petals, typically appearing in clusters. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its ornamental value and ability to colonize open spaces make it a common sight in various habitats.
