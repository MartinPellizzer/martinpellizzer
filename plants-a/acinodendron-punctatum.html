Acinodendron punctatum

Acinodendron punctatum, commonly known as the spotted acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in small, raised dots or tubercles. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters. Its bark is typically greyish and may have a rough texture.

Acinodendron punctatum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
