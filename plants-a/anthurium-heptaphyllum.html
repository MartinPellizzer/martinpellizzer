Anthurium heptaphyllum

Anthurium heptaphyllum, commonly known as the seven-leaf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored with a glossy texture. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium heptaphyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
