Aloe vaotsanda

Aloe vaotsanda, commonly known as the Madagascar aloe, is a succulent plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with white teeth along the edges. Aloe vaotsanda produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers, which bloom during the rainy season.

It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
