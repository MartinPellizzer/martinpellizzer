Asplenium pachychlamys

Asplenium pachychlamys, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its leathery, dark green fronds that are typically broader than they are long. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sorus, which are clustered on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Asplenium pachychlamys is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
