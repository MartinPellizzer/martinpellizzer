Astragalus surugensis subsp. zederbaueri

Astragalus surugensis subsp.

zederbaueri, commonly known as the Zederbauer's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Japan. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
