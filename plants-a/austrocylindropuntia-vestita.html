Austrocylindropuntia vestita

Austrocylindropuntia vestita, commonly known as the white columnar cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height, covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne at the top of the stems. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems to survive in arid environments.

Austrocylindropuntia vestita is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
