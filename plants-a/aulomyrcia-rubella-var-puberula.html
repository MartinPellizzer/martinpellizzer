Aulomyrcia rubella var. puberula

Aulomyrcia rubella var. puberula, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to pink, with a distinctive, velvety texture on its foliage. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dry seeds.

This species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
