Arundinaria fargesii var. grandifolia

Arundinaria fargesii var. grandifolia, commonly known as the giant bamboo or Chinese giant bamboo, is a species of grass native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces large, pale-green, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and often have a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique growth habit and ecological benefits.
