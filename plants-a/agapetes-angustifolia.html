Agapetes angustifolia

Agapetes angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf silverleaf, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of silvery-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

Agapetes angustifolia is often found in cool, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
