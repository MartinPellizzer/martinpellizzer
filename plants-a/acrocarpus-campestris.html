Acrocarpus campestris

Acrocarpus campestris, commonly known as the broad-leafed acrocarpus, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate, and elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardiness and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental or reforestation purposes.
