Aphanostephus pilosus

Aphanostephus pilosus, commonly known as the silky sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that helps reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in sandy soils with minimal water availability.

Aphanostephus pilosus is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.
