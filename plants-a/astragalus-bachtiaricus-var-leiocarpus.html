Astragalus bachtiaricus var. leiocarpus

Astragalus bachtiaricus var. leiocarpus, commonly known as the smoothpod milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils. Its pods are smooth and contain small, round seeds.

This species is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in nitrogen fixation and its potential for use in restoration projects.
