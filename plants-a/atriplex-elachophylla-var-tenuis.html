Atriplex elachophylla var. tenuis

Atriplex elachophylla var. tenuis, commonly known as narrow-leaved saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable component of coastal and inland ecosystems. Its flowering stems bear small, greenish flowers that are typically inconspicuous.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
