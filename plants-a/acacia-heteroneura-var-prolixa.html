Acacia heteroneura var. prolixa

Acacia heteroneura var. prolixa, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear phyllodes that are often covered with a fine layer of white to greyish pubescence. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that appear in late winter to early spring. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage.
