Astragalus onobrychis var. murrii

Astragalus onobrychis var. murrii, commonly known as hairy vetch, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It has a deep taproot system that allows it to thrive in poor soil conditions.

This species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
