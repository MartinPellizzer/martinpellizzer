Anoda acerifolia

Anoda acerifolia, commonly known as the scarlet fanflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that resemble those of maple trees, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Anoda acerifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
