Asarum heterophyllum var. ochranthum

Asarum heterophyllum var. ochranthum, commonly known as Indian snakeroot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is a member of the Aristolochiaceae family, known for its unique floral structures.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal medicine.
