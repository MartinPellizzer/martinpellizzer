Anchomanes hookeri var. pallidus

Anchomanes hookeri var. pallidus, commonly known as the pale anchomanes, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, pale green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to its wetland habitat, with rhizomatous growth aiding in survival and propagation.
