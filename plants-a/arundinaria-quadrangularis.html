Arundinaria quadrangularis

Arundinaria quadrangularis, commonly known as the bamboo palm, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that are typically four-sided, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, and it has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a fan shape. Arundinaria quadrangularis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued for its role in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and adaptability.
