Aster pappei

Aster pappei, commonly known as the Pappe's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer. Aster pappei has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
