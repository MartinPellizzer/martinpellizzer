Amaryllis solandriflora var. vittata

Amaryllis solandriflora var. vittata, commonly known as the spotted amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It produces large, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, often with distinctive spots or stripes. The plant features strap-shaped leaves that are broad and lanceolate, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its blooming period typically occurs in late winter to early spring, adding color to the landscape during the colder months.
