Allium nobile

Allium nobile, commonly known as the globe flower or chive flower, is a hardy perennial plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. The plant produces clusters of small, globe-shaped white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often used in culinary applications similar to chives.

Allium nobile is valued for its ornamental beauty and mild garlic-like fragrance.
