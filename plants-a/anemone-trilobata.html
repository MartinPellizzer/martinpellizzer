Anemone trilobata

Anemone trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white to pinkish flowers with five to seven petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has palmately divided, trifoliate leaves that are hairy and lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Anemone trilobata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
