Ampelopsis chondisensis

Ampelopsis chondisensis, commonly known as the Korean ivy, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its berries are dark blue to black when mature and are often used in traditional medicine.

Ampelopsis chondisensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
