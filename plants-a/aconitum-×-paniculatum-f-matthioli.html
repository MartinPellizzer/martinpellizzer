Aconitum × paniculatum f. matthioli

Aconitum × paniculatum f. matthioli, commonly known as the Matthiolii Aconite, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear clusters of striking blue to purple flowers. The plant's foliage consists of deeply lobed, palmately arranged leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained, moist soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care and kept away from children and pets.
