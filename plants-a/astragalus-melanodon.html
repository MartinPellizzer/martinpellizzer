Astragalus melanodon

Astragalus melanodon, commonly known as the black-horned milkvetch, is a flowering plant species belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, Mongolia, and Russia. The plant is characterized by its distinctive black-tipped bracts and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Astragalus melanodon is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its potential health benefits.
