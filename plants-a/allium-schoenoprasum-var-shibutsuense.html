Allium schoenoprasum var. shibutsuense

Allium schoenoprasum var. shibutsuense, commonly known as Japanese chives, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces edible, mild-flavored leaves that are often used in Japanese cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a valuable ornamental and culinary plant in temperate climates.
