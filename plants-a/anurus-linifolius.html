Anurus linifolius

Anurus linifolius, commonly known as the slender-leaf anurus, is a rare flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom during the cooler months, typically in spring. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Anurus linifolius is considered vulnerable and is protected in several conservation areas.
