Astragalus faizabadensis

Astragalus faizabadensis, commonly known as the Faizabad milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and may have potential medicinal uses, though further research is needed.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
