Aponogeton madagascariensis var. henkelianus

Aponogeton madagascariensis var. henkelianus, commonly known as the Madagascar water crown, is a flowering aquatic plant native to Madagascar.

It features long, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface, with a strikingly ornate inflorescence that resembles a crown. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often submerged, followed by distinctive, boat-shaped fruits. It is well adapted to still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

This variant is prized in aquascaping for its aesthetic appeal and ease of cultivation.
