Adenophora bulleyana var. angustifolia

Adenophora bulleyana var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features upright stems with lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue to purple in color, blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
