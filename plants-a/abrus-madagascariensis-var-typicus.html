Abrus madagascariensis var. typicus

Abrus madagascariensis var. typicus, commonly known as the Madagascar bean or velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, ornate pods. The plant produces bright red or orange seeds enclosed in hairy, inflated pods, which are often used in traditional crafts. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

The species is also notable for containing toxic compounds, making it important to handle with care.
