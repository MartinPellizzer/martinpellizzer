Astragalus kentrophyllus

Astragalus kentrophyllus, commonly known as the golden milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe habitats.

Astragalus kentrophyllus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
