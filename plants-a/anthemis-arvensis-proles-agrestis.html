Anthemis arvensis proles agrestis

Anthemis arvensis proles agrestis, commonly known as common tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers and hairy, aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in meadows, roadsides, and fields across temperate regions. It has a creeping growth habit with branched stems and produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

The leaves and flowers contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties.
