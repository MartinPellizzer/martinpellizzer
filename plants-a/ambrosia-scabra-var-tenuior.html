Ambrosia scabra var. tenuior

Ambrosia scabra var. tenuior, commonly known as the slender ambrosia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as roadsides and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
