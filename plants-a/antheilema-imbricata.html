Antheilema imbricata

Antheilema imbricata, commonly known as the rock fig, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, grayish-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it store water and survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is valued both for ornamental purposes and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
