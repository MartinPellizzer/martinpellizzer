Andropogon ternatus

Andropogon ternatus, commonly known as narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in prairies, open woodlands, and disturbed areas, reaching heights of 1 to 3 meters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue, and its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Andropogon ternatus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
