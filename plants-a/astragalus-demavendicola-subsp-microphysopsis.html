Astragalus demavendicola subsp. microphysopsis

Astragalus demavendicola subsp.

microphysopsis, commonly known as the small-fruited milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers and compound leaves.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller, more compact fruit pods compared to other members of the species. It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions.

This plant plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to soil stability through its root system.
