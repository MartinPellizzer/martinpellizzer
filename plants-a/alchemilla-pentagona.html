Alchemilla pentagona

Alchemilla pentagona, commonly known as the five-angled lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of rhombic, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are borne on slender, branched stems. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported soothing properties.

Alchemilla pentagona thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.
