Adinandra integerrima var. phlebophylla

Adinandra integerrima var. phlebophylla, commonly known as the broad-leafed adenium, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, woody fruits containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry or semi-arid environments.

The species is noted for its ability to store water in its stems, making it drought-tolerant.
