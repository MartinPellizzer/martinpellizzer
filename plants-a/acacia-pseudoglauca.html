Acacia pseudoglauca

Acacia pseudoglauca, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched structure and its distinctive, feathery foliage. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typically visited by a variety of pollinators. Its seeds are encased in hard, woody pods, and the plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils.

Acacia pseudoglauca is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
