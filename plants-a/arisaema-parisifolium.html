Arisaema parisifolium

Arisaema parisifolium, commonly known as the Paris-leaf arisaema, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that resemble the leaves of the Paris plant, giving it its common name. The plant produces a spadix enclosed within a spathe, a typical feature of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
