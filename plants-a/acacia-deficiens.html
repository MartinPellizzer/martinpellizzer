Acacia deficiens

Acacia deficiens, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a shrub native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and small, bipinnate leaves that reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with deep roots and a capacity for dormancy during droughts.

Acacia deficiens plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
