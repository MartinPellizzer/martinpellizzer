Abuta grandifolia

Abuta grandifolia, commonly known as the African butter bean or wild cowpea, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. Its roots are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Abuta grandifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
