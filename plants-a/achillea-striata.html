Achillea striata

Achillea striata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features finely divided, gray-green leaves that are arranged in an alternate pattern along the stems. Its flowers are typically white to pale yellow, forming flat-topped clusters known as corymbs.

Achillea striata is often found in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical medicinal uses.
