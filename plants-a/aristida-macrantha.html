Aristida macrantha

Aristida macrantha, commonly known as the broadleaf beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it is characterized by its tufted growth habit.

Aristida macrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
