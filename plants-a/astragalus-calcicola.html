Astragalus calcicola

Astragalus calcicola, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations and limited water availability. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access moisture deep underground.

Astragalus calcicola plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
