Astragalus ugamicus

Astragalus ugamicus, commonly known as the Mongolian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the steppes of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting deep root systems to access water.

Astragalus ugamicus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
