Aquilegia vulgaris var. dinarica

Aquilegia vulgaris var. dinarica, commonly known as the Dinaric columbine, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It features striking, spurred flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound, pinnately arranged leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. It is often found in rocky or woodland habitats, preferring partial shade to full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
