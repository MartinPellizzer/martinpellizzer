Argyrocalymma arboreum

Argyrocalymma arboreum, commonly known as the silver-leafed tree-lilly, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a small, shrub-like plant with a woody stem and dense, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid environments and prefers well-drained soils.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.
