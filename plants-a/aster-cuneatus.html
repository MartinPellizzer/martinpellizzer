Aster cuneatus

Aster cuneatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, producing clusters of small, blue to purple flowers.

Aster cuneatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
