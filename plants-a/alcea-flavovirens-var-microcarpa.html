Alcea flavovirens var. microcarpa

Alcea flavovirens var. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf yellow flax, is a compact variety of the yellow flax plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a vibrant touch to gardens. The plant has slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its botanical appeal lies in its delicate appearance and easy maintenance, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
