Acarna rigida

Acarna rigida, commonly known as rigid acarna, is a flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking, showy flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. Its flowers are typically tubular with vibrant colors, ranging from red to orange, and are arranged in clusters at the ends of branches.

Acarna rigida is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses due to its unique foliage and long-lasting blooms.
