Adenanthera abrosperma

Adenanthera abrosperma, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a leguminous tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are rich in nutrients and used in traditional cuisines. Its root system helps in nitrogen fixation, making it valuable for soil enrichment.

The tree is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soils, contributing to its ecological significance.
