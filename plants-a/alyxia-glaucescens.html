Alyxia glaucescens

Alyxia glaucescens, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, wooded areas. The plant features small, showy white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Alyxia glaucescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
