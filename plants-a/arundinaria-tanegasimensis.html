Arundinaria tanegasimensis

Arundinaria tanegasimensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a species of grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps, typical of many bamboo species. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in panicles, which are often wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Arundinaria tanegasimensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and structural qualities.
