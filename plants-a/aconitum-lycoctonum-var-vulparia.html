Aconitum lycoctonum var. vulparia

Aconitum lycoctonum var. vulparia, commonly known as the European monkshood, is a striking perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features striking purple-blue flowers arranged in a helmet-like shape, which are typical of the genus Aconitum. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

However, it is highly toxic, containing potent alkaloids that can be dangerous to humans and animals.
