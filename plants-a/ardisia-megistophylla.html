Ardisia megistophylla

Ardisia megistophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf beautyberry, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green elliptical leaves that can grow up to 15 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits that attract birds. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and showy fruit, making it a popular choice in tropical gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its woody structure and adaptability to moist, shaded environments.
