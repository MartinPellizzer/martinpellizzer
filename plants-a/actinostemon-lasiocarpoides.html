Actinostemon lasiocarpoides

Actinostemon lasiocarpoides, commonly known as the woolly fruit-bearing plant, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that develops from the flower and contains numerous tiny seeds.

Actinostemon lasiocarpoides is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.
