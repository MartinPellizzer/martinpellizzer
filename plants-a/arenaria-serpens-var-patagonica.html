Arenaria serpens var. patagonica

Arenaria serpens var. patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous areas.

Its compact growth habit and adaptability make it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
