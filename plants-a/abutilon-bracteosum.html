Abutilon bracteosum

Abutilon bracteosum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to several meters in height.

Abutilon bracteosum is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to warm, humid climates.
