Arachis microsperma

Arachis microsperma, commonly known as the small-seeded groundnut, is a species of legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, tubular pods that contain tiny seeds. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Arachis microsperma is primarily of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential for genetic research.
