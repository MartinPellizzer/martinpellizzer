Acacia cremiflora

Acacia cremiflora, commonly known as the white wattle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect shrub form and its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, feather-like leaflets.

Acacia cremiflora is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
