Anil paniculata

Anil paniculata, commonly known as the purple panic grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and panicles of small, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in open areas, such as fields and disturbed soils, and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces lightweight seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Anil paniculata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
