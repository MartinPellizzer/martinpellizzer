Aquilegia buergeriana

Aquilegia buergeriana, commonly known as the Japanese columbine, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it thrives in partial to full shade in moist, well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also used in traditional medicine.

The flowers bloom in early to mid-summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
