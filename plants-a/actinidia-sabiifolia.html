Actinidia sabiifolia

Actinidia sabiifolia, commonly known as the Chinese mayapple, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged in a palmate pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries that ripen to a yellowish color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Actinidia sabiifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
