Amaryllis × rubrocrocea

Amaryllis × rubrocrocea, commonly known as the red-crimson amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis paradisicola.

It is characterized by its striking, large, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to vibrant crimson. The plant typically produces multiple blooms on a single stem, showcasing its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This hybrid is popular among gardeners for its dramatic floral display and relatively easy care requirements.
