Aquilegia vulgaris var. subtomentosa

Aquilegia vulgaris var. subtomentosa, commonly known as the downy columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features striking, spurred flowers in shades of red, purple, and blue, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological landscapes.
