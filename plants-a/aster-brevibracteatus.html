Aster brevibracteatus

Aster brevibracteatus, commonly known as the short-bracted aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and the flowers are usually blue to purple in color.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
