Alstroemeria ochracea

Alstroemeria ochracea, commonly known as the yellow alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Alstroemeria ochracea is also valued for its ability to thrive in shaded areas, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
