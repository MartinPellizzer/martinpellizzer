Aspalathus lactea var. meyeri

Aspalathus lactea var. meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's milk bush, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its white to pale yellow flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate drought and saline conditions.
