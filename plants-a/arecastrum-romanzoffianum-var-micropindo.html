Arecastrum romanzoffianum var. micropindo

Arecastrum romanzoffianum var. micropindo, commonly known as the dwarf areca palm, is a compact, clumping palm native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, dark green fronds that grow in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular as a ornamental plant due to its compact size and attractive foliage.
