Antriscus

Antriscus, commonly known as good luck plant or silver spider plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to the Canary Islands and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red, depending on the species. Antriscus is well adapted to arid environments, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and indoor gardening.

Its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in various light conditions contribute to its popularity among plant enthusiasts.
