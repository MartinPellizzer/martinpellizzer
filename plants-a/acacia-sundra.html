Acacia sundra

Acacia sundra, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Acacia sundra is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along coastal areas.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
