Aquilegia anemonoides

Aquilegia anemonoides, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that typically range in color from yellow to red. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It often grows in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Aquilegia anemonoides is known for its early spring bloom and is a popular garden plant due to its ornamental value.
