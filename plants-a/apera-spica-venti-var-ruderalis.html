Apera spica-venti var. ruderalis

Apera spica-venti var. ruderalis, commonly known as windgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that are often carried on long, slender stalks. The plant thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in urban environments, along roadsides, and in waste areas. Its ability to colonize open, disturbed sites makes it an important pioneer species in ecological succession.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that helps it to retain moisture in dry conditions.
