Andropogon filipendulus f. bispiculatus

Andropogon filipendulus f. bispiculatus, commonly known as the white beard grass, is a variant of the switchgrass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive white tufts at the base of the inflorescence, giving it a unique appearance. The plant is a perennial grass that thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, including erosion control and wildlife habitat support.
