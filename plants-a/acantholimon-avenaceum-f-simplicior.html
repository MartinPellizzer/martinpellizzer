Acantholimon avenaceum f. simplicior

Acantholimon avenaceum f. simplicior, commonly known as the simpleleaf stonecrop, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to store water and reduce transpiration. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This subspecies is notable for its compact growth habit and drought tolerance.
