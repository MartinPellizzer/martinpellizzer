Atropa flexuosa

Atropa flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible belladonna, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its smooth, often hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces striking bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. Its fruits are toxic berries that contain various alkaloids, including atropine and scopolamine.

Due to its toxicity, it is considered a hazardous plant and is often found in dry, open habitats.
