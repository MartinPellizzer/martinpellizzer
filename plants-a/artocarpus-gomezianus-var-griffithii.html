Artocarpus gomezianus var. griffithii

Artocarpus gomezianus var. griffithii, commonly known as the Philippine breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, spiky fruit. The fruit, which is typically green when mature, turns yellow or orange when ripe and is edible. The plant is valued for its nutritional content and is often cultivated for both food and ornamental purposes.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the region's flora and agricultural systems.
