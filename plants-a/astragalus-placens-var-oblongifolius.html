Astragalus placens var. oblongifolius

Astragalus placens var. oblongifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features compound leaves with elongated, oblong leaflets, giving it its varietal name. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, arranged in clusters, and bloom in early summer.

It is often found in sagebrush steppe and woodland habitats.
