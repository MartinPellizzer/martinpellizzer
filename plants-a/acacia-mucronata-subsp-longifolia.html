Acacia mucronata subsp. longifolia

Acacia mucronata subsp.

longifolia, commonly known as the longifoliate acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures in mature plants.

The plant produces yellow globular flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, which are typically visited by nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its seed pods are elongated and woody, containing several elliptical seeds. Acacia mucronata subsp.

longifolia is adapted to withstand drought and is often used in revegetation projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soils.
