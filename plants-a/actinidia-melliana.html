Actinidia melliana

Actinidia melliana, commonly known as the Chinese mayapple, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern China.

It belongs to the Actinidiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, which are edible but often bitter. Actinidia melliana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological study.
