Actaea eburnea

Actaea eburnea, commonly known as white spiderling, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white, spider-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and flies.

Actaea eburnea is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
