Actinophloeus hospitus

Actinophloeus hospitus, commonly known as the yellow stinging nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems covered in fine, irritating hairs that cause a skin reaction upon contact. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is commonly found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
