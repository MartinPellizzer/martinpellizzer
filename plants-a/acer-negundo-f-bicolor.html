Acer negundo f. bicolor

Acer negundo f. bicolor, commonly known as the twinleaf maple, is a deciduous tree characterized by its distinctive foliage.

Its leaves are palmately lobed, with two prominent lobes on each side, giving it a unique appearance. The plant is native to North America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras in the fall.

The tree is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive form and seasonal color changes.
