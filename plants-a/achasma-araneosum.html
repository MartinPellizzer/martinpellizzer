Achasma araneosum

Achasma araneosum, commonly known as the spider's web plant, is a member of the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that resemble a spider's web, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in umbrella-like clusters called umbels.

Achasma araneosum is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
