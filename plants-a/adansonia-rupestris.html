Adansonia rupestris

Adansonia rupestris, commonly known as the rock date tree, is a species of baobab native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, swollen trunk and a crown of long, narrow leaves. The tree produces white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fruit resembling a date. Its bark is smooth and often fissured, providing a habitat for various animals.

Adansonia rupestris is adapted to arid environments and plays a significant ecological role in its native region.
