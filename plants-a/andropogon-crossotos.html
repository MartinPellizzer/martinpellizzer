Andropogon crossotos

Andropogon crossotos, commonly known as crossleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and distinctive, crossed leaf sheaths that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often hidden within the foliage.

Andropogon crossotos plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
