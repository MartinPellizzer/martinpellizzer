Arabis docica

Arabis docica, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or coastal regions. It features small, white or yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.
