Astragalus neopodlechii

Astragalus neopodlechii, commonly known as the Karakorum milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and high-altitude environments, showcasing resilience to harsh climatic conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
