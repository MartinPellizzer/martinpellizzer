Abies sibirica var. gracilis

Abies sibirica var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender fir, is a variant of the Siberian fir native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe and Central Asia.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its narrow, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The cones of this species are slender and often hang downward, maturing to a dark brown color. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and landscaped settings.
