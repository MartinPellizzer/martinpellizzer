Aristolochia campestris

Aristolochia campestris, commonly known as Dutchmanspipe, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a perennial vine that produces striking, hooded flowers that are often green or brown in color. The plant has a distinctive, complex morphology with a tubular corolla that is twisted into a spiral shape. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found along stream banks and in woodland areas.

However, it contains toxic compounds and is considered invasive in some regions.
