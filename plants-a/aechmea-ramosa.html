Aechmea ramosa

Aechmea ramosa, commonly known as the pink pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater for hydration. The plant's foliage is often brightly colored, with shades of pink, red, and green, adding to its ornamental appeal. It produces vibrant orange or red flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette.

Aechmea ramosa thrives in warm, humid environments and is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea ramosa has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, fleshy, and covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs, giving them a soft, grayish-white appearance.

The leaves are arranged in a spiral pattern around the central rosette and can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant has a short, thick stem that is often hidden beneath the foliage, with a rough, fibrous bark. Its inflorescence consists of a tall, spikelike cluster of pinkish-red flowers that bloom during the summer months, and it is pollinated primarily by insects.

The fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that contains numerous tiny seeds dispersed by wind.
