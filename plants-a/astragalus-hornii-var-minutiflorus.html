Astragalus hornii var. minutiflorus

Astragalus hornii var. minutiflorus, commonly known as the dwarf flaxvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and slender, upright stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, harsh environments. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
