Andromeda bracteosa var. hebecarpa

Andromeda bracteosa var. hebecarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruit wintergreen, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, typical of the Ericaceae family.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
