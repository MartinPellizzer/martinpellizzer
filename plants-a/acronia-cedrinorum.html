Acronia cedrinorum

Acronia cedrinorum, commonly known as the cedarleaf acronia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a fragrant, resinous aroma. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, arranged in clusters at the base of the leaves. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Acronia cedrinorum thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
