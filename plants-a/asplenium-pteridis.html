Asplenium pteridis

Asplenium pteridis, commonly known as the common maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the undersides of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Asplenium pteridis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and indoor settings.
