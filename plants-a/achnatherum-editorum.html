Achnatherum editorum

Achnatherum editorum, commonly known as blue grama, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. Its seed heads are spike-like and often persist through winter, providing food for birds and small mammals.

Achnatherum editorum plays an important role in stabilizing soils and maintaining grassland ecosystems.
