Adenandra marginata

Adenandra marginata, commonly known as the mountain hakea, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens due to its drought tolerance. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinctive margin with small, glandular structures.

Adenandra marginata is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and thrives in well-drained soils.
