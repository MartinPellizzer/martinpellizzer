Aphanostephus ramosus

Aphanostephus ramosus, commonly known as the slender sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale yellow, followed by small, round, dry fruits. Aphanostephus ramosus is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.
