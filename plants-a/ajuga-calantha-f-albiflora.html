Ajuga calantha f. albiflora

Ajuga calantha f. albiflora, commonly known as white-flowered bugleweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are predominantly white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a ground cover with delicate floral displays.
