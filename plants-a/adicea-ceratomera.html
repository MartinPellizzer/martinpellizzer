Adicea ceratomera

Adicea ceratomera, commonly known as the horned milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its striking, horn-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Adicea ceratomera is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
