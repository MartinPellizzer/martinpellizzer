Artemisia dracunculoides var. wolfii

Artemisia dracunculoides var. wolfii, commonly known as Gray sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and aromatic, fuzzy leaves that contain essential oils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and open plains.

Its botanical features make it an important component of sagebrush ecosystems and a valuable plant for ecological studies.
