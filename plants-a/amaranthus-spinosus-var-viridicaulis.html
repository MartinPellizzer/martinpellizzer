Amaranthus spinosus var. viridicaulis

Amaranthus spinosus var. viridicaulis, commonly known as greenleaf amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features upright stems that are often branched and covered with small, spiny structures. The leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and typically have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense panicles, producing both male and female inflorescences.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes grown as a leafy vegetable in certain regions.
