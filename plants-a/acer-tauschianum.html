Acer tauschianum

Acer tauschianum, commonly known as the Tausch's maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and distinctive palmate leaves with five lobes. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras that disperse its seeds. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, becoming more fissured with age.

Acer tauschianum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
