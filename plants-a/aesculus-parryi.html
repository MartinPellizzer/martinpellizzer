Aesculus parryi

Aesculus parryi, commonly known as Parry's horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets. The tree produces showy, white to pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring. Its fruit is a spiny capsule containing several chestnut-like seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and floral display.
