Amomum argyrophyllum

Amomum argyrophyllum, commonly known as the silver-leaved cardamom, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that have a silvery sheen. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark red berries containing aromatic seeds. It is cultivated for its seeds, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.
