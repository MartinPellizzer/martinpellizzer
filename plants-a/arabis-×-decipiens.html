Arabis × decipiens

Arabis × decipiens, commonly known as the hybrid rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, glaucous leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
