Aphelandra simplex

Aphelandra simplex, commonly known as the flame tree or torch plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendant-like flowers that range in color from yellow to red, resembling flames. The plant has glossy, dark green, leathery leaves that are opposite and elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Aphelandra simplex is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
