Aconitum scaposum var. chloranthum

Aconitum scaposum var. chloranthum, commonly known as the green-flowered aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers arranged in a spire-like inflorescence, which are typically borne on long, slender pedicels. Its leaves are palmately compound, with three to five leaflets that are deeply lobed and have a coarse texture.

This variety is notable for its striking greenish-yellow flowers, which contrast with its dark green foliage, making it a distinctive ornamental plant.
