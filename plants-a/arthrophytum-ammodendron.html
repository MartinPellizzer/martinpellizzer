Arthrophytum ammodendron

Arthrophytum ammodendron, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and small, gray-green leaves that are adapted to store water. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is highly drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens with limited water resources.
