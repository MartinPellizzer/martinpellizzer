Astrantia major var. major

Astrantia major var. major, commonly known as the common masterwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems with large, showy umbels of white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and long-lasting blooms.
