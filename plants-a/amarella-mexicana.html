Amarella mexicana

Amarella mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican dwarf cherry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, followed by edible, red berries that are prized for their tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires partial to full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in local cuisine.
