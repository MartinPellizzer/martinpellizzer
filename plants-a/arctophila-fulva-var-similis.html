Arctophila fulva var. similis

Arctophila fulva var. similis, commonly known as the yellow stonecrop, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and rocky environments in North America.

It features fleshy, succulent leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to store water and reduce transpiration. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
