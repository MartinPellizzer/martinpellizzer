Aconitum lioui

Aconitum lioui, commonly known as the Siberian monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hooded blue or purple flowers. The plant has large, palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained, loamy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Aconitum lioui contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous to handle or ingest.
