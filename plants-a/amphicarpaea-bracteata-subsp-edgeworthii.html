Amphicarpaea bracteata subsp. edgeworthii

Amphicarpaea bracteata subsp.

edgeworthii, commonly known as the Edgeworth's false indigo, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to the southeastern United States. It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branching stems and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color. It has a unique reproductive strategy, producing both free and enclosed fruits, which is a trait of its genus.

The species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
