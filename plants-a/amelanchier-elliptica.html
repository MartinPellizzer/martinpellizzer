Amelanchier elliptica

Amelanchier elliptica, commonly known as the Canadian serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It produces edible, sweet berries that ripen in the summer, attracting various wildlife species. The plant has alternate, simple, and elliptic-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

Amelanchier elliptica is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.
