Athyrium leiopodum

Athyrium leiopodum, commonly known as the smoothfoot fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense, velvety layer of hair, giving it a distinctive texture.

Athyrium leiopodum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
