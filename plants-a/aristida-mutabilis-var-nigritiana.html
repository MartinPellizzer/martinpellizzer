Aristida mutabilis var. nigritiana

Aristida mutabilis var. nigritiana, commonly known as blackstem false brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers that are usually pale green to brown.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to arid conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.
