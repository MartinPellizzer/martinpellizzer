Andropogon pentastachyus

Andropogon pentastachyus, commonly known as the tall grass bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common component of prairie and woodland ecosystems.

Andropogon pentastachyus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
