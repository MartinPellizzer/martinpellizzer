Alternanthera martinicensis

Alternanthera martinicensis, commonly known as red flame or red beauty, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or pink foliage, which becomes more intense under strong light conditions. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in shallow waters and is popular in aquariums for its ornamental value.

Its growth is typically compact, making it suitable for foreground or midground planting in freshwater setups.
