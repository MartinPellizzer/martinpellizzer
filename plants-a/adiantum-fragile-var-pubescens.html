Adiantum fragile var. pubescens

Adiantum fragile var. pubescens, commonly known as the Downy Maidenhair Fern, is a delicate fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It features intricate, lace-like fronds with a soft, downy texture on the underside, giving it its common name. The plant has slender, wiry stems that produce small, spore-bearing structures called sori. It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and shaded garden settings.
