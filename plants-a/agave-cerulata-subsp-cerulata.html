Agave cerulata subsp. cerulata

Agave cerulata subsp.

cerulata, commonly known as the blue agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico. It is characterized by its thick, blue-green leaves that are often lined with white or grayish veins.

The plant produces a tall flower stalk that bears small, greenish-white flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil.

This species is widely cultivated for its sap, which is used in the production of tequila and other agave-based beverages.
