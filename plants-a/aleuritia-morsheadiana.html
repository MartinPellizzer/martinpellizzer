Aleuritia morsheadiana

Aleuritia morsheadiana, commonly known as the Morshead's elder, is a flowering shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
