Aucuba japonica limbata

Aucuba japonica limbata, commonly known as the Japanese aucuba, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It features dark green, waxy leaves that are often edged with yellow or white, creating a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that attract birds. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for shaded garden spaces.

Its ornamental foliage and adaptability contribute to its widespread use in horticulture.
