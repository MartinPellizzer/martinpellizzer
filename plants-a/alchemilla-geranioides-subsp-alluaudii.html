Alchemilla geranioides subsp. alluaudii

Alchemilla geranioides subsp.

alluaudii, commonly known as the alluaud's lady's mantle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clustered inflorescences during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
