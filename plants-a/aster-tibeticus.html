Aster tibeticus

Aster tibeticus, commonly known as the Tibetan aster, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers. The plant is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower heads, which are surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often of interest to botanists and conservationists.
