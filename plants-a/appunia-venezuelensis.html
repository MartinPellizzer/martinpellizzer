Appunia venezuelensis

Appunia venezuelensis, commonly known as the Venezuelan daisy, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically Venezuela.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other similar plants. The plant is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and white. Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly observed in its natural wild state.
