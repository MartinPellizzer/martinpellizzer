Aechmea aripoensis

Aechmea aripoensis, commonly known as the Aripo bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the humid forests of Venezuela.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central water-holding cup. The plant's foliage is typically dark green with prominent white or silver markings, enhancing its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, shaded environments and requires high humidity to flourish.

Aechmea aripoensis is notable for its ability to adapt to various substrates, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
