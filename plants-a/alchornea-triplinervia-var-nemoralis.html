Alchornea triplinervia var. nemoralis

Alchornea triplinervia var. nemoralis, commonly known as the roughleaf alchornea, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves that are often glandular and have a distinctive three-veined leaf structure. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by oval, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
