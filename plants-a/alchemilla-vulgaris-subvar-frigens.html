Alchemilla vulgaris subvar. frigens

Alchemilla vulgaris subvar. frigens, commonly known as frost alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It features a dense mat of silver-green, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in loose panicles. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or shaded gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate partial shade.
