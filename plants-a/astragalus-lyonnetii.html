Astragalus lyonnetii

Astragalus lyonnetii, commonly known as Lyonnet's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are often hairy and narrow.

Astragalus lyonnetii is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.
