Annona dumetorum

Annona dumetorum, commonly known as the "Buckeye Apple," is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by round, greenish fruits that mature to a yellow or orange color. The fruit has a thick, leathery rind and a creamy, aromatic pulp that is edible.

It is also noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
