Achillea carpatica

Achillea carpatica, commonly known as Carpathian yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its flat, composite flower heads that range from white to pale pink. The plant features finely divided, silver-gray leaves that are aromatic and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or grassy habitats.

Achillea carpatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
