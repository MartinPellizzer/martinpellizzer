Agrostis toluccensis

Agrostis toluccensis, commonly known as the Toluca bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to high-altitude regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soils.

Agrostis toluccensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and potential for horticultural use.
