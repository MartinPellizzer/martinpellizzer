Aira breviculmis

Aira breviculmis, commonly known as the short-bract airgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, spike-like inflorescence produces numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Aira breviculmis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
