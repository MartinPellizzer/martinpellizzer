Achyrocline alata

Achyrocline alata, commonly known as the silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are characterized by their silvery or grayish underside, giving it its common name.

Achyrocline alata is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
