Agave karwinskii

Agave karwinskii, commonly known as the Karwinski's agave, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It features a rosette of thick, blue-gray leaves that are covered in a whitish bloom, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with yellow flowers, typically blooming once in its lifetime. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
