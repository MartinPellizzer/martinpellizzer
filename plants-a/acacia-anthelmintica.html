Acacia anthelmintica

Acacia anthelmintica, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with traditional medicinal uses, including antiparasitic properties.

Acacia anthelmintica is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
