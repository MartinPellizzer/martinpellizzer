Acaena leptacantha var. grosseaculeata

Acaena leptacantha var. grosseaculeata, commonly known as the giant burr plant, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is notable for its large, woody fruits that contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by animals. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forest margins and wetlands.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain environments.
