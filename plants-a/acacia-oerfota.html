Acacia oerfota

Acacia oerfota, commonly known as the silver acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, fern-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery or greyish coating. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in globular or cylindrical heads, which bloom during the warmer months. Acacia oerfota is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for dry climates.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soils.
