Allophylus leptococcus

Allophylus leptococcus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved alpine holly, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.

Allophylus leptococcus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climate conditions.
