Acacia sericata

Acacia sericata, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow to orange globular inflorescences, which are composed of numerous small, fragrant flowers. Acacia sericata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
