Apargia alpina var. gouanii

Apargia alpina var. gouanii, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and mountain slopes. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery down.

This variety is noted for its resilience to cold temperatures and is an important component of alpine ecosystems.
