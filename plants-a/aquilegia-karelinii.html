Aquilegia karelinii

Aquilegia karelinii, commonly known as the Karakachan bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces bright red or orange seed pods that split open to release seeds.

Aquilegia karelinii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
