Armeria alpina subsp. trachyphylla

Armeria alpina subsp.

trachyphylla, commonly known as the roughleaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It features basal rosettes of linear, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine, rough hairs.

The plant produces erect flowering stems bearing clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
