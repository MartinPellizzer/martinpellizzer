Ardisia perpuncticulosa

Ardisia perpuncticulosa, commonly known as the speckled ardisia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive white or pale spots, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
