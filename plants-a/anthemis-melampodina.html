Anthemis melampodina

Anthemis melampodina, commonly known as the yellow fleabane, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Anthemis melampodina is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
