Asparagus acutifolius var. ambiguus

Asparagus acutifolius var. ambiguus, commonly known as the Spanish asparagus or wild asparagus, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems with small, scale-like leaves that are often densely packed. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, red berries containing the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and is sometimes cultivated for its edible shoots.
