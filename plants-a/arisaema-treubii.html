Arisaema treubii

Arisaema treubii, commonly known as the Treub's arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and spadix inflorescence. The plant produces a unique, often toxic, berry-like structure that contains the actual flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
