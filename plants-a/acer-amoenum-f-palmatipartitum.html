Acer amoenum f. palmatipartitum

Acer amoenum f. palmatipartitum, commonly known as the Japanese maple with palmate leaves, is a variant of the Amur maple.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that are often deeply divided, giving it a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance. The plant is native to eastern Asia and thrives in temperate climates. It produces small, red flowers in early spring, followed by ornamental samaras that add visual interest in the fall.

Its foliage changes color throughout the seasons, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
