Arundina revoluta

Arundina revoluta, commonly known as the bamboo orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, bamboo-like stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and thrives in well-drained soil. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Arundina revoluta is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its cultural significance in Asian horticulture.
