Amsinckia incurva

Amsinckia incurva, commonly known as the curlyleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, curling leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Amsinckia incurva is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
