Acacia biflora

Acacia biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery foliage and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, flat seed pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional Aboriginal diets. Acacia biflora is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and shelter for various native animals.
