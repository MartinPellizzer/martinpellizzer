Ardisia conoidea

Ardisia conoidea, commonly known as the Christmas berry, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green elliptical leaves and produces small, red berries that resemble Christmas ornaments, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its attractive foliage and ornamental fruit. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant.

The berries are edible but should be consumed with caution due to their mild toxicity.
