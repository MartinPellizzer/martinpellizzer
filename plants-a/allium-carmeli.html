Allium carmeli

Allium carmeli, commonly known as Carmel garlic, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in culinary applications.

Allium carmeli is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its culinary versatility.
