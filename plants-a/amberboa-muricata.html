Amberboa muricata

Amberboa muricata, commonly known as the spiny amaranth, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often red or orange-colored bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces small, inconspicuous true flowers surrounded by showy, fleshy structures called cyathia. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and adaptability contribute to its cultivation in various climates.
