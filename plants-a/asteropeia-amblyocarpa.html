Asteropeia amblyocarpa

Asteropeia amblyocarpa, commonly known as the rough-fruited aster.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually rough and hairy, providing a degree of drought resistance.

The fruit is a dry capsule that splits open to release its seeds, aiding in its dispersal.
