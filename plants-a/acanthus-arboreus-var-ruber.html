Acanthus arboreus var. ruber

Acanthus arboreus var. ruber, commonly known as the red-flowered horse's ear, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features broad, wavy, silver-green leaves that are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant produces showy, spiky red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique foliage and vibrant floral display make it a standout addition to any garden.
