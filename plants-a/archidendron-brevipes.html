Archidendron brevipes

Archidendron brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed legume, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and pinnate leaves with small, elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several flattened, bean-like seeds.

Archidendron brevipes is often used in traditional medicine and has potential ecological value in agroforestry systems.
