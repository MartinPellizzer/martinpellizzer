Adiantum ramyam

Adiantum ramyam, commonly known as the Indian maidenhair fern, is a species native to the wet tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble the feathers of a bird, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture to flourish. Its fronds are typically bioluminescent, displaying a striking blue-green hue under certain light conditions.

Adiantum ramyam is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its traditional use in herbal medicine.
