Ambrosia caudata

Ambrosia caudata, commonly known as the roughleaf ambrosia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features alternate, rough-textured leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters. Ambrosia caudata is part of the Ambrosiaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
