Aristolochia paracleta

Aristolochia paracleta, commonly known as the Brazilian pipevine, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, intricate flowers that resemble pipes, often with a deep purple or greenish hue. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though caution is needed due to its poisonous properties.
