Asplenium shawii

Asplenium shawii, commonly known as Shaw's spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often clustered in dense rosettes. The plant typically grows on rocks or trees, anchoring itself with rhizomes and absorbing moisture from the air. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Asplenium shawii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
