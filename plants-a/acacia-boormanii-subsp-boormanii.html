Acacia boormanii subsp. boormanii

Acacia boormanii subsp.

boormanii, commonly known as the flame acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year.

The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand drought conditions.
