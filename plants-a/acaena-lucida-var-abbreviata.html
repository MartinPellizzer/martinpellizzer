Acaena lucida var. abbreviata

Acaena lucida var. abbreviata, commonly known as the small-flowered spinulose nettle, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, round, hard seeds enclosed in a prickly burr, which aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animals. It thrives in coastal and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting native insect populations and its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
