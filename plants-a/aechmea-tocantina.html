Aechmea tocantina

Aechmea tocantina, commonly known as the Tocantins pineapple plant, is a species of bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces a striking inflorescence with brightly colored bracts that range from red to yellow. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Aechmea tocantina is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.

Morphology

Aechmea tocantina has rosette-shaped leaves that are narrow, grass-like, and arranged in a compact spiral, with a dark green color and a waxy coating.

The plant lacks a true stem, instead having a short, thick, and fibrous base that resembles a pseudostem. Its flowers are borne in a spike-like inflorescence, with showy purple bracts and small, tubular flowers that bloom in the dry season, primarily pollinated by insects. The fruit is a red, fleshy berry that disperses seeds through animal consumption.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring the plant in rocky or dry soil.
