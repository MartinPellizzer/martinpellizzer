Alstroemeria ovata

Alstroemeria ovata, commonly known as the oval alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Alstroemeria ovata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
