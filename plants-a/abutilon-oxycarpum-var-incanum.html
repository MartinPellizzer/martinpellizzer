Abutilon oxycarpum var. incanum

Abutilon oxycarpum var. incanum, commonly known as the silverleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and pendulous clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain small, round seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
