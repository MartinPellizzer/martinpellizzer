Aristolochia eriantha

Aristolochia eriantha, commonly known as the velvet pipevine, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and striking, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe. The plant produces small, greenish-white berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
