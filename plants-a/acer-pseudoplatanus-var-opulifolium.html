Acer pseudoplatanus var. opulifolium

Acer pseudoplatanus var. opulifolium, commonly known as the opulus maple, is a variant of the common maple with distinctive foliage.

It features palmately lobed leaves that are often more deeply divided than those of the typical species, giving it a unique appearance. The leaves are typically bright green in summer and turn yellow in autumn, adding ornamental value. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and parks.
