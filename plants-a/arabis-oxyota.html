Arabis oxyota

Arabis oxyota, commonly known as the rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant features basal rosettes of oblanceolate, dark green leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are white or pale yellow, arranged in racemes, and bloom in early spring.

Arabis oxyota is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
