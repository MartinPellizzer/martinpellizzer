Aldama tuberosa var. guaranitica

Aldama tuberosa var. guaranitica, commonly known as the Guarani tuber, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its fibrous roots and tubers, which are rich in starch and used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lanceolate and slightly hairy, while its flowers are small and white or pale pink, often clustered in inflorescences.

Due to its cultural significance and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and ethnobotry researchers.
