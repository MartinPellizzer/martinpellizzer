Acacia seyal var. multijuga

Acacia seyal var. multijuga, commonly known as the pale acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous tiny, feather-like leaflets.

Acacia seyal var. multijuga is well adapted to drought conditions and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of gum arabic.
