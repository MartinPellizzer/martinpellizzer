Aspidium auriculatum

Aspidium auriculatum, commonly known as the ear-shaped water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are pinnate, with small, rounded lobes that resemble ears, giving it its common name.

Aspidium auriculatum plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for small organisms in its native ecosystems.
