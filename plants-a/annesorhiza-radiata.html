Annesorhiza radiata

Annesorhiza radiata, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats like prairies and roadsides.

Annesorhiza radiata is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
