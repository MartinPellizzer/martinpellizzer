Allium schmidtianum

Allium schmidtianum, commonly known as Schmidt's onion, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features bulbous roots and produces upright, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant blooms with umbels of white or pale purple flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens for both its visual appeal and culinary uses.
