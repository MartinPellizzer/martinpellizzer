Abutilon gaumeri

Abutilon gaumeri, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive texture. The plant produces showy, spathate flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Abutilon gaumeri is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and vibrant floral displays.
