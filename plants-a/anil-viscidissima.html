Anil viscidissima

Anil viscidissima, commonly known as the sticky anil, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular hairs that give its stems and leaves a sticky texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a lobed or heart-shaped appearance.

Anil viscidissima is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
