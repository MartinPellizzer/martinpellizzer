Allium fominianum

Allium fominianum, commonly known as the Fominian garlic, is a bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes various species of onions, garlic, and leeks. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are grass-like and narrow, while its bulbs are typically smaller than those of common garlic.

This species is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
