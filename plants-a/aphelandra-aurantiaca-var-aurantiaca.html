Aphelandra aurantiaca var. aurantiaca

Aphelandra aurantiaca var. aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking, elongated leaves with a glossy, dark green surface and prominent veins. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom throughout the year. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor cultivation and humid environments.
