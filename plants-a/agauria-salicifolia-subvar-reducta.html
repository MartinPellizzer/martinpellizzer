Agauria salicifolia subvar. reducta

Agauria salicifolia subvar. reducta, commonly known as the narrowleaf willow.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, willow-like stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins, and it produces small, winged seeds for wind dispersal.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing stream banks.
