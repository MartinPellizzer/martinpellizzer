Anemone peruviana

Anemone peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian anemone, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range from white to pink or purple. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine regions, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers are usually borne on long, slender stems.

Anemone peruviana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climate conditions.
