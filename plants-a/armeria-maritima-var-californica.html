Armeria maritima var. californica

Armeria maritima var. californica, commonly known as the California thrift, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces dense clusters of white or pink flowers in early spring. The plant has needle-like, silver-gray leaves that are adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
