Ageratum lasseauxii

Ageratum lasseauxii, commonly known as the Mexican blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has hairy leaves and stems, which help it retain moisture in its native arid environment. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.

Ageratum lasseauxii is often used in ornamental landscaping for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
