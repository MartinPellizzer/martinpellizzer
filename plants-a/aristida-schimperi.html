Aristida schimperi

Aristida schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian needle grass, is a perennial grass native to Ethiopia and parts of East Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of tiny, spikelet-bearing flowers, which are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in restoration projects and as a forage plant for livestock.
