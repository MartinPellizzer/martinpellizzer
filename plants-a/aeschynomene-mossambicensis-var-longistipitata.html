Aeschynomene mossambicensis var. longistipitata

Aeschynomene mossambicensis var. longistipitata, commonly known as the long-stemmed aeshynomene, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is often found in moist, sandy soils.

This variety is notable for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats and its potential use in ecological restoration projects.
