Acinos alpinus var. pseudacinos

Acinos alpinus var. pseudacinos, commonly known as the Alpine Everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils at high elevations. Its small, daisy-like flowers bloom in late summer, producing a delicate, fluffy appearance.

The plant is adapted to cold climates and is often found in meadows and slopes where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
