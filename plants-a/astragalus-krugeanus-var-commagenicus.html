Astragalus krugeanus var. commagenicus

Astragalus krugeanus var. commagenicus, commonly known as the Commagene milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
