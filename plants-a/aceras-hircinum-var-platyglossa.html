Aceras hircinum var. platyglossa

Aceras hircinum var. platyglossa, commonly known as the broad-leafed hircinum, is a variant of the hircinum species within the genus Aceras.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy underside. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in garden settings.
