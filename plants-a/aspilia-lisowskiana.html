Aspilia lisowskiana

Aspilia lisowskiana, commonly known as the Lisowskiana torchflower, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, showy yellow flowers that resemble torches, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.

Aspilia lisowskiana is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a resilient and visually distinctive species.
