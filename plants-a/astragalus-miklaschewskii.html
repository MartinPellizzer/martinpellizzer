Astragalus miklaschewskii

Astragalus miklaschewskii, commonly known as the Miklaschewsky milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus miklaschewskii is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
