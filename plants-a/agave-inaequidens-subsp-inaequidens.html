Agave inaequidens subsp. inaequidens

Agave inaequidens subsp.

inaequidens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico. It is characterized by its rosette of stiff, blue-green leaves that are narrow and often have a waxy coating.

The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with yellowish-green flowers, which bloom after several years. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil and minimal water.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
