Amorphophallus barbatus

Amorphophallus barbatus, commonly known as the African giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and a distinctive underground tuber. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The tuber is edible and used as a starchy food source in some regions.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.
