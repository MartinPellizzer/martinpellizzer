Amaryllis longifolia

Amaryllis longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered amaryllis, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 3 meters in height, supporting clusters of large, showy flowers. The blooms, which are typically pink or white, are trumpet-shaped and have a pleasant fragrance. This species is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family, known for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.

It is often cultivated for its dramatic floral display and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
