Acalypha communis f. decumbens

Acalypha communis f. decumbens, commonly known as the prostrate spurge, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features succulent, fleshy stems that often lie close to the ground, forming a dense mat. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with smooth or slightly hairy surfaces, and they often have a waxy texture. Small, greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters, and the plant produces tiny, spherical berries that are often red or yellow when mature.

It is well adapted to humid environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
