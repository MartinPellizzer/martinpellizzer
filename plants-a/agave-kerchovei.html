Agave kerchovei

Agave kerchovei, commonly known as the White Agave, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, rosette-shaped stems covered in dense, white to grayish-blue foliage. The plant produces large, branched inflorescences that bear yellow flowers, which are typically pollinated by bats and insects. Its succulent leaves store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments.

Agave kerchovei is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
