Adonis dentata

Adonis dentata, commonly known as the toothed adonis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant features a short, hairy stem with palmately divided leaves, and its flowers are usually pink or white with prominent yellow stamens. Adonis dentata is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
