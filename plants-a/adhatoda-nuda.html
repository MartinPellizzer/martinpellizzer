Adhatoda nuda

Adhatoda nuda, commonly known as the Indian Trumpet or White Trumpet, is a flowering plant native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and distinctive trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a tubular shape that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
