Aspidium alcicorne

Aspidium alcicorne, commonly known as the rough holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its robust, upright fronds that are typically divided into lance-shaped segments. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are clustered in strobila. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions.

Aspidium alcicorne is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
