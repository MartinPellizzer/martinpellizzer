Acetosella angustifolia

Acetosella angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval, pointed leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Acetosella angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
