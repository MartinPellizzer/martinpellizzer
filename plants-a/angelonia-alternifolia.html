Angelonia alternifolia

Angelonia alternifolia, commonly known as the summer snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces clusters of brightly colored blossoms in shades of red, pink, and white, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Angelonia alternifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its long blooming period and drought tolerance.
