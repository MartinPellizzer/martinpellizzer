Allophylus warneckei

Allophylus warneckei, commonly known as the African holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and riverine habitats.

Allophylus warneckei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
