Adyseton serpyllifolium

Adyseton serpyllifolium, commonly known as creeping rockrider, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically forms dense mats of foliage and is characterized by its small, dark green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Adyseton serpyllifolium is an important component of sagebrush steppe ecosystems.
