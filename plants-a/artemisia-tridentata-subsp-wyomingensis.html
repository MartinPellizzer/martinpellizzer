Artemisia tridentata subsp. wyomingensis

Artemisia tridentata subsp.

wyomingensis, commonly known as big sagebrush, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense heads. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry conditions.

Big sagebrush plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
