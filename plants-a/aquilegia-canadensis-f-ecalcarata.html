Aquilegia canadensis f. ecalcarata

Aquilegia canadensis f. ecalcarata, commonly known as the Canadian columbine with spurred petals, is a botanical variant of the native American columbine.

This subspecies is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals, which are elongated and recurved, giving it a unique floral appearance. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange, which bloom in late spring.

Its foliage consists of deeply lobed leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
