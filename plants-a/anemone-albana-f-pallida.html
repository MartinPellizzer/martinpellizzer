Anemone albana f. pallida

Anemone albana f. pallida, commonly known as the pale anemone, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a soft, silvery down. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Its delicate appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
