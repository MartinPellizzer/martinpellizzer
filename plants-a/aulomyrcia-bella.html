Aulomyrcia bella

Aulomyrcia bella, commonly known as the pink flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual impact. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that provide a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.

The flowers are not only visually appealing but also attract various pollinators such as bees and birds.
