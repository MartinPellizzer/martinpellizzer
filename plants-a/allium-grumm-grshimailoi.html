Allium grumm-grshimailoi

Allium grumm-grshimailoi, commonly known as the Armenian garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and clusters of white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, round bulbs that are often used in traditional Armenian cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
