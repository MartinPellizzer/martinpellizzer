Armeniaca vulgaris var. salicifolia

Armeniaca vulgaris var. salicifolia, commonly known as the white peach, is a variant of the peach tree characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and smooth, edible fruit.

This cultivar is native to China and is cultivated for its sweet, juicy fruit that ripens in late summer. The leaves are broad and ovate, with a silvery underside, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The tree is relatively disease-resistant and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal.
