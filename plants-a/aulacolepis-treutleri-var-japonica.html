Aulacolepis treutleri var. japonica

Aulacolepis treutleri var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese flatleaf fern, is a variant of the flatleaf fern native to East Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions. The plant features lanceolate, leathery leaves that are arranged in a compact rosette pattern. Its reproductive structures include small, clustered spore-bearing capsules that develop on the undersides of the leaves.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and indirect light.
