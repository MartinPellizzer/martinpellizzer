Agave zapopanensis

Agave zapopanensis, commonly known as the Zapopan agave, is a species native to the Mexican state of Jalisco.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears small, white flowers, which are often followed by the development of a rosette of offsets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
