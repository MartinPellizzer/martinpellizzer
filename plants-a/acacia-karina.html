Acacia karina

Acacia karina, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves that are typically green with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which bloom during the dry season, attracting various pollinators. Acacia karina is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

It is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and fodder.
