Acaciella submontana

Acaciella submontana, commonly known as the mountain acacia, is a shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its woody stems and dense foliage make it a hardy species suited to rocky soils.

Acaciella submontana plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
