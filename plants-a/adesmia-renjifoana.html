Adesmia renjifoana

Adesmia renjifoana, commonly known as the Andean burro's tail, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, fleshy, cylindrical stems that resemble a burr. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers clustered in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, adapting to the arid and high-altitude environments where it thrives.

Adesmia renjifoana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
