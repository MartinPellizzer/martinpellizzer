Ammannia capitellata

Ammannia capitellata, commonly known as the redroot dayflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and bright red stems, which become more prominent as the plant matures. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, followed by dark blue to black berries.

This plant is often found in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands.
