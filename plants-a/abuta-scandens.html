Abuta scandens

Abuta scandens, commonly known as the climbing abuta, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its woody vines that can climb to significant heights. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences called cyathia. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Abuta scandens is also noted for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.
