Antirrhinum litigiosum

Antirrhinum litigiosum, commonly known as the weedy snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bright orange or red tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in dry, open habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are adapted for pollination by insects.

Antirrhinum litigiosum is a hardy species that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil conditions.
