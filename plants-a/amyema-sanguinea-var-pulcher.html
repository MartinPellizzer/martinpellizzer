Amyema sanguinea var. pulcher

Amyema sanguinea var. pulcher, commonly known as the red-stemmed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of eucalyptus trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are typically red or orange, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is native to southeastern Australia and is often found in dry, open forests.
