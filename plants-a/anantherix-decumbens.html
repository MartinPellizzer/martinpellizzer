Anantherix decumbens

Anantherix decumbens, commonly known as the creeping antherix, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features a low-growing, sprawling habit with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in terminal inflorescences, with white or pale pink petals.

Anantherix decumbens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
