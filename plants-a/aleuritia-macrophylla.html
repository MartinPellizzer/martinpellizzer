Aleuritia macrophylla

Aleuritia macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf elder, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features compound leaves with elongated, serrated leaflets that can grow up to 15 cm in length. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
