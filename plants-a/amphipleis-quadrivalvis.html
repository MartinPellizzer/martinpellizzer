Amphipleis quadrivalvis

Amphipleis quadrivalvis, commonly known as the four-valved water lily, is a unique aquatic plant native to tropical regions.

It features striking, broad leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by long, flexible petioles. The plant's most distinctive characteristic is its four-parted seed pod, which splits open to release seeds when mature. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with showy petals that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to water bodies.
