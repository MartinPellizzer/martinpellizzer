Arisaema consanguineum f. latisectum

Arisaema consanguineum f. latisectum, commonly known as the broad-leaf arrowhead, is a variant of the arrowhead plant characterized by its distinctive, broad, arrow-shaped leaves.

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of southern China and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically a deep green, with a slightly waxy texture, and it produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences that are surrounded by a hooded spathe. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.

It is also used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
