Agrostis vulgaris var. violacea

Agrostis vulgaris var. violacea, commonly known as violet grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces spikes of purple to violet flowers in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds ornamental value to gardens.

The species is also valued for its role in ecological habitats, supporting various pollinators and soil stabilization.
