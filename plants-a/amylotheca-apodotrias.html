Amylotheca apodotrias

Amylotheca apodotrias, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink bracts that resemble coral, which are not true flowers but modified leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Amylotheca apodotrias is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
