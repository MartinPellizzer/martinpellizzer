Anticharis ebracteata

Anticharis ebracteata, commonly known as the slender antirrhinum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender stems and showy, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of red or orange. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Anticharis ebracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
