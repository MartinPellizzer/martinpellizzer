Alyssum balkanicum var. elatum

Alyssum balkanicum var. elatum, commonly known as the Balkan Alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Alyssum balkanicum var. elatum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
