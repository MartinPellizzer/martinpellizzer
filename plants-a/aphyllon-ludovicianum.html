Aphyllon ludovicianum

Aphyllon ludovicianum, commonly known as the Ludovician ghostplant, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a dense rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically light green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in a spike at the center of the rosette. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands.
