Acacia unguicula

Acacia unguicula, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and small, phyllodes that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces yellow floral spikes that bloom in spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in drought conditions.

Acacia unguicula is also valued for its hardy nature and is often used in landscaping and erosion control.
