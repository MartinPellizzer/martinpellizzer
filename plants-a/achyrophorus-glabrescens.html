Achyrophorus glabrescens

Achyrophorus glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth achyrophorus, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white. Its roots are fibrous and can form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization.

The species is notable for its ability to tolerate wet conditions and is often found in riparian zones.
