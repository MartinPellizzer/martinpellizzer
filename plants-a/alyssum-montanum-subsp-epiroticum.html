Alyssum montanum subsp. epiroticum

Alyssum montanum subsp.

epiroticum, commonly known as the Epirotic alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant has hairy, linear leaves and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is often found in rocky or limestone areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

This subspecies is closely related to other alyssum species but is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and floral characteristics.
