Alstroemeria paupercula

Alstroemeria paupercula, commonly known as the poor man's lily, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
