Athyrium solenopteris

Athyrium solenopteris, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Japanese shield fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically divided into narrow, elongated segments. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its elegant appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
