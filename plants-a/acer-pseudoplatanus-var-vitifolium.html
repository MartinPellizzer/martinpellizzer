Acer pseudoplatanus var. vitifolium

Acer pseudoplatanus var. vitifolium, commonly known as the European maple with grape leaves, is a variant of the European maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive, large, lobed leaves that resemble grape leaves in shape. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub.

Its foliage provides ornamental value, especially in the autumn when the leaves change color.
