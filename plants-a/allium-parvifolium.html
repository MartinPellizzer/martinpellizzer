Allium parvifolium

Allium parvifolium, commonly known as Sierra onion, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of pale purple to white flowers. The plant produces small, rounded bulbs that are edible and have a mild onion flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine and mountainous regions.

Sierra onion is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in local cuisines.
