Asplenium hirsutum

Asplenium hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it its common name. This fern typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as rock crevices or on the bases of trees. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread slowly and form dense mats.

Asplenium hirsutum is a hardy plant that is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various conditions.
