Aglaia odorata var. microphyllina

Aglaia odorata var. microphyllina, commonly known as the small-leaved Aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and perfumery.
