Aster umbachii

Aster umbachii, commonly known as the eastern aster, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and produces showy purple to pink flowers in the fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and a basal rosette structure, which helps it retain moisture. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its composite flower heads.

Aster umbachii plays a role in supporting pollinators and is often found in natural areas and conservation sites.
