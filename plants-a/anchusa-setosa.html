Anchusa setosa

Anchusa setosa, commonly known as the woolly blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.

Anchusa setosa is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
