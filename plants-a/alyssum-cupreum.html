Alyssum cupreum

Alyssum cupreum, commonly known as coppery alyssum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale yellow to coppery red, typically blooming in early spring. Its foliage is characterized by narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Alyssum cupreum is often grown as an ornamental plant for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
