Aster albanicus

Aster albanicus, commonly known as the Albanian aster, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its flowers are blue to violet in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Aster albanicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
