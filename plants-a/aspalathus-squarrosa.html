Aspalathus squarrosa

Aspalathus squarrosa, commonly known as the silver bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid conditions, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in Mediterranean climates.
