Aframomum macrospermum

Aframomum macrospermum, commonly known as African cardamom, is a tropical plant native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, spindle-shaped rhizomes. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to white flowers and has strap-shaped, dark green leaves. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to true cardamom.

The botanical features of Aframomum macrospermum make it a valuable species in both culinary and medicinal applications.
