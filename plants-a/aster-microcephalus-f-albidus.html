Aster microcephalus f. albidus

Aster microcephalus f. albidus, commonly known as the white wood aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has a compact growth habit with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or wet meadows.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
