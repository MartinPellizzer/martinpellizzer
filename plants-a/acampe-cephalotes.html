Acampe cephalotes

Acampe cephalotes, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant has compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its fast growth rate and ability to provide shade in urban landscapes.
