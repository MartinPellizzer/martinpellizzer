Alpinia penduliflora

Alpinia penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging-flower ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its gracefully drooping inflorescences that bear small, fragrant flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, enhancing its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.

Alpinia penduliflora is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
