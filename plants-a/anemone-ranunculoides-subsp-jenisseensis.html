Anemone ranunculoides subsp. jenisseensis

Anemone ranunculoides subsp.

jenisseensis, commonly known as the Siberian anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the cold regions of Siberia and northern Asia. It features showy, white to pale pink flowers with five to seven petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has palmate, lobed leaves that are often hairy and adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra or alpine environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
