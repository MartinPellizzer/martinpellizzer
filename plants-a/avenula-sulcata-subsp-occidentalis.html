Avenula sulcata subsp. occidentalis

Avenula sulcata subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western grovegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant has slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive blue-green color. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged along the stem.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
