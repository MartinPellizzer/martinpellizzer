Anemone tetonensis

Anemone tetonensis, commonly known as the Teton anemone, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with a yellow center. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks. It is adapted to cold climates and is typically found at elevations above 6,000 feet.

The Teton anemone is an important component of alpine ecosystems and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
