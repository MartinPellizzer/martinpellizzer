Aira pulchella subsp. provincialis

Aira pulchella subsp.

provincialis, commonly known as the provincial false oat, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, white to pale pink flower heads that bloom in summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.

Its subspecific classification highlights its distinct characteristics compared to other members of the Aira genus.
