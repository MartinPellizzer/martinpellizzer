Aristida arida

Aristida arida, commonly known as the desert dropseed, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often pale green to brown in color. The plant is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in poor soil and low-water environments. It is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is used in restoration projects due to its hardiness.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
