Acer tataricum f. rotundifolium

Acer tataricum f. rotundifolium, commonly known as the round-leaf maple, is a distinctive cultivar of the maple tree characterized by its uniquely shaped leaves.

The leaves are broadly ovate with a rounded base and a smooth margin, giving the plant a striking visual appearance. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras in late summer.

Its foliage turns vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
