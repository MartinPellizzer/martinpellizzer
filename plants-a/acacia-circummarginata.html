Acacia circummarginata

Acacia circummarginata, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact spikes, which are typically followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often growing in coastal dunes and rocky areas.

Acacia circummarginata is adapted to salty air and can tolerate moderate drought conditions.
