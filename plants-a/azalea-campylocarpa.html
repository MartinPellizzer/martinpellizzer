Azalea campylocarpa

Azalea campylocarpa, commonly known as the mountain azalea, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are elliptical and leathery, providing a dense canopy that supports various wildlife.

Azalea campylocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
