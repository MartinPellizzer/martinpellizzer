Adhatoda adhatoda

Adhatoda adhatoda, commonly known as Indian bellflower, is a tropical shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive glandular surface. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in Ayurveda.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
