Aphelandra hapala

Aphelandra hapala, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish or purplish tint. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Aphelandra hapala thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant for its ornamental foliage and unique floral display.
