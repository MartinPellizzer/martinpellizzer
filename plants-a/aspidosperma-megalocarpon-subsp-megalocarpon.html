Aspidosperma megalocarpon subsp. megalocarpon

Aspidosperma megalocarpon subsp.

megalocarpon, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Pouteria* species, is a tropical tree native to South America. It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and large, glossy leaves that are opposite in arrangement.

The plant produces clusters of small, white or yellowish flowers, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is also valued for its timber and has some traditional medicinal uses.
