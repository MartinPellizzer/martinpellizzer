Abies firma var. bifida

Abies firma var. bifida, commonly known as the twin-leaf fir, is a rare and unique variant of the fir tree native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its distinctive bifid (split) leaves, which give it a striking appearance compared to typical fir species. The plant exhibits a conical shape with dense, dark green foliage that turns a silvery-blue hue during the winter months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found at high elevations where it forms part of the subalpine forest ecosystem.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
