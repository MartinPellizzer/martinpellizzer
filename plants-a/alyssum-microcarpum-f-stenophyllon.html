Alyssum microcarpum f. stenophyllon

Alyssum microcarpum f. stenophyllon, commonly known as the narrow-leaf alyssum, is a compact, low-growing plant characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly thriving in rocky or sandy soils. The foliage is typically narrow and linear, giving the plant a distinctive, upright appearance. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
