Acaciopsis amentacea

Acaciopsis amentacea, commonly known as the white acacia, is a small, deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Acaciopsis amentacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand dry conditions.
