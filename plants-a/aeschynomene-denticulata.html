Aeschynomene denticulata

Aeschynomene denticulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aeschynomene, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. The plant features trifoliate leaves with finely toothed margins and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. Its root system is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it a valuable plant for soil enrichment.

Aeschynomene denticulata is often used in forage and as a cover crop due to its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil conditions.
