Alchemilla vulgaris var. sarmentosa

Alchemilla vulgaris var. sarmentosa, commonly known as sweet william sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features creeping stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in branched inflorescences. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
