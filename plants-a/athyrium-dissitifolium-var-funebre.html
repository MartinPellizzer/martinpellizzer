Athyrium dissitifolium var. funebre

Athyrium dissitifolium var. funebre, commonly known as the Black Fern, is a fern species native to the wet tropical forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black fronds that give it its common name, and it typically grows in shaded, moist environments. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and produces spore-bearing structures called sori on the undersides of its leaves. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine and horticulture due to its ornamental value.

The species is valued for its unique appearance and adaptability to humid, shaded conditions.
