Astragalus rumelicus subsp. euboicus

Astragalus rumelicus subsp.

euboicus, commonly known as the Euboean milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe, particularly Greece and the Balkans. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Its subspecies status reflects geographic and morphological differences from the main species.
