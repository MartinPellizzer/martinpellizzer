Arabis subpinnatifida var. impar

Arabis subpinnatifida var. impar, commonly known as the odd-leaved rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and ornamental plants. The plant features basal leaves that are pinnately divided with an odd number of lobes, giving it its characteristic appearance. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in racemes.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
