Alvaradoa amorphoides

Alvaradoa amorphoides, commonly known as the woolly alvaradoa, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage that helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle floral display. Its woody stems are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

Alvaradoa amorphoides is adapted to survive in low-nutrient soils and can tolerate periods of drought.
