Andropogon nardus var. goeringii

Andropogon nardus var. goeringii, commonly known as Goering's sweetgrass, is a variant of the sweetgrass species native to North America.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, characterized by its slender, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often used in traditional crafts and floral arrangements. Its aromatic leaves have been historically utilized by Indigenous peoples for their medicinal and ceremonial properties.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a symbol of cultural heritage.
