Aneilema beniniense

Aneilema beniniense, commonly known as the Benin arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are broad and arrow-shaped, contributing to its common name.

The plant is valued for its edible tubers, which are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
