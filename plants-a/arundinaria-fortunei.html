Arundinaria fortunei

Arundinaria fortunei, commonly known as the bamboo palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a flowering plant that produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible fruits. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, feather-like leaves that give it a graceful appearance. It thrives in humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Arundinaria fortunei is also valued for its ability to tolerate shade and its air-purifying properties.
