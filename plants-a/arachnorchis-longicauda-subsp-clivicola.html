Arachnorchis longicauda subsp. clivicola

Arachnorchis longicauda subsp.

clivicola, commonly known as the cliff orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the alpine regions of southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant typically grows on rocky outcrops and cliff faces, where it thrives in well-drained, nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single, elongated flower spike.

This subspecies is notable for its specialized habitat and is considered a rare and protected species in its native range.
