Asarina antirrhiniflora

Asarina antirrhiniflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces bell-shaped, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it is often found in alpine meadows.

Asarina antirrhiniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
