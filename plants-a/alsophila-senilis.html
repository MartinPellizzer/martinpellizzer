Alsophila senilis

Alsophila senilis, commonly known as the silver tree fern, is a tropical evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fronds that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant has a unique ability to produce new shoots from the base, allowing it to form dense clumps over time. Its fronds are usually divided into segments with a silvery-blue hue on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Alsophila senilis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
