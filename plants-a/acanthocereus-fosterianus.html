Acanthocereus fosterianus

Acanthocereus fosterianus, commonly known as the night blooming cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is a solitary, columnar cactus with a ribbed stem and spines that provide protection against herbivores. The plant produces large, fragrant, white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
