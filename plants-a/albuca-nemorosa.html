Albuca nemorosa

Albuca nemorosa, commonly known as the forest albuca, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features strap-like, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a distinctive white midrib. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Albuca nemorosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
