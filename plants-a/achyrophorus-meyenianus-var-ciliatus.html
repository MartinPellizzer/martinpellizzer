Achyrophorus meyenianus var. ciliatus

Achyrophorus meyenianus var. ciliatus, commonly known as the Sierra foothill buckwheat, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves with ciliate margins, which give it its specific epithet. The plant produces showy clusters of white to pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
