Amblyanthera palustris var. almadensis

Amblyanthera palustris var. almadensis, commonly known as the marsh amblyanthera, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in South America.

It is characterized by its slender, herbaceous stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swampy or marshy environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
