Amorpha ouachitensis

Amorpha ouachitensis, commonly known as the silky mesquite, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, feathery leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silky white pubescence. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically yellow or pale green, arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and it plays an important role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.

Amorpha ouachitensis is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks.
