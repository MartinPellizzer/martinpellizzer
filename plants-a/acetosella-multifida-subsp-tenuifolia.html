Acetosella multifida subsp. tenuifolia

Acetosella multifida subsp.

tenuifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features small, trifoliate leaves with a distinctive heart-shaped base and delicate, nodding yellow flowers.

The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
