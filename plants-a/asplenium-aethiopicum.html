Asplenium aethiopicum

Asplenium aethiopicum, commonly known as the African maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble the shape of a maidenhair. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Asplenium aethiopicum is also noted for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
