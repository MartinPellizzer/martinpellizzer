Aster robynsianus

Aster robynsianus, commonly known as Robyn's aster, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features showy, daisy-like flowers with violet-blue petals and yellow centers, blooming in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Robyn's aster is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
