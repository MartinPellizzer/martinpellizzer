Amyris pacis

Amyris pacis, commonly known as the pacis amyris, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are usually simple, alternate, and have a rough texture, while its bark is thick and deeply fissured.

Amyris pacis is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
