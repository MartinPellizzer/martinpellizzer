Astragalus wilhelminae

Astragalus wilhelminae, commonly known as the Wilhelmine milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Astragalus wilhelminae plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
