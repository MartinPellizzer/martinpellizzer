Asplenium aequibasis

Asplenium aequibasis, commonly known as the broad-leaved maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bright green and have a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate regions. Its spores are produced on the undersides of its fronds, which are clustered in sori.

Asplenium aequibasis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
