Arrabidaea pulchella

Arrabidaea pulchella, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its showy, yellow, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and its fruit is a legume that splits open to release seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
