Archibaccharis nicaraguensis

Archibaccharis nicaraguensis, commonly known as the Nicaraguan wax myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule containing several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
