Audibertiella vaseyi

Audibertiella vaseyi, commonly known as the Vasey's orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricately patterned flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often on rocky substrates or in forest understories. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces fragrant blooms in shades of white and pale yellow.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
