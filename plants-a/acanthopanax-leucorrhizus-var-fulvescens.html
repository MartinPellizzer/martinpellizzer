Acanthopanax leucorrhizus var. fulvescens

Acanthopanax leucorrhizus var. fulvescens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered five-leaf aralia, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which typically consist of five leaflets with a serrated edge. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
