Arundinaria deflexa

Arundinaria deflexa, commonly known as the flexible bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems that often bend toward the ground, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Arundinaria deflexa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its graceful form and adaptability to various soil conditions.
