Anthericum thyrsoideum

Anthericum thyrsoideum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arum, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves and a distinctive, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a showy, greenish-white spathe. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects, particularly flies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Anthericum thyrsoideum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
