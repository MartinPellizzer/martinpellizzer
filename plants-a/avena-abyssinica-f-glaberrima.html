Avena abyssinica f. glaberrima

Avena abyssinica f. glaberrima, commonly known as the Abyssinian oat, is a perennial grass species native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its glabrous (hairless) leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions and is adapted to cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both horticultural and agricultural purposes.
