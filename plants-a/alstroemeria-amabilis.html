Alstroemeria amabilis

Alstroemeria amabilis, commonly known as the Peruvian lily, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, featuring strap-like leaves and a compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Alstroemeria amabilis is also valued for its long-lasting blooms and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental qualities.
