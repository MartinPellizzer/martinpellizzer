Acacia diastemata

Acacia diastemata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typical of many Acacia species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Acacia diastemata is also valued for its hardy nature and is used in revegetation projects.
