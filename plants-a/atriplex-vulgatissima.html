Atriplex vulgatissima

Atriplex vulgatissima, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, often gray-green leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense spikes. It has a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Atriplex vulgatissima is also known for its ability to tolerate saline soils, making it a valuable plant in reclaiming degraded lands.
