Acrocephalus verbenaceus

Acrocephalus verbenaceus, commonly known as the white goosegrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Verbenaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule.

Acrocephalus verbenaceus plays a role in supporting local insect populations and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
