Amaranthus kloosianus

Amaranthus kloosianus, commonly known as the redroot amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive red-tinged stems, which become more pronounced as the plant matures. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are small and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically for its edible leaves and seeds.
