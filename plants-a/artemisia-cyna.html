Artemisia cyna

Artemisia cyna, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant contains volatile oils, including thujone, which contribute to its strong medicinal and aromatic properties. Artemisia cyna thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing in dry, open habitats.

It has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.
