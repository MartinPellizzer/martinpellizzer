Agrimonia mollis var. bicknellii

Agrimonia mollis var. bicknellii, commonly known as Bicknell's agrimony, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and aromatic foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

Bicknell's agrimony is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
