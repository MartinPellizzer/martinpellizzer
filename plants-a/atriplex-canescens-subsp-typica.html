Atriplex canescens subsp. typica

Atriplex canescens subsp.

typica, commonly known as silverleaf chenopod, is a perennial shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, powdery hairs that help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in compact clusters, and its leaves are typically alternate and lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to low moisture conditions.

This species is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of these regions.
