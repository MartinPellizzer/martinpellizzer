Aleuritia anisodora

Aleuritia anisodora, commonly known as the Chinese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching branches and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in early spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and hairy underneath. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Aleuritia anisodora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
