Arachus vicioides

Arachus vicioides, commonly known as the black gram or urad, is an annual leguminous plant native to South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain several dark brown seeds. The plant is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils. It is valued for its high protein content and is widely cultivated for both food and fodder.

The flowers are typically pale yellow to white, and the plant is often grown in rotation with other crops to improve soil fertility.
