Artemisia turczaninoviana

Artemisia turczaninoviana, commonly known as the Turczaninov's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its strong, camphor-like scent.

Artemisia turczaninoviana is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
