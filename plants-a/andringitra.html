Andringitra

Andringitra, commonly known as the Andringitra bamboo, is a tall, slender grass species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit and long, hollow stalks. The plant thrives in the humid, tropical climates of the eastern rainforests of Madagascar. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often used in traditional crafts by local communities.

Andringitra plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
