Amelanchier utahensis

Amelanchier utahensis, commonly known as the Utah serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its showy white or pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that ripen in late summer, providing food for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes.

The Utah serviceberry is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
