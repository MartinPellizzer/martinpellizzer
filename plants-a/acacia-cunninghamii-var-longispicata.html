Acacia cunninghamii var. longispicata

Acacia cunninghamii var. longispicata, commonly known as the long-spined wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain seeds, and it is adapted to dry, sandy soils. It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

The variety is distinguished by its longer, more prominent spines compared to other acacia species.
