Acacia pulchella var. villosa

Acacia pulchella var. villosa, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in compact spherical or cylindrical clusters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glandular base.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
