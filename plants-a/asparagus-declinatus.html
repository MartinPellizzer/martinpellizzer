Asparagus declinatus

Asparagus declinatus, commonly known as the drooping asparagus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces clusters of delicate, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Asparagus declinatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its graceful appearance and easy care requirements.
