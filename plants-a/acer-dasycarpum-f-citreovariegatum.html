Acer dasycarpum f. citreovariegatum

Acer dasycarpum f. citreovariegatum, commonly known as the variegated maple, is a decorative cultivar of the maple tree characterized by its distinctive leaf patterns.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a serrated margin and display a striking combination of yellow, white, and green variegation. This plant is native to China and thrives in temperate climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight. It produces small, red or orange flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds in the fall.

The variegated maple is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
