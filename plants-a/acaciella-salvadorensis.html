Acaciella salvadorensis

Acaciella salvadorensis, commonly known as the Salvadoran acacia, is a small shrub native to El Salvador.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
