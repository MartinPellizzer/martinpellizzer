Alphitonia zizyphoides

Alphitonia zizyphoides, commonly known as the Australian ash or white ash, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It has smooth, greyish-brown bark that peels in thin, papery strips. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a pointed tip, often with a slightly wavy margin. Its small, white flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by small, round, yellowish fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Indigenous practices.
