Astragalus lentiginosus var. higginsii

Astragalus lentiginosus var. higginsii, commonly known as Higgins' milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, hairy stipules and clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh, dry conditions.
