Alchemilla ericoides

Alchemilla ericoides, commonly known as silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has succulent, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

Alchemilla ericoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
