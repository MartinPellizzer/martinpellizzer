Abelmoschus multiformis

Abelmoschus multiformis, commonly known as the Indian okra or *Abelmoschus manihot*, is a flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are edible and can be cooked like other leafy greens.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy crop in various climates.
