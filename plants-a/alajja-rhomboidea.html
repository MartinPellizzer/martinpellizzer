Alajja rhomboidea

Alajja rhomboidea, commonly known as the diamondleaf spurge, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are rhomboid in shape, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Alajja rhomboidea is often found in rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
