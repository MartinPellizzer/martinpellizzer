Audibertia × palmeri

Audibertia × palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer's audibertia, is a hybrid plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid between Audibertia lindheimeri and Audibertia heterophylla.
