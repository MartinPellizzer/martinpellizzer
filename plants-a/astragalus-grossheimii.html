Astragalus grossheimii

Astragalus grossheimii, commonly known as the Sierra foothill milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as slopes and meadows, and is adapted to arid conditions. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus grossheimii plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native range.
