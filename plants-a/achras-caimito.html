Achras caimito

Achras caimito, commonly known as the custard apple tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring a dense canopy and thick, waxy leaves. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by fruits that are oval or spherical in shape. The fruit has a thick, leathery skin and a sweet, creamy pulp with a custard-like texture.

It is valued for its edible fruit and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
