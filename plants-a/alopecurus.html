Alopecurus

Alopecurus, commonly known as mouse ear grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family Poaceae.

It typically features small, tufted clumps with slender, hairy leaves and tiny, spikelet-like flowers. These plants are often found in moist, grassy areas and are characterized by their low-growing habit. Alopecurus species are generally herbaceous and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

They are sometimes used in landscaping for their ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil.
