Adesmia quadripinnata var. pygmaea

Adesmia quadripinnata var. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf velvet bean, is a small shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are pinnately compound, with multiple pairs of leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value and nutritional content, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for agricultural purposes.
