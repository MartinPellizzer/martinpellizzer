Astragalus montis-bakhtiari

Astragalus montis-bakhtiari, commonly known as the Bakhtiari mountain milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its root system is known for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
