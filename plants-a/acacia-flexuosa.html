Acacia flexuosa

Acacia flexuosa, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and phyllodes that are narrow and slightly curved. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often growing in dunes or along the shoreline.

Acacia flexuosa is well adapted to withstand salt spray and is an important component of coastal ecosystems.

Native Distribution

Acacia flexuosa is primarily native to northern Australia, including parts of Queensland, the Northern Territory, and Western Australia.

It is commonly found in arid and semi-arid regions, particularly in sandy soils. This plant is often associated with mulga woodlands and other dry shrublands. It thrives in environments with low rainfall and high temperatures.

Acacia flexuosa is well adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.
