Andropogon glomeratus var. glaucopsis

Andropogon glomeratus var. glaucopsis, commonly known as blue panicgrass, is a variant of the panic grass genus native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and blue-gray foliage, which gives it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to purplish in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
