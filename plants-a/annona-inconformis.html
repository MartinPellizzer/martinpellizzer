Annona inconformis

Annona inconformis, commonly known as the "mamey sapote," is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces a fruit with a sweet, creamy pulp. The plant belongs to the Annonaceae family and is valued for its edible fruit and ornamental qualities. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and is rich in nutrients.
