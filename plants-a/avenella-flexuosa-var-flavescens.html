Avenella flexuosa var. flavescens

Avenella flexuosa var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow sandreed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow to golden-yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer.

The species is adapted to drought conditions and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
