Aster deserticola

Aster deserticola, commonly known as the desert aster, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in the spring and early summer. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, helping to reduce water loss through transpiration.

Aster deserticola plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
