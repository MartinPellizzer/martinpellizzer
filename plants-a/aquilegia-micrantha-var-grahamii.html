Aquilegia micrantha var. grahamii

Aquilegia micrantha var. grahamii, commonly known as Graham's candytuft, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant features trifoliate leaves with finely divided lobes, and its stems are typically hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
