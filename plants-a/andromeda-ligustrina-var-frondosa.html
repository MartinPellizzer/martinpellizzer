Andromeda ligustrina var. frondosa

Andromeda ligustrina var. frondosa, commonly known as the broadleaf andromeda, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features dense, dark green, leathery leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, usually pink or white, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.
