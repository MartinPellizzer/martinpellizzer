Anemone flavescens

Anemone flavescens, commonly known as the yellow anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, pink to yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Anemone flavescens is often found in woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
