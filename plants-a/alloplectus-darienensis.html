Alloplectus darienensis

Alloplectus darienensis, commonly known as the Darien marsh grass, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It typically grows in wetland and marshy environments, where it thrives in saturated soil conditions. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes. Its root system is adapted to waterlogged soils, allowing it to anchor itself firmly in muddy substrates.

Alloplectus darienensis plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
