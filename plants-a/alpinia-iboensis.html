Alpinia iboensis

Alpinia iboensis, commonly known as the Ibo ginger, is a tropical plant native to western Africa.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important spices like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in inflorescences that resemble those of other ginger species.

The rhizomes of Alpinia iboensis are used in traditional medicine and are valued for their aromatic and medicinal properties.
