Aster expansus

Aster expansus, commonly known as the broadleaf aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its showy purple to blue flowers bloom in the late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster expansus is often found in moist, open woodlands and thrives in partial to full sunlight.
