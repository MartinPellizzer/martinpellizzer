Agave troubetskoyana

Agave troubetskoyana, commonly known as the Mexican hat agave, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette of thick, blue-green leaves that are lined with sharp spines. The plant produces an impressive inflorescence of yellow flowers that can reach several meters in height. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
