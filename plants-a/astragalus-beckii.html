Astragalus beckii

Astragalus beckii, commonly known as Beck's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinators.

Astragalus beckii is also valued for its ecological importance and is sometimes used in restoration projects.
