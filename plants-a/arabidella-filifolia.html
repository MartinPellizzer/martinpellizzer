Arabidella filifolia

Arabidella filifolia, commonly known as the silky filifera, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Arabidella filifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
