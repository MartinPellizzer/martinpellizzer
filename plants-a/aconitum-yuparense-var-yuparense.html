Aconitum yuparense var. yuparense

Aconitum yuparense var. yuparense, commonly known as the Yucatan monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant features palmately divided leaves with a hairy texture, and its root system consists of tubers that store nutrients. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and toxic properties, it is cultivated in gardens but requires caution during handling.
