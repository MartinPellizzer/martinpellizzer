Aconitum vulparia subsp. neapolitanum

Aconitum vulparia subsp.

neapolitanum, commonly known as the Naples monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features striking blue-violet flowers arranged in a spire-like inflorescence, typical of the Ranunculaceae family.

The plant has palmate leaves with deeply lobed margins, and its stems are hairy and often reddish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care.
