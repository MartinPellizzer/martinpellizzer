Amaryllis × phoenicea

Amaryllis × phoenicea, commonly known as the hybrid amaryllis, is a striking flowering plant resulting from crosses between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis paradisicola.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a range of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant typically produces strap-like, dark green leaves that are broad and fleshy. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This hybrid is popular as a decorative plant, often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and ease of care.
