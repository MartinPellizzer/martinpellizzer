Angraecoides sedifolia

Angraecoides sedifolia, commonly known as the leafy angraecoid, is a unique member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale in color. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by nocturnal moths, a relationship that highlights its adaptation to specific ecological conditions.

Angraecoides sedifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the biodiversity of its native habitat.
