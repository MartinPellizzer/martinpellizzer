Anemone albana var. armena

Anemone albana var. armena, commonly known as Armenian white anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and surrounding areas.

It features showy white flowers with five to seven petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and basal leaves that are palmately lobed. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early-season blooms.
