Aruncus dioicus var. triternatus

Aruncus dioicus var. triternatus, commonly known as the three-parted goat's beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features tall, slender stems that bear large, feathery plumes of white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, three-parted leaves that are fern-like in appearance, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream sides.

The species is notable for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
