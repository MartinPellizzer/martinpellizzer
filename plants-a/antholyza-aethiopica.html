Antholyza aethiopica

Antholyza aethiopica, commonly known as the African snowflake, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its fleshy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in hot, dry climates and is adapted to store water in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. Its blooms typically appear in the spring and are followed by the production of small, round, black seeds.

Antholyza aethiopica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
