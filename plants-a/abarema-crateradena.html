Abarema crateradena

Abarema crateradena, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock flower, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble peacock feathers. The plant features pinnate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
