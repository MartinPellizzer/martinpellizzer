Aconitum pyramidale subsp. bauhini

Aconitum pyramidale subsp.

bauhini, commonly known as the Alpine monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple spurred flowers, which are arranged in dense, conical inflorescences.

The plant has palmately lobed leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and its stems are typically hairy and upright. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
