Arabis tricornuta

Arabis tricornuta, commonly known as the three-lobed rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with four petals. The plant is native to rocky and alpine areas in Europe and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Arabis tricornuta is a perennial species that blooms in late spring to early summer.
