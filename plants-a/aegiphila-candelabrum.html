Aegiphila candelabrum

Aegiphila candelabrum, commonly known as the candlestick heath, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branching structure that resembles a candlestick, hence its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of its branches. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes or open woodlands.

Aegiphila candelabrum is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs.
