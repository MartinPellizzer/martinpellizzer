Antidesma myriocarpum var. puberulum

Antidesma myriocarpum var. puberulum, commonly known as the hairy antidesma, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence, giving it a hairy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, woody fruits. Its growth habit is usually compact, with multiple branches forming a dense canopy.

This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested or woodland areas.
