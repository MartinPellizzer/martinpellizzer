Acacia rubricaulis

Acacia rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish-brown stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system for water conservation.

Acacia rubricaulis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
