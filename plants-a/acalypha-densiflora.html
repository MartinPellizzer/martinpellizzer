Acalypha densiflora

Acalypha densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered spurflower, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant has palmate leaves with lobed margins, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in humid climates with well-drained soil.
