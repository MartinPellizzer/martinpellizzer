Aglaia roemeri

Aglaia roemeri, commonly known as the Java Aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family, which includes species such as mahogany and ash. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its fruits are fleshy drupes that contain a single seed, and the tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Aglaia roemeri is also noted for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
