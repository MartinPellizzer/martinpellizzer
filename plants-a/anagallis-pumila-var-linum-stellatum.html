Anagallis pumila var. linum-stellatum

Anagallis pumila var. linum-stellatum, commonly known as the small redflowered mouse-ear, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features tiny, bright red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling miniature bells. The plant has hairy, succulent leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in length. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is closely related to other Anagallis species and is valued for its ornamental appeal in rock gardens and alpine settings.
