Agave palmaris

Agave palmaris, commonly known as the palmate agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are broad and paddle-like, often with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears yellowish-green flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

Agave palmaris is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
