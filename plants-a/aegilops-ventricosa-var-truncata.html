Aegilops ventricosa var. truncata

Aegilops ventricosa var. truncata, commonly known as the truncated spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, truncate (cut-off) spikelets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, pale green flowers in summer.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its potential for use in phytoremediation and as a forage crop.
