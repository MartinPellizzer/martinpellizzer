Angraecum stipulatum

Angraecum stipulatum, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, leafy stems and striking, star-shaped flowers that bloom at night. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are pollinated by moths, which are attracted to the nectar-rich blossoms.

Angraecum stipulatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
