Acianthera maculiglossa

Acianthera maculiglossa, commonly known as the spotted lip orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive red and purple lip. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky areas. It has a short rhizome and relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
