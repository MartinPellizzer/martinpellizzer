Attalea spectabilis var. monosperma

Attalea spectabilis var. monosperma, commonly known as the "palm of the Amazon," is a tall, slender palm native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its long, pinnate leaves and produces large, yellowish inflorescences that bear numerous small, white flowers. The plant is monoecious, meaning it has both male and female flowers on the same individual. Its fruit is a drupe that contains a single seed, which is a key feature of the "monosperma" variety.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local communities.
