Acaena macrostemon subsp. longiaristata

Acaena macrostemon subsp.

longiaristata, commonly known as the long-stalked New Zealand bramble, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a spherical, dry achene enclosed in a persistent, woody bract.

This subspecies is adapted to a variety of habitats, including coastal and forest margins, and is often found in the lower North Island of New Zealand.
