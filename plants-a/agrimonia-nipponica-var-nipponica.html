Agrimonia nipponica var. nipponica

Agrimonia nipponica var. nipponica, commonly known as Japanese agrimony, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features upright stems with opposite, pinnately lobed leaves. Its showy yellow flowers appear in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
