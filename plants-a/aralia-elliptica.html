Aralia elliptica

Aralia elliptica, commonly known as American angel's trumpet, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves that are elliptical in shape, with a smooth, dark green upper surface and a lighter, hairy underside. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in the summer, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and dramatic floral display.
