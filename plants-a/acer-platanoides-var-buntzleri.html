Acer platanoides var. buntzleri

Acer platanoides var. buntzleri, commonly known as the Buntzler Norway maple, is a variant of the Norway maple native to Europe.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of yellow and orange in autumn. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often planted in gardens and urban landscapes.
