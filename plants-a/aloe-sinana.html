Aloe sinana

Aloe sinana, commonly known as the mountain aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are thick and fleshy. The leaves are typically gray-green in color with a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. It produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers, often blooming in summer.

This plant is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
