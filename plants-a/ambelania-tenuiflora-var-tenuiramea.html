Ambelania tenuiflora var. tenuiramea

Ambelania tenuiflora var. tenuiramea, commonly known as the slender-fruited ambelania, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and small, pale flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its fruit is a small, elongated capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Due to habitat loss, this species is considered endangered and is of particular interest to botanists and conservationists.
