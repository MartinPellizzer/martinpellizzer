Agatea violaris f. mollis

Agatea violaris f. mollis, commonly known as the soft-leaved viola agatea, is a rare and delicate plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, thick foliage that gives it a soft, almost plush texture. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a touch of color to its natural habitat. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a botanical curiosity and a subject of interest for horticulturists and botanists alike.
