Aphelandra aurantiaca

Aphelandra aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that are often a vibrant green with a reddish or bronze underside. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom during the warm seasons. Aphelandra aurantiaca thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
