Amitostigma basifoliatum

Amitostigma basifoliatum, commonly known as the basal-leaf lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing white or pale yellow petals. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Amitostigma basifoliatum is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in high-altitude environments.
