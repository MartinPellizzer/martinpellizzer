Acer laciniatum

Acer laciniatum, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 meters, with a rounded or spreading crown. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and appear in clusters before the leaves emerge in spring.

The tree prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
