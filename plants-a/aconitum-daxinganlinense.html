Aconitum daxinganlinense

Aconitum daxinganlinense, commonly known as the Daxinganlin aconite, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant contains potent alkaloids, making it highly toxic and requiring careful handling. Its botanical features include palmately divided leaves and a short, thick rhizome that stores nutrients.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and mountainous areas.
