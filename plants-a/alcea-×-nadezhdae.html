Alcea × nadezhdae

Alcea × nadezhdae, commonly known as the "Nadezhda Hollyhock," is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Alcea rosea and Alcea trinervis.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant is native to the Caucasus region and is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions. Its foliage is broad and heart-shaped, adding to its ornamental value in gardens.

Alcea × nadezhdae is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
