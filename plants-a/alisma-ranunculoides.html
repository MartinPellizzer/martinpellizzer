Alisma ranunculoides

Alisma ranunculoides, commonly known as narrowleaf water plantain, is a semi-aquatic herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating, with small white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and improve water filtration.

Alisma ranunculoides plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and amphibians.
