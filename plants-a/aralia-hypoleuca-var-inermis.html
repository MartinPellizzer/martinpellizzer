Aralia hypoleuca var. inermis

Aralia hypoleuca var. inermis, commonly known as the white aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves that are typically pinnately divided, with a light green to white coloration that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white, umbel-like inflorescences in the spring, which are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often found in moist, wooded areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
