Aconitum vaginatum var. xanthanthum

Aconitum vaginatum var. xanthanthum, commonly known as the yellow helmetflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow to orange flowers that resemble small helmets. The plant features large, palmately divided leaves with a velvety texture, and its stems are typically hairy and robust. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a suitable addition to woodland gardens.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
