Agelaea pilosa

Agelaea pilosa, commonly known as the hairy agelaea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
