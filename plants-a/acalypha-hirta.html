Acalypha hirta

Acalypha hirta, commonly known as velvetleaf or redroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its hairy, often reddish stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive three-lobed seed capsules. Acalypha hirta is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and abandoned fields.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
