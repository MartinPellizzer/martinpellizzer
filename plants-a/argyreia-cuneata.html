Argyreia cuneata

Argyreia cuneata, commonly known as the velvet leaf or velvet leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by round, red berries containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
