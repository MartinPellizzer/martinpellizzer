Aronia × spuria

Aronia × spuria, commonly known as the red chokeberry, is a hybrid shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dark red to black berries, which are rich in antioxidants and anthocyanins. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–3 meters and produces clusters of small, tart fruits in late summer. Its leaves are oval with serrated edges and turn vibrant red in the fall.

Aronia × spuria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a food source for wildlife.
