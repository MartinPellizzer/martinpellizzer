Acacia tortilis var. crinita

Acacia tortilis var. crinita, commonly known as the weeping acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its drooping, feathery branches that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in summer, followed by flat, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of small, delicate leaflets, and it has a deep root system adapted to drought conditions.

This species is often used for soil stabilization and provides habitat and food for various wildlife.
