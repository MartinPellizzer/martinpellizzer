Arrabidaea grosourdyana

Arrabidaea grosourdyana, commonly known as the Cape rose or mountain rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink or red flowers. The plant is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
