Andropogon saccharoides subsp. leucopogon

Andropogon saccharoides subsp.

leucopogon, commonly known as silver bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and tufted inflorescences, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies. It has a clump-forming growth habit and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Silver bluestem is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in stabilizing soils in native plant landscapes.
