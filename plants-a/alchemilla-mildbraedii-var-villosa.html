Alchemilla mildbraedii var. villosa

Alchemilla mildbraedii var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in summer, which are clustered in rounded heads. Its leaves are typically covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its common name.

Alchemilla mildbraedii var. villosa thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
