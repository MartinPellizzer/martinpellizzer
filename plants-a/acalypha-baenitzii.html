Acalypha baenitzii

Acalypha baenitzii, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are often covered in a fine, soft pubescence. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.
