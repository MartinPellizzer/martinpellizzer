Astragalus loanus

Astragalus loanus, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus loanus is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
