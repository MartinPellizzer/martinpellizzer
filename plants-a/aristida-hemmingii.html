Aristida hemmingii

Aristida hemmingii, commonly known as the desert threeawn, is a perennial grass native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spikelet-like inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture and is adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soils in desert ecosystems and is often found in disturbed or sandy soils.

Aristida hemmingii is drought-tolerant and has a low growth habit, making it a hardy plant for xeriscaping.
