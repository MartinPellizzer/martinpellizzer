Adesmia schneideri

Adesmia schneideri, commonly known as the Schneider's adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in arid regions of Argentina and Chile. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several small, rounded seeds.

Adesmia schneideri is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its potential use in restoration ecology.
