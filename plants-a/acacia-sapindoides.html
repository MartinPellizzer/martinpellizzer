Acacia sapindoides

Acacia sapindoides, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and clusters of yellow, fluffy flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces long, flat pods that contain seeds coated in a sticky, resinous substance. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought.

The plant has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties and as a source of fuel and timber.
