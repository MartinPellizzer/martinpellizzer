Allium macropetalum

Allium macropetalum, commonly known as the giant purple onion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It features broad, strap-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces large, showy umbels of purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary potential.
