Alkanna corcyrensis

Alkanna corcyrensis, commonly known as the red sandalwood or alkanna, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces a red, resinous substance known as alkanna, which has been used historically in dyeing and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Alkanna corcyrensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique chemical compounds.
