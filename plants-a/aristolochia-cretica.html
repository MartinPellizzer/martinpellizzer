Aristolochia cretica

Aristolochia cretica, commonly known as the Greek birthwort, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped, often aromatic leaves and produces unique, intricate flowers that resemble a pipe or a crown. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its tubular, waxy flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone areas.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
