Astragalus aduncus f. kotschyanus

Astragalus aduncus f. kotschyanus, commonly known as the Kotschyan's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique floral morphology and habitat preferences.
