Asplenium umbrosum var. australe

Asplenium umbrosum var. australe, commonly known as the Australian shield fern, is a shade-tolerant fern native to southeastern Australia.

It features a compact, dense rosette of lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky pubescence. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds, typically arranged in a row along the veins. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest undergrowth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
