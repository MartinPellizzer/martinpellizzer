Achillea umbellata var. polycephala

Achillea umbellata var. polycephala, commonly known as the many-headed yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a compact, bushy growth habit with multiple flowering heads that bloom in flat, rounded clusters. The plant has finely divided, silver-gray foliage that is aromatic and slightly hairy. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in dense umbels, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
