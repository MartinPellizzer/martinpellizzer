Arabis bryoides var. laxa

Arabis bryoides var. laxa, commonly known as the loose rockcress, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features small, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with four petals and numerous stamens, and bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in tundra and mountainous habitats.

This plant is adapted to cold climates and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value and hardiness.
