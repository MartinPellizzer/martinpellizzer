Amelanchier asiatica var. turkestanica

Amelanchier asiatica var. turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin and a glossy upper surface. The plant produces clusters of white, five-petaled flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that turn red to black as they mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and potential as a food source for wildlife.
