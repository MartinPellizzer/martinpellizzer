Apocynum × vacillans

Apocynum × vacillans, commonly known as the hybrid dogbane, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae, which includes plants with milky sap and often showy flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are clustered in umbels, with pink to purple petals that bloom in late summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
