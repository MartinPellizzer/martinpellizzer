Agrostis suavis

Agrostis suavis, commonly known as sweetgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a soft, slightly hairy texture.

Sweetgrass is also valued for its aromatic scent and has been used traditionally by various indigenous cultures for ceremonial and practical purposes.
