Acacia pulchella f. aculeata

Acacia pulchella f. aculeata, commonly known as the spiny acacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by cylindrical pods containing seeds. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with deep roots and a low water requirement.

The spines provide protection against herbivores, making it a resilient and hardy species.
