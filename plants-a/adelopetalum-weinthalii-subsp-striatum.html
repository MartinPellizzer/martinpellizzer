Adelopetalum weinthalii subsp. striatum

Adelopetalum weinthalii subsp.

striatum, commonly known as the striped velvetleaf, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia. It is characterized by its strikingly veined, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Its unique leaf structure and habitat preferences make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
