Aegiphila breviflora

Aegiphila breviflora, commonly known as the short-flowered aegiphila, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple and bloom in clusters.

Aegiphila breviflora is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Morphology

Aegiphila breviflora has small, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, with a dark green color and a slightly hairy surface.

Its stem is slender and upright, with a rough, greyish bark that becomes smoother as the plant matures. The flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, with white petals and yellow stamens, blooming in the spring and pollinated by insects such as bees. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dry seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, adapting to the arid conditions of its native habitat.
