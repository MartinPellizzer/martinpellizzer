Achimenes sanguinea

Achimenes sanguinea, commonly known as the red-flowered achimenes, is a tropical perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces showy bracts that enhance its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Achimenes sanguinea is often cultivated as a bedding plant or in containers due to its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
