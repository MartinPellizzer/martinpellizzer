Avenula pratensis subsp. lusitanica

Avenula pratensis subsp.

lusitanica, commonly known as the Lusitanian meadow oat, is a perennial grass species native to western Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and meadows, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, greenish flowers that mature to a pale brown.

It plays an important role in maintaining the biodiversity of its native habitats.
