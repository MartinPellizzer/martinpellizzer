Ardisia konishii

Ardisia konishii, commonly known as the Japanese beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Japan and Korea.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by clusters of bright red berries, which are edible but often considered astringent. Ardisia konishii thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental berries make it a popular choice for gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
