Astragalus rassulovae

Astragalus rassulovae, commonly known as the Rassulov's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Astragalus rassulovae is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its flowers are often pale pink to white, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
