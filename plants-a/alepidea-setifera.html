Alepidea setifera

Alepidea setifera, commonly known as the slender-leaved rock lily, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in a raceme, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats.

Alepidea setifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
