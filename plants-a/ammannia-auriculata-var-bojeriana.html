Ammannia auriculata var. bojeriana

Ammannia auriculata var. bojeriana, commonly known as the Bojer's Bladder nut, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and produces small, red, berry-like fruits that are clustered in groups of three. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are tiny, clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and fruiting structures.
