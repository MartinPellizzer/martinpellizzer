Alchemilla vulgaris var. caucasica

Alchemilla vulgaris var. caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features rosettes of silver-green, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its common name reflects its historical use in folk medicine for its purported healing properties.
