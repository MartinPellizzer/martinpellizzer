Atriplex laevis

Atriplex laevis, commonly known as the smooth saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, smooth, and often bluish-gray leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Atriplex laevis is adapted to saline soils and can thrive in areas with limited water availability.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife and livestock.
