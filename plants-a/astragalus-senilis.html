Astragalus senilis

Astragalus senilis, commonly known as the old man's beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain numerous tiny, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

Astragalus senilis is known for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
