Anthurium conjunctum

Anthurium conjunctum, commonly known as the clustered anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, with a waxy texture. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a spadix that can be yellow, green, or red.

Anthurium conjunctum is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded gardens.
