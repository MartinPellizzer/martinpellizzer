Aulomyrcia pulchra

Aulomyrcia pulchra, commonly known as the pretty myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fragrance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
