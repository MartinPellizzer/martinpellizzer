Acacia tetragonocarpa f. scabra

Acacia tetragonocarpa f. scabra, commonly known as the rough acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive quadrangular (four-sided) stems and compound leaves that are typically covered in fine, rough hairs. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that are clustered in dense spikes, which are a key feature for identification. Its small, leathery seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

This species is well-suited to dry environments and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
