Acrostichum intermedium

Acrostichum intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate brake, is a fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, pinnate fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its spores are produced in sori located on the underside of the fronds.

Acrostichum intermedium plays an important ecological role by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various small organisms.
