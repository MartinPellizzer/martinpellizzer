Acacia paniculata var. incana

Acacia paniculata var. incana, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that appear in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

The leaves are bipinnate, with small, feathery leaflets that contribute to its ornamental value.
