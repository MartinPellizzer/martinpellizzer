Amaranthus dinteri

Amaranthus dinteri, commonly known as the Dinter's amaranth, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences with vivid red or orange flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Amaranthus dinteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
