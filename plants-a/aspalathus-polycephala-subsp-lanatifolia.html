Aspalathus polycephala subsp. lanatifolia

Aspalathus polycephala subsp.

lanatifolia, commonly known as the African tea or rooibos, is a shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa. It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant is notable for its unique ability to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils and its resistance to drought. Its leaves are used to produce a caffeine-free herbal tea, which is rich in antioxidants.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Fabaceae family, sharing similar growth habits and ecological preferences.
