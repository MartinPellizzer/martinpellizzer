Abutilon mexiae

Abutilon mexiae, commonly known as Mexican velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fibrous bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Abutilon mexiae thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
