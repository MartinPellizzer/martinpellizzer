Arctostaphylos columbiana var. tracyi

Arctostaphylos columbiana var. tracyi, commonly known as Tracy's manzanita, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and reddish-brown bark that peels in thin, papery layers. The plant produces small, urn-shaped flowers in late spring, which are typically pink or red in color. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Tracy's manzanita thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
