Aridaria brevifolia

Aridaria brevifolia, commonly known as the shortleaf desert plant, is a succulent species adapted to arid environments.

It features small, fleshy leaves that help store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers during the rainy season, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep underground water sources.

Aridaria brevifolia is often found in rocky soils and plays a role in stabilizing desert ecosystems.
