Acacia cyanophylla var. dorrienii

Acacia cyanophylla var. dorrienii, commonly known as the Dorrien's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, yellowish-green leaves that are clustered at the base of the stems. The plant produces pale yellow to cream-colored flowers in dense, globular clusters, which appear during the warmer months. Its bark is typically smooth and may exude a milky sap when damaged.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.
