Amomum gagnepainii

Amomum gagnepainii, commonly known as the Gagnepain's cardamom, is a perennial herb belonging to the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Vietnam and Laos. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in late spring. Its rhizomes, similar to other cardamom species, are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

Amomum gagnepainii is valued both for its culinary applications and its role in local herbal practices.
