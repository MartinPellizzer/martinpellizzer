Acalypha yucatanensis

Acalypha yucatanensis, commonly known as the Yucatan beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the Yucatan Peninsula in Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 2 meters tall, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are usually pink to red in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to dark purple as it matures.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
