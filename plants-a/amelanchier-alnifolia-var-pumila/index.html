Amelanchier Alnifolia Var. Pumila

Amelanchier alnifolia var. pumila is a low-growing shrub native to the northwestern united states and canada. it is commonly known as the plains serviceberry or sand cherry. this plant thrives in sandy and rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks. its small white flowers appear in early spring, followed by edible berries in late summer. it is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits to wildlife.
