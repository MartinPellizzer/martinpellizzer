Anthurium redolens

Anthurium redolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns of veins. The plant produces striking spadices, which are the modified leaves that enclose the true flowers. Anthurium redolens is known for its fragrant foliage, which emits a subtle, sweet aroma.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
