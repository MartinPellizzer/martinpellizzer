Athyrium filix-femina var. qulterianum

Athyrium filix-femina var. qulterianum, commonly known as the Japanese maidenhair fern, is a variant of the common lady fern.

It is native to eastern Asia, including Japan, Korea, and China. This fern is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often have a pale underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
