Anacharis alsinastrum

Anacharis alsinastrum, commonly known as water wisteria, is a species of aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and oval-shaped, often floating on the water surface.

Anacharis alsinastrum is widely used in aquariums and ponds due to its rapid growth and ability to provide oxygen and habitat for aquatic life.
