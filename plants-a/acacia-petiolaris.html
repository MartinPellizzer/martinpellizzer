Acacia petiolaris

Acacia petiolaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact spikes, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Acacia petiolaris is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through its deep root system and reduced leaf surface area.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various native animals.
