Abies canadensis var. aurea

Abies canadensis var. aurea, commonly known as the golden fir, is a variant of the balsam fir native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its golden-yellow foliage, which contrasts strikingly with its dark green needles. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas. It produces cones that are cylindrical and often remain attached to the branches for several years.

This ornamental tree is valued for its unique coloration and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
