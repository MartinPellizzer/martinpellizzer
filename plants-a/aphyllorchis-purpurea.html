Aphyllorchis purpurea

Aphyllorchis purpurea, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a single, showy purple flower with a distinctive pouch-like lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a short, hairy stem. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often clustered at the base of the plant.

Aphyllorchis purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
