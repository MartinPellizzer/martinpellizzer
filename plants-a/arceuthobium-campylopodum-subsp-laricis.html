Arceuthobium campylopodum subsp. laricis

Arceuthobium campylopodum subsp.

laricis, commonly known as the larch dwarf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to larch trees. It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and berries that are dispersed by wind. It is native to the northern hemisphere and is often found in coniferous forests.

Its life cycle involves a complex relationship with its host, contributing to the ecological dynamics of forest ecosystems.
