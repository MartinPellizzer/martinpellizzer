Anchusa calcarea subsp. calcarea

Anchusa calcarea subsp.

calcarea, commonly known as the limestone hawksbeard, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and basal leaves that are lanceolate in shape.

The plant produces bright yellow composite flowers in late spring to early summer, which are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
