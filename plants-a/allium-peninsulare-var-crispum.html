Allium peninsulare var. crispum

Allium peninsulare var. crispum, commonly known as the curly leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the western United States.

It features long, slender, grass-like leaves that are often curled or twisted, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and delicate blossoms.
