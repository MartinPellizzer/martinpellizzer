Allium angulosum var. petraeum

Allium angulosum var. petraeum, commonly known as the rock garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and alpine regions of Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered purple or pink flowers in the summer. The plant is characterized by its bulbous structure, which is used for propagation and storage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, drought-tolerant plants.
