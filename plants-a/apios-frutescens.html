Apios frutescens

Apios frutescens, commonly known as wild yam, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Its roots are rich in starch and have been used by indigenous peoples for food and traditional medicine.

Apios frutescens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
