Adenophora ishiyamae

Adenophora ishiyamae, commonly known as Ishiyama's bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture.

Adenophora ishiyamae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
