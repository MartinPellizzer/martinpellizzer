Astragalus leporinus

Astragalus leporinus, commonly known as the jackrabbit milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus leporinus is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
