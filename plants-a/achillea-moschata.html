Achillea moschata

Achillea moschata, commonly known as sweet yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered with fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped umbels. Its aromatic foliage has a distinctive, pleasant scent, especially when crushed.

Achillea moschata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
