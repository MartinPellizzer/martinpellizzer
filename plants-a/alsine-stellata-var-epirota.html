Alsine stellata var. epirota

Alsine stellata var. epirota, commonly known as the Epirus alsike, is a low-growing annual herb native to the Balkan region.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a subject of interest in ecological and taxonomic studies.
