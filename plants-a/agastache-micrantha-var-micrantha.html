Agastache micrantha var. micrantha

Agastache micrantha var. micrantha, commonly known as narrow-leaved hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and spikes of pale blue to purple tubular flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a strong, mint-like fragrance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Agastache micrantha var. micrantha is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its role as a nectar source for pollinators.
