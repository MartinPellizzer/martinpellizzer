Alyssum tortuosum var. meyerianum

Alyssum tortuosum var. meyerianum, commonly known as Meyer's alpine alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is well adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
