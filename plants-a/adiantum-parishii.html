Adiantum parishii

Adiantum parishii, commonly known as the golden maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, bright green fronds that turn golden in the autumn. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its intricate, lace-like foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Adiantum parishii is also valued for its ability to tolerate low light conditions and its air-purifying properties.
