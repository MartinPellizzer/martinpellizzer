Acacia graffiana

Acacia graffiana, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless branches and bipinnate leaves that are divided into numerous small, needle-like leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-green globular flower heads, which are clustered in dense, upright spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia graffiana is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
