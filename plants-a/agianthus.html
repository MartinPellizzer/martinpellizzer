Agianthus

Agianthus, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Malvaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and includes species such as Agianthus hainanensis and Agianthus macrophylla. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery or grayish coating, giving it its common name. It produces showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Agianthus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes due to its striking foliage and floral display.
