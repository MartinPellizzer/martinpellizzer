Aconitum pyramidatum

Aconitum pyramidatum, commonly known as the Pyramidal Aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, spires of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, palmately lobed leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. Its roots are toxic and contain potent alkaloids, making it a plant of both botanical interest and caution.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.
