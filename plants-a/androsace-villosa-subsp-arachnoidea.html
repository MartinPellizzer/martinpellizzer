Androsace villosa subsp. arachnoidea

Androsace villosa subsp.

arachnoidea, commonly known as the woolly sandparsley, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, woolly appearance.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
