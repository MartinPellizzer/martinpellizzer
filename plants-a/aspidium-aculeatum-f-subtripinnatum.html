Aspidium aculeatum f. subtripinnatum

Aspidium aculeatum f. subtripinnatum, commonly known as the three-fingered water fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive fronds with a subtripinnate structure.

The plant features a creeping rhizome that produces erect, lanceolate pinnae with a serrated margin. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a glossy appearance, often with a slight waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, commonly found in wetlands and along stream banks.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in aquatic ecosystems.
