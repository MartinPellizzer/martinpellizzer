Asperula cynanchica subsp. rupicola

Asperula cynanchica subsp.

rupicola, commonly known as rock asperula, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring well-drained and sunny environments.

The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, appearing in clusters during the spring and early summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
