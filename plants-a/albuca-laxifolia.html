Albuca laxifolia

Albuca laxifolia, commonly known as the slender bulbous plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a bulbous base with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.
