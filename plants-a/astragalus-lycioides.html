Astragalus lycioides

Astragalus lycioides, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish to brownish pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through deep root systems and a short growing season.

Astragalus lycioides plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
