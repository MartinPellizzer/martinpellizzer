Acacia subretusa

Acacia subretusa, commonly known as the smooth acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are typically followed by flat, woody pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Acacia subretusa is known for its ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
