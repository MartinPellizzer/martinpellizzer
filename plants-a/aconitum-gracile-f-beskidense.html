Aconitum gracile f. beskidense

Aconitum gracile f. beskidense, commonly known as the Beskid narrowleaf monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and produces showy, hood-shaped flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
