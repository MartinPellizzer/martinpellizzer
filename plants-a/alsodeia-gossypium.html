Alsodeia gossypium

Alsodeia gossypium, commonly known as cottonweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its cotton-like seeds are enclosed in a fluffy pappus, aiding in wind dispersal.

Alsodeia gossypium thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.
