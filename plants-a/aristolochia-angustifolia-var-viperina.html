Aristolochia angustifolia var. viperina

Aristolochia angustifolia var. viperina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, veined leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces striking, hooded flowers that are often purple or greenish in color. Its fruits are elongated, slender berries that mature to a dark purple hue.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
