Annona poeppigii

Annona poeppigii, commonly known as the custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces large, fragrant, greenish-white flowers that are typically followed by yellow or orange fruits. The fruit has a thick, smooth skin and a creamy, sweet flesh with a texture similar to custard.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is valued for its edible fruit and ornamental qualities.
