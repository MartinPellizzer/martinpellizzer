Anthosachne kingiana subsp. kingiana

Anthosachne kingiana subsp.

kingiana, commonly known as the king's grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and along riverbanks.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a robust stem with nodes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
