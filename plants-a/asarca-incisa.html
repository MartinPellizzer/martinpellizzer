Asarca incisa

Asarca incisa, commonly known as the cut-leaved asarum, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Aristolochiaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a distinctive, fragrant aroma. The plant produces small, hooded flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

Asarca incisa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
