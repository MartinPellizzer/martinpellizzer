Amelanchier canadensis subsp. obovalis

Amelanchier canadensis subsp.

obovalis, commonly known as the Canadian serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and dark green on top with a lighter underside.

The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen to a red or yellow hue in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinators.
