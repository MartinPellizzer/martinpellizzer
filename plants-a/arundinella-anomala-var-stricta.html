Arundinella anomala var. stricta

Arundinella anomala var. stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf bamboo, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically with a bluish-green hue.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
