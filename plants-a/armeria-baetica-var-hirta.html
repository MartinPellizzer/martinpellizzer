Armeria baetica var. hirta

Armeria baetica var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy sea thrift, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Spain.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces dense clusters of white or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. The plant features fleshy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid environment. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in rocky coastal areas.

Its compact growth habit and showy flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
