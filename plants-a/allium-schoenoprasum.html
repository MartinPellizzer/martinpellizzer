Allium schoenoprasum

Allium schoenoprasum, commonly known as chives, is a hardy perennial herb belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow, grass-like leaves and small, spherical purple or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Chives are widely cultivated for their edible leaves, which have a mild onion flavor and are used in culinary applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in gardens.
