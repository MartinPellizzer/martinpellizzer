Acacia loxophylla var. nervosa

Acacia loxophylla var. nervosa, commonly known as the narrow-veined acacia, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves with finely divided, narrow leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
