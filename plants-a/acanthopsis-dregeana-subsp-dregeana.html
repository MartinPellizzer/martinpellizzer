Acanthopsis dregeana subsp. dregeana

Acanthopsis dregeana subsp.

dregeana, commonly known as the Cape Harebell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like blooms.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it prefers well-drained conditions.
