Aster novi-belgii subsp. brumalis

Aster novi-belgii subsp.

brumalis, commonly known as the autumn aster, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States. It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, medium-green leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators in the fall.
