Alstroemeria magenta

Alstroemeria magenta, commonly known as the Peruvian lily, is a striking perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its vivid, showy flowers that come in a range of colors, including magenta, red, and pink. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves and produces clusters of tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Alstroemeria magenta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms and vibrant hues.
