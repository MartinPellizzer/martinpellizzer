Aporum hymenopetalum

Aporum hymenopetalum, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are often pale pink or white, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Aporum hymenopetalum prefers warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
