Apollonias oppositifolia

Apollonias oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaf apollonias, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apocynaceae.

It is characterized by its opposite leaf arrangement, where leaves are paired along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It is native to parts of Africa and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists studying adaptive plant structures in harsh environments.
