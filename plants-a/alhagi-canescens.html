Alhagi canescens

Alhagi canescens, commonly known as the desert shrub or sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia and Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, thriving in harsh environmental conditions. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture. Alhagi canescens is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.

It plays a role in preventing soil erosion and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
