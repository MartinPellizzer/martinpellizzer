Astragalus pseudocaspius

Astragalus pseudocaspius, commonly known as the Caspian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to central Asia, including regions of Kazakhstan and western China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in sandy or gravelly soils.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
