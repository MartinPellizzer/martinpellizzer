Asperula fragrantissima

Asperula fragrantissima, commonly known as sweet woodruff, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and emit a pleasant aroma when crushed.

Asperula fragrantissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
