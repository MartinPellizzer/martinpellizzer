Acalypha hutchinsonii

Acalypha hutchinsonii, commonly known as the red-flowered spurflower, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red bracts that surround small yellow flowers, making it a visually distinctive plant. The leaves are typically ovate or elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
