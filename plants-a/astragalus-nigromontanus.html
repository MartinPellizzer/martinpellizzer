Astragalus nigromontanus

Astragalus nigromontanus, commonly known as the black mountain milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers, which contributes to its survival in dry climates.

Astragalus nigromontanus plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for certain wildlife species.
