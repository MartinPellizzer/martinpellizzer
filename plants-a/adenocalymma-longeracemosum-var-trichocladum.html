Adenocalymma longeracemosum var. trichocladum

Adenocalymma longeracemosum var. trichocladum, commonly known as the slender adenocalymma, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit and small, linear leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its botanical features include glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, which contribute to its specific variety name "trichocladum.".

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
