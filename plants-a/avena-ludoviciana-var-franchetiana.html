Avena ludoviciana var. franchetiana

Avena ludoviciana var. franchetiana, commonly known as the slender oat, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
