Abrus madagascariensis var. dunensis

Abrus madagascariensis var. dunensis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid bean, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking, ornamental flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces distinctive, red or orange seed pods that contain toxic seeds covered in a bright red aril. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
