Aeschynomene glauca

Aeschynomene glauca, commonly known as blue burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces small, greenish-white flowers clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy, spiny pods that mature into the characteristic "burr" structures.

Aeschynomene glauca is known for its adaptability to various soil types and is sometimes used in forage or as a cover crop.
