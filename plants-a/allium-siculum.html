Allium siculum

Allium siculum, commonly known as the Sicilian leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives. The plant features long, hollow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white, bulbous flowers. Its bulb is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor similar to that of a leek.

It is often cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
