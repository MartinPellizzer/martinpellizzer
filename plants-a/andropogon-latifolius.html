Andropogon latifolius

Andropogon latifolius, commonly known as broadleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its wide, bluish-green leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that mature into brown, fluffy seeds.

Andropogon latifolius plays an important role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
