Alchornea verticillata

Alchornea verticillata, commonly known as the whorled alchornea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant features opposite or whorled leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom during the wet season.

Alchornea verticillata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.
