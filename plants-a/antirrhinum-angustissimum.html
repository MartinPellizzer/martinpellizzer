Antirrhinum angustissimum

Antirrhinum angustissimum, commonly known as the narrowleaf snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bright orange or red tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy surface.

Antirrhinum angustissimum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
