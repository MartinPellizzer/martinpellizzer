Anthemis carpatica var. balcanica

Anthemis carpatica var. balcanica, commonly known as the Balkan ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
