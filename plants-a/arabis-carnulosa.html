Arabis carnulosa

Arabis carnulosa, commonly known as the clustered rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Arabis carnulosa is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
