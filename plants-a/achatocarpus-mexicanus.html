Achatocarpus mexicanus

Achatocarpus mexicanus, commonly known as the Mexican acacia, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow floral clusters. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and resilient. Its seed pods are woody and often used in traditional medicine.

The species plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and forage for various animals.
