Antholyza labiata

Antholyza labiata, commonly known as the redroot germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
