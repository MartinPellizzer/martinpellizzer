Acantholimon glumaceum var. breviscapum

Acantholimon glumaceum var. breviscapum, commonly known as the short-stemmed flaxvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces distinctive, glabrous, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. Its seeds are enclosed in pods that split open when mature, dispersing the seeds by wind.

This variety is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
