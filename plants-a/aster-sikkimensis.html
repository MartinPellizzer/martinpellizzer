Aster sikkimensis

Aster sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim aster, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving at elevations between 2,000 and 4,000 meters. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Aster sikkimensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional uses in local medicine.
