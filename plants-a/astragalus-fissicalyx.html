Astragalus fissicalyx

Astragalus fissicalyx, commonly known as the fissured milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its seeds are enclosed in a hairy pod and are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus fissicalyx plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its habitat.
