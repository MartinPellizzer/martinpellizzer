Arctium tomentosum subsp. palladinii

Arctium tomentosum subsp.

palladinii, commonly known as the Pallid velvetleaf, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and distinctive purple to brown bracts that surround its flower heads.

The plant produces small, spiky flower heads that are typical of the burdock genus. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found along roadsides and in meadows.

Its botanical features make it identifiable and ecologically significant in its native habitats.
