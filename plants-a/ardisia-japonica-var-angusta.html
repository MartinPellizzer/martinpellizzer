Ardisia japonica var. angusta

Ardisia japonica var. angusta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Japanese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a narrow, elongated form. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits that are highly ornamental. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This variety is valued for its attractive foliage and showy fruit, making it a popular choice in ornamental gardening.
