Adhatoda gilliesii

Adhatoda gilliesii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green color with a slightly hairy texture. Its most distinctive feature is the clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring.

Adhatoda gilliesii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
