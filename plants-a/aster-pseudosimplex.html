Aster pseudosimplex

Aster pseudosimplex, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its narrow, linear leaves are arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces showy purple or blue flowers in the fall.

Aster pseudosimplex is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
