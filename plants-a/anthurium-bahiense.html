Anthurium bahiense

Anthurium bahiense, commonly known as the Bahamian anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a metallic sheen. The plant produces clusters of bright red spadices, which are the spiky, spadix-like structures typical of the Araceae family. Anthurium bahiense thrives in humid, warm environments and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
