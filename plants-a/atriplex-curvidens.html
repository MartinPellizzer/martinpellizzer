Atriplex curvidens

Atriplex curvidens, commonly known as the narrowleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, gray-green leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep water sources and thrive in saline soils.

Atriplex curvidens plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
