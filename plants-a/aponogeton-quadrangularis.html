Aponogeton quadrangularis

Aponogeton quadrangularis, commonly known as the four-cornered arrowhead, is a aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often submerged in water. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that emerge above the water surface, adding ornamental value to ponds and water gardens. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store nutrients and survive in various aquatic environments.

Aponogeton quadrangularis is popular among aquarists for its aesthetic appeal and ease of cultivation.
