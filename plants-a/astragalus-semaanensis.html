Astragalus semaanensis

Astragalus semaanensis, commonly known as the Semaan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and may have potential medicinal uses.

Its conservation status is of interest due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity.
