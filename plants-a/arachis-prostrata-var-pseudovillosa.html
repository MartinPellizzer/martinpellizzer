Arachis prostrata var. pseudovillosa

Arachis prostrata var. pseudovillosa, commonly known as the prostrate peanut, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds, similar to other peanut species. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and potential agricultural significance.
