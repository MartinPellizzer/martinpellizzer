Acacia angustissima var. filicioides

Acacia angustissima var. filicioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, feathery leaves that resemble those of ferns, hence its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are arranged in compact clusters. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping.
