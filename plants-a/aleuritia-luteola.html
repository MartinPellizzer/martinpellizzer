Aleuritia luteola

Aleuritia luteola, commonly known as the yellow elder, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are yellow when mature, containing several seeds.

Aleuritia luteola is often found in disturbed habitats and is adapted to thrive in full sun conditions.
