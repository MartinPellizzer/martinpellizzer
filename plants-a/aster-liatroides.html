Aster liatroides

Aster liatroides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in soft, gray-green foliage. Its showy, blue to purple flower heads bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like butterflies and bees.

Aster liatroides is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
