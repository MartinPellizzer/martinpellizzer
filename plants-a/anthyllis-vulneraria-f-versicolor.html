Anthyllis vulneraria f. versicolor

Anthyllis vulneraria f. versicolor, commonly known as the Variable Heath, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that can vary in color, often displaying shades of pink, red, and white, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and heathlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
