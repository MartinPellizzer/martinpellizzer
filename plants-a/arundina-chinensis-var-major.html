Arundina chinensis var. major

Arundina chinensis var. major, commonly known as the giant bamboo or Chinese giant bamboo, is a tall, clump-forming grass native to China.

It features slender, hollow stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height, making it one of the tallest grasses in the world. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually smooth and dark green, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its striking appearance and resilience.

The inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of flowers that appear at the top of the stems.
