Aristida plumosa var. szovitsiana

Aristida plumosa var. szovitsiana, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, fluffy panicles that give it a soft, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along the edges of woodlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden use in arid or semi-arid climates.
