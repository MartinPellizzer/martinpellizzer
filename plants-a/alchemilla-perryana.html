Alchemilla perryana

Alchemilla perryana, commonly known as the Perry's lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features heart-shaped, silver-velvet leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in umbels.

Alchemilla perryana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or alpine environments.
