Aristida roemeriana

Aristida roemeriana, commonly known as the slender threeawn, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences composed of closely arranged spikelets. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Aristida roemeriana is adapted to withstand drought and is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
