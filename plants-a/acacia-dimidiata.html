Acacia dimidiata

Acacia dimidiata, commonly known as the flat-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are composed of two rows of small, linear leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. Acacia dimidiata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in dry regions.

It is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
