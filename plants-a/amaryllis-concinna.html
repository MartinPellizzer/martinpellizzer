Amaryllis concinna

Amaryllis concinna, commonly known as the narrow-leaved amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and clusters of vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its resilience and ornamental value have made it a favored plant among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
