Amomum pierreanum

Amomum pierreanum, commonly known as the Pierre's cardamom, is a tropical plant belonging to the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Vietnam and Cambodia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, finger-like rhizomes that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

The flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, clustered in spikes at the top of the plant.
