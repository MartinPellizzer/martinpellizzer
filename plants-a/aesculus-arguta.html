Aesculus arguta

Aesculus arguta, commonly known as the Chinese horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets. The tree produces showy, white to pinkish flowers in panicles during the spring. Its fruit is a spiny capsule containing one or two seeds, which are encased in a hairy, fleshy aril.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
