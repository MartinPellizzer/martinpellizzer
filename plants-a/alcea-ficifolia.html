Alcea ficifolia

Alcea ficifolia, commonly known as the giant hollyhock, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often with a distinctive white or yellow center. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are typically large and heart-shaped, contributing to its striking appearance. Alcea ficifolia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in native habitats.
