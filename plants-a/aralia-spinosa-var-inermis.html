Aralia spinosa var. inermis

Aralia spinosa var. inermis, commonly known as the smooth aralia, is a variant of the prickly aralia species.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, particularly found in moist woodlands and stream banks. The plant features compound leaves with elongated, serrated leaflets that are often arranged in a feathery pattern. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in umbels, which are typical of the Araliaceae family.

The plant is noted for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its ornamental value in garden settings.
