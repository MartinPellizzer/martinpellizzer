Acalypha exaltata

Acalypha exaltata, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble flames, consisting of bright red to orange bracts. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Acalypha exaltata thrives in full sun to partial shade and is commonly cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
