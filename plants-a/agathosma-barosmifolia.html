Agathosma barosmifolia

Agathosma barosmifolia, commonly known as sweet fennel or Cape geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its use in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its unique fragrance and adaptability contribute to its popularity among gardeners and herbalists.
