Acalypha porphyrantha

Acalypha porphyrantha, commonly known as the purple-leafed false hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple foliage and showy, clustered inflorescences that resemble hibiscus blooms. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial or shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptical in shape, and often have a velvety texture.

Acalypha porphyrantha thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
