Albuca variegata

Albuca variegata, commonly known as the variegated albuca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, striped leaves that often display a mix of green, white, and yellow. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or yellow, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate watering to prevent root rot.

Albuca variegata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
