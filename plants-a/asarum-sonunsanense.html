Asarum sonunsanense

Asarum sonunsanense, commonly known as the Korean wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, fragrant flowers. The plant produces a deep purple to reddish-brown fruit that contains several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
