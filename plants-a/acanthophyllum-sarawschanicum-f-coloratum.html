Acanthophyllum sarawschanicum f. coloratum

Acanthophyllum sarawschanicum f. coloratum, commonly known as the colorful acanthophyllum, is a rare succulent plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, spiky flowers in shades of pink to purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
