Androrchis × penzigiana

Androrchis × penzigiana, commonly known as the Penzig hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Androrchis apulum and Androrchis pseudopilosa.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its upright, hairy stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in woodland or forested areas. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
