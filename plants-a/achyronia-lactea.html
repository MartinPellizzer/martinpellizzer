Achyronia lactea

Achyronia lactea, commonly known as the milkweed plant, is a species of flowering plant in the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae).

It is characterized by its milky sap, which is a distinctive botanical feature. The plant typically has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are usually yellow or greenish in color.

Achyronia lactea is native to various regions in Africa and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
