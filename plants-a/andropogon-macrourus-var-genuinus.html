Andropogon macrourus var. genuinus

Andropogon macrourus var. genuinus, commonly known as the tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often coated with a fine layer of wax. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairie and woodland habitats. Its flowering spikes are typically erect and produce small, spikelet-like flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Andropogon macrourus var. genuinus plays an important role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
