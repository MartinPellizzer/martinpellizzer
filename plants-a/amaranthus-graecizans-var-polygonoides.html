Amaranthus graecizans var. polygonoides

Amaranthus graecizans var. polygonoides, commonly known as Greek amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems covered in small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually gray-green in color. The plant produces dense clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that mature into three-lobed, spiky seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional cuisines.
