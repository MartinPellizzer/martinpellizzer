Amanoa caribaea

Amanoa caribaea, commonly known as the Caribbean moonflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth texture, and its fruits are small, fleshy berries that contain seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
