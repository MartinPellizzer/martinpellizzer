Acnistus miersii

Acnistus miersii, commonly known as the silky nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, smooth berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes.
