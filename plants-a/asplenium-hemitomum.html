Asplenium hemitomum

Asplenium hemitomum, commonly known as the partial-leaf spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often divided into segments, giving it a feathery appearance. This plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as rock crevices or forest floors, where it can thrive in low-light conditions. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the leaves, and they produce spores for propagation.

Asplenium hemitomum is a hardy species that is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate a variety of soil conditions.
