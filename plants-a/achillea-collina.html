Achillea collina

Achillea collina, commonly known as lesser yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers in summer. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy foliage and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties.

Achillea collina is also valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
