Avena barbata var. subtypica

Avena barbata var. subtypica, commonly known as the slender oat, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration and native landscaping.
