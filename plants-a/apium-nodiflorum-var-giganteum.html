Apium nodiflorum var. giganteum

Apium nodiflorum var. giganteum, commonly known as giant celery, is a variant of the celery plant characterized by its tall, robust growth.

It features large, hollow stems that are often used as a culinary herb, similar to regular celery. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its leaves are broad and fern-like, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

This variety is valued for both its aromatic foliage and its potential medicinal properties.
