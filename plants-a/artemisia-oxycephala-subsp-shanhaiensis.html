Artemisia oxycephala subsp. shanhaiensis

Artemisia oxycephala subsp.

shanhaiensis, commonly known as Chinese wormwood, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in panicles.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves contain volatile oils, which contribute to its strong scent and medicinal properties.

It is used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
