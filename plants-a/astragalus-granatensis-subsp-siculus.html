Astragalus granatensis subsp. siculus

Astragalus granatensis subsp.

siculus, commonly known as the Sicilian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several seeds.

It is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as slopes and scrublands.
