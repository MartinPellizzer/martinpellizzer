Achimenes mexicana

Achimenes mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a tropical perennial plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped blooms that are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Achimenes mexicana is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
