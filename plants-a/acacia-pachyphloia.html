Acacia pachyphloia

Acacia pachyphloia, commonly known as the thick-barked acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical spines and small, yellow flowers that appear in globular clusters. The plant has thick, woody bark that provides protection against fire and drought. Its leaves are small, feathery, and often shed during dry periods to conserve water.

Acacia pachyphloia plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
