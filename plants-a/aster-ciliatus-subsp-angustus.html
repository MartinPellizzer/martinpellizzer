Aster ciliatus subsp. angustus

Aster ciliatus subsp.

angustus, commonly known as narrowleaf blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, blue to purple flowers in the late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. Its flowers are arranged in dense clusters and are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local insect populations and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
