Antennaria isabellina

Antennaria isabellina, commonly known as the Isabelline sandwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with dense clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has slender, hairy stems and small, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient plant suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
