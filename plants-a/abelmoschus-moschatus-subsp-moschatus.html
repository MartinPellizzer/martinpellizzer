Abelmoschus moschatus subsp. moschatus

Abelmoschus moschatus subsp.

moschatus, commonly known as the musk okra, is a flowering plant in the mallow family. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous edible, fibrous seeds. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with five petals.

It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its seeds and as a ornamental plant.
