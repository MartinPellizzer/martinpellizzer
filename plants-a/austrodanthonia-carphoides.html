Austrodanthonia carphoides

Austrodanthonia carphoides, commonly known as coastal wallaby grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Australia.

It typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and withstand harsh environmental conditions.
