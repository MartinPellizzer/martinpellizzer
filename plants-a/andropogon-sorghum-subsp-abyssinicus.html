Andropogon sorghum subsp. abyssinicus

Andropogon sorghum subsp.

abyssinicus, commonly known as Abyssinian sorghum, is a grass species native to Ethiopia and surrounding regions. It is characterized by its tall, erect stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its potential in forage and as a source of biofuel.
