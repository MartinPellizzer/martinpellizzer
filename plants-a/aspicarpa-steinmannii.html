Aspicarpa steinmannii

Aspicarpa steinmannii, commonly known as the dwarf starfruit, is a small shrub native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow fruits that are typically edible and have a tangy, citrus-like flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, tropical climate to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
