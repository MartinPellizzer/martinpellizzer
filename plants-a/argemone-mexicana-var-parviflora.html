Argemone mexicana var. parviflora

Argemone mexicana var. parviflora, commonly known as the Mexican prickly poppy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that transition to orange-red as they mature. Its seeds are encased in a hard, spiny capsule, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils and arid environments.

It has been used traditionally in folk medicine for its purported healing properties.
