Alcea rosea var. sinensis

Alcea rosea var. sinensis, commonly known as Chinese hollyhock, is a variant of the common hollyhock.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to China, characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, large flowers. The flowers typically come in shades of pink, red, and white, and are borne in clusters at the top of the plant. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
