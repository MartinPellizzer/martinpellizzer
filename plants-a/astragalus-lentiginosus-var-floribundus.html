Astragalus lentiginosus var. floribundus

Astragalus lentiginosus var. floribundus, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring. Its flowers are clustered in dense, racemose inflorescences, and the plant is known for its deep taproot system.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
