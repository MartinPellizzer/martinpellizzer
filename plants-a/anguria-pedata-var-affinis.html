Anguria pedata var. affinis

Anguria pedata var. affinis, commonly known as the broadleaf cucumber, is a variant of the anguria genus, characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves.

It is a climbing vine that produces cylindrical, green fruits with a mild, sweet flavor. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight. Its botanical features include tendrils for climbing and a robust root system.

It is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
