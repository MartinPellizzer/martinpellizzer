Asperula woloszczakii var. elongata

Asperula woloszczakii var. elongata, commonly known as the longleaf sweet pepper, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, elliptical berries. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem and hairy foliage, which help it thrive in woodland and moist soil environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
