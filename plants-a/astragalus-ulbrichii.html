Astragalus ulbrichii

Astragalus ulbrichii, commonly known as Ulbrich's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils, and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus ulbrichii is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
