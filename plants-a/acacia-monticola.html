Acacia monticola

Acacia monticola, commonly known as the mountain acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellowish to golden flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the warm season. Acacia monticola is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance through its deep root system.

It is also notable for its role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
