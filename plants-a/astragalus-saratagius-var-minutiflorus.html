Astragalus saratagius var. minutiflorus

Astragalus saratagius var. minutiflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. The plant features compound leaves with small, paired leaflets and produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers in late spring. It is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through its root nodules.

This variety is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in disturbed or sandy soils.
