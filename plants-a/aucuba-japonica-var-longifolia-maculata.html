Aucuba japonica var. longifolia-maculata

Aucuba japonica var. longifolia-maculata, commonly known as the spotted Japanese laurel, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive white or yellow spots, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full shade and is commonly used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

This variety is prized for its ability to tolerate urban conditions and its year-round visual interest.
