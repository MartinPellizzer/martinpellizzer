Asimina × nashii

Asimina × nashii, commonly known as the Nash's Pawpaw, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Asimina triloba and Asimina grandiflora.

It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces large, fragrant, yellow-green flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit, resembling a pawpaw, is edible and has a sweet, custard-like flavor.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential as a fruit crop.
