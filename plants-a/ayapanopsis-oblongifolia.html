Ayapanopsis oblongifolia

Ayapanopsis oblongifolia, commonly known as the Mexican orchid or ayapanopsis, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with a greenish tinge. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
