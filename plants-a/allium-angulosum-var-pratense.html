Allium angulosum var. pratense

Allium angulosum var. pratense, commonly known as the meadow garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, angular bulb structure. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
