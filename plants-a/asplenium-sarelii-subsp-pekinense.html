Asplenium sarelii subsp. pekinense

Asplenium sarelii subsp.

pekinense, commonly known as the Peking maidenhair, is a fern species native to eastern Asia. It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive rhomboid shape.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
