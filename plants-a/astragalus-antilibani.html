Astragalus antilibani

Astragalus antilibani, commonly known as the Lebanon milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of southern Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow or pale green flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, with multiple pairs of leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus antilibani is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid landscapes.
