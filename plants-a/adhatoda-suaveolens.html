Adhatoda suaveolens

Adhatoda suaveolens, commonly known as the Indian dove tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy white and pink flowers that resemble doves in flight, hence its common name. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in humid climates and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
