Aconitum henryi

Aconitum henryi, commonly known as Chinese aconite, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces large, showy flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, with deeply lobed foliage that adds to its ornamental value.

Aconitum henryi contains potent alkaloids, making it toxic and requiring careful handling.
