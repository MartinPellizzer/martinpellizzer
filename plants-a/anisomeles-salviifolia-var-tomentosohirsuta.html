Anisomeles salviifolia var. tomentosohirsuta

Anisomeles salviifolia var. tomentosohirsuta, commonly known as the woolly hirsute anisomeles, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape and covered with a fine, white tomentum that gives the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories or on rocky outcrops.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
