Acritopappus

Acritopappus, commonly known as the desert flax, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plants are characterized by their narrow, linear leaves and showy, yellow to orange composite flowers. Acritopappus species are typically herbaceous perennials that thrive in well-drained soils.

They play an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
