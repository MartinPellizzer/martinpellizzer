Attalea macropetala

Attalea macropetala, commonly known as the "palm of the Amazon," is a tall, woody palm native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, pinnate leaves that can reach lengths of up to 6 meters. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in a panicle inflorescence, which are followed by large, edible fruits resembling small coconuts. The fruit contains a single seed surrounded by a fibrous, starchy endosperm.

This species is an important component of the rainforest ecosystem and has traditional uses by indigenous communities.
