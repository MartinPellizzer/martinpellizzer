Asphodelus albus subsp. subalpinus

Asphodelus albus subsp.

subalpinus, commonly known as the alpine asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in alpine ecosystems.
