Actinodaphne ridleyi

Actinodaphne ridleyi, commonly known as the Ridley's camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves and distinctive, elongated fruits. The plant is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its timber and medicinal properties. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in axillary or terminal inflorescences.
