Aster purpureocaerulescens

Aster purpureocaerulescens, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its showy, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland or wetland habitats. It has a bushy growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves.

Aster purpureocaerulescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
