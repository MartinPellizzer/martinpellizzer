Azolla nilotica

Azolla nilotica, commonly known as water clover, is a floating aquatic fern belonging to the family Azollaceae.

It forms dense mats on the surface of freshwater bodies, contributing to water filtration and oxygenation. The plant has small, water-filled structures called pseudospores that aid in buoyancy and dispersal. Its rapid growth rate makes it useful in phytoremediation and as a biofertilizer.

Azolla nilotica also plays a role in nitrogen fixation through its symbiotic relationship with cyanobacteria.
