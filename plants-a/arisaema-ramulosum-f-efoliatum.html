Arisaema ramulosum f. efoliatum

Arisaema ramulosum f. efoliatum, commonly known as the leafy arrowhead, is a unique variant of the arrowhead plant characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces spadices, which are modified leaves that enclose the true flowers, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is native to parts of Asia.

Its botanical interest lies in its morphological variation and ornamental value in horticulture.
