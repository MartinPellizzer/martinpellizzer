Alchemilla glabra var. glaberrima

Alchemilla glabra var. glaberrima, commonly known as smooth lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves that become smooth in maturity. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in woodland gardens, it is often cultivated in horticulture.
