Agave communis

Agave communis, commonly known as the common agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears numerous small, greenish-white flowers, which are followed by spiky, fruit-like structures. Agave communis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used historically for its fibers and sap.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
