Allophylus brachystachys

Allophylus brachystachys, commonly known as the short-stalked holly or dwarf holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy dark green foliage and small, clustered white flowers that appear in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Allophylus brachystachys is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
