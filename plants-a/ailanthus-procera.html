Ailanthus procera

Ailanthus procera, commonly known as the tree of heaven, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a distinctive grayish-green color. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by winged fruits that disperse seeds by wind. It is often found in urban environments due to its adaptability and rapid growth.

Botanically, it belongs to the Simaroubaceae family and is known for its allelopathic properties.
