Allium atrorubens subsp. inyonis

Allium atrorubens subsp.

inyonis, commonly known as the Iyonis purple onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the western United States. It features striking purple to reddish-purple bulbs and strap-like leaves that are often used in ornamental gardening.

The plant produces umbels of white to pale pink flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species requiring conservation efforts.
