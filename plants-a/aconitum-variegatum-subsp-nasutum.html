Aconitum variegatum subsp. nasutum

Aconitum variegatum subsp.

nasutum, commonly known as the variable aconite, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are compound, with finely divided leaflets, and the stems are usually unbranched.

It is native to parts of Europe and is known for its toxic properties due to the presence of alkaloids.
