Aria × leptophylla

Aria × leptophylla, commonly known as the hybrid hibiscus, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Aria hirta and Aria paniculata.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Southeast Asia, characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters, featuring broad, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
