Acer laevigatum var. angustum

Acer laevigatum var. angustum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and finely toothed, lance-shaped leaves that turn vibrant yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds called samaras. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its unique foliage and compact size make it a valuable addition to urban landscapes.
