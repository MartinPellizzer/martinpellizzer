Astragalus atacamensis

Astragalus atacamensis, commonly known as the Atacama milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions through its deep root system and reduced leaf surface area.

It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is part of the diverse flora found in one of the harshest environments on Earth.
