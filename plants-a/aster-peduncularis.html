Aster peduncularis

Aster peduncularis, commonly known as the slender aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stem and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing purple to pinkish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open fields.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
