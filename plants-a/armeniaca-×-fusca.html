Armeniaca × fusca

Armeniaca × fusca, commonly known as the Chinese date or wild almond.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Prunus armeniaca (the common peach) and Prunus mandshurica. This plant is characterized by its woody structure and the production of small, fragrant flowers. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It is native to regions of East Asia and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.
