Astragalus titi

Astragalus titi, commonly known as the Titi milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Astragalus titi is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various herbivores.
