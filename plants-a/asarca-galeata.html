Asarca galeata

Asarca galeata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wintergreen, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other shrubs and plants adapted to acidic soils. The plant features small, dark green leaves that are narrow and pointed, with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are tiny, bell-shaped, and typically pink or white, appearing in clusters.

Asarca galeata is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
