Acacia cylindrica

Acacia cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a distinctive whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, feather-like segments.

Acacia cylindrica is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
