Ancistrochilus thomsonianus var. gentilii

Ancistrochilus thomsonianus var. gentilii, commonly known as the Thompson's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in cool, humid environments with high light intensity but requires careful management of moisture levels to avoid root rot.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and delicate species in cultivation.
