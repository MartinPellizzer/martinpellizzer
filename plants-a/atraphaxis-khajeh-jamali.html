Atraphaxis khajeh-jamali

Atraphaxis khajeh-jamali, commonly known as the Khajeh-Jamali desert thorn, is a shrub native to arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by small, bead-like fruits that contain seeds. It is well adapted to harsh, dry environments, with deep roots that help it access groundwater.

Due to its resilience, it is often found in rocky and sandy soils of desert ecosystems.
