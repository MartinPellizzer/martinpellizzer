Anagallis nana

Anagallis nana, commonly known as the dwarf pink or small red flower, is a low-growing annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, fleshy leaves and produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink or red. The plant typically blooms from late spring to early summer, and its flowers have five petals with a distinctive spur at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in gardens and along stream banks.

Anagallis nana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is easy to cultivate in temperate climates.
