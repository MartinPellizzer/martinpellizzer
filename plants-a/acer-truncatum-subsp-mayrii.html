Acer truncatum subsp. mayrii

Acer truncatum subsp.

mayrii, commonly known as the Mayr's maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
