Abies lasiocarpa var. bifolia

Abies lasiocarpa var. bifolia, commonly known as the subalpine fir, is a variant of the subalpine fir native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its conical shape and dense, dark green needles that are shorter and more rigid than those of the typical subalpine fir. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in subalpine and alpine zones where temperatures are cool and precipitation is abundant. Its cones are typically reddish-brown and hang downward, releasing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variant is important for wildlife habitat and contributes to the ecological diversity of mountainous regions.
