Astragalus bossuensis

Astragalus bossuensis, commonly known as the Bossu milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume structure and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are often mistaken for peas or beans. Its leaves are compound, with a distinctive trifoliate structure, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Astragalus bossuensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
