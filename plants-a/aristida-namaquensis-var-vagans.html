Aristida namaquensis var. vagans

Aristida namaquensis var. vagans, commonly known as the Namibian threeawn, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves, and its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle. It produces small, pale green to brown flowers, which are characteristic of the grass family.

This species is often found in open habitats such as shrublands and grasslands, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for small animals.
