Acaena × cunctatrix

Acaena × cunctatrix, commonly known as the hybrid New Zealand spinach, is a perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its hairy, spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces aggregate fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Due to its ornamental value and edible qualities, it is cultivated in gardens and for forage.
