Acinodendron amplum

Acinodendron amplum, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has a tree-like structure with a straight trunk and broad, palm-like leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Acinodendron amplum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates due to its vibrant blooms and unique appearance.
