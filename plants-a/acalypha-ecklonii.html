Acalypha ecklonii

Acalypha ecklonii, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, torch-like inflorescences that are typically red or orange in color. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Acalypha ecklonii is often used in landscaping for its vibrant floral displays and hardy nature.
