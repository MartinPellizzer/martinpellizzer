Acacia neurophylla subsp. neurophylla

Acacia neurophylla subsp.

neurophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, phyllodes that are typically narrow and linear.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are highly attractive to native bees and other pollinators. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to access groundwater.

The wood of this species is hard and durable, making it valuable for traditional and contemporary uses.
