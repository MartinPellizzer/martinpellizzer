Ammannia patentinervius

Ammannia patentinervius, commonly known as the redroot dayflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and bright red stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a bluish-green hue with prominent veins.

Ammannia patentinervius is often found in wet or disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
