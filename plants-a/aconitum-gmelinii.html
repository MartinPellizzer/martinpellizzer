Aconitum gmelinii

Aconitum gmelinii, commonly known as the Siberian monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that resemble a hood, giving it its common name. The plant produces large, palmately divided leaves and has a tall, unbranched stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

Aconitum gmelinii contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires careful handling.
