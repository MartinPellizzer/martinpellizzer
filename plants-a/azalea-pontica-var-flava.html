Azalea pontica var. flava

Azalea pontica var. flava, commonly known as the yellow azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves that provide a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
