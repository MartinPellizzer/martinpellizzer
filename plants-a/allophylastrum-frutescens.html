Allophylastrum frutescens

Allophylastrum frutescens, commonly known as the African star grass, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats. The plant has small, clustered leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds.

Allophylastrum frutescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
