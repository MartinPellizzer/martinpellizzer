Austrocylindropuntia maldonadensis

Austrocylindropuntia maldonadensis, commonly known as the Maldonadensis cactus, is a species native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its green, spiny structure. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
