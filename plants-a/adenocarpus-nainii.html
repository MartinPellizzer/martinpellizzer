Adenocarpus nainii

Adenocarpus nainii, commonly known as the Nainii burrbean, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, compound leaves.

Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish in color, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various insects and animals.
