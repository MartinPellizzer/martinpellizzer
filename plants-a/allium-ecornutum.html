Allium ecornutum

Allium ecornutum, commonly known as the narrowleaf garlic.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, often mistaken for grasses or sedges.

Allium ecornutum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
