Aira villosa

Aira villosa, commonly known as hairy airgrass, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves covered in fine, hairy texture, giving it its common name. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

Aira villosa is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and wildlife.
