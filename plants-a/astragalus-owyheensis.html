Astragalus owyheensis

Astragalus owyheensis, commonly known as the Owens Valley milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. This perennial plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as slopes and gravelly soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sensitive species.
