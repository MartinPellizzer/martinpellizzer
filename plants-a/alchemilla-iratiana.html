Alchemilla iratiana

Alchemilla iratiana, commonly known as the Irish lady's mantle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla iratiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
