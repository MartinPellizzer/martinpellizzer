Asarum ikegamii var. fujimakii

Asarum ikegamii var. fujimakii, commonly known as the Japanese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers that are often hidden beneath its foliage. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
