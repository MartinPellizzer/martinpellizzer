Acacia cycnorum

Acacia cycnorum, commonly known as the white acacia, is a flowering plant native to Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows in dry, arid environments. The plant is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Acacia cycnorum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh conditions.
