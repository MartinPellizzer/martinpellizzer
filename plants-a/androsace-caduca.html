Androsace caduca

Androsace caduca, commonly known as the summer snowflake, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in its alpine habitat. Androsace caduca is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in cool, moist environments.
