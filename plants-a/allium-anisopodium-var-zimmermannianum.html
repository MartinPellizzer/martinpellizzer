Allium anisopodium var. zimmermannianum

Allium anisopodium var. zimmermannianum, commonly known as the Zim's leek, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces bulbs that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and mild garlic-like scent.
