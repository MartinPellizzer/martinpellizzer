Allium rotundum subsp. commutatum

Allium rotundum subsp.

commutatum, commonly known as the Persian leek or mountain garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale purple or white flowers in umbels.

The plant is valued for its edible bulbs, which have a mild garlic flavor and are used in various cuisines. Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other allium species such as garlic and onions.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
