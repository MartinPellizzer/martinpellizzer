Aphanelytrum procumbens

Aphanelytrum procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its spreading, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dense, mat-forming structure that helps it thrive in disturbed or arid soils.

Aphanelytrum procumbens is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
