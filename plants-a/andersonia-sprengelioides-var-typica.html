Andersonia sprengelioides var. typica

Andersonia sprengelioides var. typica, commonly known as the coastal Andersonia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or coastal soils, often in shaded or semi-shaded environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, which bloom during the cooler months. It has linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
