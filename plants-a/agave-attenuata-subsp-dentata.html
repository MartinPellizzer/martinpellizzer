Agave attenuata subsp. dentata

Agave attenuata subsp.

dentata, commonly known as the Mexican toothed agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico. It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves with a distinctive toothed margin.

The leaves are typically blue-green in color and can grow up to 1 meter in length. It produces a tall flower stalk that bears yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer.

This plant is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
