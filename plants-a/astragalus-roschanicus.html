Astragalus roschanicus

Astragalus roschanicus, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of Central Asia, including parts of Iran, Afghanistan, and the Caucasus. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow to brown flowers arranged in clusters. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus roschanicus is also noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
