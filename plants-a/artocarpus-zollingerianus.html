Artocarpus zollingerianus

Artocarpus zollingerianus, commonly known as the Zollinger's breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and distinctive fruit structure. The fruit, which resembles a breadfruit, is typically green when unripe and turns yellow or orange when mature. The plant is valued for its edible fruit, which is rich in nutrients and has a starchy texture similar to bread.

It thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for both food and ornamental purposes.
