Aristida elymoides

Aristida elymoides, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and slender, erect stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding a delicate floral display to its appearance. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.

Aristida elymoides is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
