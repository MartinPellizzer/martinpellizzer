Amaryllis macbridei

Amaryllis macbridei, commonly known as the Macbride's amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows as a bulbous perennial, with broad, strap-like leaves that emerge from the base. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
