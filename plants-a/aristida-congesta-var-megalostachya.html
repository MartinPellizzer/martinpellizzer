Aristida congesta var. megalostachya

Aristida congesta var. megalostachya, commonly known as the large-stemmed dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for native plant gardens and restoration projects. Its compact growth habit and ornamental seed heads make it a desirable choice for landscaping.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native habitat.
