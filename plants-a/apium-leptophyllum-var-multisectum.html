Apium leptophyllum var. multisectum

Apium leptophyllum var. multisectum, commonly known as the narrowleaf celery or slender parsley, is a variant of the apium genus native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often used in culinary applications. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is known for its strong, pungent aroma. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in umbels, a common feature among the Apiaceae family.

This variety is valued both for its edible qualities and its role in ecological habitats.
