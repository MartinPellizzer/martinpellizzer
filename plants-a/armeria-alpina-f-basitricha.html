Armeria alpina f. basitricha

Armeria alpina f. basitricha, commonly known as the Alpine thrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh alpine conditions.
