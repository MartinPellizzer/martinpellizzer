Adonis vernalis var. mentzelii

Adonis vernalis var. mentzelii, commonly known as the spring adonis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its early blooming period, typically in late winter to early spring. The plant features basal leaves that are pinnately divided and clusters of yellowish-green flowers with showy, petal-like sepals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
