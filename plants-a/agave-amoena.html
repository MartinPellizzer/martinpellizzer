Agave amoena

Agave amoena, commonly known as the narrow-leaved agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence bearing white to yellow flowers, which bloom once in its lifetime. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its thick, fleshy leaves.

Agave amoena is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping in desert gardens.
