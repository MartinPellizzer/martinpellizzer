Achyronia ciliaris

Achyronia ciliaris, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.

Achyronia ciliaris is also known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
