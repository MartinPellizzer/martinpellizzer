Aechmea wrightii

Aechmea wrightii, commonly known as the Wright's pineapple plant, is a bromeliad native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The plant produces a striking inflorescence with vibrant red flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette. Its foliage is typically dark green with a waxy texture, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Aechmea wrightii is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in indoor conditions.

Morphology

Aechmea wrightii has rosette-shaped leaves that are long, narrow, and stiff, arranged in a tight spiral around the central stem, with a dark green color and a waxy surface.

The stem is short and thick, with a smooth, pale green bark that grows upright from the base of the plant. It produces compact, spadix-like inflorescences with small, pale green to white flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall, pollinated primarily by insects. The fruit is a capsule that releases small, black seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for absorbing moisture from the surrounding soil.
