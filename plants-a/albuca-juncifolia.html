Albuca juncifolia

Albuca juncifolia, commonly known as the grass-leaved bulbous plant, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clustered bulbs that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces upright flower stalks that bear small, white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.

Albuca juncifolia prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.
