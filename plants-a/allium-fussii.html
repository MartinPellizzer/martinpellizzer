Allium fussii

Allium fussii, commonly known as the purple garlic or wild leek, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features grass-like basal leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple or white. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strong, garlic-like scent. Allium fussii is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
