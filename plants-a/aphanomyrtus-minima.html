Aphanomyrtus minima

Aphanomyrtus minima, commonly known as the dwarf mallees or coastal mallees, is a small shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, white wax. It produces small, pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, followed by small, woody fruits.

Aphanomyrtus minima is well adapted to arid and coastal environments, showing resilience to salt spray and drought.
