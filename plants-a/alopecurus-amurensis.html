Alopecurus amurensis

Alopecurus amurensis, commonly known as Amur hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, tufted culms and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slight hairy texture, contributing to its common name.

Alopecurus amurensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
