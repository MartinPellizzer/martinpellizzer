Amsonia ciliata var. texana

Amsonia ciliata var. texana, commonly known as Texas bluemink, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to purple flower clusters in late spring to early summer. The plant is typically found in open woods, prairies, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
