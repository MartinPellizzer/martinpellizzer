Astragalus desperatus var. neeseae

Astragalus desperatus var. neeseae, commonly known as Neese's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow to reddish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It is a perennial herb that produces small, pod-like fruits containing seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
