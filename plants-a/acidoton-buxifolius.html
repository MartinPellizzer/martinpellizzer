Acidoton buxifolius

Acidoton buxifolius, commonly known as the waxleaf acidoton, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that is thickly covered with a waxy coating, giving it a glossy appearance. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a leathery texture and a dark green color. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale yellow, which appear in clusters during the warmer months.

This plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
