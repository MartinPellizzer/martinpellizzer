Aconogonon alpinum var. elephantinum

Aconogonon alpinum var. elephantinum, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and often arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
