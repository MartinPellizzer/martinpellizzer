Agropyron junceum var. megastachys

Agropyron junceum var. megastachys, commonly known as the giant bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often purple or blue in color, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

This variety is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
