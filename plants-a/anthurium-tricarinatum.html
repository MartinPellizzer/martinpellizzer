Anthurium tricarinatum

Anthurium tricarinatum, commonly known as the three-ribbed anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive three-ribbed pattern. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-shaped inflorescences that are often surrounded by colorful spatula-like bracts. It thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light.

Anthurium tricarinatum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
