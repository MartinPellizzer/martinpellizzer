Abutilon leschenaultianum

Abutilon leschenaultianum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The leaves are typically alternate and have a distinctive, heart-shaped base.
