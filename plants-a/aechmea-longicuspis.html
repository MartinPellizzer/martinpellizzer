Aechmea longicuspis

Aechmea longicuspis, commonly known as the long-spined pineapple plant, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaf structure, which forms a central water-holding cup. The leaves are long, stiff, and covered in fine, white hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It produces vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette.

This plant is well adapted to humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Morphology

Aechmea longicuspis has leaves that are strap-shaped, arranged in a rosette, with a bright green color and a waxy texture, reaching up to 50 cm in length.

The plant has a short, thick stem covered in a smooth, dark green bark, growing upright from the base. Its flowers are clustered in a spike-like inflorescence, with purple petals and yellow stamens, blooming during the summer months and pollinated by insects. The fruit is a red, fleshy berry containing small, black seeds, which are dispersed by birds and water.

The roots are fibrous and form a dense network around the base of the plant, helping to anchor it and absorb moisture.
