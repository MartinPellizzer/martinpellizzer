Andrographis paniculata var. glandulosa

Andrographis paniculata var. glandulosa, commonly known as the Indian echinacea, is a variant of the Andrographis paniculata species, which is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its glandular leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is typically herbaceous, with a branched stem and opposite leaves that are often covered in tiny, sticky glands. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

This variant is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional systems of medicine.
