Acer cinerascens var. boissieri

Acer cinerascens var. boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's smoke tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage that turns a striking coppery-red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Its bark is smooth and greyish, with a slightly flaky texture.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
