Acanthus ilicifolius var. subinteger

Acanthus ilicifolius var. subinteger, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acanthus, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces showy, spires of violet-blue flowers in the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its robust growth habit makes it suitable for use in gardens as a specimen plant or hedge.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, requiring minimal maintenance once established.
