Aristida purpurascens

Aristida purpurascens, commonly known as purple threeawn, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that turn a striking purple or reddish hue as they mature. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, rocky slopes, and sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and have a rough texture, often with a bluish-gray appearance.

Aristida purpurascens is adapted to arid environments and is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
