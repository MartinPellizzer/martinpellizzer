Acacia armata f. microcantha

Acacia armata f. microcantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, narrow phyllodes that are typically green with a faint whitish bloom. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads during the autumn season, which are highly attractive to nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

This form of Acacia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
