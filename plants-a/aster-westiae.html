Aster westiae

Aster westiae, commonly known as the western aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its blooms, which appear in the fall, range in color from white to purple, adding vibrant color to the landscape.

Aster westiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
