Aglaia multifoliola

Aglaia multifoliola, commonly known as the many-leaved aglaia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Meliaceae family, which includes mahogany and other ornamental trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often deeply lobed and have a distinctive, intricate pattern. It produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to yellow, followed by woody fruits containing aromatic seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
