Austrocedrus

Austrocedrus, commonly known as the Patagonian cypress, is a genus of coniferous evergreen trees native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit and dark green, needle-like leaves. The cones of Austrocedrus are small and woody, containing two to three seeds each. This genus includes two recognized species, Austrocedrus chilensis and Austrocedrus fissuratus.

Austrocedrus is valued for its durable wood and plays an important ecological role in the Andean ecosystems.
