Arundinaria herzogiana

Arundinaria herzogiana, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a species of grass native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically green or reddish in color. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth surface and a bluish-green hue.

Arundinaria herzogiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
