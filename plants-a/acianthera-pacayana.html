Acianthera pacayana

Acianthera pacayana, commonly known as the Pacayana orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in the summer months. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with rhizomes that allow it to spread and form dense colonies. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns.

Acianthera pacayana thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
