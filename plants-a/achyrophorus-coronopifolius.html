Achyrophorus coronopifolius

Achyrophorus coronopifolius, commonly known as the crown-leaf achyrophorus, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive crown-like arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface.

The flowers are small and yellowish-green, appearing in clusters at the base of the plant.
