Agrostophyllum mucronatum

Agrostophyllum mucronatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stems. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are often clustered together. Its seeds are dispersed by birds, which consume the fleshy, orange-red berries.

Agrostophyllum mucronatum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and insects while also affecting the growth of its host trees.
