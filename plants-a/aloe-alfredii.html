Aloe alfredii

Aloe alfredii, commonly known as the Madagascar aloe, is a succulent plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick, fleshy, and covered in a white powdery substance called farina. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellow flowers, which bloom in spring. Its leaves contain a gel-like substance that is used in traditional medicine and skincare products.

Aloe alfredii is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to thrive.
