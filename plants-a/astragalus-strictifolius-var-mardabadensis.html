Astragalus strictifolius var. mardabadensis

Astragalus strictifolius var. mardabadensis, commonly known as the Mardabad milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
