Amaryllis phycelloides

Amaryllis phycelloides, commonly known as the pink amaryllis, is a striking flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range from white to pink and purple hues. The plant produces thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to its arid native environment. Its bulbs are underground and serve as storage organs, supporting the plant through periods of dormancy.

Amaryllis phycelloides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
