Adenophora rotundifolia

Adenophora rotundifolia, commonly known as round-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features oval, rounded leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
