Arundinaria higoensis

Arundinaria higoensis, commonly known as the Higo bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, grass-like texture.

Arundinaria higoensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
