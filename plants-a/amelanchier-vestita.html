Amelanchier vestita

Amelanchier vestita, commonly known as the Pacific serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that ripen in late summer, turning from red to dark purple. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Amelanchier vestita is often found in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in open woodlands.
