Aesculus livida

Aesculus livida, commonly known as the European horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces spiny, capsule-like fruits containing shiny, chestnut-like seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with a distinctive, flaky texture.

Aesculus livida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in parks and gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
