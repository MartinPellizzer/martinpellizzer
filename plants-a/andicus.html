Andicus

Andicus, commonly known as the Indian nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging plants. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface that can cause skin irritation upon contact. Its small greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Andicus is often found in moist, shaded areas and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
