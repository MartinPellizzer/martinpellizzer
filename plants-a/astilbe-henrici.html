Astilbe henrici

Astilbe henrici, commonly known as the Henry's astilbe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems that bear clusters of small, showy flowers. The foliage is typically dark green and finely divided, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The plant is valued for its delicate appearance and ability to add texture and color to shaded landscapes.
