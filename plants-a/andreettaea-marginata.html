Andreettaea marginata

Andreettaea marginata, commonly known as the Brazilian violet, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple-tinged margin, and its flowers are small, clustered, and usually pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
