Ateleia apetala var. trinitensis

Ateleia apetala var. trinitensis, commonly known as the Trinidadian starfruit, is a rare and unique variant of the ateleia genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped fruits that are typically yellow when mature. The plant is native to the Caribbean, particularly Trinidad and Tobago, where it thrives in tropical climates. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a botanically significant and ecologically valuable species.
