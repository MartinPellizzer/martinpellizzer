Aster tricolor

Aster tricolor, commonly known as the three-color aster, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, three-toned flowers that typically display shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Aster tricolor thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
