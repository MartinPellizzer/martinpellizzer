Astragalus arganaticus var. campylanthus

Astragalus arganaticus var. campylanthus, commonly known as the Arganica Campylanthus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to dry environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in poor soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

This species is of interest in ecological studies due to its resilience and role in desert ecosystems.
