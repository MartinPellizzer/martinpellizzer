Alyssum murale var. tenue

Alyssum murale var. tenue, commonly known as narrowleaf mouseear, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the western United States.

It features small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring and early summer. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a soft, velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract beneficial insects.
