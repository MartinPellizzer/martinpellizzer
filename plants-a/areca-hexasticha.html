Areca hexasticha

Areca hexasticha, commonly known as the six-angled areca, is a species of palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that are divided into six distinct segments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into three-angled fruits containing a single seed. Areca hexasticha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Arecaceae family.
