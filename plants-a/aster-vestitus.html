Aster vestitus

Aster vestitus, commonly known as the naked aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

Aster vestitus is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.
