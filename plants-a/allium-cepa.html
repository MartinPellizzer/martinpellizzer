Allium cepa

Allium cepa, commonly known as the shallot, is a bulbous plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its cluster of small, elongated bulbs that are typically white or pale purple in color. The plant produces grass-like leaves and small, white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer. Its bulbs are widely used in cooking for their pungent, sweet flavor.

Botanically, it is closely related to garlic and onions, sharing similar growth habits and chemical compositions.
