Alternanthera praelonga

Alternanthera praelonga, commonly known as the red-stemmed Alternanthera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or purple stems and lance-shaped leaves that often have a metallic sheen. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or green flowers that are clustered in spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
