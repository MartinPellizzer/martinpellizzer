Aspalathus simsiana

Aspalathus simsiana, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, hard pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various local wildlife species.
