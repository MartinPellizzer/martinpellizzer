Aster vimineus var. dubius

Aster vimineus var. dubius, commonly known as the slender aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has slender, wiry stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
