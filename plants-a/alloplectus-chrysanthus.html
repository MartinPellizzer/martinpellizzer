Alloplectus chrysanthus

Alloplectus chrysanthus, commonly known as the golden flax, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees. Its fibrous roots and stems are similar to those of other flax species, making it a close relative of Linum usitatissimum.

Alloplectus chrysanthus is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
