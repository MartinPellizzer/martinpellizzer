Anemone myriophylla

Anemone myriophylla, commonly known as the mountain anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high elevations of the western United States.

It features showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are palmately divided, with a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
