Apocynum × lividum var. floribundum

Apocynum × lividum var. floribundum, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a hybrid plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes many milkweeds known for their milky sap. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and wetlands.

Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, making it an important plant in ecological systems.
