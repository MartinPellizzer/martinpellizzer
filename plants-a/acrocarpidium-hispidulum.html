Acrocarpidium hispidulum

Acrocarpidium hispidulum, commonly known as the hairy acrocarp, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that are covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by tiny, round berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental foliage, it is a popular choice among gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
