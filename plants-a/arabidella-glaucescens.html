Arabidella glaucescens

Arabidella glaucescens, commonly known as the bluish morning glory, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features slender, hairy stems that often sprawl along the ground. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine pubescence, giving them a bluish-gray appearance. Its flowers are trumpet-shaped, typically blue or purple, and open in the morning.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
