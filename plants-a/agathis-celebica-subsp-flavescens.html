Agathis celebica subsp. flavescens

Agathis celebica subsp.

flavescens, commonly known as the Celebes agathis, is a tropical tree native to the islands of Indonesia. It belongs to the family Araucariaceae and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and broad, feather-like leaves.

The plant produces large, woody cones that contain edible seeds, which are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

Due to its valuable timber and ecological significance, it is also considered a species of conservation concern.
