Asparagus tamariscinus var. issykkulensis

Asparagus tamariscinus var. issykkulensis, commonly known as the Issyk Kul asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by small, edible berries. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and mountainous regions.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
