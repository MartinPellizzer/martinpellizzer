Asplenium plumbeum

Asplenium plumbeum, commonly known as the silver fern, is a small, evergreen fern native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a dense, compact growth habit with fronds that are typically gray-green in color due to the presence of fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by spore-bearing structures called sori, which are arranged in clusters on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or woodland areas.

Asplenium plumbeum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
