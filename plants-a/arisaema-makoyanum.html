Arisaema makoyanum

Arisaema makoyanum, commonly known as the Chinese arrowroot or mountain arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

Arisaema makoyanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in various regions.
