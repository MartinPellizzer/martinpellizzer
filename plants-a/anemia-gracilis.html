Anemia gracilis

Anemia gracilis, commonly known as the slender anemone, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and showy, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cooler climates. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Anemia gracilis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
