Arisaema filiforme var. verrucosum

Arisaema filiforme var. verrucosum, commonly known as the veined thread-leaf arum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves and a distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered around the spadix. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
