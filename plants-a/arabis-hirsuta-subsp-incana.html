Arabis hirsuta subsp. incana

Arabis hirsuta subsp.

incana, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with characteristic cruciform flowers.

The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
