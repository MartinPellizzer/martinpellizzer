Andromeda lanceolata

Andromeda lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wintergreen, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. The plant is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
