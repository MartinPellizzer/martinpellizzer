Asparagus leptocladodius

Asparagus leptocladodius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved asparagus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, arching stems that produce small, white flowers clustered in umbels. The plant has delicate, feathery leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
