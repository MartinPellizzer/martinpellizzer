Astragalus renzii

Astragalus renzii, commonly known as the Chinese milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to grow in dry, rocky environments, showcasing its resilience to arid conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution in mountainous regions.
