Angraecum cornucopiae

Angraecum cornucopiae, commonly known as the Cornucopia orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble a horned cup, hence its common name. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale pink blooms that are often adorned with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Angraecum cornucopiae is a popular specimen among orchid enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and rarity.
