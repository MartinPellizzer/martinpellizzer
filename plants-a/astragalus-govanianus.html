Astragalus govanianus

Astragalus govanianus, commonly known as the Govan's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has a deep root system, allowing it to thrive in dry, sandy soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Astragalus govanianus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
