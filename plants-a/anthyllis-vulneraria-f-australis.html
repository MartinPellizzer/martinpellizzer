Anthyllis vulneraria f. australis

Anthyllis vulneraria f. australis, commonly known as the Australian milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of many leguminous species. This subspecies is native to southern Australia and thrives in sandy or rocky soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

The plant is often found in coastal and inland regions, showcasing its adaptability to diverse environmental conditions.
