Amorphophallus granatensis

Amorphophallus granatensis, commonly known as the giant swamp taro, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and a striking, spathaceous inflorescence that resembles a phallic structure, hence its scientific name. The plant produces a single, fleshy underground tuber that serves as a storage organ. It thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
