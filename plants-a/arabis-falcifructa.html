Arabis falcifructa

Arabis falcifructa, commonly known as the sickle-fruited rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its fruits are distinctive, being elongated and curved, resembling a sickle, which gives the species its common name.

Arabis falcifructa thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.
