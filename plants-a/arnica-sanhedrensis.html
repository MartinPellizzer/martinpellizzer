Arnica sanhedrensis

Arnica sanhedrensis, commonly known as the Sinai arnica, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Sinai Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky and sandy soils, thriving in harsh, dry environments. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Arnica sanhedrensis is valued for its medicinal properties, with its leaves and flowers used in traditional remedies.
