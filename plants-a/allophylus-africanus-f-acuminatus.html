Allophylus africanus f. acuminatus

Allophylus africanus f. acuminatus, commonly known as the African alderleaf, is a variant of the African alder tree native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in terminal panicles, and it produces drupes that are an important food source for local wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in coastal ecosystems.
