Artemisia polybotryoidea

Artemisia polybotryoidea, commonly known as the silverleaf artemisia, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which results from dense covering of fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in dense, spherical shapes known as capitula. Artemisia polybotryoidea is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in dry landscapes.
