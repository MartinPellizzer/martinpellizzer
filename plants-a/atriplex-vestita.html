Atriplex vestita

Atriplex vestita, commonly known as silverleaf gooseberry, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by edible, fleshy berries. Atriplex vestita is drought-tolerant and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
