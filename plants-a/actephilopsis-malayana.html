Actephilopsis malayana

Actephilopsis malayana, commonly known as the Malay orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia, where it thrives in tropical rainforests. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. It prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in its natural habitat.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
