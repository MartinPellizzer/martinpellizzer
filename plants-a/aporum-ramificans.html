Aporum ramificans

Aporum ramificans, commonly known as the branching aporum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which includes plants like philodendrons and calla lilies. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a brightly colored spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Aporum ramificans is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and unique floral structure.
