Astragalus silvasicus

Astragalus silvasicus, commonly known as the Silvis mountain milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Astragalus silvasicus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
