Andropogon pilosissimus

Andropogon pilosissimus, commonly known as silky grass or silky bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs that cover the leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

Andropogon pilosissimus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
