Acacia rhodophloia

Acacia rhodophloia, commonly known as the red-barked acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect shrub form and distinctive reddish-brown bark that peels in thin layers. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into multiple pairs of small, feather-like leaflets.

Acacia rhodophloia is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species often found in coastal and inland regions.
