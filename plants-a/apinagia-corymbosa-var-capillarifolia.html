Apinagia corymbosa var. capillarifolia

Apinagia corymbosa var. capillarifolia, commonly known as the pinkroot or redroot, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a distinctive reddish-brown root system, which is often harvested for medicinal purposes. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and streambanks.
