Alepidea wyliei

Alepidea wyliei, commonly known as the Wylie's daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flower heads surrounded by white ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a compact, low-growing habit with linear leaves and is often found in shrubland and fynbos vegetation.

Alepidea wyliei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
