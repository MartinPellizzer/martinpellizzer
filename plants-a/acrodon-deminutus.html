Acrodon deminutus

Acrodon deminutus, commonly known as the dwarf acrodon, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically hairy foliage. The plant features a compact growth habit with numerous small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine pubescence, aiding in water retention in its arid habitat.

Acrodon deminutus is adapted to cold, dry environments and is often found in rocky or alpine soils.
