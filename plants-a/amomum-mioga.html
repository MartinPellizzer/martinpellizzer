Amomum mioga

Amomum mioga, commonly known as the Chinese ginger or mioga ginger, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its aromatic, knotted rhizomes that are widely used in traditional Chinese medicine. The plant features tall, leafy stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers. Its rhizomes are valued for their pungent flavor and medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and digestive benefits.

Amomum mioga is often cultivated for both culinary and therapeutic purposes.
