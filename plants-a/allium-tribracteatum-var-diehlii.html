Allium tribracteatum var. diehlii

Allium tribracteatum var. diehlii, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada garlic, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features a bulb composed of several small, white, fleshy cloves surrounded by a papery outer layer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild garlic-like fragrance.
