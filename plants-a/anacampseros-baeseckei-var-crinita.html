Anacampseros baeseckei var. crinita

Anacampseros baeseckei var. crinita, commonly known as the curly anacampseros, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rosette-forming leaves that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the growing season. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Its unique morphology and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
