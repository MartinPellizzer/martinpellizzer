Adiantum glaucescens

Adiantum glaucescens, commonly known as the blue fern, is a species of polypod fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically light green to bluish-gray in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are bipinnate, with closely spaced pinnae that give it a feathery appearance.

Adiantum glaucescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
