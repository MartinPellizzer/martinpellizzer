Astragalus onobrychis var. aduncus

Astragalus onobrychis var. aduncus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yellow vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with small, yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to semi-arid climates.

This species is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
