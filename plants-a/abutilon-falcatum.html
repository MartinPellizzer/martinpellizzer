Abutilon falcatum

Abutilon falcatum, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a deciduous shrub native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that turn red or purple in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, lantern-shaped orange to red flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Abutilon falcatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
