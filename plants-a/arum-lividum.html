Arum lividum

Arum lividum, commonly known as the blue arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features a striking, arrow-shaped leaf that is often bluish-green in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces a spadix, which is surrounded by a colorful spathe, typically in shades of white or pink. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Arum lividum is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
