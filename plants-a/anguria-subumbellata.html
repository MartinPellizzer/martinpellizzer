Anguria subumbellata

Anguria subumbellata, commonly known as the tropical starfruit, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, yellow, star-shaped fruits that are typically harvested when they turn orange. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and thrives in humid, tropical climates. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbellate inflorescences, giving it its scientific name.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and is valued for its tangy flavor.
