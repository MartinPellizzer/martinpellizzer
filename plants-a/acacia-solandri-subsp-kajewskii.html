Acacia solandri subsp. kajewskii

Acacia solandri subsp.

kajewskii, commonly known as the Kajewskii acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and phyllodes that are typically narrow and linear.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are typical of the Acacia genus. Its leaves are modified into phyllodes, a feature that helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

This subspecies is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.
