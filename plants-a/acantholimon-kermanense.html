Acantholimon kermanense

Acantholimon kermanense, commonly known as Kerman's thornweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and spikes of small, typically pink or purple flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in dry, rocky environments, with deep root systems that help it access water from underground sources.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying xerophytic adaptations.
