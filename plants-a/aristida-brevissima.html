Aristida brevissima

Aristida brevissima, commonly known as the short-stemmed threeawn, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often described as bristly or bristly-like. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairie and woodland habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
