Argyreia adpressa

Argyreia adpressa, commonly known as the velvet flower or blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Argyreia adpressa is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb using its tendrils.
