Atropis distans var. glauca

Atropis distans var. glauca, commonly known as the blue atropis, is a variant of the atropis species characterized by its distinctive blue-gray foliage.

It is a perennial plant native to specific regions in southern Africa, where it thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue, adding to its ornamental appeal. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

This variant is often cultivated for its unique coloration and resilience in dry conditions.
