Agave simplex

Agave simplex, commonly known as the narrowleaf agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flower stalk with small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Agave simplex is often used in landscaping due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
