Alchemilla hoppeana subsp. asterophylla

Alchemilla hoppeana subsp.

asterophylla, commonly known as the star-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple.

The plant produces upright flowering stems bearing small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural habitats.
