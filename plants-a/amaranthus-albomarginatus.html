Amaranthus albomarginatus

Amaranthus albomarginatus, commonly known as the white-edged amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is native to the southern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in warm, sunny environments. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking, feather-like leaves that are often white- or cream-colored along the edges. Its small, green flowers are clustered in dense, terminal panicles, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.

Amaranthus albomarginatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
