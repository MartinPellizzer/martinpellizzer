Allium latissimum

Allium latissimum, commonly known as broadleaf garlic, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its broad, grass-like leaves and spherical bulbous structure. The plant produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in culinary applications.

Additionally, it is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
