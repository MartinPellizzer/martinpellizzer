Arrhenatherum elongatum

Arrhenatherum elongatum, commonly known as narrow-leaved wood oat, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or brown.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity and its use in ornamental and ecological landscaping.
