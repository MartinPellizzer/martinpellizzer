Alsine antillana

Alsine antillana, commonly known as the West Indian sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, green, ovate leaves and produces tiny, pale green flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Alsine antillana is adapted to arid conditions and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
