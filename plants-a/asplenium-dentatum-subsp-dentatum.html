Asplenium dentatum subsp. dentatum

Asplenium dentatum subsp.

dentatum, commonly known as the toothed spleenwort, is a fern species belonging to the Aspleniaceae family. It is characterized by its small, dark green, leathery leaves that are typically divided into tooth-like segments.

This fern is often found growing on rocks or in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or crevices. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively.

It is a relatively hardy species, capable of surviving in a variety of temperate climates.
