Arenaria boetica

Arenaria boetica, commonly known as the Canary Island sandflower, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy or rocky soils, often found in coastal areas. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in arid conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
