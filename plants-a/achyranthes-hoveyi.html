Achyranthes hoveyi

Achyranthes hoveyi, commonly known as the Hovey's smartweed, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks. The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Achyranthes hoveyi is known for its ability to thrive in wet environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
