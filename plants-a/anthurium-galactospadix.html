Anthurium galactospadix

Anthurium galactospadix, commonly known as the white-spotted anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with white or pale green markings. The plant produces bright red spadices, which are the spiky, club-shaped structures that house the tiny, yellowish flowers. Anthurium galactospadix thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
