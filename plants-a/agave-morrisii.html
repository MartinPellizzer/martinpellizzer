Agave morrisii

Agave morrisii, commonly known as the Morris agave, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a succulent perennial plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears clusters of white flowers, which appear in the spring. Agave morrisii is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its slow growth rate and striking appearance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
