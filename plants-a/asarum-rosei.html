Asarum rosei

Asarum rosei, commonly known as the roseleaf wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often hidden beneath its foliage. Its rhizomatous root system is used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Asarum rosei thrives in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its medicinal properties.
