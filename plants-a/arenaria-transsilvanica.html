Arenaria transsilvanica

Arenaria transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its mat-forming growth habit with tiny, clustered flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in harsh conditions.

The flowers, which are usually pink or white, bloom in early summer and attract various pollinators.
