Alona rostrata

Alona rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated water flea, is a small aquatic crustacean belonging to the family Daphniidae.

It is characterized by its elongated body and a distinct rostrum, which is a prominent forward-pointing structure. Its presence is often used as an indicator of water quality due to its sensitivity to environmental changes.
