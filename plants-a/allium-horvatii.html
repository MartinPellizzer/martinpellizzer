Allium horvatii

Allium horvatii, commonly known as the Horvat's leek, is a perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, strap-like leaves. The plant produces a tall flowering stem topped with a cluster of small, white or pale purple flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to rocky or alpine soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
