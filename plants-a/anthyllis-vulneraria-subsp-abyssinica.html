Anthyllis vulneraria subsp. abyssinica

Anthyllis vulneraria subsp.

abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian burnet, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of Ethiopia and Eritrea. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant has hairy, creeping stems and produces small, oval-shaped leaves with a distinctive stipulate structure. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often growing on slopes and in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh climates and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
