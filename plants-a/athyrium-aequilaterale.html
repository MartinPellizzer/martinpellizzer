Athyrium aequilaterale

Athyrium aequilaterale, commonly known as the broad-leaf mottled wood fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its large, intricately divided fronds. The plant features a short, creeping rhizome with numerous upright, spore-bearing fiddleheads. Its fronds are typically bioluminescent, with a distinctive mottled pattern of green and brown.
