Adenostemma suffruticosum

Adenostemma suffruticosum, commonly known as the silverleaf bluestem, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a perennial herb or shrub characterized by its silver-gray leaves and clusters of small blue to purple flowers. The plant has a woody stem and often grows in disturbed soils, making it adaptable to various environments. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological role in supporting insect populations.

The species is also sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and bloom.
