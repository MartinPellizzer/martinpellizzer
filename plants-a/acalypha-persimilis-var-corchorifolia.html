Acalypha persimilis var. corchorifolia

Acalypha persimilis var. corchorifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silky mallow, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features soft, fuzzy leaves that are often a deep green or reddish hue, providing a striking visual appeal. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or pale yellow, which bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for garden use, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
