Aralia spinosa var. elata

Aralia spinosa var. elata, commonly known as the tall aralia, is a variant of the spiny aralia native to eastern North America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive spines. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbels, which are followed by orange-red berries. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.

The plant is also valued for its role in wildlife habitats, providing food and shelter for birds and small mammals.
