Alcea

Alcea, commonly known as hollyhock, is a genus of flowering plants in the mallow family, Malvaceae.

These plants are characterized by their showy, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, with some varieties exhibiting white or yellow hues. Alcea species are herbaceous annuals or perennials, often growing up to 2 meters tall, with large, palmately lobed leaves. They thrive in full sun and well-drained soil, making them popular in gardens for their ornamental value.

The plant's fibrous stems and hairy leaves are typical of the mallow family, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
