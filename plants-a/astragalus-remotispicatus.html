Astragalus remotispicatus

Astragalus remotispicatus, commonly known as the remote-spurred milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.

Its scientific study contributes to understanding plant adaptations in semi-arid regions.
