Adiantum indicum

Adiantum indicum, commonly known as the Indian maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Asia, particularly India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are bipinnate, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in shady, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually bright green and have a distinctive, arching growth habit.

Adiantum indicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
