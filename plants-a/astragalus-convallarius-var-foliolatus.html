Astragalus convallarius var. foliolatus

Astragalus convallarius var. foliolatus, commonly known as the leafy milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in spikes, typical of the genus Astragalus. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal medicine.
