Achillea asiatica var. marginata

Achillea asiatica var. marginata, commonly known as the Asian yarrow, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are finely divided and serrated, giving the plant a delicate, feathery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
