Acacia myriophylla

Acacia myriophylla, commonly known as the red acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its deep root system. Its small, pod-like fruits contain seeds that are dispersed by animals and wind.

Acacia myriophylla plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
