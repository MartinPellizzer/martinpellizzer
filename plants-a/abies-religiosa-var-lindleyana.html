Abies religiosa var. lindleyana

Abies religiosa var. lindleyana, commonly known as the Mexican silver fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the high-altitude regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are coated with a whitish bloom, giving it a silvery appearance. The tree produces cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
