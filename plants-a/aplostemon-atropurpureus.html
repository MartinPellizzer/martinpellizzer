Aplostemon atropurpureus

Aplostemon atropurpureus, commonly known as the purple-flowered aplostemon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright, slender stems that are often tinged with purple, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically a deep purple or violet color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular plant among gardeners in arid regions.
