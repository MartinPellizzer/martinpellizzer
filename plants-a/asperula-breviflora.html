Asperula breviflora

Asperula breviflora, commonly known as the dwarf azalea, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and red during late spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Asperula breviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to bloom repeatedly throughout the growing season.
