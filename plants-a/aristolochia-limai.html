Aristolochia limai

Aristolochia limai, commonly known as the Brazilian pipevine, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, pipe-like flowers that are typically greenish-brown with a strong, musky odor. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique floral structure and chemical composition make it an interesting subject for botanical and ecological studies.
