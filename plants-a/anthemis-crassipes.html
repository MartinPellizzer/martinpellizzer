Anthemis crassipes

Anthemis crassipes, commonly known as the rough anthemis, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. Its leaves are rough to the touch and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
