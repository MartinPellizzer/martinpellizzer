Amorphophallus carneus

Amorphophallus carneus, commonly known as the red veined arrowhead, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent red veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces striking, spadix-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Amorphophallus carneus is also noted for its ability to store energy in its underground tuber, allowing it to survive in less-than-ideal conditions.
