Acianthera ochreata subsp. cylindrifolia

Acianthera ochreata subsp.

cylindrifolia, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America. It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and slender, cylindrical pseudobulbs.

The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps in water retention.

The orchid produces fragrant blooms that attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
