Anthemis retusa

Anthemis retusa, commonly known as the narrowleaf tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters.

Anthemis retusa is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
