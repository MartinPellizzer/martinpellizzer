Ageratinastrum goetzeanum

Ageratinastrum goetzeanum, commonly known as Goetze's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, branched stems and narrow, hairy leaves that are often gray-green in color. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as prairies and wetlands.
