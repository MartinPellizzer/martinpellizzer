Arabis flexuosa

Arabis flexuosa, commonly known as the bent-leaf rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves that are often curled or folded. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in spring.

Arabis flexuosa is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments.
