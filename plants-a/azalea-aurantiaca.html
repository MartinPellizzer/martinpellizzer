Azalea aurantiaca

Azalea aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange azalea, is a species of flowering plant in the Rhododendron genus.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like China, India, and Myanmar. The plant is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range from orange to deep red in color. Azalea aurantiaca typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth and flowering.
