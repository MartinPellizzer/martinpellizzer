Anthurium parambae

Anthurium parambae, commonly known as the red-veined anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with deep red veins. The plant produces spadices, which are inflorescences that consist of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Anthurium parambae thrives in humid, warm environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
