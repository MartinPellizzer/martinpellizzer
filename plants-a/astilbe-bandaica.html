Astilbe bandaica

Astilbe bandaica, commonly known as the Japanese feather reed, is a perennial plant native to Japan.

It features delicate, plume-like inflorescences that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has fern-like, compound leaves that are finely divided and provide a lush, green backdrop to its showy flowers. It thrives in partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Astilbe bandaica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to add texture and color to shaded garden spaces.
