Anemia schraderiana

Anemia schraderiana, commonly known as the Schrader's anemone, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has basal leaves that are palmately lobed, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable addition to woodland gardens.

Anemia schraderiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
