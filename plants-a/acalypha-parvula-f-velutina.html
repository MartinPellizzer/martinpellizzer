Acalypha parvula f. velutina

Acalypha parvula f. velutina, commonly known as velvetleaf acalypha, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to tropical regions.

It features small, ovate leaves that are densely covered with fine, soft hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that are often overlooked but contribute to its ecological value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its unique foliage, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
