Anthyllis ramburei

Anthyllis ramburei, commonly known as the Rambure's anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Anthyllis ramburei is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
