Alsophila notabilis

Alsophila notabilis, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant has a distinctive crown of fronds that forms a rosette shape, giving it a unique and elegant appearance. Its rhizome is thick and woody, providing structural support and aiding in nutrient absorption.

Alsophila notabilis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests and coastal areas.
