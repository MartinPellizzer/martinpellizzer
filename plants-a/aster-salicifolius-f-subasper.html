Aster salicifolius f. subasper

Aster salicifolius f. subasper, commonly known as the rough-leaved aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, hairy leaves that give it a rough texture, as indicated by the subspecies name "subasper.". The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers in the late summer and early fall. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
