Agapanthus umbellatus var. mooreanus

Agapanthus umbellatus var. mooreanus, commonly known as the Moorean agapanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking blue to purple spherical flower heads that bloom in summer, emerging from grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with a clumping growth habit. Its foliage consists of narrow, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

The flowers are arranged in an umbel, giving the plant its scientific name, and it is well-suited to full sun and well-drained soil.
