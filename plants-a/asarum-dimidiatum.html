Asarum dimidiatum

Asarum dimidiatum, commonly known as the lesser Indian snakeroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often mottled with dark spots, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Asarum dimidiatum is valued in herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
