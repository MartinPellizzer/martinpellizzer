Acriopsis floribunda

Acriopsis floribunda, commonly known as the clustered cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are often used in traditional agriculture.

Acriopsis floribunda is valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in sustainable farming practices.
