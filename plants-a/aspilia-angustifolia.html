Aspilia angustifolia

Aspilia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on branched inflorescences. It thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Aspilia angustifolia is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.
