Ardisia melastomoides

Ardisia melastomoides, commonly known as the glossy leaf ardisia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its attractive, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Following flowering, it develops berries that are typically red or black when mature.

Ardisia melastomoides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
