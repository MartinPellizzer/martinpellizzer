Achyranthes acuminata

Achyranthes acuminata, commonly known as the Indian knotgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its erect stem and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Achyranthes acuminata is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in ecological systems.
