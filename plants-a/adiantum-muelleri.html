Adiantum muelleri

Adiantum muelleri, commonly known as the Mueller's maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bioluminescent in the dark. The plant prefers shady, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a distinctive frond structure that allows for efficient water retention.

Adiantum muelleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
