Arisaema silvestrii

Arisaema silvestrii, commonly known as the mountain arisaema, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant produces a single, strikingly colored spathe that can range from white to green or red, depending on the variety. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly through underground stems, making it a suitable candidate for woodland gardens.

Arisaema silvestrii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
