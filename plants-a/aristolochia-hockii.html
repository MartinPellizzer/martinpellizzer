Aristolochia hockii

Aristolochia hockii, commonly known as the Hock's Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial vine that produces unique, heart-shaped flowers with a distinctive, often foul-smelling interior. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its tubular, spurred flowers that attract specific pollinators. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough, textured surface.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
