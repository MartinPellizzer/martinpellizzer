Atriplex platensis

Atriplex platensis, commonly known as the saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent leaves, which are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is well adapted to saline and alkaline soils. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain single seeds.

Atriplex platensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
