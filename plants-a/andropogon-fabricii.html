Andropogon fabricii

Andropogon fabricii, commonly known as the tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common component of prairie ecosystems.

Andropogon fabricii is valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and forage for wildlife.
