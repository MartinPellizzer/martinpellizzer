Artocarpus sumatranus

Artocarpus sumatranus, commonly known as the "sukun" or "breadfruit tree," is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, spiky fruits. The fruit, which resembles a breadfruit, is typically harvested when mature and used in various culinary applications. The tree is also valued for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.

Its growth habit includes a tall, straight trunk with a broad canopy, making it a prominent feature in tropical landscapes.
