Allamanda nobilis

Allamanda nobilis, commonly known as the noble allamanda, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, while its stems are woody and often covered in a powdery coating.

Allamanda nobilis is cultivated for its ornamental value and is often used in tropical gardens and as a houseplant.
