Asparagus asiaticus var. amharicus

Asparagus asiaticus var. amharicus, commonly known as Ethiopian asparagus, is a perennial plant native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional medicine.
