Argyreia arborea

Argyreia arborea, commonly known as the "Crimson beauty" or "Crimson flame," is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that are clustered in panicles, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are ovate and dark green, with a slightly hairy texture.

Argyreia arborea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
