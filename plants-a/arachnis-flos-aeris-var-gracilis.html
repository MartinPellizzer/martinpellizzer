Arachnis flos-aeris var. gracilis

Arachnis flos-aeris var. gracilis, commonly known as the skyflower, is a rare and delicate plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, aerial stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often without any support. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a botanical curiosity and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
