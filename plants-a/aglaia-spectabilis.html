Aglaia spectabilis

Aglaia spectabilis, commonly known as the beautiful aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family, which includes mahogany and other economically important hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark grey bark and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically yellow or greenish-yellow, clustered in dense inflorescences.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
