Anemia hispida

Anemia hispida, commonly known as the roughleaf anemone, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves that are rough to the touch. The plant produces showy, white to pink flowers with a distinctive, rough texture on the petals. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, actinomorphic flowers.
