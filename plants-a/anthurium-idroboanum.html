Anthurium idroboanum

Anthurium idroboanum, commonly known as the Idrobo anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Colombia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant spatulate flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage is often deep green with a glossy texture, and the inflorescence features a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe.

Anthurium idroboanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for decorative purposes in both indoor and outdoor settings.
