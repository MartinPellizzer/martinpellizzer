Aira patulipes

Aira patulipes, commonly known as the broadleaf air grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida, and thrives in wetland environments. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various wildlife species.
