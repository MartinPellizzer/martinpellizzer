Allium virgunculae f. albiflorum

Allium virgunculae f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered garlic chive, is a cultivated form of Allium virgunculae, a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, bulbous structures similar to garlic, which are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild garlic-like aroma.
