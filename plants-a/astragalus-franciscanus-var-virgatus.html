Astragalus franciscanus var. virgatus

Astragalus franciscanus var. virgatus, commonly known as the Franciscan milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This species plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
