Alyxia cacuminum

Alyxia cacuminum, commonly known as the coastal alyxia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are usually clustered at the base of the stems. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are consumed by various bird species.

Alyxia cacuminum is adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate salt spray, making it a common sight in coastal ecosystems.
