Aggreflorum longifolium

Aggreflorum longifolium, commonly known as the longleaf agreflorum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Aggreflorum longifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
