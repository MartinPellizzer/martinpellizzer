Austrostipa geoffreyi

Austrostipa geoffreyi, commonly known as the Geoffrey's wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland grasslands.

Austrostipa geoffreyi plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native environment.
