Aglaia palembanica var. longifolia

Aglaia palembanica var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.
