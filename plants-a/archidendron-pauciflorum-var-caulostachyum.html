Archidendron pauciflorum var. caulostachyum

Archidendron pauciflorum var. caulostachyum, commonly known as the purple wisteria, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, twining stems and clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
