Achyranthes argentea var. virgata

Achyranthes argentea var. virgata, commonly known as silverleaf achaenanthe, is a variant of the Achyranthes genus native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as an upright herb with branched stems and opposite leaf arrangement. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in spikes, and it produces capsule fruits containing several seeds.

It is commonly found in disturbed soils and along roadsides in its native habitat.
