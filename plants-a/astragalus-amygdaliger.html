Astragalus amygdaliger

Astragalus amygdaliger, commonly known as the almondleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant has a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in arid and rocky soils. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several small, hard seeds.

Astragalus amygdaliger is often found in sagebrush steppe and other dry, open habitats.
