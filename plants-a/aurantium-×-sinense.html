Aurantium × sinense

Aurantium × sinense, commonly known as the sweet orange, is a hybrid citrus plant originating from the cross between Citrus sinensis and Citrus aurantium.

It is characterized by its thick, peels that contain essential oils and its juicy, segmented fruit filled with aromatic pulp. The plant is evergreen, with glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in warm, subtropical climates and is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The tree can grow up to 5 meters tall and is valued for its ornamental and economic significance.
