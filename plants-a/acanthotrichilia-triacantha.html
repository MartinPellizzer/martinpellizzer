Acanthotrichilia triacantha

Acanthotrichilia triacantha, commonly known as the thorned acanthotrichilia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a branching stem covered in sharp, spiny tubercles, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
