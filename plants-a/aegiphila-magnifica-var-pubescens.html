Aegiphila magnifica var. pubescens

Aegiphila magnifica var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy magnifica aegiphila, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, pubescent foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are often aromatic and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
