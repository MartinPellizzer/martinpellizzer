Astragalus hurtadensis

Astragalus hurtadensis, commonly known as the Mexican milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It produces pods that contain small, round seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus hurtadensis is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in desert environments.
