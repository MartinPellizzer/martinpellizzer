Asclepias sinuosa

Asclepias sinuosa, commonly known as the zigzag milkweed, is a perennial plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its zigzagging stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Asclepias sinuosa is a vital host plant for monarch butterfly larvae, making it important for pollinators and ecological balance.

Its latex sap contains toxic compounds that deter herbivores.
