Acacia lasiocarpa var. sedifolia

Acacia lasiocarpa var. sedifolia, commonly known as the woolly acacia, is a shrub native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, grey-green foliage with soft, woolly hairs covering the leaves. The plant produces yellow, spherical flower heads that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its root system is deep and drought-tolerant, allowing it to thrive in harsh, sandy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in degraded landscapes.
