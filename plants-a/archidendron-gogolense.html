Archidendron gogolense

Archidendron gogolense, commonly known as the Gogol's legume, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with alternating leaflets and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white or pale yellow. Its root system is capable of forming symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

The species is valued for its ecological role in maintaining soil health and supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
