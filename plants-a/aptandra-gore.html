Aptandra gore

Aptandra gore, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its nocturnal blooming habit, it is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
