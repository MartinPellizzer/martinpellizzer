Agapetes oblonga

Agapetes oblonga, commonly known as the oblong mountain heather, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, arranged in whorls around the stems. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

Agapetes oblonga thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in cloud forests at high elevations.
