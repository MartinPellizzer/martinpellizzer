Allium curtum subsp. typicum

Allium curtum subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the typical bulb onion, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical bulbs that are typically white or pale yellow in color.

The plant produces clusters of small, grass-like leaves and has purple or pink flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

This subspecies is valued both for its culinary uses and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
