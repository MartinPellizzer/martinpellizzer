Achillea lanulosa var. alpicola

Achillea lanulosa var. alpicola, commonly known as the alpine yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves and flat, composite flower heads that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine meadows and subalpine zones. It is adapted to cold climates and can withstand harsh winter conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
