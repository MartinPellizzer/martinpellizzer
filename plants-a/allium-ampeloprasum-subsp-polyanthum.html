Allium ampeloprasum subsp. polyanthum

Allium ampeloprasum subsp.

polyanthum, commonly known as the purple flowering onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of lavender to purple flowers.

The leaves are grass-like, narrow, and slightly hairy, growing from a bulbous base. This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.
