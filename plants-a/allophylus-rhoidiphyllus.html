Allophylus rhoidiphyllus

Allophylus rhoidiphyllus, commonly known as the rock almond, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in dry, sandy soils. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Allophylus rhoidiphyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
