Aster angustus

Aster angustus, commonly known as narrowleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, blooming in the late summer and early fall.

Aster angustus is often used in native plant gardens due to its ecological value and ornamental appeal.
