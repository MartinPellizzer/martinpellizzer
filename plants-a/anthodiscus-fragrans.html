Anthodiscus fragrans

Anthodiscus fragrans, commonly known as the sweet-scented daisy, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture.

Anthodiscus fragrans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant aroma.
