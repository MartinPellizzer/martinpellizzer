Anthericum pendulum

Anthericum pendulum, commonly known as the hanging adder's mouth, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive reddish or purple hue. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that hang downward, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Anthericum pendulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering display.
