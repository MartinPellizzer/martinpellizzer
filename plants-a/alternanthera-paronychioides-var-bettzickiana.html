Alternanthera paronychioides var. bettzickiana

Alternanthera paronychioides var. bettzickiana, commonly known as the red velvet plant, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to purple foliage, which becomes more intense under bright light conditions. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring warm, sunny environments with consistent moisture.

This variety is popular in ornamental water gardens and aquariums due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
