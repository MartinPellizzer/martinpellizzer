Alternanthera philoxeroides var. lancifolia

Alternanthera philoxeroides var. lancifolia, commonly known as the alligator weed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color, especially under full sun. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It is known for its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, making it a problematic species in many aquatic and wetland environments.

Its adaptability to various water conditions contributes to its success as an invasive weed.
