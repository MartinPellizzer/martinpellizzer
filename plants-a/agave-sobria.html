Agave sobria

Agave sobria, commonly known as the Mexican weeping agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-gray in color with a waxy coating. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in the spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Agave sobria is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
