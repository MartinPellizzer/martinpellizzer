Arisaema purpureogeleatum

Arisaema purpureogeleatum, commonly known as the purple-veined arisaema, is a striking member of the Araceae family native to China.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent purple veins and a distinctive, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant produces a unique corm that serves as its underground storage organ. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture, though it requires careful cultivation due to its specific environmental needs.
