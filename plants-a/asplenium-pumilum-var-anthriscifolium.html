Asplenium pumilum var. anthriscifolium

Asplenium pumilum var. anthriscifolium, commonly known as the small spleenwort with parsley-like leaves, is a fern species characterized by its delicate, finely divided foliage.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rock crevices. The plant's leaves are elongated and have a feathery appearance, resembling the leaves of parsley, hence its common name. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Asplenium pumilum var. anthriscifolium is a low-growing plant that spreads by rhizomes, forming dense mats in suitable habitats.
