Astragalus eucosmus f. leucocarpus

Astragalus eucosmus f. leucocarpus, commonly known as the white-horned milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds, and its root system is adapted to survive in dry conditions.

It is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
