Aerides siamensis

Aerides siamensis, commonly known as the Siam orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces upright, branched inflorescences bearing fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its leaves are broad, strap-like, and arranged in a fan shape.

It prefers shady, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.
