Alchemilla vorotnikovii

Alchemilla vorotnikovii, commonly known as the Vorotnikov's lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Alchemilla vorotnikovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
