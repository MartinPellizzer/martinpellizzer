Achillea × serratifolia

Achillea × serratifolia, commonly known as the Sawleaf yarrow, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely serrated, dark green leaves and flat, composite flower heads that range from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in full sun to partial shade, preferring well-drained soils.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract beneficial insects like butterflies and bees.
