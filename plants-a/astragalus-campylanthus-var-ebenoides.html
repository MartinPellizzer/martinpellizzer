Astragalus campylanthus var. ebenoides

Astragalus campylanthus var. ebenoides, commonly known as the Sierra foothill milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces yellow to reddish-purple flowers in early summer. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
