Aristida purpurea var. aequiramea

Aristida purpurea var. aequiramea, commonly known as purple threeawn, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and dense clusters of spikelets that are typically purplish in color. The plant produces small, spikelet-like flowers that are wind-pollinated, and its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is often found in association with other native grasses and is an important component of local vegetation.
