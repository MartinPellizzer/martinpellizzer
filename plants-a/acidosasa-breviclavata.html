Acidosasa breviclavata

Acidosasa breviclavata, commonly known as the narrow-clawed acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant has small, serrated leaflets and produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water absorption.

Its ecological role includes soil fixation and providing habitat for various insects and animals.
