Anacamptis laxiflora subsp. elegans

Anacamptis laxiflora subsp.

elegans, commonly known as the elegant orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers.

The flowers are typically pale green to yellowish with a distinctive purple or violet lip, and they bloom from late spring to early summer. This subspecies thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands, meadows, and along riverbanks.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
