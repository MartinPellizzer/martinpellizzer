Astragalus nicolaii var. carinipilosus

Astragalus nicolaii var. carinipilosus, commonly known as the Carinipilosus milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive hairy texture, and its flowers are typically pale yellow to cream-colored, arranged in dense clusters. The plant produces small, hard pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine or steppe environments.

This variant is notable for its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native range.
