Aira neutralis

Aira neutralis, commonly known as the slender false oat, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and it is often found in disturbed soils.

Aira neutralis is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil.
