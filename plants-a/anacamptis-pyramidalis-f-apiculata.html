Anacamptis pyramidalis f. apiculata

Anacamptis pyramidalis f. apiculata, commonly known as the pyramidal orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem bearing a dense spike of violet or purple flowers. The inflorescence is typically pyramidal in shape, giving the plant its common name. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a basal rosette.

This orchid thrives in sunny, well-drained habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
