Arundinaria spathacea

Arundinaria spathacea, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 6 meters in height, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green. The plant produces panicles of small, golden-yellow flowers, which are typically clustered near the top of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Arundinaria spathacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
