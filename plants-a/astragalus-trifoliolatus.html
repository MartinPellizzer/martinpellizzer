Astragalus trifoliolatus

Astragalus trifoliolatus, commonly known as the three-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in early summer. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Astragalus trifoliolatus is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
