Acacia caffra var. campylacantha

Acacia caffra var. campylacantha, commonly known as the umbrella thorn acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-shaped crown formed by its long, drooping branches. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of true leaves. It produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fodder.
