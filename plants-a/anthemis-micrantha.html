Anthemis micrantha

Anthemis micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often arranged in flat-topped clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Anthemis micrantha is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
