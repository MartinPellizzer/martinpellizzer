Ailanthus altissima var. erythrocarpa

Ailanthus altissima var. erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited tree of heaven, is a variant of the tree of heaven, native to China.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged with 12 to 30 leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by distinctive red or orange berries that contain seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is highly adaptable to different environmental conditions.

Its rapid growth and ability to tolerate pollution make it a common invasive species in many regions.
