Allium hierosolymorum

Allium hierosolymorum, commonly known as the Jerusalem onion or black garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of small, purple or pink flowers atop tall, slender stems. The plant's leaves are narrow and grass-like, and its bulbs are typically smaller than those of common onions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

The plant is valued both for its culinary uses and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
