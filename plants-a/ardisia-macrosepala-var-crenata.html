Ardisia macrosepala var. crenata

Ardisia macrosepala var. crenata, commonly known as the glossy evergreen shrub, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly wavy margin. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by bright red berries that are edible for humans but toxic to some animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses around the world.
