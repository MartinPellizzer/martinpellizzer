Appendicula calcarata

Appendicula calcarata, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a small, perennial herbaceous plant native to the rocky habitats of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments, often on limestone or rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are usually purple or violet in color.

Appendicula calcarata is adapted to survive in nutrient-poor conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species.
