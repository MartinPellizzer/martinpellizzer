Aridarum nicolsonii

Aridarum nicolsonii, commonly known as the Nicholson's arid orchid, is a rare and specialized plant adapted to arid environments.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in regions with low rainfall. Its root system is highly developed to maximize water absorption from the dry substrate.

Due to its limited geographic distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
