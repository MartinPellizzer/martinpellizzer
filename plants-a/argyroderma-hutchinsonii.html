Argyroderma hutchinsonii

Argyroderma hutchinsonii, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, woolly leaves and bright yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. It has a taproot system and produces seeds with parachute-like structures for wind dispersal.

Its botanical features make it an attractive plant for ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
