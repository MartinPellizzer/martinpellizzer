Aeonium × holospathulatum

Aeonium × holospathulatum, commonly known as the "Crimson Crown," is a hybrid succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped growth pattern and vibrant, fleshy leaves that range from deep red to green. The plant produces striking, tall flower stalks with yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance, it is widely cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
