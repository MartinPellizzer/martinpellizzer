Anacardium microsepalum

Anacardium microsepalum, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by red, fleshy berries. Its fruits are edible but contain a toxic seed, and the plant is known for its aromatic foliage and ornamental value.

Anacardium microsepalum is also valued for its potential medicinal properties.
