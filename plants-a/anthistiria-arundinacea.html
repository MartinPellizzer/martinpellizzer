Anthistiria arundinacea

Anthistiria arundinacea, commonly known as reed canary grass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland and marshy environments.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, greenish-brown flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils.

Anthistiria arundinacea plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
