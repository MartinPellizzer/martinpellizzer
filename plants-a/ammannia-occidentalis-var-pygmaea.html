Ammannia occidentalis var. pygmaea

Ammannia occidentalis var. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf ammannia, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces clusters of pinkish-red flowers in the summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to water garden landscapes.
