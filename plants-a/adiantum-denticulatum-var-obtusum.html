Adiantum denticulatum var. obtusum

Adiantum denticulatum var. obtusum, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species characterized by its finely divided, arching fronds.

It features dark green, glossy leaves with a soft, feathery appearance, and the fronds are typically broader and more rounded compared to the typical variety. The plant produces small, pale spore cases on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in sori. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
