Anthemis Peregrina Subsp. Heracleotica

Anthemis peregrina subsp. heracleotica is a rare and intriguing plant species found in specific regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, known for its diverse and often showy flower structures. This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its limited distribution makes it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists. Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it faces increasing threats to its survival.
