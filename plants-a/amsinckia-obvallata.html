Amsinckia obvallata

Amsinckia obvallata, commonly known as the broadleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of large, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water absorption. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

Its distinctive appearance and ecological adaptations make it a notable species in its native habitats.
