Acanthopanax sciadophylloides

Acanthopanax sciadophylloides, commonly known as the Chinese devil's ivy, is a climbing shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and have a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
