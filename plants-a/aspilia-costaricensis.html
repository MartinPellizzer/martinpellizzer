Aspilia costaricensis

Aspilia costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican torch flower, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular florets arranged in a composite flower head.

Aspilia costaricensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
