Astragalus hedgeanus

Astragalus hedgeanus, commonly known as the hedge vetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Astragalus hedgeanus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
