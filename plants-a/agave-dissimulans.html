Agave dissimulans

Agave dissimulans, commonly known as the "Blue Agave," is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy, blue-gray leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in drought conditions. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that bears small, white flowers, which are typically short-lived. It is a slow-growing species, often used in landscaping for its striking appearance and drought tolerance.

Agave dissimulans is also cultivated for its potential in biofuel production due to its high biomass yield.
