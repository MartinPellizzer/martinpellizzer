Anathallis concinna

Anathallis concinna, commonly known as the slender spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, spider-like appearance, with elongated petals and a slender, erect stem. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Anathallis concinna is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its unique morphology and ecological role.
