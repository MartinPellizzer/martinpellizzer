Abies homolepis var. scottae

Abies homolepis var. scottae, commonly known as the Scott's fir, is a variant of the Japanese fir.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan, characterized by its conical shape and dense foliage. The leaves are needle-like, typically dark green with a slight blue or silvery tinge on the underside. It produces cones that mature in the second year, releasing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
