Agave seemanniana var. perscabra

Agave seemanniana var. perscabra, commonly known as the Pescabra agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often lined with spines. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence bearing yellowish-green flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid gardens.
