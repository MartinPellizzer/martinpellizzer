What To Know About Medicinal Capsicum Annuum Cream?

Capsicum annuum cream, also known as sweet pepper cream, is a culinary sauce made from roasted capsicum annuum peppers blended with cream and often seasoned with garlic or herbs. This rich and flavorful condiment is commonly used in various cuisines, particularly in Italian and Mediterranean cooking, to add depth and heat to dishes. Capsicum annuum cream can be used as a dip, a sauce for pasta or meat, or as a topping for vegetables or bread.

What are the medicinal uses of Capsicum annuum cream?

Capsicum annuum cream is used to alleviate pain and inflammation due to its capsaicin content, which has analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties.

It has been traditionally used to treat various conditions, including arthritis, muscle soreness, and minor wounds.

Additionally, capsaicin has been studied for its potential in managing chronic pain conditions and other neurological disorders.

How to make Capsicum annuum cream for medicinal use?

To make capsicum annuum cream for medicinal use, first, you need to extract the capsaicin oil from the capsicum annuum peppers, which can be done by soaking them in a carrier oil like coconut oil.

Then, mix the extracted capsaicin oil with a moisturizing base, such as shea butter or coconut oil, to create a smooth and consistent cream.
