oils

achillea millefolium

Achillea millefolium oil, also known as yarrow oil, is a plant-based essential oil extracted from the leaves and flowers of the yarrow plant. This oil has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, making it beneficial for wound healing, skin irritations, and soothing minor cuts and scrapes. The oil's antimicrobial properties also make it a popular choice for aromatherapy and skincare products, promoting a healthy and balanced complexion.

achillea millefolium oil

acorus calamus

Acorus calamus oil, also known as calamus oil, is derived from the rhizomes of the sweet flag plant, Acorus calamus. This oil has been traditionally used in aromatherapy to promote relaxation, reduce anxiety, and alleviate stress, as well as to treat digestive issues and skin conditions. However, its use is not recommended for pregnant or breastfeeding women due to potential risks associated with its volatile compounds.

acorus calamus oil

aesculus hippocastanum

Aesculus hippocastanum oil, also known as horse chestnut oil, is derived from the seeds of the Aesculus hippocastanum tree and is rich in flavonoids and saponins. This oil has been traditionally used to support vein health and reduce inflammation, making it a popular ingredient in topical creams and ointments. Studies have shown that aesculus hippocastanum oil may also have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, contributing to its potential benefits for skin health and wound healing.

aesculus hippocastanum oil

allium sativum

Allium sativum oil, also known as garlic oil, is a concentrated liquid extract derived from the bulb of the garlic plant. It is rich in bioactive compounds, including allicin, which is responsible for its pungent aroma and potential health benefits. Allium sativum oil has been traditionally used in various cuisines and has gained attention for its antimicrobial, antifungal, and antioxidant properties, although its efficacy and safety need further scientific research.

allium sativum oil

aloe vera

Aloe vera oil is a natural and versatile product derived from the aloe vera plant, known for its numerous benefits to the skin and hair. Rich in vitamins and minerals, aloe vera oil helps to soothe and calm irritated skin, reducing inflammation and promoting healing. It can also be used to nourish and moisturize the hair, leaving it soft, silky, and full of shine.

aloe vera oil

alpinia officinarum

Alpinia officinarum oil, also known as galangal oil, is an essential oil extracted from the rhizome of the Alpinia officinarum plant, commonly used in traditional medicine. It has been traditionally used to alleviate digestive issues and is also valued for its antimicrobial properties. Research has also explored its potential in reducing inflammation and improving respiratory health.

alpinia officinarum oil

ammi visnaga

Ammi visnaga oil, also known as false parsley oil, is a fragrant essential oil extracted from the seeds of the Ammi visnaga plant, native to the Mediterranean region. Traditionally used in perfumery and aromatherapy, the oil is prized for its sweet, anise-like aroma and is often used to promote relaxation and reduce stress. However, ammi visnaga oil can be quite potent and should be used with caution, as it may cause skin irritation or allergic reactions in some individuals.

ammi visnaga oil

angelica sinensis

Angelica sinensis oil, extracted from the roots of the Chinese angelica plant, is a traditional herbal remedy used in East Asian medicine to promote circulation and alleviate menstrual cramps. The oil is rich in antioxidants and has anti-inflammatory properties, which contribute to its therapeutic benefits. In modern skincare, angelica sinensis oil is also valued for its ability to soothe and calm irritated skin, reducing redness and promoting a more even skin tone.

angelica sinensis oil

apium graveolens

Apium graveolens oil, also known as celery seed oil, is derived from the seeds of the Apium graveolens plant, a member of the parsley family. This oil has been traditionally used in aromatherapy to promote digestion, reduce inflammation, and alleviate stress due to its carminative and anti-inflammatory properties. When used in moderation, apium graveolens oil may also help to soothe the skin and relieve muscle tension.

apium graveolens oil

arctium lappa

Arctium lappa oil, also known as burdock oil, is derived from the roots of the burdock plant and is rich in antioxidants and fatty acids. This oil has been traditionally used in skincare to soothe and calm irritated skin, reduce inflammation, and promote wound healing. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties also make it a popular natural remedy for various skin issues, such as acne and eczema.

arctium lappa oil

artemisia absinthium

Artemisia absinthium oil, also known as wormwood oil, is a potent essential oil extracted from the leaves of the wormwood plant. This oil has been used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and antiviral properties, and is often used to treat digestive issues and skin conditions. However, due to its high thujone content, artemisia absinthium oil should be used with caution and in moderation, as excessive consumption can be toxic.

artemisia absinthium oil

artemisia annua

Artemisia annua oil, also known as sweet wormwood oil, is a highly prized essential oil extracted from the leaves of the artemisia annua plant. Traditionally used in traditional Chinese medicine, this oil is valued for its potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a popular natural remedy for various health issues. The oil is also known for its antimicrobial properties, which help to protect against infections and promote overall well-being.

artemisia annua oil

astragalus membranaceus

Astragalus membranaceus oil, extracted from the roots of the Astragalus plant, is a traditional Chinese medicinal oil believed to possess adaptogenic and anti-inflammatory properties. This oil is often used to promote immune system function, reduce stress and anxiety, and support overall well-being. Studies have also suggested potential benefits for cardiovascular health, including lowering blood pressure and improving lipid profiles, making astragalus oil a popular natural remedy in modern herbalism.

astragalus membranaceus oil

atropa belladonna

Atropa belladonna oil, also known as deadly nightshade oil, is an extremely potent and highly toxic extract derived from the Atropa belladonna plant. It has been used in traditional herbal medicine for centuries, but its use is highly regulated due to its potential for causing serious harm or even death if not handled carefully. Caution and expertise are essential when working with atropa belladonna oil, as even small amounts can be fatal if ingested, inhaled, or applied to the skin without proper precautions.

atropa belladonna oil

azadirachta indica

Azadirachta indica oil, also known as neem oil, is a natural oil extracted from the seeds of the neem tree. It has been used for centuries in traditional medicine and as a natural insecticide, due to its antimicrobial and insecticidal properties. Neem oil is often used in aromatherapy and skincare products for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant benefits.

azadirachta indica oil

barosma betulina

Barosma betulina oil, also known as Buchu oil, is an essential oil extracted from the leaves of the Barosma betulina plant native to South Africa. It is commonly used in traditional medicine to treat urinary tract infections, kidney stones, and other kidney-related issues due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The oil's unique aroma is also used in aromatherapy to promote relaxation and reduce stress.

barosma betulina oil

bupleurum chinense

Bupleurum chinense oil is derived from the roots of the Bupleurum chinense plant, a traditional Chinese medicinal herb used to treat various health conditions. This oil has been found to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may help alleviate stress, anxiety, and improve overall well-being. Its potential health benefits also include reducing inflammation and improving skin health, making it a popular choice in aromatherapy and natural remedies.

bupleurum chinense oil
