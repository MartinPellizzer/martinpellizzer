allium sativum gum

intro

There is limited information available about Allium sativum gum, but it is believed to be a possible byproduct of the processing of garlic (Allium sativum), a plant commonly used in cooking and traditional medicine. The properties and uses of Allium sativum gum are not well-documented, and more research is needed to understand its potential benefits and applications. However, it is thought to have some antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, similar to other compounds found in garlic.
