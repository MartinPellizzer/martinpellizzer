What To Know About Medicinal Acorus Calamus Oil?

Acorus calamus oil, also known as sweet flag oil, is a calming and anti-inflammatory essential oil obtained from the rhizomes of the Acorus calamus plant. Traditionally used in Ayurvedic and traditional Chinese medicine, this oil is said to possess a range of health benefits, including reducing anxiety and stress, as well as alleviating digestive issues. When used in aromatherapy or applied topically, acorus calamus oil is believed to promote a sense of relaxation and well-being, while also possessing antimicrobial properties.

What are the medicinal uses of Acorus calamus oil?

Acorus calamus oil is used to treat digestive issues, such as nausea and indigestion, due to its carminative and anti-inflammatory properties.

It is also used to relieve anxiety and stress, promoting a sense of calm and relaxation.

Additionally, acorus calamus oil may help to alleviate symptoms of respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma.

How to make Acorus calamus oil for medicinal use?

To make acorus calamus oil for medicinal use, the roots of Acorus calamus, also known as sweet flag or calamus, must be harvested and dried.

The dried roots can be then subjected to a process of steam distillation to extract the essential oil.

The resulting oil can be filtered and stored in a dark container to preserve its medicinal properties.
