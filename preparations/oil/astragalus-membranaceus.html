What To Know About Medicinal Astragalus Membranaceus Oil?

Astragalus membranaceus oil, also known as Huang Qi oil, is derived from the roots of the astragalus plant, a traditional Chinese medicinal herb. This oil is rich in sesquiterpenes and triterpenoids, which have been shown to possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Astragalus membranaceus oil is commonly used in aromatherapy to promote relaxation, reduce stress, and support immune function.

What are the medicinal uses of Astragalus membranaceus oil?

Astragalus membranaceus oil is used to alleviate stress and anxiety due to its adaptogenic properties, which help the body adapt to physical and emotional stress.

It is also believed to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, which may help to boost the immune system and reduce inflammation.

Additionally, astragalus membranaceus oil may be used to support cardiovascular health by lowering blood pressure and improving circulation.

How to make Astragalus membranaceus oil for medicinal use?

To make astragalus membranaceus oil for medicinal use, start by drying the astragalus membranaceus root, then grind it into a fine powder using a coffee grinder or mortar and pestle.

Next, combine the powdered root with a carrier oil such as coconut or olive oil in a ratio of approximately 1:10 (root to oil), and place the mixture in a double boiler or a heat-proof glass container set over a pot of simmering water.

Allow the mixture to steep and infuse for several hours, then strain the oil through a cheesecloth or coffee filter and discard the solids.
