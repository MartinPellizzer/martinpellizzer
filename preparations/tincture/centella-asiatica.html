What To Know About Medicinal Centella Asiatica Tincture?

Centella asiatica tincture, also known as Gotu Kola, is a herbal remedy derived from the leaves and stems of the Centella asiatica plant, traditionally used in Ayurvedic and traditional Chinese medicine. This tincture is believed to possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may help to promote skin health, improve cognitive function, and support the immune system. When taken orally, centella asiatica tincture is thought to have a range of potential benefits, including enhanced wound healing and reduced stress levels.

Centella asiatica tincture is used to promote wound healing and skin regeneration due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

It is also believed to improve cognitive function and memory, while reducing stress and anxiety.

Additionally, it may help to improve circulation and reduce inflammation in the body.

How to make Centella asiatica tincture for medicinal use?

To make centella asiatica tincture for medicinal use, you will need to steep dried or fresh centella asiatica leaves in a neutral carrier oil such as jojoba or grapeseed oil for several weeks, shaking the mixture daily.

After the infusion process is complete, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean glass container, discarding the solids.

Store the tincture in a cool, dark place and use it according to the recommended dosage, typically 20-30 drops per serving.
