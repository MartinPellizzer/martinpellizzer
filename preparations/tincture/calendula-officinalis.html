What To Know About Medicinal Calendula Officinalis Tincture?

Calendula officinalis tincture is a natural remedy derived from the flowers of the marigold plant, known for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. When used topically, it can help soothe and calm irritated skin, wounds, and burns, promoting faster healing and reducing the risk of infection. Calendula tincture is often used in herbal medicine to treat minor cuts and scrapes, as well as to support skin health and reduce inflammation.

What are the medicinal uses of Calendula officinalis tincture?

Calendula officinalis tincture is used to soothe skin irritations, reduce inflammation, and promote wound healing due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

It is often used topically to treat minor cuts, burns, and eczema.

Additionally, calendula officinalis tincture may also be used to calm digestive issues and alleviate menstrual cramps when taken internally.

What are the therapeutic properties of Calendula officinalis tincture?

Calendula officinalis tincture has therapeutic properties such as anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, which can help soothe skin irritations, wounds, and minor burns.

It may also aid in promoting wound healing, reducing redness and swelling, and calming irritated skin.

How to make Calendula officinalis tincture for medicinal use?

To make calendula officinalis tincture for medicinal use, first, harvest fresh calendula flowers and dry them thoroughly to preserve their potency.

Next, combine the dried flowers with a solvent, such as 80% vodka or glycerin, in a ratio of approximately 1 part flowers to 2 parts solvent, and let it steep in a dark place for 2-6 weeks, shaking the mixture regularly.

After the steeping period, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter, discard the solids, and store the resulting tincture in a dark glass bottle to preserve its medicinal properties.

What are the health side effects of Calendula officinalis tincture?

Calendula officinalis tincture can have side effects such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, or interactions with certain medications, particularly blood thinners and diabetes medications.

However, it is essential to note that these side effects are usually rare and temporary.

It is crucial to consult with a healthcare professional before using calendula officinalis tincture, especially if you have any underlying medical conditions or are taking medications.
