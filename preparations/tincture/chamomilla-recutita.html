What To Know About Medicinal Chamomilla Recutita Tincture?

Chamomilla recutita, also known as German chamomile, is a popular herbal remedy used to promote relaxation and reduce anxiety. The tincture made from this plant is often used to soothe digestive issues, such as upset stomachs and irritable bowel syndrome. When taken internally, chamomilla recutita tincture can help to calm the mind and body, promoting a restful night's sleep and overall sense of well-being.

How to make Chamomilla recutita tincture for medicinal use?

To make chamomilla recutita tincture for medicinal use, start by harvesting or obtaining high-quality chamomile flowers, then dry them thoroughly to preserve their potency.

Combine the dried flowers with a neutral-tasting spirit, such as vodka or glycerin, at a ratio of 1 part flowers to 2-3 parts solvent, and allow the mixture to steep for 2-6 weeks, shaking occasionally.

After the steeping period, strain the liquid and discard the solids, and store the resulting tincture in a dark glass bottle, out of direct sunlight.

Individuals with allergies or sensitivities should monitor their response to this tincture and discontinue use if adverse reactions occur.
