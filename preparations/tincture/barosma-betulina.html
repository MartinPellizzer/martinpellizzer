What To Know About Medicinal Barosma Betulina Tincture?

Barosma betulina, also known as Buchu, is a plant-based tincture traditionally used for its purported medicinal properties. The tincture is often claimed to have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, which may help alleviate urinary tract issues, kidney problems, and skin conditions. However, its effectiveness and safety as a treatment for these conditions have not been extensively scientifically proven, and more research is needed to confirm its benefits.

How to make Barosma betulina tincture for medicinal use?

To make barosma betulina tincture for medicinal use, start by harvesting fresh or dried leaves of the plant, then chop them into small pieces.

Combine the chopped leaves with a solvent like vodka or glycerin in a ratio of 1 part plant material to 2-3 parts solvent, and allow the mixture to steep for 2-6 weeks in a cool, dark place.

Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter and discard the solids, then store the resulting tincture in a dark glass bottle, out of direct sunlight.
