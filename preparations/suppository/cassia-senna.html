What To Know About Medicinal Cassia Senna Suppository?

Cassia senna suppositories are used to treat constipation by stimulating bowel movements and relieving symptoms such as bloating and discomfort. The suppositories contain the extract of the senna plant, which has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to promote digestive health. When inserted rectally, the active compounds in the suppository are absorbed directly into the bloodstream, providing rapid relief from constipation.

What are the medicinal uses of Cassia senna suppository?

Cassia senna suppository is used to treat constipation by stimulating bowel movements and promoting the elimination of waste.

It works by irritating the rectal mucosa, which triggers a reflex action to move stool through the intestines.

This can be beneficial for individuals with chronic constipation or those who have difficulty passing stool due to various medical conditions.

How to make Cassia senna suppository for medicinal use?

To make cassia senna suppository for medicinal use, first, you'll need to prepare a mixture of cassia senna powder and a base material, such as cocoa butter or beeswax, in a specific ratio, usually 1-2% cassia senna powder to 98-99% base material.

Next, heat the mixture until the base material is fully melted and then cool it until it solidifies, creating a moldable mixture.

Once the mixture has cooled, you can shape it into suppository form and store it in an airtight container for later use.

What are the health side effects of Cassia senna suppository?

However, these side effects are usually temporary and resolve on their own once the suppository is fully digested.

In rare cases, frequent or prolonged use of cassia senna suppositories may lead to electrolyte imbalance and dehydration.
