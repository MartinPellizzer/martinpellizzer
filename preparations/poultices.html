achillea millefolium

Achillea millefolium, also known as yarrow, has been used for centuries to create a soothing poultice for wounds and injuries. The leaves and flowers of this plant are typically combined with a carrier oil, such as coconut or olive oil, to create a paste that can be applied directly to the affected area. When used as a poultice, achillea millefolium is said to promote healing, reduce inflammation, and prevent infection, making it a valuable remedy for minor cuts and scrapes.
