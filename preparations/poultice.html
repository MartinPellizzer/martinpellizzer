achillea millefolium

Achillea millefolium, also known as yarrow, has been used for centuries as a natural remedy to aid in wound healing and reduce inflammation. A poultice made from the leaves and flowers of this plant can be applied directly to the affected area to promote the drawing out of pus and debris, while also soothing pain and discomfort. The anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties of yarrow poultice can help to accelerate the healing process and reduce the risk of infection.

arctium lappa

Arctium lappa, also known as burdock, has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to create a poultice for various health issues. The root of the plant is typically dried and then pounded into a paste, which can be applied directly to the skin to treat conditions such as eczema, acne, and wounds. The anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties of the burdock poultice may help to reduce swelling, promote healing, and soothe irritated skin.
