acorus calamus

Acorus calamus powder, also known as calamus powder, is derived from the root of the Acorus calamus plant, a perennial herb native to Asia and Europe. Traditionally used in Ayurvedic and Unani medicine, the powder is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. When used in moderation, calamus powder may also be used as a natural remedy to promote digestion, reduce stress, and improve cognitive function.

aloe vera

Aloe vera powder is a versatile and convenient alternative to traditional aloe vera gel, offering a shelf-stable and easily digestible way to harness the benefits of this popular skincare ingredient. Rich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants, aloe vera powder can be added to smoothies, face masks, and other topical treatments to promote skin health and hydration. Additionally, aloe vera powder is also said to have anti-inflammatory properties, making it a popular choice for soothing sunburns and irritated skin.

aloe vera powder

alpinia officinarum

Alpinia officinarum powder, also known as Galangal powder, is a spice made from the dried and ground rhizome of the Alpinia officinarum plant. Native to Southeast Asia, this powder has been used for centuries in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in various dishes, particularly in Thai and Indonesian cuisine. It is said to possess antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a popular ingredient in health supplements and natural remedies.

alpinia officinarum powder

ammi visnaga

Ammi visnaga powder, also known as sea ash or ashwagandha, is a natural ingredient derived from the Ammi visnaga plant. Traditionally used in herbal medicine, the powder is believed to possess anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, making it a popular ingredient in skincare products and wound treatments. When used in a skincare routine, ammi visnaga powder is thought to help soothe and calm irritated skin, reducing redness and promoting a more even skin tone.

ammi visnaga powder

angelica sinensis

Angelica sinensis powder, also known as dong quai, is a traditional Chinese herbal remedy derived from the roots of the Angelica sinensis plant. This powder has been used in Chinese medicine for centuries to promote women's health, reduce menstrual cramps and other symptoms associated with PMS, and improve circulation. When consumed as a supplement, angelica sinensis powder is believed to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that may help to promote overall well-being and reduce the risk of certain diseases.

angelica sinensis powder

apium graveolens

Apium graveolens, also known as celery seed powder, is a natural ingredient derived from the seeds of the celery plant. It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, as well as its ability to support digestive health. Celery seed powder may also have beneficial effects on blood pressure and joint health, although more research is needed to confirm its efficacy.

apium graveolens powder

arctium lappa

Arctium lappa powder, also known as burdock root powder, is derived from the roots of the Arctium lappa plant, a perennial herb native to Europe and Asia. The powder is rich in fiber, vitamins, and minerals, and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support digestive health and immune function. When consumed as a dietary supplement, arctium lappa powder may help to promote detoxification, reduce inflammation, and alleviate symptoms associated with digestive issues.

arctium lappa powder
