achillea millefolium poultice

intro

Achillea millefolium, also known as yarrow, has been used for centuries to create a soothing poultice for wounds and skin irritations. When made into a poultice, the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties can help to reduce swelling, prevent infection, and promote healing. To make a yarrow poultice, simply steep fresh yarrow leaves in hot water, then apply the cooled mixture to the affected area and cover with a cloth or bandage.
