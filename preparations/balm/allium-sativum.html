What To Know About Medicinal Allium Sativum Balm?

There isn't much information about Allium sativum balm. However, Allium sativum is known as garlic and has been used in various forms, including balms and topical creams. Garlic balm is sometimes made with Allium sativum and other ingredients like beeswax and essential oils, but its exact composition and uses can vary depending on the product and its intended application.

What are the medicinal uses of Allium sativum balm?

Allium sativum, commonly known as garlic, is often used in the form of a balm for its medicinal properties.

Allium sativum balm is used to treat skin conditions such as acne, wounds, and fungal infections due to its antiseptic and antibacterial properties.

Additionally, it may also help to reduce inflammation and promote wound healing.

How to make Allium sativum balm for medicinal use?

To make allium sativum balm for medicinal use, combine 1/2 cup of infused garlic oil (made by infusing Allium sativum in a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil) with 2 tablespoons of beeswax and 2 tablespoons of aloe vera gel in a double boiler, stirring until the beeswax has melted and the mixture has cooled slightly.

Add 5-7 drops of a calming essential oil like lavender or chamomile to promote relaxation and reduce inflammation.

Pour the balm into a container and let it cool completely before using it to soothe skin irritations and promote wound healing.
