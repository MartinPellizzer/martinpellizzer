What To Know About Medicinal Acorus Calamus Balm?

Acorus calamus balm, derived from the root of the sweet flag plant, has been traditionally used to soothe digestive issues and reduce inflammation. Its antispasmodic properties can help alleviate cramps and spasms, making it a popular natural remedy for menstrual cramps and other gastrointestinal issues. When applied topically, the balm can also provide relief from skin irritations and minor wounds, promoting a speedy recovery.

How to make Acorus calamus balm for medicinal use?

To make Acorus calamus balm for medicinal use, start by infusing the dried roots of the plant in a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil for a period of 2-4 weeks to extract its medicinal properties.

Strain the infused oil and mix it with beeswax and a sweetener like honey or maple syrup to create a smooth, balmy texture.

Apply this balm topically to soothe digestive issues, reduce inflammation, and promote relaxation.
