What To Know About Medicinal Azadirachta Indica Balm?

Azadirachta indica balm, also known as neem balm, is a topical application made from the extracted oil of the neem tree (Azadirachta indica). This balm is often used to treat various skin issues, such as acne, eczema, and wounds, due to its antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and antibacterial properties. Additionally, neem balm is also known to have antifungal properties, making it effective in treating fungal infections and promoting overall skin health.

How to make Azadirachta indica balm for medicinal use?

To make azadirachta indica balm for medicinal use, first, infuse 1 part of dried Azadirachta indica leaves in 5 parts of carrier oil, such as coconut or olive oil, in a double boiler for 2-3 hours.

Then, strain the mixture and combine it with 2-3 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 1 teaspoon of vitamin E oil.

Finally, heat the mixture in a double boiler until the beeswax melts and the balm reaches a smooth, consistent texture, allowing it to cool before storing in airtight containers.

What are the health side effects of Azadirachta indica balm?

Azadirachta indica balm can have side effects such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and potential interactions with medications, especially blood thinners and diabetes medications.

Some users may experience digestive issues or dizziness after applying the balm.

However, individual reactions can vary greatly depending on the concentration and individual tolerance to the balm's active ingredients.
