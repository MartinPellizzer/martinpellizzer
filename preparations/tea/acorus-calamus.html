What To Know About Medicinal Acorus Calamus Tea?

Acorus calamus, also known as sweet flag or calamus, has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to promote relaxation and alleviate digestive issues. The roots of the Acorus calamus plant are commonly used to make a soothing tea, which is said to possess anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce anxiety and improve sleep quality. When consumed in moderation, Acorus calamus tea may also offer a calming effect on the nervous system, making it a popular herbal remedy for stress relief.

What are the medicinal uses of Acorus calamus tea?

Acorus calamus tea is used to aid digestion and alleviate symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome, while its anti-inflammatory properties may help soothe digestive issues and reduce inflammation.

Additionally, the tea is believed to have antimicrobial and antifungal properties, which can help combat infections and promote overall health.

Some traditional practitioners also recommend acorus calamus tea for its potential to reduce stress and promote a restful night's sleep.

How to make Acorus calamus tea for medicinal use?

To make acorus calamus tea for medicinal use, start by adding 1 teaspoon of dried Acorus calamus root to a cup of boiling water, then let it steep for 5-10 minutes.

Next, strain the tea and discard the solids, then add honey or lemon to taste if desired.

Finally, consume the tea as needed for its potential medicinal properties, such as reducing anxiety and promoting digestive health.
