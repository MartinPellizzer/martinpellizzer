achillea millefolium

Achillea millefolium, also known as yarrow tea, is a herbal infusion made from the leaves and flowers of the yarrow plant. This tea has been used for centuries in traditional medicine, particularly for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which can aid in digestion, reduce fever, and support the immune system. Drinking yarrow tea may also promote relaxation, improve sleep quality, and act as a natural antacid, making it a popular choice for those seeking a natural remedy.

allium sativum

Allium sativum tea, also known as garlic tea, has been used for centuries in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including lowering cholesterol and blood pressure. The tea is made by steeping garlic cloves in hot water, and it's believed to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. However, consuming garlic tea in large quantities or without proper precautions can be problematic due to its strong flavor and potential interactions with certain medications.

angelica sinensis

Angelica sinensis tea, also known as dong quai, is a traditional Chinese herbal remedy that has been used for centuries to promote women's health and well-being. Made from the dried root of the Angelica sinensis plant, this tea is said to possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help to alleviate menstrual cramps, reduce stress, and improve overall vitality. Regular consumption of angelica sinensis tea is believed to promote a healthy balance of hormones and support the body's natural healing processes.
