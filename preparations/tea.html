achillea millefolium

Achillea millefolium, also known as yarrow tea, is a herbal infusion made from the flowers and leaves of the yarrow plant. Rich in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds, this tea has been traditionally used to support digestion, reduce fever, and ease menstrual cramps. When consumed as a tea, yarrow is believed to promote relaxation, improve circulation, and boost the immune system.

allium sativum

Allium sativum, commonly known as garlic, has been used for centuries to brew a medicinal tea. This tea is made by infusing crushed garlic cloves in hot water, which is said to have various health benefits, including reducing inflammation and improving cardiovascular health. However, drinking garlic tea may not be suitable for everyone, particularly those with sensitive stomachs or certain health conditions, as it can cause digestive issues and interact with medications.

angelica sinensis

Angelica sinensis tea, also known as dong quai, is a traditional Chinese herbal tea made from the roots of the angelica plant. It has been used for centuries in traditional Chinese medicine to promote women's health and well-being, particularly in relation to menstrual and menopausal symptoms. The tea is believed to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may help to soothe and calm the body, promoting a sense of balance and harmony.

angelica sinensis tea

apium graveolens

Apium graveolens, also known as celery seed tea, is a herbal remedy that has been used for centuries to promote digestive health and alleviate symptoms of bloating and gas. The tea is made from the seeds of the plant and is said to have anti-inflammatory properties, making it a popular choice for those looking to soothe stomach issues naturally. However, it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before consuming apium graveolens tea, especially for those with pre-existing medical conditions or allergies.
