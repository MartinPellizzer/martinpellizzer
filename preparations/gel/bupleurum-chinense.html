What To Know About Medicinal Bupleurum Chinense Gel?

Bupleurum chinense gel, also known as Bupleurum gel or Chaihu gel, is a traditional herbal remedy derived from the Chinese thunder god vine plant. The gel is believed to have anti-inflammatory and immune-boosting properties, making it a potential treatment for various health conditions such as arthritis and skin issues. However, more research is needed to fully understand the efficacy and safety of Bupleurum chinense gel as a medicinal treatment.

What are the medicinal uses of Bupleurum chinense gel?

Bupleurum chinense gel is used to treat inflammation and skin conditions, such as eczema and acne, due to its anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties.

It may also help to soothe and calm irritated skin, reducing redness and promoting a more even skin tone.

Additionally, its gel form makes it easily applicable to affected areas, providing targeted relief and support for skin health.

How to make Bupleurum chinense gel for medicinal use?

To make bupleurum chinense gel for medicinal use, start by harvesting and cleaning the roots of Bupleurum chinense, then dry them to preserve their medicinal properties.

Next, macerate the dried roots in a carrier oil such as coconut or olive oil for several weeks to extract the bioactive compounds.

Finally, mix the oil extract with a gelling agent like agar or carrageenan and heat it gently to create a stable gel that can be stored and used as a topical remedy.
