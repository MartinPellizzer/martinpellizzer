What To Know About Medicinal Capsicum Annuum Gel?

Capsicum annuum, commonly known as sweet pepper, is a plant species that produces a gel-like substance as a defense mechanism when injured or attacked. This gel, also referred to as capsaicinoids, is a mixture of compounds that contain a chemical called capsaicin, which gives peppers their characteristic heat. The gel serves as a deterrent to herbivores and other predators, protecting the plant from damage and promoting its survival.

What are the medicinal uses of Capsicum annuum gel?

Capsicum annuum gel is used to treat various skin conditions, including pain, inflammation, and irritation due to its analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties.

It may also help reduce the severity of wounds, cuts, and burns by promoting faster healing and tissue repair.

Additionally, it has been studied for its potential in relieving minor aches and pains, such as muscle soreness and arthritis.

How to make Capsicum annuum gel for medicinal use?

To make capsicum annuum gel for medicinal use, start by infusing dried capsicum annuum peppers in a carrier oil such as coconut or olive oil for several weeks.

Next, strain the oil and mix it with a gel-forming agent like agar agar or carrageenan, then heat the mixture to dissolve the gel-forming agent.

Finally, allow the gel to cool and solidify before transferring it to an airtight container for storage.
