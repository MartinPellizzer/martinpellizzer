What To Know About Medicinal Centella Asiatica Gel?

Centella asiatica gel, also known as tiger grass or gotu kola, is a natural remedy derived from the leaves of the centella asiatica plant. This gel has been traditionally used to promote wound healing, reduce inflammation, and improve skin elasticity due to its high concentration of triterpenoid saponins and other bioactive compounds. When applied topically, centella asiatica gel is believed to enhance skin regeneration, reduce the appearance of fine lines and scars, and provide long-lasting hydration.

How to make Centella asiatica gel for medicinal use?

To make centella asiatica gel for medicinal use, start by harvesting the leaves and stems of the centella asiatica plant, then dry and clean them to remove any impurities.

Next, soak the dried centella asiatica in a carrier oil such as coconut or olive oil for several weeks to extract its medicinal properties.

Finally, mix the infused oil with a water-based gel medium, such as aloe vera gel or carrageenan, to create a soothing and medicinally active gel.
