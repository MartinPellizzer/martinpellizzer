syrups

allium sativum

Allium sativum syrup, also known as garlic syrup, is a sweet and flavorful liquid extract made from the bulb of the Allium sativum plant, commonly known as garlic. This syrup is often used in herbal medicine to provide potential health benefits, including reducing inflammation and improving cardiovascular health. Additionally, allium sativum syrup can be used as a flavoring agent in various culinary applications, adding a rich and savory taste to dishes and beverages.

alpinia officinarum

Alpinia officinarum, also known as lesser galangal, is a plant-based ingredient used to create a sweet and medicinal syrup. Traditionally, the syrup is made by infusing Alpinia officinarum rhizomes in a sugar syrup, which is then strained and bottled for consumption. Alpinia officinarum syrup is commonly used in herbal medicine to aid digestion and relieve nausea, although its use should be supervised by a healthcare professional due to potential interactions or side effects.

ammi visnaga

Ammi visnaga syrup, also known as sea lavender syrup, is a herbal remedy traditionally used to alleviate digestive issues and promote relaxation. It is often made from the flowers of the ammi visnaga plant, which are rich in coumarin and other bioactive compounds. When consumed as a syrup, ammi visnaga is said to help soothe stomach upset and promote a sense of calm, making it a popular natural remedy in traditional medicine.
