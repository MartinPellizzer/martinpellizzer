allium sativum

Allium sativum syrup, also known as garlic syrup, is a sweet and flavorful liquid extract made from the roots of garlic plants. It is commonly used in traditional medicine to provide antibacterial and antiviral properties, as well as to aid digestion and reduce inflammation. The syrup is often used as a dietary supplement or added to food and beverages to provide a boost of garlic's medicinal properties in a convenient and palatable form.

artemisia annua

Artemisia annua syrup, also known as sweet wormwood syrup, is a herbal remedy made from the leaves of the Artemisia annua plant. This syrup has been traditionally used to treat malaria, fever, and other infections due to its high concentration of artemisinin, a powerful natural compound. When consumed in moderation, artemisia annua syrup is often considered a safe and effective natural alternative to pharmaceutical treatments for certain health conditions, but its use should be guided by a healthcare professional.
