achillea millefolium

Achillea millefolium, also known as yarrow, is a popular herb used to create infused oil, which is often utilized in skincare and wound healing applications. This infused oil is rich in antioxidants and has anti-inflammatory properties, making it beneficial for soothing and calming irritated skin. When used topically, yarrow oil may help to reduce redness, promote tissue repair, and support the healing process of minor cuts and scrapes.

acorus calamus

Acorus calamus, also known as sweet flag, is a herb that has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to promote relaxation and alleviate stress. The essential oil extracted from its roots has a calming and earthy aroma, which can be used in aromatherapy to soothe the mind and body. When infused into a carrier oil, acorus calamus oil is believed to have anti-inflammatory properties and can be used topically to reduce pain and promote skin health.

aesculus hippocastanum

Aesculus hippocastanum, or horse chestnut, infused oil is a rich and soothing topical treatment often used to alleviate inflammation and discomfort associated with varicose veins and poor circulation. This herbal oil is typically made by infusing dried horse chestnut seeds in a carrier oil, such as jojoba or sweet almond oil, allowing the bioactive compounds to gently seep into the skin. When used topically, aesculus hippocastanum infused oil may help to reduce swelling and promote healthy blood flow, providing relief from tired and aching legs.
