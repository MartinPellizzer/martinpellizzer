What To Know About Medicinal Achillea Millefolium Infused Oil?

Achillea millefolium, also known as yarrow, is a natural herb that has been used for centuries to create a medicinal infused oil. The oil, typically made by steeping dried yarrow flowers in a carrier oil, is said to possess anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, making it a popular choice for wound care and skin issues. When applied topically, yarrow infused oil can help to soothe and calm irritated skin, reducing redness and promoting a faster healing process.

Achillea millefolium infused oil is used to reduce inflammation and promote wound healing due to its antimicrobial and antifungal properties.

How to make Achillea millefolium infused oil for medicinal use?

To make achillea millefolium infused oil for medicinal use, start by harvesting fresh or dried yarrow flowers (Achillea millefolium) and place them in a clean glass jar.

Pour a carrier oil, such as jojoba or sweet almond oil, over the flowers, making sure they are completely submerged, and seal the jar.

Allow the mixture to infuse in a cool, dark place for 2-6 weeks, shaking the jar occasionally, before straining and bottling the infused oil.

What are the health side effects of Achillea millefolium infused oil?

Achillea millefolium infused oil can have side effects such as gastrointestinal upset, allergic reactions, and interactions with certain medications, particularly blood thinners and diabetes medications.

As with any herbal remedy, it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using it, especially if you have pre-existing medical conditions or take prescription medications.

Additionally, pregnant or breastfeeding women should use it under the guidance of a qualified healthcare practitioner.
