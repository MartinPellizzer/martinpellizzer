What To Know About Medicinal Barosma Betulina Syrup?

Barosma betulina, also known as the Round-leaved Barosma, is a shrub native to South Africa. The syrup extracted from its leaves has been traditionally used in medicine and as a flavoring agent, particularly in the production of bitters and traditional remedies. The unique flavor profile of Barosma betulina syrup is often described as spicy, earthy, and slightly sweet, making it a prized ingredient in various applications.

What are the medicinal uses of Barosma betulina syrup?

Barosma betulina syrup is used to treat digestive issues and relieve symptoms of indigestion and nausea.

It has antimicrobial properties, which help in reducing inflammation and promoting wound healing.

Additionally, its expectorant properties can aid in relieving respiratory issues such as coughs and colds.

How to make Barosma betulina syrup for medicinal use?

To make barosma betulina syrup for medicinal use, first gather 500 grams of dried barosma betulina root and 1 liter of water, then boil the root in the water for 30 minutes to create a decoction.

Next, add 1 kg of sugar to the decoction and stir until the sugar is dissolved, then remove the syrup from heat.

Finally, filter the syrup and store it in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 6 months.
