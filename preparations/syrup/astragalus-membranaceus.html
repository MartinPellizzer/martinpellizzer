What To Know About Medicinal Astragalus Membranaceus Syrup?

Astragalus membranaceus syrup is a traditional herbal remedy made from the root of the Astragalus plant, often used in Chinese medicine to boost the immune system and reduce stress. This syrup is typically made by combining the dried root of Astragalus with other ingredients like honey and water, then boiling and filtering the mixture to create a sweet, viscous liquid. Astragalus membranaceus syrup is commonly consumed as a tonic to promote overall health and well-being, particularly during the winter months when the immune system may be more vulnerable to illness.

How to make Astragalus membranaceus syrup for medicinal use?

To make astragalus membranaceus syrup for medicinal use, combine dried astragalus membranaceus root with a sweetener like honey or glycerin, and a solvent such as water or glycerin in a saucepan.

Heat the mixture gently, stirring occasionally, until the astragalus is fully infused and the sweetener is dissolved.

Strain the syrup through a cheesecloth or fine-mesh sieve into a clean glass bottle, and store it in the refrigerator to maintain potency and shelf life.

What are the health side effects of Astragalus membranaceus syrup?

Astragalus membranaceus syrup can have side effects such as dizziness, diarrhea, and stomach upset in some individuals.

Additionally, high doses of astragalus may interact with certain medications, including diabetes medications and blood thinners.

It is also recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before using astragalus membranaceus syrup, especially for pregnant or breastfeeding women.
