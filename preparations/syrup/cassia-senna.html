What To Know About Medicinal Cassia Senna Syrup?

Cassia senna syrup, also known as senna tea syrup, is a herbal remedy made from the dried pods of the cassia senna plant. This syrup is used to promote bowel movements and relieve constipation due to its laxative properties, which work by stimulating the muscles in the intestines. Cassia senna syrup is often used as a natural alternative to over-the-counter laxatives and is typically taken in small doses before bedtime to allow for a gentle, overnight effect.

What are the medicinal uses of Cassia senna syrup?

Cassia senna syrup is used to aid in digestion and relieve constipation due to its laxative properties.

It may also help reduce bloating and alleviate symptoms associated with irritable bowel syndrome (IBS).

Additionally, it can be used to support the removal of waste and toxins from the body.

How to make Cassia senna syrup for medicinal use?

To make cassia senna syrup for medicinal use, combine 1 part of dried cassia senna leaves with 4 parts of water in a saucepan and simmer for 30 minutes to 1 hour, then strain the mixture and add equal parts of sugar and water to create a syrup.

The resulting syrup can be used to promote digestion and relieve constipation, but it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using it for medicinal purposes.

Store the syrup in a cool, dark place and use it within a few weeks to ensure its potency and effectiveness.

What are the health side effects of Cassia senna syrup?

Cassia senna syrup can have side effects such as stomach cramps, diarrhea, and nausea due to its laxative properties.

Some individuals may experience allergic reactions, including hives, itching, and swelling.

High doses of cassia senna syrup can lead to potassium depletion, which may cause muscle weakness and heart palpitations.
