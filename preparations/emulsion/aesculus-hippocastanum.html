What To Know About Medicinal Aesculus Hippocastanum Emulsion?

Aesculus hippocastanum emulsion, also known as horse chestnut emulsion, is a topical preparation used to relieve symptoms of chronic venous insufficiency, such as swelling and inflammation in the legs. The emulsion is typically derived from the seeds of the horse chestnut tree, which contain a compound called aescin that has anti-inflammatory properties. By applying the emulsion to the affected area, it can help to reduce swelling and alleviate discomfort associated with conditions like varicose veins and edema.

How to make Aesculus hippocastanum emulsion for medicinal use?

To make aesculus hippocastanum emulsion for medicinal use, first extract the aesculin from the horse chestnut plant (Aesculus hippocastanum) using a solvent such as ethanol or methanol.
