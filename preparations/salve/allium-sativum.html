What To Know About Medicinal Allium Sativum Salve?

Allium sativum, also known as garlic, has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to create various health-promoting remedies, including salves. Garlic salve, typically made by infusing garlic in oil, is often applied topically to help alleviate symptoms of cold sores, minor cuts, and scrapes due to its antimicrobial and antiviral properties. When applied to the skin, the salve can also help reduce inflammation and promote wound healing, making it a popular natural remedy for skin issues.

What are the medicinal uses of Allium sativum salve?

Allium sativum salve is used to treat various skin irritations and wounds due to its antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties.

It is also applied topically to relieve pain and reduce swelling in areas such as sprains, bruises, and minor burns.

Additionally, allium sativum salve may help to promote wound healing by stimulating tissue regeneration and reducing the risk of infection.

How to make Allium sativum salve for medicinal use?

To make allium sativum salve for medicinal use, start by infusing the dried leaves and flowers of Allium sativum (garlic) in a carrier oil such as olive or coconut oil, allowing it to steep for several weeks.

Strain the oil and mix it with a base such as beeswax and coconut oil to create a salve that can be applied topically to soothe wounds and reduce inflammation.

Be sure to follow proper safety precautions and consult with a healthcare professional before using this salve for medicinal purposes.
