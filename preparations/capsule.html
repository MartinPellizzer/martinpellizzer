acorus calamus

The capsule of Acorus calamus, also known as sweet flag, is a dry, dehiscent fruit that splits open to release its seeds. It is typically an elliptical or oval shape, with a ridged or winged appearance that helps in dispersal. The capsule usually contains 3-6 seeds, each surrounded by a fleshy, white aril that aids in seed dispersal through animals and water.

aesculus hippocastanum

The Aesculus hippocastanum, or horse chestnut tree, produces a distinctive brown capsule that splits open to reveal several seeds. The capsule is typically 5-10 cm long and 4-6 cm wide, containing 2-4 seeds each. The seeds are surrounded by a fleshy, white aril that is edible but also contains a toxic compound called aesculin.

allium sativum

The capsule of Allium sativum, commonly known as garlic, is a dry, dehiscent structure that encloses the seeds. After ripening, the capsule splits into three parts, releasing the seeds to disperse. The capsule's unique characteristics and seed dispersal mechanism contribute to the adaptability and widespread distribution of garlic in various environments.

alpinia officinarum

The Alpinia officinarum, also known as Galangal, is a type of rhizome commonly used in traditional medicine, particularly in Southeast Asian cultures. The dried capsule of the Alpinia officinarum is reported to have various medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. However, further research is needed to fully understand its potential health benefits and to confirm its safety for use as a medicinal agent.

ammi visnaga

Ammi visnaga capsule, also known as Khella, is a herbal supplement traditionally used to treat various health issues, including anxiety, insomnia, and heart palpitations. The plant, whose scientific name is Bunium bulbocastanum, has been used in traditional medicine for centuries, particularly in the Middle East and North Africa. However, its effectiveness and safety as a treatment for these conditions have not been extensively scientifically proven, and more research is needed to confirm its benefits.

angelica sinensis

Angelica sinensis capsule, also known as dong quai, is a traditional Chinese herbal remedy derived from the root of the Angelica sinensis plant. It has been used for centuries to promote women's health, alleviate menstrual discomfort, and support overall well-being. The capsule is believed to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, although its effectiveness for certain health conditions has not been extensively scientifically proven.

arctium lappa

The Arctium lappa capsule, also known as the burdock seed, is a type of fruit that grows from the burdock plant, a biennial herb native to Europe and Asia. The capsule is a dense, globular head containing numerous seeds, typically brown or gray in color, which are surrounded by a prickly, burs-like covering. When mature, the capsule splits open to release the seeds, which can disperse in the wind or be carried away by animals, contributing to the plant's widespread distribution.
