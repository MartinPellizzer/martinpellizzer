acorus calamus

The capsule of Acorus calamus, also known as sweet flag, is a dry, dehiscent fruit that splits open to release its seeds. It is typically an elliptical or oval shape, with a ridged or winged appearance that helps in dispersal. The capsule usually contains 3-6 seeds, each surrounded by a fleshy, white aril that aids in seed dispersal through animals and water.

allium sativum

The capsule of Allium sativum, also known as garlic, is a dry, dehiscent structure that splits into three-valved segments to release its seeds. Typically, the capsule of garlic is brown or reddish-brown in color and contains several seeds. After maturation, the capsule of Allium sativum dries and turns brittle, eventually splitting open to disperse its seeds.

alpinia officinarum

The Alpinia officinarum capsule, also known as the lesser galangal or Chinese ginger, is derived from the rhizome of the Alpinia officinarum plant, a tropical species native to Southeast Asia. The capsule contains seeds, which are used in traditional medicine in various countries, including China and India, where they are valued for their purported health benefits. The seeds are said to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, although further research is needed to confirm their efficacy and safety.

ammi visnaga

Ammi visnaga capsule is a herbal supplement derived from the plant Ammi visnaga, also known as the toothpick plant. Traditionally, it has been used to treat various conditions, including edema, high blood pressure, and respiratory issues. However, its effectiveness and safety for human consumption have not been extensively studied, and more research is needed to fully understand its potential benefits and risks.

angelica sinensis

Angelica sinensis capsule, also known as dong quai, is a traditional Chinese herbal remedy commonly used to promote women's health and alleviate menstrual cramps. The capsule is made from the dried root of the Angelica sinensis plant and has been used in Chinese medicine for centuries to nourish and balance the body's energy. When taken as directed, angelica sinensis capsule may help to reduce symptoms of PMS, improve circulation, and support overall well-being.

arctium lappa

The capsule of Arctium lappa, also known as the burdock plant, is a dry, many-seeded fruit that splits open to release its seeds. It is typically rounded or oval in shape, with a papery texture and a ribbed surface. The capsule of the burdock plant often remains on the stem even after the leaves have died back, serving as a distinctive and recognizable feature of the plant.

aesculus hippocastanum

The capsule of Aesculus hippocastanum, also known as the horse chestnut tree, is a spiky, green, and egg-shaped structure that contains multiple seeds. As the seeds mature, the capsule dries and splits open, releasing the seeds, which are then dispersed by wind or water. The capsules typically fall to the ground in autumn, providing a striking contrast to the tree's leaves which often turn golden or copper in color during the same season.
