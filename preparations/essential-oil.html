essential oils

achillea millefolium

Achillea millefolium, also known as yarrow essential oil, is a natural antiseptic and anti-inflammatory oil extracted from the flowers and leaves of the yarrow plant. Its properties make it effective in soothing skin irritations, wounds, and insect bites, while also offering a calming and grounding effect on the mind and body. Traditionally used in herbal medicine, yarrow oil is often used in aromatherapy and skincare products due to its antimicrobial and antifungal properties.

acorus calamus

Acorus calamus essential oil, also known as calamus oil, is derived from the rhizome of the sweet flag plant and is commonly used in aromatherapy. It is known for its earthy, woody, and slightly sweet aroma, which is said to promote relaxation and reduce anxiety. In traditional medicine, acorus calamus essential oil has been used to aid digestion, relieve headaches, and support respiratory health.

acorus calamus essential oil

alpinia officinarum

Alpinia officinarum essential oil, also known as galangal oil, is derived from the rhizomes of the Alpinia officinarum plant and is commonly used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy. This oil is valued for its warm, spicy, and earthy fragrance, which is said to have a grounding effect on the mind and body. Alpinia officinarum essential oil is also believed to possess antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a popular choice for skincare and wound healing applications.

alpinia officinarum essential oil

apium graveolens

Apium graveolens, also known as celery seed essential oil, is derived from the seeds of the Apium graveolens plant and is commonly used in aromatherapy for its potential health benefits. It is said to have anti-inflammatory properties and may help to reduce stress and anxiety due to its calming effects on the mind and body. Additionally, celery seed oil is believed to possess antimicrobial properties, making it a popular choice for natural remedies and skincare products.

apium graveolens essential oil

artemisia absinthium

Artemisia absinthium essential oil, also known as absinthe oil, is derived from the flowers and leaves of the wormwood plant. This oil has a pungent, earthy aroma and is often used in aromatherapy to promote relaxation and reduce anxiety. However, due to its potential toxicity and ability to interact with certain medications, it's essential to use absinthe oil with caution and under the guidance of a qualified aromatherapist.

artemisia absinthium essential oil

azadirachta indica

Azadirachta indica, commonly known as neem oil, is derived from the seeds of the neem tree and is widely used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy. The essential oil has anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antifungal properties, making it effective in treating various skin conditions, wounds, and infections. Neem oil is also used to repel insects and is believed to have antioxidant and anti-cancer properties, although more research is needed to confirm its efficacy in these areas.

azadirachta indica essential oil

barosma betulina

Barosma betulina, also known as Buchu oil, is a type of essential oil extracted from the leaves of the Barosma betulina plant, native to South Africa. The oil is known for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, making it effective in treating various skin and wound issues. Traditionally used by indigenous communities to treat urinary tract infections and other health conditions, Buchu oil has gained recognition for its potential therapeutic benefits and natural remedies.
