What To Know About Medicinal Artemisia Absinthium Lozenge?

Artemisia absinthium lozenges are a modern adaptation of traditional herbal remedies, typically made from the dried leaves and flowers of the wormwood plant. These lozenges are often used to promote oral health and soothe sore throats due to their antimicrobial properties. However, it's essential to note that wormwood can be toxic in large quantities, so users should consult with a healthcare professional before using artemisia absinthium lozenges for medicinal purposes.

How to make Artemisia absinthium lozenge for medicinal use?

To make artemisia absinthium lozenge for medicinal use, combine 1 teaspoon of dried artemisia absinthium flowers and leaves with 1 tablespoon of honey and 1 tablespoon of glycerin in a small saucepan.

Heat the mixture over low heat, stirring constantly, until the honey and glycerin dissolve into a syrup.

Once cooled, pour the syrup into a lozenge mold and allow it to set before removing the lozenges and storing them in an airtight container.
