What To Know About Medicinal Angelica Sinensis Lozenge?

Angelica sinensis lozenges, also known as dong quai lozenges, are a traditional herbal remedy used to promote women's health and relieve menstrual discomfort. These lozenges are typically made from the dried roots of the Angelica sinensis plant, which is rich in antioxidants and has anti-inflammatory properties. By soothing the body and promoting balance, angelica sinensis lozenges may help to alleviate symptoms of PMS, menstrual cramps, and other hormonal imbalances.

What are the medicinal uses of Angelica sinensis lozenge?

Angelica sinensis lozenge is used to relieve respiratory issues such as dry coughs and sore throats due to its expectorant properties and antiviral effects.

It is also believed to have anti-inflammatory properties that may help soothe a sore throat.

Additionally, angelica sinensis lozenge may help to reduce stress and promote overall well-being.

How to make Angelica sinensis lozenge for medicinal use?

To make angelica sinensis lozenge for medicinal use, first, prepare a decoction of dried angelica sinensis root in water, then mix the resulting liquid with a gelling agent like agar or pectin to create a gel-like consistency.

Next, add flavorings and sweeteners, if desired, and pour the mixture into molds to create the lozenge shape.

Finally, allow the mixture to set and harden before removing the lozenges from the molds and packaging them for use.

What are the health side effects of Angelica sinensis lozenge?

Angelica sinensis lozenge can have side effects such as allergic reactions, interactions with blood-thinning medications, and gastrointestinal upset in some individuals.

These potential side effects may vary depending on the individual's health status and the specific lozenge product.

It is essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using angelica sinensis lozenges, especially if you have any underlying medical conditions.
