What To Know About Medicinal Arctium Lappa Lozenge?

Arctium lappa, also known as burdock, has been used to create a lozenge that supports throat health and immune function. The lozenge is often made by infusing the roots of the burdock plant in a sweetener like honey or glycerin, resulting in a soothing and protective remedy for sore throats. Traditionally, arctium lappa lozenges have been used to combat coughs and colds, as well as to provide relief from inflammation and irritation in the mouth and throat.

How to make Arctium lappa lozenge for medicinal use?

To make arctium lappa lozenge for medicinal use, first dry the roots of Arctium lappa, then mix 1 part of the dried root powder with 2 parts of honey or glycerin to create a paste.

Add a small amount of water to the paste and stir well to achieve the desired consistency.

Pour the mixture into a lozenge mold and allow it to set before removing and storing the lozenges for later use.
