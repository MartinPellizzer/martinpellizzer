achillea millefolium

Achillea millefolium infusion, also known as yarrow tea, is a herbal remedy made by steeping the leaves and flowers of the yarrow plant in hot water. This infusion is traditionally used to promote digestion, reduce inflammation, and support wound healing due to its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties. When consumed regularly, achillea millefolium infusion may also help to calm the nervous system and promote a restful night's sleep.

acorus calamus

Acorus calamus infusion, also known as sweet flag tea, is a herbal remedy made from the rhizomes of the Acorus calamus plant. Traditionally used in Ayurvedic and traditional Chinese medicine, this infusion is believed to have anti-inflammatory and carminative properties, which may help to soothe digestive issues and alleviate stress. However, it's essential to note that Acorus calamus can interact with certain medications and has potential side effects, making it crucial to consult with a healthcare professional before using it as a treatment.

acorus calamus infusion

alpinia officinarum

Alpinia officinarum, also known as lesser galangal, is a plant-based remedy used in traditional medicine to aid digestion and alleviate symptoms of nausea and indigestion. The roots of the Alpinia officinarum plant are used to make an infusion, which is believed to have anti-inflammatory properties and can help to soothe the digestive system. When consumed as a warm infusion, Alpinia officinarum may also help to reduce menstrual cramps and promote overall well-being.

alpinia officinarum infusion

ammi visnaga

Ammi visnaga infusion is a traditional herbal remedy used to treat a variety of health issues, including anxiety, insomnia, and digestive problems. The infusion, made by steeping the dried roots of the plant in hot water, is said to have a calming effect on the body and mind, promoting relaxation and reducing stress levels. However, it is essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using ammi visnaga infusion, as it may interact with certain medications or exacerbate underlying health conditions.

ammi visnaga infusion

angelica sinensis

Angelica sinensis infusion, also known as dong quai, is a traditional herbal tea made from the roots of the Angelica sinensis plant, commonly used in Chinese medicine to promote women's health and well-being. It is believed to have various benefits, including regulating menstrual cycles, reducing menopausal symptoms, and improving overall cardiovascular health. When consumed as an infusion, the active compounds in Angelica sinensis are thought to have a soothing and balancing effect on the body, promoting relaxation and rejuvenation.

angelica sinensis infusion

apium graveolens

Apium graveolens, also known as celery seed infusion, is a herbal remedy made from the seeds of the wild celery plant. It has been traditionally used to aid digestion and relieve symptoms of indigestion, bloating, and gas. When consumed as an infusion, celery seed is believed to possess anti-inflammatory properties, which may help to soothe and calm the digestive system.

arctium lappa

Arctium lappa, also known as burdock root, has been traditionally used to create a soothing and nourishing infusion. When made as a tea, the roots and leaves of the burdock plant are said to have anti-inflammatory properties, which may help to calm digestive issues and promote a sense of well-being. The infusion is often consumed warm, allowing the herbal compounds to effectively interact with the body's systems and promote overall health and balance.

artemisia absinthium

Artemisia absinthium, also known as wormwood, is a plant-based ingredient that has been traditionally used to make a bitter and stimulating infusion. When brewed as a tea, wormwood infusion is believed to have various health benefits, including aiding digestion and reducing inflammation. However, due to its potential toxicity and interactions with certain medications, wormwood infusion should be consumed in moderation and under the guidance of a healthcare professional.
