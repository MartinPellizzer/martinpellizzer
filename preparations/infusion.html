achillea millefolium

Achillea millefolium infusion, also known as yarrow tea, is a herbal remedy made from the flowers and leaves of the yarrow plant. It has been traditionally used to aid in digestion, reduce inflammation, and promote wound healing due to its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties. Drinking yarrow tea may also help to calm the nervous system and promote a restful sleep, although more research is needed to confirm its effectiveness.

acorus calamus

Acorus calamus infusion, also known as calamus tea, is a traditional herbal remedy made by steeping the root of the Acorus calamus plant in hot water. The infusion is said to have a range of potential health benefits, including reducing anxiety and stress, improving digestion, and supporting respiratory health. However, it's essential to note that the effectiveness and safety of calamus tea can vary depending on the individual and the quality of the herb, and it's recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before using it as a treatment.

acorus calamus infusion

allium sativum

Allium sativum infusion, commonly known as garlic tea, is a traditional remedy that has been used for centuries to promote digestive health and alleviate symptoms of the common cold. The infusion is made by steeping crushed garlic cloves in hot water, releasing its active compounds and bioactive sulfur molecules. Drinking garlic tea is believed to possess antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties, although more research is needed to confirm its effectiveness and optimal dosage.

allium sativum infusion

alpinia officinarum

Alpinia officinarum, also known as Galangal, is a type of rhizome commonly used in traditional medicine. The infusion is also valued for its potential anti-inflammatory properties, which may help to reduce pain and promote relaxation.

artemisia annua

Artemisia annua infusion, also known as sweet wormwood tea, is a traditional herbal remedy derived from the leaves of the artemisia annua plant. This infusion has been used for centuries to treat various health issues, including fever, malaria, and other infectious diseases, due to its high concentration of artemisinin, a potent antimalarial compound. When consumed as a tea, artemisia annua infusion is typically brewed with hot water and consumed in moderation, as its effects can be intense and it may interact with certain medications.

atropa belladonna

Atropa belladonna, also known as deadly nightshade, is a highly toxic plant that has been used in herbal medicine for centuries. An infusion of the plant's berries or leaves is not recommended for consumption due to its extremely potent and toxic properties, which can cause hallucinations, delirium, and even death. In some cases, a highly diluted and carefully prepared infusion of belladonna may be used under the guidance of a trained medical professional or herbalist for specific therapeutic purposes.
