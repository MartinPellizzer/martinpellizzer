infusions

achillea millefolium

Achillea millefolium infusion, also known as yarrow tea, is a herbal remedy made by steeping the leaves and flowers of the yarrow plant in hot water. This infusion is traditionally used to promote digestion, reduce inflammation, and support wound healing due to its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties. When consumed regularly, achillea millefolium infusion may also help to calm the nervous system and promote a restful night's sleep.

acorus calamus

Acorus calamus infusion, also known as sweet flag tea, is a herbal remedy made from the rhizomes of the Acorus calamus plant. Traditionally used in Ayurvedic and traditional Chinese medicine, this infusion is believed to have anti-inflammatory and carminative properties, which may help to soothe digestive issues and alleviate stress. However, it's essential to note that Acorus calamus can interact with certain medications and has potential side effects, making it crucial to consult with a healthcare professional before using it as a treatment.

acorus calamus infusion

allium sativum

Allium sativum infusion, also known as garlic tea, is a natural remedy made by steeping crushed garlic cloves in hot water. This herbal infusion is believed to have various health benefits, including reducing inflammation, lowering cholesterol levels, and aiding in digestion. However, it's essential to consult a healthcare professional before consuming garlic tea, especially for people with certain medical conditions or taking medications.

allium sativum infusion

alpinia officinarum

Alpinia officinarum, commonly known as Galangal, is a rhizome-based infusion traditionally used in Southeast Asian medicine to aid digestion and relieve nausea. The Galangan root contains compounds with anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may contribute to its potential health benefits. As an herbal remedy, Alpinia officinarum infusion is often consumed hot, often combined with other ingredients such as ginger and lemongrass, to promote digestive well-being.

alpinia officinarum infusion

ammi visnaga

Ammi visnaga infusion, also known as Khella tea, is a herbal remedy traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of anxiety and insomnia. This infusion is made from the dried seeds of the ammi visnaga plant, which is native to the Mediterranean region and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries. However, it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using ammi visnaga infusion, as it may interact with certain medications or have adverse effects in some individuals.

ammi visnaga infusion

angelica sinensis

Angelica sinensis infusion, also known as dong quai, is a traditional Chinese herbal remedy made from the roots of the Angelica sinensis plant. This infusion is often used to promote menstrual health, alleviate menstrual cramps, and balance the body's energy, or "qi," according to traditional Chinese medicine principles. However, it is essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using angelica sinensis infusion, especially for individuals with bleeding disorders, high blood pressure, or other health conditions.

angelica sinensis infusion

apium graveolens

Apium graveolens, also known as wild celery or wild lovage, is a herb that has been used for centuries in traditional medicine and herbal remedies. The infusion made from its leaves is said to have a calming effect on the digestive system and can help alleviate symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome and other gastrointestinal issues. When consumed as a tea, apium graveolens infusion is believed to promote relaxation and reduce inflammation in the body.

apium graveolens infusion

arctium lappa

Arctium lappa, also known as burdock root, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits, including reducing inflammation and improving digestion. The roots and leaves of the plant can be made into an infusion, which is said to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Drinking arctium lappa infusion may also help to support the immune system and promote overall well-being.

arctium lappa infusion

artemisia absinthium

Artemisia absinthium, also known as wormwood, is a perennial herb used to make a distinctive and bitter infusion. Traditionally, the leaves and stems of the plant are steeped in hot water to create a medicinal tea that has been used to aid digestion and relieve stress. When consumed in moderation, artemisia absinthium infusion can have a calming effect, although it's essential to be cautious due to its potential toxicity in large quantities.

artemisia absinthium infusion
