What To Know About Medicinal Calendula Officinalis Glycerite?

Calendula officinalis glycerite is a herbal supplement made from the flowers of the calendula plant, typically infused in a glycerin base to create a soothing and gentle liquid extract. This preparation is often used to support skin health, reducing inflammation and promoting wound healing, while also providing relief from minor cuts and scrapes. As a glycerite, calendula officinalis is typically taken sublingually or added to water to create a calming and potentially beneficial remedy for overall well-being.

What are the medicinal uses of Calendula officinalis glycerite?

Calendula officinalis glycerite is used to soothe and calm irritated skin, reduce inflammation, and promote wound healing.

It is often used topically to treat minor cuts, scrapes, and burns, as well as skin conditions like eczema and acne.

Additionally, calendula officinalis glycerite may also be used to support immune function and reduce inflammation in the body.

What are the health benefits of Calendula officinalis glycerite?

Calendula officinalis glycerite has health benefits such as promoting wound healing, reducing inflammation, and soothing skin irritations, which can lead to improved skin health and reduced discomfort.

It may also help to protect the skin from environmental stressors and support the immune system.

Additionally, some users report a reduction in pain and discomfort associated with skin conditions and injuries.

What are the therapeutic properties of Calendula officinalis glycerite?

Calendula officinalis glycerite has therapeutic properties such as anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, which may aid in wound healing and skin regeneration.

It is often used to soothe and calm irritated skin, reducing redness and promoting a healthy complexion.

Additionally, calendula glycerite may help to protect the skin from environmental stressors and promote a balanced skin microbiome.

How to make Calendula officinalis glycerite for medicinal use?

To make calendula officinalis glycerite for medicinal use, start by infusing dried calendula flowers in a carrier oil, such as coconut or olive oil, for several weeks to extract the plant's medicinal properties.

Once the infusion is complete, mix the oil with a glycerin solution in a specific ratio, typically 1 part oil to 3-4 parts glycerin, and then stir well to combine.

This mixture is then left to mature and blend for several weeks to allow the glycerin to fully dissolve the oil and create a smooth, medicinally active glycerite.

What are the health side effects of Calendula officinalis glycerite?

Calendula officinalis glycerite can have side effects such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and interactions with certain medications, particularly blood thinners.

Additionally, it may cause gastrointestinal upset, such as nausea or diarrhea, in some individuals.

In rare cases, it may also interact with other herbal remedies, exacerbating certain health conditions.
