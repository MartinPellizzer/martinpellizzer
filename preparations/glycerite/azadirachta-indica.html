What To Know About Medicinal Azadirachta Indica Glycerite?

Azadirachta indica, commonly known as neem, is a plant-based ingredient that has been used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine for centuries. When extracted into a glycerite, the compounds from the neem plant are dissolved in glycerin to create a liquid extract that can be easily absorbed and utilized by the body. This Azadirachta indica glycerite is often used in herbal supplements and natural remedies for its potential anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties.

How to make Azadirachta indica glycerite for medicinal use?

To make azadirachta indica glycerite for medicinal use, start by harvesting fresh or dried neem leaves and flowers, which are rich in bioactive compounds.

Next, create a solvent by mixing glycerin with a carrier oil, such as coconut oil, in a specific ratio.
