What To Know About Medicinal Acorus Calamus Glycerite?

Acorus calamus glycerite is a herbal supplement derived from the rhizomes of the sweet flag plant, known for its potential health benefits and distinct aroma. This glycerite, often used in traditional medicine, is typically made by infusing the dried rhizome in a glycerin base. The resulting liquid is said to support digestive health, reduce inflammation, and promote relaxation, although more research is needed to confirm its efficacy.

How to make Acorus calamus glycerite for medicinal use?

To make acorus calamus glycerite for medicinal use, start by harvesting or purchasing high-quality Acorus calamus roots, then dry and grind them into a fine powder.

Next, mix the powdered Acorus calamus with a glycerin solution, typically 1:1 ratio, and allow it to steep for several weeks, shaking the mixture regularly.

Once the infusion is complete, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter to obtain the glycerite, which can be stored in a cool, dark place for later use.
