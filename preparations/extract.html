achillea millefolium

Achillea millefolium, also known as yarrow, is a flowering plant that has been used for centuries in traditional medicine due to its various health benefits. The extract of this plant contains antioxidants, anti-inflammatory compounds, and bioactive flavonoids, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties. Research suggests that achillea millefolium extract may have potential in treating conditions such as wounds, digestive issues, and menstrual cramps, although more studies are needed to confirm its efficacy.

acorus calamus

Acorus calamus extract, also known as calamus oil, is derived from the rhizome of the Acorus calamus plant, a native species to Asia and Europe. Traditionally used in Ayurvedic and traditional Chinese medicine, calamus oil is valued for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, as well as its ability to support digestive health and alleviate anxiety. However, its use is also associated with potential side effects and interactions, making it essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using calamus extract as a supplement.

acorus calamus extract

aesculus hippocastanum

Aesculus hippocastanum extract, also known as horse chestnut extract, is derived from the seeds of the Aesculus hippocastanum tree and has been traditionally used to support vein health and reduce swelling. The extract contains a number of bioactive compounds, including aescin, which has been shown to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Studies have suggested that Aesculus hippocastanum extract may be effective in alleviating symptoms of chronic venous insufficiency, such as varicose veins and leg swelling.

aesculus hippocastanum extract

allium sativum

Allium sativum, commonly known as garlic, has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, with its extract being found to exhibit antimicrobial and antioxidant properties. The extract of Allium sativum has been shown to have anti-inflammatory effects, which may contribute to its potential in reducing the risk of chronic diseases such as heart disease and certain cancers. However, further research is necessary to fully understand the effects and optimal dosages of Allium sativum extract in humans.

allium sativum extract

aloe vera

Aloe vera extract is a natural and versatile substance derived from the aloe vera plant, known for its soothing and moisturizing properties. It is commonly used in skincare products to help calm irritated skin, reduce inflammation, and promote wound healing. The extract is also rich in antioxidants, vitamins, and minerals, making it a popular ingredient in beauty and wellness products.

aloe vera extract

alpinia officinarum

Alpinia officinarum extract, also known as galangal extract, is derived from the rhizome of the Alpinia officinarum plant, a species native to Southeast Asia. It is rich in bioactive compounds, including flavonoids and phenolic acids, which have been shown to possess antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. Galangal extract has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes, including the treatment of digestive issues and as a natural remedy for colds and flu.

alpinia officinarum extract

ammi visnaga

Ammi visnaga extract, also known as ferulic acid, is a natural compound derived from the seeds of the Ammi visnaga plant, commonly used in herbal medicine and skincare products. It has been traditionally used to treat various health conditions, including digestive issues and anxiety, due to its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. Recent research has also highlighted its potential benefits in protecting the skin from sun damage and promoting wound healing.

ammi visnaga extract

angelica sinensis

Angelica sinensis, also known as dong quai, is a traditional Chinese herbal remedy that has been used for centuries to promote women's health and alleviate symptoms associated with menopause. The extract of this plant is rich in ferulic acid, isoflavones, and other bioactive compounds that have been shown to exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Research suggests that angelica sinensis extract may help to improve cardiovascular health, reduce menopausal symptoms, and even exhibit anti-cancer effects, making it a popular ingredient in modern herbal supplements.

angelica sinensis extract

apium graveolens

Apium graveolens extract, also known as celery seed extract, is derived from the seeds of the celery plant and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits. The extract contains a unique combination of bioactive compounds, including limonene, apigenin, and luteolin, which may contribute to its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. Research on apium graveolens extract suggests its potential applications in cardiovascular health, cancer prevention, and digestive wellness, although more studies are needed to confirm its efficacy.

apium graveolens extract

arctium lappa

Arctium lappa extract, also known as burdock root extract, is a natural supplement derived from the roots of the greater burdock plant. It is rich in antioxidants, vitamins, and minerals, and has been traditionally used to support skin health, reduce inflammation, and promote digestion. Modern research suggests that arctium lappa extract may also have anti-aging and anti-cancer properties, although further studies are needed to confirm its efficacy.

artemisia absinthium

Artemisia absinthium extract, also known as wormwood extract, is a herbal compound derived from the leaves of the wormwood plant. It is primarily used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in beverages, particularly in the production of absinthe, a popular anise-flavored spirit. The extract is known for its potential therapeutic properties, including antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects, although its use should be approached with caution due to potential toxicity and side effects.

artemisia absinthium extract

artemisia annua

Artemisia annua extract, also known as sweet wormwood extract, has been used for centuries in traditional Chinese medicine for its antimalarial properties. The extract contains artemisinin, a compound that has been shown to be effective against Plasmodium falciparum, the parasite responsible for the deadliest form of malaria. Modern research has validated the traditional use of artemisia annua extract, with many studies demonstrating its potential as a natural remedy for malaria and other diseases.

artemisia annua extract

astragalus membranaceus

Astragalus membranaceus extract, also known as Huang Qi, is a traditional Chinese medicinal ingredient derived from the root of the astragalus plant. Research suggests that this extract may possess immunomodulatory and anti-inflammatory properties, which can potentially aid in boosting the immune system and reducing oxidative stress. Studies have also explored its potential applications in improving cardiovascular health, reducing fatigue, and enhancing overall well-being.

astragalus membranaceus extract

atropa belladonna

Atropa belladonna, also known as deadly nightshade, is a highly toxic plant extract that has been used for centuries in traditional medicine, albeit with great caution. The extract, which contains tropane alkaloids such as scopolamine and atropine, has been used to treat various conditions, including pain, inflammation, and spasms. However, due to its potent toxicity, handling and consumption of atropa belladonna extract should be strictly avoided and only carried out under the supervision of a qualified medical professional or researcher.

atropa belladonna extract

azadirachta indica

Azadirachta indica extract, also known as neem extract, is derived from the leaves and seeds of the neem tree, which is native to the Indian subcontinent. This extract is rich in bioactive compounds, including limonoids, flavonoids, and terpenoids, that have been shown to possess antibacterial, antifungal, and anti-inflammatory properties. Azadirachta indica extract has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine for various health benefits, including skin and hair care, and is also being researched for its potential applications in modern medicine.

azadirachta indica extract
