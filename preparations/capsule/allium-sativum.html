allium sativum capsule

intro

The capsule of Allium sativum, commonly known as garlic, is a dry dehiscent fruit that splits open at maturity to release its seeds. The capsule is typically a trilete type, meaning it has three ridges or lines on its surface where the sepals meet. As the capsule matures, it dries and turns brown, eventually splitting open to reveal the seeds inside, allowing for seed dispersal and germination.
