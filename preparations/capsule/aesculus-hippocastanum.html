aesculus hippocastanum capsule

intro

The Aesculus hippocastanum, also known as the horse chestnut tree, produces a distinctive capsule that contains 2-4 large seeds. The capsule is typically a brown, leathery fruit that splits open to reveal its seeds, often remaining attached to the tree for some time. As the seeds mature, they are released from the capsule, allowing them to disperse and germinate into new horse chestnut trees.
