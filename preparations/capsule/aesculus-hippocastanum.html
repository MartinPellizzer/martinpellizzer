What To Know About Medicinal Aesculus Hippocastanum Capsule?

The Aesculus hippocastanum, commonly known as the horse chestnut tree, produces a distinctive capsule as a fruit. The capsule is a spiky, green or brown, four- or five-valved pod that splits open to reveal shiny red or brown seeds. Each capsule typically contains 2-4 large seeds surrounded by a fleshy, inedible pulp, and they disperse in the fall, allowing the seeds to germinate and grow into new trees.
