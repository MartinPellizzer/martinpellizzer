What To Know About Medicinal Astragalus Membranaceus Ghee?

Astragalus membranaceus ghee is a unique and potentially health-promoting type of ghee infused with the extract of astragalus membranaceus, a traditional Chinese herb known for its adaptogenic properties. This herbal ghee is believed to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, making it a sought-after ingredient in modern wellness and traditional medicine practices. When consumed, astragalus membranaceus ghee may help to boost the immune system and promote overall well-being, particularly in individuals undergoing stress or experiencing fatigue.

Astragalus membranaceus ghee is used to aid in immune system function and potentially help with chronic fatigue.

It is also believed to have anti-inflammatory properties, which may help with conditions such as arthritis.

Additionally, it may be used to support cardiovascular health and reduce stress.

How to make Astragalus membranaceus ghee for medicinal use?

To make astragalus membranaceus ghee for medicinal use, first infuse dried astragalus membranaceus in a neutral oil like ghee or avocado oil for a period of time, allowing the herbal properties to transfer into the oil.

Next, filter the infused oil to remove any plant material and clarify the liquid.

Finally, heat the infused oil in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water to create a ghee that can be used for medicinal purposes, such as supporting immune function and reducing inflammation.

What are the health side effects of Astragalus membranaceus ghee?

Astragalus membranaceus ghee can have side effects such as gastrointestinal issues, allergic reactions, and interactions with medications, particularly blood thinners and diabetes medications.

Some people may also experience dizziness, fatigue, or headaches after consuming astragalus membranaceus ghee.

It is essential to consult a healthcare professional before using it, especially if you have any underlying health conditions.
