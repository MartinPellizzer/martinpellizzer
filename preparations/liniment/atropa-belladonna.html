What To Know About Medicinal Atropa Belladonna Liniment?

Atropa belladonna liniment, also known as deadly nightshade liniment, is a topical preparation made from the extract of the Atropa belladonna plant, which contains tropane alkaloids such as atropine and scopolamine. Historically, it was used as a pain reliever and to treat various ailments including toothaches, rheumatism, and menstrual cramps, although its use has largely been discontinued due to its potential for toxicity and addiction. However, some modern herbalists and practitioners continue to use it under strict guidelines and close supervision, often as a last resort for severe, intractable pain.

What are the medicinal uses of Atropa belladonna liniment?

Atropa belladonna liniment is used to relieve pain and reduce inflammation, particularly for muscle and joint issues, due to its antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties.

It may also be used to treat respiratory issues such as bronchitis and asthma.

Additionally, it can be used to treat certain skin conditions like eczema and acne.

How to make Atropa belladonna liniment for medicinal use?

To make atropa belladonna liniment for medicinal use, combine 10 grams of dried Atropa belladonna root and 20 grams of dried Atropa belladonna leaf in a clean glass jar.

Add 500 milliliters of a high-proof neutral spirit, such as Everclear, to the jar and seal it.

Allow the mixture to steep for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily, before straining and diluting the liniment with a carrier oil, such as olive oil, to a 1:10 ratio.
