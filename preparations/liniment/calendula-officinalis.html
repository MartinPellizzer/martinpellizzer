What To Know About Medicinal Calendula Officinalis Liniment?

Calendula officinalis liniment is a topical herbal remedy derived from the flowers of the marigold plant, known for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. When applied topically, this liniment can help soothe and calm irritated skin, reducing redness and promoting wound healing. Its non-irritating and gentle nature makes it a popular choice for skin care, particularly for soothing minor burns, cuts, and eczema.

What are the medicinal uses of Calendula officinalis liniment?

Calendula officinalis liniment is used to treat skin irritations, wounds, and minor burns due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

It is also applied to soothe and calm eczema, acne, and other skin conditions.

Additionally, calendula liniment is sometimes used to relieve joint pain and reduce inflammation associated with arthritis.

How to make Calendula officinalis liniment for medicinal use?

To make calendula officinalis liniment for medicinal use, infuse dried calendula flowers in a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil for 2-6 weeks, then strain and mix with a solvent like witch hazel or vodka to create a tincture.

Combine the tincture with a base oil, such as jojoba or sweet almond oil, in a 1:3 or 1:5 ratio, respectively.

This liniment can be used topically to soothe skin irritations and promote wound healing.

What are the health side effects of Calendula officinalis liniment?

Calendula officinalis liniment can have side effects such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and interact with certain medications, including blood thinners and diabetes medications.

It may also cause stomach upset or nausea in some individuals.

In rare cases, it can cause an increase in heart rate or blood pressure.
