What To Know About Medicinal Acorus Calamus Liniment?

Acorus calamus liniment is a topical treatment made from the extracts of Acorus calamus, also known as sweet flag or calamus root. The liniment is often used to relieve pain and reduce inflammation in muscles and joints, making it a popular remedy for conditions such as arthritis and sprains. It is also believed to have antiseptic and antispasmodic properties, which can help to promote healing and reduce muscle spasms.

How to make Acorus calamus liniment for medicinal use?

To make acorus calamus liniment for medicinal use, combine 2 ounces of dried and crushed Acorus calamus root with 1 cup of vegetable oil in a clean glass jar, and let it steep in a cool, dark place for 2-4 weeks.

After the steeping period, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean container, discarding the solids.

The resulting liniment can be stored in a cool, dark place and used topically as needed for its medicinal properties.
