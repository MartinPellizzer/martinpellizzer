What To Know About Medicinal Azadirachta Indica Liniment?

Azadirachta indica liniment, also known as neem oil liniment, is a topical application derived from the neem tree, used for its antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and antipruritic properties. The liniment is often applied to the skin to treat various health issues, such as wounds, burns, and insect bites, due to its ability to reduce inflammation and promote healing. However, it is essential to use caution and consult a medical professional before applying azadirachta indica liniment, as it may cause skin irritation in some individuals.

How to make Azadirachta indica liniment for medicinal use?

To make azadirachta indica liniment for medicinal use, start by harvesting the leaves and stems of the Azadirachta indica plant, also known as neem, and then dry them thoroughly.

Next, combine the dried neem material with a carrier oil, such as coconut or olive oil, in a ratio of 1 part neem to 3 parts oil.

Finally, allow the mixture to infuse in a cool, dark place for several weeks, shaking the container regularly, before straining and bottling the liniment for use.
