What To Know About Medicinal Achillea Millefolium Liniment?

Achillea millefolium liniment, also known as Yarrow liniment, is a topical remedy made from the leaves and flowers of the yarrow plant. The liniment is often applied topically to the affected area to provide relief and support the body's natural recovery process.

How to make Achillea millefolium liniment for medicinal use?

To make achillea millefolium liniment for medicinal use, combine dried Achillea millefolium flowers with a carrier oil such as olive or grapeseed oil in a 1:5 ratio, then infuse the mixture in a warm, dark place for 2-4 weeks, shaking the mixture occasionally to facilitate extraction.

After the infusion period, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter to separate the liquid from the solids, and discard the solids.

Finally, store the resulting liniment in a clean glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid, labeling it clearly and using it as needed for medicinal purposes.
