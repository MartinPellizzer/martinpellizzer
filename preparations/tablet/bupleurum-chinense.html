What To Know About Medicinal Bupleurum Chinense Tablet?

Bupleurum chinense, also known as Chai Hu, is a traditional Chinese medicinal herb used in various tablet forms to promote liver and gallbladder health. The Bupleurum chinense tablet is believed to have anti-inflammatory properties and is often used to treat conditions such as fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues. Clinical studies have shown that Bupleurum chinense may also have immunomodulatory effects, supporting its use in traditional Chinese medicine as a potential adjunctive treatment for various health conditions.

What are the medicinal uses of Bupleurum chinense tablet?

Bupleurum chinense tablet is used to treat various conditions such as fever, rheumatism, and liver and gallbladder disorders.

It is also believed to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

How to make Bupleurum chinense tablet for medicinal use?

To make bupleurum chinense tablet for medicinal use, start by drying and grinding the Bupleurum chinense roots into a fine powder.

Mix the powder with a binding agent, such as starch or gum arabic, and add a small amount of water to create a paste.

Then, use a tablet press or a mold to shape the paste into tablets of a desired size and shape.
