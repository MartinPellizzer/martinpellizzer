Chaptalia diversifolia

Chaptalia diversifolia, commonly known as the variable chaptalia, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
