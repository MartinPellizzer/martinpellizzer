Crataegus monogyna var. pyramidalis

Crataegus monogyna var. pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal hawthorn, is a compact, shrub-like species native to southern Europe.

It features dense, upright growth with small, oval leaves that are typically glossy green. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in spring, followed by red berries in autumn. It is often used in hedging and as a ornamental plant due to its attractive form and seasonal interest.

The species is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
