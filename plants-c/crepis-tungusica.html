Crepis tungusica

Crepis tungusica, commonly known as the Tungus sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Eurasia.

It is a low-growing perennial species that typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are usually basal, linear, and covered in fine hairs.

Crepis tungusica is adapted to cold climates and is often found in tundra and alpine habitats.
