Carex ornithopoda subsp. elongata

Carex ornithopoda subsp.

elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated.

This subspecies is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It often grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wetland biodiversity.
