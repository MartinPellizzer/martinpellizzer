Crataegus pinnatifida var. pubescens

Crataegus pinnatifida var. pubescens, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its pinnately lobed leaves, which are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it the varietal name "pubescens.". The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
