Convolvulus lindleyi

Convolvulus lindleyi, commonly known as the Lindley's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual with trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple, opening in the morning and closing in the evening. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is part of the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its distinctive flowering structures and climbing or trailing growth habits.
