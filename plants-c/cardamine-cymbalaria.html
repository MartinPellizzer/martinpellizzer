Cardamine cymbalaria

Cardamine cymbalaria, commonly known as the cymbalaria toothwort, is a small perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes buttercups and other flowering plants. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a basal rosette and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
