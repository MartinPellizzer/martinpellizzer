Cytisus uniflorus

Cytisus uniflorus, commonly known as the single-flower broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has pinnate leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

Cytisus uniflorus is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.
