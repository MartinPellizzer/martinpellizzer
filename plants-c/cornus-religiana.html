Cornus religiana

Cornus religiana, commonly known as the sacred cornel.

This plant is a species of dogwood native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. It is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance.

Cornus religiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
