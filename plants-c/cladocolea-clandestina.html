Cladocolea clandestina

Cladocolea clandestina, commonly known as the hidden clove plant, is a rare and elusive species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is a small, evergreen shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces tiny, fragrant flowers that are often overlooked due to their inconspicuous size. Its botanical significance lies in its unique chemical composition, which includes essential oils with potential medicinal properties.

Due to habitat loss and limited study, Cladocolea clandestina remains a subject of scientific interest and conservation concern.
