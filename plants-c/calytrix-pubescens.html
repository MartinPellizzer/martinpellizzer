Calytrix pubescens

Calytrix pubescens, commonly known as the hairy calytrix, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with hairy, narrow leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Calytrix pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
