Crepidium comans

Crepidium comans, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a dense spike, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.

Crepidium comans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
