Centaurea paniculata var. herminii

Centaurea paniculata var. herminii, commonly known as the Herminii knapweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and slightly serrated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
