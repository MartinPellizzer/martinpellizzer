Cylicodaphne walkeri

Cylicodaphne walkeri, commonly known as the Wollongong palm, is a tropical plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that give it a feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to salt-laden air and sandy soils. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are followed by clustered, edible fruits.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
