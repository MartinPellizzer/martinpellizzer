Clematis triphylla

Clematis triphylla, commonly known as the small-leaf clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged in a trifoliate pattern. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and blue, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Clematis triphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
