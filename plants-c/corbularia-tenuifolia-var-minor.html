Corbularia tenuifolia var. minor

Corbularia tenuifolia var. minor, commonly known as the narrow-leaved corbularia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the warm seasons. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to botanical gardens and ecological studies.
