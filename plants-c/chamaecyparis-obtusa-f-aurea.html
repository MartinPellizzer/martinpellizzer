Chamaecyparis obtusa f. aurea

Chamaecyparis obtusa f. aurea, commonly known as the golden cypress, is a compact, slow-growing evergreen conifer with a distinctive pyramidal shape.

Its foliage is characterized by golden-yellow needles that remain throughout the year, offering a striking visual contrast to its dark green stems. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils with partial to full sun exposure. It is often used in ornamental horticulture for its vibrant color and hardy nature.

Due to its unique appearance, it is a popular choice for small gardens and rock gardens.
