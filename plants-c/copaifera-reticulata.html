Copaifera reticulata

Copaifera reticulata, commonly known as the yellow copaiba, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is notable for producing a valuable resin, known as copaiba oil, which is used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is also appreciated for its ecological role in supporting diverse wildlife habitats.
