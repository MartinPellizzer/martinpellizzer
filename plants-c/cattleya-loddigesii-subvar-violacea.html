Cattleya loddigesii subvar. violacea

Cattleya loddigesii subvar. violacea, commonly known as the Violet Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid originating from the hybridization of Cattleya loddigesii and Cattleya violacea.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically feature a deep violet or purple hue with intricate patterns. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences, and its flowers are often adorned with a contrasting lip that enhances its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

This orchid is prized for its beauty and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant blooms and ease of cultivation.
