Carduus × polyanthemus

Carduus × polyanthemus, commonly known as the everlasting or immortelle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its long-lasting, showy flower heads. The plant produces composite flowers that consist of numerous small florets, typically in shades of yellow and orange. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The name "everlasting" refers to its ability to retain its color and form for extended periods when dried.
