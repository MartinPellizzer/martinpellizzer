Chloraea viridiflora var. reticulata

Chloraea viridiflora var. reticulata, commonly known as the reticulated greenhood, is a striking orchid native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, hood-like flower that is typically green with intricate reticulated patterns on the dorsal sepal and petals. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often adorned with contrasting purple or reddish markings. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rainforests or under dense vegetation.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a subject of interest in botanical studies due to its distinctive morphology and ecological adaptations.
