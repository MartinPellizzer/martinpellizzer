Ceratella

Ceratella, commonly known as the water clover, is a genus of aquatic plants belonging to the family Ceratophyllaceae.

These plants are characterized by their small, delicate leaves that often resemble the shape of clover leaves, hence their common name. Ceratella species are typically found in freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. They are often used in aquariums due to their ability to thrive in low-light conditions and their aesthetic appeal.

The plants have a unique ability to absorb nutrients from the water, making them valuable in aquatic ecosystems.
