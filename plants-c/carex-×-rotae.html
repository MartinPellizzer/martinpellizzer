Carex × rotae

Carex × rotae, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
