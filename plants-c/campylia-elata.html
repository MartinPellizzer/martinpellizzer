Campylia elata

Campylia elata, commonly known as the tall cupplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, cup-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are arranged in a composite head typical of the sunflower family. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Campylia elata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
