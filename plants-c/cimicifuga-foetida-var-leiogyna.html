Cimicifuga foetida var. leiogyna

Cimicifuga foetida var. leiogyna, commonly known as black cohosh, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features tall, unbranched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, covered with dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in late summer. Its roots and rhizomes are the primary parts used in traditional and modern herbal medicine.

It thrives in rich, moist soils and is often found in woodland areas.
