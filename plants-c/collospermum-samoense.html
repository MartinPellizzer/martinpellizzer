Collospermum samoense

Collospermum samoense, commonly known as the Samoan morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy blooms that open in the morning. Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
