Carex × microstachya

Carex × microstachya, commonly known as the narrowleaf marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-green leaves that are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne on slender, branching stems. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex × microstachya is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse ecosystems.
