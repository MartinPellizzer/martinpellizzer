Casuarina quadridentata

Casuarina quadridentata, commonly known as the coastal casuarina, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, needle-like foliage and distinctive cylindrical cones. The plant has adapted well to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. Its root system helps prevent soil erosion, making it ecologically significant in coastal regions.

Casuarina quadridentata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in reforestation projects.
