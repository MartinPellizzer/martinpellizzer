Cestrum melanochloranthum

Cestrum melanochloranthum, commonly known as the dark-chloranthum nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Solanaceae family.
