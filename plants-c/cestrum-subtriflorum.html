Cestrum subtriflorum

Cestrum subtriflorum, commonly known as the three-flowered nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring aromatic, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but should be consumed with caution. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a fragrant scent when crushed.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its nocturnal fragrance and vibrant blooms.
