Cyperus enervis

Cyperus enervis, commonly known as slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus enervis is often found in tropical and subtropical regions and is adapted to thrive in waterlogged soils.
