Caesalpinia broomensis

Caesalpinia broomensis, commonly known as the Broome glorybower, is a flowering plant native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in dense, branched clusters, often appearing in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are typically woody and somewhat thorny. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas with a warm climate.

Caesalpinia broomensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
