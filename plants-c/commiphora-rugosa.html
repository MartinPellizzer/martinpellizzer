Commiphora rugosa

Commiphora rugosa, commonly known as the African frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces a fragrant resin, which is harvested from its bark and used in traditional medicine and incense. It thrives in dry, tropical climates and is often found in savannah and woodland areas.

The resin of Commiphora rugosa has been used for centuries for its aromatic and therapeutic properties.
