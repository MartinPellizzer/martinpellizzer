Convolvulus insuavis

Convolvulus insuavis, commonly known as the bitter bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It is known for its strong, often unpleasant odor, which can be quite pungent.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar climbing or trailing habits.
