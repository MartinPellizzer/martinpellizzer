Cicercula sativa var. amphicarpos

Cicercula sativa var. amphicarpos, commonly known as the water clover, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its intricate, branching structure that resembles a network of leaves.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. The plant's leaves are typically small, broad, and arranged in a rosette pattern, allowing it to maximize light absorption in low-light conditions. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often floating on the water surface, and it produces small, greenish fruits that contain seeds.

This species plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
