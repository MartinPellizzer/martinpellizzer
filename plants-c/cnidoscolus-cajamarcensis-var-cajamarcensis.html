Cnidoscolus cajamarcensis var. cajamarcensis

Cnidoscolus cajamarcensis var. cajamarcensis, commonly known as the Andean arrowleaf, is a perennial shrub native to the highlands of Peru.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by spiny, capsule-like fruits. It is adapted to cool, humid climates and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
