Cyclamen utopicum

Cyclamen utopicum, commonly known as the Utopian cyclamen, is a rare and striking flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a deep green hue and produces showy, bowl-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil, making it a challenging species to cultivate. Its unique appearance and limited distribution have made it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Cyclamen utopicum is considered endangered in the wild.
