Crassula dejecta

Crassula dejecta, commonly known as the weeping crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that often hang down in a cascading fashion. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its compact size and attractive appearance, it is a popular choice for hanging baskets and rock gardens.
