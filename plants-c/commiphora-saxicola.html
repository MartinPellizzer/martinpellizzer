Commiphora saxicola

Commiphora saxicola, commonly known as the rock myrrh, is a small shrub native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, resinous sap, which has been used traditionally for medicinal and incense purposes. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, demonstrating a high degree of adaptability to harsh environments. The flowers are inconspicuous, and the plant produces small, woody fruits that contain a single seed.
