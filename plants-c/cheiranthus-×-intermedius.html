Cheiranthus × intermedius

Cheiranthus × intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate wallflower, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Cheiranthus cheiri and Cheiranthus ramosus.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe, characterized by its erect growth habit and showy, brightly colored flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring and early summer, producing racemes of flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. Its foliage consists of basal and stem leaves that are lanceolate and hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cheiranthus × intermedius is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
