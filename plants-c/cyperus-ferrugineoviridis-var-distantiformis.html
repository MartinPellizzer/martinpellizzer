Cyperus ferrugineoviridis var. distantiformis

Cyperus ferrugineoviridis var. distantiformis, commonly known as the rusty-green sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow to greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are often covered with a fine, rusty-colored pubescence, giving it its common name.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in stabilizing wetland habitats.
