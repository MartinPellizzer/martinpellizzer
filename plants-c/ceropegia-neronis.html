Ceropegia neronis

Ceropegia neronis, commonly known as the Natal moonviolet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its trailing stems and fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that are borne in clusters, often in the early morning. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
