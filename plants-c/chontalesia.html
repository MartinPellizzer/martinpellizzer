Chontalesia

Chontalesia, commonly known as the Mexican palm, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible fruits resembling cherries. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the soil in its native habitat.

Chontalesia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its fruit, which is used in local cuisines.
