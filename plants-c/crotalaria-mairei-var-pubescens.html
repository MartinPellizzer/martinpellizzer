Crotalaria mairei var. pubescens

Crotalaria mairei var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces vibrant yellow flowers with a distinctive crown-like structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Its hairy stems and leaves give it a unique texture, making it easily identifiable in its native environment.
