Calamagrostis lalesarensis

Calamagrostis lalesarensis, commonly known as the Lalesa reed, is a grass species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in mountain meadows and streamside habitats. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues.

Calamagrostis lalesarensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
