Chrysodium heudelotii

Chrysodium heudelotii, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble stars. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its inflorescence consists of several clustered flower spikes.

Chrysodium heudelotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
