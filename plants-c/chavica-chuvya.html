Chavica chuvya

Chavica chuvya, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Chavica chuvya is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
