Cirsium rhizocephalum subsp. sinuatum

Cirsium rhizocephalum subsp.

sinuatum, commonly known as the sinuous thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant features a robust root system and spiny, lobed leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually purple or pink, arranged in a dense cluster at the top of the stem.

This subspecies is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
