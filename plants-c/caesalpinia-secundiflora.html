Caesalpinia secundiflora

Caesalpinia secundiflora, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange feathery inflorescences that appear in spring. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets. Its flowers are often followed by long, narrow pods that contain seeds.

Caesalpinia secundiflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in landscaping.
