Caryospermum alpestre

Caryospermum alpestre, commonly known as the alpine morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the early morning, giving it its common name. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain seeds with a hard, dark coat. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Caryospermum alpestre is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
