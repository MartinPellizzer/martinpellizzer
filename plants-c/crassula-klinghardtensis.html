Crassula klinghardtensis

Crassula klinghardtensis, commonly known as the Kleinhardt's crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky outcrops and has a compact, shrub-like appearance. Its leaves are usually green with a slight bluish tint, and it produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white.

Crassula klinghardtensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.
