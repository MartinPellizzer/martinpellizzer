Carex saxatilis var. distans

Carex saxatilis var. distans, commonly known as the distant sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and gravelly habitats in temperate regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike of small, inconspicuous flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex saxatilis var. distans is often found in alpine or mountainous environments, where it contributes to the ecological stability of rocky ecosystems.
