Croton cnidophyllus var. dui

Croton cnidophyllus var. dui, commonly known as the dwarf croton, is a tropical shrub characterized by its striking, ornamental foliage.

The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a vibrant mix of green, yellow, and red hues, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in warm climates.
