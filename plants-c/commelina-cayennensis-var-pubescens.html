Commelina cayennensis var. pubescens

Commelina cayennensis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a prominent midrib and are often covered in fine pubescence.

The inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by bracts, with small, bisexual flowers that are pollinated by insects.
