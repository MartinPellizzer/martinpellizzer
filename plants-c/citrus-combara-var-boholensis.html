Citrus combara var. boholensis

Citrus combara var. boholensis, commonly known as Bohol citron, is a lesser-known citrus species native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange fruits that are typically harvested when they are still unripe. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental value. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, with a strong citrus scent.

This variety is often grown for its unique flavor and potential medicinal properties.
