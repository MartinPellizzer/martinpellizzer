Cyttaranthus

Cyttaranthus, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in tropical and subtropical regions. Cyttaranthus species are often found in coastal areas and can tolerate saline conditions.

Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.
