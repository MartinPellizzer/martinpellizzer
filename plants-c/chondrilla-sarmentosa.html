Chondrilla sarmentosa

Chondrilla sarmentosa, commonly known as the creeping sandmat, is a perennial flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces yellow flowers in the summer. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, helping to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils.

Chondrilla sarmentosa is often found in disturbed areas and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
