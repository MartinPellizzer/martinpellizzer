Campanula lusitanica var. puberula

Campanula lusitanica var. puberula, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive, slightly fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with a similar hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open woodland habitats.
