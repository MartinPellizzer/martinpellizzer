Corypha guineensis

Corypha guineensis, commonly known as the African palm, is a tropical plant native to West Africa.

It is a large, evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, which are essential for its reproductive process. Its fruit is a fibrous, spiky structure that contains a single seed, and it plays a role in local ecosystems and traditional practices.

The plant is also valued for its timber and has cultural significance in some regions.
