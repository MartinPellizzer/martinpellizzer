Cyperus rigens var. tarijensis

Cyperus rigens var. tarijensis, commonly known as the Tarijensis flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist soil conditions. Its slender, grass-like stems are often found in clusters, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to various aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
