Chamaesyce skottsbergii

Chamaesyce skottsbergii, commonly known as the Skottsberg's spurge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically green or reddish in color, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics reflect its adaptation to Mediterranean climates with dry summers and mild, wet winters.
