Cryptomeria japonica var. caespitosa

Cryptomeria japonica var. caespitosa, commonly known as the Japanese cedar dwarf form, is a compact variant of the Japanese cedar.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and needle-like leaves that are short and stiff. This variety is native to Japan and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. It is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value and hardiness.

The plant exhibits a slow growth rate, making it suitable for small garden spaces.
