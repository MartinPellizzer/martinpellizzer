Carex vesicaria subsp. flavescens

Carex vesicaria subsp.

flavescens, commonly known as the yellow bladder sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, flat leaves and compact, spicate inflorescences.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

The subspecies is distinguished by its yellowish coloration of the inflorescence and its habitat preferences.
