Cyanus lanceolatus

Cyanus lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

Cyanus lanceolatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its potential use in traditional medicine.
