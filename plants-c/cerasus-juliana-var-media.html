Cerasus juliana var. media

Cerasus juliana var. media, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or dwarf cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its small, compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental horticulture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible berries that are typically used in jams and preserves. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various climates.
