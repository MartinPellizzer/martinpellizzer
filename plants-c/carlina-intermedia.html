Carlina intermedia

Carlina intermedia, commonly known as the roughhead cudweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant features upright, hairy stems and serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically blue to purple in color, forming a cluster of small florets at the top of the stem.

Carlina intermedia is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it plays a role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
