Carmona heterophylla

Carmona heterophylla, commonly known as the variable leaf carmona, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The leaves of this plant are notable for their variability in shape and size, ranging from ovate to linear, which gives it its scientific name. It produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
