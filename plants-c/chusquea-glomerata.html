Chusquea glomerata

Chusquea glomerata, commonly known as the clustered bromeliad, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in countries like Peru and Bolivia. This plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a dense cluster, which helps collect water in its native arid environment. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a waxy surface that aids in water retention.

Chusquea glomerata produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often pink or red in color, adding ornamental value to its appearance.
