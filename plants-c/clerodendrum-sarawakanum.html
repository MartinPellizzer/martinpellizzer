Clerodendrum sarawakanum

Clerodendrum sarawakanum, commonly known as the Sarawak glorybower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and branching.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant produces small, dark berries as fruit.
