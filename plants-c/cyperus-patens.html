Cyperus patens

Cyperus patens, commonly known as the flatleaf sedges, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Cyperus patens produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
