Cynorkis formosa

Cynorkis formosa, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and a distinctive, elongated flower spike that bears multiple blooms. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in tropical rainforests. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Cynorkis formosa is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in traditional medicine.
