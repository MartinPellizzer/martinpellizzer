Carex fusca subsp. dacica

Carex fusca subsp.

dacica, commonly known as the dark sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, grasses, and rushes.

This subspecies is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and wet meadows.

The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in association with other moisture-loving flora.
