Chamaesyce multinodis

Chamaesyce multinodis, commonly known as the nodular sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features a cushion-like growth form with dense, hairy foliage that helps reduce water loss. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring.

Chamaesyce multinodis is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
