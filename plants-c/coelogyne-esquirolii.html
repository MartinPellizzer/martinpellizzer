Coelogyne esquirolii

Coelogyne esquirolii, commonly known as the Esquiroli's orchid, is a striking tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often red or orange flowers with intricate patterns, which are highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical and segmented, supporting the plant's growth and storage of nutrients.

Coelogyne esquirolii thrives in warm, humid environments with bright but indirect light, making it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.
