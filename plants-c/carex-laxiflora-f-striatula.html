Carex laxiflora f. striatula

Carex laxiflora f. striatula, commonly known as the striped sedge, is a variant of the slender sedge native to wetland and marsh environments.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often striped with contrasting colors, typically green and brown. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of inconspicuous flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones or along the edges of ponds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats.
