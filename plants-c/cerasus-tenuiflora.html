Cerasus tenuiflora

Cerasus tenuiflora, commonly known as the Japanese flowering cherry or dwarf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, often in shades of pink and white. The plant has small, oval leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Cerasus tenuiflora is also valued for its edible fruits, which are small, tart cherries used in traditional cuisines.
