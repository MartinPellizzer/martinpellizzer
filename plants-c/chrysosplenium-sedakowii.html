Chrysosplenium sedakowii

Chrysosplenium sedakowii, commonly known as the Sedakow's golden spleenwort, is a small, evergreen perennial plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant features delicate, opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in cymes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.
