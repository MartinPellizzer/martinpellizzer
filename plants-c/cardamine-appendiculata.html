Cardamine appendiculata

Cardamine appendiculata, commonly known as the appendiculate watercress, is a small herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Japan.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like mustard and cress. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its small white flowers are arranged in clusters.

The plant is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
