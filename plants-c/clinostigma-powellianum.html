Clinostigma powellianum

Clinostigma powellianum, commonly known as Powell's cliffrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, spiny stems and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
