Cirsium bigelowii

Cirsium bigelowii, commonly known as Bigelow's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, purple to blue flower heads. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces and serrated edges. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

The plant plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
