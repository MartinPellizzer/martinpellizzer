Convolvulus glomeratus var. sachalitarum

Convolvulus glomeratus var. sachalitarum, commonly known as the Sachalitarum bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers with five petals. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan.

This variant is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in dry, well-drained environments.
