Carex dioica f. isogyna

Carex dioica f. isogyna, commonly known as the smooth sedges, is a variant of the common sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in early summer.

The inflorescence consists of a spike of male and female flowers, with the female flowers often positioned lower on the plant.
