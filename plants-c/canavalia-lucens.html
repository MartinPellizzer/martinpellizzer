Canavalia lucens

Canavalia lucens, commonly known as the shining cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. Its pods are typically slender and contain edible seeds that are used in various cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
