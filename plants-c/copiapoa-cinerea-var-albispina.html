Copiapoa cinerea var. albispina

Copiapoa cinerea var. albispina, commonly known as the white spined cholla, is a cactus native to northern Chile.

It features a cylindrical stem covered in white, bristly spines that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its slow growth and specific care needs, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
