Cnidoscolus acrandrus

Cnidoscolus acrandrus, commonly known as the giant arrowhead or arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its roots are known to contain toxic compounds, which have been used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.
