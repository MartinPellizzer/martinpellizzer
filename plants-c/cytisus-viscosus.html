Cytisus viscosus

Cytisus viscosus, commonly known as sticky broom, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often thorny stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant has small, compound leaves that are often covered in a sticky resin, giving it its common name. It is typically found in open, sunny habitats such as coastal areas and rocky soils.

Cytisus viscosus is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
