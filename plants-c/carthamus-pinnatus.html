Carthamus pinnatus

Carthamus pinnatus, commonly known as the slender sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant produces both ray and disk florets, typical of the sunflower family, and has feathery, finely divided leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

The seeds of Carthamus pinnatus are also used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.
