Cerasus × chichibuensis var. aizuensis

Cerasus × chichibuensis var. aizuensis, commonly known as the Aizu cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal, particularly in gardens and as a decorative element in traditional Japanese landscapes. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture, and it produces small, dark red fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed.

The Aizu cherry is also notable for its cultural significance in Japanese horticulture and seasonal festivals.
