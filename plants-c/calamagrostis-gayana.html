Calamagrostis gayana

Calamagrostis gayana, commonly known as the silver reed, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and silvery-blue foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers that are often tinged with purple or brown.

Calamagrostis gayana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
