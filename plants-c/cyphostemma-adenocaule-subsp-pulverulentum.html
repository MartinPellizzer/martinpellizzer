Cyphostemma adenocaule subsp. pulverulentum

Cyphostemma adenocaule subsp.

pulverulentum, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa. It features slender, woody stems that twine around supports and bear small, pale green leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers followed by distinctive, fleshy, red berries that are an important food source for birds and mammals. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

Its ornamental value and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitat.
