Caladenia × hypata

Caladenia × hypata, commonly known as the Purple Sun-orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking purple and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in open forest or heathland environments. Its flowers are usually arranged in a spike and are pollinated by insects, particularly wasps.

Caladenia × hypata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
