Corchorus torresianus var. yunckeri

Corchorus torresianus var. yunckeri, commonly known as the yellow or red kudzu, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, spiky flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its fibrous stems have been traditionally used for making ropes and textiles.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in stabilizing soil.
