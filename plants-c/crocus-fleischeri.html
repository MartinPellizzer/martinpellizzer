Crocus fleischeri

Crocus fleischeri, commonly known as the Fleischer's crocus, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Iris family, Iridaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, early spring blooms. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a distinctive purple or brown center. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically appearing after the flowers have bloomed.

Crocus fleischeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early-season display.
