Cynanchum costantinianum

Cynanchum costantinianum, commonly known as the Costantine's dogbane, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a serrated edge.

The plant is known for its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds and is used in traditional medicine.
