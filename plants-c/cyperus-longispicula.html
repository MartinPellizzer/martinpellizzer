Cyperus longispicula

Cyperus longispicula, commonly known as the long-spined flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and small, clustered flowers. Cyperus longispicula typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as swamps and floodplains.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
