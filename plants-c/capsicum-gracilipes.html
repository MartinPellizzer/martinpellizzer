Capsicum gracilipes

Capsicum gracilipes, commonly known as the slender pepper or narrow-leaf chili, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant with slender, upright stems and small, ovate leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into elongated, slender fruits, which can range in color from yellow to orange or red depending on maturity. Its fruits are used in local cuisines for their mild to medium heat level and distinct flavor.

Capsicum gracilipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary versatility in its native habitat.
