Capparis brassii

Capparis brassii, commonly known as the African crossberry, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and large, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces striking yellow or orange flowers, followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are highly valued in local cuisines. Capparis brassii thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
