Cucumis melo convar. reticulatus

Cucumis melo convar. reticulatus, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a botanical variant of the melon genus characterized by its elongated, ribbed fruits.

It typically produces cucumbers with a pale green skin and a crisp, mild flavor, often used in fresh consumption or pickling. The plant is a vigorous vine that thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, facilitating pollination by insects.

The fruit develops from the female flowers and matures within 50 to 70 days after planting.
