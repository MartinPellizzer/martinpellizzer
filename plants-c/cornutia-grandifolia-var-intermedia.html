Cornutia grandifolia var. intermedia

Cornutia grandifolia var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate cornutia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive yellow or orange throat. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its stems are often hairy and slightly woody.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
