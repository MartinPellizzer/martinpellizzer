Carex lagopina var. pribylovensis

Carex lagopina var. pribylovensis, commonly known as the Pribylovsky sedge, is a variant of the Lagopine sedge native to specific regions of Eastern Europe.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like foliage and small, wind-pollinated flowers arranged in dense spikes.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity and maintaining wetland habitats.
