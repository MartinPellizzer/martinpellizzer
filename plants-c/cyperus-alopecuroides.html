Cyperus alopecuroides

Cyperus alopecuroides, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in clusters at the base of the plant. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus alopecuroides is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
