Cacalia frutescens

Cacalia frutescens, commonly known as the purpletop vervain, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Cacalia frutescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
