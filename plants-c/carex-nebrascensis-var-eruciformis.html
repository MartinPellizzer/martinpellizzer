Carex nebrascensis var. eruciformis

Carex nebrascensis var. eruciformis, commonly known as the Nebraska sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in prairies, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dense, compact growth pattern that helps it thrive in competitive environments.

The inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, three-ranked flowers, typical of the sedge family.
