Callista linguiformis

Callista linguiformis, commonly known as the tongue-shaped flax, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive tongue-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a composite head. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Callista linguiformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
