Cardamine schulziana

Cardamine schulziana, commonly known as Schulz's watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as along streams and in forest floors. The plant has slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with finely divided lobes. Its flowers are small, white, and arranged in racemes, typical of the mustard family.

The plant is known for its peppery taste and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
