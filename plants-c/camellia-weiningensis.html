Camellia weiningensis

Camellia weiningensis, commonly known as the Weining camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions with a subtropical climate, preferring well-drained acidic soils. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, and the flowers bloom in late winter to early spring.

Camellia weiningensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
