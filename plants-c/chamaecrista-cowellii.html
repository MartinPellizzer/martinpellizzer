Chamaecrista cowellii

Chamaecrista cowellii, commonly known as Cowell's burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding ornamental value to its habitat.

Chamaecrista cowellii is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
