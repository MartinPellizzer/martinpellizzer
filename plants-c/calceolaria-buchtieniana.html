Calceolaria buchtieniana

Calceolaria buchtieniana, commonly known as the Buchtienian slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant grows in rocky or mountainous areas and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Calceolaria buchtieniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its striking floral display.
