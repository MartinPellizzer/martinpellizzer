Cymbidium sanderi

Cymbidium sanderi, commonly known as the Sander's orchid, is a species of orchid native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is a sympodial orchid with a rhizomatous growth habit, producing pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant features long, strap-like leaves and produces inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. It blooms during the winter or early spring, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Cymbidium sanderi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens and greenhouses for its elegant appearance.
