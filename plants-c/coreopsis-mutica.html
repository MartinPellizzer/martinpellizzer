Coreopsis mutica

Coreopsis mutica, commonly known as the rough coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with a distinctive, rough texture on its stems and leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies adds ecological value to landscapes.
