Calliandra debilis

Calliandra debilis, commonly known as the weak or small calliandra, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and showy, pink or red flowers. The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in warm climates. It has a shallow root system and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Additionally, it can fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
