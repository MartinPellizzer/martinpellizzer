Cucumis melo var. naudinii

Cucumis melo var. naudinii, commonly known as the Armenian cucumber, is a cultivar of the melon plant characterized by its elongated, thin, and often ribbed fruit.

The plant belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia. Its leaves are broad and lobed, with a rough texture, while the stems are hairy and trailing. The fruit typically has a pale green skin with a waxy coating and a mild, sweet flavor.

It is cultivated for its unique shape and culinary uses in various regional cuisines.
