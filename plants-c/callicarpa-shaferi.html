Callicarpa shaferi

Callicarpa shaferi, commonly known as the dwarf beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, vibrant purple berries that appear in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn red in the autumn, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Callicarpa shaferi is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and unique fruit display.
