Chlorophytum guineense

Chlorophytum guineense, commonly known as the African spear lily, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its strap-shaped, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the warmer months. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

Chlorophytum guineense is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
