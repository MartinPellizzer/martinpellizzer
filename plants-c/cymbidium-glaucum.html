Cymbidium glaucum

Cymbidium glaucum, commonly known as the blue orchid, is a species of orchid native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy blue or violet flowers that bloom in winter or early spring. The plant has long, narrow leaves and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cymbidium glaucum is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique coloration.
