Cassia undulata

Cassia undulata, commonly known as the wavy cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features lance-shaped, serrated leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Cassia undulata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
