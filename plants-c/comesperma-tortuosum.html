Comesperma tortuosum

Comesperma tortuosum, commonly known as the spiral wattle, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and coastal dunes. The plant produces yellow flowers in cylindrical spikes, which bloom from late autumn to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems, giving it its common name.

The species is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities and ecological importance in soil enrichment.
