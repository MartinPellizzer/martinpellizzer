Cinnamomum piniodorum

Cinnamomum piniodorum, commonly known as the Philippine cinnamon or piniodor, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant typically grows in lowland to montane forests, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped, glossy, and have a distinct cinnamon-like aroma, while its flowers are small and greenish-white.

The tree is valued for its essential oils and has cultural significance in local communities.
