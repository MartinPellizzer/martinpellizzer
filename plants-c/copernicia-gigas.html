Copernicia gigas

Copernicia gigas, commonly known as the giant copernicia, is a species of palm native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by the development of coconut-like fruits. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts, and the plant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in coastal and wetland areas.

Copernicia gigas is also notable for its ability to thrive in saline environments, making it an important species in coastal ecosystems.
