Cytisus monspessulanus var. eriocarpus

Cytisus monspessulanus var. eriocarpus, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, often spiny stems and small, leathery leaves. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds, a characteristic typical of the Fabaceae family.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in Mediterranean climates.
