Costus dendrophilus

Costus dendrophilus, commonly known as the tree costus, is a tropical plant belonging to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences with bright yellow to orange flowers, which are followed by red or orange berries. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
