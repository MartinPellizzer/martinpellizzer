Cupaniopsis serrata var. genuina

Cupaniopsis serrata var. genuina, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaves and produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance. It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in local flora.
