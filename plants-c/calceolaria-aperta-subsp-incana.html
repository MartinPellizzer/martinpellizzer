Calceolaria aperta subsp. incana

Calceolaria aperta subsp.

incana, commonly known as the hairy calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to South America. It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, pouch-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant, tubular blooms.
