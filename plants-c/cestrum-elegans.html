Cestrum elegans

Cestrum elegans, commonly known as the white angel's trumpets, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

However, it contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially hazardous to humans and animals.
