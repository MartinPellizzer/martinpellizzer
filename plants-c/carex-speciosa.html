Carex speciosa

Carex speciosa, commonly known as the showy sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Carex speciosa has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces distinctive, showy spikelets that bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
