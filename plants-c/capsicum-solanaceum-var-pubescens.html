Capsicum solanaceum var. pubescens

Capsicum solanaceum var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent chili pepper, is a variant of the chili pepper plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its hairy, pubescent leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, and it produces small, often mild, chili fruits.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities as well as its culinary uses in some regions.
