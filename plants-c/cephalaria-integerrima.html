Cephalaria integerrima

Cephalaria integerrima, commonly known as the roughhead bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems covered in rough, hairy texture. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cephalaria integerrima is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade conditions.
