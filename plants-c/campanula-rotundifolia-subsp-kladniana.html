Campanula rotundifolia subsp. kladniana

Campanula rotundifolia subsp.

kladniana, commonly known as the Kladnica bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are rounded and often hairy, with a distinctive waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
