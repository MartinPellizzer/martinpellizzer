Chrysactinium erigeroides

Chrysactinium erigeroides, commonly known as the golden thread, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
