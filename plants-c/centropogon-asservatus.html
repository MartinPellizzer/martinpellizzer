Centropogon asservatus

Centropogon asservatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces achenes, which are small, dry fruits that disperse by wind.

Centropogon asservatus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
