Cactus atroruber

Cactus atroruber, commonly known as the black-red cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dark red to black spiny stems, which are covered in a thick, waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically short-lived but attract various pollinators. Its roots are deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in harsh desert conditions.

Cactus atroruber is well adapted to extreme heat and drought, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
