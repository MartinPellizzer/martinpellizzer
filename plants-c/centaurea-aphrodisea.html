Centaurea aphrodisea

Centaurea aphrodisea, commonly known as the Venus's Looking-glass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and spiky, bristly leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its inflorescence consists of a single, showy flower surrounded by a cluster of smaller, pale blue florets.

Centaurea aphrodisea is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand saline and drought conditions.
