Chione seminervis

Chione seminervis, commonly known as the slender snowbell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves and grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

Chione seminervis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
