Carex fendleriana

Carex fendleriana, commonly known as the Desert Spoon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its unique, spoon-shaped leaves give it a distinctive appearance, and it is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
