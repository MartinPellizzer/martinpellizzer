Claoxylon wightii var. hirsutum

Claoxylon wightii var. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy claoxylon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its rough, bark-like trunk and dense foliage. The leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a waxy texture with a distinct midrib. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
