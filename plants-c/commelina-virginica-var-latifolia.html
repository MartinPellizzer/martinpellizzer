Commelina virginica var. latifolia

Commelina virginica var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf commelina, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that support heart-shaped, broad leaves with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, blue-violet flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming from late spring to early fall. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and wetlands.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants.
