Capparis crotonantha

Capparis crotonantha, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis peruviana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, inflated calyx that resembles a small lantern. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into bright orange, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

The botanical features of Capparis crotonantha make it a unique and valuable species in both horticulture and ecology.
