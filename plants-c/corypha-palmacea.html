Corypha palmacea

Corypha palmacea, commonly known as the giant fan palm, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach several meters in diameter. The plant produces long, slender inflorescences bearing small, yellowish flowers. Its trunk is smooth and covered with a fibrous sheath, which gives it a distinctive appearance.

Corypha palmacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
