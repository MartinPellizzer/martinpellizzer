Chisocheton macrophyllus

Chisocheton macrophyllus, commonly known as the broad-leaf chisocheton, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Meliaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by elongated, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
