Calamagrostis × arundinacea

Calamagrostis × arundinacea, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, clump-forming grass native to Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and long, narrow leaves that are often bluish-green in color. This plant is known for its ability to thrive in wet or moist environments, making it a common sight in marshes and along stream banks. It produces feathery, golden-yellow plumes in the fall, adding ornamental value to gardens and natural landscapes.

Calamagrostis × arundinacea is also valued for its erosion control capabilities and use in bioengineering projects.
