Carex ornithopoda var. arcuata

Carex ornithopoda var. arcuata, commonly known as the curved sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland and marshy habitats.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, which help stabilize soil and provide habitat for various organisms.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, but the plant plays a vital role in water filtration and nutrient cycling.
