Crotalaria khasiana

Crotalaria khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern region of India.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
