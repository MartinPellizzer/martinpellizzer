Cissus bracteosa

Cissus bracteosa, commonly known as the African stonecrop, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by distinctive, fleshy, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and trellises.

Cissus bracteosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb various structures.
