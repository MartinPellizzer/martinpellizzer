Careya herbacea

Careya herbacea, commonly known as the mangrove apple or sea apple, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that thrives in saline and brackish water environments, often found in mangrove forests. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are typically leathery and dark green. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale yellow and are followed by edible, fleshy berries.

Careya herbacea plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
