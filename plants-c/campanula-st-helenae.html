Campanula st-helenae

Campanula st-helenae, commonly known as St. Helen's bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its root system consists of fibrous roots. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Campanula st-helenae is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
