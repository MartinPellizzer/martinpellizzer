Cerasus rhamnoides

Cerasus rhamnoides, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Prunus rhamnoides, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart drupes. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
