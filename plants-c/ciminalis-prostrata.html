Ciminalis prostrata

Ciminalis prostrata, commonly known as creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces tiny white or pink flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its dense growth habit makes it an excellent ground cover plant in gardens and landscapes.

Ciminalis prostrata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to various climates.
