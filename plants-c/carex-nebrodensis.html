Carex nebrodensis

Carex nebrodensis, commonly known as the Sicilian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often in rocky or gravelly habitats. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers.

Carex nebrodensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
