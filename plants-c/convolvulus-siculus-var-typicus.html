Convolvulus siculus var. typicus

Convolvulus siculus var. typicus, commonly known as the Sicilian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in the early morning. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This variety is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
