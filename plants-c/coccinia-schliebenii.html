Coccinia schliebenii

Coccinia schliebenii, commonly known as the Schlieben's false cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and pumpkins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers and oval, hairy fruits that are typically covered in spines. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a waxy surface, and the plant thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is often found in forest understories and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
