Cyathea fenicis

Cyathea fenicis, commonly known as the fire fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, providing a striking visual appearance.

Cyathea fenicis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to humid, shaded environments.
