Cerasus depressa

Cerasus depressa, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or Japanese dwarf cherry, is a species of shrub in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, edible berries that are dark red in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Cerasus depressa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in traditional Japanese gardens.
