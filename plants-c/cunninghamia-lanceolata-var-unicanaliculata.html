Cunninghamia lanceolata var. unicanaliculata

Cunninghamia lanceolata var. unicanaliculata, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed Chinese fir, is a variant of the Chinese fir native to southern China.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and distinctive, narrow, ribbed bark. The leaves are needle-like, dark green, and arranged in whorls, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in horticulture.

This variety is notable for its unique bark structure, which sets it apart from other Cunninghamia species.
