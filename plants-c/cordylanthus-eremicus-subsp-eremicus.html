Cordylanthus eremicus subsp. eremicus

Cordylanthus eremicus subsp.

eremicus, commonly known as the desert flax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Cordylanthus eremicus subsp.

eremicus is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert scrub habitats.
