Cucubalus mauritanicus

Cucubalus mauritanicus, commonly known as the Mauritania cucumber, is a climbing vine native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbit plants such as cucumbers and melons. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, white pubescence.

It is adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments and is often used in traditional medicine.
