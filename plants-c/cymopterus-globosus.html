Cymopterus globosus

Cymopterus globosus, commonly known as the globe globemallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features hairy, erect stems that bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in arid ecosystems.
