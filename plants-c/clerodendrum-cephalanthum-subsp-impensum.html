Clerodendrum cephalanthum subsp. impensum

Clerodendrum cephalanthum subsp.

impensum, commonly known as the white-flowered glorybower, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green.

The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer, giving it an ornamental appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a striking yet relatively low-maintenance plant.
