Capsella bursa-pastoris ramselensis

Capsella bursa-pastoris ramselensis, commonly known as the common chickweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its small, alternate leaves and clusters of white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, round seeds enclosed in a structure called a silique, which is typical of the mustard family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, fields, and along roadsides.
