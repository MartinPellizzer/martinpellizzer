Cucumis sativus var. setosus

Cucumis sativus var. setosus, commonly known as the hairy cucumber, is a variant of the common cucumber characterized by its rough, hairy surface.

This plant belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is typically cultivated for its edible, elongated fruits. The hairy texture of its skin is a distinguishing feature, setting it apart from smooth-skinned cucumber varieties. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is usually harvested when it reaches a mature size, though it may vary depending on the specific variety and growing conditions.
