Cassia hispidula var. fagonioides

Cassia hispidula var. fagonioides, commonly known as the hairy cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy and resilient species in its native habitat.
