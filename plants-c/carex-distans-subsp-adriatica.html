Carex distans subsp. adriatica

Carex distans subsp.

adriatica, commonly known as Adriatic sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along water margins.

Adriatic sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
