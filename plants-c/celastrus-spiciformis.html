Celastrus spiciformis

Celastrus spiciformis, commonly known as the spiciform staff-vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid environments and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
