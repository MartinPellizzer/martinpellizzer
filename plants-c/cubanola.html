Cubanola

Cubanola, commonly known as the Cuban olive, is a small evergreen tree native to Cuba.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, oval fruits that are typically harvested when ripe. Cubanola is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in Cuban culture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a subtropical climate to flourish.
