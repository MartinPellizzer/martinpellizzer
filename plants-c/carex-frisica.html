Carex frisica

Carex frisica, commonly known as the Frisian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to northern Europe and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. Its flowering stems produce small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers.

Carex frisica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
