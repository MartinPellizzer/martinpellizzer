Carex × axillaris

Carex × axillaris, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a hybrid sedge species native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wetlands, and streambanks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands.

Carex × axillaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
