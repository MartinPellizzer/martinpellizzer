Cota tinctoria var. discoidea

Cota tinctoria var. discoidea, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from violet to deep purple. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy blooms throughout the growing season. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its historical use in traditional medicine and dye production.
