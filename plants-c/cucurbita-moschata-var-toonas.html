Cucurbita moschata var. toonas

Cucurbita moschata var. toonas, commonly known as the Mexican buttercup squash, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to yellow skin and sweet, nutty-flavored flesh. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers typical of the genus Cucurbita. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
