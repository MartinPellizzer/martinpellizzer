Caladenia cardiochila

Caladenia cardiochila, commonly known as the Red-stalked Spider-orchid, is an orchid species native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and striking, spider-like flowers with a deep red lip. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs during the spring, producing delicate blooms that attract various pollinators.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and threatened species.
