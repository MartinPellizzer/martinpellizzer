Crotalaria longidens

Crotalaria longidens, commonly known as the longpod cowpea, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia.

Crotalaria longidens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a forage crop.
