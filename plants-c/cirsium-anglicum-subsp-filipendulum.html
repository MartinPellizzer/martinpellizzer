Cirsium anglicum subsp. filipendulum

Cirsium anglicum subsp.

filipendulum, commonly known as the lesser thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and spiny leaves.

The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a spherical cluster.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in many temperate regions.
