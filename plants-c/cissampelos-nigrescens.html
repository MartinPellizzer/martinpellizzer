Cissampelos nigrescens

Cissampelos nigrescens, commonly known as black bittersweet, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that contain the seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
