Carex kansuensis

Carex kansuensis, commonly known as the Kansu sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil.

Carex kansuensis is also used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
