Croton chocoanus

Croton chocoanus, commonly known as the Chocoan croton, is a tropical shrub native to the humid lowland forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful foliage, with leaves that can range from green to red, yellow, or even purple. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, moist environments and is often found in shaded undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
