Crinum paludosum

Crinum paludosum, commonly known as the swamp lily, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It features strap-like, lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, with a strong, fragrant scent. Crinum paludosum thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

It is a perennial species that blooms from late spring to early summer.
