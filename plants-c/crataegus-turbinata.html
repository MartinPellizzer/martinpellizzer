Crataegus turbinata

Crataegus turbinata, commonly known as the Turbinated hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, turbinate (turbulent) flower clusters, which are typically pink to white in color. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various birds and wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and open fields.

The leaves are usually ovate to elliptic with serrated edges, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
