Cardamine × insueta

Cardamine × insueta, commonly known as the hybrid buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. It produces a capsule fruit containing numerous tiny seeds.

Due to its hybrid nature, Cardamine × insueta exhibits a mix of traits from its parent species, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
