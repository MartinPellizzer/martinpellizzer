Carex pediformis var. macrosandra

Carex pediformis var. macrosandra, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, ribbon-shaped leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
