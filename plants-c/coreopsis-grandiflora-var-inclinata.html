Coreopsis grandiflora var. inclinata

Coreopsis grandiflora var. inclinata, commonly known as the leaning coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its growth habit is upright but often leans, giving it its common name.

It is a popular choice for gardens due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
