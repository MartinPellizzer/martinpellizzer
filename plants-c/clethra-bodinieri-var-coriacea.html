Clethra bodinieri var. coriacea

Clethra bodinieri var. coriacea, commonly known as the rough-leaved sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a rough, textured surface that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
