Chuquiraga sprengeliana f. paraguariensis

Chuquiraga sprengeliana f. paraguariensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan chuquiraga, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features soft, hairy stems and lobed, silvery-green leaves that are adapted to arid conditions. Its yellowish-orange flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often found in dry grasslands and scrub habitats, showcasing its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
