Cynosurus elegans subsp. effusus

Cynosurus elegans subsp.

effusus, commonly known as the silky bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It features fine, soft, and silky-textured leaves that are typically light green in color.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous spikelets that are usually arranged in dense, compact clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ecological value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
