Cincinalis chrysophylla

Cincinalis chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden leaf cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that are a striking golden-yellow color. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
