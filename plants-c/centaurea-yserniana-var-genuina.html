Centaurea yserniana var. genuina

Centaurea yserniana var. genuina, commonly known as the Ysern's cornflower, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. It produces small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
