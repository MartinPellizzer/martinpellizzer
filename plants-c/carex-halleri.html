Carex halleri

Carex halleri, commonly known as Haller's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-brown spikelets that contain tiny flowers. Native to the temperate regions of Europe and western Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively and colonize new areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
