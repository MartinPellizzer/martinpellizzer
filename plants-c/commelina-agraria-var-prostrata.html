Commelina agraria var. prostrata

Commelina agraria var. prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate Commelina, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous species characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with slender stems that often lie flat on the ground. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that are clustered in umbels, and its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a waxy surface. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed soils.
