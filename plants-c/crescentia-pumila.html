Crescentia pumila

Crescentia pumila, commonly known as the little horned cucumber, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces small, edible fruits resembling miniature cucumbers. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and is characterized by its yellowish-green, hairy stems. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blooms, and it relies on insect pollination.

Crescentia pumila thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist forest understories.
