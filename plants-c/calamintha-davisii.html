Calamintha davisii

Calamintha davisii, commonly known as Davis' germander, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its leaves are often fragrant and have a mint-like scent, contributing to its use in traditional herbal remedies.

Calamintha davisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
