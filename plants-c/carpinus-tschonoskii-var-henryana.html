Carpinus tschonoskii var. henryana

Carpinus tschonoskii var. henryana, commonly known as the Chinese hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, rounded samaras that are dispersed by wind. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
