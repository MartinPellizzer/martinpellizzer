Conoclinium greggii

Conoclinium greggii, commonly known as Gregg's hummingbird mint, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically bright yellow or orange, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
