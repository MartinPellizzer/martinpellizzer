Chrysophyllum philippense

Chrysophyllum philippense, commonly known as the Philippine cherimoya, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces fragrant, white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, creamy fruits with a sweet, custard-like texture. The fruit is typically ellipsoidal, with a thick, leathery rind and a segmented interior containing a soft, flavorful pulp.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and culinary uses in local cuisines.
