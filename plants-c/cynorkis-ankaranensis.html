Cynorkis ankaranensis

Cynorkis ankaranensis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and vibrant colors. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the island's rainforests. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract specific pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

Due to habitat loss, Cynorkis ankaranensis is considered endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
