Campylotropis falconeri

Campylotropis falconeri, commonly known as the Falconeri's false indigo, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain viable for several years, contributing to its persistence in harsh conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
