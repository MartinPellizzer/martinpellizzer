Canarium galokense

Canarium galokense, commonly known as the Galokense candlenut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, oval-shaped fruits containing a single seed. Its latex is used in traditional medicine and for making varnishes, while the seeds are sometimes used as a substitute for nutmeg.

The tree thrives in humid, lowland areas and plays a role in local ecosystems and cultural practices.
