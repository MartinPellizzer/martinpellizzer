Catasetum stenoglossum

Catasetum stenoglossum, commonly known as the narrow-lipped orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, and often pendulous flower spikes that bear showy, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are notable for their specialized structure, including a unique lip that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

The orchid's botanical features make it a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
