Calyptranthes brasiliensis

Calyptranthes brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces dark purple, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species but toxic to humans. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface.

Calyptranthes brasiliensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
