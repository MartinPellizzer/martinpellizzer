Ceratostylis sarcostomatoides

Ceratostylis sarcostomatoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, hooded flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with a distinctive hooded structure that protects the reproductive organs.

Ceratostylis sarcostomatoides is known for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
