Coleus linearifolius

Coleus linearifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved coleus, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, linear leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from deep red to vibrant orange. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in spikes at the ends of its stems. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Its foliage is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
