Cerasus diffusa

Cerasus diffusa, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or yuzen cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin, and the bark is smooth with a grayish hue.

Cerasus diffusa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
