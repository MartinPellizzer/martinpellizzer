Crepis tectorum

Crepis tectorum, commonly known as the common wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils, often along walls or in dry habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring.

Crepis tectorum is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
