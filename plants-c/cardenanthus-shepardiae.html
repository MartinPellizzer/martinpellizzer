Cardenanthus shepardiae

Cardenanthus shepardiae, commonly known as Shepard's bleeding heart, is a striking perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its pendulous, heart-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, with a distinctive central red or orange spot. The plant features slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its unique floral display and drought tolerance contribute to its appeal in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
