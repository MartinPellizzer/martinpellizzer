Cuphea pseudovaccinium

Cuphea pseudovaccinium, commonly known as the scarlet cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has upright, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract hummingbirds.
