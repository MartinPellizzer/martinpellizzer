Crepidocarpus

Crepidocarpus, commonly known as the pittosporum, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Pittosporaceae.

These plants are native to Australia and New Zealand, and they typically feature evergreen foliage with a variety of leaf shapes. The flowers are usually small and clustered, often in racemes or panicles, and they can be fragrant. Crepidocarpus species are valued for their ornamental appeal, with some varieties exhibiting attractive bark and colorful autumn foliage.
