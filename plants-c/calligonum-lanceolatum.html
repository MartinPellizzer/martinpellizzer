Calligonum lanceolatum

Calligonum lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it well-suited for harsh environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deep underground.

Calligonum lanceolatum plays an important role in stabilizing soils and preventing erosion in degraded landscapes.
