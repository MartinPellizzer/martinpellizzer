Chondrosea valida

Chondrosea valida, commonly known as the rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are usually small, leathery, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

Chondrosea valida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and vibrant floral display.
