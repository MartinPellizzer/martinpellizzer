Carex divulsa var. virens

Carex divulsa var. virens, commonly known as the green sedge, is a perennial plant native to moist, temperate regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically forms dense clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often a vibrant green. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered along the stems.

Carex divulsa var. virens is often used in wetland restoration and as a ornamental plant in water gardens.
