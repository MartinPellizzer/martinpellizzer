Crataegus collinsiana

Crataegus collinsiana, commonly known as Collins hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 feet, featuring thorned branches and oval, dark green leaves. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
