Campomanesia malifolia

Campomanesia malifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian star-apple or pitahaya, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits with a sweet, tropical flavor.

Its fruit is often used in juices, desserts, and as a natural sweetener due to its high content of natural sugars and nutrients.
