Cactus hernandezii

Cactus hernandezii, commonly known as the Mexican cactus or the red cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically ribbed and covered with spines. The plant produces vibrant red flowers during the spring and summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert environments.

Cactus hernandezii is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant in arid landscapes.
