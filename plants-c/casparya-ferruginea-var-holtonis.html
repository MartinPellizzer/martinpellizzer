Casparya ferruginea var. holtonis

Casparya ferruginea var. holtonis, commonly known as the Holton's ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features upright stems covered in rusty-brown hairs. The plant produces small, pale purple to pink flowers in dense, spike-like inflorescences during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Casparya ferruginea var. holtonis is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
