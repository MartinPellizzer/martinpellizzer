Chaetospora microstachya

Chaetospora microstachya, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight ridge or prominence on the upper surface.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
