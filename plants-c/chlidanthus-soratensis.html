Chlidanthus soratensis

Chlidanthus soratensis, commonly known as the Soratensis Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, columnar stems and clusters of vibrant yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant has a shallow root system adapted to absorb moisture from the surface after rare rainfall events. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help minimize water loss and deter herbivores.

Chlidanthus soratensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various desert animals.
