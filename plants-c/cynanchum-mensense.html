Cynanchum mensense

Cynanchum mensense, commonly known as the white milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia, including China and Russia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its roots contain milky sap, which is a distinguishing feature of the genus Cynanchum.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
