Chrysanthemum oltense

Chrysanthemum oltense, commonly known as the Olt Valley chrysanthemum, is a perennial herb native to Romania.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy and erect.

Chrysanthemum oltense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
