Calamus albidus

Calamus albidus, commonly known as the white calamus, is a tropical plant belonging to the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is characterized by its long, slender rhizomes and broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces an inflorescence known as a spadix, which is surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Calamus albidus is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of streams in Southeast Asia.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
