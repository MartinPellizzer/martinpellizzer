Carex atrata subsp. atratiformis

Carex atrata subsp.

atratiformis, commonly known as the black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants.

This subspecies is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of brownish flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
