Crataegus greggiana

Crataegus greggiana, commonly known as Gregg’s hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southwestern United States, particularly found in Texas and New Mexico. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant often grows as a shrub or small tree.

Gregg’s hawthorn is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
