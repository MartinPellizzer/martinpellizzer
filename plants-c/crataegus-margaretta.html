Crataegus margaretta

Crataegus margaretta, commonly known as Margaret's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, elliptical leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils and full sun to partial shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
