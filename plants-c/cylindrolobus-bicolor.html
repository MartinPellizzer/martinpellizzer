Cylindrolobus bicolor

Cylindrolobus bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored cylinder vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are small and usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters along the stems.

The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature.
