Culcita blepharodes

Culcita blepharodes, commonly known as the blushing bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Culcita blepharodes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
