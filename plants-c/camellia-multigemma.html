Camellia multigemma

Camellia multigemma, commonly known as the snow camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its striking white, waxy flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in USDA hardiness zones 7 through 9, preferring partial shade and well-drained soil.

Camellia multigemma is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant floral display.
