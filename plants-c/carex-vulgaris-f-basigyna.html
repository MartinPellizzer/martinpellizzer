Carex vulgaris f. basigyna

Carex vulgaris f. basigyna, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually broad and flat, with a smooth surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense spikes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
