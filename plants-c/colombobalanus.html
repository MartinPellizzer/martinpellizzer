Colombobalanus

Colombobalanus, commonly known as the Caribbean mangrove, is a species of mangrove tree native to the coastal regions of the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its salt-tolerant adaptations, allowing it to thrive in brackish water environments. The plant has pneumatophores, which are specialized root structures that facilitate gas exchange in oxygen-poor soil. Its leaves are typically small and thick, reducing water loss in saline conditions.

Colombobalanus plays a crucial role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
