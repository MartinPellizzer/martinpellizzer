Coffea kapakata

Coffea kapakata, commonly known as the Kapakata coffee.

It is a species of coffee native to Tanzania, characterized by its small, red berries that contain the coffee beans. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

The beans are usually processed using the wet method, resulting in a distinct flavor profile that is often described as fruity and acidic.
