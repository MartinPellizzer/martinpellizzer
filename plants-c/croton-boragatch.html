Croton boragatch

Croton boragatch, commonly known as the red croton, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that range in color from green to red, depending on the season and light exposure. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
