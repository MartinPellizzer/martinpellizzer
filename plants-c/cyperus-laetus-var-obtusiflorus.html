Cyperus laetus var. obtusiflorus

Cyperus laetus var. obtusiflorus, commonly known as the broadflower sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features slender, triangular stems and produces small, clustered spikelets with pale green to brown flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly rough texture.

This variety is adapted to wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or along water bodies.
