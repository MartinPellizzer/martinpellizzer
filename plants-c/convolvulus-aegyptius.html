Convolvulus aegyptius

Convolvulus aegyptius, commonly known as Egyptian bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are usually pale yellow to reddish in color. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Egyptian bindweed is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its invasive nature.
