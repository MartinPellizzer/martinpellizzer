Crepis smithiana

Crepis smithiana, commonly known as the Smith's mouse-ear.

It is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, belonging to the Asteraceae family. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, and its leaves are hairy and lobed.

Crepis smithiana is often found in open habitats and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
