Carex salina f. corylorum

Carex salina f. corylorum, commonly known as salt marsh sedge, is a variant of the salt marsh sedge species native to coastal regions.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, sword-shaped leaves. This plant thrives in saline and brackish wetlands, often found in tidal areas and estuaries. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of small, three-ranked flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated.

Carex salina f. corylorum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
