Cyperus xerophilus

Cyperus xerophilus, commonly known as the desert flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Cyperus xerophilus is often found in disturbed areas and can be an indicator of dry, sandy environments.
