Capsicum annuum var. ceratocarpum

Capsicum annuum var. ceratocarpum, commonly known as the bird pepper or yellow bird pepper, is a variant of the common chili pepper.

It is characterized by its small, elongated fruits that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The plant is an annual herb native to the Americas and thrives in warm, sunny climates. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, and the fruits develop from these flowers after pollination.

The plant is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and, to a lesser extent, for its edible fruits.
