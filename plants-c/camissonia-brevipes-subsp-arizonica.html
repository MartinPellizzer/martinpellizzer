Camissonia brevipes subsp. arizonica

Camissonia brevipes subsp.

arizonica, commonly known as Arizona dwarf flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is a low-growing perennial with slender, hairy stems and small, blue to purple flowers.

The species is characterized by its narrow leaves and ability to thrive in arid conditions. It typically blooms in spring and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

This subspecies is well adapted to the harsh desert environment, showcasing features that enhance its survival in dry climates.
