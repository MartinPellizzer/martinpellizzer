Cleome heterotricha

Cleome heterotricha, commonly known as the spider flower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that resemble spider legs. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Cleome heterotricha is often found in disturbed soils and thrives in full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
