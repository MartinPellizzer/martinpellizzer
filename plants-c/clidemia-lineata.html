Clidemia lineata

Clidemia lineata, commonly known as the creeping marshplant, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with slender stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that are typically clustered in the axils of the leaves. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a velvety texture.

Clidemia lineata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy areas and along stream banks.
