Cucurbita melopepo var. pileiformis

Cucurbita melopepo var. pileiformis, commonly known as the giant bottle gourd, is a variant of the Cucurbita melopepo species.

It is characterized by its large, elongated, bottle-shaped fruits that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant is a vigorous vine that thrives in warm climates and requires full sun and well-draining soil. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and it produces yellow flowers that are typically separate from the fruiting ones.

This variety is often cultivated for its unique fruit shape and historical use in traditional crafts and cooking.
