Crotalaria camisassae

Crotalaria camisassae, commonly known as the Camisassa crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features palmate leaves with three to five leaflets and produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry to moderately moist conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
