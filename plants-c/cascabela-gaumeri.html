Cascabela gaumeri

Cascabela gaumeri, commonly known as the Mexican cascabela, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture, while its fruits are fleshy berries that contain several seeds.

Cascabela gaumeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
