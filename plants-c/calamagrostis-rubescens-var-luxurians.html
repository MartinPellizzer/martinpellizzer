Calamagrostis rubescens var. luxurians

Calamagrostis rubescens var. luxurians, commonly known as the crimson moor grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and dense, feathery panicles that display a striking reddish hue, especially in autumn. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in water features and bog gardens.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are typically reddish-brown, contributing to its visual appeal throughout the growing season.
