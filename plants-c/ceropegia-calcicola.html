Ceropegia calcicola

Ceropegia calcicola, commonly known as the limestone ceropegia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found growing on rocky outcrops and limestone formations. The plant features fleshy, tubular stems and leaves that are adapted to store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. Its flowers are typically small and pale, with a unique structure that facilitates pollination by specific insects.

Ceropegia calcicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in succulent gardens.
