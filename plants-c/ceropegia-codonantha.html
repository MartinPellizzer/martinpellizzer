Ceropegia codonantha

Ceropegia codonantha, commonly known as the silver leaf or moonvine, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, tubular leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a unique, intricate structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Ceropegia codonantha is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and resilience in dry conditions.
