Capparis danielii

Capparis danielii, commonly known as the Daniel's caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, green berries that mature to a dark red color. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

Capparis danielii is known for its ability to thrive in rocky and coastal environments.
