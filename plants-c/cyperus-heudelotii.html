Cyperus heudelotii

Cyperus heudelotii, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and linear, with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are often white or pale green, arranged in a spiky inflorescence. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along water edges.

Cyperus heudelotii is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various water conditions.
