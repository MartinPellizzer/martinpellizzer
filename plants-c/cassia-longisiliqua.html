Cassia longisiliqua

Cassia longisiliqua, commonly known as the long pod senna, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, slender leaflets and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Cassia longisiliqua is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
