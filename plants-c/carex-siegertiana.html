Carex siegertiana

Carex siegertiana, commonly known as Siegert's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands, where it thrives in well-drained soil. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, produce small, clustered flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Carex siegertiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
