Coppensia bifolia

Coppensia bifolia, commonly known as the two-leaved lily, is a striking member of the Melanthiaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in wetland and marshy environments. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink. Its blooming period typically occurs in early spring, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Coppensia bifolia is often found in riparian zones and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
