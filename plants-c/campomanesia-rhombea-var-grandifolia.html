Campomanesia rhombea var. grandifolia

Campomanesia rhombea var. grandifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree or *Pequi*, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into elongated, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its botanical characteristics make it adapted to humid, warm climates with well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible seeds.
