Calendula thapsiaecarpa

Calendula thapsiaecarpa, commonly known as the Greater Celandine, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant features a rough, branched stem and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color. Its flowers are typically bright yellow, with a central disk that is darker in hue.

Calendula thapsiaecarpa is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and skin-soothing effects.
