Carphephorus coridifolius

Carphephorus coridifolius, commonly known as the fragrant grass or sweetgrass, is a member of the Poaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that emit a pleasant aroma when crushed. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the use of its aromatic leaves in traditional practices.
