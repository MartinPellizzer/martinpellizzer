Cordia coloradiphila

Cordia coloradiphila, commonly known as the Colorado cordia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, shrub-like species with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that split open to release seeds.

Cordia coloradiphila is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.
