Cousinia tamarae

Cousinia tamarae, commonly known as the Kashmiri cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing adaptations to cold and arid conditions. Its leaves are basal, hairy, and often arranged in a rosette pattern, while its flowers are small and yellowish.

Cousinia tamarae plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators in its native range.
