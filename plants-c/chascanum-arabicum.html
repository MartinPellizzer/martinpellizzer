Chascanum arabicum

Chascanum arabicum, commonly known as the sweet basil, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic and fragrant. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for both culinary and ornamental use.

Chascanum arabicum is widely cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in cooking, perfumery, and traditional medicine.
