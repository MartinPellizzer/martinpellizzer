Clematis fusijamana

Clematis fusijamana, commonly known as the Japanese clematis, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a hairy texture. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Clematis fusijamana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
