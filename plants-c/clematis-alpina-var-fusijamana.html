Clematis alpina var. fusijamana

Clematis alpina var. fusijamana, commonly known as the Alpine Clematis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or lilac. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with finely toothed margins, and it thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to cooler, high-altitude environments.
