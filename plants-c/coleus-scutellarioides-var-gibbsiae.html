Coleus scutellarioides var. gibbsiae

Coleus scutellarioides var. gibbsiae, commonly known as Gibbs' coleus, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of red, orange, and purple, making it highly sought after for garden use. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and typically features square stems and opposite leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it ideal for shaded garden beds or containers.

Its ornamental value is primarily due to its foliage, as it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.
