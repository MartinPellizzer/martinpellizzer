Cuphea longiflora f. minor

Cuphea longiflora f. minor, commonly known as the dwarf flame flower, is a compact, flowering shrub native to Mexico.

It features vibrant, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value lies in its striking color and unique flower structure.
