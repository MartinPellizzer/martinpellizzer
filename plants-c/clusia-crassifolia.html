Clusia crassifolia

Clusia crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by orange to red berries. Clusia crassifolia is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments and its tolerance to poor soil conditions.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and resilience.
