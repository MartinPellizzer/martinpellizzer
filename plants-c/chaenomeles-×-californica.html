Chaenomeles × californica

Chaenomeles × californica, commonly known as the California crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to California.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or red, though they are not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.
