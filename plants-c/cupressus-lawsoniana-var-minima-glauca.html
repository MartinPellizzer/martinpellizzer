Cupressus lawsoniana var. minima-glauca

Cupressus lawsoniana var. minima-glauca, commonly known as the Lawson's cypress, is a compact, slow-growing evergreen conifer native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves that are a bluish-green color, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish flowers followed by cone-like structures that release seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental value, it is often used in gardens and as a specimen plant.
