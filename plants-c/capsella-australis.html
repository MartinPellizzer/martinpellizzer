Capsella australis

Capsella australis, commonly known as the Australian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release seeds, a feature typical of the Brassicaceae family. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of native flora.
