Chrysosplenium wrightii subsp. saxatile

Chrysosplenium wrightii subsp.

saxatile, commonly known as the rock chrysosplenium, is a low-growing perennial herb native to rocky, alpine environments in North America. It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are usually white or pale yellow, producing nectar that attracts pollinators.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in crevices or on rocky slopes.
