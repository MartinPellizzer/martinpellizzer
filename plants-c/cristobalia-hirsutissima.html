Cristobalia hirsutissima

Cristobalia hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy cristobal, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dry seeds. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy or limestone soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
