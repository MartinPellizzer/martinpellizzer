Crotalaria unicaulis

Crotalaria unicaulis, commonly known as the single-stem cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with a single stem, which is often branched towards the top. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.

Crotalaria unicaulis is also noted for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
