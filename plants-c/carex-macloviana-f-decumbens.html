Carex macloviana f. decumbens

Carex macloviana f. decumbens, commonly known as the decumbent sedge, is a variant of the species Carex macloviana, characterized by its prostrate growth habit.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually inconspicuous, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

This ornamental plant is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for small aquatic organisms.
