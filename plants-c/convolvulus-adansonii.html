Convolvulus adansonii

Convolvulus adansonii, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.
