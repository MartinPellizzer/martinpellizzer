Carex lucorum

Carex lucorum, commonly known as the common sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along water edges. The plant has triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-brown spikelets that contain numerous tiny flowers. It typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and is characterized by its ability to thrive in moist to wet soil conditions.

Carex lucorum plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
