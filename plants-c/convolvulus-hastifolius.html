Convolvulus hastifolius

Convolvulus hastifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, violet to blue flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is an annual or biennial herb that spreads through its twining stems and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
