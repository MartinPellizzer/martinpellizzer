Cordia bahamensis

Cordia bahamensis, commonly known as the Bahamian cordia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean, particularly the Bahamas.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by oval, woody fruits. Its bark is thick and often fissured, and it can grow up to 5 meters in height.

Cordia bahamensis is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a common species in dune ecosystems.
