Colania

Colania, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are typically found in arid and rocky environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh conditions. They produce small, often fragrant flowers that are usually yellow or greenish in color. Colania species are characterized by their unique inflorescence and specialized root systems that help them survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Their botanical significance lies in their role in ecological systems and their value in horticultural cultivation.
