Caragana decorticans var. hissarica

Caragana decorticans var. hissarica, commonly known as the Hissar thornstep, is a shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems, small compound leaves, and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces legume pods containing seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its hardy nature makes it suitable for erosion control in degraded soils.
