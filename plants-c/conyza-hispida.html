Conyza hispida

Conyza hispida, commonly known as roughleaf fleabane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small yellow flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along fence rows. Its flowers bloom from summer to fall, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Conyza hispida is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
