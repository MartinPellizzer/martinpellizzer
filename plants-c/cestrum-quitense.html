Cestrum quitense

Cestrum quitense, commonly known as the night-blooming jasmine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface.

Cestrum quitense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its nocturnal blooms.
