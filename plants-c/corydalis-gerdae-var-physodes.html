Corydalis gerdae var. physodes

Corydalis gerdae var. physodes, commonly known as the pink corydalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features slender, hairy stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowers have a distinctive spurred petal, a characteristic feature of the Corydalis genus.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its charming floral display.
