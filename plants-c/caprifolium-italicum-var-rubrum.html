Caprifolium italicum var. rubrum

Caprifolium italicum var. rubrum, commonly known as the red honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe.

It features fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to deep red, depending on the variety. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical characteristics include opposite leaves with serrated edges and a woody stem structure typical of the Caprifoliaceae family.
