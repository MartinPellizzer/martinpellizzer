Cuphea campestris var. brachystemon

Cuphea campestris var. brachystemon, commonly known as the woolly cuphea, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, tubular red or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its unique appearance and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees add both aesthetic and ecological value to the environment.
