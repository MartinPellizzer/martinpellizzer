Chamaeraphis setosa var. vulpiseta

Chamaeraphis setosa var. vulpiseta, commonly known as fox tail grass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, feathery inflorescence resembling a fox's tail. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its unique appearance makes it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitats.
