Ceratolobus laevigatus var. regularis

Ceratolobus laevigatus var. regularis, commonly known as the smooth-flowered ceratolobus, is a member of the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow to greenish in color. The leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate and slightly hairy. This plant is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia and is adapted to low-nutrient soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for livestock in its native habitat.
