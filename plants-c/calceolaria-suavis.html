Calceolaria suavis

Calceolaria suavis, commonly known as the sweet calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in the spring and early summer.

Calceolaria suavis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
