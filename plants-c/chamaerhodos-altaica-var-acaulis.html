Chamaerhodos altaica var. acaulis

Chamaerhodos altaica var. acaulis, commonly known as the slender fireweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are typically narrow and linear, adapted to cold and harsh environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and rocky habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is a resilient species that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native ecosystems.
