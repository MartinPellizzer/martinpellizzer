Cyperus consors var. riedelianus

Cyperus consors var. riedelianus, commonly known as the swamp cyperus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically brown or reddish in color. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, making it a common sight in swampy areas and along water bodies.

Its adaptability to moist conditions makes it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
