Centaureopappus × hybridus

Centaureopappus × hybridus, commonly known as the hybrid blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its striking blue to purple flower color. It produces fluffy, pappus-bearing seeds that aid in wind dispersal, a common adaptation in arid environments.

Centaureopappus × hybridus is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
