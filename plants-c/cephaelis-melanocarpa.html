Cephaelis melanocarpa

Cephaelis melanocarpa, commonly known as black-flowered celandine, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, dark purple to black flowers. Its flowers are usually borne on slender, wiry stems and bloom during the monsoon season.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
