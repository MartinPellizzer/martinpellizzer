Carduus onopordioides subsp. hajastanicus

Carduus onopordioides subsp.

hajastanicus, commonly known as the Hajastani thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing a robust root system and a tall, sparsely leafed stem.

The plant produces purple to pinkish composite flower heads, which are composed of numerous small florets. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough, hairy texture, and the species is characterized by its distinctive, spiny bracts.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
