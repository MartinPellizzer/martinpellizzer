Cousinia buphthalmoides

Cousinia buphthalmoides, commonly known as the eye-bulb saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions.

Cousinia buphthalmoides is often found in alpine and steppe regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
