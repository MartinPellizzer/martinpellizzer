Cyperus odoratus var. curtispiculus

Cyperus odoratus var. curtispiculus, commonly known as the sweetgrass or fragrant flatsedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often green or reddish in color. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in wet or moist soil conditions. It is often found in marshy areas, along riverbanks, and in disturbed sites.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for ornamental and aromatic uses in horticulture.
