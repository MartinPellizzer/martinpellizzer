Consolida schlagintweitii

Consolida schlagintweitii, commonly known as the Afghan sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds, and its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for gardens in temperate climates.

The flowers typically bloom in spring and summer, adding a vibrant touch to the landscape.
