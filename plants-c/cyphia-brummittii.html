Cyphia brummittii

Cyphia brummittii, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, segmented stems and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in the winter months. The plant belongs to the family Cactaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Its leaves are usually modified into spines, and it produces bright red, pink, or orange flowers.

Cyphia brummittii is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
