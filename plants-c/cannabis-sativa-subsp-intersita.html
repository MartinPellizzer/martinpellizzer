Cannabis sativa subsp. intersita

Cannabis sativa subsp.

intersita, commonly known as the European hemp variety, is a dioecious plant characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow leaves. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in temperate climates.

This subspecies is notable for its low THC content and high fiber yield, making it suitable for industrial applications. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often appearing in clusters at the nodes of the stems.

The plant's rapid growth and adaptability contribute to its historical use in agriculture and textile production.
