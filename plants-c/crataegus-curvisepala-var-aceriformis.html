Crataegus curvisepala var. aceriformis

Crataegus curvisepala var. aceriformis, commonly known as the maple-leaved hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive maple-like leaves, which are ovate to elliptic in shape with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits, supporting local wildlife.
