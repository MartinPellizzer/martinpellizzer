Clausena loheri

Clausena loheri, commonly known as the African yellow-wood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers followed by orange to red berries that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal forests and secondary growth areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
