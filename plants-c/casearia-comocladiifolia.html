Casearia comocladiifolia

Casearia comocladiifolia, commonly known as the golden casearia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, red berries that are edible. Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticulture.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
