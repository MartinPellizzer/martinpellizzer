Clematis chiisanensis

Clematis chiisanensis, commonly known as the Japanese clematis, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are heart-shaped and have a soft, velvety texture.

Clematis chiisanensis prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental foliage and blooms.
