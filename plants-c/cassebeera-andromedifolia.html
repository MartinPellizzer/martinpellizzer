Cassebeera andromedifolia

Cassebeera andromedifolia, commonly known as the red-veined flame tree, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a contrasting yellow or orange center. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also known for its rapid growth and ability to tolerate various soil types.
