Cassine orientalis

Cassine orientalis, commonly known as the oriental boxwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Asia.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that provides a compact, ornamental shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by velvety, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for hedging and landscape design.

Cassine orientalis is also valued for its aromatic leaves and historical use in traditional medicine.
