Calceolaria glacialis

Calceolaria glacialis, commonly known as the glacier calceolaria, is a delicate alpine plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that resemble tiny cups, often in shades of white, pink, or purple. The plant thrives in cold, high-altitude environments, typically growing in rocky soils and alpine meadows. Its leaves are usually hairy and adapted to retain heat and moisture in harsh conditions.

Calceolaria glacialis is a member of the Campanulaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in botanical gardens.
