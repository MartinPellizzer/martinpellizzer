Caprifolium chrysanthum

Caprifolium chrysanthum, commonly known as the golden honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 6 feet tall, producing red berries that are edible for birds and small mammals.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
