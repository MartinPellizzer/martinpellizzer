Cyathea robinsonii

Cyathea robinsonii, commonly known as the Robinson's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a distinctive crown of fronds that can reach several meters in length, giving it a lush, tropical appearance. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting its growth in moist, shaded environments.

Cyathea robinsonii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
