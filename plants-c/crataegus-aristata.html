Crataegus aristata

Crataegus aristata, commonly known as the prickly pear or the thorned hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

The botanical features of Crataegus aristata make it both ecologically significant and aesthetically pleasing in natural landscapes.
