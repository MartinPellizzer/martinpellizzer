Cissampelopsis buimalia

Cissampelopsis buimalia, commonly known as the white-veined ivy.

It is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves with prominent white veins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
