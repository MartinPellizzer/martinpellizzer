Carum petroselinum

Carum petroselinum, commonly known as parsley, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and umbel-shaped flowers. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are used both as a culinary herb and a garnish. Its flowers are small, white or greenish, and attract pollinators such as bees.

Parsley is also valued for its medicinal properties and is often grown in gardens for both ornamental and practical purposes.
