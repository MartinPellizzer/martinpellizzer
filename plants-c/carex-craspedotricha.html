Carex craspedotricha

Carex craspedotricha, commonly known as the rough-fringed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically brown or reddish in color, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.

Carex craspedotricha is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
