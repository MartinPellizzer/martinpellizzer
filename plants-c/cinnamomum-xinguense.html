Cinnamomum xinguense

Cinnamomum xinguense, commonly known as the Xingu cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries containing seeds. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves and bark, are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
