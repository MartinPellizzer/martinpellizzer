Cerastium erectum var. linneanum

Cerastium erectum var. linneanum, commonly known as Linne's prostrate mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its mat-forming growth habit with numerous small, hairy stems. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
