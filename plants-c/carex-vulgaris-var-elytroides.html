Carex vulgaris var. elytroides

Carex vulgaris var. elytroides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences of greenish-yellow flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, fens, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex vulgaris var. elytroides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
