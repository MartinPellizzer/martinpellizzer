Cardia didyma

Cardia didyma, commonly known as the double-flowered cardia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, double flowers with both ray and disk florets, giving it a lush, ornamental appearance. Cardia didyma typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
