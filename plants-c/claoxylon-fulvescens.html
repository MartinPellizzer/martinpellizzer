Claoxylon fulvescens

Claoxylon fulvescens, commonly known as the yellow ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has a straight, cylindrical trunk with smooth, light brown bark that often develops a reddish tint as it ages. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.
