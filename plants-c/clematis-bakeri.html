Clematis bakeri

Clematis bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, appearing in late summer. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires support for its vigorous growth.

Clematis bakeri is valued for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
