Cardamine tenella var. quercetorum

Cardamine tenella var. quercetorum, commonly known as the oakleaf buttercup, is a small herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks, preferring rich, loamy soils. The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves that resemble oak leaves, hence its common name. Its flowers are bright yellow and consist of five petals arranged in a cup-like shape.

It is often found in association with oak and other hardwood trees, contributing to the biodiversity of forest understories.
