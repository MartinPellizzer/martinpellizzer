Clerodendrum ramosissimum var. barbatum

Clerodendrum ramosissimum var. barbatum, commonly known as the hairy clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and profuse display of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The flowers are typically clustered in panicles, making it a visually striking addition to gardens.
