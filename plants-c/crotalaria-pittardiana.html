Crotalaria pittardiana

Crotalaria pittardiana, commonly known as the Pittard's spurred cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and compound leaves. Its yellow flowers are followed by pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Crotalaria pittardiana is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
