Coursetia caribaea var. caribaea

Coursetia caribaea var. caribaea, commonly known as the Caribbean coursetia, is a shrub native to the Caribbean region.

It typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are yellowish-green and appear in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh coastal environments.
