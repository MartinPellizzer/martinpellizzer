Cassia procumbens

Cassia procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Cassia procumbens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
