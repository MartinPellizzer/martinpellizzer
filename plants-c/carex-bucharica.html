Carex bucharica

Carex bucharica, commonly known as the Bukharan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually wind-pollinated.

Carex bucharica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
