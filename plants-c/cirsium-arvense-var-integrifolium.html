Cirsium arvense var. integrifolium

Cirsium arvense var. integrifolium, commonly known as the smooth field thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, spiny, purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are broadly lanceolate, with a smooth margin and a rough, hairy surface. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields, roadsides, and wastelands.

This plant is known for its aggressive growth and ability to form dense colonies, making it a common weed in many regions.
