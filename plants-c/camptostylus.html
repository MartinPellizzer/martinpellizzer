Camptostylus

Camptostylus, commonly known as the flame acanthus, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color, arranged in dense clusters. The leaves are usually opposite and have a velvety texture, often with a distinct veining pattern. Native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, these plants are often cultivated for their ornamental value.

They thrive in moist, well-drained soils and require partial to full sun exposure.
