Cleome austroarabica

Cleome austroarabica, commonly known as the African spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Cleomaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiky flowers that resemble spider legs. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Cleome austroarabica is cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
