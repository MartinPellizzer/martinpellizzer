Chenopodium ambiguum var. minus

Chenopodium ambiguum var. minus, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in disturbed soils and arid environments. The plant has slender, erect stems with reddish hues, and its leaves are ovate to lanceolate with a slightly hairy surface. It produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

This species is often considered a weed but has some historical uses in traditional medicine.
