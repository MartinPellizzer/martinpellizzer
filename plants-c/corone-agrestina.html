Corone agrestina

Corone agrestina, commonly known as the field crownflower, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in early summer.

Corone agrestina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
