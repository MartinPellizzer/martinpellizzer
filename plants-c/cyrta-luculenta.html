Cyrta luculenta

Cyrta luculenta, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Cyrta luculenta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable climates.
