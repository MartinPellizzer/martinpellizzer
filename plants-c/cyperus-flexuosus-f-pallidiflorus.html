Cyperus flexuosus f. pallidiflorus

Cyperus flexuosus f. pallidiflorus, commonly known as the pale-flowered flatsedge, is a variant of the Cyperus genus characterized by its distinctive, pale-colored flowers.

It is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions, typically found in wetland or marshy environments. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. Its botanical significance lies in its ecological role within aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.

The pale floral coloration distinguishes it from other Cyperus species and contributes to its ornamental value in wetland gardens.
