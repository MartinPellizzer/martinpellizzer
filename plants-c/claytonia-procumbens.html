Claytonia procumbens

Claytonia procumbens, commonly known as the moss pink, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating called farina. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and moist, shady landscapes.
