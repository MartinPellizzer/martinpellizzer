Cereus pectinatus f. rufispinus

Cereus pectinatus f. rufispinus, commonly known as the red spiny cactus, is a popular ornamental plant native to South America.

It is a hybrid of Cereus pectinatus and Cereus rufispinus, characterized by its tall, columnar stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant features sharp, red-tipped spines that give it its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Its striking appearance makes it a favorite among cactus enthusiasts and indoor gardeners.
