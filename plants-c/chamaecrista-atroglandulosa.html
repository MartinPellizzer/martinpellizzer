Chamaecrista atroglandulosa

Chamaecrista atroglandulosa, commonly known as black glandular crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in dense, upright clusters, which are typical of the genus Chamaecrista. It has small, hairy stipules and possesses glandular structures on its stems and leaves, which are distinctive features.

This species is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
