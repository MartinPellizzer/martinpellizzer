Cheilanthes austrotenuifolia

Cheilanthes austrotenuifolia, commonly known as the slender fringed rock fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant has a compact, creeping rhizome with small, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its sporangia are clustered on the underside of the fronds, a characteristic feature of many ferns.

This species is found primarily in southern Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils and sunny exposures.
