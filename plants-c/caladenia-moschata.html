Caladenia moschata

Caladenia moschata, commonly known as the musk orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and a single, lance-shaped leaf. The flower spike bears one to three fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive musk-like scent. The petals and sepals are narrow and pointed, while the labellum is broad and notched.

This species is often found in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils.
