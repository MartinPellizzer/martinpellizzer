Cassine laurifolia

Cassine laurifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf cassine, is a deciduous tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests or near water bodies.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ornamental horticulture and ecological studies.
