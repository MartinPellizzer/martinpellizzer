Cattleya irrorata

Cattleya irrorata, commonly known as the White Lady, is a tropical orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white petals and a yellow lip, which give it a striking and elegant appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are known for their strong, sweet fragrance.

Cattleya irrorata is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
