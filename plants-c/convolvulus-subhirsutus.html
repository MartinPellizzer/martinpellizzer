Convolvulus subhirsutus

Convolvulus subhirsutus, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are usually pale blue to purple in color. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
