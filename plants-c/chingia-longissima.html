Chingia longissima

Chingia longissima, commonly known as the long-stemmed beauty, is a rare flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It features elongated, slender stems that can reach several feet in length, supporting clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment. It blooms during the cooler months, producing fragrant, pale yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, Chingia longissima is considered a valuable species in botanical research and conservation efforts.
