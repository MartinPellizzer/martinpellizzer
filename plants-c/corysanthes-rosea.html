Corysanthes rosea

Corysanthes rosea, commonly known as the rose sandalwood, is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is native to Australia.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its lance-shaped, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often with a faintly fragrant scent. Its wood is highly valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional crafts and incense.

Corysanthes rosea thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.
