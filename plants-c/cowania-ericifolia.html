Cowania ericifolia

Cowania ericifolia, commonly known as the heather cowania, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, while its flowers are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Cowania ericifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
