Cephalaria squamiflora subsp. balearica

Cephalaria squamiflora subsp.

balearica, commonly known as the Balearic squamiflora cephalaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balearic Islands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaf structures.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, thriving in coastal and mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually lanceolate or ovate, with a hairy texture, and its flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
