Carex aquatilis f. sciaphila

Carex aquatilis f. sciaphila, commonly known as the shade-loving sedge, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas or along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly and form dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization.

This variety is particularly adapted to low-light conditions, making it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects in shaded wetlands.
