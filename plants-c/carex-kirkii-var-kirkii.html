Carex kirkii var. kirkii

Carex kirkii var. kirkii, commonly known as the Kirk's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or damp habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and swamps. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many sedges.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
