Convolvulus subglobosus

Convolvulus subglobosus, commonly known as the globular morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual with a compact, globular growth habit, often forming a dense, rounded cluster of leaves and flowers. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color, blooming throughout the warmer months. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often trailing or ascending.

Convolvulus subglobosus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
