Camelina pilosa

Camelina pilosa, commonly known as the hairy camelina, is an annual plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many other economically important plants such as mustard and cabbage. Camelina pilosa is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
