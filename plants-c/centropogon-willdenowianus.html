Centropogon willdenowianus

Centropogon willdenowianus, commonly known as the willdenow's fireplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red or orange tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly hummingbirds, due to their shape and color.
