Citrus juko

Citrus juko, commonly known as the Japanese mandarin, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, round, orange-colored fruits with a thin, aromatic peel. The plant has glossy dark green leaves and bears fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The botanical characteristics of Citrus juko make it a valuable species in both horticulture and agriculture.
