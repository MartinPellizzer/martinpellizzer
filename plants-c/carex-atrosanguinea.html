Carex atrosanguinea

Carex atrosanguinea, commonly known as black blood sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

It is characterized by its dark reddish-brown stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Carex genus. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat preferences, it is a valuable plant for wetland restoration and ecological studies.
