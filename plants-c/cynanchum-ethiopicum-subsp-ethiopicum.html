Cynanchum ethiopicum subsp. ethiopicum

Cynanchum ethiopicum subsp.

ethiopicum, commonly known as Ethiopian milkweed, is a perennial herb native to Ethiopia and surrounding regions. It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, characterized by its milky sap and showy flowers.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, featuring succulent stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with a distinctive corona surrounding the reproductive structures.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
