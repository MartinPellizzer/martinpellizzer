Crataegus mira

Crataegus mira, commonly known as the fairy hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its small, thorned branches and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Native to parts of Asia, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical medicinal uses.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to gardens and ecological landscapes.
