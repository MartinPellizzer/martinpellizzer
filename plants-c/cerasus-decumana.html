Cerasus decumana

Cerasus decumana, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Prunus decumana, is a species of flowering shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly its showy pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. It produces small, edible fruits known as cherries, which are typically red or dark purple when mature.

Cerasus decumana is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
