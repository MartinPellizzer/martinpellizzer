Castanea vesca var. domestica

Castanea vesca var. domestica, commonly known as the common horse chestnut.

It is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe and western Asia. The plant is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, white or pinkish flowers that appear in late spring. Its fruit is a spiny capsule containing one or more chestnuts, which are edible after removing the bitter husk.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
