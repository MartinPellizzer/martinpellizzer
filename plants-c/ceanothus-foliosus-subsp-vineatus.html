Ceanothus foliosus subsp. vineatus

Ceanothus foliosus subsp.

vineatus, commonly known as the coastal ceanothus, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California and southern Oregon. It is characterized by its aromatic, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has slender, often hairy stems and small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with frequent fog and mild temperatures.

This subspecies is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.
