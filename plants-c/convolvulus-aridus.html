Convolvulus aridus

Convolvulus aridus, commonly known as desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has small, heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing several seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry environments with deep roots and reduced leaf surface.

Convolvulus aridus is often found in sandy soils and can thrive with minimal water.
