Cyperus nigripes var. grandiceps

Cyperus nigripes var. grandiceps, commonly known as the tall purple flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically brown or purple in color. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found in disturbed soils near water bodies.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptability make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
