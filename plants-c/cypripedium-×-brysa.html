Cypripedium × brysa

Cypripedium × brysa, commonly known as the hybrid lady's slipper orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cypripedium calceolus and Cypripedium × morganianum.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its showy, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and mountainous regions. Its leaves are oval and mid-green, and it produces a single, large flower per stem.

Cypripedium × brysa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
