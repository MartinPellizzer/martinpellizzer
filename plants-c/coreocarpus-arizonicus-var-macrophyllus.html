Coreocarpus arizonicus var. macrophyllus

Coreocarpus arizonicus var. macrophyllus, commonly known as the Arizona coreocarpus, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features woody stems and lanceolate, dark green leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive, fleshy, red berries. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in xerophytic plant communities.
