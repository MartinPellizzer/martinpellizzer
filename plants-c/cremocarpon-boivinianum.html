Cremocarpon boivinianum

Cremocarpon boivinianum, commonly known as the Boivin's cup-plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers arranged in composite heads. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cremocarpon boivinianum is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along the edges of ponds, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
