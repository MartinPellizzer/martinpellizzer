Cassia singueana var. glabra

Cassia singueana var. glabra, commonly known as African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red inflorescences that appear in late summer. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often trifoliate, with a smooth surface due to the "glabra" variety name. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for warm climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
