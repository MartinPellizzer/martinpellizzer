Chosenia eucalyptoides

Chosenia eucalyptoides, commonly known as the giant watergrass, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length, often floating on the water surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in slow-moving or still freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes.

Its rhizomes are fibrous and help stabilize the sediment in its habitat.
