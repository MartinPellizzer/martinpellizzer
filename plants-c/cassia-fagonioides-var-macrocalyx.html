Cassia fagonioides var. macrocalyx

Cassia fagonioides var. macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that consist of clustered, tubular flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
