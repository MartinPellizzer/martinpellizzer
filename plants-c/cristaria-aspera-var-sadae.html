Cristaria aspera var. sadae

Cristaria aspera var. sadae, commonly known as the Sadae's rough lily, is a rare and striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its robust, fibrous rhizomes and striking, often multicolored flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are broad, strap-like, and often have a waxy texture, while the flowers are borne on tall, erect stems.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
