Caesalpinia anacantha

Caesalpinia anacantha, commonly known as the red thorn apple, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive red, spiny pods. The plant produces small yellow flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its seeds are encased in hard, woody pods that split open when mature, releasing the seeds.

Caesalpinia anacantha is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
