Cyperus trinervis var. laxus

Cyperus trinervis var. laxus, commonly known as the loose-veined flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinct veining pattern, and its inflorescence consists of small, clustered spikes. It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist soil conditions.

The species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
