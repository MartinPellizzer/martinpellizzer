Cacalia alliariifolia

Cacalia alliariifolia, commonly known as the garlic leaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal leaves that are narrow and resemble garlic leaves, hence its common name. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Cacalia alliariifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in moderate climates.
