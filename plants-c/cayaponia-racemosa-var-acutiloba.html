Cayaponia racemosa var. acutiloba

Cayaponia racemosa var. acutiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cayaponia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in racemes. Its botanical features make it suitable for ornamental purposes in warm climates.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.
