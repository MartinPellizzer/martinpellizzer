Carex supina var. korshinskyi

Carex supina var. korshinskyi, commonly known as the Korshinsky's sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variant is typically found in wet or moist environments such as marshes and along stream banks. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or brown, and it thrives in temperate climates with consistent moisture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
