Corylopsis microcarpa

Corylopsis microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf flame bush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that turn reddish in autumn. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Corylopsis microcarpa is often used in garden landscapes for its ornamental value and hardiness.
