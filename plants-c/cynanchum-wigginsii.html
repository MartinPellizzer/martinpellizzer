Cynanchum wigginsii

Cynanchum wigginsii, commonly known as Wiggins' milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes other milkweeds, and is characterized by its milky sap. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are usually pink to purple in color, attracting various pollinators.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting native insect populations.
