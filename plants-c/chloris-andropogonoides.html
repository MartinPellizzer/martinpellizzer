Chloris andropogonoides

Chloris andropogonoides, commonly known as slender grass or blue grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, bluish-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
