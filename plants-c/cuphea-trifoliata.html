Cuphea trifoliata

Cuphea trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, trifoliate (three-leaf) leaves that are oval and slightly hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Cuphea trifoliata is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to add color to gardens throughout the growing season.
