Clematis vitalba var. argentilucida

Clematis vitalba var. argentilucida, commonly known as the silverleaf clematis, is a variant of the common clematis that exhibits distinctive silver-white variegation on its foliage.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its slender, twisting stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of white, pink, or lilac. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This cultivar is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or structures.
