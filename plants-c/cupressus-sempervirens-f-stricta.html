Cupressus sempervirens f. stricta

Cupressus sempervirens f. stricta, commonly known as the Italian cypress, is a cultivar of the Mediterranean cypress.

It is characterized by its narrow, columnar growth habit, making it a popular choice for formal garden designs. The plant features scale-like leaves that are dark green and densely packed, giving it a dense, upright appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by woody cones.

This cultivar is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid and coastal climates.
