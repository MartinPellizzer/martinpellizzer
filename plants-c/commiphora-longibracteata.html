Commiphora longibracteata

Commiphora longibracteata, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, resinous bark and leaves that are opposite and elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by dark reddish-brown, woody fruits. Its resin, known as myrrh, has been used traditionally for its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Commiphora longibracteata thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry woodlands and rocky habitats.
