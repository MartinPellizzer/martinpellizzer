Campanula × arrecta

Campanula × arrecta, commonly known as the upright bellflower, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically reaches heights of 2 to 4 feet and features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Campanula × arrecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
