Calliandra trinervia var. arborea

Calliandra trinervia var. arborea, commonly known as the tree calliandra, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its branching structure and compound leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the dry season. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

The leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture.
