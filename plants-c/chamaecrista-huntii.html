Chamaecrista huntii

Chamaecrista huntii, commonly known as the yellow cassia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compound leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woods and along roadsides.

Chamaecrista huntii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
