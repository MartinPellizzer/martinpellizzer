Cheiridopsis pachyphylla

Cheiridopsis pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved ice plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the winter months, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise rugged appearance. Its growth habit is typically low and spreading, forming a compact mat of foliage.

Cheiridopsis pachyphylla is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun exposure, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
