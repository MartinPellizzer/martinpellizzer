Chironia baccifera var. burchellii

Chironia baccifera var. burchellii, commonly known as the Burchell's blackwood, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy bark and small, elliptical leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by black, berry-like fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
