Cyperus polystachyos var. miser

Cyperus polystachyos var. miser, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a flowering plant in the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.
