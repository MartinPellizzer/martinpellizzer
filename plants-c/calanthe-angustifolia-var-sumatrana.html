Calanthe angustifolia var. sumatrana

Calanthe angustifolia var. sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran narrow-leaved calanthe, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive color pattern. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
