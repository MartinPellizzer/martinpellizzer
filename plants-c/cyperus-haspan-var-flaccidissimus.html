Cyperus haspan var. flaccidissimus

Cyperus haspan var. flaccidissimus, commonly known as the flaccid swamp cyperus, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is typically found in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are often green or reddish in color. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and its tolerance to fluctuating water levels.

This variant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological importance and adaptability.
