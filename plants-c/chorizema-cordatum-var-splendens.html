Chorizema cordatum var. splendens

Chorizema cordatum var. splendens, commonly known as the splendour heartleaf, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy, glossy surface that reflect light, giving it a shimmering appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its foliage. Its succulent leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native arid environment.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
