Centaurea chrysocephala

Centaurea chrysocephala, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that resemble a crown, which are surrounded by spiny bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Centaurea chrysocephala is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
