Crosperma angustifolia

Crosperma angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf crosswort, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grassy areas and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white or pale blue flowers are clustered in dense racemes, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

Crosperma angustifolia is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in semi-arid conditions.
