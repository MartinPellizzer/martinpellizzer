Croton borhidii subsp. borhidii

Croton borhidii subsp.

borhidii, commonly known as the Borhidii croton, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful, and varied leaf morphology, which can range from green to red or purple.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are usually alternate and can be either simple or lobed, depending on the specific variety.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to different growing conditions.
