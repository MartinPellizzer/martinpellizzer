Campanula sporadum

Campanula sporadum, commonly known as the sporadic bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. Campanula sporadum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.

It is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
