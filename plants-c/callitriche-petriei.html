Callitriche petriei

Callitriche petriei, commonly known as the Petrie's water star, is a small aquatic plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant has slender, branching stems with small, opposite leaves that are usually submerged. Its flowers are tiny, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, often floating on the water surface.

Callitriche petriei plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
