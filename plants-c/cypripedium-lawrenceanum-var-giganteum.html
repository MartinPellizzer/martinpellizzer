Cypripedium lawrenceanum var. giganteum

Cypripedium lawrenceanum var. giganteum, commonly known as the giant lady's slipper orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in Vietnam and Laos. This orchid is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch, which is a key feature of the Cypripedium genus. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats and has thick, fleshy roots adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is highly sought after in horticulture and is listed as endangered in many regions.
