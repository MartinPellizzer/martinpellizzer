Cuphea polymorpha var. vincoides

Cuphea polymorpha var. vincoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive, velvety appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cuphea polymorpha var. vincoides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
