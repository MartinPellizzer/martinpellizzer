Centaurea czerepanovii

Centaurea czerepanovii, commonly known as the Czerepanov's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, arid environments. Its bright blue to purple flowers are often surrounded by pale yellow ray florets, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Centaurea czerepanovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
