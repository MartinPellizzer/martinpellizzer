Cyphostemma camerounense

Cyphostemma camerounense, commonly known as the Cameroon moon vine, is a climbing plant native to central and western Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by fleshy, red or orange berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical studies.
