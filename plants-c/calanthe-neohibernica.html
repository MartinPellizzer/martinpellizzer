Calanthe neohibernica

Calanthe neohibernica, commonly known as the Hibernian calanthe, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinct, three-lobed lip. It thrives in cool, shaded environments with high humidity.

Calanthe neohibernica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
