Calamus castaneus

Calamus castaneus, commonly known as the chestnut sedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often found in temperate regions. The plant has triangular stems that are usually dark brown to reddish in color, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-lobed flowers, which are typically greenish or brownish.

Calamus castaneus plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
