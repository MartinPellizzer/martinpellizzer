Corispermum hyssopifolium var. papillosum

Corispermum hyssopifolium var. papillosum, commonly known as the woolly bladder campion, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive, inflated seed pods that resemble small bladders.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise rugged appearance. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.

This variety is notable for its unique morphology and ecological resilience in harsh environments.
