Crataegus monogyna var. poloniensis

Crataegus monogyna var. poloniensis, commonly known as the Polish hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red berries in the autumn, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
