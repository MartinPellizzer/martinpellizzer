Caladenia lateritica

Caladenia lateritica, commonly known as the laterite orchid, is a species of orchid native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and striking, spurred flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant grows in sandy or lateritic soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it is adapted to survive in harsh environmental conditions.

Caladenia lateritica is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its unique floral structure and ecological significance.
