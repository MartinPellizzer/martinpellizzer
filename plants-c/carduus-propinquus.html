Carduus propinquus

Carduus propinquus, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a serrated edge, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant is known for its ability to spread rapidly through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
