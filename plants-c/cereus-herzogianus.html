Cereus herzogianus

Cereus herzogianus, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its segmented, leaf-like stems that store water. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically during the holiday season. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Cereus herzogianus is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
