Croton thorelii

Croton thorelii, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful foliage, with leaves that can range from green to red, orange, or yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal, it is widely cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
