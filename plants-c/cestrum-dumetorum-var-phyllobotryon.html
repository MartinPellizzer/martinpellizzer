Cestrum dumetorum var. phyllobotryon

Cestrum dumetorum var. phyllobotryon, commonly known as the "Whiteweed" or "Chamomile Trumpet," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a strong, unpleasant odor. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, elliptical seeds.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in various herbal remedies.
