Carex blepharicarpa var. dueensis

Carex blepharicarpa var. dueensis, commonly known as the Due's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often found in areas with high humidity and well-drained soil.
