Centaurium umbellatum f. dichroum

Centaurium umbellatum f. dichroum, commonly known as the dichroum form of the lesser centaury, is a variant of this flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered flowers. The foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and a distinctive central boss.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
