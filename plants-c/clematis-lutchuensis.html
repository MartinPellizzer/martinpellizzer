Clematis lutchuensis

Clematis lutchuensis, commonly known as the Lutchu Clematis, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of China, Japan, and the Philippines.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its slender stems and showy, often fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in cymes, and its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Clematis lutchuensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
