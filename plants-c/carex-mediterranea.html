Carex mediterranea

Carex mediterranea, commonly known as Mediterranean sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along water edges. Its leaves are grass-like, arranged in dense tufts, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Carex mediterranea plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
