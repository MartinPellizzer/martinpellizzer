Carex setosa var. setosa

Carex setosa var. setosa, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling.

Carex setosa var. setosa is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in wetland environments.
