Carex salina var. tristigmatica

Carex salina var. tristigmatica, commonly known as salt marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions.

It thrives in saline and brackish wetlands, often found in marshes and tidal flats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. Its spike-like inflorescence contains small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
