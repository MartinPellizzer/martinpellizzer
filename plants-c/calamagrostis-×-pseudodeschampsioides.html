Calamagrostis × pseudodeschampsioides

Calamagrostis × pseudodeschampsioides, commonly known as the silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-tipped foliage and upright growth habit, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant produces fluffy, silver-gray seed heads in the fall, adding visual interest to the landscape. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its grass-like appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and erosion control.
