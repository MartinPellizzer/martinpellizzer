Carex thunbergii var. pallidisquama

Carex thunbergii var. pallidisquama, commonly known as the pale-squamate sedgeland sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically pale green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
