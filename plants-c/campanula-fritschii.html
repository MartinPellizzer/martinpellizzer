Campanula fritschii

Campanula fritschii, commonly known as the Fritsch's bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Campanula fritschii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
