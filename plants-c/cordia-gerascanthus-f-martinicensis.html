Cordia gerascanthus f. martinicensis

Cordia gerascanthus f. martinicensis, commonly known as the Martinique cordia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its fruit is a fleshy, reddish berry that contains several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
