Caleana brockmanii

Caleana brockmanii, commonly known as the pinkhooded orchid, is a striking member of the orchid family native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, brightly colored labellum that resembles a bee, which plays a crucial role in attracting pollinators. The plant typically grows in sandy soils and is often found in heathland environments. Its flowering period occurs during the cooler months, producing clusters of pink and white flowers.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
