Caesarea albiflora

Caesarea albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking white, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has silver-gray foliage and is adapted to salty, windy environments, making it a hardy species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found growing along the edges of dunes or in rocky coastal areas.

Caesarea albiflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to withstand harsh coastal conditions.
