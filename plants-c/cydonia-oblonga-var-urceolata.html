Cydonia oblonga var. urceolata

Cydonia oblonga var. urceolata, commonly known as the Indian date palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers, which develop into edible, sweet, and fibrous fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its fruit, which is used in various culinary applications.
