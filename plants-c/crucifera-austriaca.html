Crucifera austriaca

Crucifera austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian crucifer, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds. Native to Central Europe, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
