Campanula carpatica var. turbinata

Campanula carpatica var. turbinata, commonly known as the Turban bellflower, is a compact, flowering perennial native to Central Europe.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
