Centaurea babylonica

Centaurea babylonica, commonly known as the Babylonian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiky, purple or pink flower heads that resemble those of other members of the Asteraceae family. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, often with a bluish or grayish tint.

Centaurea babylonica is known for its ornamental value and has been cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
