Citrus × aurantium hystrix

Citrus × aurantium hystrix, commonly known as the hystrix orange, is a hybrid citrus plant originating from Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, rough, and spiny rind, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are dark green, glossy, and opposite, while the fruit is typically small, round, and has a thick, segmented peel. This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and fragrant flowers.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.
