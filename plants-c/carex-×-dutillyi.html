Carex × dutillyi

Carex × dutillyi, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically forms dense clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes, which are usually borne on short, erect stems.

Carex × dutillyi is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in moist to wet soil conditions.
