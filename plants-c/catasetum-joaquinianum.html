Catasetum joaquinianum

Catasetum joaquinianum, commonly known as the Joaquin's orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) native to Central America.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a specialized column that plays a role in pollination. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or green with red or purple markings, and it blooms during the dry season.

Catasetum joaquinianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
