Convolvulus piloselloides

Convolvulus piloselloides, commonly known as the hairy knotweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and sunny environments. The plant features small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are hairy and often twining, which is characteristic of the Convolvulaceae family.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
