Chaenactis imbricata

Chaenactis imbricata, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Chaenactis imbricata is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access water.
