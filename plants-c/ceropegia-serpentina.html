Ceropegia serpentina

Ceropegia serpentina, commonly known as the snake's head or snake plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and tubular, waxy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia serpentina is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
