Ceropegia dryophila

Ceropegia dryophila, commonly known as the rock ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and small, fleshy leaves that are adapted to arid environments. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, attracting specific pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
