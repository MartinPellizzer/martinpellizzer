Cianitis

Cianitis, commonly known as the bluebell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Boraginaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Cianitis has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

It blooms in early spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to forest floors.
