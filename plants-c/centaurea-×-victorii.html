Centaurea × victorii

Centaurea × victorii, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers and spiny bracts, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is native to southern Europe. It has a bushy growth habit with upright stems and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Centaurea × victorii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its showy blooms.
