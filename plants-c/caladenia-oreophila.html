Caladenia oreophila

Caladenia oreophila, commonly known as the mountain spider-orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to southern Australia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in open woodlands and heathlands. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a single, showy orchid-like flower that is usually pink or pale purple in color. Its floral structure is characterized by a narrow, spurred labellum and elongated petals.

Caladenia oreophila is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its delicate and ephemeral blooms.
