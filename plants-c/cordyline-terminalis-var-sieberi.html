Cordyline terminalis var. sieberi

Cordyline terminalis var. sieberi, commonly known as the silver cordyline, is a striking ornamental plant native to New Zealand.

It features upright, sword-shaped leaves that are usually a mix of green and silver, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in summer, followed by red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its hardy nature and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for landscaping and indoor cultivation.
