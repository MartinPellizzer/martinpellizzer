Canistrum flavipetalum

Canistrum flavipetalum, commonly known as the golden petal currant, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruits are red, fleshy berries that contain seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Canistrum flavipetalum is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and full sun exposure.
