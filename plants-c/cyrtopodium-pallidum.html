Cyrtopodium pallidum

Cyrtopodium pallidum, commonly known as the pale cyrtopodium, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It produces delicate, pale green to white flowers that bloom during the summer months. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Cyrtopodium pallidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
