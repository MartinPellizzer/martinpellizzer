Cyathocalyx osmanthus

Cyathocalyx osmanthus, commonly known as the Osmanthus flower, is a species native to East Asia, particularly China.

It is a member of the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, small yellow or golden flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine. Its flowers are also harvested for use in teas and perfumes due to their aromatic properties.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.
