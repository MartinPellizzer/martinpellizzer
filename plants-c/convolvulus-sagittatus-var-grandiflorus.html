Convolvulus sagittatus var. grandiflorus

Convolvulus sagittatus var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered arum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in umbels. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
