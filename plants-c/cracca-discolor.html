Cracca discolor

Cracca discolor, commonly known as the white cranesbill, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically oval and slightly hairy, with a distinctive crinkled appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often used in garden settings for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
