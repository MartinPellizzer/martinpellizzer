Centaurea salsuginea

Centaurea salsuginea, commonly known as the saltbush or sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to purple flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions. Centaurea salsuginea is adapted to saline soils and can thrive in coastal environments.

It is also known for its ability to tolerate drought, making it a hardy species in challenging climates.
