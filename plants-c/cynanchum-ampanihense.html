Cynanchum ampanihense

Cynanchum ampanihense, commonly known as the Madagascar milkweed, is a perennial herb native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, and it is known for its latex-like sap. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
