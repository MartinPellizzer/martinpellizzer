Cirsium grecescui

Cirsium grecescui, commonly known as the Greek thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in harsh environmental conditions.

Cirsium grecescui is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
