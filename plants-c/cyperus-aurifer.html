Cyperus aurifer

Cyperus aurifer, commonly known as golden flatsedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and shallow water areas, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are often adorned with golden or reddish bracts. Its rhizomes allow it to spread vegetatively, making it a common pioneer species in disturbed wetland habitats.

Cyperus aurifer is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wetland ecosystems.
