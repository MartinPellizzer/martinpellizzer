Carex recurva

Carex recurva, commonly known as the bentstem sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are typically arranged in a loose, cylindrical cluster. Carex recurva thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
