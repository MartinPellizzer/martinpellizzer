Carex × castriferrei

Carex × castriferrei, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer.

Carex × castriferrei is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
