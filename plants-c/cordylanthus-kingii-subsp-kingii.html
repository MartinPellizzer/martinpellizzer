Cordylanthus kingii subsp. kingii

Cordylanthus kingii subsp.

kingii, commonly known as the King's cordylanthus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces stipitate (stalked) pods that contain several seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils in open, sunny habitats.

It is often found in coastal sage scrub and chaparral ecosystems, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
