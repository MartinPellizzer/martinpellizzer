Centella affinis

Centella affinis, commonly known as Asiatic pennywort, is a creeping perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small white flowers and is often found growing in moist, shaded environments. It is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional medicine for its purported skin-healing benefits.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Rubiaceae and is characterized by its stoloniferous growth habit.
