Clematis viticella venosa

Clematis viticella venosa, commonly known as the variegated vine clematis, is a deciduous climbing plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and long, flexible stems that can reach up to 10 meters in length. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and often exhibit variegation patterns.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
