Capparis prisca

Capparis prisca, commonly known as the ancient caper, is a flowering plant belonging to the Capparaceae family.

It is characterized by its woody shrub structure and has small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Capparis prisca is native to regions in the Mediterranean and parts of southern Europe, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.
