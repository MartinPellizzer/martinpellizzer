Coreopsis woytkowskii

Coreopsis woytkowskii, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and produces showy, bright yellow composite flowers in the summer months. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Coreopsis woytkowskii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
