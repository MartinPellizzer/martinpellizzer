Calystegia japonica var. pentapetala

Calystegia japonica var. pentapetala, commonly known as the Japanese morning glory, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in the morning. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white, which attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are covered in fine, sticky hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
