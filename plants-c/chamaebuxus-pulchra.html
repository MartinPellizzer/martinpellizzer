Chamaebuxus pulchra

Chamaebuxus pulchra, commonly known as the silver leaf buckthorn, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage with a silvery-gray appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to conserve water in its arid native environment.

Chamaebuxus pulchra is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
