Croton kimosorum

Croton kimosorum, commonly known as the African croton, is a deciduous shrub native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with bright yellow and green patterns. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its unique leaf coloration.
