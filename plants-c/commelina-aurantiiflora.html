Commelina aurantiiflora

Commelina aurantiiflora, commonly known as the orange-flowered spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features striking orange-red flowers with a distinctive three-lobed spathe and a spadix, typical of the Commelina genus. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its vibrant flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
