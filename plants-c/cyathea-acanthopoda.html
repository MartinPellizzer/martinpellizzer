Cyathea acanthopoda

Cyathea acanthopoda, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in a fibrous, hairy layer that gives it its common name. The plant produces large, compound fronds that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the trunk. Cyathea acanthopoda thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of its fronds, contributing to its reproductive cycle.
