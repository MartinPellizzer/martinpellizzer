Cyperus eriocauloides

Cyperus eriocauloides, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as swamps and floodplains, where it thrives in moist soil conditions. Its slender, grass-like stems are usually erect, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Cyperus eriocauloides is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
