Crataegus oxyacantha var. villosa

Crataegus oxyacantha var. villosa, commonly known as the downy hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with a serrated margin, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and gardens.
