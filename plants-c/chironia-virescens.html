Chironia virescens

Chironia virescens, commonly known as the green chironia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, adding a delicate aesthetic to its appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
