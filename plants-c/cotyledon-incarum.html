Cotyledon incarum

Cotyledon incarum, commonly known as the "Fireplant," is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that are often red or purple, especially when exposed to sunlight. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are adapted to store water, making it drought-tolerant.

Cotyledon incarum is popular among gardeners for its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
