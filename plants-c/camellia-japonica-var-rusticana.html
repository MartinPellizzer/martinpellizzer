Camellia japonica var. rusticana

Camellia japonica var. rusticana, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, requiring moderate watering.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to add color to gardens during the colder months.
