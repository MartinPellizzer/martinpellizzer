Calophyllum trapezifolium

Calophyllum trapezifolium, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Africa and parts of the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and large, elliptical leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by orange-red berries containing a single seed. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.

Additionally, it has traditional medicinal uses in various African cultures.
