Centaurea splendens var. coronopifolia

Centaurea splendens var. coronopifolia, commonly known as the golden crownflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features bright yellow ray flowers surrounded by a dense, spiny involucre, giving it a crown-like appearance. The plant has linear, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in dry, open habitats.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and native plant landscapes.
