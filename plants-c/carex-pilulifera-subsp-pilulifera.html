Carex pilulifera subsp. pilulifera

Carex pilulifera subsp.

pilulifera, commonly known as the pill-like sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and the plant is often found in association with other wetland vegetation.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
