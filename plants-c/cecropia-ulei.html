Cecropia ulei

Cecropia ulei, commonly known as the giant cecropia, is a fast-growing tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and distinctive, hollow stems that provide habitat for various organisms. The plant has a symbiotic relationship with ants, which live in the hollow stems and protect the tree from herbivores. Cecropia ulei is also known for its ability to rapidly colonize disturbed areas, making it an important pioneer species in secondary forests.
