Croton macrostigma f. macrophyllus

Croton macrostigma f. macrophyllus, commonly known as the giant leaf croton, is a striking plant characterized by its large, glossy, and often variegated leaves.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant features a woody stem with thick, fleshy leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded gardens.
