Campanula oligosantha

Campanula oligosantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Campanula oligosantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate floral display.
