Chenopodium berlandieri subsp. yucatanum

Chenopodium berlandieri subsp.

yucatanum, commonly known as wild spinach or Mexican spinach, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas. It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.

This species is valued for its edible leaves, which are used in traditional cuisines across parts of Mexico and the southern United States.
