Colubrina greggii var. yucatanensis

Colubrina greggii var. yucatanensis, commonly known as Yucatan beautyberry, is a shrub native to the Yucatan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to blue berries that appear in clusters, which are a key feature of the plant. The leaves are simple, opposite, and have a smooth texture with a slightly waxy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract wildlife.
