Cuphea lanceolata

Cuphea lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and prefers well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant floral display and compact growth habit.

Cuphea lanceolata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
