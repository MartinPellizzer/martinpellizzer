Calanthe triplicata f. purpureoflora

Calanthe triplicata f. purpureoflora, commonly known as the purple-flowered calanthe, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are typically borne on erect, slender spikes. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in shaded, humid environments. It is a terrestrial orchid that prefers well-drained, organic soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
