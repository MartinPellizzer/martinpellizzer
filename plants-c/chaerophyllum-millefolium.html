Chaerophyllum millefolium

Chaerophyllum millefolium, commonly known as bishop's weed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided leaves and umbel-shaped flowers. The flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
