Cousinia neubaueri

Cousinia neubaueri, commonly known as the Neubauer's cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures.

Cousinia neubaueri is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, making it a hardy and resilient species.
