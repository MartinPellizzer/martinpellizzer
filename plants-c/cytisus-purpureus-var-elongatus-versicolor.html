Cytisus purpureus var. elongatus-versicolor

Cytisus purpureus var. elongatus-versicolor, commonly known as the purple broom or variable elongated broom, is a flowering shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, often twisted stems that are typically covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep purple, depending on the variety. Its leaves are alternate, trifoliate, and have a distinctive, fern-like appearance.

This species is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.
