Cymbopogon afronardus

Cymbopogon afronardus, commonly known as African lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its fragrance, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
