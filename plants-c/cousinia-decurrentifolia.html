Cousinia decurrentifolia

Cousinia decurrentifolia, commonly known as the decurrent leaf Cousinia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming basal leaves and tall, branched stems. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Cousinia decurrentifolia is often found in rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
