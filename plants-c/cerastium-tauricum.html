Cerastium tauricum

Cerastium tauricum, commonly known as the Tauric chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers and opposite leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are usually prostrate or ascending, and it produces numerous small, oval-shaped leaves.

Cerastium tauricum is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance and ability to spread readily.
