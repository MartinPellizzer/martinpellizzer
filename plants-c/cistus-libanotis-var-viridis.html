Cistus libanotis var. viridis

Cistus libanotis var. viridis, commonly known as the green-leaved rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically a vibrant green, distinguishing it from other varieties of Cistus. The plant produces clusters of pink or white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and garden design.
