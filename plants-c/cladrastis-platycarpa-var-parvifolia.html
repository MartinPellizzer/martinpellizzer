Cladrastis platycarpa var. parvifolia

Cladrastis platycarpa var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaf yellowwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are typically less than 1 inch in length. The plant produces fragrant, pendulous clusters of white to pale yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy blooms and unique foliage, which add visual interest throughout the growing season.
