Cardamine trichocarpa var. usambarensis

Cardamine trichocarpa var. usambarensis, commonly known as the Usambara watercress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of East Africa, particularly the Usambara Mountains in Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately divided leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors and stream banks.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
