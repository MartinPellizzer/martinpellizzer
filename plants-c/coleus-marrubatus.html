Coleus marrubatus

Coleus marrubatus, commonly known as the rough-leaved coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage.

Coleus marrubatus is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
