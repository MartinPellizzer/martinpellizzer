Cavendishia gaultherioides

Cavendishia gaultherioides, commonly known as the Brazilian violet, is a striking plant native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its velvety, purple-hued leaves and clusters of fragrant, violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Violaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a sought-after species among horticultural enthusiasts.
