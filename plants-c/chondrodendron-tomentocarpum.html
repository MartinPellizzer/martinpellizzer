Chondrodendron tomentocarpum

Chondrodendron tomentocarpum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a woody shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a waxy texture and a distinctive grayish-white underside.

It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, shaded environments.
