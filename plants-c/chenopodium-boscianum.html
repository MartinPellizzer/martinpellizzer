Chenopodium boscianum

Chenopodium boscianum, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and reddish roots. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Chenopodium boscianum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
