Cyphostemma microdipterum

Cyphostemma microdipterum, commonly known as the small-winged cyphostemma, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers, which are followed by small, berry-like fruits. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
