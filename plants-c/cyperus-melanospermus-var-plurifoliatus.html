Cyperus melanospermus var. plurifoliatus

Cyperus melanospermus var. plurifoliatus, commonly known as the blackseed flatsedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found at the base of the leaves. Its seeds are dark and densely packed, contributing to its common name.

It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
