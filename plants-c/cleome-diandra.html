Cleome diandra

Cleome diandra, commonly known as spider flower, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has palmately lobed leaves. Its stems are often covered in fine hairs, and the flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of the branches.

Cleome diandra is a member of the Cleomaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
