Carex nachiana

Carex nachiana, commonly known as the Nachi sedge, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Carex nachiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland areas.
