Calendula fulgida

Calendula fulgida, commonly known as the fiery marigold, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, orange to red flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, with the central disk being tubular.

Calendula fulgida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
