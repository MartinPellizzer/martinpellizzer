Callicarpa lobata

Callicarpa lobata, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright purple berries that appear in the fall, attracting birds and other wildlife. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often tinged with red or bronze in the autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Callicarpa lobata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant fruit and seasonal foliage.
