Chamaefistula micradenia

Chamaefistula micradenia, commonly known as the narrowleaf boxwood, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a glossy dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in spring, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is often used in landscaping and garden design.
