Cohniella cebolleta

Cohniella cebolleta, commonly known as the Mexican onion or cebollita, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its seeds are often used in traditional Mexican cuisine, particularly in the preparation of certain types of tamales and soups.

Cohniella cebolleta is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its vibrant flower clusters.
