Cyperus diffusus subsp. chalaranthus

Cyperus diffusus subsp.

chalaranthus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) but is often classified under the sedge family (Cyperaceae) due to its morphological similarities.

The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to saturated soils. Its slender, grass-like stems are often clustered, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

The species is characterized by its narrow leaves and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
