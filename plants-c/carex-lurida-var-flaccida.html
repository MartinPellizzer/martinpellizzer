Carex lurida var. flaccida

Carex lurida var. flaccida, commonly known as the slender bog sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense mats that help stabilize aquatic ecosystems. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, with male and female flowers usually separated on different stems.

This variant is distinguished by its pale green to reddish stems and is often found in association with other wetland vegetation.
