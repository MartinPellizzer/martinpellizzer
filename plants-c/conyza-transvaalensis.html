Conyza transvaalensis

Conyza transvaalensis, commonly known as Transvaal weed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, and it produces small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, with a rough texture, and it often forms dense mats in its native environment.

Conyza transvaalensis is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
