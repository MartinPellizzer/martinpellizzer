Camellia ilicifolia f. rubimuricata

Camellia ilicifolia f. rubimuricata, commonly known as the Crimson-veined Camellia, is a unique cultivar characterized by its striking foliage.

The leaves are dark green with prominent red veins that create a dramatic visual contrast. This plant is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, preferring a humid climate.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in winter, adding to its aesthetic appeal.
