Carex fleckeri

Carex fleckeri, commonly known as the Flecker's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, spike-like flowers. Native to specific regions of southern Africa, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant has a compact growth habit and produces inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Carex fleckeri is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
