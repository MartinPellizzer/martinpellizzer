Coelogyne murudensis

Coelogyne murudensis, commonly known as the Murudensis orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Borneo.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, and it produces a single inflorescence with several showy, lance-shaped flowers.

Coelogyne murudensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
