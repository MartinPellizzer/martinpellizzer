Campanula fedorovii

Campanula fedorovii, commonly known as the Fedorov's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Campanula fedorovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
