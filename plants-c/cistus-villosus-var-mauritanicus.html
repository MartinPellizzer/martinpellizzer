Cistus villosus var. mauritanicus

Cistus villosus var. mauritanicus, commonly known as the Mauritanian rock rose, is a shrub native to North Africa and the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its gray-green, woolly foliage and clusters of pink to reddish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it contributes to local biodiversity.

Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves and flowers, are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
