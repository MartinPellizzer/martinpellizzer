Citrus rangpuriensis

Citrus rangpuriensis, commonly known as the Rangpuri citrus, is a lesser-known species within the citrus genus.

It is native to the Indian subcontinent, particularly found in the states of Uttar Pradesh and Uttarakhand. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically harvested when they reach a mature yellow color. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, with a slightly serrated edge.

The fruit contains a high concentration of citric acid, making it valuable for both culinary and medicinal uses.
