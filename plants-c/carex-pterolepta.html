Carex pterolepta

Carex pterolepta, commonly known as the slender flat-sheath sedge, is a perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex pterolepta is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse ecosystems.
