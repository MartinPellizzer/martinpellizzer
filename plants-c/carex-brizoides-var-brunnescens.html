Carex brizoides var. brunnescens

Carex brizoides var. brunnescens, commonly known as the brownish sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often reddish-brown in color, giving it its common name. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
