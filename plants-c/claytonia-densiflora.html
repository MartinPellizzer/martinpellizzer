Claytonia densiflora

Claytonia densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered springbeauty, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features rosettes of basal, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant gardening.
