Campanula phytidocalyx var. sulcata

Campanula phytidocalyx var. sulcata, commonly known as the grooved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet. The leaves are lanceolate, often with a distinctive grooved or ridged texture, contributing to its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
