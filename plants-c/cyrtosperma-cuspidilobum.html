Cyrtosperma cuspidilobum

Cyrtosperma cuspidilobum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed arrowroot, is a tropical plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its large, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Its roots are used as a starchy food source, similar to traditional arrowroot.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.
