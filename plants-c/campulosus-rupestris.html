Campulosus rupestris

Campulosus rupestris, commonly known as the rock campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to rocky and arid environments in southern Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Campulosus rupestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and dry landscapes.
