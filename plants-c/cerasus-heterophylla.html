Cerasus heterophylla

Cerasus heterophylla, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its alternate, ovate to elliptical leaves that vary in size and shape along the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible drupes that range in color from yellow to red. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy blossoms and is also valued for its fruit in some regions.

Cerasus heterophylla prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
