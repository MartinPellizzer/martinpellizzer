Cinnamomum rubrum

Cinnamomum rubrum, commonly known as the red cinnamon or red camphor laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant, aromatic bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its essential oils, derived from the bark and leaves, are valued for their medicinal and culinary uses.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and economic importance in traditional medicine and perfumery.
