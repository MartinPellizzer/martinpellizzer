Croton glandulosus var. sieberi

Croton glandulosus var. sieberi, commonly known as the Sieber's croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped or ovate. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, spiny capsules containing seeds. Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and growth habit.
