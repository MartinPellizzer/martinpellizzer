Ceropegia migiurtina

Ceropegia migiurtina, commonly known as the "Bead Leaf" or "String of Hearts," is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a trailing or climbing succulent with fleshy, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale green, which are pollinated by specific insects. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia migiurtina is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and ease of care.
