Crataegus altaica var. fusca

Crataegus altaica var. fusca, commonly known as the dark-fruited hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, simple oval leaves, and small, dark red to black berries that appear in the fall. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to temperate climates. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its astringent properties.

The species is also notable for its role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds that feed on its berries.
