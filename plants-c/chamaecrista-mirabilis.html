Chamaecrista mirabilis

Chamaecrista mirabilis, commonly known as the goldenball burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, feather-like leaflets.

Chamaecrista mirabilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
