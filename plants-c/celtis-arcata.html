Celtis arcata

Celtis arcata, commonly known as the American hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern and central United States.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into spherical, berry-like fruits. Celtis arcata is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland areas, stream banks, and urban landscapes.

Its fruits are an important food source for birds and small mammals.
