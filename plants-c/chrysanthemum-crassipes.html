Chrysanthemum crassipes

Chrysanthemum crassipes, commonly known as the water chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers and its ability to thrive in wetland environments. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture, and it often grows in shallow water or along the edges of ponds. Its botanical features make it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
