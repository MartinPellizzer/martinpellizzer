Carex sublivida

Carex sublivida, commonly known as the gray sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowering spikes are usually pale green to reddish brown, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex sublivida plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
