Crataegus bronxensis

Crataegus bronxensis, commonly known as the Bronx hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
