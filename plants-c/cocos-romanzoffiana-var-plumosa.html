Cocos romanzoffiana var. plumosa

Cocos romanzoffiana var. plumosa, commonly known as the plume palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its leaves are typically arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its ornamental value in landscaping.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively low maintenance once established.
