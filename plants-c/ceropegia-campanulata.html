Ceropegia campanulata

Ceropegia campanulata, commonly known as the bell-shaped ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens with suitable conditions.
