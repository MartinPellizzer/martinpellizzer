Carduus decandollei

Carduus decandollei, commonly known as the Decandolle's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
