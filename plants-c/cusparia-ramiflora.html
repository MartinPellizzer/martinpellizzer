Cusparia ramiflora

Cusparia ramiflora, commonly known as the branched spiky plant, is a unique species characterized by its intricate branching pattern and spiky, leaf-like structures.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions, where it has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant's foliage consists of modified leaves that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures, which help reduce water loss. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale in color, and it relies on wind pollination.

Due to its specialized morphology, Cusparia ramiflora plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
