Crataegus scabrida var. balkwillii

Crataegus scabrida var. balkwillii, commonly known as the Balkwill hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a rough, sandpapery texture. The fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer, providing food for birds and small mammals.

This plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, Mediterranean-type climates.
