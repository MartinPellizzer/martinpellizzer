Cyperus phleoides

Cyperus phleoides, commonly known as the flatleaf sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. Its slender, grass-like leaves are arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Cyperus phleoides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
