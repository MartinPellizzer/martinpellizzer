Crataegus philadelphica

Crataegus philadelphica, commonly known as the Philadelphia thornapple, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
