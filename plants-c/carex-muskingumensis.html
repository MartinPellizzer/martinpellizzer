Carex muskingumensis

Carex muskingumensis, commonly known as the Muskingum sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This species typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its slender, triangular stems are often covered with dense, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that bloom in the summer, contributing to its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
