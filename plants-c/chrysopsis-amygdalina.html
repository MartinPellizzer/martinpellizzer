Chrysopsis amygdalina

Chrysopsis amygdalina, commonly known as the almond leaf chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a silvery-gray underside, and it produces small yellow flowers in the summer.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.
