Carex tricostata var. praticola

Carex tricostata var. praticola, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly curved. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike that bears small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
