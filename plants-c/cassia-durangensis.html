Cassia durangensis

Cassia durangensis, commonly known as Durango cassia, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Cassia durangensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
