Campanula macrorhiza var. rotundata

Campanula macrorhiza var. rotundata, commonly known as the roundleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are broad, rounded, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
