Critoniopsis standleyi

Critoniopsis standleyi, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and thrives in high altitudes. Its inflorescence consists of a spike bearing numerous small, clustered flowers that bloom in a range of colors from white to pink.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
