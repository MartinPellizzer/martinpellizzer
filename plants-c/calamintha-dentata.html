Calamintha dentata

Calamintha dentata, commonly known as roundleaf germander, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and thyme. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers. It is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and has a mild, mint-like fragrance.

Calamintha dentata is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
