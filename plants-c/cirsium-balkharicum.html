Cirsium balkharicum

Cirsium balkharicum, commonly known as the Balkh thistle, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it produces small, achene-like seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
