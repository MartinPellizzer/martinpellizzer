Centaurea pallescens f. hyalolepis

Centaurea pallescens f. hyalolepis, commonly known as the pale centaury, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is native to southern Europe and thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
