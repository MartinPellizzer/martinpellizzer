Caperonia heteropetala var. genuina

Caperonia heteropetala var. genuina, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
