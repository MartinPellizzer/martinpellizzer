Coleus klossii

Coleus klossii, commonly known as Kloss's coleus, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and display a variety of hues, including red, purple, and green. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or violet in color.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
