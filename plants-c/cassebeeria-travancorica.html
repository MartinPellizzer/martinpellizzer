Cassebeeria travancorica

Cassebeeria travancorica, commonly known as the Travancore Cassia, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The flowers are often used in traditional medicine and are also a source of nectar for pollinators.
