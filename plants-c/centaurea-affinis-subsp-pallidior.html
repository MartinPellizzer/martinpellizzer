Centaurea affinis subsp. pallidior

Centaurea affinis subsp.

pallidior, commonly known as the pale centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often spiny.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
