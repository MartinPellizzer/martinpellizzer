Cassia secedens

Cassia secedens, commonly known as the spreading cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically green with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing numerous seeds. Cassia secedens thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common ornamental plant in warmer climates.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable in its native habitats.
