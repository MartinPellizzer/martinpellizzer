Cyanthillium cinereum var. lanatum

Cyanthillium cinereum var. lanatum, commonly known as the woolly blueweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands.

Due to its unique texture and color, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
