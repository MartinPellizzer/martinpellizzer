Crassocephalum paludum

Crassocephalum paludum, commonly known as swamphead sunflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in wet or swampy areas, thriving in moist soil conditions. The plant features large, showy yellow flowers with prominent dark centers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in wetland gardens.
