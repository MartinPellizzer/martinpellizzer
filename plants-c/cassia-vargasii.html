Cassia vargasii

Cassia vargasii, commonly known as the Vargas's cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, feather-like leaflets, and it produces yellow-orange flowers in clusters.

The seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine due to their potential pharmacological properties.
