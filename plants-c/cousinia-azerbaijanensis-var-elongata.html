Cousinia azerbaijanensis var. elongata

Cousinia azerbaijanensis var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated tansygrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Azerbaijan.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its floral structure consists of ray florets surrounded by disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
