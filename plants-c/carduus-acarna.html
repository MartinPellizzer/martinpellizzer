Carduus acarna

Carduus acarna, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with dark green, deeply lobed foliage. Its flowers are usually purple to pink, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom from late summer to early autumn.

The plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
