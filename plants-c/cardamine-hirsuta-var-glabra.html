Cardamine hirsuta var. glabra

Cardamine hirsuta var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth watercress, is a variant of the hairy watercress species.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, hairless leaves compared to its typical hairy counterpart. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately divided, with a distinctive peppery aroma when crushed.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, rounded pods containing several seeds.
