Clematis intricata var. intrapuberula

Clematis intricata var. intrapuberula, commonly known as the woolly clematis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

Due to its unique appearance and blooming habit, it is often cultivated for both its aesthetic value and ecological benefits.
