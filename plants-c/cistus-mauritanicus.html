Cistus mauritanicus

Cistus mauritanicus, commonly known as the Moroccan rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Morocco and Algeria.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is well adapted to dry, rocky environments and is drought-tolerant. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Cistus mauritanicus is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
