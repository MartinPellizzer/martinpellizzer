Carex baltzellii

Carex baltzellii, commonly known as Baltic sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

It belongs to the Carex genus, which is part of the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along streams. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green to brown in color.

Carex baltzellii is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
