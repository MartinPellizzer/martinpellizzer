Cayaponia botryocarpa

Cayaponia botryocarpa, commonly known as the clustered fruit tree, is a species native to Central America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, round fruits that are often clustered together, giving it its scientific name. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
