Carmichaelia grandiflora var. dumosa

Carmichaelia grandiflora var. dumosa, commonly known as the dwarf mountain daisy, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It features small, dark green leaves that are densely packed and covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, showy yellow flowers with prominent orange centers, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
