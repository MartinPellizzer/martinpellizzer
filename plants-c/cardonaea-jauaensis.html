Cardonaea jauaensis

Cardonaea jauaensis, commonly known as the Jauaena grass, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to blue-gray flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered with fine hairs, and it thrives in high-altitude environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
