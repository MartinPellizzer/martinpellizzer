Carphobolus regnellii

Carphobolus regnellii, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant has a taproot system and is often found in moist, wooded areas. Its flowers are typically clustered in racemes and bloom in early spring.

The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
