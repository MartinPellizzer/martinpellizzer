Citrus × aurantium var. fructo-depressa

Citrus × aurantium var. fructo-depressa, commonly known as the depressed fruit pummelo, is a variant of the pummelo citrus species characterized by its distinctive, flattened or depressed fruit shape.

This variety is native to Southeast Asia and is widely cultivated for its large, sweet fruit, which is often used in both culinary and medicinal applications. The plant typically grows as a small evergreen tree with thick, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit develops over several months and is known for its thick rind and high juice content.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important species in citrus genetics and horticulture.
