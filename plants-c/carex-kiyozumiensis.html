Carex kiyozumiensis

Carex kiyozumiensis, commonly known as the Kiyozumi sedge, is a perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale to dark brown flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex kiyozumiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
