Ceanothus sanguineus

Ceanothus sanguineus, commonly known as the red ceanothus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and inland areas, often in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are usually evergreen, with a leathery texture and a bluish-green color.

Ceanothus sanguineus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in dry, sunny conditions.
