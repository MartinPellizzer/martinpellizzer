Cyperus ascohemisphaericus

Cyperus ascohemisphaericus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus ascohemisphaericus is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
