Calceolaria bartsiifolia

Calceolaria bartsiifolia, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Sierra Nevada in Colombia.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, resembling small cups. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapted to the harsh mountain climate.

Calceolaria bartsiifolia is a rare and endangered species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
