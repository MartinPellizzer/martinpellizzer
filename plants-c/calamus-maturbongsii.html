Calamus maturbongsii

Calamus maturbongsii, commonly known as the Bornean calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arales.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in Borneo and parts of Indonesia. The plant is a rhizomatous herb that grows in wet, tropical environments, often near rivers and swamps. Its long, slender stems are used in traditional crafts and as a source of aromatic oils.

The plant's botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences.
