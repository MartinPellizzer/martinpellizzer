Crepidium quadridentatum

Crepidium quadridentatum, commonly known as the four-toothed orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, typically arranged in a rosette pattern.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in warm, humid environments.
