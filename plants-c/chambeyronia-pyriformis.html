Chambeyronia pyriformis

Chambeyronia pyriformis, commonly known as the pear-shaped fern, is a unique species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pear-shaped fronds that are often deeply lobed and have a delicate, feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories, where it thrives in high humidity and partial shade. Its reproductive structures include spore-producing sorus clusters located on the underside of its fronds.

Chambeyronia pyriformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
