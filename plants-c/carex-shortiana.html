Carex shortiana

Carex shortiana, commonly known as the shortleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Carex shortiana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
