Croton argentinus var. chloropetalus

Croton argentinus var. chloropetalus, commonly known as the green-petaled croton, is a striking tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, variegated foliage, with leaves that often display shades of green, yellow, and red. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from yellow to orange, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
