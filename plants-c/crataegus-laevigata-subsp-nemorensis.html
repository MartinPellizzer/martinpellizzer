Crataegus laevigata subsp. nemorensis

Crataegus laevigata subsp.

nemorensis, commonly known as the white thorn or common hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces red berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soils but prefers well-drained conditions.

This subspecies is often used in hedgerows and as a ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ecological value.
