Cassia bicapsularis var. chilensis

Cassia bicapsularis var. chilensis, commonly known as Chilean golden bush, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive two-lobed pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
