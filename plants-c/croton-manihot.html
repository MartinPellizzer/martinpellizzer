Croton manihot

Croton manihot, commonly known as arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is cultivated for its starchy, edible roots, which are used as a thickening agent in cooking. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Croton manihot is also valued for its ornamental foliage, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
