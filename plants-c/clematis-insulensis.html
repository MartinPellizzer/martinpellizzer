Clematis insulensis

Clematis insulensis, commonly known as the Japanese clematis, is a deciduous vine native to Japan.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets and produces showy, blue-violet flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation. Its vigorous growth habit requires support structures, and it is often used in ornamental landscaping.

Clematis insulensis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
