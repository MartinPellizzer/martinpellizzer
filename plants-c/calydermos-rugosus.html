Calydermos rugosus

Calydermos rugosus, commonly known as the rough-skinned calyderm, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive, rugged, and textured foliage.

It belongs to the family Calydermaceae and is native to arid and semi-arid regions where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant features a compact growth habit with dark green, waxy leaves that are covered in fine, raised bumps, giving it a leathery appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically displaying shades of yellow to orange, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain seeds.

Due to its specialized adaptations, Calydermos rugosus is an important species in its native ecosystem, contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various small organisms.
