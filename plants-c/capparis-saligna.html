Capparis saligna

Capparis saligna, commonly known as the coastal caper, is a shrub native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, green, round fruits that turn red when ripe. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in maritime climates.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to salt spray and coastal winds.
