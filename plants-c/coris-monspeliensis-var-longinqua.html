Coris monspeliensis var. longinqua

Coris monspeliensis var. longinqua, commonly known as the long-stemmed coris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 60 cm in height, often branching near the base. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.
