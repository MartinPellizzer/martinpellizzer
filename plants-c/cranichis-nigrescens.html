Cranichis nigrescens

Cranichis nigrescens, commonly known as the black orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which are striking in appearance and often covered in a dense layer of fine, hairy structures. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives in the understory with high humidity and moderate light. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a unique structure that includes a spurred lip, a feature common in many orchid species.

Cranichis nigrescens is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal.
