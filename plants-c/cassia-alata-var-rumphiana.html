Cassia alata var. rumphiana

Cassia alata var. rumphiana, commonly known as the flame tree or golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. The plant has feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. Its bark is smooth and light gray, and it produces elongated, flat pods that contain flat, oval seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
