Cyperus strigosus var. elongatus

Cyperus strigosus var. elongatus, commonly known as the long-leaved flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are often hairless or slightly hairy, with a distinctive, narrow, elongated shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along waterways.

Its botanical structure makes it adaptable to a variety of wetland environments.
