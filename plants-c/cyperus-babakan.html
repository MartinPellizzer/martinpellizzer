Cyperus babakan

Cyperus babakan, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a flowering plant species belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. It has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.

Cyperus babakan is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
