Codonopsis thalictrifolia var. mollis

Codonopsis thalictrifolia var. mollis, commonly known as the silky codonopsis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, soft stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas or rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
