Chlorocyperus congestus

Chlorocyperus congestus, commonly known as the dense chlorocyperus, is a grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in moist soil conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, often forming dense inflorescences, and it is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Chlorocyperus congestus is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland environments.
