Cupressus arizonica subsp. montana

Cupressus arizonica subsp.

montana, commonly known as the Arizona cypress, is a subspecies native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its scale-like leaves and aromatic wood.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in arid to semi-arid environments. It has a conical to cylindrical shape with a dense, pyramidal crown.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental use in landscaping due to its attractive appearance and hardiness.
