Clusia melchiorii

Clusia melchiorii, commonly known as the Melchior's clusia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae, which includes species known for their showy flowers and often toxic sap. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in the axils of the leaves. It is typically evergreen and can grow as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Clusia melchiorii is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to partial shade.
