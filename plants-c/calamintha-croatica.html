Calamintha croatica

Calamintha croatica, commonly known as Croatian germander, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

Calamintha croatica thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.
