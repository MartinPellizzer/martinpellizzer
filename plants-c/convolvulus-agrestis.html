Convolvulus agrestis

Convolvulus agrestis, commonly known as the field bindweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are typically light green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Convolvulus agrestis is a perennial plant that spreads through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural areas.
