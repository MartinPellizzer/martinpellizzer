Carex tsukudensis

Carex tsukudensis, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex tsukudensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
