Calorophus gracillimus

Calorophus gracillimus, commonly known as the slender flameflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, tubular flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange-red, resembling small flames. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves that help reduce water loss. It blooms during the summer months, producing nectar that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
