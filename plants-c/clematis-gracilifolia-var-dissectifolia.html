Clematis gracilifolia var. dissectifolia

Clematis gracilifolia var. dissectifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf clematis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens and native plant landscapes.
