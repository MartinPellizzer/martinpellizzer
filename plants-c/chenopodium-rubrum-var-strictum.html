Chenopodium rubrum var. strictum

Chenopodium rubrum var. strictum, commonly known as red goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive red-tinged leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines, while the seeds can be processed into a flour-like substance.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments.
