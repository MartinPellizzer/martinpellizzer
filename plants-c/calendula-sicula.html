Calendula sicula

Calendula sicula, commonly known as Sicilian marigold, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with hairy stems and serrated leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Calendula sicula is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant due to its vibrant color and resilience.
