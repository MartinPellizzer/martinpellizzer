Chrysopogon fuscus

Chrysopogon fuscus, commonly known as the blackgrass or sweetgrass, is a perennial grass native to various regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, wetlands, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic oils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
