Crataegus blairensis

Crataegus blairensis, commonly known as the Blairensis hawthorn, is a species of shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along riverbanks.
