Croton larensis

Croton larensis, commonly known as the Larensis croton, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful foliage. The plant features alternate, palmately lobed leaves that can vary in shape and hue depending on the variety. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in warm climates for its unique leaf patterns.
