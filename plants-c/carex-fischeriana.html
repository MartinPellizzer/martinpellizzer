Carex fischeriana

Carex fischeriana, commonly known as Fischer's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, bogs, and stream banks. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered at the tips of the stems.

Fischer's sedge is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wetland biodiversity.
