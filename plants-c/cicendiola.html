Cicendiola

Cicendiola, commonly known as the Indian nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes various species known for their stinging hairs. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture and produces small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Cicendiola is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
