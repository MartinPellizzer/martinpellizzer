Cyperus ciliatopilosus var. longifolius

Cyperus ciliatopilosus var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist habitats. Its leaves are often covered in fine, hair-like structures, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variant is noted for its ecological importance in wetland ecosystems, supporting various insect and bird species.
