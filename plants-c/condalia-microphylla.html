Condalia microphylla

Condalia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved condalia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping and for soil stabilization.
