Casearia rufinervis

Casearia rufinervis, commonly known as the red-veined casearia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often elliptical leaves with distinct red veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, followed by small, berry-like fruits. Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
