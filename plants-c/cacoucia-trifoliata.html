Cacoucia trifoliata

Cacoucia trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaved bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being oval-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines.

Its growth habit is typically herbaceous, with a climbing or trailing structure that allows it to thrive in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
