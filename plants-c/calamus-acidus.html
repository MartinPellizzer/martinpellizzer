Calamus acidus

Calamus acidus, commonly known as sour sedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Calamus acidus produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family.

It is often found in moist soils and can thrive in a variety of wetland habitats.
