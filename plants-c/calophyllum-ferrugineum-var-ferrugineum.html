Calophyllum ferrugineum var. ferrugineum

Calophyllum ferrugineum var. ferrugineum, commonly known as the red mangrove, is a tropical tree native to coastal areas of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, flaky bark that often appears reddish in color. The plant has aerial roots that help it anchor itself in shallow water and absorb oxygen. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a glossy appearance.

The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed.
