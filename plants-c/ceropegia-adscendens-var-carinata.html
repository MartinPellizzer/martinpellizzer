Ceropegia adscendens var. carinata

Ceropegia adscendens var. carinata, commonly known as the African violet or velvet plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, somewhat succulent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white in color. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires moderate watering to prevent root rot.

This species is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and ease of care.
