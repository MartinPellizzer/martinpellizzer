Centranthus longiflorus var. kellereri

Centranthus longiflorus var. kellereri, commonly known as Keller's redflower.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of bright red, tubular flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its striking floral display makes it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
