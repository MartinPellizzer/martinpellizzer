Campanula cespitosa var. hirta

Campanula cespitosa var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, clump-forming stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically hairy, giving the plant its varietal name, and are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is noted for its attractive appearance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
