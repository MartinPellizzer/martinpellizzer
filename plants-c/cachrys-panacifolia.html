Cachrys panacifolia

Cachrys panacifolia, commonly known as the panacifolia orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has a compact growth habit with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts who appreciate its unique floral display.
