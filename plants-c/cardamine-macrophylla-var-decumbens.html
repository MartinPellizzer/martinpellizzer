Cardamine macrophylla var. decumbens

Cardamine macrophylla var. decumbens, commonly known as the broad-leaf watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with broad, lance-shaped lobes and produces small white or greenish flowers in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor similar to other watercress species.

Botanically, it is characterized by its basal rosette growth habit and its ability to tolerate wet conditions.
