Carduus kerneri subsp. scardicus

Carduus kerneri subsp.

scardicus, commonly known as the narrowleaf thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features spiny, lanceolate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, which are surrounded by bracts. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
