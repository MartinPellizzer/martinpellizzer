Calamagrostis manshurica

Calamagrostis manshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian reed, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland gardens and naturalized landscapes.
