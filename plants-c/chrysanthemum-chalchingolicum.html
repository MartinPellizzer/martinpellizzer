Chrysanthemum chalchingolicum

Chrysanthemum chalchingolicum, commonly known as the golden chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features sturdy, upright stems with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that have a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers bloom in shades of yellow to orange, often in late summer to early autumn.

Chrysanthemum chalchingolicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
