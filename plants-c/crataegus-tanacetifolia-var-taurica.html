Crataegus tanacetifolia var. taurica

Crataegus tanacetifolia var. taurica, commonly known as the Taurian hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are aromatic and resemble those of yarrow. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
