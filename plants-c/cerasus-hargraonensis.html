Cerasus hargraonensis

Cerasus hargraonensis, commonly known as the Hargraon cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
