Cydonia minor

Cydonia minor, commonly known as the quince, is a deciduous fruit tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces a fragrant, yellowish fruit that ripens in late autumn. The plant has a shallow root system and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.

The fruit is often used in cooking and has a long history of cultivation in traditional medicine.
