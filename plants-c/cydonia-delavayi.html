Cydonia delavayi

Cydonia delavayi, commonly known as the Chinese quince, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces edible, orange-colored fruit that is typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Cydonia delavayi is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional Chinese medicine.
