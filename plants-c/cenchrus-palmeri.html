Cenchrus palmeri

Cenchrus palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and dense, fibrous root system that helps stabilize sandy soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with bristly hairs, which are adapted for wind dispersal of its seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a common component of desert grasslands.

Due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements, it is often used in restoration projects and as a forage plant for livestock.
