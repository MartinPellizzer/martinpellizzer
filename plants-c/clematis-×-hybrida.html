Clematis × hybrida

Clematis × hybrida, commonly known as the garden clematis, is a hybrid species resulting from the crossbreeding of various clematis species.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth, often reaching heights of several meters, and its ability to climb using its twining stems. The plant produces showy, often fragrant flowers in a wide range of colors, including blue, purple, white, and pink. Its botanical features include palmately divided leaves and a complex inflorescence structure.

Clematis × hybrida is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.
