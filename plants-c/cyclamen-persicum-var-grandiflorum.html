Cyclamen persicum var. grandiflorum

Cyclamen persicum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the Persian cyclamen, is a species native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety foliage and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant produces clusters of tubular flowers that are typically borne on long, slender stalks. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant for indoor or outdoor gardens.

The leaves and flowers are edible, though they are not commonly consumed due to their mild toxicity.
