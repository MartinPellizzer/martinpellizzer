Chirita umbricola

Chirita umbricola, commonly known as the shady chirita, is a species of flowering plant in the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. This plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant.
