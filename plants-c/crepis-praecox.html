Crepis praecox

Crepis praecox, commonly known as early crested grass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, spiky seed heads that are adapted for wind dispersal, aiding in its spread.

Its delicate appearance and early blooming period make it a notable wildflower in its native habitats.
