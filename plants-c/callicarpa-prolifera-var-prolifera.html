Callicarpa prolifera var. prolifera

Callicarpa prolifera var. prolifera, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to red berries that appear in clusters, which are a key feature of its botanical description. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 feet and has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various wildlife species.
