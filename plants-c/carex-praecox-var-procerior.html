Carex praecox var. procerior

Carex praecox var. procerior, commonly known as early sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-angled berries.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
