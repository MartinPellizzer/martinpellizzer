Centroglossa glaziovii

Centroglossa glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's centrosepal, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its unique, colorful flowers that resemble a centaur's tongue, hence its scientific name. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a single, showy flower with a distinctive spurred lip.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
