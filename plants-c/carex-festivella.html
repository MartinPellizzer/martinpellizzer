Carex festivella

Carex festivella, commonly known as the festive sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. This species typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, bogs, and wet meadows. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are usually arranged in three ranks, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters.

The plant plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
