Cyperus subcastaneus

Cyperus subcastaneus, commonly known as the red rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along water bodies.

Cyperus subcastaneus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
