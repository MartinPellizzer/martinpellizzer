Crataegus durobrivensis

Crataegus durobrivensis, commonly known as the Dane's Thorn, is a species of hawthorn native to Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red berries in the autumn, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Crataegus durobrivensis typically grows in woodland edges, hedgerows, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of ecological habitats and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
