Combretum malabaricum

Combretum malabaricum, commonly known as Malabar flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of India and Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, and its bark is often smooth with a reddish hue. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The tree is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.
