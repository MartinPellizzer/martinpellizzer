Colchicum buchubergense

Colchicum buchubergense, commonly known as the Buchuberg colchic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Colchicaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a single, showy flower per stem, with reflexed petals and a prominent central stamen. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
