Campanula truncata

Campanula truncata, commonly known as the cut-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are distinctive, with a notched or truncated appearance, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
