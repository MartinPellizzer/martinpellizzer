Crucifera bellidoides

Crucifera bellidoides, commonly known as the bellidium, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and has a compact, bushy habit. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its fruits are small, dry pods that split open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
