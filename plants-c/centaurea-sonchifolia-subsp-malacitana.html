Centaurea sonchifolia subsp. malacitana

Centaurea sonchifolia subsp.

malacitana, commonly known as the Malacitana thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to purple composite flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it produces spiny bracts around its flower heads. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
