Caxamarca ayabacensis

Caxamarca ayabacensis, commonly known as the Ayabaca cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Peruvian Andes.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found at high elevations. Its structure is adapted to conserve water, with thick stems that store moisture.

The species plays a role in its local ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various desert animals.
