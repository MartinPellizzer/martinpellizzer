Centaurea × saratoi

Centaurea × saratoi, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers, which bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with erect, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and wildflower meadows.

Centaurea × saratoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to support biodiversity in cultivated landscapes.
