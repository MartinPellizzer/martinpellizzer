Cistus majoranifolius

Cistus majoranifolius, commonly known as sweet rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and showy pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for Mediterranean climates. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive fragrance.

The species is also valued for its ecological role, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
