Cynanchum palmeri var. peninsulare

Cynanchum palmeri var. peninsulare, commonly known as the Peninsular milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which is characterized by its milkweed-like appearance and milky sap. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Its ecological significance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
