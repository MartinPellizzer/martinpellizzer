Columnea costaricensis

Columnea costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy coating, aiding in water retention.

Columnea costaricensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
