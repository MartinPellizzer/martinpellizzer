Cirsium luchuense

Cirsium luchuense, commonly known as the Luchuan thistle, is a flowering plant native to the island of Luchuan in southern China.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and covered in stiff hairs, while its flowers are purple to pink in color.

Cirsium luchuense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
