Conyza laurifolia

Conyza laurifolia, commonly known as the laurel leaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow with white ray florets.

Conyza laurifolia is known for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
