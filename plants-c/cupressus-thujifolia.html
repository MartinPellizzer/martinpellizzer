Cupressus thujifolia

Cupressus thujifolia, commonly known as the Japanese cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Cupressaceae and is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. Cupressus thujifolia thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.

Its slow growth rate and long lifespan make it a valuable specimen in both natural and cultivated settings.
