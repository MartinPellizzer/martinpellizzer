Callitris rhomboidea

Callitris rhomboidea, commonly known as the coastal or mountain cedar, is a species of gymnosperm native to Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant has scale-like leaves that are tightly packed, giving it a dense, compact appearance. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is well adapted to dry and saline environments, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
