Cattleya × macguiganii

Cattleya × macguiganii, commonly known as the Macguigan's Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has long, strap-like leaves and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This orchid is popular among horticulturists for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
