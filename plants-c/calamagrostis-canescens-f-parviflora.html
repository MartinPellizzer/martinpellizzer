Calamagrostis canescens f. parviflora

Calamagrostis canescens f. parviflora, commonly known as the slender threadgrass, is a flowering grass species characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and delicate, spike-like inflorescences.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant produces small, pale green to white flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a subtle ornamental value to its habitat. It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses and cereal crops.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for wetland restoration and ecological landscaping.
