Crataegus succulenta var. pisifera

Crataegus succulenta var. pisifera, commonly known as the rock crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in jams and preserves. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural use in drought-tolerant landscaping.
