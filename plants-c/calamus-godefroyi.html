Calamus godefroyi

Calamus godefroyi, commonly known as the Godefroy's arrowroot, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender plant that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its pinnate leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by fibrous, palm-like fruits. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their starchy, edible roots, which are used in traditional cuisines.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appearance and its economic importance in local communities.
