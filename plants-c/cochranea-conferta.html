Cochranea conferta

Cochranea conferta, commonly known as the clustered cochineal, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cactaceae.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of small, spiny stems and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It is adapted to survive in harsh conditions with minimal water, making it a resilient member of desert ecosystems.

Cochranea conferta is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
