Calamus heudelotii

Calamus heudelotii, commonly known as the giant calamus, is a tall, robust plant belonging to the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is characterized by its long, slender rhizomes and large, arrow-shaped leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces striking inflorescences with a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in wet, swampy environments and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
