Corallodendron excelsum

Corallodendron excelsum, commonly known as the giant coral tree, is a striking tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble coral, hence its common name. The plant features woody stems with a rough, bark-like texture and produces clusters of bright red or orange blossoms. Its leaves are typically arranged in whorls and are often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Corallodendron excelsum thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found growing on rocky or coastal cliffs.
