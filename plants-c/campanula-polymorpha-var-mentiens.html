Campanula polymorpha var. mentiens

Campanula polymorpha var. mentiens, commonly known as the blue bell bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
