Cieca oliviformis

Cieca oliviformis, commonly known as the olive-shaped cieca, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive olive-green, elongated leaves that resemble the shape of an olive.

It is native to specific regions with a humid climate and rich, well-draining soil. The plant's foliage is often covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

While not widely cultivated, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
