Chamaesphacos longiflorus

Chamaesphacos longiflorus, commonly known as the long-flowered chamaesphacos, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming basal leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
