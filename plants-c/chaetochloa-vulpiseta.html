Chaetochloa vulpiseta

Chaetochloa vulpiseta, commonly known as the fox tail grass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fox-like appearance, with long, slender leaves and a tufted inflorescence that resembles a fox's tail. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, while the inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a loose, drooping cluster.

Chaetochloa vulpiseta plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
