Calamagrostis naiguatensis

Calamagrostis naiguatensis, commonly known as the Naiguatensis reed, is a grass species native to the Caribbean, particularly found in the Bahamas and the Turks and Caicos Islands.

It is a tall, perennial grass that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, often near coastal areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces inflorescences that are often described as panicle-like, with a delicate, feathery appearance. It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing habitat for various insects and birds.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.
