Centaurea saccensis

Centaurea saccensis, commonly known as the sacrum centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, bracted flower heads.

Centaurea saccensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
