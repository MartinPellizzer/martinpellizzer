Cistus × florentinus var. grandiflorus

Cistus × florentinus var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered rock rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with leathery, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, often with a distinctive yellow center. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rocky or coastal gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
