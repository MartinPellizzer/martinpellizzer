Carex lepidocarpa var. nelmesiana

Carex lepidocarpa var. nelmesiana, commonly known as the Nelmesian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often green to reddish in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

The Nelmesian sedge is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
