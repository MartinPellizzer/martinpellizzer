Corybas betsyae

Corybas betsyae, commonly known as the Betsy's hooded orchid, is a species of terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hood-like floral structure, which is typical of the Corybas genus. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers that are usually clustered in a loose spike. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas.

The botanical features of Corybas betsyae make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
