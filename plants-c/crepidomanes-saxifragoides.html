Crepidomanes saxifragoides

Crepidomanes saxifragoides, commonly known as the rock saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Saxifragaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a suitable plant for rock gardens and alpine habitats.
