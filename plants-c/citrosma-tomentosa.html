Citrosma tomentosa

Citrosma tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy citrosma, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically less than 1 cm in diameter, with a thick, leathery skin. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
