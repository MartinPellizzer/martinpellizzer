Cephalocereus dybowskii

Cephalocereus dybowskii, commonly known as the night blooming cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are covered in spines and are often ribbed. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique blooming habit and striking appearance, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and cactus collections.
