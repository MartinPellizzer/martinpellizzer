Calligonum microcarpum

Calligonum microcarpum, commonly known as the small-fruited goatweed, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It is well adapted to harsh environments, showing resistance to drought and poor soil conditions.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing sandy soils and providing forage for livestock in certain regions.
