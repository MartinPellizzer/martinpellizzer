Cassia brachycarpa

Cassia brachycarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruit cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.

Cassia brachycarpa thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.
