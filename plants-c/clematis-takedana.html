Clematis takedana

Clematis takedana, commonly known as Japanese clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to Japan.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue with a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis takedana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes.
