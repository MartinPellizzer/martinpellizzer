Canavalia cathartica

Canavalia cathartica, commonly known as the coffee bean plant, is a leguminous species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, green pods that contain seeds resembling coffee beans, though it is not related to true coffee. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Canavalia cathartica also has medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
