Catasetum maroaense

Catasetum maroaense, commonly known as the Maroa orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly found in the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure. The plant produces striking, often brightly colored flowers with a specialized mechanism for pollination involving specific insect species. Its leaves are typically elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to the humid and shaded conditions of its habitat.

Catasetum maroaense is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native ecosystems.
