Clerodendrum aucubifolium var. giganteum

Clerodendrum aucubifolium var. giganteum, commonly known as giant elephant ear, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 feet in length.

The foliage is typically a deep green with a velvety texture, and it often exhibits a reddish or purple tinge on the underside. It produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of pink to red, which attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil and partial to full sun exposure.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its dramatic foliage and showy blooms.
