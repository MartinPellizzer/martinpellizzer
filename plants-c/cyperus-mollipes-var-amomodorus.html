Cyperus mollipes var. amomodorus

Cyperus mollipes var. amomodorus, commonly known as the fragrant flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that often form dense clumps, and its leaves are narrow and grassy in appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along water bodies.

Its aromatic foliage and adaptability make it a notable species in wetland ecosystems.
