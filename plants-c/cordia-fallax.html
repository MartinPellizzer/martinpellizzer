Cordia fallax

Cordia fallax, commonly known as the Florida hibiscus, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow throat. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to sandy or well-drained soils.

Cordia fallax is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
