Carex tentaculata var. rostrata

Carex tentaculata var. rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated sedgeland sedge, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. This variety is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of small, inconspicuous flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

The plant plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
