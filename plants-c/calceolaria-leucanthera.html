Calceolaria leucanthera

Calceolaria leucanthera, commonly known as the white slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Calceolaria leucanthera is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate, long-lasting blooms.
