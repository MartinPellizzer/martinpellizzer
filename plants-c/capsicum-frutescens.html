Capsicum frutescens

Capsicum frutescens, commonly known as bird's pepper or chili pepper, is a flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its small, often fiery red or orange fruits. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow or white, and the fruits are used in various cuisines around the world.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and medicinal properties.
