Caraipa myrcioides

Caraipa myrcioides, commonly known as the Caraipa myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, woody capsules containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland forest areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
