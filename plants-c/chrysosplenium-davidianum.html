Chrysosplenium davidianum

Chrysosplenium davidianum, commonly known as David's golden spleenwort, is a member of the Saxifragaceae family and is native to eastern Asia.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features delicate, opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shady gardens for its foliage and subtle floral display.
