Chamelum bodenbenderi

Chamelum bodenbenderi, commonly known as the Bodenbender's ghost orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and striking, pale pink or white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers are often pollinated by specific species of bees and flies, which are attracted by its scent and nectar.

Due to habitat destruction and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
