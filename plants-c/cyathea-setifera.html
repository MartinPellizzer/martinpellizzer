Cyathea setifera

Cyathea setifera, commonly known as the silky tree fern, is a tropical fern species native to Southeast Asia and northern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant has a slow growth rate and is often found in shaded, humid environments such as rainforests. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting its upright structure.

Cyathea setifera plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various small animals and insects.
