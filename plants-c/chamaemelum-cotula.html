Chamaemelum cotula

Chamaemelum cotula, commonly known as common yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing herb with slender, branching stems and small, pale yellow to white flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Chamaemelum cotula is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
