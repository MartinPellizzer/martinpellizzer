Canavalia grandiflora

Canavalia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are widely used in various cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for both its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.

Canavalia grandiflora also plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.
