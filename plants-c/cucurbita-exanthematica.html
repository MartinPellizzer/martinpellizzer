Cucurbita exanthematica

Cucurbita exanthematica, commonly known as the Mexican pumpkin or wild pumpkin, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like cucumbers and squash. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers, followed by fruits that can vary in shape and color. Its vines can grow quite long, often climbing or spreading across the ground.

The fruit is typically used in traditional Mexican cuisine and has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
