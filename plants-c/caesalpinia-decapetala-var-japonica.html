Caesalpinia decapetala var. japonica

Caesalpinia decapetala var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese flame bean, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets that are often a striking yellow or green color. The plant produces bright orange to red cylindrical flower clusters, which bloom in the spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a long, slender pod that splits open to release seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
