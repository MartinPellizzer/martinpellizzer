Cerastium nemorum

Cerastium nemorum, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, hairy leaves that are often heart-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of tiny white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky areas.

Cerastium nemorum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wet conditions.
