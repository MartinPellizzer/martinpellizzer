Clinopodium vargasii

Clinopodium vargasii, commonly known as Vargas' mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves, and small, clustered flowers that are typically lavender to purple in color. It is often found in rocky or open habitats at higher elevations.

Its aromatic foliage and adaptability to harsh environments make it a notable species in its native range.
