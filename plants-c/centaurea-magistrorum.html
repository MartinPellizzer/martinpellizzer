Centaurea magistrorum

Centaurea magistrorum, commonly known as the Magistrate's Centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its striking blue to purple flowers. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, bract-like structures around its inflorescence.

Centaurea magistrorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
