Cleome kelleriana

Cleome kelleriana, commonly known as the Mexican spider flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spindly stems and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture, while its flowers have five petals and a prominent, central column.

Cleome kelleriana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
