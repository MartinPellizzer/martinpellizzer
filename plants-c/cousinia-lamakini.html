Cousinia lamakini

Cousinia lamakini, commonly known as the Persian tansy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Cousinia lamakini is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and unique appearance.
