Calamintha

Calamintha, commonly known as caraway or sweet cicely, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Lamiaceae.

It includes several species, such as Calamintha ciliata and Calamintha nepeta, which are native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. These plants are characterized by their aromatic, often fragrant leaves and small, clustered flowers. They typically grow in moist, shaded environments and are valued for their medicinal and culinary uses.

The genus is also noted for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive foliage and flowering habits.
