Crataegus oxyacantha var. praecox

Crataegus oxyacantha var. praecox, commonly known as the early hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed sites.
