Cassia stenophylla

Cassia stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically narrow and arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by elongated pods containing several seeds. Cassia stenophylla is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
