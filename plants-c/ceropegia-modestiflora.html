Ceropegia modestiflora

Ceropegia modestiflora, commonly known as the "Baby's Breath" or "Lion's Tail," is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, succulent stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture, aiding in water retention.

Ceropegia modestiflora is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of care in indoor and outdoor settings.
