Convolvulus purshianus

Convolvulus purshianus, commonly known as the Pursh's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces showy, five-petaled blooms in shades of pink to purple, typically in the spring and early summer. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often twining.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including gardens and along roadsides.
