Cinnagrostis viridiflavescens var. viridiflavescens

Cinnagrostis viridiflavescens var. viridiflavescens, commonly known as the yellow false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish-green flowers and basal rosette of leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and streambanks. It has a short flowering period in the spring and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant gardening.
