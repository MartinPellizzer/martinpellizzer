Cyperus holoschoenus

Cyperus holoschoenus, commonly known as the marsh flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered spikelets. This plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. Cyperus holoschoenus is often found in coastal areas and can tolerate brackish water conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
