Crassula caffraria

Crassula caffraria, commonly known as the African ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red during the warmer months. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact growth habit and drought tolerance.

Crassula caffraria is also valued for its ability to thrive in well-drained soil and full sun conditions.
