Cosmos ocellatus

Cosmos ocellatus, commonly known as the starry false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, pink to purple, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. It grows in a variety of habitats, including prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

Cosmos ocellatus is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 3 feet, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
