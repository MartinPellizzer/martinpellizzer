Cardamine corsica

Cardamine corsica, commonly known as Corsican watercress, is a small herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as along streams and in woodland areas. The plant features delicate, fern-like leaves that are often submerged or partially submerged in water. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces tiny, elongated fruits.

Botanically, it is characterized by its basal leaves and its ability to thrive in aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
