Chamaecrista tragacanthoides var. tragacanthoides

Chamaecrista tragacanthoides var. tragacanthoides, commonly known as the yellow burr legume, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in clusters. The plant is notable for its distinctive, spiny, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental or for its potential ecological benefits.
