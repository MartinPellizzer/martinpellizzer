Cassine rotundata

Cassine rotundata, commonly known as the roundleaf cassine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant features simple, alternate, and usually elliptic or ovate leaves that are often glossy and dark green. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and typically have five petals.

Cassine rotundata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
