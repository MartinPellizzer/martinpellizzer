Centaurea dalmatica subsp. lubenicensis

Centaurea dalmatica subsp.

lubenicensis, commonly known as the Dalmatian knapweed subspecies lubenicensis, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, bristly stems and yellow to orange flower heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
