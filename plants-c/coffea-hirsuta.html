Coffea hirsuta

Coffea hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy coffee plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries containing seeds used for coffee production. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and potential for cultivation, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
