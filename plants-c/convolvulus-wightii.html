Convolvulus wightii

Convolvulus wightii, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a silvery, powdery coating. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
