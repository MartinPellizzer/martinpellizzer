Chisocheton brachyanthus

Chisocheton brachyanthus, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant belonging to the Clusiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with elliptical leaflets and can grow as a shrub or small tree. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.
