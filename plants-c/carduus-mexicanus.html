Carduus mexicanus

Carduus mexicanus, commonly known as Mexican thistle, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed areas such as fields and roadsides. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in poor soil conditions.

However, it is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and competitive growth.
