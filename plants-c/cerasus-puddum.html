Cerasus puddum

Cerasus puddum, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or Japanese dwarf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible but typically tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental gardens.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping and bonsai cultivation.
