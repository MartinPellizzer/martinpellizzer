Cucumis melo var. hooseini

Cucumis melo var. hooseini, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a variety of melon native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, thin fruit with a smooth, light green rind and a crisp, sweet flesh. The plant is an annual vine that thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It produces yellow flowers and is typically harvested when the fruit is still immature.

This variety is prized for its mild flavor and versatility in both fresh consumption and culinary use.
