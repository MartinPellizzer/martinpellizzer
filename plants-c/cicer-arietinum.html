Cicer arietinum

Cicer arietinum, commonly known as chickpea, is an annual leguminous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and pods containing edible seeds. The plant is valued for its high protein content and ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility. Chickpeas are cultivated in various climates and are a staple in many cuisines worldwide.

They are also used in traditional medicine for their nutritional and health benefits.
