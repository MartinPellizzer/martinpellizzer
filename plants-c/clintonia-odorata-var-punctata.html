Clintonia odorata var. punctata

Clintonia odorata var. punctata, commonly known as the spotted white-flowered dogwood, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white, bell-shaped flowers that are often speckled with red or purple markings, giving it its common name. The plant has elliptic to ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland understories.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
