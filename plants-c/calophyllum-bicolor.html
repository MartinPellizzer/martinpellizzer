Calophyllum bicolor

Calophyllum bicolor, commonly known as the yellow mangostin, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fruit is a round, segmented berry that turns from green to yellow when ripe.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
