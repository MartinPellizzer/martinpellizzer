Carduus syncephalus

Carduus syncephalus, commonly known as the Jerusalem artichoke, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by edible tubers that resemble ginger in texture. It is often cultivated for its nutritional value and has been used historically for food and medicinal purposes.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to a variety of soil types and climates.
