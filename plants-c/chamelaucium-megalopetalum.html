Chamelaucium megalopetalum

Chamelaucium megalopetalum, commonly known as the large-petaled waratah, is a striking Australian plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is native to the eastern regions of Australia, particularly in New South Wales and Queensland. The plant is characterized by its large, showy, crimson-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Chamelaucium megalopetalum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
