Cruciata glabra f. robusta

Cruciata glabra f. robusta, commonly known as the robust form of the smooth cruciata, is a variant of the genus Cruciata, which belongs to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its sturdy, upright growth habit and robust leaves that are typically lance-shaped and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often in shades of pink or red. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in arid or semi-arid climates.
