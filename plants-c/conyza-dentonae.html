Conyza dentonae

Conyza dentonae, commonly known as the Texas fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a serrated edge, while its flowers are yellow with white ray florets.

Conyza dentonae is often found in arid environments and is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient soils.
