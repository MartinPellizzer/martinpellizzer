Cocos fusiformis

Cocos fusiformis, commonly known as the fusiform coconut, is a unique species within the palm family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated fruit that resembles a spindle shape, hence its scientific name. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline environments, making it a hardy species. Its leaves are large and pinnately compound, providing shade and contributing to the ecosystem.

The fruit contains a single seed, which is dispersed by water, facilitating its spread along shorelines.
