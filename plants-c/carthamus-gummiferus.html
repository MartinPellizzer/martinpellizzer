Carthamus gummiferus

Carthamus gummiferus, commonly known as the gumweed or gum plant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, glandular leaves that produce a sticky resin. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed or arid environments. Its scientific name reflects its ability to produce a gummy substance, which has been used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its medicinal properties.
