Chaerophyllum byzantinum var. hirtum

Chaerophyllum byzantinum var. hirtum, commonly known as the hairy false parsley, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and border plantings.
