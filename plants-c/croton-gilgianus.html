Croton gilgianus

Croton gilgianus, commonly known as the African croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful foliage. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant leaves.

Croton gilgianus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
