Commelina saxatilis

Commelina saxatilis, commonly known as the rock commelina, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its erect, often hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in rocky or disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Commelina saxatilis is known for its adaptability and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
