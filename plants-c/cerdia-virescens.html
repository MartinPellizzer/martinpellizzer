Cerdia virescens

Cerdia virescens, commonly known as the green cerdia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, herbaceous stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Cerdia virescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
