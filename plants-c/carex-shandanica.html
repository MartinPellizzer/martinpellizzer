Carex shandanica

Carex shandanica, commonly known as the Shandong sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or marshy areas. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated.

Carex shandanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
