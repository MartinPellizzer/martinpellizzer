Chloranthus inconspicuus

Chloranthus inconspicuus, commonly known as the inconspicuous chloranthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Chloranthaceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in umbels and are often overlooked due to their subtle appearance.

The fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its role in forest ecosystems.
