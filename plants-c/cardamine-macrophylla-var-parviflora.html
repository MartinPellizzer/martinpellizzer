Cardamine macrophylla var. parviflora

Cardamine macrophylla var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. Native to moist, shaded environments in temperate regions, it thrives in rocky or streamside habitats. The plant has a compact growth habit with basal leaves that are often submerged or floating.

Its reproductive structures are adapted for pollination by small insects, contributing to its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
