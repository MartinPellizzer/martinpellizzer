Camelina hispida var. grandiflora

Camelina hispida var. grandiflora, commonly known as the hairy camelina, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in sagebrush communities.

Camelina hispida var. grandiflora is also known for its potential as a wild oilseed crop.
