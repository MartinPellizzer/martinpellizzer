Calamus hystrix

Calamus hystrix, commonly known as the zigzag sedge, is a perennial plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, zigzag-shaped stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Calamus hystrix typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often near water bodies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
