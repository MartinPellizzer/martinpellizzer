Calytrix acutifolia

Calytrix acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical, red or pink flowers that resemble a bottlebrush. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils.

Calytrix acutifolia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained conditions.
