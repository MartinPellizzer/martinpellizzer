Centaurea reflexa

Centaurea reflexa, commonly known as the reflexed cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with reflexed (bent backward) leaves that give it its common name.

Centaurea reflexa is known for its ornamental value and has been cultivated for its attractive appearance.
