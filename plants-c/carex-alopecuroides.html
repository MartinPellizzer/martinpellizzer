Carex alopecuroides

Carex alopecuroides, commonly known as silver sedge, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

Carex alopecuroides is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its ornamental foliage and low maintenance requirements.
