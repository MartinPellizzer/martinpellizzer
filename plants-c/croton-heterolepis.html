Croton heterolepis

Croton heterolepis, commonly known as the white croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that range in color from bright green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom during the wet season. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with a height ranging from 1 to 3 meters.

Croton heterolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
