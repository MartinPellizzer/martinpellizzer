Conopodium denudatum var. ramosum

Conopodium denudatum var. ramosum, commonly known as the branched bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, floating stems and tiny, bladder-like structures called vesicles.

These vesicles help the plant float and are used to trap small organisms, which the plant then absorbs through osmosis. It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and ditches. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Its reproductive structures are inconspicuous, with small flowers that are usually submerged and pollinated by water currents.
