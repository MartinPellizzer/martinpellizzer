Cirsium brachiatum

Cirsium brachiatum, commonly known as the bent-thorned thistle, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and deeply lobed, gray-green leaves. The plant produces purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Cirsium brachiatum is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
