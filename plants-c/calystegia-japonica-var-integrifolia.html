Calystegia japonica var. integrifolia

Calystegia japonica var. integrifolia, commonly known as the Japanese morning glory, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically 5-10 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, opening in the morning and closing by midday. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is known for its rapid growth and is often used in ornamental gardening.
