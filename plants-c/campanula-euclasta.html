Campanula euclasta

Campanula euclasta, commonly known as the rock bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often used in rock gardens and is valued for its ornamental beauty and drought tolerance.
