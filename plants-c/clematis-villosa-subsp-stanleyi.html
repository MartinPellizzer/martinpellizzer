Clematis villosa subsp. stanleyi

Clematis villosa subsp.

stanleyi, commonly known as Stanley's Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America. It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, often covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its vigorous growth habit requires support, such as a trellis or fence, for optimal display.
