Calamintha pseudosimensis

Calamintha pseudosimensis, commonly known as the Sierra calamintha, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of California.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or open habitats.

Calamintha pseudosimensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
