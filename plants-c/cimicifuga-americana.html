Cimicifuga americana

Cimicifuga americana, commonly known as American bugbane, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features tall, straight stems that bear clusters of white, lace-like flowers at the top. The plant has compound leaves with feather-like divisions, and its roots are commonly used in herbal medicine. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Cimicifuga americana is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional remedies.
