Curcuma erubescens

Curcuma erubescens, commonly known as red turmeric, is a rhizomatous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant orange to red-colored rhizomes. The plant produces tall, leafy stems with small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its rhizomes are valued for their medicinal properties and are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The botanical features of Curcuma erubescens make it an important species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
