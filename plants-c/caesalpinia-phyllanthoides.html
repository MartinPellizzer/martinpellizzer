Caesalpinia phyllanthoides

Caesalpinia phyllanthoides, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble cascading showers of flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in agricultural settings.
