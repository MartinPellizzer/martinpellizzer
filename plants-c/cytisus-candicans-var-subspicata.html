Cytisus candicans var. subspicata

Cytisus candicans var. subspicata, commonly known as the golden broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen, it is often used in ecological restoration projects.
