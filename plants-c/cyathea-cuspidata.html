Cyathea cuspidata

Cyathea cuspidata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical environments. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its tall stem, while the fronds are responsible for photosynthesis.

Cyathea cuspidata plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
