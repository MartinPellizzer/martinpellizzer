Chabertia discedens

Chabertia discedens, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming habit with narrow, grass-like leaves and showy blue to violet flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Chabertia discedens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
