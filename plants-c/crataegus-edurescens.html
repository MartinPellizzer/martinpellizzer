Crataegus edurescens

Crataegus edurescens, commonly known as the Florida hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red to orange berries in the fall. Its botanical characteristics include a smooth, grayish bark and a preference for well-drained soils in sunny to partially shaded locations.

The species is often found in moist habitats such as swamps, stream banks, and pine flatwoods.
