Calea zacatechichi

Calea zacatechichi, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its unique, showy flowers that open at night. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that emit a faint scent to attract nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Calea zacatechichi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and cultural significance in traditional Mexican medicine.
