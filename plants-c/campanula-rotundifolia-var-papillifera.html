Campanula rotundifolia var. papillifera

Campanula rotundifolia var. papillifera, commonly known as the roundleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are rounded and often covered in small, raised bumps called papillae, giving it its specific name. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ease of care.
