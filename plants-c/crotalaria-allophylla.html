Crotalaria allophylla

Crotalaria allophylla, commonly known as the smallflower cowpea, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of many legumes. It produces small, hard pods containing several seeds, which are often used as a food source in some regions.

Crotalaria allophylla is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in agricultural settings.
