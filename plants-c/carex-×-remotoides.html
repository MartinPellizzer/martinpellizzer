Carex × remotoides

Carex × remotoides, commonly known as the hybrid sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a sterile hybrid resulting from the cross between Carex stricta and Carex microphylla, and it is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest floors. The plant produces dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, grass-green leaves that are often clustered at the base. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, inconspicuous flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex × remotoides plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates in its native ecosystems.
