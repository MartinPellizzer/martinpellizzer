Coleus tenuicaulis

Coleus tenuicaulis, commonly known as the slender-leaved coleus, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, narrow leaves that are typically a vibrant shade of purple or red. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale green or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
