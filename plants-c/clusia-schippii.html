Clusia schippii

Clusia schippii, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant is evergreen and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas or disturbed soils. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping, while its ability to tolerate salt spray makes it suitable for seaside gardens.

The flowers are typically pollinated by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
