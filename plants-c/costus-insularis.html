Costus insularis

Costus insularis, commonly known as the island costus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric, and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by colorful bracts, typically in shades of red or yellow. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a glossy surface, while the stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Costus insularis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens due to its vibrant appearance and resilience in warm climates.
