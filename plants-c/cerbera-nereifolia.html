Cerbera nereifolia

Cerbera nereifolia, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Philippines, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are usually yellow or orange, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies in its natural habitat.

The plant has cultural and medicinal significance in various regions of Southeast Asia.
