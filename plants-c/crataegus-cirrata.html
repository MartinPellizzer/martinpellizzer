Crataegus cirrata

Crataegus cirrata, commonly known as the curly thornapple, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces red, juicy berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Crataegus cirrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
