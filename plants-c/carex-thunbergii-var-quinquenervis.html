Carex thunbergii var. quinquenervis

Carex thunbergii var. quinquenervis, commonly known as the five-veined sedge, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its leaves are usually a deep green, with prominent veins that give it its common name.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
