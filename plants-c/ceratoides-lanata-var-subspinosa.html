Ceratoides lanata var. subspinosa

Ceratoides lanata var. subspinosa, commonly known as the silky goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a soft, silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring, which are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. Its leaves are typically gray-green and covered with fine, soft hairs.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sagebrush steppe and rocky habitats.
