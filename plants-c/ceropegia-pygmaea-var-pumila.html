Ceropegia pygmaea var. pumila

Ceropegia pygmaea var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf string of hearts, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features slender, trailing stems with heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or yellow, which are pollinated by specific insects. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appearance, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and hanging baskets.
