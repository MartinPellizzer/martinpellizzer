Carex novae-angliae

Carex novae-angliae, commonly known as the New England sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated, and it thrives in full to partial sunlight.

Carex novae-angliae is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
