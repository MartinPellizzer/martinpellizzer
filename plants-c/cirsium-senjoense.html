Cirsium senjoense

Cirsium senjoense, commonly known as the Japanese thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Cirsium senjoense is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional Japanese medicine.
