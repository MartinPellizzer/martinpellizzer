Cytisus decumbens var. glabratus

Cytisus decumbens var. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to spread along the ground. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

This variety is adapted to arid conditions, making it a hardy plant in dry, rocky soils.
