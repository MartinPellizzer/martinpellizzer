Carex dipsacea

Carex dipsacea, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, bogs, and wet meadows. Its flowering structures consist of small, three-ranked spikelets, and it produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex dipsacea is often found in temperate regions and is an important component of wetland ecosystems.
