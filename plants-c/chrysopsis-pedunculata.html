Chrysopsis pedunculata

Chrysopsis pedunculata, commonly known as the slender goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, which aid in its propagation across arid environments.

Chrysopsis pedunculata is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
