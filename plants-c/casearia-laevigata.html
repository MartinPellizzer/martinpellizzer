Casearia laevigata

Casearia laevigata, commonly known as the silky casearia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
