Cyperus zonatus

Cyperus zonatus, commonly known as the zoned flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Cyperus zonatus typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Its flowering spikes are usually small and clustered, producing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.
