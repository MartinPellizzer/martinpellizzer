Crotalaria gazensis subsp. herbacea

Crotalaria gazensis subsp.

herbacea, commonly known as the herbaceous crotalaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of yellow, spurred flowers.

The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The species is notable for its potential medicinal properties and its role in nitrogen fixation in soil.
