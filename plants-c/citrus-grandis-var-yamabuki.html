Citrus grandis var. yamabuki

Citrus grandis var. yamabuki, commonly known as the Yamabuki mandarin, is a citrus variety native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, sweet, and seedless fruit with a thin, easy-to-peel rind. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, with dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.

The fruit is particularly popular during the winter months and is prized for its mild flavor and high vitamin C content.
