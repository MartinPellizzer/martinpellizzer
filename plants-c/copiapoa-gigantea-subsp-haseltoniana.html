Copiapoa gigantea subsp. haseltoniana

Copiapoa gigantea subsp.

haseltoniana, commonly known as the Giant Copiapoa, is a cacti species native to the Atacama Desert in Chile. It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem that can reach up to 3 meters in height.

The plant produces large, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid conditions.

It is a slow-growing species that requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.
