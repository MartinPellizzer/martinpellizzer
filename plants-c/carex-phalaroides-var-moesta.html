Carex phalaroides var. moesta

Carex phalaroides var. moesta, commonly known as the moody sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. This variant is typically found in wetland or moist soil environments, often in marshes or along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, upright leaves and is known for its ability to thrive in waterlogged conditions.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
