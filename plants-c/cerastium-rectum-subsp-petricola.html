Cerastium rectum subsp. petricola

Cerastium rectum subsp.

petricola, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant features small, spoon-shaped leaves and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in rocky areas.
