Cromidon plantaginis

Cromidon plantaginis, commonly known as the common bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is often found in grassy areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
