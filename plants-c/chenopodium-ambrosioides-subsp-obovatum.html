Chenopodium ambrosioides subsp. obovatum

Chenopodium ambrosioides subsp.

obovatum, commonly known as pigweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas. It features alternate, ovate to obovate leaves that are often hairy and have a bluish-green color.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is considered a weed in many regions.
