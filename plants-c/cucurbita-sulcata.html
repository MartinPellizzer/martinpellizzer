Cucurbita sulcata

Cucurbita sulcata, commonly known as the African horned cucumber, is a unique member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, warty, and spiky exterior, which gives it a striking appearance. The plant produces large, elongated fruits that can grow up to 3 feet in length. Its leaves are broad and lobed, typical of many cucurbit species.

This plant is native to Africa and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
