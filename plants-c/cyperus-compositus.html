Cyperus compositus

Cyperus compositus, commonly known as the flatleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland areas in Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Cyperus compositus produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
