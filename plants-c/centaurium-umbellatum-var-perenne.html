Centaurium umbellatum var. perenne

Centaurium umbellatum var. perenne, commonly known as the perennial mouse-ear or perennial centaury, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the family Gentianaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in a loose umbel. The plant is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It is known for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is perennial, meaning it lives for more than two years, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
