Chamaesyce festiva

Chamaesyce festiva, commonly known as the festive sandmat, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with fleshy, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It is adapted to survive in drought conditions, storing water in its succulent stems and leaves.

Chamaesyce festiva is often found in rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine by local communities.
