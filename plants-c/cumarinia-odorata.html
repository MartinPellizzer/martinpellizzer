Cumarinia odorata

Cumarinia odorata, commonly known as the sweetgrass or the fragrant grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that emit a sweet, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, adding to its ornamental appeal. Cumarinia odorata is often cultivated for its pleasant fragrance and is used in traditional medicine and perfumery.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and natural ecosystems.
