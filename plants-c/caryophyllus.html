Caryophyllus

Caryophyllus, commonly known as cloves, is a flowering plant native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark brown flower buds. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into the spice used in cooking and traditional medicine. The essential oil extracted from its flowers contains eugenol, which contributes to its strong scent and medicinal properties.

Caryophyllus is widely cultivated for its economic and culinary value.
