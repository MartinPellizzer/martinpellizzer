Carex thailandica

Carex thailandica, commonly known as Thai sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. Its flowering spikes consist of small, green to brownish flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Carex thailandica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
