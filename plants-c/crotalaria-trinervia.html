Crotalaria trinervia

Crotalaria trinervia, commonly known as the three-veined cowpea or small cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its legume pods and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant has compound leaves with three main veins, giving it its scientific name. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated seed pods.

Crotalaria trinervia is often grown as a forage crop and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
