Crepis biennis var. pusilla

Crepis biennis var. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf annual hawk's-beard, is a small flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 10 cm, with hairy stems and basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape. It produces yellow flowers from late spring to early autumn, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
