Charia indeniensis

Charia indeniensis, commonly known as the Indeniense Cactus, is a flowering plant native to the dry regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow or white flowers. The plant has adapted to arid environments through its succulent structure, which stores water efficiently. Its flowers are typically short-lived but attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
