Cleochroma

Cleochroma, commonly known as the "butterfly plant," is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking, butterfly-shaped flowers. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are typically bright red or orange, with a prominent yellow throat, attracting various pollinators.

Cleochroma thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
