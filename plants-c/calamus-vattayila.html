Calamus vattayila

Calamus vattayila, commonly known as the red-flowered calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bright red flowers that bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are long and narrow, often used in traditional crafts and as thatching materials.

Calamus vattayila is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities in gardens.
