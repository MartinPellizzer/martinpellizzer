Carex festucacea

Carex festucacea, commonly known as slender flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex festucacea plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
