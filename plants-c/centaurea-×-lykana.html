Centaurea × lykana

Centaurea × lykana, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers consist of both ray florets and disk florets, creating a composite flower head.

Centaurea × lykana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
