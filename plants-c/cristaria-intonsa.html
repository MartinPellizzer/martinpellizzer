Cristaria intonsa

Cristaria intonsa, commonly known as the glassy marsh fern, is a species of aquatic fern found in wetlands and marshy areas.

It has delicate, feathery fronds that are often submerged or floating on the water's surface. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called soruses, which are typically located on the underside of its leaves. Cristaria intonsa thrives in nutrient-rich, still or slow-moving water environments.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important indicator species for wetland health.
