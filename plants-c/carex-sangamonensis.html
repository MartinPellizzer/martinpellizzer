Carex sangamonensis

Carex sangamonensis, commonly known as the Sangamon sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It has grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or brown in color.

Carex sangamonensis plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
