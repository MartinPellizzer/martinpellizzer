Cleome montevidensis

Cleome montevidensis, commonly known as the yellow cleome, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cleomaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and serrated leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cleome montevidensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
