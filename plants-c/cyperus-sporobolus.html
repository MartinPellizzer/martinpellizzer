Cyperus sporobolus

Cyperus sporobolus, commonly known as the purple flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, often growing in wet or moist soils. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or purple in color.

Cyperus sporobolus is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along waterways, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
