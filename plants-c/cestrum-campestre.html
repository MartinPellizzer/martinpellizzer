Cestrum campestre

Cestrum campestre, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.
