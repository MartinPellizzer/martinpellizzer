Citrus × sinensis var. vulgaris

Citrus × sinensis var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common orange.

This variety is a hybrid citrus plant originating from Southeast Asia, characterized by its orange-colored, segmented fruits. The fruit's peel contains essential oils and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications. The plant thrives in subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

It is widely cultivated for its juice, zest, and fruit, contributing significantly to global agriculture.
