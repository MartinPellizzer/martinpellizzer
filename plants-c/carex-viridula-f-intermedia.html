Carex viridula f. intermedia

Carex viridula f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-green leaves that form dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
