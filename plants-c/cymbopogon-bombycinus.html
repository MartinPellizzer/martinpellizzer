Cymbopogon bombycinus

Cymbopogon bombycinus, commonly known as the golden grass or sweet grass, is a species of grass native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, golden-yellow inflorescences and aromatic leaves, which emit a pleasant, sweet scent. This plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and forest clearings. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported calming and antiseptic properties.

The plant's botanical features make it both ecologically significant and culturally valued in various regions.
