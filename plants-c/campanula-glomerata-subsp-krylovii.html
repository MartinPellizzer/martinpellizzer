Campanula glomerata subsp. krylovii

Campanula glomerata subsp.

krylovii, commonly known as the Krylov's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
