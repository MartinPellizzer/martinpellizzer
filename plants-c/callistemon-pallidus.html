Callistemon pallidus

Callistemon pallidus, commonly known as the grey bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical, pale pink to greyish red flower spikes that resemble bottle brushes. The plant typically grows in heathlands and dry open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered in a fine, silvery bloom.

Callistemon pallidus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its unique floral display.
