Cousinia mozaffarianii

Cousinia mozaffarianii, commonly known as the Persian sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Iranian plateau.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments.

Cousinia mozaffarianii is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
