Clathrotropis rosea

Clathrotropis rosea, commonly known as the pink flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange during the autumn season. The tree produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark is smooth and light gray, with a tendency to peel in thin layers.
