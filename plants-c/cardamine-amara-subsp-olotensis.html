Cardamine amara subsp. olotensis

Cardamine amara subsp.

olotensis, commonly known as the Olotenese water crowfoot, is a perennial aquatic plant native to parts of Europe. It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds, streams, and wet meadows.

The plant has finely divided, floating leaves that form a dense mat on the water surface. Its small, white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early spring.

The subspecies is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-rich waters.
