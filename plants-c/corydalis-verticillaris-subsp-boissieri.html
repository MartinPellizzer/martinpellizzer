Corydalis verticillaris subsp. boissieri

Corydalis verticillaris subsp.

boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's clustered fumitory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and herbal gardens.
