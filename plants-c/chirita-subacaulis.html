Chirita subacaulis

Chirita subacaulis, commonly known as the small-stemmed gesneriad, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a member of the Gesneriaceae family, characterized by its fleshy leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows as a trailing or shrub-like species, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are usually small, tubular, and come in shades of white, pink, or purple, attracting pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in greenhouses or shaded gardens.
