Cornuella venezuelanensis

Cornuella venezuelanensis, commonly known as the Venezuelan horned orchid, is a unique species native to Venezuela.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, horn-like floral structure. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the tropical rainforests of the country. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish in color, with a complex arrangement of petals and sepals.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Cornuella venezuelanensis is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
