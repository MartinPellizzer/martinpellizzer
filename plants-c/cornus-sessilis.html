Cornus sessilis

Cornus sessilis, commonly known as red osier dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown stems that become more pronounced in winter. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during early spring, followed by red or purple berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn yellow in autumn.

Cornus sessilis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
