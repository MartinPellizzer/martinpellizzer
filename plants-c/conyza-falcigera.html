Conyza falcigera

Conyza falcigera, commonly known as the narrowleaf horseweed, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Conyza falcigera is known for its ability to produce a large number of small seeds, which are dispersed by the wind.
