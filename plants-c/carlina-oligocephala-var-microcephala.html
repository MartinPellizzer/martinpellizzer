Carlina oligocephala var. microcephala

Carlina oligocephala var. microcephala, commonly known as the narrow-headed thistle, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and dense clusters of blue to purple flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun exposure.

The species is noted for its showy composite flowers, which attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
