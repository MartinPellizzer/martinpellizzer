Carex dimorphotheca

Carex dimorphotheca, commonly known as the two-form flower sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swales.

Carex dimorphotheca is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
