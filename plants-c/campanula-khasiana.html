Campanula khasiana

Campanula khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are lanceolate and hairy, providing a textured appearance.

Campanula khasiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
