Cyrtochilum aurantiacum

Cyrtochilum aurantiacum, commonly known as the orange-lipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and its striking orange-red petals and sepals. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Cyrtochilum aurantiacum thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

It is a popular species among orchid enthusiasts due to its vibrant color and relatively easy care requirements.
