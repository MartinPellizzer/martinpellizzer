Cestrum psittacinum

Cestrum psittacinum, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces showy, tubular yellow flowers, which are typically borne in clusters. The plant is characterized by its opposite, simple, ovate leaves and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The flowers attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees, making it an important plant in ecological settings.
