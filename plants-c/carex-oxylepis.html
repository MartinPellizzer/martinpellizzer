Carex oxylepis

Carex oxylepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This species typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct midrib, and it produces small, clustered spikes of flowers.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
