Calceolaria arborea

Calceolaria arborea, commonly known as the tree calceolaria, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to several meters in height, featuring a woody stem and dense foliage. Its most distinctive feature is its showy, calyx-like flowers, which are typically orange or yellow and resemble a pouch or a slipper. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its unique morphology and vibrant blooms make it a notable species in botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
