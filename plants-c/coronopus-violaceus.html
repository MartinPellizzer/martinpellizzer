Coronopus violaceus

Coronopus violaceus, commonly known as the violet crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and a branched stem, often growing in dry, rocky habitats. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
