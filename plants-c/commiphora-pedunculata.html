Commiphora pedunculata

Commiphora pedunculata, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small, deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It produces a resinous sap that has been traditionally used for its medicinal and aromatic properties. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The tree is valued for its unique fragrance and has been utilized in traditional medicine for centuries.
