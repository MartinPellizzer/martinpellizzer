Cyperus surinamensis var. surinamensis

Cyperus surinamensis var. surinamensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually narrow and slightly pointed. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike that bears small, three-lobed flowers.

This variety is characterized by its adaptability to moist soils and its ability to thrive in a variety of aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
