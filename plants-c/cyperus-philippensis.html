Cyperus philippensis

Cyperus philippensis, commonly known as the Philippine flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Cyperus philippensis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
