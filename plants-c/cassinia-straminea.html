Cassinia straminea

Cassinia straminea, commonly known as the slender cassinia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its yellow flower heads are surrounded by pale green bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Cassinia straminea is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
