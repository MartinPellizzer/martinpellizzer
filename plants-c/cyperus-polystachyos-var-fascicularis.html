Cyperus polystachyos var. fascicularis

Cyperus polystachyos var. fascicularis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features linear, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and arranged in a dense, cylindrical cluster. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, wetlands, and along waterways.

Its botanical structure makes it well-adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
