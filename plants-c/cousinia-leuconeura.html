Cousinia leuconeura

Cousinia leuconeura, commonly known as the white hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Cousinia leuconeura is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and visual interest.
