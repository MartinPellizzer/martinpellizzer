Campanula persicifolia subsp. subpyrenaica

Campanula persicifolia subsp.

subpyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.

The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces slender, flowering stems that bear bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet. Its flowers are typically tubular with five lobes, characteristic of the Campanulaceae family.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in mountain ecosystems.
