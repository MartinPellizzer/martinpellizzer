Cyperus mohrii

Cyperus mohrii, commonly known as Mohri's flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along waterways. Its slender, triangular stems are covered in fine, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Cyperus mohrii is adapted to humid environments and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
