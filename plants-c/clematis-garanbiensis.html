Clematis garanbiensis

Clematis garanbiensis, commonly known as the Brazilian clematis, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its climbing growth habit and showy, usually white or pink flowers. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, green berries as fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
