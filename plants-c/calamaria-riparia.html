Calamaria riparia

Calamaria riparia, commonly known as the river calamaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 60 cm in height, often with a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Calamaria riparia thrives in moist soils and is often found along riverbanks and in marshy areas.
