Cassinia cinerea

Cassinia cinerea, commonly known as silver everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flower heads. The plant has silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands.

Cassinia cinerea is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
