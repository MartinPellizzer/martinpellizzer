Carex stricta f. pendula

Carex stricta f. pendula, commonly known as the drooping bulrush, is a variant of the common bulrush.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America. The plant features slender, hanging inflorescences that give it a distinctive, weeping appearance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense clumps.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic life.
