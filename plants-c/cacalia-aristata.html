Cacalia aristata

Cacalia aristata, commonly known as the purple crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy purple to pink flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cacalia aristata is typically found in rocky or sandy soils in dry, open habitats.
