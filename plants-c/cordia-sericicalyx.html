Cordia sericicalyx

Cordia sericicalyx, commonly known as the silky cordia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale green bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Cordia sericicalyx is often found in disturbed habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
