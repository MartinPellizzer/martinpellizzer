Conophytum spathulatum

Conophytum spathulatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved stone plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its striking appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
