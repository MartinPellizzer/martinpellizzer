Cistus heterophyllus nothosubsp. marzoi

Cistus heterophyllus nothosubsp.

marzoi, commonly known as the Alboraya rock rose, is a hybrid plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and aromatic, glandular leaves that emit a distinctive scent.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional herbal medicine.
