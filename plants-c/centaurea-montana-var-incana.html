Centaurea montana var. incana

Centaurea montana var. incana, commonly known as the Hoary Centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its grayish-blue, fuzzy leaves and striking cobalt-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are composed of a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
