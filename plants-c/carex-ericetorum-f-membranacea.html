Carex ericetorum f. membranacea

Carex ericetorum f. membranacea, commonly known as the membranaceous sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. This subspecies is typically found in moist, acidic soils in woodland or heathland environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually wind-pollinated.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
