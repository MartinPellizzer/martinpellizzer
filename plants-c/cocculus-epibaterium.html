Cocculus epibaterium

Cocculus epibaterium, commonly known as the Indian cocklebur, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, viscid substance. Its botanical features include a woody stem, palmately lobed leaves, and a unique, tuberous root system.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
