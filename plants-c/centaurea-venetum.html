Centaurea venetum

Centaurea venetum, commonly known as Venetian knapweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking purple to pink flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
