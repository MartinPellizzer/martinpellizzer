Cissus peltata

Cissus peltata, commonly known as the stonecrop fig or heartleaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Cissus peltata is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
