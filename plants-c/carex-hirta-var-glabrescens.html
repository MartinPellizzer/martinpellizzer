Carex hirta var. glabrescens

Carex hirta var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth sedges, is a variety of the sedge plant characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and cylindrical flowering spikes.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The leaves are usually smooth and glabrous, giving it its common name, while the inflorescence consists of small, three-lobed flowers arranged in a spike. This plant is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.

It is also sometimes used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability.
