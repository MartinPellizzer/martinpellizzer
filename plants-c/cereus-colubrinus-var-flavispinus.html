Cereus colubrinus var. flavispinus

Cereus colubrinus var. flavispinus, commonly known as the yellow-spined cactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically green or yellowish in color. The plant produces white flowers that bloom at night, a common trait among many cacti. It has spines that are yellow or orange, giving it its distinctive appearance.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to thrive.
