Carex nova var. pelocarpa

Carex nova var. pelocarpa, commonly known as the New Zealand sedge, is a perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are often hidden among its foliage.

Carex nova var. pelocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
