Convolvulus cneorum

Convolvulus cneorum, commonly known as the Cyprus morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 30 centimeters tall, featuring slender, branching stems and hairy leaves. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

The plant is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
