Centaurea alba var. costae

Centaurea alba var. costae, commonly known as the white centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its pale blue to white flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and gravelly areas. It is an annual or biennial plant, producing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
