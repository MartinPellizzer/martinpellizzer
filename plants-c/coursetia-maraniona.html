Coursetia maraniona

Coursetia maraniona, commonly known as the Maranon coursetia, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features small, compound leaves and produces yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and it thrives in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
