Crinum ornatum var. latifolium

Crinum ornatum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf crinum, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, strap-like leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and lavender, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its dramatic foliage and fragrant blooms.
