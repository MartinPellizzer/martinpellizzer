Clitoria chapadensis

Clitoria chapadensis, commonly known as the Brazilian butterfly pea, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It features striking violet-blue flowers that resemble butterflies, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing along fences or in moist, well-drained soils.

Clitoria chapadensis is also valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
