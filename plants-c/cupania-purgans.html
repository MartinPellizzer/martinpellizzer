Cupania purgans

Cupania purgans, commonly known as the American sarsaparilla, is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds, and it is often found in moist, wooded areas.

Its roots and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported detoxifying properties.
