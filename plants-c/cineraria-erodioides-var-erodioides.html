Cineraria erodioides var. erodioides

Cineraria erodioides var. erodioides, commonly known as the woolly cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance.

The species is also valued for its resilience to coastal conditions and drought tolerance.
