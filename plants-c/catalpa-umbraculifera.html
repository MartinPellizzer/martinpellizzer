Catalpa umbraculifera

Catalpa umbraculifera, commonly known as the Spanish catalpa, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The tree produces long, cylindrical seed pods that split open to release its winged seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooming make it a notable species in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
