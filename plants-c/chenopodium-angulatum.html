Chenopodium angulatum

Chenopodium angulatum, commonly known as narrowleaf goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive angular appearance. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in late summer.

Chenopodium angulatum is often used in traditional medicine and can be found in various ecological studies due to its adaptability and ecological significance.
