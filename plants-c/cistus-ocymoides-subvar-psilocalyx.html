Cistus ocymoides subvar. psilocalyx

Cistus ocymoides subvar. psilocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and gray-green, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Its aromatic foliage and drought tolerance make it a hardy and ornamental plant suitable for xeriscaping.
