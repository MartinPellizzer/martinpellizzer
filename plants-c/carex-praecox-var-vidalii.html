Carex praecox var. vidalii

Carex praecox var. vidalii, commonly known as the early sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along streambanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wetland biodiversity.
