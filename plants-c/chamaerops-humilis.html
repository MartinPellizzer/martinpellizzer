Chamaerops humilis

Chamaerops humilis, commonly known as the Mediterranean fan palm, is a hardy, evergreen plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fan-shaped leaves that form a dense, umbrella-like crown. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers followed by edible, black berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and arid environments.

Its compact size and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
