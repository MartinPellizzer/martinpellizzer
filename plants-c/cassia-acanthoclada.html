Cassia acanthoclada

Cassia acanthoclada, commonly known as the golden cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Cassia acanthoclada is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
