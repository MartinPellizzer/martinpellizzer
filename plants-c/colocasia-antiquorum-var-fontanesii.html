Colocasia antiquorum var. fontanesii

Colocasia antiquorum var. fontanesii, commonly known as the giant elephant ear, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, often with a deep green to almost black coloration. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers, though these are less showy than its foliage.

The plant is a member of the Araceae family, known for its unique inflorescence structure.
