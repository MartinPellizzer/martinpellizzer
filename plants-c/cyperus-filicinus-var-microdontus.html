Cyperus filicinus var. microdontus

Cyperus filicinus var. microdontus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight sheen, and it produces small, clustered flower spikes that are usually inconspicuous.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
