Cousinia schepsaica

Cousinia schepsaica, commonly known as the Schepsaica cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It produces small, daisy-like flowers that are usually white or pale yellow.

Cousinia schepsaica is often found in steppes and mountainous areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
