Cerasus avium var. sativa

Cerasus avium var. sativa, commonly known as the sweet cherry, is a deciduous tree native to Asia and Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its pinnate leaves and showy, white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The fruit, which is the cherry, is a drupe with a fleshy outer layer surrounding a hard pit. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is widely cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
