Camellia lanosituba

Camellia lanosituba, commonly known as the woolly camellia, is a species native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It is a evergreen shrub that thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Camellia lanosituba is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
