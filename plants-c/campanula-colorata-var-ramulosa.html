Campanula colorata var. ramulosa

Campanula colorata var. ramulosa, commonly known as the pink bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, branched stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to lavender. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
