Coryphantha strobiliformis var. orcuttii

Coryphantha strobiliformis var. orcuttii, commonly known as the Orcutt's coryphantha, is a compact, spineless cactus native to the southwestern United States.

It features a globular or cylindrical stem that is typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered at the top of the stem. It has small, rounded areoles that bear white to pale yellow hairs.

This variety is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
