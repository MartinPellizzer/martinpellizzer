Croton bifurcatus var. humblotii

Croton bifurcatus var. humblotii, commonly known as the Humblot's croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with leaves that often display shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
