Cheiranthus × helveticus

Cheiranthus × helveticus, commonly known as the Swiss wallflower, is a hybrid plant species native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and mountainous environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cheiranthus × helveticus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
