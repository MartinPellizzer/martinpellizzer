Campanopsis cernua

Campanopsis cernua, commonly known as the drooping bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that hang downward, typically in shades of blue to purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Campanopsis cernua is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
