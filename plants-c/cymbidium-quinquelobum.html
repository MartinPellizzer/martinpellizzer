Cymbidium quinquelobum

Cymbidium quinquelobum, commonly known as the five-lobed orchid, is a perennial evergreen herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of long, narrow, lance-shaped leaves and has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive five-lobed lip, and bloom during the winter or early spring.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a prized orchid species.
