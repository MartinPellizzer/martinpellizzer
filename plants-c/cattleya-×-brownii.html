Cattleya × brownii

Cattleya × brownii, commonly known as the Brown's Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid that originates from the cross between Cattleya labiata and Cattleya mossiae.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from orange to red, often with a contrasting lip. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other plants without deriving nutrients from them. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Cattleya × brownii is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
