Crambe pannonica

Crambe pannonica, commonly known as the Pannonian crambe, is a perennial plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant features lobed leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its seeds are encased in a hard, smooth shell, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

Crambe pannonica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
