Cestrum reflexum var. densiflorum

Cestrum reflexum var. densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.
