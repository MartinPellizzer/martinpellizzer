Cogswellia pallida

Cogswellia pallida, commonly known as the pale cogswellia, is a rare and striking succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often tinged with a pale green or bluish hue. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and white during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited natural range, it is highly sought after by collectors and conservationists alike.
