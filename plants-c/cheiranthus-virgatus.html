Cheiranthus virgatus

Cheiranthus virgatus, commonly known as the slender wallflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces small, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically blooms in spring and early summer, often in rocky or disturbed soils. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, and the stems are often covered in fine hairs.

It is native to southern Europe and is often found in coastal areas and dry, sunny habitats.
