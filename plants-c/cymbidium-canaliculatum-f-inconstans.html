Cymbidium canaliculatum f. inconstans

Cymbidium canaliculatum f. inconstans, commonly known as the variable crested orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, arching inflorescences bearing numerous fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial orchid, preferring warm, humid environments with good air circulation. Its leaves are strap-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps in water retention.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
