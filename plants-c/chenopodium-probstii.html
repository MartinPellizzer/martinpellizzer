Chenopodium probstii

Chenopodium probstii, commonly known as Desert spinach, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions. Its leaves are edible and have been used by indigenous peoples for food and medicinal purposes.

Chenopodium probstii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils in desert ecosystems.
