Carex capillaris subsp. fuscidula

Carex capillaris subsp.

fuscidula, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants.

This subspecies is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
