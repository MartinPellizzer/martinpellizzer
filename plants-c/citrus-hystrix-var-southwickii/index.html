Citrus Hystrix Var. Southwickii

Citrus hystrix var. southwickii is a rare and unique citrus plant known for its striking appearance and limited natural habitat. It is a variant of the kaffir lime, characterized by its twisted, aromatic leaves and small, fragrant fruit. This plant is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia and is highly valued for its distinctive scent and culinary uses. Due to its rarity and specific environmental needs, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts and botanists. Its cultivation requires careful attention to soil, climate, and care to thrive.
