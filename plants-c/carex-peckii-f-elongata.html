Carex peckii f. elongata

Carex peckii f. elongata, commonly known as the elongated sedge, is a variant of the Carex peckii species, characterized by its distinctive elongated leaves.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, three-ranked flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

The leaves are usually narrow, grass-like, and often have a prominent midrib, contributing to its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
