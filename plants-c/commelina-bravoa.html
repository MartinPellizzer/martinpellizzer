Commelina bravoa

Commelina bravoa, commonly known as the "blue-eyed Mary," is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Commelinales order and the Commelinaceae family, which includes other ornamental and medicinal plants. The plant features striking blue-violet flowers with white petals, often surrounded by three lance-shaped bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Commelina bravoa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
