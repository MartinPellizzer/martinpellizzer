Centaurea margaritacea subsp. proto-margaritacea

Centaurea margaritacea subsp.

proto-margaritacea, commonly known as the narrowleaf cornflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are blue to purple, with a distinctive white center, forming small, composite-like inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
