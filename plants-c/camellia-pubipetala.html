Camellia pubipetala

Camellia pubipetala, commonly known as the hairy petal camellia, is a species native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with five petals that are often covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant is an evergreen shrub that can grow up to 3 meters in height, featuring dark green, leathery leaves. Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Camellia pubipetala is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and its potential medicinal properties.
