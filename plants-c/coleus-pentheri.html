Coleus pentheri

Coleus pentheri, commonly known as the purple heart plant, is a tropical perennial shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep purple to bright red and green. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and produces small, two-lipped flowers in shades of violet or white. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant hues and textured appearance.

Coleus pentheri thrives in partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
