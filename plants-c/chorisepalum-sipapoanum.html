Chorisepalum sipapoanum

Chorisepalum sipapoanum, commonly known as the swamp milkweed, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Chorisepalum sipapoanum plays an important role in supporting pollinators, including monarch butterflies.
