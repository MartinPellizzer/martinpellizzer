Chrozophora glabrata

Chrozophora glabrata, commonly known as the smooth bluestem, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are smooth and linear, and it produces seeds in small, capsule-like structures.

Chrozophora glabrata is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
