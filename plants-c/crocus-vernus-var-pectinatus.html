Crocus vernus var. pectinatus

Crocus vernus var. pectinatus, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, early spring blooms with bright yellow petals that often have a distinctive, waxy texture. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge after the flowers.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
