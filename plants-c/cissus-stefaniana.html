Cissus stefaniana

Cissus stefaniana, commonly known as the Stefan's devil's ivy, is a climbing vine native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing habit and distinctive, waxy leaves. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a prominent midrib and often have a slightly serrated margin. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are not showy but are followed by red, fleshy fruits.

This plant is well-suited for hanging baskets or trellises due to its vigorous growth and adaptability to various light conditions.
