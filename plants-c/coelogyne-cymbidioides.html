Coelogyne cymbidioides

Coelogyne cymbidioides, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, strap-like leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, and the species is valued for its ornamental beauty and aromatic qualities.

Coelogyne cymbidioides requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture to thrive.
