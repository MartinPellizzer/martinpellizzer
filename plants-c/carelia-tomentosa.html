Carelia tomentosa

Carelia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly carelia, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, white, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
