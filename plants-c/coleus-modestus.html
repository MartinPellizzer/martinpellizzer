Coleus modestus

Coleus modestus, commonly known as the modest coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage with leaves that come in shades of red, purple, and green. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems.

Coleus modestus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil and partial shade.
