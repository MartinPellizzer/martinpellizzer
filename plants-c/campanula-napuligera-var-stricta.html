Campanula napuligera var. stricta

Campanula napuligera var. stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically ranging in color from blue to purple. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often with a hairy texture, and are arranged alternately along the stems. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
