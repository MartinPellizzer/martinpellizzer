Crinum erubescens var. berbicense

Crinum erubescens var. berbicense, commonly known as the Red Crinum, is a striking bulbous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The bulbs are perennial, and the plant is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display and resilience in warm climates.
