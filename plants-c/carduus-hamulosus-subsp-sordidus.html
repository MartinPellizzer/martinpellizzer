Carduus hamulosus subsp. sordidus

Carduus hamulosus subsp.

sordidus, commonly known as the sordid thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a distinctive, spiky flower head that blooms in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and the flower heads are usually a mix of purple and yellow.

It is considered a hardy species that can thrive in poor soil conditions.
