Convolvulus ascendens

Convolvulus ascendens, commonly known as the ascending bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant has distinctive heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. Its flowers bloom from late winter to early summer, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Convolvulus ascendens is often found in gardens and disturbed areas, where it can spread aggressively.
