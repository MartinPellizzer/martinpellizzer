Cynanchica pyrenaica subsp. pyrenaica

Cynanchica pyrenaica subsp.

pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
