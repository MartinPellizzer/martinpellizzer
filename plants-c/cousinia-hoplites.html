Cousinia hoplites

Cousinia hoplites, commonly known as the spiked tansy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features dense, woolly foliage and produces yellow to orange disc flowers surrounded by pale yellow to brown ray florets. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often growing at high altitudes.

Cousinia hoplites is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
