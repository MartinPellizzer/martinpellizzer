Carex pingleensis

Carex pingleensis, commonly known as the Pingle sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to China and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as mountainous regions and forest floors. The plant has grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny flowers. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
