Cercanthemum squamiferum

Cercanthemum squamiferum, commonly known as the woolly thyme or woolly sand thyme, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in spring. The plant has hairy, grayish-green leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Cercanthemum squamiferum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in dry, sunny environments.
