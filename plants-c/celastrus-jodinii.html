Celastrus jodinii

Celastrus jodinii, commonly known as the Jodin's staff-vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and twining stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by woody, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a waxy coating.

Celastrus jodinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
