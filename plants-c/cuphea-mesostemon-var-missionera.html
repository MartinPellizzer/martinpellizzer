Cuphea mesostemon var. missionera

Cuphea mesostemon var. missionera, commonly known as the Missionera cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slight pubescence. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and vibrant floral display.
