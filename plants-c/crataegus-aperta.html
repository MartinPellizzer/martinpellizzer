Crataegus aperta

Crataegus aperta, commonly known as the open thornapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and showy white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals but are generally considered toxic to humans. Crataegus aperta thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and habitat for wildlife.
