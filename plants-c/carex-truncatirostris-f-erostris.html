Carex truncatirostris f. erostris

Carex truncatirostris f. erostris, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, cylindrical inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually a dark green, with a slightly rough texture, and the inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats.
