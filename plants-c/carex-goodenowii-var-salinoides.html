Carex goodenowii var. salinoides

Carex goodenowii var. salinoides, commonly known as salt marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of North America.

It thrives in brackish or saline wetlands, often found in tidal marshes and estuaries. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. It produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers in the summer.

This variety is adapted to withstand periodic flooding and saltwater exposure, making it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
