Cheirostylis marmorifolia

Cheirostylis marmorifolia, commonly known as the marbled orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, mottled leaves that display a unique marbled pattern of green and white. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a subtle purple hue. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cheirostylis marmorifolia is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
