Carex jamesii var. austromontana

Carex jamesii var. austromontana, commonly known as the Austromontana sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands and meadows. The plant features grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and have a cylindrical stem. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are arranged in a dense, cylindrical inflorescence.

This sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
