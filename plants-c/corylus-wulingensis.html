Corylus wulingensis

Corylus wulingensis, commonly known as the Wuling hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Betulaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and catkin-like inflorescences. The plant produces edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, which are a food source for various wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
