Capsicum baccatum var. umbilicatum

Capsicum baccatum var. umbilicatum, commonly known as the "Aji Amarillo," is a tropical plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange fruits, which are typically oval or pear-shaped. The plant produces small, white flowers and has a bushy growth habit with compound leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is widely used in Peruvian and other Latin American cuisines.
