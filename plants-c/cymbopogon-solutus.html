Cymbopogon solutus

Cymbopogon solutus, commonly known as lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves that are rich in essential oils. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like blades. Its rhizomes and roots are often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

Cymbopogon solutus thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
