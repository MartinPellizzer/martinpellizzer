Crataegus crus-galli var. macra

Crataegus crus-galli var. macra, commonly known as the tall hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and along roadsides.
