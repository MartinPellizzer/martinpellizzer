Crossandra primuloides

Crossandra primuloides, commonly known as the flame flower or red flame plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Crossandra primuloides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
