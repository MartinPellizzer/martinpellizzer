Carex scolopendriformis

Carex scolopendriformis, commonly known as the snake-like sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This species is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

Carex scolopendriformis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
