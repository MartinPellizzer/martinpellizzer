Chamaecrista acicularis

Chamaecrista acicularis, commonly known as the slender crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow to orange pea-like flowers, which are followed by narrow, bean-like pods. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments.

Chamaecrista acicularis is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
