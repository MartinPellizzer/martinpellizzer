Centaurea montana subsp. semidecurrens

Centaurea montana subsp.

semidecurrens, commonly known as the alpine centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, conical clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is cultivated in gardens and naturalized in various parts of the world.
