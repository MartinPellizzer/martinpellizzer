Citrus × truncata

Citrus × truncata, commonly known as the trifoliate orange, is a hybrid citrus species native to China.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange fruits that are typically not consumed by humans but are used in traditional medicine. The plant has trifoliate leaves, with three leaflets arranged in a fan-like pattern. Its flowers are fragrant and white, often attracting pollinators such as bees.

The fruit contains a high concentration of flavonoids and is used in herbal remedies for its potential health benefits.
