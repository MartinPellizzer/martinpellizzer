Citrus × pseudogulgul

Citrus × pseudogulgul, commonly known as the Java citron.

This hybrid citrus plant is characterized by its thick, rough, and often fragrant rind, which can be yellow or green depending on maturity. The fruit typically has a high concentration of essential oils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its evergreen foliage and fragrant flowers.
