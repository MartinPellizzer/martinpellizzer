Crataegus prominens

Crataegus prominens, commonly known as the common hawthorn.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa. The plant features thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers in spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with serrated edges, and it thrives in a variety of soil types.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance, providing habitat and food for wildlife.
