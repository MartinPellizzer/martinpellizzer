Carduus laciniatus subsp. novorossicus

Carduus laciniatus subsp.

novorossicus, commonly known as the Novorossiysk thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Russia and parts of eastern Europe. It is characterized by its deeply lobed, silver-green leaves and tall, spiny stems that bear yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a robust root system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
