Cousinia xiphacantha

Cousinia xiphacantha, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cold climates. It is often found in meadows and slopes at high altitudes, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of these ecosystems.

Cousinia xiphacantha is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific horticultural needs.
