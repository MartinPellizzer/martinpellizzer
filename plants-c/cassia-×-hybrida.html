Cassia × hybrida

Cassia × hybrida, commonly known as golden shower tree, is a hybrid species within the genus Cassia, belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Cassia × hybrida is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
