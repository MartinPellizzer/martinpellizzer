Carludovica elegans

Carludovica elegans, commonly known as the elegant cactus or the Mexican cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are typically green and covered with white spines. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are often followed by red or orange fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
