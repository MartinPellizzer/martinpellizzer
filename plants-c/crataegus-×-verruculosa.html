Crataegus × verruculosa

Crataegus × verruculosa, commonly known as the European hawthorn or common hawthorn, is a hybrid shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptical leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Crataegus × verruculosa is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
