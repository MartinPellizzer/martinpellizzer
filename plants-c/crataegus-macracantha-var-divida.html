Crataegus macracantha var. divida

Crataegus macracantha var. divida, commonly known as the Divaricating hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, which are often zigzagging and dividing, giving it its scientific name "divaricata.". The plant produces white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with serrated edges.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens for its seasonal interest and wildlife benefits.
