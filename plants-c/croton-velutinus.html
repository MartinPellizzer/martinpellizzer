Croton velutinus

Croton velutinus, commonly known as velvet croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
