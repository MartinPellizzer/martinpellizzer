Carex david-smithii

Carex david-smithii, commonly known as David-Smith's sedge, is a perennial plant native to the wetlands and marshy areas of eastern North America.

It belongs to the Carex genus, which includes over 2,000 species of sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces spikes of small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swampy forests or along the edges of ponds.

Carex david-smithii plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
