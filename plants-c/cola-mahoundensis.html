Cola mahoundensis

Cola mahoundensis, commonly known as the African cola tree, is a species native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces woody pods containing seeds surrounded by a stimulant-rich pulp. It is often found in moist, shaded areas of rainforests.

Due to its ecological and potential economic importance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
