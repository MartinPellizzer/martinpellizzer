Cocos nucifera var. fibrosa

Cocos nucifera var. fibrosa, commonly known as the fibrous coconut, is a variant of the coconut palm characterized by its distinctive fibrous husk.

This plant is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are large and feather-like, while the fruit develops in a thick, fibrous outer layer. The fibrous husk is highly valued for its use in making ropes, mats, and other crafts.

It also produces edible coconuts that are widely used in culinary and commercial applications.
