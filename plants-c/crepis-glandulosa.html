Crepis glandulosa

Crepis glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with glandular dots, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing yellow blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
