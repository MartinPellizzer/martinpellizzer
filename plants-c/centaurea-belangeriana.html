Centaurea belangeriana

Centaurea belangeriana, commonly known as the Belanger's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Centaurea belangeriana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
