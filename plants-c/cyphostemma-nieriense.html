Cyphostemma nieriense

Cyphostemma nieriense, commonly known as the African moon vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its unique appearance.
