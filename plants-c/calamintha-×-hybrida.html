Calamintha × hybrida

Calamintha × hybrida, commonly known as hybrid mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and culinary applications due to their mint-like aroma.

Calamintha × hybrida is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
