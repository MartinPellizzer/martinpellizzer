Chrysogonum procumbens

Chrysogonum procumbens, commonly known as the creeping goldenroot, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It features wiry, prostrate stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Chrysogonum procumbens is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.
