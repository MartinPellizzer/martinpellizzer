Centaurea subciliaris

Centaurea subciliaris, commonly known as the lesser centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. It produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with some bearing white or pale blue hairs.
