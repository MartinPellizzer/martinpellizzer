Clerodendrum huegelii

Clerodendrum huegelii, commonly known as the purple passionvine, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, often with a contrasting white throat. The plant produces small, green, egg-shaped fruits that mature to a dark purple or black. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly rough texture.

Clerodendrum huegelii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral display.
