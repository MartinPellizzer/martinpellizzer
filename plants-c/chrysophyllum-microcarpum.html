Chrysophyllum microcarpum

Chrysophyllum microcarpum, commonly known as the dwarf wax apple, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe, with a waxy coating. The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood.
