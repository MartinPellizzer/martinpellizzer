Crossandra crocea

Crossandra crocea, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Crossandra crocea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
