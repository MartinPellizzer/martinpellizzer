Centaurea czerkessica

Centaurea czerkessica, commonly known as the Caucasian centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

Centaurea czerkessica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
