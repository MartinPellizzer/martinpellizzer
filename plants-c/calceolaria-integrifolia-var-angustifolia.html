Calceolaria integrifolia var. angustifolia

Calceolaria integrifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive calyces that resemble small slippers, which enclose the petals and sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It produces vibrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and orange, making it a popular choice for gardeners and botanists alike.
