Cissus morifolia

Cissus morifolia, commonly known as the rock fig or African ice plant, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Cissus morifolia is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
