Crataegus lancei

Crataegus lancei, commonly known as the lanceleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Crataegus lancei typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
