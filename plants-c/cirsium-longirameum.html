Cirsium longirameum

Cirsium longirameum, commonly known as the long-stemmed thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring silver-green, lance-shaped leaves with prominent spines. Its flowers are purple to pink, arranged in a composite head, and bloom from late summer to autumn.

The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.
