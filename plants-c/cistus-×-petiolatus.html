Cistus × petiolatus

Cistus × petiolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rockrose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its evergreen foliage provides year-round interest, while its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cistus × petiolatus is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
