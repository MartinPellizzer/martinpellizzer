Cuphea fruticosa var. chamaedendrum

Cuphea fruticosa var. chamaedendrum, commonly known as the Mexican heather or chamaedendron, is a shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a dense, bushy growth habit and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its flowers attract hummingbirds and butterflies, adding ecological value to the garden.
