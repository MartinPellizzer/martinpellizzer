Carica papaya f. correae

Carica papaya f. correae, commonly known as the Correa's papaya, is a botanical variant of the papaya plant characterized by its unique leaf and fruit morphology.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and a distinct, elongated fruit that is typically smaller than the standard papaya. The fruit has a pale yellow skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp with a higher moisture content. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its potential for producing a more delicate flavor.

It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
