Carex emmonsii var. ellpitica

Carex emmonsii var. ellpitica, commonly known as the elliptic sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
