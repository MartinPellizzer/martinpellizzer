Clusia gardneri

Clusia gardneri, commonly known as the wax plant, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white markings. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas.

Clusia gardneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
