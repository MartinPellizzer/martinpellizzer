Chiococca brachiata var. diplomorpha

Chiococca brachiata var. diplomorpha, commonly known as the pink-flowered chiococca, is a climbing vine native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its vibrant floral display.
