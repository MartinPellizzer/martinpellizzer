Colchicum chimonanthum

Colchicum chimonanthum, commonly known as the Chinese meadow saffron, is a bulbous perennial plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Colchicaceae and is characterized by its striking, saffron-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a single flower per stem, which has six reflexed petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
