Chenopodium burkartii

Chenopodium burkartii, commonly known as desert spinach, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, ovate leaves. The plant produces tiny green flowers in clusters, and its seeds are often dispersed by wind. Chenopodium burkartii is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with mechanisms to conserve water and tolerate high temperatures.

It is sometimes used as a wild vegetable in local cuisines due to its edible leaves and nutritional value.
