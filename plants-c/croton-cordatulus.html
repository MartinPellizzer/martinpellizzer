Croton cordatulus

Croton cordatulus, commonly known as the heart-shaped croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its attractive foliage, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening in tropical and subtropical regions.
