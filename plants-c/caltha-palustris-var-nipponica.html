Caltha palustris var. nipponica

Caltha palustris var. nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese marsh marigold, is a variant of the marsh marigold native to eastern Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and streambanks. The plant features bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
