Camaridium amazonicum

Camaridium amazonicum, commonly known as the Amazonian star vine, is a climbing plant native to the dense rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically borne in clusters, followed by bright orange berries. Its roots contain alkaloids, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
