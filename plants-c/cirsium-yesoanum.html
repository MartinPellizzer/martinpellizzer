Cirsium yesoanum

Cirsium yesoanum, commonly known as the Yesso thistle, is a perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
