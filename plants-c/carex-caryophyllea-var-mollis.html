Carex caryophyllea var. mollis

Carex caryophyllea var. mollis, commonly known as the softleaf sedges, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wetland biodiversity.
