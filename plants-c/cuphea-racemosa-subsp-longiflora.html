Cuphea racemosa subsp. longiflora

Cuphea racemosa subsp.

longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered cigar plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico. It features vibrant, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in dense, racemose inflorescences.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract hummingbirds.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
