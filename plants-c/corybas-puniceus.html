Corybas puniceus

Corybas puniceus, commonly known as the crimson torch orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its striking, red-orange, hood-like flower that resembles a torch, with a single, erect spur. The plant typically grows in forested areas, often in shaded or semi-shaded conditions. Its flowers bloom from late winter to early spring, and the species is notable for its unique floral structure and vivid coloration.

Corybas puniceus is an important component of New Zealand's native flora and is often found in association with other orchid species in its habitat.
