Cestrum brevifolium

Cestrum brevifolium, commonly known as the short-leaved nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting moths and other nocturnal pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, black seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
