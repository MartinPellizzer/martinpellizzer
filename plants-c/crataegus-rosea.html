Crataegus rosea

Crataegus rosea, commonly known as the rose dogwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, pinkish-white flowers that appear in spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its bark is typically smooth with a reddish or grayish hue. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Crataegus rosea is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
