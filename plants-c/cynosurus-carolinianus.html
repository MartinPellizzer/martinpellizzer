Cynosurus carolinianus

Cynosurus carolinianus, commonly known as Carolina hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wildlife.

Cynosurus carolinianus is an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and habitat structure.
