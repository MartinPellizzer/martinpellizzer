Chirita capitis

Chirita capitis, commonly known as the "head chirea," is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces bright orange or yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.
