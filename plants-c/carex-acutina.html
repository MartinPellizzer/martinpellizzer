Carex acutina

Carex acutina, commonly known as the sharpleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and pointed, with a bluish-green coloration. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated, a common trait among sedges. Carex acutina thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
