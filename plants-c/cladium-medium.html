Cladium medium

Cladium medium, commonly known as the slender sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and swamps. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often ribbed and can reach up to several feet in length. Its flowering spikes are small and inconspicuous, producing tiny white flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Cladium medium plays an important ecological role by stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
