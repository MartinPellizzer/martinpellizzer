Carex petelotii

Carex petelotii, commonly known as the Petelot's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their distinctive three-ranked leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its flowering spikes are usually short and inconspicuous, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex petelotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
