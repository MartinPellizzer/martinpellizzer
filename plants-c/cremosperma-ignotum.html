Cremosperma ignotum

Cremosperma ignotum, commonly known as the obscure creeping milkweed, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. The plant's leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Cremosperma ignotum has a unique seed dispersal mechanism involving a sticky, viscid substance that adheres to animals and clothing.
