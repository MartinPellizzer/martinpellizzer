Camellia longicuspis

Camellia longicuspis, commonly known as the long-toothed camellia, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is a evergreen shrub or small tree characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers that range in color from white to deep red, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its flowers are borne on long, slender pedicels, giving it the species name "longicuspis," which refers to its elongated floral bracts.

Camellia longicuspis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
