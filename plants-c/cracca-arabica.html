Cracca arabica

Cracca arabica, commonly known as the Arabian coffee plant, is a species native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub-like plant that grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, green berries that contain the seeds used to make coffee. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to conserve water.

Cracca arabica is closely related to other coffee species and has historical significance in the development of coffee cultivation.
