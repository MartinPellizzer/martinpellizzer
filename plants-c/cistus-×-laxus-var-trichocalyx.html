Cistus × laxus var. trichocalyx

Cistus × laxus var. trichocalyx, commonly known as the woolly rock rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, gray-green foliage covered in soft, woolly hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rocky or coastal landscapes.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardens and as a source of essential oils.
