Crepis grammolepis

Crepis grammolepis, commonly known as the slender mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Crepis grammolepis is an annual or biennial plant that produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.
