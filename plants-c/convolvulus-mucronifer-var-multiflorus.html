Convolvulus mucronifer var. multiflorus

Convolvulus mucronifer var. multiflorus, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail make it versatile for various garden settings.
