Canavalia maxima

Canavalia maxima, commonly known as the horse gram or cluster bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often grown as a forage crop or for its seeds.

Canavalia maxima is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
