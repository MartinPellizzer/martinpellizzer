Convolvulus supinus var. tripolitanus

Convolvulus supinus var. tripolitanus, commonly known as the Tripolitan morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual with prostrate growth habit, featuring hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically pale blue to purple, with five petals and a distinct spur. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
