Caesalpinia bonduc

Caesalpinia bonduc, commonly known as the Indian tamarind or the red tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, bright red, pod-like fruits that contain hard, angular seeds. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a unique, spurred structure that attracts pollinators.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, warm climates.
