Carex fuscula var. ciliata

Carex fuscula var. ciliata, commonly known as the hairy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have fine, hair-like structures along the edges.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing small, three-lobed seeds that are dispersed by wind.
