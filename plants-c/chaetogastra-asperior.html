Chaetogastra asperior

Chaetogastra asperior, commonly known as the rough-haired grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant features dense, upright growth with rough, hairy leaves that give it its common name. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny flowers.

Chaetogastra asperior is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
