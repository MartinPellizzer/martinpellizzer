Cassia ekmaniana

Cassia ekmaniana, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces long, slender pods that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

Cassia ekmaniana is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
