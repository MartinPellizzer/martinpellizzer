Ceropegia tenuissifolia

Ceropegia tenuissifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, delicate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces distinctive, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or yellowish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Ceropegia tenuissifolia is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
