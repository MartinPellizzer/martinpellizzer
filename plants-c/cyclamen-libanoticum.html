Cyclamen libanoticum

Cyclamen libanoticum, commonly known as the Lebanon cyclamen, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Lebanon and Syria.

It features distinctive heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a deep green color and silver-veined patterns. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Cyclamen libanoticum is known for its short growing season and dormancy period, making it a unique addition to gardens in temperate climates.
