Cestrum alternifolium var. mitanthum

Cestrum alternifolium var. mitanthum, commonly known as the white trumpetflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fragrance.

The variety mitanthum is distinguished by its pale white petals and is a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
