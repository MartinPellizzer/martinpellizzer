Chamaeraphis viridis

Chamaeraphis viridis, commonly known as green sandspur, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or disturbed soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are alternate and lance-shaped, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Chamaeraphis viridis is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
