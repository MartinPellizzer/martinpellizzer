Coronilla orientalis subsp. orientalis

Coronilla orientalis subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the oriental coronilla, is a flowering plant native to parts of the Mediterranean region. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters.

The plant produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
