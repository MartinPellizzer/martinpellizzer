Calamus cockburnii

Calamus cockburnii, commonly known as the Cockburn's rattling sedge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, and it thrives in wetland or swampy environments.
