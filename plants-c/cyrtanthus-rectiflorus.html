Cyrtanthus rectiflorus

Cyrtanthus rectiflorus, commonly known as the straightflower gladiolus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, funnel-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing clusters of white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive straight, narrow corolla. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value and relatively easy care make it a favored plant among gardeners.
