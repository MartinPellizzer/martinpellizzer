Champaca

Champaca, commonly known as *Cestrum nocturnum*, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom at night, emitting a sweet, jasmine-like scent. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a reddish or purple underside.

Champaca is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
