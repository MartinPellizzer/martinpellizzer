Carex calbucana

Carex calbucana, commonly known as the yellow-eyed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features grass-like leaves that are usually dark green with a yellowish or reddish tint on the underside. Its flowering structure consists of spikes of small, greenish-brown flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Carex calbucana plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
