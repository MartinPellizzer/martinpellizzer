Cyperus truncatulus

Cyperus truncatulus, commonly known as the flatleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it produces three-sided, boat-shaped fruits.

Cyperus truncatulus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various wetland conditions.
