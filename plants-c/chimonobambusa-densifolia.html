Chimonobambusa densifolia

Chimonobambusa densifolia, commonly known as the denseleaf bamboo, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly found in China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and stout, closely spaced culms that form a thick, bushy appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and covered in a fine layer of hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.

Chimonobambusa densifolia is a clump-forming bamboo that is relatively slow-growing and suitable for small garden spaces.
