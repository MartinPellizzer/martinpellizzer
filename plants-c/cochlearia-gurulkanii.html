Cochlearia gurulkanii

Cochlearia gurulkanii, commonly known as the Gurulkan's scurvy grass, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many species with small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are white or pale yellow.

It is valued for its potential medicinal properties and its role in ecological habitats.
