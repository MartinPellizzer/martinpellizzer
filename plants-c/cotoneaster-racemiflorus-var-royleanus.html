Cotoneaster racemiflorus var. royleanus

Cotoneaster racemiflorus var. royleanus, commonly known as the Kashmir cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in dense racemes during the spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils at higher altitudes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in temperate regions.
