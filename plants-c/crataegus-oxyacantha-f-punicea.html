Crataegus oxyacantha f. punicea

Crataegus oxyacantha f. punicea, commonly known as the scarlet hawthorn, is a cultivar of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a striking visual element to gardens. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the fruit is a small, red-pink pome that ripens in late summer.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and seasonal interest.
