Calliandra angustifolia

Calliandra angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its feathery, pink or red inflorescences that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, narrow leaflets, giving it a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Additionally, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
