Clematis pseudopterantha

Clematis pseudopterantha, commonly known as the Chinese clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a distinctive reddish or bronze coloration. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, blue, or purple, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. Its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sun.

Clematis pseudopterantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
