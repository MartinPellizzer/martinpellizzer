Cyperus adustus

Cyperus adustus, commonly known as the dusty flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as marshes and wetlands. Its slender, triangular stems are often adorned with small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into three-lobed, dry fruits.

Cyperus adustus is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
