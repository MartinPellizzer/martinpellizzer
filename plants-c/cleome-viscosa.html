Cleome viscosa

Cleome viscosa, commonly known as spider flower, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branching stems and showy, yellow or pink flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and gardens.

Cleome viscosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
