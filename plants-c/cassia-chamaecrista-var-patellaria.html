Cassia chamaecrista var. patellaria

Cassia chamaecrista var. patellaria, commonly known as golden cassia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it suitable for both garden landscaping and medicinal use.
