Clerodendrum eriophyllum

Clerodendrum eriophyllum, commonly known as the flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and bees. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and lavender.

Clerodendrum eriophyllum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in warm, humid climates.
