Cynanchum brevicoronatum

Cynanchum brevicoronatum, commonly known as the short-crowned milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which includes milkweeds, and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, and its fruits are follicles containing toxic seeds.

It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with limited water availability, showcasing deep root systems and drought tolerance.
