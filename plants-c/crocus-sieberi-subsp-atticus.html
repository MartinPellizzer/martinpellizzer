Crocus sieberi subsp. atticus

Crocus sieberi subsp.

atticus, commonly known as the Attic crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different flower structure compared to other crocus species.
