Caesalpinia microphylla

Caesalpinia microphylla, commonly known as the velvet tamarind or dwarf tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are small and often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. Caesalpinia microphylla thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical landscapes.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal contribute to its use in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
