Conyza linearis

Conyza linearis, commonly known as the slender horseweed, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in open areas, such as fields and roadsides, and has linear, hairy leaves that are often mistaken for grasses. It produces small yellow flowers that develop into fluffy seeds, which are dispersed by the wind.

Conyza linearis is often considered a weed but has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
