Cnicus acaulis

Cnicus acaulis, commonly known as the creeping thistle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and is often found in dry, rocky soils. The plant features dark green, woolly leaves that are typically lobed or toothed. Its flowers are yellow and appear in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.

Cnicus acaulis is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
