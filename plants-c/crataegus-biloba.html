Crataegus biloba

Crataegus biloba, commonly known as the small-fruited hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that ripen in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it is part of the Rosaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance in native habitats.
