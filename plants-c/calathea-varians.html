Calathea varians

Calathea varians, commonly known as the variegated calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, mottled leaves that display a combination of green, white, and silver patterns. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a velvety texture that adds to its ornamental appeal.

Calathea varians requires consistent moisture and high humidity to maintain its vibrant appearance.
