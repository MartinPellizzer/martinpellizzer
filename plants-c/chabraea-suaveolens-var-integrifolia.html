Chabraea suaveolens var. integrifolia

Chabraea suaveolens var. integrifolia, commonly known as the sweet-scented chabraea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually broad and entire, contributing to its varietal name "integrifolia," meaning "with entire edges.".

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
