Cucumis sativus var. battich-djebbal

Cucumis sativus var. battich-djebbal, commonly known as the Battich-Djebbal cucumber, is a variety of cucumber characterized by its unique botanical features.

It typically has elongated, ribbed fruits with a pale green skin and a crisp, juicy flesh. The plant is an annual herbaceous vine that produces monoecious flowers, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

This variety is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, making it suitable for cultivation in regions with limited water resources.
