Copiapoa bridgesii

Copiapoa bridgesii, commonly known as the Pachyveria, is a species of cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, powdery bloom, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
