Citrus × limetta var. bergamia

Citrus × limetta var. bergamia, commonly known as bergamot, is a hybrid citrus plant originating from the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped, thick-skinned fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature. The fruit's interior contains a segmented white pulp, which is rich in essential oils and is used in the production of perfumes and aromatics. Bergamot plants thrive in warm, temperate climates and require well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant in both horticulture and the fragrance industry.
