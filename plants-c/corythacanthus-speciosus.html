Corythacanthus speciosus

Corythacanthus speciosus, commonly known as the ornate corythacanthus, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, spirally arranged leaves that form a dense, cylindrical structure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often hidden within the foliage. Its growth habit is slow, and it thrives in shaded, moist environments with rich, well-draining soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
