Croton oxypetalus

Croton oxypetalus, commonly known as the flame tree or fire tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its bark is thick and fissured. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Croton oxypetalus is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
