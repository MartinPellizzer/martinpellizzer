Cussonia dioica

Cussonia dioica, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a tropical shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Cyclanthaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are either male or female, reflecting its dioecious nature. Its roots are edible and have been traditionally used as a starch source.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
