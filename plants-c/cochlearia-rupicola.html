Cochlearia rupicola

Cochlearia rupicola, commonly known as the rock cochlearia, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. This species is characterized by its small, white or purple flowers and its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows in crevices and on slopes, adapting to harsh, high-altitude environments.

Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.
