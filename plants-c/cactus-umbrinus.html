Cactus umbrinus

Cactus umbrinus, commonly known as the shade cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hairs. These hairs help the plant retain moisture and reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the cooler months, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted appearance.

Despite its name, it is not particularly shade-tolerant and thrives best in full sun with well-draining soil.
