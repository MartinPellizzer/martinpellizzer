Chrysopteris grandis

Chrysopteris grandis, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow inflorescences, which resemble clouds or mists, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Chrysopteris grandis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
