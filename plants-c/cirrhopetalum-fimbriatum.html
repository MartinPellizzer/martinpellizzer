Cirrhopetalum fimbriatum

Cirrhopetalum fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed petal orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fringed petals that give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and has a slender, climbing stem. Its flowers are usually pale green to white in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
