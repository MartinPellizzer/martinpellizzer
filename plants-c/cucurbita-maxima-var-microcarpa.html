Cucurbita maxima var. microcarpa

Cucurbita maxima var. microcarpa, commonly known as the miniature pumpkin, is a cultivated variety of the species Cucurbita maxima.

It is characterized by its small, round, and often colorful fruits, which range in size from 2 to 5 inches in diameter. The plant is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and squash. It typically grows as a trailing vine with large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in fall decorations and as a decorative element in gardens.
