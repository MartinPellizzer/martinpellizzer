Christensonella pachyphylla

Christensonella pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other sunflower-like plants.

Its unique adaptations make it well-suited for survival in harsh, desert-like conditions.
