Calamagrostis calamagrostis

Calamagrostis calamagrostis, commonly known as the tall reed or tall gray grass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces feathery panicles of small, whitish flowers in the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, wet meadows, and along riverbanks.

Calamagrostis calamagrostis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
