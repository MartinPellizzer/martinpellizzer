Centaurea minima

Centaurea minima, commonly known as the lesser centaury, is a small annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 10-30 centimeters and features narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom from June to August. It is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in dry grasslands.

Centaurea minima is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
