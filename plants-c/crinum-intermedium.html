Crinum intermedium

Crinum intermedium, commonly known as the Brazilian lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in umbels at the top of sturdy, upright stems. The plant has strap-like, lanceolate leaves that are broad at the base and taper towards the tips. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Crinum intermedium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
