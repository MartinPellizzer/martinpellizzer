Canarium virgatum

Canarium virgatum, commonly known as the candlenut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are alternate and glossy. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. The seeds are used for oil extraction and have traditional medicinal uses.

It plays an important ecological role in coastal and lowland forests.
