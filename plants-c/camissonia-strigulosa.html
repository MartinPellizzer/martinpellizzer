Camissonia strigulosa

Camissonia strigulosa, commonly known as the hairy camissonia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many familiar plants like cabbages and mustards. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Camissonia strigulosa thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
