Carex turkestanica

Carex turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks. It produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Carex turkestanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
