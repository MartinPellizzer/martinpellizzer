Campanula tridentata var. petrophila

Campanula tridentata var. petrophila, commonly known as the rock bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while the stems are often covered in fine pubescence. This variety thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging growing conditions.
