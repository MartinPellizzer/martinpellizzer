Cyanus strictus

Cyanus strictus, commonly known as the blueweed or blue thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, spiny bracts that protect its seed heads, which are composed of numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Cyanus strictus is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
