Clethra alnifolia var. rosea

Clethra alnifolia var. rosea, commonly known as the rose-leaf sweet pepper, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a dark green color. This variety is particularly noted for its showy, rose-like flower clusters that attract pollinators.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.
