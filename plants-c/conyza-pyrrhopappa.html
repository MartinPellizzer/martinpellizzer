Conyza pyrrhopappa

Conyza pyrrhopappa, commonly known as the redseed weed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pinkish to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its seeds are small and covered in a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal.

Conyza pyrrhopappa is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain ecosystems.
