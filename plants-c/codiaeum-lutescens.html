Codiaeum lutescens

Codiaeum lutescens, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display shades of yellow, orange, and red, making it a popular ornamental plant. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, adding to its visual appeal. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light to maintain its vibrant coloration.

Due to its aesthetic value, it is often used in landscaping and as a houseplant.
