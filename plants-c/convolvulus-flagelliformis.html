Convolvulus flagelliformis

Convolvulus flagelliformis, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in the morning, giving it its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it is native to regions of Africa and the Middle East.

Convolvulus flagelliformis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in semi-arid environments.
