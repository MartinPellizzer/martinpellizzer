Clitoria javitensis f. pilosa

Clitoria javitensis f. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy butterfly pea, is a variant of the butterfly pea plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple or violet flowers that resemble butterflies, and its leaves are covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant is a climbing vine with trifoliate leaves and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

The flowers are used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye due to their vibrant color and medicinal properties.
