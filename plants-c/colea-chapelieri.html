Colea chapelieri

Colea chapelieri, commonly known as the chapelieri's sedge, is a perennial plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, forming dense clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowering stems produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered in spikelets.
