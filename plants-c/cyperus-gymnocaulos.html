Cyperus gymnocaulos

Cyperus gymnocaulos, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy areas in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense colonies that can stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it plays a role in providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
