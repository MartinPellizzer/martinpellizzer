Clematis cirrhosa var. semitriloba

Clematis cirrhosa var. semitriloba, commonly known as the trailing clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, climbing stems with fine, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a silvery-gray pubescence. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer, which are typically borne in clusters. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability in garden settings.
