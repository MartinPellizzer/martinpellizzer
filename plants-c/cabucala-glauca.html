Cabucala glauca

Cabucala glauca, commonly known as the blue glaucous plant, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas. Its foliage is covered in a waxy cuticle that gives it a distinctive bluish sheen.

Cabucala glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
