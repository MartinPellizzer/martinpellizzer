Cereus pentlandii var. puniceus

Cereus pentlandii var. puniceus, commonly known as the red cereus, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems that are covered in sharp, spiny areoles. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color, blooming at night. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
