Centaurea calophylla

Centaurea calophylla, commonly known as the pink centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. The plant features bright pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its stems are erect and branched.

Centaurea calophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
