Cipura linearis

Cipura linearis, commonly known as the linear cipura, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
