Cordia laxiflora

Cordia laxiflora, commonly known as the white cordia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces showy, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

Cordia laxiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
