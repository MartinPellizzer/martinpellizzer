Carpha solitaria

Carpha solitaria, commonly known as the solitary reed, is a perennial grass native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wetlands. The plant has slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

The small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family.
