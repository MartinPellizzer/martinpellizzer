Coryphantha clavata var. radicantissima

Coryphantha clavata var. radicantissima, commonly known as the root-stem cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a short, thick stem covered in spiny areoles, with clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of yellow or orange. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in sandy, well-draining soils. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its stem for survival during dry periods.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and hardy nature in desert environments.
