Calamagrostis koelerioides

Calamagrostis koelerioides, commonly known as Koeler's reed, is a perennial grass native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, stream sides, and wet meadows. The plant features tall, slender culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or blue-green. Its inflorescence consists of dense, panicle-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
