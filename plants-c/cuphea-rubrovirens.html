Cuphea rubrovirens

Cuphea rubrovirens, commonly known as the red-veined cuphea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and green foliage, with striking tubular flowers that range from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 feet, and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds, making it a valuable addition to pollinator gardens.

Cuphea rubrovirens is relatively low maintenance, preferring well-drained soil and regular watering during the growing season.
