Chrysodium cuspidatum

Chrysodium cuspidatum, commonly known as the spiked chrysodium, is a herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
