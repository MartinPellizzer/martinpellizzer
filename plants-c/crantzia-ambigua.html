Crantzia ambigua

Crantzia ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous crantzia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to parts of South Africa and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant.

Crantzia ambigua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
