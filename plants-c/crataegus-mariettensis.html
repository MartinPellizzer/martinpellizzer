Crataegus mariettensis

Crataegus mariettensis, commonly known as Marietta hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals but are generally not consumed by humans.

Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.
