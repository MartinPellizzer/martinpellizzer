Cardamine forsteri

Cardamine forsteri, commonly known as the Forster's buttercup, is a small herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers with five petals. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has a short stem and produces small, inconspicuous fruits that contain several seeds.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and botanical gardens.
