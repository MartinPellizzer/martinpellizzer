Calceolaria thymifolia

Calceolaria thymifolia, commonly known as the thyme-leaved slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that resemble small cups. The plant has hairy, aromatic leaves that resemble those of thyme, giving it its common name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Calceolaria thymifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for its delicate, colorful blooms.
