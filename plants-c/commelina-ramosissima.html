Commelina ramosissima

Commelina ramosissima, commonly known as the slender commelina, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Commelinaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and arrow-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, blue-violet flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and thrives in moist, shady environments.

Commelina ramosissima is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
