Cyperus paposanus

Cyperus paposanus, commonly known as the swamp rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowering spikes are usually borne on tall, slender stems and produce small, inconspicuous flowers.

Cyperus paposanus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
