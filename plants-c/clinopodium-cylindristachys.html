Clinopodium cylindristachys

Clinopodium cylindristachys, commonly known as narrowleaf mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in open woods, meadows, and rocky slopes, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its small, tubular flowers are usually white to pale blue and bloom in late summer.

The leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and emit a strong minty fragrance when crushed.
