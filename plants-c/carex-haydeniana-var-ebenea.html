Carex haydeniana var. ebenea

Carex haydeniana var. ebenea, commonly known as the Hayden's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. This variety is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

The plant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
