Chrysothemis adenosiphon

Chrysothemis adenosiphon, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers that resemble a crown, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and often branched.

Chrysothemis adenosiphon is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
