Cirsium albicans

Cirsium albicans, commonly known as white thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, silvery-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with rough, hairy surfaces and a distinctive basal rosette of foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Cirsium albicans is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
