Callianthemum cuneilobum

Callianthemum cuneilobum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent odor. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are basal, pinnately divided, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Callianthemum cuneilobum is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions, making it a hardy species in its native range.
