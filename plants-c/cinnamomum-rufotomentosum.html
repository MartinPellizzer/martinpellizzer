Cinnamomum rufotomentosum

Cinnamomum rufotomentosum, commonly known as the red-tomentose cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by oval, dark blue berries. Its essential oils, derived from the bark and leaves, are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
