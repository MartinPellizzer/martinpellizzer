Corion halophilum var. urbicum

Corion halophilum var. urbicum, commonly known as the urban stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to urban environments.

It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, salty conditions. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid climates. Its small, star-shaped flowers are typically yellow or greenish-white.

This variety is often found growing on building walls, sidewalks, and other urban substrates.
