Coelogyne cuspilabia

Coelogyne cuspilabia, commonly known as the narrow-lipped coelogyne, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant features a pseudobulb structure with elongated, strap-like leaves. Its flowers are borne on a single, erect stem and are often clustered in a raceme.

Coelogyne cuspilabia thrives in tropical climates and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
