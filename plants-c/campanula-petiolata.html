Campanula petiolata

Campanula petiolata, commonly known as the petioled bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue, violet, or white. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
