Carex cordillerana

Carex cordillerana, commonly known as the Sierra sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. It is often found in meadows, streambanks, and open forests at higher elevations.

Carex cordillerana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
