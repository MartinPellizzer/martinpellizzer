Chenopodium album subsp. borbasii

Chenopodium album subsp.

borbasii, commonly known as red-rooted goosefoot, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its succulent, reddish stems and alternate, lobed leaves that are typically hairy.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is considered a common weed in agricultural settings.
