Cerasus serrulata var. taishanensis

Cerasus serrulata var. taishanensis, commonly known as the Taishan cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.

The leaves are elliptical with serrated edges, giving it its scientific name.
