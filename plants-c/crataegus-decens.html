Crataegus decens

Crataegus decens, commonly known as the showy crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its ornamental flowers, which range from white to pink and appear in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically red or yellow, maturing in the fall. Crataegus decens thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
