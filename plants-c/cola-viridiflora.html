Cola viridiflora

Cola viridiflora, commonly known as green-flowered cola, is a species of flowering plant native to western Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain stimulant compounds similar to those found in other cola species.

Due to its unique botanical features, it is of interest in both horticultural and pharmacological studies.
