Calamintha alpina f. cuneata

Calamintha alpina f. cuneata, commonly known as alpine thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has aromatic, narrow leaves that are often used in herbal teas and culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
