Claytonia perfoliata subsp. mexicana

Claytonia perfoliata subsp.

mexicana, commonly known as Mexican spring beauty, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Mexico. It features rosettes of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped white or pink flowers in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or shaded habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Portulacaceae family.
