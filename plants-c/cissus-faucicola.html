Cissus faucicola

Cissus faucicola, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky surfaces.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
