Campanula rotundifolia var. macrorhiza

Campanula rotundifolia var. macrorhiza, commonly known as the large-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its round, broad leaves and large, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and spreads through rhizomes, forming dense colonies.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitats.
