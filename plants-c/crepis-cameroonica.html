Crepis cameroonica

Crepis cameroonica, commonly known as the Cameroon thistle, is a flowering plant native to Cameroon and neighboring regions of Central Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough texture and hairy surfaces, while the stem is erect and often branched.

The flowers are yellowish to brownish in color, and the plant produces small, dry fruits known as achenes.
