Calanthe brachychila

Calanthe brachychila, commonly known as the narrow-leaved calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright pseudobulbs and linear, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Calanthe brachychila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
