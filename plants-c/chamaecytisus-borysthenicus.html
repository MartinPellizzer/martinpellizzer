Chamaecytisus borysthenicus

Chamaecytisus borysthenicus, commonly known as the Crimean broom, is a shrub native to the Black Sea region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils. It is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.

Botanically, it belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
