Carduus platypus subsp. maurus

Carduus platypus subsp.

maurus, commonly known as the Mauvus thistle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a distinctive purple to pink flower head that resembles a dandelion.

The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in open fields. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
