Cotyledon subspicata

Cotyledon subspicata, commonly known as the silver leaf cotyledon, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically covered in a waxy, silver-gray coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a subtle ornamental touch. It is a low-growing species, often forming dense clusters that make it ideal for rock gardens or container planting.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
