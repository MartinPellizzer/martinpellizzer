Ceropegia floriparva

Ceropegia floriparva, commonly known as the miniature string of hearts, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, succulent stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or white, with a distinctive hood-like structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in hanging baskets.
