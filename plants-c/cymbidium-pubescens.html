Cymbidium pubescens

Cymbidium pubescens, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing numerous showy, fragrant flowers. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

Cymbidium pubescens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various growing conditions.
