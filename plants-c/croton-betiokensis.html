Croton betiokensis

Croton betiokensis, commonly known as the Bight croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often strikingly colored plants. The plant is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically green with prominent veins and sometimes adorned with red or yellow markings. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some areas.
