Carex lerchenfeldiana

Carex lerchenfeldiana, commonly known as the Lerchenfeld's sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers. Native to southern Africa, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions.

Its ornamental value comes from its dense, compact growth habit and the subtle, pale green color of its foliage.
