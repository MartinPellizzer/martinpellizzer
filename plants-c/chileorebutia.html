Chileorebutia

Chileorebutia, commonly known as the desert rose, is a genus of cacti native to South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in areoles, from which spines, flowers, and branches emerge. The flowers are typically showy, with vibrant colors such as pink, purple, and white, blooming during the warmer months. Chileorebutia species are drought-tolerant and thrive in well-draining soil, making them popular among succulent enthusiasts.

They are often cultivated for their ornamental value and ability to withstand arid conditions.
