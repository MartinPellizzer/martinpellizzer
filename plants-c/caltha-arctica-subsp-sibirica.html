Caltha arctica subsp. sibirica

Caltha arctica subsp.

sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Arctic and alpine regions. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often covered with a fine layer of white hairs.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in tundra ecosystems and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
