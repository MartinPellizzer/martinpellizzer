Caryophyllus cotinifolius

Caryophyllus cotinifolius, commonly known as the velvetleaf or redroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and clusters of small, pink to red flowers. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in moist, disturbed soils. It is often found in wetlands, along stream banks, and in swampy areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
