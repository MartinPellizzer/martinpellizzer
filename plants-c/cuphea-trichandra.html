Cuphea trichandra

Cuphea trichandra, commonly known as the velvetleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Cuphea trichandra is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
