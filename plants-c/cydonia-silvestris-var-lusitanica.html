Cydonia silvestris var. lusitanica

Cydonia silvestris var. lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese quince, is a lesser-known variant of the quince species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the development of its distinctive, aromatic fruit. The leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a slightly rough texture.

This variety is valued for its culinary uses and is often used in traditional recipes across its native regions.
