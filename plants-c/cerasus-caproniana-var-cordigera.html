Cerasus caproniana var. cordigera

Cerasus caproniana var. cordigera, commonly known as the Chinese wild cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible cherries that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
