Cynoctonum muricatum

Cynoctonum muricatum, commonly known as the pineapple guava, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to South America.

It produces a small, yellowish fruit with a rough, spiky skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp. The plant has a shrub-like structure with thick, leathery leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and is valued for its unique flavor and nutritional content.
