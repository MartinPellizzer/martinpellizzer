Carduus cymosus

Carduus cymosus, commonly known as the clustered thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs and have a rough, serrated texture.

The clustered thistle is known for its ability to thrive in various environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitats.
