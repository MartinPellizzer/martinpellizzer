Clusia ovigera

Clusia ovigera, commonly known as the wax plant or golden wax plant, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are usually dark green with a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Clusia ovigera is also valued for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and partial shade.
