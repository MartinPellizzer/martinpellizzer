Chailletia flexuosa

Chailletia flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible chailletia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems that often trail or cascade, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silvery hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Chailletia flexuosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to rocky or well-drained soils.
