Callicarpa pentandra f. dentata

Callicarpa pentandra f. dentata, commonly known as the toothed beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of vibrant purple berries that appear in the fall, which are a key feature of its botanical profile. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking fruit and foliage.
