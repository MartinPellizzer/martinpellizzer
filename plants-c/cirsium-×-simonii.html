Cirsium × simonii

Cirsium × simonii, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cirsium × simonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
