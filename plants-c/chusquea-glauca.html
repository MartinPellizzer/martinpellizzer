Chusquea glauca

Chusquea glauca, commonly known as the blue bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect culms that often display a bluish-green hue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as cloud forests and mountainous areas. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Chusquea glauca is valued for its aesthetic qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
