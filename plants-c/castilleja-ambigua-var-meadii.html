Castilleja ambigua var. meadii

Castilleja ambigua var. meadii, commonly known as the Meade's Indian paintbrush, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its bright red to orange tubular flowers. The plant lacks true leaves and relies on its host plants for water and nutrients. It typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.
