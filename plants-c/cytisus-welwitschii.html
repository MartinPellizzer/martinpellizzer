Cytisus welwitschii

Cytisus welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch's broom, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Cytisus welwitschii is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive floral display.
