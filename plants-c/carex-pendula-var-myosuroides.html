Carex pendula var. myosuroides

Carex pendula var. myosuroides, commonly known as the hanging sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its drooping, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its flowering spikes produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers, and it is often used in water gardens or as a ground cover in damp areas.

The variety myosuroides is distinguished by its slightly different leaf structure and flowering habits compared to the typical Carex pendula.
