Cactus linkeanus

Cactus linkeanus, commonly known as the desert hedge cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, spiny areoles, which provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green, succulent body. Its roots are shallow and widespread, allowing it to efficiently absorb moisture from the soil.

Cactus linkeanus is well adapted to extreme desert conditions, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
