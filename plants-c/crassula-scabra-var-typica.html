Crassula scabra var. typica

Crassula scabra var. typica, commonly known as the rough crassula, is a compact, succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, fleshy, triangular leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique foliage and resilience make it a striking addition to both indoor and outdoor collections.
