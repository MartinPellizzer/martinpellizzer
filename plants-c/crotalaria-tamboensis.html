Crotalaria tamboensis

Crotalaria tamboensis, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately divided, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Crotalaria tamboensis is often cultivated for its beauty but can be invasive in certain regions.
