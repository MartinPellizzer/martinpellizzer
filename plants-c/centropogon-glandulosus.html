Centropogon glandulosus

Centropogon glandulosus, commonly known as the hairy centropogon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
