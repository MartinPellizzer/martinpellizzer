Chamaecostus lanceolatus subsp. lanceolatus

Chamaecostus lanceolatus subsp.

lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceleaf chamaecostus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
