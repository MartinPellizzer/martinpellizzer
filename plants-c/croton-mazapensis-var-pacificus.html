Croton mazapensis var. pacificus

Croton mazapensis var. pacificus, commonly known as the Pacific croton, is a species native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Hawaii.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves, which often display shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This plant plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various native insects and birds.
