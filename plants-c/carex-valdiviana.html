Carex valdiviana

Carex valdiviana, commonly known as the Valdivian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly in the temperate rainforests of Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or wetlands, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late spring to early summer, produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are often wind-pollinated.

Carex valdiviana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
