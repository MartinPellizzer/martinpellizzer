Catasetum justinianum

Catasetum justinianum, commonly known as the Justinian's Catasetum, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that can range from yellow to red, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of flowers, and it produces a unique, elongated spur that houses the nectar.

Catasetum justinianum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
