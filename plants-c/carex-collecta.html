Carex collecta

Carex collecta, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower spikes that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. Carex collecta prefers moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream edges.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
