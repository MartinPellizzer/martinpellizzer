Crataegus nigra f. borosii

Crataegus nigra f. borosii, commonly known as the blackthorn rose or blackthorn.

This plant is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark berries. It is native to parts of Europe and western Asia, often found in woodland edges and hedgerows. The leaves are typically elliptical with serrated edges, and the flowers are white or pale pink, appearing in early spring.

The fruit, known as a drupe, ripens in late summer and is an important food source for birds and other wildlife.
