Cardamine resedifolia var. rotundifolia

Cardamine resedifolia var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the roundedleaf watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to moist, shaded environments in North America.

It features trifoliate leaves with rounded lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to thrive in wet soils along stream banks and in woodland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
