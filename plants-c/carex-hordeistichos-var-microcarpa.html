Carex hordeistichos var. microcarpa

Carex hordeistichos var. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf beak sedge, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which are usually greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This species is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
