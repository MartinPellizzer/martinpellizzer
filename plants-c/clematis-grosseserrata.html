Clematis grosseserrata

Clematis grosseserrata, commonly known as the broad-toothed clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated margins, typically with 5 to 9 leaflets. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs, and it thrives in partial shade to full sun.

Clematis grosseserrata is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
