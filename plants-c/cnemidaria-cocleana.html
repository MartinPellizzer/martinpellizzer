Cnemidaria cocleana

Cnemidaria cocleana, commonly known as the white-veined fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with distinct white veins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The fronds are also valued for their ornamental appeal in horticulture.
