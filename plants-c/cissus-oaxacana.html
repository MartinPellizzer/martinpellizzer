Cissus oaxacana

Cissus oaxacana, commonly known as the Oaxaca devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and distinctive, heart-shaped leaves. The leaves are typically green with a waxy texture and often have a slightly serrated edge. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits.
