Capparis eucalyptifolia

Capparis eucalyptifolia, commonly known as the eucalyptus caper, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features woody stems with thorny branches and produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has pinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathland and woodland habitats.

Capparis eucalyptifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract native pollinators.
