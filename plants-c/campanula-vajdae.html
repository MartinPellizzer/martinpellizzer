Campanula vajdae

Campanula vajdae, commonly known as the Vajda bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It is a perennial herb with upright stems and lance-shaped leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
