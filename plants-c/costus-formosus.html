Costus formosus

Costus formosus, commonly known as the Taiwan costus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric, and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with pale yellow to orange flowers. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden settings.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated for both horticultural and traditional uses.
