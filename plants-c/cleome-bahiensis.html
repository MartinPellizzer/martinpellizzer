Cleome bahiensis

Cleome bahiensis, commonly known as the Mexican spider flower, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cleomaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spidery flower clusters. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves and showy, yellow-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Cleome bahiensis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
