Cereus blanckii

Cereus blanckii, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its vibrant, showy flowers. The plant produces large, white or pale pink flowers that bloom at night, a trait typical of many nocturnal cacti. Its roots are shallow and adapted to absorb moisture from the soil quickly.

Cereus blanckii is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
