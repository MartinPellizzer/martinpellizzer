Cupressus lusitanica subsp. torulosa

Cupressus lusitanica subsp.

torulosa, commonly known as the Portuguese cypress, is a subspecies native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, upright form and scale-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments and is well adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticultural landscapes.
