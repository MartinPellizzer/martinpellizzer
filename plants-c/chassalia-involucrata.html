Chassalia involucrata

Chassalia involucrata, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and has a compact, bushy habit. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, papery bracts around its flower heads.

Chassalia involucrata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
