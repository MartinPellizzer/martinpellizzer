Cousinia koelzii

Cousinia koelzii, commonly known as the Koelz's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features basal leaves that are typically hairy and linear, with showy yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to withstand cold and drought conditions.

Cousinia koelzii is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience.
