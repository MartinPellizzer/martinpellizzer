Cucumeropsis

Cucumeropsis, commonly known as the "cucumber plant," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Cucurbitaceae.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plants produce small, yellow flowers that give way to green, elongated fruits resembling cucumbers. Cucumeropsis species are often cultivated for their edible fruits, which are used in various culinary applications.

These plants thrive in warm climates and require well-drained soil for optimal growth.
