Cirsium hookerianum

Cirsium hookerianum, commonly known as Hooker's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, purple-blue composite flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its robust, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, prickly bristles. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural areas.
