Colobanthus apetalus var. apetalus

Colobanthus apetalus var. apetalus, commonly known as the Antarctic hair grass, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the Antarctic region.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and inconspicuous flowers. This species is adapted to extreme cold and has a unique ability to survive in nutrient-poor soils. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems in its harsh environment.

The plant is also notable for its rapid growth and ability to reproduce vegetatively.
