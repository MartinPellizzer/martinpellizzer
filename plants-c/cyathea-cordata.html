Cyathea cordata

Cyathea cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, pinnate fronds that can reach up to several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are often used in traditional medicine and horticulture due to their ornamental value.

Cyathea cordata plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various organisms.
