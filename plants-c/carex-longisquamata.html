Carex longisquamata

Carex longisquamata, commonly known as the long-scaled sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks.

Carex longisquamata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
