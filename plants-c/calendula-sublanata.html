Calendula sublanata

Calendula sublanata, commonly known as the rough calendula, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom from late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Calendula sublanata is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
