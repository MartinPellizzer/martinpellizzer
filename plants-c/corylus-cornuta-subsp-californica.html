Corylus cornuta subsp. californica

Corylus cornuta subsp.

californica, commonly known as the California hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States. It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged alternately along the stems.

The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by edible nuts that mature in the fall. Its botanical characteristics include rough, hairy stems and yellowish-brown bark that becomes fissured with age.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
