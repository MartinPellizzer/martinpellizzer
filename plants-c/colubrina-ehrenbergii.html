Colubrina ehrenbergii

Colubrina ehrenbergii, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright purple berries in the fall. These berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
