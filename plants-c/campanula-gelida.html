Campanula gelida

Campanula gelida, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Campanula gelida has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ability to grow in challenging climates.
