Cambessedesia hilariana var. minor

Cambessedesia hilariana var. minor, commonly known as the lesser goldenbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and yellowish-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in xeriscapes and rock gardens.
