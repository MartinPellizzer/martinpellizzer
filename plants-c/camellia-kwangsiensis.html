Camellia kwangsiensis

Camellia kwangsiensis, commonly known as the Guangxi camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to southern China.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant is part of the Theaceae family and is closely related to camellias used in tea production. It thrives in humid, temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Camellia kwangsiensis is also valued for its potential in horticulture and botanical research due to its unique floral characteristics.
