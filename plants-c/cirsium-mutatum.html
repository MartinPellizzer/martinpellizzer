Cirsium mutatum

Cirsium mutatum, commonly known as the slender thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped with prominent serrations, and the flower heads are surrounded by bracts that give it a distinctive appearance.

Cirsium mutatum is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
