Cinnamomum riparium

Cinnamomum riparium, commonly known as water cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments near water bodies, making it a common wetland species.

Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves and bark, are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.
