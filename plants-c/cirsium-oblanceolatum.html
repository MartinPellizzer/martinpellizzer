Cirsium oblanceolatum

Cirsium oblanceolatum, commonly known as the broadleaf thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features spiny leaves with a bluish-gray underside and produces purple to pinkish flowers in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
