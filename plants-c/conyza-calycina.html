Conyza calycina

Conyza calycina, commonly known as the common blueweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Americas.

It features slender, branched stems and small, blue to violet flower heads that are typically surrounded by white to pale blue bracts. The plant produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds, which contribute to its widespread distribution. Its leaves are usually hairy and alternate, with a slightly serrated margin.

Conyza calycina is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including fields and roadsides.
