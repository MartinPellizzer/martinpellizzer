Calamus spathulatus

Calamus spathulatus, commonly known as the broad-leaved calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and broad, paddle-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Calamus spathulatus is often found in wetlands and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
