Croton terminalis

Croton terminalis, commonly known as the red croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that range from bright red to orange and yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
