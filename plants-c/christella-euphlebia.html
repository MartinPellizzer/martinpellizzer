Christella euphlebia

Christella euphlebia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or violet in color, blooming in late summer. Its flowers are tubular and have five lobes, with prominent stamens and a distinctive style.

Christella euphlebia is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in well-drained environments.
