Centaurea hoffmannseggii

Centaurea hoffmannseggii, commonly known as Hoffmann's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.

Centaurea hoffmannseggii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
