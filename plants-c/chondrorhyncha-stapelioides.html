Chondrorhyncha stapelioides

Chondrorhyncha stapelioides, commonly known as the African violet, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, often with a strong fragrance. It is a popular houseplant due to its ease of care and ornamental value.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its unique leaf structure and flowering habits.
