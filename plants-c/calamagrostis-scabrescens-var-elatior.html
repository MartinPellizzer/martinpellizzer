Calamagrostis scabrescens var. elatior

Calamagrostis scabrescens var. elatior, commonly known as the tall bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blue-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other riparian habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
