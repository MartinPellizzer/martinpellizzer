Capsella bursa-pastoris f. simplex

Capsella bursa-pastoris f. simplex, commonly known as the common chickweed, is a low-growing annual herb with a distinctive appearance.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early autumn. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. This plant is characterized by its simple, unbranched stems and is often found in disturbed soils, such as gardens, fields, and along roadsides.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, known for its cruciform flowers and edible leaves.
