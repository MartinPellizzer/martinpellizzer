Cyperus peruvianus

Cyperus peruvianus, commonly known as the Peruvian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to waterlogged soils. Its slender, triangular stems are often clustered, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Cyperus peruvianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
