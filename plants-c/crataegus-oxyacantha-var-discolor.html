Crataegus oxyacantha var. discolor

Crataegus oxyacantha var. discolor, commonly known as the white thornapple, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and small white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows or woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
