Cassia collae

Cassia collae, commonly known as golden cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. Cassia collae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and the plant prefers well-drained soil and full sunlight.
