Cuphea calophylla f. plumbaginea

Cuphea calophylla f. plumbaginea, commonly known as the blue cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is a shrub characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in dense clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.

The varietal form 'plumbaginea' is distinguished by its more intense blue flower color compared to the typical form.
