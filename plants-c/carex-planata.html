Carex planata

Carex planata, commonly known as flatleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it produces starchy, edible rhizomes.

Carex planata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
