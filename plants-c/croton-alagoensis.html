Croton alagoensis

Croton alagoensis, commonly known as the Alagoas croton, is a shrub native to the humid forests of northeastern Brazil.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves with a distinctive reddish or yellowish coloration. The plant produces small, clustered greenish flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary growth areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
