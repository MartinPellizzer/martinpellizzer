Chlorocyperus intermedius

Chlorocyperus intermedius, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Chlorocyperus intermedius is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic habitats.
