Citrus × nobilis subf. yonezawa-unshu

Citrus × nobilis subf.

yonezawa-unshu, commonly known as the Yonezawa unshu mandarin, is a hybrid citrus plant originating from Japan. It is characterized by its small, round fruits with a thin, smooth peel and a sweet, tangy flavor.

The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, with glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. It thrives in warm, subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit.

This subvariety is often cultivated for both commercial use and as a decorative plant in gardens.
