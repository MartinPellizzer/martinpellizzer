Carex atrovirens

Carex atrovirens, commonly known as blackleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowering spikes are usually borne on tall, slender stalks and produce small, inconspicuous flowers.

Carex atrovirens plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
