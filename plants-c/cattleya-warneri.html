Cattleya warneri

Cattleya warneri, commonly known as the Warner's Cattleya, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, white, and yellow. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often arranged in a fan shape.

Cattleya warneri is prized for its ornamental value and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
