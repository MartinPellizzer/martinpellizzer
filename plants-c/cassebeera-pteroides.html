Cassebeera pteroides

Cassebeera pteroides, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Brazilian hibiscus, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and lighter in color on the underside. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often with a prominent central stigma. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Cassebeera pteroides is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
