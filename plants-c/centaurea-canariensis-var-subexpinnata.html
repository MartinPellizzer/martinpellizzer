Centaurea canariensis var. subexpinnata

Centaurea canariensis var. subexpinnata, commonly known as the Canary Islands thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and spiny stems. The plant produces striking purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is adapted to the volcanic soils and Mediterranean climate of its native habitat.
