Choeradoplectron

Choeradoplectron, commonly known as the water lily, is a genus of aquatic plants characterized by their floating leaves and showy flowers.

These plants are typically found in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. The leaves are usually broad and have a waxy surface to prevent water from penetrating, while the roots anchor the plant in the sediment. Choeradoplectron species produce flowers that are often brightly colored, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
