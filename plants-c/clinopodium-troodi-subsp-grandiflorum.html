Clinopodium troodi subsp. grandiflorum

Clinopodium troodi subsp.

grandiflorum, commonly known as mountain betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. Clinopodium troodi subsp.

grandiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
