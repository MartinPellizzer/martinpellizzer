Clerodendrum ternifolium var. velutinosum

Clerodendrum ternifolium var. velutinosum, commonly known as velvetleaf clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers, which are often followed by distinctive, lantern-shaped fruits. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and ornamental value.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental appeal and can thrive in warm, humid climates.
