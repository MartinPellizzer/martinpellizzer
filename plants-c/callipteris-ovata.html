Callipteris ovata

Callipteris ovata, commonly known as the broadleaf palmetto, is a shrub-like plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, fan-shaped clusters of long, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into dark blue berries, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

Callipteris ovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in maritime environments.
