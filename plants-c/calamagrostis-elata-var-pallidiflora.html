Calamagrostis elata var. pallidiflora

Calamagrostis elata var. pallidiflora, commonly known as the silver feather reed grass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-blue foliage and panicles of pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and naturalized landscapes.

It is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
