Centaurea × baumgarteniana

Centaurea × baumgarteniana, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with lance-shaped leaves and strikingly showy composite flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Centaurea × baumgarteniana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
