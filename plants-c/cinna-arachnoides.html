Cinna arachnoides

Cinna arachnoides, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, often reddish stems and trifoliate or pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported diuretic properties.

Cinna arachnoides thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.
