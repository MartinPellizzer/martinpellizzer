Chrysophyllum acuminatum

Chrysophyllum acuminatum, commonly known as the Natal plum or yellow bell, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to yellow or orange when ripe. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought.

The tree is also valued for its edible fruit and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
