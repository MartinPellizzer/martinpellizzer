Convolvulus koieanus

Convolvulus koieanus, commonly known as the Japanese morning glory, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a trailing growth pattern that makes it suitable for garden borders or containers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often blooming from spring to early summer.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its vibrant flowers and adaptability.
