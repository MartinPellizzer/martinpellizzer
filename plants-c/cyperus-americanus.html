Cyperus americanus

Cyperus americanus, commonly known as the spike rush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and cylindrical flower spikes.

It is an herbaceous plant that typically grows in wetland environments, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often wind-pollinated. Cyperus americanus is known for its ability to thrive in saturated soils and is often used in wetland restoration projects.

Its ecological importance lies in providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
