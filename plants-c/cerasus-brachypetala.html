Cerasus brachypetala

Cerasus brachypetala, commonly known as the Chinese dwarf cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly cultivated for commercial use. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its compact size and attractive floral display.

Cerasus brachypetala prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes in temperate climates.
