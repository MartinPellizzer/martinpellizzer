Chrysocoma acicularis

Chrysocoma acicularis, commonly known as the needle-leaved chrysocoma, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and needle-like, which helps reduce water loss in its dry habitat.

Chrysocoma acicularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
