Ceratopsis rosea

Ceratopsis rosea, commonly known as the rose sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, and sometimes lobed leaves that are typically blue or gray-green in color. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, white, or purple, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Ceratopsis rosea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in coastal environments.
