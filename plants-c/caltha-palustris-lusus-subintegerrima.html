Caltha palustris lusus subintegerrima

Caltha palustris lusus subintegerrima, commonly known as the yellow crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has basal leaves that are broadly ovate with a waxy surface, and its stems are slender and often hairy. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
