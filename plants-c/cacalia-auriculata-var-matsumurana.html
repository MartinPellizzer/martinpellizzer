Cacalia auriculata var. matsumurana

Cacalia auriculata var. matsumurana, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often hairy as well.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
