Cebatha balfourii

Cebatha balfourii, commonly known as the Kashmir Himalayan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist climates. Its leaves are lanceolate and hairy, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Cebatha balfourii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
