Calamus radiatus

Calamus radiatus, commonly known as the radiate calamus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Aroid family (Araceae) and is characterized by its long, slender rhizomes and broad, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are typically hidden beneath a leaf-like spathe. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Calamus radiatus thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or marshy areas.
