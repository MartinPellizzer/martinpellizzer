Calycanthus philadelphicus

Calycanthus philadelphicus, commonly known as the Philadelphia shrub or sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features aromatic, red-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Calycanthus philadelphicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
