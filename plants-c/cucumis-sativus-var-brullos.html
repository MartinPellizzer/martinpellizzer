Cucumis sativus var. brullos

Cucumis sativus var. brullos, commonly known as the brullos cucumber, is a variety of cucumber characterized by its unique, wrinkled skin and slightly sweet flavor.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like melons and squashes. The plant is typically grown in warm climates and thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight. Its fruit is often used in salads and pickling due to its mild taste and crisp texture.

This variety is prized for its distinct appearance and versatility in culinary applications.
