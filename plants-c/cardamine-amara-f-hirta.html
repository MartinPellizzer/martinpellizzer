Cardamine amara f. hirta

Cardamine amara f. hirta, commonly known as the hairy bittercress, is a variant of the bittercress species characterized by its hairy foliage.

It is a small herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, which distinguishes it from other similar species. Its flowers are small, white, and borne in clusters, adding to its delicate appearance.

The plant is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often showy flowers.
