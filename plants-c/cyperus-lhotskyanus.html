Cyperus lhotskyanus

Cyperus lhotskyanus, commonly known as the narrowleaf paperplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along water bodies.

Cyperus lhotskyanus is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
