Cinnamomum nooteboomii

Cinnamomum nooteboomii, commonly known as the Nooteboom's cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue, berry-like fruits. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic compounds.

However, it is relatively rare and faces threats due to habitat loss and overharvesting.
