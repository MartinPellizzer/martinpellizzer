Cardiospermum halicacabum var. grandiflorum

Cardiospermum halicacabum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern and produces showy, pale yellow flowers that are often followed by inflated, orange-red seed pods. The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often used as a ornamental plant in gardens. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

The fruit, which resembles a small gourd, contains seeds that can be dispersed by wind or animals.
