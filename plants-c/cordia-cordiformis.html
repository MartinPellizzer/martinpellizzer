Cordia cordiformis

Cordia cordiformis, commonly known as the heart-shaped tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it valuable in herbal remedies.

Cordia cordiformis thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.
