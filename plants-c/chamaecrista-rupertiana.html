Chamaecrista rupertiana

Chamaecrista rupertiana, commonly known as the purple crown wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive purple flowers and feathery leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is well adapted to sandy soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Chamaecrista rupertiana is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
