Castanea prolifera

Castanea prolifera, commonly known as the Chinese horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the family Betulaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces catkin-like male flowers and female flowers in separate clusters, followed by spiny, woody fruits containing one or two seeds. Its leaves turn yellow in autumn, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance.

Castanea prolifera is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
