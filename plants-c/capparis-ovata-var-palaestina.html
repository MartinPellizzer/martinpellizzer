Capparis ovata var. palaestina

Capparis ovata var. palaestina, commonly known as the Palestine caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spherical, greenish-yellow fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

The leaves are typically narrow and have a slightly succulent texture, adapted to conserve water.
