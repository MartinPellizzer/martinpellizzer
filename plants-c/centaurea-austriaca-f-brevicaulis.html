Centaurea austriaca f. brevicaulis

Centaurea austriaca f. brevicaulis, commonly known as the Austrian knapweed dwarf form, is a compact variant of the Austrian knapweed.

It is a perennial herb native to Central Europe, characterized by its short stature and dense growth habit. The plant features narrow, blue-green leaves and produces small, reddish-purple flowers in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy meadows and rocky areas.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
