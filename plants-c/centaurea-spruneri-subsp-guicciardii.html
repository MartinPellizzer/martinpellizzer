Centaurea spruneri subsp. guicciardii

Centaurea spruneri subsp.

guicciardii, commonly known as the Sicilian centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily. It is a herbaceous perennial with upright stems and bright blue to purple flower heads.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
