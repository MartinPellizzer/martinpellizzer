Cyperus obtusiflorus var. sphaerocephalus

Cyperus obtusiflorus var. sphaerocephalus, commonly known as the globe sedge, is a perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a dense growth habit. The plant produces spherical, spike-like inflorescences that are often pale green or reddish in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and swampy areas.

Its botanical structure makes it adaptable to various aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
