Carex muricata var. laricina

Carex muricata var. laricina, commonly known as the roughleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular, grass-like stems. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically rough to the touch, giving it its common name. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks.

Its flowering spikes, which appear in late spring to early summer, produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.
