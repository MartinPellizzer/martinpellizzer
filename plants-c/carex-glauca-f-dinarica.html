Carex glauca f. dinarica

Carex glauca f. dinarica, commonly known as the Dinaric blue sedge, is a variant of the blue sedge species native to the Balkan region.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance and dense, compact growth habit. The leaves are typically bluish-green, with a waxy coating that gives them a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
