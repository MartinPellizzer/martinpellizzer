Critesion murinum subsp. leporinum

Critesion murinum subsp.

leporinum, commonly known as the moonflower or night-blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close by morning.

The plant has a succulent stem with spiny ridges, and its leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-drained soil and minimal water.

The species is popular in horticulture for its striking nocturnal blooms and relatively low maintenance requirements.
