Castanea chrysophylla var. minor

Castanea chrysophylla var. minor, commonly known as the golden-leaf chestnut, is a variant of the Chinese chestnut.

It is characterized by its striking yellow foliage, which turns golden in the autumn. This deciduous shrub or small tree is native to China and thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, edible nuts enclosed in spiny husks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential for cultivation in gardens.
