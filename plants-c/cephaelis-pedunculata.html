Cephaelis pedunculata

Cephaelis pedunculata, commonly known as the flame violet, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy. Its most striking feature is its clusters of bright red or orange tubular flowers, which bloom in spring and summer.

Cephaelis pedunculata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
