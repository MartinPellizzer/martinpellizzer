Carex subglobosa

Carex subglobosa, commonly known as the globular sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Carex subglobosa typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
