Corydalis longiflora var. caudata

Corydalis longiflora var. caudata, commonly known as the long-flowered corydalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, often hairy stems that grow from 30 to 60 centimeters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers with two upper petals that are typically pink to purple in color. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a somewhat hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value in gardens and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
