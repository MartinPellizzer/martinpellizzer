Cyperus fulvoalbescens

Cyperus fulvoalbescens, commonly known as the yellow-white flatsedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and pond margins, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the sedge family. Its roots are often used in phytoremediation due to their ability to absorb heavy metals from contaminated water.

Cyperus fulvoalbescens is an important species for wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
