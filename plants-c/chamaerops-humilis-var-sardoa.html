Chamaerops humilis var. sardoa

Chamaerops humilis var. sardoa, commonly known as the Sardinian fan palm, is a variety of the Mediterranean fan palm native to the island of Sardinia.

It is a small, cold-hardy palm characterized by its fan-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clusters of black, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appearance and resilience in Mediterranean climates.
