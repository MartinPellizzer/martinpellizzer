Cerasus erythrocarpa f. glandulosa

Cerasus erythrocarpa f. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular cherry, is a botanical variant of the cherry species characterized by its glandular structures on the leaves and stems.

This plant is native to parts of Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges, and the flowers are small, white or pink, appearing in clusters. The fruit is a small, red drupe that ripens in late summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
