Campanula baborensis

Campanula baborensis, commonly known as the Baborensis bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. It produces showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Campanula baborensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
