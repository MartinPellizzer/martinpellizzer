Cynoglossum officinale micranthum

Cynoglossum officinale micranthum, commonly known as narrowleaf forget-me-not, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

Its botanical name reflects its historical use in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
