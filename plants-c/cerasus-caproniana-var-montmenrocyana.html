Cerasus caproniana var. montmenrocyana

Cerasus caproniana var. montmenrocyana, commonly known as the Montmenrocyana cherry, is a rare and ornamental species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of white to pale pink. The plant produces small, sweet-tart cherries that are edible but not widely cultivated for commercial use. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a cold winter dormancy period to produce fruit.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a botanical curiosity and is often grown in specialized gardens.
