Campanula × murrii

Campanula × murrii, commonly known as the Bluebell bellflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its long flowering period and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
