Chamaesium paradoxum

Chamaesium paradoxum, commonly known as the paradoxical sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage near the ground. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are succulent and adapted to store water, reflecting its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments.

Chamaesium paradoxum is often found in sandy or rocky soils, where it plays a role in stabilizing the soil and supporting local biodiversity.
