Cyperus papyrus subsp. siculus

Cyperus papyrus subsp.

siculus, commonly known as the Sicilian papyrus, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family.

This subspecies is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as ponds and lakeshores.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color.
