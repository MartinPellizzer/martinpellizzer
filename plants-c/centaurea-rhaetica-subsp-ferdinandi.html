Centaurea rhaetica subsp. ferdinandi

Centaurea rhaetica subsp.

ferdinandi, commonly known as the Rhaetian centaury, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bristly seeds. Native to the Alps, it thrives in rocky and alpine meadows at high elevations.

It is a perennial herb with a compact stature, often found in mountainous regions of Europe.
