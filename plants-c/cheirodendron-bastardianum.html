Cheirodendron bastardianum

Cheirodendron bastardianum, commonly known as the Philippine mahogany or red cedar, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing smooth, black seeds.

Due to its ornamental and commercial value, it is often cultivated in horticulture and forestry.
