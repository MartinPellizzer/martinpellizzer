Crataegus mollis f. mecocantha

Crataegus mollis f. mecocantha, commonly known as the white thornapple, is a compact shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has thorny stems and oval, dark green leaves with serrated edges. Its fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in the fall.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
