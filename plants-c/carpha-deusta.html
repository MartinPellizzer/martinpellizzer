Carpha deusta

Carpha deusta, commonly known as the common reed, is a tall, grass-like plant that thrives in wetland environments.

It has long, slender leaves and produces fluffy seed heads that disperse its seeds by wind. This plant is often found in marshes, along riverbanks, and in other moist habitats. Carpha deusta plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas make it both ecologically significant and sometimes invasive.
