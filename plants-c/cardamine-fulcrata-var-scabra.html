Cardamine fulcrata var. scabra

Cardamine fulcrata var. scabra, commonly known as the scabrous watercress, is a perennial herb native to moist, shaded environments in North America.

It features slender, hairy stems that often grow from the base and are typically less than 30 cm in height. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from May to July. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and have a distinctive, serrated appearance.

The species is characterized by its preference for calcareous soils and its ability to thrive in wetland habitats.
