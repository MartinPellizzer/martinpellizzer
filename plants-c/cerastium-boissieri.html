Cerastium boissieri

Cerastium boissieri, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, hairy leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens as an ornamental plant.
