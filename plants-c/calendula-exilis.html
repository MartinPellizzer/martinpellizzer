Calendula exilis

Calendula exilis, commonly known as the slender calendula, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats and features small, yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive central disc. Calendula exilis has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
