Capparis spinosa var. vulgaris

Capparis spinosa var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces edible, green buds that are harvested before they fully open. These buds are used in various culinary applications, including pickling and seasoning.

Botanically, it belongs to the Capparaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in poor, rocky soils.
