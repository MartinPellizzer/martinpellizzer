Clethra longispicata var. novoguineensis

Clethra longispicata var. novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea clethra, is a species native to the tropical regions of New Guinea.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several small, winged seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
