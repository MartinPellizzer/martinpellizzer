Castanea crenata var. dulcis

Castanea crenata var. dulcis, commonly known as the Japanese sweet chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its oval, spiny fruits that mature to a chestnut-like appearance. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value. The tree produces edible nuts enclosed in a thick, hairy husk, which are prized for their sweet flavor.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and its culinary uses.
