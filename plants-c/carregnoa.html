Carregnoa

Carregnoa, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Carregnoa is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soils.
