Crinum cruentum

Crinum cruentum, commonly known as the crimson crinum, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces tall, erect stems with narrow, strap-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its flowers are typically pink to red in hue, with six petals and a prominent central spadix.

Crinum cruentum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
