Callitriche stagnalis f. submersa

Callitriche stagnalis f. submersa, commonly known as the submerged water starwort, is a aquatic plant that thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its small, star-shaped leaves that are typically fully submerged, adapting to underwater conditions. The plant produces tiny, white flowers that emerge above the water surface, though this feature may vary depending on environmental factors. Its reproductive strategy involves both sexual and asexual propagation, allowing it to spread efficiently in suitable habitats.

The species plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
