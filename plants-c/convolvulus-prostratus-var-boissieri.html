Convolvulus prostratus var. boissieri

Convolvulus prostratus var. boissieri, commonly known as the prostrate convolvulus, is a low-growing, trailing plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pale yellow or white, often with a slight reddish tinge. The plant has hairy, slender stems and heart-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and hardy plant for coastal and arid environments.
