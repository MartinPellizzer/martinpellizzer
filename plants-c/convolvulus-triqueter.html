Convolvulus triqueter

Convolvulus triqueter, commonly known as the three-angled morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, winding stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive three-angled appearance. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the early morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

Convolvulus triqueter is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
