Crotalaria retusifolia

Crotalaria retusifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes. The leaves are typically narrow and pointed, with a slightly hairy surface. This species belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and dry forests.
