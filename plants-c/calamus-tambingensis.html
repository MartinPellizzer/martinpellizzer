Calamus tambingensis

Calamus tambingensis, commonly known as the Tambing reed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in mangrove forests.

The plant has been used traditionally for its fibrous material, which is harvested for making baskets and other crafts.
