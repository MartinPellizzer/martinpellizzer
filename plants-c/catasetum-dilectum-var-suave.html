Catasetum dilectum var. suave

Catasetum dilectum var. suave, commonly known as the Sweet Catasetum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

This orchid is characterized by its striking, elongated flowers that often display a vibrant mix of red, orange, and yellow hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of flowers, with each bloom featuring a unique, intricate structure.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its exotic appearance in botanical gardens and private collections.
