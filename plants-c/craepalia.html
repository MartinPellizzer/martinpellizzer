Craepalia

Craepalia, commonly known as the mountain gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers, which typically bloom in late summer. The plant is native to alpine regions of Europe and is often found in rocky or meadow environments. Craepalia has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
