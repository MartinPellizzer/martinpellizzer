Catasetum ornithoides

Catasetum ornithoides, commonly known as the bird's nest orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, with a distinctive, elongated spur that resembles a bird's beak. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Catasetum ornithoides is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its striking appearance and rarity in cultivation.
