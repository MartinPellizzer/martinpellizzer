Carex saxatilis f. pudica

Carex saxatilis f. pudica, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences that are usually greenish or brownish in color. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

Due to its preference for specific habitats, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
