Coreopsis aspilioides

Coreopsis aspilioides, commonly known as the prairie coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its flowers are composed of ray florets and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Coreopsis aspilioides is well adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
