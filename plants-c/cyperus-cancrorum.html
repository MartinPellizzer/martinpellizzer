Cyperus cancrorum

Cyperus cancrorum, commonly known as the cancer root or swamp sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the Americas.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus cancrorum is adapted to tolerate waterlogged conditions and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
