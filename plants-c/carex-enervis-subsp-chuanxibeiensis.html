Carex enervis subsp. chuanxibeiensis

Carex enervis subsp.

chuanxibeiensis, commonly known as the Chuanxi bog sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves.

This subspecies typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-poor soils. Its inflorescence consists of a single, cylindrical spike that bears small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and supporting local biodiversity.
