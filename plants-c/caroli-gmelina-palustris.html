Caroli-gmelina palustris

Caroli-gmelina palustris, commonly known as swamp ironwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small tree or shrub that typically grows in wetland or swampy environments. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. Its wood is dense and resistant to decay, making it valuable for construction.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various species.
