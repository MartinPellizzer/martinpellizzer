Caricina paradoxa

Caricina paradoxa, commonly known as the paradoxical grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

Caricina paradoxa is notable for its ability to tolerate periodic flooding, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
