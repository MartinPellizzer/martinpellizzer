Cirsium arvense var. discolor

Cirsium arvense var. discolor, commonly known as yellow cockle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant features spiny leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its stems are often reddish and covered with fine hairs.

It is characterized by its variable coloration, with some leaves showing a distinct whitish or pale green underside.
