Commiphora longipedicellata

Commiphora longipedicellata, commonly known as the African myrrh tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing species. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by fleshy, red or orange berries.

The plant is valued for its aromatic resins, which have been used traditionally in incense and medicinal applications.
