Cnicus liebmannii

Cnicus liebmannii, commonly known as the Liebmann's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. Its leaves are spiny and lobed, while its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive central disc.

Cnicus liebmannii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
