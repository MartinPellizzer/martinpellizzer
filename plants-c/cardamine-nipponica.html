Cardamine nipponica

Cardamine nipponica, commonly known as Japanese water shield, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It typically grows in wet or aquatic environments, such as marshes and the edges of ponds. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often floating or submerged, with a smooth, waxy surface. Its small white or greenish flowers appear in clusters and are pollinated by insects.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
