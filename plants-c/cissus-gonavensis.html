Cissus gonavensis

Cissus gonavensis, commonly known as the Madagascar grape ivy, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Cissus gonavensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
