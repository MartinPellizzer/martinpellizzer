Calorophus impolitus

Calorophus impolitus, commonly known as the roughleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant features rough, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, and its stems are often reddish or purple-hued. It produces yellow ray flowers surrounded by numerous yellow to orange disk flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Calorophus impolitus is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy addition to xeriscaped gardens.
