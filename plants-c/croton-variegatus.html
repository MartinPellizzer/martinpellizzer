Croton variegatus

Croton variegatus, commonly known as the variegated croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with variegated patterns of green, yellow, and red. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits. Its leaves are typically broad and have a rough, textured surface.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
