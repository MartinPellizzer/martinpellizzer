Cuphea killipii

Cuphea killipii, commonly known as the flame flower, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red flowers that resemble flames, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has hairy, branching stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking heat-tolerant plants.

Cuphea killipii is also valued for its ability to attract hummingbirds and other pollinators.
