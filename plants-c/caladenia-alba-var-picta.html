Caladenia alba var. picta

Caladenia alba var. picta, commonly known as the White Spider Orchid, is a striking orchid species native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, white flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in open habitats, often in regions with a temperate climate. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, particularly wasps, due to their unique coloration and scent.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
