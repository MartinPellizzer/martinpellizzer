Cicerbita petiolata

Cicerbita petiolata, commonly known as the leafy groundsel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features finely divided, silvery-green leaves that are often covered in a fuzzy texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Cicerbita petiolata is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats including meadows and woodland edges.
