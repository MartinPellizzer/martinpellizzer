Campanula elatior

Campanula elatior, commonly known as the tall bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, making it one of the tallest species in the Campanulaceae family. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are sturdy and branching.

Campanula elatior thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and woodland edges.
