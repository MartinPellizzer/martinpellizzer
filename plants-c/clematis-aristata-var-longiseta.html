Clematis aristata var. longiseta

Clematis aristata var. longiseta, commonly known as the longiseta Clematis, is a climbing vine native to the western United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, blue-black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

Its vigorous growth habit requires support structures like trellises or fences for optimal display.
