Cadia pendula

Cadia pendula, commonly known as the hanging cadia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with slender, branching stems. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters that give it a delicate, cascading appearance.

Cadia pendula thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
