Capsella viaria

Capsella viaria, commonly known as the common mouse-ear or mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and open areas. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are small, yellowish-white, and arranged in racemes. Capsella viaria produces small, round pods that contain several seeds.

It is often found in temperate regions and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
