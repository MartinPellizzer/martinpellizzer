Cynoglossum cheirifolium subvar. mariolense

Cynoglossum cheirifolium subvar. mariolense, commonly known as the Moroccan bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
