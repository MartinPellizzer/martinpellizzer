Chrysocephalum apiculatum subsp. semiamplexicaule

Chrysocephalum apiculatum subsp.

semiamplexicaule, commonly known as the narrow-leaf golden everlasting, is a perennial shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The leaves are narrow and often slightly clasping the stems, giving it its subspecies name. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
