Crassula garibina subsp. garibina

Crassula garibina subsp.

garibina, commonly known as the Garibina crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features fleshy, thick leaves that store water, typical of succulents adapted to arid environments.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
