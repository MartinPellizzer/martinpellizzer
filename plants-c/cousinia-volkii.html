Cousinia volkii

Cousinia volkii, commonly known as the Volk's cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, harsh environments. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Cousinia volkii is often found in alpine or steppe habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
