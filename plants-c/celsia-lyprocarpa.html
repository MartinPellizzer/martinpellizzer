Celsia lyprocarpa

Celsia lyprocarpa, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowers typically bloom in late summer and early fall, producing clusters of yellow to orange disk flowers surrounded by white ray florets.

Celsia lyprocarpa thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
