Carex phaeothrix

Carex phaeothrix, commonly known as the black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dark green to almost black coloration, giving it its common name.

The inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, inconspicuous flowers, which are wind-pollinated.
