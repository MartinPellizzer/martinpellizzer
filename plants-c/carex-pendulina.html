Carex pendulina

Carex pendulina, commonly known as the hanging sedge, is a perennial plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Carex genus, which is part of the grass family (Poaceae). This plant is characterized by its drooping, cylindrical inflorescences that give it its common name. Carex pendulina typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

Its foliage is grass-like, with narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating.
