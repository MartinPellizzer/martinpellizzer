Crocodilodes erysithales

Crocodilodes erysithales, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking red-tipped leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and marshlands. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, organic soil.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated primarily by bees and other small insects.
