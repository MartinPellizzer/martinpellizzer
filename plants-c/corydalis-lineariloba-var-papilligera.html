Corydalis lineariloba var. papilligera

Corydalis lineariloba var. papilligera, commonly known as the papillate linear Corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and delicate, spurred flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high altitudes. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

The variety is distinguished by the presence of small papillae on the petals, a unique morphological feature.
