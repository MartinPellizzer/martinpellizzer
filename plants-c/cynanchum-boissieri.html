Cynanchum boissieri

Cynanchum boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, and it is known for its milky sap, which is a common feature in the Asclepiadaceae family.

Cynanchum boissieri is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
