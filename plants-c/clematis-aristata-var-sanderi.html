Clematis aristata var. sanderi

Clematis aristata var. sanderi, commonly known as the Sander's Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, rusty-colored hairs. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
