Cantua cordata

Cantua cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf gentian, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for.

Cantua cordata is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
