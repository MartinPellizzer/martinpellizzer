Carex augustinowiczii var. sharensis

Carex augustinowiczii var. sharensis, commonly known as the Sharenskii sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly found in wet or moist environments. This variety is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, making it a common sight in marshes and wetlands.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic habitats.
