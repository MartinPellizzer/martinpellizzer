Campanula schleicheri

Campanula schleicheri, commonly known as the Schleicher's bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy and robust.

Campanula schleicheri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
