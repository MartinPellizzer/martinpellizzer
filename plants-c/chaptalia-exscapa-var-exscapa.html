Chaptalia exscapa var. exscapa

Chaptalia exscapa var. exscapa, commonly known as the alpine chaptalia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes with cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, forming a cluster at the top of the stem.

This variant is distinguished by its compact growth form and adaptability to harsh mountain climates.
