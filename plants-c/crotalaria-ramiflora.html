Crotalaria ramiflora

Crotalaria ramiflora, commonly known as the branched cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and produces vibrant yellow flowers with distinctive spurred petals. Its seed pods are typically elongated and contain several angular seeds.

Crotalaria ramiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
