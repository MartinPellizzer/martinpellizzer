Ceropegia sphenanthera

Ceropegia sphenanthera, commonly known as the African violet or the string of hearts, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has adapted to arid environments with its succulent leaves and ability to store water. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Ceropegia sphenanthera is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
