Cordia corymbosa f. typica

Cordia corymbosa f. typica, commonly known as the common hibiscus or red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink, often with a yellow throat. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Cordia corymbosa f. typica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
