Catasetum × splendens var. obrienianum

Catasetum × splendens var. obrienianum, commonly known as the O'Brien's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that often display a mix of red, yellow, and orange hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its inflorescence consists of multiple flowers arranged in a spike, with each bloom featuring a unique structure that aids in pollination by specific insects.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated by enthusiasts for its striking appearance.
