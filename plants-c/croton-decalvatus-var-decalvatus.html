Croton decalvatus var. decalvatus

Croton decalvatus var. decalvatus, commonly known as the smooth croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, often reddish bark and alternate, simple, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
