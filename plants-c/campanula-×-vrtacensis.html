Campanula × vrtacensis

Campanula × vrtacensis, commonly known as the hybrid bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.

Campanula × vrtacensis is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
