Convolvulus sherardii

Convolvulus sherardii, commonly known as the Persian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it a distinctive addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
