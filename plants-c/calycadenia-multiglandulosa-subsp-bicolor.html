Calycadenia multiglandulosa subsp. bicolor

Calycadenia multiglandulosa subsp.

bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, narrow leaves covered in fine, glandular hairs.

The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.
