Cyperus mysurensis

Cyperus mysurensis, commonly known as the Mysore flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. It has slender, triangular stems and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or reddish in color.

Cyperus mysurensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
