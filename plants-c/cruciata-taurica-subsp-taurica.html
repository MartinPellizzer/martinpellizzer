Cruciata taurica subsp. taurica

Cruciata taurica subsp.

taurica, commonly known as the Taurian cruciata, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is characterized by its upright stems and small, white or pale pink flowers.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It blooms from June to August, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
