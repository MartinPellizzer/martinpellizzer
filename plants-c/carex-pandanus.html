Carex pandanus

Carex pandanus, commonly known as the pandanus sedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands. The plant features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and elongated, with a fibrous root system. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in humid climates.
