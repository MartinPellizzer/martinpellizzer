Ceropegia arenaria

Ceropegia arenaria, commonly known as the sand ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and small, fleshy leaves adapted to arid environments. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or yellowish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for dry climates.

Ceropegia arenaria is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
