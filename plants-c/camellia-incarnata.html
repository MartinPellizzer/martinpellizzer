Camellia incarnata

Camellia incarnata, commonly known as the rose of Sharon, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and produces red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Camellia incarnata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
