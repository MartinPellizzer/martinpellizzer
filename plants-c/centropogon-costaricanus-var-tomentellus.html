Centropogon costaricanus var. tomentellus

Centropogon costaricanus var. tomentellus, commonly known as the Costa Rican centropogon, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as under forest canopies. Its leaves are usually covered in a fine, fuzzy coating, giving it a soft, velvety appearance.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
