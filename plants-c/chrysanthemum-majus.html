Chrysanthemum majus

Chrysanthemum majus, commonly known as the Great Marigold, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and prefers well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers come in a range of colors including yellow, orange, and red.

Chrysanthemum majus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
