Cirsium arizonicum var. bipinnatum

Cirsium arizonicum var. bipinnatum, commonly known as the Arizona thistle, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a robust root system that helps it survive in arid environments. Its leaves are typically covered in fine hairs, which reduce water loss.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
