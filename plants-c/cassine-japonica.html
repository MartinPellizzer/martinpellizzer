Cassine japonica

Cassine japonica, commonly known as Japanese boxleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves with a hairy underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks.

Cassine japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its foliage and seasonal interest.
