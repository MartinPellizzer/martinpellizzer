Chamaecyparis nootkatensis pendens

Chamaecyparis nootkatensis pendens, commonly known as the drooping Alaska cedar, is a member of the cypress family (Cupressaceae).

It is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the coastal regions of Alaska and western Canada. The plant features scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its cones are small and spherical, and the tree is known for its graceful, weeping form.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique shape and hardiness.
