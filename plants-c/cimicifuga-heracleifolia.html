Cimicifuga heracleifolia

Cimicifuga heracleifolia, commonly known as American bugbane, is a perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy white or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are compound, with multiple leaflets arranged in a feather-like pattern.

Cimicifuga heracleifolia is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for pollinators.
