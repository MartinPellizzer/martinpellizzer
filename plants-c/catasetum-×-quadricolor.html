Catasetum × quadricolor

Catasetum × quadricolor, commonly known as the rainbow orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers that often display a mix of red, yellow, and orange hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of showy, spurred flowers that attract specific pollinators.

The botanical features of Catasetum × quadricolor make it a popular choice for both horticultural display and botanical study.
