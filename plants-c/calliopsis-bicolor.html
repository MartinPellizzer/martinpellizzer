Calliopsis bicolor

Calliopsis bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bilaterally symmetrical flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, acidic soils. Its flowers are usually white with a deep purple or violet hue on the lower petals, creating a distinctive two-color appearance.

Calliopsis bicolor is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
