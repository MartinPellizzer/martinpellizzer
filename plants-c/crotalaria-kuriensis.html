Crotalaria kuriensis

Crotalaria kuriensis, commonly known as the Kuriensis false cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and palmate leaves. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

Crotalaria kuriensis is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats such as savannas and forest edges.
