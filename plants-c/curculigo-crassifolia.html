Curculigo crassifolia

Curculigo crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its arrowhead-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
