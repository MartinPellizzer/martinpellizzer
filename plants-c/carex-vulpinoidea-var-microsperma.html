Carex vulpinoidea var. microsperma

Carex vulpinoidea var. microsperma, commonly known as the small-seeded fox sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. This variety is native to North America and thrives in moist to wet environments such as marshes and wetlands. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
