Colutea davisiana

Colutea davisiana, commonly known as the Davis' Cabbage Tree, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with a woody stem and can grow up to several feet in height. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely known outside of botanical circles.
