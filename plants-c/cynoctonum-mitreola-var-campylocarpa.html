Cynoctonum mitreola var. campylocarpa

Cynoctonum mitreola var. campylocarpa, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cactaceae family.

It is characterized by its segmented, branching stems that bear vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid environments. It produces flowers during the holiday season, typically around December, hence its common name.

The leaves are usually modified into thorn-like structures, and the plant is often cultivated as an ornamental species due to its aesthetic appeal.
