Capparis cambodiana

Capparis cambodiana, commonly known as the Cambodian caper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant has woody stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Capparis cambodiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
