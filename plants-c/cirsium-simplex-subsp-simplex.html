Cirsium simplex subsp. simplex

Cirsium simplex subsp.

simplex, commonly known as the slender thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its spiny, woolly leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with a robust root system.

Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
