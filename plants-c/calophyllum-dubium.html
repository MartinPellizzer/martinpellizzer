Calophyllum dubium

Calophyllum dubium, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a member of the Clusiaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release winged seeds.

The tree is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
