Coleus anthonyi

Coleus anthonyi, commonly known as Anthony's coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage with leaves that can range from deep red to bright green and variegated patterns. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in clusters.

Coleus anthonyi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in warm, humid environments.
