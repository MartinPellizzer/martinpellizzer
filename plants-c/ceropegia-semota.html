Ceropegia semota

Ceropegia semota, commonly known as the "Lion's Tail," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features trailing stems with fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces unique, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive, intricate structure. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in hanging baskets.
