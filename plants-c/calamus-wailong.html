Calamus wailong

Calamus wailong, commonly known as the Wailong fragrant reed, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 3 meters in height, often growing in wetland or marshy environments. The plant produces long, narrow leaves with a smooth, waxy surface, and its inflorescence consists of clustered, spadix-like flowers. Calamus wailong is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its rhizomes are also utilized in the production of natural fragrances and incense.
