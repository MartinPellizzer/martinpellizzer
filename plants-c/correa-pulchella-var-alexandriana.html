Correa pulchella var. alexandriana

Correa pulchella var. alexandriana, commonly known as the Alexandrian correa, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract native pollinators such as birds and insects.
