Campomanesia rhombea var. kleinii

Campomanesia rhombea var. kleinii, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree or *Jaboticaba*, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible, dark purple fruits that are rich in antioxidants and often used in jams and beverages. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Its ornamental value and fruit production make it a popular plant in horticulture.
