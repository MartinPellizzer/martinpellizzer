Crepis virens var. dentata

Crepis virens var. dentata, commonly known as the toothed grass.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its slender stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a single head, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are often serrated or toothed, giving it the specific epithet "dentata.".

This variety is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.
