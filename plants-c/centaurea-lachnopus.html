Centaurea lachnopus

Centaurea lachnopus, commonly known as the downy centaurea, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

The flowers are usually pink or purple, and they bloom from late spring to early autumn.
