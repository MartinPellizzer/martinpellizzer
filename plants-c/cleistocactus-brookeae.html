Cleistocactus brookeae

Cleistocactus brookeae, commonly known as the Brooke's close-fleshed cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Argentina.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense, ribbed stem that stores water efficiently. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom at night, a trait typical of many desert cacti. Its spines are short and cylindrical, providing protection from herbivores and reducing water loss.

Cleistocactus brookeae is well adapted to extreme desert conditions, making it a resilient and unique member of the Cactaceae family.
