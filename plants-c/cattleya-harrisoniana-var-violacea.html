Cattleya harrisoniana var. violacea

Cattleya harrisoniana var. violacea, commonly known as the violet-flowered Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid prized for its striking violet and white flowers.

It originates from the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid environments. The plant features long, arching pseudobulbs and broad, lance-shaped leaves that support its showy blooms. Its flowers are fragrant and typically appear in late spring or early summer.

This variety is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively ease of cultivation.
