Crotalaria kandoensis

Crotalaria kandoensis, commonly known as the Kando's crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in racemes, and are attractive to pollinators.

Crotalaria kandoensis is noted for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
