Centaurea tingitana

Centaurea tingitana, commonly known as the Moroccan knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, purple to pinkish-red composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed or arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Centaurea tingitana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
