Calamagrostis epigejos var. major

Calamagrostis epigejos var. major, commonly known as the giant calamagrostis, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces feathery panicles of small, whitish to purplish flowers in the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
