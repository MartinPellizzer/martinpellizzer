Carpodinus complicata

Carpodinus complicata, commonly known as the clustered leaf cactus, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, branched stems that bear small, fleshy leaves arranged in a compact, rosette-like formation. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are typically thick and waxy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Despite its delicate appearance, it is remarkably resilient and thrives in well-drained soil with minimal water.
