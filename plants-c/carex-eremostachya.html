Carex eremostachya

Carex eremostachya, commonly known as the desert sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Carex eremostachya plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
