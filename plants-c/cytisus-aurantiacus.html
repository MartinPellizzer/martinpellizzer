Cytisus aurantiacus

Cytisus aurantiacus, commonly known as the orange broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, often along coastal areas. The plant produces bright orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are small, narrow, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
