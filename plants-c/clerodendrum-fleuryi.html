Clerodendrum fleuryi

Clerodendrum fleuryi, commonly known as the African flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

The bark is smooth and may exude a milky sap when damaged.
