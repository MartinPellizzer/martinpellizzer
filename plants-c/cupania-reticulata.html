Cupania reticulata

Cupania reticulata, commonly known as the Chinese elderberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces aggregate fruits that mature into red, fleshy berries, which are edible for humans and animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Cupania reticulata is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses.
