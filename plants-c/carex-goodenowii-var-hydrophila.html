Carex goodenowii var. hydrophila

Carex goodenowii var. hydrophila, commonly known as the water sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to aquatic environments and is often found in marshes, pond margins, and along streams.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a key role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
