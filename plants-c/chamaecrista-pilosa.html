Chamaecrista pilosa

Chamaecrista pilosa, commonly known as the sicklepod or velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that are typically curved and covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of climates.

Chamaecrista pilosa is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing abilities and is used in some traditional medicinal practices.
