Convolvulus scrobiculatus

Convolvulus scrobiculatus, commonly known as the rough morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual with trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The flowers are typically pale blue to purple, with five petals and a distinctive scrofulous appearance on the inner surface. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds and runners.
