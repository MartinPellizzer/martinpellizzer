Cuphea guelichii

Cuphea guelichii, commonly known as the firecracker plant, is a flowering shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that resemble firecrackers, hence its common name. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy blooms throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Cuphea guelichii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
