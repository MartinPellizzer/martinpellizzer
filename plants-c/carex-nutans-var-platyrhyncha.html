Carex nutans var. platyrhyncha

Carex nutans var. platyrhyncha, commonly known as the broadbeak sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Carex nutans var. platyrhyncha is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality in aquatic environments.
