Chamaeiris

Chamaeiris, commonly known as the rock heath, is a genus of flowering plants in the Ericaceae family.

These plants are typically found in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. They are characterized by their low-growing habit and evergreen foliage. Chamaeiris species produce small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white.

The genus is notable for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
