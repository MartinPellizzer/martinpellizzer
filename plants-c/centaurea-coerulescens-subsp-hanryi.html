Centaurea coerulescens subsp. hanryi

Centaurea coerulescens subsp.

hanryi, commonly known as the Alpine blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and slender, branching stems.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. It produces small, bracteate leaves and has a compact growth habit.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
