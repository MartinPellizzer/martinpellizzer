Campanula precatoria

Campanula precatoria, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and grayish-green in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Campanula precatoria is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
