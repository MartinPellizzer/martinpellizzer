Crocus incompletus

Crocus incompletus, commonly known as the incomplete crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, typically found in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pale pink, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often appearing after the flowers.

The plant is characterized by its incomplete tepals, giving it its scientific name.
