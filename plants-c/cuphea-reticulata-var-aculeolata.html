Cuphea reticulata var. aculeolata

Cuphea reticulata var. aculeolata, commonly known as the spiked cuphea, is a striking ornamental plant native to Central America.

It features vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces spiny bracts that give it its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens and as a container plant.
