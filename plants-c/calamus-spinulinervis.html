Calamus spinulinervis

Calamus spinulinervis, commonly known as the spinulose calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arales.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, slender rhizome and long, narrow leaves with a distinctive spinulose texture along the midrib. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, typical of the genus Calamus. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value is sometimes appreciated for its unique leaf structure and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
