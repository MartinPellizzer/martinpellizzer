Carduus pulcher

Carduus pulcher, commonly known as the handsome thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and large, showy purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help to reduce water loss.

The flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, making it an important plant in ecological gardens.
