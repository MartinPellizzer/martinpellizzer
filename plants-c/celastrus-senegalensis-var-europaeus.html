Celastrus senegalensis var. europaeus

Celastrus senegalensis var. europaeus, commonly known as European African bindweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Europe.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its opposite, simple, and often trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it can become a nuisance in certain ecosystems.
