Cistus umbellatus var. verticillatus

Cistus umbellatus var. verticillatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, evergreen leaves that are narrow and arranged in whorls, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The plant's essential oils, derived from its leaves and flowers, are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
