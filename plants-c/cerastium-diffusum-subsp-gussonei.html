Cerastium diffusum subsp. gussonei

Cerastium diffusum subsp.

gussonei, commonly known as the creeping mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its mat-forming growth habit with numerous small, rounded leaves.

The plant produces clusters of tiny white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it a useful plant in ecological restoration projects.
