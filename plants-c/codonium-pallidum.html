Codonium pallidum

Codonium pallidum, commonly known as the pale codon, is a small, annual plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in umbels. The leaves are pinnately divided, with a delicate, fern-like appearance. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

The plant is known for its subtle fragrance and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
