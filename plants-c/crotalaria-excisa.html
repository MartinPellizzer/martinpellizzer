Crotalaria excisa

Crotalaria excisa, commonly known as the cutleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are deeply lobed and often appear cut or fragmented. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by elongated, spiny pods containing several seeds. Crotalaria excisa is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobium bacteria.

It is often cultivated as a forage crop and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
