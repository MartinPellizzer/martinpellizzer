Ceratocephalus exasperatus

Ceratocephalus exasperatus, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It produces a capsule fruit that releases seeds with tufts of hair for wind dispersal.

Ceratocephalus exasperatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its ecosystem.
