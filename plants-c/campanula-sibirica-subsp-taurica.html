Campanula sibirica subsp. taurica

Campanula sibirica subsp.

taurica, commonly known as the Tauric bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus and parts of southern Russia. It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
