Chamaecrista portoricensis var. granulata

Chamaecrista portoricensis var. granulata, commonly known as the granulated crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Puerto Rico.

It is a shrub characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a distinctive granular texture on its stems and leaves, which gives it its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry forests.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
