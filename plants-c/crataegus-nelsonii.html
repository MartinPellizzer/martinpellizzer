Crataegus nelsonii

Crataegus nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands or along riverbanks.

Crataegus nelsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
