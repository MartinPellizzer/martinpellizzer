Claoxylon brachybotryon

Claoxylon brachybotryon, commonly known as the thick-leafed sandarac tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody fruits containing several seeds. Its bark has been historically used to produce a resin known as sandarac, which was used in various industrial applications.

The plant is valued for both its ecological role and its economic potential in traditional practices.
