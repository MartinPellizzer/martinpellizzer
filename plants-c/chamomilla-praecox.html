Chamomilla praecox

Chamomilla praecox, commonly known as German chamomile, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, aromatic leaves and produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers. The plant is widely cultivated for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming and anti-inflammatory effects. Its essential oil, derived from the flowering tops, contains compounds like bisabolol and chamazulene.

Chamomilla praecox thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a popular choice for both ornamental and therapeutic gardening.
