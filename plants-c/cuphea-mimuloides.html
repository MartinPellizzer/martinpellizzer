Cuphea mimuloides

Cuphea mimuloides, commonly known as the "Whispering Bells," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, tubular, red or pink flowers that resemble tiny bells. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often grown as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and unique flower shape.

Cuphea mimuloides is well-suited for containers and garden beds, requiring well-draining soil and moderate watering.
