Citharexylum quadrangulare

Citharexylum quadrangulare, commonly known as the four-angle cithara, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree with smooth, quadrangular stems that are often aromatic. The leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, round, woody fruits.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and scent.
