Centranthus ruber

Centranthus ruber, commonly known as red valerian, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and rock gardens.

Centranthus ruber is also valued for its aromatic foliage and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
