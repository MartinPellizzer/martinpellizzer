Coleus bullulatus

Coleus bullulatus, commonly known as the woolly coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, hairy leaves that range in color from silver to deep purple. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and is popular in gardens and as a houseplant.
