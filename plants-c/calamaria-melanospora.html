Calamaria melanospora

Calamaria melanospora, commonly known as the black-spored calamaria, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in a basal rosette pattern.

The dark, spore-like structures that give it its name are produced in the flower heads and play a role in its reproductive cycle.
