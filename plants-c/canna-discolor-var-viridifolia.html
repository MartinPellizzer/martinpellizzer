Canna discolor var. viridifolia

Canna discolor var. viridifolia, commonly known as the green-leafed canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to Central and South America.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, providing a lush backdrop for its showy, orange to red flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage and inflorescence contribute to its popularity in tropical and subtropical gardens.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and can be used in water gardens or as a border plant.
