Calamagrostis purpurascens subsp. tasuensis

Calamagrostis purpurascens subsp.

tasuensis, commonly known as the Tasu reed, is a grass species native to Japan. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking purple-tinged foliage.

The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
