Capparis elegans

Capparis elegans, commonly known as the sweet-scented caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pink flowers and its woody, shrub-like structure. The plant produces small, edible buds that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Capparis elegans thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal gardens.
