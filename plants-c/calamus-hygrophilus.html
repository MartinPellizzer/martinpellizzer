Calamus hygrophilus

Calamus hygrophilus, commonly known as the water calamus, is a tropical plant belonging to the Arecaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. This plant thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks, where it can absorb excess moisture. Its rhizomes contain essential oils and have been used for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

Calamus hygrophilus plays an important role in the ecological balance of its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
