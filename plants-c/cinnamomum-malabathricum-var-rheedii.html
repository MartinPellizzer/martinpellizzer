Cinnamomum malabathricum var. rheedii

Cinnamomum malabathricum var. rheedii, commonly known as the Malabar cinnamon or rheedii cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its aromatic, aromatic bark that is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by dark purple berries, which contain small, hard seeds. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a glossy surface and a strong, spicy aroma.

This variety is closely related to other cinnamon species but is distinguished by its unique fragrance and habitat preferences.
