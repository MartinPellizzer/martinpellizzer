Chrysophyllum reitzianum

Chrysophyllum reitzianum, commonly known as the African cherry, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits with a sweet, edible pulp and a hard, starchy seed. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit, which is used in local cuisines.

The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
