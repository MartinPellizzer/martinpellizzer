Centaurea phrygia subsp. alutacea

Centaurea phrygia subsp.

alutacea, commonly known as the Anatolian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to Turkey and parts of the Mediterranean region. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, with a distinctive central disk surrounded by ray florets.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
