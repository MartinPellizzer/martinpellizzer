Clinopodium chinense var. shibetchense

Clinopodium chinense var. shibetchense, commonly known as Japanese mint or Shibu mint, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a serrated margin. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pale blue to violet, arranged in whorled clusters at the leaf axils.

This variety is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
