Cordia revoluta var. nigricans

Cordia revoluta var. nigricans, commonly known as black cordia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by orange to red fruits, which contain several seeds. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
