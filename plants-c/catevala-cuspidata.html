Catevala cuspidata

Catevala cuspidata, commonly known as the pointed leaf orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and lance-shaped leaves that are often pointed at the tips. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, with intricate patterns resembling those of other orchid species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Catevala cuspidata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
