Cassia hildebrandtii

Cassia hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's cassia, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain seeds covered in a fine, hairy coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for arid and semi-arid environments.

Cassia hildebrandtii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
