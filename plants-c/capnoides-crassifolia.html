Capnoides crassifolia

Capnoides crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf capnoid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, aiding in water retention. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology makes it an interesting subject for botanical study and ecological research.
