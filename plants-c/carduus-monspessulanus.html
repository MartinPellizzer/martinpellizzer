Carduus monspessulanus

Carduus monspessulanus, commonly known as the Spanish thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with a robust root system that allows it to thrive in various soil types. Its leaves are covered in coarse, prickly hairs, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

The flowers appear in late summer and are visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
