Cinnamomum purpureum

Cinnamomum purpureum, commonly known as the purple cinnamon or purple camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark, which contain essential oils and are used in traditional medicine. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple on the underside, giving it its common name. It produces small yellow flowers and berry-like fruits that contain aromatic seeds.

The tree is valued for its medicinal properties and is also used in the production of perfumes and spices.
