Cyperus stradbrokensis

Cyperus stradbrokensis, commonly known as the Stradbrok sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its slender, triangular stems are often covered with small, scale-like leaves.

Cyperus stradbrokensis produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the sedge family.
