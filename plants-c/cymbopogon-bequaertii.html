Cymbopogon bequaertii

Cymbopogon bequaertii, commonly known as the African lemongrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and aromatic, lemon-scented leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. The plant belongs to the Poaceae family and is closely related to other lemongrass species. Its essential oils, rich in citral, are valued for their fragrance and potential therapeutic properties.

Cymbopogon bequaertii thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
