Clematis heracleifolia var. taiwanica

Clematis heracleifolia var. taiwanica, commonly known as the Taiwan Clematis, is a climbing vine native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with three to five oval leaflets, and it thrives in partial shade to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its floral display.
