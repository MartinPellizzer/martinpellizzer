Carpinus tschonoskii var. falcatibracteata

Carpinus tschonoskii var. falcatibracteata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hornbeam, is a variant of the hornbeam genus native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often slightly curved, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along riverbanks or in mountainous regions. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, while its fruits are woody drupes that contain a single seed.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
