Cymbopogon modicus

Cymbopogon modicus, commonly known as lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and aromatic, lemon-scented leaves that are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its rhizomes and roots are often harvested for their essential oils, which contain compounds like citral and myrcene.

Cymbopogon modicus thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its fragrance and versatility in various industries.
