Carduus elongatus

Carduus elongatus, commonly known as the slender thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, purple-blue flower heads that bloom in summer. The plant has deeply lobed, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands.

The flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, making it an important species in supporting biodiversity.
