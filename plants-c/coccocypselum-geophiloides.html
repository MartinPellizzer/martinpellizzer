Coccocypselum geophiloides

Coccocypselum geophiloides, commonly known as the rock cypress, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features dense, compact foliage with small, leathery leaves that are often blue-green in color. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round fruits.

It is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
