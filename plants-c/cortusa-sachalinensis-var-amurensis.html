Cortusa sachalinensis var. amurensis

Cortusa sachalinensis var. amurensis, commonly known as the Amur bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern regions of Asia.

It features a rosette of basal leaves and produces showy blue to purple flowers in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage is characterized by lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to temperate climates.
