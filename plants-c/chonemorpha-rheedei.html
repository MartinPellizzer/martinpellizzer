Chonemorpha rheedei

Chonemorpha rheedei, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that help it retain water in harsh environments. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is often used in xeriscaping and as an ornamental plant in dry climates.
