Chaenorhinum rubrifolium subsp. gerense

Chaenorhinum rubrifolium subsp.

gerense, commonly known as the Gerense horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its striking red to orange-red leaves and showy, spurred flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
