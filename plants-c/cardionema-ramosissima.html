Cardionema ramosissima

Cardionema ramosissima, commonly known as the silky oaks, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its dense, spreading canopy and bipinnate leaves. The leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets that give the plant its "silky" appearance. It produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months.

The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value.
