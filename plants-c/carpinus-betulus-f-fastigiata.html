Carpinus betulus f. fastigiata

Carpinus betulus f. fastigiata, commonly known as the columnar hornbeam, is a cultivar of the hornbeam tree characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter green underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by small, spherical fruits that are dispersed by wind. It is a hardy deciduous tree that thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

This form is often used in landscaping for its compact size and ornamental appeal.
