Curcuma trichosantha

Curcuma trichosantha, commonly known as the hairy turmeric, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow-orange flowers, and its leaves are broad and lance-shaped. The rhizomes of Curcuma trichosantha are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
