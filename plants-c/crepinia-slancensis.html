Crepinia slancensis

Crepinia slancensis, commonly known as the slender crepe jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling crepe paper. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant aroma.

Crepinia slancensis is typically found in coastal areas and near riverbanks, where it can tolerate saline conditions.
