Carex argyrostachys

Carex argyrostachys, commonly known as the silverhair sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with silvery-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. Native to the northern hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun. The plant produces small, inconspicuous brown flowers in summer, which are clustered in spikes.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate wet conditions.
