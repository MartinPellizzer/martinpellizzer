Carex siccata subsp. pallida

Carex siccata subsp.

pallida, commonly known as the pale sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert environments. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it survive in harsh conditions.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and habitat restoration projects.
