Cyperus impubes var. fallax

Cyperus impubes var. fallax, commonly known as the fallax flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its flowering spikes are usually short and inconspicuous, bearing small, three-lobed flowers.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.
