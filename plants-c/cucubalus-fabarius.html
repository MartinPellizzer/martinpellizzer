Cucubalus fabarius

Cucubalus fabarius, commonly known as the African eggplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, round, purple fruits that resemble eggs, hence its common name. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The leaves are typically broad and lobed, with a slightly hairy texture.
