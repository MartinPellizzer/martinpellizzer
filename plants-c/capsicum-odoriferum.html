Capsicum odoriferum

Capsicum odoriferum, commonly known as the bird pepper or stink pepper, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and the flowers are yellow with five petals.

The fruit is usually green when unripe and turns red when mature, containing numerous seeds.
