Cardamomum dallachyi

Cardamomum dallachyi, commonly known as the Socotra cardamom, is a flowering plant native to the island of Socotra.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, branched inflorescence and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky substrates.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
