Clematis apiifolia subsp. franchetii

Clematis apiifolia subsp.

franchetii, commonly known as the Franchet's Clematis, is a flowering plant native to East Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its showy, usually blue or purple flowers.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or moist, shaded areas. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
