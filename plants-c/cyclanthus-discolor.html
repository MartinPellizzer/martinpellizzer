Cyclanthus discolor

Cyclanthus discolor, commonly known as the white cyclanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyclanthaceae and is characterized by its unique, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of small white flowers. The plant has strap-like leaves that are typically green and often have a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cyclanthus discolor is not commonly found in the wild due to its specific habitat requirements and limited distribution.
