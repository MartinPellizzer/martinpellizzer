Curcuma ecalcarata

Curcuma ecalcarata, commonly known as the spiky ginger or mountain ginger, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan. The plant is characterized by its ornamental, sword-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to orange flowers. Its rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though it is primarily cultivated for its aesthetic value.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens.
