Canarium ellipsoideum

Canarium ellipsoideum, commonly known as the elliptic candlenut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed surrounded by a hard, oily shell. It is valued for its oil-rich seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of biodiesel.

The plant also plays a role in local ecosystems and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
