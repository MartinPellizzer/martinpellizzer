Cupressus chengiana var. wenchuanhsiensis

Cupressus chengiana var. wenchuanhsiensis, commonly known as the Wenchuanhsiensis Cupressus, is a rare and endemic coniferous species native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and compact, pyramidal growth habit. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and is adapted to high-altitude environments with cool, moist climates. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
