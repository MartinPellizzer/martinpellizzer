Clematis cirrhosa f. micrantha

Clematis cirrhosa f. micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered clematis, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that are covered in fine, grayish hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three heart-shaped leaflets that are often pubescent.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its graceful form and seasonal blooms.
