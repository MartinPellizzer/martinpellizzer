Cytisus tridentatus

Cytisus tridentatus, commonly known as the three-toothed broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, often thorny stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various environments.

Cytisus tridentatus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
