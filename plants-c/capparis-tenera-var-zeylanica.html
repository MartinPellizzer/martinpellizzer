Capparis tenera var. zeylanica

Capparis tenera var. zeylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon glorybower, is a flowering plant native to Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas or on rocky slopes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
