Codiaeum pilgrimii

Codiaeum pilgrimii, commonly known as the Pilgrim's croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that display shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its striking foliage rather than its flowers. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
