Crataegus scabrida var. cyclophylla

Crataegus scabrida var. cyclophylla, commonly known as the smooth thorned hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, thornless stems and oval, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open woods.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological and ornamental purposes.
