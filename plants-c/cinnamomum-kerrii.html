Cinnamomum kerrii

Cinnamomum kerrii, commonly known as Kerri's cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant bark, which is used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by round, dark blue berries. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a pleasant, spicy scent.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and its potential medicinal properties.
