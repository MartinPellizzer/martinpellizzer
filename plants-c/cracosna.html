Cracosna

Cracosna, commonly known as the common thistle.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is found across Europe and parts of Asia. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The flowers are composite, consisting of many small florets that bloom in the summer months.
