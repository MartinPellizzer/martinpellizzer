Cheirostylis tortilacinia

Cheirostylis tortilacinia, commonly known as the spiral-styled orchid, is a tropical plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of petals and sepals, which gives it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the undergrowth of tropical forests. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, attracting various pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
