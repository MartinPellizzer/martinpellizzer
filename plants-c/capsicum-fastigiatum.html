Capsicum fastigiatum

Capsicum fastigiatum, commonly known as the horned pepper, is a species of chili pepper native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-like shape and vibrant red or orange color when mature. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a slightly hairy surface.

The fruit develops in clusters and is often used in traditional cuisines for its spicy flavor.
