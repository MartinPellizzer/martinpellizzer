Celastrus articulatus var. humilis

Celastrus articulatus var. humilis, commonly known as the low prickly currant, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and along stream banks. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, red, fleshy berries.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
