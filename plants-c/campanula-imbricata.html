Campanula imbricata

Campanula imbricata, commonly known as the rock campanula, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and sometimes branched.

Campanula imbricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and alpine settings.
