Capsicum silvestre

Capsicum silvestre, commonly known as wild pepper or mountain pepper, is a perennial shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that range in color from green to red, depending on maturity. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

Capsicum silvestre is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
