Ceropegia arcuata

Ceropegia arcuata, commonly known as the silver string plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and small, waxy, succulent leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are often pale green or yellowish in color. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Ceropegia arcuata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
