Campanula rapunculus var. grandiflora

Campanula rapunculus var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, with a distinctive basal rosette formation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This plant is noted for its ornamental value and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
