Codia serratifolia

Codia serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated-leaf codia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by oval, woody fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
