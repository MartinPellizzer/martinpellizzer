Coilonox concordianum

Coilonox concordianum, commonly known as the Concordian flamebush, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. Its stems are woody and can grow up to several meters in height, making it a striking addition to botanical gardens.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its ecological benefits in native habitats.
