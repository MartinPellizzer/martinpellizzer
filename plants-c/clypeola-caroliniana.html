Clypeola caroliniana

Clypeola caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina spurge, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Clypeola caroliniana is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread vegetatively, making it both a common and sometimes invasive species in its native habitat.
