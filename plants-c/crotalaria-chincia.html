Crotalaria chincia

Crotalaria chincia, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and distinctive seed pods that split open when mature. The plant is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep root system that helps stabilize soil. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
