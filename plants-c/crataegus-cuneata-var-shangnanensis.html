Crataegus cuneata var. shangnanensis

Crataegus cuneata var. shangnanensis, commonly known as the Shangnan hawthorn, is a shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, deciduous leaves. The plant produces white or pink flowers in spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. It is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in regional flora studies.
