Cynomarathrum megarrhizum

Cynomarathrum megarrhizum, commonly known as the broadroot grass, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fibrous root system and tufted, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often in rocky or sandy soils. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Cynomarathrum megarrhizum is adapted to withstand drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
