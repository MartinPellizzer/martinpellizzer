Carex lyngbyei subsp. prionocarpa

Carex lyngbyei subsp.

prionocarpa, commonly known as Lyngby's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, bogs, and wet meadows. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with three stamens and a single pistil, typical of the sedge family.

It plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
