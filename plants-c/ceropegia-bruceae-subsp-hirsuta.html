Ceropegia bruceae subsp. hirsuta

Ceropegia bruceae subsp.

hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy ceropegia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features fleshy, branching stems that are often covered in fine, hairy pubescence.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive, intricate structure. It thrives in arid or semi-arid conditions and is adapted to store water in its thick, fleshy stems.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
