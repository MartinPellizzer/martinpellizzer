Calanthe anthropophora

Calanthe anthropophora, commonly known as the human-faced calanthe, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on forest floors or rocky slopes. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth, waxy surface, and it produces spikes of pale green to white flowers.

Calanthe anthropophora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
