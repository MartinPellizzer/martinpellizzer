Crotalaria vasculosa

Crotalaria vasculosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cowpea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers that are often clustered in racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are used as a food source in some regions.

The plant is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
