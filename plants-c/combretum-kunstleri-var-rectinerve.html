Combretum kunstleri var. rectinerve

Combretum kunstleri var. rectinerve, commonly known as the narrow-veined flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange floral bracts that appear in clusters, giving it its common name. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a prominent midrib, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various tropical and subtropical environments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
