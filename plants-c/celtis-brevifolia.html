Celtis brevifolia

Celtis brevifolia, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically 2-5 cm in length. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, red berries that are edible for some bird species. Celtis brevifolia is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes.
