Cola tsandensis

Cola tsandensis, commonly known as the Tsando cola, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its rough, woody stems and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that develop into hard, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. The seeds are often used in traditional medicine and have been studied for their potential pharmacological properties.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
