Camellia subglabra

Camellia subglabra, commonly known as the smooth camellia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Theaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its glossy, dark green, leathery leaves that are smooth to the touch. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Camellia subglabra is an evergreen shrub that thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance once established.
