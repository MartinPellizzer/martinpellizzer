Cousinia pycnoloba

Cousinia pycnoloba, commonly known as the dense-leaved fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features compact, mat-forming growth with silvery-gray leaves that help reduce water loss. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense heads, often appearing in late summer.

Cousinia pycnoloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and aesthetic qualities.
