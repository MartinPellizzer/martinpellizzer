Cassinia rosmarinifolia

Cassinia rosmarinifolia, commonly known as silverleaf cassinia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant features narrow, silvery-gray leaves that give it its common name, and it produces small, yellowish flower heads that bloom in summer. Cassinia rosmarinifolia is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

It is also used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
