Cineraria anampoza f. hygrophila

Cineraria anampoza f. hygrophila, commonly known as the water cineraria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is native to regions with moist, temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss and reflect sunlight.

This variety is appreciated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
