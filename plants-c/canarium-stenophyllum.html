Canarium stenophyllum

Canarium stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved candlenut, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces edible seeds encased in a hard shell, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
