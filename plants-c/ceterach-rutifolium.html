Ceterach rutifolium

Ceterach rutifolium, commonly known as the silverweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and wetlands.

Ceterach rutifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone environments.
