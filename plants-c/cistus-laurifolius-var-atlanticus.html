Cistus laurifolius var. atlanticus

Cistus laurifolius var. atlanticus, commonly known as the Atlantic laurel rockrose, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of western Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with leathery, dark green leaves that are oval and slightly serrated. The plant produces showy, pale yellow to orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in terminal umbels. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh maritime conditions.
