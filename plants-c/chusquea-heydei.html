Chusquea heydei

Chusquea heydei, commonly known as the Andean bamboo, is a perennial evergreen shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach up to 6 meters in height. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in montane forests.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
