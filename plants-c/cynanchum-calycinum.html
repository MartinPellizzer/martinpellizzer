Cynanchum calycinum

Cynanchum calycinum, commonly known as the purple milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features compound leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Cynanchum calycinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
