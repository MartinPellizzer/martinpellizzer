Crassula sieberiana subsp. sieberiana

Crassula sieberiana subsp.

sieberiana, commonly known as the Sieber's crassula, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green to blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale pink during the growing season. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact size and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
