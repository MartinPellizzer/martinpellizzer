Croton fulvus

Croton fulvus, commonly known as the yellow croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often variegated leaves that display shades of yellow, green, and red. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
