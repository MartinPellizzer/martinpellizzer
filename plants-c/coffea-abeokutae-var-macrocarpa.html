Coffea abeokutae var. macrocarpa

Coffea abeokutae var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the West African coffee tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of West Africa.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces red, fleshy fruits known as berries, each containing two seeds used for coffee production. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil.

This variety is notable for its unique flavor profile and is cultivated for both its ornamental value and potential agricultural use.
