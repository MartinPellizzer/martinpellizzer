Croton polytrichus

Croton polytrichus, commonly known as the woolly croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to red, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique foliage and adaptability, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in tropical landscapes.
