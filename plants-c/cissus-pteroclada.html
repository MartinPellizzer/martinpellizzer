Cissus pteroclada

Cissus pteroclada, commonly known as the African Stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its stems can grow horizontally along the ground or climb using tendrils, making it adaptable to various growing conditions.

Cissus pteroclada is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
