Cleome ariana

Cleome ariana, commonly known as the African spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spindly stems and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, yellow or white flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Cleome ariana is typically grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in full sun.
