Carex atrata var. spiculosior

Carex atrata var. spiculosior, commonly known as the dark sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and wetlands, and is often found in shaded areas.

Its botanical structure supports its role in wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and contributing to soil stabilization.
