Conchocarpus limae

Conchocarpus limae, commonly known as the lima tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The tree produces small, edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. Its botanical features include a woody stem, alternate leaves, and a flowering pattern that typically occurs in the dry season.

The plant is also valued for its timber and has some medicinal uses in local communities.
