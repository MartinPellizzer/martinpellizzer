Calligonum medusecephalum

Calligonum medusecephalum, commonly known as the Medusa's head saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded, and often fleshy leaves that resemble the head of the mythological Medusa. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It is well adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable plant for soil stabilization in harsh environments.

Its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
