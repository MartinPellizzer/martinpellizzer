Carex media

Carex media, commonly known as narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, grasses, and rushes. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges. Carex media has grass-like leaves that are narrow and slightly pointed, with a distinctive triangular stem.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, followed by clusters of three-sided, spike-like fruits.
