Crotalaria lotifolia

Crotalaria lotifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered cowpea, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with prominent black veins, and its trifoliate leaves. It produces elongated pods containing kidney-shaped seeds, which are often used as a food source in some cultures.

Crotalaria lotifolia is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural practices.
