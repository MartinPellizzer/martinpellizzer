Chusquea fimbriata

Chusquea fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms with a distinctive fringed appearance on the leaf sheaths. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes, often forming dense clumps or tussocks. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Chusquea fimbriata is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
