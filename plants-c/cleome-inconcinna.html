Cleome inconcinna

Cleome inconcinna, commonly known as the slender cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, branching stems that often reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in groups of three at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Cleome inconcinna is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
