Carex aperta var. viridans

Carex aperta var. viridans, commonly known as the green-winged sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically green with a subtle bluish tinge, and it produces spikes of small, brownish flowers in the summer. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies. It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.

Carex aperta var. viridans is an important component of aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
