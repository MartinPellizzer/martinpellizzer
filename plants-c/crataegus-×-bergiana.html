Crataegus × bergiana

Crataegus × bergiana, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a hybrid species native to China.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3–8 meters. The plant features thorny branches, oval to elliptical leaves, and small, white or pinkish flowers that appear in spring. Its fruit is a red or orange-red berry that matures in late summer or early autumn.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
