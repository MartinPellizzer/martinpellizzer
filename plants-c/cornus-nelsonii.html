Cornus nelsonii

Cornus nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's dogwood, is a species of shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy white bracts that surround small, greenish-yellow flowers, which appear in late spring. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and mountainous regions.

Cornus nelsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
