Copiapoa hypogaea var. montana

Copiapoa hypogaea var. montana, commonly known as the Monte Desert cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is a columnar cactus with a short, thick stem and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. The plant is adapted to extreme arid conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. It has a slow growth rate and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The varietal name "montana" refers to its occurrence in the mountainous regions of the Atacama Desert.
