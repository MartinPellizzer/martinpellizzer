Campanula thomasii

Campanula thomasii, commonly known as Thomas's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often at elevations above 6,000 feet. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and sometimes branched.

Campanula thomasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
