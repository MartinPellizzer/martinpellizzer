Catalpa speciosa f. albovariegata

Catalpa speciosa f. albovariegata, commonly known as the white-veined catalpa, is a cultivar of the catalpa tree characterized by its striking variegated foliage.

Its leaves are typically a mix of white, yellow, and green, creating a visually appealing contrast against the dark green stems. The plant produces large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring, adding to its ornamental value. It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
