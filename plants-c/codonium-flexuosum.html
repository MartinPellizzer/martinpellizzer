Codonium flexuosum

Codonium flexuosum, commonly known as the flexible codon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, creeping stems that can form dense mats, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped. Small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptability and role in supporting local biodiversity.
