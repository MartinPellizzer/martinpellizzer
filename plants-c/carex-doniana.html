Carex doniana

Carex doniana, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to various regions of Asia, including China, India, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks.

Carex doniana is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
