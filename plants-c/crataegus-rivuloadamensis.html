Crataegus rivuloadamensis

Crataegus rivuloadamensis, commonly known as the riverbank hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to specific regions in China and is characterized by its small, thorny branches and white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found along riverbanks and in woodland edges.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
