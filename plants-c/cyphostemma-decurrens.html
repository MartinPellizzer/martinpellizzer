Cyphostemma decurrens

Cyphostemma decurrens, commonly known as the African moon vine, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, simple, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary racemes, followed by edible, round, yellow fruits. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Cyphostemma decurrens is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local communities.
