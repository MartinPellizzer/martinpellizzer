Carex schreberi f. podolica

Carex schreberi f. podolica, commonly known as the Podolic sedge, is a botanical variant of the sedge genus Carex.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of greenish-yellow flowers. This plant is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in temperate regions. It is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in wetland environments.

The subspecies is notable for its adaptability to various soil conditions and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
