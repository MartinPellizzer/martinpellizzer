Centaurea grisebachii subsp. grisebachii

Centaurea grisebachii subsp.

grisebachii, commonly known as the grey centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces small, spiny bracts beneath the flower heads, which are a distinguishing feature. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens and natural habitats.
