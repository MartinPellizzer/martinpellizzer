Calamus monticola

Calamus monticola, commonly known as mountain calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in wet, tropical environments and is often found in swampy or marshy areas.

Calamus monticola is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
