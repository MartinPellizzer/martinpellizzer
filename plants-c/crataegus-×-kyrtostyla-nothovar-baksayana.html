Crataegus × kyrtostyla nothovar. baksayana

Crataegus × kyrtostyla nothovar. baksayana, commonly known as the Baksayana hawthorn, is a hybrid plant belonging to the genus Crataegus in the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible and used in culinary or medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in temperate regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and hedges.
