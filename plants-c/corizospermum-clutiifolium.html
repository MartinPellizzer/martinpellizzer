Corizospermum clutiifolium

Corizospermum clutiifolium, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in the morning. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Convolvulaceae, which includes other morning glory species.
