Cirsium ochrocentrum var. townsendii

Cirsium ochrocentrum var. townsendii, commonly known as Townsend's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
