Cassia goratensis var. kethulleana

Cassia goratensis var. kethulleana, commonly known as the Kethulleana cassia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged in an alternate pattern along the stems. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are encased in elongated, pod-like structures that split open when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
