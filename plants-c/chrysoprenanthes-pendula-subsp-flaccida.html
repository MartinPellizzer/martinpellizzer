Chrysoprenanthes pendula subsp. flaccida

Chrysoprenanthes pendula subsp.

flaccida, commonly known as the hanging chrysoprenanthes, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its pendulous growth habit, with long, drooping stems that bear clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers.

The leaves are typically alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This species thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological study.
