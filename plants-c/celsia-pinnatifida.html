Celsia pinnatifida

Celsia pinnatifida, commonly known as the featherleaf celsia, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant features finely divided, feathery leaves that give it its common name, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
