Chrysodium sagenioides

Chrysodium sagenioides, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and vibrant yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sunlight. Its flowers bloom in the spring and are often clustered at the ends of the stems.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
