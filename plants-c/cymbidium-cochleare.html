Cymbidium cochleare

Cymbidium cochleare, commonly known as the spiral orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its spiral-shaped flower spike, which gives it its common name. The plant typically produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cymbidium cochleare is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
