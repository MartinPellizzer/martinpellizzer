Cydonia japonica var. roseaplena

Cydonia japonica var. roseaplena, commonly known as the Japanese quince rose.

This variety is a cultivar of the Japanese quince, known for its ornamental value and unique floral characteristics. It produces showy, double pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The plant is deciduous, with a compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
