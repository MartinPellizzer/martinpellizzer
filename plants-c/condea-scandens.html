Condea scandens

Condea scandens, commonly known as the climbing condea, is a vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing habit and ability to climb using aerial roots. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Condea scandens is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
