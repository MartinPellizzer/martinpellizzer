Centaurea isernii var. latiloba

Centaurea isernii var. latiloba, commonly known as the broad-leaved centaurea, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads surrounded by bright yellow ray florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branching.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
