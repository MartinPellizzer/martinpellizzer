Caleana gracilicordata

Caleana gracilicordata, commonly known as the slender-heart orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, spurred labellum that resembles a heart shape, which is a distinctive botanical feature. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with a striking contrast provided by the dark red or brown markings.

Caleana gracilicordata is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms involving specific insect species.
