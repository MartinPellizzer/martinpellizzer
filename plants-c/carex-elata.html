Carex elata

Carex elata, commonly known as tall sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and produces spikes of small, greenish flowers. Native to North America, it thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and can grow up to 2 to 3 feet tall.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
