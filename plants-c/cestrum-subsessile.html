Cestrum subsessile

Cestrum subsessile, commonly known as the night-blooming jasmine, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that open at night and emit a strong, sweet scent. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its flowering pattern makes it a popular choice for evening gardens and floral arrangements.
