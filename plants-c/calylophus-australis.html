Calylophus australis

Calylophus australis, commonly known as the Australian bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically red or pink in color. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is known for its adaptability to dry conditions. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Calylophus australis thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and vibrant floral display.
