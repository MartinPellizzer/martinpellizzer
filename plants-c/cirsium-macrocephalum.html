Cirsium macrocephalum

Cirsium macrocephalum, commonly known as the giant thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, spiny, purple-tinged flower heads that can reach up to 20 centimeters in diameter. The plant has robust, hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
