Clusia centricupula

Clusia centricupula, commonly known as the waxy leaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, providing a striking visual appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Clusia centricupula is also valued for its ability to tolerate shade and poor soil conditions, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
