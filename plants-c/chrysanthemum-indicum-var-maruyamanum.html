Chrysanthemum indicum var. maruyamanum

Chrysanthemum indicum var. maruyamanum, commonly known as the Japanese chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, daisy-like flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically blooms in the fall and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is also cultivated for its use in traditional medicine and as a symbol of longevity and good fortune in some cultures.
