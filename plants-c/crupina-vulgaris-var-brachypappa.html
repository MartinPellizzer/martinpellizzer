Crupina vulgaris var. brachypappa

Crupina vulgaris var. brachypappa, commonly known as narrow-leaved crupina, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its ornamental value and hardy nature make it a popular choice for landscaping in dry climates.
