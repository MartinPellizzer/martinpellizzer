Campanula alphonsii

Campanula alphonsii, commonly known as the Alphonse bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula alphonsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
