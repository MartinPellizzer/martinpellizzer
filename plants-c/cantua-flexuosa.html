Cantua flexuosa

Cantua flexuosa, commonly known as the bent-calyx bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, often with a distinctive bent calyx. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Cantua flexuosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom in cooler climates.
