Coleochloa abyssinica

Coleochloa abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to Ethiopia, characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Coleochloa abyssinica is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, playing a role in soil stabilization and as forage for livestock.
