Crataegus integerrima

Crataegus integerrima, commonly known as the smooth hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves that are typically smooth and dark green on top with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

Crataegus integerrima is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
