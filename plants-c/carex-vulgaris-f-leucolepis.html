Carex vulgaris f. leucolepis

Carex vulgaris f. leucolepis, commonly known as the white-leaved sedges, is a variant of the common sedge.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically pale green to white in color. This plant thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, three-sided flowers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
