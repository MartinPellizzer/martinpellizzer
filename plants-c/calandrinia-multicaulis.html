Calandrinia multicaulis

Calandrinia multicaulis, commonly known as the multibranched portulaca, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical features include small, opposite leaves and a branched inflorescence that enhances its ornamental appeal.
