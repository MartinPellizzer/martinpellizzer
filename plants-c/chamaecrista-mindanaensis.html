Chamaecrista mindanaensis

Chamaecrista mindanaensis, commonly known as the Mindanao ticktree, is a shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in tropical climates. Its seeds are encased in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind.

Chamaecrista mindanaensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local communities.
