Chrysanthemum balsamitoides

Chrysanthemum balsamitoides, commonly known as the balsam leaf chrysanthemum, is a species native to southern China and northern Vietnam.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and clusters of small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The flowering period occurs in the late summer to early autumn, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking late-season blooms.
