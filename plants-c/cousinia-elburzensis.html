Cousinia elburzensis

Cousinia elburzensis, commonly known as the Elburz mountain cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to harsh climatic conditions.

Cousinia elburzensis is often found in elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters.
