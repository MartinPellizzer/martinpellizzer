Calamagrostis purpurea subsp. trinii

Calamagrostis purpurea subsp.

trinii, commonly known as purple moor grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and panicles of purple-tinted flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils, often found in heathlands and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
