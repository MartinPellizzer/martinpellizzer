Crotalaria eriantha

Crotalaria eriantha, commonly known as the silverleaf crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant is known for its hairy, silver-green foliage, which gives it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

Crotalaria eriantha is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
