Carex procerula

Carex procerula, commonly known as slender sedges, is a species of sedge native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in temperate climates.

Carex procerula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
