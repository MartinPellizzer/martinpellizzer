Calamus maritimus

Calamus maritimus, commonly known as sea reed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is a tall, perennial grass that typically grows in coastal areas and saline wetlands. The plant has long, slender leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and crafts. Its rhizomes are fibrous and have been utilized for various practical purposes.

Calamus maritimus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various organisms.
