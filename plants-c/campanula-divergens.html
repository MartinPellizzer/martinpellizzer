Campanula divergens

Campanula divergens, commonly known as the spreading bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a soft texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, often found in meadows and rocky areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
