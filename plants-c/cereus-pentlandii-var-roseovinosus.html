Cereus pentlandii var. roseovinosus

Cereus pentlandii var. roseovinosus, commonly known as the Pink Venus Cactus, is a flowering cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming at night. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance, it is a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
