Cissampelos pilgeri

Cissampelos pilgeri, commonly known as the African moon vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its stems are woody and often twine around supporting structures, making it a common ornamental in warm climates.

Cissampelos pilgeri is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
