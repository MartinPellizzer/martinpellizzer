Coronilla chia

Coronilla chia, commonly known as the "chia plant," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, round seeds that are rich in omega-3 fatty acids, fiber, and protein. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually blue or purple and attract pollinators such as bees.

The seeds are often used in food products and have been valued for their nutritional benefits for centuries.
