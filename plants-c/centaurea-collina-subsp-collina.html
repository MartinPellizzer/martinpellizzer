Centaurea collina subsp. collina

Centaurea collina subsp.

collina, commonly known as the Hoary Centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, spiny leaves covered in fine white hairs.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with red-brown centers, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
