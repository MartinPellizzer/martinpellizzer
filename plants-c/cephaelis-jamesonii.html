Cephaelis jamesonii

Cephaelis jamesonii, commonly known as the Jameson's celandine, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and medicinal properties.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
