Carex canescens

Carex canescens, commonly known as the gray sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex canescens plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
