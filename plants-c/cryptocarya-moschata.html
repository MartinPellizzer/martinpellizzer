Cryptocarya moschata

Cryptocarya moschata, commonly known as the muskwood or fragrant cryptocarya, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, woody berry containing several seeds.

Cryptocarya moschata is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
