Carex fusca f. intricata

Carex fusca f. intricata, commonly known as the intricately branched sedges, is a variant of the common sedge, Carex fusca.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and the presence of specialized leaf structures called culms. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its botanical features include narrow, cylindrical leaves and small, clustered inflorescences.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
