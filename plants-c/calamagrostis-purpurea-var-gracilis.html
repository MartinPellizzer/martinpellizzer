Calamagrostis purpurea var. gracilis

Calamagrostis purpurea var. gracilis, commonly known as purple moor grass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and striking purple or pinkish tints in its inflorescence. This plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, bogs, and heathlands. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet conditions.
