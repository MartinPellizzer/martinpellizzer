Clethra alnifolia var. pubescens

Clethra alnifolia var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy clethra, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
