Caesalpinia minutiflora

Caesalpinia minutiflora, commonly known as the dwarf flame tree, is a small shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually pinnate with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces bright orange or red flowers that are clustered in racemes, adding a striking visual display during the flowering season. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Caesalpinia minutiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
