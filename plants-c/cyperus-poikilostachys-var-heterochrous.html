Cyperus poikilostachys var. heterochrous

Cyperus poikilostachys var. heterochrous, commonly known as the variable flatsedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It features narrow, linear leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences with small, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant is often found in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in saturated soils. Its variability in stamen number and arrangement gives it the specific epithet "heterochrous," reflecting its morphological diversity.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
