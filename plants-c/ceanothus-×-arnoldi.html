Ceanothus × arnoldi

Ceanothus × arnoldi, commonly known as the California lilac, is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rhamnaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for coastal and Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are typically leathery and dark green, while its flowers bloom in early spring.

Ceanothus × arnoldi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
