Crataegus crus-galli var. attenuata

Crataegus crus-galli var. attenuata, commonly known as the slender dogwood, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches and clusters of white to pinkish flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are a food source for various birds and small mammals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The leaves are typically oval with serrated edges and turn red in the fall, adding ornamental value to the landscape.
