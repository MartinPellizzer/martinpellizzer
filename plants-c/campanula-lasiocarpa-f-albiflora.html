Campanula lasiocarpa f. albiflora

Campanula lasiocarpa f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered bellflower, is a horticultural form of the species Campanula lasiocarpa.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped, white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is a perennial herb with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators.
