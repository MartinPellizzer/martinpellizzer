Calacinum

Calacinum, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, which give it its characteristic appearance. Calacinum produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
