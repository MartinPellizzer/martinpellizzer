Cirsium acuatum

Cirsium acuatum, commonly known as the sharpleaf thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, serrated leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

The plant's sharp leaves and stems serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores.
