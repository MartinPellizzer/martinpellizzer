Centaurea bourgaei

Centaurea bourgaei, commonly known as the Bourgai's cornflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, sparsely branched stems that can grow up to 60 centimeters in height. The plant produces bright blue to purple composite flowers with ray florets and a central disk, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Centaurea bourgaei is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
