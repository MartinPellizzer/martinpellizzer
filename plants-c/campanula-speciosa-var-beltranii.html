Campanula speciosa var. beltranii

Campanula speciosa var. beltranii, commonly known as the Beltran's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and attractive foliage.
