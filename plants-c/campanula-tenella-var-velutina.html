Campanula tenella var. velutina

Campanula tenella var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "velutina.". The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Campanula tenella var. velutina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
