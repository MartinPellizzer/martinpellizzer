Convolvulus platycarpos

Convolvulus platycarpos, commonly known as the hardstem morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often opening in the morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.

Its seeds are small and dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
