Carex flacca f. melanostachya

Carex flacca f. melanostachya, commonly known as the black-stalked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of spikelets that are typically dark brown to black in color. The plant thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, bogs, and stream banks, and is often found in temperate regions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense vegetation, contributing to soil stabilization.

The inflorescence consists of two to three spikelets, which are often subtended by a single bract, giving it a distinctive appearance.
