Calathea bullii

Calathea bullii, commonly known as the velvet leaf calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage with intricate patterns of deep green, silver, and purple. The leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often featuring a unique, mottled appearance that changes with light exposure. It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining soil.

This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
