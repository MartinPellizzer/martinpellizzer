Corydalis ochotensis var. litoralis

Corydalis ochotensis var. litoralis, commonly known as the coastal corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and purple. The plant has slender, creeping stems and lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils. It is often found in moist, well-drained habitats along the shoreline.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
