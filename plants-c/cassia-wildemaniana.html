Cassia wildemaniana

Cassia wildemaniana, commonly known as the wildeman's cassia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Cassia wildemaniana thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.
