Carex hirta f. villosa

Carex hirta f. villosa, commonly known as the hairy sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a rough texture, contributing to its common name.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in clusters that produce small, wind-pollinated seeds.
