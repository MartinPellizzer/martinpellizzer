Cantinoa racemulosa

Cantinoa racemulosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. Its flowering period occurs during the spring and early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cantinoa racemulosa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
