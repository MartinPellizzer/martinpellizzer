Campanula sarmatica f. ramosissima

Campanula sarmatica f. ramosissima, commonly known as the丛生钟花, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant in gardens with suitable conditions.
