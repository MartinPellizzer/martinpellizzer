Convolvulus melanostictus

Convolvulus melanostictus, commonly known as the black-stained bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes several species of flowering plants known for their climbing or trailing growth habits. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It is characterized by its distinctive dark purple or black markings on the stems and leaves, which give it its common name.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
