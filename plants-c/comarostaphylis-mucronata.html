Comarostaphylis mucronata

Comarostaphylis mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock heath, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of pale pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to coastal and arid environments.

Comarostaphylis mucronata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
