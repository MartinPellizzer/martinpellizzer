Campanula glomerata var. fatrae

Campanula glomerata var. fatrae, commonly known as the Fatra campanula, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine down.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
