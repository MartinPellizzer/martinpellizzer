Carex fyllae

Carex fyllae, commonly known as the Fylla sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and wetlands. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often wind-pollinated.

Carex fyllae is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
