Calendula arvensis var. bicolor

Calendula arvensis var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored field marigold, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow and orange bi-colored flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Calendula arvensis var. bicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
