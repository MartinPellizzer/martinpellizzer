Cenchrus macrourus

Cenchrus macrourus, commonly known as the long-beaked grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces panicles of small, pale green to brownish flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Cenchrus macrourus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
