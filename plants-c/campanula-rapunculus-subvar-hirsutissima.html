Campanula rapunculus subvar. hirsutissima

Campanula rapunculus subvar. hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
