Copiapoa pseudocoquimbana var. chaniarensis

Copiapoa pseudocoquimbana var. chaniarensis, commonly known as the Chaniarensis cactus, is a columnar cactus native to the Atacama Desert in northern Chile.

It features a tall, cylindrical stem with a gray-green color and sparse, white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It is adapted to extreme aridity, storing water in its thick stem and having a shallow root system.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh environments.
