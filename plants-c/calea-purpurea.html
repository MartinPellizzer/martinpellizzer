Calea purpurea

Calea purpurea, commonly known as the purple calea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. Its flowers bloom in the early morning and close by midday, a behavior known as nyctinasty.

Calea purpurea is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in folk remedies.
