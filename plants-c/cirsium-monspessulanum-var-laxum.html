Cirsium monspessulanum var. laxum

Cirsium monspessulanum var. laxum, commonly known as the lax thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves and produces purple to pink flowers in late summer. Its inflorescence is a spherical cluster of ray florets surrounded by bracts.

The species is characterized by its erect growth habit and is often found in Mediterranean climates.
