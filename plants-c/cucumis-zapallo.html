Cucumis zapallo

Cucumis zapallo, commonly known as the Mexican ridge gourd, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it thrives in warm, temperate climates. The plant produces large, elongated fruits with a ridged, warty surface and a mild, sweet flavor. Its leaves are broad and lobed, and it has tendrils that help it climb and support its growth.

The fruit is often used in traditional dishes and is valued for its nutritional content and versatility in cooking.
