Cyathula angustifolia

Cyathula angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf sandmat, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in cyathia, which are modified leaf-like structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Cyathula angustifolia is often found in sandy or disturbed soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
