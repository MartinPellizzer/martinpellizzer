Campanula rotundifolia subsp. pusilla

Campanula rotundifolia subsp.

pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to northern regions of Europe and Asia. It features small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has rounded, lobed leaves that are typically covered in a fine, downy coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its compact size and delicate appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and moist garden beds.
