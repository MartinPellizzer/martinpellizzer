Cavendishia panamensis

Cavendishia panamensis, commonly known as the Panamanian orchid, is a flowering plant native to Central America, particularly Panama.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its botanical features include narrow, strap-like leaves and a compact, pseudobulbous structure that supports its growth.

Cavendishia panamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or conservatories.
