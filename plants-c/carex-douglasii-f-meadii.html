Carex douglasii f. meadii

Carex douglasii f. meadii, commonly known as Douglas's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This subspecies is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in forested or open habitats. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered in terminal inflorescences.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
