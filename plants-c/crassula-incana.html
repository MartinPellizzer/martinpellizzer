Crassula incana

Crassula incana, commonly known as the woolly crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly coating of fine white hairs on its stems and leaves, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant typically grows in a compact, bushy form, with small, fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring or summer.

Crassula incana is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and drought tolerance.
