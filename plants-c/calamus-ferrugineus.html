Calamus ferrugineus

Calamus ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty reed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in a rusty-colored pubescence. Calamus ferrugineus typically grows in wet, swampy areas and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
