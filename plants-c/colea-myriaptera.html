Colea myriaptera

Colea myriaptera, commonly known as the many-winged cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

Colea myriaptera is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
