Claytonia virginica var. acutiflora

Claytonia virginica var. acutiflora, commonly known as the acute-flowered spring beauty, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features rosettes of ovate, hairy leaves that are often tinged with reddish or purple hues. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental member of the Portulacaceae family.
