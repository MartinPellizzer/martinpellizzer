Carex stenophylla subsp. rigescens

Carex stenophylla subsp.

rigescens, commonly known as the rigid sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually wind-pollinated, and it is often found in wetland ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland biodiversity and ecological stability.
