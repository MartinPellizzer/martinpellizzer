Cerastium gracile var. gayanum

Cerastium gracile var. gayanum, commonly known as the slender mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces delicate white flowers with five petals. The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a ground cover in dry, sunny areas.
