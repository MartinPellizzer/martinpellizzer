Caiophora cinerea

Caiophora cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf caiophora, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Caiophora cinerea is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
