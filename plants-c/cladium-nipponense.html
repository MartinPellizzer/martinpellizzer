Cladium nipponense

Cladium nipponense, commonly known as the Japanese sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into spathes. Cladium nipponense is characterized by its ability to form dense stands, contributing to the structure and biodiversity of its wetland ecosystem.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, making it an important component of riparian and coastal wetlands.
