Carex × alsatica subsp. subviridula

Carex × alsatica subsp.

subviridula, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, wetlands, and stream banks. Its leaves are usually a bluish-green color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
