Creusa sarcocaulis

Creusa sarcocaulis, commonly known as the mountain creosote.

It is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Chile and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Creusa sarcocaulis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
