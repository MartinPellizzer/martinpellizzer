Cucubalus italicus var. pilosus

Cucubalus italicus var. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy Italian cucumber, is a variant of the Italian cucumber species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinct appearance compared to other cucumber varieties. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are typically harvested when immature for culinary use. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its unique texture and flavor in Mediterranean cuisine.
