Calligonum bakuense

Calligonum bakuense, commonly known as the Baku spurge, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its erect or decumbent growth habit and produces yellow flowers in clusters. The plant has smooth, often hairy stems and leaves that are usually opposite or whorled. It thrives in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Calligonum bakuense is often found in steppe and forest-steppe habitats.
