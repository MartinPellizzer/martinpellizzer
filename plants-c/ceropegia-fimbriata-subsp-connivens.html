Ceropegia fimbriata subsp. connivens

Ceropegia fimbriata subsp.

connivens, commonly known as the African violet-like ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its trailing stems and fleshy, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a unique structure that attracts specific pollinators. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant.
