Crataegus × viidumaegica

Crataegus × viidumaegica, commonly known as the hybrid hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree that exhibits a blend of characteristics from its parent species.

It typically grows to a height of 3–6 meters, featuring thorny branches and oval to ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and used in landscaping.
