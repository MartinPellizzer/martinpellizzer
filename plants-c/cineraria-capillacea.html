Cineraria capillacea

Cineraria capillacea, commonly known as the silky cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
