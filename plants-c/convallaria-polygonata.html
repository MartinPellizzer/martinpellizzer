Convallaria polygonata

Convallaria polygonata, commonly known as the clustered lily of the valley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is known for its fragrant scent and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
