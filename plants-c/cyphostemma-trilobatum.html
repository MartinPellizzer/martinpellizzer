Cyphostemma trilobatum

Cyphostemma trilobatum, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, trifoliate leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing along fences or trees.

Cyphostemma trilobatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
