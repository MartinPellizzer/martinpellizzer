Cyathea confucii

Cyathea confucii, commonly known as the Confucius tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Cyathea confucii is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is used in horticulture and landscaping.
