Chenopodium foliosum subsp. montanum

Chenopodium foliosum subsp.

montanum, commonly known as mountain goosefoot, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family. It is native to North America and is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, typically in late summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and along stream banks.

Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it an important component of alpine and arid ecosystems.
