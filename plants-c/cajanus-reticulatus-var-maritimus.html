Cajanus reticulatus var. maritimus

Cajanus reticulatus var. maritimus, commonly known as the coastal clusterbean, is a perennial legume native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to saline and coastal environments. It produces lens-shaped pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein.

This species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility in agricultural settings.
