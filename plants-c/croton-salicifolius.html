Croton salicifolius

Croton salicifolius, commonly known as the willow-leaf croton, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, willow-like leaves that are often variegated with yellow and green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Croton salicifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage.
