Clinopodium pojarkovae

Clinopodium pojarkovae, commonly known as the Pojarkov's mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Clinopodium pojarkovae is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
