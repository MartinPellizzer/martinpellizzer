Crocus calanthus

Crocus calanthus, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia, including parts of Turkey and Iran. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive purple or red throat. It typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.

The corms of Crocus calanthus are used in traditional medicine and are also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
