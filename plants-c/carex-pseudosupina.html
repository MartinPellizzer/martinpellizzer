Carex pseudosupina

Carex pseudosupina, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, with male and female flowers often occurring on separate spikelets.
