Cenchrus longisetus

Cenchrus longisetus, commonly known as the hairy grass or longisetous grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its erect, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with prominent hairs along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish in color.

Cenchrus longisetus is adapted to arid and disturbed environments, making it a common weed in agricultural and coastal areas.
