Calceolaria utricularioides

Calceolaria utricularioides, commonly known as the bladderflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, pouch-like flowers that resemble small bladders. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers come in shades of purple, pink, and white.

Calceolaria utricularioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive blooms.
