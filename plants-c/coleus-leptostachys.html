Coleus leptostachys

Coleus leptostachys, commonly known as the narrow-stalked coleus, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage with leaves that can range from deep red to bright orange and purple. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Coleus leptostachys prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens.
