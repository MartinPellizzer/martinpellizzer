Ceanothus laniger

Ceanothus laniger, commonly known as the woolly ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage that gives it a soft, woolly appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant blue or purple flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Ceanothus laniger is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
