Chusquea caamanoi

Chusquea caamanoi, commonly known as the Andean bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant forms dense clumps with slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional basketry and construction. Its leaves are long, narrow, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Chusquea caamanoi plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species in its native range.
