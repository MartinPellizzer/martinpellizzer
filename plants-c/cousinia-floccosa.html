Cousinia floccosa

Cousinia floccosa, commonly known as the silky couchgrass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces fluffy, white to pale purple flower heads. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in full sun.

Cousinia floccosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
