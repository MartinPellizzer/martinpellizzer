Corylopsis glaucescens

Corylopsis glaucescens, commonly known as the golden thread, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, red berries. The plant has slender, arching branches and dark green, elliptical leaves that turn red in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Corylopsis glaucescens is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.
