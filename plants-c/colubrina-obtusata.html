Colubrina obtusata

Colubrina obtusata, commonly known as the broadleaf colubrina, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a distinct, waxy coating. Its showy, clustered flowers are usually white or pale blue, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.
